# The Pleroma Exchange Formula

## The Claim
[[Blake Ostler]] argues the New Testament teaches an "exchange formula": the Father gives a *fullness of deity* (Greek *plērōma*) to Christ, Christ gives that fullness to believers, and so believers come to share in the fullness of God. [[Colossians 2.9|Colossians 2:9]]: "in Christ all the fullness of the deity lives bodily... and in Christ you have been brought to fullness." [[Colossians 1.15-17|Colossians 1:15-19]], [[John 1.14|John 1:14-16]], and [[1 John 3.1-3|1 John 3]] carry the same *plērōma* language. Ostler claims the early church fathers built the "God became man so man might become God" theosis formula on exactly these verses. [^1][^2]

## The Rebuttal (Supporting Evidence)

1. **Colossians 2:9-10 is the hinge.** The fullness that dwells in Christ is the same fullness "you have been brought to." "The fullness that indwells in Christ we've been brought to this fullness." *Rating: strong* — the two clauses sit in the same sentence. [^1][^3]

2. **plērōma = shared divine glory, not bare "deity."** Ostler glosses *theotēs* (Col 2:9, KJV "Godhead") and *plērōma* as "the fullness of all divine glory shared." *Rating: contestable lexically* (*theotēs* most naturally = deity/Godhead), but the *participatory* thrust of *plērōma* in Colossians/John is defensible. [^4]

3. **The patristic "exchange formula" is real.** Ostler claims "more than 80 occurrences in the patristic literature" of the God-became-man-so-man-might-become-God formula (Irenaeus, Athanasius, etc.). *Rating: historically accurate* that the formula is widespread; it is the basis of Eastern theosis. [^5]

4. **Steelman of the classical reading.** Patristic theosis is *participation by grace* in the divine *energies/life*, explicitly *not* becoming God by nature or crossing the Creator/creature line; Athanasius who coined "He became man that we might become God" *also* wrote *On the Incarnation* defending homoousios and creatio ex nihilo. So Col 2:9 ("the fullness of *deity* dwells bodily in Christ") is in fact a *high-Christology* verse — the whole *plērōma* uniquely indwelling the incarnate Son — and the believer's "fullness" is union *with* him, not equality of essence. *Rating: a strong rebuttal* — the very fathers and verses Ostler cites distinguish participatory deification from same-kind godhood. [^6]

## Best Quotes
> "For in Christ all the fullness of deity lives in bodily form and in Christ you have been brought to fullness." [^3]

> "God the Father gives a fullness of deity in Christ. Christ gives us a fullness of deity in him and therefore we have a fullness of deity in God... That's the exchange formula." [^5]

> "Isn't this what the early church fathers picked up on with the entire notion of Christ became man so that man may become God? — Exactly. And they're reading these verses and they're seeing what we're seeing." [^2]
