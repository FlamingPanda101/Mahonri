# Gregory of Nyssa First to Condemn Slavery Wholesale

## The Claim

[[Glenn Scrivener]]: "The wholesale condemnation of slavery — Gregory of Nyssa, about 379 AD — is the first in human history." That such a condemnation is even *thinkable* to us is a Christian inheritance: "we are the heirs of a tradition that makes us see slavery as obviously wrong... that is unique in history."[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **A genuine historical first.** [[Gregory of Nyssa]]'s Fourth Homily on Ecclesiastes (c. 379) contains an unqualified denunciation of slaveholding as such — widely cited by historians as the earliest extant wholesale condemnation.[^1] *(Strong — this is a real and striking datum; pre-Christian and most ancient sources at best regulated slavery.)*

2. **The "unthinkability" argument.** Scrivener: "We find it impossible to think ourselves back into the sandals of a first- or fourth-century person... there is an unthinkable character to abolitionism." We might *wish* Paul had written what Gregory wrote, but the conviction was historically unavailable.[^2] *(This is the strongest form of the [[Unthinkable Without Christianity vs Blossoming Thesis|"unthinkable without"]] claim — abolition-as-obvious is a culturally late achievement.)*

3. **Gregory was preaching the OT.** Notably, Gregory's condemnation came "when he was preaching on an Old Testament scripture from Ecclesiastes" — undercutting O'Connor's framing of the OT as merely pro-slavery. See [[The Old Testament Was a Hammer for Abolitionists]].[^2] *(Strong rejoinder — the first abolitionist voice rose from OT exposition.)*

4. **Steelman of O'Connor.** O'Connor's pressure: a single 4th-century homily, against centuries of Christian slaveholding and pro-slavery theology, fits the [[Only Christianity Reached the Moon - The NASA Analogy|NASA analogy]] problem — the church was "on the wrong side quite a lot." And the *value* (don't oppress the weak) predates Christianity even if the *wholesale institutional condemnation* is Christian-first. See [[Western Values Predate or Arise Independently of Christianity]].[^3] *(Fair — "first wholesale condemnation" is a real Christian first, but its rarity and lateness, plus abundant counter-testimony, limit how much causal weight it can carry alone.)*
