#type/concept

# parallelomania

## Overview

**Parallelomania** (a term coined by Samuel Sandmel, 1962) is the methodological error of overstating the significance of surface similarities between texts and then inferring literary dependence or borrowing from them. This is an overview/hub note for an existing cluster; the linked notes apply the principle in both directions.

## The Claim

The core discipline: a list of parallels, by itself, proves nothing. As the vault puts it, [[A Parallel List Without Textual Analysis Carries No Burden|a parallel list without textual analysis carries no burden]] — coincidental resemblance is cheap, and real dependence must be shown through distinctive, non-generic, textually anchored correspondences, not tallied resemblances.

The principle cuts **both ways**, which is the cluster's recurring move:

- **Against critics.** The classic anti-LDS parallel arguments are themselves parallelomania: [[The View of the Hebrews Parallels Are Parallelomania|View of the Hebrews]], the [[The Ethan and Ether Parallel Roberts Rejected|Ethan/Ether]] parallel, and the [[The Quetzalcoatl Parallel Names Moses Not Christ|Quetzalcoatl]] parallel all collapse under scrutiny; critics' analogies often [[Joe Heschmeyers Quran Parallel Proves Too Much Against the Resurrection|prove too much]] (e.g., a Quran parallel that would equally undercut the Resurrection).
- **Against over-eager apologists.** The same caution restrains LDS use of ancient-temple parallels ([[Avoiding Parallelomania Between Ancient Rites and LDS Temple]]) and insists that [[Recognizing the Parallel Is Not the Same as the Theological Conclusion|recognizing a parallel is not the same as the theological conclusion]].
