# A Parallel List Without Textual Analysis Carries No Burden

## The Claim
A bare list of "parallels" — columns of *View of the Hebrews* items beside [[Book of Mormon]] items, with no textual analysis establishing that any parallel is genuine — imposes no obligation on a respondent to refute each one. The burden of proof lies with the person asserting dependence; a reader should be *skeptical* of an impressive-looking list precisely because its length is doing the persuasive work that argument should be doing.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Asserting a parallel does not establish it (rated: strong, methodological).** "If someone puts up a list of parallels and does nothing to establish the legitimacy of those parallels, no textual analysis, no explanation, then you have no obligation in responding to it to make in-depth counter arguments, and you as a reader should be skeptical of just a long list like this."[^2] The CES Letter list is "scattershot."
2. **Length creates a false impression of weight (rated: strong, psychological).** Even Smoot admits "you see this list and you say, wow, there's a lot there." The rhetorical force is volume, not validity — which is why each entry must be tested individually, where most collapse (see [[The View of the Hebrews Parallels Are Parallelomania]]).[^3]
3. **The burden-shift critics attempt is illegitimate (rated: strong, documented).** When FAIR responded to the CES Letter, [[Jeremy Runnells]] complained they "didn't go in depth to debunk every single parallel" — but Runnells "did absolutely nothing to establish the legitimacy of a single one of these parallels."[^4] *Steelman:* a critic can argue that a long convergent list is itself a form of cumulative-case evidence, and that demanding rigorous textual analysis for every popular-level claim sets an unfair bar. Smoot's reply: cumulative cases only work when the individual items survive scrutiny; an unestablished parallel contributes nothing to sum, and the proper response to an unargued list is to note that it is unargued.

## Best Quotes
