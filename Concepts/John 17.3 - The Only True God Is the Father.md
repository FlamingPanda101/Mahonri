# John 17:3 — "The Only True God" Is the Father

## The Claim
[[Jacob Hansen]] presents [[John 17.3|John 17:3]] as evidence that the scriptural data does not cleanly yield Trinitarian consubstantiality. Jesus prays: "this is life eternal, that they might know thee, the only true God, and Jesus Christ whom thou hast sent." [^1] On a plain reading, "the only true God" is *the Father* — distinguished from Jesus Christ within the same sentence. "If you went up to Jesus and asked him that question in John 17, it seems to say that the only true God is thee — the he was referring to was the Father." [^2] Hansen frames this not as a "gotcha" but as showing "contradictions and/or ambiguities within the scriptural record" that the Trinity must then *interpret*. [^3]

## The Rebuttal (i.e., the contested reading, steelmanned)

1. **The distinction-of-persons reading (LDS / unitarian-leaning).** The verse explicitly separates "the only true God" (Father) from "Jesus Christ whom thou hast sent." *Rating: the natural surface reading* and a genuine pressure point for any model that makes Father and Son numerically the same God. It dovetails with [[Philippians 2.9-11|Philippians 2:9-11]] ending "to the glory of God the Father." (See [[Worshiping the Right Jesus - Created vs Uncreated]].)

2. **Steelman of the Trinitarian reply (not voiced in this video).** Classical exegesis holds that "only true God" excludes *idols/false gods*, not the Son; the Son is included via the second clause ("and Jesus Christ whom thou hast sent") as co-object of the saving "knowledge," and elsewhere John ascribes full deity to the Logos ([[John 1.1|John 1:1]], [[John 20.28|John 20:28]]). *Rating: a standard, defensible harmonization* — but note Ortlund does not deploy it here; the verse is left as an open tension within the dialogue.

3. **Hansen's meta-point.** The existence of texts pulling in different directions ([[John 8.24|John 8:24]] "I am" vs. John 17:3 "the only true God is the Father") is exactly why the Trinity is an *interpretation* layered onto underdetermined data (see [[Trinity Is an Interpretation Not Explicit in Scripture]]). [^3] *Rating: a fair framing of the hermeneutical situation;* whether the Trinitarian synthesis is the *best* interpretation is the unresolved question.
