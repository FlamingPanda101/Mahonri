# Trinity Cannot Be Derived From the New Testament - Swinburne

## The Claim

Even a sympathetic, top-tier Christian philosopher concedes the doctrine of the Trinity cannot be *derived* from the New Testament. Hansen cites [[Richard Swinburne]]: "Even if you regard the New Testament as an infallible source of doctrine, you cannot derive from it a doctrine of the Trinity." The Trinity is therefore a later interpretive construction, not a plainly scriptural teaching. [^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **A hostile-witness concession.** Swinburne is "probably the greatest Christian philosopher of the past 100 years" and a *defender* of (a social model of) the Trinity. That such a figure grants the doctrine is not derivable from scripture is a strong admission against interest. (Evidence: strong as an appeal to a credentialed authority's concession; note Swinburne still *affirms* the Trinity on other grounds — see steelman.) [^1]
2. **It reframes the debate.** If the Trinity isn't in the text, its authority rests on creedal/traditional development. Theologians who grasp the [[The Who or What Test for the Word God|who/what]] distinction "recognize that you can't derive this idea of three persons who are the same being from the text of the Bible." (Evidence: Hansen generalizes the point; the claim that *informed* theologians concede this is somewhat overstated but Swinburne is a real example.) [^2]
3. **John 17 as the seed.** Barnes notes the Trinity functions as one proposed *solution* to the oneness language of the intercessory prayer — but it is an *interpretation*, and other viable interpretations (the Godhead) exist. (Evidence: fair framing; the doctrine did develop partly to systematize such texts.) [^3]
4. **Steelman.** Swinburne's full position is that the Trinity, while not *derivable by deduction* from the NT, is nonetheless *well-supported* by it and by a priori argument (his "social" account argues a perfectly loving God would be tripersonal). Many Trinitarians hold the doctrine is the best *synthesis* of scriptural data (Father, Son, Spirit each divine; one God) reached by *good and necessary consequence* — not every true doctrine must be a proof-text. So "not derivable" ≠ "unscriptural." (Evidence: this is Swinburne's actual nuance; quoting only the concession risks misrepresenting his conclusion.) The LDS reply: granting it is a *synthesis*, the question is whether it's the *best* one — and the Godhead synthesizes the same data without the [[Mystery Is Special Pleading Not Humility|incoherence]]. [^4]

## Best Quotes

> "Even if you regard the New Testament as an infallible source of doctrine, you cannot derive from it a doctrine of the Trinity." — Richard Swinburne, as quoted [^1]

> "The theologians who actually know the difference between a who and a what ... recognize that you can't derive this idea of three persons who are the same being from the text of the Bible." [^2]
