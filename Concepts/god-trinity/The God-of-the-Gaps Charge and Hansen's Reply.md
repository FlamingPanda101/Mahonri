# The God-of-the-Gaps Charge and Hansen's Reply

## The Point
Hansen's whole defense against Seth's master charge (see [[Theism Is a God-of-the-Gaps Project Doomed by the Track Record]]) is that the design argument is an *inference to the best explanation grounded in positive knowledge*, not an appeal to ignorance. "I'm actually going to what we do know about causation, and we know that minds can cause things to happen." The signature of agency is *high improbability plus order*: we infer design for a house or a pocket watch because "order does not emerge from random processes."[^1] So the move is not "we don't know, therefore God," but "we do know the universe is fine-tuned, and the best explanation of that — from our uniform and repeated experience — is a mind."[^2] The dispute thus turns into a symmetry contest: each accuses the other of filling a gap, and the question is whose inference rests on a known causal pattern.

## Evidence
1. **From causation, not ignorance.** "It's not an inference to 'oh we don't know therefore God did it'; no, it's that we do know that the universe is fine-tuned, and the best explanation... is that finely tuned things like keys and houses... are the product of design."[^2]
2. **The "atheism of the gaps" countercharge.** Hansen argues Seth fills the same gap with naturalism: "what's really happening is that there is a gap and he's filling it with an atheism of the gap"; to credit unexplained order to evolution is to "wave the magical wand of what I call evolution of the gaps."[^3] He presses Seth in cross-examination on whether positing undiscovered metaphysical principles is itself "a naturalism of the gaps fallacy," since "you don't know what those things are."[^4]
3. **Inference to the best explanation, openly labeled.** In his closing of the fine-tuning case Hansen says plainly: "This is not a god of the gaps argument; this is an inference to the best explanation based on what we do know, not based on what we don't."[^5] See [[Intelligent Design as Inference to the Best Explanation]].
4. **Seth's defense of inductive gap-reasoning.** Seth concedes hypothetically "there could be an intelligent agent behind it," but argues that in "areas of human ignorance" one must reason inductively, and his naturalistic induction has a winning record across fields, whereas "theism at rock bottom just doesn't" have inductive justification.[^6]

## Best Quotes
> "I'm actually going to what we do know about causation, and we know that minds can cause things to happen." (Hansen) [^1]

> "It isn't an inference to 'oh we don't know therefore God did it.' No, it's that we do know that the universe is fine-tuned, and the best explanation of that... is that finely tuned things... are the product of design." (Hansen) [^2]

> "Do you feel like you're moving into an evolution or naturalism of the gaps fallacy there, considering that you don't know what those things are?" (Hansen, in cross-examination) [^4]

## LDS Connection
The reply reflects the broader LDS apologetic preference for inference-to-best-explanation over stopgap reasoning, and for a God who works *through* causal order rather than in its cracks — the same logic developed in [[God Is Not a God of the Gaps - Henry Ford vs the Laws of Internal Combustion]]. It claims only a generic mind, leaving the identity of that mind to revelation rather than to the argument.
