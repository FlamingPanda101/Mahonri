# William Lane Craig Concedes Logos Christology Is a Platonic Relic

## The Claim
The narrator argues the "Greek influence corrupted the doctrine of God" thesis is not a fringe LDS talking point: the famous Protestant philosopher [[William Lane Craig]] **agrees** that Middle Platonism misled the early apologists and that the resulting *logos Christology* — codified at Nicaea as the eternal generation of the Son and procession of the Spirit — is a Platonic "relic" with "virtually no warrant in the biblical text" that introduces **subordinationism** into the godhead. [^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Craig blames Middle Platonism by name.** "Some of these early Christian apologists like Athenagoras, Justin Martyr, Tatian and so on were misled by this logos christology that they inherited from middle platonism... and I am not at all supportive of that strand within Christian theology." *Rating: strong; a direct concession from a leading Trinitarian philosopher.* [^1]
2. **Craig says the generation/procession language has no biblical warrant.** From his paper *Is God the Son Begotten in His Divine Nature?*: "Although credally affirmed, the doctrine of the generation of the Son and the procession of the Spirit is a relic of logos Christology, which finds virtually no warrant in the biblical text and introduces a subordinationism into the godhead, which anyone who affirms the full divinity of Christ ought to find very troubling." *Rating: strong; this is Craig's actual published position (he favors a non-derivation, "three-persons-each-fully-God" model).* [^2]
3. **It corroborates the Hellenization case from a hostile witness.** Because Craig is a committed Trinitarian, his agreement that an unbiblical Platonic strand entered the creeds strengthens the narrator's claim that the *historical* development was philosophy-driven. *Rating: strong dialectically; an admission against interest.* [^2]

### Steelman (the opposing view)
Craig's concession does **not** make him an anti-Trinitarian — quite the opposite. He rejects *eternal generation* precisely to *defend* the **co-equality** of the three persons (each is *autotheos*, God in himself, not derived). So a Trinitarian can grant that one *historical sub-theory* (derivationist logos Christology) was Platonic and mistaken while holding the core Nicene doctrine of one God in three co-equal persons as biblical. The relic is the *generation* language, not the Trinity itself.

### Counter-objection
The narrator's reply: Craig's move concedes the decisive premise — that an *unbiblical, philosophically-driven* element was "codified into the Nicene creed." Once you admit the creed embeds a Platonic relic that "finds virtually no warrant in the biblical text," you have granted that the conciliar process was not a pure transmission of apostolic revelation but a fallible synthesis using "the philosophical tools of the day" — exactly the LDS claim. Latter-day Saints simply extend the same critique to *homoousios* and divine simplicity. See [[The Creedal God Is the Platonic One Not the God of the Bible]].
