# Theosis as Likeness and Union So Far as Possible

## The Point
Daniel Becerra traces the **vocabulary** of deification and its eventual definition. The earliest Christians wrote in Greek and used several terms — *theosis*, *theopoiesis*, *apotheosis* — all translated "divinization" or "deification." [^1] Gregory of Nazianzus (c. 329-390) "was the first Christian author to use the term *theosis* to refer broadly to the process of humans becoming like God." [^2] Two centuries later Dionysius the Areopagite supplied the classic definition: theosis is "the attaining of likeness of God and union with him so far as is possible." [^3] The qualifier "so far as is possible" is the patristic guardrail: real likeness and union, but never crossing the Creator/creature line.

## Evidence
1. **Multiple Greek terms, growing systematization.** Becerra: discussions "become more numerous and systematic over time" as Christians reflected on Genesis 1:26-27, Psalm 82:6, John 10:34-36, 2 Peter 1:4, and related texts. [^1]
2. **The language family of deification.** New Testament concepts of unity, vitality, **adoption** (Romans 8), kinship, and **participation** (2 Peter 1:4, "participants of the divine nature") "would pervade discussions of deification." [^4]
3. **Gregory of Nazianzus coins *theosis*.** Becerra: he "was the first Christian author to use the term *theosis*" broadly. [^2]
4. **Dionysius's definition.** "The attaining of likeness of God and union with him so far as is possible." [^3]

## Best Quotes
> "[Theosis is] the attaining of likeness of God and union with him so far as is possible." — Dionysius the Areopagite [^3]

> "[Humans] may become participants of the divine nature." — 2 Peter 1:4 (NRSV) [^4]

## LDS Connection
The settled patristic definition — *likeness and union "so far as is possible"* — is both an asset and a limit for LDS apologetics. Asset: it confirms deification was a mainstream, technically defined ancient doctrine, not a fringe idea, supporting [[Disciples Share the Throne and Divine Glory]] and the LDS reading of [[2 Corinthians 3.18|2 Corinthians 3:18]] ("transformed... from one degree of glory to another"). Limit: "so far as is possible" encodes the bounded participation that [[Same-Species-as-God as the Core LDS Distinctive in Theosis]] shows the LDS model exceeds. The shared term *theosis* lets Latter-day Saints situate exaltation within historic Christianity; the differing ontology keeps it distinctive.
