# God Found Spirits He Did Not Create - Book of Abraham Solve

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
The decisive LDS escape from the creation dilemma and the predestination syllogism is to **deny creation *ex nihilo*** (premise #3 of [[The Three Facts About God Entail Predestination]]). [[Luke Hansen]]: per the Book of Abraham, God "merely... finds himself in the midst of spirits and then decides to try to invite those spirits into the kind of life that he has." He did not create the intelligences out of nothing; He "found it in its beginning state" and now works with it. Therefore "there is some part of you that is not God... not generated by God," making you fully morally responsible for your own outcome — and God not morally responsible for failures.[^1]

## The Rebuttal
1. **Co-eternal intelligences sever the causal chain that grounds predestination — rate: strong within LDS metaphysics.** If a self-existent, uncreated core of each person ("intelligence," per D&C 93:29, "was not created or made, neither indeed can be") predates God's organizing work, then God did not author the initial conditions from nothing; the agent contributes something God did not give. This breaks the determinism that *ex nihilo* creation implies (see [[Creation Ex Nihilo Leads to Determinism]], [[Free Will - Atheist Determinism vs LDS Co-Eternality]]).[^1] **Steelman of the creedal critic:** (a) This abandons classical monotheism — a God who "finds" eternal co-existent reality is finite and dependent, not the absolute ground of being; many regard that as theologically fatal and as conceding that LDS theology answers the dilemma only by ceasing to describe the God under dispute. (b) Even a non-*ex-nihilo* God who *organizes* spirits and *invites* them into mortality, foreknowing some will be lost, still makes a creative/organizational choice — so the "first moral act" worry may persist unless paired with premortal consent (see [[LDS Premortal Consent Removes Gods Culpability]]).
2. **It is an internal-consistency move, not a Joseph-Smith-says claim — rate: methodologically clean.** Hansen frames it as the *philosophically* available exit (deny #3) that *happens* to be what LDS scripture already teaches, rather than imposing Restoration authority on the creedal Christian. This fits Carroll's internal-critique method (see [[The Creation Dilemma Is an Internal Catch-22]]).[^1]
3. **Scriptural anchor — rate: textually grounded for LDS.** The Book of Abraham's council-of-spirits ([[Abraham 3.25|Abraham 3:22-23]]) and D&C 93's uncreated intelligence are the textual basis; the historical claim that *ex nihilo* itself was a late (2nd-century) development supports the move by denying that the creedal alternative is biblically original (see [[Creation Ex Nihilo Originated Late 2nd Century CE]], [[2 Maccabees 7.28 Does Not Teach Creation Ex Nihilo]]).

## Best Quotes
> "If God merely as the Book of Abraham says, finds himself in the midst of spirits and then decides to try to invite those spirits into the kind of life that he has, then he is not morally responsible for people failing to do so." [^1]

> "He didn't create it out of nothing. He found it in its beginning state. And now he's working with it like that. So, there is some part of you that is not God... not generated by God. And so, that is how you can be fully morally responsible for your own outcome." [^1]

> "What I think Christians automatically agree with Latter-day Saints on is in the rejection of number three and this is how Latter-day Saints get around the problem." [^1]
