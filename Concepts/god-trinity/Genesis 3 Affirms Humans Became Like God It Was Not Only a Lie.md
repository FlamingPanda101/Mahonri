# Genesis 3 Affirms Humans Became Like God It Was Not Only a Lie

#type/rebuttal

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
**Objection (from the Instagram reel "Are Mormons really Christian?"):** the Latter-day Saint hope that "you can become a god" is "the same lie from Genesis" — the serpent's "ye shall be as gods" ([[Genesis 3.5|Genesis 3:5]]) — so deification is Satan's deception, disqualifying Mormonism.

**Rebuttal:** [[Cardon Ellis]] and the panel ([[Dalton Williams]], [[Devin Barnes]], [[Braden Gospel Guardian|Braden]]) answer that the critic stopped reading. Seventeen verses later, **God himself** says, "Behold, the man is become as one of us, to know good and evil" ([[Genesis 3.22|Genesis 3:22]]). The very outcome the serpent named — becoming "as gods" in the knowledge of good and evil — is then *confirmed by the LORD*. So "becoming like God" cannot be merely the serpent's lie; if it were a lie, God would not have repeated it as fact.[^1]

## The Rebuttal

### 1. God ratifies the serpent's described outcome — rate: strong textual point
[[Genesis 3.5|Genesis 3:5]] (serpent): "ye shall be as gods, knowing good and evil." [[Genesis 3.22|Genesis 3:22]] (God): "the man is become as one of us, to know good and evil." The panel's move is simply to read both verses: the *content* of becoming god-like-in-knowledge is affirmed by the narrator-as-God, not denied. *Strong as a reading of the text: the divine speech in v.22 is hard to square with treating "become as gods" as wholly false.*[^1]

### 2. Restricting v.22 to "only knowledge of good and evil" backfires — rate: strong
Critics often grant v.22 but limit it: humans became like God *only* in knowing good and evil. The panel notes that is *exactly* what the serpent specified in v.5 ("knowing good and evil") — so the qualification concedes the point rather than escaping it. *Strong: the limiting move does not create the daylight the objection needs.*[^1]

### 3. The deception lay elsewhere, not in the deification motif — rate: sound theologically
On this reading the serpent's sin was not the *claim that they would become god-like in knowledge* (true) but the framing/timing/means — the implied denial of consequences ("ye shall not surely die," v.4) and the seductive shortcut. Becoming like God is, in the broader canon, God's actual purpose for humanity (theosis), not the temptation. *Moderate-to-strong: coheres with the patristic exchange-formula tradition the same episode cites (see below).*[^1]

### 4. Connected to the broader theosis case — rate: strong (well-documented in the vault)
The episode roots human deification not only in Genesis 3 but in the church fathers' "exchange formula" (Irenaeus, Clement, Athanasius, the Gregories) and NT proof-texts (2 Peter 1:4 "partakers of the divine nature"; Romans 8:17 "joint-heirs with Christ"). See [[All the Church Fathers Taught Apotheosis]] and [[Christ Became Man That We Might Become God - The Exchange Formula]]. *Strong as cumulative context; the patristic data is extensively documented.*[^2]

## Steelman of the Opposing View
The traditional Christian has real replies. (a) **"As one of us" need not mean ontological godhood.** Many read [[Genesis 3.22|Genesis 3:22]] as *ironic* or as a divine-council plural; humans gained *moral knowledge* (the capacity to discern, now burdened by guilt), which is a tragic likeness, not an elevation to deity — the very next clause bars them from the tree of life and expels them, hardly a promotion. So God affirms a *limited, fallen* likeness, not "you are becoming gods." (b) **Knowledge-likeness ≠ being-likeness.** Sharing one attribute (moral discernment) with God does not entail becoming a god; the serpent's lie can be the *implication* that this likeness makes them God's peers/independent of him — which is exactly the autonomy the Fall enacts. (c) **The Genesis 3 argument and LDS exaltation are not the same claim.** Even granting humans became "like God" in knowledge, that is a long way from the King-Follett teaching that humans are the *same species* as God and may become Gods of their own worlds; patristic theosis is participation/adoption within an absolute Creator-creature distinction (see the steelman in [[All the Church Fathers Taught Apotheosis]] and [[Same-Species-as-God as the Core LDS Distinctive]]). The defensible core for the panel is narrow but genuine: *the specific debate move* "becoming god-like is just the serpent's lie" is refuted by [[Genesis 3.22|v.22]] — God does affirm a god-like likeness — so that particular gotcha fails. What [[Genesis 3.5|Genesis 3:5]]/22 does **not** by itself establish is the full LDS doctrine of exaltation; it neutralizes one bad objection rather than proving the maximal claim.
