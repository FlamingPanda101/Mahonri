# Understanding the Trinity Is Not Required for Salvation

## The Claim
[[Blake Ostler]] argues that no one needs a correct understanding of the Trinity or the Godhead to be saved or exalted. If precise creedal theology were the gate, "probably 99.9% of all people would simply be excluded" because they lack the capacity, training, or interest to grasp the fine Greek distinctions of the creeds. Jesus never made theological precision a condition of discipleship. [^1][^2]

## The Rebuttal (Supporting Evidence)

1. **The required competence is unrealistic.** Most people "are just not even capable of grasping the kinds of distinctions that are being made in the Greek in the Creed," and acquiring the capacity "would take years of study of theology." *Rating: a strong pastoral/empirical point* — salvation tied to advanced theological literacy would exclude nearly everyone. [^3]

2. **Jesus tied discipleship to love, not theology.** "Jesus never once said, 'Blessed are those who understand the Trinity.'" He said "by this shall all men know that ye are my disciples, if ye have love one to another" (John 13:35). *Rating: a fair appeal* to the Gospels' own stated markers of discipleship (cf. [[Mark 12.30-31|Mark 12:30-31]]). [^4][^5]

3. **There is no single LDS view either.** Ostler stresses "there's not a univocal view of the Godhead within the LDS community," and one need not resolve it to be exalted. *Rating: an honest concession* that defuses the demand for doctrinal uniformity on either side. [^6]

4. **Steelman of the Trinitarian (cf. [[Trinity Is a First-Rank Salvation Issue]]).** The claim is not that one must *understand* the metaphysics but that one must *worship the right God* and not *deny* Christ's deity; saving faith requires the *object* (the true God in Christ, John 17:3; John 8:24) even if the believer cannot articulate *homoousios*. So "understanding isn't required" is true but compatible with "the *identity* of God is salvation-relevant." *Rating: a strong reply* — it distinguishes *implicit faith in the right Jesus* from *technical comprehension*; Ostler's point lands against creedal *gatekeeping* but not against the deeper claim that *whom* you worship matters. [^7]

## Best Quotes
> "One does not have to have a clear understanding of the Godhead or Trinity to be saved, to be exalted. If it did, probably 99.9% of all people would simply be excluded because they're smart enough not to care that much." [^1]

> "Jesus never once said, 'Blessed are those who understand the Trinity.' Not once." [^4]

> "It's akin to asking how many angels can dance on the tip of a pinhead." [^8]
