# The Creeds Built a Self-Contradictory God

## The Claim
The problem of hell, the video argues, is not a problem with the God of the Bible but with the God *the creeds built*. Early church councils, trying to square strict monotheism with scriptures that speak of three beings (Father, Son, Holy Ghost), "made a mistake": they stacked God's love, power, and knowledge into a definition so extreme that the attributes cannot all be true at once, then declared dissent heresy [^1]. A God who is simultaneously all-knowing, all-powerful, totally free, and perfectly good, yet creates people destined for eternal torment, "just doesn't hold together" [^2]. The problem of hell, on this view, "only exists because of the creeds and the councils."

## Supporting Evidence
1. **Locates the contradiction in conciliar formulation, not Scripture — rate: framing, central LDS move.** The argument shifts blame from God to the human philosophical apparatus of [[Council of Nicaea]] and later creeds; see the vault's [[Trinity - Historical Development]] and [[Trinity Was Constructed Not Revealed]]. **Steelman of the creedal Christian:** the councils did not *invent* the divine attributes — omniscience, omnipotence, and goodness are read straight from Scripture (Ps 147:5; Job 42:2; Mark 10:18), and the creeds were *constraining* heresy (Arianism, modalism), not manufacturing contradictions. Classical theists also deny the attributes conflict, deploying analogical predication and theodicy (see [[The Appeal to Mystery Empties the Word Good of Meaning]] for the rebuttal exchange). So the claim that the *creeds* created the problem is itself contestable: the problem of hell can be posed from the bare biblical attributes without any conciliar formula.
2. **Ties hell to the Trinity specifically — rate: moderate, the distinctive thesis.** The video's novel linkage is that the *same* maximalist, philosophically over-specified God who generates the Trinity also generates the problem of hell — both are artifacts of forcing biblical language through Greco-Roman metaphysics. Compare [[Trinity Built to Satisfy Greco-Roman Philosophy]]. **Caution:** the logical connection is loose — one could affirm the Trinity and resolve hell via universalism, or reject the Trinity and still face the problem of hell. The pairing is rhetorical more than strictly entailed.
3. **Sets up the constructive alternative.** The destructive claim clears the ground for [[Most Christians Describe Three Beings Not the Trinity]] and [[Pre-Mortal Existence Dissolves the Problem of Hell]] — recover a literal Father and Son and deny *ex nihilo*.

## Best Quotes
> "These early church councils were people trying to square the idea of strict monotheism with the scriptures that talk about three beings... And just like everything else humans create, they made a mistake. They invented a version of God whose attributes contradict each other." [^1]

> "The true God of the Bible is not the problem. The problem is the God the creeds built... The creeds created a God that just doesn't hold together." [^2]
