# Classical Theism Cannot Be Reconciled With the Bible

## The Claim
The whole LDS case is that **classical theism** (the impassible, simple, timeless, immaterial God of the philosophers) cannot be squared with the biblical God — who grieves, responds, has a will, sends and is sent. When a Trinitarian like Avery defends the Trinity by *abandoning* classical-theist attributes (impassibility) to keep the biblical data, he concedes the LDS thesis.

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. The opponent kept "jumping into heresy"
Hansen says he prepared cross-examination expecting *classical-theist* answers, but Avery repeatedly "jumped right into heresy" — affirming divine emotion, treating the natures loosely — because "the Bible doesn't say this [impassibility]." Hansen: "You do realize our whole point is that classical theism can't be reconciled with the Bible." So the opponent's biblicism *funds* the LDS argument. *(Strong as an internal-tension argument; cf. [[Divine Impassibility Is Classical-Theist Heresy]].)* [^1]

### 2. The doctrine survives only by "mystery" or heresy
Pressed on the contradictions (one being submitting will to itself; a Father who knows what the Son does not), the Trinitarian's only escapes are to **commit heresy** (deny an attribute) or to invoke **"it's a mystery."** "Two plus two is 38 — it's a mystery, God's ways are higher." Neither is an argument. *(Moderate-strong; rhetorically sharp, though "mystery" has a serious theological pedigree the panel waves off.)* [^2]

### 3. The rejection is over-determined
Hansen says even *without* modern revelation he would reject the Trinity: it is "not supported by the text, not supported by logic, not supported by history," and additionally refuted by revelation. "The emperor has no clothes." *(Opinion-weighted (D-leaning), but states the LDS confidence level clearly.)* [^3]

### Steelman (the opposing view)
The classical theist denies the dilemma is real. Biblical "emotion" texts are anthropopathic accommodation; "mystery" is not a dodge but the appropriate epistemic humility before an infinite God whose mode of being exceeds creaturely categories (1 Cor 13:12; Isa 55:8-9). Far from being unbiblical, divine simplicity and immutability are read as *entailed* by God's being the uncreated source of all (Exod 3:14; Mal 3:6; Jas 1:17). On this account it is the LDS "passible, embodied, progressing-from-intelligences" deity that fails to be the God of the Bible, having traded the Creator for a glorified creature. The disagreement thus turns on hermeneutics (literal vs accommodated divine emotion) and on which attributes are negotiable — not on whether either side "reads the Bible."
