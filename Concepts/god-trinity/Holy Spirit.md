# Holy Spirit

The Holy Spirit (Holy Ghost) is, in Christian theology, the third person of the Godhead/Trinity. In Latter-day Saint teaching the Holy Ghost is a distinct personage of spirit, one of three separate beings united in purpose, whose role is to witness of the Father and the Son, sanctify, and reveal truth. Trinitarian creeds instead define the Spirit as consubstantial with the Father and Son within one divine essence.

## Relevance

The Holy Spirit recurs in the vault's history-of-doctrine arguments: that the earliest Christian writers identified "the word of God" with Christ and a spoken message rather than a written corpus, and that the [[Filioque]] clause ("and the Son") was a later addition to the creed that helped precipitate the Great Schism — examples used to show that creedal formulations developed over time rather than descending intact from the apostles.
