# God and Man Are the Same Species

#type/claim

## The Claim

The core substance of the [[King Follett Discourse]], per [[Casey Paul Griffiths]], is that God and man are "the same species, the same type of being" — differing in degree of progression, not in kind. He uses [[Tad Callister]]'s acorn-and-oak image to capture both the gulf and the kinship.[^1] (See also the existing note [[Acorn and Oak Analogy for Theosis]].)

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Same kind, vast difference in degree.** "The difference between God and man is significant, but it's like the difference between an oak tree and an acorn — the potential is all there."[^2] Griffiths is careful: co-eternal with God does *not* mean equal to God — "we're not saying we're on the same level... it's acorn and oak tree, and not every acorn is going to become an oak tree."[^3] (This guards against the regression worry; cf. [[LDS Father Was Always Fully Divine (No Eternal Regression)]].)
2. **"Made of the same stuff as God."** "We are saying that you're made of the same stuff as God, which is a big deal" — a point Griffiths says Latter-day Saints take for granted because they grow up with a physical, fatherly, gendered, embodied God.[^4]
3. **The capstone chain.** Griffiths sequences it: angels and humans are the same type of being → [[D&C 130.20-21|D&C 130]], we will see the Savior "as a man like ourselves" → King Follett, God too is of this kind, so human potential is "limitless."[^5]
4. **The contrast class.** "In most of Christendom that's not what God is" — he is immaterial spirit, not "the best form of what a human being can be."[^6]
5. **Steelman of the opposing view.** Classical theism insists God is *sui generis* — being itself, not a member of any genus or species. To put God and man in one "species" (even with infinite degree-difference) is, for the classical theist, a category error that collapses the Creator/creature distinction and imports finitude into God (parts, location, a beginning). They would add that biblical "image of God" language (Genesis 1:26–27) signals *representation and relationship*, not biological con-substantiality. Griffiths' reply is that an embodied, relational God is exactly what scripture portrays and what makes the divine-human relationship intelligible (see [[Anthropomorphic God Grounds Heavenly Mother and Eternal Families]]).

## Best Quotes

> "The substance of what Joseph Smith is saying is that God and man are the same species, they're the same type of being."[^7]

> "The difference between God and man is significant, but it's like the difference between an oak tree and an acorn — the potential is all there."[^8]

> "We are saying that you're made of the same stuff as God, which is a big deal."[^9]
