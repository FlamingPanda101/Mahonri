# A Reality More Fundamental Than God Just Is God

#type/claim

## The Claim
The Eastern Orthodox apologist [[Christian Mario]] presses the central syllogism of his debate with [[Jacob Hansen]] against the Latter-day Saint *organizer*-God: if God exists *within* some reality or "realm" that he did not create, that reality is — by definition — **more fundamental** than God, and "the most fundamental reality is what everything depends on." Therefore the LDS deity "is not the ultimate." Mario's identity claim is that **whatever is most fundamental simply is God** — "if there's a reality that's more fundamental than God, then by definition that reality is God" — because the word *fundamental* means "self-existent, necessary and uncaused," and "you can't have a more fundamental than the fundamental... that's like saying there's something more necessary than necessity itself." The LDS God is thereby "demoted to a creature... part of the furniture of the house that is the universe," "just another guy," no longer "the I AM."[^1]

## Supporting Evidence
**Steelman (Orthodox / classical-theist side).** The argument is a clean reductio that turns the LDS *organizer* model against itself. (1) *Definitional force.* "Fundamental," "ultimate," and "God" are being treated as co-referring: that on which all else depends and which depends on nothing. If the LDS Father depends on a prior realm to exist or to act, then by the classical-theist's own vocabulary the *realm* satisfies the definition of deity and the Father does not — so the Latter-day Saint has, unwittingly, "demoted God to a creature living under a higher law."[^2] (2) *The trap closes either way.* Mario notes Hansen oscillates: pressed, Hansen concedes "the eternal mind organizing everything... yes, that is God," but then re-describes God as merely organizing pre-existing matter, at which point the matter (or the realm) reclaims the title of ultimate.[^3] (3) *Scriptural stakes.* The maneuver is meant to show the LDS deity cannot be the self-existent "I AM" of Exodus 3:14 — "a being that's not the ground of reality can't be divine." This is the same engine as the classical contingency proof: the terminus of dependence must be self-existent (cf. [[Why the Necessary Being Must Be God Not the Universe]]).

**Steelman (LDS reply — Hansen).** Hansen does not flinch; he *accepts* a more-fundamental reality and denies that "fundamental = God." (1) *The personhood objection.* "If God was fundamental reality itself, then he's not a person. He is like a reality" — and an impersonal "ground of being" is "not consistent with Jesus," who was "a person who was walking around in Palestine." Hansen reads classical theism's *actus purus* as having "reduced God from a father and a person to an abstraction that is partless, passionless," incapable of the relationship the gospel describes.[^4] (2) *Distinguish "eternal" from "divine."* The Restoration treats matter, intelligences, and a fundamental "spirit-matter" substrate as **eternal and uncreated without being divine** (D&C 93:29) — so Mario's identity premise ("most fundamental = God") is precisely the disputed move, not a neutral definition (cf. [[The Uncreated Element Is Only the Most Fundamental Substrate]] and [[Maybe the Universe Itself Is the Necessary Being]]). (3) *Worship is not of the substrate.* "I don't worship reality itself. I worship my father in heaven who is within that reality." Hansen owns the label that Mario intends as a defeater: there is "a reality even more fundamental than God," and God is the supreme *person* within it, the *Logos*/organizer who chooses and embodies the light. The disagreement reduces to whether "God" names *the impersonal terminus of metaphysical dependence* (Mario) or *the supreme personal agent* (Hansen) — the contingency-permits-plurality move (cf. [[Contingency Argument Permits Plural Elohim]]).

## Best Quotes
> "If there's a reality that's more fundamental than God, then by definition that reality is God. The most fundamental reality is what everything depends on... you've demoted God to a creature living under a higher law." — Christian Mario [^1]

> "You can't have a more fundamental than the fundamental because God is the fundamental. That's like saying there's something more necessary than necessity itself. The idea just self-destructs the moment you define it." — Christian Mario [^5]

> "I do believe that there is a reality that is even more fundamental than God. Because if God was fundamental reality itself, then he's not a person. He is like a reality, and that is not consistent with Jesus." — Jacob Hansen [^4]
