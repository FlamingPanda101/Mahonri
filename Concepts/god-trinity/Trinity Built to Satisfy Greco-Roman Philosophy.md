# Trinity Built to Satisfy Greco-Roman Philosophy

## The Claim
[[Dan McClellan]] argues the Trinity was constructed "under the aegis and the umbrella of Greek philosophy" — specifically **Middle Platonic / Neoplatonic** norms — because the goal was to harmonize the diverse biblical data "in a way that was philosophically defensible... that would satisfy Greco-Roman intelligentsia." [^1] The project began with the **apologists** trying to account for Jesus "being worshipped and not being God, but also in some sense being God," at which point they shifted audience "from trying to satisfy Jewish interlocutors" to satisfying "Greco-Roman interlocutors... to make the gospel palatable to the Greco-Roman intelligentsia." [^2]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The technical vocabulary is Greek-philosophical.** *Ousia* (substance/essence), *hypostasis* (person/subsistence), and *physis* (nature) are all terms drawn from Greek metaphysics, not from Hebrew or Aramaic scriptural idiom. *Rating: strong, uncontroversial — even orthodox historians grant the conceptual apparatus is Hellenistic.* *Steelman:* defenders (e.g., the "Hellenization" debate against Adolf von Harnack) argue the Fathers *baptized* Greek terms to protect biblical content from misreading, using philosophy as a servant, not a master — so Hellenistic vocabulary does not entail Hellenistic substance.

2. **The shift in target audience is historically attested.** Second-century apologists (Justin Martyr's *Apologies* addressed to Roman emperors; his *Logos* Christology drawing on Stoic/Platonic logos speculation) demonstrably aimed at educated pagans. *Rating: strong.* McClellan's claim that this audience shift shaped the doctrine is a reasonable inference, though critics would say apologists adapted *presentation*, not *content*.

3. **The "philosophically minded" NT texts already lean Platonic.** McClellan notes only the more philosophical NT texts (e.g., the Johannine Logos prologue, Hebrews) approximate the later terms because they had "already adopt[ed]... Middle Platonic philosophical norms." [^3] *Rating: defensible; the Logos concept in John 1 has clear contact with Hellenistic-Jewish (Philonic) and Middle Platonic thought.*

## Best Quotes
> "The construction of the doctrine of the Trinity was done under the aegis and the umbrella of Greek philosophy because the whole point was to try to construct a way to harmonize all the very different things we saw going on in the Bible in a way that was philosophically defensible, in a way that would satisfy Greco-Roman intelligentsia." [^1]

> "This started with the apologists trying to account for what was going on with Jesus being worshipped and not being God, but also in some sense being God. And they kind of left off from trying to satisfy Jewish interlocutors and began to try to satisfy Greco-Roman interlocutors." [^2]
