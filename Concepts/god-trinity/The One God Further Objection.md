# The One God Further Objection

## The Point
**Objection:** Christians, Jews, and Muslims already reject Zeus, Venus, Thor, and Quetzalcoatl, just as atheists do; to be consistent they should "go one god further" and reject the God of monotheism too (Richard Dawkins; Colin McGinn).[^1] **Reply:** First, there are at least five demonstrations that the God of Western monotheism exists, while no one claims arguments for Zeus or Thor — so denying the latter while affirming the former is not inconsistent.[^2] More deeply, the objection rests on a **category mistake**: it treats the God of classical theism as one member of a class of "gods" (supernatural beings like werewolves or Santa Claus), when classical theism's God is not "a being" alongside others but subsistent Being itself.[^3]

## Evidence
- The God of classical theism is not a member of any species or genus — not even the genus "gods" — because membership would entail composition (genus + difference), whereas He is absolutely simple.[^4]
- He does not "share an essence" with other gods, because that would entail a real distinction between His essence and existence, whereas "he just is existence itself"; He is the purely actual actualizer, the source of the causal power of all else (including Zeus or Thor, "if they existed").[^4]
- The pagan gods are limited — fluctuating emotions, bodies, parents, ignorance, vices — while the God of classical theism is immutable, immaterial, eternal, uncaused, omnipotent, omniscient, perfectly good. Each pagan god "has being"; the God of classical theism "is rather underived or subsistent being itself."[^5]
- Feser's analogies: it is like a skeptic who, granting that drawn triangles have imperfectly straight sides, urges Euclideans to doubt the straightness of **triangularity itself**; or who urges Platonists that the **Form of the Good** is itself "less than a fully good instance of goodness." These are confused, because triangularity is not one more drawn triangle and the Form of the Good is not one more good object.[^6]
- The point holds "even if the atheist were correct": atheism would not be vindicated by the God of classical theism turning out to be "another nonexistent member of a class of 'gods'" — "that sort of suggestion doesn't even make sense."[^7]

## Best Quotes
> "To suppose that the God of classical theism is 'a god' or 'a being' alongside Zeus, Venus, Thor, Quetzalcoatl, and so forth is like supposing that triangularity in the abstract is one triangle alongside the others one finds on chalkboards ... It is to commit a crude category mistake." [^7]

> "Each of these various gods is 'a being' alongside other beings, whereas the God of classical theism is not 'a being' ... but is rather underived or subsistent being itself, that from which anything else that exists or could exist derives its being." [^5]

## LDS Connection
The first half of Feser's reply — that monotheism is backed by demonstrative arguments where polytheism is not — is fully available to a Latter-day Saint engaging an atheist. The second half is where the Restoration and classical theism part most sharply, and a Latter-day Saint should handle it carefully rather than simply borrowing it. Feser defends a God who is "not a being" but **Being Itself**, with no essence/existence distinction (see [[The Real Distinction Between Essence and Existence]] and [[Divine Attributes Follow From Pure Actuality]]). LDS theology, by contrast, affirms that the Father *is* "a being" in the robust sense — an exalted, embodied Person who is the supreme God of this creation, and it is comfortable with a divine plurality (the Father, the Son, and exalted beings) in a way classical monotheism forbids (see [[Classical Theism Cannot Be Reconciled With the Bible]] and [[The Uncaused Cause Is Aristotle Not Scripture]]). So a Latter-day Saint can wholeheartedly press the "category mistake" point *against the atheist's flattening of God into a Marvel-style superbeing*, while declining Feser's premise that the only alternative to Zeus is the simple Being-Itself of Aquinas. Be fair to Feser: his core move — that worshipped Deity is the ground of all being, not one item in a cosmic inventory — protects against a genuinely shallow atheist caricature, and the God revealed in scripture as the Source and Sustainer is no mere member of a pantheon (see [[The Source and Sustainer - Who the God of the Bible Actually Is]]).
