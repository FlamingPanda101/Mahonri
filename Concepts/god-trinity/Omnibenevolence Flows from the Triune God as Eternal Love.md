# Omnibenevolence Flows from the Triune God as Eternal Love

## The Claim
[[Gavin (Catholic Debater)]]'s lead affirmative argument: omnibenevolence "follows necessarily from God's nature as triune love." [[1 John 4.8|God *is* love]] — not merely loving — and that is "ontological reality," only possible if God is triune. The Father eternally loves the Son, the Son returns that love, and the Spirit is "the personal communion of that love." Because this love is eternal, it is "not reactive, not earned, not conditioned by worthiness, covenant status, or institutional mediation." Therefore God's willing of the good of every moral subject is prior to anything we do.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **The "God is love" inference** — rate: theologically standard but contested. The move from "God is love" to "God must be triune" assumes love requires an eternal *interpersonal* object. *(scriptural-philosophical)* [^1] **Steelman of the opponent:** a unitarian or LDS theist can affirm "God is love" without the trinitarian metaphysic — love could be expressed toward eternally co-existent intelligences (LDS) or be eternally actualized in other ways; the inference is not strictly entailed.
2. **Baptismal Trinity tableau** — Gavin reads Christ's baptism (Spirit resting, Father speaking "in whom I am well pleased") as a manifestation of the three persons in loving communion. *(narrative-illustrative)* [^2] Evidence value: illustrative, not probative — the scene is also read non-trinitarianly.
3. **Priority of love over response** — the strongest practical payoff: if love is eternal and intra-divine, God's benevolence cannot be something humans trigger or earn ([[Romans 5.8|Rom 5:8]], "while we were yet sinners"). *(scriptural)* [^3] See [[Omnibenevolence Is Unconditional Grace Not a Contract]].

**Hayden's structural objection (steelmanned the other way):** even granting God *is* eternal love, the resolution is about willing the good of *every created subject without exception* — and that collides with God's free choice to create those he foreknows will be damned. Eternal intra-trinitarian love does not entail benevolence toward each contingent creature. See [[The Creation Dilemma]].

## Best Quotes
> "God is love. That's First John 4:8. This is not poetic metaphor. It is ontological reality. God is not merely loving. God is love himself. That is only possible if God is triune."[^1]

> "Love that exists eternally is not reactive, is not earned, it is not conditioned by worthiness, covenant status or institutional mediation."[^3]

> "The father eternally loves the son. The son eternally receives and returns that love. The holy spirit is the personal communion of that love."[^1]
