# Genesis 1 Image of God as Seedbed of Rights

## The Claim

[[Glenn Scrivener]] (citing [[Tom Holland]] and [[John Locke]]) argues that the *imago Dei* of [[Genesis 1.26-27|Genesis 1:26-27]] is the ultimate source of the Western rights revolution. Holland is quoted: "the ultimate seedbed of the American Revolution was, no matter what some of its framers might have thought, the book of Genesis."[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Two load-bearing elements of Genesis 1.** (a) Humans are made *in God's image* — grounding inherent worth and dignity; (b) humans are made *to have dominion* — i.e. to rule, "not to be slaves."[^2] *(Strong — these two motifs do real theological work and were explicitly invoked by abolitionists like [[Olaudah Equiano]].)*

2. **The Lockean transmission chain.** Genesis 1-2 → [[John Locke]] (rights grounded in the image of God and natural law) → [[Thomas Jefferson]] → [[Benjamin Franklin]] → modern "self-evident" assumption. See [[Secularized Founders Used Christian Language]].[^3] *(Coherent intellectual genealogy; Locke's biblical framing of rights is well-documented.)*

3. **"The one self-evident thing about rights is that they're not self-evident."** Scrivener: if rights were truly self-evident, they would be "much more widespread in human civilizations." Their *rarity* points to a specific (Christian) seedbed.[^4] *(Clever inversion of Jefferson's "self-evident"; but O'Connor's [[Western Values Predate or Arise Independently of Christianity|Hammurabi counter]] shows protection-of-the-weak was not unique to the biblical world.)*

4. **Steelman of O'Connor.** O'Connor presses on what *specifically* in Genesis does the work, getting Scrivener to commit to 1:26-27 (image + dominion).[^2] He counters that Jefferson — a slaveholder and "famously anti-Christian" — and the framers did not see themselves in a Christian project, and that the value's bare existence elsewhere (Hammurabi) defeats the *uniqueness* claim. See [[The Christian Origin of Equality]].[^5] *(Fair — the genealogy from Genesis through Locke is real, but "ultimate seedbed" overclaims if the same protective value has independent ancient sources.)*

## Best Quotes

> "The ultimate seedbed of the American Revolution was, no matter what some of its framers might have thought, the book of Genesis." — Tom Holland, quoted by Scrivener[^1]

> "The one thing that's self-evident about rights is that they're not self-evident. If they were, they would be much more widespread."[^4]
