# Ex Nihilo Entails Occasionalism - The Hidden Determinism of Classical Theism

## The Point
[[Blake Ostler]] presses an argument from his second volume of *Exploring Mormon Thought*: the doctrine of **creation ex nihilo** is not a harmless metaphysical preamble — it logically entails **occasionalism**, the view that virtually every event is *directly* caused by God in the moment it occurs. The chain runs: ex nihilo → *divine sustenance* (contingent things cannot keep themselves in existence, so God must re-create them moment by moment) → *occasionalism* (God re-creates each thing exactly as it is, with all its properties, every instant). The upshot is that classical theism — Calvinist, Thomist, even Arminian — cannot coherently house libertarian free will, and therefore cannot escape the problem of evil. The hidden determinism is the *root* of the classical problem. [^1][^2]

## Evidence
1. **Why sustenance follows from ex nihilo.** "It's not the nature of things that exist to exist; it's their nature to not exist without God creating it." Only God "has the kind of existence that is necessary"; everything else "is contingent on God, a cause to keep it in existence." So the doctrine of *divine sustenance* "is logically entailed by the doctrine of creatio ex nihilo." [^3]
2. **Why sustenance collapses into occasionalism.** Because a thing's nature "is to not exist," God "has to create it in that moment... to be just what it is in each moment" — including "all of its properties." Hence "virtually everything that happens, God's directly causing by creating it to be what it is in that moment." Ostler says he gives "three different arguments" in volume two for this conclusion. [^4]
3. **It defeats the Arminian escape.** Arminians try to preserve free will by saying God "got things rolling" but lets free humans act outside the determined order. Ostler answers that they "accept creation out of nothing," and ex nihilo's entailed sustenance means "every single event that occurs is created to be exactly the way it is" by God — so "the Arminians have an incoherent view of free will." [^5]
4. **Aquinas and Calvin already concede the structure.** Thomists say God causes everything "proximally"; Calvinists say "everything that happens happens because God wanted it to happen." Ostler's claim is that ex nihilo forces *all* of them toward occasionalism whether or not they own the label. [^6]
5. **Foreknowledge is a second, independent determinism.** "If God knew 600 years ago that I was gonna mow my lawn tomorrow, I can't change what God knew 600 years ago... To be free I have to have this power to do other than I do." Exhaustive foreknowledge of free acts is itself inconsistent with libertarian freedom. [^7]

## Best Quotes
> "Occasionalism is the view that virtually everything that occurs in the moment that occurs, it's directly caused by God, because God has to sustain everything in existence in every moment... and that's what creatio ex nihilo entails — it entails the denial of free will." [^1]

> "It's not the nature of things that exist to exist; it's their nature to not exist without God creating it... God has to create it in that moment because it's its nature to not exist." [^3]

> "I argue that the Arminians have an incoherent view of free will, because their view is inconsistent with the notion of creation out of nothing — it's also inconsistent with the notion that God has foreknowledge." [^5]

## LDS Connection
This is the metaphysical engine of the LDS theodicy. If ex nihilo entails occasionalism, then the classical God is the *direct* cause of every event including every evil — which is precisely why the classical problem of evil bites so hard and why its escapes degrade into [[Moral Quietude - Why God Has His Reasons Backfires as a Theodicy|moral quietude]]. Latter-day Saint theology cuts the chain at its first link: it **rejects creation ex nihilo** (see [[Creation Ex Nihilo Is Not Biblical]]). Matter and [[Intelligence Is Uncreated and Eternally Has Agency|intelligences]] are co-eternal and *self-existent*, so they do **not** depend on God's moment-by-moment re-creation for their being — there is no divine sustenance of the ex-nihilo kind, hence no occasionalism, hence room for genuine libertarian agency. This is the same fork developed in [[Creation Ex Nihilo Leads to Determinism]] and [[Free Will - Atheist Determinism vs LDS Co-Eternality]]: ex-nihilo Christians and hard-determinist atheists both make the will an effect of something outside the agent, while LDS co-eternality locates the agent's ultimacy in the uncreated intelligence. Note the honest cost (developed in [[God Is Constrained by Eternal Law Not Unconstrained]]): a God who does not create being from nothing trades absolute aseity for a coherent free will and a clean answer to evil. The classical reply — *divine concurrence* preserves creaturely powers (see [[Divine Conservation and Concurrence]]) — is exactly the position Ostler argues ex nihilo cannot consistently maintain.
