# John 8:58 — "Before Abraham Was, I Am" (Divine Name Agency)

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
[[John 8.58|John 8:58]] — Jesus says "Before Abraham was, I AM" — using the divine name from [[Exodus 3.14|Exodus 3:14]], proving he IS Yahweh/God.

## The Rebuttal

### 1. "I AM" is a coded reference — but to divine NAME, not divine IDENTITY
In the Greek Septuagint, *ego eimi* ("I am") translates two DIFFERENT Hebrew phrases:[^1]
- **Exodus 3:14:** *ehyeh asher ehyeh* → *ego eimi ho on* ... *ego eimi* sent you
- **Deutero-Isaiah:** *ani hu* ("I am he") → also *ego eimi*

These two distinct Hebrew divine self-identifications collapse into the same Greek phrase, making *ego eimi* "pregnant with import regarding the divine name — even though neither of them is actually Adonai and neither of them is the Tetragrammaton."[^2]

### 2. The divine name was a communicable vehicle of divine agency
In ancient Near Eastern thought, the divine name could be conferred onto a standing stone, an image, or an authorized agent. "When you impose the divine name, that charged the material media with the divine presence, which allowed the material media to presence the deity, manifest the presence, the power."[^3]

Jesus saying *ego eimi* is claiming to be the **authorized bearer** of the divine name — the one who can say "guess who got the divine name?"[^4] This is a claim to divine *agency*, not ontological *identity* with God.

### 3. This is still blasphemy — without being a claim to BE God
The Jews pick up stones, which Trinitarians argue proves Jesus claimed to be God. But blasphemy covers "so many more things than just claiming to be God" — denigrating God, misusing God's name, or claiming divine authority without being God can all be blasphemous.[^5]

### 4. The grammar is a cue, but doesn't settle the meaning
The unusual tense combination (past "was" + present "am") is "grammatically unusual enough to be a cue" that something more is going on. The consensus view is this signals the divine name reference. But this doesn't tell you *what kind* of claim is being made — ontological identity or authorized agency.[^6]

## Best Quote
> "Jesus is saying, 'Hey, guess who got the divine name? Yeah, wasn't you.' And then they picked up stones and he had to hide and run away." [^7]

## Counter-arguments
- Trinitarians argue the stoning proves the Jews understood Jesus as claiming to be God. Response: (a) "the Jews" in John is a generalized narrative device, not a reliable historical record; (b) blasphemy includes many offenses beyond claiming to be God; (c) [[John 10-33 - Making Yourself God (Missing Article)|John 10:33]] shows the accusation was about claiming divine status, not identity with Yahweh.
