# The Enochic Son of Man Matches LDS Christology Not Nicene Homoousios

#type/claim

## The Claim

The Son of Man of [[1 Enoch]]'s Parables — divine, pre-existent, **distinct in person** from the Father, sharing divine authority *by the Father's appointment*, and named and foreordained before creation — maps directly onto the LDS understanding of Jesus Christ as the pre-existent firstborn Son, distinct from the Father while sharing divine nature and purpose. It does **not** map onto the Nicene formula (*homoousios*, "same substance"). The common charge that Christ's pre-existence and Father–Son distinction are Joseph Smith inventions is therefore wrong: these are ancient, not new.

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. The Enochic Portrait Is Relational and Hierarchical
> "The Son of Man in First Enoch is divine, pre-existent, distinct from the Father as a person, sharing divine authority by the Father's appointment, and named and foreordained before creation. That description maps directly onto the LDS understanding of Jesus Christ."[^1]

Two figures in one throne room with distinct identities and a relational, hierarchical framework (1 Enoch 46, 48, 51) is "more naturally consistent with LDS theology than with a philosophical system that Jesus never used."[^2] See [[Book of Enoch - Enoch as the Son of Man]].

### 2. *Homoousios* Is a Non-Biblical Fourth-Century Term
> "Nicaea in 325 AD defined the Son as homoousios with the Father. Homoousios is a Greek philosophical term meaning same substance. It's not a biblical word. It doesn't appear anywhere in the Old or New Testament. It was coined to resolve fourth-century political and theological controversy, and the emperor who convened the council wasn't even baptized yet."[^2]

Arnold's framing: "a Greek metaphysical term invented by an unbaptized Roman emperor's committee."[^2] See [[Council of Nicaea]] and [[Homoousios Same Substance Not Same Person]].

### 3. It Rebuts the "Joseph Smith Invented It" Charge
> "The charge critics most commonly make against LDS theology is that the pre-existence of Christ and the distinction between the Father and the Son are Joseph Smith inventions with no ancient roots. The Parables of Enoch... contain explicit language about a pre-existent divine Son of Man figure distinct from the Father. This is not a new idea. It's an ancient one that got managed out of the canon."[^3]

Because the Parables are dated by mainstream non-LDS scholars to before or contemporaneous with Jesus (see [[The Parables of Enoch Are Pre-Christian Not a Post-Jesus Forgery]]), the LDS Christological shape predates Nicaea by centuries. This is the doctrinal payoff of the whole argument and fits [[LDS Theology as Restoration of Early Christianity]].

**Evidence rating: secondary** — the textual portrait is primary; the "maps onto LDS not Nicene" reading is interpretive and contested by Trinitarians.
