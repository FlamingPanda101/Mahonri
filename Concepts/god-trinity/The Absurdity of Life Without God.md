# The Absurdity of Life Without God

## The Point

[[William Lane Craig]] argues that the question "Does life have any real significance?" cannot be answered without first answering "Does God exist?" If God does not exist, then the physical universe is all there is, human beings are "accidental byproducts of nature" who were "not intentionally designed," there is "no absolute standard of moral value," and death is annihilation. Each of these three losses — meaning, value, and purpose — follows from atheism, and together they entail that "life is absurd." Craig stresses that this is not a hostile caricature: "many atheists themselves recognize this." [^1] The argument is not that atheists are bad or cannot live good lives, but that atheism cannot supply an *objective* foundation for the significance most people take for granted. This is Craig's framing of what other thinkers call [[Nihilism as Logical Endpoint of Atheism]].

## Evidence

- **No God entails no purpose.** "If God does not exist then the physical universe is all there is which means you and I are just accidental byproducts of nature… we were not intentionally designed so there's no purpose for us being here." [^2]
- **No God entails no objective value.** "If God does not exist there is no absolute standard of moral value." Craig enlists an atheist witness, [[Richard Dawkins]], who says a materialistic universe has "at bottom no design, no purpose, no evil, no good, nothing but pointless indifference." [^3]
- **The "many atheists agree" move.** Craig repeatedly anchors the thesis in atheist testimony — "If atheism is true life is absurd" — rather than asserting it as a theist's prejudice, which blunts the charge of caricature. [^1]
- **The distinction that does the work.** Craig is explicit that the claim is about *grounding*, not about behavior: he is "not saying atheists can't be good people," only that "atheism fails to provide an objective basis for seeing any particular action as good or evil." [^4] Confusing these two claims is the most common misreading of the argument.

## Best Quotes

> "If God doesn't exist it doesn't matter what you believe — our lives would have no objective meaning, value, or purpose. Many atheists themselves recognize this. If atheism is true, life is absurd." [^1]

> "We are just accidental byproducts of nature… we were not intentionally designed, so there's no purpose for us being here." [^2]

> "I'm not saying atheists can't be good people. Many atheists live good lives. What I'm saying is atheism fails to provide an objective basis for seeing any particular action as good or evil." [^4]

## LDS Connection

Where Craig argues only to "objective meaning, value, and purpose" in general, Latter-day Saint theology supplies a uniquely robust positive ground. The plan of salvation frames mortality as a purposeful second estate; God declares that His very "work and glory" is "to bring to pass the immortality and eternal life of man" (Moses 1:39), making each person an intended end rather than an accident. Eternal progression and the doctrine of co-eternal intelligences mean human worth is woven into the structure of reality itself, not projected onto a blank cosmos. Thus the LDS answer dissolves Craig's three deficits at once: intentional creation supplies *purpose*, God as the standard of goodness supplies *value*, and eternal life supplies enduring *meaning*. This complements the critique that the atheistic frame mistakes a metaphysical assumption for a finding (see [[Meaninglessness Is a Philosophical Assumption Not a Scientific Result]]).
