# Recapitulation - Christ Reverses Adam in Irenaeus

## The Point
Irenaeus of Lyons (c. 130-202) explained the Atonement as **recapitulation** (Latin *recapitulo*, "to go over the main parts again"): Christ recapitulates the whole history of Adam, but "in every particular in which Adam failed, Christ succeeds and restores the salvation of man that was lost in Adam." [^1] By becoming incarnate, Christ "commenced afresh the long line of human beings," recovering "what we had lost in Adam — namely, to be according to the image and likeness of God." [^2] Crucially, Irenaeus does not stop at restored immortality: Christ "did, through His transcendent love, become what we are, that He might bring us to be even what He is himself" — the seed of the **exchange formula** and of patristic **theosis** (see [[Christ Became Man That We Might Become God - The Exchange Formula]]). [^3]

## Evidence
1. **Built on Romans 5.** Irenaeus quotes Romans 5:19 ("by the obedience of one man... many should be justified") and writes: "God recapitulated [*recapitulavit*] in Himself the ancient formation of man, that He might kill sin, deprive death of its power, and vivify man." [^1] This single sentence fuses Restorer and Victor models.
2. **Grace begins at creation.** For Irenaeus "the first manifestation of God's grace is the creation of human beings in the image and likeness of God" — grace is present in human history "from the creation," not only from forgiveness. [^4]
3. **Eucharistic recapitulation.** Bread blessed becomes "the Eucharist, consisting of two realities, earthly and heavenly; so also our bodies, when they receive the Eucharist, are no longer corruptible, having the hope of the resurrection." (*Against Heresies* 4.18.5) [^5]
4. **Anti-Valentinian balance of grace and works.** Against Gnostics who claimed *gnōsis* freed them from good works, Irenaeus insisted "good works and proper Christian behavior remain important" — an early articulation of the delicate grace/works balance. [^6]

## Best Quotes
> "When [Christ] became incarnate, and was made man, He commenced afresh the long line of human beings and furnished us... with salvation; so that what we had lost in Adam — namely, to be according to the image and likeness of God — we might recover in Christ Jesus." [^2]

> "The Word of God, our Lord Jesus Christ... did, through His transcendent love, become what we are, that He might bring us to be even what He is himself." [^3]

## LDS Connection
Irenaeus's recapitulation makes the **Incarnation** itself salvific and ties restoration of the "image and likeness" directly to becoming "even what He is himself" — language Latter-day Saints read as an ancient anticipation of exaltation/theosis (see [[Human Deification in Mormonism]], [[Acorn and Oak Analogy for Theosis]]). The crucial qualifier, drawn out in [[Same-Species-as-God as the Core LDS Distinctive]], is that Irenaeus held God and humans to be different *kinds*, so his "become what He is" is participation, not same-species identity.
