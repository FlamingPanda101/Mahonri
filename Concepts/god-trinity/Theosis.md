# Theosis

## Overview

**Theosis** (deification, divinization) is the doctrine that the purpose of salvation is for human beings to be made partakers of the divine nature and brought into union with God. It is an overview/hub for an existing cluster in this vault rather than a standalone argument — see the linked notes for the specific claims.

The classic patristic formula is the **exchange**: *God became man that man might become God* ([[Christ Became Man That We Might Become God - The Exchange Formula]]). On the historic-Christian side, theosis is union and likeness "so far as possible" ([[Theosis as Likeness and Union So Far as Possible]]) and an eternal progression *toward* an infinite God that never arrives ([[Gregory of Nyssa's Epektasis - Eternal Progression Toward God]]).

The **LDS distinctive** is sharper: exaltation as becoming the *same species* as God, not merely God-like ([[Same-Species-as-God as the Core LDS Distinctive in Theosis]]; [[Human Deification in Mormonism]]). The vault argues this is rooted in a [[Divine Council and Deification Were Native to the Hebrew Bible|Hebrew divine-council background]], framed by analogies like the [[Acorn and Oak Analogy for Theosis|acorn and oak]], and shaped morally as the overcoming of the passions ([[Moral Formation as Deification - Overcoming the Passions]]). Critics' caricatures (e.g. "gods of their own planets") are addressed in [[Gods of Their Own Planets Is a Caricature of Exaltationist Theosis]].

## The Claim

Salvation culminates not merely in pardon but in transformation into the divine likeness and union with God — and on the Restoration reading, in genuine joint-heirship that shares God's own nature and glory.
