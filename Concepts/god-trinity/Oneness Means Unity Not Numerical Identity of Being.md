# Oneness Means Unity Not Numerical Identity of Being

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
Scripture's "the Father and I are one" / "one God" language proves the persons are **numerically the same being** (consubstantial), so the Trinity is biblical.

## The Rebuttal

### 1. Every other "oneness" text is unity, not sameness of being
The same biblical vocabulary of "one" is used where numerical identity is impossible: a husband and wife "shall be **one flesh**"; in [[John 17.20-23|John 17]] Jesus prays "that they may be one, **as** we are one" — wanting the disciples to be one *with* the Godhead. Nobody thinks a married couple becomes one ontological being, or that the disciples merge into God's essence. So "one" denotes deep unity/harmony of will, not identity of being. *(Strong: the burden shifts — the Trinitarian must show a single instance where biblical "oneness" means sameness of being.)* [^1]

### 2. There is no biblical instance of "one" meaning ontological sameness
The challenge laid down: "Can you provide me with one instance in the Bible that shows where he's talking about oneness referring to an ontological sameness? No, you can't." What scripture *does* supply is oneness as total unity of purpose and will. *(Moderate-strong; an argument from absence, but a well-aimed one.)* [^2]

### 3. Shared *nature* explains the deepest unity without sameness of being
Because humans share the divine nature in potential ([[2 Peter 1.4|2 Pet 1:4]]; [[Acts 17.28-29|Acts 17:28-29]]), believers can become "one" with God the same way the persons are one with each other — by complete harmony of will, not by collapsing into one being. See [[Humans Are the Same Species as God (Acts 17 Offspring)]]. [^3]

### Steelman (the opposing view)
The Trinitarian does not rest the whole case on "one"; the claim is that the *combination* of (a) strict monotheism (the Shema, [[1 Corinthians 8.4-6|1 Cor 8:4]]), (b) full deity ascribed to the Son ([[John 1.1|John 1:1]], [[John 20.28|John 20:28]]), and (c) the Son's distinctness from the Father forces *one being in three persons* as the only non-contradictory synthesis — "unity of will" allegedly under-explains texts that call the Son "God" outright and apply Yahweh-only texts to him. A purely "harmony of will" model, they argue, would equally make any obedient saint "God," dissolving the Creator/creature line. The LDS answer is that the line is preserved by the Father's relational primacy and by humans holding the nature only in potentiality (see [[Ontological Equality With Relational Subordination]]).

## Best Quotes
> "The man and woman shall become one flesh... John 17, that they will be one in us as I am one in thee. So anytime it's talking about oneness, it's not talking about the same... ontological creature." [^1]

> "Can you provide me with one instance in the Bible that shows where he's talking about oneness referring to an ontological sameness? No, you can't." [^2]

> "It's as if you have persons that are so in harmony that... even though we each have our own will, it's as if it's one will because we just want the same thing." [^4]
