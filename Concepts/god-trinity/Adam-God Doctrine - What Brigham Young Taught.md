# Adam-God Doctrine — What Brigham Young Taught

## The Claim
Both [[Joe Heschmeyer]] and [[Luke Hansen]] agree on the basic fact: **[[Brigham Young]] taught the Adam-God doctrine.** Hansen concedes it directly — "He did teach it, moving on" — short-circuiting the LDS folk-apologetic that Young never really said it [^1]. The doctrine, as Heschmeyer frames it, is that **Adam on earth is God and the father of Jesus Christ.**

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. The 1852 inaugural sermon (strong evidence — primary text)
The first appearance is the sermon of **April 9, 1852** (titled "Self-Government, Mysteries, etc."), eight years after Joseph Smith's 1844 death. Young: "Our Father and our God and the only God with whom we have to do is Adam" [^2]. This is the foundational text; its date and wording are not disputed by either side.

### 2. Young's own Godhead changes over time (strong — shows the doctrine is unstable)
In **1852** Young listed "three distinct characters, namely Elohim, Jehovah, and Michael" as a quorum (Father, Son, Holy Ghost). By the **October 8, 1854** "This Is Life Eternal" discourse he had *merged* Jehovah and Michael into one being (Adam) and made Jesus the son of *that* second person — yielding Elohim (1st), Jehovah-Michael-Adam (2nd), Jesus (3rd) [^3]. Hansen agrees Young "misidentifies" and is "muddled," "contradictory... even within himself" [^4].

### 3. Adam is subordinate, not the apex (important nuance both grant)
Crucially, **Adam is not the top of the hierarchy.** Young has "Jehovah Michael" obeying **Elohim**, who commands him to go organize a world [^5]. So even in Young's scheme there remains a God above Adam. Hansen leans on this: Young never denied the existence of the higher Father, he only relocated *who fathered Jesus* onto Adam [^6].

### Steelman (Heschmeyer's side)
The defender will say Adam-God was never coherent enough to be "the doctrine." But Heschmeyer turns this against the LDS: the very *instability* — "it's not even defined the same way every single time" — is the problem for a prophet claiming to speak God's mind [^7]. The 1852 line "Jesus... was begotten in the flesh by the same character that was in the Garden of Eden and who is our Father in Heaven" is, he says, "unambiguously Adam-God" [^8].

## Best Quotes
> "Our Father and our God and the only God with whom we have to do is Adam." — Brigham Young, 1852, quoted [^2]

> "Jesus, our elder brother, was begotten in the flesh by the same character that was in the Garden of Eden and who is our Father in Heaven." — Brigham Young, 1852, quoted [^8]

> "He did teach it, moving on." — Luke Hansen, conceding the historical fact [^1]
