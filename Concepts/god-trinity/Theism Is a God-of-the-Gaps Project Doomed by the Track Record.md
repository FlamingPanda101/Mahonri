# Theism Is a God-of-the-Gaps Project Doomed by the Track Record

## The Point
Seth's unifying thesis is that theism — ancient and modern — is structurally a *god-of-the-gaps* argument: it points at whatever humans do not yet understand and says "God did it," then retreats as natural explanations advance. He argues this is not an occasional theistic mistake but the whole enterprise: "the entire project of theism is one massive god of the gaps project."[^1] Because the pattern "is already proven" — natural explanations have repeatedly displaced supernatural ones — Seth predicts that fine-tuning, the Big Bang, and abiogenesis are simply the current frontier of ignorance from which God will likewise be evicted.[^2] Hansen's reply is that his own argument is the opposite: an inference *from* what we know about causation (minds produce finely tuned things), not from what we don't. See [[The God-of-the-Gaps Charge and Hansen's Reply]].

## Evidence
1. **Mythology as gap-filling.** Across religions Seth finds one pattern: an ancient sees a phenomenon, "and what do they do with mythology? They make something up" — Egyptian gods with "divine buckets" for the flooding Nile, Greek sun- and moon-chariots, Zeus's lightning, a subterranean snake making mountains.[^3] All are "structured like a god of the gaps argument... constantly targeting our ignorance."[^4]
2. **Modern theism just relocated the gap.** "Modern theists, they no longer do these ancient mythologies... now they're targeting the Big Bang, they're targeting abiogenesis... as our knowledge increases there's less and less ignorance, and so the theists are constantly moving the goal post toward that ignorance."[^5]
3. **The inductive track record.** "The pattern is already proven. We already know where this leads: every time you do god of the gaps, the natural explanations keep coming in... eventually theism is just going to get completely kicked out of the box."[^2] In cross-examination Seth defends his own "evolution of the gaps" as legitimate *because* it has an inductive success record: "evolution works across many different fields of study... if it works everywhere else why wouldn't it work here at the level of metaphysics."[^6]
4. **Hansen turns the charge around.** Hansen labels Seth's move "an atheism of the gaps" — "if you don't know something, evolution did it" — and insists order "does not emerge from random processes," so inferring a mind is grounded in "what we do know," not a gap.[^7] Compare the existing replies in [[God Is Not a God of the Gaps - Henry Ford vs the Laws of Internal Combustion]] and [[Natural Law of the Gaps Fallacy]].

## Best Quotes

## LDS Connection
Latter-day Saint apologetics generally resists god-of-the-gaps reasoning, preferring inference-to-best-explanation and grounding belief in covenant and personal revelation rather than scientific lacunae; the LDS picture of God as an *organizer* working through natural law (a Lawgiver behind the laws, not a stopgap for the unexplained) is precisely the move Hansen makes here. See [[God Is Not a God of the Gaps - Henry Ford vs the Laws of Internal Combustion]].
