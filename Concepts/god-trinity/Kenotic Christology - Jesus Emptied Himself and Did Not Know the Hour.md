# Kenotic Christology - Jesus Emptied Himself and Did Not Know the Hour

## The Claim

LDS Christology rejects the two-natures distinction. There is one nature: "divinity is humanity fully realized." Humans are the same *species* as God, differing only in *degree* — "acorns" to his "oak." Jesus was an oak who voluntarily *emptied himself* ([[Philippians 2.5-8|kenosis]]) down to our level to perform the atonement. So his ignorance and growth were genuine, not a nature-indexed fiction. [^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **One nature, difference of degree.** "We're the same species as God ... the only difference is a difference in degree. It's like we're acorns and he's an oak." This dissolves the [[Divine and Human Natures Are Mutually Exclusive|incompatible-natures]] problem at its root: there is no second, contradictory nature to reconcile. (Evidence: a coherent alternative *if* one grants LDS ontology of eternal intelligences and theosis; it trades the two-natures problem for a distinctive metaphysics.) [^1]
2. **Genuine kenosis.** Per Philippians 2, Christ "emptied himself" (*ekenōsen*) of the fullness of divinity and "leveled down" to mortality. So when scripture says he grew, learned, and did not know the hour, it means it literally. [^2]
3. **He really didn't know.** On this model the answer to "did Jesus know when the Second Coming would be?" is simply *no* — matching [[Matthew 24.36|Matthew 24:36]] without special pleading. The omniscience was a *potentiality* always latent in him, actualized after his exaltation. [^3]
4. **Soteriological payoff.** Because Jesus was "legitimately one of us" — feeling the same confusion, hopelessness, and pain — the incarnation accomplishes solidarity, not a disguise. (Evidence: theological motivation rather than proof; coheres with the LDS emphasis on Christ's relatability.) [^4]
5. **Steelman.** The classical theist objects that kenotic Christology either makes God *mutable* and *able to lose* essential properties (incoherent if those properties are essential to divinity), or quietly redefines "divinity" so that an ignorant, growing being still counts as God — which is precisely what the creeds deny. If divinity can be set aside and resumed, the critic says, it was never essential, and the "God who atones" is a being who was temporarily *not* God. (Evidence: a real bullet the kenoticist must bite; mainstream kenotic theories restrict the emptying to *contingently* exercised properties to avoid it.) The LDS reply embraces the bite: divine properties are dynamic and held in potentiality, and "always fully divine" is not, for LDS theology, equivalent to "always omniscient in act." [^5]

## Best Quotes

> "We say that divinity is humanity fully realized. There are no two natures. ... We're the same species as God. ... it's like we're acorns and he's an oak." [^1]

> "He voluntarily shed or emptied himself, as Philippians says, of the fullness of divinity, and he basically like leveled down to our level so that he could do the atonement." [^2]

> "Did Jesus know when the Second Coming would be? No, he didn't. He legitimately didn't know. ... It means Jesus was legitimately one of us." [^3]
