# Five Divine Attributes Cannot All Be True

## The Claim
Mainstream Christianity (Catholic, Protestant, Orthodox) affirms five claims about God that the video argues form an **inconsistent set**:
1. **Omniscience** — God knows the entire future with certainty, including how every life ends, before anyone is born.
2. **Omnipotence + freedom** — nothing forces, pressures, or obligates God to create; every choice is fully his own.
3. **Creation from nothing** — God brings each person into existence *ex nihilo*; no one simply shows up.
4. **The reality of hell** — some people end in eternal conscious suffering.
5. **Omnibenevolence** — God genuinely wants what is best for every person, no exceptions.

The claim: combine (1)+(2)+(3) and God knowingly and freely starts lives he foreknows will end in eternal ruin (4) — which is incompatible with (5). One of the five must be given up, and whichever you abandon, you are no longer describing the God mainstream Christianity professes [^1].

## Supporting Evidence
1. **The set is genuinely jointly inconsistent given a plausible bridge premise — rate: strong.** The bridge is moral: *a perfectly good being who is free and foreknows an avoidable, gratuitous, eternal harm will not actualize it.* With that premise, (1)–(5) cannot all hold [^2]. This is a recognized form of the [[Problem of Hell and Creation]]. **Steelman of the theist:** the inconsistency is only as strong as the bridge premise. Defenders deny it via (a) the free-will defense — a world of free creatures who *can* reject God is more valuable (see [[Free Will Cannot Justify the Decision to Create]]); (b) divine goodness being analogical, not univocal with human goodness; (c) middle-knowledge accounts where no feasible world saves everyone freely. Each is a way to keep all five by weakening the bridge.
2. **The video offers a clean disjunctive conclusion — rate: strong dialectically.** "There's no sixth option": deny perfect foreknowledge (open theism), deny God's freedom in whom he creates, deny *ex nihilo* (the LDS exit — see [[Pre-Mortal Existence Dissolves the Problem of Hell]]), deny hell's reality/permanence (annihilationism/universalism), or empty "good" of meaning [^3]. Listing the exits and naming their cost is rhetorically powerful and roughly tracks the actual map of philosophical responses.
3. **Locates the LDS move precisely.** Latter-day Saint theology resolves the set by denying (3): intelligences are co-eternal and uncreated, so God did not *manufacture* the damned. Compare [[Free Will - Atheist Determinism vs LDS Co-Eternality]] and [[Creation Ex Nihilo Leads to Determinism]]. **Caution:** this relocates rather than eliminates every theodicy problem (foreknowledge vs. agency persists).

## Best Quotes
> "These five things cannot be all true at the same time." [^1]

> "Either God doesn't actually know the future perfectly, or God isn't completely free in who he creates, or people aren't created from nothing, or hell isn't real or isn't permanent, or good doesn't mean what we think it means. There's no sixth option." [^3]

> "Whichever one you choose to give up, you are no longer describing the God that mainstream Christianity claims to worship." [^3]
