# Moses 1 Shows Satan Falsely Claims to Be the Only Begotten

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
Evangelical apologist [[Cliff Knechtel|Cliffe Knechtle]] asserts on the PBD podcast that a "fundamental teaching" and "big error" of Joseph Smith is that "Jesus and Lucifer are brothers… Jesus is the eternal God, Lucifer is a created angel who fell — they are not brothers." The hosts (with [[Luke Hansen]] reading the text) answer that **nowhere** in LDS scripture are Christ and Satan presented as begotten-equal brothers, and they locate the likely *source* of Knechtle's confusion in [[Moses 1.10|Moses 1]], where Satan **falsely** claims to be the Only Begotten and Moses rejects him — a passage that *distinguishes* the two rather than equating them.[^1]

## The Rebuttal

### 1. Moses 1 has Satan impersonating, not sharing, the Only Begotten title — rate: strong (textual)
When Satan confronts Moses ("Moses, son of man, worship me"), Moses answers "Behold, I am a son of God, in the similitude of his Only Begotten; and where is thy glory, that I should worship thee?" ([[Moses 1.10]]). Further down, "Satan cried with a loud voice… and commanded, saying: I am the Only Begotten, worship me." The hosts argue an evangelical who cherry-picks that one line ("I am the only begotten") and stops reading could mistakenly conclude Latter-day Saints equate Satan with the Only Begotten — but the passage's whole point is that the claim is a *lie* Moses refuses: "for this one God only will I worship, which is the God of glory."[^1] *Strength: the text explicitly opposes Christ and Satan; it is the opposite of the equation Knechtle alleges.*

### 2. "Brothers" never appears in the scandalous sense — rate: strong (LDS-internal, canonical survey)
Cardon stresses: "nowhere in our scriptures — Bible, Book of Mormon, Doctrine and Covenants, not even in the temple — are Christ and Satan ever presented as brothers" in the begotten-equal way Knechtle's audience will hear it. The kinship Latter-day Saints affirm is the premortal *spirit-child* sense (all are children of the Father), with Christ as the unique Only Begotten — not Satan's equal. See [[Jesus and Satan Being Spirit Brothers Follows From Rejecting the Trinity]] and [[Jesus and Lucifer Were Two Plans in the War in Heaven]].[^2]

### 3. The agreement neither side notices: Lucifer is a fallen angel for *both* — rate: moderate (irony)
The hosts note Knechtle says "Lucifer is a created angel who fell, who rebelled against God" — *which Latter-day Saints also affirm* — yet he doubles down on "they are not brothers" as if the LDS denied the fall. The disagreement is over *vocabulary and metaphysics* (spirit-sibling kinship under a shared Father), not over whether Satan rebelled, so Knechtle is misrepresenting the actual LDS position. See [[Jesus and Satan Are Brothers Only If Nothing Is Created]].[^2]

## Steelman of the Opposing View
The creedal critic answers that the objection was never about the Moses-1 passage and is not dissolved by it. (a) **The scandal is the shared category, not a shared title.** Even granting Moses 1 *distinguishes* Christ from Satan, the LDS framework still files Christ as the *firstborn* of a family of spirit children that also includes Lucifer — and that is precisely what the Nicene Christian rejects: the eternal Son is *homoousios* with the Father, categorically other than every creature, not the senior sibling of the highest fallen angel. So "they're not brothers in the way you think" still concedes a generic divine family the critic finds incompatible with Christ's unique deity. (b) **"Fundamental teaching" is defensible as *implied* doctrine.** Knechtle could reply that spirit-sibling kinship *follows* from LDS premortal theology even if no catechism lists "Jesus and Lucifer are brothers" as a discussion-one tenet — so calling it fundamental is not simply false. (c) **Source-tracing is not refutation.** Showing where a critic's confusion *might* originate (Moses 1) does not by itself establish the LDS view is true; it only shows the slogan is imprecise. The honest reading: the hosts are *correct* that Moses 1 opposes Christ and Satan and that LDS scripture never makes Satan Christ's begotten equal — so Knechtle's framing is a real distortion — but the deeper creedal objection (that *any* shared "spirit child" category subordinates the Son) survives and is addressed on its own terms in the related ontology notes, not by the Moses-1 point alone.
