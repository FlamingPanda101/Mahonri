# What Caused God Is a Straw Man - The No Special Pleading Reply

## The Point
Feser dismantles the stock atheist retort "If everything has a cause, then what caused God?" The argument never claims everything has a cause; it claims only that "whatever goes from potential to actual has a cause." [^1] Treating those as equivalent "is as silly as claiming that there is no significant difference between saying that all triangles have three sides and saying that all geometrical figures have three sides." [^2] Nor is exempting God arbitrary special pleading: what is purely actual "has no potentialities, and so there is nothing in it that needs to be, or indeed could be, actualized." It is "the one thing that need not have, and indeed could not have, a cause of its own." [^1]

## Evidence
- Two related misunderstandings Feser heads off:
  - The proof does **not** argue to a temporal beginning of the universe; even an eternally old universe "would be irrelevant," because the claim is that things "must be here and now sustained in existence by God." [^3]
  - The proof does **not** assume "the entire universe has to have some cause." You can "start with any old trivial object existing here and now — a stone, a cup of coffee." The universal claim "was a consequence of the argument, not a premise." [^4]
- The "special pleading" charge fails on three counts: (1) supposing a claim is ad hoc would be an **ad hominem** about motive, irrelevant to truth; (2) it is historically false — "for more than twenty-three hundred years, from Aristotle to Aquinas to the present day," proponents said *what goes from potential to actual has a cause*, never *everything has a cause*; (3) it is not ad hoc, following "naturally from Aristotle's analysis of change." [^5]
- "One hardly needs to believe in God in order to find it implausible to suppose that something that is merely potential could actualize itself." [^6]

## Best Quotes
> "What it says is that any change requires a cause; more precisely, it says that whatever goes from potential to actual has a cause. That is very different from saying that everything whatsoever has a cause." [^1]

> "The only thing that is ad hoc here is some critics' desperate attempt to salvage the 'What caused God?' objection in the face of the overwhelming evidence that it is directed at a straw man." [^6]

## LDS Connection
This is a **strongly shared apologetic** and almost entirely neutral on the classical-vs-Restoration question. Latter-day Saint apologists make the identical move against the "who made God?" gotcha: only what begins (or, here, what goes from potential to actual) requires a cause, so an eternal, uncaused God is no special pleading (see [[Who Made God - Only What Begins Needs a Cause]] and [[Special Pleading and God Logic]]). Feser's careful narrowing — *not* "everything has a cause" — is exactly the precision LDS thinkers rely on to affirm an uncaused God alongside eternal matter and intelligences. The only latent divergence is *why* God is uncaused: Feser grounds it in **pure actuality** (zero potentiality), whereas the Restoration grounds God's necessity differently and rejects the pure-actuality premise (see [[The Purely Actual Actualizer - Aristotle's Unmoved Mover]]). On the rebuttal itself, Feser and the LDS apologist are firm allies against the atheist objection.
