# Infinite and Eternal Are Not the Same — The Regress Objection Rebutted

## The Claim

The stock anti-LDS objection — "if God progressed to become God, then God had a God who had a God, on and on forever, which is a vicious infinite regress" — rests on a category error. [[Alex Arnold]] argues it confuses **infinite** with **eternal**, two words that mean fundamentally different things, and that the LDS model is eternal, not infinite, so no regress ever gets started.

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The definitional distinction.** *Infinite* means something that **has a starting point but no end** — "a laser fired into space, beginning somewhere and extending without terminus." *Eternal* means **no beginning and no end** — "like a ring with no point where it starts and no point where it stops." [^1]
2. **The objection smuggles in a beginning.** The regress complaint "assumes the LDS cosmological model is infinite, meaning that there's a starting point somewhere back that we just can't find." But the model is eternal: "God, intelligences, and matter have always existed in one form or another." The question *who made God?* "already assumes a kind of beginning that our framework explicitly rejects. It's a complete category error and not a genuine regress." [^2]
3. **What the Church actually canonizes (and does not).** Arnold is candid that "the church doesn't canonize any doctrine of God's prior progression in any specific detail. Point blank." What is taught is that **intelligences are eternal and uncreated**, and that "God is the supreme intelligence who most fully comprehends and operates in alignment with the eternal laws of existence." The mechanics beyond that "are genuinely unknown and the church leaves them that way deliberately for now." [^3] [^4]

This connects to the uncreated-intelligence ontology in [[God Found Spirits He Did Not Create - Book of Abraham Solve]] and the same-kind reading in [[Humans Are the Same Kind as God (LDS Ontology)]]: because intelligence/matter is beginningless, exaltation is participation in an eternal order rather than a link in an infinite causal chain.

## Best Quotes

> "Infinite means something has a starting point but no end... But eternal means no beginning and no end, like a ring with no point where it starts and no point where it stops." [^1]

> "The question of who made God already assumes a kind of beginning that our framework explicitly rejects. It's a complete category error and not a genuine regress." [^2]

## Steelman the Critic

(1) **It relabels rather than solves** — calling the chain "eternal" does not by itself dissolve the worry; an actually-existing beginningless sequence of Gods-who-had-Gods is exactly the kind of completed infinite that the classical theist (and the [[The Uncaused Cause Is Aristotle Not Scripture|Kalam-style]] arguer) finds incoherent, so asserting "eternal, not infinite" may beg the question. (2) **It concedes the disputed content** — Arnold defends the model by noting the Church *does not* canonize prior divine progression, which blunts the regress but also withholds the very claim the objection targets (the King Follett picture), so the critic can say the strong version remains unanswered. (3) The ring/laser analogy distinguishes *temporal* structure but does not address the *explanatory* regress (each God deriving divinity from a prior one) the objection is really pressing.
