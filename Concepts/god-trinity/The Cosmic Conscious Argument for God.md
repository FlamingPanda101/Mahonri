# The Cosmic Conscious Argument for God

## The Point
This is the formal syllogism at the center of the video. [[Inspiring Philosophy|Michael Jones]] takes a line of reasoning that Michio Kaku labeled "cosmic consciousness" — "there must be a cosmic consciousness reading the universe; objects sprang into being when measurements are made, and measurements are made by conscious beings"[^1] — and that Nobel laureate Eugene Wigner thought proved God, and recasts it as a tight deductive argument from the nature of consciousness to a personal God.[^2] Unlike a [[The Cosmological Argument - The Universe Began to Exist|cosmological argument]] from the universe's *beginning* or [[Cosmic Fine-Tuning Points to a Designer (Turek)|fine-tuning]] from its *order*, this argues from the *fundamentality of mind itself*.

## Evidence
The argument as Jones states it:[^2]
1. **Premise 1** — Contingent minds have either a personal explanation or a natural explanation. (Borrowed from JP Moreland's argument from consciousness.)
2. **Premise 2** — Quantum mechanics and other sciences imply the natural universe emerged from consciousness. (Jones does not defend this here, referring to his other videos.)[^3]
3. **Conclusion 1** — The natural universe cannot be the explanation of contingent minds. (If everything physical is itself emergent from consciousness, consciousness cannot in turn be an emergent feature of the physical.)[^4]
4. **Premise 3** — The explanation of conscious minds is personal.
5. **Premise 4** — A personal source is what we call God. (Deliberately modest — not a *specific* God, just a personal ground of consciousness.)[^5]
6. **Conclusion** — "God exists and is the reason contingent conscious agents exist."[^5]

The logical engine is the interaction of Premise 1 and Premise 2: a natural/emergent explanation is *removed* by the physics (Premise 2), leaving only the personal disjunct of Premise 1. The argument's force therefore rests on (a) the success of the reductionism critique (see [[Consciousness Is Fundamental and Irreducible]]) and (b) the quantum evidence for an emergent universe, which Jones imports from his prior work.

## Best Quotes
> "Premise 1: contingent minds either have a personal explanation or a natural explanation. I've borrowed this premise from JP Moreland['s] argument from consciousness." [^2]

> "Therefore God exists and is the reason contingent conscious agents exist." [^5]

## LDS Connection
The argument's deistic-thin conclusion ("a personal source we call God") is easy for Latter-day Saints to affirm and then enrich. Where Jones reaches a single personal mind that grounds all consciousness, LDS theology adds that reality is fundamentally personal and mental "all the way down": intelligence is eternal and uncreated (D&C 93:29), and God is an embodied, personal being who organizes rather than conjures conscious agents. The premise that "persons come only from persons" resonates with the LDS picture of a divine family. Honest caveat: the argument as a *deductive proof* is only as strong as its quantum premise, which Jones imports rather than defends, and which is contested among physicists — so it functions better as a cumulative-case consideration than a knock-down demonstration. Compare [[Maybe the Universe Itself Is the Necessary Being]] for the rival move of grounding everything in the cosmos rather than a mind.
