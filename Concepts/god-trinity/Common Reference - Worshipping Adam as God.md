# Common Reference — Was Brigham Young Worshipping a False God?

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
[[Joe Heschmeyer]] says [[Brigham Young]] worshipped a **false god** ("I only worship Adam"). [[Luke Hansen]] rebuts with a theory of **reference**: Young pointed at the true God of scripture and merely mis-identified him as the first man. Same referent, different (wrong) description.

## The Rebuttal

### 1. The Lois Lane / Clark Kent analogy (moderate — illustrative)
Hansen: asking "Is Lois Lane dating Superman?" can be answered yes *and* no — "same person under two aspects" [^1]. So Young calling Adam "God" is a "common reference" to the one true God, not a new deity. Heschmeyer himself supplies the parallel: someone who "loves Jesus" but is "an atheist" believes in God in one sense and not another [^1].

### 2. Vatican II: Muslims worship the same God (moderate — Catholic precedent)
Hansen cites *Nostra Aetate*: Catholics teach "Muslims and Catholics worship the same God" — "the same reference" — even though Muslim ideas about God are, in Catholic eyes, badly wrong. So same-referent-with-wrong-beliefs is already a category Catholics accept [^2]. Heschmeyer agrees this holds for Jews and Muslims re: "the God of Abraham."

### 3. Young preserved God's attributes (strong — internal consistency)
Hansen: Young never denied that God "created the earth," is "loving," is "the father of Jesus Christ," sent him "to die for us" — and even kept a higher **Elohim** above Adam. He only assigned "first man born on Earth" to that same referent [^3]. See [[Adam-God Doctrine - What Brigham Young Taught]].

### Steelman (Heschmeyer's side)
Heschmeyer's decisive distinction: **"Is his referent Adam, or is his referent the God of Adam? Because he says his referent is Adam, who is God"** [^4]. Where common reference breaks is the **uncreated Creator** question: "is there a creator of all things visible and invisible... an uncreated creator? We would say yes, you would say no" — a "huge chasm" that no amount of shared "God of Abraham" language closes [^5]. The Superman analogy *fails* if there is no single person who is both Clark Kent and Superman. He then deploys the [[Golden Calf and Bronze Serpent as Idolatry Tests]] to show "true attributes, false object" is still idolatry.
