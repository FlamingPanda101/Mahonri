# The Superlative God Deflection Collapses Into Nothing

## The Point
When the resolution ("Is the God of Calvinism morally reprehensible?") turned against him, [[James White]] pivoted to a different debate — *"your God is an exalted man on another planet,"* pressing perfect-being / classical-theist intuitions that only a God who is "existence itself" can ground morality. The panel reads this two ways. First, as a **rhetorical, not substantive, move**: a "my dad can beat up your dad" / "my God is bigger than your God" deflection that abandons the actual resolution ("comparing sizes of gods right now"). Second, as a self-defeating *theology of superlatives*: a God defined as the maximal extreme of every attribute ("the mostest of this, the mostest of that") gets "tangled up" and "ends up being nothing" — the very superlatives that were meant to exalt him (omnipotence → creation ex nihilo → authorship of evil) are what generate the reprehensible conclusion. Notably, the panel also turns a critical eye on **Hansen's own handling** of the pivot.

## Evidence
1. **The deflection named.** "James take the 'my dad can beat up your dad' route... Is he really comparing sizes of gods right now? My god's bigger than your god." The debate "isn't which god's better. It's: is the God of Calvinism morally abhorrent?" — but "that's the debate James wanted to have." [^1]
2. **The superlative God self-destructs.** "If God has to be everything to its absolute extreme, then he ends up being nothing. You get tangled up." The "parental perfection fallacy": people want a maximally-super God for the security a child wants from a parent, and "take these extremes to the extremes" until omnipotence entails creation from nothing and all that follows. [^2] See [[Creatio Ex Nihilo Is the Lynchpin Difference]].
3. **Hansen's punt — a candid in-house critique.** Pressed "does your church teach God is an exalted man?", Hansen kept answering "my God is eternal" and reading verses rather than saying yes. The panel grants this "kind of seems a little bit weaselly" to online viewers and "in some ways weakens his case," even while judging he won — *because* White "was helping him." [^3]
4. **Why Hansen punted, and the internal split.** He stayed at the level "all Christians agree you're crazy," a slam-dunk that didn't require defending LDS theology; and he genuinely rejects **infinite regress**, holding one eternal Father (the [[LDS Father Was Always Fully Divine (No Eternal Regression)|always-fully-divine Father]] view shared with [[Blake Ostler]]) against the [[Lorenzo Snow]] couplet, which guest Christopher Blythe defends. The breakdown airs this as an unresolved LDS family disagreement, not a settled point. [^4]

## LDS Connection
The breakdown distinguishes a *rhetorical* deflection (changing the resolution) from the *substantive* problem it papers over — that maximal perfect-being theology is what produces the monstrous God in the first place ([[Calvinism Is Consistent but Built on a Single Bad Premise]]). The Latter-day Saint alternative is not a "smaller" God but a differently-grounded one: an eternal Father within reality whose perfections are *moral* rather than the metaphysical superlatives of classical theism ([[Human Deification in Mormonism]]). The candid critique of Hansen's punt also models the vault's steelman discipline — conceding a rhetorical weakness in one's own champion while affirming the underlying position.
