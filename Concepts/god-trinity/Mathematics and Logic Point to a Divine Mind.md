# Mathematics and Logic Point to a Divine Mind

## The Point
A distinct strand within the Augustinian proof runs specifically through **mathematics and logic**. Their truths are **necessary, unalterable, and eternal**: 2 + 2 = 4 was true before anyone realized it and would remain true even if every human died, even if the entire material world ceased to exist.[^1] The material world and the human mind, by contrast, are contingent and changing. So mathematical and logical truths cannot be grounded in either — and since the series of numbers is *infinite* while material things and human ideas are only finite, mathematics cannot depend on them.[^2] These necessary, infinite truths point beyond matter and finite minds to a necessary, infinite intellect.[^3]

## Evidence
Feser assembles several mutually reinforcing arguments:
- **From geometry.** We *discover* rather than invent that a Euclidean triangle's angles sum to two right angles; we could not change it if we wanted to, it held before any mind existed, and no material object has the perfection geometrical objects have. So geometrical truth depends on neither mind nor matter.[^4]
- **From mathematics in general.** Mathematical truths are necessary while mind and world are contingent; and the number series is infinite while material things and human ideas are finite — so the objects these truths concern cannot depend on either.[^2]
- **The infinity + necessity move toward one mind.** The propositions and logical relationships among abstracta are infinite in number (for every number there is a true and a false proposition about its parity, and every other proposition is consistent or inconsistent with each). These relationships hold whether or not any material world or finite mind exists, so there must be a necessarily existing intellect grasping the *entire infinite system* — a "conceptually omniscient being, a being that eternally understands all essences, possibilities, and necessary truths."[^5]
- **Necessity is grounded in the divine nature.** Because the Scholastic holds God is simple, to say possibilities and necessities are grounded in ideas in the divine intellect is to say they are grounded in God's nature.[^6] Feser defends this against Brian Leftow's objection that it makes God's nature "depend" on facts about water (water = H₂O): just as 2 + 2 = 4 entails necessary truths about rocks without depending on mineralogy, the divine nature entails necessary truths about water without being hostage to hydrology.[^7]

## Best Quotes
> "Mathematical truths in general are necessary and unalterable, while the material world and the human mind are contingent and changing—for example, that 2 + 2 = 4 was true long before anyone realized it, and would remain true even if every human being forgot about it or died out." [^1]

> "The series of numbers is infinite, but there are only finitely many material things and only finitely many ideas within any human mind or collection of human minds. Hence, the series of numbers cannot be dependent for its existence on either human minds or the material world." [^2]

## LDS Connection
This is one of the cleanest allies against atheism in the chapter: the naturalist uses mathematics and logic constantly yet, on a "matter in motion" ontology, cannot account for their necessity, infinity, or eternal truth — exactly the gap a divine Mind fills (compare [[The Laws of Logic Are Immaterial and Point to a Mind]]). For Latter-day Saints the eternity and unalterability of such truths is not a problem but an expectation: LDS scripture treats law and truth as everlasting and not subject to revision (D&C 88:13; D&C 130:20–21 ties blessings to "irrevocably decreed" eternal law). Here the *partial convergence* with classical theism is real and worth naming honestly — both sides agree there are eternal, mind-independent necessary truths. The *divergence*, fair to Feser, is over whether those truths are ideas God *authors* from his own simple nature (Feser's deity theory) or eternal realities God *honors* and works within ([[God Is Constrained by Eternal Law Not Unconstrained]]). The LDS instinct that even God cannot make 2 + 2 = 5 lines up with Feser's necessity claim; the two part ways only on the metaphysics of *why*.
