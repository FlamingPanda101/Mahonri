# Salvation by Theology Damns Almost Everyone

## The Point
If correct Trinitarian metaphysics is a *salvation issue* — as [[Avery (God Logic)|Avery]] holds — then salvation turns on passing a graduate exam in Christology. The episode presses the reductio: the doctrines are so technical that the man defending them on stage failed (see [[Avery Tripped the Apollinarian Wire]]); the ordinary believer "has never even heard of this question"; so on this standard "1% of people that think they are saved are actually saved... or less." Worse, it damns *backwards in history* — fathers like Origen, condemned posthumously, and their whole congregations. And it produces a perverse incentive: since *understanding-then-rejecting* a dogma damns you while ignorance is excused, the safest move is to **not read** — "just keep your head in the sand." A salvation scheme whose optimal strategy is willful ignorance, the hosts argue, refutes itself. LDS soteriology is grace- and discipleship-based, not theology-gated.

## Evidence
1. **The exam standard:** "if having the right conception of theology is what gets you saved, 1% of people that think they are saved are actually saved — or less."[^1]
2. **It indicts the lay believer:** "your lay Christian who's trying to follow Jesus... he's concerned with the teachings of Jesus, he's not concerned with the teachings of Gregory the Theologian."[^2]
3. **The requirements keep stacking:** the first believers "all they had to do is have faith in Jesus," yet later one must also accept dogmas "they didn't have to accept... the requirements for salvation just keep stacking."[^3]
4. **The perverse incentive:** "if you understand it and you reject it... it's probably just play it safe and don't read it. Cuz if you read it, then you're going to be damned. That's not a good system at all."[^4]
5. **It damns the fathers retroactively:** "a lot of historical Christians that are going to be damned by these guys, that have been condemned by the church posthumously... the Origenists, yeah."[^5]

## Best Quotes
> "If having the right conception of theology is what gets you saved, 1% of people that think they are saved are actually saved. — Or less."[^1]

> "Just play it safe and don't read it. Cuz if you read it, then you're going to be damned. That's not a good system at all."[^4]

> "We believe in salvation by grace alone, not by theology alone."[^6]

## LDS Connection
This is the soteriological half of the episode's case: even granting the creedal Trinity, *making it salvific* yields absurd results, whereas the LDS frame — grace through Christ, worked out in discipleship and covenant — does not condition heaven on metaphysical precision (see [[Understanding the Trinity Is Not Required for Salvation]], [[Orthopraxy over Orthodoxy in LDS Salvation]], [[Creedalism Replaced Relationship With Theology]]). Note the fair distinction the other side draws — that *knowing* rejection differs from *imperfect understanding* (see [[Distinction Between Knowing Rejection and Imperfect Understanding]]) — and the direct counter-thesis catalogued in [[Trinity Is a First-Rank Salvation Issue]].
