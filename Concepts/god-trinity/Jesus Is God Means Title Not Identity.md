# Jesus Is God Means Title Not Identity

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)

"Jesus is God" — therefore Jesus and God are one and the same. The Trinitarian presses statements equating Jesus with God ([[John 1.1|John 1:1]], [[John 20.28|"My Lord and my God"]]) as proof of consubstantiality. [^1]

## The Rebuttal

1. **Disambiguate the copula.** "Is" can express the *is of identity* (Jesus is the very same being as God) or the *is of predication/title* (Jesus is divine, "a God," godly). These yield radically different logics. [^1]
2. **Identity reading self-destructs into modalism.** If "Jesus is God" and "the Father is God" are both identity (J=G, F=G), then by transitivity J=F — Jesus *is* the Father. That is modalism, which all parties confess as heresy (it makes Jesus pray to himself). Therefore "Jesus is God" *cannot* be the is of identity. (Evidence: strong; Trinitarians themselves reject modalism, so they must reject the identity reading — Hansen's "that's modalism, Patrick" meme point.) [^2]
3. **Title reading is harmless — and is what scholars grant.** Read as a title, "the Word is God" means "the Word is fully divine." Hansen notes even [[James White]] concedes this is how the predicate-noun construction of John 1:1c reads ("the Word was divine"). Then "the Father is God / the Son is God / the Spirit is God" simply means each is fully divine — three persons with the fullness of the divine nature, the Godhead. No contradiction. (Evidence: the grammatical point about anarthrous *theos* in John 1:1c is widely discussed; "qualitative" readings are mainstream.) [^3]
4. **Steelman.** The Trinitarian replies that the title reading, taken alone, *under*-reads the data: [[John 20.28|Thomas's confession]] uses the articular *ho theos mou* ("my God") *of Jesus*, and worship/divine prerogatives are ascribed to him — so "merely divine title" is too weak. The orthodox claim is not crude identity (which would be modalism) but that Jesus is a distinct *person* who is *the same being/ousia* as the Father — a third option the who/what test is meant to expose, not a confusion the Trinitarian has overlooked. (Evidence: fair; the strongest Trinitarian never asserts simple identity.) The LDS reply routes back to [[The Who or What Test for the Word God|the who/what test]]: "same being" is precisely the term that must be cashed out as either nature (agreed, Godhead) or person (modalism). [^4]
