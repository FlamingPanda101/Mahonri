# Loss of the Divine Family Is the Core Apostasy

## The Claim
The "most pernicious" element of the Great Apostasy is the loss of the **divine-family** theology: the estrangement of believers from a literal Heavenly *Father*. Greek-philosophical, *ex nihilo* monotheism dissolved God's fatherhood and personhood into an unknowable, amorphous Absolute — cutting humanity off from its "divine inheritance."

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. Jesus's own language is familial (rating: strong; canonical)
[[John 20.17|John 20:17]]: "I ascend unto my Father, and your Father; and to my God, and your God." Butler: "in what world is there a son and a father without a mother?" The number-one title Jesus gives God is *Father*. The prodigal-son and pearl parables make sense only if "you're God's child... on your maturation journey." [^1]

### 2. Joseph McConkie: loss of divine inheritance (rating: moderate; LDS authority)
[[Joseph McConkie]] (*Sons and Daughters of God*): the apostasy's deepest harm is "the loss of our divine inheritance — as soon as you cut yourself off from God the Father and he's no longer your Father, everything changes." [^2]

### 3. The ancient Near Eastern background (rating: moderate; via Butler / Ugarit)
Butler (*In the Language of Adam*) reconstructs an original Israelite theology of a divine family — El the father, his consort, the council of divine sons — preserved in [[Deuteronomy 32.8-9|Deut 32:8-9]] (nations apportioned among the sons of God). Creedal monotheism erased this. See [[Ugaritic Texts Reveal Israels Original Pantheon]]. [^3]

### 4. It dissolves God into a concept (rating: moderate; theological consequence)
Hayden: the creedal/philosophical God becomes "a concept, not an actual being that has a will" — like "the universe brought us together." The unknowable God of the creeds ("you can't understand it") is contrasted with a comprehensible, personal Father. Day ties this to Joseph Smith's *Lectures on Faith*: to have faith in God you must understand his nature. [^4]

### 5. Steelman of the opposing view
Trinitarian Christians affirm a robust divine fatherhood and adoption (Rom 8:15, "Abba, Father") *without* a literal divine mother or a corporeal God — holding that "Father" denotes an eternal relation within the Godhead and our adopted sonship through Christ, not a biological family. The evangelical reading Hayden voices: anthropomorphic language ("Father," "hand," "eyes") is accommodation to help finite minds grasp an incomprehensible God. Butler's reply: maybe the "picture" is of something genuinely comprehensible — the actual familial nature of God — and the incomprehensibility is the Greek import, not the biblical datum.
