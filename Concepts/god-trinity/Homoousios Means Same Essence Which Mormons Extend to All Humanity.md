# Homoousios Means Same Essence Which Mormons Extend to All Humanity

## The Claim
The Nicene term **homoousios** (ὁμοούσιος) simply means "same essence." Far from being a Mormon stumbling block, the guest claims Latter-day Saints can affirm it enthusiastically *and* extend it: not only is Christ "same essence" as the Father, but **all of humanity** is the same essence as the Father. "Let's double and triple down on the homoousios." [^1]

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. The definitional move (the core)
"What did they think homoousios meant in the early 4th century when this was written down? ... Same essence. Mormons don't disagree with that." [^2] The guest then escalates: "We even believe that humans are homoousios with the Father. So, not only is Christ consubstantial ... we believe all of humanity is the same essence." [^3] **Rating: lexically defensible at the surface** — *homo-* (same) + *ousia* (being/essence) — **but it equivocates on *ousia*.** Nicaea used *ousia* in the strong numerical sense (one identical divine being), whereas the LDS extension to all humanity requires the *generic* sense (the same *kind* of being), which is exactly the reading (*homoiousios*-adjacent / generic unity) the council rejected.

### 2. Distinguishing 4th-century from 21st-century homoousios (the framing)
"I don't believe in the 21st century interpretation of homoousios, but we sure as heck do believe in the fourth-century interpretation." [^4] On the guest's telling, the modern Western reading smuggles in **co-equality** and **divine simplicity**; "some Mormons when they hear consubstantial, they automatically presuppose co-equality and divine simplicity ... and that came along with the Athanasian metaphysics 100 200 300 years later." [^5] **Rating: the historical layering point has merit** — divine simplicity was elaborated later — but the claim that 4th-century *homoousios* excluded numerical unity is historically weak; the anti-Arian intent was precisely numerical sameness of being.

### 3. The scholarship cited
The guest again points to **Beau Branson** and **David Bercot** "amongst other patristic scholars" for "the original context of homoousios," while candidly admitting "none of these people are Mormon" and "would take issue with other beliefs that we had." [^6] **Rating: honest sourcing, but the cited scholars do not endorse the human-homoousios extension.**

### 4. Steelman of the opposing view
Orthodox/Protestant reply: the extension "all humans are homoousios with the Father" is exactly the error Nicaea anathematized. If "same essence" were generic ("same kind"), then *homoousios* would have settled nothing against Arius, who could happily agree the Son was the same *kind* of derived being. The fathers chose *homoousios* over the milder *homoiousios* (like-essence) to assert that the Son is the **numerically identical** divine being — a claim that, by definition, *cannot* be true of creatures. The LDS extension thus affirms the word while denying its function. Compare the existing note [[Homoousios Same Substance Not Same Person]], which distinguishes shared substance from shared personhood; this concept goes further by universalizing the substance to humanity.
