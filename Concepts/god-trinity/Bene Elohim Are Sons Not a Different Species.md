# Bene Elohim Are Sons Not a Different Species

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)

The objection (associated with [[Michael Heiser]]'s "species-uniqueness"): the *bene elohim* ("sons of God") and other *elohim* are an *ontologically different species* from Yahweh — created spirit-beings, categorically not the same kind as God — so their existence doesn't imply humans or the Son share God's nature. [^1]

## The Rebuttal

1. **"Son" means same kind.** "If I have a son, I don't have a cat. I have something that is of my species." Calling the divine beings *sons of God* (and even human beings *elohim*) signals shared kind, not categorical difference. To read a dog or human "son" as actually a "cat" (different species) ignores the family language the authors deliberately chose. (Evidence: a real lexical/conceptual point about *ben*; whether it implies *ontological* same-kind is the contested move.) [^1]
2. **The authors knew the distinctions and still said "son."** Textual variants prove awareness: [[Deuteronomy 32.8-9|Deut 32:8]] reads "sons of God" (Dead Sea Scrolls / LXX *angeloi*), with the Masoretic "sons of Israel" a later change; they distinguished angels, humans, and sons but, where they meant *son*, "they really do mean son." (Evidence: the textual-variant data is solid; the inference that the original always means literal divine sonship-of-kind is an interpretation.) [^2]
3. **Differences are of glory, not species.** What the Hebrews tracked were *differences in degree/glory* within one family, with God highest — not Greek-style species barriers. This grounds [[Kenotic Christology - Jesus Emptied Himself and Did Not Know the Hour|theosis]]: all are the same kind at different levels. [^3]
4. **Steelman.** Heiser's actual view is more careful than a flat "different species": he argues Yahweh is *species-unique* among the *elohim* (the only uncreated, uniquely-attributed deity), while granting the others are real *elohim* (a "place of residence" / spirit-realm category, not a fixed essence). On this reading "son of God" denotes *membership in the divine council/family* without entailing the *same uncreated nature* — exactly the LDS inference Heiser would resist. The Creator/creature line (creatio ex nihilo) does real work for him. (Evidence: this is closer to Heiser's published position; the LDS reading presses his data past his conclusions — Blake Ostler likewise treats Heiser's species-uniqueness as an anachronistic metaphysical overlay, see [[Heiser Species-Uniqueness Is an Anachronistic Metaphysical Distinction]].) The LDS reply: "species-unique" smuggles a later ontology into a text whose family language and degrees-of-glory framework know no such barrier. [^4]

## Best Quotes

> "Son, if I have a son, I don't have a cat. Like, I have something that is of my species." [^1]

> "When they say son, they really do mean son. ... What they had was differences in glory. ... you're all part of the same species, but you're all at different levels and God is the highest of that species." [^3]
