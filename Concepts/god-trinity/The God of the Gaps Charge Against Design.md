# The God of the Gaps Charge Against Design

## The Point
Atkins' sharpest charge is that intelligent design is "intellectually lazy" — that ID proponents "say there comes a point where the only answer will be that God did it," stopping inquiry where hard work should continue, which is why he calls teaching children "we can't know" a form of "child abuse."[^1] On this telling ID is a **god-of-the-gaps** move and a science-stopper. Meyer's reply has three prongs: (1) his argument is *not* from ignorance but from knowledge — what we *do* know about the powers of undirected causes *and* about the known cause of information, namely mind;[^2] (2) ID is *not* a science-stopper — it generates research questions and has made a confirmed prediction (functional "junk DNA");[^3] and (3) historically, the founders of modern science *assumed* design and it never stopped inquiry — "in 300 years it's never been a science stopper."[^4] Meyer and Haville add a counter-charge: it is *materialism* that becomes "a god of the gaps in terms of lack of knowledge," promising that some materialistic process did it whatever the evidence shows.[^5]

## Evidence
1. **Atkins' charge.** "If I were to identify a single deficiency of your procedures it's your intellectual laziness, because you say... we need the finger of God effectively... you're terrified to call it God."[^6] And: teaching children there is something we cannot find out "I think is child abuse, quite honestly."[^1]
2. **Not an argument from ignorance.** "The argument that I developed... and the argument that Michael Behe develops in *Darwin's Black Box* — these are not arguments from ignorance, not arguments based on what we don't know. They're arguments based upon what we do know" about both undirected processes and about mind as a cause of information.[^2]
3. **Veracausal reasoning.** Meyer invokes Darwin's "principle of vera causa": "What we know from our experience is that a mind is necessary to produce functionally specified digital information." (Atkins: "Nonsense.")[^7]
4. **A confirmed prediction (ENCODE).** From the mid-1990s ID predicted the non-coding genome would prove "not junk but importantly functional," against the Darwinian expectation of "gibberish"; "since the publication of the ENCODE project in 2007 there's been... a wealth of information showing that prediction is correct."[^3]
5. **Ten predictions, a research agenda.** Meyer notes *Signature in the Cell* ends with an epilogue of research questions and "an appendix listing 10 separate predictions" the theory makes — "so it's not a science stopper at all."[^8]
6. **The founders assumed design.** "If you go back to... Newton and Boyle and Kepler, those scientists accepted intelligent design as a starting point... they thought nature was intelligible because it had been designed by a rational agent... and in 300 years it's never been a science stopper."[^4] Atkins: "that was 300 years ago, we've moved on."[^9]
7. **The counter-charge.** Haville: "the atheistic materialist position is actually a god of the gaps in terms of lack of knowledge"; the materialist assumes a material cause "whatever the evidence."[^5]

## Best Quotes
> "These are not arguments from ignorance... they're arguments based upon what we do know — not only about what undirected processes of evolution can and can't produce but also about what we know... about another type of cause, that cause being agency or mind." [^2]

> "In 300 years it's never been a science stopper; from the beginning it's really been literally the [engine of] science." [^4]

> "I think the atheistic materialist position is actually a god of the gaps in terms of lack of knowledge." (Haville) [^5]

## LDS Connection
The reply that design (or theism) is not a gap-filler but a *precondition* of intelligible science is shared ground: the Restoration teaches that a divine, ordering intelligence pervades and sustains the cosmos — "the light... which is in all things, which giveth life to all things, which is the law by which all things are governed" (D&C 88:6–13) — so an orderly, law-governed creation open to rational study is exactly what one should expect. A Latter-day Saint can fully endorse exposing materialism's own promissory "gap" (a material cause assumed in advance "whatever the evidence"). The usual caveats hold: ID's designer is generic, not the embodied, organizing God of the Restoration, and Meyer's framing assumes a creator-mind behind matter rather than a God who organizes eternal matter. For the developed treatment of the demonstration-vs-gaps distinction, see [[God of the Gaps - Demonstration vs Scientific Hypothesis]] and [[Theism Makes Science Possible]].
