# God Is the Divine Kinsman and Goel-Redeemer

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)|Dave Butler]] argues that the **oldest Israelite anthropology of God** is *kinship*: God is not a Platonic abstraction but the **senior relative of the tribe** — the grandfather/uncle/clan-head — and that humans are literally *in his family*, animals and gods included. Following [[Frank Moore Cross]] (*Canaanite Myth and Hebrew Epic*, "God as the divine kinsman") and W. Robertson Smith's anthropology of Semitic religion, Butler shows this kinship grammar surfacing in three biblical institutions: the command to **cleave** (*devak*) to Yahweh as a bride cleaves to a husband, the **goel** (kinsman-redeemer) duty, and Paul's "we are his **offspring** / same *genus*" in [[Acts 17.28-29|Acts 17]]. This grounds the LDS "family of heaven" in the structure of the text itself, contrasting sharply with the *Enuma Elish*, where humans are made to be the gods' **slaves**.

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. W. Robertson Smith / Cross: the nomad family includes the gods (rating: moderate; cites scholarship)
On Smith's anthropological reading of Semitic religion, a person lives in an **extended family group** in which the animals (goat, camel, ox) *and the gods* are kin — so slaughtering an ox to eat is "killing a family member," a sacred act. [[Frank Moore Cross]]'s essays on **"God as the divine kinsman"** make this the old Israelite idea: "God is not the abstraction that later urban intellectuals wanted, but God is my relative." [^1] [^2]

### 2. The "cleave" (*devak*) and marriage metaphor (rating: moderate; lexical/exegetical)
The verb in Genesis 2 — a man shall "**cleave** (*devak*) unto his wife" — is the *same verb* Israel is commanded with in Joshua and elsewhere: ***devak* unto Yahweh.** The kinship is spousal as well as paternal; hence Hosea casts Israel as Yahweh's bride. So "God is our divine kinsman in the sense that we're supposed to marry him." [^3] (Compare the parental kinship of [[Hosea 11.1|Hosea 11]], "out of Egypt I called my son.")

### 3. The *goel* / kinsman-redeemer (rating: moderate)
A senior kinsman is the ***goel*** — the **redeemer** — with the obligation to buy back family land lost to outsiders (the Jubilee right, Lev 25) and to "buy you out of debt." God as *goel* is therefore God acting out the legal duties of the clan's senior man on Israel's behalf. [^4]

### 4. Paul: humans are God's *genus* (rating: moderate; NT)
In [[Acts 17.28-29|Acts 17:28-29]] Paul tells the Greeks "we are also his **offspring**... we are the offspring of God." Butler stresses the Greek is *genos* — "**we are the same race, the same kind.** Whatever God is, we are that — just at a lower state." [^5] This is the kinship claim in the New Testament, undergirding the LDS "same species" ontology and complementing [[Anthropomorphic God Grounds Heavenly Mother and Eternal Families]].

### 5. Contrast with *Enuma Elish* slavery (rating: moderate; comparative)
The biblical anthropology is *not* universal in the ANE: in the **Enuma Elish**, humanity is created to be **slaves of the gods.** Israel's distinctive note is kinship — "we're not created to be God's slaves... God is the senior man in our tribe." [^6] The kinship picture also explains *why* a personal, relational God can be reasoned and pled with — see [[The Intuitive Personal God Points Back to the Family of Heaven]].

### 6. Steelman of the opposing view
A traditional theologian reads "kinsman," "father," "redeemer," and "offspring" as **covenantal metaphors**, not literal genealogy: God *adopts* Israel and (in Paul) all humanity as children by grace, and Acts 17:28-29's point is precisely *anti*-idolatry — since we are God's offspring we should *not* think the divine is like gold or stone, i.e. it elevates God above material images rather than making him a literal progenitor of the same kind. The *goel* and marriage language describe God's covenant faithfulness, not a divine clan. Butler's reply: the relentlessly *familial* vocabulary, the shared *devak* verb, and Paul's *genos* are more economically explained if the kinship is real and ancient — the family of God from which humans came and to which they return.
