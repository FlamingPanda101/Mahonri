# John 4:24 — "God Is a Spirit" Rebutted

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
[[John 4.24|John 4:24]] — "God is a Spirit" — disproves the LDS teaching that God the Father has a body of flesh and bone.

## The Rebuttal

### 1. Context: the woman at the well, about *where* to worship
Jesus is answering the Samaritan woman, who is barred from the Jerusalem temple. His point is that God can be worshipped **anywhere, in spirit** — not that God is exclusively immaterial.

> "He's like, 'God's a spirit and he'll be worshiped in spirit. You can pray to God. You can worship God wherever you need to.'" [^1]

### 2. The Greek is qualitative, not possessive
The verse was written in Greek; *pneuma* here is qualitative.

> "God is a spirit is pneuma... which does not have any — it's qualitative. It's not possessive. So what this is essentially saying is God is spirit in the same way that God is love or God is truth." [^2]

"God is spirit" parallels "God is love" / "God is truth" — it characterizes how he is worshipped, not a metaphysical denial of embodiment.

### 3. Latter-day Saints affirm God has a spirit *and* a body
Just as humans are physical beings with a spirit, the Father has a glorified body of flesh and bone *and* a spirit; our spirit is what communicates with God. The verse encourages spiritual worship, it does not disembody the Father [^3].
