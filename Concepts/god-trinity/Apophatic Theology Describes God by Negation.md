# Apophatic Theology — Describing God by Negation

## The Point
Apophatic (or "negative") theology — from the Greek *apophasis*, "denial" — is the practice of describing God by saying what he is *not*: not visible, not mortal, not corporeal, not passible. It is the engine behind classical doctrines of God's incorporeality and impassibility. The author concedes apophatic *language* genuinely appears in scripture, but the *extreme conclusions* (a God who is bodiless and emotionless) are an extension of the logic, not a scriptural requirement — and Latter-day Saints "can still admire" the apophatic impulse to glorify God even while rejecting those conclusions.

## Evidence
1. Definition: apophatic theology "is an attempt to describe God by describing what God is not. For example, God is defined by some Christians as 'not passible' and as 'not corporeal.'"[^1]
2. Scriptural roots: Paul calls God's judgments "unsearchable" (Romans 11:33), his gift "unspeakable" (2 Corinthians 9:15), and God "invisible" (Colossians 1:15), "immortal," and "unapproachable" (1 Timothy 6:16).[^2]
3. The logic: if human things are searchable, speakable, visible, mortal, and approachable, and "God's ways are higher than our ways" (Isaiah 55:8–9), then God "cannot be any of these things" — extended further, one reaches a bodiless, emotionless God.[^3]
4. "But that is only part of the story. The ideas of a passionless and bodiless God began with Christians defending themselves against paganism" — contrasting God with the passion-driven Olympian gods.[^4]
5. The LDS posture: "Even while we may disagree with other Christians about the nature of God as embodied, we can still admire their use of apophatic theology to glorify God."[^5]

## Best Quotes
> Apophatic theology "is an attempt to describe God by describing what God is not." [^1]

> "If the things of this world … are searchable, speakable, visible, mortal, and approachable, and if God's ways are higher than our ways … then so the logic goes God cannot be any of these things." [^3]

## LDS Connection
Apophatic theology is the philosophical pathway by which mainstream Christianity arrived at a bodiless God — a destination Latter-day Saints reject (D&C 130:22). But the author models a generous reading: the *impulse* to magnify God beyond human limits is admirable; it is the *over-extension* into incorporeality and emotionlessness that the Restoration corrects.
