# All the Church Fathers Taught Apotheosis

## The Claim
[[Luke Hansen]] and [[Jonah Barnes]] argue that **apotheosis** (deification — humans truly becoming gods, lowercase-g, beneath the Father) was taught across the church fathers of the first five centuries, so the doctrine modern evangelicals brand a Mormon heresy is in fact ancient catholic Christianity. Drawing on Scott R. Petersen's *Do the Mormons Have a Leg to Stand On?*, they parade quotation after quotation and provocatively "claim them as Mormons."[^1][^2]

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. A century-by-century quote chain (rated: strong as to wording)
- **[[Justin Martyr]]** (d. ~165): "all men are deemed worthy of becoming gods and of having power to become sons of the highest."[^1]
- **[[Irenaeus of Lyons]]** (135–202): "the Word of God ... did become what we are, that he might bring us to be even what he is himself."[^1]
- **[[Theophilus of Antioch]]** (~180): if man keeps the commandments "he should become god."[^1]
- **[[Hippolytus of Rome]]** (170–236): "thou hast become God ... thou hast been deified ... know thyself, i.e. discover God within thyself."[^2]
- **[[Clement of Alexandria]]** (150–215): "the Word of God became man, that thou mayest learn from man how man may become God."[^2]
- **[[Origen]]** (200–254): from Jesus "began the union of the divine with the human nature, in order that the human ... might rise to be divine."[^2]
- **[[Athanasius]]** (4th c.): "he was made man that we might be made God."[^2]
- **[[Gregory of Nazianzus]]**: God became man "in order that I too may be made God so far as he is made man."[^2]
Plus later witnesses [[Thomas Aquinas]] ("[God] might make men gods") and [[C.S. Lewis]] ("every Christian is to become a little Christ").[^2]

### 2. Scriptural anchor (rated: moderate)
The hosts root it in [[John 17.22|John 17:22]] — believers are to be "one" *as* the Father and Son are one — and the New-Testament-hymn / [[Philippians 2.6-7|Philippians 2:6]] "thought it not robbery to be equal with God," reading Paul as *rebutting* the very "that's robbery" objection evangelicals still raise. [[Romans 8.14-17|Romans 8:16-17]] makes believers "joint-heirs with Christ."[^2]

### 3. A historian's offhand appeal (rated: moderate)
Athanasius could invoke deification "as the major premise of his argument for the full divinity of the Redeemer ... and yet offer no support ... because it was by that time common knowledge" — evidence the doctrine was assumed, not fringe.[^2]

## Steelman of the Opposing View
The mainstream and evangelical reply (and the scholarly caution): patristic *theosis* is **participation/adoption**, not the LDS claim that God and humans are the *same species* and that exalted humans become Gods of their own. The fathers held an absolute Creator–creature ontological divide; "made god" means glorified union and moral likeness by grace, not numerical addition to the Godhead. Several quotes are also abbreviated or context-stripped, and [[Dan McClellan]] argues the *later* (4th–5th c.) formula is **diluted** from the more radical 1st-century one, so it cannot serve as a clean Mormon proof text (see [[Patristic Theosis Was Watered Down From the Biblical Version]]). The honest LDS apologetic, made elsewhere in the vault, concedes the ontological gap and claims only the *broad* theosis tradition — see [[Same-Species-as-God as the Core LDS Distinctive]] and [[Christ Became Man That We Might Become God - The Exchange Formula]].
