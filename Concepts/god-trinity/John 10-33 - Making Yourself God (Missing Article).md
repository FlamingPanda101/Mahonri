# John 10:33 — "Making Yourself God" (Missing Article)

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
[[John 10.33|John 10:33]] — the Jews accuse Jesus of "making yourself God" — proves Jesus claimed to be God, and the Jews (and the author of John) understood him as such.

## The Rebuttal

### 1. The Greek lacks the definite article — again
The KJV and most English translations obscure the grammar. In Greek:[^1]
- "Being a man" — *anthropos on* (no article: qualitative — "being human")
- "Makest thyself God" — *theon* (no article: qualitative — "making yourself divine")

The parallelism indicates a qualitative reading: "You, BEING HUMAN (having human qualities), are making yourself DIVINE (claiming divine qualities)." It is NOT "You, being THE human, are making yourself THE God of Israel."

### 2. Jesus's response proves the qualitative reading
Jesus responds by quoting [[Psalm 82.6|Psalm 82:6]]: "Is it not written in your law, I said, Ye are gods?"[^2]

If the accusation were that Jesus claimed to be THE God of Israel, quoting a psalm that says humans are called "gods" is a straw man — completely non-responsive. But if the accusation is that Jesus claimed divine *status* (being a god / having divine qualities), Jesus's response is perfectly targeted: "Scripture calls humans gods, so I'm off the hook — humans can be gods, you've got nothing on me."[^3]

"The argument that Jesus makes is entirely facetious if we understand John 10:33 to be saying Jesus is making himself the very God of Israel."[^4]

### 3. This fits the broader pattern
Across John, Jesus consistently claims divine *agency* and *authority* (bearing the divine name, manifesting God's presence, being one with the Father in purpose) without claiming to BE the God of Israel. The accusations against him are about claiming divine *status* — which is blasphemy — not about claiming identity with Yahweh.

## Best Quote
> "The Jews are accusing Jesus of claiming to be a God, claiming to be divine. Jesus says, 'The scriptures say humans are gods, so I'm off the hook.' If they understood Jesus to be saying 'I'm the God of Israel,' his response would be a complete straw man." [^3]
