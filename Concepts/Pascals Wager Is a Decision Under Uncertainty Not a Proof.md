# Pascals Wager Is a Decision Under Uncertainty Not a Proof

#type/argument

## The Point
[[Blaise Pascal]]'s wager is not an attempt to prove that God exists, nor even to show that God's existence is more probable than not. It is a piece of **decision theory** "lifted from the gambling hall": given the odds and the size of the payoff, belief in God is the rational bet even if the probability that God exists is low.[^1]

## Argument
1. **Bets are evaluated by probability *and* payoff.** Using the lottery analogy ([[Susanna Rinard]]): holding the payoff fixed, a higher chance of winning is better; holding the chance fixed, a larger payoff is better. So a low-probability bet can still be rational when the payoff is large enough — e.g., a one-in-a-hundred shot at a billion dollars for a one-dollar ticket.[^1]
2. **The God payoff is infinite.** "If God exists and you believe, the payoff is an eternity of happiness — an outcome of infinite value."[^2] If God exists and you don't believe, you do worse. So *conditional on God existing*, you are better off believing.
3. **The non-existence outcomes are finite.** If God does not exist, then whatever happens to believer or unbeliever, "the value of each outcome will at least be finite."[^2] An infinite expected payoff therefore dominates any finite loss from believing in vain.
4. **Therefore** rationality "compels us to wager for God," because no finite gain is worth forgoing a potential infinite reward.[^3]

**Steelman of the opposing view:** The expectation calculus is mathematically fragile. (a) *Many gods*: rival religions posit gods who would punish belief in the wrong one, so "belief in God" is not a single well-defined bet. (b) *Involuntariness*: one cannot decide to believe as a matter of policy, so the argument at most motivates *feigned* belief. (c) *The unworthy gambler*: a God worth believing in might not reward self-interested wagering. [[Susanna Rinard]] grants all three as "valid critiques" of this weak version; the video concedes them and retreats to the dominance form (see [[The Dominance Version of Pascals Wager]]). Critics also note that multiplying by "infinity" makes the expected value undefined and lets any nonzero probability swamp the calculation.

## Best Quotes

> "He did not try to prove that God exists. He didn't even try to argue that God's existence is more likely than not. Instead he lifted an approach from the gambling hall and argued that, given the odds and the potential payoffs, belief in God is a really good deal even if the chance that God actually exists is low."[^4]

> "Even if the chance of winning is only one out of a hundred, if the payoff is a billion dollars and the cost of the ticket is say only a dollar, it makes sense to buy a ticket. Pascal claims that belief in God is, by similar reasoning, an infinitely better deal than this."[^1]

> "If God exists and you believe, the payoff is an eternity of happiness, an outcome of infinite value."[^2]
