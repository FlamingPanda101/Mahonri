# NDE - Feelings of Peace and Quiet

## The Point
In the early stages of the NDE, dying persons very commonly report an overwhelming sense of **peace, comfort, and the cessation of all pain** [^1]. The shift is often abrupt: a flash of injury-pain gives way instantly to warmth and ease [^1]. This pleasant calm is one of the most consistent features and recurs even among those whose bodies were grievously injured.

## Evidence
1. A man with a severe head injury and undetectable vital signs felt a "momentary flash of pain," then all pain vanished, replaced by warmth and "the most extreme comfort I have ever experienced," prompting the thought "I must be dead" [^1].
2. A woman resuscitated after a heart attack felt "nothing in the world except peace, comfort, ease — just quietness," and that "all my troubles were gone" [^1].
3. Another man recalled "a nice, great feeling of solitude and peace.... I was at such peace in my mind" [^1].
4. A soldier who "died" of wounds in Vietnam described "a great attitude of relief. There was no pain, and I've never felt so relaxed.... it was all good" [^1].

## Best Quotes
> "I couldn't feel a thing in the world except peace, comfort, ease—just quietness. I felt that all my troubles were gone, and I thought to myself, 'Well how quiet and peaceful, and I don't hurt at all.'" [^1]

## LDS Connection
The pervasive peace reported at death echoes the LDS understanding of the righteous dead entering "a state of rest... a state of peace, where they shall rest from all their troubles and from all care, and sorrow" (Alma 40:12). The immediate release from pain and anxiety fits a paradise of the spirit world rather than oblivion.
