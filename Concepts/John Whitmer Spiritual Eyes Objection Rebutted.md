# John Whitmer "Spiritual Eyes" Objection Rebutted

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
[[John Whitmer]], one of the Eight Witnesses, is quoted (via *History of the Church* vol. 3) as saying the plates "were shown to me by a supernatural power." Critics argue this means the Eight did **not physically handle** the plates and that their "saw and hefted" testimony is therefore unreliable or spiritualized [^1].

## The Rebuttal

### 1. Period usage of "supernatural"
Held against John's and the other seven's statements, and given how "supernatural" was used at the time, "we can comfortably infer that he simply meant there is a divine element to the work" — not that the handling was non-physical. To call something supernatural was to mark it as "beyond human capacity," not as a disclaimer that it never happened in the body [^2]. *Rating: a reasonable linguistic-context argument; "supernatural" in 1830s usage did not carry the modern "merely subjective" connotation.*

### 2. Consistency with the rest of his record
"Other than this, there's no credible reports of any witnesses denying their testimony," and John "couldn't technically be described as denying his testimony" — his lifelong record affirms the witness even after excommunication [^3]. *Rating: supported by the broader Whitmer documentary record.*

### 3. It is the only inconsistent-statement candidate
The episode concedes this is "the only credible source... that implies an inconsistent statement" among the Eight — and argues even it dissolves on inspection [^1]. *Rating: an honest concession that strengthens the credibility of the treatment.*

## Steelman (Opposing View)
A critic argues this is **special pleading**: when a witness uses physical language ("hefted," "handled") it is taken literally, but when one uses "supernatural / spiritual eyes" it is reinterpreted to preserve the physical claim. Combined with Martin Harris's reported "spiritual eyes" language and the [[Empty Box Story Rebutted|empty-box]] tradition, a skeptic sees a pattern suggesting the witness experiences were more visionary than the printed "saw and hefted" formula admits.

## Best Quotes
> "He said that they were shown to me by a supernatural power." (John Whitmer) [^1]

> "The nature of the word supernatural as used in the time and with those people, we can comfortably infer that he simply meant there is a divine element to the work." [^2]
