# The Slave Bible Confirms the OT Was Dangerous to Slaveholders

## The Claim

[[Glenn Scrivener]]: the 19th-century **Slave Bible** (produced for British West Indian colonies, cutting ~90% of the OT and ~50% of the NT, notably the Exodus) proves slaveholders were "terrified of the whole Bible." The full Scripture was a liberating threat; they "did what [[Marcion]] proposed."[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **What was removed.** The Exodus liberation narrative was cut — because the enslaved "might read it and be filled with delusions of grandeur," which "is what the black church was."[^2] *(Strong — the Slave Bible's omissions are documented and revealing about what slaveholders feared.)*

2. **It is a compilation, not an edit (O'Connor's clarification).** O'Connor: people misread the Slave Bible as a censored Bible; it is better understood as "a compilation of biblical quotes" — like an anthology of psalms for grief. So its existence shows curation, not that the *removed* parts are anti-slavery in the way claimed.[^3] *(Fair clarification — the genre matters.)*

3. **The crux: who misread the Exodus?** O'Connor concedes a slaveholder would rationally remove Exodus, but argues the *message* of the OT as a whole is not abolitionist: the freed Hebrews take foreign slaves ([[Leviticus 25.44-46|Lev 25]]). So removing Exodus doesn't establish that the uncut Bible opposes slavery — [[Exodus 21.20-21|Ex 21]] and Lev 25 remain and support it. See [[The Exodus Is Israelite-Specific Not Universal Abolition]].[^4] *(Sharp — the Slave Bible cuts the *liberation* texts but leaves *pro-slavery* texts intact in the full Bible, which complicates Scrivener's "terrified of the whole Bible" framing.)*

4. **Steelman of Scrivener.** The very fact slaveholders had to *mutilate* Scripture, while abolitionists wielded it whole ([[Frederick Douglass]]'s "hammer"), is asymmetric evidence: Scripture's natural trajectory favored liberation, requiring suppression to weaponize for bondage. See [[The Old Testament Was a Hammer for Abolitionists]].[^1] *(Strong — the asymmetry is real and historically telling; the dispute is whether the *whole* Bible's plain sense is liberationist [Scrivener] or mixed-to-pro-slavery [O'Connor].)*

## Best Quotes

> "Those slave owners were terrified of the Bible — terrified of the whole Bible... they did what Marcion proposed."[^1]

> "What did they take out? The Exodus story. Why? Because people might read it and be filled with delusions of grandeur — which is what the black church was."[^2]
