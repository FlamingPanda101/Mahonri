# Informal Witnesses of the Golden Plates

## The Claim
Beyond the 11 official witnesses printed in the Book of Mormon, there are **at least 11 additional "informal" witnesses** — people who encountered the plates by happenstance rather than by being deliberately convened. Because their testimony was unstaged and incidental, the episode argues it strengthens the overall case [^1].

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. Natural informal witnesses (physical contact)
- **[[Josiah Stowell]]** — "the first person who took the plates out of Joseph Smith's hands the morning he brought them in"; later testified in court that he glimpsed the plates as the linen slipped back and gave their dimensions [^2].
- **[[Catherine Smith]]** (Joseph's sister) — hefted the package, judged it heavy "like gold," rippled the edges and heard the metallic "tinkle" [^3].
- **[[William Smith]]** (Joseph's brother) — thumbed the metal and judged them "thin sheets of some kind of metal" [^3].
- **[[Alva Beeman]]** — helped hide the plates from a mob and heard them rustle [^4].
- **[[Lucy Harris]]** and her daughter (Martin Harris's wife and daughter) — handled the box; said the plates were "very heavy" [^4].
- **[[Emma Smith]]** — the plates lay under the bed and on the table wrapped in cloth; she moved and dusted around them and "thumbed the leaves as one does the leaves of a book, and they rustled with metallic sound" [^5].

### 2. Supernatural informal witnesses (divine encounter)
- **[[Mary Whitmer]]** (David Whitmer's mother) — shown the plates by a being she called "Brother Nephi" (the episode argues this was likely [[Angel Moroni|Moroni]], the record-keeper), as "encouragement" for her labor [^6].
- **[[Luke Johnson]]** (original Quorum of the Twelve) — affirmed after leaving the church (per John D. Lee in Howe's *Mormonism Unveiled*) that he saw an angel and the plates [^7].
- **Alvah Beeman Culpepper** — after prayer "thought I saw the angels with the Book of Mormon in their hands" [^8].
- **[[Harrison Burgess]]** — "a glorious personage clothed in white stood before me and exhibited to my view the plates" [^8].

*Rating: the documentary spread is impressive and many of these are genuine historical statements; the "11 additional" count is the episode's, and the informal supernatural reports are late and second-hand in several cases (Johnson via Lee via Howe).*

## Steelman (Opposing View)
The natural informal witnesses establish only that **a heavy, plate-shaped object existed** — fully compatible with [[Metallurgical Fraud Theory Rebutted|crafted plates]] under cloth; almost none of them saw uncovered, engraved gold. The supernatural informal accounts are mostly **uncorroborated, late, or filtered through hostile intermediaries** (the Luke Johnson line runs Lee → Howe), and the Mary Whitmer "Brother Nephi / actually Moroni" reassignment is admittedly a guess ("I forgot who later clarified... I can't tell you where it is").

## Best Quotes
