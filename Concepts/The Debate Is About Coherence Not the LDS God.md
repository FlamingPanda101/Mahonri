# The Debate Is About Coherence Not the LDS God

## The Claim
[[Hayden Carroll]] frames his negative case as an **internal-coherence critique**, not an attack on theism or a defense of Mormonism. He states up front: "I personally believe that the Jesus Christ of the New Testament is fully divine... I believe God truly does will the greatest good of every person. But I am not a creedal Christian and not a trinitarian. My purpose is not contempt but coherence." The question is "whether the attributes creedal Christianity posits about God can actually fit together without contradiction."[^1]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **Internal critique is the rigorous form** — rate: strong methodologically. Hayden grants the affirmative's premises *for the sake of argument* and shows they self-destruct, which is more powerful than a premise-rejecting external attack. "I'm not questioning God's power. I'm not questioning if he exists." *(method)* [^2]
2. **Burden stays on the affirmative** — as the negative, Hayden need only show *one* internal contradiction; he need not offer a rival theodicy. *(debate-logic)* [^2]
3. **Insulates against tu quoque** — because the target is *creedal coherence*, Gavin's repeated pivot to LDS theology ([[Conditional Access Tu Quoque Against the LDS God]]) is, by Hayden's framing, off-resolution: "Mormonism could be totally false and that would mean nothing for the debate resolution." *(scope-defense)* [^3]
4. **Concedes the biblical God** — Hayden distinguishes "the biblical God" (whom he affirms wills all good) from "the trinitarian God" (the specific creedal construct under test), localizing the contradiction to the doctrine of the Trinity. *(distinction)* [^4]

**Steelman of the affirmative:** an internal critique is only as good as its reading of the *opponent's actual* commitments. A classical theist can argue Hayden's pentad ([[The Five Incompatible Claims of Creedal Theism]]) subtly mis-states claim 5 (conflating *antecedent* with *unconditional-toward-each-individual* will) or claim 2 (treating divine freedom as if "refrain from creating X" were always feasible, ignoring Molinist constraints). If so, the alleged contradiction is between Hayden's *reconstruction* and itself, not within creedal theism — and the internal critique fails on accuracy, not logic.

## Best Quotes
> "My purpose in this debate is not contempt, but coherence... whether the attributes creedal Christianity posits about God can actually fit together without contradiction."[^1]

> "I'm not saying God is evil. I'm not attacking believers. I'm not mocking faith. I am saying that under classical creedal trinitarian theology, God knowingly and freely initiates lives that will end in eternal conscious torment."[^5]
