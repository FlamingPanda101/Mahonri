# D&C 132 Internal Details Match Nauvoo July 1843

## The Claim

The "Remnant"/denial movement holds that [[Brigham Young]] forged [[Doctrine and Covenants 132|D&C 132]] in ~1852 and attributed it to [[Joseph Smith]]. [[Don Bradley]] applies the same method he used to vindicate the First Vision — testing whether a document's incidental details fit its *claimed* time and place — and argues Section 132's details lock onto **Nauvoo, July 12, 1843**, the date the record assigns, not 1850s Utah.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. The "don't put your property out of your hands" verse matches Clayton's journal for that day — rate: strong (independent convergence)
D&C 132:57 tells Joseph not to "put his property out of his hands, lest any enemy... destroy him." [[William Clayton]]'s journal for July 12, 1843 records that the revelation was read to [[Emma Smith]], that Joseph and Emma argued, made a tearful agreement (resembling a separation agreement), and that Joseph then instructed Clayton to deed most of his Nauvoo lots and his half-interest in the steamboat *Maid of Iowa* to Emma. The revelation's property language fits the day's documented events.[^2]

### 2. The surviving deed is dated July 12, 1843 — rate: strong (artifact)
There exists an actual deed, **dated July 12, 1843**, in which Joseph deeds Emma exactly the property Clayton's journal says he instructed Clayton to deed. The revelation, the journal, and the deed interlock on a single day.[^3]

### 3. The "First Vision" method, applied to 132 — rate: moderate (parallel reasoning)
Bradley notes that just as Joseph's First Vision accounts contain incidental period-correct details (e.g., the father reaching for spectacles to read family prayers; clearing trees in early spring), a forger working in 1852 Utah would have had to consult exactly what was happening in Nauvoo on July 12, 1843 to plant these matches — and Brigham had no motive to embed property minutiae irrelevant to persuading people to accept polygamy.[^4]

## Steelman of the Opposing View

The convergence is impressive but interpretable. (a) A *genuine* 1843 revelation and a *partly* later-edited text are not mutually exclusive — a denier can grant that an authentic 1843 document underlies the property verses while still alleging that the *polygamy* core (the verses that matter doctrinally) was expanded later; matching the deed does not by itself authenticate the plural-marriage content. (b) The deed and Clayton journal establish that *something* happened between Joseph and Emma on July 12, 1843 over a revelation; they do not independently prove that the surviving Section 132 *is verbatim* that revelation. (c) "Brigham had no motive to plant property details" assumes the forgery, if any, was Brigham's solo work in 1852; a more sophisticated denial posits gradual textual accretion, against which "incidental realism" is weaker evidence. So the detail-matching strongly supports an authentic 1843 revelation event behind Section 132 — defeating a crude "Brigham invented it whole in 1852" — while a determined denier retreats to a harder-to-falsify "edited later" position that the wordprint evidence ([[Doctrine and Covenants 132 Wordprint Analysis]]) must address.
