# Whole United States Arrayed Against the Saints Prophecy

## The Claim
According to [[Heber J. Grant]], [[Joseph Smith]] predicted a day would come when not only a city, county, and state but "the whole United States should be arrayed against" the Latter-day Saints — at which men "laughed at the absurdity." The Saints were indeed driven from city to city, county to county, state to state, and finally out of the United States to settle in the Salt Lake Valley.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The historical persecution is real.** The serial expulsions (Jackson County, broader Missouri, Illinois) and ultimate exodus beyond U.S. borders did occur. (Evidence rating: the *outcome* is solid history.)[^1]
2. **Recorded by a much later church president.** The wording comes via Heber J. Grant (church president 1918–1945), "recorded many years later after the fact." (Evidence rating: weak source proximity.)[^2]
3. **Retrofit risk.** The host flags it directly: "perhaps something like this was said by Joseph, but you could also argue this is just retrofitted as well." See [[Retrofitting a Prophecy After the Fulfillment]]. (Evidence rating: moderate concern.)[^2]
4. **Steelman of the believing view.** Predicting that the *entire* United States would turn against a tiny frontier sect was, in the early 1830s, genuinely counterintuitive — the kind of escalation a self-interested founder would be unlikely to forecast publicly. If the prophecy predates the federal "Mormon War"/Utah Expedition era, its scope is notable. The deflation is that the prediction is impressionistic ("arrayed against") rather than dated and specific.

## Best Quotes
> "The day would come when not only a city, a county, and a state would be arrayed against the Latter-day Saints, but the day would come when the whole United States should be arrayed against them."[^1]

> "Perhaps something like this was said by Joseph, but you could also argue this is just retrofitted as well."[^2]
