# Orrin Porter Rockwell Cut Not Thy Hair Prophecy

## The Claim
[[Joseph Smith]] reportedly prophesied over [[Orrin Porter Rockwell]]: "so long as ye shall remain loyal and true to thy faith, [you] need fear no enemy. Cut not thy hair. No bullet or blade can harm thee." Rockwell served as a bodyguard and lawman through a violent career and was never killed by gunfire — invoking a Samson-like conditional invulnerability.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Rockwell's survival is historical.** A famously dangerous figure, Rockwell died of natural causes despite his profession. The host: "he was his bodyguard and never shot. That's kind of cool." (Evidence rating: outcome real; impressiveness limited.)[^1]
2. **Conditional and Samson-patterned.** The promise is contingent ("so long as ye remain loyal... cut not thy hair"), echoing Judges' Samson narrative, which makes any outcome interpretable and gives it a mythic literary shape. (Evidence rating: weak as evidence; conditional + archetypal.)[^1]
3. **Late, hagiographic transmission.** The blessing circulates in later Rockwell lore; the host offers no contemporaneous source. (Evidence rating: standard late-record caveat — see [[The Late-Reminiscence Problem in Joseph Smith Prophecy Claims]].)[^1]
4. **Steelman of the believing view.** That a man in repeated gunfights lived to old age, having kept his hair long per the blessing, made the prophecy a celebrated faith story. The deflation is survivorship bias (we remember the bodyguard who lived) and the unfalsifiable conditional.
