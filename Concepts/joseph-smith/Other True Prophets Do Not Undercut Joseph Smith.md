# Other True Prophets Do Not Undercut Joseph Smith

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
The objection assumes that if Muhammad were a genuine prophet, that would somehow threaten Joseph Smith — that there can be only one. Peterson rejects the premise outright: the existence of other prophets, even other *inspired* ones, does nothing to undercut Joseph's story.[^1]

## The Rebuttal
1. **Prophetic exclusivity is not an LDS commitment.** "First of all, it doesn't bother me that there are other prophets out there. Why should it?"[^1] LDS theology already affirms a long succession of prophets and is open to God speaking to many peoples — so a true Muhammad fits the worldview rather than refuting it. *(Strong as a statement of the LDS framework.)*
2. **Prophets are fallible, so partial error in another prophet is no scandal.** Peterson holds that one "can see grounds for believing that [Muhammad] was inspired" *and* "grounds for believing that he got some things wrong" — "but that is possible for a prophet. Prophets are not infallible."[^2] This is the same fallibilist principle the Church applies to its own prophets. See [[Prophets Are Fallible Today]], [[God Works Through Fallible Prophets]]. *(Strong — internally consistent with LDS teaching on prophetic fallibility.)*
3. **Doctrinal divergence is the real test, not the mere existence of a rival prophet.** Peterson locates the genuine tension not in Muhammad existing, but in where his message conflicts on "the all-important issue of the deity of Christ," which Islam denies.[^3] So the objection misidentifies the threat: it is doctrinal content, not prophetic plurality, that matters. See [[Islam Denies the Deity and Divine Sonship of Christ]].
4. **Steelman.** A critic could argue that a religion claiming a unique restoration through Joseph cannot consistently bless a rival revelation that *contradicts* it on the nature of God. Peterson concedes the contradiction on Christ's deity is real and substantive — but distinguishes "inspired in part" from "authoritative in full." Affirming that Muhammad received some divine light is not endorsing the Quran as binding scripture; it is the same graded model the [[Early LDS Leaders Praised Muhammad as Inspired|1978 First Presidency statement]] applies to Confucius and Plato.

## Best Quotes
> "I don't think it undercuts the story of Joseph Smith at all... it doesn't bother me that there are other prophets out there. Why should it?" — [[Daniel C. Peterson|Daniel Peterson]] [^1]

> "I can see grounds for believing that he was inspired. I can see grounds for believing that he got some things wrong. But that is possible for a prophet. Prophets are not infallible." [^2]
