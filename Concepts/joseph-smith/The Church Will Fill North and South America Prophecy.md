# The Church Will Fill North and South America Prophecy

## The Claim
At an 1834 elders' meeting, [[Joseph Smith]] is reported (by [[Wilford Woodruff]]) to have said the assembled priesthood understood the church's destiny no better than "a babe upon its mother's lap," and that "this church will fill North and South America. It will fill the world." Members read the church's global spread as fulfillment.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The church did spread globally.** The geographic claim has substantially come true — the church is established across the Americas and worldwide. (Evidence rating: the *outcome* is real; impressiveness is the issue.)[^1]
2. **Late recording.** The host believes "this quote came from an 1834 elders meeting and this wasn't recorded till many years later... here from Wilford Woodruff." This is the recurring [[The Late-Reminiscence Problem in Joseph Smith Prophecy Claims]]. (Evidence rating: weakens the claim significantly.)[^2]
3. **The skeptic's deflation: religions grow.** "Skeptics might think, well yeah, religions grow. There's many religions that have spread across the world. So it's not that remarkable a prophecy." Predicting growth for a movement you are founding is low-specificity and partly self-fulfilling. (Evidence rating: strong deflation.)[^3]
4. **The believing counterweight.** The host answers that "when you think about all the different religions that have started and failed... this one is quite remarkable" given the long odds against any 1834 sect surviving and globalizing. (Evidence rating: moderate — survivorship is notable but not supernatural.)[^3]
5. **The Wentworth Letter parallel.** The host links the related published declaration that "no unhallowed hand can stop the work from progressing... until it has penetrated every continent." He treats the ongoing missionary effort as continuing fulfillment, which makes it perpetually unfalsifiable. (Evidence rating: moderate; the open-ended framing limits evidential force.)[^4]

## Best Quotes
> "This church will fill North and South America. It will fill the world."[^1]

> "Skeptics might think, well yeah, religions grow. There's many religions that have spread across the world. So it's not that remarkable a prophecy."[^3]

> "No unhallowed hand can stop the work from progressing... until it has penetrated every continent, visited every climb, swept every country."[^4]
