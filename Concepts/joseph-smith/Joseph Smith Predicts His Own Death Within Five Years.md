# Joseph Smith Predicts His Own Death Within Five Years

## The Claim
Per [[Lucy Mack Smith]]'s history (c. 1838), [[Joseph Smith]] told the brethren, "I shall see you again, let what will happen, for I have a promise of 5 years, and they cannot kill me until that time is expired." Lucy later wrote that the promise — given in Missouri, that "in 5 years Joseph should have power over all his enemies" — was fulfilled. Joseph was killed in 1844, roughly five years after 1838–39.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The timing fits.** From 1838–39 to the 1844 martyrdom is about five years, matching the "promise of 5 years." The host: "if he really did give this prophecy in 1838... that's kind of a cool prophecy and cool timing." (Evidence rating: notable *if* genuinely given in 1838.)[^1]
2. **Corroborated by a second witness.** [[Wilford Woodruff]]'s journal records Lyman Wight saying Joseph told him in Liberty Jail (1839) "he would not live to see 40 years," to be revealed only after Joseph's death (he died at 38). The host: "this does sort of corroborate what Lucy Mack Smith said." See [[Multiple Attestation - Corroborating Apocryphal Sources]]. (Evidence rating: moderate — two independent-ish witnesses, both with caveats.)[^2]
3. **The reveal-after-death loophole.** The host raises the obvious objection to the Lyman Wight version: because Wight "didn't talk about it until Joseph was dead," he could have constructed the claim post hoc. (Evidence rating: weakens that strand.)[^2]
4. **Late maternal source for the main version.** Lucy's account is a reminiscence; the "power over all his enemies" framing reads as consoling hindsight. (Evidence rating: standard late-record caveat — see [[The Late-Reminiscence Problem in Joseph Smith Prophecy Claims]].)[^1]
5. **Steelman of the skeptic.** A persecuted leader under constant death threats might frequently speak of his limited time; with many such statements, one matching the eventual date is expected. The believing strength is two converging witnesses to a *five-year/under-40* window; the deflation is post-hoc selection and reveal-after-death testimony.

## Best Quotes
> "I have a promise of 5 years, and they cannot kill me until that time is expired."[^1]

> "Joseph told him while in Liberty jail in 1839 he would not live to see 40 years, but he was not to reveal it till the prophet was dead." — Lyman Wight, per Wilford Woodruff[^2]
