# Abraham's First Vision Pattern

## The Claim
[[Abraham 1.15-19|Abraham 1]] contains a theophany that follows the *exact same pattern* as Joseph Smith's First Vision (and Moses 1): a man seeking God, an adversary holding him bound and trying to destroy him, then the Lord descending, addressing him *by name*, calling him "my son," and calling him as a prophet. [[Don Bradley]] labels this "Abraham's first vision" — the same theophanic template across dispensations.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The structural parallel is laid out point by point (rated: moderate, typological).** "A man is seeking God... and there's an adversary who has held him bound and is trying to destroy him. And then the Lord descends, and addresses him by name, calls him my son, and calls him as a prophet."[^1] Joseph Smith likewise was bound by an adversarial power before the light appeared, and in his earliest First Vision account God says "Joseph, my son" — the same address God gives Abraham ("Abraham, my son").[^2] *Steelman:* a skeptic would say the parallel is *manufactured*, not discovered — if Joseph Smith authored both texts, shared structure is expected and is evidence of common authorship, not of an authentic ancient pattern. The argument only carries apologetic weight if Joseph's prophetic role is granted.

2. **Seeking precedes theophany in every case (rated: moderate).** Joseph was acting on James 1:5 ("If any of you lack wisdom, let him ask of God") — "Joseph's not just taking a walk in the woods"; Moses "goes to the mountain to see Him"; Nephi "seeks Him." All are seekers who are then met.[^3] See [[Seeking God Versus Arbitrary Election]].

3. **God introduces Himself and reveals the covenant and sonship (rated: moderate).** God "tells him His name's Jehovah, and He addresses Abraham by name. He's, in effect, He's introducing Himself," then reveals a covenant and that Abraham is His son — paralleling Moses 1, where the Lord reveals to Moses that he is His son.[^4] The earthly father tries to kill the son; the Heavenly Father rescues and adopts him.

## Best Quotes

> "A man is seeking God... And there's an adversary who has held him bound and is trying to destroy him. And then the Lord descends, and addresses him by name, calls him my son, and calls him as a prophet. Have you heard this story anywhere before? ... This is Abraham's first vision."[^1]

> "When the Lord addresses Joseph Smith... in his earliest account of the First Vision, [it] is 'Joseph, my son.' He says the same thing to Abraham. ... He calls him 'Abraham, my son.'"[^2]

> "His own dad is trying, in Abraham's case, [to] sacrifice him to some fake god, and He's saying, no, no, no, no. I'm God, and I'm the one who's saving you."[^2]
