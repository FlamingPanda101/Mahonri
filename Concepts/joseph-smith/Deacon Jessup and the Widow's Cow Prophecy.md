# Deacon Jessup and the Widow's Cow Prophecy

## The Claim
According to [[Lucy Mack Smith]]'s manuscript history, this is [[Joseph Smith]]'s "first supposed prophecy," given to his family in 1823. The young Joseph predicted that the outwardly pious Deacon Jessup, despite his wealth, "would not scruple to take the last cow from the poor widow and orphans in order to secure the debt." Lucy records that "one year had scarcely expired when we saw Joseph's prophecy literally fulfilled."[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **A character-reading, not a supernatural forecast.** The "prophecy" is essentially a confident prediction about a known man's behavior under hypothetical pressure — a shrewd judgment of character rather than disclosure of a future fact. (Evidence rating: weak as supernatural prophecy.)[^1]
2. **Sole-source and late.** It rests entirely on Lucy Mack Smith's manuscript, "obviously being a later recollection." There is no independent corroboration. (Evidence rating: weak; see [[The Late-Reminiscence Problem in Joseph Smith Prophecy Claims]].)[^2]
3. **The host's own agnosticism.** Unusually, the host declines to defend it: "I don't really know enough to validate if it was fulfilled or not." He neither endorses nor dismisses it. (Evidence rating: the proponent withholds judgment, itself telling.)[^2]
4. **Steelman of the believing view.** A teenager accurately forecasting a respected community figure's predatory financial conduct could be read as early spiritual discernment. But the prediction's content (a greedy man acts greedily) is mundane enough that fulfillment establishes little, and the only witness is the prophet's mother.

## Best Quotes
> "I tell you that Deacon Jessup, religious as he is, would not scruple to take the last cow from the poor widow and orphans in order to secure the debt."[^1]

> "The time this seemed impossible to us. Yet one year had scarcely expired when we saw Joseph's prophecy literally fulfilled."[^1]

> "I don't really know enough to validate if it was fulfilled or not, but that's according to Lucy."[^2]
