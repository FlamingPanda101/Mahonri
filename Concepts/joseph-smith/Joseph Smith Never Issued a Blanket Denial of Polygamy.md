# Joseph Smith Never Issued a Blanket Denial of Polygamy

#type/rebuttal

## The Claim
Polygamy deniers' strongest move is that Joseph Smith *publicly denied* practicing polygamy, so to affirm it makes Joseph a liar. [[Brian Hales]] (who catalogued every alleged denial in a 2018 *Journal of Mormon History* article) and [[Jacob Hansen]] respond that Joseph *never gave a blanket denial*: the handful of statements are few and qualified, and what Joseph actually condemned was **spiritual wifery** and **unauthorized** plural marriage — not the authorized eternal plural marriage he was quietly practicing.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **The alleged denials are surprisingly few — too few for an article.** Hales: when he "tallied all of Joseph Smith's so-called denials, there were so few I didn't have enough for a full article," so he added Emma and Hyrum. Hansen independently found "six or seven statements" total and was "surprised" how weak they were ("where's the smoking gun?"). The denier's case rests on a thin, much-hyped handful.[^2] *(Strong — both independently reach the same finding from the primary record.)*
2. **What Joseph condemned was *spiritual wifery* and *unauthorized* polygamy.** Joseph denounced John C. Bennett's "spiritual wife" system and Hyram Brown's *unauthorized* plural marriages — "you can't freelance plural marriages." (When W.W. Phelps's companion performed unauthorized sealings, Brigham excommunicated him the next day, then rebaptized him.) These are condemnations of *abuse*, which deniers illegitimately "flip over to mean all polygamy." Joseph never condemned Abraham or Jacob, never called plural marriage as such sinful.[^3] *(Strong — distinguishes the target of Joseph's statements from the practice itself.)*
3. **The specific "denials" all dissolve on inspection.** Hansen walks through them: the 1835 D&C and Book of Mormon make monogamy the *norm* (but [[Jacob 2.27-30|Jacob 2]] allows commanded exceptions); the 1842 Relief Society letter condemns going outside monogamy "unless it be by a message... by actual revelation and commandment" (the original draft contains that very carve-out, in the Joseph Smith Papers); the Willard Richards 1843 entry says "*on this law* a man shall have but one wife" — "*on this law*" implying another; the "I can only find one [wife]" quip is a deflection, not a denial. None is a "full-throated denial."[^4] *(Strong — point-by-point neutralization of the proof-texts.)*
4. **Hyrum and Emma also never gave a clean blanket denial.** Even adding [[Hyrum Smith]] (a couple of "problematic" statements) and [[Emma Smith]], Hales found no unqualified blanket denial from Joseph; deniers who "imply that he did" are "going beyond the evidence."[^5] *(Strong — closes the gap deniers try to fill with family statements.)*

**Steelman of the denialist view.** The denier replies that this is special pleading: when Joseph said the kinds of things he said (publicly disclaiming "polygamy," touting that he had only one wife while accusing enemies of slander), an ordinary listener would hear a denial of the practice, and parsing each statement as "only condemning *unauthorized* polygamy" is a post-hoc rescue that lets *any* denial be reinterpreted. If Joseph was practicing eternal plural marriage, the denier says, then these statements were at best deeply misleading and at worst lies — and "he only meant the unauthorized kind" is exactly what a deceiver would later claim. The LDS rejoinder (developed in [[The Confidentiality Model Best Fits the Polygamy Evidence]]) is that the statements were *carefully worded* precisely because Joseph was keeping an authorized practice confidential under mortal threat, and that even granting Joseph (or Hyrum) sometimes spoke misleadingly, Hales argues such concealment was *justified* (lives were at stake), with scriptural precedent (Abraham and Sarah before Pharaoh) — so the existence of guarded statements is *predicted by*, not contrary to, the practice.

## LDS Relevance
Removes the denier's single best weapon — "Joseph denied it, so you're calling him a liar." Hales and Hansen show there is no blanket denial to weaponize; the real statements condemn abuses (spiritual wifery, unauthorized sealings) and fit a man guarding a confidential, authorized practice. This clears the ground for the affirmative documentary/eyewitness case in [[Polygamy Denialism]] and the [[The Eyewitness Asymmetry Refutes the Polygamy Conspiracy Theory|eyewitness asymmetry]].

## Best Quotes
> "Joseph Smith never denied what he was doing... He denied spiritual wife, or he condemned it. He denied unauthorized polygamy. You can't freelance plural marriages." — [[Brian Hales]] [^3]

> "When I tallied all of Joseph Smith's so-called denials, there were so few I didn't have enough for a full article. And so I added in Emma, added in Hyrum, and... Joseph never gave a blanket statement. So if people imply that he did, they're going beyond the evidence." [^2]

> "On this law a man shall have but one wife — which I'm always like, 'on this law,' so that implies there's another law." — [[Jacob Hansen]] [^4]
