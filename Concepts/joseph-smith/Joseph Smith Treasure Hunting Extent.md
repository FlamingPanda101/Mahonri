# Joseph Smith Treasure Hunting Extent

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Joseph Smith]] did participate in treasure hunting, but the historically defensible extent is modest and uncertain — roughly "a dozen or so" digs at most — and most sensational portrayals of him as a prolific, professional money-digger are overstated. The serious scholarly question is not *whether* he hunted treasure (he did and admitted it) but *how much*, and on that the evidence is thin.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Joseph's own admission (strong, primary).** In his canonized Joseph Smith History he records the 1825 [[Josiah Stowell]] expedition to find a Spanish silver mine in Harmony, Pennsylvania.[^2] In the 1838 *Elders' Journal* Q&A, asked "Was not Joseph Smith a money digger?" he answered yes, while noting it paid poorly and lasted a short time.[^3] *Evidence rating: high — first-person, published acknowledgment.*

2. **Multiply-attested across the bias line (strong).** Treasure seeking is corroborated by both friendly and hostile sources, in both the Harmony/Susquehanna area and the Palmyra–Manchester area, including believing witness [[Martin Harris]].[^4] *Evidence rating: high — independent corroboration.*

3. **[[Dan Vogel]]'s 1994 catalog (moderate).** Vogel's *Dialogue* article lists ~18 alleged dig locations; Mark says he has found a few more.[^5] But the list has "no real source criticism," and "a lot of these treasure digs in this list of 18 probably never happened."[^6] *Evidence rating: moderate — useful inventory, weak on reliability of individual accounts.*

4. **Steelman of the maximalist critic.** A critic could argue Joseph systematically *downplayed* his involvement in 1838 for reputational reasons, and that the sheer number of independent neighbor reports implies heavy, ongoing activity. Mark grants Joseph "is downplaying it a little bit," so the admission is not fully candid; the honest answer to extent is "I don't know."[^7]

5. **The lacrosse analogy (interpretive).** Mark frames treasure hunting's cultural prevalence as "like lacrosse" — real, around you, occasionally encountered, but not as ubiquitous as basketball or soccer.[^8] This calibrates "common" against critics who imply it was universal *and* against apologists who might imply it was vanishingly rare.

## Best Quotes

> "In serious scholarly Mormon history, that's not a huge issue whether or not he hunted for treasure ... there is kind of a lively debate over the years and decades of how much he was involved in treasure hunting."[^1]

> "He probably was involved in I don't know, a dozen or so. I mean, who knows?"[^7]

> "A lot of these treasure digs in this list of 18 are probably never happened. There may be some that happened but nobody ever ... it's unclear."[^6]
