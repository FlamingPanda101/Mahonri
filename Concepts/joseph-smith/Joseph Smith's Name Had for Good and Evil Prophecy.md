# Joseph Smith's Name Had for Good and Evil Prophecy

## The Claim
[[Joseph Smith]] recorded that in 1823, when he was 17, the [[Angel Moroni]] told him his "name should be had for good and evil among all nations, kindreds, and tongues." The prophecy is recorded in Joseph's 1838 history. Whether or not one accepts the angelic visitation, the prophecy stands as Joseph's own 1838 declaration, and it has demonstrably come true — Joseph is spoken of worldwide as both righteous prophet and charlatan.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The fulfillment is genuinely undisputed.** The host calls it the rare item that holds "whether or not you believe Joseph Smith is a fraud, a false prophet or a prophet" — his name is in fact known across the world for both good and evil. (Evidence rating: the *fulfillment* is strong and uncontested; its *predictive* impressiveness is the contested part.)[^1]
2. **It is a bold claim if Joseph were not a prophet.** The host notes it is "quite bold... almost a bit narcissistic to think that his name would be spread across the whole world good and evil spoken about him." Boldness raises the prior cost of being wrong. (Evidence rating: rhetorical, moderate.)[^2]
3. **The skeptic's deflation.** The host immediately steelmans the critic: "it's because he was doing some dodgy things at the time, so he knew people would say evil about him." A man already attracting controversy can reasonably predict mixed reputation; and any successful religious founder's name spreads for good and evil, so the prediction is low-specificity. (Evidence rating: strong deflation — the outcome is near-generic for any notable founder.)[^2]
4. **Dating caveat.** The prophecy is securely Joseph's own (1838 history) even if the 1823 angelic origin is doubted, so this item is less exposed to [[The Late-Reminiscence Problem in Joseph Smith Prophecy Claims]] than most — but the 1838 record still postdates the start of the controversy that would generate the "evil" half. (Evidence rating: moderate.)[^1]
