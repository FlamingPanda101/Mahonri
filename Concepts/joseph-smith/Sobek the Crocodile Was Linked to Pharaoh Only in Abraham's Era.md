# Sobek the Crocodile Was Linked to Pharaoh Only in Abraham's Era

## The Claim
In Facsimile 1 Joseph Smith identifies the **crocodile** as representing **Pharaoh**. Critics call this an obvious miss (a crocodile is just a crocodile, or water). [[Kerry Muhlestein]] replies that the crocodile god **Sobek** had a strong association with Pharaoh **specifically during Abraham's lifetime** (the Middle Kingdom) — a connection that faded later. So a reading that looks wrong by general Egyptology is right *for the right period*, the same "right thing, right era" pattern as Olishem and Shinehah.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. The Sobek–Pharaoh link is era-bound (rated: moderate)
"During Abraham's lifetime, there was a strong connection between Sobek, who is the crocodile god, and Pharaoh ... Sobek could represent Pharaoh. It was an aspect of Pharaoh."[^1] In the Middle Kingdom, as pharaohs colonized and reclaimed the marshy Faiyum, "they become associated with that area and the crocodiles there" — there is a Middle-Kingdom Sobek temple tied to Pharaoh's claim on the region, "which is Abraham's lifetime."[^1]

### 2. Muhlestein excavates the relevant region (rated: contextual authority)
Muhlestein grounds the claim in his own fieldwork in the Faiyum, where an ancient inland sea dried up — lending firsthand Egyptological weight to the dating of the Sobek–Pharaoh association.[^1]

### 3. The "B-17 / rapper" analogy (rated: illustrative)
Cardon Ellis illustrates the logic of era-bound symbolism: a visual symbol (a bomber-tail logo on the Beastie Boys' cover, later copied by Eminem) can be tightly associated with one figure in one era and lost on a later audience. Symbols "fall in and out of association" — so mocking the Sobek identification assumes a static iconography Egypt never had.[^1]

## Steelman of the Opposing View
A critic responds: (a) Sobek's associations are multivalent across all periods (water, fertility, the Nile, royal power generally), so "linked to Pharaoh in Abraham's day" risks retrofitting whichever attested association happens to match Joseph's caption; (b) the facsimile is a standard funerary lion-couch scene whose figures have conventional Egyptological readings, and choosing the Pharaoh-valence is post-hoc; and (c) Muhlestein himself frames facsimile work as *interpreting a drawing* with multiple valid meanings (see [[Translating a Text Versus Interpreting a Drawing Are Different Claims]]), which cuts both ways — if many readings are licit, a "hit" is less improbable. The LDS reply concedes facsimile readings are softer evidence than the textual bullseyes, and rests the strong case on the cumulative pattern.

## Best Quotes
> "During Abraham's lifetime, there was a strong connection between Sobek, who is the crocodile god, and Pharaoh ... he gains an association with Sobek during that time period which is Abraham's lifetime." [^1]

> "Different symbols just like the B-17 tail can fall in and out of association ... when Abraham was around, Sobek and the crocodile, that's associated with Pharaoh. Years later, not so much." [^1]
