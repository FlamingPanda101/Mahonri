# Three Degrees of Glory in the Bible

## The Point
[[D. Jill Kirby]]'s "Three Degrees of Glory in the Bible?" sidebar addresses a common LDS proof-text move. Some ancient Christians (notably Paul) believed in **multiple levels of heaven** and "degrees of glory" — but, Kirby cautions, "there is no evidence that they understood heavenly degrees of glory in the same way it was revealed to Joseph Smith." The LDS three-kingdom schema (celestial / terrestrial / telestial) comes from modern revelation ([[D&C 76]]; [[D&C 88]]), and the Joseph Smith Translation of 1 Corinthians 15:40 *adapts* Paul's original bodily-resurrection argument to that revelation.

## Evidence
1. **Paul's multiple heavens.** "Some ancient Christians, such as the Apostle Paul, believed in multiple levels of heaven and degrees of glory (see, for instance, 2 Corinthians 3:18; 12:2)" [^1].
2. **The caveat.** "There is no evidence that they understood heavenly degrees of glory in the same way it was revealed to Joseph Smith" [^1].
3. **Paul's original point was bodily resurrection.** In 1 Corinthians 15:40 Paul contrasted "heavenly bodies" (*corpora caelestia* — sun, moon, stars) with "earthly bodies" (*corpora terrestria*) to convince the Corinthians of the *reality of bodily resurrection*, rendered in the KJV as "celestial bodies, and bodies terrestrial" [^1].
4. **The JST adaptation.** Joseph Smith altered the verse to read: "Also celestial bodies, and bodies terrestrial, and bodies telestial; but the glory of the celestial, one; and the terrestrial, another; and the telestial, another" — adapting Paul's argument "to reinforce the modern revelation about heavenly degrees of glory" [^1].
5. **McConkie's both/and.** [[Bruce R. McConkie]] recommended approaching differences between the original biblical text and the JST "with the understanding that 'both of them are true' from a doctrinal or theological perspective" [^1].

## Best Quotes
> "Some ancient Christians, such as the Apostle Paul, believed in multiple levels of heaven and degrees of glory … but there is no evidence that they understood heavenly degrees of glory in the same way it was revealed to Joseph Smith." [^1]

> "The JST adapted Paul's original argument for a bodily resurrection to reinforce the modern revelation about heavenly degrees of glory." [^1]

## LDS Connection
A model of *honest* LDS apologetics from a Maxwell Institute volume: it affirms a real biblical seed (Paul's multiple heavens; cf. Ephrem's tiered paradise in [[Syriac Poetry Pictures Heaven as a Garden with Degrees of Glory]]) while *declining to overclaim* that the ancients held the specific three-kingdom doctrine. It also clarifies the nature of the JST — not a recovery of Paul's autograph but an inspired *adaptation* of the text toward modern revelation, with McConkie's "both are true" framing. Useful against critics who treat the JST of 1 Cor 15:40 as a botched translation.
