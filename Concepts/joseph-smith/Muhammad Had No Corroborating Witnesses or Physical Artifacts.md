# Muhammad Had No Corroborating Witnesses or Physical Artifacts

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
The "Joseph copied Muhammad" objection treats the two prophets' founding experiences as equivalent. But on the evidential dimensions that matter most for verification — witnesses and tangible objects — the two cases are sharply asymmetrical, and the asymmetry favors Joseph Smith.

## The Rebuttal
1. **Muhammad never shared a revelatory experience with anyone else.** Peterson: "Muhammad never has an experience that he shares with anybody else... If you wanted to say that it's solely Muhammad, it is. He's never a partner in vision. No one else ever sees anything."[^1] *(Strong on the Islamic side — the revelations were private; Muhammad is the sole experiencer.)*
2. **Joseph Smith repeatedly had visions in company.** Only the First Vision was solitary; afterward Joseph "almost never" received revelation alone — he was "always with Sidney Rigdon or Oliver Cowdery or even other people," and "sometimes there are multiple people who see these things with him."[^2] *(Moderate-to-strong — the LDS witness tradition, e.g. the Three and Eight Witnesses, is documentary; the precise claim "almost never alone" is Peterson's generalization.)* See [[Oliver Cowdery]], [[Sydney Rigdon]].
3. **Joseph's revelations involved physical objects; Muhammad's never did.** "There are also physical objects associated with them. The golden plates, the Urim and Thummim, the breastplate... Nothing like that ever appears in the story of Muhammad."[^3] *(Strong — Islamic tradition records no comparable physical relics produced by revelation; the brocaded cloth Gabriel showed Muhammad was a vision, not an artifact left behind.)*
4. **Steelman.** A skeptic will note that (a) the existence of witnesses is not the same as the witnesses being *reliable*, and that the Book of Mormon witnesses' testimonies are themselves contested; and (b) physical objects that no longer exist (the plates were returned) cannot be independently examined. Peterson's point survives this: even granting the skeptic's discount, the *structure* of the two cases differs — Joseph's claims are intrinsically falsifiable-in-principle (multiple named witnesses, handleable objects), whereas Muhammad's are irreducibly private. The comparison "they did the same thing" ignores that difference.
