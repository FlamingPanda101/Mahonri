# Moses 4 and Abraham 3 Are Two Different Premortal Scenes

## The Claim
[[Jonah Barnes]] argues that Latter-day Saints have **conflated two distinct premortal scenes**: [[Moses 4]] (the council in heaven that selects the **redeemer**) and [[Abraham 3.22-23|Abraham 3:22-27]] (which Barnes says selects the **first man / Michael / Adam**). The conflation was encouraged by the modern chapter heading of Abraham 3 — which speaks of "the choosing of a redeemer" — even though, Barnes notes, **the word "redeem" never appears anywhere in Abraham 3**. Distinguishing the two is what lets Joseph "check the box" of the apocryphal envy-of-Adam motif (see [[Lucifer Fell Out of Jealousy of Adam in the Apocrypha]]).[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. Moses 4 is explicitly about the redeemer — rate: strong (textual)
[[Moses 4]] has Satan say "I will be thy son, and I will redeem all mankind… give me thine honor," and the Beloved Son answer "Father, thy will be done." The word *redeem* and the salvific contest are right there. This is the canonical council-in-heaven that LDS art and lessons depict. Barnes does not dispute it.[^1]

### 2. Abraham 3 never uses "redeem" — rate: moderate (argument from silence + chapter-heading critique)
Barnes' pivot: the *chapter heading* of Abraham 3 (written by later editors — he names the genre of Bruce R. McConkie-era summaries) says it concerns "the choosing of a redeemer," but the *text* of Abraham 3 "doesn't say the word redeem anywhere in this chapter." He argues the heading-writer was "influenced by Moses 4" and read Moses-4 content into a different Abraham-3 scene. "Because of that, we've gotten it wrong for 200 years."[^2] *Strength: the lexical observation (no "redeem" in Abraham 3) is checkable and correct; the inference that it must therefore be a wholly different event is interpretive.*

### 3. The sequence differs — "I will send the first" — rate: moderate (internal detail)
In Moses 4 the *first* to volunteer is **Lucifer** (then Jehovah). In [[Abraham 3.25|Abraham 3:27]] the Lord says "whom shall I send?" one answers "here am I, send me," another answers "here am I, send me," and "the Lord said, I will send the first" — and the first is *accepted*. Barnes argues this cannot be the same scene as Moses 4 (where the first volunteer, Lucifer, is *rejected*), so Abraham 3 must be a different selection. See the payload in [[Abraham 3 Is the Selection of Adam Not the Redeemer]].[^2]

## Steelman of the Opposing View
The traditional single-scene reading is defensible and arguably stronger. (a) **The two passages share signature language.** Both have a divine "whom shall I send?" / "here am I, send me" call-and-response (the Isaiah 6 idiom), and Latter-day Saint tradition has long, reasonably, taken them as two angles on *one* premortal council — not two separate councils. The burden is on Barnes to show they are different events, and "the word redeem is absent" does not establish it (Abraham 3 is terse and thematic, and could simply *presuppose* the redeemer context Moses 4 supplies). (b) **"I will send the first" need not invert Moses 4.** In Abraham the "first" who is sent is the one who says "thy will be done"-type submission in the parallel Moses account (Jehovah), so "send the first" can be read as choosing the *faithful* volunteer, not a different office. (c) **Chapter headings are not scripture, but neither is Barnes's re-reading.** Faulting the heading cuts both ways: the absence of an authoritative interpretation means Barnes's "selection of the first man" is *his* interpretation, presented (honestly) as a personal discovery — "I had been misreading it for years" — not as the Church's exegesis. The measured position: it is *legitimate and interesting* to ask whether Abraham 3 and Moses 4 are the same scene, and the missing-"redeem" datum is a real prompt; but the case is a novel harmonization, not a demonstrated result, and the older single-council reading remains coherent.

## Best Quotes
> "We always think that this is the selection of a redeemer because the chapter heading says it is. But if you read it carefully… in Moses chapter 4 the first person to volunteer is Lucifer; the second is Jehovah. In Abraham's version, the first person to volunteer gets accepted." [^2]
