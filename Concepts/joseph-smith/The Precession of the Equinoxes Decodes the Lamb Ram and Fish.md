# The Precession of the Equinoxes Decodes the Lamb Ram and Fish

#type/claim

## The Claim

[[Kamron Coleman]] argues that the **precession of the equinoxes** — the slow drift of the sun's springtime position backward through the zodiac — encodes salvation history as a sequence of "ages," each with a sacrificial animal that the god of the age dies as and is resurrected beyond. **Taurus** (the bull, ~4320–2160 BC) → **Aries** (the ram/lamb, 2160 BC–0) → **Pisces** (the fish, 0 AD onward). [[Genesis 22|Abraham and Isaac]] is the Taurus→Aries hinge; Jesus is the **SUN of righteousness** dying as the lamb of Aries and rising as the fish of Pisces. We are now crossing into Aquarius.

## Supporting Evidence (steelman)

### 1. The age sets the sacrifice (rating: internally systematic; speculative)
"All of the biblical wisdom is pertaining to the zodiacal sign that the sun is in." The Temple of Solomon "was built in the age of Aries," and Israel "was particularly known for its lamb/ram symbols because it got the astronomy right in the age of Aries." To *end* an age, "the god of that age is killed as the sacrifice to end all sacrifices, and then resurrected as the son of God in the next age" — lamb (Aries) killed, resurrected as fish (Pisces).[^1] Builds on [[The Bull of Jacob Shows Yahweh Was Depicted as a Bull]].

### 2. Abraham and Isaac as the Taurus→Aries transition (rating: the signature reading)
The El council "was the representation of God in the age of Taurus" — all the figures wear bull horns — "and typical of that age was the sacrifice of the firstborn sons in similitude of the bull." [[Genesis 22|Abraham goes to sacrifice Isaac]] ("his bull") and is stopped by **a ram in the thicket**: "The age of Taurus is over. We are now in the age of Aries. You don't have to sacrifice your son, the bull... lamb is now going to be offered."[^2] The golden calf ([[Exodus 32|Exodus 32]]) is read as anticipating the *next* bovine sign, given "the wrong time." See [[Golden Calf and Bronze Serpent as Idolatry Tests]].

### 3. The SUN of righteousness and the fishermen (rating: a pun-driven motif)
[[Malachi 4.5-6|Malachi 4:2]]'s "Sun of righteousness... with healing in his wings" is read as **S-U-N = Son** moving out of Aries into Pisces; the world, knowing the dying god must be an Israelite (the Aries nation), looks to Israel for "the Son of God." Jesus then "goes immediately and finds his disciples from fishermen... so that they can catch fish — the Pisces fish — and start them on the covenant path."[^3] See [[Astronomy Is the Lost Universal Language of Scripture]].

### 4. The cycle length fits the record (rating: weak; the supporting datum)
Each age is ~2,160 years; the biblical record is "long enough to have stories relating to the age of Taurus... but most of the stories come from the time of Aries (2160 BC to zero)," then "we hit the fish, the Pisces, at zero" — which Coleman offers as why salvation imagery runs "from the lamb of God to the fish."[^4] *Rating: the age boundaries are stylized; assigning specific scriptures to precessional ages is interpretive, not demonstrable.*

### 5. Steelman of the opposing view
Astronomically, "Age of Aries/Pisces" boundaries are arbitrary (constellations have unequal widths and no fixed edges), and there is no evidence biblical authors thought in precessional ages — the ram-for-bull and lamb-for-fish symbolism is fully explicable from Near Eastern sacrificial cult and Christology without precession. Reading [[Genesis 22|the Aqedah]] as an astronomical age-marker risks **eisegesis**: the text's own point is obedience and substitutionary provision. The Malachi "Sun/Son" move is an *English* homophone with no force in Hebrew. Coleman grants the speculative status, presenting the scheme as a recovered "language" rather than a proof — see [[Avoiding Parallelomania Between Ancient Rites and LDS Temple]].
