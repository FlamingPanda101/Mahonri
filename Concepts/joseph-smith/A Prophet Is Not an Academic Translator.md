# A Prophet Is Not an Academic Translator (Objection Rebutted)

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
The objection (which [[Don Bradley]] says he once held himself): if Joseph Smith were a true prophet, his "translation" of the Egyptian papyri should match what Egyptologists say the papyri actually say. Since the surviving Joseph Smith Papyri are a funerary "Book of Breathings," not the story of Abraham, the Book of Abraham is exposed as a fraud.[^1]

## The Rebuttal

1. **The objection assumes prophet = superhuman literalist (rated: strong, conceptual).** "We tend to think that what it means to be a prophet is to be a superhuman" who should "just [give] us a literal rendering of exactly what that text said."[^2] But "a prophet's role is not to be an academic translator. A prophet's role is to bring forth truths from God."[^3] The biblical prophets are the model: "Doesn't say anything about [Isaiah] reading Egyptian or Chinese or Sanskrit. ... It says he spoke words from God."[^4] *Steelman:* a critic ([[Dan McClellan]]-type) will respond that Joseph explicitly claimed to *translate* a specific document — he produced an "Egyptian Alphabet," labeled facsimiles with Egyptological claims, and presented Abraham 1 as the literal content of a scroll — so reframing him as a non-translator "revelator" is a retreat from his own claims, not a defense of them. This is the strongest counter and Bradley does not fully neutralize it; he brackets the translation question rather than resolving it.

2. **The catalyst theory neutralizes the papyrus mismatch (rated: moderate, internal).** Bradley refuses to adjudicate *which* scroll (extant, missing, or none) — the text may have come via a scroll "serving as a kind of catalyst for revelation."[^5] On a catalyst model, the Egyptological content of the surviving papyrus is simply irrelevant to the revealed content.

3. **The counterfactual test (rated: moderate, rhetorical).** Would the Book of Breathings' instructions on preserving entrails have been *more* valuable than the doctrinal truths of the Book of Abraham? "How would that have been of more benefit than the truths of the scripture that we have in the Book of Abraham? I don't think so."[^6] The objection prizes the wrong kind of output.

## Best Quotes

> "A prophet's role is not to be an academic translator. A prophet's role is to bring forth truths from God."[^3]

> "Think about the Prophet Isaiah. Doesn't say anything about him reading Egyptian or Chinese or Sanskrit. ... It says he spoke words from God."[^4]

> "Are you really saying it would be more valuable to us if Joseph Smith had translated the Book of Breathings? ... I don't think so."[^6]
