# The Eyewitness Asymmetry Refutes the Polygamy Conspiracy Theory

#type/rebuttal

## The Claim
[[Jacob Hansen]] and [[Brian Hales]] argue the denial position requires an unbelievable conspiracy. If you put everyone who lived in Joseph's time into two rooms — those who affirmed (from firsthand knowledge) that he practiced plural marriage, and those who denied it — the rooms would be "wildly disproportionate." The affirming room is enormous (church presidents, the plural wives themselves, the [[Doctrine and Covenants 132|D&C 132]] witnesses, even anti-Brigham figures), and *no one ever broke ranks* to expose a fabrication — which is exactly what a large conspiracy could never sustain.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **The affirming witnesses are numerous and cross-factional.** They include church presidents ([[Brigham Young]], [[John Taylor]], [[Wilford Woodruff]], [[Lorenzo Snow]]), [[Eliza R. Snow]], the plural wives themselves, members of the Quorum of the Twelve (George A. Smith, Orson Hyde), and the witnesses to the July 12, 1843 revelation ([[Doctrine and Covenants 132|Section 132]]) — a group that "includes many people who weren't Brighamites, who didn't go west, who even were members of the RLDS church." The affirmation crosses factional and even anti-Brigham lines.[^2] *(Strong — cross-factional testimony is very hard to explain as coordinated fabrication.)*
2. **Almost no plural wife ever recanted.** A conspiracy this size risks even one participant "spilling the beans" — yet of the women named as Joseph's plural wives, essentially none later said "Joseph wasn't a polygamist; we were coerced into lying." The lone partial exception, **Sarah Lawrence**, later denied being sealed to Joseph after leaving the church — but "never said there was a conspiracy" or that anyone pressured her. The expected conspiracy-defectors simply don't exist.[^3] *(Strong — the near-total absence of recantation is powerful against the conspiracy hypothesis.)*
3. **Non-denial under no pressure is itself telling.** [[Fanny Alger]], approached later as a non-member with every freedom to deny, did *not* deny the relationship — treating it as "her own business" rather than saying it never happened. People deny things that didn't happen; her non-denial fits the practice being real.[^4] *(Moderate-to-strong — a behavioral datum consistent with the affirmative case.)*
4. **The conspiracy hypothesis has no defector evidence; the simple hypothesis needs none.** Hansen: it's not that there are *zero* anomalies in the data (deniers seize on these), but that they are *clearly the anomalies*, and the denier "has no theory that accounts for all the rest of the data" except "a great big conspiracy... with no evidence of anyone ever breaking mum." The competing hypothesis — [[The Confidentiality Model Best Fits the Polygamy Evidence|Joseph practiced confidentially with a select group]] — is "a very simple theory" that fits everything.[^5] *(Strong — frames the choice as massive-unbroken-conspiracy vs. one quiet secret, an explanatory-cost mismatch.)*

**Steelman of the denialist view.** A denier replies that "conspiracy" mischaracterizes their position: they need not posit coordinated lying but *sincere error* propagating from Brigham-era leaders who had institutional and doctrinal incentives to retroject polygamy onto Joseph to legitimize their own practice — once the affidavit campaign (1869–93) created a documentary record, later witnesses could honestly "remember" within that framework. The denier also notes that most "eyewitness" affidavits were gathered *decades later* in Utah under church auspices, so their number reflects an organized collection effort, not independent corroboration; and that the wives had strong social/religious incentives to affirm a sealing to the prophet. The LDS rejoinder is that (a) sincere-error-propagation still cannot explain the *cross-factional* and *anti-Brigham* and *RLDS* witnesses who had no incentive to flatter Brigham's program; (b) the 1843 dating and wordprint of [[Doctrine and Covenants 132|D&C 132]] anchor the practice to Joseph's lifetime independent of later affidavits; and (c) the decisive asymmetry remains — a fabricated program of dozens of marriages would have produced *defectors*, and the historical record contains essentially none alleging fabrication, only the lone Sarah Lawrence who claimed no conspiracy. The denier's model must convert an absence of defectors into evidence, which it cannot.

## LDS Relevance
This is the affirmative capstone of the anti-denialism case: having shown the denials are few and qualified ([[Joseph Smith Never Issued a Blanket Denial of Polygamy]]) and the right frame is hypothesis-comparison ([[The Confidentiality Model Best Fits the Polygamy Evidence]]), the eyewitness asymmetry demonstrates the *positive* record is overwhelming and cross-factional, leaving denial to survive only by an unsustainable, defector-free conspiracy. It directly supports the existing [[Polygamy Denialism]] verdict that denial is "historically unsustainable."
