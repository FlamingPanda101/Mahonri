# The Confidentiality Model Best Fits the Polygamy Evidence

#type/rebuttal

## The Claim
[[Jacob Hansen]] reframes the polygamy debate as a contest between *hypotheses* that explain the data, and argues the deniers frame it wrongly. The question is **not** "Joseph lied vs. Brigham lied" but: does the historical record show a man (a) **practicing plural marriage while seeking to keep it confidential**, or (b) **not practicing it at all**? On Hansen's reading, every datum — including Joseph's guarded statements — aligns with the *confidentiality model*, which is the simplest hypothesis that fits all the evidence.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **Correct framing dissolves the "who lied" trap.** Deniers force a binary: "believe Joseph or believe Brigham/Emma." Hansen: "you're just framing it wrong... the question is which hypothesis best fits the data." Once the model is "Joseph practicing plural marriage but seeking to keep it confidential," Joseph's careful or evasive statements *fit the model* rather than refuting it: "if he denies it, that fits my model... you're just making my point."[^1] *(Strong — a sound application of inference-to-best-explanation; the denier's proof-texts become predictions of the favored hypothesis.)*
2. **Justified confidentiality has scriptural precedent.** Hales holds that *if* Joseph (or Hyrum) lied about it, the concealment was *justified*: "if Nazis come to my house... asking if I'm hiding Jews and I tell them no, that is a justified lie," and "justified lying is in the scriptures" (Abraham misrepresenting Sarah to Pharaoh, a marriage situation). In the Nauvoo context of mob violence, open disclosure "would have unleashed all hell." So guarded statements are explained, not embarrassing.[^2] *(Moderate-to-strong — the ethics of the "justified lie" are debatable, but the explanatory point stands: secrecy is expected given the danger.)*
3. **"Joseph wasn't perfect but was always worthy."** Hansen and Hales reject the "fairy-tale" open-book Joseph who never makes hard choices about what to disclose to enemies. Section 132 itself has Emma counseled to forgive Joseph's "trespasses" — so the record shows a Joseph who erred yet remained worthy, consistent with confidential practice rather than either sinless transparency or fraud.[^3] *(Strong — a realistic model of the historical Joseph that the evidence supports.)*
4. **The competing (denialist) hypothesis requires a massive silent conspiracy.** The only alternative that fits the eyewitness record is "a great big conspiracy with all these people" and *no one ever breaking ranks* — which Hansen calls "a really hard theory to endorse." The confidentiality model needs no conspiracy: Joseph practiced quietly with a select group and sought to keep it confidential. (Developed in [[The Eyewitness Asymmetry Refutes the Polygamy Conspiracy Theory]].)[^4] *(Strong — contrasts explanatory cost: one simple secret vs. a vast unbroken conspiracy.)*

**Steelman of the denialist view.** A denier objects that "confidentiality" is an *unfalsifiable* hypothesis: if Joseph affirms polygamy it confirms the model, and if he denies it that *also* confirms the model ("he was keeping it confidential"), so the theory predicts everything and therefore explains nothing. The denier argues the confidentiality model smuggles in the conclusion (that he practiced it) and then re-labels every denial as evidence *for* it. A fair response: a hypothesis that accommodates apparent counter-evidence is not thereby vacuous *if* it is the best explanation of the *total* dataset — and here the confidentiality model is anchored by *independent positive evidence* (dozens of eyewitnesses, the 1843 dating and wordprint of [[Doctrine and Covenants 132|D&C 132]], plural-wife testimonies) that the denial model must explain away as forgery. So the denials are not the *basis* of the model; they are residual data the model comfortably absorbs, while the denier's model can only survive by discounting the positive evidence wholesale (special pleading — see [[Polygamy Denialism]]).

## LDS Relevance
Supplies the *methodological* core of the anti-denialism case: shift from a "whose word do you trust?" credibility contest (which deniers exploit) to "which model best explains all the data?" Framed that way, the confidentiality model wins decisively, and Joseph's guarded statements stop being a liability. This is the connective tissue between [[Joseph Smith Never Issued a Blanket Denial of Polygamy]] (the denials are few and qualified) and [[The Eyewitness Asymmetry Refutes the Polygamy Conspiracy Theory]] (the affirmative record is overwhelming).
