# Seven Restoration Doctrines the Broader Church Is Drifting Toward

## The Point
[[Brian Thomassen]] reframes the worn "Are Mormons Christian?" debate by asking the opposite: "are the rest of Christians becoming more Mormon?"[^1] Building on a lineage of BYU scholarship ([[Truman Madsen]]'s 1974 paper, expanded by [[David Paulsen]] in 2006), he argues that over ~200 years the trend lines of broader Christianity have bent *toward* historically Latter-day Saint positions. His method is a three-part model applied to seven doctrines: (1) [[Joseph Smith]]'s original position, (2) the 19th-century **divergence** — the hostile reaction against it, and (3) a 20th–21st-century **convergence** — mainstream Christianity drifting back. The cumulative claim is that while each doctrine is now a live topic somewhere in Christendom, "only with Joseph Smith are all of these brought together in one modeling."[^2]

## Evidence
The seven points, each showing the same Joseph-then-reaction-then-return arc:

1. **Charismata and a reopened canon.** Joseph produced 872 pages of new scripture with 99 "thus saith the Lord" declarations against a cessationist "the heavens are closed" consensus; the convergence is Pentecostalism (65M US, 600M+ worldwide), new-text discoveries (Dead Sea Scrolls, Nag Hammadi), and the fact that no settled canon exists (Protestant 66, Catholic 73, Orthodox 76–81). "If you recognize revelation, ipso facto, new scripture is on the board."[^3][^4]
2. **A personal, passible God** who weeps (Moses 7) and feels — recovered in mainstream theology from 1912 onward. See [[Passibility - Mainstream Theology Now Affirms a God Who Suffers]].
3. **A social, embodied Godhead** — social trinitarianism's return ("God is not a solitary being but a living society"), though embodiment stays rejected.[^5] See [[The Superlative God Deflection Collapses Into Nothing]], [[Embodied God in Christian Scholarship]].
4. **Deification / theosis** — an explosion of scholarship and the 1993 Catholic Catechism quoting Athanasius. See [[The Deification Publishing Boom Since 1950]], [[Divine Council and Deification Were Native to the Hebrew Bible]].
5. **The divine feminine** (Joseph-adjacent) — now near-consensus that ancient Israel "knew a goddess." See [[Feminine Divine in Ancient Near Eastern Worship]], [[Wisdom Personified as the Mother in Heaven]].
6. **A self-surpassing God in time** — process theology against classical immutability. See [[Open Theism and a Self-Surpassing God]].
7. **Salvation for the unevangelized** — a cross-traditional move away from "no repentance after death." See [[Salvation Is Binary In Christ or Out of Christ Not a Spectrum]].

A recurring sub-thesis: the academy leads and the pulpit follows. Thomassen argues "the academic space is going to inform the cultural space," so when scholarship locks in on a position (a goddess in Israel, deification, process theism), "it naturally feeds into the religious space" and eventually the preaching.[^6]

## Best Quotes
> "Our creedal Christian brothers and sisters are coming closer to becoming like Latter-day Saints in both doctrine and practice. So let's stop asking the question, are Mormons Christian? And maybe let's ask the question of, are the rest of Christians becoming more Mormon?" [^1]

> "All seven have become topics of discussion within contemporary Christianity. However, only with Joseph Smith are all of these brought together in one modeling... he hit all seven, and he did them before it was cool." [^2]

> "What I'm hoping to demonstrate through today is the robustness of our position vis-à-vis everybody else. The trend lines favor us. We are in a strong theoretical position and do not have to feel apologetic on any level." [^7]

## LDS Connection
This is a meta-argument *for* the Restoration's coherence rather than for any single doctrine. Its apologetic force is cumulative: a 19th-century farm boy independently asserted seven theological positions that the surrounding Christian world reacted to as absurd or blasphemous, and the long-run drift of mainstream scholarship and theology has been *toward* each of them — and uniquely, toward all of them at once. The note functions as the hub for the convergence cluster; it complements [[Convergence of Mainstream Christianity Toward LDS Doctrines]] (which catalogs the publishing wave and pre-existence) and connects to the broader claim that [[LDS Theology as Restoration of Early Christianity|LDS theology restores early Christianity]]. The honest counterweight is the representativeness problem: a drift in scholarship and sympathetic theologians is not the same as institutional doctrine or lay belief, and some of these convergences (notably embodiment and a literal Mother in Heaven) remain firmly rejected at the official and pulpit level.
