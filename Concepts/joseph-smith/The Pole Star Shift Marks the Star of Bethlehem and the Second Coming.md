# The Pole Star Shift Marks the Star of Bethlehem and the Second Coming

#type/claim

## The Claim

[[Kamron Coleman]] argues that, alongside the zodiacal precession, a second and harder-to-observe cycle — the slow change of the **North Pole star** over ~26,000 years — encodes the deepest biblical history. The sequence: **Thuban** (in Draco, the pole star through the age of Taurus) sits at the *udder* of a giant cow-constellation (Hathor, which Coleman names "Bossy"); the pole then drifts into the cow's **empty "womb"** of starless dark sky (the formless **Yahweh**, ~1000 BC); and finally to **Polaris** (countable as pole star from ~500 AD). The **Star of Bethlehem** is the sign appearing in that empty womb — the "golden calf" / SUN born as the Son — and the **Magi follow only the North Pole star**. The arrival of Polaris signals the Second Coming.

## Supporting Evidence (steelman)

### 1. The cow constellation and Thuban at the udder (rating: novel asterism; speculative)
From the **Dendera zodiac**'s central standing cow plus a "cow leg" identified with the Big Dipper, Coleman reconstructs a giant northern cow-constellation (Cepheus + Draco + Big Dipper) he names "Bossy," identifying it with **Hathor**, the cow-headed Queen of Heaven. **Thuban** (alpha Draconis), the pole star for ~2 millennia, falls "at the udder of the cow" — and Egypt's highest celestial-kingdom rite was to "nurse from the udder," marked by smearing milk on the mouth.[^1] *Rating: the "cow" asterism and the udder-placement are Coleman's own construction.*

### 2. The empty womb and the formless Yahweh (rating: the interpretive core)
When the pole drifts off Thuban into the cow's **empty womb** (a region with no stars), "the cult of Yahweh commences around 1000 BC" — a **formless, imageless** God, while everyone else worshipped embodied bull-gods. Israel's prophets "correctly identified that we had moved out of that sign," a transition "encoded in Abraham and Isaac."[^2] This is why the **Deuteronomists** rightly forbade worship of the host of heaven: they had identified that the pole now resided in the empty, formless womb — and any new sign there (a comet, a nova) would threaten the cult. See [[Josiah's Reform Removed the Mother from the Temple]].

### 3. Bethlehem's star is the pole-star "golden calf" (rating: the payoff; speculative)
"Around the year zero something happens in the womb of the cow and the whole world goes: the Son of God is born" — the long-anticipated **golden calf** ([[Exodus 32|Exodus 32]] being a premature anticipation of it). The Magi "don't follow any other stars," because every other star wheels in circles; "the only star you follow is the North Pole star," and the sign "appeared exactly where it was supposed to appear, in the womb of the mother cow" — possibly a comet or supernova visible just long enough to be announced ([[Matthew 2.1-12|Matthew 2]]).[^3] See [[The Wise Men Were Bearers of Preserved First-Temple Covenant]].

### 4. Polaris and the Second Coming millennium (rating: a dated prediction; speculative)
"The second coming... is the shifting of the pole star from the empty space to Polaris," countable from ~500 AD; Polaris reaches its exact center ~100 years from now and holds that position ~900 years — "that's the millennium, that's the thousand years of Polaris being in position." Coleman also offers the "nail in a more sure place" image: the *true* celestial pole sits in dark sky, not on visible Polaris — knowable only through "centuries of observation."[^4] Compare [[D&C 121.32|D&C 121:26-32]] and [[The Precession of the Equinoxes Decodes the Lamb Ram and Fish]].

### 5. Steelman of the opposing view
Mainstream scholarship reads the Star of Bethlehem (if astronomical at all) as a conjunction, comet, or nova — *not* a pole star, and the Magi narrative as theological rather than a navigational pole-fix. Yahwistic aniconism is explained by Israelite religious development, not by the pole entering a "cow's womb," and the Dendera "cow" plus "Bossy" is an idiosyncratic asterism unattested in the sources. Dating the Second Coming to a Polaris alignment is the kind of astronomical date-setting scripture discourages ([[Matthew 24.36]], "of that day knoweth no man"). Coleman frames the whole scheme as "my hypothesis" and a recovered language, not a proof — and even jokes that critics will say "you're wrong."
