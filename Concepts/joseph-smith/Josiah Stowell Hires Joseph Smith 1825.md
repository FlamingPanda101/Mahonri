# Josiah Stowell Hires Joseph Smith 1825

#type/claim

## The Claim
In 1825, [[Josiah Stowell]] hired [[Joseph Smith]] to help locate a legendary lost Spanish silver mine near Harmony, Pennsylvania. This is the one treasure hunt Joseph acknowledges in his own canonized history, the occasion on which he met [[Emma Smith]], and the venture later referenced in both the 1838 *Elders' Journal* and the 1826 court case.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Joseph Smith History (primary, canonized).** Joseph records being hired by Stowell to dig for the Spanish silver mine, the failure to find it, and his eventually persuading "the old gentleman" Stowell to stop.[^2] *Rating: high — first-person canonical text.*

2. **[[Lucy Mack Smith]]'s family history (primary, friendly).** Lucy recounts Stowell coming to Manchester, NY, to recruit Joseph because he "had means or keys whereby which he could discern things not otherwise visible to the natural eye" — i.e., seer stones, since this predates the Nephite interpreters.[^3] *Rating: high but partisan.*

3. **1838 Elders' Journal (primary).** When Joseph itemizes how little he was paid as a "money digger," he is referring back to this same Stowell search.[^4] *Rating: high.*

4. **Convergence with the 1826 trial.** The same Stowell relationship produces the 1826 disorderly-person case (brought by Stowell's nephew [[Peter Bridgeman]]), tying this episode to the "con man" debate.[^5] *Rating: high — multiple document streams converge on one event.*

5. **Steelman.** A critic could note Joseph's framing (he "prevailed on" Stowell to stop) self-servingly casts Joseph as the reluctant skeptic rather than the eager money-digger; Mark concedes Joseph downplays his involvement somewhat. The friendly sources (Joseph, Lucy) are not neutral. Still, the *fact* of the expedition is uncontested across hostile and friendly sources.

## Best Quotes

> "Joseph Smith tells us about going down there to help dig for this treasure ... and that he eventually prevails with the old gentleman Josiah Stowell to cease looking for this treasure."[^2]

> "Josiah Stowell came and tried to recruit Joseph Smith because ... Joseph Smith had means or keys whereby which he could discern things not otherwise visible to the natural eye."[^3]
