# No Lives Lost in Liberty Jail Prophecy

## The Claim
From the comprehensive *History of the Church*: while imprisoned, [[Joseph Smith]] told his fellow captives, "Be of good cheer, brethren. The word of the Lord came to me last night that our lives should be given us, and that whatever we may suffer during this our captivity, none of our lives shall be taken." All the Liberty Jail prisoners did survive their captivity.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The prisoners did survive.** No one held with Joseph in Liberty Jail died there. (Evidence rating: outcome real.)[^1]
2. **Late, comprehensive-history source.** It comes from the *History of the Church* compilation, again a later record. (Evidence rating: standard late-record caveat; see [[The Late-Reminiscence Problem in Joseph Smith Prophecy Claims]].)[^1]
3. **Low base-rate risk.** Prisoners awaiting transfer/trial usually are not executed in captivity; predicting survival is not a long shot, so fulfillment is unremarkable. (Evidence rating: weak as evidence.)[^1]
4. **Steelman of the believing view.** Given the genuine death threats and mob violence the Saints faced in 1838–39 Missouri, an assurance that *none* would die in captivity was pastorally bold, and it held. The deflation is that survival in jail is the ordinary outcome and the record is late.

## Best Quotes
> "The word of the Lord came to me last night that our lives should be given us, and that whatever we may suffer during this our captivity, none of our lives shall be taken."[^1]
