# The Carthage Door Did Not Lock and Only Two Pistols Were in the Room

## The Claim
Two physical facts, per [[Shannon Tracy]], constrain any reconstruction of the [[Joseph Smith|Smith]] killings at Carthage. First, **the upstairs door did not lock** — its latch was broken and misaligned, so the prisoners had spent their first days whittling the frame with pocketknives trying to make it close, and when the mob came they could only hold it shut by hand. Second, **the documentation places only two pistols in the room**, "not six, not eight." The landing outside is small: realistically five people at the door and ten on the stairs, not a crowd of thirty.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **A small landing caps the attacking force.** Tracy's spatial point is that "mobs are not big" *at the point of contact*: the architecture allows maybe five attackers on the landing and a handful on the stairs, against four defenders pushing the door — which reframes the event from a faceless horde to a tight, countable struggle. (Evidence rating: moderate — a defensible inference from the preserved floor plan, which his scan would test.)[^1]
2. **The broken door explains the defenders' behavior.** Because the door would not latch, the four men (Joseph, Hyrum, [[John Taylor]], [[Willard Richards]]) had to barricade it bodily; this is why the initial volley came *through* the door and why it could be forced open. The detail that they whittled the frame for days is offered as a little-known but documented hardship. (Evidence rating: moderate — coheres with the standard account that the first shots came through the door panel.)[^2]
3. **"Two pistols" disciplines the ballistics.** Limiting the in-room firearms to two pistols (plus the mob's rifles outside, which must be angled awkwardly to fire on the landing) restricts which weapons could have produced which trajectories — the kind of constraint a reconstruction needs. (Evidence rating: moderate, contingent on Tracy's documentary sources being accurate.)[^3]

## Steelman of the Opposing View
These are Tracy's characterizations of the record, not independently verified in the episode, and "only two pistols in the room" is doing real work — a critic would want the source documents, since the count bears on later disputes about who was armed and whether [[Joseph Smith]] returning fire with a pepper-box was anomalous. The "small landing" argument also cuts both ways: a smaller attacking party makes individual identification *easier*, which is precisely why descendants and historians have litigated who fired the fatal shots. The fair residue is modest but real: the non-locking door is well attested and materially shaped the event, and any honest reconstruction must work within the jail's actual, preserved dimensions rather than a cinematic mob.
