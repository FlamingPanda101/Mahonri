# Orson Pratt Mission to Jerusalem Prophecy

## The Claim
From the *History of the Church*: [[Joseph Smith]] gave a blessing/prophecy (attributed to the early 1830s) that the recipient would "in due time... go to Jerusalem, the land of thy fathers, and be a watchman unto the house of Israel," preparing the way for the gathering of that people. The host associates the fulfillment with Orson Pratt's mission to Jerusalem around 1840.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **A mission to Jerusalem did occur.** (Orson Hyde's 1841 dedication of Palestine is the historically famous instance; the host names "Orson Pratt... 1840 on a 2 or 3-year mission" and flags he needs to fact-check.) Either way, an apostle did go to Jerusalem on a gathering-themed mission. (Evidence rating: outcome real; the host is uncertain on the exact person/date.)[^2]
2. **Textbook self-fulfilling prophecy.** The host's decisive objection: "if Joseph Smith said in the early 1830s, you're going to go to Jerusalem, then Joseph could easily fulfill that own prophecy by sending him on a mission." The prophet controlled the assignment. See [[Self-Fulfilling Prophecy Objection]]. (Evidence rating: strong deflation.)[^2]
3. **Proponent rates it weak.** "I don't find this a very strong fulfilled prophecy." (Evidence rating: proponent concession.)[^2]
4. **Steelman of the believing view.** A blessing naming a specific future destination and role that later materializes can still be read as inspired direction, and the gathering of Israel theme is theologically central. But because the fulfillment lay wholly within Joseph's administrative power, it carries little weight as evidence of supernatural foreknowledge.

## Best Quotes
> "In due time thou shalt go to Jerusalem, the land of thy fathers, and be a watchman unto the house of Israel."[^1]

> "Joseph could easily fulfill that own prophecy by sending him on a mission. So... I don't find this a very strong fulfilled prophecy."[^2]
