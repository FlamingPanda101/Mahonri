# Kirtland a Stronghold for Five Years Prophecy

## The Claim
On 4 September 1831, [[Frederick G. Williams]] was told not to sell his farm because Kirtland was to be a stronghold for the church for five years. The revelation is canonized in [[D&C 64.30-33|Doctrine and Covenants section 64]]: "I will retain a stronghold in the land of Kirtland for the space of five years... in the which I will not overthrow the wicked." After roughly five to six years, the Kirtland Safety Society failed, apostasy spread, and the Saints fled Kirtland for Missouri.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Strong source pedigree.** Unlike most items in the list, this is a *canonized, contemporaneous* revelation (1831), not a late reminiscence — its strongest evidential feature. (Evidence rating: source is strong; the prediction's specificity is the weaker point.)[^1]
2. **Approximate fit.** The host notes the stronghold held "for 5 years until apostasy came," with the Kirtland collapse and exodus arriving "after about six years." The five-year window is roughly, not exactly, met. (Evidence rating: moderate — "about six years" is a slight overshoot of the stated five.)[^2]
3. **The deflation: a leader can set the horizon.** A five-year confidence interval for a church's regional base is the sort of medium-term plan a leader can shape and, to a degree, self-fulfill; "stronghold" is also vague enough to accommodate varied outcomes. See [[Self-Fulfilling Prophecy Objection]]. (Evidence rating: moderate deflation.)[^2]
4. **Steelman of the believing view.** That a contemporaneous 1831 text named a finite duration after which Kirtland would cease to be the church's stronghold — and that the place was indeed abandoned in roughly that window — is more impressive than the open-ended, late prophecies precisely because it is datable and bounded. The cost is the imprecise "five vs about six years" fit.

## Best Quotes
> "I will not that my servant Frederick G. Williams should sell his farm, for I the Lord will retain a stronghold in the land of Kirtland for the space of 5 years."[^1]

> "After about six years, that's when the Kirtland Bank happened and the apostasy and then they fled from Kirtland to Missouri."[^2]
