# Joseph Smith and Helen Mar Kimball Sealing

## The Claim
[[Joseph Smith]]'s sealing to [[Helen Mar Kimball]] — who was "just shy of 15 years old" and sealed "at her father's request" — was almost certainly a dynastic sealing intended to bind the Smith and Kimball families together, not a sexual marriage. This sealing has been mischaracterized by critics as evidence that Smith was a "pedophile," a charge that collapses under both historical context and the specific nature of LDS sealings.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Dynastic, Not Sexual:** The sealing was "almost certainly just a sealing to bind the Smith and Kimball family and not a sexual marriage."[^1] This reflects the early LDS practice of plural sealings for familial and spiritual bonding — a distinct category from sexual polygamous marriages as they later developed.

2. **At Her Father's Request:** Helen Mar Kimball was sealed to Joseph Smith "at her father's request"[^1] — [[Heber C. Kimball]], a senior LDS apostle, sought this sealing for dynastic reasons. This was not a unilateral action by Smith but involved the family's active participation and approval according to their own cultural framework.

3. **Previously Addressed by Others:** The subject "has been dealt with by a bunch of others"[^1] including LDS apologists like [[David Snell]], suggesting a well-established apologetic tradition around this specific case that argues the sealing was non-sexual and dynastic in nature.

4. **Historical Comparators:** When compared to marriages in the same era — notably [[Edgar Allan Poe]] marrying a 13-year-old at age 27 in 1836 — the age gap does not stand out as uniquely scandalous. About 1 in every 10 to 15 rural marriages in that period was to a woman in her mid-teens, and those women married established, older men capable of providing.[^2]

## Counter-Arguments

- **Age was still young by contemporary standards:** Even acknowledging different norms, a bride "just shy of 15" marrying a man in his late 30s was on the young end even for the 1840s. The "dynastic vs. sexual" distinction must be established with primary evidence, not merely asserted.
- **Polygamy adds a layer:** A monogamous marriage to a 14-year-old (like Poe's) differs in morally relevant ways from a polygamous sealing to a 14-year-old when the man already has multiple wives. The power dynamics are more complex.
- **Prophetic authority asymmetry:** Smith was not merely an older man but Kimball's spiritual leader — this power differential has no parallel in the Poe comparison and requires its own analysis.

## Best Quote

> "Joseph's sealing to Helen was almost certainly just a sealing to bind the Smith and Kimball family and not a sexual marriage."[^1]
