# Brigham Young Saved the Church Through the Keys

## The Claim
At the 1844 succession crisis, [[Brigham Young]] — as president of the Quorum of the Twelve, which held the priesthood keys — was the figure (under the Lord) who preserved the Church. Without him (or the Twelve), there would be no temples, no westward gathering, and likely no Church as it exists.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The Twelve alone continued Joseph's full program (rated: strong; scholarly).** Historian [[Ron Esplin]] (whose Interpreter piece "Joseph Smith: A Life Lived in Crescendo" Peterson calls "the definitive statement") found that in 1844 "there was only one group that was planning to carry out Joseph's program. That was the Twelve led by Brigham Young." Every other claimant wanted to "dispense with or devalue some aspect of what Joseph taught." Two key planks — finish *and then abandon* the Nauvoo Temple, and go West — only the Twelve committed to both.[^2]

2. **The keys, not mere leadership (rated: strong within LDS framework).** "Brigham and the Twelve had the keys. Sidney, apparently, did not." Without the keys to administer priesthood ordinances, the Church under [[Sydney Rigdon]] "would have been... a kind of odd Protestant church with an extra volume of scripture" — probably no temples, no migration (Rigdon went to Pennsylvania).[^3] See [[Sydney Rigdon]].

3. **Brigham as the indispensable executor (rated: moderate; "who else?" argument).** Peterson's core conclusion after the research: "Who else could have done it?" Joseph was the visionary; Brigham was "the operations," "the executor" — the man who could actually lead a mass migration. Peterson even doubts Joseph himself could have led the trek ("Different gift").[^4] *Steelman:* the "who else?" argument is partly an argument from ignorance; Peterson concedes God could have used a backup ([[Heber C. Kimball]], or others), floating a speculative "backup prophet" theory. The claim is that *Brigham's particular skillset fit the need*, not that he was logically irreplaceable.

4. **Brigham recognized the keys had transferred (rated: moderate; testimony).** On hearing of Joseph's death Brigham worried "What happened to the keys?" then "slapped my knee" realizing "they're here with us" — Joseph had "rolled off the kingdom onto their [the Twelve's] shoulders."[^5]

5. **Succession is instantaneous via the Twelve (rated: applied doctrine).** Peterson uses the present (President Nelson's death → Dallin H. Oaks president "within a split second") to illustrate the principle established in 1844: there is never a moment without a President, because the senior apostle who presides over the presiding quorum *is* the President.[^6]

## Best Quotes

> "I think he saved the Church in a way. Now, obviously, the Lord saved the Church. But had someone else come to the fore, Sidney Rigdon or someone else... we wouldn't have the Church we have today."[^1]

> "We would have been, I think, under Sidney, a kind of odd Protestant church with an extra volume of scripture. But would we have temples? I'm not sure we would."[^3]

> "Then I slapped my knee and realized they're here with us. That's what Joseph was doing. He held a meeting with them and rolled off the kingdom onto their shoulders and gave them the keys."[^5]
