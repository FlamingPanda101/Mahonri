# Golden Calf and Bronze Serpent as Idolatry Tests

## The Claim
[[Joe Heschmeyer]] uses two Old Testament cases — the **golden calf** ([[Exodus 32.md|Exodus 32]]) and the **bronze serpent** ([[Numbers 21.8-9|Numbers 21]] / 2 Kings 18) — to argue that ascribing the *true qualities* of God to a *false object of worship* is still idolatry. This presses against Hansen's [[Common Reference - Worshipping Adam as God|common-reference]] defense of Adam-God.

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. The golden calf had a "true" caption (strong — primary text)
At the calf, Israel said, "This is the god that brought you out of Egypt" — and "there really was a god that brought them out of Egypt." So the *attributes* attached were true, yet it was idolatry. Heschmeyer: "wouldn't the golden calf not be an idol then?" if Hansen's reference-rule held [^1].

### 2. The bronze serpent — God-made, later destroyed (strong — primary text)
The bronze serpent was "something that was created by God," yet "when people eventually did worship the bronze serpent on the staff, it was destroyed because then they were treating it as an idol." The boundary: "once they're ascribing divine worship to the thing that isn't God, that's where the boundary line seems to be" [^2].

### 3. Is worshipping Adam closer or further than the calf? (the rhetorical thrust)
Heschmeyer: "is worshipping Adam closer or further than worshipping the golden staff or the bronze serpent? Because all of them seem very clearly not to be worshipping the true God" [^3].

### Steelman (Hansen's replies)
Hansen offers three counters: (a) the calf was made by **Aaron, not the authorized oracle Moses**, by "somebody who was not the oracle of God... drawing them off to something else" [^4]; (b) the calf was modeled "in the image of these other deities they know is not the God of Israel," a "composite," so they may not have truly believed it was Yahweh [^4]; (c) the bronze serpent was a *commanded* image (like Catholics' own religious images), idolatrous only once worshipped [^5]. With Adam, by contrast, Young got *only* the "firstborn man" detail wrong, while keeping creation, love, fatherhood of Christ, and even a higher Elohim intact [^6].

## Best Quotes
> "Wouldn't the golden calf not be an idol then? Because they said, 'This is the god that brought you out of Egypt,' and there really was a god that brought them out of Egypt." — Joe Heschmeyer [^1]

> "Once they're ascribing divine worship to the thing that isn't God, that's where the boundary line seems to be." — Joe Heschmeyer, on the bronze serpent [^2]

> "Moses was the liaison between God and man... and then somebody else who was not the oracle of God is out there telling them, drawing them off to something else." — Luke Hansen [^4]
