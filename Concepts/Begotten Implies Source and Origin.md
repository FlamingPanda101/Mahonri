# Begotten Implies Source and Origin

## The Claim

If the Father *begets* the Son, the Father is the Son's source and origin — so the same being cannot beget itself. "Eternally begotten" does not escape this: changing the *time* (or aspect) of the verb does not change the grammatical relationship in which the Son is the direct object proceeding from the Father. [^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Begetting denotes derivation.** To be "begotten of" something is for that thing to be your source and origin. A being that is its own source is incoherent. (Evidence: solid as ordinary semantics of "beget.") [^1]
2. **"Eternal" doesn't neutralize the verb.** Barnes' grammatical point: "changing the time, the perfectivity of the verb, doesn't change that one is a direct object." Whether the begetting is timeless or temporal, the Son remains the one *begotten* and the Father the one *begetting* — an asymmetric source-relation. (Evidence: a fair point about verb aspect vs. argument structure; the orthodox grant the asymmetry but deny it implies temporal/causal origination — see steelman.) [^2]
3. **Coheres with the hierarchy data.** Supports [[The Father Is Greater Than I|"the Father is greater than I"]] and the broader picture of the Father as the fountainhead of the Godhead. [^3]
4. **Steelman.** Nicene orthodoxy *affirms* the Son's eternal generation precisely to capture the Father as source (*archē*) while denying it implies the Son began to exist or is lesser in being: "begotten, not made," "eternally begotten," same essence. The asymmetry is one of *relation of origin*, not of time, rank, or divinity — the Son is "Light from Light," fully God, deriving only relationally. So "begotten implies a temporal/inferior origin" is the very inference Nicaea was crafted to block. (Evidence: this is the heart of pro-Nicene theology; it is a principled distinction between *relation of origin* and *priority of being*.) The LDS reply: "eternal relation of origin with no priority of being" is asserted rather than explained, and the natural reading of *begotten* — derivation — supports distinct beings (Father as literal source) over one self-begetting being. [^2]

## Best Quotes

> "If the Father begets the Son — wait a minute, you're saying the same being begets itself? And they'll say, well, he was eternally begotten. It doesn't matter. It still doesn't matter." [^1]

> "Begotten of something means that it is your source and origin. Changing the time, the perfectivity of the verb, doesn't change that one is a direct object." [^2]
