# Seer Stones and Divining Rods as Revelatory Means

#type/claim

## The Claim
For Latter-day Saints, [[Joseph Smith]]'s seer stones and [[Oliver Cowdery]]'s divining rod are not embarrassing folk-magic artifacts to be explained away but legitimate God-ordained **"means"** for receiving revelation — standing in continuity with Old Testament instruments like the Urim and Thummim and the rods of Moses and Aaron. This is part of the foundation of the Restoration and "we have to own that."[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **They are the best-documented items on the folk-practice list.** Of all the practices attributed to the Smiths, only seer stones, divining rods, and treasure seeking have credible, multiply-attested, contemporaneous support from both sides of the bias line.[^2] Joseph used seer stones; the Smith family used divining rods (Joseph Sr "almost for sure," Joseph Jr "probably," [[Oliver Cowdery]] "certain" per [[D&C 6|D&C 6]]/8).[^3] *Rating: strong.*

2. **Joseph used a seer stone to translate (foundational).** [[Martin Harris]] and others affirm Joseph used seer stones in the Book of Mormon translation; Joseph saw the stones as "a valid tool that he is receiving revelation through."[^4] *Rating: strong — central to LDS origins.*

3. **Old Testament instrument precedents.** The high priest's [[Exodus 28.30|Urim and Thummim]], [[Genesis 44.5|Joseph of Egypt's divining cup]], the [[Exodus 7.9-12|rods of Moses and Aaron]], and [[Numbers 17|Aaron's budding rod]] (which Mark calls "divination") all show scripture endorsing revelation through physical objects.[^5] *Rating: moderate-to-strong — direct scriptural parallels, though critics dispute the analogy's tightness.*

4. **The rod-of-nature revelation ([[D&C 6|D&C 6]]).** The revelation to Cowdery, in Christ's voice, says "there is no other power save the power of God which can cause this rod of nature to work in your hands" — explicitly designating it a spiritual gift, not natural science.[^6] *Rating: strong (for LDS) — canonical self-witness.*

5. **Own the kernel, not the whole culture (nuance).** Mark distinguishes the *core* — receiving revelation from God through these means — from the *cultural accretions* around them, which Latter-day Saints need not endorse.[^7] *Rating: strong — important theological discrimination.*

6. **Steelman.** A naturalist objects that calling a divining rod a "spiritual gift" is special pleading: the very revelations were edited from "sprout" to "rod" to "gift of Aaron" (1829→1833→1835), which looks like progressive sanitization of an embarrassing folk practice rather than stable revelation.[^8] Mark grants the textual changes openly; his counter is that the *underlying* claim (God works through instruments) is biblically attested, so the practice is theologically owned rather than denied.

## Best Quotes

> "Joseph Smith sees his seer stones as a valid tool that he is receiving revelation through. And he and Oliver Cowdery see Cowdery's rod ... as a legitimate device just like the Urim and Thummim that the high priest of Israel wore."[^4]

> "There is no other power save the power of God which can cause this rod of nature to work in your hands."[^6]

> "We have to own that ... the core of receiving revelation from God through seer stones and divining rods ... is part of the foundations of the Restoration."[^1]
