# Endowment Elements Predate Joseph Smith's Freemasonry

## The Point
A common critique is that the temple endowment is merely borrowed Freemasonry: Joseph Smith became a Mason on March 16, 1842, and introduced the endowment seven weeks later (May 3, 1842), so (critics say) the ceremony was simply lifted from the lodge. Fife concedes real similarities (and many differences) but argues the timeline defeats the dependence claim — recognizable elements of the endowment appear scattered through Joseph Smith's revelations and translations 13 to 20 years *before* he ever became a Freemason.

## Evidence
Fife lays out a chronological chain of pre-1842 endowment elements [^1]:
- **~20 years prior (1820):** the First Vision contains echoes and allusions to the endowment (see [[Abraham's First Vision Pattern]]).
- **1823 (~19 years prior):** Joseph Smith Sr. reportedly told a non-Mormon, Fayette Lapham, that the plates bore the masonic-style compass and square with the interpreters overlaid — and Joseph Sr. died in 1840, two years before his son's initiation.
- **1828 (14 years prior):** the lost 116 pages reportedly contained endowment elements.
- **1829 (13 years prior):** the visions of Lehi and Nephi (1 Nephi 8-13), the book of Ether (Ether 3), and especially **King Benjamin's address** in Mosiah carry endowment patterns — a worthy assembly (Mosiah 1:11), a new name (Mosiah 5:11-12), a warning not to trifle with sacred things (Mosiah 2:9), the Creation/Garden/Fall sequence, covenants of obedience, sacrifice, chastity, and consecration, taking upon oneself the name of Christ, unified prayer with symbolic posture, and promised sealing blessings.
- **1831-1836 (11-6 years prior):** the Lord repeatedly tells Joseph that the Saints are to be "endowed with power" (D&C 38:32, 38; 95:8; 105:12, 18, 33; 110:9).
- **1841 (1 year prior):** D&C 124:39-42 names anointings, washings, baptisms for the dead, and ordinances to be revealed in the holy house — "things which have been kept hid from before the foundation of the world."

Fife adds the post-Kirtland evidence that Joseph already had more to teach: from Liberty Jail (March 1839) he wrote that he "never [had the] opportunity to give [the Saints] the plan that God has revealed to me," and later in 1839 Parley P. Pratt records receiving from Joseph "the first idea of eternal family organization, and the eternal union of the sexes" [^2].

## Best Quotes
> "Critics claim that the temple endowment came into being only after Joseph Smith became a freemason. If that is true, then how do critics explain the following?" [^1]

> "I never have had [the] opportunity to give [the Saints] the plan that God has revealed to me." — Joseph Smith, Liberty Jail, March 1839, quoted by Fife [^2]

## LDS Connection
This is a methodological cousin of [[Avoiding Parallelomania Between Ancient Rites and LDS Temple]]: shared symbols do not establish derivation, and here the chronology runs the wrong way for the borrowing thesis. It ties the temple back to the foundational revelations and to the endowment-echoes Fife also finds in the First Vision ([[Abraham's First Vision Pattern]], [[First Vision Accounts Are No More Contradictory Than the Gospels]]), framing Freemasonry as (at most) a familiar ritual vocabulary for restoring something the [[Latter-day Saints|Latter-day Saint]] sources already pointed toward.
