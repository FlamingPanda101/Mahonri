# The Seven Seals and the Age of the Earth Show Continuing Revelation

## The Point

The popular Latter-day Saint idea that the earth has had "roughly a seven-thousand-year history since the Fall" rests on D&C 77:7, where Joseph Smith (1832) read the seven seals of Revelation 5:1 as the seven thousand years of earth's temporal history (the "millennial day" theory; cf. 2 Pet. 3:8). But Joseph himself taught a *different* view a decade later. In 1843 he declared that "none of the things John saw had any allusion [to] the days of Adam, Enock, Ab[raham] or Jesus . . . John saw what was to shortly come to pass." [^1] Frederick's point is methodological: to cite D&C 77:7 as "the final word on the age of the earth when Joseph would preach a very different perspective a decade later leaves us using only half the pieces of the puzzle." [^2]

## Evidence

- D&C 77 (canonized 1876) gives a "historicist" reading: "the first seal contains the things of the first thousand years . . . and so on until the seventh" (D&C 77:7) — the basis of the seven-thousand-year-earth idea. [^3]
- Joseph's 1843 statement restricts the seals to events "shortly to come to pass," with "no allusion to any things that had been on the earth" in past ages — closer to historicists who limited the seals to the Christian era. [^1]
- Frederick suggests "subsequent revelations and experiences along with a deepening understanding of scripture" may have led Joseph to a different answer in 1843 than in 1832 — "as sometimes happened with the JST and other revelations." [^2]
- The takeaway is to be "wary of privileging one source over the other." [^2]

## Best Quotes

> "None of the things John saw had any allusion [to] the days of Adam, Enock Ab[raham] or Jesus—only as represented to John & clearly set forth—John saw that which was shortly to come to pass." (Joseph Smith, 1843) [^1]

> "To cite Doctrine & Covenants 77:7 as the final word on the age of the earth when Joseph would preach a very different perspective a decade later leaves us using only half the pieces of the puzzle." [^2]

## LDS Connection

This is a clean case of [[Editing Revealed Scripture Is Compatible with Divine Origin|revelation as an unfolding process]]: the Prophet's own understanding deepened over time, so a single proof-text cannot be frozen into binding cosmological doctrine. It models the broader principle that the age of the earth is not settled by scripture alone — the same conclusion Spackman reaches about [[No Death Before the Fall Is a Twentieth-Century Interpretation Not Settled Doctrine|"no death before the Fall"]].
