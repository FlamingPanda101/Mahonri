# Quranic Textual History Is Cleaner Than the Bible's

## The Claim
Muslims commonly boast that the Quran lacks the complicated textual history of the Bible — a single standardized text transmitted essentially without variance. Peterson agrees this is *largely* true, but adds two important caveats: it was achieved by deliberately destroying variant texts, and the surviving evidence for variants was itself (literally) incinerated in World War II.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **The Uthmanic recension standardized and then destroyed alternatives.** The third caliph, Uthman, ordered all written and memorized versions brought together, established one correlated text, and **destroyed the alternate versions** so disputes would not arise in the community — producing "basically the one we have today."[^2] *(Strong — the Uthmanic codification and suppression of variant codices is standard Islamic-history scholarship.)* So the Quran's cleanliness is partly an artifact of editorial suppression, not only of faithful transmission.
2. **The claim is "true... but not quite as true as they may think."** Variant texts did exist. A scholarly project (Peterson recalls Arthur Jeffery, associated with Columbia, in cooperation with the University of Berlin) gathered variant Quran manuscripts to produce a critical edition.[^3] *(Moderate — Jeffery's "Materials for the History of the Text of the Qur'an" and the Bergsträsser/Pretzl archive in Munich/Berlin are real; Peterson's recollection of institutions is approximate.)*
3. **The variant archive was destroyed by a WWII bombing.** The gathered manuscripts, stored in a warehouse, "took a direct hit during World War II," which — as Peterson wryly puts it — "simplified the textual history of the Quran considerably," because the variants "were all destroyed, incinerated."[^1] *(Moderate-to-strong — the loss of the German Quran-archive materials in WWII is widely reported, though some photographic records survived.)*
4. **Possible early/variant evidence remains under-studied.** Peterson notes the Sana'a (Yemen) palimpsest discoveries with "slight variants," not yet much studied, and a contested gilded-plate Quran reportedly made for the emperor of China during Muhammad's lifetime and found in a Chinese tomb — metallurgically old, but Peterson is "divided internally about whether it's authentic."[^4] *(Weak-to-moderate — Sana'a is genuine and significant; the China gold-plate Quran is an unverified, possibly apocryphal account Peterson himself flags as uncertain.)*
5. **Steelman.** A Muslim apologist can fairly insist the consonantal *rasm* of the Quran is remarkably stable and that the destroyed variants were minor (orthographic, dialectal), so the boast stands in substance. Peterson does not deny relative stability — "they've kept it pretty clean since then" — he only resists the *absolute* version of the claim. The honest conclusion is comparative: cleaner than the Bible's, but not pristine and partly enforced.
