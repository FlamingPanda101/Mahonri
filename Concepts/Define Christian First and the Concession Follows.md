# Define Christian First and the Concession Follows

#type/claim

## The Claim

The most efficient way to win the "are Mormons Christian?" question against a **non-creedal Evangelical** is not to argue Christology but to ask the interlocutor to *state their own definition* of "Christian" up front — because a Bible-only Protestant who rejects creeds as authoritative will almost always supply a **minimal, scripture-only** definition that the Latter-day Saint already satisfies. [[Hayden Carroll]] runs exactly this play on the Evangelical student **Titha**: he draws out her definition ("they agree on the fact that Jesus is the son of God, that he died on the cross for our sins. To me, the other stuff doesn't really matter" [^1]), confirms she does not require trinitarianism ("you don't have to be a trinitarian to be a Christian?" — "Yes" [^2]), pins down the one ambiguous term ("when you say God... what do you mean by God?" → "the all-knowing being who created Genesis 1:1," which Carroll affirms [^3]), and harvests the concession: "then... you're a Christian. **Mormons are Christian**." [^4]

This is distinct from the [[Latter-day Saints Are Christians Just Not Creedal Christians|"creedal vs. non-creedal"]] move (where the *Latter-day Saint* self-defines and concedes the creedal label) and from the [[Same Reference Different Beliefs - You Worship the Same Jesus|same-reference]] argument (which is about the *object* of worship). Here the **opponent** supplies the definition, and the work is just clearing the ambiguity in "God" so the definition can be applied honestly. It operationalizes the vault's [[The Creeds Cannot Be the Neutral Baseline - The Circularity of Creedal Disqualification|circularity point]]: once you refuse to smuggle the creeds in as the silent baseline, the explicit Bible-only criterion lets Mormons in.

## Supporting Evidence

1. **A non-creedal Protestant has no principled stopping point above the minimal definition (structural).** Titha's own framing — "as long as they agree... that Jesus is the son of God, that he died on the cross for our sins... the other stuff doesn't really matter" [^1] — is the predictable output of sola-scriptura-without-creeds. Because she treats denominational distinctives as "details that actually don't have anything to do with salvation" [^5], she cannot consistently elevate the Trinity (a creedal, extra-biblical term she admits is "never in the Bible" [^6]) into the definition of "Christian." *Strength: strong against this interlocutor type; it is an internal-consistency point, not an imposed standard. It does not bind a confessional Protestant who treats the creeds as authoritative.*

2. **The only real ambiguity is the referent of "God," and it is cheaply resolved (lexical).** Carroll's one careful step is to refuse to let "God" pass undefined: "I agree with that statement, but I wonder if we disagree... what do you mean by God?" [^3] Titha answers with a *function* (Creator of [[Genesis 1.1|Genesis 1:1]]), not a *metaphysics* (simplicity, *ex nihilo*, consubstantiality) — and on the functional definition the Latter-day Saint and the Evangelical genuinely converge, so the concession is honest rather than equivocal. This is the same reference-vs-belief distinction as [[Same Reference Different Beliefs - You Worship the Same Jesus]], reached from the definitional side. *Strength: strong; the move is valid precisely because it surfaces the term most likely to hide a false agreement.*

3. **The live, on-camera concession is the proof of concept (demonstrative).** The student moves from "I don't know if I agree with that" about the banner [^7] to "Mormons are Christian" [^4] within one conversation, driven by her own stated criterion rather than by being argued out of the Trinity (she never abandons it). *Strength: persuasive as illustration; weak as generalization — n = 1, an unscripted exchange with a self-described non-specialist, on a partisan channel.*

**Steelman of the opposing view (the confessional / creedal reply).** A thoughtful creedal Christian answers that Titha simply gave a *defective* definition, and her concession is worth exactly nothing — "Christian" is not a label each believer redefines but a historic term whose content is the rule of faith expressed in the Nicene Creed (one God in three consubstantial persons). On this view Carroll has not shown Mormons are Christian; he has shown that an under-catechized layperson can be led to apply a sub-Nicene definition. The proper response (per [[Same Reference Different Beliefs - You Worship the Same Jesus|Driscoll/Ortlund]]) is that a wrong account of *who God is* yields "a different gospel," so the functional "Creator of Genesis 1:1" handshake conceals a substantive divide at the level of God's being and kind. The tactic therefore only "works" against interlocutors who already grant the disputed premise — that the creeds are not constitutive of the word "Christian" — which is the very point at issue. It establishes a *consistency* result (this person cannot, on her own terms, exclude Mormons), not the stronger *truth* that Mormons meet the historic definition.
