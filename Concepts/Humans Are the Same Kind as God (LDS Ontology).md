# Humans Are the Same Kind as God (LDS Ontology)

## The Claim
[[Jacob Hansen]] holds that humans are *not* ontologically a different kind of being from God. All beings have existed eternally as "intelligences" — "in some sort of an essential sense we have always existed, but in a lower form even than we are now" — and God "brought us into a higher state of existence," after which we continue "to become as he is." [^1][^2] Crucially, "we believe that we fundamentally are of the same kind as Jesus." [^3] Hansen names "you are not ontologically a different kind from God" as a "massively important" truth. [^4]

## Supporting Evidence (and steelman of the opposing view)

1. **It follows directly from rejecting ex nihilo.** Because "the distinction between created and uncreated beings... doesn't exist in our world," everything is on one ontological continuum differing only in "what type of existence you have." [^5] *Rating: internally consistent* — the same-kind claim is the anthropological face of [[Creatio Ex Nihilo Is the Lynchpin Difference]].

2. **"Intelligences" as eternal proto-persons.** Hansen describes a fundamental "spiritual realm" / "consciousness is fundamental," from which the physical realm is emergent, that "did not ever come into being." [^6] *Rating: a developed metaphysic* (idealist-leaning), explicitly flagged by Hansen as "deep water" and partly symbolic. [^7]

3. **Steelman of the classical-theism objection.** [[Gavin Ortlund]] affirms a "thick ontological distinction between God and everything that's not God." [^8] On his view God is *necessary being*; creatures are *contingent*; therefore creatures and Creator can never be the same kind, and theosis (if affirmed at all) means participation by grace, not identity of kind. *Rating: the strongest counter* — it denies the shared substrate the LDS claim requires.

4. **Ortlund's verdict: this is a paradigm departure, not a surprise.** Hearing Hansen's ontology, Ortlund says it "is not what I have previously understood of Mormon views of God" and is "sufficiently different from historic Christian views" that being classed as a departure "should not be a surprise." [^9][^10] *Rating: fair characterization* — both agree the gap is "gargantuan"; they disagree only on whether it is damning.

5. **Heiser caveat.** Hansen and Ortlund both invoke [[Michael Heiser]]: the biblical *elohim* can include other "gods," but Heiser calls Yahweh "species-unique." [^11] *Rating: Heiser is a double-edged citation* — he grants a divine council (helping the LDS) yet insists on Yahweh's uniqueness (helping Ortlund), illustrating that the same data underdetermines the ontology dispute.

## Best Quotes
> "We believe that we fundamentally are of the same kind as Jesus and we are like acorns where they are like oak trees." [^3]

> "The distinction between created and uncreated beings just... doesn't exist in our world. It's all a matter of progression and what type of existence you have." [^5]

> "You are not ontologically a different kind from God... it says something about who you are as a child of God." [^4]

> "There is a thick ontological distinction between God and everything that's not God." [^8]
