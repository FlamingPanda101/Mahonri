# Four-Tier Doctrinal Taxonomy

#type/rebuttal

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)

Critics treat every provocative statement in the [[King Follett Discourse]] as equally binding "Mormon doctrine," then indict the church for the most extreme line. [[Casey Paul Griffiths]] rebuts this by distinguishing four categories of doctrine, so that not every teaching carries the same weight or certainty.[^1]

## The Rebuttal

1. **The four tiers (Griffiths' framework).** (a) *Core eternal doctrines* — unchanging, linked to salvation; (b) *supporting doctrines* — somewhat more changeable and less essential; (c) *policies* — change all the time; (d) *esoteric doctrine* — "stuff that we don't have fully explained to us yet."[^2] This is a teaching heuristic, not an official church classification, so its authority is the speaker's own (tier B).
2. **Application to King Follett.** Exaltation ("men can become exalted and become like God") is core; "God was once a man" is esoteric — "we believe it but we don't know very much about it" (see [[God Was Once a Man Is Esoteric Not Core Doctrine]]).[^3] The taxonomy lets an apologist affirm the sermon's spine while bracketing its least-understood claim.
3. **Steelman of the critic.** The critic can fairly object that the line between "core" and "esoteric" is movable and convenient: a doctrine taught by the founding prophet at general conference and echoed in a popularized couplet does not obviously sit in the lowest tier. If "esoteric" simply means "embarrassing when pressed," the taxonomy risks being a rhetorical escape hatch rather than a principled distinction. Griffiths partly concedes this by admitting the once-a-man idea "is handled with care" precisely because of its theological cost.[^4]
4. **Evidence rating.** As philosophy of doctrine the taxonomy is reasonable and mirrors distinctions other traditions make (dogma vs. theologoumenon). But it is asserted, not derived from a canonical list, so it persuades only if one already accepts the speaker's authority to triage.

## Best Quotes

> "Let's take doctrines and let's put them in categories. There's core eternal doctrines... then there's supporting doctrines... then there's policies that change all the time in the church. And then there's esoteric doctrine, which is stuff that we don't have fully explained to us yet."[^5]

> "The idea that men can become exalted and become like God is probably the least controversial idea in the sermon... but the idea that God was once a man is an esoteric teaching that we believe but we don't know very much about if we're being totally honest with ourselves."[^6]
