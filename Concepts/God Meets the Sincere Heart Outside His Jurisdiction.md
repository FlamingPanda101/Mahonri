# God Meets the Sincere Heart Outside His Jurisdiction

#type/claim

## The Claim

A soteriology that weights **heart posture over doctrinal precision** lowers the bar for "Christian" to a place Latter-day Saints clear — and an LDS apologist can affirm it with a near-identical principle. The Evangelical student **Titha** grounds her concession (see [[Define Christian First and the Concession Follows]]) on a sermon about Solomon, who "had a heart for God, but he still worshiped on the high places" — places of idol worship, "outside of his jurisdiction" — and yet "God still met him on the high place... because we have the correct heart posture." [^1] She generalizes: "as humans, we get caught up in details that actually don't have anything to do with salvation," and "I'm not the judge... God knows," so "it truly doesn't matter if I think you're a Christian or not." [^2] [[Hayden Carroll]] does not contest this; he **matches** it: Latter-day Saints "hold a similar stance" — "if people are actually doing their best, if they're not fighting against what they know is truth... they're going to be okay," since the unknowing cannot be held accountable. [^3]

The note records a point of **convergence** rather than a clash: the heart-posture principle is the soteriological premise that makes the [[Define Christian First and the Concession Follows|definitional concession]] feel costless to the Evangelical, and the LDS "doing their best / not fighting known truth" doctrine is its functional twin.

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Titha states the principle in a hard case (scriptural exemplar).** Solomon is the maximal test — knowingly worshipping idols "on the high places" while being "the wisest king" — yet on her telling God "still chooses to meet us because we have the correct heart posture." [^1] The strength of using Solomon is that it is *not* a case of innocent ignorance; it concedes wrong *action* while preserving relationship through orientation of the heart. *Strength: rhetorically strong as a within-Protestant warrant; exegetically contestable — the Deuteronomistic narrator frames the high places as a real failure, and Solomon's later apostasy (1 Kings 11) cuts against treating it as costless.*

2. **It dissolves the "details" that the cluster fights over (structural).** If denominational distinctives "don't have anything to do with salvation," then so does the Trinity — which Titha already classes among "the other stuff" [^4]. The principle thus does the same work as the explicit Bible-only definition, from the *soteriological* side rather than the *lexical* side: it removes the motive to police the boundary at the creed. *Strength: strong for *this* interlocutor; a confessional Protestant denies that the identity of God is a mere "detail."*

3. **The LDS twin is doctrinally anchored, not ad hoc (corroboration).** Carroll's "not fighting against what they know is truth" reply [^3] is a standard Latter-day Saint formulation of accountability-tracks-knowledge (cf. the postmortal second chance he describes earlier in the same conversation, where one who never knowingly rejected Christ may still accept Him). That the two traditions land on the *same* sincere-effort standard is what lets the concession be mutual rather than a one-sided rhetorical trap. *Strength: solid as description of the convergence; it does not by itself adjudicate whose underlying theology is correct.*

**Steelman of the opposing view (the confessional reply).** A creedal critic answers that "heart posture over doctrine" is precisely the error — it makes sincerity, not the object of faith, the saving thing, so that "I've seen God with my own eyes... to disprove all that just because his moral code doesn't align with mine feels very prideful" [^5] becomes unfalsifiable and can sanctify *any* devout commitment, including a false one. This is the boundary problem the vault raises against experiential criteria in [[A Feeling-Defined God Cannot Adjudicate Contradictions]]: a sincere heart cannot adjudicate between contradictory accounts of who God is, so it cannot certify that the worship reaches the true God. On this view Titha's own father — who "truly wanted to believe in Mormonism" but found it self-contradictory [^6] — illustrates that sincerity and doctrinal truth can come apart, and that the latter is what "Christian" properly tracks. The principle therefore yields *charity in judgment* (leave it to God) without establishing the *truth claim* (Mormons worship the same God) — and Carroll's matching standard, while irenic, likewise trades on leaving the verdict to God rather than demonstrating it.

## Best Quotes

> "His actions were out of line, but his heart was for God. And God is so loving that even when we are outside of his jurisdiction... he still chooses to meet us because we have the correct heart posture." [^1]

> "It truly doesn't matter if I think you're a Christian or not, because God knows." [^2]

> "If people are actually doing their best, if they're not fighting against what they know is truth... they're going to be okay." [^3]
