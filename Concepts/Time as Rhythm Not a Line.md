# Time as Rhythm Not a Line

## The Point
Greeks experience time as a *line* of discrete atomized moments (past gone, present now, future ahead). Hebrews experience time as a *rhythm* — circular, musical. On the rhythmic model, a present act of repentance can retroactively "rewrite" the meaning of the past, which dissolves several puzzles about forgiveness and how forgiven sins can "return."

## Argument
1. **The Greek model: time as a line of atoms.** Past is fixed-past, future is future, the present is a tick on a line — each moment a discrete "atom" of time. This is "malarkey," the talk argues — it is *our* inherited intuition, not a necessary truth [^1].
2. **Semitic verbs reveal a different time-sense.** Perfective/imperfective aspect (also in Spanish/Russian): "I am calling" answers "did you call your mother?" by meaning *the action is unfinished* — not a yes/no point but an ongoing reality. "I go to school" means "I am a student," not "I am walking there now." A native speaker drew the difference as a *dot* (completed) versus a *line* (a span). Time is experienced as duration and aspect, not as a yes/no atom [^2].
3. **The musical model: tonal ambiguity.** In music, two notes without the defining third leave the chord ambiguous; your brain *assumes* a chord, and when the third note finally sounds it "goes back in time and rewrites what it thought it heard" (Beethoven and Tchaikovsky exploit this). Like learning a movie villain was the hero's friend — it "retroactively changes the narrative" [^3].
4. **Application to repentance.** "This is how Hebrews think of time — it's a rhythm." Adding "a note of repentance, a rhythm of repentance" reveals "ah, this is the melody — your story is one of redemption." But returning to a sinful melody re-reveals *that* as the dominant tune. This explains D&C 82:7 ("if you sin... they return back to you"): on a *line* that is impossible, but on a *rhythm* the meaning of the past is rewritten by the present [^4].
5. **The governing question.** Therefore the past count of failures "doesn't matter"; what matters is "what melody is your heart playing *right now*" — repentance or sin. Identity and forgiveness are about present orientation, not a linear ledger of completed acts (links to [[Repentance as Willingness Not a Cleared Ledger]]) [^5].

## Best Quotes
> "If time is a line then the past is past and the future is future. But if time is a circle, if time is a rhythm, then when you add a note of repentance... you realize, ah, this is the melody — your story is one of redemption." [^4]

> "When your brain finally hears that third note it goes back in time and rewrites what it thought it heard... this is how Hebrews think of time: it's a rhythm." [^3]
