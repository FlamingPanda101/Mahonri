# Literalism Is Ideological Not Textual

## The Claim

[[Dan McClellan]] distinguishes two things that share a name. **Textual literalism** — actually reading every text by its plain sense — yields contradictions, scientific errors, and historical claims that conflict with reality. **Ideological literalism** — what self-described literalists actually practice — starts from predetermined dogmas and reverse-engineers a reading that protects them. "We're starting from the dogmas and then we're constructing the rationale, the rationalization for the dogmas." [^1] This is the show's signature thesis, [[Putting Dogma Over Data]], applied to hermeneutics.

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Textual literalism self-destructs.** "Textual literalism results in acknowledging contradiction. It results in acknowledging scientific falsehoods. [It] results in acknowledging perspectives and facts and historical events that don't line up with what we know about reality." [^2] Because no inerrantist accepts these outcomes, their literalism cannot be the textual kind. **(Strong internal critique.)**
2. **The direction of reasoning runs dogma → text, not text → conclusion.** Six-day creation, a global flood, a historical Adam and Eve, and the [[Genesis 10|table of nations]] genealogies "were asserted as 100% true. Not because we had been studying it and we arrived at this conclusion, but because we needed to assert this as a rhetorical line in the sand." [^3] The conclusions precede the evidence. **(Strong.)**
3. **The tell is selective application to inconvenient texts.** Passages openly acknowledging other gods — the embarrassed report that Adonai "got beat by the god of another nation" in [[2 Kings 3.26-27|2 Kings 3]], the Egyptian magicians matching Yahweh's miracles in Exodus, the [[Deuteronomy 32.17|shedim]] — are read literally "until that point in which case those are just demons... or that's the devil." [^4][^5] The literal switch is flipped off precisely where doctrine requires. See [[Textual Literalism Forces Acknowledging Other Gods]]. **(Strong.)**
4. **Steelman.** A defender could say: every reader has *some* prior framework; expecting "presuppositionless" reading is naïve, and Christians simply read scripture within the rule of faith. McClellan's answer is not that frameworks are avoidable but that the inerrantist framework is **reactive and evidence-free** — "none of these things are based on any kind of evidence or pure logic or reason" [^1] — and that it dictates *to* the Bible what it is allowed to say rather than listening to it. See [[Literalism Collapses Into Authority of Tradition]]. **(Moderate — a sophisticated presuppositionalist would contest the "evidence-free" charge; the rebuttal is that the dogmas are held independent of, and against, the textual data.)**

## Best Quotes

> "We're starting from the dogmas and then we're constructing the rationale, the rationalization for the dogmas." [^1]

> "I guess it's all literal until that point in which case those are just demons and... that's the devil or whatever." [^5]
