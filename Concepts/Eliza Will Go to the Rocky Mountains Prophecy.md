# Eliza Will Go to the Rocky Mountains Prophecy

## The Claim
Around late April/early May 1832, visiting Jackson County, Missouri, [[Joseph Smith]] reportedly blessed five-year-old Paulina Eliza Phelps and said she "should live to go to the Rocky Mountains." She later recalled, "I did not know at the time what the term Rocky Mountains meant." Drawn from Brian Stutzman's book.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Corroborates the broader [[Rocky Mountains Migration Prophecy]].** The child's blessing is one of several threads suggesting Joseph spoke of the West. (Evidence rating: weak as a standalone item; minor corroboration at best.)[^1]
2. **A childhood memory recorded ~70 years later.** The event is dated 1832 but the affidavit is "now being recorded like 1903," and the witness "was 5 years old at the time." The host: "it's very easy for people to have distorted memories... I am skeptical of these sorts of affidavits." (Evidence rating: very weak; see [[The Late-Reminiscence Problem in Joseph Smith Prophecy Claims]].)[^2]
3. **The detail that flags reconstruction.** "I did not know at the time what the term Rocky Mountains meant" reads as later framing to underscore the prophecy's improbability — a hallmark of reminiscence shaped by hindsight. (Evidence rating: weak; suggests memory colored by outcome.)[^1]
4. **Steelman of the believing view.** If a small child genuinely retained even a vague "Rocky Mountains" blessing that later came true, it is touching corroboration of the migration prophecy cluster. But the seventy-year gap and the witness's age make this, in the host's own words, a source he is "very skeptical of."

## Best Quotes
> "Joseph said that I should live to go to the Rocky Mountains. I did not know at the time what the term Rocky Mountains meant."[^1]
