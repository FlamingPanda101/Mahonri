# Isaiah 43:10 — "Before Me There Was No God Formed"

## The Charge
> "God says there's no God formed before or after Him. Mormonism teaches humans can
> become gods — this is blasphemy and contradicts the Bible."

## The Verse (KJV)
> "Ye are my witnesses, saith the LORD, and my servant whom I have chosen: that ye
> may know and believe me, and understand that I am he: before me there was no God
> formed, neither shall there be after me." (Isaiah 43:10)

## Rebuttal Points

### 1. Context: Idolatry, Not Ontology
- Isaiah 40–48 is a sustained polemic against **idolatry** — Israel surrounded by
  nations worshiping Baal, Asherah, Moloch, and the gods of Babylon.
- YHWH isn't making an ontological claim ("no other divine beings exist"); He's
  making a **functional** claim: "None of those other gods can save you. I am the
  only one."
- Dalton: "He just went in and lined up all the culprits... and was like, 'How did
  any of those save you? No, I'm the one standing. I'm your God. I am the only one
  that can take your sin.'"

### 2. The Word "Formed" (Hebrew *yatsar*)
- *Yatsar* means "fashioned" or "shaped" — the same verb used for a potter forming
  clay (Isaiah 64:8).
- The "gods" being referenced are the ones the nations literally **chiseled out of
  stone** and **fashioned from pottery** — manufactured idols.
- Dalton: "The gods that they were worshiping were ones they chiseled out of stone,
  they formed their own gods literally."
- YHWH is saying: no manufactured god preceded Me or will outlast Me — not a
  statement about divine beings in general.

### 3. Immediate Context Reinforces This
Isaiah 43:11: "I, even I, am the LORD; and beside me there is no **saviour**."
The "no other god" claim is about **saviorship**, not ontological exclusivity.

Isaiah 44:6-8 has the same pattern: YHWH mocks idols that can't predict the future
or save — the context is always about function, not population count of divine beings.

### 4. The Bible Itself Uses "Gods" Language
- Psalm 82:1 — "God standeth in the congregation of the mighty; he judgeth among
  the **gods**."
- Psalm 82:6 — "I have said, Ye are **gods**; and all of you are children of the
  most High."
- Jesus quotes this in John 10:34-36 to defend His own divinity claim.

The biblical text itself uses *elohim* language for beings other than YHWH.

### 5. Michael Jordan Analogy
Cardon: "It's like Michael Jordan — the NBA was just different when he was in. He's
the greatest. There never will be anybody greater before him or after him."
LeBron James as "proof text" for Isaiah 43:10 — the claim is about preeminence and
uniqueness of role, not exclusive existence.

## Bottom Line
Isaiah 43:10 is a polemic against manufactured idols — not a declaration about the
maximum possible number of divine beings. The Hebrew *yatsar* (formed/fashioned)
and the surrounding context of idol polemic make this clear.
