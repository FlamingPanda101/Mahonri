# The HANDS Acronym Does Not Prove Deity

#type/claim

## The Claim
Christian apologist [[Wes Huff]], citing Rob Bowman and Ed Komoszewski's *Putting Jesus in His Place* / *Christ and His Critics*, argues for the deity of Christ (and the Spirit) with the acronym **HANDS**: the Father, the Son, and the Spirit are each given the **H**onors, **A**ttributes, **N**ames, **D**eeds, and **S**eat of God — and "those are things that are only given to God."[^1] [[Dan McClellan]] grants that the data exist but denies the inference. He raises **two problems**: (1) inferring that a New Testament figure who bears these prerogatives *must be God* presupposes [[Pan-Canonical Univocality Is Invalid Interpretation|univocality]] across texts written centuries apart; and (2) the Hebrew Bible, Second Temple, New Testament, and rabbinic literature *already grant the very same prerogatives to entities that are not God*, with an explanation that "has nothing to do with the philosophical framework of the Trinity."[^2]

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. Problem one: the inference smuggles in univocality — rating: strong (methodological)
To say "only God has these things in the Hebrew Bible, therefore anyone who has them in the New Testament must be God" assumes the later texts "are not allowed to disagree with the text of the Hebrew Bible."[^2] This is the same presupposition McClellan attacks throughout — that every biblical author shares one conceptual framework. *Rating: strong as a logical point; the HANDS argument does silently require cross-canonical uniformity to license the move from "God's prerogatives" to "is God."* See [[Pan-Canonical Univocality Is Invalid Interpretation]].

### 2. Problem two: the prerogatives are already democratized to non-God entities — rating: strong (textual)
The "honors, attributes, names, deeds, seat" are granted to figures who are *not* identified as the one God:
- **The messenger / angel of Yahweh** acts with divine prerogatives and is even called God (Exodus 3; Genesis 16, 22; Judges 2, 6, 13), explained by "my name is in him" ([[Exodus 23.20-21|Exodus 23:21]]).[^3] See [[The Messenger of Yahweh Explicitly Identifies as God]].
- **Yahoel** (Apocalypse of Abraham) and **Metatron** (b. Sanhedrin) bear the ineffable name and can be called Adonai.[^4] See [[Metatron and the Divine Name Are the Taproot of Christology]].
- **The Seat is explicitly democratized to humans.** In Revelation, "the one who overcomes, I will grant to sit in my throne" ([[Revelation 3.21|Rev 3:21]]); people "worship before" the overcomers; the Son of Man is worshiped by everyone in the Parables of Enoch; and in Ezekiel the Tragedian, Moses is seated on a "high and lofty throne" and given a scepter and diadem.[^5] *Rating: strong — the "S" of HANDS, the hardest prerogative to share, is the one McClellan shows being handed to glorified humans.*

### 3. The unifying explanation is name-bearing agency, not consubstantiality — rating: strong
All five prerogatives are "communicable to other entities that are not God through this rationalization of the name as a communicable vehicle of divine agency and authority and presence."[^6] McClellan develops this fully in his open-access monograph *YHWH's Divine Images*. The NT applies the *same* logic to Jesus — the "name above every name" (Philippians 2), "your name which you have given me" (John), the encoded "I am" of [[John 8.58|John 8:58]] — so the HANDS data are evidence of **name-bearing mediation**, not of Trinitarian identity.[^7] See [[Name-Bearing Agency in Exodus 23]] and [[Partible Personhood and the Logic of Divine Images]].

### Steelman (Bowman, Komoszewski, and Huff)
The strongest form of HANDS is not "X has a divine attribute, therefore X is God," but a **cumulative-case "divine identity" argument** (cf. Richard Bauckham): the New Testament places Jesus on the *creator* side of the creator/creature line — he shares the divine throne, receives the worship reserved for the one God (and *refuses* none of it, unlike the angel of [[Revelation 22.8-9|Rev 22:8-9]] who *forbids* worship), bears the *unshared* name "LORD" (Phil 2:9-11 applying Isa 45:23), and is the agent of creation (John 1:3; Col 1:16). On this reading the mediator figures McClellan cites are *creatures*, while Jesus is included *within* the unique identity of Yahweh — so the convergence of *all five* HANDS categories on Jesus exceeds anything granted to Metatron or the overcomers. A defender adds that worship of the agent who bears the Name is precisely what marks Jesus out, since Second Temple agents are characteristically *not* worshiped.

## LDS Relevance
McClellan's rebuttal is congenial to the LDS [[The Godhead Model vs the Trinity Model|Godhead]] model without being an LDS argument. If the honors, attributes, names, deeds, and *seat* of God can be conferred on entities who remain distinct from the Father — including **glorified humans** enthroned alongside Christ (Rev 3:21) — then bearing divine prerogatives does not collapse a being into the one divine *ousia*. This is the structural space the LDS view occupies: a [[Divine Council and Deification Were Native to the Hebrew Bible|divine council]] of distinct beings unified by shared divine name, glory, and authority rather than by numerical sameness of substance. The "you shall sit with me in my throne" promise to overcomers reads naturally as the deification/exaltation trajectory ([[Psalm 82 Is About Divine Council Not Human Judges|cf. Psalm 82]]) the LDS tradition affirms.

## Best Quotes
> "One of my favorite ways of describing this is by the scholars Rob Bowman and Ed Komoszewski… the acronym HANDS, that the Father, the Son, and the Spirit are all given the honors, attributes, names, deeds, and seat of God. And those are things that are only given to God." [^1]

> "We already have those things being granted to other entities that are not God — in the Hebrew Bible, in Greco-Roman period Jewish literature, in the New Testament, as well as in later rabbinic literature. And there's already an explanation for how that works. And it has nothing to do with the philosophical framework of the Trinity." [^2]

> "In Revelation, you have Jesus saying, 'The one who overcomes, I will grant to sit in my throne.' So the S part of that acronym, seat of God, that's going to be democratized to humans as well." [^5]
