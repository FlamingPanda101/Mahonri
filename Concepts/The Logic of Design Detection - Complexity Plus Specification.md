# The Logic of Design Detection - Complexity Plus Specification

## The Point
Drawing on mathematician [[William Dembski]]'s *The Design Inference*, Meyer argues that there is a rigorous, repeatable method for detecting design: any system that jointly exhibits *high complexity* (small probability / an irregular, aperiodic arrangement) **and** *specification* (a match to an independently recognizable pattern or set of functional requirements) invariably results from an intelligent cause, never from chance or physical-chemical law.[^1] Because DNA's coding regions exhibit both properties, this method confirms that the information in DNA points to prior intelligent activity.[^2]

## Evidence
1. **Design detection is an ordinary rational practice.** Archaeologists assume rational agents produced the Rosetta Stone's inscriptions; fraud investigators detect "cheating patterns"; cryptographers distinguish random signals from encoded messages. Recognizing the activity of intelligent agents is "a common and fully rational mode of inference."[^3]
2. **The two joint criteria.** *Complexity* = an irregular, improbable arrangement that defies a simple rule or algorithm; *specification* = a correspondence between the system and an independent pattern or set of functional requirements. Only their conjunction signals design.[^4]
3. **The three-sequence illustration.** (a) `nehya53nslbyw1...` is complex but not specified; (b) `TIME AND TIDE WAIT FOR NO MAN` is both complex and specified — it matches English vocabulary and grammar, so it performs a communication function; (c) `ABABABAB...` is specified (orderly) but not complex. Only sequence (b) implicates an intelligent cause.[^5]
4. **DNA satisfies both criteria.** In *Signature in the Cell* Meyer shows that Dembski's joint criteria are equivalent to "functional/specified information," and that DNA's coding regions exhibit both high complexity and specification — hence design.[^2]
5. **Not a god-of-the-gaps argument.** The inference rests not on ignorance but on positive scientific discoveries about DNA plus "established scientific methods of reasoning in which our uniform experience of cause and effect guides our inferences."[^6]

## Best Quotes
> "Systems or sequences with the joint properties of 'high complexity' (or small probability) and 'specification' invariably result from intelligent causes, not from chance or physical-chemical laws."[^4]

> "The theory of intelligent design is not based upon ignorance or gaps in our knowledge, but on scientific discoveries about DNA and on established scientific methods of reasoning."[^6]

## LDS Connection
The "not god-of-the-gaps" framing parallels a caution voiced within LDS apologetics that arguments resting on present ignorance are weak; see [[Miracle-Tests for God Commit the God-of-the-Gaps Fallacy]]. The complexity-plus-specification argument also echoes the LDS-apologetic use of the [[Infinite Monkey Theorem Against Random Chiasmus|infinite-monkey rebuttal]] — that undirected randomness cannot generate ordered, functional information.
