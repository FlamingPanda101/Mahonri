# Sense Data Is Filtered Through a Valuing Self

## The Point

Against the empiricist intuition that raw sense data is the foundation of knowledge, the host argues sense data **never arrives in a vacuum**: it must be filtered, selected, and intuited by a self/consciousness oriented by a hierarchy of values. He uses [[Jordan Peterson]]'s account of the brain simplifying an over-complex reality, plus the *invisible gorilla / Monkey Business* selective-attention experiment, to show perception is value-laden from the start.[^1][^2][^3]

## Argument

1. **Reality is too complex to perceive whole.** Peterson: the brain's central problem is that "reality is so complicated ... complex beyond comprehension," so the nervous system must "ignore almost everything" to act at all.[^1]
2. **Perception is already heavily filtered, biologically and attentionally.** We sense only a sliver of the electromagnetic spectrum, lack senses other animals have, and — the *Monkey Business* illusion shows — fail to notice a gorilla, a departing player, and a curtain changing color while counting passes. We are "narrowed in what we can perceive far more than anybody ever guessed."[^2]
3. **What we attend to is set by values, not the data.** Why did counting the passes capture attention at all? Because the instruction "taps into a pre-existent value structure" — wanting to be competent, to "get the answer right." Attention is motivated; the salient subset of reality is selected by the self's goals.[^3] *(Steelman: this aligns with mainstream cognitive science — attention and perception are top-down and predictive; pure "given" sense data is a myth.)*
4. **Therefore sense data is not foundational.** "Sense data does not exist in a vacuum — the raw data itself must interface with a subject, a self or a consciousness that intuits this data in relationship to a value structure ... or maybe what you call Spirit."[^3] So even empiricism's candidate foundation presupposes a valuing, intuiting self. *(Caveat: the slide from "perception is value-laden" to "the self is Spirit" is the host's confessional addition, not entailed by the cognitive-science material.)*

## Best Quotes

> "Reality is so complicated, it has so many layers and so many interconnected causal links that it's complex beyond comprehension ... you have to screen all of that out."[^1]

> "We're actually narrowed in what we can perceive far more than anybody ever guessed."[^2]

> "Sense data does not exist in a vacuum — the raw data itself must interface with a subject, a self or a consciousness that intuits this data in relationship to a value structure based on the goals of this self ... or maybe what you call Spirit."[^3]
