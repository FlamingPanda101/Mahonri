# The Quran's Testimony to the Previous Scriptures

#type/claim

## The Claim
The first horn of [[The Islamic Dilemma]] rests on a positive premise: that the Qur'an itself testifies to the Torah and Gospel as authentic, authoritative, divine revelation — and treats them as *present and usable* in Muhammad's own time, not as a lost ideal. [[Avery (God Logic)|Avery]] assembles a chain of texts to establish this:

> "The Quran repeatedly, over and over and over again, speaks well of the previous scriptures of the Bible as revelation, as preserved revelation and authentic revelation. Even in Muhammad's time... it says that the Quran is sent down confirming what is with you."[^1]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **Commands to judge by the Torah and Gospel.** [[Quran 5.47|5:47]]: "Let the people of the Gospel judge by what Allah has revealed in it; and if you don't, you are of the wrongdoers." [[Quran 5.43|5:43]]: "Why do they come to you [Muhammad] for judgment when they have the Torah, in which is Allah's judgment?"[^2] Avery's point: you cannot be commanded to *judge by* a book unless that book is both available and trustworthy — which forecloses the "it was already corrupt/lost" escape for the 7th century.
2. **The *musaddiq* ("confirming") formula.** The Qur'an describes itself as "confirming what is with you" (e.g. [[Quran 2.85|2:41]]). Avery reads this as the Qur'an vouching that the scriptures *the Jews and Christians then possessed* were genuine.[^1] *(Muslim steelman: "confirming" need only mean confirming the prophetic message/monotheism the prior books originally carried, not endorsing every clause of the received text — a reading classical commentators also entertain.)*
3. **The prophets' covenant — [[Quran 3.81|3:81]].** God takes a covenant from the prophets: a coming messenger will "confirm what is with you," and they must believe and help him. Avery argues the *identification mechanism* presupposes a reliable prior text: the prophets recognize the true messenger precisely because he confirms (not contradicts) what they already have.[^3] See [[Surah 3-55 The Followers of Jesus Remain Superior Until the Resurrection]] for the parallel "preservation" inference.
4. **The non-supersession argument.** Against the reply "the Qur'an is here now, so the prior books are obsolete," Avery presses [[Quran 5.43|5:43]] ("they have the Torah... they don't need to come to you"): the Qur'an still *defers* to the Torah/Gospel as carrying Allah's judgments, which (he says) "knocks this concept of 'we have the Quran now, so we don't need the previous scriptures.'"[^4]
5. **Steelman of the Muslim position overall.** A consistent Muslim can grant that the Qur'an *honors* the prior revelations in their original form while holding that (a) the received Torah/Gospel has since suffered *tahrif* (see [[The Tahrif Debate Among Classical Muslim Scholars]]), and/or (b) the Qur'an functions as *muhaymin* — guardian and corrective — over them (see [[The Guardian and Criterion Reply to the Islamic Dilemma]]). The whole force of the dilemma turns on whether those two moves can be made *without any Qur'anic text licensing selective acceptance* — which is exactly where Avery presses [[Quran 2.85|2:85]].
