# Brethren Chiasmus in Alma 24

## The Claim
[[Jonah (Plain and Precious Publishing)|Jonah]] argues that the word **"brethren"** forms a *second*, larger chiasm in King [[Anti-Nephi-Lehi]]'s speech ([[Alma 24.7|Alma 24:7]] ff.), distinct from the [[Stain Chiasmus in Alma 24|stain chiasm]]. It marches "five in and then five out" and turns on the same central verse, [[Alma 24.12-13|Alma 24:12-13]]. The referent of "brethren" shifts symmetrically: on the way in the brethren are the **Nephites** who came to convert them; at the center they are the kin whose blood must not be spilt; on the way out the brethren are the **Lamanites** they refuse to kill [^1][^2].

## Supporting Evidence
1. **Semantic inversion across the axis.** The cleverest feature claimed is that the *meaning* of "brethren" flips around the pivot (Nephite brethren → blood-of-brethren → Lamanite brethren), which Jonah treats as evidence of intentional design rather than mere word-repetition [^2]. *Rating: moderate — if real, a meaning-inversion at the axis is a more sophisticated marker than bare keyword repetition.*
2. **Double-peak structure.** Jonah says the center contains "the two brethrens in that verse" — i.e., the axis is itself a small doublet ("blood of our brethren... blood of our brethren"), which he reads as the climax both chiasms share [^3].
3. **Steelman of the skeptic.** "Brethren" is a pervasive Book-of-Mormon term and a natural address-word in any tribal speech; counting "five in, five out" requires the analyst to choose which occurrences count and where boundaries fall. Without an independent stichometry, the symmetry risks being [[Pareidolia Objection to Chiasmus Finding|imposed]] by selective charting. Co-host [[Cardon Ellis]] himself raises the "are we seeing chiasms everywhere now?" worry (the Trader Joe's pita-and-Takis gag) [^4].
4. **Dependence on a proprietary chart.** The structure is shown via a Scripture Notes slide, not laid out verse-by-verse in the audio, so the listener cannot independently verify the bracketing — a transparency weakness [^5].
