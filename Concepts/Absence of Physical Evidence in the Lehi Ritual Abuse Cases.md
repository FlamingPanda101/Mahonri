# Absence of Physical Evidence in the Lehi Ritual Abuse Cases

## The Claim
The Lehi accusations rested entirely on therapist-elicited testimony, with no physical evidence — an implausibility given the scale of the alleged crimes.

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. Mass allegations, zero corroboration (strong)
Police were "confused how such a huge portion of the neighborhood was actively raping children but left no physical evidence behind. Nothing more than the word of the therapists." 40 adults in one ward stood accused [^1].

### 2. Children recanted under oath (strong)
One child testified under oath she had been "intimidated by Snow into offering an accusation"; another child called the whole scandal a "fairy tale concocted to satisfy Snow," as reported by William Grigg of *Chronicles Magazine* via Snow colleague Judy Pew [^2].

### 3. Official conclusion: no abuse occurred (strong)
"Accumulated misgivings about Snow's reliability coupled with an utter paucity of physical evidence led the Utah County Sheriff's Office to conclude that no abuse had taken place in Lehi." Family services returned the Burnham children to their parents [^3].

## Steelman (Opposing View)
Defenders note that much child sexual abuse leaves no lasting physical evidence and that recantation can result from family or community pressure on victims. True in general — but the scale here (dozens of perpetrators, ritual elements, satanic cult initiation) would be expected to leave corroborating traces, and the recantations specifically named coercion by the interviewer, not by accused family members.
