# Hell — The Free Will Defense Rebutted

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
The strongest defense: God gives humans free will, and hell merely **honors their own choice** to reject him. [[C.S. Lewis]]: "the doors of hell are locked from the inside." Hell isn't punishment but respect for freedom.

## The Rebuttal

### 1. Creation undercuts the freedom
If God, with perfect foreknowledge, creates me from nothing with exactly the attributes I have and sustains them, am I really free? The **video-game-character analogy**: you set the character's sliders (strength, intelligence, propensity to faith) and power the console — the character isn't free in any meaningful sense. God could have "turned up the slider" of devotion [^1].

### 2. A loving parent wouldn't honor such a choice
The **eternal-conscious-torment-machine analogy**: if your child freely chose to enter a machine of unending psychological torment, no loving parent would honor that choice — especially given the child's limited knowledge. Misbehavior may warrant *some* correction, but never honoring an *eternal, irreversible* self-harm after a finite mistake [^2].

## Best Quote
> "How could a parent be justified in allowing eternal horrifying effects to play out after a finite misbehavior?" [^2]
