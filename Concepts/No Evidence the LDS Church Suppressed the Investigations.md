# No Evidence the LDS Church Suppressed the Investigations

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
Critics alleged the LDS Church pressured investigators to drop the ritual-abuse cases to avoid scandal — that the cases collapsed because of institutional cover-up rather than lack of evidence.

## The Rebuttal

### 1. A hostile witness reached the opposite conclusion (strong)
Sociologist [[Anson Shupe]], explicitly a critic of the LDS Church, investigated and found no such interference. As an adversarial source, his exoneration of the Church on this point carries unusual weight [^1].

### 2. Relief is not pressure (strong)
Shupe distinguished motive from action: "LDS Church leaders were undoubtedly relieved to see the controversy die. But on the other hand, there is no evidence that they played any role in discouraging further prosecution" [^2].

### 3. The cases failed on their own merits (strong)
"The scandal collapsed of its own weight, not on account of outside pressure." The collapse is fully explained by absent physical evidence, recanting children, and discredited interview methods — no external suppression is needed [^3].

### 4. The 2018 Nelson relitigation alleged coercion — investigators denied it under oath (strong — added from Part 5)
A 2018 suit (filed months after [[Russell M. Nelson]] became church president) re-alleged the [[Brenda Miles]] charges and accused Nelson by name of "using his position in the church to cover up investigation" and of pressuring "local law enforcement, state departments, judges, lawyers, and even the Federal Bureau of Investigations." Three investigators rebutted this directly: [[Michael George]] (Salt Lake County Attorney's Office) — "No one from the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints attempted to influence or interfere with our investigation in any way"; [[Diana Stevens]] (Bountiful PD) — "No church officials attempted to interfere with the criminal investigations"; and [[Leslie Lewis]] (Salt Lake County) — "No church officials attempted to influence their work." The judge dismissed the case *with prejudice* [^4][^5]. See [[Accusations Cannot Transmute Into Evidence]].

### 5. The cover-up premise rests on a degrees-of-separation artifact (strong — added from Part 5)
The only "link" tying Nelson to the dismissal is that the lawyer who drafted the (granted) motion to dismiss once belonged to the same University of Utah honor society as Nelson 70 years earlier — not evidence of interference. See [[The Nelson Skull and Bones Claim Confuses Utah's Owl and Key With Yale]] [^6].
