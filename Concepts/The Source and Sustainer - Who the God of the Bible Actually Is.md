# The Source and Sustainer - Who the God of the Bible Actually Is

## The Point
Before arguing *that* God exists, Turek insists on clarifying *which* God is in question, because the New Atheists reject "not the actual God of the Bible" but "a caricature of Him" — a finite superhero "akin to Zeus or Thor," a gap-filler inside the universe.[^1] Dawkins' quip that he just goes "one god further" than the believer (rejecting Zeus, Apollo, Thor, etc.) only "highlights his ignorance of biblical theism," because the God of the Bible "is not a created being among other beings inside the universe. He is Being itself and transcends the entire created order!"[^2] Turek's preferred handle is **the Source and Sustainer of all things** — the self-existing, uncaused ground and ongoing cause of everything that exists.[^3]

## Evidence
Turek lists the attributes of the Source and Sustainer, drawn from classical philosophy and theology (he invokes Aristotle's "unmoved mover" and Augustine, Aquinas, and Anselm):[^4] **self-existing** (uncaused, the foundation of all being), **infinite**, **simple** (undivided, "not made up of parts"), **immaterial** (spirit, "not made of matter"), **spaceless**, **timeless** ("had no beginning and will have no end"), **omnipotent**, **omnipresent**, **omniscient**, **immutable** ("changeless; the anchor and standard by which everything else is measured"), **holy**, and **personal** (mind, emotion, and will).[^5] These, he says, are "coexistent, infinite, and unified in the Source and Sustainer."[^6] His apologetic payoff: "Dawkins is knocking over a straw god," so even if *The God Delusion* sinks Zeus and Thor, its arguments "don't get within a thousand miles of the God of the Bible."[^7] He grants that many *Christians* also mishandle the doctrine, imagining God as "a big angel or just a bigger version of themselves," and urges readers to "renew your mind" by removing all limits from the concept.[^8] God, he adds, reveals Himself through "two books: the book of nature (which everyone has) and the Bible."[^9]

## Best Quotes
> "God is not a created being among other beings inside the universe. He is Being itself and transcends the entire created order! He is the ultimate and sustaining cause of all created things, including angels, human beings, and the material world we call the universe." [^2]

> "Dawkins is knocking over a straw god, not the self-existing, eternal, immaterial, simple, all-powerful God of the Bible." [^7]

> "Immutable: changeless; the anchor and standard by which everything else is measured." [^5]

## LDS Connection
Turek's apologetic *move* is one Latter-day Saints can applaud: the New Atheists routinely attack an idol and declare victory over God, and clearing away the "big-angel-in-the-sky" caricature is healthy. Restoration scripture likewise affirms God as self-existing, all-knowing, all-powerful, the sustaining source of all things, personal, holy, and worthy of worship. The point that God is known through both "the book of nature" and revelation harmonizes with Alma 30:44 ("all things denote there is a God").

But this note marks the **clearest doctrinal divergence in the whole book**, because Turek's specific attribute list is the *classical-theist* (Aristotelian–Thomistic) package, and several items collide head-on with the Restoration:
- **Immaterial / spaceless.** The Restoration teaches that "The Father has a body of flesh and bones as tangible as man's" (D&C 130:22) and that "There is no such thing as immaterial matter. All spirit is matter" (D&C 131:7–8). For Latter-day Saints God is embodied and located, not classically immaterial.
- **Simple.** Divine simplicity (God "not made up of parts") is a metaphysical commitment LDS theology does not hold and which sits awkwardly with an embodied, personal God and a plurality of divine Persons.
- **Timeless / immutable / had no beginning.** The Restoration's King Follett trajectory holds that God is the *exalted* head of an eternal order who progressed to His state, that intelligences are co-eternal and uncreated, and that God organizes pre-existing matter rather than creating from nothing (Abraham 3:18–22; D&C 93:29). See [[God Was Once a Man]] and [[Spirit Birth and Intelligences]].
- **"Being itself" / ultimate cause of all.** Turek frames God as the cause of *all* created things, which in his system means *creation ex nihilo*; LDS theology instead has God organizing co-eternal matter and intelligences, so He is supreme over reality without being the sole uncaused ground of literally everything.

The fair summary: against atheism, Turek is an ally and his "don't attack a caricature" instinct is sound — but his portrait of God *is itself* a particular (creedal) theology, and a Latter-day Saint should affirm the self-existing, personal, sustaining God while declining the divine-simplicity-and-immateriality specifics. The one point Turek, Dawkins, and the Restoration can all agree on is the one he names himself: idols don't really exist.[^10]
