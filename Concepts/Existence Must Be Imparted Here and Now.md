# Existence Must Be Imparted Here and Now

## The Point
Because a thing's essence does not contain its existence, that existence must be *imparted* to it — and not merely once, at the moment it came to be, but **at every instant it exists, here and now**. [^1] A thing cannot impart existence to its own essence, for to cause anything it must first exist; "nothing can be the cause of its own existence." [^2] So the existence of every essence-existence-composite thing must be continually caused by something outside it. The dependence is far more radical than common sense suggests: Fido needs a cause not only to be born but to keep from "blink[ing] out" of existence this very moment. [^3]

## Evidence
1. **A thing cannot give itself existence.** "A thing can't impart or add something, or indeed do anything at all... unless it first exists. But a thing whose essence and existence are distinct cannot exist until existence is added or imparted to its essence." To cause its own existence "it would have to exist before it exists" — incoherent. [^4]
2. **Nor does existence follow from the essence.** Unlike a circle's circumference, which follows from being a circle, a lion's existence does not follow from being a lion — you can grasp the essence without knowing whether any lion exists. And if existence *did* follow from the essence, the thing would exist necessarily, not contingently. [^5]
3. **The dependence is present-tense, not past-tense.** "Everything said so far applies to a thing not only before it comes into being and as it comes into being but always, even after it has come into being." Fido's existence "here and now is distinct from his essence and doesn't follow from his essence. So, here and now there must be some cause which adds or imparts existence to that essence. Otherwise Fido wouldn't exist here and now any more than he did before he was conceived." [^6]
4. **This launches a hierarchical (not temporal) series.** If the cause C that imparts existence to Fido is itself an essence-existence composite, C needs its own here-and-now imparter B, and B needs A, and so on — a series whose members have no power to impart existence "independently of some prior cause." [^7] (Why it cannot regress infinitely is taken up in [[The Conserving Cause - An Infinite Series of IOUs Cannot Make Real Money]].)

## Best Quotes
> "Nothing can be the cause of its own existence." [^2]

> "Fido's existence here and now is distinct from his essence and doesn't follow from his essence. So, here and now there must be some cause which adds or imparts existence to that essence. Otherwise Fido wouldn't exist here and now any more than he did before he was conceived." [^6]

> "Anything whose essence is distinct from its existence must have a cause of its existence at any moment that it exists, here and now and not merely at some point in the past." [^8]

## LDS Connection
This is one of Feser's strongest contributions against atheism, and it overlaps almost exactly with a theme Latter-day Saints can embrace: God is not a deist watchmaker who wound up the cosmos and walked away, but a present, sustaining power "right now" (compare [[God as the Present Sustaining Cause - The Band Plays On]] and Paul's "in him we live and move and have our being," Acts 17:28). The Restoration confesses a God whose light and power are "in all things, which giveth life to all things" (D&C 88). Two honest qualifications. First, what the LDS God sustains is *organized* reality — He gives form, life, and order to eternal, uncreated matter and intelligence, rather than holding bare *existence* in being from nothing (see [[Intelligence Is Uncreated and Eternally Has Agency]] and [[Creation Ex Nihilo Is Not Biblical]]). Second, Feser's "here and now imparter of existence" presupposes the essence/existence composition that, in the LDS view, does not apply to eternal intelligences. So the shared, defensible core is moment-by-moment dependence of the world on a present divine cause; the divergence is whether that dependence is dependence for *sheer existence* (Feser) or for *life, organization, and exaltation* (Restoration).
