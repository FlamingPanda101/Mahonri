# Literalism Produces Flat-Earthers as Its Logical End

## The Claim

[[Dan Beecher]] argues that hard literalism contains a runaway logic. Inerrantists "start with the notion of... dogmas that we are going to hold to," then craft a literalism that protects those dogmas against modern science — but the principle, once released, "gets pushed too far. Other people take that and run with it. And then we got flat-earthers again, which we didn't have any of." [^1][^2] Flat-earth and "firmament" believers are not a fringe deviation but "the natural conclusion to what they themselves have put in place." [^3]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The framers wanted only limited literalism.** The architects of inerrancy crafted "a sentiment about literalism that gives us space for these dogmas to stay real even in the face of modern science." [^1] They never intended flat-earthism; but their principle — scripture overrules science (see [[Chicago Statement on Inerrancy Redefines Literal]]) — licenses it. **(Strong as an internal-logic critique.)**
2. **The slippery slope is observed, not merely predicted.** "There's so many people on social media, the whole 'what the firmament' people... we've never been to the moon because you just bounce off the firmament." [^4][^5] Modern flat-earth/firmament communities apply the inerrantist principle consistently to biblical cosmology. **(Strong — phenomenon is real.)**
3. **It exposes the threshold problem.** Flat-earthers simply refuse to relabel the firmament texts as "figurative." If the framers may stop the literalism wherever doctrine is satisfied, flat-earthers may extend it wherever the text seems plain — and there is no principled, text-internal place to halt. See [[The Threshold Problem - Where to Draw the Literal-Figurative Line]] and [[Literalism Collapses Into Authority of Tradition]]. **(Strong.)**
4. **Steelman.** A mainstream inerrantist would object that this is a slippery-slope fallacy: responsible inerrancy uses grammatical-historical method and accommodation (phenomenological language of "sunrise," etc.), which rules out flat-earthism without abandoning scripture's truthfulness. McClellan/Beecher's reply is that "accommodation" and "phenomenological language" are themselves selective escape hatches deployed only when the plain sense embarrasses — the same move flat-earthers decline to make — so the boundary is drawn by tradition, not by the text. **(Moderate — slippery-slope critiques are contestable, but the point about an unprincipled stopping line stands.)**
