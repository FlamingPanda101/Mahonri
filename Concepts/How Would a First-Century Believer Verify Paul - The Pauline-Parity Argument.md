# How Would a First-Century Believer Verify Paul - The Pauline-Parity Argument

## The Claim
Hayden Carroll's central tu quoque against the demand "how do you test a modern prophet like Joseph Smith?" He answers with a parity case built on Paul. [[Paul the Apostle|Paul]] delivered genuinely **new revelation** — "bodies celestial and bodies terrestrial," the resurrection in differing glories ([[1 Corinthians 15.24-28|1 Corinthians 15]], vv. 40-42) — that is *not* found anywhere in the Hebrew scriptures. A first-century follower who had never met Jesus therefore could not validate Paul's teaching by checking it against an existing text; he could only accept it because **Paul was a recognized apostle bearing authority, plus the hearer's own faith / witness of the Spirit**. That is *exactly* the epistemic footing on which Latter-day Saints accept [[D&C 76|D&C 76]]'s three degrees of glory — an apostolic expansion of the very passage Paul began. So the Evangelical "gives first-century Christians grace" for accepting new apostolic revelation by authority-and-faith while denying that same grace to modern apostles. Either both moves are legitimate or neither is.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence (steelman)

### 1. 1 Corinthians 15 really is new content, not OT exegesis — rate: strong
Paul's celestial/terrestrial taxonomy and his "so also is the resurrection of the dead" go beyond anything in the Tanakh; he presents it on apostolic authority, and the Corinthians had no prior scripture to cross-check it against. The challenger concedes Paul is teaching "this new understanding of what takes place when we die… not recorded anywhere else."[^2] If acceptance-by-authority was valid then, the LDS appeal to [[Three Degrees of Glory in the Bible|the same three-glories framework]] in D&C 76 is structurally identical. See [[D&C 76 Dates the Distinct-Persons View to Early 1832]].

### 2. The challenger's own verification list is authority + faith — rate: strong
Asked how a first-century Christian would know Paul was true, the Evangelical's answer is essentially the LDS answer: Paul "is an apostle of Jesus Christ," "understood amongst the apostles to be in authority," and the rest is taken on faith.[^3] Hayden notes the asymmetry: had a Latter-day Saint said "an apostle taught it and I have faith," the challenger "would give me a hard time about it," yet extends precisely that grace to Paul.[^1] This is the burden-shifting pattern catalogued in [[The Tu Quoque Deflection and the Burden of Proof]].

### 3. Going "back to the text" does not rescue the asymmetry — rate: fair
When Kelly proposes checking a new revelation against the actual text, Hayden agrees and points to the parallel: D&C 76's celestial/terrestrial language tracks [[1 Corinthians 15.24-28|1 Corinthians 15]]:40 closely (Hayden and Kelly read the two side by side and agree they are "not identical… but definitely some parallels").[^4] The expansion is therefore tethered to scripture in the same way Paul's teaching was tethered to, yet exceeded, the OT — undercutting the claim that modern revelation is uniquely unmoored.

### Steelman of the Evangelical rebuttal — rate: this is the strongest reply
Kelly's best answer is that the cases are **disanalogous on grounds independent of "newness."** (a) **Foundational vs. post-foundational era:** Paul belongs to the apostolic foundation that *produced* scripture; with the canon closed, the standard for testing later claimants is the completed deposit, not a fresh apostolic say-so ([[Galatians 1.8|"though an angel… another gospel… accursed"]]). (b) **Non-contradiction test:** Paul's revelation, while new, did not *contradict* God's prior self-revelation, whereas the challenger argues LDS doctrines of the [[The Trinity and Consubstantiality Are Not in the Bible|nature of God]] and [[God Was Once a Man|exaltation]] do. (c) **Falsifiability:** Hayden himself concedes "Is D&C 76 falsifiable? Absolutely not"[^5] — to which Kelly replies that an unfalsifiable claim cannot be validated by the evidence-checking method Hayden elsewhere endorses. See [[Scripture as the Test of Prophets and Revelation]] and [[Heads Faith Wins, Tails Reason Loses - The Unfalsifiability Charge]]. The LDS rejoinder is that Paul's resurrection-glories were equally unfalsifiable to a first-century hearer, returning the parity.
