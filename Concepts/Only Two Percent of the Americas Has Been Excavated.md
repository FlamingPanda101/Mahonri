# Only Two Percent of the Americas Has Been Excavated

## The Claim

Only a tiny fraction of ancient American sites — somewhere between 1% and 5% — has
actually been archaeologically excavated. The remaining ~95%+ lies under jungle,
unexplored. This means absence of evidence for a Book of Mormon detail today is far
weaker than it sounds, and paradigm-shifting discoveries remain entirely possible.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. Published estimates run 1–5%

Rappleye cites estimates between 1% and 5% of Mesoamerica excavated. The point is not
to denigrate existing data — there is a lot of it, and much of the reconstruction is
likely accurate — but that it is still "a drop in the bucket" of what is potentially
available.[^2]

> **Evidence rating:** Moderate (a commonly repeated figure; range reflects genuine uncertainty).

### 2. An archaeologist's candid admission

Archaeologist [[George Stewart]] publicly said scholars had excavated only ~5% of
Mesoamerica and "we don't know squat"; in private among peers he lowered the figure
to 1% (with stronger language). The bulk of ancient ruins remain under jungle,
unexplored.[^3]

> **Evidence rating:** Moderate (relayed anecdote with a named public figure; private remark second-hand).

### 3. The unexcavated remainder could overturn paradigms

Most of the unexplored 95%+ will likely confirm what is known, but it could also
contain paradigm-shifting finds — e.g., a smelting oven dated to Book of Mormon
times. Because that cannot be ruled out, no one can be certain a given anachronism is
genuinely impossible rather than merely undiscovered. See
[[One Anachronism Does Not Disprove the Book of Mormon]].[^4]

> **Evidence rating:** Moderate-to-strong (a sound epistemic point about negative evidence).

## Steelman of the Opposing View

A critic can reply that excavated sites are not random — archaeologists target the
largest, most promising centers, so the sampled 2–5% is heavily *enriched* for the
very evidence (cities, monuments, metallurgy) one would expect. Thus absence across
the best sites carries more weight than the raw percentage implies. The reply: even
targeted excavation has repeatedly missed major realities later overturned (the
"peaceful Maya"), so the inference from sampled silence to global absence remains
fragile.

## Best Quotes

> "I've heard estimates between one and five [percent]... we're dealing with 95 plus percent of the ancient ruins are just under jungle still, haven't been explored."[^2]

> "We've only excavated about 5% of Mesoamerica and then he says we don't know squat... in private conversations with his peers... he would bring that number down to 1%."[^3]

> "You can't tell me you're not going to find a smelting oven that dates to Book of Mormon times within that 95% somewhere. You can't, because you can't know."[^4]
