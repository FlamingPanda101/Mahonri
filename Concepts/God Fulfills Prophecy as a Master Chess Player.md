# God Fulfills Prophecy as a Master Chess Player

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
[[Jacob Hansen]] (as the critic) raises a third worry: a God who can adapt, change his plans, and err is *fallible* and *changeable* — "a Superman, not the one true God." How could a fallible God be the standard of morality, and how could he reliably prophesy or guarantee his purposes? [^1] See [[Foreknowledge and Free Will Are Logically Incompatible]].

## The Rebuttal
1. **God knows all *possibilities*, not a fixed future** — rate: the constructive open-theist model. [[Blake Ostler]]: "God knows all possibilities. He knows the present probability of everything that could occur. And given anything that can occur, he can bring about the plan that he has." *(theological model)* [^2]
2. **The chess-master analogy** — rate: vivid and apt for the model. "He's like a master chess player. I'm a master playing a novice. I don't know exactly which moves the novice will make, but it doesn't matter — any move he makes, I've got a counter, and I can always win every single time. God is a much better chess player than I am." So God guarantees outcomes "without violating free will." *(analogy)* [^3]
3. **"All power to the fulfilling of his word"** — rate: scriptural anchor. [[1 Nephi 9.17|1 Nephi]]: God "has all power to the fulfilling of his word." "When God speaks something, he knows that he can make it happen." This is the second category of prophecy — events God *intends to bring about himself* (e.g., the Incarnation, the Restoration), distinct from [[All Prophecies Are Conditional|conditional]] ones. *(scriptural)* [^4]
4. **God is not "fallible" in the alleged sense** — rate: a precise denial. Ostler distinguishes *capable of revising provisional plans* from *liable to be defeated*: God's plan to send Christ to overcome death is certain ("we all overcome death"), even though individual outcomes dependent on free will (overcoming sin) are not guaranteed. The third prophecy-category is *causally determined* outcomes (a doctor reading a fatal X-ray): "God knows with certainty what the causal results of existing conditions are." *(distinction)* [^5]

**Steelman of the critic:** The classical theist objects that the chess model still concedes a God who *learns* the opponent's actual moves in time and *reacts* — a God with changing mental states, increasing knowledge, and a future he cannot specify, which strains [[Isaiah 46.9|Isaiah 46:9-10]] ("declaring the end from the beginning... my counsel shall stand"). A chess master can *lose* to a perfect opponent or an unforeseen move; guaranteeing victory "every time" against genuinely free agents arguably requires either exhaustive foreknowledge or coercive intervention — reintroducing the very control Ostler rejects. Ostler's reply: knowing *all possibilities* plus having *power to respond to any of them* secures the outcome without knowing which actual path is taken, so omnipotence-over-possibilities replaces foreknowledge-of-actuals.
