# Most Who Leave the LDS Church Do Not Become Atheists

#type/rebuttal

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
Two related assumptions are challenged: (a) the host's intuition that those persuaded by anti-LDS ministries don't join the ministry's faith but "throw their hands up" into unbelief, and (b) the broader stereotype that anyone leaving the Church becomes atheist/agnostic.[^1]

## The Rebuttal

1. **No elevated atheism rate vs. other leavers (Evidence: strong, comparative).** "We're no more likely to become an atheist ... than those leaving of other religions."[^2] The LDS exit funnel is not unusually secularizing relative to other traditions.

2. **Faith keepers overwhelmingly stay religious (Evidence: strong).** Among the favorable group, **84% joined another religion** (Christian or other) and **only 3%** became atheist/agnostic.[^3]

3. **The distant group still mostly religious (Evidence: strong).** Even the more distant cohort: **60% part of another religion, only 9% atheist/agnostic.**[^4]

4. **Only the faith-rejectors secularize (Evidence: strong).** It is "really only that final group, those faith rejectors" where most secularize — **~73% atheist/agnostic.** This passionate minority is the one "we just hear from more," producing the illusion that all leavers go secular.[^5]

**Steelman of the opposing view:** "Joined another religion" can include very low-commitment nominal affiliation, so the religious-retention picture may be rosier than lived practice. The data measures destination labels, not the depth of belief; someone who checks "Christian" after leaving may be functionally secular. And from an LDS-truth-claims standpoint, a former member becoming an evangelical or a "none" are both losses — the comfort here is sociological, not doctrinal.

## Best Quotes

> "We're no more likely to become an atheist and not of another religion than those leaving of other religions."[^2]

> "Our faith keepers ... 84% were part of another religion ... you only had 3% of those that ended up as say atheist or agnostic."[^3]

> "It's really only that final group, those faith rejectors ... like 73% of them said they were atheist agnostic."[^5]
