#type/claim

# Differing-But-Overlapping Vocabulary Signals Two Different Authors

## The Claim

The two creation accounts use terminology that *overlaps but differs* when naming the same things (e.g., the categories of animals). McClellan argues this is "precisely because these are two different accounts coming from two different authors" — a classic marker of distinct sources.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Observed lexical divergence (moderate).** Comparing [[Genesis 1.24-26|Genesis 1]] and [[Genesis 2.18-19|Genesis 2]]: "you'll notice that the terminology is slightly different. There's certainly overlap, but there's also difference."[^1] Evidence rating: **moderate** — true as far as it goes; vocabulary alone is suggestive rather than probative.

2. **The divine-name criterion (corroborating, classic).** Beyond this video's examples, the textbook source-critical signal is the divine name: the Priestly account uses *Elohim* ("God"), while the second account uses *YHWH Elohim* ("the LORD God") throughout — a shift McClellan's quoted NRSV text reflects. This pattern is one of the original pillars of the Documentary Hypothesis. Evidence rating: **strong** (well-established criterion; see [[Julius Wellhausen]]).

3. **Converges with sequence contradictions (strong).** Vocabulary differences corroborate, but the *decisive* evidence is the contradictory order of creation. See [[Sequence of Creation Differs Between Genesis 1 and 2]]. Evidence rating of the combined case: **strong**.

**Steelman:** Stylistic and lexical variation can also arise from a *single* author varying diction for rhetorical or generic reasons (overview vs. close narrative), so vocabulary differences are not, by themselves, proof of multiple authors. This is why source criticism rests on *converging* lines of evidence — vocabulary plus the divine name plus doublets plus the irreconcilable sequence — rather than on word choice alone.

## Best Quotes

> "Now, you'll notice that the terminology is slightly different. There's certainly overlap, but there's also difference. And that is precisely because these are two different accounts coming from two different authors." [^1]
