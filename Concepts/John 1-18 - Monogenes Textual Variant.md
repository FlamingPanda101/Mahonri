# John 1:18 — Monogenes Textual Variant

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
[[John 1.18|John 1:18]] calls Jesus *monogenes theos* — "the only/unique God" — proving John identifies Jesus as God.

## The Rebuttal

### 1. The manuscripts disagree on what the verse says
The verse has two competing readings in the manuscript tradition:[^1]
- **monogenes huios** — "only/unique Son" (the majority reading; used by John elsewhere)
- **monogenes theos** — "only/unique God" (found in some early manuscripts)

The NRSV updated edition tries to split the difference: "the only Son himself, God" — which [[Dan McClellan]] calls "outright nonsensical."[^2]

### 2. The difference may be a single letter
Due to *nomina sacra* (sacred name abbreviations), divine names and titles in early manuscripts were abbreviated to first and last letters with a line above. The difference between *huios* (Son) and *theos* (God) in this system could come down to a single letter — easily confused in transmission.[^3]

### 3. *Lectio difficilior* is contested
The principle of *lectio difficilior* (the more difficult reading is likely earlier) would favor *monogenes theos* since it's anomalous. But:
- This only tells you which of two manuscripts has the earlier reading — NOT that it was the *original* reading.[^4]
- During the Christological controversies of the 3rd-4th centuries, *monogenes theos* became the more *useful* reading for certain theological positions, so the principle may not apply here.[^5]

### 4. Even Metzger's committee pushed back
[[Bruce Metzger]]'s *Textual Commentary on the Greek New Testament* notes that *lectio difficilior* favors *theos*, BUT "the editors of the volume have a little note saying this is not likely because this would require the author actually have written this — and that just doesn't make any sense."[^6]

### 5. Internal consistency favors "Son"
John uses *monogenes huios* several times throughout the Gospel. *Monogenes theos* would be the ONLY occurrence of this phrase anywhere. Consistency with the author's usage favors "Son."[^7]

## Best Quote
> "It's not settled what is supposed to be here, and so John 1:18 — I don't think anybody can appeal to that as proof that John calls Jesus God." [^8]

## Counter-arguments
- Trinitarian scholars prefer *monogenes theos* precisely because it's the theologically stronger reading. Response: this is circular — preferring a reading because it supports your theology, not because the manuscript evidence demands it.
