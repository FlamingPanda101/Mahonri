# Hebrews 1:1-2 — "Jesus the Last Prophet" Rebutted

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
[[Hebrews 1.1-2|Hebrews 1:1-2]] — God formerly spoke "by the prophets" but "in these last days... by his Son" — means Jesus was the final prophet, so modern prophets are illegitimate.

## The Rebuttal

### 1. The New Testament records prophets after Christ
The book of Acts itself names them — "have you read the book of Acts?"

> "Acts 11:27... 'And in these days came prophets from Jerusalem unto Antioch.' Acts 21:10, 'there came down from Judea a certain prophet named Agabus.' The Bible itself teaches that there were prophets after Jesus Christ." [^1]

See [[Acts 11.27|Acts 11:27]] and [[Acts 21.10|Acts 21:10]].

### 2. God's pattern is unchanging
"God is the same yesterday, today, and forever," and will always call prophets because a people always needs a central source of authority — otherwise it splinters into competing denominations [^2]. [[Amos 3.7|Amos 3:7]]: God "revealeth his secret unto his servants the prophets" [^3].

### 3. Warning of false prophets presupposes true ones
Jesus warned that false prophets would come *later* — which only makes sense if genuine prophets would also come. He did not say "no more prophets" [^4].

## Counter-arguments / related
- Note the verse's other content ("by whom also he made the worlds") is usually ignored by critics fixed on the "last prophet" reading [^4].
