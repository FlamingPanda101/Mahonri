# Hebrews 1:1-2 — God Has Spoken by His Son (Final Prophet?)

## The Charge
> "God spoke in the past by prophets, but in these last days He's spoken by His
> Son. Jesus is the final prophet. Mormons having a living prophet contradicts this."

## The Verse (KJV)
> "God, who at sundry times and in divers manners spake in time past unto the
> fathers by the prophets, hath in these last days spoken unto us by his Son, whom
> he hath appointed heir of all things, by whom also he made the worlds." (Hebrews 1:1-2)

## Rebuttal Points

### 1. The Bible Itself Contradicts This Interpretation
- **Acts 11:27** — "And in these days came **prophets** from Jerusalem unto
  Antioch." (This is AFTER Jesus's ascension.)
- **Acts 21:10** — "And as we tarried there many days, there came down from Judaea
  a certain prophet named **Agabus**." (Post-ascension prophet.)
- Braden's short answer: "Have you read the book of Acts? Acts is the first book
  after the Gospels. It talks about what the apostles did after Jesus Christ left."

### 2. Jesus Warned About False Prophets — Implying True Ones
- Matthew 24:11, 24 — Jesus warns about false prophets arising. Warning about
  counterfeits only makes sense if the genuine exists.
- If prophets ceased with Jesus, He'd have said "no more prophets will come" — not
  "watch out for false ones."

### 3. Amos 3:7 Establishes the Pattern
> "Surely the Lord GOD will do nothing, but he revealeth his secret unto his
> servants the prophets."

Braden: "God is the same yesterday, today, and forever. He will always call
prophets because we will always need prophets."

### 4. Hebrews's Point Isn't Cessation — It's Supremacy
Hebrews 1 is about Christ's **superiority** over the prophets and angels — not
about ending prophecy. The author is saying the ultimate revelation is through
the Son (in contrast to the fragmentary revelations of the past), not that all
revelation is now closed.
- The result of rejecting ongoing prophecy: "Otherwise you fall into a branch off
  of all these different denominations. Oh, wait. That happened." (Braden)

### 5. The "Made the Worlds" Point
Cardon: "Christians tell us these verses mean Jesus is the last prophet... I got
out of it that he made all the worlds. You'd think that would be the highlight, but
no." The verse's own theological payload (Christ as creator) is overshadowed by
debating a non-point about prophecy.

## Bottom Line
Acts names multiple post-ascension prophets; Hebrews 1 is about Christ's
supremacy, not prophetic cessation.
