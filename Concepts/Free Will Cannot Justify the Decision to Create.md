# Free Will Cannot Justify the Decision to Create

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
The most common reply to the problem of hell: "Those people *chose* hell. God gave them free will and they used it badly — that's on them, not God." Hell is the self-inflicted result of human choice, so God is exonerated.

## The Rebuttal
1. **Free will only operates *after* you exist — rate: strong.** You must be alive before you can make any choice, and the decision to make you alive was God's alone, made before you had any say. Therefore you cannot use a person's *future* choices to justify the *prior* decision to create them; that reverses the order of the causal chain. The first and decisive choice — pressing the button — was God's [^1]. This is the same move as the upstream-responsibility argument in [[Hell - Free Will Defense Rebutted]] but framed around the act of creation rather than the act of damnation.
2. **The deathly-allergic-meal parallel — rate: moderate.** If I knowingly serve a fatally allergic guest the dish, "they chose to eat it" does not erase my responsibility, because I had both the knowledge and the option not to serve it. The diner's choice does not cancel the cook's [^2]. **Steelman of the theist:** the disanalogy is consent and value — the free-will defender claims a world containing genuine libertarian agents who can truly reject God is more valuable than a world of guaranteed-saved automata, and that God could not create *free* people while *also* ensuring none ever freely reject him without destroying the very freedom that makes love possible. The video's reply (implicit, developed elsewhere in the channel) is that God could simply create only those he foreknew would freely choose good — a Molinist "feasibility" dispute the clip does not fully engage.
3. **It does not even touch the problem — rate: framing.** The video's verdict is that free will "doesn't solve this — it doesn't even touch it," because the objection answers a question (who is to blame for the sin?) different from the one posed (why create the person at all?) [^1].

## Best Quotes
> "Free will only kicks in after you exist. You have to be alive before you can make choices. And the decision to make you alive, well, that was God's. He made that call before you ever got a say in anything." [^1]

> "You can't use someone's future choices to justify creating them in the first place. That's backwards. The very first decision in the chain... was God's choice to press the button." [^1]

> "Their choice does not erase mine. Free will doesn't solve this. It doesn't even touch it." [^2]
