# Epistle of Barnabas

The Epistle of Barnabas is an early Christian treatise (commonly dated c. 70–132 CE), anonymous but traditionally ascribed to Paul's companion Barnabas. Strongly anti-Judaic and allegorical in its reading of the Hebrew scriptures, it was esteemed enough that the fourth-century Codex Sinaiticus includes it alongside the New Testament, though it was ultimately excluded from the canon. It is counted among the Apostolic Fathers.

## Relevance

The vault cites Barnabas as a data point for the argument that the earliest Christian writers reserved the phrases "scripture" and "the word of God" for the Old Testament and for Christ himself, not for the New Testament corpus. Barnabas's warning that "you shall not let the word of God issue from your lips with any kind of impurity" treats the word of God as a spoken message, supporting [[Calling Scripture the Word of God Is a Late Patristic Development]].
