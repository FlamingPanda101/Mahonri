# Book of Enoch - Conspiracy Theories of Hidden Knowledge Rebutted
#type/claim

## The Claim
A popular online narrative treats 1 Enoch as forbidden, suppressed scripture — a book "they" (the church, the councils, the elites) deliberately hid because it reveals secret/dangerous truths about angels, giants, or the cosmos. [[Dan McClellan]] rebuts this conspiracy framing: 1 Enoch was never a secret. It dropped out of the Western and Orthodox canons through ordinary, documented reasons, and it survived openly and continuously in the Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo canon the whole time. There is no hidden-knowledge cover-up — only a book that fell out of fashion in one part of Christendom and stayed in canon in another.

## Supporting Evidence
- **It survived in plain sight.** 1 Enoch was openly preserved as canonical scripture in the Ethiopian Orthodox Church for the entire period it was "lost" to the West, which is the opposite of suppression. Its later [[Book of Enoch - Western Rediscovery 17th-19th Centuries|Western rediscovery]] (e.g., James Bruce's 1773 Ge'ez manuscripts) was the recovery of a publicly held text, not the cracking of a vault.
- **Mundane reasons for canon exclusion.** Per the source, it fell out of the Western/Orthodox canon by the 4th century over ordinary concerns — its claim that a pre-flood writing survived the flood, internal contradictions (the same events told several incompatible ways), and the broader apologetic agenda of 2nd-century Christian intellectuals. These are the normal mechanics of canon formation, not a conspiracy. See [[Book of Enoch - Exclusion from the Canon]].
- **The hype rides on bad editions.** The "hidden knowledge" excitement is fed by cheap 19th-century editions (especially R.H. Charles's) marketed as forbidden lore, when the responsible move is to read the modern critical translation. The aura of secrecy is a marketing artifact, not a property of the text.
- **Steelman.** A sympathetic reader can grant there was no literal conspiracy yet insist the instinct points at something real: a book quoted as scripture by Jude and used heavily at Qumran *was* effectively written out of the Western Bible, so "suppressed" feels apt even if the mechanism was canon politics rather than a cover-up. The corrective is precise: institutional non-canonization is real and worth studying; a deliberate hiding of dangerous secret knowledge is not what happened.
