# Uncreated Intelligences Make Free Love Possible

## The Claim
[[Blake Ostler]] argues that the distinctive LDS metaphysic — that persons are **eternal, uncreated "intelligences"** — is precisely what makes free, non-coerced love (and thus morality) possible. Because we are *not* created ex nihilo and sustained moment-to-moment by God, our love can be our own genuine contribution rather than [[Creation Ex Nihilo Entails Occasionalism and Precludes Free Love|God loving himself through us]]. [^1] See [[The Law of Love Is the Single Ground of Morality]].

## Supporting Evidence
1. **We are eternal uncreated beings** — rate: the foundational LDS premise. "We're eternal uncreated beings. And the word used for us is *intelligences* — means that somehow we express intelligence." This self-existence gives the creature an independent standing God did not manufacture. *(theological)* [^2]
2. **Flourishing emerges from eternal relationality** — rate: ties metaphysics to ethics. "Because we exist in the eternal relationality, what leads to our flourishing as persons... as communities... as nations... as an entire world is the love that we give to one another." Love is the natural outgrowth of co-existing free agents. *(relational ontology)* [^3]
3. **Solves the consent and coercion problems at once** — rate: systematic payoff. Uncreated agents were not "thrown into a world without consent" and are not recreated each instant, so their love is freely given — the condition Ostler insists love requires (see [[Love Cannot Be Coerced or Created]]). *(systematic)* [^4]
4. **The eschatological telos: indwelling unity** — rate: the goal the metaphysic serves. Learning to love fits us "to enter into the loving relationship of Father, Son, and Holy Ghost," where "we come into a unity of oneness... and fully share in the glory of God," brought "to the fullness of God and his stature." *(soteriological)* [^5]

**Steelman of the creedal critic:** The traditional theist objects that uncreated co-eternal persons compromise God's *aseity* and *sovereignty* — if intelligences exist independently of God, God is no longer the sole ultimate reality (Creator of *all* things, [[Genesis 1.1|Genesis 1:1]]) but one being among self-existent others, which many regard as a downgrade from "God" to a powerful demiurge. Ostler accepts this trade deliberately: he holds that *only* such a picture preserves genuine love, genuine free will, and a God who is not the author of evil — and that the creedal "sovereignty" purchased by ex nihilo costs God the ability to love at all.
