# Authentic Ancient Texts Contain Uncorroborated Unique Data

## The Claim

Real, authentic ancient sources frequently preserve unique information about their
world that cannot be corroborated from any other source. Therefore demanding that
the Book of Mormon contain *no* uncorroborated detail is demanding that it tell us
nothing unique — an impossible standard for any genuine ancient text of its
length.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. Archaeology and historical texts routinely disagree

Most scholars recognize a gray area: the material record and written records often
tell us different things about the same time and place. Authentic sources from the
right time and place sometimes report things no other evidence corroborates — that
is normal, not disqualifying.[^2]

> **Evidence rating:** Strong (a mainstream historiographical commonplace).

### 2. The Dead Sea Scrolls overturned "Christian-only" assumptions

Before the Scrolls, scholars judged certain ideas to be distinctively Christian; a
text containing them "had to be" Christian. The Scrolls proved these were
pre-Christian Jewish ideas already circulating in the Jewish milieu. Discoveries
like this force re-evaluation: what looked anachronistic for a Jewish setting
suddenly was not.[^3]

> **Evidence rating:** Strong (a celebrated, well-documented paradigm shift in biblical studies).

### 3. A corpus the size of the Book of Mormon must have unique data

Given its length, the Book of Mormon is *bound* to report environmental details we
cannot independently confirm. Treating each such detail as a fatal anachronism
misapplies the standard. See [[One Anachronism Does Not Disprove the Book of Mormon]].[^4]

> **Evidence rating:** Moderate-to-strong (an a fortiori argument from text length).

## Steelman of the Opposing View

A skeptic can object that "uncorroborated unique data" is not the same as
"contradicted data." A horse in pre-Columbian Mesoamerica is not merely
uncorroborated — it appears to be positively excluded by a large body of negative
evidence. The reply: the strength of negative evidence is itself revisable (the
"no warfare" Maya consensus reversed in the 1960s; metallurgy dates have moved
earlier), so today's "contradiction" can become tomorrow's corroborated detail —
see [[The Anachronism List Is Trending Toward the Book of Mormon]].

## Best Quotes

> "There are times when legitimate, authentic, ancient sources from the time and place they're talking about tell us things that we don't learn and can't corroborate through other sources."[^2]

> "The discovery of the Dead Sea Scrolls... proves certain ideas that people thought were Christian and originated in Christianity were actually pre-Christian Jewish ideas."[^3]

> "It would be insane for a text as long as the Book of Mormon from the ancient world to not tell us anything unique about its surroundings and its environment that we can't corroborate through any other sources."[^1]
