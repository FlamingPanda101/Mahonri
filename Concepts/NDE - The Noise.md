# NDE - The Noise

## The Point
At or near the moment of death, many people report an unusual **auditory sensation** — sometimes a harsh buzzing or ringing, sometimes a beautiful music [^1]. It frequently occurs together with, and as a prelude to, the sensation of moving through a dark space [^2]. The phenomenon is consistent enough to count as a distinct recurring element, even as its emotional tone ranges from disturbing to sublime.

## Evidence
1. Unpleasant forms: a man who "died" for twenty minutes during abdominal surgery described "a really bad buzzing noise coming from inside my head"; another woman heard "a loud ringing... a buzzing" while in "a sort of whirling state" [^1].
2. Moody catalogs the range of descriptions: a loud click, a roaring, a banging, and "a whistling sound, like the wind" [^1].
3. Pleasant/musical forms: a man pronounced dead on arrival heard "bells tingling, a long way off... like Japanese wind bells" [^1].
4. A young woman who nearly died of internal bleeding heard, at the moment she collapsed, "music of some sort, a majestic, really beautiful sort of music" [^1].

## Best Quotes
> "I would hear what seemed to be bells tingling, a long way off, as if drifting through the wind. They sounded like Japanese wind bells…. That was the only sound I could hear at times." [^1]

## LDS Connection
No specific doctrinal anchor in LDS afterlife theology corresponds to "the noise" as such; it is best treated as a phenomenological detail of the transition rather than a point of doctrine. (Connection omitted where no honest tie exists.)
