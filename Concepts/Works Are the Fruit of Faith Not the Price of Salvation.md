# Works Are the Fruit of Faith Not the Price of Salvation

## The Point
If salvation is wholly by grace, why do Latter-day Saints emphasize obedience, ordinances, and covenants? Because works are the **manifestation** of saving faith, not its **purchase price**. "We are saved by the grace of Christ through our faith in Him which is manifested in the works He commands of us (Ephesians 2:8-10)." Obedience flows *from* faith and love; it does not *cause* justification. [^1]

## Evidence
1. **Article of Faith 3 read with Article of Faith 4.** "Saved... by obedience to the laws and ordinances of the Gospel" does not make human action the *source* of justification; rather, faith is expressed through obedience to laws and ordinances God provides "as a means of both safety, learning, and sanctification." [^1]
2. **Ephesians 2:8-10 itself.** Saved "by grace... through faith... not of works, lest any man should boast" — and *then* "created in Christ Jesus unto good works." Good works are the *consequence and purpose* of grace-saved life, the order Paul gives. [^1]
3. **Early-Christian parallel (Gaskill).** "The early Christians commonly held that one was exalted entirely by grace, but that works vouchsafed that grace... Grace could not be earned... But it wasn't given to those who made no effort either." The fallacy to avoid: "Jesus saves us, but then our works exalt us" — which "implies a self-sufficiency that is... damning." [^2]

## Best Quotes
> "Repentance and obedience to His laws and ordinances is not what saves us — He does." [^1]

> "Our works cannot save us but are necessary as a manifestation of true or sincere faith... Only the works of Jesus Christ can actually save or exalt." — Alonzo Gaskill [^2]

## LDS Connection
This resolves the apparent tension between the grace texts and the obedience texts, and answers the "faith vs. works" framing directly: it is faith *and its fruits*, with grace as the sole saving cause. It refines, without contradicting, [[LDS Salvation Is by Grace Not Earned by Works]] and engages the Evangelical [[Sola Fide vs Faith Plus Ordinances|sola fide]] objection.
