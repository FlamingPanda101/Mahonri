# Digital Information in DNA Requires a Master Programmer

## The Point
Meyer's **third discovery** is the digital *information* stored in DNA. Watson and Crick (1953) found the molecule's structure; Crick's "sequence hypothesis" (1957–58) proposed that the nucleotide bases function like alphabetic characters, providing coded instructions for assembling proteins.[^1] In our "uniform and repeated experience," whenever we trace information back to its ultimate source we always arrive at a **mind**, not a material process — so the information in even the simplest cell is best explained by intelligent design: "the information in DNA... requires a master programmer."[^2] The same logic exposes an *engineering problem* at every major jump in life's history, including the Cambrian explosion: building new forms requires new information, and undirected processes have no account of where it comes from.[^3]

## Evidence
1. **DNA is a code, not mere chemistry.** The bases' function "is not determined by their physical properties, their shapes, their masses — it's determined by their arrangement in accord with an independent symbol convention," the genetic code. The cell contains "a sophisticated information storage, transmission, and processing system."[^4]
2. **Expert admissions that it is digital code.** Bill Gates: DNA "is like a software program, but much more complex than any we've ever devised." Richard Dawkins acknowledges DNA "contains machine code." Leroy Hood calls DNA "chock-full of digital code."[^5]
3. **The uniform-experience inference.** "Whenever we find information — whether in a section of software or a paragraph in a book or a hieroglyphic inscription or... embedded in a radio signal — and we trace that information back to its ultimate source, we always come to a mind, not a material process."[^2]
4. **Materialistic origin-of-life accounts fail.** Chance, law/necessity, or any combination have not solved the information problem; even Dawkins concedes, "No one knows how life arose from a strictly materialistic chemical evolutionary process."[^6]
5. **The Cambrian explosion as an engineering problem.** Most major animal forms "first appear in the fossil record in a geologically abrupt fashion" ~530 million years ago (and other groups — winged insects, birds, flowering plants, mammals — likewise appear abruptly). Beyond the famous *missing ancestors*, the deeper mystery is informational: "if you want to give your computer a new function... you have to provide new code. Same thing turns out to be true... in the biological realm." Where does the new information come from? Neither chemical nor biological evolutionary theory has answered.[^3]

## LDS Connection
An information-based design argument is a congenial natural-theology resource for Latter-day Saints, who affirm a designed, ordered cosmos and a Creator behind life. The fit is broad rather than doctrinally specific: Meyer infers an undefined "intelligent source" (and ultimately a transcendent God), not the distinctive LDS picture of a God who *organizes* eternal, uncreated matter and intelligences rather than creating from nothing. The shared conviction is that mind, not blind chance, lies behind the information in living things.
