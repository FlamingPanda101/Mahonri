# Jesus Used the Apocrypha — Luke 11:47-51

## The Core Claim
When Jesus references "the blood of Abel unto the blood of Zacharias" in Luke
11:47-51, He is drawing on **extra-biblical traditions** about two infamous
instances of blood that could not be cleansed — traditions found in the
Apocrypha, pseudepigrapha, and rabbinical literature, not in the Old Testament
canon.

This demonstrates that Jesus Himself treated extra-biblical texts as legitimate
reference points, undermining the Protestant *sola scriptura* objection to LDS
use of additional scripture.

## The Context
Pharisees criticize Jesus for not washing before eating (Luke 11:38) — a ritual
hand-washing law derived from Deuteronomistic/Pharisaic oral tradition. Jesus
responds by invoking the murdered prophets whose blood stains the very temple
they claim to honor.

## What "Everybody Knew" (But Isn't in the Bible)

### Abel
- The earth refused to bury Abel's body (earth wouldn't accept anyone until
  Adam was buried first)
- Abel's blood never dried — a perpetual visible testimony
- Cain was permanently stained with Abel's blood
- Sources: Armenian History of the Sons of Adam, Cave of Treasures, Greek Life
  of Adam and Eve, Penitence of Our Forefather Adam, Quran

### Zechariah (2 Chronicles 24:20-21)
- Stoned to death **inside the temple courtyard** for preaching messianic prophecy
- His blood boiled on the temple stones for **200 years** (Mishnah Sanhedrin 96b)
- Nebuchadnezzar found it still boiling when he entered Jerusalem
- After this murder, the temple lost its sanctity — no more angels, no voice
  of God, the altar went dark (*Lives of the Prophets*)

## Jesus's Rhetorical Move
The Pharisees say: "You're unclean — you didn't wash."

Jesus says: "The blood of Abel never washed away. The blood of Zechariah boiled
on your temple stones for two centuries. Your fathers murdered the prophets who
preached about ME in this blood-soaked temple — and YOU lecture ME about
cleanliness?"

This is not abstract "A to Z" shorthand. It's a culturally embedded reference
everyone present would have understood.

## Apologetic Implications

1. **Sola scriptura problem**: If Jesus freely used non-canonical traditions as
   authoritative reference points, the Protestant restriction to 66 books has
   no dominical warrant.

2. **LDS consistency**: The LDS openness to extra-biblical revelation (Book of
   Mormon, Doctrine & Covenants, Pearl of Great Price) follows the pattern
   Jesus Himself established.

3. **Brass Plates context**: Jonah's broader thesis — Lehi took the brass plates
   containing these traditions (not the corrupt Deuteronomistic version) out of
   Jerusalem before its destruction. The Book of Mormon preserves what the
   Deuteronomists excised.

4. **Multiple attestation**: The Abel/Zechariah traditions appear across
   independent sources (Jewish Mishnah, Armenian Christian, Ethiopic, Greek,
   Islamic) — stronger attestation than many accepted historical claims.
