# NDE - The Spiritual Body

## The Point
Most of Moody's subjects report that, once out of the physical body, they find themselves in **another body** — which Moody, borrowing his subjects' own term, calls the **"spiritual body"** [^1]. It is at first perceived through its *limitations* (inaudible, invisible, intangible to the living), but these are equally an *absence of limitations*: it is weightless, passes through physical objects, travels almost instantly, and often retains a form analogous to the physical body with "parts" like arms and legs [^2][^3]. Its description is one of the most language-defying aspects of the whole experience [^1].

## Evidence
1. Naming: a "new body" so consistent across accounts that Moody adopts a subject's term — the "spiritual body" [^1].
2. Apparent limits: it is inaudible ("I tried talking to them but nobody could hear me"), invisible (staff "look straight towards where he is... without giving the slightest sign of ever seeing him"), and non-solid (hands pass through objects; people "would just walk through me") [^2].
3. Reframed as freedom: "He can see and hear them, but they can't see or hear him.... he can just go through the door. Travel... is apparently exceptionally easy" — movement is "almost instantaneous" [^2].
4. Form and substance: though described as a mist, cloud, vapor, or "energy pattern," it is agreed to be *something* with "a form or shape" and "even parts" [^3]; one woman "still felt an entire body form, legs, arms, everything—even while I was weightless" [^4].
5. Heightened perception: vision and "hearing" persist and seem sharpened — one man marveled he could "see so far"; "hearing" is really direct pickup of others' thoughts [^5].
6. Wholeness restored: a man who lost most of a leg in his fatal accident reported, in the spiritual body, "I could feel my body, and it was whole.... I felt whole, and I felt that all of me was there, though it wasn't" [^6].
7. Isolation: because he cannot be seen, heard, or touched, profound loneliness sets in — "I was desperately alone" [^6].

## Best Quotes
> "I could feel my body, and it was whole. I know that. I felt whole, and I felt that all of me was there, though it wasn't." [^6]

## LDS Connection
This is perhaps the strongest convergence with LDS doctrine in the chapter. Latter-day Saint theology teaches that every person has a **spirit body** in the form of the physical body — "the spirit of man [is] in the likeness of his person" — which survives death intact and whole. The reported restoration to wholeness, the retained human form, and the spirit's heightened faculties all parallel the LDS picture of the disembodied spirit in the spirit world (Alma 40; D&C 138; cf. Ether 3:16, the spirit body of the premortal Christ).
