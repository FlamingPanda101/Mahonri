# Scripture Is Self-Authenticating Because God Is Self-Contained

## The Claim
[[Anthony Rogers]] argues that Scripture is **self-authenticating**: because the triune God is **self-contained** — having all life and glory in and of Himself, needing no creature — He is **self-referencing** and therefore speaks with **self-attesting, ultimate authority**. God does not need His "papers stamped by popes and bishops." When God named Himself to Moses, He said "I am who I am," pointing to nothing outside Himself; when He swears, He swears by Himself, "there being none greater." Analogously, His word carries its own authentication and imposes itself on those "of the truth."[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. The self-contained God speaks self-referentially — rate: strong (as theology), contested (as canon criterion)
Rogers' theological move is internally coherent: an aseity-possessing God who grounds His own being can also ground His own testimony.[^1] The argument from God's self-reference (Exodus 3:14; Hebrews 6:13, swearing by Himself) to His word's self-attestation is a respectable Reformed epistemology (cf. [[Cornelius Van Til]], [[John Calvin]] on the *internal testimony of the Holy Spirit*). **Steelman of Horn:** even granting God *can* speak self-attestingly, this establishes the authority of God's *speech*, not that a *particular bound collection of 66 books* is that speech. Self-authentication of the *speaker* does not deliver the *table of contents*. See [[The Canon Premise Is an Infallible Rule Outside Scripture]].

### 2. Jesus spoke with self-attesting authority — rate: strong
Rogers marshals Jesus' hundreds of "Amen, amen, I say to you" prefaces, suspending truth on the bare fact that He said it, and the crowd's reaction ("Never has any man spoken like this").[^2] In [[John 8.58|John 8]], when the leaders said "your testimony is not true," Jesus replied "I am he who testifies about myself, and my father who sent me also testifies about me," and "he who is of the truth hears my voice" ([[John 18.37|John 18:37]]). This is a strong intra-textual case that the word imposes itself on the regenerate. **Steelman of Horn:** Jesus also appealed to *external* corroboration — "believe the works" (John 10:38) — so even Jesus did not rest solely on bare self-assertion. Rogers concedes the Spirit's inward work but insists the objective authority is prior.

### 3. Light manifests itself — rate: moderate
Rogers argues from Ephesians 5 ("light manifests itself") plus "your word is light" to conclude Scripture self-evidences.[^3] The syllogism is valid but rests on a metaphor; whether *every* canonical book bears self-evidencing light is exactly what Horn disputes (Philemon, 3 John). See [[Burning in the Bosom vs Self-Evidencing Light]] for Rogers' [[Luke 24.32]] distinction between objective light and the Spirit-given eyes to see it.

### 4. The patristic witness (Rogers' cross-ex) — rate: moderate
Rogers quotes **Nemesius of Emesa** ("the teaching of holy scripture which is self-authenticating because inspired by God") and **Clement of Alexandria** ("he who believeth the divine scriptures ... receives in the voice of God ... a demonstration that cannot be impugned").[^4] **Steelman of Horn:** these same fathers also appeal to the church's authority (Clement used the church to condemn the Quartodeciman Easter practice), so they did not hold Scripture's self-authentication *to the exclusion* of ecclesial authority. See [[The Early Church Did Not Operate Under Sola Scriptura]].

## Best Quotes
> "The triune God, the author of scripture, is self-contained. He has all life and glory in and of himself ... As self-contained, God is self-referencing, which is to say, he speaks with self-authenticating authority."[^1]

> "The true God is his own notary. He stamps his own papers."[^1]

> "If your mother calls you on the phone, do you have to farm out the question to somebody else? ... This is the God of the universe. You don't think he can speak to his creatures in such a way that his word gets through to them?"[^5]

> "The sun is shining whether anybody recognizes it or not. But it's because God gives people eyes that they can see the sun."[^6]
