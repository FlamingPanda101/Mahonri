# Josiah Stowell Defends Joseph Smith

#type/rebuttal

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
Critics frame the 1826 case as Joseph defrauding people with a fake seer stone. But the supposed victim — [[Josiah Stowell]], who hired Joseph — did **not** bring charges and testified *in Joseph's defense*, undercutting the "con man / fraud" narrative at its root.[^1]

## The Rebuttal

1. **The complainant was not the victim (decisive structural point).** The charge was brought by Stowell's nephew [[Peter Bridgeman]], apparently to "save his uncle," while Stowell himself testified for Joseph.[^2] A fraud case is "kind of hard to reconcile" when "the supposed victim ... says he's not a victim."[^3] *Rating: strong.*

2. **Stowell's positive testimony (primary, in the notes).** Stowell stated that Joseph looked into a seer stone and accurately described Stowell's distant land, home, and other buildings — in enough detail to distinguish the property.[^4] Christian raises the "every house looks the same" objection; Mark answers that the Stowell riverside property (floodplain, road, hillside homes on the Susquehanna) was clearly distinct from the Smith farm in Manchester, so generic guessing won't explain it.[^5] *Rating: moderate — eyewitness courtroom claim, but recorded only in the later notes.*

3. **Probable cause of discharge.** Mark argues this is "probably why he was discharged" — with the victim defending the accused, the prosecution lacked footing.[^6] *Rating: moderate — plausible legal inference.*

4. **Steelman.** A skeptic can reply that Stowell was a credulous true believer whose testimony proves only that Joseph was a persuasive cold-reader, not a genuine seer; the detailed "description" could reflect prior knowledge or vague hits. Mark's rebuttal is that the description was specific enough to identify the property, but the source concedes the data are sparse and "you've got to be careful about your biases." The host explicitly frames Stowell's defense as *his own* faith-based "takeaway," not a knock-down proof.[^7]

## Best Quotes

> "Josiah Stowell who hired him to do it didn't bring the charges and defended Joseph in this case ... that's probably why he was discharged, because the supposed victim ... says he's not a victim."[^3]

> "I went way up in a whole another section of the country and he described my property and my home and my outbuildings accurately. What is that?"[^7]
