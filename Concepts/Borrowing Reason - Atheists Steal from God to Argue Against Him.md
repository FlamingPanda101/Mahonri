# Borrowing Reason - Atheists Steal from God to Argue Against Him

## The Point
This is the "stealing from God" thesis that titles Turek's book, applied to reason. If the laws of logic and our trustworthy rational faculties exist only in a theistic universe, then every time an atheist constructs an argument against God, he must borrow tools that only God's universe supplies — "they are actually presupposing God exists!"[^1] The atheist's own reasoning is therefore evidence against his conclusion. Borrowed from [[Greg Bahnsen]]'s transcendental approach, the move shows atheism cannot even get off the ground without smuggling in theistic capital.

## Evidence
Turek traces the strategy to the 1985 Bahnsen–Stein debate, where Bahnsen argued that logic, science, and morality "cannot be explained by atheism but are explained well by Christian theism," and answered the demand for something immaterial besides God by naming "the laws of logic."[^2] Turek replays the same move with a UW–Madison questioner who insisted logic does not really exist — while using the law of noncontradiction to say so.[^3] He generalizes: "It's impossible to deny the laws of logic without using them."[^4] Since these immaterial, unchanging laws fit a divine Mind and not bare matter, "anytime atheists offer an argument against God or for atheism, they are actually presupposing God exists. By using reason, atheists are stealing from God in order to argue against Him."[^1] The same applies to free will and trustworthy cognition: the atheist "has to steal tools from God's universe because no such tools exist in the world of atheism."[^5] Turek closes the chapter on the same note, observing that even [[Richard Dawkins]] keeps "stealing reason and free will from God in order to support atheism. Yet when he does, he still fails."[^6] He cites the gospel's anchoring of rationality in God: "in the beginning was the Word" (or rationality), John 1:1.[^7]

## Best Quotes
> "By using reason, atheists are stealing from God in order to argue against Him." [^1]

> "In order to construct any valid argument for atheism, the atheist has to steal tools from God's universe because no such tools exist in the world of atheism." [^5]

## LDS Connection
The Restoration agrees that reason itself is a gift sourced in God, not a self-standing possession of matter. The Light of Christ "enlighteneth your eyes" and "quickeneth your understandings" (D&C 88:11), and it is the very "light which is in all things... the law by which all things are governed" (D&C 88:13) — so the rational order an atheist relies on is, in LDS terms, already God's law operating in him. Latter-day Saints affirm with Turek that "the glory of God is intelligence" (D&C 93:36) and that the divine "Word" who was "in the beginning with God" ([[D&C 93]]; John 1:1) is the ground of reason. To argue at all is to draw on light that proceeds "from the presence of God to fill the immensity of space" (D&C 88:12) — borrowed capital, exactly as Turek says.
