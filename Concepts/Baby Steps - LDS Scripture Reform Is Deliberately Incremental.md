# Baby Steps - LDS Scripture Reform Is Deliberately Incremental

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Dan McClellan]], a former scripture-translation supervisor for the church, argues that the LDS
institution changes its relationship to scripture only in **"baby steps."** Leaders "like the baby
steps… because they don't want to rock the boat" — there are "a lot of traditionalists who are
going to get upset."[^1] The practical predictions: the church will issue its *own* revised,
updated KJV-based Bible long before it ever authorizes a non-KJV-style Book of Mormon, and only
*after* that first step would a modern-language Book of Mormon even become discussable.[^2]

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. The pace is a governing institutional philosophy — rate: strong
"Baby steps is the mantra of most of the changes that they make when it comes to scripture."[^3]
McClellan invokes the film *What About Bob?* — "Baby steps is their mantra" — and explains the
brake: traditionalist backlash and a reluctance to "rock the boat."[^1] This is the descriptive
mechanism behind the otherwise-celebrated openness documented in
[[A Living Canon Is a Feature Not a Bug]]: the canon is open in principle but moved cautiously in
practice.

### 2. A non-KJV Book of Mormon is "a bridge too far" — rate: strong
Asked about a rumored non-KJV-style Book of Mormon, McClellan: "I think that's a bridge too far."
The two recent Bible-translation policy shifts were themselves "two baby steps."[^3] The ordering
is explicit: "they would issue a church translation of the King James Version that has been revised
and updated long before they will ever issue a new articulation of the Book of Mormon."[^2] He
adds he "advocated for [a church English Bible] every workday for… a little over 10 years."[^4]
This sequences the reforms tracked in [[LDS Multi-Translation Policy Shift]].

### 3. The same brake explains publishing restrictions — rate: moderate
The scriptures committee at one point barred church-affiliated presses from publishing scripture
editions that diverged from standard formatting/translation, which took Grant Hardy's Maxwell
Institute study edition of the Book of Mormon out of print and pushed [[Thomas A. Wayment]]'s
second New Testament edition to a non-church press.[^5] The institutional caution operates not only
on doctrine but on what scholars may publish — though McClellan notes the policy "ebbed and flowed"
and may be relaxing. **Steelman:** an apologist can frame incrementalism positively, as prudent
pastoral care for a global membership rather than timidity; McClellan grants the bureaucratic
realities while lamenting the cost to literacy and creativity.

## Best Quotes
> "Baby steps is their mantra because they don't want to rock the boat… there are a lot of traditionalists who are going to get upset."[^1]

> "They would issue a church translation of the King James Version that has been revised and updated long before they will ever issue a new articulation of the Book of Mormon."[^2]

> "If you see the church produce its own English translation of the Bible — which again is something I advocated for every workday for a little over 10 years — then we can start talking about a Book of Mormon."[^4]
