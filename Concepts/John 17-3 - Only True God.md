# John 17:3 — "The Only True God"

## The Charge
> "Jesus said the Father is the ONLY true God. Therefore there cannot be other
> gods, and Mormon theology of exaltation is false."

## The Verse (KJV)
> "And this is life eternal, that they might know thee the only true God, and
> Jesus Christ, whom thou hast sent." (John 17:3)

## Rebuttal Points

### 1. Jesus Distinguishes Himself from the Father
The verse itself distinguishes "the only true God" (the Father) AND "Jesus Christ
whom thou hast sent." If "only true God" excludes Jesus, the verse undermines
Trinitarianism — not just LDS theology. Trinitarians must read it as "only true God
[inclusive of the Trinity]" which requires reading the doctrine into the text.

### 2. "Only" (Greek *monos*) Doesn't Mean "Only One That Exists"
The Greek *monos* often means "alone" or "unique" rather than "the sole existent
being." For example:
- John 1:14 — Jesus is the "only begotten" (*monogenes*) — unique, not the only
  child ever.
- 1 Timothy 1:17 — God is "the only wise God" — does this mean no one else is wise?

### 3. The Purpose Is Relational Knowing, Not Ontological Exclusion
The verse defines eternal life as KNOWING God relationally, not as a metaphysical
statement about the maximum number of divine beings. It's about covenant
relationship — "you shall have no other gods before me" (Exodus 20:3) is about
allegiance, not ontology.

### 4. Functional vs. Ontological Language
In biblical language, "gods" that aren't YHWH are discussed in functional terms —
Baal can't save, idols can't speak. The Bible's "no other god" language is about
there being no other SAVIOR, not no other divine being (see [[Isaiah 43-10 - No God Formed]]).

## Bottom Line
Jesus addressing the Father as "the only true God" in this prayer is relational
and covenantal, not a statement about the maximum number of divine beings.
