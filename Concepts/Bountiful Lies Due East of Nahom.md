# Bountiful Lies Due East of Nahom

## The Claim

After turning east at Nahom, Nephi's party reaches **Bountiful** ([[1 Nephi 17.5|1
Nephi 17:5]]) — a fertile coastal place with trees, fruit, and honey (hence bees and
flowers) where they build a ship. Such a place exists in the **Dhofar region of Oman**,
roughly 700 miles almost exactly due east of the Nahom/Wadi Jawf turn — locking the
eastward leg into place.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. The Dhofar coast matches Nephi's Bountiful description

Eastern Yemen / western Oman (the Dhofar region) is the primary frankincense-growing
zone and has lush, fertile inlets with trees, abundant fruit, and honey — matching the
features Nephi describes after the barren stretch he calls sojourning in the
wilderness (1 Nephi 17–18). One reaches it via a difficult barren passage, exactly as
the text implies.[^2]

> **Evidence rating:** Strong (a rare fertile pocket on the otherwise arid south Arabian coast matching multiple specifics).

### 2. It sits almost exactly due east of Nahom

Bountiful lies roughly 700 miles and nearly due east of the Nahom turn — consistent
with Nephi's report that after Ishmael's burial they traveled "nearly eastward." This
is the natural terminus of the eastward trails that branch at Wadi Jawf. See [[Nahom
and NHM Converge with Nephis Eastward Turn]].[^3]

> **Evidence rating:** Strong (directional + distance correspondence chaining off the Nahom anchor).

### 3. A fixed anchor that cannot be moved

The location of trees and lush fertility on the Arabian coast is a geographic given,
not something an apologist can position to fit. Combined with the Valley of Lemuel
river and Nahom, Bountiful is a third independent anchor — three convergent fixed
points, not one. See [[Convergence Not Proof Is the Standard for Book of Mormon
Historicity]].[^4]

> **Evidence rating:** Moderate-to-strong (the anchor-point logic; strength rests on the chained directional reading).

## Steelman of the Opposing View

A critic can argue that once Nahom is granted, Bountiful is somewhat forced: the only
fertile coast east of Yemen *is* Dhofar, so finding it there is unremarkable rather
than a separate confirmation, and "trees, fruit, honey" are generic markers of any
fertile coast. The reply: the chain's force is cumulative and geographic — a fabricator
in 1829 New York had no way to know that turning east at the one viable trail junction
would land precisely at the one fertile, ship-buildable coast ~700 miles due east.

## Best Quotes

> "You come to this region, the Dhofar region, that has lush fertility... that has inlets that have all the details described by Nephi when he's talking about Bountiful."[^2]

> "It's got trees, it's got honey, which means there's bees, which means there's flowers... we find an area just like that roughly 700 miles to the east... almost exactly due east."[^3]

> "We can't move where trees and lush fertility happens to be along the Arabian coast... those are these anchor points. Those are where they are."[^4]
