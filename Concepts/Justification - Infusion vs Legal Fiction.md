# Justification — Infusion vs Legal Fiction

## The Claim
The core Catholic/Protestant difference: Protestant *sola fide* tends to treat justification as God **declaring** the sinner righteous (imputation — a "legal fiction"); the Catholic view is that God **makes** the sinner actually righteous (an infusion of his own righteousness).

## Supporting Evidence
- "The Catholic view of justification recognizes that God doesn't merely declare us righteous as a legal fiction. God makes us righteous. He transforms our souls with an infusion of his own righteousness to make us new creations." *(Trent Horn)* [^1].
- Catechism: "Justification is not only the remission of sins, but also the **sanctification and renewal of the interior man**" — so justification and sanctification are not cleanly separable as in Protestant thought *(magisterial source)* [^2].

## Best Quote
> "God doesn't merely declare us righteous as a legal fiction. God makes us righteous... an infusion of his own righteousness to make us new creations." — [[Trent Horn]] [^1]
