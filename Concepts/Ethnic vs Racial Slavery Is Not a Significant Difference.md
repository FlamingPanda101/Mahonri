# Ethnic vs Racial Slavery Is Not a Significant Difference

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
Joshua HS argued biblical chattel slavery was "still not the kind of slavery you would think of with the transatlantic slave trade, which is more race-based slavery" — implying the biblical version was categorically milder.[^1]

## The Rebuttal (Numbered)

1. **The only real difference is the basis of categorization (rated: strong).** McClellan: "the only real difference is that the transatlantic slave trade was based on race as a question of skin color and the cattle slavery that is endorsed in Leviticus 25:44-46 is ethnically based."[^2]

2. **The difference is morally negligible (rated: strong; argued).** "While there is a difference there, that's really the only difference and it's not a significant difference." Sorting humans into ownable property by **ethnicity** is not relevantly better than sorting them by **skin color**.[^2]

3. **Joshua's further distinctions fail (rated: moderate).** McClellan flags in advance that "the differences that Joshua here is going to try to conjure up are not accurate" — anticipating the kidnapping/rights/abuse arguments dismantled in [[Old Testament Repudiates Antebellum Slavery Rebutted]].[^2]

4. **Steelman.** The strongest form of the objection: transatlantic chattel slavery was uniquely dehumanizing because it was undergirded by a pseudo-scientific racial ideology absent in the ancient world. McClellan grants the ideological mechanism differed but argues the **practical institution** — heritable ownership of out-group humans as property — is the same in kind.[^2]

## Best Quotes

> "The only real difference is that the transatlantic slave trade was based on race as a question of skin color and the cattle slavery that is endorsed in Leviticus 25:44-46 is ethnically based. So while there is a difference there, that's really the only difference and it's not a significant difference."[^2]
