# The Priesthood Ban Was the Development Not Its Reversal (Objection Rebutted)

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
[[Joe Heschmeyer]] says Latter-day Saints "no longer believe that being black is a curse from God, which was *originally taught* and then was reversed in 1978" — framing the priesthood-and-race restriction as original LDS doctrine and the 1978 revelation as the change/cave.[^1]

## The Rebuttal

1. **The chronology is reversed (rated: strong, historical).** [[Jacob Hansen]]: "It was not originally taught. Joseph Smith was baptizing Black freemen and African-Americans... It actually wasn't until Mormons under persecution and duress adopted the Protestant curse-of-Cain doctrine from the surrounding area that we developed the ban. The priesthood ban is the development. Getting rid of the priesthood ban is... the *un*development of the development."[^2] Joseph Smith ordained [[Q. Walker Lewis|Q. Walker Lewis]] and [[Elijah Abel]]; the ban consolidated under [[Brigham Young]]. See [[Joseph Smith Ignored Race-Based Scriptural Distinctions]].

2. **The error was *learned from* the critics' own tradition (rated: moderate, polemical).** Hansen: "When you want to come and say that we're racists — who'd we learn it from? We learned it from you guys. We joined your club. We didn't leave your club. We *joined* your club." The curse-of-Cain/curse-of-Ham framework was 19th-century American Protestant common stock, not an LDS invention.[^3]

3. **The "color" vocabulary postdates the source texts (rated: moderate, linguistic).** Ellis cites that red/white/black/brown/yellow as racial skin-tone terms were a product of Johann Friedrich Blumenbach's 18th-century racial science; "no one called black people black before that term," so the lexicon is anachronistic to the [[Book of Mormon Skin of Blackness Is Not About Race|Book of Mormon]] and to scripture.[^4] *Steelman:* a critic will note that whatever the *origin* of the ban, LDS leaders (including Brigham Young and later Bruce R. McConkie) did teach race-based curse doctrine *officially* for over a century, and the 2013 "Race and the Priesthood" essay disavows those teachings — so a real, century-long official error existed regardless of who seeded it. The LDS reply is that disavowal of an *absorbed* error is not the reversal of an *original* doctrine.

4. **Catholic comparison (rated: strong, documentary).** The panel notes the Council of Florence's still-on-the-books dogma that those outside the Church "will go into the eternal fire," and Catholic orders' direct participation in the slave trade — "anything you can say about us is on steroids" with Catholicism.[^3]

## Best Quotes

> "The priesthood ban is the development. The getting rid of the priesthood ban is not the development. That's actually the un-development of the development."[^2]

> "When you want to come and say that we're racists — who'd we learn it from? We learned it from you guys. We joined your club. We didn't leave your club."[^3]

> "Joseph Smith was baptizing black freemen and African-Americans left and right... It wasn't until Mormons under persecution and duress adopted the Protestant curse of Cain doctrine."[^2]
