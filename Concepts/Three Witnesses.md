# Three Witnesses

## Overview

The **Three Witnesses** — Oliver Cowdery, David Whitmer, and Martin Harris — are the men whose joint testimony is printed at the front of the Book of Mormon, declaring that an angel showed them the gold plates and that they heard the voice of God affirm the translation. This is an overview/hub note for an existing witness cluster; see the linked notes for specific arguments.

## The Claim

Their experience was **supernatural and corporate**: an angel, a heavenly voice, and the plates shown together — a different *kind* of experience from the [[Eight Witnesses|Eight]], who handled and hefted the plates in an ordinary, tactile setting ([[Three vs Eight Witnesses Different Kinds of Experience]]). The two sets thus span both ends of the natural/supernatural spectrum ([[Natural vs Supernatural Witnesses]]).

The evidential force the vault presses:

- **None ever recanted.** All three [[All Three Witnesses Upheld Their Testimony for Life|upheld their testimony for life]] — even after all three were estranged from the Church for periods — and [[No Witness Ever Recanted|no witness ever recanted]]. Estrangement without retraction is hard to square with conspiracy or known fraud.
- **The strongest single line of evidence.** The vault treats the combined witness as [[Book of Mormon Witnesses Are the Strongest Evidence|the strongest evidence]] for the Book of Mormon, with naturalistic alternatives (hypnosis, priming, hallucination) rebutted: [[Hypnotism Theory of the Witnesses Rebutted]], [[The Combined Improbability of the Naturalistic Witness Feat]].
