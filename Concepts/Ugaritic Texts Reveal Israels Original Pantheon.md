# Ugaritic Texts Reveal Israels Original Pantheon

## The Claim
The Ugaritic texts (discovered 1929 at Ugarit/Ras Shamra in Syria) reveal the polytheistic background of early Israelite religion: an original divine family — El the father, his consort, and a council of divine sons, each the god of a nation — with Yahweh as the son who inherited Israel. Israel's God was not always an amorphous monotheistic absolute; that is a later development.

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. Ugarit is Israel's nearest neighbor in space and time (rating: strong; archaeological)
Butler: after the 1929 discovery of the Ugaritic texts, scholars found "a whole bunch of information that seems to be the background of much of the Old Testament." Ugarit (destroyed ~1200 BCE by the Sea Peoples, a few hundred miles north of Israel) is the closest large documentary cache to Israel's origins. [^1]

### 2. The reconstructed structure (rating: moderate; mainstream-leaning)
"Israel seems originally to have had a father who was El, who lived in his tent... and the host, who were stars, who were the gods, came to his court when he held council, and he had a wife who was the mother of all those gods. And each of those gods was the god of a different nation... and Yahweh was the son whose inheritance was Israel." See [[Deuteronomy 32.8-9|Deut 32:8-9]] and [[Psalm 82.1|Psalm 82]]. [^2]

### 3. Near-consensus on early polytheism (rating: moderate; Butler's characterization)
Butler: "it's not just one scholar saying this... it's almost as close to consensus" that polytheism was happening; reading [[Genesis 1.1|Genesis 1]] as monotheism is reading monotheism *into* the text. (Note: this is Butler's framing; "consensus" is overstated for the specific El/Yahweh-as-son reconstruction, which is debated.) [^3]

### 4. Theological payload (rating: moderate; LDS application)
The recovered divine-family picture undergirds the LDS claim that creedal *ex nihilo* monotheism is the apostate deviation, and that a literal Heavenly Father (and Mother) is the original biblical theology. See [[Loss of the Divine Family Is the Core Apostasy]]. [^4]

### 5. Steelman of the opposing view
Traditional and many critical scholars argue Israel's religion *developed toward* exclusive monotheism (whether by inner-biblical revelation or historical evolution), so that the canonical text — even if it preserves older mythic fragments — teaches one God, and the divine council members are demoted to creatures/angels (Neh 9:6). On this view the Ugaritic background is the *raw material* Israel transcended, not the normative theology. Butler concedes the data are read differently but insists the plain sense of texts like Deut 32:8-9 (LXX/DSS "sons of God") favors the pantheon reading.

## Best Quotes

> "Israel seems originally to have had a father who was El... and he had a wife who was the mother of all those gods... and Yahweh was the son whose inheritance was Israel." [^2]

> "If you read Genesis 1 into monotheism, you're reading monotheism in the text. The text is not saying that." [^3]

> "This idea that the God of Abraham, Isaac and Jacob was always this monotheistic amorphous being — clearly not true." [^4]
