# Integrated Epistemology - Calibrating the Faculties

## The Point

The host's concluding synthesis: the five "witnesses" — **intuition, sense data, rationality, outcomes, and authority** — are not rivals but a single integrated system. Properly calibrated by experience, they let us "see reality in its full depth and complexity." Experience/spirit is foundational, but it does not displace the other faculties; it grounds them.[^1][^2]

## Argument

1. **The faculties form a dependency chain.** Earlier in the video the host argued authority rests on outcomes, outcomes on reason, reason on intuition, and intuition (with sense data) on experiential contact with reality — a single descent rather than five independent sources. See [[The Epistemic Regress Problem]], [[Reason Is Emergent From Intuition]], [[Sense Data Is Filtered Through a Valuing Self]].[^3]
2. **Experience is fundamental but low-resolution.** Direct spiritual experience is the most basic and "in some sense undeniable" layer, yet "not the highest resolution"; reason refocuses and articulates it. See [[Perfect Certainty of Experience Not of Interpretation]].[^4]
3. **Humans are spiritual, not purely rational, actors.** "We don't exist as purely objective rational actors; we are fundamentally spiritual creatures in an ocean of spiritual forces ... at the core of existence itself is a spiritual struggle more than a rational one."[^1]
4. **Guardrails against fideism.** The host explicitly disclaims the caricature: "I am not saying that a person's interpretation of their spiritual experience is always reliable ... I am not saying that feelings are the trump card." The claim is only that the *root* of knowledge is experience with reality, not that feelings override everything.[^1] *(Steelman: the disclaimers make this more defensible than naive "follow your feelings" fideism — interpretation remains fallible and correctable by reason.)*
5. **Integration thesis.** "It is my theory that intuition, sense data, rationality, outcomes, and authority are not ultimately at odds but are meant to be integrated into a complete system that, when properly calibrated, allows us to see reality in its full depth and complexity."[^2] *(Cross-examination: "properly calibrated" is doing unspecified work — the framework gives no independent criterion for when the faculties are well-calibrated vs. when a confident spiritual intuition is simply wrong, which is exactly the point a critic of experience-based epistemology presses.)*

## Best Quotes

> "We don't exist as purely objective rational actors. We are fundamentally spiritual creatures in an ocean of spiritual forces ... at the core of existence itself is a spiritual struggle more than a rational one."[^1]

> "Intuition, sense data, rationality, outcomes, and authority are not ultimately at odds but are meant to be integrated into a complete system that, when properly calibrated, allows us to see reality in its full depth and complexity."[^2]
