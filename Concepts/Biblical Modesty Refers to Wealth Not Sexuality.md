# Biblical Modesty Refers to Wealth Not Sexuality

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
Joshua HS has argued that women must dress modestly to avoid causing men to "stumble," calling "female sensual self-expression... a core idol of our feminist culture" and coining "modest is hottest." [[Dan McClellan]] addresses this in *The Bible Says* (ch. 15) and recaps it in this video as background on Joshua's biblical literacy.[^1]

## The Rebuttal (Numbered)

1. **Biblical modesty is about wealth, not sex (rated: strong; primary text).** "Biblical modesty always and only refers to avoiding displays of wealth and status. There isn't a single syllable of the Bible that admonishes women to cover up so they don't distract/attract men."[^2]

2. **Lust is the looker's fault (rated: strong; primary text).** McClellan notes the Bible, where it addresses lust, places fault on the man who lusts, not on the woman's clothing.[^3]

3. **Identity politics misread as Scripture (rated: moderate; argued).** "Joshua here blithely confuses his right-wing authoritarian identity politics for the Bible."[^2] See [[Apologetics as Identity Politics Over Evidence]].

4. **Women as sexual objects (rated: moderate; argued).** McClellan reads the "modest is hottest" framing as revealing that "the primary value of women in many Christian circles is still as sexual objects."[^3]

5. **Steelman.** A defender could cite 1 Timothy 2:9 and 1 Peter 3:3 as calls to modest dress. McClellan's point is precisely that those texts target ostentatious displays of wealth (gold, pearls, costly attire), not the prevention of male lust — so they do not support the "cover up for men's sake" reading.[^2]

## Best Quotes

> "Biblical modesty always and only refers to avoiding displays of wealth and status. There isn't a single syllable of the Bible that admonishes women to cover up so they don't distract/attract men."[^2]

> "Because at the end of the day the primary value of women in many Christian circles is still as sexual objects."[^3]
