# Augustine Set Death as the Boundary of Salvation in the West

## The Point
[[Augustine]] "had something of the last word" in the Latin West on who could be saved after death: he denied that the descent of Christ produced *postmortem conversions*. His dictum was that postmortem faith "was not a conversion" — those who heard Christ after death "had no need of salvation after death because they had established a proper orientation toward God before their death." [[D. Jill Kirby]] notes Augustine thereby set death as the **"boundary of salvation"** in the West, and explicitly compares this to [[Alma 34.32-33|Alma 34:33]].

## Evidence
1. **Postmortem faith is not conversion.** Those who had achieved wisdom before Christ "had been illuminated by the same truth in accord with the opportunity of their own respective periods of life"; hearing Christ after death added nothing salvific [^1].
2. **Augustine's tentativeness.** Writing to bishop Evodius (Epistle 164), Augustine admits [[1 Peter 3.18-20|1 Peter 3:18–20]] "disturbs him" and cannot explain why only those of Noah's flood are mentioned. He affirms only a "very limited agenda": Christ "loosed and released the souls whom he chose" [^2].
3. **Condemnation of an emptied hell.** In *On Heresies*, Augustine condemns as heresy the belief "that upon Christ's descent into Hell the unbelievers believed and all were liberated from Hell" — rejecting both (1) postmortem conversion of unbelievers and (2) the emptying of hell [^3].
4. **The decisive boundary.** "Augustine's conclusion that the decisions of this life are spiritually definitive — whether one lived before or after Christ — set death as the 'boundary of salvation' in the West (compare [[Alma 34.32-33|Alma 34:33]])" [^3].

## Best Quotes
> "Those who heard Christ after death had no need of salvation after death because they had established a proper orientation toward God before their death." [^1]

> "Another heresy believes that upon Christ's descent into Hell the unbelievers believed and all were liberated from Hell." — Augustine, *On Heresies* [^3]

## LDS Connection
This is a hinge for LDS apologetics. On one hand, Kirby's own comparison shows a *convergence*: both Augustine and Amulek ([[Alma 34.32-33|Alma 34:32–33]]) teach that one's eternal state follows from one's mortal orientation. On the other, Augustine's hard "boundary of salvation" — no conversion possible after death — is precisely what the Restoration *modifies*: D&C 138 affirms genuine postmortem preaching, repentance, and acceptance of the gospel for those who never had a fair chance. The LDS position keeps Augustine's insistence on the seriousness of mortal probation while rejecting his closure of the door at death for the unevangelized.
