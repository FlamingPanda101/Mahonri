# The Worst Possible Misery Is Bad — Harris's Moral Floor

## The Point

[[Sam Harris]] grounds objective morality in a single fixed point: "the worst possible misery for everyone is bad." He uses it to halt G.E. Moore's infinite regress (the open-question argument) and insists it is a *factual* claim. [[Jordan Peterson]] agrees it is bad but denies it is a fact — calling it instead a foundational moral *intuition* — which is the crux of their disagreement.

## Argument (steelman)

1. **The thought experiment.** "A universe where every conscious mind is tuned to the worst possible experience for as long as possible... no silver lining, no lessons learned." Harris: "that's bad if anything is bad."[^1]

2. **It ends Moore's regress.** Moore's open-question argument lets you ask "but is that *really* good?" of any proposed good. Harris closes it: "what would really bad be if the worst possible misery for everyone didn't get you there? There's no other space to occupy."[^2]

3. **Harris: it is more than a factual claim — it is presupposed by all value talk.** "It's a claim that is required to make any value judgment intelligible." You cannot use the word "bad" of anything (a bus cutting off your hand) except by reference to this underlying scale.[^3]

4. **The bootstrapping analogy.** Every system rests on unprovable intuitions: "two plus two makes four" cannot be cashed out by arithmetic itself; Gödel showed arithmetic has truths unprovable within the system. Morality bottoms out the same way — "we pull ourselves up by our bootstraps, and that's not embarrassing."[^4]

5. **Peterson's precise dissent.** "I'm disputing something very specific: that that's a factual claim." He accepts it ends the regress, that hell is bad, that we should avoid it — but says "it's deeper than a factual claim; it's a claim required to make any value judgment intelligible," more like an axiom than a fact.[^5]

6. **Peterson reframes it as heaven/hell.** Harris's two contrasting stories (a hellish jungle life vs a flourishing one) are "two-thirds of the way to a landscape of hell and heaven" — i.e. Harris has smuggled in a *narrative* moral intuition and made it his axiom. See [[God as the Highest Value and Truthful Speech]] and [[Metaphorical Truth and Narrative Reading]].[^6]

## Best Quotes

> "The worst possible misery for everyone is bad. That's hell... if anything is bad, that's bad."[^1]

> "Every claim we make about anything, at a certain point... is turtles all the way down to something we can't explain and justify. This is true of physics, it's true of mathematics — Gödel proved it's true of arithmetic — and it's true morally."[^4]

> *(Peterson)* "I'm disputing the issue that that's a factual claim. That's all... it's deeper than a factual claim."[^5]
