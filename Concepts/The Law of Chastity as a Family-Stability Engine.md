# The Law of Chastity as a Family-Stability Engine

## The Point
The hosts argue the strong family outcomes in heavily-LDS states trace to one covenant in particular: the **law of chastity**, which they reframe as a *positive* program — "get married, be faithful, have children" — rather than a list of prohibitions. Latter-day Saints covenant to keep it at baptism and again, explicitly, in the temple; the hosts contend this is the upstream cause of lower divorce, fewer out-of-wedlock births, and fewer single-parent households, with large downstream effects on poverty and incarceration.

## Evidence
1. **Chastity as a covenant, made twice.** Members covenant to keep the law of chastity at baptism and again in the temple, where it is one of five named covenants — "we take it very, very seriously."[^1] The hosts read this seriousness as the mechanism behind the family-structure numbers in [[Utah and Idaho Top the Family Structure Index]].

2. **Reframed as a positive law.** "The law of chastity is not just a 'don't' law ... It is: get married, be faithful, have children. In that order."[^2] The claim is that keeping this sequence is what produces a stable home; breaking it is what makes families "fall apart."

3. **Downstream social statistics (cited secondhand).** The hosts assert single-parent households are "one of the biggest indicators for incarceration," that out-of-wedlock pregnancy sharply raises a child's odds of poverty and other adverse outcomes, and they cite infidelity/divorce figures: people who cheat are ~2x as likely to divorce; ~60% of relationships with infidelity divorce within five years; ~80% of secret-then-discovered affairs end in divorce.[^3] These numbers are stated on-air without named sources — see [[Correlation Is Not Proof - The Honest Limit of the Fruits Argument]].

4. **Scriptural framing.** They anchor the stakes in Alma 39:3-5 (Alma's rebuke of Corianton, sexual sin "most abominable above all sins" save bloodshed and denying the Holy Ghost) and the Family Proclamation's warning that family disintegration brings "the calamities foretold by ancient and modern prophets."[^4]

## Best Quotes
> "The law of chastity is not just a 'don't' law like don't do this. It is: get married, be faithful, have children. That's the law of chastity. So when you don't keep the law of chastity ... it will fall apart." [^2]

> "One of the biggest indicators for incarceration and things like that is single-parent households." [^3]

> "Individuals who cheat are twice as likely to be divorced or separated ... about 60% of [relationships] where there's infidelity are divorced within five years." [^3]

## LDS Connection
This supplies the *causal story* the hosts attach to the correlational fruit of [[Utah and Idaho Top the Family Structure Index]] and complements the broader "program that works" case in [[The Fruits of the Church - Do Critics Have a Better Program]]. The honest limit — that the on-air statistics are uncited and that covenant-keeping is entangled with many other variables — is treated in [[Correlation Is Not Proof - The Honest Limit of the Fruits Argument]].
