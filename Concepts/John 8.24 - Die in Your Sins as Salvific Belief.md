# John 8:24 — "Die in Your Sins" as Salvific Belief

## The Claim
[[Gavin Ortlund]] makes [[John 8.24|John 8:24]] — "unless you believe that I am (*ego eimi*), you will die in your sins" — the centerpiece of his case that belief *about Jesus' identity* is salvation-relevant. [^1] Read with [[John 8.58|John 8:58]] ("before Abraham was, I am") and [[Exodus 3.14|Exodus 3:14]], he takes the "I am" to be "a claim to participate in the divine identity," so that Jesus himself ties one's eternal state to recognizing who he is. [^2] "I would say that's one text that... is a thread all throughout John's gospel... every single 'I am' statement in John is pregnant with meaning." [^3]

## The Rebuttal (i.e., the contested interpretation, steelmanned)

1. **The "I am" as divine-identity claim (Ortlund).** *Rating: a serious reading* with strong intertextual support (Exodus 3, the *ani hu* of Deutero-Isaiah). It establishes a high Christology if granted. *Caveat:* it shows Jesus claims to bear/participate in the divine name, which an LDS or divine-agency reading can accommodate without consubstantiality.

2. **Steelman: divine-name *agency*, not ontological identity.** A non-Trinitarian reading (cf. [[Dan McClellan]] in [[John 8.58]]) holds that bearing/uttering the divine name is a claim to be its *authorized agent*, not to *be* Yahweh ontologically. *Rating: a live alternative* that loosens the verse's grip on consubstantiality — the verse demands belief about Jesus' identity, but which identity is the disputed question.

3. **"Die in your sins" — what destination?** Ortlund reads it as a binary call out of a real peril (sheep vs. goats, outer darkness). [^4] [[Jacob Hansen]] reads it through the progression model: dying in your sins means unrepented sin remaining with you, and since "none" sin is allowed in heaven, the binary is really "have you taken the medicine / kept following Christ," not "do you affirm the Trinity." [^5][^6] *Rating: Hansen's reading is internally consistent* but must explain why Jesus phrases the condition as *belief* ("unless you **believe** that I am") rather than perseverance; Ortlund presses exactly this point: "Jesus doesn't say you'll only die in your sins if you hate the light." [^7]

4. **The unresolved crux.** Both grant the verse makes belief-about-Jesus salvation-relevant; they disagree on (a) what content "I am" requires and (b) whether "die in your sins" marks an irreversible binary or a stalled progression. *Rating: the verse advances Ortlund's "belief matters" thesis but does not by itself adjudicate created-vs-uncreated.*

## Best Quotes
> "Jesus says... unless you believe that I am, you will die in your sins... the 'I am' I do take to be a claim to participate in the divine identity." [^2]

> "Jesus doesn't say you'll only die in your sins if you hate the light and run away from it. Jesus's plain words are 'unless you believe this about me you will die in your sins.'" [^7]

> "If you die in your sins... then those remain with me... how much sin is allowed in heaven? It's like none." [^5]

> "Every single 'I am' statement in John is pregnant with meaning. And I think Jesus is saying this is who I am and you need to believe in this person specifically." [^3]
