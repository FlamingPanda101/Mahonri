# Postmortem Salvation Resolves the Soteriological Problem

#type/claim

## The Claim

[[David Paulsen]] argues that the [[The Soteriological Problem of Evil - The Inconsistent Triad|soteriological inconsistent triad]] is dissolved by adding a **fourth premise** restored through Joseph Smith: **those who die without a chance to respond to the gospel will have that chance after death.** The triad was inconsistent only because it was incomplete. [^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Premise 4.** "Those who live and die without having a chance to respond positively to the gospel of Jesus Christ will have that chance **postmortemly**." With premise 4 in place, premises 1 (God desires all to be saved), 2 (salvation only through Christ), and 3 (millions never heard) are no longer in tension: salvation through Christ remains exclusive, yet becomes genuinely *available to all*. [^2]
2. **Not merely solving a puzzle.** Paulsen stresses the deeper point: Joseph Smith was "the instrument through whom God restored the knowledge and priesthood powers that make the **redemption of the dead** possible" — vicarious ordinances, not just an abstract postmortem chance. This connects to the early-Christian practice traced in [[Almsgiving Became the Economy of Salvation for the Dead]]. [^3]
3. **John Taylor's tribute.** Paulsen closes the section with [[D&C 135.3]]: "Joseph Smith... has done more, save Jesus only, for the salvation of men in this world, than any other man that ever lived in it" — precisely because he reopened salvation to the uncounted dead. [^4]

## Best Quotes

> "Those who live and die without having a chance to respond positively to the gospel of Jesus Christ will have that chance postmortemly." [^2]

> "Thank God for Joseph Smith! And not merely for resolving one more thorny problem of evil... but for being the instrument through whom God restored the knowledge and priesthood powers that make the redemption of the dead possible." [^3]
