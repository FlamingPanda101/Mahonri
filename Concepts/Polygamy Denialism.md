# Polygamy Denialism
#type/claim

## The Claim
"Polygamy denialism" is the (historically untenable) position — associated with certain RLDS/Community-of-Christ–descended and modern "Josephite" apologists — that Joseph Smith did *not* practice or institute plural marriage, that it was instead introduced by [[Brigham Young]], and that [[Doctrine and Covenants 132|D&C 132]] is a later Brigham-era forgery falsely attributed to Joseph. The vault treats this denial as *historically unsustainable*: the documentary, testimonial, and statistical evidence ties plural marriage and Section 132 firmly to Joseph Smith, so the attempt to "flatten" the doctrine onto Brigham fails.

## Supporting Evidence
1. **Wordprint / stylometric analysis points to Joseph.** Per [[Daniel C. Peterson]], the Interpreter Foundation's wordprint and statistical analysis (Fields et al.) shows that the language of [[Doctrine and Covenants 132|Section 132]] matches Joseph Smith's authorship and that the text "was already in circulation while Joseph was alive," making a Brigham-era forgery "almost certainly impossible." (Developed in [[Doctrine and Covenants 132 Wordprint Analysis]].)
2. **The revelation is internally dated to Joseph.** Section 132 is recorded July 12, 1843 — a year before Joseph's June 1844 death — and is framed as the Lord answering Joseph's inquiry about "Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob... Moses, David and Solomon... touching the principle and doctrine of their having many wives." It presents itself as Joseph's revelation, not Brigham's.
3. **The succession evidence.** New research by [[Ron Esplin]] on the 1844 succession crisis shows the Quorum of the Twelve were the *only* faction that continued Joseph's full program — including plural marriage — which presupposes that program already existed under Joseph (see [[Succession Crisis of 1844]]).
4. **Why the denial is attractive but wrong.** Denialism lets a believer keep Joseph "clean" by relocating an uncomfortable practice onto Brigham (the recurring move catalogued in [[Brigham Young as the Fall Guy]]). The vault's point is that this is motivated reasoning that the primary sources do not support.

**Steelman of the denialist view.** The most sympathetic case: the public record in Joseph's lifetime *denied* polygamy (Nauvoo-era statements, the 1835 D&C's monogamy article), most "evidence" of Joseph's plural marriages is later affidavit testimony gathered in Utah under Brigham's influence, and motive existed to backdate the practice to Joseph for legitimacy. A denialist therefore argues the contemporaneous documents say monogamy and the incriminating ones are post-Joseph. The vault's rebuttal is that the convergence of stylometric data, the 1843 dating of Section 132, multiple independent witnesses, and the succession evidence overwhelms the public-denial counter-record — so denialism survives only by discounting nearly all of it as forgery, which is special pleading.
