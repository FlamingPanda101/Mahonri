# Spirit Birth and Intelligences

A key interpretive debate arising from the [[King Follett Discourse]]: are human
spirits (intelligences) co-eternal with God, or does God *organize* pre-existing
intelligence-material into spirit children through some process of "spirit birth"?
This seemingly narrow question drove a decades-long theological dispute among
LDS leaders.

## The Key Passage

From the King Follett Discourse:

> God found himself in the midst of spirit and glory because he was greater and
> saw proper to institute laws whereby the rest could have a privilege to advance
> like himself.[^1]

## The Two Interpretations

Benjamin Park frames this as a fundamental division:

1. **Co-eternal spirits (Penrose view):** The spirits themselves — individual
   intelligences, the core "self" — are eternal with God. God did not create them;
   he found himself among them and organized a path for their advancement.[^2]

2. **Organized spirits (Roberts view):** There is some kind of spiritual "matter"
   or raw intelligence that God *forms* into personhood — "moving from a soul to a
   spirit" through a process of spirit birth. The intelligences are not persons
   until God organizes them.[^3]

## The BH Roberts / Charles Penrose Dispute

This became a "crucial debate in LDS theological discussions for decades."[^4]

- [[BH Roberts]] was "in the camp saying that there were intelligences that God
  organized into spirits" — a two-step process where raw intelligence is formed
  into spirit children.[^5]
- [[Charles Penrose]] "fundamentally disagreed" with Roberts's interpretation,
  believing "that the spirits themselves were eternal with God."[^5]
- This dispute was one reason Penrose (as part of the First Presidency) was
  comfortable suppressing Roberts's version of the King Follett Discourse from
  the *History of the Church* in 1912.[^6]

## Continued Disagreement

- [[Joseph Fielding Smith]], while championing the discourse, edited out portions
  about spirit birth and "correlated" the discussion "into the mainstream LDS
  teaching."[^7]
- [[Bruce R. McConkie]] also disagreed with Roberts's footnotes on spirit birth.[^8]
- Park notes this may seem "a bit trifling like determining how many angels can
  dance on the head of a needle," but "this ends up being a crucial debate in LDS
  theological discussions for decades."[^9]

## The Core Question

> What is co-eternal with God? The spirit or the soul? Are they the same? Are
> they different?[^10]
