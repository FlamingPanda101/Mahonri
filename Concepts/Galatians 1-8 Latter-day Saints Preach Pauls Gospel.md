# Galatians 1:8 — Latter-day Saints Preach Paul's Own Gospel

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
The [[Galatians 1.8|Galatians 1:8]] attack assumes the Restoration is "another gospel." [[Ward Radio]] argues this begs the question: Paul *defines* his gospel, and the Book of Mormon and [[Joseph Smith]] affirm exactly that definition.[^1]

## The Rebuttal

### 1. Paul refuses to leave "gospel" ambiguous — strong
Paul does not let "gospel" float as "anything outside the Bible." In [[1 Corinthians 15.1-4|1 Corinthians 15:1-4]] he states it: "Christ died for our sins according to the scriptures... was buried... rose again the third day." Three bullet points. See [[Definition of Gospel]].[^2] Rating: **strong** (Paul's explicit self-definition).

### 2. The Book of Mormon preaches the same gospel — strong within the source's frame
[[3 Nephi 11.10-11|3 Nephi 11:10-11]] has the *resurrected* Christ declare he was "whom the prophets testified shall come" and that he "taken upon me the sins of the world" — matching Paul's prophesied-died-rose pattern. The hosts call this "the fifth gospel."[^3]

### 3. Joseph Smith explicitly subordinated all else to this gospel — strong
The hosts cite [[Joseph Smith]]: "the fundamental principles of our religion are the testimony of the apostles and prophets concerning Jesus Christ, that he died, was buried, and rose again on the third day... and all other things which pertain to our religion are only appendages to it."[^4] This makes the resurrection gospel the *core*, not a deviation.

### Steelman of the opposing view
A careful critic would say "gospel" (εὐαγγέλιον) in Paul includes the *whole* apostolic deposit — Christology, soteriology, the nature of God — not merely a three-line résumé of facts; and LDS additions (eternal progression, plurality of gods, temple ordinances) constitute a materially different *system* even if the resurrection slogan matches. **Response:** the rebuttal's force is that Paul himself reduces "the gospel I preached" to the death-burial-resurrection kernel in 1 Cor 15, and Joseph Smith calls everything else "appendages." Whether the LDS *system* differs is a separate debate from whether the *gospel Paul names* is shared — and on Paul's own terms it is shared, so Galatians 1:8 (about "another **gospel**") is the wrong proof-text. The dispute belongs under doctrine-of-God topics, not this verse.
