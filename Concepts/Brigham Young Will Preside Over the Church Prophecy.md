#type/claim

# Brigham Young Will Preside Over the Church Prophecy

## The Claim
[[Joseph Smith]] reportedly asked [[Brigham Young]] to pray; Young spoke in tongues, and Joseph declared, "It is of God, and the time will come when brother Brigham will preside over this church." Young did become the second church president after Joseph's death.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The outcome occurred.** Brigham Young did lead the church, so the prediction "came true" at the level of result. (Evidence rating: outcome real.)[^1]
2. **Recorded in the later *History of the Church*.** The host calls it "a memory and a reminiscence... or this could be a later edition." Given the contested 1844 [[Brigham Young Saved the Church Through the Keys|succession crisis]] (Young vs. Sidney Rigdon, with claims for Joseph Smith III), a prophecy that legitimates Young's leadership is exactly the kind of statement that could be shaped after the fact. (Evidence rating: high retrofit suspicion — see [[Retrofitting a Prophecy After the Fulfillment]].)[^2]
3. **Strong succession-politics motive.** Because the prophecy underwrites the winning faction's legitimacy, it had powerful institutional incentives to be remembered or sharpened in Young's favor. (Evidence rating: a serious confound on the testimony.)[^2]
4. **Steelman of the believing view.** [[Sidney Rigdon]] and others did contest succession, yet the body of the Twelve under Young prevailed and the church survived and grew — a faith narrative reads Joseph's confidence in Young as vindicated foresight. The deflation is that the very dispute over succession gives the strongest reason to distrust a post-hoc, leadership-legitimating prophecy.

## Best Quotes
> "It is of God, and the time will come when brother Brigham will preside over this church."[^1]

> "This could be a prophecy. This is obviously a memory and a reminiscence. Or this could be a later edition."[^2]
