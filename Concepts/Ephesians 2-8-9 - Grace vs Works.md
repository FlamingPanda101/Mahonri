# Ephesians 2:8-9 — Saved by Grace, Not Works

## The Charge
> "Mormons are works-based salvation. Ephesians 2:8-9 says you're saved by grace
> through faith, NOT of works. Mormonism adds works to grace."

## The Verses (KJV)
> "For by grace are ye saved through faith; and that not of yourselves: it is the
> gift of God: Not of works, lest any man should boast." (Ephesians 2:8-9)

## Rebuttal Points

### 1. James 2: Faith Without Works Is Dead
> "Even so faith, if it hath not works, is dead, being alone... Ye see then how
> that by works a man is justified, and not by faith only." (James 2:17, 24)

Paul and James don't contradict — they address different problems:
- **Paul**: combating legalism — you can't earn salvation by law-keeping.
- **James**: combating antinomianism — genuine faith produces action.
- LDS theology sits where both apostles point: grace saves, and faith is
  demonstrated through works.

### 2. The Scooby Analogy (Peter Walking on Water)
Credit: LDS Scooby (Instagram). Matthew 14:28-31.

- **Faith alone**: Peter stays in the boat and says, "I believe that's Jesus" —
  never steps out. Not saved by the experience of walking on water.
- **Works alone**: Peter hops off the boat with zero faith — immediately sinks.
- **What actually saves**: Peter steps out IN FAITH, walks toward Christ, and when
  his faith falters, **the Savior's grace** reaches out and catches him.

> "The faith and the works put us in a position to be saved by the grace of Jesus
> Christ." — Dalton

### 3. C.S. Lewis's Scissors Analogy
Cardon: "Trying to say what is more important, faith or works, is like saying which
blade on a pair of scissors is more important."

### 4. LDS Distinction: Salvation vs. Exaltation
Braden (brief aside): LDS define "salvation" differently:
- **Salvation** (resurrection + forgiveness from sin) = by grace alone, for all.
- **Exaltation** (degrees of glory / returning to God's presence) = requires
  covenant faithfulness.
- Most Christian critics conflate these two categories.

### 5. Demarcating Faith from Works Is Artificial
The woman with the issue of blood (Mark 5:25-34): It wasn't touching Jesus's cloak
that healed her — but the faithful ACT of reaching out triggered the healing grace.
"Her faithful action put her in a position for His magnanimous grace."

## Bottom Line
LDS believe grace alone saves — and that faith is demonstrated through works, as
both Paul and James teach. The faith/works dichotomy is a false dilemma.
