# Avoiding the Pejorative Magic

#type/claim

## The Claim
Serious historians "stay the heck away from" the word **magic** because it is a pejorative used to "other" disfavored groups. The same supernatural act is labeled "religion" or "miracle" by insiders and "magic" or "necromancy" by opponents; better, neutral terms are "folk belief," "folk practice," or even "religious."[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **"Magic" is an othering weapon (definitional).** "It's like what I believe is religion and what you believe is magic"; the word "has been used horribly for othering over the centuries."[^2] *Rating: strong — reflects mainstream religious-studies critique of the magic/religion binary.*

2. **The accusation is endlessly recycled (historical pattern).** The earliest Christian critics called Jesus's healing "magic" (spitting in the ground, making mud); then "the Protestants do it to the Catholics, and then the Protestants do it to the Mormons. Everybody plays this game."[^3] *Rating: strong — well-documented polemical lineage.*

3. **Symmetry with one's own miracles (self-application).** Christian notes the resurrection itself could be branded "necromancy" by a hostile observer, while believers call it a miracle — so wielding "magic" against others rebounds on one's own supernatural commitments.[^4] *Rating: strong — connects to [[Mutual Vulnerability of Supernatural Claims]].*

4. **Practical concession.** Mark admits he may use "magic" occasionally "for ease of reference," but flags that the best historians avoid it; "folk belief" is the disciplined term.[^5] *Rating: contextual — honest about usage.*

5. **Steelman.** A critic could argue that refusing the word "magic" is itself a rhetorical move — sanitizing seer stones and divining rods by relabeling them "folk practice" to dodge the connotations that would otherwise embarrass the Restoration. The reply: the objection to "magic" is methodological (it imports a value judgment masquerading as a category) and is endorsed across secular religious studies, not merely by LDS apologists.

## Best Quotes

> "What I believe is religion and what you believe is magic."[^2]

> "The Protestants do it to the Catholics, and then the Protestants do it to the Mormons, and everybody plays this game ... but historians are trying not to play that game."[^3]

> "Really good historians stay the heck away from using that word."[^5]
