# The Urim and Thummim Is Not a Real Parallel

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
**Objection:** Both *View of the Hebrews* and the [[Book of Mormon]] feature a "Urim and Thummim," proving dependence. [[Ethan Smith]] notes that Native Americans wore a "pontifical dress" (ceremonial clothing) resembling the Urim and Thummim of the Israelite high priest.[^1]

**Rebuttal:** This is a Category-1 non-parallel: **the Book of Mormon never mentions the Urim and Thummim.** The Book of Mormon text speaks only of "interpreters." The "Urim and Thummim" label was applied by later Latter-day Saints, conflating the interpreters with the biblical high-priestly device — the very thing Ethan Smith was actually discussing.[^2]

## The Rebuttal

1. **The term is absent from the Book of Mormon (rated: strong, textual).** "There is no Urim and Thummim. The Book of Mormon never mentions the Urim and Thummim. It mentions interpreters."[^2] The "Urim and Thummim" identification is a later LDS convention (a "conflation, piggybacking off of the biblical Urim and Thummim").
2. **The conceptual frames don't match (rated: strong, textual).** Ethan Smith's whole point is high-priestly ceremonial dress as evidence of Israelite descent. But the Book of Mormon "never talks about high priestly clothing," "never connects the interpreters to the high priest," and contains "no Levitical priesthood ... whatsoever."[^3] The supposed parallel runs in a direction the Book of Mormon never goes.
3. **Both terms trace to the same biblical source (rated: moderate, contextual).** The Urim and Thummim of [[Exodus 28.30|Exodus 28:30]] is a *shared biblical source*; any resemblance is inherited from the Bible, not borrowed from Ethan Smith.[^4] *Steelman:* a critic can say the *later* LDS use of "Urim and Thummim" for the interpreters shows Joseph's milieu (perhaps via Ethan Smith) shaping the translation's vocabulary. Smoot's reply: that is a claim about post-translation nomenclature, not about the Book of Mormon text, and the biblical Urim and Thummim was common cultural property — no path through *View of the Hebrews* is required.

## Best Quotes

> "There isn't a Urim and— There is no Urim and Thummim. The Book of Mormon never mentions the Urim and Thummim. It mentions interpreters."[^2]

> "In fact, there's no Levitical priesthood in the Book of Mormon whatsoever ... it never connects the interpreters to the high priest."[^3]

> "The Urim and Thummim example kind of qualifies under a sort of shared biblical source for both of them."[^4]
