# Heretics Twist Scripture Rather Than Add to It

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
[[Anthony Rogers]] argues a hallmark of heresy is *going beyond* Scripture (adding traditions, other authorities), so sola scriptura is the antidote. [[Trent Horn]] rebuts: heretics characteristically do *not* add to Scripture — they **twist** it. The Jehovah's Witness at your door "turns scripture into a pretzel." So the existence of heresy argues *for* a binding interpretive authority, not for Scripture alone, which heretics happily wield.[^1]

## The Rebuttal

### 1. The Irenaeus mosaic illustration — rate: strong
Horn invokes [[Irenaeus of Lyons]]' image: heretics are like a vandal who takes the gemstones forming a mosaic portrait of a *king* and rearranges them into a *fox* — the same stones (Scripture's words), a corrupted picture.[^1] He cites [[Jerome]] (*Letter to the Luciferians*): "the essence of scripture is not the letter but the meaning." Conclusion: "a book cannot interpret itself, especially when written within an apostolic community to give meaning to it."[^2] **Steelman of Rogers:** Rogers grants heretics twist Scripture but locates the *cause* in their submission to an external authority (the Watchtower) and the noetic effects of sin — the twisting is a symptom of *not* truly going by Scripture alone. See [[Noetic Effects of Sin Explain Interpretive Disagreement]].

### 2. The Trinity debate as exhibit — rate: moderate
Horn points to the Trinity-vs-Unitarian debate earlier at the same conference: one side "expounded scripture correctly" ([[James White]]), the other "twisted" it — both claiming Scripture.[^3] This shows Scripture alone did not adjudicate; an external standard did the sorting. **Steelman of Rogers:** Rogers would say the Trinity *is* clearly in Scripture and the Unitarian is the blind/twisting party — but that just relocates the question to *who decides* which side twists, which is Horn's [[No Final Interpreter - The Protestant Adjudication Problem]].

### 3. "A book cannot interpret itself" — rate: contested
Horn's strong claim against "scripture interprets scripture": meaning requires an interpreting community.[^2] **Steelman of Rogers:** Rogers accepts a *narrow* "scripture interprets scripture" (clearer passages illuminate obscurer ones) but denies the *wide* claim that no authority beyond Scripture is ever needed; he cites Augustine's "consult the rule of faith ... and the authority of the church" as putting *both* on the platform — conceding more than strict formal sufficiency. See [[Material vs Formal Sufficiency - The Home Depot Analogy]].

## Best Quotes
> "You want to know what heretics do? They twist scripture. How many times have you talked to a Jehovah's Witness at your door and all they did was really try to turn scripture into a pretzel?"[^1]

> "Just as a vandal takes these stones and rearranges them to look like a fox instead of the king, heretics take the words of scripture and rearrange them to mean something different."[^1]

> [Jerome, quoted by Horn] "The essence of scripture is not the letter but the meaning ... a book cannot interpret itself."[^2]
