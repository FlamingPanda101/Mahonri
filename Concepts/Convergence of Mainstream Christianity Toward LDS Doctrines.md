# Convergence of Mainstream Christianity Toward LDS Doctrines

## The Claim
Across the denominational spectrum, mainstream Christian scholars and theologians are converging in growing numbers on what were once dismissed as "uniquely Mormon heresies."[^1] Doctrines that historically placed LDS theology "outside the margins of mainstream Christianity are now being reinvestigated just vigorously by theologians."[^2]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Expert Testimony — [[Terrell Given]]:** A professor of religion and Latter-day Saint academic, Given states flatly that "Christians across the denominational spectrum are converging in growing numbers on what used to be considered uniquely Mormon heresies."[^1] He notes this is no longer a fringe development — it is a "vigorous" reinvestigation by credentialed theologians.[^2]

2. **Publishing Wave:** As evidence that this is "more than just a fringe conversation," the video points to a "flurry of books that have come out in the last 20 years" on distinctively LDS claims: *The Embodied God*, *Heavenly Flesh*, *Does God Have a Body?*, *God Had a Wife*, and *Did God Have a Wife?*.[^3] The quantity and recency of academic and popular works on these topics signal mainstream engagement.

3. **Specific Convergent Doctrines:**
   - **Pre-existence:** [[David Bentley Hart]] (Eastern Orthodox theologian, Yale UP author) "has talked about pre-existence."[^4]
   - **Expanded plan of salvation:** "Representatives of almost every church on the planet" now argue God must have "a more generous, capacious plan for those who die without hearing the gospel," rejecting the idea that "God has foreordained vast multitudes to hell."[^5]
   - **Embodied God:** Books on God having a body (*The Embodied God*, *Heavenly Flesh*, *Does God Have a Body?*) address a doctrine central to LDS theology.[^3]
   - **Feminine divine:** Recognition that "the feminine divine was an important part of worship" in the ancient Middle East, with books titled *God Had a Wife* and *Did God Have a Wife?*.[^3]

4. **Trickle-Down Beyond Academia:** Everyday content creators are "presupposing what Latter-day Saints believe" — the video cites a "random content creator" who discussed pre-existence (without using the word) as though it were a normal Christian idea.[^6]

5. **Non-Religious Corroboration:** A world-famous neuroscientist (unnamed in the source) concluded that "consciousness precedes existence" — and when asked point-blank whether individual spirits could have existed before incarnation, replied "Yeah, that makes sense to me. That seems to be a possibility."[^7]

## Best Quote

> "It's also the case that the Christians across the denominational spectrum are converging in growing numbers on what used to be considered uniquely Mormon heresies." — [[Terrell Given]], on Be Faithful podcast[^1]

## Counter-Arguments

- **Representativeness objection:** A "flurry of books" from sympathetic scholars may not reflect the actual beliefs of mainstream Christian laity or institutional doctrine. Publishing trends and ecumenical theology sometimes differ significantly from what churches officially teach.
- **Correlation vs. causation:** Academic interest in these topics may be driven by internal developments in Christian theology (e.g., renewed study of early Jewish divine council traditions) rather than any convergence *toward* Mormonism specifically.
- **Selection bias:** The scholars cited (Hart, the neuroscientist) may be outliers rather than representatives of a broad trend.
