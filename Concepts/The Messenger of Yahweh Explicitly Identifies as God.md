# The Messenger of Yahweh Explicitly Identifies as God

## The Claim
[[Dan McClellan]] argues that the angel/messenger of Yahweh (*malʾak YHWH*) "is not Yahweh — he's the angel of Yahweh, the messenger of Yahweh, but he's treated as if he's Yahweh." [^1] Crucially, twice in the Hebrew Bible the messenger does not merely speak *for* God but **explicitly identifies as God in the first person**: in [[Scriptures/Genesis 31.13|Genesis 31:13]] the messenger tells Jacob "I am the God of Bethel," and in [[Scriptures/Exodus 3.6|Exodus 3:6]] he tells Moses "I am the God of your father." [^2] This explicit self-identification is the datum every competing framework must explain.

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Two unambiguous first-person identifications.** The texts are not vague: the messenger says "I am the God of Bethel" and "I am the God of your father." *Rating: strong as a textual observation — these are the clearest agent-as-deity statements in the Hebrew Bible, and McClellan repeatedly returns to them as the controlling data.* [^2]

2. **The messenger is more explicit than Jesus.** McClellan stresses Jesus "never says what the messenger says... never says 'I am the God of your father,' never says 'I am the God of Bethel.'" The Hebrew messenger is therefore "far more explicit than Jesus ever is." *Rating: a sharp comparative point that undercuts arguments treating Jesus' indirect language as proof of deity.* [^3] *Steelman of the Trinitarian side:* Jesus' implicit claims (the "I am" sayings, forgiving sins, John 1:1, 20:28) may carry deity-implication precisely because explicit "I am God" statements were blasphemous in a first-century monotheistic setting, so directness is not the only measure.

3. **Only one contemporaneous framework explains it.** McClellan argues that of the candidate explanations — LDS divine investiture, Trinitarianism/Binitarianism, and the intuitive logic of divine images — only the **divine-image** logic was actually "in circulation when these texts were written," where "a divine image could be explicitly identified as the deity that the image indexed." *Rating: the core of his constructive case; depends on his claim that the other frameworks are anachronistic (see related notes).* [^4]

## Best Quotes
> "The angel of the Lord is not Yahweh. He's the angel of Yahweh. He's the messenger of Yahweh, but he's treated as if he's Yahweh." [^1]

> "We have the messenger of Adonai twice in the Hebrew Bible explicitly identifying as God. In Genesis 31:13, the messenger tells Jacob, 'I am the God of Bethel.' In Exodus 3:6, the messenger tells Moses, 'I am the God of your father.'" [^2]

> "Jesus never says what the messenger says... So the messenger is far more explicit than Jesus ever is." [^3]
