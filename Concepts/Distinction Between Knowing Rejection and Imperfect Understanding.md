# Distinction Between Knowing Rejection and Imperfect Understanding

## The Claim
[[Gavin Ortlund]] repeatedly invokes a distinction that softens his exclusivist claim: there is a difference between **a knowing rejection** of a truth about God and **an imperfect understanding** of it. [^1] "God knows the heart and he knows the details... I'm not trying to make an exact pronouncement on this exact person." [^2] He applies it to a young child becoming a Christian who lacks technical vocabulary [^3] and to a sincere modalist, whom he grants is not thereby damned. [^4] His claims of salvation-necessity attach to the *substance* of belief and to *knowing* rejection, not to technical precision or honest confusion.

## Supporting Evidence (and steelman of the tension)

1. **It preserves epistemic humility within exclusivism.** "I don't know exactly what God is going to do in every individual case. I certainly hope for salvation for people." [^5] *Rating: a principled qualifier* — it lets Ortlund affirm a hard boundary while disclaiming knowledge of who falls where, framing his role as faithful witness, not judge.

2. **It is load-bearing for the modalism concession.** Because a sincere modalist merely *misunderstands*, Ortlund can say "of course not" they are not unable to be saved (see [[Fruit Versus Theology - Are They Separable]]). [^4] *Rating: consistent application,* and it disarms Hansen's "would God damn a confused believer?" objection.

3. **The tension Hansen presses (steelman).** If imperfect understanding is excusable, why is the LDS view not also "imperfect understanding" rather than "knowing rejection"? Hansen casts his own position as honest inability — "it isn't that I don't believe it, it's that I... can't even make sense of it." [^6] *Rating: a sharp challenge* — it forces Ortlund to claim the LDS view is *substantively a different God* (a knowing departure in content), not mere fuzziness, which routes back to the ontology dispute (see [[Humans Are the Same Kind as God (LDS Ontology)]]).

4. **Ortlund's implicit reply.** The modalist still worships *the same uncreated God* (just mis-modeling the persons), whereas the LDS, by rejecting creatio ex nihilo and the Creator/creature divide, worship (on Ortlund's analysis) a *different kind of being*. [^7] *Rating: this is the crux that the distinction cannot settle by itself* — whether LDS belief is a misunderstanding of the same God or commitment to a different one is precisely what is disputed.

## Best Quotes
> "I do make a distinction between a knowing rejection of something and an imperfect understanding of it. And so God knows the heart and he knows the details." [^1]

> "Think of a young child... who becomes a Christian. They may not know all the technical stuff. Again, there's that distinction between a knowing rejection and an imperfect understanding." [^3]

> "[A sincere modalist] — does that change their relation in a substantial way to where they now can't be saved? I would say no, of course not." [^4]

> "It isn't that I don't believe it. It's that I... can't even make sense of it. It doesn't seem to even be able to jive rationally." [^6]
