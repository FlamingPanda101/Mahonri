# Thermometer vs Thermostat — The Church Declares the Canon

## The Claim
[[Joe Heschmeyer]] articulates the Catholic model of the church's relationship to the canon using [[Michael Krueger]]'s own illustration, then sharpens it with a *third* option Krueger omits. A **thermometer** reports the temperature; a **thermostat** sets it. The Catholic view is neither pure thermometer nor thermostat but a precise middle: the church **infallibly declares** the canon [^1].

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. Three positions, not two — rate: clarifying
Heschmeyer distinguishes:
1. The church **infallibly determines / creates** the canon (thermostat — making uninspired books inspired). *Catholics deny this* (condemned by Vatican I; see [[Krugers Strawman of the Catholic View of the Canon]]).
2. The church merely **discovers** the canon with no special authority (a fallible thermometer that happens to read correctly). *Catholics deny this too.*
3. The church **infallibly declares** the canon — recognizing, under the Spirit's guidance, books that were inspired "from the moment they were written" [^1]. This is the Catholic position. See [[Authorization - Divine Designation Required for an Infallible Canon]].

### 2. The thermometer analogy fits the early-Christian data — rate: strong
[[Trent Horn]] likes the image: an individual without a thermometer "can still have a rough idea — I can feel the difference between 50 and 80 degrees, but I'm not going to know precisely." Likewise early Christians had a general sense (Matthew, Mark, Luke, John clearly in; Hebrews, 2 Peter, Revelation disputed) but needed the church to settle the borderline "72 or 74 degrees" cases — the deuterocanon / antilegomena [^2].

### 3. Declaration is stronger than discovery — rate: strong
The crucial word is *infallibly*. A merely human discovery (even a correct one) leaves the canon a "fallible collection of infallible books" ([[R.C. Sproul]]) and hostage to revision. An *infallible declaration* gives a closed, secure canon. See [[The Fallible Canon Is Hostage to Critical Scholarship]] and [[Inspired vs Infallible vs Ultimate - A Distinction]] [^3]. **Steelman of the Protestant:** a Reformed thinker may say "infallible declaration" just smuggles in an infallible church Scripture never authorizes — but the Catholic reply is that recognition of inspired books *requires* a Spirit-protected recognizer, exactly the gap self-authentication cannot fill.

## Best Quotes
> "We're not saying that the church infallibly determines the canon, but we're also not saying that the church infallibly discovers the canon either. We have a middle position, which would be that the church infallibly declares the canon." [^1]

> "As an individual non-thermostat, I can still have a rough idea. I can feel the difference between 50 and 80 degrees, but I'm not going to know precisely... you might say, is it 72 or 74 degrees?" [^2]
