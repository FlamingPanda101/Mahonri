# Brigham Young as Spiritual Man Not Just Colonizer

## The Claim
The popular (and even faithful) image of [[Brigham Young]] reduces him to a practical genius — "the colonizing genius, the organizer, the great practical man of affairs," a "natural-born engineer and MBA." Peterson argues this flattens away a genuinely *spiritual* Brigham: a man of revelation, spiritual gifts, and temple devotion.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Speaking in tongues to the oxen (rated: moderate; documented anecdote).** *Six Days in August* opens with Brigham speaking in tongues to soothe two oxen mired in mud and blocking the road; they rise and move. "People have said to me... You made that up. No, we didn't. That is the historical document. That actually happened."[^2] The scene is staged precisely to subvert the "mere organizer" image.

2. **Speaking in tongues before Joseph Smith (rated: strong; multiply attested).** At Brigham's first meeting with [[Joseph Smith]], Brigham spoke in tongues. [[Sydney Rigdon]] objected ("gibberish... not a gift from God"); Joseph affirmed, "It is from God." On that same occasion Joseph reportedly said of Brigham, "That man will succeed me in the leadership of the Church" — heard by [[Heber C. Kimball]] and Joseph Young.[^3]

3. **He claimed revelation more than commonly thought (rated: moderate).** Brigham downplayed revelation so as not to "seem to rival Joseph," but did claim it. A journal entry records him putting to the Lord whether to finish the Nauvoo Temple under mounting violence: "the answer was yes." "Clearly he's gotten an answer from the Lord."[^4] *Steelman:* sparse self-reporting makes the *extent* of his revelatory claims hard to quantify; Peterson's point is directional (more than the stereotype allows), not statistical.

4. **Temple-building as his organizing passion (rated: strong; thematic).** Peterson, writing a Gospel Doctrine lesson, deliberately emphasized "Brigham Young the temple builder." At the St. George Temple dedication Brigham said he wanted "the tongues of seven thunders to wake this people up" — if they grasped the work's importance "this house would be open 24 hours a day, seven days a week." From a worldly view temples are "pointless," yet Brigham spent day and night doing ordinances at Nauvoo while he "should be out outfitting wagons," and marked the Salt Lake temple site first thing on arrival. "It was to build the kingdom, and the kingdom was about temples."[^5] (Notably, correlation later reverted that lesson to "Brigham the colonizer.")

## Best Quotes

> "Shows Brigham Young speaking to an ox. ... begins to speak to them in tongues. ... People have said... You made that up. No, we didn't. That is the historical document."[^2]

> "Brigham Young was the first one to speak in tongues in front of Joseph Smith. And Sidney Rigdon... objected. It's gibberish... And they said, Well, what do you think, Joseph? Joseph says, It is from God."[^3]

> "Sometimes I want the tongues of seven thunders to wake this people up. If they knew how important this work is, this house would be open 24 hours a day, seven days a week."[^5]
