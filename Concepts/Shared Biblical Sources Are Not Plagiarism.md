# Shared Biblical Sources Are Not Plagiarism

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
**Objection:** [[Ethan Smith]] writes of the lost tribes wandering "to a land where mankind had never before dwelt," and the [[Book of Mormon]] (Book of Ether) says the Jaredites went "to that part of the country where there never had man been." This shared phrasing shows the Book of Mormon copied *View of the Hebrews*.[^1]

**Rebuttal:** Category-3 parallel: both texts independently draw on a **shared biblical/apocryphal source**. As [[Blake Ostler]] pointed out, Ethan Smith is quoting **2 Esdras (the Apocrypha)** — so the Book of Mormon and Ethan Smith resemble each other only because both echo a third, prior text available to any 19th-century Bible reader.[^2]

## The Rebuttal

1. **The phrase comes from 2 Esdras, not Ethan Smith (rated: strong, source-critical).** "That's from the Bible. ... Ethan Smith is quoting Second Esdras. He's quoting the Apocrypha. So that's a shared biblical source."[^2] A common ancestor explains the resemblance without any link between the two later works.
2. **The figures don't even match (rated: strong, textual).** Ethan Smith applies the phrase to the *lost ten tribes of Israel*; the Book of Mormon applies it to the *Jaredites* — who are "not the lost ten tribes of Israel. In fact, not even Israelites at all."[^3] The borrowed biblical motif is deployed for entirely different peoples.
3. **Same pattern as the Urim and Thummim (rated: moderate, contextual).** Smoot notes the [[Exodus 28.30|Urim and Thummim]] resemblance "kind of qualifies under a sort of shared biblical source" too — and likens it to claims that LDS temple worship "plagiarizes" Freemasonry when the supposedly damning element is "also in the Bible."[^4] *Steelman:* a critic can argue that the *specific selection* of an obscure 2 Esdras passage by both authors is itself suggestive — most writers don't quote the Apocrypha. Smoot's reply: a shared source is the textbook alternative explanation that defeats a dependence claim, and dependence requires ruling out all such alternatives (see [[Direct Literary Dependence Requires Exclusivity]]).

## Best Quotes

> "Kind of reminds me of when people criticize like the temple being a plagiarism of Freemasonry ... others are just like, 'But you also find that in the Bible.'"[^4]
