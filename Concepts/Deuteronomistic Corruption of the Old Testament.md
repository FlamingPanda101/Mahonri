# Deuteronomistic Corruption of the Old Testament

## The Thesis
The dominant religion of Jesus's day was not the ancient religion of Israel —
it was **Deuteronomism**. The Deuteronomists were the school of scribes and
lawyers who:
- Murdered the prophets (Isaiah, Zechariah, Uriah)
- Drove out Lehi
- Killed Jeremiah
- "Found" Deuteronomy in the temple and rewrote the canon around it
- Suppressed messianic prophecy in favor of legalistic ritual

## The Historical Context

### Josiah's "Discovery" (2 Kings 22)
During Josiah's reign, Hilkiah the priest "found the book of the law" in the
temple. This book — Deuteronomy — became the basis for Josiah's reforms. The
Deuteronomistic school then edited the historical books (Joshua, Judges,
Samuel, Kings) to interpret Israel's history through the lens of Deuteronomy's
blessing-and-curse theology.

### What Was Suppressed
- Messianic prophecies
- Theophanies and angelic visitations
- Temple as a place of revelation (reduced to sacrifice-only)
- The brass plates' content (taken by Lehi)

### The Blood-Soaked Temple
This is the temple where Zechariah was murdered for preaching messianic
prophecy — and where his blood boiled on the stones for 200 years. "This
defiled, blood-soaked temple is the temple in which they go, 'Hey, look,
we found a new book, and let's rewrite all the old books.'"

## Jesus's Confrontation with Deuteronomism

### The Hand-Washing Confrontation (Luke 11)
The Pharisees' hand-washing law derives from Deuteronomistic oral tradition.
Jesus's response invokes murdered prophets — including Zechariah who was
killed by the same school.

### "You Sit in Moses' Seat" (Matthew 23:2)
Jesus acknowledges the scribes and Pharisees have institutional authority
but says they are spreading false religion — "you dishonor the prophets'
memory by lying about their legacy."

### The Lawyers
"Woe unto you lawyers!" (Luke 11:46, 52) — Jesus explicitly names the
scribal/legal class as perpetuators of the corrupted canon.

## LDS Implications

### The Brass Plates
Jonah's thesis: Lehi took the brass plates — containing the uncorrupted
prophetic tradition including messianic prophecies, theophanies, and the
stories the Deuteronomists excised — out of Jerusalem before its
destruction. The Book of Mormon preserves what was lost.

### Deuteronomy's Late "Discovery"
If Deuteronomy was "found" and edited into the canon during Josiah's
reforms (~622 BC), then the "canon" was already being rewritten before
the exile. The idea of a pristine, unchanged Old Testament is historically
untenable.

### Jesus's Canon Was Broader
Jesus references traditions from the Apocrypha and oral tradition as common
knowledge. The Protestant 66-book canon is narrower than the canon Jesus
Himself used.

## Key Apologetic Points
- The Old Testament was edited by a specific theological school with an
  agenda (anti-messianic, pro-legalistic)
- Deuteronomy is not "the law of Moses" in its original form — it's a
  Deuteronomistic redaction
- LDS claims about "plain and precious things" being removed (1 Nephi 13)
  align with the historical reality of Deuteronomistic editing
- Jesus Himself criticized this corruption and drew on the broader
  tradition the Deuteronomists suppressed
