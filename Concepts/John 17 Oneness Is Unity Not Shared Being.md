# John 17 Oneness Is Unity Not Shared Being

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)

The objection: the "oneness" texts — "I and my Father are one" ([[John 10.30|John 10:30]]), the high-priestly prayer ([[John 17.20-23|John 17:20-23]]) — prove the Father and Son share one *being*. [^1]

## The Rebuttal

1. **Jesus defines the oneness as the kind disciples can share.** In [[John 17.20-23|John 17]] Jesus prays "that they all may be one, as thou, Father, art in me, and I in thee; that they also may be one in us." The *same* oneness he has with the Father he asks his disciples to have. If that oneness were *shared being*, disciples would become consubstantial with God — absurd. Therefore it is a oneness of *unity* (will, glory, relationship). (Evidence: very strong — the prayer's explicit "as we are one" applied to humans is decisive against the shared-being reading.) [^2]
2. **It's about glory and perfection, not ministry logistics.** Against the deflation "he just means they'll be united for ministry," Jesus grounds it in "the glory which thou gavest me I have given to them, that they may be one even as we are one ... that they may be made perfect in one." Perfection *is* a unified relationship into which they are brought. (Evidence: solid contextual reading; the glory-language exceeds mere cooperation.) [^3]
3. **The diagnostic.** Ask of John 17: "Is this a oneness of *unity* or a oneness of *ontology*?" The answer the text forces — unity — is the answer for the Father/Son oneness too. [^4]
4. **Steelman.** The Trinitarian replies that the comparison "*as* we are one" is *analogical*, not identical: the believers' unity is a created *participation* in (and pattern after) the uncreated unity of Father and Son, which is of a different and higher order (consubstantial). The same Gospel ([[John 10.30|John 10:30]] in context, [[John 1.1|1:1]], [[John 20.28|20:28]]) ascribes to the Son what is true of God alone, so John's "oneness" carries an ontological weight the believers' unity images but doesn't equal. (Evidence: a fair reading of analogy; "as" need not mean "to the identical degree.") The LDS reply: granting analogy, the burden is to show the Father/Son oneness is *ontological* rather than the *same* relational unity raised to a higher degree — and nothing in John 17 supplies "same being," which must be imported. [^5]

## Best Quotes

> "I want them to be one with us. Is that a oneness of unity that Jesus has with the Father? Or is it a oneness of ontology? It's a oneness of unity. They're not the same being." [^2]

> "The glory which thou gavest me I have given to them, that they may be one even as we are one ... perfection is a type of unified relationship." [^3]
