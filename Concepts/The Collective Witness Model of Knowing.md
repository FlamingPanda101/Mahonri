# The Collective Witness Model of Knowing

## The Point
[[Jacob Hansen]]'s constructive epistemology: knowledge is *confidence in the reality of something*, and that confidence is built by five independent "witnesses" — **intuition, sense data, outcomes, reason, and authority**. No single witness is infallible or a "trump card." Truth is approached by **triangulation**: a system of checks and balances in which independent witnesses mutually validate (or falsify) a claim. Confidence rises when many witnesses align and falls when they conflict.[^1][^2]

## Argument
1. **Knowledge = confidence in reality, gained through five witnesses.** Hansen recaps the series: knowledge is ultimately confidence in the reality of something, and that confidence comes via intuition, sense data, outcomes, reason, and authority.[^1]
2. **No witness is infallible or supreme.** The recurring error he targets is the idea that "one of these witnesses trumps all of the others" or is infallible, giving 100% certainty.[^1] Both the [[The Heresy of Authority Alone|authority-alone]] and [[The Heresy of Feelings Alone|feelings-alone]] errors are special cases.
3. **Witnesses check one another (triangulation).** "Our intuitions can be mistaken and we can know that they're mistaken because of things like reason, the words of the prophets, sensory data or outcomes." The model is "like a system of checks and balances or a system of triangulation where independent factors come together to mutually validate the reality of something."[^2][^3]
4. **Confidence scales with convergence.** When witnesses conflict, "the truth is simply less clearly manifest" and one should be cautious about declarations; when "all of the witnesses align or many of them speak in unison... we should feel more confident that it's real and true."[^2]
5. **The tools are domain-weighted.** Some witnesses suit some inquiries better: sense data and empirical evidence weigh heavily in the physical world; intuition weighs heavily in beauty and moral obligation; in institutional religious practice authority "may be given more weight than mere intuition."[^4]
6. **Even science is not single-witness.** Empirical experience and mathematical reasoning can conflict (quantum mechanics, relativity); scientific consensus is a reliable authority but "certainly is not infallible" and must be checked against reason, outcomes, and evidence.[^3]
7. **No magic formula — walk by faith.** "There is no magic formula, there is no trump card." As Paul said, "we see through a glass darkly" ([[1 Corinthians 13.12]]) and "we walk by faith" ([[2 Corinthians 5.7]]). The goal is to use *all* the tools and not assume one "Reigns Supreme."[^2]

## Best Quotes
> "This collective witness model of knowing the world is like a system of checks and balances or a system of triangulation where independent factors come together to mutually validate the reality of something." [^3]

> "When all of the witnesses align or many of them speak in unison on a particular subject we should feel more confident that it's real and true. Here's the thing: there is no magic formula, there is no trump card." [^2]

> "We have been endowed with a collective set of tools to come to know the reality that surrounds us, and if we want to see all of reality properly we must utilize all of these tools and not assume that one Reigns Supreme." [^4]
