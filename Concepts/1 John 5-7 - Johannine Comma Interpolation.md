# 1 John 5:7 — The Johannine Comma (Interpolation) Rebutted

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
[[1 John 5.7|1 John 5:7]] — "the Father, the Word, and the Holy Ghost: and these three are one" — is the Bible's clearest statement of the Trinity.

## The Rebuttal

### 1. The clause is a late interpolation
The phrase (the **Comma Johanneum**) is absent from the earliest Greek manuscripts and surfaces only centuries later.

> "What we come to learn about 1 John 5:7 is that it was not in any of the earliest manuscripts that we have of the New Testament. It came hundreds of years later... meaning it was added. It was not originally written." [^1]

### 2. How it crept in
Likely a marginal gloss — a copyist's note on verses 6 and 8 — that a later transcriber mistook for original text and folded in. "It's called interpolation" [^2].

### 3. Modern translations drop it
Critical translations (NRSV and others) omit it; there the verse speaks of "the Spirit, the water, and the blood," which "agree" — quite different from the Trinitarian wording. Even the Catholic textual tradition differs from the KJV here [^3].

### 4. The apologetic turnabout
A critic who objects to "adding to or removing from the Bible" should be troubled that a verse had to be *added* to state the Trinity at all — demonstrable evidence the received text is fallible "because of the mistakes of men," not the word of God [^4].
