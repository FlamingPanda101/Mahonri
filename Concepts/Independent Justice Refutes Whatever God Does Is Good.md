# Independent Justice Refutes Whatever God Does Is Good

## The Point
The breakdown identifies the *load-bearing* disagreement of the whole debate: [[James White]] "makes this case over and over again that whatever God does is inherently good," so that if God acts unjustly, "now it's become just." The panel's rebuttal is that this empties the word *good* of all content — it becomes "an empty signifier" into which you can put anything ("put anything in that X"). Against it, the Latter-day Saint holds that **justice exists on an independent principle**: it is not "just whatever God says is just." This is the substantive (not merely rhetorical) core of why the Calvinist God can be called reprehensible at all — because there is a standard of justice external to God's bare will by which his decrees can be measured.

## Evidence
1. **White's position: God-defines-good by fiat.** "James White, he makes this case over and over and over again, that whatever God does is inherently good... If God does something unjust, well, now it's become just. It doesn't care. It's just the definition that you give it." [^1]
2. **The empty-signifier diagnosis.** "Whatever God does by definition is good. It's an empty signifier... Put anything in that X." Once "good" means only "whatever God wills," moral language collapses and you "can't even talk about love cuz love is just anything God does." [^2] See [[The Appeal to Mystery Empties the Word Good of Meaning]].
3. **The LDS counter: justice is independent.** "The differentiation is justice exists outside of God. It's not just whatever God says is just... justice exists on an independent principle." The canon's repeated refrain — God "would cease to be God" if he destroyed justice ([[Alma 42.13|Alma 42]]) — only makes sense if justice is a standard God *conforms to*, not one he invents. [^3]
4. **The room couldn't tolerate definitions.** Tellingly, the audience grew angry when Hansen simply *defined* a word (love, justice) without quoting a verse — revealing a framework in which words have no fixed meaning apart from proof-texts, the very move that lets "good" float free. [^4] See [[The Euthyphro Dilemma Defeats Divine Command Theory]].

## Best Quotes
> "James White makes this case over and over... that whatever God does is inherently good. But what we're saying is justice exists on an independent principle." [^3]

## LDS Connection
This is the Latter-day Saint answer to divine-command metaethics in vernacular form: like [[Blake Ostler]]'s appeal to the [[The Law of Love Is the Single Ground of Morality|law of love]] as a brute relational reality, the panel grounds morality in principles God Himself honors rather than authors. The supporting canon is dense — [[Alma 42.13|Alma 42]] ("God would cease to be God"), [[2 Nephi 2.13|2 Nephi 2:13]] ("if these things are not, there is no God"), [[Acts 10.34|Acts 10:34]] ("God is no respecter of persons"). A God constrained by independent justice also dissolves the problem of evil better than an unconstrained one — see [[A Constrained God Solves the Problem of Evil Better Than Creedal Christianity]] — and exposes the Euthyphro horn White impales himself on in [[The Euthyphro Dilemma Defeats Divine Command Theory]].
