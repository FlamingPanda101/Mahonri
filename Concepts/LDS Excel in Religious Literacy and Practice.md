# LDS Excel in Religious Literacy and Practice

#type/claim

## The Claim
Latter-day Saints score notably high in religious knowledge, devotional practice, and intergenerational transmission — to the point that a non-LDS author coined the phrase **"Mormon Envy"** to describe how well the Church does with its youth.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **"Mormon Envy" — external testimony (Evidence: strong, outsider source).** The Christian book *Almost Christian* — about how mainstream/evangelical youth are merely "almost Christian," "at the threshold ... not all in or all out" — contains a chapter titled **"Mormon Envy"** about LDS youth engagement. An outsider's praise is harder to dismiss as in-group boasting.[^2] (Author: Kenda Creasy Dean.)

2. **Doctrinal literacy (Evidence: research-cited).** "The research finds that our youth know more about their religion than those of other religions ... you ask them doctrinal questions and they're able to answer on average better."[^2] Corroborated independently by Pew's U.S. Religious Knowledge surveys, where Mormons consistently rank near the top.

3. **Devotional practice, from Pew (Evidence: strong).** "We tend to pray more. We tend to read scriptures more." Critically, Latter-day Saints "pray and read scriptures with our children more than those of other faiths" — a leading indicator for transmitting religion to the next generation.[^3]

4. **Favorable demographics (Evidence: demographic).** Relatively young membership and higher fertility, versus "top heavy" aging in many religions, "bespeaks ... really good things for the church" demographically.[^4]

**Steelman of the opposing view:** Religious *knowledge* and *practice* measure catechetical effectiveness and behavioral compliance, not whether the doctrines are true — a highly literate, highly observant membership is equally consistent with a well-run false religion. Pew knowledge scores partly reflect distinctive vocabulary and correlate with education/seminary structure rather than spiritual depth. Demographic advantages (youth, fertility) are themselves eroding among younger LDS cohorts (see [[Younger Generations Less Religious Forecasts Continued Decline]]), so present demographic strength may not persist.
