# Owning the Reprehensible God Concedes the Debate

## The Point
The resolution was **"Is the God of Calvinism morally reprehensible?"** — a question about a *moral predicate*, not about which tradition is true. The *Stick of Joseph* panel's central analytical claim is that [[James White]] effectively lost it by *affirming* the predicate: both men agreed the Calvinist God, as described, is abhorrent, and "one was saying that's God, the other was saying that's not God." Once White owned the wrath-display deity — and, in the closing Q&A, declined to deny that the rape of little girls glorifies God — there was nothing left to debate. The argument is that the debate is structurally won the *second* a Calvinist acknowledges the portrait and still calls it God.

## Evidence
1. **Both debaters were "on the same team" describing the God.** The panel notes that Hansen and White agreed on the *content* of the Calvinist deity and disagreed only on whether to worship it — so the only live question (is it reprehensible?) was conceded by White's affirmation. [^1]
2. **White owned the wrath as God's essential nature.** Channeling Jonathan Edwards' *Sinners in the Hands of an Angry God* ("the spider being held over a fire"), the panel says White made wrath and the threat of torment God's *basic* nature — "that God is that torture, that executioner." A being so described "is abhorrent. Like the debate is won." [^2] See [[Hell for Gods Glory Is Narcissism Not Love]].
3. **The closing Q&A sealed the optics.** Asked whether the rape of little girls glorifies God, Hansen said "no, obviously"; White, the panel reports, would not give the same plain answer — on Good Friday, after Hansen's testimony that "Christ does not believe in a different Jesus than you... he loves every single one of you." The contrast ("Jesus, Good Friday" vs. "I'm not going to play an emotions game") is read as decisive. [^3]
4. **The book-dodge confirms the concession.** Rather than rebut, White retreated repeatedly to "have you read my book *The Potter's Freedom*?" and, asked to name *one* thing Hansen got wrong, said only that it "would take a whole lot more time than I've got." A refusal to specify the error is treated as having none. [^4]

## Best Quotes
> "That being's abhorrent. Like the debate is won. The second this guy can acknowledge... they were both on the same team in this conversation. They were both saying the Calvinist God is abhorrent. One was saying that's God. The other was saying that's not God." [^2]

> "And then you have Q&A's and you say that the rape of little girls glorifies God and it's like, 'Why are we still in this room?'" [^3]

> "Well, just give me just name one. — Uh, well, I already did... Oh, that would take a whole lot more time than I've got." [^4]

## LDS Connection
The Latter-day Saint position needs no metaphysical heavy lifting to win this particular resolution: it simply refuses to identify God with the portrait White accepts. Because Latter-day Saints hold that justice is an independent principle ([[Independent Justice Refutes Whatever God Does Is Good]]) and that God is the loving Father of every person ([[Doctrine of Divine Childhood Versus Total Depravity]]), "morally reprehensible" cannot attach to the God they worship — whereas the Calvinist, having owned the description, has no exit. Compare the systematic version of this argument in [[The Calvinist God Cannot Love Because He Controls Everything]] and the full debate at [[Thoughtful Faith - James White vs Jacob Hansen - Is the God of Calvinism Morally Reprehensible (Debate)]].
