# Freemasonry

**Freemasonry** is a fraternal order, formalized in early-18th-century Britain, whose lodges confer a graded series of degrees (Entered Apprentice, Fellow Craft, Master Mason, and higher "appendant" degrees) through ritual dramas that employ stonemason's tools as moral symbols, secret signs, tokens (handclasps), passwords, and penalties. [[Joseph Smith]] and many early Latter-day Saint leaders were initiated into Masonry at **Nauvoo in March 1842**, and the LDS **temple endowment** was introduced weeks later (May 1842), producing a long-running debate over the relationship between the two rites. Surface similarities — ritual handclasps, signs, tokens, an oath structure, and aprons — are real and acknowledged; the dispute is over what they *mean*.

## Relevance
Freemasonry is the hinge of the **"borrowing vs. restoration"** debate the vault engages repeatedly. The critic's charge (e.g. in the CES Letter / "Light and Truth Letter" critiques) is that Joseph simply *plagiarized* the endowment from the Masonic ritual he had just received. The apologetic replies in the vault run on three tracks: (1) the endowment's *content* — creation, fall, redemption, enthronement — follows an ancient Near Eastern temple pattern that Masonry lacks and that Joseph could not have known in 1842 ([[Temple Origins Run Deeper Than Freemasonry]]); (2) "restoration" properly means *adaptation and accommodation*, so even genuine Masonic borrowing functioned as a *catalyst for revelation*, not the source ([[Restoration Is Adaptation and Accommodation Not a One-to-One Copy]]); and (3) the critics whipsaw between calling Joseph an anti-Mason and a Mason-plagiarist, an inconsistency that weakens the charge.
