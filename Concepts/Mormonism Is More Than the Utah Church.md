# Mormonism Is More Than the Utah Church

## The Claim
[[Kyle Beshears]] argues that "one of the biggest misconceptions about Mormonism is that all of it is concentrated in Utah" — and, relatedly, that "Mormonism" equals only the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints headquartered in Salt Lake City. His answer to both is "no." [^1][^2]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **A rich diversity of restoration branches.** Beshears defines the family broadly as movements "founded by people who believe Joseph Smith was a prophet and that the Book of Mormon is scripture," then surveys several "in just a handful of paragraphs." [^3] *Rating: accurate taxonomy* — the standard scholarly way to bound the Latter Day Saint movement.

2. **The named branches.** Second-largest today is **Community of Christ** (formerly RLDS), headquartered in Independence, Missouri; then the **Church of Jesus Christ ("Bickertonites")** after prophet [[William Bickerton]]; and his favorite, the **Strangites** of [[James Strang]]. [^4] *Rating: correct;* these are the principal surviving and historically significant branches.

3. **Majority ≠ monopoly on definition.** The Salt Lake church is "the majority by far and away, probably 90% or more... but simply because they're the majority doesn't mean that they should get to dominate how we understand Mormonism." [^5] *Rating: a sound methodological point* — a fair description of the movement cannot collapse it into its largest faction.

**Steelman of the opposing view.** A Latter-day Saint could reply that since the Salt Lake church holds ~90%+ of adherents and continuous prophetic succession, treating it as "Mormonism" proper is reasonable shorthand, and that the smaller branches diverge so far (Community of Christ now ordains women and has moved toward mainline Protestantism) that lumping them together obscures more than it clarifies. Beshears's point is descriptive completeness, not theological equivalence among the branches.

## Best Quotes
> "One of the biggest misconceptions about Mormonism is that all of it is concentrated in Utah." [^1]

> "Is Mormonism only the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints headquartered in Salt Lake City? And the answer is also no." [^2]

> "Simply because they're the majority doesn't mean that they should get to dominate how we understand Mormonism." [^5]
