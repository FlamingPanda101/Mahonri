# Ritual Abuse Crime Unit Found Ninety-Nine Percent Wild Goose Chases

## The Claim
Utah's state-funded ritual-abuse crime unit, staffed with a cult-experienced investigator and given nearly 300 cases, substantiated only three or four — about a 99% failure rate that empirically refuted the existence of widespread satanic ritual abuse.

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. A dedicated, funded unit (strong)
Lawmakers set aside $250,000 to create a ritual-abuse crime unit within the Utah Attorney General's office; [[Mike King]] was invited to join because of his cult-investigation experience, calling the mandate "way outside of normal police work... we're going to pursue Satan" [^1].

### 2. ~300 cases, 3–4 substantiated (strong — primary investigator)
The unit was handed "almost 300 cases of ritual sexual abuse." Asked how many proved out, King answered: "Three or four. That's around 99% wild goose chases" [^2].

### 3. This is the strongest class of evidence in the video (assessment)
Unlike a defense attorney or a sympathetic narrator, King was the official tasked with *finding* such crimes and motivated to substantiate them. His near-total failure rate, from inside the prosecution apparatus, is the most probative datum against the reality of the panic.

## Steelman (Opposing View)
A defender could argue that ritual abuse is uniquely hard to prove and that even three or four substantiated cases mean some real abuse occurred. Fair — but a 99% non-substantiation rate from a purpose-built, well-funded unit is incompatible with the public belief (90% of Utahns) in widespread, organized satanic ritual abuse including infant sacrifice.
