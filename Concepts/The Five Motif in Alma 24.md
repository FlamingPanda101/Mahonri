# The Five Motif in Alma 24

## The Claim
[[Jonah (Plain and Precious Publishing)|Jonah]] observes a recurring **"five"** in King [[Anti-Nephi-Lehi]]'s speech and asks whether it is meaningful: the king opens with **five** thanksgivings ([[Alma 24.7|Alma 24:7-11]]); the [[Stain Chiasmus in Alma 24|stain chiasm]] is **five** points (ABCBA); the [[Brethren Chiasmus in Alma 24|brethren chiasm]] runs **five** in and **five** out; the [[Hiawatha Belt Parallel - Buried Weapons Under a Tree|Hiawatha belt]] joins **five** nations; and the Lamanites slay **1,005** ([[Alma 24.22|Alma 24:22]]) — "What a weird number." He offers "pure speculation" that five symbolizes an **empty hand** (five fingers, no weapon) — even noting that in Russian, *pyat* ("five") and *piad/palm* sound alike [^1][^2][^3].

## Supporting Evidence
1. **Pattern density.** Multiple independent "fives" in one chapter is the observation's whole force; if the chiasm counts are real, the convergence with the thanksgivings and the 1,005 figure is at least curious [^1]. *Rating: weak — the count of "fives" depends on the disputed chiasm structures, so it inherits their uncertainty.*
2. **"Empty hand" reading.** The five-fingers-open-hand-as-disarmament image is thematically apt for a pacifism chapter (laying down weapons) [^3].
3. **Steelman of the skeptic.** (a) **Cherry-picked numerology:** once you go looking for a number you will find it; the "1,005" is a casualty count, not a structural five, and reading it as part of a "five motif" is post-hoc. (b) The empty-hand etymology and the Russian *pyat/palm* link are admitted "pure speculation" with no evidentiary basis in the Book of Mormon's claimed languages. (c) Jonah explicitly says he states it "as absolute irrefutable fact just to make the academics angry" — i.e., it is a bit, not a thesis [^4]. (d) Numerological pattern-finding is the textbook case of [[Pareidolia Objection to Chiasmus Finding|apophenia]]; Jonah even mocks numerologists who "go nutso" over seven and gematria moments before doing the same with five [^2].
