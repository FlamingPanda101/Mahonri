# Convergence Not Proof Is the Standard for Book of Mormon Historicity

## The Claim

No single piece of evidence proves or disproves the Book of Mormon; the honest
standard is **convergence** — many independent data points trending the same direction
— plus spiritual conviction. There is no defeater that disproves it, and no proof that
compels belief; the right posture is to be *open* to evidence without *needing* it.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. The case is cumulative convergence, not a smoking gun

The Old World data (Valley of Lemuel river, Nahom, Bountiful, the Ishmael stela, the
Frankincense Trail directions) are persuasive not individually but as *converging*
fixed anchors that Rappleye says suggest an eyewitness account rather than a Joseph
Smith invention. "There's a lot of smoke" even if there is no compelling visible
flame. See [[Nahom and NHM Converge with Nephis Eastward Turn]].[^2]

> **Evidence rating:** Moderate-to-strong (cumulative-case reasoning is legitimate; weight is judgment-dependent).

### 2. Faith liberates rigorous evaluation, not bias

Because his faith does not hang on the evidence, Rappleye feels free to *reject* weak
evidence (Haplogroup X2A) and evaluate each claim on its merits. Spiritual conviction
makes him open to the book being true without being "so desperate to need the evidence"
that he clings to bad arguments. See [[Haplogroup X2A Is Not Valid Evidence for the
Book of Mormon]].[^3]

> **Evidence rating:** Moderate (a stance on the faith-evidence relationship; coherent and self-disciplining).

### 3. No historical document is free of open questions

There is no historical period or document about which all questions are settled;
unresolved questions are normal to historical study, not evidence of fabrication. The
remaining anachronisms and unanswered questions are puzzles to explore within the
ancient-text paradigm, not defeaters. See [[Read the Book of Mormon as a Translated
Ancient Text]].[^4]

> **Evidence rating:** Strong (an accurate generalization about historiography).

## Steelman of the Opposing View

A critic can argue "convergence" without a single decisive datum can be manufactured for
almost any text by selecting hits and discounting misses, and that pairing it with
"I don't need the evidence anyway" insulates the conclusion from disconfirmation.
Rappleye partly grants this: he concedes the evidence is "probably not enough to
persuade someone" without spiritual conviction, and that a skeptic can rationally read
the data as non-historical — so the claim is about reasonable openness, not coercive
proof.

## Best Quotes

> "There are no unassailable objections. There's no defeater... but there's also probably not enough to persuade someone who doesn't have a spiritual conviction. I think there's a lot of smoke."[^1]

> "The word that gets used sometimes is convergence... there are so many data points that I see converging... that suggest to me that it probably was an eyewitness account."[^2]

> "I feel liberated to not need the evidence either... I'm not so desperate to need the evidence that I'm not willing to be critical about what people claim is evidence."[^3]
