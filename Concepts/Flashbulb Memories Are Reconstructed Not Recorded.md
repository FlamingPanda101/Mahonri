# Flashbulb Memories Are Reconstructed Not Recorded

## The Claim
Even the most vivid "flashbulb" memories — the kind seared in by a shocking public event — are reconstructed, not recorded, and can be confidently *wrong*. If memory is this unreliable for shared, well-documented events, coached child testimony about secret rituals cannot be trusted.

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. The 9/11 longitudinal study (strong; cites research)
Duke psychologist [[David Rubin]] had subjects write down everything they remembered the day after 9/11, then again a year later. The later accounts "remembered things they had not witnessed" and contradicted the earlier ones: "the subjects had reconstructed their memories and contrived hallucinations... just like we see in artificial intelligence" [^1].

### 2. The "Bush saw the first plane" case (strong; striking example)
President George W. Bush told reporters he saw the **first** plane hit live on television — but no footage of the first plane aired until around midnight that day, so it was impossible. Many ordinary people share the same false memory. "They truly believe that they saw the first plane hit live... even though that's impossible" [^2][^3].

### 3. The Mandela effect (strong)
When vivid, detailed memories of a public event are shared by many people and are all wrong, researchers call it the **Mandela effect** — named for the false collective memory that Nelson Mandela died in prison in the 1980s. Other examples: Darth Vader never says "Luke, I am your father"; the Monopoly mascot has no monocle [^4][^5].

### 4. Stress amplifies susceptibility (moderate)
"Everyone is susceptible to them, especially when they're under stress or pressure" [^5] — precisely the condition of children in coercive abuse interviews. See [[Suggestive Interviewing Induces False Allegations]].

### Steelman
One might object that flashbulb errors are minor distortions, not the wholesale fabrication of complex abuse narratives. The rebuttal: the *mechanism* is the same — reconstruction from suggestion — and Loftus showed entire false episodes can be implanted (see [[False Memory Syndrome Explains the SRA Accusations]]).
