# The Divine Name Yahweh Was Given to Jesus

## The Claim
[[Blake Ostler]] argues that "Yahweh" functions in the New Testament as a **divine name that has been given** to Jesus by the Father, carrying divine power and prerogatives — not as a marker that Jesus is numerically the one God. The same pattern appears in [[John 17.11|John 17:11]] (the Father's name "given" to the Son), [[Philippians 2.9-11|Philippians 2:9-11]] (the "name above every name," where Isaiah's "every knee shall bow" originally read *Yahweh*), and [[1 Corinthians 8.4-6|1 Corinthians 8:6]] (Jesus as *kyrios* = Adonai/Yahweh). [^1][^2]

## The Rebuttal (Supporting Evidence)

1. **Phil 2:10-11 quotes Isaiah 45:23, where the bowing is to *Yahweh*.** "Where it has the name Jesus, it originally had Yahweh... At the name of Yahweh, every knee should bow." A second, related text has *Adonai*. So the conferred name is the divine Name itself. *Rating: strong on the OT source*; the allusion to Isaiah 45 is uncontested. [^2][^3]

2. **The name is *honorific and given*, not Jesus' birth name.** In John 17:11 "the name you gave me" has "power to make them one." *Rating: coherent* and ties the name-giving across John, Philippians, and Hebrews (enthronement). [^4]

3. **Bearing the Name confers divine power and appearance.** "By being given a divine name he has the divine power, can appear just as though he's God, executes all of the divine prerogatives." This is the same logic as the name-bearing Angel of Yahweh (see [[The Angel of Yahweh Bears the Divine Name]]). *Rating: a consistent internal model.* [^5]

4. **Steelman of the Trinitarian.** Phil 2 is one of the strongest NT texts *for* high Christology: the Father bestows on Jesus "the name above every name" — the Tetragrammaton — and demands universal worship that Isaiah 45 reserves for Yahweh alone ("I am God, and there is none else"). Giving Jesus *the* divine Name and divine *worship* arguably *includes* him in the unique divine identity rather than appointing him a delegate. *Rating: a powerful counter* — "given" need not mean "did not previously possess as God"; it can mark the *public vindication* of the incarnate Son. The data is genuinely double-edged. [^6]
