# Salvation Is Binary In Christ or Out of Christ Not a Spectrum

## The Point
The clearest structural clash in the debate is over the *shape* of the afterlife. [[Jacob Hansen]] describes salvation as a spectrum of light — three degrees of glory (sun/moon/stars) plus outer darkness reserved only for those who, with "a perfect knowledge of God," hate Him (a possible Judas). One "will get eternally what you really want"; rejecting Christ only "sells yourself short of your potential," it does not send a good person to torment.[^1] [[Allie Beth Stuckey]] rejects the spectrum outright: by Acts 4:12 ("no other name... by which one must be saved") and John 14:6, the only division is "those who are in Christ and those who are out of Christ" — "It's dead in sin or made alive in Christ," with "full relationship with God, and there is hell."[^2]

## Evidence
- Stuckey repeatedly probes the *qualifications* for each tier ("Are there any concrete qualifications?... what qualifies someone as bad enough to go to outer darkness?"), and treats the lack of crisp criteria as "arbitrary and slippery" — a weakness of the spectrum model.[^3]
- Hansen ties the spectrum to LDS theosis: the lowest kingdoms keep "whatever light and goodness" a person will accept, while the "fullness" requires a covenant relationship through the priesthood (see [[Human Deification in Mormonism]], [[Acorn and Oak Analogy for Theosis]]).
- Stuckey's binary is the engine of her "are Mormons Christians" verdict: if salvation is in-or-out of Christ, then a system that places faithful Protestants in a lesser glory has denied them salvation in the only sense that counts.

## Best Quotes
> "It's in Christ or out of Christ. It's dead in sin or made alive in Christ... There aren't three levels of heaven. There is full relationship with God, and there is hell." [^2]

> "You will get eternally what you really want, and if you don't want Jesus Christ, then you'll sell yourself short of your potential." [^1]

## LDS Connection
The spectrum is the soteriological face of LDS theology: a generous, graded afterlife flowing from divine kinship and progression. Stuckey's binary reframes the same generosity as a denial of the gospel, because in her frame anything short of "in Christ" is "out of Christ" (cf. [[Sola Fide vs Faith Plus Ordinances]]).
