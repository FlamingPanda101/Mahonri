# Faith and Works — Two Blades of the Scissors

## The Claim
Faith and works are not competitors to be ranked; they operate together, and grace is what actually saves. The debate over "which matters more" is a category error.

## Supporting Evidence
- [[C.S. Lewis]]: arguing whether faith or works is more important is "like saying which one of the blades on a pair of scissors is more important" *(cited expert analogy)* [^1].
- **The Peter-on-the-water test** ([[Matthew 14.28-31|Matthew 14:28-31]]): faith alone = stay in the boat; works alone = step out and sink. Both together put Peter in position — but grace is what lifts him out *(scriptural analogy)* [^2].
- Living faith demonstrates itself — "demonstrations of faith are what trigger grace"; cf. [[James 2.14-26|James 2]] [^3].

## Best Quote
> "If I have faith but no works... I believe it, but I'm staying in the boat. If I just have works, I'm going to step off the boat and immediately sink... So which one of those two saved me? Neither. Because at the end of the day, what actually saved him was the grace of the Savior picking him up out of the water." [^2]
