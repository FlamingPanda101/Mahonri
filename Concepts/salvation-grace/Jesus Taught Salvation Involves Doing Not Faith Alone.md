# Jesus Taught Salvation Involves Doing, Not Faith Alone

## The Claim
[[Alex Arnold]] argues that "Jesus didn't teach faith alone. Not once. Not in a single passage. What he did teach over and over again is that salvation involves *doing*."[^1] His case is a cluster of dominical sayings:
- **[[Matthew 7.21|Matthew 7:21]]** — "Not everyone who says to me, 'Lord, Lord,' will enter the kingdom of heaven, but he who **does** the will of my Father." Arnold notes the Greek is present active — "ongoing continuous action... a lifestyle," not a single moment. [^1]
- **[[Matthew 25.31-46|Matthew 25:31-46]]** (the sheep and the goats) — "the whole judgment" turns on what people *did* (fed the hungry, clothed the naked, visited the prisoner); "no one asks the goats and the sheep, 'But did you have a genuine internal faith state?'" [^2]
- **Matthew 24:13** — "He who endures to the end shall be saved" — endurance "implies the possibility of not enduring," which is "noise" in an once-saved-always-saved framework. [^3]
- **John 14:15** — "If you love me, keep my commandments" — "not... believe correctly about me." [^3]

"At some point you have to ask, who is actually departing from Jesus here?"[^3] Arnold's target is specifically the *sola fide* + "once saved, always saved" package, which he says makes Matthew 25 "a serious problem... because that's Jesus talking."[^2]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **The texts say what is claimed.** [[Matthew 7.21|Matt 7:21]] conditions entry on *doing* the Father's will; [[Matthew 25.31-46|Matt 25]] grounds the separation entirely on deeds of mercy. These are red-letter sayings in Gospels "that everyone accepts." *Rating: the prima facie reading is strong and widely conceded even by Protestants (who then harmonize it).* [^1][^2]
2. **The grammatical note on Matthew 7:21 is fair.** The present-tense participle (*ho poiōn*, "the one doing") does connote ongoing action, supporting a lifestyle-not-moment reading. *Rating: a legitimate, modest grammatical point.* [^1]
3. **Endurance language presupposes contingency.** "Endures to the end" (Matt 24:13; cf. 10:22) and the many NT warnings against falling away fit awkwardly with unconditional eternal security. *Rating: a genuine tension for the OSAS view, long debated within Protestantism itself (Calvinist perseverance vs. Arminian conditional security).* [^3]
4. **It dovetails with the LDS orthopraxy emphasis.** The vault develops the "doing over assent" theme in [[Orthopraxy over Orthodoxy in LDS Salvation]] and [[Matthew 7.21]], and the grace-frame in [[Works Are the Fruit of Faith Not the Price of Salvation]]. *Rating: well integrated with the corpus.*

**Steelman of the opposing view.** The Protestant reply is not that Jesus *denies* works but that these texts describe the *evidence and fruit* of saving faith, not its *ground*: (a) **the sheep are surprised** ("when saw we thee an hungred?", Matt 25:37) — their works were *unselfconscious fruit* of a regenerate heart, exactly the Reformed picture of works flowing from faith, not a meritorious checklist; (b) **"doing the Father's will" (John 6:29) is itself defined by Jesus as "believe on him whom he hath sent"** — so Matthew 7:21's "doer" is the believer, and the contrast is with *false professors* ("I never knew you"), not with faith; (c) **endurance is the *means* God preserves the elect** (Phil 1:6; "he that endureth" is *kept* by grace), so warning passages function pastorally without making salvation works-earned; (d) Jesus pre-cross often addressed people *under the law* and pressed the law's full demand to drive them to grace (the rich young ruler). A Latter-day Saint reply (and Arnold's): even granting works-as-fruit, the judgment scenes still make *deeds* the operative criterion at the bar, and an "once saved, always saved" system that lets a baptized person "spend the next 80 years doing whatever they want and still receive the same eternal reward" is "not justice... not even coherent"; the LDS frame keeps grace as the *cause* while insisting, with Jesus, that what a saved person *does* is not optional — see [[Works Are the Fruit of Faith Not the Price of Salvation]] and [[LDS Salvation Is by Grace Not Earned by Works]].

## Best Quotes
> "Jesus didn't teach faith alone. Not once. Not in a single passage. What he did teach over and over again is that salvation involves doing." [^1]

> "No one asks the goats and the sheep, 'But did you have a genuine internal faith state?' ... The judgment is entirely based on what you did. If you hold a theology where works don't contribute to salvation, then you have a Matthew chapter 25 problem, a serious one, because that's Jesus talking." [^2]
