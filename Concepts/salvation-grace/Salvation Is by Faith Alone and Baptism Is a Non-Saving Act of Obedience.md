# Salvation Is by Faith Alone and Baptism Is a Non-Saving Act of Obedience

## The Claim
[[Ryan (NeedGod)|Ryan]] (Reformed Baptist) argues the negative: water baptism does *not* save. Salvation is by faith alone in Christ's finished atonement; baptism is a commanded, important act of obedience — like caring for the poor or making disciples — "just not as the way to be saved." His model is Abraham ([[Romans 4]]), justified by being "fully convinced that God was able to do what He had promised" (Rom 4:21), *before* and apart from any work (including circumcision) that could give ground for boasting. To make baptism *required* for salvation, Ryan says, is to split one's trust — "one foot on the bus and one foot on the ground" — so that "Christ will be of no advantage to you" (Galatians 5).[^1]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **Abraham is the model and was justified by faith apart from works (rating: strong; Pauline).** Romans 4:2-5: "If Abraham was justified by works, he has something to boast about... to the one who does not work but believes... his faith is counted as righteousness." Abraham was counted righteous in Genesis 15:6, before circumcision (Genesis 17) — so the sign did not justify him, and "anyone else who just believes like Abraham did, they get righteousness counted to them too."[^1]
2. **Requiring a work splits saving trust (rating: moderate; theological).** "We need to depend 100% on what Jesus did to save us. Not 50% Jesus and 50% the fact I got baptized." The Galatians who added circumcision as a requirement were told "Christ will be of no advantage to you" — and "the same principle can be applied to any other New Testament law," including baptism.[^2]
3. **Faith is a conviction, not a work (rating: moderate; definitional).** "Faith is not an action I do. It's a conviction I have. It's trust in someone else's actions, Jesus Christ" — and Paul "explicitly says that faith is not a work in Romans 4:5."[^1]
4. **Believers are saved before/without baptism (rating: moderate; narrative).** Cornelius (Acts 10) and the Philippian jailer (Acts 16, "Believe in the Lord Jesus Christ and you'll be saved") believe and are saved *before* baptism; the thief on the cross had no baptism at all.[^3]

## Apologetic Engagement
Ryan's case is the classic Reformed *sola fide* position, and its strongest element is the Abraham/Romans 4 structure: Paul does ground justification in faith over against works that occasion boasting. Its principal weakness, pressed hard by [[Joe Heschmeyer]], is **methodological** — Ryan argues the baptism question almost entirely from passages that never mention baptism, deducing his answer from a general principle rather than from the topical texts (see [[The New Testament Repeatedly Says Baptism Saves]]). His "splitting trust" argument also proves too much by Heschmeyer's lights: obeying Christ's *command* to be baptized is trust *in* Christ, not distrust, since "I'm literally only getting baptized because Jesus told me to." And the meaning of "works" in Romans 4 is contested — Heschmeyer (via [[James Dunn]]) restricts it to Mosaic boundary markers, not obedience as such (see [[Works of the Law Means Mosaic Boundary Markers Not Obedience as Such]]). The vault's [[Baptism Saves but Is Not the Cause of Salvation]] offers a more exegetically careful version of Ryan's conclusion (metonymy) that engages the baptism texts directly rather than bypassing them.

## LDS Relevance
The Latter-day Saint tradition rejects Ryan's faith-alone conclusion: faith, repentance, *baptism*, and the gift of the Holy Ghost are the gospel's foundational ordinances (Articles of Faith 1:4; 3 Nephi 11), and baptism by proper authority is necessary for the remission of sins. Where Ryan treats baptism as a non-saving symbol that would otherwise compromise grace, the Restoration treats it as a grace-conveying ordinance commanded by Christ — and extends it to the dead by proxy ([[1 Corinthians 15.29 Points to an Actual Rite of Baptism for the Dead]]), which is unintelligible on a faith-only model. The LDS reply to the "splitting trust / boasting" worry parallels Heschmeyer's: covenant obedience is the *form* faith takes, not a rival to grace (cf. James 2:24, faith shown in works). See [[Baptism Is Gods Work Not a Human Work That Earns Salvation]].

## Best Quotes
> "Christ saved me on the cross, and I receive that by faith... baptism is important because it's an act of obedience to God. And so we should get baptized but just not as the way to be saved."[^1]

> "It's like catching a bus somewhere and you decide to put one foot on the bus and one foot on the ground. What's going to happen? You'll fall out. You must be depending on the bus entirely."[^2]
