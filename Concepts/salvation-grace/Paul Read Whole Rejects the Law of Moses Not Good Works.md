# Paul Read Whole Rejects the Law of Moses, Not Good Works

## The Claim
[[Alex Arnold]] argues that the Pauline case for *sola fide* depends on reading half a sentence. [[Ephesians 2.8-9|Ephesians 2:8-9]] ("by grace... through faith... not of works, lest any man should boast") is real and Latter-day Saints "totally agree with it" — but verse 10 immediately adds: "For we are his workmanship, created in Christ Jesus **for good works**, which God prepared beforehand that we should walk in them."[^1] "Both sentences are in the same paragraph... You can't use verse 9 to build a theology and pretend that verse 10 doesn't exist. That's not Bible study, that's selective reading."[^1] What Paul rejects in vv.8–9 is "a specific thing... that keeping the **law of Moses** earns righteousness before God" — the live controversy of circumcision and Torah-observance for Gentile converts — "not... good works" as such.[^2] [[Romans 2.6-7|Romans 2:6-7]] makes the point in the same letter cited for faith alone: God "will render to each one according to his works... to those who by patience in well-doing... he will give eternal life" — "a chapter before the verse that they always love to quote."[^3] "You're not saved by performing the Mosaic law... but a saved person lives a life of active faithfulness. Those two statements are not in conflict."[^3]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **Ephesians 2:10 is right there.** The "unto good works" clause immediately follows the prooftext and is part of the same sentence-unit; quoting 8–9 without 10 is a real omission. *Rating: textually decisive against the *bare* faith-alone use of the passage; see [[Ephesians 2-8-9 - Saved by Grace Not Works Rebutted]].* [^1]
2. **"Works of the law" is a recognized Pauline term of art.** Mainstream scholarship (including the New Perspective on Paul) reads *erga nomou* in Romans/Galatians as the *Mosaic-covenant* works (esp. circumcision, food laws, calendar) that marked Jewish identity — exactly Arnold's point that Paul targets law-righteousness, not good deeds. *Rating: strong; this is not an idiosyncratic LDS reading.* [^2]
3. **Romans 2:6-7 precedes Romans 3.** Paul affirms judgment "according to works" and links well-doing to eternal life before the [[Romans 3.28|3:28]] prooftext, so the letter's own flow resists a works-excluding reading. *Rating: a fair contextual observation, long noted by interpreters of all stripes.* [^3]
4. **It harmonizes the grace and obedience texts.** This is the same resolution the vault reaches in [[Works Are the Fruit of Faith Not the Price of Salvation]]: grace is the sole *cause*, works the *consequence and purpose*. *Rating: coherent and corpus-consistent.*

**Steelman of the opposing view.** A careful Protestant agrees with much of this and still keeps *sola fide*: (a) **everyone affirms Ephesians 2:10** — Reformed theology *insists* the saved are "created for good works"; the dispute is whether those works are *the ground* of justification (no) or *its necessary fruit and goal* (yes), so quoting v.10 does not by itself overturn faith-alone; (b) **"works of the law" debate is unsettled** — many exegetes (the "Old Perspective," e.g. Schreiner) argue Paul means *all* works, not merely boundary-markers, since Romans 4 grounds the point in Abraham *before* the law and Romans 3:20 says "by works of the law shall no flesh be justified" universally; (c) **Romans 2:6-7 describes the law's standard / the verdict that confirms a faith-wrought life**, not a second, works-based road to heaven — Paul's conclusion in 3:20 is that *no one* meets the 2:6-7 standard, driving all to grace. A Latter-day Saint reply: even on the "fruit" reading, the practical LDS position is identical in substance to Ephesians 2:8-10 — saved by grace through faith, *created unto* the works one then walks in — so the remaining quarrel is over *the role of covenant ordinances*, not over grace as the source; and Romans 2:6-7's plain coupling of "well-doing" with "eternal life" is at least a caution against treating works as soteriologically inert. The narrow claim holds: Paul read whole excludes *law-righteousness*, not the obedient life.

## Best Quotes
> "Paul says in verse 9 that you are not saved by your own works, and then immediately in verse 10 says that you were created unto good works that you're supposed to walk in. ... You can't use verse 9 to build a theology and pretend that verse 10 doesn't exist." [^1]

> "He will render to each one according to his works. To those who by patience in well-doing seek for glory and honor and immortality, he will give eternal life. Paul connects patient well-doing with eternal life, a chapter before the verse that they always love to quote." [^3]
