# Worship and Praise Initiates Gods Power Before Feeling Follows

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Todd McLauchlin]] argues that the single most practical lever for drawing God's power into daily life is to **worship and praise God by an act of will, before and regardless of feeling anything** — what the book of Hebrews calls "the sacrifice of praise" (Hebrews 13:15). On waking with no spiritual desire ("give me my phone... I gotta go to work"), one can still *choose* to say "I worship Christ; he is the Redeemer," and that deliberate, unfelt act is like "hitting the generator button on a jumbo jet": it orients the mind wholly to God so that grace and the Spirit then flow in. The principle: **motivation is the adversary's tool; acting against motivation is the power of faith and agency** [^1][^2].

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. Grace flows from *initiating* by free will — rate: moderate (scriptural + experiential)
McLauchlin's missionary illustration: no one *feels* like saying "excuse me" to a stranger in a foreign language, but doing it anyway makes "grace flow into you" — "when you initiate the Lord by your own free will and choice, grace flows into you. This is where you get your D&C 121:45-46." The act of worship is *receptivity*, not payment: it does not earn grace but orients the person so Christ's power "flows to you without compulsory means." Pairs with [[The Initiatory Is a Daily Order of Operations for Drawing Down Grace]] [^2].

### 2. Praise that precedes (or contradicts) circumstance is the "sacrifice" — rate: moderate
McLauchlin glosses the "sacrifice of praise" as praising God *when your life teaches you he is not doing you any favors* — "you can still give a sacrifice of that perception and praise him nonetheless." Nephi models it: after "O wretched man that I am" he chooses, "Nevertheless I know in whom I have trusted... I will lift up my voice unto thee" (2 Nephi 4); and bound on the ship he "did look unto my God, and I did praise him all the day long" (1 Nephi 18:16). The praise comes *first*, the deliverance and the feeling *after* [^3][^4].

### 3. Alma 32 ranks the self-initiated above the compelled — rate: moderate
The principle is keyed to [[Alma 32.27-43|Alma 32:16]]: "blessed are they who humble themselves *without being compelled to be humble*... blessed is he that believeth in the word of God and is baptized without stubbornness of heart." More grace comes to the one who initiates worship by choice than to the one later *compelled* to be humble by hardship — though the compelled is still blessed [^5].

### 4. You worship whatever you attend to — rate: moderate
The converse: "whatever you put your attention on is what you worship and what you'll submit to." Manifestation boards and status-seeking are inadvertent worship of a thing other than Christ; deliberate praise re-aims attention at the only source of saving power, because "you drift toward whatever you put your attention on." See [[Worship Is Devotion to What Can Save Not Ritual Appeasement]] and [[Faith Is Trust in a True Future and Fear Is Faith in a False One]] [^6].

## Steelman of the Opposing View
An evangelical critic will hear "initiate the Lord by your free will and grace flows" as **synergism** — the worry that a human act *triggers* or *merits* grace, against sola gratia. (McLauchlin partly pre-empts this: he insists one has "no power to subdue darkness as an individual"; the worship merely orients the person so Christ's power, the only real power, can flow.) A second objection is that "praise regardless of feeling" can shade into emotional suppression or a works-treadmill if untethered from genuine repentance and relationship — praise as technique rather than devotion. And the "generator button" claim is experiential testimony, not a generalizable mechanism; some who praise faithfully still wait long in spiritual dryness (which the Nephi examples actually concede — deliverance came *after* prolonged affliction). A measured Latter-day Saint reply: the act is *receptive*, not transactional; its scriptural anchors (the sacrifice of praise; Nephi's chosen trust; Alma 32's blessing on the self-humbled) are independently grounded; and the point is precisely that grace is unconditional ([[Grace Is Unconditional Love Not an Earned Wage]]) but *received* through an oriented, willing heart rather than a feeling one happens to have.

## Best Quotes
> "No matter how I feel I can make a conscientious decision to worship God... this is hitting the generator button on a jumbo jet." [^1]

> "Motivation is the adversary's tool... acting against motivation is the power of faith and agency. It does not matter how I feel — I can choose to worship God." [^2]

> "The sacrifice of praise in one dimension means that when your perception and your life teaches you that God is not necessarily your friend... you can still give a sacrifice of that perception and praise him nonetheless." [^3]
