# James 2:24 — Justified by Works, Not by Faith Alone

## The Claim
[[James 2.24|James 2:24]] — "a man is justified by works and not by faith alone" — is the only place the phrase "faith alone" appears in scripture, and it *negates* it. The verse refutes *sola fide* as usually stated — though it must be read with the Catholic distinction between initial and ongoing justification.

## Supporting Evidence
- The verse explicitly couples "by works" with "**not** by faith alone" *(primary text)* [^1].
- Two Protestant escape routes exist, and both fail: the "different kind of faith" reply ([[James - Different Kind of Faith Reply Rebutted]]) and the "different kind of justification / before men" reply ([[James - Justification Before Men Reply Rebutted]]) [^2].
- The correct frame is [[Initial vs Ongoing Justification]]: works increase a righteousness first given by grace alone [^3].

## Best Quote
> "James does not say 'he who is justified does good works.' Instead, he says 'a man is justified by works and not by faith alone.'" — [[Trent Horn]] [^2]

## Counter-arguments / related
- The Protestant formula "justified by faith alone, but faith is never alone": [[Faith Alone but Never Alone]].
