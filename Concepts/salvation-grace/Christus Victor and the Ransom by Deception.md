# Christus Victor and the Ransom by Deception

## The Point
The "classic" atonement model — termed *Christus Victor* by Gustaf Aulén — casts Christ's life, death, and Resurrection as the decisive victory in a cosmic war against Satan, sin, and death. [^1] A striking sub-tradition holds that the victory was won by **deception**: Satan brought about Jesus's death not realizing it would be his own ruin. Gregory of Nyssa compared it to a fish swallowing a baited hook ("the hook of the deity might be gulped down along with the bait of the flesh"); Augustine called the cross "a trap for the devil" and Christ's death "the food by which he was ensnared." [^2]

## Evidence
1. **Warfare imagery is pervasive.** Christ is "the conquering hero" while sin, death, demons, and the devil are "the defeated foes." Justin reads the ascension as victory; Origen says Christ's very conception in Mary "constituted a victory over demons." [^3]
2. **Athanasius dwells on victory over death.** He calls the cross "the mark of victory and the triumph over death" and says death is "done away by the power of the Savior," echoing 1 Corinthians 15:55: "O death, where is thy victory?" Christian martyrdom is itself evidence of the conquest. [^4]
3. **Ransom-by-deception (the fishhook / mousetrap).** Gregory of Nyssa: "deity was hidden under the veil of our [human] nature that, as is done by greedy fish, the hook of the deity might be gulped down along with the bait of the flesh." Augustine: "The cross of the Lord became a trap for the devil; the death of the Lord was the food by which he was ensnared." [^2]
4. **Two schools on Satan's claim.** Irenaeus treats Satan as a **usurper** with no rights — God reclaims what is rightfully his; Tertullian suggests the devil has genuine rights. [^5] The later apocryphal *Gospel of Nicodemus* dramatizes the descent into hell: "You wished to kill the King of glory, but have killed yourself." [^6]

## Best Quotes
> "Deity was hidden under the veil of our [human] nature that, as is done by greedy fish, the hook of the deity might be gulped down along with the bait of the flesh and thus life be introduced into the house of death." — Gregory of Nyssa [^2]

> "The devil exulted when Christ died, and by that very death of Christ the devil was overcome: he took food as it were from a trap." — Augustine [^2]

## LDS Connection
*Christus Victor* — triumph over sin, death, and the devil — maps onto the LDS emphasis on the Resurrection and Christ's conquest of "the bands of death." But the **ransom-by-deception** motif is exactly the kind of "victory" framing that can become an idol if pressed as a debating trophy; see [[Victory as an Idol in Apologetics]]. The "two schools" question of whether Satan held genuine rights also touches the LDS view that an unconstrained, ex nihilo God would make the ransom logic incoherent — see [[An Unconstrained God Makes the Atonement Pointless]].
