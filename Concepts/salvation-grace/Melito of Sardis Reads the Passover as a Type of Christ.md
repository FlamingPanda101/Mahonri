# Melito of Sardis Reads the Passover as a Type of Christ

## The Point
[[Melito of Sardis]], a leading second-century Christian voice, preached the Holy Week homily *On the Pasch* ("On the Passover"), a rhetorically and typologically rich reading of Christ's death through the lens of the Exodus Passover. For Melito the Passover lamb was only "a type, an impression, a sketch of the true story" — the redemption accomplished by Christ at Easter.

## Evidence
1. **Melito's stature and the text's survival.** "One of the most important Christian voices of the second century"; ancient sources say "he always lived his life in the Holy Spirit." Apart from a few fragments, *On the Pasch* is his only surviving work, "only discovered and published in the last century." [^1]
2. **Passover as the lens for Christ's death.** "From the earliest period of Christian history, the death of Christ was understood in terms of the Passover lamb and the Exodus story." The sermon follows the Emmaus pattern (Luke 24:27) of finding Christ throughout the scriptures. [^1]
3. **The vivid death of the firstborn.** Melito dramatizes the night of the tenth plague: a firstborn, clasping his dead father/brother in the graspable darkness, cries out, "Whom does my hand hold? … If it is father, help; if mother, comfort." Death answers: "You are my first-born; I am your destiny, the silence of death." [^2]
4. **The mystery of the blood.** Israel is saved by the blood on the doorposts (Exodus 12): "The death of the sheep became a wall for the people." Melito asks the angel what he truly respected — "the slaughter of the sheep or the life of the Lord? … It is clear that your respect was won when you saw the mystery of the Lord occurring in the sheep." [^3]
5. **Christ speaks the invitation.** Melito ends by giving voice to Jesus: "I am your forgiveness, I am the Passover of salvation, I am the lamb slain for you; I am your ransom, I am your life, I am your light, I am your salvation, I am your resurrection, I am your king." [^4]

## Best Quotes
> "The death of the sheep became a wall for the people." (Melito, *On Pascha*) [^3]

> "I am the Passover of salvation, I am the lamb slain for you; I am your ransom, I am your life, I am your light, I am your salvation, I am your resurrection, I am your king." (Melito, *On Pascha*) [^4]

## LDS Connection
Melito's reading recasts "the power of these great moments of Israel's past … all actually found in Christ" — the same typological conviction expressed in the Book of Mormon, that the law of Moses pointed to Christ (2 Nephi 11:4; Mosiah 13:31, "all these things were types of things to come"). Melito's Christ-voiced "I am" sequence parallels the Savior's self-declarations in 3 Nephi.
