# Coherentism Replaces the Chain with a Web of Beliefs

## The Point

**Coherentism** is the response to the [[The Epistemic Regress Problem|regress problem]] corresponding to the trilemma's third horn — the chain of reasons "circles back on itself." But [[Kevin McCain]] stresses that describing coherentism this way is "somewhat misleading," because coherentists actually **reject the entire linear setup** of the regress. Instead of a chain that stops, loops, or runs forever, they picture justification as a **web (or system) of beliefs**: justification arises when that collection of beliefs is related to itself in the appropriate ways. McCain reports it as the second-most-popular view, behind foundationalism.[^1][^2]

## Argument

1. **Coherentism denies the linear assumption behind the regress.** The regress problem assumes justification "proceeds in a linear fashion" — P because Q, Q because R, R because S — a chain in which one reason or set of reasons supports the next. Coherentists reject this picture at the root.[^1]

2. **Justification is holistic — a web, not a chain.** Coherentists "think of justification more along the lines of a web of beliefs where you have various beliefs that are connected to one another in different ways." There is no regress of *linear* reasons because the reasons form an interconnected system.[^2]

3. **Coherence among the parts is what justifies.** "Justification arises when this collection of beliefs is related to one another in the appropriate ways" — when the components of the system or web "are connected in the right ways."[^2][^3] *Steelman of the circularity worry:* the natural objection is that this is just disguised circular reasoning. The coherentist replies that the objection presupposes the very linear model they deny: in a web, a belief is supported by its mutual fit with the *whole* system, not by a single loop of premises feeding back into themselves, so the "vicious circle" charge misfires.

## Best Quotes

> "It's somewhat misleading to describe coherentism in this way because coherentists in fact deny the entire setup of the regress of reasons."[^1]

> "Coherentists think of justification more along the lines of a web of beliefs where you have various beliefs that are connected to one another in different ways."[^2]

> "When this system of beliefs bears the right sort of relationships within itself, then you get justification."[^3]
