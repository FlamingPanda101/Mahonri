# Fruit Versus Theology — Are They Separable (Objection Rebutted)

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
[[Jacob Hansen]] argues Jesus prioritized **fruit** (a lived walk with God) over **theology** (correct doctrinal formulation). He cites John the Baptist's rebuke of the religious experts — "do not presume to say... we have Abraham as our father, for... God can raise up children for Abraham... every tree that does not bear good fruit will be cut down" ([[Matthew 3.9|Matthew 3:9]] / [[Luke 3.8|Luke 3:8]]). [^1] The scribes and Pharisees "probably had a pretty good understanding of who God was, but they lacked the fruits." [^2] He also cites the unknown exorcist ([[Mark 9.38-40|Mark 9:38-40]]) casting out demons in Jesus' name to argue Jesus blesses faithful action despite imperfect theology. [^3] Conclusion: a sincere, Christ-following modalist is not thereby damned. [^4]

## The Rebuttal
[[Gavin Ortlund]] argues fruit and theology are **organically connected**, not separable.

1. **Confession is *part of* the fruit.** Ortlund: "the confession and apprehension of the identity of Jesus is... baked right into the fruit and our relationship," citing [[1 Corinthians 12.3|1 Corinthians 12:3]] ("no one can say 'Jesus is Lord' except by the Holy Spirit") and Philippians 2's confession. [^5] He notes the Pharisees "sinned... because they did not recognize the Lord of glory." [^6] *Rating: strong on the texts that fuse confession and faithfulness;* weaker against the Baptist passage, where the rebuke is explicitly about *fruit*, not Christology.

2. **The Mark 9 exorcist counter.** Ortlund replies the text says nothing about the exorcist's *theology* — only that the disciples didn't know him ("mere relational aloofness"); "there's nothing said that he has bad theology." [^7] *Rating: a precise exegetical correction* — the passage cannot bear the weight of "right action despite wrong doctrine."

3. **"Following Jesus" is itself a theological term.** Ortlund: "as soon as we start to define the phrase 'following Jesus' and even just the word 'Jesus,' we're doing theology." Someone could claim to follow a "Jesus" so unlike the real one that "you would say, well, that's not following Jesus." [^8] *Rating: a strong conceptual point* — it collapses the clean fruit/theology divide Hansen needs.

4. **The modalism concession (where they converge).** Ortlund grants a sincere modalist is "of course not" thereby unable to be saved, invoking imperfect understanding vs. knowing rejection (see [[Distinction Between Knowing Rejection and Imperfect Understanding]]). [^9] *Rating: this narrows the gap* — both agree sincere misunderstanding is not damning; the live dispute is whether the *LDS* view is mere misunderstanding or a different God altogether.

## Best Quotes
> "His concern wasn't with the theology... the scribes and Pharisees... probably had a pretty good understanding of who God was, but they lacked the fruits." [^2]

> "The confession and apprehension of the identity of Jesus is... baked right into the fruit and our relationship." [^5]

> "As soon as we start to define the phrase 'following Jesus' and even just the word 'Jesus,' we're doing theology and it really matters what we mean by that." [^8]

> "There's nothing in that text that says there's bad theology in that exorcist. All it tells us is he was casting out demons in Jesus' name." [^7]
