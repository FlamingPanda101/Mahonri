# Orthopraxy over Orthodoxy in LDS Salvation

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
[[Joe Heschmeyer]] argues that if Adam-God is a damnable error, the LDS should treat orthodoxy as salvation-defining. [[Luke Hansen]] rebuts that LDS salvation turns on the **testimony of Jesus Christ**, not on intellectually affirming correct propositions about God — which is exactly what the LDS find "abominable" about the creeds.

## The Rebuttal

### 1. Joseph Smith: everything else is "appendages" (strong — internal primary)
Hansen quotes Joseph Smith: "the fundamental principles of our religion are the testimony of the apostles and prophets concerning Jesus Christ, that he died, was buried, and rose again the third day... and all other things which pertain to our religion are only appendages to it" [^1]. Heschmeyer notes this "almost quotes verbatim" Paul's creed in **[[1 Corinthians 15.1-4|1 Corinthians 15]]**.

### 2. D&C 76 makes the testimony of Jesus the dividing line (strong — internal primary)
**[[D&C 76.md|D&C 76]]** sorts humanity into three kingdoms of glory, and "in every instance, the dividing line between who's in this higher versus this lower kingdom is the testimony of Jesus" [^2]. So orthodoxy is "really important... but not damnable."

### 3. What the LDS find "abominable" in the creeds (moderate — reframing)
Hansen turns Heschmeyer's point around: what is abominable is not the *content* of the creeds but their making a metaphysical distinction (three persons one being vs. three persons one purpose) "the dividing line between heaven and hell." Heschmeyer "rightly pointed out that we're not doing that here" — and is "kind of arguing that we should" [^3].

### Steelman (Heschmeyer's side)
Heschmeyer's counter: "the Christians whose creeds were regarded as an abomination all affirm that Pauline creed." If everything but 1 Cor 15 is an appendix, "it doesn't seem like you need a church that's a restoration... nothing has gone seriously astray." Worse, "your view of social Trinity compared to Trinitarianism is arguably *smaller* than your view of the Godhead and Brigham Young's view of the Godhead" — so the LDS already tolerate, internally, a gap bigger than the one that supposedly required the Restoration [^4]. He also cites McConkie that teaching Young's false doctrine "will damn us," showing orthodoxy is not really optional [^5].

## Best Quotes
> "All other things which pertain to our religion are only appendages to it." — Joseph Smith, quoted by Hansen [^1]

> "Your view of social Trinity compared to Trinitarianism is arguably smaller than your view of the Godhead and Brigham Young's view of the Godhead." — Joe Heschmeyer [^4]

> "What is abominable about the creeds is that they said, 'This is the dividing line between heaven and hell.'" — Luke Hansen [^3]
