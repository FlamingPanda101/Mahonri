# Scripture Explicitly Affirms Universal Salvific Will

## The Claim
[[Gavin (Catholic Debater)]]'s second affirmative pillar: scripture "explicitly and not ambiguously" teaches God wills the good of all without exception. He stacks four texts: [[1 Timothy 2.4|1 Tim 2:4]] ("God desires all people to be saved"), [[2 Peter 3.9|2 Pet 3:9]] ("not wishing that any should perish"), [[Matthew 5.45|Matt 5:45]] ("makes his sun rise on the evil and on the good"), and [[John 12.32|John 12:32]] ("when I am lifted up I will draw all people to myself").[^1]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **[[1 Timothy 2.4|1 Tim 2:4]] and [[2 Peter 3.9|2 Pet 3:9]]** — rate: strong prima facie for a *desire*-claim. Both verbs (*thelei*, *boulomenos*) assert a divine wish that all be saved. *(scriptural)* [^1] **Steelman of the Reformed reply:** classical theology distinguishes God's *antecedent* will (he genuinely desires all be saved) from his *consequent* will (what he ordains given foreseen rejection); the verses establish the former, which does not by itself prove omnibenevolence toward each individual.
2. **[[Matthew 5.45|Matt 5:45]] common grace** — God's temporal goods fall on the just and unjust alike. *(scriptural)* [^1] Evidence value: solid for *temporal* benevolence; the dispute is whether it extends to the eternal good of the eventually-damned.
3. **The key distinction** — Gavin pre-empts a misread: "omnibenevolence does not equal universalism... does not equal coercion." Universal *will* of good is compatible with non-universal *acceptance* because "forced love is not love." *(definitional)* [^2] See [[Omnibenevolence Does Not Equal Universalism or Coercion]].

**Hayden's rebuttal (steelman):** these texts describe God's *desire* once persons exist. They are silent on the prior, decisive act — God's free decision to *create* those he foreknows will reject him. A desire that none perish, held by a being who freely created those he knew would perish and could have refrained, is exactly the incoherence the resolution must answer. See [[The Creation Dilemma]] and [[The First Morally Decisive Moment Is Creation Not Rebellion]].

## Best Quotes
> "God in 1 Timothy chapter 2:4 — God desires all people to be saved and come to the knowledge of the truth."[^1]

> "Notice God's benevolence is universal even when salvation is not universally accepted. This is the crucial distinction: omnibenevolence does not equal universalism. Omnibenevolence does not equal coercion... because forced love is not love."[^2]
