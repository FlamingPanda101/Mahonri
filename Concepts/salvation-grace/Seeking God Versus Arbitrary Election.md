# Seeking God Versus Arbitrary Election (Objection Rebutted)

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
The objection (which [[Don Bradley]] attributes to Calvinist readings of Genesis): God chose Abraham *arbitrarily* — for "no special reason" — to save, while leaving "all these other people just go to hell." On this reading election is unconditional and divine choice is sovereign and unmerited.[^1]

## The Rebuttal

1. **The Book of Abraham reverses the order (rated: strong within LDS framework).** In [[Abraham 1.1-4|Abraham 1]], Abraham "already is refusing to worship idols. He's not an idolater. He's seeking God. And so God comes to Abraham because Abraham has been trying to come to God."[^1] Election is *responsive*, not arbitrary — it answers human initiative. *Steelman:* a Reformed theologian ([[John Calvin]], [[Thomas Schreiner]]) would reply that the very seeking is itself a gift of prevenient grace, so monergism is preserved — Bradley's move only pushes the question back a step. Bradley partly concedes this ("there was initiative from God's side all the time... I think there always is"), making the position closer to a synergism than a flat denial of grace.[^2]

2. **The pattern has exceptions, which proves it is a rule (rated: moderate).** Bradley grants that some are called without seeking — [[Alma the Younger]], [[Paul the Apostle]] — "an angel smacks them upside the head." But "that is actually the exception, not the rule. We usually find God because we're seeking God."[^3] He cites [[Lehi]], [[Nephi]], even Jesus going to seek the Father as seekers.

3. **Grounded in the knock/seek logic of scripture (rated: strong, scriptural).** "Knock and it shall be opened... ask and ye shall receive, seek and ye shall find." God "is always there, ready" but waits to be sought, refining agency so the relationship is genuinely chosen rather than overwhelming.[^4] This connects to the veil/agency rationale: an "overpowering awareness of God all the time" would leave "no choice but to tune in," so the veil exists "so that we can have agency."[^5]

## Best Quotes

> "They think if God just chose Abraham arbitrarily, no special reason — Calvinists think this... He's choosing Abraham to save, and He's gonna let all these other people just go to hell. What we have in the Book of Abraham is something very different."[^1]

> "God comes to Abraham because Abraham has been trying to come to God."[^1]

> "We usually find God because we're seeking God. The Lord reaches, He's always there. He's always pursuing us."[^3]
