# Ephesians 2:8-9 — "Saved by Grace Not Works" Rebutted

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
[[Ephesians 2.8-9|Ephesians 2:8-9]] — "by grace are ye saved through faith... not of works" — proves the LDS emphasis on works is a false, works-based gospel.

## The Rebuttal

### 1. Grace operates *through faith* — and faith acts
The verse itself says "through faith." Real faith expresses itself in action.

> "Demonstrations of faith are what trigger grace." [^1]

The woman with the issue of blood was not healed *by* touching the cloak, but her faithful act triggered Christ's grace [^1]. Compare [[James 2.14-26|James 2]]: faith without works is dead.

### 2. Faith and works are not rivals
[[C.S. Lewis]]'s image: asking whether faith or works matters more is "like saying which one of the blades on a pair of scissors is more important" [^2]. See [[Faith and Works - Two Blades of the Scissors]].

### 3. The Peter-on-the-water test
Faith alone (stay in the boat) saves no one; works alone (step out with no faith) sinks. Both together put Peter in position — but what actually saves is grace.

> "What actually saved him was the grace of the Savior picking him up out of the water. So the faith and the works put us in a position to be saved by the grace of Jesus Christ." [^3]

### 4. "Salvation" is defined differently
Much of the dispute is equivocation: in LDS theology grace *alone* grants resurrection and forgiveness (salvation from death and sin) for all, while [[Degrees of Glory vs Heaven|exaltation]] is a further question. Critics collapse these [^4].
