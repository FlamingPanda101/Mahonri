# Broad-Market Investing Is Not a Lobbying Tool

## The Point
A second strand of the Tucker Carlson segment claims the Church uses its holdings as a covert lobbying engine — owning Pfizer means it "went around telling people to get" the COVID vaccine, owning defense stocks means it promotes war "because they know it'll make them money."[^1] The hosts answer with an **internal-consistency disproof**: a fund's behavior, if it were really steering members toward its own positions, would track the *size* of those positions. Ensign Peak's largest holdings are Microsoft, Apple, and Nvidia — which "dwarf our investment in Pfizer." So if the Church were secretly lobbying for its book, "they're not doing it in the right way at all."[^2] The premise predicts conduct the evidence flatly contradicts.

## Evidence
1. **The conspiracy's own logic backfires.** "If the church were doing that... the church would be telling everybody to buy Microsoft, Apple, and Nvidia. Because those investments dwarf our investment in Pfizer. So what you're saying doesn't make any sense."[^2] A rational manipulator pushes its *biggest* bets; the Church promotes none of them. The vaccine/Pfizer charge fails because the holding is small and no corresponding promotion of the giant holdings exists.

2. **Index exposure is structural, not directional.** Defense contractors, pharma, and tech appear in the portfolio because Ensign Peak participates in "the growth of global markets" via broad, S&P 500-style investing that "rise[s] and fall[s] with the markets."[^3] Holdings reflect the composition of the market, not a thesis about which industries to grow. A passive holder does not gain by "promulgating" the underlying activity; it gains or loses with the whole economy.

3. **A 13F is an informational disclosure, not a market lever.** The hosts explain (via Apffel's own description) that a fund over the threshold files Form 13F simply to disclose holdings "because you could technically sway the market" — and Ensign Peak now files a consolidated 13F (latest cited February 2026).[^4] Public quarterly disclosure of holdings is the opposite of a covert influence operation. (On the 13F/SEC history itself, see [[The SEC Settlement Does Not Prove the Church Hid Its Wealth]].)

4. **Divestment is a live, prudential option — not evidence of guilt.** The hosts grant that if a holding's character changed (a portfolio company going "full-on AI-generated porn"), "the church might go, well, maybe we're not going to invest in you anymore... the church should be sensitive to those changes."[^5] Treating divestment as a normal stewardship decision shows the holdings are managed for prudence and ethics, not weaponized for advocacy.

5. **The size argument scales to defense, too.** The same disproof applies to the war charge: a ~$25M defense position inside a ~$3.5B portfolio, with a contractor ranking ~92nd, is far too small to be a lobbying instrument — consistent with [[Profiting From War Charge Confuses Passive Holdings With Endorsement]]. Lobbying theories require the Church to act on its holdings; passivity is what the record shows.

## LDS Connection
The Church's stated investment intent — voiced by a participant from Ensign Peak's founding under [[Gordon B. Hinckley]] — is to be "good stewards" who "make the most" of consecrated reserves "in a socially responsible manner," screening out direct participation in sin industries.[^6] That is a fiduciary and [[parable of the talents]] posture, not a propaganda apparatus. The lobbying charge mistakes ordinary, disclosed, broad-market fund management for covert manipulation. See also [[Profiting From War Charge Confuses Passive Holdings With Endorsement]].
