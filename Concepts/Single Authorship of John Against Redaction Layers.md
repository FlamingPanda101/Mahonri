# John Had One Main Author (Against Multiple Redaction Layers)

## The Claim
Although older scholarship explained John's "seams" and geographic "skips" by positing successive authors/editors expanding the gospel, the better explanation is **one main author** with a clear vision and distinctive style, who developed his gospel **gradually in drafts and stages** — leaving a few seams the way any large hand-composed book might. (Two passages remain genuine later *additions* — see related notes.)

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The "skips" are real but minor.** John 5 has Jesus in Jerusalem, then John 6 puts him across the Sea of Galilee ("like saying Jesus is in North Carolina, then went to the other side of Lake Michigan"); in John 14 Jesus says he can't talk much longer, then speaks three more chapters. The redaction hypothesis was a reasonable response to these. *(Strong as data; the seams exist.)* [^1]
2. **Overwhelming unity swamps the seams.** Across the book one finds consistent language and style, simple predictable Greek patterns, unified terminology, and unity of thought — and the John 14 "speech" is actually a cohesive, continuous discourse on both sides of the odd verse. *(Strong; the unity is the heart of the case.)* [^2]
3. **Ancient gospels were composed in drafts.** [[Hugo Mendez]] cites [[James Barker]]'s *Writing and Rewriting the Gospels*: authors sketched stories on tablets, reworked them, and only later committed them to a scroll — so a single author could leave seams and even typos. *(Strong; grounded in a recent monograph on ancient compositional practice.)* [^3]
4. **The author's own books leave seams too.** Méndez notes that even his own modern book has "a skip or seam or two" and the occasional typo — ancient authors, lacking even "Clippy," were more prone to such artifacts, not less. *(Moderate; an a fortiori illustration.)* [^4]

**Steelman of the opposing view.** The classic source/redaction critics (Bultmann's signs-source and discourse layers, multiple "editions" of John) were responding to genuinely hard data — the aporias, doublets, and the seemingly displaced chapters 5–6 — and the "we" of [[John 21.24|John 21:24]] still suggests more than one hand. Single authorship "with drafts" must absorb all those tensions into one writer's revision process, which some find less economical than positing editors.

## Best Quotes
> "We're probably instead looking at really one author... one author who developed his gospel gradually." — [[Hugo Mendez]] [^2]

> "An excellent book... is James Barker's Writing and Rewriting the Gospels... authors were sketching out particular stories on one tablet, maybe another tablet... before they ever actually hit their final scroll." [^3]

> "My book has a skip or seam or two if you look very closely, and the occasional typo that missed the press... Ancient authors were just as prone to those sorts of things." [^4]
