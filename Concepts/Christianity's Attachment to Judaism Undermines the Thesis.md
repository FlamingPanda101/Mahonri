# Christianity's Attachment to Judaism Undermines the Thesis

## The Claim (Objection)

[[Alex OConnor|Alex O'Connor]]: "My biggest problem with Christianity as responsible for these developments is its attachment to Judaism." The Old Testament God commands brutalities (Canaanite genocide, [[Exodus 22.29|firstborn sacrifice]], [[Leviticus 25.44-46|chattel slavery]]) that are "unthinkable today." So Christianity cannot coherently both *own* this God and *claim* the values that condemn his commands.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The Marcionite dilemma.** O'Connor frames it sharply: either you become a [[Marcion|Marcionite]] (treat the OT as wrong, "only Jesus matters") — but then you're a heretic and the thesis loses its scriptural base; or you "own" the OT — but then you must explain why Yahweh's actions "weren't wrong" while condemning Rome for similar acts.[^2] *(Strong structural argument — it forces a genuine trilemma on the Protestant who grounds values in Scripture.)*

2. **[[Marcion]]'s historical precedent.** Marcion's *Antithesis* compared OT-God and Jesus and concluded "these cannot be the same kind of person."[^1] *(Accurate church history; shows the tension is ancient, not a modern atheist invention.)*

3. **Specific texts.** O'Connor cites [[Exodus 22.29|Exodus 22:29]] (give me your firstborn son), [[Genesis 22|Abraham and Isaac]], and the Canaanite conquest as "bracing" texts.[^3] *(See [[Surviving Traces of Human Sacrifice in the Hebrew Bible]]. O'Connor admits using these as "proof-texts" is "a bit too lazy / too simple" — he deploys them to *demonstrate* the simplistic method, then asks for the big picture.)*

4. **Steelman of Scrivener — narrative, not proof-text.** Scrivener's reply: Christians have never read the Bible as a collection of stand-alone timeless propositions; they read it as a single narrative culminating in Christ crucified, taking troubling details "through the cross." "You're going to the Bible in a way that's alien to the way anybody has ever gone to the Bible."[^4] He also argues the Jewish recoil *within* its own context (Jews opposed Roman infanticide and pederasty) shows the OT tradition was already counter-cultural.[^5] See [[The Narrative Reading of Scripture vs Proof-Texting]] and [[The Recoil from OT Violence Is Itself Christian]]. *(Strong — this is the standard and defensible hermeneutical answer; its weakness is that "read it all toward Christ" can also be used to neutralize *any* inconvenient text, which is O'Connor's falsifiability worry transposed to hermeneutics.)*
