# Journal of Discourses Misrepresents Brigham Young

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
The harsh, autocratic, blood-curdling [[Brigham Young]] quotations that circulate online — drawn largely from the *Journal of Discourses* — are taken as a faithful record proving he was a cruel, authoritarian man.

## The Rebuttal (Numbered)

1. **The shorthand expert's verdict (rated: strong; specialist primary-source testimony).** [[LaJean Carruth]], an expert in 19th-century Pitman shorthand at the Church History Department, can return to the *original* sermon notes. Asked the single biggest misrepresentation of Brigham Young, she answered: "the Journal of Discourses." Transcriber George D. Watt, when his shorthand was ambiguous, consistently "would make Brigham Young look tougher, more autocratic... than the text really says."[^1]

2. **Concrete textual examples (rated: strong).** Watt rendered "I am perfect in my generation," but the originals say "*If* I am perfect." Watt has Brigham bluntly telling the Saints they had lost faith on the trail West; the shorthand shows Brigham *asking* — "Have many of you lost your faith? Are many of you in that condition?" — and then expressing sympathy. "What you see [in the originals] is a kinder man than what is often represented."[^2]

3. **Lost paralinguistic context (rated: moderate; general principle).** Even accurate transcripts strip tone, facial expression, and the relationship between speaker and audience. The Saints called him "Brother Brigham" and liked him; he could say rough-sounding things "with a twinkle in his eye" or "a wink," the way old friends insult each other playfully — context invisible in print.[^3] Example: a woman complained her husband told her to "go to hell"; Brigham's reply, "Well, don't go," is humor lost without tone.[^4] *Steelman:* humor/tone is unverifiable and can be invoked to wave away any uncomfortable quotation; this argument is strongest where (as in #1–2) it rests on documentary collation, weaker where it rests on inferred tone.

4. **The pastoral counter-archive (rated: moderate; forthcoming scholarship).** The Church historian's office is preparing a two-volume set of Brigham's letters to ordinary Saints, which (Peterson is told) reveal "a pastoral side of Brigham Young that you don't normally see" — the thundering pulpit voice contrasted with patient, helpful private correspondence.[^5]

## Best Quotes

> "I asked her once, what's the biggest misrepresentation of Brigham Young? ... She said, the Journal of Discourses."[^1]

> "He would say, I am perfect in my generation. She said, No. He said, If I am, if I am perfect. ... What you see is a kinder man than what is often represented."[^2]

> "You can take a quotation out of context from him and think, Oh, that's blood curdling. What a horrible man."[^3]
