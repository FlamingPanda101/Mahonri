# I Believe in One God the Father Almighty Encodes the Monarchy of the Father

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
Protestants and Catholics assert that affirming the Nicene Creed entails affirming co-equal Trinitarianism — "you don't believe in the Nicene Creed, you're not Christian." [^1] The guest turns this around: the Creed's very first clause refutes co-equality.

## The Rebuttal

### 1. The opening clause asserts the Father's unique deity (the central argument)
"The first sentence of the creed: 'I believe in one God, the Father Almighty.' The bishops at the Council of Nicaea understood the monarchy of the Father." [^2] The guest reads the singular *one God* as identifying the **Father alone** as the one true God, with the Son subordinated to that monarchy: "That shows there is not co-equality between the Father, Son, Holy Spirit. It puts the Father in this role of monarchy that the Son is subordinated to." [^3] **Rating: a genuine patristic theme, overpressed.** The monarchy of the Father (the Father as *sole source/principle* within the Godhead) is real Eastern Orthodox doctrine, but Orthodoxy holds it *alongside* consubstantial co-equality, not against it.

### 2. The "you don't believe it" reversal (rhetorical)
"If you ever try and tell me I don't believe the Nicene Creed — you don't believe the Nicene Creed. You do not believe in one God, the Father almighty. You believe in co-equality between three beings." [^4] The co-host summarizes: "You kind of debunked the Trinity using the Creed of the Trinity." [^5]

### 3. Patristic backing cited (appeal to authority)
The guest invokes **Justin Martyr** — "The Son is a derivative of the one true God, the Father" — and points the audience to **Beau Branson's** six-part YouTube series "Monarchy of the Father" and **David Bercot's** *Will the Real Heretics Please Stand Up* for the early-church picture. [^6] He also cites the patristic distinction that the Father alone is **autotheos** (God in himself) while the Son is **deuterotheos** (God in a second/derived sense): "that is subordination. So, don't tell me that subordination is heresy." [^7] **Rating: the autotheos/deuterotheos and Justin material is real**, though the deuterotheos terminology is more associated with later (e.g. Reformed-era Calvin debates and Origenist) usage than with Nicaea itself.

### 4. Steelman of the opposing view
The orthodox reply: "one God, the Father Almighty" reflects the *taxis* (order) of the persons and the Father's role as unbegotten source, but the Creed immediately calls the Son "true God from true God ... homoousios with the Father" — language coined to assert the Son shares the *same* divine being, ruling out a ranked second deity. Eastern Orthodoxy affirms the monarchy of the Father precisely *without* subordinating the Son's *essence*; Branson's own project is to defend Nicene orthodoxy, not LDS Godhead theology. The guest's inference from "one God = the Father only" therefore proves less than claimed: it shows personal distinction and a source-relation, not ontological inequality.
