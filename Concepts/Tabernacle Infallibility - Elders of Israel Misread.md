# Tabernacle Infallibility — "Elders of Israel" Misread

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
[[Joe Heschmeyer]] argued on *Pints with Aquinas* that [[Brigham Young]] claimed something like papal **ex cathedra** infallibility for words spoken in the **Tabernacle** — that even though prophets can err, this error "doesn't bind in the Tabernacle." [[Luke Hansen]] rebuts that the quote, read in full context, is about **orthopraxy** (Christ-like living) among the whole priesthood body, not doctrinal infallibility for the prophet.

## The Rebuttal

### 1. "Elders of Israel" means the whole priesthood, not the prophet (strong — lexical/contextual)
The quote (from "Self-Government, Mysteries, etc.") addresses "a bishop, high priest, a president of a stake or quorum... or any individual saint." Hansen: "elders of Israel is very clearly relating to just the priesthood body of the church as a whole," and "infallibility is not granted to just any member of the entire priesthood body" — doctrine comes "from the top," from the Q15 [^1]. Heschmeyer concedes this is "an important caveat."

### 2. The passage is about a *mode of being*, not a doctrine (moderate — exegetical)
Young's surrounding text frames the "greatest mystery" as learning "how to control the human mind and bring every faculty... in subjugation to Jesus Christ" — i.e., walking "in the paths of righteousness." Hansen: "he's not attaching it to a specific doctrine. He's talking in an orthopraxy rather than an orthodoxy kind of way" — the body protects each other from *un-Christlike action* [^2].

### 3. "Protection from error," not "infallibility" (moderate — the key distinction)
Hansen draws the LDS line: infallibility means "it's impossible for anything to be wrong," whereas LDS hold "you can't be led astray" — like a flight that hits turbulence and lands hard but does not crash [^3]. Heschmeyer presses that "protection from serious enough error that you would go astray" is *itself* a form of infallibility — "qualified infallibility" [^4].

### Steelman (Heschmeyer's side)
Heschmeyer's strongest counter is the **1870 JoD vol. 13** quote, where Young defends sending out discourses that "they may call... scripture... as good scripture as is couched in this Bible" — escalating well past "a turbulent plane that doesn't go off course" [^5]. He also cites [[J. Reuben Clark]] that the President "alone has the right to receive revelations for the church... or to give authoritative interpretations" — which sounds "bigger than a plane that's turbulent" [^6].

## Best Quotes
> "Here is the place for you to teach great mysteries to your brethren because here are those who can correct you. This fault the elders of Israel do not fall into in this tabernacle." — Brigham Young, quoted by Hansen [^2]

> "It'd be like a plane — there's no such thing as an infallible flight... but countless flights do not go astray." — Luke Hansen [^3]

> "It seems like you're protected from serious enough error that you would go astray... which is what infallibility is meant to also say." — Joe Heschmeyer [^4]
