# LDS Afterlife — Degrees of Glory and Post-Mortal Progression

## The Claim

Latter-day Saint (LDS / Mormon) theology avoids the **Problem of Hell and
Creation** through three distinctive doctrines:

1. **Pre-existence of souls** — souls weren't created from nothing (*ex nihilo*)
   but existed eternally in a primitive state
2. **Varying degrees of glory** — no binary heaven/hell; all receive a level
   of happiness commensurate with their willingness and capacity
3. **Post-mortal progression** — the door isn't closed at death; individuals
   can continue to progress, repent, and accept Christ in the afterlife

## How It Solves the Problem

### 1. No Creation of Depraved Souls from Nothing

On the LDS view, God did **not** create souls ex nihilo with depraved
attributes destined for damnation. Souls have always existed in some primitive
state. God proposed a plan wherein we could enter mortality — **if we so chose**
— to learn loving relationships, forgiveness, and how to be as God is. [^1]

### 2. No Eternal Conscious Torment

God doesn't cause eternal conscious torment for those who don't meet the
requirements. Rather:

> "Each person will receive the degree of glory that is as good as they are
> willing to receive." [^1]

### 3. Post-Mortal Opportunity

> "Those who had limiting or unfair circumstances in this life will have
> sufficient opportunity to forgive, to be forgiven, and to receive Christ's
> grace after death, receiving the degree of glory that they so desire." [^1]

### 4. All Degrees Are Incomprehensibly Good

> "There is no binary heaven or hell dichotomy. Rather, there are varying
> degrees of glory that are all said to be incomprehensibly happy and good." [^1]

### 5. Justice Without Eternal Suffering

> "God is still just in the LDS view because the requirements still have to
> be met. The difference is God doesn't close the door on your progress before
> it is completely over or damn you to hell for all eternity." [^2]

There are still punishments and retributions for the evil and vile, but **not
eternal suffering that serves no ultimate purpose**. All will have the ability
to accept Christ in the afterlife without the limitations or imperfections of
mortal probation. [^2]

## The Key Distinction

Classical theism (creation ex nihilo + eternal hell) creates the problem.
LDS theology sidesteps it entirely by denying both premises:

| Classical Theism | LDS Theology |
|------------------|--------------|
| Souls created from nothing | Souls pre-exist eternally |
| Binary heaven/hell | Degrees of glory |
| Door closes at death | Post-mortal progression possible |
| God responsible for depravity | Souls chose mortality freely |
| Eternal conscious torment | All will be as happy as they're willing to be |
