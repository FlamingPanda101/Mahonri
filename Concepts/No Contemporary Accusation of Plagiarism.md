# No Contemporary Accusation of Plagiarism

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
**Objection:** Joseph Smith plagiarized the [[Book of Mormon]] from [[Ethan Smith]]'s *View of the Hebrews*, the "most common explanation" critics give for the book's origin.[^1]

**Rebuttal:** The accusation is conspicuously *absent* from the historical record during Joseph Smith's lifetime. The people best positioned to notice — Joseph's contemporaries, his critics, and Ethan Smith himself — never made the charge. It surfaces for the first time in 1902 and only becomes prominent decades later.

## The Rebuttal

1. **Silence from every contemporary critic (rated: strong, argument from documented absence).** "There is no contemporary accusation from any of Joseph Smith's contemporaries, any of his critics, not even Ethan Smith himself."[^2] Smoot has searched for one and found none. In Joseph's day, the rival naturalistic theories were that Joseph wrote it himself or stole the [[Solomon Spalding]] manuscript — not Ethan Smith.[^3]
2. **Ethan Smith's own silence is telling (rated: strong, documented).** Ethan Smith was a living, combative author who publicly feuded in the press with William Miller over prophecy. Yet "I have no record of Ethan Smith saying, 'Harumph, that Joseph Smith stole my idea for his Book of Mormon.'"[^4] The man with the most motive to cry theft never did.
3. **The informed audience saw no connection (rated: moderate, inferential).** "The immediate audience who would have been most familiar with things like Ethan Smith's arguments and the Book of Mormon, they saw no connection between these two things."[^2] *Steelman:* absence of evidence is not proof of independence — *View of the Hebrews* had only "limited circulation" at first (mostly Vermont/New York), so contemporaries near Joseph may simply not have read it. Smoot's reply: the 2nd edition got national distribution (reviews as far as South Carolina), and LDS writers *did* read it — they cited it favorably, never as a smoking gun (see [[Joseph Smith Cited View of the Hebrews as Evidence]]).

## Best Quotes

> "There is no contemporary accusation from any of Joseph Smith's contemporaries, any of his critics, not even Ethan Smith himself ... that we think Joseph Smith stole his golden Bible from Ethan Smith."[^2]

> "I have no record of Ethan Smith saying, 'Harumph, that Joseph Smith stole my idea for his Book of Mormon.' ... there's no contemporary accusation of plagiarism. It appears much later."[^4]

> "Pretty much in Joseph Smith's day, the accusations are either like Joseph wrote it himself or Solomon's Spalding."[^3]
