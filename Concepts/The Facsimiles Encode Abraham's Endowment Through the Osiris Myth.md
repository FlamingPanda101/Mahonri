# The Facsimiles Encode Abraham's Endowment Through the Osiris Myth

#type/claim

## The Claim
The Book of Abraham facsimiles are not a misread funerary vignette but a **snapshot of the whole Osiris death-resurrection myth**, used to encode **Abraham's endowment**. Two specific moves: (1) The lion-couch figure that critics call "the dead god Osiris" is read by Joseph Smith as a *living* Abraham — and Egyptian convention backs the "living" reading: **feet apart = alive, feet together = dead**, and **one or both hands raised = speaking / supplication**, not a corpse. The recovery of the Metropolitan Museum parchment in 1967 reportedly *vindicated* this: the figure's feet are apart and a hand is up, matching live-Osiris temple scenes (Opet, Dendera) rather than the standard arms-crossed mummy. [^1][^2] (2) The book maps onto the myth's arc: facsimile 1 = the **death of the king/god** (Abraham as substitute on the couch), facsimile 2 = **resurrection and ascension**, facsimile 3 (the missing third of the book) = the **presentation/embrace** — "basically what you're getting is the endowment… Abraham's endowment." [^3] The hosts press that even [[Robert Ritner]], who "wrote a whole book on the Joseph Smith papyri and hammers Joseph Smith," "knows it inside and out and holds back" on the Osiris-as-[[Osiris Is a Type Not a Person - The Blessed Dead Become Osiris|type]] reading that makes "Abraham Osiris" unremarkable. [^4]

## Supporting Evidence (steelman)

1. **The iconographic conventions are checkable.** Feet-position and hand-gesture as life/speech markers, and the existence of *living* (temple, non-funerary) lion-couch / Osiris-with-feet-apart scenes at Opet and Dendera, are claims an Egyptologist can adjudicate against published reliefs — not pure assertion. If sound, they blunt the flat "this is just a dead-Osiris funerary scene" version of the critique. *Rating: a real, falsifiable point.*
2. **"It's his funerary text → it's *for him*."** Granting the [[Osiris Is a Type Not a Person - The Blessed Dead Become Osiris|Osiris-as-type]] premise, identifying the central figure with the document's owner ("Abraham Osiris") is exactly what funerary texts do, so "that's Osiris, not Abraham" is a category error rather than a knockout. *Rating: internally coherent given the type premise.*
3. **External corroboration arrived after 1842.** The note pairs naturally with [[Apocryphal Sources Corroborate the Book of Abraham After 1842]] and [[Judge the Book of Abraham by Its Fruits]]: the apologetic does not rest on the surviving fragments alone. *Rating: strengthens the cumulative case.*

**Steelman of the skeptical counter.** The mainstream Egyptological reply (Ritner et al.) is that the surviving Hor Book of Breathings is an ordinary Greco-Roman funerary text; Joseph's explanations of the facsimiles do not match what the figures conventionally mean; and the restored lacunae (the priest, the figures' identities) were filled by Joseph in ways Egyptology rejects. On the specifics: even if "feet apart" can mark a living figure, that does **not** make the figure *Abraham* — it is still the document-owner assimilated to Osiris, so the convention rescues "alive," not "Abraham." The "Ritner holds back / knows it's really Abraham" move is an **unfalsifiable appeal to a hostile witness's silence**: an expert declining to endorse a devotional reading is not evidence he secretly accepts it. And the clean facsimile-1/2/3 = death/resurrection/endowment scheme leans on the conveniently *missing* third panel, where the strongest "endowment" content cannot be tested. The honest Latter-day Saint posture (which the hosts gesture at) is that this is a **faith-illuminated** reading of genuine iconography, persuasive within the temple frame, not a translation Egyptology will concede; see also [[The Book of Abraham Has Only Three Apologetic Exits - Reel]] for the constrained option-space.
