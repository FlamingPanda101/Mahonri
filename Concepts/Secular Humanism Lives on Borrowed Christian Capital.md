# Secular Humanism Lives on Borrowed Christian Capital
## The Point
Secular humanism is not a freestanding worldview built from reason alone. Its core moral commitments — universal human dignity, equality, rights, and compassion for the weak — are *historically inherited* from Christianity. Humanism is an attempt to keep these moral fruits while cutting away the theological root that produced and grounded them: it spends down "borrowed capital." Take the verdict of Tom Holland's *Dominion* (the video's central source): much of Western intellectual culture over the past 150 years "has been an attempt by various people who no longer believe in the Christian God to find a justification for the beliefs that derive from Christianity."[^1]

## Evidence
1. **The fruits-without-roots warning.** [[Pope Benedict XVI]], addressing the UN, is paraphrased as saying "the whole idea of human rights is absolutely rooted in the Judeo-Christian tradition, and if you try and get the fruits of that without the roots all you'll get is the thing will collapse into shrill special interest rhetoric, everyone claiming the status of victims."[^2] The image is precisely borrowed capital: the harvest continues for a while after the tree is felled, then fails.

2. **Humanism as abstraction from a matrix.** Humanism is "essentially an attempt to abstract" the notion of universal human dignity "from the matrix of Christianity and to allow it to kind of live without the cultural framework that had originally given it" life-force.[^3] The dignity is real; the claim is that its *grounding* was Christian and is now missing.

3. **Modern moral philosophy is Christian inheritance unrooted.** Holland's observation, which the narrator calls "truly brilliant," is "that most of modern moral philosophy is people trying to hold on to their Christian moral inheritance but without rooting it in Christian theology."[^4] Western peoples morally orient themselves by a "depth charge dropped by Paul and others via the New Testament" without realizing its source.[^5]

4. **Even Marxism is unrooted Christian morality.** Marx's "indignation at the sufferings of the poor at the hands of the rich" and his "conviction that there will be a day of judgment when the poor will inherit the earth and the rich will be cast down" go "way beyond his number crunching"; descended from Jews who converted to Lutheranism, he is "clearly drawing on the moral inheritance of both Jewish and Christian teachings" while letting one "buy into these teachings without actually having to buy into Christian belief."[^6] (This connects to the broader [[Borrowing Objective Morality to Condemn Evil]] move.)

## Best Quotes

## LDS Connection
The restored gospel locates the worth of souls not in a free-floating axiom but in a divine source: every person is the literal spirit child of God and bears inherent, eternal worth ("the worth of souls is great in the sight of God," D&C 18:10). This supplies exactly the *root* the borrowed-capital argument says secular humanism lacks — human dignity is grounded in real kinship with a real God, not in inherited sentiment. The same logic appears across the apologetic tradition in the claim that morality must be *grounded*, not merely felt; see [[The Value of Human Life Is a Faith Claim Not a Rational One]].
