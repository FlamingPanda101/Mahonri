# Trinitarianism Is Anachronistic to the Biblical Period

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
The Trinitarian objection (voiced by [[Avery (God Logic)]]) is that the angel-of-Yahweh and New Testament data point to a Trinitarian or at least Binitarian Godhead already present in the biblical texts. [[Dan McClellan]] argues this is "even less reasonable a conclusion to draw from the available data" than the LDS divine-investiture reading — it is "something that one just asserts arbitrarily, counting on the agreements of the audience rather than on... the data." [^1]

## The Rebuttal

1. **The Trinity is a late philosophical construction.** McClellan dates the relevant frameworks to "between the late 2nd and the 5th centuries CE" — they "didn't even exist when the Gospel of John was written." *Rating: aligns with mainstream historical-theology dating of conciliar Trinitarian formulation (Nicaea 325, Constantinople 381, Chalcedon 451); strong on the philosophical articulation, though development-of-doctrine advocates would say seeds are earlier.* [^2]

2. **No first-century (much less mid-first-millennium-BCE) source carries the philosophical apparatus.** "Folks who just assert that it's Trinitarianism cannot show that anything remotely approximating the philosophical framework of the Trinity was in circulation in the 1st century CE, much less centuries earlier." *Rating: a strong burden-of-proof challenge.* [^3] *Steelman of the Trinitarian side:* defenders argue the doctrine is the necessary later systematization of genuine first-century data (worship of Jesus, the Logos hymn, triadic formulas), so absence of the *vocabulary* does not entail absence of the *reality* the vocabulary later named.

3. **It misreads what is actually happening with Jesus.** McClellan says the Trinitarian reading "isn't actually what's going on with Jesus in the New Testament" — the operative logic is name-bearing divine mediation / divine images, not consubstantial tri-personhood. *Rating: depends on accepting his divine-images model (see related note).* [^4]

4. **Both debaters lose on the data.** McClellan's verdict: "neither of these folks has really drawn an accurate bead on what's going on in these texts" — God Logic wins the narrow point against Hansen, but God Logic's own Trinitarian position is "even less supported by the data." *Rating: a pox-on-both-houses conclusion characteristic of his non-confessional method.* [^5]

## Best Quotes
> "A Trinitarian or at least Binitarian position is even less supported by the data. That's something that one just asserts arbitrarily, counting on the agreements of the audience." [^1]

> "They didn't even exist when the Gospel of John was written. They are constructed between the late 2nd and the 5th centuries CE." [^2]

> "Folks who just assert that it's Trinitarianism cannot show that anything remotely approximating the philosophical framework of the Trinity was in circulation in the 1st century CE, much less centuries earlier in the mid first millennium BCE." [^3]
