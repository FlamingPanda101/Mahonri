# Two Distinct Slaveries Debt-Bondage and Chattel

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
Apologists (and Joshua HS) claim Old Testament slavery was essentially **one thing** — voluntary, temporary debt-servitude functioning as a social safety net for people who fell on hard times.[^1]

## The Rebuttal (Numbered)

1. **Two separate institutions (rated: strong; primary text).** McClellan: debt slavery was "primarily, but not exclusively for native Israelites," while "there was absolutely child slavery in the Hebrew Bible... intended for everyone who was not a native Israelite." The chattel slavery of [[Leviticus 25.44-46|Leviticus 25:44-46]] is "a different type of slavery altogether" from the bond-service of [[Leviticus 25.39-43|Leviticus 25:39-43]].[^2]

2. **Debt slavery was not a safety net (rated: strong).** "Debt slavery was not a social safety net. And debt slavery was not the only type of slavery." The framing of it as benign social welfare is "wrong on two counts."[^1]

3. **Foreign chattel slaves were heritable property (rated: strong; primary text).** Foreign slaves could be bought and "pass[ed] down to their children" as permanent possessions — the hallmark of chattel slavery, not debt-bondage.[^3]

4. **Sourcing was indiscriminate (rated: moderate).** Prisoners of war were one source, but "however foreign peoples got their slaves didn't matter to the Israelites. If there were foreign enslaved folks for sale, they could buy them."[^3]

5. **Steelman.** The apologist is right that **Israelite debt-bondage** had distinctive features (Jubilee, release-gift) that softened it relative to chattel slavery. McClellan concedes "there is a bondservant type of slavery." The error is treating that milder tier as the *whole* of biblical slavery while ignoring the foreign chattel tier sitting in the very next verses.[^2]

## Best Quotes

> "Debt slavery was primarily, but not exclusively for native Israelites. But there was absolutely child slavery in the Hebrew Bible. And that was intended for everyone who was not a native Israelite."[^2]

> "So there is a bondservant type of slavery. But the problem is there's also another kind of slavery that is not bondservant slavery. Namely in Leviticus 25 that talks about foreign slaves that you can pass on to your children."[^4]
