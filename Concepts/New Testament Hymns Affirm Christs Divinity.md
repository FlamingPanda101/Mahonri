#type/claim

## The Point
Several early Christian hymns appear to be quoted in the New Testament, and they share recurring themes that reveal what the earliest believers held most important — above all, the divinity of Jesus Christ. Pliny the Younger's report (ca. AD 112) that Christians sang "a hymn to Christ as God" corroborates this from an outside source.

## Evidence
1. Likely embedded NT hymns include [[John 1.1|John 1:1–18]]; [[Philippians 2.5-8|Philippians 2:5–11]]; [[Colossians 1.15-17|Colossians 1:15–20]]; 2 Timothy 2:11–13; and 1 Peter 2:21–25.
2. Their shared themes: praising Jesus Christ, quoting scripture, referring to Jesus's premortal divinity, celebrating Jesus's role in showing what God is like, describing his self-humbling and salvific death, and testifying that Jesus brings life, reconciliation, healing, and freedom from sins.
3. Pliny's "hymn to Christ as God" calls to mind NT hymns emphasizing Christ's divinity: "In the beginning was the Word ... and the Word was God" (John 1:1); "In him all the fullness of God was pleased to dwell" (Colossians 1:19); "Christ Jesus ... was in the form of God" (Philippians 2:5–6).
4. Some hymns were written by church leaders, but many by ordinary, anonymous believers.

## Best Quotes
> "Several early Christian hymns seem to be quoted in books of the New Testament. Some of these hymns ... share recurring themes, giving us insight into what early Christians held to be most important: the hymns praise Jesus Christ, quote scripture, refer to Jesus's premortal divinity ..." [^1]

> "Pliny's reference to 'a hymn to Christ as God' calls to mind New Testament hymns that emphasize Christ's divinity." [^2]

## LDS Connection
The hymns' references to Jesus's premortal divinity offer scriptural support that resonates with Latter-day Saint belief in the premortal Christ, while their early date and external corroboration speak to a primitive high Christology.
