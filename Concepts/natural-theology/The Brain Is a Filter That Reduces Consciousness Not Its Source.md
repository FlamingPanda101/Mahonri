# The Brain Is a Filter That Reduces Consciousness Not Its Source

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Robert Greene]] argues (drawing on Aldous Huxley and Henri Bergson) that the brain is a **filtering / reducing valve**, not the *producer* of consciousness. Its job is to narrow an overwhelming flood of reality down to a survivable trickle. When that filter is loosened — by psychedelics, deep meditation, or near-death — the constriction relaxes and people perceive *more* than ordinary waking life affords: colors, emotions, and a sense of the "whole warehouse" lit up. If the brain *generated* consciousness, loosening it should diminish experience; instead experience *expands*.

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. The "reducing valve" tradition (medium)
Huxley (after Bergson) held that mind-at-large is funneled through the brain and nervous system, which "eliminate" most of what we could perceive so we are not overwhelmed. Greene frames ordinary perception as a flashlight in a vast dark warehouse; an NDE subject described her experience as the *whole* warehouse suddenly illuminated.[^1]

### 2. Measured brain activity *drops* under psychedelics (medium, second-hand)
Greene and O'Connor cite fMRI studies in which cortical activity *decreases* under psychedelics even as subjective experience becomes vastly richer — the opposite of what a "brain produces mind" model predicts, and consistent with the brain *restricting* rather than generating experience.[^2] (This summarizes work such as Carhart-Harris's psilocybin imaging; see the primary literature.)

### 3. Cross-cultural convergence of "opened" states (medium)
The same phenomenology — oceanic oneness, dissolution of the discriminating self, perception of new "colors" — recurs across psychedelics, NDEs, initiation ordeals, and contemplative practice in cultures with no contact. Greene takes the universality as evidence that something real about consciousness is being uncovered when the filter drops, not merely hallucinated.[^3]

## Steelman of the Opposing View
The materialist reply: decreased *measured* activity does not mean decreased *information processing* — disinhibition (taking the brakes off normally-suppressed networks) can produce richer experience while lowering metabolic signal, all fully within a brain-generates-mind model. "Opened" states are then disorganized neural activity, and their cross-cultural similarity is explained by shared neuro-architecture, not access to an external reality. The filter metaphor is evocative but underdetermined by the data.

## Counter-Objection
Greene's and O'Connor's rejoinder is to push the question one step: *if* loosening the brain expands consciousness, what happens as activity approaches zero (clinical death)? The materialist must say experience should vanish; yet reports cluster precisely there (see [[Near-Death Experiences in the Brain-Dead Challenge Materialism]]). The disinhibition story also still owes an account of *veridical* perceptions acquired while the cortex is most suppressed. O'Connor remains "agnostic" but insists the filter model is at least as live as the production model and should not be ruled out by metaphysical fiat.

## Apologetic Engagement
A secular best-selling author and an agnostic philosopher independently arguing that *the brain restricts rather than makes the mind* is useful corroboration for the dualist intuition the apologetic tradition defends (see [[The Mind Is Not the Brain]], [[Brain-Mind Correlation Is Not Causation - Mental States Have Causal Power]]). The caution: the "filter" model alone is neutral between Platonic dualism, panpsychism, and Eastern monism — Greene leans pagan/Zen, not theist. Its apologetic value is *defeating reductive materialism*, opening conceptual space for an immaterial soul, not by itself establishing the LDS account.

## LDS Relevance
The Restoration's anthropology already predicts a tight-but-non-reductive body-mind relation: "the spirit and the body are the soul of man" ([[Doctrine and Covenants 88.15|D&C 88:15]]), the spirit being an eternal intelligence that *acts through* a physical body rather than being produced by neural tissue. A brain that *channels and constrains* an independently-existing mind is exactly what one expects if the person is a pre-existent spirit clothed in a body — see [[Consciousness Precedes Existence - Neuroscience and Pre-Existence]].
