# Specified Complexity - The Cloud and the Statue

## The Point
How can you *detect* whether an intelligence was at work? [[Jacob Hansen]], following mathematician [[William Dembski]], answers: not by complexity alone, but by **specified complexity** — "two independent patterns that match up."[^1] A thing can be wildly improbable ("complex") and still be the product of chance; what signals design is when that improbable arrangement *also* conforms to an *independently given* pattern. Hansen illustrates with clouds spelling "Hi, I'm God" and with rocks shaped into a human form (a statue), and argues that "our universe is rife with specified complexity," whose only known cause is intelligence.[^2] This is the video's compact statement of the design inference developed more fully in [[The Logic of Design Detection - Complexity Plus Specification]].

## Evidence
1. **Complexity alone is cheap.** "The probability of getting two clouds that are exactly alike is almost nothing" — clouds "vary in all sorts of subtle ways," so any particular cloud is "quote-unquote complex." The same is true of rocks: "you will never find two rocks that are the same."[^3] Improbability by itself, therefore, cannot be the mark of design.
2. **Specification is the added ingredient.** The "Hi, I'm God" cloud is striking because a cloud's pattern "conforms to an independent pattern" — in this case the English language. The statue is recognized as designed because "the pattern of the human form" *aligns with* the pattern of a rock, "combining in this really unique way."[^4] Two independent patterns matching is the signature.
3. **The archaeologist's everyday inference.** Hansen notes this is how an archaeologist reasons: knowing "the normal patterns that rocks have," they infer a statue when a rock matches the independent pattern of the human form — design detection is an ordinary, rational practice, not special pleading.[^4]
4. **The universe is "rife" with it.** Studying Dembski, Hansen concludes "there exists specified complexity in the universe," and "based on what we know the only known cause of high levels of specified complexity is some sort of an intelligence," citing DNA's coding sequences.[^2] See [[DNA as Digital Code - The Information Enigma]].
5. **Critics dodge the argument.** Hansen complains that people "allow [intelligent design] as a pseudoscience" but "never deal with the actual arguments" of [[William Dembski]] and [[Stephen C. Meyer]], whose reasoning he finds "very compelling."[^5]

## Best Quotes
> "It's not just that things are complex; it's that they have two independent patterns that match up." [^1]

> "There exists specified complexity in the universe, and... the only known cause of high levels of specified complexity is some sort of an intelligence." [^2]

> "When you find a pattern of rocks that conform to the pattern of a human form, you would say this is some sort of a statue... you have what's called specified complexity." [^4]

## LDS Connection
A cosmos "rife with specified complexity" best explained by intelligence fits LDS theism's ordered, purposeful Creator — the same intuition Alma presses when "the planets which move in their regular form do witness that there is a supreme creator" ([[Alma 30.44|Alma 30:44]]; see [[Fine-Tuning and the Teleological Argument from Alma 30]]). Keep it honest: Hansen (and Dembski) infer a *generic* designing intelligence, while the distinctive Restoration claim is that God *organized* eternal materials into ordered, information-bearing patterns rather than creating them from nothing. The inference to "a designing mind" is shared natural theology; the *identity and mode* of that mind is where LDS doctrine adds its own content.
