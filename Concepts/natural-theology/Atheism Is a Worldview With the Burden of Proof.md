# Atheism Is a Worldview With the Burden of Proof

## The Point
Turek rejects the now-fashionable claim that atheists "merely lack a belief in God" and therefore carry no burden of proof.[^1] A "true atheist," he argues, is "someone who believes there is no God," and atheism is "a worldview with beliefs just as much as theism is a worldview with beliefs."[^2] Most atheists are not merely skeptical of God; they are committed *materialists* who positively believe that only matter exists and try to explain all of reality without God.[^3] Because everyone "has the burden of proof to support his or her position," Turek prefers to debate not "Does God exist?" but "What better explains reality: atheism or theism?" — a framing that puts both sides on the hook to make a positive case.[^4]

## Evidence
Turek gives several arguments against the "lack of belief" dodge. (1) If atheism is just the *absence* of a belief, it is "a claim about the atheist's state of mind, not a claim about God's existence" — and "rocks, trees, and outhouses" would all count as atheists, since "it doesn't take any brains to 'lack a belief' in something."[^5] (2) Atheists don't behave like people who merely lack a belief: they "write book after book insisting that God is out of a job because of quantum theory, multiple universes, and evolution."[^6] (3) Quoting Phillip Johnson — "He who is a skeptic in one set of beliefs is a true believer in another set of beliefs" — Turek notes that lacking belief in God no more establishes materialism than lacking belief in atheism establishes Christianity.[^7] (4) He cites Bo Jinn's analogy: an anarchist may say he "rejects politics," but still faces "the inescapable problem of how human society is to organize itself"; likewise atheists still must explain ultimate reality.[^8] (5) Citing Richard Howe, he notes the definition is self-defeating because it makes "atheism logically compatible with theism" — atheism would be "true even if God really exists."[^9] The materialist's bill comes due across a long list — the beginning and fine-tuning of the universe, the laws of nature, logic, and mathematics, genetic information, life, mind and consciousness, free will, objective morality, and evil — and "it's rare to find an atheist attempting to explain more than one or two of these things materially."[^10] Turek stresses the stakes: "Does God exist?" is "literally life's most important question," since on it hangs whether anything ultimately matters.[^11]

## Best Quotes
> "A true atheist is someone who believes there is no God. And atheists have the burden of proof to show how materialism is true and reality can be explained without God." [^2]

> "if atheism is merely a lack of belief in God, then rocks, trees, and outhouses are all 'atheists' because they, too, lack a belief in God." [^5]

> "instead of debating 'Does God exist?' I prefer to debate the question 'What better explains reality: atheism or theism?'" [^4]

## LDS Connection
Latter-day Saint apologetics shares Turek's refusal to let naturalism pose as the neutral default. The Restoration affirms a long catalogue of realities that materialism struggles to ground — agency, intelligence, moral law, the rational order of the cosmos — and treats the existence and character of God as a genuine, investigable question rather than a settled non-issue (compare Alma 30, where Korihor's materialism is challenged as a positive, defensible worldview, not a mere absence of belief). The demand that the materialist make a positive case parallels the LDS conviction that "all things denote there is a God" (Alma 30:44; see [[Fine-Tuning and the Teleological Argument from Alma 30]]). The broader diagnosis that consistent atheism cannot fund free will, morality, or consciousness is shared territory (see [[Atheisms Four Bad Implications]], [[Free Will - Atheist Determinism vs LDS Co-Eternality]]). This note concerns method and burden of proof, where Turek and Restoration apologetics stand together with no significant divergence; doctrinal differences surface only later, when Turek specifies *which* God the evidence points to.
