# What the Official Statements on Evolution Actually Say and Deliberately Do Not

## The Point
The single most useful apologetic fact about Latter-day Saints and evolution is the **gap between what the official First Presidency statements affirm and what they pointedly decline to say**. The three formal sources — the 1909 "The Origin of Man," the 1925 "'Mormon' View of Evolution," and the 2016 *New Era* clarification — **unequivocally affirm one thing**: God created human beings in his own image, on purpose, as his literal children. They **deliberately refrain from saying anything about the mechanism** by which he did it. The Church's own 2016 wording is blunt: *"The Church has no official position on the theory of evolution... Nothing has been revealed concerning evolution."* So the critic who says "Mormonism rejects evolution" and the over-eager member who says "the Church teaches evolution" are **both wrong about the official position** — the *what* (God created on purpose) is fixed; the *how* (process, mechanism, timing) is open. [^1]

## Evidence
1. **The affirmation is about identity and purpose, not biology.** Both 1909 and 1925 hammer one revealed truth: *"All men and women are in the similitude of the universal Father and Mother, and are literally sons and daughters of Deity."* The point being defended is human **divine parentage and destiny**, not the absence of common descent. [^2]
2. **The 2016 statement draws the line explicitly.** *"Organic evolution... is a matter for scientific study. Nothing has been revealed concerning evolution. Though the details of what happened on earth before Adam and Eve, including how their bodies were created, have not been revealed, our teachings regarding man's origin are clear and come from revelation."* The revealed part (*we are children of God*) and the unrevealed part (*the process*) are cleanly separated. [^3]
3. **The Church's own history department says the same.** The "Organic Evolution" topic entry states that leaders "did not take an official stance on the theory of evolution, but they did take steps to clarify the Church's teachings related to human origins." Even the 1925 statement *"used the term 'evolve' in a positive sense,"* pointing to humans "evolving into a God." [^4]
4. **The companion 2016 dinosaurs entry confirms the principle.** Asked whether dinosaurs lived and died before man, the Church answers: *"There have been no revelations on this question, and the scientific evidence says yes."* The scripture accounts *"are not meant to provide a literal, scientific explanation of the specific processes, time periods, or events involved."* [^5]
5. **The summary from the editors.** Spackman: Church leaders "have considered scientific research a valid way to seek knowledge about human origins and have not excluded evolution as a means by which God created." The non-negotiable was only *"that God created people on purpose according to a plan."* [^6]

## Best Quotes
> "The Church has no official position on the theory of evolution. Organic evolution, or changes to species' inherited traits over time, is a matter for scientific study. Nothing has been revealed concerning evolution." [^3]

> "Though the details of what happened on earth before Adam and Eve, including how their bodies were created, have not been revealed, our teachings regarding man's origin are clear and come from revelation." [^3]

> "Church leaders tolerated the expression of a variety of strong and incompatible views, only maintaining as non-negotiable that God created people on purpose according to a plan." [^6]

> "There have been no revelations on this question, and the scientific evidence says yes." (on dinosaurs before man) [^5]

## LDS Connection
This is the cleanest illustration in the whole evolution debate of the Latter-day Saint distinction between **revealed doctrine** and **everything else**. The official position is not silence born of evasion; it is a *deliberate refusal to convert a scientific question into a doctrinal one*. That refusal is itself a teaching: as the Encyclopedia of Mormonism puts it, *"the scriptures tell why man was created, but they do not tell how."* It also leaves the door open to **[[Seven Seals and the Age of the Earth Show Continuing Revelation|continuing revelation]]** — the 2016 wording ("nothing *has been* revealed") is provisional, not a closed verdict. The apologetic move, then, is never to defend or attack evolution as Church doctrine, but to point to the actual statements and show that the Church has reserved the mechanism to *"scientific study"* while holding firm only to the revealed *what* and *why*. See the related [[Operation Science vs Origin Science|operation-vs-origin science]] distinction for why the *how* question is properly scientific.
