# Every Atheist Must Choose Between Being Happy or Consistent

## The Point

[[William Lane Craig]] frames the practical upshot of [[The Absurdity of Life Without God]] as a forced choice: "every atheist has to choose between being happy or being consistent." If you draw out atheism's implications consistently — no objective meaning, value, or purpose, and annihilation at death — you arrive at Russell's "foundation of despair," which is unlivable. So in practice atheists live *as if* their lives, choices, and loves carry real significance, which their worldview cannot underwrite. "You can tell the whole world you're an atheist, but you can't really live like one." [^1] The argument is existential rather than strictly deductive: it claims atheism is *unlivable when held consistently*, exposing a performative tension between the atheist's professed worldview and the meaning they cannot help acting upon.

## Evidence

- **The dilemma stated.** "Every atheist has to choose between being happy or being consistent. You can tell the whole world you're an atheist, but you can't really live like one." [^1]
- **The setup: despair is the consistent option.** The choice only bites because the consistent terminus has already been established — Russell's "firm foundation of despair," which Craig's interlocutor flatly refuses: "No thanks! I'd rather live a happy life." That refusal *is* the choice of happiness over consistency. [^2]
- **Theism removes the dilemma.** On Christianity "each one of us is here for a reason and life does not end at the grave and God… is the absolute standard of goodness… so your life ultimately does have objective meaning, value, and purpose. That means you can live a life that's both happy and consistent." [^3]
- **A modest, honest scope.** Craig concedes the limit of the argument: "that doesn't prove Christianity is true… Agreed. I'm simply pointing out that for Christians living a life that is both happy and consistent is possible; for atheists it's not." [^4] It is an argument about *livability*, not a proof of God's existence — which forecloses the objection that Craig has overclaimed.

## Best Quotes

> "Every atheist has to choose between being happy or being consistent. You can tell the whole world you're an atheist, but you can't really live like one." [^1]

> "For Christians, living a life that is both happy and consistent is possible. For atheists, it's not. So what are you going to choose?" [^4]

## LDS Connection

Craig's dilemma assumes a stark binary — happy *or* consistent — that Latter-day Saint theology resolves on the "happy and consistent" side with unusual force, because it makes happiness the very *object* of existence rather than a coping mechanism against despair. Joseph Smith taught that "happiness is the object and design of our existence," and the plan of salvation supplies the metaphysics that makes such happiness consistent: intentional creation, God as the standard of goodness, and eternal life beyond the grave. Lehi's teaching that "men are, that they might have joy" (2 Nephi 2:25) frames joy not as a defiant choice against a meaningless cosmos but as the coherent end toward which a God-ordered reality is aimed. Thus the consistent Latter-day Saint need not, like Craig's atheist, purchase happiness at the price of self-contradiction.
