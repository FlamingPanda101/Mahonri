# Nothing Has Been Revealed Concerning Evolution

## The Point
The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints has no official position on the theory of evolution. While 100% of prophets and apostles teach that God created on purpose as part of a divine plan, "there is no apostolic consensus" and "no explicit scriptural record detailing *how* God created."[^1] The most recent official statements are "emphatically neutral": organic evolution "is a matter for scientific study. Nothing has been revealed concerning evolution."[^2] This silence is not a gap to be embarrassed about — it is a space of genuine, faithful latitude in which a believer may accept the science of evolution without contradicting any revealed doctrine.[^3]

## Evidence
- The 2016 *New Era* states plainly: "The Church has no official position on the theory of evolution... Nothing has been revealed concerning evolution... The details of what happened on earth before Adam and Eve, including how their bodies were created, have not been revealed."[^2]
- A companion article (with a painting of a feathered dinosaur) says "the details of what happened on this planet before Adam and Eve aren't a huge doctrinal concern of ours."[^4]
- The 2022 official history *Saints* describes the early-20th-century "modernist" vs. "fundamentalist" controversies "without choosing sides," noting President Heber J. Grant "did not feel he had to 'tell people what to believe.'"[^5]
- The 1931 First Presidency told the General Authorities (who were themselves divided) to "leave geology, biology, archaeology, and anthropology, no one of which has to do with the salvation of the souls of mankind, to scientific research."[^6]
- Joshua Sears's formulation: "at this time, the official doctrine is that there is no official doctrine."[^7]

## Best Quotes
> "The Church has constantly maintained the revealed truth that God planned for the eternal life of people and therefore created us on purpose, while also maintaining that 'nothing has been revealed concerning evolution.'" [^3]

> "As far as the details of how that Creation was carried about, the Church has said it leaves that up to 'scientific study.'" [^8]

## LDS Connection
This is the load-bearing thesis of the BYU volume: between the firmly revealed truths (purposeful creation by God through the Son; Adam and Eve as first parents; a Fall requiring redemption through Christ) and the unrevealed mechanism, there is wide room.[^1] What *is* doctrine precludes some views — pure random chance, or that humans are no different from any other species — but it does not legislate the biological process.[^9] Sears anchors the point in the careful definition of doctrine: a single statement by a single leader is not binding doctrine, which is established only by scripture and the united voice of the First Presidency and Quorum of the Twelve consistently proclaimed in recent official publications. The many older anti-evolution statements by individual leaders, however sincere, do not meet that bar.[^10]
