# Genetic Evidence for Human Evolution - Shared DNA and Chromosome 2 Fusion

## The Point

The molecular record, [[Seth M. Bybee]] argues, is the most rapidly growing and "scientifically conclusive" body of evidence for human evolution. All life shares **DNA** built from the same four bases (A, C, T, G), read and expressed the same way; human and chimp/bonobo genomes are "nearly identical in sequence, distribution, and function." [^1] The apparent puzzle — apes have 24 chromosome pairs, humans 23 — is resolved by a specific, testable prediction: **human chromosome 2 is a fusion of two ancestral ape chromosomes**, and the molecular scars of that fusion are exactly where the theory says they should be. [^2] Genetics also records that modern humans **interbred with Neanderthals and Denisovans**. [^3]

## Evidence

- **Universal, identical genetic machinery.** All organisms use DNA with the same four bases; humans, apes, fruit flies, and even plants share genes so similar in sequence and function that animal clinical trials inform human medicine and cross-species diseases (e.g. COVID-19) can infect us. [^1]
- **Chromosome 2 fusion — the telomere test.** Chromosome ends (**telomeres**) are short repeats (TTAGGG-type) up to ~15,000 base pairs. If human chromosome 2 is two fused ape chromosomes, telomeric sequence should sit *in its middle* — and it does. [^4]
- **Chromosome 2 fusion — the centromere test.** Centromeres carry their own DNA signature; the merged human chromosome 2 contains **multiple centromeric regions**, again the predicted signature of a fusion event unique to genus *Homo*. [^5]
- **Inversions.** Portions of chromosomes 4, 5, and 18 are flipped relative to apes — reordering without changing the chromosome's overall genetics. [^6]
- **Archaic interbreeding (hybridization).** Most living humans carry **1.5–2.5% Neanderthal DNA**, plus Denisovan ancestry; these genes plausibly contributed lighter skin (for vitamin D at higher/lower latitudes) and immune adaptations after humans left Africa. [^3]
- **Phylogeny converges.** Trees built from whole genomes independently recover chimps/bonobos as our closest relatives and place fossil hominins (e.g. Neanderthals) near humans — matching the anatomical trees. [^7]

## Best Quotes

> "If human chromosome 2 is actually a fusion of two ape chromosomes, then there should be telomeric sequences in the middle of this chromosome. And indeed there are... it seems that at some point in the evolution of the greater apes, there was a chromosome fusion that is unique to species of the genus Homo." [^4]

> "Most modern-day humans share between 1.5 and 2.5 percent of their DNA with Neanderthals." [^8]

## LDS Connection

Bybee presents this molecular evidence as bearing on the **physical body** and its lineage, not on the existence or worth of the spirit. A Latter-day Saint can, on his framing, accept shared DNA, the chromosome 2 fusion, and archaic interbreeding as the genetic history of the body God assembled "from the dust," while holding that "nothing has been revealed concerning evolution" as to *how* God directed it (see [[A Body from the Physical Creation Joined to a Premortal Spirit]]). The genetic continuity humans share with all life echoes — at the level of the body — the LDS sense of kinship across creation, while the distinctively human spirit remains, for Bybee, the one thing genetics cannot capture. Questions about Adam and Eve he assigns to continuing revelation, not to the genome.
