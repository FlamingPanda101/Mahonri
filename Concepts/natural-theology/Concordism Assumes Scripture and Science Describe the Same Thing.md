# Concordism Assumes Scripture and Science Describe the Same Thing

## The Point

*Concordism* is the assumption that scripture and science must agree because they are describing the same thing — the natural world — so that Genesis (and 2 Nephi) "must match what physics, geology, biology, and other sciences reveal." [^1] Spackman names it as the first and most consequential of the three assumptions behind "no death before the Fall." Its trap is that, because concordists expect scripture and science to say the same thing, they end up either (1) forcing science to fit scripture (young-earth creationism), (2) forcing scripture to be modern science in disguise, or (3) rejecting scripture because it fails to match science. [^2] The striking illustration: both Ken Ham and Richard Dawkins are concordists — they agree Genesis must match science to be true, and disagree only on which one wins. [^3] Non-concordists hold that science-religion harmony is possible precisely "because scripture is not necessarily describing the natural world."

## Evidence

- Concordism "has been an extremely common one among Christians for the last few hundred years" and "has been the dominant assumption among Church members in nearly every Latter-day Saint attempt at reconciling scripture with science." [^4]
- Joseph Fielding Smith's concordism: "these theories [such as an old earth] are man-made deductions but the testimony of the prophets are actual facts" — Genesis and 2 Nephi provided "absolute scientific facts from God." [^4]
- Concordism's dominance "seems to be fading"; since 1954, scholars across traditions — inerrantist Evangelicals (John Walton), Catholics, Jews (Nahum Sarna), and Latter-day Saints — have rejected it. [^5]
- The chief reason for rejecting it is the rediscovery and translation of ancient Near Eastern texts, which show ancient creation accounts did not function as "scientific report" or "historical documentary." Concordism is "a modern imposition on ancient texts, not something native to them." [^6]

## Best Quotes

> "Concordism is the assumption that harmony between science and religion is possible because scripture and science are describing the same thing: the natural world." [^1]

> "Both Ham and Dawkins agree that to qualify as inspired and true, Genesis must match science. This is the diversity of concordism, and Latter-day Saints tend to fall into both extremes." [^3]

## LDS Connection

Spackman argues there are "no strong Latter-day Saint justifications for concordism"; it is "theologically unnecessary," and Church leaders have rejected it. [^7] Dropping concordism is what makes Elder Stephen L Richards's promise — that true science will never clash with correctly interpreted scripture — hold even on a non-concordist reading: "science and scripture will not clash because they are not necessarily describing the same phenomena." [^7]
