# Biblical Interpretation Has Always Renegotiated Under New Cosmologies

## The Point

The supposed "collision" between scripture and evolution is not new — it is the latest in a long series of collisions between biblical interpretation and improving cosmology, and in every prior case interpreters eventually renegotiated their reading of the text rather than the science. The primitive three-tiered cosmos gave way to Aristotelian geocentrism, which gave way to Copernican heliocentrism; each time, interpreters who had read scripture "plainly" (the earth does not move, Ps. 104:5; the sun stood still, Josh. 10:12) developed more sophisticated interpretive strategies. Greenwood's lesson: "eventually interpretations of scripture are subject to negotiation when confronted with compelling external evidence." [^1]

## Evidence

- Post-Aristotelian interpreters reread the "foundations of the earth" as a metaphor for God's sustaining power rather than literal pillars, and accommodated a spherical earth with antipodes. [^2]
- The Copernican revolution was resisted precisely because Aristotelian cosmology had been fused with Catholic (and Protestant) theology and anthropology; Galileo was forced to recant and was placed under house arrest. [^3]
- Scientific turns are usually refinements, not reversals: "We have not seen a reversal of Copernican cosmology, but a refinement of it" — just as evolutionary theory has been refined, not overturned, since 1859. [^4]
- Modern interpreters learned they "must rely on more than just their sense of what the text means" — considering original culture, language, rhetoric, idiom, and genre. [^4]

## Best Quotes

> "If we have learned anything from cosmological history, it is that eventually interpretations of scripture are subject to negotiation when confronted with compelling external evidence." [^1]

> "Not all collisions are destructive. Some collisions are constructive, resulting in fusion. The church is at its best when it recognizes that all truth is God's truth . . . Scripture and science complement, rather than contradict, each other." [^5]

## LDS Connection

The pattern fits the Restoration's commitment to continuing revelation and to reading scripture "as far as it is translated correctly" (A of F 1:8). It also parallels [[Retroactive Reinterpretation of Creation Texts|the documented way doctrines and readings shift]] as new contexts are recovered, and supports treating no single interpretation of the creation mechanism as fixed doctrine.
