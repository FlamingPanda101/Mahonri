# The Waiting-Time Problem and the Cambrian Explosion

## The Point
Darwinian evolution is gradual and step-by-step, yet in the **Cambrian explosion** (~half a billion years ago) a large number of distinct animal **body plans** — unique configurations of body parts and tissues — appear "very abruptly in the fossil record without discernible connection to earlier precursors."[^1] Darwin himself knew this and called it inexplicable on his view, hoping future fossil finds would fill the gap.[^2] Instead, the window has *narrowed* with better dating to roughly 5-10 million years.[^3] The problem is not only geological but mathematical: **population genetics** lets us calculate expected "waiting times" for evolutionary change given mutation rates, generation times, and population sizes — and those waiting times run to "hundreds of millions or billions of years," so even 70 million years is "a blink of an eye."[^4]

## Evidence
1. **Abrupt appearance, no precursors.** In the Cambrian "you get a huge number of... animal body plans... they arrive very abruptly in the fossil record without discernible connection to earlier precursors or earlier ancestors in the pre-Cambrian record."[^1] In one Chinese site, "13 to 16 different major groups of animals" arise in "a five to six million year window."[^3]
2. **Darwin saw the problem.** "It was a problem that even Darwin was aware of, and he wrote about it in the Origin of Species. He said it was inexplicable on his view of life."[^2]
3. **The waiting-time calculation.** Population genetics "allows us to calculate how much evolutionary change we ought to expect in a given amount of time," and "the expected waiting times for the amount of change that's evident in the Cambrian blow out the time scale... to hundreds of millions or billions of years."[^4] So the event is "really unexpected... both biologically, mathematically, and geologically on a Darwinian view of things."[^5]
4. **Information, not just form.** "The Cambrian explosion is an explosion of biological form, but it's also an explosion of biological information," which reframes the puzzle in terms of where new information could come from.[^6] See [[New Biological Form Requires New Information]].

## Best Quotes
> "They arrive very abruptly in the fossil record without discernible connection to earlier precursors." (Meyer) [^1]

> "Five, ten, even 70 million years is a blink of an eye in terms of... the expected waiting times for the amount of change that's evident in the Cambrian." (Meyer) [^4]

> "It's incredibly abrupt geologically... it's also very abrupt biologically." (Meyer) [^5]

## LDS Connection
The waiting-time problem is an argument about the *sufficiency of the Darwinian mechanism within deep time*, not a young-earth claim — the panel explicitly works with an earth "four and a half billion years" old.[^5] Latter-day Saint thought has no fixed stance on evolutionary mechanism and readily accommodates a long cosmic history and an organizing God who works through ordered processes. The apologetic value here is narrow and honest: if the abrupt arrival of new body plans outpaces what unguided mutation and selection can plausibly produce, that gap is more naturally filled by direction than by chance.
