# God Is Not a Deceiver - The Evidence for Evolution Is Real

## The Point
[[T. Heath Ogden]] offers a distinctively theological argument for taking the evidence of evolution at face value: **God is not a deceiver.** "He understands and lives in perfect harmony with natural laws," so it is "hard to accept the idea that kind, loving heavenly parents would leave entire pathways of evidence in the fossils, morphology, and molecules that all point toward evolution when in reality, Creation occurred in an entirely different way."[^1] Coupled with this is his claim that evolution is no longer tentative: it "is now considered settled science, similar to other ideas like gravity, the structure of an atom, plate tectonics, and germs as the cause of many diseases," resting on a scientific consensus built over a century of testing.[^2]

## Evidence
1. **Settled by consensus, not assumption.** From the 1930s–1960s, population genetics, the modern synthesis, and molecular biology each independently corroborated evolution; fossils and molecular data over the last fifty years "further solidif[y] evolution as the best explanation for the diversity of life." Hence "serious and honest examination... leads to the scientific consensus."[^2]
2. **Genetic similarity tracks relatedness.** Humans share ~98% of genes with chimpanzees, ~92% with mice, ~44% with fruit flies, ~26% with yeast — similarity decreasing with evolutionary distance, exactly the predicted pattern.[^3]
3. **Human chromosome 2 fusion.** Humans have 46 chromosomes; other great apes have 48. Losing a whole pair is lethal, so that cannot explain the difference. Instead, human chromosome 2 matches *two* shorter ape chromosomes (2p + 2q) fused end-to-end — same genes, same order, confirmed by G-banding. "Because I believe that God is not a deceiver, I doubt that he would make our DNA... look like it evolved if it didn't."[^4]
4. **Mitochondria and endosymbiosis.** The mitochondrial genome is more similar to certain *bacteria* than to our own nuclear DNA — best explained by endosymbiotic theory (a bacterium engulfed ~1.5 billion years ago became the mitochondrion; the same explains chloroplasts). "Convincing evidence for evolution."[^5]
5. **Reconciling human evolution with sonship.** Ogden accepts that *Homo sapiens* share a common ancestor with chimps ~7–8 million years ago while affirming "man is the literal offspring of God" — because divine sonship rests on God being "the father of my spirit," not the biological maker of the body. "How Adam's body was created is unknown."[^6]

## Best Quotes
> "Because I believe that God is not a deceiver, I doubt that he would make our DNA (and the pattern of DNA in all organisms) look like it evolved if it didn't." [^4]

> "It is now considered settled science, similar to other ideas like gravity, the structure of an atom, plate tectonics, and germs as the cause of many diseases." [^2]

> "God is the ultimate scientist and evolutionary biologist." [^7]

## LDS Connection
The "God is not a deceiver" argument turns a perceived faith problem into a faith *resource*: trusting the plain testimony of the created order is an act of confidence in an honest God, not a concession to secularism. Ogden's separation of *body* (evolved) from *spirit* (begotten of God) preserves the doctrine of divine sonship intact while accepting the genetic evidence in full — and the Church's packet leaves "how Adam's body was created" open, affirming only the *why*. Ogden pictures God as "at the helm of the creative process," intervening as He wills (D&C 88:78–79; D&C 93:24, "things as they are"). This is the evidential backbone beneath [[Accepting Evolution with Joy Is Possible]]; the broader, non-molecular evidence is gathered in [[The Evidence for Common Descent]], and the boundary that keeps this from touching God or ultimate origins is set in [[What Evolution Is and Is Not - Descent with Modification]].
