# Reason Presupposes Emotion - The Neuroscience Reply to Materialism

## The Point

Against the secular-materialist charge that spiritual experience is untrustworthy because it is "mere emotion," [[Blake Ostler]] turns the neuroscience back on the objector: emotion is **not opposed to reason but is a necessary substrate of it**. Citing [[Antonio Damasio]] and [[Oliver Sacks]], he argues that without properly functioning emotion, rational practical decision-making collapses — so "trust your emotion, because you can't trust your reason unless you do." [^1]

## Argument

1. **The materialist argument.** Reliance on spiritual experience is "simply irrational" because only publicly available, replicable, scientific-method evidence is a trustworthy basis for knowledge. [^2]
2. **Counterexample — private but rational.** Ostler "had a thought about buying a hamburger three minutes ago." That thought is not public, not replicable — yet it would be *insane* to doubt he had it. So not all rational knowledge is public/replicable; introspective experience can be trustworthy. Spiritual experience is then like "having an extra sense." [^2]
3. **All reasoning rests on un-provable givens.** Even formal logic and mathematics cannot proceed without axioms, formation/transference rules, and rules of inference that "cannot be proven by the same rigorous reasoning." These are justified only as basic givens that "just seem right" — so intuition undergirds even the most rigorous reasoning. [^3]
4. **The empirical case (Damasio, Sacks).** Laboratory and clinical studies show persons "lacking properly functioning emotions are incapable of... fully cognize and... making rational decisions in cases involving their own welfare." Practical rationality depends on neural subsystems tied to emotional processing (amygdala, limbic system; tonically active neurons in the caudate integrate prefrontal-cortex reasoning with amygdala emotional input). Without functioning emotion "the decision-making landscape is flat" — no reason to prefer any goal over another. [^4]
5. **Conclusion — Plato was wrong.** The picture from Plato's *Republic* Book X, that emotion is *at war with* reason, "just isn't true." Reason *presupposes and requires* emotion; therefore dismissing spiritual experience as untrustworthy because affective is self-undermining. [^5]

Ostler also independently challenges materialism: it struggles to explain qualia and undercuts the reliability of reason itself, since evolution fitted us "for surviving," not for quantum physics and abstract mathematics. [^6] He treats Bob McHugh's appeal to a "God spot" in the brain as a case study — noting the God-spot theory "has been disproven... never replicated." [^7]

## Best Quotes
> "Reason presupposes and requires emotion, feeling and affect. In fact, anybody who studies the brain will see that we can't reason at all without it." [^5]

> "Trust your emotion, because you can't trust your reason unless you do so." [^5]

> "I had a thought about buying a hamburger three minutes ago... this thought is not available as public evidence, it's not replicable, but I would be insane to doubt that I actually had this thought." [^2]
