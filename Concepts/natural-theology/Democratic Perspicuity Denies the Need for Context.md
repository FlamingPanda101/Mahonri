# Democratic Perspicuity Denies the Need for Context

## The Point

*Perspicuity* is the Reformation claim that scripture is plain and clear — originally, clear enough on what is necessary for salvation that any reader could be saved without church, priest, or scholar. In 1800s America this narrowed claim was inflated into *democratic perspicuity*: the idea that "any reasonably intelligent person [could] read the Bible in his or her own language and correctly understand the plain meaning of the text" on *every* topic. [^1] This is the second assumption behind "no death before the Fall." Its error, in Spackman's glasses metaphor, is that it "denies that modern readers are wearing different glasses than ancient Israelites," presuming we can read translated scripture at face value and arrive at the ancient author's meaning. [^1] A face-value reading is *not* a literal reading, because a truly literal reading "requires active work to recover those contexts that scriptural authors did not include because they could go without being said." [^2]

## Evidence

- Joseph Fielding Smith "argued that scripture was so plain that no interpretive or contextual work was needed; you could only misunderstand scripture if you willfully denied the plain meaning or lacked the Spirit." [^3]
- By contrast, Talmage and Widtsoe held that understanding scripture "required careful attention to languages and translation, history, culture, and genre," and that leaders "could misunderstand scripture just as they could misunderstand science." [^3]
- Joshua Sears's analogy: "Captain America may have beat the Nazis . . . but he's no Captain Moroni" makes sense only to American Latter-day Saints sharing the cultural references; readers 3,000 years later would miss the implicit contexts — illustrating the implicit, "goes-without-being-said" context all communication assumes. [^4]
- Widtsoe (quoting Brigham Young) urged reading scripture "as though you were writing them a thousand, two thousand, or five thousand years ago" — the opposite of face-value reading. [^5]

## Best Quotes

> "Democratic perspicuity denies that modern readers are wearing different glasses than ancient Israelites, and so presumes that modern readers can simply read translated scripture at face value and yet come away with the same understanding as ancient Israelites." [^1]

> "A face-value reading is not a literal reading, because face-value reading does not seek out context." [^2]

## LDS Connection

Perspicuity and "plain reading" are what let Joseph Fielding Smith deny he was interpreting at all — and thus treat his reading of 2 Nephi as beyond question. [^6] Recovering the distinction between a *face-value* and a genuinely *literal* (context-seeking) reading reopens [[No Death Before the Fall Is a Twentieth-Century Interpretation Not Settled Doctrine|the creation/Fall questions]] and harmonizes with the Restoration call to read scripture as its ancient authors did. Compare the Protestant doctrine in [[The Light Manifests Itself - Perspicuity of Scripture|its original, salvation-limited form]].
