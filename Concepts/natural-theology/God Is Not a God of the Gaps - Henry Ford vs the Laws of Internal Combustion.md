# God Is Not a God of the Gaps - Henry Ford vs the Laws of Internal Combustion

## The Point
A common atheist assumption is that God merely "plugs gaps" in our knowledge and will be retired once natural processes are found. Turek answers with John Lennox's illustration: ask whether a Ford motorcar is explained by Henry Ford *or* by the laws of internal combustion. The answer is obviously *both* — an agent explains the car's origin, and natural laws explain its operation.[^1] No matter how much we learn about how the engine works, "the need for a designing engineer will never change."[^2] Likewise, learning more about natural law can never disprove the Being who set up and sustains it: "To say that a scientist can disprove the existence of God is like saying a mechanic can disprove the existence of Henry Ford."[^2] God is required not to fill gaps but to explain why a rationally intelligible, law-governed universe exists and persists at all.[^3]

## Evidence
1. **Origin causes and operation causes don't compete.** Lennox notes that when Newton discovered universal gravitation he did not conclude "therefore there is no agent God who designed it"; rather, understanding the mechanism increased his admiration for its Designer.[^4] "The existence of secondary operational causes does not negate the need for a primary origin cause."[^2]
2. **God sustains, not just starts.** The illustration is good but incomplete: a built car can run without Henry Ford, but the universe and its consistent laws "require a sustaining cause, not just a first cause. God is to the universe as a band is to music."[^5]
3. **Methodological naturalism overreaches.** The atheist "rule" of admitting only natural causes ("the rule"), applied universally, yields absurdities: geologists would have to say natural forces (not sculptors) carved Mount Rushmore, archaeologists that nature (not humans) made arrowheads, detectives that no one was murdered.[^6] By the **principle of uniformity** — that present causes resemble past ones — we infer intelligent causes for the Rosetta Stone and, Turek argues, for the genetic code, "only... if we don't rule out intelligent causes in advance."[^7] Even Richard Dawkins concedes "biology is the study of complicated things that give the appearance of having been designed"; his materialism, by his own admission to Phillip Johnson, philosophically stacks the deck against a Designer.[^8]
4. **The real science-stoppers.** The charge that intelligent design is a "science stopper" is backwards. ID theorists predicted function in the noncoding ("junk") DNA regions; Darwinists who dismissed 98% of the genome as useless leftover "stopped investigating," while a 2012 *Nature* consortium reported "biochemical functions for 80% of the genome."[^9] "Since the Darwinists had ruled out intelligence from the get-go, they stopped doing science. In that case, they were the real science stoppers."[^10]
5. **Not an argument from ignorance.** Design proponents "are not merely plugging in God because they haven't found a natural cause"; they cite "positive evidence that intelligence was involved (like a three-billion-letter genome written in the language of the genetic code)."[^11] Turek's summary: "God is not a 'God of the gaps.'... God is required to explain why nature — with its consistent natural laws and goal-directed processes — continues to exist at all, and why our minds can discover and measure this rationally intelligible universe."[^3]

## Best Quotes
> "To say that a scientist can disprove the existence of God is like saying a mechanic can disprove the existence of Henry Ford. It doesn't follow. The existence of secondary operational causes does not negate the need for a primary origin cause." [^2]

> "God is not a 'God of the gaps.' He isn't an imaginary being that we invent to explain gaps in our knowledge about nature. God is required to explain why nature... continues to exist at all, and why our minds can discover and measure this rationally intelligible universe." [^3]

## LDS Connection
The "God who sustains," not merely "God who started," is congenial to Restoration theology, in which a divine light actively upholds and governs the cosmos: it is "the light which is in all things, which giveth life to all things, which is the law by which all things are governed" ([[D&C 88|D&C 88:6–13]]), and God's glory is the very "intelligence... light and truth" pervading reality ([[D&C 93|D&C 93:36]]). That a mind underlies a discoverable, law-ordered universe is precisely Alma's point that "all things denote there is a God... [the] planets which move in their regular form do witness that there is a Supreme Creator" ([[Alma 30.44|Alma 30:44]]; see [[Fine-Tuning and the Teleological Argument from Alma 30]]). A useful LDS caveat: Latter-day Saint thought generally frames God as organizing and sustaining a cosmos of eternal law and matter rather than creating it ex nihilo, so the "primary cause" is understood as the supreme Organizer-Sustainer. Note too that the gaps caution cuts both ways within LDS apologetics — see [[Miracle-Tests for God Commit the God-of-the-Gaps Fallacy]].
