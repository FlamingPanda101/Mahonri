# Divine Images Explain Jesus Relationship to God

## The Claim
[[Dan McClellan]] proposes that the biblical authors' actual model for Jesus' relationship to God is the **"intuitive logic of divine images"** — not the later philosophical Trinity. In the ancient world it was ubiquitous knowledge that there were "material media, either inanimate objects and sometimes animate objects, that bore the presence and the power and the authority of gods who were located elsewhere." [^1] Jesus, on this model, is a person who can exercise God's authority and power and "even claim to manifest God's very presence" — while remaining a distinct person from the Father (A ≠ B). [^2] He develops this in his open-access monograph *YHWH's Divine Images*.

## Supporting Evidence

1. **"To see me is to see the Father" = manifestation, not identity.** In [[John 14.9|John 14:9]] Jesus tells Philip that seeing him is seeing the Father — McClellan stresses "not because Jesus is the Father, remember A does not equal B, but because Jesus bears the Father's presence and manifests it." [^2] *Rating: a genuinely viable reading of the seeing-language; competes with the Trinitarian reading rather than refuting it outright.*

2. **Thomas's confession fits the divine-image pattern.** In [[John 20.28|John 20:28]] Thomas "sees that Jesus is manifesting God's presence and so exclaims 'my Lord and my God.'" [^3] *Rating: coherent within the framework; this verse is the hardest case for any non-identity Christology, and McClellan's reading is contested.* *Steelman of the orthodox side:* the most natural plain-sense reading of *ho theos mou* addressed directly to Jesus is a confession of his deity, and the Gospel's inclusio with the Logos-is-God prologue (John 1:1) frames it that way.

3. **Divine images were a real, pervasive ancient institution.** Cult statues and other consecrated media throughout the ancient Near East were treated as bearing a deity's presence and could be addressed as the deity. *Rating: strong, well-attested in ANE studies — this is the empirical anchor of the model.*

4. **It resolves the apparent contradiction without redefining "person."** Where the Trinity must invent novel senses of *person* and *substance* (see [[Trinity Is Fundamentally Contradictory]]), divine mediation explains both Jesus' unity with and distinction from God using categories the original audiences already held. *Rating: this is the proposed advantage; its force depends on accepting that the relevant texts stop short of identity.*
