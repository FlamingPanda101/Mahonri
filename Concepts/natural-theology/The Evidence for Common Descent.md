# The Evidence for Common Descent

## The Point
[[Tyler A. Kummer]] and [[Jamie L. Jensen]] assemble the converging lines of evidence that "a shared common ancestry for life on earth" is "the best scientific explanation" for biological diversity.[^1] The case does not rest on any single observation but on independent data sets — anatomy, embryology, the fossil record, vestiges, and shared molecular "errors" — all pointing the same direction.

## Evidence
1. **Homology (comparative anatomy).** A human arm, a bat wing, and a whale flipper differ in function yet share the same underlying skeletal pattern — "one bone... two bones... a group of bones and phalanges." Best explained by inheritance from a common tetrapod ancestor.[^1]
2. **Embryology.** Diverse vertebrate embryos share structures — "a large, unfolded brain, an eye, four limb buds, and a tail of some kind" — even when the adult (e.g., a tailless human) loses them. Why grow a tail only to reabsorb it? Common ancestry.[^2]
3. **Transitional fossils (a tested prediction).** Common ancestry *predicts* intermediates. *Tiktaalik* — "the head and limb pattern of a tetrapod but the body of a fish" — was found by searching rocks of the right age and environment in the Canadian Arctic; *Archaeopteryx* shows dinosaur features (teeth, bony tail, fingers) with feathered wings.[^3]
4. **Vestiges and atavisms.** The human arrector pili ("goose bumps"), coccyx, and appendix make sense as inherited leftovers; humans occasionally born with tails carry silenced genes for a structure present in ancestors.[^4]
5. **Shared "errors" = common source (the plagiarism analogy).** Just as a misspelled answer copied verbatim, or a deliberate trap-error in a map, reveals a copyist, identical molecular signatures across species betray inheritance from a common ancestor. The whale case: *Pakicetus* fossils were reclassified as whale ancestors once they were found to carry the whale-unique tympanic bulla/involucrum, knitting modern whales to land mammals.[^5]

## Best Quotes
> "When we see the exact same 'mistake' or change in two very different organisms, it most likely indicates a common source." [^5]

> "When we compare the developmental paths of various organisms, we see numerous shared structures and patterns that have no reason to exist unless they were inherited from a common ancestor." [^2]

## LDS Connection
The strength of this evidence is precisely what motivates [[T. Heath Ogden]]'s "God is not a deceiver" principle: kind, honest heavenly parents would not "leave entire pathways of evidence in the fossils, morphology, and molecules that all point toward evolution" if creation happened some entirely different way (see [[God Is Not a Deceiver - The Evidence for Evolution Is Real]]). For the Latter-day Saint, reading these "two books" — nature and revelation — as mutually truthful is native to the tradition: "All truth is part of the gospel of Jesus Christ" (Russell M. Nelson). The data describe the *means* of creation; they leave the Creator and purpose untouched (see [[What Evolution Is and Is Not - Descent with Modification]]). [[Charles Darwin]] first read this pattern in domesticated pigeons and Galápagos finches.
