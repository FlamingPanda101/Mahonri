# Genesis Is Theology Not Science - Sears's Reconciliation Journey

## The Point
Old Testament scholar Joshua M. Sears recounts moving from a freshman who bracketed evolution as "This is false — learn it well enough for the test but don't buy into it!" to a religion professor "much more comfortable with the idea of evolution today than I was as a biology student."[^1] He attributes the shift to three factors: (1) a better grasp of how official doctrine is defined, (2) study of what Genesis does and does not actually claim, and (3) a growing comfort with ambiguity and unresolved questions.[^2] The crux is that Genesis is not a modern scientific treatise: it is a theological text answering ancient Israelites' spiritual questions, and it even deliberately employs an inaccurate ancient Near Eastern cosmology as a vehicle for spiritual truth.[^3]

## Evidence
- Sears's earlier reading found Genesis "clearly opposed" to evolution ("after his kind," "there was not a man," "surely die"), but thirteen further years of Old Testament study convinced him "the clarity I once saw in Genesis was an illusion."[^4]
- Genesis is theological, not scientific: it pushes back against neighboring cultures' false ideas (many quarreling gods; creation as a battle against chaos; gods who don't care about humans) by teaching one Creator God who simply commands "Let there be!" and makes humans the purpose of Creation.[^5]
- The **accommodation** model of revelation: God spoke "unto my servants in their weakness, after the manner of their language, that they might come to understanding" (D&C 1:24). Genesis adopts the ancient flat-earth/firmament cosmology — "wrong, but... still useful" — just as high-school chemistry teaches the obsolete Bohr atom because it "gets the job done for beginners."[^6]
- On doctrine: "Not every statement made by a Church leader... necessarily constitutes doctrine"; doctrine is what the united First Presidency and Quorum of the Twelve teach consistently in recent publications. Most anti-evolution statements Sears found "were made before I was born."[^7]
- The **two-maps analogy**: a political (state-border) map and a topographical map of the same U.S. territory look incompatible because they answer different questions — as may the Fall (scripture) and evolution (science).[^8]

## Best Quotes
> "Genesis was not written to answer my modern questions about the age of the earth, or dinosaurs, or Adam's relationship to other hominids. The ancient Israelites would not have even understood those questions." [^9]

> "I no longer take my inability to explain how this would work as sure evidence it couldn't work." [^10]

> "However the world was created, I know who my Creator is, and however God's children got here, I know we can all return home someday." [^11]

## LDS Connection
Sears embodies the volume's thesis in autobiography: once he understood that "the official doctrine is that there is no official doctrine" on evolution, the perceived scriptural barrier fell away.[^12] His accommodation theory of revelation — that inspired text can carry culturally limited, even inaccurate, models in service of spiritual truth — gives Latter-day Saints a principled way to read Genesis without treating it as geology or biology. He freely lists deep gospel doctrines he cannot explain mechanically (the Resurrection, how prayer works, how the Atonement works) and concludes it is "perfectly all right to say, 'I do not know'" (M. Russell Ballard), making intellectual humility a feature, not a failure, of faith.[^13]
