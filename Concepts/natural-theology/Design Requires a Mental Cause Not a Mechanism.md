# Design Requires a Mental Cause Not a Mechanism

## The Point
A live disagreement runs through the panel: critics demand that intelligent design propose a *materialistic mechanism*, and [[Stephen C. Meyer]] argues this demand is question-begging. When ID invokes a mind, it is "invoking a non-material cause," and "we don't need to play by the rules of scientific materialism to invent a mechanism to explain the origin of information."[^1] We know minds produce information from "uniform and repeated experience," even though we cannot specify the mind–body interface — Meyer can choose to move a water glass without knowing how his volition moves matter, yet the *effect* (information) reliably traces back to a mind.[^2] [[Michael Behe]] adds that science routinely accepts causes whose mechanism is unknown: Newton said of gravity *hypotheses non fingo* ("I feign no hypothesis"), and no one knows the mechanism of the Big Bang or radioactive decay, yet "we see patterns and we can deduce explanations."[^3]

## Evidence
1. **A mental cause, not a mechanistic one.** Demanding ID "formulate its alternative explanation as a mechanistic cause" smuggles in materialism; ID proposes "a different kind of cause, a mental cause, which is something we recognize from our ordinary experience."[^4]
2. **Information always traces to a mind.** "Whenever we see information and we trace it back to its source — whether… computer code or a hieroglyphic inscription or a paragraph in a book or… a radio signal — we always come back to a mind."[^2]
3. **Newton accepted causes without mechanisms.** *Hipotesi non fingo*; "What's the mechanism for the Big Bang? What's the mechanism for radioactive decay?" Patterns license inference even absent a mechanism.[^3]
4. **Behe's "subtle God" is a tool, not a mechanism.** Behe's quoted line — a "subtle God" could design "without overriding natural law" by influencing undetectable quantum events (e.g., radioactive decay) — is meant as "a tool… wielded by a model [mind]," not a Darwinian mechanism; Meyer still demurs, preferring to leave the interface unexplained.[^5]
5. **ID infers design, not the designer.** Behe: "after we conclude design, I do not infer to God"; the inference reads "a purposeful arrangement of parts" (mousetrap, outboard motor, the cell's machines), and stops at intelligence — some, he notes, even credit space aliens.[^6]

## Best Quotes
> "We don't need to play by the rules of scientific materialism to invent a mechanism to explain the origin of information… information always arises from a mind." [^1]

> "There's lots of things that happen people don't know what the mechanism is, but we see patterns and we can deduce explanations from the patterns." (Behe) [^3]

## LDS Connection
The "mental cause" framing fits a Latter-day Saint metaphysics in which mind is not reducible to matter — spirit is a refined kind of "matter" and intelligence is uncreated (compare [[Atheism Undercuts the Rationality Science Requires]] and [[The Mind Is Not the Brain]]). The caution cuts the other way too: because ID deliberately infers only a generic designer ("I do not infer to God"), it cannot by itself deliver the embodied, *organizing* God of the Restoration who works within and upon eternal matter; the design inference is a doorway, not the destination. For the demarcation debate this presupposes, see [[Intelligent Design as a Scientific Inference]].
