# The Multiverse Cannot Escape Prior Fine-Tuning

## The Point
Meyer takes the leading materialist alternative to a cosmic fine-tuner — the multiverse — and argues it fails on its own terms. To make a life-permitting universe probable, multiverse advocates must posit not only a vast ensemble of other universes but also a *universe-generating mechanism* to produce them.[^1] But every such mechanism (inflationary cosmology, string theory) itself requires fine-tuning, so the proposal merely pushes the question back: it explains fine-tuning by invoking prior, unexplained fine-tuning.[^2]

## Evidence
1. **Why a generating mechanism is required.** Multiverse proponents treat the other universes as causally isolated, so nothing in one affects the probabilities in another. To raise the odds of a life-friendly universe they must therefore postulate a common underlying mechanism that "spins" out universes with varied parameters — a "cosmic lottery" or "giant slot machine."[^3]
2. **The mechanism smuggles fine-tuning back in.** "The speculative cosmologies (such as inflationary cosmology and string theory) they propose for generating alternative universes invariably invoke mechanisms that themselves require fine-tuning, thus begging the question as to the origin of that prior fine-tuning."[^4]
3. **A general pattern, not a one-off.** Meyer generalizes: "all the various materialistic explanations for the origin of the fine-tuning... invariably invoke prior unexplained fine-tuning."[^5]
4. **So design wins by inference to the best explanation.** Since the multiverse cannot explain fine-tuning without prior fine-tuning, and since tuning a system toward a propitious end is characteristic of intelligent agents, "intelligent design stands as the best explanation for the fine-tuning of the universe."[^6]

## Best Quotes
> "The speculative cosmologies they propose for generating alternative universes invariably invoke mechanisms that themselves require fine-tuning, thus begging the question as to the origin of that prior fine-tuning."[^4]

> "Because the multiverse theory cannot explain fine-tuning without invoking prior fine-tuning... it follows that intelligent design stands as the best explanation for the fine-tuning of the universe."[^6]
