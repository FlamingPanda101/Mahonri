# Divine Accommodation Means Revelation Meets Humans in Their Context

## The Point

Because God's ways and thoughts are higher than ours (Isa. 55:8–9), He "come[s] down to human level and accommodate[s] revelation to the human condition, communicating to humans in a way they could understand in their time, place, and context" (cf. D&C 1:24; 2 Ne. 31:3). [^1] This principle of *accommodation* is the alternative to inerrancy: it means "even direct revelation might be partial or incomplete," and might "temporarily adapt or tolerate human ideas or culture that weren't eternally true." [^1] Jesus himself teaches it in Matthew 19:8, explaining that Moses permitted divorce "because you were so hard-hearted" — a divine accommodation, not an eternal ideal. Revelation is therefore "God's word in human words," not divine dictation of timeless facts.

## Evidence

- Galileo invoked accommodation, writing that the Holy Ghost teaches "how one goes to heaven, not how the heavens go" — explaining why inspired biblical authors did not know the earth circles the sun. [^2]
- The Book of Mormon title page concedes it may contain "the mistakes of men"; the Bible was "handed down, recopied, modified, and translated," what Joseph Smith called being "translated incorrectly." [^3]
- Robert J. Matthews on the JST: it "was not a simple, mechanical recording of divine dictum, but rather a study-and-thought process accompanied and prompted by revelation." [^4]
- Steven Harper: Joseph "knew better than anyone else that the words he dictated were both human and divine, the voice of God clothed in the words of his own limited, early American English vocabulary" — and so felt free to revise his revelations. Grant Underwood concurs that the revelations "were not understood as infallible texts written in stone by the finger of God." [^5]

## Best Quotes

> "God had to come down to human level and accommodate revelation to the human condition, communicating to humans in a way they could understand in their time, place, and context." [^1]

> "Jesus thus implies that revelation did not entail eternal correctness; divorce was a divine accommodation to the hard-heartedness of humans." [^1]

## LDS Connection

Accommodation is the deep reason scripture can carry [[Scripture Reflects Ancient Cosmology Not Modern Science|ancient cosmology]] without error in its true purpose: God spoke to ancient Israel "according to their language, unto their understanding" (2 Ne. 31:3). It pairs with a Restoration view of [[God Works Through Fallible Prophets|fallible prophets]] and an [[Editing Revealed Scripture Is Compatible with Divine Origin|unfolding, revisable revelation]], dissolving the demand that inspired texts deliver modern science.
