# Music Conveys Transcendence Better Explained by Theism

## The Claim
[[Gavin Ortlund]] offers music as an **abductive** argument for God — an inference to the best explanation, not a deductive proof. The basic observation: music conveys meaning and transcendence "in this very mysterious way," and it does so *non-representationally* — "unlike language, the words don't refer... the music doesn't refer to specific objects, but music can convey meaning, music can convey sadness, it can convey jubilation," and "it conveys transcendence."[^1] Almost everyone grants there are times "when you're listening to music when it at least feels significant, it feels important in some way."[^2] The argument then asks: which worldview better accounts for that experience, "both emotionally but also intellectually"? Ortlund's claim is that theism — on which music is, in the Christian tradition, "the first language" by which "God created the world" — supplies richer explanatory tools than naturalism, which is "stuck with the evolutionary mechanisms" and faces "greater mystery and greater uncertainty as to where does it get this transcendence."[^3] This is the same explain-the-experience-better strategy as the design inferences in [[Fine-Tuning - Why the Universe Is Best Explained by a Mind]], applied to aesthetics rather than cosmology.

## Supporting Evidence (steelman)
- **The philosophers of music are genuinely puzzled.** Ortlund's entry point is that "all of the top philosophers of music are puzzling over the question" of how music conveys meaning — so the dismissive skeptic is invited to "go read the philosophers of music and... tell me you're smarter than all these people who have no idea how does music convey meaning."[^4] The argument leans on a real, unresolved question in aesthetics, not a manufactured gap.
- **Non-representational meaning is the crux.** Language refers to objects; music does not, yet music still communicates sadness, jubilation, and transcendence. That a medium with no referential semantics nonetheless carries determinate emotional and transcendent content is the datum theism is offered to explain.[^1]
- **A deep Christian tradition treats music as primary.** Ortlund marshals [[Thomas Aquinas]], "this whole Christian tradition of thinking about music as the first language," Peter Kreeft ("music is the original language"), and the imaginative witness of Tolkien and Lewis — "I share from Lord of the Rings, Chronicles of Narnia."[^3] On this view music is "the language by which God created the world," giving theism a built-in account of why music feels like contact with something beyond nature. (Tolkien's *Silmarillion* literally opens with creation-by-music.)
- **The naturalist alternative is left uncertain.** Ortlund's contrast is not that evolution explains *nothing*, but that "you just go through the explanations within evolutionary psychology for music and there's so many different ones of them and people are just uncertain, they just don't know why is music so powerful."[^5] The abductive claim is comparative: theism is the better explanation, not the only conceivable one.
- **Modest by design — the "back door."** Ortlund stresses he is "very adverse to triumphalist approach to apologetics" and deliberately under-promises: "I'm not proving God, but I'm saying he's a better explanation." He "show[s] them the back door — if you want to reject this argument, here's how you can do so," so the skeptic doesn't "feel like the deer in the headlights" but is invited to weigh worldviews.[^6] Fradd notes this disarms the "inner skeptic" that recoils from airtight arguments.[^7] The same modest, invitational posture is the method described in [[The Charitable-but-Critical Believer Posture]].

## Best Quotes
> "Music conveys transcendence and meaning in this very mysterious way... it conveys meaning in a non-representational manner. So unlike language, the words don't refer... but music can convey meaning, music can convey sadness, it can convey jubilation." [^1]

> "Go read the philosophers of music and... tell me you're smarter than all these people who have no idea how does music convey meaning." [^4]

> "I'm not proving God, but I'm saying he's a better explanation... I give this skeptic, I show them the back door. If you want to reject this argument, here's how you can do so." [^6]
