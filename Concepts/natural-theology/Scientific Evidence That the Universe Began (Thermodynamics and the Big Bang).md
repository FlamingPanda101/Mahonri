# Scientific Evidence That the Universe Began (Thermodynamics and the Big Bang)

## The Point
The Kalam argument's controversial step is its second premise — *the universe began to exist*. Atheists "have typically said that the universe has been here forever... The universe is just there, and that's all."[^1] Against this, Craig's video offers two independent **scientific** lines of evidence (distinct from the purely philosophical arguments against an infinite past — for which see [[Hilbert's Hotel - Why an Actual Infinite Cannot Exist]] and [[An Actual Infinite Cannot Be Formed by Successive Addition]]): the **second law of thermodynamics** and the **Big Bang discovery chain**. Both converge on the same verdict — the cosmos has a finite past. (The third and most general line, the BGV theorem, is treated separately in [[Borde-Guth-Vilenkin Theorem - Any Expanding Universe Had a Beginning]].)

## Evidence
1. **The thermodynamic argument.** The second law of thermodynamics "tells us the universe is slowly running out of usable energy." But "if the universe had been here forever it would have run out of usable energy by now." Since usable energy remains (the universe has not reached equilibrium / heat death), the past cannot be infinite: "The second law points us to a universe that has a definite beginning."[^2]
2. **Einstein's general relativity (1915).** Einstein's general theory of relativity "allowed us for the first time to talk meaningfully about the past history of the universe" — it made cosmic history a subject of rigorous physics rather than speculation.[^3]
3. **The Friedmann–Lemaître expansion prediction.** Working independently from Einstein's field equations, Alexander Friedmann and Georges Lemaître "predicted that the universe is expanding" — a theoretical result derived *before* the observational confirmation.[^3]
4. **Hubble's redshift (1929).** Edwin Hubble "measured the red shift and light from distant galaxies," empirically confirming "not only that the universe is expanding, but that it sprang into being from a single point in the finite past" — "a monumental discovery almost beyond comprehension."[^4]
5. **Alternative models failed.** Because "not everyone is fond of a finite universe," rival eternal-universe models were proposed, but "one by one, these models failed to stand the test of time."[^5] This sets up the BGV theorem as the general result that closes the remaining escapes.

## Best Quotes
> "If the universe had been here forever it would have run out of usable energy by now. The second law points us to a universe that has a definite beginning." [^2]

> "This empirical evidence confirmed not only that the universe is expanding, but that it sprang into being from a single point in the finite past." [^4]

## LDS Connection
These scientific results are a strong *ally against atheism* for a Latter-day Saint: they refute the picture of a self-existing, beginningless cosmos and point to a cause beyond brute matter. The familiar caveat applies to the *interpretation*, not the data. The video glosses the Big Bang as the universe "springing into being from a single point" and elsewhere as creation from nothing — but the empirical findings (energy degradation, expansion, an initial high-density state) establish a **finite past for this expanding spacetime**, not the philosophical thesis of creation **ex nihilo** (being from non-being), which LDS theology rejects. Restoration scripture teaches that God *organized* eternal, uncreated matter and intelligences: "the elements are eternal" and "intelligence... was not created or made, neither indeed can be" (D&C 93:29; cf. [[Abraham 3.25|Abraham 3:25]], [[Intelligence Is Uncreated and Eternally Has Agency]]). A Latter-day Saint can therefore embrace the thermodynamic and Big Bang evidence as confirming the beginning of *this* organized cosmos while reading "beginning" as God's organizing act rather than the absolute origination of being from nothing (see [[Creation Ex Nihilo Is Not Biblical]]).
