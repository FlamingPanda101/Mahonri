# One Mind Dilemma Against Two Natures

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)

The two-natures defense of Jesus' ignorance: "human Jesus" was limited in knowledge while "divine Jesus" had all knowledge. [^1]

## The Rebuttal

1. **Count the minds.** How many minds did Jesus have? If *two*, he is two persons, not one (two centers of consciousness = two subjects). So he must have *one* mind. [^2]
2. **The fork on the single mind.** Does that one mind contain all knowledge or not? If *yes*, Jesus is not fully human (humans have limited knowledge). If *no*, Jesus is not fully divine (divinity, in classical theism, entails omniscience). You must choose; both "fully" claims cannot stand. (Evidence: a clean dilemma *given* one-mind Christology and the classical equation of divinity with omniscience.) [^2]
3. **Scripture sides with limited knowledge.** [[Matthew 24.36|Matthew 24:36]] / [[Mark 13.32|Mark 13:32]]: "of that day and hour knoweth no man, no, not the angels of heaven, *neither the Son*, but the Father." Jesus genuinely did not know the hour — only the Father did. The text asserts limitation, not a nature-indexed sleight. (Evidence: strong prooftext; the "neither the Son" of Mark is explicit.) [^3]
4. **Steelman.** The orthodox tradition answers in two ways. (a) Some hold Jesus had *two minds/wills* (dyothelitism, affirmed at Constantinople III) — a divine and a human consciousness — and deny that two minds entail two persons, since personhood is constituted by the hypostasis, not by counting minds. (b) Others say the one person knew the hour in his divine nature but, by the *kenosis*, did not access it in his human consciousness. (Evidence: dyothelitism is the conciliar position, so premise 1's "one mind" is not forced on the orthodox — though it raises its own unity-of-consciousness problems.) The LDS reply: two minds is exactly the "two Jesuses" the objection exposes, dressed in conciliar vocabulary; and "knew but didn't access" is a distinction the text shows no awareness of. [^4]

## Best Quotes

> "How many minds did Jesus have? He had one mind. Because guess what, if you have two minds, are you one person or you two? You're two people." [^2]

> "Does that mind have all knowledge in it, or does it not? If it does, then Jesus is not fully human. ... or he doesn't, then he can't be fully divine. You have to choose." [^2]

> "No man knoweth the hour, not even the angels, nor the Son of Man ... only the Father in heaven knows." [^3]
