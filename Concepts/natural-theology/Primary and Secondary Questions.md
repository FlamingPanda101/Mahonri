# Primary and Secondary Questions

## The Point
Not every gospel question carries equal weight. Borrowing a distinction from Elder Lawrence E. Corbridge, Jared Lee argues that **primary questions** concern our "basic testimonies" — God, Christ, the Restoration — "and there are relatively few of them," while **secondary questions** "are endless and rarely relate to saving ordinances and covenants."[^1] Evolution is a *secondary* question. Triaging questions this way is the discipline that lets a believer sit with unresolved secondary issues without endangering the foundation — and it guards against the failure mode of "let[ting] secondary questions distract us from primary questions."[^2]

## Evidence
- The first few temple-recommend interview questions are offered as a model of *primary* questions; uncertainty clusters in secondary questions far more than in primary ones.[^1]
- **Evolution is officially secondary.** Amid the 1931 B. H. Roberts / Joseph Fielding Smith debate, the First Presidency wrote: "Leave geology, biology, archaeology, and anthropology, no one of which has to do with the salvation of the soul of mankind, to scientific research, while we magnify our calling in the realm of the Church." Lee reads this as direct evidence that evolution "is not a fundamental doctrine (primary question)."[^3] See [[Nothing Has Been Revealed Concerning Evolution]].
- Secondary questions may simply stay open: "How did God create the earth? How do dinosaurs fit into the plan of salvation? Did other hominids develop religion...?" — all secondary, and the scientific evidence around them "cannot be discarded just because the Church of Jesus Christ's theology is silent."[^2]
- A warning against overreach: treating "the English translation of Genesis 1 and 2 as a refutation of the evidence for human evolution is poor gospel scholarship" — we must "not assert or overinterpret doctrine as settling a question."[^2]

## Best Quotes
> "Primary questions deal with our basic testimonies, and there are relatively few of them. Secondary questions are endless and rarely relate to saving ordinances and covenants." [^1]

> "Leave geology, biology, archaeology, and anthropology, no one of which has to do with the salvation of the soul of mankind, to scientific research, while we magnify our calling in the realm of the Church." [^3]

> "We cannot let secondary questions distract us from primary questions." [^2]

## LDS Connection
The primary/secondary frame is a practical antidote to faith crises that begin with a peripheral issue and metastasize. A great deal of apologetic energy is spent on secondary questions (Book of Mormon geography, the mechanism of creation, the timing of the Resurrection); Corbridge's and Lee's point is that none of these can topple a testimony anchored in the primary questions — unless we *let* them by misranking their importance. This pairs with the counsel to [[Diagnose the Struggle - Moral Disagreement vs Intellectual Doubt|diagnose what is actually driving a struggle]] before treating it, and with the broader skill of [[Living With Uncertainty Is a Faith Skill|living with uncertainty]] on the questions revelation has left open.
