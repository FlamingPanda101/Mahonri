# CRIMES - The Intellectual Crimes of Atheism

## The Point
Turek organizes the whole book around the acrostic **CRIMES**, where each letter names one or more aspects of reality that (he argues) "wouldn't exist if atheism were true," yet which atheists routinely invoke to argue against God.[^1] Because stealing is a crime — "especially stealing from God!" — the acronym frames the New Atheist case as a series of intellectual thefts:[^1]

- **C = Causality**
- **R = Reason**
- **I = Information and Intentionality**
- **M = Morality**
- **E = Evil**
- **S = Science**[^2]

Turek concedes it "may seem odd to cite reason, evil, and science as aspects of reality stolen from God since atheists trumpet them as evidence against God," but contends the ensuing chapters show "why reason, evil, and science wouldn't exist unless God existed."[^3]

## Evidence
The acrostic doubles as the book's chapter-by-chapter structure; each CRIME is treated in order.[^4] Turek's larger claim is bolder than "theism is true" — he argues the foundational assumptions of atheism "make it impossible to make a sound intellectual case for atheism. If atheism is true, there's no way to know it with any confidence. In fact, if atheism is true, there's no way to know anything with any confidence."[^4] That self-undermining quality is why he says it is "more certain that atheism is false than Christianity is true."[^5] He uses a construction metaphor to justify the inspector's-eye approach: a building inspector need not master every trade to spot a fatal flaw in the foundation, and "if there were a fatal flaw in the foundation, it wouldn't matter how good the workmanship was above it—the entire structure would soon collapse."[^6] The most common foundational flaw he flags is a self-exempting violation of the law of noncontradiction: atheists "put forth a theory about reality that is supposed to be universal, but then they exempt themselves from it."[^7] Turek cites atheist philosopher Thomas Nagel's *Mind and Cosmos* — subtitled *Why the Materialist Neo-Darwinian Conception of Nature Is Almost Certainly False* — as a sign that even within the academy materialism is cracking.[^8]

## Best Quotes
> "Since stealing is a crime (especially stealing from God!), this book will use CRIMES as an acrostic to show the scope of the intellectual crimes atheists are committing." [^1]

> "If atheism is true, there's no way to know it with any confidence. In fact, if atheism is true, there's no way to know anything with any confidence." [^4]

> "Atheism is like a house with fatal flaws in its foundation... With a flawed foundation, the entire superstructure of atheism comes crashing down." [^9]

## LDS Connection
Most of the CRIMES are congenial to Restoration thought. Latter-day Saints affirm an intelligible, law-governed cosmos (the "S" — Science), the reality of objective morality and a moral Lawgiver (the "M" — Morality; see [[Moral Argument Against Atheism]]), genuine agency and reason as more than chemistry (the "R"; see [[Free Will - Atheist Determinism vs LDS Co-Eternality]]), and design legible in nature (Alma 30:44; see [[Fine-Tuning and the Teleological Argument from Alma 30]]). The acrostic's overall verdict — that atheistic materialism cannot account for the most basic features of experience — is one an LDS apologist can endorse.

The honest divergence is concentrated in the first CRIME, **Causality**. Turek's causality argument moves from "the universe began" to a *creation ex nihilo* by a timeless, immaterial, immutable, simple God — a classical-theist package. Restoration theology rejects creation from nothing in favor of God organizing eternally existing matter and intelligences (Abraham 3:18–22; D&C 93:29; cf. [[God Was Once a Man]], [[Spirit Birth and Intelligences]]). So a Latter-day Saint can wield CRIMES as a cumulative case against atheism while reading the Causality chapter critically rather than adopting its ex-nihilo metaphysics wholesale.
