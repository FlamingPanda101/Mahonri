# Methodological Physicalism Is Not Ontological Physicalism

## The Point
A key move in [[Steven L. Peck]]'s defense of science is the distinction between two very different claims that share a word.[^1] **Methodological physicalism** is a *working assumption*: in order to proceed, science studies only "the material, repeatable, objective aspects of the world" and looks for causes "involving matter and its associated fields." **Ontological physicalism** is a *metaphysical creed*: the claim that "material forces are the only kind of influences that there are — everything that does or can exist is only matter in motion." Science *requires* the first; it does **not** entail the second.[^1] Conflating the two is the engine behind the false notion that accepting science means accepting atheism.

## Evidence
1. **Science brackets God; it does not deny Him.** "If there are divine or genuinely miraculous influences, then they cannot be investigated by science and indeed should not be." God, angels, blessings, and grace are "outside its domain of concern," not shown to be unreal.[^2]
2. **The mechanic analogy.** Latter-day Saints already *use* methodological physicalism daily: a customer expects her mechanic to explain a dead car in terms of "pistons, carbonators, belts" — not "interstitial elves from the land of Fantomia." Demanding natural explanations *within* the garage says nothing about whether God exists outside it.[^3]
3. **A caveat against the god-of-the-gaps reflex — in both directions.** Peck warns that when a physical cause is not "readily at hand," patience and humility are needed before declaring the matter supernatural *or* exotic. The interstellar object ʻOumuamua was prematurely called an "alien spacecraft" by Avi Loeb; patient analysis later explained its behavior as a nitrogen-ice fragment of a Pluto-like body. The mystery yielded to ordinary science "without resorting to extraordinary and unlikely claims."[^4]
4. **Non-overlapping magisteria.** Peck invokes Stephen J. Gould's framing: science and religion are "often focused on different concerns," so a finding in one domain need not collide with a truth in the other.[^5]

## Best Quotes
> "Do not confuse methodological physicalism with ontological physicalism, however... Methodological physicalism, in contrast, just uses physicalism as a working assumption to allow science to proceed." [^1]

> "This does not mean that things like God, angels, divine interventions, blessings, or the grace we find in our relationship with God are not real. It just means science is not an appropriate way to study these things. They are outside its domain of concern." [^2]

## LDS Connection
This distinction is the single most important defuser of the "evolution leads to atheism" fear. Science's silence about God is a *self-imposed limit of method*, not a verdict of metaphysics — so a Latter-day Saint can accept evolutionary biology wholesale while affirming a Creator, revelation, and grace, all of which simply lie outside the laboratory's reach. The Restoration's doctrine that truth comes "from whatever source" assumes exactly this: a fossil or a DNA analysis and a prophetic revelation are different windows on the one reality D&C 93:24 calls "things as they are." The error to avoid is letting the *method* of science be smuggled in as a *worldview* — the very overreach exposed in [[Scientism Cannot Describe or Explain All Reality]].
