# A Body from the Physical Creation Joined to a Premortal Spirit

## The Point

After surveying the scientific evidence, [[Seth M. Bybee]] offers his personal reconciliation: the one feature that uniquely makes us human is **not** any anatomical, genetic, or behavioral trait — art, language, tools, fire, empathy, cooperation, and reason all appear to **predate modern humans** and exist among our relatives — but the **divine spirit**. [^1] On his model the spirit was "created in the premortal creation and placed in a body derived from the physical creation," and the union of those two creations — "divine events carried out by God himself (see Gen. 2:7 and Moses 2:27)" — produced beings who can become like God. [^2] Humans are thus, at the level of the body, products of evolution *and*, at the level of the spirit, purposeful divine creations. The "how" of the body's making is left open; the existence of Adam and Eve is left to revelation.

## Evidence

- **No trait is uniquely human.** Asked "what makes humans human?", rigorously established anthropology suggests every candidate trait — art, large brains, clothing, diet, fire, language, religion, social structure, tools, upright walking, even empathy, cooperation, and reason — "may have originated before the evolution of modern humans" and is seen among close relatives. [^3]
- **The uniquely human thing is the spirit.** Bybee's "personal opinion is that the one unique feature that makes us human is our divine spirit," created premortally and placed in a body from the physical creation. [^2]
- **Evolved attributes predate Adam by design.** The attributes "that foster spirituality and enable us to become godlike" were "a necessary part of God's creative process," so "the evolution of these attributes would predate Adam and be seen among other creations" (see Moses 2:27). [^4]
- **The natural man as the test of mortality.** Drives "encoded in our physical bodies by natural selection over billions of years" — above all the desire to live and reproduce — are the "natural man" (Mosiah 3:19); the test of life is to overcome these undesirable biological attributes "in a single human lifetime" through covenant and the restored gospel. [^5]
- **Science is silent on God, Adam, and worth.** The evidence "does not and cannot comment directly on the existence of God, the actual existence of Adam and Eve, our place before God, or how we personally view the Creation"; it speaks only "from the agnostic vantage point of the scientific method." [^6]

## Best Quotes

> "My personal opinion is that the one unique feature that makes us human is our divine spirit. That spirit was created in the premortal creation and placed in a body derived from the physical creation. The combination of those two creations—divine events carried out by God himself (see Gen. 2:7 and Moses 2:27)—resulted in an opportunity for us all to master the natural man." [^2]

> "Fear does not come from the Lord... We can draw on personal revelation throughout our life to calm our anxieties until the full light and knowledge of the gospel, including the exact origins of mankind, are shown to us in great detail." [^7]

## LDS Connection

This is the chapter's reconciliation: humans were created **on purpose and in God's image** (Moses 2:27) **and** descended with modification — the body from the physical creation, the spirit from the premortal one, with the *mechanism* left open because "nothing has been revealed concerning evolution." The framing turns the evolved "natural man" into the very thing mortality is designed to overcome (see [[The Dual Potential of Human Nature as Moral Test]]), and reads the strongest evolved drives as a test rather than a defeater of the gospel. It dovetails with the argument that a non-random evolutionary process points toward family and meaning (see [[Evolution Points to Family as the Locus of Meaning]]). On the disputed questions — a literal Adam and Eve, "no death before the Fall," our standing before God — Bybee counsels patience and continuing revelation (D&C 101:32–34), refusing to let either science or anxiety force a premature answer. [^7]
