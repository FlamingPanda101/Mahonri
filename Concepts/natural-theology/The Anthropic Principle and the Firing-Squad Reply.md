# The Anthropic Principle and the Firing-Squad Reply

## The Point
One stock atheist response to fine-tuning is the *anthropic principle*: the universe must permit observers, because if it didn't, no one would be here to notice — so no further explanation of the life-permitting constants is needed. Hansen rejects this as a non-explanation, using the classic firing-squad analogy: a prisoner faced by 50 Navy SEALs who all miss cannot shrug, "well, I'm still here, so no further explanation is necessary." The very fact that we survive to observe is "what makes the whole thing so mysterious," not what dissolves the mystery.[^1] He buttresses the point by noting even Richard Dawkins "rejects this sort of reasoning" that the universe simply had to be this way, and argues all the science shows the universe "didn't have to be this way" — its constants "emerged from a point in the past."[^2] Seth's underlying position is the mirror image: he leans toward necessity or selection, holding that "it's possible that the way reality is, things just necessarily occur."[^3]

## Evidence
1. **The anthropic move stated and rejected.** "It's to say that our universe couldn't have been any other way because if it were different we wouldn't be here to observe it — but that's silly; it's the fact that we are here observing it that makes the whole thing so mysterious."[^1]
2. **The firing-squad analogy.** A man before 50 Navy SEALs who all miss "and they say, well, I'm still here so no further explanation is necessary... do you see how silly this is?" Survival is the thing demanding explanation, not a license to stop asking.[^1]
3. **Dawkins as a hostile witness.** "Even Richard Dawkins rejects this sort of reasoning, that oh the universe just had to be this way... all of the science points to the fact that the universe didn't have to be this way; it emerged from a point in the... past."[^2] This connects to the contingency theme in [[Maybe the Universe Itself Is the Necessary Being]].
4. **Seth's necessity counter.** Seth grants he doesn't fully "go that direction," but offers necessity as live: a deck where "every single card... was the same card" yields the same draw "by necessity," so "it's possible that the way reality is, things just necessarily occur." Hansen replies that asserting the universe "had to be this way" is itself "absurd" without evidence — like claiming a life-prohibiting universe is impossible.[^4]

## Best Quotes
> "It's the fact that we are here observing it that makes the whole thing so mysterious." (Hansen) [^1]

> "Imagine a person is standing in front of a firing squad of 50 Navy Seals, and when they all fire they all miss... and they say, well, I'm still here so no further explanation is necessary." (Hansen) [^1]

> "It's possible that the way reality is, things just necessarily occur." (Seth) [^3]

## LDS Connection
The exchange is general natural theology, but the contingency intuition Hansen presses — that the universe "didn't have to be this way" and so calls for an explanation beyond itself — is congenial to the LDS picture of a cosmos *organized* by God rather than self-explaining. The necessity option Seth floats is the same one examined in [[Maybe the Universe Itself Is the Necessary Being]] and [[Why the Necessary Being Must Be God Not the Universe]].
