# No Death Before the Fall Is a Twentieth-Century Interpretation, Not Settled Doctrine

## The Point

The reading that there was "no death before the Fall" (NDBF) — no death of any kind anywhere before c. 4000 BC, grounded in 2 Nephi 2:22 — is not settled Latter-day Saint doctrine. It is the product of three particular interpretive assumptions (concordism, perspicuity, inerrancy) advanced especially by Elder Joseph Fielding Smith and his son-in-law Bruce R. McConkie, and *rejected* by Elders Talmage and Widtsoe, Presidents J. Reuben Clark and David O. McKay, and multiple First Presidencies. Spackman traces this history to show "that a significant number of Church leaders and approved publications have rejected the idea that 2 Nephi establishes no death of any kind before 4000 BC as either core doctrine or scientific fact." [^1] The 1931 First Presidency explicitly ruled that the Church "had no doctrine one way or the other on death before the Fall." [^2]

## Evidence

- **1930–31 debate:** In the Smith vs. B. H. Roberts controversy, the First Presidency rejected *both* Roberts's book *and* Smith's assumptions, instructing General Authorities to "leave geology, biology, archaeology and anthropology . . . to scientific research." [^2]
- **Talmage, "Earth and Man" (1931):** Commissioned by the First Presidency, the geologist-Apostle taught an old earth and that fossils "lived and died, age after age, while the earth was yet unfit for human habitation"; it was vetted, approved, and widely republished (10,000+ pamphlets, plus British and German magazines). [^3]
- **J. Reuben Clark:** Held that death operated in Eden (plants, perhaps animals) and told Smith, "I am no more bound by your interpretation . . . than you are by mine." [^4]
- **David O. McKay:** Approved pro-evolution articles (e.g., "The Relatedness of Living Things"), praised an evolutionary book in the 1963 general conference, and reprinted "Earth and Man." [^5]
- **Later official materials:** Though the 1979 Bible Dictionary reflected Smith's view, it carried a disclaimer that it is "not . . . an official or revealed endorsement"; later *Ensign* and *New Era* articles affirmed that dinosaurs "lived and died" over millions of years and that "there have been no revelations on this question." [^6]

## Best Quotes

> "A significant number of Church leaders and approved publications have rejected the idea that 2 Nephi establishes no death of any kind before 4000 BC as either core doctrine or scientific fact." [^1]

> "The Church, said the First Presidency, had no doctrine one way or the other on death before the Fall (nor on preadamites)." [^2]

## LDS Connection

NDBF is a textbook case of mistaking a *recent interpretation* for *settled doctrine*. The history shows several First Presidencies treating the age of the earth and death before the Fall as open scientific questions, not gospel essentials — exactly the space the [[Interpretive Assumptions Are the Glasses We Read Scripture Through|assumptions we bring to scripture]] either open or close. Recognizing that [[God Works Through Fallible Prophets|prophets can hold differing personal views absent clear revelation]] (per Brigham Young) lets faithful members accept the scientific evidence for an ancient, death-filled earth without abandoning scripture.
