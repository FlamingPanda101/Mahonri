# Unthinkable Without Christianity vs Blossoming Thesis

## The Claim

There are two distinct strengths of the Christianization thesis, and the distinction is load-bearing. [[Tom Holland]]'s version (especially in 2019) treats Western developments as a **"blossoming"** of an inherent Christianity. [[Glenn Scrivener]] adopts a deliberately **more modest** formulation: those developments "are **unthinkable without** Christianity and have drawn on profoundly Christian intuitions" — without claiming everything good is *itself* Christian.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Scrivener explicitly disowns "blossoming."** He says Holland "would more own the word blossoming," while "I would make a slightly more modest claim... that those developments were unthinkable without Christianity."[^1] He prefers "ripple effects" to "blossoming."[^2] *(Genuine and important: it lets Scrivener treat abortion/euthanasia as post-Christian deviations rather than Christian blossomings — see [[Abortion and Euthanasia as Pagan Not Christian]].)*

2. **The two claims have different burdens of proof.** "Influenced by" (modest) is nearly uncontroversial; "unthinkable without" (counterfactual necessity) is a much stronger historical claim. O'Connor targets precisely the stronger one: "when you say these developments are unthinkable without Christianity, then it does become relevant that important parallels... crop up independently of Christianity."[^3] *(Logically sharp — the strength Scrivener needs for the thesis to be interesting is exactly the strength O'Connor attacks.)*

3. **Steelman of Scrivener:** "Unthinkable without" is a claim about *historical contingency*, not logical impossibility — it says that, given how history actually ran, these specific Western forms required the Christian pathway, even if some abstract analog could in principle arise elsewhere. This is why he later resists [[The Falsifiability Demand Against the Air-We-Breathe Thesis|the falsifiability demand]]: counterfactual history is untestable.[^4] *(Coherent, but O'Connor's rejoinder is that "untestable" cuts both ways — it makes the thesis unfalsifiable rather than confirmed.)*

4. **Both agree Holland has hardened over time.** Scrivener and O'Connor concur Holland "has become more specifically Christianized," moving "more of a line than a circle"; the *Dominion* opening (a "proclamation of the cross") was written last and placed first.[^2] *(Anecdotal but telling about the thesis's trajectory.)*

## Best Quotes

> "I would make a slightly more modest claim... those developments were unthinkable without Christianity and have drawn on some profoundly Christian intuitions."[^1]

> "I think 2019 Tom Holland would say blossoming. I would say ripple effects that... is unthinkable without — with Christianity as the stone that drops into the pond."[^2]
