# 100 Percent Rational Certainty Is Essentially Impossible

## The Point
Drawing on [[René Descartes]]'s methodic doubt, [[Jacob Hansen]] argues that 100% rational certainty "is essentially impossible and is not the standard by which we say that we know something." [^1] The only thing one can be absolutely certain of is that the thinking self exists; everything else is open to doubt. Therefore demanding absolute certainty before claiming knowledge sets an unreachable — and mistaken — bar.

## Argument
1. Descartes, "a fierce rationalist," tried to find which of his beliefs he "could be one hundred percent rationally certain of." [^2]
2. He found the senses — "even our eyes" — and reason itself "are not 100% reliable." [^3]
3. The single exception is the existence of the thinker: "you actually cannot ever have 100 rational certainty about anything except for the fact that you the entity doing the thinking actually exist" — *I think therefore I am*. [^3]
4. Radical skeptical scenarios cannot be ruled out: one "can't ever be 100 sure" that reality is not "some kind of simulation" or that "the universe was actually created five seconds ago" with memories pre-programmed in — the *Matrix* / *Truman Show* point. [^4]
5. Conclusion: since absolute certainty is unavailable for nearly everything, it "is not what we are describing when we say that we know something." Knowledge claims must therefore mean something other than certainty. [^5]

## Best Quotes
> "He came to realize that all of our senses even our eyes and our reason are not 100 reliable. He discovered that you actually cannot ever have 100 rational certainty about anything except for the fact that you the entity doing the thinking actually exist — this is where his famous line I think therefore I am came from." [^3]

> "You can't ever be 100 sure that everything that you think is real is not actually some kind of simulation or… that the universe was actually created five seconds ago and that all your memories were programmed into you by some Creator." [^4]

> "100 rational certainty is essentially impossible and is not the standard by which we say that we know something." [^5]
