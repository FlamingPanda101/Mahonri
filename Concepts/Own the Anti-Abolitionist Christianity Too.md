# Own the Anti-Abolitionist Christianity Too

## The Claim (Objection)

[[Alex OConnor|Alex O'Connor]]: "If you want to own the Christian element of the abolitionist movement, you have to also own the Christian element of the anti-abolitionist movement, which was just as strong." Both sides quoted the same God and the same Bible.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Symmetry of appeal.** In America, "the issue was settled with physical violence. If the other side had won, you'd have a just-as-Christian nation appealing to exactly the same God and the same Bible."[^1] *(Strong — Southern pro-slavery theology was abundant and biblically argued; the Bible was cited on both sides.)*

2. **Pro-slavery proof-texts.** A slaveholder could quote [[Exodus 21.20-21|Exodus 21]] (the beaten slave who survives "a day or two" — "he is his money") and [[Leviticus 25.44-46|Leviticus 25]] (buy foreign slaves as heritable property). O'Connor walks Scrivener through Ex 21 verse by verse.[^2] *(Strong — these texts genuinely supported pro-slavery readings; see [[Hebrew Bible Endorses Chattel Slavery of Foreigners]].)*

3. **Steelman of Scrivener — "not the same Bible."** Scrivener's rebuttal: the slaveholders "actually did what [[Marcion]] proposed" — they used the **Slave Bible**, excising ~90% of the OT and ~50% of the NT. "Those slave owners were terrified of the *whole* Bible." So the abolitionists had Scripture; the enslavers needed to mutilate it. See [[The Slave Bible Confirms the OT Was Dangerous to Slaveholders]].[^3] *(Strong and historically grounded — the Slave Bible is real. But O'Connor notes it was a curated *compilation*, not an edited Bible, and that what was cut (Exodus liberation) doesn't show the *un*cut parts oppose slavery — Ex 21 and Lev 25 remain in the full Bible and support slavery.)*

4. **The reversibility logic.** This is an instance of O'Connor's broader [[The Reversibility Test for Bad Arguments|reversibility]] move: if a text "caused" abolition, the same text "caused" its defense; one cannot bank the wins and disown the losses.[^4] *(Methodologically sharp; Scrivener accepts "sure" that Christians were "on the wrong side quite a lot" — see [[Only Christianity Reached the Moon - The NASA Analogy]].)*
