# Light of Christ

## Overview

The **Light of Christ** is the Latter-day Saint doctrine of a divine influence given to every person who comes into the world — the inner light that enlightens conscience, prompts toward good, and prepares a person to receive the fuller gift of the Holy Ghost. It is distinct from the gift of the Holy Ghost (conferred by ordinance) and is universal rather than covenantal. This is an overview note for the referenced topic.

## The Claim

In this vault the doctrine does two kinds of work:

1. **It softens the [[Great Apostasy]].** Because the Light of Christ is universal and never withdrawn, the centuries before the Restoration were not a moral void: [[Medieval Christians Kept the Light of Christ Alive Not Apostates|medieval Christians kept the Light of Christ alive]], living faithfully by the light they had even while priesthood keys were absent. The apostasy is an institutional/authority loss, not a total extinction of grace or goodness.
2. **It reframes "conscience."** What Protestants experience and appeal to as [[Conscience Is the Protestants True Ultimate Authority|conscience]] can be read as the Light of Christ operating short of the fuller covenant gifts — a universal endowment that all people share.

The doctrine also informs diagnostic tests like [[The Negative Distance Test for a Broken Theology]], where a theology that drives a person *away* from the good they sense by that inner light is suspect.
