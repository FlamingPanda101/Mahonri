# The Essence-Energies Distinction Distinguishes Orthodoxy From Pantheism

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Christian Mario]]'s one constructive Eastern Orthodox move in the debate is the **essence/energies distinction**, deployed to escape [[Jacob Hansen]]'s charge that "God is reality itself" collapses into pantheism. Mario: "everything that truly exists receives its being from the one whose being is uncreated. That's not pantheism. **In pantheism God and the world share one essence. In orthodoxy there is a gulf between the uncreated essence of God and the created essence of everything else. And so we participate in God through his energies. That's the essence energy distinction.**" The distinction lets Mario affirm both that God is the unconditioned ground from whom all being derives *and* that the world is genuinely other than God (created essence) — creatures commune with God's uncreated **energies** (operations/grace) without ever sharing or comprehending his unknowable **essence**.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence
**Steelman (Orthodox / Palamite side).** (1) *Solves the pantheism dilemma.* The objection "if God is ultimate reality, isn't the world God?" assumes God relates to creation in only one mode. The Palamite distinction supplies two: by *essence* God is utterly transcendent and incommunicable; by *energies* he is immanent and participable. So one can say "all things participate in God" (against deism/competing-substance dualism) while denying "all things are God" (against pantheism) — exactly the seam Mario needs against Hansen. (2) *Anchors theosis without ontological merger.* Deification is real *union by participation in the energies*, not absorption into the divine essence — preserving the creature/Creator gulf even at the summit of salvation (cf. [[Theosis as Likeness and Union So Far as Possible]] and [[Human Deification in Mormonism]] for the contrasting LDS construal). (3) *It is the Orthodox alternative to absolute divine simplicity.* Where the Thomist tradition makes God's essence and operations identical (see [[Divine Simplicity - God Has No Parts of Any Kind]] and [[The Real Distinction Between Essence and Existence]]), Palamas posits a real-yet-not-compositional distinction in God — which is why Orthodoxy can hold both transcendence and participability at once.

**Steelman (LDS reply — Hansen / and an honest tension).** Hansen does not engage the distinction technically (the debate moves on to dualism), but the LDS rejoinders are clear. (1) *It may not actually answer the metaphysical objection Hansen was pressing.* Hansen's worry was not soteriological pantheism but the **ground-of-being** problem — that calling God "reality itself" makes God impersonal and identical with the cosmos. The essence/energies move secures the *creature/Creator* gulf, but Mario still owes an account of how the *uncreated essence* is a personal Father rather than an impersonal absolute — the very point Hansen presses with "if God is fundamental reality itself, then he's not a person" (see [[A Reality More Fundamental Than God Just Is God]]). (2) *The LDS solution to pantheism is different and arguably simpler.* Latter-day Saints avoid pantheism not by an essence/energies gulf but by denying *creatio ex nihilo* outright: creatures are *eternal, uncreated* realities (intelligences, spirit-matter) that God *organizes*, so they were never "God" to begin with and need no Palamite firewall to keep them distinct (see [[The Uncreated Element Is Only the Most Fundamental Substrate]] and [[Creation Ex Nihilo Is Not Biblical]]). (3) *Same-species theosis vs. participation-only theosis.* The deepest divergence: Orthodoxy's energies-participation deliberately *forbids* sharing the divine essence, whereas the LDS distinctive is that humans are the **same kind** as God and can fully partake of the divine nature (see [[Same-Species-as-God as the Core LDS Distinctive in Theosis]] and [[Homoousios Means Same Essence Which Mormons Extend to All Humanity]]). So the very tool Mario uses to *protect* the gulf is the doctrine an LDS apologist would identify as the core point of disagreement about deification.
