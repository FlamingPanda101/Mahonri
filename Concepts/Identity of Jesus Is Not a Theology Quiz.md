# Identity of Jesus Is Not a Theology Quiz (Objection Rebutted)

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
[[Jacob Hansen]] objects that requiring Trinitarian belief for salvation amounts to a "theology test" — as though God damns people for failing an exam on technical metaphysics. [^1] He notes most lay Christians cannot coherently explain the Trinity and often default to modalism, so it seems unjust to make salvation hinge on a doctrine "that many people struggle to understand themselves." [^2][^3]

## The Rebuttal
[[Gavin Ortlund]] reframes the issue from *test* to *accuracy of relationship*, using his recurring **"Cynthia vs. Sally" analogy**.

1. **Worship is an action directed at an identity, not a quiz answer (strong reframe).** "It's not a theology quiz because it's an action — worship and prayer and an existential relation. And it's just a matter of accuracy... is Jesus created or is he uncreated God?" [^4] *Rating: a genuine category shift* — it locates the stakes in relational accuracy rather than intellectual performance.

2. **The Cynthia/Sally analogy distinguishes identity-errors from trivial errors.** If you cannot remember whether your date was Cynthia or Sally, "you literally don't know the identity of the person." [^5] Ortlund deliberately uses *two different names* (not "which high school did the same person attend") because "the difference between a created being and an uncreated being is more significant than just what high school someone went to." [^6] *Rating: the analogy's force depends entirely on whether created-vs-uncreated is a difference of identity or of attribute.*

3. **Steelman of Hansen's counter-analogy.** Hansen presses back: suppose I correctly identify Cynthia but am merely wrong about which high school she attended — does that "ruin the whole date"? [^7] *Rating: an effective challenge* that relocates the disagreement to whether consubstantiality is identity-constituting (Ortlund) or a secondary attribute (Hansen). The two never resolve this.

4. **The modalism concession.** Ortlund grants that a sincere believer with a *modalist* misunderstanding is not thereby damned, invoking the distinction between imperfect understanding and knowing rejection (see [[Distinction Between Knowing Rejection and Imperfect Understanding]]). [^8] *Rating: this concession weakens the "must affirm precise doctrine" reading and shows the real line is relational, not technical.*

## Best Quotes
> "It's like you go on a date with someone and it's like, did you go on a date with Sally or Cynthia? ... it's kind of important to know which one of the two it is." [^9]

> "That's not a quiz or arbitrary. That's just, do you have accurate understanding of the person in question." [^5]

> "The reason I use two different names... rather than which high school the same person attended, is because I think the difference between a created being and an uncreated being is more significant than just what high school someone went to." [^6]

> "It kind of feels like there's a theology test... if I don't pass the question on the Trinity... that I then go to hell." [^1]
