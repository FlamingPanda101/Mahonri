# Christianity Began as a New Religious Movement

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
The objection "Mormonism is just a cult/NRM" assumes that being a new religious movement is itself disqualifying. But by the same sociological criteria, **early Christianity was a new religious movement too.**

## The Rebuttal
- A Roman citizen looking at the early Jesus movement — one charismatic leader, twelve followers, reported miracles, small and strange — would have applied every pejorative "cult" connotation that a 1970 American applied to Hare Krishna *(scholar's standard teaching point)* [^1].
- So "it started as an NRM" is a description of *every* religion's origin, not a mark against truth. The label cannot, by itself, settle whether a faith is true or legitimate [^1].

## Best Quote
> "Christianity, like all religions... started as a new religious movement... you have one guy named Jesus, he's got 12 guys around him... let's look at the early Christian church from the perspective of a Roman citizen. They would see it looking just as strange, just as weird, just as outlandish." — [[Lorne Dawson]] [^1]
