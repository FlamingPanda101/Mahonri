# Christianity Abolished Slavery Twice

## The Claim

[[Glenn Scrivener]]: "Christianity abolished slavery twice." First, "the long slow burn" of medieval Christendom, where slavery "melted away" through a sacramental logic; second, the preacher-led abolitionism of the 18th-19th centuries.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Medieval dissolution.** In Christendom slavery "dissolves away" because you baptize, kiss, and eat with every member of your household — the sacramental and communal practices erode the master-slave distinction (alongside economic factors and plagues with "massive social implications").[^1] *(Moderate — the decline of chattel slavery in medieval Europe is real, though its causes are debated [serfdom replaced slavery; economic and demographic factors loom large]; the "sacramental" account is one contested explanation.)*

2. **New World re-emergence and second abolition.** Slavery returns via New World slavery and is abolished again — this time through "very much a preacher abolitionism" ([[William Wilberforce]], Quakers, the UK abolitionist society, later rolled out through international law).[^2] *(Strong on the UK; O'Connor concedes the UK case.)*

3. **The imago Dei as engine.** Both abolitions trace to [[Genesis 1.26-27|Genesis 1]] (humans made to rule, not be slaves) and the [[Leviticus 25.10|Jubilee]] fulfilled in Christ. See [[The Old Testament Was a Hammer for Abolitionists]].[^3] *(Coherent within the canonical reading.)*

4. **Steelman of O'Connor.** O'Connor presses two ways: (a) **reversibility** — France abolished then [[Napoleon]] reinstated; Haiti was self-emancipation, not churchly. See [[Abolition Tracks Material Conditions Not Christian Conviction]]. (b) **Own the other side** — Christianity also fueled the anti-abolitionist movement "just as strong." See [[Own the Anti-Abolitionist Christianity Too]].[^4] Scrivener's reply: the [[Only Christianity Reached the Moon - The NASA Analogy|NASA analogy]] — Christians failed often, but *only* Christian ideals "proved themselves remarkably abolitionist in ways others haven't."[^5] *(The NASA analogy is rhetorically strong but, as O'Connor notes, breaks down where the church *discouraged* the goal — see that note.)*

## Best Quotes

> "Christianity has ended slavery, both in the long slow burn of the sacramental way — medieval Christendom — and... a very much preacher abolitionism. Christianity has done it twice."[^1]

> "In Christendom it melts away. It comes back through the New World slavery. It is abolished again."[^2]
