# Brainwashing Does Not Exist

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
Members of cults/NRMs (and, by extension, of the Church) were "brainwashed" — psychologically coerced into joining against their will.

## The Rebuttal

### 1. Origin: Korean War propaganda, not science
"Brainwashing" was coined from Chinese **thought reform** of American POWs in the Korean War (a calque of the Chinese characters). [[Robert J. Lifton]] wrote the 1961 study; pop culture (*The Manchurian Candidate*) cemented the zombie-assassin image [^1].

### 2. The declassified CIA study refuted it
The CIA commissioned two psychologists to study ~60 returned POWs (declassified in the 1970s). Conclusion: **there is no such thing as brainwashing.** The prisoners capitulated under torture out of fear, and renounced the coerced statements once safely home.

> "They basically demonstrated... that there is no such thing as brainwashing. They said these prisoners were subject to torture and... out of fear of being subjected to further torture, some were willing to capitulate. But as soon as they got home, most of them said... 'I don't believe any of that stuff.'" — [[Lorne Dawson]] [^2]

### 3. The empirical death blow
Two large, independent studies (one psychiatrist, ~800 ex-members; one sociologist, every UK Unification Church attendee over a year) found, in the same year with no contact: **only a fraction of 1% of those contacted ever join, and ~90% of those who join leave voluntarily within two years** [^3].

> "If brainwashing exists, why aren't way more people joining these groups? And... if you're brainwashed, why aren't you staying in these groups?" — [[Lorne Dawson]] [^4]

### 4. The courts agree
In **US v. Fishman (1990)**, with APA and ASA amicus briefs, brainwashing testimony was ruled not scientifically viable and inadmissible; European courts eventually followed [^5]. (Deprogrammers like [[Ted Patrick]] had relied on the myth.)
