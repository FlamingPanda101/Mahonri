# Revelation 14:6 — An Angel With the Everlasting Gospel

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
The [[Galatians 1.8|Galatians 1:8]] attack treats *any* angel bringing the gospel as accursed. [[Ward Radio]] answers that [[John the Evangelist|John]] prophesies precisely such an angel in [[Revelation 14.6|Revelation 14:6]], so the critic's reading sets Paul against John.[^1]

## The Rebuttal

### 1. John foresees a future gospel-bearing angel — strong (textual)
"I saw another angel fly in the midst of heaven, having the everlasting gospel to preach." The hosts stress John is seeing "some future date." If Galatians forbids angels from ever bringing the gospel, then John's vision is false. The two can only be harmonized if Galatians condemns a false gospel's *content*, not angelic delivery.[^2] Rating: **strong** — a direct intra-canonical tension the critic must resolve.

### 2. Latter-day Saints identify this angel with the Restoration — interpretive
The episode implies the Revelation 14 angel matches the angelic restoration of "the everlasting gospel" (see [[Angel Moroni]]). The Greek **εὐαγγέλιον αἰώνιον** ("everlasting gospel") signals *one* timeless gospel, not a contradictory new one — consistent with [[1 Corinthians 15.1-4|Paul's gospel]].[^3] Rating: **interpretive** (an identification, not a proof).

### Steelman of the opposing view
The critic can reply that Revelation 14:6's angel preaches *the* everlasting gospel — the same one already delivered — not a *new* or *different* gospel, so it never conflicts with Galatians 1:8 (which condemns a "different" gospel). On this reading both verses are consistent without any Restoration: an angel re-proclaiming the unchanged gospel is fine; an angel bringing additional scripture is not. **Response:** this steelman actually *grants* the rebuttal's central point — Galatians 1:8 is about gospel *content*, not the angelic messenger — which is exactly the LDS escape from the proof-text. It then relocates the debate to whether the Restoration's gospel is "the same" everlasting gospel, handled in [[Galatians 1-8 Latter-day Saints Preach Pauls Gospel]].
