# Matthew 22:30 — "No Marriage in the Resurrection" Rebutted

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
[[Matthew 22.30|Matthew 22:30]] — "in the resurrection they neither marry, nor are given in marriage" — means eternal/temple marriage is impossible, so LDS sealing of spouses and families is unbiblical.

## The Rebuttal

### 1. It addresses *marrying in* the resurrection, not marriages sealed before it
The verse speaks to the *act* of contracting marriage at the resurrection — not the *continuation* of a marriage already sealed.

> "There is a long period of time between death and the resurrection... where a lot can happen like proxy work... So if you are already married and or sealed before the resurrection, you will continue to be. If you choose not to be after the resurrection, you will not." [^1]

### 2. Requires the plan of salvation to parse
The objection assumes a single-stage afterlife. In LDS theology the interval between death and resurrection (the spirit world, proxy ordinances, acceptance) is exactly where the distinction lives — so a marriage validly sealed and kept persists, while no *new* marrying occurs in the resurrection itself [^1].
