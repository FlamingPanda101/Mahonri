# Darwinian Ethics Licenses Eugenics and Tribe-Supremacy

## The Point
If morality is read off biology — survival of the fittest in "a competition for survival in a world of limited resources" — then the conclusions the modern secularist recoils from follow naturally: sacrifice "those who are a detriment to the tribe," favor your own tribe over outsiders, and practice eugenics, since "inferior and Superior is the name of the game in biological reproduction." [[Jacob Hansen]] presses this *reductio* on the critic [[Bill Reel]]: biology can tell you what *does* survive, never what *ought* to; and the boundary of the "tribe" (family, race, species) is arbitrary, so the Nazi "Aryan superior tribe" logic is as biologically respectable as universal humanism. This is the metaethical engine of [[Evolution Cannot Ground Objective Morality]] dramatized live — evolution describes outcomes, it cannot prescribe obligations.

## Evidence
Hansen walks the Darwinian premise to its end. On "a darwinistic perspective," he argues, "you do sacrifice those who are a detriment to the tribe in order that the tribe May Thrive."[^1] The tribe-boundary is arbitrary: "you're just changing the Dynamics of where we draw the boundaries of the tribe" — family, racial group, "all of humanity" — so there is no biological reason to prefer universal humanity, and the Nazis' choice of "the white Aryan race" as "the superior tribe" is on the same footing.[^2] He drives the eugenics point with the horse-breeding analogy: "when you breed horses you don't breed the crappy horses, you breed the best horses," so biology gives no foothold for a "Universal Humanity… all life matters ethic."[^3]

Crucially, Reel's own answers slide down the slope. He agrees society will "kill people" whose "inclinations are to impose harm,"[^4] grants that a person's "worth is how much you contribute to the society's goal,"[^5] and concedes that on the breeding logic "Eugenics makes sense… a human form of biologically controlled biological reproduction… discouraging the reproduction of inferior members."[^6] Hansen's point is not that Reel endorses atrocity but that Reel's naturalism cannot *rule it out* — exactly the failure that shows the framework borrows, rather than grounds, the humane ethic it wants.

## Best Quotes
> "no, you do sacrifice those who are a detriment to the tribe in order that the tribe May Thrive, and people don't have rights, that's a myth." [^1]

> "why is the morality of the Nazis who basically said the white Aryan race are the superior tribe… how is biology in any way pointing us towards this Universal Humanity… all life matters ethic?" [^2]

> "Eugenics makes sense. Eugenics is just a human form of biologically controlled biological reproduction, or at least discouraging the reproduction of inferior members of a herd." [^6]

## LDS Connection
The exchange is a vivid case of the is–ought gap: no fact about what survives yields a duty about what ought to be done (see [[Evolution Cannot Ground Objective Morality]]). Latter-day Saint theology locates human worth not in reproductive fitness but in every person bearing the image of God and being an eternal soul of infinite value — which is precisely the fixed, non-biological ground the Darwinian account lacks and the source of the universal-dignity intuition the critic cannot otherwise justify. That a thoughtful secularist *recoils* from eugenics while his stated premises license it is itself evidence that he is operating on a borrowed transcendent standard (see [[A Critic Must Ground His Moral Truth Claims or He Is Borrowing Them]]; [[Moral Argument Against Atheism]]).
