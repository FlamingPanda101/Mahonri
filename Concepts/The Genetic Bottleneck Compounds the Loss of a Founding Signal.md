# The Genetic Bottleneck Compounds the Loss of a Founding Signal

#type/claim

## The Claim

Beyond the founder effect and genetic drift, a **population bottleneck** — a cataclysmic die-off that sharply shrinks a population — further obscures rare genetic signatures by removing most of the lineages that carried them. The Americas suffered exactly such bottlenecks (Book of Mormon mass-exterminations, and above all the ~90% post-Columbian population collapse from disease), making any small Near-Eastern founding signal even harder to detect.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. What a bottleneck does to a gene pool

A bottleneck is a catastrophic event — war, famine, disease, extermination — that "dramatically diminishes the sample size" of genetic signatures available to propagate. [[Stephen Smoot]]'s toy model: three people with blue, green, and brown eyes; if the blue- and green-eyed two are killed, only brown-eyed genes survive and "boom" in the rebound population — the other variants existed but are now unrecoverable from descendants.[^2]

> **Evidence rating:** Strong (a correct, textbook description of a bottleneck).

### 2. The Americas were bottlenecked repeatedly

Within the text, the Nephites are systematically exterminated (though "Nephite" is by then a cultural-religious label as much as a biological one). More decisively, European colonization triggered tens of millions of deaths — an estimated **~90% of the population over 100–200 years from disease alone**, before counting war and subsequent admixture with European arrivals. Any faint 600 BC signal had to survive all of that.[^3]

> **Evidence rating:** Strong (the post-contact demographic collapse is well attested).

### 3. The mechanisms compound

Bottlenecks do not act alone: combined with the [[Founder Effect and Genetic Drift Erase a Small Founding Signal|founder effect, drift]], natural selection against non-adapted colonist genes, and admixture into a far larger host population (the [[The Philistine DNA Study Shows Genetic Signals Vanish into Host Populations|Philistine pattern]]), they make detecting a small incursion "across a continent in the millions" practically impossible after two and a half millennia.[^4]

> **Evidence rating:** Moderate-to-strong (the cumulative inference is sound).

## Steelman of the Opposing View

A critic can note that bottlenecks reduce *diversity* but also *amplify* whatever survives — a founder allele that makes it through can become common, so bottlenecks do not only erase signals, they can entrench them. If a Lehite marker had survived the first generations, a later bottleneck might have *fixed* it rather than removed it. The reply: the prior steps (founder effect + admixture + selection) make survival of a distinctive Near-Eastern marker into the bottleneck unlikely in the first place, and the relevant collapse (post-1492) came thousands of years and dozens of generations after Lehi, by which point the signal was already diluted into the host population. The honest claim is not "the DNA vanished" but that it became "diminishingly difficult to detect with specificity."

## Best Quotes

> "It's a cataclysmic event that dramatically diminishes the sample size of what different genetic signatures you can detect... typically war, famine, disease, extermination."[^2]

> "If you had three people, one with blue eyes, green eyes, and brown eyes, and two of those got killed and you only had brown eyes left, now you only have the genetic material of that brown-eyed person."[^2]

> "Fast forward to the European colonization of the Americas... the estimates are like tens and tens of millions of deaths from disease alone — 90% of the population during just 100, 200 years."[^3]
