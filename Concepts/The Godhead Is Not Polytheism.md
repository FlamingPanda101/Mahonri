# The Godhead Is Not Polytheism

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)

The objection: if you have three distinct divine beings, you have *polytheism*, which the Bible condemns. [^1]

## The Rebuttal

1. **Polytheism in the ancient world meant something specific.** The polytheism the Bible opposes was *warring gods with contrary wills*, capricious and limited in power. The Godhead is the opposite: three persons with a *perfectly united will* forming the single ultimate power over all things. So the Godhead lacks the very features that made ancient polytheism objectionable. (Evidence: reasonable as a historical description of ANE polytheism's contrast with a unified divine council.) [^1]
2. **"Polytheism" does not entail the inferences leveled against it.** At its base the word just means *multiple theoi* (divine beings). Trinitarians smuggle in extra premises ("then one must be ultimate," "then Jesus isn't fully God," "then they contradict the oneness of God"). But "multiple divine beings" by itself implies none of those. Even Latter-day Saints affirm the Father as the "Most High God" and so the one "true God" in that sense — without denying the Son's full divinity. [^2]
3. **Steelman.** The critic responds that the biblical polemic (Deuteronomy, Isaiah, the Shema) targets *any* worship or recognition of other gods, regardless of whether they war or agree; and that a "perfectly united council of three" is, ontologically, still three gods — so calling it "the Godhead" rather than "polytheism" is a relabeling. (Evidence: the terminological dispute is real; "monolatry," "henotheism," and "inclusive monotheism" are the scholarly labels for the in-between position the LDS side occupies.) The LDS reply leans on the scholarship that ancient Israel itself held exactly this in-between view (see [[Ancient Judaism Affirmed a Divine Spectrum]]), so the "Godhead" is not a novelty but the biblical position. [^3]
4. **Why is plurality "evil"?** Barnes presses that there is nothing self-evidently wicked about *theos*-plurality per se; the offense the critics feel is supplied by added theological commitments (creedal monotheism), not by the bare concept. [^4]

## Best Quotes

> "The polytheism of the ancient world were warring gods with contrary wills, capricious gods that were limited in their power. ... The godhead model says all three of these together form the ultimate power and singular will over all things." [^1]

> "The word polytheism does not necessitate any of those inferences that they add upon it to get offended from." [^2]

> "I believe in divine nature. I don't understand why that is such an offensive evil concept." [^4]
