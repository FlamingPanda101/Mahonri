# Are Mormons Christian (Beshears)

## The Claim
Asked the "elephant in the room" question, [[Kyle Beshears]] refuses a one-word answer. At the institutional level Mormonism "has so radically departed from traditional Christian orthodoxy that evangelicals... are correct to want to challenge and push against that"; at the individual level he affirms that some Latter-day Saints "are in potential or actual communion with Christ." [^1][^2]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Institution vs. individual distinction.** The organization's departure from creedal orthodoxy (esp. "God and his nature... the Godhead" vs. trinitarianism) is real and "you can't just discount that"; yet individuals "at very different stages of their life... I believe are in potential or actual communion with Christ." [^3] *Rating: a careful two-level answer,* refusing both blanket inclusion and blanket exclusion.

2. **The cleverly-reversed question.** Beshears cites a book that asks "Are Christians Mormon?" — i.e., "what is it about Mormonism that we see in Christianity?" [^4] *Rating: a productive reframe* that exposes shared content (Christ-centeredness) without erasing the gap.

3. **The two cheap answers both rejected.** "It's a really easy thing to just say Mormonism is not Christian and no Latter-day Saints have any kind of faith worth speaking of. That's just as easy as saying... Latter-day Saints are as Christian as anybody else." [^5] *Rating: the heart of his method* (see [[Holding the Tension Between Anti-Mormonism and Syncretism]]).

4. **The Sandra Tanner test case.** [[Sandra Tanner]] left the LDS church but, per her biography, "still believed in the Book of Mormon" for a time — so "when did she become a Christian? When she... left the church and had her rolls removed?" [^6] *Rating: a sharp diagnostic* showing that "Christian" cannot be reduced to church membership status.

5. **Even Rome draws the line.** "Why would a Latter-day Saint have to be rebaptized if they were joining the Roman Catholic Church? Even Rome recognizes there's significant enough differences here to draw lines." [^7] *Rating: an external, non-evangelical corroboration* that the doctrinal gap is objectively significant (the Vatican's 2001 ruling that LDS baptism is invalid).

**Steelman of the inclusive view.** A Latter-day Saint would insist the church's official name centers Jesus Christ, that members affirm Christ as the only means of salvation, and that excluding sincere Christ-worshippers over creedal metaphysics is uncharitable (cf. [[Are Latter-day Saints Christians]]). Beshears honors this by affirming individual believers while maintaining the institutional/doctrinal distinction — declining to make "Christian" either free or cost-free.

## Best Quotes
> "Mormonism has so radically departed from traditional Christian orthodoxy that evangelicals in particular are correct to want to challenge and push against that." [^1]

> "When it comes to individuals that I know within the LDS church... I believe are in potential or actual communion with Christ. I can't deny that, and I think it would be dishonest if I did." [^2]

> "It's a really easy thing to just say Mormonism is not Christian... That's just as easy as saying Latter-day Saints are as Christian as anybody else." [^5]

> "Why would a Latter-day Saint have to be rebaptized if they were joining the Roman Catholic Church? Even Rome recognizes there's significant enough differences here to draw lines." [^7]
