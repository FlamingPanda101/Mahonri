# Joseph Smiths First Interpreter Revelation Pointed to Martin Harris

## The Claim
[[Don Bradley]] argues that the *very first* revelation Joseph Smith received through the Nephite interpreters (Urim and Thummim) — his first use of them after bringing the plates home — was the Lord showing him [[Martin Harris]] as the answer to "how are you going to provide for this work?" By Lucy Mack Smith's dating that first use fell on October 6, 1827, the **Feast of Tabernacles**, the feast commemorating God's provision of manna in the wilderness — a fitting day to ask *how God will provide*. Because Martin became the funder, this single revelation cascades into the Book of Mormon's publication and, generations later, the succession of [[Dallin H. Oaks]].

## Supporting Evidence
1. **The first interpreter question was about provision.** "Joseph Smith asked the Lord through the interpreters that day, How are we going to do this work? How are you going to provide for this work of translation and publication?… and the Lord shows him Martin Harris, and says, This guy. Go to this guy." Joseph sent his mother to the Harris home in Palmyra.[^1]
2. **The date is documented and thematically apt.** Lucy Mack Smith records October 6, 1827; on the Jewish calendar that is the Feast of Tabernacles, which "commemorates the wandering in the wilderness and how… God provided for the Israelites with manna… their daily bread." The first revelation about *provision* falls on the feast of *provision*.[^2]
3. **The feast also points to Christ.** Bradley notes the Greek of John 1:14 ("the Word was made flesh") says Christ "tabernacled… pitched his tent among us," so Tabernacles looks forward to the Incarnation — tying the calendar to the Christ-centered reading the episode sustains throughout.[^3]
4. **The downstream chain is the payoff.** "That first use of the Nephite Urim and Thummim… brings about the way that the Book of Mormon is funded… brings the family, the Harris family, into the Church, and therefore, by extension, now gives us our 18th prophet." Martin's $50 also let Joseph relocate to Pennsylvania when the plates were being raided. (See [[The Harris Bloodline Connects the First Revelation to the 18th Prophet]].)[^4]
5. **Steelman of the skeptic.** A critic answers that the Feast-of-Tabernacles dating rests on a single late source (Lucy Mack Smith's 1845 memoir), that a lunisolar feast and a remembered date can be made to align after the fact, and that "first revelation through the interpreters" is a reconstruction, not a contemporaneous record. Bradley's defense is that he is using the one firmly dated early event and reading it with the period's own covenant calendar (the same feast-day pattern Jack Welch has applied to the Cumorah visits), not inventing the date — and that even granting uncertainty, the load-bearing historical fact (Martin's funding launched the publication) is independently secure. The Tabernacles overlay is offered as illumination, not proof.

## Best Quotes

> "Joseph Smith asked the Lord through the interpreters that day… How are you going to provide for this work…? He asks and he looks in the interpreters and the Lord shows him Martin Harris, and says, This guy. Go to this guy."[^1]

> "The first revelation that he has through the interpreters after he brings them home from the hill is the Lord shows him Martin Harris."[^1]

> "That first use of the Nephite Urim and Thummim… brings about the way that the Book of Mormon is funded… and now gives us our 18th prophet through that family line."[^4]
