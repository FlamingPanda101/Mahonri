# We Are Goldfish Who Cannot See the Christian Water
## The Point
The reason secular humanism mistakes its borrowed convictions for neutral common sense is that Christianity's influence is *ambient*: it forms the unexamined background of Western moral life. Like goldfish that cannot see the water they swim in, moderns "take for granted" values that are in fact a distinctive, contingent, Christian inheritance. The aim of Tom Holland's *Dominion* — the video's governing source — is to make the water visible.

## Evidence
1. **The goldfish-in-the-bowl image.** "We are the goldfish swimming in this water; we don't see it, we don't recognize that we're in water, we don't recognize we're in a bowl... and the ambition of the book is to try and open the eyes of readers to the fact: yeah, we are in a bowl, and this bowl has a very distinctive contour and shape."[^1]

2. **Ripple effects we no longer trace.** Christianity set off successive revolutions — the 11th-century papal reformation, then the Reformation, then the Enlightenment — that "spilled out so much that now in the 21st century we don't even realize where these ripple effects are coming from; we just take them for granted."[^2]

3. **Categories we treat as obvious are imports.** Even the modern concept of "religion" as one option among many ("the notion of something called Hinduism being a religion") is "an alien way of seeing the world" — a Christian-shaped lens we mistake for the view from nowhere.[^3] What looks like neutral secular ground is a particular cultural bowl. This is the methodological core of [[The Dominion Thesis - Western Values Are Christianized]].

4. **Caveat against triumphalism (steelman).** The narrator concedes Holland "does not see this as a linear line of progress"; the Christian ideal "had to claw its way out of the default darkness of the human condition" and contended against those who mixed un-Christian interests with Christian teaching.[^4] And critics reply that some protective values appear in pre-Christian law, so "we can't see the water" can be over-applied — see [[Western Values Predate or Arise Independently of Christianity]]. The defensible claim is about the *dominant formative source* of Western moral intuitions, not sole causation.

## LDS Connection
The Restoration's narrative of a Great Apostasy and Restoration assumes exactly this kind of unseen inheritance: much that the Christian world takes as self-evident is downstream of choices and revelations whose origins were forgotten or distorted. Recognizing that we "swim" in a Christian-shaped moral world invites the further Latter-day Saint question of *which* inheritances are the pure stream and which are corrupted — and points back to revelation, not cultural osmosis, as the way to tell. See [[Secular Humanism Lives on Borrowed Christian Capital]].
