# Shazer Names the Fine-Twined-Linen Door of the Tabernacle

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)|David Butler]] argues that [[Lehi]]'s wilderness itinerary is mapped not against Solomon's Temple (as his [[Lehi's Dream Encodes the Three Temple Rooms by Hebrew Name-Puns|dream]] is) but against the **Tabernacle**, and that the place-name **Shazer** is a Hebrew pun marking the *door of the tabernacle*. Because "nothing happens" at Shazer — the party arrives, hunts, and leaves over two verses ([[1 Nephi 16.13|1 Nephi 16]]) — Butler reasons the name itself must carry the meaning: Nephi, writing on plates where "stuff has to matter," put Shazer in the record only so the temple-literate reader would *hear the word*. [^1] The root behind *Shazer* means "to twist/twine"; its Exodus form *mashzar* describes the **fine twined linen** (*shesh mashzar*) of the tabernacle hangings, priestly garments, and veil — and specifically the **screen/door** through which one enters. So passing "Shazer" is the family *passing through the tabernacle door* from the courtyard into the holy place. This is a **distinct** name-pun from the three-room scheme in [[Lehi's Dream Encodes the Three Temple Rooms by Hebrew Name-Puns]]: that note maps Lehi's *vision* (1 Nephi 8) onto Solomon's Temple via *ulam/heikhal/debir*; this maps Lehi's *physical journey* onto the *Tabernacle* via a single travel-stop name.

## Supporting Evidence

1. **A scriptural footnote already glosses Shazer as "twisting."** [[1 Nephi 16.13|1 Nephi 16:13]] footnote *a* gives the Hebrew sense "twisting, intertwining" for *Shazer* — so the twist/twine root is not Butler's invention but is in the LDS edition's apparatus. The verb *shazar* ("to twist/twine") does not itself appear in the Old Testament, but its participial form **mashzar** appears **21 times, all in Exodus**, all in the construct *shesh mashzar*, "fine twined linen." [^2]
2. **What *shesh mashzar* describes is the entire fabric of the tabernacle — including its door.** The fine-twined-linen specification covers the priests' breeches and ephod, the curtains and walls of the tabernacle and its courtyard, the veil before the holy of holies, and **the screen/door by which one enters** the tent. Butler's point: of all the architecture, the word most pointedly evokes the *entryway*, so a travel-stop named "twisting/twined linen" signals the threshold one passes to enter the middle room. [^3]
3. **The structural payoff: everything before Shazer is "courtyard," everything after is the holy place.** On Butler's mapping, the Valley of Lemuel (where Lehi builds an altar, [[1 Nephi 2.6|1 Nephi 2]]) is the open courtyard; Shazer is the door; the long middle stretch (the broken bow, Ishmael's death, the murmuring-and-comfort cycles of [[1 Nephi 16.34|1 Nephi 16]]) is the holy place; and Bountiful is Eden / the holy of holies. Shazer's only function is to be the *named hinge* between courtyard and interior — which is exactly why nothing else happens there. [^4]
4. **Nephi elsewhere "calls out" room-names this way.** In [[1 Nephi 8|1 Nephi 8]] Lehi narrates his ascent by naming the rooms (*ulam*, *heikhal*, *debir*) and the tree; Butler reads Shazer as Nephi doing the same thing in the *travel* narrative — "calling out the name of the door, the material of which the door is made." The technique (a place that gets a *name* but no *events*, vs. places that get *events* but no name, like the unnamed mountain) is presented as Nephi's deliberate signposting. See [[The Detailed Narrative Outranks the Chapter Summary in First Nephi]]. [^5]

**Steelman of the skeptic.** The pun is **suggestive, not demonstrative**, and several joints are loose. First, the controlling verb *shazar* is *not attested in Biblical Hebrew* (only the derived *mashzar* is), so "Shazer = the twisting-word" is an inference about a root, and the leap from "twisting" to *specifically the tabernacle door* selects one referent of *shesh mashzar* out of many (garments, walls, veil, curtains) — a door is not privileged by the text, only by the argument's needs. Second, the whole reading presupposes the **Tabernacle mapping** of the journey, which is itself a devotional overlay; a critic notes the itinerary is preserved only in Joseph Smith's English, that "Shazer" has other proposed etymologies, and that Hebrew puns are cheap to manufacture post hoc — the same objection that dogs [[Lehi's Dream Encodes the Three Temple Rooms by Hebrew Name-Puns]] and is generalized in [[A Parallel List Without Textual Analysis Carries No Burden]]. Third, "nothing happens at Shazer, therefore the name must be a code" is a *gap argument*: terse travel notes are normal in ancient itineraries and need not hide liturgy. **A Latter-day Saint reply:** the move has a genuine anchor the dream-puns lack — an *official scriptural footnote* already supplying the twist/twine sense — and the cumulative, *ordered* mapping (altar-courtyard → twined-linen door → murmur-and-feast holy place → Eden) is harder to dismiss as one lucky coincidence than any isolated pun, cohering with the broader [[The Book of Mormon Matches Pre-Reform First Temple Theology|First-Temple reading]]. As with the rest of this material, the discipline is to keep the parallel *functional* and provisional rather than to treat a single travel-stop as load-bearing proof.
