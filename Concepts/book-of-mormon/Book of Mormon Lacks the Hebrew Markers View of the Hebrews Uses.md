# Book of Mormon Lacks the Hebrew Markers View of the Hebrews Uses

## The Claim
If Joseph Smith had *View of the Hebrews* in front of him as a source, the most useful material in it — the very "proofs" Ethan Smith marshals — would be the Hebrew words, Hebrew names, feast days, and customs supposedly found among Native Americans. Yet **none of those specific markers appears in the [[Book of Mormon]]**. The dog that didn't bark argues against dependence: a plagiarist would have mined exactly this material, and didn't. This is a point [[Jack Welch]] makes in his article "An Unparallel."[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The book's signature evidence is absent from the Book of Mormon (rated: strong, textual).** Ethan Smith's proofs are "Hebrew words that are in Native American languages ... names that are Hebrew." Smoot: "None of those things show up in the Book of Mormon."[^1] A forger wanting "a very believable account of Israelite people in the Americas" had "great material" sitting in front of him and "doesn't work it into the narrative at all."
2. **The Book of Mormon's names point the opposite way (rated: strong, scholarly).** Instead of Ethan Smith's pseudo-Hebrew indigenous names, Joseph "chooses names like Sam and Nephi and Lehi, who we actually now have Hebrew attestation for" — names that "never showed up in View of the Hebrews."[^2] [[Jack Welch]] catalogs these counter-parallels in "An Unparallel."
3. **The Book of Mormon is an atypical form of the theory (rated: moderate, comparative).** Even on the one genuine broad overlap (Israelite ancestry of Native Americans), the Book of Mormon "is different in like every respect" — e.g., it describes a small Josephite group leaving before the Babylonian destruction, not the lost ten tribes crossing the Bering Strait.[^3] *Steelman:* a critic can reply that an intelligent borrower would deliberately *avoid* copying the most distinctive surface details to disguise the theft, so absence of Ethan Smith's Hebraisms is consistent with a clever plagiarist. Smoot's implicit reply: that move is unfalsifiable (any absence becomes "evidence" of cunning), whereas the positive presence of *independently attested* ancient names is evidence the names did not come from Ethan Smith at all.
