# Moroni Took the Plates to Preserve the Sealed Portion

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
[[Mark Gagnon]] recounts that after the translation [[Moroni]] "comes back and takes the plates with him," and says "I don't know if anyone really knows why he takes the plates with him."[^1]

## The Rebuttal

### 1. The Book of Mormon itself gives the reason
[[Keystone]]: "The Book of Mormon actually tells us why Moroni took the plates back." [[2 Nephi 27]]: "when thou hast read the words which I have commanded thee, and obtained the witnesses which I have promised unto thee, then shalt thou seal up the book again, and hide it up unto me, that I may preserve the words which thou hast not read, until I shall see fit in mine own wisdom to reveal all things."[^2]
> **Evidence rating:** Strong — a direct, on-point text; the "why" Gagnon says is unknown is stated in the record.

### 2. Part of the plates was sealed and untranslated
"Part of the golden plates was sealed shut. They were turned back over to Moroni in order to preserve and protect that portion" — the still-unrevealed text.[^3] The withdrawal protects sacred material reserved for a future time, consistent with [[The Sacred Book of Mormon Artifacts Were Withdrawn Not Lost]].
> **Evidence rating:** Strong — the sealed portion is described throughout the witness accounts and the text.

### 3. The honest concession
Keystone is careful: "Now, you can still say Joseph lied and just never had any plates, but this is the explanation the Book of Mormon gives." The point is not that the explanation proves the plates were real, only that the *internal* answer to "why did Moroni take them?" plainly exists — and that this reframes attention onto the witnesses who handled the plates.[^4] See [[Book of Mormon Witnesses Are the Strongest Evidence]] and the empty-stone-box motif of [[The Stone Box Is the Empty Tomb of the Book of Mormon]].
> **Evidence rating:** Strong as a concession — it distinguishes "the record answers the question" from "the record is true."

## Steelman of the Opposing View
The skeptic's reply is exactly the one Keystone concedes: a convenient scriptural explanation for why the one piece of physical evidence is unavailable is precisely what a fabricator would also produce — "the angel took the plates" and "part was sealed" are unfalsifiable, removing the artifact from any test. So "the Book of Mormon explains it" answers the *narrative* question while doing nothing to verify the plates existed. The disciplined conclusion: Gagnon's specific claim ("no one knows why") is simply wrong — the text answers it — but the apologetic value is limited, which is why the case properly shifts to the eleven witnesses who said they saw and hefted the plates rather than to the (now absent) plates themselves.

## Best Quotes
> "The Book of Mormon actually tells us why Moroni took the plates back. In 2 Nephi 27, we read... 'then shalt thou seal up the book again, and hide it up unto me, that I may preserve the words which thou hast not read.'"[^2]

> "You can still say Joseph lied and just never had any plates, but this is the explanation the Book of Mormon gives for why we don't have them."[^4]
