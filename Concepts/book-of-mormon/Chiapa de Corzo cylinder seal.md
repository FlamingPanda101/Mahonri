# Chiapa de Corzo cylinder seal

#type/term

## The Claim
The **Chiapa de Corzo cylinder seal** is a Mesoamerican artifact (from the Chiapa de Corzo site in Chiapas, Mexico) cited on [[Ward Radio - Brave Academic Admits Book of Mormon Premise is Possible|Ward Radio]] by [[Jerry Grover]] as a piece of **trace Old World / Egyptian-style evidence** in the New World. Cylinder seals are a characteristically Near Eastern technology (Mesopotamian/Egyptian), so their appearance in Mesoamerica is offered as one of several small anomalies — alongside La Venta's bearded, turbaned figures and a Mediterranean-style purple-dye (murex/snail) parallel — consistent with the kind of trans-oceanic contact the Book of Mormon implies for the Lehite and [[Mulek|Mulekite]] migrations. It is a minor, supporting datum in Grover's larger Olmec correlation, not a stand-alone proof.

## Supporting Evidence
1. **Cylinder seals are an Old World technology.** The cylinder seal is a Near Eastern invention with deep roots in Mesopotamia and Egypt; finding seal use in Mesoamerica is the sort of culture-trait anomaly diffusionists cite. *Rating: weak-to-moderate as evidence of contact — Mesoamerican stamp and cylinder seals are independently attested and usually explained as local development, so this is suggestive at best.*
2. **It clusters with other La Venta-area anomalies.** Grover pairs the seal with bearded/turbaned Olmec figures, a non-Mesoamerican clay bust, and a purple-dye parallel near [[John Sorenson]]'s candidate Zarahemla — a *convergence* of small oddities rather than one decisive find. *Rating: the value, if any, is cumulative; each item alone is contestable.* See [[Olmec Bearded Figures and an Academic Concession of Possible Transoceanic Contact]].
3. **It fits the Mulek/seal motif.** The note travels with Grover's reading of the [[The Characters Document Translated as Abbreviated Hieratic and Maya|characters document]] and the broader "a seal vindicated Mulek" theme — Near Eastern sealing practice surfacing in the Book of Mormon's New World. *Rating: thematic; depends on Grover's contested glyph readings.* See [[Mulek Son of Zedekiah Was Vindicated by Linguistics and a Seal]].

**Steelman.** Mainstream archaeology treats Mesoamerican seals as an indigenous development and rejects pre-Columbian trans-oceanic contact absent decisive genetic, linguistic, or botanical evidence; a single cylinder seal proves diffusion no more than bearded statues do. The item is cherry-picked into a contact narrative it does not require. **Reply (LDS):** the seal is explicitly offered as *trace* evidence within a cumulative anomaly set, not a proof — its modest role is to show that Old-World-style culture traits are *present*, which is what one would expect if small Near Eastern colonies reached the area, while conceding that academia "has no metric" short of overwhelming impact to accept contact.
