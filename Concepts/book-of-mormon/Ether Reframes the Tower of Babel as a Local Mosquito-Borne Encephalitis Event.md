# Ether Reframes the Tower of Babel as a Local Mosquito-Borne Encephalitis Event

#type/claim

## The Claim

[[Jerry Grover]] — a licensed geologist/engineer who reads scripture against scientific and
historical-linguistic evidence — proposes that the "confounding of the language" at the tower
was **not** the instantaneous creation of all the world's language families, but a *local*,
*gradual*, physically-caused breakdown of speech: most plausibly a **mosquito-borne
encephalitis** spreading through a flooding Mesopotamian river-plain. He argues the Book of
Ether supplies details Genesis lacks that fit a disease etiology far better than the "Disney
version" in which God instantly scatters humanity into French, Swahili, German, etc.[^1] The
apologetic payoff: the Book of Mormon's account is *consistent with* historical linguistics
(which denies a single 2500 BC mother-tongue), whereas a wooden reading of Genesis 11 is not —
so Ether "clarifies the scope" rather than compounding the problem.[^2]

This is a distinct argument from the vault's [[The Tower Was a Temple Recontextualized From an Older Story|tower-as-temple]] thesis: that note reads the tower as *temple* code and the
narrative as recontextualized polemic; Grover's note is a *naturalistic etiology* for the
confounding itself. The two share only the premise that the Book of Mormon says "the tower,"
never "the Tower of Babel."

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. Ether shows the event was gradual and local, not instantaneous — rate: moderate (text-driven inference)
The brother of Jared and Jared pray about **two** things — not being driven out, and not being
"confounded" ([[Ether 2|Ether 1:33-37]]). Grover argues you only pray *not to be affected* by
something you can see *already happening to others* — so the confounding was a spreading
phenomenon, not a flash event, and it threatened a specific group who feared they "could not
understand one another," not "all the world's languages."[^3] [[Omni 1.22|Omni 1:22]] and
[[Ether 1.3|Ether 1:3]] reinforce that the Jaredite record speaks only of "the tower" and "the
people," never a universal mother-tongue.

### 2. Encephalitis matches the described symptoms — rate: moderate (medical fit)
Viral encephalitis (inflammation of the brain, spread chiefly by mosquitoes and ticks) produces
slurred speech, *anomia* (difficulty finding words), echolalia (reflexive repetition),
perseveration, impaired comprehension, hearing loss, and even muteness — i.e., exactly a
breakdown in *understanding and producing speech* between people, which is how Ether frames the
worry, rather than a sudden multiplication of distinct tongues.[^4]

### 3. Extended flooding fits both the "driven out" and the disease vector — rate: moderate (convergence)
Archaeology attests major floods along the Euphrates (visible flood-plain mud layers in
excavations). A slow, rising, swamp-creating flood would (a) force abandonment of the city —
explaining "driven out" — and (b) breed the mosquitoes that carry encephalitis. Grover adds a
linguistic flourish: several reconstructed roots behind "Babel" (e.g., *bal* "to saturate,"
forms tied to streams, currents, and water emerging from the ground, per Abarim Publications)
cluster around *flooding/saturation*, consistent with a flood-plain event — though he stresses
the tower is "not called Babel in the Book of Ether," that name being a later Hebrew addition.[^5]

### 4. It fits God's consistent modus operandi through natural means — rate: weak (homiletic)
Grover and host [[Cardon Ellis]] frame this as God "working through natural means," as with the
plagues of frogs and locusts, or the thorns-and-thistles curse of Eden — divine judgment
executed through nature, sickness, or human agents. The ancients would read the confounding as
God's punishment precisely *because* its true cause (mosquito-borne virus) was invisible to them;
the insect-virus link was not understood until the 1800s.[^6]

## Steelman of the Opposing View

Several real weaknesses, some conceded by Grover himself ("this has not gone through peer
review"):
- **The traditional reading is the plain sense.** Genesis 11 plainly presents the confounding as
  the *origin of distinct languages*; "mosquito encephalitis" is an external overlay, not exegesis.
- **The ancients would have noticed a plague.** Cardon presses this hard: peoples acutely aware of
  death and disease wrote of frog/locust plagues explicitly, yet *no* plague is mentioned at the
  tower. Grover's reply (the mosquito vector was imperceptible, so it registered only as
  inexplicable divine confounding) is plausible but unfalsifiable.
- **No archaeological confirmation.** Grover grants there is no excavation tying encephalitis or a
  specific flood to ~2500 BC: "it's not supported in the archaeological record, but it's not
  disproven either."
- **The "Babel = flood" etymology is contested.** The mainstream derivation of *Babel* is
  Akkadian *Bāb-ilu* ("gate of God"), reinterpreted by the Hebrew pun *balal* ("to confuse"); the
  water-root cluster is a minority/devotional reading.

**What survives the steelman:** the *modest* core — that the Book of Ether describes a localized
confounding among a specific people who feared mutual unintelligibility, and that this does **not**
require the discredited "one universal language until 2500 BC" myth — stands on the text regardless
of whether the encephalitis mechanism is correct. The specific disease hypothesis is speculative and
explicitly pre-peer-review.
