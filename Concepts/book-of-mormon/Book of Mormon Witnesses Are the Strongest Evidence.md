# Book of Mormon Witnesses Are the Strongest Evidence

## The Claim
The testimony of the Book of Mormon / golden-plate witnesses provides the single most "airtight, unimpeachable" case for the authenticity of the Book of Mormon, the prophethood of [[Joseph Smith]], and the truth claims of the LDS Church. The episode argues this evidence outranks chiasmus, Nahom, Hebraisms, and other "internal" evidences, and that the Church should foreground it [^1][^2].

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. The case is "direct evidence"
McDonald classifies witness testimony as **direct evidence** — evidence that supports the claim "without any further need for further inference" — for three LDS Truth Claims: (1) Joseph possessed golden plates, (2) the plates were translated into English, (3) Joseph and [[Oliver Cowdery]] were given divine authority [^3]. *Rating: rhetorically strong but overstated — testimony is still testimony, and "direct" here means eyewitness, not incontrovertible.*

### 2. The printing-press argument
Unlike ancient religious founders, Joseph Smith started his movement "during the age of the printing press," so "we have access to myriad records and statements from and about these witnesses," enabling a fuller reliability assessment than for, e.g., the Gospel witnesses [^4]. *Rating: a genuine methodological advantage; the documentary base on the witnesses is unusually rich.*

### 3. Convert testimony of the presenters
[[Jonah Barnes]] frames this as the episode's payoff, with the host comparing it to "drinking from a fire hose." Barnes recounts that he once thought the witnesses "don't really add anything," then reversed: "God knew what he was doing" [^5]. *Rating: this is testimonial framing, not evidence — relevant to the rhetoric, not the proof.*

## Steelman (Opposing View)
A critic grants that the witnesses are well-documented and sincere, then notes that **sincerity of an eyewitness establishes the witness's belief, not the truth of what was believed** — the co-host repeatedly presses exactly this point ("sincere belief doesn't mean that the object of belief is true") [^6]. A critic also notes the "direct evidence" label hides the inferential leap from "saw shining plates / had a vision" to "an ancient Nephite record translated by God." The episode's own concession that character/competency analysis is "the least concrete" historically (see [[Witnesses Never Recanted Despite Estrangement]]) limits how decisively the case can be pressed.

## Best Quotes
> "The Book of Mormon witnesses provide the most airtight, just unimpeachable case for the veracity of the Book of Mormon, Joseph Smith as a prophet, and... the church as a whole as it is today." [^1]

> "The Book of Mormon witnesses is the greatest evidence for the claim of prophethood of Joseph Smith and the authenticity of the Book of Mormon." [^2]

> "Joseph Smith dared to start his religion during the age of the printing press, meaning we have access to myriad records and statements from and about these witnesses." [^4]
