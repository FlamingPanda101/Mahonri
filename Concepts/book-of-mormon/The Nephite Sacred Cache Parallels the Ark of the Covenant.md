# The Nephite Sacred Cache Parallels the Ark of the Covenant

## The Claim

Historian [[Don Bradley]], on [[Ward Radio]], argues that the **cache of sacred relics handed down among the Nephites and deposited by Moroni** is a point-by-point parallel to the **Ark of the Covenant** and its associated tabernacle relics — and that the Liahona belongs to this cache (per Katherine Smith Salisbury, the Liahona was in the stone box). The parallels:

- **Container.** The Ark is a gold box housing stone tablets God touched on the mount; the stone box is a *stone* "ark" housing *gold* plates and the interpreter-stones God touched on the mount ([[Ether 3.14|Ether 3]]) — an inversion (gold/stone ↔ stone/gold). Martin Harris himself used the word "ark" for the stone box.
- **Oracle stones.** The biblical **Urim and Thummim** ([[Exodus 28.30|Exodus 28]]) attach to the high priest's breastplate; the Nephite **interpreters** are mounted on a breastplate in the box.
- **Manna ↔ Liahona.** [[Hebrews 9.11-12|Hebrews 9]] places a pot of **manna** in the Ark; manna was a round ball found on the ground each morning that gave sustenance. The **Liahona** likewise appeared one morning as a round ball on the ground outside Lehi's tent and led the company to sustenance — the same function, so it is placed in the Nephite ark as the manna was in the biblical one.
- **Sword.** The **sword of Goliath** was kept in the biblical tabernacle behind the priestly garment; the **[[Laban|sword of Laban]]** is kept in the Nephite cache, wielded by Nephi (the new David/Joshua) — see [[The Sword of Laban Was the Sword of Joshua Forged by Joseph in Egypt]].[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. The biblical inventory is accurate — rate: strong (textual)
[[Hebrews 9.11-12|Hebrews 9]] does list the pot of manna (and Aaron's rod and the tablets) in connection with the Ark; the Urim and Thummim are on the high priest's breastplate ([[Exodus 28.30|Exodus 28]]); and the sword of Goliath was indeed kept at the sanctuary (1 Samuel 21). The biblical half of each parallel checks out.[^1]

### 2. The functional manna–Liahona match is striking — rate: moderate
The specific convergence — a round ball, appearing on the ground in the morning, that sustains a migrating Israelite company — is a genuine and non-obvious functional parallel, not merely a loose thematic echo, which is what gives the "ark cache" reading its force.[^1]

### 3. The cache is attested as a unit — rate: moderate
Multiple early sources (Martin Harris's "ark" language; Katherine Smith Salisbury on the Liahona in the box) treat the relics as a single sacred deposit, supporting Bradley's reading of them as a tabernacle-style assemblage rather than unrelated artifacts.[^1]

## Steelman of the Opposing View

A critic raises method worries. (a) **Parallelomania risk** — with enough creativity, almost any sacred objects can be mapped onto the Ark; the manna–Liahona match is strong, but the gold/stone "inversion" and the sword parallel are looser and could be over-read. (b) **Source dependence** — the claim that the Liahona was *in the box* leans on later family recollection (Salisbury), and "Martin Harris called it an ark" is doing interpretive work. (c) **Direction of influence** — even if the parallel is real, a skeptic attributes it to a 19th-century author steeped in the King James Bible deliberately echoing the tabernacle, not to ancient provenance. A Latter-day Saint reply: the parallel is offered as *internal coherence and typology*, not as standalone proof of antiquity — it shows the Book of Mormon's relic-theology is deeply, structurally biblical (the same God furnishing each dispensation's "ark"), and the manna–Liahona correspondence in particular is precise enough (round ball / morning / ground / sustenance) to be more than a generic echo. The typological reading stands regardless of how one rules on provenance.
