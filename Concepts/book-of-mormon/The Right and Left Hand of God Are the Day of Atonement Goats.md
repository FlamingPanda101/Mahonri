# The Right and Left Hand of God Are the Day of Atonement Goats

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Dave Butler|David Butler]] argues that the **"right hand / left hand of God"** in [[Mosiah 5.8|King Benjamin's sermon (Mosiah 5:9-10)]] — and the **sheep on the right / goats on the left** in [[Matthew 25.31-46|Matthew 25:31-46]] — encode the **Day of Atonement two goats** ([[Leviticus 16|Leviticus 16]]). On Yom Kippur the high priest casts lots over two identical goats: the **right-hand lot** makes one *la-Yahweh* (slain, atoning); the **left-hand lot** makes the other *la-Azazel* (driven into the wilderness, hurled into a pit). Reading the Hebrew *lamed* as "as," to have the right-hand lot drawn is to *be* Yahweh — "to become godlike," called by the name of Christ; the left-hand lot means being called "by some other name," which Butler says Benjamin discreetly leaves unnamed "because the only other name is Azazel." This extends the vault's two-goats template ([[Barabbas and Jesus Are the Two Goats of the Day of Atonement]], [[Laban and Nephi Enact the Two Goats of the Day of Atonement]]) into Mosiah 5 and Matthew 25.[^1][^2]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Mosiah 5:9-10 sets right hand against "some other name."** Benjamin: those who know the name are "found at the right hand of God," but "whosoever shall not take upon him the name of Christ must be called by some other name; therefore he findeth himself on the left hand of God." Butler: "the only other name is Azazel" — to have the la-Yahweh lot is to "be called by the name of Christ... which is to say Yahweh." See [[The Name Written in the Heart Is the Yahweh Lot of Atonement]]. *Rating: the right/left + Christ-name/other-name structure is in the text; the Azazel identification is the inference.*[^1]
2. **Matthew 25 is the Day of Atonement enacted, not a farm scene.** The Son of Man coming in glory with the holy angels ([[Matthew 25.31-46|Matt 25:31]]) "is the same scene we saw in Revelation... this is the day of atonement," the temple host with the linen — "this is actually not a farm story, this is a temple story." *Rating: the enthronement-judgment scene does echo temple/Revelation imagery; the Yom-Kippur identification is interpretive.*[^3]
3. **Matthew's "innovation": sheep on the right, goats on the left.** Butler flags that Leviticus has goat-vs-goat, but Matthew puts the **sheep** (the flock of the Lord, the sacrifice) on the right and the **goats** (driven out) on the left — possibly "an innovation," "an old memory," or textual development. So the right hand = the sacrificed flock of Yahweh; the left = those driven out. *Rating: a candid acknowledgment that the mapping is not one-to-one; the proposal is tentative.*[^2]
4. **Continuity with the established two-goats pattern.** The claim sits in the vault's existing cluster where the two identical goats (one slain as Yahweh, one expelled as Azazel) underlie the Passion ([[Barabbas and Jesus Are the Two Goats of the Day of Atonement|Barabbas/Jesus]]) and 1 Nephi 4 ([[Laban and Nephi Enact the Two Goats of the Day of Atonement|Laban/Nephi]]). *Rating: situates the claim within an established temple template, strengthening plausibility while inheriting its contested premises.*[^4]

**Steelman of the skeptical counter.** "Right hand / left hand" is *ubiquitous* honor-language across the Bible (right = favor/blessing, left = disfavor) and needs no Yom-Kippur subtext; Matthew 25's sheep/goats is a pastoral simile about a shepherd separating a mixed flock at evening, not a coded Leviticus 16 rite (note Matthew has *sheep* vs *goats*, not two goats — Butler concedes the mismatch). "Azazel = the only other name" assumes the *later* Enochic demonization of Azazel and reads it into Benjamin's "some other name," which the text leaves open precisely so as *not* to specify. **A Latter-day Saint reply:** the cumulative two-goats pattern (identical figures, one chosen/sacrificed and one expelled, the name/lot determining which) recurs widely enough that its appearance behind right/left and sheep/goats is more than coincidence; the claim is offered as the *best unifying* reading of the name/lot motif in Mosiah 5, held provisionally alongside the rest of the vault's Day-of-Atonement cluster.
