# The Lesser Portion and the State of Condemnation

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Todd McLauchlin]] argues that the Book of Mormon's own self-description as a **"lesser portion"** ([[3 Nephi 19|3 Nephi 26:6-11]]) entails that Latter-day Saints today remain in a **state of condemnation** until they obtain "the greater things." He calls 3 Nephi 26 "the most avoided scripture in the Book of Mormon." [^1] The logic:
- Christ taught the Nephites far more than was written; "there cannot be written in this book even a hundredth part" ([[3 Nephi 19|3 Nephi 26:6]]), and what we have is deliberately "a lesser part... to try their faith" — if they believe, "then shall the greater things be made manifest"; if not, "withheld from them unto their condemnation." [^2]
- Therefore, McLauchlin reasons **"by deduction,"** since the church does **not yet possess** the greater portion — whether individually (by revelation, in the [[The Brother of Jared Models the Endowment Ascent Through the Veil|brother-of-Jared pattern]]) or collectively (the sealed plates remaining untranslated) — "we can know... that we're in a state of condemnation." [^3]
- He ties this to the open question of [[D&C 84.33-44|D&C 84]]'s condemnation ("are we still under condemnation? ... we're not ascended up to heaven yet"). Crucially, he stresses this **condemnation is *not* final judgment** — it means "being in a state of sinfulness, which we all are," and "the punishment is not having the knowledge, the thing." [^4]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **The lesser/greater + condemnation language is explicit.** [[3 Nephi 19|3 Nephi 26:9-10]] genuinely says the greater things are withheld "unto their condemnation" if the lesser is not believed — McLauchlin is reading the text's own words, not importing the category. *Rating: directly textual.* [^2]
2. **The "try their faith" frame is the chapter's stated purpose.** "I will try the faith of my people" ([[3 Nephi 19|3 Nephi 26:11]]) supplies the conditional structure (faith → greater things) the argument runs on. *Rating: solid.* [^2]
3. **The non-final reading of "condemnation" is a fair gloss.** Treating condemnation as a remediable *state* (sinfulness / withheld light) rather than damnation is consistent with [[D&C 84.33-44|D&C 84:54-57]]'s usage, where the remedy is to repent and "remember... the Book of Mormon." *Rating: defensible.* [^4]
4. **It links to the withheld-artifacts theme.** The sealed portion as a presently-withheld "greater thing" coheres with [[The Sacred Book of Mormon Artifacts Were Withdrawn Not Lost]] — the greater record is *reserved*, awaiting faith, not destroyed.

**Steelman of the skeptical counter.** The **deduction is shaky**: "we lack the greater portion → therefore we are under condemnation" treats *not having received* the sealed record as equivalent to *failing the faith-test that withholds* it, but [[3 Nephi 19|3 Nephi 26]] conditions the greater things on the *Gentiles' belief of the lesser*, not on whether any individual has had a personal theophany — the timing of the sealed plates is the Lord's, and the church's possession of them is eschatological, so present non-possession need not imply present condemnation. The official LDS reading of [[D&C 84.33-44|D&C 84]]'s "condemnation" is that it was *lifted* (or is liftable) by taking the Book of Mormon seriously (so taught by President Benson), which cuts against a blanket "we are all condemned" inference. And "the most avoided scripture" is rhetorical — 3 Nephi 26 is openly discussed in LDS curriculum. A measured reply: McLauchlin explicitly de-fangs the word ("not final judgment... a state of sinfulness we all are in"), so his claim is closer to "we have not yet risen to the fulness offered us" than "we are damned"; and the chapter *does* tie withholding to condemnation, so the **direction** of his reading — that the canon holds back a greater portion pending greater faith — is the text's own, even if the strict deduction overreaches.

## Best Quotes

> "If you do not receive the greater portion, it's because I'm trying your faith and you've not exercised faith. We are in condemnation... we can therefore know by deduction that we're in a state of condemnation because we do not yet possess the greater things." [^3]

> "Condemnation is not a final state... it just means being in a state of sinfulness, which we all are... the punishment is not having the knowledge, the thing." [^4]
