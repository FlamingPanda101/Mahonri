# Volcanic Eruptions May Be the Cloud That Guided Ancient Israel and the Jaredites

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Jerry Grover]] proposes that the "cloud" by which the Lord led the Jaredites ([[Ether 2]]) was, at key moments, a **volcanic eruption** — God working through natural law rather than instead of it. His pattern: wherever scripture has the Lord speak or lead from a cloud, a volcano may be in view — Sinai (which some scientists read as a volcano), the destruction of [[3 Nephi 9|3 Nephi]], and the pillar of cloud/fire of the Exodus. [^1] Applied to Ether: the valley of Nimrod, the brother of Jared's mount (Shelem), and the later Mesoamerican legs all sit near volcanoes (Teide in the Canaries; Mount Pelée; Pico de Zaza) that were **erupting in the relevant timeframe** (eruption dates being independently datable). A volcanic plume on the open ocean would function as a navigational "signal flare," especially at the point where the vessels needed to exit the North-Equatorial current into the Caribbean current. [^2]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **"Led by a cloud / God speaks from a cloud" is a real scriptural pattern.** The pillar of cloud and fire ([[Exodus 14.17-18|Exodus 13–14]]) and the voice from the cloud are canonical; reading these as possibly volcanic is a recognized (if minority) scholarly move for Sinai. *Rating: the pattern is textual; the volcanic identification is a hypothesis.* [^1]
2. **Eruption timing is independently datable.** Volcanism leaves a datable record, so the claim "volcano X was active circa 2500 BC" is in principle checkable rather than ad hoc — Grover anchors the navigational "cloud" to specific candidate volcanoes along the proposed route. *Rating: a genuine empirical handle, though the correlation to the voyage remains inferential.* [^2]
3. **It dovetails with the Atlantic route.** The volcanic-cloud mechanism is the navigational engine of [[The Jaredites Took an Atlantic Crossing Staged at the Canary Islands]] and parallels the vault's naturalistic reading of [[3 Nephi 9|3 Nephi]]'s catastrophe (cf. [[The New Madrid Earthquake Explains Third Nephi's Darkness Without Volcanoes]], which prefers seismic over volcanic). *Rating: coherent within the model; note the vault itself hosts a competing naturalistic account of 3 Nephi.*

**Steelman of the skeptical counter.** The hosts press the obvious objection directly: **why not just let the cloud be miraculous?** Like the burning bush or the Exodus pillar of fire, a guiding cloud needs no volcano — and inserting one risks a "strat-cumulus that went all the way around the earth" absurdity if pushed too hard. Correlating *specific* erupting volcanoes to *specific* legs of a barely-described voyage is unfalsifiable pattern-fitting; the text never mentions ash, lava, or eruption. A measured reply: Grover explicitly does **not** deny the miracle ("it's not that it wasn't miraculous") — his method asks whether a natural mechanism *could* underlie the described phenomenon, which for some believers makes the account "more real," a case of God working "in an interesting way by natural laws." The claim is offered as a *possible* naturalistic substrate, not a replacement for divine agency.

## Best Quotes
> "Whenever you're talking about being led in a cloud... I'm just saying, okay, that was an understood concept. Sinai — some scientists propose it's actually a volcano." [^1]

## LDS Connection
The note models the vault's "naturalistic-but-not-anti-miraculous" apologetic: identifying a plausible physical mechanism (a volcanic plume as the guiding cloud) without removing God's hand, the same move the vault makes for the shining stones ([[The Shining Stones Were Heated From Translucent Minerals Not Smelted Crystal]]) and for the natural-glass answer to the transparency anachronism.
