# The Synagogue Is a Post-Exilic Institution Absent From the Old Testament

## The Point

The critic charge: the Book of Mormon mentions "synagogues" (e.g., among the Nephites in Alma), but the synagogue is a post-exilic — really a Second-Temple/Hasmonean — institution, so its appearance in a record set ~600–400 BC is an anachronism.

The rebuttal does not dispute the dating; it *uses* it. The synagogue genuinely is late: the word never appears in the Old Testament, and there is no confirmed archaeology for synagogue buildings before the 1st century BC. But that cuts against critics, not the Book of Mormon. The Nephite "synagogues" are best read as a translation of an ancient assembly/gathering-place rather than the formal Second-Temple building (see [[Translation Renders an Ancient Assembly With a Familiar Word]]), and the broader point is that the institution Jesus knew did not yet exist when [[Lehi]] left Jerusalem.

## Evidence

- **Textual silence.** "The Hebrew Bible covers Israelite history from the patriarchs through the Persian return, and it never once mentions a synagogue. The word isn't anywhere in the Old Testament." Moses, Abraham, David, and Isaiah never attended one [^1]. *Rating: Strong — a verifiable fact about the OT text.*
- **Archaeological silence before the 1st century BC.** [[Yonatan Adler]] documents "no confirmed physical evidence for synagogue buildings in Israel before the 1st century BC," and [[Lee Levine]] of Hebrew University — author of "the definitive study on the subject," also through Yale — "reached the same conclusion completely independently" [^2]. *Rating: Strong — two independent specialists, both Yale-published.*
- **The standard origin story fails.** The common assumption that synagogues arose during the Babylonian exile (after the 586 BC temple destruction) "is a reasonable assumption, but it doesn't hold up" — the institution "doesn't actually appear in the archaeological record when it's supposed to" [^3]. *Rating: Moderate — an argument from absence, but a sustained and cross-checked one.*

## Best Quotes

> "The Hebrew Bible covers Israelite history from the patriarchs through the Persian return, and it never once mentions a synagogue. The word isn't anywhere in the Old Testament." [^1]

> "Adler documents that there is no confirmed physical evidence for synagogue buildings in Israel before the 1st century BC. Lee Levine... who wrote what is considered the definitive study on the subject, also published through Yale, reached the same conclusion completely independently." [^2]

## LDS Connection

When [[Lehi]]'s family departed Jerusalem (~600 BC), "the synagogue didn't exist yet. The Pharisaical interpretation system hadn't developed yet." So when people say the Book of Mormon "doesn't look like ancient Judaism," they are right — "it doesn't look like the Judaism that was assembled after Lehi left, which is exactly what you'd expect from a record originating when and where the Book of Mormon says it did" [^4]. This dovetails with [[Deuteronomistic Reform Explains the Book of Mormon Theology]]: the text reflects the pre-reform religious world, not the late Second-Temple one critics measure it against.
