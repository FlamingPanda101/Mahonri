# Book of Mormon Evidences

"Book of Mormon Evidences" is an umbrella category for the empirical and textual arguments offered for the ancient historicity of the Book of Mormon — including geographic correspondences (e.g. [[Nahom and NHM Converge with Nephis Eastward Turn|Nahom/NHM]]), the shrinking-anachronism and "reverse-anachronism" data, the witness testimony, and the dictation feat. It functions as a topical hub for the vault's historicity arguments rather than a single claim.

## Relevance

This note exists as a planned category landing page: the vault's home map of content lists [[Book of Mormon Evidences]] among the topic hubs to be filled out as the library grows. It gathers the evidentiary positions that bear on whether Joseph Smith could have produced the text naturalistically, drawing on venues such as the [[Interpreter Foundation]] and [[BYU Studies]].
