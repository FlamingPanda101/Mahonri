# The Jericho Steel Sword Reverses the Nephi Steel Anachronism

## The Claim

The single most-repeated Book of Mormon anachronism — that Nephi could not have wielded a steel sword (Laban's, "the most precious steel," [[1 Nephi 4.9|1 Nephi 4:9]]) around 600 BC because steel "was not known to man" — has been *reversed* by an actual artifact: an iron, carburized (hardened) sword dated to roughly Nephi's day, excavated in 1982 at Jericho, about 17 miles from where Nephi grew up. The objection is no longer "alleged"; it is overturned in the right place and the right period.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. The find matches the text on date, material, and geography
By His Fruits reports the sword as "iron hardened steel," dated to "Nephi's day, 600 BC," found at Jericho — "only 17 miles from where Nephi grew up." Carburized iron *is* a form of steel; the convergence of period, material, and proximity to Lehi's Jerusalem is exactly what the text would require.[^1]

> **Evidence rating:** Moderate–strong — a real, datable archaeological object answering a specific objection; the inference depends on the dating and on "precious steel" being satisfied by carburized iron rather than modern crucible steel.

### 2. It converts the textbook objection into evidence for the text
The steel sword was, for over a century, the *flagship* anachronism (a 1911 critic led with it; see [[The Three Types of Anachronism - Verified Alleged and Reversed]]). A find that places carburized-steel blades in the southern Levant by ~600 BC removes the impossibility claim and turns the item into a point of incidental accuracy.[^1]

> **Evidence rating:** Moderate — strong against the "impossible" framing; it shows steel was *available*, not that this particular sword is Laban's.

### 3. It fits the broader metallurgical trend
The reversal is not isolated: New World metallurgy is also trending earlier toward Book of Mormon times (see [[Mesoamerican Metallurgy Is Trending Earlier Toward Book of Mormon Times]]), so the steel reversal is one instance of a pattern, not a one-off rescue.

## Steelman of the Opposing View

A critic can press several points. (a) **Old World vs. New World:** the Jericho sword answers *Laban's* sword (forged in the Near East before Lehi left Jerusalem), but the Book of Mormon also describes steel and "swords" *in the Americas* centuries later, where the metallurgical evidence is far weaker — so the Jericho find does not resolve every steel reference. (b) **"Precious steel" vs. carburized iron:** whether "the most precious steel" is satisfied by early carburized iron, or implies a more refined product, is a translation-and-definition question (cf. [[Translation Introduces Anachronism Even in Divine Translation]]). (c) **Provenance and dating:** a single excavated blade's date and metallurgy can be contested. The measured claim is that the find removes the *categorical* objection ("steel did not exist") for the Old World setting of 1 Nephi, which is precisely where the original 1911 charge was aimed — not that it proves the Americas had a steel industry.

## Best Quotes

> "That Israelite sword dates back to Nephi's day, 600 BC. It is made of iron hardened steel and was found in 1982. It's found in Jericho. Jericho is only 17 miles from where Nephi grew up. That is a reversed anachronism." [^1]

> "So, it's no longer alleged. It's been proven to be correct in the correct time period." [^1]
