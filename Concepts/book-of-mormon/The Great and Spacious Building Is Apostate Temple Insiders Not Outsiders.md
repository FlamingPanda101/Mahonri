# The Great and Spacious Building Is Apostate Temple Insiders Not Outsiders

#type/claim

## The Claim
The popular reading of [[Lehi]]'s dream treats the mockers in the "great and spacious building" as outsiders — "the non-members, all the people that make fun of the Mormons." [[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)|David Butler]] argues the opposite: the building is the corrupted **Jerusalem temple**, and its richly-dressed occupants are **initiates of the temple establishment** who have gone wicked — the priestly, royal party that wants the Visionary Lehi dead.

## Supporting Evidence
1. **"Fine" clothing is temple language.** Across the KJV Old Testament the word "fine" is applied "over and over, above everything else" to "things that are made for and used in the temple" — the priest's garments and the temple furniture. So the building's people in "exceedingly fine" apparel are dressed in *priestly/temple* clothing, not merely worldly finery. (Butler flags that "fine" does not translate a single Hebrew word, raising a separate question about the nature of the Book of Mormon translation.) [^1]
2. **Lehi is fleeing the temple establishment that wants him dead.** Lehi is "one of the Visionary men... on the run because the Jews at Jerusalem — the royal tribe of Judah who are in charge of the temple and the military — want to kill him." His dark vision therefore teaches that "the temple has been seized and is controlled by corrupt people." The mockers in the building are the very priests jeering at the tree. [^2]
3. **The building sits in temple space.** Read with [[Lehi's Dream Encodes the Three Temple Rooms by Hebrew Name-Puns]], the "great and spacious building... above all the earth" is the *heikhal*; its height matches the temple's ascent ideology. The mockers are thus positioned *inside* the temple structure, not outside the religious world. [^3]
4. **The mockery is religious, not secular.** Butler ties this to his rereading of [[The Northern Lineage Explains Laman and Lemuel and the Brass Plates|Laman and Lemuel]]: they are not "atheists who think dad's a loser" but devout deuteronomist patriots "on the side of the priests," who "go to that building and mock the tree." The conflict in the dream is intra-religious — reformed temple religion against the older Visionary tree-of-life religion. [^4]
5. **"Strange" means *foreign*, not merely odd.** In the later Mike-and-Dave Deuteronomy episode, Butler presses the philology: when Nephi's summary says the lost "wandered in strange roads" and entered "that strange building" ([[1 Nephi 8|1 Nephi 8]]), the KJV "strange" renders the Hebrew *neṣer* or *zar*, which mean **foreign**, not "weird" or "unexpected." So the corruption of the temple is being attributed to *foreign influence* — fitting Weinfeld's case that the Deuteronomist scribes borrowed the intellectual culture (and even word-for-word treaty curses) of the Assyrian superpower to the north. The building is "strange" because the temple "has been turned into a strange [foreign] building." [^5] *(Moderate — the lexical claim is checkable; tying it specifically to Assyrian influence is the interpretive layer, drawn from [[Deuteronomy Was Patterned After the Vassal Treaty of Esarhaddon|Weinfeld's treaty thesis]].)*

**Steelman.** A critic notes the text never says the building *is* the temple, and that "great and spacious building" most naturally reads as a symbol of worldly pride (Nephi's own later gloss calls it "the pride of the world," 1 Nephi 11:36). **But** Butler's reading does not deny the pride theme; it specifies *whose* pride — "the wealth and arrogance of those in power" inside the captured temple — and the "fine"-as-temple-vocabulary datum is a concrete textual hook that the generic "worldly pride" reading leaves unexplained.

## Best Quotes
> "Everything that's 'fine' is associated with the temple. So essentially... those people in the great and spacious building are the initiates — those who are a part of the group — and who yet are wicked." [^1]

> "His vision is teaching us that the temple has been seized and is controlled by corrupt people... the temple priests are jeering at him." [^2]

## LDS Connection
This reframes the dream from a generic believers-vs-world tableau into a specific indictment of the [[Hezekiah Josiah and the Deuteronomist Reforms|reformed Jerusalem temple]] of Lehi's day, consistent with the larger thesis that [[Lehi]] preserves a pre-reform temple religion the establishment had abandoned. It pairs with [[Lehi's Dream Encodes the Three Temple Rooms by Hebrew Name-Puns]] (the building's *name*) and with [[Nephi's Tree Is Asherah the Divine Mother]] (what the mocked tree *is*).
