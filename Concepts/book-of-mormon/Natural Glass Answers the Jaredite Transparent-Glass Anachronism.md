# Natural Glass Answers the Jaredite Transparent-Glass Anachronism

#type/claim

## The Claim
A standard critical objection: [[Ether 3.14|Ether 3:1]] describes stones "transparent as glass," but in ~2500 BC artisans could not make *transparent* glass (early manufactured glass was colored/opaque), so the comparison is an anachronism betraying a 19th-century author. [[Jerry Grover]] answers that the Jaredites need not have known *manufactured* transparent glass — they could have known **naturally occurring transparent glass**: [^1]

- **Fulgurites** — hollow glass tubes formed when **lightning strikes sand** (e.g., in the Sahara); brittle, translucent-to-transparent.
- **Tektites and Libyan Desert Glass** — natural glass formed by **meteoric impacts**, known and collected anciently as "desert pearls."

So "transparent as glass" is a reference to a real natural substance an ancient Near-Eastern people could have seen, not to a technology they lacked. (Grover even floats that "the finger of the Lord" forming glass could echo *lightning* striking sand.) The Canary Islands themselves had natural (volcanic) glass, though not necessarily transparent. [^2]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **Natural transparent glass genuinely exists and was anciently known.** Fulgurites (lightning-fused sand) and tektites / Libyan Desert Glass (impact glass) are real geological phenomena; Libyan Desert Glass was prized in antiquity (a scarab of it sits in Tutankhamun's pectoral). *Rating: strong, externally verifiable geology.* [^1]
2. **The objection conflates "glass" with "manufactured glass."** The critic's premise is that *transparency required technology the Jaredites lacked*; pointing to natural transparent glass dissolves the premise without special pleading — the text says "as glass" (a comparison), not "manufactured glass." *Rating: a clean logical answer to the stated objection.* [^2]
3. **It pairs with the shining-stones argument.** Together with [[The Shining Stones Were Heated From Translucent Minerals Not Smelted Crystal]], it removes the need for any anachronistic glassmaking from the brother of Jared's account, and fits the vault's reverse-anachronism theme ([[Confirmed Anachronisms Are Reverse-Anachronisms Joseph Could Not Have Known]]). *Rating: coherent and additive.*

**Steelman of the skeptical counter.** A critic may grant the geology yet argue it doesn't rescue the *narrative*: the text implies the brother of Jared *made* clear stones to a degree (he "did molten out of a rock"), and naturalistic glass is being introduced to patch a gap the author likely never considered — a *possibility* argument, not evidence the author knew about fulgurites. The Canary glass, Grover concedes, "wasn't necessarily transparent." A measured reply: the claim's burden is modest and defensive — to show the "transparent glass = anachronism" charge is *not decisive*, because transparent glass existed in nature within the ancient world's reach. It shifts the item from "impossible/anachronistic" to "possible," which is exactly the weight-of-evidence move the model makes throughout (cf. [[The Jaredites Took an Atlantic Crossing Staged at the Canary Islands]]).

## Best Quotes
> "There was natural glass — transparent glass that occurred naturally. Lightning would strike sand, like in the Sahara, and create glass... that's how you create glass out of sand." [^1]

> "Tektites and Libyan Desert glass are formed by meteoric impacts; fulgurites are the glass made when lightning strikes sand — so it was known." [^2]

## LDS Connection
The note exemplifies the vault's preferred handling of Book of Mormon "anachronisms": rather than denying the detail, locate a real ancient phenomenon that satisfies it, downgrading a confident "this proves fraud" to "this is possible." It complements the broader argument that several alleged anachronisms have turned into *reverse*-anachronisms once the relevant ancient data surfaced.
