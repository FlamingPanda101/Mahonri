# BICC Method of Impeachment

## The Claim
[[Brett McDonald]]'s framework for evaluating witness reliability tests each witness against four possible grounds of impeachment — **B**ias/conspiracy, **I**nconsistent statement, **C**haracter, and **C**ompetency. The claim is that a witness who survives all four cannot reasonably be dismissed, and that all 22 Book of Mormon witnesses survive [^1].

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. The four prongs defined
- **Bias / conspiracy** — "if there is evidence that the witness had ulterior motives to uphold their claim, then the trustworthiness of their claim goes down" (money, power, fame). 
- **Inconsistent statement** — "if inconsistent or contradictory statements were made, it stands to reason that the claim is untrustworthy."
- **Character** — "the witnesses are known to have poor character can weaken the trustworthiness of their claim."
- **Competency** — "if the witness is known to be incompetent, it introduces the possibility that they were duped" [^1]. *Rating: a recognizable adaptation of courtroom impeachment categories; reasonable as a heuristic.*

### 2. Applied source base
The framework draws primarily on two scholarly works: *From Darkness unto Light* by MacKay and [[Gerrit Dirkmaat]], and Richard Lloyd Anderson's *Investigating the Book of Mormon Witnesses* [^2]. *Rating: B-tier underlying sources lend the framework more weight than the video's own tier.*

### 3. The verdict pattern
For every witness the episode reaches the same four-part conclusion: no bias (estrangement, lost money, death threats cut against motive), no inconsistent statement, good character, and demonstrated competency — therefore "reasonable to accept [X] as a reliable witness" [^3].

## Steelman (Opposing View)
The episode itself concedes the **character and competency prongs are "the least concrete" and "subjective"** form of historical analysis (see the presenter's extended riff on how even saints and Nazis attract both praise and slander) [^4]. A critic notes the framework is constructed to be passed: "competency" is defined as ordinary mental fitness rather than expertise in metallurgy or psychology, so it cannot screen out sincere dupes — which is precisely the [[Mass Hallucination Hypnosis Theory Rebutted|hallucination]] thesis. The framework also treats "no impeachment found" as "reliable," shifting the burden onto critics.
