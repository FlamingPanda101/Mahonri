# Haplogroup X Marks a Hebrew Migration to North America - Meldrum

## The Claim
[[Rod Meldrum]] argues that mitochondrial **Haplogroup X** in northeastern Native American populations is positive DNA evidence for a Near-Eastern (Lehite) migration into **North America**, anchoring his Heartland model. He frames it as the answer to the *DNA vs. the Book of Mormon* objection: critics say Native American mtDNA is "Asiatic," but Haplogroup X — he says, present in ~70% of the Algonquian-speaking Miꞌkmaq and also in Israel, Italy, and Tunisia — is the Hebrew marker the critics overlooked.[^1] **Note:** this directly contradicts the vault's [[Haplogroup X2A Is Not Valid Evidence for the Book of Mormon]], in which qualified Latter-day Saint geneticists reject exactly this argument.

## Supporting Evidence (as Meldrum presents it)

### 1. Haplogroup X is non-Asiatic and Old-World-linked
Meldrum recounts that ~2002–2003, after President Hinckley publicly declined to concede "no Hebrew DNA," journal articles (e.g., *American Journal of Physical Anthropology*) reported Haplogroup X in northeastern Native Americans lacking Asiatic markers and shared with populations in Israel, Italy, and Tunisia — which he reads as Israelite.[^2]
> **Evidence rating:** Weak. The Old-World *presence* of X is real, but (per the rebuttal note) American X2A is a deep *cousin* of Near-Eastern X, not descended from it, and dates to ~10,000 BC — millennia before any Book of Mormon migration.

### 2. The Miꞌkmaq concentration
He claims ~70% of the Miꞌkmaq carry Haplogroup X markers "which link them to every Israelite population on the planet," and pairs this with Miꞌkmaq hieroglyphs that (via Wayne May / Shad Verity) resemble the characters document.[^3]
> **Evidence rating:** Weak-to-moderate on the frequency datum (X is genuinely elevated in some northeastern groups); weak on the Israelite inference for the dating/phylogeny reasons above.

### 3. "Pick your anomaly" parity argument
The host presses that Australo-Melanesian DNA shows up anomalously in Central/South America (Graham Hancock; Shad Verity's co-hosts) and could equally support a *Mesoamerican* transoceanic contact — so isn't Meldrum just "choosing his genetic anomaly"? Meldrum's reply leans on the Book-of-Mormon-internal argument (no host population named) rather than disputing the parity directly.[^4]
> **Evidence rating:** The parity objection is fair and partly concedes the method's softness.

## Steelman of the Opposing View
The vault's own [[Haplogroup X2A Is Not Valid Evidence for the Book of Mormon]] is the steelman, and it is decisive on the genetics: (a) American Haplogroup X dates to ~10,000 BC — pre-Jaredite, pre-Lehite — so it cannot mark a 600 BC arrival; (b) phylogenetically it is a *cousin* of Near-Eastern X (shared deep ancestor), not a descendant, consistent with Bering-route entry; and (c) the most motivated experts — population geneticists [[Ugo Perego]] and [[John Butler (DNA scientist)]], who would cite it instantly if valid — explicitly refuse to use it, with Rappleye warning "we don't need to use bad junk data... to support the Book of Mormon." Meanwhile, the *defensive* DNA case does not need Haplogroup X at all: founder effect, bottleneck, and drift already predict no detectable Lehite signal ([[Founder Effect and Genetic Drift Erase a Small Founding Signal]]), and "no DNA" is not a test until one specifies a signature ([[No DNA Evidence Requires Specifying What Signature to Expect]]). So Meldrum's flagship evidence is, by the assessment of faithful LDS geneticists themselves, not usable; the honest Latter-day Saint position treats DNA as neutral, not as Heartland proof.
