# The Quetzalcoatl Parallel Names Moses Not Christ

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
**Objection:** Both books describe a "Messiah visiting the Americas" — [[B.H. Roberts]] glossed the *View of the Hebrews* figure as "the white-bearded Mexican Messiah" Quetzalcoatl, paralleling the Book of Mormon's visit of the resurrected Christ to the Nephites.[^1]

**Rebuttal:** Another Category-1 non-parallel, and a double failure. First, the [[Book of Mormon]] "never mentions the name Quetzalcoatl." Second, and decisively, in *View of the Hebrews* Quetzalcoatl is **not** Jesus Christ at all — [[Ethan Smith]] identifies him as **Moses**.[^2]

## The Rebuttal

1. **The name is absent from the Book of Mormon (rated: strong, textual).** "Book of Mormon never mentions the name Quetzalcoatl. He never shows up."[^2] LDS amateurs and enthusiasts have linked Quetzalcoatl to Christ, but that is later apologetic enthusiasm, not the Book of Mormon text.
2. **Ethan Smith's Quetzalcoatl is Moses, not Christ (rated: strong, primary).** Ethan Smith asks "Who else could this be but the great lawgiver Moses?" reading the Mexican lawgiver legend as a corruption of Moses giving the law at Sinai.[^2] So the two "messiah" figures are not even the same character. Smoot: "If Joseph Smith were plagiarizing this, he would have then used Moses as the example," not Christ.[^3]
3. **Roberts overreached here (rated: moderate, concession-based).** Smoot, who reveres Roberts, says plainly "he was just stretching there."[^3] *Steelman:* a critic might say both books still reflect a shared cultural fascination with a bearded culture-hero arriving in the Americas, regardless of the figure's identity — a thematic, not verbatim, parallel. Smoot's reply: a parallel that requires swapping Moses for Christ and supplying a name the Book of Mormon never uses is not a parallel; it is parallelomania (see [[The View of the Hebrews Parallels Are Parallelomania]]).

## Best Quotes

> "Book of Mormon never mentions the name Quetzalcoatl. He never shows up. ... In View of the Hebrews, Quetzalcoatl is not Jesus Christ. It is Moses."[^2]

> "Ethan Smith ... says, 'Who else could this be but the great lawgiver Moses?'"[^2]

> "If Joseph Smith were plagiarizing this, he would have then used Moses as the example. ... I'm sorry, I love Elder Roberts, but he was just stretching there."[^3]
