# Doctrine and Covenants Is Harder Than the Book of Mormon for Evangelicals

#type/objection

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
[[Kyle Beshears]] reports that the [[D&C 6|Doctrine and Covenants]], not the Book of Mormon, is the harder LDS text for him as an evangelical. The reason: the D&C presents God "actively speaking through a prophet" in "the first person active voice of Jesus Christ," and a man producing such revelations "bothers me deeply." This is an objection an LDS apologist must engage. [^1][^2]

## The Rebuttal

1. **The objection isolates a genuine structural difference (steelman first).** The Book of Mormon presents itself as an ancient translated record; the D&C presents itself as contemporary, first-person divine speech mediated through Joseph. For someone who denies Joseph's prophetic office, the latter is necessarily more provocative — it is not a record *about* God but God purportedly *speaking now*. [^2] *Rating: a fair statement of the difficulty;* it should be conceded rather than dodged.

2. **First-person divine voice is not unique to Joseph Smith.** The biblical prophets routinely speak in the first-person voice of Yahweh ("Thus says the LORD... I will...") — Isaiah, Jeremiah, and Ezekiel are saturated with it. The evangelical accepts this for canonical prophets; the objection therefore reduces to whether Joseph belongs in that category, not whether first-person revelation is intrinsically suspect. *Rating: strong as a tu quoque-style parity argument* (cf. the canonical prophets at [[Amos 3.7]]).

3. **The objection is candidly bounded by Beshears himself.** He frames it as *his* sticking point given his prior conclusion that Joseph is not a prophet, not as a knock-down disproof — "I have to hold him at arm's length for that reason." [^1] *Rating: this limits the objection's force;* it is a confession of where the evidence stops persuading him, not a demonstration that the D&C is fraudulent.

4. **It coexists with his refusal to call Joseph a fraud.** The same interview has Beshears insisting Joseph was sincere and pastoral (see [[Joseph Smith Was Sincere Not a Fraud]]). The D&C problem, then, is about *authority*, not *honesty* — Joseph may have sincerely believed he received these words. *Rating: moderate;* it reframes the dispute from character to office.

## Best Quotes
> "For Joseph Smith to produce revelations that are in the first person active voice of Jesus Christ, that bothers me deeply. And I have to hold him at arm's length for that reason." [^1]

> "People ask me... what do you think about the Book of Mormon and its relationship with authority? And I say actually the Doctrine and Covenants is harder for me... because of the way that God is presented in those as actively speaking through a prophet." [^2]
