# The Oliver Cowdery Link Is Chronologically Impossible

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
**Objection:** [[Oliver Cowdery]] is the "missing link" who carried *View of the Hebrews* to Joseph Smith. Ethan Smith pastored the Congregational church in Poultney, Vermont, where some of Oliver's stepmother and half-sisters worshiped; perhaps Oliver knew Ethan Smith, perhaps even apprenticed at the Smith & Shute print shop that printed the book, then brought it to Joseph.[^1]

**Rebuttal:** Every link in this "conspiracy board" chain is unevidenced *and* the chain is chronologically impossible. Oliver Cowdery did not meet Joseph Smith until **April 1829, in Harmony, Pennsylvania** — roughly two years *after* Joseph began producing the Book of Mormon text. Oliver "arrives too late to be the missing link."[^2]

## The Rebuttal

1. **No evidence Ethan Smith was Oliver's pastor (rated: strong, documented).** The family attended the congregation before Ethan Smith became pastor (baptized c. 1815–16; Smith arrives early 1820s). "We do not have historical evidence that Ethan Smith was Oliver Cowdery's pastor. ... It is an inference."[^3]
2. **No evidence Oliver was a printer in Poultney (rated: strong, documented).** Claims that Oliver apprenticed at Smith & Shute (Van Wagoner; John L. Brooke, *The Refiner's Fire*) rest on "no evidence." Later sources placing Oliver as a printer locate him in *New York*, "probably conflating him with his cousin." Oliver's own words suggest the Book of Mormon was his first typesetting experience: "How recently I have become a printer."[^4]
3. **The dates make the whole theory too late (rated: strong, chronological).** By April 1829 Joseph had been translating since 1827 with Martin Harris and Emma Smith. Worse, Joseph was reportedly having Moroni visions in September 1823 — *two months before* the first edition of *View of the Hebrews* (December 1823) — and was describing the plates' contents (inhabitants of the Americas) before the 1825 second edition.[^5] *Steelman:* a critic who rejects the supernatural origin story can deny those early dates as later faith-promoting back-dating, collapsing the timing objection; and "no evidence" is not "disproof," so the Cowdery path remains logically possible. Smoot concedes mere possibility ("possible there's aliens on Mars") but insists possibility without concrete linking evidence is worthless for a dependence claim (see [[Direct Literary Dependence Requires Exclusivity]]), and that the same too-late problem sinks the parallel [[Solomon Spalding]]/[[Sydney Rigdon]] theory.

## Best Quotes

> "Oliver arrives too late to be the missing link ... He's already producing content for the Book of Mormon narrative."[^2]

> "We do not have historical evidence that Ethan Smith was Oliver Cowdery's pastor. ... It is an inference we make."[^3]

> "It's all too late ... certainly any link through Oliver Cowdery is going to be too late. ... by then Joseph Smith is already claiming to have the plates."[^5]
