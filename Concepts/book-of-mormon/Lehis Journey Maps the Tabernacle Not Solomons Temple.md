# Lehis Journey Maps the Tabernacle Not Solomons Temple

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Dave Butler|David Butler]] argues (developing chapter 9 of his book *In the Language of Adam*) that [[Lehi]]'s wilderness itinerary is patterned specifically on the **Tabernacle** — Israel's *mobile* sanctuary — rather than Solomon's Temple, and that the named travel-stops mark its zones in order: **Valley of Lemuel** (with the river of Laman) = the **courtyard**; **Shazer** = the **fine-twined-linen door**; the long middle stretch (broken bow, Ishmael's death at Nahom) = the **holy place**; **Bountiful** = **Eden / the holy of holies**. The "tell" that it is the *Tabernacle* (not the temple) is the place-name **Shazer** = *mashzar* ("fine twined linen," *shesh mashzar*), which describes the tabernacle's entry screen — a material referenced in Exodus but not in the temple accounts. The choice of the *mobile* sanctuary is theologically pointed: Lehi's dream shows the temple corrupted, so the temple "goes with him" as a portable tabernacle. This unifies the vault's [[Shazer Names the Fine-Twined-Linen Door of the Tabernacle|Shazer]] and [[Lehi's Dream Encodes the Three Temple Rooms by Hebrew Name-Puns|three-room]] notes into a single itinerary-wide mapping.[^1][^2]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Valley of Lemuel = courtyard (the altar).** Lehi "builds an altar and offers sacrifices... three times, all while they are dwelling in the valley of Lemuel" ([[1 Nephi 2.6|1 Nephi 2]]), and not thereafter — altar-and-laver activity belongs to the tabernacle *courtyard*. Lehi's poem wishing the river of Laman would flow "into the fountain of all righteousness" connects the courtyard water to the holy of holies. *Rating: the three sacrifices in the Valley of Lemuel are textual; the courtyard mapping is the inference.*[^1]
2. **Shazer = the twined-linen door.** "Nothing happens" at Shazer ([[1 Nephi 16.13|1 Nephi 16:13]]) because its only job is to be the *named door*: the root means "to twist/twine," and its Exodus form *mashzar* ("fine twined linen," 21× in Exodus) describes the tabernacle hangings, garments, veil, and entry screen. The LDS edition footnote already glosses Shazer as "twisting." See [[Shazer Names the Fine-Twined-Linen Door of the Tabernacle]]. *Rating: documented in detail in the linked note; an official footnote anchors the twist/twine sense.*[^2]
3. **The middle stretch = the holy place; Bountiful = the holy of holies.** Two incidents (broken bow; Ishmael's death/burial at Nahom) occupy the "big room"; then "8 years go right by" and the party arrives at **Bountiful** = Eden, named for "much fruit" and "wild honey" ([[1 Nephi 17.1|1 Nephi 17:5-6]]), where Nephi builds an ark (the ship), references the Nehushtan, and meets God on a mountain. See [[Eden Means Bountiful So Lehis Journey Ends in the Holy of Holies]] and [[Nahom Is the Tabernacle Room of Mourning and Comfort]]. *Rating: the structural placement is coherent; the room identifications are interpretive.*[^3]
4. **Why the Tabernacle, not the Temple.** Lehi's dream shows the *temple* seized by "greedy mockers," so the subliminal message is the temple goes *with* him — a mobile sanctuary, the Tabernacle. Butler adds that scholars think the Tabernacle description is closer to the actual first temple than Kings/Chronicles (which exaggerate "too many tables and lamps... a cherub throne"). See [[The Tabernacle Is a Retrojection of the Temple]]. *Rating: the theological rationale is suggestive; the Kings/Chronicles-exaggeration point is a defensible critical position Butler endorses.*[^4]
5. **The pattern of named-but-eventless vs. eventful-but-unnamed stops.** Butler notes Nephi gives *names* to some stops where little happens (Shazer) and *events* to others he leaves unnamed (an unnamed mountain) — evidence the names are deliberate "signposting," not a travel journal. *Rating: a real narrative feature; supports intentional patterning.*[^3]

**Steelman of the skeptical counter.** The itinerary survives only in Joseph Smith's English, so the controlling Hebrew puns (Shazer/*mashzar*, Nahom/*nacham*) are reconstructions, and "Shazer" has competing proposed etymologies; "nothing happens at Shazer, therefore it's a coded door" is a gap argument, since terse stops are normal in ancient itineraries. The whole tabernacle mapping is a devotional overlay that a believer can impose after learning the sanctuary's zones — the [[Avoiding Parallelomania Between Ancient Rites and LDS Temple|parallelomania]] worry. And a real desert journey *would* pass an altar-site, a threshold, hardship, and a fertile end without any liturgical intent. **A Latter-day Saint reply:** the mapping is not a single lucky pun but an *ordered* convergence — altar-courtyard → twined-linen door → murmur-and-feast holy place → Eden holy-of-holies — with an *official scriptural footnote* anchoring the Shazer/twine sense and an *independent non-LDS scholar* glossing Eden as "bountiful"; the cumulative, sequential fit is harder to dismiss than any isolated parallel, and it coheres with the broader [[The Book of Mormon Matches Pre-Reform First Temple Theology|first-temple reading]]. The discipline is to keep it functional and provisional, not load-bearing proof.
