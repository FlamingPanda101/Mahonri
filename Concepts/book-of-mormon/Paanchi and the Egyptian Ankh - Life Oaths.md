# Paanchi and the Egyptian Ankh — Life Oaths

## The Claim
**Paanchi** is Egyptian *Pa-ʿankh*, "the living one," from *ʿnkh* (ankh = life). Because *ankh* also yields the verb "to swear (an oath by one's life)," the narrative around Paanchi's faction — who **swear life-oaths "by their everlasting Maker"** — plays on the meaning of his name and stages the birth of the oath-bound secret combinations. [^1]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **The etymology.** *Pa-ʿankh* = "the living one"; the symbol of the ankh (life) is widely recognized. As a verb, *ankh* means "to live / to be alive"; as a noun, "life." Strength: **high** (well-attested Egyptian). [^1]
2. **Ankh and oath-swearing.** One swore an oath *by one's life*, so *ankh* becomes a verb for swearing oaths. Paanchi's supporters "swore life-oaths by their everlasting Maker." Bowen notes he can't know whether the underlying plate-verb was Hebrew (*shaba*) or Egyptian (*ankh*), but the **concept** — swearing by one's life — fits the name's field. Strength: **moderate-high** (conceptual, not lexically certain). [^2]
3. **Narrative function.** Mormon wants to show how the oath-bound **secret combinations** that destroyed both Nephite and Jaredite society *began*; the Paanchi faction is the seed, propped up to seize the judgeship. The "life" wordplay underlines the deadly oaths. Strength: **moderate**. [^2]

**Steelman:** A skeptic grants the *Pa-ʿankh* etymology is strong but argues the "life-oath" connection is an interpretive overlay — the text doesn't say the oaths pun on Paanchi's name, and Bowen himself admits not knowing the underlying verb. The wordplay is therefore *thematic resonance* more than demonstrable pun. The reply: the resonance is still notable given that the one chapter pairs a name meaning "life" with a faction defined by *life-oaths*.
