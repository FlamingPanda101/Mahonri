# The Two-Cumorahs Theory

## The Claim
The "two-Cumorahs" issue is the dispute at the heart of Book of Mormon geography, and a case study in how the same word is used for different things. [[Jonathan Neville]] uses the label "two Cumorahs" (the Mesoamerican side's preferred acronym is "M2C") to describe the Mesoamerican position: that the *real* hill Cumorah of Mormon 6 is somewhere in southern Mexico, while the New York hill where Joseph found the plates is a *separate, falsely-named* "church-history Cumorah." Neville holds there is only **one** Cumorah — in New York.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **The model's actual origin.** Neville traces the southern-Mexico Cumorah to a single RLDS researcher, "Stearns," and his colleague **Louis E. Hills**, who in 1917 published a map relocating Cumorah to southern Mexico because the New York hill was "too far away" from the Central-American ruins. (Neville relishes that the man who "invented the two-hills-Cumorah theory" was literally named *Hills*.) Both the RLDS first presidency and James Talmage's committee declined to adopt any such map.[^1] *(Moderate — the historical lineage is documentable; it shows the model is an interpretive development, not an original teaching.)*
2. **The hosts' clarification of the label.** The hosts press Neville that "two Cumorahs" can be a straw man: Mesoamerican advocates "don't believe there's two Cumorahs — they believe there's one Cumorah" (in Mexico) plus a mistakenly-named New York hill. Neville concedes the framing needs care but maintains that recognizing "a false Cumorah and a real Cumorah" *is* affirming two Cumorahs.[^2] *(This is the note's most useful content: it models charitable disambiguation, which Neville himself elsewhere demands — see [[Clarity Charity and Understanding and the Faith Model]].)*
3. **Joseph Fielding Smith's warning.** As church historian and an apostle, [[Joseph Fielding Smith]] wrote that the two-Cumorahs idea would "cause people to be disturbed in their faith," since the church had taught a New York Cumorah for ~80 years.[^1] *(Weak-to-moderate — an authoritative *concern*, not a doctrinal ruling; later leaders have left geography open.)*

## Steelman of the Opposing View
The Mesoamerican reply is substantive. (a) The Book of Mormon's *internal* geography requires the final battles within a limited region with specific features; if that region is Mesoamerican (the [[The Limited-Geography Reading Predates the DNA Debate|limited-geography model predates the DNA debate]] and is driven by the text, not by embarrassment), then "Cumorah" names that battle-hill, and the New York hill simply acquired the name by association — no deception required, so "false Cumorah" is a loaded mischaracterization. (b) The church's official position is *neutral* on geography; Joseph Fielding Smith's view was personal and not binding, and pinning revelation to a specific hill risks the very faith-disturbance he feared if that hill is later doubted. (c) Genuinely, the label "two Cumorahs" can prejudge the question by implying duplication where advocates posit one battle-Cumorah plus a nickname. The fair takeaway — which both sides on the video reach — is that the dispute should be stated precisely (one battle-hill, debated location) rather than as a caricature, and that it ultimately reduces to [[Oliver Cowdery's Letter VII States Cumorah Is in New York as Fact|whether Letter VII's New York identification is authoritative]].

## Best Quotes
> "They say it's a false Cumorah and a real Cumorah, so it is two Cumorahs."[^2]

> "Joseph Fielding Smith... wrote an editorial saying this idea of two Cumorahs is going to cause people to be disturbed in their faith, because we've been teaching Cumorah's in New York for [80 years]."[^1]

## LDS Connection
"Two Cumorahs" is the flashpoint of the whole Heartland-vs-Mesoamerican debate and a live example of the [[Clarity Charity and Understanding and the Faith Model|clarity-charity-understanding]] method the hosts advocate: both guests and hosts agree the *label* must be used carefully so neither side is straw-manned, even as they disagree on the substance. It depends directly on [[Oliver Cowdery's Letter VII States Cumorah Is in New York as Fact]].
