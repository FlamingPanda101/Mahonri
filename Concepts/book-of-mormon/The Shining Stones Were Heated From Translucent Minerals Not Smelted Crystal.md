# The Shining Stones Were Heated From Translucent Minerals Not Smelted Crystal

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Jerry Grover]] argues the brother of Jared's sixteen shining stones need not be miraculously manufactured crystals. Two moves:

1. **"Molten out of a rock" may mean *heated and cracked*, not smelted.** The original 1830 spelling is **"moulten"** (m-o-u-l-t-e-n), which can mean simply *to heat and break*, distinct from "molten" (m-o-l-t-e-n) implying an industrial melt. So the brother of Jared could have **heated rock in a fire and quenched it to crack translucent crystals out of it** ([[Ether 3.14|Ether 3:1]], "molten out of a rock") rather than smelting glass from scratch. [^1]
2. **The Canary Islands supply candidate minerals.** Grover counts ~30 translucent minerals on the islands sufficient to transmit light; whether they could *generate* light is a separate question, and the text actually places the glow *in the boats* (not, as in popular art, lighting up when touched atop the mount). The Lord could have prepared them to luminesce by natural means — even **kinetic**, like a "shake-flashlight," activated by the barges' rocking. [^2] The two **interpreter** stones are treated separately: a distinct, God-given technology the brother of Jared did *not* quarry, later mounted by the Nephites (Grover argues in platinum, the only anciently-produced metal that resists tarnish). [^3]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **The "moulten" spelling distinction is in the textual record.** The earliest text's orthography supports a *heat-and-fracture* reading over a *smelt-from-nothing* reading — a real, checkable lexical point, not a convenient retrofit. *Rating: solid philological observation.* [^1]
2. **The text really does locate the glow in the barges.** [[Ether 2.23-25|Ether 2]]–[[Ether 3.14|3]] has the Lord touch the stones and they are placed in the vessels to give light; the "lit up atop the mount" image is artistic, not scriptural — so removing that embellishment is faithful to the text. *Rating: accurate to the narrative.* [^2]
3. **It distinguishes the interpreters from the light-stones.** Keeping the seer/interpreter stones as a separate, given technology avoids over-claiming and aligns with the vault's caution that loose "Urim and Thummim" parallels can be overstated (cf. [[The Urim and Thummim Is Not a Real Parallel]]). *Rating: a disciplined distinction.* [^3]

**Steelman of the skeptical counter.** Co-host Josh pushes back that this **drains the miracle**: many readers' plain sense is that the Lord *lit the stones* in answer to a faith-question ("how shall we have light?"), and a shake-flashlight gloss "rains on the parade." Grover's mineral list shows *translucence*, not light-*generation*, so the naturalistic half of the claim is incomplete; and the platinum-spectacles argument layers speculation on speculation. A critic adds that "moulten" is being leaned on hard to do exegetical work. A measured reply: Grover concedes the point is "eye of the beholder" and that he "leans toward the miraculous" on the lighting itself — his aim is only to show the stones are *explainable* (the brother of Jared cracking crystals from heated rock; God then preparing them to glow by some natural law) so the account need not be cartoonish, while the faith-prompting core remains intact.

## Best Quotes
> "The original spelling is m-o-u-l-t-e-n, which is a different meaning — it can just mean to heat and break... he could have just heated rock and busted crystals out of it." [^1]

> "The Lord could have prepared them in a way where they could generate light, like our shake-flashlights... it doesn't necessarily have to be illuminescence." [^2]
