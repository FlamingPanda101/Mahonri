# The Book of Mormon Is Measured Against the Wrong Period

## The Point

The objection "the Book of Mormon's religious world doesn't match ancient Judaism" smuggles in a false benchmark. It measures the text against *Second-Temple* Judaism — synagogues, mikva'ot, Pharisaic tradition — but that system was assembled centuries *after* [[Lehi]] left Jerusalem (~600 BC). The right benchmark is the pre-exilic, pre-reform religion of Lehi's day. Against that benchmark the apparent mismatch becomes expected, and in places the text scores a positive hit. As Arnold puts it, Adler's work "doesn't prove the Book of Mormon is true" — what it does is "dismantle a specific objection," because the Jewish practice critics say it fails to match "wasn't ancient."

## Evidence

- **Off by ~400 years.** "Critics measuring it against Second Temple Judaism are measuring it against the wrong period by roughly four centuries at least, and Adler documented why" [^1]. *Rating: Strong as a logical correction — it follows directly from the late dating of the Second-Temple apparatus.*
- **The mismatch is the expected result.** "When critics say that the Book of Mormon's religious world doesn't match the Second Temple Judaism, they're totally right. It doesn't, because it reflects a tradition that departed before Second Temple Judaism was assembled" [^2]. *Rating: Moderate — reframes absence into corroboration; depends on accepting the Book of Mormon's own chronology.*
- **From absence to positive evidence.** Arnold grants that absence of an anachronism "alone doesn't prove any authenticity," then pivots: the text "isn't just avoiding a mistake," it gets the pre-reform divine-council theology "positively and specifically right about the theological world of pre-reform Jerusalem that no one in 1830 would have had the scholarship to fabricate accurately" [^3]. *Rating: Moderate — the strong claim leans on [[Margaret Barker]]'s contested reconstruction.*

## Best Quotes

> "Critics measuring it against Second Temple Judaism are measuring it against the wrong period by roughly four centuries at least, and Adler documented why." [^1]

> "When critics say that the Book of Mormon doesn't match Judaism, ask them which Judaism. Because the Second Temple system that it doesn't match was itself a late construction. But the pre-exilic theology that it does reflect is what Barker spent her career recovering." [^4]

## LDS Connection

The takeaway Arnold gives LDS viewers is a one-line reframe: "the next time someone tells you that the Book of Mormon doesn't match Judaism, ask them which Judaism" [^4]. The restoration that came through [[Joseph Smith]] "bypassed not just the Catholic councils and the Protestant reformers, but the entire Pharisaical and rabbinic layer as well" [^2]. Honest limit, stated in the source: Barker "interprets that tradition as finding its fulfillment in Christ" and does not endorse the restoration; her *findings*, not her theology, are what carry the argument [^5].
