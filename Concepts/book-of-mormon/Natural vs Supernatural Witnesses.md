# Natural vs Supernatural Witnesses

## The Claim
The Book of Mormon witnesses fall into two categories that, taken together, defeat the major naturalistic theories. **Natural witnesses** had physical contact with the golden plates (hefting, thumbing leaves, hearing the metallic rustle). **Supernatural witnesses** encountered the plates through a divine being such as Angel [[Angel Moroni|Moroni]]. The two-tier structure is itself an argument: the natural witnesses prove the plates physically existed; the supernatural witnesses prove they were of divine origin [^1][^2].

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. The structural pincer argument
"The fact that we have these two separate kinds of witnesses adds validity to the ancient and divine claims." A forger could in principle dupe natural witnesses with hand-crafted plates ([[Metallurgical Fraud Theory Rebutted|metallurgical fraud]]) — but "fabricated metal plates can't make people see visions," so the supernatural witnesses close that escape hatch [^2]. *Rating: a clean logical structure; its force depends entirely on the supernatural reports being genuine and not psychogenic.*

### 2. Each category requires a different counter-theory
Because the two witness types demand different debunkings, a critic must hold **both** metallurgical fraud **and** mass hallucination simultaneously, and the episode argues the theories actually contradict one another (e.g., fraud requires conspiracy; hallucination requires sincere dupes) [^3]. *Rating: a real tension in the critical literature, though a critic can apply different theories to different witnesses without contradiction.*

## Steelman (Opposing View)
The two-tier framing assumes the "supernatural" reports are a separate evidentiary track. A naturalist replies that **subjective visionary experience is exactly what hypnosis/expectancy/religious excitement would produce**, so the supernatural tier is not independent corroboration of physical plates — it is the very phenomenon under dispute. The episode's own John Whitmer discussion (see [[John Whitmer Spiritual Eyes Objection Rebutted]]) shows the natural/supernatural line is blurry even among the witnesses.

## Best Quotes
> "When I say natural, I just mean that they had physical contact with the golden plates in some way, shape, or form... the second type of witnesses are the supernatural witnesses, meaning that they had experience with the plates through non-mortals or divine encounters, like Angel Moroni." [^1]

> "Fabricated metal plates can't make people see visions." [^2]
