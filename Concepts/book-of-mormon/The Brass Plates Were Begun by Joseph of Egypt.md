# The Brass Plates Were Begun by Joseph of Egypt

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Don Bradley]] argues that the [[brass plates]] — the scriptural record [[Laban]] held and that [[Lehi]]'s sons retrieved ([[1 Nephi 5.11|1 Nephi 5]]) — were **begun by [[Joseph of Egypt]]** himself and handed down his descendants' royal/scribal line. The record contains Joseph's own genealogy and his first-person prophecies (quoted by Lehi in [[2 Nephi 3]]), is written in Egyptian, and is custodied by a family of the tribe of Manasseh — all best explained if the patriarch Joseph started the record in Egypt. This is the internal-evidence companion to the claim that the sword Laban held was also a Josephite relic (see [[The Sword of Laban Was the Sword of Joshua Forged by Joseph in Egypt]]); both relics travel together out of Jerusalem and down to Moroni.

## Supporting Evidence
1. **The plates carry Joseph's first-person prophecies, absent from Genesis.** [[2 Nephi 3]] preserves Joseph of Egypt prophesying by name of Moses, a spokesman, a latter-day "choice seer," and the bringing-forth of scripture to his seed — detailed material the King James Genesis (a Judahite line) does not contain. A record holding Joseph's *own* words is naturally one Joseph helped begin. *Rating: strong as an internal datum; the inference "therefore Joseph started it" is reconstruction, but it fits the text's self-presentation.*
2. **The record is written in Egyptian by an Egyptian-raised lineage.** Joseph's sons Manasseh and Ephraim were born to "the daughter of an Egyptian priest" and raised in Pharaoh's court; [[Noel Reynolds]] argues the brass-plate tradition was maintained in Egyptian, which dovetails with [[Nephi]] writing in the language "of the Egyptians" (1 Nephi 1:2). A Josephite-Egyptian scribal origin ties the "Reformed Egyptian" claim to a genealogical fact already sitting in Genesis. *Rating: a strong convergence; see [[The Northern Lineage Explains Laman and Lemuel and the Brass Plates]].*
3. **Custody runs through Joseph's birthright son's tribe.** Lehi learns from the plates that he is a descendant of Joseph through Manasseh; a private archive custodied by Joseph's birthright line is what you would expect if the patriarch initiated it and passed it as an heirloom. *Rating: coherent; the family's tribal identity is stated in the text, the "founding by Joseph" is the added step.*
4. **It parallels the twin-relic descent of the sword.** Bradley's larger model has *two* Josephite relics — the record and the sword — handed down together, a real historical pattern for royal regalia (book + sword + orb). The plates' Josephite origin is the better-evidenced leg that makes the sword's claimed origin antecedently plausible. *Rating: an internally consistent package; its persuasiveness is cumulative.*

**Steelman of the skeptical counter.** The argument is internal and circular at the edges: the only evidence that Joseph of Egypt *began* the plates is that the plates (as the Book of Mormon presents them) contain Joseph-material — which presupposes the record's authenticity to argue for its antiquity. The detailed Joseph-prophecies in 2 Nephi 3 are precisely the sort of thing a 19th-century author writing pseudepigraphically would supply, and the JST expands the very same Genesis-Joseph material, so "absent from Genesis" can be read as "added by Joseph Smith" rather than "preserved from a purer source." **A Latter-day Saint reply:** the claim is offered as the best explanation of a *converging* set of features the text did not have to include — Joseph's genealogy, his named prophecies, the Egyptian language, and Manasseh custody all pointing the same direction — and it interlocks with the independent northern-lineage and Deuteronomist-reform cases ([[Brass Plates Bypassed the Textual Corruption Chain]]). The internal coherence is the evidence; proof is not claimed.
