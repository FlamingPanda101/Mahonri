# Interpreter Foundation

The Interpreter Foundation is an independent, nonprofit Latter-day Saint organization founded in 2012 that publishes faithful scholarship defending and illuminating the scriptures and history of the Restoration. Its flagship venture is the peer-reviewed open-access periodical *Interpreter: A Journal of Latter-day Saint Faith and Scholarship*, alongside conferences, books, and film projects. It emerged from the apologetic tradition associated with the older FARMS/Maxwell Institute milieu, with figures such as Daniel C. Peterson among its leadership.

## Relevance

In the hermes-advocate vault the Foundation is cited as a venue for the empirical apologetic data used in [[Book of Mormon Evidences]] arguments. [[Hayden Carroll]] draws on a [[Matt Roper]] 2025 Interpreter inventory tabulating how many of the Book of Mormon's ancient-world elements were archaeologically confirmable in Joseph Smith's day (~7%) versus today (75%+), the basis of the "reverse-anachronism" argument.
