# The Book of Mormon Was Translated Mosiah-First

## The Claim
[[Mike Day]] argues that the surviving Book of Mormon was *not* dictated in its printed order (1 Nephi → Moroni). After the [[The 116 Pages as Evidence of Foreknowledge|116 pages]] were lost, Joseph Smith and [[Oliver Cowdery]] resumed at the **book of Mosiah** (~April 7, 1829) and worked straight through to Moroni; the small plates (1 Nephi through [[Words of Mormon 1.7|Words of Mormon]]) were translated *last*, in the [[David Whitmer|Whitmer]] home (~June 1829). This order is itself, on Day's account, an evidence of authenticity, because it removes any chance for Joseph to plan early material around later content.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **The dated milestones.** Cowdery arrives April 5, 1829; they begin April 7; on **May 15** they are in [[3 Nephi]] (the verse on baptismal authority that prompts the [[Joseph Smith History 1.68-69|Aaronic priesthood restoration]]); the copyright title page (the last leaf of the plates) is submitted ~June 11; and the small plates are worked on around June 8–10 in Fayette. Historians note ~11 of the 85 calendar days were travel, leaving ~75 working days — and reaching 3 Nephi by mid-May rules out a slow 1-Nephi-first sequence.[^2] *(Moderate-to-strong — the dated benchmarks are documentary; the inference from them is sound.)*
2. **John Whitmer's handwriting on the small plates.** John Whitmer — who lived in Fayette and had never met Joseph before — has handwriting on the small-plates portion of the original manuscript, consistent with those books being dictated last, in the Whitmer home.[^3] *(Strong — a physical, manuscript-based datum, per the Joseph Smith Papers.)*
3. **Words of Mormon explains the seam.** [[Words of Mormon 1.7|Words of Mormon 1:7]] has Mormon append the small plates "for a wise purpose" he does not understand — which, on the LDS reading, is the providential mechanism by which the small plates replace the lost 116 pages, and which fits their being added at the *end* of the dictation.[^4] *(Moderate — internal-text support that coheres with the external evidence.)*

## Steelman of the Opposing View
A critic can grant the translation order and deny the apologetic payoff. (a) The Mosiah-first sequence is *standard* LDS scholarship (it does not originate with Day), and a naturalistic author who plans to write a frame-narrative (1 Nephi–Omni) *last* could deliberately seed forward references into the main text and then satisfy them when composing the prologue — exactly what a skilled novelist building a prequel does. (b) The dated milestones establish *speed*, which is a separate argument (see [[Dictation Speed and Method Argue Against Fabrication]]); order alone does not preclude a retained outline or memory. (c) The manuscript handwriting fixes *where* the small plates were produced, not that their content was unplanned. The believer's stronger move is therefore the *conjunction*: Mosiah-first order **plus** the dictation rate **plus** the no-going-back method (he could not revise) means any cross-reference from the main text to the unwritten small plates had to be held in memory with no draft to consult — which is the argument developed in [[Internal Cross-References to the Unwritten Small Plates]].

## Best Quotes
> "April 7th 1829 Oliver shows up... the evidence indicates that they start in Mosiah. That's where they're starting."[^1]

> "John Whitmer, who lives in Fayette, never met Joseph — his handwriting is on the manuscript... he kind of [transcribes] the small plates. That's mind-blowing."[^3]

## LDS Connection
The Mosiah-first order is the load-bearing premise for the channel's strongest internal-evidence argument: because the small plates came last, the many references to small-plates material scattered from Mosiah to Moroni point *forward* to text Joseph had not yet dictated. It supports the broader case that [[No Naturalistic Source Accounts for the Whole Book of Mormon|no naturalistic source accounts for the whole book]] and complements [[The Detailed Narrative Outranks the Chapter Summary in First Nephi|the structure of 1 Nephi]].
