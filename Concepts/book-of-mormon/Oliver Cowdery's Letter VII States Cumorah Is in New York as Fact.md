# Oliver Cowdery's Letter VII States Cumorah Is in New York as Fact

## The Claim
[[Jonathan Neville]] makes [[Oliver Cowdery]]'s 1835 "Letter VII" the foundation of the New-York-Cumorah case. In that letter — written with Joseph Smith's involvement and republished repeatedly in church periodicals — Cowdery states *as a fact* that the final Nephite and Jaredite battles took place between two hills near Palmyra, New York, the larger of which is the hill Cumorah. Neville argues that to dismiss this as "speculation" is, in effect, to call Cowdery a liar, which destabilizes his other testimony.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **Cowdery framed it as fact, not theory.** Letter VII was written to rebut *Mormonism Unvailed*; Cowdery deliberately spoke in terms of "facts," describing the hill as the largest in the area on the road to Canandaigua, with a parallel ridge about a mile west, "and it was between these two hills" that the battles occurred. "He didn't say 'I think' or 'I guess' or 'I hope' — he said it's a fact."[^1] *(Moderate — the letter does use factual language; whether Cowdery's geographic identification is itself reliable is the disputed point.)*
2. **The witness-credibility lever.** Cowdery is "the only other witness to John the Baptist['s] restoration of the priesthood, Peter James and John, as well as all the temple blessings." Neville argues you cannot accept his testimony of angels while rejecting his factual claim about Cumorah without special pleading: "if they lied about Cumorah, they lied about the translation — what's left?"[^2] *(Weak-to-moderate as logic — a witness can be reliable about a vision yet mistaken about geography; but it is rhetorically forceful within a believing frame.)*
3. **Canonical and family corroboration.** [[D&C 128.20|D&C 128:20]] ("Glad tidings from Cumorah!") names the hill in 1842; [[Lucy Mack Smith]] reports Moroni called it Cumorah on his first visit; and Heber C. Kimball recorded seeing the hill's embankments in 1832. Neville notes the *Saints* history quotes Lucy ~137 times but omits her Cumorah statements — "that's the cherry-picking."[^3] *(Moderate — the sources are real, though all are later reminiscences, and [[Joseph Smith History 1.34|the canonized account]] does not name the hill.)*

## Steelman of the Opposing View
Mesoamerican advocates and many LDS scholars answer that Letter VII is a *non-revelatory* statement: Cowdery never claimed revelation for the geography, so his confident tone reflects the early Saints' shared assumption (a New York hill where the plates were found, casually called "Cumorah"), not a binding fact. (a) The text of the Book of Mormon and the canonized Joseph Smith—History never place the final battles in New York, and indeed never name the deposit hill "Cumorah" — see [[The Limited-Geography Reading Predates the DNA Debate]]. (b) The "they lied / what's left" lever is a false dilemma: being a reliable eyewitness to an angelic visit is logically independent of correctly locating a battlefield from a record one translated but did not survey. (c) The corroborating statements (Lucy, Kimball, even D&C 128) are consistent with the hill being *called* Cumorah by association rather than being the literal Mormon-6 battle site. The believer's fair, narrower point survives the critique: the New-York-Cumorah identification is *early, explicit, and from the most authoritative possible witness*, so treating it as mere folklore — while accepting Cowdery on everything else — does require a principled reason the Mesoamerican side has to supply, not just assert.

## Best Quotes
> "If you don't start with what [Oliver Cowdery] said was a fact — and if anyone would know, it'd be him as much as Joseph Smith... if you say he was lying about that, then you kind of undermine the whole restoration."[^2]

> "They reject everything [Whitmer, Harris, Cowdery] said about Cumorah, they reject everything Lucy said about it — but they accept everything else she said. That's a massive amount of cognitive dissonance."[^3]

## LDS Connection
Letter VII is the linchpin of Neville's whole project (the geography *and* the [[The Two-Cumorahs Theory|two-Cumorahs]] and [[The Times and Seasons Mesoamerican Articles Were Benjamin Winchester Not Joseph Smith|Times-and-Seasons]] arguments hang on it) and a sharp case study in how the vault weighs early, authoritative-but-non-revelatory testimony. For Neville the deeper stake is conversion and retention: if the Saints' own witnesses are quietly set aside, "what else did he say that is a fact that is not a fact?"
