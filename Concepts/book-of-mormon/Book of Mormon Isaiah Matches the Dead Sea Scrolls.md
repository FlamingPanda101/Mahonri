# Book of Mormon Isaiah Matches the Dead Sea Scrolls

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
If [[Joseph Smith]] merely copied or paraphrased KJV Isaiah, then nothing in the Book of Mormon's Isaiah text should require knowledge unavailable in 1829. Arnold argues the opposite: some of the book's variants align with manuscripts discovered more than a century later [^11][^12].

## The Rebuttal
1. **The 1947 Qumran discovery reset the comparison.** Archaeologists found near Qumran "the oldest Isaiah manuscripts ever found, about 1,000 years older than what the King James translators worked from." Compared with the KJV, these scrolls show "real distinguishable variants." *Rating: factually accurate — the Great Isaiah Scroll (1QIsaᵃ) predates the Masoretic base of the KJV by roughly a millennium.* [^11]
2. **Directional match.** "Some of the Book of Mormon's variations from the King James version match the Dead Sea Scrolls better than the King James version does." Because the scrolls were not discovered until 117 years after the 1830 publication, the fraud theory would require Joseph to have "correctly guessed which textual variants from manuscripts buried in desert caves represented the more accurate ancient readings" — manuscripts he had no way of knowing existed [^12]. *Rating: a strong anachronistic-knowledge argument **if** the matches are non-trivial; the count and quality of true DSS-agreeing variants is the load-bearing detail not given in the video.*
3. **Connects to the broader thesis.** This is one of the lines Arnold lists in his summary as evidence that "postdates" the book — "Dead Sea Scrolls variants it shouldn't know about" — and it underpins [[Unconscious Absorption Cannot Explain Suppressed Content]] [^33][^35].
4. **Steelman of the skeptic.** (a) The DSS and KJV/Masoretic Isaiah agree the *vast majority* of the time; isolated agreements with 1QIsaᵃ can arise by chance, by Joseph's own editorial smoothing, or by coincidental agreement on obvious readings. (b) Some Book of Mormon Isaiah variants agree with the *KJV against* the scrolls, which cuts the other way and is not mentioned. (c) "Better than the KJV" needs a controlled, peer-reviewed tally to distinguish signal from noise. Arnold asserts the favorable pattern without enumerating the cases or the counter-cases [^12].

## Best Quotes
> "In 1947, archaeologists discovered the Dead Sea Scrolls near Qumran in Israel, with the oldest Isaiah manuscripts ever found, about 1,000 years older than what the King James translators worked from." [^11]

> "Some of the Book of Mormon's variations from the King James version match the Dead Sea Scrolls better than the King James version does. The scrolls weren't discovered until 1947. So, 117 years after the Book of Mormon was published." [^12]

> "Joseph would have needed to correctly guess which textual variants from manuscripts buried in desert caves represented the more accurate ancient readings. Manuscripts that he would have had no way of knowing existed." [^13]
