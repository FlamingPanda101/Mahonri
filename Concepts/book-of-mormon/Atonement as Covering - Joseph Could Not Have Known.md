# Atonement as Covering — Joseph Could Not Have Known

## The Claim
[[Jonah (Plain and Precious Publishing)|Jonah]] argues that the central verse of his Alma 24 chiasm ([[Alma 24.12-13|Alma 24:12-13]]) clusters "liquid imagery" — *stain, blood, wash, shed* — crowned by **"atonement."** He claims the *original* Hebrew sense of atonement (root **kpr**) is **"to cover"** (specifically, blood blotting out / sprinkled as a covering), *not* the popular "at-one-ment." Because in Joseph Smith's day "atonement" carried the Latin-derived "at-one" gloss, Joseph "could not have known" the cover-etymology — yet the verse's blood-covering imagery fits it perfectly, which Jonah offers as a touch of authenticity [^1][^2][^3].

## Supporting Evidence
1. **kpr = "cover."** The Hebrew root *k-p-r* (kaphar) does carry the sense "to cover / cover over / wipe," and the Day of Atonement (Yom Kippur) blood-sprinkling ritual ties atonement to covering — so the linguistic premise is broadly defensible in Hebrew lexicography. *Rating: the etymology is real; the inference drawn from it is weak.* [^2]
2. **Imagery coherence.** Jonah notes the verse stacks blood/wash/shed/stain right before "atonement," so the liquid-covering reading is contextually apt and, he argues, too "ordered" to be a guess [^4]. He italicizes the liquid words in his book *The Lost Gems of Genesis* [^5].
3. **Steelman of the skeptic.** The "[[Atonement as Covering - Joseph Could Not Have Known|Joseph could not have known]]" move is a classic argument-from-ignorance: (a) the cover-sense of atonement was *not* secret in 1829 — Hebrew lexica, Adam Clarke's commentary, and revival-era sermons discussed kpr/kaphar and "covering"; (b) the English coinage "atonement" (Tyndale's "at-one-ment") and a blood-covering theology coexisted in Protestant preaching Joseph was steeped in; (c) blood-as-covering imagery is pervasive throughout the King James Bible (Leviticus, Hebrews), which Joseph knew well. So clustered blood imagery beside "atonement" requires no special knowledge. The presenters do not engage these counterpoints [^3].
4. **Self-described speculation.** Jonah's etymological claims are drawn from his own popular book, not cited peer-reviewed Semitics, and the segment is part of an episode the hosts flag as speculative fun [^5].

## Best Quotes
> "The original meaning of the word atonement was not to be one, which everybody says... It's to cover. And the thing that was covering you was the blood." [^2]

> "Joseph just happens to guess perfectly right about the real etymology of the word atonement... at the time Joseph Smith translates the Book of Mormon, atonement was the Latin word that was pasted over the covering." [^3]

> "Pretty good for Joseph who could not have known that that's what the word should have been." [^4]
