# Eden Means Bountiful So Lehis Journey Ends in the Holy of Holies

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Dave Butler|David Butler]] argues that the place-name **Bountiful** at the end of Lehi's wilderness journey ([[1 Nephi 17.1|1 Nephi 17:5]]) is Nephi naming the journey's end **"Eden"** — and that since Eden *is* the holy of holies, this confirms the journey is a temple ascent ending in the holiest place. His evidence is that non-LDS Hebrew Bible scholar **Ziony Zevit**, in *What Really Happened in the Garden of Eden* (2013), offers a philological translation of the name **Eden** as **"bountiful"** — "he uses that exact word, several times." Butler stresses he has *not* told Zevit about the Book of Mormon, "I'm afraid he'll retract his definition." So a hostile witness independently glosses *Eden* as exactly the word Lehi's party chose, at exactly the structural point where a temple journey's holy of holies should fall. Builds on [[Lehi's Dream Recapitulates the Eden-Temple Return]] and [[The Garden of Eden Was a Prototype Sanctuary]].[^1][^2]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Zevit translates Eden as "bountiful."** Butler: Zevit "does the sort of things that you do in biblical scholarship... Aramaic, Akkadian root, an Egyptian word *adina*... and on the basis of all of that he suggests the name Eden means *bountiful* — he uses that exact word." *Rating: a real scholarly proposal (Zevit, *What Really Happened in the Garden of Eden*, ~p.89); the value depends on the reader accepting that etymology, which is itself debated.*[^1]
2. **The Nephites name the place, and reuse the name.** Butler notes the Lehites *give* names to places on the journey (Valley of Lemuel, River of Laman, Shazer, Bountiful), and later plant a *land Bountiful* (with a city) in the New World — where Christ appears "as king" at the temple in [[3 Nephi 11.10-11|3 Nephi]]. The repetition signals the name's importance. *Rating: the named-places point is textual; the Eden-identification is the inference layered on Zevit's gloss.*[^2]
3. **It sits where the holy of holies belongs.** On the [[Lehis Journey Maps the Tabernacle Not Solomons Temple|tabernacle mapping]], Bountiful is the final room — Eden / the holy of holies — reached after the courtyard (Valley of Lemuel), the door (Shazer), and the holy place (the Nahom stretch). "It's at the end of the journey." See [[Bountiful and Khor Kharfot Match the Book of Mormon]]. *Rating: coherent within the mapping; the structural fit is the argument's strength.*[^2]
4. **A hostile-witness move.** Butler's refusal to alert Zevit ("I want... not to let him change his mind") frames the gloss as undesigned testimony — a non-LDS scholar arriving at "bountiful" with no Book-of-Mormon motive. *Rating: rhetorically effective; the strength still rides on whether Zevit's etymology is sound and whether "bountiful" is distinctive enough to be non-coincidental.*[^1]

**Steelman of the skeptical counter.** Hebrew *‘ēden* is most commonly connected to roots meaning "delight/pleasure/luxury" (whence Eden = "garden of delight"), and "bountiful" is one *interpretive* rendering among several — Zevit's proposal is not consensus, so resting an identification on it is resting on a contested etymology. Even granting it, "bountiful" is a fairly *generic* descriptor (Nephi says the place was named for "much fruit" and "wild honey," 1 Nephi 17:5-6) — an actual fertile coastal oasis would naturally earn such a name without any Eden coding. And Joseph Smith's English "Bountiful" is what we have; we cannot independently check the underlying Hebrew. **A Latter-day Saint reply:** the claim's force is *cumulative* — it is not "bountiful is a rare word" but that an independent scholar glosses *Eden* as "bountiful" *and* the place falls exactly where a temple ascent's holy of holies should *and* the surrounding itinerary maps the tabernacle by other puns ([[Shazer Names the Fine-Twined-Linen Door of the Tabernacle|Shazer]]); any one coincidence is cheap, the *ordered convergence* less so, and it coheres with the broader [[The Book of Mormon Matches Pre-Reform First Temple Theology|first-temple reading]]. Held provisionally per [[Avoiding Parallelomania Between Ancient Rites and LDS Temple]].
