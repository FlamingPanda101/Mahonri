# Sound Correspondences Distinguish Descent from Coincidence in Language

## The Claim
The methodological backbone of [[Brian Stubbs]]'s case is the comparative method's gold standard: **regular sound correspondences**. When languages descend from a common ancestor, each inherited sound shifts in a *consistent, rule-governed* way down each branch (e.g. Proto-Indo-European *k* → Germanic *h* → English *hound*, German *Hund*, but Greek *kyon*, Latin *canis*). Stubbs argues the alleged Semitic/Egyptian layer in [[Uto-Aztecan Contains a Large Semitic and Egyptian Lexical Layer|Uto-Aztecan]] is not a "Texas-sharpshooter" pile of lookalikes (the "Brit-ish = man of the covenant" error) but obeys such systematic shifts — making chance "really quite impossible" past a certain volume.[^1][^2] He claims regular correspondences also let linguists tell **inherited descent from contact borrowing**: loanwords enter a language *without* obeying the inherited correspondence set, whereas the Uto-Aztecan items fit it — which is why he moved from a "Semitic group came in later" view to placing the layer in Proto-Uto-Aztecan itself.[^3]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **The posited shifts are stated as rules, not one-offs (rated: moderate, contested).** Semitic *b* → Uto-Aztecan *p* (*baraq* → *berok*); the voiced stops *b/d/g* devoice to *p/t/k* (*dubur* "hind end" → *tupur*; *yagar* "hill" → *yakar*); the pharyngeals *ḥ* and *ʿ* round adjacent vowels to *o/u/w*; the glottal stop → *w* (Hebrew *yaʾaminu* "believe him" → Uto-Aztecan *yawaminu*). Egyptian items obey the *same* rules.[^4][^5] See [[Uto-Aztecan Contains a Large Semitic and Egyptian Lexical Layer]].
2. **The "combination of meanings" test (rated: moderate, distinctive).** Stubbs's strongest sub-argument: Egyptian *hotep* uniquely bundles three *unrelated* senses — "be at peace," "set/bury (go below the earth)," and "(hair) fall out" — and the *same* improbable triple cluster reappears in Uto-Aztecan. A shared *polysemy bundle* is far harder to attribute to chance than a single matched word.[^6]
3. **Volume converts coincidence into signal (rated: weak-to-moderate, the crux of the dispute).** "When you get certain numbers that fit these patterns over a certain number, then it's really quite impossible that that happens by chance ... the larger the number, the stronger the case."[^7] *Steelman:* this is exactly where mainstream skeptics push back. A loose enough inventory of *permitted* sound-shifts and *permitted* meaning-shifts vastly inflates the pool of "allowable" matches, so a large raw count is not self-validating — critics argue the method must be pre-registered and the correspondences exceptionless, and they note Stubbs sometimes appeals to reconstructed-but-unattested Hebrew forms (e.g. *boser*, *sigob*) to complete a match. The discipline's reluctance to adopt the thesis (see [[Uto-Aztecan Contains a Large Semitic and Egyptian Lexical Layer]]) is itself the standing rebuttal. Compare the parallel debate over whether [[Chiasmus as Evidence of Ancient Authorship in the Book of Mormon|chiasmus]] is real structure or [[Pareidolia Objection to Chiasmus Finding|pareidolia]].

## Best Quotes
> "Two words in different languages can coincidentally look alike ... So it's a numbers game. When you get certain numbers that fit these patterns ... it's really quite impossible that that happens by chance."[^7]

> "Loans will come into a language with different sounds — they don't fit those sound correspondences necessarily."[^3]
