# Mixture of Lying and Deception Theory Rebutted

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)

When the pure "they all lied" and pure "they were all tricked" theories fail, a fallback is a *mixture*: some witnesses lied (a smaller conspiracy) while others were genuinely deceived.[^1]

## The Rebuttal

### 1. The mixture inherits both theories' evidential emptiness
Peterson's response is simply "show me the evidence." He sees no evidence that any individual witness was a liar, and no evidence that any individual one was deceived, crazy, or prone to hallucination — so a blend of two unsupported claims is doubly unsupported, not more plausible.[^2]
> **Evidence rating:** Strong (a mixture cannot be better-evidenced than its unsupported components).

### 2. It is a "Hail Mary"
Peterson and the host agree the mixture is a desperation move — reached only because the cleaner theories collapsed, not because evidence points to it.[^3]
> **Evidence rating:** Moderate (a characterization of the dialectical situation, not new data).

### 3. It still must answer each witness individually
A mixture theory has to specify *which* witnesses lied and *which* were fooled, and supply evidence for each assignment. No such case-by-case evidence exists for any of them.[^2]
> **Evidence rating:** Strong (the per-witness burden is never discharged).

## Steelman of the Opposing View

A mixture is, in principle, the *most* realistic shape for a debunking account: real frauds usually involve a couple of knowing instigators surrounded by sincere dupes, so "Joseph plus one or two confederates fooling the rest" is more historically typical than either pure extreme. The skeptic argues Peterson's "show me the evidence" cuts both ways — absence of a documented confession is weak counter-evidence given thin frontier records. Peterson's rejoinder is that the believer is not obliged to refute a theory for which *no* positive evidence is offered; the mixture is conceivable but not supported, which feeds directly into [[Inference to the Best Explanation for the Witnesses]].

## Best Quotes

> "I just say, show me the evidence. I don't see it. I don't see it for any individual one of them."[^2]

> "It's kind of a Hail Mary. Yeah, it is a Hail Mary."[^3]
