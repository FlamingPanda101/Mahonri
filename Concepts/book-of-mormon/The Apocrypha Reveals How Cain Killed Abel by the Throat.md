# The Apocrypha Reveals How Cain Killed Abel by the Throat

## The Claim
[[Jonah Barnes]] argues that the canonical Genesis text is silent on *how* Cain killed Abel ("Cain rose up against Abel his brother, and slew him" — [[Genesis 4]]), but a cluster of apocryphal Adam-and-Eve texts converge on a specific method — Cain **sawed through Abel's windpipe with a sharp flint stone**, after struggling to discover how to kill at all — and that Joseph Smith's [[Moses 5.10-11|Book of Moses]] preserves the matching *hint*: Satan's oath "swear unto me by thy throat." Barnes presents this as a "tiny home run" Joseph hits casually, without explaining it.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. The canon is silent; the apocrypha are detailed — rate: strong (textual fact)
Genesis gives no method, weapon, or motive for the murder; it only says Cain "rose up… and slew him." The apocryphal corpus, by contrast, supplies an hour-long struggle: "the sons of Adam (Armenian) says he killed him with great difficulty; for an hour he slaughtered him violently," and "one particular account says he saws through his windpipe with a black flint stone… as sharp as a razor, and Satan helped him find the flint stone."[^2] That the canon omits what the apocrypha elaborate is not in dispute; the evidential weight rests on Joseph's apparent awareness of it.

### 2. Joseph's "by thy throat" is the proleptic hint — rate: moderate (suggestive convergence)
Joseph's Cain receives Satan's oath "swear unto me by thy throat, and if thou tell it thou shalt die" ([[Moses 5.10-11|Moses 5]]). Barnes reads the otherwise-odd throat reference as Joseph's understated pointer to the throat-cutting method the apocrypha describe: "if you cut someone's throat, they die — wink wink."[^1] *Strength: the link is real but inferential — Joseph names a throat *oath*, not a throat *wound*, so the connection is interpretive, not explicit.*

### 3. Blood-drinking and "the blood is the life" — rate: moderate (thematic)
Several accounts (the Zohar; the dream of Eve in the *Vita Adae*, the Greek/Armenian/Slavonic lives) have Cain *drink* Abel's blood. Barnes ties this to the ancient belief, codified in [[Leviticus 17]] ("the blood is the life"), that consuming a victim's blood transfers or lengthens life — a belief the book of Enoch ascribes to the antediluvians drinking their enemies' blood. Joseph's framework (Satan present all along, goading Cain) makes the otherwise-bizarre detail coherent.[^2]

### 4. The oldest datable witness postdates the events — rate: weak-to-moderate (the honest limit)
Under Cardon's questioning, Barnes concedes the oldest *verifiable* source for the flint-throat detail is the **Cave of Treasures** attributed to **Ephrem the Syrian** (b. ~306, d. 373; manuscript tradition ~6th–7th c.), with the related *Conflict of Adam and Eve* perhaps 5th–6th c. The Dead Sea Scrolls and the canonical text are silent on method. So the "most dependable" source, for Barnes, is Joseph's Moses itself — the apocryphal agreement is corroboration, not an independent ancient chain.[^3] This is the same evidential posture as [[Authentic Ancient Texts Contain Uncorroborated Unique Data]].

## Steelman of the Opposing View
The skeptic has several strong replies. (a) **Late, derivative sources prove little about 1830.** If the detailed traditions are 4th–7th century and were available in print (the *Conflict of Adam and Eve* and Cave of Treasures circulated in 19th-century scholarship and translations), then a "method" detail Joseph never actually states cannot be a hit — he wrote a throat *oath*, and the reader supplies the throat *wound*. (b) **The throat oath has an obvious internal source.** Swearing "by thy throat" with a death penalty for telling reads naturally as a secrecy oath (the throat as the organ of speech/betrayal), needing no knowledge of decapitation lore. (c) **Cherry-picking from a contradictory corpus.** Barnes admits "the apocrypha provide like 10 different options" for the method (flint, jawbone of an animal, biting, a stone, a staff); selecting the one that matches and calling it a bullseye is post-hoc target-painting. (d) **"Murder to get gain" is the firmer datum.** The genuinely distinctive Joseph-specific contribution is not the weapon but the *motive* — gain (flocks, wife, inheritance) and the secret-combination formula — which Genesis lacks; that is treated in [[Murder to Get Gain Inverts the Order of Sacrifice]] and is a better-grounded argument than the flint-throat convergence.

**What survives:** the *motive* and *secret-oath* structure Joseph adds to the bare Genesis murder is genuinely apocryphal-flavored and is the load-bearing claim; the specific flint-to-the-throat "method" match is suggestive but weakened by the lateness of the witnesses, the contradictory menu of methods, and the fact that Joseph names an oath, not a wound.
