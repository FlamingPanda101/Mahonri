# Internal Cross-References to the Unwritten Small Plates

## The Claim
Given the [[The Book of Mormon Was Translated Mosiah-First|Mosiah-first translation order]], [[Mike Day]] argues that the Book of Mormon contains references — from Mosiah through Moroni — that quote or allude to *small-plates* material (1–2 Nephi, Jacob, Enos, Omni) which had **not yet been dictated**. Since Joseph dictated forward without going back to revise and without a draft to consult, reproducing these "Easter eggs" would require holding the entire later-to-be-written prologue in memory — which Day offers as evidence the text was received, not composed.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **Moroni's farewell quotes five small-plates authors.** [[Moroni 10.27-34|Moroni 10:27–34]] weaves together the words of Nephi, Jacob, Enos, and Omni — "five quotations off the small plates" — into Moroni's closing testimony. Moroni was dictated long before those books, so the quotations point forward to undictated text.[^1] *(Moderate-to-strong — the verbal overlaps are checkable in the text; whether they require supernatural memory is the contested step.)*
2. **Alma quotes Lehi's vision verbatim.** [[Alma 36.22|Alma 36:22]] ("methought I saw, even as our father Lehi saw... numberless concourses of angels") reproduces [[1 Nephi 1.8|1 Nephi 1:8]] almost word for word — at the exact center of Alma's chiasm, no less — yet 1 Nephi came later in dictation.[^2] *(Strong as a textual parallel; doubles as a [[Chiasmus as Evidence of Ancient Authorship in the Book of Mormon|chiasmus]] datum.)*
3. **The "manner of happiness" echo.** [[Alma 50.23|Alma 50:23]] ("there never was a happier time... since the days of Nephi") echoes [[2 Nephi 5.27|2 Nephi 5:27]] ("we lived after the manner of happiness") — again, a small-plates phrase reused in earlier-dictated text.[^3] *(Moderate — a thematic/verbal echo; lighter than the Alma 36 case.)*

## Steelman of the Opposing View
The forward-reference pattern is real but admits a naturalistic reading. (a) A composer who *intends* to write the 1 Nephi–Omni prologue last can deliberately plant references in the main body and then write the prologue to match — this is ordinary prequel construction, not evidence of dictation from a pre-existing plate. (b) Several "quotations" are short, formulaic, or thematic ("numberless concourses of angels," "manner of happiness") and could reflect a consistent author reusing his own pet phrases rather than citing a fixed prior text. (c) The argument leans entirely on the no-revision premise; if Joseph retained notes or a mental outline (which a fabrication hypothesis posits anyway), holding a handful of motifs in memory is unremarkable. The believer's fair, sharpened claim is again the *conjunction* with method and rate: forward references are easy for a writer with a manuscript and time, but Day's case is that Joseph had neither — six-plus pages a day, dictated once, no going back — so the cumulative bookkeeping required is what strains the storyteller theory, not any single echo.

## Best Quotes
> "From Mosiah to Moroni there are these references to stuff going on in the small plates... if Joseph Smith wrote the Book of Mormon he would have to remember, or have planned in his head, stuff from the beginning — but he hasn't written it yet."[^1]

> "In Moroni 10:27–34 there are five quotations off the small plates... he has to remember it, and he has to get it right."[^1]
