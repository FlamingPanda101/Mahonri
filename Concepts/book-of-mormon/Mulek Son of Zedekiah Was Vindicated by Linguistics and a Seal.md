# Mulek Son of Zedekiah Was Vindicated by Linguistics and a Seal

## The Point

**Critic's claim:** The Book of Mormon blunders when it names Mulek, a son of King Zedekiah, who led a second group out of Jerusalem ([[Helaman 8.7|Helaman 8:21]]). The Bible never mentions a "Mulek," and 2 Kings 25:7 says *all* of Zedekiah's sons were slain. If Joseph Smith knew the Bible as well as critics insist, inventing a surviving royal son with a non-biblical name should have been an obvious, self-inflicted error.

**Fife's response:** What looked like a mistake for over a century has turned into a quiet bullseye. Both the survival of a royal son and the name itself now have linguistic and archaeological support that was unavailable to Joseph Smith in 1829.

## Evidence

- **A mistranslation hid the king's son.** Jeremiah 38:6 (KJV) names "Malchiah the son of Hammelech." The underlying Hebrew, *MalkiYahu ben-hamMelek*, actually means "MalkiYahu, son of *the king*" — *hammelek* is a title, not a proper name. Modern translations correct this, yielding contextual evidence that Zedekiah had a son (Malchiah / MalkiYahu) who may not have been among those slain in 2 Kings 25:7.[^1]
- **Mulek as a nickname.** Scholars note that *Mulek* plausibly functions as a shortened form of *MalkiYahu* — the way "Mike" stands for "Michael" — sharing the consonantal root MLK.[^2]
- **A non-LDS scholar was impressed.** David Noel Freedman reportedly remarked of the Mulek correlation, "If Joseph Smith came up with that one, he did pretty good!"[^2]
- **A physical seal.** A clay stamp seal bearing *Malkiyahu ben hamelek* ("MalkiYahu, son of the king"), dated to the late 7th–early 6th century BC, was discovered in Jerusalem in the 1980s — viable archaeological evidence for the very figure the Book of Mormon calls Mulek.[^3]

This is a textbook case of an alleged anachronism reversing into supporting evidence (see [[The Anachronism List Is Trending Toward the Book of Mormon]]), and it follows the [[Convergence Not Proof Is the Standard for Book of Mormon Historicity|convergence standard]] rather than offering single-point proof.

## Best Quotes

> "How in the world did Joseph Smith know that one of the sons of Zedekiah likely survived? How did he make up a plausible nickname (Mulek) for Malchiah or MalkiYahu? Is that not a bullseye or close to it for the Book of Mormon?" [^2]

> "If Joseph Smith came up with that one, he did pretty good!" — David Noel Freedman [^2]

## LDS Connection

Mulek is the eponymous ancestor of the people of Zarahemla, whom the Nephites later joined ([[Helaman 8.7|Helaman 8:21]]; Omni 1:14–19). The vindication of a surviving Davidic son matters theologically as well as historically: it preserves a continuing royal line out of Jerusalem alongside Lehi's family, reinforcing the Book of Mormon's self-presentation as a record of real Israelite migrations rather than nineteenth-century fiction.
