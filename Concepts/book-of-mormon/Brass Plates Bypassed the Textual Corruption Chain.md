# Brass Plates Bypassed the Textual Corruption Chain

## The Claim
Arnold's central theological frame: the Bible "wasn't protected" — "institutions that controlled the text changed the text" — so a record was needed that escaped that process. The Book of Mormon is "our answer to that problem, a record that left before the corruption fully arrived." This answers the question, "Why couldn't God just protect the Bible?" [^25][^34]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **The escape mechanism.** "[[Lehi]] took the brass plates, which was scripture predating the Deuteronomistic revisions, out of Jerusalem just before Babylon destroyed the city and the post-exilic reconstruction consolidated the new theological program." *Rating: an internal-narrative claim; it presupposes the historicity of the brass plates but is internally coherent with the [[Deuteronomistic Reform Explains the Book of Mormon Theology|Deuteronomistic-reform]] framing.* [^30]
2. **What it bypassed.** "The record bypassed everything that followed, the Babylonian exile, the Masoretic standardization, the scribal copying chains, and the councils that voted on the canon. None of it touched the plates." [^31]
3. **What it preserved.** "What came out of the other end preserves a bold, embodied, pre-existing Christ... divine council imagery... open theophany, prophets seeing God face-to-face. And... a Christology that the Dead Sea Scrolls scholarship has confirmed was in circulation before the standardization period suppressed it." This ties the frame to [[Book of Mormon Isaiah Matches the Dead Sea Scrolls]] [^31][^32].
4. **The "first 10 episodes" support.** Arnold notes his channel's prior episodes argue the broader case that "institutions that controlled the text changed the text," positioning corruption of the Bible as a documented premise rather than an LDS assumption [^25].
5. **Steelman of the skeptic.** (a) The argument depends on the *existence* of the brass plates, for which there is no independent evidence — it cannot be cited as evidence for itself. (b) "The Bible was corrupted" overstates the textual-criticism consensus: scholars find the Hebrew/Greek text remarkably stable, with variants mostly minor; "councils that voted on the canon" is a simplification of canon formation. (c) The theology said to be "suppressed" (divine council, embodied Christ) is *still present* in the canonical Bible (Psalm 82, John 1, Daniel 7), undercutting the claim it was fully edited out. Arnold uses "corruption" broadly where critics would demand precision [^31].
