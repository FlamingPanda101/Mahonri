# LiDAR Reveals a Dense Advanced Maya Civilization Matching the Book of Mormon

## The Point

**Critic's claim:** Joseph Smith fabricated a fantasy of ancient Americans who built large cities, temples, fortresses, and highways and numbered in the millions. Real pre-Columbian peoples, critics long said, were too few and too primitive for such a picture.

**Fife's response:** Remote-sensing technology that did not exist in Joseph Smith's day — LiDAR — has revealed exactly the kind of dense, advanced, fortified, highway-connected civilization the Book of Mormon describes, turning a former object of ridicule into a striking correspondence.

## Evidence

- **The Pacunam LiDAR Initiative.** Featured in *National Geographic*'s 2018 documentary *Lost Treasures of the Maya Snake Kings*, the project mapped about 2,100 square kilometers in northern Guatemala near the Mirador Basin — an area less than half the size of Utah County.[^1]
- **What this small scan found:** roughly **65,000 previously unknown structures**; vast elevated highways usable in the rainy season; ubiquitous fortresses, ramparts, and defensive walls; dikes, dams, canals, and reservoirs; irrigated agricultural terraces; animal pens and quarries; and evidence of endemic, centuries-long warfare (including in the 250–500 AD era).[^2]
- **Population and intensity.** Researchers estimated the lowland Maya peak at **15–20 million** — half the population of contemporary Europe on one-thirtieth the land — with industrial-scale food production and land use nearing 100% in places.[^2]
- **Specific textual parallels.** Fife lines up the findings with the Book of Mormon: fortifications, towers, and ditch-and-palisade defenses in Alma 48–50; great cities and highways (3 Nephi 6:8; Helaman 3:14–15); dense populations and warfare (Mormon 1:7; Alma 1:29). The earthwork fortifications of Alma match pre-Columbian construction — earthen ridge, exterior ditch, timber palisade, and towers with bastions.[^3]

This corroborates the more focused [[Mesoamerican Warfare and Fortifications Match the Book of Alma|warfare-and-fortifications correspondence]] and exemplifies why the [[The Anachronism List Is Trending Toward the Book of Mormon|anachronism list keeps shrinking]] — though, consistent with [[Convergence Not Proof Is the Standard for Book of Mormon Historicity|the convergence standard]], it is corroboration, not proof.

## Best Quotes

> "Critics mocked Joseph Smith because the Book of Mormon talked about an advanced, numerous, and ancient American people who built large cities, temples, fortresses, and highways." [^1]

> "A 23-year-old farmer produced a book of scripture with what was considered outrageous claims about Indigenous American people at that time — and those claims are proving more correct almost 200 years later." [^3]

## LDS Connection

The picture LiDAR paints — interconnected cities, monumental public works, and intensive warfare — directly mirrors the social world of Alma through Mormon, where rival cities, road networks, and fortified frontiers drive the narrative. That a barely-sampled scan of one basin validates so many incidental details supports the Book of Mormon's claim to be an authentic ancient record (cf. [[Absence of Evidence Is Not Evidence of Absence in New World Archaeology]]).
