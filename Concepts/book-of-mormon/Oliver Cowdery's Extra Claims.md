# Oliver Cowdery's Extra Claims

## The Claim
[[Oliver Cowdery]] is "way more important than the other two witnesses" because, beyond the Three Witnesses' vision, he carries **three additional claims** whose reliability is therefore critical: he was the primary scribe of the translation, he received priesthood authority with Joseph, and he witnessed a vision of divine beings in the Kirtland Temple [^1].

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. Primary translator/scribe
Oliver "scribed" essentially all of the surviving Book of Mormon text (Martin's lost 116 pages aside), making him "the best resource we have to first-hand accounts of how the Book of Mormon was translated" — and the person best positioned to detect fraud during the process [^2]. *Rating: historically accurate; Oliver was the principal scribe.*

### 2. Priesthood restoration
He claims to have received the **Aaronic priesthood** ([[D&C 13]]) from John the Baptist and the **Melchizedek priesthood** from Peter, James, and John, alongside Joseph [^3]. He reaffirmed this even after excommunication: "I was present with Joseph Smith when an holy angel from God came down from heaven and conferred or restored the Aaronic priesthood" [^4]. *Rating: a central LDS authority claim; rests on the two men's joint testimony.*

### 3. Kirtland Temple vision
He claims to have seen Jesus, Moses, Elias, and Elijah in the Kirtland Temple, where keys were delivered ([[D&C 110]]) [^5]. *Rating: a supernatural claim resting on Oliver and Joseph; the episode uses it against the metallurgical theory.*

### 4. Competency and fake pamphlets
Oliver was an admitted-to-the-bar lawyer and politician ("he was a lawyer, for God's sake"), undercutting incompetency. Attempts to show him recanting rely on **two pamphlets later proven plagiarized/fake** — *Confessions of Over Street* and *Defiance* [^6][^7]. *Rating: the forgery point is well-taken; the competency claim is supported by his legal career.*

## Steelman (Opposing View)
Stacking extra claims **raises the stakes both ways**: it makes Oliver pivotal, but it also means his testimony now depends on multiple unverifiable supernatural events (priesthood angels, temple theophany) that share the same evidentiary weakness as the plate vision — they are uncorroborated except by the two principals. The co-host's "pressure test" worry is apt: late-life praise from Richmond associates says nothing about the founding events; the apologetic reply (Oliver kept testifying throughout) is the real load-bearing point, not the character testimonials.

## Best Quotes
> "He's way more important than the other two witnesses... he has extra claims beyond simply just experiencing the vision of the three witnesses." [^1]

> "He was in a unique position to expose Joseph Smith's fraud due to first-hand experience to the translation, the vision of angels, plates, the restoration of the Aaronic and Melchizedek priesthoods, and the vision of Jesus in the Kirtland Temple." [^5]

> "The two fake pamphlets in his name of him denouncing... are clearly fake. Had he denied, this would have been well known and used by his political campaigns, which he lost." [^7]
