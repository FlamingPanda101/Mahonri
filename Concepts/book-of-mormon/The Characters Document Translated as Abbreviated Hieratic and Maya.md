# The Characters Document Translated as Abbreviated Hieratic and Maya

## The Claim

[[Jerry Grover]] — a licensed geologist and engineer with a long amateur interest in ancient number systems — argues that the so-called "characters document" (a page of copied plate characters from [[John Whitmer]] via [[David Whitmer]]) is **genuine reformed Egyptian**: mostly Egyptian *hieratic* with some *demotic*, abbreviated and logographic, and interleaved with **Maya-style distance-number / "anterior–posterior" date glyphs**. He renders the first four lines as a lost chronological preface to Mosiah, keyed to a "Lehi-departure" calendar glyph.[^1][^2]

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. It is not the Anthon transcript
Handwriting analysis identifies the document as [[John Whitmer]]'s, with provenance through [[David Whitmer]] to the RLDS church; [[Charles Anthon]]'s own description (a document bearing an emblem) does not match it. So the page is a separately-provenanced specimen of copied characters, not the disputed transcript [[Martin Harris]] carried east.[^3]
> **Evidence rating:** Moderate (the handwriting/provenance points are checkable; "reformed Egyptian" identity is Grover's inference). See [[Charles Anthon Transcript Episode]].

### 2. Number sets and date glyphs were noticed before
An Egyptologist (William Hayes) flagged hieratic number sets and a royal hieroglyph in the 1940s–50s. Grover says he recognized a hieratic "9" bar-and-dots and a four/ten/five number set, then identified glyphs adjacent to the numbers as Maya "it came to pass"/distance-number infixes that function the same way (a base date plus a forward/backward count). He claims the Egyptian meanings of those glyphs ("it happened / it came to pass") match.[^2][^4]
> **Evidence rating:** Weak-to-moderate (the Egyptian-number familiarity is plausible; the Maya-Egyptian glyph equivalence is Grover's novel, unrefereed proposal).

### 3. Logographic compression explains plate capacity
Grover stresses the script is *constructed for compactness* — "it came to pass" is a single glyph; the scribe consistently selects the one-glyph word among synonyms and uses demotic only when shorter than hieratic. This dovetails with the vault's [[Translation Density Shows the Book of Mormon Text Fits]] and [[Reformed Egyptian Characters Were Small]] (a logographic script is far denser than alphabetic/syllabic).[^5][^6]
> **Evidence rating:** Moderate as a *capacity* point (compression is a real property of logographic scripts); the specific decipherment is what carries the risk.

### 4. The decipherment is presented as fully academic and falsifiable
Grover insists he had no angelic aid ("if I formed a church only three people would join"), shows the Monumental ("D-number") and hieratic forms for each glyph, and says the resources are online for anyone to check. He concedes the complex date math is what convinced *him* the rendering is right (the numbers fit the Book of Mormon's known chronology).[^7]
> **Evidence rating:** Method is commendably transparent; the conclusion remains a single researcher's unreviewed translation.

## Steelman of the Opposing View

The decipherment has not been peer-reviewed by Egyptologists or Mayanists, and "the numbers all fit the Book of Mormon chronology" is exactly what a motivated translator who *already knows* the target text would produce — a textbook confirmation-bias risk. Maya long-count machinery and Egyptian hieratic belong to unrelated scripts a millennium and an ocean apart; positing that one borrowed "came to pass" glyphs from the other is extraordinary and, absent independent attestation, may be pattern-matching on a noisy 19th-century copy of uncertain fidelity (Grover himself notes some glyphs are "combined" and ambiguous). The honest reply is Grover's own: the work is laid out transparently enough to be *checked* and refuted — so it should be evaluated, not assumed; but until specialists engage it, it is a provocative hypothesis, not established fact, and the vault rates it tier-C accordingly.

## Best Quotes

> "It's mostly hieratic but there is some demotic in the characters document, so it's a bit of a mix... I figured out the number system and all the dates because there were date sets."[^2]

> "It functions the same exact way as the Maya system... and if I look at the meaning of those glyphs in Egyptian they mean 'it happened,' it matches 'it came to pass.'"[^4]

> "Everything I give you is out of the same resources that are used to translate Egyptian texts... you can verify all this yourself."[^7]

## LDS Connection

If even partly correct, the characters document would be a small "Rosetta Stone" for reformed Egyptian and direct physical support for the Book of Mormon's self-description (Mormon 9:32–34). More cautiously, the *capacity* argument stands independently of the full decipherment: a compact logographic script answers the "the text could not fit on the plates" objection (see [[Trent Horns Word-Count Argument Uses Wrong Parameters]]). The vault flags this as tier-C, single-researcher, unrefereed work — valuable as a lead, not as a settled result.
