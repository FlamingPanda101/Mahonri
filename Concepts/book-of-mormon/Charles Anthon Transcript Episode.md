# Charles Anthon Transcript Episode

## The Claim
[[Martin Harris]] carried a copy of the "Reformed Egyptian" characters Joseph had transcribed from the plates to several scholars — Luther Bradish, [[Samuel Mitchell]], and most famously [[Charles Anthon]] of Columbia College. The episode argues that whatever the disputed details, the episode (a) **further convinced Martin** (he then mortgaged his farm to fund printing) and (b) constrains the critic's available theories [^1][^2].

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. Two conflicting accounts
- **Harris / Joseph Smith History:** Anthon pronounced the characters genuine — "Egyptian, Chaldaic, Assyric, and Arabic" — and gave a certificate, then tore it up on learning their source [^3].
- **Anthon:** the document was a hoax, "a compilation derived from an alternate of Greek and Hebrew letters, crosses and flourishes," "evidently copied after the Mexican calendar given by Humboldt" [^2].

The episode grants "it's clear someone was lying" [^4]. *Rating: an honest concession; the historical core (Harris visited Anthon) is solid, the content is genuinely contested.*

### 2. Either way, Martin was convinced
Whichever account is true, Martin came away convinced enough to mortgage his farm for the first 5,000 copies — a costly, irreversible commitment paralleling the early apostles' life-changes [^1][^5]. *Rating: sound — the financial commitment is documented and hard to fake.*

### 3. The dilemma for critics
"If Anthon is telling the truth, this requires conspiracy with Martin Harris and Joseph Smith, not mass hallucination as is usually claimed." So embracing Anthon's debunking forces critics off their preferred [[Mass Hallucination Hypnosis Theory Rebutted|hallucination]] theory and onto a conspiracy theory that the rest of the evidence resists [^4]. *Rating: a clever internal-tension argument, though Anthon being right about the characters need not implicate Harris in conscious fraud.*

## Steelman (Opposing View)
A critic can hold that **Anthon honestly judged the characters fake while Harris honestly misremembered or over-read the encounter** — no conspiracy required, just a credulous, motivated witness. The "Mormonism Unveiled" tradition and Anthon's own later letters are typically read as the more reliable account by non-LDS historians. That Harris was convinced shows his sincerity, not the document's authenticity.

## Best Quotes
> "After Anthon examined the characters, he told him that they were of Egyptian, Chaldaic, Assyric, and Arabic, and he said that they were true characters." [^3]

> "It's clear someone was lying. Regardless of who's lying... Martin Harris is further convinced of the authenticity of the work and goes to obtain a mortgage on his farm." [^4]

> "If Anthon is telling the truth, this requires conspiracy with Martin Harris and Joseph Smith, not mass hallucination as is usually claimed." [^4]
