# Eight Witnesses

## Overview

The **Eight Witnesses** — four Whitmers, three Smiths, and Hiram Page — signed the second testimony printed in the Book of Mormon, affirming that Joseph Smith showed them the plates, that they **saw and handled** them, and that the engravings had "the appearance of ancient work." This is an overview/hub note for an existing witness cluster.

## The Claim

The Eight provide the **ordinary, tactile counterpart** to the Three: where the [[Three Witnesses]] report an angel and a heavenly voice, the Eight report a plain physical encounter — lifting the plates, turning the leaves, examining the characters in daylight ([[Three vs Eight Witnesses Different Kinds of Experience]]). This matters because the two testimonies together neutralize opposite objections: the Three cannot be dismissed as merely handling something mundane, and the Eight cannot be dismissed as mere visionaries ([[Natural vs Supernatural Witnesses]]).

Evidential points the vault marshals:

- The **physical heft and detail** of their account [[Witness Descriptions Constrain Any Fabrication Hypothesis|constrain any fabrication hypothesis]] — a convincing prop heavy and detailed enough to satisfy eight close examiners is itself hard to explain naturalistically.
- Like the Three, [[No Witness Ever Recanted|none ever recanted]] despite later distance from the Church.
