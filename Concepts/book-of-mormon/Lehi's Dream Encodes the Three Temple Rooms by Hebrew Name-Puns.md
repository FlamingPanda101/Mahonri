# Lehi's Dream Encodes the Three Temple Rooms by Hebrew Name-Puns

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)|David Butler]] argues that [[Lehi]]'s tree-of-life dream ([[1 Nephi 8|1 Nephi 8]]) is structured as an ascent through the **three rooms of Solomon's Temple**, and that the underlying Hebrew names of those rooms are deliberately punned in the text. This is a *distinct* argument from the vault's [[Lehi's Dream Recapitulates the Eden-Temple Return]] (which reads the dream as Eden typology and rests on Skousen's "sword/word" variant): here the load-bearing evidence is a chain of **name-puns** matching room names, not Eden motifs.

## Supporting Evidence
1. **Room 1 — the *ulam* (porch), punned on *olam* (world).** Lehi's "large and spacious field" is the threshold where the journey begins; a field corresponds to the roofless porch / outside the tabernacle. Nephi specifies the field was wide "as it was a world" — and the Hebrew for "world" is *olam*, differing from *ulam* (the porch) only by the strength of a glottal stop, "pronounced the same" in contemporary Hebrew. Butler reads this as "a call-out to the name." [^1]
2. **Room 2 — the *heikhal* (nave), which simply *means* a great spacious building.** The middle room's name *heikhal* (from Sumerian *E.GAL*) denotes "a palace or any large building." The "great and spacious building" of the dream is described as standing "in the air, above all... high above all the earth" — matching both the *meaning* of *heikhal* and the temple's ascent ideology (you "go up" to the *heikhal*, which is "above everything"). So "the name is being called out, the height is being described." [^2]
3. **Room 3 — the *debir* (holy of holies), the "place of speech."** Beside the tree, Lehi "cried with a loud voice" for his family to come — speech performed in the room whose name *debir* (root *davar*, "word") makes it "the place of the Oracle." Supporting holy-of-holies markers Butler stacks here: a **river/water** by the throne (Ezekiel 47; Revelation 22 — a tree beside the throne with a river and twelve fruits) and the fruit "desirable above all other fruit," the most sweet and most white. [^3]
4. **A temple-ascent textual variant.** The 1830 text and printer's manuscript (per the discussion) read people who "had come **up** and were partaking of the fruit" — later flattened to "come at." "Come up" is the "linguistic ideology of the temple": you *ascend* to it. The detail reinforces that the dreamers are moving through temple space, up to the tree. [^4]
5. **The architecture comes from named primary texts.** The three-room scheme and their names are drawn from Exodus, 1 Kings 6–7, and 2 Chronicles 3–4 — the same backbone Butler used in [[The Stick of Joseph - The Ancient Temple and the Sermon on the Mount Part I|Part I]] for the Sermon on the Mount, so the dream and the Sermon become "two different Visionary looks at the same ordinance." [^5]

**Steelman of the skeptic.** Butler concedes this is a "hypothesis" and that *ulam*/*olam* are not identical (different glottal stops), so the pun is suggestive, not demonstrative; a critic will note that Lehi's vision is preserved only in Joseph Smith's *English*, that "great and spacious building" need not encode *heikhal*, and that Hebrew puns are easy to find post hoc. **But** the force is cumulative and *ordered*: three room-names appearing in the correct ascent sequence (field → elevated building → speaking-place-with-tree-and-river), each matched by an independent feature (meaning, height, oracle-speech, water), is harder to write off as coincidence than any single pun — and it coheres with the broader [[The Book of Mormon Matches Pre-Reform First Temple Theology|First-Temple]] reading.

## Best Quotes
> "Nephi specifically says that this field was wide as it was a world. The word for 'world' in Hebrew is *olam*... so I think this is a call-out to the name [*ulam*, the porch]." [^1]

> "There is a word in Biblical Hebrew that means 'great spacious building,' and it is *heikhal*. Really? That's it. Yeah." [^2]

> "This tree is interesting... Lehi is calling out with a loud voice; he is speaking from here" — the *debir*, "the place of speech... the place of the Oracle." [^3]

## LDS Connection
If the dream's geography really is the temple's three rooms named in code, then [[1 Nephi 8|1 Nephi 8]] is independent attestation that [[Nephi]]'s family carried a pre-exilic temple liturgy — reinforcing [[The Book of Mormon Matches Pre-Reform First Temple Theology]] and dovetailing with the Eden-return reading in [[Lehi's Dream Recapitulates the Eden-Temple Return]]. The same building, read for its *occupants* rather than its name, yields [[The Great and Spacious Building Is Apostate Temple Insiders Not Outsiders]].
