# The Anachronism List Is Trending Toward the Book of Mormon

## The Claim

When the full history of anachronism accusations from 1830 to today is inventoried,
the great majority have been resolved in the Book of Mormon's favor, a substantial
minority are "trending" that way, and only a small remainder lack any supporting
evidence. The list shrinks over time, which is the opposite of what a fabrication
should produce.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. Matt Roper's inventory: 226 alleged anachronisms

[[Matt Roper]]'s study (the entire *Interpreter* journal vol. 65) scoured roughly a
thousand publications from 1830–2024, cataloging **226** alleged anachronisms,
limited to claims testable archaeologically (plants, animals, materials,
technology, warfare) — excluding revelation-based items like naming Christ in 600
BC, which archaeology cannot test.[^2]

> **Evidence rating:** Strong (a systematic, citation-heavy academic inventory).

### 2. The breakdown trends positive

Of the 226: roughly **174 settled** (evidence found the item was present),
**31 trending** (some evidence/hints, not yet settled), and only **21 currently
unsupported** by any evidence. Counting trending + unsupported gives ~52 that pose
any degree of problem.[^3]

> **Evidence rating:** Strong (specific tallies attributed to a published source).

### 3. Old "anachronisms" were disproved long ago

Cement, roadways, steel in the Old World, and scimitars were once standard critic
claims; many were refuted 60–150 years ago, and critics quietly dropped them.
Critics often inherit lists from predecessors without checking what is still
accurate.[^4]

> **Evidence rating:** Moderate-to-strong (illustrated with named examples; see linked concepts).

## Steelman of the Opposing View

A statistician could object that "settled in favor" sometimes means only that the
item is now *attested somewhere* in the broad time window, not that it fits the
specific Book of Mormon context; and that "trending" is doing rhetorical work for
items (horses, metallurgy) that remain unconfirmed. Roper's charts breaking claims
down by chronological period are meant to answer this by showing genuine directional
movement rather than cherry-picking, but the remaining 21–52 are conceded to be real
open problems.
