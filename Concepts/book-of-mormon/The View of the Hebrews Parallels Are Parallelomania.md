# The View of the Hebrews Parallels Are Parallelomania

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
**Objection:** *View of the Hebrews* and the [[Book of Mormon]] share a long list of "striking parallels" — destruction of Jerusalem, scattering and restoration of Israel, Israelite ancestry of Native Americans, Egyptian writing, lost records, breastplate, Urim and Thummim, a Messiah visiting the Americas, denunciation of pride — that cannot be coincidence.[^1]

**Rebuttal:** The list is a textbook case of *parallelomania* — the term Smoot learned in ancient Near Eastern studies courses at BYU for "overseeing an abundance of parallels because you're looking for them," a confirmation-bias artifact. Sorted honestly, the alleged parallels collapse into three categories that carry no probative weight.[^2]

## The Rebuttal

1. **Category 1 — non-parallels (rated: strong).** Some "parallels" are simply false. The "Ethan/Ether" name parallel and the "Urim and Thummim" parallel evaporate on inspection (see [[The Ethan and Ether Parallel Roberts Rejected]] and [[The Urim and Thummim Is Not a Real Parallel]]). [[B.H. Roberts]] himself called the Ethan/Ether item "not a serious suggestion ... the only most bare possibility."[^3]
2. **Category 2 — superficial parallels (rated: strong).** Other parallels are real but "so vague and broad as to just not really mean anything." Smoot's favorite: "pride is denounced in both." "Every single cultural religious text in the world denounces pride and hubris ... it's in the Quran, it's in the Bible, it's everywhere."[^4] The same goes for "destruction of Jerusalem" (in "every history textbook") and "seas of many waters."
3. **Category 3 — shared biblical sources (rated: strong).** A third set of parallels both books inherit from the Bible/Apocrypha rather than from each other (see [[Shared Biblical Sources Are Not Plagiarism]]). *Steelman:* a critic can argue that the *cumulative weight* of many weak parallels is itself evidence, even if no single one is decisive (Brodie's "cannot be coincidence" move; David Persuit's sustained, more in-depth case). Smoot answers that cumulative force requires each parallel to actually be probative, and a pile of non-parallels, truisms, and biblical commonplaces sums to zero — and that genuine literary dependence demands exclusivity (see [[Direct Literary Dependence Requires Exclusivity]]).

## Best Quotes

> "What we're dealing with here is the phenomenon of parallelomania. ... The idea of like overseeing an abundance of parallels because you're like looking for them. It's kind of a confirmation bias thing."[^2]

> "Pride is denounced in both. For crying out loud, every single cultural religious text in the world denounces pride and hubris."[^4]

> "I see kind of three categories ... parallels that are not actual parallels ... superficial parallels ... shared biblical sources."[^2]
