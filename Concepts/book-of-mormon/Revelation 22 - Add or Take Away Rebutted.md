# Revelation 22 — "Add or Take Away" Rebutted

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
[[Revelation 22.18-19|Revelation 22:18-19]] forbids adding to the Bible; [[Joseph Smith]] added the Book of Mormon; therefore the Book of Mormon is demonic and Joseph Smith is cursed.

## The Rebuttal

### 1. Revelation predates the Bible
The warning attaches to "the words of **the prophecy of this book**" — the book of Revelation, written ~90s AD on Patmos, long before the canon was compiled.

> "If the book of Revelation was written before the Bible even existed, how could he be talking about not adding to the Bible in this verse? Spoiler alert, he wasn't. It's not about adding to the Bible. It's about adding to the book of Revelation." [^1]

The bound King James volume *ends* with Revelation, which makes the misreading understandable — but it is a **compilation**; the warning seals the individual book, not the anthology [^2].

### 2. The same logic refutes itself (Deuteronomy)
[[Deuteronomy 4.2|Deuteronomy 4:2]] uses near-identical "add not / diminish not" language near the *start* of the canon. If such a formula barred all later scripture, most of the Old Testament and the entire New Testament would be illicit additions [^3].

### 3. It was a standard closing formula
Curse-clauses against tampering were common on ancient sacred writings *and* legal contracts (e.g. Byzantine work contracts cursing a defaulter "and their children's children"). It is "the FBI $250,000 fine clause at the end of the book of Revelation," not a ban on new scripture [^4].

### 4. The threatened plagues never came
The text threatens specific plagues — hail, fire, blood, waters turned to blood. None befell Joseph Smith; "a couple boxes are missing." If the verse meant what critics claim and Joseph had violated it, the plagues should have followed [^5].

### 5. Nine New Testament books were written after Revelation
On the Dr. Daff reaction, the hosts press a second chronological blade: charting the composition order (per Bart Ehrman and the Catholic scholar Raymond Brown), Revelation (~94 AD) was *not* the last NT book written — Ephesians, Jude, 2 Thessalonians, 1 Timothy, Titus, 1–3 John, and 2 Peter come after it. If Revelation 22:18-19 closed the canon, every one of those later books — by Paul, John, Jude, and Peter — would itself be an accursed "addition" [^6].
