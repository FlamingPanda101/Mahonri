# The Brother of Jared Models the Endowment Ascent Through the Veil

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Todd McLauchlin]] and [[Hayden Paul]] read the brother of Jared's experience in [[Ether 2]]–[[Ether 3.14|Ether 3]] as the Book of Mormon's most explicit depiction of the temple ascent — a man who, **coming out of the corrupted Babel/Nimrod order**, recovers true priesthood by [[Calling Upon the Name of the Lord Is the True Order of Prayer|calling upon the name of the Lord]] and is brought through the veil into the Lord's presence. The structure they trace:
1. **Chastisement for not calling on the name** ([[Ether 2|Ether 2:14]]) — three hours, in a cloud, for failing to pray in the true order. [^1]
2. **The veil prayer** ([[Ether 3.14|Ether 3:2-5]]) — the brother of Jared, acting as a *high priest on behalf of his brethren*, brings sixteen molten stones to the mount and prays "in the *we*," asking the Lord to touch the stones with his finger; McLauchlin notes the sear-stone is "one of the core things involved in the true order of prayer." [^2]
3. **Passing the veil** — the Lord stretches forth his finger, "the veil was taken from off the eyes of the brother of Jared," and after a fourfold question-and-answer at the veil he is "redeemed from the fall... brought back into my presence" ([[Ether 3.14|Ether 3:13]]). [^3]

Crucially, the brother of Jared sees the *finger* "as the finger of a man, like unto flesh and blood" — a revelation, fresh out of Babel (where God was the impersonal "great unifying oneness of existence"), that the Lord is **anthropomorphic**, embodied. Because he came with such faith *without* the ancient fathers' preparation, the Lord says no man ever came with such exceeding faith ([[Ether 3.14|Ether 3:9, 15]]). [^4] [[Ether 12|Ether 12]] / Ether 4 then universalize it: Moroni invites the Gentiles to "come unto the Lord in the same way that the brother of Jared did" and receive the same — "this is the process of salvation." [^5]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **The fourfold veil dialogue is in the text.** [[Ether 3.14|Ether 3]] does record an initial cry that parts the veil at the hand, followed by three further exchanges before the brother of Jared is brought into the presence — a structure the hosts (and other LDS expositors) reasonably read as a temple-veil pattern. *Rating: the literary structure is really there.* [^3]
2. **Acting "in the *we*" for his brethren is explicit.** The prayer of [[Ether 3.14|Ether 3:2-3]] is intercessory ("do not be angry with thy servant... look upon *us*"), so the high-priest-on-behalf-of-the-people reading is grounded, not imposed. *Rating: solid.* [^2]
3. **It connects to the vault's wider thesis.** This is a specific instance of [[The Book of Mormon Already Contains the Endowment]] and parallels [[Lehi's Dream Recapitulates the Eden-Temple Return]] — multiple Book-of-Mormon theophanies sharing veil/ascent grammar strengthens the pattern claim beyond a single proof-text.
4. **The anthropomorphic-God payoff is text-anchored.** [[Ether 3.14|Ether 3:6-9]] turns on the brother of Jared's *surprise* that the Lord has "flesh and blood," which genuinely supports reading the scene as a corrective to an abstract, Babel-derived deity. *Rating: a strong, often-overlooked detail.* [^4]

**Steelman of the skeptical counter.** A critic notes that **Ether never mentions a temple, a veil-ceremony, or priesthood ordination** — "the veil was taken from off the eyes" is plausibly an idiom for *granted sight/vision*, not a literal ritual curtain, and the sixteen stones are a light source for the barges, given a ritual gloss after the fact. Reading the episode as the *endowment* projects a 19th–21st-century rite back onto an ancient narrative (the same parallelomania risk the vault flags in [[Avoiding Parallelomania Between Ancient Rites and LDS Temple]]). The "came out of Nimrod's Babel" framing also leans on extra-canonical apocrypha (see [[The Garment of Power Descended From Adam to Nimrod]]). A measured reply: the claim is typological, not historical-critical — it does not assert Ether *describes* the modern ceremony, only that the ascent *pattern* (chastening → intercessory veil-prayer → staged dialogue → entering the presence → embodied God) matches it; and the fourfold veil structure and intercessory framing are features of the text itself, so the resemblance is more than free association even if "endowment" is the reader's category.

## Best Quotes
> "He's conversing with the Lord through the veil... and the veil was taken from off the eyes of the brother of Jared, and he saw the finger of the Lord, and it was as the finger of a man, like unto flesh and blood." [^3]

> "He's also acting as a high priest on behalf of his brethren... this is why he's asked to do it." [^2]

> "If you'll exercise faith in me, even as the brother of Jared did... I will manifest unto them the things which the brother of Jared saw. This is the process of salvation — to be in his presence, and you are redeemed from the fall." [^5]
