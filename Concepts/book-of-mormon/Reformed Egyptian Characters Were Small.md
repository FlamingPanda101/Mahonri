# Reformed Egyptian Characters Were Small

## The Claim

Popular replicas depict the plate characters as large, swirling, widely spaced
glyphs. The witnesses and ancient comparanda instead point to *very small,
compact* characters (~1.6–4 mm, roughly 12-point font), which is both attested in
antiquity and necessary for the text to fit.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. Ancient metal inscriptions get this small

Measurements of real ancient inscriptions, including metallic ones, yield
characters as small as ~2 mm; [[Neal Rappleye]] cites examples as small as **1.6
mm**.[^2]

> **Evidence rating:** Strong (direct measurement of physical artifacts).

### 2. About 12-point font size

The witnesses describe the characters as "small and fine," covering the pages. At
~2–4 mm, that corresponds to roughly 12-point font — the panel jokes that even a
trained Egyptologist must squint at hieroglyphs typeset that small.[^3]

### 3. This explains the internal Book of Mormon complaints

The small, laborious engraving explains why Book of Mormon authors (Jacob, Moroni)
complain about the difficulty of writing and the "awkwardness of our hands."[^4]

> **Evidence rating:** Moderate (an internal-consistency / undesigned-coincidence argument).

### 4. It also motivates the black stain

Tiny engraved characters would be hard to read; filling them with a black
stain/cement to stand out against the golden surface (as some witnesses report)
makes practical sense at this scale.[^5]

## Steelman of the Opposing View

A skeptic could argue that selecting the *smallest* attested inscriptions stacks
the deck, and that legibility at 1.6 mm by hand-engraving on metal is itself
remarkable — arguably reinforcing how hard fabrication would be (which the panel
happily concedes; see [[Joseph Smith Lacked the Metallurgical Skill and Time]]).
But the point here is narrower: such small characters are *attested*, so the text
*can* fit — addressing the capacity objection in
[[Trent Horns Word-Count Argument Uses Wrong Parameters]].

### 5. Keystone: the eyewitness and Egyptological testimony

A [[Keystone]] rebuttal corroborates the small-character claim from the documents: Joseph Smith's Wentworth letter says the plates were "filled with engravings... the characters were small and beautifully engraved"; [[John Whitmer]] reported "fine engravings on both sides"; and Egyptologist [[John Gee]] found "even this font size is comparable to the character size of many other ancient engraved Hebrew characters." Keystone also notes a single plate model holds ~128 characters per side (full-scale ~230 on the first plate), far more than critics assume.[^7] See [[The Book of Mormon Fits on the Plates - Sjodahl Hebrew Test]].

## Best Quotes

> "Some of them come out to as small as like 2 millimeters... I have examples that are as small as 1.6 millimeters."[^2]

> "They're not these giant swirling sweeping things like you see in replicas... they're going to be very small, very compact."[^6]

> "It's a marvel that they're able to get these characters that small by hand."[^4]
