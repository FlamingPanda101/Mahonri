# Prophets Were Killed for Preaching the Messiah Before 600 BC

#type/claim

## The Claim

[[Jonah (Plain and Precious Publishing)|Jonah Barnes]] argues that four independent witnesses — [[Lehi]], Jesus, Peter, and Abinadi — all assert that prophets *before* 600 BC explicitly prophesied a coming Messiah and were stoned, cast out, and slain for it. The surviving Old Testament records neither the explicit pre-exilic messianism nor the martyrdoms, so the critic who faults the Book of Mormon for "anachronistic" Christ-talk must level the identical charge against the New Testament [^1]. The apocryphal texts Barnes mines supply the missing record: Isaiah sawn in half, and the deaths of Jeremiah, Micah, and Habakkuk for the same offense [^2].

This converts the standard anachronism objection into a problem for the *Bible*: if pre-Christian Messiah-preaching is impossible, why do Jesus and the apostles insist it happened?

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. Lehi states it directly in 1 Nephi 1 — rate: primary (text)
In 1 Nephi 1:19-20 the Jews mock Lehi for denouncing their wickedness, but turn murderous the moment he "manifested plainly the coming of a Messiah" — "they were angry with him, yea, even as with the prophets of old, whom they had cast out and stoned and slain" [^3]. Lehi equates his own danger with the fate of earlier Messiah-preaching prophets, c. 600 BC.

### 2. Jesus, Peter, and Abinadi say the same thing — rate: primary (multiple attestation)
The pattern repeats across the canon, giving four mutually corroborating voices [^1]:
- **Jesus** — Matthew 23:37: "O Jerusalem... thou that killest the prophets and stonest them which are sent unto thee"; and Luke 11 names the blood of slain prophets [^4].
- **Peter** — Acts 7:51-52 (Stephen quoting the tradition): "which of the prophets have not your fathers persecuted? and they have slain them which showed before of the coming of the Just One" [^5].
- **Abinadi** — Mosiah 13: "there have not been any prophets... but they have spoken concerning... the coming of the Messiah," including that "Moses prophesied unto them concerning the coming of a Messiah" [^6].

Barnes's challenge: ask a Protestant pastor *which* prophets Jesus and Stephen mean — the Old Testament names no prophet martyred specifically for Messianic preaching [^7]. In a second, independent presentation of the same thesis, Barnes adds that Jesus' "woe unto you that are rich" (Luke 6:24) is itself a quotation of the [[1 Enoch|Book of Enoch]] — a text "nobody knew about" until the Ethiopic recovery — reinforcing that the New Testament writers shared "texts and prophecies that they all knew and we don't have" [^11][^12].

### 3. The critic's own objection backfires — rate: strong (turnabout)
Eber Howe's *Mormonism Unveiled* — "the great-grandfather of anti-Mormonism" — charged Joseph Smith with inventing the idea that prophets were slain for preaching the Messiah, since no such record exists. Barnes's reply: "Peter thinks they did, Jesus thinks they did — what were they reading that we don't have?" The objection indicts the New Testament identically [^8].

### 4. The apocrypha supplies the missing martyrdoms — rate: moderate (apocryphal attestation)
The texts Barnes mines record what the traditional canon omits: Isaiah sawn in half, and the persecutions of Micah, Jeremiah, and Habakkuk — deaths "you wouldn't know from reading the traditional Canon" [^2]. The **Testament of Levi** (from the same Testaments of the Twelve Patriarchs as the [[Testament of Naphtali Was Vindicated by the Dead Sea Scrolls|vindicated Naphtali text]]) has Levi tell his own Levite descendants they will "raise him up on a tree and crucify" the Son of God — far too specific for the academy, and exactly the kind of self-incriminating prophecy a priestly editor would suppress [^9].

## Why the Record Was Removed

Barnes's motive-theory: a prophecy naming Judah, Levi, and the reigning priesthood as the future murderers of God was "politically, personally volatile" — "you couldn't design a less attractive prophecy to give." A scribe-editor compiling the texts under the [[Deuteronomistic Corruption of the Old Testament|Deuteronomist reform]] who pulled a scroll saying *your boss will murder the Messiah* would suppress or brand it a hoax rather than risk exile or death [^10]. That those killed for Messiah-preaching were disproportionately *northern* dovetails with the vault's [[The Surviving Old Testament Is the Southern Kingdom's Edition|southern-edition]] thesis.

## Steelman of the Opposing View

A critic can grant the New Testament's "you killed the prophets" language while reading it as *general* prophetic persecution (Jeremiah in the stocks, Uriah in Jer. 26) rhetorically heightened, not a claim that prophets died specifically for *explicit Messianic naming*. On that reading Jesus and Stephen are invoking a known motif of rejected prophets, not a lost corpus of suffering-Messiah preaching — so the Book of Mormon's far more explicit messianism is still a step beyond what the NT requires. The reply: Stephen's wording ("slain them which showed before of the coming of the Just One") ties the killing *to the Messianic content*, which is precisely the datum absent from the surviving Old Testament and present in the apocrypha.
