# Bountiful and Khor Kharfot Match the Book of Mormon

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
After NHM, Arnold offers a second geographical confirmation: the Book of Mormon describes a fertile coastal place called **Bountiful**, where [[Lehi]]'s family built a ship after turning east. Critics treat such a lush spot on the Arabian coast as implausible for a desert journey [^24].

## The Rebuttal
1. **The 12-point match.** "Researchers have also since identified a location in Oman called Khor Kharfot matching all 12 specific requirements that the Book of Mormon gives for the place called Bountiful." *Rating: the Khor Kharfot identification is real in LDS scholarship (Aston, Wellington), but "all 12 requirements" is an apologist tally, not a neutral checklist; some criteria are loose (e.g., "much fruit," "ore," "a mountain").* [^24]
2. **Geographic plausibility restored.** The existence of a fertile, freshwater inlet on the otherwise arid Dhofar coast — directly east of the Nahom turning point — converts an alleged impossibility into a located place: "The place exists. The burial name exists." [^24]
3. **Postdates Joseph.** Like Nahom, the identification rests on field reconnaissance "confirmed by inscriptions found a century and a half after Joseph died," so it could not have been a 19th-century source [^24].
4. **Steelman of the skeptic.** (a) A 12-criterion match is only impressive if the criteria are *specific and independent*; several Bountiful features are generic enough that more than one Dhofar inlet could plausibly satisfy them, and LDS researchers have proposed competing candidates (e.g., Wadi Sayq vs. Salalah). (b) Some "requirements" are inferred from the text rather than stated, giving the matcher freedom. (c) Unlike NHM, there is no *inscription naming Bountiful*; the match is environmental, not epigraphic. Arnold frames the 12/12 match as decisive without noting the criteria's softness or the candidate competition [^24].

## Best Quotes
> "Researchers have also since identified a location in Oman called Khor Kharfot matching all 12 specific requirements that the Book of Mormon gives for the place called Bountiful." [^24]

> "The place exists. The burial name exists. And both are confirmed by inscriptions found a century and a half after Joseph died." [^24]
