# Abish Means "My Father Is a Man" — Vision of Her Father

## The Claim
**Abish** = *Abi* ("my father") + *ish* ("man"), "my father is a man." She is introduced — though both a woman and a servant/slave, doubly unlikely to be named — "on account of a remarkable vision of her **father**" ([[Alma 19.16|Alma 19:16]]), a pun on the *Abi-* element. The genitive is ambiguous, opening rich interpretive possibilities. [^1]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **The etymology.** *Abi* "my father" is common in Hebrew names; [[Paul Hoskisson]] argued the *-ish* element means "man." Strength: **moderate-high**. [^1]
2. **Introduction plays on "father."** Abish is introduced via "a remarkable vision of her **father**," matching *Abi-*. That a *named* woman who is also a *servant* gets this treatment is striking (see [[Women and Slaves Rarely Named in the Book of Mormon]]). Strength: **moderate-high**. [^2]
3. **Grammatical ambiguity = interpretive depth.** "Vision of her father" can be (a) **subjective genitive** — her father had a vision (which converted her); or (b) **objective genitive** — she had a vision *of* her father, i.e. a postmortal appearance of an ancestor; or (c) a vision of her *divine* Father (God). Each is theologically interesting. Bowen treats the grammar fully in his Religious Educator article. Strength: the ambiguity is **real and fertile**, though it means no single reading is certain. [^3]
4. **Cultural fit.** A vision of an ancestor fits a **Mesoamerican** context — e.g. the Yaxchilan lintel depicting a queen's bloodletting and vision-serpent with an ancestor appearing. (Speculative cultural parallel.) Strength: **low-moderate**. [^4]

**Steelman:** A critic argues "Abish" looks like an arbitrary coinage; that *Abi*+*ish* can be assembled to mean almost anything; and that "vision of her father" is a phrase whose ambiguity is being mined for significance after the fact. The genuine grammatical ambiguity also cuts both ways — it lets the analyst pick whichever reading is most striking. Bowen's defense rests on the *pattern* (Abish joining Alma, Nephi, Enos in name-revealing introductions) rather than this case alone.
