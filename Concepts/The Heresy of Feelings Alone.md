# The Heresy of Feelings Alone

## The Point
The mirror image of [[The Heresy of Authority Alone|authority alone]]: granting **ultimate authority to one's own intuitions** — "personal feelings that we think are coming from God" — so they trump every other witness. [[Jacob Hansen]] argues this is just as much an error, because intuitions can be shown mistaken by reason, prophetic teaching, sense data, and outcomes; genuine personal revelation should *align* with the other witnesses rather than override them.[^1][^2]

## Argument
1. **The position defined.** It is "the notion that our personal feelings that we think are coming from God ultimately Trump any of the other Witnesses" — "to Grant ultimate authority to our intuitions." Those holding it "will often reject Church Authority simply because their intuition tells them otherwise."[^1]
2. **The interlocutors' framing.** Hansen samples voices expressing the view: claiming "personal authority over my life that ultimately trumps everything else," framing obedience purely as "my choice," and appealing to the LDS doctrine that "we are entitled to personal revelation" so "a self... has to be our guide."[^1] (Note: the LDS *do* prize personal revelation — see [[Personal Revelation Is the Most Legitimate Path to God]] — Hansen targets only its absolutization.)
3. **Intuitions are defeasible.** "Our intuitions can be mistaken and we can know that they're mistaken because of things like reason, the words of the prophets, sensory data or outcomes" — these "can testify contrary to maybe what we're feeling intuitively."[^2]
4. **Hence the alignment test.** "What we feel God is telling us should align with what the prophets of God have said." Hansen quotes [[Russell M. Nelson]] / Church teaching: someone may be "receiving Revelation or inspiration, but it is not from the source they suppose"; "the personal line does not function independent of the priesthood line"; personal revelation must be "consonant with the teachings of the Lord and His prophets."[^2][^3] (The "personal line / priesthood line" language is [[Dallin H. Oaks]]'s.)
5. **Hansen's own extension.** He adds that personal revelation "should align with reason and empirical evidence," not only with the prophets — folding feelings into the full [[The Collective Witness Model of Knowing|collective witness]] check.[^3]

### Steelman (the feelings-alone side)
The view protects moral conscience and agency against coercive institutions; sincere conscience can be the last safeguard against an erring authority, and (as Hansen's interlocutor notes) it has been "very helpful to my mental health" and may, on its own terms, justify leaving an organization one cannot integrate with one's sense of self.[^1]

## Best Quotes
> "This is the notion that our personal feelings that we think are coming from God ultimately Trump any of the other Witnesses — in other words, it is to Grant ultimate authority to our intuitions." [^1]

> "What we feel God is telling us should align with what the prophets of God have said." [^2]

> "It is crucial that any personal revelation we feel we received be... consonant with the teachings of the Lord and His prophets... In its fullness the personal line does not function independent of the priesthood line." — quoted by Hansen [^3]
