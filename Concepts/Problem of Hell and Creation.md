# The Problem of Hell and Creation

## The Claim

The traditional Christian doctrine of **eternal conscious torment** (ECT) —
whereby finite sinful humans suffer endlessly in hell — cannot be reconciled
with a **perfectly loving and just God** who creates everything **from nothing**
and sustains everything in existence. This constitutes a form of evil that is
**immune to all traditional theodicies**.

## Why It's Stronger Than the General Problem of Evil

Standard theodicies argue that God allows evil because it serves a greater good
(free will, soul-making, virtue cultivation). But as philosopher **[[Jonathan Kvanvig]]**
notes, hell provides no such compensation:

> "At no point in the future will something of value make up for the evil of
> hell or will some reward be granted to those who endure the suffering of hell." [^1]

This makes hell the **paradigm of gratuitous suffering** — pointless evil that
produces no future good for the sufferer.

## The Creation Compound

The problem is *forcefully compounded* by the doctrine of creation from nothing
(*creatio ex nihilo*):

1. God creates each soul from nothing with specific attributes
2. God **sustains** those attributes at every moment
3. God has **perfect foreknowledge** of what each soul will do
4. Yet God creates souls he knows will end in eternal torment
5. God could have created them differently — or not at all

This makes God not merely the *permitter* of hell's evil but its **ultimate
author and sustainer**.

## The Roller Coaster Analogy

Imagine you can conjure objects from nothing — you picture their attributes
and they appear, sustained by your will. If a roller coaster flies off its
tracks, it's because **you willed** its speed, its materials, the physics that
govern it. Now imagine you create *people* this way — their attributes,
propensities, and ultimate destinies are products of your creative will. Would
their eternal torment be just? [^2]

## Scope

This objection does **not** apply to:
- **Universalist Christians** (everyone saved)
- **Annihilationist** views (hell as destruction, not eternal torment)
- **More revisionist/nuanced** conceptions of hell

It targets specifically the view that "humans are so sinful that all of us
*deserve* eternal conscious torment, and God saving *any* of us is an act of
incredible mercy." [^3]

## Patristic Support

[[St. Isaac of Nineveh]] (7th century):

> "It is not the way of a compassionate maker to create rational beings in
> order to deliver them over mercilessly to unending affliction and
> punishment — for things of which he knew even before they were fashioned,
> aware of how they would turn out when he created them, and who nonetheless
> he created." [^4]
