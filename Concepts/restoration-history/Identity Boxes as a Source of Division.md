# Identity Boxes as a Source of Division

## The Claim
Human beings cannot function without categories ("boxes"), since to define is literally to set finite limits and say what something is *not*. But over-investing in *short-term* identity boxes — racial, national, denominational — is a major engine of division, including the friction created by the "box" critics have put [[Brigham Young]] in (autocrat, racist, sexist). The remedy is to subordinate transient identities to the eternal one: child of God.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Definition entails exclusion (rated: strong; conceptual).** "To use language is to define." Calling you "tall" implies "not short"; "male" implies "not female." Dictionaries explain "this is what it is — it's not that." A colleague's wish for "a world without definitions" is incoherent: "When I say I'm going on a trip, that's a specific thing. I'm not cooking."[^2] So the answer is not to abolish categories.

2. **Othering is ancient and cross-cultural (rated: strong; comparative).** Medieval Egyptians read blue eyes as "demonic"; Greeks split the world into Greeks and *barbaroi* (the word mocks non-Greek speech as gibberish); white filmmakers in Africa and a homogeneous St. George community alike react to unfamiliar appearance with "whoa." Looking down on the different is "human nature."[^3] This roots racism partly in a universal cognitive tendency, not merely 19th-century malice — connecting to [[A Man of His Time Without Excusing the Sin]].

3. **Naming as division in Genesis (rated: moderate; scriptural-thematic).** Host [[Stephen Jones]], studying Genesis, notes that creation proceeds by *naming* and dividing (light from dark): "The name he gave it is what made the division." Identity-as-division is woven into the created order, which is why it cannot be eliminated, only rightly ordered.[^4]

4. **President Nelson's eternal-identity teaching (rated: applied).** The cure is prioritizing the eternal/core identity over short-term labels: "All the friction is in the short-term identities." Some boxes matter pragmatically (language, locale), "but the more fundamental core is he's a child of God."[^5] Boxes that "waste away" should not outrank the one that endures.

5. **Application to Brigham (rated: applied).** Much of the hostility to Brigham "comes from these boxes." When Brigham is taken *out* of the box — as in the [[Billy Johnson]] vision — "it's the bigger picture... the eternal perspective."[^6] *Steelman:* a critic may object that calling racism a "box" risks minimizing real harm; Peterson/Jones guard this by insisting truth must be faced ("there's also danger with acting like stuff doesn't exist"), not that categories excuse conduct.

## Best Quotes

> "We can't function without definitions. ... definition by definition is defining, setting up the finite limits of something."[^2]

> "The Greeks divided the world into the Greeks and what they called the barbaroi, the barbarians. ... the word barbaroi... just comes from Greeks imitating their language. It's gibberish."[^3]

> "All the friction is in the short-term identities. ... the more fundamental core is he's a child of God."[^5]
