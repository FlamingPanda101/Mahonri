# NDE - The Dark Tunnel

## The Point
Often at the same time as "the noise," dying persons feel themselves **pulled rapidly through a dark space** — most famously a tunnel [^1]. Moody emphasizes that although informants reach for many different metaphors (cave, well, valley, funnel, void, cylinder), they are all straining to express the **same single experience** [^1]. The convergence of so many independent witnesses on one underlying image is part of what makes the NDE pattern notable.

## Evidence
1. The range of terms used: "a cave, a well, a trough, an enclosure, a tunnel, a funnel, a vacuum, a void, a sewer, a valley, and a cylinder" — different words for one idea [^1].
2. A man recalling a childhood arrest under ether described a rhythmic ringing and "moving through this long dark place... like a sewer or something" [^1].
3. Another likened the passage to "riding on a roller coaster train... going through this tunnel at a tremendous speed" after a respiratory arrest [^1].
4. A man near death felt himself in "an utterly black, dark void... like being in a cylinder which had no air in it... halfway here, and halfway somewhere else" [^2].
5. Religious framing: one man, traveling down a path through "a very dark, very deep valley," later realized "now I know what the Bible means by 'the valley of the shadow of death,' because I've been there" [^2].

## Best Quotes
> "I was in an utterly black, dark void.... I felt as if I were moving in a vacuum, just through blackness. Yet, I was quite conscious.... It was a feeling of limbo, of being halfway here, and halfway somewhere else." [^2]

## LDS Connection
The "valley of the shadow of death" (Psalm 23:4) is the very phrase one informant reached for, and it frames death not as an end but as a passage one moves *through*. This fits the LDS view that death is a transition of the conscious spirit from mortality to the spirit world (Alma 40), not extinction — the darkness is a corridor, not a terminus.
