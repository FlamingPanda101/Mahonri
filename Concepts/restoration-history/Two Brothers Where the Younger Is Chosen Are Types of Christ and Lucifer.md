# Two Brothers Where the Younger Is Chosen Are Types of Christ and Lucifer

#type/claim

## The Claim
Host **Hayden Carroll** argues that the Bible's recurring **"two brothers, the elder forfeits the birthright and a younger/righteous brother is chosen"** motif is a *type of the premortal relationship of Christ and Lucifer*. In case after case the firstborn-by-birth is passed over for a chosen son who is "in similitude of Christ," while the displaced elder is cast out for sin/pride: Cain/Abel, Jacob/Esau, Ishmael/Isaac (the "strongest," with [[Hebrews 11.17|Isaac called "only begotten"]] though Ishmael preceded him), Reuben (loses the birthright to Joseph), Adonijah/Solomon ("Prince of Peace"), and in the Book of Mormon Nephi over Laman/Lemuel. The **Day of Atonement two goats** ([[Leviticus 16|Leviticus 16]]) — one slain as Yahweh, one cast out as Azazel ("could be in similitude of Lucifer") — and Jesus likening himself to the lifted-up serpent ([[Numbers 21.8-9|Numbers 21]]; John 3:14) extend the pattern. Hayden offers it tentatively ("swimming in 'I don't know' soup") as evidence the LDS doctrine "has a scriptural leg to stand on." This is the *Christological* application of the [[The Younger-Brother Motif Legitimizes Solomons Succession|younger-brother motif]] the vault documents as Davidic apologetic.[^1][^2]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The chosen-younger list is relentless and Christ-typed.** Abel (shepherd, killed for righteousness), Isaac, Jacob, Joseph (over Reuben, who forfeits for sin), Solomon — repeatedly the chosen son is "in similitude of Christ" and the elder cast out for "sin, foolishness, pride." (The vault independently catalogs this list at [[The Younger-Brother Motif Legitimizes Solomons Succession]].) *Rating: the pattern is real and pervasive in Genesis–Kings; the Christ/Lucifer *referent* is the LDS interpretive overlay.*[^1]
2. **Isaac the "only begotten" though not firstborn.** [[Hebrews 11.17|Hebrews 11:17]] calls Isaac Abraham's "only begotten son" despite Ishmael — Hayden's "strongest" case that *the chosen son need not be the biological firstborn*, applied to Christ ("firstborn" by righteousness) and Lucifer. See [[Jesus and Satan Being Spirit Brothers Follows From Rejecting the Trinity]]. *Rating: Hebrews 11:17 does call Isaac "only begotten"; the premortal application is speculative.*[^2]
3. **The two goats as Christ and Azazel.** From the Mishnah (*Yoma*), two identical goats: one bears **Yahweh** (sacrificed to redeem Israel), the other **Azazel** (a wilderness demon, cast off a cliff) — "two beings of the same stuff," one chosen as sacrifice, one cast down. See [[Leviticus 16]] and [[The Right and Left Hand of God Are the Day of Atonement Goats]]. *Rating: the two-goats datum is solid; "Azazel = Lucifer" leans on later Enochic tradition (a contested move the vault flags elsewhere).*[^3]
4. **Jesus likens himself to the serpent.** [[Numbers 21.8-9|the brazen serpent]] applied to himself ([[John 3.14 Compares Christ to the Serpent Not Just the Lifting Up|John 3:14]]) shows Christ comparing himself "to the same type of being that Lucifer is" (the Eden serpent), yet as the Son lifted up for life — the good/counterfeit pair again. See [[The Nehushtan Is a Healing Divine Serpent the Nephites and Early Christians Valued]]. *Rating: a real Johannine self-comparison; the "same stuff as Lucifer" gloss is the inference.*[^4]
5. **"First shall be last."** Lucifer as "son of the morning" (seemingly first) cast down for pride; Christ lifted up for goodness — "the first shall be last and the last shall be first," so one "becomes the firstborn" by choosing God. Hayden repeatedly brackets this as conjecture. *Rating: explicitly offered as speculation, not assertion.*[^5]

**Steelman of the skeptical counter.** (a) The chosen-younger-son is a *cross-cultural folkloric type* found far beyond Israel, so its presence need not encode any premortal Christ/Lucifer drama; mainstream readers take it as election-by-grace (God choosing the unexpected) or, critically, as [[The Younger-Brother Motif Legitimizes Solomons Succession|Davidic-dynastic apologetic]]. (b) Most of these elder brothers (Cain, Esau, Reuben, Adonijah) are *not* Satan-figures but flawed humans, so reading them all as "Lucifer" flattens distinct stories. (c) "Azazel = Lucifer" and the serpent-as-Christ both rely on *later* traditions read back into the Torah. (d) Calling Christ "firstborn" *by achievement* sits uneasily with the New Testament's "firstborn of all creation" (Colossians 1:15), which Trinitarians read as eternal priority, not earned status. **A Latter-day Saint reply:** the claim is offered as *typology and cumulative resonance*, not proof — the repeated form (a chosen son in Christ's similitude; an elder who forfeits) is suggestive of a deeper pattern, and the LDS premortal-council framework supplies a coherent referent; Hayden's own "I don't know soup" caveat keeps it provisional, and the apologetic goal is modest: that the doctrine "has a scriptural leg to stand on," not that the types compel the conclusion.

## Best Quotes
> "There is this motif that happens over and over and over again in the Old Testament where there are two brothers — one of them seems to either be more righteous or is chosen, and the other one seems to deserve the throne or the birthright but instead gives that up because of sin, foolishness, pride. And many of these people are in similitude of Christ." — Hayden Carroll[^1]

> "What if all of these stories are trying to tell us something about what happened before this earth?... Lucifer's name is son of the morning, which seems like he's the first son, but he's cast down because of his pride, and Christ because of his goodness is lifted up." — Hayden Carroll[^5]
