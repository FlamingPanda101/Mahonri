# "Cult" — A Contaminated Pejorative Term

## The Claim
The word "cult" carries no neutral meaning in popular use; its negative sense was *manufactured* by mid-20th-century evangelicals against groups including the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. Scholars therefore prefer **"new religious movement" (NRM)**.

## Supporting Evidence
- Originally "cult" was a neutral scholarly term for ritual-focused devotion (e.g. "the cult of the Virgin Mary" within Catholicism) *(scholar's account)* [^1].
- [[Ernst Troeltsch]] introduced "cult" as a fuzzy residual category for mystical/metaphysical groups — not pejorative *(history of the term)* [^2].
- By mid-century the term was "hijacked" by American Protestant evangelical theologians disputing the LDS, Jehovah's Witnesses, Christian Scientists, and Seventh-day Adventists — applying it to mean "not Christian / not a proper church" *(scholar's account)* [^3].

## Best Quote
> "The term cult got hijacked by essentially American Protestant evangelical theologians who were engaged in... doctrinal disputes with groups like the Church of Latter-day Saints... they applied the term cult in a highly pejorative sense... as a way of basically saying they're not Christian." — [[Lorne Dawson]] [^3]

## Counter-arguments / related
- The neutral replacement and its definition: [[Family Resemblance Definition of Cults]].
- The host's concession: by a "strangeness/heresy" criterion, LDS *are* a "cult" — which only shows the criterion is useless (see [[Where Mormons Rank on the Church-Sect Continuum]]).
