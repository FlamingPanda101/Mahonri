# Prophets Point to Christ Through Their Failings

## The Claim
The patriarchs and prophets — including [[Joseph Smith]] — point us to Christ not only through their strengths but *through their failings*. Because every scriptural hero "sins, they all fall down, they all fall short," they function as imperfect signposts whose very inadequacy directs attention beyond themselves to the one perfect Exemplar.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The Hebrew Bible refuses to airbrush its heroes (rated: strong, scriptural).** [[Don Bradley]] enumerates: Noah "gets off the Ark, grows some grapes, makes wine, gets drunk"; Abraham "has an unhappy sort of family story... with Sarah and Hagar — that's not a model for family life"; Isaac plays favorites; Jacob deceives his father for the blessing; Jacob's sons mistreat their brother Joseph.[^2] The scriptures present "the people that God chooses [as] very, very human."

2. **The function is theological, not merely honest (rated: moderate, interpretive).** "This isn't to say... let's look down on them, because actually what it's showing is even the people that God chooses are very, very human. And we need... redemption. ... We have an Exemplar. That's Jesus Christ."[^3] Their flaws make them fellow sinners needing the Savior, so they witness to Christ by contrast. *Steelman:* a critic can flip this into an unfalsifiable shield — if prophetic *virtue* proves divine calling and prophetic *vice* also "points to Christ," then no behavior could ever count against a prophet's authority. The move risks immunizing the office from disconfirmation; it works as devotional theology but not as an evidential test. (Bradley elsewhere supplies an evidential test via fruits and actions — see [[Judge the Book of Abraham by Its Fruits]] and [[Testing Interpretation by the Prophet's Actions]].)

3. **Applies to Joseph Smith and the prophets-as-patterns point (rated: moderate).** "Joseph Smith presents us certain patterns... But those patterns are not the same as them being our exemplar." "Nobody's a perfect example... Well, one person is" — Christ.[^1] The prophets are patterns to imitate in part, exemplars only in pointing past themselves.

## Best Quotes

> "In some ways, the people in the Bible and even Joseph Smith, the prophets, they point beyond themselves by the very fact of their failings. They don't just point us to Christ by their strengths. They also point us to Christ through their failings."[^1]

> "Noah gets off the Ark, grows some grapes, makes wine, gets drunk. ... Jacob deceives his father to get the blessing."[^2]

> "Even the people that God chooses are very, very human. And we need redemption. ... We have an Exemplar. That's Jesus Christ."[^3]
