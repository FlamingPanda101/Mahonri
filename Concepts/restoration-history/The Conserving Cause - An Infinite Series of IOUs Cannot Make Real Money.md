# The Conserving Cause - An Infinite Series of IOUs Cannot Make Real Money

## The Point
The Thomistic proof is not about how the universe *began*; it is about what **conserves** things in existence at every moment "as opposed to being annihilated." [^1] So it is untouched by Hume's question, "if each member of a causal series is explained by an earlier member, why need a first cause?" Hume is right about *linear* series of things generated over time (Fido from his parents, theirs from theirs, possibly back forever). But the proof concerns a **hierarchical** series in which "all causes other than the first are purely instrumental, having no causal power of their own." [^2] Stretching such a series to infinity changes nothing: a chain of borrowers can never produce what none of them owns. "An infinite series of such conserving causes can no more get you a real dog than an infinite series of IOUs can give you real money." [^3]

## Evidence
1. **Two kinds of series.** Hume's infinite regress is "a series of things generated in time out of preexisting materials." The Thomistic question is different: why does Fido, "or anything at all — including the matter out of which they are made and their causal powers — persist in existence even for an instant," given that nothing in their essence entails their existence? [^4] Even an infinite past chain of dogs leaves *each* dog needing to be conserved in being right now. [^5]
2. **The IOU analogy.** "Just as IOUs have to be backed at some point with real money, so too must any hierarchical series of causes which impart existence to Fido at any moment terminate in something which, since it is Subsistent Existence Itself, needn't have existence imparted to it by anything else." [^3]
3. **The logical form forces a first member** (Barry Miller). An explanation of the form "A is being caused to G by [B inasmuch as it is being caused to G by (C inasmuch as ...)]" cannot actually be *completed* while leaving the regress open; the only way to finish the sentence is to name a first member M. [^6] Substituting "Fido" and "exist," the only non-arbitrary stopping point is an M that just *is* Subsistent Existence Itself. [^7]
4. **No "fallacy of composition."** First, part-to-whole reasoning is not always fallacious (every Lego brick red → the wall is red). [^8] Second and more importantly, the proof never reasons from parts to the whole universe: it gets going from *any single* essence-existence composite — one stone, one dog — which already requires a conserving cause. The claim about the universe as a whole is a *consequence*, never a *premise*. [^9]
5. **Not "everything has a cause."** The premise is only that "something whose essence and existence are really distinct requires a cause"; God falls outside it because in Him essence and existence are identical, so the "What caused God?" retort "utterly... miss[es] the point." [^10]

## Best Quotes
> "The Thomistic proof is concerned instead with what conserves a thing in existence at any moment of time, as opposed to being annihilated." [^1]

> "An infinite series of such conserving causes can no more get you a real dog than an infinite series of IOUs can give you real money. Just as IOUs have to be backed at some point with real money, so too must any hierarchical series of causes which impart existence to Fido at any moment terminate in something which, since it is Subsistent Existence Itself, needn't have existence imparted to it by anything else." [^3]

> "Even if the universe has always existed, or is part of a multiverse which has always existed, this would not change the outcome of the Thomistic proof in the slightest." [^11]

## LDS Connection
Feser's distinction between *originating* and *conserving* causation is a genuine gift to theistic apologetics against atheism, and a Latter-day Saint can wield it freely: it neutralizes the lazy "but the universe might be eternal, so no Creator is needed" move by showing that an eternal cosmos would *still* depend, moment by moment, on a sustaining cause (compare [[God as the Present Sustaining Cause - The Band Plays On]]). It also shows why "Who made God?" misfires (see [[Who Made God - Only What Begins Needs a Cause]]). The divergence is what the chain must terminate in. For Feser the IOUs must be backed by **Subsistent Existence Itself** — a simple, purely actual Being (see [[Subsistent Existence Itself - Ipsum Esse Subsistens]]). The Restoration agrees the regress of *conditioned, organized* being must terminate in God, but holds that the ultimate "currency" is not bare existence conferred *ex nihilo*: matter and intelligence are themselves eternal and uncreated (D&C 93:29), so they are not IOUs awaiting backing in *existence* at all (see [[Intelligence Is Uncreated and Eternally Has Agency]] and [[Creation Ex Nihilo Is Not Biblical]]). God conserves by *organizing, vivifying, and governing* what eternally is, not by holding sheer being over nothingness. The here-and-now dependence of the world on God is common ground; the analysis of that dependence as dependence for *existence itself* upon a *simple* God is where the Restoration parts from Feser.
