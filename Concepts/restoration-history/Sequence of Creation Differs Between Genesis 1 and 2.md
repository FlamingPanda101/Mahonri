#type/claim

# The Order of Creation Differs Between Genesis 1 and Genesis 2

## The Claim

The two creation accounts narrate the *sequence* of creation in incompatible orders. This is the unifying point underlying both the plants and the animals contradictions, and it is what makes the "zoomed-in sixth day" harmonization impossible.[^1]

| Element | Genesis 1 (Priestly) | Genesis 2 (non-Priestly) |
|---|---|---|
| Plants | Day 3 — **before** humanity | **After** the human |
| Fish & birds | Day 5 — **before** humanity | Birds **after** the human |
| Land animals | Day 6 — **before** humanity | **After** the human |
| Humanity | Day 6 — **last** | **First** living thing formed |

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Plants ordered oppositely (decisive).** Genesis 1 = vegetation on day three before humans; Genesis 2 = no vegetation until after the human. See [[Humanity Created Before Plants in Genesis 2]].[^2] Evidence rating: **decisive**.

2. **Animals ordered oppositely (decisive).** Genesis 1 = land animals on day six before humans, birds/fish earlier; Genesis 2 = all animals and birds after the human. See [[Humanity Created Before Animals in Genesis 2]].[^3] Evidence rating: **decisive**.

3. **Humanity's position is inverted (decisive).** In Genesis 1 humanity is the *climax*, created last; in Genesis 2 the human is the *first* living thing formed, with everything else following. Evidence rating: **decisive** — this is the structural heart of the contradiction.

**Steelman:** A harmonizer can attempt to neutralize *each* element individually (pluperfect verbs for animals; "field plants" only for vegetation). But the contradictions stack: even if one device rescued the animals, it would not rescue the plants, and vice versa. The inverted *position of humanity* (first vs. last) is not reachable by either device. McClellan's conclusion: the account "places the creation of humanity before the existence of any plant life or animal life on earth."[^1]

## Best Quotes

> "There's no plausible case to make that what we have in Genesis 2:4b through the end of Genesis 3 is a zoomed-in version of the sixth day of creation because it places the creation of humanity before the existence of any plant life or animal life on earth." [^1]
