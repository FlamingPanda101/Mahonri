# Ruach Elohim - The Feminine Spirit Hovering Over Creation

## The Point
[[Mandy Green]] finds the divine feminine in the grammar and imagery of [[Genesis 1.1|Genesis 1:2]] itself: the *ruach Elohim* — "Spirit of God" — is a grammatically feminine noun, and the verb describing her action over the waters means to fold over or incubate, evoking a mother bird. Read with the plural *Elohim*, the opening of Genesis presents deity as a plurality that includes a feminine, maternal presence.

## Evidence
- **A plurality of gods from the first word.** *Elohim*, used throughout Genesis, "is a Hebrew plural"; Green follows Joseph Smith ("the head of the gods called a council of the gods") and notes the Dead Sea Scrolls — which "predate our Hebrew Bible by about 100 years" — contain "203 instances used as a plural."[^1][^2] The creation verb *bara* is "a verb used for divine activity," the same word used for building the temple and tabernacle.[^3]
- **The Spirit is feminine, "always and forever."** "The spirit of God, the *ruach Elohim*... *ruach* is the word for spirit, but it's a feminine noun. Always and forever, it's a feminine noun. Now in Hebrew, things are masculine or they are feminine. And guess what? The masculine and feminine matter."[^4]
- **Maternal, incubating imagery.** The participle rendered "hovering" "actually means to like fold over, almost incubate. It has some very feminine imagery." Green links it to Christ's image of "the hen gathering her chicks" — "this divine wing, this divine incubator that's hovering over the face of the great deep."[^5]
- **Three feminine clues in two verses.** With *Elohim* (plural), *ruach* (feminine Spirit), and *Wisdom* (the creative agent of Wisdom of Solomon), Green concludes the Mother is present at creation: "We've got Elohim, we've got spirit, and wisdom. I mean, that's two verses in. Do you think that she was not part of it?"[^6] See [[Wisdom Personified as the Mother in Heaven]].

## Best Quotes
> "*Ruach* is the word for spirit, but it's a feminine noun. Always and forever, it's a feminine noun... the masculine and feminine matter."[^4]

> "This word hovering actually means to like fold over, almost incubate. It has some very feminine imagery... this divine wing, this divine incubator that's hovering over the face of the great deep."[^5]

## LDS Connection
The reading grounds a feminine divine not in later or syncretistic sources but in the Hebrew of the canonical creation text and in pre-Masoretic witnesses (the Dead Sea Scrolls' plural *Elohim*). For Latter-day Saints this dovetails with the doctrine of Heavenly Parents and divine plurality recovered from Israel's older religion — see [[Ugaritic Texts Reveal Israels Original Pantheon]] for the El-and-consort divine family, and [[Wisdom Personified as the Mother in Heaven]] for the same Mother appearing under the title Wisdom.
