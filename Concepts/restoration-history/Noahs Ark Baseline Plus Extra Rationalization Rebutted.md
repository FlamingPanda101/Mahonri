# Noah's Ark Baseline Plus Extra Rationalization Rebutted

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)

Christian apologists argue that the two animal-count instructions in the flood narrative do not contradict: "Two of every kind is the baseline so life doesn't go extinct. Seven of some animals is because they were used for sacrifice and survival afterward. That's not two versus seven like a mistake. It's two of every kind and an extra of specific animals." [^1] In this view, Genesis 6:19–20 establishes the minimum (one pair each for survival), and Genesis 7:2–3 adds supplementary animals (extra clean pairs for sacrifice) — a consistent, two-step instruction rather than two conflicting accounts.

## The Rebuttal — Why This Rationalization Fails

### 1. It Presupposes Univocality (Circular Reasoning)

Like the God's-visibility harmonizations, this argument only exists because the apologist has ALREADY decided the texts cannot contradict. "Again we have text A and text B. They seem to contradict. If you presuppose univocality, you refuse to accept the notion that they contradict, then you have to create idea or argument or rationalization C — the entirely new separate third thing that's not in the text that functions as a unifying framework." [^2] The conclusion is assumed in the premise. See [[Begging the Question in Biblical Inerrancy Apologetics]] and [[Rationalization C - The Third Thing Harmonization]].

### 2. The Bird Problem — the Rationalization's Fatal Flaw

The baseline-plus-extra theory works for land animals: unclean animals get two (baseline), clean animals get seven pairs (baseline + extra). But Genesis 7:3 says "fowls also of the air by sevens" — ALL birds get seven pairs, with NO clean/unclean distinction for birds. Meanwhile, Genesis 6:20 says "two of every sort" of birds. One text says ALL birds = two; the other says ALL birds = seven pairs. "There are no birds of the air that only come in one pair. They're all seven pairs." [^3]

For the harmonization to work, the apologist must either:
- Ignore the phrase "fowls also of the air by sevens" (ignoring the text)
- Invent an unstated clean/unclean distinction among birds not found in any biblical text (inventing the "third thing")
- Argue the bird clause doesn't mean what it says (special pleading)

### 3. The "Baseline + Extra" Framework Is Not in Either Text

"This is not what the text says because it says two of everything. All the *behemah*, which means the land animals, all the birds of the air. Two of all the birds of the air." [^4] Neither passage acknowledges the other or describes its own number as a "baseline." The framework is entirely imposed by the harmonizer — it is the "rationalization C" that exists nowhere in Genesis.

### 4. The Source-Critical Consensus Explains the Contradiction Better

The two animal counts are not two stages of one instruction; they are two COMPLETE, SELF-CONTAINED instructions from two different sources. In the Priestly account (P), the instruction is simply "two of everything" — complete and sufficient. In the non-Priestly account (J), the instruction is a differentiated command with clean/unclean categories — also complete and sufficient. Each account, read alone, makes perfect internal sense. The contradiction only emerges when a redactor wove them together. See [[Noahs Ark Source Criticism Two Flood Accounts]].

### 5. The Scholarship Overwhelmingly Affirms the Contradiction

McClellan cites seven major Genesis commentaries — David Carr, David Petersen, Jan Christian Gertz, Ron Hendel, Claus Westermann, Bill Arnold, and E.A. Speiser — all of which affirm that the two-source flood hypothesis is well-supported and that the animal count is one of the contradictions between P and J. "All of these are going to point out, look, we've got two sources here that have been woven together. And they're going to talk about the evidence for this. And they're going to point out the fact that one says only two of all kinds and another says two of most kinds, seven pairs of others, is a contradiction." [^5]

## Best Quotes

> "The notion that two is the baseline but then you add on to it seven pairs of the clean animals so that they can be later sacrificed is the most common attempt to rationalize away the contradiction." [^2]

> "It doesn't fit the text... When you get to the distinction between clean and unclean, no such distinction is asserted for the birds of the air. So you have in one passage two of all the birds of the air and in another passage seven pairs of all the birds of the air." [^6]

> "To just assert no, they actually fit is just false. They don't fit because you have to account for the fact that one says two of every bird of the air and the other says seven pairs of every bird of the air." [^7]
