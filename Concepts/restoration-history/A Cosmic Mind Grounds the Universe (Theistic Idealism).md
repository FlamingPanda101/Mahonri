# A Cosmic Mind Grounds the Universe (Theistic Idealism)

## The Point
The conclusion of the [[The Cosmic Conscious Argument for God|cosmic conscious argument]]: the physical universe is grounded in mind, and the best reading of that fact is *theistic idealism* — reality is the creation of, and dependent on, a cosmic conscious God.[^1] [[Inspiring Philosophy|Michael Jones]] holds the quantum evidence makes "an idealistic or dualistic picture of reality" unavoidable, "and the conclusion of theism simply follows."[^1] Physical reality is "a creation for us," not a self-standing substance independent of mind.[^2]

## Evidence
Jones closes by defending this idealism against the *Quantum Enigma* objection: if an omniscient observer existed, his observation would already have collapsed every wavefunction, so he cannot be constantly observing — hence "he is not omniscient."[^3] Jones gives two replies grounded in God's nature, not in physics:
1. **God is timeless.** The objection "assumes God is a contingent being like us who has to obtain knowledge in space-time."[^4] But God "would be outside of space-time altogether and contain all the information of space-time in a single timeless instance."[^5] He does not pass *from* ignorance *to* knowledge of a collapse, so "there would never be a time before from his perspective where he did not have knowledge of the system" — which is why his knowing does not cause collapse in our experiments.[^5]
2. **God is immaterial, not a physical observer.** God "would be an immaterial conscious mind," and "having a conscious experience of physical reality independent of ours would not be necessary."[^6] Physical reality is a creation *for us*; God "would simply know what we choose and experience in each moment of time in a single timeless instance."[^2]

The capstone quotation comes from Richard Conn Henry and Stephen Palmquist: if there is no mind-independent reality, "then a theistic view of our existence becomes the only rational alternative to solipsism" — and Jones has already argued (see [[Solipsism Collapses into Theism Not Realism]]) that solipsism itself collapses into theism.[^7] The result is a cosmic mind as the ground of all reality, complementing the independent line in [[Consciousness Precedes Existence - Neuroscience and Pre-Existence]] that "matter arises out of consciousness."

## Best Quotes
> "The evidence is too overwhelming to deny an idealistic or dualistic picture of reality, and the conclusion of theism simply follows." [^1]

> "God would be outside of space-time altogether and contain all the information of space-time in a single timeless instance." [^5]

## LDS Connection
A cosmos *grounded in and made for conscious agents* harmonizes with the LDS purpose of creation: the worlds were created so that intelligences could progress, and "this is my work and my glory — to bring to pass the immortality and eternal life of man" (Moses 1:39) — i.e., physical reality is, as Jones says, "a creation for us." Two honest qualifications keep the comparison straight: (1) LDS theology is not strict *idealism* — "all spirit is matter" (D&C 131:7–8) and God is an embodied person, so reality is mind-and-(refined-)matter rather than mind-only; and (2) the LDS God is *within* time in his experience more than Jones's strictly timeless deity, which is closer to classical theism. The shared core is that consciousness/mind is the fundamental and creative reality, not an accidental byproduct of dead matter. Compare [[Maybe the Universe Itself Is the Necessary Being]] for the materialist alternative this argument rejects.
