# Accusations Cannot Transmute Into Evidence

## The Claim
The Brenda Miles / Marion Smith case never produced a single piece of physical evidence across more than a decade and three rounds of litigation. The Ward Radio thesis is that the accusers' strategy *substitutes accumulation for corroboration*: pile up enough allegations, media, and lawsuits and treat the pile itself as proof. But "all the accusations in the world simply cannot combine and magically transmute into evidence." The tell is the *order of operations* — victims pursued newspapers, book deals, and podcasts before (or instead of) law enforcement, because those venues, unlike police, do not demand evidence.

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. The alleged crimes would have left abundant traces — and left none (strong)
The charges against the Mileses included near-drowning, forcing children to drink fecal matter, rape, and videotaped abuse — yet "no one ever got sick," there were "no marks, no blood, no bodily fluids, no trace," and "no videotapes were ever found." Crimes of this physical magnitude, repeated on small children, cannot plausibly leave zero forensic footprint [^1]. This parallels the Lehi pattern (see [[Absence of Physical Evidence in the Lehi Ritual Abuse Cases]]).

### 2. Missing evidence was patched with unfalsifiable add-ons (strong — mechanism)
Each evidentiary gap was filled by a new auxiliary hypothesis rather than treated as disconfirmation: the children stayed silent because the Mileses "threatened to kill the children if they told"; they sat quietly through prolonged abuse because they "must have injected them with sedatives to numb the pain." [[Marion Smith]]'s own stated reasoning was a non-sequitur — a granddaughter being "flat and unresponsive" one day "clearly … meant a local man had secretly nearly drowned her" [^2]. A theory that converts every absence into confirmation is unfalsifiable.

### 3. The venue order betrays the evidentiary weakness (strong)
"Alleged victims went to newspapers and book deals before they went to law enforcement. Today, they seek out podcasts instead of police. Unlike podcasts, police demand evidence" [^3]. Two of Smith's daughters co-authored a 1992 *book* accusing Salt Lake suburbanites of SRA (forward by [[Paul Whitehead]]) — publishing the allegations rather than presenting prosecutable evidence [^4].

### 4. Courts treated the accusations as legally empty (strong)
After the 1995 investigation "turned up absolutely nothing" and the 2018 relitigation was dismissed *with prejudice*, the episode's summary judgment is epistemic, not merely rhetorical: "to the lament of the conspiracy theorists, all the accusations in the world simply cannot combine and magically transmute into evidence" [^5]. See [[No Evidence the LDS Church Suppressed the Investigations]].

## Steelman (Opposing View)
The strongest opposing case: much child sexual abuse genuinely leaves no lasting physical evidence, abusers are routinely believed only after many disclosures, and a "go to the media" pattern can reflect victims who feel failed by police rather than weakness in the underlying claim. Demanding forensic proof can re-victimize real victims. This rebuttal grants all of that *in general* — but it turns on the specific charges here (drowning, mutilation, sedation, mass videotaping, human sacrifice) that *necessarily* produce corroborating traces (medical records, bodies, tapes, birth certificates), and on the fact that the disconfirmations were absorbed by ad hoc patches and the accusations migrated to *book deals and podcasts*, the venues that pay and do not test. The claim is not "abuse never lacks evidence"; it is that *this* class of extraordinary claim, with this evidentiary record, fails.

## Counter-objection: "absence of evidence is not evidence of absence"
A critic may invoke the maxim to say the missing tapes/bodies prove nothing. But absence of evidence *is* evidence of absence precisely when evidence *should* be present if the claim were true (the expectation condition). Sacrificed infants imply missing birth certificates and bodies; videotaped abuse implies tapes; mutilation implies medical findings. When the predicted evidence is systematically absent across a decade and multiple investigations, the rational posterior drops sharply. See [[Absence of Physical Evidence in the Lehi Ritual Abuse Cases]] and [[An Innocent Cleared by Every Investigator Was Still Driven to Death]].
