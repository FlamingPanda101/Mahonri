# Younger Generations Less Religious Forecasts Continued Decline

#type/claim

## The Claim
Despite reports that younger generations are "flattening the curve" of religious decline, the cohort-replacement math points the other way: younger generations are less religious than older ones, so as older, more-religious cohorts die off, average U.S. religiousness will likely keep declining "if nothing else changes."[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The cohort-replacement mechanism (Evidence: strong demographic logic).** "The younger generation is less religious than the older generations.... they will become the older generations, and unless they get a lot more religious ... you're going to see ... a decrease in religiousness over time, just because the older generations are more religious."[^2] This is straightforward population dynamics.

2. **"Flattening" may be temporary (Evidence: hedged).** "The flattening out might just be for now ... we might continue to see a decline. Again, if nothing else changes."[^3] Dyer resists over-reading recent leveling-off as a true reversal.

3. **Counter-possibility acknowledged (Evidence: balanced).** He cites President Nelson's expectation of "a lot of miracles before the end" and allows for genuine resurgence, plus the soul-filling pull of religion (see [[Secularism Does Not Fill the Soul Like Religion]]). The LDS Church specifically may "stay fairly strong" even amid general decline — "we just don't know."[^4]

**Steelman of the opposing view:** Linear cohort-replacement projections have repeatedly failed because they assume no within-cohort change, yet people often become *more* religious with age, parenthood, and mortality salience ("religious aging"). Recent data (the very "flattening" Dyer half-dismisses) and some signals of Gen-Z religious curiosity could mark an inflection the model can't capture. The "if nothing else changes" hedge is doing heavy lifting — in religion, things change (revivals, conversions, immigration of religious populations). The forecast is a projection of current trends, not a prediction.

## Best Quotes

> "The younger generation is less religious than the older generations."[^2]

> "Unless they get a lot more religious when the older generations start to die off ... you're going to see ... a decrease in religiousness over time."[^2]

> "President Nelson said that, 'Hey, we're going to see a lot of miracles before the end.'"[^4]
