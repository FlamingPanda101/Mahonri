# Utah Suicide Rate Implicates the Church (Objection Rebutted)

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
A common claim holds that the LDS Church has a uniquely bad mental-health problem — citing high Utah suicide rates and "Mormon depression" — and infers that the Church itself causes these outcomes.

## The Rebuttal

1. **The forest-for-the-trees error (rated: strong).** The narrative looked only at Utah without its neighbors. Utah sits inside the national "suicide belt," a corridor of mountain-west states with the highest suicide rates. See [[Big Data Cannot Diagnose the Individual]].[^1]

2. **Regional comparison reverses the inference (rated: strong).** Within its region Utah is "absolutely average... not unique." Crucially, Colorado and Wyoming — which border Utah and have far *lower* percentages of Latter-day Saints — have *higher* suicide rates than Utah. So if anything, the regional data run opposite to the Church-blaming thesis.[^2]

3. **Perspective determines the conclusion (rated: strong, epistemic).** Looking only at Utah, "we're doing good"; looking nationally, "we're not doing so good." The same numbers support opposite headlines depending on the comparison frame — a caution that "this is data, therefore objective truth" is naive.[^3] *Steelman:* Dyer is emphatic that no suicide rate is acceptable — "if it's not zero, we still have work to do" — and that he would never call one rate "better" than another; the rebuttal targets the *causal attribution to the Church*, not the seriousness of the problem.[^4]

## Best Quotes

> "Utah sits in the middle of what's referred to as the suicide belt... if you take our region, we're absolutely average. We're not unique in terms of suicide rate."[^2]

> "Colorado and Wyoming, who touch us and who have far lower percentage of Latter-day Saints, have much higher suicide rates than Utah."[^2]

> "If a person wanted to implicate the church in suicides, actually the suicide rate, if you look at regionally, tells just the opposite story than what people are sometimes trying to tell."[^4]
