# Isaiah 43:10 — "No God Formed Before Me" Rebutted

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
[[Isaiah 43.10|Isaiah 43:10]] — "before me there was no God formed, neither shall there be after me" — denies any other divine beings, so LDS divine plurality is polytheism.

## The Rebuttal

### 1. The "trial of the gods" context
Isaiah 40-48 is a sustained courtroom polemic against idols. [[Michael Heiser]] frames Yahweh here as the destroyer of other gods.

> "Isaiah's chapter 40 through 48 are known as what's called the trial of the gods... Isaiah paints a courtroom setting where Israel... are guilty for being unfaithful to God." [^1]

The image: Yahweh lines up the idols Israel kept "stashing" and asks, "How did any of those save you? I'm the one standing. I'm your God" [^2].

### 2. "Formed" is the decisive word
The rival gods were **manufactured** — chiseled from stone, shaped from pottery. Yahweh denies that any *formed* (man-made) deity is real, not that the Father, Son, Spirit, and angels exist.

> "The word form is particularly important because the gods that they were worshiping were ones that they chiseled out of stone... They formed their own gods literally." [^3]

### 3. The Bible already assumes multiple divine beings
The text itself shows Jesus has a Father; there are the Father, the Son, the Spirit, angels. So Isaiah 43:10 cannot be a flat denial of all divine plurality without contradicting the rest of scripture. In context the claim is that no other being can take away Israel's sin [^4].
