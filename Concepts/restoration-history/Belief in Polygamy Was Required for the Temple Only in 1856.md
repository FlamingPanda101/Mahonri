# Belief in Polygamy Was Required for the Temple Only in 1856

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
The misconception is that "modern Latter-day Saints must believe 19th-century polygamy was right in order to be considered faithful today."[^1]

## The Rebuttal

### 1. Faithfulness is measured by the temple-recommend questions
Nothing official requires endorsing historical polygamy, but "for the sake of this conversation," historian [[Britney Chapman Nash]] proposes a concrete rubric: the temple-recommend interview questions, the Church's own standing test of worthiness/faithfulness.[^2]
> **Evidence rating:** Moderate–strong (a reasonable, checkable proxy for "what the Church actually requires to be in full fellowship").

### 2. The polygamy question was asked in exactly one year
"The only time during a temple-recommend interview that a belief in polygamy was required was in 1856" — "right in the heart of the Reformation." "It has not been asked or required of members since."[^3] So by 1857 it was gone, and a modern member's belief about 19th-century plural marriage is simply not on the test.
> **Evidence rating:** Strong (a specific, datable historical fact about recommend requirements).

### 3. The right question is present-tense
"Really what we need to ask ourselves is not 'do I have to believe polygamy is right' but... 'can I live the gospel now? Do I believe what the church teaches now?'" Faithfulness attaches to present belief and practice, not to ratifying a discontinued practice.[^4] This pairs with [[The Covenants and Blessings Are Identical in Monogamous and Plural Sealings]] (ritual exaltation does not depend on polygamy) and [[Understanding Replaces Fear - the Knowledge Cure for the Polygamy Stigma]].
> **Evidence rating:** Strong as guidance; it reframes the burden the objection assumes.

## Steelman of the Opposing View
A critic counters that the *practice* may be suspended but the *doctrine* is not repudiated: [[Doctrine and Covenants 132]] remains canon, plural sealings still operate for men, and leaders teach polygamy "served its purpose" rather than that it was wrong — so a fully faithful member arguably must still regard it as once divinely commanded, even if no interview question asks. The disciplined reply: the *narrow* claim holds (no current test of faith requires endorsing historical polygamy, and the recommend question existed only in 1856), while the *broader* question of whether one must believe it was God-ordained is left, as the Church leaves it, to individual conscience — see [[Rejecting Polygamy Unravels Sealing and the Plan of Salvation]] for the harder version.

## Best Quotes
> "The only time during a temple-recommend interview that a belief in polygamy was required was in 1856. And that was a long time ago... it has not been asked or required of members since."[^3]

> "Really what we need to ask ourselves is not 'do I have to believe polygamy is right,' but... 'can I live the gospel now? Do I believe what the church teaches now?'"[^4]
