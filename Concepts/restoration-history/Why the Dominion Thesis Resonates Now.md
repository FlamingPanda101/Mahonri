# Why the Dominion Thesis Resonates Now

## The Claim

[[Glenn Scrivener]] and [[Alex OConnor|Alex O'Connor]] both address why [[Tom Holland]]'s thesis — "unremarkable and quite modest" — became a cultural phenomenon after 2019, and why figures like [[Ayaan Hirsi Ali]] turned toward Christianity.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Collapse of "end of history" optimism.** Scrivener: had Holland written 20 years earlier, the [[Steven Pinker|Pinker-style]] "end of history" confidence (liberal democracy wins, peace and prosperity rise) would have made the thesis unnecessary. The intervening upheavals (9/11 and its wars, failed democratization of Afghanistan/Iraq) shattered that confidence.[^2] *(Plausible cultural diagnosis.)*

2. **Dechurching and the "low tide" metaphor.** "40 million Americans left church this century." Scrivener: "At low tide you see what the sea had done" — only as faith withdraws (Matthew Arnold's "melancholy, long, withdrawing roar" of the Sea of Faith) do people notice its shaping influence and miss what they had.[^3] *(Evocative; the dechurching figures are real, the interpretation is the thesis itself.)*

3. **Mass immigration confronts people with difference.** O'Connor: "mass immigration, which does seem genuinely novel," forces people to "reassess the defining characteristics of their belief system." When "the air starts smelling of something different, you start to notice it."[^4] *(Strong — O'Connor offers a sociological, non-theological account of the same resonance.)*

4. **Hirsi Ali's turn.** O'Connor: she dealt with "two things at once" — personal depression that religion helped address, and political disillusionment as "New Atheism failed to deliver" its humanist utopia.[^5] *(See [[Ayaan Hirsi Ali]]; a psychological/political rather than evidential explanation.)*

5. **Steelman / point of agreement.** Both broadly agree on the *diagnosis* of resonance even while disagreeing on the *truth* of the thesis. O'Connor: "Maybe what we lost is belief in Christianity... a lot of people are attracted to it as a plausible option." This is rare common ground.[^6] *(Genuine convergence on the cultural moment.)*

## Best Quotes

> "At low tide you see what the sea had done — at high tide you have no idea about the shaping influence of the sea."[^3]

> "If something comes along and starts changing the nature of the air, starts smelling of something different, then you start to notice it." — O'Connor[^4]
