# Harmonization Only Works When the Need to Harmonize Overrides Critical Thinking (Dogma Over Data)

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)

The general apologetic instinct is that apparent contradictions in Scripture *must* be reconcilable, so any harmonization that removes the tension is preferable to admitting a contradiction. Applied here: because the Bible cannot contradict itself, the "zoomed-in sixth day" reading must be correct.

**Steelman:** The harmonizing impulse is not irrational on its own terms. If one has independent reasons to hold a high view of biblical inerrancy (theological tradition, perceived reliability elsewhere, a coherent canon), then a charitable reading that seeks coherence before concluding "error" is a defensible interpretive principle — and it is one historians apply to many ancient texts. The instinct to harmonize is, in moderation, ordinary good-faith reading.

## The Rebuttal

1. **The method is question-begging when it is non-negotiable (strong).** McClellan's charge is that the harmonization here works *only* because the conclusion (no contradiction) is fixed in advance: "These passages are only harmonizable if your need to harmonize them overrules your ability to think critically about them."[^1] When the need to harmonize cannot be defeated by any amount of contrary data, it has stopped being interpretation. Evidence rating: **strong** (a methodological point).

2. **The data are decisive against this particular harmonization (decisive).** The harmonization does not merely strain the text — it inverts explicit sequences (humanity before vs. after plants and animals). See [[Sequence of Creation Differs Between Genesis 1 and 2]]. "It is not a plausible way to harmonize these passages."[^2] Evidence rating: **decisive** for this case.

3. **"Dogma over data" framing (rhetorical, supporting).** McClellan reduces the move to a slogan: "It's just putting dogma over data."[^1] This is a values-claim about what should drive interpretation (evidence) versus what is in fact driving it (a prior commitment). Evidence rating: **rhetorical** — persuasive framing, not itself a datum, but it correctly locates the dispute at the level of method.

## Best Quotes

> "These passages are only harmonizable if your need to harmonize them overrules your ability to think critically about them. It's just putting dogma over data." [^1]

> "The notion that the second one is just a zoomed-in account of the sixth day of creation from the first account still flatly contradicts the details of the first account and is not a plausible way to harmonize these passages." [^2]
