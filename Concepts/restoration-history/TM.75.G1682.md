# TM.75.G1682

TM.75.G1682 is the museum designation of a Sumerian literary tablet from the third-millennium-BCE Ebla archives (Tell Mardikh, Syria), a four-column composition with a smaller variant parallel catalogued as TM.75.G2500. It is the actual text behind apologetic claims that the Ebla archives attest creation *ex nihilo* and biblical parallels.

## Relevance

The vault uses the correct designation to expose errors in a popular apologetic claim: the tablet is sometimes mislabeled "EA 250" (an Amarna diplomatic letter, unrelated to Ebla), and its supposed creation-from-nothing reading derives from Giovanni Pettinato's discredited 1981 translation. Alfonso Archi's later epigraphic work found the text largely incomprehensible, undercutting any use of it to support creation ex nihilo against the LDS doctrine of organization from pre-existing matter.
