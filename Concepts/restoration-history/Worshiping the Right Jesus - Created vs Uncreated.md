# Worshiping the Right Jesus — Created vs Uncreated

## The Claim
[[Gavin Ortlund]] argues the decisive christological line is **created vs. uncreated**: "is Jesus created or is he uncreated God?" [^1] He grounds Jesus' divine status in early high-Christology worship texts — [[Philippians 2.9-11|Philippians 2:9-11]] (Jesus given "the name above every name," every knee bowing), which Ortlund (following [[Richard Bauckham]]) reads as Jesus "participating in the identity of God" already "in the 50s AD." [^2][^3] Therefore every act of prayer to Jesus presupposes an answer to "who is this person?" — and getting it wrong is worshiping a different Jesus. [^4]

## The Rebuttal (the LDS counter, steelmanned)

1. **Ortlund's positive case (strong).** Philippians 2 applies Isaiah 45's exclusive-worship language ("every knee shall bow") to Jesus; bestowing "the name above every name" is plausibly the divine name. *Rating: powerful evidence for early devotion to Jesus as divine.* Bauckham's "divine identity" category is well regarded in NT scholarship.

2. **Steelman: predicative divinity without consubstantiality.** [[Jacob Hansen]] affirms "Jesus is fully divine in the predicative sense" — divine in nature — while noting Philippians 2 itself ends "to the glory of God the **Father**," distinguishing the two. [^5] He also points to the creeds and scripture calling *the Father* "the Almighty," with the Son distinguished. [^6] *Rating: a coherent subordinationist/social reading* — the same text affirms Jesus' exaltation *and* his distinction from the Father, which the LDS take as one divinity-of-nature shared by distinct persons rather than numerical sameness of substance.

3. **The "created" word smuggles in ex nihilo.** Hansen objects that calling Jesus "created vs. uncreated" presupposes creatio ex nihilo; in LDS metaphysics *nothing* was created from nothing, so the created/uncreated binary is a category that "doesn't exist in our world." [^7] *Rating: a sharp move* — it shows Ortlund's framing question is not neutral but loaded with his ontology (see [[Creatio Ex Nihilo Is the Lynchpin Difference]]).

4. **What must one believe?** Hansen asks Ortlund to inventory the minimum required beliefs about Jesus; Ortlund answers "Jesus is God / Jesus is Lord," then insists they must "double click" on what those words mean, since both men affirm the words but "mean something very different by them." [^8][^9] *Rating: this honestly locates the disagreement* — not in the confession "Jesus is Lord" but in its metaphysical content.

## Best Quotes
> "Is Jesus created or is he uncreated God?" [^1]

> "You affirm those things, but we mean something very different by them... that's why I'm suggesting we get into the definition of God." [^9]
