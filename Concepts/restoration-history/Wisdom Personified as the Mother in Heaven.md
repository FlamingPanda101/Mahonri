# Wisdom Personified as the Mother in Heaven

## The Point
[[Mandy Green]] argues that personified *Wisdom* (Hebrew *Hokmah*, Greek *Sophia*) is a recurring title and presence of the Mother in Heaven running from Genesis through the wisdom literature, the Apocrypha, the New Testament, the early church fathers, and into the Latter-day Saint canon. "When you see anything that talks about wisdom, that's going to be another way of addressing her."

## Evidence
- **Wisdom as creative agent in Genesis 1.** Reading the creation account, Green counts Wisdom alongside the plural *Elohim* and the feminine Spirit as a feminine clue in the first two verses, quoting the Wisdom of Solomon: "Creation was the domain of wisdom, who, being but one, can do all things, and while remaining in herself, she renews all things. In every generation she passes into holy souls and makes the friends of God and prophets."[^1] "So wisdom is a key word for our mother in heaven."[^2]
- **Wisdom restored is the work of Christ.** [[Philo of Alexandria|Philo]] called Jesus "Son of wisdom, his mother, through whom the universe came into being"; Isaiah blesses the Messiah with "the spirit of wisdom... understanding... counsel" (feminine in the Greek); and Luke says Jesus "grew in wisdom first and stature." Green concludes "Jesus is the direct offspring and recipient and beneficiary of wisdom, his mother," and that the signs of his ministry are "the signs of wisdom restored."[^3]
- **Wisdom was operational in the early church.** From the *Teachings of Silvanus*, [[Clement of Alexandria|Clement]] (c. 150–210 AD, whose teachers were taught by the apostles) writes that "Wisdom clothed, fed, and gave water to her children," and "I am giving you a high priestly garment... woven from every wisdom... return to your first father and Wisdom your mother."[^4] "Clothe yourself with wisdom like a robe, put knowledge upon your head like a crown."[^5]
- **Wisdom and the Restoration.** The LDS Word of Wisdom promises that the obedient "shall find wisdom and great treasures of knowledge, even hidden treasures"; and the Restoration itself "begins with a verse from James" — "if any of you lack wisdom, let him ask of [[Elohim]], who giveth to all men liberally." Green: "Wisdom is the thing that started the restoration of the church in the last days."[^6][^7]

## Best Quotes

> "Return to your first father and Wisdom your mother."[^4]

## LDS Connection
The personified-Wisdom thread gives a scriptural and patristic vocabulary for the Latter-day Saint Heavenly Mother that does not depend on the contested Asherah archaeology alone: the Mother appears under the name *Wisdom* in canonical and deuterocanonical texts that survived the editing of Genesis. This complements [[Feminine Divine in Ancient Near Eastern Worship]] and the King-Follett rationale that an embodied, relational God entails a divine counterpart (see [[Anthropomorphic God Grounds Heavenly Mother and Eternal Families]]). The iconographic and Edenic counterpart is developed in [[Asherah Pillar Iconography Depicts Wisdom Who Gives Sight]].
