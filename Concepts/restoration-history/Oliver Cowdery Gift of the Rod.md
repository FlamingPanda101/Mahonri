# Oliver Cowdery Gift of the Rod

#type/claim

## The Claim
The earliest revelations to [[Oliver Cowdery]] ([[D&C 6|D&C 6]]/[[D&C 8|8]], with [[D&C 9|9]]) affirm that his **divining rod** is a genuine spiritual gift from God. This is the theologically hard datum Latter-day Saints "really have to deal with" — not a peripheral cultural quirk but canonized scripture treating a rod as a revelatory instrument.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The textual trajectory (documented).** The revelation tells Cowdery he has "the gift of working with the sprout" (earliest manuscript), changed to "working with the rod" (1833 Book of Commandments), then "gift of Aaron" (1835 Doctrine and Covenants).[^2] *Rating: strong — textually traceable across three editions.*

2. **Explicitly a spiritual gift, not science.** The text says the rod "has told you many things" and that "there is no other power save the power of God which can cause this rod of nature to work in your hands" — God claiming the agency behind it.[^3] *Rating: strong (within LDS canon).*

3. **The Aaron / Old Testament linkage.** The 1835 "gift of Aaron" reading ties the rod to Moses-and-Aaron rod imagery — [[Numbers 17|Aaron's budding rod]] revealing the Levitical priesthood — framing Cowdery's rod as continuous with scriptural precedent rather than folk superstition.[^4] *Rating: moderate — interpretive linkage Mark draws explicitly.*

4. **Steelman.** A critic reads the edits as damage control: "sprout" (a literal forked branch) → "rod" → the biblical-sounding "gift of Aaron" tracks an effort to dignify an embarrassing dowsing practice as scripture matured. Mark openly reports all three readings, which is itself evidence the LDS position here is to *own* the rod rather than hide it, and to interpret it theologically via the Aaron parallel.[^5]

## Best Quotes

> "The gift of working with the sprout ... changed from working with the sprout to working with the rod ... and then that's changed again in 1835 ... to gift of Aaron."[^2]

> "It's not science or some kind of science we don't understand. It's a spiritual gift."[^3]
