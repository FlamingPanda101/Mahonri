# The Sin of the Nephilim Was Sterilization Abortion and Child Sacrifice

## The Claim

[[Jonah Barnes]] argues that the great sin that provoked the Flood was not generic "wickedness" or even the intermarriage itself, but a specific demographic atrocity: the fallen Sethites (the [[The Nephilim Were Apostate Sethite Priesthood-Holders Not Supernatural Giants|Nephilim/Watchers]]) **sterilized the women, aborted or sacrificed the children, and consumed blood to extend their own multi-century lifespans**. On this reading, God's verdict "the end of all flesh is before me" ([[Genesis 6.1-4|Genesis 6:13]]) means literally *the human race was being terminated* — which is why "the first thing the Lord does after the Flood" is **shorten human lifespans**. Barnes builds the case from the *Mandean Book of Enoch*, the *Cave of Treasures*, *Jubilees*, and *Pirke de-Rabbi Eliezer*, read through Joseph Smith's contributions. [^1] [^2]

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. A "drought" (drug) that sterilizes the women — rate: weak-moderate (apocryphal, Barnes disclaims)
"The problem among the daughters of Cain is childbirth… every time they want to have fun, a baby's born. And the sons of Seth say, 'We have a remedy for that' — and they introduce sterilization… abortion." The texts speak of "a drought that they drink" causing barrenness, so "they're drugging these women so that they can't have children." Barnes likens it to "the pill that destroyed the birth rate of American civilization, but in like 2000 BCE." (He repeatedly hedges: "I'm just reading the text… don't come after me.") [^1]

### 2. The lifespan logic — rate: weak (speculative demographic model)
With 900-year lifespans women are fertile for centuries, so "the population just explodes… it broke my Excel model." Sterilization is the watchers' tool to keep "hedonism totally unrestrained" without runaway birth — and because there is "not a lot of difference between a person who is 550 and 575," social constraints on incest and exploitation collapse. The Flood is thus a *reset* on a self-terminating population. [^2]

### 3. Child sacrifice and blood-consumption to extend life — rate: weak-moderate (multiple apocrypha cited)
*Pirke de-Rabbi Eliezer*: "they turned children into ritual… they prided themselves on not having children… if ever a child was born they would either sacrifice it and eat it for its blood, or throw it into the abyss." The *Mandean Book of Enoch*: Satan tells his angels "we're going to take the germ of fertility from them — sterilize them all so that no one else can be born"; the *Hebrew Book of Enoch*: God says "don't come crying to me because you're sterile — you made yourselves sterile." Barnes ties this to the **Cain motif** — Cain drank "the blood of Abel" to "extend his life" — and to the **Jaredite/Gadianton** secret combinations that took the Jaredites "from millions to one guy" ([[Ether 8.13-18|Ether 8]]). [^3]

### 4. "The end of all flesh is before me" reread — rate: moderate (the interpretive payoff)
Barnes: Genesis 6:13 is not "I'm going to destroy all flesh" but "all flesh is *ending* before our eyes" — a description of a species being snuffed out by its own sterility and child-killing, which the Flood and the post-Flood lifespan-shortening answer. This is the claim's keystone and its most defensible exegetical move. [^4]

## Steelman of the Opposing View

This is the most speculative claim in the segment, and the objections are heavy. (a) **The texts do not say what Barnes needs them to say in one voice**: 1 Enoch's catalog of Watcher sins is *violence, sorcery, illicit knowledge, and the giants' cannibalism of humans* — not a coordinated abortion-and-sterilization program; the "drought/drug" and "germ of fertility" lines come from late, marginal sources (Mandean, medieval midrash) that Barnes himself flags as "what the texts are hinting at cryptically," i.e., he is reconstructing, not reporting. (b) The reading is **transparently shaped by a contemporary culture-war frame** — the hosts repeatedly map it onto "the pill," "boomers sucking resources," and declining American birth rates, which is precisely the kind of presentist projection that should lower confidence in the exegesis. (c) "The end of all flesh is before me" is standard biblical Hebrew for *imminent destruction decreed by God* (qets, "end/doom"), not "humanity is going extinct by its own hand"; the mainstream reading needs no abortion subtext. (d) Even granting the Cain/blood and Jaredite secret-combination parallels, those are about **murder and conspiracy for power**, not contraception. The fair residue: Barnes has assembled a genuinely *interesting* thread (long lifespans + child-sacrifice apocrypha + the post-Flood lifespan cap) and his Genesis 6:13 re-reading is exegetically clever, but the specific "sterilization and abortion were *the* sin" thesis rests on a chain of late sources welded together by his own synthesis and a modern moral lens — which he, to his credit, says out loud.
