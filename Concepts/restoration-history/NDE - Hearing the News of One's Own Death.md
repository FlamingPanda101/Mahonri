# NDE - Hearing the News of One's Own Death

## The Point
A recurring early element of the NDE is that the dying person **overhears himself being pronounced dead** by a doctor or bystander, while remaining fully aware [^1]. This element carries unusual evidential weight because, in several cases, the words the patient reports hearing **match what the medical staff actually said** during a period when the patient was clinically unresponsive [^2]. It suggests continued, accurate perception at the very threshold of death.

## Evidence
1. A woman who arrested during a liver scan heard the radiologist phone her doctor and say, "Dr. James, I've killed your patient, Mrs. Martin" — yet she "knew" she was not dead and could hear the resuscitation instructions, though she felt no needles [^1].
2. A heart-attack patient, in "deep blackness," heard her husband say "This is it, this time!" and inwardly answered, "Yes, it is" [^1].
3. A young man thought dead after a car accident heard a woman ask, "Is he dead?" and another reply, "Yeah, he's dead" [^1].
4. Corroboration from the other side: a surgeon recounted telling his colleague, "Let's try one more time and then we'll give up"; after revival, the patient remembered little of her "death" except that she had heard him say exactly that [^2].

## Best Quotes
> "I heard him say, 'Dr. James, I've killed your patient, Mrs. Martin.' And I knew I wasn't dead. I tried to move or to let them know, but I couldn't." [^1]

## LDS Connection
That the conscious self perceives and remembers while the body lies inert supports the LDS teaching that the spirit is a distinct, sentient entity that continues to think and perceive when separated from the body. Alma 40 teaches that at death the spirit is taken "home to that God who gave them life" — conscious survival, not annihilation, is the expectation.
