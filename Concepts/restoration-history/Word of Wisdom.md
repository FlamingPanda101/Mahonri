# Word of Wisdom

## Overview

The **Word of Wisdom** is the Latter-day Saint health code (Doctrine and Covenants 89): a dietary and lifestyle standard now generally observed as abstinence from alcohol, tobacco, coffee, and tea, with counsel favoring wholesome foods and moderation. This is an overview note for a referenced topic; the vault discusses it functionally rather than litigating its medical merits.

## The Claim

The angle developed in the cluster is [[Daniel Peterson]]'s **functional-marker** reading on [[Holy Envy - What Latter-day Saints Can Learn from Islam|Holy Envy]]: like Islamic dietary regulation and modest dress, the Word of Wisdom makes religion "a religion with you every day." Declining a glass of wine repeatedly flags one's identity, reminds the practitioner that they are "a little different," and can even open gospel conversations. Peterson offers this tentatively — "it may or may not be the intrinsic merits of the thing" — locating part of the practice's value in its role as a constant, embodied identity marker rather than solely in any health benefit.

This connects to the broader vault discussion of how a living church handles evolving directives — the symmetry-of-uncertainty point in [[A Fallible List of Infallible Teachings - The Symmetry of Uncertainty]].

## The authority angle (caffeine reversal)

A second cluster, from [[Trevan Hatch]] on the [[Cwic Show - The Official Position on Official Positions|Cwic Show]], uses the Word of Wisdom's history with **caffeine** as a worked example of the prophet-vs-scripture tension in deciding LDS doctrine: ~80 prophetic counsels against caffeine (1908–2002) were effectively reversed, with a church PR rationale appealing to the *text* of D&C 89 over the prophetic teaching — while the text itself, read historically, permits "mild barley drink" (beer) and restricts meat. See [[The Caffeine Reversal Exposes the Prophet-Versus-Scripture Tension in the Word of Wisdom]].
