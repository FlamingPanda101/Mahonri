# Doctrine of Christ as the Only Thing You Cannot Get Wrong

## The Claim
If prophets and Church programs can err (see [[Prophets Are Fallible Today]]), what is safe to anchor to? Peterson's answer: the *doctrine of Christ* — faith in Jesus, repentance, service, prayer, kindness, the temple ordinances. Programs "come and go" and can be wrong, but the path that leads to Christ is the unchanging essence, and "you can't go very far wrong by trying to serve people or by praying."[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Distinguish essence from administration (rated: strong; structural).** "The doctrine of Christ... is fundamental. ... that's the essence of what the gospel is. And is it this or that program? No, those things can come and go, and... sometimes they're wrong. But you won't go too far astray if you follow them."[^1] This gives a discernment principle that neither demands blind obedience nor licenses cherry-picking.

2. **The unloseable practices (rated: strong; practical).** "You're never going to be wrong on... faithful discipleship, trying to be kind, trying to serve. ... How wrong can you be?"[^2] These cannot be the locus of a prophet's error.

3. **Scriptural backing — mercy for the erring (rated: strong).** [[2 Nephi 28.30-32|2 Nephi 28:30-32]]: God gives "line upon line," warns against trusting "the arm of flesh," yet promises "I will be merciful unto them... if they will repent and come unto me; for mine arm is lengthened out all the day long."[^3] Peterson and host [[Stephen Jones]] read this as: you will get details wrong, but turning to Christ keeps you safe.

4. **Eyring/First Presidency humility (rated: moderate).** Lay members are often *more* dogmatic about unrevealed doctrinal minutiae than the First Presidency; the Lord "doesn't have to tell us every single little detail," and over-certainty about peripheral doctrine is a failure mode.[^4] See [[The Three Levels of the Gospel]].

5. **The preschool/kindergarten analogy (rated: illustrative).** Peterson likens human theological speculation to preschoolers gravely debating how tough the kindergarten teacher will be — "cute" but radically under-informed. The basics (salvation in Christ, faith, hope, charity, ordinances) we *do* know; the metaphysical particulars we should hold loosely.[^5] *Steelman:* a critic may say this conveniently quarantines every awkward doctrine as "peripheral." Peterson's limiting move is that the *core* (doctrine of Christ) is non-negotiable and knowable — it is not relativism all the way down.

## Best Quotes

> "But the basic thing is follow the doctrine of Christ. ... You can't go very far wrong by trying to serve people or by praying. How wrong can you be?"[^1]

> "Those things can come and go, and they can be adjusted and improved, and sometimes they're wrong. But you won't go too far astray if you follow them."[^1]

> "Salvation is in Christ. Faith and service and hope are good. Charity is good. ... And temple ordinances are important. Do we understand exactly how and why? No. Does that matter? No."[^6]
