# Plural Marriage Read Through Hypergamy and the Asymmetry of Reproduction

## The Claim
[[Selene Anderson]] frames plural marriage anthropologically rather than scandalously: across "83% of all world civilizations" some form of **polygyny** (one man, multiple women) appears, while polyandry is vanishingly rare, and even "monogamy has only existed as a legally imposed system." She grounds this in two claims about human mating — **female hypergamy** (women filter for a partner perceived as equal or better) and the **asymmetry of reproductive cost** (women bear the physical cost; men pay an economic/protective cost) — and argues plural marriage, properly bounded, *serves* women by widening their hypergamous choice.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Hypergamy as a filtering instinct (rated: presenter-level social science).** "Hypergamy is the tendency of women to seek a partner that they view as good or better than them in whatever regard" — not merely economic (it can be intelligence, physicality, etc.). She illustrates with college-educated women marrying blue-collar business owners, and with dating-app "hyper-hypergamy" concentrating mating on fewer men.[^2] *Rating: this is mainstream-adjacent evolutionary-psychology framing presented popularly, not a peer-reviewed result; the "83%" figure echoes the ethnographic record (most societies permit polygyny) but the interpretive gloss is hers.*

2. **Reproductive asymmetry as the engine (rated: interpretive).** "The reproductive relationship between men and women is... inherently asymmetrical. Women pay the physical cost of reproduction and men necessarily pay an economic cost." A woman entering that vulnerability "needs to be paired with someone she feels can do the things she would normally do for herself as good or better."[^3]

3. **Plural marriage widens, not narrows, female choice (rated: the apologetic core).** Because in a socioeconomically empowered setting a woman can "will it over needing it," plural marriage lets her make a *full* hypergamous selection (and secure the help of another woman in the home) rather than settle. This connects to the *fertility* argument in [[Polygamy Raised Fertility by Raising the Marriage Rate]] — polygyny raises the marriage rate by letting more women pair with the partners they prefer.[^4]

## Steelman of the Opposing View
Critics will say this is a just-so story: evolutionary-psychology claims about "revealed preferences" and hypergamy are contested, often retrofitted to justify whatever arrangement the author already favors, and the leap from "polygyny is statistically common" to "therefore it blesses women" is a naturalistic fallacy (common ≠ good). Anderson herself concedes the agricultural-era version produced exploitative "harems" — so the same biology that supposedly vindicates plural marriage also underwrites its worst abuses. The argument also relies on a particular, essentialist reading of gender that many would reject. Its defensible core is modest: that female choice and the costs of reproduction are *relevant variables*, not that they license polygyny.

## Counter-Objection
A critic: "If male sexual strategy drives polygyny, you've described a male advantage, not a female blessing." Anderson's reply is the bounding conditions — plural marriage is just *only* when woman-driven and economically non-coercive ([[Plural Marriage and the Law of Consecration Are the Same Law]]); absent female agency it collapses into the very "hoarding" she condemns.

## Best Quotes
> "83% of all world civilizations engage with polygyny... polyandry... almost doesn't show up. Monogamy has only existed as a legally imposed system."[^1]

> "The reproductive relationship between men and women is... inherently asymmetrical. Women pay the physical cost of reproduction and men necessarily pay an economic cost in supporting her."[^3]

> "What plural marriage allows for is allows for women to have a full breadth of making that hypergamous decision."[^4]
