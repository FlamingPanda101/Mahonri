# The Laws of Logic Are Immaterial and Point to a Mind

## The Point
The laws of logic — such as the law of noncontradiction (opposite ideas cannot both be true at the same time and in the same sense) — are immaterial, universal, and unchanging. They apply everywhere, at every time, and to everyone, like the laws of physics and math.[^1] Because they are timeless, spaceless, immaterial, and unchangeable, Turek argues they "seem to be grounded in a timeless, spaceless, immaterial, and unchangeable Mind."[^2] A purely material universe of "matter in motion" has no resources to produce or ground such realities; theism does.

## Evidence
Turek recounts how atheist Gordon Stein, in a 1985 debate, admitted that the laws of logic are not material ("How could a law be material?"), even while insisting they were human conventions.[^3] Turek answers the convention claim several ways: (1) the statement "there are no human beings on the earth" was true before any minds existed to conceive it, so logic cannot be a mere human invention;[^4] (2) human beings change but logic does not — it is "an unchanging independent measuring stick of truth";[^1] (3) communication requires accessing "something unchanging that transcends us yet is common to us," a "bridge between minds";[^5] (4) all debate presupposes an objective truth outside each debater's mind.[^6] Laws come from lawgivers, Turek argues — "that's what all experience shows us" — so the laws of logic trace back to "the same eternal Mind that created the universe."[^2] He notes this is not a "God of the gaps" move but a positive argument: these immaterial realities are themselves evidence for an intelligent Being.[^7]

## Best Quotes
> "They are fixed, immaterial, eternal laws that would not exist if the purely material world of atheism were correct. While atheists use them, they cannot explain them." [^8]

> "Since the laws of logic are timeless, spaceless, immaterial, and unchangeable, they seem to be grounded in a timeless, spaceless, immaterial, and unchangeable Mind. Laws only come from lawgivers." [^2]

## LDS Connection
The Restoration shares Turek's conviction that rationality is anchored in a divine Mind, and it draws the link even more tightly through its high doctrine of intelligence and light. "The glory of God is intelligence, or, in other words, light and truth" (D&C 93:36), and "the light of truth... is in all things, which giveth life to all things, which is the law by which all things are governed" (D&C 88:6–13). Latter-day Saint scripture even calls Christ "the Spirit of truth" who was "in the beginning with God" ([[D&C 93]]). Where Turek says logic is "grounded in a Mind," LDS theology affirms an eternal Mind whose intelligence pervades and governs reality — so universal, unchanging law is exactly what we should expect.
