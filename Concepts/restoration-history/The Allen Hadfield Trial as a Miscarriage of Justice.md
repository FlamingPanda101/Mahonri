# The Allen Hadfield Trial as a Miscarriage of Justice

## The Claim
The 1986 conviction of [[Allen Hadfield]] — the only adult charged in the Lehi cases — was a wrongful conviction built on coerced child testimony, no physical evidence, and a fabricated timeline.

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. From advocate to accused (strong — documents the reversal)
Hadfield had supported [[Barbara Snow]] and joined an advocacy group demanding action. After Snow phoned him at length on April 9, 1986, his children — after nine months of her therapy — accused him; all initially denied it. Snow produced only a slip of paper reading "My dad has touched me," having recorded none of her sessions [^1].

### 2. A "monkey court" of contradictions (moderate — narrator characterization)
The trial is described as "filled with contradictions and implausibilities," the most outrageous being the fabricated date (see [[The No-Alibi Date as Manufactured Evidence]]). Hadfield was narrowly convicted but received 10 years probation and 6 months jail rather than the usual 10-year minimum [^2].

### 3. Clinical evaluation found him normal (strong)
Court-ordered therapist [[Robert Card]] — to whom treatment was reassigned after Hadfield refused to admit guilt to [[Paul Whitehead]] — reported Hadfield was "vibrantly normal" with nothing indicating deviance or pedophilia [^3].

### 4. Independent vindication (strong)
Sociologist [[Anson Shupe]] concluded Hadfield "was no more likely to be an abuser than were his neighbors," and the community raised funds for his defense. Filmmaker [[Rhea Gavry]]'s 1992 documentary *Promise Not to Tell* was read as sympathetic to him [^4].

## Steelman (Opposing View)
A juror in 1986 could reasonably point out that a jury did convict after hearing the evidence, that children sometimes recant true abuse under family pressure, and that a defendant maintaining innocence proves nothing. But the conviction depended on testimony from a discredited interviewer, an unrecorded process, a timeline Snow herself supplied, and no physical evidence — and the official county investigation had already concluded no abuse occurred in Lehi.

## Best Quotes

> "The unfortunate truth for Hadfield was that he was no more likely to be an abuser than were his neighbors." — [[Anson Shupe]] [^4]
