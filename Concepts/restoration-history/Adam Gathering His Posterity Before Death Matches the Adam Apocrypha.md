# Adam Gathering His Posterity Before Death Matches the Adam Apocrypha

## The Claim
[[Joseph Smith]]'s [[D&C 107|D&C 107:53–56]] describes a scene found nowhere in Genesis: three years before his death, [[Adam]] gathers his righteous posterity (Seth, Enos, Cainan, Mahalaleel, Jared, [[Enoch]], Methuselah — "all high priests") into the valley of [[D&C 116|Adam-ondi-Ahman]], bestows on them a "last blessing," and — "being full of the Holy Ghost" — prophesies "whatsoever should befall his posterity unto the last generation." [[Jonah (Plain and Precious Publishing)|Jonah Barnes]] argues that this exact deathbed-gathering scene is independently attested in the *Life of Adam and Eve* apocrypha (Greek, Latin, Armenian, Slavonic), texts dating to the early Christian era that were untranslated and inaccessible to Joseph in 1830.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. A righteous, priestly, prophetic Adam contradicts the Protestant reading
In the King James / Protestant tradition, Barnes notes, Adam "never gives a sacrifice, never says a prayer, never does a darn righteous thing" — "as good as a heathen." Joseph instead makes Adam an awesome man, a high priest, and a prophet who blesses his seed and foretells the future from "the midst of the congregation." The Adam apocrypha likewise preserve a venerated, priestly Adam who possesses a sacred "mystery."[^2]
> **Evidence rating:** Moderate (the contrast with the bare Genesis Adam is real; that the apocrypha "agree" depends on reading priestly veneration as the same datum).

### 2. The deathbed assembly matches almost verbatim
The Latin *Vita Adae* (11:1): after Adam is 930 years old, knowing his days are ending, he says **"Let all my sons assemble themselves to me that I may bless them before I die and speak with them."** Barnes: "that's exactly what Joseph describes in section 107... the exact same thing." The Armenian *Penitence of our Forefather Adam* (6:2) adds that all Adam's sons "who were in every part of the world gathered by him," "assembled by him inside the place which Eve had entered," and "he prayed to the Lord God" — paralleling the gathering, the congregation, and the prophesying of D&C 107:56.[^3][^4]
> **Evidence rating:** Moderate-to-strong as a parallel (the assemble-bless-before-death structure is genuinely close); weaker as proof of dependence (Adam-deathbed traditions are a diffuse genre).

### 3. The texts were inaccessible to Joseph Smith
Barnes stresses the manuscripts derive from the third–fifth-century Christian era but were first translated only in the later 19th and 20th centuries (he cites a first translation he thought was 1896) — "long after Joseph is gone." If so, convergence cannot be explained by ordinary access, which is the engine of the whole argument. This is the same inference-to-best-explanation move catalogued in [[Authentic Ancient Texts Contain Uncorroborated Unique Data]] and [[Book of Mormon as American Pseudepigrapha]].[^5]
> **Evidence rating:** Moderate (the "could not have known" premise is load-bearing and harder to verify than the parallel itself; Adam literature circulated in summary form in some 19th-century compendia).

## Steelman of the Opposing View
A skeptic will note that "a dying patriarch gathers his sons to bless them and foretell the future" is a stock ancient deathbed-testament form (cf. Jacob in Genesis 49, the *Testaments of the Twelve Patriarchs*), so independent appearance proves no literary link. The "all over the apocrypha" framing also smooths over real divergence — the recensions disagree among themselves about who gathers whom and why — and the host concedes on-air "this is just what the texts say... heaven knows." Crucially, the comedic format and self-disclaimed non-scholarship mean the citations are not vetted, and "Joseph could not have known" understates how much Adam-and-Eve legendary material was diffused in print before 1830. The defensible residue: Joseph's Adam-ondi-Ahman scene shares a notably specific cluster (righteous priestly Adam + pre-death assembly + blessing + prophecy to the last generation) with the Adam apocrypha; the strong claim that this *proves* restored ancient content rests on the contested access premise and remains tier-C popular apologetics.

## LDS Connection
This is the textual core of the Latter-day Saint Adam-ondi-Ahman teaching (see [[D&C 116]], cf. [[Daniel 7.13-14|Daniel 7]]'s "Ancient of Days"), reframed as evidence: the *details* surrounding the site, not just the "Missouri" location skeptics mock, are what Barnes says Joseph gets right against texts he could not read. It pairs with [[The Book of Eve Has Eve Dictate the First Scripture]] and [[Adam Lived on a Different Continent Than Noah Landed]], and complements the Moses 5 parallel in [[The Life of Adam and Eve Fills the Gaps in Joseph Smith's Moses 5]]. The "mystery = ordinance" aside connects to the vault's temple-restoration material.
