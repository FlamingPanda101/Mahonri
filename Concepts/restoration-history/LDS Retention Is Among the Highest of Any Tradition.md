# LDS Retention Is Among the Highest of Any Tradition (Objection Rebutted)

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
[[Joe Heschmeyer]] predicts decline: "most Mormons are leaving," citing a retention rate of "like 47%," and Matt Fradd adds he's "seeing tons of people converting from Mormonism to Catholicism" — framing the Church as squishy and hemorrhaging.[^1]

## The Rebuttal

1. **The raw number is close to Catholicism's (rated: strong, data).** [[Jasmine Rapley]]: the actual Pew Religious Landscape Survey retention figure for Latter-day Saints is ~54%, with Catholics next at ~57% — "we're way closer to Catholic retention than he would like to admit." Heschmeyer's ~47% was both low and not meaningfully worse than the comparison.[^2] See [[LDS Retention Is Higher Than Most Other Christian Traditions]].

2. **Controlling for activity, LDS rank #1 (rated: strong, data).** Rapley cites "figure 15 of that same paper": when you "control for people who are going to church," Latter-day Saints have "the highest retention of any religion currently in the United States." People who are *actually active* stay at the highest rate of any tradition.[^3] See [[Why the LDS Membership Lacks a Large Nominal Middle]] and [[High-Demand Religion Retains More Members]].

3. **Catholic *active* retention is lower (rated: moderate, data).** Rapley: when you look at Catholics "who are actually attending, the numbers are much lower," so Fradd's "they're all converting to Catholicism" may be backwards — "you might actually be seeing more Catholics converting to Mormonism."[^4]

4. **Decline is universal, not LDS-specific (rated: strong, contextual).** "All religions have been seeing decline over the last 15 years and we've seen a bit of a plateau." Attributing LDS losses to "weak theology" or "squishy doctrine" ignores the across-the-board secular trend.[^5] See [[LDS Retention Decline Mirrors All Religions]]. *Steelman:* a critic can fairly note that (a) absolute convert baptisms and birthrates have softened, (b) "control for the active" is partly tautological — high-demand traditions shed nominal members faster, so survivors look loyal by construction, and (c) anecdotal high-profile LDS-to-Catholic conversions are real even if not statistically dominant. The LDS reply is that the *headline* claim ("most Mormons are leaving / uniquely declining") is simply not what the data show. See [[Informed Saints - The Surprising REAL Reason People Are Leaving The Church]].

## Best Quotes

> "We're even better than he said — 54%, with Catholics next at 57%. We're way closer to Catholic retention than he would like to admit."[^2]

> "When you actually control for people who are going to church, Latter-day Saints are number one for retention."[^3]

> "To just say that Mormons are leaving because the theology is weak or the doctrine is squishy is just not an accurate look at the picture."[^5]
