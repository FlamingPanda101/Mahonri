# Ezer Kenegdo Names Eve as Adam's Salvation Not His Assistant

#type/claim

## The Claim

The phrase rendered "help meet for him" in Genesis 2:18 ([[Ezer Kenegdo Names Eve as Adam's Salvation Not His Assistant|*ezer kenegdo*]]) is not a translation but a *transliteration* — and a degrading one that makes Eve sound like Adam's "little helper" or assistant. The underlying Hebrew means the opposite: *ezer* is a salvation-word, so Eve's first "title" is **Adam's savior**, a foreordination of the salvific role she fulfills by partaking of the fruit. [[J Richard Lambert]] argues the translators "translated every other word in the Bible up to this point and then chose not to translate that," producing the single worst rendering in the text.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence (steelman)

- **The narrative structure demands a deliverer, not an aide.** Lambert reads Genesis 2 as ending on a cliffhanger (marked by the *pe* / pilcrow / *tifcha*): Adam is commanded to be fruitful and multiply but *cannot* — the only path is a tree he himself may not partake of. The next unit opens with the *solution*: "it is not sufficient that man is alone." Eve enters precisely as the resolution to an impossible problem, which fits "salvation" far better than "assistant."[^2]
- **Lexical rarity points to weight, not chores.** Hebrew had "a million" available words for a mere helper; *ezer* is used only ~25 times in the entire Bible, and (Lambert claims) *every* occurrence is connected to some form of salvation — the Messiah's salvation, Yahweh's salvation, and Eve's salvation of Adam. He directs readers to verify each instance via the concordance at biblehub.com.[^3]
- **The *Ebenezer* parallel.** The same *ezer* root appears in *Ebenezer* — "here I raise my Ebenezer," the *stone of help / stone of salvation* and a plea of salvation in 1 Samuel 7 (cf. [[1 Samuel]]). The word carries deliverance, not domestic assistance.[^4]
- **"Kenegdo" denotes an equal, joining force.** The *kenegdo* element describes a power or force that meets an opposing equal force and is able to join with it — a poetic image of equal partnership "coming together," not subordination.[^1]
- **This dignifies rather than denigrates.** The reading turns the "most damning verse for women" interpretation on its head: Eve is introduced by her *role* before she is ever named, and that role is to save Adam by making the Fall possible. It coheres with the LDS teaching that Eve acted with "courage and wisdom" (see [[The Serpent Is a Symbol of the Messiah So Eve Foresaw the Savior]]).[^5]

## Best Quotes

> "It's the worst combination of the terms because it's so degrading. It makes it sound like Eve's this assistant to Adam... You translated every other word in the Bible up to this point and then you chose not to translate that but instead do a transliteration of literally 'help.'"[^1]

> "*Ezer* is only used 25 times in the entire Bible... and every single time it is connected to some form of salvation. It's used for the Messiah and his salvation. It's used for Yahweh and salvation, and it's used for Eve in providing salvation for Adam."[^3]

> "Eve is Adam's salvation. This is her first title. She's never introduced as Eve — she's only introduced as this kind of foreordination of what she will do, which is save him by partaking of the fruit."[^5]
