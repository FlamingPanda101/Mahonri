# Satanic Panic Statistics Show Less Than One Percent of Abuse Cases Reached Trial

## The Claim
The 1980s–90s satanic panic produced a vast volume of sex-abuse accusations whose underlying evidentiary base was extraordinarily thin. National 1989 data illustrate how few referred cases actually held up under legal scrutiny.

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. The 1989 national breakdown (strong — concrete figures)
Of 384,000 sex-abuse reports referred to law enforcement in 1989, 34.5% never went to trial: roughly half because there was "no evidence," about a quarter because accusers recanted, and the rest because the family opposed prosecution, the victim refused to testify, or the allegations were deemed false [^1].

### 2. The funnel to trial (strong)
Of the remaining ~250,000 cases, 118,000 (just under half) were plea-bargained to lesser charges, 84,000 pleaded guilty, and others remained undetermined three years later. Only 3,400 of the original 384,000 — **less than 1%** — went to trial and resulted in prison time [^2].

### 3. Cultural accelerants (moderate — contextual)
The McMartin preschool trial, the missing-children campaign, the popularity of Dungeons & Dragons, and horror films "gripped" the nation, paralyzing rational thinking "before the real psychologists and criminal investigators could get involved" [^3].

## Steelman (Opposing View)
A defender of the accusations could note that low trial rates do not prove innocence: child sex abuse is genuinely underreported and hard to prosecute, plea bargains can reflect real guilt, and "no evidence" can mean evidentiary difficulty rather than fabrication. The statistic alone cannot adjudicate any individual case. The video's force comes not from the raw funnel but from pairing it with the specific Utah cases where investigators actively concluded no crime occurred.

## Best Quotes
> "Out of the original 384,000 cases, only 3,400, less than 1%, ended up going to trial and serving prison time." [^2]

> "Before the real psychologists and criminal investigators could get involved, before cooler heads could prevail, the accusations had already paralyzed rational thinking." [^3]
