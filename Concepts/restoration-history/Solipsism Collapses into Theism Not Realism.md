# Solipsism Collapses into Theism Not Realism

## The Point
A second escape route from the cosmic conscious argument is *solipsism* — the claim that only your own mind exists and everything else is its product. [[Inspiring Philosophy|Michael Jones]] argues solipsism cannot stand on its own and, when followed through, lands on theism rather than the materialist realism its defenders hope for.[^1] This blocks the objection that "the universe is grounded in mind" might just mean *my* mind, not God's.

## Evidence
Jones builds on physicist David Deutsch, who argues in *The Fabric of Reality* that solipsism is "unsustainable on its own" and "logically it must collapse to something else."[^2] Deutsch's own conclusion is that solipsism collapses into *realism*: the solipsist must admit his mind contains "person-like thoughts, planet-like thoughts, [and] laws of physics like thoughts" that are real, autonomous, and "surprise, disappoint, enlighten or thwart" him — so solipsism is "just realism disguised and weighed down by additional unnecessary assumptions."[^3]

Jones agrees solipsism collapses but disputes the destination: "I would argue it collapses to theism."[^4] His reasoning: the aspects of experience the solipsist *cannot control* are, on his own view, still *mental and non-physical*. So there is "a more powerful aspect ... that is in control in governing the experience, which would be godlike in controlling reality."[^5] A mind in control of one's experience and "creation" that is *not you* "would more align with theistic idealism instead of realism."[^6] Either there is another mind (God), or the godlike governing part of one's own mind is something the self is subject to — either way the theistic implication is inescapable. Jones adds he does not actually find solipsism reasonable, since assuming other people are not conscious is unwarranted.[^6] This dovetails with the positive case in [[A Cosmic Mind Grounds the Universe (Theistic Idealism)]].

## Best Quotes
> "Solipsism, far from being a worldview stripped to its essentials, is actually just realism disguised ... worthless baggage introduced only to be explained away." — David Deutsch[^3]

> "Since solipsism still means there is a mind in control of experience and creation that is not you, that would more align with theistic idealism instead of realism." [^6]

## LDS Connection
The move from "reality is mental" to "a personal God governs it" is congenial to the LDS conviction that existence is fundamentally personal and relational — there is no impersonal Absolute behind the personal God, and other minds are genuinely real co-eternal agents, not projections (cf. [[Spirit Birth and Intelligences]]). Jones's insistence that it is *unreasonable* to deny other people are conscious echoes the LDS emphasis on real, irreducible persons in community rather than a single mind dreaming the world. Honest note: this section is the most speculative in the video — it answers a position (solipsism) almost no one holds, and the inference from "uncontrollable mental content" to "a governing godlike mind" is suggestive rather than demonstrative.
