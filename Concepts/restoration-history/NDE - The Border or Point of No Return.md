# NDE - The Border or Point of No Return

## The Point
A recurring later element of the near-death experience is the sense of approaching a **border or limit** — a threshold which, if crossed, would mean no return to physical life.[^1] The boundary takes culturally varied forms — a body of water, a gray mist, a door, a fence across a field, or simply a line — leading Moody to speculate that one "basic experience" may underlie the differing images.[^2] At the border the dying often meet deceased loved ones or the being of light beckoning them across; yet, told their work is unfinished, they are turned back, frequently against their will.[^3]

## Evidence
1. **A fence.** A cardiac-arrest patient found himself in a "rolling field" of "intense green," saw a fence with "a man on the other side... moving towards it as if to meet me," then "felt myself being drawn back, irresistibly."[^4]
2. **A body of water.** A nurse hemorrhaging after childbirth seemed to sail "to the other side of a large body of water" where her dead mother, father, and sister "seemed to be beckoning me to come on over," while she insisted, "No, no, I'm not ready to join you."[^5]
3. **A line.** A coma patient heard the white light say, "Come over this line and you will learn." Crossing it brought "feelings of peace, tranquility, a vanishing of all worries."[^6]
4. **A gray mist.** A heart-attack patient rushed toward a "gray mist" lit by "a living, golden yellow glow," but "instantly from the other side appeared my Uncle Carl, who had died many years earlier," blocking the path: "Go back. Your work on earth has not been completed."[^7]
5. **A door.** A dying woman passed through a "narrow, v-shaped passage" and saw "a beautiful, polished door, with no knob," its edges streaming brilliant light, with happy beings "reeling around" inside.[^8]

## Best Quotes
> "Beyond the mist, I could see people, and their forms were just like they are on the earth... The whole thing was permeated with the most gorgeous light—a living, golden yellow glow." [^7]

> "Yet, it wasn't my time to go through the mist, because instantly from the other side appeared my Uncle Carl, who had died many years earlier. He blocked my path, saying, 'Go back. Your work on earth has not been completed.'" [^7]

## LDS Connection
The threshold guarded by deceased kin who tell the dying their "work on earth has not been completed" coheres with the Restoration's doctrine of a **spirit world** — a real place, populated by the departed, who retain their identities and relationships and remain interested in the living. That the dead appear "just as they were when I knew them on earth" fits the LDS teaching that "that same sociality which exists among us here will exist among us there" ([[D&C 130.20-21|D&C 130:2]]). The repeated message that one is sent back because a divinely appointed task remains unfinished accords with the LDS conviction that mortal life is a purposeful probation whose length is in God's hands, not a meaningless interval.
