# The Cutlerites Preserved the Endowment Outside Utah

## The Claim
[[Christopher Blythe]] argues the Cutlerite Church — the tiny movement founded by [[Alpheus Cutler]] — is "the only other branch of the church other than that by [[Brigham Young]] that has maintained the endowment ceremony since 1844." It is Blythe's favorite faction precisely because it preserves both a credible early succession claim and the temple liturgy, even though it lost sealing. [^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **A continuous endowment in the "upper room" (rated: strong, fieldwork).** At the Cutlerite chapel in Missouri, visitors may use only the main floor; upstairs is "the upper room" where the endowment is performed. Blythe recounts a Cutlerite prophet returning from the Nauvoo Temple open house in 2002 and saying, "Chris, we believe in every one of those rooms" — a hint that their upper-room rite preserves the 1844 endowment. They should also retain a Doctrine and Covenants "maintained from the way it was in 1844." [^2]

2. **They kept the endowment but lost sealing in the 1850s (rated: strong, doctrinal).** Blythe can show the Cutlerites originally *did* believe in sealing but lost it in the 1850s — the same polygamy-driven unraveling described in [[Rejecting Polygamy Unravels Sealing and the Plan of Salvation]]. Cutler suspended plural marriage "until Jesus Christ returns" and lived as a monogamist; "the endowments remain strong" but the sealing did not. [^3]

3. **Cutler's succession claim is substantive (rated: moderate, historical).** Cutler received his **second anointing** under Joseph Smith — "the sixth person to ever receive it," just before Brigham Young — and gave Nauvoo sermons affirming "my salvation depends on sustaining the Twelve." He claimed Joseph privately ordained him one of **seven** prophets, becoming church leader when Joseph's uncle John Smith died (Cutler styled himself "head counselor over the church," distinct from a separate church president). In the order of second anointings he appears to be number six, not seven, but the experience he describes "sounds like they're hinting at the fullness of the priesthood." [^4]

4. **A movement at the edge of extinction (rated: strong, sociological).** There are "about six members left," "all very, very old" — "we are at the possible end of this community." They practice the law of consecration / United Order in a family-based form, pride themselves on their priesthood ordinances, are anti-polygamy, and have a prophecy the church could dwindle to three while they await a "one mighty and strong" / "Moses man." See [[Growth Past the Second Generation Validates a Movement]]. [^5]

**Steelman / tension.** That the Cutlerites kept the endowment but dropped sealing complicates any tidy LDS narrative — it shows a non-Brighamite line preserving temple liturgy, which a critic could cite against the claim that the full temple system survives only in Utah. The panel's reply is twofold: Cutler himself testified his salvation depended on sustaining the Twelve (i.e., the keys were with Brigham, see [[Succession Resolved Through the Keys of the Twelve]]), and the Cutlerite loss of sealing illustrates that a fragment of the Restoration cannot sustain the whole — the very point of preserving it intact.
