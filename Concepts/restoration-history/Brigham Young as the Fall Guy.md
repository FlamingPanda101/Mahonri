# Brigham Young as the Fall Guy (Objection Rebutted)

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
Many who want to remain Latter-day Saints but dislike certain features of the Church's history — plural marriage, the priesthood-and-race restriction, the oppression of women, authoritarianism — resolve the tension by blaming [[Brigham Young]] rather than [[Joseph Smith]]. Joseph is held "beyond reproach"; Brigham is "a little bit dispensable," "the fall guy." If you don't like something, "it's Brigham's fault. Joseph would never have done that."[^1]

## The Rebuttal

1. **Many "Brigham" problems trace to Joseph (rated: strong on polygamy).** Plural marriage is the clearest case. Some denialists claim [[Doctrine and Covenants 132|D&C 132]] is a Brigham-era forgery; but the Interpreter Foundation's wordprint and statistical analysis shows it was "almost certainly impossible" for it to be a later invention — it was already in circulation while Joseph was alive. "If Section 132 goes back to Joseph, then Joseph was involved with plural marriage. There's no way out."[^2] *Steelman:* not every uncomfortable item traces cleanly to Joseph — Peterson concedes "some don't; we're not clear on where they come from." The argument defeats the *blanket* exoneration of Joseph, not every individual attribution.

2. **Brigham's defining trait was loyalty to Joseph (rated: strong).** Research for the *Becoming Brigham* project surfaced "how loyal Brigham was to Joseph. Whatever Joseph taught, Brigham would try to do." The Far West episode is emblematic: a prophecy said the Twelve would depart for England from the Far West temple site on a set date; after the Saints were driven from Missouri it made no sense, but Brigham snuck in under cover of darkness to lay the cornerstone — even knocking on an apostate's door to announce "we were here" so the prophecy would be documented as fulfilled.[^3] A man this loyal is implausible as a freelance inventor of doctrines against Joseph.

3. **Complete submission to Joseph's rebuke (rated: strong, anecdotal).** When Joseph publicly dressed Brigham down, the "Lion of the Lord" — who "did not bend the knee to just anybody" — answered only, "Joseph, what would you have me do?"[^4] His dying words were reportedly "Joseph, Joseph, Joseph."[^5]

4. **The structural motive (rated: moderate / sociological).** The scapegoating "preserves Joseph" as the untouchable origin of the tradition while making Brigham the disposable cause of everything uncomfortable.[^1] *Steelman:* there are genuine differences between Joseph and Brigham (e.g., Adam-God, the explicit race teachings), so distinguishing them is legitimate; Peterson's claim is that the *blame-shifting* is selective and historically false, not that the two men were identical.

## Best Quotes

> "Brigham Young is a little bit dispensable. He's the fall guy. ... if you don't like something, it's Brigham's fault. Joseph would never have done that. And that's just not true in a lot of these cases."[^1]

> "If Section 132 goes back to Joseph, then Joseph was involved with plural marriage. There's no way out. You may not like it... but that doesn't allow you to change history."[^2]

> "How is Brigham going to respond to being dressed down in public by Joseph? ... But no, he says, Joseph, what would you have me do? Complete submission, which I think Brigham would not have shown to just about anybody else on the planet."[^4]
