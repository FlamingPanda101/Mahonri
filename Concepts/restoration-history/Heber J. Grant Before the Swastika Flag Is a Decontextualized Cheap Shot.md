# Heber J. Grant Before the Swastika Flag Is a Decontextualized Cheap Shot

## The Claim

The widely circulated photograph of LDS president [[Heber J. Grant]] seated before a large swastika flag (Frankfurt, July 1937) is, [[Stephen Smoot]] argues, "a total cheap shot" when stripped of context. Grant did not display the flag, did not endorse the regime, and preached purely apolitical content; the image is shared "for shock value" without the facts that fully explain it.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The setting was a Nazi-owned public hall.** During a 1937 grand tour of Europe, Grant preached in Frankfurt to a crowd of about a thousand (members and non-members) in a rented venue owned by the National Socialist Teachers League. It therefore came equipped with the swastika flag — which at the time was the **national flag of Germany by law**. The flag's presence was outside Grant's control.[^2]

2. **The Gestapo was watching.** The dossier shows informants planted in Grant's audiences, including a "Fräulein" in Berlin taking notes. Smoot's point: any anti-Nazi gesture (the *Sound of Music* flag-tearing image) would have ended catastrophically with Gestapo agents present — Grant had no freedom to repudiate the flag even had he wished. See [[The Gestapo Dossier Reveals Systematic Nazi Surveillance of the Latter-day Saints]].[^3]

3. **The Switzerland parallel.** Weeks earlier Grant was photographed preaching before the Swiss national flag in Bern/Zurich, and "nobody gets mad at that one." Standing before a host nation's flag at a public event is normal diplomatic practice — like heads of state photographed before rows of flags of countries they oppose — and is not an endorsement of that government.[^4]

4. **The content was apolitical.** From both LDS and Nazi reports of the speeches (no transcript survives), Grant went through the Articles of Faith one by one and expounded them in Frankfurt and Berlin. "There's no political content in Heber J. Grant's speeches"; he taught what Latter-day Saints believe, not support for Nazism. This dovetails with [[The Church's Accommodationist Survival Strategy in Nazi Germany]].[^5]

## Best Quotes

> "It's a total cheap shot to use this as some kind of gotcha against Heber J. Grant. It's totally out of his control when he's speaking in these venues." [^1]

> "More important than just the picture of him in front of the flag, what was he saying? We know what he's saying. He's not endorsing Nazism." [^5]
