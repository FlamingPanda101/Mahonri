# Daniel 7 Son of Man Is Enthroned by the Ancient of Days

## The Claim
[[Blake Ostler]] argues that [[Daniel 7.13-14|Daniel 7:13-14]] depicts *two distinct divine figures*: the **Ancient of Days** (the Father) seated on a fiery throne, and "one like a son of man" who *approaches* him, is *led into his presence*, and is *given* authority, glory, and a kingdom — then is worshiped. Because the Son of Man *receives* dominion from a higher figure, he cannot be inherently/identically that figure; he is elevated to share the divine throne. [^1][^2]

## The Rebuttal (Supporting Evidence)

1. **There are *thrones* (plural) and a transfer of authority.** "Thrones were set in place and the Ancient of Days took his seat." The Son of Man is "given authority, glory and sovereign power... his dominion is an everlasting dominion." *Rating: strong* — the giving/receiving structure is explicit in the text. [^3][^2]

2. **"Coming with the clouds of heaven" is Yahweh storm-god imagery applied to the Son of Man.** The cloud-rider motif (used of Baal in Canaanite texts and of Yahweh in the OT) is here predicated of the Son of Man, whom Jesus identifies himself with in the Synoptics. *Rating: accurate* on the ANE background and the Synoptic self-designation; it bolsters a high-but-distinct Christology. [^4]

3. **The Son of Man is *not* the Ancient of Days.** "He was led into his presence. So the Son of Man is not the Ancient of Days." *Rating: a fair reading* of the spatial movement in the vision; the two figures are distinguished. [^5]

4. **Steelman of the Trinitarian/classical reading.** The Son of Man's reception of *eternal, indestructible, universal dominion* and *worship* (Aramaic *pelach*, cultic service) is exactly what Daniel elsewhere reserves for God — so the scene can argue the Son of Man *shares* the one divine identity, with distinction-of-persons but not subordination of deity. Later Jewish/Christian tradition even read the "thrones" as Father and Son co-reigning (cf. b. Sanhedrin's "two powers" worry). *Rating: a strong counter* — distinctness of persons does not entail inequality of nature; Daniel 7 establishes *two* divine figures, which Trinitarians happily accept. [^6]

## Best Quotes
> "He approached the Ancient of Days and was led into his presence. So the Son of Man is not the Ancient of Days." [^5]

> "The Ancient of Days is giving the kingdom and all sovereign power to the Son of Man here." [^2]

> "The Son of Man can't have all authority and glory if he's being given it by the Ancient of Days. The Ancient of Days is in this case higher than the Son of Man." [^7]
