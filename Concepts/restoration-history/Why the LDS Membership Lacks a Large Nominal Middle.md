# Why the LDS Membership Lacks a Large Nominal Middle

## The Claim
Unlike many traditions with a big "Christmas-and-Easter" middle, LDS membership is bimodal: people are either actively engaged or have left, with a comparatively small gray zone of nominal-but-inactive identifiers. This is a signature of a high-demand religion.

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The Catholic contrast (rated: moderate, illustrative).** The host frames it: Catholics have the very active, the disaffiliated, and a *large* middle who attend only on Christmas or Easter; in the LDS Church "that gray area is a lot smaller" — people are "actively engaged or have left."[^1]

2. **The middle has shrunk over time (rated: moderate).** In the 1980s "there were a lot more people who identified as Latter-day Saint but weren't really going to church all that much. Today that's dropped dramatically. Where if you're going to identify as a Latter-day Saint, you're also probably very active."[^2]

3. **The high-demand mechanism (rated: moderate).** When a religion demands little, members can keep the identity nominally; when it demands more, dropping participation tends to take the identity with it. "In a low-demand religion you might still say that you're a member... but you're not really going. But if you're more a high-demand religion, then you're going to connect even more."[^3] See [[High-Demand Religion Improves Retention and Well-being]]. *Steelman:* the same dynamic means the LDS Church likely *loses the label* of people other traditions would keep on the rolls — so its "smaller middle" is partly a measurement artifact of how identity tracks practice, not purely a strength.

4. **Pastoral caveat (rated: n/a, normative).** Dyer says the Church would "love to see more individuals who for whatever reason can't make it to church very often... still identify as Latter-day Saint," affirming marginal members.[^4]

## Best Quotes

> "In our church, there tends to be a much larger percentage of people who are actively engaged or have left the church, and that gray area is a lot smaller."[^1]

> "If you're going to identify as a Latter-day Saint, you're also probably very active... people are likely not going to identify as a Latter-day Saint if they're not going to church a lot."[^2]

> "In a low-demand religion you might still say that you're a member of the religion but you're not really going. But if you're more a high-demand religion, then you're going to connect even more."[^3]
