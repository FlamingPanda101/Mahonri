# Journal of Discourses

The *Journal of Discourses* is a 26-volume collection of sermons by Brigham Young and other early Latter-day Saint leaders, published in England between 1854 and 1886. The addresses were taken down in Pitman shorthand (chiefly by reporter George D. Watt) and transcribed for a paying subscriber audience. Because it is a privately published, transcribed record rather than canonized scripture, its standing as authoritative doctrine is contested.

## Relevance

The Journal is central to the vault's treatment of Brigham Young. The argument that it is an imperfect mirror — sometimes making Brigham "sound more authoritarian" than he was — is used to defend him against [[Hero-Flattening as a Cultural Disease|hero-flattening]] and against treating every recorded line as binding ([[Tabernacle Infallibility Misreads Brigham Young]]). New transcription work (see [[LeGene Kuth]] / [[LaJean Carruth]]) on Watt's shorthand bears directly on how reliably it preserves what was actually said.
