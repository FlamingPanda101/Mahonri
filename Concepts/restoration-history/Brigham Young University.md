# Brigham Young University

Brigham Young University (BYU) is the flagship private research university of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, located in Provo, Utah, and named for the Church's second president. It is the institutional home of much faithful LDS scholarship, including the journals [[BYU Studies]] and the apologetic publishing tradition tied to the former FARMS and Neal A. Maxwell Institute.

## Relevance

BYU appears in the vault both as an academic affiliation of cited scholars (e.g. New Testament papyrologist [[Lincoln H. Blumell]]) and as the setting for the argument that evolutionary biology has been taught there as a mainstream academic discipline — a point used against the claim that LDS faith requires creationism.
