# The Serpent Is a Symbol of the Messiah So Eve Foresaw the Savior

#type/claim

## The Claim

The serpent of Eden is not Satan and Eve was not duped. In proto-Hebrew the serpent is a *symbol of the Messiah*, so when Eve says "the serpent beguiled me, and I did eat," she is really saying **"I foresaw the Savior, and so I partook."** [[J Richard Lambert]] argues the verse traditionally read as proof that "by Eve's own admission she was duped" — "the most damning verse of the Bible for women" — actually records Eve's *wisdom* in recognizing that the Fall was necessary and that humanity would be saved through the Savior she perceived.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence (steelman)

- **"Beguiled" is a later, loaded insertion.** The root behind the word is simply "to lead / to guide / be led to" — a concept with no nefarious connotation. Because proto-Hebrew is conceptual, that idea "would not have used those roots"; the negative "beguilement" is a later translation layer detectable by diachronic linguistics. The most negative defensible word is merely "tempted" or "led."[^2]
- **The serpent breaks every Hebrew snake-naming rule.** The Near East had many snakes and the Bible names each by a distinguishing feature (stripes, scales, etc.). The Eden serpent is the *only* snake in the Bible that follows no Hebraic grammatical pattern: it is given the bare word *nachash*, which Lambert says is "not Hebrew at all," signaling that it functions as a symbol, not a zoological animal. (Latter-day Saints already know this: in the temple "you don't see a snake slither out on screen hissing.")[^3]
- **The serpent is humble and exalted, not evil.** In the Midrash the serpent is "the most humble of all creatures" — legless, closest to the earth, always prostrate before God; the word usually rendered "subtle" should be "humble." It is simultaneously a symbol of power, of Yahweh, and of Jesus Christ.[^4]
- **Scripture itself equates the serpent with Christ.** Moses' staff becomes a serpent as the sign that he comes in Yahweh's name (Exodus 4/7); and [[John 3.16|John 3:14]] has Christ say that "as the serpent was raised on the staff to heal the Israelites... so too will be the Son of Man" — Christ identifying himself with the serpent. No deep linguistics is even required to see the connection.[^5]
- **The reading is corroborated by LDS prophetic teaching.** It fits [[Russell M. Nelson|President Nelson]] (Eve acted by "courage and wisdom... she did what had to be done") and Sister Dennis ("a transgression but eternally a glorious necessity"), and pairs with the "woman and the tower" image — Eve in a "higher, holier place" who could "see afar off" and *divine* that the serpent was Christ and her seed.[^6]

## Best Quotes

> "When she's saying 'the serpent beguiled me, thus I partook,' what she's really saying is 'I foresaw the Savior, and so I partook.'"[^1]

> "The serpent in the Adam narrative is the only snake in all of the Bible that does not follow any Hebraic patterns properly... It gives you the base word for snake, which is *nachash* — which is very wrong. It's not Hebrew at all."[^3]

> "Now the narrative is: Eve was wise — wise enough to see that we had to fall, but that we'd be saved through the Savior. And so she partook."[^1]
