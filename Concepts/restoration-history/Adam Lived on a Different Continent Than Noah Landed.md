#type/claim

# Adam Lived on a Different Continent Than Noah Landed

## The Claim
Latter-day Saint teaching places [[Adam]] (and the post-Eden valley of [[D&C 116|Adam-ondi-Ahman]]) in Missouri / North America, which critics mock because it puts Eden's environs far from the Holy Land. [[Jonah (Plain and Precious Publishing)|Jonah Barnes]] answers from the "Egyptian Life of Adam and Eve" (53:7), where God prophesies that he will take [[Noah]]'s line "to a distant land" while "the land in which you live now shall remain desolate and without one inhabitant in it." Barnes reads this as the apocryphon itself distinguishing Adam's continent from Noah's landfall — so "the only place we're sure Adam-ondi-Ahman was not is the Middle East," because that is where Noah (Ararat, Armenia) ends up.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. The apocryphon separates Adam's land from Noah's
The cited prophecy contrasts two lands: the one Adam inhabits (to "remain desolate") and the "distant land" to which Noah's righteous remnant will be brought. Barnes paraphrases: "Adam did not live in the same place that Noah died... Noah left one world for the other."[^2]
> **Evidence rating:** Weak-to-moderate (the two-lands reading is a plausible-but-not-forced construal of a single apocryphal verse).

### 2. Noah's landfall is the Old World, so Adam's is elsewhere
Since tradition (and the apocrypha) put Noah at Ararat in Armenia / the Middle East, the contrast implies Adam's continent is *not* the Middle East — which Barnes uses to neutralize the "Missouri is absurd" objection: Eden's expulsion-land "has to be somewhere," and the texts at least permit a non–Middle-Eastern Adam.[^3]
> **Evidence rating:** Weak-to-moderate (a permission argument: it removes an objection rather than positively locating Adam).

### 3. The "Missouri layup" rhetorical move
Barnes's framing: given that Joseph allegedly gets the *who/how/when* of the Adam-ondi-Ahman scene right (see [[Adam Gathering His Posterity Before Death Matches the Adam Apocrypha]]), the mere *where* ("Missouri") is the easy part — "he could say Antarctica and I'd believe him." The continental separation is offered as removing the sting from the geography, not as standalone proof.[^4]
> **Evidence rating:** Rhetorical (an argument-from-track-record, not independent evidence).

## Steelman of the Opposing View
This is the weakest link in the episode. It hangs on one verse of a late, text-critically messy "Egyptian" recension, and "a distant land" need not mean *another continent* — ancient geography routinely calls modest distances "distant," and the Genesis flood narrative itself situates Ararat in the same broad Near-Eastern world as Eden (between the Tigris and Euphrates per Genesis 2). The host himself waffles ("we don't know how far they traveled... heaven only knows where the Garden of Eden was... I don't really care"), and even floats that Eden was "taken up" off the earth — which would moot the whole geography. So the apocryphon does not establish a North-American Adam; at most it is *not inconsistent* with the Latter-day Saint placement of Adam-ondi-Ahman in Missouri ([[D&C 116]]), which remains a revelation-based claim, not an archaeological one. It connects to the broader Heartland-vs-Mesoamerica dispute (see [[The New York Hill Is Not the Book of Mormon Cumorah]]) but settles none of it.

## Best Quotes
> "I will bring them to a distant land, and the land in which you live now shall remain desolate and without one inhabitant in it."[^2]

> "We know the Garden of Eden — the only place we're sure the Garden of Eden was not is Missouri. And the only place we're sure that Adam-ondi-Ahman was not is the Middle East."[^3]

> "It has to be somewhere... Missouri or some gas station in Iraq... if he gets all those [details] right, he could say Antarctica and I'd believe him."[^4]

## LDS Connection
The note documents how the Adam apocrypha get pressed into the **Heartland** geography conversation (Adam-ondi-Ahman in Daviess County, Missouri, per [[D&C 116]]). It is the geography-facing tail of the same argument as [[Adam Gathering His Posterity Before Death Matches the Adam Apocrypha]] and [[The Book of Eve Has Eve Dictate the First Scripture]], and overlaps thematically with the New-World-Cumorah material in [[The New York Hill Is Not the Book of Mormon Cumorah]] and [[Oliver Cowdery's Letter VII States Cumorah Is in New York as Fact]] — though, as the steelman notes, it does far less work than its rhetorical framing suggests.
