# "Blood of Abel to Zechariah" — Not Merely "A to Z" Rebutted

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
A common reading treats Jesus's "from the blood of Abel unto the blood of Zacharias" ([[Luke 11.47-51|Luke 11:47-51]]) as just an alphabetic merism — "all the blood from A to Z."

## The Rebuttal
- Abel and Zechariah are paired not as alphabetical bookends but because both shed **blood that could not be washed away** — Abel's earth-rejected blood ([[Blood of Abel - Unburiable Tradition]]) and Zechariah's boiling temple blood ([[Blood of Zechariah - Perpetual Boiling Blood]]) [^1].
- This makes Jesus's retort cohere: the Pharisees lecture him on ritual *washing* while standing on temple stones stained with prophet-blood that *never washes off* [^2].

## Best Quote
> "I heard somebody say, 'Oh, it just means A to Z'... Please don't ever say that from the pulpit. That is such garbage. No, it's Abel's blood that couldn't be buried and Zachariah's blood that couldn't be buried — this blood that stains forever." — [[Jonah (Plain and Precious Publishing)|Jonah]] [^1]
