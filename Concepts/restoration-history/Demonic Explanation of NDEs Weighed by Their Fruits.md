# Demonic Explanation of NDEs Weighed by Their Fruits

## The Point
Among supernatural readings, a few of Moody's hearers proposed that NDEs are demonic deceptions — experiences "directed by inimical forces" to delude the dying [^1]. Moody answers with a fruits test: the way to tell a God-directed from a Satan-directed experience is to watch what the person *does and says afterward* [^1]. A loving deceiver makes no sense; God would make his visitants loving and forgiving, Satan would make his servants hateful and destructive — and Moody's subjects "come back with a renewed commitment to follow the former course and to disavow the latter" [^1]. If a demon engineered all this to recruit emissaries of hate, "he certainly has failed miserably... to make persuasive emissaries for his program!" [^2]

## Evidence
1. The objection: "someone in one of my audiences has proposed demonic explanations of near-death experiences, suggesting that the experiences were doubtless directed by inimical forces" [^1].
2. The proposed criterion of discernment: "the best way of distinguishing between God-directed and Satan-directed experiences would be to see what the person involved does and says after his experience" [^1].
3. The expected fruits: "God, I suppose, would try to get those to whom he appears to be loving and forgiving. Satan would presumably tell his servants to follow a course of hate and destruction" [^1].
4. The observed fruits: "my subjects have come back with a renewed commitment to follow the former course" — love and forgiveness — "and to disavow the latter" [^1].
5. The reductio against the demonic hypothesis: any demon who produced these results has "failed miserably... to make persuasive emissaries for his program" [^2].

## Best Quotes
> "It seems to me that the best way of distinguishing between God-directed and Satan-directed experiences would be to see what the person involved does and says after his experience." [^1]

> "In the light of all the machinations which a hypothetical demon would have to have carried out in order to delude his hapless victim... he certainly has failed miserably—as far as I can tell—to make persuasive emissaries for his program!" [^2]

## LDS Connection
Moody's fruits test is the scriptural test for spirits and prophets: "by their fruits ye shall know them" (Matthew 7:16–20). A true encounter with light bears the fruits of love, forgiveness, and reformed life; a deception bears hatred. The transformed lives of NDE subjects — toward charity and away from destruction — are evidence that the experience is benign, consistent with the Restoration's confidence that the light of Christ draws souls toward good and that the spirit world is not a snare but a real intermediate state (Alma 40; Moroni 7 on discerning good from evil).
