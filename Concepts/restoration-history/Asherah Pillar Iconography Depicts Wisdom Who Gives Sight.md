# Asherah Pillar Iconography Depicts Wisdom Who Gives Sight

## The Point
[[Mandy Green]] reads the form of the Judaean "Asherah pillar" figurine as a visual theology of the divine feminine: a tree-trunk base topped by a woman holding her breasts, with strikingly oversized eyes. The tree links the goddess to the Tree of Life; the eyes encode the conviction that "wisdom gives you sight" — spiritual sight and discernment. The same motif, she argues, structures Eden, where the serpent usurps Wisdom's proper role of opening the eyes.

## Evidence
- **The figurine's two halves both signify the Mother.** Green describes the Judaean Asherah pillar: "the base is like a tree trunk, and then the top is a woman holding her breasts... and she has unusually big eyes for a sculpture like that. It's because wisdom gives you sight."[^1] The tree-trunk base ties the goddess to the fruit-bearing Tree of Life, "also a symbol of our mother."[^2]
- **Wisdom is the one who gives sight.** "Anciently in Isaiah and in so many other texts of our Old Testament, wisdom is known to be the one who gives sight... wisdom gives you spiritual sight."[^3] The large eyes are therefore not stylistic accident but iconographic statement.
- **Eden as an inversion of Wisdom's role.** The serpent — "always the symbol of the son of God," which is why Satan adopts a form Eve "would recognize" — approaches the woman who is "after" wisdom and "usurps wisdom's role."[^4] He inverts the pairing: traditionally "knowledge is a masculine construct and wisdom is a feminine construct," and "right there the serpent is inverting those two."[^5] Eve "desires to become wise," and what Satan hijacks is precisely Wisdom's gift "to give you eyes to see and discernment to be able to see the good from the evil."[^6]
- **The archaeology this rests on.** Green's iconographic reading sits atop the mainstream archaeological recovery of Asherah as a feminine divine in ancient Israel — see [[Feminine Divine in Ancient Near Eastern Worship]] and [[William Dever]]'s synthesis of the Kuntillet Ajrud / Khirbet el-Qom "Yahweh and his Asherah" inscriptions.

## Best Quotes
> "If you look at a Judaean Asherah pillar, the base is like a tree trunk. And then the top is a woman holding her breasts... and she has unusually big eyes for a sculpture like that. It's because wisdom gives you sight. Wisdom gives you spiritual sight."[^1]

> "Satan's going to take on this role... Eve, to me it seems like, she desires to become wise. Now traditionally knowledge is a masculine construct and wisdom is a feminine construct. So right there the serpent is inverting those two."[^5]

## LDS Connection
If the pillar's tree-and-eyes form really encodes "Wisdom gives sight," then a goddess the Hebrew prophets came to condemn carried, in her oldest iconography, a theology compatible with the Latter-day Saint Heavenly Mother — a divine feminine bound to the Tree of Life and to spiritual discernment. The reading reframes the Eden narrative as the displacement of the Mother's gift, anticipating its deliberate removal from the temple under Josiah (see [[Josiah's Reform Removed the Mother from the Temple]]) and aligning with the broader convergence documented in [[Feminine Divine in Ancient Near Eastern Worship]].
