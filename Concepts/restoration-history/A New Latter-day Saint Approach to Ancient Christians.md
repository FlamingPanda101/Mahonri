# A New Latter-day Saint Approach to Ancient Christians

## The Point
Combs lays out the volume's two governing methodological commitments. First, differences between ancient Christians and Latter-day Saints must not be *automatically* read as evidence of apostasy; they should be explained on the ancient Christians' own terms, with respect and "holy envy." Second, the truth of the Restoration does not depend on proving other churches deficient.

## Evidence
1. **No template assumption (either direction).** "We cannot assume that something found in The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints today necessarily existed in the ancient Church" — and the reverse holds too. Today's Church is not a template for the first-century Church, nor vice versa (cf. D&C 124:38, 41). [^1]
2. **Differences ≠ apostasy.** When the authors encounter differences, they "work to explain how and why ancient Christians developed beliefs and practices that contrast with our own — working toward understanding them from a position of respect and even 'holy envy.'" [^2]
3. **"Holy envy" attribution.** The term was coined by Krister Stendahl, Lutheran bishop and biblical scholar, and used to persuade Stockholm to allow construction of an LDS temple. [^3]
4. **Restoration not dependent on others' deficiency.** "We believe that the truth regarding the power and purposes of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints is not dependent on proving that other Christian churches are deficient, not guided by God, or worse." [^2]
5. **An act of devotion.** The book is framed as turning hearts to "spiritual fathers and mothers" (Malachi 4:5–6; JS-H 1:38–39; D&C 2:1–3), so that readers "can learn to love and appreciate them" and gain insights to "deepen our own devotion as Latter-day Saints." [^4]

## Best Quotes
> "Our authors acknowledge the differences between ancient Christians and Latter-day Saints without automatically assuming such differences to be evidence of apostasy." [^2]

> "Welcome to the world of ancient Christians!" [^4]

## LDS Connection
This concept *is* the LDS connection — it is the hermeneutic frame the entire Maxwell Institute volume operates under, integrating [[The Gathering of Truths from All Churches]], the disavowal in [[Great Apostasy Is Not a Scriptural Term in LDS Standard Works]], and the "early Christian studies" posture of [[Patristics vs Early Christian Studies]].
