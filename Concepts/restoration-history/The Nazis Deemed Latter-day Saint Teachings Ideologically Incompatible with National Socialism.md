# The Nazis Deemed Latter-day Saint Teachings Ideologically Incompatible with National Socialism

## The Claim

From their own private, internal documents, top Nazi officials judged the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints to be **fundamentally incompatible with National Socialism**. [[Stephen Smoot]] argues this is "a great resounding endorsement" precisely because it is unfiltered: the regime that murdered millions concluded that LDS theology and practice could not be reconciled with its worldview, and that the Church's American leadership was actively hostile to it.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **A composite Nazi indictment of the Saints.** Across the reports, Smoot reconstructs *why* the regime distrusted the Church: (a) it was an **American** church with foreign connections — a category of suspicion the regime applied to Jehovah's Witnesses, Adventists, Baptists, Methodists, and Quakers alike; (b) its **centralized tithing** routed money to a church rather than the state — anathema to a regime obsessed with controlling currency; (c) perceived **"Jewish influence"** on its theology, since concepts like Zion, Sabbath, and even the word *tithing* read as Old Testament–rooted, and the Berlin mission building was Jewish-owned; (d) a theology judged "bizarre," a "mishmash from Buddhism and Islam and Christianity"; and (e) suspicion that **polygamy** was still secretly practiced among leaders.[^2]

2. **The 1938 report to Rosenberg — the smoking gun.** A report written to Reichsleiter [[Alfred Rosenberg]] states flatly: "the doctrine of the Mormons is incompatible with the national socialist worldview. Furthermore, it is a fact that the Mormons in Germany show no interest in political events... members of the sect categorically refuse to use the German salute... the American sect leadership is still shamelessly agitating against national socialist Germany today. Thus, there can be no talk of a pro-German attitude." Smoot stresses this was for private internal use, so it reflects "their honest unfiltered raw opinion."[^3]

3. **They saw through the PR.** When local mission presidents tried to portray the Church favorably, the Nazis did not buy it: the report dismisses one leader's claim that Mormonism and National Socialism are "very similar" as "a deliberate distortion of the facts." This sets up the tension resolved in [[The Church's Accommodationist Survival Strategy in Nazi Germany]] — official accommodation paired with official condemnation of the ideology.[^4]

4. **An individual Saint drew the same line.** [[Paul Herbert Schick]] refused the Nazi salute precisely on the ground that one cannot "serve both Zion and another Reich," independently confirming from the Latter-day Saint side the incompatibility the Nazis asserted from theirs.[^5]
