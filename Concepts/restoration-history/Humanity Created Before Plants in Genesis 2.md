#type/claim

# Genesis 2 Creates Humanity Before Plant Life (Contradicting Genesis 1)

## The Claim

In the second creation account, the human is formed when "there were no plants ... no vegetation on the earth," and plant life appears only *after* the human is made (the garden of [[Genesis 2.4-7|Genesis 2:8]]). This directly contradicts [[Genesis 1.11-13|Genesis 1]], which creates all vegetation on day three — three days *before* humanity.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Explicit textual statement (decisive).** [[Genesis 2.4-7|Genesis 2:5]]: "no plant of the field was yet in the earth, and no vegetation of the field had yet sprung up," with the reason given (no rain, no human to till), followed by the forming of the human in v.7. McClellan: "this describes an earth devoid of plant life and then the human was created and it was not until after the human was created that we have the creation of plant life."[^1] Evidence rating: **decisive**.

2. **Comprehensive scope (strong).** The two terms cover *both* categories of vegetation — wild (no rain) and cultivated (no tiller) — so the absence is total, not partial. See [[Siach and Eseb - Wild vs Cultivated Vegetation]].[^2] Evidence rating: **strong**.

3. **No trees before the garden (supporting).** McClellan notes that in this account "no trees seem to have existed until God planted the garden in or next to Eden," reinforcing that flora postdates the human.[^3] Evidence rating: **moderate**.

4. **Direct conflict with day three (decisive).** Genesis 1 has grass, seed plants, and fruit trees "all created on day three, 3 days prior to the creation of humanity."[^4] The orders are mutually exclusive. Evidence rating: **decisive**.

**Steelman:** A harmonizer might argue 2:5 refers only to *agricultural* plants ("of the field") that needed human cultivation, not all vegetation, so day-three wild plants could already exist. McClellan pre-empts this: the two terms together denote *both* wild and cultivated vegetation, and the stated cause is the absence of rain *and* a tiller, covering all of it.[^2]

## Best Quotes

> "So when the human was created, there were no plants. There was no vegetation on the earth." [^5]

> "This describes an earth devoid of plant life and then the human was created and it was not until after the human was created that we have the creation of plant life. This directly contradicts the creation account in Genesis 1, which has plant life all created on day three, 3 days prior to the creation of humanity." [^1]
