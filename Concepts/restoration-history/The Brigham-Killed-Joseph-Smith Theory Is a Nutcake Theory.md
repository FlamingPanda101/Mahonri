# The Brigham-Killed-Joseph-Smith Theory Is a Nutcake Theory

## The Point
A fringe conspiracy theory holds that [[Brigham Young]] orchestrated the 1844 murder of [[Joseph Smith]] — using apostles John Taylor and [[Willard Richards]] as his instruments — so that he, as president of the Quorum of the Twelve, could seize the Church and introduce his own nefarious doctrines (polygamy, temple oaths). Dr. [[Daniel C. Peterson]] calls it, in his "technical term," a "nutcake theory," and gives several reasons it is "inconceivable."

## Evidence
- **The forensic basis is laughable.** Much of the claimed evidence "is ballistic evidence and so on" for "a murder that happened in 1844 — how much ballistic evidence do you really have, really?"[^1]
- **Brigham's total loyalty to Joseph makes it psychologically inconceivable.** The *Becoming Brigham* research repeatedly illustrated "how absolutely loyal and deferential Brigham Young was to Joseph Smith." The Far West episode is emblematic: under an active extermination order in Missouri, Brigham went out of his way to lay the temple cornerstone there at peril of his life — even knocking on an apostate's door at dawn — simply because Joseph had earlier said the Twelve would depart for England from that spot. When Joseph once publicly rebuked him, the "Lion of the Lord" answered only, "Joseph, what would you have me do?" His reported dying words were "Joseph, Joseph, Joseph."[^2]
- **He wanted to *continue* Joseph's program, not replace it.** Ron Esplin's point (in the series): of all the 1844 succession claimants, only Brigham and the Twelve sought to carry out Joseph's full Nauvoo program — the temple, the gathering, plural marriage. The conspiracy's motive (Brigham "itching to take control" to introduce *his own* innovations) inverts the actual record: "He absolutely does not fit that profile."[^3]
- **The real killers publicly boasted — and never named Brigham.** "The people that did murder him were publicly claiming it afterwards." Thomas C. Sharp and his associates "were not hiding their involvement at all. They were proud of it." Peterson's reductio: if Willard Richards and John Taylor had been the assassins "with smoking guns, smirks on their faces," the actual mob defendants on trial for murder would surely have said so — "When you're on trial for murder, you don't bring up the fact that these guys already did it?"[^4]

## Best Quotes
> "We started off with one on who killed Joseph Smith, because there's a — to me — nutcake theory out there... that says it was Brigham Young using as his instruments John Taylor and Willard Richards. The thing is nuts." [^5]

> "I think the idea that Brigham Young would ever raise his hand against Joseph Smith is just inconceivable the more you get to know the man. And it's instructive that his dying words are 'Joseph, Joseph, Joseph.'" [^2]

> "Wait — when you're on trial for murder, you don't bring up the fact that these guys already did it? They already did it when we showed up... No, it's a crazy theory." [^4]

## LDS Connection
This is the most extreme form of the broader move that makes Brigham the disposable villain of LDS history — see [[Brigham Young as the Fall Guy]]. Refuting it reinforces the legitimacy of the succession: Brigham did not seize power by murder but received it as president of the Twelve who held the keys, the only faction committed to Joseph's full program — see [[Brigham Young Saved the Church Through the Keys]].
