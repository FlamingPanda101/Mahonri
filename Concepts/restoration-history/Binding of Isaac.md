# Binding of Isaac

## Overview

The **Binding of Isaac** (the *Akedah*, Genesis 22) is the account in which God commands Abraham to offer his son Isaac, then provides a ram in his place. In this vault it is referenced less as a standalone Genesis episode than as a **typological and Abrahamic-restoration** hinge; see the linked notes for the developed arguments.

## The Claim

Several strands carry the topic:

1. **Type of Christ.** The dominant reading is that the Akedah prefigures the Atonement — a beloved son carried to a mountain to be offered, a substitute provided, the father-son relationship at the center ([[The Binding of Isaac as a Type of Christ]]). The episode is treated as deliberate Christological foreshadowing rather than mere narrative.
2. **Abrahamic context for the Book of Abraham.** Because Abraham's own near-sacrifice (Abraham 1) and his covenant story frame the [[Book of Abraham]], the Akedah belongs to the wider Abraham material the vault evaluates — including the methodological point to [[Judge the Book of Abraham by Its Fruits|judge the Book of Abraham by its fruits]].
3. **Apocryphal reconstructions.** Ward Radio explores apocryphal Akedah accounts (Jubilees, Jasher, pseudo-Jubilees, targums, the Old Slavonic Palaea) that diverge from canonical Genesis. One strand holds the command was a **trick of Satan** impersonating the Lord ([[Apocryphal Traditions That Satan Tricked Abraham Into the Sacrifice]]), which is paired with a reading of James that credits Abraham's *belief* rather than the completed act ([[James Says Abraham Believed Not That He Completed the Work]]); a parallel strand reconstructs a **messianic Akedah in which Isaac was actually offered** ([[Joseph Smith Restored a Messianic Akedah in Which Isaac Was Actually Sacrificed]]). Both rest on the observation that the NT itself draws on a broader Abraham tradition ([[The New Testament Draws on Non-Canonical Abraham Traditions]]).
