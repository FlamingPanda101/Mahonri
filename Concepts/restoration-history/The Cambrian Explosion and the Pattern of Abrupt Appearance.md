# The Cambrian Explosion and the Pattern of Abrupt Appearance

## The Point
[[Stephen C. Meyer]] argues that the fossil record looks nothing like Darwin's branching tree. In the **Cambrian explosion** the major animal body plans "appear very abruptly… with no discernible connection to ancestral precursors," and this pattern of abrupt appearance "recurs up and down the sedimentary rock column" — first winged insects, dinosaurs, birds, mammals, flowering plants.[^1] Instead of one tree growing from a single trunk, the record looks "more like a lawn or… an orchard of separate trees."[^2] Crucially, the data have moved *away* from Darwin over time, not toward him — the opposite of what a true theory should do.[^3]

## Evidence
1. **Abrupt appearance, not gradual branching.** New body plans appear suddenly with no intermediates in the lower pre-Cambrian strata; Darwin himself knew this was a problem in 1859.[^1]
2. **The artifact hypothesis fails its own test.** The claim that intermediates are merely an artifact of incomplete sampling or preservation predicts we should find them with more digging — but 160+ years on, the Cambrian has become "even more explosive": more new forms known, "and yet… no more of the intermediate."[^4]
3. **Punctuated equilibrium described the pattern but supplied no mechanism.** Gould and Eldredge (late 1970s) accurately described abrupt appearance and *stasis* ("the crocodile appears… and remains"), but "never came up with a mechanism" to explain how so much change could occur so quickly.[^5]
4. **Opening a niche does not build the animal.** A meteor wiping out the dinosaurs may open an ecological niche, but "the problem is you have to build the animal" — which requires "a lot of new information," and "just opening up the niche doesn't explain the origin of the information."[^6]
5. **The same pattern in human origins.** Anthropologist John Hawks: the genus *Homo* "just appears" ~2 million years ago with no clearly transitional australopithecine; and a later "cultural Big Bang" (agriculture, cities, written language, representational art) erupts within ~40,000 years.[^7]

## Best Quotes
> "The major groups of organisms appear abruptly, without connections to those ancestral precursor forms." [^2]

> "The problem is you have to build the animal… if you wanna generate a new form of life, you have to have a lot of new information." [^6]

## LDS Connection
The Cambrian argument is a natural-theology observation, not a doctrinal one, and Latter-day Saint thought has no commitment to Darwinian gradualism that abrupt appearance would threaten. The honest caveat is that "abrupt appearance" is a *negative* point about Darwinian mechanism; the positive inference to a designer (and from there to the embodied, organizing God of the Restoration rather than a generic intelligence) is a further step the fossil pattern alone does not take. The deeper claim — that new forms require new *information* — connects to the information argument in [[DNA as Digital Code - The Information Enigma]] and the mental-cause inference in [[Design Requires a Mental Cause Not a Mechanism]].
