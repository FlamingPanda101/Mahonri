# Genesis Contains Two Independent, Originally Distinct Creation Accounts

## The Claim

The book of Genesis opens with **two** creation accounts that were originally separate compositions: the Priestly account ([[Genesis 1.1|Genesis 1:1]]–2:4a) and the older non-Priestly account ([[Genesis 2.4-7|Genesis 2:4b]]–end of Genesis 3). They are not one continuous narrative but two distinct texts, by different authors, later joined editorially. McClellan divides them at 2:4, with 2:4a closing the Priestly account and 2:4b opening the earlier account.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Majority scholarly consensus (strong).** "The majority of scholars agree that these were two independent and originally distinct creation accounts."[^1] McClellan calls it "the overwhelming scholarly consensus."[^2] Evidence rating: **strong** — represents mainstream Pentateuchal scholarship.

2. **Recent university-press commentaries (strong).** McClellan grounds the claim in four current works: [[David Carr]]'s Kohlhammer *Genesis 1–11* (2021) and *The Formation of Genesis 1–11*; [[Jan Christian Gertz]]'s *Genesis 1–11* (2023); [[Ron Hendel]]'s Anchor Yale Bible *Genesis 1–11* (2024); and [[David Petersen]]'s Old Testament Library *Genesis* (2025).[^3] Evidence rating: **strong** — peer-reviewed, recent, convergent.

3. **Internal contradictions in sequence (decisive).** The two accounts disagree on the order of creation (plants and animals relative to humanity), which is intelligible only if they are independent. See [[Sequence of Creation Differs Between Genesis 1 and 2]]. Evidence rating: **decisive** as evidence of distinctness.

4. **Distinct vocabulary (moderate).** Overlapping-but-different terminology points to different authors. See [[Vocabulary Differences Signal Two Authors]]. Evidence rating: **moderate**.

**Steelman of the single-account view:** A unitary reader can note that the final-form editor evidently *intended* the two to be read together (they are placed back-to-back with a *toledot* hinge), and that "originally independent" is a reconstruction, not a directly attested fact. McClellan's reply is that authorial unity in the final form does not erase the demonstrable contradictions in the underlying sources — the harmonization "still flatly contradicts the details of the first account."[^4]

## Best Quotes

> "The majority of scholars agree that these were two independent and originally distinct creation accounts. The one in Genesis 2:4b through the end of Genesis 3 is almost certainly the earlier of the two." [^1]

> "The overwhelming scholarly consensus is that Genesis 2:4 ... all the way through the end of Genesis 3 is a distinct originally independent account of creation that likely came long before the priestly account of creation that exists from Genesis 1:1 through Genesis 2:3 or 2:4a." [^2]
