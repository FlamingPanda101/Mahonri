# False Memory Reports Caused Documented Wrongful Convictions

## The Claim
The unreliability of memory is not a mere theory — it has produced measurable miscarriages of justice. DNA exonerations and mass confession-reversals trace large fractions of wrongful convictions to false-memory reports, vindicating skepticism of the recovered-memory testimony that drove the satanic panic.

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. 90% of studied DNA exonerations involved false-memory IDs (strong — government study)
A 1996 National Institute of Justice study of cases where "defendants were exonerated by later DNA evidence … concluded that 90% of these wrongful convictions could be traced to a specific type of false memory report … namely, positive identifications of innocent suspects as eyewitnesses" [^1].

### 2. 200+ confessions thrown out for suggestion (strong)
"More than 200 murder confessions were thrown out by courts in Cook County, Illinois. The predominant reason was that the confessions were judged to be based on unreliable memory reports which had been induced through the use of a variety of suggestive interrogation methods" [^2].

### 3. The professions responded with safeguards (moderate — institutional)
"As the house of cards fell, the American Medical Association issued a set of new recommendations to therapists … Foremost among those principles, recording interviews" — directly targeting the suggestive, unrecorded practices that generated false accounts. See [[Leading SRA Therapists Were Discredited Sued or Delicensed]] [^3].

### 4. Named real victims of the false-memory regime (strong — concretizing)
The episode lists who was actually harmed: "the McMartins and the Hadfields and the Burnhams and the Yost family, the people of Utah, the taxpayers, and the leaders of the Church of Jesus Christ … who had their names dragged through the mud." University of Utah professor [[David Raskin]] called Utah "gripped in mass hysteria … chasing a fantasy despite a lack of supporting evidence," and the harm later reached church president [[Russell M. Nelson]]. See [[The Allen Hadfield Trial as a Miscarriage of Justice]] [^4][^5].

## Steelman (Opposing View)
A defender could note that eyewitness-ID errors and coerced confessions are a *distinct* problem from therapeutic recovered memory, so importing courtroom-ID statistics to discredit clinical SRA testimony is a category slip — and that the same studies, by exonerating the innocent, show the justice system self-corrects. The rebuttal: the common mechanism across all three (eyewitness ID, interrogation-induced confession, therapy-induced recollection) is the documented malleability of human memory under suggestion, established by the same research program (e.g., Loftus) the series already cites; the NIJ and Cook County findings demonstrate, in adversarial legal settings with high stakes, that confident memory reports can be wholly false. That is exactly the vulnerability recovered-memory therapy exploited, so the analogy is structural, not rhetorical.
