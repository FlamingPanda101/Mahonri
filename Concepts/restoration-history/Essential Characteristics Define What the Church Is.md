# Essential Characteristics Define What the Church Is

## The Claim
To answer "what can and cannot change in the Church?" [[Jacob Hansen]] argues you must first identify the Church's **essential characteristics** — the philosophical features that, if removed, would make it "no longer be that thing." Just as warm blood is essential to being a mammal, certain features are essential to being "the true church of Jesus Christ"; alter those and it ceases to be what it claims to be, while non-essentials can change freely. [^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The essence/accident distinction is sound philosophy.** "What something is is defined by its essence or what in philosophy are called essential characteristics... things that if removed would cause something to no longer be that thing." The mammal/warm-blood example illustrates a real metaphysical category. *Rating: strong as philosophy; this is standard essentialism — cf. [[Reforming a Thing's Essence Is Destroying It Not Reforming It]].* [^1]
2. **Test cases sort intuitively.** If the Church "denied Christ was the savior," it would no longer be Christ's church (essential); if it dropped the dress code for missionaries, it would still be Christ's church (accidental). The hard cases (temple ceremony changes, the priesthood ban, polygamy) sit in between and require the model to adjudicate. *Rating: strong; the easy cases calibrate the method.* [^2]
3. **The proposed essential: uniquely authoritative prophets.** Hansen nominates as essential "prophets who hold unique authority to speak the truth of God and who act as the best source for revealing... the spiritual truths God wishes to convey to man." If some other source were *better*, "why wouldn't we follow that person or group?" *Rating: moderate-strong; identifies the load-bearing essential that grounds the [[The Collective Prophetic Witness|collective witness]] model.* [^3]

### Steelman (the opposing view)
A critic responds that *who decides* which characteristics are "essential" is exactly the contested point: the Church can quietly **reclassify** a former "doctrine" (e.g., the priesthood ban once defended as God's will) as a mere "practice" *after* changing it, making "essence" an after-the-fact rationalization that immunizes any change. On this view the essentialist frame is unfalsifiable boundary-drawing. See [[Religions Persist by Feeling Unchanging While Actually Changing]].

### Counter-objection
Hansen's reply (developed across the series): the **collective prophetic witness** supplies a non-arbitrary ruler — a teaching counts as core to the degree it is attested "over and over again throughout multiple dispensations," not by present convenience. Reclassification is disciplined by that cross-temporal record, so the priesthood ban (one dispensation, no scriptural basis) was always a *weak* candidate, while the nature of marriage (every dispensation) is core. See [[Doctrine vs Practice vs Culture - The Three-Tier Taxonomy]] and [[Gay Marriage Is Not Analogous to the Priesthood Ban or Polygamy]].

## Best Quotes
> "What something is is defined by its essence or what in philosophy are called essential characteristics. Essential characteristics are things that if removed would cause something to no longer be that thing. So... if an animal does not have warm blood, it can't be a mammal because to be a mammal, warm blood is an essential characteristic." [^1]

> "An essential characteristic of the Church... would be prophets who hold unique authority to speak the truth of God... If some other person or group of people was a better source for revealing God's truth for the world today, why wouldn't we follow that person or group?" [^3]
