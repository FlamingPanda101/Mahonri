# Sufficient Evidence Exists for the Seeker but Desire Distorts Belief

## The Point
Against the premise that God is *too* hidden, Jones argues that hiddenness is not total: natural theology and the historical evidence for the Resurrection arguably provide "enough evidence to support the inference" for anyone who genuinely wants to seek God — but it is "not given in a way that forces people to believe against what they desire to be true."[^1] The evidence is calibrated to the seeker, not the scoffer. So the real variable is not the *quantity* of evidence but the *posture* of the one weighing it.[^2]

## Evidence
- **A cumulative case already on the table.** "One could argue God has actually provided enough evidence in natural theology and historical evidence for the resurrection to make it reasonable to believe in God." The atheist who insists it is "too hidden" must show *why* the cumulative case fails objectively, "beyond their subjectivity on what constitutes enough evidence."[^1]
- **God may give each person what they will freely receive.** Because God knows "the thoughts in all possible futures of each individual," he "knows which individuals will freely come to know and love God" and gives them sufficient evidence to "freely accept His grace and lordship."[^3] Unequal distribution is not unfairness: "God being omniscient knows who needs more information to change and who needs less."[^4]
- **No one is condemned for ignorance.** Drawing on John 9:41 and John 15:22, Jones argues "people are not condemned on the basis of lack of information"; everyone is given a real choice in this life or "the beginning of the next." CS Lewis: "honest rejection of Christ, however mistaken, will be forgiven and healed."[^5]
- **The fairness objection cuts the wrong way.** The complaint that some get more proof than others "assumes everyone is the same and needs the same amount of information." Given omniscience, God "can accurately judge when an attempt to change someone will work and how much influence they need."[^6]

## Best Quotes
> "If anyone wants to seek Him, there is enough evidence to support the inference." [^1]

> "No one is condemned for ignorance. God knows the hearts of all and knows all who will freely surrender." [^5]

> "The objection assumes everyone is the same and needs the same amount of information. Again, God being omniscient knows who needs more information to change and who needs less." [^4]

## LDS Connection
The claim that revelation is *individually calibrated* rather than uniform fits the Restoration's emphasis on personal revelation and a God who deals with each soul according to its capacity and its honest desires. The veil ensures that the evidence available is "enough for the seeker" but never coercive — exactly the condition that distinguishes a genuine search from a demand for proof (compare [[Seeking God Versus Arbitrary Election]] and [[Faith Is Blind vs Faith Is Evidence-Based]], which likewise frames faith as evidence-responsive trust, not a leap in the dark). The pastoral payoff is large: because no one is condemned for mere ignorance and "everyone will be given a choice in this life or the beginning of the next," the LDS doctrine of post-mortal evangelization and proxy ordinances answers the *fairness* worry behind hiddenness more completely than a single-mortal-probation framework can — the apparently "hidden" can still receive the fullness later.
