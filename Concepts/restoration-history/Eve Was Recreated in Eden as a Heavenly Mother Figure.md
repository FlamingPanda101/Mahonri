# Eve Was Recreated in Eden as a Heavenly Mother Figure

#type/claim

## The Claim

Eve was not surgically built from Adam's rib out in the dust; she was *found* and *recreated in Eden* — set apart from humanity and re-formed in the image of God (or of Heavenly Mother) — making her an elevated, Heavenly-Mother-type figure rather than a derivative afterthought. [[J Richard Lambert]] argues the "rib" account is a metaphor he can "prove linguistically," and host Joselyn ([[The Woman in the Wilderness]]) adds that whereas *ha-adam* is made from the dust, Eve is created *in Eden*, in a "higher, holier place," and later titled "mother of all living."[^1]

## Supporting Evidence (steelman)

- **"Adam" is a polysemic concept, not a single man's name.** Proto-Hebrew is a "soft," conceptual language; *ha-adam* simultaneously means humanity, man, earth, and rock. So "Eve taken from Adam" is not a surgical event but the setting-apart of part of *humanity* — a sanctified group "taken out and recreated in the Garden of Eden in the image of God."[^2]
- **The verbs encode searching and finding, not surgery.** "Took" (*wayyiqach*) is used metaphorically for going out, searching, and then taking — there is action, not a "close surgical removing of the rib." The surrounding grammar is explicit: after Adam names the animals "they went out and searched for Eve and did not find her... there was not found a help meet for Adam."[^3]
- **Joseph Smith corrects it with a single word.** [[Abraham 5.14-21|Abraham 5:21]] changes one word so that Eve was in fact *found* for Adam — "not that she was made from his rib." The Restoration rendition matches what the Hebrew grammar already implies.[^4]
- **Being "taken out" = sanctification.** Lambert reads Eve's removal as the pattern of holy ones who are "set apart" and "taken out from humanity," recreated in Eden in the image of God / Heavenly Mother — then sent back with a salvific role to help Adam "awake and arise."[^5]
- **The name *Chavah* is the mother's name, conferred after the Fall.** Eve receives the name only *after* partaking; God's words are "what will you *call* her," a rhetorical question (like "where were you when I laid the stars," Job 38) that confers a new name once she has wisdom. The onomastics of *Chavah* are rooted in "life / everlasting life," which Lambert identifies as the name/title of the divine Mother — so the temple now reads it as "the name of the mother of all living."[^6]
- **The temple already teaches the elevated reading.** Lambert insists none of this is "the book of Jared": it comes "straight from the temple," where women "represent something so elevated," consistent with the recent emphasis that the temple is a "house of learning" inviting members to notice lines that depart from the traditional narrative.[^7]

## Best Quotes

> "She represents that part of humanity that now is set apart, that is taken out and recreated in the image of God or of our Heavenly Mother."[^5]

> "When it says Eve was taken from Adam's rib, we're not talking about this literal surgical event... I know 100% I can prove linguistically this is a metaphor. It is not to be taken literally."[^3]

> "Her name linguistically — the onomastics of that name — is rooted in the word for life and everlasting life, which is the name of the mother."[^6]
