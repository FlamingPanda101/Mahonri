# DNA as Digital Code - The Information Enigma

## The Point
Meyer argues that DNA stores information as a literal four-character digital code: precisely sequenced nucleotide bases function like letters in a written language or symbols in a computer program, carrying the assembly instructions for proteins.[^1] This is not merely Shannon-style mathematical improbability but *specified* (functional) information — arrangements that produce a specific effect — and in our uniform experience such information invariably traces back to a mind. No theory of undirected chemical evolution explains its origin, so intelligent design is the best explanation for the information in the first cell.[^2]

## Evidence
1. **The 1953 discovery and the sequence hypothesis.** When Watson and Crick elucidated DNA's structure, they found it stores information in a four-character digital code; Francis Crick's "sequence hypothesis" held that the chemical bases function like letters or computer symbols, so DNA has the "sequence specificity" that characterizes codes and language.[^3]
2. **Specified information, not just Shannon information.** DNA does not merely exhibit mathematical improbability (Claude Shannon's measure); it conveys *instructions* that perform functions and produce specific effects — "specified or functional information."[^4]
3. **Expert testimony that DNA is code-like.** Meyer cites Richard Dawkins ("the machine code of the genes is uncannily computer-like"), Bill Gates ("DNA is like a computer program, but far, far more advanced than any software we've ever created"), and Leroy Hood ("digital code").[^5]
4. **Chance and chemistry both fail.** There is "too much information in the cell to be explained by chance alone," and prebiotic natural selection "presuppose[s] precisely what needs explaining" — preexisting genetic information. Appealing to chemical laws is "like saying a newspaper headline might arise from the chemical attraction between ink and paper."[^6]
5. **The uniform-experience / SETI logic.** We infer minds from information all the time — archaeologists infer scribes from hieroglyphics; SETI presupposes that information in a space signal would indicate intelligence. The same logic applied to the cell's information implies an intelligent source.[^7]
6. **The information inference: a "master programmer."** Meyer presents the digital information in DNA as the *third* of three discoveries (after Big-Bang cosmology and cosmic fine-tuning) pointing to a designing mind. In our "uniform and repeated experience," whenever information is traced to its ultimate source we arrive at a mind, not a material process — so "the software program requires a programmer; the information in DNA, I argue, requires a master programmer."[^9]
7. **The Cambrian explosion as an engineering problem.** Most major animal forms "first appear in the fossil record in a geologically abrupt fashion" (~530 mya), as do other groups (winged insects, birds, flowering plants, mammals). Beyond the famous *missing ancestors*, the deeper mystery is informational: building a new biological form requires *new* information, "and same thing turns out to be true... in the biological realm" as in giving a computer a new function — yet neither chemical nor biological evolutionary theory has explained where the new information comes from.[^10] See [[The Cambrian Explosion and the Pattern of Abrupt Appearance]] and [[New Biological Form Requires New Information]].

## Best Quotes

> "Creation of information is habitually associated with conscious activity." (Henry Quastler, quoted by Meyer)[^8]

> "The software program requires a programmer. The information in DNA, I argue, requires a master programmer." [^9]

> "No one knows how life arose from a strictly materialistic chemical evolutionary process." (Richard Dawkins, quoted by Meyer)[^11]

## LDS Connection
Latter-day Saint theology is comfortable with a designed, ordered cosmos and a Creator, so an information-based design argument is a congenial natural-theology resource. The fit is broad, not specific: Meyer argues for an undefined "intelligent source" (and ultimately a transcendent God), not for the distinctive LDS picture of a God who *organizes* eternal, uncreated matter and intelligences rather than creating from nothing. See [[Creation Ex Nihilo Is Not Biblical]] and [[Uncreated Intelligences Make Free Love Possible]].
