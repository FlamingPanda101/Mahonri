# The One Is Identical to the Unmoved Mover

## The Point
Feser argues that the Neo-Platonic proof and the Aristotelian proof terminate in *the same being* reached from different starting points. [^1] Because the One must be purely actual (a partless thing can have no potential part), everything the Aristotelian proof established of the Unmoved Mover applies to it as well — and since "there cannot even in principle be more than one thing that is purely actual," the One and the Unmoved Mover "are really identical." [^1] So the simple source of composite things is not a bare abstraction but is uncaused, unique, immutable, eternal, immaterial, a mind or intellect, perfect, omnipotent, fully good, and omniscient — in short, God. [^2]

## Evidence
- The One must be a *mind*: everything is either a mind, a mental content, a material entity, or an abstract entity; it is not abstract (those are causally inert), not material (those have parts), and not a mere mental content (those depend on a mind), so it "is either a mind, or more like a mind than anything else." [^3]
- It must be *purely actual*: "if it was less than purely actual, then it would be partially potential. In that case it would have parts—an actual part and a potential part—and it has no parts." [^1]
- Identity with the Unmoved Mover: "the One and the Unmoved Mover are really identical. For both are purely actual, and… there cannot even in principle be more than one thing that is purely actual." [^1]
- Adding the principle of proportionate causality yields the full set of divine attributes — "perfect, omnipotent, fully good, and omniscient" — so "to prove the existence of an absolutely simple or noncomposite cause of things is indeed to prove the existence of God." [^4]
- This blocks the "lazy" atheist objection that even a first cause "need not be God": Feser holds he has *shown* it must be divine. [^5]

## Best Quotes
> "the One and the Unmoved Mover are really identical. For both are purely actual… there cannot even in principle be more than one thing that is purely actual." [^1]

> "to prove the existence of an absolutely simple or noncomposite cause of things is indeed to prove the existence of God." [^4]

## LDS Connection
Against atheism, the convergence is rhetorically strong: two independent lines of reasoning land on one and the same ultimate reality, which Feser argues must already possess the classical divine attributes — defeating the "even if there's a first cause, why call it God?" dodge. Latter-day Saints can grant the anti-atheist force while flagging that the *destination* is the philosophers' God, not the Father of the Restoration. The identification of God with a "purely actual," partless Unmoved Mover is exactly the Hellenizing move that, on the LDS reading, reshaped the biblical Father into the immutable Absolute of the creeds — see [[Unmoved Mover Reshaped God the Father]]. Restoration theology instead worships an embodied, dynamic Father (D&C 130:22; see [[God Was Once a Man]] and [[Divine Simplicity - God Has No Parts of Any Kind]]) who acts within co-eternal law and matter rather than as creator-from-nothing — see [[God Is Constrained by Eternal Law Not Unconstrained]] and [[Classical Theism Cannot Be Reconciled With the Bible]].
