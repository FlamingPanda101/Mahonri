# Great Apostasy Is Not a Scriptural Term in LDS Standard Works

## The Point
Combs argues that Latter-day Saints should exercise "greater care in discussing a great apostasy — a term that does not appear in our standard works." The Restoration, properly stated, does not depend on proving other churches apostate. The Doctrine and Covenants never even speaks of a "restored church"; the phrase is shorthand for specific restorations (priesthood keys, covenants, gathering of Israel, binding of dispensations).

## Evidence
1. **The term is extra-canonical.** "We acknowledge the need for greater care in discussing a great apostasy — a term that does not appear in our standard works." [^1]
2. **"Restored church" is also unscriptural.** Combs notes (citing Patrick Mason, *Restoration*) that "the Doctrine and Covenants never speaks explicitly of a restored church." The word should be understood as shorthand for the restorations the D&C *does* address: priesthood power, authority and keys for covenants, establishing Zion, gathering scattered Israel on both sides of the veil, and binding all dispensations for the Second Coming. [^2]
3. **The Church is not "version 2.0."** "The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints is not merely version 2.0 of the Church established in the days of Jesus Christ's ancient apostles." It was insufficient for John the Baptist, or even Peter, James, and John, alone to grant authority; figures from all dispensations (Adam, Abraham, Moses, Elijah, Elias, Moroni) came, and ultimately the Father and the Son appeared. The dispensation of the fulness of times "necessarily includes 'those things which never have been revealed from the foundation of the world'" (D&C 128:18). [^3]
4. **Joseph Smith's "corrupt" statements are limited.** The Joseph Smith History "abomination/corrupt professors" language can be read narrowly as "referring to those ministers … with which Joseph Smith was involved" (per Robert Millet and Elder William Grant Bangerter). Joseph listed "designing and corrupt priests" as only one of three explanations for biblical errors. [^4]
5. **"Only true and living church" reread.** Just as Joseph called Emma his "one true and living friend on Earth" without implying she had no other friends, D&C 1:30 does not mean God ignores or fails to inspire other churches. Elder Ezra Taft Benson (quoting Orson F. Whitney): "God is using more than one people for the accomplishment of His great and marvelous work." [^5]

## Best Quotes
> "We acknowledge the need for greater care in discussing a great apostasy — a term that does not appear in our standard works." [^1]

> "The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints is not merely version 2.0 of the Church established in the days of Jesus Christ's ancient apostles." [^3]

## LDS Connection
The whole concept is internal to LDS self-understanding: it reframes the Restoration as expansive and additive rather than as a mere replacement for a totally fallen Church, defusing the anti-Catholic and triumphalist reflexes the tradition inherited from Protestantism. Connects to [[The Gathering of Truths from All Churches]] and [[A New Latter-day Saint Approach to Ancient Christians]].
