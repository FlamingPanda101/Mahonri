# The Accusation Wheel Self-Recruits Accusers Through Therapy

## The Claim
The Marion Smith / ISAT episode did not grow because abuse was discovered; it grew because the *therapy apparatus itself* recruited and multiplied accusers in a feedback loop. [[Marion Smith]] co-founded a treatment center (ISAT), funneled her own family into [[Barbara Snow]]'s interviews, accepted disclosures from pre-verbal children, and watched the count of accused adults and child witnesses inflate one another — "the accusation wheel spun and spun." The mechanism is structural: a self-selecting clinical pipeline manufactures the very allegations it then treats.

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. The institution was built by the accuser, then fed by the accuser (strong)
In 1982 Marion Smith co-founded the Intermountain Sexual Abuse Treatment Center (ISAT) and "trained alongside therapists in the cutting edge of national debate," right after *Michelle Remembers* but before the McMartin and Hadfield trials. When her grandchildren's babysitter was accused, Smith "brought her own grandchildren to ISAT for treatment from Dr. Barbara Snow" — making herself the supplier of the clinic's subjects [^1].

### 2. Pre-verbal children were treated as witnesses (strong)
Smith "took the word of the children immediately, including a 9-month-old baby who couldn't speak and a 2-year-old who mentioned bad people. Smith just knew that it must mean something and she knew exactly what it must mean." Confirmation bias supplied the content the children could not [^2].

### 3. The count of accused and accusers inflated each other (strong — the wheel)
"As the number of adults accused increased and the number of children to interview increased, then the number of children increased and the number of adults increased." Snow then "pointed at the wealthy president of a local gas company," grocery-chain president [[Richard Miles]], "the local bishop … of course," and finally [[Brenda Miles]], daughter of newly-ordained apostle [[Russell M. Nelson]] [^3]. See [[Barbara Snows Interview Methods Manufactured Abuse Allegations]].

### 4. The independent control case broke the loop (strong)
When the local bishop urged some parents to seek a *second opinion* from more objective therapists, "none of the children revealed abuse," and the bishop's own son "completely recanted his coerced accusation." Removing the ISAT/Snow pipeline removed the allegations — the signature of an interviewer-generated artifact (see [[Identical Cross-Case Allegations Point to the Interviewer Not the Crime]]) [^4].

## Steelman (Opposing View)
A defender of the investigations would argue that abuse rings *do* surface one disclosure at a time, that an expanding victim count can reflect a real network rather than contamination, and that therapists who specialize in abuse will naturally see clustered cases. Granted — but the diagnostic question is whether the allegations track an *external* crime or the *internal* dynamics of one clinic. Here they collapsed the moment children were interviewed *outside* ISAT/Snow, the seed witnesses were a 9-month-old and a 2-year-old, and the accuser personally recruited the subjects. That pattern fits interviewer-driven contamination, not a discovered ring.

## Counter-objection: "the bishop was protecting his community"
[[Paul Whitehead]] reframed exactly this kind of community resistance as proof of conspiracy ("to protect one is to protect all"). That move is unfalsifiable — it converts every exculpatory result into inculpatory evidence. See [[Community Support Reinterpreted as Conspiracy]] and [[No Evidence the LDS Church Suppressed the Investigations]].
