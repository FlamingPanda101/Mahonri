# Information Only Comes from Minds - Turek

## The Point
Turek argues that in all of our uniform experience, information-rich messages come only from minds, never from undirected natural forces.[^1] A name written in the sky, "JOHN LOVES MARY" in the sand, or the inscription on the Rosetta Stone is immediately recognized as the product of intelligence, because natural laws produce repetitive order (crystals, vortices) but never the unpredictable, specified sequences that characterize a code or text.[^2] DNA is exactly such a code — over three billion characters long — so the same inference that detects a mind behind a sky-message should detect a Mind behind the cell.[^3]

## Evidence
1. **The sky-message thought experiment.** Even atheists Lawrence Krauss and "Roger" concede that a message like "I AM HERE" rearranged in the stars, or "HEY ROGER, THIS IS GOD!" written in the sky, would be evidence for God — because no one would attribute an information-rich message to "chance" cloud material or "cloud evolution." Any such message "had to be the product of intelligence."[^1]
2. **Order vs. information.** Physical and chemical laws act in "highly predictable and regular" ways. Gravity makes snow fall but "can't write your name and address in the snow"; chemistry explains why ink binds to paper but not the order of the letters that carry the message. Information requires a different kind of cause than law.[^2]
3. **The genome is a literal message.** "Your genome is a message like your name and address, except it's written in DNA and it's over three billion characters long," and the letters must be in the right order for you to survive.[^3] Physics and chemistry no more determine that order than they determine the order of English letters in a sentence — "Minds determine messages and codes; natural forces do not."[^4]
4. **Not an analogy but an identity.** Information scientist Hubert Yockey (a Manhattan Project veteran) insisted the comparison between the genetic alphabet and written language is "not reasoning by analogy" — "the treatment is mathematically identical." If a short English message requires a composer, so does a genetic message thousands of books long.[^5]
5. **Scale rebuts "simple first life."** Dawkins concedes a single-celled amoeba carries as much information in its DNA as 1,000 *Encyclopaedia Britannicas*. Believing that arose unguided is "like believing that an entire bookstore resulted from an explosion in a printing shop."[^6]

## Best Quotes
> "In all our experience, the only forces we see creating informational messages are intelligent minds. Natural forces never do it."[^1]

> "Minds determine messages and codes; natural forces do not."[^4]

## LDS Connection
The design inference from information is congenial to Latter-day Saint natural theology, which is comfortable with a designed, ordered cosmos and a Creator behind life. The fit is real but broad: Turek's argument concludes only that the cause is "intelligent and has purpose," not the distinctively LDS picture of a God who *organizes* eternal, uncreated matter and intelligences rather than creating from nothing (see [[Intelligence Is Uncreated and Eternally Has Agency]]). Turek expressly notes that biology alone yields only an intelligent, purposeful cause and that the further step to a *theistic* God requires combining it with cosmology, morality, and philosophy — a humility about the limits of the inference that LDS readers can affirm.[^7] For Meyer's fuller scientific case, see [[DNA as Digital Code - The Information Enigma]].
