# Church / Sect / Cult Distinction

## The Claim
Sociology classifies religious organizations along a continuum — **church → sect → cult** — that is descriptive, not evaluative, and that groups move along over time.

## Supporting Evidence
- **Church**: large, inclusive, people are *born* into it, hierarchical, professionalized leadership, doctrine-and-ritual focused *(scholar)* [^1].
- **Sect**: breaks away from a church via schism (e.g. Protestantism from Rome), led by charismatic figures, emphasizes piety and strong behavioral demands, **exclusive**, grows by conversion *(scholar)* [^2].
- **Cult**: even more personal, charismatic, and loosely organized (the metaphysical/new-age end) *(scholar)* [^3].
- The typology comes from [[Max Weber]] and [[Ernst Troeltsch]]; [[H. Richard Niebuhr]] showed **sects evolve into churches** across generations *(canonical scholarship)* [^4].

## Best Quote
> "Sects are groups that break free of churches. They engage in what's called schisms... usually under the leadership of some kind of charismatic strong leader... they end up being exclusive." — [[Lorne Dawson]] [^2]
