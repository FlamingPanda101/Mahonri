# Swedenborg's Parallels to the NDE

## The Point
[[Emanuel Swedenborg]] (1688-1772), a renowned scientist who later reported communication with spiritual beings, wrote vivid descriptions of life after death that correlate "amazingly" with modern near-death reports [^1]. He describes the dying as separated from the body but not annihilated, still in a body resembling the physical, met by "angels" and departed friends, communicating by a direct transfer of thought, and shown a perfect, inescapable memory of every deed in "a light as clear as day" [^2]. Crucially he records a detail — that the newly dead do not at first realize they are dead — absent from the Bible and Plato but present in the Tibetan text he could not have read [^3].

## Evidence
1. **Death as passage, not extinction.** "Still man does not die, but is only separated from the corporeal part... Man, when he dies, only passes from one world into another." Swedenborg says he himself was drawn into "the state of the dying" and felt "a drawing and... pulling of... mind, thus of my spirit, from the body" [^1].
2. **Met by angels.** He encounters beings he identifies as "angels," who inquire whether he is prepared to die and "wished to keep my mind in that thought" [^4].
3. **Communication by thought.** Spirits converse in a "universal language" that is a direct transfer of thought, so "there is no possibility of misunderstanding"; an angel's speech "flows first into the man's thought" and is heard by him alone [^4].
4. **Not realizing one is dead.** "The first state of man after death is similar to his state in the world... he knows no otherwise than that he is still in the world" — paralleling [[NDE - Hearing the News of One's Own Death]] [^4].
5. **Fuller faculties; meeting the known dead.** All faculties are "in a more perfect state"; time and space no longer pose obstacles; the recently departed is "recognized by his friends" who instruct him about eternal life — see [[NDE - Meeting Others]] [^5].
6. **The total life review.** The "interior memory" has "inscribed in it all the particular things... from his earliest infancy to extreme old age," made manifest "in a light as clear as day," so that "there is nothing so concealed in the world that it is not manifested after death"; he also glimpsed the "light of the Lord," a light "of truth and of understanding" [^6].

## Best Quotes
> "Still man does not die, but is only separated from the corporeal part which was of use to him in the world... Man, when he dies, only passes from one world into another." [^1]

> "there is nothing so concealed in the world that it is not manifested after death... as if seen in effigy, when the spirit is viewed in the light of heaven." [^6]

## LDS Connection
Swedenborg's deceased who is met and "instructed by their friends concerning the state of eternal life" closely mirrors the LDS spirit world, where the righteous dead teach the newly arrived (D&C 138). His "light of the Lord... of truth and of understanding" parallels the scriptural light of Christ that "giveth light to every man" (D&C 88:7-13), and his inescapable, total memory parallels the bright recollection at judgment (Alma 11:43). His independent attestation reinforces [[NDE Cross-Cultural Convergence of Afterlife Testimony]].
