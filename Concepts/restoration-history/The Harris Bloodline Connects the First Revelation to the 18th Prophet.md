# The Harris Bloodline Connects the First Revelation to the 18th Prophet

## The Claim
[[Don Bradley]] argues that the line running from the Restoration's earliest revelations to the Church's 18th president, [[Dallin H. Oaks]], is one unbroken **family thread** that "would be easy to miss if we don't connect the dots." The decoded clue is the middle initial: *Dallin Harris Oaks*. His mother was Stella Harris Oaks; her great-grandfather was [[Emer Harris]] of upstate New York, whose younger brother was [[Martin Harris]] — the Book of Mormon's chief financial witness. So the prophet succeeding in 2025 descends directly from the family that funded and witnessed the Book of Mormon, a continuity Bradley reads as providential rather than coincidental.

## Supporting Evidence
1. **The genealogy is exact.** "The name is Dallin Harris Oaks… President Oaks' mother was Stella Harris Oaks… her great-grandfather was a guy in upstate New York named Emer Harris… Emer had a younger brother named Martin Harris." Emer Harris is Oaks's second great-grandfather; the third great-grandparents are Nathan and Rhoda Harris, devout Quakers with several sons including Emer and Martin.[^1]
2. **The chain is mechanistic, not merely sentimental.** Martin's funding brought the Harris family into the Church, "and therefore, by extension, now gives us our 18th prophet." Joseph Smith's first interpreter revelation → Martin's money → the published Book of Mormon → the Harris family's conversion → Dallin Harris Oaks. (See [[Joseph Smiths First Interpreter Revelation Pointed to Martin Harris]].)[^2]
3. **The thread is a witness-heritage, not just a bloodline.** Martin, as one of the Three Witnesses, helped *choose* the original Quorum of the Twelve in 1835; in 1984 his brother's great-great-grandson joined that same quorum — "there's this continuity across time." Bradley frames the apostolic office as definitionally a witness of Christ, the same role Martin filled (Oaks, "Witnesses of Christ," 1990).[^3]
4. **A calendar "wink."** Bradley notes that the Feast of Tabernacles in 2025 begins October 6 — the same date (per Lucy Mack Smith) on which Joseph first looked into the interpreters and saw Martin Harris — right as Oaks becomes president. Stephen Jones and Bradley concede this is interpretive ("people who are watching this could be thinking, these guys are crazy"), calling it an "intentional coincidence," a wink rather than proof.[^4]
5. **Steelman of the skeptic.** A critic answers that selective ancestry can be drawn from almost any prophet, and a recurring lunisolar feast will inevitably realign with a given solar date; finding meaning is hindsight pattern-matching. Bradley half-grants this — he files the calendar point as a faith-affirming "wink," not evidence — but the load-bearing claim (Oaks genuinely descends from the Harris witnesses) is a verifiable genealogical fact, independent of the typology. The note's value lies in the documented lineage; the providential gloss is offered, by the speakers' own framing, as devotional.

## Best Quotes

> "The name is Dallin Harris Oaks. Harris is a familiar name to people in the Restoration… Emer had a younger brother named Martin Harris… It's that Martin Harris."[^1]

> "It brings the family, the Harris family, into the Church, and therefore, by extension, now gives us our 18th prophet… Dallin Harris Oaks. That's another result of that very first revelation."[^2]

> "There's this continuity across time that would be easy to miss if we don't connect the dots and piece the larger story together."[^3]
