# The No-Alibi Date as Manufactured Evidence

## The Claim
The specific date assigned to Hadfield's alleged abuse — April 9, 1986 — was chosen by [[Barbara Snow]] precisely because it was the one evening she knew he could not produce an alibi, revealing the accusation as constructed rather than recalled.

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. Snow supplied the date herself (strong)
When the prosecution was pressed on when the abuse occurred, "Dr. Snow readily provided the perfect setting, the evening of April 9th, 1986" [^1].

### 2. The date was the night she phoned him (strong — the tell)
April 9, 1986 was the very evening Snow had "talked at length with Mr. Hadfield on the phone" — meaning she knew, better than anyone, that he had no other alibi for those hours. "This was a time she was positive Hadfield had no other alibi" [^2].

## Steelman (Opposing View)
One could argue Snow simply correlated the children's disclosures with a date she happened to remember, and that knowing a defendant's whereabouts is not the same as fabricating a crime. But the coincidence is damning: of all possible dates, the accusation landed exactly on the window the accuser herself had occupied the defendant's time — the structure of a frame, not a recovered memory.

## Best Quotes
> "Dr. Snow readily provided the perfect setting, the evening of April 9th, 1986, when she had talked at length with Mr. Hadfield on the phone. This was a time she was positive Hadfield had no other alibi." [^2]
