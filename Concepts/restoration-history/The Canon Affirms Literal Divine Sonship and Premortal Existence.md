# The Canon Affirms Literal Divine Sonship and Premortal Existence

## The Claim
Against the "God Loves Mormons" position that humans are God's children only by *adoption* (not literally) and did not premortally exist, [[Cardon Ellis]], [[Jonah Barnes]], and [[Oak Norton]] mount the positive case: the canon repeatedly calls God the literal **Father of spirits** and humans His **offspring/children**, and at least one prophet is addressed as known to God *before* the womb — and the apocrypha independently describe premortal spirits, corroborating the doctrine Joseph Smith restored. The panel's polemical edge is that the creedal denial makes God cruel and alien ("you're not even the same species"), whereas the restored reading — "you are a child of God" — is the missionaries' first message.[^1][^2][^3]

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. The divine-sonship texts are explicit — rate: strong, scriptural
The stack: [[Hebrews 12.9|Hebrews 12:9]] — "the **Father of spirits**" (contrasted with "fathers of our flesh"); [[Acts 17.28-29|Acts 17:28-29]] — "we are the **offspring of God**" (Paul at Mars Hill); [[Romans 8.16|Romans 8:16-17]] — "the Spirit… beareth witness… that we are the **children of God**… joint-heirs with Christ."[^2] These are not stray metaphors but a consistent canonical idiom of literal sonship and shared inheritance. *Strong: the "joint-heirs" and "Father of spirits" language is hard to reduce to mere adoption.*

### 2. A premortal-knowledge text — rate: moderate
[[Jeremiah 1.5|Jeremiah 1:5]] — "**Before I formed thee in the belly I knew thee**… and I ordained thee a prophet" — reads naturally as a premortal interaction; 1 Peter 1 says Christ was foreordained "before the foundation of the world."[^3] *Moderate: "foreknew/foreordained" is the standard counter-reading, but the verse at least comports with premortal existence.*

### 3. The apocrypha corroborate premortal spirits — rate: interesting, extra-canonical
[[Jonah Barnes]] runs a chain: the **Slavonic Palea** (the "light" of day one = angels/spirits "created of fire and light"); **3 Enoch** (the souls of the righteous in the chamber of creation before the world); **2 Baruch 21:6** ("the countless holy beings which you did make from the beginning"); the **Apocalypse of Abraham** (Abraham shown spirits "born before your day and afterwards"), paralleling [[Abraham 3.22-23|Abraham 3]]'s "noble and great ones."[^3] *Interesting as a "player three" — multiple independent texts attest premortal spirits — but these are non-canonical and of varied date.*

### 4. The theological cost of denial — rate: rhetorical
The panel argues the adoption-only view yields a God who "demands you call him Father" while denying you are his child — distant, Hellenistic, and at odds with the canon's family language ("co-participants in his glory," "thrones with him").[^1] This is framing, not proof, but it sharpens the contrast the doctrine is meant to resolve. See [[Every Dispensation Opens With God Declaring Divine Sonship]].

## Steelman of the Opposing View
The evangelical reply is well developed: (a) **"offspring/children of God" is covenantal/adoptive, not ontological** — [[Acts 17.28-29|Acts 17:28]] quotes a *pagan poet* (Aratus) to build a bridge, and Paul elsewhere makes sonship an *adoption* ("the Spirit of **adoption**," Rom 8:15; Gal 4:5), i.e., a status granted in Christ, not a pre-existing kinship; (b) **"Father of spirits"** (Heb 12:9) most plausibly means God as Creator/origin of human spirits, fully compatible with creationism/traducianism and not requiring premortal personal existence; (c) **Jeremiah 1:5** is divine *foreknowledge/foreordination* (God knowing the not-yet-existent), the same idiom as Eph 1:4, not a memory of a pre-existent Jeremiah. On this view the LDS reading over-literalizes family metaphors and a foreknowledge text. A Latter-day Saint reply (in the episode and the vault): the adoption language is real but does not *exclude* a prior literal sonship (one can be a child by nature and *re*-adopted into covenant inheritance); "joint-heirs with Christ" and "partakers of the divine nature" (2 Pet 1:4) press past mere adoption toward genuine deification (cf. [[Spirit Birth and Intelligences]]); and the *convergence* of the sonship texts, the foreordination texts, the apocryphal premortal-spirit material, and the early-Christian belief later purged (see [[Pre-existence Was a Mainstream Early-Christian Belief Later Purged]]) makes premortal divine sonship a coherent reading the canon invites rather than forbids. The defensive companion note clears the contrary prooftexts: [[The Pre-Existence Prooftexts Against It Are Misread Metaphors]].

## LDS Connection
This is the *positive* half of the vault's pre-existence apologetic (the defensive half being [[The Pre-Existence Prooftexts Against It Are Misread Metaphors]]). It connects the canonical sonship/inheritance language to the restored doctrines of [[Spirit Birth and Intelligences]] and [[God Found Spirits He Did Not Create - Book of Abraham Solve]], and to the historical claim that premortality was an early-Christian belief later purged. The episode's closing irony — that the evangelical "faith alone" critique collides with [[James 2.24|James 2:24]] — is treated in the existing note [[Faith Alone Appears Once in Scripture and Is the Verse That Denies It]]. Honest takeaway: the sonship/joint-heir texts genuinely strain a pure adoption-only reading, but each can be given an adoptive/foreknowledge gloss, so the case is cumulative and interpretive rather than a knock-down proof; the apocryphal chain is corroborative color, not canon.
