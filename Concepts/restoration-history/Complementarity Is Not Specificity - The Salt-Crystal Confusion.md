# Complementarity Is Not Specificity - The Salt-Crystal Confusion

## The Point
The Veritasium cartoon shows blobs that "find their complements" — red attracts green, purple attracts yellow — snapping together to drive replication.[^1] Meyer warns this smuggles in a category mistake. It implies life's molecules are highly *ordered* by chemical attraction, like a crystal of salt where Na+ inevitably attracts Cl-. But the thing that makes DNA carry information is *not* attraction. Yes, DNA's two strands pair complementarily (A with T, C with G), but **along the message-bearing axis there are no chemical forces that fix the sequence** — the bases are highly irregular yet specified to function. Life depends on a *specificity of arrangement* (information), not a mere *complementarity of pairing* (order). The cartoon's "things find their opposites" picture quietly erases the very feature that needs explaining.[^2]

## Evidence
1. **The cartoon's complementarity mechanism.** In the animation each blob "always attracts" a specific partner, and these opposites "snap into position" to produce replication.[^1]
2. **Order is not information.** "That implies that everything is highly ordered in biological chemistry... in the sense of like a crystal of salt. The Na attracts Cl."[^3]
3. **Cross-strand pairing is real — but limited.** "It is true that along the DNA molecule there's a complementary strand and A's attach to T's and C's to G's."[^4]
4. **The information axis is not chemically determined.** "Along the message-bearing axis, there are no chemical forces that determine the sequential arrangement, and instead they're highly irregular but quite specified to function."[^2]
5. **Specificity, not complementarity, is what life needs.** "It's not simply a complementarity of pairing but rather a specificity of arrangement that is information-bearing that's absolutely necessary to life" — a requirement "completely elided" in the video.[^5] (See the fuller treatment of DNA as a sequence-specific code in [[DNA as Digital Code - The Information Enigma]] and [[New Biological Form Requires New Information]].)

## Best Quotes
> "Along the message-bearing axis, there are no chemical forces that determine the sequential arrangement... they're highly irregular but quite specified to function." [^2]

> "You need specificity in chemistry to build anything." [^6]

## LDS Connection
The distinction between mere order and information-bearing specificity is a natural-theology resource that fits the Latter-day Saint picture of a God who *organizes* eternal, uncreated matter into functional, specified forms — as opposed to producing order by brute chemical necessity or by creation *ex nihilo*. Crystalline order arises from chemistry alone; specified, functional sequence does not, and in our uniform experience it traces to a mind. The inference lands on a generic designing intelligence, which an LDS reader can affirm while leaving the biological mechanism open. See [[The RNA Replicator Smuggles In the Designer]].
