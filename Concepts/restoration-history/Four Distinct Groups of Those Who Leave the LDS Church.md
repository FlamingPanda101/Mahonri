# Four Distinct Groups of Those Who Leave the LDS Church

#type/claim

## The Claim
Those who leave the Church are not a monolith. Analysis of [[Ryan Burge]]'s 100,000+ "spiritual seismology" dataset identifies **four distinct groups** of former Latter-day Saints, the majority of whom remain favorable toward the Church or toward religion generally; only a small, "loud" minority are antagonistic.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Faith keepers / favorable (~50%) (Evidence: strong, large sample).** About half "were still pretty favorable towards Latter-day Saints, still felt religion, spirituality were important." Their most common reason for leaving was **"they moved or other priorities"** — relocation followed by failure to re-integrate, not doctrinal rejection. "Many of them said they would go back to church simply if they were invited by a friend."[^2]

2. **Distant (~40%) (Evidence: strong).** A second group "really don't feel that religion is important," are "not particularly warm towards Latter-day Saints," and "most of them left because of belief."[^3]

3. **Faith rejectors (~10%) (Evidence: strong).** A final 10% for whom "religion and spirituality held no place whatsoever" — "they didn't believe in Jesus Christ, they thought that religion was bad for society," leaving primarily over belief. These are the "pretty antagonistic" cohort.[^4]

4. **The visibility bias mechanism (Evidence: plausible, observational).** The antagonistic 10% are disproportionately loud online: "it's a loud 10%." This produces a sampling illusion in which "we see somebody online and we just think, 'Oh, everybody's like that,'" when most leavers still like religion and Latter-day Saints.[^5]

**Steelman of the opposing view:** Self-reported "favorability" and "would return if invited" are notoriously weak predictors of actual behavior; stated willingness rarely converts to attendance. The four-group taxonomy is generated by the researchers' clustering choices and could be drawn differently. And "left because of belief" (the cited reason for ~50% of leavers across the distant + rejector groups) is precisely the doctrinally serious category the framing tends to minimize.

## Best Quotes

> "We have found four distinct groups of individuals. And 50% of them actually were still pretty favorable towards Latter-day Saints."[^1]

> "The reason most commonly cited for them leaving was they moved or other priorities."[^2]

> "There's another 10% that we refer to as faith rejectors, where religion and spirituality held no place whatsoever in their lives."[^4]
