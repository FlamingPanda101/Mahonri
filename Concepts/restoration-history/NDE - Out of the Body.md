# NDE - Out of the Body

## The Point
After the dark tunnel, the dying person often finds himself **outside his physical body**, viewing it from a distance "as though a spectator" [^1]. He typically watches the resuscitation attempt, sees and hears the medical staff accurately, yet cannot make them perceive him [^1][^2]. The persistence of clear, ordered perception — and reactions of confusion, concern, or strange detachment toward one's own body — is among the most arresting and (where details are verifiable) evidentially suggestive elements of the NDE.

## Evidence
1. A drowning teenager felt himself "away from my body... in space by myself," watching his own form bob in the water "three or four feet away," still feeling he had "an entire body form" [^1].
2. A cardiac patient floated to the ceiling, saw nurses and her doctor rush in, watched mouth-to-mouth resuscitation, and noticed precise details — a nurse's short haircut, her body jolting as the defibrillator fired [^1].
3. An accident victim floated "about five feet above the street," saw his friend climb from the wreck in shock, and saw his own twisted, bloodied body being extricated [^2].
4. Veridical accuracy: such reports "accord quite well with what the doctors and others present remember" (see [[NDE - Hearing the News of One's Own Death]]) [^2].
5. Varied emotional responses: a desperate desire to re-enter the body; fear; or serene detachment ("I had no feelings for it whatsoever... I knew it was my body but I had no feelings for it") [^2].
6. Realization of death dawns slowly and with shock — "Oh, I'm dead! How lovely!" / "This must be what they call 'death'" [^2].

## Best Quotes
> "I watched them reviving me from up there! My body was lying down there stretched out on the bed, in plain view, and they were all standing around it.... As I saw them below beating on my chest and rubbing my arms and legs, I thought, 'Why are they going to so much trouble? I'm just fine now.'" [^1]

## LDS Connection
The out-of-body episode is a near-experimental witness to LDS anthropology: the conscious self is a **spirit** that can exist and perceive apart from the physical body (see [[NDE - The Spiritual Body]]). Alma 40 teaches that at death "the spirits of all men... are taken home to that God who gave them life," and D&C 138 depicts the disembodied spirit as fully alert and active — exactly the continuity of mind these accounts describe.
