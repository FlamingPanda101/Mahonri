# The Pastor Misattributed the Anarthrous Reading to the Joseph Smith Translation

## The Claim
In the reacted-to clip, the pastor (Nick Jones) recites the Greek of [[John 1.1|John 1:1]] — *en archē ēn ho logos…* — and grants that the **Joseph Smith Translation "got the translation right,"** taking the JST to read "the word was divine," which (he says) matches the anarthrous-*theos* grammar. The hosts seize on this as a **self-refuting move**: the actual JST of John 1:1 reads nothing like that. It reads, "In the beginning was the gospel preached through the Son, and the gospel was the word, and the word was with the Son, and the Son was with God, and the Son was of God." [^1] The pastor therefore (a) **invented a JST that does not exist**, and (b) by his own appeal to [[Daniel Wallace]]'s grammar, conceded the very anarthrous reading — "what God was, the Word was," or "the Word was divine" — that Latter-day Saints and academic grammarians defend, while a Trinitarian "library = the word" gloss does not survive it. The hosts read this as proof the pastor "doesn't know what he's talking about" precisely where he sounds most authoritative. [^2]

## Supporting Evidence (steelman of the pastor)
1. **The pastor's Greek was substantially correct.** He cites [[Daniel Wallace]]'s *Greek Grammar Beyond the Basics*: because the predicate *theos* in 1:1c is **anarthrous** (no definite article) and pre-verbal, it is qualitative — "the best possible translation… what God was, the Word was, or the Word was divine." *That much is the scholarly consensus* and the pastor stated it accurately (see [[John 1-1 - Qualitative Theos (And the Word Was God)]]). [^2]
2. **His rhetorical aim was coherent.** The pastor's broader point — that *the Word* (Logos) in John 1:1 is **Christ, not the Bible** — is exegetically sound, and the hosts agree with it; they note he "knows the word of God in John 1:1 is Jesus Christ, not the Bible," which is why he would not correct the on-camera believer who said *the word = the Bible*. *His Christological reading of John 1:1 is right.* [^3]
3. **Where the steelman breaks.** The pastor over-reached by attributing a specific *rendering* to the JST from memory — admitting he had only "glanced at" it — and got it exactly backwards. The JST does not tighten John 1:1 toward "divine"; it **rewrites the verse entirely** around the gospel preached through the Son. Crediting Joseph Smith with a "correct" anarthrous translation he never produced collapses the pastor's authority on the spot. *Rating: a decisive own-goal* of the sort catalogued in [[Apologist Misremembers and Hallucinates Bible Passages]]. [^1]
4. **The deeper irony.** If the anarthrous datum is the standard the pastor invokes, it is the *Latter-day Saint / academic* subordinate-divinity reading (see [[John 1-1c Anarthrous Theos and Subordinate Divinity]]) that it best supports, not the consubstantial-identity reading he needs. He conceded the grammar that undercuts his own Trinitarian conclusion. [^2]
