# The Tibetan Book of the Dead and the NDE

## The Point
Moody calls the correspondence between the early stages of death in the Tibetan Book of the Dead and modern near-death reports "nothing short of fantastic" [^1]. Compiled from the teachings of Tibetan sages and written down around the eighth century A.D., the book treats dying as a *skill* and was read to the dying to guide them through each stage [^2]. Its sequence — leaving the body, a void with roaring noises and grey light, a non-material "shining" body, restored senses, meeting other beings, a "clear light," and a life-reflecting "mirror" — tracks the NDE element by element [^3].

## Evidence
1. **Leaving the body into a void.** The soul departs the body, enters a "swoon," and finds itself in a void (not physical) where consciousness persists, hearing "roaring, thundering, and whistling noises, like the wind," amid a "grey, misty illumination" [^3].
2. **Unseen by the living; not yet aware of death.** He sees and hears relatives mourning and preparing the funeral, but they cannot hear or see him; confused, he asks whether he is dead, then feels regret and lingers near familiar places [^3].
3. **The "shining" body.** He is still in a body — the "shining" body — of no material substance: he passes through rocks, walls, mountains; travel is instantaneous to wherever he wishes; mind is lucid, senses keener; if formerly blind, deaf, or crippled, all powers are "restored and intensified" [^3].
4. **Other beings and the clear light.** He may meet other beings in the same kind of body and "a clear or pure light"; the dying are counseled to feel "only love and compassion towards others" as they approach it — paralleling the NDE [[NDE - The Being of Light|being of light]] [^3].
5. **Peace and the mirror of judgment.** He feels immense peace and contentment, and a "mirror" reflects his "entire life, all deeds both good and bad" for him and his judges to see — "lying about one's life is impossible" [^4].

## Best Quotes
> The correspondence "is nothing short of fantastic." [^1]

> a "mirror" in which "his entire life, all deeds both good and bad, are reflected... there can be no misrepresentation; lying about one's life is impossible." [^4]

## LDS Connection
The "shining" body that passes through matter yet has every sense "restored and intensified" resonates with the LDS spirit body and with the restorative logic of the resurrection, in which "every limb and joint shall be restored" (Alma 40:23). The unbiased life-"mirror" in which deceit is impossible parallels the LDS bright recollection of all our deeds at judgment (Alma 11:43; 2 Ne. 9:14). That an ancient, isolated tradition independently records these specifics strengthens [[NDE Cross-Cultural Convergence of Afterlife Testimony]], consonant with a universal plan of salvation witnessed across cultures.
