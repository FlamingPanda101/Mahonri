# Immigrants Perceive the UK as a Christian Country

## The Claim

[[Glenn Scrivener]]: outsiders perceive Britain's Christian character that natives miss. "When everyone else comes to the UK, it's like — well, I've come to a Christian country, haven't I?" He cites this as evidence the "air we breathe" is real but invisible to those who breathe it.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The outsider's vantage.** A devout Muslim, a Chinese visitor, or [[Ayaan Hirsi Ali]] (who "thought the Netherlands was a Christian country") sees Western secular liberalism as a recognizably *Christian* (even "decadent Christian") formation, because they lack the Western "separation of church and state" and "the secular" as native categories.[^1] *(Plausible — the framing genuinely differs by cultural starting point; a society defining itself in "religionless terms" is itself unusual.)*

2. **Devout Muslims read the West as decadent Christianity.** Scrivener: they "look into the West and think, ah, this is what Christianity is — it has become so decadent, so self-obsessed." The *form* is still legibly post-Christian to them.[^2] *(Interesting — even the critique presupposes a Christian baseline.)*

3. **Steelman of O'Connor — this is anecdotal and unverifiable.** O'Connor: "Have you talked to immigrants? Anybody listening — if you've walked around London and thought 'wow, how profoundly Christian everything is here'..." He notes the "people who give the anecdotes aren't even here," making it "impossible to follow" and "essentially anecdotal."[^3] *(Fair — the claim rests on reported impressions, not data; it is rhetorically vivid but evidentially weak, and connects to O'Connor's broader [[The Falsifiability Demand Against the Air-We-Breathe Thesis|unfalsifiability]] worry: outsider perception can be read to confirm the thesis whatever it is.)*

4. **The disagreement is partly empirical.** Both concede it "depends on who you ask" and is "impossible to prove." It functions less as an argument than as an illustration of the "invisible air" intuition.[^3] *(Honest mutual acknowledgment that this point cannot settle the dispute.)*

## Best Quotes
