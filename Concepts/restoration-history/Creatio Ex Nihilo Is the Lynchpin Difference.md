# Creatio Ex Nihilo Is the Lynchpin Difference

## The Claim
Both interlocutors identify **creatio ex nihilo** (creation from nothing) as the deepest root of their disagreement — "a lynchpin issue at the core of where we will differ." [^1] [[Jacob Hansen]] rejects ex nihilo creation entirely: "we don't believe anything was created" out of nothing; all beings and a fundamental "spiritual realm" have existed for "all eternity past," and God *organizes* a pre-existing chaos rather than calling being from non-being. [^2][^3] [[Gavin Ortlund]] affirms classical creatio ex nihilo: "there is a time when nothing but God exists. In fact, time itself is a creation of God." [^4]

## Supporting Evidence (and steelman of both sides)

1. **Ex nihilo establishes the Creator/creature divide.** Ortlund: on ex nihilo, God is *necessary* and all else is *contingent*; this yields "a thick ontological distinction between God and all that is not God." [^5] Hansen agrees the stakes are total: "if I were to tell you... that there's no such thing as creation out of nothing, that would change everything." [^1] *Rating: both affirm this is the hinge* — the soteriology, theosis, and Christology debates are all downstream of it.

2. **Hansen's "painter" model of creation.** Creation is real but is *organization* of existing material: "I can create something as a painter; that doesn't mean that I made the paint out of nothing... you can take existing things and organize them and create order." [^6] *Rating: a coherent alternative metaphysic* (creatio ex materia) that he claims better fits "the actual biblical views of the ancient Hebrews." [^7] (See [[Greco-Roman Jewish Creation View Was Material Substrate]].)

3. **Necessity/contingency collapses without ex nihilo.** Ortlund notes "the entire notion of necessity and contingency collapses within creatio ex materia... if everything has always existed then everything is eternal." [^8] *Rating: an accurate logical consequence* — Hansen embraces it, holding the Father has *always* been fully divine (no eternal regression; see [[LDS Father Was Always Fully Divine (No Eternal Regression)]]).

4. **Steelman of Ortlund's biblical case.** He cites [[Nehemiah 9.6|Nehemiah 9:6]] (God made all the host of heaven and they are drawn into being), [[Psalm 90.2|Psalm 90:2]] ("from everlasting to everlasting, you are God"), and [[Romans 4.17]]-type "calls into being" language, plus [[Michael Heiser]]'s "species-unique" framing of Yahweh among the elohim. [^9][^10] *Rating: strong within the canonical/classical-theism tradition;* contested by critical scholarship on the *historical* origin of ex nihilo (see [[Creation Ex Nihilo Originated Late 2nd Century CE]]).

5. **Steelman of Hansen's historical claim.** Hansen urges evaluating "if ex nihilo creation was the actual original view of... the Hebrew Bible," implying it is a later philosophical overlay. [^7] *Rating: aligns with a real scholarly debate* (Gerhard May, etc.); Ortlund does not contest the history here, only the theology.

## Best Quotes
> "First and foremost is that we don't believe any of us are created. We don't, because we reject creatio ex nihilo. We don't believe anything was created." [^2]

> "There is a time when nothing but God exists. In fact, time itself is a creation of God. And so creatio ex nihilo is probably a lynchpin issue at the core of where we will differ." [^4]

> "Creation is more like a painter. I can create something as a painter; that doesn't mean that I made the paint out of nothing." [^6]

> "Ex nihilo creation is a massive lynchpin that so many of the things within the credal tradition ride on." [^11]
