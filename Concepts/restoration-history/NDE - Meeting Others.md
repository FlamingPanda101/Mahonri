# NDE - Meeting Others

## The Point
The loneliness of the disembodied state is soon dispelled when **other spiritual beings come to meet the dying person** [^1]. Most often these are the spirits of **deceased relatives and friends**, who seem to have come to ease the transition or, in a few cases, to send the person back [^1][^2]. A smaller number encounter unfamiliar spirits or "guardian spirits," and some perceive only a felt presence and reassuring thoughts rather than visible figures [^2][^3].

## Evidence
1. A woman dying in childbirth saw "multitudes" of departed loved ones "hovering around the ceiling" — her grandmother, a school friend, and many relatives — who seemed to have "come to protect or to guide me... as if I were coming home" [^1].
2. A man, the moment he left his body, sensed his recently-killed friend Bob standing beside him — present and recognizable, yet with "a clear body," not a physical one — who waited silently and departed only when the doctors said the man would live [^1].
3. Unfamiliar spirits: a woman saw the transparent spirit of another person who had "died very recently," whom she did not know and who seemed to have no particular age [^2].
4. Guardian spirits: one man was told, "I have helped you through this stage of your existence, but now I am going to turn you over to others"; another woman's two beings identified themselves as her "spiritual helpers" [^2].
5. A voice "beyond the physical senses" told two people they "must go back" [^2].
6. Amorphous presence: one man in the void felt people around him he never saw, and "whenever I wondered what was going on, I would always get a thought back... that everything was all right" [^3].

## Best Quotes
> "They were all people I had known in my past life, but who had passed on before. I recognized my grandmother and a girl I had known when I was in school, and many other relatives and friends.... It was almost as if I were coming home, and they were there to greet or to welcome me." [^1]

## LDS Connection
The reunion with departed kin maps directly onto LDS afterlife theology. Alma 40:11 teaches that the spirits of the dead are "taken home to that God who gave them life," and D&C 138 portrays the spirit world as a society of the righteous dead — including family — who labor to welcome and minister to new arrivals. The recurring sense of "coming home" to known loved ones is precisely the LDS expectation of the post-mortal spirit world (see [[LDS Afterlife - Degrees of Glory]]).
