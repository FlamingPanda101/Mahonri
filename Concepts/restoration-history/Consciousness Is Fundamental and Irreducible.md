# Consciousness Is Fundamental and Irreducible

## The Point
The pivotal premise of the [[Sources Index|cosmic conscious argument]] is that consciousness is *fundamental* — it "cannot be reduced to smaller parts like chemical processes in the brain."[^1] [[Inspiring Philosophy|Michael Jones]] borrows premise 1 from JP Moreland's argument from consciousness: contingent minds have *either* a personal explanation *or* a natural explanation.[^2] The naturalist says mind is an emergent product of brain chemistry. The alternative — which Jones defends — is that to be conscious is to be "a self-aware mind or agent," and if the mind "was not built by smaller parts and is fundamental," it cannot have a natural, bottom-up origin.[^3] Reductionism is therefore the dividing line of the whole argument.

## Evidence
Jones's reasoning is a dilemma about origins. We are conscious, yet "we came into existence at a point in time."[^3] A fundamental, irreducible thing cannot be assembled from non-conscious parts, so a conscious mind that begins to exist "must come from a personal source which produces other persons."[^4] In other words, *if reductionism is false* and the mind cannot be reduced to smaller natural or informational parts, then consciousness "would come from a necessary source of consciousness which would be personal by definition since we are personal agents."[^4] This mirrors the structure of [[The Mind Is Not the Brain]] (immateriality, subjectivity, intentionality resist physical reduction) and of the [[Consciousness and First Cause Problem for Atheism]] (non-conscious matter cannot generate consciousness), but Jones turns it into a positive inference to a personal cause rather than merely a defeater for materialism. He explicitly brackets the *defense* of premise 2 — that physics shows the universe emerges from consciousness — referring viewers to his other videos.[^5]

## Best Quotes
> "Consciousness is fundamental as it is and cannot be reduced to smaller parts like chemical processes in the brain." [^1]

> "If the mind was not built by smaller parts and is fundamental ... it must come from a personal source which produces other persons." [^4]

## LDS Connection
Restoration theology is unusually congenial to a fundamental, irreducible mind. The intelligence at the core of every person "was not created or made, neither indeed can be" (D&C 93:29), and "man was also in the beginning with God" (D&C 93:29). Mind/intelligence is thus *eternal and uncreated* — exactly the irreducibility Jones argues for — though LDS thought differs from his conclusion in a key way: it treats individual intelligences as themselves beginningless rather than as effects produced by a single personal source (see [[Spirit Birth and Intelligences]]). Honest note: Jones's argument concludes to a *necessary creating mind that produces other persons*, whereas the LDS model has God organizing co-eternal intelligences — the family resemblance is "mind is fundamental," not "minds are caused." Compare [[Consciousness Precedes Existence - Neuroscience and Pre-Existence]].
