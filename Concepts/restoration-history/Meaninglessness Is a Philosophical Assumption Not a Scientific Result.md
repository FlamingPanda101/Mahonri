# Meaninglessness Is a Philosophical Assumption Not a Scientific Result

## The Point

The widespread claim that science has shown life to be purposeless is not itself a scientific finding. As [[Samuel T. Wilkinson]] argues, no experiment was ever run whose "conclusion is life has no purpose"; the verdict of meaninglessness is "a philosophical assumption" layered on top of the science. That assumption is in turn propped up by a second claim — that evolution is "a totally random and haphazard process" — which Wilkinson says is "simply not the case, especially in the last 20 or 30 years." Disentangling the metaphysical add-on from the empirical biology is the first move in showing that an evolutionary account does not, by itself, negate purpose.

## Evidence

- **The "no purpose" verdict is smuggled in, not derived.** Wilkinson insists the leading evolutionary thinkers did not reach meaninglessness experimentally: "it's not like they ran an experiment and at the end of it they said oh the results, the conclusion is life has no purpose. That's sort of a philosophical assumption." [^1]
- **It depends on a contestable premise about randomness.** The assumption "is grounded in this notion that evolution is a totally random and haphazard process," which Wilkinson rejects in light of recent biology. [^1] The empirical correction comes from convergence research (see [[Evolutionary Convergence Implies a Directed Process]]).
- **Authoritative atheist rhetoric, not data, carries the weight.** Wilkinson notes that "many of the leading thinkers in evolution have said statements to that effect," and cites a memorable dismissal of life as "a mere eddy in the primeval slime" — the kind of quote that "15 years ago... was very disturbing to me" but that he came to see as philosophy dressed as science. [^2]
- **Even granting non-randomness, the inference to purpose is modest.** Wilkinson is careful: non-randomness "doesn't necessarily prove" design and is "not a home run quite yet" — it merely "makes a lot more space" for a guiding deity and removes the supposed scientific defeater for purpose. [^3]

## Best Quotes

> "It's not like they ran an experiment and at the end of it they said oh the results, the conclusion is life has no purpose. That's sort of a philosophical assumption, and a lot of it is grounded in this notion that evolution is a totally random and haphazard process — and that's simply not the case." [^1]

> "Even Darwin's staunchest defenders have now acknowledged this... there seem to be higher-order chemical and physical laws that have guided evolution to produce the same biological forms over and over again." [^4]

## LDS Connection

Latter-day Saint theology already locates purpose in the structure of reality rather than in a feeling projected onto a blank cosmos: the plan of salvation frames mortality as a purposeful "second estate," and eternal progression makes meaning a fact about what kind of beings we are, not a comforting fiction. Recognizing that "science proves life is meaningless" is a smuggled philosophical premise clears the ground for the LDS claim that the cosmos is ordered toward the exaltation of God's children. This complements the critique that an atheistic frame collapses into [[Nihilism as Logical Endpoint of Atheism]] precisely because it mistakes a metaphysical assumption for a scientific result.
