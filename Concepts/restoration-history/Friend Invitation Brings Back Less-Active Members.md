# Friend Invitation Brings Back Less-Active Members

#type/claim

## The Claim
A large share of less-active Latter-day Saints — particularly the ~50% "faith keeper" group who left over relocation or competing priorities rather than belief — say they "would go back to church simply if they were invited by a friend." The relational quality of the invitation, not mere contact, is what matters.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Self-reported willingness (Evidence: moderate; large sample but stated-intention).** Many in the favorable group "said they would go back to church simply if they were invited by a friend."[^1] Given that this group left for non-doctrinal reasons (a move, life transition, social friction), low-friction re-entry is plausible.

2. **"Friend," not institutional contact (Evidence: emphasized by researcher).** Dyer stresses the wording: "would you come back if a *friend* invited you?" — "not just a somebody from the church knocking on their door." Invitations "land very differently when they come from a friend versus somebody else."[^2]

3. **The mechanism: re-integration friction (Evidence: plausible, observational).** The host and Dyer describe relocation as the choke point — a new ward, uncertainty about whether "the kids at church going to like me," and never "getting going." A trusted relationship removes that social risk.[^3]

**Steelman of the opposing view:** Stated willingness to return is a hypothetical that costs nothing to affirm and routinely overstates real behavior; people say yes to "would you come back if a friend asked?" far more than they act on it. The finding also doesn't apply to the ~50% who left over belief, for whom an invitation addresses none of the actual barrier. Framing reactivation as "just invite them" risks oversimplifying a relationship-building process Dyer himself says takes genuine ministering.

## Best Quotes

> "Many of them said they would go back to church simply if they were invited by a friend."[^1]

> "Those invitations land very differently when they come from a friend versus somebody else."[^2]

> "Statistically, like just inviting them, like that's that's insane to me and really eye-opening just to know that that might be all it takes for some people."[^3]
