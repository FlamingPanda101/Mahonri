# The Three Options for the Seeker - True Church Other Church or No True Church

## The Claim
[[Jacob Hansen]] argues that anyone who affirms the resurrection of Jesus is forced into "comparative Christianity," where the question "is there a true church?" admits **only three logical options**: (1) the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints is the true church; (2) some *other* church is the true church; or (3) there is no true church (i.e. any view that is not 1 or 2). "The true church" is defined functionally as "the church which the Risen Lord would want all of his followers to be part of." Hansen says he is relieved if (1), genuinely open to (2), and content to remain LDS if (3) — which sets up the critic's dilemma developed separately.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The trichotomy is exhaustive (rate: strong, logical).** Given a yes/no question ("is there a true church?"), the options partition cleanly: it is the LDS church, or another church, or none. Option 3 absorbs every position that denies a uniquely true church.[^2]

2. **Hansen is open to option 2 (rate: framing).** He quotes [[Brigham Young]]: "it is our duty and calling to gather every item of truth and reject every error, whether that truth be found with ... the universalists or the Church of Rome or the Methodists ... or any other of the various and numerous different sects." Hansen: "if there is something better and more true that I should place my faith in, I want to know about it." See [[The Gathering of Truths from All Churches]].[^3]

3. **Option 3 makes membership mere preference (rate: moderate, conditional).** If there is no true church, "what church I am a part of is ultimately ... just a matter of personal preference," so preferring the restored gospel "makes perfect sense" — and a critic who concedes option 3 has no standing to object to that preference. "If it's only a matter of preference, why do they care if I prefer the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints?"[^4]

4. **A taxonomy of believers follows (rate: descriptive).** The options generate categories: the *believing* Latter-day Saint, the *LDS-by-preference*, the *unhappy Mormon*, the *non-LDS Christian* (e.g. Catholics, who hold option 2), and the *alternative Christian* (option 3, preferring some other church or none).[^5]

> The move pairs with [[Pascal's Wager]]: Hansen explicitly frames his stance as Pascalian — staying where the payoff is best while remaining open to a demonstrably better option.

## Steelman of the Opposing View
The trichotomy is valid but the *interesting* work is smuggled into how "true church" is defined and into option 2. (a) A pluralist holds option 3 sincerely and consistently: no church is uniquely true, all preferences are permissible, and they genuinely do *not* think Mormons are damned — for them the dilemma never arises (it bites only the *inconsistent* critic; see [[The No-True-Church Critic Smuggles In a Creedal Minimum]]). (b) Defining "true church" as "the one the Risen Lord wants everyone in" presupposes a single divine institutional will, which many Christians deny in favor of a mystical/invisible church. (c) "Openness to option 2" is rhetorical unless the criteria for adjudication are specified — the same problem the [[Name the True Church or Concede the Apostasy - the Negative's Burden|negative's burden]] cuts both ways. The framing is a sound opening move, not a proof of the LDS option.

## Best Quotes

> "There are only three possibilities. The first option is that the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints is the true church. The second is that some other church is the true church. And the third is that there is no true church."[^2]

> "If it's only a matter of preference, why do they care if I prefer the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints over another church?"[^4]
