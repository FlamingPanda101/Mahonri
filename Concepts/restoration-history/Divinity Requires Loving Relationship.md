# Divinity Requires Loving Relationship

## The Claim
[[Blake Ostler]] argues that **being in loving relationship is essential to divinity** — a person cannot be divine alone. Love is a *dependence*: to love requires another to love. Hence the Father, Son, and Holy Ghost are mutually dependent for their divinity, and humans become divine by entering the same relationship. This directly attacks the classical "God is love" / divine-aseity picture. [^1]

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. Love is a dependence relation (rating: strong; philosophical)
"Love is a dependence. I can't love you unless you exist. So at least I have to have some relationship in order to be fully loving. But God essentially is fully loving to be divine." Against Thomism's "He *is* love," Ostler asks what that *means* if God exists wholly alone. [^1]

> "love is a dependence. I can't love you unless you exist... God essentially is fully loving to be divine." [^1]

### 2. Swinburne: love requires another, so the Son is generated (rating: moderate; scholarly)
Ostler cites [[Richard Swinburne]]: "there has to be another to love, and that means God has to create the son in order to be God... as a matter of his essence, he creates the son." Swinburne is a social trinitarian whom most call "thoroughly tritheistic," but Ostler calls it "a good argument." [^2]

### 3. The Father too must love to be divine (rating: strong; systematic)
"Does the father have to love in order to be divine? The answer is yes." Though the Father is the *initiator*, "the father is as dependent on them as they are dependent on the father for their divinity." See [[No Diminished Divinity Despite Subordination]]. [^3]

### 4. Theosis = entering the same relationship (rating: moderate; soteriological)
Humans "are dependent on them to become... divine like they are. In other words, to fully partake of the divine nature, as second Peter would say" ([[2 Peter 1.4|2 Peter 1:4]]). "When we are in the same relationship that they are, we have the full divinity that they have." See [[Human Deification in Mormonism]]. [^4]

### 5. Steelman of the opposing view
Classical theism replies that the Trinity *already* secures love-within-God without dependence on creatures or on a *generated* being: the Father eternally loves the Son in the single divine life, so God is love essentially and necessarily, with no external or contingent dependence — and divine aseity is preserved because the persons are not three separate beings who need one another to *exist*. Swinburne's "God must create the Son to love" strikes them as collapsing the Creator/creature distinction (a created Son is not God). Ostler's counter: a Trinity under divine simplicity cannot host the *real* I-Thou relations that genuine love requires, so only a social model (LDS or Swinburnean) makes "God is love" intelligible. See [[Latin Trinity and Eastern Trinity Both Fail]].

## Best Quotes

> "to be divine, a person has to be loving in loving relationships. It's essential to divinity." [^5]

> "What does it mean to say that God is all loving if he's the only being in existence?" [^2]

> "to fully partake of the divine nature, as second Peter would say... when we are in the same relationship that they are, we have the full divinity that they have." [^4]
