# Clement and Origen Allegorized Away the Physical Second Coming

## The Point

Against the literalist premillennialists, the Alexandrian theologians [[Clement of Alexandria]] and [[Origen]] developed an allegorical reading of Christian eschatology that "effectively spiritualized" the Second Coming and the Millennium. [[Nicholas J. Frederick]] presents this as the alternative reaction to the delay of the Parousia — instead of defending a literal future kingdom, the Alexandrians relocated salvation to the present spiritual life and the soul's contemplation of God. [^1]

## Evidence

1. **Clement's rational Christianity had little need for a physical return.** Clement "sought to make Christian ideas philosophically sound" and believed true salvation "primarily occurs through finding Christ spiritually and ultimately through an 'assimilation with God.'" The ideal life was "not one directed toward the end but the present — a life devoted to the eternal contemplation of God." [^2] Clement was the student of Pantaenus, credited with originating the Alexandrian allegorical school. [^3]
2. **Origen criticized literal readings as "Jewish."** Origen "criticized those theologians who interpreted the millennial description in the book of Revelation literally or, as he called it, in a 'Jewish sense.'" Literal interpretations, he felt, were merely "pedagogic devices to assist Christians who were not quite prepared for the theological depth of Christianity." [^4]
3. **Origen found liberation in *leaving* the world, not in Christ's return to it.** "I must progress beyond this world, to be able to see what these 'tents' are, which the Lord has made." Brian Daley calls this "the eschatological contemplation of God as constant spiritual movement and growth." [^5]
4. **Critics saw it as an evasion of eschatology itself.** Daley judged Origen's eschatology "not a Platonized form of genuine Christian eschatology, but an alternative to eschatology, indeed an evasion of it." To contemporaries it "sounded dangerously close to the Platonist hope for an immortality simply of the soul, or to a Gnostic devaluation of the material cosmos." [^6]
5. **Literalists struck back.** The Egyptian bishop **Nepos** wrote *Against the Allegorists* defending a literal Second Coming and Millennium; **Sextus Julius Africanus** (AD 160-240) adopted the seven-thousand-year scheme of human history with the final thousand years as the Millennium. [^7]

## Best Quotes
