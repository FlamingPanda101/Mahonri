# Great Apostasy and Need for Restoration
#type/claim

## The Claim
The Great Apostasy is the foundational Latter-day Saint claim that, after the deaths of the apostles, the early church fell into a universal apostasy — losing the priesthood authority, the fulness of the gospel, and the divinely authorized organization Christ established — so that no existing church retained the keys to act in God's name. Because the loss was *total* (not merely local error that could be corrected from within), the remedy could not be reformation but only **Restoration**: a fresh divine intervention re-establishing apostolic authority, ordinances, and revelation through Joseph Smith. The doctrine is the load-bearing premise behind the whole LDS project: if the apostasy was real and complete, a restored church is *necessary*; if it was not, the Restoration is superfluous.

## Supporting Evidence
1. **The apostasy began early.** As argued in [[Galatians 1-6 Marvel Ye Are So Soon Removed Early Apostasy]], Paul "marvels" the Galatians are "so soon removed... unto another gospel" ([[Galatians 1.6|Galatians 1:6]]) — defection already underway within his lifetime. The New Testament epistles are read as a record of churches drifting, not thriving, supplying a pattern that (LDS apologists argue) accelerated once the apostles died and the keys were no longer transmitted.
2. **Foretold, not merely inferred.** Scripture is read to *predict* apostasy: "a falling away first" before the Lord returns ([[2 Thessalonians 2.3|2 Thessalonians 2:3]]); "grievous wolves" entering after Paul's departure (Acts 20:29-30); "the times of restitution of all things" (Acts 3:21) implying something must first be lost to be restored.
3. **Loss of authority, not just morals.** The claim is specifically about *priesthood keys* and authorized ordinances departing (developed in [[Apostasy Was Complete When the Keys Departed]]) — so even genuine virtue, scripture, and sincere believers persisting through the centuries do not refute it, since one can keep the light of Christ without holding the sealing authority.
4. **Restoration as the matched remedy.** Angelic ordination ([[D&C 13]], [[D&C 27]]) and a re-established church with living apostles answer a *complete* apostasy in the only adequate way: not by editing a surviving institution but by re-conferring what was lost.

**Steelman of the opposing view.** Mainstream Christianity (Catholic, Orthodox, Protestant) denies any total apostasy, appealing to Christ's promise that "the gates of hell shall not prevail against" the church ([[Matthew 16.18|Matthew 16:18]]) and "I am with you alway, even unto the end of the world" (Matthew 28:20) — a complete failure of the church would falsify these. Catholics and Orthodox add an unbroken episcopal succession; Protestants add the survival of the true gospel in a remnant (cf. [[Protestantism Inherited a Divided Church It Did Not Break Unity]]). On this reading, [[Galatians 1.6|Galatians 1:6]] shows *local, correctable* error (Paul writes precisely to fix it and expects success), not the universal severing of authority the Restoration requires. The contested step is always the leap from "some early churches strayed quickly" to "the whole church apostatized and the priesthood was lost."
