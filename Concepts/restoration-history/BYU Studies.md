# BYU Studies

BYU Studies (published as *BYU Studies Quarterly*) is a peer-reviewed academic journal of Brigham Young University, founded in 1959, covering Latter-day Saint history, scripture, theology, and the intersection of faith and scholarship across many disciplines. It is one of the principal venues for faithful LDS academic work and is frequently cited in apologetic and historical discussion.

## Relevance

The vault cites it as the publication venue for Jeff Rappleye's work on the Lehite migration — specifically the argument that NHM/Nahom integrates the place and the tribe — supporting the [[Nahom and NHM Converge with Nephis Eastward Turn]] case for Book of Mormon historicity.
