# Where Mormons Rank on the Church-Sect Continuum

## The Claim
The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints has no fixed sociological label — the categories are relative to **time and geography**, so the answer changes by place.

## Supporting Evidence
- **Utah**: functions and thinks of itself as a *church* (with residual sectarian features) *(scholar's analysis)* [^1].
- **Southern Alberta** (where ~⅓ are Mormon, site of the Cardston/McGrath temple): a well-established, respectable *sect* *(scholar's analysis)* [^2].
- **Quebec** (historically Catholic, now highly secular): would be seen as an "outlandish cult" *(scholar's analysis)* [^3].
- **United States overall**: has arrived at *church / denomination* status *(scholar's analysis)* [^3].

## Best Quote
> "If strangeness or heresy is what a cult is, then I'll be the first one to tell you Latter-day Saints are a cult, man. We're really strange and we believe all sorts of Christian heresies. So it just depends on what you say is the criteria." — Ward Radio host [^4]
