# Demographic Indicators Predict a Bright LDS Future

## The Claim
Despite real retention decline, the LDS Church's demographic and institutional indicators — high convert baptisms, high participation in its educational system, high birth rates, and a young membership — point toward a strong future, and the cyclical history of social connectedness leaves ample room for recovery.

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Burge's demographic forecast (rated: moderate).** Demographer [[Ryan Burge]], in his book on the American religious landscape, notes that Latter-day Saints have high birth rates ("really good for a religion"), high participation rates, and a relatively young membership — features that "spell a lot of good things," leading him to say Latter-day Saints "have a bright future ahead," while acknowledging difficulties too.[^1]

2. **Institutional vitality signals (rated: moderate).** "We see higher rates of convert baptisms than ever, including in the United States... massive participation in our church educational system."[^2]

3. **The cyclical hope from Putnam (rated: moderate).** Because connectedness fell, rose, and fell again historically, "there is no reason to think that we're just going to keep going down." See [[Decline in Religion Tracks the Broader Collapse of Social Connectedness]].[^3] *Steelman:* Burge himself flags difficulties, and "anything could happen" plus an appeal to prophetic prediction (President Nelson on "the greatest miracles") is faith-based forecasting, not statistical projection — optimism is warranted but not data-guaranteed.

4. **Faith-based and media tailwinds (rated: weak/observational).** Dyer cites President Russell M. Nelson's teaching that "the greatest miracles will happen in the coming years," and the host adds a favorable media moment (reality TV, Hollywood attention) and a wave of pro-faith content creators. See [[Russell M. Nelson]].[^4] Pew data suggesting the broader decline of religion may be slowing or plateauing add a further glimmer.[^5]

## Best Quotes

> "Latter-day Saints have high birth rates, which is really good for a religion, and we have high participation rates, and we're pretty young... it looks like Latter-day Saints have a bright future ahead."[^1]

> "We're down, but certainly we shouldn't count ourselves as out by any means."[^4]

> "Not despairing is probably the number one thing... because there's all kinds of hope that we can have moving forward."[^4]
