# Martyrs Were the Venerated Intercessors of the Early Christian Family

## The Point
[[Catherine Gines Taylor]] shows how familial veneration of the dead expanded into the **cult of the martyrs**: martyrs became "powerful agents of intercession within this new family of God." Christians sought burial *ad sanctos* (near the saints), built martyrium churches over their tombs, and collected blessed objects (*eulogia*), believing proximity to holy bodies and relics aided salvation. The framework was Roman **patron-client** relationship, with Christ as the true *Pater Patriae*.

## Evidence
1. **Martyrs as celebrities and intercessors.** "Early Christian martyrs were the venerated 'celebrities' of the Christian world," associated with iconic sacred sites; "hundreds of martyrium churches were built over or near places of martyrdom" [^1].
2. **Peter's basilica and relics.** Constantine built Old Saint Peter's Basilica over Peter's martyrdom site; his relics drew pilgrims who came "to see, touch, or be near them" and took away *eulogia*. The Pola casket depicts devotees descending beneath the ciborium to touch Peter's tomb [^2].
3. **Patron-client logic.** "Roman society, even Christian society, was based on patron-client relationships, and Christ was the true *Pater Patriae*, able to bestow his particular gifts and benevolence to his followers" [^2].
4. **Burial near the holy.** Christians "desired to be buried alongside these martyrs." Burial *ad sanctos* (close to saints/relics) "was thought to aid in the salvation of the deceased and was attested from the fourth century onward" — e.g. the **sarcophagus of Junius Bassus** (AD 359) placed near Peter's tomb [^3].
5. **Martyrs still wait.** Per [[Revelation 6.9-11|Revelation 6:9–11]], martyrs "rest under the altar" yet "must still abide in peace, cognizant of their waiting for all of God's children to find their way to safe refuge." [[N.T. Wright]]: "this is why the Orthodox pray for the saints as well as with them, that they with us when we join them may come to the fulfillment of God's complete purposes" [^4].

## Best Quotes
> "Familial remembrance was adapted into the veneration of martyrs who were likewise powerful agents of intercession within this new family of God." [^5]

> "Receiving burial in proximity to holy people and sanctified objects or relics was thought to aid in the salvation of the deceased and was attested from the fourth century onward." [^3]

## LDS Connection
Taylor invites Latter-day Saints to relate via "the natural impulse … to visit places sacred to our history, such as the Sacred Grove, Carthage Jail, Winter Quarters, Kirtland, Nauvoo." The martyrs' *waiting* state (Rev 6) and Wright's "pray for the saints as well as with them" cohere with the LDS spirit world and the bond between the living and faithful dead (D&C 138). [[Joseph Smith]]'s own martyrdom at Carthage gives the parallel added resonance.
