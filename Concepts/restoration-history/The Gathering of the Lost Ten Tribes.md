# The Gathering of the Lost Ten Tribes

## The Claim

Latter-day Saints believe in a *literal* gathering of Israel, including the restoration of the lost ten tribes scattered by Assyria. On [[Ward Radio]], [[Jonah Barnes]] lays out the historical-scriptural baseline and then argues that the gathering is already visibly underway: patriarchal blessings in Russia identified members of rare tribes (Gad, Reuben, Naphtali, Simeon, Levi, Manasseh), [[Russell M. Nelson]] gathered Siberian saints "into their tribes" in 2004, and a DNA-migration map converging on the Caucasus (north of the Euphrates, where Josephus last located the ten tribes) is offered as later scientific confirmation of the gathering geography.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. The scriptural-historical baseline is mainstream
The ten northern tribes were deported when Assyria conquered the northern kingdom (~722 BC); [[Josephus]] (1st c. AD) is the last historical reference, placing them "beyond the Euphrates... an immense multitude," after which their location is conjecture. The Manasseh of [[Lehi]]'s line left separately for the New World. This frame is uncontroversial history and aligns with the vault's [[The Surviving Old Testament Is the Southern Kingdom's Edition|northern-kingdom material]].[^1]

> **Evidence rating:** Strong on the deportation and Josephus citation; the "where did they go" is openly conceded to be conjecture.

### 2. The 2004 Siberia gathering as enacted prophecy
Barnes reports [[Russell M. Nelson]] flew to Siberia in 2004, paid travel for members across a continent-sized mission, and directed them to "divide into your tribes," with six of the tribes represented — pronouncing an apostolic blessing that "we're beginning to gather the ten tribes." He cites his contemporaneous mission journal listing members by tribe.[^2]

> **Evidence rating:** Weak as evidence — a secondhand devotional account; even if accurate, it documents a *ritual* gathering by self-declared tribe (via patriarchal blessing), not an external confirmation that these converts descend from the deported tribes.

### 3. A DNA-migration map as claimed confirmation
The hosts show a near-Eastern DNA-marker map whose migration lines converge on the Caucasus then scatter west (to Europe) and east (to Siberia), arguing the patriarchs identified the tribes in 2004 *before* this genetics, so "science verified the patriarchs." See [[Patriarchal Blessings Reveal Tribal Lineage and Cannot Be Faked]].[^3]

> **Evidence rating:** Weak — the map shows general human migration through the Caucasus, not tribe-of-Israel ancestry; it is impressionistic and conflates population movement with specific lineage assignment.

## Steelman of the Opposing View

A critic responds that the "confirmation" is circular and category-confused. (a) The tribal assignments come from *patriarchal blessings*, which are themselves the thing in question; pointing to a gathering "by tribe" that was defined by those blessings cannot then confirm the blessings. (b) The DNA map shows ordinary human migration out of the Near East through the Caucasus — true of essentially all West Eurasian populations — and says nothing about descent from the specific tribes Assyria deported; "DNA supports the patriarchs" overreads a generic migration graphic. (c) Mainstream scholarship treats the "lost ten tribes" as largely assimilated into surrounding populations rather than a preserved diaspora awaiting gathering, and the apocryphal numbers (2 Baruch's "nine and a half tribes") the hosts cite are legendary, not historical. (d) The whole segment is interleaved with the hosts' own jokes about hollow-earth and pterodactyl ten-tribes theories, signaling they hold the *literal-location* speculation loosely. A Latter-day Saint reply: the doctrinal core — a literal covenant gathering — does not depend on the Siberia anecdote or the map; those are offered as faith-promoting illustrations, and the lineage language is covenantal/adoptive ([[Genealogical Descent Is Not Genetic Descent]]), so it is not refuted by population genetics.

> "DNA supports what LDS patriarchs have discovered in nowhere Siberia... please tell me this genetic information came out after the patriarchs had already done the patriarchal blessings." [^3]

## Related (another source)

A separate [[Ward Radio]] episode, [[Ward Radio - We Found the Lost Ten Tribes of Israel]], fleshes out the *historical mechanism* this note's eschatology presupposes. There [[Don Bradley]] explains the **Assyrian scatter strategy** — conquered peoples were dispersed through the empire and mixed with imported settlers to dissolve their ethnic identity, so the deportees' descendants are simply the peoples of that region, not a hidden preserved nation.[^4] He further argues the **Samaritans (descended from remnant northern Israel) are the ancestors of today's Palestinians**, making the Israeli–Palestinian conflict a clash within the House of Israel — see [[The Palestinians and Jews Are Both House of Israel]].[^5] The panel also locates **Ephraim in the British Isles** via the patriarchal blessings of British converts, reinforcing [[Patriarchal Blessings Reveal Tribal Lineage and Cannot Be Faked]].[^6] That episode holds the *literal-location* speculation (hollow earth, North Pole) explicitly as comedy, consistent with this note's steelman that the doctrinal core is a covenant gathering, not a geography.
