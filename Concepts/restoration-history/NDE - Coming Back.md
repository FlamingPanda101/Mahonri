# NDE - Coming Back

## The Point
Everyone Moody interviewed had, by definition, to **return** to the body — but a striking reversal of attitude usually precedes it.[^1] Whereas the first moments after death bring a "desperate desire to get back into the body," once a person reaches a certain depth, "he does not want to come back, and he may even resist the return," especially after encountering the being of light.[^2] When subjects do return, they attribute it variously to their own decision, to a sense of duty (often toward young children or an unfinished task), to God's purpose for them, or to the **love and prayers of others** drawing them back.[^3]

## Evidence
1. **"I never wanted to leave."** One man said "most emphatically, 'I never wanted to leave the presence of this being.'"[^2]
2. **Duty to family overrides desire to stay.** A mother of three: "When I had this wonderful feeling, there in the presence of that light, I really didn't want to come back. But I take my responsibilities very seriously... So I decided to try to come back."[^4]
3. **Allowed to live by God for a purpose.** "I say God surely was good to me, because I was dead, and he let the doctors bring me back, for a purpose. The purpose was to help my wife."[^5]
4. **Love pulls a person back.** A woman felt "called back—magnetized back—through the love of my sister and my husband. Since then, I have believed that other people can draw you back."[^6] Conversely, a dying aunt told her niece, "Your prayers are holding me over here. Please don't pray any more"; when the family stopped, "shortly after that she died."[^7]
5. **Mode of return is hazy.** Most "simply felt that... they 'went to sleep'... later to awaken in their physical bodies."[^8] A few recall a jolt; in rare detailed accounts re-entry is said to occur "through the head."[^9]
6. **The feelings linger.** "After I came back, I cried off and on for about a week because I had to live in this world after seeing that one. I didn't want to come back."[^10]

## Best Quotes
> "When I had this wonderful feeling, there in the presence of that light, I really didn't want to come back. But I take my responsibilities very seriously, and I knew that I had a duty to my family. So I decided to try to come back." [^4]

> "I felt as though I had been called back—magnetized back—through the love of my sister and my husband. Since then, I have believed that other people can draw you back." [^6]

## LDS Connection
That the return is so often tied to an unfinished, divinely sanctioned mission — and that the dying are "permitted" or "sent back" by God or the being of light — accords with the Restoration's teaching that the span of mortal life is purposeful and bounded by God's wisdom, part of a plan of probation rather than an accident. The reluctance to leave a presence of perfect love, and the lingering grief at re-entering mortality, fit the LDS picture of the spirit world as a familiar home from which we came and to which we return. The repeated theme that the **love and prayers of others** can hold a soul or draw it back resonates with the LDS emphasis on the bonds of family and the efficacy of faith and prayer reaching beyond the veil.
