# Revelation 22:19 — "Do Not Add or Take Away"

## The Charge
> "The Bible says you cannot add or take away from it. Joseph Smith added the Book
> of Mormon, therefore the Book of Mormon is demonic and Joseph Smith is cursed."

## The Verse (KJV)
> "For I testify unto every man that heareth the words of the prophecy of this
> book, If any man shall add unto these things, God shall add unto him the plagues
> that are written in this book."

## Rebuttal Points

### 1. The "Book" Is Revelation, Not the Bible
- Revelation was written ~late 90s AD by John on Patmos.
- **The Bible as a compiled canon did not exist yet.** The canon wasn't settled
  until the 4th–5th centuries (and even later for some traditions — the Ethiopian
  Orthodox Church has additional books).
- John is clearly referring to "the words of the prophecy **of this book**" — i.e.,
  the scroll of Revelation, not the entire biblical canon.
- If the verse refers to "the Bible," John couldn't have been referring to a book
  that wouldn't exist for centuries.

### 2. Deuteronomy 4:2 Uses Identical Language
> "Ye shall not add unto the word which I command you, neither shall ye diminish
> ought from it."

If Revelation 22:19 means "don't add to the Bible," then Deuteronomy 4:2 means
"don't add anything after Deuteronomy." That would make the entire New Testament an
illegitimate addition. The logic is self-defeating for Christians.

### 3. The Plagues Didn't Happen to Joseph Smith
The verse specifies the plagues "written in this book" as the penalty: hail, fire,
blood, sea turning to blood, waters poisoned, earthquakes, painful sores, scorching
heat, worldwide warfare. Joseph Smith experienced none of these specific
Revelation-plagues — a pragmatic test the argument fails.

### 4. Ancient "FBI Warning" Practice
- Similar inviolability clauses were standard in ancient legal documents and sacred
  texts — essentially an ancient copyright/anti-tampering warning (Braden: "ancient
  Byzantine work contracts" with multi-generational curses).
- Paul dealt with people forging letters in his name (2 Thessalonians 2:2); such
  clauses protected individual documents from interpolation.
- This is the "Mosaic FBI warning," not a canon-closure declaration.

## Bottom Line
The verse warns against tampering with John's Apocalypse — not a declaration that
God would never reveal more scripture.
