# Logical Consistency Is a Necessary Condition for Truth

## The Point
[[Jacob Hansen]] argues that internal logical consistency is a *necessary* (though not sufficient) condition for any worldview to be true: "without logical consistency a worldview cannot be true."[^1] A view that contradicts itself is "disqualified from the start... by the very laws of logic itself," regardless of how appealing it is.[^1] Hansen sharply separates this from mere *implausibility*: "the miraculous may be hard to believe, but a logically contradictory worldview makes no sense at all."[^2] A miracle is improbable but possible; a contradiction is impossible in every possible world. This is the standard underwriting [[The Sin of an Inconsistent Worldview]], and it explains why exposing self-refutation (e.g., [[The Argument from Reason - Atheism Undermines Reason Itself]]) is fatal rather than merely awkward.

## Evidence
1. **Consistency as a truth-condition.** "The reality is that without logical consistency a worldview cannot be true."[^1]
2. **Hard-to-believe is not the same as incoherent.** "The miraculous may be hard to believe, but a logically contradictory worldview makes no sense at all. It's disqualified from the start. It's like saying 2 + 2 equals 6 — it's a broken worldview by the very laws of logic itself."[^2]
3. **Applied as a self-assessment.** Hansen grants the believing worldview "has its challenges," but claims its decisive advantage is that "at least it's relatively consistent," standing "head and shoulders above others" that are "arbitrary and inconsistent."[^1][^3]

## Best Quotes
> "I often say that the miraculous may be hard to believe, but a logically contradictory worldview makes no sense at all. It's disqualified from the start." [^2]

> "Without logical consistency a worldview cannot be true." [^1]

## LDS Connection
This principle keeps Latter-day Saint apologetics honest in both directions. It lets the believer press the critic — a contradictory naturalism is "disqualified from the start," not merely unproven (compare [[Nihilism as Logical Endpoint of Atheism]]). But it equally binds the believer: faith claims must clear the bar of non-contradiction even though they need not clear the bar of ordinary probability. The Restoration locates truth in convergent witnesses rather than a single empirical method, yet it does not license incoherence; the contradiction-vs-improbability distinction is exactly why "hard to believe" miracles (a resurrected Christ, an embodied God) are admissible while a self-refuting worldview is not. The relevant subtlety — that "not impossible" is not the same as "no contradiction" — is treated in [[Not Impossible Is Not the Standard for Contradiction]].
