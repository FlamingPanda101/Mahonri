# The Problem Is Dogmatism Not Religion

## The Point

Pressed by [[Jordan Peterson]], [[Sam Harris]] concedes that the dangerous element shared by religious fundamentalism, Stalinism, and Nazism is **dogmatism** — the holding of absolute, unrevisable truth-claims — rather than belief in God as such. This leaves open, in Peterson's words, "the possibility that something could exist over on the religion side that doesn't have that characteristic."

## Argument (steelman)

1. **Secular totalitarianisms share the pathology.** Peterson notes the USSR and Nazi Germany produced atrocities "as pernicious as any religious dogmas" without grounding their axioms in God. So the common factor cannot be theism.[^1]

2. **Harris locates it in unrevisability.** "The overarching problem is believing things strongly without evidence... dogmas have to be enforced by force or the threat of force, because the moment someone has a better idea you have to shut it down."[^2]

3. **Stalinism resembled a religion.** Harris: it had "a personality cult... dogmatism that held sway to a point where apostasy and blasphemy were killing offenses." North Korea is "a religious cult" without supernatural claims.[^3]

4. **Harris's residual qualifier.** Not all dogmas are equal: religious dogma can be uniquely pernicious because of "the otherworldly variable... you're going to get everything you want after you die, so this life doesn't matter."[^4]

5. **The concession.** Peterson: "the reason to fear religious dogma is really on the dogma side and not the religion side." Harris: "the only thing that's wrong with the religion is the dogmatism. If you get rid of the dogma, I've got no problem with the buildings and the music and the paintings." (Peterson insists the buildings and music are not trivial.)[^5]

## Best Quotes

> "The problem is dogmatism. The overarching problem is believing things strongly without evidence... it doesn't allow us to revise our bad ideas in real time through conversation."[^2]

> "Nor — to take a more modern example — North Korea is a religious cult; it just doesn't happen to be one that is focused on the next life."[^3]
