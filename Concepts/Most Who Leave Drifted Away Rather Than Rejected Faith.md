# Most Who Leave Drifted Away Rather Than Rejected Faith

## The Claim
The most common path out of the Church is not intellectual rejection but quiet attrition — drifting or fading after a move or a major life disruption, while still holding belief and warm feelings toward the Church.

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Stated reasons skew incidental (rated: strong).** For faithkeepers and faith-leaners, "the most common reason they said that they disconnected from religion was their moving or other priorities" — not doctrinal disbelief.[^1]

2. **The relocation mechanism (rated: moderate).** Dyer likens it to a child changing schools: after a move, restarting socially is hard, and "it kind of can be hard to want to make new friends, fit in," so people "simply... kind of drifted away or faded away."[^2]

3. **They would reframe it as not leaving (rated: moderate).** "Most individuals who no longer say that they're a Latter-day Saint — maybe they wouldn't even frame it 'I left the church.' I just have had a hard time getting back to it after a move, after a severe life experience."[^3] *Steelman:* survey self-reports of "why I left" are subject to social desirability and post-hoc rationalization; a respondent may cite "moving" because it is less confrontational than naming a doctrinal or moral objection.

4. **Pastoral payoff (rated: n/a, normative).** Because the barrier is logistical/relational rather than convictional, reactivation is plausible. See [[Faithkeepers Would Return if a Friend Invited Them]].

## Best Quotes

> "The most common reason they said that they disconnected from religion was their moving or other priorities."[^1]

> "It seems like a lot of individuals simply... kind of drifted away or faded away. They still feel pretty good about church members and church and still have beliefs, but they just kind of disconnected for various life circumstances."[^2]

> "Maybe they wouldn't even frame it 'I left the church.' ... I just have had a hard time getting back to it after a move, after a severe life experience that kind of derailed them."[^3]
