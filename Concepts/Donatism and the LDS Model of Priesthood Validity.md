# Donatism and the LDS Model of Priesthood Validity

## The Claim
The Donatist controversy (does a *wicked* minister's baptisms and ordinations remain valid?) maps onto a question about LDS priesthood. The Catholics rejected Donatism (validity does not depend on the minister's worthiness), and Latter-day Saints likewise reject it — but the LDS model has a distinctive **two-dimensional** structure: a horizontal (person-to-person) transmission and a vertical (God-given) dimension.

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. The Donatist position stated (rating: strong; historical)
Butler in LDS terms: for the Donatists, "if you are a wicked priest, everyone you baptize is not baptized; anyone you ordained, not ordained." Wicked priests break the chain of transmission. [^1]

### 2. Catholics and Latter-day Saints both reject it (rating: strong; comparative)
"We don't look at it the same way... if the missionary who baptized you was secretly dating a girl, are you not baptized? That doesn't actually work." The validity of an ordinance does not hinge on the secret sins of the officiant — the crooked-cop analogy: a corrupt officer's lawful arrests are not all retroactively void. [^2]

### 3. The LDS two-dimensional model (rating: moderate; Butler's systematic proposal)
Butler distinguishes: a **horizontal** dimension — priesthood passed person to person, "something like an invitation or an instruction"; and a **vertical** dimension — "real priesthood comes and goes vertically," and per [[D&C 121.39|D&C 121:37-39]] God *withdraws* it when one aspires to power or exercises unrighteous dominion. Validity is finally God's call, "not your decision." [^3]

### 4. The Alma-the-Elder test case (rating: moderate; Book of Mormon)
Alma the Elder was ordained by the wicked king Noah ("a wine-bibbing, philandering, murderous toad"), yet later exercises real priesthood and is told by Mosiah he can run the whole church. This shows the vertical dimension can validate an ordination whose horizontal origin was corrupt — exactly opposite to Donatism. [^4]

### 5. Steelman of the opposing view
A strict-succession view (and [[Bruce R. McConkie]]'s "keys" argument, see [[Apostasy Was Complete When the Keys Departed]]) holds that authority *is* a transmitted thing that can be wholly lost: once the keys depart, no later ordination — however sincere — is valid, which is precisely why a restoration was needed. The Donatist instinct (purity matters for transmission) thus contains a truth the LDS apostasy narrative relies on. Butler's model threads the needle: ordinance *validity* doesn't depend on the officiant's worthiness, but the *priesthood/keys* themselves can be vertically withdrawn by God from an entire church — which is how both "the missionary's sin doesn't void your baptism" and "the apostasy removed authority" can be true at once.

## Best Quotes

> "For Donatists, if you're a wicked priest, everyone you baptize is not baptized... We don't accept Donatism." [^1]

> "Priesthood has a horizontal dimension passed from person to person... real priesthood comes and goes vertically. Section 121 says it can be taken." [^3]

> "Alma seems to get the priesthood... so there seems to be a vertical dimension to priesthood." [^4]
