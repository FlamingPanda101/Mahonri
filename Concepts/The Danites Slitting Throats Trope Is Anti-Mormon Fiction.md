# The Danites Slitting Throats Trope Is Anti-Mormon Fiction

## The Point
A staple of 19th-century anti-Mormon literature holds that life in Utah meant constant mortal danger: utter "a single word of defiance to Brigham Young" and the Danites would slit your throat, or you would be secretly "blood-atoned." Dr. [[Daniel C. Peterson]] argues this image is largely fiction — its own anecdotes collapse on inspection, and the best frontier-violence scholarship shows Utah was no more violent than comparable territories.

## Evidence
- **The horror stories debunk themselves.** Critics point to someone supposedly "blood-atoned by Brigham Young" who turns up alive "10 years later in California." The classic case is Ann Eliza Webb Young, who (in her own telling, mocked by Hugh Nibley) fled across the Wyoming border, climbed down from her carriage, and kissed the ground for having escaped — yet she only "could almost in my mind hear the hooves of the Danites' horses." As Nibley quipped, "You think the Danites cared about the Wyoming border?" She "never actually hears any Danites coming after" — the threat exists only "in her mind."[^1]
- **It was frontier life, not a uniquely Mormon reign of terror.** Utah did have rough "frontier violence" and some "judicial and extra-judicial killings" — Peterson does not deny the Wild West was real. But the point is comparative: enforcing law was hard everywhere because "communication lines weren't that strong," and the lone town sheriff "was usually just barely better than the outlaws." You couldn't "dial 911 and get the police there in 5 minutes."[^2]
- **The scholarly verdict: no worse than its neighbors.** Peterson cites Craig Foster's Interpreter article on violence in Utah: the study "shows that under Brigham Young, it's no more violent than anywhere else — in New Mexico, in California, in these other places." Whatever frontier violence occurred "wasn't Brigham Young's fault."[^3]

## Best Quotes
> "Nibley says, 'You think the Danites cared about the Wyoming border?'... It's instructive that she only hears the Danites' heartbeats in her mind. She never actually hears any Danites coming after." [^1]

> "Craig Foster's study shows that under Brigham Young, it's no more violent than anywhere else — in New Mexico, in California, in these other places." [^3]

> "And you don't just dial 911 and get the police there in 5 minutes." [^2]

## LDS Connection
The throat-slitting Danite is the lurid endpoint of the "Brigham controlled everything" caricature — see [[Brigham Young Was Not the Autocrat of Utah]] — and the same exaggeration underwrites the charge that he ordered Mountain Meadows (see [[Brigham Young Did Not Order the Mountain Meadows Massacre]]). Reading the frontier on its own terms, rather than through a sensationalist anti-Mormon lens, is the corrective; compare [[Frontier People Were In Touch With Reality]].
