# Love Cannot Be Coerced or Created

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
On Calvinism, God causally determines all things, including the love creatures render to him — yet this is still genuine love and worship directed to a loving God. [^1] See [[The Calvinist God Cannot Love Because He Controls Everything]].

## The Rebuttal
1. **Coerced love is a contradiction in terms** — rate: strong, near-analytic. [[Blake Ostler]]: "The God of Calvinism isn't free... He can't really love at all because love can't be coerced. It can't be determined causally by another. It can't be a matter of predestined causal determination. Love isn't even possible in that environment." *(conceptual)* [^2]
2. **Determined love is God loving himself** — rate: the core reductio. If God causes the creature's love, "God would simply be creating whatever love exists by giving it to himself." Worship in a Calvinist universe is "really just God creating love for himself through us. That's a narcissism." *(reductio)* [^3]
3. **Love requires a real possibility of not-loving** — rate: the libertarian premise, stated plainly. "It has to be a full-blooded choice, where there's possibility of not loving. In fact, that's what we're doing here — we're learning how to love." [[Jacob Hansen]] adds the logical edge: "you can't create love; love has to be given by the beloved." *(metaphysical)* [^4]
4. **Connects to the creation argument** — the same principle drives Ostler's claim that [[Creation Ex Nihilo Entails Occasionalism and Precludes Free Love|creation ex nihilo precludes free love]], since a moment-to-moment recreated creature contributes nothing uncaused. *(systematic link)* [^5]

**Steelman of the compatibilist Calvinist:** Reformed compatibilism (Jonathan Edwards, *Freedom of the Will*) denies that freedom requires the libertarian "power to do otherwise." We act freely when we act *from our own strongest desires*, even if God ordains those desires; coercion means acting *against* one's will, which is not what regeneration does — God changes the *heart* so the person *wants* to love him, then loves willingly. So determined love is not coerced love. Ostler's rejoinder: if God *installs* the very desire that produces the love, the loop still closes on God — the creature adds nothing of its own, so it remains God eliciting love from himself, however "willing" the puppet feels.
