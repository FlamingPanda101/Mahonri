# Two Churches Are Symbolic Good and Evil Not Denominations

## The Claim
When the Evangelical wields [[1 Nephi 14.10|1 Nephi 14:10]] — "there are save two churches only… the church of the Lamb of God, and… the church of the devil… whoso belongeth not to the church of the Lamb… belongeth to that great church, which is the mother of abominations" — as proof that the Book of Mormon damns every non-LDS denomination as satanic, Hayden Carroll answers that the **two-churches language is not literal and not denominational**. It is a symbolic good-versus-evil dichotomy embedded in [[1 Nephi 8|Lehi's tree-of-life vision]] (interpreted for [[Nephi]] in chapter 14): "the church of the Lamb" is the whole company aligned with God, "the great and abominable church" is the whole company aligned with the devil — not the Catholics, not the Protestants, not any congregation. Hayden notes (a) the Church officially **rebuked** the older denomination-specific reading "in the 1980s," (b) Latter-day Saints **do not use this chapter in their apologetics**, and (c) in 600 BC [[Nephi]] belonged to no church or synagogue at all, so the angel cannot be ranking congregations that did not yet exist.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence (steelman)

### 1. The genre is vision-allegory, not ecclesiastical taxonomy — rate: contextually strong
1 Nephi 14 is the angelic interpretation of the symbolic [[1 Nephi 8|tree-of-life dream]], a text saturated with figurative imagery (rod, mist, great-and-spacious building). Reading "two churches" as a literal census of denominations is a category error about the passage's genre; "by necessity… God is good, the devil is evil" yields exactly two moral camps.[^2] Compare [[The Narrative Reading of Scripture vs Proof-Texting]].

### 2. The official-reading shift is real and disarms the gotcha — rate: fair
Hayden concedes earlier LDS leaders *did* read it denomination-specifically and were corrected; the modern Church does not teach that the great-and-abominable church is any particular communion.[^3] The Latter-day Saint position is therefore *more* charitable than [[Joseph Smith History 1.68-69|the First Vision's "all their creeds are an abomination"]] is often taken to be — Hayden insists Mormons "actually love the other churches… as long as they're not causing harm," are "grateful for the Reformation" and the church fathers.[^4] See [[Catholicism Does Not Preach a False Gospel]].

### 3. The 600 BC setting blocks the literal reading — rate: strong internal point
At the dramatic date, [[Nephi]] is "in the wilderness," attending no congregation; the angel is not telling him "your church is of God and every other church is of the devil," because there is no other church on the scene.[^5] This is a clean internal-coherence argument that does not depend on later magisterial correction.

### Steelman of the Evangelical rebuttal — rate: this is the genuine tension
Kelly's strongest replies are documentary, not merely rhetorical. (a) **Modern revelation cuts the other way:** [[Doctrine and Covenants 132|D&C]] / Joseph Smith's [[Joseph Smith History 1.68-69|First Vision]] has the divine speaker call *all* the denominations "wrong," their creeds "an abomination," their professors "corrupt" — language Hayden concedes is "still true… on many of their core doctrines."[^6] If the Restoration narrative itself indicts every other church's *doctrine*, the symbolic reading of 1 Nephi 14 does not soften the exclusivity; it relocates it. (b) **Leader-versus-leader:** that earlier prophets taught the literal reading and were later overruled is, on Kelly's framing, prophets contradicting prophets — the very instability the LDS charge against Protestant schism. (c) **No modern apostolic citation** establishing the symbolic reading was produced on the spot when Kelly asked for one.[^7] The Latter-day Saint rejoinder is that "wrong on doctrine / lacking saving authority" is categorically different from "demonically possessed and bound for hell," which is the equivalence Kelly's [[When Catholics Argue Like Jews - The Demonic Charge Is Unfalsifiable|"demonic" charge]] requires.

## Best Quotes
> "The language of the two churches is not literal… we would say it's a symbolism of good versus evil… God is good, the devil is evil. This is not referring to particular denominations."[^1]

> "What church is Nephi a part of? … He's not part of a church. That's why this is not denomination specific."[^5]

> "We're not going around saying other people are demonically inspired… We actually take a stance that says we love the other churches and what they're doing."[^4]
