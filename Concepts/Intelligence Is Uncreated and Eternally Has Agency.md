# Intelligence Is Uncreated and Eternally Has Agency

#type/claim

## The Claim

[[D&C 93|D&C 93]] teaches that "intelligence" — the irreducible core of a person — was never created and has always possessed some form of agency. [[Casey Paul Griffiths]] makes this the philosophical engine of the [[King Follett Discourse]]: God is an *organizer* of eternal materials, not a creator from nothing.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The canonical text (strong).** D&C 93:29–30: "Man was also in the beginning with God. Intelligence, or the light of truth, was not created or made, neither indeed can be... all truth is independent in that sphere in which God has placed it to act for itself, as all intelligence also; otherwise there is no existence."[^2] Griffiths says only two things are *clearly* established about intelligence here — it cannot be created or made, and it has some form of agency — and warns against over-speculating beyond that (citing Cleon Skousen as an example of overreach).[^3]
2. **Eternal matter too (strong, textual).** D&C 93:33: "The elements are eternal, and spirit and element, inseparably connected, receive a fulness of joy." So both spirit-substance and matter pre-exist; God organizes rather than originates the universe.[^4]
3. **Section 93 as anomaly (contextual).** Griffiths notes 93 is "a total aberration" — it appears without the usual historical setup, and its prologue parallels [[John 1.1|John 1]] but adds material suggesting the source is John the Baptist, who "saw that he [Christ] grew from grace to grace," language then turned onto us: "if you keep my commandments you shall grow grace for grace."[^5]
4. **Ex nihilo is the contrast class.** This "runs against all the Christian thought of his day and ours," which inherited creation *ex nihilo* — a doctrine Griffiths (echoing standard LDS apologetics) calls non-biblical, arguing the Hebrew of Genesis describes God *organizing* an earth (see [[Creation Ex Nihilo Is Not Biblical]]).[^6]
5. **Steelman of the opposing view.** Classical theists reply that *ex nihilo* is precisely what secures God's aseity and ultimacy: if intelligence and matter are co-eternal and independent, God is no longer the absolute source of all being but one organizer among eternal givens — a metaphysically smaller God. They also note D&C 93's "intelligence" is famously underdetermined (is it a substance? a person? a property?), so the doctrine's philosophical payoff rests on contested exegesis. Griffiths concedes the term invites speculation and that the church deliberately limits its claims to the two established points.

## Best Quotes

> "Intelligence or the light of truth was not created or made, neither indeed can be... behold, here is the agency of man."[^7]

> "The two things that are clearly established here about intelligence is that it can't be created or made and that it has some form of agency."[^8]

> "Everything in the universe has always existed, and that God is an organizer. This runs against all the Christian thought of his day and ours."[^9]
