# The Christian Origin of Equality

## The Claim

[[Glenn Scrivener]] argues human equality is a fundamentally Christian — and ultimately biblical — idea, grounded in the *imago Dei* of [[Genesis 1.26-27|Genesis 1:26-27]] and the unity-in-Christ of [[Galatians 3.28|Galatians 3:28]], then institutionally embodied through the church's radical mixing of social strata.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **A secular witness: [[Yuval Noah Harari]].** Harari argues equality is "a fundamentally biblical idea, generated from a belief in God and souls" — and warns that without God and souls, the grounding dissolves. See [[Equality Loses Its Grounding Without God and Souls]].[^1] *(Strong rhetorically — a non-believer concedes the genealogy.)*

2. **Institutional embodiment in the early church.** In a Greco-Roman world rigidly segregated by male/female, slave/free, barbarian/citizen, Christians ate one bread together across strata, exchanged the "holy kiss" (commanded ~5x in the NT), and were told "the image of God is one another" — serving God by serving one another. See [[Institutionalization Is the Unique Christian Contribution]].[^2] *(Strong — the social leveling of early Christian assemblies is historically attested and genuinely countercultural.)*

3. **Why not Platonic ideals?** Scrivener: because Christianity grounds equality in *incarnation* and *the body of Christ* — Christ becoming man means all humans share an identical nature ("Jesus of Nazareth became man for all, therefore all humans are saved in the man Christ Jesus"). This embeds the value rather than leaving it abstract.[^3] *(Strong — explains the institutionalizing impulse theologically.)*

4. **Steelman of O'Connor — equality "between whom"?** O'Connor presses that "equality" is context-dependent: racial, gender, societal-standing equality. [[Thomas Jefferson|Jefferson held 600 slaves]] as he wrote "all men are created equal." Modern *gender* equality (equal societal standing) has no biblical embryo and is indebted to feminism. See [[Modern Gender Equality Is Not Grounded in the Bible]].[^4] *(Strong — "equal worth before God" (no one should be killed) is not "equal societal standing," and the Bible affirms the former far more clearly than the latter.)*

5. **Scrivener's nuance — equality *and* difference.** Scrivener insists the Christian view affirms both "ontological equality" and real "difference" of male and female — the modern culture war is precisely the loss of that pairing.[^5] *(A live theological position, contested by egalitarians; relevant to [[Galatians 3-28 Is a Theological Reality Not a Rights Charter]].)*
