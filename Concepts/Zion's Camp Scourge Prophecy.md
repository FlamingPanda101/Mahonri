# Zion's Camp Scourge Prophecy

## The Claim
On 3 June 1834, amid Zion's Camp, [[Joseph Smith]] gathered the men and prophesied (per the *History of the Church*) "that a scourge would come upon the camp in consequence of the fractious and unruly spirits... and they should die like sheep with the rot. Still, if they would repent and humble themselves before the Lord, the scourge in a great measure might be turned away." A cholera outbreak soon struck the camp; many fell sick and some died.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The cholera outbreak is historical.** Zion's Camp did suffer a deadly cholera epidemic shortly after, fitting the predicted "scourge." (Evidence rating: the *outcome* is real.)[^1]
2. **Conditional structure makes it hard to falsify.** The scourge would come "but... in a great measure might be turned away" if they repented — so both a severe and a mild outbreak count as confirmation. (Evidence rating: weak as evidence; strong as an [[Apologetic vs Evidence Distinction for Prophecy|apologetic device]].)[^2]
3. **Plausible natural read.** The host observes "there was a lot of contention on the travel" over ~800 miles, and disease outbreaks among exhausted, crowded travelers were common — making both the prediction and the outcome unremarkable in their setting. (Evidence rating: strong natural deflation.)[^2]
4. **Steelman of the believing view.** The pointed linkage — internal "unruly" contention followed promptly by lethal disease, framed in advance as consequence — gave the episode lasting didactic force for the Saints. If Joseph genuinely named the scourge before cholera appeared, the timing is notable even if the mechanism (disease on a long march) was natural. The host stays "open to that could have been an actual prophecy given."

## Best Quotes
> "A scourge would come upon the camp in consequence of the fractious and unruly spirits that appeared among them, and they should die like sheep with the rot."[^1]

> "Still, if they would repent and humble themselves before the Lord, the scourge in a great measure might be turned away."[^1]
