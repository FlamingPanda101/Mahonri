# James — "Different Kind of Faith" Reply Rebutted

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
Protestants ([[John MacArthur]], [[James White]]) say James condemns a *dead / false* faith, not true saving faith — so James 2 doesn't touch *sola fide*.

## The Rebuttal

### 1. James never speaks of two kinds of faith
Throughout the letter, faith is always good; what is bad is faith **missing works**. He never contrasts "true" vs "false" faith [^1].

### 2. The Greek won't bear "that faith"
James 2:14 has *hē pistis* — "**the** faith" or simply "faith," not "*that* faith." The KJV rightly reads "Can faith save him?" Translations like NIV ("such faith") and ESV ("that faith") smuggle in the interpretation [^2].

### 3. The substitution test
Replace "faith" with "dead/false faith" elsewhere in the chapter and it becomes nonsense ("show me your *dead faith*..."). James talks about faith **in two situations** (with works / without works), not two kinds [^3].

### 4. Scholarly concession
Even Protestant scholar [[Scot McKnight]]: "the bottom line for James is having works." [[Jimmy Akin]]: "the faith isn't the problem — it's being *alone* that's the problem." Works give faith life as the spirit gives the body life (James 2:26); cf. [[Galatians 5.6|Galatians 5:6]] "faith working through love" [^4].

## Best Quote
> "No matter how hard we Protestants might try to work this out, the bottom line for James is having works." — [[Scot McKnight]] [^4]
