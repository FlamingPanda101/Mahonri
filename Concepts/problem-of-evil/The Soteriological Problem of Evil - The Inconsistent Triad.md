# The Soteriological Problem of Evil - The Inconsistent Triad

#type/claim

## The Claim

[[David Paulsen]] identifies a second, distinct problem of evil — the **soteriological** problem. The New Testament teaches that salvation comes "only through Christ" ([[John 14.6]]; [[Acts 4.12]]), yet millions lived and died never having heard of Him. Paulsen frames the tension as an **inconsistent triad**: three individually compelling premises, any two of which seem to deny the third. [^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The triad.** (1) *God is perfectly loving and just and desires that all His children be saved.* (2) *Salvation comes only in and through one's acceptance of Christ.* (3) *Millions of God's children have lived and died without ever hearing of Christ.* Premise 3 is indisputable, forcing us — it seems — to give up either 1 or 2, both biblically warranted. [^2]
2. **The two standard escapes both fail.** **Universalists** keep premise 1 but must deny the explicit NT teaching (premise 2). **Exclusivists** keep premise 2 but conclude the unevangelized are lost, which they cannot square with premise 1. "Neither view is satisfactory." [^3]
3. **Morris's "scandal."** [[Thomas V. Morris]] (Notre Dame), in *The Logic of God Incarnate*, calls it a "scandal": how can a just God set up a condition of salvation "inaccessible to most people"? Is God's love not better understood as universal than as "limited to a mediation through the one particular individual, Jesus of Nazareth"? [^4]
4. **Davis's "Oohku."** [[Stephen T. Davis]] (Claremont) dramatizes it with **Oohku**, a woman in the interior of Borneo, 370–320 B.C., who "couldn't have" heard of Christ — "born in the wrong place and at the wrong time." Is it right for God to condemn her to hell? "Of course not... God is just and loving." [^5]

## Best Quotes

> "How could a just God set up a particular condition of salvation, the highest end of human life possible, which was and is inaccessible to most people?" — Thomas Morris [^4]

> "Is it right for God to condemn this woman to eternal hell just because she was never able to come to God through Christ? Of course not." — Stephen Davis [^5]
