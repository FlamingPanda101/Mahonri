# Permitting Is Just a Softer Word for Choosing

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
A third defense leans on the scholastic distinction between God *willing* something and God *permitting* it: "God doesn't send people to hell; he just allows it." The damnation is a permission, not an act, so God is not its author.

## The Rebuttal
1. **Permission only exonerates when you lack the power to stop it — rate: strong.** The drowning analogy: if I can't swim and watch someone drown, "I allowed it" is a real defense. But if I am a perfect swimmer standing on the shore, watching someone I *know* can't swim — someone *I* put in the water — "I just allowed it" doesn't cut it [^1]. The willing/permitting distinction does moral work only under limitation; for an omnipotent, omniscient creator who *also chose to create the person*, the distinction collapses [^2]. **Steelman of the theist:** the doctrine of double effect and the intend/foresee distinction are genuine in human ethics — a surgeon who foresees a patient may die is not a murderer. A defender argues God *intends* the good of free creatures and only *permits*, without intending, the misuse of freedom; permitting evil for a sufficient reason (preserving genuine agency) is not the same as authoring it. The video's swimmer adds the crucial disanalogy that God *placed the person in the water*, which the intend/foresee defense must answer — and arguably cannot for *ex nihilo* creation.
2. **Total knowledge plus total power equals authorship — rate: strong.** "When you have that kind of knowledge and that kind of power, permission is just a softer word for choice. The button was still pressed" [^2]. The argument folds the permitting defense back into [[Five Divine Attributes Cannot All Be True]]: omnipotence and omniscience leave no gap between permitting and choosing.
3. **It is the same upstream-responsibility move.** As in [[Free Will Cannot Justify the Decision to Create]], the decisive fact is the creative act; "permission" relabels but does not relocate responsibility.

## Best Quotes
> "If I'm a perfect swimmer standing on the shore watching someone drown that I know for a fact can't swim and someone that I put in the water, I just allowed it doesn't cut it." [^1]

> "When you have that kind of knowledge and that kind of power, permission is just a softer word for choice. The button was still pressed." [^2]
