# The Problem of Hell and Creation

## The Claim
Eternal conscious torment is irreconcilable with a perfectly loving and just God **especially given creation from nothing**. If God conjures each soul from nothing with its exact attributes and sustains it at every moment, he is ultimately responsible for the atheist's unbelief or the thief's theft — so damning them is unjust. This is a species of gratuitous evil immune to the standard theodicies.

## Supporting Evidence
- Standard theodicies say evil serves a greater good (virtues, free will). But [[Jonathan Kvanvig]] notes the problem of hell "leads nowhere" — no future good ever offsets it; it is "the paradigm of a truly pointless, gratuitous suffering" *(cited philosopher)* [^1].
- The **roller-coaster / created-person thought experiment**: if you conjure objects (and people) from nothing with whatever attributes you will, and sustain them, you own the outcomes — so God owns ours *(thought experiment)* [^2].
- [[Isaac of Nineveh]]: "It is not the way of a compassionate maker to create rational beings in order to deliver them over mercilessly to unending affliction... aware of how they would turn out when he created them" *(patristic source)* [^3].

## Best Quote
> "If God is ultimately responsible for the sins or attributes or actions of each creature, then why is he justified in sending each of us to eternal torment? And why didn't he just decide to create each of us differently?" [^2]
