# Greek Philosophy Corrupted the Christian Concept of God

## The Claim
The decisive doctrinal corruption of the apostasy was the **Hellenization of the doctrine of God**: 2nd–4th century apologists married Christian scripture to Greek (Platonic/Aristotelian) metaphysics, transforming the embodied, passionate, family-headed God of the Bible into an immaterial, impassible, abstract Absolute. This is the thesis of [[Richard Hopkins]]'s *How Greek Philosophy Corrupted the Christian Concept of God*.

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. The apologists deliberately used Greek categories as an evangelistic hook (rating: strong; well-documented)
To reach Greek intellectuals who found a crucified Messiah absurd (Day's "David Koresh executed by Janet Reno" analogy; the [[Celsus]] quote that only the ignorant accept Christianity), apologists argued the Greek *logos* foreshadowed Christ. [[John 1.1|John 1:1]] ("In the beginning was the *Logos*") became the bridge: identify the philosophers' cosmic Reason with Jesus. [[Justin Martyr]] said pre-Christian philosophers were "Christians before Christ"; [[Origen]] and Clement called philosophy a "schoolmaster to bring us to Christ." [^1]

### 2. A traceable chain of borrowed ideas (rating: moderate; intellectual history)
Day sketches: **Parmenides** (change/motion are illusion; sensory world unreal — "father of metaphysics") → **Heraclitus** (*logos* as cosmic reason) → **Plato** (the world of Forms; the cave allegory) → **Aristotle** (the "unmoved mover," pure actuality without matter). The apologists then map these onto God. See [[Unmoved Mover Reshaped God the Father]]. [^2]

### 3. This produced a God at war with scripture (rating: moderate; theological)
Greek metaphysics demands an immaterial, transcendent, impassible God "everywhere, part of the *nous* or the *logos*." But the biblical Yahweh "has passions... gets angry... smells the sacrifice," and Jesus is embodied. The "synretism" (syncretism) of the two yields the *homoousios* God of Nicaea, contradicting [[D&C 130.20-21|D&C 130:22]] ("the Father has a body of flesh and bones"). [^3]

### 4. Day verified Hopkins after learning Greek (rating: moderate; testimonial)
Day read Hopkins ~30 years before graduate school, then "did a deep dive on whether his arguments are valid" after studying Greek, and "so far I'm not finding fault in his arguments." [[Barry Bickmore]]'s *Restoring the Ancient Church* documents pre-Nicene fathers holding more "LDS-like" views before the Hellenizing settlement. [^4]

### 5. Steelman of the opposing view
Defenders argue the apologists did not *corrupt* the doctrine of God but *translated* it — using the best available conceptual vocabulary to articulate what scripture already taught about God's transcendence, aseity, and unity (cf. "I AM," Mal 3:6 "I change not"). The immateriality and impassibility of God, they say, are biblically grounded (John 4:24, "God is spirit"), not Greek imports; and identifying Christ with the *Logos* is John's own move, not Justin's invention. Even Day concedes John "is doing this" first and offers a striking steelman that the Hellenization may have *preserved* the faith (see [[Apostasy Preserved the Text Steelman]]).

## Best Quotes

> "How Greek philosophy corrupted the Christian concept of God... what this book is doing is it shows how these thinkers are trying to make Christianity look acceptable." [^1]

> "Greek philosophy deals in this idea of an immaterial, abstract, transcendent god that's everywhere... How do I marry that transcendent god with an embodied god?" [^3]

> "This is called syncretism... there are religious ideas that meet and then they take a little bit from each other." [^3]
