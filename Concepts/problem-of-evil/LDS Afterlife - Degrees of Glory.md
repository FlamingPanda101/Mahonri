# LDS Afterlife — Degrees of Glory (Resolving the Problem of Hell)

## The Claim
LDS theology dissolves the [[Problem of Hell and Creation]] because souls were **not created from nothing** in a depraved state — they always existed in a primitive form. God offered a plan of mortal probation and progression; there is no binary heaven/hell, but **degrees of glory**, each "incomprehensibly happy."

## Supporting Evidence
- Souls "have always existed, but in some kind of primitive state... not created from nothing to be depraved" — God took them and proposed a plan to learn love, forgiveness, and to become as God is *(LDS framework)* [^1].
- "Each person will receive the degree of glory that is as good as they are willing to receive"; those with unfair mortal circumstances get **post-mortal opportunity** to repent, forgive, and receive Christ's grace [^2].
- God remains *just* (requirements must still be met) but not cruel — he "doesn't close the door on your progress before it is completely over." No eternal conscious torment that serves no purpose [^3].

## Best Quote
> "There is no binary heaven or hell dichotomy. Rather, there is varying degrees of glory that are all said to be incomprehensibly happy and good." [^2]
