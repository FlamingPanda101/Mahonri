# Hell — The "Wind Up and Go" (Watchmaker) Defense Rebutted

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
God creates everything from nothing, "winds it up" like a watchmaker, then lets it run — so he isn't responsible for how it turns out.

## The Rebuttal
- The analogy is disanalogous: God would have to create the **materials** of the watch *and* the **universal constants** (gravity, entropy, physical law) from nothing and sustain them — so any malfunction traces back to him [^1].
- [[Jonathan Kvanvig]]: the analogy is "defective to the core," because a real watchmaker exploits a *pre-existing* structured universe — which God would himself have to create and sustain [^2].
- [[Gregory of Nyssa]]: creation from nothing is not merely metaphysical but a **moral claim** about God. [[David Bentley Hart]]: "the moral destiny of all creation and the moral nature of God are absolutely inseparable" — so a freely creating, all-loving God and an eternal hell cannot be reconciled [^3].

## Best Quote
> "Attempting to avoid the implications of creation from nothing is a hopeless project." [^3]
