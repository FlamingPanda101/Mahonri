# Pre-Existence Solves the Problem of Evil

## The Claim
[[Joe Heschmeyer]] argues LDS pre-existence theology is a *liability*: because Mormons believe in uncreated, co-eternal intelligences, "God is just kind of powerless too" and not morally responsible for evil. He frames the *Catholic* position as superior. The panel inverts this — pre-existence is precisely what *resolves* the problem of evil that creation ex nihilo creates.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Ex nihilo makes God author of every evil disposition (rated: strong, internal-logic).** [[Luke Irwin]] lays out Heschmeyer's own admission: under classical theism, since God created everyone from nothing without their consent, "everyone created by God did not consent to be created, and so God is morally responsible for the evils in the world." If all of a person's "desires, predilections, circumstances, motivations" trace back wholly to a God who made them ex nihilo, then "when somebody goes to hell, that's not you — that's God."[^2] See [[Creation Ex Nihilo Leads to Determinism]] and [[Problem of Hell and Creation]].

2. **Pre-existence introduces genuine agency God did not author (rated: strong, theological).** Because LDS intelligences are *co-eternal* and *consented* to mortal existence, "God didn't just pick you to be the type of person that's not going to convert" — the self that chooses is not a divine artifact. "That solves a lot of the issues." Irwin stresses this targets "the problem people actually care about" — why there is suffering and why people go to hell — not the abstract end of the boxcar chain.[^3] See [[Free Will - Atheist Determinism vs LDS Co-Eternality]].

3. **Heschmeyer's own framing concedes the force (rated: moderate, dialectical).** Irwin notes that Matt Fradd "doesn't even register this problem" — he moves on to joking about naming the LDS God "Jeff" — which Irwin reads as evidence the interlocutors did not grasp how serious a theodicy advantage pre-existence claims to be.[^4] *Steelman:* a classical theist has real replies the panel does not engage — (a) divine permission of freely chosen evil need not make God its *author* (the free-will defense plus the Creator/creature distinction); (b) pre-existence merely *relocates* the question (why did God organize *these* free intelligences into a world where He foreknew many would be lost?); and (c) co-eternal matter/agency arguably *limits* God's sovereignty, which is itself a theological cost. The LDS claim is comparative, not a knockdown: pre-existence trades classical omnipotence for a cleaner theodicy, and the panel argues that trade is worth it.

## Best Quotes

> "When somebody goes to hell, that's not you, that's God — because nothing about you came from somewhere outside of God. So he's responsible. This is the problem of evil. This is one people actually care about."[^2]

> "The fact that you consented to be here, and that God didn't just pick you to be the type of person that's not going to convert — it's you. That solves a lot of the issues."[^3]

> "Whereas in Mormonism, God's not morally responsible for the evils in the world."[^1]
