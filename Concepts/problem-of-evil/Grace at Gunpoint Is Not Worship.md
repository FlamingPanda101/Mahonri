# Grace at Gunpoint Is Not Worship

## The Point
Reacting to the **I** and **P** of TULIP — *irresistible grace* and *perseverance of the saints* — the panel coins the breakdown's sharpest line: **"Grace at gunpoint is irresistible grace."** If salvation is imposed without the creature's consent and cannot be resisted, then the resulting "worship" is not worship and the resulting "love" is not love, because both require a free response. The corollary argument is that irresistible grace and perseverance are logically *incompatible with repentance itself*: if your final state was fixed from eternity, there is no real turning, no genuine watchfulness, and the apparent convert who later falls away "never really was saved." The Latter-day Saint counter is that grace can be *forfeited* — a doctrine stated in the very first revealed section of the D&C.

## Evidence
1. **The coinage.** "Grace at gunpoint is irresistible grace. That's what it is." Imposed salvation voids the moral category it claims to fulfill. [^1]
2. **Coerced worship is not worship.** "It's not worship unless it's willing. If it's irresistible, it's not true worship... So you can't worship God actually in their theology. It's not real. It doesn't mean anything." Without free will "there's no purpose to anything." [^2] See [[Love Cannot Be Coerced or Created]].
3. **Irresistible grace + perseverance cancel repentance.** "If you believe in the thing called repentance, then you cannot believe in the perseverance of the saints and irresistible grace." The system makes repentance illusory: the elect were always elect, the wicked always wicked. [^3]
4. **The unfalsifiable "never really saved" move.** The panel cites a former Calvinist who once testified he *knew* he was elect, then left the faith — on Calvinist terms, "he was predestined to say those things falsely, and then to leave and be wicked." Assurance becomes retroactively unknowable, which the panel reads as self-refuting. [^4]
5. **The canonical rebuttal: grace can be lost.** [[Doctrine and Covenants 20.32-34|D&C 20:32–34]] (April 6, 1830): "there is a possibility that man may fall from grace and depart from the living God. Therefore let the church take heed and pray always... even let those who are sanctified take heed also." No one is beyond grace, but no one is beyond the *need* for watchfulness. [^5]

## Best Quotes
> "Grace at gunpoint is irresistible grace. That's what it is." [^1]

> "It's not worship unless it's willing. If it's irresistible, it's not true worship... it doesn't mean anything." [^2]

> "If you believe in the thing called repentance, then you cannot believe in the perseverance of the saints and irresistible grace." [^3]

## LDS Connection
This applies the broader principle that [[Love Cannot Be Coerced or Created|love cannot be coerced]] to the soteriological end of TULIP: salvation that bypasses consent is not the loving relationship the gospel describes but reprogramming. Latter-day Saint grace is [[Grace Is Unconditional Love Not an Earned Wage|unconditional love freely accepted]], not an irresistible decree — "it's a gift that we accept," not "something you're either forced to do or forced not to do." The same logic underwrites [[An Omnipotent God Cannot Force a Free Creature to Choose Good]]: even omnipotence cannot manufacture a free act of love or worship.
