# Hell as a Greater Good Treats People as Tools

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
A second defense: maybe hell serves a greater purpose — maybe hell "makes heaven meaningful," or some suffering is necessary for a bigger plan. The damned are a regrettable but necessary cost of a greater good.

## The Rebuttal
1. **It instrumentalizes persons — rate: strong, Kantian.** If some people are created to fail so others can succeed, those lives are reduced to *tools* for someone else's benefit. We reject this reasoning everywhere else: "I ruined this person's life, but it helped five other people" is condemned as *using* people, not praised as good [^1]. This is a clean appeal to the principle that persons must never be treated merely as means. **Steelman of the theist:** the soul-making theodicist denies the damned are *mere* means — each person is created as an end with a genuine chance at salvation, and the "greater good" is the existence of free moral agents, not a transaction in which some are spent for others. The video's parallel assumes the damned are *deliberately* created *in order to* benefit others, which a careful greater-good theorist would reject as a caricature.
2. **No benefit-to-others ratio licenses the harm — rate: strong.** The video's stronger form: *no amount* of benefit to others makes it acceptable to deliberately bring a person into existence foreknowing their life ends in permanent suffering [^2]. This denies that aggregating welfare can outweigh the deontological wrong of creating a doomed person — a rejection of consequentialist theodicy. **Steelman:** a defender can argue the relevant good is *internal* to the doomed person's own existence (the gift of being, of free agency), not merely external benefit to others, which sidesteps the "tools" charge.
3. **Consistency with everyday moral judgment — rate: framing.** "We reject that logic everywhere else. Why would we accept it from God?" — the rhetorical engine is that exempting God from ordinary moral reasoning is special pleading, the same move attacked in [[The Appeal to Mystery Empties the Word Good of Meaning]].

## Best Quotes
> "It means some people are created to fail so that others can succeed. It means that some lives are basically tools, instruments for someone else's benefit. That's not love." [^1]

> "If someone said, 'I ruined this person's life, but it helped five other people,' we wouldn't call that good. We would call it using people." [^1]

> "No amount of benefit to others makes it okay to deliberately bring someone into existence knowing their life ends in permanent suffering." [^2]
