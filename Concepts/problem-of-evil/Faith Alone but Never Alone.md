# "Faith Alone, but Faith Is Never Alone" Rebutted

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
The Protestant formula (Westminster Confession): we are justified by faith *alone*, but that faith is *never* alone — good works *necessarily* and automatically accompany true faith.

## The Rebuttal

### 1. James says justified BY works, not that the justified DO works
James doesn't say "he who is justified does good works"; he says "a man is **justified by** works." Works are part of the cause of (ongoing) justification, not just an inevitable symptom [^1].

### 2. Works are not automatic — free will is required
A person with genuine faith can still give in to temptation and fail to do good (hence Christ said "pray that you may not enter into temptation"). The Council of Trent **condemned** the idea that "works are merely the fruits and signs of justification." James 4:17 makes failing to do good a *sin*, presupposing real choice [^2].

### 3. A properly understood "faith alone" is acceptable
[[Pope Benedict XVI]]: "'faith alone' is true if it is not opposed to faith in charity, in love" — because true faith is conformity to Christ, whose life is love. The line is drawn against [[Norman Geisler|Geisler-style]] claims that even sanctification is "by faith alone," and against antinomianism [^3].

## Best Quote
> "Luther's phrase 'faith alone' is true if it is not opposed to faith in charity, in love." — [[Pope Benedict XVI]] [^3]

## Counter-arguments / related
- Paul's own synergistic language: [[Philippians 2.12-13|Philippians 2:12-13]] ("work out your own salvation").
