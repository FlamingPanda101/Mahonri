# Who Creates the Soul - Humans or God

## The Claim (Objection)
In cross-examination [[Hayden Carroll]] pins down whether God *freely* creates each person, using the soul's origin as the lever. He repeatedly asks whether God is *forced* or *free* in creating a soul at conception. [[Gavin (Catholic Debater)]]'s answers waver: he says the soul "comes from God" and is created "at the moment of conception," yet also that "a human free will... creates that soul" and that ensoulment is "a natural byproduct of two humans procreating" — even in non-consensual conceptions, "God still permits it."[^1]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **The trap and its point** — rate: strong as a logic move. Hayden needs Gavin to affirm premise 2 of [[The Five Incompatible Claims of Creedal Theism]] (God *freely* creates, not compelled). If Gavin says humans/God-is-bound create souls, that flirts with denying divine freedom; if God freely creates each soul, the creation dilemma bites in full. *(elenctic)* [^2]
2. **Gavin eventually concedes the load-bearing point** — pressed ("is God forced or not forced... you've already affirmed God is not forced — he freely chooses, right?"), Gavin answers "right." That concession is what the dilemma requires. *(concession)* [^3]
3. **Catholic creationism (of the soul)** — Gavin's mainstream-Catholic position is *creationism*: God directly creates each rational soul at conception (vs. *traducianism*, soul-from-parents). His muddling of "humans create / God creates" reflects the standard Catholic view that procreation provides the bodily occasion while God infuses the soul — but Hayden exploits the ambiguity to test divine freedom. *(doctrinal-context)* [^1]

**Steelman of the affirmative:** Gavin's considered view is coherent even if poorly articulated live: humans freely cause the *bodily* conception; God, observing that free human act, freely creates and infuses a soul. God is *not compelled* (he could decline), so divine freedom is intact; the "byproduct" language was loose. This actually *grants* Hayden's premise cleanly — God freely creates each person — so the soul-origin detour, while exposing rhetorical fumbling, does not change the dilemma's force either way. Some Catholic thinkers would also note ensoulment-at-conception is the modern magisterial default, so Gavin is doctrinally orthodox here.

## Best Quotes
> "Are you saying God is forced to create spirits by human actions, or is he able to refrain from creating?" — Hayden [^2]

> "You've already affirmed that God is not forced — he freely chooses to, right? — Right." [^3]
