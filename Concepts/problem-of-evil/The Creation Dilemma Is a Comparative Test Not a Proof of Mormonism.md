# The Creation Dilemma Is a Comparative Test Not a Proof of Mormonism

## The Claim

By the close of the discussion, [[Hayden Carroll]] and the Evangelical hosts agree on the dilemma's *epistemic status*: it is **not a proof of Latter-day Saint theology**. It only shows that LDS theology *can answer* the dilemma where creedal theism (especially Calvinism/Catholicism) cannot. The real test is then **comparative**: of the worldviews that can answer it, which is "most parsimonious with ultimate reality and what is really real and truly true." [^1]

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. Both sides explicitly disclaim a proof — rate: strong (mutual concession)

Host: "Assuming your argument holds, it's not really a proof for LDS theology. You're just offering that LDS theology can make sense of [it]." Carroll: "Correct." So even on its own terms the argument is a *defeater* aimed at rival systems, not a positive demonstration of the Restoration. This matches the vault's posture that the Restoration's *theodicy advantage* is comparative, not a standalone proof (see [[A Constrained God Solves the Problem of Evil Better Than Creedal Christianity]]). [^1]

> **Evidence rating:** Strong — both interlocutors state it plainly.

### 2. The follow-up move is parsimony — rate: moderate

Once you list the theologies that can answer the dilemma (LDS finitism, open theism, annihilationism, universalism, Molinist hopeful universalism), "then we would compare... which one is most parsimonious with ultimate reality." The dilemma narrows the field; a separate, harder comparison must then decide among the survivors. [^2]

> **Evidence rating:** Moderate — identifies the next argumentative step without settling it.

### 3. It reframes the debate as worldview-coherence, not point-scoring — rate: moderate

Carroll's broader posture (echoed across his work): "This debate is not about winning arguments" but about which account keeps the word *good* meaningful when applied to God. The dilemma is a stress test for internal coherence; the LDS claim is only that its finitist, premortal-consent theology survives the test (see [[LDS Premortal Consent Removes Gods Culpability]]). [^1]

> **Evidence rating:** Moderate — a methodological framing both sides accept.

## Steelman of the Opposing View

A creedal critic can argue that "LDS theology can answer it" only by paying ruinous prices a comparative test should *penalize*: a non-sovereign God bound by external eternal law, a pre-existent uncreated humanity, and a God who did not create *ex nihilo* — doctrines most of the Christian tradition regards as graver problems than the dilemma itself. On this view, even granting that classical theism must "bite a bullet" somewhere, the bullets the Restoration bites (finite God, eternal matter) are larger, so parsimony with the broader data of revelation and natural theology favors classical theism. Carroll accepts the comparative framing precisely because he thinks the LDS package pays the *smaller* price; the hosts think otherwise — which is why both agree the argument settles nothing by itself and only sets up the real contest.
