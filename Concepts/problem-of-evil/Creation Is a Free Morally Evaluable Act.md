# Creation Is a Free Morally Evaluable Act

## The Claim (Objection)
[[Hayden Carroll]]'s supporting premise for [[The Creation Dilemma]]: in creedal theology God "lacks nothing — not lonely, not incomplete, dependent, or deficient" (divine aseity), so creation is wholly *voluntary*. "He does not have to create anyone. He would lose nothing by refraining." And "whenever an agent freely brings about a significant outcome that did not have to occur, that action becomes morally evaluable." Therefore God's creative act expresses intention and is open to moral assessment.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **Aseity → voluntary creation** — rate: strong, and conceded by Gavin in cross-exam. Classical theism's own doctrine of divine self-sufficiency entails God gains nothing from creating and is not compelled. *(doctrinal)* [^1] Gavin affirms God is not forced/compelled to create any individual. See cross-exam [^2].
2. **Freedom generates responsibility** — moral parallels: a company releasing a defective product knowing it will harm; a government adopting a harmful policy. "Freedom creates responsibility. Because creation is free, it is morally meaningful." *(moral-parallel)* [^3]
3. **Perfect knowledge sharpens it** — unlike human parents (who don't know how a child's life ends), God knows "every sin... every opportunity rejected... and their final outcome" before creating. "Creation is not a gamble for God. He doesn't roll dice." See [[Foreknowledge Plus Power Turns Permission Into Choice]]. *(disanalogy with human procreation)* [^4]

**Steelman of the affirmative:** a classical theist may grant creation is free and morally evaluable yet deny it is *blameworthy*, on grounds that (a) existence + the genuine offer of beatitude is itself a good even for those who refuse it, or (b) the value of a world of free creatures justifies the risk. Hayden rebuts (b) as utilitarian instrumentalizing (see [[The Greater Good Defense Instrumentalizes Persons]]) and (a) via the Judas datum that non-existence can be preferable (see [[The Judas Counterexample]]).

## Best Quotes
> "God does not have to create anyone. He would lose nothing by refraining. Creation is therefore not a necessity. It is a gift... something God freely chooses to do."[^1]

> "When God creates, he is not merely allowing something to happen. He is choosing that it happens. And that choice reflects what he wills."[^3]

> "Creation is not a gamble for God. He doesn't roll dice. He doesn't take chances. He creates with full knowledge of the outcome."[^4]
