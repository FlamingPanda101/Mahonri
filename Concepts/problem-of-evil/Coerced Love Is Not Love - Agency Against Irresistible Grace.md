# Coerced Love Is Not Love - Agency Against Irresistible Grace

## The Point
Against irresistible grace and compatibilist determinism, [[Jacob Hansen]] argues that love is impossible without a genuine choice: "Can love exist without choice? If you cannot choose to love or not, are you free? Or are you a programmed object?" [^1] On Calvinism, he says, "your behavior ultimately flows from your programming, which he will call your nature," and that nature "has to be reprogrammed by God just so that you will love God" — but a reprogrammed love "is that even love at all? Can you make someone love you?" [^1] He marshals Scripture that presupposes resistible grace: "choose ye this day whom ye will serve" (Joshua 24:15), "I have set before you life and death; therefore choose life" (Deut 30:19), "O Jerusalem... how often would I have gathered thy children... and ye would not" (Matt 23:37), and "ye do always resist the Holy Ghost" (Acts 7:51). [^2] [[James White]] answers with John 6:44 ("No one can come to me unless the Father who sent me draws him") and the raising of Lazarus: salvation is God speaking life into the spiritually dead, who no more "cooperate" than a corpse cooperates with "Lazarus, come forth." [^3]

## Evidence
- **Hansen's love-requires-choice argument.** "I always thought that the two greatest commandments were an invitation to enter into a loving relationship... can love exist without choice?" [^1] The premise: relationship is reciprocal — "to have a loving relationship, you have to choose to love him back." [^4]
- **The "imperatives presuppose ability" battery.** Hansen's recurring form: "If people can't choose, why command them? If Christ died for all, then why limit it?... If grace cannot be resisted, why does scripture say that it does?" [^5] He cites God's appeal to Cain ("if thou doest well, shalt thou not be accepted?... thou shouldest rule over [sin]") as proof God expects real choice even from the wicked. [^6]
- **White's Lazarus reply: regeneration precedes faith.** "What did Lazarus have to do to cooperate with Jesus when he raised him from the dead?... He was dead. Don't talk to me about his free will." [^3] On John 6:44 in cross-examination: it is impossible "for those who are in Adam to repent and turn to Christ outside of the work of the Holy Spirit." [^7]
- **White's compatibilist account of love.** Asked whether God can command love, he affirms God both commands it and "grant[s] the ability to love... by the spirit of God releasing us from slavery to sin," and declines on time to define "genuine choice." [^8] This is the compatibilist core also analyzed in [[Calvinism Consistency with Atheism on Free Will]].

## LDS Connection
This is the same fault line the vault treats in [[An Omnipotent God Cannot Force a Free Creature to Choose Good]] and [[Free Will - Atheist Determinism vs LDS Co-Eternality]], here aimed specifically at irresistible grace. The Restoration grounds Hansen's intuition metaphysically: because the human intelligence is **uncreated and co-eternal**, God cannot "reprogram" a will He never authored, so a coerced "love" is not merely distasteful but ontologically impossible — exactly the structure of the "forced free choice is a square circle" argument. The Book of Mormon makes agency the architecture of mortality (2 Nephi 2): "men are free... to choose liberty and eternal life... or captivity and death." White's Lazarus analogy is powerful for the *unilateral* picture (the dead contribute nothing), but it assumes the very point in dispute — that fallen humans are spiritually *dead* (incapable) rather than *ill* (in need of healing they can accept or refuse), a distinction Hansen draws against Calvin's "diagnosis" framing. The fairest concession: the imperatives Hansen cites do not *logically* prove ability — a Reformed reader takes commands as the God-ordained *means* by which He grants the ability He commands (so "choose life" is the instrument of regeneration, not evidence of native power). The LDS reply is that this reading empties the commands of sincerity if the outcome was fixed before the hearer existed — which loops back to whether the Calvinist God can be called *loving* ([[The Calvinist God Cannot Love Because He Controls Everything]]).
