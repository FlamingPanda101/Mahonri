# Hell for God's Glory Is Narcissism Not Love

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
A fourth defense (especially Reformed): God permits the damned because it **brings him glory** — his justice is displayed, his power demonstrated, and his mercy toward the saved looks more amazing by contrast. The damned are the dark backdrop that makes divine glory shine.

## The Rebuttal
1. **A being who causes suffering to look good is, by human standards, a monster — rate: strong intuition.** If any human caused others to suffer purely to make himself look better, we would call him a narcissist, not a good person. Applying the same standard to God, "that's not love, that's narcissism" [^1]. This parallels the existing [[Hell - For Gods Glory Defense Rebutted]] but sharpens it into a character indictment. **Steelman of the theist:** the Reformed defender denies the analogy because God's glory is not vanity — it is the manifestation of the highest good (God himself), and creatures' highest end *is* to glorify God; displaying justice on the impenitent is righteous, not self-aggrandizing. Jonathan Edwards-style theology holds that the full range of divine attributes (including justice/wrath) must be displayed for creation to reflect God completely. The video does not engage this systematic-theology version, treating "glory" univocally with human reputation-seeking.
2. **It corrupts the salvation of the saved too — rate: moderate, original.** A clever secondary move: if the saved are saved partly so the damned can make grace "look gracious by contrast," then salvation "isn't purely an act of love anymore — it's a performance." Grace that *needs* someone else to be burning is "manipulation with extra steps" [^2]. This attacks the glory-defense from the saved person's side, arguing it instrumentalizes everyone, echoing [[Hell as a Greater Good Treats People as Tools]].
3. **A God who needs the damned wants what is best for himself, not for all — rate: strong dialectically.** The conclusion ties back to omnibenevolence: a God whose glory is contingent on others' eternal suffering "is not a God who wants what's best for everyone; he's a God who wants what's best for himself" — the opposite of what "good" means [^3]. See [[Five Divine Attributes Cannot All Be True]].

## Best Quotes
> "This is saying that God creates people, real conscious feeling people, knowing they will suffer forever because it makes him look good... That's not love. That's narcissism." [^1]

> "Their salvation isn't purely an act of love anymore. It's a performance. It only looks gracious because someone else is burning... That's not grace. That's manipulation with extra steps." [^2]

> "A God who needs people to suffer forever so that he can be glorified by it is not a God who wants what's best for everyone. He's a God who wants what's best for himself." [^3]
