# Hell — The "For God's Glory" Defense Rebutted

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
Some hold God ordains damnation **for his own glory** — the display of his justice and wrath. [[John Calvin]]: God "pre-ordained for his own glory... a part of the human race... to eternal salvation, and... to eternal damnation." [[Thomas Aquinas]]: the saints are "allowed to see perfectly the sufferings of the damned" so as to be more thankful.

## The Rebuttal
- Is glory obtained through wrath and vengeance really glory — or a vice? [^1]
- The dilemma: *Could* this glory have been obtained without damning souls? If **no**, God's glory is contingent on external outcomes — incompatible with his being necessarily perfect. If **yes**, why obtain it through the eternal torture of billions? Either way the defense is morally bankrupt [^2].

## Best Quote
> "Is a glory that is obtained through wrath and vengeance really glory at all? Does a glory achieved through such vile means really justify the moral monstrosity of hell?" [^1]
