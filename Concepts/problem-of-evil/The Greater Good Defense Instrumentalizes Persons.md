# The Greater Good Defense Instrumentalizes Persons

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
When free will fails, defenders appeal to a **greater good**: hell makes heaven meaningful, risk makes love authentic, damnation serves a higher cosmic purpose. [[Hayden Carroll]] rebuts that "this approach treats some people as instruments — it implies that some lives are created to fail so that others may flourish... That's not love. That's utilitarianism."[^1]

## The Rebuttal
1. **Instrumentalization is a recognized moral wrong** — rate: strong, Kantian. Creating a person *for ruin* so others benefit uses them as a mere means. "No amount of benefit to others justifies intentionally creating someone for ruin. We reject that reasoning everywhere else in moral life." *(deontological)* [^1]
2. **Connects to the glory-defense** — overlaps the vault's [[Hell - For Gods Glory Defense Rebutted]]: "the damned's suffering glorifies God" is the Calvinist version Hayden elsewhere notes "bites the bullet" ([[Even If the Trinity Is True Your God Is Evil]]). *(cross-reference)* [^1]
3. **Applied to Judas** — in closing, Hayden ties this to Gavin's [[Appeal to Prophetic Necessity Rebutted|prophetic-necessity]] answer: "the prophecy had to be fulfilled... but these arguments treat some people as instruments for outcomes which ignore their personal good." *(application)* [^2]

**Steelman of the greater-good theist:** (a) *Org-whole goods* — some genuine goods (the defeat of evil, the manifestation of mercy *and* justice) may be impossible without the real possibility, and even actuality, of loss; this is not crude utilitarian aggregation but a claim about the architecture of a meaningful moral universe. (b) *Each person still has their own sufficient opportunity* — the damned are not *only* means; they were genuinely offered beatitude and freely refused, so they are also ends. Hayden's reply: if a person's *net* outcome is eternal ruin, then whatever systemic good their existence serves, it was not done *for their good* — and the resolution is about willing *each* subject's good "without exception."

## Best Quotes
> "It implies that some lives are created to fail so that others may flourish... And let's be clear, that's not love. That's utilitarianism."[^1]

> "No amount of benefit to others justifies intentionally creating someone for ruin. We reject that reasoning everywhere else in moral life and we should not abandon it here."[^1]
