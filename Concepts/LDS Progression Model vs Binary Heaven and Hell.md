# LDS Progression Model vs Binary Heaven and Hell

## The Claim
[[Jacob Hansen]] argues that LDS soteriology is **not binary** (saved/damned, heaven/hell) but a **spectrum of progression** toward one's "fullness of potential as a child of God." [^1] Rejecting a truth does not damn a person; it merely "stunts your growth" — you keep all the light and truth you have accepted and "you will retain what you've been willing to let in." [^2][^3] Damnation is recast as the *stopping of progression*, and "hell" (outer darkness) is reserved for those who "know the light, they hate light" — not for the merely mistaken. [^4][^5] On this view Hansen can tell a Trinitarian, "I would never say... you're going to hell," only that an incorrect view leaves growth "incomplete." [^6]

## The Rebuttal (Ortlund's counter, steelmanned)

1. **Ortlund argues the binary is unavoidable and biblical.** "Every human soul is either connected to God or not connected to God. So it's a binary in that sense." [^7] He points to Jesus' "lurid categories" — outer darkness, weeping and gnashing of teeth, sheep vs. goats. [^8][^9] *Rating: strong textual case for a decisive threshold;* the Gospels do speak in binary terms.

2. **Ortlund claims the disagreement is smaller than Hansen thinks.** He notes Hansen *also* affirms a real cutoff (outer darkness for those who hate the light) and boundary beliefs (Jesus is the way; you are a child of God), so "I don't think it's all that different." [^10] *Rating: a fair observation* — both systems have an ultimate either/or; they differ on *where the line falls* and *what lies on the far side.*

3. **Steelman of Hansen.** The progression model arguably better fits intuitions of fairness ("the fairest view... you get what you want," as Ortlund himself concedes about his own view [^11]) and avoids consigning manifestly good, Christ-following people to torment over a metaphysical disagreement. [^12] *Rating: strong on the problem-of-hell front;* it dissolves the "would God really damn a sincere modalist?" worry that Ortlund must answer with the knowing-rejection/imperfect-understanding distinction.

4. **The hidden crux: theosis presupposes shared ontology.** Progression "to become as he is" only makes sense because LDS theology denies the Creator/creature divide (see [[Humans Are the Same Kind as God (LDS Ontology)]] and [[Creatio Ex Nihilo Is the Lynchpin Difference]]). On classical theism the creature can never *become* God, so the spectrum model is unavailable. *Rating: this exposes that the soteriology disagreement is downstream of the ex-nihilo disagreement.*

## Best Quotes
> "I don't see this as a binary thing. It isn't you're saved or you're not saved... Latter-day Saints view it as Christ has opened the door to allow for you to progress to your fullness of your potential as a child of God." [^1]

> "You will retain what you have... the light and truth that you've been willing to let in." [^3]

> "We don't believe in damnation that people go to hell. We believe that what happens is when people cut themselves off from God, they end up stopping their ability to continue their progression." [^4]

> "Every human soul is either connected to God or not connected to God. So it's a binary in that sense." [^7]
