# Source Criticism in Mormon History

#type/claim

## The Claim
Sound conclusions about [[Joseph Smith]]'s treasure hunting and folk practices require **basic historical method** — corroboration, bias evaluation, eyewitness vs. nth-hand testimony, and contemporaneity — applied evenhandedly to all sources. Critics, apologists, and Latter-day Saints alike "draw on everything we can," but the duty is to do so *carefully*.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The four tests (method).** Mark lays out the criteria: What can be corroborated? Evaluate bias. Is it eyewitness or "secondhand or thirdhand or fourthhand"? Is it contemporaneous or "decades later or half a century later"?[^2] *Rating: strong — standard professional methodology.*

2. **The data set is mostly late and biased (application).** Much of the evidence is half-a-century-removed (Neely notes, Purple, the trial articles) or commissioned-hostile (Hurlbut), so confident verdicts are unwarranted; this is *why* the conviction and folk-magic questions resolve as "inconclusive."[^3] *Rating: strong.*

3. **Cataloging is not analysis (key distinction).** [[Dan Vogel]]'s 18-location list and [[D. Michael Quinn]]'s magic compilation are valuable *catalogs* but contain "no real source criticism"; later authors who copy these lists "with absolutely no source criticism or historical analysis" commit a basic error.[^4] *Rating: strong.*

4. **Symmetry requirement.** The method applies to *both* sides — "anti-Mormons and Latter-day Saints, we all draw on everything we can, but we should do so carefully." Confirmation bias (emphasizing favorable data, ignoring the rest) is bad history regardless of who does it.[^5] *Rating: strong.*

5. **Steelman.** One might argue that demanding contemporaneous corroboration sets the bar so high that almost nothing about Joseph's early life can be known, conveniently shielding faith claims behind perpetual "inconclusiveness." Mark's answer is that the same standard *establishes* the well-attested items (seer stones, rods, treasure seeking) and *withholds* judgment only where evidence is genuinely thin — the method cuts for and against the believer.[^6]

## Best Quotes

> "Is this eyewitness testimony or is it secondhand or thirdhand or fourthhand or fifthhand or worse? ... Is this contemporaneous evidence or is it ... half of a century later?"[^2]

> "Anti-Mormons and Latter-day Saints ... we all draw on everything we can. But we should do so carefully."[^5]
