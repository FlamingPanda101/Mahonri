# The Law of Witnesses Establishes Truth

## The Point
The scriptural "**law of witnesses**" — "in the mouth of two or three witnesses shall every word be established" — has "always been a part of the Lord's work on earth." [[Jacob Hansen]] enlists it as the divine warrant for his **collective / appeal-to-authority epistemology**: confidence that "certain events took place" and that "God-given doctrine and principles are true" is properly built by *converging independent witnesses*, not by a single observer's certitude. This is the principle that licenses the whole video's method of stacking trustworthy sources.

## Argument
1. "The law of witnesses has always been a part of the Lord's work on earth." [^1]
2. The law states "that in the mouth of two or three witnesses shall every word be established." [^1]
3. Its function is dual: "this witness confirms that certain events took place, and that God-given doctrine and principles are true." [^1]
4. Method implication (stated by Hansen at the top of the video): rather than arguing "from reason," one can "call on the witness of authority" by "showing how other trustworthy people and sources seem to be… implying a similar way of viewing knowledge." [^2] The law of witnesses is the scriptural basis for treating pooled, independent testimony as truth-establishing.
5. This dovetails with [[The Blind Men and the Elephant - Partial Truth Mistaken for the Whole]]: where a single vantage yields partial-truth-mistaken-for-whole, multiple corroborating witnesses correct and establish the full picture.
6. **Steelman of the objector.** A critic notes that the number of witnesses does not by itself confer truth — witnesses can share a bias, a tradition, or a culture, so corroboration may only reproduce a common error (see the "tradition that might have changed the stories" worry in [[Knowledge Runs on Degrees of Probability Not Certitude]]). The reply is that the witnesses must be *relevantly independent*; the value of the law of witnesses lies in independence reducing the chance of correlated error, not in mere headcount.

## Best Quotes

> "The law of witnesses has always been a part of the Lord's work on earth. This law states that in the mouth of two or three witnesses shall every word be established. This witness confirms that certain events took place, and that God-given doctrine and principles are true." [^1]

> "In this video I will actually be doing something different… I will be calling on the witness of authority, by showing how other trustworthy people and sources seem to be at the very least implying a similar way of viewing knowledge." [^2]
