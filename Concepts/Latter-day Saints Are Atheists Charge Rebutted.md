# Latter-day Saints Are Atheists Charge Rebutted (Objection Rebutted)

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
[[Joe Heschmeyer]] runs a syllogism with Matt Fradd: "What we mean by God" is the unconditioned ground of existence, the uncaused cause, the metaphysically necessary being, the creator of all creation. Mormons reject *that* concept of God (regarding it as "the corrupting influence of pagan philosophy"). Therefore "they don't believe in God" — and the arguments from reason that disprove atheism "are almost all of them also arguments that disprove Mormonism."[^1]

## The Rebuttal

1. **It equivocates on the word "God" (rated: strong, logical).** The argument smuggles in a *contested definition* of God (the Thomistic *actus purus* / uncaused cause) as if it were neutral, then declares anyone who rejects that definition an atheist. [[Luke Irwin]]: "Matt Fradd goes into 'what God is,' proceeds to quote *zero* scriptures, and just quotes Aquinas a thousand years later." The foundation of the claim is Aquinas, "not scripture."[^2] To call LDS "atheists" is to define theism so narrowly that only Thomists qualify. See [[Validity vs Soundness - Why the Catholicism Syllogism Fails]].

2. **The definition is borrowed pagan philosophy (rated: strong, self-undermining).** [[Cardon Ellis]]: Fradd recites "the uncaused cause," which mirrors Aristotle's "unmoved mover / prime mover." So the very move used to accuse Mormons of being insufficiently philosophical — and to deny that Greco-Roman philosophy shaped the Christian God — itself quotes Aristotle. "You can't say God is the uncaused cause and then say Greco-Roman philosophy didn't influence Christianity and then turn around and literally quote Aristotle's unmoved mover. That doesn't work."[^3] See [[The Uncaused Cause Is Aristotle Not Scripture]] and [[Trinity Built to Satisfy Greco-Roman Philosophy]].

3. **LDS ground God in revelation, not syllogism (rated: strong, epistemological).** The LDS basis for God is the restoration's revelatory epistemology, not a chain of metaphysical proofs. Even [[Alex OConnor]] — who knows the contingency argument well — says the thing that would make *him* convert "is not once I finally understand the contingency argument," but "an authentic spiritual experience," and that he is "not sure anybody has ever converted because of the contingency argument."[^4] LDS affirm a real, personal, worship-worthy God; they reject only the *philosophers'* definition. See [[Spirit Over Argument - The Limits of Apologetics]].

4. **Covenant, not metaphysics, is the LDS "game" (rated: moderate, theological).** [[Jacob Hansen]]: "Their game is a theology game; our game is a covenant-relationship game." Getting the ontological model of God philosophically "right" is not what saves; "fishermen could get it. You don't need a degree in theology to understand the hypostatic union."[^5] See [[Covenant Relationship Over Theology Quiz]]. *Steelman:* a Thomist will respond that this is precisely the point — if the LDS "God" is contingent/material, then by classical theism's lights LDS are not worshiping the metaphysically ultimate being, and the label "atheist" (relative to that definition) is technically apt, not a slur. The LDS rebuttal grants the *definitional* disagreement but denies that the classical definition is biblically mandated rather than philosophically imported.
