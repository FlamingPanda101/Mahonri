# The Blood of Zechariah — Perpetual Boiling Blood

## The Biblical Account (What We Have)

**2 Chronicles 24:20-21**:
> "The Spirit of God came upon Zechariah the son of Jehoiada the priest...
> and they conspired against him, and stoned him with stones at the
> commandment of the king in the court of the house of the LORD."

Key details from the biblical text alone:
- Zechariah was the son of the priest Jehoiada
- He was a prophet who preached against the people's apostasy
- He was **stoned to death in the temple courtyard** — on holy ground
- This was ordered by the king

## The Rest of the Story (Apocryphal Traditions)

### The Mishnah: Sanhedrin 96b
When Nebuchadnezzar entered Jerusalem (~200 years after Zechariah's murder),
he entered the temple and found **a pool of blood — a whole body's worth —
sitting on the stones of the courtyard, boiling.** It had never stopped since
the murder.

Nebuchadnezzar asked: "What is this?"
The priests said: "That is the blood of Zechariah the priest. He preached
about the Messiah and we murdered him here."

Nebuchadnezzar, trying to appease the blood, slaughtered priests by the
thousands. The blood only stopped boiling after immense carnage.

### The Lives of the Prophets
After Zechariah's murder:
- The priests could no longer see angels in the temple
- The voice of God was no longer heard
- The altar fire went dark
- The temple lost its sanctity entirely

Zechariah is regarded as the last messianic prophet before the temple's
defilement and Judah's condemnation — at which point Jeremiah appears,
Lehi takes the brass plates and flees, and Nebuchadnezzar destroys everything.

## Why Jesus References This

### The Defiled Temple Connection
The temple in which Zechariah was murdered is the **same temple** (Solomon's)
in which Deuteronomy was allegedly "found" during Josiah's reign (2 Kings
22:8). Jonah's thesis: "This defiled, blood-soaked temple is the temple in
which they go, 'Hey, look, we found a new book, and let's rewrite all the
old books.'"

### The Hand-Washing Irony
The Pharisees' hand-washing laws derive from Deuteronomistic oral tradition —
the very tradition created by the school that murdered Zechariah. Jesus's
rebuttal essentially says: "The blood of the prophets YOU murdered is still
on your hands — literally, on the stones of the temple you rebuilt — and
you're worried about MY hands being unwashed?"

### "A to Z" Is Garbage
Some commentators claim "Abel to Zechariah" just means "A to Z" — all the
prophets. Jonah: "Please don't ever say that from the pulpit. That is such
garbage." Both Abel and Zechariah are specific instances of **blood that
could not be buried or washed away** — perpetual testimony against murderers.

## Apologetic Significance

1. **Jesus validates extra-biblical tradition**: The boiling blood story is
   not in the Old Testament. Jesus treats it as common knowledge His audience
   shares — legitimizing apocryphal sources.

2. **The temple was already defiled before Herod**: Jesus isn't criticizing
   the Herodian temple alone — He's indicting a centuries-long pattern of
   prophetic murder and scriptural corruption centered on the temple.

3. **Multiple attestation**: Mishnah Sanhedrin 96b, Lives of the Prophets,
   and Jesus's own reference form a convergence of independent witnesses to
   this tradition.
