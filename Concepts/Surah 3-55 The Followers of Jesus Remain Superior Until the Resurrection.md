# Surah 3-55 The Followers of Jesus Remain Superior Until the Resurrection

#type/claim

## The Claim
This is [[Avery (God Logic)|Avery]]'s "second strand" of [[The Islamic Dilemma]], deployed against the "Batman" caller, who argued Christianity "had to fail" so that Islam could replace it. Avery answers from [[Quran 3.55|Surah 3:55]]: Allah promises Jesus, "I will make those who follow you superior to those who disbelieve **until the Day of Resurrection**." If the followers of Jesus are guaranteed to remain superior to the disbelievers until the end of the world, then Jesus' message can never have been corrupted, overcome, or lost in history.

> "So there's no point at which Christianity is supposed to fail. It's supposed to remain successful until the day of resurrection."[^1]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **The preservation inference (Christian steelman).** If at any point disbelievers "overcame the believers... and diluted their message, then this would mean that Allah failed to keep his promise and the Quran is false." So "the message of Jesus had to be preserved and uncorrupted, and the followers of Jesus have to be successful even to this day."[^2] This converts a triumph-promise into a *textual-preservation* guarantee — the opposite of *tahrif* (see [[The Tahrif Debate Among Classical Muslim Scholars]]).
2. **It turns the caller's own theory against him.** "Batman" holds an Islamic restorationist view: the original followers of Jesus were strict monotheists (*tawhid*, no Trinity, no church councils), and Trinitarians later "infiltrated" and corrupted the message — a view Avery notes is "what Mormons believe too."[^3] Avery's counter: on that scenario the *disbelievers* (the Trinitarian corruptors) *became superior* over the true monotheist believers — which is exactly what [[Quran 3.55|3:55]] says can never happen. So the restoration narrative contradicts the Qur'an.[^4]
3. **The [[Quran 3.81|3:81]] companion (covenant of the prophets).** Avery pairs 3:55 with 3:81: a coming messenger will "confirm what is with you," and the prophets recognize him *because* he confirms (not contradicts) their existing revelation. Same logic — a reliable prior text is presupposed.[^5]
4. **Muslim steelman / live counters.** "Batman" offers three: (a) "superior" applies only to *those personally close to Jesus*, not "Christianity"; (b) "purify you from those who disbelieve" may mean separating Jesus from unbelief, not preserving a community; (c) the superiority might begin only *at* the Day of Resurrection ("until" as terminus a quo). Avery rejects (c) grammatically ("until the day of resurrection" marks the *end* of an ongoing state) and treats (a)/(b) as not touching "the crux" — that the *followers* remain "superior... until the day of resurrection."[^6] *(A fairer reading would grant that "superior/uppermost" (Ar. *fawqa*) is genuinely ambiguous between doctrinal truth, political dominance, and eschatological vindication; classical tafsir often reads it as the worldly/eschatological ascendancy of Christians-then-Muslims over Jews, not as a guarantee that the New Testament text is uncorrupted. So the verse is suggestive for the dilemma but not airtight.)*

## Best Quotes
