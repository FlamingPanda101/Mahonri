# Matthew 22:30 — "Neither Marry Nor Given in Marriage"

## The Charge
> "Jesus said in the resurrection people neither marry nor are given in marriage.
> Mormons being sealed as eternal families contradicts Jesus's own words."

## The Verse (KJV)
> "For in the resurrection they neither marry, nor are given in marriage, but are
> as the angels of God in heaven." (Matthew 22:30)

## Rebuttal Points

### 1. What the Verse Actually Says (and Doesn't Say)
- The verse describes what happens **at the resurrection** — not what happened
  before it or what can happen after.
- It says marriages aren't **performed** at that point — it doesn't say existing
  marriages are dissolved.
- Braden: "If you are already married and/or sealed before the resurrection, you
  will continue to be."

### 2. The LDS Plan of Salvation Provides a Framework
- There is a long period between death and resurrection.
- Proxy work (baptisms for the dead, sealings) happens during this intermediate
  period.
- Sealing is an ordinance performed by priesthood authority — it must be done
  before the resurrection.
- This is exactly consistent with "in the resurrection they neither marry" — the
  ordinance isn't performed then.

### 3. Context: The Sadducees' Trick Question
- Matthew 22:23-33 — the Sadducees (who don't believe in resurrection) ask about a
  woman married to seven brothers sequentially. Whose wife is she in the
  resurrection?
- Jesus's answer targets their bad-faith premise: they don't understand resurrection
  OR scripture OR the power of God (v. 29).
- He's not delivering a systematic treatise on eternal marriage — He's shutting
  down a trap question from non-believers about a woman with seven husbands.

### 4. 1 Corinthians 15:29 — Baptism for the Dead
Paul references proxy ordinances for the dead as a *current practice* the
Corinthians accept: "Else what shall they do which are baptized for the dead, if
the dead rise not at all?" This shows early Christians practiced ordinances on
behalf of the deceased — consistent with LDS temple practice.

## Bottom Line
Matthew 22:30 says marriages aren't *performed* at the resurrection moment — not
that marriages can't exist eternally. The context is a trap question from
resurrection-deniers, not a doctrinal treatise on family sealing.
