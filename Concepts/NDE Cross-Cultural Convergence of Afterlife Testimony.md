# NDE Cross-Cultural Convergence of Afterlife Testimony

## The Point
Having laid out parallels in the Bible, [[Plato]], the Tibetan Book of the Dead, and [[Emanuel Swedenborg]], Moody asks whether the agreement is merely the result of these authors copying one another — and concludes it is not [^1]. While *some* lines of influence are plausible (Plato drew on Eastern mysticism; Greek thought shaped New Testament writers), in most cases such influence "is not easy to establish," and each writer supplies details he could not have borrowed [^2]. The convergence of Tibetan sages, Paul, Plato, Swedenborg, and modern Americans remains, in Moody's words, "a striking, and, so far, not definitively explicable fact" [^3].

## Evidence
1. **The influence hypothesis, granted in part.** Plato "admits that he derived some of his insights partly from the religious mysticism of the East," and Greek philosophy "influenced certain New Testament writers," so Paul's spiritual body might have Platonic roots [^1].
2. **But influence usually cannot be shown.** "In most cases it is not easy to establish that such influence could have taken place. Each writer seems to bring up a few interesting details... which he could not have gotten from earlier authors" [^2].
3. **The decisive example.** Swedenborg repeatedly notes that the newly dead may not realize they are dead — a detail absent from the Bible and Plato, emphasized in the Tibetan Book of the Dead, "a work which Swedenborg could not possibly have read. Indeed, it was not even translated until 1927" [^2].
4. **The modern subjects were not primed.** All had some exposure to the Bible and two or three knew Plato, but *none* knew Swedenborg or the Tibetan text — yet details absent from the Bible and Plato "constantly crop up" in their accounts and "correspond exactly" with those esoteric sources [^4].

## Best Quotes
> "How is it, we might well ask ourselves, that the wisdom of Tibetan sages, the theology and visions of Paul, the strange insights and myths of Plato, and the spiritual revelations of Swedenborg all agree so well... and with the narratives of contemporary individuals who have come as close as anyone alive to the state of death?" [^3]

## LDS Connection
A testimony of the afterlife that surfaces independently across radically separated cultures and centuries is precisely what one would expect under a *universal* plan of salvation in which God "speaketh unto all nations" and gives light to every people (2 Ne. 29:7-12; Alma 29:8). The Restoration affirms that fragments of true knowledge about the spirit world have been scattered among many peoples — the cross-cultural NDE convergence is a natural empirical echo of that claim.
