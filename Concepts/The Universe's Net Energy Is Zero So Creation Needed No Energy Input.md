# The Universe's Net Energy Is Zero So Creation Needed No Energy Input

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Hollis R. Johnson]] argues that the universe's *total net energy appears to be about zero*, which removes the energetic "miracle" Copan and [[William Lane Craig]] need for creation from nothing. The total energy of a system is kinetic plus potential, E = T + V, where the potential energy of all fields (gravity stored in empty space) is *negative*. "Strangely, for the entire universe, it appears that these two enormous quantities, T and V, are almost exactly equal but opposite in sign, so their sum is very close to zero."[^1] In other words, the positive rest-mass and kinetic energy of every star and galaxy is paid for "at the expense of an increasingly large negative energy due to various fields stored in empty space (the vacuum or the cosmological term)."[^1] Johnson's financial parallel: "it is as if one could spend whatever one wanted by going deeper and deeper into debt (so that the sum of the debts always balanced the value of the stuff), but without anyone being called to account."[^1] If the books balance at zero, no vast injection of energy "from nothing" was required — and a model in which the cosmos is a "free lunch" is exactly the opposite of the energy-violating ex nihilo event Copan and Craig posit.

## Supporting Evidence
- **Rees on the zero-energy universe.** Astrophysicist Sir Martin Rees: "however much inflation occurs, the total net energy is zero. It is as though the universe were making for itself a gravitational pit so deep that everything in it has a negative gravitational energy exactly equal to its rest-mass energy (mc²)."[^2]
- **The dominant ingredient is vacuum energy.** The universe is ~5% common matter, ~25% dark matter, and ~70% "dark energy," the last "likely related to the energy of the vacuum or Einstein's cosmological term."[^3] Because that vacuum reservoir is real and enormous, "a claim that scientific evidence supports the postulate of creatio ex nihilo must be received with great skepticism."[^4]
- **The vacuum may forbid a true "nothing."** Quoting John Barrow's *Book of Nothing*, "the vacuum energy of the Universe may prevent the Universe from having a beginning," and an imaginary region "containing nothing at all would collapse to zero size."[^5] The combination of vacuum fluctuations and inflation thereby "provides a reason or cause for the big bang."[^6]
- **Energy conservation cuts against ex nihilo.** Fred Adams notes that assuming "nothing at all in existence" before t = 0 "would imply that an extraordinarily large violation of energy conservation took place at the beginning of time."[^7]

## Best Quotes
> "For the entire universe, it appears that these two enormous quantities, T and V, are almost exactly equal but opposite in sign, so their sum is very close to zero." [^1]

> "It is as though the universe were making for itself a gravitational pit so deep that everything in it has a negative gravitational energy exactly equal to its rest-mass energy (mc²)." (Martin Rees, quoted by Johnson) [^2]

For a Latter-day Saint the zero-net-energy picture is an ally against the claim that physics demands an energy-violating creation out of nothing; it fits a cosmos of eternal, organized matter/energy far more comfortably than ex nihilo (see [[Modern Physics Is Consistent With Eternal Matter Not Ex Nihilo]]). It is the constructive twin of Johnson's other rebuttal, that [[Quantum Mechanics Forbids a True Singularity So Creation From Nothing Does Not Follow|quantum mechanics forbids the singularity]] the argument requires, and it should be read alongside the apologetic mirror-image [[Nothing Comes from Nothing - The Krauss Equivocation|Krauss equivocation]] over what "nothing" means. Honest caveat: a balanced energy ledger explains how the *stuff* could arise without net input; it does not by itself answer why there is a cosmos at all — which is where the [[The Aquinas Fallacy - Pinning Theology to Current Science|Aquinas-fallacy]] caution about overclaiming applies in both directions.
