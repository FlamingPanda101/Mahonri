# Judge the Book of Abraham by Its Fruits

## The Claim
The right test for the [[Book of Abraham]] is not Egyptology but *fruits*: "by their fruits ye shall know them" ([[Matthew 7.16-20|Matthew 7]]). If the book yields spiritual truths that draw people closer to God and produce "morally better, practically better, spiritually better lives," that is the evidence of its divine origin — testable in one's own life, like the seed experiment of [[Alma 32.27-43|Alma 32]].[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The fruits are doctrinal substance (rated: moderate, internal/doctrinal).** The larger Book of Abraham "gives us greater detail about the Creation... the pre-existence and the plan of salvation," laying out the premortal existence and humans as "co-creators with God more fully than any other scripture."[^2] [[Abraham 1.1-4|Abraham 1]] alone supplies the seeking-God model, the backstory enriching the [[Binding of Isaac]], and the curse-reversal. *Steelman:* a critic will note that "good fruits" is not truth-tracking — many texts of disputed or false origin produce sincere devotion and life improvement (the placebo/meaning objection), so subjective fruits cannot establish historicity. Bradley's reply is that the *content* must also "fit certain scriptural patterns" and "bring illumination to the scriptures" — i.e., coherence with prior revelation, not bare emotional payoff. The argument is strongest as a defeater of the "Egyptology disproves it" claim and weakest as a positive proof of ancient authenticity.

2. **Recasting the question (rated: strong, framing).** "If we want to know, is Joseph Smith a prophet? ... Then we have evidence for that right here." The decisive question is "did he speak for God? Did he give us new truths?" — not "Is Joseph Smith an Egyptologist? Because we know he's not that."[^3]

3. **An experimental epistemology (rated: moderate).** "We can do the experiment like in [[Alma 32.27-43|Alma 32]] and see what the fruits are in our own lives."[^1] Truth claims are put to a lived test rather than settled purely by external scholarship.

## Best Quotes

> "If we look at the fruits — and Christ said by their fruits you shall know them — if we look at the fruits that we can glean from this book of scripture that are amazing, we can judge it based on those fruits."[^1]

> "He's bringing us spiritual truths from God that lead us closer to God and lead us to have better lives, morally better, practically better, spiritually better lives."[^1]

> "Is Joseph Smith a prophet? Is Joseph Smith an Egyptologist? Because we know he's not that."[^3]
