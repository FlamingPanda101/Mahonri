# LDS Panpsychism and the Intelligence Threshold

#type/claim

## The Claim

Pressed by the atheist students on *where consciousness and agency come from*,
[[Hayden Carroll]] offers an unusually explicit metaphysic: he self-identifies as
a **panpsychist** — "I'm actually more of a panpsychist, which means that there's
consciousness in everything"[^1] — and pairs it with LDS [[Intelligence Is Uncreated and Eternally Has Agency|uncreated intelligence]].
On this picture, because God organizes rather than [[Creation Ex Nihilo Leads to Determinism|creates from nothing]],
"he has to pull from somewhere," so eternal matter is already suffused with
consciousness at varying grades.[^2] Crucially, Carroll adds a **gradation /
threshold** claim: intelligences are *not equal*; some "have different capacities
than others,"[^3] and those that had "the capability to fulfill a certain
threshold of capacity to become like God became humans."[^4] So the
intelligence → spirit → physical-body progression is selective: human-grade
intelligences are those that met a capacity bar, while lower-grade conscious
matter did not.

This functions in the conversation as an *anti-reductionist* answer to the
[[Consciousness and First Cause Problem for Atheism|consciousness-from-non-consciousness]]
objection (consciousness never "emerged" because it was never absent) **and** as a
free-will answer (an uncreated, eternally-conscious intelligence is an uncaused
agent, not a product of prior physical causes). Carroll flags it as a **debated,
non-authoritative** view — "that's a debated topic amongst my group of
theologians or want-to-be theologians"[^5] — and twice offers to "be argued the
other way."[^6]

## Supporting Evidence (steelman)

This is a speculative private synthesis, not LDS doctrine, so the honest task is
to give the *strongest* form and mark its load-bearing assumptions and its
fault-lines.

1. **It dissolves the hard problem by relocating it (strong as a parry).** If
   consciousness is fundamental to matter, then "How does the non-conscious
   produce the conscious?" never arises — there is no non-conscious starting
   point. This is exactly the move serious naturalist panpsychists (Galen
   Strawson, [[Philip Goff]]) make against both eliminativism and emergentism,
   and Carroll borrows its force while adding a theistic organizer to ground the
   *unification* of micro-minds that secular panpsychism struggles with (the
   combination problem; see [[Panpsychism Collapses into Reductionism]]).

2. **It is genuinely continuous with LDS scripture (textual anchor).**
   [[Abraham 3.25|Abraham 3]] describes intelligences that were "more intelligent
   than they all," explicitly teaching a *hierarchy* of pre-mortal intelligences —
   so Carroll's gradation claim is not freelancing; it has a canonical hook.
   D&C 93's "intelligence... was not created or made" supplies the uncreated,
   self-acting core (see [[Intelligence Is Uncreated and Eternally Has Agency]]).
   The threshold gloss (capacity-to-become-like-God as the cut for humanity) is
   the speculative add-on layered on top of the textual hierarchy.

3. **The hardest objection — gradation looks like determinism in disguise.**
   The students press precisely here: if intelligences were *already* unequal
   "from the very beginning," with some carrying greater "capacity for bad,"
   then a person's moral trajectory is fixed by an eternal nature they did not
   choose — "we didn't really have a choice, right? We were always
   different."[^7] This is the same [[Free Will - Atheist Determinism vs LDS Co-Eternality|nature-determines-choice]]
   objection that haunts co-eternality generally: locating agency in an
   *uncreated* nature only escapes external determinism if the eternal
   intelligence is a genuine self-determining agent rather than a fixed program.
   Carroll's reply narrows the claim to a *capacity to choose* ("capacity to
   choose evil")[^8] rather than a fixed disposition, and concedes biology can
   *limit* but not erase agency — "I don't have an issue saying our free will can
   be impacted by our biology... you're limited to your mortal body."[^9]

4. **The classical-theist cost (steelman of the opponent's opponent).** Even
   granting the panpsychist parry, the trade is the same one all
   [[Three LDS Models of God and Creation - Finitism, Process, and Organizer|organizer-model]]
   theologies pay: a God who *pulls from* eternal, independently-conscious
   materials is an organizer among eternal givens rather than the absolute source
   of all being. A creedal critic will say Carroll has purchased a free-will
   answer at the price of divine aseity — and that "panpsychism" is doing heavy
   metaphysical work the LDS canon underdetermines (D&C 93's "intelligence" is
   famously ambiguous as to whether it is substance, person, or property).
