# Two-Stage Creation Is an Ad Hoc Rescue Device

#type/claim

## The Claim

Whenever a text plainly describes God creating by **organizing pre-existing matter**, [[Paul Copan]] and [[William Lane Craig]] rescue *creatio ex nihilo* by positing a hidden **two-stage creation**: God (stage 1) first created the raw matter out of nothing, then (stage 2) shaped that matter into the cosmos — and the text in question is said to be describing only stage 2. [[Blake Ostler]] argues this device is **purely *ad hoc***: it is imposed on text after text "for which there is no textual support at all," precisely to neutralize evidence for *creatio ex materia*.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

Ostler documents the maneuver across the corpus:

1. **2 Peter 3:5–6.** The text says heaven and earth were "formed out of water and through water." Copan and Craig claim it "focuses on the second stage" and that God first created the water *ex nihilo* (appealing to Proverbs 8:24). Ostler: "their ad hoc explanation consists of imposing an assumption on the text for which there is no textual support at all. Second Peter 3:5 gives no indication of any prior creation ex nihilo."[^1]

2. **2 Enoch.** They argue the invisible things were created from nothing (stage 1) and then visible things from the invisible (stage 2). But 2 Enoch says God *moves among* the invisible things and is himself "invisible among the invisible" — so they already exist; God cannot move around in what does not exist in any way.[^2] See [[Invisible Things Are Not Nothing in Biblical Creation Texts]].

3. **Wisdom of Solomon 11:17.** The text says God "created the world from formless matter" (ἐξ ἀμόρφου ὕλης). Copan and Craig propose the formless matter was the "earth" already created in Genesis 1:1, *ex nihilo*. Hubler calls this reading-into-the-text "contempt[ible]"; Ostler asks what evidence could ever count for *creatio ex materia* if a text that *expressly* says God made the whole cosmos out of unformed matter does not.[^3]

4. **2 Maccabees 7:28.** They split the mother's words about creating man "in the same way" into a two-stage creation, even though the text says God *moulds* (πλάσας) man from pre-existing material and gives no hint of two stages.[^4] See [[2 Maccabees 7.28 Does Not Teach Creation Ex Nihilo]].

5. **Justin Martyr, Athenagoras, Aristides.** The same device is applied to each, against their explicit potter-and-clay / shapeless-matter statements.[^5] See [[Athenagoras of Athens]] and [[Aristides of Athens]].

The pattern itself is the argument: a reading invoked *only* to save a theory, with no independent textual warrant in any instance, is the textbook profile of an *ad hoc* hypothesis.

## Supporting Evidence (Copan & Craig, steelmanned)

A two-stage creation is not intrinsically absurd — it is, in fact, how the later orthodox tradition harmonized Genesis 1:1 ("created the heavens and the earth") with Genesis 1:2 (the formless, dark, watery state): verse 1 is the *ex nihilo* creation of raw material, verse 2 its unformed condition, and the six days its ordering. On this view Copan and Craig are not inventing a device but applying a coherent, time-honored reading; texts that describe the *shaping* phase need not mention the prior *ex nihilo* phase, just as a builder's account of framing a house need not mention the manufacture of the lumber.

## Why the Steelman Fails (per Ostler)

The two-stage reading is question-begging exactly where it matters: it is invoked *only* because the doctrine is already assumed (see [[Copan and Craig Use a Debaters Brief That Begs the Question]]), and **not one** of the texts supplies the alleged stage-1 *ex nihilo* event. Worse, several texts affirmatively block it: 2 Enoch and the Odes of Solomon depict God acting *among* already-existing invisible things; Wisdom 11:17 names the unformed matter as the stuff God used for "the all," with no prior creation in view; 2 Maccabees says God *moulded* man. An interpretive move that must be added to every passage, is supported by none, and is contradicted by some, is the definition of *ad hoc*.

## Best Quotes

> "Their ad hoc explanation consists of imposing an assumption on the text for which there is no textual support at all. Second Peter 3:5 gives no indication of any prior creation ex nihilo." — Blake Ostler[^1]
