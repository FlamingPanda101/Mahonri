# The Gospel of John Resembles the Apocryphal Gospels

## The Claim
In genre and technique the Gospel of John "is much more like the Gospel of Thomas, the Gospel of Mary, some of these so-called gnostic gospels than it is like the other gospels of the New Testament." Its symbolic, pseudepigraphal character aligns it with non-canonical literature, and it spawned a stream of pseudepigraphal works in its wake.

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Shared pseudepigraphal device.** John presents itself as the work of an eyewitness it was not (see [[Gospel of John Is Pseudepigraphal Not Eyewitness]]) — the same pseudonymous, apostolically-attributed framing characteristic of apocryphal gospels. *(Moderate-strong; depends on the pseudepigraphy thesis, which Méndez argues at length.)* [^1]
2. **Shared symbolic / esoteric mode.** John's pervasive symbolism (see [[John's Pervasive Symbolism]]) — encoding teaching in gestures and key words that even the disciples "don't get" — resembles the reflective, secret-meaning mode of texts like Thomas more than the plain narrative of the Synoptics. *(Moderate; a genre/technique judgment.)* [^2]
3. **John fathered later pseudepigrapha.** The Gospel "had an impact on Christian literature where it would spawn all kinds of pseudepigraphal literature in its wake" — Méndez counts 1–3 John, and suggests perhaps Revelation, among the downstream non-canonical-style works. *(Moderate; situates John at the head of a literary stream.)* [^3]
4. **It is a deliberately boat-rocking thesis.** Méndez flags this as cutting against "still very popular ideas in the field" — he is "excited to debate" it, signaling it as a strong, contestable claim rather than consensus. *(Context; epistemic status flagged by the author himself.)* [^4]

**Steelman of the opposing view.** John was received into the canon and read as authoritative by the very Fathers ([[Athanasius]], the Cappadocians) who opposed "gnostic" texts; its incarnational, pro-material theology ("the Word became flesh") runs *against* the docetic/dualist tendencies of much apocryphal literature. Many scholars would insist John belongs firmly with the canonical gospels in genre (a *bios* of Jesus) and that surface similarities to Thomas/Mary do not make it kin to them.
