# Definition of Gospel

## The Claim
Many anti-LDS arguments only work because the parties use a word ("gospel," "one," "spirit") with **different definitions** and then talk past each other. Pinning the definition first defuses several proof texts at once.

## Supporting Evidence
- **"Gospel"** — an honest definition is "the good word of Jesus Christ: that he is the savior of the world, and we are saved by his grace." Latter-day Saints hold *this* gospel (cf. [[1 Corinthians 15.1-4|1 Corinthians 15:1-4]]), so the charge of preaching "another gospel" presupposes its own conclusion *(primary — speaker's stated definition)* [^1].
- The caricature ("you think Jesus is created and the brother of Lucifer, so it's a different gospel") is not the gospel's definition — so agreement on the gospel still holds *(assertion by speaker)* [^2].

## Best Quote
> "I think an honest and fair definition is that the gospel is the good word of Jesus Christ. That he is the savior of the world. We are saved by his grace." — Braden [^1]
