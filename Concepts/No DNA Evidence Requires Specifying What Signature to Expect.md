# No DNA Evidence Requires Specifying What Signature to Expect

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)

The bumper-sticker objection "zero DNA evidence for the Book of Mormon" is, on
examination, not a real test: to say there is no evidence, one must first specify what
genetic signature *should* be present — and almost no one making the claim can.[^1]

## The Rebuttal

### 1. The claim presupposes a known target

[[Ugo Perego]] and [[John Butler (DNA scientist)]] argue that to assert "no DNA
evidence," you must know what signature you expect to find. Most people saying "zero
DNA evidence" could not name which haplogroups are Jewish, which are ancient Near
Eastern, or where to look.[^2]

> **Evidence rating:** Strong (a clean burden-of-proof point).

### 2. There is no single "Jewish" or "Manassite" marker

There is no isolable Manassite DNA, and no single marker defines a Jewish individual
— Israelite/Jewish ancestry is highly variable. (The Cohen modal haplotype indicates
priestly *Levite* lineage, not Lehi's tribe of Manasseh, and not a general Jewish
marker.) So "we'd expect Jewish DNA and don't find it" is not a coherent expectation.[^3]

> **Evidence rating:** Strong (correct on the genetics of Jewish ancestry).

### 3. Absence of evidence is not disproof

Given the founder effect, drift, and host-population admixture (see [[Founder Effect and Genetic Drift Erase a Small Founding Signal]] and [[The Philistine DNA Study Shows Genetic Signals Vanish into Host Populations]]), we should not expect a
detectable signal. The absence of expected evidence does not disprove the claim; the
better response is to ask the objector what evidence they expect and where they would
look.[^4]

> **Evidence rating:** Moderate-to-strong (sound, though "absence of evidence" must be used carefully).

## Steelman of the Opposing View

A critic can fairly reply that shifting the burden ("name the haplogroup") is partly a
dodge: the *prima facie* expectation is simply that a Near-Eastern migration would
leave *some* uniquely Old-World autosomal trace detectable by modern genome-wide
methods, and none is found. The reply: with no dated/located founder sample and full
host admixture, even genome-wide methods have nothing distinctive to detect — so the
expectation collapses for population-genetic reasons, not special pleading.

## Best Quotes

> "What you can say is, what kind of DNA evidence do you expect? I suspect most people who say zero DNA evidence couldn't tell you what kind of DNA evidence should be there."[^2]

> "There isn't one marker for a Jewish individual... it's just so variable."[^3]

> "The absence of evidence doesn't disprove things... if you're going to go on social media and talk about DNA and the lack of evidence, you should be able to articulate what kind of evidence you expect to find."[^4]
