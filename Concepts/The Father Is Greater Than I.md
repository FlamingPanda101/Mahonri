# The Father Is Greater Than I

## The Claim

Jesus' statement "my Father is greater than I" ([[John 14.28|John 14:28]]) implies a hierarchy between two beings. One being cannot be "greater than" itself; if Father and Son were the same being, the comparison would be incoherent. [^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Comparatives require two relata.** "Greater than" is a relation between two distinct things. If they were numerically one being, the statement says the being is greater than itself — incoherent. It fits the Godhead's hierarchy of glory (the Father preeminent) naturally. (Evidence: strong as plain reading; the orthodox must qualify it — see steelman.) [^1]
2. **Consistent with the wider data.** It coheres with [[John 17.3|"the only true God" = the Father]], Jesus' being *given* authority, and his having a God — a cluster of texts that all rank the Father above the Son. [^2]
3. **Hierarchy of glory, not of species.** In the LDS model the Father is "the most high God," "preeminent in the Godhead," and so the one "true God" in that relational sense, without the Son being a different kind of being or less than fully divine. (Evidence: this is the LDS systematizing move, coherent within its framework.) [^3]
4. **Steelman.** The classical answer is twofold: (a) the Son is *functionally/economically* subordinate while *ontologically* equal — "greater" refers to the Father's role as unbegotten source (the *monarchia* of the Father) or to the Son's incarnate state, not to a difference of being or deity; (b) read incarnationally, the Son speaks "according to his humanity," in which the Father is of course greater. So eternal equality of essence and "the Father is greater" are held together. (Evidence: this is the mainstream patristic reading, e.g., Athanasius, Augustine; it is principled, not invented for this verse.) The LDS reply: "equal in being but the Father is greater" again leans on the [[The Who or What Test for the Word God|who/what]] equivocation, and the eternal-generation "source" language itself implies the Son derives from the Father (see [[Begotten Implies Source and Origin]]). [^4]
