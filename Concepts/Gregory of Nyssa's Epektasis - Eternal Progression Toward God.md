# Gregory of Nyssa's Epektasis - Eternal Progression Toward God

## The Point
Gregory of Nyssa (c. 335-395) is famous for **epektasis** (from the Greek for "stretching" / "extension"; the participle appears in Philippians 3:13) — the doctrine that the soul progresses toward God **eternally and ever-increasingly, without end**. [^1] Where Origen held that progression terminates in rest, Gregory held it never stops: "the soul that is joined to God is not satiated by her enjoyment of him," rather "the more abundantly she is filled up with his beauty, the more vehemently her longings abound." [^2] This is the closest patristic analogue to LDS **eternal progression**.

## Evidence
1. **Three assumptions ground epektasis.** (a) God is "perfect and infinite," so an "unbridgeable gulf" always exists between Creator and creature — we can never become like God "in the fullest sense." (b) Human nature is "malleable and has an infinite capacity for growth." (c) Because God is infinite and human capacity infinite, "becoming like God is a never-ending process." [^3]
2. **Human nature is shaped by what it pursues.** "Human nature came into being as something capable of becoming whatever it determines upon, and to whatever goal the thrust of its choice leads, it undergoes alteration in accord with what it seeks." [^4]
3. **Each attainment becomes a new starting point.** "That which is apprehended at any given time is in all respects greater than anything that has been apprehended previously, but it does not set limits to the object of the search" — it becomes "the starting point of a search after more exalted things." [^2]
4. **We are the mirror of God's light.** God reveals himself "in the mirror that we are," conforming our character to his so we "come to know experientially who God is." [^5]
5. **Post-resurrection orientation.** Much of this transformation occurs after death and resurrection, "when the passions that now afflict us through the flesh do not rise up." [^6]

## Best Quotes
> "The soul that is joined to God is not satiated by her enjoyment of him; rather, the more abundantly she is filled up with his beauty, the more vehemently her longings abound." — Gregory of Nyssa [^2]

> "Human nature came into being as something capable of becoming whatever it determines upon... it undergoes alteration in accord with what it seeks." [^4]

> God's "greatness will ever be out of our reach, [but] it can always be on our horizon." [^7]

## LDS Connection
Epektasis is arguably the strongest patristic precedent for the LDS doctrine of **never-ending progression** — the engine of [[Human Deification in Mormonism]] and the [[King Follett Discourse]]'s "exaltation to exaltation." Both affirm infinite human capacity for growth toward God and an afterlife of continued becoming, against a binary heaven/hell terminus ([[LDS Progression Model vs Binary Heaven and Hell]]). The key divergence is Gregory's "unbridgeable gulf": he keeps an infinite Creator/creature distance so we never become God "in the fullest sense," whereas LDS theology collapses that gulf via same-species ontology (see [[Same-Species-as-God as the Core LDS Distinctive in Theosis]]). The shape of eternal progression is shared; the metaphysics of the goal differs.
