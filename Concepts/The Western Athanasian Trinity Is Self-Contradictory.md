# The Western Athanasian Trinity Is Self-Contradictory

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
The objection being rebutted is the **Western (Athanasian) Trinity** itself — the doctrine that the three persons are one being with co-equality and divine simplicity. The guest argues this construction is incoherent and self-defeating, in contrast to the social/subordinate model.

## The Rebuttal

### 1. The seating-at-the-right-hand argument (the core)
Reading the Creed's "he ascended into heaven and is seated at the right hand of God the Father," the co-host objects: "How can you argue in the exact same creed that Jesus Christ is ascended to heaven and is seated at the right hand of the Father and then say they're one person that is sitting next to itself?" [^1] The guest agrees: "The Western Trinity, the Athanasian Trinity is silly." [^2] **Rating: a genuine prima facie tension** (cf. Psalm 110:1) — the spatial "at the right hand" language fits two distinct persons more naturally than numerically-one being; orthodoxy answers via the distinction of persons within one essence, which the guest finds strained.

### 2. The "mystery" critique (moderate)
The co-host challenges appeals to the Trinity as an inexplicable "mystery": "Doesn't that sound like a cop out to you?" [^3] The guest distinguishes legitimate from illegitimate mystery: "I'm not saying it's a mystery that has to be something that's illogical, but the mystery of the Trinity is a very beautiful thing" — and reframes the beautiful mystery as belonging to the *social/subordinate* Trinity, not the Western one. [^4] **Rating: the "mystery = cop-out when it shields contradiction" point is a fair philosophical worry**, though "beautiful mystery" is itself a soft response.

### 3. The plain Godhead alternative (the positive case)
The co-host offers the folk-LDS picture: "God's our father, Jesus Christ is his son, and the Holy Spirit is his messenger. It's really that simple. ... the biggest one, the chief, the boss, that's God. His son, who obeys him ... that's Jesus ... their servant ... that's the Holy Ghost. And it's the Godhead." [^5] The implied argument: the Godhead avoids the contradictions because it does not assert numerical unity of being. **Rating: rhetorically vivid; trades the Trinity's metaphysical problems for the charge of tritheism** (addressed in [[The Godhead Is Not Polytheism]]).

### 4. Steelman of the opposing view
The classical Trinitarian replies that "sitting next to itself" is a category error: the persons are *relationally* distinct (the Son is not the Father) while being *one in essence*, so the Son sits at the right hand of the *Father* as a distinct person, not "next to himself." Divine simplicity and the *opera ad extra indivisa* are precisely the conceptual tools that block the alleged contradiction; calling them "silly" is assertion, not demonstration. Moreover, "at the right hand" is widely read as a metaphor of *honor and shared rule*, not literal location. The guest's case works only if one already rejects that the persons can be really distinct within one being — which is the point at issue, not a proven contradiction. See [[The Five Incompatible Claims of Creedal Theism]] for the developed LDS version of the inconsistency charge.
