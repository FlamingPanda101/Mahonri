# LDS Father Was Always Fully Divine (No Eternal Regression)

## The Claim
When [[Gavin Ortlund]] asks whether God "was once sort of the acorn and now is the full tree," [[Jacob Hansen]] explicitly **rejects** the eternal-regression-of-gods view: "I actually don't hold to that view. There is a Latter-day Saint view that holds to an eternal regression of gods. I don't hold that... I believe the Father has always been fully divine." [^1] For Hansen, "fully divine" means the Father "exists within a certain kind of loving relationship with the Son and the Spirit... eternally," i.e., divinity is "a type of relationship." [^2] He claims "there's nothing in Latter-day Saint scripture that holds to" eternal regression. [^3]

## Supporting Evidence (and the internal-LDS steelman)

1. **Hansen distinguishes his position from the King-Follett "as God now is, man may become" trajectory.** He affirms the Father's eternal full divinity, not a god who progressed from manhood. *Rating: a defensible reading of LDS scripture* (the regression doctrine is sermon-derived, e.g., the King Follett Discourse and Lorenzo Snow couplet, not canonized in the Standard Works). It is a *minimizing*/intellectually cautious LDS stance.

2. **Steelman of the traditional-LDS objection.** Many Latter-day Saints and historical leaders (e.g., teachings associated with [[Brigham Young]], [[Joseph Smith]]'s [[King Follett]] Discourse) *did* affirm a God who was once a man and an eternal progression of gods. *Rating: Hansen's view is a live but contested intra-LDS position;* an outside critic could argue he is presenting a streamlined apologetic that not all Latter-day Saints share. (See [[Human Deification in Mormonism]].)

3. **"Divinity as relationship."** Defining divinity as a mode of eternal loving relationship among Father, Son, and Spirit is striking — it sounds almost social-trinitarian, yet without consubstantiality or creatio ex nihilo. [^2] *Rating: an interesting convergence-point* with social-Trinity language, but the underlying metaphysics (eternal intelligences, organized matter) remains incompatible with classical theism.

4. **Ortlund's reaction.** He notes this "is not what I have previously understood of Mormon views of God" and presses what *else* is eternal — eliciting Hansen's "intelligences" doctrine (all beings eternal in a lower form). [^4] *Rating: shows the Father's eternal divinity is part of a larger eternal-cosmos package*, not classical aseity (see [[Creatio Ex Nihilo Is the Lynchpin Difference]]).

## Best Quotes
> "I actually don't hold to that view. There is a Latter-day Saint view that holds to an eternal regression of gods. I don't hold that... I believe the Father has always been fully divine." [^1]

> "By fully divine, I mean that he exists within a certain kind of loving relationship with the Son and the Spirit... eternally, that is that we call divinity, which is a type of relationship." [^2]

> "That to me is sufficiently different from historic Christian views of creation and the Trinity that I would look at that and say I don't think there should be surprise that this is going to be looked at as a departure." [^4]
