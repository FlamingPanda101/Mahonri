# Consciousness Precedes Existence — Neuroscience and Pre-Existence

## The Claim
A world-famous neuroscientist (unnamed in the source) has concluded that "consciousness precedes existence" — that "there was consciousness before there was matter. Matter arises out of consciousness, not the other way around."[^1] When asked whether individual human spirits could therefore have existed before incarnation, he affirmed this as a logical possibility. This provides an independent, scientific line of reasoning consistent with the LDS doctrine of pre-mortal existence.

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The Neuroscientist's Conclusion:** "Probably the world's most famous neuroscientist. Ultimately, his conclusion is that consciousness precedes existence. There was consciousness before there was matter. Matter arises out of consciousness, not the other way around."[^1] This is a fundamental challenge to materialism — the view that consciousness is an emergent property of physical brains.

2. **Application to Pre-Existence:** The Ward Radio host asked the neuroscientist directly: "So, if we thought of that consciousness as being individuated, would that mean that we as human spirits could have existed before we were incarnate as human beings?" The neuroscientist replied: "Yeah, that makes sense to me. That seems to be a possibility."[^1]

3. **Independent Corroboration:** Unlike theological arguments that require shared scriptural or creedal commitments, this line of evidence comes from empirical science — a neuroscientist arriving at a conclusion compatible with pre-existence through the study of consciousness itself, not through religious reasoning. This makes it accessible to audiences who are skeptical of religious claims but open to scientific arguments.

4. **Convergence Context:** The source presents this as part of a broader pattern: "leading researchers outside of the field of religion are beginning to see merit in LDS ideas."[^1] The neuroscientist is not a theologian or an apologist — he is an independent researcher whose conclusions happen to align with LDS doctrine.

## Best Quote

> "Probably the world's most famous neuroscientist. Ultimately, his conclusion is that consciousness precedes existence. There was consciousness before there was matter. Matter arises out of consciousness, not the other way around. And I asked him the question point blank. I said, 'So, if we thought of that consciousness as being individuated, would that mean that we as human spirits could have existed before we were incarnate as human beings?' And he said, 'Yeah, that makes sense to me. That seems to be a possibility.'"[^1]

## Counter-Arguments

- **Unnamed source — cannot verify:** The neuroscientist is not named in the video. Without a name, the claim cannot be checked against his actual published work, and his credentials and representativeness within neuroscience cannot be assessed. This is hearsay evidence.
- **"World's most famous neuroscientist" is hyperbolic framing:** The phrase is promotional, not diagnostic. Even if identifiable (likely candidates might include Donald Hoffman, Christof Koch, or David Chalmers — though Chalmers is a philosopher), the claim that a single neuroscientist's view supports pre-existence does not establish scientific consensus.
- **Consciousness-precedes-matter ≠ individuated pre-mortal spirits:** Even if consciousness is fundamental to reality (as in panpsychism or idealism), this does not entail that *individual human persons* existed as discrete conscious agents before their biological birth. The jump from "consciousness is fundamental" to "you existed before you were born" is a non-sequitur without additional metaphysical premises.
- **Materialism remains the dominant view:** The overwhelming consensus in neuroscience and philosophy of mind is that consciousness depends on brain function. One famous neuroscientist entertaining idealism does not overturn the mainstream view.
