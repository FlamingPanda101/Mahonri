# Trinity Is Fundamentally Contradictory

## The Claim
[[Dan McClellan]] argues the doctrine of the Trinity is **internally incoherent and self-contradictory** — it asserts, in effect, that "a square equals a circle." Expressed as a math problem with A=Father, B=Son, C=Holy Spirit, G=God:
- A+B+C = G
- A = G, B = G, C = G
- yet A ≠ B, A ≠ C, B ≠ C [^1]

The Trinity's distinctive move is to say A, B, and C are **persons** (*who* the Father, Son, and Spirit are) while G is a **substance / essence / nature / ousia** (*what* God is). McClellan holds this resolution fails because "it does not make sense of how persons have always been understood, and it does not make sense of how substance has always been understood." [^2]

## The Rebuttal (i.e., why he thinks it fails — with a steelman)

1. **The person/substance distinction requires redefining both terms. (Strong as critique; contestable as a knock-down.)** McClellan: three persons "simultaneously and fully independently and in combination comprising the single substance" requires "entirely novel and distinct conceptualizations of persons, of substance, of the relationships to each other." [^2] *Steelman of the orthodox side:* Trinitarian theologians (e.g., the Cappadocians, later Aquinas, and modern analytic theologians like Brian Leftow or William Lane Craig) freely concede that *person* (hypostasis) and *substance* (ousia) are being used as technical terms of art, not in their everyday senses, and argue that conceptual novelty is not the same as logical contradiction — God may simply be a unique kind of being. McClellan's reply is essentially that the novelty is not principled discovery but *ad hoc* gap-filling (see point 3).

2. **It asserts an answer, then back-fills the rationale.** "The Trinity asserts an answer but then has to go and figure out how to rationalize and explain that answer." [^3] He contrasts this with genuine inductive discovery: it "was not the inductive observation and discovery of something that was always true." *Rating: strong as a historical observation* — the doctrine demonstrably developed over centuries (see [[Trinity Was Constructed Not Revealed]]).

3. **The residual "gap" has never been closed.** No proposed model of the Trinity "has achieved universal acceptance because ultimately... it's incoherent." [^4] Philosophers "down to today" keep introducing novel framings "to cover a gap that resulted from trying to square that circle." *Rating: moderately strong.* The persistence of rival models (social trinitarianism, Latin/relative-identity trinitarianism, etc.) is real; whether ongoing debate proves *incoherence* rather than *difficulty* is the contested philosophical premise. The strongest orthodox reply is that many true and coherent doctrines remain perennially debated.

4. **The lone near-explicit biblical proof text is spurious.** The clearest "Trinity in the Bible" verse, [[1 John 5.7|1 John 5:7]], is the **Johannine Comma**, absent from the earliest Greek manuscripts — a later interpolation (see [[1 John 5-7 - Johannine Comma Interpolation]]). This supports the thesis that the doctrine was constructed rather than read off the page. *Rating: strong, well-established textual fact.*

5. **A better-fitting model exists.** McClellan offers **divine mediation / the logic of divine images** as the explanation the biblical authors actually used (see [[Divine Images Explain Jesus Relationship to God]]). *Rating: this is the constructive payoff; persuasive within his cognitive-science framework, but it presupposes a non-Trinitarian reading of the relevant texts.*

## Best Quotes
> "What the Trinity asserts is that A plus B plus C equals G, but also A equals G, and B equals G, and C equals G. But A does not equal B, A does not equal C, and B does not equal C. How does this all work?" [^1]

> "It is not who the Father, Son, and Holy Ghost are, it is what God is." [^2]

> "The Trinity asserts an answer but then has to go and figure out how to rationalize and explain that answer." [^3]

> "Fundamentally what we're doing is trying to assert that a contradiction is not a contradiction... And that's the fundamental contradiction of the Trinity. The notion that a square equals a circle." [^5]
