# Abortion and Euthanasia as Pagan Not Christian

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)

The objection (O'Connor): the air-we-breathe thesis is unfalsifiable because *every* Western practice gets traced to Christianity (see [[The Falsifiability Demand Against the Air-We-Breathe Thesis]]). Name something that *isn't*.

## The Rebuttal

[[Glenn Scrivener]] answers that he does **not** hold "everything in the West is Christian" — there are post-Christian deviations that are genuinely *pagan*. His named falsifiers: **industrial-scale abortion** and **euthanasia**.[^1]

1. **Abortion is "totally pagan."** Scrivener: it is "a pagan practice which we are heading full steam towards," continuous with Greco-Roman infant exposure, not with Christianity. He says so in his book.[^1] *(This is a genuine falsifier — it shows the thesis is not vacuous; Scrivener draws a line.)*

2. **Euthanasia likewise.** "Utterly in keeping with paganism, not with Christianity" — and even its justifications (e.g. "the NHS is creaking, this might be cheaper") are "pagan kinds of justifications."[^2] *(Consistent application of the same line.)*

3. **The "Christian language for pagan ends" complication.** Scrivener also says modern people forward "pagan ends" (abortion) using "Christian-ish language" — bodily autonomy, gender equality, the dignity/rights of the person.[^3] O'Connor seizes on this as reabsorption: "It seems like it doesn't matter what you point to."[^4] *(This is the crux of the dispute. O'Connor reads it as the thesis swallowing its own counter-example; Scrivener insists he is being mischaracterized "quite substantially" — the *practice* is pagan even if the *rhetoric* is borrowed.)*

4. **Steelman of O'Connor:** If a practice counts as a falsifier yet its *moral framing* is still credited to Christianity, then Christianity gets credit for the framing of even anti-Christian acts — which is precisely the unfalsifiable move he objected to. The distinction Scrivener needs (pagan act / Christian-flavored justification) may be too fine to do real falsificatory work.[^4] *(Fair — the falsifier is partly undercut by the rhetoric concession.)*

5. **Scrivener's clean falsifier.** When O'Connor pushes, Scrivener concedes a fully non-Christian case: pure indifference — "shout your abortion," "I don't care" — "that's not Christian."[^5] *(This is the cleanest falsifier offered, and it does answer the unfalsifiability charge in principle.)*

## Best Quotes

> "Oh, industrial scale abortion... it's a totally pagan practice which we are heading full steam towards. I think euthanasia is another pagan practice."[^1]

> "They're doing what I consider to be a pagan practice for some profoundly Christian reasons... you do tend to have to use some Christian-ish language in order to do something profoundly unchristian."[^3]

> "If it's just 'I don't care' — shout your abortion — that's not Christian. I think you're mistaking me."[^5]
