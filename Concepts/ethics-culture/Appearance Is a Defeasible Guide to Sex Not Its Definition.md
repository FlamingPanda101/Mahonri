# Appearance Is a Defeasible Guide to Sex Not Its Definition

## The Claim
[[Steven Woodford]] proposes that a woman is "any adult human who has enough correlating and observable female sexual characteristics" — so sex is, in effect, defined by appearance and secondary traits. [[Trent Horn]] answers that "appearances are usually safe guides to determine reality *unless contrary evidence exists*." Looks are *defeasible evidence* of an underlying biological fact, not the fact itself. The CAIS case ("Jane") illustrates the asymmetry: Jane looks female but does not therefore qualify for, say, infertility treatment "because she is a woman who can't get pregnant" — she is "a man with a tragic sex developmental disorder." Reducing womanhood to correlated external features both *misclassifies* masculine-presenting women (who are sometimes mistaken for men yet are firmly women) and *flatters caricatures* — men like Dylan Mulvaney who "present themselves as a caricature of a woman" without understanding what being a woman is.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **(Strong, defeasibility)** Horn's general epistemic point — appearance is reliable-but-overridable evidence — is sound and standard: we revise an appearance-judgment when we learn the underlying reality (a wax fruit looks edible; it is not). Applied: female appearance is *evidence of* being female, defeated by contrary biological evidence.[^1]
2. **(Strong, two analogies)** *Apparent age:* Shauna Rae (an adult who looks ~8) and the look-18-but-13 case show we do not redefine age by appearance; we accommodate misleading appearances. *Albinism:* an ethnically Indian woman with albinism lacks "traditionally Indian external characteristics" but is genetically Indian — appearance does not redefine ethnicity, which is "primarily based on something biological like genetics."[^2]
3. **(Moderate, harm to women)** Defining woman by appearance "does a great disservice to women that simply happen to have more masculine features" and rewards men who perform a stereotyped femininity — the same appearance-reductionism Horn diagnoses in Andrew Tate's "I'd rather be with a 10 who is trans than a 1 who is a woman" remark.[^3]

## Opposing View (Steelmanned)
The appearance-based view can reply that, in practice, *everyone* assigns sex by observable cues — at birth, in daily life, in language — so appearance is not merely evidence but the operative criterion for the social category "woman." If gender is a social construct that "heavily correlates" with secondary characteristics, then a person who reliably presents and is received as a woman *is*, for social purposes, a woman; the underlying chromosomes are irrelevant to the social fact. Insisting on a hidden biological essence behind the appearance, the critic says, is itself a metaphysical add-on.

## Counter-objection
That we *use* appearance as a cue does not make appearance the *definition* — we use a thermometer to detect fever without identifying fever with the mercury level, and we revise the reading when we learn it is broken. The "social fact" reply proves too much: if womanhood just is successful social reception, then it would change with an audience's beliefs (a woman alone in a room, or among people who misperceive her, would flicker in and out of womanhood), which is absurd. And appearance-reductionism has a victim class the essentialist view does not: masculine-presenting biological women, who *are* women regardless of reception, and who are erased the moment "woman" means "presents as one." The defeasibility framing keeps appearance doing its proper evidential work while grounding the category in the body's orientation toward a reproductive role ([[Boundary Cases Do Not Dissolve the Sex Binary - The Fallacy of the Beard]]), which is why Jane's female appearance does not make her eligible for treatments predicated on being female.

## Apologetic Engagement
A natural-law point shared by Latter-day Saints and Catholics. The effective engagement is to grant appearance its evidential role, then deploy the apparent-age and albinism parallels to show that *no one* actually treats appearance as definitional for age or ethnicity, so there is no principled reason to treat it as definitional for sex.

## LDS Relevance
Coheres with the Restoration's grounding of sex in eternal, embodied identity rather than presentation ([[Male and Female Balance Is Required for the Divine Image and Human Flourishing]]). The "redefine by appearance/feeling" tactic parallels the redefinition moves the vault tracks for *person/being* ([[Persons-and-Beings Were Redefined Like Gender and Sex]]) and for *woman/gender* in translation debates ([[Hebrew Grammatical Gender Is Lost in Translation Undercutting a Biblical View of Gender]]).
