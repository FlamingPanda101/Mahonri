# Western Values Predate or Arise Independently of Christianity

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)

The objection (Scrivener/Holland): the core values — compassion, protection of the weak, human dignity — are uniquely indebted to Christianity, such that they would be "unthinkable without" it.

## The Rebuttal

[[Alex OConnor|Alex O'Connor]]'s central counter-argument: the very same values appear in pre-Christian and non-Christian cultures, so they cannot be *uniquely* or *necessarily* Christian.

1. **Pre-Christian law codes protect the weak.** [[Hammurabi]]'s prologue states its purpose is "that the strong shall not be able to oppress the weak."[^1] *(Strong — a documented ~1750 BCE legal text articulating exactly the value Scrivener calls Christian. Scrivener's counter: it is class-stratified, not universal — but O'Connor notes Scrivener's own preferred origin [Gregorian Reforms] is also non-universal.)*

2. **The [[Hippocrates|Hippocratic Oath]] is egalitarian.** "Whoever's house I walk into, rich or poor, male or female, I am to serve them" — language parallel to [[Galatians 3.28|Galatians 3:28]], centuries earlier.[^2] *(Strong — see [[The Hippocratic Oath Predates the Christian Medical Ethic]].)*

3. **Non-Western traditions independently affirm compassion.** Confucian relational ethics, Buddhist compassion, "caring for the weak and not allowing the strong to oppress the weak... do crop up independently of Christianity."[^3] *(Moderate — Scrivener's rejoinder is that the thesis is limited to the West, so non-Western parallels are off-topic; O'Connor replies that "unthinkable without" makes them on-topic again.)*

4. **[[Aristotle]] limits just war.** Aristotle held the military "should only be used defensively... not for conquest."[^4] *(Weaker — Scrivener counters that Aristotle also held some peoples are "natural slaves," a just cause of war, so the parallel is partial.)*

5. **Steelman of the opposing view:** Scrivener's strongest reply is [[Noticing Compassion Elsewhere Is Itself Christian|that the very act of cherry-picking "compassion" from these traditions while recoiling from "Spartan brutality" reflects a Christianized lens]] — we select the good bits *because* of values we already breathe.[^5] O'Connor concedes he only looks for these parallels because Scrivener claims uniqueness — but maintains their bare *existence* still defeats the uniqueness claim regardless of why he noticed them.[^6]

## Best Quotes

> "Important parallels with the kind of ideas we're talking about — compassion, caring for the weak, not allowing the strong to oppress the weak just by right of power — do crop up independently of Christianity."[^3]

> "You've got the prologue of Hammurabi's code quite explicitly... it says that this is such that the strong shall not be able to oppress the weak."[^1]
