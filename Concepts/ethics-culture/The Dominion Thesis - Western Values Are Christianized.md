# The Dominion Thesis — Western Values Are Christianized

## The Claim

Modern Western values — compassion, equality, freedom, progress, consent — are pervasively, and often invisibly, the product of "20 centuries of Christianization." This is [[Tom Holland]]'s thesis in *Dominion* (2019), presented in confessional form by [[Glenn Scrivener]] in *The Air We Breathe*. The metaphor: the cross is "the stone that drops into the pond," and Western values are its "ripple effects."[^1] [[Alex OConnor|Alex O'Connor]] summarizes it as the claim that "what we often perceive of as secular advancements against church doctrine... is in fact a blossoming of the Christian spirit."[^2]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The thesis is endorsed even by non-Christian historians.** Scrivener notes [[Tom Holland]] is agnostic and [[Larry Siedentop]] secular, yet both trace Western liberalism to Christianity; [[Yuval Noah Harari]] grants equality is "a fundamentally biblical idea."[^1] *(Strong — independent witnesses reduce special pleading. But these are popular syntheses, not consensus among historians of the period.)*

2. **It is framed as modest, not triumphalist.** Scrivener: it is "quite a modest position" that Christianization "has had a profound impact on our moral sensibilities."[^3] *(Rhetorically strong, but the modest version ["influenced by"] is trivially true; the contested version is "unthinkable without" — see [[Unthinkable Without Christianity vs Blossoming Thesis]].)*

3. **Steelman of the opposing view:** O'Connor concedes the influence freely — "if the thesis is just that Western civilization has been greatly influenced by Christianity, it's like, yeah, of course."[^2] His real objection is to *uniqueness* and *necessity*: the same values appear in pre-Christian and non-Christian cultures (Hammurabi, Hippocrates, Buddhism), so they "can't be uniquely indebted to Christianity."[^4] He also argues the thesis is **unfalsifiable** — see [[The Falsifiability Demand Against the Air-We-Breathe Thesis]]. *(This is the live disagreement; the historical-causation claim is genuinely hard to test because history has no control group.)*

4. **Scholarly context:** The thesis draws on real scholarship — [[Joseph Henrich]]'s *WEIRDest People in the World* (Church marriage program reshaped Western psychology), Siedentop's *Inventing the Individual*, and medieval rights historiography (Brian Tierney, behind Scrivener's [[Rights Talk Traces to Medieval Canon Lawyers|canon-lawyer claim]]). It remains contested by historians who emphasize material, economic, and technological drivers. *(Mixed — serious scholarship exists on both sides.)*

## Best Quotes

> "We are the heirs of a tradition that makes us see slavery as obviously wrong... that is unique in history."[^5]

> "Christianity is the air we breathe... you don't even notice that it's there because it's been so successful."[^2]

> "If the thesis is just that Western civilization has been greatly influenced by Christianity, it's like, yeah, of course... but the idea, as you worded it, Glenn... these kind of developments would be unthinkable without Christianity."[^6]
