# Ryan Burges Four-Part Typology of the Nones

#type/historical

## The Point
[[Ryan Burge]], the political scientist of American religious demography behind graphsaboutreligion.com, took a large survey (~12,000 respondents, of whom ~25% claimed *no* religious affiliation) and used AI clustering to show that the "Nones" are **not one group** but at least four distinct clusters with different beliefs and behaviors. [[Jonah Barnes]] and [[Cardon Ellis]] relay the typology and add commentary. The practical upshot, in the hosts' words: "when people say 'none,' they're not all meaning the same thing… which one am I dealing with here?"[^1][^4]

## The Four Clusters (as relayed)
1. **NINOs — "None In Name Only" (~21%).** Decline a religious label yet remain fairly religious by other metrics: over half pray daily and many "believe in God without a doubt"; some still attend. The hosts suggest non-denominational Christianity has itself become a label-averse "culture/franchise."[^1]
2. **SBNRs — "Spiritual But Not Religious" (~36%, the largest).** Deeply skeptical of organized religion but highly interested in spirituality (crystals, yoga, meditation, nature); ~93% seldom/never attend, most hardly ever pray, very few affirm certain belief in God, many prefer "some higher power." The "hippie"/street-corner type.[^2]
3. **Duns — "Done with religion."** Angrier and more decisively out: ~2% attend any house of worship, ~99% do not pray, almost none affirm certain belief in God; "when I die, existence ends." Described as reactive ("rebellion against parents and society").[^3]
4. **Zealous Atheists.** The most hostile: ~75% tried to talk someone out of their faith in the past year; "sky daddy"/"flying spaghetti monster" rhetoric — whom Burge himself, the hosts note, dubs "Reddit atheists." Paradoxically ~17% still attend a service yearly and pray "a little." See [[Zealous Ex-Mormon Atheists Engage the Church More Than Members Do]].[^4]

## Supporting Detail
- **Roughly even split.** The clusters run ~21/36/~?/~? — close enough that no single type dominates; "the nones" is a statistical aggregate, not a worldview.[^2]
- **Clergy unbelief.** A related Burge finding the hosts cite: only ~70% of mainline Christian clergy report a "certain belief in God" — read as evidence of institutional hollowing.[^2]
- **Source quality.** The typology and numbers come from Burge (a tier-A quantitative scholar); the Ward Radio layer is popular commentary mapping the clusters onto LDS-adjacent culture.

## Significance for the LDS Conversation
- **Missionary and pastoral strategy.** Treating "nones" as a monolith mis-targets them; a NINO (still praying) and a Zealous Atheist need very different approaches. This complements the vault's separate LDS-specific disaffiliation work ([[Four Distinct Groups of Those Who Leave the LDS Church]], [[Politics Predicts Religious Disaffiliation]]), which slices *leavers* of the Church rather than the general unaffiliated.
- **Post-Christian, not non-religious.** The persistence of prayer/belief among NINOs and even some "zealous atheists" fits the vault's thesis that Western secularity is [[Western Secularism Is Post-Christian Not Non-Christian|post-Christian]] and that secularism [[Secularism Does Not Fill the Soul Like Religion|does not fully replace religion]].

## Best Quotes
> "They're not the same — there are different subgroups of nones. When people say 'none,' they're not all meaning the same thing, so it's interesting to understand which one am I dealing with here."[^4]

> "NINOs — none in name only… over half of them say that they pray daily, about half say they believe in God without a doubt, and they're [classified as] atheists or nones."[^1]

## Note on framing
The episode is enthusiast commentary, not peer review; some characterizations (especially of the "zealous atheist"/ex-Mormon cluster) are anecdotal and polemical. The underlying typology and statistics, however, are Burge's published data work and are the reliable core of this note.
