# Sojourner Passages Are Misread as Slavery Protections

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
Joshua HS argued that the Mosaic law repeatedly commands Israel to treat the *sojourner* — including, he claimed, an enslaved "brother" — with utter respect, kindness, and as an image-bearer of God, proving enslaved people had rights. He cited a passage "in Leviticus, I think it's 24" commanding Israel to "treat your brother who's enslaved as you treat the sojourner."[^1]

## The Rebuttal (Numbered)

1. **Sojourner ≠ slave (rated: strong; linguistic).** The *ger* (resident alien) is a free non-Israelite, a distinct legal category from the *eved* (enslaved person). [[Deuteronomy 10.19|Deuteronomy 10:19]] ("love the sojourner") is about the free alien. "Joshua seems to be squinting too hard at passages about sojourners and imagining that they're talking about enslaved folks when they're not."[^2]

2. **The Leviticus 24 text is hallucinated (rated: strong; primary text).** McClellan: "that's all hallucinated." There is no such command. [[Leviticus 25.39-43|Leviticus 25:39-43]] does the opposite of what Joshua described.[^2] See [[Apologist Misremembers and Hallucinates Bible Passages]].

3. **What Leviticus 25 actually says (rated: strong; primary text).** It instructs that a poor Israelite be treated like a resident alien in the sense of being **supported** (no interest, no food sold at profit). If he then sells himself as a debt slave, he is to be treated like a hired worker — and at that point "it stops talking about the sojourner." Then vv. 44-46 say the foreigner *may* be treated harshly as an enslaved person.[^3]

4. **Steelman.** Joshua is right that the law shows real concern for the *ger* and frames Israel's empathy in terms of its own Egyptian bondage. McClellan's point is that this concern is for free aliens and impoverished Israelites, and explicitly does **not** extend the same dignity to foreign chattel slaves.[^4]

## Best Quotes

> "Where he says he thinks there's this thing in Leviticus 24 about treating your enslaved brother as the sojourner, that's all hallucinated."[^2]

> "Then it's just talking about how to treat Israelites who are your debt slaves, which says you should treat them like a hired worker... And then verses 44-46 say, 'But as for the foreigner, yeah, you can treat them harshly like an enslaved person.'"[^4]
