# Religion Gives Bad Reasons to Be Good

## The Point
[[Sam Harris]]'s summary thesis: "religion gives people bad reasons to be good, where good reasons are available." The genuine goods religion delivers — contemplative experience, ethical motivation, the power of story — can be had without believing anything "on insufficient evidence," and the supernatural overlay does net harm.[^1]

## Argument

1. **Bad reasons scale worse than good ones.** "Good reasons scale better than bad reasons." Wherever religion is clearly life-savingly useful, Harris claims he can "get you there by some other way without the downside."[^1]

2. **The babies worth keeping.** The contemplative experience (waking "feeling like Meister Eckhart," self-transcending union) and the ethical life are "babies we can save" — available "just a matter of paying close enough attention to the nature of consciousness," with no dogma required.[^2]

3. **Belief in paradise is ruinous.** A belief that "this life probably doesn't matter very much at all because we get what we really want after we die" is "ruinous for prioritizing what we should be prioritizing in this life" — independent of its (absent) evidentiary basis.[^3]

4. **Willed belief is incoherent (the Pascal point).** You cannot "decide to believe something for which you have no evidence simply because of the good effects." This is why [[Blaise Pascal]]'s wager "never made any sense": to *really* believe (not merely act "as if"), you must stand in some relationship to the claim's *truth*. Peterson replies that most religious "belief" is exactly the metaphorical "acting as if" / suspension-of-disbelief we do at a movie. See [[Metaphorical Truth as a Useful Fiction]].[^4]

5. **Peterson's pushback (steelman).** The *supernatural* belief itself can alter behavior for the good — the conviction that one's good work "is going to be observed" can supply courage a person "might not have the courage to do otherwise." Harris concedes it alters behavior "but rather often for the bad."[^5]

## Best Quotes
> "Religion gives people bad reasons to be good where good reasons are available." [^1]

> "You don't have to presuppose anything on insufficient evidence to argue about what is right and wrong and good and evil in the 21st century." [^2]

> "You can't decide to believe something for which you have no evidence simply because of the good effects... that's why Pascal's wager never made any sense." [^4]
