# Sexual Signaling in Moderation Is God-Designed and Good

## The Claim
[[Jacob Hansen]] argues that **sexual signaling through dress and appearance is, in moderation, a God-designed good** — not something shameful. Much of what we call beautiful in men and women is tied to our complementary sexual natures by design; the problem is never sexuality itself but its *immoderate* expression. He is "glad [he is] not part of a religion that says our sexual natures are bad." [^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Attraction cues are built-in and not bad.** Fashion research ties what we find attractive to subconscious health/fertility signals: men's suits with shoulder pads (broad shoulders signal fitness), clothing that accentuates a narrow waist, makeup accentuating signs of fertility, men dressing to signal status. "We have an animal nature and that animal nature is not inherently bad; it just needs to be... moderated." *Rating: moderate; the evolutionary-signaling claims are popular-science generalizations, but the theological use (the body and its cues are good) is the load-bearing point.* [^2]
2. **Moderated sexual signaling produces real beauty.** "So much of what we call beautiful in both men and women is tied to this design by God for us to be sexual beings." Ballet and ballroom dance are beautiful precisely because of "our complementary sexual natures as men and women, and that is okay. A world without any sexuality is not the world that God wants." *Rating: moderate-strong as a theological aesthetics claim within an embodied-God framework.* [^3]
3. **The fire analogy fixes the boundary.** Sexuality is "the best analogy [for] fire": "a controlled flame can cut through steel — it's extremely powerful, but only when it's controlled," whereas "bringing a flamethrower to roast a marshmallow is a bad idea." So the goal is "to be attractive without either being gaudy and vain or frumpy and flat" — beauty without crossing "into lewdness and vanity." *Rating: strong as the organizing image; ties the positive good to the [[Modesty Is Moderation and the Avoidance of Vanity|moderation]] thesis.* [^4]
4. **Context sets the signal's meaning.** "In a tribe in Africa a woman going topless does not sexually signal... the way a topless woman would [at] your local neighborhood barbecue. A one-piece swimsuit in 1900 would have been a big sexual signal whereas today it's not." So "how we dress is about sending a message," and one should be *intentional* about that message. *Rating: moderate-strong; grounds why standards shift across cultures and eras without being arbitrary.* [^5]

### Steelman (the opposing view)
Two pushbacks. (a) A **conservative** critic worries that affirming "sexual signaling is good, just moderate it" hands teenagers a rationalization — the line between "genuinely attractive" and "lewd" is exactly where temptation operates, and telling youth to feel for it by the Spirit may invite boundary-testing. (b) A **critical-studies** reply (cf. [[Biblical Modesty Refers to Wealth Not Sexuality]]) notes the evolutionary-signaling framing is contested science and risks re-centering women's clothing on male sexual response — the very move [[Dan McClellan]] argues the Bible never makes. On this view, even a *positive* theology of sexual signaling keeps women's bodies under a male-gaze evaluative frame.

### Counter-objection
Hansen's reply: the framing applies *symmetrically* to men (shoulders, status display) and is aimed at *internal* virtue, not external policing — "make modesty a quest not only in how you dress but... in the kind of person that you are." The point of affirming the body's goodness is pastoral: to counter shame-based teaching that treats sexuality as bad, locating the moral question in *moderation and intent* rather than in the body itself. He keeps chastity central (sexuality finds "its full fulfillment and expression within the context of our marriages"), so the "good when moderated" claim is bounded by covenant, not a license for promiscuity. See [[The Law of Chastity as a Family-Stability Engine]].

## Best Quotes
> "Sexual signaling by dressing in an attractive manner is not bad. So much of what we call beautiful in both men and women is tied to this design by God for us to be sexual beings, and sexual signaling in moderation is what creates this beauty." [^3]

> "Fire is a wonderful and useful and beautiful thing in the right context, but only when it's controlled... but bringing a flamethrower to roast a marshmallow is a bad idea." [^4]

> "I'm so glad that I'm not part of a religion that says our sexual natures are bad. They're not; they just need to be moderated." [^1]
