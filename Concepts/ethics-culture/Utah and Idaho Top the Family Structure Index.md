# Utah and Idaho Top the Family Structure Index

## The Point
The Institute for Family Studies' 2026 Family Structure Index ranks **Utah #1 and Idaho #2** in the nation — the two states with the highest concentration of Latter-day Saints. The hosts argue this is "no coincidence": where a critical mass of practicing Latter-day Saints shapes the surrounding society, the result is measurably stronger families, which they read as observable "fruit" of the restored gospel (Matthew 7:16-20).

## Evidence
1. **The index itself.** Utah scores 102 on the 2026 Family Structure Index, built from three benchmarks — married prime-age (≈30-33) adults, married-parent families, and birth rates. Utah has the highest share of married prime-age adults and of teens living with married parents, plus the sixth-highest fertility rate; Idaho is second.[^1]

2. **The LDS-concentration overlap.** The hosts' central inference: "the two states that rank the highest on the family structure index have the highest population of members of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints in the entire country."[^2] This is the same "by their fruits" empirical move developed at length in [[The Fruits of the Church - Do Critics Have a Better Program]] and [[Where Are the Fruits - Following the Critics' Narrative to Its Conclusion]].

3. **Corroborating rankings (cited secondhand).** Beyond family structure, Utah is cited as #1 for volunteerism, economic outlook, births to married mothers, weekly church attendance, and charitable donation, plus "best state in the nation" four years running per US News, and sometimes the "happiest state."[^3] These attributions are made on-air to a "Clint Teppel" index and US News and are *not* independently verified in the episode — see [[Correlation Is Not Proof - The Honest Limit of the Fruits Argument]].

4. **The "it bleeds out" mechanism.** The hosts argue the benefit is not confined to members: a heavily-LDS state becomes known as "family-friendly," which draws and retains family-minded people of every faith, so the fruit "emanates to others as well."[^4]

## Best Quotes
> "I don't think it's any coincidence at all that the two states that rank the highest on the family structure index have the highest population of members of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints in the entire country." [^2]

> "It's not because everyone in the state is a Latter-day Saint, it's because Latter-day Saints, when they're involved in the structure of society, they build a place that supports everybody ... and that is one of the fruits of the gospel." [^4]

> "If that doesn't qualify as fruit, I don't know what does." [^5]

## LDS Connection
This is a popular-media instance of the social-science "fruits" apologetic catalogued in [[The Fruits of the Church - Do Critics Have a Better Program]] and [[Latter-day Saints Rank High in Well-being and Spiritual Peace]]. It applies the Savior's diagnostic — "by their fruits ye shall know them" ([[Matthew 7.16-20|Matthew 7:16-20]]) — to a fresh dataset (the 2026 Family Structure Index). Its honest limit, that aggregate state-level correlation cannot by itself establish either causation or the gospel's *truth*-claims, is treated in [[Correlation Is Not Proof - The Honest Limit of the Fruits Argument]].
