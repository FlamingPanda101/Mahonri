# LDS Abortion Position Follows From Pre-Existence Not Cultural Caving (Objection Rebutted)

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
[[Joe Heschmeyer]] predicts an "ongoing watering down" of LDS distinctives "on the sexual front," and on abortion charges that the Church "refuses to call abortion murder because they sometimes permit it" — "logically, this is just caving" to culture, such that "you can sometimes kill an innocent human being and be justified."[^1]

## The Rebuttal

1. **The exceptions are narrow, not "convenience" (rated: strong, factual).** [[Jacob Hansen]] and [[Cardon Ellis]]: the permitted cases are rape, incest, and life of the mother — not premeditated, not elective. Ellis: such cases are "less than one-half of 1% of all abortions. The 99.5% that are of convenience or ideological, we wholeheartedly say are murder." Understanding rare exceptions "doesn't mean we are somehow morally incapable of understanding evil and murder."[^2] He gives the concrete case of his wife's life-threatening hematoma, where their bishop/OB-GYN counseled that termination would be justified to save her life — "that would not be a murder."[^3]

2. **The deeper reason is pre-existence theology, which Catholics lack (rated: strong, doctrinal).** Hansen: "We do not have a position [on murder of the unborn in these cases] because we *pre-exist*." For every other Christian theology, the soul is created ex nihilo at conception; for LDS, the person is already a spirit with rational faculties, and the open question is *when* that spirit becomes inextricably linked to the body. Since LDS "don't know when the spirit enters the body," the moral calculus differs categorically — and there is hope (not doctrine) that a terminated pregnancy's spirit "gets another chance" or had not yet entered.[^4] See [[Pre-Existence Solves the Problem of Evil]] and [[Spirit Birth and Intelligences]].

3. **The "house with a possible occupant" analogy (rated: moderate, illustrative).** Hansen: firing into a house *known* to be occupied differs legally from a situation where someone *might* be there; abortion's narrow LDS exceptions occur early, with required spiritual confirmation — "it's never the woman's choice; she always has to get a spiritual confirmation first."[^5] *Steelman:* a pro-life Catholic responds that (a) permitting the *intentional* killing of an innocent even in 0.5% of cases is, on classical natural-law grounds, intrinsically wrong regardless of motive; (b) the "we don't know when ensoulment occurs" move could, taken consistently, justify far more than the LDS Church permits; and (c) the panel's own internal disagreement (Rapley says she "probably wouldn't have taken that position" Hansen articulated) shows the position is genuinely contested, lending some weight to Heschmeyer's "in flux" framing. The LDS reply is that *bounded* exceptions grounded in a *different metaphysics of personhood* are not the same as "caving to culture."

4. **The political point (rated: moderate, rhetorical).** Hansen: given the Church's resources on social issues, "you'd really want them in your corner if you were passionate about that subject" — so misrepresenting the LDS position is self-defeating for a pro-life ally.[^6]

## Best Quotes

> "We do not have a position on [this] because we pre-exist. Catholics don't believe we pre-exist. The moment the sperm and the egg go together... a new human being is created out of nothing... Whereas for us, you are a spirit already with cognitive faculties."[^4]

> "Less than one-half of 1% of all abortions. The 99.5% that are of convenience or ideological, we wholeheartedly say they're murder."[^2]

> "It's never the woman's choice. She always has to get a spiritual confirmation first that that pregnancy can be terminated."[^5]

## Corroboration: Thoughtful Faith "Mormon Beliefs On Abortion"
[[Jacob Hansen]] develops the same position at length with the guest "Luke." Key additions: (a) the church's exact verbiage is softer than "always forgivable" — "as far as has been revealed, a person may repent and be forgiven for the sin of abortion," holding the cards close to the chest.[^7] (b) The exceptions are not a blank check: they require *spiritual confirmation* ("a discussion... between you and your father in heaven"), and even then Hansen guesses justification applies maybe ~10% of even the rape/incest cases.[^8] (c) The grounding is the **sacred-human-body-on-a-spectrum** model — a body alive and developing but not yet a full person, illustrated by the exhumed-corpse and dead-grandma examples (a dead body is still treated as sacred, yet may be disturbed for a higher purpose like solving a murder).[^9] This is the same metaphysic as [[The IVF Reductio Against Conception-as-Ensoulment]]; the consistency challenge that drives it is [[The Morning-After-Pill Prosecution Dilemma]].
