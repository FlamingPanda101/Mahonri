# You Cannot Get From Facts to Values Without an Interpretive Structure

## The Point

[[Jordan Peterson]]'s central philosophical move: you cannot derive values directly from facts, because an *a priori interpretive structure* always mediates between the perceiver and the data. He argues this structure is best understood, at least in part, as a **story or personality** — an evolved framework that the bare facts of one lifetime cannot generate.

## Argument (steelman)

1. **The Kantian premise.** "I can't see how you can interpret the world of facts without an a priori interpretive structure... it's the claim of Kant that you can't get directly from the fact to the value because there's an interpretive framework that mediates between you and the facts."[^1]

2. **Harris partly concedes the framework but denies it is a story.** Our intuition of truth "is certainly deeper than religion; it's not best captured by stories." Even if its historical roots are in myth, that is not an argument that it is *now* best captured by myth.[^2]

3. **Three kinds of a priori intuition.** Peterson: alongside Kant's space and time, there is "a third category of a priori intuitions that are in fact stories, or personalities." Harris counters that intuitions (e.g. 2+2=4) are unanalyzed bedrock we "bootstrap" from — but they are not narratives.[^3]

4. **The evolutionary timescale.** The interpretive structures are products of "3.5 billion years" of evolution; "you're evanescent and you can't derive the right values from the facts that portray themselves to you in your lifespan, which is why you have a biological structure that's 3.5 billion years old."[^4]

5. **Not all intuitions are equal — a hierarchy.** Both agree the framework doesn't make every intuition respectable (Harris: an intuition that "Joseph Smith saw golden tablets" is not the kind in play). Peterson proposes a *hierarchy of moral intuitions*: the deepest (like "hell is bad, heaven is good") are "context-independent" the way protons are real "no matter what span of time and space you pick," while shallow ones are local adaptations.[^5]

6. **The unresolved tension.** The debate ends (tabled "for tomorrow night") on exactly this point: Harris grounds values in present facts; Peterson grounds them in the evolved, story-shaped interpretive structure that the facts produced. See [[The Worst Possible Misery Is Bad - Harris's Moral Floor]].[^6]

## Best Quotes

> "I can't see how you can interpret the world of facts without an a priori interpretive structure... you can't get directly from the fact to the value because there's an interpretive framework that mediates between you and the facts."[^1]

> "There's a third category of a priori intuitions that are in fact stories — or their personalities, or stories."[^3]
