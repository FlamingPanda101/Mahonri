# Harris's Offensive - Problem of Evil, Divine Command, and Euthyphro

## The Point
Rather than defend his own metaethics line by line, Harris spends much of his rebuttal time attacking the *moral consequences* of Craig's theism — arguing that "a belief in God is not only unnecessary for a universal morality… it is itself a source of moral blindness."[^1] He marshals four linked charges: **(1) the problem of evil** — "9 million children die every year before they reach the age of five," with believing parents praying in vain; any God who permits this "either can do nothing to help them or doesn't care to. He is therefore either impotent or evil."[^2] **(2) the problem of the unevangelized** — 1.2 billion Hindus and the world's Muslims are "doomed… through no fault of their own" for being "born into the wrong culture," which "has absolutely nothing to do with moral accountability."[^3] **(3) biblical morality** — a God who would "guide us morally with a book" that "supports slavery" and commands killing for "witchcraft," and who orders the slaughter of the Amalekites, is morally indefensible.[^4] **(4) Euthyphro / divine command** — on Craig's view "God doesn't have to be good. Whatever he commands is good," which Harris calls "a psychopathic and psychotic moral attitude" that "offers no retort to the jihadist other than… you happen to have the wrong god."[^5]

## Evidence
Harris sharpens the Euthyphro horn into a practical reductio: divine command theory "suggests that if God only tells you to sacrifice your firstborn son, it is good to do it," so "there are people who kill their children thinking they're Abraham who just didn't get interrupted by an angel."[^6] He presses the double standard: believers cite "merely human understanding of God's will" to "establish his goodness" when good things happen, but retreat to "God is mysterious" when "children by the tens of thousands are torn from their parents' arms and drowned" — "this is how you play tennis without the net."[^7]

**Craig's replies (stated fairly).** Craig calls the problem of evil and biblical-atrocity charges "red herrings" — strictly *irrelevant* to two *conditional* claims about whether God *would* ground morality *if* he exists (see [[Craig's Two Contentions - The Conditional Strategy of the Moral Argument]]). He counter-presses that evil is actually evidence *for* God: "if evil exists, then it follows that objective values and duties do exist… so evil actually proves the existence of God" ([[Borrowing Objective Morality to Condemn Evil]]).[^8] On Euthyphro, Craig denies the voluntarist horn Harris attacks — God's commands "must be consistent with his holy and loving nature," so God "did not command" the jihadist's atrocities; Harris and Craig "say the very same thing to the Taliban," the difference being only whether God exists to ground the judgment.[^9] On the biblical-slavery charge Craig defers to Paul Copan's *Is God a Moral Monster?* On Euthyphro proper, Harris in the Q&A states the dilemma classically and argues God's say-so "doesn't make it more good" — it is "either intrinsically good or it isn't" (the dichotomy the vault answers via the third horn).[^10]

## Best Quotes
> "Any god who would allow children by the millions to suffer and die in this way… is therefore either impotent or evil." [^2]

> "God doesn't have to be good. Whatever he commands is good… here we're being offered a psychopathic and psychotic moral attitude." [^5]

> "That's exactly your retort, Sam — that God has not issued such a command… If God did, he would be evil." (Craig) [^9]

## LDS Connection
Harris's Euthyphro and "psychopathic divine command" charges land hardest against a *pure voluntarist* theism — and the Restoration already rejects that target ([[Restored Gospel Rejects Divine Command Theory]]; [[Divine Command Theory Is Metaethics Not Ethics]]). LDS theology grounds the good not in God's bare will but in **eternal law** that God himself honors ([[God Is Constrained by Eternal Law Not Unconstrained]]; [[D&C 130.20-21]]), the "third horn" of Euthyphro ([[God's Nature as the Standard - The Third Horn of Euthyphro]]); so a command to sacrifice the innocent for no morally sufficient reason could not be a genuine divine command — which is precisely Craig's reply, made even firmer. The problem-of-evil and unevangelized charges, far from being mere "red herrings," receive *positive* Restoration answers the debate format excludes: pre-mortal existence and progression ([[Pre-Mortal Existence Dissolves the Problem of Hell]]; [[Pre-Existence Solves the Problem of Evil]]) and degrees of glory replacing a binary heaven/hell ([[LDS Progression Model vs Binary Heaven and Hell]]). On biblical atrocities the LDS apologist can grant more than Craig does — that scriptural texts can carry the marks of their human authors and editors ([[Deuteronomistic Corruption of the Old Testament]]) — without conceding Harris's deeper point, since the *ground* of morality remains the eternal law, not the contested text. The sibling debate [[Problem of Evil and the Harms of Religion - Craig vs Hitchens]] catalogs Craig's standard handling of these same New-Atheist attacks.
