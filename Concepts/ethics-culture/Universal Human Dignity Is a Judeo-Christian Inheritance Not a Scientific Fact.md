# Universal Human Dignity Is a Judeo-Christian Inheritance Not a Scientific Fact

## The Point
The conviction that *every* human being possesses equal, inviolable worth is not a finding of science or a deliverance of biology — it is a moral inheritance from the Judeo-Christian tradition that "transformed Western society." [[Jacob Hansen]] argues that the critic [[Bill Reel]]'s belief in "Universal human dignity" is "the product of the modern culture" he was raised in, which is in turn "the product of a judeo-christian ethic." Historically it is "a total anomaly," and biologically it "makes no sense," since "inferior and Superior is the name of the game in biological reproduction." The upshot: a secular critic who wields universal human dignity against the Church is wielding a Christian instrument while denying the workshop it came from — a concrete instance of borrowing objective morality (see [[Borrowing Objective Morality to Condemn Evil]]).

## Evidence
Hansen offers this as the steel-manned version of his own case. Your moral system is culturally seated: "if you grew up in Rome you'd have a totally different moral system; if you grew up in ancient Aztec society… your moral view… is the product of the modern culture that you're a part of."[^1] That modern culture is downstream of revelation: it "is the product of a judeo-christian ethic which transformed Western society's intellectual way that they view the world," so "now everyone in Western society believes in Universal human dignity."[^2] And that belief is doubly unexpected — "a total anomaly in history" that "scientifically makes no sense because biologically like inferior and Superior is the name of the game in biological reproduction."[^3]

Reel concedes the historical genealogy: "I agree with you that the judeo-christian framing deeply contributed to us moving into the modern moment where folks do feel safer."[^4] His attempted escape is to relocate the ground in [[Sam Harris]]-style science — that we can "use science to dictate morality."[^5] But this only relocates the problem, since (as Hansen presses elsewhere in the interview) science describes cause and effect and cannot by itself prescribe that all humans matter equally. The vault source [[Uncommon Ground - Did Christianity Give Us Our Morality]] develops the same historical thesis at length.

## Best Quotes
> "the modern culture that you're a part of is the product of a judeo-christian ethic which transformed Western society's intellectual way that they view the world, to where now everyone in Western society believes in Universal human dignity." [^2]

> "which is a total anomaly in history and scientifically makes no sense, because biologically like inferior and Superior is the name of the game in biological reproduction." [^3]

> "while I agree with you that the judeo-christian framing deeply contributed to us moving into the modern moment where folks do feel safer…" [^4]

## LDS Connection
This argument supplies the positive ground the Darwinian account lacks (see [[Evolution Cannot Ground Objective Morality]]): universal human worth follows from every person being a child of God made in His image, not from utility or fitness. The historical claim — that the dignity of the individual entered the West through the Judeo-Christian stream — is congenial to a restored-gospel reading of God working through covenant history to teach the value of the soul. And it disarms a common critical posture: the secular reformer who indicts the Church in the name of equal human dignity is appealing to a value his own naturalism cannot generate, which both undercuts the borrowed critique and points back toward its transcendent source (see [[A Critic Must Ground His Moral Truth Claims or He Is Borrowing Them]]; [[Moral Argument Against Atheism]]).
