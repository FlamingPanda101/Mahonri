# Intersex Conditions Do Not Prove Gender Is Separate From Sex

## The Claim
[[Steven Woodford]] (Rationality Rules) argues that rare intersex conditions show some biological males are women, so [[Matt Walsh]]'s "adult human female" definition fails. His centerpiece is **complete androgen insensitivity syndrome (CAIS)** — "Jane," a person with XY chromosomes and internal testes who develops outwardly female (wide hips, breasts, shallow vagina, no uterus), is raised as a girl, marries, adopts children, and only later learns she is genetically male. Woodford says calling Jane "really a man" would be cruel and "untenable," so we should say her *sex* is male but her *gender* is woman. [[Trent Horn]] answers that hard intersex cases are precisely cases where we are unsure whether **male or female** applies at all — not evidence that the gendered concepts "man/woman" float free of biology. Jane is "a man with a tragic sex developmental disorder," and the shock of that discovery no more dissolves the category of sex than discovering a 13-year-old who looks adult dissolves the category of age.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **(Strong, the key move)** Intersex disorders are *disorders of sex development* — failures to realize a determinate male/female pathway — so they concern the *clarity of sex*, not the *separability of gender from sex*. "Our inability to determine sex would also crop up in determining gender," since the video offers no workable definition of a female *gender*.[^2]
2. **(Strong, the survey)** Horn walks the conditions Woodford raises — CAIS, Klinefelter (XXY), Swyer/XY gonadal dysgenesis, de la Chapelle (XX male), persistent Müllerian duct syndrome — and shows each is intelligibly classified by *orientation toward gamete production/gestation*, and that proper medical treatment (e.g. hormone therapy for Klinefelter males) "only makes sense if [they] are men, not women."[^3]
3. **(Moderate, age analogy)** The "Martha" thought experiment: a girl who looks 18 but is 13 is still 13; we do not invent a "schender" category reducing age to appearance to spare the difficulty, so we should not invent gender-apart-from-sex from rare cases like Jane.[^1]

## Opposing View (Steelmanned)
Woodford's strongest version is the *utility-plus-compassion* argument: whatever the metaphysics, treating Jane — who has lived her whole life as a woman, with female anatomy and social role — as a *man* would inflict real harm (dissolving her marriage, expelling her from women's spaces) for no benefit, so the *useful* definition counts her a woman. And CAIS genuinely shows that chromosomes alone do not fix social or even anatomical sex presentation, so "sex is a cluster, not a single switch" is a defensible empirical claim.

## Counter-objection
The compassion appeal misfires because it compares only the costs of the traditional view while ignoring the far larger costs of the alternative (see [[The Utility Argument Cuts Against Severing Gender From Sex]]): admitting Jane as a "woman" on the basis of *enough female characteristics* does nothing to count most transgender women as women (they lack those characteristics), so Woodford's definition either fails his own goal or collapses into "woman = whoever claims to be one." And "sex is a cluster" does not entail "sex is separable from biology" — a cluster concept with disputed borders (the *fallacy of the beard*: stubble has no sharp start, yet clean-shaven and bearded are still real) still has clear central cases and a biological basis in orientation toward reproductive role ([[Boundary Cases Do Not Dissolve the Sex Binary - The Fallacy of the Beard]]). Compassion toward a person with a misleading presentation is satisfied by *accommodation*, not by erasing the objective category.

## Apologetic Engagement
This is a natural-law / anthropology dispute that Latter-day Saints and Catholics share. The effective engagement concedes that CAIS and similar conditions are real and tragic, then presses the two pivots: (a) intersex cases are *sex-ambiguity*, not *gender-without-sex*; (b) any proposed female-gender criterion based on "enough characteristics" has no non-arbitrary threshold and slides into pure self-identification.

## LDS Relevance
The Restoration's account of male and female as eternal, complementary, and grounded in the body coheres with Horn's defense — see [[Male and Female Balance Is Required for the Divine Image and Human Flourishing]] and [[Masculinity Should Preside Within Each Person Not Just Between the Sexes]]. The "redefine the words" tactic Horn resists is the same one the vault flags for *person/being* in trinitarian debate ([[Persons-and-Beings Were Redefined Like Gender and Sex]]).
