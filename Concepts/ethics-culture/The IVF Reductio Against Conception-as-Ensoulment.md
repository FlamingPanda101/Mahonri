# The IVF Reductio Against Conception-as-Ensoulment

## The Claim

[[Jacob Hansen]] argues that the premise *"a human person (an ensouled life) exists from the moment of conception"* generates absurd conclusions, and therefore should be rejected in favor of a *delayed-ensoulment* view (which he attributes to [[Augustine]] and "the more ancient" tradition). His central instrument is a *reductio ad absurdum* built on reproductive biology: if every fertilized egg is already a person, then ordinary facts about human reproduction force monstrous moral verdicts that no one is willing to accept. He stresses this is *not* a defense of elective abortion — the official LDS position opposes it with only narrow exceptions — but a refutation of the specific metaphysical claim that *biological life = ensouled personhood at conception*.[^1]

The reductio has three prongs:[^2]
1. **The IVF-murderer prong.** If conception makes a person, then women who use IVF — in which multiple eggs are fertilized and the surplus discarded — have committed murder, "the equivalent of shooting her child in the head," and (since murderers go to jail even for negligence) ought to be prosecuted. Hansen is "not prepared to have... grandmothers and murderers go to jail," so he rejects the premise.
2. **The failed-implantation prong.** In normal intercourse 30–60% of fertilized eggs never implant. If each is an infant, every such loss is "the equivalent of the death of an infant," making ordinary procreative sex a mass-casualty event.
3. **The ban-sex prong.** If we then imagine a technology with a 90% single-embryo implantation rate, the "moral, logical consequence" would be to *ban sex* and mandate IVF as the safer way to bring life into the world — which Hansen grants "sounds absurd," precisely his point: the absurdity tracks back to the conception premise.

Hansen pairs this with a positive account: an early embryo is "a sacred human body in development" whose desecration would be gravely wrong (his exhumed-and-paraded-corpse analogy), *without* being murder — so one can hold abortion to be "deeply and horribly wrong" while denying it is always homicide of a person.[^3]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The reductio is valid in form (rate: strong as a dilemma; contested in soundness).** The argument does not assert that IVF is fine; it asserts that *if* conception-ensoulment is true, IVF-and-surplus-disposal and even procreative sex entail conclusions (mass prosecution, banning sex) that pro-life conception-defenders themselves reject. That is a genuine cost the conception view must pay or absorb. (Evidence: a clean modus-tollens structure — deny the consequent, deny the antecedent.)[^2]
2. **A patristic pedigree for delayed ensoulment (rate: real but partial).** [[Augustine]] did hold that ensoulment ("animation") came sometime after conception, and the delayed-hominization view was widespread in the ancient and medieval West (cf. [[Thomas Aquinas]]'s reliance on Aristotelian successive-soul biology). Hansen leverages this to show that "life begins at conception" is not the unanimous Christian tradition. *Caveat raised in the debate:* Augustine's biology was simply *wrong* — he thought there was no movement for 30–60 days; modern embryology shows early animation — so the empirical premise Augustine used is false even if the metaphysical distinction (body vs. ensouled person) survives.[^4]
3. **Fits the LDS metaphysic of pre-existent spirits (rate: strong, doctrinal).** Because Latter-day Saints hold the person pre-exists as a spirit and the open question is *when* the spirit becomes inextricably linked to the developing body, "a biological thing that is alive" need not yet be a complete soul. This is why Hansen can say a body can be alive and growing without yet being "ensouled," and even entertain (without endorsing) that a lost pregnancy's spirit "gets another chance." See [[A Body from the Physical Creation Joined to a Premortal Spirit]] and [[LDS Abortion Position Follows From Pre-Existence Not Cultural Caving]].

### Steelman of the Catholic / pro-life reply ([[Joe Heschmeyer]])

Heschmeyer does **not** concede the reductio. His responses, fairly stated:
- **Embryology, not Augustine, is decisive.** Augustine "is just biologically wrong"; the early embryo *is* a living, self-directing human organism, so the conception view rests on biology Augustine lacked. Hansen's appeal to a pre-modern delayed-animation view borrows an obsolete empirical claim.[^4]
- **Russian-roulette intent breaks the prongs — but only for *intentional* destruction.** Heschmeyer is willing to say "don't do destructive IVF," and that intentionally destroying embryos *is* homicide. The reductio's force depends on collapsing *foreseen-but-unintended* embryo loss (failed implantation, which no one chooses) into *chosen* killing — a collapse natural-law ethics rejects via the principle of double effect. Natural sexual relations do not *intend* the loss of any embryo, so no murder occurs there; deliberately creating and discarding surplus embryos is a different act.[^5]
- **The line should default to protecting life under uncertainty.** "You defer on not murdering": if we are unsure whether a person is present, the morally safe default is not to kill — the inverse of using uncertainty to permit killing. And Luke 1:41 (the unborn John leaping in the womb) suggests full personal life very early.[^6]
- **A bullet Heschmeyer will bite.** He grants the hard edge directly: a woman who *intentionally* takes innocent human life at *any* developmental stage "has committed murder... I'm sorry to be the first one to explain to you what the definition of murder is" — while immediately adding that even murderers are offered immediate forgiveness in Christ (Psalm 51). So for Heschmeyer the reductio is not absurd; it is simply unwelcome, and the conception view accepts its implications rather than abandoning the premise.[^7]

### Where they converge

Both Hansen and Heschmeyer end up agreeing that **abortion is not *always* murder** — but for different reasons. Hansen gets there by denying conception-ensoulment (no person yet to be murdered); Heschmeyer gets there by the [[Homicide Is Not Always Murder - The Mens Rea Distinction|mens-rea distinction]] (a person is present, but culpable murder requires knowledge and intent). Heschmeyer uses this convergence rhetorically — "you yourself seem to be indicating that what they're doing isn't murder" — to argue Hansen did not actually need the delayed-ensoulment metaphysics to reach his moral conclusion.[^8]

> "If anyone in here has intentionally taken human life at any stage of development and it's innocent life, you have committed murder." — Heschmeyer, biting the bullet[^7]

## Corroboration: Thoughtful Faith "Mormon Beliefs On Abortion"
[[Jacob Hansen]] runs the **same IVF reductio** with the guest "Luke": if every fertilized egg is a person, then IVF (which fertilizes many eggs and discards the surplus) makes the woman a mass murderer, and one would have to "ban sex" in favor of higher-implantation IVF — absurd consequences that, Hansen argues, track back to the conception-ensoulment premise.[^9] He adds the natural-loss point (30–60% of fertilized eggs never implant) and the **double-effect** framing borrowed from just-war reasoning (you may justly kill in war for a higher good; surplus IVF embryos may be "disposed of" in pursuit of children because they are sacred *bodies*, not yet ensouled persons).[^10] The church's IVF guidance ("discourages" donor gametes but leaves it to "prayerful consideration") fits the *gray-area*, sacred-body model rather than a strict person-at-conception rule.[^11] The consistency lever Hansen uses to motivate the whole position is [[The Morning-After-Pill Prosecution Dilemma]].
