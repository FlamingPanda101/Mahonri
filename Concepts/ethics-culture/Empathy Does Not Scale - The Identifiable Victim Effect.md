# Empathy Does Not Scale — The Identifiable Victim Effect

## The Point
[[Sam Harris]] argues that our evolved, story-driven moral intuitions produce a "moral illusion": compassion is maximal for one identifiable victim and *diminishes* as more victims are added, so moral progress requires *correcting* protagonist-shaped empathy with quantified reasoning.[^1]

## Argument

1. **The [[Paul Slovic]] study.** Tell subjects about one named, pictured needy little girl → maximal altruistic response. Tell another group about the same girl *plus* her needy brother → the response *drops*. "Just the addition of a single person diminishes the response."[^1]

2. **It is incoherent.** If you care at "level 10" for Lila, you cannot coherently give *less* when told it is Lila *and* her brother — yet people do. Hence we watch "four hours a day with tear-stained compassion the saga of the little girl who fell down the well" while "blindly turning the channel" on a genocide killing hundreds of thousands.[^2]

3. **Moral progress = de-biasing via quantification.** For how we spend foreign aid, fight wars, and allocate resources, "we have to correct for what is in fact a moral illusion," moving "from our story-driven, protagonist-driven intuitions to something far more quantified." This invokes the heuristics-and-biases program of [[Daniel Kahneman]] and [[Amos Tversky]]. See [[The Moral Landscape - Grounding Value in Conscious Well-Being]].[^3]

4. **Peterson's rejoinder — the heuristic is reliable, not broken.** That a heuristic can be placed in a frame producing contradictory outcomes "doesn't mean that the heuristics themselves are deeply flawed"; they evolved precisely because they "produce a reliable result" most of the time — "why in the hell would they evolve if they didn't produce" a useful outcome? Empathy has "a narrow domain of utility," and the mismatch with genocide-scale numbers reflects our *limits*, an evolved structure not matching the present, not irrationality. *Steelman: a tool that misfires under contrived framing is still the tool that built cooperation.*[^4]

## Best Quotes
> "Just the addition of a single person diminishes the response... this is a moral fallacy that we're all living out every day." [^1]

> "People who will watch four hours a day with tear-stained compassion the saga of the little girl who fell down the well but who will blindly turn the channel when they're hearing about a genocide." [^2]

> "You can put them in a situation where they produce a counterproductive response, but that doesn't mean that generally speaking in most situations they don't produce a useful outcome." [^4]
