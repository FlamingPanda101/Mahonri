# Masculinity Should Preside Within Each Person Not Just Between the Sexes

#type/claim

## The Claim

[[Tikla]] (with [[Andrea Woodmansee]]) advances a psychological reading of "masculinity presides":
the claim is not merely that men preside over women, but that **within each individual person** —
male or female — a masculine principle (boundaries, justice, assertiveness, stability) should
*preside over* a feminine principle (openness, nurture, mercy, creativity), and that this internal
ordering is what *frees* the feminine to flourish. Crucially, "masculine/feminine" is explicitly
**not** "man/woman": everyone is an admixture of both, like the yin-yang's dot of each in the other.[^1]

The argument's striking move is a *partial concession to feminism*: when feminists tell women to be
assertive, to set boundaries, to refuse to be a "doormat," they are — without naming it — telling
women to **let masculinity preside** within themselves, "and that part they have right." Where
feminism goes wrong, on this view, is the *second* step: declaring masculinity good *inside* a woman
but bad in a man and bad for the world, thereby "elevating femininity as the thing that should
lead," which would be "chaos."[^2] This is the same author-circle's [[Womanism Is Misnamed Masculinism That Devalues the Feminine|womanism critique]] turned inward and psychologized.

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. The internal-ordering thesis — rate: moderate (psychological framing)
Tikla maps the principle onto the body's right ("presiding") and left ("life-giving") sides and onto
the square (bounded, stable) needing the plumb-bob to align it to God — humility. Applied within the
self: a person "oriented only to themselves" (square without calibration) lacks true authority; only
when the masculine is "in line with God" can it serve as the stable foundation that *protects*, and a
shelter exists to be *filled* — so the masculine creates the bounded "sacred space" the feminine then
fills with life.[^3] Compare [[Genesis 3.16 Means Rule Not Preside]] for the lexical debate over
"preside" vs "rule" the presenters are (perhaps unknowingly) leaning on.

### 2. The feminist half-truth — rate: moderate (cultural diagnosis)
Feminism rightly identifies that some women are *so* feminine they have no assertiveness or
boundaries and need to develop the "right-side square words"; and some men need to integrate more of
the feminine. The error is universalizing "masculine is bad" rather than stopping at this
re-balancing. Tikla notes that culturally the *masculine* form is the one used to represent everyone
("actors," not "actresses"; "gay marriage," not "lesbian marriage"), which she reads as evidence the
masculine is the naturally *presiding/universal* register.[^4]

### 3. Justice precedes mercy; the first commandment frees the second — rate: weak (homiletic)
"When there's a justice system we can have mercy; when we're allied with God we can love our
neighbor." If the commandment were simply "love everyone," the feminine would preside and produce
chaos — "you can't just love everyone unless you love God first." The reward for getting the balance
right is consistently *peace* (a "crown of peace"): the forces are creative only when balanced.[^5]

## Steelman of the Opposing View

- **"Masculine presides" is doing contested normative work.** Even granting that everyone blends both
  principles, the leap to "the masculine should *preside* over the feminine" (rather than: they should
  be balanced/integrated as equals) is an assertion, not a demonstration — and sits in tension with the
  presenters' own "balance"/yin-yang language. [[Dan McClellan]]'s point in [[Genesis 3.16 Means Rule Not Preside]] is that the Hebrew of the proof-texts says *rule* (*mashal*), and "preside" is a modern
  softening; a critic will say "masculine presides within the self" inherits that same baggage.
- **The actor/"gay marriage" linguistic data is weak evidence** for a metaphysics of gender — generic
  masculine grammar reflects historical patriarchy, which is precisely what is in dispute.
- **A feminist would reject the framing**, not concede it: telling women to be assertive need not be
  "letting masculinity preside" — one can call assertiveness a *human* virtue mislabeled "masculine."
  The note's own caution (masculine ≠ male) makes the gendered labels look optional.

**What survives:** the *integration* insight — that healthy persons need both bounded structure and
open nurture, and that a society (or self) starved of either becomes unbalanced — is defensible and
coheres with [[Male and Female Balance Is Required for the Divine Image and Human Flourishing]]. The
specific claim that the masculine should *preside* (rather than merely be *present and balanced*) is
the contestable, theology-laden core.
