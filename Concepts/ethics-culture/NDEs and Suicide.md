# NDEs and Suicide

## The Point
Asked whether NDEs following a suicide attempt differ, Moody reports that they do: such experiences "were uniformly characterized as being unpleasant" [^1]. Subjects describe a "limbo" in which the very conflicts they tried to escape persist, now without any power to act, while they watch the harm their act caused [^2]. Both suicide survivors and others who "died" of unrelated causes report being given the sense that suicide is a grave violation — throwing back "God's gift" and abandoning an "assignment" — a view Moody notes coincides with the classic moral arguments of Aquinas, Locke, and Kant [^3].

## Evidence
1. **Uniformly unpleasant.** The few suicide-attempt cases were all unpleasant; one woman: "If you leave here a tormented soul, you will be a tormented soul over there, too" [^1].
2. **Conflicts persist, powerless.** The problems they fled "were still present when they died, but with added complications"; disembodied, "they were unable to do anything about their problems," and had to view the consequences of their acts [^2].
3. **The widower's regret.** A man who shot himself after his wife's death: "I didn't go where [my wife] was. I went to an awful place.... I immediately saw the mistake I had made.... I thought, 'I wish I hadn't done it'" [^2].
4. **Breaking the rules / the assignment.** Others felt they would remain in this state "a long time" as a "penalty for 'breaking the rules' by trying to release themselves prematurely from what was, in effect, an 'assignment'" [^2].
5. **Corroboration from non-suicide NDEs.** A man who nearly died in an accident: "two things it was completely forbidden for me to do would be to kill myself or to kill another person.... If I were to commit suicide, I would be throwing God's gift back in his face" [^2].
6. **Convergence with classical ethics.** These sentiments are "identical to" the oldest moral arguments against suicide — Aquinas (life is God's gift, his alone to take back) and Kant (the suicide arrives "as a rebel against his creator"); Moody stresses he reports rather than passes moral judgment [^3].

## Best Quotes
> "If you leave here a tormented soul, you will be a tormented soul over there, too." [^1]

> "If I were to commit suicide, I would be throwing God's gift back in his face.... Killing somebody else would be interfering with God's purpose for that individual." [^2]

## LDS Connection
The reported sense that mortal life is a divine "gift" and an "assignment" with a purpose one must not abandon harmonizes closely with LDS teaching that this life is a probationary "time to prepare to meet God" (Alma 34:32) and that the body is a sacred gift essential to the plan of salvation. At the same time, LDS doctrine tempers the harshness of these reports with mercy: final judgment of one who dies by suicide belongs to a perfectly just and compassionate God who weighs every circumstance, not to men.
