# Sin in the Heart — Beyond Ledger and Action

## The Point
On sin, the Hebrew mind ("you can only sin in action, not in thought") and the Greek mind ("keep a tally of discrete sins") are *both* presented as incomplete. Jesus transcends both: what matters is neither the outward act alone nor the score, but the orientation of the **heart** — a continuous state of being, not a count of deeds.

## Argument
1. **The Hebrew pole: no sin in thought.** Because Hebrews locate reality in action, "you can't sin in your thought, you sin in your action." Dennis Prager defended this in a debate on pornography — a thought "doesn't even register as a possibility" of sin "because I didn't do anything wrong" [^1].
2. **The Greek pole: the ledger.** Greeks "want a ledger that says what's a sin and count them up — if you get 99 you get into heaven and 100 go to hell." This tallying mindset is how "the Hellenistic Christians wanted to think about it" [^2].
3. **The hard case: Matthew 5:28 (lust).** Jesus says one who looks lustfully "hath committed adultery already in his heart." A pure Hebrew action-ethic would say "no harm, no foul — I didn't *do* anything." So the Hebrew pole, pushed too far, fails here [^3].
4. **The resolution — it's the heart/will, not the act and not the count.** Lust is itself "an act of the will" — a chosen intention — so it *is* doing something, in the heart. Yet Jesus is "not saying that will count a checkmark against you"; he is saying "all that matters is your heart, your intent." Prager "was giving a Hebrew answer to a Greek question"; the question ("is it sin if…?") is itself flawed because "no one's tallying your sins" [^4].
5. **The synthesis: be, don't score.** The worst option is the "horrible marriage" of both errors — Jewish "what did you actually do?" plus Greek "tally it up for the score." Jesus rejects both: "you need to have a heart that is right continuously with God, so that you are a Celestial being in who you are, rather than if you checked all those boxes." Being over bookkeeping [^5].

## Best Quotes
> "It's the Greeks who want a ledger that says what's a sin and count them up, and if you get 99 you get into heaven and 100 you go to hell. Jesus never did that... all that matters is your heart, all that matters is your intent." [^2]

> "That's like the horrible marriage of the worst of both: you have the Jews all about what did you actually do, and then the Greeks that tally all those up for the score — and Jesus is like, you both got it wrong." [^5]
