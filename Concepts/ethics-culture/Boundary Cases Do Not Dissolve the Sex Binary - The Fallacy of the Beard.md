# Boundary Cases Do Not Dissolve the Sex Binary - The Fallacy of the Beard

## The Claim
[[Trent Horn]] argues that the existence of disputed boundary cases does not disprove a categorization system — "every categorization system will have disputed boundary cases, but that does not disprove the entire categorization system." Applied to sex: that there are rare cases (intersex conditions) where it is hard to tell whether *male or female* applies "does not negate our general ability to determine that someone is a man or a woman." He names this the **fallacy of the beard**: "just because I may not know when stubble ends and a beard begins... I certainly know when someone is clean-shaven and when they have a beard." The vast majority of undisputed cases are not undone by the existence of hard cases, so the binary stands even though its edges are fuzzy.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **(Strong, the principle)** Sorites-style vagueness is universal: heaps, bald/hairy, alive/dead all have indeterminate borders yet pick out real, well-populated categories. Horn applies it directly — disputed cases "do not justify turning gender into an amorphous concept that can be applied in virtually any way a person feels."[^1]
2. **(Strong, parallel case)** He runs the same logic on *life/death*: there are boundary cases (brain-dead bodies sustained by nutrients) where it is hard to tell if someone is conscious, yet "I certainly know when someone is" clearly alive or dead. The hard case does not abolish the category.[^1]
3. **(Moderate, burden-shift)** The critic owes a *non-arbitrary threshold*: "How many secondary sexual characteristics does a male need to have in order to be a woman?" Without one, the correlation-definition collapses into self-identification — "the Alice in Wonderland mentality of a woman being anyone who simply claims that they are a woman."[^2]

## Opposing View (Steelmanned)
The defender of a spectrum view can press that *sex itself* is the cluster with fuzzy borders — chromosomes, gonads, hormones, anatomy, and gamete type can come apart (Klinefelter, Swyer, CAIS) — so the "binary with hard cases" framing already concedes that no single criterion is decisive. If the category is constituted by a *bundle* of correlated traits rather than one essence, then a sufficiently atypical bundle (Jane) might reasonably be classified by its *predominant* presentation, which is just what Woodford proposes. On this view Horn's beard analogy cuts both ways: clean-shaven and bearded are clear, but "how much stubble counts" is exactly the live question for borderline persons.

## Counter-objection
A cluster concept with fuzzy borders is still anchored by a *functional* core, and for sex that core is orientation toward one of two reproductive roles (producing small or large gametes) — a genuinely binary teleology even when its developmental markers are disrupted. So the borderline cases are *failures or ambiguities of that binary*, not a third category or a continuum of equally-valid options; "predominant presentation" is not a principled criterion because it imports exactly the appearance-based standard that misclassifies masculine-presenting women and flatters caricatures ([[Appearance Is a Defeasible Guide to Sex Not Its Definition]]). The beard analogy does not cut both ways: the *point* of it is that indeterminacy at the margin is fully compatible with determinacy at the center, so "we can't sort every case" never licenses "therefore no case can be sorted." The burden remains on the spectrum view to state the threshold it needs and cannot supply.

## Apologetic Engagement
This is a general logic-of-vagueness argument usable by any natural-law defender; it is the formal backbone under the intersex discussion ([[Intersex Conditions Do Not Prove Gender Is Separate From Sex]]). The effective engagement is to (a) grant the fuzzy borders, (b) insist on clear central cases, and (c) demand the critic's missing threshold.

## LDS Relevance
The Restoration treats male and female as real, eternal kinds, so the defense of a robust (if vague-edged) sex binary is congenial — see [[Male and Female Balance Is Required for the Divine Image and Human Flourishing]]. The note also exemplifies a method the vault uses elsewhere: refusing to let *hard cases* (boundary disputes) overturn *central cases* (the rule), as in scriptural-literalism threshold debates ([[The Threshold Problem - Where to Draw the Literal-Figurative Line]]).
