# The Falsifiability Demand Against the Air-We-Breathe Thesis

## The Claim (Objection)

[[Alex OConnor|Alex O'Connor]] presses the thesis as unfalsifiable: "It seems like basically anything that the West does or is said to do can in some vague way be traced back to the Bible or the Christian tradition." He repeatedly asks for a **falsifying condition** — "an example of something the West could be doing that would make me say, okay, that's not grounded in Christianity."[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The vegan analogy.** O'Connor: if I claimed "the West is profoundly vegan," you'd refute me by pointing at hot dogs everywhere. A meaningful claim must specify what would count against it. If *no* observation could count against "the West is Christian," the claim is empty.[^2] *(Strong philosophical point — a thesis compatible with every possible observation explains nothing.)*

2. **The trap closes on each candidate.** O'Connor offers the gay-rights movement, feminism, and abortion as plausibly *anti*-Christian; Scrivener can fold them back in ("the motivations are essentially Christian," or "Christian-ish language for pagan ends").[^3] *(This is the heart of the objection: every counter-example gets reabsorbed.)*

3. **Steelman of Scrivener's reply — falsifiability is the wrong tool.** Scrivener: "You're looking for falsifiability when we're not talking about science... You cannot do that with the past. There is only A — and that A has been 20 centuries of profound Christianization."[^4] History has no control group (no population B, no control C), so counterfactuals are "notoriously" untestable.[^5] *(Legitimate — falsificationism is a poor fit for singular historical causation. But it is a double-edged concession: it removes the demand for falsification at the cost of admitting the thesis cannot be empirically confirmed either.)*

4. **Scrivener does eventually name a falsifier.** Under pressure he offers "industrial-scale abortion" and euthanasia as genuinely *pagan*, non-Christian — and "shout your abortion," sheer "I don't care," as not-Christian.[^6] *(Partial answer — see [[Abortion and Euthanasia as Pagan Not Christian]]. O'Connor charges that Scrivener still says abortion is forwarded "using Christian language," reabsorbing it; Scrivener replies O'Connor is mischaracterizing him "quite substantially.")*

## Best Quotes

> "Can you think of a single example of something the West could be doing that would make you say, actually, no, that's definitely not Christian?"[^1]

> "You're looking for falsifiability when we're not talking about science... You literally — we look back at the past. There is not A, B or C past. There is only A."[^4]

> "It seems like it doesn't matter what you point to... you can point to the gay rights movement, to feminism, even to industrial abortion and still say there's some Christian influence going on here."[^7]
