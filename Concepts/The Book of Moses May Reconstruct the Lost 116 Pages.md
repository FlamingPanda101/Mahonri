# The Book of Moses May Reconstruct the Lost 116 Pages

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Jonah Barnes]] floats a speculative theory — which he explicitly flags as "just a theory, so don't shoot me" — that the three [[Book of Moses]] revelations of 1830 may be [[Joseph Smith]] quietly *reconstructing the content of the lost 116 pages* (the Book of Lehi). The reasoning: (1) the Book of Mormon was published in March 1830 and claimed to contain the "fullness of the gospel," so why would Joseph need to reveal *more* scripture weeks later?; (2) the lost Book of Lehi was almost certainly Genesis-heavy, because [[Lehi]] is obsessed with Genesis; (3) Joseph *could not* simply re-dictate the 116 pages (the forgers around [[Lucy Harris]] were waiting to expose any discrepancy); so (4) "suspiciously," right after the Book of Mormon goes to press, he begins receiving dense, unexplained revelation that happens to be all about Genesis — the fall, the expulsion, Enoch. The theory is that the Book of Moses *is* the lost Genesis-content, relabeled and re-received rather than re-translated.[^1][^2][^3]

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. Lehi is statistically obsessed with Genesis
Barnes' load-bearing data point: Lehi speaks **5,552 words** in the Book of Mormon, and **63%** of them reference Genesis ("and yes, I counted"). His most famous sermon, [[2 Nephi 2.11|2 Nephi 2]], "is all Genesis." So a lost *Book of Lehi* would plausibly have contained "similar Genesis-heavy content" — making a Genesis-centered restoration the natural thing to recover.[^4][^5] *(Rate: suggestive correlation, not proof — that Lehi quoted Genesis does not establish the 116 pages were a Genesis restoration.)*

### 2. The timing is "suspicious"
The three revelations arrive June–December 1830 — "literally weeks" after the Book of Mormon is published — "out of nowhere," "and it happens to be about Genesis," with Joseph giving "no explanation as to why he receives them."[^2][^6] Barnes leans on this proximity: "It's awfully suspicious that all of a sudden, right after the Book of Mormon, he's like 'uh,' and then there's these other things."[^7]

### 3. Joseph could not re-issue the 116 pages directly
The South Park-popularized story (translate from the plates of Nephi instead) omits that [[Martin Harris]]'s circle — [[Lucy Harris]] and the document-forger son-in-law — were poised to produce an altered manuscript and cry fraud if Joseph re-dictated the lost text. So a *direct* reconstruction was impossible; an independently *revealed* Genesis text was not.[^3] This is the motive for why the recovery would be relabeled rather than acknowledged.

### 4. The "fullness of the gospel" gap leaves room for it
Because the published 1829 Book of Mormon is only ~60% of the 1823 plates (lost 116 pages + sealed portion), Barnes argues there is missing revealed material the Pearl of Great Price could be supplying — connecting this theory to [[The Book of Mormon Fullness of the Gospel Is a Misread of D&C 20 9]] and [[D&C 20.6-9]].[^8]

## Steelman of the Opposing View
Barnes himself concedes this is unfalsifiable speculation ("Is it the lost 116 pages? I don't know. I don't know"). Several weaknesses: (a) Lehi's *quoting* Genesis in surviving text says nothing about whether the *lost* portion was a Genesis restoration vs. 6th-century-BC Jerusalem narrative — the 116 pages were described as the Book of Lehi's history, not an ancient-patriarch text. (b) The Book of Moses is presented as **Moses's** vision, not Lehi's record, so equating them requires assuming Joseph silently re-channeled the same content through a different ancient author. (c) The mainstream-critical account needs no such theory: the simplest reading is that Joseph began a revelatory revision of Genesis (later the JST) shortly after finishing the Book of Mormon. The theory's appeal is entirely the "suspicious timing," which is equally consistent with Joseph simply turning next to the Bible. It functions as faith-promoting conjecture, not evidence — and is distinct from the more defensible [[The 116 Pages as Evidence of Foreknowledge]] argument, which is about the *small plates*, not the Book of Moses.

## LDS Connection
This is the centerpiece of Barnes' book *The Lost Gems of Genesis* and the episode's most distinctive (and most speculative) claim. It depends on the genre reframing in [[Annidictions - Joseph Smiths Restorations Look Backward Not Forward]] (the Book of Moses as backward-looking *restoration*) and on [[Book of Moses Is a Posthumous Label Joseph Smith Never Gave]] (since Joseph never said what the text *was*, there is room to propose it was the recovered Lehi content). Apologetically it converts an old liability (the lost 116 pages) into a providential asset, paralleling but going well beyond [[The 116 Pages as Evidence of Foreknowledge]].
