# Eternal Damnation Is Gods Not Endless

## The Claim
[[Cardon Ellis]] explains the LDS reading of "eternal damnation": the word *eternal* describes *whose* punishment it is, not its unending duration. Per the Doctrine and Covenants (D&C 19:6-12 logic, referenced loosely), "eternal damnation is eternal because it is mine and I am eternal. It's not eternal because it lasts forever from your conception. It's eternal because I am eternal and it is my damnation." This implies God "can choose to undamn them" once a soul has "eternally progressed" or "paid for their sins" — analogous to a parent ending a child's time-out.[^1] For most who suffer in hell (the spirit-prison/telestial path), the suffering is temporary, not everlasting torment.[^2]

## The Rebuttal
1. **It defuses the "finite sin, infinite punishment" objection by denying the punishment is infinite — rate: strong within LDS scripture.** If most of the damned eventually progress out, the disproportion at the heart of the problem of hell largely dissolves for them (see [[Hell - Finite Sin vs Infinite Punishment]], [[LDS Progression Model vs Binary Heaven and Hell]]). The "eternal = God's" reading is grounded in D&C 19's explicit gloss on "endless/eternal punishment."[^1] **Steelman of the creedal critic:** the LDS gloss is a *minority* reading; the dominant Christian exegesis of *aiōnios* (Matthew 25:46) treats the punishment as everlasting in duration, parallel to "eternal life." Critics argue the D&C reinterpretation is a Restoration overlay, not the plain sense of the NT. Carroll's own panel concedes this is *debatable* even among Latter-day Saints (see point 3).
2. **It preserves a remainder problem for the sons of perdition — rate: honest caveat.** Ellis distinguishes the temporary suffering of most from **outer darkness**, which "does seem pretty primally final in scripture" and is "reserved for so few."[^3] So the LDS solve does not eliminate a final hell entirely; it shrinks its population drastically and leaves the nature of outer darkness genuinely open (see [[Outer Darkness and Whether Progression Ends]]).
3. **Internal LDS dispute flagged — rate: candidly noted.** Carroll states his own doctrinal position differs from a fuller "eternal progression between kingdoms" view: he holds the only post-mortem progression is *out of outer darkness into the telestial kingdom* for telestial heirs, "and that was what Joseph taught," while granting other authorities have taught broader progression and that "it's debatable."[^4] [[Luke Hansen]] reads D&C 76's "never be redeemed... unlike other people" as evidence the sons of perdition lack progression entirely.[^5]

## Best Quotes
> "Eternal damnation is eternal because it is mine and I am eternal. It's not eternal because it lasts forever from your conception. It's eternal because I am eternal and it is my damnation. But I can choose to undamn them if I feel they have eternally progressed or they've paid for their sins." [^1]

> "Most people are going to hell for a certain amount of time, but you can choose to stay there if you want, but I think most of them won't." [^2]

> "Outer darkness does seem pretty primally final in scripture... that's why it's reserved for so few." [^3]
