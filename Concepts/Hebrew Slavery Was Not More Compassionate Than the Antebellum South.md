# Hebrew Slavery Was Not More Compassionate Than the Antebellum South

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
Joshua HS concluded that "the biblical slavery system was actually an incredibly compassionate system that was for the betterment of everyone involved" — and that "apologists are right to say that slavery in the Old Covenant was different than you're picturing."[^1]

## The Rebuttal (Numbered)

1. **Flat rejection (rated: strong).** "All of that is not just wrong, but profoundly ignorant." And: "No, they're not. They're entirely mistaken."[^2]

2. **The law-to-law comparison reverses it (rated: strong).** The secular [[Louisiana 1825 Code Protected Slaves More Than the Bible|Louisiana Civil Code of 1825]] forbade beating a slave to death; [[Exodus 21.20-21|Exodus 21:20-21]] permits it if death is delayed. So biblical law is *less* compassionate on this point.[^3]

3. **Apples-to-oranges error (rated: strong; methodological).** Joshua compared idealized biblical law to the documented atrocities of the South. The fair comparison is law-to-law.[^4] See [[Laws on the Books vs Facts on the Ground]].

4. **Likely brutal in practice (rated: moderate; inference).** What actually happened under biblical slavery was "probably closer to what went on on the ground in the pre–Civil War American South."[^5]

5. **Steelman.** The compassion thesis has a kernel: Israelite debt-bondage included Jubilee release and exit-gifts genuinely absent from chattel regimes. McClellan grants the milder Israelite tier but shows it does not characterize the whole system, and that the protections claimed for slaves generally were narrower (Israelites only), unenforceable ideals, or simply misread texts.[^2]

## Best Quotes

> "The point is to get across that the biblical slavery system was actually an incredibly compassionate system that was for the betterment of everyone involved. — Yeah. All of that is not just wrong, but profoundly ignorant."[^2]

> "So the pre–Civil War American South laws are actually more compassionate than the Bible's laws."[^3]
