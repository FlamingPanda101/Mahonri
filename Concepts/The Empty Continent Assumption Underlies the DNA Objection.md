# The Empty Continent Assumption Underlies the DNA Objection

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)

The DNA objection runs: the Book of Mormon says Jews migrated to the Americas, so
Native American DNA should look Jewish / Near-Eastern; it does not, therefore the
Book of Mormon is false. This argument secretly assumes Lehi's group entered an
**empty continent** and that their descendants are the *sole* ancestors of Native
Americans.[^1]

## The Rebuttal

### 1. Archaeology shows the continent was not empty

The Americas were already populated when Lehi arrived (~600 BC). The premise that
Book of Mormon peoples were the only inhabitants is simply false on the
archaeological record.[^2]

> **Evidence rating:** Strong (uncontested archaeological fact).

### 2. Origin stories everywhere read as "we alone settled here"

Groups routinely tell origin stories that make themselves sound isolated and
self-founding. [[Anne Gibbons]]' 2017 *Science* article on "the myth of pure genetic
heritage" shows European origin myths (Germans, Anglo-Saxons) portray isolated
conquest, while genetics reveals admixture. The stories are not false — just narrow.
The Israelite story traces through Abraham → Isaac → Jacob, yet by the monarchy
Israel numbered tens or hundreds of thousands; Abraham is one covenant ancestor among
many, not the only one. See [[Mormon Is an Ethnocentric Redactor Focused on the Witness of Christ]].[^3]

> **Evidence rating:** Strong (named source + a clean genealogical analogy).

### 3. The text itself implies other peoples

In [[2 Nephi 2.8|2 Nephi 2:8]] Lehi tells the young Jacob "how great the importance
to make these things known unto the **inhabitants of the world**" — odd counsel if
the only people present were Lehi's own family who already knew. [[Matt Roper]] and
[[John Sorenson]] have catalogued many such textual clues pointing to others nearby.[^4]

> **Evidence rating:** Moderate-to-strong (an internal textual argument with scholarly support).

## Steelman of the Opposing View

A critic can argue some Book of Mormon and prophetic statements *do* read the New
World as a land "kept from the knowledge of other nations" and treat the Lamanites as
the principal ancestors of the American Indians — so the empty-continent reading is
not merely a critic's strawman but a traditional LDS reading. The reply: the text's
internal data (and mainstream genetics on admixture) favor the limited-geography,
other-peoples model, and the traditional reading should yield to the better reading
of the same text.

## Best Quotes

> "That's kind of the assumption of the DNA argument... the Book of Mormon says Jews came here and there must have been nobody else here... but just out of the gate that's not true. We know from archaeology it was not an empty continent."[^1]

> "How great the importance to make these things known unto the inhabitants of the world. Well, what inhabitants? If they're the only people there, who are they to?"[^4]

> "There's actually genetic admixture happening... it's not that those stories are totally false... they're just narrow."[^3]
