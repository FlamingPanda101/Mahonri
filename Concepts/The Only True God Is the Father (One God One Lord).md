# The Only True God Is the Father (One God One Lord)

## The Claim
[[Blake Ostler]] argues that the New Testament identifies "the only true God" and "the one God" specifically as **the Father**, while Jesus is "the one Lord" who has been *given* the divine name. In [[John 17.3|John 17:3]] the Father is *ho theos*, "the only true God"; in [[1 Corinthians 8.4-6|1 Corinthians 8:4-6]] Paul writes "for us there is but one God, the Father... and one Lord, Jesus Christ." This is an LDS-friendly Godhead: the Father is supreme, the Son holds a *given* divine name and role. [^1][^2]

## The Rebuttal (Supporting Evidence)

1. **John 17:3 uses the article and a superlative.** "The only true God" (*ho theos*, only-true) is predicated of the Father, the one who *sends* and *gives authority*. *Rating: strong* on the Greek; the text plainly distinguishes the Father as sender from the Son as sent (cf. [[John 17.1-5|John 17:1-5]]). [^1][^3]

2. **1 Corinthians 8:6 splits "God" and "Lord."** Paul appears to refract the Shema (*the LORD our God is one*) onto two figures: *one God = the Father* (source of all things), *one Lord (kyrios = Adonai/Yahweh) = Jesus* (through whom all things came). So the divine *name* (Lord) is borne by Christ while "the one God" is the Father. *Rating: a real exegetical observation* widely noted by scholars; it grounds [[1 Corinthians 8 Paul Affirms Many Gods Exist]] and links to [[The Divine Name Yahweh Was Given to Jesus]]. [^2][^4]

3. **The Father is the *source*, the Son the *executor*.** "The Father is the one who is the source of all things and Christ is the one who executes all things." *Rating: coherent* and consistent with the throne/authority motif (see [[1 Corinthians 15 Christ Hands the Kingdom Back to the Father]]). [^5]

4. **Steelman of the Trinitarian.** 1 Cor 8:6 is a classic Trinitarian prooftext *for* Christ's deity: Paul *includes Jesus within the Shema* (Bauckham's "divine identity") — splitting "God/Lord" between Father and Son means Jesus is named with the *kyrios* (Yahweh) of the Shema itself, which argues he shares the one divine identity, not that he is a subordinate second god. "Only true God" can mark the Father as *fons divinitatis* within the Trinity (monarchy of the Father) without denying the Son's consubstantiality. *Rating: a powerful counter* — the same data underdetermines the metaphysics; it shows the Father has a primacy of *source/honor*, which Ostler himself concedes is the real distinction (one of honor, not lesser deity). [^6]
