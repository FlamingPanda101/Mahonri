# The Gathering of Truths from All Churches

## The Point
Combs marshals statements from Joseph Smith and [[Brigham Young]] to argue that Latter-day Saints are positively *obligated* to seek and gather truth wherever it is found — including among other churches and among ancient Christians — rather than to dismiss other faiths as worthless. The truth of the Restoration "is not dependent on proving that other Christian churches are deficient."

## Evidence
1. **Joseph Smith defended the old churches.** Joseph warned that "the character of the old churches have [sic] always been slandered by all apostates since the world began," and insisted: "The old Catholic church traditions are worth more than all you have said." Combs notes (thanks to Gerrit Dirkmaat) this sermon was actually a rebuke of the apostate William Law, not a treatise on religious history. [^1]
2. **Brigham Young's gathering mandate.** "It is our duty and calling, as ministers of the same salvation and Gospel, to gather every item of truth and reject every error. Whether a truth be found with professed infidels, or with the Universalists, or the Church of Rome, or the Methodists, the Church of England, the Presbyterians, the Baptists, the Quakers, the Shakers, or any other of the various and numerous different sects … it is the business of the Elders of this Church … to gather up all the truths in the world pertaining to life and salvation … and bring it to Zion." [^2]
3. **Application to ancient Christians.** Combs draws the inference explicitly: "Certainly, this practice should also include gathering truths from ancient Christians." The book is thus framed as "an act of devotion" and an act of "turning our hearts to our spiritual fathers and mothers" (cf. Malachi 4:5–6). [^3]

## Best Quotes
> "It is our duty and calling, as ministers of the same salvation and Gospel, to gather every item of truth and reject every error." (Brigham Young) [^2]

> "The old Catholic church traditions are worth more than all you have said." (Joseph Smith) [^1]

## LDS Connection
This is the devotional engine of the entire volume: the gathering-of-truths mandate transforms the study of patristic sources from a threat into a religious duty. It pairs with [[Great Apostasy Is Not a Scriptural Term in LDS Standard Works]] and the principle of "holy envy" articulated in [[A New Latter-day Saint Approach to Ancient Christians]].
