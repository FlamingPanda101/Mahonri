# Jackson County Fire and Sword Prophecy

## The Claim
[[Joseph Smith]] is reported (in later editions of the *History of the Church*) to have warned a man named Donovan against taking Jackson County land in payment of a debt: "God's wrath hangs over Jackson County... you will live to see the day when it will be visited by fire and sword... the fields and farms and houses will be destroyed, and only the chimneys will be left to mark the desolation." During the Civil War, Missouri's border counties were indeed ravaged; a soldier, A. Saxie, described houses and barns burned in 1864 with "nothing left standing but the chimneys."[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The Civil War devastation matches strikingly.** The "only the chimneys left" detail aligns with documented border-war destruction in Missouri, where outside-built chimneys did survive burned structures. (Evidence rating: the *imagery-to-event* fit is vivid — its strength entirely depends on when the prophecy was recorded.)[^1]
2. **It is explicitly absent from contemporary records.** The host checked and reports the quote "was not part of the contemporary history of the church... it first appears in later historical compilations, not written until decades after the US Civil War." (Evidence rating: this is the decisive weakness — see [[Absence of Contemporaneous Sources Undermines a Prophecy]].)[^2]
3. **Compilation timing.** The comprehensive *History of the Church* was completed under [[BH Roberts]] in 1930; the prophecy text does not appear in the volumes compiled from Joseph's own era. (Evidence rating: strong; the chimneys detail could be post-Civil-War reminiscence retrofitted.)[^3]
4. **The host flags his own source caution.** He attributes the "absent from contemporary records" finding to a Google/ChatGPT search and asks viewers to "fact check me," modeling [[The Charitable-but-Critical Believer Posture]]. (Evidence rating: the finding is plausible but not independently verified within the video.)[^2]
5. **Steelman of the believing view.** If even a vaguer "fire and sword" warning about Jackson County genuinely predates the Civil War, the later, fuller wording could be a faithful elaboration of a real prediction rather than invention. But the host concludes the absence of a contemporaneous record "does undermine this being an impressive fulfilled prophecy."

## Best Quotes
