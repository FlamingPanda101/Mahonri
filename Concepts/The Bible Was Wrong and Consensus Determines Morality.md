# The Bible Was Wrong and Consensus Determines Morality

## The Claim
[[Dan McClellan]] argues that because the Bible endorses slavery — which is in fact evil — the Bible is morally **wrong** on this point, and that moral authority rests not in Scripture but in human **consensus**: "It is the consensus that determines what is right and wrong. It is not the Bible because the Bible is wrong about so many things."[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The shared premise inverted (rated: strong; argued).** McClellan and Joshua agree the Bible condones slavery (see [[Hebrew Bible Endorses Slavery from Beginning to End]]). Joshua concludes slavery is therefore "not inherently evil"; McClellan concludes the Bible is wrong: "we know better now."[^2]

2. **Moral progress as evidence (rated: moderate; argued).** The very fact that modern people recognize chattel slavery as evil — against the text — shows morality is not simply read off Scripture.[^1]

3. **Owning a person is wrong (rated: strong; argued).** Against the "it is not inherently evil for one person to own another" thesis, McClellan: "The Bible just does not say that... And the Bible is wrong just like you are wrong. And it's up to us to determine what's right."[^3]

4. **Steelman.** The strongest objection is the classic Euthyphro/grounding problem: if consensus grounds morality, then a consensus *in favor* of slavery (as existed for millennia) would make slavery right — which McClellan would reject. A defender of biblical authority would press that "consensus" cannot by itself condemn the past consensus without an external standard. McClellan does not resolve this meta-ethical tension in the clip; he asserts present moral consensus against the text rather than defending a full grounding theory.[^1]

## Best Quotes

> "The Bible was wrong. And what Joshua is doing is trying to leverage the Bible in order to just endorse a countercultural ethical stance as a piece of costly signaling so he can get attention."[^2]

> "It is the consensus that determines what is right and wrong. It is not the Bible because the Bible is wrong about so many things. And it's right about a lot of things too. But for this discussion, the Bible is woefully and profoundly wrong."[^1]
