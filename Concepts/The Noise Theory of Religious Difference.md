# The Noise Theory of Religious Difference

## The Claim
The pluralist student **Alexa** advances a perennialist thesis: every religion contains the same underlying truth, and **what divides the traditions is "noise."** "I think all of [what] differentiates each religion is all noise" — beneath it lies "a capital-T truth for everybody... the universal things that we can all agree on," and "when there's contention, that's noise." [^1] On this picture God "started all the religions" and "everybody has morphed them"; each culture had "access to God" and merely refracted it into rival doctrines and rules. [^2] The practical upshot: doctrinal contradictions "just don't matter," so one may "throw a dart, pick one, and follow it" so long as it "resonates" and "makes you a better person." [^3] [[Hayden Carroll]] treats this as the heart of the pluralist case and the thing his Resurrection test is designed to break.

## Supporting Evidence (steelman)
1. **A real signal exists under the variation.** The strongest version is not "all religions are identical" but that the great traditions converge on a core — gratitude, self-transcendence, compassion, an ultimate Good — while diverging on culturally-conditioned packaging. Alexa locates the signal precisely in "the universal things that we can all agree on" and the divergence in "contention." [^1] This is the classic *perennial philosophy* / common-core thesis (Huxley, Hick), and the convergence is empirically nontrivial: the Golden Rule and prohibitions on murder, theft, and cruelty recur across unrelated traditions. *Rating: moderate — the descriptive convergence is real; the inference that the divergences are therefore inessential is the contested step.*
2. **Cultural transmission demonstrably shapes doctrine.** Alexa grounds the theory in observation: "the only reason I believe that is because it was brought to this area" by "culture and colonialism." [^4] That much is true and even conceded by Carroll — geography and upbringing heavily condition which tradition one inherits (cf. [[The Genetic Fallacy Against Religious Origins]]). If the *accidental* features are culturally sourced, it is reasonable to suspect the contradictions track the accidents rather than the core. *Rating: strong as stated; it is a fact that culture transmits religion, though this does not by itself locate the core/noise boundary.*
3. **Epistemic humility about the unverifiable.** Alexa repeatedly appeals to ignorance of the deep past — committing hard to one tradition's distinctives is "an act of arrogance" because "you realistically don't know" what happened. [^5] Where claims are historically irrecoverable, treating them as secondary "noise" rather than salvific essentials is a humility move, not mere relativism. *Rating: moderate — humility is appropriate, but it proves too much if it also dissolves claims that are in fact testable.*
4. **The "noise" reframe defuses inter-religious violence.** Alexa's closing motive is moral: devotion to a tradition's distinctives "becomes like an ego thing," and "when you kill people over [it], that's an issue." [^6] If the dividing doctrines are noise, the wars fought over them are tragic category errors. *Rating: rhetorically powerful but double-edged — Carroll turns it around (see weaknesses).*

### Where it breaks (Carroll's reply)
- **It cannot survive a binary historical claim.** "Was Jesus Christ resurrected? The answer is not yes and no." [^7] A perennialist "noise" filter works for ethical convergence but not for an event that either happened or did not; calling the Resurrection "noise" is not humility but a refusal to let the question be asked. See [[Contradictory Truth Claims Cannot All Be True - The Resurrection Test]].
- **"Truth cannot contradict."** When Alexa says contradiction *is* the noise, Carroll's rejoinder is that genuinely contradictory truth-claims (Messiah / not-Messiah; crucified / not-crucified) cannot both belong to one capital-T truth; one of them is simply false, not noise. [^1]
- **It collapses moral discrimination.** If the dividing content is noise, then "some religions justify killing people" is also noise — a conclusion Alexa flinches from ("killing people is noise?"), exposing that she does not really hold all divergence to be inessential. [^8] This is where the theory shades into the problems treated in [[A Feeling-Defined God Cannot Adjudicate Contradictions]] and [[Relativism Kills Itself - The Hitler Argument Against Self-Created Reality]].

## Best Quotes
> "I think all of [what] differentiates each religion is all noise... there is a truth there that will be a capital-T truth for everybody... and when there's contention, that's noise." [^1]

> "I think God started all the religions but everybody has morphed them... everybody has that access to God, and it's just the culture that has morphed them into all these different beliefs." [^2]

> "I think you could honestly throw a dart, pick one, and follow it to the best of your ability and see how you feel." [^3]
