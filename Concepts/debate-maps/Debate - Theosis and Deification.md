# Debate: Is "Becoming Gods" a Biblical and Ancient-Christian Teaching — and Is LDS Exaltation a Recovery of It, the Same Thing, or a Corruption?

## The Question
"Deification" (Greek *theosis*, *theopoiesis*, *apotheosis*) — the claim that the end of salvation is for human beings to be made "partakers of the divine nature" (2 Peter 1:4) and even called "gods" (Psalm 82:6; John 10:34) — is not a Mormon invention. It has a real biblical seedbed and an unbroken patristic vocabulary, summed up in the exchange formula *"God became man that man might become god"* ([[Christ Became Man That We Might Become God - The Exchange Formula]]), and it survives today as Eastern Orthodox *theosis*. The dispute is therefore not "did anyone teach deification?" — everyone grants someone did — but a **three-way disagreement over identity**: (a) the **biblical + patristic case for deification** as a genuine, ancient, mainstream Christian doctrine; (b) the **LDS exaltation** claim that this was always *same-species* divinization — God and humanity one kind at different stages — recovered by Joseph Smith ([[Human Deification in Mormonism]], [[King Follett Discourse]]); and (c) the **critic's distinction**, that patristic/biblical theosis is *participation* guarding an absolute Creator/creature line, while LDS exaltation is a *materialist same-species* doctrine the fathers explicitly denied — so the LDS appeal is either an overreach or a corruption. The live trichotomy is **recovery of the original**, **the same broad tradition with a uniquely LDS ontology**, or **a watered-down-then-re-radicalized thing that is not what either the Bible or the fathers taught** ([[The Four Constellations of Deification Belief]]).

## Case FOR deification as biblical + ancient Christian
- **The scriptural seedbed is real and pre-critical.** Deification was built on Genesis 1:26-27; Psalm 82:6 ("ye are gods"); John 10:34-36; 2 Corinthians 3:18; Romans 8:14-17; 1 John 3:1-2; and 2 Peter 1:4 ("participants of the divine nature") — see [[Christ Became Man That We Might Become God - The Exchange Formula]] (tier B; Becerra via [[Ancient Christians - An Introduction for Latter-day Saints]]). Justin Martyr was "the first time such language appears so explicitly," teaching from Psalm 82 that "all humans are capable of progressing toward godhood."
- **The exchange formula runs unbroken from the 2nd century.** Irenaeus: "the Son of God became what we are in order to make us like himself"; Athanasius: the Word "was incarnate that we might be made god" — see [[Christ Became Man That We Might Become God - The Exchange Formula]] (tier B). Against Arius, Athanasius reasoned that *full* divinity was required *to deify* — deification is load-bearing for Nicene Christology, not a fringe gloss.
- **It was technically defined, not vague.** Gregory of Nazianzus coined the term *theosis*; Dionysius the Areopagite fixed the classic definition — "the attaining of likeness of God and union with him so far as is possible" — see [[Theosis as Likeness and Union So Far as Possible]] (tier B). The qualifier "so far as is possible" is the patristic guardrail, but the *doctrine of real union and likeness* is mainstream.
- **John supplies the strongest biblical proof text — a divinization soteriology.** Jesus quotes Psalm 82 in [[Johannine Divinization Soteriology|John 10:34-36]] and folds believers into the Father–Son indwelling in John 17:20-23: "they're all going to be divinized… participate in this mutual indwelling" — see [[Johannine Divinization Soteriology]] and [[Psalm 82 as Israel Divinized at Sinai]] (tier B, [[Dan McClellan]]). Even a non-LDS critical scholar calls this the defensible biblical ground.
- **Eternal progression has a patristic analogue.** Gregory of Nyssa's *epektasis* — the soul stretching toward an infinite God "ever-increasingly, without end" — is the closest ancient precedent to LDS never-ending progression; see [[Gregory of Nyssa's Epektasis - Eternal Progression Toward God]] (tier B). (The note flags the divergence: Gregory keeps an "unbridgeable gulf.")
- **The "creator-only" objection to human godhood is post-biblical.** [[David Litwa]] (tier A monograph): in the first-century world divinity was defined by *immortality and power*, not the power to create — "none of the Olympian gods were creators," and **created, non-creating gods were "fairly staple theology"** until 4th-century strict monotheism — see [[Created and Non-Creating Gods Were Staple Pre-Nicene Theology (Litwa)]] and [[Ancient Judaism Affirmed a Divine Spectrum]].

## Case FOR LDS exaltation (recovery / same tradition)
- **The doctrine is explicit and central, not esoteric folklore.** In the [[King Follett Discourse]] Joseph Smith taught humans must "learn to be a God yourself… from exaltation to exaltation, until you arrive at the station of God"; Lorenzo Snow's couplet ("As man now is, God once was; as God now is, man may become") crystallized it — see [[Human Deification in Mormonism]] (tier B/C, [[Benjamin Park]]).
- **It claims the historic theosis tradition outright.** The Church's "Becoming Like God" essay explicitly connects exaltation to patristic and Orthodox deification; a Catholic critic concedes the accurate label is **"exaltationist theosis,"** not "gods of their own planets" — see [[Gods of Their Own Planets Is a Caricature of Exaltationist Theosis]] (tier C) and [[Theosis as Likeness and Union So Far as Possible]]. The crude "personal-planet" image is folklore the steelman drops.
- **The Hebrew-Bible substrate is a divine council with humans elevated toward divine status.** Heiser's *Unseen Realm*, the Dead Sea Scrolls' Deut 32:8 "sons of God," and Margaret Barker's First Temple work recover "multiple divine beings in a hierarchical structure… deification language… genuinely present throughout the text" — unavailable to Joseph Smith in 1830 — see [[Divine Council and Deification Were Native to the Hebrew Bible]] (tier C apologist; the cited Heiser/DSS scholarship is mainstream B/A).
- **The same-species ontology is the actual LDS distinctive — and it is owned, not hidden.** [[Parley P. Pratt]]: "God, angels and men, are all of one species, one race, one great family" — see [[Same-Species-as-God as the Core LDS Distinctive in Theosis]] and [[Same-Species-as-God as the Core LDS Distinctive]] (Becerra/[[Daniel C. Peterson]]). Expressed for laypeople by the [[Acorn and Oak Analogy for Theosis|acorn-and-oak analogy]]: an acorn already *is* an oak in kind and only needs to grow.
- **The pantheism / creature-Creator objection is dissolved differently.** LDS theology denies *creatio ex nihilo*: intelligences/spirit-matter are *eternal and uncreated*, organized by God — so creatures were never "God" and need no Palamite firewall to stay distinct — see [[The Essence-Energies Distinction Distinguishes Orthodoxy From Pantheism]] (the LDS reply inside the note).
- **Mainstream Christianity is drifting back toward the cluster.** [[Terryl Givens]]: scholars "across the denominational spectrum are converging… on what used to be considered uniquely Mormon heresies" (embodied God, premortality, a more capacious salvation) — see [[Convergence of Mainstream Christianity Toward LDS Doctrines]] (tier C channel; Givens tier B). Offered as the frame the critic must answer.

## Case FOR the critic's distinction (patristic ≠ LDS; "watered down" / corruption)
- **The fathers drew the line LDS exaltation crosses: God and humans are different *kinds*.** By an LDS scholar's own admission ([[Daniel Becerra]]), Irenaeus and Athanasius "understood God (as Creator) and humans (as creations) as different species… rather than the same species at different points of development" — see [[Same-Species-as-God as the Core LDS Distinctive in Theosis]] (tier B). Irenaeus capped it: created things are "inherently inferior" and can "never fully be like God." This is the **honest fault line** the apologist must concede.
- **Patristic theosis is participation, not ontological god-making.** [[Dan McClellan]]: by the 4th–5th centuries the fathers' deification was "passive participation in the divine qualities… [rather] than actual ontological divinization where you can speak of humans as becoming gods" — see [[Patristic Theosis Was Watered Down From the Biblical Version]] (tier B→A). So citing Athanasius/Cyril as if they proved *Mormon* deification is a category error.
- **Orthodoxy's own guardrail forbids the LDS move.** The essence/energies distinction makes deification real *union by participation in the energies*, never sharing the divine *essence* — preserving the Creator/creature gulf at the summit of salvation — see [[The Essence-Energies Distinction Distinguishes Orthodoxy From Pantheism]] (tier C channel; the Palamite content is mainstream Orthodox). The very tool that anchors Orthodox theosis is what an LDS apologist must reject.
- **"Watered down" cuts both ways: three distinct ideals, not one.** McClellan's own 2007 verdict (as a then–Latter-day Saint): "the patristic idea of deification is not what's in the Bible and it's not what's in Mormonism — these are three very distinct ideals" — see [[Patristic Theosis Was Watered Down From the Biblical Version]] and the taxonomy in [[The Four Constellations of Deification Belief]]. The dilution had datable causes: strict monotheism, *creatio ex nihilo*, and Jerome's *Hebraica Veritas* removing Septuagint proof texts.
- **The critic's strongest single concession against the LDS side is also McClellan's.** Even [[David Litwa]]'s "created gods were staple theology" is "describing ancient categories of divinity, not endorsing the LDS same-species ontology; he would not say the Father is an exalted man" — see [[Created and Non-Creating Gods Were Staple Pre-Nicene Theology (Litwa)]]. The scholarship neutralizes the *creator-only* objection without conceding the King Follett cosmology.
- **The wrong arguments make the LDS case look weaker than it is.** Leading with Revelation 3:21, the acorn analogy, or the fathers "is not the passage you should lead with… not very convincing to people who have spent centuries interpreting these passages very differently" — see [[LDS Should Lead With John 10 Not Revelation 3 or Patristics]] (tier B). The patristic appeal both misses ("not biblical") and is substantively different.

## Where it turns
The debate does not turn on whether *some* deification was ancient and biblical — Litwa, McClellan, Becerra, and the Orthodox all grant that, and the exchange formula is preserved verbatim from Irenaeus to Maximus. It turns on three pivots:
1. **Continuity of *word* vs. continuity of *content*.** The technical term *theosis* runs unbroken; the *meaning* demonstrably shifts. The LDS/Orthodox apologist argues the continuity of the tradition matters more than the later philosophical guarding ([[Christ Became Man That We Might Become God - The Exchange Formula]]); McClellan replies that "legitimate witness to *a* deification" is not "witness to the *biblical* or the *Mormon* deification," and conflating the three is the exact category error his four-constellation frame exposes ([[Patristic Theosis Was Watered Down From the Biblical Version]], [[The Four Constellations of Deification Belief]]). This is where the LDS appeal to the fathers is weakest by the vault's own scholarly voices.
2. **Participation vs. same-species — the real metaphysical fork.** Both sides agree humans are united to God and "become like" him; they split on *kind*. Patristic/Orthodox theosis is participation across an unbridgeable gulf ([[Theosis as Likeness and Union So Far as Possible]], [[Gregory of Nyssa's Epektasis - Eternal Progression Toward God]], [[The Essence-Energies Distinction Distinguishes Orthodoxy From Pantheism]]); LDS exaltation is one continuous species, acorn-to-oak ([[Same-Species-as-God as the Core LDS Distinctive in Theosis]], [[Acorn and Oak Analogy for Theosis]]). Becerra — an LDS scholar — concedes the fathers stood on the *participation* side. So "is LDS exaltation the same as patristic theosis?" gets a disciplined **no** even from sympathetic sources; the live question becomes whether the *biblical* (esp. Johannine) data sits closer to the LDS or the Orthodox construal.
3. **Is strict numerical monotheism the biblical baseline?** If "no other gods *exist*" is a post-biblical renegotiation and the biblical/early-Jewish worlds affirmed a divine spectrum with a porous human/divine boundary ([[Ancient Judaism Affirmed a Divine Spectrum]], [[Divine Council and Deification Were Native to the Hebrew Bible]], [[Created and Non-Creating Gods Were Staple Pre-Nicene Theology (Litwa)]]), then the *creator-only / creature-can-never-be-god* objection assumes a definition of "god" the canonical authors did not share — and the LDS picture sits closer to constellations (3) and (4) than the creedal one does. The classical reply (the steelman inside those notes): Deutero-Isaiah makes creation central to deity and the "other gods" are idols/nothings, so the firm line is prophetic, not a Greek overlay. The grammar and the council texts underdetermine the metaphysics.

## Most authoritative view
The strongest, most defensible synthesis the vault supports is a **three-part split decision, and the cleanest result is the one the vault's own LDS scholars concede.** (1) *Deification is genuinely biblical and genuinely ancient-Christian* — this is not in serious dispute. The single most authoritative datum on the pro side is **adversarial-leaning**: [[David Litwa]] (tier A) showing that created, non-creating gods were ordinary ancient theology, which neutralizes the objection that most directly targets the LDS view, and [[Dan McClellan]] (tier B→A) confirming the strongest *biblical* ground — [[Johannine Divinization Soteriology|John's divinization soteriology]] — is real and was simply left unused by the LDS debater. (2) *LDS exaltation is **not** identical to patristic theosis* — and here the most authoritative voice is again *internal*: [[Daniel Becerra]], an LDS scholar, concedes the fathers held God and humans to be different *kinds*, the precise point LDS [[Same-Species-as-God as the Core LDS Distinctive in Theosis|same-species ontology]] rejects. So the honest apologetic **claims the *tradition* of theosis while owning that the *ontology* is uniquely LDS** ([[King Follett Discourse]]) — the "even the Church Fathers taught men become gods" move is only half-true and should be retired ([[Patristic Theosis Was Watered Down From the Biblical Version]]). (3) *"Recovery of the original" vs. "corruption" is underdetermined and turns on the monotheism question.* McClellan's double-edged claim — that the *earliest* (NT and early-Jewish/Christian) deification was *more* radical than the creedal version — actually relocates the strongest pro-deification evidence to exactly the texts the LDS reading prizes (John 10; Psalm 82), which favors the LDS framing of a later *dilution*; but it does **not** vindicate the same-species cosmology, only the porousness of the ancient human/divine boundary. Net: deification is biblical and ancient (the pro case wins this cleanly); patristic theosis ≠ LDS exaltation (the critic's *distinction* is correct, and LDS scholars grant it); and whether exaltation is a *recovery* or a *corruption* of the biblical original is a genuinely live question the data permits but does not settle — the disciplined LDS move is to argue the first-century data and the definition of "god," not the watered-down fourth-century gloss.
