# Debate: Did the Glenn L. Pace Memo Expose Real Satanic Ritual Abuse — or Was It the Satanic Panic?

## The Question
In July 1990 [[Glenn L. Pace]], a counselor in the LDS Presiding Bishopric, circulated a confidential 12-page internal memo reporting that, of 60 self-described victims he had interviewed (one per week for 60 weeks), 45 claimed to have witnessed or participated in human sacrifice, implicating 800+ unnamed perpetrators "right here on the Wasatch Front." The memo was leaked, published by the anti-Mormon [[Lighthouse Ministries]], and has circulated for over thirty years as apparent proof that a satanic-ritual-abuse (SRA) ring operated inside the Church and was covered up by its highest leaders — a charge later pinned by internet sleuths on apostles and even President [[Russell M. Nelson]]. Critics read the memo as a suppressed insider confession; defenders read it as one earnest welfare officer's tier-bottom artifact of a nationwide moral panic — the 1980s SRA hysteria that began with the book *Michelle Remembers*, ran through the McMartin preschool trial, and has since been comprehensively debunked. The live question is not "was anyone ever abused" (everyone grants ordinary abuse exists) but **whether the *satanic-ritual* claims describe real crimes that were concealed, or memories manufactured by recovered-memory therapy and recycled panic tropes** ([[Recovered Memory Therapy Manufactures Abuse Memories]]).

## Case for REAL ABUSE / COVER-UP
- **A general authority put it in writing.** The memo is a real internal document by a real Church officer reporting horrific allegations from 60 members — not an outsider's invention; its existence and provenance are not in dispute ([[The Pace Memo Was Not an Investigation but a Persuasive Essay]] frames it, conceding the document is genuine even while disputing its character).
- **The *Spotlight* analogy: a large religious institution really did conceal clergy abuse.** Since the Catholic Church demonstrably reassigned predator priests to dodge prosecution, the prior that a wealthy, hierarchical, secretive church would bury abuse is not paranoid — see [[The Spotlight Analogy Equates a Celibate Cardinal With a Volunteer Bishop]] (presented here as the critic's framing; the note itself rebuts the equation).
- **The reporting catch-22 looks like a shield for the institution.** Critics read the abuse helpline as a device to "divert allegations away from law enforcement and to church attorneys who may bury the problem" — [[The Abuse Helpline Routes Bishops to Legal Compliance Not Cover-Up]] and [[The Reporting Catch-22 and the Johnson Case]] (both stated as the objection, then answered).
- **Confessional privilege makes the cover-up unfalsifiable from outside.** Because clergy swear secrecy about confessions, an outsider can never see what was disclosed or buried — the same asymmetry that protects the Church from disproof — see [[Confessional Privilege Creates a One-Way Street for Accusations]] (note argues the asymmetry actually cuts the other way).
- **Absence of evidence isn't evidence of absence.** Genuinely clandestine, competent ritual crime would leave little documentary trace — the steelman behind the "breeder" explanation for missing infants and "complicit morticians" for missing adults — see [[The Breeder Myth Was Invented to Explain Missing Bodies]] and [[Noemi Mattis Turned From Skeptic to True Believer on the Ritual Abuse Task Force]] (each records this defense, then shows why it fails).
- **Believers held on for a reason: convergent testimony.** Pace trusted the 60 because their independent stories matched; a task-force co-chair who began as a "quintessential skeptic" was converted by "the cumulative experience of many therapists" ([[Noemi Mattis Turned From Skeptic to True Believer on the Ritual Abuse Task Force]]) — the strongest psychological pull toward belief.

## Case for MORAL PANIC / DEBUNKED
- **The whole genre traces to one debunked book.** SRA as a category was launched by the 1980 *Michelle Remembers* ([[Lawrence Pazder]] and patient [[Michelle Smith]]); its 81-day "ritual" left no missing persons, no mail buildup, no school absence — actively falsified, not merely uncorroborated — see [[Michelle Remembers Sparked the Satanic Panic]] and [[Satanic Panic Is a Recurrence of Salem-Style Hysteria]] (tier: strong — the foundational claim collapsed).
- **Recovered-memory therapy manufactures the memories it "recovers."** [[Elizabeth Loftus]] implanted false memories experimentally; the memories form sincerely, like an AI hallucination ([[Julia Shaw]]) — the technique generates, not retrieves, the abuse — see [[Recovered Memory Therapy Manufactures Abuse Memories]] and [[False Memory Syndrome Explains the SRA Accusations]] (tier: strong — peer-reviewed psychology).
- **No perpetrators, no bodies, no prosecutions.** Pace deliberately collected no names, dates, or places, leaving 800+ "nameless people implicitly accused" and prosecution impossible ([[The Pace Memo Names No Perpetrators Making Prosecution Impossible]]); a $250,000 state crime unit found ~99% "wild goose chases" ([[Ritual Abuse Crime Unit Found Ninety-Nine Percent Wild Goose Chases]]); fewer than 1% of abuse reports nationally reached trial ([[Satanic Panic Statistics Show Less Than One Percent of Abuse Cases Reached Trial]]).
- **Identical cross-case stories point to the interviewer, not the crime.** Unconnected Utah cases produced near-identical allegations and even identical children's drawings; the only shared variable was therapist [[Barbara Snow]] — a judge's affidavit said so — see [[Identical Cross-Case Allegations Point to the Interviewer Not the Crime]] and [[Barbara Snows Interview Methods Manufactured Abuse Allegations]] (tier: strong — court record).
- **The memo is unoriginal and self-refuting.** Its motifs (the "coven of 13," mock burials, doctrinal inversion) are lifted from *Michelle Remembers*, *The Satanic Underground*, and [[Mike Warnke]]'s *The Satan Seller* ([[The Pace Memo Recycled Existing Satanic Panic Tropes]]); its own arithmetic collapses (60 × 13 ≠ 800 when only 37 victims were even from Utah) ([[Pace's Coven Math Refutes His Own 800-Perpetrator Estimate]]).
- **The discredited therapists, and the founder's own recantation.** [[Bennett Brown]] settled memory-implantation suits for $10.6M and $7.5M and lost his license; [[Barbara Snow]] was charged by Utah DOPL with implanting false memories; even Pazder conceded in 1994 the memories were "more an expression of … violation … than actual accounts" — see [[Leading SRA Therapists Were Discredited Sued or Delicensed]] (tier: decisive — the architect abandoned the literal claim).
- **The "missing bodies" patches are unfalsifiable and, where checkable, falsified.** The "breeder" who personified the missing-infant explanation ([[Lauren Stratford]]) was proven never pregnant and a serial fabricator — see [[The Breeder Myth Was Invented to Explain Missing Bodies]] and [[Lauren Stratford Was a Serial Fabricator of Victim Identities]].
- **Investigators denied any cover-up under oath; the relitigation was dismissed with prejudice.** In the 2018 suit naming President Nelson, investigators [[Michael George]], [[Diana Stevens]], and [[Leslie Lewis]] each testified that no Church official interfered, and the judge dismissed the case *with prejudice* — see [[No Evidence the LDS Church Suppressed the Investigations]] and [[An Innocent Cleared by Every Investigator Was Still Driven to Death]].
- **Even the hostile publisher balked.** [[Lighthouse Ministries]], eager to embarrass the Church, still conceded 45 hidden human sacrifices "would be very difficult to cover up" — an adversarial admission of implausibility ([[Even Lighthouse Ministries Doubted the Pace Memo's Credibility]]).
- **And it changed nothing.** The Utah Governor's ritual-abuse subcommittee formed *before* the memo, so the claim that the memo "actioned" a church or state response is false ([[The Pace Memo Did Not Cause Any Church or State Action]]).

## Where it turns
The debate does not turn on whether the Pace memo exists, whether *some* members were genuinely abused, or whether a wealthy church *could* in principle conceal wrongdoing — all conceded. It turns on three pivots:
1. **Where did the memories come from — the crime, or the couch?** This is the hinge. If recovered-memory therapy reliably manufactures vivid, sincere, false memories — and the experimental ([[Recovered Memory Therapy Manufactures Abuse Memories]], [[False Memory Syndrome Explains the SRA Accusations]]) and forensic ([[Identical Cross-Case Allegations Point to the Interviewer Not the Crime]]) evidence says it does — then convergent testimony, the cover-up side's *strongest* asset, inverts into evidence of a *shared input* (one interviewer, the same saturating media), not a shared crime. The believer's best datum (everyone tells the same story) becomes the skeptic's best datum.
2. **What does the absence of perpetrators, bodies, and prosecutions weigh?** The cover-up case must lean on unfalsifiable patches — "breeders," "complicit morticians," "they were sedated" — to explain why 800+ accusations and 45 alleged murders left no names and no corpses ([[The Pace Memo Names No Perpetrators Making Prosecution Impossible]], [[The Breeder Myth Was Invented to Explain Missing Bodies]]). A hypothesis that predicts "no evidence" whether true or false carries little evidentiary weight; and where the patches were checkable ([[Lauren Stratford]]), they were *falsified*, not merely unproven. The funded investigation's ~99% failure rate ([[Ritual Abuse Crime Unit Found Ninety-Nine Percent Wild Goose Chases]]) is what a panic predicts, not what a real ring does.
3. **Is the *Spotlight* prior load-bearing or misapplied?** The cover-up case borrows credibility from the real Catholic scandal. But the structural disanalogy is large — married, rotating, unpaid lay bishops with parents invited to interviews, versus celibate solo priests reassigned by administrators ([[The Spotlight Analogy Equates a Celibate Cardinal With a Volunteer Bishop]]) — and in the one *real* LDS abuse case folded into the narrative (Adams), no clergy abused anyone, the bishop urged reporting, and the court dismissed the suit. The reporting catch-22 ([[The Reporting Catch-22 and the Johnson Case]]) shows the Church was sued *for reporting* a confessed abuser, which dissolves the "they always bury it" frame into a genuine two-sided legal bind.

## Most authoritative view
The strongest, most defensible position the vault supports: **the Pace memo did not expose a concealed satanic-ritual-abuse ring; it is a tier-bottom artifact of the recovered-memory / Satanic Panic, and the SRA claims have been comprehensively debunked — while ordinary abuse, which does occur, is neither sanctioned nor systematically covered up by the Church.** The single most authoritative datum is an *internal* concession: [[Lawrence Pazder]], who coined "satanic ritual abuse," recanted the literal claim in 1994 ([[Leading SRA Therapists Were Discredited Sued or Delicensed]]) — the architect abandoning the foundation. Converging with it: a hostile publisher's own admission of implausibility ([[Even Lighthouse Ministries Doubted the Pace Memo's Credibility]]); a funded state investigation's ~99% null result ([[Ritual Abuse Crime Unit Found Ninety-Nine Percent Wild Goose Chases]]); experimental implantation of false memories ([[Recovered Memory Therapy Manufactures Abuse Memories]]); a judge's affidavit that the only thread linking the cases was one therapist ([[Identical Cross-Case Allegations Point to the Interviewer Not the Crime]]); delicensing and multi-million-dollar malpractice settlements against the key therapists; and sworn denials of interference with a dismissal *with prejudice* in the relitigation against President Nelson ([[No Evidence the LDS Church Suppressed the Investigations]]). What the cover-up side retains is a fair *prior* — the Catholic *Spotlight* scandal proves large religious institutions sometimes do conceal abuse — and a real moral residue: the memo's own credulity, the genuine suffering of falsely accused innocents, and the unresolved policy tension over whether mandatory clergy reporting should override confessional privilege ([[Confessional Privilege Creates a One-Way Street for Accusations]], [[The Reporting Catch-22 and the Johnson Case]]), which even the defenders grant is "a debate to be had." Net: the *satanic-ritual* charge is a debunked panic, not a cover-up; the honest live questions are about reporting law and confession, not about covens on the Wasatch Front.
