# Debate: Is Libertarian Free Will Real, or Is Hard Determinism True?

## The Question
Do human beings genuinely author their choices — or is the felt sense of "choosing" a user-illusion riding on top of a closed chain of prior causes? The hard determinist says every intention traces to neural and physical events the conscious self neither chose nor controls, so libertarian free will is "wholly illusory" ([[The Illusion of Free Will - Harris's Hard Determinism]]). The libertarian says agency is a primitive feature of persons that no causal story exhausts, and that denying it is self-refuting and morally suicidal. The live positions are three, not two: **hard determinism** (no free will, and that's the honest cost of materialism), **compatibilism** (a thin, redescribed "freedom" that survives determinism but, critics charge, can no longer ground desert), and **libertarian / agent-causal free will** (a self that is neither pushed by prior causes nor scattered by chance). The vault's center of gravity is the third, anchored in the LDS metaphysic of the uncreated, co-eternal intelligence ([[Free Will - Atheist Determinism vs LDS Co-Eternality]]). The whole fight is unusually high-stakes because **moral responsibility rides on the answer**: if no one ever truly chooses, praise, blame, justice, and repentance all lose their subject ([[Moral Responsibility Presupposes Genuine Agency]]).

## Case for HARD DETERMINISM
- **The honest materialist concession.** If persons are purely physical, choices are fixed by genetics, environment, and brain chemistry — and the leading atheists *say so*: [[Sam Harris]], Christopher Hitchens, and Alex O'Connor all grant that on materialism "we actually don't have free will" — see [[Free Will - Atheist Determinism vs LDS Co-Eternality]] (tier: strong as a *cited-opponent* datum — the consistency of the concession is the point).
- **Harris's flagship argument.** Thoughts and intentions "merely appear... as though sprung from the void"; the conscious self is not their author — see [[The Illusion of Free Will - Harris's Hard Determinism]] (tier: strong statement of the position; the note also lodges the standard rebuttals).
- **The Libet / fMRI neuroscience.** Motor-region readiness potentials precede the felt decision by ~350 ms, and one fMRI study predicted "conscious" choices up to 10 seconds early — see [[The Illusion of Free Will - Harris's Hard Determinism]] (the *evidence* leg) and the determinist reading recapped in [[Free Will - Atheist Determinism vs LDS Co-Eternality]].
- **Randomness is not a rescue.** Harris's strongest move: quantum indeterminacy would *destroy* agency, not ground it — a choice from "a random release of neurotransmitters... would obliterate any semblance of human agency" — see [[The Illusion of Free Will - Harris's Hard Determinism]] (tier: strong, and conceded across the vault as the decisive reply to the quantum gambit *on materialist premises*).
- **The genetic-determinism extension.** Pushed to the hardest case — child predation framed as "genetic or epigenetic," so predators "aren't choosing" — the determinist generalizes that *no* disposition is ultimately chosen — see [[The Genetic-Determinism Argument Against Moral Responsibility]] (tier: rhetorically forceful; the steelman is that the horror of the test case makes "it's just biology" feel inescapable).
- **Responsibility can be rebuilt without free will.** Harris tries to keep moral responsibility as a *descriptive fit* between act and character, conceding only that retributive justice falls — see [[Moral Responsibility Without Free Will - Harris's Reconciliation]] (tier: the load-bearing compatibilist reconstruction the defense must answer).
- **Purpose survives determinism too.** Two atheist students grant "we are dominoes" yet deny that purposelessness follows — purpose is *function and impact*, not chosen aim — see [[No Free Will Does Not Entail No Purpose - Carroll's Reply]] (tier: the cleanest determinist steelman in the cluster; the dispute turns out to be semantic — *chosen aim* vs *causal function*).

## Case for LIBERTARIAN FREE WILL
- **The self-defeat of denying it.** A determinist argument is offered as the conclusion of *reasoning* meant to make rational agents *revise* their beliefs — but if thoughts "simply arise... unauthored," the argument is one more neural weather-pattern and the reader cannot *choose* to accept it — see [[The Illusion of Free Will - Harris's Hard Determinism]] (the self-defeat leg) and [[We All Act As Though We Have Agency - The Performative Contradiction of Determinism]] (tier: strong — even the determinist cannot *live* his determinism; the lived practice of treating ourselves as agents is itself evidence).
- **The Libet rebuttal.** [[William Lane Craig]] answers the headline experiment: because nerve signals travel at finite velocity, a lag between the soul's decision and *conscious awareness* of it is exactly what dualist-interactionism predicts — the data is what dualism expects, not evidence against freedom — see [[The Libet Experiments Are What Dualism Predicts Not Evidence Against Free Will]] (tier: strong as a defeater of the *inference* — and Libet himself did not draw the deterministic conclusion).
- **Quantum indeterminacy reopens the future.** [[Inspiring Philosophy|Michael Jones]] runs the *opposite* inference from Harris: the [[John Conway|Conway]]–[[Simon Kochen|Kochen]] Free Will Theorem plus Bell-inequality violations ([[Anton Zeilinger]]) mean "no one can determine your future events given your past history" — see [[The Conway-Kochen Free Will Theorem - Indeterminism Preserves Freedom]] (tier: solid on the physics, interpretively loaded — indeterminism is *necessary but not sufficient* for agency; it removes the closed-future defeater without by itself delivering authorship).
- **The determinist inference is invalid as stated.** Having a *reason* for an action is not proof the reason *caused* it; a reason can **incline without necessitating** — to slide from "there was a prior factor" to "that factor determined the choice" assumes the very causal closure under dispute — see [[A Reason for an Action Is Not Proof It Was Caused]] (tier: a clean logical point — it shifts the burden back onto the closure premise).
- **Harris refutes a freedom no one claimed.** Following [[Alvin Plantinga]]'s "Bait and Switch," the demand that we must have *chosen our genes, past, and character* ("maximal autonomy") is a standard not even God meets — ordinary free will is left untouched — see [[Maximal Autonomy Is Not Free Will - Plantingas Bait and Switch]] (tier: decisive against the maximal-autonomy *formulation*; the steelman is that the deeper *sourcehood* worry survives reformulation).
- **Inclination is not action.** A person may not choose to *desire* something yet still choose whether to *do* it — and responsibility attaches to the deed, not the unchosen urge; admitting even one chosen act breaks the determinist's *universal* claim — see [[Inclination Is Not Action - The Predisposition Does Not Compel the Act]] (tier: strong internal-defeater of the genetic argument).
- **Moral responsibility requires it.** Responsibility is conceptually parasitic on genuine choice; deny agency and "blame" loses its subject — [[Jacob Hansen]] springs this on critic [[Bill Reel]], who excuses predators as "predisposed" yet still wants to condemn the Church — see [[Denying Free Will Dissolves the Moral Responsibility a Critic Needs]], [[Moral Responsibility Presupposes Genuine Agency]], and [[Bill Reel's Free-Will Double Standard - Determinism Cannot Ground Moral Blame]] (tier: strong — a self-consistency trap on the critic).
- **The LDS metaphysic answers the caused-or-random dilemma.** Persons are uncreated, co-eternal *intelligences* — not configurations of caused matter — so agent-causation is a primitive: the intelligence "act[s] for itself," using reasons without being compelled by them, escaping both horns — see [[Free Will - Atheist Determinism vs LDS Co-Eternality]] and [[Intelligence Is Uncreated and Eternally Has Agency]] (tier: the distinctive positive account; note classical *ex nihilo* theism shares the materialist's vulnerability).
- **Opposition in all things.** Lehi grounds agency in the structure of being: "the Lord God gave unto man that he should act for himself" ([[2 Nephi 2.11]]), and joy is unreachable without "an opposition in all things" — see [[Lehi's Theodicy - Opposition in All Things]] (tier: the scriptural keystone; man must be "free to choose," [[2 Nephi 2.25]]).

## Where it turns
The debate does not turn on whether prior causes *influence* choices — both sides grant genes, upbringing, and brain states shape us. It turns on three pivots:
1. **The Libet experiments.** The headline neuroscience is the determinist's best *empirical* card, but it is interpretively fragile. The deterministic spin is "an interpretation, not the result" — Libet himself declined it — and finite signal velocity makes a decision-to-awareness lag exactly what a dualist *predicts* ([[The Libet Experiments Are What Dualism Predicts Not Evidence Against Free Will]]). So Libet does not establish the conclusion at all; it establishes a timing fact compatible with agency. Advantage: defense, on this pivot.
2. **The quantum / randomness fork.** Here the two *pro-freedom* and *pro-determinism* camps trade blows that cancel one level too low. Jones says indeterminism *preserves* freedom (the closed clockwork future is empirically dead — [[The Conway-Kochen Free Will Theorem - Indeterminism Preserves Freedom]]); Harris says undetermined-because-random is *not authorship* — "you would live as one blown about by an internal wind" ([[The Illusion of Free Will - Harris's Hard Determinism]]). **Both are right about half the truth.** Indeterminism defeats *determinism* but not the luck objection; determinism's closed future is falsified at the quantum level but randomness alone is not agency. The fork only resolves *one level up*, in the metaphysics of an agent who *uses* an indeterministic substrate without being either determined or randomized by it ([[Free Will - Atheist Determinism vs LDS Co-Eternality]]).
3. **The self-defeat / moral-responsibility argument.** This is the libertarian's strongest leg and the determinist's deepest cost. A position that must be *contradicted in the very act of stating it* — addressing choosers, urging them to *revise* — is self-undermining ([[We All Act As Though We Have Agency - The Performative Contradiction of Determinism]]). And the same determinism that excuses the wrongdoer dissolves the standing to condemn *anyone*, including the critic's own target ([[Denying Free Will Dissolves the Moral Responsibility a Critic Needs]]). Harris feels the blade and concedes retributive justice falls, keeping only a "sham" deterrence-retribution ([[Moral Responsibility Without Free Will - Harris's Reconciliation]]). The honest determinist must therefore pay in the coin of *desert, fairness, and answerability* — which is a very high price.

## Most authoritative view
The strongest, most defensible position the vault supports: **hard determinism is the honest endpoint of materialism, and that is precisely its reductio** — it cannot fund the rational authorship its own arguments presuppose, nor the moral responsibility every party reaches for in practice. The single most authoritative concession is *adversarial*: Harris and the leading atheists openly grant that on materialism free will is illusory ([[Free Will - Atheist Determinism vs LDS Co-Eternality]]) — which converts the debate from "is there free will?" into "is materialism the whole story?" On the **empirical** pivot, Libet should carry *low* weight against freedom: it is a timing fact dualism predicts, not a refutation ([[The Libet Experiments Are What Dualism Predicts Not Evidence Against Free Will]]). On the **physics** pivot, the disciplined reading is that quantum indeterminism removes the closed-future defeater but does *not* by itself deliver agency — Jones overstates, Harris's randomness objection lands, and neither wins without a metaphysics of the agent ([[The Conway-Kochen Free Will Theorem - Indeterminism Preserves Freedom]]). The libertarian's *positive* burden — exhibiting a choice that is "uncaused-yet-not-random" — is real, and is met not by the physics but by **agent causation**: a non-reducible source that selects among reasons without being pushed by them. Materialism cannot supply such a self (its only ingredients are caused matter and chance), and classical *creation ex nihilo* inherits the same determinism; the LDS uncreated, co-eternal intelligence is offered as the missing term that grounds genuine choice and the accountability the moral life requires ([[Intelligence Is Uncreated and Eternally Has Agency]], [[Lehi's Theodicy - Opposition in All Things]]). Net: determinism may be *consistent*, but it is self-defeating and morally bankrupt; libertarian free will is a metaphysical commitment the evidence permits and the moral and rational life all but demands.
