# The Genetic Fallacy Against Religious Origins

## The Point
[[Sam Harris]] argues that even if a moral capacity (delayed gratification, picturing the future) is *historically* fused with an abhorrent practice (child sacrifice), it is the **genetic fallacy** to think the shared origin obliges us to retain, venerate, or treat as true the abhorrent part. Origin does not confer value or obligation.[^1]

## Argument

1. **Separability.** "Delayed gratification is fully separable from a notion that it might ever be rational or good to sacrifice a child as an offering to an invisible other."[^1]

2. **Even granting fusion, the fallacy stands.** "Let's just say as a matter of our origins they're united, they're of a piece. It is the genetic fallacy to care about that origin" — to say it is "the only path forward" or "somehow necessary to venerate now."[^2]

3. **The witchcraft parity argument.** Whatever uplifting interpretive move Peterson performs on religion "you can do with everything... you could do it with witchcraft." Since we are right *not* to rehabilitate witchcraft (albinos hunted for body parts, children killed as witches), the same charity to ignorance-derived belief is no virtue. *Steelman: a method that can dignify anything dignifies nothing.*[^3]

4. **The naturalistic fallacy, named by [[Bret Weinstein]].** If sentimental, "irrational" attachments (to land, to dogma) persist because they were evolutionarily *selected*, one is "stuck with the naturalistic fallacy" — "just because something is doesn't mean it ought." That the Aztecs' sacrifices were "good for continuing Aztec-ness" does not make them good "in some absolute moral sense."[^4]

5. **Peterson's rejoinder.** Survival across millennia is *evidence* of a meta-rationality we discard at our peril; abandoning a "sentimental" attachment because it looks irrational might drive a lineage extinct, and Harris's confidence is not "so thoroughly grounded that it can justify" that. *Steelman: the genetic/naturalistic-fallacy charge cuts both ways — Harris reads persistence as accident, Peterson as signal.* See [[You Cannot Derive Values From Facts Without an A Priori Structure]].[^5]

## Best Quotes
> "It is the genetic fallacy to care about that origin." [^2]

> "You could do it with witchcraft. Why not do the exact same thing you're doing with religion to the history of witchcraft?" [^3]

> "Just because something is doesn't mean it ought... when the Aztecs sacrifice their enemies it is good for continuing Aztec-ness, but it may not be good in some absolute moral sense." [^4]
