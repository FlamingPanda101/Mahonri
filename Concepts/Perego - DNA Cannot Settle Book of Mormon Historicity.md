# Perego - DNA Cannot Settle Book of Mormon Historicity

## The Point

Ugo A. Perego is uniquely placed to speak to "Lamanite DNA": he spent nearly two decades
working directly with ancient and modern DNA from populations across the Americas, and his
doctoral work under Antonio Torroni at the University of Pavia reconstructed Native
American origins from complete mitochondrial genomes. His verdict, from inside the data, is
that **DNA is simply the wrong tool for the question** — it can neither prove nor disprove
the historicity of Book of Mormon peoples. A founding family of ~30 individuals arriving
~2,600 years ago into an already-populated continent would be "swallowed in the indigenous
gene pool," leaving no detectable genetic legacy. Matching the right tool to the right
question is the whole game.[^1]

## Evidence

- **The expert was never troubled by the question.** Working alone in the Pavia lab in
  2007 preparing plates of Native American mtDNA — knowing "no one had at their disposal as
  much data and DNA samples as I had" — Perego reflected on the Book of Mormon controversy
  and concluded "it was clear in my mind that the answers... were not to be found in the
  DNA samples in my hands." His grasp of population-genetics principles, not faith alone,
  produced that calm.[^2]

- **The swamping mechanism.** "The DNA of a single family of about thirty individuals
  coming 2,600 years ago to an already populated region of the American continent would be
  swallowed in the indigenous gene pool, leaving no genetic legacy detectable in the
  generations to follow." This is the founder-effect / drift / host-admixture logic applied
  by a working geneticist — compare [[Founder Effect and Genetic Drift Erase a Small Founding Signal]]
  and the "already populated" premise in [[The Empty Continent Assumption Underlies the DNA Objection]].[^3]

- **Tool–question mismatch is the core error.** "Certain tools work well for certain
  questions, and matching the tool to the proper question is both wise and productive, while
  applying a tool to a question it is not designed to address brings confusion and
  dissatisfaction." This is the same methodological point as
  [[No DNA Evidence Requires Specifying What Signature to Expect]].[^4]

- **Vetted by the Church.** Perego's comprehensive essay (with Jayne E. Ekins, *Interpreter*
  2020) was reviewed, edited, and shortened by scientists and a Church committee and folded
  into the Gospel Topics Essay "Book of Mormon and DNA Studies." His chapter affirms he still
  stands by that conclusion after nearly two decades with the data.[^5]

## Best Quotes

> "The answers to Book of Mormon peoples' origin questions were not to be found in the DNA samples in my hands." [^2]

> "DNA is not a viable tool to prove or disprove an ancient origin of the Book of Mormon." [^4]

> "The DNA of a single family of about thirty individuals coming 2,600 years ago to an already populated region of the American continent would be swallowed in the indigenous gene pool, leaving no genetic legacy detectable in the generations to follow." [^3]

## LDS Connection

This is faithful scholarship at its most credible: a domain expert declining to overclaim
in *either* direction. Perego will not use DNA to "prove" the Book of Mormon, and he shows
why critics cannot use it to disprove it — the data are silent because the question exceeds
the tool's design. The posture flows from his "faith first" method
([[Faith before Science and Then Science]]): testimony rests on the fundamental questions,
while the science is held to exactly what it can deliver. Far from a conflict between true
science and the gospel, his work removes a supposed conflict by clarifying what genetics can
and cannot say.
