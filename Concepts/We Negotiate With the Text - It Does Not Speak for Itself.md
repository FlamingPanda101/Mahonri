# We Negotiate With the Text - It Does Not Speak for Itself

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Dan McClellan]]'s signature hermeneutical thesis: a text "doesn't speak for itself — we interpret
the text in negotiation with it."[^1] Because believing communities treat scripture as inspired,
timeless, and authoritative, every passage "has to be meaningful and useful to you," which
*forces* reinterpretation when the plain ancient sense is uncomfortable. This reinterpretation is
"inevitable," but it "betrays that there's not a lot of critical thinking going on" about how the
Bible gets operationalized.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. Worked examples of softening — rate: strong
- **Proverbs' "rod":** "He who withholds the rod hates his son" gets reread as "a shepherd's
  crook… for gently guiding," when it plainly means an instrument of corporal punishment.[^1]
- **[[Genesis 3.16|Genesis 3:16]]:** "rule" softened to "preside" (see
  [[Genesis 3.16 Means Rule Not Preside]]).
- **[[Isaiah 28.10|Isaiah 28:10]]:** *qav laqav, tsav latsav* is deliberate gibberish (paired with
  "stammering lips and another tongue"), but "we've turned it into this notion of line upon line,
  precept upon precept because that was somebody's best attempt at trying to make sense of these
  words."[^2]
- **"Eye of the needle":** the apologetic invention of a Jerusalem gate so a camel can squeeze
  through.[^3]

### 2. The mechanism: inspired + timeless ⇒ forced relevance — rate: strong
"If you treat the text as something inspired and something timeless… then it has to be meaningful
and useful to you. And so the text doesn't speak for itself."[^1] The doctrine of an authoritative,
all-times-and-places scripture is what *generates* the negotiation. This is the doctrinal-hermeneutic
cousin of the cosmological pattern in
[[Biblical Interpretation Has Always Renegotiated Under New Cosmologies]], and a concrete case of
[[Interpretive Assumptions Are the Glasses We Read Scripture Through]].

### 3. Doctrine can rest on a contingent rendering — rate: strong
The Isaiah 28:10 case shows a *doctrine* (gradual "line upon line" revelation) anchored to a KJV
mistranslation: "if we endorsed a critical approach… suddenly, where do we get that doctrine
from?"[^2] [[Benjamin Park]]'s gloss: "It's almost like you're supposed to be wrestling with the
scriptures and not just memorizing them."[^4] **Steelman:** a confessional reader can affirm that
*all* reading is interpretation (a hermeneutically respectable point) and that productive
re-readings are legitimate; McClellan agrees re-reading is inevitable — his critique targets
re-readings that *deny* they are re-readings and pass themselves off as the plain sense.
