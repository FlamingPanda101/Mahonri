# Hebrew Bible Endorses Chattel Slavery of Foreigners

## The Claim
The Hebrew Bible explicitly authorizes **chattel slavery** — the permanent, heritable ownership of foreign persons as property — in [[Leviticus 25.44-46|Leviticus 25:44-46]]. [[Dan McClellan]] argues this, contra apologists who restrict biblical slavery to temporary Israelite debt-bondage.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Heritable property language (rated: strong; primary text).** Foreign slaves "become property of an Israelite and they could pass those slaves down to their children." Leviticus 25:46 calls them an inheritance held "for ever."[^2]

2. **Indiscriminate sourcing (rated: strong).** The main source was foreigners "willing to sell them to you and they didn't care where they came from." Prisoners of war were only one channel.[^2]

3. **Distinct from Israelite bond-service (rated: strong; primary text).** This is "a different type of slavery altogether" from the debt-bondage governed by [[Leviticus 25.39-43|Leviticus 25:39-43]], which forbids ruling over fellow Israelites "with rigour."[^1]

4. **Steelman.** An apologist might argue ancient chattel slavery still differed from transatlantic slavery (no race ideology, embedded in a kinship economy). McClellan addresses this directly in [[Ethnic vs Racial Slavery Is Not a Significant Difference]], conceding the ethnic-vs-racial distinction exists but arguing it is morally negligible.[^3]

## Best Quotes

> "In Leviticus 25 that talks about foreign slaves that you can pass on to your children... This is a different type of slavery altogether."[^1]

> "The main source for chattel slaves was the fact that foreigners were willing to sell them to you and they didn't care where they came from, and they would become property of an Israelite and they could pass those slaves down to their children."[^2]
