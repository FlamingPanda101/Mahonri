# Equal Treatment Mistaken for Persecution (Objection Rebutted)

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
The Catholic critic argues the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints has "rebranded" — that something has *changed* about the Church, because Mormons now push back when their faith is attacked, are entering the public consciousness, and are taken more seriously. The implied premise is that newfound LDS assertiveness signals a recent strategic shift.[^1]

## The Rebuttal

1. **The premise confuses a change in the *environment* with a change in the *Church* (rated: strong, sociological).** [[Cardon Ellis]] invokes the [[Ryan Burge|Thomas Sowell]] line — "Some people have gotten so used to preferential treatment that when they get equal treatment, they regard it as persecution." For ~50 years post-war, critics who misrepresented LDS doctrine faced nothing harsher than a strongly worded newsroom letter. Now, when someone burns a Book of Mormon on Instagram and gets organized pushback from members, critics infer "something's changed — it must be their rebrand," rather than recognizing they have simply lost their preferential immunity from rebuttal.[^1] *Steelman:* the Church *has* made deliberate communication shifts (e.g., the Russell M. Nelson push to use the full name, retiring "Mormon" branding), so the perception of a rebrand is not pure invention; the rebuttal's force is that the substantive *doctrine* and leadership structure are unchanged, only the apologetic responsiveness has grown.

2. **The leadership model produces continuity, not flux (rated: moderate).** Ellis frames the alternative hypothesis: "Has the seniority-based leadership model of the church produced the exact same thing for the past hundred years?" A gerontocratic succession of apostles selected decades in advance is structurally a continuity engine, not a rebranding machine.[^2]

3. **The "unchallenged for a decade" diagnosis (rated: moderate, rhetorical).** Ellis argues the critic "has really just gone unchallenged for probably a decade and doesn't know how to handle it" — the new-media LDS apologetic ecosystem (Ward Radio, Scripture Central, [[Jasmine Rapley|Informed Saints]]) is what changed, supplying the pushback that previously did not exist.[^2]

## Best Quotes

> "Some people have gotten so used to preferential treatment that when they get equal treatment, they regard it as persecution."[^1]

> "When all of a sudden you're getting pushback you're not used to getting in your entire adult life... it's very tempting to say, 'Well, something's changed.' And what's changed? It must be their rebrand."[^1]

> "Did we rebrand, or has the seniority-based leadership model of the church produced the exact same thing for the past hundred years?"[^2]
