# Truth Is Law Is Morality - The D&C 88 Equivalence Chain

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Shad Brooks]] reads [[D&C 88]] as asserting a chain of **equivalences** — not a causal sequence — that collapses truth, light, law, and morality into one reality. D&C 88:7-13 says "whatsoever is truth is light, and whatsoever is light is spirit, even the spirit of Jesus Christ," and that this light "is the law by which all things are governed." Shad stresses: "It's not saying... truth leads to light. It's saying... whatsoever is truth is light, and whatsoever is light is spirit."[^1] Because light/truth "is the law by which all things are governed" — "even the power of God"[^2] — and because [[D&C 29|D&C 29:34]] states "all things unto me are spiritual... never... a law which is temporal" (so even the laws of physics are, to God, *moral* commandments),[^3] the chain runs **truth = light = law = morality**. His conclusion: "truth *is* morality... these are actually one and the same."[^4] A practical corollary: keeping the commandments yields *more* truth not as an arbitrary reward but as a "natural byproduct," because "truth embraceth truth" (D&C 88:40) — living in line with law *is* receiving truth.[^5]

## Supporting Evidence
- **The light↔truth substitution rule.** Shad claims D&C 88 licenses readers to "switch [light] to truth and read it again... and you will find some very awesome stuff," because the passage itself declares the two synonymous.[^6] This is his hermeneutical engine for the whole chain. Rate: **the load-bearing reading** — the entire identity thesis depends on treating D&C 88's "is" as strict equivalence rather than predication or association.
- **Comprehension distinguishes "light of truth" from bare truth.** To the objection "if light and truth are synonymous, how can Christ be the *light of* truth?", Shad answers with a blueprint analogy: raw truth (blueprints) is inert until comprehended; comprehension is what "gives that truth light," i.e. makes it usable.[^7] Christ "ascended up on high and descended below all things," and so "comprehended all things" (D&C 88:6) — comprehension is "a necessary part in his exaltation." Rate: a deft save that reconciles "truth = light" with "light *of* truth" by indexing light to *active, comprehended* truth.
- **Physics as moral law (D&C 88:25, 29:34).** The earth "abideth the law of a celestial kingdom... and transgresseth not the law," so it "will be sanctified."[^8] If the earth can be *sanctified* by keeping its laws, those laws are moral, not merely physical — "to God they are spiritual laws and they are laws of morality even."[^9] Rate: the strongest textual leg; it directly ties law-keeping to sanctification.
- **Connection to LDS finitism.** D&C 88:13 says even "the power of God" is "governed" by this law — Shad ties this to the constrained-God metaphysic the vault develops in [[A Constrained God Solves the Problem of Evil Better Than Creedal Christianity]]: these eternal, "self-existent, uncreated, ever-present" truths "determine what we must do to fulfill... the measure of our creation."[^10] Rate: situates the claim inside the broader Restoration doctrine of God bound by eternal law.
- **Steelman the critic.** (1) *Equivocation on "law":* the verse's "law by which all things are governed" most naturally reads as *descriptive* natural law (how things behave), and sliding from that to *prescriptive moral* law is the is/ought gap in scriptural dress. (2) *"Is" need not mean identity:* poetic apposition ("the word of the Lord is truth... is light") can express tight association or predication without strict identity, so "truth = morality" may over-read. (3) The companion vault note [[Greater Light Reveals Pre-Existing Truth]] treats light/truth as *revelatory* rather than as a flat identity, suggesting the tradition itself does not require Shad's strict equivalence. The reading is internally elegant but leans hard on one construal of "is."
