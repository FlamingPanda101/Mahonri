# Appeal to Mystery Empties the Word Good

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
The last-ditch defense is **mystery**: "God's ways are higher than ours," so the apparent contradiction is beyond our comprehension. [[Hayden Carroll]] rebuts that "mystery is not the same as meaninglessness." If "good" can include knowingly and freely creating someone who will enter hell, then "good no longer has a recognizable moral content; it becomes synonymous with whatever God does." Saying "God is good" then collapses into "God is God." See [[Good Becomes Synonymous with Whatever God Does]].[^1]

## The Rebuttal
1. **The medicine analogy (two halves)** — rate: strong semantic point. "*This medicine will heal you, but I cannot explain how*" is mysterious yet *meaningful* — "heal" still refers to restoration. But "*This medicine will kill you, but it is still healing you in a higher sense*" has *redefined* "heal" out of recognition: "the claim is no longer mysterious — rather, it is incoherent." Likewise "good" that includes creating-for-hell is incoherent, not mysterious. *(analogy)* [^2]
2. **Mystery preserves *unknown mechanism*, not *contradictory content*** — legitimate mystery is ignorance of *how*; illegitimate mystery smuggles in a *contradiction* of the term's core meaning. *(distinction)* [^1]
3. **Gavin in fact retreats here** — under cross-exam, when asked how creating someone foreknown for hell wills their good, Gavin answers "that's where I would leave it at mystery... we will not know in this life," confirming Hayden's prediction that mystery is the fallback. *(observed)* [^3]

**Steelman of the mystery defense:** the *analogical predication* tradition (Aquinas) holds that "good" said of God is neither univocal nor equivocal but *analogical* — really meaning something, but stretched beyond creaturely comprehension, the way "wise" applied to God exceeds human wisdom without being meaningless. On this view permitting hell can be consistent with divine goodness *as analogically understood*, and our inability to see how is genuine, principled mystery (creator/creature gap), not a redefinition. Hayden's reply: analogy still requires that divine goodness be *recognizably continuous* with the human concept (else it's covert equivocation); a "goodness" that does what no good human agent could do has crossed from analogy into equivocation.

## Best Quotes
> "Mystery is not the same as meaninglessness... it becomes synonymous with whatever God does. Instead of saying that God is good, it's like saying God is God. It renders the word meaningless."[^1]

> "Now, imagine I say this medicine will kill you, but it is still healing you in a higher sense. In that case, the word heal has been completely redefined... the claim is no longer mysterious. Rather it is incoherent."[^2]

> "That's where I would leave it at mystery. I just don't know why this prophecy was written so a man like Judas would end up in hell." — Gavin [^3]
