# Trinity Is an Interpretation Not Explicit in Scripture

## The Claim
[[Jacob Hansen]] objects that the doctrine of the Trinity "is not explicitly laid out in scripture." [^1] He grants the **raw data** — scripture speaks of one God and of three persons (Father, Son, Holy Spirit) — but argues the Trinity is "a particular interpretation of what that means," not the data itself. [^2] Because so much later conciliar conversation was needed to formulate the *homoousios* relationship, and because Jesus never "gave an exposé on the triune nature" in, e.g., the Sermon on the Mount, Hansen questions whether this interpretation was ever a "top-rank issue for Jesus." [^3] To bind salvation to a non-explicit interpretation, he says, "doesn't seem fair." [^4]

## The Rebuttal (Ortlund's reply, steelmanned)

1. **Ortlund concedes genuine doctrinal *development*.** "Scripture teaches in its own idiom... it's not speaking at this really high philosophical level... then as you go forward, you get more technicality" to oppose error. [^5] *Rating: an honest concession* that the *terminology* (homoousios) is post-biblical and that no one is saved by the word itself.

2. **He grounds the doctrine in Jesus' own self-claims.** Ortlund offers [[John 8.24|John 8:24]] ("unless you believe that I am, you will die in your sins") read with [[John 8.58|John 8:58]] and [[Exodus 3.14|Exodus 3:14]], plus Thomas's confession in [[John 20.28|John 20:28]] ("My Lord and my God") which Jesus answers with a beatitude on faith. [^6][^7] *Rating: a substantive textual case that belief about Jesus' identity is salvation-relevant* — though it establishes high Christology, not the full triune formula.

3. **Steelman of Hansen via John 17:3.** Hansen marshals [[John 17.3|John 17:3]] — "this is life eternal, that they might know thee, the only true God, and Jesus Christ whom thou hast sent" — where "the only true God" appears to be *the Father*, distinguished from Jesus. [^8] *Rating: a real prooftext tension* (see [[John 17.3 - The Only True God Is the Father]]); Hansen presents it as evidence the data underdetermines the Trinitarian reading.

4. **The fairness premise.** Hansen: tying damnation to a non-explicit, much-disputed interpretation while "the great Christological debates are still going on" is unjust. [^4] *Rating: rhetorically strong, philosophically contestable* — Ortlund's standing reply is point 5 of [[All Doctrine Requires Interpretation Rebuttal]]: explicitness is not the criterion of importance, since the resurrection also requires interpretation yet is essential.

## Best Quotes
> "The Trinity, so far as I can tell, it is not explicitly laid out in scripture... you have sort of in the scriptural record the raw data of three persons and one God... the doctrine of the Trinity is a particular interpretation of what that means." [^2]

> "Was the doctrine of the Trinity... a top-rank issue for Jesus? Because in the scriptures I just don't see in the New Testament where Jesus... gave an exposé on the triune nature." [^3]

> "Unless you agree with a particular interpretation that we have of scripture, you are not a Christian and you are not going to be saved... that doesn't seem fair." [^4]

> "I think most people can agree that the trinitarian formulation of the homoousios relationship between God the Father and the Son is not explicitly laid out in scripture." [^9]
