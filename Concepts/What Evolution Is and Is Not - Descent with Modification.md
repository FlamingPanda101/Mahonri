# What Evolution Is and Is Not - Descent with Modification

## The Point
[[Tyler A. Kummer]] and [[Jamie L. Jensen]] argue that much rejection of evolution stems "from an incomplete knowledge of the science," so they begin with a precise definition.[^1] Quoting a standard textbook (*Campbell Biology*): **"Evolution: Descent with modification; the idea that living species are descendants of ancestral species that were different from the present-day ones"** — or, genetically, "the change in the genetic composition of a population from generation to generation."[^2] In simplest terms, **evolution is descent with modification**. Equally important is what evolution is **not**: it is "concerned *only* with descent and modification, **not with origination**." Evolution "does not provide an explanation for the origin of life" — only "how life got from its origins (however that may have occurred) to its current form."[^3]

## Evidence
1. **Descent (common ancestry).** From a roughly homogeneous Rock Dove, humans bred wildly distinct pigeon varieties by artificial selection; on the Galápagos, one continental finch ancestor diversified into many species by natural selection. Both illustrate "organisms being modified as they descended from a common ancestor."[^4]
2. **Modification (the mechanisms).** "With modification" unpacks into five processes: **natural selection** (rock pocket mice match their lava/sand background under predation), **genetic drift** (founder effect — e.g., elevated fumaric aciduria in an FLDS community; bottleneck — northern elephant seals), **gene flow** (grizzly–polar "pizzly" hybrids), **nonrandom / sexual selection** (the peacock's tail), and **mutation** (the MC1R gene and fair, freckled skin favored in low-sun latitudes).[^5]
3. **Evolution is NOT a claim about the origin of life.** Abiogenesis (RNA-world, etc.) is "still an active question in science" that evolutionary theory deliberately leaves open — a critical boundary, because the *origin* of life is exactly where many locate God's creative act.[^3]
4. **Evolution is NOT "just a theory."** A scientific *theory* is "the end point of scientific explanation. Nothing is better; a theory is as good as it gets." Science is "falsifiable but not 'true-ifiable,'" so "just a theory" misunderstands how science works.[^6]
5. **Evolution is NOT atheism, and is the foundation of biology.** Dobzhansky's dictum frames the whole field: "Nothing in biology makes sense except in the light of evolution." Kummer and Jensen present it as "a beautiful explanatory theory" describing "God's workmanship here on earth."[^7]

## Best Quotes
> "In simplest terms, evolution is descent with modification." [^2]

> "Evolutionary theory is concerned only with descent and modification, not with origination. Evolution does not provide an explanation for the origin of life." [^3]

> "[Evolution] is a beautiful explanatory theory that ties together an otherwise endless collection of facts into a testable model... of biodiversity, and of the interactions we, as stewards, have with God's creations." [^7]

## LDS Connection
The definition itself dissolves the most common faith-conflicts. Because evolution says **nothing about the origin of life or of matter**, it leaves untouched the questions Latter-day Saints care most about — creation, purpose, and God — which is why the Church's packet can affirm "the scriptures tell *why* man was created, but they do not tell *how*." Evolution describes the *how* of the mortal-body lineage; revelation supplies the *why* and the spiritual sonship. Kummer and Jensen frame the science as the study of "God's great works of creation," echoing Elder Russell M. Nelson at the BYU Life Sciences Building dedication: "There is no conflict between science and religion... All truth is part of the gospel of Jesus Christ." The First Presidency's 1910 standard applies cleanly: "That which is demonstrated, we accept with joy."
