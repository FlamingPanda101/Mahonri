# Trinity Was Constructed Not Revealed

## The Claim
[[Dan McClellan]] argues the doctrine of the Trinity "was not something that dropped from the sky" as a revealed answer, but was **slowly constructed over time** — "between the middle of the 2nd century CE down into the 5th century CE when it really was formalized in more or less the same sense that it is understood today." [^1] The development pattern, he says, reveals "there were terms that were trying to be met," which is the signature of construction rather than inductive discovery. [^2]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The documented chronology fits gradual development.** Mainstream historical theology dates the key milestones across exactly this window: Tertullian coins *trinitas* and *persona* language (c. 200 CE); the Arian controversy and Council of Nicaea introduce *homoousios* (325); the Cappadocian Fathers refine the one-ousia/three-hypostases formula; Constantinople (381) and the later articulation of the *Athanasian Creed* fix the classical form. *Rating: strong — McClellan's dating tracks the standard scholarly timeline.*

2. **The biblical data is plural, not a single Trinitarian formula.** "We find a variety of different positions in the biblical text," only the "more philosophically minded texts of the New Testament" approximate the later terms, "already adopting some of the frameworks... namely Middle Platonic philosophical norms." [^3] *Rating: strong; the diversity of NT Christology (Synoptics vs. John vs. Paul) is widely recognized.* *Steelman:* an orthodox theologian would say the raw materials (Father, Son, Spirit each treated as divine; rigorous monotheism) are all biblical, and that creedal formulation is legitimate *development* of revelation, not invention — Newman's "development of doctrine."

3. **The two governing "terms" are external constraints, not exegetical conclusions.** The constraints were (a) "you're only allowed one God" and (b) "the Father, Son, and the Holy Spirit are clearly distinct," forcing the construction "to make A plus B plus C equal G." [^4] *Rating: persuasive as a description of the problem the Fathers faced; whether the constraints are "external" or themselves biblical is the point under dispute.*

4. **The lone explicit proof text is an interpolation.** [[1 John 5.7|1 John 5:7]] (the Johannine Comma) is absent from the earliest manuscripts (see [[1 John 5-7 - Johannine Comma Interpolation]]). *Rating: strong, established textual fact, and a notable absence given how central the doctrine became.*

## Best Quotes
> "This was not something that dropped from the sky as 'oh here's the answer, now let's figure out what this answer means.' It is something that was slowly constructed over time." [^1]

> "This was not the inductive observation of and discovery of something that was always true. This was the construction of a way to meet two different terms." [^2]

> "One of those terms was you're only allowed one God. The other term was the Father, Son, and the Holy Spirit are clearly distinct. So you basically had to find a way to make A plus B plus C equal G." [^4]
