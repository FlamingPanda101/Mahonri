# Misusing the Sexual Faculty Is Wrong Apart from Harm or Consent

## The Claim

[[Trent Horn]] argues that a "as long as everyone consents / don't cause unnecessary pain" sexual ethic — the default secular framework — **cannot explain a whole class of acts that almost everyone judges wrong**, and that their wrongness consists not in harm to the participants but in **misusing a biological process (sexuality) that carries far more value than any other biological process**. Just as the wrongness of murder can be reduced to a set of *puzzling killings* hard for atheists to explain, "you can take the wrongness of rape and reduce it to a set of puzzling cases of sexual evil that are also hard for atheists to explain."[^1] This is the second pillar of his [[The Modest Moral Argument from Particular Puzzling Acts|modest moral argument]].

Horn's strategy is to strip away every *non-sexual* explanation until only the misuse of sexuality remains:[^1]
- **Incest (the twin-brother case, from Andrew Wilson vs. Naima).** The genetic-deformity objection fails: we don't forbid women over 35 from conceiving despite higher birth-defect risk, and it doesn't apply to *same-sex* incest (no reproduction) or to *adult* twins (no grooming). Wilson removes emotional-harm by stipulation ("your whole family dies except your twin"); the best the consent-ethicist can say is "it might upset other family members" — but they wouldn't accept "it upsets the family" as grounds to condemn coming out as gay or trans. Naima concedes it's "gross" but cannot call it immoral without opening the door to condemning sodomy and other acts many find gross.
- **Genetic sexual attraction.** Separated blood relatives meeting as adults — including sperm-donor half-siblings who never knew each other — sometimes fall in love; no childhood grooming occurred, defeating the power-imbalance objection.
- **Bestiality.** The "animals can't consent" objection is wielded by people who *eat* animals without consent and accept police canine units (far more hazardous to a dog than non-penetrative acts). Destiny and Jasmine ultimately "bite the bullet"; Destiny says the only reason he could call it wrong "was because he's not religious." Philosophers **Peter Singer** and David Benatar grant that if casual hookups are permissible, some bestiality and necrophilia are too, "since animals are not always harmed by sexual activity with them, and living people can give consent to the use of their later dead bodies."
- **Necrophilia with prior consent**, and the BBC clip floating "drugged consenting partners" for a rape-fantasy kink. In each, "harms like pain or infection can be reduced or eliminated" — so harm cannot be what makes them wrong.

Horn's positive account: "What really strikes us to the core about why sex acts with animals, dead bodies, inanimate objects, or people pretending to be unconscious rape victims… is not because of what it does to the recipients… It's about the wrongness of *misusing a particular biological process* that has much, much more value than any other mere biological process."[^2] This is just what we'd expect if God designed human sexuality "for union with other humans outside their immediate family in order to create new families."[^3] Notably, the *non-theist* reviewer [[Joe Schmid]] concedes the point lands: a better account than bare natural-law theory "cites God specifically setting aside the sexual faculty as distinctively sacred with a life-giving and sacramental purpose and serving as an icon of the Trinity," and admits the atheistic explanations "can get strained, so you might doubt that they're plausible."[^4]

## Supporting Evidence (steelman both sides)

1. **The case selection is surgical (rate: strong as analysis).** Each example is built to kill a *specific* secular escape: deformity (→ over-35 pregnancy, same-sex pairs), grooming (→ adult twins, genetic sexual attraction), consent (→ eating animals, canine units), and emotional harm (→ stipulated deaths, or the family-disapproval double standard with sodomy). What survives every subtraction is the bare *misuse of the faculty*, which is precisely the theist's thesis.[^1]
2. **The bullet-biting is real and documented (rate: strong, primary).** Horn is not attacking a strawman: named, respected philosophers (Singer, Benatar) and prominent streamers (Destiny, Jasmine) *do* accept the permissibility implications rather than abandon the consent/harm ethic — and Benatar explicitly ties it to the casual-hookup premise. That makes the reductio bite: the consistent secular position endorses conclusions most people find monstrous.[^1]
3. **A non-theist concedes the explanatory gap (rate: strong, hostile-witness).** [[Joe Schmid]], who reviewed the script as a non-theist, grants that the God-grounded account of the sexual faculty's sacredness is "much better" than natural-law theory and that secular accounts "get strained." A concession from the opposing side is the strongest evidence the surplus is genuine, even if it does not by itself establish theism. See [[The Modest Moral Argument from Particular Puzzling Acts]].[^4]

### Steelman of the atheist

The secular ethicist is not defenseless. (a) **Bullet-biting as honesty:** Singer and Benatar would say following the consent/harm principle to its real edges is *intellectual integrity*, and that "it just feels sacred" is a gut reaction the theist has dressed up as metaphysics — the wrongness-intuition may be an evolved disgust response, not perception of a violated cosmic design. (b) **Thicker secular accounts exist:** a virtue-ethicist or relational account can call these acts wrong because they express disordered character, instrumentalize bodies, or corrupt one's capacity for genuine union — *without* invoking God; Schmid grants there are "non-silly, non-theistic accounts," only that they are *harder*. (c) **"Misusing a biological process" needs grounding too:** absent a designer, *why* does the reproductive process have "much more value than any other," and who fixed its proper use? The theist answers "God's design," but the atheist can reply that *strained* is not *refuted*, and that "icon of the Trinity" is doing no explanatory work an atheist must accept. (d) **Disgust is an unreliable guide:** many acts once judged "gross and therefore wrong" (interracial marriage, homosexuality) were later seen as harmless, so the very intuitions Horn leans on have a poor track record — the consent/harm ethic is what *corrected* them. Horn's reply is that this is exactly why he frames it modestly (see [[The Modest Moral Argument from Particular Puzzling Acts]]): the cases open a door toward design, they do not slam it shut on the naturalist.
