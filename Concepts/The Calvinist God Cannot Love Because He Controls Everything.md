# The Calvinist God Cannot Love Because He Controls Everything

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
The Calvinist holds that God's total sovereignty and control are perfections, and that creating reprobate persons (and even Satan) to "demonstrate his wrath" alongside his love glorifies God. [[Jacob Hansen]] voices this: "if he didn't have these sorts of things that he created, he couldn't demonstrate his wrath, and he needs to show us both his wrath and his love." [^1] See [[Meticulous Providence Makes God the Author of Evil]].

## The Rebuttal
1. **Damning the innocent is by definition unloving** — rate: the central moral intuition, forcefully stated. [[Blake Ostler]]: "Your God's not loving, because if a person loves another person, they don't send them to hell for no act of their own, but just because I decided to. It's that simple." *(moral intuition)* [^2]
2. **Creating Lucifer to wreak havoc is monstrous** — rate: a pointed reductio of "wrath display." "He created Satan... knowing that all he was going to do was wreak havoc, and that he was going to be the most miserable person in the universe for all of existence. And he created him anyway. What kind of being does that?" ([[Revelation 12.4|cf. the dragon sweeping a third of the stars]].) *(reductio)* [^3]
3. **Total control precludes flourishing and love** — rate: ties to [[Love Cannot Be Coerced or Created]]. "This God can't love because he controls everything. He's a very controlling person. But nobody can flourish when they're being totally controlled." A God who creates us so we can give love to him is "simply giving love to himself." *(systematic)* [^4]
4. **"Wrath to display love" is incoherent narcissism** — rate: strong. "I'm going to create a being just so he can wreck havoc... what kind of being is that? I need to create you because I'm angry and I need to express my anger at you." That is not the God Jesus revealed. *(theological)* [^5] See [[God's Wrath Is the Natural Consequence of Rejecting Love]].

**Steelman of the Reformed view:** Calvinism appeals to [[Romans 11.6|Romans 9]] (the potter and the clay; vessels of wrath "prepared for destruction" to make known the riches of glory on vessels of mercy) and to the principle that God's chief end is his own glory, displayed through *both* mercy and justice. On this view God owes salvation to no one; passing over the reprobate is *justice*, not cruelty, and the contrast magnifies grace. Ostler's reply: a "justice" that condemns persons for a guilt God himself decreed they would have is not justice but theater, and a glory that requires manufacturing eternal misery is not glory but the narcissism of a being eliciting praise from puppets — see [[Meticulous Providence Makes God the Author of Evil]].
