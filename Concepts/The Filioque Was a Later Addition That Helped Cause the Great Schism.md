# The Filioque Was a Later Addition That Helped Cause the Great Schism

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
Catholics and Protestants charge Latter-day Saints with "adding to the gospel" and with not believing the Nicene Creed (specifically the clause that the Holy Spirit "proceedeth from the Father and the Son"). The guest flags this clause as the one genuine point where the *received* Creed says something Mormons reject — but argues the offending words were themselves a later addition. [^1]

## The Rebuttal

### 1. The "and the Son" is not in the 325 Creed (the core)
"We don't believe the Holy Spirit proceeds from the Father and the Son. Notice 'and the Son' — another thing that the Catholics and Protestants added into the Nicene Creed." [^2] The guest explains the Latin **Filioque** ("and the Son"): "Filioque means 'and the Son' in Latin. The original Creed doesn't say the Father and the Son. The original Creed only says the Father." [^3] **Rating: historically accurate** — the Filioque is a Western interpolation (added in the Latin West over centuries, notably promoted from the 6th–11th c.), absent from the original 381 conciliar text.

### 2. The tu quoque reversal (rhetorical centerpiece)
"If they try and tell you you're not a Christian because you add to the Gospel — really? So, why did you add to the Nicene Creed 6 to 700 years after?" [^1] The guest weaponizes the critics' own "don't add to scripture" principle (cf. Revelation 22:18-19) against the Filioque. **Rating: effective tu quoque**, though it does not establish the LDS pneumatology positively — it only embarrasses the additive critic.

### 3. The Filioque and the 1054 Schism (historical backing)
"The Catholics and the Orthodox split over the Filioque ... They split over the authority of the Pope and because of the Filioque. But they split over the authority of the Pope because the Pope added the Filioque and said, 'You have to listen to me.'" [^4] **Rating: substantially correct** — the Filioque and papal authority were the two principal doctrinal flashpoints of the 1054 East–West Schism, exactly as the co-host recalls.

### 4. Steelman of the opposing view
The Catholic reply: the Filioque is not an *addition of new doctrine* but a *clarification* the Western Church holds was implicit in Nicene faith and biblically grounded (e.g. the Spirit as "the Spirit of the Son," Gal 4:6; John's "he will take what is mine"). Rome would say a legitimate ecumenical authority may dogmatically clarify the creed, so "adding" words is categorically different from a private group editing scripture. The Orthodox reply, conversely, agrees the *addition was illicit* (unilateral, without an ecumenical council) — which means the guest's point lands against Rome but is shared with Orthodoxy, not unique to the LDS position. Either way, the dispute concerns the Spirit's *eternal procession*, a question the LDS Godhead model reframes entirely rather than answers on the creed's terms.
