# Modern Gender Equality Is Not Grounded in the Bible

## The Claim (Objection)

[[Alex OConnor|Alex O'Connor]]: "There is absolutely no way you can look at the Bible or the history of the church and think there is even an embryo of the idea of something like our modern conception of gender equality." Modern gender equality (equal *societal standing*, not merely equal worth before God) is indebted to feminism, not Scripture.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The distinction modern people actually make.** When asked if men and women are equal, people don't merely mean "both have worth / neither should be killed" — they mean "equal societal standing." O'Connor challenges Scrivener to locate *that* in the Christian tradition.[^1] *(Sharp — separates "equal in dignity before God" (arguably biblical) from "equal social and political standing" (the modern claim).)*

2. **Pauline subordination texts.** [[1 Corinthians 14.34-35|1 Cor 14:34-35]] ("women keep silence... a shame for a woman to speak in church"), [[1 Timothy 2.11|1 Tim 2:11]], and a church history that barred women from office. O'Connor: the tradition "has quite clearly been interpreted throughout history as saying women can't hold church office."[^2] *(Strong — the reception history overwhelmingly subordinated women; Protestants "changed their mind" only recently.)*

3. **The double-edged authorship argument.** O'Connor: 1 Cor 14:34-35 contradicts [[1 Corinthians 11.5|1 Cor 11:5]] (women pray and prophesy), so Paul likely didn't write it — see [[1 Corinthians 14 Was Not Written by Paul]]. Irony: "I, a skeptic, can absolve Paul of sexism; the Christian has to own them."[^3] *(Clever — turns critical scholarship into a *problem* for the inerrantist who must own the canonical text regardless.)*

4. **Steelman of Scrivener.** (a) [[Galatians 3-28 Is a Theological Reality Not a Rights Charter|Galatians 3:28]] and the marriage program ([[Joseph Henrich|Henrich]]) furnish the intellectual genealogy of feminism; (b) the book of Acts shows prominent women — Philip's four prophesying daughters, Priscilla correcting, "Junia outstanding among the apostles," Phoebe the deacon — a trajectory that, extended, yields suffrage; (c) "who has overturned patriarchy more than Western societies?"[^4] *(Strong on trajectory and on the comparative record; weak on whether *the texts themselves* contain the modern egalitarian principle, which O'Connor specifically denies. See [[Feminism Tracks Material Conditions Not Christian Doctrine]].)*
