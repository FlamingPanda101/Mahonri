# Same-Species-as-God as the Core LDS Distinctive in Theosis

## The Point
In a candid sidebar ("Comparing Ancient Christian and Latter-day Saint Teachings"), Daniel Becerra names exactly where patristic deification and LDS exaltation **diverge** — and it is the same-species ontology. He flags two differences between the fathers' exchange formula and Lorenzo Snow's couplet ("As man now is, God once was; as God now is, man may be"): (1) the **identity of God** — the fathers meant the Incarnation of *Christ*, whereas Snow alludes to *the Father* via Joseph Smith's King Follett Sermon; and (2) **human nature** — Irenaeus and Athanasius "understood God (as Creator) and humans (as creations) as different species, so to speak, rather than the same species albeit at different points of development." [^1] This is, by an LDS scholar's own admission, the line that separates patristic theosis from LDS exaltation.

## Evidence
1. **The Father's "humanity" is non-standard, even in LDS terms.** Becerra notes the King Follett Sermon ("God himself was once as we are now... dwelt on an earth, the same as Jesus Christ") "is not considered a 'doctrinal standard' in the Church," and modern leaders "have not speculated further on the idea of Heavenly Father's potential 'humanity.'" [^2] (Compare [[God Was Once a Man Is Esoteric Not Core Doctrine]].)
2. **The species claim is the genuine LDS distinctive.** Becerra cites [[Parley P. Pratt]]: "God, angels and men, are all of one species, one race, one great family." [^3] This is the position the fathers explicitly *denied*: for them humans can become "immortal and incorruptible like God" but "did not understand these divine attributes to be proper to our nature." [^1]
3. **Irenaeus's own ceiling.** Becerra: Irenaeus "believed that created things are inherently inferior to that which created them, and for this reason, humans can never fully be like God, who, unlike humans, is immortal and incorruptible by nature." [^4]

## Best Quotes
> "Irenaeus and Athanasius understood God (as Creator) and humans (as creations) as different species, so to speak, rather than the same species albeit at different points of development." [^1]

> "God, angels and men, are all of one species, one race, one great family widely diffused among the planetary systems." — Parley P. Pratt [^3]

## LDS Connection
This is the **honest fault line** the apologist must own. The fathers supply the *vocabulary* of deification (see [[Christ Became Man That We Might Become God - The Exchange Formula]]), and Becerra concedes the parallel to Lorenzo Snow is real. But the load-bearing LDS distinctive — that God and humanity are the **same kind** at different developmental stages (see [[God and Man Are the Same Species]], [[Humans Are the Same Species as God (Acts 17 Offspring)]]) — is precisely what Irenaeus and Athanasius rejected. The strongest apologetic claims the *tradition* of theosis while acknowledging the *ontology* is uniquely LDS and rooted in [[King Follett Discourse]].
