# altar boy

An altar boy (altar server or acolyte) is a lay assistant — traditionally a boy — who aids the priest during Catholic and other liturgical services, carrying candles and the cross, presenting the water and wine, and handling other ceremonial duties at the altar. The role is a common entry point into devout Catholic religious life.

## Relevance

The term appears in the vault as a biographical marker for [[Avraham Gileadi]], who was raised a devout Catholic altar boy (taught English by monks) and briefly entered a seminary before leaving it as "too much ritual and not enough holy spirit" — a step on the path that eventually led him through Judaism into the [[Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints]].
