# Calling Someone a Cultist Is Counterproductive

## The Claim
[[Kyle Beshears]] argues that even *if* an evangelical believes Mormonism is a cult, it is unwise to approach a Latter-day Saint in that mode. Drawing on a PhD seminar in new religious movements, he reports a striking observation: "actual cult leaders depend on their members being called cultists." [^1][^2]

## The Rebuttal / Supporting Evidence

1. **The "wall" mechanism.** When an outsider says "you're in a cult," "the wall gets built up immediately and they climb it and then they're ready to defend, because nobody likes to be told that they're in a cult." [^3] *Rating: psychologically plausible* and matches standard exit-counseling literature on reactance.

2. **It confirms the leader's persecution script.** Being labeled "reaffirms the fact that they're different and this is precisely what their leader told them would happen, that they're going to be persecuted because the world doesn't understand them." [^4] *Rating: strong.* The label hands a genuine high-control leader exactly the in-group/out-group reinforcement he wants.

3. **It forecloses all future communication.** Beshears: even with a real cult like NXIVM, "I still wouldn't go up to those people and be like, you know, you're in a cult... because you've just completely abolished any hope of having any kind of communication with people again." [^5] *Rating: a consistent application* — he holds the principle even where he grants the "cult" label is accurate.

4. **The agency irony for the specific LDS case.** Calling Latter-day Saints a "mind control cult" "strips Latter-day Saints of agency, which they're huge on," and ignores the church's tolerated internal diversity. [^6] *Rating: doubles as an internal-consistency objection* (see [[Mormonism Is Not a Cult]]).

**Steelman of the opposing view.** A countercult evangelical might answer that naming a cult is a *warning* owed to outsiders and a wake-up call to members, and that bluntness, not bridge-building, has rescued people from genuinely dangerous groups. Beshears does not deny that warnings have a place; his narrower claim is strategic — the word "cult" as an opening move toward a *member* almost always defeats its own purpose.

## Best Quotes
> "Actual cult leaders depend on their members being called cultists." [^2]

> "First, the wall gets built up immediately and they climb it... nobody likes to be told that they're in a cult. Second, it just reaffirms the fact that they're different, and this is precisely what their leader told them would happen." [^4]

> "Even if I knew of a cult in my own city... like NXIVM for example, I still wouldn't go up to those people and be like, you know, you're in a cult, because you've just completely abolished any hope of having any kind of communication with people again who are actually in a cult." [^5]
