# The "Brigham Young Is a False Prophet" Argument

## The Claim
[[Joe Heschmeyer]] lays out a tight syllogism to argue [[Brigham Young]] is a false prophet — independent of whether Adam-God ever became canon:

1. A true prophet will **never teach false and damnable doctrine as if it came from God.**
2. **Adam-God is recognized as a damnable doctrine today** — by mainstream Christians *and* by the LDS Church itself.
3. **Brigham Young taught Adam-God** (already conceded).
4. ∴ Brigham Young is not a true prophet [^1].

The conclusion "holds whether his teachings ever reached the highest level of canonicity or anything else."

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. The Moloch reductio (strong — isolates the real problem)
Heschmeyer: even if the LDS would never canonize a future president saying "let's all worship Moloch," the failure to canonize "doesn't get rid of the problem." The problem is "this person who thinks they're talking to God and claims to be the mouthpiece of God is talking to a demon or is delusional" [^2].

### 2. The internal confusion is itself disqualifying (strong — uses LDS concessions)
Because Young "doesn't know whether he's talking to Heavenly Father or Adam or Michael," he fails even the lower **[[Reliability Not Infallibility Standard|reliability]]** bar, not just infallibility [^3].

### 3. McConkie calls it damning (strong — internal LDS authority)
[[Bruce R. McConkie]]: Young "erred in some of the statements on the nature and kind of being that God is... If we choose to believe and teach the false portions of his doctrines, we are making an election that will damn us" [^4]. Heschmeyer: this shows orthodoxy is not "second-tier" inside the LDS tradition itself.

### Steelman (Hansen's side)
[[Luke Hansen]] denies premise 1's force: "an incorrect teaching doesn't a false prophet make, as Yoda might have put it" [^5]. He reframes McConkie: the sin is not *believing* wrong but **publicly rebelling against the living oracle** — "an act of rebellion against the prophet of the church," opposing "the oracles of God on the earth" [^6]. And he insists Young never denied the *existence* or *attributes* of God, only mis-identified the referent (see [[Common Reference - Worshipping Adam as God]]).
