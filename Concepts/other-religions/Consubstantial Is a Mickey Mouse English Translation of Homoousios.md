# Consubstantial Is a Mickey Mouse English Translation of Homoousios

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
Critics press Mormons with the Creed's word **"consubstantial with the Father"** as a non-negotiable Trinitarian commitment — the very point Jacob Hansen debates against Catholics and Protestants. The co-host stops the reading exactly here: "We always take issue with this this word right here, consubstantial." [^1]

## The Rebuttal

### 1. The translation-quibble move (the central rebuttal)
The guest concedes the *English* word but denies it carries the freight critics load on it: "that's a bad translation, but sure" [^1]; later, "that's a kind of a Mickey Mouse English translation, but we'll go with it. Same essence." [^2] The strategy is to **collapse "consubstantial" into "same essence" (homoousios)** and then accept the Greek while disowning the Latinized English connotations of one undivided substance. **Rating: partly fair as translation history** — "consubstantial" is the Latin *consubstantialis* rendering of *homoousios*, and English "substance" does drift toward "stuff/material" — **but the dodge does real work**: it lets the guest accept the term while declining its intended sense of numerically one divine being.

### 2. The "translational error / just change" framing
On the second pass through the Creed the guest labels it: "Begotten not made, consubstantial with the Father. I get that. Translational error, not error, but just change." [^3] He folds it under his broader point that "English ... wasn't developed until a thousand years later," so translation slippage is expected. [^4] **Rating: the diachronic-language point is true but generic** — it does not by itself show the standard rendering is wrong, only that translation is hard.

### 3. The debate-authority appeal
The guest repeatedly anchors the dispute in **Jacob Hansen's** public debates — against an unnamed doctor on **Cameron Bertuzzi's** channel, an upcoming debate with **God Logic** in March, and the "Blessed God conference" alongside **Wes Huff** and **Mike Winger** [^5] — positioning "consubstantial" as live, contested ground rather than settled doctrine. **Rating: appeal to ongoing debate, not to a decisive argument.**

### 4. Steelman of the opposing view
A Catholic/Protestant reply: calling "consubstantial" a "Mickey Mouse translation" is rhetoric, not refutation. *Consubstantialis* was a careful, council-sanctioned Latin equivalent, and the English "consubstantial" was deliberately restored to the Roman Missal (2011) precisely to preserve the strong sense of *one being* against weaker glosses like "one in being." Reducing it to "same essence" in the generic sense is the very softening Nicaea coined *homoousios* (over *homoiousios*) to block. The guest's own ally framing — that this is what Hansen *debates* — concedes the orthodox side has substantive arguments, not merely a bad translation.
