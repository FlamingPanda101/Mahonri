# Two Mistakes That Turn a Denomination's Distinctives Into the Gospel

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Trent Horn]] diagnoses **two mistaken lines of thought** by which "unpleasant Protestants" arrive at the conclusion that Catholicism teaches a false gospel: [^1]

1. **They equate *the gospel* with *all essential doctrines of the faith*.** But "the Bible never defines the gospel in this way." The Trinity and Jesus's messianic status are essential Christian doctrines, "but Scripture never says that these truths are part of the gospel or the good news."
2. **They treat *their denomination's* essential doctrines as essential to *Christianity itself*** — and therefore as part of the gospel. So a Reformed distinctive like [[Double Imputation Is Not the Gospel|double imputation]] gets elevated into "the gospel that we preach," and anyone who rejects it is judged sub-Christian.

The two errors compound: first the category "gospel" is swollen to absorb every essential, then "essential" is filled in with sectarian content. The result is a definition of the gospel that no other tradition shares — which is how the false-gospel charge is manufactured. [^2]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **Essential ≠ gospel.** Horn's wedge is that even doctrines all Christians confess (Trinity, messiahship) are never *called* the gospel in Scripture. If the gospel and "the set of all essential doctrines" were identical, these would have to appear in the gospel — but they do not. So the equation in mistake (1) is unbiblical on its face. [^1]
2. **Protestants already concede the gospel is not "everything the NT teaches."** Horn notes that "Protestants know the gospel is not identical to everything the New Testament teaches because each of the major Protestant denominations differ on what they think the New Testament teaches" — yet they do not anathematize one another over every disagreement. The inconsistency: they grant latitude to fellow Protestants but not to Catholics. [^3]
3. **The danger is a brittle, ever-shrinking church.** When [[Randal Rauser]] critiques [[Voddie Baucham]], he warns the move "leads to people treating Christians who disagree... as being less Christian or not even Christian at all." This is the dynamic captured in [[Mistaking Tradition for the Gospel Sets Up Brittle Faith]]. [^2]
4. **Steelman of the Protestant counter.** A thoughtful Reformed reader would say mistake (2) is not a mistake at all: the Reformation's material principle is that **justification by faith alone is the gospel** ("the article by which the church stands or falls"), so getting *that* wrong is getting the gospel wrong by definition — it is not a mere denominational distinctive but the hinge of salvation. Horn's reply is that this simply *restates* the disputed premise; the Reformer must still show **where Scripture calls sola fide "the gospel,"** and he cannot, because the word *euangelion* is never attached to it (see [[The Gospel Is the Good News of God's Kingdom Not a Doctrinal Formula]]). The same circularity is why he turns at the end to [[A Living Magisterium Settles What Sola Scriptura Cannot|a teaching office]] rather than [[Sola Scriptura - Scripture Self-Attests Its Authority|sola scriptura]] to adjudicate essentials.

## Best Quotes
> "First, they assume the gospel is identical to all essential doctrines of the faith. But as we've seen, the Bible never defines the gospel in this way." [^1]

> "Second, these critics assume essential doctrines for their denomination must also be essential doctrines of the Christian faith. And so those doctrines must be part of the gospel as well." [^1]
