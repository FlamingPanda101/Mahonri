# A False Gospel Is One That Contradicts the Kingdom Message

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Trent Horn]] argues the gospel must be neither over-defined nor under-defined. Against under-definition: it "has essential elements a Christian cannot reject," so in [[Galatians 1.6|Galatians 1]] Paul "chastises the Galatians for deserting the faith for a different gospel." Against over-definition: Paul "reserves the charge of having another gospel or a false gospel for doctrines that **contradict the core message** of the gospel" — not for every theological error he corrects in his letters. [^1]

The criterion is therefore content-specific: a teaching is "another gospel" only if it contradicts the announcement that **God's kingdom has come universally through the death and resurrection of his Son**. [^2]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **Islam is a true counter-example.** Islam "teaches a false gospel because its good news about salvation rejects Christ's death and resurrection," which Horn has already established are "fundamental parts of the gospel." Denying the resurrection contradicts the core, so it qualifies. [^1]
2. **The circumcision party is Paul's own test case.** Requiring circumcision for salvation "constituted a false gospel" because it "basically said to be a Christian you have to become a Jew first" — which contradicts the good news "that through the Son, God's kingdom would be universal and not belong only to one group of people." So even in Galatians, the disqualifier is contradiction of the *kingdom-universality* message, not bare doctrinal error. [^2]
3. **Paul corrects many errors *without* the "anathema."** Horn's point is that Paul rebukes numerous theological mistakes in his epistles but uses the "another gospel" curse sparingly — reserved for core-contradicting teachings. This narrowness is what disqualifies elevating [[Double Imputation Is Not the Gospel|double imputation]] (a non-core mechanism) to gospel status. [^2]
4. **Steelman of the Protestant counter.** A Reformed reader applies the same Galatians 1 logic *against Catholicism*: Trent's teaching on meritorious works "perverts" grace, which (the argument runs) *does* contradict the core because it changes how the kingdom's salvation is received. This is the standard evangelical use of [[Galatians 1-8 - Angel Preaching Another Gospel|Galatians 1:8]]. Horn's reply is that Catholicism affirms the resurrection and grace-initiated salvation, so it does not contradict the kingdom announcement; the disagreement is over the *mode of appropriation*, which Paul treats as a corrigible error, not "another gospel." The structural irony is sharp: this is the **mirror image** of how Latter-day Saint apologists answer the same proof-text in [[Galatians 1-8 Latter-day Saints Preach Pauls Gospel]] — both Catholic and LDS apologists argue the evangelical reading of Galatians 1 over-extends the curse beyond what Paul intended.
