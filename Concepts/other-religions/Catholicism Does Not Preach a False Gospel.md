# Catholicism Does Not Preach a False Gospel

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Trent Horn]] argues that because the [[The Gospel Is the Good News of God's Kingdom Not a Doctrinal Formula|biblical gospel]] is "the salvation that comes through God's power being implemented in his kingdom through his Son," and the Catholic Church **affirms exactly this truth**, the Catholic Church is not preaching a false gospel. He cites the glossary of the *Catechism of the Catholic Church*, which defines the gospel as "the good news of God's mercy and love revealed in the life, death, and resurrection of Christ... that the apostles and the church following them are to proclaim to the entire world." [^1]

The charge that Catholicism teaches "a false gospel and so isn't even Christian" is the hallmark of what Horn calls **unpleasant Protestants**; most Protestants ("pleasant Protestants") do not make this claim. The whole video is a response to the unpleasant minority. [^2]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **The Catechism's definition matches the biblical one.** The catechetical gloss anchors the gospel in "the life, death, and resurrection of Christ" — the same kingdom-and-cross announcement of [[1 Corinthians 15.1-4|1 Cor 15:1-4]] and [[Romans 1.16|Rom 1:16]] — not in any later soteriological theory. If the gospel is what the Bible says it is, Catholicism preaches it. [^1]
2. **The false-gospel charge depends on a non-biblical definition.** Horn's argument is conditional: Catholicism only "preaches a false gospel" if the gospel is redefined to include doctrines (e.g. [[Double Imputation Is Not the Gospel|double imputation]], sola fide) that "the Bible never defines the gospel" as containing. Remove the smuggled definition and the charge evaporates — see [[Two Mistakes That Turn a Denomination's Distinctives Into the Gospel]]. [^3]
3. **Steelman of the Protestant counter.** A Reformed critic (e.g. [[Voddie Baucham]]) holds that Trent's anathemas against *sola fide* and its teaching on merit, purgatory, and Marian doctrine **add works to grace**, which is precisely the [[Galatians 1-8 - Angel Preaching Another Gospel|Galatians 1]] error of "another gospel." On this view Catholicism doesn't deny the resurrection but corrupts the *application* of the cross, and a corrupted application is still a false gospel. Horn's reply (developed in [[A False Gospel Is One That Contradicts the Kingdom Message]]) is that Paul reserves the "another gospel" charge for doctrines that **contradict the core kingdom message** (denying the resurrection, requiring circumcision), not for every disputed soteriological mechanism — and that Catholicism, like the magisterial Reformers themselves, affirms salvation by grace through Christ. Note the parallel with Latter-day Saint apologetics, where [[Galatians 1-8 Latter-day Saints Preach Pauls Gospel|the same Galatians 1 text is wielded against the Restoration]]: Horn's narrowing of what counts as "another gospel" is a move both Catholic and LDS apologists make against evangelical maximalism.

## Best Quotes
> "Since Catholicism affirms this truth, that means the Catholic Church is not preaching a false gospel." [^1]

> "As a Catholic, it's not hard to find other Christians who say, 'My religion teaches a false gospel and so it isn't even Christian.' Thankfully, most Protestants don't make these unpleasant claims." [^2]
