# Holding the Tension Between Anti-Mormonism and Syncretism

## The Claim
[[Kyle Beshears]] describes the methodological heart of his book as "holding the tension as tightly as possible" between two opposite failures: anti-Mormon caricature on one end and a syncretism that pretends "differences don't really matter" on the other. The goal is to "meet somewhere in the middle." [^1][^2]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Both poles named precisely.** One end: misrepresenting Latter-day Saints, going "anti-Mormon" — "anybody who believes this is dumb and in a cult." [^3] The other end: interfaith dialogue that "discounts our disagreements... at the end of the day our disagreements don't really matter." [^4] *Rating: a genuinely two-sided framing,* uncommon in countercult material.

2. **Accurate representation even where he most disagrees.** "I tried very very hard to do unto others as I would have done unto myself and present Latter-day Saint faith and tradition and history in as accurate of a light as possible even at points where I strongly and severely disagree." [^5] *Rating: a robust hermeneutical commitment* (the principle of charity applied to a rival faith).

3. **The temptation to soften is itself the danger.** When a disagreement is stark — he names the LDS reading of the Fall as heroic vs. his view that it was "tragic... evil in the deepest sense" — "the easy thing to do is to just mitigate it... to talk around it... but the problem with that is you're going to slip into 'yeah, at the end of the day it doesn't really matter.'" [^6][^7] *Rating: a sharp insight* — softening differences is not neutrality but a slide toward syncretism.

4. **The symmetrical formula.** "We can't say differences are all that matters... We also can't say differences don't matter. We have to meet somewhere in the middle." [^2] *Rating: clean and memorable;* it is the book's thesis in one sentence.

**Steelman of both opposing poles.** The anti-Mormon would say sin and false doctrine demand confrontation, not balance; the ecumenist would say shared love of Christ should outweigh metaphysical quarrels. Beshears answers the first with the duty of *accurate* (not contemptuous) disagreement, and the second by insisting that on first-order questions about God's nature the differences are "a huge deal" and cannot be dissolved.

## Best Quotes
> "We don't want to slip into anti-Mormonism, but we also don't want to slip into that syncretism either where differences don't matter." [^2]

> "I tried very very hard to do unto others as I would have done unto myself and present Latter-day Saint faith and tradition and history in as accurate of a light as possible even at points where I strongly and severely disagree." [^5]

> "The easy thing to do is to just mitigate it. It's to soften it... and try to lessen the tension as much as possible. But the problem with that is you're going to slip into, yeah, at the end of the day it doesn't really matter — when actually it's a huge deal." [^7]
