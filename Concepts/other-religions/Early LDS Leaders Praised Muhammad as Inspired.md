# Early LDS Leaders Praised Muhammad as Inspired

## The Claim
Where 19th-century critics weaponized the Joseph–Muhammad link as a smear ([[Joseph-Muhammad Comparison as Islamophobic Smear]]), early Latter-day Saint leaders **inverted it**, speaking of Muhammad warmly as an inspired man who brought truth to his people. The Church has historically extended this generous "portion of God's light" reading — making LDS attitudes toward Islam strikingly positive for their time.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **Back-to-back sermons in the Journal of Discourses praised Islam.** Peterson recounts that in volume 3 of the *Journal of Discourses*, George A. Smith spoke at length and positively about "Muhammadanism" (the period term), and Parley P. Pratt then rose and said he had enjoyed the book so much he would speak on the same thing.[^2] *(Strong — these JoD sermons are real, identifiable documents.)* See [[Parley P. Pratt]].
2. **The "enemy of my enemy" dynamic.** Peterson and the host name the logic: because anti-Mormon critics also hated Islam, Latter-day Saints reasoned "if the critics hate Islam and they hate us, then Islam must not be all that bad." The result was "amazingly positive things said about Islam in the 1850s in Salt Lake City."[^3] *(Moderate — a fair characterization of the rhetorical climate; the motive attribution is interpretive.)*
3. **A First Presidency statement honored Muhammad's portion of light.** Peterson notes the 1978 First Presidency statement under President Spencer W. Kimball that paid tribute to Muhammad as having received "a portion of God's light," alongside figures like Confucius and Plato.[^4] *(Strong — the February 1978 First Presidency statement on God's love for all mankind names such figures.)* See [[Spencer W. Kimball]].
4. **Steelman.** A critic might say these warm statements are damning: they show the Church treating Muhammad as a genuine prophet, which (the critic claims) only proves Mormonism's lax standards. Peterson's framework answers this directly — for an LDS theology of [[Other True Prophets Do Not Undercut Joseph Smith|fallible, plural prophets receiving partial light]], honoring Muhammad's inspiration is a feature, not an embarrassment.

## Best Quotes
> "In the third volume of the Journal of Discourses you have back-to-back speeches... George A. Smith first... and then Parley Pratt stands up after him... and he says, 'Oh, George A. Smith shouldn't apologize for this. I enjoyed it so much I'm going to talk about the same thing.'" — [[Daniel C. Peterson|Daniel Peterson]] [^2]

> "You get some amazingly positive things said about Islam in the 1850s in Salt Lake City. They'll say, 'Well, he was an inspired man. He brought truth to his people as much as they could handle.'" [^3]

> "First Presidency under President Kimball issued a statement in which they paid tribute to Muhammad as having received a portion of God's light, along with other great leaders, Confucius, Plato, and so on." — [[Daniel C. Peterson|Daniel Peterson]] [^4]
