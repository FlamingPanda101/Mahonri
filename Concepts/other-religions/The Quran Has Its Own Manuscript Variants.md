# The Quran Has Its Own Manuscript Variants

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Wes Huff]] argues that the traditional Sunni boast — that the Quran has been transmitted without the alteration of "a single word or a single letter... a single punctuation mark or a single diacritical mark" (he quotes Mazhar Qazi) — is "just incorrect," because we possess **early Quran manuscripts that show textual variation**, and because two divergent textual traditions of the Quran are in active use today. The point is not that the Quran is wildly corrupt, but that the claim of perfect, miraculous preservation is historically false.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **The Sana'a palimpsest (1972).** Discovered during repairs to the Great Mosque in Sana'a, Yemen, this is a *palimpsest*: an original Quran text washed off and overwritten with a different copy. Ultraviolet and radiocarbon analysis reveal the lower (erased) text differs from the upper, and the upper differs in scribal accuracy — direct manuscript evidence of variation. *Rating: the Sana'a palimpsest is genuine and significant; widely studied (Sadeghi & Goudarzi); strong.*[^1]
2. **The Birmingham Quran (2015).** A late-7th / early-8th-century manuscript — among the earliest extant — which, Huff says, also shows textual variation; one of his own graduate papers analyzed differing diacritical marks in it. *Rating: the Birmingham folios are authentic and very early; the variation claim is defensible at the level of orthography/diacritics.*[^1]
3. **Two living traditions: Hafs vs. Warsh.** ~97% of the Muslim world reads a **Hafs** Quran (the 1924 Egyptian/Cairo printing); Huff reports reading the **Warsh** Quran (~3%, parts of North/West Africa) in person, and that it differs from Hafs. So "it's incorrect to say there's only one Quran." *Rating: Hafs and Warsh are real, distinct *qira'at* (canonical readings); the differences are mostly minor but real.*[^2]
4. **Paris MS 328 (Surah 3:158).** Huff cites the "great Paris manuscript" (BnF Arabe 328), an early Quran with a different **diacritical mark** at Surah 3:158 yielding a substantive difference: the standard 1924 text reads "you will be **gathered** before Allah," while the manuscript reads "you will **not** be gathered before Allah." *Rating: a genuinely substantive variant (presence/absence of negation), comparable in significance to the kinds of NT variants critics highlight.*[^2]
5. **Scholarly disarray.** Huff quotes [[Fred Donner]] (U. of Chicago): "Quranic studies as a field... appears today to be in a state of disarray," with basic questions about the Quran's origin, language, and codification still open. Huff thinks Donner slightly too pessimistic and gives more credit to the hadith, but uses him to show the academic mainstream does not share the traditional certainty. *Rating: Donner's quote is accurate and well-known.*[^2]

## Apologetic Engagement
The **strongest Muslim reply** is that the cited variants are exactly what Islamic tradition already accommodates: the canonical *qira'at* (Hafs, Warsh, and others) descend from the "seven ahruf" the Prophet authorized, so Hafs/Warsh differences are *sanctioned* readings, not corruptions; the Sana'a lower text may be a discarded personal codex (e.g. Ibn Mas'ud-type) from *before* Uthman's standardization, which is precisely what standardization was meant to resolve; and diacritical/vocalization differences are not changes to the consonantal *rasm*, which remains stable. A fair assessment: this reply blunts the rhetorical force of "not a single letter has changed" (which Huff has, in fact, falsified at the level of the received claim) but preserves a more modest and defensible Muslim position — the *rasm* is remarkably stable and the variation is bounded. Huff's actual target is the *maximal* preservation claim (Qazi's), and against that maximal claim the manuscripts are decisive. Compare the complementary point in [[Quranic Textual History Is Cleaner Than the Bible's]] (variants existed and were suppressed/destroyed) and the classical Muslim debate in [[The Tahrif Debate Among Classical Muslim Scholars]].

## LDS Relevance
Latter-day Saints generally welcome this evidence: it deflates a polemical contrast Muslims sometimes draw against *both* the Bible and the Book of Mormon ("your scriptures have textual histories; ours is pristine"). It also models a healthy posture for LDS apologetics — owning that *all* hand-transmitted scripture has a textual history (including LDS scripture, e.g. Book of Mormon printer's-manuscript variants and D&C revisions) rather than claiming miraculous immunity. The honest comparative frame Huff uses — judge every text by the *same* methodology — is one Latter-day Saints can adopt with integrity. See [[The Quran Is a Controlled Transmission and the New Testament Is a Free Transmission]].

## Best Quotes
> "It is a miracle of the Quran that no change has occurred in a single word or a single letter of the alphabet, a single punctuation mark or a single diacritical mark... With all due respect to my Muslim friends, what Qazi is saying here is just incorrect." — [[Wes Huff]] [^1]

> "There are actually two different texts of the Quran floating around the world today. 97% of the Muslim world is reading a Hafs Quran... but I have been to areas of North Africa where the Warsh Quran is being read, and it differs." — [[Wes Huff]] [^2]

> "It says 'whether you die or are martyred, all of you will **not** be gathered before Allah.' So there's a difference of being gathered or not being gathered — that's a significant variant." — [[Wes Huff]], on Paris MS 328 [^2]
