# Orthopraxy over Orthodoxy

## The Claim
The "you're not even Christian because of your view of the Godhead" accusation overvalues **orthodoxy** (right belief) at the expense of **orthopraxy** (right action). Throughout the gospels Christ condemns wrong *acting*, not wrong *believing*.

## Supporting Evidence
- The Good Samaritan: the priest and temple worker had correct belief but passed by the wounded man — they had orthodoxy without orthopraxy *(scriptural argument)* [^1].
- "God, Jesus Christ throughout scripture never condemned anybody for believing wrong. He condemned them for acting wrong, lacking faith, lacking charity, lacking patience." *(assertion, scripturally grounded)* [^2].
- Latter-day Saints do not deny others the name "Christian" — "Do you believe in Jesus Christ? Then you're a Christian"; the exclusion runs only one direction [^3].

## Best Quote
> "God would rather have a million heretical believers out there... comforting those that are in need of comfort and mourning with those that mourn, than a million believers who know he doesn't have a tail and aren't comforting those." [^2]

## Counter-arguments / related
- Balancing point in the episode: right belief about the Godhead still matters because it shapes self-understanding (son/daughter of God vs. creation) and "often inspires right action" [^4].
