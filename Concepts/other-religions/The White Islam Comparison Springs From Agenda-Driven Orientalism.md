# The White Islam Comparison Springs From Agenda-Driven Orientalism

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)

The "Mormonism is American Islam / white Islam" label is often presented as a neutral, even scholarly, comparative-religion observation. All Those In Favor argues the opposite: the comparison originated as Protestant polemic in Joseph Smith's own lifetime and was later dressed in academic clothing by orientalists whose underlying motives were "very questionable" — so its scholarly pedigree is an illusion concealing an agenda of criticism.[^1]

## The Rebuttal

1. **It began as a turning of the tables by early American Protestants.** Equating Joseph with Muhammad "emerged during Joseph Smith's own lifetime, often serving as the polemical tactic by early Protestants." Having themselves been likened to heretics by Catholics during the Reformation, they "turned the tables on the Mormons by linking Joseph Smith to Muhammad, a figure often presumed to be a fraud and a heretic in Protestant culture." *(Strong — the trope is well attested; see [[Joseph-Muhammad Comparison as Islamophobic Smear]].)*[^1]

2. **The "scientific" orientalists imported a Hegelian template.** [[Eduard Meyer]], "a German Hegelian historian, believed that similar societal conditions could produce comparable religious movements" — that every age "reincarnates the same stories" — and so "thought that Mormonism was just the reincarnation of Islam," attempting "to jam his research into that interpretation rather than letting the data speak for itself." He even proposed studying Mormonism as a *proxy* for an "underdocumented" Islam. *(Strong on Meyer's Hegelian frame and proxy proposal; the channel's read that he forced the data is a fair but interpretive judgment.)*[^2]

3. **The clergyman-sociologist used it to brand both as heresies.** [[Hans Thimme]], "an amateur sociologist and Protestant clergyman, despite claiming that he was impartial, ultimately used this comparison to argue that both faiths were Christian heresies because they supposedly undervalue human sinfulness and divine sovereignty" and "lack this message of the cross." The presenter notes the comedy of the charge — "He thought that we didn't think we were sinful enough." *(Moderate–strong — a documented example of impartial-sounding work doing polemical work.)*[^3]

4. **The pattern is criticism wearing the mask of inquiry.** "While they presented their work with the illusion of scientific detachment or comparative religion, their underlying motives were very questionable... Such approaches reveal an agenda of criticism rather than actually just studying what the religions believe." *(Moderate — a claim about motive; the *function* of the works as polemic is the firmer point.)*[^1]

5. **Steelman.** Not every Meyer-style comparison is malicious: comparative religion legitimately notes structural similarities (charismatic founder, new scripture, rapid expansion), and a sociologist *can* study one movement to illuminate another without smearing it. The channel's own position elsewhere allows respectful comparison (see [[Holy Envy - What Latter-day Saints Can Learn from Islam]]). The diagnostic is whether the comparison is deployed to *understand* or to *discredit by association* — and whether it lets the data speak or forces it into a prior schema (Hegelian reincarnation, "both are heresies"). On that test, the historical "white Islam" literature fails.
