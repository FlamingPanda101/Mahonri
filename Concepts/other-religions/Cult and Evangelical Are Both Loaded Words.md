# Cult and Evangelical Are Both Loaded Words

## The Claim
[[Kyle Beshears]] draws a parallel: the words "cult" and "evangelical" have each drifted so far from their original descriptive meaning that they now "conjure in [people's] mind things automatically," making them nearly useless as analytic categories. [^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **"Evangelical" began as description, not prescription.** It named "a branch of Protestantism that is very porous among the denominations and holds four theological positions" — so a Methodist, Presbyterian, or Baptist can all be "theologically evangelical." [^2] *Rating: accurate;* this is essentially the Bebbington-quadrilateral usage.

2. **But it has become a political term since the 1980s.** "When you talk to 99% of people and you say the term evangelical, it has become inherently a political term... I don't think it's possible to retrieve the original intent behind that word. The same thing's happening with the word cult." [^3] *Rating: a widely-shared sociological observation.*

3. **"Cult" has the same drift.** Academically *cultus* = "a specific group of people behaving religiously in a specific way"; culturally it = "the Jim Jones's... the Marshall Applewhites." [^4] *Rating: sound.* The technical meaning survives only in the academy.

4. **Rigidity backfires.** Beshears and host [[Steven Pynakker]] note that insisting on rigid definitions ("Steve Pynakker is not evangelical, let me tell you why") turns living people into caricatures; "these are not useful terms anymore." [^5] *Rating: a pragmatic point about communication, not a denial that definitions can be made.*

**Steelman of the opposing view.** A scholar of new religious movements might insist the academic definition of "cult" is worth defending precisely because abandoning it concedes the field to sensationalism; and some evangelicals would argue "evangelical" still has doctrinal teeth worth fighting for. Beshears's reply is not that the words *can't* be defined but that, in practice with a lay audience, the connotations swamp the definitions.

## Best Quotes
> "I think there are parallels in the terms cult and evangelical in the sense that when people hear those words, they conjure in their mind things automatically." [^1]

> "When you talk to 99% of people and you say the term evangelical, it has become inherently a political term since the 1980s. And at this point I don't think it's possible to retrieve the original intent behind that word. The same thing's happening with the word cult." [^3]

> "In an academic or scholarly setting... the term cult comes from the Latin cultus and it means a specific group of people behaving religiously in a specific way... [but] the word cult as far as our culture is concerned are the Jim Jones's." [^4]
