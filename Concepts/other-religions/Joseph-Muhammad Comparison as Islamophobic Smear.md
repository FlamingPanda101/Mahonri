# Joseph-Muhammad Comparison as Islamophobic Smear

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
When critics liken Joseph Smith to Muhammad, the rhetorical move is rarely a neutral comparative-religion observation. Historically it has functioned as **guilt by association**: Muhammad is presumed already discredited, so attaching Joseph to him "takes care of" Joseph too. Peterson identifies this as carrying anti-Islamic "cultural baggage."[^1]

## The Rebuttal
1. **The tactic is documented in 19th-century anti-Mormon literature.** "People in the 19th century would routinely compare Joseph or Brigham to Muhammad. Polygamy made it easy, because Muslims were famously polygamous."[^1] *(Strong — the trope is well attested.)* Peterson cites period book titles such as *Mormonism: The Islam of America* and "the Mormon prophet and his harem" (harem being, he notes, a genuine Arabic word).[^2] *(Strong — such titles and framings are real artifacts of the era.)*
2. **The logic is explicitly transitive smear, not argument.** "The idea was that if you associate Joseph with Muhammad, Muhammad's already discredited. So connect Joseph with Muhammad, you've taken care of him as well."[^1] This is an ad hominem by association: it presupposes Muhammad's illegitimacy rather than establishing Joseph's.
3. **It trades on present-day Islamophobia too.** The host adds that Islam is now "culturally associated with violence and terrorism," so people assume Muhammad and the Quran are "guilty by association," importing that negative valence onto Joseph.[^3] Peterson's separate treatment of Islamic violence ([[Islamic Violence Is Mostly Political Not Quranic]]) undercuts the premise.
4. **Independent corroboration (All Those In Favor).** A separate LDS treatment makes the same diagnosis and traces the trope to early American Protestants who, having been likened to heretics by Catholics, "turned the tables on the Mormons by linking Joseph Smith to Muhammad, a figure often presumed to be a fraud and a heretic in Protestant culture" — and adds that the comparison now also trades on Islam's modern association with violence. See [[The White Islam Comparison Springs From Agenda-Driven Orientalism]]. *(Strong — converging witness to the same well-attested polemical history.)*[^4]
5. **Steelman.** Not every comparison is a smear — Peterson himself draws careful, respectful comparisons (the [[The Quran Parallels the Doctrine and Covenants Not the Book of Mormon|Quran/D&C analogy]]). The charge of Islamophobia applies specifically where the comparison's *purpose* is to discredit by associating with a despised group, not where it is genuinely analytical. The diagnostic is whether the arguer relies on Muhammad being "already discredited" rather than arguing the point.
