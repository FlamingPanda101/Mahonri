# Love of Neighbor Requires Understanding

## The Claim
[[Kyle Beshears]] grounds the entire project of studying Mormonism not in apologetic strategy but in the greatest commandment. The "why" of engaging Mormonism, he argues, must be "to love the Lord your God with all of your heart, soul, mind, and strength and to love your neighbor as yourself" ([[Matthew 22.37-39|Matt 22:37-39]]; cf. [[Mark 12.30-31|Mark 12:30-31]], [[Leviticus 19.18|Lev 19:18]]). [^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The argument's logical core.** "How can evangelicals be obedient to that commandment to love our neighbor as our self if we don't understand our neighbor... how can we love Latter-day Saints if what we believe about Latter-day Saints is a caricature?" [^2] *Rating: a tight syllogism* — love requires knowledge of the actual person, so caricature is a failure of love, not merely of accuracy.

2. **Fear inherited from family.** Beshears names the inherited "have nothing to do with the Mormons" vibe and asks, "how can I love a people that I have instinctual fear towards?" [^3] *Rating: pastorally pointed;* it diagnoses the affective barrier, not just the informational one.

3. **The chapter is deliberately pastoral.** "I am a pastor by profession. It's really hard to take that hat off... it does have kind of a devotional tone to it." [^4] *Rating: contextual* — the appeal is framed as discipleship for evangelicals, not PR for Mormonism.

**Steelman of the opposing view.** A countercult evangelical could answer that love of neighbor sometimes *requires* warning — that the most loving thing is to tell someone they are in error about salvation, even bluntly. Beshears would not deny the duty of candor (see [[Holding the Tension Between Anti-Mormonism and Syncretism]]); his point is that candor built on a caricature is not love but slander, and that understanding must come first.

## Best Quotes
> "The why has to be the greatest commandment that Christ gives in the gospels, which is to love the Lord your God with all of your heart, soul, mind, and strength and to love your neighbor as yourself." [^1]

> "How can evangelicals be obedient to that commandment to love our neighbor as our self if we don't understand our neighbor? And in this case, how can we love Latter-day Saints if what we believe about Latter-day Saints is a caricature?" [^2]

> "How can I love a people that I have instinctual fear towards?" [^3]
