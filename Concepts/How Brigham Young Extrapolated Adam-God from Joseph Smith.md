# How Brigham Young Extrapolated Adam-God from Joseph Smith

## The Claim
[[Brigham Young]] repeatedly said he learned Adam-God from [[Joseph Smith]]. Critics find this implausible, since Joseph never plainly taught it. [[Chris Blyth]] and Brad Whitfield supply a **plausible genealogy**: Joseph taught two principles — apotheosis/infinite regression (the King Follett Discourse) **and** an elevated Adam ("the **ancient of days** is Adam," "**Michael** the archangel," "the top dog"). Brigham, plus a personal 1847 vision, **extrapolated** the rest. So whether or not the doctrine is true, Brigham's claim to have gotten the seed from Joseph is historically credible — he did not invent it from nothing [^1].

## Supporting Evidence (steelman)

1. **Joseph identified the ancient of days as Adam — and that figure was traditionally God the Father (strong — primary genealogy).** Whitfield: "[[Helen Mar Kimball]] actually tells us where it comes from... Joseph Smith identifies the ancient of days as Adam. And traditionally the ancient of days... was understood to be God the Father." So Brigham's slide from "Adam = ancient of days" to "Adam = God the Father" is "not all that difficult" [^2]. Blyth adds that **[[Daniel 7.13-14|Daniel 7]]** is "one of the best proof texts in the world to say God is a physical being" — the ancient of days sits on a throne and judges — and Joseph said "this being is Adam," which led "some early Latter-day Saints [to] walk away thinking Adam is more exalted than Jesus" [^3].

2. **[[Charles B. Thompson]] independently extrapolated the same move (strong — control case).** Thompson — first author of a book on the Book of Mormon, later founder of his own church — taught a ranked series of estates with **Adam as the highest rank**, "with no connection to Brigham Young," apparently from "Joseph's interpretation of [the] ancient of days." Blyth: "it's very possible Brigham's doing a really similar thing" — an independent example of the same misunderstanding arising from the same source [^4].

3. **Joseph called Adam "Michael," not "a" archangel (supporting).** "For Joseph Smith there are no other archangels. This is the top dog." Joseph elevated Adam's status "in a crazy way"; even [[Joseph Fielding Smith]] later salvaged this as "Adam is the general... under Jesus," preserving a chain of command without making Adam supreme [^5].

4. **The 1847 Winter Quarters vision supplied the sealing framework (strong — biographical origin).** On February 16, 1847, during a two-day conference on the **law of adoption**, Brigham fell sick, said he "dies," and had a near-death vision of Joseph. Asked how to sort out the sealings, Joseph told him "get the Holy Ghost and everything's going to sort itself." Brigham then "sees a vision of all of mankind sealed together from the pre-existence," with **Adam at the head** of that family — "the purpose of sealing is to get us all sealed back to Adam." Blyth: "that's what makes most sense to me — that some of this is revelation... and some of it is extrapolation from things he learned from Joseph Smith" [^1].

### Counterpoint within the dialogue (steelman of the critic)
"Carden" presses the weakness: it is "one thing to say this is the top dog of the archangels... another to say he's top dog of everything," and Joseph teaching an *exalted* Adam is "so far from" full Adam-God that "it's hard... to believe Brigham" got it straight from him [^6]. The genealogy makes Brigham's claim *intelligible*, not *correct* — it is reconstructive apologetics, and the panel concedes Joseph "doesn't explain all these things," leaving room for misreading.
