# Brain-Mind Correlation Is Not Causation - Mental States Have Causal Power

## The Point
Even granting everything the neuroscience shows, Craig argues the most it establishes is a *correlation* between brain states and mental states.[^1] A correlation does not prove that there are no mental states, nor that mental states are causally inert — that they have "no causal connection or impact upon brain states."[^1] The materialist quietly upgrades "the brain correlates with the mind" into "the brain *is* the mind / the brain alone does the causing," but that stronger claim does not follow from the data. The mind can use the brain as an instrument while genuinely causing things.[^2]

## Evidence
1. **The ceiling of the evidence.** "At the most, what these experiments show would be that there are correlations between brain states and mental states."[^1]
2. **What correlation does not license.** That correlation "doesn't mean that there are no mental states, or that mental states have no causal connection or impact upon brain states."[^1] Top-down mental causation remains live.
3. **Positive reason to posit mental states.** Craig's argument from intentionality "shows that there have to be these mental states," and they are "the properties of a self, a soul, a mind."[^2] (See [[Intentionality Requires a Mind Distinct from the Brain]].)
4. **The brain as instrument, not identity.** A mind can be "distinct from the brain even if it uses the brain as an instrument for thought."[^3] Using a tool is not the same as being the tool — so brain involvement in every thought does not reduce thought to the brain.

## Best Quotes
> "At the most, what these experiments show would be that there are correlations between brain states and mental states. But that doesn't mean that there are no mental states, or that mental states have no causal connection or impact upon brain states." [^1]

> "You've got to have a mind that is distinct from the brain, even if it uses the brain as an instrument for thought." [^3]

## LDS Connection
The reductive move Craig blocks — collapsing mind into brain — is the same move that, taken to its end, dissolves the moral agent (see [[Moral Responsibility Presupposes Genuine Agency]]). Latter-day Saint anthropology insists the person is a spirit indwelling and acting through a physical body: "the spirit and the body are the soul of man" (D&C 88:15), and that spirit is an eternal intelligence, not a byproduct of neural tissue (D&C 93:29). On this view a tight body-mind correlation is exactly expected — the spirit operates *through* the body — without conceding that the body alone does the choosing. The materialist's leap from correlation to identity is also a cousin of the self-refuting reductions catalogued in [[Self-Defeating Statements Refute Materialism]].
