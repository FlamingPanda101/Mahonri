# Free Speech as the Corrective to Dogmatism

## The Point

[[Jordan Peterson]] and [[Sam Harris]] agree that free, truthful discourse is the mechanism that corrects dogmatic and totalitarian error, and that a healthy system of governance must place *the process of correcting errors* above any particular dogma. For Peterson this becomes the bridge to his theology of the "Word."

## Argument (steelman)

1. **Dogmatism's defining flaw is unrevisability.** Because dogma "doesn't allow us to revise our bad ideas in real time through conversation," it must be enforced by force. See [[The Problem Is Dogmatism Not Religion]].[^1]

2. **Free discourse is the error-correction mechanism.** Peterson: "I think of free discourse... as the mechanism that corrects totalitarian excess or dogmatic excess." Systems of governance "have to elevate the process by which dogmatic errors are corrected over the dogmas themselves."[^2]

3. **The First Amendment placed correctly.** "The process of free speech is the process by which dogmatic errors are rectified, and so it has to be put at the pinnacle of the hierarchy of values." Harris: "I think you and I totally agree about the primacy of free speech."[^3]

4. **The update mechanism must outrank dogma.** Returning to it later, Peterson: "the process by which your knowledge is updated has to occupy a position in the hierarchy of values that supersedes your reliance on dogma" — which he says is the fundamental claim of the most profound texts.[^4]

5. **The theological turn.** Peterson identifies this corrective truthful speech with the Christian "Word" — redemption found in truthful speech, embodied — and asks why that is not the same claim he and Harris just agreed on. See [[God as the Highest Value and Truthful Speech]].[^5]

## Best Quotes

> "The process of free speech is the process by which dogmatic errors are rectified, and so it has to be put at the pinnacle of the hierarchy of values."[^3]

> "The process by which your knowledge is updated has to occupy a position in the hierarchy of values that supersedes your reliance on dogma."[^4]
