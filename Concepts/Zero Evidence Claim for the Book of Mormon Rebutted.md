# Zero Evidence Claim for the Book of Mormon Rebutted

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
The most common objection [[Alex Arnold]] reports from his comment section is that "there is absolutely zero evidence for the Book of Mormon," deployed against [[Latter-day Saints]] as if it ends the conversation [^1]. Arnold's counter-strategy is methodological: he answers "not with a testimony, but with archaeology, manuscript scholarship, linguistics, and history," and he deliberately frames the burden of proof so that the *strength of the sources* — not LDS authority — carries the argument [^1].

## The Rebuttal
1. **The sources are mostly non-LDS and have no incentive to favor the church.** Arnold cites [[Emanuel Tov]] (Orthodox Jew, editor of the Dead Sea Scrolls Publication Project), [[Frank Moore Cross]] (Harvard), [[Margaret Barker]] (British Methodist), and [[Bart Ehrman]] (agnostic). He flags [[Matt Roper]] as the lone LDS scholar. *Rating: strong rhetorically — using hostile/neutral witnesses neutralizes the "apologists special-pleading" reply.* [^2]
2. **"Zero evidence" is an empirical claim that can be falsified by a single counterexample.** Arnold's move is that the skeptic need not be *convinced*, only honest: "even if you reject all of my theological conclusions, you can't make that claim honestly anymore." This reframes the debate from "is the church true?" to "does *any* evidence exist?" — a far lower and more defensible bar. *Rating: logically sound as stated.* [^4]
3. **He concedes scope limits up front.** Arnold states this is "not an exhaustive list" and that the eliminations he cites came "from outside, not from within" LDS scholarship — pre-empting the charge of cherry-picking [^4][^8].
4. **Steelman of the skeptic.** "Zero evidence" is usually shorthand for "no *dispositive, mainstream-accepted* evidence (e.g., no confirmed Nephite cities, no Reformed Egyptian corpus, no plates)." On that stricter reading the slogan is defensible: the items Arnold lists are *consistency arguments* and *probabilistic* (stylometry, chiasmus odds, a consonantal place-name) rather than direct archaeological confirmation of the narrative. Arnold answers the loose reading ("literally nothing"), which is the weaker form actually used in comment sections, but does not fully engage the stronger form. The distinction between "evidence exists" and "the case is proven" is one he himself concedes at the end ("we don't claim that the evidence proves the church") [^6].

## Best Quotes
> "One of the most common things that I hear, especially in my comment section, is that there is absolutely zero evidence for the Book of Mormon. And this gets thrown at LDS members as if it legitimately ends the conversation." [^1]

> "If your rebuttal requires these entire fields of scholarship to be wrong, then you're not making an evidential argument." [^2]

> "Zero evidence is the claim that gets made constantly by people who haven't actually looked. So, even if you reject all of my theological conclusions, you can't make that claim honestly anymore." [^4]

> "We don't claim that the evidence proves the church. Conversion is a personal decision that goes far beyond any archaeological record... the archaeological record is there. It's real and it still matters." [^6]
