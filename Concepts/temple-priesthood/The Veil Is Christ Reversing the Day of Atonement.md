# The Veil Is Christ Reversing the Day of Atonement

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Andrea Woodmansee]] argues the central meaning of the temple **veil** is a *reversal* pivoting on Christ. The ancient veil (and the high priest's matching robe) was woven of colors representing the material elements — air, water, fire, earth — so "the veil is mortality, the material world that is keeping us from God." Before Christ, the movement through the veil ran *downward*: God condescending, and at the incarnation **Christ passed through the veil of the elements** to take on mortal flesh, descending from the heavenly to the earthly realm. After the cross this reverses: now **mortals ascend** through a *heavenly* veil, taking on the characteristics of the celestial realm — which is why "modern temples should not be exact copies of ancient temples," and why the rent veil signals not the end of temples (the Protestant reading) but "time to get a new veil." [[Hebrews 10.19-22|Hebrews 10:19-20]] makes Christ himself the veil ("through the veil, that is to say, his flesh").[^1][^2]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The veil's colors are the material world.** The ancient veil and high-priestly robe shared the blue/purple/crimson elements-symbolism; Woodmansee reads them as air/water/fire/earth — "the colors of the temple represent the material world... essentially the veil is mortality." See [[The High Priest Wore the Veil - The Garment Is the Material World]]. *Rating: the elements-symbolism of the veil colors is attested in Josephus and Philo; the "veil = mortality" gloss is the interpretive frame.*[^1]
2. **Christ descended *through* the veil (incarnation).** "Which direction was he going through the veil? ...from the heavenly realm to the earthly. He was passing through a veil that represented the elements of the earth — he was taking on him mortal flesh." Some apocryphal writers, she notes, describe Christ's condescension this way. *Rating: the directional reading is a creative typology; the apocryphal support is gestured at, not cited specifically.*[^3]
3. **Yom Kippur foreshadows the reversal.** On the Day of Atonement the high priest removed his colored robe and put on **white linen** to enter the holy of holies — moving (for one day) from the material into the celestial. After Christ, *everyone* redeemed crosses, not one priest on one day. *Rating: the white-linen Yom Kippur vestment is biblical (Leviticus 16:4); the "reversal" application is the inference.*[^4]
4. **Christ is the veil (Hebrews 10).** [[Hebrews 10.19-22|Hebrews 10:19-20]] — boldness to enter "through the veil, that is to say, his flesh" — is read to mean the only remaining separation from God *is* Christ, who, having "the keys of death and hell" ([[Revelation 1.8|Revelation 1:18]]), is "no separation at all" but the way. Coheres with [[The Garment Is the Covering of Christ]]. *Rating: Hebrews does explicitly call Christ's flesh the veil — the strongest scriptural leg.*[^5]
5. **It explains why modern temple ritual differs.** Because the reversal happened at the cross, the modern ordinance rightly differs from the first temple's (ascending, not descending; celestial, not material veil), answering the "your temple isn't identical to Solomon's" objection. See [[Restoration Is Adaptation and Accommodation Not a One-to-One Copy]]. *Rating: a coherent apologetic use; presupposes the whole reversal frame.*[^6]

**Steelman of the skeptical counter.** Hebrews 10:20 does call Christ's flesh "the veil," but the epistle's own argument is that his single sacrifice **opened the way once for all and ended the need for a repeated, gated holy of holies** — i.e. it points *away* from an ongoing ritual veil, which is precisely the Protestant reading the claim resists. The elaborate "downward then upward" reversal, the elements-symbolism, and the apocryphal "Christ descended through the veil" motif are extra-biblical overlays; the torn temple veil (Matthew 27:51) most plainly signifies *access opened*, not "install a new veil." **A Latter-day Saint reply:** Hebrews simultaneously says the way is opened *and* exhorts readers to "draw near" and "enter into the holiest," language of ongoing approach; the LDS claim is that the *mode* of approach is transformed (ascending through Christ) rather than abolished, which fits a temple-continuing reading and coheres with the vault's broader [[The First Temple Contained the Endowment Pattern Barker Reconstructed Independently|first-temple]] case — held as typology, not proof.

## Best Quotes

> "The rending of the veil after Christ's resurrection doesn't, as the Protestant world says, mean we don't need temples anymore — it's like, no, you need a *new* veil. It's time to get a new veil... now we need a celestial veil." — [[Andrea Woodmansee]][^2]

> "Who is the veil? It's Christ... the only thing that separates us from God is Christ, and guess how much of a separation he is? None." — [[Andrea Woodmansee]] (with hosts)[^5]
