# The Forbidden Oath Is the Dark Mirror of the Covenant Oath

#type/claim

## The Claim
Reading the [[The Sermon on the Mount Is an Ordinance in Three Rooms|Sermon on the Mount as an ordinance]], [[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)|David Butler]] makes two moves about its oath material. First, the **"adversary" of Matthew 5:25** ("agree with thine adversary quickly") is, in Greek, the *antidikos* — the **plaintiff/prosecutor in a lawsuit** — which is precisely what the Hebrew *satan* denotes. Butler reads this as ritual "stage directions": a priest **personates Satan/the accuser** in the ordinance, and the initiate is warned to "settle the debt with the adversary" so he gains no legal claim — "Satan appears, there's a warning, presumably then he goes away because we change subject." [^1] Second, the oath instruction of **Matthew 5:33-37** ("swear not at all; neither by heaven . . . by the earth . . . by Jerusalem . . . by thy head" — just say yes or no) teaches the initiate the *simple* covenant oath. Its **dark mirror** is the Jaredite secret-combination oath of [[Ether 8.13-18|Ether 8]], where Akish's conspirators swear "by the God of heaven, and also by the heavens, and also by the earth, and by their heads" — the *forbidden list exactly*, minus Jerusalem (which New-World conspirators would not invoke). [^2] This instances the broader thesis that the Book of Mormon's righteous, initiatic "Visionary men" and the Gadianton/Jaredite robbers are mirror conspiracies — "a conspiracy for good and . . . a conspiracy for evil." [^3]

## Supporting Evidence (steelman)

1. **The lexical identification is solid.** *Antidikos* is straightforwardly a legal-adversary term, and the Hebrew *satan* is indeed "accuser/prosecutor" (Job 1–2; Zech 3). Mapping Matthew 5:25's "adversary" onto the courtroom-Satan figure is philologically clean, independent of the ritual overlay. *Rating: strong on the words themselves.*

2. **The oath-list overlap is striking and specific.** Matthew forbids swearing by heaven, earth, Jerusalem, and one's head; Ether 8's oath swears by the heavens, the earth, and the head — three of the four, dropping only the geographically-impossible Jerusalem. A near-complete match between a *forbidden* formula and a *villains'* formula is exactly what a "righteous rite vs. its counterfeit" model predicts. *Rating: a genuinely arresting parallel.*

3. **The mirror-conspiracy frame is a recognized Book-of-Mormon structure.** That the book pits a covenant in-group against a secret-combination anti-group is a mainstream reading of the Gadianton material; Butler's contribution is to tie the *oath-form* to that mirror, which the Ether/Matthew overlap concretely supports. *Rating: coheres with established structure.*

**Steelman of the skeptical counter.** Swearing "by heaven, by earth, by one's head" is *conventional* ancient oath language (it is precisely the everyday swearing Matthew is curbing), so its appearance in Ether 8 needs no esoteric mirror — both texts simply reflect how oaths were sworn, and a KJV-formed author would naturally echo Matthew's list when writing a wicked oath. The priest-personating-Satan claim is unfalsifiable staging read into a verse that, plainly, is moral counsel to settle disputes before they reach court. A Latter-day Saint reply: granted the oath-vocabulary is conventional, the *pointed inversion* — the righteous taught to renounce exactly the formula the robbers embrace, in a book that elsewhere pairs covenant-keeping with the [[The Salt-Savor Covenant Penalty Enacted by Korihor and the Title of Liberty|salt-savor penalty]] — reads more economically as a deliberate good-vs-counterfeit design than as accidental KJV residue; the *antidikos*/Satan identification, at least, stands on the Greek regardless of whether one accepts the staging.
