# The True Order of Prayer Is the Heartbeat of the Endowment

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Todd McLauchlin]] argues that the *core* of the temple endowment is not the covenants (most of which one has already made elsewhere) but **learning to engage in the true order of prayer** — "the heartbeat of the endowment," because "it's the true order of prayer that puts you into possession of the knowledge and the mystery to overcome all things and all things pertaining to salvation." [^1] The pattern, he says, is: receive an *initiator* (washing/anointing), be *taught* a cosmic perspective, and be given "the keys of prayer" — which is what [[Joseph Smith]] most often meant by "the keys of the kingdom." [^2] The decisive mechanism is that in the true order one is **taught what to pray**: as with the Nephite disciples in [[3 Nephi 19|3 Nephi 19:24]] ("it was given unto them what they should pray"), the worshipper receives the word from the Father, gives voice to it, and is thereby unified and sanctified — "that which is above is being drawn down and they are giving space and their word to it." [^3] This is why the framing question of the whole conversation — how do a handful of Latter-day Saints "steer the ship" of a chaotic world ([[D&C 123|D&C 123]]'s "very small helm")? — is answered inwardly first: "if you want to heal the world, look at your own darkness and shadow first," chasing darkness through the true order ([[D&C 50|D&C 50:23-29]], [[D&C 88|D&C 88:67-68]]). [^4] The effect is **concentric**, radiating from spirit → body → marriage → family → ward → nation. [^5]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **The "bodies full of light" text is plural and concentric, as claimed.** [[D&C 88|D&C 88:67-68]] does say "your whole *bodies* shall be filled with light" (plural), inviting the spirit/flesh/marriage/community reading McLauchlin draws; and v. 68 ("sanctify yourselves that your minds may become single to God... you shall see him") genuinely links purification to theophany. The reading tracks the text's own wording. *Rating: well-anchored in the verse.* [^4]
2. **[[3 Nephi 19|3 Nephi 19]] is a clean scriptural model of "being taught what to pray."** The disciples "did not multiply many words, for it was given unto them what they should pray," and are then encircled in light "even as Jesus" (vv. 24-30) — a canonical depiction of guided, sanctifying prayer that fits the thesis without strain. *Rating: strong proof-text for the mechanism.* [^3]
3. **The "keys of prayer" gloss has a Joseph-era basis.** Joseph Smith did at times speak of revelatory "keys" tied to prayer and asking of God (e.g. the [[D&C 8]]/Oliver pattern, and his teachings on the second comforter), so reading "keys of the kingdom" through prayer is not invented from nothing, even if it is selective. *Rating: defensible but partial — not Joseph's only use of "keys."* [^2]
4. **It coheres with the vault's endowment frame.** The "learn heaven, don't earn heaven" thesis ([[The Endowment Is Learning Heaven Not Earning Heaven]]) and the Sermon-as-ordinance reading ([[The Sermon on the Mount Is an Ordinance in Three Rooms]], where "after this manner pray ye" is treated as a true-order pattern) both support locating prayer at the endowment's center.

**Steelman of the skeptical counter.** A critic — and a careful Latter-day Saint — will note that "the true order of prayer is *the* heartbeat of the endowment" is **McLauchlin's interpretive ranking, not a Church teaching**: the ceremony has multiple elements, and singling out one as the "core" is a devotional judgment he concedes he could be "dead wrong" about. The chain of proof-texts ([[D&C 123|123]] → [[D&C 88|88]] → [[3 Nephi 19|3 Nephi 19]] → [[D&C 50|50]]) is assembled across very different contexts; [[3 Nephi 19|3 Nephi 19]] depicts a unique post-resurrection theophany, not a temple liturgy, so reading it *as* the temple rite is a typological move. And "chase darkness from yourself and the world will follow" risks an over-internalized politics that can sound like a quietist substitute for actual reform. A measured reply: McLauchlin repeatedly flags his speculation and frames the practice as *additive* to ordinary discipleship, not a replacement; the texts he chains do share a real thread — light increasing unto a vision of God, conditioned on purity and on receiving the word to pray — so the synthesis follows the scriptures' own logic even if the "heartbeat" ranking remains his.

## Best Quotes
> "It's the true order of prayer that puts you into possession of the knowledge and the mystery to overcome all things and all things pertaining to salvation... this is why the true order is the heartbeat of the endowment." [^1]

> "If you want to heal the world, look at your own darkness and shadow first. Face the darkness within you... you must be purified and cleansed from all sin." [^4]

> "They did not multiply many words, for it was given unto them what they should pray... they receive the word and they give their word and that word becomes unified and they become sanctified in that prayer." [^3]
