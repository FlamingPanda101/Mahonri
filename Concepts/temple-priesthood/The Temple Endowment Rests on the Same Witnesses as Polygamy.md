# The Temple Endowment Rests on the Same Witnesses as Polygamy

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Brian Hales]] argues that polygamy deniers face a fatal double standard: the temple **endowment** was never written down in Joseph Smith's lifetime — it was recorded in 1877 by [[Wilford Woodruff]] (under [[Brigham Young]]'s direction) as his *recollection* of Joseph's teachings. That is the *very same* late-recollection source whose polygamy testimony deniers dismiss as deception. You cannot consistently reject Woodruff's late memory of Joseph teaching plural marriage while trusting Woodruff's late memory of Joseph teaching the endowment.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **The endowment is a post-Joseph written record.** Nothing of the endowment was written during Joseph's lifetime; Heber C. Kimball recorded that "these things are not to be written." It was performed for ~5,000 in the Nauvoo Temple (1845–46), rehearsed in the Endowment House (1855–77), and only *committed to writing* in 1877 when a temple existed to house and systematize it — by Wilford Woodruff, recalling Joseph's instructions.[^2] *(Strong — accurate to the documented history of the endowment text.)*
2. **Same source, same recollection-type, as the polygamy testimony.** Woodruff also "recounted later in life things that Joseph had taught him," including that Joseph taught and practiced plural marriage. So both the endowment and the polygamy testimony are *late recollections by the same witness*. To call Woodruff a deceiver on polygamy while crediting him on the temple is to "believe some of what Wilford Woodruff recalled but ignore other things that Wilford Woodruff recalled" — an arbitrary, motivated split.[^3] *(Strong — the methodological symmetry is the whole point.)*
3. **Plural sealings and the endowment are historically parallel.** Hundreds of plural-marriage sealings were performed in the Nauvoo Temple and Endowment House *alongside* the endowment ceremonies — the two are "parallel items in the places where they're manifest." They rise and fall together historically; one cannot surgically remove polygamy and keep the temple.[^4] *(Strong — ties the two practices to the same institutional settings and personnel.)*
4. **The authority problem compounds it.** Because the sealing authority is taught only in [[Doctrine and Covenants 132|D&C 132]] (which deniers must discard — see [[The Implications Cascade of Polygamy Denial]]), rejecting 132 *and* distrusting Woodruff leaves no basis for the temple ordinances at all. "So you kind of have to give up not just eternal families but a belief in the temple."[^5] *(Strong within the LDS frame — the dependency is real.)*

**Steelman of the denialist view.** A denier could argue the cases are not parallel: the endowment was *publicly performed* for thousands in Nauvoo (so its content was corporately witnessed and remembered, not dependent on one man's private memory), whereas Joseph's specific *plural marriages* were secret and rest on later affidavits — so trusting the publicly-rehearsed endowment while doubting privately-claimed sealings is not obviously inconsistent. A denier might also accept the endowment's *substance* as developed by Brigham-era leaders in continuity with Joseph, while still denying Joseph practiced polygamy, treating the two as separable streams. Hales's rejoinder is that the *written text* of the endowment still depended on Woodruff's late recollection of Joseph's words (Kimball: not to be written), so the "publicly performed" point doesn't rescue the *recorded* ceremony from the same late-memory provenance — and that the plural sealings were performed in the *same* temple by the *same* leaders, so the streams are not cleanly separable.

## LDS Relevance
A sharp internal-consistency argument that raises the cost of polygamy denial for temple-believing Latter-day Saints specifically: the endowment they hold sacred and the plural marriage they wish to deny come through the same witnesses, the same Nauvoo/Endowment-House settings, and (for authority) the same revelation. Denial cannot be confined to polygamy without destabilizing the temple. This is pillar (4) of the broader [[The Implications Cascade of Polygamy Denial|implications cascade]].

## Best Quotes
> "If we're going to discount what Wilford Woodruff taught about Joseph Smith as a later recollection about polygamy... I don't think these people can then say, 'but we are going to believe his late recollections about the temple.'" — [[Brian Hales]] [^3]

> "The temple was not written down during Joseph's lifetime. If we are going to limit our belief system to the things that are written down in Joseph's lifetime, then we are lost with the temple, because nothing was written." [^1]

> "Hundreds of plural marriage ceremony sealings [were] performed [in the Nauvoo Temple], as they also did in the Endowment House. So the endowment and the plural marriage sealings are parallel items." [^4]
