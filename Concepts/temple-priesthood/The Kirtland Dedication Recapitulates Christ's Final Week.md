# The Kirtland Dedication Recapitulates Christ's Final Week

#type/claim

## The Claim
Historian [[Don Bradley]] (with host [[Stephen Jones]]) argues that the events surrounding the 1836 Kirtland Temple dedication are a deliberate, day-for-day **recapitulation of Jesus's final week**, and that the meaning is unlocked only by getting the chronology right: "much of history is just getting the events in the right order." [^1] Two Sundays anchor it. **Sunday, March 27, 1836** — when the Saints offered the dedicatory prayer ([[D&C 109]]), shouted hosanna, and waved handkerchiefs — was **Palm Sunday**, the day commemorating Christ's triumphal entry into Jerusalem, where crowds welcomed him with hosannas and palm branches and he went straight to **cleanse the temple**; the Latter-day Saints were likewise welcoming Christ in to cleanse and take possession of his house. [^2] **Sunday, April 3, 1836** — when the risen Christ appeared in the temple ([[D&C 110]]) and accepted the house — was simultaneously **Easter** (the Christian feast of the Resurrection) *and* **Passover** on the Jewish calendar, two reckonings that rarely coincide. [^3] So the resurrected Lord declared his acceptance on the very day that celebrates his resurrection, while **Elijah** appeared on Passover — the feast at which Jews leave an empty seat for the returning Elijah, fulfilling [[Malachi 4.5-6|Malachi]]. [^4] Bradley layers a further New-Testament echo: just as Christ, Moses, and Elias appeared at the **Mount of Transfiguration** ([[Matthew 17.10-13|Matthew 17]]), so Christ, Moses, Elias, and Elijah appeared at Kirtland — and the pattern of mortals being **transfigured to endure divine glory** runs from Moses and the Three Nephites to the First Vision to the [[Informal Witnesses of the Golden Plates|Three Witnesses]]. [^5] The governing thesis: "if we're not understanding these things in connection with Jesus Christ, we're understanding them wrong." [^6]

## Supporting Evidence (steelman)

1. **The calendar coincidence is the load-bearing datum, and it is checkable.** That April 3, 1836 fell on Easter is fixed by the Christian calendar; that the same date aligned with Passover, and that March 27 was therefore Palm Sunday, are astronomical/calendrical facts independent of LDS belief. The Passover identification is not new to Bradley — he credits earlier LDS scholars (he names [[Stephen Smoot|Stephen Ricks]] tentatively) — and the *Easter* overlay is the fresh contribution. The dedication echoing the triumphal-entry-then-cleansing sequence is then a genuine structural parallel, not a vibe. *Rating: the chronology is real; its theological weight is the interpretive step.*

2. **The ritual acts map onto the model with specificity.** Palm Sunday → hosanna shout and handkerchiefs ([[The Hosanna Shout and Handkerchief Reenact the Palm Branches|the palm branches]]); washings and anointings in the dedicatory week → the priestly washings of the biblical temple; the lamb selected in the Passover preparatory period → Christ the Lamb who "dies at Passover so that the others don't have to." Each leg of the parallel attaches to a concrete practice, not a generic resemblance. *Rating: tightly specified, which is harder to dismiss than a single analogy.*

3. **It coheres with the vault's wider case.** The transfiguration-of-witnesses pattern matches [[Seeing Gods Face as Costly Signaling and Offering]] and the Three-Witnesses material; the temple-as-recapitulation-of-Christ logic is the dedication-side complement to [[The Book of Mormon Already Contains the Endowment]] and [[The Sermon on the Mount Is an Ordinance in Three Rooms]]. The Passover-as-type-of-Christ reading is independently ancient (see [[Melito of Sardis Reads the Passover as a Type of Christ]]). *Rating: well-networked, not isolated.*

**Steelman of the skeptical counter.** The decisive objection is **retrofitted symbolism plus selective calendar-stacking**. (a) Joseph Smith *chose* when to dedicate; if a Latter-day Saint already knew the Passover/Palm-Sunday significance, scheduling the dedication for those Sundays makes the "coincidence" a design, not a providential sign — and Bradley concedes the Passover link was known to 19th-century Saints. (b) Easter and Passover near-coincidences are not astronomically rare in any given spring, so finding *some* significance for a spring Sunday is cheap. (c) The transfiguration parallel is loose: "Elias" is a title the New Testament itself applies to multiple figures, so reading the Kirtland "Elias" as a second distinct personage is interpretive. (d) Most fundamentally, that a 19th-century rite *resembles* Christ's final week does not show the resemblance was *intended by God* rather than *constructed by devout participants and later readers* — the same [[Avoiding Parallelomania Between Ancient Rites and LDS Temple|parallelomania]] caution the LDS scholars themselves urge. A Latter-day Saint reply: even granting deliberate scheduling, the *density* of the convergence (two feasts on one acceptance-day, the triumphal-entry liturgy on the matching Sunday, the Elijah-at-Passover fulfillment of Malachi, the named appearances in [[D&C 110]]) is better read as Joseph consciously *patterning* the dedication on Christ's week under revelation than as empty coincidence — which is exactly the point, that the temple is *about* Christ by design, not by accident.

## Best Quotes
> "Much of history is just getting the events in the right order. When we know the chronology of the events, then we can see the relationships between the events." [^1]

> "Not only does Elijah show up on Passover, Jesus Christ shows up on Easter that celebrates his resurrection from the dead... on the Sunday that celebrates His resurrection, Easter Sunday." [^3]

> "What's one week before Easter? It's Palm Sunday... They welcome the Messiah into Jerusalem with shouts of hosanna and waving palm branches, and then he goes straight into his temple to cleanse his temple. That's what the Latter-day Saints, March 27, 1836, Palm Sunday, are doing." [^2]
