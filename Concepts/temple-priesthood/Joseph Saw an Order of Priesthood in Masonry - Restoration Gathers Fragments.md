# Joseph Saw an Order of Priesthood in Masonry - Restoration Gathers Fragments

## The Claim
On [[Stick of Joseph]], two practicing Master Masons who are also endowed Latter-day Saints (Josh Flansberg and Robert Kay) argue that [[Joseph Smith]] joined Masonry partly *to understand what was in his own mind* — and that what he saw there was a degenerate remnant of priesthood. Restoration, on this account, is not creation *ex nihilo* in a vacuum: "a restoration does not happen in a vacuum... Joseph just doesn't go to God and say 'give'... he's being exposed, probably led to being exposed, to varying elements" and then asks questions. The key text is [[Heber C. Kimball]]'s letter to Parley Pratt (June 17, 1842): "There is a similarity of priesthood and Masonry. Brother Joseph says Masonry was taken from priesthood but has become degenerated. But many things are perfect." Joseph gathered preserved fragments and reassembled them in a restored order — branches gathered back into the mother tree.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **(Moderate, primary source)** The Heber C. Kimball–Parley Pratt letter is a genuine contemporary (1842) statement of how at least one senior leader understood the Masonry/priesthood relation — Masonry as *derived from and degenerated out of* an original priesthood, not as the source.[^1] See [[Heber C. Kimball]].
2. **(Moderate, concurrent introduction)** The guests stress that the Nauvoo blue-lodge degrees and the endowment were given *concurrently*, in different buildings but overlapping days, so "one's not replacing the other... one is a school for the other" — a preparatory education the Saints could not get from contemporary Protestantism.[^2]
3. **(Moderate, framing)** The "scattered sparks / gathered branches" metaphor (read into Jacob 5 / Zenos) frames restoration as recovery of dispersed truth from many traditions, consonant with the LDS doctrine of gathering truth wherever found.[^3]

## Opposing View (Steelmanned)
The critic's reply (the CES-Letter line) is that "Masonry was taken from priesthood but degenerated" is a self-serving rationalization: the *plain* historical fact is that Joseph received the Master Mason degree on March 16, 1842, and introduced an endowment full of Masonic signs, tokens, grips, and penalties seven weeks later, so the simplest explanation is borrowing, not restoration. Saying Masonry is the *degenerate descendant* of an ancient priesthood is unfalsifiable — it lets any similarity count as evidence *for* the Restoration. And the "concurrent, not replacing" point cuts the other way: concurrency is exactly what you'd expect if the lodge ritual was the template being adapted in real time.

## Counter-objection
The borrowing charge and the restoration claim are not symmetric once one asks *what* was borrowed. The endowment's recognizable *content* — creation, fall, redemption, ascent through a veil, enthronement — follows an ancient Near Eastern temple pattern that Masonry does not contain and that Joseph could not have known in 1842 ([[Temple Origins Run Deeper Than Freemasonry]], [[The Masonry Objection Must Locate the Specific Content Not the General Pattern]]). Many endowment elements are documented in Joseph's revelations *years before* his initiation ([[Endowment Elements Predate Joseph Smith's Freemasonry]]). And "restoration as adaptation" is not unfalsifiable hand-waving but a stated method: Joseph used a familiar ritual *vocabulary* (signs, tokens) to clothe a recovered *content* ([[Restoration Is Adaptation and Accommodation Not a One-to-One Copy]]). The Kimball letter's value is precisely that it shows this was the *contemporary self-understanding*, not a modern retrofit — the earliest insiders already described Masonry as derivative and degenerate, not as the wellspring.

## LDS Relevance
This is the affirmative LDS framing of the Masonry question: not "we didn't borrow signs" but "the signs are a shared, degenerated ritual language, while the temple's *meaning* is restored." It supplies the contemporary primary source (the Kimball letter) behind the vault's existing borrowing-vs-restoration cluster and the doctrine that the Restoration *gathers* scattered truth — see [[Convergence of Mainstream Christianity Toward LDS Doctrines]].
