# Succession Resolved Through the Keys of the Twelve (Objection Rebutted)

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
[[Joe Heschmeyer]] argues Joseph Smith "leaves three different sets of criteria for who his successors are going to be and they don't match up," yielding the "succession crisis of 1844" (compared to the Sunni-Shia split), and that there were ~60 breakoff groups — evidence that "everything's constantly changing, nothing is pinned down."[^1]

## The Rebuttal

1. **The canon resolves it cleanly via D&C 107 (rated: strong, doctrinal).** [[Jacob Hansen]]: [[D&C 76|Doctrine and Covenants 107]] establishes that the combined voice of the Twelve Apostles "operates at the same level of authority as the First Presidency" — in some respects *higher* than the prophet alone, because a decision must be unanimous. So when the prophet dies, the President of the Quorum of the Twelve ([[Brigham Young]]) and the gathered quorum constitute the leadership. The very source Heschmeyer claims to be searching for — "the highest sources of LDS authority" — answers the question, but "Joe doesn't ever seem to really appeal to" the canon.[^2] See [[Brigham Young Saved the Church Through the Keys]].

2. **Later accounts confirm the transfer of keys (rated: moderate, historical).** Hansen cites accounts of Joseph Smith conferring the keys on the Twelve ("I roll the work off onto your shoulders with the keys and everything") that corroborate the D&C 107 structure.[^3]

3. **The breakoffs are negligible (rated: strong, sociological).** [[Cardon Ellis]] / Hansen note that when Matt Fradd asked about ~60 breakoffs, Heschmeyer himself conceded "the other ones are super tiny and almost irrelevant" — "there really actually is basically just one."[^4]

4. **Catholicism's succession problems dwarf 1844 (rated: strong, comparative).** The panel: Catholicism produced "the 50,000 Christian churches that all trace their lineage to Catholicism," plus "the three competing popes all at the same time" (the Western Schism). A succession crisis is hardly a uniquely Mormon embarrassment.[^5] See [[Catholic Doctrinal Development Is the Same Double Standard]]. *Steelman:* a Catholic distinguishes *schism* (groups leaving an enduring see) from a *genuine ambiguity about who holds legitimate authority*; Rome maintained an unbroken Petrine office through the schism (resolved at Constance), whereas 1844 involved real competing claims (Rigdon, the Twelve, later Strangites, RLDS) with no antecedent office to adjudicate. The LDS reply is that D&C 107 *was* the antecedent rule and the quorum *did* adjudicate — the analogy to the Petrine office holds.

## Best Quotes

> "D&C 107 lays out pretty clearly that the combined voice of the Twelve Apostles operates at the same level of authority as the First Presidency... Joe doesn't ever seem to really appeal to [the canon]."[^2]

> "Talking about the breakoffs of Catholicism — you mean the hundreds and hundreds of Christian religions that have come in the succeeding hundreds of years?"[^5]

> "Matt's like, 'How many breakoffs were there?' And Joe's like, 'Oh, there's like 60.' ... then concedes, well, no, the other ones are super tiny and almost irrelevant."[^4]
