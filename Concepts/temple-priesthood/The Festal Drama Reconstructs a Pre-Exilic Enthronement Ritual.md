# The Festal Drama Reconstructs a Pre-Exilic Enthronement Ritual

## The Claim
[[John Eaton]], in *Festal Drama in Deutero-Isaiah*, reconstructs (in 38 points) a pre-exilic Israelite **autumn festival** — the ancestor of Rosh Hashanah, Yom Kippur, and Sukkot — in which the Davidic king, acting for Yahweh, ritually defeats cosmic chaos, suffers and descends, is redeemed, married to Zion, anointed, and enthroned. The festival's imagery and language, Eaton argues, are the backdrop to Isaiah 40–55.

## Supporting Evidence
1. **Creation by combat, then triumphal procession (rated: Eaton's reconstruction; cult-functional school).** In the drama "Yahweh goes out of the city of Jerusalem and defeats enemy gods… and the creation of the world happens out of the bodies of the dead enemy gods" (he defeats Rahab/Tiamat); then "Yahweh's victorious procession into Zion" along a refurbished *via sacra* / pilgrim road.[^1] The combat-creation parallels Canaanite Baal vs. Yam and Babylonian Marduk vs. Tiamat — and the vault's [[The Temple Reenacts the Primordial Mound Rising from the Waters of Chaos]].
2. **Sacral kingship: the king as the nexus (rated: Eaton).** "The king represents God to the people… but the king represents the people to God." He suffers, "goes down into Sheol and comes out," is "ritually married to his wife," then "anointed" and "enthroned… under the shadow of Yahweh's wings" (the cherubim over the ark in the holy of holies).[^2]
3. **Reconstructed from the Psalms (rated: method).** Eaton reconstructs the drama from the Psalms (his companion volume *Kingship and the Psalms*), using *Sitz im Leben* ("setting in life") and form criticism: the Psalms are "the backstory to the festal drama, and the festal drama is the backstory to Isaiah 40–55."[^3]
4. **Cross-cultural attestation (rated: comparative).** The "going down into the abyss and rising victorious" pattern recurs across the ancient Near East; the hosts note Joseph Campbell's monomyth and Psalm 22 ("they parted my garments") as "the bedrock with which the [passion] text is written."[^4]

**Steelman of the critic.** The festal-drama / divine-kingship reconstruction belongs to the **Scandinavian (Mowinckel) cult-functional school**, which is influential but contested: many scholars doubt that so elaborate an annual enthronement ritual (with a suffering-dying-rising king) can be securely recovered from the Psalms, and warn it imports Babylonian *akitu* patterns onto Israel. Eaton himself concedes it is "a work of imagination," educated guessing about what the texts *did* in performance. The Psalms' royal/enthronement language is agreed; the full ritual drama is a reconstruction.

## Best Quotes

## LDS Connection
The hosts read the festal drama as a lost temple liturgy whose elements — descent, conquest of chaos, ritual marriage, anointing, enthronement as a "sacral king," with Adam and Eve as the first royal pair — survive in fragments in the endowment ("consider yourself as Adam… this is your story"). It supplies the ritual matrix for the Book of Mormon's enthronement and atonement themes; see [[Psalm 110 and Psalm 2 Are Enthronement Texts of the Festal Drama]] and [[The Servant Songs Portray the Suffering King of the Festival]].
