# Teleios Means Initiate - You Will Be Endowed Not Be Flawless

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)|David Butler]] argues that "be ye therefore perfect" ([[Matthew 5.48|Matthew 5:48]]) is, in Greek, **a future indicative — *esesthe*, "you *will* be" — not an imperative**, and that *téleios* there does not mean "morally flawless" but **"ritually complete / initiated / endowed."** Read as the *close* of the ordinance's third chapter, the verse becomes a **change-of-status / new-name** moment: "you will be initiated, even as your Father in heaven is initiate" — squarely in "King Follett land," where the initiate is becoming what God already is. [^1] Butler then reconstructs the underlying Hebrew as *shalem* (from *shalom*) — "complete, whole, healthy, at peace" — and traces a **"peaceable followers of Christ"** thread through the Book of Mormon as the *title bestowed at this point in the ordinance*: in **Moroni 7** Mormon addresses "the peaceable followers of Christ" who have "obtained a sufficient hope" to "enter into the rest of the Lord" — and "the rest of the Lord" is the **holy of holies**, the divine presence one may enter *now*. [^2] He corroborates with **D&C 42:61**, where revelation yields "the mysteries and **peaceable** things — that which bringeth joy, that which bringeth life eternal," pairing *peaceable* with *mysteries* as covenant-path attainments. [^3]

## Supporting Evidence (steelman)

1. **The grammar point is defensible.** Greek *esesthe* is morphologically a **future indicative**, and ancient usage does let a future stand where English expects a command; the resurrected Christ's parallel in 3 Nephi 12:48 ("I would that ye should be perfect") reads as invitation, not bare imperative — consistent with a status conferred rather than a standard imposed. *Rating: a real, citable grammatical observation (a future used as a promise).* 

2. **The *téleios* = "initiate" gloss has lexical warrant.** Beyond "complete/mature," *téleios/teleō* carries cultic senses ("to consecrate, to bring to ritual completion"; *ta teleia* of the mysteries), so "initiate/endowed" is within the word's range, not invented. It also defuses the toxic-perfectionism problem the existing [[Matthew 5.48|Matthew 5:48]] note tracks. *Rating: a legitimate minority sense, well-suited to the ordinance frame.*

3. **The *shalem*/"peaceable" thread is a genuine intratextual pattern.** "Peaceable followers of Christ" + "enter into the rest" (Moroni 7) and "mysteries and peaceable things" (D&C 42:61) really do cluster *shalom*-language with hope, rest, and mysteries — the vocabulary one expects of an endowment-completion title, and it links to [[The Book of Mormon Already Contains the Endowment]]. *Rating: a coherent word-thread across distinct books.*

**Steelman of the skeptical counter.** A future indicative can function as an imperative in Koine (Septuagintal commands routinely use the future — "thou shalt not . . ."), so "*esesthe* is grammatically future" does **not** establish that it is a *promise* rather than a *command*; the traditional "be perfect" rendering is defensible. *Téleios*'s cultic sense is real but secondary; its dominant New-Testament meaning is "mature/complete in character" (cf. James 1:4; 1 Cor 14:20), and Matthew's own context (exceeding the righteousness of the Pharisees) favors moral completeness. The *shalem* back-translation of a verse Matthew preserves only in Greek is speculative, and "enter into the rest" already has a non-temple sense (Heb 3–4: rest = eschatological/sabbath rest). A Latter-day Saint reply: the reading is offered as the *best fit for the verse's position* at an ordinance's hinge, where a new title is expected; the *shalom*/peaceable cluster is doing the real work (it is textual, not conjectural), and even on the cautious view "complete/mature toward a divine goal" already pulls against flawlessness — the endowment gloss sharpens, rather than contradicts, the lexicon (cf. [[The Endowment Is Learning Heaven Not Earning Heaven]]).

## Best Quotes
> "You will be *téleios* . . . a person who is *téleios* is also . . . ritually complete, or he is an initiate — or we would say he is endowed." [^1]

> "Thou shalt know the mysteries and peaceable things — that which bringeth joy, that which bringeth life eternal . . . the things that belong to those who are advancing along this covenant path." [^3]
