# The Square and Compass Are Masculine and Feminine Temple Symbols

#type/claim

## The Claim

[[Tikla]] and [[Andrea Woodmansee]] (the women of [[Ward Radio]]) argue that two paired symbols
present in temple iconography — the **square** and the **circle/compass** — encode the
**masculine** and the **feminine** respectively, and that learning to "read" them recovers a
symbolic literacy modern culture has lost. The square (and the builder's square, the cross,
foundation/cornerstone imagery) carries masculine attributes: stability, uprightness, justice,
boundedness, *presiding*. The circle and its tool the compass carry feminine attributes:
nurture, encircling, compassion, planning, cycles, infinity. They are explicit that this is an
*exploratory* reading — symbols are "multifaceted," not one-to-one — and that the temple itself
never glosses its symbols, leaving them to "a process of discovery and revelation."[^1]

The frame is offered partly as a *defense of the temple*: against the charge that its symbols are
arbitrary Joseph Smith inventions (or merely borrowed Freemasonry), Tikla and Andrea insist these
are "deeply embedded in ancient symbology," predating Joseph Smith, Christ, and even appearing on
Egyptian mummies and ancient Chinese silk — so the right response to anti-temple mockery is to
"shine a light on the beauty and the ancient character of these things."[^2] This complements the
vault's [[Endowment Elements Predate Joseph Smith's Freemasonry|pre-Masonic-origins]] arguments.

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. The square: justice, foundation, the Stone/Cornerstone — rate: moderate (symbolic exegesis)
The square is "a perfect shape" (four corners = four corners of the earth), and the carpenter's
square lays out true right angles. Scriptural word-clusters Andrea/Tikla tie to it: *uphold,
upright, righteous, square, foundation*. They link it to Christ as the **foundation stone /
cornerstone** ([[Matthew 16.18|Matthew 16:18]], "upon this rock I will build my church," spoken at
Caesarea Philippi before a literal rock face and spring) and to the tradition that Christ was a
*stonemason* (a "builder," a defensible rendering of *tektōn*), so a square would be used to lay
courses of stone true — illuminating "the stone which the builders rejected."[^3]

### 2. The compass: nurture, encircling, compassion, cycles — rate: moderate (symbolic exegesis)
The compass draws the circle (also "perfect" and symmetrical) and orients/measures, so it is a
*planning* tool — fitting the feminine role of preparing (e.g., a mother planning nine months for
a child). Word-clusters: *encircling, encompass, compassion* (compass + *passio*, "to suffer
with"). They read Ruth asking Boaz to spread his robe over her, and the [[1 Nephi 11.18|1 Nephi 11]]
Tree of Life — a feminine symbol the angel interprets by showing Mary — as encircling/feminine
imagery, dovetailing with the vault's [[Nephi's Tree Is Asherah the Divine Mother|Tree-as-divine-
mother]] reading.[^4]

### 3. The two are paired in genuinely ancient artifacts — rate: moderate (material culture, popular-level)
A functional square with a plumb-bob was found as an amulet on an Egyptian mummy; the "squared
circle" / overlaid-squares "Seal of Melchizedek" rosette appears on early Christian mosaics
(Ravenna), on fabric from Masada, and on Egyptian mummy robes. Andrea cites [[Hugh Nibley]]'s
*Temple and Cosmos* for a Chinese silk-tomb image of a king holding a **square** and a queen
holding a **compass**, intertwined as serpents amid star-charts, depicting the creation of their
world — masculine and feminine each bearing a tool of cosmic creation.[^5]

### 4. "Squaring the circle" models the male-female dynamic — rate: weak (illustrative)
The unsolvable geometry problem (a square and circle of identical area is impossible because π is
infinite) becomes a parable: the bounded, "embodied/earthly" square and the infinite, "spiritual"
circle can never be made identical, so one must encompass the other — read as a symbol of the
complementary, alternating roles of masculine bounding and feminine nurture.[^6]

## Steelman of the Opposing View

- **This is devotional symbol-reading, not exegesis.** None of the square/compass gender
  assignments are stated in scripture or temple text; they are imaginative associations the
  presenters repeatedly flag as "my theory."
- **The square-and-compass is most famously Masonic.** A critic will note the obvious modern
  referent and argue the ancient parallels are cherry-picked or coincidental — the same objection
  the vault answers at [[The Masonry Objection Must Locate the Specific Content Not the General
  Pattern]].
- **"Christ the stonemason" and several artifact claims are popular-level**, not settled
  scholarship (*tektōn* is usually "carpenter/builder"; the Masada/mummy items are debated).
- **Gendering geometry can smuggle in essentialism.** The presenters themselves caution that
  "masculine/feminine" is not "man/woman," but the slide is easy to make.

**What survives:** the *modest, well-supported* core — that paired square/circle (and overlaid-
square) symbols are genuinely ancient and cross-cultural, predating Joseph Smith — is real and
supports the broader [[Temple Origins Run Deeper Than Freemasonry|deep-origins]] case. The specific
gender symbolism is an edifying overlay, presented as such.
