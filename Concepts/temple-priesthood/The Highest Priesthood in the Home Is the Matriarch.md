# The Highest Priesthood in the Home Is the Matriarch

## The Claim
[[Bruce Porter]] challenges a common Latter-day Saint folk practice: when a father leaves home, telling the 16-year-old son "you're the priesthood holder of the home now." Porter says this is "not necessarily true." Because the **patriarchal** priesthood (held equally by a sealed husband and wife) is *higher* than the **Aaronic** priesthood the teenage son holds, the **matriarch** — the sealed mother — is "the highest priesthood holder in that home," and the church itself "must acquiesce" to the authority of father and mother in their own home, "be they member or non-member."[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The folk practice corrected (rated: the headline claim).** "When a father leaves the home, they often tell their 16-year-old priest, 'You're the priesthood holder of the home now.' That's not necessarily true. The highest priesthood in the home is the matriarch."[^1] The Aaronic-holding son does not outrank a mother who holds the patriarchal priesthood. This depends on [[Joseph Smith Taught Three Priesthoods Including the Patriarchal]].

2. **The church acquiesces to the home (rated: strong; striking ecclesiology).** "The church itself must acquiesce to the authority of the father and the mother in the home — even though we may have a priesthood leader, the bishop or the stake president ... they must bow down to that authority."[^2] Parental authority in the home is, on Porter's reading, the highest practical authority, above the ward hierarchy in that domain.

3. **Applies to member and non-member parents alike (rated: notable corollary).** Because the patriarchal order is grounded in parenthood, the deference applies "be they member or non-member" — the authority of a father and mother over their own children is recognized regardless of church membership.[^2] This reframes priesthood as fundamentally *relational and familial* (cf. [[The Patriarchal Order Is the Order of Fatherhood Not Male Rule]]).

**Steelman of the opposing/cautious view.** Official Church teaching does affirm that a mother presides in the home jointly with the father and that priesthood *authority* is not the measure of a person's worth or spiritual stature; but framing the mother as literally the "highest priesthood holder" rests on Porter's contested premise that women "hold the patriarchal priesthood" as a *priesthood office* (see the steelman in [[Joseph Smith Taught Three Priesthoods Including the Patriarchal]]). A more cautious reading is that the boy holds Aaronic priesthood *office* while the mother exercises the higher *authority and stewardship* of a parent in the patriarchal order — a distinction between office and presiding authority that yields Porter's pastoral point without the stronger "office" claim. The corrective against telling a teenage son he "presides over his mother" is, however, widely echoed by Church leaders and is the soundest takeaway.

## Best Quotes
> "The highest priesthood in the home is the matriarch. ... not the Aaronic priesthood holder, but the matriarch."[^1]

> "The church itself must acquiesce to the authority of the father and the mother of that home, be they member or non-member."[^2]
