# The Day of Atonement
#type/claim

## The Claim
The Day of Atonement (Yom Kippur, Leviticus 16) is the annual high-priestly rite at the heart of the Israelite temple system: the high priest casts lots over **two identical goats**, one "for Yahweh" (slain, its blood carried into the Holy of Holies) and one "for Azazel" (the scapegoat, which has the people's sins confessed over its head and is driven out into the wilderness). In this vault the rite functions as a master template: its two-goat structure, its imputation of sin to an innocent victim, and its priest-identifies-with-sacrifice logic are argued to lie beneath several biblical and Book of Mormon narratives, and its knowledge by Nephi is used to date temple theology before the exile.

## Supporting Evidence
- **The ritual core.** Leviticus 16 pairs a sacrificed Yahweh-goat with an expelled Azazel-goat, chosen by lot — a single sin-removal rite enacted in two complementary movements (blood brought near / sin sent away). This dual structure is what later readings detect elsewhere.
- **The two-goats template in narrative.** The vault applies it to the Passion in John ([[Barabbas and Jesus Are the Two Goats of the Day of Atonement]], where Barabbas is released as the scapegoat and Jesus slain as the Yahweh-goat) and to 1 Nephi 4 ([[Laban and Nephi Enact the Two Goats of the Day of Atonement]], with Laman as the cast-out scapegoat and Nephi/Laban as the white Yahweh-goat). Jacob's temple sermon in [[2 Nephi 9.30-38|2 Nephi 9:30-38]] is likewise read as a Day-of-Atonement speech. The same lots are read behind King Benjamin's "[[The Right and Left Hand of God Are the Day of Atonement Goats|right hand / left hand of God]]" (Mosiah 5; Matthew 25's sheep and goats) and behind the Old Testament [[Two Brothers Where the Younger Is Chosen Are Types of Christ and Lucifer|two-brothers motif]] (one chosen, one cast out as Azazel/Lucifer).
- **A dating argument.** Because Nephi appears to know the Day of Atonement's theology, LDS apologists argue the rite and its temple framework are pre-exilic, not a post-exilic priestly invention (see [[The Day of Atonement Is Pre-Exilic Because Nephi Knows It]]).
- **Steelman / caveats.** Critical scholars often assign Leviticus 16's developed form to the post-exilic Priestly source, so using a Book of Mormon character's familiarity with it to prove pre-exilic antiquity will not persuade those who reject the book's historicity — it presupposes the conclusion. And typological readings that find the two goats "everywhere" risk pattern-imposition: a slain figure and a released figure can be matched to the goats in many stories. The careful claim is that Leviticus 16 supplies a real, well-defined ritual grammar; whether a given narrative deliberately encodes it is the contested interpretive step.
