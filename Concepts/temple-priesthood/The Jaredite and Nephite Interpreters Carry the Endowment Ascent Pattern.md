# The Jaredite and Nephite Interpreters Carry the Endowment Ascent Pattern

#type/claim

## The Claim

[[Don Bradley]] argues that the two interpreters (the white "seer" stones set in the rims of a bow) are tied, at every step of their Book-of-Mormon transmission, to a temple-ascent pattern that anticipates the LDS endowment — and that this pattern was already in the text (and in the lost narrative behind the text) long before Joseph Smith's 1842 exposure to Freemasonry. The interpreters descend from the Jaredites to the Nephites to [[Moroni]], and at the two ends of that chain the acquisition scene is an ascent-through-the-veil into the divine presence:

1. **Jaredite acquisition.** The [[Brother of Jared]] ascends Mount Shelem, prays as a high priest on behalf of his brethren, is brought through the veil, sees the finger of the premortal Christ "as the finger of a man," and receives the two interpreters (white stones) with a sealed revelation ([[Ether 3.14|Ether 3]]). See [[The Brother of Jared Models the Endowment Ascent Through the Veil]].
2. **Nephite acquisition.** The surviving Book of Mormon states that [[Mosiah]] II used the interpreters to translate the 24 Jaredite plates ([[Mosiah 28.11-16|Mosiah 28:11-16]]) but never says *how* the Nephites first obtained them. Bradley argues the missing acquisition account was in the Lost 116 Pages and is partly preserved in the non-Mormon [[Fayette Lapham]] interview with [[Joseph Smith Sr]]: a leader (Bradley identifies him as Mosiah I) is led by the Liahona to the interpreters and told to **cover his face with an animal skin** to use them — a Tabernacle/veil pattern echoing Moses veiling his face on Sinai, and anticipating Joseph Smith's own use of a hat over the seer stone.

The two acquisition scenes thus share an ascent grammar (divine guidance → veil/covering → reception of the seer instrument in the presence), which Bradley reads as the endowment's structure recurring across the Book of Mormon's interpreter tradition.

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. The Nephite possession of the interpreters is explicit in the surviving text
[[Mosiah 28.11-16|Mosiah 28:11-16]] states plainly that Mosiah II translated the Jaredite record "by the means of those two stones which were fastened into the two rims of a bow," and that "whosoever has these things is called seer, after the manner of old times." So the interpreters demonstrably passed from the Jaredites through the Nephites — the transmission claim is textual, not invented.
> **Evidence rating:** Strong for the transmission itself; the *endowment* reading of how they were acquired is the interpretive layer.

### 2. The acquisition gap is real, and a lost source plausibly filled it
The current Book of Mormon never narrates how the Nephites first got the interpreters, which is exactly the kind of material the Lost 116 Pages covered (the Book of Lehi / early Mosiah reigns). That a missing acquisition account once existed is a reasonable inference from the structure of the text, not special pleading. See [[Mosiah 28.11-16]].
> **Evidence rating:** Moderate — the gap is genuine; that it contained a veil/animal-skin scene rests on the Lapham testimony.

### 3. The Lapham interview is independent (non-Mormon) testimony
[[Fayette Lapham]] was a non-Mormon Palmyra businessman who interviewed [[Joseph Smith Sr]] in the 1830s and later published the account. Because he was hostile-to-neutral and recording from a primary witness, Bradley treats it as one of "our best sources" on the lost narrative — and it reports the animal-skin/veil detail. That an outsider preserved a Tabernacle-style covering motif lends it weight it would not have as in-house apologetic.
> **Evidence rating:** Moderate — Lapham is late, second-hand (Joseph Sr.'s memory), and was published decades after the fact, but its independence is a real strength.

### 4. The pattern coheres with the Jaredite end of the chain
The animal-skin covering at the Nephite end parallels the veil ascent at the Jaredite end ([[Ether 3.14|Ether 3]]), so the two acquisition scenes are not isolated proof-texts but a repeated grammar — strengthening the claim beyond a single instance. See [[The Brother of Jared Models the Endowment Ascent Through the Veil]] and [[Endowment Elements Predate Joseph Smith's Freemasonry]].
> **Evidence rating:** Moderate — internal coherence with the wider thesis; not independent confirmation.

## Steelman of the Opposing View

A critic answers: (a) the surviving Book of Mormon contains **no** animal-skin acquisition scene at all — the entire Nephite "veil" detail depends on a single late, second-hand, non-extant source (Lapham reporting Joseph Sr.'s recollection of pages no one can check), so it cannot bear the weight of a doctrinal pattern; (b) even granting the detail, covering one's face to use a bright seer stone has an obvious *practical* reading (excluding ambient light, exactly the function of Joseph's hat) that needs no temple gloss; (c) identifying the unnamed leader as Mosiah I is Bradley's reconstruction, not something the source states; and (d) reading "endowment ascent" into the Ether 3 theophany is the same parallelomania the vault itself flags in [[Avoiding Parallelomania Between Ancient Rites and LDS Temple]]. A Latter-day Saint reply: the claim is typological, not a historical reconstruction of the modern ceremony — it asserts only that the *pattern* (divine guidance, a covering/veil, reception of the seer instrument in a sacred encounter) recurs at both ends of the interpreter chain; and the convergence of an independent non-Mormon source with the canonical Ether ascent is more than free association, even if "endowment" is the reader's category and the practical reading of the hat is granted.
