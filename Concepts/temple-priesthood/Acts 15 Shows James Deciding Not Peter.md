# Acts 15 Shows James Deciding Not Peter

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
The standard LDS reading (e.g. [[Bruce R. McConkie]], Robert J. Matthews) is that [[Peter]] was the unambiguous head of the early church — president of a First-Presidency-like structure. The objection raised in the episode is that a plain historical reading of [[Acts 15|Acts 15]] instead shows [[James the brother of Jesus|James]] rendering the decision, and the movement looking Jerusalem-headquartered under James — complicating the LDS picture.

## The Rebuttal (as developed in the episode)

The episode does not so much rebut the objection as *sit in the tension*; Day and Butler present both readings and decline to settle it.

### 1. James gives the verdict (rating: strong; canonical text)
In Acts 15, after "much disputing," Peter speaks (vv. 7-11) and Paul/Barnabas report (v. 12), but it is **James** who concludes: "Wherefore my sentence is..." / "I judge" (v. 19), and proposes the four prohibitions (vv. 20, 29). Butler: "James is calling the shot... that's not how it's presented" in LDS teaching. [^1]

### 2. The council is conciliar, not monarchical (rating: moderate; textual)
The decision involves "the apostles and elders... and the whole church" (vv. 22-23), not the Twelve alone — pushing against the LDS expectation that the Quorum and First Presidency decide unilaterally. [^2]

### 3. Galatians corroborates James's clout (rating: moderate; cross-text)
[[Galatians 2.14|Gal 2:11-14]]: "men from James" make Peter change his behavior — consistent with James outranking, or at least pressuring, Peter in Jerusalem. See [[Paul vs James and the Judaizer Conflict]]. [^3]

### 4. The LDS counter-reading (steelman)
Day fairly states the LDS position he was trained in: Peter is the presiding apostle; he establishes the doctrine by revelation (the Cornelius vision, vv. 7-11), and James, as resident presiding officer in Jerusalem, merely *administers* and announces the already-revealed decision — much as a stake president implements policy set by the President of the Church. Day notes [[Bruce R. McConkie|McConkie]]'s NT commentary insists "Peter's in charge," and concedes "I don't know that we have a Latter-day Saint position, but we have some Latter-day Saint writers who have said this." He refuses to settle it: "I just look at the text and go, I see both sides. I wasn't there." [^4]

## Best Quotes

> "Verse 19, it says, 'Do therefore I judge.' James is calling the shot. That's not how it's presented. Acts 15. This is the challenge." [^1]

> "If we just had this... we would say this is a Jerusalem-headquartered movement and James is in charge." [^3]

> "When I go to the trainings, it's like it's Peter, Peter, Peter... But here it is James doing the stuff." [^4]
