# Joseph Smith Ignored Race-Based Scriptural Distinctions

## The Claim
[[Joseph Smith]] "consistently ignores apparent race-based distinctions in ancient scripture." Whenever a text seemed to mandate exclusion or non-intermarriage by lineage, Joseph went the *opposite* direction in practice — ordaining Black men, promoting intermarriage, and anticipating all races in the temple.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **He ordained Black men to the priesthood (rated: strong, documented).** Most clearly [[Elijah Abel]], ordained "right around the same time that he translates" the Book of Abraham — direct evidence Joseph did not read Abraham 1 as a racial priesthood bar. Also [[Q. Walker Lewis]].[^2] This is the load-bearing fact: the prophet who translated "cursed as pertaining to the priesthood" ordained Black men *contemporaneously*.

2. **1831 revelation promoting intermarriage with Native Americans (rated: moderate, reconstructed).** In July 1831, just over a year after the Church's organization, Joseph had a revelation "promoting interracial marriage with Native Americans," saying it was "a good thing" for the (then all-Anglo) Saints to marry Lamanites — the *opposite* of the Nephite norm against marrying Lamanites.[^3] The text was dictated but not written down at the time; it is known from [[Ezra Booth]]'s 1831 letter and later contemporaneous recollections. *Steelman:* because the revelation is uncanonized and reconstructed from memory (and from a hostile ex-member's letter), its exact wording is uncertain; a skeptic could question its content or motives (it overlapped with the controversial early "Indian mission" and later plural-marriage developments). Bradley insists "we know it happened... we know basically what it said," but concedes the textual gap.

3. **He preached to the cursed "Canaanites" anyway (rated: moderate, scriptural-behavioral).** [[Moses 6|Moses 6]] describes a cursed, segregated group of "Canaanites" to whom the gospel was withheld. Yet at the very time Joseph translated that, he sent missionaries to the Lamanites (the Nephites' functional "Canaanites").[^4] He did not replicate the ancient segregation.

4. **Kirtland Temple dedication anticipated all races (rated: strong, documented).** In the dedicatory prayer Joseph prayed that diverse peoples would come and worship, naming "Hottentots" (a sub-Saharan African people) and "Laplanders" (Northern Scandinavians) — "clearly anticipating that you're going to have people from all over the world."[^5]

5. **Anti-slavery and Black suffrage (rated: strong).** Joseph "opposed slavery and... advocated for African Americans to be able to vote," and his 1844 presidential platform proposed ending slavery by compensated emancipation. See [[Cursed to Be Kings - The Pharaoh Reversal]].

## Best Quotes

> "Joseph Smith actually consistently ignores apparent race-based distinctions in ancient scripture."[^1]

> "He actually does have Black men ordained to the priesthood. Most obviously, the case of Elijah Abel, which is right around the same time that he translates this."[^2]

> "He's anticipating sub-Saharan African tribes coming and worshiping in the temple. Joseph Smith is not taking what it says in these accounts of ancient events and saying, 'Oh, well, that's what we need to do now.'"[^5]
