# The Garden of Eden Was a Prototype Sanctuary

## The Point
[[T. Benjamin Spackman]], drawing on BYU professor [[Donald W. Parry]]'s article "Garden of Eden: Prototype Sanctuary," argues that Genesis 2–3 is written in temple language: the Garden is the original Holy of Holies, and the Israelite tabernacle/temple is a deliberate architectural re-staging of Eden. The payoff is the Day of Atonement (Leviticus 16), which becomes a *ritual undoing of the Fall* — the high priest carries atoning blood back into God's presence, reversing the expulsion of Adam and Eve. In the modern temple, Spackman says, "we are all essentially doing what the high priest does."

## Evidence
1. **Eden's guarded eastern gate.** After the Fall, God drives Adam and Eve out "from west to east" and stations cherubim and a flaming sword "on the east" of the garden — so Eden has an *entrance* on the east now guarded by divine figures.
2. **The tabernacle laid over Eden.** The Holy of Holies "is actually decorated like a garden"; both the door into the holy place and the veil into the Holy of Holies bear cherubim — the same guardian figures. Human doorkeepers guard the outer court so non-Israelites cannot enter.
3. **The Day of Atonement reverses the expulsion.** On the one day a year the high priest may enter the Holy of Holies (Leviticus 16), he offers sacrifice "not merely for sin, but for all of creation to cleanse it and start it over," then carries the blood "into the garden from east to west past the guardian figures" and "ritually re-enters God's presence through atonement" — "a ritual undoing of the fall."
4. **Symbolic, not genetic.** Spackman stresses this is "not a one-to-one" match and the Israelite temple itself changed over time; the relationship is "a symbolic analog relationship," not a genetic-historical descent. He repeatedly defers credit: "this is not my stuff... read the article and look at the pictures."

## Best Quotes
> "Garden of Eden, Adam and Eve are driven out from west to east. How do we know this? Because God puts cherubim, cherubs, guardian figures, and a flaming sword on the east." [^1]

> "With this sacrificial blood, he walks into the garden from east to west past the guardian figures and ritually re-enters God's presence through atonement... it is a ritual undoing of the fall on the day of atonement." [^2]

> "In our temple, we are all essentially doing what the high priest does. We are fallen and through atonement, we walk back past guardian figures and ritually re-enter God's presence." [^3]

## LDS Connection
The Eden-as-sanctuary reading reframes the endowment's Creation–Garden–Fall sequence not as a borrowing but as a restoration of a pattern "sitting there in plain sight" once someone "draw[s] some pictures and connect[s] things explicitly." It pairs naturally with [[Gradations of Holiness Structured the Ancient Temple]] (the guardian figures one must pass) and with [[Beatific Vision in the Israelite Temple]] (entering the divine presence), while [[Restoration Is Adaptation and Accommodation Not a One-to-One Copy]] supplies the caution that the link is symbolic rather than one-to-one.
