# Joseph Smith Taught Three Priesthoods Including the Patriarchal

## The Claim
[[Bruce Porter]] argues that [[Joseph Smith]] taught **three** priesthoods, not two: the **Aaronic**, the **Melchizedek**, and the **patriarchal**. The patriarchal priesthood is received in the temple when a man and woman are sealed in the new and everlasting covenant of marriage; both spouses "hold the patriarchal priesthood equally" as an authority *within the home*. It is, Porter says, "a higher priesthood" — "the priesthood of the Celestial Kingdom" — distinct from the Melchizedek priesthood that governs the temporal and spiritual welfare of mankind more broadly.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The threefold schema (rated: moderate; reflects Joseph's Nauvoo-era teaching).** "Joseph said and taught that there were three different priesthoods ... a Melchizedek priesthood, a patriarchal priesthood, and an Aaronic priesthood." The patriarchal "we get in the temple," entered via the sealing covenant.[^1] *Rating:* this tracks a genuine strand of Joseph Smith's later teaching and of D&C 131 (the new-and-everlasting-covenant order of marriage), though current correlated usage typically speaks of two priesthoods with the "patriarchal order" as a function within the Melchizedek.

2. **Held equally by husband and wife (rated: the load-bearing claim).** "The man and the woman hold the patriarchal priesthood equally within the home," and "every woman holds that patriarchal priesthood who is sealed in the temple to her husband."[^2] This is the premise that reframes the "women and the priesthood" question (see point 4).

3. **An authority of the Celestial Kingdom (rated: interpretive).** Porter: the patriarchal is "the authority of the celestial kingdom — that of a father and that of a mother, a husband and a wife"; the Melchizedek, by contrast, is "for the temporal and spiritual welfare of mankind" and its keys are eventually "given back to Christ."[^3] See [[The Aaronic-Melchizedek Dualism Maps the Temple's Kings and Priests]].

4. **Reframes the ordination debate (rated: apologetically significant).** Because sealed women *already* hold the patriarchal priesthood, Porter says the real question behind "women ought to hold the priesthood" is about the *Melchizedek* priesthood specifically — which he then explains by its substitutionary purpose. See [[The Melchizedek Priesthood Substitutes for a Missing or Unworthy Patriarch]] and [[The Patriarchal Order Is the Order of Fatherhood Not Male Rule]].

**Steelman of the opposing/cautious view.** A correlated-doctrine Latter-day Saint or a scholar may object that the Church today teaches **two** priesthoods (Aaronic and Melchizedek), treating the "patriarchal order" as the highest *order within* the Melchizedek priesthood (D&C 107:1–6) rather than a third, separate priesthood — so Porter's tripartite scheme is a defensible reading of Joseph's Nauvoo statements but not the official framing. Likewise, saying women "hold the patriarchal priesthood" can outrun official language, which speaks of women and men entering the patriarchal *order* together and of women exercising priesthood *authority* in callings and ordinances without holding priesthood *office*. Porter presents this explicitly as his own synthesis from a 40-year reading project ("I've come to ... a different view ... than what is traditionally taught"), so it should be cited as one scholar's reconstruction, valuable for its pastoral payoff but not as settled doctrine.

## Best Quotes
> "Joseph said and taught that there were three different priesthoods: a Melchizedek priesthood, a patriarchal priesthood, and an Aaronic priesthood."[^1]

> "Every woman holds that patriarchal priesthood who is sealed in the temple to her husband. They both hold that patriarchal priesthood, and that's a higher priesthood."[^2]
