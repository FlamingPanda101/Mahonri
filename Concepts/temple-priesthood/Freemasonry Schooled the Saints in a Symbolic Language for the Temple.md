# Freemasonry Schooled the Saints in a Symbolic Language for the Temple

## The Claim
On [[Stick of Joseph]], the two Master-Mason guests argue that Masonry's chief gift to the Restoration was not its specific signs but a *trained capacity to read symbolic, multivalent, mnemonic language* — the very literacy a "Greek-linear" Western mind lacks. Masonry preserves catechism (question-and-answer memory drill), cipher (encoded text), rhythm, and the principle that a symbol is "multivalent... you can zoom in and see the finer meanings and you can zoom out and get the same meaning but bigger." This, they say, prepared the Saints to receive a temple whose scriptures are "sealed" in the sense of *concealed in plain sight*. Robert Kay goes further: Joseph's "Egyptian Alphabet and Grammar" is not invented but draws on a Royal-Arch cipher tradition that overlaps the Hebrew cipher (the *Atbash*-type system), which "goes back to Egypt" — "you had it too; you just didn't know you had it."[^1]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **(Moderate, mnemonics)** The catechism/rhythm point is sound and general: question-answer drill and metered text are well-attested memory technologies (the guests cite the medieval *Regius Manuscript*, c. 1390, as metered verse; the LDS *Lectures on Faith* catechism; doctrinal-mastery scripture drill). This grounds the "Masonry as a school" framing rather than "Masonry as the source."[^2]
2. **(Moderate, "sealed = concealed")** Reading "sealed" as *concealed-yet-present* (the sealed portion "right in front of your face... written in English") is a hermeneutical claim consonant with the channel's wider symbolic method — see [[Astronomy Is the Lost Universal Language of Scripture]].
3. **(Weak–speculative, the cipher claim)** The assertion that the Kirtland "Egyptian Alphabet and Grammar" derives from a Royal-Arch/Hebrew cipher going back to Egypt is the *least* supported part; it is practitioner conjecture, not documented philology, and should be weighted at the channel's C tier. Compare the vault's more cautious treatment in [[The Kirtland Egyptian Papers Are a Cipher Project Not a Translation Tool]] and [[Grammar and Alphabet of the Egyptian Language]].

## Opposing View (Steelmanned)
A critic will say this elegantly reframes a liability as an asset. If Masonry taught Joseph the *cipher* and *catechism* forms, that is further evidence the Kirtland Egyptian Papers and the endowment are *Masonic productions*, not ancient restorations — the "school" language concedes dependence while renaming it. And "sealed means concealed in plain sight" is unfalsifiable: it lets any preferred reading be declared the hidden meaning the text "always" carried, immune to disconfirmation. The Royal-Arch-cipher-to-Egypt genealogy in particular has no scholarly support and looks like motivated parallelism.

## Counter-objection
"Masonry as a school of symbolic literacy" is a weaker and more defensible claim than "Masonry as the source of temple content," and the two should not be conflated. Learning *how to read* multivalent symbols (a skill) is not the same as deriving *what the temple means* (a content), and the content is independently traced to an ancient temple pattern Masonry lacks ([[Temple Origins Run Deeper Than Freemasonry]]). The "concealed in plain sight" hermeneutic is disciplined when it is *constrained* by the text's own structure (chiasmus, name-puns, festival cycles the vault documents in the Book of Mormon) rather than free-associated — so it is not inherently unfalsifiable, though this episode's looser applications are. The cipher-to-Egypt genealogy *should* be flagged as speculative; the honest move is to keep the strong, general point (Masonry trained symbolic literacy) and bracket the weak, specific one (a documented Royal-Arch→Hebrew→Egypt cipher chain), which the vault's Kirtland-papers notes already treat with appropriate caution.

## LDS Relevance
This note records the channel's *epistemological* claim about why the Saints needed a preparatory school in symbol — useful for understanding the LDS reading of the temple as multivalent and "encoded," and for situating the Restoration's recovery of a symbolic literacy the modern West lost ([[Astronomy Is the Lost Universal Language of Scripture]]). It is deliberately rated cautiously: the strong part (symbolic schooling, mnemonics) is plausible; the specific cipher-genealogy is speculative and is cross-checked against the vault's documented Kirtland-papers analysis.
