# The Garment of Skin and the Garment of Light

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Jonah Barnes]] and [[Dave Butler]] argue that the "coats of skins" God makes for Adam and Eve ([[Genesis 3.21|Genesis 3:21]]) carry an **atonement pun** that the English Bible loses. Two moves:

1. **Skin / light (*or* / *or*).** The Hebrew word for **skin** is *ʿôr* (ע-ו-ר) and for **light** is *ʾôr* (א-ו-ר) — near-homophones (identical in modern Hebrew, distinguished anciently only by similar glottal stops). A famous opinion in *Genesis Rabbah* (the rabbi Butler can't name precisely) reads Adam and Eve's *kothoneth ʿor* ("coats of skin") as **"garments of light."** [^1] Butler's "both-and": the **garment of skin = the mortal body** ("a way of talking about your mortal body... here's your garment of skin"); but **to return to Eden you must reverse the process and be re-clothed in a garment of light** — "you have to reverse the process by which you came down to earth." So "it's not your garment of skin that atones for you; you need to get covered in a garment of light." [^2]

2. **Covering / atonement (*kaphar*).** Tyndale, facing *kaphar* ("a covering," "a covering of blood," the Day-of-Atonement covering) across the Old Testament, **coined "at-one-ment"** from a Latin liturgical term — a defensible rendering ("we do want to become one"), but it **obscures the covering symbolism**: in English "you don't get covering, you get atonement." [^3] So the Eden garment, the sacrificial blood-covering, and the high-priestly robe are one shrouded-over theme of **being covered in Christ** (cf. [[The Garment Is the Covering of Christ]], [[Atonement as Covering - Joseph Could Not Have Known]]).

## Supporting Evidence
1. **The *or*/*or* homophone and the "garments of light" reading are real.** *Genesis Rabbah* 20:12 records the tradition that Adam's garments were "garments of light" (reading *ʿor* as *ʾor*), attributed to figures like R. Meir's Torah scroll — so the pun is an attested rabbinic interpretation, not an LDS invention. *Rating: solid; checkable in the rabbinic corpus.* [^1]
2. **The descent/return "two garments" structure is widely attested.** The motif that the soul exchanges a garment of light for a garment of skin on the way down, and must regain the garment of light to return (the "robe" of the *Hymn of the Pearl*; Syriac and patristic "robe of glory" theology), is well documented — so Butler's "reverse the process" reading sits in a real ancient stream. *Rating: strong tradition-historical footing.* [^2]
3. **The Tyndale *kaphar* → "atonement" coinage is historically accurate.** Tyndale did mint "at-one-ment" and the Hebrew *k-p-r* does carry "cover/wipe," so the claim that English obscured a covering-image is linguistically sound (the vault's [[Atonement as Covering - Joseph Could Not Have Known]] documents the same etymology). *Rating: accurate, if the inference drawn from it is devotional.* [^3]
4. **It complements the vestment argument.** [[The Garment Shares the Hebrew Word of the Priestly Vestments]] shows *kethoneth* links Eden to the priestly tunic; the skin/light pun adds a second layer (the garment's *substance*, skin→light) to the same Genesis 3 garment. *Rating: additive, non-duplicative.*

**Steelman of the skeptical counter.** The pun is **interpretation, not the plain sense**. [[Genesis 3.21|Genesis 3:21]] reads *ʿor* ("skin"), and "garments of light" is a *midrashic re-reading* (a deliberate, playful re-vocalization), **not** what the verse says — so building atonement doctrine on the homophone is homiletics, not exegesis. The "reverse the descent / regain the garment of light" schema is drawn from **later** Gnostic-flavored and Syriac traditions, which a critic will say are being read *back into* Genesis. And Tyndale's "atonement," while a coinage, was a *legitimate* rendering of reconciliation (*kaphar* does also carry "to make at-one/propitiate"), so calling it an obscuring "knot" overstates the loss. A measured reply: Barnes and Butler explicitly flag this as **bonus, multivalent** temple symbolism ("symbols are multivalent... a both-and"), not a claim about the verse's lone original meaning — and the load-bearing pieces (the *Genesis Rabbah* "garments of light," the real *or*/*or* homophone, the genuine *kaphar* = cover) are all checkable facts; the devotional synthesis (skin = mortal body, light = covering of Christ) is offered as a *pattern* the ancient sources themselves play with, not as the consensus reading of Genesis.

## Best Quotes
> "The word skin in Hebrew is *or*... and the word light is *or*... in contemporary Hebrew they sound identical... the Bible is all puns. Here's a pun." [^1]

> "It's not your garment of skin that atones for you. You need to get covered in a garment of light. That is how atonement is accomplished — you are covered." [^2]

> "Tindale... looks at *kaphar*, covering... and he goes, I think I'm going to translate that as atonement. And so when you read the Old Testament, you don't get covering — you get atonement, becoming one... but it obfuscates this beautiful symbolism of the covering." [^3]
