# Omnibenevolence vs Conditional Access Models

## The Claim
[[Gavin (Catholic Debater)]]'s polemical contrast: if God is omnibenevolent, "the gift of himself cannot be restricted to an exalted elite. His presence cannot be institutionally gated. His love cannot be escalated through progressive worthiness." He explicitly names [[D&C 76]] as the foil. In Christianity, by contrast: Christ is the temple ([[John 2.19-21|John 2:19-21]]), Christ is the veil torn open ([[Hebrews 10.19-22|Heb 10:19-22]]), Christ is the one mediator for all ([[1 Timothy 2.5|1 Tim 2:5]]). "Access to God is not earned upward. It is given downward."[^1]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **Christ-as-temple / torn-veil** — rate: strong intra-Christian theology. The NT motif that Christ's body and atonement render the holy place universally accessible. *(scriptural)* [^1]
2. **The "elite access" charge** — Gavin reads worthiness-gated temple/exaltation systems (he cites D&C 76's tiered glories) as incompatible with universal benevolence. *(polemical)* [^2] **Steelman of the LDS view:** Hayden answers that LDS theology is arguably the *most* loving Christian formulation because *all* people — even those who never heard of Christ in mortality — receive a postmortem opportunity for salvation, whereas Catholicism historically taught unbaptized infants go to hell ([[Infant Exaltation]], limbo). So "structured access" need not hinder God's love. See [[Conditional Access Tu Quoque Against the LDS God]].
3. **Self-undercutting?** — Hayden notes Gavin, as a Roman Catholic, *also* holds sacraments are authorized through the Church. Gavin concedes structure exists in the NT (apostles, public revelation) but denies it "hinders God's love." *(internal-critique)* [^3]

**Relevance caveat:** this entire thread is an *inter-tradition* skirmish that Hayden repeatedly flags as irrelevant to the resolution — "Mormonism could be totally false and that would mean nothing... for the debate resolution." See [[The Creation Dilemma]].

## Best Quotes
> "His presence cannot be institutionally gated. His love cannot be escalated through progressive worthiness as outlined in D&C 76."[^2]

> "Access to God is not earned upward. It is given downward. And this is omnibenevolence."[^1]

> "Latter-day Saint doctrine actually allows for God to be the most loving... because we believe that all people will be given an opportunity for salvation whether or not they've heard of God in this life." — Hayden [^3]
