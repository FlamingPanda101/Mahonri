# Melchizedek Is Yahweh Presiding Over the Temple Feast

#type/claim

## The Claim
In the middle room of the [[The Sermon on the Mount Is an Ordinance in Three Rooms|Sermon-as-ordinance]], [[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)|David Butler]] argues that Matthew 6:25-33 ("take no thought... behold the fowls of the air... consider the lilies") stages a **feast at which the Lord descends to feed and clothe the worshipper**, and that the presiding figure is **[[Melchizedek]] — read as Yahweh himself**. [^1] The bridge is Genesis 14:18: the KJV's "Melchizedek king of Salem" may not reference the (archaeologically unattested) city Salem at all, since the Hebrew *melek shalem* can mean "the king who is *shalem*" — the **Peaceable / perfect King** (cf. [[Alma the Younger|Alma 13]]'s "Prince of Peace"). So "the Lord is the king of the *shalom*... the guy who comes down and presides over this peaceable feast, the feast beside the still waters." [^2] Because his name means "king of righteousness" (*melek-tsedeq*), the Beatitude "blessed are they that hunger and thirst after **righteousness**, for they shall be **filled**" pays off here: they are filled *with righteousness* by eating "the feast of his own flesh and blood" — the *epiousios* "[[Epiousios Is Yahweh Bread Not Daily Bread|Yahweh bread]]." [^3] The second-room priest is read as **Elijah**, since the feast's *birds* (Elijah fed by ravens), *clothing* (the mantle to Elisha), and *resurrection* overtones (raising the widow's son; 2 Nephi 9's "robe of righteousness" tied to the resurrection) are all Elijah signs. [^4]

## Supporting Evidence (steelman)

1. **The *melek-tsedeq* / *melek-shalem* wordplay is biblical, not invented.** Hebrews 7:1-3 builds its entire High-Priest typology on exactly this double reading — "king of righteousness" and "king of peace [Salem]" — so the royal/peace etymology is embedded in the canonical handling of Melchizedek (see [[Melchizedek]]). Reading *shalem* adjectivally ("the peaceable king") rather than as a placename is a recognized possibility. *Rating: the etymology and the king-of-peace reading have firm scriptural pedigree.*

2. **Genesis 14 already pairs Melchizedek with a bread-and-wine feast and tithing.** The one Genesis scene featuring Melchizedek has him bring out *bread and wine* and bless Abraham, who pays tithes — a feast presided over by a priest-king. Reading the Matthew 6 feast through that lens is a controlled typological move, not a free association. *Rating: the feast/priest-king structure is present in the source text.*

3. **The Elijah associations are textually real.** Elijah *is* fed by ravens (1 Kings 17), *does* pass his mantle to Elisha (clothing), and *does* raise the widow of Zarephath's son — and Matthew 6's feeding/clothing plus 2 Nephi 9's resurrection-keyed "robe of righteousness" line up with those motifs. Butler adds that Gabriel announces the Elijah-role *in the middle room* ("to the right of the incense altar," Luke 1). See [[The Three Temple Rooms Map Justice-Mercy-Grace and Moses-Elijah-the Lord]]. *Rating: each Elijah datum is accurate; the room-assignment is the interpretive step.*

4. **It coheres with the Eucharistic payload.** The feast cashes out the *epiousios* "Yahweh bread" narratively — the Lord descends and *is* the substance eaten — so the Melchizedek=Yahweh reading is not a bare etymology but the dramatic climax the chapter builds toward. This is the LDS counterpart to the broader [[The Eucharist as Communion with Christ|Eucharist-as-communion]] motif.

**Steelman of the skeptical counter.** Two soft spots. First, the **Salem-is-not-a-place** move is contested: Psalm 76:2 parallels "Salem" with "Zion," and most translators take Genesis 14:18's Salem as (proto-)Jerusalem, so dissolving the placename to extract "the peaceable king" is a minority reading chosen because it serves the feast thesis. Second, identifying Melchizedek *as Yahweh* (rather than as a *type* of Christ, which is all Hebrews claims) imports the equation by way of the *melek-tsedeq* / Yahweh-bread chain rather than from Genesis itself — the same retrofitting pattern flagged in the parent ordinance note. Mainstream exegesis reads Matthew 6:25-33 as wisdom teaching on anxiety and providence, with no feast, no Melchizedek, and no descent. A Latter-day Saint reply concedes the Yahweh-identification is interpretive but notes it is *licensed* by the channel's wider case that [[The Divine Name Yahweh Was Given to Jesus|the divine name belongs to the Son]], so "the Lord presides as Melchizedek" is internally consistent with the rest of the temple cluster — and the convergence of king-of-righteousness, the "filled with righteousness" Beatitude, and the bread-of-his-flesh is tighter than coincidence.

## Best Quotes
> "What I think we have here is the Lord, who is Melchizedek, who is the king of the *shalom*... specifically the guy who comes down and presides over this peaceable feast, the feast beside the still waters." [^2]

> "Melchizedek is Yahweh, and he's present, and he is the Lord, and he brings the feast of his own flesh and blood. And those who have hungered and thirsted after righteousness are filled by having a feast of the *epiousios* bread — by eating the bread of righteousness." [^3]

> "Elijah is fed by birds in the book of Kings, and we have here a feast with birds. And Elijah is also associated with a story of clothing — he passes his mantle on to Elisha." [^4]
