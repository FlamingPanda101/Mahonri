## The Point
Since the first century, Jesus followers (along with other Jews) envisioned God's true temple as existing in heaven, with the Jerusalem temple merely an earthly reflection of it. Convinced that Jesus was the mediating High Priest of the heavenly temple, early Christians looked forward to Christ's priestly intercession granting them access to the true Holy of Holies in heaven. Monumental churches later brought earthly and heavenly worship together by replicating features of the heavenly temple, especially as described in Revelation.

## Evidence
1. Christians who met in residential settings "kept their eternal hopes focused on the heavenly temple, where Jesus as the 'Great High Priest' was before God's throne making intercession on their behalf" (concepts developing since the first century; cf. Hebrews 2–4, 9; Revelation 1, 4–5).
2. Monumental churches replicated the heavenly sanctuary of Revelation: placing the altar in the apse (Holy of Holies) rather than the outer court (Revelation 8:3–4); placing crypts or martyr relics under the altar (Revelation 6:9); seating clergy in a semicircle around the throne/cathedra (Revelation 4:2–4).
3. Church apses depicted Jesus enthroned among angelic beings (Isaiah 6; Revelation 4–5), and the congregation joined the hymns of praise sung by angelic attendants around God's throne (Isaiah 6:3; Revelation 4:8).
4. By the late Roman/Byzantine period, Christian worship had "come full circle" with its first-century roots: God's presence again housed in temples, access offered through priest-mediated rituals, and heaven brought to earth through sacred liturgy.

## Best Quotes
> "For the most part, Christians meeting in residential settings kept their eternal hopes focused on the heavenly temple, where Jesus as the 'Great High Priest' was before God's throne making intercession on their behalf and preparing a way for the faithful to one day enter the divine presence." [^1]

> "Monumental churches ... brought earthly and heavenly worship together in the same space by replicating features of the heavenly temple, particularly as described in the biblical book of Revelation." [^2]

## LDS Connection
The conviction that an ordained priesthood mediates access to God's presence, bringing heaven to earth through sacred liturgy, resonates with Latter-day Saint priesthood and temple theology — a point Grey develops as shared "conceptual vocabulary."
