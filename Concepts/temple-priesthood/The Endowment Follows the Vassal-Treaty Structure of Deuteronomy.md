# The Endowment Follows the Vassal-Treaty Structure of Deuteronomy

## The Claim
[[Mike Day]] argues that the LDS temple endowment follows the same **suzerain-vassal treaty** structure as the book of Deuteronomy (and as King Benjamin's speech in Mosiah): preamble, historical prologue, covenant stipulations, formal witnesses, penalties, and deposition/recital. He presents this shared liturgical *form* as evidence of an ancient covenant-making pattern — not a borrowing from Freemasonry.

## Supporting Evidence
1. **The treaty form (rated: mainstream ANE scholarship).** Day lists the elements of the second-millennium/Neo-Assyrian vassal treaty embedded in Deuteronomy: "a preamble… the history… the bulk is the covenants… formal witnesses… a whole bit on penalties if you break this… deposition of the text and a recital of the covenant."[^1] The vault documents the underlying parallel in [[Deuteronomy Was Patterned After the Vassal Treaty of Esarhaddon]].
2. **The endowment matches it (rated: the host's claim).** "If you've been to the temple, that's the endowment": preamble and history, then the bulk is the covenants, then formal witnesses, then "this is serious" (the penalties, whose explicit form was removed in 1990), then "a recital of what you just said… at a specific place at the end."[^2]
3. **Repackaging in Moses's words (rated: Day's reading, with Friedman/Levenson).** Day argues Deuteronomy itself *retextualizes* the Assyrian overlord's treaty, swapping the human suzerain for Yahweh as king — "I owe my fidelity to him [Yahweh], not to [the Assyrian king]"; King Benjamin does "the same order." So covenant-making by treaty form is "the ancient tradition of setting people under covenant."[^3]
4. **Form is not content (rated: cross-link).** This is paired with [[Ordinance Creation Recontextualizes Ordinary Actions]]: a covenant *structure* (like Masonic forms) can be repurposed, but "the liturgy and the structure isn't necessarily as important as the content."[^4]

**Steelman of the critic.** The Deuteronomy-as-vassal-treaty parallel is solid ANE scholarship, but mapping the *modern* endowment onto that six-part form is the host's own analysis, and a skeptic notes the fit can be made loose enough to match many covenant rituals (including Masonic initiation). Critics also observe that some "matching" elements (e.g. explicit penalties) were later *removed*, which complicates a claim of an unchanging ancient pattern. The Deuteronomy treaty-form is established; the endowment-maps-onto-it claim is a devotional reading, offered (the host concedes) as "Mike Day opinion."

## Best Quotes
> "The entire endowment fits the structure of Deuteronomy… preamble, the history, then the bulk is here's the covenants, then witnesses, then this is serious [penalties], and then a recital of the covenant."[^2]

> "What if this is part of the ancient tradition of setting people under covenant? Because 23-year-old Joseph Smith — we have it in Mosiah, we have it in Deuteronomy, and we have it in the endowment."[^3]

## LDS Connection
This reframes the endowment's covenant sequence as a recovery of the biblical treaty-covenant form rather than a Masonic import, complementing [[Endowment Elements Predate Joseph Smith's Freemasonry]] and [[The Covenant Idea Is Borrowed From Assyrian Vassalage]]. It also models the host's broader "retextualization/repackaging" thesis: God gives covenants in the covenant-language of the age (Deuteronomy in treaty form; the endowment in a partly Masonic vocabulary), with the divine *content* the constant.
