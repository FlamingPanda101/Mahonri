# Murder to Get Gain Inverts the Order of Sacrifice

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Todd McLauchlin]] argues that the Book of Mormon's recurring secret-combination motto **"murder to get gain"** is the precise **inversion of the priesthood's defining act, sacrifice**. Where Jehovah teaches [[Cain]] **sacrifice** (to "draw light and power of God and redemption"), Lucifer teaches **murder to get gain** — and this becomes "the covenant structure that runs counter to the order of God," the "counter order" introduced the moment Cain kills Abel ([[Moses 5.10-11|Moses 5]]). [^1] The key asymmetry is thermodynamic: **murder can only consume what is already given**, but **sacrifice can yield more power than is put in** — which is why it "can go one step further," and why Christ overcomes the devil "not by beating them at their game" but by sacrificing himself ([[2 Nephi 2|2 Nephi 2]]'s opposition in all things). [^2] McLauchlin stretches "murder" along a **continuum**: from the smallest resistance to the light of Christ within you — "telling a white lie, digging a pit for your neighbor," curating a false image on social media ("the greatest amplifier of murder to get gain ever," extracting light/attention "to make yourself a god in the world") — out to literal ritual bloodshed and "formalized... ordinances in opposition to the ordinances of God." [^3] Because dark orders are always **inversions** (the "black mass" takes whatever the God-version is and reverses it), the whole apparatus of war and secret combinations is "murder to get gain" writ large. [^4]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **The motto and its Satanic origin are canonical.** [[Moses 5.10-11|Moses 5:29-31]] explicitly has Satan teach Cain the oath "Cain, that thou mayest murder and get gain," and Helaman/Ether tie "secret combinations" to "murder and get gain" — so the inversion-of-sacrifice frame rests on the text's own pairing of murder with a *counter-covenant*. *Rating: strongly canonical.* [^1]
2. **Sacrifice-vs-murder as a structuring polarity fits the Book of Mormon.** The book repeatedly pits a covenant in-group against a blood-oath anti-group; the vault's [[The Forbidden Oath Is the Dark Mirror of the Covenant Oath]] shows the Jaredite oath of [[Ether 8.13-18|Ether 8]] is the *exact forbidden inverse* of the covenant oath — independent support for "good order vs. mirrored counter-order." *Rating: coheres with an established structure.*
3. **The "evil as positive inversion" point has a real theological pedigree.** Reading evil not as mere privation but as an active counterfeit order matches the vault's [[Eternal Light-Darkness Dualism Gives Evil a Positive Ontology]] and the [[2 Nephi 2|2 Nephi 2]] opposition principle. *Rating: a defensible, internally consistent metaphysic within the LDS frame.* [^2]

**Steelman of the skeptical counter.** The **continuum move overreaches**: calling a white lie or an Instagram filter a "degree of murder" stretches "murder" past any ordinary or legal sense, and risks a scrupulosity in which every social compromise is a capital sin — a rhetorical escalation, not an exegetical result. The thermodynamic flourish ("sacrifice yields more energy than is input," contra the conservation of energy) is a metaphor dressed as physics; it illustrates rather than proves. And reading *all* war and politics as "murder to get gain" can slide into a totalizing, conspiratorial lens (McLauchlin himself gestures at JFK, Stalin-to-Gorbachev) that explains everything and therefore tests nothing. A measured reply: the *core* claim is textually solid — the Book of Mormon literally frames the secret-combination covenant as murder-for-gain opposite a sacrifice-covenant — and the continuum is offered as moral-psychological pedagogy ("the smallest resistance to the spirit... becomes ontological") rather than a legal definition; one can accept the inversion thesis while declining the maximalist application.

## Best Quotes
> "Lucifer, Satan... teaches Cain murder to get gain. The Lord Jehovah teaches sacrifice — to draw light and power of God and redemption. And Cain and Lucifer invert it... murder to get gain becomes the covenant structure that runs counter to the order of God." [^1]

> "Murder can only consume that which is given... In sacrifice, you can get more power than you sacrifice, and so it can go further. This is how you can step into exaltation because of the sacrifice of Jesus Christ." [^2]

> "Social media is like the greatest amplifier of murder to get gain ever, because you murder the truth or you compromise the truth... in order to get gain, to make yourself a god in the world." [^3]
