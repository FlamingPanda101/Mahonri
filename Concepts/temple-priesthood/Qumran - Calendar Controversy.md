# Qumran - Calendar Controversy

The Qumran calendar controversy refers to the dispute, attested in the Dead Sea Scrolls, between the 364-day solar calendar favored by the Qumran community (and by [[1 Enoch]] and Jubilees) and the lunar-based calendar used in the Jerusalem temple establishment. Because the two schemes fixed the festivals on different days, the sect regarded the temple authorities as keeping the holy days at the wrong times — a key marker of its separation from mainstream Second Temple Judaism.

## Relevance

The controversy supplies the historical setting for the vault's note on the [[Book of Enoch - 364 Day Solar Calendar]], showing that Enoch's solar calendar was not an idiosyncratic curiosity but a live sectarian dispute in the centuries around the turn of the era — relevant to dating the Enochic literature and to its standing as scripture at Qumran.
