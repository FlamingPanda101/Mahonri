# The Garment of Adam Was the Serpent-Skin Mantle Stripped From Lucifer

#type/claim

## The Claim

[[Jonah Barnes]] relays an apocryphal tradition that the "coats of skins" God made for Adam and Eve (Genesis 3:21) were not ordinary animal hide but a **serpent-skin**: the immortal, glorious vestment **stripped from Lucifer** at his fall and given to Adam. On this reading the garment is a *kaphar* (a "covering" → atonement) that lets Adam stand again in the divine presence, and it explains why Satan hates **Adam** specifically and bitterly — the garment of power that was once his now clothes the man who replaced him. This serpent-skin mantle is then the first link in the Garment-of-Adam relic chain that the source traces through Noah, Ham, Nimrod, Esau, Jacob, and Joseph. See [[The Garment of Power Descended From Adam to Nimrod]] and [[The Garment of Skin and the Garment of Light]].

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. "Coats of skins" as a divine covering (kaphar) is text-anchored
Genesis has *God himself* make and clothe Adam in "coats of skins," and the Hebrew *kaphar* ("to cover") is the root of the atonement vocabulary, so reading the garment as a sacral covering that enables return to God's presence is grounded in the language, independent of the serpent-skin detail.
> **Evidence rating:** Moderate-to-strong for the covering/atonement reading; the *serpent-skin* identification is the apocryphal add-on.

### 2. The serpent-skin motif appears in the apocryphal vein
The serpent-skin / garment-stripped-from-the-devil tradition is drawn from the apocryphal Adam literature (the *Life of Adam and Eve* family, with later rabbinic and pseudepigraphal echoes) and is summarized in [[Stephen D. Ricks]]'s essay on the Garment of Adam, which the source leans on. It is a real strand of ancient tradition, not a modern invention.
> **Evidence rating:** Moderate — the motif is genuinely attested, but it is one variant among several (light-garment, skin-garment) and the traditions are late and non-uniform.

### 3. It supplies a motive for Satan's enmity toward Adam
The reading explains a detail the bare Genesis text leaves open — why the adversary's hatred fixes on Adam — by making the garment the prize Lucifer lost, giving the enmity a concrete object.
> **Evidence rating:** Weak-to-moderate — explanatorily neat, but this is the kind of gap-filling that later legend specializes in; it is satisfying rather than demonstrative.

### 4. It connects to the Book of Mormon relic chain
The serpent-skin origin is the head of the garment lineage that, the source argues, surfaces in [[Alma 46.21-22|Alma 46:23-24]] (Jacob's prophecy of a *preserved, undecayed remnant* of Joseph's coat) — remnant theology the source says is absent from Genesis but present in the brass-plate tradition.
> **Evidence rating:** Moderate as coherence; the Alma connection is its own claim (The Joseph-Coat Remnant Theology Matches Jasher and the Book of Mormon Not Genesis) and does not by itself establish the serpent-skin origin.

## Steelman of the Opposing View

A critic answers: (a) Genesis 3:21 says only "coats of skins" — **serpent-skin, Lucifer's mantle, and the stripping scene are entirely extra-canonical**, drawn from late and mutually inconsistent legends (the Adam literature does not speak with one voice), so this is midrashic embroidery, not recoverable history; (b) the same apocrypha elsewhere make the garment one of *light* rather than skin, so picking the serpent-skin recension is selective; (c) "skins" most naturally denotes animal hide marking the first death/sacrifice, a reading that already carries the atonement sense without any serpent; and (d) threading a single physical relic from Adam to Joseph across millennia treats folkloric motifs as a chain of custody. A Latter-day Saint reply: the claim is offered as **type, not chronicle** — the value is the recurring theological grammar (a divine covering, stripped from the adversary, enabling return to God's presence), and the serpent-skin variant dramatizes the atonement-as-covering and the enmity of Genesis 3:15; the source itself frames the relic chain as typological and notes the Deuteronomist tradition would have had reason to excise such corporeal-Messiah/temple symbolism (see [[Deuteronomistic Corruption of the Old Testament]]).
