# The Blessing of Jacob Is a Veil Recognition Scene

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Andrea Woodmansee]] argues that the "strangest story" of [[Genesis 27|Jacob obtaining the birthright blessing]] — blind Isaac, Rebekah covering [[Jacob (patriarch)|Jacob]]'s hands and neck with goat hair, Isaac identifying the son by *feeling his hands* — is best read as a **veiled temple recognition scene**. Isaac's blindness functions as "a veil over the eyes"; a son who has *become* the rightful birthright-holder approaches the father behind that veil and is **identified by the marks on his hands**, then blessed. This makes the episode a type of Christ — the true birthright Son recognized at the veil by the **marks in his hands** (as Thomas recognized the risen Lord, [[John 20.28|John 20:27]]). On this reading [[Esau]], a *non-wicked* elder brother, *forfeits* the birthright so the next may have it — the Christ pattern of a firstborn yielding his birthright to us. It is a worked example of [[The Veil Is Christ Reversing the Day of Atonement|traversing the veil by identification with Christ]].[^1][^2]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Identification by hands at a "veil."** "Isaac can't see which son this is, so he feels the marks on Jacob's hands, and that's how he identifies him." Woodmansee reads blindness as a veil and the hand-marks as the identifying tokens — "he's coming to a father who's behind a veil and saying, I'm the birthright son, and you can identify me by feeling my hands." *Rating: the hand-identification is explicit in Genesis 27:22-23; the "veil ceremony" overlay is the interpretation.*[^1]
2. **The marks in the hands point to Christ.** The hosts connect it to how "Thomas recognized that it is Christ" — "the marks in his hands" ([[John 20.28|John 20:27]]) — and to temple recognition: "that's how the father on the other side of the veil identifies you and blesses you." See [[Guardians of Sacred Space and the Password Motif]] and [[Knowing Christ Means Bearing His Image Not Performing Works]]. *Rating: a typological link; the Thomas parallel (recognition by hand-marks) is a real Johannine motif.*[^2]
3. **Esau as the non-wicked forfeiting firstborn.** Unlike other displaced-firstborn stories (Reuben, Cain), "Esau doesn't seem like he's a super bad dude — he's just a regular guy," which the hosts say "makes way more sense that this is a story about Christ" giving us his birthright; "the mess of pottage represents our weak attempts," and Christ says in effect "I'll take the mess of pottage, you take the birthright." See [[Two Brothers Where the Younger Is Chosen Are Types of Christ and Lucifer]]. *Rating: an inversion of the usual reading (Jacob the supplanter) into a Christ-gives-birthright type; creative but coherent within the motif.*[^3]
4. **Why the story is "strange" without it.** The hosts note Jacob/Esau breaks the usual pattern (the displaced firstborn is normally *wicked*), and the temple/Christ reading resolves the anomaly — "I can finally make sense of that weird story." *Rating: a real narrative puzzle; the temple reading is offered as its solution, not proven by it.*[^2]

**Steelman of the skeptical counter.** Genesis 27 reads straightforwardly as a *deception* narrative: Rebekah and Jacob trick a blind, dying Isaac to steal Esau's blessing — the goat hair is a disguise, the hand-feeling a ruse that *succeeds because Isaac is fooled*, not a sacred recognition that *succeeds because Jacob is worthy*. Reading blindness as "the veil" and the marks as Christ's wounds imports the temple and the Passion into a text about familial fraud, and the "Esau is innocent / Christ gives his birthright" inversion runs against the narrative's own framing (Esau is wronged; Jacob must flee). **A Latter-day Saint reply:** the deception layer is real, but the *form* of the scene — a son identified at a veil by the marks on his hands and blessed by the father — is a striking template that recurs (Thomas; temple recognition), and reading the non-wicked forfeiting elder as a Christ-type is offered as typology layered on the plain sense, held provisionally per [[Avoiding Parallelomania Between Ancient Rites and LDS Temple]].
