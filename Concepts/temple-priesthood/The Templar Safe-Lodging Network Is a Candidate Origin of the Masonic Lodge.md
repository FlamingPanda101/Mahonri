# The Templar Safe-Lodging Network Is a Candidate Origin of the Masonic Lodge

## The Claim
Following John J. Robinson's *Born in Blood*, [[Mike Day]] and [[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)]] present the **Knights Templar** as one candidate origin of Freemasonry: the Templars ran a medieval pilgrim banking / safe-lodging network in which a traveler proved his identity at each lodge with a **name, a token, and a password** — a three-part authentication echoed in Masonic degrees, and read as a journey of ascent toward the Temple Mount under guarded passwords.

## Supporting Evidence
1. **Pilgrim banking by name, token, and password (rated: Robinson's reconstruction).** Rather than carry gold to Jerusalem and risk robbery, a pilgrim deposited it at a lodge in (say) Paris and received "a token… a name with a word associated with it and a sign"; at each lodge en route he gave his name on a paper, showed a sign, and spoke a password — "three forms of authentication" — for a small percentage fee. The hosts note this is "really the roots of our banking system… and our PIN numbers."[^1]
2. **Ascent to the Temple Mount (rated: the hosts' framing).** "When people are making their journey they are trying to ascend to the Temple Mount… and on the way they have to give certain passwords and codes in order to keep things precious safe until they get there" — paralleling the Masonic (and temple) motif of guarded passage. See [[Guardians of Sacred Space and the Password Motif]].
3. **Lodges and Templar names in the degrees (rated: circumstantial).** The word *lodge*, and the use of Templar/monastic names and figures (e.g. Jacques de Molay) in the higher degrees and in the youth order, are cited as Templar residue in Masonry.[^2]
4. **The 1307 suppression and flight to Britain (rated: history + reconstruction).** When King Philip IV and Pope Clement V suppressed the Templars in 1307 (the hosts' "Order 66" analogy), a forewarned British contingent escaped to Britain/Scotland — never subject to the Inquisition — and blended into local society and the operative stonemasons' guilds, a two-streams confluence (speculative gentlemen + operative masons) that Robinson uses to bridge Templars to Freemasonry.[^3]
5. **Earlier-than-1717 attestation (rated: documentary).** Against the standard 1717 founding date, the hosts cite Elias Ashmole's diary recording his being made a Mason c. 1646 — pushing speculative Freemasonry back at least to the early-mid 1600s and into the "murky" period where a Templar inheritance is conceivable.[^4]

**Steelman of the critic.** The Templar-origin thesis is a popular but **minority and largely circumstantial** reconstruction; mainstream historians (e.g. Steven Bullock's academic history of early American Freemasonry) derive the fraternity from medieval/early-modern **stonemasons' guilds** evolving into "speculative" lodges, with no documented institutional line from the Templars (suppressed 1307) to organized Freemasonry (first Grand Lodge 1717). The hosts themselves repeatedly concede "I don't know, I wasn't there" and "you can never be 100% certain with a secret society." The banking-network facts are historical; the Templar-to-Mason continuity is unproven legend.

## Best Quotes
> "We establish a lodge in Paris and we give them our gold and then we get a token… and we travel, and on the way to Jerusalem there are many lodges where we get lodging… do you have a name on this paper… and they ask for a certain sign… so there's like three forms of authentication."[^1]

> "Of course all this is speculative as far as did the Masons come out of this. It's not speculative that the Templars existed… but where are the connections?"[^3]

## LDS Connection
For the Latter-day Saint argument this matters because it shows Freemasonry's *own* origin is murky and may itself trace to a Temple-Mount-guarding order with an ascent-and-password motif — undercutting the assumption that Masonry is a fixed, late, purely-modern invention from which Joseph simply copied. Combined with [[Temple Origins Run Deeper Than Freemasonry]] and [[Ordinance Creation Recontextualizes Ordinary Actions]], it reframes Masonry as one late, fragmented carrier of an older temple pattern rather than the source of the endowment.
