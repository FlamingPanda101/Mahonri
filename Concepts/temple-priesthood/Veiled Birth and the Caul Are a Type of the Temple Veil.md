# Veiled Birth and the Caul Are a Type of the Temple Veil

#type/claim

## The Claim
The Ward Radio women's panel (presenter **Tia**, with **[[Britney Ward Radio|Britney]]**, **Megan**, **Joy**) argues that human birth is a built-in **type of the temple**, and the strongest single peg is the **amniotic sac as a veil**. The membrane surrounding the baby — the "bag of waters" — is naturally rent when a woman's water breaks; and in the rare case where it does *not* break, the child is said to be born **"in the caul," a "veiled birth."** The panel reads this medical vocabulary as independent confirmation that the protective membrane is properly a *veil*: just as Christ's death **rent the veil of the temple** ([[Matthew 27.51|Matthew 27:51]]) to admit worshippers into the presence of the Father, a mother's "veil" is rent to deliver a child into the world. The sacrament cloth, the temple veil, the bag of waters, and the burial veil are read as one nesting symbol of passage between worlds. The three birth elements — **water, spirit, blood** — are mapped onto [[Moses 6.60|Moses 6:60]] ("by the water... by the Spirit... by the blood").[^1][^2][^3]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **"Caul / veiled birth" is real obstetric vocabulary.** A birth with the amniotic sac intact is genuinely called being born "in the caul" or "en caul," and "veiled" language attaches to it in folk and some clinical usage. So the claim that the membrane is *independently* called a veil is checkable and substantially correct. *Rating: the lexical datum is accurate; the typological inference (caul-veil = temple-veil) is the interpretive step.*[^3]
2. **The rent-veil parallel is structurally exact.** [[Matthew 27.51|Matthew 27:51]] has the temple veil torn top-to-bottom at Christ's death, opening access to the presence of God (the theology of Hebrews 10:19-20). A membrane that must rupture to bring new life into a new world is a tight structural rhyme — the same "barrier rent → passage into a new presence" shape. Coheres with [[The Veil Is Christ Reversing the Day of Atonement]]. *Rating: a genuine and apt parallel within the temple-veil cluster.*[^3]
3. **Water/Spirit/blood has a scriptural anchor.** [[Moses 6.60|Moses 6:60]] and 1 John 5:8 both group water, Spirit, and blood; the panel's mapping onto the amniotic water, the spirit received in the womb, and the mother's delivering blood is a controlled (not free) association because the triad is already a scriptural unit. *Rating: the triad is textual; the birth-mapping is devotional but disciplined.*[^2]
4. **It extends an existing vault motif rather than inventing one.** The vault already documents the veil as a *traversable* boundary marking sanctity ([[The Veil Marks Sanctity Not Shame and Is Meant to Be Traversed]]) and birth-through-the-veil imagery ([[The Narrow Gate to Life Is Birth Through the Wall of Thorns and Thistles]]); the caul claim adds a concrete physiological instance to the same symbol set. *Rating: additive, non-duplicative.*
5. **It reframes the women's temple veil as fitting, not demeaning.** In a companion episode, Tia presses the same caul/veiled-birth peg toward a pastoral payoff: since a woman literally carries a "veil" within her body and her veil is *rent* to deliver a child (just as the temple veil was rent at the crucifixion, [[Matthew 27.46|Matthew 27:50–51]]), "who [is] more fitted to wear the symbol of the veil than the woman." The episode plays a clip of a woman who felt "erased," "faceless," a "white blob" while veiled; the argument recasts the veil as a marker of sacred, Godly presence behind it — not erasure (cf. [[The Veil Marks Sanctity Not Shame and Is Meant to Be Traversed]] and the priesthood-veil symbolism of [[We Wear the Divine Name - The Tetragrammaton on the High Priest and the Garment]]). *Rating: a constructive reframe, not a proof; a critic notes it does not fully answer why* only *women were veiled.*[^4][^5]

**Steelman of the skeptical counter.** This is **typology, not derivation**. The amniotic sac is named a "caul"/"veil" by ordinary metaphor (any thin covering gets called a veil), so its sharing a word with the temple veil is **homonymy, not a hidden revelation** — the same membrane is called a "bag of waters" with no temple resonance at all. A critic notes the panel itself hedges everything as devotional ("you could go for years studying the symbolism of the veil") and leans on a near-universal human experience that *any* religious tradition could allegorize toward its own rites; finding "the temple" in it is reading the conclusion into the data. The capstone — that **[[Joseph Smith]] was born in the caul** — is explicitly sourced to a viewer comment and folk stories, not a record, and the panel flags it as such. **A Latter-day Saint reply:** the claim is offered as edifying *pattern-recognition*, not proof; its modest, checkable core (the obstetric "veiled birth," the genuinely torn temple veil, the scriptural water/Spirit/blood triad) is sound, and the convergence of so many birth-words onto temple themes is presented as catechesis for the already-endowed, not as evidence that would compel an outsider.
