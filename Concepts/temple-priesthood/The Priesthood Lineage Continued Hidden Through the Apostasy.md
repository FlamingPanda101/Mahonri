# The Priesthood Lineage Continued Hidden Through the Apostasy

## The Claim
[[Scott Woodward|Woodward]] and [[Casey Paul Griffiths|Griffiths]] highlight the closing verses of [[D&C 86]] (vv. 8-11) as teaching priesthood "in a way we're typically not used to talking about it." The Lord tells Joseph Smith that "the priesthood hath continued through the lineage of your fathers" — the *legal right* to the eternal priesthood order was preserved through the bloodline of the covenant faithful, "probably unbeknownst to them," all the way down through the centuries of the Great Apostasy. The implication: even when apostolic keys were taken to heaven, "His work never paused during what we call the apostasy. It continued in stride just in some different ways than we're used to talking about."[^1]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **The text says the priesthood "remained" through lineage (rating: strong; textual).** D&C 86:8-9: "with whom the priesthood hath continued through the lineage of your fathers — for ye are lawful heirs, according to the flesh, and have been hid from the world with Christ in God." The right to the priesthood "must needs remain through you and your lineage until the restoration of all things."[^1]
2. **It echoes the Abrahamic covenant (rating: moderate; intertextual).** Verse 11 ("a light unto the Gentiles, and through this priesthood, a savior unto my people Israel") restates the Abrahamic promise that through Abraham's seed all nations would be blessed (Abraham 2). The priesthood is "like a branch of the family of Abraham," spread through covenant posterity across the world during the apostasy.[^1]
3. **Lineage plus agency (rating: moderate; theological).** The hosts stress this is not deterministic: a blessing promised to an ancestor must still be *chosen* by the descendant (the example of Alma the Younger, rebuked "because your mom and dad are really concerned about you," who then "didn't have to" repent but did). Adoption into Israel grants the same standing as literal descent.[^2]

## Opposing View (Steelmanned)
A traditional Christian objects that this is unfalsifiable genealogical theology with no historical anchor: an invisible "legal right" silently transmitted through unknown bloodlines for ~1,700 years cannot be evidenced or tested, and conveniently underwrites Joseph Smith's own claim to be a "lawful heir." It also sits in tension with the stronger LDS claim that the priesthood *keys* were genuinely lost — if the priesthood "remained," why was a restoration of authority (Aaronic and Melchizedek priesthood via John the Baptist, Peter-James-John) necessary at all?

## Counter-Objection
The note's own distinction answers the tension: what "remained" is the *lawful right/heirship* to the priesthood (a latent covenant entitlement spread through Israel's seed), not the *operative keys and authority*, which were taken to heaven and had to be restored by angelic ministration. The two are complementary — the Restoration confers the authority upon those who already held, by covenant lineage, the *right* to receive "all the covenant promises." On falsifiability, the claim functions theologically (an account of how God's covenant purposes persisted) rather than as an empirical genealogy; its payoff is the broader thesis that "he never abandoned his people, ever," reframing the apostasy as continuity-with-hidden-preparation (see [[The Apostasy Was the Church Driven Into the Wilderness Not Annihilated]]) rather than a closed heaven.

## Best Quotes
> "The legal right to belong to the eternal holy priesthood order was preserved through the lineage of the covenant faithful, probably unbeknownst to them, down through the centuries, even in the midst of what we call the Great Apostasy."[^1]

> "His work never paused during what we call the apostasy. It continued in stride just in some different ways than we're used to talking about."[^1]
