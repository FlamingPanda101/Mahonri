# Blood of Zechariah — Perpetual Boiling Blood

## The Claim
The "blood of Zacharias" Jesus names is [[Zechariah son of Jehoiada]], stoned in the temple court ([[2 Chronicles 24.20-21|2 Chronicles 24:20-21]]); apocryphal tradition holds his blood **boiled in the temple for ~200 years** as a sign of the temple's defilement.

## Supporting Evidence
- Canonical: Zechariah, filled with the Spirit, rebukes Judah and is stoned "in the court of the house of the LORD" *(2 Chronicles 24)* [^1].
- Mishnah Sanhedrin 96b: when [[Nebuchadnezzar]] entered, he saw Zechariah's blood still boiling (uncalmed for ~200 years); he killed priests until it stilled *(rabbinic tradition)* [^2].
- The *Lives of the Prophets*: after Zechariah's murder the priests "no longer saw any angels... the altar was not lit" — the temple **lost its sanctity** *(apocryphal source)* [^3].
- He may be the messianic prophet of [[Zechariah 9.9|Zechariah 9:9]], killed for that very preaching [^4].

## Best Quote
> "He saw the blood of Zachariah the priest boiling... it had not been calmed since he was killed in the temple, for 200 years, sitting there boiling as a reminder that the temple was defiled." — [[Jonah (Plain and Precious Publishing)|Jonah]], paraphrasing Sanhedrin 96b [^2]
