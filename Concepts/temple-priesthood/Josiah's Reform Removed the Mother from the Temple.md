# Josiah's Reform Removed the Mother from the Temple

## The Point
[[Mandy Green]] frames the loss of the divine feminine as a concrete historical event: a shift from a first-temple religion that included the Mother to a reformed, law-centered cult that purged her. Beginning "about the time of Isaiah, King [[Hezekiah]]" and solidifying under [[Josiah]], the reform burned the sacred groves, removed the tree from the Holy of Holies, and "totally removed" the Mother from the temple.

## Evidence
- **Two diverging temple traditions.** "After about the time of Isaiah, King Hezekiah, we start to have these two different traditions emerge." The first temple is "Melchizedek priesthood... about ascension... about returning to the presence of God... about the sons and daughters of God, and involves the mother, the menorah, the tree" in the Holy of Holies (the *debir*).[^1]
- **The Mother burned out of the cult.** The reform "really solidifies with the reign of King Josiah, who's 12 at the time. Sacred groves — because trees are sacred to her — and they burn this holy tree. She's totally removed from the temple."[^2] (On the goddess's tree iconography see [[Asherah Pillar Iconography Depicts Wisdom Who Gives Sight]].)
- **From "why" to "how": law over ascension.** What replaces her is "the law, but it's not the law of the Elohim, it's the how. The law increases almost 500%. It becomes about Aaronic priesthood, because that goes through family lines. We're trying to consolidate power."[^3] Melchizedek priesthood "can be given to anyone, as demonstrated by Abraham," whereas the reform shifts worship from "temples all over the land" to a centralized "temple at Jerusalem."[^4]
- **The Exodus/Moses elevation.** Green notes a corresponding "massive push for the law and for Moses": in Exodus the Sabbath becomes "less of a why and... that prohibitive how," even as figures like [[Philo of Alexandria|Philo]] "still knew about wisdom and the lady."[^5]

## Best Quotes
> "The first temple... is Melchizedek priesthood. It's about ascension. It's about returning to the presence of God... and involves the mother, the menorah, the tree is in the holy of holies."[^1]

> "It really solidifies with the reign of King Josiah, who's 12 at the time. Sacred groves, because trees are sacred to her, and they burn this holy tree. She's totally removed from the temple."[^2]

## LDS Connection
This is the same reform that LDS apologists cite for the Book of Mormon's pre-exilic theology — see [[Hezekiah Josiah and the Deuteronomist Reforms]] and [[Deuteronomistic Reform Explains the Book of Mormon Theology]] — but read specifically for the Mother. If a Heavenly Mother, the Tree of Life, and Melchizedek-priesthood ascension were deliberately edited out of Israel's temple just before the exile, then the Latter-day Saint restoration of Heavenly Parents and a temple of ascension recovers exactly what the reformers suppressed, corroborated by the archaeology of Asherah worship in [[Feminine Divine in Ancient Near Eastern Worship]] and the survival of the Mother under the title Wisdom in [[Wisdom Personified as the Mother in Heaven]].
