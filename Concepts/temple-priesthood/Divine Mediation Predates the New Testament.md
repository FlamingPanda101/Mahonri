# Divine Mediation Predates the New Testament

## The Claim
[[Dan McClellan]] argues that **divine mediation was not new to the New Testament**. The phenomenon of an agent who acts and speaks as God appears "in the early narratives of the Hebrew Bible," "was further developed in Greco-Roman period Judaism," and "is something that we see consolidated in Jesus in the New Testament." [^1] Therefore the church did not *need* the philosophical Trinity to explain Jesus — a ready-made, indigenous model already existed and is even rationalized within rabbinic Judaism. [^2]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The Hebrew Bible has a divine agent who acts as God.** McClellan points to "an agent who talks as God and does what only God is supposed to be able to do" — the messenger of Adonai of [[Exodus 23.20-21|Exodus 23:20-21]], who holds authority over the forgiveness of sins "because my name is in him." [^3] See [[Name-Bearing Agency in Exodus 23]]. *Rating: strong; the "angel of YHWH" texts (Gen 16, 22, 31; Exod 3, 23; Judg 6, 13) are a recognized scholarly crux of identity-yet-distinction.*

2. **Second Temple Judaism developed mediator figures.** "Greco-Roman period Judaism" elaborated divine intermediaries — e.g., personified Wisdom, the Logos in Philo, exalted-patriarch and principal-angel traditions (Metatron, the Son of Man of 1 Enoch). *Rating: strong; this is the well-known "two powers" / divine-agency scholarship (Hurtado, Boyarin, Segal, Bauckham debates).* *Steelman:* scholars like Richard Bauckham argue that NT writers go *beyond* mere mediation to include Jesus within the unique divine identity, which is precisely what later Trinitarianism formalized — so mediation may be the seed *of*, not the alternative *to*, high Christology.

3. **Even rabbinic Judaism tried to rationalize it.** "We even see attempts to rationalize it within rabbinic Judaism." [^4] *Rating: defensible; cf. the rabbinic "two powers in heaven" heresy discussions, which presuppose the tradition existed and needed policing.*

4. **The model best fits the NT's diversity.** "This is what best explains what's going on with the different ways to account for Jesus's relationship to God in the New Testament." [^4] *Rating: a strong claim; its force depends on the comparative argument against the Trinity (see [[Trinity Is Fundamentally Contradictory]]).*

## Best Quotes
> "Divine mediation was not new to the New Testament. Divine mediation was already something that we see in the early narratives of the Hebrew Bible. It was something that was further developed in Greco-Roman period Judaism. It is something that we see consolidated in Jesus in the New Testament." [^1]

> "We have an agent who talks as God and does what only God is supposed to be able to do. And God says in Exodus 23 that this agent, the messenger of Adonai, has authority over the forgiveness of sins because my name is in him." [^3]

> "This is what best explains what's going on with the different ways to account for Jesus's relationship to God in the New Testament, but they didn't know about the cognitive sciences." [^4]
