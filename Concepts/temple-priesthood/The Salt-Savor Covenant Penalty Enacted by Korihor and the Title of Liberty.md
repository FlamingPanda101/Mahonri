# The Salt-Savor Covenant Penalty Enacted by Korihor and the Title of Liberty

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)|David Butler]] reads "ye are the salt of the earth; but if the salt have lost his savour . . . it is . . . cast out, and . . . trodden under foot of men" ([[Matthew 5.13|Matthew 5:13]]) not as a discipleship metaphor but as a **covenant penalty** built into the [[The Sermon on the Mount Is an Ordinance in Three Rooms|temple-ordinance]] sequence: positioned right after the entry requirements, it warns that violating the covenants brings a death-sanction "by being cast out and trodden underfoot of men." [^1] His evidence is that the *same fate* is narrated twice in the Book of Mormon. **Korihor** is, after losing his confrontation with Alma, literally "cast out, and trodden under foot . . . even until he was dead" ([[Alma 30.27|Alma 30]]) — which, Butler argues, reveals Korihor to be **not a secular intellectual but an apostate covenant man** who dies the covenant penalty. [^2] And in the **Title of Liberty** ([[Alma 46.21-22|Alma 46]]), Moroni's people cast their rent garments under their feet and swear they will be "trodden under foot" if they transgress — "deliberately enacting the covenant penalty." [^3]

## Supporting Evidence (steelman)

1. **The verbal match is exact and doubled.** "Cast out . . . trodden under foot" appears in Matthew 5:13 and recurs *verbatim* in both Alma 30 (suffered) and Alma 46 (sworn). A phrase appearing as the *outcome* of apostasy in one place and the *self-malediction* of an oath in another behaves like a fixed covenant formula, not a coincidence of imagery. *Rating: a strong intratextual pattern.*

2. **It re-reads Korihor more coherently.** The standard "Korihor = village atheist" reading leaves his gruesome trampling-death unexplained; "Korihor = apostate covenant-breaker suffering the stipulated penalty" gives the narrative detail a reason. Alma 30's own talk of mysteries and angels supports reading Korihor inside a covenant frame. *Rating: genuinely improves the fit of an otherwise odd detail.*

3. **Alma 46 is explicitly a sworn self-malediction.** The Title-of-Liberty oath is, on its face, a "may it be done to me as to this garment" rite — the people *say* the penalty over themselves while *performing* the trampling. That the text spells out the enactment is hard to read as accidental. *Rating: the clearest of the three data points.*

**Steelman of the skeptical counter.** "Salt that loses its savor is thrown out and trampled" is an ordinary agrarian image, and "trodden under foot" is stock biblical idiom for defeat/contempt (Isa 28:3; Mic 7:10; Mal 4:3) — so its recurrence in Alma needs no covenant-penalty machinery; a 19th-century author steeped in the KJV would reach for that phrase naturally. The Korihor re-reading is an inference (the text never calls him a covenant initiate), and Alma 46's garment-oath, while a real self-malediction, is a *political* loyalty covenant, not evidently a temple penalty. A Latter-day Saint reply: the force is cumulative — it is the *conjunction* of (a) the verse sitting exactly where a penalty clause belongs in the ordinance sequence and (b) two independent Book-of-Mormon scenes pairing the identical phrase with covenant-making/breaking that is better explained by a shared ritual formula than by stock idiom alone.
