# The Endowment of Power Recapitulates Luke-Acts and Pentecost

#type/claim

## The Claim
Historian [[Jonathan Stapley]] argues that the Latter-day Saint temple "endowment of power" was, from the start, a *self-conscious reenactment* of the biblical pattern in Luke–Acts: Jesus tells the disciples to "tarry in Jerusalem until you are endowed with power from on high" ([[Luke 24.49|Luke 24:49]]), fulfilled at Pentecost in Acts. Before the church had bishops, apostles, or presidents, Joseph Smith revealed that the Saints must gather to Ohio because "there you will be endowed with power from on high" — biblically literate members recognized the Luke-Acts language and expected a Pentecostal outpouring so they could go preach to all nations.

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. The phrase is lifted from Luke-Acts (strong)
"Endowed with power from on high" is the exact language of [[Luke 24.49|Luke 24:49]] (and the commission of Acts 1). Stapley notes "Luke-Acts" are two volumes of one work: the Great Commission ("take this gospel to every nation, kindred, tongue, and people") is paired with the command to *wait* in Jerusalem for empowerment first. The early Church of Christ consciously cast itself into these New Testament characters.[^1]

### 2. The Kirtland outpourings were understood as the fulfillment (strong)
Early members anticipated, and after the Kirtland temple's dedication reported, Pentecostal phenomena — visions, speaking in tongues, prophecy — after which they left on missions to preach worldwide, recapitulating the Acts sequence (empowerment → mission).[^2]

### 3. The pattern is "a self-conscious recapitulation" (medium)
Stapley is explicit that this is not coincidental parallel but deliberate reenactment: the Saints "see themselves in these characters from the New Testament" and stage the biblical empowerment as a lived rite.[^1]

## Steelman of the Opposing View
A critic objects that the *modern* temple endowment — a dramatic liturgy with covenants, priestly robes, washings, anointings, and a creation-fall-return narrative — bears little resemblance to the Pentecost of Acts 2 (a one-time outpouring of the Spirit with tongues of fire). Quoting "endowed with power" does not make a developed Nauvoo ceremony *the* thing Luke described; the connection is a verbal hook retrofitted onto an evolving ritual whose form Joseph Smith was still revealing as the buildings were being built.

## Counter-Objection
Stapley's framework actually *grants and explains* the developmental gap: the Kirtland "endowment of power" (Pentecostal outpouring + washings/anointings drawn from the priestly consecration of Leviticus) and the later Nauvoo endowment (the dramatic liturgy, sealing cosmology, men and women) are *successive stages* of one project — materializing the heavenly vision on earth — not a single static rite claimed to be identical to Acts 2. The biblical recapitulation names the *theological logic* (empowerment for mission, restoring an apostolic pattern), while the ceremonial *form* developed over time. See [[An Ordinance Must Change to Stay the Same]] and [[Ordinance Meant Law Not Liturgy When Joseph Smith Said Ordinances Cannot Change]].
