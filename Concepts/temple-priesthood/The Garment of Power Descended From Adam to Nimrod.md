# The Garment of Power Descended From Adam to Nimrod

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Todd McLauchlin]] relays an **apocryphal tradition** (he names *Jubilees*-type sources) that the holy **garment of the priesthood** made for [[Adam]] and Eve in Eden ([[Genesis 3.21|Genesis 3:21]]) was passed down as a physical relic of priestly power: to [[Enoch]] (those who saw Enoch saw the garment and knew it was "a key to ascension"), then to [[Noah]] — and that when Noah's sons "beheld his nakedness" ([[Genesis 10|Genesis 9]]) it was because the folded garment lay beside him, which [[Ham]] **stole**. [^1] The garment passes Ham → Cush → **[[Nimrod]]**, who becomes the "great hunter" *only because the garment makes the animals come to him* (as they came to Noah) — but he uses it to **spread death**, the archetype of the antichrist. [^2] Nimrod uses the garment to try to replicate [[Enoch]]'s city by building the Tower of Babel ([[Genesis 10|Genesis 10:8-10]]), even (in the legend) corresponding with the still-living Noah for a blessing. [^3] The garment is finally recovered into the Abrahamic line when **Esau** — "a great hunter" returning with the garment — trades his birthright for a "mess of pottage," so it descends to Israel. [^4] The payoff: the brother of Jared and his people come *out of this Babel/Nimrod world* (their valley is literally named "Nimrod," [[Ether 2|Ether 2:1]]), which frames the [[The Brother of Jared Models the Endowment Ascent Through the Veil|brother of Jared's veil ascent]] as a man recovering *true* priesthood from a corrupted counterfeit. [^5]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **A garment-of-Adam relic tradition genuinely exists in the apocrypha.** Jewish legend (preserved in midrash, *Pirqe de-Rabbi Eliezer*, and rabbinic retellings of Genesis Rabbah) does narrate the "garments of skin" passing from Adam to Noah, being taken by Ham/Nimrod, and tied to Nimrod's hunting prowess — so McLauchlin is reporting a *real* strand of tradition, not inventing it. *Rating: the tradition is attested, though its provenance is legendary, not canonical.* [^1]
2. **The Nimrod-as-mighty-hunter hook is in Genesis itself.** [[Genesis 10|Genesis 10:8-9]] calls Nimrod "a mighty hunter before the LORD" and links him to Babel — a textual anchor the legend elaborates. *Rating: the canonical seed is real; the garment explanation is extra-canonical.* [^2]
3. **The Esau/birthright recovery is a known midrashic motif.** Some Jewish traditions do connect Esau's "despising" of the birthright (Gen 25) to Nimrod and the garment, so this is part of the same legendary corpus rather than McLauchlin's free invention. *Rating: attested in the tradition.* [^4]
4. **It coheres with the vault's tower material.** [[The Tower Was a Temple Recontextualized From an Older Story]] already treats Babel as a counterfeit temple; the garment genealogy supplies a relic-level mechanism for that counterfeit, and ties into [[The Garment Is the Covering of Christ]] (the Eden garment as priestly investiture).

**Steelman of the skeptical counter.** This is a **chain of apocryphal legends, not history or scripture**, and McLauchlin says so ("I should have prepped this... I think it's in Jubilees," "if someone says you're up in the night, fine"). The details are *not* uniform across the sources, the *Joseph-and-Aseneth*/Jubilees/PRE traditions are late and folkloric, and threading them into a single continuous relic-history smooths over real textual gaps (e.g., Genesis never says Ham stole a garment; the "nakedness of Noah" has its own contested exegesis). Treating the legend as background to the *Book of Mormon* (Ether's valley of Nimrod) risks lending canonical weight to non-canonical lore. A measured reply: the claim is explicitly offered as *apocryphal pattern*, valuable for the **type** it preserves — a holy garment as transmissible priestly power, and a counterfeit "hunter" order that inverts it — and that type illuminates the canonical data it touches (Genesis's "mighty hunter," Eden's God-made garments, Ether's "Nimrod") without requiring anyone to treat the legend as fact.

## Best Quotes
> "That garment is then passed down to Noah... when they beheld Noah's nakedness... they saw the garment folded up next to him, and Ham steals the garment. This is the garment that was made for Adam and Eve in the garden — it's the garment of power." [^1]

> "Nimrod has the great garment... in the apocryphal account, this is why Nimrod's considered a great hunter, because he puts on the garment and all the animals come to him... and Nimrod is the archetype of the antichrist, because he's using it to spread death." [^2]

> "When Esau sells his birthright for a mess of pottage... it's because he's coming back with the garment and he trades it for the food, and that's how it gets back into the lineage of Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob." [^4]
