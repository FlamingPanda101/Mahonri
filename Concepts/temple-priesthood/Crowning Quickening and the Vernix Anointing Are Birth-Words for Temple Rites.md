# Crowning Quickening and the Vernix Anointing Are Birth-Words for Temple Rites

#type/claim

## The Claim
Beyond the veil/caul, the Ward Radio panel collects a cluster of **English (and Hebrew) "birth-words"** they argue double as temple vocabulary:

1. **Naming and clothing.** The newborn is **given a name** (not self-chosen; in the Bible often announced by an angel) and **clothed for the first time** — paralleling the temple's bestowal of a new name and the clothing of the initiate.[^1]
2. **The vernix as an anointing oil.** Babies are born coated in **vernix caseosa**, an oily, antimicrobial skin-protectant that modern practice leaves on rather than washing off — read as the mother's own production of a whole-body **anointing oil**, paralleling the whole-body anointing of the initiatory.[^2]
3. **Crowning.** The emergence of the baby's head is **"crowning,"** a word that also denotes coronation and "highest achievement" — read against the temple promise that initiates become kings and queens.[^3]
4. **Quickening.** Archaically, **"quickening"** is the moment the mother first feels the child move; scripture speaks of God "quickening" understanding, and *quicken* also denotes being made alive / translated / resurrected — so a "mother-word" underlies the language of spiritual enlivening.[^4]
5. **Opened eyes.** A baby opens its eyes to see for the first time only outside the womb; [[D&C 110|D&C 110:1]] joins veil and sight — "the veil was taken from our minds, and the eyes of our understanding were opened."[^5]

The unifying thesis: the body's own birth process is a lexicon for the ordinances — name, garment, anointing, coronation, quickening, opened eyes.

## Supporting Evidence
1. **The etymologies are largely real.** *Quicken* genuinely means "to make alive" (archaically the first felt fetal movement), *crowning* is genuine obstetric and coronation vocabulary, and *vernix* is a real antimicrobial coating now often left intact. So the word-data the panel builds on is checkable and mostly accurate. *Rating: the lexical facts are sound; the temple-mapping is interpretive.*[^2][^3][^4]
2. **[[D&C 110|D&C 110:1]] does pair veil and opened eyes.** The "eyes of our understanding were opened" / "veil... taken from our minds" pairing is in the text, so the eyes-opened-at-birth parallel is licensed by a real scriptural collocation, not invented. *Rating: the scriptural anchor is exact.*[^5]
3. **Whole-body anointing is a genuine temple/early-Christian feature.** The vault documents [[Baptismal Washing and Anointing in Early Christianity|early-Christian washing and anointing]]; reading the vernix as a natural whole-body unction extends a real rite, and the initiatory's whole-body anointing (vs. head-only elsewhere) is the panel's point of contact. *Rating: the rite is real; the vernix-as-oil reading is a devotional analogy.*[^2]
4. **Name + clothing are core initiatory elements.** The new name and the clothing of the initiate are uncontested features of the endowment; the newborn parallel is one-to-one in structure. *Rating: structurally tight; the inference that birth *encodes* the rite is the leap.*[^1]

**Steelman of the skeptical counter.** Every item here is **analogy harvested in English** (and a little Hebrew), which is the classic shape of **parallelomania** the vault itself warns against ([[Avoiding Parallelomania Between Ancient Rites and LDS Temple]]). "Crowning," "quickening," and "vernix-as-oil" resonate with temple themes only because the panel is *looking* for temple themes; the words carry no liturgical content of their own, and most are accidents of English (the temple text is not composed in English). A critic notes the reasoning is unfalsifiable — any feature of birth can be assigned a rite — and that the panel repeatedly flags its own associative, non-scholarly mode. **A Latter-day Saint reply:** the cluster is offered as *devotional mnemonics* for the endowed, not as historical-linguistic argument; its checkable substrate (the real meanings of *quicken*, *crowning*, *vernix*; the genuine [[D&C 110|D&C 110:1]] veil/sight pairing; the real whole-body anointing) is accurate, and the value claimed is catechetical resonance, not proof — exactly the modest register in which it is presented.
