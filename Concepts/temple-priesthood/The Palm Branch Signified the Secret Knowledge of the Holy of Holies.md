# The Palm Branch Signified the Secret Knowledge of the Holy of Holies

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Margaret Barker]] argues that in later temple tradition a **palm branch** was a **token of initiation** — a sign that its bearer "had been entrusted with the secret knowledge of the holy of holies." [^1] She introduces it to explain why [[Deborah]], the "great lady," sits *under a palm tree* in [[Judges 4|Judges 4:5]]: the palm marks her as one who holds the inner-sanctuary knowledge. [^1] The payoff is a re-reading of **Palm Sunday**: the crowd waving palms and crying *hosanna* were not generically cheering. ***Hosanna* means "save us," not "hurray"** ([[Zechariah 9.9|Zechariah 9:9]]; Ps. 118:25), and the palm-wavers were "people who remembered the significance of what he was doing" — riding in as the king to **cleanse the temple** and "save us from this lot" (the corrupt leadership) "up in the temple." [^2] In other words, those who greeted Jesus with palms were signaling that *they recognized the initiate-king coming to purge the sanctuary* — which is why, Barker notes, the authorities "would have taken fright," and "we know the end of that story." [^3]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **The palm is a real sacred/royal-victory symbol.** Palms appear in [[Revelation 7.9-14|Revelation 7:9]] (the white-robed multitude "with palms in their hands"), in temple architecture (1 Kings 6:29 carved palms), and at Sukkot (the *lulav*), so reading the palm as a charged cultic/royal token rather than a mere festive prop is well-grounded. *Rating: the symbol's sacred weight is solid; "token of holy-of-holies initiation specifically" is Barker's sharper claim.* [^1]
2. **"Hosanna = save us" is straightforwardly correct.** The cry transliterates Hebrew/Aramaic *hôšîʿâ-nāʾ* ("save now," Ps. 118:25), so Barker's gloss is accurate, and it reframes the triumphal entry as a *plea for deliverance* tied to the temple, not a coronation parade. *Rating: lexically sound.* [^2]
3. **The welcome → temple-cleansing sequence is in the Gospels.** Jesus enters Jerusalem to palms and *immediately* moves on the temple (Matt 21; Mark 11), so connecting the palm-welcome to the cleansing is textually tight — the same sequence the vault notes in [[The Hosanna Shout and Handkerchief Reenact the Palm Branches]] and [[Cleansing of the Temple]]. *Rating: strong narrative anchor.* [^2]
4. **It coheres with Barker's Deborah reading.** The palm under which the "lady of the torches" judges ([[Deborah and Asenath Preserve the Lady of the Bush]]) becomes legible as an initiation marker, tying the divine-feminine thread to the temple-knowledge thread. *Rating: internally consistent within her framework.* [^1]

**Steelman of the skeptical counter.** That a palm branch was a *technical token of holy-of-holies initiation* is **Barker's reconstruction, not a documented institution** — the biblical palms are more naturally explained as **royal-victory and festal (Sukkot/lulav) symbolism** (greeting a victor/king, as at 1 Macc 13:51; John 12:13's "branches of palm trees"), which requires no esoteric initiation meaning. Deborah's "palm tree of Deborah" reads most plainly as a **landmark/judgment-seat location**, not a credential. And while *hosanna* does mean "save us," inferring that the crowd specifically grasped a temple-cleansing, anti-establishment program (rather than hoping for political/Roman deliverance) goes beyond what the texts say — indeed the Gospels suggest the crowd's messianic expectations were *misdirected*. So the chain "palm = inner-sanctuary secret → Palm Sunday crowd were initiates" stacks an unattested symbol-meaning onto a plausible misreading of the crowd's intent. A measured reply: Barker offers this as temple-tradition reconstruction, and even the cautious reader can grant the *anchored* parts — palms carry real cultic/royal weight, *hosanna* is a salvation-plea, and the entry leads straight into the temple cleansing — while treating the "initiation token" gloss as the interpretive layer; the LDS interest is in the *pattern* (palms + hosanna + temple purification), which the canonical sequence supplies regardless.
