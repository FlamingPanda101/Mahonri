# Priesthood Keys Frame the Seven Dispensations

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Caleb Williams (Godwin)|Godwin's]] illustration argues visually that **priesthood keys are the structural backbone of all of human history**, organized as seven dispensations. The plate descends from Christ — "the great high priest over all," the source of all priesthood — to [[Adam]] (the Ancient of Days), who receives the first keys and will return them at the end of time, then to [[Melchizedek]] and Aaron, Moses, Moroni, Elias and [[Elijah]], and finally to [[John the Baptist]] and [[Peter]], James, and John, who deliver the Aaronic and Melchizedek keys into the latter-day dispensation. [^1] A globe in the image bears **seven keyholes** and Joseph holds a **seven-sealed scroll** (Revelation 5), each seal corresponding to a dispensation whose presiding head was "committed keys": Adam, Enoch, Noah... down to the last days. "The story of the priesthood is the story of humanity from the very beginning to the very end... and those are linked." [^2]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Each dispensation head delivers literal keys.** The hosts stress that "all of the figures here are mentioned at some point by Joseph Smith as having given him keys over their stewardship or over their dispensation," giving the abstract word "keys" (which readers "skip over") a concrete referent. [^3] *Rating: internally consistent with LDS scripture* — most of these conferrals are named in the Doctrine and Covenants.

2. **A specific, citable example: Moroni and the record.** [[D&C 27.5|D&C 27:5]] names Moroni as the one "to whom I have committed the keys of the record of the stick of Ephraim" — keys tied to the *ancient record itself*, not merely to its translator. [^4] This anchors the "keys per dispensation" pattern in a particular verse rather than a generality.

3. **The Kirtland conferrals as the hinge.** [[D&C 110]] (Elijah, Elias, Moses, and the Lord at the Kirtland Temple) supplies the latter-day "gathering" of keys the plate depicts converging on Joseph. Note the LDS distinction the hosts assume — **Elias and Elijah as two figures**, not one — which is idiosyncratic to the Restoration. *Rating: characteristic LDS reading*, contested by other Christian traditions that treat *Elias* as simply the Greek form of *Elijah*.

4. **The seven seals = seven dispensations reading.** Tying Revelation's seven-sealed scroll and the opening of the seventh seal to seven dispensations is a recognized LDS interpretive move (cf. D&C 77). *Rating: a tradition-specific typology*, not a consensus exegesis of Revelation.

**Steelman of the skeptical counter.** A biblical scholar will object that Revelation's seven seals are an apocalyptic literary device about judgment unsealing a single scroll, with no first-century notion of "seven dispensations of priesthood keys"; the dispensational grid is a later systematization read *back into* the text. Likewise, "keys" as a transferable institutional commodity handed from Adam through a relay of prophets is a Restoration construct, not something the Hebrew Bible articulates. The honest LDS position is that this is *revealed structure* (D&C 27, 110, 128), offered as a theology of history rather than a claim derivable from Revelation by grammatical-historical exegesis alone — and that its payoff is coherence: it explains why authority can be *lost* (see [[Apostasy Was Complete When the Keys Departed]]) and *restored* (see [[Brigham Young Saved the Church Through the Keys]]) as a single continuous story.

## Best Quotes
> "The story of the priesthood is the story of humanity from the very beginning to the very end... and that those are linked. All of these figures are part of that story, step at a time, laid out from the beginning and prophesied from the beginning." [^2]

> "The keys over the Book of Mormon — that there are priesthood keys tied to the ancient record, and that those were given to him by Moroni." [^3]

> "Around the globe... you've got these keyholes, and there are seven of them representing also the seven dispensations of time. And for each dispensation, there are keys that were committed to the head of that dispensation." [^5]
