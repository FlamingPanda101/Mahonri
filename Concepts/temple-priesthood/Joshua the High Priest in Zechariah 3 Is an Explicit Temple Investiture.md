# Joshua the High Priest in Zechariah 3 Is an Explicit Temple Investiture

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Andrea Woodmansee]] argues that [[Zechariah 3|Zechariah 3:1-10]] is "one of the most explicit temple things" in the Old Testament — a full **investiture-at-the-veil** scene that Joseph Smith could not have assembled from scratch. The high priest **Joshua** (Hebrew *Yeshua*, the name of Jesus) stands before the angel of the Lord with **Satan resisting at his right hand**; he is **stripped of filthy garments**, his iniquity removed, **reclothed** in "change of raiment," **mitred**, then **warned** by the angel to walk in the Lord's ways and keep his charge, with the promise that he shall "**judge my house**" and walk among "**those that stand by**" (read as the divine council). The chapter ends with the **BRANCH** ([[Isaiah 11.1|Isaiah 11:1]]), a **stone with seven eyes**, and "**every man under the vine and under the fig tree**" — read as tree-of-life and eternal-increase imagery at the threshold of the holy of holies.[^1][^2]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The vesting sequence is in the text.** Zechariah 3:3-5 has Joshua stripped of filthy garments, told "I have caused thine iniquity to pass from thee," reclothed with "change of raiment," and given a "fair mitre" — a clean investiture sequence. Woodmansee: "this is one of the most explicit temple things that I've read." Coheres with [[The Garment Is the Covering of Christ]]. *Rating: the vesting is explicit and uncontested in the text; the temple-ordinance reading is natural here.*[^1]
2. **Satan resisting at the threshold.** "Satan standing at his right hand to resist him" — Woodmansee notes Satan appears in temple settings (Eden; here) "to try to stop your progress towards the Lord," with the high priest "on his way to the veil." Coheres with [[Guardians of Sacred Space and the Password Motif]]. *Rating: Satan as adversary at the priest's investiture is in v.1; the "on his way to the veil" staging is the overlay.*[^2]
3. **Joshua = Yeshua = the Name taken in the temple.** Woodmansee stresses "Joshua is actually in the name of Jesus... Yeshua," and that "when we are going through the temple we are taking on the name of Yeshua." See [[We Wear the Divine Name - The Tetragrammaton on the High Priest and the Garment]]. *Rating: the name identity (Yeshua/Joshua/Jesus) is correct; the temple-name application is the inference.*[^3]
4. **"Protest" = "repeat/warn."** Where the KJV says the angel "protested unto Joshua," Woodmansee (looking up the Hebrew root) reads it as "to return, to repeat, to do again" — so "the Lord repeated unto Joshua," reframing the angel as *warning/instructing* him to approach worthily, not protesting. *Rating: the Hebrew *‘ud* (Hiphil) does carry "testify/admonish/warn," so "warn" is defensible over "protest"; a reasonable lexical point.*[^4]
5. **The BRANCH, stone, and fig tree as holy-of-holies symbols.** The closing "my servant the BRANCH" ([[Isaiah 11.1|Isaiah 11:1]]), the seven-eyed stone, and "every man under the vine and fig tree" are read as Christ (the Branch / tree of life), the foundation stone, and the seed-bearing fig (eternal increase) "right before the holy of holies." *Rating: the messianic Branch is standard; the fig-as-increase reading is devotional.*[^5]

**Steelman of the skeptical counter.** Zechariah 3 is a *post-exilic vision* about the rehabilitation of the Second-Temple priesthood (Joshua/Jeshua son of Jehozadak) and the promise of the Davidic "Branch" (Zerubbabel-era hope) — its reinvestiture imagery is real but addresses *that* historical restoration, and reading it as a template for the modern LDS endowment is anachronistic retrojection. The "Joshua = Jesus" point, while linguistically true, proves only that they share a common Hebrew name, not that the chapter encodes a Christian temple rite. **A Latter-day Saint reply:** the claim is not that Zechariah *predicts* the LDS ceremony but that the *pattern* (adversary at the threshold → stripping of filth → reclothing → mitre → charge → admission among the standing ones → Branch/tree) is a genuine ancient investiture form that the restored ordinance recovers — and the candor that Joseph Smith "is not reading Zachariah and putting it together" frames it as cumulative evidence, held within the vault's [[The First Temple Contained the Endowment Pattern Barker Reconstructed Independently|first-temple]] case, not as proof from a single chapter.
