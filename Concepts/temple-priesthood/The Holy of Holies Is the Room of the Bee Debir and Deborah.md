# The Holy of Holies Is the Room of the Bee Debir and Deborah

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Dave Butler|David Butler]] and [[Mike Day]] argue that the **holy of holies** is, by Hebrew wordplay, "**the room of the bee**." The innermost temple room is the *debir* (D-B-R); the **honeybee** is *d'vorah* / **Deborah** (also D-B-R); and the prophetess **Deborah** in Judges 4 sits "under the palm tree of Deborah" on a mountain, where people "came up to her for judgment" — mirroring Jesus seated on the mount in Matthew 5-7, approached for judgment. So the *debir* is the bee's place: the room of **manna** (which "tasted like honey"), kept in the ark, and the goal of the journey "to the land of milk and honey." The Jaredites carrying **deseret** (the honeybee) and "[[Ether 2.3|seeds of every kind]]" echoes God in the holy of holies beckoning "everyone to come." This is the *debir* layer of [[Lehis Journey Maps the Tabernacle Not Solomons Temple]] and [[Eden Means Bountiful So Lehis Journey Ends in the Holy of Holies]].[^1][^2]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Debir / d'vorah / Deborah share the consonants.** Mike Day: "the bee is Deborah... it's *d'vorah*" — the same D-B-R as *debir*, the holy of holies. So "the holy of holies is in some sense the room of the bee." *Rating: the consonantal overlap (d-b-r) is real; *debir* and *d'vorah* share the root letters, though they derive from different senses ("back/innermost" vs. "bee/word"). The pun is genuine; the *identification* is the interpretive move.*[^1]
2. **Deborah in Judges 4 mirrors Jesus on the mount.** Judges 4:4-5: "Deborah, a prophetess... dwelt under the palm tree of Deborah... in mount Ephraim: and the children of Israel came up to her for judgment" — the bee-woman, under the bee-tree, on a mountain, approached for judgment, paralleling Matthew 5:1 / Matthew 7 (Jesus sits on the mount, people approach). Butler also notes "Lapidoth" (Deborah's epithet) means "torches / flashing lights." *Rating: the Judges 4 details (tree, mountain, judgment) are textual; the Matthew parallel is the synthesis.*[^2]
3. **Honey = manna in the ark = milk-and-honey goal.** Honey signals manna ("tasted like honey," kept in the ark, Hebrews 9:4), and "the land of milk and honey" (leaving Egypt/*Mitzraim*, "the straight places," for the room with milk and honey) is the holy of holies in the Exodus narrative. Connects to [[Epiousios Is Yahweh Bread Not Daily Bread]]. *Rating: manna-in-the-ark is biblical; "milk and honey = holy of holies" is the typological extension.*[^3]
4. **The Jaredites carry the bee and seeds.** [[Ether 2.3|Ether 2:3]]: they carry "deseret, which... is a honey bee" and "seeds of every kind" — read against God in the holy of holies wanting "seeds of every kind" (everyone) to come. Butler ties **deseret** (via Hugh Nibley) to "the red [feminine] land," and the bee to kingship (Egyptian royal bee). *Rating: the deseret-bee gloss is from the Book of Mormon text itself; the Nibley etymology and royal-bee point are secondary support.*[^4]

**Steelman of the skeptical counter.** Hebrew *debir* (innermost sanctuary) and *d'vorah* (bee) are *homographs by accident of the consonantal script*, not cognates — they come from unrelated roots — so "the holy of holies is the room of the bee" is a *pun*, not an etymology, and puns prove resonance to a willing reader, not authorial intent. Deborah-under-the-palm in Judges 4 is a judge holding court at a landmark tree; reading it as a temple-mount judgment scene parallel to Matthew is a stretch built on the bee homograph. The Jaredite "deseret = honeybee" gloss is in the text, but tying it to the *debir* requires the homograph chain. **A Latter-day Saint reply:** Hebrew wordplay is a *known and pervasive* device of the biblical and Nephite authors (the vault documents many cases), so a pun linking the bee, the innermost room, and the prophetess-judge is exactly the kind of intentional resonance these writers favored; the Judges-4/Matthew parallel (tree + mountain + people approaching for judgment) is independently striking, and the cluster coheres with the manna/milk-and-honey holy-of-holies imagery. Held as suggestive typology, provisional per [[Avoiding Parallelomania Between Ancient Rites and LDS Temple]].
