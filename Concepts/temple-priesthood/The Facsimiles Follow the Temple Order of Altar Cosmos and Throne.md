# The Facsimiles Follow the Temple Order of Altar Cosmos and Throne

## The Claim
[[Kerry Muhlestein]] argues that Joseph Smith's three facsimiles, taken together and in the order he placed them in the [[Book of Abraham]], reproduce the **three-stage ascent pattern of the ancient temple**: **altar → cosmic dome (the heavens) → throne of God**. Crucially, Facsimiles 1 and 3 were on the *same* papyrus while Facsimile 2 (a hypocephalus) was on a *different* one — yet Joseph interleaved all three into a sequence that matches the temple progression. Muhlestein, who was simultaneously writing a scholarly book on Egyptian temples, calls this one of his most compelling reasons to believe.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. The temple has a fixed three-stage structure (rated: moderate, scholarly)
In the ancient tabernacle/temple you pass **altar → the holy place (the "cosmic dome," leaving this world and traveling the cosmos) → the throne of God**. Muhlestein independently finds the same altar/zodiac-cosmos/throne sequence in post-Temple Jewish synagogue mosaic floors (e.g. an altar or Akedah scene, then a zodiac, then a throne scene) — evidence the pattern is "really kind of the basis of at least Jewish temples."[^1]

### 2. Joseph's ordering matches it (rated: moderate)
"Think of Facsimile 1 — altar. Facsimile 2 — cosmic dome. Facsimile 3 — throne. You put all three together ... Joseph puts the three together in the book of Abraham in the order that matches temples." That he did so across two separate papyri is the part Muhlestein finds hard to attribute to guesswork.[^1]

### 3. The facsimile content is itself temple-themed (rated: moderate)
Muhlestein notes the hypocephalus (Facsimile 2) Egyptologically concerns creation, purity in the afterlife, and eternal procreation — "all temple themes" — converging with Joseph's explanation that "this is about the temple."[^1]

## Steelman of the Opposing View
A critic answers: (a) altar/heaven/throne is a near-universal sacred-architecture gradient (it appears in many traditions), so a three-item set can be mapped onto it after the fact without implying ancient design; (b) Joseph's *sequencing* of the facsimiles into Abraham 1–3 could simply follow his own narrative needs, and the "two different papyri" point shows the grouping is *Joseph's editorial choice*, not an inherited unit; and (c) the synagogue-floor parallel is centuries *post*-Temple and itself debated, so it cannot straightforwardly date the pattern to Abraham. The LDS reply is that this is offered as one of several *converging* lines (Muhlestein rates it spiritual/temple-revelatory, not a knock-down proof), and that the convergence of an independently-reconstructed temple pattern with Joseph's arrangement is striking even if not decisive.

## Best Quotes
> "Facsimile 1 altar, Facsimile 2 cosmic dome, Facsimile 3 throne. You put all three together — even though Facsimile 1 and Facsimile 3 were on the same papyrus and Facsimile 2 was on a different papyrus — Joseph puts the three together in the order that matches temples." [^1]
