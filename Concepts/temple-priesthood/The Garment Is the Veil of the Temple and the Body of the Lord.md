# The Garment Is the Veil of the Temple and the Body of the Lord

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Dave Butler]] argues that the high priest's robes were **made to match the veil of the temple**, so that to put on the priestly garment is to "**put on the body of the Lord**." [^1] The chain:
- On the Day of Atonement the high priest emerges wearing robes that **match the temple veil**, bearing the divine Name on his forehead ([[Exodus 28]]; cf. [[We Wear the Divine Name - The Tetragrammaton on the High Priest and the Garment]]); "it seems to be the case that wearing the veil of the temple means putting on the body of the Lord." [^1]
- This decodes why, when **Jesus dies, the temple veil tears** ([[Matthew 27.46|Matthew 27:51]]). Butler rejects the common Protestant reading ("a sign the temple is no longer relevant"): rather, *for centuries high priests had been coming out wearing the veil — to be the Lord* — and "when the real Lord had come, and his body was destroyed, the veil of the temple was broken as a sign of Jesus's identity" as the great High Priest. [^2]
- Hence the temple **veil itself is symbolic of Christ's body**, the way "through the flesh" into God's presence ([[Hebrews 10.19-22|Hebrews 10:19-20]], "through the veil, that is to say, his flesh"). A 2024 First Presidency teaching that the **garment "reminds us of the veil in the temple," and "that veil is symbolic of Jesus Christ,"** lands exactly here: putting on the garment is putting on a symbol of the body of the Lord (one more layer beside *covering* and *name-bearing*). [^3]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **The veil-as-flesh equation is explicitly biblical.** [[Hebrews 10.19-22|Hebrews 10:20]] says believers enter "by a new and living way, which he hath consecrated for us, **through the veil, that is to say, his flesh**." So Scripture itself identifies the temple veil with Christ's body — the load-bearing premise is not LDS-specific. *Rating: strong; it is in the text of Hebrews.* [^3]
2. **The high-priestly robes and the veil share materials and motifs.** Both the veil (Exod 26:31, blue/purple/scarlet with cherubim) and the high priest's ephod/robe (Exod 28) use the same tri-colored fine-twined linen, so "the robes match the veil" is a recognized observation in scholarship (Barker, and others), not a stretch. *Rating: well-grounded.* [^1]
3. **The torn-veil reading is exegetically defensible.** [[Matthew 27.46|Matthew 27:51]]'s veil-tearing *at the moment of Jesus' death* invites a Christological reading; tying it to the High-Priest-Christ theme (rather than only to "temple obsolete") fits Hebrews' whole argument that Christ is the true high priest who enters once for all. *Rating: a serious, defensible interpretation.* [^2]
4. **It complements, not duplicates, the other garment notes.** Covering ([[The Garment Is the Covering of Christ]]), name-bearing ([[We Wear the Divine Name - The Tetragrammaton on the High Priest and the Garment]]), skin→light ([[The Garment of Skin and the Garment of Light]]), and now **veil = body of the Lord** are four distinct biblical warrants for one object. *Rating: additive.*

**Steelman of the skeptical counter.** Two over-reaches. First, the **modern garment is an undergarment**, whereas the veil-symbolism in Hebrews concerns *Christ's flesh* and the high priest wore visible *outer* robes — so "wearing the garment = putting on the body of the Lord" is a *thematic* extension, and the link from the Aaronic veil-robes to the 1842 LDS garment is asserted, not demonstrated (the same continuity gap flagged in the vault's other garment notes). Second, the **torn-veil verse is genuinely ambiguous**: many scholars read [[Matthew 27.46|Matthew 27:51]] as signifying *access opened* (the way into the Holy of Holies now available to all) or *judgment on the temple*, and Butler's "sign of Jesus's identity as High Priest" is one reading among several, not the consensus. A measured reply: the *core* identification (temple veil = Christ's flesh) is **stated outright in [[Hebrews 10.19-22|Hebrews 10:20]]**, so it is not speculative; and the LDS application is modest — the garment *reminds of* the veil, which Scripture and the First Presidency both tie to Christ — so even granting the access/judgment readings of the torn veil, the symbolic equation the claim depends on is biblically secured.

## Best Quotes
> "The high priest's robes that he puts on the Day of Atonement... match the veil of the temple. He comes out — he is wearing the veil of the temple. And it seems to be the case that wearing the veil of the temple means putting on the body of the Lord." [^1]

> "This is why when Jesus dies the temple veil tears. It's not... a sign the temple is no longer relevant. No — it is telling you that Jesus was the great high priest... and when his body was destroyed, the veil of the temple was broken as a sign of Jesus's identity." [^2]

> "Your garment of light is the veil of the temple, because in some sense you are putting on the body of the Lord as part of gaining access to the atonement." [^2]
