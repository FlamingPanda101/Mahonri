# Calling and Election Is the Oath of the Father Sealed in the Endowment

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Todd McLauchlin]]'s "lightning-strike" thesis is that [[D&C 84.33-44|D&C 84]] **teaches about** the oath and covenant of the priesthood but is not itself the oath and covenant — and that the oath and covenant *is* the **temple endowment**, "an endowment of priesthood." [^1] The reason the chapter feels "clunky" when people try to recite it as the oath and covenant is that no verse ever says "this is the oath and covenant"; it describes one. [^2] The actual oath and covenant is the sealed culmination of the endowment — the **calling and election made sure** and the **second comforter**, "the oath from the Father's mouth that you'll have eternal life," which McLauchlin identifies as "the great oath." [^3] On this reading, *ordination* is only the *calling* into the order ([[D&C 95|D&C 95:5-6]] equates "ordained" with "called": "many are ordained… but few are chosen"), granting merely the *right to officiate* in outward ordinances; the "making sure" — the oath proper — is ritualized across the endowment from the initiatory through the **second anointing**, the sealing and fulfillment. [^4][^5] So the stake leader who told a young McLauchlin he would lose forgiveness "if you don't do your home teaching" misread [[D&C 84.33-44|D&C 84:41]]: turning from *that covenant* (the sealed election) is what forfeits forgiveness, because it is sonship sealed by the Father. [^6]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The text genuinely never labels itself.** McLauchlin's strongest move is exegetical and checkable: [[D&C 84.33-44|D&C 84:33-44]] speaks *of* "the oath and covenant which belongeth to that priesthood" (v. 39-40) in the third person — "*this* is according to the oath and covenant" — rather than constituting it. Reading the passage as a *description* of a covenant fulfilled elsewhere relieves a real interpretive strain. *Rating: a clean, text-based observation.*

2. **D&C 95 really does equate ordination with calling.** "There are many who have been **ordained** among you, whom I have **called**, but few of them are chosen" ([[D&C 95|D&C 95:5-6]]) maps ordination → calling, leaving "chosen" (elect/sealed) as a further step. This dovetails with [[D&C 121.32|D&C 121:34]] ("many are called, but few are chosen"). *Rating: a defensible synthesis of two priesthood revelations.*

3. **"Calling and election made sure" is a scriptural and Latter-day Saint category.** The phrase is Petrine (2 Peter 1:10) and was taught by Joseph Smith as a real sealing of exaltation; identifying it with [[D&C 84.33-44|D&C 84]]'s oath is a development, not an invention. [[Mosiah 5.7|Mosiah 5:7]] ("the Lord… will seal you his") and Alma the Younger's experience supply Book-of-Mormon analogues. *Rating: grounded in the tradition's own vocabulary.*

4. **It reframes the "lose your salvation over home teaching" misuse.** Treating the forfeiture clause (v. 41) as the breaking of a *sealed election* rather than a missed assignment is both pastorally healthier and a better fit for the gravity of "neither in this world nor the world to come." *Rating: a coherent and humane reading.*

**Steelman of the skeptical counter.** A critic — and a careful Latter-day Saint reader — can object that the official, canonical heading and common usage simply *call* [[D&C 84.33-44|D&C 84:33-44]] "the oath and covenant of the priesthood," so relocating the "real" oath and covenant into the temple (and especially into the rarely-discussed second anointing) is an esoteric overlay that the plain text does not require; the passage's promises ("all that my Father hath") can be read as the ordinary covenantal fruit of faithful Melchizedek ordination without any sealed-election machinery. McLauchlin himself repeatedly hedges ("forgive me in advance if I get dogmatic"), and the move risks making the everyday priesthood covenant feel second-class. A measured reply: the reading does not *deny* the conventional label, it *deepens* it — it takes seriously that the chapter is self-described as a temple text (gathering to Zion, "the place of the temple," vv. 2-4) and that its escalating promises terminate in receiving "the Father's kingdom," language the endowment stages — so the temple reading is an enrichment of, not a contradiction to, the standard view. Compare [[The Endowment Is Learning Heaven Not Earning Heaven]].

## Best Quotes
> "The oath and covenant of the priesthood as talked about in D&C 84 is talking about the oath and covenant, it's teaching about the oath and covenant, but it itself is not — but it's not the oath and covenant." [^2]

> "The highest oath is the oath from the Father's mouth that you'll have eternal life. That is the great oath. We call it sometimes the calling and election made sure… the second comforter." [^3]

> "When you're ordained, you're being called or you're being initiated into the order… it doesn't give you the fullness of that priesthood yet. You're in the process of endowment." [^4]
