# "For God's Glory" Defense of Hell — Rebutted

## The Defense Being Rebutted

When asked why God allows the eternal torture of souls, some Christians reply:
**"For God's glory."** Eternal damnation displays God's attributes of justice
and mercy, redounding to his greater glory.

### Historical Expressions

**John Calvin**:

> "God pre-ordained for his own glory and the display of his attributes of
> mercy and justice, a part of the human race without any merit of their own
> to eternal salvation, and in another part, in just punishment of their sin,
> to eternal damnation." [^1]

**Thomas Aquinas**:

> "In order that the happiness of the saints may be more delightful to them
> and that they may render more copious thanks to God for it, they are allowed
> to see perfectly the sufferings of the damned." [^1]

## The Rebuttal

### 1. Glory Through Vile Means Is Not Glory

> "Is a glory that is obtained through wrath and vengeance really glory at
> all? Does a glory achieved through such vile means really justify the moral
> monstrosity of hell? At what point does this glory that is being obtained
> through such vile means become a vice rather than a virtue?" [^2]

### 2. The Contingency Problem

Ask yourself: **Could this glory have been obtained without the damnation of
these souls?**

- **If NO**: Then God's glory is **entirely contingent on certain external
  outcomes** — which is incompatible with the classical theist notion that God
  is a *necessarily* perfect and glorified being. A God who *needs* the
  suffering of billions to achieve his glory is not self-sufficient.
- **If YES**: Then why would he obtain this glory through the eternal torture
  of billions of souls? Choosing the cruelest possible means when gentler
  alternatives are available makes God gratuitously cruel. [^2]

### 3. Aquinas: Saints Enjoying the Suffering of the Damned

The Thomistic notion that the saved will derive *greater happiness* from
witnessing the eternal torture of the damned is singled out as particularly
morally appalling. The host notes: "I'm not even really sure what to make of
such an objection... primarily because such an attempt to rationalize such a
horrible concept is often quoted without any further reflection." [^1]

### 4. Practical Apologetic Advice

> "Either way, this defense is morally wrong and should be absolutely
> abandoned if we want to ever convince anyone to participate in the
> Christian message." [^3]
