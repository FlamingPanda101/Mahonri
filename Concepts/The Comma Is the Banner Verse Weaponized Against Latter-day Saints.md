# The Comma Is the Banner Verse Weaponized Against Latter-day Saints

#type/claim

## The Claim

The stakes of the [[1 John 5-7 - Johannine Comma Interpolation|Comma Johanneum]] are not merely academic. [[1 John 5.7|1 John 5:7]] is the *single verse* most often produced to prove that Latter-day Saints, who reject the co-equal Western Trinity, are therefore "not real Christians" — and that label, the panel argues, licenses the centuries-old Christian treatment of "heretics": "What do you do with heretics according to Christian tradition? You burn them. You crush them. You stamp them out." [^1] The hosts name a contemporary lineage of evangelical figures — [[Mark Driscoll]], Mike Winger, Apologia Studios, [[James White]], [[Jeff Durbin]] — who "have been declaring the heresy of Mormonism," and tie that drumbeat to the **Grand Blanc, Michigan church shooting**, whose perpetrator reportedly said Latter-day Saints are the Antichrist. [^2] The argument: a proof text that is itself a scribal fraud is being used to dehumanize a religious minority, and "they have killed people in the name of this fraudulent verse." [^3]

The hosts are careful to firewall this from a general attack on Christianity: "It doesn't mean Christianity is false. It doesn't mean Jesus wasn't real... It's just don't persecute people for trinitarianism. You can think we're wrong. That's fine. But you can't think we're crazy." [^4]

## Supporting Evidence

**The argument structure.** This is a *tu quoque* fused with a moral-consequences appeal: (1) the Comma is a demonstrable interpolation (granted even by critical scholarship — see [[1 John 5-7 - Johannine Comma Interpolation]]); (2) it functions as the boundary-marker that defines LDS out of "Christian"; (3) that boundary-policing has a body count, historically (the wars and executions over trinitarian orthodoxy) and recently (Grand Blanc). Therefore the burden of "adding to / subtracting from the word of God" falls back on the accuser. The hosts make the inference explicit: "If they were willing to shamelessly insert this verse to defend the Trinity, then where are their priorities really? Are they really in the word of God or in the word of the creeds?" [^5]

**The steelman of the side being criticized.** An honest defender of the historic position would object on two fronts, and the note should grant both. *First*, naming specific living apologists (Winger, White, Durbin, Driscoll) in the same breath as a mass shooting risks a guilt-by-association fallacy: none of those figures endorse violence, and the actions of a deranged gunman are not a fair indictment of their theology — the link is rhetorical, not causal. *Second*, the logical point stands independent of the polemic: even a forged proof text does not by itself disprove the Trinity, which trinitarians derive from the wider canon (the Johannine prologue, Matthew 28:19, the Pauline benedictions); see [[Trinity Is an Interpretation Not Explicit in Scripture]]. The defensible residue of the panel's argument is narrower but real: *if* the only near-explicit textual warrant is a known interpolation, then certainty strong enough to brand outsiders as damned heretics is unwarranted, and the "you add to scripture" charge is hypocritical when leveled by the tradition that printed the Comma. That residue connects to the vault's standing analysis of how doctrinal labels do social work — [[Anti-Mormonism Is Attacking the Person Not the Idea]], [[Trinity as Identity Marker and Costly Signal]], [[Equal Treatment Mistaken for Persecution]].

**The forgiveness coda.** Notably, the hosts close not on grievance but on a Christian duty to forgive the animosity "which we have to forgive as Christians, and that's something difficult to do... baby steps." [^6] This blunts the charge that the segment is merely retaliatory.
