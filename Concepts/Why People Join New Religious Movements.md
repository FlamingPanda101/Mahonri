# Why People Join New Religious Movements

## The Claim
People do not join NRMs because they are weak, gullible, or brainwashed. They join through **normal processes** to meet real, usually **existential, identity-level needs**.

## Supporting Evidence
- Converts were overwhelmingly **middle-class, educated** young people (often off university campuses) — which undercuts the "weak and vulnerable" stereotype *(empirical)* [^1].
- Disproportionately from **secular** backgrounds (notably very secular Jewish homes) — people with a sense of identity but little practice, who had an unmet spiritual void *(empirical)* [^2].
- People reach unusual positions "through very normal processes — curiosity, inquisitiveness, doing their own research" — and stay because the ideas meet a need, especially for **certainty and meaning** in a confusing world *(scholar)* [^3].
- The deepest needs are **existential**: meaning, value, significance — feeling part of something with positive impact *(scholar)* [^4].

## Best Quote
> "The fools and fanatics are always certain of themselves and the wiser people are full of doubts." — Bertrand Russell, quoted approvingly in the interview [^5]

## Counter-arguments / related
- Replaces the discredited brainwashing model: [[Brainwashing Does Not Exist]].
- Echoes Christ's warning that drawing toward truth can set one at variance with family: [[Matthew 10.34-36|Matthew 10:34-36]].
