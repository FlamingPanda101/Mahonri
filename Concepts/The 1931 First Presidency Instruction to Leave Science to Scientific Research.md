# The 1931 First Presidency Instruction to Leave Science to Scientific Research

## The Point
When a doctrinal fight over evolution and "death before the Fall" escalated all the way to the top of the Church in 1931 — driven largely by Apostle Joseph Fielding Smith on one side and the scientist-Apostles James E. Talmage and John A. Widtsoe on the other — the **First Presidency under Heber J. Grant refused to settle the science**. Their formal ruling did two things: (1) it declared that **neither** the existence of pre-Adamites **nor** their non-existence, and **neither** death before the Fall **nor** its absence, is "a doctrine of the Church"; and (2) it instructed the Brethren to *"leave Geology, Biology, Archaeology and Anthropology, no one of which has to do with the salvation of the souls of mankind, to scientific research."* This is the clearest internal precedent for the modern "no official position." [^1]

## Evidence
1. **The 1931 adjudication declared neutrality both ways.** Talmage recorded that the First Presidency ruled "that obviously the doctrine of the existence of races of human beings upon the earth prior to the fall of Adam was *not* a doctrine of the Church; and further that the conception... that there were no such Preadamite races, and that there was no death upon the earth prior to Adam's fall is *likewise* declared to be no doctrine of the Church." Neither side won; both speculations were ruled non-doctrinal. [^2]
2. **The instruction handed the empirical questions to science.** President Grant and counselors "urged leaders not to take sides on the issue, requesting in 1931 that they 'leave Geology, Biology, Archaeology and Anthropology, no one of which has to do with the salvation of the souls of mankind, to scientific research, while we magnify our calling in the realm of the Church.'" [^3]
3. **It defused the controversy by design.** Widtsoe, watching from England as a mission president, wrote that he understood "the Presidency had ruled on the matter, and that was the end of the controversy as far as I was concerned." The First Presidency treated the dispute as one to be *closed off as non-doctrinal*, not adjudicated on the merits. [^4]
4. **The principle had a salvation-relevance test built in.** The phrase "no one of which has to do with the salvation of the souls of mankind" is the operative logic: questions that do not bear on salvation are released to "scientific research." This is the institutional form of the [[Operation Science vs Origin Science|how-vs-why division of labor]]. [^3]
5. **It produced real intellectual freedom.** By 1934, after both statements and the 1931 ruling, Widtsoe could write privately that "the principle or law of evolution cannot be gainsaid," that he held the question of the human body's ancestry "in suspense" — and crucially, that he did so "not because of any fear of being in opposition to Church doctrine but candidly because existing facts do not satisfy my mind." He saw "no 'Church doctrine' around evolution that he could be opposing." [^5]

## Best Quotes
> "Leave Geology, Biology, Archaeology and Anthropology, no one of which has to do with the salvation of the souls of mankind, to scientific research, while we magnify our calling in the realm of the Church." — First Presidency memorandum, 1931 [^3]

> "Obviously the doctrine of the existence of races of human beings upon the earth prior to the fall of Adam was not a doctrine of the Church; and further... that there was no death upon the earth prior to Adam's fall is likewise declared to be no doctrine of the Church." — James E. Talmage's journal record, 1931 [^2]

> "I must hold my judgment with respect to the origin of man in suspense. I am doing this not because of any fear of being in opposition to Church doctrine but candidly because existing facts do not satisfy my mind." — John A. Widtsoe, 1934 [^5]

## LDS Connection
The 1931 instruction is the **bridge** between the early statements and the modern 2016 "the Church has no official position." It shows the First Presidency exercising a distinctly Latter-day Saint kind of restraint: **declining to make a binding doctrine out of an empirical question that does not touch salvation.** It also clarifies what "no death before the Fall" actually is in LDS terms — a *non-doctrinal* reading, not a required belief — which neutralizes one of the most common evolution objections leveled at the Restoration. The ruling embodies [[Doctrine Is Defined by the Unified Voice of the Highest Authorities|how doctrine is actually established]]: not by the loudest Apostle, but by what the unified First Presidency is willing to declare — and here they declared the science *open*.
