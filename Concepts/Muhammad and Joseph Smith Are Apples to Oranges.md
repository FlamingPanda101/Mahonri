# Muhammad and Joseph Smith Are Apples to Oranges

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
Critics argue: "Muhammad did the same thing with the Quran that Joseph Smith did with the Book of Mormon" — both were uneducated young men visited by an angel, directed to produce a book of scripture that founded a world religion.[^1] If Muhammad's production of the Quran requires no supernatural explanation, then neither does Joseph's Book of Mormon.

## The Rebuttal
1. **Method and timeframe are radically different.** The Book of Mormon was dictated in roughly two to two-and-a-half months; the Quran was "parcelled out" piecemeal over about 22 years of Muhammad's prophetic career.[^2] *(Strong — this is a basic, uncontested fact of both traditions' histories.)* The single most-repeated point Peterson and the host make is "Muhammad didn't write it down in two months. It didn't all come to him at once."[^3]
2. **Genre is different.** The Book of Mormon is a sustained narrative spanning roughly a thousand years, with embedded letters, sermons, and Isaiah quotations. The Quran is *not* a narrative at all — it is a series of discrete revelations arranged in (roughly) reverse-chronological order by descending chapter length.[^4] *(Strong — the structural contrast is verifiable by inspection.)*
3. **The Quran's only sustained story is borrowed and brief.** Its single narrative chapter is Surah Yusuf (chapter 12), retelling the biblical [[Genesis 37|Joseph in Egypt]] story; even the story of Abraham is scattered allusively across ~35 chapters, presupposing an audience that already knew the tales.[^5] *(Strong — Surah 12 and the allusive Abraham material are well known to Islamicists.)* This is the opposite of the Book of Mormon's self-contained, internally cross-referenced narrative.
4. **Steelman.** A critic can fairly respond that the *sociological* parallel still holds — both are unlettered founders of scriptural world religions, and the differences Peterson cites (length, genre, speed) are about the *texts*, not about whether the founding experience needs a supernatural cause. Peterson's reply is not that the parallel is zero, but that the specific claim "they did *the same thing*" collapses once you compare the actual books: the right LDS analog to the Quran is the [[The Quran Parallels the Doctrine and Covenants Not the Book of Mormon|Doctrine and Covenants]], not the Book of Mormon.[^4]
