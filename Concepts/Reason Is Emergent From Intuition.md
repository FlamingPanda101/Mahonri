# Reason Is Emergent From Intuition

## The Point

The host asks "is reason fundamental?" and answers no: reason is **emergent from a more basic intuitive system.** He grounds this on [[Jennifer Nagel]]'s account (via [[John Locke]]) that intuition is immediate, non-inferential judgment of abstract truths, and that even explicit demonstration is built out of intuitive single steps. Reason, on this view, is a "map" that organizes intuitions about the regularities of reality.[^1][^2]

## Argument

1. **Intuition is immediate, non-inferential judgment.** Nagel: intuition tells us *directly* that no round thing is square, or that a circle is not a triangle — not mediated by stages of thought, and about abstract/a-modal content (geometry, number, causation, justice, knowledge), not particular sense objects.[^1]
2. **Demonstration is intuition chained.** Locke's contrast: demonstration (e.g. that a triangle's angles sum to two right angles) goes through conscious steps — but "each individual step in a chain of demonstrative reasoning is, or at least should be, intuitive." 5×11=55 is intuitive; long division decomposes into single-digit operations that are each intuitive.[^2]
3. **Therefore reason rests on intuition, not vice versa.** If every step of reasoning bottoms out in an immediate intuitive judgment, then reasoning is not the most basic faculty; it is downstream of intuition. The host: "reason itself is actually emergent from more basic intuitions."[^3] *(Steelman: this tracks a real position — rationalist intuitionism from Locke onward treats self-evident intuitions as the warrant for inference; the claim that inference cannot generate its own first premises is well-motivated.)*
4. **Reason is a descriptive map.** The host reframes reason as "a sort of map that we use to describe these regularities that exist in the reality that we inhabit," with intuition interfacing directly with those regularities through experience.[^3] *(Caveat: "emergent" is doing double duty — Nagel/Locke's logical point that inference steps are intuitive is distinct from the host's developmental/psychological claim that reason grew out of intuition. He blurs the two.)*

## Best Quotes

> "Each individual step in a chain of demonstrative reasoning is, or at least should be, intuitive."[^2]

> "Reason itself is actually emergent from more basic intuitions."[^3]

> "Intuition is immediate judgment, not necessarily in the sense that it's very fast ... but in the sense that it's not mediated by stages of thinking the way reflection is."[^1]
