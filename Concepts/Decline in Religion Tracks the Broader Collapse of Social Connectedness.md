# Decline in Religion Tracks the Broader Collapse of Social Connectedness

## The Claim
Religious disaffiliation among Latter-day Saints is best explained not as a Church-specific scandal but as one expression of a general, multi-decade American decline in *all* social connectedness — civic, philanthropic, and religious — documented by Harvard political scientist [[Robert Putnam]] in *Bowling Alone*.

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Putnam's longitudinal connectedness curve (rated: strong).** Per Dyer's summary, connectedness was very low in the 1880s — "makes a lot of sense post-Civil War" — rose dramatically to a peak around the 1960s, then declined steadily. Putnam's recent update found the decline continuing. "All organizations were experiencing these kinds of declines in connectedness, in participation, and religion was just one of those things."[^1] *Steelman:* a critic could argue religion has fallen *faster* than bowling leagues, so subsuming it under generic decline understates a distinctly theological/credibility crisis; Dyer's reply is the comparative data showing LDS decline is milder than peer traditions (see [[LDS Retention Is Higher Than Most Other Christian Traditions]]).

2. **The LDS curve mirrors the national one (rated: strong).** "We find that we follow the same general trends — that there was a decline in connectedness to the church just like there was for almost every other religion."[^2]

3. **Fracturing over disagreement compounds it (rated: moderate, sociological).** Today, disliking a person's political opinion leads people to "distance" rather than coexist; the online world "would have you feel like you should disconnect from that individual," pulling people away from congregations that mix opposing views. See [[The Online Echo Chamber Accelerates Disaffiliation]].[^3]

4. **The cyclical pattern grounds hope (rated: moderate).** Because connectedness has risen and fallen before, "there is no reason to think that we're just going to keep going down." See [[Demographic Indicators Predict a Bright LDS Future]].[^4]

## Best Quotes

> "About 20 years ago, Harvard professor Robert Putnam wrote the book Bowling Alone... he noticed there was a decline in social connectivity across the board. Civic organizations, philanthropic organizations, religious organizations, all these organizations have been in decline for quite some time."[^1]

> "Religion hasn't been spared those general trends... we follow the same general trends."[^2]

> "As Robert Putnam says, we were low in the 1980s in terms of connectedness. We went up, now we're down. There is no reason to think that we're just going to keep going down."[^4]
