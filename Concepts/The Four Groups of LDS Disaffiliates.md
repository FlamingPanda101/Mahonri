# The Four Groups of LDS Disaffiliates (Objection Rebutted)

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
The stereotype — fueled by the most vocal ex-Mormon activists online — is that a typical former Latter-day Saint is liberal, progressive, secular, and atheist, having intellectually rejected the faith and now critiquing it publicly. The objection treats this loud minority as the representative profile.

## The Rebuttal

1. **A four-group cluster solution (rated: strong, statistical).** Using [[Ryan Burge]]'s Spiritual Seismology study (~100,000 respondents, with a good LDS subsample), Dyer's team applied statistical clustering to indicators of religiosity, stated reasons for leaving, and politics, identifying four groups: **faithkeepers, faith-leaners, faith-distant, and faith-rejectors.**[^1]

2. **Half remain warm toward faith (rated: strong).** Faithkeepers and faith-leaners together are "about 50% of those who left." They still feel warm toward Latter-day Saints, still value religion and spirituality, and "still believe in general that Jesus Christ is the Son of God."[^2] See [[Most Who Leave Drifted Away Rather Than Rejected Faith]].

3. **The vocal-atheist profile is a minority (rated: strong).** The faith-rejectors — lowest on rating religion/spirituality as important, most likely to think religion is harmful — are only "about 10% of the sample." This is the loud-online cohort, not the median leaver.[^3] See [[Faith-Rejectors and Adverse Childhood Religious Experience]].

4. **Geography of the warm leavers (rated: moderate).** Faithkeepers and faith-leaners were disproportionately *outside* Utah and Idaho, consistent with leaving via relocation rather than rejection.[^4] *Steelman:* clustering solutions are partly analyst-defined (number of groups, labels, thresholds), and "warm feelings" on a survey are not the same as practice — some faithkeepers may still be functionally gone even if affectively positive.

## Best Quotes

> "The statistics identified four different groups. And we labeled them as faithkeepers, faith-leaners, faith-distant, and faith-rejectors."[^1]

> "Those first two, the faithkeepers and the faith-leaners, that makes up about 50% of those who left. And they still feel actually pretty warm towards Latter-day Saints."[^2]

> "These are individuals — and that's about 10% of the sample — that is like zero on thinking religion is a good thing."[^3]
