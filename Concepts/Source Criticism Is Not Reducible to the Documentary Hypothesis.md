# Source Criticism Is Not Reducible to the Documentary Hypothesis

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)

The Word Room frames McClellan's reading of Genesis 2:4b–7 as a single complex sentence as merely "related to a belief in... the documentary hypothesis" — the theory that the Pentateuch is a compilation of (classically) four sources (J, E, D, P) stitched by a later editor. The implication is that the source-critical reading rises or falls with the Documentary Hypothesis (DH), which the apologist treats as a single, monolithic, and assailable target.[^1]

## The Rebuttal

1. **The DH is one theory, not all source criticism (factual correction).** McClellan: the DH "has certainly long been the dominant source critical theory of pentateuchal composition. However, it is not all source critical theories of pentateuchal composition. There are others and it's no longer the dominant position."[^2] Supplementary models, fragmentary models, and neo-documentarian variants coexist; some posit fewer than four sources, or P-vs-non-P rather than full JEDP.

2. **Configuration of sources ≠ "the Documentary Hypothesis" (taxonomic).** "Just because a theory involves J E D and P or maybe just D P and non-P or some other combination of sources doesn't make it the documentary hypothesis. There are other hypotheses out there."[^3] McClellan's own argument needs only that Genesis 1 and Genesis 2 are *distinct sources* (Priestly vs non-Priestly) — a far weaker and more broadly held claim than the full DH.

3. **Conflation is a diagnostic of secondhand knowledge (decisive for credibility).** Treating any J/E/D/P configuration as "the DH" is, McClellan argues, "a dead giveaway that they're not actually reading the critical scholarship." See [[Apologists Engage Pre-Digested Caricatures of Critical Scholarship]].[^3]

4. **Rate the evidence: strong.** This is a documentable fact about the field's history (Wellhausen's DH dominated c. 1880–1970s; it has since fractured under Rendtorff, Blum, Schmid, Carr, Gertz, and others). The apologist's framing collapses a diverse research program into a single straw target.

**Steelman of the opposing view:** A defender could note that "Documentary Hypothesis" is often used loosely in popular discourse to mean *any* multi-source theory of the Pentateuch, so the apologist is merely using common parlance, not misrepresenting the field. McClellan's reply is that precision matters precisely because critics rest their rebuttal on attacking *the DH specifically*; if the source-distinction argument survives without the full DH, refuting Wellhausen accomplishes nothing.

## Best Quotes

> "The documentary hypothesis has certainly long been the dominant source critical theory of pentateuchal composition. However, it is not all source critical theories... and it's no longer the dominant position."[^2]

> "Just because a theory involves J E D and P or maybe just D P and non-P or some other combination of sources doesn't make it the documentary hypothesis. There are other hypotheses out there."[^3]

> "When apologists treat any source critical theories of pentateuchal composition as the documentary hypothesis... that's usually a dead giveaway that they're not actually reading the critical scholarship."[^3]
