#type/claim

# Saints Driven from City to City Prophecy

## The Claim
In a letter dated 16 August 1832 to brethren in Missouri, [[Joseph Smith]] wrote that if the church failed in its duties, "there remaineth a scourge for the church, even that they shall be driven from city to city, and but few shall remain to receive an inheritance." Recorded in the *History of the Church*, the Saints were indeed driven — out of Jackson County (1833), from Kirtland to Missouri, and amid further persecution in Missouri in 1838.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The strongest source feature: a dated 1832 letter.** The host emphasizes "if this comes from a letter dated to August 16th, 1832," it would be a *contemporaneous* document predating the 1833 Jackson County expulsions. (Evidence rating: strong if the letter's date and wording are authentic and early — a real point in its favor versus the late reminiscences.)[^1]
2. **Clear, repeated fulfillment.** The driving "from city to city" maps onto the actual serial displacements of 1833–1838. (Evidence rating: solid historical fit.)[^2]
3. **Conditional framing.** The prophecy is conditioned on the church failing its duties ("if they do not... behold, there remaineth a scourge"), which makes it partly a warning and somewhat unfalsifiable. (Evidence rating: moderate; conditionals resist disproof — compare [[Apologetic vs Evidence Distinction for Prophecy]].)[^1]
4. **Steelman of the skeptic.** Even a contemporaneous 1832 letter predicting persecution is unremarkable for a movement already generating local hostility; "they will drive you out" is a plausible read of the political weather, not necessarily supernatural foresight. The believing strength is the early date; the deflation is the genericness of the content.

## Best Quotes
> "There remaineth a scourge for the church, even that they shall be driven from city to city, but few shall remain to receive an inheritance."[^1]
