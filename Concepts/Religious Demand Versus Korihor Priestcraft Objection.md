# Religious Demand Versus Korihor Priestcraft Objection

#type/rebuttal

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
The "Korihor perspective": that "all of these performances and ordinances are just meant to keep their heads down" and bind members "in bondage" — a secular charge that religious demand exists to subjugate adherents rather than benefit them. See [[Alma 30.27|Alma 30:27]].[^1]

## The Rebuttal

1. **The demands are pro-social, not subjugating (Evidence: definitional).** "I could agree with that if the rituals and what we do weren't things like going to visit your neighbor and bringing them some food."[^2] The content of LDS "demand" is largely service to others, which inverts the bondage framing.

2. **Demand produces flourishing through opposition (Evidence: theological + developmental).** "It's to lift us up and help us flourish and grow ... growth doesn't happen unless there's opposition."[^3] This pairs the developmental argument of [[High-Demand Religion Retains More Members]] with LDS opposition theology.

3. **Service blesses the world, not just the self (Evidence: research-backed).** "One of the reasons why religion on average is so good for individuals is because it's helping you to bless other people. That is so good for us and the whole world."[^4]

**Steelman of the opposing view (Korihor's case):** The strongest version of the objection isn't that *charity* is bondage — it's that obligatory ritual observance, tithing, temple-worthiness gatekeeping, and authority structures can function to maintain institutional control and material support ("glut yourselves with the labors of their hands," Alma 30:27) independent of the genuine good done. Pointing to neighborly food-delivery is a selective sample of "what we do"; it does not engage the costlier, more controlling obligations the critic actually targets. A demand system can do real good *and* exert control simultaneously.

## Best Quotes

> "You have the Korihor perspective where he says, 'All of these performances and ordinances are just meant to keep their heads down.'"[^1]

> "Growth doesn't happen unless there's opposition and unless there's a challenge involved."[^3]
