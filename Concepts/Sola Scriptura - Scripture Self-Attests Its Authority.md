# Sola Scriptura — Scripture Self-Attests Its Authority

## The Claim
Kelly's affirmative case for *sola scriptura*: scripture is the inspired word of God that **testifies to its own authority**, and this is corroborated by how Jesus, the apostles, and the early church *used* scripture. Because God "preserves his word" forever, the 66 books we possess are the reliable, recognizable standard of doctrine.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence (Kelly's case, with assessment)

### 1. The proof-text: 2 Timothy 3:16–17 — rate: foundational but contested
"All scripture is given by inspiration of God [θεόπνευστος] and is profitable for doctrine, for reproof, for correction, for instruction in righteousness, that the man of God may be perfect, thoroughly furnished unto all good works."[^2] See [[2 Timothy 3.14-17|2 Timothy 3:14-17]]. **Steelman of the rebuttal:** in context Paul refers to the *Tanakh* (the "sacred writings" Timothy knew "from childhood"), not a closed 66-book canon; the verse asserts inspiration and usefulness, not *infallibility*, *sufficiency-to-exclusion*, or canon boundaries. Kelly himself notes the OT-reference context.[^3]

### 2. Jesus and the apostles treated scripture as authoritative — rate: strong as far as it goes
Kelly's marquee data: Jesus says "it is written" against Satan and the Pharisees "over 300 times"; the NT contains "over 1,600" OT references / "295 quotations," ~10% of the NT being OT content.[^4] Berea is the model: the noble Bereans "searched the scriptures daily to see whether those things were so" ([[Acts 17.10-11|Acts 17:10-11]]).[^5] **Assessment:** this robustly establishes that scripture is *an* authority used to test teaching — Hayden concedes this — but it shows the OT functioning authoritatively for first-century Jews, not God designating *the 66-book canon* as *the ultimate, infallible* authority.

### 3. The early church fathers cited nearly the whole NT — rate: overstated
Kelly claims "over 36,289 references" from early fathers covering the whole NT "except for about five verses," before any formal canon.[^6] **Scholarly context:** the famous "we could reconstruct the NT from patristic quotations" figure traces to 19th-c. claims (Bernard/Dalrymple) and is repeated by apologists; it is *roughly* true for breadth of citation but does not show the fathers held *Kelly's exact 66-book list* — Hayden's rebuttal that no father affirmed the identical Protestant canon in the first 3–4 centuries is accurate.[^7]

### 4. Even LDS authorities exalt the Bible — rate: clever but disputed in cross-ex
Kelly marshals Elder M. Russell Ballard ("the Bible is a miracle... the bedrock of all Christianity") and Brigham Young ("we take this book, the Bible, for our guide... the foundation of our faith").[^8] **Steelman of the rebuttal:** Hayden replies that praising the Bible's importance ≠ affirming it as *infallible / ultimate*, and that Brigham Young explicitly *rejected* scripture as the sole ultimate authority — so the quotes do not mean what Kelly wants.[^9] See [[Scripture as the Test of Prophets and Revelation]].

### 5. Reductio: remove the book — rate: rhetorically strong
Kelly's closing thrust: "Remove the Book of Mormon — would we still have Christianity? Yes. Remove the Bible — would we? No. If Joseph Smith never existed we still have Christianity; remove the Bible and we have no Christianity and no Joseph Smith."[^10] **Assessment:** establishes the Bible's *causal indispensability*, not its *infallibility/ultimacy* — the precise gap Hayden exploits all debate.

## Best Quotes
> "We will start out with the Bible alone, taking it as the standard. Take up the Bible, compare the religion of Latter-day Saints with it, and see if we'll stand the test." (Kelly quoting Brigham Young)[^11]

> "Without knowing what we have in the scriptures, how would we know what Jesus taught?"[^12]

> "Remove the Bible — let's say the Bible never existed — would we be talking today?... You remove the Bible, we have no Christianity and no Joseph Smith."[^10]
