# LDS Salvation Is by Grace Not Earned by Works

## The Point
The frequent Evangelical charge that Latter-day Saints believe in a "works-based salvation" is false. Restoration scripture and modern prophets teach emphatically that salvation comes through the **merits, mercy, and grace of Christ** — not by earning it. Fallen humanity "could not merit anything of himself" (Alma 22:14); "it is only in and through the grace of God that ye are saved" (2 Nephi 10:24). [^1]

## Evidence
1. **The Book of Mormon is saturated with grace.** "No flesh that can dwell in the presence of God, save it be through the merits, and mercy, and grace of the Holy Messiah" (2 Nephi 2:8); relying "wholly upon the merits of Him who is mighty to save" (2 Nephi 31:19); even maximal obedience leaves us "unprofitable servants" still "indebted" to God (Mosiah 2:20-24). [^1]
2. **Modern apostles say salvation cannot be earned.** Dallin H. Oaks: "Man cannot earn his own salvation... The commandments, ordinances, and covenants of the gospel are not a list of deposits required to be made in some heavenly account." [^2]
3. **Bruce R. McConkie:** "Does salvation come by grace without works? It surely does... We are saved by grace, without works; it is a gift of God... We are saved by the blood of Christ." [^2]
4. **Dieter F. Uchtdorf:** "Salvation cannot be bought with the currency of obedience; it is purchased by the blood of the Son of God." [^2]
5. To claim salvation is "**caused by any human act**," as Hayden Carroll puts it, would be to "preach blasphemy" — the entire religion centers on "our complete and utter dependence on" Christ. [^3]

## Best Quotes
> "Man cannot earn his own salvation... The commandments, ordinances, and covenants of the gospel are not a list of deposits required to be made in some heavenly account." — Dallin H. Oaks [^2]

> "Salvation cannot be bought with the currency of obedience; it is purchased by the blood of the Son of God." — Dieter F. Uchtdorf [^2]

## LDS Connection
This is the core Restoration soteriology and a direct answer to a top Evangelical objection: we are saved by grace, through faith in Christ, which faith is *manifested* (not purchased) by the works He commands (Ephesians 2:8-10). The remaining LDS distinctives concern the *role* of ordinances and covenants as expressions of that faith — see [[Works Are the Fruit of Faith Not the Price of Salvation]] and [[Sola Fide vs Faith Plus Ordinances]] — not the *source* of salvation, which is Christ alone.
