# The Genealogical Adam and Universal Common Ancestry

#type/claim

## The Claim

The one thing Latter-day Saints "really have to be dogmatic about," per President [[Gordon B. Hinckley]], is "that Adam and Eve existed and we descend from them."[^1] The **genealogical Adam** proposal shows this minimal commitment is fully compatible with mainstream science: because of *pedigree collapse*, the most recent couple from whom *everyone now alive* descends lived only on the order of 6,000 years ago. So a literal first couple can be genealogically ancestral to all of humanity *without* being the only humans alive at the time — preserving "Adam and Eve existed and we descend from them" while leaving room for [[Nothing Has Been Revealed Concerning Evolution|other humans, evolution, and an old earth]]. [[T. Benjamin Spackman]] flags this as "intriguing... but also kind of out of my area" — math and genealogy rather than history — and explicitly declines to "claim anything hard there."[^2]

## Supporting Evidence (steelman)

- **The math of ancestry explodes, then collapses.** "You have two parents, four grandparents, eight great-grandparents... go back 30 generations, which is roughly 600, 700, 800 years, you have a billion ancestors." Obviously those are not all unique individuals — "the further you go back the more you have what's called pedigree collapse," where the same ancestors recur across many lines.[^3]
- **The most recent universal ancestor is surprisingly recent.** Asked how far back one must go to find "a couple that everyone alive is descended from," the answer is "6,000 years." "If you have kids that survive and they have kids that survive, 6,000 years from now, everyone on Earth will have you in their genealogy."[^4]
- **It harmonizes the genealogical claim with a populated world.** Universal *genealogical* descent does not require Adam and Eve to be the sole biological progenitors or the first *Homo sapiens*; it only requires that their line eventually threads into everyone's family tree. This "opens up a whole bunch of perspectives and doors."[^5]
- **It engages the 6,000-year scriptural figure.** The number is "relevant because the idea in many circles is that... the earth is only six or seven thousand years old," and because D&C 77 references "the seven thousand years of [the earth's] temporal existence." Spackman treats such figures as a reason to "rethink" reading scripture "like the divine encyclopedia" of consistent facts, noting the Old and New Testaments themselves "say different things" (circumcision, dietary law) and even contain internal tensions (the Passover boiled vs. roasted, harmonized in Chronicles).[^6]
- **Held loosely.** Spackman repeatedly hedges: it is "interesting to find," "math and genealogy and pedigrees that I don't mess with as a historian," and "not claiming anything hard there."[^2]

## Best Quotes

> "President Hinckley... said the only thing we really have to be dogmatic about is that Adam and Eve existed and we descend from them."[^1]

> "Mathematically how far back do you have to go to find a couple that everyone alive is descended from?... It's 6,000 years. If you have kids that survive and they have kids that survive, 6,000 years from now, everyone on Earth will have you in their genealogy."[^4]

> "That opens up a whole bunch of perspectives and doors. Not claiming anything hard there, but... that's math and genealogy and pedigrees that I don't mess with as a historian."[^2]

## LDS Connection

Genealogical Adam lets the Restoration's *one* non-negotiable about origins — historical first parents from whom all descend — coexist with everything science says about deep time and human evolution, without the apologetic strain of insisting Adam and Eve were the only humans c. 4000 BC. It pairs naturally with [[The Fall Was a Change of Degree or Scope Not an Absolute Reversal|a Fall that enlarges scope rather than reversing cosmic law]] and with [[Nothing Has Been Revealed Concerning Evolution|the Church's deliberate doctrinal silence on mechanism]]. The D&C 77 "seven thousand years" discussion also reinforces [[Seven Seals and the Age of the Earth Show Continuing Revelation|reading even modern revelation as accommodated rather than as an encyclopedia of timeless facts]].
