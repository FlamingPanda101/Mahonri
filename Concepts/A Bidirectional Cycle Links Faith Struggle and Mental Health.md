# A Bidirectional Cycle Links Faith Struggle and Mental Health

## The Claim
The relationship between mental health and faith is not one-directional. [[Sam Hardy]] and [[Justin Dyer]] describe a **bidirectional, self-perpetuating link**: poorer mental health makes a person more likely to struggle with faith, *and* losing faith removes a source of connection and comfort, which deepens the distress. The result is a "vicious cycle" in which a lack of faith spawns more emotional difficulty, which drives the person still further from the religious setting that might have helped. This reframes a common critic's move — "religion causes the distress, so leaving cures it" — as backwards: distress and disaffiliation feed each other.

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The bidirectional link, stated plainly (rated: moderate).** "There's somewhat of a bidirectional link between mental health... People who have more suicidality and depression are going to be more likely to struggle with their faith."[^1] Causation runs *both* ways, so cross-sectional "religion correlates with X" claims cannot establish direction.

2. **The cycle compounds (rated: moderate).** The findings "suggest that a vicious cycle is created for those struggling in which a lack of faith spawns increased issues, which then drive them further away from the connection and comfort found in the religious setting."[^1] *Steelman:* "vicious cycle" is an interpretive gloss on correlational panel data; the article does not provide a cross-lagged model isolating each direction, and confounders (a stressful life event driving both at once) could mimic a feedback loop.

3. **Reframes the causal arrow (rated: moderate).** Because depression and suicidality *predict* faith struggle, the popular inference that the Church *produces* youth distress inverts a relationship that runs the other way — consistent with the regional rebuttal in [[Utah Suicide Rate Implicates the Church]] and the positive baseline in [[Latter-day Saints Rank High in Well-being and Spiritual Peace]]. It also explains why simply leaving fails to deliver relief (see [[Leaving Religion Does Not Bring the Relief People Expect]]).
