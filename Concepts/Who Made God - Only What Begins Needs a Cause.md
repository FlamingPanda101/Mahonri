# Who Made God - Only What Begins Needs a Cause

## The Point
"Who made God?" is the atheist's supposed trump card, deployed (by Dawkins, Krauss, and others) to block any inference to a Creator.[^1] Turek answers that it misunderstands the law of causality, which "does not say that everything has a cause" but that "everything that *has a beginning* has a cause."[^2] Because an infinite regress of causes is impossible, the chain must terminate in "a self-existent, uncaused first cause." Since God created time, He is timeless and therefore beginningless — and "one reason God had no cause is because He had no beginning."[^3]

## Evidence
1. **The law states beginnings need causes, not everything.** "The law of causality says that everything that has a beginning has a cause, or every effect has a cause." Not everything can be an effect; motion and change require "an eternal, uncaused first cause — an 'unmoved mover.'"[^2]
2. **No infinite regress.** "We can't go on an infinite regress of causes. When we trace the causal chain back into history, it must end at a self-existent, uncaused first cause." Asking "Who made that?" is incoherent "when you've arrived at an eternal uncaused first cause."[^4]
3. **The objectors picture an idol.** Atheists who ask "Who made God?" imagine "a created, Zeus-like idol." The Dawkins–Lennox exchange makes the symmetry explicit: both admit theist and atheist alike must explain "how things got started"; Lennox's "eternal Logos" is no more in need of a maker than Dawkins's "brute fact" universe.[^5]
4. **God is not "complicated."** Against Dawkins's claim that a designer must be more complex than the universe: "The true God has no parts. His essence is simple, yet powerful spirit." And Dawkins's own macroevolution asserts the opposite — that "simple causes give rise to more complex effects" — so the "causes must be more complicated" rule undercuts his evolutionism.[^6]
5. **The archaeologist analogy.** You can know an immediate cause without knowing the whole chain: an inscription warrants inferring a human inscriber even if you can't say who made *that* human. Demanding the cause of the cause before any conclusion "would never" let science proceed.[^7]
6. **Why balk at an eternal God?** "For centuries atheists had no problem believing in an eternal universe. Why do they suddenly now have a problem believing in an eternal God?" Since the evidence shows the universe began, the theist follows the evidence; the atheist has "blind faith that some other explanation will be found."[^8]

## Best Quotes
> "The law of causality says that everything that has a beginning has a cause, or every effect has a cause." [^2]

> "If God exists, He is the self-existent, uncaused first cause. Since God created time, He is timeless or eternal. If you're timeless, do you have a beginning? Of course not." [^3]

> Dawkins: "But you haven't explained where the Logos came from in the first place." Lennox: "Well of course not, because the Logos didn't come from anywhere." [^5]

## LDS Connection
Turek's reply to "Who made God?" — that an eternal, beginningless being needs no cause — is one a Latter-day Saint can affirm against atheism: God is self-existent and uncaused. Two divergences deserve honesty. (1) *Ex nihilo*: Turek roots God's beginninglessness in His having "created time" and the universe "out of nothing," whereas LDS theology holds that matter and intelligences are themselves eternal and uncreated (D&C 93:29; [[Abraham 3.25|Abraham 3:25]]), so it is not only God but the "elements" and human intelligence that have no beginning. (2) *Divine simplicity*: Turek says "the true God has no parts" — a classical attribute drawn largely from Greek philosophy (Aristotle's unmoved mover) that the Restoration rejects in favor of an embodied, anthropomorphic God; see [[The Uncaused Cause Is Aristotle Not Scripture]] and [[Unmoved Mover Reshaped God the Father]]. The shared, defensible point stands: the "Who made X?" regress must stop at something eternal — and the evidence of a beginning favors that "something" being God rather than the cosmos. See also [[Intelligence Is Uncreated and Eternally Has Agency]].
