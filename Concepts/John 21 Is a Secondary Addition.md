# John 21 Is a Secondary Addition

## The Claim
Chapter 21 is a **later addition** to the Gospel of John, which originally ended at the close of chapter 20. The first ending (John 20:30–31) already ties the book off with a clear purpose statement; chapter 21 then awkwardly resumes and supplies a *second* ending.

## Supporting Evidence

1. **John 20:30–31 is a proper book-ending.** The narrator says there were many other things Jesus did, then states the purpose — "I wrote these things so that you may believe that Jesus is the Christ, the Son of God" — "a beautiful bow on everything." Such summarizing endings are paralleled in other ancient books. *(Strong; the rhetorical closure is textually evident.)* [^1]
2. **We know it's an ending because the book ends again a chapter later.** After this closure, chapter 21 "confusingly" narrates another episode, then closes a *second* time ("not all the books in the world could contain..."). A double ending signals an appended chapter. *(Strong; the doubling is the core argument.)* [^2]
3. **Other features of the chapter are unusual.** Scholars have flagged additional anomalous features of the passage beyond the double ending, reinforcing the secondary-addition reading. *(Moderate; gestured at rather than enumerated in the interview.)* [^3]
4. **The authorial colophon sits inside the appendix.** The "this is the disciple who wrote these things... and we know his testimony is true" of [[John 21.24|John 21:24]] occurs within this added chapter — see [[The We of John 21-24 Single Author Explanation]]. *(Context; the verse's location matters for both questions.)* [^4]

**Steelman of the opposing view.** Some scholars argue chapter 21 is original (or by the same author): there is **no manuscript evidence** of John ever circulating without chapter 21 (unlike the *pericope adulterae*), the vocabulary and style are thoroughly Johannine, and ancient works can have an "epilogue" after a summary statement. Méndez acknowledges colleagues who hold this and simply sides with the later-hand view.

## Best Quotes
> "He kind of puts this beautiful bow on everything he's done in 20 chapters. It's an appropriate ending for a book... It is literally the ending of the book a chapter later." — [[Hugo Mendez]] [^2]

> "After the author has told you there are lots of other things that Jesus did, he starts confusingly narrating something else that Jesus did right when you don't expect it." [^2]

> "It's probably by a later hand, a later scribe who added to the Gospel of John." [^3]
