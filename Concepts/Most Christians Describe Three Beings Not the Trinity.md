# Most Christians Describe Three Beings Not the Trinity

## The Claim
When ordinary Christians are asked who God is, they don't reach for a creed — they say "my Father in heaven," and of Jesus, "the Son of God, my Savior." Asked to explain the Trinity, most describe it as "God is the Father, Jesus is the Son, and the Holy Ghost is the spirit" — i.e., **three separate beings**, which is precisely what the creeds reject as heresy [^1]. The video reads this as evidence that "deep down they know" the creeds don't make sense: the lived, intuitive faith is a family of distinct persons, not "three persons, one substance" [^2].

## Supporting Evidence
1. **Folk descriptions match tritheism / social trinitarianism, not Nicene homoousion — rate: strong as observation.** The claim that lay explanations of the Trinity routinely lapse into "three beings" is widely corroborated; surveys and catechetical experience show most believers cannot articulate consubstantiality and default to a social model the creeds condemn. This dovetails with [[Trinity Is Fundamentally Contradictory]] and [[Council of Nicaea]]. **Steelman of the creedal Christian:** lay theological imprecision is not evidence the doctrine is false — most believers also can't explain the hypostatic union, the canon, or transubstantiation, yet those are not thereby refuted. The Trinity is explicitly held to be a revealed mystery requiring careful formulation; the existence of bad folk paraphrases ("partialism," "tritheism," "modalism" — all named heresies) shows the doctrine is *hard*, not that it is incoherent. The "deep down they know" inference is a psychologizing move, not an argument.
2. **Reframes LDS theology as the intuitive default — rate: framing, the rhetorical payoff.** The video casts the LDS view — Father and Son as distinct, embodied persons — as what people *already* believe before the creeds correct them, making Restoration theology feel like common sense rather than novelty. Compare [[Convergence of Mainstream Christianity Toward LDS Doctrines]] and [[Subordinationism Was the Early Church View]]. **Caution:** that early Christians or modern laypeople *speak* in distinct-persons language doesn't establish the LDS metaphysic (embodiment, ontological kinship with God); it shows shared anti-modalist intuition at most.
3. **Connects to the "philosophy degree" complaint.** The video's barb — that the creeds twisted "a father who loves his children" into something "you need a philosophy degree to pretend it works" — is the same critique of over-philosophized theology found in [[The Creeds Built a Self-Contradictory God]].

## Best Quotes
> "Most Christians who say they believe in God in the form of the Trinity can't actually explain the Trinity. If you ask them to, they would describe it in terms that the creeds themselves would call heresy." [^1]

> "God is the father, Jesus is the son, and the Holy Ghost is the spirit. But that's not the trinity. That's three separate beings. And that's what the creeds specifically reject. But people express it that way anyway because deep down they know." [^2]

> "Only humans could take something as beautiful and simple as a father who loves his children and twist it into something so contradictory that you need a philosophy degree to pretend it works." [^3]
