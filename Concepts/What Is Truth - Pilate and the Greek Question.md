# What Is Truth — Pilate and the Greek Question

## The Point
The exchange in John 18:37 is presented as a recorded collision between "the ultimate Greek" (Pilate, educated ruling-class Greek thinker) and "the ultimate Hebrew" (Jesus). Pilate's "What is truth?" is the paradigmatically *Greek* question — it wants truth as a static, written, seeable fact. Jesus answers with a Hebrew epistemology of truth: truth is something you *do, live, hear, and experience*.

## Argument
1. **The setup.** In John 18:37 Pilate interrogates Jesus: "Art thou a king then?" Jesus replies he was born and came into the world "to bear witness unto the truth," and "everyone that is of the truth heareth my voice." Pilate's response — "What is truth?" — is treated as the crossroads of the two minds [^1].
2. **Pilate is asking a Greek question.** He wants truth "written down," a thing he can *see* — "get to the Polaroid, I want to just see it written down, I'm a Greek, tell me what the truth is." This is the seeing-is-knowing epistemology applied to truth itself [^2].
3. **Jesus gives a Hebrew answer.** Truth cannot be handed over as an inert proposition on a "fortune cookie." Per the Hebrew ontology, truth is *lived and heard*: "the truth is what you do, what you live, what you hear, what you experience." Jesus says it is *heard* by "everyone that is of the truth" — truth is relational and active, not static and optical [^3].
4. **Why the question itself can be "flawed."** Because the two paradigms ask differently, some questions are malformed from the start — "a square peg in a round hole." A Greek question about truth (give me the proposition to inspect) cannot capture a reality that, in Hebrew terms, exists only in the living and the doing [^4].
5. **The integration caveat.** The talk does not say propositional truth is worthless — Greek precision is "very useful" for science and law. The claim is narrower: *truth as a whole* (the kind Jesus bears witness to) is centered in the experiential and lived, not merely in the stated [^5].

## Best Quotes
> "Pilate's just like, well what is truth, get to the Polaroid, I want to just see it written down — I'm a Greek, tell me what the truth is. And Jesus is like, bro, you can't, I can't write it on a fortune cookie for you... the truth is what you do, what you live, what you hear, what you experience." [^3]

> "Here it is — pay attention everybody — because here's the crossroads of the Greek mind and the Hebrew mind crossing." [^1]
