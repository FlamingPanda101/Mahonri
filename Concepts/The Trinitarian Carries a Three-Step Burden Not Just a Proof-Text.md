# The Trinitarian Carries a Three-Step Burden Not Just a Proof-Text

## The Claim

[[Luke Hansen]] reframes the "you must be Trinitarian to be Christian" demand by spelling out the **full burden** a creedal Christian has to discharge — not one step but three, where the usually-skipped third is decisive:

1. Show the Trinitarian metaphysical description of God is **compatible with the Bible**.
2. Show it is the **only possible** explanation of God in the Bible — "not just one of several that are plausible."
3. Show it is an **essential belief** — something one *must* believe to be counted Christian.[^1]

The point is that proving step 1 (or even 2) does nothing to establish the disqualifier. Even a doctrine "clearly taught in the Bible" need not be in the category of *must-believe-to-be-Christian*. To exclude Latter-day Saints, the critic needs step 3, and step 3 is where Hansen says the case collapses historically.

His historical lever is an **inversion**: in the mid-3rd century (the "250s"), Trinitarianism "was not a widely held Christian belief… it was something that was still developing over time," whereas **baptismal regeneration was 100% universal — every Christian believed it.** Today the two have flipped: most Protestants have dropped baptismal regeneration while "everybody believes in the Trinity." So a belief's later universality cannot be read back as an always-essential boundary marker; "it took 300 years to quote-unquote find the Trinity in the Bible," let alone to find anywhere in the Bible that *believing* it is necessary.[^2]

## Supporting Evidence (steelman)

### 1. The three-step structure is logically clean — rate: strong
The move isolates a real gap in the standard argument. "Biblical" and "essential-for-the-label" are different predicates; a creedalist who only argues biblicality has not touched the disqualification. This is the same fault line, from the burden-of-proof side, that [[The Creeds Cannot Be the Neutral Baseline - The Circularity of Creedal Disqualification]] attacks from the circularity side and that [[A Parallel List Without Textual Analysis Carries No Burden]] presses about mere proof-text lists. It dovetails with [[Trinity Cannot Be Derived From the New Testament - Swinburne]], which contests even step 2.

### 2. The baptismal-regeneration inversion is a genuine historical datum — rate: moderate-to-strong
That early-Christian sources broadly assume baptismal regeneration while a developed Trinitarian metaphysic is not yet fixed in the 3rd century is defensible church history. Used as an analogy it is potent: if universality-now does not make baptismal regeneration an eternal essential (Protestants themselves dropped it), then universality-now cannot, by itself, make Trinitarianism one either. The argument is about the *form* of the boundary claim, not the truth of either doctrine.

### 3. It reframes rather than denies — rate: fair
Hansen is not asserting the Trinity is unbiblical here; he is relocating the dispute to *essentiality*, which keeps the LDS "Christian, just not creedal" self-description intact (see [[Latter-day Saints Are Christians Just Not Creedal Christians]]) and connects to the soteriological reductio in [[Understanding the Trinity Is Not Required for Salvation]] and [[Salvation by Theology Damns Almost Everyone]].

### Steelman of the creedal reply — rate: this is the strongest counter
A Trinitarian can grant the three-step framing and still meet step 3 by **distinguishing the doctrine from its later technical vocabulary**: the *worship of the right God* (the deity of Christ, John 8:24) was always essential even when *homoousios* was not yet coined, so the 4th-century *terminology* developing late does not show the underlying *identity-of-God* boundary developed late. On the inversion, the reply is disanalogy: baptismal regeneration is a soteriological *practice* about which Scripture is arguably less univocal, whereas the identity of the One worshipped is treated as a first-order boundary throughout (cf. the Shema, the exclusivity texts). The LDS rejoinder returns to [[The Shema Asserts Exclusive Relationship Not Sole Existence]] and to the claim that the *being*-level reading is precisely the post-biblical accretion.

And then I think people usually miss number three… you have to show that it's an essential belief to be considered Christian." [^1]
