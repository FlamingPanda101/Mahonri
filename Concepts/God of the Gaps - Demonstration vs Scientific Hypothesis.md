# God of the Gaps - Demonstration vs Scientific Hypothesis

## The Point
**Objection (the "central conceit of the entire New Atheist project"):** The cosmological argument is a "god of the gaps" — it posits God to explain something that science either already explains or eventually will, and as a quasi-scientific hypothesis it is a bad one (Hitchens: it violates Ockham's razor; Dawkins: a "big bang singularity" is more parsimonious; Hawking, Mlodinow, Krauss: science already explains the universe's existence).[^1] **Reply:** This either misses the point or begs the question. Feser's arguments are *not* god-of-the-gaps explanations at all — they are attempts at **strict metaphysical demonstration**, and the rival "scientific" account (a universe from "nothing") is a manifest non-starter.[^2]

## Evidence
- **A "god of the gaps" explanation is probabilistic; a demonstration is not.** A gaps-explanation concedes that some non-divine explanation *might in principle* be correct and claims only that God is *more probable*. Feser's proofs instead claim to show there is *no way in principle* to account for the explanandum except by a purely actual / absolutely simple / essence-is-existence / necessary cause. They "do not stand or fall by the standards by which empirical hypotheses are evaluated — parsimony, fit with existing theories, and so forth."[^3]
- **The starting points are metaphysical, not physical.** They concern "what any possible empirical theory must itself take for granted" — that whatever the empirical facts turn out to be, they will involve the actualization of potential, composite beings, essence conjoined to existence, contingency. To insist that only an empirical theory could explain this is merely to *assume* the proofs are mistaken, not to show it.[^4]
- **"A universe from nothing" is a bait-and-switch.** "A law like gravity is not nothing," so an explanation invoking such a law is obviously not an account of how the universe arises from *nothing* (contra Hawking and Mlodinow).[^5] Krauss spends 152 of 185 pages arguing the universe arose from *empty space with energy and physical laws* — which he admits "does not count as nothing" — then retreats to the laws of quantum gravity (also not nothing), then to "layer upon layer of laws."[^6] His "nothing" turns out to be *something*; nothing "is just the absence of anything," not an exotic kind of stuff to puff one's chin over. And a basic level of physical laws cannot be the ultimate explanation anyway, for the reasons given against treating laws as a terminus.[^7]
- Even the philosopher-of-physics David Albert (no theological agenda) demolished Krauss's book in the *New York Times*.[^8]

## Best Quotes
> "The versions of the cosmological argument I've been defending... are all attempts at strict metaphysical demonstration. They claim to show that there is no way in principle to account for what they set out to explain other than by reference to a purely actual cause, or an absolutely simple or noncomposite cause, or a cause whose essence just is existence, or a necessary being." [^3]

> "'A law like gravity' is not nothing; hence, an explanation of the existence of the universe that makes reference to such a law is rather obviously not... an account of how the universe might arise from nothing." [^5]

## LDS Connection
This reply is a powerful, shared move against atheism: it refuses the weak posture secularists love to attack and insists the question of *why anything exists at all* is metaphysical, not a gap awaiting the next physics paper. A Latter-day Saint can fully endorse the exposure of Krauss's vanishing "nothing" — indeed the maxim *ex nihilo nihil fit* ("out of nothing, nothing comes") lands *closer* to Restoration cosmology than to either Krauss or classical theism, since LDS theology holds that God organized *eternally existing* matter rather than summoning being from absolute non-being (D&C 93:29; see [[Nothing Comes from Nothing - The Krauss Equivocation]] and [[Creation Ex Nihilo Is Not Biblical]]). The honest divergence is downstream: Feser's "demonstration" is meant to deliver the God of *classical theism* (simple, immutable, purely actual), the very attributes the Restoration questions (see [[The Uncaused Cause Is Aristotle Not Scripture]]). On method — that a first cause is a matter of strict metaphysics and that "the universe made itself from nothing" is incoherent — Feser is a strong ally; the LDS engagement targets only what *kind* of God the proof is said to yield. For the Ch. 1 framing of the same demonstration-vs-gaps point, see [[What a Proof of God Is - Demonstration vs God of the Gaps]].
