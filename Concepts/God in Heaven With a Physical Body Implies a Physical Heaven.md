# God in Heaven With a Physical Body Implies a Physical Heaven

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)

Two related Trinitarian commitments are turned against the view: (1) the risen Jesus has a *physical body* in heaven; and (2) "God is spirit" ([[John 4.24|John 4:24]]), so God is non-material. Hansen argues these generate contradictions the Trinitarian must own — while LDS theology embraces an embodied God without contradiction. [^1]

## The Rebuttal

1. **A physical body entails a physical place.** If Jesus has a physical body in heaven, heaven is a *physical place* somewhere in time and space — so "where is it? Point." Saying a physical body accesses a "non-material spiritual realm" makes the Trinitarian "start to sound like a Mormon," talking about spirit realms as if they were physical. (Evidence: a fair pressure point on popular Trinitarian under-theorizing of the resurrection body; mocked via "a planet called Kolob.") [^2]
2. **The same-being + body/no-body contradiction.** On the orthodox account the Son (with a body) and the Father (no body) are *the same being as God*. So "one being can both have a body and not have a body at the same time" — a square circle / married bachelor. The escape "he has a body only in his humanity" is the recurring nature-indexing dodge. (Evidence: presses the [[Hypostatic Union Creates Two Jesuses|two-natures]] machinery on embodiment specifically.) [^3]
3. **"God is spirit" dissolves under the who/what test.** Ask: is the "God" who "is spirit" a who or a what? If "a who," which one? — the Father (spirit, no body) but not the Son (embodied), even though both are "God." And "I have a spirit too — what's the point?" John 4:24 says nothing about a single disembodied divine being. (Evidence: solid — John 4:24 predicates spirit-ness, it is not a metaphysics of the divine substance.) [^4]
4. **Steelman.** The orthodox reply: the resurrection body is a *glorified* (*sōma pneumatikon*, 1 Cor 15:44) body not bound by ordinary physics (Jesus passes through doors, ascends), so "physical body ⟹ locatable mundane place" misconstrues glorified embodiment; heaven is God's *presence/realm*, not a coordinate. And "God is spirit" describes the *divine nature* (immaterial, omnipresent), which the Son shares as to deity while having assumed a human body — no contradiction in different respects. The Father's "lacking a body" is just His being pure spirit; the Son's body belongs to His humanity. (Evidence: coherent within the two-natures + glorified-body framework; the "different respects" move again does the work.) The LDS rejoinder: this multiplies category-indexed exceptions ("physical but not locatable," "God is spirit but the Son is embodied") where the embodied-God model needs none. [^1]
