# Galatians 1:6 — "I Marvel Ye Are So Soon Removed" Proves an Early Apostasy

## The Claim
[[Ward Radio]] argues critics "always skip verse six." [[Galatians 1.6|Galatians 1:6]] — "I marvel that ye are so soon removed... unto another gospel" — shows Paul astonished that the Galatians were *already* apostatizing within his own lifetime, supporting the LDS doctrine of a [[Great Apostasy and Need for Restoration|Great Apostasy]] and a needed Restoration.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. "So soon" signals speed — strong (textual)
Paul marvels they are "so soon removed," i.e. the defection happened almost immediately after he left. The hosts paraphrase: "Geez, guys, I barely left and you're already apostatizing." The Greek **οὕτως ταχέως** ("so quickly") underscores the rapidity.[^2] Rating: **strong** for "early," moderate as a leap to a *universal* apostasy.

### 2. Every Pauline epistle is corrective — moderate
The hosts generalize that no letter says "keep up the good work"; each is written to halt a developing apostasy ("the house is burning down"). As corroboration this is impressionistic but broadly accurate — the epistles are largely occasioned by error and division.[^3] Rating: **moderate** (rhetorical generalization, not a verse-by-verse audit).

### 3. Skipping verse 6 concedes "bankruptcy" — interpretive
The hosts frame critics' omission of v.6 as an unwilling admission that "there actually was an apostasy that was incredibly early," which they say is "an admission of bankruptcy in cradle Christianity."[^4] Rating: **rhetorical**; the omission is more likely about sound-bite brevity than concealment.

### Steelman of the opposing view
A mainstream Christian would grant that *the Galatian churches* drifted toward Judaizing legalism, but deny this proves a *total, universal* apostasy that severed authority and required a 19th-century Restoration. Local, correctable error (Paul writes precisely to fix it, and expects success) is the opposite of an irreversible loss of the church. The leap from "one congregation strayed quickly" to "the whole church apostatized and priesthood authority was lost" is the contested step, and Galatians 1:6 alone does not carry it. **Response within the source:** the hosts treat v.6 as one data point in a cumulative pattern across all the epistles, not a standalone proof — its apologetic value is rebutting the *image* of a pristine early church, not single-handedly establishing the Great Apostasy.
