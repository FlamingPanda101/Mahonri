# A Crypto-Evangelical Remnant Within the LDS Church

## The Claim
[[Ruslan KD]] raises an evangelical hypothesis: there is "a remnant within the greater Latter-day Saints church" that quietly distances itself from Joseph Smith and the church's fundamental teachings, "secretly evangelical and Protestant," but — because LDS identity is so deeply cultural — is "afraid to just flat out say it."[^1] He offers two firsthand anecdotes: a friend who attends Ruslan's church and has been baptized there yet "will not renounce" his LDS roots ("I love growing up LDS"), and another whose evangelical-stream church "speaks to" him and for whom "the gospel clicks," but whose "identity and cultural heritage is so connected to this LDS subculture" that he cannot fully step away.[^2] The implied conclusion: for some, LDS affiliation is ethnic-cultural identity, not theological conviction.

## Supporting Evidence (steelman of the claim)

1. **Culture can outlast belief.** The observation tracks a real sociological pattern in high-cohesion religions: people retain the *belonging* long after the *believing* wavers, because leaving costs family ties and heritage, not just doctrine. This is the same dynamic catalogued in [[Cultural Pressure to Conform Produces Pretense]] and [[Culture Can Crash Over Doctrine in a Faith Crisis]]. *Rating: a genuine phenomenon,* even if the size of any "remnant" is undemonstrated and anecdotal.

2. **It reframes the "same Jesus" question pastorally.** Ruslan ties it to a mutual unnamed high-profile friend who said he and a Latter-day Saint "worship the same Jesus" — the remnant theory is Ruslan's attempt to make sense of that claim charitably: maybe what looked like agreement was really these culturally-LDS-but-evangelically-believing individuals. *Rating: an honest steelman of an awkward concession,* explaining it without conceding the theology.

## Hansen's Rebuttal (steelman of the LDS reply)

[[Jacob Hansen]] grants the cultural attachment is real ("I don't know necessarily") but isolates the load-bearing assumption: the whole framing "presupposes that what matters to be saved is your theology — like you have to believe in quote-unquote the right Jesus."[^3] He pushes that to require beliefs "not explicitly laid out in scripture" is the actual point of disagreement (cf. [[Identity of Jesus Is Not a Theology Quiz]]). He also notes Latter-day Saints reject a binary heaven/hell, so "right Jesus or damnation" is not even the LDS frame. *Rating: a sharp counter* — it relocates the dispute from "are these people secretly evangelical?" to "is salvation gated by correct christological metaphysics at all?" The unresolved tension: Ruslan holds that placing faith in Jesus *for the forgiveness of sins* is exactly what (on his view) the LDS gospel cannot do, so the cultural-remnant people would, by his lights, have to leave to be saved — which is the very claim Hansen disputes.

## Best Quotes
> "There is a remnant within the greater Latter-day Saints church that is rejecting and distancing themselves from some of the fundamental teaching... they're kind of secretly evangelical and Protestant, but because it's such a cultural thing, they're afraid to just flat out say it." [^1]

> "I resonate more with this evangelical Protestant stream of church and it speaks to me and the gospel clicks... but my identity and my cultural heritage is so connected to this LDS subculture." [^2]

> "There's a presupposition in that — that what matters to be saved is your theology, like you have to believe in quote-unquote the right Jesus." (Hansen) [^3]

## LDS Connection
The exchange isolates the crux of the whole "are Latter-day Saints Christian / saved?" debate: whether salvation is gated by correct theology ("the right Jesus") or by discipleship and faith in Christ. Ruslan's remnant theory presses that LDS identity is partly ethnic-cultural; Hansen agrees culture is sticky but denies that right christological metaphysics is the salvation test — connecting to [[Fruit Versus Theology - Are They Separable]] and [[Identity of Jesus Is Not a Theology Quiz]].
