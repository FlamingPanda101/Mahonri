# Concentric Chiasmus in Alma 24

## The Claim
[[Jonah (Plain and Precious Publishing)|Jonah]] claims to have found a "previously undiscovered" **concentric (double) chiasmus** in King [[Anti-Nephi-Lehi]]'s pacifism speech in [[Alma 24.12-13|Alma 24]]: two distinct chiastic structures — one built on the word **"stain"** and one built on the word **"brethren"** — that "peak at the same concept," i.e., pivot on the same central verse ([[Alma 24.12-13|Alma 24:12-13]]). He dubs it a "kayazm inception," a "kayazm within a kayazm" [^1][^2].

## Supporting Evidence
1. **Shared pivot point.** Jonah's central argument is structural: the [[Stain Chiasmus in Alma 24|stain chiasm]] begins *after* and ends *before* the [[Brethren Chiasmus in Alma 24|brethren chiasm]], yet both turn on the same verse — "let us retain our swords that they be not stained in the blood of our brethren." The nesting (one chiasm enclosed by the other, both centered) is what makes it "concentric." *Rating: the geometric description is internally coherent, but rests entirely on the two underlying chiasms being real and non-arbitrary.* [^2]
2. **Thematic fit of the pivot.** The shared center is doctrinally weighty — staining the sword stains the soul, and only the blood of Christ ([[Alma 24.12-13|atonement]]) can wash it — so a literary spotlight on this verse is plausible *if* the author was composing chiastically [^3].
3. **Steelman of the skeptic.** A critic (here, even co-host [[Cardon Ellis]]) would note that "concentric chiasmus" is not an established, defined category in biblical-studies literature; Jonah himself says "I'm not sure if that's a thing or even like a thing." Two overlapping ABA patterns sharing a center can arise whenever a short passage repeats two key words around a thematic climax — which is common in any rhetorically heightened speech, English or Hebrew [^4]. Chiasmus scholarship (e.g., the criteria associated with [[John W. Welch]]-style analysis, not invoked here) stresses that statistical significance, not mere noticeability, distinguishes intentional chiasm from coincidence — a test this presentation does not apply.
4. **Self-flagged speculation.** The presenters repeatedly concede the finding is unvetted and joke about avoiding scholars, so even on its own terms the claim is offered as a hypothesis for "Near-Eastern studies majors" to test, not a demonstrated result [^5].
