# Old Testament Slavery Was Benign Debt-Bondage Rebutted

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
Joshua HS argued that "when you hear the word slavery, it looks nothing like" the slavery people imagine: in the Old Covenant it was merely "bondservant slavery... basically an economic arrangement where they were even paid," and that apologists who frame it as benign debt-servitude/safety-net are correct.[^1]

## The Rebuttal (Numbered)

1. **It does not "look nothing like" slavery (rated: strong).** McClellan grants a milder bond-service tier exists but insists it is real slavery, and that the benign framing ignores the foreign chattel tier entirely.[^2] See [[Two Distinct Slaveries Debt-Bondage and Chattel]].

2. **Not a safety net (rated: strong).** "Debt slavery was not a social safety net. And debt slavery was not the only type of slavery."[^3]

3. **Not paid (rated: strong; primary text).** "There is no part of the Bible that suggests that debt slaves were paid." The closest is the release-gift of [[Deuteronomy 15.12-15|Deuteronomy 15]], which is not wages.[^4] See [[Debt Slaves Were Not Paid Wages]].

4. **The Leviticus self-redemption text is misread (rated: strong; primary text).** Joshua cited Leviticus to claim a slave could grow rich and buy himself out, implying he earned wages. McClellan: the passage concerns an Israelite enslaved **to a foreigner** whose family can redeem him, or who can redeem himself if he prospers — it "in no way indicates that prospering is as a result of payment."[^5]

5. **Steelman.** Joshua's best point — that biblical bond-service was time-limited and aimed at debt relief for Israelites — is partly true for that tier. McClellan's reply is that this does not generalize: the system also licensed permanent foreign chattel slavery and uncompensated labor.[^2]

## Best Quotes

> "Debt slavery was not a social safety net. And debt slavery was not the only type of slavery."[^3]

> "There is no part of the Bible that suggests that debt slaves were paid."[^4]

> "Why would you enslave someone so that you can earn back the debt that you are owed and then pay them that money anyway? That's not how any of this worked. They're just making stuff up."[^6]
