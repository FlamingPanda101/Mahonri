# Chance, Necessity, or Design - The Fine-Tuning Trilemma

## The Point
The fine-tuning presentation in this clip argues that the universe's fundamental constants are "carefully dialed" to fall within an "exceedingly narrow life-permitting range," and that there are only "three live options" to explain it: **physical necessity, chance, or design.**[^1] Necessity fails (the constants are not fixed by the laws of nature and "could be otherwise"); chance fails (the probabilities are "beyond the reach of chance"); so design is "the best explanation."[^2] The argument closes by reading cosmic order as the witness of a "Supreme Creator." This is the same teleological case made in [[Cosmic Fine-Tuning Points to a Designer (Turek)]] and [[Anthropic Fine-Tuning Points to a Designer]], here built on specific constants.

## Evidence
1. **The constants are "dialed."** "From galaxies and stars down to atoms and subatomic particles, the very structure of our universe is determined by these numbers" — the fundamental constants — each "carefully dialed to an astonishingly precise value... within an exceedingly narrow life-permitting range." Alter one "by even a hair," and "no physical interactive life of any kind could exist."[^3]
2. **Worked examples.** *Gravity:* if the gravitational constant "varied by just one in 10 to the 60th parts... none of us would exist" — the universe would expand too fast for stars or collapse back on itself.[^4] *Expansion:* the cosmological constant changed "by a mere one part in 10 to the 120th" would make the universe expand too fast or too slow to be life-permitting.[^5] *Smoothness:* if the early universe's mass-energy "were not evenly distributed to an incomprehensible precision of one part in 10 to the 10 to the 123rd, the universe would be hostile to life."[^6]
3. **Necessity is implausible.** On this option "the universe must be life-permitting" — but "a life-prohibiting universe" is "not only possible, it's far more likely than a life-permitting" one. "The constants and quantities are not determined by the laws of nature," so "there's no reason or evidence to suggest that fine-tuning is necessary."[^7]
4. **Chance is unreachable.** "Did we just get really, really, really lucky? No. The probabilities involved are so ridiculously remote as to put the fine-tuning well beyond the reach of chance."[^8] The multiverse, offered to keep chance alive, fails on its own terms — see [[Why the Multiverse Fails to Explain Fine-Tuning (Craig)]].
5. **Design is the best explanation.** "Given the implausibility of physical necessity or chance, the best explanation for why the universe is fine-tuned for life may very well be it was designed that way." The presentation quotes Fred Hoyle ("a super intellect monkeyed with physics... no blind forces worth speaking about in nature") and Paul Davies ("the impression of design is overwhelming").[^9] This parallels [[Intelligent Design as Inference to the Best Explanation]].

## Best Quotes
> "There are three live options: the fine-tuning of the universe is due to either physical necessity, chance, or design. Which of these options is the most plausible?" [^1]

> "A super intellect monkeyed with physics... and there are no blind forces worth speaking about in nature. The numbers one calculates from the facts seem to me so overwhelming as to put this conclusion almost beyond question." (Fred Hoyle, quoted) [^9]

## LDS Connection
The trilemma's conclusion — a precisely ordered, life-permitting cosmos best explained by a supreme intelligence — fits Latter-day Saint theism, and the clip itself lands on Alma's argument that "the planets which move in their regular form do witness that there is a supreme creator" ([[Alma 30.44|Alma 30:44]]; see [[Fine-Tuning and the Teleological Argument from Alma 30]]). The honest divergence is *ex nihilo*: the presentation says the universe "was designed that way," whereas Restoration theology holds that God *organized* an eternal cosmos and operates in harmony with co-eternal law rather than authoring the constants out of nonbeing. A Latter-day Saint thus affirms "design over chance and necessity" while reading the Designer as the Organizer-and-Orderer who set the parameters. See [[Cosmic Fine-Tuning Points to a Designer (Turek)]].
