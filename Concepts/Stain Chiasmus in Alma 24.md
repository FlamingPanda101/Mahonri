# Stain Chiasmus in Alma 24

## The Claim
[[Jonah (Plain and Precious Publishing)|Jonah]] argues that the rare word **"stain"** organizes a five-point chiasm in [[Alma 24.12-13|Alma 24:12-13]]. The word appears in three grammatical forms across the passage — a **noun** ("our stains"), a **verb** ("stain our swords"), and a **past participle** ("be not stained") — arranged symmetrically: **noun → verb → past participle → verb → noun**, pivoting on "that they be not stained in the blood of our brethren" [^1][^2].

## Supporting Evidence
1. **Lexical rarity.** "Stain" occurs in only two other places in the entire Book of Mormon; its concentration here (multiple occurrences in adjacent verses) is statistically unusual and, Jonah argues, signals deliberate patterning rather than incidental usage. *Rating: the rarity claim is checkable and, if accurate, is the strongest single data point — concentration of a rare term is a recognized marker in chiasmus analysis.* [^3]
2. **Morphological symmetry.** The proposed structure depends not just on the word but on its *form* (noun/verb/participle) mirroring outward from the center. Jonah notes that in the underlying Semitic language ("Hebrew, reformed Egyptian, or whatever") these would be visibly distinct forms, so the symmetry is not merely an English artifact [^2].
3. **Steelman of the skeptic.** A short pacifist exhortation about not bloodying swords will *naturally* repeat "stain/blood/swords" in several grammatical forms; finding an ABA arrangement in a 2-3 verse span is low-bar. The presentation supplies no statistical test (e.g., probability the arrangement is chance), and Jonah even concedes an earlier finder spotted the stain pattern — so attribution and novelty are muddy [^4]. Rigorous chiasmus criteria would require ruling out that the symmetry is imposed by the analyst's choice of which words to chart.
4. **Prior attestation.** Jonah candidly says "the stain one had been... somebody had found the stain one before," so this layer is not original to him; only the concentric pairing with the brethren chiasm is claimed as new [^4].
