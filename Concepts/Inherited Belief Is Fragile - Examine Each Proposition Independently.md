# Inherited Belief Is Fragile - Examine Each Proposition Independently

## The Point
[[Jacob Hansen]] diagnoses a common faith-crisis pattern: people who lose belief that Joseph Smith was a prophet "almost immediately become atheists."[^1] The cause, he argues, is that their belief in God was never independently examined — it was simply **inherited** alongside belief in Joseph Smith, so when the top of the stack falls, the whole structure goes with it.[^2] The remedy is to examine each proposition (God, Christ, the Restoration) **independently**, finding one's *own* reasons at each level.

## Explanation
1. **The observed pattern is a tell of structural dependence.** That deconversion from Joseph Smith *causes* atheism implies belief in God had been resting on belief in Joseph Smith — "Was their entire belief in God predicated on Joseph Smith?"[^2] A well-built belief in God should be insensitive to a verdict about a nineteenth-century prophet.

2. **Inheritance, not examination, is the root.** Hansen confesses the diagnosis includes himself "to some degree": "many people... just sort of inherit their belief in God and of Christ without ever really examining those propositions independently."[^2]

3. **The diagnostic test.** He poses the question that started his journey: "If Joseph Smith was not who the church claims he was, would I still believe in God?" The honest answer — uncertainty — "worried me" and prompted him to seek independent reasons for belief in God.[^3]

4. **The fix is the anatomy of belief.** Examining each link separately is exactly the three-independent-conversations structure (see [[Anatomy of Belief - Three Independent Conversations]]) and motivates starting apologetics at Level 1 rather than the Restoration (see [[The Four-Level Conversation Structure of Apologetics]]).

5. **Steelman of a critic.** A skeptic might reply that "independent" reasons are illusory — that Level 1 theism is itself culturally inherited and rationalized after the fact. Hansen's counter is procedural: the point is to *actually run* the investigation and not be sure of the outcome in advance, as his own destabilizing uncertainty illustrates.

## Best Quotes
> "Over and over in my life, I would see people lose their belief that Joseph Smith was a prophet and then see them almost immediately become atheists. I would ask myself, 'Why would a person lose their belief in God just because Joseph Smith was a fraud? Was their entire belief in God predicated on Joseph Smith?'" [^1]

> "Many people, including myself to some degree, just sort of inherit their belief in God and of Christ without ever really examining those propositions independently." [^2]

> "If Joseph Smith was not who the church claims he was, would I still believe in God? And when I was not totally sure of that answer, it worried me." [^3]
