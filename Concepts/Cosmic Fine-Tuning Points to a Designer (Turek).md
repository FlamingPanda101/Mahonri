# Cosmic Fine-Tuning Points to a Designer (Turek)

## The Point
Beyond the *fact* of a beginning, Turek argues that the universe's initial conditions and constants are "extremely fine-tuned" for the existence of the universe and of life — and that even atheists admit the appearance of fine-tuning.[^1] There are only three candidate explanations — chance, physical necessity, or design — and Turek judges design the most reasonable: the probabilities are far too small for chance, and the constants *could* have been otherwise, ruling out necessity.[^2] Fine-tuning thus shows that the First Cause is not merely powerful but "supremely intelligent."[^3]

## Evidence
1. **The expansion rate.** Hawking estimates that if the expansion rate one second after the big bang "was different by one part in a hundred thousand million million," the universe would have recollapsed or never formed galaxies — a value "simply put in at the beginning," beyond any evolutionary process.[^1]
2. **The gravitational force.** If gravity "were different by one part in 10^40... our sun would not exist and neither would we" — a precision Turek pictures as "one inch over a scale as wide as the entire known universe," and "there are more than a dozen of these precise values."[^4]
3. **Three options, one best explanation.** "There are actually only three possibilities for the apparent fine-tuning of the universe: chance, physical necessity... or design." Chance fails ("the probabilities are too small"); necessity fails (the conditions could have differed); "the most reasonable conclusion is that the fine-tuning is due to a Designer."[^2]
4. **Why laws at all?** Echoing agnostic cosmologist Paul Davies, Turek presses the prior question of why there are orderly, mathematically describable laws — "laws always come from lawgivers" — and notes the multiverse only "shifts the problem up a level" to unexplained meta-laws.[^5]
5. **Dawkins's recoil.** Asked the origin of the laws of physics, Dawkins answered that invoking a designing intelligence "simply makes a bigger question than what you've solved" — which Turek says merely sets up the "Who made God?" trump card, answered in the next section of the chapter.[^6]

## Best Quotes
> "The most reasonable conclusion is that the fine-tuning is due to a Designer." [^2]

> "That's one inch over a scale as wide as the entire known universe... And there are more than a dozen of these precise values." [^4]

> "Fine-tuning shows that this Being is also supremely intelligent. He not only created the universe — He set up just the right conditions and laws for our existence." [^3]

## LDS Connection
The teleological core — a precisely ordered, life-permitting cosmos best explained by a supreme intelligence — fits Latter-day Saint theism naturally; indeed the Book of Mormon has Alma reason from cosmic order to a "Supreme Creator" (Alma 30:44; see [[Fine-Tuning and the Teleological Argument from Alma 30]]). The honest divergence is again *ex nihilo*: Turek says the Designer "created the universe" and "set up... laws" from nothing, whereas LDS theology holds that God *organized* eternal matter and that fundamental law is co-eternal with Him rather than authored out of nonbeing — "the elements are eternal" (D&C 93:29; cf. [[Abraham 3.25|Abraham 3:25]]). A Latter-day Saint therefore affirms "design over chance and necessity" while reading the Designer as the organizer-and-orderer of an eternal cosmos. See [[Anthropic Fine-Tuning Points to a Designer]] and [[God Is Constrained by Eternal Law Not Unconstrained]].
