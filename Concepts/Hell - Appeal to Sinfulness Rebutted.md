# Appeal to Sinfulness — Rebutted

## The Defense Being Rebutted

To justify God sending humans to eternal damnation, many Christians appeal to
**human sinfulness**: we are all so sinful that we all *deserve* hell, and God
saving even one soul is an incredible act of mercy. True justice would be God
allowing *everyone* to go to hell.

[[Wes Huff]] represents this:

> "There's no such thing as an innocent person... I deserve hell, me, Wes
> Huff, I deserve hell 100% of the time... I am not going to heaven because
> of anything I've done, right? I can't do that." [^1]

## The Rebuttal

### 1. Moral Repugnance

The host finds this defense "extremely distasteful and borderline repulsive."
It makes God's mercy contingent on damning the majority — a vision of divine
love that celebrates mass torture as the baseline of justice. [^2]

### 2. God Created the Depravity

> "If none is good but God, whose fault is that?"

God is the **creator and sustainer** of every soul. If humans are depraved and
worthy of eternal damnation, they were **created that way from nothing**. The
all-powerful, omni-benevolent God either:

- **(a)** Knowingly creates depraved souls doomed for eternal damnation (not
  omni-benevolent or just), OR
- **(b)** Cannot create souls that aren't depraved (not all-powerful) [^3]

Neither option preserves classical theism.

### 3. God's Impassibility Undermines the Offense

If God is impassible — unable to be affected by anything external, "without
passions" as the creeds state — how can any sin committed against him warrant
**eternal suffering**? If God remains entirely unmoved or unaffected by sinful
actions, then eternal damnation for those actions becomes even more
unjustifiable. The punishment is wildly disproportionate to the harm (which,
on impassibility, is zero). [^4]

## The Core Problem

This defense rests on an unstated assumption: that finite, mortal sin warrants
**infinite** punishment in duration and intensity. That assumption is
independently rebutted in [[Hell - Finite Sin vs Infinite Punishment]].
