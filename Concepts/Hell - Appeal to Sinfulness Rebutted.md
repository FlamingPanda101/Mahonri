# Hell — The Appeal to Sinfulness Rebutted

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
Defenders ([[Wes Huff]]) argue God is just in damning humans because *none of us is good* — saving even one soul is pure mercy ([[Romans 6.23|Romans 6:23]], [[Luke 18.19|Luke 18:19]]).

## The Rebuttal
- It rests on the supposition that sin warrants punishment **infinite in duration and intensity** — so trivial unbelief and genocide draw the *same* eternal torment (developed in [[Hell - Finite Sin vs Infinite Punishment]]) [^1].
- It ignores **creation**: if "none is good but God," whose fault is that? God is the creator and sustainer of every soul. So either he knowingly creates depraved souls doomed to hell (not omnibenevolent) or he cannot create non-depraved ones (not omnipotent) [^2].

## Best Quote
> "There's no such thing as an innocent person... I deserve hell 100% of the time... I am not going to heaven because of anything I've done." — [[Wes Huff]] (the view rebutted) [^3]
