# Translation Renders an Ancient Assembly With a Familiar Word

## The Point

"Synagogue" in the Book of Mormon need not denote the formal Second-Temple building that critics date to the Hasmonean period. The concept of a gathering-place for worship — an assembly — is older and looser than that specific institution, and a translation legitimately renders such an ancient assembly with the most familiar English word available. The English text is a *translation* (dated 1829), so an out-of-period label can sit over an in-period referent without the underlying record being wrong. This is the same mechanism the King James translators used when they wrote "candles" for first-century "lamps."

This is the softer, complementary line to [[The Synagogue Is a Post-Exilic Institution Absent From the Old Testament]]: even granting the institution is late, the *word* in the text is a translation choice, not a claim that Nephites built Herodian-style synagogues.

## Evidence

- **The institution is genuinely late, so the label is doing translation work.** Adler/Levine place synagogue buildings no earlier than the 1st century BC, and the word is absent from the OT [^1] — so wherever the Nephite record described a place of assembly, an English rendering would have to reach for a later, familiar term. *Rating: Moderate — applies the established late-dating to the translation question.*
- **Translation anachronism is unavoidable and well-precedented.** The principle that translation imports out-of-period vocabulary over a sound source text is documented from the KJV itself and conceded by modern critical translations (see [[Translation Introduces Anachronism Even in Divine Translation]]). *Rating: Strong — supported by a separate, verifiable line of evidence in this vault.*
- **The mismatch is expected, not damning.** Arnold's broader frame: the Book of Mormon "doesn't look like the Judaism that was assembled after Lehi left, which is exactly what you'd expect" [^2] — i.e., the institution's later naming is downstream of when the record originated. *Rating: Moderate — interpretive, tied to the text's own chronology.*

## Best Quotes

> "When Lehi's family departed, the synagogue didn't exist yet... So when people say that the Book of Mormon doesn't look like ancient Judaism, they're right. It doesn't look like the Judaism that was assembled after Lehi left, which is exactly what you'd expect from a record originating when and where the Book of Mormon says it did." [^2]

> "The Hebrew Bible covers Israelite history from the patriarchs through the Persian return, and it never once mentions a synagogue." [^1]

## LDS Connection

Reading the Book of Mormon as a translated ancient text (see [[Read the Book of Mormon as a Translated Ancient Text]]) dissolves the "synagogue" charge twice over: the formal institution post-dates Lehi (so it is not claimed to be present), and any genuine assembly the record described would naturally be rendered with the reader's familiar word. The origins of the institution itself remain debated in scholarship, which further weakens any confident anachronism verdict. Pairs with [[One Anachronism Does Not Disprove the Book of Mormon]].
