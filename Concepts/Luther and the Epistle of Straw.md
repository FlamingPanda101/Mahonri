# Luther and the "Epistle of Straw"

## The Claim
[[Martin Luther]] had a deeply conflicted relationship with the letter of James because it teaches justification by works — but, contrary to a common Catholic overstatement, he never removed it from the Bible.

## Supporting Evidence
- Luther's 1522 preface derided James as "an epistle of straw"; he later admitted it "promulgates the laws of God" *(primary, Luther)* [^1].
- He mused about "throwing Jimmy into the stove" (alluding to a priest who burned apostle statues for warmth) and said "some Jew wrote it... I'll oppose them and urge works alone" *(primary, Luther)* [^2].
- Yet he never tried to have it removed from the canon — "Luther walked really close to that line, but he didn't cross it" *(Trent Horn's correction of the Catholic claim)* [^3].

## Best Quote
> "I almost feel like throwing Jimmy into the stove, as the priest in Kalenberg did." — [[Martin Luther]] [^2]
