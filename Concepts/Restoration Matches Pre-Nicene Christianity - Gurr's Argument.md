# Restoration Matches Pre-Nicene Christianity - Gurr's Argument

## The Point
**Claim ([[Robert Gurr]]):** Joseph Smith "did not innovate. He restored the original Christian formula." Reading the earliest post-apostolic writers — Clement of Rome, Ignatius, [[Justin Martyr]], [[Irenaeus of Lyons|Irenaeus]], [[Tertullian]], [[Hippolytus of Rome|Hippolytus]], Cyprian, Cyril, Origen, [[Augustine]] — Gurr argues they attest a cluster of doctrines the later creeds erased and the Restoration recovered: (1) the gospel *sequence* faith → repentance → baptism by immersion for remission of sin → the gift of the Holy Ghost by laying on of hands, with infant baptism appearing only ~215 AD (Hippolytus), called "tradition, not scripture" by Origen, canonized at Carthage 250 AD, and universalized by Augustine's claim that unbaptized infants go to hell; (2) chrismation/anointing as the moment one was actually *called* a Christian (Cyril of Jerusalem) — restored in the temple initiatory; (3) a *social, subordinationist* godhead ("there is another god subject to the Father" — Justin; "two gods," *duos theous* — Tertullian; "the Son is the second God" — Origen), not the one-mind Trinity; and (4) deification, premortality, the two-tier priesthood, and free will against Luther's "bonded will." His refrain: "Joseph Smith did not know this... when you have eliminated the impossible, whatever remains... must be the truth."[^1]

**Rebuttal ([[Bill Reel]]):** Reel's structural reply is that "appealing to shared Christian beliefs doesn't prove Mormonism true. It just shows overlap. Lots of religions in Christianity get part of Christianity right. The real test of Mormonism is its unique claims — and that's exactly where the evidence falls short." He also notes the deeper oddity that the argument must "assume that Christianity is true to even have a starting place."[^2]

## Evidence
1. **The patristic catalogue (rated: moderate-to-strong on individual data points, contested in synthesis).** Many of Gurr's specifics align with the vault's own restoration notes: infant baptism post-dating its rationale ([[Infant Baptism Preceded Its Theological Rationale]], [[Infant Salvation and the Pelagius-Augustine Dispute over Original Sin]]), early baptism as washing/rebirth ([[Early Christian Baptism as Death and Rebirth]], [[Baptismal Washing and Anointing in Early Christianity]]), pre-Nicene "three beings" language ([[Most Christians Describe Three Beings Not the Trinity]], [[Trinity Was Constructed Not Revealed]]), and the broad thesis [[LDS Theology as Restoration of Early Christianity]].
2. **Reel's "overlap ≠ proof" is the decisive counter (rated: strong, framing).** Establishing that the early church looks *more LDS than creedal* shows the creeds drifted, but does not establish the Book of Mormon, the Book of Abraham, or Joseph's prophetic calling — those rest on the *unique* claims Reel attacks elsewhere. This is the same logic the vault applies in reverse to parallels: a parallel is not a proof ([[A Parallel List Without Textual Analysis Carries No Burden]]).
3. **The bootstrapping worry (rated: moderate).** Reel flags that Gurr's whole second segment presupposes Christianity's truth; for an audience not already Christian, "restored *Christianity*" is question-begging. *Steelman of Gurr:* his first segment tries to earn theism independently (creation → a powerful, loving, therefore communicating God), so the restoration argument is meant to *follow* that foundation, not float free — see [[Skepticism Must Be Applied Consistently to Atheism Too]].

## Best Quotes
> "Joseph Smith did not innovate. He restored the original Christian formula — the same formula taught by the New Testament apostles and practiced by the earliest disciples." [^1]

> "It was right around the year 215 AD when Hippolytus was about the first to defend infant baptism, and Origen calls it a tradition but not scripture." [^1]

> "Appealing to shared Christian beliefs doesn't prove Mormonism true. It just shows overlap... The real test of Mormonism is its unique claims, and that's exactly where the evidence falls short." — Reel [^2]

## LDS Connection
This is the affirmative core of the LDS apologetic for an apostasy-and-restoration: the patristic record is read as a snapshot of pre-creedal belief that the Nicene settlement overwrote ([[Canonization Forced the Harmonization That Produced the Trinity]], [[The Godhead Model vs the Trinity Model]]). The vault treats these patristic claims as among the stronger arrows in the LDS quiver — but Reel's "overlap is not proof" rejoinder is also endorsed in principle elsewhere in the vault, so the honest verdict is that this argument *clears the ground* (showing the creeds are not the original faith) without by itself *building the house* (Joseph's unique restored scriptures still bear their own evidentiary burden).
