# To Be Means To Breathe — Hebrew Ontology of Action

## The Point
In Hebrew, *to exist* is not to be a static object but to *act*. The verb "to be" carries the sense "to breathe," so a thing **is what it does**. This collapses the Greek noun/verb divide: there are no inert essences, only living action. It is the ontology beneath the whole talk's view of truth, repentance, and identity.

## Argument
1. **"To be" = "to breathe."** Greek treats a man as "the thing that breathes" — breathing is a verb you *do*, separable from the existing thing. Hebrew (*hayah*) fuses them: "if you don't breathe, there is no Jacob." Existence is the act of breathing, not a substance that happens to breathe [^1].
2. **Scriptural anchor: God breathed life into Adam** (Genesis 2:7). So when Jehovah says "I AM THAT I AM," another reading is "I am the Giver of breath, I am the Breather" — breath as action, not static being [^2].
3. **A thing is what it does.** A tree is not a tree because you can dissect a trunk, branches, leaves; it is a tree because it *grows* leaves, *gives* shade, *gives* fruit. "If you don't give fruit, you're not a tree." Hence when Christ curses the fig tree, he is not saying "this is a fig tree lacking figs" but "this *isn't* a fig tree" [^3].
4. **Identity follows action — the corpse example.** A Greek sees a grave: "here lies Bob." A Hebrew says "that's not Bob — Bob walked and talked and liked chocolate ice cream and played with my kids; that's a corpse." The person *was* his actions; the static body is not him [^4].
5. **"Face" confirms the pattern.** Greek *prosopon* ("face") literally means "the thing you look at" — passive, acted-upon, lifeless. Hebrew *panim* ("face") is "what a person *turns toward* me" — active. Again: Greek = the dead seen object; Hebrew = the living acting subject [^5].
6. **Even speech is action.** Greek *logos* (word) derives from a root "to gather" — you gather thoughts privately, *then* speak; the thinking is the real thing. Hebrew *dabar* (word) is the *same idea as acting*: "when you speak you put life and action into the world." Nothing has happened until you speak/act [^6].

## Best Quotes
> "A tree is a tree because it grows leaves, it sheds leaves, it gives shade, it gives fruit. If you don't give fruit, you're not a tree. So when Christ curses the fig tree... he's saying this isn't a fig tree. You are what you do." [^3]

> "A Greek would look at a grave and say 'here lies Bob.' A Hebrew would say that's not Bob — Bob was a man who walked and talked and liked chocolate ice cream and played with my kids... that's a corpse." [^4]
