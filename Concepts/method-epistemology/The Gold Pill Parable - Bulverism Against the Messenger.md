# The Gold Pill Parable - Bulverism Against the Messenger

## Overview

A teaching parable from [[Cardon Ellis]] on the [[Ethers Elephant - When History Challenges Testimony Can Your Faith Survive|Ether's Elephant]] livestream, illustrating C.S. Lewis's idea of **bulverism** — the fallacy of refuting a claim by attacking the person who made it instead of the claim itself. A terminally ill patient is healed by a gold pill given by a kind stranger. Later a nurse claims the stranger murdered her brother. "Certainly, it would be a mistake to claim that because of that knowledge, the gold pill didn't work." The cure's efficacy is independent of the curer's moral character.

## The Claim

Applied to [[Joseph Smith]]: even granting (for argument) that he did something immoral, "we should not reject the message because of the messenger" — to do so is "a textbook example of bulverism." The historical/moral character of the founder is a separate question from whether the Restoration's claims are true. The scriptural twin is the man born blind ([[John 9.1-3|John 9]]): the healed man refuses to litigate whether Jesus is "a sinner" — "What I can speak to though is the fact that I can see." The testimony rests on the fruit (sight, healing), not on adjudicating the messenger's biography.

This connects to the broader diagnosis that a [[Inverted Testimony - Why Faith Built Top-Down Collapses|testimony built top-down on history]] collapses, while one resting on a spiritual witness survives a historical shock, because [[History Is Bad Data - The Wrong Instrument to Falsify Faith|history is the wrong instrument]] to falsify faith.
