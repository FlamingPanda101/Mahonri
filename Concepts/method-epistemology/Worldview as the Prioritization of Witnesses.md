# Worldview as the Prioritization of Witnesses

## The Point
A corollary [[Jacob Hansen]] draws from [[The Collective Witness Model of Knowing]]: because the [[The Five Witnesses of Reality|five witnesses]] are "literally the tools we use to map reality," *how a person feels about them — which ones they prioritize and how they apply them — is the foundation of that person's worldview*. Disagreements between worldviews are, at root, disagreements about which witnesses to trust and how to weigh them.[^1]

## Argument
1. **Witnesses are the mapping tools.** The five witnesses are the faculties by which anyone maps reality, so one's stance toward them shapes one's whole picture of the world.[^1]
2. **Worldview = the weighting.** The relative priority a person assigns to intuition, outcomes, sense data, reason, and authority — and how they apply each — *constitutes* their worldview. "This should make perfect sense."[^1]
3. **Two failure modes (steelman).** (a) Those who **refuse to account for all** the witnesses will have a limited view of reality. (b) Those who **over-emphasize or misapply** witnesses end up with a distorted view — "even if they're quite sincere."[^2]
4. **Programmatic upshot.** Hansen frames the model not as settling every nuance of knowledge but as a lens you "will see all the time," and previews future videos on how the witnesses get *misapplied.*[^3]

## Best Quotes
> "The way people feel about these witnesses and which ones they prioritize and how they apply them is the foundation of a person's worldview... as these witnesses are literally the tools we use to map reality." [^1]

> "Those who refuse to account for all of these witnesses will be limiting their view of reality, and those who over-emphasize or misapply them will end up with a distorted view of reality even if they're quite sincere." [^2]
