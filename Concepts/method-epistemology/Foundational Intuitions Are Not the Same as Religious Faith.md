# Foundational Intuitions Are Not the Same as Religious Faith

## The Point
[[Jordan Peterson]] argues every domain — even math, logic, physics — eventually rests on a non-self-justifying move he calls a "statement of faith." [[Sam Harris]] resists the word: such foundations are *intuitions*, defeasible and revisable, categorically unlike the dogmatic propositional claims of religion.[^1]

## Argument

1. **Peterson's bootstrapping claim.** "In every domain of human inquiry, no matter how hard-headed... at some point we have to pull ourselves up by our bootstraps; at some point we make a move that is not self-justifying." He labels this a universal "statement of faith."[^2]

2. **Harris: that is intuition, not faith.** "My intuition that this is a valid and replicable and generalizable principle" is not faith — these "are intuitions that can run afoul of other discoveries and other intuitions." *Steelman: faith resists revision; intuition invites it.*[^3]

3. **The Riemann example.** For ~2,000 years humanity shared well-worked Euclidean intuitions about flat space; then a "brilliant guy" (Riemann) showed you can curve space so a triangle exceeds 180°. The intuition was "tuned up pretty quickly" once seen. Intuition is "the thing you're using to understand something that you are not in a position to analyze" — and it is correctable.[^4]

4. **The contrast with religious faith.** This is "not faith of the sort which is: I know the Bible was dictated by the creator of the universe, I know Jesus was his son, I know he rose from the dead, I know he'll be coming back" — a stack of fixed propositional claims "decided for all time." Peterson presses that a value-domain intuition still "is not derived from fact"; Harris holds the asymmetry is precisely *revisability*. See [[You Cannot Derive Values From Facts Without an A Priori Structure]].[^5]

## Best Quotes
> "At some point we make a move that is not self-justifying and is not justified by any other move that's more rudimentary; that's a statement of faith." [^2]

> "These are intuitions — and they're intuitions that can run afoul of other discoveries and other intuitions." [^3]

> "That's not faith of the sort which is: I know the Bible was dictated by the creator of the universe, I know Jesus was his son, I know he rose from the dead." [^5]
