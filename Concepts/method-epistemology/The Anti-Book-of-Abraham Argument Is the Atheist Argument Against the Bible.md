# The Anti-Book-of-Abraham Argument Is the Atheist Argument Against the Bible

## The Claim

A central [[Ward Radio]] thesis against [[Mike Winger]]: nearly every argument Winger deploys against the [[Book of Abraham]] is **the same move atheists make against the Bible** — and Winger, an evangelical, rejects those moves when his own scripture is the target. The argument exposes a double standard rather than refuting any specific datum.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. "Late manuscripts" cuts both ways — rate: strong (parity)
Winger faults the Book of Abraham because the papyrus postdates Abraham by ~1,800 years. But the oldest manuscripts of Genesis/Exodus/Deuteronomy postdate Moses by centuries; the oldest copies of Mark/John/Acts postdate Jesus by generations (and Mark's long ending and the woman-taken-in-adultery pericope are textually late). An atheist uses "our copies are centuries later" against the Bible; Winger answers (rightly) "those are copies of an original" — the exact answer available for Abraham. See [[The Hand of Abraham Phrase Is Ancient Copyist Usage Not Autograph]].[^2]

### 2. "Scholars say it's fabricated" cuts both ways — rate: strong (parity)
Winger quotes Egyptologists calling the Book of Abraham "a pure fabrication." A 30-second search yields scholars calling the *Bible* "neither good history nor good science," "fiction," "imaginative entertainment." Winger discounts those because "there are good answers" he has researched — the very stance he denies Latter-day Saints.[^3]

### 3. "No archaeological proof" cuts both ways — rate: moderate (parity)
Winger leans on Egyptology's confidence, but biblical archaeology cannot name the Pharaoh of the Exodus, has no chariot wheel from the Red Sea, no evidence Jesus' family sojourned in Egypt, and long denied figures/events (Daniel's lions, Israel before the Merneptah Stele) later found or still disputed. The fields he trusts say "we don't know enough" exactly as Egyptology does. (King Tut's tomb was found only ~1922.)[^4]

## Steelman of the Opposing View

The parity argument is rhetorically strong but has limits. (a) *Tu quoque* is a fallacy: showing that Winger is inconsistent does not show the Book of Abraham is *true* — both his scripture and Joseph's could face genuine problems, and "you have the same issue" leaves the Book of Abraham's specific difficulties (the facsimile interpretations Egyptology rejects) unanswered. (b) Winger can claim a *relevant disanalogy*: for the Book of Abraham we possess the *very source document* and can check the translation against the characters, whereas for most biblical books we lack the autograph — so the "late copies" parity is imperfect (this is precisely his framing, contested in [[Joseph Was Not Translating the Surviving Papyrus]]). (c) The Bible's historicity, while debated, enjoys far more external corroboration (named kings, places, the Tel Dan and Merneptah inscriptions) than the Book of Abraham's narrative, so "they're in the same boat" understates the asymmetry. So the legitimate payload is about *consistency of method*: Winger should apply to the Book of Abraham the same charity (copies-of-originals, "there are good answers," evolving archaeology) he applies to the Bible — a fair demand — while recognizing that establishing the Book of Abraham's truth requires the *positive* case (corroborating later texts, facsimile hits), not merely tu-quoque against the critic.
