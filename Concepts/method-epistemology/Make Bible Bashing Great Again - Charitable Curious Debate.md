# Make Bible Bashing Great Again - Charitable Curious Debate

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Cardon Ellis]], [[Jacob Hansen]], and [[Luke Hansen]] want to *reclaim* religious debate — "make Bible bashing great again" — but redefined as **charitable, curious conversation** rather than the contentious, score-settling "Bible bash" the term usually connotes in Latter-day Saint culture. The aim of an exchange should be to *build bridges and understand*, using Socratic questioning and steelmanning (they cite Greg Koukl's *Tactics*), not to "own" or humiliate an opponent. Genuine curiosity about why the other person believes what they do is treated as both more Christlike and more persuasive than combative point-scoring.

## Supporting Evidence
1. **Socratic questioning shifts the burden gently.** Asking sincere questions ("What do you mean by that?" "How did you come to that conclusion?" — Koukl's "Columbo" tactic) lets the interlocutor examine their own view without being attacked, lowering defenses and keeping the conversation alive. This is the relational form of the vault's [[Apologetics Is Socratic and Aimed at the Audience Not the Interlocutor]]. *Rating: strong — well-attested as both more ethical and more effective than adversarial debate.*
2. **Steelmanning earns credibility.** Stating the opponent's position better than they could before responding signals fairness and ensures you are answering the *real* argument, not a caricature — which makes any subsequent response land harder. *Rating: strong methodological norm.*
3. **The goal is the relationship and the audience, not the "win."** Reframing debate as bridge-building means a "loss" of the argument can still be a "win" for the relationship and for any onlookers — consistent with the view that apologetics aims at the *audience* and at long-term trust, not at crushing the person across the table. *Rating: a humane and durable reframing.*

**Steelman of the skeptical counter.** A critic notes the slogan is in tension with itself: "Bible bashing" is precisely the *contentious* mode the same hosts elsewhere disavow ("contention is of the devil"), and rebranding it risks *aestheticizing* combat while keeping the adversarial frame. There is also a real worry that "just ask questions" can be a disguised debate tactic — Socratic method weaponized to *trap* rather than to *understand* — which would betray the charity it advertises. And curiosity can shade into a performance for the audience rather than genuine regard for the person. A measured reply: the proposal's explicit content is the *opposite* of weaponization — steelmanning and sincere curiosity are defined *against* gotcha tactics — and "make Bible bashing great again" is self-aware wordplay, reclaiming a culturally loaded phrase rather than endorsing its old meaning. The honest core: replace contentious point-scoring with charitable, curious, well-prepared conversation; the slogan is rhetorical packaging for that substance.

## Best Quotes
> Reclaim charitable, curious debate — Socratic questioning and steelmanning (Koukl's *Tactics*) to build bridges rather than "own" opponents.

## LDS Relevance
This supplies the *method* and *tone* for the institutional proposal in [[A Church-Sanctioned Apologetics Program to Inoculate the Youth]]: equipping members is only safe if the equipping teaches *charitable* engagement, not combative apologetics. It also answers the cultural baggage of the term "Bible bash" (long discouraged among Latter-day Saints) by redefining the activity rather than abandoning engagement. Pairs with [[The Scale Replaces the Shelf as the Model of Belief]] and [[Apologetics Is Socratic and Aimed at the Audience Not the Interlocutor]].
