# Action Is the Greater Expression of Truth

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Shad Brooks]] argues that **acted truth outranks spoken truth** by "orders of magnitude": "Truth expressed and acted and shown is vastly, in orders of magnitude, more important and more powerful than truth expressed in word."[^1] His illustration: "I can say till the cows come home that murder is bad... but if I go out and murder someone, it means the words I said were lip service... The way I act reveals the truth that I know, the amount of truth that I possess, and how much I live in line with it."[^2] Because of the truth=morality identity (see [[Truth Is Law Is Morality - The D&C 88 Equivalence Chain]]), God is therefore "vastly more concerned with how we act and what we do than necessarily the facts that we know or what we believe."[^3] This yields a provocative downstream claim: the reason Joseph Smith's first vision called the creeds "an abomination" is that **creedalism inverts the God-given priority** — making "belief in a code... more important than action in real life," when "behavior is more important than belief."[^4]

## Supporting Evidence
- **The two-sons parable (Matthew 21).** Cardon and Shad anchor the claim in [[Matthew 21.28-32|Matthew 21:28-32]]: of two sons, one says "I will not" but goes, the other says "I go, sir" but goes not — and the first "did the will of his father."[^5] "Saying the right thing but not doing it is cheap." Action is "the actual true manifestation and proof of what you believe and the truth that you know." Rate: **the load-bearing proof-text** — Jesus Himself ranks doing over saying.
- **"Not everyone that saith Lord, Lord" (Matt. 7:21).** [[Jonah Barnes]] reinforces: "not all who say unto me, Lord, Lord, will enter... but he that doeth the will of my father." "It's an action principle. Truth requires action."[^6] The same logic appears in [[D&C 93]]:28 ("he that keepeth his commandments receiveth truth and light") — obedience yields truth as a *natural byproduct*, because keeping commandments *is* acting out truth. Rate: strong convergent scriptural support.
- **The anti-Mormonism diagnosis.** The group notes (citing Jonah Goldberg after the Grand Blanc, Michigan attack) the "oddity of anti-Mormonism": Latter-day Saints are model neighbors "in terms of how they behave," yet are deemed non-Christian on *belief* grounds — exhibiting precisely the belief-over-behavior inversion the creeds represent.[^7] Rate: a sharp applied case; ties the abstract claim to a concrete sociological observation.
- **Connection to the fruit-over-theology debate.** This is the same nerve the vault treats in [[Fruit Versus Theology - Are They Separable]] and [[Creedalism Replaced Relationship With Theology]] — Shad's contribution is to *ground* the priority in his theory of truth (action is the higher expression of truth itself), not merely assert it pastorally.
- **Steelman the critic.** A creedal interlocutor will object that this is a false dichotomy: orthodoxy claims right belief and right action are *both* required, that James's "faith without works is dead" already integrates them, and that Christ also says "if ye love me, keep my commandments" — making belief the *root* of action, not its rival. The "behavior > belief" slogan, pressed hard, risks reducing doctrinal truth-claims to mere instruments — yet Shad himself concedes "what we believe is important to be true." The strong form (creeds are an abomination *because* they prioritize belief) also reads a specific modern thesis back into the 1820 vision; compare the narrower vault reading [[The Creeds Were an Abomination Meant Sectarian Dogmatism Not the Ecumenical Creeds]].

## Best Quotes

And if God is saying that no, it's actually the other way around — behavior is more important than belief... that's why he would say the creeds are [an] abomination, because they've become the inversion of the way I want you to behave."
