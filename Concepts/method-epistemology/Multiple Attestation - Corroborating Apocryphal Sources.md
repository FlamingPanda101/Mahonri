# Multiple Attestation — Corroborating Apocryphal Sources

## The Claim
An apocryphal tradition gains credibility when **many independent sources** attest it — the same historiographical method used to reconstruct ancient history. A single source warrants suspicion; broad, independent agreement does not.

## Supporting Evidence
- The Alexander the Great analogy: one city's self-serving claim is suspect, but ~15 independent historians describing his presence make it credible *(methodological argument)* [^1].
- The unburiable-Abel tradition is attested across the Armenian *History of the Sons of Adam*, the *Cave of Treasures* ([[Ephrem the Syrian]]), the Qur'an, the Greek and Egyptian *Life of Adam and Eve*, and the *Penitence of Adam* — they differ on details (who tried to bury him) but agree no one could *(multiple attestation)* [^2].
- "Whenever there's like one source, I'm always like, you got to watch out" *(stated principle)* [^3].

## Best Quote
> "There's lots of sources... one will say Cain tried burying him, Cain's children tried, or Adam and Eve tried — but they all agree that nobody could bury him." — [[Jonah (Plain and Precious Publishing)|Jonah]] [^2]
