# Greater Light Reveals Pre-Existing Truth

## The Point
Truth exists independently of whether anyone perceives or believes it; what changes over time is **our capacity to see it**, not the truth itself. A [[Dieter F. Uchtdorf|Uchtdorf]] clip uses the astronomical discovery of other galaxies as the paradigm: the universe was always vast, but humanity could not "see and understand this truth" until instruments could "gather celestial light." Applied to religion, this reframes the rejection of spiritual truth not as the truth's absence but as a limit of sight — and, where willful, as "self-inflicted spiritual blindness" ([[Henry B. Eyring]]).

## Argument
1. Less than a century ago most astronomers assumed the Milky Way was the only galaxy and beyond it lay "an immense nothingness… an infinite void." [^1]
2. As telescopes (including space telescopes) improved, astronomers grasped that "the universe is mind-bogglingly bigger than anyone had previously believed," filled with "numberless galaxies." [^1]
3. The key inference: "The immensity of the universe didn't suddenly change, but our ability to see and understand this truth changed dramatically." Before instruments powerful enough to "gather celestial light," "we did not believe such a thing was possible." [^2]
4. Generalization: "Often truth is rejected because it doesn't appear to be consistent with previous experiences." But "truth… exists beyond belief; it is true even if nobody believes it." [^3]
5. Epistemic humility follows: "We simply don't know all things… we see through a glass darkly," so we "have to trust the Lord, who sees all things clearly." There is "one source of truth that is complete, correct, and incorruptible" — the Father, who knows truth "as it was, as it is, and as it yet will be." [^4]
6. Conversely (per [[Henry B. Eyring]]), those who lose faith "because of secular teachings" suffer "self-inflicted spiritual blindness" — "their problem does not lie in what they think they see; it lies in what they cannot see." [^5]
7. **Steelman of the objector.** A skeptic notes the disanalogy: the galaxy case was confirmed by *publicly verifiable, repeatable instruments* that anyone can check, whereas "spiritual sight" is private and unfalsifiable, so calling disbelief "blindness" can beg the question (the truth might genuinely be absent, not merely unseen). The LDS reply leans on the prior arguments — that nearly all knowledge is probabilistic and tradition-borne (see [[Knowledge Runs on Degrees of Probability Not Certitude]]), and that spiritual perception, like scientific instrumentation, is a means appropriate to its object.

## Best Quotes

> "The immensity of the universe didn't suddenly change, but our ability to see and understand this truth changed dramatically." [^2]

> "Truth… exists beyond belief. It is true even if nobody believes it." [^3]

> "We see through a glass darkly. We have to trust the Lord, who sees all things clearly. There is one source of truth that is complete, correct, and incorruptible." [^4]

> "Those who once had spiritual vision can suffer from self-inflicted spiritual blindness… their problem does not lie in what they think they see; it lies in what they cannot see." [^5]
