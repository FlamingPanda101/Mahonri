# The Least-Conjecture Test and the Goalpost-Moving Pattern

## The Point
**Claim ([[Bill Reel]]):** This is the methodological backbone of Reel's whole case. The right question in any dispute "isn't who has the answer — both sides are going to have answers"; it is "which explanation makes the most sense with the least amount of stretching." A rational thinker is "obligated to pick the conclusion that requires the least amount of conjecture or allowances." On that test Mormonism shows a recurring *pattern*: it starts with a definite, falsifiable claim; disconfirming data arrives; and the claim is then retreated to something unfalsifiable. His worked examples: Israelites as the principal ancestors of Native Americans → moved by DNA; the Book of Abraham as "the writings of Abraham by his own hand" → moved to the catalyst theory; Joseph's "inspired" Bible as a restoration of the original → now conceded (even by BYU's Thomas Wayment) to lean on Adam Clark's commentary and to move *away* from the earliest manuscripts; a tight word-for-word Book of Mormon translation (imposed by D&C 10) → moved to Blake Ostler's "expansion theory" once 19th-century material and KJV parallels surfaced. "The critic hasn't budged... it's the believer, the apologist, and the church that has had to change."[^1]

**Rebuttal ([[Robert Gurr]]):** Gurr turns the same blade back: Reel uses an unfalsifiable argument himself when convenient. Reel says "horses didn't exist," but "you're trying to prove a negative" — and "absence of evidence is not evidence of absence." So Reel "only wants to criticize unfalsifiable arguments when they support your side rather than using critical thinking." More broadly, expert consensus "is nothing more than groupthink," and groupthink "is wrong oftentimes" (the geocentric consensus lasted millennia), so both sets of evidence must be weighed, not one side deferred to.[^2]

## Evidence
1. **The least-conjecture razor is a real principle, but its application is contested (rated: moderate).** Parsimony is legitimate; the difficulty is that "fewest allowances" is itself a judgment call, and the believer can argue that a single coherent thesis (God restored truth through a prophet) is *more* parsimonious than a pile of independent naturalistic just-so stories. The vault's standing reply is that the proper bar is *convergence*, not deductive proof ([[Convergence Not Proof Is the Standard for Book of Mormon Historicity]]).
2. **The goalpost catalogue is Reel's strongest cumulative move (rated: strong rhetorically, partly answerable case-by-case).** Listing DNA, the Book of Abraham, the JST, and translation theory as serial retreats is potent — and Gurr's catalyst concession ([[The Book of Abraham Has Only Three Apologetic Exits - Reel]]) hands Reel one clean instance. The LDS counter is that *refining* a claim under new evidence is normal inquiry, not bad faith — but Reel pre-empts this: refinement that always lands on the unfalsifiable side is the tell.
3. **Gurr's "you do it too / consensus is groupthink" tu quoque (rated: mixed).** The "proving a negative" point has force in the abstract — but Reel answers it precisely: "absence of evidence is not evidence of absence" only licenses *plausibility/what-if*, whereas "my critical brain has to go with the best conclusion in the here and now," which is the 98.7% expert view, not a future maybe.[^3] The "groupthink" move is weaker; reflexively discounting a near-unanimous specialist consensus is itself the failure mode Reel names. *Steelman of Gurr:* his real point is symmetry — the unfalsifiability charge cuts against naturalism too ("heads naturalism wins, tails give it time"), the substance of [[Heads Faith Wins, Tails Reason Loses - The Unfalsifiability Charge]] and [[The Falsifiability Demand Against the Air-We-Breathe Thesis]].

## Best Quotes
> "The real question isn't who has the answer. Both sides are going to have answers. The question is which explanation makes the most sense with the least amount of stretching." [^1]

> "If you're allowed to move the goalpost on every issue, who is most likely telling you the truth? The critic hasn't budged... Maybe it's the one that stayed consistent." [^1]

> "You only want to criticize unfalsifiable arguments when they support your side rather than using critical thinking." — Gurr [^2]

> "Consensus is nothing more than groupthink, and groupthink can and is wrong oftentimes." — Gurr [^2]

## LDS Connection
Reel's least-conjecture/anti-goalpost argument is the unifying logic behind every specific challenge in this debate (anachronisms, the Book of Abraham, polygamy), so the LDS reply must operate at the same meta level. The vault's answer is three-pronged: (1) the symmetry rejoinder — naturalism is no less prone to promissory unfalsifiability ([[Heads Faith Wins, Tails Reason Loses - The Unfalsifiability Charge]]); (2) the standard-of-evidence rejoinder — historicity claims are vindicated by *convergence*, not by surviving every conceivable disconfirmation ([[Convergence Not Proof Is the Standard for Book of Mormon Historicity]]); and (3) the "could God vs. would God" rejoinder, distinguishing what the evidence permits from what a critic *expects* it to look like ([[Could God vs Would God on the Book of Mormon]]). The honest concession is that an apologetic which *only* ever retreats to the unfalsifiable has lost the argument on Reel's terms — which is exactly why the vault favors trend-line and convergence arguments that make positive, riskable predictions.
