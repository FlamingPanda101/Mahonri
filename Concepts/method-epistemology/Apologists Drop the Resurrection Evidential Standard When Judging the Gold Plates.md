# Apologists Drop the Resurrection Evidential Standard When Judging the Gold Plates

#type/claim

## The Claim
Evangelical apologists argue *like believers* when defending the Resurrection — inference to the best explanation from named, multiply-attested, willing-to-die witnesses — but switch to arguing *like skeptics* the moment the very same evidential method is applied to the Book of Mormon's gold plates and their 11+ named witnesses. The double standard is the point: the rule used to license belief in the empty tomb is suspended when it would license the plates. As a sharpening example, [[Frank Turek]] is shown overstating the resurrection case (treating Luke and Paul as resurrection *eyewitnesses* when Luke was not, and Paul reports a vision), even while demanding far stricter standards of the plate witnesses. Raised by [[Cardon Ellis]] and [[Jonah Barnes]] reacting to a *Cross Examined* campus clip in which [[Hayden Carroll]] presses Turek on inerrancy and method.

## Supporting Evidence
1. **The methods really are the same — minimal-facts / best-explanation.** The standard evangelical resurrection case (Habermas/Licona "minimal facts": multiple early sources, eyewitness testimony, transformation of the disciples, willingness to suffer/die) is the *identical* logic the vault applies to the plates in [[The Minimal Facts Method Applied to the Gold Plates]]. If the criteria are sound for the Resurrection, evenhandedness requires applying them to the plate witnesses, who are *named, signed, and (unlike the 500 of 1 Cor 15) individually identifiable*. *Rating: strong as a consistency argument — it uses the critic's own accepted tools.*
2. **The 1 Corinthians 15:6 asymmetry.** Apologists lean on Paul's "above five hundred brethren" ([[1 Corinthians 15.6]]) as mass corroboration, yet no extant first-hand account *names* any of those five hundred — the believer is *inferring* witnesses from a single second-hand line — exactly the kind of inference they deny the Book of Mormon's eleven *named and signed* witnesses. *Rating: strong — a clean, documented instance of the asymmetry.*
3. **Turek's eyewitness overstatement.** Turek's "the apostles were confirmed by miracles" and his treatment of Luke/Paul as resurrection eyewitnesses overstate the evidence in the *favorable* direction, the mirror image of the deflation applied to the plates. *Rating: moderate — illustrative of motivated standard-setting, drawn from one clip.*

**Steelman of the skeptical counter.** A critic answers that the cases are *not* parallel, so applying one standard to both is itself the error: the Resurrection has many independent first-century sources across the Gospels and Paul, an enemy attestation (Paul's conversion), and a public, falsifiable claim (an empty tomb in Jerusalem), whereas the plate witnesses are a small, religiously affiliated group whose testimony was published together, several of whom later left the church (though most reaffirmed their testimony), and whose "seeing" some critics read as visionary rather than physical. A measured reply: several of those disanalogies cut the other way — the plate witnesses are *better* documented as individuals than the 500, they signed *fixed* statements, hostile and later-estranged witnesses (e.g., who left the church yet never recanted) carry the same "against interest" force the apologist prizes in Paul, and the "merely visionary" charge, if fatal to the plates, is equally fatal to Paul's Damascus-road experience. The honest core that survives is narrow but real: *whatever* evidential bar is set for the Resurrection should be set for the plates, and apologists who raise it selectively are special-pleading.

## LDS Relevance
This is a *method* note, not a historicity claim: it does not argue the plates are genuine, only that the standard evangelical resurrection apologetic cannot consistently exclude them. It pairs with [[The Minimal Facts Method Applied to the Gold Plates]] (the positive application) and supplies the rhetorical counter to inerrancy/eyewitness arguments raised in the source — see [[John 10-35's Unbreakable Scripture Is the Deification Text Ye Are Gods|John 10:35's Unbreakable Scripture Is the Deification Text Ye Are Gods]] for the companion John 10:35 catch in the same clip.
