# Ecumenical Foundation - Borrow Non-LDS Christian Resources for Levels One and Two

## The Point
[[Jacob Hansen]] argues that on the foundational conversations — that God exists (Level 1) and that the New Testament Jesus is the Savior (Level 2) — Latter-day Saints and other Christians are "on the same page," and that Christians of other denominations have produced "amazing resources" in these areas.[^1] His course therefore deliberately draws on **both LDS and non-LDS thinkers**,[^2] with [[C.S. Lewis]]'s *Mere Christianity* as the model Level 2 text. Ignoring such works, he says, "does ourselves a great disservice."[^1]

## Explanation
1. **Shared ground at the foundation.** "Our Christian friends and neighbors are on the same page with us in establishing that God exists and that the Jesus Christ in the New Testament is the savior of the world."[^1] The disagreements that divide denominations live mostly at Level 3 (the Restoration), not Levels 1–2.

2. **Borrowing is rational stewardship, not compromise.** Because the foundations are shared, the best apologetic resources there are usable regardless of who wrote them. *Mere Christianity* is cited as "a classic example of the kind of level two conversation content we have available because of our Christian brothers of other denominations."[^3]

3. **The course is explicitly cross-confessional.** It is "a library of videos, podcasts, and other resources from both LDS and non-LDS thinkers that have shaped my understanding in each of these conversations."[^2]

4. **A diagnosis is attached.** The Restoration "has always had the biggest impact on people who had a foundation of level one and two in place" — hence historic European conversions among disaffected Protestants and Catholics, versus a "barren and gray" field in now-secular Europe where that foundation has eroded.[^4] Rebuilding Levels 1–2, partly with non-LDS tools, is thus framed as missiologically strategic. See [[The Four-Level Conversation Structure of Apologetics]].

5. **Steelman of a critic.** A skeptic might note real metaphysical divergence even at Level 2 (e.g., the nature of God), so "same page" overstates the agreement. Hansen's framing concedes this implicitly: Level 2 still includes contested questions about God's nature, and the deepest LDS distinctives are deferred to Level 3 — so the borrowed resources are foundational scaffolding, not a claim of full doctrinal consensus.

## Best Quotes
> "What's wonderful about exploring these conversations is that our Christian friends and neighbors are on the same page with us in establishing that God exists and that the Jesus Christ in the New Testament is the savior of the world. And frankly, they have amazing resources in these areas." [^1]

> "Someone like C.S. Lewis writing Mere Christianity is a classic example of the kind of level two conversation content we have available because of our Christian brothers of other denominations. And we do ourselves a great disservice if we ignore the great works done by other Christians out there." [^3]

> "It's a library of videos, podcasts, and other resources from both LDS and non-LDS thinkers that have shaped my understanding in each of these conversations." [^2]
