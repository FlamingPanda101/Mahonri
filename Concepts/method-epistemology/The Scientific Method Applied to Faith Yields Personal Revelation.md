# The Scientific Method Applied to Faith Yields Personal Revelation

## The Claim

[[Ether's Elephant]] argues that although science cannot *prove* God to everyone, an individual can run a private experiment that applies the *form* of the scientific method to faith: **observe** the lives of believers, **hypothesize**, and **experiment** by actually living the practice. The outcome is not public proof but personal evidence — what Latter-day Saints call personal revelation — which is "evidence for you and only you." Crucially the experiment requires *works*, not mere assent: "faith without works is dead."[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Observation: religious practice tracks measurable well-being.** Citing [[David DeSteno]], the video notes that community and social ties improve health and happiness, "but the effect size is larger for religious community" — and that praying regularly correlates with never feeling alone. These are observations one can make before believing.[^2] *Rating: moderate-to-strong; the social-capital and religiosity literature is real (cf. [[Robert Putnam]]), though correlation-vs-causation and the active-ingredient question remain open.*

2. **Hypothesis and experiment: act first, understand later.** The video retells Moses teaching the Israelites: "What? I don't quite understand this, but okay, I'm going to do it... sometimes it's in the doing of the practice that the understanding comes later." Compare [[John 7.17|John 7:17]] ("if any man will do his will, he shall know of the doctrine") and [[Matthew 7.7-8|Matthew 7:7–8]] ("seek and ye shall find").[^3] *Rating: strong as a description of the LDS experiential model; it mirrors [[Faith before Science and Then Science]] and [[Belief Must Precede Knowledge]].*

3. **Works are the experimental condition, not optional.** "Simple belief without practice is pointless... You have to be actively engaged in order to have a proper experiment." DeSteno: those who say they believe but do not practice show no health benefit. The "experiment" is invalid without the intervention being applied.[^4] *Rating: strong internal logic — an experiment with no treatment yields no result.*

4. **The result is private, not transferable proof.** "The outcome of your experiment cannot be used to convince others that God exists. Instead, it provides a catalyst for personal revelation." This is consistent with [[Do Not Argue Spiritual Experience to Be Valid]] and the [[Alma 32]] seed experiment.[^5] *Rating: strong; the video honestly disclaims public probative force.*

## Steelman of the Opposing View

The critic raises the **discernment-of-spirits** and **confirmation-bias** problems. (a) A felt "welling up of neural activity" (the video's phrase) is exactly what naturalism predicts and what other, mutually contradictory religions also report after their practices — so the experiment cannot distinguish the true God from a placebo or from a rival faith's god (the [[Praying to Know Truth and the Discernment-of-Spirits Objection|discernment objection]]). (b) An experiment whose protocol requires you to *already act in faith* and expect a positive feeling courts confirmation bias; the "treatment" and the "measurement" are entangled. (c) Calling a private, non-repeatable, observer-dependent experience "the scientific method" stretches the term past recognition — science's signature is *inter*-subjective replication, which the video concedes is absent. The strongest LDS reply accepts (c) terminologically — this is an *analogy* to the method, not literal science — and answers (a)/(b) by locating the warrant in the cumulative, lived fruits over time ([[Moroni 7]]'s "by their fruits") rather than a single emotional episode, while granting that the result remains personal evidence, not a proof binding on others.

## Best Quotes

> "Try first. Observe those who put their faith in God and those who put their faith in something else. What do you notice?"[^3]

> "Faith without works is dead... You have to be actively engaged in the practices."[^4]

> "It provides a catalyst for personal revelation, an experience that is personal evidence for you and only you."[^5]

## LDS Connection

This is a near-direct gloss of [[Moroni 10.4-5|Moroni 10:4–5]] and [[Alma 32]]: ask, do, and the knowledge follows by the Spirit. It sits downstream of [[Five Classes of Truth Science Cannot Establish]] (since science cannot settle the God question, the experiential route is the legitimate one) and alongside [[Personal Revelation Is the Most Legitimate Path to God]]. The video's framing — invite others to "find out for themselves" rather than demand assent because God's existence "cannot be proven wrong" — is presented as a distinctive Latter-day Saint posture.
