# Self-Fulfilling Prophecy Objection

## The Claim (Objection)
A prophecy is evidentially weak when its fulfillment lies within the ordinary power of the prophet or his followers to bring about. If Joseph Smith predicts an outcome that he or the Church can simply choose to enact, the prediction proves nothing supernatural — it is a goal, a plan, or a self-fulfilling statement, not a window into the future.[^1]

## The Rebuttal / Supporting Evidence

1. **Orson Pratt and Jerusalem.** For the [[Orson Pratt Mission to Jerusalem Prophecy]], the host notes Joseph prophesied in the early 1830s that the man would go to Jerusalem, and Pratt went around 1840 on a mission: "if Joseph Smith said in the early 1830s, you're going to go to Jerusalem, then Joseph could easily fulfill that own prophecy by sending him on a mission." (Evidence rating: strong for this case — the fulfillment was entirely within Joseph's administrative control.)[^1]
2. **The Rocky Mountains.** For the [[Rocky Mountains Migration Prophecy]], "the saints could always migrate to the Rocky Mountains to Salt Lake as a way to fulfill it. So it's not an impossible future event. They were able to fulfill that prophecy." A people who hear they are destined to go west, and then deliberately go west, have not validated a supernatural forecast. (Evidence rating: strong as an objection, though the prophecy's specificity and Nauvoo-era multiple attestation partly answer it.)[^2]
3. **Dan Jones and Wales.** Even the touching [[Joseph Smith Predicts His Own Martyrdom]] cluster includes the prediction that Dan Jones would "yet see Wales and fulfill the mission appointed" — which the host grants "could be an easily fulfilled one for him to then go on to Wales after." (Evidence rating: moderate.)[^3]
4. **Steelman of the believing view.** Self-fulfillment does not by itself make a prophecy false or even unimpressive. A leader who confidently directs events the world deems impossible (relocating an entire persecuted people 1,000+ miles to an unsettled basin) still displays remarkable foresight and resolve. The objection lowers the *evidential* value of a prophecy as proof of the supernatural; it does not show the statement was never made or that the outcome was trivial.

## Best Quotes
