#type/claim

# See Mormons as Victims Is Condescending Not Loving

## The Claim

The countercult ministry **Truth and Love Ministry** (founded by [[Mark J. Cares]], author of *Speaking the Truth in Love to Mormons*) runs a free course built on **"Five Pillars" for witnessing to Latter-day Saints**: (1) *see Mormons as victims, not enemies*; (2) treat them with genuine love and respect; (3) focus on "Mormon stress points"; (4) speak the "Mormon language"; (5) witness Christ rather than debate Mormonism.[^1] The [[Ward Radio]] hosts grant that pillars 2 and 5 are a real improvement over the "debate-me-bro" street-preacher mode, but argue **pillar one quietly poisons the whole project**: framing a believer as a *victim* is "the epitome of just placing yourself above someone to look down on them," a built-in condescension that no amount of surface niceness can cancel.[^2]

The deeper charge: the only Mormons this model can imagine being saved are "the ones that don't know what the church actually teaches" — i.e., it presupposes the believer is too stupid to read his own scriptures, which the hosts call "the most condescending" possible posture.[^3]

## Supporting Evidence (steelman)

### 1. "Victim" smuggles in superiority that defeats the witness
[[Michael Myrrono (OrthoCardia)|Michael Myrrono]], a former evangelical who once "circulated such literature," argues that humility is "inherently godly" — citing [[Augustine]]'s line in *City of God* that the three chief Christian virtues are "humility, humility, and humility."[^4] A method whose first pillar requires you to classify your interlocutor as a victim cannot produce that humility; it is "a social defense mechanism more than a proselytizing technique that's smiled upon by God."[^2] Myrrono presses the test case: it is not how Jesus treated "the woman at the well."[^2]

### 2. The "feigned interest" tell
Baylor Johnson notes that pillar one asks the witnesser to *manufacture* concern: "you have to feign interest in order to think somebody… is actually a victim. That's the number-one bully tactic."[^5] When the hosts role-play the pillars (Myrrono as missionary, Johnson as an evangelical target), the "victim" approach collapses immediately into telling the other person "you've been taken advantage of and you might not know it" — which lands as an insult, not love.[^6]

### 3. The asymmetry with LDS missionary training
The hosts contrast the Five Pillars with the Church's own manual, *Preach My Gospel*, ch. 10 ("Teach to Build Faith in Jesus Christ"): "Each [person] you teach is unique. Seek to understand his or her spiritual interests, needs, and concerns… Heavenly Father does [understand]."[^7] Where Cares says *treat them as victims* and *focus on their stress points*, the LDS manual says *adapt to their needs* and *let the Spirit guide* — never "focus on Protestant/Catholic stress points," which would be self-evidently "a whack way to evangelize."[^8] This connects to the broader LDS objection that its own missionaries are not trained to tear down other faiths (see [[Anti-Mormonism Is Attacking the Person Not the Idea]] and [[Missionaries Are Tour Guides Not Question Answerers]]).

### Steelman (Truth and Love Ministry's side)
The hosts concede the ministry is a genuine step up: it is "willing to engage with Mormons in a way that's not just condemning them to hell [[Mark Driscoll]]-style," and Cares explicitly centers the *gospel of Christ's saving work* rather than the usual catalog of "every racist quote I could find."[^9] Cares' own diagnosis — that most evangelical witnessing fails by collapsing into a debate over LDS *history* or the *nature of God* while the gospel "takes a back seat" — is one Myrrono calls "right," wishing more evangelicals adopted it.[^10] A defender could add that "victim" is meant pastorally (the member is seen as deceived *by leaders*, not stupid), and that warning outsiders away from error is a real duty. The hosts' narrower point stands: as an *opening posture toward a member*, "victim" is condescension that undoes the love the other four pillars are trying to build.

## Best Quotes
> "Pillar one: see Mormons as victims… that's when they're, like, condescending. The only Mormons that are saved are the ones that don't know what the church actually teaches. It's the most condescending — like they're all so stupid they can't even read their own book."[^3]

> "Seeing Mormons as victims is just such an arrogant take… It is still condescending — and not in the good condescension of God."[^2]

> "Imagine if it said focus on Protestant stress points… focus on the Marian dogma, focus on the papacy. It's like — what? That's a whack way to evangelize."[^8]
