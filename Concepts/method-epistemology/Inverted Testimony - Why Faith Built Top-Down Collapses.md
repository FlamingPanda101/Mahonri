# Inverted Testimony - Why Faith Built Top-Down Collapses

## The Point
[[Jacob Hansen]] diagnoses why so many "shelves" break: most Latter-day Saint testimonies are built **upside-down**. The believer accepts God and the New Testament Jesus *because of* the Joseph Smith story, so when Joseph is doubted, God and Christ fall with him.[^1] Hansen reports the opposite order in his own life — "I believed in Joseph Smith's story because of my belief in Jesus Christ, and I believed in Jesus Christ because of my belief in the existence of a higher power."[^2] When faith is built foundation-up, a problem with the Restoration is a "small crack in the paint," not a collapse — because the lower floors carry their own independent weight.[^3]

## Evidence
1. **The observed anomaly.** Hansen "noticed something strange": people troubled by polygamy or Book of Mormon anachronisms "were not just abandoning Joseph Smith — they were abandoning Jesus Christ and their belief in God."[^1] On his own structure this made no sense: "even if Joseph Smith was a fraud, that would not negate my belief in God or even Jesus Christ."[^1]

2. **Direction of dependence is everything.** The three propositions form a dependency chain — God → New Testament Jesus → Restoration — and *which way the support runs* determines stability. Many "believed in God and Jesus Christ because of the Joseph Smith story," inverting the proper order; Hansen ran it the other way.[^2] This is the directional complement to the structural claim in [[Anatomy of Belief - Three Independent Conversations]] and the fragility diagnosis in [[Inherited Belief Is Fragile - Examine Each Proposition Independently]].

3. **The calculus analogy.** "The Joseph Smith story is absolute nonsense if you no longer believe in the Jesus of the New Testament, and the Jesus of the New Testament is a total absurdity when you abandon the idea of a God who interacts with the world. It's kind of like how calculus would seem like nonsense until you understand the arithmetic and algebra that it's based on."[^4] Each higher level is intelligible only on the foundation of the lower one — building belief in Joseph first is like teaching calculus to someone who rejects arithmetic.

4. **Secularism is the acid on the foundation.** The inversion has become catastrophic because "for the past 60 years secularism has been pouring acid on the foundations of belief in Western society."[^5] The Church spent most of its history "having conversation three with a world that already had conversation one and two"; now the West "brings a secular lens to the first and second conversation," so testimonies resting on Joseph alone have nothing beneath them.[^6] Rebuilding therefore starts at [[The Level One Conversation - From Cosmic Order to a Higher Power]].

5. **Steelman of a critic.** A critic might say the order is irrelevant — if the Restoration is false, an honest person should follow the evidence wherever it leads, even to atheism. Hansen's reply is that the inverted believer never actually examined the lower levels at all; their atheism is not a reasoned conclusion from Joseph's failure but an unexamined dependency exposed. The fix is to "go back to the foundations and reexamine them for damage," not to assume the foundation was sound.[^5]

## Best Quotes
> "Many people believed in God and Jesus Christ because of the Joseph Smith story, but I believed in Joseph Smith's story because of my belief in Jesus Christ, and I believed in Jesus Christ because of my belief in the existence of a higher power. I began to notice that many people's testimonies were totally inverted." [^2]

> "The Joseph Smith story is absolute nonsense if you no longer believe in the Jesus of the New Testament... It's kind of like how calculus would seem like nonsense until you understand the arithmetic and algebra that it's based on." [^4]

> "Do you see how the issues like the Book of Mormon anachronisms, which are still bothersome, seem like small cracks in the paint of a solid building?" [^3]

## LDS Connection
The Restoration locates the foundation of testimony in Christ himself — "it is upon the rock of our Redeemer, who is Christ, the Son of God, that ye must build your foundation" (Helaman 5:12) — so a testimony resting first on Joseph rather than on Christ is built on the wrong stone. Hansen's bottom-up order matches Alma's experimental method, which begins with the most basic question (is there a God who answers?) and only then builds upward (see [[Alma 32.27-43]]). The pastoral payoff is reassurance to strugglers: discovering that a "shelf" item troubles you need not threaten faith in God or Christ, if that faith was ever grounded in its own right rather than borrowed from the top down.
