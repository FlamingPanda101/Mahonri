# Historicity

## Overview

**Historicity** is the property of being historically real — whether the events, persons, or texts in question actually existed or occurred in history, as opposed to being myth, allegory, or pious fiction. In this vault the term most often attaches to the **Book of Mormon**: is it an authentic ancient record of real peoples, or a 19th-century composition?

## The Claim

The vault's methodological stance is that historicity is established by **convergence, not proof**: no single artifact "proves" the Book of Mormon, and the honest standard is the accumulation of many independent lines of evidence pointing the same way ([[Convergence Not Proof Is the Standard for Book of Mormon Historicity]]). Correspondingly, single-discipline "disproofs" are rejected as category errors — e.g., [[Perego - DNA Cannot Settle Book of Mormon Historicity|DNA cannot settle Book of Mormon historicity]] because population genetics cannot detect a small founding lineage absorbed into a large existing population.
