# Interpersonal Knowledge - Ginosko Not Just Propositions

## The Point

[[Blake Ostler]] argues that what Mormonism offers is **not propositional knowledge *about* God but interpersonal relationship *with* God** — knowledge in the sense of the Greek *ginōskō* (acquaintance/relationship), not discursive knowledge of facts. Eternal life is to *know* God in this relational sense, and to treat one person's spiritual experience as *evidence* for another misunderstands the role such experiences play. [^1]

## Argument

1. **What Mormons actually offer.** Not "a superior theology of axioms," not the best biblical scholarship, not "overwhelming empirical evidence" — but "a way to enter into interpersonal relationship directly with God." Like the first disciples in John who simply said "come and see," the invitation is to see with "deeper and more spiritually insightful eyes," not to win an argument. [^2]
2. **Two kinds of knowing (Latin / Greek).** Ostler distinguishes *scire* (discursive/propositional knowledge) from *cognoscere* (interpersonal knowledge); in Greek the relevant verb is *ginōskō*, relational acquaintance. [[John 17.3|John 17:3]] — eternal life is "to know... God and Jesus Christ" — "doesn't say to know *all about* God." One gains this not by a theology degree or memorizing a catechism but by "coming to know God interpersonally." English faintly preserves this with "acquaintance." [^3]
3. **A relationship, not a verdict.** "What really matters isn't whether you agree with me... what really matters is whether you know God." Ostler offers his own conversion at 14 (reading the D&C, the burning, later a prompting that he felt saved a classmate's life) as a *first-person* relational datum — "the world's authority" only on his own experience. [^4]
4. **Why another's experience is not your evidence.** "It is a mistake to take spiritual experiences as evidence for anyone but the person who has the experience." To use them as evidence for others "misunderstands the role they play," which Ostler likens, strikingly, to "idolatry" or attempting adultery — substituting a secondhand report for the firsthand relationship one is meant to enter. [^5]

## Best Quotes
> "What we offer as Mormons is not simply a truth — we're offering a relationship... what really matters is whether you know God." [^4]

> "One does not have this type of knowledge by getting a degree in theology... one has this kind of knowledge by simply coming to know God interpersonally." [^3]

> "It is a mistake to take spiritual experiences as evidence for anyone but the person who has the experience." [^5]
