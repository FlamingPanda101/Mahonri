#type/claim

# Revelatory Authority Must Match Interpretive Authority

## The Claim
[[Jared Halverson]] argues that the level of authority required to *interpret* scripture must be matched to the level of authority required to *reveal* it — and that only prophets, who possess revelatory authority, possess the corresponding interpretive authority. His paradigm case is [[Daniel 2|Nebuchadnezzar's dream]]: the king's wise men say only God can reveal the forgotten dream, yet anyone can offer an interpretation; the "genius" of Nebuchadnezzar's demand (tell me the dream *and* its meaning) is that it forces interpretive authority up to the level of revelatory authority. "If you can prove you can do the impossible, then I'll trust that you're able to do the very possible." Halverson calls this "the best argument for the restoration of the gospel ever."[^1]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **The Nebuchadnezzar structure.** Anyone "can come up with" a dream interpretation and "you can't prove me wrong"; but revelation — telling the dream itself — "is something only God can do." Tying the two together means interpretive authority is only trustworthy when backed by demonstrated revelatory authority.[^1] *(Strong as an analogy; it reframes a familiar text into a clean epistemic principle.)*
2. **Doctrine grows out of revelation, not exegesis.** Eternal marriage "didn't grow out of" [[1 Corinthians 11.11|1 Corinthians 11:11]]; it "grew out of revelation to prophets" ([[D&C 132]]) who *then* noticed Paul hinting at it. Likewise baptism for the dead came from [[D&C 128|D&C 127–128]], not [[1 Corinthians 15.29|1 Corinthians 15:29]] ("there's not enough there"); and degrees of glory ([[D&C 76]]) came from the JST of [[John 5.39|John 5]], not from 1 Corinthians 15.[^2] *(Moderate-to-strong — an internally consistent account of how LDS doctrine actually arose.)*
3. **A prophet can say "this is what the scriptures mean."** Halverson points to D&C 74, where Joseph Smith explicates [[1 Corinthians 7.3-5|1 Corinthians 7:14]] and ends, "and this is the way that the scriptures mean." For a scholar to say "this is what so-and-so thought it meant" is one thing; for a prophet to "invoke that level of interpretive authority based on his revelatory authority" is, Halverson says, "Joseph Smith going to Nebuchadnezzar and saying this is what your dream means."[^3] *(Moderate — turns on accepting that revelatory authority is real and present.)*

## Steelman of the Opposing View
A critic can press several points. (a) The Nebuchadnezzar analogy proves too little: Daniel's *demonstration* of revelatory power (naming the dream) was publicly verifiable in a way a modern prophet's interpretive pronouncements are not — so the analogy borrows Daniel's checkable miracle to license unverifiable interpretation. (b) "Doctrine grows out of revelation" is unfalsifiable: any reading can be retrofitted onto a text after the fact, and the claim that Paul "must have known" is exactly the kind of harmonizing the argument elsewhere criticizes. (c) Protestants would answer that the Spirit illuminates *every* believer's reading (the priesthood of all believers), so locating interpretive authority in an office, not the text, simply relocates the problem rather than solving it — now you must adjudicate *which* claimant has revelatory authority, with no neutral test. Halverson's fair reply is narrower than the strong claim: he is not proving the restoration true but identifying *what kind of thing* would have to be true for biblical disputes to be resolvable at all — namely, a living authority, since the text plus interpretation has demonstrably not produced agreement.

## Best Quotes
> "Revelation is something only God can do; interpretation, oh, any old mortal can do that. So let's tie the two together... force the level of interpretive authority to match the level of revelatory authority. Because if you can prove you can do the impossible, then I'll trust that you're able to do the very possible."[^1]

> "It's not like baptisms for the dead grew out of 1 Corinthians 15:29 — there's not enough there. It grew out of section 127 and 128 and revelations that God gives to prophets... that then you happen to look and go, oh, I'm not the only prophet that's had hints of this."[^2]
