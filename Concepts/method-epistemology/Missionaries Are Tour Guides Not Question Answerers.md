# Missionaries Are Tour Guides Not Question Answerers

## The Claim
Reacting to a pastor who corners two missionaries with the metaphysics of the hypostatic union, the (returned-missionary) hosts argue the elders' job is **not to win philosophical debates on the doorstep**. The missionary's role is that of a **tour guide**, not the *question-answerer*: "You are the tour guides. You say, 'Here's the Book of Mormon. Here's the Joseph Smith revelations in the Doctrine and Covenants. Here's what we think… and if you have any questions, I can give you some resources.'" [^1] The competence being deployed is **connecting people with the divine** — teaching them to pray, to read scripture, to meet God in grief or crisis — not adjudicating Aristotle versus the New Testament. [^2] Admitting "I don't know the details, but here's what we *do* believe, and I can get back to you" is praised as the *correct* posture, not a failure; the elders are commended precisely for refusing to speculate on outlier theories. [^3]

## Supporting Evidence (steelman)
1. **Division of intellectual labor.** The hosts grant the pastor sounds learned and that LDS Christology *is* genuinely complex (premortal spirits in a "primitive state," not yet in the [[Blake Ostler|Ostlerian]] "relationship of indwelling unity" that constitutes the Godhead). The point is that **eighteen-year-olds are not catechists**; expecting them to extemporize on Chalcedon "presupposes a highly specific formulation of Christology" the Christian tradition itself took 450 years to fight over. *Rating: a fair allocation of burden* — the missionary mandate is invitation, not disputation. [^4]
2. **"Let the book do the legwork."** A recurring tactic: when stumped, open the scriptures rather than improvise. "Don't try to explain this stuff on your own. Let the Book of Mormon do the legwork." The hosts model it by answering the pastor's own question — *could anyone else have atoned?* — from [[Alma 34.32-33|Alma 34:10-11]] ("not a sacrifice of man… but an infinite and eternal sacrifice"). *Rating: methodologically consistent* with [[God Himself Not a Book Is the Ultimate Authority]] — the witness points past himself to the source. [^5]
3. **Connection over argument.** The deepest version of the claim: conversion runs through the Spirit and lived need, not syllogisms. "These elders are trained to help [people] pray… connect with the divine in a time of need," echoing [[Spirit Over Argument - The Limits of Apologetics]] and [[Persuasion Must Address the Elephant First]]. *Rating: a strong account of what missionary work is actually for*, though it concedes the apologetic field to better-prepared interlocutors. [^2]
4. **The honest limit.** Steelman of the critic: a tour guide who cannot answer hard questions may leave a sincere investigator's objections unmet, and "I'll get back to you" can read as evasion. The hosts' reply is that **humility is not defeat** — "Clearly I don't know. I'm being humble about it" — and that a genuine teacher "meets them where they're at" rather than performing erudition. *Rating: a defensible reframe* that relocates credibility from cleverness to candor. [^6]

## Best Quotes
> "Just know your role. You are not the question answerer, guys. You are the tour guides." [^1]

> "Elders are not there to teach complex philosophical principles. They're there to connect people with the divine." [^2]

> "You don't have to know the answer to every question on the spot, and you can admit that without seeming like you're too afraid to answer." [^3]
