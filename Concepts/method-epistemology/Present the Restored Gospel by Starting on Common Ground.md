# Present the Restored Gospel by Starting on Common Ground

## The Claim
[[Shiloh Lofgren]] models a method for presenting Latter-day Saint faith to evangelicals that lowers defenses and earns a hearing for the distinctives. Its moves: (1) **open on shared ground** in Christ and salvation by grace, citing the Book of Mormon's most Christ-centered verses; (2) **disarm with humor**, inviting the room to call out "heresy" together (1-2-3, "heresy!") before each controversial claim, so disagreement becomes collegial rather than combative; (3) **flag the epistemic status** of each claim — distinguishing "doctrine, authoritative sources" from "opinion or more speculative" content; and (4) **build from the audience's own King James Bible** toward LDS distinctives, showing the seeds of each doctrine in texts they already accept. The goal is persuasion through relationship and shared text, not confrontation.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Common ground first (rated: strong; sound rhetoric).** Lofgren opens: "Let's start out on some common ground ... we love Jesus ... it is because of Jesus that I'm forgiven of my sins," reading Mosiah 3:17 and 2 Nephi 10:24 ("only in and through the grace of God ye are saved").[^1] Establishing agreement before disagreement is classic irenic apologetics (cf. Paul at Athens, Acts 17). See [[Both Sides Overcorrected in the LDS-Evangelical Divide]].

2. **Playful "heresy" framing (rated: moderate; a real de-escalation device).** Before each distinctive (two separate divine persons in the First Vision; becoming like God; the apostasy), he cues the room: "one, two, three — heresy," turning anticipated objection into a shared joke.[^2] *Rating:* rhetorically effective at defusing the in-group/out-group tension; not an argument, a tone-setter.

3. **Doctrine vs. speculation labeling (rated: strong; intellectually honest).** "Whenever I'm trying to pull from doctrine, authoritative sources ... if we get into things that might be opinion or more speculative, I'll always try to distinguish."[^3] This protects both sides from treating one person's musing as the Church's position — a discipline the vault values (cf. [[Conflating Anecdote and Culture With Doctrine Misrepresents the Church]]).

4. **Use their Bible to reach the distinctives (rated: strong; the core technique).** For each LDS claim he produces a King James proof-text the audience already grants — the apostasy from [[Acts 20.29-30|Acts 20:29]], deification from [[Romans 8.14-17|Romans 8:17]], spirit-world preaching from [[1 Peter 3.18-20|1 Peter 3:19]], baptism for the dead from [[1 Corinthians 15.29|1 Cor 15:29]], degrees of glory from 1 Cor 15:41 — so the conclusion feels like a *reading* of their own scripture, then "parsed out a little bit" by the restoration.[^4] See [[Three Degrees of Glory in the Bible]].

5. **Testimony, not just argument (rated: moderate).** He closes with his own near-atheism reversed by the Book of Mormon, framing the book as a *type of the resurrection* — an appeal to lived experience that the [[Both Sides Overcorrected in the LDS-Evangelical Divide]] note also commends ("listen to the story of what the individual has experienced").[^5]

**Steelman of the opposing view.** A creedal critic will object that "common ground in Christ" can paper over the very disagreement at issue — if the LDS Jesus is a separate being from the Father and a spirit-brother of Lucifer, then the shared vocabulary masks a different referent, and the friendly frame risks "bait and switch" (warm agreement, then heterodox payload labeled merely "heresy" as a joke). The using-your-own-Bible move is also double-edged: each proof-text (1 Cor 15:29; 1 Pet 3:19; Rom 8:17) has standard non-LDS readings, so an evangelical may feel the verses are being *recruited* rather than exegeted. The honest defense is that Lofgren explicitly *names* the disagreements ("are Mormons Christian? If you define it by the Trinity, no — that's okay"), flags speculation, and aims at mutual understanding, not a concealed agenda; the method's integrity depends on that candor being maintained when the harder cross-examination comes (see the formal debate, [[Gray Area Faith - Sola Scriptura Debate LDS vs Calvinist (Apologia Utah)]]).

## Best Quotes
> "Let's start out on some common ground. The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints — we love Jesus ... it is because of Jesus that I'm forgiven of my sins."[^1]

> "Whenever I'm pulling from doctrine, authoritative sources ... if we get into things that might be opinion or more speculative, I'll always try to distinguish."[^3]

> "If you believe Jesus loves all of his children ... it's not that crazy to think that maybe he was calling prophets to them too."[^4]
