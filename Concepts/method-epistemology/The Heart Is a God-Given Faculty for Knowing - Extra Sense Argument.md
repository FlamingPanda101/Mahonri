# The Heart Is a God-Given Faculty for Knowing - Extra Sense Argument

## The Point

[[Blake Ostler]]'s central thesis: the **heart** — taken in the full Hebrew sense of *leb* (uniting mind and emotion, cognitive and affective) — is a God-given **faculty or "extra sense"** for knowing God, divinely *implanted* in us. The "burning in the bosom" is the operation of this faculty, not a muscle and not a mere feeling. [^1]

## Argument

1. **Heart = *leb*, not feeling vs muscle.** Ostler is "speaking of the heart not as a muscle... nor as an emotional experience," but in the Hebrew sense of *leb*, "the very center and seat of spiritual life," uniting "both cognitive and affective functions" — what modern Western culture wrongly bifurcates into "mind and emotions." [^1]
2. **Scripturally the heart is the organ of knowing.** For the Hebrews "the heart is the place where thinking occurs" and "where human decisions are made"; Ostler reads a New Testament Greek dictionary defining *kardia*/*leb* as "the center and seat of spiritual life... the fountain and seat of thoughts, passions, desires... the understanding, the faculty and seat of the intelligence." [^2]
3. **It was divinely implanted.** [[Deuteronomy 30.6,11-14|Deuteronomy 30]]: "the LORD thy God will circumcise thine heart" — circumcision being the image of a *soft, exposed, open* heart — and "the word is very nigh thee... in thy heart." [[Paul the Apostle|Paul]] reworks this in [[Romans 10.6-9|Romans 10]] so the implanted *dabar* is Christ himself: "with the heart man believeth unto righteousness." So "the very basis of this religious knowledge is something that's been implanted into us by God." [^3]
4. **It functions like an extra sense (the *liahona* metaphor).** Scripture presents the experience sensorily — it "tastes good," it involves feeling — i.e., as "an extra sense given to us by God." Ostler compares it to a personal *liahona* "planted in our very hearts": it works when we are spiritually sensitive (soft-hearted) and simply "isn't there" when we are hardened. [^4]
5. **Why a heart-faculty is the *ideal* design.** It "does not discriminate based on intellect or literary acumen," is "sensitive to what really matters most," and is "sensitive to individual accountability," since a person must *choose* openness of heart as a precondition. For a God who wants children to come to him *by choice* rather than be overwhelmed by his glory, "this may be the perfect way." [^5]

## Best Quotes
> "I speak of the human heart in the fullest Hebrew sense of *leb*... that includes both cognitive and affective functions; in fact, it is the very center and seat of spiritual life." [^1]

> "The very basis of this religious knowledge is something that's been implanted into us by God." [^3]

> "We have implanted within us our own personal liahona within our hearts. It works when we're spiritually sensitive... and when we're not spiritually sensitive it simply doesn't work at all." [^4]
