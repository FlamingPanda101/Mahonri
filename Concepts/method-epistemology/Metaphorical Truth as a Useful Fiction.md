# Metaphorical Truth as a Useful Fiction

## The Point
A claim can be **literally false** yet such that acting as if it were true leaves you better off than acting on the literal facts. [[Bret Weinstein]] proposes this category — *metaphorical truth* — as the bridge between [[Jordan Peterson]]'s defense of religious narrative and [[Sam Harris]]'s rationalism: religions would be "encapsulations of stories and prescriptions" that confer advantages "irrespective of whether they literally describe the universe."[^1]

## Argument

1. **Definition.** "There are concepts which are literally false... but that if you behave as if they were true you come out ahead of where you were if you behave according to the fact that they are false."[^1] *Steelman: this dissolves the false binary between "literally true" and "worthless."*

2. **The gun example.** Treat *every gun as if it is loaded*. This is strictly false at the level of explicit knowledge yet "absolutely necessary," and people who violate it shoot themselves and others. The useful fiction is "more useful than the truth."[^2]

3. **Harris's qualification — it is parasitic on literal truth.** Harris grants the gun case but insists the only way the phrase "metaphorical truth" is even *comprehensible* is "in the context of distinguishing it from literal truth." And when "a casino opens across the street" offering a million-dollar bet on whether *this* gun is loaded, he'd bet every time it isn't — because he knows the literal truth. *Steelman: useful fictions are tools we deploy knowingly, not beliefs we hold.*[^3]

4. **Peterson's extension — living "as if."** When Harris alleged Peterson "believed in God," Peterson corrected: he *lives as if* God exists. The gun reflex is, for Peterson, the same reflex any metaphorical truth invites — it "can always be unpacked," but operating on it has advantages.[^4]

5. **Where it breaks.** Harris concedes the cases where the fiction beats the truth are "few and far between" (guns are one), low-cost, and "not dividing societies and causing people to go to war." The dispute is therefore over *which* religious fictions remain net-positive once the casino opens. See [[Religion Gives Bad Reasons to Be Good]].[^5]

## Best Quotes
> "There are concepts which are literally false that we can falsify in a scientific rational sense but that if you behave as if they were true you come out ahead." [^1]

> "It really is crazy at the level of our explicit knowledge of a situation and yet absolutely necessary to do." [^2]

> "The only way I can understand its utility... is in the context of distinguishing it from literal truth." [^3]
