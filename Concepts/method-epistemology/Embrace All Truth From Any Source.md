# Embrace All Truth From Any Source

## The Point
A foundational LDS epistemic principle, montaged here as authority-witness: the Church "embraces all truth that God conveys to his children," **regardless of channel** — whether discovered "in a scientific laboratory" or "received by direct revelation." Truth is to be received "let it come from whence it may," without being "circumscribed or prohibited by the creeds or superstitious notions of men." This grounds the video's ecumenical, source-agnostic method of citing Catholic (Barron) and secular-adjacent (Peterson) authorities alongside LDS prophets.

## Argument
1. "God is the source of all truth," and "the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints embraces all truth that God conveys to his children, whether learned in a scientific laboratory or received by direct revelation from him." [^1]
2. [[Joseph Smith]]: "The first and fundamental principle of our holy religion is that we believe that we have a right to embrace all and every item of truth, without limitation or without being circumscribed or prohibited by the creeds or superstitious notions of men." [^2]
3. The reach is total: "if Mormonism is not my life I do not know that I have any… for it embraces everything that comes within the range of the understanding of man" (a [[BH Roberts]] sentiment in the montage). [^3]
4. Closing principle: "One of the grand fundamental principles of Mormonism is to receive truth, let it come from whence it may." [^4]
5. As a seeker disposition: accepting "the responsibility to seek after truth with an open mind and a humble heart" makes one "more tolerant of others, more open to listen, more prepared to understand." [^5]
6. **Steelman of the objector.** A critic asks how a source-agnostic "embrace all truth" coheres with a church that also claims exclusive priesthood authority and binding revelation — does it not risk either incoherence (anything can be "truth") or selective application (embrace outside truth only when it confirms doctrine)? The LDS reply distinguishes *truth* (universally welcomed wherever found) from *authority/ordinances* (institution-specific): borrowing a Catholic's epistemology or a psychologist's analysis is consistent with holding that saving authority is restored, since truth and authority are different categories.
