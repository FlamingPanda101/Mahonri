# Fallible But Reliable — Why Authority Still Counts

## The Point
[[Jacob Hansen]]'s guard against over-correcting the [[The Heresy of Authority Alone|authority-alone]] error into a *no-authority* error. **Every** authority — secular or religious — is fallible, but fallibility does not entail unreliability. Authority is established by **trust**, trust is built on **outcomes**, and Christ's "by their fruits" test ([[Matthew 7.16-20]]) is exactly an outcomes-based warrant for trusting a prophet. This is the **reliability-not-infallibility** standard applied to authority generally.[^1][^2]

## Argument
1. **Don't swing to the opposite ditch.** "Please be careful not to go too far in the other direction." When the twelve greatest brain surgeons devise a cancer procedure, "it would be stupid for me to Simply say that they are not trustworthy because they are fallible."[^1]
2. **Universal fallibility ≠ unreliability.** "Every authority figure is fallible, but that does not mean that they are not a trustworthy and reliable source of Truth." Repeated for emphasis: "every authority figure, be it secular or religious, is fallible, but that does not mean [they] are not worthy of our trust or unreliable."[^1] See [[Reliability Not Infallibility Standard]], [[Prophets Are Fallible Not Divine]].
3. **Trust is grounded in outcomes.** "Authority is ultimately established by trust, and trust in a person or an institution is built on outcomes." Christ taught this — "we would know a Prophet by the fruits that came from them" ([[Matthew 7.16-20]]).[^2]
4. **Applied to the prophets.** Hansen's rhetorical test: have the prophets "been a reliable guide" and "a reliable source for helping us to find satisfaction for our thirsty Souls"? He answers yes and places "a great deal of trust... in the institution of priesthood Authority in matters relating to the welfare of my soul, even if I believe that they're fallible."[^2]
5. **Authority is domain-weighted, not nil.** Within the [[The Collective Witness Model of Knowing|collective witness model]], authority can rightly be "given more weight than mere intuition" in institutional religious practice — it is one strong witness, not a discarded one.

### Steelman (the skeptic's reply)
"Reliable" needs an independent measuring stick: if the fruits/outcomes test is itself read through the authority being tested, the warrant risks circularity. Hansen's answer is the broader triangulation — outcomes, reason, and evidence are independent witnesses that can corroborate *or* undercut a claimed authority.

## Best Quotes
> "Every authority figure is fallible, but that does not mean that they are not a trustworthy and reliable source of Truth." [^1]

> "Authority is ultimately established by trust, and Trust in a person or an institution is built on outcomes. Christ himself taught this when he said that we would know a Prophet by the fruits that came from them." [^2]

> "I put a great deal of trust into the institution of priesthood Authority in matters relating to the welfare of my soul, even if I believe that they're fallible." [^2]
