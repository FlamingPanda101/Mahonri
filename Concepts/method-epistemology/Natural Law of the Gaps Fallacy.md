# Natural Law of the Gaps Fallacy

## The Point
Turek turns the familiar "God of the gaps" accusation back on the atheist.[^1] The design inference is not an argument from ignorance — it rests not on what we *don't* know but on what we *do* know: our uniform, repeated experience that only intelligence produces specified information.[^2] By contrast, the atheist who insists "science will eventually find a natural cause for the genetic code" is the one reasoning from a gap — exercising *faith* that an unknown future discovery will vindicate materialism. Turek calls this the "natural law of the gaps" fallacy.[^3]

## Evidence
1. **Design is positive evidence, not a knowledge-gap.** "We don't just lack a materialistic explanation for the origin of genetic information. We have positive, empirically verifiable evidence for an intelligent cause." Archaeologists and detectives infer intelligence not from a gap in their knowledge of natural forces but from positive knowledge that "inscriptions require inscribers and crimes require criminals."[^2]
2. **The atheist's faith move.** When Lennox noted that natural forces cannot explain life's origin, Dawkins replied, "you're asserting that there is no way. We don't yet know what it is... There are still gaps." Michael Shermer asked for "fifty more years" before drawing a conclusion. Turek: "How does Dawkins know science will close those gaps? He doesn't. He has faith."[^3]
3. **The entropy analogy.** We could keep looking for a natural cause for genetic information the way we could keep looking for a perpetual-motion machine — but no one does the latter, because thermodynamics is "virtually a closed science." The second law (entropy) tells us "nature will take a building and turn it into a pile of bricks, but nature will never take a pile of bricks and turn it into a building." Why expect blind forces to generate thousands of pages of specified information?[^4]
4. **Selective skepticism exposed.** Dawkins and Shermer never claim science will one day find a *natural* cause for the Rosetta Stone or for Darwin's *Origin of Species* — both of which are "filled with the same kind of functional information" as DNA — because their uniform experience tells them inscriptions and books come only from minds.[^5]
5. **Science sometimes *opens* gaps.** Turek notes the gap between life and non-life, and between the universe and a natural cause, has *widened* with discovery: Darwin's contemporaries saw the cell as a "simple blob of protoplasm," but modern biology reveals "a world of astonishing complexity." Antony Flew followed this evidence from atheism to theism precisely because of DNA.[^6]

## Best Quotes
> "He is committing the 'natural law of the gaps' fallacy. Dawkins has faith that one day we will find a natural cause for digital information."[^3]

> "It's not a 'gap' in their knowledge about natural forces that led them to conclude intelligence was required. It's the positive knowledge that inscriptions require inscribers and crimes require criminals!"[^2]

## LDS Connection
The methodological point — that inferring an intelligent cause from specified effects is legitimate, not a mere argument from ignorance — supports any theistic natural theology, the Latter-day Saint one included; the same "inference to the best explanation" undergirds the teleological reasoning of [[Fine-Tuning and the Teleological Argument from Alma 30]]. An honest caution: Turek frames the inferred cause in classical, transcendent terms, whereas LDS thought conceives God as organizing eternal matter within time rather than creating from nothing (see [[A Transcendent Designer - Why Panspermia Fails]]). The shared conviction is that mind, not a yet-undiscovered blind mechanism, best explains the order and information of the cosmos.
