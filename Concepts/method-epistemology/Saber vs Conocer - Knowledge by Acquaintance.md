# Saber vs Conocer - Knowledge by Acquaintance

## The Point

The host distinguishes two kinds of knowing using Spanish (and Greek) vocabulary: **saber** = to know something rationally/cognitively (know-*about*), and **conocer** = to be familiar with something through direct experience (know-*by-acquaintance*). He argues that scripture's knowledge of God is *conocer*, not *saber* — relational, experiential knowing.[^1][^2]

## Argument

1. **Two verbs, two epistemologies.** Spanish *saber* is propositional ("know that"); *conocer* is acquaintance ("be familiar with"). "I may know *about* love by listening to a lecture (saber), but I *conocer* love when my child gives me a hug."[^1] Greek has a parallel division.[^2] *(Steelman: this maps onto a real philosophical distinction — knowledge-by-description vs. knowledge-by-acquaintance (Russell) — so the linguistic point is not merely rhetorical.)*
2. **Scripture's "know God" is conocer.** When [[John 17.3|Jesus says eternal life is to know God]], the host argues the word used is the equivalent of *conocer* — relational acquaintance, not cognitive information.[^2]
3. **Moroni's promise is conocer too.** When [[Moroni 10.4-5|Moroni]] says we "shall know the truth of the Book of Mormon," that knowing is again *conocer* — experiential confirmation by the Spirit, not the conclusion of an argument.[^2]
4. **Payoff.** Therefore religious knowledge is fundamentally a matter of direct contact, and the demand to reduce it to propositional/argued knowledge (*saber*) misclassifies it.[^3] *(Cross-examination: acquaintance-knowledge of an experience is hard to deny, but the disputed claim is acquaintance with *God* specifically — the experience could be genuine while its object/interpretation is mistaken, which the host concedes elsewhere. So "I conocer X" secures the having of an experience, not the identity of what was encountered.)*

## Best Quotes

> "I may know about love by listening to a lecture about it — you know, saber — but I conocer love when my child gives me a hug and a kiss when I get home from work."[^1]

> "When Jesus says life eternal is to know God, which word does he use? He uses the equivalent of conocer. And in Moroni's promise ... again, which word is used? Conocer."[^2]
