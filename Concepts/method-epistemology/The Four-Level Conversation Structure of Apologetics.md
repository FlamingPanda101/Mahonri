# The Four-Level Conversation Structure of Apologetics

## The Point
[[Jacob Hansen]]'s course is organized around **four ascending levels of conversation**, each defined by the questions it asks and the audience it equips you to engage. Level 1 is the conversation about reality itself (and whether God exists); Level 2 is about the nature of God and Jesus; Level 3 is about the unique doctrines of the Restoration; and Level 4 is the internal conversation Latter-day Saints have about their own doctrines.[^1] Mastering each level lets one "engage in great conversations with people at any level they are at."[^2]

## Explanation
1. **Level 1 — reality and the existence of God.** A conversation "going on for thousands of years about the very fundamentals of reality itself": "What am I? What is the meaning of my life? How can I be sure that I know what I know? Are there good reasons to believe in some kind of higher power or intelligence?"[^3] Competence here equips conversations with **agnostics and atheists**.[^2]

2. **Level 2 — the nature of God and the truth of Jesus.** Once one becomes a theist, new questions open: "What is the nature of this God? Has this God ever interacted with the world? Was Jesus telling the truth about God?"[^4] Competence here equips conversations with people who are **"spiritual but not Christian."**[^2]

3. **Level 3 — the Restoration.** Once someone embraces the New Testament Jesus, "it's at this point where the restored gospel's message has such a powerful resonance."[^5] Competence here equips conversations with **other Christians** about the unique doctrines and theologies of the Restoration.[^2]

4. **Level 4 — internal LDS topics.** An added level focused on "things relevant to Latter-day Saints specifically and the conversations that we have about our own doctrines."[^6]

5. **The levels are foundational, not optional.** Each rests on the one below; Levels 1–2 are "the foundation upon which we build the restoration," and Hansen warns the secular West — Latter-day Saints included — has "forgotten" them.[^7] This mirrors the three-independent-conversations anatomy (see [[Anatomy of Belief - Three Independent Conversations]]) and explains why he counsels starting at Level 1 (see [[Inherited Belief Is Fragile - Examine Each Proposition Independently]]).

6. **The course is for everyone.** "An atheist could enjoy the level one content. Any Christian could really enjoy level two."[^8] Hansen's own "10-year faith remodeling journey" was "essentially me starting from level one and working my way up."[^9]

## Best Quotes
> "A conversation that has been going on for thousands of years about the very fundamentals of reality itself... Questions like, 'What am I? What is the meaning of my life? How can I be sure that I know what I know? Are there good reasons to believe in some kind of higher power or intelligence?'" [^3]

> "Once a person embraces the idea that God exists and becomes a theist, it leads to a new sort of conversation, what I call the level two conversation... 'What is the nature of this God? Has this God ever interacted with the world? Was Jesus telling the truth about God?'" [^4]

> "If you become well-versed in the level one conversation, you will have the ability to engage in very interesting conversations with agnostics and atheists. If you become well-versed in level two, you will be knowledgeable about topics relevant to your friends who are spiritual but not Christian. And at level three, you will be more equipped for conversations with other Christians about the unique doctrines and theologies of the Restoration." [^2]

> "My 10-year faith remodeling journey was essentially me starting from level one and working my way up." [^9]
