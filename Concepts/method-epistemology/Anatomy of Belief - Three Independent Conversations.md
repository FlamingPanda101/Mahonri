# Anatomy of Belief - Three Independent Conversations

## The Point
[[Jacob Hansen]] frames a mature testimony as the **"anatomy of belief"**: faith is not one monolithic claim but **three independent conversations**, each resolved by inference to the best explanation, that build *increasing clarity* about life's big questions.[^1] His own testimony is staged as a sequence of best-explanation judgments — reality is best explained by God; God is best explained by the New Testament Jesus; Jesus is best explained by the Restoration.[^2] Because each link is examined on its own evidential footing rather than inherited as a package, the structure resists collapse.

## Explanation
1. **The method is abductive, not deductive.** Each stage is phrased identically: "I once had a conversation about X, and among the available explanations, the best was Y."[^2] The recurring "among the available explanations, the best was" is the signature of inference to the best explanation — a cumulative case rather than a proof chain.

2. **Each conversation is independent.** Reality → God is settled *before* and *apart from* God → Jesus, which is settled before Jesus → the Restoration. The independence is the whole point: a problem at one level need not topple the others, because no level's support is borrowed from the level above it. See [[Inherited Belief Is Fragile - Examine Each Proposition Independently]].

3. **It maps onto the course's level structure.** The three conversations correspond to the course's Levels 1–3 (with an added Level 4 for internal LDS topics). See [[The Four-Level Conversation Structure of Apologetics]].

4. **Steelman of a critic.** One could object that "best explanation" judgments are subjective and that staging belief this way is post-hoc rationalization of a conclusion already held. Hansen's own narrative partly anticipates this: he reports genuine uncertainty ("when I was not totally sure of that answer, it worried me") and an investigation that "changed everything,"[^3] presenting the structure as discovered under pressure rather than assumed.

## Best Quotes
> "More than anything, the thing that has helped me in my own faith journey was understanding the anatomy of my beliefs... my beliefs are built on three independent conversations that bring increasing clarity to the big questions about life." [^1]

> "I once had a conversation about reality, and among the available explanations, the best was that God exists. I then had a conversation about God, and among the available explanations, the best was that Jesus was who the New Testament claims he was. Then I had a conversation about Jesus, and among the available explanations, the best was that he had restored his gospel in the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints." [^2]
