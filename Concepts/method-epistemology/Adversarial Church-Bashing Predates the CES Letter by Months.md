# Adversarial Church-Bashing Predates the CES Letter by Months

#type/claim

## The Claim

[[Michael Peterson]]'s central documentary argument is a *timeline*: that [[Jeremy Runnells]] was publicly and adversarially attacking the Church for roughly six to nine months before he wrote the [[CES Letter]] (~March–April 2013), under the Reddit pseudonym "kabot" (a play on *Kolob*) with the flair "I'm on a tapir" (mocking Book of Mormon horse apologetics). If true, this undercuts the marketed claim that the letter arose from a sincere member's unanswered questions — the writing looks less like the *start* of an exploration and more like "the capstone of his radicalization."[^1]

The force of the claim rests on dated, publicly archived primary sources (ExMormon Reddit, the now-defunct Mormon Curtain), which the panel stresses any viewer can verify independently.[^2]

## Supporting Evidence (steelman)

- **The dated post sequence (~11 posts, mostly in a 4.5-month window).** Peterson walks a timeline: July 2012 "coming out" on ExMormon Reddit as "kabot"; **Oct 2, 2012** first attack on [[Brigham Young]] and modern prophets; **Oct 10, 2012** "Open Letter to Quentin L. Cook" on Mormon Curtain whose topic list closely prefigures the CES Letter's sections; **Oct 11** attack on the Book of Mormon translation; **Oct 25** "100% LDS sources that Joseph Smith was a fraud"; **Nov 11** coaching a young man to lead his father out of the Church; **Nov 12** "how do I save my kids from the church"; **Nov 25** linking a [[Grant Palmer]] takedown video; **Jan 21, 2013** daring doubters to "leave the faith"; **Feb 2013** smears of [[Gordon B. Hinckley]] and soliciting a rebuttal to Kerry Muhlestein's Book of Abraham defense.[^3]
- **The "Open Letter to Quentin L. Cook" as a template.** Peterson presents a side-by-side showing the topics in the October 2012 mocking letter reappearing in the CES Letter six months later — and a further parallel to the introduction of Palmer's *Insider's View of Mormon Origins*.[^4]
- **Already using insider-critic vocabulary.** Peterson notes Runnells was deploying derogatory acronyms (TSCC, TBM) during the period he claims to have been in a sincere faith crisis — evidence, Peterson argues, of prior immersion in anti-LDS online culture rather than naive seeking.[^5]
- **One-on-one "ministering" against the faith.** Beyond declarations, Runnells coached others to pull family members out (pointing them to MormonThink), which Peterson reads as a marker of the *level* of his disaffection well before the letter.[^6]

## Best Quotes

> "He dives into the most vicious anti-Mormon forum he can find and he mocks the church openly... that's nine months prior to writing. And then six months prior, in October, he comes out under kabot attacking Brigham Young." [^1]

> "Now look at the right of this slide at what actually showed up in the CES letter six months later." (on the Quentin L. Cook open-letter topic list) [^4]

## A Note on the Limit of This Argument

Even granting every dated post, the *contents* of the CES Letter must still be answered on their own merits; a true argument is not refuted by an unflattering account of its author's psychology (see [[The Genetic Fallacy Against Religious Origins]]). The panel itself concedes this and reframes the relevance: because the letter's thesis is that the *Church* is dishonest, the honesty of the origin claim the author markets is itself a contested factual claim worth testing — see [[Exposing a Public Deception Is Not an Ad Hominem]]. The steelman is therefore narrow: the timeline impeaches the *marketed narrative*, not necessarily each individual historical objection.
