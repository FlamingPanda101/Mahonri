# Presentism in Historical Judgment

## The Claim
Critics who label [[Joseph Smith]] a "pedophile" for his sealing to [[Helen Mar Kimball]] (age 14) commit the fallacy of **presentism** — judging historical figures by modern moral standards without accounting for the radically different social, economic, and cultural circumstances of their era. People should be judged "according to the light and knowledge they have or had available to them as well as according to the practical circumstances they find themselves in."[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Definition and Mechanism:** Presentism is "where we look at the past not through the lens and circumstances of our ancestors but through our modern lens."[^2] The video argues that "we have to remember how much of the culture and circumstances we grow up with frames the way we see the world."[^3]

2. **Edgar Allan Poe as Test Case:** [[Edgar Allan Poe]], widely celebrated as one of America's greatest poets, married his 13-year-old cousin in 1836 when he was 27 years old. This was not a major public scandal at the time, demonstrating that such marriages — while young even by 1836 standards — were not categorized as predatory or pathological in that culture.[^4] If modern presentist standards are applied consistently, Poe would also have to be labeled a pedophile — which virtually no one does.

3. **The "Teenager" Category Did Not Exist:** The very category of "teenager" as a distinct life stage with its own culture is largely a product of the second half of the 20th century, when high school and high school culture became universal in America. In the 1800s, adulthood was defined not by age number but by whether one had gone through puberty.[^5]

4. **Modern framing distorts historical categories:** The video does not endorse marriages between 27-year-olds and 13-year-olds — "if all of this seems a little icky, I agree. The past is a weird place"[^6] — but insists that understanding context is essential before applying moral labels like "pedophile," which carries clinical, legal, and moral meanings specific to modern society.

## Counter-Arguments

- **Moral realism objection:** Some acts are wrong regardless of cultural context — if marrying a 14-year-old is inherently exploitative, then cultural normalization does not excuse it. The presentism charge must answer whether there are any trans-historical moral truths.
- **Asymmetry objection:** Even by 19th-century standards, a 14-year-old bride was on the young end. The Poe comparison may not be fully parallel if the circumstances of the sealing (dynastic vs. romantic, polygamous vs. monogamous) differ in morally relevant ways.
- **Power differential:** [[Joseph Smith]] was not just an older man but a prophet and religious leader with spiritual authority over the Kimball family — a power asymmetry beyond what Poe had. This may distinguish the cases even within the historical context.

## Best Quote

> "There is something called presentism which is where we look at the past not through the lens and circumstances of our ancestors but through our modern lens. Now again I'm not here to defend every marriage in history. But we have to remember how much of the culture and circumstances we grow up with frames the way we see the world. We should judge all people according to the light and knowledge they have or had available to them as well as according to the practical circumstances they find themselves in."[^1]
