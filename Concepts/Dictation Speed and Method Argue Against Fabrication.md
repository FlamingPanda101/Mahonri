# Dictation Speed and Method Argue Against Fabrication

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
"What if Joseph was just a gifted writer who was able to pull it off anyway?" Arnold answers by examining the raw production numbers and the documented method of dictation [^13][^14].

## The Rebuttal
1. **The scale and pace.** The Book of Mormon is 584 pages, "over 269,000 words," dictated aloud to a scribe in "approximately 65 working days, with no notes, no manuscript, and no revisions" — roughly 3,000–4,000 words per day. *Rating: the broad figures (length, ~3 months, dictated, minimal revision) are well documented in LDS and witness sources; "no revisions" is a simplification given the printer's manuscript edits.* [^13][^14]
2. **The method described by witnesses.** Witnesses said Joseph "walking away for meals and then returning and picking up mid-sentence exactly where he left off," maintaining "internal consistency across hundreds of named characters, locations, timelines, and theological arguments spanning 1,000 years of narrative" [^14].
3. **Comparison baseline.** "Professional authors with outlines and months of planning average around 1,000 words a day, and they get to edit for continuity." The contrast is meant to make unaided fabrication at 3–4× that rate, without notes or revision, implausible [^14]. *Rating: rhetorically effective; the word-per-day average for novelists is loose and varies widely.*
4. **Steelman of the skeptic.** (a) Oral composition of long narrative is attested in other traditions (epic bards, improvisers), so raw speed alone is not decisive. (b) Joseph may have used prior reflection, family discussion, or a developing mental framework not captured by "no notes." (c) Witness accounts were recorded decades later and are partly faith-promoting; "picking up exactly where he left off" is a recollection, not a stopwatch record. (d) Internal inconsistencies and the printer's-manuscript corrections complicate the "no revisions" claim. Arnold leans on the witness testimony at face value [^14].

## Best Quotes
> "Joseph dictated the entire thing out loud to a scribe in approximately 65 working days, with no notes, no manuscript, and no revisions." [^14]

> "The witnesses described him as walking away for meals and then returning and picking up mid-sentence exactly where he left off." [^14]

> "Professional authors with outlines and months of planning average around 1,000 words a day, and they get to edit for continuity." [^14]
