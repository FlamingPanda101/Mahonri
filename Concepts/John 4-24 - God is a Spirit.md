# John 4:24 — "God Is a Spirit"

## The Charge
> "Mormons believe God has a physical body of flesh and bone. But John 4:24 says
> 'God is a Spirit.' Mormonism contradicts the Bible."

## The Verse (KJV)
> "God is a Spirit: and they that worship him must worship him in spirit and in
> truth." (John 4:24)

## Rebuttal Points

### 1. Greek Analysis: Qualitative, Not Possessive
- Greek: *pneuma ho theos* (πνεῦμα ὁ θεός) — literally "spirit [is] God."
- The construction is **qualitative**, not a statement of exclusive composition.
- Braden: "It does not have any possessive quality. It's qualitative. God is spirit
  in the same way that God is love or God is truth."
- Compare: "God is love" (1 John 4:8) — does this mean God is ONLY love and has no
  other attributes? No. Similarly, "God is spirit" means God is spiritual in nature,
  not that He is exclusively spirit and nothing else.

### 2. Context: The Samaritan Woman at the Well
- John 4: The Samaritan woman asks about WHERE to worship — the Samaritan mountain
  vs. the Jewish temple in Jerusalem.
- Jesus's answer is about the MODE and UNIVERSALITY of worship: you don't need a
  specific geographic location. Worship happens in spirit, anywhere.
- Dalton: "The time is coming where you don't have to go to that spot on that hill
  to worship God. You can worship Him with your spirit anywhere."

### 3. Alma's People (Book of Mormon Parallel)
- When Alma's people were forbidden from praying aloud, they "did pour out their
  hearts" to God silently (Mosiah 24:12).
- This is exactly what Jesus describes — worshipping in spirit when external
  worship is restricted.

### 4. LDS Belief: God Has BOTH Spirit AND Body
- LDS don't claim God is ONLY a body. "We do believe God has a spirit. We just
  believe He also has a body" (Britney).
- Humans are spirits housed in bodies — the spirit is what communicates with God.
  The same is true of God the Father in LDS theology.
- Luke 24:39 — the resurrected Christ: "a spirit hath not flesh and bones, as ye
  see me have" — Jesus in His resurrected state explicitly has a body AND is fully
  divine.

### 5. The Verse's Actual Point
The emphasis is on the second clause: "they that worship him MUST worship him in
spirit and in truth." It's a commandment about **how** to worship, not a
metaphysical description of God's composition.

## Bottom Line
"God is spirit" is qualitative (like "God is love"), not a denial of embodiment.
The context is worship accessibility for a marginalized Samaritan woman, not a
creedal statement on divine corporeality.
