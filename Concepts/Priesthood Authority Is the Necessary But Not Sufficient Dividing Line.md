# Priesthood Authority Is the Necessary But Not Sufficient Dividing Line

## The Point
[[Allie Beth Stuckey]]'s sharpest cross-examination drives [[Jacob Hansen]] to admit what, on the LDS view, a believing Baptist still lacks. Hansen affirms the first principles and ordinances — faith, repentance, baptism by immersion, the gift of the Holy Ghost — which "are all things that happen in a Baptist church." So what is missing? Hansen: "priesthood authority... the ordinances of the priesthood have to be administered by those who have the authority," found in fullness "only in the LDS church right now." Pressed whether one must therefore "go through the LDS church to get to the highest form of heaven," Hansen reframes: the ordinances are "necessary but not sufficient conditions" (John 3:5, "except a man be born of water and the spirit").[^1] Stuckey's response is that this exposes the real gospel as "grace, faith, baptism *plus going through the Mormon church*."[^2]

## Evidence
- Hansen repeatedly relocates the boundary from the *church* to "the priesthood structure that Christ has organized on earth," but concedes the fullness of that priesthood exists in no other body — so the practical answer is the LDS church (see [[Apostasy Was Complete When the Keys Departed]]).
- The Catholic parallel is explicit: "no salvation outside the Catholic Church" because apostolic succession governs who may baptize; Hansen claims the LDS restoration recovers the authority even Rome lost.[^3]
- Stuckey's force comes from agreement-then-subtraction: she grants every step Hansen lists, then notes a Spirit-filled, baptized Protestant still falls short by his theology — making priesthood the true dividing line, not faith in Christ.

## Best Quotes
> "Those are necessary but not sufficient conditions, because as Jesus said to Nicodemus, except a man be born of water and the spirit, he cannot enter the kingdom of God." [^1]

> "It's not just grace, faith... it's grace, faith, baptism plus going through the Mormon church." [^2]

## LDS Connection
This is the structural heart of the LDS exclusivity claim and the point Stuckey treats as disqualifying: if Christ's atonement received by faith is not *sufficient* for the fullness of relationship with God, then (on the evangelical reading) something has been added to the gospel of grace — see [[Sola Fide vs Faith Plus Ordinances]] and [[Covenant Relationship Over Theology Quiz]].
