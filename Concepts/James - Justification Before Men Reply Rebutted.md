# James — "Justification Before Men" Reply Rebutted

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
Protestants ([[John Calvin]]) say James's "justified by works" means *vindicated / shown righteous before men* — works only *demonstrate* a righteousness already had, they don't increase it before God.

## The Rebuttal

### 1. No audience for Abraham's work
[[Genesis 22|Genesis 22:5]]: Abraham and Isaac went away *from their servants* to the sacrifice. There were no witnesses to be impressed — so "justified before men" has nothing to work with [^1].

### 2. "Shown" is not in the text
Robert Zins notes "the word *shown* is not in the passage." [[Thomas Schreiner]] (himself Reformed) concedes there is "no evidence" James means justification before people rather than God [^2].

### 3. James says "active" and "completed," not "shown"
James 2:22: faith "was **active** along with his works and faith was **completed** by works" — language of cooperation and increase, not mere demonstration. James cites [[Genesis 15.6|Genesis 15:6]] as *fulfilled* in the offering of Isaac [^3].

## Best Quote
> "Paul means... the gratuitous imputation of righteousness before the tribunal of God, and James the manifestation of righteousness... before men." — [[John Calvin]] (the reading being rebutted) [^4]
