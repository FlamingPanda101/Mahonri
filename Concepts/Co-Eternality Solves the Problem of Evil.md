# Co-Eternality Solves the Problem of Evil

#type/claim

## The Claim

Because, per [[D&C 93|D&C 93]], part of every person is uncreated and has always had agency (see [[Intelligence Is Uncreated and Eternally Has Agency]]), [[Casey Paul Griffiths]] argues Mormonism dissolves the classic problem of evil: God is not the author of human evil because he did not program the human will.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The David Paulsen argument.** Griffiths invokes [[David Paulsen]]'s devotional "Joseph Smith and the Problem of Evil." Standard theodicy answers "why does God allow evil?" with free will; the rebuttal "why didn't God just make us unable to choose evil?" is answered by D&C 93 — "there's a part of you that God did make and it's always had agency," so a bad choice "is not God's fault."[^2]
2. **Agency is primeval.** God "nurtured" and "tried to teach" the eternal intelligence, but "agency is primeval, it's always existed." The serial-killer analogy: a parent can do everything right and still have a child who, by agency, turns out badly — "we could say the same thing about God."[^3]
3. **The Truman Madson endorsement.** Griffiths cites philosopher [[Truman Madsen]] that section 93 "cuts through the Gordian knot with one slice" by simply stating that free will is eternal and God is not responsible for evil — "God did the best he could with the materials that were there."[^4]
4. **Multi-section support.** Griffiths stresses this is "not just a section 93 thing — this is a section 76 thing, it's a section 130 thing," presenting it as a coherent revelatory package (see [[D&C 76]], [[D&C 130.20-21|D&C 130]]).[^5]
5. **Steelman of the opposing view.** The classical theist replies that this "solves" evil only by *shrinking God*: a deity who "did the best he could with the materials that were there" is a finite craftsman constrained by co-eternal stuff, not the omnipotent God of classical theism — trading the problem of evil for a limited God (a cost Griffiths implicitly accepts at [^4]). Free-will theodicies within classical theism (Plantinga's free-will defense) claim to preserve genuine libertarian freedom *without* positing uncreated souls, so the LDS move is not the *only* route to the same conclusion. And the framework arguably relocates rather than removes the problem: God still chose to organize *these* intelligences into *this* world knowing some would become "the other guy."

## Best Quotes

> "Section 93 would say there's a part of you that God did make and it's always had agency, and therefore when a person makes a bad decision it's not God's fault."[^6]

> "Truman Madsen, who was a philosopher, said that section 93 cuts through the Gordian knot with one slice... Is God responsible for evil in the world? No. God did the best he could with the materials that were there."[^7]

> "If everything goes according to plan, you're going to turn out to be like Jesus. But there's also got to be the philosophical possibility that you make all the wrong decisions and you end up like the other guy."[^8]
