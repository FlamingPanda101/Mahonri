# The Recoil from OT Violence Is Itself Christian

## The Claim

[[Glenn Scrivener]] argues that the very fact all three interlocutors *recoil* from Old Testament violence (Joshua's conquest, [[Exodus 21.20-21|slave-beating]], Exodus 21) is evidence of their Christianization. The book of Joshua "puts a lump in both our throats. But it wouldn't have put a lump in a Roman throat... in Marcus Aurelius's throat... in Muhammad's throat... in Sun Tzu's throat."[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The "lump in the throat" asymmetry.** Our moral disgust at conquest narratives is, Scrivener claims, a culturally acquired sensibility, not a universal human reaction.[^1] *(Provocative — there is real truth that moral sensibilities vary by era; but it is hard to verify that *no* ancient figure felt such disgust.)*

2. **Shared intuition, different metaethics.** Scrivener generalizes this at the end: what the three share at [[Exodus 21.20-21|Exodus 21]] "is not a metaethical theory at all... we have quite different metaethical theories and very similar moral intuitions." Those intuitions "have a history behind them."[^2] *(Strong and conciliatory — see [[Shared Intuitions Despite Different Metaethics]]. Even O'Connor concedes his emotions "would be very different had I been born in another time, place or culture.")*

3. **O'Connor's concession.** Asked whether his outrage is a product of Christian culture, O'Connor: "I have no doubt."[^3] *(Major concession — O'Connor grants the historical-shaping point at the level of *intuitions*, while denying it settles the *uniqueness/necessity* question.)*

4. **Steelman of the opposing view.** O'Connor's limit: granting that *his recoil* is Christianized does not establish that the *values themselves could only* have come from Christianity — the recoil is an effect, not proof of unique causation. He also notes Aristotle "might" have recoiled at the conquest (unless he deemed the Canaanites natural slaves).[^4] *(Fair — the lens argument explains the disposition without proving exclusivity; it shares the [[The Falsifiability Demand Against the Air-We-Breathe Thesis|unfalsifiability]] vulnerability that the same move could absorb any data.)*

## Best Quotes

> "There are all sorts of aspects of the book of Joshua that... put a lump in all our throats. But they wouldn't have put a lump in a Roman throat... in Marcus Aurelius's throat."[^1]

> "What we three share at that point is not a metaethical theory at all... we have quite different metaethical theories and very similar moral intuitions."[^2]
