# Preaching Reorients All Scripture Toward Christ

## The Point
The foundational hermeneutic of early Christian preaching of the Old Testament was set by the risen Christ on the road to Emmaus: "beginning with Moses and all the prophets, he interpreted to them in all the scriptures the things concerning himself" (Luke 24:27). The good news of Jesus became "the interpretative key to understanding scripture," so that the Old Testament was read as "a treasury of prophetic words and types" all pointing to Christ.

## Evidence
1. **The Emmaus origin.** The disciples were "dazed and confused, unaware of the full shape of the Messiah's redeeming mission"; because "they could not recognize Jesus in the scriptures, they also could not recognize him then." Christ's roadside exposition opened their minds — "Did not our hearts burn within us?" (Luke 24:32). [^1]
2. **Christ as interpretive key.** "The good news of Jesus Christ — his birth, life, ministry, suffering, death, and Resurrection — became the interpretative key to understanding scripture and the basis of Christian preaching of the Old Testament." [^1]
3. **The OT as types and prophecy.** "For early Christians, the Old Testament became a treasury of prophetic words and types." This conviction is most extreme in Jacob of Serugh, of whom a scholar writes: "there is not even one line in the Old Testament that does not proclaim the way of the Son." [^2]
4. **Concluding summary.** "The sermon reoriented all scripture towards Christ and constantly demonstrated how the life and ministry of Jesus was the key to scriptural understanding." [^3]

## Best Quotes
> "Beginning with Moses and all the prophets, he interpreted to them in all the scriptures the things concerning himself." (Luke 24:27 RSV) [^1]

> "The good news of Jesus Christ … became the interpretative key to understanding scripture and the basis of Christian preaching of the Old Testament." [^1]

## LDS Connection
The principle that "all things … are the typifying of [Christ]" is itself a Book of Mormon teaching (2 Nephi 11:4; cf. Moses 6:63, "all things are created and made to bear record of me"). Heal even footnotes that the chapter's recurring phrase about preachers who "talked of Christ, rejoiced in Christ, and preached of Christ" borrows language from 2 Nephi 25:26 — directly tying the patristic Christocentric reading of scripture to Nephite testimony.
