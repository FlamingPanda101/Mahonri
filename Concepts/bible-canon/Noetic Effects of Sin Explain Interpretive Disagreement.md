# Noetic Effects of Sin Explain Interpretive Disagreement

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
[[Anthony Rogers]] answers the perspicuity objection (see [[Perspicuity Fails the Hard-Cases Test]]) by arguing that interpretive disagreement is fully explained *without* impugning Scripture's objective clarity. The chief cause is the **noetic effects of sin** — "sin makes us stupid" — plus upbringing, blindness, and (above all) people *not actually* going by Scripture alone but "hearkening to the voice of others." The light is clear; the defect is in the eye.[^1]

## The Rebuttal

### 1. Blindness, not obscurity — rate: internally coherent, externally question-begging
Rogers' analogy is consistent: a blind man's failure to see the sun proves nothing against the sun; cataracts don't darken the object.[^2] Scriptural backing: John 10:26 ("you do not believe because you are not my sheep"), [[John 18.37]] ("he who is of the truth hears my voice").[^3] **Steelman of Horn:** this risks *unfalsifiability* — any disagreement, however sincere and learned, gets re-described as spiritual blindness. Horn presses the dilemma in cross-ex: the room full of sola-scriptura Protestants who reject paedobaptism — are they all "stupid"/blind? Horn would rather say "they made a mistake," which concedes the text was not clear enough. See [[Perspicuity Fails the Hard-Cases Test]].

### 2. The disagreers aren't really doing sola scriptura — rate: moderate, partly circular
Rogers' strongest version: JWs, Mormons, Unitarians disagree precisely because they "hearken to the voice of others" (the Watchtower, the Book of Mormon), so their errors are *not* products of Scripture alone but of competing authorities.[^4] This is a fair diagnosis of *cultic* hermeneutics. **Steelman of Horn:** it does not explain disagreement *among* sincere sola-scriptura Protestants (Baptist vs Presbyterian vs Lutheran on baptism/Eucharist), who all claim Scripture alone. Rogers grants even committed sola-scriptura believers can "inadvertently" add to the text — which quietly concedes that "Scripture alone" does not guarantee convergence.

### 3. The Watchtower exchange — rate: a draw
In cross-ex, Rogers locates the JW error in their "primacy of the Watchtower"; Horn replies JWs deny the Watchtower is *infallible*, paralleling how Protestants hold creeds as authoritative-but-fallible.[^5] **Assessment:** both land a hit — Rogers rightly notes an external authority is operative; Horn rightly notes "authoritative but fallible" is the Protestant self-description too.

## Best Quotes
> "It's because people aren't really going by sola scriptura. Look at the sects and the cults ... They all hearken to the voice of others."[^4]

> [Rogers] "The noetic effects of sin. In other words, sin makes us stupid." — [Horn] "So sin made them all stupid."[^1]

> "If a blind man doesn't see the light, there's nothing wrong with the light. It's something wrong with his eyes."[^2]

> [Horn] "I don't think you're spiritually blind. I think you may have made a mistake. ... it's easy to misunderstand scripture because Peter tells us that in second Peter 3:16."[^6]
