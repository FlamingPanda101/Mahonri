# Apostasy Preserved the Text Steelman

## The Claim
A deliberate steelman from Day: even granting a Great Apostasy, the very imperial-Christian apparatus that corrupted doctrine *also preserved the Bible*, the cathedrals, and the tradition for a thousand years. "What if it was a great apostasy [that] preserved the text?" The apostasy was not simply darkness; Joseph Smith condemned the *creeds* as an abomination, not all of Christianity.

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. The apologists took Christianity from "superstition" to survivable philosophy (rating: moderate; Day)
By making Christianity intellectually respectable to the educated classes, the apologists let it survive and spread in the Roman world — "from bad superstition to decent philosophy." Without that, the faith might not have endured to be restored. See [[Greek Philosophy Corrupted the Christian Concept of God]]. [^1]

### 2. The empire preserved the physical/textual heritage (rating: moderate; Day)
When Christianity fused with empire it "preserved the text... the cathedrals and the architecture." Day's contrast: the Nephites' record survived only because it was buried in a hill; absent the Catholic preservation of the Bible "we wouldn't even know what they believed." [^2]

### 3. Jerome and the Vulgate (rating: moderate; case study)
[[Jerome]], connected to ecclesiastical power, retreated to Jerusalem, learned Hebrew, and produced the Latin Vulgate (~405) — "the dude who gave us the Bible." A corrupt power system nonetheless produced the text that moved Day when he first read John. [^3]

### 4. Joseph Smith condemned creeds, not all Christianity (rating: moderate; clarification)
Day and Hayden stress that in the Joseph Smith History the *creeds* are called "an abomination," not the whole Christian tradition; the apostate church still "affected the grace of Christ... to millions of people who otherwise would not have had that." [^4]

### 5. Steelman of the opposing view (i.e., the position Day is steelmanning *against* his own thesis)
This concept *is* the steelman — Day arguing the opposing side's best point against the apostasy narrative. The natural rebuttal from a strict apostasy view (e.g. [[Bruce R. McConkie]]'s "keys departed," see [[Apostasy Was Complete When the Keys Departed]]) is that preserving a *text* is not preserving *authority*: a transmitted Bible without priesthood keys still leaves the church in apostasy. Day holds both in tension, giving "props to these guys even though they didn't get everything right."

## Best Quotes

> "What if it was a great apostasy [that] preserved the text? It preserved the cathedrals and the architecture." [^2]

> "He says the creeds are an abomination... Joseph Smith said that everything was bad and it was all darkness — no, actually, he says the creeds are an abomination." [^4]

> "The Bible's coming out of this. And I remember the first time it moved me... I give props to these guys even though they didn't get everything right." [^3]
