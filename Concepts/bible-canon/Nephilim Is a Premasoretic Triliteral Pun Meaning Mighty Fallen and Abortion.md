# Nephilim Is a Premasoretic Triliteral Pun Meaning Mighty Fallen and Abortion

## The Claim

[[Jonah Barnes]] argues that the word **"giants" in [[Genesis 6.1-4|Genesis 6:4]] is a mistranslation**: the underlying Hebrew *nephilim* never meant "giant." Because **premasoretic Hebrew was written with consonants only** (the root *n-p-l*), the same three consonants can be vocalized to yield a deliberate **triliteral pun** carrying three meanings at once — **"mighty," "fallen ones" (*nephal*), and "abortions/miscarriages" (*nephel*).** The Greek *gigantes* (the Septuagint rendering behind English "giants") itself means **"earth-born,"** not "giant" — and is faintly preserved in the KJV phrase "giants *in the earth*." The literal-stature "giants" tradition (e.g., the "13-cubit bed") is a **later Deuteronomist/Ethiopic gloss**, not the original sense. [^1] [^2]

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. Consonantal Hebrew lets one root carry multiple vocalizations — rate: moderate (sound linguistics, overstated application)
Barnes: premasoretic Hebrew has no written vowels, so a triliteral root is a *семantic kernel* that "you can insert any vowels to change." He illustrates with his own name ("Jonah… Jan… Jon… as long as the consonants are the same"). The Masoretes (Tiberian scribal school, ~500-1000 CE) later fixed one vocalization, "killing" the multidimensionality. This is the same mechanism the vault documents in [[Masoretic Vowels Reversed the Active Verb to Passive]]. [^1]

### 2. The *n-p-l* root yields mighty / fallen / abortion — rate: weak-moderate (he cites others, not himself)
Reading a paragraph he attributes to other scholars: "through word plays on orthographically similar Hebrew terms, the Nephilim (giants) of Genesis 6:4 came to be understood in Jewish texts as **fallen ones** (*nephila*) using the same consonants, and **abortions**." Barnes: "The word actually means three things — it means **mighty, fallen, and abortion**." He stresses "this is not me… people have known about this for a long time." The three senses converge on his larger thesis (mighty men who fell and who aborted children). [^2]

### 3. Greek *gigantes* = "earth-born," not "giant" — rate: moderate (etymology is correct; semantic drift to "giant" is real)
"In Greek it's *gigantes*, and *gigantes* is actually not 'giant' — it means **men of the earth**," faintly echoed in "there were giants *in the earth*." Barnes compares the chthonic Titans/Kronos "that lived underground." So even the Greek the translators used does not straightforwardly mean huge-statured beings. [^3]

### 4. The stature/"13-cubit bed" is a later overlay — rate: weak (contested; cuts against plain Numbers/Deuteronomy text)
Barnes claims "it isn't until the Deuteronomists get hold of the text that they start saying 'and he was 13 cubits,'" and the Ethiopic Book of Enoch inflates them to "3,000 miles high" — evidence to him that the literal-giant reading is a corruption layered onto an original word that just meant fallen/mighty apostates. (See the companion claims [[The Nephilim Were Apostate Sethite Priesthood-Holders Not Supernatural Giants]] and [[The Sin of the Nephilim Was Sterilization Abortion and Child Sacrifice]].) [^4]

## Steelman of the Opposing View

The philology is partly sound and partly overreaching. (a) **"Earth-born" for *gigantes* is correct, and "fallen ones" for *nephilim* is a respectable etymology** (from *naphal*, "to fall") — so far Barnes tracks real scholarship. But (b) the **"abortion/miscarriage" sense (*nephel*) is a genuine Hebrew word, yet there is no evidence the Genesis 6 author intended it**; that *the same consonants can* mean miscarriage does not show the text *does* mean it — this is the etymological/root fallacy (a word's meaning is fixed by usage and context, not by every form its consonants could take), the very error the vault flags elsewhere ([[The Root Fallacy - Theopneustos Parts Do Not Yield Infallible]]). (c) The **giant-stature reading is not a late Deuteronomist invention**: [[Numbers 13]]:33 ("we were as grasshoppers" beside the Nephilim/sons of Anak) and Deuteronomy 3:11 (Og's iron bedstead) are part of the conquest narrative, and the host immediately presses Barnes on exactly this — that the spies' report and Goliath of Gath imply *physical* size, to which Barnes can only answer "I don't believe that… the Bible's corrupted," conceding he is overriding the text. (d) The Septuagint translators chose *gigantes* precisely because Second Temple Jews *did* understand these beings as prodigious/mighty offspring of the Watchers, so the "giant" connotation is ancient, not a mistranslation. The defensible core: "giants" is a **loose English rendering** of a word whose primary force is "fallen/mighty ones," and *gigantes* literally means "earth-born" — a worthwhile corrective — but the maximal claim that *nephilim* "means abortion" and that all stature language is corruption is an etymological overreach Barnes adopts to serve his reconstruction, and the transcript shows him admitting he is reading *against* the received text.
