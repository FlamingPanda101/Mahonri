# The Narrative Reading of Scripture vs Proof-Texting

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)

The objection (O'Connor's method): isolating a verse like [[Exodus 22.29|Exodus 22:29]] ("the firstborn of your sons you shall give to me") and reading it as a stand-alone timeless command exposes the Bible's brutality.

## The Rebuttal

[[Glenn Scrivener]]: Christians have *never* read Scripture this way. "To go into a verse and take out of it a timeless principle that stands by itself and probably could cause somebody to kill their son — it's just not the way... You know that's not the way Christendom has read the Bible."[^1]

1. **Scripture is a story, not an aggregated philosophy.** Scrivener: "When Christians say the Bible says X, what they don't mean is: if we aggregate all these propositions and synthesize a philosophy, that's what we'd get. We're saying those verses are being swept up into a grand narrative that culminates in Christ."[^2] *(This is mainstream Christian hermeneutics — christotelic / redemptive-historical reading. Genuinely the way the tradition reads, not an ad hoc dodge.)*

2. **Worked example: [[Genesis 22|the Binding of Isaac]].** Rather than "Abraham was told to kill his son, so I can too," Scrivener reads it typologically — Isaac the promised seed carries the wood uphill toward Moriah (the temple mount) to be a burnt offering, the ram substitutes, "the LORD will provide" a lamb — pointing to Christ. See [[The Binding of Isaac as a Type of Christ]].[^3] *(Internally coherent and textually attentive; Scrivener insists it is "utterly consonant with the Hebrew details.")*

3. **O'Connor's concession and counter.** O'Connor *agrees* his proof-texting is "illegitimate to some degree" — he was "demonstrating the simplicity in my very own approach" to show the symmetrical move is too easy for *both* sides (one can proof-text Rome as anti-sacrifice too).[^4] His real demand: stop proof-texting and look at "the overall picture" — and he argues the big-picture OT still endorses [[The Exodus Is Israelite-Specific Not Universal Abolition|Israelite-specific slavery]].[^5] *(Fair-minded — O'Connor is not naively literalist; he uses the method self-consciously as a reductio.)*

4. **Steelman of O'Connor:** A narrative-toward-Christ reading is *non-falsifiable* in the same way the broader thesis is — any embarrassing text can be "swept up" and redeemed, so the method cannot in principle yield a verse that counts against Christian morality. The historian-critical reading (e.g. [[Surviving Traces of Human Sacrifice in the Hebrew Bible|Exodus 22:29 as a real survival of child sacrifice later renegotiated]]) is at least testable against ANE evidence. *(Strong methodological caution — the narrative reading's virtue, coherence, is also its epistemic vulnerability.)*
