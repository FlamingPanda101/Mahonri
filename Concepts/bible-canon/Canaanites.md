# Canaanites

The Canaanites were the Semitic-speaking peoples inhabiting the land of Canaan (the southern Levant) in the second and first millennia BCE — the population the Hebrew Bible depicts Israel encountering, displacing, and being commanded to destroy in the conquest narratives. In the biblical genealogy they descend from Canaan, son of Ham.

## Relevance

The Canaanites figure in the vault's treatment of the Bible's morally troubling commands: the herem ("ban") texts ordering their extermination are cited in the argument that rejecting strict biblical inerrancy dissolves the moral-contradiction problem, since such commands can be read as the human-conditioned voice of the text rather than a timeless divine order. They also stand behind the [[curse of Canaan]] and [[curse of Ham]] interpretive traditions.
