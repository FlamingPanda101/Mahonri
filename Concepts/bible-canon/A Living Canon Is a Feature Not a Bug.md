# A Living Canon Is a Feature Not a Bug

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Stephen Smoot|Steven Smoot]] argues Latter-day Saints have not merely an *open* canon but a **living** canon, and that its mutability — textual variants, content added and removed, editions rearranged — is "a feature, not a bug." Members should not be "scandalized" or experience "angst" over canonical change, *provided* the changes come from legitimate prophetic authority holding the priesthood keys. The pastoral payoff is defensive: understanding this history inoculates believers against "bad faith actors" who weaponize the fact that an old Pearl of Great Price contained something now absent ("it's a cheap shot").

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Change is intrinsic to a genuinely open canon.** "If you have an open canon, that means that canon can change. And we shouldn't... be scandalized or have a lot of angst over changes in our canon, so long as we recognize it is from legitimate prophetic authority." Smoot stresses this is not "freewheeling" — there is a "structural hierarchical organization that does this properly within the bounds the Lord has set" [^1].

2. **Human involvement guarantees imperfection — and scripture admits it.** Because "humans are involved in making these books... human error is going to be in these books at certain times" — true of the Bible, Book of Mormon, D&C, and Pearl of Great Price alike. Smoot appeals to the Book of Mormon's own title-page concession that mistakes are "the fault of men," and the hosts add the LDS rejection of revelation *ex nihilo* [^2][^3].

3. **The doctrinal upside: a God who adapts.** The hosts frame canonical dynamism positively — "I have a better appreciation for how the Lord speaks to the church and how he can really... adapt to our changes in time." A living canon is evidence of ongoing divine communication, not of an unreliable text [^4].

4. **It is also a defense against weaponized history.** The explicit aim is "so that bad faith actors can't blindside you with, oh, did you know in the old Pearl of Great Price had this and it's not there anymore? ... It's a cheap shot." Resources like a study edition supply the "fuller context" that defuses the gotcha [^5].

## Best Quotes
> "We have a living canon, not just an open canon... This is a feature, not a bug in terms of how scripture works." [^1]

> "Humans are involved in making these books and so human error is going to be in these books at certain times." [^2]

> "So that bad faith actors can't blindside you with, oh, did you know in the old Pearl of Great Price had this and it's not there anymore? ... It's a cheap shot." [^5]

## LDS Connection
This is the normative, pastoral counterpart to the descriptive [[The Pearl of Great Price Is the Most Unstable Volume of LDS Scripture]]. It complements [[An Open Canon Allows Unbounded Ecclesiastical Authority]] (which stresses the *authority cost* of openness) by stressing its *devotional benefit*, and it answers the Protestant charge in [[The LDS Tradition Emptied the Canon Rather Than Expanded It]] by reframing mutability as honesty about every canon's real status. Contrast [[Closing the Canon Was Used to Declare Prophecy Ended]].
