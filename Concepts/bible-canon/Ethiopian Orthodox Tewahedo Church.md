# Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church

The Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church is one of the Oriental Orthodox (non-Chalcedonian) churches, with roots in the fourth-century Christianization of the Kingdom of Aksum. *Tewahedo* ("made one") signals its miaphysite Christology. It is notable for maintaining the broadest biblical canon in Christendom — an 81-book canon that includes works absent from other traditions.

## Relevance

The Church is cited in the vault as the sole tradition to preserve [[1 Enoch]] in its full scriptural canon (in Ge'ez/Ethiopic). This bears on arguments about the fluidity of the biblical canon and the contested boundary between canonical and extracanonical scripture — a theme relevant to Latter-day Saint claims about an open canon.
