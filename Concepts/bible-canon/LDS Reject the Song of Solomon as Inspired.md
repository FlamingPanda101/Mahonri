# LDS Reject the Song of Solomon as Inspired

## The Claim
A factual point Hayden raises to break Kelly's "we share the same 66 books" framing: **Latter-day Saints do not hold the identical 66-book canon.** Joseph Smith judged the **Song of Solomon** to be *not inspired*, so functionally "we have 65... plus more" (the Book of Mormon, D&C, Pearl of Great Price).[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. Joseph Smith's judgment — rate: accurate
"We have a specific book in the 66 books you use that we deny as scripture. So if anything, we have 65."[^2] **Historical context (correct):** Joseph Smith's Bible-translation manuscript (the JST) carries the note that "the Songs of Solomon are not inspired writings," and the book is omitted from the JST. The LDS edition still *prints* the Song of Solomon in its KJV but flags this view. Hayden recalls a footnote to that effect: "while included in the biblical canon, [it] is often viewed by Latter-day Saints as non-inspired scripture."[^3]

### 2. Kelly's rebuttal: LDS.org lists all 39 OT books — rate: partially right, talking past
Kelly insists the Church's own website enumerates "39 books in the Old Testament" and "27 books of the New Testament" as accepted canon, and that the Song of Solomon is quoted in LDS publications, so it is treated as canon.[^4] **Assessment:** both can be true — the book is *included in the printed canon* yet *regarded as uninspired* by the JST tradition; the two men partly talk past each other (canonical inclusion vs. inspiration status). Hayden allows "there could be an error on the website," conceding he came unprepared to document the precise current position.[^5]

### 3. Why it matters to the resolution — rate: tactical
Hayden's purpose is narrow: to deny Kelly's recurring premise that "if there's a problem with how we got our 66, so do Latter-day Saints — they're on the same page."[^6] By showing the canons are *not* identical (and that the LDS canon is *open*, not closed at 66), Hayden blocks Kelly from leveraging shared agreement and keeps the burden on the *Protestant* infallibility claim. See [[The Canon Question Is Logically Prior to Canon Authority]].

## Best Quotes
> "We actually don't hold to the 66-book canon that you do... we have a specific book in the 66 books that you use that we deny as scripture. So if anything, we have 65. So it's not the same canon. And plus, we have more."[^2]

> "The Songs of Solomon, while included in the biblical canon, is often viewed by Latter-day Saints as non-inspired scripture." (Hayden, recalling a footnote)[^3]

> "Both both things were quoted directly from LDS.org." (Kelly, citing all 39 OT + 27 NT as accepted)[^4]
