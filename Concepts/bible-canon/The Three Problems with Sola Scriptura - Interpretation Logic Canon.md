# The Three Problems with Sola Scriptura - Interpretation, Logic, Canon

## The Point
Hayden Carroll frames the Latter-day Saint rejection of *Sola Scriptura* (the Bible as the sole infallible authority) around **three problems**, which he argues are the *root* of nearly every Latter-day Saint vs. Protestant disagreement, the Trinity included: the problem of **interpretation**, the problem of **logic**, and the problem of **canon**. [^1]

## Evidence
1. **The problem of interpretation.** If the Bible is infallible and clear, why is there pervasive disagreement about what it teaches? Appeals to "the essentials" fail because the Bible contains no divine list of which doctrines are essential, and Sola Scriptura forbids appealing to anything outside the Bible to adjudicate. "Who gets to decide what is clear and what is not?" [^2] (cf. [[No Final Interpreter - The Protestant Adjudication Problem]])
2. **The problem of logic.** Using the Bible to validate the Bible (e.g., citing 2 Timothy 3:16) is **circular** and self-defeating — "what argument do you have against Mormonism... that doesn't already appeal to and assume the correct nature of my own?" A self-authenticating book is, on this view, self-defeating. [^3] (cf. [[The Circularity Charge Against Self-Attesting Scripture]])
3. **The problem of canon.** A *closed* canon cannot be taught by the Bible, because while the Bible was being written the canon was still open. Carroll cites James White conceding that "Protestants do not assert that Sola Scriptura is a valid concept during times of revelation." The Bible gives no inspired table of contents. [^4] (cf. [[Authorization - Divine Designation Required for an Infallible Canon]])

## Best Quotes
> "Our issue with Sola Scriptura can essentially be broken down into 3 major problems... the problem of interpretation, the problem of logic, [and] the problem of canon." [^1]

> "Appealing to the Bible to show that the Bible is true is an incoherent argument and massive blow to how mainstream Christians view the nature of the Bible." [^3]

## LDS Connection
This is the organizing framework behind Carroll's whole apologetic: settle the *authority/nature-of-scripture* question first, and the downstream disputes (Trinity, etc.) follow. It consolidates his canon-debate concepts ([[The Circularity Charge Against Self-Attesting Scripture]], [[Authorization - Divine Designation Required for an Infallible Canon]], [[No Final Interpreter - The Protestant Adjudication Problem]], [[Inspired vs Infallible vs Ultimate - A Distinction]]) into a three-problem schema, and sets up the positive alternative: [[God Himself Not a Book Is the Ultimate Authority]].
