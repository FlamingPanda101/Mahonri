# Documentary Hypothesis Strengthens the Restoration

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Mike Day]] argues that the Documentary Hypothesis — far from threatening Latter-day Saint faith — *strengthens* the case for the Restoration. His reasoning is biographical and evidential rather than apologetic-defensive: a 23-year-old [[Joseph Smith]] in upstate New York, who had never read Spinoza, [[Philo of Alexandria|Philo]], or [[Julius Wellhausen|Wellhausen]], produced a book that already encodes the very features critical scholarship later discovered — explicit named **sources**, visible **editors/redactors**, and a religious system in 600 BC that the Book of Mormon depicts as *opposed* to Lehi's. "In my opinion, the documentary hypothesis does not hurt the cause for restoration, it strengthens it."[^1][^2]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The Book of Mormon names sources and editors (rated: internal-textual; strong as description).** Unlike the silent Pentateuchal redactors, [[Mormon Is an Ethnocentric Redactor Focused on the Witness of Christ|Mormon]] openly tells the reader he is synthesizing many records into one. The Book of Mormon "says there are sources... it says there's editors." Day treats this as a structural match to what source critics reconstruct *behind* the Old Testament's anonymous editing.[^2]

2. **First Nephi systematically reverses Deuteronomy's distinctives (rated: strong as a pattern).** Day (crediting a podcast with [[Hayden Carroll|Hayden]]) lists the claims of [[Deuteronomy 12]] and the broader Deuteronomic program — you cannot see God, you cannot know the mysteries, God dwells only where his "name" is, no altars outside the one chosen place — and shows them "checked off one by one" with counters in the first six or seven chapters of [[1 Nephi 1.1|1 Nephi]]: Lehi and Nephi *see* God (throne-vision), build an altar in the wilderness, and Nephi opens by declaring "I... having had a great knowledge of... the mysteries of God" ([[1 Nephi 1.1|1 Nephi 1:1]]). "It's literally undoing the Deuteronomistic perspective."[^3][^4]

3. **It explains the family conflict.** Laman and Lemuel, raised under [[Hezekiah Josiah and the Deuteronomist Reforms|Josiah's reform]], would have regarded Lehi's pre-reform practice as dangerous deviance — an internal-coherence point that parallels the vault's [[Deuteronomistic Reform Explains the Book of Mormon Theology|existing treatment]].[^4]

4. **The Book of Mormon licenses an honest, non-inerrantist Bible.** Because the Book of Mormon title page concedes "if there are faults they are the mistakes of men," and because Joseph Smith only ever affirmed the Bible "as it read... from the pen of the original writers," Day says he cannot sustain the 1979 Chicago Statement on biblical inerrancy — "the Bible doesn't say that it's inerrant." The Restoration thus *predicts* a humanly-edited Bible rather than being embarrassed by one.[^5]

5. **Bradley Kramer's frame.** Day leans on [[Bradley Kramer]]'s *Beholding the Tree of Life: A Rabbinic Approach to the Book of Mormon*, which argues the Book of Mormon is engaging the very arguments being had in the First and Second Temple eras — supplying a scholarly-adjacent rationale for reading the Book of Mormon as a participant in ancient Israelite debates.[^6]

**Steelman of the skeptic.** (a) Revival-era American Christianity already affirmed an embodied theophany, a pre-existent incarnate Christ, and personal visionary religion, so Joseph had abundant *non-academic* sources for "you can see God" and "the mysteries can be known" without needing Wellhausen. (b) The "Deuteronomistic program suppressed an older religion" reconstruction (esp. [[Margaret Barker]]'s temple theology) is a *contested minority* thesis, not settled consensus — so "1 Nephi reverses the reform" assumes a framework many scholars would not grant. (c) The 2 Kings 22–23 narrative of Josiah's reform was readable in any KJV, so the *bare* idea that worship was once centralized was available; what was *not* available in 1830 is the full 20th-century editorial-school synthesis — which is the load-bearing part of Day's claim. (d) Day himself repeatedly says "I'm not saying this is true... this is the closest thing I can do," presenting the argument as a personal resolution, not a proof.[^4][^7]

## Best Quotes

> "You have a 23-year-old in upstate New York who's never — Wellhausen is not doing this stuff. He's never read Spinoza. He's probably never heard of Philo. And... the Book of Mormon exists. It says there are sources... it says there's editors... and it says there's a different religious system going on in 600 BC."[^2]

> "Can you see God? Yes. Can you build an altar outside Jerusalem? Yes. Can you know the mysteries? That's verse one of First Nephi... It's literally undoing the Deuteronomistic perspective... and Joseph's 23 years old."[^4]

> "In my opinion, the documentary hypothesis does not hurt the cause for restoration, it strengthens it."[^1]
