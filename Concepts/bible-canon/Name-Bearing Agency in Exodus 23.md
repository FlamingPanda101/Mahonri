# Name-Bearing Agency in Exodus 23

## The Claim
[[Dan McClellan]] points to [[Exodus 23.20-21|Exodus 23:20-21]] as the paradigm case of **name-bearing divine agency** in the Hebrew Bible: God sends a messenger (*malʾāk*) whom Israel must obey because "my name is in him," and who consequently holds authority over the forgiveness of transgressions. This illustrates "the idea that the divine name is a communicable vehicle of divine agency and authority and presence" — "the intuitive logic of divine images." [^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The divine name confers divine prerogative.** Forgiveness of sins is normally God's exclusive prerogative, yet the messenger exercises it precisely *because* the name indwells him. McClellan: the name is "a communicable vehicle of divine agency and authority and presence." [^1] *Rating: strong; the grammar of Exod 23:21 ties the messenger's authority directly to the indwelling name.*

2. **Name theology in the ANE.** A deity's name was widely understood as an extension of the deity's own power and presence (cf. Deuteronomy's "name theology," the *šem* dwelling in the temple). *Rating: strong, well-attested.* *Steelman:* a Trinitarian could grant all of this and say it *prefigures* the incarnation rather than replacing it — the angel-of-YHWH who bears the name and forgives sins is, on a traditional reading, a christophany.

3. **It supplies a non-philosophical template for Jesus.** This name-bearing-agent model gives the NT a home-grown way to say Jesus exercises God's authority and manifests God's presence without asserting strict identity — undercutting the claim that only a Greek-philosophical Trinity could account for the data. See [[Divine Images Explain Jesus Relationship to God]]. *Rating: persuasive within McClellan's framework; depends on the comparative case against the Trinity.*

## Best Quotes
> "God says in Exodus 23 that this agent, the messenger of Adonai, has authority over the forgiveness of sins because my name is in him." [^1]

> "This is the idea that the divine name is a communicable vehicle of divine agency and authority and presence. This is the intuitive logic of divine images." [^1]
