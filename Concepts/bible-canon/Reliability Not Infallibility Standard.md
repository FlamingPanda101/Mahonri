# Reliability, Not Infallibility (The LDS Prophetic Standard)

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
[[Joe Heschmeyer]] applies the **[[Deuteronomy 18.20-22|Deuteronomy 18]]** false-prophet test: if a prophet teaches falsehood "as if it came from God," he is disqualified. [[Luke Hansen]]'s central rebuttal across the whole conversation is that the LDS hold prophets to **reliability**, not **infallibility** — a deliberately lower bar than the one Heschmeyer assumes.

## The Rebuttal

### 1. The standard is stated on the Book of Mormon title page (strong — primary, foundational)
Hansen: "right from the beginning, title page of the Book of Mormon, Latter-day Saints have had an understanding of reliability rather than infallibility." So Heschmeyer is "holding [himself] to a higher standard" of OT infallibility that the LDS never claimed for their own scripture [^1].

### 2. The "double standard" charge (moderate — tu quoque)
Hansen accuses Heschmeyer of "a double standard going on with how high you hold your own people to versus others" — Catholics excuse Old Testament "incompleteness" as **divine pedagogy** (slavery not abolished at once; henotheism "affirmed" per [[Trent Horn]]) but won't extend the same charity to Brigham Young [^2]. See [[Henotheism and Divine Pedagogy in the Old Testament]].

### 3. A "more natural standard" (weak — assertion, not argument)
Hansen claims reliability is "just because it's a more natural standard to have" [^3] — but offers no independent criterion, which is precisely the gap Heschmeyer exploits.

### Steelman (Heschmeyer's side) — the unfalsifiability problem
Heschmeyer's strongest point: when Young gives "a revelation against the reliability of revelation," **what standard is left** to test his authenticity? "Is it just something like a burning in the bosom?" If no theological claim can falsify the prophet, the silver-bullet always dissolves into "we don't believe that today, who cares?" [^4]. He grants the reliability framing has "enough wiggle room" to absorb any contradiction — which is the bug, not the feature [^5]. He later argues even *reliability* fails: a prophet who can't tell "whether he's talking to Heavenly Father or Adam or Michael" is "not reliable" [^6].

## Best Quotes
> "Right from the beginning, title page of the Book of Mormon, Latter-day Saints have had an understanding of reliability rather than infallibility. So you're holding yourself to a higher standard." — Luke Hansen [^1]

> "When he's giving a revelation against the reliability of revelation... what standard is left to test his authenticity? Is it just something like a burning in the bosom?" — Joe Heschmeyer [^4]

> "Even if you hold the lower standard of reliability, that's not reliable would be the argument." — Joe Heschmeyer [^6]
