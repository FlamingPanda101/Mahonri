# The Psalms Predate and Were Not Rewritten by the Deuteronomists

## The Claim
[[Aubrey Johnson]] argues that the Psalms are **old** (going back to the early Davidic monarchy, c. 1000 BC) and were *not* a product of the Deuteronomic school's reworking of the text. Where the Psalms and Deuteronomy share language, the influence runs the *other* way: the later Deuteronomists and prophets borrowed from the Psalms, which carry the older "OG" cultic religion.

## Supporting Evidence
1. **The Psalms are Davidic-era (rated: Johnson's dating).** Johnson dates many Psalms (e.g. 74, 89, 91) to "the early monarchy… a thousand BC," and reconstructs the cultic prophets' doctrines and "stereotyped phrases" from them.[^1]
2. **Priority over Deuteronomy (rated: Johnson's argument).** Against the claim that the Psalms are "late and have been rewritten by the Deuteronomic school," Johnson holds "it's the other way around… later prophets borrow from the Psalms." The shared vocabulary reflects the Psalms' priority, not Deuteronomistic editing of them.[^2]
3. **The "original gangster" religion (rated: framing).** The hosts paraphrase Johnson: the Psalms preserve "the OG religion," the cultic prophets' way-truth-life creed, which predates and is independent of the law-centered reform.[^3] See [[The Cultic Prophets Creed Was the Way the Truth and the Life]].
4. **Converges with Barker and the festal drama (rated: cross-link).** The claim parallels [[Margaret Barker]]'s "first-temple religion suppressed by the reform" and [[John Eaton]]'s reading of the enthronement Psalms as old festal liturgy — three scholars locating the oldest layer of Israelite worship in material the Deuteronomists did not control.

**Steelman of the critic.** Psalm dating is one of the most contested questions in Hebrew Bible studies: a large body of scholarship dates much of the Psalter to the exilic and post-exilic periods, and some Psalms clearly show Deuteronomistic and wisdom influence. Johnson's early-monarchy dating and his "Deuteronomy borrowed from the Psalms" direction are a position within the debate, not a settled result. The general point (some Psalms are old and the direction of borrowing is arguable) is defensible; the blanket priority claim is contested.

## Best Quotes
> "There are claims that the Psalms are late and have been rewritten by the Deuteronomic school. His argument is no… the cult is embedded in the Psalms, and Deuteronomy and other writings later borrow from [them]."[^2]

> "This is like the OG religion… the Original Gangster."[^3]

## LDS Connection
If the Psalms are the oldest, un-reformed layer of Israelite worship, then the temple theology they encode (enthronement, ascent, the way-truth-life creed, the cultic prophets) escaped the Deuteronomist purge the vault documents in [[Deuteronomists Rewrote the Histories of Israel]]. This strengthens the Restoration's appeal to a first-temple stream behind both Christianity and the Book of Mormon, and dovetails with the source-critical picture in [[Wellhausen Thesis on Hebrew Bible Origins]].
