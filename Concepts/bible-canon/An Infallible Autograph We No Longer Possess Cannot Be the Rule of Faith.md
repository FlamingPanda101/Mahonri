# An Infallible Autograph We No Longer Possess Cannot Be the Rule of Faith

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
[[Shiloh Lofgren]]'s fourth argument against the resolution: even granting inspired *original* manuscripts, sola scriptura needs an infallible Bible **in hand** to function as "the sole infallible rule of faith." But the autographs are lost, and the text we possess demonstrably absorbed errors that were read as Scripture for centuries (the **Johannine Comma**, [[1 John 5.7|1 John 5:7-8]]; the long ending of Mark). If a Protestant retreats to "the autographs were infallible," he concedes the debate — because a text the church no longer has cannot be the governing rule of a living church.[^1]

## The Rebuttal

### 1. The Johannine Comma was preached as the Trinity for centuries — rate: strong
"Modern textual criticism has conclusively proven [the Comma] was a Latin marginal note that accidentally slipped into the Greek text in the late Middle Ages." For centuries pulpits read "there are three that bear record in heaven, the Father, the Word, and the Holy Ghost, and these three are one" — a Trinity proof-text — which modern translations have now removed.[^1] "Mainstream Christians for centuries proclaimed the infallibility of scripture while simultaneously reading from the pulpit this fraudulent verse." See [[Erasmus Was Handed a Fabricated Manuscript to Restore the Comma]] and [[The Codex Sinaiticus Marginal Note Postdates Jerome]]. **Steelman of [[Timothy J. Whitehead]]:** this shows only that *textual criticism works* — Protestants "do not willingly change or augment the scriptures" but seek the infallible autograph "as far back as we can go," the opposite of (he charges) the ~4,000 changes to the Book of Mormon. *Reply:* recovering an autograph "as far back as we can go" is precisely *not* possessing it; the rule is then "our best reconstruction," which is reliable but not infallible.

### 2. The retreat to autographs forfeits the resolution — rate: strong (the dilemma)
"TJ may want to argue that the Bible we have today is not inerrant ... but the original autographs were. Okay — we don't have the original autographs. So at that point he has conceded the debate, because a text that we don't have can't be the governing doctrine for a church."[^2] The resolution is about *the Bible* as the operative rule, not an inaccessible ideal. **Steelman of Whitehead:** he never claimed the *copies* are inerrant; the inspired-autograph + faithful-transmission position yields a Bible "reliable" enough to function. *Reply:* "reliable" is exactly [[Shiloh Lofgren]]'s own position — the debate was over **infallible**, "incapable of error," which the in-hand text is not. See [[Reliability Not Infallibility Standard]].

### 3. Infallibility requires four impossible guarantees — rate: moderate (frames the burden)
Even granting inspired authors, to call our Bible infallible Whitehead would have to prove: (1) human language can infallibly relay God's meaning; (2) transmission was infallible over 2,000 years; (3) translation across languages was errorless; (4) we interpret it so as to receive infallible truth. "Pastor TJ cannot demonstrate any of these."[^3] See [[A Text Cannot Be Infallible Because Language Is Fallible]] and [[Fallible Humans Cannot Produce an Infallible Bible]]. **Steelman of Whitehead:** he reframes the whole demand — "without sola scriptura there is no truth you would *care* to preserve"; the doctrine is a necessary *presupposition*, not a conclusion proved by transmission history. *Reply:* a presupposition that the manuscript record contradicts (errors that were canon for centuries) is not thereby rescued; it is the presupposition under strain.

**Net assessment.** The argument does not show the Bible is unreliable — both debaters affirm a reliable Bible — but it does cut against the *specific* claim of an in-hand **infallible** rule. Its force is the dilemma: infallible-autograph-we-lack (forfeits "the Bible is the rule") vs. infallible-copies-we-have (falsified by the Comma and Mark's ending). The Latter-day Saint payoff is that an authority *beyond* the static text — a living, Spirit-led church — is what the situation requires (cf. [[The Church Is the Pillar of Truth and Scripture Is Its Byproduct]]).
