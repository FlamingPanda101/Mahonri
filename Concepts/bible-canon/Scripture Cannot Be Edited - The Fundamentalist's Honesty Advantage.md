# Scripture Cannot Be Edited — The Fundamentalist's Honesty Advantage

## The Point

[[Sam Harris]] argues that the central danger of scripture is that it **cannot be revised**: the texts plainly endorse slavery, sex slavery, and brutal injunctions "for all time." Fundamentalists honestly read the plain sense and so hold an "asymmetric" advantage over liberal believers, who quietly import modern ethics from *outside* the text.

## Argument (steelman)

1. **The fundamentalist conflation.** Peterson sharpens Harris's target: fundamentalists claim not only that the book is God's literal word but that *their reading* of it is correct — "they make an assumption of their own omniscience and then pass that off onto God."[^1]

2. **Plain, unallegorical injunctions.** Some lines resist contextual softening: the Qur'anic remedy for theft is to cut off the thief's hand — "it's not an allegory... you have to indulge some tortured interpretive scheme to avoid the shocking fact."[^2]

3. **Slavery is condoned in both Testaments and the Qur'an.** "If you go to the books and try to figure out what the creator of the universe wants with respect to owning other people... he expects you to keep slaves and he's told you how to do it." ISIS's sex-slavery was "not an accident."[^3]

4. **The doors open only from the outside.** "The doors leading out of this kind of fundamentalism don't open from the inside; they get bashed open from the outside" — by humanism, secularism, and scientific rationality. This is why we no longer meet 14th-century Christians but do meet 14th-century-minded fundamentalists.[^4]

5. **The fundamentalist's honesty advantage.** "Fundamentalism always has an edge over more sophisticated theology" because the literal reading is straightforward; the liberal believer who imports outside ethics "found some of God's words unacceptable" and every fundamentalist "can sniff that out." Harris calls this "an asymmetric war." When it says stone the girl, "you know what stone means, you know what girl means — you're 90% there to an obvious atrocity."[^5]

6. **The charge against Peterson.** By making religion "palatable," Harris worries, one keeps people "shackled to these Iron Age philosophies" instead of having a 21st-century conversation.[^6]

## Best Quotes

> "Most of what's in the books is clearly not the best that humanity is capable of in the ethical domain... slavery is condoned in the Bible in both Testaments and in the Quran. There's no getting away from that."[^3]

> "The doors leading out of this kind of fundamentalism don't open from the inside; they get bashed open from the outside — and it's humanism and secularism and scientific rationality."[^4]
