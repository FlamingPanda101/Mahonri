# Protestant Disunity Is Evidence Against Perspicuity

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
[[Shiloh Lofgren]]'s third argument: if the Bible, stripped of a living teaching church, were a sufficient guide, the Reformation's return to the text should have produced unity. Instead it produced fracture — thousands of denominations disagreeing even on first-rank, salvation-bearing questions (baptism's efficacy; the nature of God). So the doctrine of **perspicuity** (Scripture is clear on what is necessary for salvation) is empirically false, contradicting Paul's repeated demand for one mind ([[1 Corinthians 1.17|1 Cor 1:10]]; [[Ephesians 4.4|Eph 4:5]] "one Lord, one faith, one baptism"; [[John 17.20-23|John 17]]).[^1]

## The Rebuttal

### 1. Baptism — Protestants cannot agree what it does — rate: strong
"Lutherans believe baptism regenerates and they baptize infants. Presbyterians do not believe baptism regenerates and they do baptize infants. Baptists do not believe baptism regenerates and they do not baptize infants. All affirm sola scriptura," yet each accuses the others of misreading the clear text.[^2] Lofgren adds that the *symbolic* Protestant view is a historical novelty: pre-Reformation traditions (Catholic, Orthodox, Coptic, Assyrian, Lutheran, Anglican) and "all early church fathers" read baptism as regenerative — citing Philip Schaff and David Bercot.[^3] **Steelman of [[Timothy J. Whitehead]]:** disagreement proves people *misuse* Scripture, not that Scripture is unclear ("cataracts don't dim the sun"); and "Satan sows tares in the same field," so many disputants are false brethren or undiscipled Christians who would repent when shown the truth. See [[Perspicuity Fails the Hard-Cases Test]].

### 2. The Trinity — even "the one thing we all agree on" fractures — rate: strong (striking data)
Lofgren cites Ligonier Ministries' *State of Theology* survey (founded by Calvinist R.C. Sproul): in 2020, **43% of U.S. evangelicals agreed "Jesus was a great teacher, but he was not God"**; in 2025, **43% agreed "the Holy Spirit is a force, not a personal being"** — "that is not the Trinity." 100% of those respondents also said the Bible is their highest authority.[^4] He adds Carl Rahner's observation that "it often looks like each theologian has his own private Trinity," and challenges the audience to ask five Trinitarians how three persons are one essence and "get different answers ... some stumbling into modalism or Arianism."[^5] **Steelman of Whitehead:** survey ignorance among nominal evangelicals is a catechesis failure, not proof the doctrine is unclear in Scripture; the creeds *normed by Scripture* settle it. *Reply:* if the doctrine claimed to be most central and most salvation-relevant is missed by ~half who hold the Bible as highest authority, perspicuity-for-salvation is doing little work without a teaching authority.

### 3. The thousands-of-denominations problem — rate: moderate-strong
Estimates "range from 19[,000] to 33,000" Protestant or Protestant-adjacent denominations; by contrast, Lofgren notes ~98% of the broader Restoration movement is within the main LDS Church, blunting the tu quoque.[^6] **Steelman of Whitehead:** denomination counts are inflated and conflate organizational with doctrinal division; unless Scripture is true and clear "you do not have a target to aim at to bring about unity," so disunity indicts interpreters, not the text. See [[No Final Interpreter - The Protestant Adjudication Problem]].

**Net assessment.** Whitehead's "the sun is clear even if eyes are diseased" reply is logically available — disagreement does not *strictly entail* the text's unclarity. But Lofgren's point bites on the *practical* claim of perspicuity-unto-salvation: a rule that "the unlearned, in due use of ordinary means," can follow to the saving essentials should not leave ~43% of Bible-supreme evangelicals denying the deity of Christ or the personhood of the Spirit. The Latter-day Saint conclusion is that the missing element is a living, authoritative church to adjudicate (cf. [[The Church Is the Pillar of Truth and Scripture Is Its Byproduct]]) — the same "guard rail" Trent Horn reaches in [[Perspicuity Fails the Hard-Cases Test]].
