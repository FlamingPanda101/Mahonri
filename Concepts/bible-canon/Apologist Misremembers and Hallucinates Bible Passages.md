# Apologist Misremembers and Hallucinates Bible Passages

## The Claim
[[Dan McClellan]] argues that Joshua HS, attempting to defend biblical slavery from memory, repeatedly misattributes citations, misreads texts, and invents passages that do not exist — demonstrating that "Joshua doesn't have the first clue what he's talking about."[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Misattributed citation (rated: strong).** Joshua cited the kidnapping law as "Deuteronomy 21:16"; it is actually [[Exodus 21.16|Exodus 21:16]].[^2]

2. **Misread clause (rated: strong).** Joshua claimed Exodus 21:16 made any *possessor* of a kidnapped person liable to death; the clause actually refers to the kidnapper "whether he has sold him or he has found in his hands."[^2]

3. **Hallucinated text (rated: strong).** Joshua's "Leviticus 24" command to treat an enslaved brother as a sojourner is, per McClellan, "all hallucinated" — no such passage exists.[^3] See [[Sojourner Passages Are Misread as Slavery Protections]].

4. **Forgotten content used as proof (rated: strong).** Joshua invoked [[Exodus 21.20-21|Exodus 21:20-21]] to prove abuse was punished, then admitted "I can't remember exactly what it said," not realizing the verse undermines his case.[^4]

5. **Wrong word-meaning (rated: strong; linguistic).** Joshua claimed "punished" in Exodus 21:20 means the death penalty; the verb is **naqam** ("be avenged"), with an unspecified penalty.[^5]

6. **Steelman.** Joshua's underlying instinct — that the Hebrew text is precise and worth quoting — is sound; McClellan's complaint is execution, not the impulse to cite. A defender could note that extemporaneous podcast recall is error-prone and not the same as a written brief. McClellan's reply is that the errors all run one direction (toward exonerating the text) and reflect not slips but a systematic lack of competence.[^1]

## Best Quotes

> "Joshua is seems to be going off of memory, does not have very clear or good notes in front of him, and is wildly misreading and misrepresenting what's actually in these passages."[^6]

> "Even in trying to make up lies about what the Bible says, he stumbles over his own ignorance because he doesn't have the first clue what he's talking about."[^4]
