# A Living Magisterium Settles What Sola Scriptura Cannot

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Trent Horn]] argues that the disagreement over whether Catholicism preaches a true gospel is itself **evidence against [[Sola Scriptura - Scripture Self-Attests Its Authority|sola scriptura]]** and for a "living teaching office or a magisterium capable of settling disputes definitively through authoritative, universal, and infallible teachings." [^1]

The argument turns on **perspicuity**. Many Protestants hold that Scripture clearly reveals the essential doctrines. Horn quotes the Westminster Confession: the things "necessary to be known, believed, and observed for salvation are so clearly propounded... that not only the learned, but the unlearned in a due use of the ordinary means may attain unto a sufficient understanding of them." But, Horn asks, "isn't it necessary for salvation to know if the largest Christian denomination in the world, Catholicism, preaches a true gospel or a false gospel?" — and on *that* question "even learned Protestants using ordinary means do not have agreement." [^2]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **A real, unresolved hard case.** The video itself documents Protestants publicly debating "whether Catholics are Christian or if they have a false gospel." If a doctrine this momentous cannot be settled by Scripture's alleged clarity, then perspicuity fails exactly where it is most needed — the dynamic of [[Perspicuity Fails the Hard-Cases Test]] and [[The Light Manifests Itself - Perspicuity of Scripture]]. [^2]
2. **The Westminster standard is self-undermining here.** Westminster says salvation-essentials are graspable by "the unlearned in a due use of the ordinary means," yet the *learned* cannot agree whether the world's largest church preaches the saving gospel — so either the question isn't essential (implausible) or the perspicuity claim is false. [^2]
3. **Only an infallible office can deliver closure.** Horn's positive proposal is a magisterium issuing "authoritative, universal, and infallible teachings" — the structural fix for the under-determination that [[Sola Scriptura Cannot Navigate the Heresy Minefield|sola scriptura cannot escape]]. He frames apologetic readiness via [[1 Peter 3.15|1 Peter 3:15]] ("always be ready to give a reason... with gentleness and reverence"). [^3]
4. **Steelman of the Protestant counter.** The Protestant replies that perspicuity was never meant to guarantee agreement on **every** disputed question — only on the *core* gospel needed for salvation; whether a *particular church* faithfully preaches it is a secondary judgment that Scripture's clarity does not promise to adjudicate. Moreover, appealing to a magisterium only relocates the problem: one must still privately interpret which church *is* the infallible one, so the regress is not actually closed. Horn's rejoinder is that the *identity of the true gospel* is precisely a salvation-essential by the Protestant's own logic, so it cannot be quarantined as "secondary"; and that the choice of an authoritative Church is a one-time act of historical/credibility judgment, not the open-ended, doctrine-by-doctrine private judgment sola scriptura requires. This is the same authority gap Latter-day Saint apologists press from the other direction — appealing to **living prophetic authority** rather than a magisterium, but agreeing with Horn that [[Sola Scriptura - Scripture Self-Attests Its Authority|bare Scripture cannot ground its own sufficient interpretation]].

## Best Quotes
> "Ironically, this provides evidence that God's authority structure for the church is not grounded in something like sola scriptura, but a living teaching office or a magisterium capable of settling disputes definitively through authoritative, universal, and infallible teachings." [^1]

> "But isn't it necessary for salvation to know if the largest Christian denomination in the world, Catholicism, preaches a true gospel or a false gospel? Even learned Protestants using ordinary means do not have agreement on this question." [^2]
