# Self-Authentication via Church Consensus Backs Into the Catholic Model

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
Modern defenders relocate self-authentication from the individual to the *church*: [[Robert Godfrey]] writes, "The self-authenticating character of the canon is demonstrated by the remarkable unanimity reached by the people of God on the canon," and [[Michael Krueger]] argues the canon's divine qualities are recognizable "not necessarily to the individual believer, but to the church" [^1]. [[Joe Heschmeyer]] argues this quietly **reconstructs the Catholic position** [^2].

## The Rebuttal

### 1. It is self-defeating relative to the doctrine's purpose — rate: strong
"The whole reason the reformers are creating this doctrine of self-authentication is to get rid of the need for the church to know the Bible." [[John Calvin]]'s own question was "how do we know which books are in the Bible *without the church*?" If you now answer "by the church's unanimous recognition," you have conceded the premise the Reformation was built to deny — and "that undermines the Reformation project in a pretty serious and pretty obvious way" [^2].

### 2. Appealing to church unanimity as proof of *self*-authentication is question-begging — rate: strong
Godfrey's move treats the church's consensus as "just further evidence that it's self-authenticating," which Heschmeyer calls "another bold gambit": the consensus is doing the authenticating work, so the canon is being known *through the church*, not through the text's self-evidence [^1].

### 3. Once you do this, you have the Catholic model "in a weird and confusing way" — rate: strong
When Krueger says the canon's qualities are recognizable by the *Spirit-guided church*, "you've just backed into the Catholic model, but in a really weird and confusing kind of way... basically we still know it through the church" [^3]. **Steelman of the Protestant:** the Reformed claim is that the church *recognizes* an intrinsic quality the books already possess, whereas (they allege) Catholicism makes the church the *cause* of canonicity. Heschmeyer answers that this rests on a strawman of the Catholic view — see [[Krugers Strawman of the Catholic View of the Canon]] and the precise distinction in [[Thermometer vs Thermostat - The Church Declares the Canon]].

## Best Quotes
> "The self-authenticating character of the canon is demonstrated by the remarkable unanimity reached by the people of God on the canon." (Godfrey, quoted) [^1]

> "The whole reason the reformers are creating this doctrine of self authentication is to get rid of the need for the church to know the Bible... if you admit that you have to have the Catholic church to know which books are in the Bible, that undermines the Reformation project." [^2]

> "Once you do that, you've just backed into the Catholic model, but in a really weird and confusing kind of way... basically we still know it through the church." [^3]
