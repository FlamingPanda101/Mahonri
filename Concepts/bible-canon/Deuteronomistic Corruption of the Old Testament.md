# Deuteronomistic Corruption of the Old Testament

## The Claim
The dominant religion of Jesus's day was **Deuteronomism** (= Phariseeism) — the prophet-killing school that "found" Deuteronomy in the defiled temple and rewrote the canon to suppress messianic prophecy. This is a recurring LDS framing of Old Testament textual corruption (cf. the Book of Mormon's "plain and precious things" removed).

## Supporting Evidence
- "The dominant religion of the day when Christ was there was... Deuteronomism. That's Phariseeism" — the same school that killed Isaiah, Zechariah, Jeremiah, Uriah, and drove off Lehi *(speaker's thesis)* [^1].
- Deuteronomy was "found" (2 Kings 22, under Josiah) in the blood-soaked temple of Solomon, after which Jerusalem was promptly destroyed by [[Nebuchadnezzar]] *(speaker's argument)* [^2].
- The hand-washing laws the Pharisees press on Jesus derive from this Deuteronomistic legal layer — so his rebuke targets the corrupt source, not just the critics *(argument)* [^3].

## Best Quote
> "This defiled, blood-soaked temple is the temple in which they go, 'Hey, look, we found a new book and let's rewrite all the old books' — and they write Deuteronomy." — [[Jonah (Plain and Precious Publishing)|Jonah]] [^2]
