# NDE Parallels in the Bible

## The Point
Moody finds that although the Bible says relatively little about the precise events of dying — only two Old Testament passages (Isaiah 26:19; Daniel 12:2) speak unequivocally of life after death, and both stress bodily resurrection and compare death to sleep — several New Testament passages echo modern near-death testimony [^1]. The strongest parallels cluster around the apostle [[Paul]]: his Damascus-road encounter with a personal "being of light," and his teaching on the "spiritual body" [^2]. Moody is careful to note real differences as well, so the parallel is suggestive rather than identical [^3].

## Evidence
1. **The valley of the shadow and the light of the world.** Some subjects spontaneously reached for biblical language — one identified the dark passage at death as the "valley of the shadow of death," and two identified the light they met as Christ on the basis of "I am the light of the world" [^1].
2. **Paul's encounter (Acts 26:13-26).** A light "above the brightness of the sun" surrounds Paul; a personal voice asks a question and issues instructions; when Paul reports it he is mocked as "mad." Like the NDE [[NDE - The Being of Light|being of light]], the being has personality and "voice" though no physical form is seen [^2].
3. **A noted difference.** Paul was *not* near death, and he was *blinded* for three days — whereas NDE subjects report the light, however brilliant, never blinds them [^3].
4. **The spiritual body (1 Corinthians 15:35-52).** Paul answers "with what body do they come?" by distinguishing the natural body from a "spiritual body" — immaterial, unlimited, strong and beautiful where the physical was weak. This matches accounts in which the spiritual body seemed whole though the physical body was mutilated, and of "no particular age," i.e. not limited by time [^4].
5. **Out of the body (2 Corinthians 12:2-4).** Paul reports being "caught up to the third heaven... whether in the body, or out of the body, I cannot tell," hearing "unspeakable words" — out-of-body uncertainty plus ineffability in a single passage (see [[2 Corinthians 12.2]]) [^4].

## Best Quotes
> "There are also celestial bodies, and bodies terrestrial... It is sown a natural body, it is raised a spiritual body." [^4]

> "whether in the body, or out of the body, I cannot tell: God knoweth" [^4]

## LDS Connection
Paul's "celestial" and "terrestrial" bodies (1 Cor 15:40) supply the very vocabulary of the LDS degrees of glory (D&C 76), and his out-of-body rapture to "paradise" fits the Restoration's insistence that the spirit is a real, sentient entity that can be "caught up" to other realms — the visionary mode Joseph Smith and others continued. The NDE testimony of a body restored "whole and complete" and beautiful harmonizes with the LDS doctrine of a perfected resurrected body.
