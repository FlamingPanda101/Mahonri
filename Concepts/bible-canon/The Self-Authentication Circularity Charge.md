# The Self-Authentication Circularity Charge

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
[[Trent Horn]] charges that [[Anthony Rogers]]' core epistemology — "Scripture is self-authenticating; it's inspired because it (in effect) says so" — is **viciously circular**, the same defense Muslims give for the Quran and Latter-day Saints give for the Book of Mormon. Worse, it is *falsified* by history: if Scripture self-authenticated, the early church would not have doubted half a dozen NT books for centuries. So self-authentication cannot ground the canon.[^1]

## The Rebuttal

### 1. "Inspired because it says so" parallels the Quran/Book of Mormon — rate: strong rhetorically, contested philosophically
Horn: "I feel like I'm debating Jake, our Muslim debater ... 'it's inspired because it says so' — that's what people will defend the Quran, the Book of Mormon."[^2] **Steelman of Rogers:** Rogers distinguishes *circular* from *self-evidencing*: "the sun shining and you seeing it is not circular — it's self-evidencing, just like logic is self-evidencing." All ultimate authorities are self-referential without vicious circularity; an *ultimate* norm cannot be validated by a *higher* norm or it would not be ultimate. See [[Scripture Is Self-Authenticating Because God Is Self-Contained]]. Rogers further argues the Quran's god is *not self-contained* (swears by created things — sun, fig, etc.), so its book *cannot* speak self-attestingly, unlike the aseity-possessing triune God.[^3]

### 2. The historical defeater: early doubts about NT books — rate: strong
Horn's positive evidence: serious sustained doubts about 2 Peter, James, 2–3 John, Revelation; Revelation absent from canon lists of Gregory of Nazianzus and Cyril of Jerusalem; [[Martin Luther]] nearly threw James "into the fire."[^1] If books were genuinely self-authenticating, this disagreement is inexplicable, whereas the universally-recognized self-evident truths (God exists, Jesus is Messiah, God is triune) were *not* disputed.[^4] **Steelman of Rogers:** Rogers (via Nemesius of Emesa) argues self-authentication is a property of inspiration that the *regenerate* perceive by the Spirit's work — so non-perception by some, or delayed corporate recognition, no more refutes it than a blind man refutes the sun. He concedes minor books (Philemon, 3 John) don't conspicuously "scream" inspiration.

### 3. The canon doesn't "coalesce" until the 4th century — rate: strong
Horn: the canon "would not become coalesced until the councils of Carthage and Hippo in the 4th century."[^4] This timeline pressures any claim that the *list* was self-evident. **Steelman of Rogers:** recognition-over-time is *ministerial discernment* of an inspired reality, not the *creation* of the canon; see [[The Internal-Seams Argument for the Canon]] and the "we derive the list from the books" move.

## Best Quotes
> "This circular reasoning — well, it's inspired because it says so — that's what people will defend the Quran, they'll defend the Book of Mormon."[^2]

> "If scripture were self-authenticating, how come there were doubts about many of the books of scripture? How come books like Revelation don't appear in some canon list?"[^1]

> [Rogers] "You could call it circular, but ... the sun shining and you seeing it is not circular. It's self-evidencing. Just like logic is self-evidencing."[^5]

> [Rogers] "The God of Islam and Mormonism can't speak with self-authenticating authority because the God of Islam and Mormonism is not self-contained."[^3]
