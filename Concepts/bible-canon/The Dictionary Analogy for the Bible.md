# The Dictionary Analogy for the Bible

## The Claim
[[Dan McClellan]] illustrates "there is no such thing as *the* Bible" with a parallel: "there's no such thing as *the* dictionary. There are dictionaries, but there's no *the* dictionary."[^1] People speak about "the dictionary" as if "we have all agreed on one universally authoritative, consistent dictionary" — but Merriam-Webster, Johnson, and Oxford have *not* "agreed on everything that's in all of them."[^2] In the same way, "the Bible" names a category of curated reference works, not one agreed-upon object. See [[There Is No Such Thing as The Bible]].

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. The analogy isolates the equivocation — rate: strong
The analogy targets a real linguistic move: the definite article smuggles in an assumption of singular, settled authority that does not exist. Dictionaries differ in inclusion (which words/senses), in edition, and in editorial philosophy — exactly the three axes (canon, manuscript, translation) McClellan applies to Bibles in [[Three Judgments Stand Between You and a Bible]]. **Steelman:** a critic could note the analogy is imperfect — dictionaries are descriptive and continuously revised by design, whereas a confessional Bible claims a fixed divine origin, so "the Bible" might be more like "the Constitution" (one authoritative document with disputed interpretations) than "the dictionary." McClellan's rejoinder is that even the *text* of the Bible (manuscripts) and its *boundaries* (canon) are disputed, unlike a single ratified constitution — so the dictionary analogy holds at the level of the object's existence.

### 2. "Big dictionary energy" — the authority is assumed, not demonstrated — rate: moderate
McClellan and [[Dan Beecher]] joke about "big dictionary energy," the cultural posture that treats a reference as self-evidently authoritative.[^2] The serious point: authority for any given dictionary (or Bible) is *conferred by a community*, not intrinsic to the object. This connects to the vault's canon-authority notes (see [[The Canon Question Is Logically Prior to Canon Authority]]).

## Best Quotes
> "There's no such thing as the dictionary. There are dictionaries, but there's no *the* dictionary. People speak about it as if we have all agreed on one universally authoritative, consistent dictionary. And that's just not true."[^1]

> "Just like there's no such thing as the dictionary, there's no such thing as the Bible."[^2]
