# John Knew the Synoptics

## The Claim
The Gospel of John **knew and used** the Synoptic Gospels (Mark, Matthew, Luke). The interesting question is not *whether* but *why*: if John knew them, **why did he change them** the way he did, and what did he add?

## Supporting Evidence

1. **John looks structurally like the earlier gospels.** It "starts with a baptism, ends with an empty tomb" — the broad shape of the Synoptic story — which is "in part why it looks kind of so similar to them." *(Moderate; structural similarity is consistent with dependence though not proof of it.)* [^1]
2. **John borrows their literary techniques.** John takes the symbolic narrative device used occasionally in Mark (e.g. the temple-cleansing symbolizing the temple's destruction) and runs with it across the whole gospel — see [[John's Pervasive Symbolism]]. Borrowing a technique implies reading the source. *(Moderate-strong; technique transfer.)* [^2]
3. **Recent monographs make the case in detail.** [[Hugo Mendez]] cites [[James Barker]]'s *Writing and Rewriting the Gospels* and [[Mark Goodacre]]'s forthcoming *The Fourth Synoptic Gospel* as recent works demonstrating John's use of the Synoptics — a reversal of earlier skepticism. *(Strong; appeals to focused peer scholarship.)* [^3]
4. **It reframes the whole project.** Méndez's distinctive contribution is to take dependence as given and press the *why* — what theological vision drives John's changes and additions. *(Context; this is the book's organizing question.)* [^4]

**Steelman of the opposing view.** For much of the 20th century the consensus (following P. Gardner-Smith and Bultmann) was that John was **independent** of the Synoptics, drawing on separate traditions — which would explain the many divergences (different chronology, no exorcisms, no parables, a different Last Supper). The similarities can be attributed to shared oral tradition rather than literary dependence; Méndez concedes "a lot of previous generations of scholars were skeptical" of the dependence he now argues.

## Best Quotes
> "The Gospel of John knows the Gospels of Mark, Matthew, and Luke. I think it's read some of these earlier accounts of the life of Jesus, which is in part why it looks kind of so similar to them." — [[Hugo Mendez]] [^1]

> "James Barker's book has argued that John knew the synoptics. Mark Goodacre's book is coming out... The Fourth Synoptic Gospel, that will make this argument." [^3]

> "If John knows the synoptics, which I argue he did, then why does he want to change them in the way that he does? What does he add to them that's different?" [^4]
