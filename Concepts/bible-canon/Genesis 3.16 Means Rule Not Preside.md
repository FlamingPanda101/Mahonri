# Genesis 3.16 Means Rule Not Preside

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Spencer W. Kimball]] taught that [[Genesis 3.16|Genesis 3:16]] — "he shall rule over thee" — was a
mistranslation and should read "preside." [[Dan McClellan]] rejects this: the Hebrew verb is
*mashal*, "to rule, dominate, have dominion," and "preside is not an appropriate translation." It
is an "egalitarian-oid" softening that Latter-day Saints use ("no, no, I don't rule, I preside"), but
the text "pretty clearly says *mashal*, to rule — and it's an ideology for the patriarchy."[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. The lexical case — rate: strong
The verbal root *mashal* means "have dominion, rule, dominate."[^1] "Preside" imports a modern,
egalitarian register foreign to the Hebrew; McClellan calls it "an attempt at egalitarian-oid
language."[^2] This is a clean, verifiable point of Hebrew lexicography, not an interpretive
preference.

### 2. The verse is an etiology for patriarchy — rate: strong
Genesis 3 is "an ideology for all of the curses": Adam must labor by the sweat of his brow, Eve
gets painful childbirth and subordination to her husband, the snake gets human hatred. "It's an
ideology for how things got to be the way they were in the middle of the first millennium BCE" —
a "highly, highly patriarchal culture" explaining its own gender order.[^3] Reading "preside" into
it erases that historical-cultural distance.

### 3. It exemplifies fitting the Bible to modern sensibilities — rate: strong (framing)
[[Benjamin Park]]: this shows "both the centrality of the Bible… but trying to fit that Bible to
modern sensibilities" rather than "acknowledging the historical and cultural distance between us
and the ancient Israelites."[^4] The move is the same pattern catalogued in
[[We Negotiate With the Text - It Does Not Speak for Itself]] and the Proverbs "rod"/shepherd's-crook
reinterpretation. **Steelman of the LDS reading:** a believer may grant *mashal* means "rule"
descriptively yet argue the *prescriptive* ideal God intends for marriage is non-domineering
("preside"); McClellan's point is narrower and holds — that is a theological *re-reading*, not what
the verse *says*.

## Best Quotes
> "The verbal root there is *mashal*, which means have dominion, rule, dominate… preside is not an appropriate translation."[^1]

> "It pretty clearly says *mashal*, to rule, and it's an ideology for the patriarchy."[^1]

> "What's Eve's curse? Oh, she's going to have painful childbirth and she's going to be subordinated to her husband. That's the patriarchy."[^3]
