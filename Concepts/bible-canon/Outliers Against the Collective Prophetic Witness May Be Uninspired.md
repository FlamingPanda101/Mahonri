#type/claim

# Outliers Against the Collective Prophetic Witness May Be Uninspired

## The Claim
Because Latter-day Saints hold an **open canon** and the witness of "God's prophets throughout time," they possess a hermeneutical filter unavailable to inerrantists: any biblical passage that "seem[s] out of character with the collective prophetic witness" can be identified as an **outlier** that "we may be justified in thinking... [is] not inspired" [^1]. The Bible remains "highly reliable" but is not treated as uniformly inerrant [^2].

## Supporting Evidence
1. **The method is comparative, not arbitrary.** The criterion is internal consistency across a large prophetic corpus, not the reader's preference. The narrator explicitly forecloses cafeteria reading: "I'm not saying that each person has the right and authority to reject any of the LDS canon because they don't like it" [^2]. Rating: **conceptually sound**; its strength depends entirely on having a sufficiently rich, mutually-reinforcing canon to define the baseline — which the LDS extra-biblical canon supplies.
2. **Worked test case — women's silence.** "Let your woman keep silence in the churches" ([[1 Corinthians 14.34-35|1 Cor 14:34-35]]) is judged an outlier because it conflicts with attested NT female missionaries and with women who pray and prophesy ([[1 Corinthians 11.5|1 Cor 11:5]]), and with universal LDS practice of women teaching and speaking in meetings and general conference [^3]. Rating: **strong as an illustration** — the tension between 1 Cor 14:34-35 and 1 Cor 11:5 is acknowledged across the scholarly spectrum.
3. **Mutual reinforcement as the positive principle.** "There are major themes and ideas that mutually reinforce one another" [^2] — the canon is treated like a network where consensus carries weight and isolated nodes are suspect. Rating: **defensible** as a coherentist epistemology of scripture.
4. **Steelman the opposing view.** The inerrantist's strongest counter: an "outlier" filter risks circularity — the modern church's current values (e.g., women speaking) become the very ruler used to excise inconvenient ancient texts, smuggling cultural change in as revelation. A critic could argue the LDS position here is functionally indistinguishable from liberal Protestant "trajectory hermeneutics" it elsewhere critiques. The note's defense — that the filter is the *prophetic collective* under continuing revelation, not private taste — answers the *who decides* question only if one already grants LDS prophetic authority. Honest tension: the method is powerful precisely to the degree the extra-biblical canon is granted as authoritative.

## Best Quotes
> "We can identify outliers that seem out of character with the collective prophetic witness and recognize that we may be justified in thinking such outliers are not inspired." [^1]

> "Does God really want women to keep silent in the churches? If that's true, why were there female missionaries in the New Testament?" [^3]

> "We don't have to defend every outlier in scripture as if it was inerrantly spoken by God himself." [^2]
