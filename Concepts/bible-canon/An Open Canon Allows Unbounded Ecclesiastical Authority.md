# An Open Canon Allows Unbounded Ecclesiastical Authority

## The Point
[[Thomas A. Wayment]]'s closing reflection: because a canon is fundamentally "a function of ecclesiastical authority," an **open** canon "allows for boundless or unlimited ecclesiastical authority." Both the open and the closed canon are instruments of power. For [[Latter-day Saints]], reopening the canon relocates authority from the traditional book-list to living apostles and prophets — which is both the strength and the responsibility of the model. This is presented honestly as a double-edged claim, not a triumphalist one.

## Evidence

### 1. Closed canon as a weapon; open canon as a counter-weapon
Wayment: "For some, the closed canon functioned as a weapon to push other Christians out of the Church. For others, an open canon functioned similarly to declare that other Christians were closed off from the Holy Spirit." Both serve "larger structures of authority." [^1]

### 2. The balance problem is perennial
"With too many people offering revelations, the message of the faith quickly spirals out of control and loses its unique identity, and with too strict control over revelation, the institution suppresses the voice of the Holy Spirit." This balance "is as true of early Christianity as it is true today." [^2]

### 3. LDS reopening relocates authority
"In reopening the canon, Latter-day Saints have shifted authority away from those who support the traditional canon and relocated that authority in modern Latter-day Saint apostles and prophets." An open canon "allows for boundless or unlimited ecclesiastical authority … similar to Roman Catholicism and other forms of Christianity with a strong hierarchical system of governance." [^3]

### 4. The Hiram Page test case
The early restoration faced precisely this crisis: Hiram Page circulated revelations from a seer-stone, and the response (D&C 28) was to channel revelatory authority to "my servant Joseph Smith, Jun., for he receiveth them even as Moses." Openness to revelation created a "potential crisis of authority" resolved by centralizing the prophetic office. [^4]

## Best Quotes
> "Given that a canon is a function of ecclesiastical authority, an open canon allows for boundless or unlimited ecclesiastical authority." [^3]

> "the closed canon functioned as a weapon to push other Christians out of the Church. For others, an open canon functioned similarly to declare that other Christians were closed off from the Holy Spirit." [^1]

## LDS Connection
This is itself the LDS application: the open canon is not framed as obviously safer than the closed canon, but as a different distribution of authority that must "maintain an absolute position of authority to reveal the word of God." It parallels [[Montanus and the Overcorrection Against Prophecy|the ancient charismatic/control pendulum]] and the [[D&C 26|Hiram Page resolution]]. [^3]
