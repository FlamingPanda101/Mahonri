#type/claim

# Gospel Imagery Is Too Specific to Be Mere Metaphor for the Ineffable

## The Claim (skeptical)
[[Dinesh D'Souza]] defends the bizarre imagery of the Book of Revelation — the seven-headed dragon, the prostitute — by appeal to *ineffability*: when an ancient seer is shown something beyond his experience (D'Souza floats a thermonuclear blast), "when words and images fail, metaphor is the actual conduit to truth." He likens it to being asked to describe a mushroom's taste to someone who has never eaten one: language simply cannot render it.[^1]

[[Alex OConnor|Alex O'Connor]]'s rebuttal grants the *premise* but denies the *inference*. Genuine ineffability would produce vague, awe-saturated approximations — "the gods of fire descending upon man with their flaming swords." It would **not** produce highly specific, culturally loaded symbols. "Where's he going to get the seven-headed dragon and the prostitute from?" A prostitute is a concrete social role; a seven-headed dragon is a concrete mythological figure. These are not the linguistic *failures* of a man grasping at the indescribable — they are the borrowed furniture of an existing symbolic world (apocalyptic Judaism, Near-Eastern myth). The ineffability defense thus over-explains: it can absorb *any* content, which is precisely why it explains nothing.[^2]

## Supporting Evidence
1. **The mismatch between "ineffable" and "specific."** O'Connor concedes he would accept the accommodation move for diffuse imagery ("gods of fire... flaming swords") — the very kind of language a stunned observer might reach for. The objection bites only because Revelation's imagery is *not* diffuse; it is taxonomically precise (a dragon with a determinate number of heads, a named figure-type). Specificity is evidence of a cultural source, not of a vision that defeated language.[^2]
2. **The mushroom analogy proves too little.** D'Souza is right that one cannot convey a mushroom's taste in words — but a person who *had* never tasted a mushroom would not spontaneously invent a "seven-headed" flavor. Ineffability yields *silence or vagueness*, not invented detail. The analogy shows language is limited; it does not license reading arbitrary specific symbols as transparent windows onto the unknowable.[^1]
3. **It collapses into unfalsifiability.** If any image, however specific, can be retro-justified as "the conduit metaphor uses when words fail," then no image could ever count against the reading. This connects to the broader complaint that [[Christian Metaphorical Reading Is Unfalsifiable Retreat|the metaphorical reading becomes unconstrained]] — Sam Harris's "tennis without the net" objection in [[Metaphorical Truth and Narrative Reading]].

## Apologetic Engagement
The accommodation tradition (see [[Divine Accommodation Means Revelation Meets Humans in Their Context]]) supplies a stronger version of D'Souza's case than he states. The apologetic reply is that apocalyptic imagery is not a *failed* attempt to describe the ineffable but a *deliberate, conventional genre* — the seer reaches for the stock vocabulary of his tradition (Daniel's beasts, the chaos-dragon of Hebrew cosmology) precisely *because* it is a shared symbolic language, not because he is privately groping for words. On this reading the specificity is not embarrassing but expected: a first-century apocalyptist *would* render judgment as a beast and Rome as a "great harlot," because that is the idiom in which his audience reads cosmic meaning. The imagery is culturally sourced, just as O'Connor says — but that cultural sourcing is a feature of inspired communication-in-context, not evidence of fabrication.

From a Latter-day Saint vantage this is unembarrassing in a further way: [[Divine Accommodation Means Revelation Meets Humans in Their Context|revelation is "God's word in human words"]], adapted to "their language, unto their understanding" (2 Ne. 31:3). The seer's symbols are *his*; the vision behind them is God's. The honest cost the apologist must pay, though, is the one O'Connor names: once imagery is conceded to be culturally constructed conventional symbol rather than transparent report, the text loses its power to *settle* disputed metaphysical specifics (how many heads, which beast = which empire) — which is why this concedes ground to [[Christian Metaphorical Reading Is Unfalsifiable Retreat]] and must be paired with independent reasons for trusting the tradition.
