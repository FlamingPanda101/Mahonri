# Presuppositional Self-Authentication Is Unbiblical Human Philosophy

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
The presuppositional framework (in which Heschmeyer locates [[Michael Krueger]] and many modern Reformed apologists) holds that **because Scripture is the word of God, you cannot appeal to anything outside it to prove it** — to do so would set some authority above God's word. Therefore the canon *must* be self-authenticating; one starts from Scripture as the unquestionable presupposition [^1].

## The Rebuttal

### 1. It is not the biblical model — rate: strong
"One of the problems with that is it's not the biblical model at all. This is just human philosophy, and it's human philosophy that doesn't really work." Scripture nowhere instructs believers to adopt this self-grounding circle as the path to recognizing the canon [^1]. See [[The Circularity Charge Against Self-Attesting Scripture]]. **Steelman:** presuppositionalists (Van Til, Bahnsen) argue every worldview rests on ultimate, self-attesting first principles, so demanding the Christian *not* do so privileges autonomous reason — a real philosophical claim, not mere evasion. Heschmeyer does not engage this transcendental argument in depth; his critique targets its *fruitfulness*, not its formal coherence.

### 2. It arbitrarily licenses feelings but forbids reason — rate: strong
The framework posits "a lumina to scripture that isn't proved by any of its phenomena" — an internal light not demonstrated by any external evidence. So "anything you would point to as evidence... this view would say, well, don't do that, because you're putting human reason above scripture." Yet it still appeals to interior feelings/conviction. Heschmeyer: "for some reason you can appeal to feelings, but you can't appeal to reason." This asymmetry is unprincipled [^2].

### 3. It cannot defend the very thing it sets out to defend — rate: strong
"It just strikes me as a failed intellectual venture... an intellectual wrong turn that doesn't bear a lot of fruit, and it is sort of floundering without being able to defend the very thing it is trying to set out to defend" — namely, a secure, recognizable canon. You "just have to irrationally accept all of this stuff is true" [^3].

## Best Quotes
> "It's not the biblical model at all. This is just human philosophy and it's human philosophy that doesn't really work." [^1]

> "There's a lumina to scripture that isn't proved by any of its phenomena, and this is completely unworkable... you're putting human reason above scripture." [^2]

> "For some reason you can appeal to feelings, but you can't appeal to reason... it just strikes me as a failed intellectual venture." [^3]
