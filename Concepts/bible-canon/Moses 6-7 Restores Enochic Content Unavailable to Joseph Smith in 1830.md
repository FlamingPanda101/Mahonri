# Moses 6-7 Restores Enochic Content Unavailable to Joseph Smith in 1830

#type/claim

## The Claim

[[Moses 6]]–[[Moses 7]] (Book of Moses, 1830) is the most extensive Enoch material in any Christian or Jewish scripture outside [[1 Enoch]] itself — covering a pre-existent Son of Man, the translated city of Zion, and a weeping God. Arnold argues this maps onto **ancient Enochic content that was not available to Joseph Smith as a structured theological system in 1830**: the only English translation (Laurence, 1821) was obscure, and the scholarship needed to recognize these parallels is 20th-century. The "Joseph synthesized his environment" explanation runs into "a specific historical wall."

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. The Scope of the Moses Enoch Material
Moses 6 covers Enoch's call, ministry, and preaching; Moses 7 records his cosmic vision — a pre-existent Son of Man figure, the establishment of the city of **Zion** (taken up bodily to heaven), and Enoch seeing **God weep** over his children's suffering.[^1] "This is, by a significant margin, the most extensive Enoch material in any Christian or Jewish scripture outside of First Enoch itself."[^1]

### 2. The 1821 Translation Was Too Obscure
The first English translation of 1 Enoch was published by Richard Laurence in 1821 — nine years before Moses 6–7 — but it was "a scholarly text with limited circulation, published only in London with minimal theological apparatus... not the kind of thing circulating in rural upstate New York in the 1820s."[^2]

**Evidence rating: secondary** — the limited-circulation claim is a historical judgment; mere chronological priority of Laurence's text is conceded.

### 3. The Synthesizing Scholarship Is 20th-Century
> "The scholarly literature that would allow someone to understand the Enochic tradition deeply enough to synthesize Moses chapter 6 and 7 from it — the analysis identifying the Son of Man traditions, the Zion theology, and the weeping God as coherent ancient content with specific textual parallels — wasn't developed until the 20th century, decades after Joseph Smith was dead."[^3]

The overlap "was documented by scholars who were not looking for it and had no stake in where it led for the church."[^3] The LDS claim: Joseph Smith **restored** the content before the world became aware of its real existence.[^4]

### 4. The Weeping God vs. Divine Impassibility
The grieving, emotionally present God of Moses 7 is "dramatically different from the impassible God of classic Greek-influenced Christian theology, the God who by definition cannot suffer or feel," yet consistent with the Enochic tradition and the LDS Heavenly Father.[^4] See [[Divine Impassibility Is Classical-Theist Heresy]].
