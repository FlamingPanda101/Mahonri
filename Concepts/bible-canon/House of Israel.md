# House of Israel

The "House of Israel" is the covenant people descended from the patriarch Jacob (renamed Israel), traditionally organized into the twelve tribes. The phrase carries both an ethnic sense (the literal lineage) and a covenantal sense (those gathered into the Abrahamic covenant), and in Latter-day Saint usage it extends to the gathering and restoration of Israel in the last days.

## Relevance

The term appears in the vault's discussion of Genesis 9 and the [[curse of Canaan]], where the division-of-labor reading distinguishes the covenant line of the House of Israel from the line of Canaan. It is foundational vocabulary for the covenant-lineage themes that run through the Book of Abraham and Restoration scripture.
