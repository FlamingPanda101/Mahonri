# The Circularity Charge Against Self-Attesting Scripture

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
Kelly argues scripture self-attests: "scripture claims to be authority, scripture claims to be inspired."[^1] Hayden's rebuttal: this is **circular reasoning / begging the question** — "pointing to the text to validate the text," i.e. assuming scripture is authoritative in order to prove scripture is authoritative.[^2]

## The Rebuttal

### 1. Self-attestation cannot establish authority — rate: strong
A claim of authority is not evidence *of* authority; every purported scripture (Qur'an, Book of Mormon) makes the same self-claim, so self-attestation cannot discriminate true from false canon.[^2] **Scholarly context:** Protestant presuppositionalists (Van Til, and reformed Baptists Hayden references) *grant* the circularity and defend it as a virtuous "ultimate-authority" circularity — Hayden notes "the presuppositional Calvinist reformed Baptists I know simply bite the bullet and say, 'Yeah, it is circular.'"[^3] So the charge is conceded by one whole wing of Protestantism.

### 2. Kelly's own answer is *not* presuppositional — rate: revealing
Kelly refuses the circle: "I don't believe in circular reasoning. I don't do presuppositional apologetics. I believe we should examine and test things."[^4] **Tension exposed:** in cross-examination Kelly then describes coming to scripture's authority via *external historical evidence* — investigating whether Jesus lived, was crucified, and rose: "based on what I found outside of Christian influence... I looked to the Bible as the inspired word of God."[^5] Hayden seizes this: "So you would take the evidences that pointed you to the authority of the Bible... you came to the conclusion based on other evidences that the Bible's the ultimate authority."[^6] If external evidence grounds the Bible's authority, then the Bible is **not** the *ultimate* authority — something prior (historical reasoning) is. This is the trap. See [[Authorization - Divine Designation Required for an Infallible Canon]].

### 3. "Can something be the ultimate authority if something else has to tell us what it is first?" — rate: strong
Hayden's repeated cross-examination question presses the same lever from the canon side: if humans must *identify, preserve, translate, interpret, and delimit* the text, the text is not self-standing as ultimate.[^7] **Steelman of Kelly:** evidence can *occasion* belief (a ladder kicked away) without being a higher *authority* — natural reason may lead one to recognize God's word without outranking it, much as the Samaritans first believed because of the woman's testimony but then "because of his own word." Hayden does not address this distinction; Kelly gestures at it ("when I looked at the Bible, I looked to it as inspired") but does not develop it crisply, so the point lands against him in the exchange.

## Best Quotes
> "This is classic circular reasoning, pointing to the text to validate the text. And that's a very common principle from Protestants. They say scripture is self-affirming."[^2]

> "The presuppositional Calvinist reformed Baptists that I know simply bite the bullet and they say, 'Yeah, it is circular.'"[^3]

> "I don't believe in circular reasoning. I don't do presuppositional apologetics. I believe that we should examine and test things, be open-minded... Scripture claims to be authority. Scripture claims to be inspired." (Kelly)[^4]

> "Can something be the ultimate authority if something else has to tell us what it is first?"[^7]
