# One Member of a Kind Is Not the Only Member — The Black Swan Reply

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
[[Gavin Ortlund]]'s syllogism is: (1) the Bible is an infallible rule; (2) there is no *other* infallible rule; (3) therefore the Bible is the *only* infallible rule.[^1] [[Trent Horn]]'s central rebuttal targets premise 2 as a **fallacious appeal to ignorance**. Ortlund has not *shown* other infallible rules do not exist — at most that they "have not been proven to exist," which licenses only *agnosticism*, not the universal negative the conclusion requires. The underlying error: **assuming one member of a kind is the only member of that kind.**[^2]

Horn's illustration: "White is one color of swans. People thought it was the only color until black swans were discovered." Likewise "atheists are correct the natural world is one existing thing, but they're incorrect to assume it's the only existing thing." So just as the atheist's inference "naturalism is true because only the natural world has been proven" fails, "Protestants should reject sola scriptura and be open to other infallible rules of faith existing."[^3] In closing, Horn drives the burden home: Ortlund "is making a claim... that scripture is the only infallible rule and there are no others — just like atheists say the natural world is the only existing thing... you have the burden of defending that, and Gavin simply didn't do that tonight."[^4]

## The Rebuttal

### 1. The logical asymmetry is real — rate: strong
"No other X has been proven" entails "there are no other X" *only* if one has surveyed the whole domain. For infallible rules of faith, no such exhaustive survey exists, so the move from "unproven" to "nonexistent" is an appeal to ignorance. This is the same burden-of-proof structure the vault applies to atheism in [[Atheism Is a Worldview With the Burden of Proof]] — here turned, pointedly, *against the Protestant* by analogizing sola scriptura to naturalism.

### 2. The arguments "implode on themselves" — rate: moderate, contested
Horn presses a parallel self-reference: "to know what science is you need a reliable non-scientific rule of knowledge like philosophy; to know what scripture is you need an infallible non-scriptural rule of faith like sacred tradition."[^5] If identifying the *first* member of the kind requires a *second*, then the claim "there is only one" is self-undermining. **Steelman (Ortlund's reply):** Scripture is *not* relevantly like a white swan or one science among many — it is **ontologically unique** as the only God-breathed speech of God, a uniqueness of *kind*, not a contingent first-of-several. Horn's swan analogy assumes Scripture is one homogeneous instance of a populated category, whereas Ortlund's whole case is that its nature is *sui generis* (see [[The Word of God Fallacy - The Load-Bearing Premise Is Unproven]]). Ortlund: "the scripture is the only inspired word of God — it's not like a white swan either."[^6]

### 3. Ortlund's empirical rejoinder — rate: framing
Ortlund concedes he offers "an empirical argument," not a proof-text: sola scriptura is "the cumulative entailment" of Scripture's nature *plus* its role *plus* the failure of every proposed alternative — and "it's not my burden to prove a universal negative; let another candidate come forward."[^7] The debate thus reduces to *who bears the burden*: Horn says the one asserting "only one" must prove it; Ortlund says the one positing a *second* infallible rule that is *not* God-breathed must justify elevating it to parity with God's speech.

## LDS relevance
The black-swan logic is a **two-edged sword Latter-day Saints can wield against both** Protestants *and* Catholics. Against the Protestant it reopens the canon as in principle un-closeable (an open canon is exactly the "other member of the kind" — see [[An Open Canon Allows Unbounded Ecclesiastical Authority]] and [[God Himself Not a Book Is the Ultimate Authority]]). But it also rebounds on Horn: if "one infallible rule does not mean only one," then the *Catholic* cannot a priori exclude *continuing revelation through living prophets* as a further member of the kind — the LDS claim that God "has not constrained himself to any amount of books." The agnostic conclusion Horn extracts ("be open to other infallible rules existing") is precisely the door the Restoration walks through.
