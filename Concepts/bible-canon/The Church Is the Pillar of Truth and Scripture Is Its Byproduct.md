# The Church Is the Pillar of Truth and Scripture Is Its Byproduct

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
Against the affirmative that the Bible is the sole infallible rule of faith, [[Shiloh Lofgren]] argues the New Testament locates authority in a **living, commissioned church**, of which Scripture is a *byproduct* — "scripture plus church," not "scripture alone." [[1 Timothy 3.15|1 Tim 3:15]] calls **the church** "the pillar and ground of the truth," not the texts the church produced; and the model continues until a unity of faith ([[Ephesians 4.11|Eph 4:11-13]]) that has not arrived, so there is no scriptural cutoff at which living authority gives way to a closed book.[^1]

## The Rebuttal

### 1. The pillar is the church, not the book — rate: strong
"The church of the living God [is] the pillar and ground of the truth ... not necessarily the scriptures that church produces. The scriptures are a byproduct of that authoritative church. The church is the foundation, and there's nothing to indicate that God will tear that foundation out and replace it."[^1] The predicate in [[1 Timothy 3.15|1 Tim 3:15]] is applied to the *ekklēsia*. **Steelman of Whitehead:** every example of such authority "was written down as scripture," and Protestants do honor church offices — but distinguish Spirit-*guidance* (fallible) from *inspiration* (infallible), so the church's authority is real but not canonical. *Reply:* that distinction is asserted, not derived from a verse that limits the church's binding authority to the moment of inscripturation.

### 2. Authority was vested in commissioned persons — rate: strong
Jesus "at no point in his earthly ministry commissions the writing of the New Testament." Instead he commissions *people*: [[Luke 10.16|Luke 10:16]] "the one who hears you hears me." "The authority ... was vested in the people Jesus commissioned, not necessarily in the text they produced, although that authority may follow."[^2] **Steelman of Whitehead:** the apostolic office was *foundational* (Eph 2:20) and therefore unrepeatable, completed once the foundation was laid. *Reply:* the text says the offices continue "till we all come in the unity of the faith," a goal demonstrably unmet.

### 3. The Acts 15 council models the New Testament way of settling disputes — rate: strong
When the circumcision question arose, the apostles "gathered, received revelation or guidance by the Spirit ('it seemed good to the Holy Spirit and to us,' [[Acts 15|Acts 15:28]]), and gave authoritative direction." Lofgren: "The Protestant model for settling disputes is consult your Bible — and if you can't agree, consult your Bible harder. But in the New Testament, the model is for authorized church leaders to gather, receive guidance by the Spirit, and give authoritative direction."[^3] He notes this revelation *superseded* the earlier instruction not to go to the Gentiles (Matthew 10:5-6) — exactly what a "sola-scriptura-minded Pharisee" would have rejected as contradicting the old. See [[The Restoration Answers an Authority Problem Scholarship Cannot Solve]].

### 4. The pattern never changes — rate: moderate-strong
"God's model from all time was to reveal his will through living representatives" ([[Amos 3.7|Amos 3:7]] "the Lord God does nothing without revealing his secret to his servants the prophets"), and "nothing in the Bible indicates this model would change." Offices are given **"until we all attain unto the unity of the faith"** — "Have we come to a unity of the faith? Clearly not."[^4] **Steelman of Whitehead (his actual rebuttal):** revelation tracks *covenants*; the new covenant is "new and everlasting" (Hebrews 13), so — unlike the gap before Christ — no further covenant, and thus no further canon, is to be anticipated. *Reply:* the "unity of the faith" telos is textual and unmet, whereas the covenant-cessation inference is, by Whitehead's own concession, "an assumption" not a verse.

## Best Quotes
> "The church of the living God is the pillar and ground of the truth ... the scriptures are a byproduct of that authoritative church."[^1]

> "The Protestant model for settling disputes is: consult your Bible. And if you can't agree, consult your Bible harder. But in the New Testament, the model is for authorized church leaders to gather, receive guidance by the Spirit, and give authoritative direction."[^3]

> "Paul says the Lord will give apostles and prophets *until we all come to a unity of the faith*. Have we come to a unity of the faith? Clearly not."[^4]
