# Jesus Referenced the Apocrypha

## The Claim
Critics fault Latter-day Saints for valuing extra-biblical texts — but Jesus himself drew on apocryphal traditions, legitimizing the practice. His "blood of Abel to Zechariah" in [[Luke 11.47-51|Luke 11:47-51]] presupposes traditions found outside the canon.

## Supporting Evidence
- "Everybody gets on us for reading these extra-biblical texts. You know who else read them? Jesus read them." *(speaker's thesis)* [^1].
- His audience "knew what he was talking about" — the allusion only works because the Abel/Zechariah blood traditions were common knowledge *(argument from audience competence)* [^2].
- These are **pre-Christian** traditions (Enoch-era and the apocrypha later removed from the Catholic Bible), not late Christian inventions *(speaker)* [^3].

## Best Quote
> "Everybody gets on us for reading these extra-biblical texts. You know who else read them? Jesus read them." — [[Jonah (Plain and Precious Publishing)|Jonah]] [^1]
