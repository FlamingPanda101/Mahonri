# Metatron and the Divine Name Are the Taproot of Christology

## The Claim
[[Dan McClellan]] argues that the rationalization for a mediator who possesses and asserts the **divine name and divine identity** was "already baked into" Greco-Roman and rabbinic Judaism, and that this name-bearing logic — anchored in [[Scriptures/Exodus 23.20-21|Exodus 23:21]] ("for my name is in him") — is "the taproot of early Christology." It made it "so easy for people to accept that Jesus could be the manifestation of God's presence." [^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The Rav Idit / Metatron tradition cites Exodus 23.** A heretic asks Rav Idit why God tells Moses "Come up to **Adonai**" (Exod 24:1) instead of "Come up to me." Rav Idit answers that this "Adonai" is **[[Metatron]]** — "whose name is like his master's" — and grounds it in Exodus 23, "for my name is in him." So Metatron, identified with the messenger of Adonai, "can be referred to as Adonai." *Rating: strong — this is a real Talmudic passage (b. Sanhedrin 38b) and a textbook case of name-bearing mediation.* [^2]

2. **Yahoel is a parallel name-bearing mediator.** McClellan cites [[Yahoel]] alongside Metatron as a Greco-Roman period figure showing the same logic: a mediator in whom the divine name dwells. *Rating: well-attested in Second Temple scholarship (Apocalypse of Abraham).* [^3]

3. **John applies the same divine-name logic to Jesus.** "Even in places like the Gospel of John, we see the role of the divine name in endowing Jesus with divine power and authority," with a "mutual indwelling" of Father and Son. *Rating: a defensible reading connecting John's name/glory motifs to the mediation stream.* [^4] *Steelman of the orthodox side:* Bauckham-style "divine identity" Christology argues the NT places Jesus *within* the unique identity of God in a way that exceeds Metatron-type mediation — so this stream may be the seed *of* high Christology rather than an alternative to it.

4. **Cognitive ease explains rapid acceptance.** Because the name-bearing-agent category was already intuitive, accepting Jesus as the bearer of God's presence required no new philosophy. *Rating: coherent within McClellan's cognitive-science-of-religion program; the causal claim is harder to prove than the existence of the category.* [^1]

## Best Quotes
> "Rabbi Idit says, 'That's not Adonai. That's Metatron'... 'His name is like his master's.' In other words, Metatron can be referred to as Adonai. And then what does Rabbi Idit quote? Exodus 23, 'For my name is in him.'" [^2]

> "That rationalization for divine mediation and even for the mediators' possession of and assertion of the divine name and divine identity — that's already there. It's already baked into what's going on in Greco-Roman period Judaism and even rabbinic Judaism." [^1]

> "That, I have argued and will continue to argue, is the taproot of early Christology. That is the intuitive logic that made it so easy for people to accept that Jesus could be the manifestation of God's presence." [^5]
