# 1 Corinthians 8 Paul Affirms Many Gods Exist

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
[[Gavin Ortlund]] argues that [[1 Corinthians 8.4-6|1 Corinthians 8:4–6]] — "for us there is one God the Father, **from whom are all things**" — shows Paul is a strict monotheist: the qualifier "all things" means the Father created everything, including any spiritual beings, so there are not really many gods, only one God who matters *and* exists. The fact that an evil entity (or Corinthian slogan) can be quoted does not undercut this.

## The Rebuttal

### 1. Paul explicitly grants that many gods exist (rating: strong; the text)
The passage says "there are many gods and many lords" — Paul concedes their existence and then asserts exclusivity *for us*: "yet for us there is one God the Father." This is exclusive-relationship language, parallel to the Shema (see [[The Shema Asserts Exclusive Relationship Not Sole Existence]]). [^1]

> "although there may be socalled gods in heaven or on Earth as indeed there are many gods and many lords yet for us there is one God the father" [^1]

### 2. "From whom are all things" doesn't settle existence (rating: strong; comparative)
Even granting that the Father created "all things" — including spiritual beings — that is *exactly* what is said of the gods relative to Amun-Ra and of the host of heaven relative to Yahweh in [[Nehemiah 9.6|Nehemiah 9:6]]: created beings who worship their maker. Being created does not make a being non-divine; the Amun-Ra parallel shows created-and-worshiping gods are still gods. [^2] See [[Rhetoric of Incomparability Is Not Monotheism]].

> "just like in Nehemiah 9:6 the god of Israel was the one who created all these other quote unquote Gods because they worship him and he made them and as I have already demonstrated that doesn't really mean anything to the question of whether or not they exist as Gods" [^2]

### 3. Paul's idol/demon logic confirms the indexing model (rating: strong)
In the same letter Paul says "an idol is nothing in the world" yet pagan sacrifice is "to demons" — real entities distinct from the idol. The idol is nothing; the deity behind it is real. See [[Idol Polemic Denies the Idol Not the Deity It Indexes]]. [^3]

### 4. Steelman of the opposing view
Ortlund's best move is that Paul elsewhere says spiritual "rulers and authorities" were created *through* the Son (Col 1:16), so "all things" really does sweep up the spiritual realm under the one God — leaving the "many gods" as a concession to *how pagans talk* ("so-called gods"), not Paul's own ontology. [^4] McClellan answers that "so-called" modifies the *idols/their identification*, not the underlying entities (which Paul calls demons), and that subsuming created divine beings under one supreme creator is precisely the ancient polytheistic pattern (Amun-Ra, Nehemiah 9:6), not monotheism. He also notes Ortlund's own concession that there is "a real entity at work" behind pagan worship. [^5]

## Best Quotes

> "it seems like Paul thinks that the god of Jesus Christ is the god from whom are all things and for whom are all things just like in Nehemiah 9:6 the god of Israel was the one who created all these other quote unquote Gods" [^2]

> "monotheism in the Bible yeah there's one God from whom are all things and for whom are all things it's just that we can also use the same word namely God or Elohim to describe some of the created beings that he made" — Ortlund's summary, which McClellan rejects as renegotiating the term [^6]
