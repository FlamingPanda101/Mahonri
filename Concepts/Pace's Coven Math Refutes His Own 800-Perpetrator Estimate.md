# Pace's Coven Math Refutes His Own 800-Perpetrator Estimate

## The Claim
Pace's headline figure — ~800 satanic perpetrators "on the Wasatch Front" — is disproved by the memo's own data. He inflated the number by misapplying a borrowed assumption, and the error reveals he was advocating a case rather than reading his own evidence.

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. The extrapolation Pace actually made (strong — quoted)
Pace wrote: "I have met with 60 victims. Assuming that each one comes from a coven of 13, we are talking about the involvement of 800 or so right here on the Wasatch Front" (60 × 13 ≈ 780–800) [^1].

### 2. His own memo undercuts the geography (strong)
"He said only 37 of his interviewees came from Utah" — so even on his own coven assumption the Wasatch Front figure cannot derive from all 60. Applying the multiplier only to Utah victims, or accounting for overlap, collapses the number [^2].

### 3. He assumed zero overlap among covens (strong — methodological error)
Pace was "assuming no overlap at all among these mysterious covens." If victims shared covens (as a real conspiracy would imply), the perpetrator count shrinks dramatically. The 800 figure requires every victim to belong to a wholly distinct 13-person cell [^2].

### 4. The motive was urgency, not accuracy (moderate — assessment)
"Bishop Pace is so concerned with pressing his case that he slips up on the math. He wants to inflate the number to create urgency because from the very start, Pace was a true believer." The arithmetic error is downstream of advocacy. See [[The Pace Memo Was Not an Investigation but a Persuasive Essay]] [^3].

## Steelman (Opposing View)
A sympathetic reader could say the 800 figure was explicitly flagged by Pace as an *assumption* ("assuming that each one comes from a coven of 13"), so it is a rough illustrative estimate, not a claim of 800 confirmed criminals — and rough estimates are not "refuted" merely by being rough. Fair as far as it goes; but the estimate is built on a number Pace borrowed from fiction (the coven of 13), applied to a victim count that includes 23 non-Utahns while labeling the result "right here on the Wasatch Front," with an explicit no-overlap assumption a real conspiracy would violate. As a basis for alarm it is not merely imprecise but internally contradictory.
