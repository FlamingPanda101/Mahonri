# Intelligent Design as a Scientific Inference

## The Point
Atkins' opening verdict is categorical: intelligent design is "not science at all," merely "a straw man in a cheap tuxedo."[^1] Meyer's answer is a demarcation argument: the charge rests not on the *evidence* but on "an arbitrary definition... not of evolution but of science."[^2] He contends that ID uses the very method Darwin pioneered — *historical* (abductive) scientific reasoning, the "method of multiple competing hypotheses," which infers the cause "known to produce the effect in question."[^3] Since information in our uniform experience always comes from a mind, and DNA is sequence-specific information, intelligence is the best explanation. If Darwin's reasoning in the *Origin of Species* counts as science, Meyer argues, then ID — using "exactly the same mode of reasoning" — must too; to exclude it is to settle the question "by definition" and risk excluding the true explanation.[^4]

## Evidence
1. **Same logic, opposite conclusion.** "The debate about intelligent design and Darwinism is not a debate about the facts; the same facts are conceded on both sides. The question is about how to interpret the facts and what we will allow as possible explanations."[^5]
2. **Darwin's own method.** "If you're trying to explain an event in the remote past you should be looking for causes that are known to produce the effect in question." For the origin of life that effect is information — and the only known cause of information is a mind.[^3]
3. **Creationism vs. ID.** "Creationism is an attempt to understand... how life got here by using the Bible as a source document... a deduction or interpretation of religious authority. Intelligent design is an inference from biological data using the same principles of reasoning that are used by other scientists such as Darwin."[^6]
4. **The Rosetta Stone analogy.** "If you're looking at the Rosetta Stone you can look in vain if you limit yourself to materialistic explanations of wind and erosion... and don't open yourself to the possibility that mind played a role." Atkins' rejoinder: "mind is a reality, mind is material, mind is an activity of the brain, and the brain is solely material."[^7]
5. **The arbitrary-definition charge.** Meyer: scientists "categorically denounce" ID "because it doesn't meet an arbitrary definition of science... promulgated by many materialist scientists since the late 19th century" — namely that a scientist "must limit yourself to only materialistic explanations."[^2]
6. **The Sternberg affair as exhibit.** The whole *Expelled* frame is that Richard Sternberg — himself an evolutionary biologist with two PhDs — had his career damaged for peer-reviewing and publishing Meyer's Cambrian paper, illustrating that the demarcation is enforced socially, not argued.[^8]

## Best Quotes
> "If Darwin's method of reasoning in the Origin of Species is properly considered scientific, which I believe it is, then the case for intelligent design should also be considered scientific because it's using exactly the same mode of reasoning." [^4]

> "There's no science at all involved in it... it's a straw man in a cheap tuxedo." (Atkins) [^1]

> "It doesn't meet an arbitrary definition of science... that if you're going to be a scientist you must limit yourself to only materialistic explanations and you can't even consider the possibility of the design hypothesis." [^2]

## LDS Connection
Meyer's complaint — that an *a priori* commitment to materialism forecloses an intelligent cause before the evidence is weighed — parallels the Latter-day Saint critique of methodological naturalism wherever it is treated as metaphysics rather than method. LDS natural theology reasons comfortably from observable order to a Designer. Two honest caveats: the inference, taken alone, yields only "a designing intelligence," not the specific embodied, organizing God of the Restoration (ID argues for a generic designer); and Meyer's design is a *creator-mind* behind matter, whereas the Restoration casts God as one who organizes eternal matter and intelligences rather than creating ex nihilo. For the book-length version of this same method argument, see [[Intelligent Design as Inference to the Best Explanation]] (which also records Atkins calling Meyer's case "nonsense").
