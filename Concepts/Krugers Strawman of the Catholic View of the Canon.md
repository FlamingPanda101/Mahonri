# Kruger's Strawman of the Catholic View of the Canon

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
[[Michael Krueger]] uses a thermometer/thermostat illustration to characterize the Catholic position as the claim that the church is a **thermostat** — that it *creates* canonicity by decree, such that "if the church had chosen 73 other books, Catholics would consider those inspired, not because of anything in the books themselves, but just because of the church's subsequent decree" [^1]. [[Joe Heschmeyer]] replies this is a strawman: it attacks a view Catholicism explicitly rejects [^2].

## The Rebuttal

### 1. Vatican I explicitly condemns the "thermostat" view — rate: strong (primary magisterial source)
"That position is explicitly condemned by the First Vatican Council. It says explicitly, we are not saying this. We are not claiming to be a thermostat making uninspired books inspired. These books were inspired from the moment they were written. The church is guided by the Holy Spirit to recognize that, not to create that" [^2]. The actual Catholic view (Dei Filius, 1870) is that the church *receives and declares* books "held as sacred and canonical... because, having been written by the inspiration of the Holy Spirit, they have God for their author" — i.e. inspiration is *prior to and independent of* the church's recognition. See [[Thermometer vs Thermostat - The Church Declares the Canon]].

### 2. The "fun house mirror": Protestants defend the real Catholic view while branding it Reformation — rate: incisive
The deep irony Heschmeyer presses: these apologists "work by ironically defending the *real* Catholic view against a *fake* Catholic view, but they defend the real Catholic view by claiming it's the view of the reformers when it's not — it's the view the reformers were actually arguing *against*." The actual debate is distorted into "a fun house mirror" [^3]. **Steelman of Kruger:** some popular Catholic apologetics *do* overstate the church's role in ways that sound thermostat-like, so Kruger's target is not wholly invented — but the *magisterial* Catholic teaching is unambiguously the recognition (declaration) model, so attributing the thermostat view to Catholicism as such is a misrepresentation.

### 3. The real Catholic view is recognition, not creation — rate: strong
"The church is guided by the Holy Spirit to recognize that, not to create that" — books inspired at the moment of writing. This is the thermometer-plus-infallible-declaration position, not divine fiat over inert texts. See [[Authorization - Divine Designation Required for an Infallible Canon]] [^2].
