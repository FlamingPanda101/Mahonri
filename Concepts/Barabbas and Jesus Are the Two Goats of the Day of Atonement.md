# Barabbas and Jesus Are the Two Goats of the Day of Atonement

#type/claim

## The Claim
Following [[Margaret Barker]], [[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)|Dave Butler]] argues that the **Day of Atonement** (Leviticus 16) is the interpretive key to the Passion in the **Gospel of John**. On the Day of Atonement the high priest takes two identical, unblemished goats and casts lots: one becomes "*la-Yahweh*" (for/as Yahweh) and is slain, its blood thrown in the Holy of Holies onto the Ark to atone; the other becomes "*la-Azazel*" and is driven out into the wilderness. Re-reading the preposition *lamed* as "**as**" (not merely "for"), one goat *is* Yahweh and one *is* Azazel — and **Azazel is a name of the leader of the rebel angels** in 1 Enoch. John then stages the trial as the two goats: **Jesus** (the Lord, sacrificed) versus **Barabbas** — whose name means "**son of the father**" (*bar-abba*) — the near-identical twin who is released and driven out. [^1] [^2]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The two-goats / "lamed-as" reading is a real exegetical move.** Leviticus 16 does pair two identical goats by lot, one for Yahweh and one for Azazel; the Hebrew *lamed* can carry the sense "as / appointed as," not only "for." On that reading the rite stages **Yahweh slain and atoning, Satan (Azazel) expelled** — a coherent atonement drama rather than a sacrifice "to a goat-demon." *Rating: the linguistic option is defensible; the dramatic identification is interpretive.* [^3] [^4]
2. **Barabbas = "son of the father."** *Bar-abba* genuinely means "son of the father," making Jesus and Barabbas **two sons of the Father** offered to the crowd — one sacrificed, one released — exactly the structure of the two goats. *Rating: the etymology is solid; the typology is the inference.* [^2]
3. **The empty tomb as the Ark of the Covenant.** In John 20 the witnesses see the linen cloths lying with an angel at the head and an angel at the foot — the image of a box flanked by two cherubim, i.e. the **mercy seat of the Ark** where the atoning blood was sprinkled. John is read as signaling "the atonement has been accomplished": the blood of the Lord was spilled, the body has ascended, the *real* Day of Atonement has happened. See [[John 20.17|John 20:17]]. *Rating: an evocative visual parallel; not stated by the text.* [^5]
4. **The high priest's Name and the falling crowd.** On the Day of Atonement the high priest pronounces the divine Name and worshippers fall down; in John 18:6 Jesus says *egō eimi* ("I am," the italicized "he" not in the Greek) and the arresting party "went backward and fell to the ground" — Jesus acting as the high priest pronouncing the Name (cf. Exodus 3:14). *Rating: a strong intra-Johannine echo independent of the goats argument.* [^6] [^7]
5. **Continuity with a known pair-motif.** This is the same "two identical sons / good twin and counterfeit" pattern the vault already traces in the two seraphim and the [[The Two Serpents of Eden and the Divine Name Switch|two serpents of Eden]], where "the two goats of the Day of Atonement" are listed as one instance. *Rating: situates the claim within an established temple pattern.*

**Steelman of the skeptical counter.** A critic objects that (a) Leviticus 16's standard reading sends one goat *to* (not *as*) Azazel, a wilderness destination, and "Azazel = Satan" is a *later* (Enochic/rabbinic) development read back into the Torah; (b) the Gospel writers never explicitly identify Jesus or Barabbas with the goats — the typology is supplied by the modern interpreter; (c) Barabbas's name yielding "son of the father" is suggestive but the Synoptics, not John, carry the Barabbas scene most fully, complicating a *Johannine* Day-of-Atonement reading; and (d) "two angels at the tomb" is a thin basis for "Ark of the Covenant." The reply (Barker's, via Butler) is the *cumulative* fit: two identical figures, one sacrificed and one expelled, the "son of the father" name, the high-priestly Name in the garden, and the Ark-shaped tomb all converge on a single ritual template that the Evangelist — writing for Jewish-background Christians who "knew what John was talking about" — could expect readers to catch.

## Best Quotes
> "Barabbas, whose name means son of the father, is released and let go, and those are the two goats — the two sons of the father — and the one is sacrificed because he's the atoning son, he is Yahweh, and the other one is Satan and he is chased out of camp." [^2]

> "What is the image of a box with an angel at each end? Turns out that is the Ark of the Covenant… the day of atonement, the real thing, has happened." [^5]

> "As soon as he said *egō eimi*, 'I am,' they went backward and fell to the ground." [^7]
