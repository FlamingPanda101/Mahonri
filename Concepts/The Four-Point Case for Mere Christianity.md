# The Four-Point Case for Mere Christianity

## The Point
Borrowing C. S. Lewis's phrase "mere Christianity" for the fundamental beliefs shared by all Bible-believing Christians, Turek argues there is a simple way to show those beliefs are true: answer four questions in order.[^1] (1) Does **truth** exist? (2) Does **God** exist? (3) Are **miracles** possible? (4) Is the **New Testament** historically reliable? If the answer to all four is yes, "then we have good reason to believe that the entire Bible is true."[^1] The four points form a logical chain — each depends on the one before it.

## Evidence
Turek frames the case negatively to show the stakes: if any one link fails, much of the Bible falls with it.[^2] The Bible cannot be true if there is no objective truth (and neither could any atheist's book). There can be no "Word of God" if there is no God — but if God exists, the Bible *might* be true. If miracles are impossible, the Bible cannot be believed. And if the New Testament documents are not historically reliable, the case collapses; but if they are, "one can make a good case that the entire Bible is true."[^2] The architecture is cumulative and conditional: truth → God → miracles → New Testament. Because earlier chapters argued for truth, God (the SURGE/CRIMES evidence), and the possibility of miracles, Turek spends most of his effort on the fourth point — the historical reliability of the New Testament, which carries the records of Jesus's claims and Resurrection.[^3] He stresses this is a deliberately "truncated treatment," pointing readers to his fuller *I Don't Have Enough Faith to Be an Atheist*.[^3]

## Best Quotes
> "MERE CHRISTIANITY. That's what C. S. Lewis called the fundamental beliefs of all Bible-believing Christians across denominations. There is a simple way to show that those fundamental beliefs are actually true." [^1]

> "If the answer is yes to these four questions, then we have good reason to believe that the entire Bible is true. But if the answer to any one of these four questions is no, then much of the Bible is false." [^2]

## LDS Connection
Latter-day Saints can affirm every one of the four points: that truth is real and knowable, that God exists, that miracles are possible, and that the New Testament reliably witnesses to the death and Resurrection of Jesus Christ. The chain "truth → God → miracles → reliable witness" mirrors the LDS confidence that revelation is both rational and testable. The honest LDS distinctive is what gets *added* on top of "mere Christianity": the Restoration brings living prophets and additional scripture (the Book of Mormon, Doctrine and Covenants, Pearl of Great Price) as further witnesses of Christ, rather than treating the biblical canon as closed and complete. Where Turek ends the chain at "the entire Bible is the Word of God," Saints would say the same God who spoke anciently still speaks — so the testimony of Christ is corroborated, not contradicted, by continuing revelation.
