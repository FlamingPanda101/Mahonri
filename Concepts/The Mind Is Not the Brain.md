# The Mind Is Not the Brain

## The Point
Materialists claim neuroscience has shown that "the mind is the brain," pointing to brain-scan correlations between neural activity and thought.[^1] Turek answers that correlation is not identity: science has not *discovered* that materialism is true, it has merely *assumed* it. No experiment can prove the mind is the brain, because running and interpreting any experiment requires a free, rational mind in the first place.[^2] Beyond that defeater, he offers five positive reasons that mind and brain are distinct — that you are "a soul and a body," not a mere body.[^3]

## Evidence
Turek's five arguments:[^4] (1) **Persistence of identity** — your body and brain replace their molecules completely over time, yet "you" and your memories persist; the brain changes materially while the mind does not. (2) **Thoughts are immaterial** — no neurosurgeon cracking open your skull would find the "two-ton pink elephant" you are imagining; thoughts are not physical parts of the brain. (3) **Thoughts are subjective** — your brain is a public object others can observe, but only you, from the inside, know your specific thoughts; first-person experience "can't be explained by mere physics and chemistry." (4) **Intentionality** — you can understand and answer a unique question accurately ("Where were you born?"); blind, repetitive chemical laws cannot account for goal-directed intelligence — "you are not just a bag of rocks." (5) **Mind over matter** — cognitive therapy and the placebo effect show the immaterial mind changing brain chemistry; *New Scientist* ranked the placebo effect #1 of "13 Things That Don't Make Sense," which it only is "if you assume that the mind either does not exist or is powerless."[^5] Turek concludes that whatever the precise mind-body relation, mind and intentionality "are not merely physical," and the brain is at most "the instrument through which you have thoughts."[^6] Former atheist Antony Flew put it: "Science cannot discover the self; the self discovers science."[^7]

## Best Quotes
> "If we are just molecules in motion, as materialism asserts, then a human has no more ability than a bag of rocks to make an intellectual case for anything, including atheism." [^8]

> "You are not a mere body—you are a soul and a body." [^3]

## LDS Connection
Restoration scripture explicitly identifies the human person as the union of an immaterial (yet refined-material) spirit with a body: "The spirit and the body are the soul of man" (D&C 88:15). This is exactly Turek's body-soul dualism, with a distinctive twist — LDS theology denies a strict spirit/matter divide, teaching that "all spirit is matter, but it is more fine or pure" and "discernible only by purer eyes" (D&C 131:7–8). The mind is therefore not the brain, and the conscious self is not extinguished when the brain changes or dies, because the spirit is a distinct, enduring reality. The intelligence at the core of that spirit "was not created or made" ([[D&C 93]], v.29), grounding precisely the persistent self-identity and genuine intentionality Turek argues materialism cannot explain.
