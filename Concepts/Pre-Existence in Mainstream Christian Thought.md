10|# Pre-Existence in Mainstream Christian Thought
11|
12|## The Claim
13|The LDS doctrine of pre-existence — that human spirits existed as conscious, individuated beings before mortal birth — is increasingly taken seriously by mainstream Christian theologians, not dismissed as a "Mormon heresy." [[David Bentley Hart]] (Eastern Orthodox philosopher, Yale UP author) "has talked about pre-existence."[^1] Beyond academia, everyday Christian content creators are "presupposing what Latter-day Saints believe" about pre-existence as though it were a natural Christian idea.[^2]
14|
15|## Supporting Evidence
16|
17|1. **David Bentley Hart:** The prominent Eastern Orthodox philosophical theologian, author of *That All Shall Be Saved* (Yale UP), has written about pre-existence — a striking example of a mainstream Christian scholar with top-tier academic credentials engaging with a doctrine long dismissed as uniquely Mormon.[^1]
18|
19|2. **Content Creator Adoption:** The Ward Radio host recounts encountering "some random content creator talking about the pre-existence. She didn't use that word, but she was talking about it. She was talking about it that it was a thing. And I was like, 'Wait a second. Is this a thing now for them?'"[^2] This suggests the idea is filtering into lay Christian discourse without LDS framing.
20|
21|3. **Neuroscientific Corroboration:** A world-famous neuroscientist (unnamed in source) concluded "consciousness precedes existence. There was consciousness before there was matter. Matter arises out of consciousness, not the other way around." When asked point-blank whether individuated human spirits could have existed before incarnation, he replied: "Yeah, that makes sense to me. That seems to be a possibility."[^3] This provides an independent, non-theological line of evidence consistent with pre-existence.
22|
23|4. **Convergence Context:** Pre-existence is one of several "uniquely Mormon heresies" that mainstream Christian scholars are converging toward, alongside embodied God and feminine divine.[^4]
24|
25|## Best Quote
26|
27|> "Probably the world's most famous neuroscientist. Ultimately, his conclusion is that consciousness precedes existence. There was consciousness before there was matter. Matter arises out of consciousness, not the other way around. And I asked him the question point blank. I said, 'So, if we thought of that consciousness as being individuated, would that mean that we as human spirits could have existed before we were incarnate as human beings?' And he said, 'Yeah, that makes sense to me. That seems to be a possibility.'"[^3]
28|
29|## Counter-Arguments
30|
31|- **Hart's actual view:** David Bentley Hart is a universalist, not necessarily a pre-existence advocate in the LDS sense. "Talking about" pre-existence is not the same as endorsing the full LDS doctrine of premortal spirit existence with agency, a divine council, and a war in heaven.
32|- **Unnamed neuroscientist:** Without a name, the neuroscientist's claim cannot be verified or assessed for authority. Many philosophers of mind (e.g., eliminative materialists) would flatly reject consciousness-precedes-matter.
33|- **Content creator anecdote:** A single TikTok or YouTube creator is weak evidence of a broad trend.
