# The Uncaused Cause Is Aristotle Not Scripture (Objection Rebutted)

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
Matt Fradd defines God as "the unconditioned ground of existence, the only uncaused cause, the metaphysically necessary being whose non-existence is impossible, the creator of all creation," and [[Joe Heschmeyer]] holds that Mormons are *wrong* to claim Greco-Roman philosophy influenced the Christian conception of God.[^1]

## The Rebuttal

1. **"Uncaused cause" is Aristotle's unmoved mover (rated: strong, historical).** [[Cardon Ellis]] points out the parallel directly: Aristotle's *unmoved mover / prime mover* is "the foundational, eternal, and immaterial cause of all motion and change in the universe... that initiates movement without movement itself." Fradd's "uncaused cause" is the same concept in different words. To deny pagan-philosophical influence while quoting the paradigmatic pagan philosopher "is making a point for us."[^2] See [[Greek Philosophy Overwrites Hebrew Divine Corporeality]].

2. **No scripture is cited for the definition (rated: strong, observational).** [[Luke Irwin]]: when asked what God is, Fradd "proceeds to quote zero scriptures and just quotes Aquinas a thousand years later." The classical definition is built from Aristotelian/Thomistic metaphysics, not from the Hebrew Bible's embodied, anthropomorphic God.[^3]

3. **The historical context explains (not excuses) the borrowing (rated: moderate, charitable-historical).** Ellis offers a sympathetic account: early Christians were *underdogs*, "the laughingstock of the Greco-Roman world," persecuted and slaughtered, who reached for "the verbiage and nomenclature that surrounded them" to explain *up* to their cultured despisers why they lived as they did. The Hellenization of the God-concept was the apologetic vocabulary of an underdog movement, not a top-down imposition — but it is Hellenization nonetheless.[^4] *Steelman:* a classical theist responds that using Greek *vocabulary* to articulate a truth does not make the truth Greek (just as using English does not make the gospel English); the doctrine of divine simplicity can be defended as a reading of biblical monotheism, and the "you quoted Aristotle, therefore it's pagan" move is a genetic fallacy. The LDS counter is that the *substance* — immateriality, simplicity, timelessness — is alien to the Hebrew scriptures and traceable to philosophy, not merely the wording.

## Best Quotes

> "When you say Mormons are wrong and paganism didn't influence Christianity, and then quote 'the uncaused cause' sounding just like the unmoved mover — I just feel like you're making a point for us."[^2]

> "These weren't top dogs looking down. These were underdogs trying to explain up why they felt and how they lived the life they did."[^4]

> "No scriptures quoted or referenced."[^3]
