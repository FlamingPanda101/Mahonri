# The Prooftext Double Standard on Grace and Works

## The Point
Critics build the "Mormons believe in works-salvation" charge by lifting a few verses (2 Nephi 25:23; Article of Faith 3) out of context. The handout's reply is a **fairness/consistency argument**: it would be equally dishonest for a Latter-day Saint to take Matthew 7:21, Mark 16:16, John 3:5, Acts 2:38, Hebrews 5:9, James 2:24, 1 Peter 3:21, or Revelation 20:12 and use them to accuse mainstream Christians of works-salvation. Neither side bases its whole soteriology on a handful of verses; both must be read against the full witness of scripture. [^1]

## Evidence
1. **The symmetry.** "Would it be appropriate to take a single verse or half-quote from the Bible and use it to accuse mainstream Christians of believing something they don't? Of course not... We are simply asking for that same standard to be applied to our doctrine." [^1]
2. **Protestants themselves have works-sounding verses.** James 2:24 ("by works a man is justified, and not by faith only") is exactly the kind of text a hostile reader could weaponize against an Evangelical — yet Evangelicals (rightly) harmonize it with their grace texts. [^1]
3. **The standard:** "Biblical verses must be understood in their proper context and in conjunction with all authoritative sources which speak on the matter." [^1]
4. This is a specific instance of the broader catalog of critic tactics in [[The Critics' Manipulation Tactics and Logical Fallacies Toolkit]] (half-quote, false premise, double standard).

## Best Quotes
> "Do Christians base their whole theology on only a few verses of the Bible? Of course not... We are simply asking for that same standard to be applied to our doctrine." [^1]

## LDS Connection
A reusable move in any LDS-Evangelical exchange: grant the verse, then ask whether the critic applies the same hermeneutic to their own scripture. It reframes the debate from prooftext-trading to context and good-faith reading, and it pairs with the contextual reading of [[After All We Can Do Means In Spite of All We Can Do]].
