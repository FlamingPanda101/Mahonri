# Spiritual Gifts Differ - Not Everyone Receives the Burning

## The Point

[[Blake Ostler]] offers a pastoral correction to LDS culture: it is **not promised that everyone will receive a dramatic burning-in-the-bosom experience**. By [[Doctrine and Covenants 46.11-14|D&C 46]], spiritual gifts differ — some are given to *know* directly, others the gift to *believe on the words* of those who know — so a faithful member who never feels the burning, despite earnest study and prayer, may still have sufficient faith. [^1]

## Argument

1. **Cautions to Mormons.** Having the confirmation does *not* make one "morally or spiritually superior"; the experience is open to doubt (one may once have felt it and not now — Alma 5:26, "if ye have felt to sing the song of redeeming love... can ye feel so now?"); it does *not* make one omniscient or infallible about gospel principles; and not every spiritual experience counts as scripture. [^2]
2. **The differentiation of gifts.** [[Doctrine and Covenants 46.11-14|D&C 46:11-14]]: "to some it is given by the Holy Ghost to know that Jesus Christ is the Son of God... to others it is given to believe on their words." Ostler reads this to mean some will *not* have the direct experience — and that for them, the fact that others have it "is sufficient to sustain their faith." [^1]
3. **This qualifies [[Moroni 10.4-5|Moroni 10]].** It tempers the common expectation, drawn from Moroni's promise, that *everyone* who asks will receive the burning. Ostler notes he knows "lots of faithful Latter-day Saints" who, "notwithstanding earnest study and long prayer, have not received the burning in the bosom." [^3]
4. **A pastoral case.** A Relief Society president in Genoa was "constantly in crisis" because missionaries had promised that "all she had to do was ask" to have experiences like theirs. Ostler suggests "maybe her faith is sufficient" — she *did* have faith. [^3]

Ostler ties this to his broader claim that revelation is *distributed*: the goal "has always been a nation of prophets... governed by personal revelation," each accountable for revelation in their own stewardship — arguably "more revelation in the church now than in the time of Joseph Smith." [^4]

## Best Quotes
> "Not everyone will have such spiritual confirmation, because for them the fact that others have such experiences is sufficient to sustain their faith." [^1]

> "I know lots of faithful Latter-day Saints, notwithstanding earnest study and long prayer, have not received the burning in the bosom of which I speak." [^3]

> "There is a vastly important distinction between having a testimony and having a memory of having once had a testimony." [^2]
