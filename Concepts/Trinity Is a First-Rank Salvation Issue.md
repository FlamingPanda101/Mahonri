# Trinity Is a First-Rank Salvation Issue

## The Claim
[[Gavin Ortlund]] argues the Trinity is "first rank" — i.e., a doctrine such that "you cannot be saved without it... if someone rejects this, you do not have confidence that they can be saved." [^1] He grounds this not in an arbitrary rule but in two foundations: **whom** we worship (is Jesus eternal, uncreated, consubstantial with the Father?) and **how** reconciliation works (the need for a God-man mediator). [^2][^3] He is careful to say no one is damned for lacking the *word* "trinity" or the term *homoousios*; the substance of the belief — relating to Jesus as uncreated God — is what is at stake. [^4]

## The Rebuttal (i.e., counter-considerations, steelmanned)

1. **The "whom we worship" argument (strong on its own terms).** Ortlund: "whether or not the Father, the Son, and the Spirit are all eternal and are all God and are consubstantial... affects how we pray and worship." [^2] *Rating: internally coherent.* If worship is directed at a particular identity, mis-identifying that being is materially different from a trivial error. *Steelman of Hansen:* one can sincerely worship Jesus as fully divine (predicatively) and as the only means of salvation without affirming consubstantiality; the LDS worship the same person who died on Calvary, so the "wrong person" charge is overstated. [^5]

2. **The God-man / atonement argument.** Drawing on [[Anselm of Canterbury]]'s *Cur Deus Homo*: "only God can pay the debt, but only man needs to pay the debt," so the mediator must be both. [^3] *Rating: strong within satisfaction-theory; load-bearing only if one accepts that atonement framework.* The argument presupposes the very Creator/creature ontology the LDS reject (see [[Creatio Ex Nihilo Is the Lynchpin Difference]]).

3. **Steelman of the LDS objection (the explicitness gap).** Hansen: it is "strange" that a doctrine carrying such weight ("you are damned... unless you get this thing right") is itself not "explicitly laid out in scripture." [^6] *Rating: a real pressure point* — see [[Trinity Is an Interpretation Not Explicit in Scripture]]. Ortlund's reply is that *all* doctrine requires interpretation (the resurrection too), so the need for interpretation does not by itself demote a doctrine. [^7]

4. **Pastoral framing as duty, not hostility.** Ortlund: "the most unloving thing I could ever do is give someone false assurance." [^8] *Rating: a sincere reframing of an exclusivist claim as candor;* it answers the charge of nastiness but not the substantive question of whether the dial is set correctly.

## Best Quotes
> "It has to do with whom we worship as well as how we worship." [^2]

> "To honor your question I would say that yeah, this is a matter of salvation." [^1]

> "No one is going to hell for not knowing or understanding or using the term homoousios per se... The belief though is important, that is foundational." [^4]

> "The most unloving thing I could ever do is give someone false assurance and say, 'Oh yeah, you're fine, this is not something you need to believe,' and then it turns out this is actually really important." [^8]
