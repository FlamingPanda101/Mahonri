# Scripture as the Test of Prophets and Revelation

## The Claim
Kelly's practical thesis for *sola scriptura*: God may still speak (visions, dreams, revelation), but **scripture is the God-given standard to test** any claimed prophet, apostle, or supernatural experience. This is the "heart of the matter" — how does one verify a Joseph Smith?[^1]

## Supporting Evidence (Kelly's case, with assessment)

### 1. Scripture is *the standard*, not the only voice — rate: nuanced, often missed
Kelly is careful: "I'm not saying God cannot speak to us today... through visions, dreams, a revelation. But when a person claims to be a prophet... how do we test that?"[^2] His four-fold conclusion: *sola scriptura* is the standard to (a) know true from false doctrine, (b) test claimed revelation, (c) test supernatural experiences (God vs. devil), (d) provide a foundation to test even ourselves.[^3] **Assessment:** this is the strongest, most defensible form of his position — it does not deny continuing revelation, only subordinates it to a fixed canon.

### 2. The Berean and "angel from heaven" model — rate: strong proof-texts
[[Acts 17.10-11|Acts 17:10-11]]: Bereans "searched the scriptures daily to see whether those things were so."[^4] [[Galatians 1.8|Galatians 1:8]]: "though we, or an angel from heaven, preach any other gospel... let him be accursed" — Kelly's direct lever against the [[Angel Moroni|Moroni]] visitation.[^5] **Steelman of the rebuttal:** the vault's existing note [[Galatians 1-8 - Angel Preaching Another Gospel]] argues the "other gospel" is Judaizing legalism, not angelic visitation per se; and the Bereans tested Paul against the *OT*, which presupposes an already-recognized scripture rather than establishing canon closure.

### 3. The list of false claimants — rate: rhetorical
Kelly's closing names Joseph Smith alongside Charles Taze Russell, Ellen G. White, Sun Myung Moon, David Koresh, Harold Camping, Jim Jones — all claiming modern prophethood; scripture is offered as the discriminating test.[^6] **Assessment:** effective rhetoric, but Hayden's standing reply is that this still presumes the canon's *ultimate* authority, which is the very thing in dispute (it tests prophets *by* the canon without authorizing the canon).

### 4. Hayden's counter-mechanism — rate: the live-authority alternative
Pressed on how *he* tests a prophet, Hayden answers: the same way first-century Christians verified Peter or Paul — by a personal/spiritual experience and faith.[^7] Kelly turns this against him via the spirit-witness reliability problem: believers who accepted Tobit/Wisdom also "thought they were being Spirit-led."[^8] See [[The Church Once Accepted Books It Later Rejected]] and [[No Final Interpreter - The Protestant Adjudication Problem]]. The exchange exposes the core LDS-vs-Protestant fault line: *living authorized witness* vs. *fixed written standard*.

## Best Quotes
> "Sola scriptura is the standard to know true doctrine from false doctrine... to know if a person claims divine revelation lines up with the scriptures... to test supernatural experiences to see if it's from God or the devil."[^3]

> "Has God given us something today to know whether or not someone like a Joseph Smith, a Charles Taze Russell, an Ellen G. White... a Jim Jones, a whoever who claims to be a modern prophet — has he given us something we can examine? I believe the answer is clearly yes."[^6]

> "Scripture testifies that scripture is an authority to know truth from error, to examine whether or not an angel from heaven comes and preaches a different gospel."[^9]
