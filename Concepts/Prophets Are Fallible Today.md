# Prophets Are Fallible Today (Objection Rebutted)

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
A common move: "If prophets made mistakes back then, what mistakes are they making now?" — used to manufacture an "out," i.e., a license to disregard current prophetic counsel as "probably just his opinion."[^1]

## The Rebuttal

1. **Yes, prophets are fallible — but not "grossly" (rated: strong within LDS framework).** Peterson answers the question directly: "Are prophets fallible today? Yes, they are. ... But I don't think they're fallible in any gross way. They're not leading us astray."[^2] The boundary is [[Wilford Woodruff]]'s assurance that the Lord "won't allow that" — He would remove the President of the Church before letting him lead the people astray.

2. **Mistakes are in *programs and details*, not the core (rated: strong).** "Can there be Church programs that we try out and then say, Meh, didn't work? I'm sure that happens all the time." The Lord says "Do X" but "won't always tell you exactly *how*" — so the how is human, adjustable, and sometimes wrong.[^3] This is reinforced by examples: Elder Perry's young-adult reforms in Boston, Harold B. Lee's welfare program — leaders trying things, some working, some not.[^4]

3. **The danger is the motive, not the fact (rated: the apologetic crux).** The fallibility itself is not the problem; *looking for it as an excuse* is. "Are we looking for an out? ... I think that's dangerous. I think the safest thing is to say, I'm going to take very seriously what he says and try to do it."[^1] *Steelman of the critic:* if prophets can err on race or programs, a member needs *some* principle for discernment, not blind obedience — otherwise the office is unfalsifiable. Peterson's answer is not blind obedience but prioritizing the [[Doctrine of Christ as the Only Thing You Cannot Get Wrong]]: "You won't go too far astray if you follow them," and you can never be wrong by trying to serve, pray, and be kind.

4. **Henry Eyring's quip (rated: moderate, anecdotal).** "The average high priest group is a lot more certain about particular doctrinal issues than the First Presidency are." Confident lay certainty often outruns what has actually been revealed.[^5] See [[The Three Levels of the Gospel]].

## Best Quotes

> "If prophets made mistakes back then, what mistakes are they making now? ... I don't know how we jump there so quickly. ... Are we looking for an out? Like I don't have to do that because that's probably just his opinion. I think that's dangerous to do."[^1]

> "Are prophets fallible today? Yes, they are. ... They're not leading us astray. Wilford Woodruff said, The Lord won't allow that. He'll remove the President of the Church rather than allow that."[^2]

> "The average high priest group is a lot more certain about particular doctrinal issues than the First Presidency are."[^5]
