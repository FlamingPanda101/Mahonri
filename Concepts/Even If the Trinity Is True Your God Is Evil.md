# Even If the Trinity Is True Your God Is Evil

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
[[Hayden Carroll]] runs a "grant-the-Trinity-anyway" argument: even if a critic refuses every reason to reject the Trinity, accepting **perfect-being theology + creation *ex nihilo*** entails that God creates people out of nothing, *without their consent*, foreknowing many will be damned, and creates them anyway. A being who does that "is not good." Therefore the doctrine of God it produces is morally incoherent.[^1] The clip shows him pressing an interlocutor with a parallel: would parents be "good" if they conceived a child *intending* to torture it for life?[^2]

## The Rebuttal

### 1. The structure is a valid *reductio* against ex-nihilo + foreknowledge — rate: strong
The argument is a recognized form of the **problem of evil / problem of hell**: (P1) God creates each person ex nihilo and freely; (P2) God foreknows the eternal fate of each before creating; (P3) God creates persons he knows will be damned; (C) a perfectly good being would not do (P3) when he could refrain. This is logically tight given its premises and overlaps the vault's existing [[Problem of Hell and Creation]] and the Hell-rebuttal cluster. The torture-baby analogy is a vivid intuition pump for the moral premise. **Steelman of the classical theist:** standard theodicies block the reductio — (a) *free-will defense*: a world with libertarian free creatures who can reject God is more valuable than a world of pre-programmed saints; (b) *greater-good / soul-making*: the possibility of damnation is the cost of genuine love; (c) God's goodness is *not* univocal with human goodness (Thomistic analogy), so "good" applied to God need not forbid permitting hell. Carroll engages (a) by arguing God could simply "create only people who would freely choose good," and treats divine "goodness" that permits eternal torture as emptying the word of meaning ("now what does the word *good* mean?").[^3]

### 2. Calvinism "bites the bullet" — rate: strong as observation
Carroll's sharpest move is sociological-logical: Calvinists do not dodge the reductio — they *accept* it. "God is able to save everybody; not everyone is saved; therefore God chooses not to save everybody," and they say this is good because the damned's suffering "glorifies him."[^4] Carroll says he therefore *doesn't* use this argument on Calvinists (they'll say "yeah, so what") but does use it on non-Calvinist Christians to push them toward the Calvinist horn or out of ex-nihilo entirely. See [[Calvinism Consistency with Atheism on Free Will]]. He cites the Quu/Aaron debate where the Calvinist affirms Anne Frank is in hell for not accepting Christ.[^5] **Honest note:** this accurately represents *consistent* Calvinism (double predestination), but many Reformed thinkers reject that hell "glorifies God" in that crude sense, so the steelman is the harder, less rhetorically convenient Reformed position.

### 3. The LDS solution: co-eternal intelligences — rate: framing, the real payoff
The constructive alternative: because LDS doctrine holds that intelligences are **uncreated and co-eternal** with God (not made ex nihilo), God did not *manufacture* anyone destined for hell — agents have always existed and chose their own path. This dissolves (P1)/(P3) at the root. See [[Free Will - Atheist Determinism vs LDS Co-Eternality]], [[Creation Ex Nihilo Leads to Determinism]], and the existing [[Spirit Birth and Intelligences]]. An attendee adds the LDS framing that "we have always chosen each for our existence."[^6] **Caution:** LDS theology still faces its own hard cases (foreknowledge vs. agency, the fate of the un-exalted), so this relocates the problem rather than eliminating every form of it — but it does block the specific *ex-nihilo* version Carroll targets.

## Best Quotes
> "How can God be just and good if he's creating people knowing they're going to be burned in hell forever? ... Why not just not create those people?"[^7]

> "Calvinists say God is able to save everybody. Not everyone is saved. Therefore God chooses not to save everybody. That's why Calvinism is awesome because they just bite the bullet."[^4]

> "They say, well, their suffering in hell glorifies him. It's like, okay, now what does the word *good* mean?"[^3]

> "Even Jesus is saying it's better to not exist than to be in hell." (on [[Matthew 26.24|Matthew 26:24]], "better if he had not been born")[^8]
