# Nazarite vow

The Nazarite (Nazirite) vow is the consecration described in Numbers 6 by which an Israelite sets himself apart to the Lord for a period: abstaining from grapes and wine, avoiding contact with the dead, and — most visibly — leaving the hair uncut. Biblical figures such as Samson and Samuel are associated with it, and the New Testament records Paul observing a related vow.

## Relevance

The vow appears in the vault through the conversion story of [[Avraham Gileadi]], who kept a Nazarite vow (long hair and beard) during his passage from Catholicism through formal Judaism into the [[Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints]]. President Harold B. Lee reportedly allowed him to keep it after baptism ("he'll grow as he grows in the gospel"), making it a concrete detail of his Jewish-to-Latter-day-Saint testimony.
