# Bible Contradiction — Noah's Ark Animal Count

## The Contradiction

The flood narrative in Genesis contains two irreconcilable directives about how many animals Noah brought aboard the ark:

- **Genesis 6:19–20 (Priestly source):** "two of every sort shalt thou bring into the ark... Of fowls after their kind... two of every sort." — ALL land animals (*behemah*) and ALL birds (*oph*) come in exactly one pair each, male and female.
- **Genesis 7:2–3 (Non-Priestly source):** "Of every clean beast thou shalt take to thee by sevens, the male and his female: and of beasts that are not clean by two... Of fowls also of the air by sevens." — Clean land animals get seven pairs; unclean land animals get one pair; and ALL birds get seven pairs.

A Christian apologist presents this as a supposed contradiction to disprove the Bible, then dismisses it as a misunderstanding. Dan McClellan argues it IS a genuine contradiction — one that reveals two originally separate flood accounts later interwoven into a single narrative.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence — Why This IS a Genuine Contradiction

### 1. Both Passages Cover ALL Birds — and Contradict Directly

The clearest evidence of contradiction is the treatment of birds. Genesis 6:20 says "two of every sort" of birds. Genesis 7:3 says "fowls also of the air by sevens" — seven pairs of ALL birds. The non-Priestly source makes NO clean/unclean distinction among birds. There is no subset of birds that gets only one pair. "So you have in one passage two of all the birds of the air and in another passage seven pairs of all the birds of the air. There are no birds of the air that only come in one pair. They're all seven pairs." [^2]

### 2. The Apologetic Harmonization Cannot Account for Birds

The standard apologetic response — "two was the baseline, and the extra pairs were for sacrifice" — only works if the "extra" animals are CLEAN. But birds are all seven pairs regardless. The harmonization must EITHER:
- Ignore the bird clause entirely (ignoring the text), OR
- Invent an unstated clean/unclean bird distinction (inventing a "third thing" not in the text — see [[Rationalization C - The Third Thing Harmonization]])

### 3. Source Criticism: Two Originally Separate Flood Accounts

The scholarly consensus is that Genesis 6–9 weaves together two originally independent flood traditions — the Priestly (P) account and the non-Priestly (J/Yahwist) account. McClellan describes this as "one of the most long-standing, widely agreed upon, best-supported source critical arguments that there is in the Hebrew Bible. There's pretty much no serious scholar who rejects the separate existence of a Priestly account in the Pentateuch." [^3] See [[Noahs Ark Source Criticism Two Flood Accounts]].

### 4. Multiple Contradictions Beyond Animal Count

The animal count is just one of several irreconcilable differences between the two flood accounts. They also contradict on:
- How long the rain lasted (40 days in J vs. 150 days in P)
- How long the waters remained
- When the flood started and ended
- What caused the flood (YHWH's grief in J vs. cosmic un-creation in P)
- The divine name used (YHWH in J vs. Elohim in P)

These multiple, independent points of divergence make the source-critical solution far more plausible than a series of ad-hoc harmonizations.

### 5. The Harmonization Ignores the Scholarly Evidence

"If you just gloss over that entirely, ignore it, and say, 'No, it was just a baseline of two and then they added seven pairs for the clean animals so that they could be sacrificed,' you're ignoring everything. You're just asserting this rationalization over and against absolutely everything." [^4] The apologetic harmonization requires ignoring not only the textual data but the entire body of source-critical scholarship — spanning methods, evidence types, and decades of converging conclusions.
