# God Frames Truth Pedagogically to Change Behavior

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[Shad Brooks]] argues that God deliberately **frames and accommodates truth** to the hearer's capacity in order to maximize right behavior — and that this is *more* truthful, not less, given that truth is defined by its effect (see [[Truth Is Defined by What It Causes Not Merely Correct Facts]]). God "very often speaks to us in reference to beginnings and endings because that is our frame of reference," even though His works in reality have "no end, neither beginning" ([[D&C 29|D&C 29:33]]).[^1] [[Alma 29.8|Alma 29:8]] — "the Lord doth grant unto all nations, of their own nation and tongue, to teach his word... all that he seeth fit that they should have" — means God gives each people the level of truth "that is going to most optimally help them become like him."[^2] The decisive proof-text is [[D&C 19|D&C 19:6-7]], where God says He speaks of "endless torment" and "eternal damnation" "that it might work upon the hearts of the children of men altogether for my name's glory"[^3] — i.e. He chooses *stronger* (figurative) language for behavioral effect, "not with the intent to deceive."

## Supporting Evidence
- **"Endless torment" is figurative, not a lie (D&C 19).** Shad parses D&C 19 closely: it is *not* written "that there shall be no end to this torment," yet God says "endless torment" and "eternal damnation," then explains the wording is "more express... that it might work upon the hearts."[^4] His gloss: the torment will feel endless when endured, and strong language prevents the hearer from assuming "there's an out." Rate: **the strongest single text** — the scripture self-describes God's pedagogical framing of truth.
- **Milk before meat; figurative scripture.** God teaches "concepts that you can process," then "as your understanding grows you're going to be ready for... something a bit more deeper," explicitly because some realities (e.g. existence outside of time) are beyond mortal comprehension "without God bestowing... some greater intelligence."[^5] Parental threats and Santa Claus are everyday analogs. Rate: coherent extension of the truth-as-effect thesis.
- **Developmental accommodation in law: Moses, slavery, tithing, divorce.** God "suffered" imperfect/lesser practices because people "weren't ready" for a higher law — "you can't jump from zero to 100."[^6] The law of Moses was a "lesser law"; biblical slavery was tolerated (with "treat your slave as your brother" undercutting it) to "reach more people"; tithing is a lesser law than consecration; divorce a present-day allowance.[^7] Joseph Smith's 1844 presidential plan to buy slaves' freedom is read as a higher law society rejected by killing him.[^8] Rate: a wide-ranging application; it doubles as a theodicy of changing commandments.
- **Maximal pleading (D&C 43:24-25).** "How often would I have gathered you... by the voice of thunderings... tempest... earthquakes..." shows God exhausting strong, even alarming, language to provoke repentance — paralleled with Jacob 5's "what more could I have done for my vineyard?"[^9] Rate: corroborates that God's rhetoric is calibrated to behavioral effect.
- **Tie to prophetic fallibility.** The same accommodation logic lets God call imperfect leaders and tolerate imperfect doctrine "at different times" because it still moves people toward Him — Shad's mission-president story (contradictory counsel to different missionaries) and his own forgotten-sacrament story illustrate it. See [[Prophets Are Fallible Not Divine]].
- **Steelman the critic.** Accommodation can become an unfalsifiable escape hatch: any uncomfortable or later-revised teaching gets relabeled "figurative" or "milk," so the framework risks immunizing every scriptural difficulty (the worry the vault frames in [[The Threshold Problem - Where to Draw the Literal-Figurative Line]]). A critic (e.g. on slavery) will say "God *tolerated* an evil for effect" is itself a moral cost, not a clean defense. And a skeptic can press that "God says X for effect though reality is not-X" is hard to distinguish from God speaking falsely — Shad's reply (it leads to *correct* behavior, hence counts as truth) only works if his consequentialist definition of truth is granted.

## Best Quotes
> "God actually... shares us a spiritual mystery... 'My works have no beginning and end. I'm only using this language for you so you can understand a bit better... but you're not there yet.'" [^1]

> "It is written endless torment... wherefore it is more express than other scriptures, that it might work upon the hearts of the children of men, altogether for my name's glory." (paraphrasing D&C 19:6-7) [^3]

> "God suffered them to live a lesser law temporarily, because that was what would [be] most optimal for them in becoming like him... You can't jump from zero to 100." [^6]
