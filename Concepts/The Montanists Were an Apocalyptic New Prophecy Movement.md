# The Montanists Were an Apocalyptic "New Prophecy" Movement

## The Point

The Montanists were a second-century Christian movement that took eschatological frustration in the direction of *hastening* the end through fresh prophetic revelation. [[Nicholas J. Frederick]] presents them as a group "who would challenge the role of text and tradition in the early Church." [^1] [[Montanus]] emerged from Phrygia (modern Turkey) in the AD 160s, declared himself a prophet, and was accompanied by two prophetesses, **Maximilla** and **Prisc(ill)a**, who "viewed themselves as key figures in the unfolding apocalypse." [^1] [[Tertullian]] later associated himself with the movement, and his eschatology is "perhaps... most notable" for that connection. [^1]

## Evidence

1. **Montanus claimed to be a passive vessel of the divine.** "Man is like a lyre, and I flit about like a plectron [a pick]; man sleeps, and I awaken him; behold, it is the Lord who changes the hearts of men." [^1]
2. **The prophetesses pinpointed the end.** Maximilla declared "after me there will no longer be a prophet, but the end," while Prisc(ill)a reportedly had a dream revealing where the Second Coming would occur. [^1]
3. **The New Jerusalem had an address: Pepouza.** In an oracle attributed to Prisc(ill)a, Christ "came to me in a bright robe... and revealed to me that this place is holy, and that it is here that Jerusalem will descend from heaven." The "new Jerusalem" was located at the Phrygian town of **Pepouza**, where adherents were urged to follow "strict ethical practices in preparation for the descent of Jerusalem from heaven." [^2]
4. **The role of the women is debated.** Frederick flags scholarly discussion (Anne Jensen; Christine Trevett) of "the importance of these two prominent females within the Montanist movement," and notes uncertainty over whether Tertullian's eschatology preceded or was indebted to the Montanists. [^3]

## Best Quotes

> "Maximilla claimed that 'after me there will no longer be a prophet, but the end.'" [^1]

> "Having assumed the form of a woman... Christ came to me in a bright robe and put wisdom in me, and revealed to me that this place is holy, and that it is here that Jerusalem will descend from heaven." — oracle attributed to Prisc(ill)a [^2]
