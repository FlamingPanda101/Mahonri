# My God and Your God - Jesus Shares Our Relationship to God

## The Claim

In [[John 20.17|John 20:17]] the risen Jesus says, "I ascend unto my Father, and your Father; and to my God, and your God." He puts himself in the *same relationship* to God that his disciples have — implying the Father is Jesus' God in the same sense he is the disciples' God, i.e., Father and Son are distinct beings. [^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The parallel construction.** "My Father / your Father; my God / your God" lays the two relationships side by side. The natural inference: as the Father is the disciples' God, so he is Jesus' God — Jesus is in the creaturely-relational posture toward the Father, not identical to him. (Evidence: strong as plain reading; "he is laying out the relationship.") [^1]
2. **Reinforced by the cross-cry.** [[Matthew 27.46|Matthew 27:46]]'s "my God, my God" independently shows Jesus has a God. Two texts, one picture: Jesus relates *to* God. [^2]
3. **Death-blow framing.** Hansen and Barnes treat these as among the hardest texts for same-being Trinitarianism: a being cannot stand in the "my God" relation to itself. [^3]
4. **Steelman.** Orthodoxy reads "my God" as spoken by the incarnate Son *according to his assumed humanity*: as true man, the Son rightly worships and depends on the Father, while remaining, as to his deity, consubstantial. The Greek even preserves a subtle distinction (some note Jesus does not say "*our* Father/God," keeping his sonship unique). So the verse shows the reality of the incarnation, not a denial of deity. (Evidence: a coherent and ancient reading; the non-"our" point is textually real and cuts both ways.) The LDS reply: the qualifier "according to his humanity" is the recurring nature-indexing device the [[The Who or What Test for the Word God|who/what test]] targets, and the most economical reading is that the Father is genuinely the God the Son relates to. [^4]

## Best Quotes

> "Go and tell the others that I am returning to my father, to your father, to my God, and your God. ... in the same way that he is your God and your father, he is also my God and my father. He's literally laying out the relationship." [^1]

> "These are like death blows." [^3]
