# Covenant Relationship Over Theology Quiz

## The Claim
[[Joe Heschmeyer]] frames salvation as getting God *philosophically right*: "if you think the wrong thing about God in your brain, that's the thing that sends you to hell" — hence Mormons' alleged idolatry over Adam-God damns them. The panel responds that LDS soteriology is about *covenant relationship*, not passing a theology exam.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The Adam-God material is deep speculation, explicitly non-salvific (rated: strong, doctrinal).** [[Jasmine Rapley]]: Adam-God is "what we call deep doctrine, but really it's not doctrine — it's something the church today disavows." It is "such a tangential thing" that staking the faith's coherence on it "doesn't compute" for Latter-day Saints. The missionary discussions begin with "is there a God, is he real, does he love you, is there a plan" — never with Adam-God.[^2] See [[Doctrine Is Defined by the Unified Voice of the Highest Authorities]].

2. **Brigham Young himself treated the nature of deity as non-salvific (rated: strong, primary source).** [[Brigham Young]], Feb. 8, 1857: "Whether Adam is the person that we should consider our heavenly father or not... I do not care for one moment how that is... for in either case we are one species of one family, and Jesus Christ is also our species." He further allowed that prophets across dispensations would show "a great variety in their views" on deity "without direct revelations from the Lord."[^3] The point: for Brigham, worshiping Jesus and living the covenant — not metaphysical precision — was the thing.

3. **Covenant, not ontology, is the LDS "game" (rated: moderate, theological).** [[Jacob Hansen]]: "Their game is a theology game; our game is a covenant-relationship game." The ontological model (three beings vs. one being in three persons, two natures, etc.) "doesn't really matter that much... fishermen could get it. You don't need a degree in theology to understand the hypostatic union." What mattered to Jesus was a loving covenantal relationship with God, treating others rightly, and building the kingdom.[^4] See [[Orthopraxy over Orthodoxy]] and [[Identity of Jesus Is Not a Theology Quiz]].

4. **Worship of the wrong-named God is non-fatal under this frame (rated: moderate).** Hansen and [[Luke Irwin]]: "as long as you're worshiping Jesus, you got it." This is why LDS "don't have a heart attack" over differing ontological models. *Steelman:* a classical theist responds that this is exactly the disagreement, not a refutation — if the LDS "God" is a different *being* (finite, embodied, one of many), then "worshiping Jesus" may be worshiping a different referent, and Heschmeyer's idolatry charge stands on his own premises (see [[Worshiping the Right Jesus - Created vs Uncreated]]). Moreover, the NT does contain doctrinal boundary-markers (e.g., 1 John on confessing Christ), so "orthopraxy over orthodoxy" is a matter of *emphasis*, not the elimination of doctrinal content. The LDS claim is that the emphasis is biblically primary, not that doctrine is irrelevant.

## Best Quotes

> "Their game is a theology game. Our game is a covenant-relationship game — which is, by the way, just freaking way cooler."[^4]

> "I do not care for one moment... whether we are to consider him our God or whether his father or his grandfather, for in either case we are one species of one family, and Jesus Christ is also our species." — Brigham Young, 1857[^3]

> "Fishermen could get it. You don't need a degree in theology to understand the hypostatic union. It doesn't matter — he didn't care."[^4]
