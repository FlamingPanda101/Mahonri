# Calvinism Is Consistent but Built on a Single Bad Premise

## The Point
A distinctive feature of this breakdown is its refusal to strawman: the panel repeatedly insists [[John Calvin]] "wasn't unintelligent" and that [[James White]] is "quite consistent in his theology — it's just abhorrent." The analytical move is to grant Calvinism's *internal logic* and relocate the error *upstream* to a single shared premise — creation **ex nihilo** plus a maximally-defined ("superlative / neo-Platonist superhero") God. Given that premise, omniscient foreknowledge, divine authorship of every choice, the absence of free will, unconditional election, limited atonement, and irresistible grace all follow honestly. Calvinism is therefore not an aberration to be mocked but the most *consistent* development of a corrupt starting point — which is exactly why it is so useful to Latter-day Saints as a cautionary case.

## Evidence
1. **The consistency is conceded, even praised.** "You can't blame him for being unintelligent. James White is quite consistent in his theology. It's just abhorrent." Calvinists "pride themselves" on consistency, and the panel grants it. [^1]
2. **The premise that does the damage.** "If God creates everything out of nothing, then you and your will and your choice were created by God. If God knows the future... then he knows all the choices you're going to make before you ever make them. So, do you have free will? No." From small, plausible seeds "John Calvin grows Calvinism, and it isn't illogical." [^2] See [[Creation Ex Nihilo Leads to Determinism]].
3. **Charity toward the apostasy.** "If I were in the depths of the apostasy like John Calvin and all I had was the Bible, then... we got to build on this thing. This is all we got." Calvin (and the Catholic tradition) "did a very good job considering what they've been given." The takeaway is gratitude for the Restoration, not contempt. [^3]
4. **Calvinism as the *consistent* outlier.** 97% of Christians recoil and route around the conclusion, but the panel argues they do so *inconsistently* — keeping the premise while dodging its output — whereas Calvin "just happened to choose predestination as the thing that he was going to base his whole theology around" and followed it to the end. [^4] See [[Calvinism Is the Logical Conclusion of Classical Theism]].

## Best Quotes
> "You can't blame him for being unintelligent. James White is quite consistent in his theology. It's just abhorrent." [^1]

> "From those small seeds, John Calvin grows Calvinism, and it isn't illogical. It isn't inconsistent. It's actually quite logical and consistent." [^2]

> "If I were in the depths of the apostasy like John Calvin and all I had was the Bible... some of these people have done a very good job considering what they've been given." [^3]

## LDS Connection
This is the steelman that makes the [[The Negative Distance Test for a Broken Theology|negative-distance test]] bite: precisely *because* the deduction is valid, the absurd conclusion convicts the premise. Latter-day Saints reject creation ex nihilo in favor of creation *ex materia* and uncreated intelligences ([[Uncreated Intelligences Make Free Love Possible]]), which is why the same logical machinery yields a loving God and genuine agency rather than determinism. The panel frames the whole exercise as evidence for the Restoration: Calvinism is "kind of the only other consistent option," and seeing where consistency-on-a-bad-premise leads is "thank God for the restoration."
