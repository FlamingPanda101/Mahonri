# The Light Manifests Itself — Perspicuity of Scripture

## The Claim
[[Anthony Rogers]] argues Scripture is **formally sufficient** — sufficiently *clear* (perspicuous) — to communicate the words of life and salvation, against the Catholic charge that Scripture is "a mute book, a dead letter, a wax nose." Because Scripture is "full of life, light, and saving power," and light by definition illuminates, the word *gets through* to those born of the Spirit. Hard passages exist but can be resolved by comparing Scripture with Scripture; failure to understand lies in the blind reader, not the light.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

### 1. Scripture is light, fire, and life — rate: moderate
Rogers cites Deut 30:11–14 ("not too difficult for you, nor out of reach"), Jeremiah 23:29 ("like fire ... like a hammer"), John 6:63 ("the words I've spoken are spirit and life"), Habakkuk 2:2 ("make it clear so that the one who reads it may run").[^1] These establish *accessibility* as God's intent. **Steelman of Horn:** intent-to-communicate ≠ guaranteed clarity on *every salvation-relevant doctrine for every reader*; the Westminster claim is the stronger one (even "the unlearned ... may attain a sufficient understanding"), which is what Horn attacks. See [[Perspicuity Fails the Hard-Cases Test]].

### 2. "Have you never read?" presupposes clarity — rate: moderate
Rogers argues Jesus' repeated rebuke "Have you never read?" and John 10's "I told you and you didn't believe ... because you are not my sheep" prove the fault is unbelief, not obscurity.[^2] This is a fair reading of those pericopes. **Steelman of Horn:** Jesus' interlocutors were resisting His *identity*, a first-order clear doctrine Horn *concedes* is perspicuous; the disputed cases are second-order (baptismal regeneration, the Eucharist, perseverance) where sincere believers — not the "blind" — disagree.

### 3. 2 Timothy 3:16 and the inspiration-to-clarity link — rate: weak as stated
Rogers leans on [[2 Timothy 3.16-17|2 Tim 3:16]] (via the Vulgate's *inspirata*) and on many "from God" texts. **Steelman of Horn (strong):** inspiration entails *truth*, not *clarity*; and [[2 Peter 3.15-16]] explicitly says parts of Paul are "hard to understand" and twisted "to their own destruction" — Scripture's *own* admission that obscurity has fatal stakes. Rogers' rejoinder: "*some* things" ≠ most things, so the bulk is clear and the obscure parts are resolved by clearer ones.

### 4. The principal cause of disagreement is *not* sola scriptura — rate: framing
Rogers' deeper claim: sects (JWs, Mormons, Unitarians) disagree precisely because they "hearken to the voice of others" rather than truly going by Scripture alone; and even committed Protestants can inadvertently add to the text. See [[Noetic Effects of Sin Explain Interpretive Disagreement]].

## Best Quotes
> "Light is by very definition that which illuminates, makes clear. It comes to and gets through to God's people, those born of the spirit."[^1]

> "If a blind man doesn't see the light, there's nothing wrong with the light. It's something wrong with his eyes."[^2]

> "On the whole, scripture is clear and the occasional hard things can be understood by comparing scripture with scripture."[^3]

> "Jesus said ... 'Have you never read?' The implication ... is, if you've read it, then you'd understand it."[^2]
