# Yahweh El Shaddai Is a Throne-Name Preserved in the Book of Mormon

#type/claim

## The Claim
[[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)|Dave Butler]] argues that the title **"Lord God Almighty"** — which back-translates to the Hebrew **Yahweh El Shaddai** — is a *throne-name* that recognizes the divine family (Father, Mother, Son) at God's throne. The name does **not** occur in the Old Testament, but appears **four times in the Book of Mormon and four times in Revelation**, and in nearly every case it surfaces specifically at a *throne scene* or a *judgment scene*. Because [[Shaddai Is the Breasted God and Points to the Mother|Shaddai is the "breasted" (mother) name]] and the throne itself is part of the iconography of the divine woman, Butler reads these occurrences as the Book of Mormon preserving a name and a throne-theology that the [[Hezekiah Josiah and the Deuteronomist Reforms|Deuteronomistic reform]] deleted from the Hebrew Bible. [^1] [^2]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The name is absent from the Old Testament but present where the throne is.** *Yahweh El Shaddai* as a fixed compound title is not found in the Masoretic text; it shows up in the Book of Mormon and Revelation — both texts saturated with throne and temple imagery. Revelation, Butler notes, "is entirely a temple vision," and "Lord God Almighty" appears there in the presence of the throne (cf. [[Revelation 1.8|Revelation 1:8]]). *Rating: the distribution is real and suggestive; the inference depends on the throne-name premise.* [^2] [^3]
2. **Three of the four Book of Mormon occurrences are throne or judgment scenes.** In Lehi's opening vision ([[1 Nephi 1.8|1 Nephi 1:8]]) Lehi sees God on the throne and "one descending," then cries "Lord God Almighty" — read as Father, Mother (the throne), and Son together. The other passages (in Jacob's and Nephi's writings) occur at *judgment*, where the speaker addresses Yahweh El Shaddai either pleading for or acknowledging just judgment. *Rating: the throne/judgment clustering is the strongest part of the case.* [^4] [^5]
3. **The fourth occurrence is a judgment-execution.** Butler places the remaining occurrence at the condemnation and execution of a man "hanged from a tree" (the tree then cut down), where "Lord God Almighty" is again invoked — another judgment context, fitting the proposed pattern. *Rating: coherent with the thesis; the specific reference is given loosely from memory.* [^6]
4. **The Greek qualification (Revelation).** Butler concedes the Revelation data is softer: the Hebrew-to-KJV correspondence between *Shaddai* and "Almighty" is one-to-one, but once Greek is involved (the Septuagint renders *Shaddai* variously, sometimes *pantokratōr*) the back-translation is less tight, so he declines to "lean too hard" on Revelation. *This is a built-in steelman: he flags the weakest link himself.* [^3]

**Steelman of the skeptical counter.** A critic answers that (a) "Lord God Almighty" is a perfectly ordinary KJV phrase Joseph Smith could lift straight from the English Bible (it occurs in Revelation in the KJV he knew), so its appearance in the Book of Mormon needs no Hebrew throne-name; (b) the back-translation to *Yahweh El Shaddai* is the apologist's reconstruction, not something in the text; (c) throne and judgment language is exactly where any author would reach for a high divine title, so the "clustering" is expected, not evidential; and (d) the loose, from-memory citation of the passages invites confirmation bias. Butler's reply is the *convergence*: the title is absent from the OT yet appears in two independently throne-soaked texts, and the [[Shaddai Is the Breasted God and Points to the Mother|Shaddai = mother]] reading supplies an independent reason the name belongs at the *family* throne.

## Best Quotes
> "There is a sacred name of God that does not appear in the Old Testament but appears four times in the Book of Mormon and… four times in the book of Revelation, and that is Lord God Almighty… it's Yahweh El Shaddai." [^1]

> "When you understand that part of the iconography of the divine woman is that she is the throne, God on the throne and one descending is the whole family of God — father, mother, son." [^4]

> "Yahweh El Shaddai appears to be a throne name of God, and we don't see it because the throne of God is basically written out of the Old Testament." [^6]
