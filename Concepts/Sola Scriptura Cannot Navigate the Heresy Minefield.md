# Sola Scriptura Cannot Navigate the Heresy Minefield

## The Point
The creedal Trinity is not a single doctrine so much as a narrow ridge picked out between a ring of condemned errors — **modalism/Sabellianism** (the persons collapse into one), **Arianism** (the Son is a creature), **tritheism** (three gods), **partialism** (each person is a part of God), **subordinationism**, and **Apollinarianism** (Christ lacks a human mind). Orthodox precision was built over centuries by an *ecclesiastical body* defining terms beyond the bare text. A Protestant who derives the Trinity *from the Bible alone* has no such map, so — the episode argues — he "will inevitably commit all those heresies." The flub is therefore diagnosed as structural, not personal: the Bible underdetermines the metaphysics, and *sola scriptura* removes the only instrument that could navigate it.

## Evidence
1. **The thesis stated plainly:** "if you rely on the Bible alone for understanding the Trinity, you're going to inevitably commit all those heresies that he did." A Catholic or Orthodox debater, "engrossed in the mind of the fathers," would not have committed the Apollinarian heresy.[^1]
2. **The questions are unanswerable from the text:** "how many minds does Christ have, a human mind? You cannot even come close to answering that through the Bible." The claimed "strength of the epistemic systems of orthodoxy and Catholicism is that they... don't go to the Bible alone."[^2]
3. **The mechanism — an interpreting body:** liturgical churches "have the ability to reveal or to color in inside the lines"; for the Protestant "it's literally just your own view... There's no ecclesiastical body that's relaying information to you or clarifying."[^3]
4. **It is not a criticism of the Bible but of a job it was never given:** "you're not even criticizing the Bible. You're just saying they're asking the Bible to do something that it doesn't do."[^4]

## LDS Connection
The point cuts two ways for Latter-day Saints. **First, as a tu quoque against Protestant sola scriptura:** the conservative critic who says "just focus on scripture" is using a tool that, on the speakers' showing, cannot even keep its own champion out of heresy — so demanding the LDS prove the Godhead "from the Bible alone" is a standard the demander's own tradition fails. **Second, the LDS exit is different from the Catholic/Orthodox one:** rather than an *interpreting magisterium* coloring inside the lines, Latter-day Saints appeal to *continuing revelation* and a *literal* reading of the Father–Son texts, so the metaphysical minefield never has to be threaded — there is no one-substance puzzle to protect (see [[The LDS Godhead Sidesteps the Named Heresies]], [[Understanding the Trinity Is Not Required for Salvation]]).
