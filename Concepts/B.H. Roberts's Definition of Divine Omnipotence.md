# B.H. Roberts's Definition of Divine Omnipotence

#type/claim

## The Claim

If the classical, "philosophical" definition of omnipotence — *there are no (or no non-logical) limits to what God can do* — is what generates the problem of evil, then [[David Paulsen]] (following [[BH Roberts]]) urges we should **revise the definition** to fit the inspired text. Roberts proposed that God's omnipotence be understood as **"the power to bring about any state of affairs consistent with the natures of eternal existences."** [^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Why a revision is warranted.** [[Lehi's Theodicy - Opposition in All Things|Lehi's theodicy]] (2 Nephi 2) implies "there are apparently states of affairs that even God, though omnipotent, cannot bring about": He cannot bring about joy without righteousness, righteousness without moral freedom, or moral freedom without an opposition in all things ([[2 Nephi 2.11]]). So the inspired text already presupposes a non-classical omnipotence. [^2]
2. **Roberts's formula.** God can do anything **consistent with the eternal natures of things that He did not create** — co-eternal matter, intelligences, and "laws of eternal and self-existent principles." This is not a *weakening* of God so much as a denial that "logically/metaphysically impossible-given-eternal-natures" counts as a thing to be done. [^3]
3. **The "instrumentalist" payoff.** On this view we "can coherently adopt an instrumentalist view of evil wherein pain, suffering, and opposition become means of moral and spiritual development. God is omnipotent, **but He cannot prevent evil without preventing greater goods or ends** — the value of which more than offsets the dis-value of the evil: soul-making, joy, eternal (or godlike) life." [^4]
4. **The cost (steelman of the critic).** A classical theist will say this "solves" evil only by *shrinking God* into a finite craftsman constrained by co-eternal stuff he must work around — trading the problem of evil for a limited deity (see [[Finite Theism Is the Honest Answer to the Problem of Evil]], and the cost flagged in [[Co-Eternality Solves the Problem of Evil]]). Paulsen accepts this trade as the price of dissolving the contradiction.

## Best Quotes

> "God's omnipotence [is] the power to bring about any state of affairs consistent with the natures of eternal existences." — B. H. Roberts, as cited by Paulsen [^1]

> "God is omnipotent, but He cannot prevent evil without preventing greater goods or ends." [^4]
