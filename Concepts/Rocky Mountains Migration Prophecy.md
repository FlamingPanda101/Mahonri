# Rocky Mountains Migration Prophecy

## The Claim
[[Joseph Smith]] is reported to have prophesied that the Saints would be driven to the Rocky Mountains and become "a mighty people in the midst of the Rocky Mountains." Versions are dated to a Montrose, Iowa statement of 6 August 1842 (recorded in his history); a [[Wilford Woodruff]] account dated to 26 April 1834; and Brigham Young's recollection that Joseph said the Saints "will go to the mountains." The Saints did migrate to the Salt Lake Valley in 1847.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Multiple independent attestations.** The host cites a fair (FAIR) article noting the prophecy traces to the biography of [[Anson Call]] (August 1842), with Benjamin F. Johnson and "many people years after" also recalling Joseph discussing the Rocky Mountains. This breadth makes him think "it's very likely that Joseph Smith did prophesy... that the saints would go to the Rocky Mountains." See [[Multiple Attestation - Corroborating Apocryphal Sources]]. (Evidence rating: relatively strong for the *Nauvoo-era* kernel.)[^2]
2. **The 1834 date is the weak link.** The Woodruff "fill the Rocky Mountains" version is a later reminiscence (1898 conference report) claiming he first heard it in 1834. The host: "whether or not that was said in 1834 is where I think maybe that could have been added in." See [[Retrofitting a Prophecy After the Fulfillment]]. (Evidence rating: the early date is suspect; the Nauvoo kernel is not.)[^3]
3. **Self-fulfillment.** "The saints could always migrate to the Rocky Mountains... it's not an impossible future event. They were able to fulfill that prophecy." A people told they will go west, who then go west, do not validate a supernatural forecast. See [[Self-Fulfilling Prophecy Objection]]. (Evidence rating: strong deflation of the *evidential* force.)[^4]
4. **Weak corroborating affidavits.** Paulina Eliza Phelps (the "[[Eliza Will Go to the Rocky Mountains Prophecy]]") recalled at age 5 in 1832 hearing she would go to the Rocky Mountains, recorded c. 1903; the host is "skeptical of these sorts of affidavits" given childhood memory distortion. (Evidence rating: weak.)[^5]
5. **Steelman of the believing view.** Brigham Young claimed "hundreds of people in this house are my witnesses who heard Joseph say... they will leave here and go to the mountains" — a public appeal to many living witnesses. The host concludes he has "no doubt that Joseph probably said" the Saints would go west; the genuine question is only the *specificity* and *early dating*.

## Best Quotes
> "I prophesy that the saints would continue to suffer much affliction and would be driven to the Rocky Mountains."[^1]

> "Hundreds of people in this house are my witnesses who heard Joseph say... the saints will leave Nauvoo... and go to the mountains." — Brigham Young[^4]

> "It's very likely that Joseph Smith did prophesy or state that the saints would go to the Rocky Mountains."[^2]
