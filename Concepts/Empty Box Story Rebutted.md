# Empty Box Story Rebutted

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
A widely circulated critical anecdote holds that Joseph assembled his followers, opened a **box that was empty**, and when they said "Brother Joseph, we do not see the plates," he set them to praying for over two hours until "they were now persuaded that they saw the plates." The story is used to argue the Eight Witnesses saw nothing real and were psychologically pressured into "seeing" [^1].

## The Rebuttal

### 1. The source chain is anonymous and degrading
The earliest version is **Governor [[Thomas Ford]]'s *History of Illinois* (1852)**, sourced only to "men who were once in the confidence of the prophet" — no names. The episode traces how later authors inflated this: Harry Beardsley applied it to the Three Witnesses; [[Fawn Brodie]] in *No Man Knows My History* upgraded the source to "Joseph's key men" and called it "one of the most plausible descriptions"; Edmund Wilson then cited Brodie [^2][^3]. *Rating: this is a strong source-criticism point — an anonymous late report inflated by repetition.*

### 2. It contradicts the signed testimony
The Eight Witnesses' published statement says they "saw and hefted" the plates and "did handle [them] with our hands" — flatly incompatible with an empty box. To accept the empty-box story "you need to discard all of the evidence... which is the exact story that you can find in the front of your Book of Mormon and signed by the witnesses themselves" [^4]. *Rating: valid as far as it goes, though it weighs a signed group statement against a hostile anecdote without independent adjudication.*

### 3. The line of transmission, summarized
"An anonymous source to Joseph's key men to the Eight Witnesses. That's the line of sources" — each step adding specificity the original lacked [^4].

## Steelman (Opposing View)
A critic notes that **the Eight Witnesses' own statement and the empty-box story are not the only data** — John Whitmer's "supernatural power / spiritual eyes" language (see [[John Whitmer Spiritual Eyes Objection Rebutted]]) is sometimes read as partial corroboration that the experience was less straightforwardly physical than the printed testimony implies. Ford, though late, was a sitting governor with access to disaffected insiders, and the story's persistence reflects that the witness pool was overwhelmingly Smith family and close associates.

## Best Quotes
> "The box is empty they said, 'Brother Joseph, we do not see the plates.'... The disciples began to pray for more than two hours, at which time they looked again at the box and they were now persuaded that they saw the plates." [^1]
