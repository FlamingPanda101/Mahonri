# 1QIsaiah-a Aligns With the Masoretic Text Under 92.5 Percent

## The Claim

The popular apologetic figures for the Great Isaiah Scroll — "word for word identical" ([[Wes Huff|Wes Huff]]), "more than 95%" identical, and "almost letter for letter" — are all wrong. [[Dan McClellan]], counting the variants in the published critical edition, puts the actual word-level alignment of [[Donald W. Parry|1QIsaᵃ]] with the Masoretic Text at **fewer than 92.5%**. The differences in that gap are *textual* (meaning-bearing) variants, not spelling — see [[Orthographic and Textual Variants Are Separate Lists in DJD 32]].[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The arithmetic.** Masoretic Isaiah contains 25,608 words. McClellan says he counted 2,771 variants listed for 1QIsaᵃ in DJD 32, of which **1,943 (70%)** are differences specifically from the Masoretic Text. Every such variant is at least one word; several are two or more, "up to 19 words." Even charitably assuming one word per variant gives 92.5% alignment — "but the actual number is lower than that," because multi-word variants pull it down. Hence: 1QIsaᵃ "matches the Masoretic Text in fewer than 92.5% of the words."[^1] *(Evidence rating: strong — a transparent count from the critical edition; the "fewer than" framing is conservative because it under-counts multi-word divergences.)*

2. **"More than 95%" is false.** The "word for word identical with our standard Hebrew Bible in more than 95% of the text" figure (quoted from older apologetics) is rejected outright: "it is not more than 95% of the time, it is less than 92.5% of the time."[^2] *(Evidence rating: strong — directly contradicts the cited number with the editorial count.)*

3. **The "letter for letter" claim for 1QIsaᵇ also fails.** The companion claim that the *second* Cave 1 Isaiah scroll (1QIsaᵇ) is "almost letter for letter" with the Leningrad manuscript is likewise false. DJD 32: "1QIsaᵇ exhibits 183 textual variants in addition to the 161 differences in orthography from other preserved witnesses to the Masoretic family; thus previous descriptions of the near-identity of 1QIsaᵇ with M need to be tempered." McClellan: the "5% variation… mainly variations in spelling" gloss is "entirely false" — the 7.5%-plus textual variation between 1QIsaᵃ and MT "includes no variations in spelling."[^3] *(Evidence rating: strong — again sourced to the edition; note this datum names 1QIsaᵇ specifically.)*

4. **"That's still an A-minus" begs the question.** McClellan anticipates the reply that ~92% is "still a lot." His retort: that assumes "some clear idea of where the threshold is between an acceptable alignment with MT and an unacceptable alignment with MT — I would love to see someone tell me where that threshold is." The apologetic rhetoric needs a fixed pass/fail line that does not exist.[^4] *(Evidence rating: a fair methodological point — the persuasive force of "95%" depends on an unstated standard.)*

5. **The error is shared and inherited.** Huff said "word for word identical"; Ortlund and the [[Norman Geisler|Geisler]] & Archer source said "more than 95%." McClellan: "as usual, Geisler and Archer is incorrect," and the apologists are "just repeating things they have heard from other apologists." See [[Orthographic and Textual Variants Are Separate Lists in DJD 32]].

**Steelman of the apologetic reading:** A ~92% word-level match across a roughly 1,000-year manuscript gap *is* a genuinely impressive testimony to careful scriptural transmission, and McClellan concedes the generic point ("nobody's going to dispute that… we should all say wow"). The objection is not that the scrolls fail to corroborate Isaiah's antiquity but that *specific quantified* claims ("word for word," ">95%," "letter for letter") are measurably false and are deployed "to achieve specific apologetic rhetorical goals."

## LDS Relevance

This concept disciplines LDS use of DSS Isaiah from the other direction. The Latter-day Saint argument that Book of Mormon Isaiah preserves authentic ancient variants ([[Book of Mormon Isaiah Matches the Dead Sea Scrolls]]) depends on the Qumran and Masoretic Isaiah texts genuinely *differing* in meaning-bearing ways — which McClellan's ~7.5%-plus textual gap establishes. But the same rigor warns against LDS apologists overstating in the opposite direction: the honest frame is a real-but-minority body of meaning variants, counted from DJD 32, not a slogan. Precision is the apologetic asset here, not a liability.
