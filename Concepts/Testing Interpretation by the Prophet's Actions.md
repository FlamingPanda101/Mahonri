# Testing Interpretation by the Prophet's Actions

## The Claim
We can recover what [[Joseph Smith]] *meant* by a contested text not only from words but from deeds: "we can check it by his actions." Where a man's behavior systematically contradicts a proposed reading of his words, that reading is probably not his.[^1]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **Actions disambiguate ambiguous text (rated: strong, methodological).** [[Don Bradley]] applies this to the "cursed as pertaining to the priesthood" phrase: if it meant a racial priesthood bar, Joseph would have withheld the priesthood from Black men. He did the reverse — ordaining [[Elijah Abel]]. "Didn't Joseph Smith deprive African American men of the priesthood? ... the answer would be actually no."[^1] See [[Joseph Smith Ignored Race-Based Scriptural Distinctions]].

2. **A converging behavioral pattern, not one data point (rated: strong).** Bradley stacks multiple independent actions — Black ordinations, the 1831 pro-intermarriage revelation, missions to the Lamanites, the Kirtland Temple prayer for "Hottentots," anti-slavery advocacy, Black suffrage, the presidential emancipation plan — all pointing the same direction. Convergence from independent behaviors is stronger than any single inference. *Steelman:* the method has a selection-bias risk — a defender can foreground the anti-racist actions while bracketing or minimizing harder data (e.g., racially charged language elsewhere in 19th-century LDS sources, or the disputed status of the 1831 revelation). The inference is only as good as the completeness of the behavioral record surveyed, and Bradley acknowledges other scholars (Casey Griffiths, Scott) have gone deeper.

3. **Consistent with "fruits" epistemology (rated: moderate).** This dovetails with judging a prophet by results rather than by an idealized superhuman standard — see [[A Prophet Is Not an Academic Translator]] and [[Judge the Book of Abraham by Its Fruits]].

## Best Quotes

> "We can check it by his actions, right? ... Something that someone might ask is, 'Didn't Joseph Smith deprive African American men of the priesthood?' And the answer would be actually no."[^1]

> "Joseph Smith actually consistently ignores apparent race-based distinctions in ancient scripture."[^2]

> "He's thinking of it with a totally different mindset than the people around him would see it."[^3]
