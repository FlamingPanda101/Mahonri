# Spirit-Guidance of Every Individual Is an Inefficient Model of Inspiration

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
[[John Calvin]]'s self-authentication implies the Holy Spirit guides *each individual believer* to recognize the canon, bypassing any need for an infallible external church (see [[Self-Authenticating Canon - Feel It in Your Bones]]). [[Joe Heschmeyer]] rebuts this as a "weird model of infallibility and inspiration" [^1].

## The Rebuttal

### 1. It privatizes infallibility absurdly — rate: strong
On Calvin's model the Holy Spirit declines to guide "the external visible church" and instead guides every Christian severally: "I'm infallible and you're infallible and so is everybody else." Heschmeyer calls this "a very inefficient way for the Holy Spirit to work" [^1]. **Steelman:** Reformed theology does not claim each believer is *personally infallible*; it claims the Spirit's testimony reliably produces recognition in the elect collectively. **But** that retreat to the *collective* is exactly the concession that reintroduces a corporate, church-like authority — see [[Self-Authentication via Church Consensus Backs Into the Catholic Model]].

### 2. It contradicts the observed pattern of how people come to faith — rate: strong
If the Spirit individually authenticates the canon to each reader, conversion to belief in Scripture's inspiration should be largely unmediated. In fact "for most people, the reason they came to believe the Bible is inspired is because somebody else told them" — true "for nearly all Christians throughout history." The mechanism Calvin describes is not the mechanism anyone actually experiences [^2].

### 3. It is a poor fit with the visible-church language of the NT — rate: moderate
The Catholic alternative is that the Spirit guides the church to *recognize* (not create) inspired books, which fits 2 Timothy's image of the church as "the pillar and ground of the truth" better than a model of millions of independently-infallible readers. See [[Authorization - Divine Designation Required for an Infallible Canon]] [^2].

## Best Quotes
> "That is a weird model of infallibility and inspiration and Holy Spirit guidance that the Holy Spirit's not going to guide the external visible church. He's going to guide the individual Christians. This is a very inefficient way for the Holy Spirit to work." [^1]

> "The church isn't infallible. I'm infallible and you're infallible and so is everybody else." [^1]

> "The reason they came to believe the Bible is inspired is because somebody else told them. I think that's probably true for nearly all Christians throughout history." [^2]
