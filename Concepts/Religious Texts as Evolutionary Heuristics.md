# Religious Texts as Evolutionary Heuristics

## The Point

[[Bret Weinstein]] reframes religious scriptures as **heuristics** — simplifying rules that let people make adaptive decisions they could not compute directly. All three interlocutors converge: such stories may genuinely have been fitness-enhancing, but because they are "responsive to past environments," they cannot be trusted as timeless on faith.

## Argument (steelman)

1. **What a heuristic is.** Weinstein: to drive a car you don't model quarks; you use the heuristic "vehicle." Heuristics vary in quality (the periodic table is good; gravitational potential energy is "crappy" in irregular cases) but are indispensable for agents with limited information.[^1]

2. **The proposal.** "What if these religious texts are heuristics through which most people simplify calculations they are in no position to do?" If a religious heuristic raised well-being on average, Harris would "worry much less about them" — which is why he doesn't think about religions whose casualties he never sees.[^2]

3. **Concrete test case: masturbation/reproduction.** The three work through why a "God who cares if you masturbate" could be a fitness-enhancing adaptation: the taboo channels physiological drive toward earlier mating and reproduction. The "answered prayer" of penitent self-examination is likewise explained mechanistically, "no supernatural explanation required."[^3]

4. **The evolutionary corollary that undercuts faith.** Weinstein presses the bitter pill: if the stories are functional adaptations, they are "responsive to past environments," so "the claim that these things might be timeless would be suspect." All agree.[^4]

5. **Useful fictions vs useful truths.** Harris's punchline: "Useful fictions have to be retired at a certain point; useful truths stay true... you want to be making sense for the right reason," because a heuristic blind to the actual variables (e.g. germ theory hidden in dietary law) "comes crashing down" once the real variables are known.[^5]

6. **Peterson's qualifier.** The most profound texts elevate *the process of updating knowledge* above any particular dogma — "the process by which your knowledge is updated has to occupy a position in the hierarchy of values that supersedes your reliance on dogma." See [[Free Speech as the Corrective to Dogmatism]].[^6]

## Best Quotes

> *(Weinstein)* "What if these religious texts are heuristics through which most people simplify calculations that they are in no position to do?"[^2]

> *(Harris)* "Useful fictions have to be retired at a certain point. Useful truths stay true... you don't want it making sense for the wrong reason."[^5]
