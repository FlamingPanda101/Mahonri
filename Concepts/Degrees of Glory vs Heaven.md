# Degrees of Glory vs Heaven

## The Claim
Latter-day Saints define **salvation** differently from most Christians, which dissolves several grace/works and afterlife objections. "Salvation" (resurrection + forgiveness, by grace alone) is distinct from **exaltation** (degrees of glory).

## Supporting Evidence
- "Salvation, which is by grace alone, Jesus Christ's grace will save the wicked and the righteous. Salvation saves us from death... and it saves us from sin... But that doesn't mean you're going to heaven" — there are different degrees of glory *(speaker's doctrinal statement)* [^1].
- The thief on the cross: Jesus said "today thou shalt be with me in **paradise**" — and paradise is not the same as the Father's presence, since the risen Christ later tells Mary "I have not yet ascended to my Father." Paradise ≠ final heaven [^2].

## Best Quote
> "To us, salvation, which is by grace alone... saves us from death. We will all be resurrected, and it saves us from sin... But that doesn't mean you're going to heaven... we believe in different degrees of glory." [^1]
