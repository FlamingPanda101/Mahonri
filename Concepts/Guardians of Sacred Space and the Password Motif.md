# Guardians of Sacred Space and the Password Motif

#type/claim

## The Claim
Across ancient cultures, **hybrid guardian-figures protect sacred space and admit only those who give the correct password** — a pattern the hosts read as the ancient backdrop to the temple's sentinels, keywords, and veil. The **Sphinx** stands before the Giza pyramids as a guardian of sacred space; "the cherubim, the seraphim, the Sphinx — they're all the same thing… guardians of sacred space," set (like Eden's cherubim with the flaming sword at the tree of life, Genesis 3:24) to "ward off those that are not worthy to come in." [^1] The *password* dimension is the second half: in the **Book of Gates**, the sun-god passes twelve night-gates each guarded by a serpent that can only be passed by giving "the proper name" of the guardian; [^2] the Greek **Oedipus** myth makes the Sphinx's riddle function as "a password, a keyword, to be able to get past the Sphinx to sacred space"; [^3] and Butler adds a Scandinavian scholar's reading of the burning-bush "**Ehyeh asher ehyeh**" ([[Exodus 3.14|Exodus 3:14]]) as "a password being given to Moses, so that Moses will go repeat it to the Israelites to prove he knows their God." [^4] The hosts land the parallel explicitly: temple **sentinels** are "guards… and in order to get through the sentinels to the sacred space of the celestial room… you have to give the keywords." [^3]

## Supporting Evidence (steelman)

1. **Apotropaic guardian-figures are a genuine ANE pattern.** Sphinxes, cherubim (Mesopotamian *lamassu*-type winged hybrids), and serpent-guardians demonstrably mark thresholds of sacred/royal space across Egypt and the Near East — the Eden cherubim "to keep the way of the tree of life" (Genesis 3:24) is the biblical instance. *Rating: solid comparative-religion footing.*
2. **The gate-password motif is attested, not invented.** The Book of Gates / Amduat tradition really does require the deceased to *name* gatekeepers to proceed through the night-hours; the Oedipus riddle really does gate passage past the Sphinx. So "name/word as access-token at a guarded threshold" is a documented ancient idea, not a retrojection from LDS practice. *Rating: the sources support the structure.*
3. **It connects to the vault's spatial-holiness material.** The guarded-threshold logic dovetails with [[Gradations of Holiness Structured the Ancient Temple]] and [[Chancel Screens and Veils Demarcating Sacred Space]], situating the claim in a broader, better-sourced case about graded sacred space. *Rating: coheres with stronger adjacent notes.*

**Steelman of the skeptical counter.** (a) **Archetype, not transmission**: guardians-at-thresholds and secret-words-for-entry are near-universal folkloric/ritual archetypes (the riddle-at-the-gate, the shibboleth, the doorkeeper-deity) found far beyond Egypt; their recurrence proves a common human ritual grammar, not a line of descent into the LDS endowment — the [[Avoiding Parallelomania Between Ancient Rites and LDS Temple|parallelomania caution]] is acute here. (b) **The Exodus-3 "password" reading is one scholar's interpretation**, not the plain sense; "I AM THAT I AM" is more usually read as a name/self-identification of God than as a literal access-code, so building a temple-keyword parallel on it is interpretively loaded. (c) **Equating cherubim, seraphim, and sphinxes flattens distinct beings** with different forms and functions; the identification is thematic, not exegetical. A Latter-day Saint reply: the claim is modest and *typological* — that the temple's guardian/keyword grammar is anciently at home, "very familiar space for people who are endowed" — and the convergence of guardian *plus* required word at the threshold (not guardians alone) is the specific, less-generic feature being pointed to.
