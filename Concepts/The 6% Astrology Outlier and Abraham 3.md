# The 6% Astrology Outlier and Abraham 3

#type/claim

## The Claim
Among the surveyed beliefs, **astrology** scored near zero across all clergy ("nobody really believes in astrology"), with non-Christians highest at ~33% and a residual ~6% elsewhere.[^1] The panel jokingly claims that lonely 6% for Latter-day Saints and non-Christians, pivoting to the Book of Abraham's cosmology ([[Abraham 3.25|Abraham 3]]) and the recurring observation that the "non-Christian" category likely absorbs Latter-day Saints and skews Burge's data.[^2]

## Supporting Evidence

1. **The astrology data point (primary).** Astrology is the one item where belief is uniformly low and where non-Christians (~33%) outscore all Christian clergy; the panel pegs the cross-tradition residual believers at "6% ... us, the Latter-day Saints and the non-Christians."[^3] *Rating: the 6% is the hosts' playful gloss, not a precise reported figure.*

2. **Book of Abraham cosmology (LDS doctrinal hook).** The hosts pivot from "astrology" to LDS astronomy: "We need to lean into Abraham chapter 3... Abraham chapter 2 and 3 and 4."[^4] [[Abraham 3.25|Abraham 3]] presents a hierarchy of governing heavenly bodies culminating in Kolob and a council of intelligences — a *theological astronomy*, distinct from horoscopic astrology. *Rating: the conflation of "astrology" with Abraham's astronomy is a joke, but it surfaces a genuine LDS distinctive.*

3. **The "Ephraim is the bull / Taurus" riff (cultural).** "We're the bull. Ephraim's the bull, guys" — alluding to the biblical blessing of Joseph/Ephraim ("his horns are like the horns of unicorns/wild ox," Deut. 33:17) — with hosts joking about being a "Taurus" and a "double bull."[^5] *Rating: humor, not doctrine; but it ties LDS Ephraimite identity to the astrological sign motif.*

4. **The "non-Christian" category absorbs Latter-day Saints (the serious sub-claim).** [[Ryan Burge]] is described as repeatedly flagging that Latter-day Saints "throw off all his data" because surveys often bin them as "non-Christian," a "very broad and vague" category.[^6] LDS respondents may be the "positive markers" pushing up odd non-Christian numbers. *Rating: a real and well-documented classification problem in survey research on American religion.*

**Steelman of the opposing view.** The astrology result is most likely just that — clergy across traditions reject astrology as superstition, and the elevated non-Christian figure reflects the genuine diversity of that residual category (New Age, spiritual-but-not-religious, etc.), not Latter-day Saints. Treating Abraham 3's astronomy as vindication of "astrology" equivocates between two unrelated things: a revealed cosmology of governing stars vs. divination from star-signs. The serious point (LDS being mis-binned as non-Christian) stands on its own and does not need the astrology joke to support it.

**Context.** This concept captures the episode's comedic coda, but it embeds a substantive methodological caveat: when a survey's categories cannot cleanly place Latter-day Saints, *any* cross-tradition comparison involving the "non-Christian" bucket should be read with care — reinforcing the broader [[Survey Data Is Unreliable Objection]] without conceding the between-group [[Variance Between Groups Is Real Signal Rebuttal|variance argument]].
