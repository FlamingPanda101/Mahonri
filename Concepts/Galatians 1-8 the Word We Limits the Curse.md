# Galatians 1:8 — The Word "We" Limits the Curse

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)
Critics quote [[Galatians 1.8|Galatians 1:8]] as a verse aimed at *messengers*: any angel — i.e. [[Angel Moroni]] — who brings a gospel is automatically accursed, so the Restoration is condemned.[^1]

## The Rebuttal

### 1. Paul includes himself ("though *we*") — evidence rated strong
The verse reads "though **we**, or an angel from heaven." [[Ward Radio]] points out that Paul places himself, an apostle, under the same hypothetical curse. If the criterion were the *source* of the message, Paul would be conceding he could become an "antichrist" preacher — absurd for someone evangelicals regard as writing the infallible, "God-breathed" word. Therefore the disqualifier cannot be the messenger.[^2] This is a clean grammatical observation directly in the text; rating: **strong**.

### 2. The test is the *content*, not the messenger — strong
Because both "we" and "an angel" are bracketed by "any other gospel," the operative variable is the gospel preached, not who preaches it. Paul is establishing the gospel itself as the fixed standard against which even apostles and angels are measured. See [[Definition of Gospel]].[^3] Rating: **strong** (follows from the sentence structure).

### 3. Angels are ordinary divine messengers — strong
Scripture routinely has angels deliver God's word (Gabriel to Mary, the angel to the shepherds, and notably [[Revelation 14.6|Revelation 14:6]]). Treating "an angel brought it" as disqualifying would indict the Bible itself. See [[Revelation 14-6 Angel With the Everlasting Gospel]].[^4]

### Steelman of the opposing view
The strongest critic reading: Paul deliberately escalates to the *most authoritative conceivable* source ("an angel from heaven") precisely to say *no* source, however exalted, may overturn the once-delivered gospel — and an angelic visitation introducing new scripture is exactly that kind of overturning. **Response:** this steelman actually concedes the rebuttal's core — the rule is about *not contradicting the gospel's content*. It only condemns Moroni if one first assumes the Restoration's gospel contradicts Paul's, which begs the question (see [[Galatians 1-8 Latter-day Saints Preach Pauls Gospel]]).

## Best Quotes
> "Did you catch the the the we? We? So, wait a minute. So, when evangelicals quote this ad nauseam, are they saying that Paul would be capable of preaching an antichrist gospel? Because that's what Paul's saying." [^2]

> "Even here, Paul is saying, 'Hey, even if I say the wrong thing, I'm not the authority on truth. I'm an apostle, but if I say the wrong thing, it's still the wrong thing.'" [^5]

> "Though we, or an angel from heaven, preach any other gospel unto you than that which we have preached unto you, let him be accursed." [^1]
