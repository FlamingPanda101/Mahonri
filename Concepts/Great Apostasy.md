# Great Apostasy

## Overview

The **Great Apostasy** is the Latter-day Saint claim that, after the deaths of the apostles, the church Christ established lost priesthood authority and key ordinances and teachings, creating the need for a [[Great Apostasy and Need for Restoration|Restoration]] rather than a mere reformation. This is an overview/hub note for a large existing cluster; see the linked notes for the specific sub-arguments.

## The Claim

Several distinct theses sharpen the concept across the cluster:

- **Authority, not total goodness.** The apostasy is primarily a loss of priesthood keys and authority — [[Apostasy Was Complete When the Keys Departed|complete when the keys departed]] — not a claim that all goodness vanished. The [[Light of Christ]] and faithful believers ([[Medieval Christians Kept the Light of Christ Alive Not Apostates]]) persisted throughout.
- **Gradual, not a single event.** The falling away is best read as [[Apostasy as Gradual Falling Away Not Single Event|a gradual process]], with early symptoms already visible in the New Testament ([[Galatians 1-6 Marvel Ye Are So Soon Removed Early Apostasy]]).
- **Doctrinal corruption via Hellenization.** A major strand argues [[Greek Philosophy Corrupted the Christian Concept of God|Greek philosophy reshaped the concept of God]] — though the vault also steelmans the counter ([[Hellenization Was Not Corruption of an Original Pure Church]]).
- **Honest terminological caveat.** [[Great Apostasy Is Not a Scriptural Term in LDS Standard Works|"Great Apostasy" is not itself a scriptural term]] in the LDS standard works, a point the vault concedes openly.

Common objections — indefectibility, divine foreknowledge guaranteeing the church — are engaged in [[A God-Headed Church Is Guaranteed Against Corruption by Divine Foreknowledge]] and [[Daniel 2's Stone Kingdom Dates Indefectibility to the Roman Empire]].
