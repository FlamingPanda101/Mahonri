# None Beside Me Is an ANE Boast of Supremacy

## The Claim (Objection Rebutted)

Verses like "there is no god besides me" ([[Isaiah 43.10|Isaiah 43]], 44–46) are pressed as flat denials that any other divine being exists — strict numerical monotheism. [^1]

## The Rebuttal

1. **It's a known ANE idiom of supremacy.** As [[Michael Heiser]] and other scholars note, "none besides me" was a well-attested boast of *incomparability/preeminence*, not a metaphysical census. Assyrian kings issued decrees declaring "there is no one besides me, I am the greatest" — without believing other kings didn't exist. (Evidence: well-supported by Heiser's work and ANE royal-inscription parallels.) [^1]
2. **Heiser's Packers analogy.** Like a Green Bay Packers fan saying "there's no team but the Packers" — hyperbolic supremacy, not literal denial of other teams. [^2]
3. **The internal control: Zephaniah's irony.** [[Zephaniah 2.15|Zephaniah 2:15]] puts the *identical* boast — "I am, and there is none beside me" — into the mouth of a *city* (Nineveh). Since no one thinks the prophet means there are no other cities on earth, the formula is plainly idiomatic; the biblical authors are *deliberately* copying the foreign kings' boast and even using it ironically. (Evidence: strong — this is an in-text demonstration that the formula is idiom, arguably the single best argument in the cluster.) [^3]
4. **Steelman.** The monotheist replies that Isaiah 40–48 is not mere royal bombast but sustained *theological* polemic: it mocks idols as nothing, declares Yahweh alone foreknows and creates, and uses "there is none else" in argumentative contexts that go beyond a boast of rank toward genuine exclusivity. Even if the *form* is an ANE idiom, Deutero-Isaiah may be *repurposing* it to assert something stronger than the Assyrian kings meant. (Evidence: a serious reading; many scholars locate the emergence of something close to monotheism precisely in Deutero-Isaiah.) The LDS reply: the idiom's pedigree plus Zephaniah's ironic reuse shows the *default* sense is supremacy, and the surrounding affirmations of a divine council (e.g., [[Deuteronomy 32.8-9|Deut 32:8-9]], [[Psalm 82.6|Ps 82]]) keep Israel's religion an *inclusive* monotheism. [^4]

## Best Quotes

> "As Michael Heiser points out ... this is a well-known boast of supremacy, not a denial of the existence of anyone else. The ancient kings in Assyria would actually write decrees which would say ... there's no one besides me, I'm the greatest." [^1]

> "They're talking about a city in Assyria ... 'I am and there is none beside me.' ... Does that mean that they thought that there were no other cities? No. You're confusing an idiom." [^3]
