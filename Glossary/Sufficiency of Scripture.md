# Sufficiency of Scripture

**Definition.** The sufficiency of Scripture is the claim that the Bible contains everything needed for faith and salvation. Debate hinges on a distinction: *material* sufficiency means the needed content is all *contained* in Scripture (explicitly or implicitly); *formal* sufficiency means Scripture is clear and self-interpreting enough to be rightly assembled *without* a living teaching authority. The proof-text is [[2 Timothy 3.16-17|2 Timothy 3:16-17]] ("profitable... that the man of God may be complete").

**Why it matters here.** Many Catholic apologists concede *material* sufficiency but deny *formal* sufficiency (Trent Horn's "Home Depot" analogy: the materials are all there, but you need a builder), making formal sufficiency the real crux. Reformed defenders argue the perspicuity texts establish both, so Scripture alone equips for "every good work."
