# Predestination

**Definition.** Predestination is the doctrine that God determines beforehand the eternal destiny of individuals. In its Calvinist (Augustinian) form it is *unconditional*: God elects some to salvation and (in "double predestination") passes over or reprobates the rest, irrespective of foreseen merit or choice. Arminians soften it to election *conditioned* on God's foreknowledge of faith. The term must be distinguished from the LDS concept of pre-mortal *foreordination*, which assigns roles and opportunities without overriding agency.

**Why it matters here.** Predestination is the load-bearing target of this library's critique of classical theism: arguments hold that an omniscient creator who makes ex nihilo and decrees all things thereby fixes every outcome, so unconditional predestination follows — and a God who predestines the damned is the author of their sin. Defenders answer with Molinist "middle knowledge" or compatibilism; the library argues these do not actually dissolve the determinism.
