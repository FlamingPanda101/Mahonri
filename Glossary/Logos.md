# Logos

**Definition.** ***Logos*** (Greek λόγος, "word, reason, account") is the title the Gospel of John gives the pre-existent Christ: "In the beginning was the Word... and the Word was God" (John 1:1), who then "became flesh" (John 1:14). The term carried freight in both Jewish wisdom traditions and Greek (Stoic, Middle-Platonic) thought, where the *logos* is the rational principle mediating between God and the world. John deploys it to present Jesus as the divine agent of creation and revelation.

**Why it matters here.** How "the Word was God" is read divides the traditions. Trinitarians take it as consubstantial deity; critics in this library argue John presents the Logos as a distinct, subordinate divine figure who mediates God's presence (an "image christology") rather than as the one God himself. The Logos is also central to whether John teaches the [[Preexistence of Christ|preexistence of Christ]].
