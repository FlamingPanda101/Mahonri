# Latter-day Saints

**Definition.** Members of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, the restorationist church founded by [[Joseph Smith]] in 1830. The terms "Latter-day Saint" and "LDS" denote both the people and the tradition, which holds that Christ's original church fell into apostasy and was restored through Joseph Smith, with the Book of Mormon as new scripture, restored priesthood authority, temple ordinances, and a living prophet. The Church asks that "Mormon" and "LDS" be used sparingly in favor of its full name.

**Why it matters here.** "LDS" recurs throughout this vault as the theological vantage point from which sources are engaged. The central apologetic dispute is whether this is authentic restored Christianity or a distinct new religion: critics often deny it is Christian, while Latter-day Saints argue their Christ-centered scripture and worship place them within, not outside, the faith.
