# Compatibilism

**Definition.** The view that free will and causal determinism are *compatible*: an act can be both fully determined by prior causes and free, provided it flows from the agent's own desires and is not externally compelled or coerced ("soft determinism"). On this account, "freedom" means acting in accordance with one's own will, not possessing the libertarian power to have done otherwise with the past held fixed. It is the chief rival to both libertarianism and hard determinism.

**Why it matters here.** Compatibilism does double duty in these debates. Theologically, Reformed/Calvinist thinkers use it to reconcile meticulous divine sovereignty (or exhaustive foreknowledge) with human responsibility — God determines all, yet agents act "freely." Critics in this vault charge that compatibilist freedom is too thin to ground *desert* (praise, blame, just punishment), so it cannot rescue God from authoring evil, and that even some atheists (e.g. Harris) grant a compatibilist freedom while denying it supports retributive responsibility. The LDS position rejects compatibilism in favor of libertarian agency.
