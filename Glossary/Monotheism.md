# Monotheism

**Definition.** Monotheism (Greek *monos*, "single," + *theos*, "god") is the belief that exactly one God exists and that no other gods are real. In its strict, philosophical form it is tied to claims of God being unique, uncreated, and ontologically incomparable. Many biblical scholars argue this strict sense is a relatively late development and that earlier Israelite texts are better described as monolatrous or henotheistic, using "incomparability" rhetoric ("there is none like me") rather than flat denials of other beings' existence.

**Why it matters here.** Critics press strict monotheism (e.g., Isaiah 43-44) against the LDS plurality of divine persons. Latter-day Saints and some scholars reply that the negation texts assert Yahweh's supremacy and incomparability, not the non-existence of all other *elohim*, and that "monotheism" functions as a modern identity marker read back onto the text.
