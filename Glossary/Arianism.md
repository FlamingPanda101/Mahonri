# Arianism

**Definition.** Arianism is the fourth-century teaching of Arius, a presbyter of Alexandria, that the Son is a created being — the highest creature, through whom God made all else, but not eternal and not of the same substance as the Father ("there was a time when he was not"). It was condemned at the Council of Nicaea (325), which answered it with the term *homoousios*, and the controversy dominated the church for decades afterward.

**Why it matters here.** "Arian" is often used as a polemical label against any view that subordinates the Son or denies consubstantiality, including LDS Christology. The vault notes that the term doesn't fit cleanly: Latter-day Saints affirm the Son is fully divine and uncreated as to His intelligence, unlike Arius's created Logos, and that pre-Nicene "subordinationist" theology was widespread and not simply Arian heresy.
