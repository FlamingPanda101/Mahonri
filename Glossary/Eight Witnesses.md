# Eight Witnesses

**Definition.** Eight men — four Whitmers, three Smiths, and Hiram Page — who signed the second testimony printed in the Book of Mormon. Unlike the Three Witnesses, they reported a plain, daylight, *physical* encounter: Joseph Smith showed them the plates, and they saw and "hefted" them and handled the leaves, which had "the appearance of ancient work." No angel and no heavenly voice are claimed.

**Why it matters here.** The Eight supply the tactile counterpart to the Three, so that the two testimonies together close off opposite objections: the Three cannot be dismissed as merely handling a mundane object, and the Eight cannot be dismissed as mere visionaries. Critics argue the divergence instead means the Eight were simply shown a fabricated prop; defenders note that an object detailed and heavy enough to satisfy eight close examiners is itself hard to explain naturalistically, and that none ever recanted.
