# Naturalism

**Definition.** The view that nature is all there is — that everything that exists is part of the physical/causal order and there are no supernatural agents or realities. Two senses must be distinguished. **Metaphysical (ontological) naturalism** is the *creed* that matter and its fields are the only things that exist. **Methodological naturalism** is merely a *working rule* for inquiry: science investigates only material, repeatable causes and brackets the supernatural, without thereby denying it. Conflating the two underwrites the false inference that doing science commits one to atheism.

**Why it matters here.** The distinction is a load-bearing apologetic move: a believer can accept evolutionary biology and the scientific method (methodological naturalism) while rejecting the metaphysical creed. Plantinga's evolutionary argument targets the metaphysical version. Notably, some hold Latter-day Saint metaphysics (an eternal universe, no creation *ex nihilo*, a finite God, "spirit matter") sits an unusually short step from naturalism.
