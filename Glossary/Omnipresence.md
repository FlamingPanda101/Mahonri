# Omnipresence

**Definition.** The attribute of being present everywhere (Latin *omnis* "all" + *praesens* "present"). In classical theism God is present to all of space not as a body occupying it but by sustaining every existing thing in being and by his knowledge and power reaching all places without spatial extension. The presence is whole and undivided at every point, since a simple, immaterial God has no parts to distribute.

**Why it matters here.** Latter-day Saint theology affirms that the Father has "a body of flesh and bones" (D&C 130:22) and is therefore spatially located, which appears to conflict with bodily omnipresence. LDS thinkers resolve this by locating ubiquity in the Spirit or "light of Christ" that fills the universe, achieving the *function* of omnipresence without the Father's body being everywhere. Critics argue an embodied, located God who needs an auxiliary medium to be present everywhere is a finite being *within* the cosmos, not its transcendent ground.
