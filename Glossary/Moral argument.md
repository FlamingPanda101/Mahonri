# Moral argument

**Definition.** An argument that infers God's existence from the reality of objective moral values and duties. In its standard contemporary form (associated with William Lane Craig) it runs: (1) if God does not exist, objective moral values and duties do not exist; (2) objective moral values and duties do exist; therefore (3) God exists. Proponents distinguish *ontology* (what grounds morality in reality) from *epistemology* (how we know it) and from *semantics* (what moral words mean), insisting the argument concerns grounding, not whether atheists can be good or know right from wrong.

**Why it matters here.** It targets the metaethical foundations of secular ethics, pressing critics either to ground objective morality or to admit they are borrowing it. Craig deploys it as two conditional premises, so it bites even if atheism is true. Both creedal and LDS apologists use it; debate centers on the Euthyphro dilemma and whether evolution can supply an objective standard.
