# Incarnation

**Definition.** The **Incarnation** (from Latin *in carne*, "in flesh") is the Christian doctrine that the divine Son of God took on a complete human nature and was born as the man Jesus of Nazareth. Its classic proof-text is John 1:14, "the Word became flesh" (*ho logos sarx egeneto*), where the verb *eskēnōsen* ("tabernacled, dwelt") evokes God's presence in the tent of meeting. As fixed at Chalcedon (451), the one person Christ is held to be at once fully God and fully human.

**Why it matters here.** All sides affirm an incarnation, but disagree on its metaphysics. Latter-day Saints frame it as the supreme instance of the [[Condescension of God]]—a god voluntarily descending into mortal limitation—within a descent-and-ascent pattern leading to human exaltation. Trinitarian critics tie the Incarnation to the two-natures formula and argue the LDS picture collapses the Creator–creature distinction it presupposes.
