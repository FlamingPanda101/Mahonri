# Fideism

**Definition.** From the Latin *fides* ("faith"): the position that religious belief rests on faith rather than reason, and in its stronger forms that faith is independent of — or even opposed to — rational evidence and argument. Associated (rightly or as a caricature) with Tertullian, Kierkegaard, and aspects of Pascal and Wittgenstein, fideism treats the demand for proof as misplaced, since the deepest commitments are made by the will or the heart, not established by inference. "Faith grounding faith" is its self-referential ultimate standard.

**Why it matters here.** Fideism is more often a charge leveled than a banner claimed. Apologists across traditions position themselves *against* it: natural theologians seek "a third way between rationalism and fideism," holding we may have good reasons for God short of certain proof. Latter-day Saint debaters likewise resist being read as fideists, insisting a spiritual witness is *evidence* placed alongside reason, not a retreat from it. Critics counter that immunizing belief from disconfirmation collapses into fideism by another name.
