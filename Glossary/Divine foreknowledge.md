# Divine foreknowledge

**Definition.** God's knowledge of what will happen before it happens, especially his knowledge of future *free* human choices ("exhaustive" foreknowledge covers all such events). Three families of view compete: *simple foreknowledge* (God just knows the actual future, often via timeless eternity in which all moments are present to him — the Boethian solution); *middle knowledge* (Molinism, foreknowledge grounded in counterfactuals of freedom); and *denial* of exhaustive foreknowledge (open theism, on which the open future contains no facts to foreknow).

**Why it matters here.** The contested question is whether infallible foreknowledge is compatible with libertarian freedom. The open-theist argument (Ostler): if God knew in 1900 that I would act tomorrow, I would need power to change a settled past fact in order to do otherwise — which I lack — so foreknowledge and free will are incompatible. Defenders reply that knowledge is not causation, or that on timelessness/Molinism the past truth *depends on* the future free act rather than constraining it. The stakes touch prophecy, predestination, and whether God is "sovereign" or a "prisoner" of a fixed script.
