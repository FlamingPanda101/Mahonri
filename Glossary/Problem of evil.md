# Problem of evil

**Definition.** The **problem of evil** is the challenge of reconciling the existence of evil and suffering with belief in a God who is omnipotent, omniscient, and omnibenevolent. Two main forms are distinguished. The **logical** problem (associated with J. L. Mackie) claims these attributes are strictly inconsistent with any evil at all. The **evidential** (or probabilistic) problem concedes formal consistency but argues that the amount and distribution of evil — especially apparently gratuitous or "pointless" suffering — counts as strong evidence against God.

**Why it matters here.** It is the central atheistic argument the vault engages, and the dividing line between traditions. Classical (creedal) theism, holding to creation *ex nihilo* and absolute omnipotence, must explain why an unconstrained God permits evil. Latter-day Saint and "finite theist" thinkers argue that uncreated matter and co-eternal intelligences let God *organize* a fallen world without authoring its evils, dissolving the strongest versions of the problem.
