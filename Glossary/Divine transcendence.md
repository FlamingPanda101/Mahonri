# Divine transcendence

**Definition.** The attribute by which God exceeds and stands beyond the created order — distinct from, and not contained by, the universe, space, or time (Latin *transcendere*, "to climb over, surpass"). In classical theism, transcendence is radical: as the simple, immaterial, timeless source of all being, God is unlike any creature, so ordinary words ("exists," "good," "knows") apply to him only *analogically*. Transcendence is the standard counterpart to *immanence* (God's nearness to and presence within creation).

**Why it matters here.** How far God transcends creation is contested. Classical theists make transcendence near-absolute, requiring analogical or apophatic God-talk and ruling out any shared "kind" between God and creatures. Latter-day Saints hold a more moderate transcendence: God the Father is an exalted, embodied person who shares a *kind* with his children, so language about his love, knowledge, and personhood can be far more univocal — God is supremely *above* us in degree without being wholly *other* in nature.
