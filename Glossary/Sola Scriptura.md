# Sola Scriptura

**Definition.** *Sola scriptura* (Latin, "by Scripture alone") is the Reformation doctrine that the canonical Scriptures are the *sole infallible* rule of faith for the church — uniquely God-breathed, materially sufficient, and clear enough to serve as the final standard over every rival authority. It does not deny that creeds, councils, and tradition have real (subordinate) authority; it denies that any of them is *infallible* or stands alongside Scripture as a second, independent source of revelation.

**Why it matters here.** It is the central fault line between Protestants and their Catholic and LDS critics. Protestants argue Scripture self-attests and is sufficient; Catholics counter that an infallible canon and interpretation require a teaching church, and Latter-day Saints argue an open canon plus living prophets are needed to identify, secure, and apply the word.
