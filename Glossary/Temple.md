# Temple

**Definition.** In the biblical world, the central sanctuary (Israel's Tabernacle, then Solomon's and the Second Temple) where heaven and earth meet, sacrifice is offered, and the high priest enters the divine presence. In Latter-day Saint usage a temple is a dedicated "house of the Lord" — distinct from a meetinghouse — reserved for the highest ordinances: the endowment, eternal marriage and family sealings, and proxy work (including baptism) for the dead.

**Why it matters here.** Much of this vault argues the LDS temple recovers a suppressed First-Temple theology and ritual pattern (divine council, a heavenly Mother, ascent through a veil) that the Deuteronomist reforms and the Great Apostasy obscured. Critics counter that the practices are nineteenth-century constructions, often pointing to Freemasonry; the vault treats temple work as the engine behind eternal families and salvation for the dead.
