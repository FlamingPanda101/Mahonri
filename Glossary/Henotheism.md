# Henotheism

**Definition.** Henotheism (Greek *heis/henos*, "one," + *theos*, "god") is the worship of one supreme deity while accepting that other real gods exist. It differs from monotheism, which denies that any other gods exist, and from monolatry, which stresses exclusive worship without making strong claims about the others' rank. Many scholars describe portions of the Hebrew Bible — with its divine council and "sons of God" — as henotheistic or monolatrous rather than strictly monotheistic.

**Why it matters here.** Latter-day Saints cite this scholarship to argue ancient Israel acknowledged a plurality of divine beings, consonant with the Godhead and exaltation. Critics such as Trent Horn grant the data but argue ancient Israelite henotheism is categorically unlike "Mormon henotheism," because Yahweh was held to be the uncreated, incomparable Creator rather than one exalted man among many.
