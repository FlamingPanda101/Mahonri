# Book of Mormon

**Definition.** The keystone scripture of the Restoration, published by [[Joseph Smith]] in 1830 and presented as his translation of an ancient record engraved on gold plates. It narrates the history of migrant peoples in the Americas — chiefly the Nephites and Lamanites — and is framed as a second witness written to testify of Jesus Christ. Latter-day Saints hold it as scripture alongside the Bible; Joseph said he translated it by the gift and power of God with the aid of seer instruments.

**Why it matters here.** Much of this vault's apologetic material turns on it. Critics argue it is a 19th-century composition betraying anachronisms (horses, steel, Hebraisms drawn from the KJV) and lacking archaeological or DNA support. Defenders point to the witnesses, chiasmus, Nephi's Arabian route (Nahom/Bountiful), and a convergence-not-proof standard for ancient origin.
