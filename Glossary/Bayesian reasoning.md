# Bayesian reasoning

**Definition.** A method of updating the probability of a hypothesis in light of evidence, formalized by Bayes' theorem. One starts with a *prior* probability for a hypothesis, then revises it to a *posterior* in proportion to how strongly the hypothesis predicts the observed evidence relative to its rivals (the *likelihood*). Evidence that is far more expected on one hypothesis than another shifts the balance toward it, even when the prior was low. It supplies a rigorous framework for comparing explanations rather than treating any single fact as decisive.

**Why it matters here.** Apologists increasingly cast the fine-tuning and design arguments in Bayesian terms: a life-permitting universe is astronomically improbable on chance but expected on design, so the posterior for a designer is driven sharply up. Bayesian "stacking" is likewise used to combine independent Book of Mormon anomalies into a cumulative case. Critics reply that priors and likelihoods can be assigned to favor naturalism, and that probabilities for unique events like the whole universe are contestable; the vault stresses weighing God's probability against *all* the evidence.
