# Masoretes

**Definition.** Jewish scribes (roughly 6th–10th centuries AD, centered at Tiberias) who standardized the Hebrew Bible, adding vowel points, accents, and marginal notes (the *masorah*) to a previously consonant-only text. Their product, the **Masoretic Text (MT)**, became the received Hebrew Bible and the base for most modern Old Testament translations. Because vowels were supplied centuries after composition, the Masoretes' vocalization fixed one reading where the consonants alone could allow several.

**Why it matters here.** The MT is one of three witnesses — alongside the Septuagint and the Dead Sea Scrolls — used to reconstruct the original text. Where they disagree, the question of which preserves the earlier reading drives debates over messianic passages, divine plurality, and alleged Deuteronomist edits. LDS and critical scholars argue Masoretic vocalization sometimes obscures older meanings.
