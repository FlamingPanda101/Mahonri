# Masoretes

Jewish scribes (c. 6th–10th centuries AD) who standardized the consonantal text, vocalization, and marginal notes of the Hebrew Bible, producing the **Masoretic Text** — the basis of most modern Old Testament translations. Referenced in this vault's discussions of textual transmission and variants (versus the Septuagint and the Dead Sea Scrolls), and of divine corporeality / anthropomorphism in the Hebrew Bible.
