# Hellenization

**Definition.** Hellenization is the spread of Greek language, culture, and especially philosophy across the ancient Mediterranean and Near East after Alexander the Great, and its absorption into other traditions. In Christian studies, the "Hellenization thesis" (associated with Adolf von Harnack) is the claim that early Christian theology was reshaped by Greek philosophical categories — particularly Platonism — so that doctrines like divine simplicity, immutability, impassibility, and immateriality entered Christian thought from outside the Bible.

**Why it matters here.** This is central to the LDS critique of the creeds. The argument holds that the "creedal God" — timeless, partless, passionless, immaterial — is Plato's "the One" and Aristotle's unmoved mover read back into Scripture, not the embodied, responsive God the Bible portrays. Defenders reply that the Fathers *baptized* Greek vocabulary to protect biblical content, and that Second Temple Judaism (e.g., Philo) was already Hellenized, so "Greek categories were alien to the Bible" is overstated. The live question is whether the Greek frame preserved or distorted the Hebrew data.
