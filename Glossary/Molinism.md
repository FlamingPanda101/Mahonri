# Molinism

**Definition.** A theory of providence named for the Jesuit Luis de Molina (1535–1600) that posits *middle knowledge* (*scientia media*): logically prior to his creative decree, God knows all true *counterfactuals of creaturely freedom* — what any free creature *would* freely do in any possible circumstance. By knowing these and choosing which world to actualize, God secures both exhaustive foreknowledge/providence and genuine libertarian freedom, since he does not cause the choices, only places agents in circumstances whose free outcomes he already knows.

**Why it matters here.** Molinism is the sophisticated middle path between Calvinist determinism and open theism, and apologists offer it as the "best solve" for foreknowledge-plus-freedom. The vault's critique: because God still *selects* which world to actualize, he chooses the precise set of people who will be lost — so the predestination/creation-dilemma problem returns at the level of the decree ("whistling past the graveyard"). Molinists reply that God is constrained by which worlds are *feasible* (Plantinga's "transworld depravity") and actualizes the best feasible balance; others deny the counterfactuals have truth-makers at all.
