# Original Sin

**Definition.** Original sin is the doctrine that Adam's fall corrupted human nature and transmitted to all his descendants both a disordered nature and, in the Augustinian/Western form, inherited *guilt*—so that even newborns stand condemned and need baptismal regeneration. The Greek Fathers and Eastern Orthodoxy typically hold a softer "ancestral sin": humanity inherits the *effects* of the Fall (mortality, a weakened will) but not Adam's personal guilt. The strong Western version rests heavily on a Latin reading of Romans 5:12 (*in quo*, "in whom").

**Why it matters here.** This doctrine is the hinge of the baptism debate: inherited guilt is what makes infant baptism urgent. Latter-day Saints deny it outright—"men will be punished for their own sins, and not for Adam's transgression" (Articles of Faith 1:2)—aligning with the pre-Augustinian Greek Fathers and removing the rationale for paedobaptism in one stroke.
