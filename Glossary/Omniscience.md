# Omniscience

**Definition.** The attribute of being all-knowing (Latin *omnis* "all" + *scientia* "knowledge"). On the classical and "perfect-being" account, omniscience means God knows all true propositions — past, present, and future — including every future free choice (exhaustive divine foreknowledge). A weaker construal limits omniscience to all that is *logically knowable*: God knows every actuality and possibility but, if the future is genuinely open, there are no settled facts about future free acts to be known.

**Why it matters here.** The two readings carry the debate. Classical theists treat exhaustive foreknowledge as essential to divine perfection and the basis of biblical prophecy. Open-theist and many LDS thinkers (e.g. Givens, Ostler) argue that exhaustive foreknowledge of *free* acts is incompatible with libertarian freedom, so a self-limiting God who knows all possibilities — but not a fixed future — is no less perfect. This bears directly on the problem of evil and predestination.
