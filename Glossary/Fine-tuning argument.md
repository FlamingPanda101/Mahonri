# Fine-tuning argument

**Definition.** A modern, cosmological form of the teleological argument. It observes that roughly two to three dozen physical constants and initial conditions (the gravitational constant, the strong nuclear force, the cosmological constant tuned to about 1 part in 10^120, and others) fall within extraordinarily narrow life-permitting ranges. The argument typically runs as a trilemma — the values are due to physical necessity, chance, or design — and concludes that design is the most probable explanation. Increasingly it is framed in explicitly Bayesian terms, where low-prior design is driven up by the fine-tuning evidence.

**Why it matters here.** Often called the strongest contemporary argument for God; even some atheists concede its force. Disputes center on the *multiverse* reply (does positing many universes dissolve the improbability, or merely relocate it?) and the *anthropic* objection (is fine-tuning a mere selection effect?). LDS thinkers connect it to scripture treating physical law as "irrevocably decreed," compatible with a God who works within eternal law rather than creating it arbitrarily.
