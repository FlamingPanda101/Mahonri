# Paedobaptism

**Definition.** Paedobaptism (Greek *pais/paidos*, "child," + baptism) is the technical term for infant baptism—the baptizing of children before a personal profession of faith. It is the historic practice of Catholic, Orthodox, Lutheran, Anglican, and Reformed/Presbyterian churches, justified by appeals to original sin, covenant theology (baptism as the New-Covenant successor to infant circumcision), and the New Testament "household" baptisms. It stands opposed to *credobaptism* (believer's baptism), and the two terms name the two sides of the baptism-subjects debate.

**Why it matters here.** Defenders argue paedobaptism is an apostolic *discipline* implied by baptismal regeneration plus universal need for grace, so the early silence on infants is what one would expect. Latter-day Saints reject it: with no inherited guilt, infants are whole and need no washing, and forcing baptism on them inverts the gospel order of faith-then-baptism.
