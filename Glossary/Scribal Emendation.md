# Scribal Emendation

**Definition.** A change to a text introduced by a copyist or proposed by an editor. As a textual-critical act, *emendation* means correcting a wording judged corrupt — sometimes on manuscript authority, sometimes by *conjecture* where no surviving witness preserves the supposed original. As a description of transmission, it covers the alterations scribes made over centuries, whether accidental (haplography, misreading unspaced text) or deliberate (harmonizing, "improving," or doctrinally adjusting a reading).

**Why it matters here.** Emendation is where transmission and interpretation meet. The vault uses it both ways: to show the canon was reshaped by fallible hands (supporting a Restoration), and as a working tool — e.g., reading 2 John's "elect lady" as a scribal error for "the lady Eklekte," a routine emendation that critics resist only because the text is scriptural. The double standard between biblical and ordinary manuscripts is itself a recurring argument.
