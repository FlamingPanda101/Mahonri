# Dead Sea Scrolls

**Definition.** A collection of roughly 900–1,000 Jewish manuscripts (c. 3rd century BC–1st century AD) discovered from 1947 onward in caves near Qumran by the Dead Sea. They include the oldest surviving Hebrew biblical texts (over a thousand years older than the Masoretic codices), non-canonical works such as 1 Enoch and Jubilees, and sectarian writings of the community that preserved them. They attest a pluriform scriptural landscape before the text was standardized.

**Why it matters here.** The scrolls reshaped textual debate: most biblical texts broadly match later tradition, yet some preserve variant readings agreeing with the Septuagint — or, LDS apologists argue, with Book of Mormon Isaiah against the KJV. The Great Isaiah Scroll's differences from the Masoretic Text are a recurring battleground over how much the text changed.
