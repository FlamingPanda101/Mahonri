# Magisterium

**Definition.** The magisterium (Latin *magister*, "teacher") is the official teaching authority of the Catholic Church — the pope and bishops in communion with him — charged with authentically interpreting the word of God in Scripture and Tradition. Per *Dei Verbum*, it "is not above the word of God, but serves it," and on defined matters of faith and morals it can teach infallibly. Papal infallibility is framed as a *negative* protection from error, not a guarantee that every teaching is the best possible formulation.

**Why it matters here.** It is the Catholic answer to the under-determination critics attribute to *sola scriptura*: only a living, infallible office, the argument runs, can settle disputes "definitively" where Scripture's clarity allegedly fails. Protestants reply that this merely relocates private judgment (one must still decide *which* church is infallible) and concentrates interpretive finality in an institution. LDS apologists make a structurally similar appeal to living prophets instead.
