# Heavenly Mother

**Definition.** **Heavenly Mother** is the Latter-day Saint belief in a divine Mother, the female counterpart to God the Father and a parent of human spirits — the doctrine that humanity has Heavenly Parents, not a Father alone. The teaching follows from the LDS view of an embodied, gendered, anthropomorphic God and of exaltation as eternal family. Within this vault she is also connected to the figure of the goddess **Asherah**, argued to have been worshiped in pre-reform Israelite religion before being edited out by later scribes.

**Why it matters here.** Critics call Heavenly Mother an extra-biblical invention with no scriptural warrant. Defenders argue the canon's silence reflects later suppression rather than original absence, marshaling evidence that a divine consort (Asherah) once stood beside Yahweh, and that a Mother in Heaven is a natural corollary of an embodied God and eternal family.
