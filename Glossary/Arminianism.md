# Arminianism

**Definition.** Arminianism is the Protestant tradition descending from Jacobus Arminius (and developed in the Wesleyan/Methodist stream) that affirms God's sovereignty while preserving libertarian free will in salvation. Against [[Calvinism]]'s TULIP, it holds that election is conditional on foreseen faith, that Christ's atonement is universal in scope, that grace can be resisted, and that believers can fall away. Salvation is thus synergistic — a freely accepted gift — rather than monergistic.

**Why it matters here.** Arminianism marks the nearer Protestant neighbor to LDS soteriology: both reject unconditional reprobation and affirm genuine human cooperation, so LDS apologists sometimes note their reply is "functionally Arminian" on the human side. Two wrinkles recur here: critics of classical theism argue ex nihilo creation and divine foreknowledge make the Arminian's free will incoherent, and the persistent Calvinist–Arminian stalemate is cited as evidence the creeds got God's nature wrong.
