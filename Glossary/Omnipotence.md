# Omnipotence

**Definition.** The attribute of being all-powerful (Latin *omnis* "all" + *potentia* "power"). Standard formulations hold that God can do anything logically possible; doing the logically contradictory (a square circle, a stone too heavy for him to lift) counts as no limitation, since such "tasks" are not coherent states of affairs. Classical theism grounds omnipotence in God as pure actuality and the source of all being — the unconditioned cause on which everything else depends.

**Why it matters here.** Critics deploy omnipotence in the problem of evil (an all-powerful, all-good God should prevent gratuitous suffering) and in the "creation dilemma." Latter-day Saints and finite-theist apologists often reinterpret it: God is *self-limiting*, working within uncreated co-eternal realities (matter, intelligences, eternal law) and respecting agency, rather than being an unconditioned creator *ex nihilo*. Creedal theists counter that a constrained God is no longer the ultimate ground of being.
