# Sola Fide

**Definition.** *Sola fide* (Latin, "by faith alone") is the Reformation doctrine that a sinner is justified before God by faith alone, apart from any meritorious works—works being the necessary *fruit* of true faith but never part of its cause. It is one of the five *solas* and is anchored in Paul (Rom 3:28; Eph 2:8–9). Catholic, Orthodox, and LDS traditions reject it as stated, holding that faith, grace, repentance, and obedient works (including ordinances) cooperate in an ongoing justification—and noting the one biblical occurrence of "faith alone" (Jas 2:24) *denies* it.

**Why it matters here.** *Sola fide* is the engine of the evangelical objection that LDS salvation is "works righteousness." LDS apologists respond that salvation is by grace (siding against works-merit) while denying *alone*: baptism and discipleship are covenant responses to grace, not wages earned—so the debate is whether faith is genuinely solitary or the leading edge of a cooperative, covenant whole.
