# Premortal existence

**Definition.** **Premortal existence** (or pre-existence) is the doctrine that human beings lived as spirits in the presence of God before their mortal birth. In Latter-day Saint theology this is tied to the teaching that the eternal "intelligence" of each person is uncreated, was organized into a spirit child of Heavenly Parents, and participated in a premortal council and the choice to come to earth. Forms of pre-existence also appear in some early Christian and Jewish thought, most prominently in Origen.

**Why it matters here.** Critics argue pre-existence is unbiblical and was condemned as Origenist heresy, and that uncreated human spirits undercut God's status as sole Creator. Defenders cite texts read as implying pre-mortal life (Jeremiah 1:5; Job 38:7; John 9:2), note its presence in the early church, and argue it dissolves problems like the fairness of who hears the gospel and the problem of hell.
