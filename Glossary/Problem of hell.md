# Problem of hell

**Definition.** The **problem of hell** is a focused version of the problem of evil: it asks how a perfectly loving and just God could consign any person to **eternal** punishment. The sharpest forms press the apparent *disproportion* between a finite creature's finite sins and an infinite penalty, and — when combined with creation *ex nihilo* — the charge that God knowingly creates persons He foresees will be damned (the "soteriological" or "creation" problem of hell).

**Why it matters here.** This is a primary front in LDS–creedal debate. Defenders of traditional hell appeal to the infinite worth of the God offended, or to freely-rejected grace ("doors locked from the inside"). LDS responses lean on premortal existence (so no one is created *from nothing* into a fixed destiny), graded "degrees of glory" instead of a binary heaven/hell, postmortem missionary work, and the claim that scriptural "eternal" punishment means *God's* punishment, not unending duration.
