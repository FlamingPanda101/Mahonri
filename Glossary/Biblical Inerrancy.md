# Biblical Inerrancy

**Definition.** The doctrine that Scripture, at least in its original autographs, is wholly without error in everything it affirms — historical, scientific, and theological. In its strong (e.g., Chicago Statement) form it is tied to plenary verbal inspiration; softer forms limit inerrancy to matters of faith and practice. It is a primarily conservative Protestant and evangelical commitment, distinct from Roman Catholic and Latter-day Saint views of Scripture.

**Why it matters here.** The vault treats inerrancy as a fragile, self-imposed liability: because we no longer possess the autographs and the text was demonstrably edited, an inerrant-original claim cannot anchor present faith. Several notes argue the LDS standard is *reliability, not infallibility*, and that words like *theopneustos* ("God-breathed") never meant error-free. Critics counter that without inerrancy Scripture loses its authority — the contested premise here.
