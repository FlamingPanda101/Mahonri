# Sacred Tradition

**Definition.** In Catholic and Orthodox theology, Sacred Tradition is the living transmission of the apostolic deposit of faith — handed on by preaching, practice, and the church's life — distinct from but inseparable from Scripture, and sharing one divine source. It is not merely human custom but, on this view, a mode of God's self-revelation that the magisterium guards and interprets. The Latin slogan is *Scriptura et Traditio* (Scripture *and* Tradition), against *sola scriptura*.

**Why it matters here.** It is the second authority Protestants reject and Catholics affirm. The sharpest apologetic move argues that load-bearing Protestant beliefs — *which* books are canonical, and even *that* "Scripture is the word of God" — are nowhere stated in Scripture and so are themselves deliverances of tradition, making tradition logically prior to confidence in Scripture. Latter-day Saints press a parallel point through living prophetic authority rather than a fixed deposit.
