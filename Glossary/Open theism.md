# Open theism

**Definition.** The view that the future of free creaturely action is genuinely *open* — not yet settled — and therefore not exhaustively foreknown even by God. God is held to be omniscient in that he knows all that is logically knowable (every actuality and every possibility), but since there are no facts yet about undetermined future free acts, his not "knowing" them is no defect. Open theism typically pairs this with a temporal, responsive, passible God who learns and reacts as history unfolds, and it overlaps with process theology's "self-surpassing" God.

**Why it matters here.** Open theism is the position toward which several LDS thinkers (Ostler, Givens) and much of the vault gravitate, because it dissolves the predestination/creation dilemma at its root: a God who does not foreknow the actual future cannot be charged with creating the damned with foresight. Classical theists object that it sacrifices the God of biblical prophecy ("declaring the end from the beginning," Isaiah 46) and reduces God to a temporal learner. Molinism and Boethian timelessness are the main attempts to keep foreknowledge without it.
