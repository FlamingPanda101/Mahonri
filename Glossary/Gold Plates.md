# Gold Plates

**Definition.** The metal record Joseph Smith said he received from the angel Moroni in 1827 and translated as the Book of Mormon — engraved leaves of a gold-colored alloy, bound with rings, with a portion "sealed." Witnesses described them as roughly six to seven inches by eight, several inches thick, and weighing on the order of 40–60 pounds; the "tumbaga" (gold-copper alloy) hypothesis is offered to explain a golden appearance at a liftable weight. Joseph returned the plates to Moroni after translating, so they are not available for examination today.

**Why it matters here.** The plates are the central physical claim of the Restoration. Critics treat their disappearance and the "too heavy / solid gold" objections as fatal; defenders marshal the named witnesses, alloy chemistry, and the cost and skill a forgery would require, arguing the documentary case is strong even without the artifact.
