# Documentary Hypothesis

**Definition.** The theory, classically formulated by Julius Wellhausen, that the Pentateuch was woven from four originally independent sources — J (Yahwist), E (Elohist), D (Deuteronomist), and P (Priestly) — composed at different periods and later combined by redactors. Evidence cited includes divine-name alternation, doublets (two flood or creation accounts), vocabulary and theological shifts, and anachronisms. It remains a foundational model of source criticism, though many scholars now favor revised or supplementary models rather than four clean strands.

**Why it matters here.** Traditionally it threatens Mosaic authorship of the Torah, a stumbling block for biblical literalists. This vault notably runs the opposite way: several notes argue the hypothesis *strengthens* the Restoration, since a layered, editorially shaped Old Testament is exactly what a story of textual loss and prophetic restoration predicts. Critics of that move reply that source criticism is not reducible to the full Wellhausen synthesis.
