# Canonization

**Definition.** Canonization is the historical process by which the church came to recognize *which* writings count as inspired Scripture, forming the *canon* (Greek *kanōn*, "measuring rod, standard"). It unfolded over centuries through usage, dispute, and conciliar judgment rather than a single divine declaration, yielding canons that still differ (e.g., 66 Protestant, 73 Catholic, broader Ethiopian/Orthodox). Catholics frame the church as *declaring* a canon God *determined* (thermometer vs. thermostat); the deuterocanon was contested until Trent.

**Why it matters here.** This is the hinge of the whole *sola scriptura* debate: the inspired table of contents is not itself found in any inspired book, so identifying the canon seems to require an authority *outside* Scripture. Protestants answer that the list is *derived from* the books (recognition is posterior to inspiration); critics reply this still demands naming *which* church recognized *which* list, which is the authority question itself.
