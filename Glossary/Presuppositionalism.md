# Presuppositionalism

**Definition.** A Reformed apologetic method, founded by Cornelius Van Til and systematized by Greg Bahnsen, that denies any neutral common ground between believer and unbeliever. Instead of reasoning *to* God from shared evidence, it argues that the Christian worldview must be **presupposed** as the precondition for intelligibility itself — for logic, induction, and morality. Its signature move is the *transcendental argument*: rival worldviews are refuted by showing they cannot account for the very rationality they use ("the impossibility of the contrary"). Every worldview, on this view, rests on an ultimate, self-authenticating standard.

**Why it matters here.** The method is potent against atheism but contested when wielded in intra-Christian or LDS debate. Critics (including some Latter-day Saints) argue it merely *asserts* its standard and cannot dislodge a rival worldview that is internally coherent, so it stalls against Mormonism. Defenders reply that coherence is necessary but not sufficient — only Trinitarian theism, they claim, truly grounds intelligibility.
