# Restorationism

**Definition.** The conviction that the true New Testament church was lost in an apostasy and must be *restored* — not merely reformed — to its original faith, authority, and ordinances. It names a broad family of 19th-century American movements (the Stone–Campbell "Restoration Movement," the Latter-day Saints, and others), distinguished from Protestant Reformation thought, which sought to correct an existing church rather than re-found it. In LDS usage, "the Restoration" denotes specific restorations: priesthood keys, covenants, the gathering of Israel, and the binding of all dispensations.

**Why it matters here.** Restorationism presupposes a Great Apostasy, so critics who deny a total apostasy thereby deny the need for restoration; some also argue the Book of Mormon's "Protestantism" reflects Joseph Smith's restorationist milieu rather than ancient origin. Defenders frame the Restoration as additive — gathering truth and authority — rather than a simple replacement of a wholly fallen church.
