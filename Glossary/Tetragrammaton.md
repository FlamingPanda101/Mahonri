# Tetragrammaton

**Definition.** From Greek for "the four letters," the personal name of the God of Israel written with the four Hebrew consonants **YHWH** (יהוה), conventionally vocalized "Yahweh." Out of reverence, ancient and later Jews avoided pronouncing it, substituting *Adonai* ("Lord") in reading; English Bibles render it "LORD" in small capitals. Its original vocalization is uncertain because the consonant-only text predates the Masoretes' vowel points, and "Jehovah" reflects a later hybrid pronunciation.

**Why it matters here.** The divine name anchors debates over the identity of Israel's God and of Jesus. LDS theology identifies the premortal Christ as Jehovah/Yahweh; critical scholars trace YHWH's origins to a regional deity later merged with El. New Testament "I am" sayings and Septuagint name-substitutions are read as claims to bear the divine name.
