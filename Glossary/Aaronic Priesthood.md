# Aaronic Priesthood

**Definition.** In Latter-day Saint usage, the lesser of two priesthood orders, named for Aaron the brother of Moses and associated with the Levitical/temporal ministry of ancient Israel — baptism, the administration of the sacrament, and the outward "preparatory" ordinances. The New Testament calls it the priesthood "after the order of Aaron" (Hebrews 7:11); LDS doctrine holds it was restored to Joseph Smith and Oliver Cowdery in 1829 by John the Baptist through the laying on of hands.

**Why it matters here.** Latter-day Saints cite the restoration as evidence Joseph Smith would not baptize or officiate until authority was explicitly conferred, distinguishing his claim from self-appointment; critics reply that sincerity is not authenticity and an angelic ordination cannot be externally corroborated. The Aaronic (priest) / Melchizedek (king) pairing also maps onto the temple's "kings and priests" language.
