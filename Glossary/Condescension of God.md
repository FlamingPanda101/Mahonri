# Condescension of God

**Definition.** The **condescension of God** is the act of a divine being voluntarily descending from glory to a lower, mortal condition — supremely the Incarnation, in which God takes on flesh and human limitation. The phrase is prominent in the Book of Mormon (1 Nephi 11), where Nephi is shown "the condescension of God" in the coming of Christ. The same theme animates classical Christian preaching on the Incarnation, which used paradox and rhetorical contrast to convey the marvel of the infinite God become a helpless infant.

**Why it matters here.** Latter-day Saints read "condescension" as part of a descent-and-ascent pattern: God comes down so that humanity may be raised up ([[exaltation]]), reflecting the couplet "as God now is, man may become." Traditional Christians share the language of divine condescension in the Incarnation but reject pairing it with human deification. The two traditions thus affirm the same term while disagreeing about where the pattern leads.
