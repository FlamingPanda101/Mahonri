# Exaltation

**Definition.** **Exaltation** is the Latter-day Saint term for the highest degree of salvation: to inherit the fulness of God's glory, to live eternally in family relationships, and to become a joint-heir with Christ — in short, to become like God. It is the LDS form of *theosis* / deification, the ancient Christian idea (2 Peter 1:4; Athanasius's "God became man that man might become God") that the redeemed are made partakers of the divine nature. Exaltation goes beyond mere forgiveness to a transformation of the person's very nature and status.

**Why it matters here.** This doctrine draws the sharpest fire from critics, who portray it as "becoming a god of your own planet" and a denial of the unique deity of God. Defenders answer that exaltation is theosis taken to its logical end, that it is grounded in patristic deification language, and that exalted humans remain forever subordinate to and dependent on the Father — not rivals to Him.
