# Canon

**Definition.** From Greek *kanōn*, "measuring rod" or "rule" (originally a straight reed). In biblical studies, the canon is the authoritative list of books a community recognizes as Scripture. Early Christians first used *kanōn* for the "rule of faith" (the standard of orthodox belief) and only later for a fixed catalogue of books; canonization was a gradual, contested communal process rather than a single dated decree.

**Why it matters here.** The basis for canon authority is central to LDS–Protestant–Catholic debate. Protestants typically treat the canon as self-authenticating; Catholics ground it in the Church's authority; LDS apologists argue an open canon and continuing revelation are the older pattern, since no book lists its own table of contents and the limits stayed fluid for centuries.
