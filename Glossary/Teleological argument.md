# Teleological argument

**Definition.** An argument from apparent purpose or design in nature to a designing intelligence. From the Greek *telos* ("end, goal, purpose"), it reasons that ordered, goal-directed features of the world are better explained by a mind than by chance or blind necessity. Classic biological versions (Paley's watch on the heath) point to the contrivance of organisms; modern cosmological versions point to the fine-tuning of physical constants. Today it is most often cast as an inference to the best explanation or a Bayesian probability comparison rather than a strict deductive proof.

**Why it matters here.** It is a centerpiece of natural theology shared across Christian traditions. The vault highlights a distinctively LDS angle: the Book of Mormon prophet Alma states a recognizably teleological argument in roughly 74 BC, which apologists treat as evidence both for God and for the text's antiquity, since the cosmological form of the argument was unknown in 1829.
