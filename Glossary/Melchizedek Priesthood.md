# Melchizedek Priesthood

**Definition.** In Latter-day Saint usage, the higher and greater of two priesthood orders, named for Melchizedek, the king-priest of Salem (Genesis 14:18; Hebrew *melek-tsedeq*, "king of righteousness"). Renamed to avoid repeating the name of Deity, it is properly "the Holy Priesthood after the Order of the Son of God" (D&C 107). It holds the keys of spiritual blessings, the sealing power, and presidency over the Church, and was restored to Joseph Smith and Oliver Cowdery by Peter, James, and John.

**Why it matters here.** Hebrews 7 builds Christ's eternal high-priestly office on exactly this king-priest typology, which LDS apologists read as a recovered order rather than a Joseph Smith invention; critics call the *melek-tsedeq* etymology and bloodline-kingship analogy a post hoc harmonization.
