# Plural Marriage

**Definition.** The nineteenth-century Latter-day Saint practice of a man being sealed to more than one wife (polygyny), introduced privately by Joseph Smith in the 1840s, taught publicly under Brigham Young from 1852, and officially ended by the 1890 Manifesto. Latter-day Saints frame it as a commanded restoration of an Old Testament patriarchal order (Abraham, Jacob) tied to the temple sealing power, not merely a social custom.

**Why it matters here.** This is one of the most attacked points in LDS history: critics cast Joseph Smith as a predator, stressing sealings to other men's wives and to a fourteen-year-old. The vault's defenders argue the documented pattern fits *sealing* rather than sex (no recorded offspring, married pregnant wives, no husband complaints), and that the doctrine of eternal plurality is logically separable from whether Joseph practiced it.
