# Homoousios

**Definition.** *Homoousios* (Greek *homos*, "same," + *ousia*, "substance/being/essence") means "of the same substance." It is the key non-biblical term inserted into the Nicene Creed (325) to affirm that the Son is the same essence as the Father, coined to exclude Arius, who held the Son was created and of a different or merely *like* substance (the rival *homoiousios*, "of like substance"). It later grounded the formula of one *ousia* (essence) in three *hypostaseis* (persons).

**Why it matters here.** This word is the historical fault line. Critics note it is extra-biblical and was imposed amid imperial controversy. Latter-day Saints additionally argue *ousia* is equivocal: "same essence" can mean numerically one being (the creedal reading) or sharing a common nature the way humans share humanity — a reading they extend to deified humanity.
