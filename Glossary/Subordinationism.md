# Subordinationism

**Definition.** Subordinationism is the view that the Son (and often the Spirit) is subordinate to the Father in being, rank, or origin — not merely in role. It ranges from the radical Arian form (the Son is created) to milder pre-Nicene forms held by figures like Justin, Origen, and Tertullian, in which the Son is fully divine yet derived from and ordered under the Father (the "monarchy of the Father"). Nicene orthodoxy later affirmed full co-equality, allowing only a "relational" or functional ordering.

**Why it matters here.** Latter-day Saints argue that some form of subordinationism was the dominant view of the earliest Christian centuries, supporting their reading of the Father as supreme over the Son. Trinitarian critics distinguish acceptable *relational/economic* subordination from condemned *ontological* subordination, and argue the church rightly clarified rather than reversed the earlier language.
