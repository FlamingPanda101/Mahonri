# Plenary Verbal Inspiration

**Definition.** The view that divine inspiration extends to the very *words* of Scripture (verbal), not merely its ideas, and to *all* of it (plenary, "full"), not only its doctrinal portions. On this account God so superintended the human authors that their wording is exactly what He intended; it is the usual basis for strong biblical inerrancy in conservative Protestantism. It is contrasted with "dynamic" or "concept" inspiration, which inspires the message while leaving the wording human.

**Why it matters here.** The vault frames this doctrine as the hidden premise that forces univocal, harmonized readings of a diverse library — for instance, reading later Trinitarian metaphysics back into texts that disagree. If every word is uniformly God's, contradictions and development become intolerable; if inspiration is more accommodating, the Bible can be a chorus of voices. Latter-day Saint and many critical readings reject verbal plenary inspiration for that reason.
