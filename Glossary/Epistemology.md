# Epistemology

**Definition.** From the Greek *epistēmē* ("knowledge") + *logos* ("account, study"): the branch of philosophy that asks what knowledge is, what makes a belief justified, and how we can be warranted in holding it. Classically it studies the structure of justification — how reasons relate to beliefs — and the conditions under which a true belief rises to knowledge. Major positions on that structure include foundationalism, coherentism, and infinitism, framed as answers to the regress of "reasons for your reasons."

**Why it matters here.** Almost every apologetic disagreement is ultimately epistemological: by what standard do you know God exists, that a text is scripture, or that a spiritual witness is reliable? Latter-day Saint, Reformed, and naturalist debaters share evidence yet differ on which faculties (reason, sensation, conscience, the Spirit) count as legitimate sources of warrant, so naming the operative epistemology often dissolves a stalled dispute.
