# Christophany

**Definition.** A **christophany** (Greek *Christos* + *-phaneia*, "appearing") is a pre-incarnate appearance of Christ—the claim that an Old Testament figure who "saw God" or met the Angel of Yahweh actually encountered the divine Son before his birth, not God the Father. It is a species of **theophany** (an appearance of God). The reading is anchored in John 1:18 ("no one has seen God... the only Son has made him known"), taken to mean the visible divine agent of the Hebrew Bible was the Son.

**Why it matters here.** The christophany reading functions in two directions. Traditional apologists use it to harmonize texts where people see God with texts saying God is unseen. Critics (e.g. Dan McClellan) argue it begs the question—assuming biblical inerrancy and retrojecting later Trinitarian categories onto authors who thought they were describing YHWH directly. Latter-day Saints, by contrast, can accept a pre-mortal divine Christ encountered in vision while rejecting the harmonizing motive.
