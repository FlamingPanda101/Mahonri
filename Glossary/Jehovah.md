# Jehovah

**Definition.** **Jehovah** is an early-modern English rendering of the Hebrew divine name **YHWH** (the Tetragrammaton), formed by combining its consonants with the vowels of *Adonai* ("Lord"); scholars reconstruct the original pronunciation as **Yahweh**. It is the personal covenant name of the God of Israel, conventionally represented as "the LORD" in English Bibles. In Latter-day Saint usage **Jehovah** is identified specifically as the premortal Jesus Christ — the God of the Old Testament — as distinct from **Elohim**, God the Father.

**Why it matters here.** The relationship between Yahweh, El/Elohim, and other deities is heavily contested: some scholars argue Yahweh was originally distinct from the high god El before the two merged, which LDS apologists read as supporting a Father–Son distinction within the Old Testament. The LDS Jehovah=Christ equation also figures in debates over the Adam-God teaching and over which divine person speaks in given texts.
