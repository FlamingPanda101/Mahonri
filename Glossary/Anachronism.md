# Anachronism

**Definition.** An anachronism is an element placed in a time period where it does not belong — a thing, word, animal, plant, or technology mentioned in a text before it actually existed in that setting. In Book of Mormon debate the term covers items such as horses, steel, wheat, barley, silk, and chariots that critics say did not exist in the pre-Columbian Americas during the narrative's timeframe.

**Why it matters here.** Critics treat the anachronism list as direct evidence the text is a 19th-century composition. Apologists respond on several fronts: that the list has shrunk as discoveries (e.g., pre-Columbian barley) accumulate, that loan-shifting could rename unfamiliar New World species with familiar Old World words, and that absence of evidence in a poorly excavated region is weak. Both sides agree the count and its trajectory over time are the crux.
