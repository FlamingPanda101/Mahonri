# Limited Geography Theory

**Definition.** The view that the Book of Mormon's events unfolded within a relatively small region — on the order of a few hundred miles — rather than spanning both American continents, with most proponents placing that region in Mesoamerica (southern Mexico and Guatemala). It reads the text's travel times, "narrow neck of land," and references to writing, cities, and calendar as fitting a compact, high-culture setting, and distinguishes the New World Cumorah from the New York hill.

**Why it matters here.** LGT reframes two stock objections: it predicts that Lehi's descendants were a small group absorbed into a much larger existing population (blunting the DNA argument), and it confines the search for archaeology to a bounded area. Critics call it an unfalsifiable retreat from earlier hemispheric readings; defenders argue the limited scale is what the text itself describes and predates the modern DNA controversy.
