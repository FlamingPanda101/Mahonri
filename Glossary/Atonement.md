# Atonement

**Definition.** The **Atonement** is the saving work by which Christ reconciles humanity to God, repairing the breach caused by sin. The English word ("at-one-ment") names the restored union; the New Testament uses terms such as *katallagē* (reconciliation), *apolytrōsis* (redemption), and *hilastērion* (propitiation/mercy seat). Historically there is no single creedal definition: scholars distinguish overlapping models—exemplar/illuminator, recapitulation, *Christus Victor* (ransom), and sacrifice/satisfaction—which early authors freely combined rather than ranked.

**Why it matters here.** The contested question is *how* the Atonement works and whether any one theory is binding. Latter-day Saint writers often treat its mechanism as partly incomprehensible and resist reducing it to penal substitution, favoring relational, indwelling, and *Christus Victor* readings; many evangelical critics make penal substitution the gospel's core. The plurality of ancient models is itself used apologetically to relativize that claim.
