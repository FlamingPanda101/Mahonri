# Form Criticism

**Definition.** The method (German *Formgeschichte*) that classifies the small, originally oral units behind a text by their literary *form* or genre (*Gattung* — hymn, proverb, lament, miracle story, legal formula) and asks for each its *Sitz im Leben* ("setting in life"): the concrete communal situation — worship, preaching, instruction — in which it was used and shaped before being written down. It dominated early-20th-century Gospel and Psalms study.

**Why it matters here.** *Sitz im Leben* is a recurring lever in the vault: a Psalm or speech is read against a reconstructed temple-festival setting, and a passage's "Synoptic" rather than "Johannine" form helps flag it as foreign to its host gospel. The form-critical assumption that anonymous *communities* generated the tradition is also contested here, where some notes argue the texts came from literate networks of individual authors instead.
