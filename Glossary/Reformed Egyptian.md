# Reformed Egyptian

**Definition.** The term the Book of Mormon uses (Mormon 9:32–34) for the script in which its abridged record was engraved — described as an Egyptian-derived writing "altered by us, according to our manner of speech," compact enough to fit on metal plates because Hebrew would have taken more room. It is presented as a modified, evolving script and language rather than a standard known one.

**Why it matters here.** Critics note that no script called "reformed Egyptian" is independently attested by Egyptologists, treating this as a fatal anachronism. Defenders respond that ancient Egyptian was in fact written in successively reformed cursive systems (hieratic, demotic), that Israelite scribes used Egyptian script, and that a small émigré community could naturally develop an idiosyncratic, unattested variant.
