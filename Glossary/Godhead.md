# Godhead

**Definition.** In Latter-day Saint usage, the **Godhead** is the term for the divine triad — the Father, the Son (Jesus Christ), and the Holy Ghost — understood as three distinct, personal beings who are "one God" in perfect unity of mind, purpose, will, and glory rather than one undivided substance. It contrasts deliberately with the creedal **Trinity**, in which the three persons share one numerically identical divine essence (*homoousios*). The English word translates the Greek *theotēs* / *theiotēs* ("divinity, deity") used in passages like Colossians 2:9.

**Why it matters here.** This is the central battleground of the LDS–creedal debate: critics charge that three separate beings is tritheism or polytheism, while Latter-day Saints argue the New Testament depicts unity-of-will, not unity-of-substance, and that the Trinity is a later philosophical construction. The two sides use "one God" in incompatible senses — relational/social unity versus a single shared being.
