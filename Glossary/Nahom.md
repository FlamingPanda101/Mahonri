# Nahom

**Definition.** The place named in 1 Nephi 16:34 where Ishmael is buried and from which Lehi's party turns "nearly eastward" across Arabia. Apologists connect it to the tribal region and place-name NHM (vocalized Nehem/Nihm) in Yemen, attested on Old South Arabian altar inscriptions dated to roughly Lehi's era and located near a known turn in the incense trade route toward the coast.

**Why it matters here.** Nahom is the Book of Mormon's most-cited candidate for a verifiable Old World "hit": a specific name in a specific direction that Joseph Smith could not have read off any map available in 1830. Critics argue the consonants NHM are a common Semitic root, that the inscriptions name a tribe rather than the burial site, and that the eastward turn is geographically loose — so the dispute is over how improbable the convergence really is.
