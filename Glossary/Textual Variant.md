# Textual Variant

**Definition.** A textual variant is any place where manuscripts of the same text disagree in wording. Text critics distinguish *orthographic* variants (spelling and meaning-neutral differences) from *substantive* or *textual* variants (those that change the sense). The Greek New Testament has hundreds of thousands of variants across its witnesses, the vast majority trivial; the discipline of textual criticism weighs them to recover the earliest recoverable reading.

**Why it matters here.** Variant counts are wielded in opposite directions. Skeptics cite huge totals to suggest the text is unstable; apologists reply that abundance of witnesses makes errors *more* correctable, not less. A recurring vault point is that comparing the Great Isaiah Scroll to the Masoretic Text requires keeping spelling variants separate from genuine meaning variants, since conflating them distorts the debate.
