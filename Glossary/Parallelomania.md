# Parallelomania

**Definition.** A term popularized by Samuel Sandmel in a 1962 lecture for "that extravagance among scholars which first overdoes the supposed similarity in passages and then proceeds to describe source and derivation as if implying literary connection flowing in an inevitable direction." In short: collecting surface resemblances between two texts and treating the pile of parallels as proof that one borrowed from the other, without controlling for coincidence, common culture, or independent invention.

**Why it matters here.** The charge cuts both ways in this vault. LDS apologists invoke it against critics who line up the Book of Mormon beside Ethan Smith's *View of the Hebrews* and infer plagiarism from loose thematic overlaps. Conversely, critics and cautious scholars raise it against over-eager temple-typology and Old World "parallels," insisting that real dependence requires distinctive, textual, directional links — not a long list.
