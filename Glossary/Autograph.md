# Autograph

**Definition.** In textual criticism, the *autograph* is the original manuscript of a text as first written by its author (or dictated to a scribe) — for example, the very scroll Paul's secretary penned. No biblical autograph survives; every copy we possess is a later transcription. Scholars reconstruct the wording of the lost autograph by comparing the thousands of surviving copies and versions.

**Why it matters here.** The autograph is pivotal to inerrancy and *sola scriptura* debates. Many Protestants locate biblical infallibility only in the (lost) autographs, not in any extant copy. LDS apologists press the difficulty: an infallible original we no longer have cannot function as a present rule of faith, which they argue points to the need for living authority and continuing revelation.
