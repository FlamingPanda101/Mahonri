# Grace

**Definition.** Grace (Greek *charis*, "favor, gift, gratitude") is God's unmerited goodwill and enabling power toward humanity, supremely expressed in the atonement of Christ. In Augustinian-Reformed thought it is chiefly God's one-way, unearned saving favor that excludes any human contribution; in much patristic and LDS usage it is also a *transforming* power that enables obedience and the believer's growth into Christ's likeness, and in its Greco-Roman background *charis* named a reciprocal gift that expected a grateful response.

**Why it matters here.** The whole faith-versus-works debate turns on this word. Protestant critics read grace as strictly monergistic ("saved by grace, not works"), while LDS and Catholic apologists argue Paul's *charis* is a relational, reciprocal gift compatible with covenant obligation and ordinances—so "grace" alone does not settle whether baptism or works cooperate in salvation.
