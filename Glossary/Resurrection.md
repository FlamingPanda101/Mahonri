# Resurrection

**Definition.** The **resurrection** is the bodily raising of Jesus from the dead on the third day, and, by extension, the promised bodily raising of all the dead. The New Testament treats Christ's resurrection (Greek *anastasis*, "a standing up again") as a physical, embodied event and the firstfruits of a general resurrection (1 Corinthians 15), not a merely spiritual survival. Early Christian theology read it as "the beginning of a new creation"—a present pledge (*prolepsis*) of the world's final renewal.

**Why it matters here.** Within this library the resurrection grounds an *eschatological* answer to the problem of evil: evil and death are real but "not the last word." Latter-day Saints stress its concretely embodied character (a body is needed for eternal family bonds), against purely visionary or spiritualized readings such as Goff's. A further apologetic thread notes that an embodied risen Christ—God with a glorified physical body—strains the classical Trinity's immaterial deity.
