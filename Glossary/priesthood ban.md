# priesthood ban

**Definition.** The former Latter-day Saint policy, in force from the 1850s until 1978, barring men of Black African descent from priesthood ordination and barring Black members from temple ordinances. Though Black men were ordained under [[Joseph Smith]], the restriction took hold under Brigham Young and was long defended with appeals to the curses of Cain and Ham. It was lifted by the 1978 revelation now canonized as Official Declaration 2, and in 2013 the Church disavowed the racial theories once used to justify it.

**Why it matters here.** It is a focal case in debates over prophetic fallibility, folk doctrine, and continuing revelation. Critics argue prophets taught the ban as God's will for over a century, so its reversal concedes major error. Defenders frame it as a culturally borrowed practice and false doctrine, corrected back toward the collective prophetic witness.
