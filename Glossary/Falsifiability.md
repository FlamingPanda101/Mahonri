# Falsifiability

**Definition.** Karl Popper's proposed criterion demarcating science from non-science: a claim is *falsifiable* if it forbids some observable outcome — if there is, in principle, evidence that would count against it. A theory compatible with *every* possible observation explains nothing and is unscientific. To be falsifiable is not to be false; it is to stick one's neck out. "Unfalsifiable" is the corresponding charge that a belief has been rigged so that no evidence could ever tell against it.

**Why it matters here.** Critics like Sam Harris allege religious apologetics is unfalsifiable — "heads, faith wins; tails, reason loses" — counting every datum as confirmation. Defenders reply two ways: that placing God "outside Nature" is a pre-stated metaphysical definition, not an ad hoc dodge, and that naturalism has its own protective "we'll explain it eventually." Latter-day Saint apologists add that claims tied to witnesses, history, and confirmable fruits *could* in principle fail, so are not unfalsifiable in the objectionable sense.
