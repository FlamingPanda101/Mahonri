# Cosmological argument

**Definition.** A family of arguments that infers the existence of God from the sheer fact that a contingent universe exists at all. Two main branches dominate: *temporal* versions (e.g. the Kalam) argue that whatever begins to exist has a cause, and the universe began to exist; *contingency* versions (Leibniz, Aquinas, Clarke) argue that contingent things require a necessary being to ground their existence, whether or not the universe had a beginning. The conclusion is a first cause that is uncaused, transcendent, and the explanatory terminus of the chain.

**Why it matters here.** It is the leading natural-theology weapon against materialist atheism, blocking the move of treating the universe as a self-sufficient brute fact. Latter-day Saints press it against atheism but diverge from classical theism at the terminus: where Thomists end in an absolutely simple, purely actual being, the Restoration locates the necessary, self-existent layer in eternal matter, intelligences, and law.
