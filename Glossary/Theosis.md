# Theosis

**Definition.** Theosis (Greek *theōsis*, "becoming god," also called deification or divinization) is the doctrine that the goal of salvation is for human beings to be transformed into the likeness of God and to share in the divine life. It is summarized in the patristic "exchange formula" — God became man so that man might become god — and was taught by Athanasius, Irenaeus, and the Cappadocians. Eastern Orthodoxy frames it as partaking of God's *energies* but never His *essence*; Latter-day Saints frame it as exaltation into the same kind of being as God.

**Why it matters here.** The contested point is how strong "becoming god" is. Critics treat the LDS reading (humans becoming gods of the same species) as a departure from the patristic sense, while Latter-day Saints argue the early sources are stronger than later Orthodox restatements admit, and that the difference is the essence-energies fence.
