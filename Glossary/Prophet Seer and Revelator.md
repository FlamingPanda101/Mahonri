# Prophet, Seer, and Revelator

**Definition.** The threefold title sustained upon the senior leadership of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints — the First Presidency and Quorum of the Twelve Apostles. A *prophet* speaks for God, a *seer* perceives hidden or future things (often associated with seer stones; cf. Mosiah's "a seer is greater than a prophet"), and a *revelator* receives and conveys new revelation. The office presupposes that the heavens remain open and the scriptural canon is not closed.

**Why it matters here.** Living prophets and an open canon are a defining LDS contrast with both Protestant *sola scriptura* and Islam's "seal of the prophets," and the vault treats this continuing-revelation claim as a feature, not a defect. Critics press it as a mechanism for doctrinal change; defenders answer that a living canon is precisely what lets the Church adapt while preserving an unchanging core.
