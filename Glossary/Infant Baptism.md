# Infant Baptism

**Definition.** Infant baptism (paedobaptism) is the practice of baptizing infants and young children before they can profess personal faith. It is defended on grounds such as original sin (the child needs regenerating grace), covenant inclusion (the New Covenant analogue of circumcision), and household-baptism precedents in Acts. Catholic apologists frame it as a *discipline* (a practice gradually and unevenly implemented) downstream of the *doctrine* of baptismal regeneration, rather than a practice needing its own explicit early proof.

**Why it matters here.** The dispute is largely downstream of original sin: if infants inherit guilt, baptizing them follows; if they do not, the rationale collapses. Latter-day Saints reject infant baptism outright—Moroni 8 calls it "solemn mockery" because children are innocent until accountable—resolving by a fixed age of accountability (eight) what credobaptists handle by improvised ages.
