# Ordinance

**Definition.** In Latter-day Saint usage, a sacred rite performed by priesthood authority — baptism, confirmation, the sacrament, the endowment, sealing — through which covenants are made and grace is conveyed. "Ordinance" is a Protestant loanword (as in a *municipal* ordinance, a law) that Protestants coined to deny that such rites do saving work; the Restoration kept the word but, per Jonathan Stapley, used it in Joseph Smith's *legal* sense ("having faith and repentance is just as much of a law as baptism"), while the rites themselves function much like Catholic/Orthodox *sacraments*.

**Why it matters here.** The term carries a buried theological dispute: critics charge that LDS "ordinances" amount to works-righteousness dressed in Protestant vocabulary; defenders reply the rites genuinely mediate grace ("learning heaven, not earning heaven"). The law-vs-liturgy distinction also resolves how an ordinance can be called "unchangeable" yet visibly develop over time.
