# Preexistence of Christ

**Definition.** The **preexistence of Christ** is the doctrine that the Son existed as a divine person *before* his mortal birth, indeed before creation. Its key texts include John 1:1 (the [[Logos]] "in the beginning"), John 17:5 (the glory "before the world existed"), and Philippians 2:6 (existing "in the form of God"). Distinct from the broader idea of human premortal existence, it concerns specifically the Son's prior divine life and his role as the agent of creation.

**Why it matters here.** This doctrine is largely shared ground, but its framing is contested. Latter-day Saints read it as the head case of a wider [[Premortal existence]] applying to all spirits, and link it to the pre-mortal divine Messiah they argue the Deuteronomists edited out of Israelite religion. Trinitarian readers affirm the Son's preexistence as eternal, uncreated consubstantial deity, and resist extending premortal existence to ordinary humans.
