# Endowment

**Definition.** In Latter-day Saint practice, a temple ceremony — a "gift" or investiture of "power from on high" — in which the patron receives instruction through a symbolic creation-to-veil drama, makes covenants, and is given sacred clothing, signs, and tokens. The name follows Luke 24:49, where Jesus tells the disciples to "tarry... until ye be endowed with power from on high," fulfilled at Pentecost; Joseph Smith introduced the modern form at Nauvoo in 1842.

**Why it matters here.** Critics note overlaps with Freemasonry and argue the rite is a nineteenth-century borrowing; defenders respond that the deeper endowment *pattern* (ritual ascent, veil, divine name, garment) predates Masonry and is attested in ancient temple and apocryphal sources, with Masonry supplying only a symbolic vocabulary. The vault frames it as "learning heaven, not earning heaven."
