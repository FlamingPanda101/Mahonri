# Book of Abraham

**Definition.** A short book of Latter-day Saint scripture in the Pearl of Great Price, presented as [[Joseph Smith]]'s translation of Egyptian papyri the Church acquired in 1835. It gives a first-person account attributed to the biblical Abraham — his deliverance from sacrifice, a vision of the cosmos and premortal spirits, and a creation narrative — and includes three printed "facsimiles" reproduced from the papyri. It is the second of the two long-form scriptural works (alongside the Book of Mormon) that Joseph Smith claimed to produce by revelation.

**Why it matters here.** It is among the most contested LDS texts: critics argue the surviving papyri are ordinary funerary documents (the Book of Breathings) that do not match Joseph's translation or his facsimile explanations, treating this as decisive evidence against his prophetic gift. Defenders reply with catalyst and missing-scroll theories, ancient corroboration of details Joseph could not have known, and disputes over Egyptological method.
