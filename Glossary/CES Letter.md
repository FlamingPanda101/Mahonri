# CES Letter

**Definition.** *Letter to a CES Director* (2013), a widely circulated document by [[Jeremy Runnells]] compiling objections to Latter-day Saint truth claims. Framed as an earnest list of questions addressed to a Church Educational System director, it gathers criticisms spanning Book of Mormon and Book of Abraham translation, plural marriage, the witnesses, Church history, and science. It became one of the most distributed pieces of LDS criticism online and a common reference point for the "faith crisis" genre.

**Why it matters here.** It functions as a catalogue of the questions many of this vault's notes answer. Critics treat its sheer accumulation of problems as cumulatively damning. Defenders argue much of it recycles older anti-Mormon material behind a sympathetic "honest seeker" framing, double-counts sources, and that its individual claims dissolve under closer examination.
