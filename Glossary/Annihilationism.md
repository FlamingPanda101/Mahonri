# Annihilationism

**Definition.** **Annihilationism** is the view that the unsaved are ultimately *destroyed* — cease to exist — rather than suffering forever. It is closely tied to **conditional immortality**: the claim that immortality is not innate to the soul but a gift granted only to the redeemed, so the wicked simply perish. Proponents read the biblical language of "destruction," "perishing," and "the second death" literally, against the traditional reading of unending conscious punishment.

**Why it matters here.** Annihilationism is one of three historic options for the fate of the damned (alongside eternal conscious torment and universal salvation), and the vault shows all three already competing within Second Temple sources like 1 Enoch, which the New Testament inherited unsystematized. It offers a softer answer to the problem of hell than everlasting torment. Latter-day Saint theology generally *declines* annihilationism: souls are eternal and even "outer darkness" is continued (if diminished) existence, not extinction.
