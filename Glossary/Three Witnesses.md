# Three Witnesses

**Definition.** Oliver Cowdery, David Whitmer, and Martin Harris, whose joint signed statement is printed at the front of the Book of Mormon. They testified to a *supernatural and corporate* experience: an angel showed them the gold plates, and they heard the voice of God declare the translation true. This is contrasted with the Eight Witnesses, whose encounter was ordinary and tactile.

**Why it matters here.** Their testimony is treated as one of the strongest evidential lines for the Book of Mormon, because all three upheld it for life even after each became estranged from the Church — hard to square with conscious fraud. Critics counter that the experience was "visionary" (language like "eyes of our understanding") and so reducible to suggestion, hallucination, or religious enthusiasm; defenders reply that the physical detail and the never-recanting under estrangement resist those naturalistic readings.
