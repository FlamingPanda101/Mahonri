# Dei Verbum

**Definition.** The Second Vatican Council's *Dogmatic Constitution on Divine Revelation* (1965), one of the four constitutions of Vatican II. It sets out the Roman Catholic understanding of how God's revelation is transmitted: through Sacred Scripture and Sacred Tradition together, which "flow from the same divine wellspring," authoritatively interpreted by the Church's teaching office (the Magisterium). It affirms the inspiration and inerrancy of Scripture while locating its right reading within the living Tradition rather than in private judgment alone.

**Why it matters here.** It is a key Catholic counter to Protestant *sola scriptura* and is cited in this vault's debates over canon and the rule of faith. Catholics read it as grounding Scripture's authority in the Church; Protestants object that it subordinates Scripture to Tradition, while Latter-day Saints note its appeal to a living teaching authority resembles their case for continuing revelation.
