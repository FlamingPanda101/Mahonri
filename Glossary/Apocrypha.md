# Apocrypha

**Definition.** From Greek *apokryphos*, "hidden away." The word carries two distinct senses. (1) In Protestant usage it names the Old Testament books in the Septuagint and Vulgate but not the Hebrew Bible — the same books Catholics call the **deuterocanon**. (2) More broadly it labels early Jewish and Christian writings outside any biblical canon (e.g., apocryphal gospels, acts, and apocalypses). The label ranges in tone from "non-canonical but edifying" to "spurious," depending on the speaker.

**Why it matters here.** Critics often use "apocryphal" to mean "fake." LDS apologetics instead mines apocrypha for ancient traditions that parallel Restoration scripture, arguing some preserve genuine early material; D&C 91 calls the Apocrypha mostly true but unrevised. Catholics reject the Protestant "apocrypha" label for the deuterocanon entirely.
