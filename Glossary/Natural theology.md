# Natural theology

**Definition.** The project of arguing for God's existence and attributes from reason and observation of the natural world, independent of special revelation (scripture, prophecy, religious experience). Its classic instruments are the cosmological, teleological, ontological, and moral arguments. It is distinguished from *revealed theology*, which draws on God's self-disclosure, and from *fideism*, which grounds belief in faith apart from argument. Historically it presupposes that the cosmos is rationally intelligible — itself often defended as a theological assumption behind the rise of science.

**Why it matters here.** Natural theology supplies the common ground on which theists of every tradition engage atheism, since its arguments appeal to evidence both sides accept. Latter-day Saints embrace it enthusiastically against materialism, while noting that its arguments yield a generic deity whose precise nature — simple or embodied, sole creator or organizer of eternal matter — is then contested between classical theism and the Restoration.
