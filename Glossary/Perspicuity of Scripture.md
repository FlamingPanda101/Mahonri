# Perspicuity of Scripture

**Definition.** Perspicuity (from Latin *perspicuus*, "transparent, clear") is the Protestant doctrine that the saving message of Scripture is clear enough that ordinary believers, using ordinary means, can grasp what is necessary for salvation without a mediating teaching authority. As the Westminster Confession qualifies it, the claim is not that *every* passage is plain, but that the essentials "necessary to be known, believed, and observed for salvation" are clearly set forth.

**Why it matters here.** It is the support beam under *sola scriptura*: if Scripture were not clear, it could not function as the final standard. Critics argue that sincere, learned readers divide on salvation-relevant questions (even whether a given church preaches a true gospel), so clarity fails exactly where it is most needed; defenders reply the fault lies in the blind reader, not the light.
