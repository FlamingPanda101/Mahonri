# Libertarian free will

**Definition.** The view that genuine freedom requires the ability to do otherwise — that an agent's choice is neither determined by prior causes nor a product of chance, but originates in the agent (often modeled as *agent causation*). It is an *incompatibilist* position: it holds that free will and causal determinism cannot both be true. The signature test is the "principle of alternative possibilities": a choice is free only if, with everything prior held fixed, the agent could have chosen differently.

**Why it matters here.** Libertarian freedom is the vault's center of gravity on agency. It underwrites the free-will defense in the problem of evil, the LDS insistence that moral responsibility requires real choice, and the open-theist argument that exhaustive foreknowledge of free acts is incompatible with their being free. Its rivals are *hard determinism* (no free will) and *compatibilism* (a redescribed freedom consistent with determinism). Critics press the "sourcehood/luck" objection: an undetermined choice looks random, not authored — answered in LDS thought by appeal to the uncreated, co-eternal intelligence.
