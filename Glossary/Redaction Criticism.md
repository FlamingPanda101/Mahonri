# Redaction Criticism

**Definition.** The method (German *Redaktionsgeschichte*) that studies how a final editor — the *redactor* — selected, arranged, joined, and reshaped earlier sources and traditions to advance a theological agenda. Where source criticism isolates the underlying documents and form criticism the small units, redaction criticism asks what the compiler did with them: the seams, transitions (such as the *toledot* formula in Genesis), and editorial framing become evidence of deliberate authorial purpose.

**Why it matters here.** The presence of editorial seams cuts two ways in this vault. Apologists for the Restoration use redactional layering to argue that the canon was shaped, trimmed, and slanted by later hands — making room for lost or altered material. Conversely, several notes resist over-reading seams, defending the single authorship of texts like John against multi-layer redaction theories. So the question is often *how much* editing the evidence really licenses.
