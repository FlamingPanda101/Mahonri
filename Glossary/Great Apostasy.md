# Great Apostasy

**Definition.** The Latter-day Saint claim that, after the death of the original apostles, the church Christ founded lost priesthood authority, keys, and certain doctrines and ordinances — necessitating a later Restoration. Greek *apostasia* means "a falling away" (2 Thessalonians 2:3). LDS scholars stress the apostasy was a *gradual* corruption (often tied to Hellenization and the loss of temple/divine-family theology), not the disappearance of all Christians; notably, "Great Apostasy" is not itself a term in the LDS standard works.

**Why it matters here.** It is the hinge of the whole Restoration debate: if nothing was lost, nothing needed restoring. Critics (e.g., Catholic apologists) cite indefectibility texts — "I am with you always" (Matthew 28:20) — and argue Scripture predicts only a *partial* falling away. Defenders relocate the loss to priesthood keys and temple theology rather than to the existence of believers, reading the church as "driven into the wilderness," not annihilated.
