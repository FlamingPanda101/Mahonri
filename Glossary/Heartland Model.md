# Heartland Model

**Definition.** A Book of Mormon geography that locates the narrative's main events in the eastern United States — centered on the Mississippi/Ohio River valleys and the Great Lakes — and identifies the New York Hill Cumorah as the actual final battlefield. Associated with Rod Meldrum, Wayne May, and Jonathan Neville, it appeals to the Hopewell mound-builder cultures, Joseph Smith's statements, and a North American DNA argument (haplogroup X).

**Why it matters here.** The Heartland model competes directly with the [[Limited Geography Theory]] (a Mesoamerican setting) and the dispute is internal to believing Latter-day Saints, not just believer-versus-critic. Proponents argue it honors prophetic statements and a single, New York Cumorah; most LDS scholars counter that the text's distances, climate, and high-culture details fit Mesoamerica far better and that the "two-Cumorahs" reading is textually grounded.
