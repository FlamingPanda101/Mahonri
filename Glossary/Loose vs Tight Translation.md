# Loose vs Tight Translation

**Definition.** Two competing models of how Joseph Smith produced the Book of Mormon. *Tight control* (associated with David Whitmer) holds that exact English words appeared on the seer stone and were read off and would not vanish until correctly transcribed — translation as near-dictation. *Loose control* (a participatory or "expansion" model) holds that Joseph received ideas or impressions and rendered them in his own language and idiom, leaving room for nineteenth-century phrasing, KJV echoes, and later editing.

**Why it matters here.** The two models trade off against each other apologetically. Tight control maximizes the miracle but makes textual *changes* awkward and invites the charge that mistakes prove the words were not God's; loose control absorbs edits and anachronisms but blurs the line between revelation and ordinary authorship — which critics say is the point. Apologists are accused of citing tight control when it impresses and loose control when edits embarrass.
