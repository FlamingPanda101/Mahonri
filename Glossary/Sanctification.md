# Sanctification

**Definition.** Sanctification (Latin *sanctificare*, "to make holy") is the process by which a person is made actually righteous and conformed to the image of Christ. In classic Protestant theology it is sharply distinguished from justification: justification is the instantaneous legal verdict, sanctification the lifelong moral renewal that follows it ("justified by faith alone, but the faith is never alone"). In Catholic theology *sanctifying grace* is the regenerating divine life infused at baptism, and justification and sanctification are not cleanly separable.

**Why it matters here.** Whether becoming holy is *part of* being saved or merely its downstream evidence shapes the entire grace-and-works dispute. The LDS and patristic emphasis on theosis treats salvation as a real transformation into a Christlike being, aligning sanctification closely with justification and with ordinances—against the Protestant insistence that moral change can never contribute to one's standing before God.
