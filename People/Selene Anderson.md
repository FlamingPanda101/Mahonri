# Selene Anderson

**Role:** Film producer; Latter-day Saint commentator on plural-marriage history
**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints

## Bio

Selene Anderson is a film producer who joined the project that became *The Angel* (2021 onward), a folk-horror short by filmmaker Barrett Bergen set in a 19th-century Latter-day Saint polygamous family. Researching the period for the film drew her into the history of plural marriage and Latter-day Saint women's history; she went on to share her research at the (then newly launched) Journal of Mormon Polygamy conference and on the [[Cwic Show]].

## Contribution to the source

In the [[Cwic Show - A Film Producers Research On LDS Polygamy|Cwic Show interview]] (host [[Greg Matson]]), Anderson argues for engaging plural-marriage history "loudly and boldly" rather than timidly, and against ceding the topic to fundamentalists or the polygamy-denial movement. Her case is interdisciplinary and reads the history through evolutionary anthropology and "integral theory":
- The denial movement exploits "women's insecurity and men's guilt," and the victim-framing of plural wives is itself misogynistic ([[Polygamy Denial Capitalizes on Female Insecurity and Male Guilt]]).
- Female hypergamy and the asymmetry of reproductive cost explain the cross-cultural prevalence of polygyny; monogamy is a legally-imposed equalizer that enabled democracy and women's rights ([[Plural Marriage Read Through Hypergamy and the Asymmetry of Reproduction]]).
- Early Utah Saints tied women's emancipation to the Restoration; she reads [[George Q. Cannon]]'s 1884 *Juvenile Instructor* essay as casting plural marriage as the lifting of Eve's curse ([[Early Saints Tied Womens Emancipation to the Restoration]]).
- Plural marriage was meant to be woman-driven and bounded ([[Exodus 21.10|Exodus 21:10]]), and is the feminine counterpart of the law of consecration ([[Plural Marriage and the Law of Consecration Are the Same Law]]).
- The objection "God could never command this" is a debate about God's nature ([[The Polygamy Objection Is Really a Debate About the Nature of God]]).
