# Brother of Jared

**Era:** Jaredite era (post-Babel, Book of Mormon / book of Ether)
**Tradition:** Book of Mormon figure (the book of Ether)

## Identity

The brother of Jared is the unnamed prophet-leader of the Jaredites, who left the Old World at the time of the confounding of languages ("the tower"). In [[Ether 3]] he ascends Mount Shelem, prays for the Lord to touch sixteen molten stones to give light to the barges, and is brought through the veil into the Lord's presence — seeing the premortal Christ's finger "as the finger of a man." He receives the two interpreters (white stones) and a sealed revelation of all things from the beginning to the end. The text withholds his personal name (LDS tradition: Mahonri Moriancumer).

## Apologetic significance

- **Endowment ascent pattern.** [[Don Bradley]] and others read the Ether 3 veil dialogue, the redemption-from-the-fall, the new white stones, and the withheld name as a Book-of-Mormon depiction of the temple endowment — predating Joseph Smith's 1842 Freemasonry. See [[The Brother of Jared Models the Endowment Ascent Through the Veil]] and [[The Jaredite and Nephite Interpreters Carry the Endowment Ascent Pattern]].
- **The interpreters' shape.** Bradley argues the interpreters the brother of Jared received were shaped like the Masonic compass and square (equilateral + right triangle), per [[Joseph Smith Sr]] and [[Lucy Mack Smith]]'s descriptions. See [[The Nephite Interpreters Were Shaped Like the Masonic Compass and Square]].
