# Jim Bennett

**Era:** Contemporary (2010s–2020s)
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint (LDS)
**Role:** Apologist / author

## Bio
Jim Bennett is a Latter-day Saint apologist best known as the author of **A CES Letter Reply** — the lengthy, point-by-point annotated rebuttal to Jeremy Runnells's *Letter to a CES Director* (the "CES Letter"). His reply works through the CES Letter's criticisms one at a time and has become one of the most widely circulated lay responses to it.

## Why he matters
Bennett is invoked in the vault for his maxim that **"frauds always look worse over time"** — i.e., genuine historical claims tend to accrue corroboration as evidence accumulates, while fabrications are progressively exposed. This is applied to the **shrinking Book of Mormon anachronism list**: of 200+ alleged anachronisms catalogued since 1830, the great majority have been struck off, which is how authentic claims behave and frauds do not. See [[The Anachronism List Is Trending Toward the Book of Mormon]] and the related cluster [[Critics Compiling Anachronism Lists Did Apologists a Favor]]. His maxim functions as a heuristic about the *direction* of the evidence over time, not a standalone proof.
