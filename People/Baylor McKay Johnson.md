# Baylor McKay Johnson

Baylor McKay Johnson is a Latter-day Saint commentator cited in the vault for coining (in debate commentary) the label **semantic gerrymandering** — redrawing the contours of a definition "to ensure a victory." He uses it to answer [[Noah Biser]]'s Catholic objection that admitting co-eternal matter means Mormons "don't believe in God."

## Relevance

His rebuttal anchors the position note [[Semantic Gerrymandering Redefines God to Exclude Pre-Existing Matter]], arguing the objection is question-begging because it smuggles classical theism's conclusion (God as the sole eternal existent) into the very definition of "God."
