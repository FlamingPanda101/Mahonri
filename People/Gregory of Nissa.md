# Gregory of Nissa

4th-century Cappadocian Church Father, often cited as an early **universalist**
voice within the patristic tradition.

## Key Contribution (on Creation Ex Nihilo)

Gregory argued that the doctrine of creation from nothing (*creatio ex nihilo*)
is not merely an **ontological/metaphysical claim** but a **moral claim** about
the nature of God himself. If God is the only uncreated being and creates
everything freely, then the moral destiny of creation and the moral nature of
God are inseparable. [^1]

This argument is deployed in [[Divine Responsibility and Creation Ex Nihilo]]
and [[Problem of Hell and Creation]] to argue that a perfectly good God who
creates freely from nothing cannot also sustain an eternal hell.
