# James Strang

James Jesse Strang (1813-1856) was a convert to the Latter Day Saint movement in February 1844, months before [[Joseph Smith]]'s assassination, who became the principal rival to [[Brigham Young]] in the succession crisis. He produced a "Letter of Appointment" purportedly from Joseph Smith naming him the next prophet, claimed to discover and translate buried plates (the Voree plates), and produced new scripture — the Book of the Law of the Lord from the "plates of Laban." He founded a colony on Beaver Island in Lake Michigan, had himself crowned "king of the kingdom of God on earth," secretly entered plural marriage (concealing wife Elvira Field as his "nephew" in male dress), and was assassinated in 1856 by disaffected followers with the involvement of the U.S. Navy. He is the subject of [[Kyle Beshears]]'s PhD dissertation. [^1]

## In Beshears's account
Strang is Beshears's favorite case study for the breadth of the restoration beyond Utah, and a striking parallel to Joseph Smith's own plates-and-translation pattern. See [[James Strang and the Strangite Movement]], [[Mormonism Is More Than the Utah Church]].
