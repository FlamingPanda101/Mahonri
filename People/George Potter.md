# George Potter

**Role:** Independent researcher and explorer (Lehi's Arabian trail)
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint (LDS)

## Bio

George Potter, with co-author Richard Wellington, explored and documented candidate sites along [[Lehi]]'s route through Arabia (notably in *Lehi in the Wilderness*). Their distinctive contribution is on-the-ground measurement — they physically traveled the legs of the journey rather than estimating distances from maps.

## Role in this video

[[Neal Rappleye]] draws on Potter and Wellington for the journey's measured particulars and notes where Potter diverges from [[Warren Aston]]:

- **Measured distances and pace.** Potter and Wellington reported ~74 miles from Aqaba to Wadi Tayyib al-Ism (the Valley of Lemuel candidate) and a daily caravan pace of ~15–25 miles, figures Rappleye cross-checks against non-LDS sources like Nigel Groom. [^1]
- **Best hunting at Shazer.** Locals told Potter and Wellington that the mountains around Wadi Sharma (the Shazer candidate) were the best hunting in the region, fitting Nephi's account. [^2]
- **Bountiful: Khor Rori and imported teak.** Potter favors **Khor Rori** (over Aston's Khor Kharfot) and argues the ship's hardwood would have been imported teak via maritime trade, rather than sourced entirely from local timber — see [[Bountiful and Khor Kharfot Match the Book of Mormon]]. [^3]
