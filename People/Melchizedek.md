# Melchizedek

**Melchizedek** is the priest-king of **Salem** who blesses Abraham and receives his tithes in [[Genesis 14.18|Genesis 14:18-20]]. The name combines Hebrew *melek* ("king") and *tsedeq* ("righteousness") — "king of righteousness" — and, as king of Salem (*shalem*), also "king of peace," the wordplay [[Hebrews 7.1-3|Hebrews 7:1-3]] develops into a type of Christ's eternal priesthood ("a priest for ever after the order of Melchizedek," Psalm 110:4; Hebrews 5-7).

## Bio / Significance
In the Hebrew Bible Melchizedek appears abruptly and "without genealogy," which Hebrews reads typologically — an everlasting, non-Levitical priesthood superior to Aaron's, fulfilled in Jesus. In **Latter-day Saint** theology the higher priesthood is *named after him* (to avoid the too-frequent repetition of the name of Deity, D&C 107:2-4), so Melchizedek stands as the archetypal holder of the order that governs the sealing power and the higher ordinances.

## Contribution
In the [[The Stick of Joseph - Sacred Temple Connection to Priesthood Keys|Stick of Joseph interview]] Melchizedek is the linchpin of a temple argument: because he is a **king** (and Aaron a **priest**), the Melchizedek/Aaronic duality is read as the structure behind the temple's promise that initiates become "kings and queens, priests and priestesses," with the Melchizedek (royal) order tied to the **sealing power** and adoption into Christ's royal line. The *melek-tsedeq* etymology does the work in that reading. See [[The Aaronic-Melchizedek Dualism Maps the Temple's Kings and Priests]] and [[Priesthood Keys Frame the Seven Dispensations]].
