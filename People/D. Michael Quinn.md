# D. Michael Quinn

D. Michael Quinn (1944–2021) was a Yale-trained American historian of Mormonism. In this source he is cited as the author of *Early Mormonism and the Magic World View* (second edition), described as "still the best source" if you "want to see everything laid out" — a book "all about just collecting and cataloging everything that's ever said about the Smiths and magic."[^1]

## Folk Magic and the Smith Family

Quinn's work functions in the source as the master catalog of *every* claim ever made about the Smith family's folk-magic practices (magic circles, animal sacrifice, daggers, necromancy, astrology, charms, divining rods, seer stones).[^1] The source distinguishes between Quinn's comprehensive cataloging and later authors (such as Richard Van Wagoner) who reproduce "the laundry list of all of the possible things" Quinn compiled "with absolutely no source criticism or historical analysis."[^2] Of the full list, only seer stones, divining rods, and treasure seeking are said to have credible, multiply-attested, contemporaneous support.[^3]
