# Cornelius Van Til

**Era:** 1895–1987
**Tradition:** Reformed (Dutch Calvinist)
**Role:** Theologian, apologist, seminary professor

## Bio
Cornelius Van Til was a Dutch-American Reformed theologian and the **founder of presuppositional apologetics**. He earned a Ph.D. from Princeton and taught apologetics for decades at Westminster Theological Seminary. His method argues that one cannot reason to God from neutral ground; instead, the Christian worldview must be *presupposed* as the precondition for intelligibility itself, and rival worldviews are refuted by showing they cannot account for logic, morality, or science on their own terms. [[Greg Bahnsen]] was his most prominent systematizer.

## Topics

### Presuppositionalism and "Replacing Our Thoughts with the Bible"
[[Hayden Carroll]] cites Van Til as the originator of presuppositional apologetics and quotes a Van Til line (paraphrased in a same-day video) to the effect that the method "replaces our thoughts essentially with the Bible" — i.e., the framework assumes the Bible is true while being unable, on Carroll's reading, to *justify* that assumption non-circularly.[^1] Carroll uses this to argue presuppositionalism is powerful against atheism but self-undermining when wielded by Protestants who cannot justify the canon. See [[Internal Critique - Two Questions for Every Critic]] and [[The Three Unjustified Protestant Canon Presuppositions]].
