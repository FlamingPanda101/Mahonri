# T. Heath Ogden

**T. Heath Ogden** is a professor in and Chair of the Department of Biology at Utah Valley University. He earned a BS in zoology and a PhD in biology with an emphasis in molecular phylogenetics and evolution from BYU, and an MS from the Universidad de Concepción, Chile. His research spans bioinformatics, evolutionary biology, systematics, phylogenetics, comparative genomics, and entomology.[^1] He is a believing Latter-day Saint who writes on reconciling evolutionary science with the restored gospel.

## Evolution and Faith
Ogden's chapter "Accepting Evolution with Joy Is Possible" argues that evolution can be embraced without renouncing core Latter-day Saint beliefs. He distinguishes *believing* (reserved for God, revelation) from *accepting* evolution on the evidence; surveys the Church's neutrality ("no official position"); and frames acceptance as joyful rather than fearful. See [[Accepting Evolution with Joy Is Possible]].

## God Is Not a Deceiver
His signature argument is theological: because God "is not a deceiver" and "lives in perfect harmony with natural laws," the convergent genetic evidence (human chromosome 2 fusion, mitochondrial endosymbiosis, the gradient of genetic similarity across species) should be taken at face value. He reconciles human evolution with divine sonship by locating sonship in the *spirit*, not the biological body. See [[God Is Not a Deceiver - The Evidence for Evolution Is Real]].
