# Bradley Kramer

**Full name:** Bradley J. Kramer
**Role:** LDS author
**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint

## Bio
Bradley J. Kramer is the author of *Beholding the Tree of Life: A Rabbinic Approach to the Book of Mormon* (Greg Kofford Books, 2014), which reads the Book of Mormon using interpretive habits drawn from rabbinic Judaism (close attention to wordplay, intertextuality, and argument). [[Mike Day]] cites the book warmly ("I don't get a penny from this guy... never met him") as foundational to his own approach, while noting it is "not a Documentary Hypothesis book per se."[^1]

## Contribution to the lecture
Kramer supplies the interpretive frame behind Day's [[Documentary Hypothesis Strengthens the Restoration|"Book of Mormon as linchpin"]] argument: that the **Book of Mormon is engaging the very theological arguments being had during the First and Second Temple eras.** On Day's reading, this is why a believer can take source criticism seriously — the Book of Mormon is itself a participant in ancient Israelite debate, not a text that needs to be insulated from it. Day: "what he says is the Book of Mormon is engaging the arguments that they're having during the first and second temple era."[^1]
