# Marcus Aurelius

**Era:** 121 – 180 CE
**Tradition:** Roman Stoic
**Role:** Roman emperor and philosopher

## Bio

Marcus Aurelius was the Stoic "philosopher emperor," author of the *Meditations*.[^1]

## Topics

### A Roman Throat That Would Not Catch
[[Glenn Scrivener]] uses Marcus Aurelius (alongside Muhammad, Sun Tzu, and Aristotle) as a foil: the brutalities of the book of Joshua "wouldn't have put a lump in Marcus Aurelius's throat" — only in throats already shaped by the biblical sensibility. The argument: our *recoil* from OT violence is itself evidence of Christianization. See [[Both the Jewish and Roman Worlds Were Brutal]] and [[The Recoil from OT Violence Is Itself Christian]].[^1]
