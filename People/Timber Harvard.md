# Timber Harvard

**Full Name:** Tember Harward (rendered "Timber Harvard" in some auto-transcripts)
**Era:** Contemporary (2020s)
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint (LDS)
**Role:** Online minister; founder of **Beacon SSA Ministry**; LGBTQ+/LDS voice

## Bio

Timber Harvard is an LDS man who experiences same-sex attraction and is open
about his journey online. He is married to a woman in the temple and has
children. [[Hayden Carroll]] cites him as an example of someone who "is using
his free will to keep covenants with God" despite experiencing same-sex
attraction.[^1] Harvard's social media presence focuses on his experience
navigating faith and sexuality within an LDS framework.

## Topics

### Free Will and Same-Sex Attraction
Carroll presents Harvard as a counterexample to the claim that same-sex
attraction determines identity and destiny within a deterministic framework:
"He experiences same-sex attraction and he is married to a woman — to his
wife — in the temple. He has kids."[^1] Carroll acknowledges the controversy:
"A lot of people hate him or hate on him rather because they would say, 'Oh,
you can't love your wife. You can't have a fulfilling marriage because you're
denying yourself of what your inclinations are.'"[^1] See [[Homosexuality Problem for Atheistic Natural Selection]].

### Beacon SSA Ministry and the Jubilee Media episode
Harward founded **Beacon SSA Ministry** (beaconssaministry.org), where he blogs on
"sensitive topics surrounding the LGB issue" and what "the church actually teaches...
in a compassionate and loving way."[^2] He appeared in a **Jubilee Media** "Mormons
vs. Ex-Mormons" video as the sole active gay Latter-day Saint on the panel, where
(by his and Ward Radio's account) ex-Mormon panelists — including [[John Dehlin]] —
"badgered" him because his thriving in the Church contradicted their narrative. On a
Ward Radio reaction episode he rebuts the doom narrative with wellbeing statistics and
his own history (two prior suicide attempts while living "a secular lifestyle").[^2]
See [[Mixed-Orientation Marriages and LGBTQ Latter-day Saints Show Higher Wellbeing]]
and [[Dehlins Faulty-Pool Study Skews LDS LGBTQ Outcomes]].
