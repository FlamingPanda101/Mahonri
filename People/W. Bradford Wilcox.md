# W. Bradford Wilcox

American sociologist (University of Virginia; National Marriage Project) specializing in marriage, fatherhood, and family. Known for the empirical claim that marriage produces measurable behavioral and economic benefits, especially for men. *Note: not to be confused with the Latter-day Saint speaker "Brad Wilcox" or with [[Miranda Wilcox]].*

## On marriage civilizing men
[[Trent Horn]] cites Wilcox for the claim that "marriage is good for society and good for men because women civilize men when they are united to them in matrimony" — explaining why married men "engage in less antisocial behavior, work harder, make more money, and in general are happier than unmarried men." Horn uses this to identify the *good* female desire (to receive and order a man's strength) that pornography aimed at women corrupts into bodice-ripper fantasies of taming a dangerous man. See [[Pornography Corrupts Distinct Gendered Desires]] [^1].
