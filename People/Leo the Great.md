# Leo the Great

Bishop of Rome (Pope Leo I, r. AD 440–461). He had attended the Council of Ephesus as a deacon and envoy of the bishop of Rome before becoming bishop himself.

## Significance
Author of **Leo's Tome**, the letter that became one of the doctrinal foundations of the Council of Chalcedon (AD 451). Alarmed at [[Eutyches]]'s teaching, Leo warned how dangerous it is "to believe the Lord Jesus Christ to be merely God." He argued Christ must retain *both* natures distinctly: "lowliness is assumed by majesty, weakness by power, mortality by eternity … one and the same 'mediator between God and humankind' … might from one element be capable of dying, and from the other be incapable" (citing 1 Timothy 2:5). He also wrote to Empress [[Pulcheria]] asking her to intervene. The council "read and accepted as doctrinally accurate" his Tome. See [[Ephesus and Chalcedon Defined the Two Natures of Christ]].
