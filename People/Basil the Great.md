# Basil the Great

Fourth-century Bishop of Caesarea and one of the Cappadocian Fathers (c. 330–379), a central figure in Trinitarian theology and Eastern monasticism.

## "Scripture Is the Word of God" — A Later Development
Cited by [[Trent Horn]] as an example of a *later* father who "do[es] offer clear statements identifying scripture as being the word of God" — in contrast to the earliest fathers, who reserved "word of God" for the Christian message or Christ. Horn uses this to argue that the explicit identification of Scripture with the word of God emerged gradually, as a deliverance of sacred tradition. See [[Calling Scripture the Word of God Is a Late Patristic Development]] [^1].
