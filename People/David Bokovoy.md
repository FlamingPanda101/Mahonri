# David Bokovoy

**Role:** Hebrew Bible scholar; author and educator
**Era:** Contemporary (21st century)
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint / academic biblical studies

## Bio

David Bokovoy holds a PhD in Hebrew Bible and the Ancient Near East from Brandeis University. He is known among Latter-day Saints for applying mainstream critical methods — especially the Documentary Hypothesis and the study of inner-biblical allusion (how later biblical authors reuse earlier texts) — to Restoration scripture, most prominently in his book *Authoring the Old Testament: Genesis–Deuteronomy* (Greg Kofford Books, 2014). He has taught in institutional settings ranging from universities to (per this source) a seminary class, and performs in a band on the side.

## Contribution in This Source

In [[All Those In Favor - Joseph Smith vs Catholic on Creation]], the hosts lean on Bokovoy's 2014 paper **"The Theological Use of Biblical Creation in Alma 32"** to argue that [[Alma 32.27-43|Alma 32]]'s "whatsoever is light… is discernible" deliberately alludes to the creation account of [[Genesis 1.1|Genesis 1:1]]–2 — light as the first *discernible* thing emerging from formless, watery chaos. They note Bokovoy argues specifically for the **J source** (Genesis 2–3) while reading the formless-waters imagery alongside it. This intertextual reading is the backbone of [[Alma 32 Light Alludes to Genesis 1 Creation]], and it doubles as a Book of Mormon argument for [[Creatio Ex Nihilo Is the Lynchpin Difference|creation *ex materia*]].
