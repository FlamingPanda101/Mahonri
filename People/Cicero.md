# Cicero

**Full Name:** Marcus Tullius Cicero (106–43 BCE)
**Era:** Late Roman Republic (1st century BCE)
**Tradition:** Roman; transmitter of Greek (Academic/Stoic) philosophy
**Role:** Statesman, orator, philosopher

## Bio
Cicero was a Roman statesman and the most influential conduit of Greek philosophy into Latin thought. His *De Legibus* and *De Re Publica* are classic statements of **natural law** in a Stoic key, and *De Officiis* and *De Natura Deorum* transmit Stoic ethics and theology to later Western readers.

## Cited For
[[Brian Thomassen]] cites Cicero as a primary witness that the Stoics originated **natural law** — "law is something eternal that directs the whole world by its wisdom in ordering and prohibiting" — a concept later inherited by Aquinas and, through him, by the American Founders. He also cites Cicero's body-politic image (the members of the body harming one another would be self-defeating; society is kept whole "by mutual protection of love for all its parts") as a Stoic parallel to Paul's one-body teaching in Romans 12.
