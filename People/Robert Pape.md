# Robert Pape

Robert A. Pape is an American political scientist (University of Chicago) best known for his empirical study of suicide terrorism, *Dying to Win: The Strategic Logic of Suicide Terrorism* (2005). Compiling a database of suicide-bombing cases, Pape argued the phenomenon is driven primarily by strategic and political goals — especially resistance to perceived foreign occupation — rather than by religious doctrine.

## On the Causes of Suicide Terrorism
[[Daniel C. Peterson|Daniel Peterson]] cites Pape's *Dying to Win* to rebut the equation of Islam with terrorism: Pape "analyzed... all of the suicide bombing cases they could gather information about" and concluded the practice "has almost nothing to do with the religion; it has to do with politics," a "desperate step by people who don't feel that they have any power."[^1] Peterson uses this alongside the historical fact that suicide bombing was pioneered by the Marxist Tamil Tigers (Hindus) in Sri Lanka, not by Muslims.
