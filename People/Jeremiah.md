# Jeremiah

**Who:** Hebrew prophet active in Judah during the final decades before the Babylonian destruction of Jerusalem (c. 586 BCE). The book of Jeremiah bears his name and records his prophecies of Jerusalem's fall and his persecution for proclaiming them.

**Role:** Jeremiah is invoked by [[Jacob Wright]] as an example of the biblical pushback against triumphalist theology. Jeremiah proclaimed Jerusalem would be destroyed and was imprisoned for it — illustrating the tension within the biblical tradition between those who insisted on invulnerability ("our God is big, Jerusalem is never going to be destroyed") and those who insisted on facing the reality of defeat.
