# Marcian

Eastern Roman Emperor (r. AD 450–457). "A well-respected senator who had been a personal assistant and friend to the commander of the eastern army."

## Significance
After [[Theodosius II]] died heirless, [[Pulcheria]] married Marcian, making him emperor and herself official empress; he publicly agreed to honor her vow of celibacy so she retained religious authority. "One of her first acts was to have Marcian convene a Church council at Chalcedon" (AD 451). Marcian is thus the emperor who formally called the council that produced the Chalcedonian Definition of Christ's two natures. See [[Ephesus and Chalcedon Defined the Two Natures of Christ]].
