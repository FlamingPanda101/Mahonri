# René Descartes

**Role:** French philosopher and mathematician; father of modern Western philosophy
**Era:** 1596–1650 (early modern period)
**Tradition:** Christian (Roman Catholic); rationalist

## Bio

René Descartes was a French philosopher and mathematician whom [[Jacob Hansen]] introduces as "one of the greatest philosophers of all time… a French Christian" and "a fierce rationalist who wanted to logically ground all of his beliefs." [^1] In his program of methodic doubt, Descartes set out to examine all of his beliefs to find which could be held with 100% rational certainty, and discovered that the senses and even reason "are not 100% reliable." The single indubitable point that survived was that the thinking thing itself exists — captured in his famous line **"I think therefore I am"** (*cogito ergo sum*). [^2]

## Relevance to Epistemology

Hansen uses Descartes as the pivot of his epistemology primer: the cogito shows that **100% rational certainty is essentially impossible** for almost everything, since one cannot rule out radical skeptical scenarios (the modern *Matrix* / *Truman Show* "simulation" or "universe created five seconds ago" thought experiments). The lesson Hansen draws is that absolute certainty is *not* the standard meant by ordinary claims to "know" something. See:
