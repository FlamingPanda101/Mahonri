# David Berlinski

**Role:** Philosopher, mathematician, and author; senior fellow at the Discovery Institute and editor of *Inference: International Review of Science*.

## Bio
David Berlinski is a mathematician and essayist who holds a Ph.D. in philosophy from Princeton and has taught mathematics and philosophy at several universities. He is the author of popular books on mathematics and science — including *A Tour of the Calculus* and *The Devil's Delusion: Atheism and Its Scientific Pretensions* — and the essay collection *The Deniable Darwin and Other Essays*.[^1] A self-described secular Jew and religious agnostic, he is nonetheless a sharp critic of neo-Darwinism; he describes his own attitude toward intelligent design as "warm but distant."[^2] On the Hoover panel he presses the case that Darwin posed a "premature" question and built "a 19th-century local theory" that succeeds for small variations (beak size, fur density) but "entirely failed to explain" the emergence of biological complexity at the species level and above.[^3]
