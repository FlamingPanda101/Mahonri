# Deborah

**Who:** Prophetess and judge of Israel in the Book of Judges (chapters 4–5). She leads Israel in battle against the Canaanite king Jabin and his commander Sisera, alongside Barak.

**Role:** [[Jacob Wright]] cites Deborah (along with [[Esther]]) as an example of women in biblical narrative who model the survival-oriented leadership a small, defeated people needs — contrasting her with [[Gideon]]'s more direct military model.

## As a trace of the suppressed "great lady" (Barker)
[[Margaret Barker]] reads the introduction of Deborah in [[Judges 4|Judges 4:4-5]] as carrying titles of the divine-feminine "great lady": her name means **bee** (cf. Asenath's honeycomb), the archaic *d*/*g* interchange lets "Deborah" be read as ***gevirah*** (queen-mother / great lady), "wife of *Lapidoth*" is better "lady of the **torches/lamps**" (menorah lights), and she sits under the **palm tree** — the token of one entrusted with the secret knowledge of the holy of holies. See [[Deborah and Asenath Preserve the Lady of the Bush]] and [[The Palm Branch Signified the Secret Knowledge of the Holy of Holies]].
