# Elijah

Elijah is the Old Testament prophet of the northern kingdom of Israel, central to 1-2 Kings. His most famous miracle is the contest on Mount Carmel against the prophets of Baal, where he calls down fire from heaven to consume a water-soaked sacrifice ([[1 Kings 18.24|1 Kings 18:24]]).

## In the Miracle-Test Debate
The Carmel episode is invoked by skeptics (e.g., Doug of the Pine Creek channel) as a challenge: if Elijah could ignite a soaked sacrifice, Christians should be able to set a wet napkin alight through prayer. The source counters that such demands rest on the [[Miracle-Tests for God Commit the God-of-the-Gaps Fallacy|God-of-the-gaps fallacy]].
