# Philastus Hurlbut

Philastus Hurlbut was an early anti-Mormon agent whose first given name was actually "Doctor" — a folk tradition because he was a seventh son, not an earned title.[^1] In 1833 he was hired by Eber D. Howe, a skeptical freethinker living in Painesville, Ohio, near the Mormons in Kirtland, to travel to New York and collect derogatory statements from former neighbors of the Smith family.[^2]

## The 1833–34 "Affidavits"

Hurlbut gathered statements in 1833 that were published in 1834 (often called "the 1834 affidavits"), though the source stresses these are not formal legal affidavits but interview statements, a few notarized only to confirm identity, not truth.[^3] Because Hurlbut "was hired to go get the dirt" — "that was his mission" — the statements carry an obvious bias problem, and Lorenzo (or Benjamin/Orlando) Saunders later said Hurlbut could not get him to say the things he got others to say.[^4]

Hurlbut also gathered interviews in Conneaut, Ohio, to establish the now-discredited **Spalding theory** of the Book of Mormon.[^5] See:
