# Atahualpa

**Role:** Last independent Sapa Inca (emperor of the Inca Empire)
**Era:** 16th century (c. 1502–1533)
**Tradition:** Secular (Inca)

## Bio

Atahualpa was the last independent emperor of the Inca Empire, captured by [[Francisco Pizarro]]'s Spanish forces at Cajamarca in 1532 and executed in 1533.

## Relevance to the vault

Atahualpa appears in [[Ward Radio - Deep Dive Debunking Doubts About Horses in the Book of Mormon]] solely as the Inca ruler in the iconic story of Pizarro's mounted confrontation — the Spanish narrative of an emperor terrified by a horse he had never seen. [[Jonah Barnes]] argues this Andean episode, dramatized to assert Spanish superiority, became the cultural seed of the assumption that *all* of pre-Columbian America lacked horses. See [[The Pizarro-Atahualpa Story Is the Source of the No-Horses Assumption]].
