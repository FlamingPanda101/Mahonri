# Josh Coats

**Era:** Contemporary (2020s)
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint (LDS)
**Role:** Researcher

## Bio

Josh Coats is a Latter-day Saint researcher who published a paper in *Interpreter*
and presented at the FAIR conference applying a combinatorial mathematical model
to the question of whether the entire Book of Mormon text could fit on the gold
plates.[^1]

## Translation Density and Plate Count

Coats developed the "translation density" approach — counting English *characters*
(not words) and comparing the ratio of English to source-language characters
across Hebrew (~2.4:1), Demotic/hieratic Egyptian (~2.6–3.1:1), and Chinese
(~3.9:1) — to model how compactly reformed Egyptian could encode the text.[^2] He
also performed a garage experiment stacking copper discs of common-tin thickness
to measure inter-plate void space, yielding an estimate of **187–259 plates**.[^3]
He estimates the alloy contained less than 20% gold. See
[[Translation Density Shows the Book of Mormon Text Fits]] and
[[Tumbaga Alloy Explains Golden Appearance and Green Oxidation]].
