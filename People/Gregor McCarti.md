# Gregor McCarti

LDS author of *Eight Myths of the Great Apostasy* (Signature Books), a short revisionist book Day repeatedly cites and admires. (The transcript renders the name inconsistently — "McCarti," "McGuardi," "McGard" — so the spelling is uncertain.) McCarti argues *against* the traditional LDS apostasy picture: that the apostles were not necessarily all martyred, that ordinations continued, and that authority may not have been removed in a single clean break. Day uses him as the chief steelman for a **gradual** apostasy and against the idea that the priesthood vanished instantly.

## On apostasy and authority
- Chases down the relics/martyrdom traditions of the apostles.
- Argues that even after the apostles died, remaining ordained men would still hold authority — pressing the question against [[Bruce R. McConkie]]'s "keys departed" view. See [[Apostasy Was Complete When the Keys Departed]], [[Donatism and the LDS Model of Priesthood Validity]].
