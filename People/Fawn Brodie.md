# Fawn Brodie

Fawn McKay Brodie (1915–1981) was an American biographer and historian (later a professor at UCLA), and a former Latter-day Saint. Her 1945 biography of Joseph Smith, *No Man Knows My History*, is the single most influential naturalistic account of [[Joseph Smith]] and the [[Book of Mormon]].[^1]

## Role in the View of the Hebrews Theory

Brodie is the hinge figure who popularized the claim that the Book of Mormon depends on [[Ethan Smith]]'s *View of the Hebrews*. She devoted "two or three pages" to it and wrote that "although a direct dependence cannot be proven, the striking parallels cannot be a coincidence." After 1945, anti-Mormon publications "all just like are citing Brodie," and modern critics largely "piggyback off of Fawn Brodie."[^2] Notably she also "curb-stomps" the rival [[Solomon Spalding]] manuscript theory as incredible — replacing it with *View of the Hebrews*.
