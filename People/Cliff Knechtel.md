# Cliff Knechtel

**Tradition:** Protestant (evangelical)
**Role:** Christian apologist / campus evangelist

## Bio

Cliff Knechtel is an evangelical Christian apologist who appears on the Valuetainment channel (hosted by Patrick Bet-David) and is known for campus-evangelism videos defending theism and the Christian faith against atheism. The Stick of Joseph host [[Hayden Carroll]] expresses genuine appreciation for that apologetic work even while disputing him on the brothers question.[^1]

## The "Jesus and Satan are brothers" charge

In the clip the Stick of Joseph responds to, Knechtel lists Latter-day Saint theology among "big errors" Joseph Smith made, asserting: "Mormonism in its fundamental teachings teaches that Jesus and Lucifer are brothers… No. Jesus is the eternal God. Lucifer is a created angel who fell… They are not brothers." The Stick of Joseph treats this as the representative form of a widespread online polemic and answers it. See [[Jesus and Satan Being Spirit Brothers Follows From Rejecting the Trinity]] and [[Two Brothers Where the Younger Is Chosen Are Types of Christ and Lucifer]]; Hayden offers Knechtel an on-camera conversation.[^1]

## The same charge on the PBD / Valuetainment podcast (Ward Radio reaction)
Knechtle repeated the "Jesus and Lucifer are brothers" charge on Patrick Bet-David's Valuetainment podcast, where Bet-David's own framing grouped Latter-day Saints "within Christianity." A Ward Radio livestream ([[Cardon Ellis]] with [[Jacob Hansen]], [[Luke Hansen]], [[Brad Whitbeck]]) responded: the charge is not a "fundamental teaching," LDS scripture never makes Satan Christ's begotten equal ([[Moses 1.10|Moses 1]]), and the singling-out of the Trinity is religious bigotry.
