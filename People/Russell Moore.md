# Russell Moore

Evangelical theologian and editor-in-chief of *Christianity Today*; formerly president of the Southern Baptist Convention's Ethics & Religious Liberty Commission. He is the author of the article "Mormons Expect More of the Next Generation. Why Don't We?" that frames the [[Ward Radio - Why Everyone Is Jealous of Our High Demand Religion]] episode.

## Mormons Expect More of the Next Generation
Reflecting on his son Jonah's fascination with Salt Lake City and on Stephen Bullivant's book *The Nonverts*, Moore concedes that the Latter-day Saint missionary program is a transformative good even when it yields no converts — "even if you don't convert anybody, the benefits ... are immense."[^1] He argues that evangelicals fail their youth by expecting too little, diagnosing that young people leave "not because they've concluded that they don't need the church, but because they've concluded that the church doesn't need them."[^2] The hosts treat him as arguing in good faith ("your prose is second to none") while pressing that his article still euphemizes that Latter-day Saints are not Christians, citing their non-creedal view of the Trinity.[^3]

This concession is developed in [[The Mission Builds the Missionary Even Without Converts]] and [[Trust the Next Generation With Real Responsibility]]; the response to his Trinitarian boundary-drawing is [[Creedal Latitude on Baptism but Not the Trinity]].
