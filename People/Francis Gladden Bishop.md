# Francis Gladden Bishop

**Francis Gladden Bishop** (1809-1864) was an early convert to the Church of Jesus Christ and a serial schismatic who claimed prophetic authority and led a small breakaway movement (the "Gladdenites"); he was disciplined and excommunicated multiple times.

## Bio / Significance
Bishop is doctrinally a fringe claimant, not an authority. His relevance to the vault is strictly as a **textual witness**: for decades in Kirtland he was a neighbor of [[Martin Harris]] — the principal scribe for the lost 116 pages — and he published material apparently gleaned from Harris.

## Contribution
In the [[Ward Radio - The Sword of Laban Was the Sword of Joshua|Don Bradley interview]], Bishop is the source of the key external statement about the sword: in the early 1850s he printed that "the history of this sword is as follows: it was caused to be made by [[Joseph of Egypt|Joseph]] of old in Egypt, by the direction of God, and was in the hand of [[Joshua]] when he led the house of Israel into the land of Canaan, and after him it came down into the lineage of Joseph to [[Laban]]." [[Don Bradley]] treats this as a [[Multiple Attestation - Corroborating Apocryphal Sources|corroborating external source]] for the contents of the lost pages, credible because it converges with the internal Book of Mormon evidence rather than because Bishop himself is trustworthy. See [[The Sword of Laban Was the Sword of Joshua Forged by Joseph in Egypt]].
