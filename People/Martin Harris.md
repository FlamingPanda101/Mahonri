# Martin Harris

Martin Harris (1783–1875) was an early financial supporter of [[Joseph Smith]] and one of the Three Witnesses of the Book of Mormon. He lived in the Palmyra–Manchester area of New York.

## A Hostile-but-Believing Witness

The source uses Harris as an example of evidential strength: although Harris "has his problems with Joseph Smith" and left the church for a long time, he remained "consistently a true believer in Joseph Smith's prophetic abilities, in the golden plates, in the Book of Mormon translation, and in Joseph Smith's use of seer stones to translate the Book of Mormon."[^1] His testimony that Joseph participated in treasure hunting in the Palmyra–Manchester area is therefore corroboration from a believing source, helping establish the historical reliability of the treasure-seeking claims across the bias line.[^2]
