# Peter Singer

**Full Name:** Peter Albert David Singer
**Era:** Contemporary (1946–present)
**Tradition:** Secular utilitarian (atheist)
**Role:** Moral philosopher; professor of bioethics at Princeton University

## Bio

Peter Singer is an Australian moral philosopher, best known for his work in
applied ethics from a preference-utilitarian standpoint. He has held the
Ira W. DeCamp Professorship of Bioethics at Princeton University. His positions
on the moral status of infants and on euthanasia are among the most
controversial in contemporary ethics and are frequently cited by critics as
the logical endpoint of a purely rational, non-theistic ethics.

## Topics

### Infanticide as a Rational Conclusion
In the PragerU video on ethics, Prager cites "a distinguished professor of
philosophy at Princeton University" — Peter Singer — who, when asked whether he
would kill a disabled baby, answered "Yes, if that was in the best interests of
the baby and the family as a whole." Prager uses this to argue that no *purely
rational* refutation of the position is available; only a faith-based belief in
the image of God can supply one.[^1]

> "Can you offer a purely rational reason why the professor is wrong? The only
> reason I can offer is a belief that all human beings are created in God's
> image and are therefore infinitely precious."[^1]
