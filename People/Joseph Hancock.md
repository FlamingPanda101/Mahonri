# Joseph Hancock

Early Latter-day Saint who is the firsthand figure in the [[Falling of the Stars Prophecy]] narrative.

## The Night of the Falling Stars
According to the account [[Philo Dibble]] later recorded (in his 1892 diary), Hancock, lost while hunting on the 39th day after Joseph Smith's prophecy, lodged with a skeptic who mockingly produced a written copy of the prophecy. Hancock replied, "There is one night left of the time, and if Joseph said so, the stars will certainly fall tonight." That night — 12–13 November 1833 — the Leonid meteor storm fell, and Hancock watched the skeptic "turn pale as death." His testimony, transmitted through Dibble decades later, is the sole narrative source. See [[Mormonism with the Murf - Joseph Smith's Top 20 Fulfilled Prophecies]].[^1]
