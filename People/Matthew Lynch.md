# Matthew Lynch

**Role:** Old Testament scholar
**Affiliation:** Regent College (Vancouver); previously Westminster Theological Centre
**Era:** Contemporary
**Authority:** Tier A — peer-reviewed scholarship on monotheism in the Hebrew Bible.

## Bio

Matthew Lynch is a biblical scholar working on the Hebrew Bible, divine sovereignty, and the rhetoric of monotheism. His article **"Mapping Monotheism: Modes of Monotheistic Rhetoric in the Hebrew Bible"** (*Interpretation*, 2014) offers a taxonomy of the different rhetorical strategies by which the Old Testament affirms Yahweh's supremacy.

## Mapping Monotheism

In the indexed video, [[Gavin Ortlund]] recommends Lynch's "Mapping Monotheism" article as "a good taxonomy of various different rhetorical ways that the Old Testament affirms its own kind of monotheism." [[Dan McClellan]] responds that the arguments and scholarship he has marshaled "respond to all the arguments that Matthew's article makes." [^1]

> "if you want to see a good taxonomy of various different rhetorical ways that the Old Testament affirms its own kind of monotheism see Matthew Lynch's article mapping monotheism modes of monotheistic rhetoric in the Hebrew Bible." [^1]
