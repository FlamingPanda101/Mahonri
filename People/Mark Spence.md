# Mark Spence

**Full Name:** Mark Spence
**Era:** Contemporary (2020s)
**Tradition:** Evangelical Protestant
**Role:** Apologist / evangelist, Living Waters ministry

## Bio

Mark Spence is an evangelical apologist and evangelist associated with **Living Waters**, the ministry founded by [[Ray Comfort]] (the "Are you a good person?" / Way of the Master street-evangelism method). The Living Waters YouTube channel has well over a million subscribers. Spence appears in street-evangelism footage confronting members of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints with the standard Living Waters playbook: pressing the [[Sola Scriptura - Scripture Self-Attests Its Authority|sola scriptura]] claim that the 66-book Protestant Bible is the "sole infallible rule of faith and practice," and charging Mormonism with works-righteousness.

He is the named antagonist of [[Lets Talk w Hayden Carroll - Evangelical Pastor Is Ignorant About Mormonism]], an unscripted rebuttal video by LDS apologist [[Hayden Carroll]]. Carroll accuses Spence of misrepresenting LDS belief — equating salvation "after all we can do" with earned salvation (see [[After All We Can Do Means Despite All We Can Do]] and [[LDS Salvation Is by Grace Not Earned by Works]]) — and of abusing scripture, especially deploying [[Deuteronomy 18.20-22|Deuteronomy 18]] as a mechanical false-prophet test against [[Joseph Smith]] (rebutted in [[Deuteronomy 18 Condemns the Presumptuous Prophet Not the Mistaken One]]). Carroll repeatedly questions whether the young Latter-day Saint Spence interviews was a "paid actor," and faults Spence for telling the convert not to study other sources while himself pointing to a single source (the Protestant canon).

## Topics

### Living Waters Method — "Are You a Good Person?"
Spence operates within Comfort's evangelistic script, which opens by getting an interlocutor to admit they are "a good person," then walks them through the Decalogue (lying, theft, lust, taking the Lord's name in vain) to convict them of sin and their need of the cross. Carroll grants the underlying point — that human works "can only condemn us" and salvation is by the merits of Christ — while arguing Spence wrongly assumes Latter-day Saints deny it.

### Sola Scriptura / Final Authority
Spence asserts the Bible is "God's inerrant infallible word that cannot change" and the believer's "ultimate source of authority," and presses LDS interlocutors on why they would pray about the truth of the Book of Mormon. Carroll counters with the standard LDS challenge — that no prophet ever said the present 66-book canon is the sole infallible authority — locating the doctrine in the post-biblical creeds rather than scripture itself.
