# Randolph Jefferson

**Era:** 18th–19th century (1755–1815)
**Role:** Historical figure (Thomas Jefferson's brother)

## Bio
Randolph Jefferson was the younger brother of [[Thomas Jefferson]]. Because the Jefferson Y-chromosome is shared across the paternal line, Randolph (and his sons) carry the same haplotype identified in the 1998 [[Eugene Foster]] DNA study of [[Sally Hemings]]'s descendants.

## Cited For
On Ward Radio, Randolph is offered as the more probable father of Sally Hemings's children. The hosts cite historical records of Randolph frequenting the Monticello slave quarters and being "very friendly" with the enslaved — and note he was present around the time of conception while Thomas was in Paris. Combined with Foster's own caution that the DNA proves only *a* Jefferson, this makes Randolph, not Thomas, the "inference to the best explanation."
