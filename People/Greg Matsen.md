# Greg Matsen

**Full Name:** Greg Matsen
**Era:** Contemporary (2020s)
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint (LDS)
**Role:** Content creator (Cwic Media / cwicmedia.com), Egypt tour leader with Go and Do Travel

## Bio

Greg Matsen is an LDS content creator and the founder of Cwic Media, who leads
study tours of Egypt for Go and Do Travel. On site he focuses on reading
Egyptian temple iconography as a window onto Latter-day Saint temple worship,
the Pearl of Great Price, and the restored gospel.[^1] He reads hieroglyphs at a
serious-amateur level and frames Egyptian religion not as "mythology" but as a
genuine, if incomplete, "form of the truth."[^2]

## Topics

### Egyptian Temple Typology and the Endowment
Matsen's central thesis is that "what we have in the Book of Abraham and what we
have in the temple can be found everywhere" in Egypt — guardian-serpents and
sphinxes, the [[Osiris Is a Type Not a Person - The Blessed Dead Become Osiris|Osiris death-resurrection myth]],
washing/anointing and "ritual embrace" scenes at the veil, and Abrahamic-covenant
blessing-rows.[^2] See [[The Stick of Joseph - Temple Knowledge Encoded in Egyptian Mythology]].

### The Facsimiles as Snapshots of a Whole Myth
He argues each Book of Abraham facsimile is "a snapshot of the whole myth" of
death and resurrection, defending Joseph Smith's "alive Abraham" reading against
the critics' "dead Osiris" charge via the feet-apart / hands-up iconographic
convention.[^3] See [[The Facsimiles Encode Abraham's Endowment Through the Osiris Myth]].
