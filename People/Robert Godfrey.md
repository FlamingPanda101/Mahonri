# Robert Godfrey

**W. Robert Godfrey** is a Reformed church historian and president emeritus of **Westminster Seminary California**. He contributed the essay "What Do We Mean by Sola Scriptura?" to the 1995 anthology *Sola Scriptura! The Protestant Position on the Bible*.

## Self-Authentication via Unanimity
[[Joe Heschmeyer]] quotes Godfrey as a representative of the move that grounds self-authentication in *church consensus*:

> "The self-authenticating character of the canon is demonstrated by the remarkable unanimity reached by the people of God on the canon" [^1].

Heschmeyer's reply: invoking the church's collective recognition as evidence of *self*-authentication is a "bold gambit" that concedes the very thing the Reformers built the doctrine to deny — that the **church** is needed to know the canon. See [[Self-Authentication via Church Consensus Backs Into the Catholic Model]].
