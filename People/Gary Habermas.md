# Gary Habermas

**Role:** Evangelical philosopher, historian, and apologist; specialist on the resurrection of Jesus
**Era:** Contemporary (b. 1950), American
**Tradition:** Evangelical Protestant

## Bio

Gary Habermas is an American Evangelical philosopher and historian, distinguished research professor at Liberty University, best known for developing and popularizing the **minimal facts** argument for the resurrection of Jesus. The method establishes a base of *early, independent, even unsympathetic* sources, distills from them a short list of *core facts that critical scholars across the spectrum also grant*, and then runs a best-explanation analysis asking which hypothesis accounts for those facts with the greatest scope and power and the least *ad hoc* contrivance. He has catalogued the views of thousands of scholars on the resurrection and is widely cited (alongside William Lane Craig) in contemporary resurrection apologetics.

## Relevance to the vault

Habermas matters in this vault not for the resurrection directly but because his method is borrowed and re-applied to the Restoration. [[Josh Gailey]] takes the minimal-facts argument "unchanged" and runs it on the coming forth of the Book of Mormon — see [[The Minimal Facts Method Applied to the Gold Plates]]. Gailey's "PET" rubric (**proximity, enemies, unlikely testimonies**), drawn from Habermas and Craig's case for the empty tomb, is then applied to the empty stone box in the New York hill — see [[The Stone Box Is the Empty Tomb of the Book of Mormon]]. The argument is deliberately ecumenical: it asks Christians who accept the resurrection on Habermas-style grounds to apply the *same* method to the gold plates.
