# Daniel Simons

**Role:** Experimental / cognitive psychologist; researcher of attention and perception
**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** Academic cognitive science

## Bio

Daniel Simons is a cognitive psychologist best known for the "invisible gorilla" line of research on **inattentional blindness** — the finding that observers focused on a task routinely fail to notice salient, unexpected events in plain view. [[Jordan Peterson]] introduces him as "Dan Simons," noting that Simons created the follow-up "monkey business illusion" because "his original video got so popular… virally popular that everybody has seen the invisible gorilla." [^1]

## Relevance to Epistemology

Simons's experiments are the empirical backbone of Peterson's argument that human perception is far more limited and selective than common sense assumes. Viewers counting basketball passes miss a gorilla walking through the scene, a departing player, and a color change to the backdrop — even when primed to expect something unusual. Peterson uses this to overturn the older hypothesis that anomalies automatically capture attention. See:
