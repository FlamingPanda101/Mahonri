# Peter Enns

Biblical scholar (Ph.D., Harvard) and author of *The Bible Tells Me So*, a popular-level book Butler recommends. Its thesis (as relayed): we typically read the Bible through the lens of what we *want* it to say and what our preachers taught, which can make us unable to read what it actually says.

Butler invokes Enns for the unvarnished translation of [[Galatians 5.12|Galatians 5:12]] — Paul wishing the circumcision party would emasculate themselves — to argue the New Testament honestly preserves books and authors in real conflict, not "different perspectives" on the same thing. See [[Paul vs James and the Judaizer Conflict]], [[New Testament Books Reflect Rival Communities]].
