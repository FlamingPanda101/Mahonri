# Ben Kaiser

**Era:** Contemporary (2020s)
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint (LDS)
**Role:** Content creator, Ward Radio guest panelist (based in Michigan — "the belly of the Midwestern Evangelical Beast"). On X as "Benjamin Kaiser" (two R's).

## Bio

Ben Kaiser is a Latter-day Saint commentator who appeared as a guest on the [[Ward Radio]] livestream discussing [[Hayden Carroll]]'s creation dilemma. Hosted by [[Cardon Ellis]], he was introduced as joining from Michigan. He is not academically credentialed; his role on the stream was to probe the argument's logic, especially the foreknowledge-vs-causation distinction and the role of agency.[^1]

## Topics

### Foreknowledge, Causation, and John 9
Kaiser pressed Carroll on whether divine foreknowledge entails causation (Carroll: no, and the argument doesn't need it), and offered [[John 9.1-3|John 9:1-3]] — where Jesus says the man's blindness was caused by neither his sin nor his parents' — as a scriptural separation of suffering from guilt.[^2] See [[Foreknowledge Is Not Causation Objection]].

### Agency as the Core LDS Strength
Kaiser argued that the creedal-Christian difficulty with predestination "boils down to" an incomplete understanding of agency, contrasting mere freedom of choice with full LDS agency (multiple choices, real differing outcomes, and knowledge of them).[^3] See [[Free Will - Atheist Determinism vs LDS Co-Eternality]], [[LDS Premortal Consent Removes Gods Culpability]].
