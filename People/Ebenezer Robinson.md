# Ebenezer Robinson

**Role:** Early LDS printer and editor (Nauvoo); later RLDS-affiliated
**Era:** 19th century (1816–1891)
**Tradition:** LDS, later RLDS

## Bio

Ebenezer Robinson was an early Latter-day Saint printer and an editor of the Nauvoo *Times and Seasons*. Though later associated with the Reorganized (RLDS) tradition that denied Joseph Smith's polygamy, Robinson wrote to [[Joseph Smith III]] that [[Hyrum Smith]] had personally taught him the doctrine of plural marriage and told him he had heard the revelation given to Joseph — concluding that a denial of these facts "sounds to me like a great lie."[^1]

## Polygamy Testimony

Robinson is a key insider witness in [[Brian Hales]]'s case against polygamy denial: a man embedded in the very RLDS milieu that built its identity on Joseph's monogamy, yet who affirmed from personal knowledge that Hyrum taught him the doctrine. See:
