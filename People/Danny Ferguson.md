# Danny Ferguson

**Role:** Postdoctoral researcher, Department of Biology, North Dakota State University
**Field:** Biology education; evolution acceptance and pedagogy
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint scientist

## Credentials
Danny Ferguson (Daniel G. Ferguson) is a postdoctoral researcher in biology education at North Dakota State University, with a PhD from BYU where he studied pedagogical approaches to teaching evolution to religious audiences while preserving their religious identity. He grew up in an active Latter-day Saint family in Mapleton, Utah, served a mission in the Philippines, and also investigates the influence of popular culture (e.g., Pokémon) on student understanding of evolution. His research interests include systems-thinking skills and modeling in students' evolution knowledge.[^1]

**Why cited / authority basis:** A credentialed researcher in evolution pedagogy writing for a Latter-day Saint readership about empirically tested ways to teach evolution without eroding faith. Authority is *B* (scholarly, on-topic, in-faith).

## Contribution
Ferguson is lead author (with Ethan Tolman, Spencer Shumway, and Cassidy Shively) of "Using a Reconciliation Approach to Teach Evolution May Help Religious Students Remain Faithful." As former student researchers in [[Jamie L. Jensen]]'s BYU lab, the authors present the empirical finding that a reconciliation pedagogy (patterned on Barnes and Brownell's ReCCEE) raises religious students' acceptance of evolution while leaving their religiosity intact — and they share personal reconciliation stories and student survey/essay data.[^1] His own turning point came when a faithful Latter-day Saint professor at Utah Valley University shared, in a voluntary seminar, that he accepted evolution and had reconciled it with his faith.
