# Kelly (Brain Perspective)

## Who
Kelly is an **evangelical, self-described "born-again" Protestant apologist** who runs the YouTube channel **"Brain Perspective"** and engages Roman Catholics, Eastern Orthodox, and Latter-day Saints in debate.[^1] He mentions a master's-level class on the Bible while citing OT-citation statistics.[^2] He states he is **not** Calvinist / Reformed / presuppositionalist.[^3]

## Why he matters here
In the debate [[Lets Talk w Hayden Carroll - Is the Bible the Final Authority Debate|"Is the Bible the Final Authority?"]] Kelly argues the **affirmative** — defending *sola scriptura* and the 66-book canon as the infallible, ultimate authority. His method is **cumulative/evidential** rather than presuppositional: scripture self-attests, Jesus and the apostles treated it as authoritative, the early fathers cite nearly the whole NT, and even LDS authorities exalt the Bible — therefore it is the standard by which any later prophet must be tested.[^4]

## Key arguments (linked concepts)
- [[Sola Scriptura - Scripture Self-Attests Its Authority]] — his main case.
- [[Scripture as the Test of Prophets and Revelation]] — his practical thesis (test a Joseph Smith by scripture).
- [[The Church Once Accepted Books It Later Rejected]] — he counters with the Spirit-witness reliability problem.
- [[LDS Reject the Song of Solomon as Inspired]] — he disputes Hayden's "we have 65" claim.

## Debate posture
Evidential and historically-driven; recounts coming to scripture's authority via *external* investigation of Jesus' life, death, and resurrection — a stance Hayden exploits to argue the Bible is therefore not *ultimate* (something prior grounds it). See [[The Circularity Charge Against Self-Attesting Scripture]].
