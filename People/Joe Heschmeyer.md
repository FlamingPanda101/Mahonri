# Joe Heschmeyer

Catholic apologist with **Catholic Answers** and host of the **Shameless Popery** podcast (referred to playfully in this video as "Shameless Potpourri"). A former seminarian and the author of *The Early Church Was the Catholic Church* and *Pope Peter*, Heschmeyer specializes in the patristic and canonical case for Catholicism against Protestant and Latter-day Saint positions.

## The Self-Authenticating Canon
Guesting on [[Trent Horn]]'s *Council of Trent*, Heschmeyer argues that [[John Calvin]]'s "feel it in your bones" model of canon recognition is self-refuting (the Reformers disagreed with each other on the books), historically false (the [[Augustine|church fathers]] decided by consensus, not feelings), and structurally identical to [[Angel Moroni|Moroni]]'s burning-bosom test. See [[Self-Authenticating Canon - Feel It in Your Bones]], [[Reformers Disagree With One Another on the Canon]], and [[Moroni-Calvin Parallel - The Self-Authentication Gambit]] [^1].

## Against Kruger's Self-Authentication
He contends that [[Michael Krueger]]'s and [[Robert Godfrey]]'s appeals to *church recognition* of self-authenticating qualities quietly "back into the Catholic model," while attacking a strawman ("thermostat") Catholic view that the First Vatican Council explicitly condemned. See [[Self-Authentication via Church Consensus Backs Into the Catholic Model]] and [[Krugers Strawman of the Catholic View of the Canon]] [^2].

## Critique of Mormonism on Pints with Aquinas
On Matt Fradd's *Pints with Aquinas* (Daily Wire), Heschmeyer delivered an ~80-minute critique of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, reacted to by Ward Radio. His arguments: Mormonism lacks fixed doctrine and constantly changes ([[Doctrine Is Defined by the Unified Voice of the Highest Authorities]], [[Catholic Doctrinal Development Is the Same Double Standard]]); Brigham Young claimed tabernacle infallibility ([[Tabernacle Infallibility Misreads Brigham Young]]); the race/priesthood teaching was original and reversed in 1978 ([[The Priesthood Ban Was the Development Not Its Reversal]]); Joseph Smith fails the failed-prophecy test ([[Prophetic Failed-Prophecy Test Is Self-Refuting for Cessationists]]); the First Vision accounts contradict ([[First Vision Accounts Are No More Contradictory Than the Gospels]]); the contingency argument disproves both atheism and Mormonism, so "Latter-day Saints are atheists" ([[Latter-day Saints Are Atheists Charge Rebutted]], [[The Uncaused Cause Is Aristotle Not Scripture]], [[Contingency Argument Permits Plural Elohim]]); and the Church will cave on abortion and homosexuality ([[LDS Abortion Position Follows From Pre-Existence Not Cultural Caving]], [[LDS Retention Is Among the Highest of Any Tradition]]). The panel credited him with being unusually charitable and caveated for the genre [^3].
