# Mary Magdalene

A prominent follower of [[Jesus of Nazareth|Jesus]] in the Gospels, present at the crucifixion and the first witness of the resurrection. In Luke she is introduced (Luke 8:2) immediately after the account of the woman who anoints Jesus from an alabaster box.

## As the Bride Anointing the Bridegroom
[[Andrea Woodmansee]] suggests the woman of [[Luke 7.36-50|Luke 7]] who anoints Jesus from the alabaster box — and the parallel anointers in Matthew 26 and Mark 14 — may be Mary Magdalene, and reads the act as a **wedding rite**: the alabaster oil was dowry ointment kept to anoint a husband at death, so the woman recognizes Christ as the **bridegroom** who would redeem her from captivity to sin. Woodmansee notes the anointer "had to be very close" to Jesus to know he would die. See [[The Alabaster Box Was the Bride's Dowry Oil to Anoint the Bridegroom]].
