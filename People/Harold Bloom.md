# Harold Bloom

Yale literary critic (1930–2019), known for his theory of the "anxiety of influence"
and his unorthodox appreciation for religious texts as literature. Though not a
Mormon, Bloom had a notable fascination with [[Joseph Smith]].

## Assessment of the King Follett Discourse

Bloom once said that the [[King Follett Discourse]] is "one of the truly remarkable
sermons ever preached in America."[^1] This judgment — coming from a non-Mormon,
secular literary critic at Yale — is frequently cited by LDS scholars as evidence
of the discourse's significance beyond the Mormon tradition.
