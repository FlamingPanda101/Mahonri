# Polycarp of Smyrna

One of the **apostolic fathers** — early bishops who personally knew the apostles. Tradition holds that Polycarp sat at the feet of [[John the Evangelist|John]] and knew others of the Twelve; he died a martyr in 156 AD at age 86. Day uses Polycarp (with [[Ignatius of Antioch]] and [[Clement of Rome]]) to illustrate his model of early authority: the closer you are to the source, the more authoritative you are — Polycarp is "legit" because he knew the men who knew Jesus. See [[Authority in the Early Church Was Proximity to the Apostles]].

Day's reference for the Polycarp/John connection: Eusebius, *Ecclesiastical History* 5.20.
