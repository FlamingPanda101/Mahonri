# Robert Card

Therapist reassigned to treat [[Allen Hadfield]] after Hadfield refused to admit guilt to his first court-ordered therapist [[Paul Whitehead]]. Card reported that Hadfield was "vibrantly normal" and could find nothing to indicate he was disposed to any kind of sexual deviance, pedophilia, or the like — a clinical assessment directly undercutting the conviction.

## Assessment of Hadfield
His evaluation is a key piece of evidence in the case against the panic's reliability. See [[The Allen Hadfield Trial as a Miscarriage of Justice]] and [[Recovered Memory and Coercive Therapy Produce False Accusations]] [^1].
