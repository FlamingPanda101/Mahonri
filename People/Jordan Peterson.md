# Jordan Peterson

**Full Name:** Jordan Bernt Peterson
**Era:** Contemporary (1962–present)
**Tradition:** Christian-adjacent / mythological-psychological (declines a literal-supernatural creed; "acts as if God exists")
**Role:** Clinical psychologist, former professor (University of Toronto), author, public intellectual

## Bio

Jordan Peterson is a Canadian clinical psychologist and former University of Toronto professor who became a prominent public intellectual in the late 2010s. He is the author of *Maps of Meaning* (1999) and *12 Rules for Life* (2018). His work fuses depth psychology (Jung), evolutionary biology, and narrative/mythological interpretation of religious texts, especially the Bible read "psychologically."

## Positions in this dialogue

In his Vancouver debate with [[Sam Harris]] (moderated by [[Bret Weinstein]]), Peterson argued that:

- **Metaphorical / narrative truth.** Some claims are "literally false" but produce better outcomes if acted upon, occupying an intermediate truth-space; complex narratives must be read holistically, not proof-texted. See [[Metaphorical Truth and Narrative Reading]].
- **Facts require an a priori interpretive structure.** Drawing on Kant, he argued you cannot derive values directly from facts because an evolved, story-shaped interpretive framework mediates all perception. See [[You Cannot Get From Facts to Values Without an Interpretive Structure]].
- **An evolutionary / functional definition of God.** "I act as if he exists"; God as the highest value, the voice of conscience, "that which selects among men," and truthful speech embodied. See [[God as the Highest Value and Truthful Speech]].
- **Religious stories as evolutionary heuristics** that encode practical wisdom from past environments. See [[Religious Texts as Evolutionary Heuristics]].

He cites [[Friedrich Nietzsche]] on the collapse of belief systems and references E.O. Wilson's *consilience* method.
