# Cardinal Cajetan

**Full name:** Thomas Cajetan (Tommaso de Vio), 1469–1534
**Role:** Cardinal, Dominican theologian, papal legate
**Era:** Reformation (early 16th century)
**Tradition:** Roman Catholic (Thomist)

## Bio

Thomas Cajetan was a Dominican, cardinal, and the preeminent Thomist commentator of his age, famous for his commentary on Aquinas's *Summa Theologiae*. As papal legate he interrogated Martin Luther at Augsburg in 1518. Despite his orthodoxy, in his biblical commentaries he questioned the canonicity of the Old Testament deuterocanon and even doubted several New Testament books (Hebrews, James, 2–3 John, Jude).

## Relevance to the vault

Cajetan is a key witness for the argument that the biblical canon remained genuinely open until the Council of Trent (1546). Because "one of Luther's strongest opponents" himself questioned the deuterocanon, the vault uses him to rebut the charge that Protestants "removed books" — there was no settled, defined canon to remove from. He anchors the cluster on rival, unsettled canon traditions before Trent.
