# Abinadi

**Role:** Book of Mormon prophet and martyr
**Era:** Nephite history (~150 BC, narrative setting)
**Tradition:** Book of Mormon figure (LDS scripture)

## Bio
Abinadi is a Nephite prophet who confronts the wicked King Noah and his priests, expounds the law and the coming of the Redeemer, and is martyred by fire. His sermon in Mosiah 12-16 includes the much-discussed passage on Christ as "the Father and the Son."

## The Father and the Son
Abinadi's words in [[Mosiah 15.1-5|Mosiah 15:1-5]] — that Christ is "the Father and the Son" and "one God" — are cited as a Modalist/Trinitarian proof-text. The panel argues that, read with [[Mosiah 3.8|Mosiah 3:8]] and [[Mosiah 5.7|Mosiah 5:7]], Abinadi is describing Christ's *roles* (Father by divine conception/creation and covenant adoption; Son by the flesh), not collapsing the persons of the Godhead. See [[Mosiah 15 Father and Son Explained]].
