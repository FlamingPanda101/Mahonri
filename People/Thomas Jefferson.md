# Thomas Jefferson

**Era:** 1743–1826
**Tradition:** Deist (anti-creedal; "famously not a Christian")
**Role:** Principal author of the U.S. Declaration of Independence

## Bio

Thomas Jefferson drafted the Declaration of Independence and was the third U.S. president. In the dialogue he is the worked example of a non-Christian founder using secularized-Christian language.[^1]

## Topics

### "Sacred and Undeniable" to "Self-Evident"
[[Glenn Scrivener]] notes Jefferson's first draft read "we hold these truths to be **sacred and undeniable**," which [[Benjamin Franklin]] edited to "**self-evident**" — Scrivener's emblem of the move from explicitly Christian to "air we breathe" assumption. Scrivener also claims Jefferson wrote "created" but not "creator," with "creator" added later (by Congress). See [[Secularized Founders Used Christian Language]].[^1]

### The Slaveholder Contradiction (O'Connor's Counter)
[[Alex OConnor|Alex O'Connor]] notes "Jefferson held 600 slaves as he wrote that," and that Jefferson was "famously anti-Christian," to argue the founders did not see themselves in a Christian project. See [[Secularized Founders Used Christian Language]].[^2]
