# Steven Bullock

**Full name:** Steven C. Bullock
**Role:** Historian of early America; scholar of American Freemasonry
**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** Academic (non-LDS)

## Bio
Steven C. Bullock is a professor of history and the author of *Revolutionary Brotherhood: Freemasonry and the Transformation of the American Social Order, 1730–1840* (University of North Carolina Press, 1996), a standard academic history of Freemasonry in the early American republic.

## Cited on the content of Masonic ritual
[[Alex Arnold]] uses Bullock's *Revolutionary Brotherhood* as the "baseline" documentation of what early-19th-century Masonic ritual actually contained — handshakes, tokens, and words; a three-degree structure; oaths of secrecy; and symbolic content centered on the **Hiram Abiff** legend (a craftsman murdered for refusing to reveal Masonic secrets), which the initiate *observes or represents* rather than enacting as a first-person character. Arnold's argument is that Bullock's documentation shows Masonry lacks the *specific* endowment content — a first-person creation drama, a veil ceremony, sacred garments encoding First-Temple theology, deification theology, and divine-council cosmology — so the "Joseph borrowed it from Masonry" charge fails on its own terms. See [[The Masonry Objection Must Locate the Specific Content Not the General Pattern]].[^1]
