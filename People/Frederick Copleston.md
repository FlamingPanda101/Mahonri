# Frederick Copleston

**Full name:** Frederick Charles Copleston (1907–1994)
**Role:** Jesuit priest, historian of philosophy
**Era:** Contemporary (20th century)
**Tradition:** Roman Catholic (Jesuit)

## Bio

Frederick Copleston was an English Jesuit priest and philosopher best known for his magisterial nine-volume *A History of Philosophy*, long a standard reference in the field. He famously debated Bertrand Russell on the existence of God on BBC radio in 1948, defending a version of the argument from contingency. His work bridged scholastic metaphysics and modern philosophy.

## Relevance to the vault

Copleston appears in the moral-and-existence-of-God material as shorthand for the rigorous "contingency" style of natural-theology argument. The vault notes that C. S. Lewis's *Mere Christianity*, written for wartime radio audiences, deliberately *avoided* "Copleston-style contingency arguments" in favor of a popular moral argument — situating Copleston as the more technical philosophical alternative to Lewis's accessible approach.
