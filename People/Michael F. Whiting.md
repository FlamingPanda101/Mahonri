# Michael F. Whiting

**Full name:** Michael F. Whiting
**Role:** Professor of Biology, BYU; Director, Bean Life Science Museum; evolutionary biologist (entomology/phylogenetics)
**Era:** Contemporary (BYU faculty since 1997)
**Tradition:** The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (LDS)

## Bio

Michael F. Whiting is a professor in BYU's Department of Biology and director of the BYU
Bean Life Science Museum. After graduating from BYU in 1990, he earned a doctorate in
entomology at Cornell University and completed a postdoctoral fellowship at the American
Museum of Natural History, returning to BYU as faculty in 1997. He has served as director
of the BYU DNA Sequencing Center, head curator of insects, and a program director for the
National Science Foundation in Systematic Biology, and holds the Robertson Endowed
Professorship in genetics. His research reconstructs evolutionary relationships among
insects using DNA and morphology; he has authored 150+ publications and received 20+ NSF
grants, including an Early Career Award.[^1]

## On Evolutionary Biology at BYU

Whiting's chapter documents that BYU has taught evolution for over a century and built a
nationally recognized, prolifically funded research program — teaching the subject better
than most universities while doing so "in the light of the gospel." See
[[Evolutionary Biology at BYU Is an Academic Success Story]] and
[[Teach the Best Science in the Light of the Gospel]].[^2] He notes that BYU evolutionary
biologists' scientific credentials have positioned them to help address criticisms of the
faith, including questions about [[Founder Effect and Genetic Drift Erase a Small Founding Signal|DNA and the Book of Mormon]],
and he cites his own 2003 *Journal of Book of Mormon Studies* essay "DNA and the Book of
Mormon: A Phylogenetic Perspective."[^3]
