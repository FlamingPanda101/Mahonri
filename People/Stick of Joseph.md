# Stick of Joseph

**Type:** YouTube channel / podcast (entity)
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint (LDS)
**Hosts:** [[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)|David Butler]] & Mike Day
**Focus:** Deep-dive temple and scripture apologetics; retention-oriented teaching

## Overview
**Stick of Joseph** is an LDS apologetics YouTube channel and podcast hosted by David Butler and Mike Day, known for long-form "deep dives" into temple symbolism, ancient context, and the scriptures. Its self-described mission is primarily *retention* — "to wake Latter-day Saints up to the beauty of the scriptures so that they can become firm on that foundation of Christ" — an inward-facing posture that contrasts with outward campus evangelism channels. The name alludes to Ezekiel 37:16 ("the stick of Joseph"), a passage Latter-day Saints read as prophetic of the Book of Mormon. The channel also hosts the **Stick of Judah** lecture series featuring scholars such as [[Stephen Smoot]], and brings on guests like [[Daniel C. Peterson|Daniel Peterson]], [[Hayden Carroll]], and others for topical episodes.

## Relevance
Stick of Joseph is one of the vault's recurring **source entities**: many notes cite its episodes for LDS-friendly readings of the temple, the divine feminine, Hebrew ritual, and responses to atheism and the Trinity. Episodes feed arguments on fine-tuning ([[Fine-Tuning and the Teleological Argument from Alma 30]]), presuppositional method ([[Presuppositional Apologetics - CS Lewis Sun Analogy]]), the divine council / divine feminine, and whether Muhammad "undercuts" Joseph Smith. Because its content is host commentary (often with scholarly guests) rather than peer-reviewed work, it sits at a mid evidence tier in the vault — valuable for framing and for surfacing scholarship, weighted accordingly.
