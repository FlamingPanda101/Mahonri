# John Conway

**Full Name:** John Horton Conway
**Era:** 1937–2020
**Field:** Mathematics (group theory, number theory, foundations of physics)
**Affiliation:** University of Cambridge; later Princeton University

## Bio

John Horton Conway was one of the most inventive mathematicians of the
twentieth century — inventor of the cellular-automaton "Game of Life," the
surreal numbers, and a major contributor to group theory and knot theory. He
held the John von Neumann Chair at Princeton.

## Topics

### The Free Will Theorem
With his Princeton colleague [[Simon Kochen]], Conway proved the **Free Will
Theorem** (2006, refined 2009), which states roughly that *if experimenters have
a certain freedom in choosing how to set up a measurement, then elementary
particles must have an equivalent freedom in their response* — a response not
determined by the prior history of the universe. [[Inspiring Philosophy|Michael Jones]] cites the theorem as showing that genuine freedom requires an
indeterministic universe rather than a clockwork-deterministic one.[^1]
