# James Barker

**Role:** New Testament scholar; author of *Writing and Rewriting the Gospels*
**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** Academic biblical studies

## Bio
James Barker is a New Testament scholar cited by [[Hugo Mendez]] as the author of *Writing and Rewriting the Gospels*, a recent book on the compositional process of the canonical Gospels.

## Gospels Composed in Drafts and Stages
Méndez cites Barker's chapter one for the point that ancient authors sketched stories on tablets and reworked drafts before committing them to a final scroll — explaining how a single author could leave minor "seams" in John. See [[Single Authorship of John Against Redaction Layers]] [^1].

## John Knew the Synoptics
Barker is named alongside [[Mark Goodacre]] as a recent scholar who argues that John knew and used the Synoptic Gospels. See [[John Knew the Synoptics]] [^2].
