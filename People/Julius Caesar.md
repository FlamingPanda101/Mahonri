# Julius Caesar

**Era:** 100 – 44 BCE
**Tradition:** Roman
**Role:** Roman general and statesman

## Bio

Julius Caesar conquered Gaul and left his own account in the *Commentarii de Bello Gallico*.[^1]

## Topics

### Human Sacrifice as a Pretext for Conquest
[[Alex OConnor|Alex O'Connor]] cites Caesar's stated justification for invading Gaul — that the Gauls practiced human sacrifice — as a parallel "civilizing" rationale that one *could* (too simply) credit Rome with for abolishing human sacrifice. O'Connor's point: by the same lazy logic one could pin anti-human-sacrifice values on Rome rather than the "Judeo-Christian" tradition (which contains [[Genesis 22|Abraham and Isaac]] and [[Exodus 22.29|Exodus 22:29]]). See [[Surviving Traces of Human Sacrifice in the Hebrew Bible]] and [[Both the Jewish and Roman Worlds Were Brutal]].[^1]
