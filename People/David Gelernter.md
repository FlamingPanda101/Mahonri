# David Gelernter

**Role:** Professor of computer science at Yale University; author and essayist.

## Bio
David Gelernter is a professor of computer science at Yale and the author of several books, including *The Tides of Mind: Uncovering the Spectrum of Consciousness*.[^1] A pioneer in parallel computation, he is best known to a wider public for the essay "Giving Up Darwin," published in the *Claremont Review of Books*, in which he reports that Stephen Meyer's *Darwin's Doubt* and David Berlinski's *The Deniable Darwin* "convinced me that Darwin has failed."[^2] On the Hoover panel he stresses that he still finds Darwin's theory "beautiful" and abandons it only with regret, locating the decisive problem in molecular biology — the difficulty of generating a new protein, and therefore a new gene, by chance.[^3] He also describes the "bitter," sometimes "violent" academic rejection that meets anyone who challenges Darwinism, calling it an attack on a worldview functioning as "an emergency religion."[^4]
