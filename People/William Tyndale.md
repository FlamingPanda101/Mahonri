# William Tyndale

**Full name:** William Tyndale (Tindale)
**Era:** c. 1494–1536
**Tradition:** Protestant (English Reformation)
**Role:** Bible translator, reformer, martyr

## Bio

William Tyndale was an English reformer and the first to translate the New Testament into English directly from the Greek (1526), along with portions of the Old Testament from the Hebrew. Executed for heresy in 1536, his renderings were absorbed into successive English Bibles and form much of the verbal substrate of the King James Version.

[[Dan McClellan]] cites Tyndale to make a chronological point against KJV-onlyism: the King James Version (1611) is "a very conservative revision of a very conservative revision of a very conservative revision going back almost a century" to the New Testament of Tyndale and the work of [[Miles Coverdale]] — so its English was *already* archaic when it was first published. See [[The KJV Was Already Out of Date in 1611]].[^1]
