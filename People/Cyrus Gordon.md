# Cyrus Gordon

Cyrus H. Gordon (1908–2001), eminent American Orientalist and Semitic philologist (Brandeis University, New York University), a founding figure of Ugaritic studies and author of standard reference works including *Ugaritic Textbook*. In [[Alex OConnor - There Are Multiple Gods in the Bible Not Just One - Dan McClellan]], [[Dan McClellan]] cites Gordon (alongside [[Anne Draffkorn]]) as having argued in a published paper that the Hebrew *ha-elohim* in Exodus 21–22 means "the gods," **not** "the judges" — the conventional gloss being a later harmonization.

McClellan's point is that a NET Bible note paradoxically cites Gordon and Draffkorn to support the "judges" reading, when their papers argue the opposite. See [[Exodus 21-22 Ha-Elohim Means the Gods Not the Judges]].
