# Bernhard Duhm

**Who:** German biblical scholar (1847–1928). In 1892, published a commentary on Isaiah that crystallized the three-author theory of Isaiah's composition.

**Role in biblical studies:** Duhm's commentary formally argued for a First Isaiah (chs. 1–39), Second Isaiah / Deutero-Isaiah (chs. 40–55), and Third Isaiah / Trito-Isaiah (chs. 56–66). His work built on late-18th-century scholars ([[Johann Benjamin Koppe|Koppe]], [[Johann Christoph Döderlein|Döderlein]], [[Johann Gottfried Eichhorn|Eichhorn]]) who had begun identifying passages that appeared to come from a later period.

## Deutero-Isaiah
Duhm's 1892 commentary is the classical formulation of the [[Deutero-Isaiah Theory]]. Later scholars (e.g., [[Marvin Sweeney]]) have refined and complicated the model, arguing that literary layers from later periods also appear within chapters 1–39.
