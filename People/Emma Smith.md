# Emma Smith

Emma Hale Smith (1804–1879) was the first and only publicly recognized wife of [[Joseph Smith]]. In this source she is mentioned because Joseph first met her during the 1825 treasure-hunting expedition for [[Josiah Stowell]], when Joseph went to Harmony, Pennsylvania, to search for a legendary lost Spanish silver mine.[^1] The detail ties the treasure-hunting episode directly into the founding biography of [[Joseph Smith]].

- See [[Josiah Stowell Hires Joseph Smith 1825]]

## As the Context of the Word of Wisdom
In the [[Let's Get Real with Stephen Jones - Joseph Smith's Biggest Discovery (Don Bradley E0039)|Let's Get Real interview]], [[Don Bradley]] places Emma at the heart of the [[Word of Wisdom]]'s deep context: the 1833 revelation came less than four months after she nearly died bearing [[Joseph Smith III]], and while she struggled with her postpartum health and (Bradley infers) probable caffeine dependence. The revelation's promises of "health in the navel, marrow in the bones," and strength to "run and not be weary" answered her household's real need, making the Word of Wisdom a likely shared sacrifice of both spouses rather than an abstract code. See [[Emma's Difficult Childbirth Is the Immediate Context of the Word of Wisdom]].[^2]
