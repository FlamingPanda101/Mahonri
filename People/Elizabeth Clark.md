# Elizabeth Clark

American historian of early Christianity (1938–2021), longtime professor at Duke University and a major scholar of late-antique asceticism, the Origenist controversy, and women in the early church. Author of *The Origenist Controversy* (Princeton, 1992) among many works.

## Cited by Terryl Givens (Keystone)
[[Terrell Givens|Terryl Givens]] cites Clark — noting "I don't think she was even a believer" — as the author of "a magnificent book on [[Origen|Origen]]," and endorses her verdict that "Christianity chose a tragic path when it chose the path of [[Augustine]] over the path of Origen."[^1] Givens uses her as a non-LDS scholarly witness for his thesis that the defeat of Origen (premortality, universalism) was one of the two great wrong turns of Christian history. See [[The Two Great Wrong Turns - Overthrow of Origen and Pelagius]].
