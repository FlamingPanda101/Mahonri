# Moshe Weinfeld

**Who:** Israeli biblical scholar (1925–2009), professor at the Hebrew University of Jerusalem, known for major work on Deuteronomy and the Deuteronomistic school (*Deuteronomy and the Deuteronomic School*, 1972) and on ancient Near Eastern covenant and treaty backgrounds.

**Role in this discussion:** [[Mike Day]] and [[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)|Dave Butler]] mention Weinfeld twice as the subject of a forthcoming Stick of Joseph episode. Notably, Butler cites Weinfeld in the context of arguing that the Deuteronomist editors "seem to be ideologically aligned with murderers" — raising [[Josiah]]'s reform as a violent program — and contrasts that with his stated gratitude that the Book of Mormon's editors "reveal their hand" and lack those problems. Butler also notes Weinfeld's position on relative dating: that **P preceded D** — one option within the documentary-hypothesis debate over source order.[^1][^2]
