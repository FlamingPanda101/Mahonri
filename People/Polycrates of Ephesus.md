# Polycrates of Ephesus

Bishop of Ephesus in the **late 2nd century CE**, best known as a leading voice for the Quartodeciman dating of Easter in the controversy with Rome (his letter is preserved by [[Eusebius of Caesarea|Eusebius]], *Ecclesiastical History* 5.24). In [[Dan McClellan - Where Did the 12 Apostles Go|McClellan's survey of apostolic martyrdom traditions]], Polycrates supplies the **earliest notice of the apostle Philip's death** — and, tellingly, says only that Philip died and was buried in a certain place, with *no* mention of martyrdom. He thus functions as a data point that the dramatic crucifixion stories for Philip are later apocryphal embellishments, not early tradition. See [[Apostolic Martyrdom Traditions Are Late Apocryphal Legend]].
