# David

**Who:** Second king of Israel/Judah (traditional dates: c. 1010–970 BCE), portrayed in 1–2 Samuel, 1 Kings 1–2, and 1 Chronicles as the founder of the Davidic dynasty in Jerusalem. In the biblical narrative, David unites the tribes of Israel and Judah under a single monarchy.

**Role:** [[Jacob Wright]] argues that the biblical portrayal of David ruling a united Israel is historically "totally fictional" — a later Judean political fabrication under kings [[Hezekiah]] and [[Josiah]] designed to claim the north's legacy after its destruction by Assyria. In Wright's framework, David represents the "palace history" of Judah, which was retroactively "bracketed" behind the older "people's history" from the northern tradition (Patriarchs, [[Exodus]]).
