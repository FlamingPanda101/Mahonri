# Grant Hardy

**Who:** Grant Hardy, a Latter-day Saint scholar (PhD, Yale) and professor of history and religious studies at UNC Asheville, author of *Understanding the Book of Mormon: A Reader's Guide* (Oxford University Press, 2010).

**Role in this source:** [[Daniel C. Peterson]] cites Hardy's book as a more demanding but powerful resource. Hardy did not write it as apologetics and not for an LDS audience, yet Peterson found it "so powerfully apologetic even if he didn't intend it" — because Hardy reads the text closely and finds distinct authors with different themes, a literary feat Peterson considers "beyond Joseph's capacity" to fabricate during rapid dictation.[^1]

## On Book of Mormon Authorship
Hardy's identification of separate narrative voices is a centerpiece of the argument against solo authorship.
