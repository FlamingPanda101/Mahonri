# Mary Whitmer

**Who:** Mary Musselman Whitmer (1778–1856), mother of the Whitmer family in whose Fayette, New York home much of the Book of Mormon translation took place; an **informal witness** to the gold plates.

**Role in this source:** [[Daniel C. Peterson]] features Mary as a paradigm of an *unprimed* witness. Overworked and "a little tired" of feeding [[Joseph Smith]] and his scribe while the men discussed "national policy" and she did the housework, she encountered a messenger with a knapsack who said she was under stress and deserved a view of the plates, then took them out and let her turn the pages. Peterson argues she may even be the **first** witness — possibly seeing the plates before the official witnesses did.[^1]

## On Unprimed Witnesses
Her experience defeats the "they were primed to see what they expected" objection, since she was annoyed and expecting nothing.
- [[Informal Witnesses Were Not Primed]]
- [[Witnesses Were Deceived Theory Rebutted]]

[[Fawn Brodie]] could only handle Mary's account by saying "even Joseph must have been amazed at his power to induce visions in other people" — which Peterson notes explains nothing, since Joseph was not present.[^2]
