# Tom Holland

**Era:** Contemporary (b. 1968)
**Tradition:** Agnostic / culturally Christian
**Role:** British historian, author, broadcaster (*The Rest Is History*)

## Bio

Tom Holland is a British popular historian whose 2019 book *Dominion: The Making of the Western Mind* popularized the thesis that Western secular modernity is pervasively, often invisibly, shaped by Christianity. He is not present in this dialogue but is its constant reference point; [[Justin Brierley]] repeatedly invites him to a future episode.[^1]

## Topics

### The Dominion Thesis
Holland argues that even apparently anti-Christian movements (the Enlightenment, human rights, secular liberalism) carry Christian "DNA." [[Glenn Scrivener]] presents his own book as a confessional, more modest version, while noting Holland uses the stronger word "blossoming." Both agree Holland has become "more specifically Christianized" since 2019; Scrivener reports the famous opening "proclamation of the cross" was the *last* thing Holland wrote and was moved to the front. See [[The Dominion Thesis - Western Values Are Christianized]] and [[Unthinkable Without Christianity vs Blossoming Thesis]].[^1]

### Rights and Genesis
Holland is quoted as writing that "the ultimate seedbed of the American Revolution was... the book of Genesis." See [[Genesis 1 Image of God as Seedbed of Rights]].[^2]

### Distinguished from Scrivener's Position
Scrivener distinguishes himself from Holland: for Scrivener there is "a Christianity beyond which we have developed ideas" (e.g. romanticism, individualism, abortion as *post*-Christian deviation), whereas Holland's "blossoming" tends to fold everything back into Christianity. See [[Abortion and Euthanasia as Pagan Not Christian]].[^3]
