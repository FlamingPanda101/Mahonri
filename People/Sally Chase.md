# Sally Chase

**Era:** 1820s, Palmyra/Manchester, New York
**Tradition:** Neighbor of the Smith family (not LDS)

## Identity

Sally Chase was a Palmyra-area neighbor of the Smith family who owned a green seer stone and was herself reputed as a "glass looker." In the account [[Don Bradley]] relays (from Dr. [[William Purple]]'s notes on the 1826 hearing), the young Joseph Smith felt prompted to look into Sally Chase's green stone, in which he reportedly saw a white stone ~150 miles away near the western border of New York, under the roots of a tree — the stone he later recovered as his own [[seer stone]].

## Apologetic significance

The episode is part of Bradley's argument that Joseph's seer-stone discovery (and the "all-seeing eye"/theosis language attached to it) shows the endowment's deification structure was present from the very beginning of the restoration, not lifted from Freemasonry in 1842. See [[Joseph's Seer-Stone Discovery Frames Theosis From the Start of the Restoration]].
