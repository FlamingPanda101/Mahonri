# Oliver Cowdery

Oliver Cowdery (1806–1850) was the principal scribe for the translation of the Book of Mormon and one of its Three Witnesses. He arrived in Harmony, Pennsylvania, on April 5, 1829, and began acting as scribe on April 7.[^1]

## The Rod of Nature Revelations (D&C 6, 8, 9)

Several early revelations through [[Joseph Smith]] for Cowdery — [[D&C 6|D&C 6]], [[D&C 8|D&C 8]], and [[D&C 9|D&C 9]] — concern whether Cowdery will help translate.[^2] A revelation (the source thinks section 6) tells Cowdery he has "the gift of working with the sprout"; the wording was later changed in the 1833 Book of Commandments to "working with the rod" and again in the 1835 Doctrine and Covenants to "gift of Aaron."[^3] The text calls it a "rod of nature" yet insists "there is no other power save the power of God which can cause this rod of nature to work in your hands" — framing the divining rod as a legitimate **spiritual gift**, not folk superstition.[^4]

[[D&C 9|D&C 9]] records Cowdery's failed attempt to translate and the instruction that he must "study it out in his mind" before asking.[^5]

## On the 1826 Trial

Cowdery reported that Joseph was "honorably acquitted" at the 1826 proceeding — testimony the source notes is discounted by critics as partisan but counts as one of the five later sources.[^6] See [[Joseph Smith 1826 Trial Conviction Question]].
