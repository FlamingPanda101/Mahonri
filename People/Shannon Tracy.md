# Shannon Tracy

**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint (LDS)
**Role:** Avocational forensic-reconstruction researcher; author

## Bio
Shannon Tracy is a Latter-day Saint researcher who has spent decades studying the June 1844 killing of [[Joseph Smith]] and [[Hyrum Smith]] at Carthage Jail. He began collecting data in 1996 for a book on Joseph Smith, and in 1995–96 produced an early 3D project reconstructing the brothers' anatomy from their death masks and — by permission of the RLDS (Community of Christ) church — photographs of their skeletal remains.

## Relevance to the vault
On [[Ward Radio - A Forensic Deep Dive into the Martyrdom of Joseph Smith]] he proposes a crime-scene reconstruction of the martyrdom: laser-scanning the jail, building digital avatars, and running ballistic tests, while insisting the method should *eliminate* hypotheses rather than start from one. See [[Forensic Reconstruction Eliminates Theories Rather Than Inventing Them]], [[The Carthage Door Did Not Lock and Only Two Pistols Were in the Room]], [[Joseph Smith's Pepper-Box Pistol Misfired Half Its Rounds at Carthage]], and [[John Taylor's Pocket Watch Was Crushed by a Fall Not a Bullet]].[^1]
