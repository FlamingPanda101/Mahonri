# Joseph F. Smith

Sixth president of the LDS Church (1838–1918), nephew of [[Joseph Smith]]. As part
of the First Presidency, he was instrumental in the 1912 suppression of the
[[King Follett Discourse]] from the *History of the Church*.

## Opposition to the Discourse

Joseph F. Smith "had several problems with the version of the King Follett Discourse
[[BH Roberts]] had put together, not the least of which was the infant exaltation
part."[^1]

The critical biographical fact: Joseph F. Smith had lost 13 children of his own, and
"he could not fathom not being able to watch them grow in the eternities." The
doctrine of [[Infant Exaltation]] — that children who die remain children forever,
"not one cubit added to their stature" — was personally unbearable to him.[^1]

## The Suppression

Together with [[Charles Penrose]], he had pages 302–317 physically removed from the
*History of the Church* typescript while Roberts was away on a mission.[^2]

## Irony

His son, [[Joseph Fielding Smith]], would later become one of the greatest champions
of the King Follett Discourse — "he did not share his father's hesitations" —
though he edited out the infant exaltation portions.[^3]
