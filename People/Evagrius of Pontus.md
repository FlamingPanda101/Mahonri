# Evagrius of Pontus (c. 345-399)

Fourth-century Christian monk and ascetic theologian, one of the foundational figures of Christian monastic spirituality and the systematic analysis of the inner life.

## The eight vices and *apatheia*
Becerra cites Evagrius as a key theorist of **moral formation as deification**. He identified eight vices to be conquered — gluttony, lust, avarice, sadness, anger, despondency, vainglory, and pride — a catalog later shortened and revised into the "seven deadly sins." [^1] For each vice he prescribed spiritual remedies (prayer, fasting, scripture study, singing psalms, almsgiving, gift giving, service). Against Origen's *metriopatheia* (merely controlling sinful inclinations), Evagrius taught **apatheia**, the complete eradication of the passions — the "health of the soul" that enables one to love completely and commune with God undistracted.
