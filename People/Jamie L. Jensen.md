# Jamie L. Jensen

**Role:** Professor of Biology at Brigham Young University; co-editor of *The Restored Gospel of Jesus Christ and Evolution* (BYU, 2025).

## Bio
Jamie L. Jensen is a professor in the Department of Biology at BYU and head of the department's assessment committee. Her research focuses on educational implementations for teaching biology, with a large part of her work examining the reconciliation of religious faith with topics in science, especially evolution.[^1] She serves on the Broader Social Impacts Committee of the Human Origins Project at the Smithsonian's National Museum of Natural History, working with scientists of various denominations to help the public feel more comfortable with human evolution. With funding from the Howard Hughes Medical Institute's Department of Science Education, she has convened biologists, theologians, and local pastors and ministers to help students accept the science of evolution without losing religious faith, and has built a resource website for "faithful seekers of reconciliation" (biology.byu.edu/reconciling-evolution).[^1]

She authored the volume's chapter "Accepting Evolution: Why Does It Matter?", in which she develops the BYU "reconciliation approach" and argues that [[Science Is Agnostic Not Atheistic]], that the [[The False Dichotomy Between Evolution and Faith|science-vs-faith dichotomy is false]], and that believers should avoid a [[God of the Gaps - Demonstration vs Scientific Hypothesis|"God of the gaps"]].[^2] Her most fulfilling role, she writes, is being a wife and mother of four boys.[^1]
