# David John Butler

**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint (LDS)
**Role:** Independent author on temple theology and the Book of Mormon

## Bio
D. John Butler is an independent Latter-day Saint author whose books — notably *In the Language of Adam* — argue that the Book of Mormon is a **liturgical/temple text** keyed to the furniture, architecture, and ascent ideology of the Israelite temple, and that this reading reflects the religion of the patriarchs that the Deuteronomist reformers despised. (He is a different person from [[Dave Butler]] of *The Stick of Joseph*.)

## Relevance to the vault
[[Mike Day]] cites Butler approvingly in the Mike-and-Dave Deuteronomy episode, presenting *In the Language of Adam* as a complement to [[Moshe Weinfeld]]: where Weinfeld reconstructs the scribal/lawyerly religion of Deuteronomy, Butler reads the Book of Mormon as preserving the temple-liturgical world that reform displaced. See [[The Vassal-Treaty Covenant Pattern Links Deuteronomy King Benjamin and the Endowment]] and [[The Book of Mormon Matches Pre-Reform First Temple Theology]].[^1]
