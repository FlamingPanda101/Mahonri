# Jonathan Haidt

**Role:** Social psychologist; author; public intellectual
**Affiliation:** Professor, NYU Stern School of Business (formerly University of Virginia)
**Tradition:** Secular social psychology

## Bio

Jonathan Haidt is an American social psychologist best known for **moral foundations theory** and the **social intuitionist model** of moral judgment, and for the books *The Happiness Hypothesis*, *The Righteous Mind*, and *The Coddling of the American Mind*. He describes himself as a secular Jew.[^1]

## Positions in this dialogue

Haidt supplies the **rider-and-elephant** metaphor that the Thoughtful Faith host treats as the empirical backbone of the video:

- The mind is divided like a small **rider** (conscious reasoning) on a large **elephant** (the other ~99% of the mind — automatic intuition) that runs most behavior.
- Intuition makes a quick pattern-matched judgment "within a second," and reasoning is recruited *afterward* to justify it.
- Persuasion works by speaking to the elephant first; reasons aimed only at the rider fail if the elephant is "digging in its heels."

See [[The Rider and the Elephant - Intuition Drives Reason]]. The host then identifies the elephant with the scriptural "spirit" / Hebrew heart (a confessional move that is the host's, not Haidt's). See [[The Elephant Is the Spirit - The Hebrew Heart]].
