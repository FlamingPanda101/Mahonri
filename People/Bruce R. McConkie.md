# Bruce R. McConkie

LDS apostle (1915–1985), son-in-law of [[Joseph Fielding Smith]], and author of
the influential reference work *Mormon Doctrine*. Along with Joseph Fielding
Smith, he was one of the two figures "who more than anyone else emphasized the
importance of the King Follett Discourse and moving it more toward the center
of LDS teachings."[^1]

## Champion of the Discourse

McConkie "frequently cited the King Follett Discourse as the best sermon that a
Mormon leader had ever taught, even at one point arguing that it should be added
to the LDS canon."[^1]

## Critic of Roberts's Footnotes

Despite his enthusiasm for the discourse, McConkie was "upfront of his critiques
of BH Roberts's footnotes regarding the King Follett Discourse because they
disagreed both on the infant exaltation and the spirit birth."[^2]

This continued the pattern: even the discourse's greatest champions edited or
critiqued its most controversial elements — [[Infant Exaltation]] and [[Spirit Birth and Intelligences]].[^2]
