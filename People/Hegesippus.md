# Hegesippus

A **2nd-century Christian writer** (fl. c. 150-180 CE) often called the first church historian; his five books of *Hypomnemata* ("Memoirs") survive only in fragments quoted by [[Eusebius of Caesarea|Eusebius]]. He is a key source for early traditions about the relatives of Jesus and the succession of bishops in various sees.

He appears in the [[Capturing Christianity - Debate The Great Apostasy Joe Heschmeyer vs Jacob Hansen|Heschmeyer–Hansen debate]] as a star witness for [[Jacob Hansen]]'s apostasy case. Hansen quotes Hegesippus — writing, he says, "a mere 50 years after the death of the apostles" — to the effect that the church "continued until then as a pure and uncorrupt virgin," but that once "the sacred choir of apostles became extinct," false teachers arose and "attempted without shame to preach their false doctrine." For Hansen this is near-contemporary testimony that doctrinal corruption set in precisely when the apostolic generation passed. [[Joe Heschmeyer]] responds in the broader debate that heresies arising are not the same as a *universal* apostasy, and that the same period shows the church growing explosively rather than collapsing.
