# Kevin McCain

**Role:** Philosopher; epistemologist
**Affiliation:** Professor of Philosophy, University of Alabama at Birmingham (UAB)
**Tradition:** Analytic philosophy (epistemology)

## Bio

Kevin McCain is an American philosopher who teaches philosophy at the University of Alabama at Birmingham, where he works primarily in **epistemology**, the philosophy of science, and evidentialist theories of justification.[^1] In this explainer he presents the **epistemic regress problem** as a neutral, non-confessional piece of general philosophy, surveying the four standard responses (foundationalism, infinitism, coherentism, and skepticism) without advocating for a particular religious tradition.

## Relevance to Epistemology

McCain frames the regress problem not primarily as a road to skepticism but as a diagnostic tool that "helps us get clear about the structure of epistemic justification" — how reasons must relate to one another and to our beliefs for those beliefs to be justified.[^2] He reports the contemporary state of play in the field: foundationalism as the dominant view, coherentism as the runner-up, and infinitism as a recent and still-unpopular minority position.
