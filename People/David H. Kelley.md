# David H. Kelley (1924–2011)

**Era:** 20th–21st century
**Tradition:** Non-LDS (secular scholar)
**Role:** Epigrapher; Mayanist; Mesoamerican linguist

## Bio
David H. Kelley was an internationally recognized scholar (Harvard PhD) who published in Uto-Aztecan linguistics and made early, influential contributions to the **decipherment of the Maya script**.[^1]

## On Stubbs's Work
In the [[Informed Saints - Book of Mormon Evidence Hidden in Ancient American Language|Informed Saints interview]], [[Brian Stubbs]] reports that Kelley — having received an early draft of the book from [[John Sorenson]] and initially reluctant to "tackle it" — read the whole thing and called it "the most interesting and significant piece of research I have seen in years."[^1] The endorsement is offered as outside, non-LDS corroboration; as a private appraisal of a draft it is anecdotal rather than a published peer review. See [[Uto-Aztecan Contains a Large Semitic and Egyptian Lexical Layer]].
