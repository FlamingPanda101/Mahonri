# Levi Hancock

**Role:** Early Latter-day Saint; uncle-by-marriage of Fanny Alger
**Era:** 19th century (1803–1882)
**Tradition:** LDS

## Bio

Levi Ward Hancock was an early Latter-day Saint and one of the first Presidents of the Seventy. He married Clarissa Alger, sister of [[Fanny Alger]], making him Fanny's uncle by marriage and father of the memoirist [[Mosiah Hancock]].

## Relevance to the vault

Levi's **own autobiography** is a load-bearing source for [[Don Bradley]]'s reconstruction in [[Ward Radio - Debunking Joseph Smiths Fake Affair with Fanny Alger feat Don Bradley]]. Critically, Levi mentions Fanny Alger but *does not* claim to have performed her marriage to Joseph — that claim was added to the autobiography by his son Mosiah only after Levi's death. Levi does record that in the summer of 1836 "Joseph told me to take Fanny Alger and go to Missouri," anchored to datable events (his brother Solomon's marriage; the day Joseph received the letter about Saints being attacked in Clay County). Bradley uses these markers to fix the discovery of the relationship to a few days in **July 1836**, refuting the 1833 date. See [[The Fanny Alger Relationship Dates to July 1836 Not 1833]].
