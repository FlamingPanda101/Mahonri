# Jasmine Rapley

Latter-day Saint apologist and content creator (the self-described "pseudonymous" / "anonymous" handle **Jasmine Rapley** across Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, X, and YouTube) and co-host, with her husband Neil and Steven Smith, of the **Informed Saints** podcast. Half-Argentine; appears as a panelist on Ward Radio. Known for polished, source-driven short-form rebuttals.

## Book of Mormon and Race
Argues the Book of Mormon's "skin of blackness" is not a race-based curse — there were no Africans in the Book of Mormon world, the best textual readings are non-pigmentary, and the narrative *condemns* Nephite prejudice. See [[Book of Mormon Skin of Blackness Is Not About Race]] and [[2 Nephi 5.21|2 Nephi 5:21]] [^1].

## First Vision and the Gospels
Compares First Vision account differences to the (non-harmonious) four Gospels — different audiences and purposes explain the variation. See [[First Vision Accounts Are No More Contradictory Than the Gospels]] [^2].

## Retention Data
Marshals Pew Religious Landscape Survey figures (and [[Ryan Burge]]'s analysis) to show LDS retention (~54%) is near Catholicism's and #1 once controlled for activity. See [[LDS Retention Is Among the Highest of Any Tradition]] [^3].
