# Lee Levine

**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** Jewish
**Role:** Historian of the ancient synagogue, Hebrew University of Jerusalem

## Bio
Lee Levine is a professor at the Hebrew University of Jerusalem who "wrote what is considered the definitive study on the subject" of the ancient synagogue, published through Yale.[^1] [[Alex Arnold]] cites him as independent corroboration of [[Yonatan Adler]]: working separately, Levine "reached the same conclusion completely independently" — that there is no confirmed physical evidence for synagogue buildings in Israel before the 1st century BC.[^1]

## On the Origins of the Synagogue
Levine's significance in the argument is the *independence* of his conclusion. Two specialists, from different starting points and both Yale-published, agree that the synagogue does not appear in the archaeological record when the traditional "Babylonian-exile origin" story says it should. That convergence is what makes the synagogue the cleanest test case for the wider thesis. See [[The Synagogue Is a Post-Exilic Institution Absent From the Old Testament]].
