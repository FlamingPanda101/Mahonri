# Robert Eisenman

Scholar of Christian origins and the Dead Sea Scrolls, author of the very large book *James the Brother of Jesus*. Day cites Eisenman (alongside [[Raymond Brown]]) for the argument that **[[James the brother of Jesus|James]] was the central, Torah-observant leader of the Jerusalem church**, and that there was real tension between James's law-keeping community and Paul's law-free mission. Day flags that they are only "skimming the surface" of Eisenman's much larger (and contested) reconstruction. See [[Paul vs James and the Judaizer Conflict]], [[Acts 15 Shows James Deciding Not Peter]].
