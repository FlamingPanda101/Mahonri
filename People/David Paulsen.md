# David Paulsen

BYU philosophy professor and LDS scholar who has written influential works on
Mormon theology. Known for his forceful defense of the [[King Follett Discourse]]
against colleagues who sought to downplay it.

## "The Church of King Follett"

At an interfaith gathering between BYU professors and evangelical professors in
Nauvoo, Paulsen was "alarmed" at colleagues like [[Steven Robinson]] and [[Robert Millet]] who were downplaying the discourse as "fringe doctrines" or questioning
whether it was "fully canonized."[^1]

Paulsen's response became legendary in LDS academic circles:

> "I belong to the Church of King Follett. We are not moving away from it. I will
> always be a professor at the University of King Follett."[^2]

This captured the divide between LDS scholars who embrace Mormon theological
exceptionalism and those who want to emphasize common ground with mainstream
Christianity (see [[Modern LDS Tensions on King Follett Discourse]]).[^2]

## "Joseph Smith and the Problem of Evil" (1999 BYU Forum)

Paulsen's best-known philosophical contribution is his September 21, 1999 BYU forum address [[Paulsen - Joseph Smith and the Problem of Evil]], which argues that Joseph Smith's revelations **dissolve** the classical problems of evil. Drawing on [[Truman Madsen]]'s *Eternal Man* and collaborative work with [[Blake Ostler]], he distinguishes three problems:[^3]

- **Logical** — by denying [[Creatio Ex Nihilo Is the Lynchpin Difference|creation ex nihilo]], Joseph Smith escapes [[Flew's Divine Complicity Argument]]: God is not "the total or even the ultimate explanation of all else." Paulsen contrasts [[John Hick]]'s soul-making theodicy (which keeps ex nihilo and is forced into an incoherent universalism) with the Restoration's. See [[Soul-Making Theodicy - Hick and Joseph Smith]], [[Hick's Universalism Collapses Into Determinism]], [[B.H. Roberts's Definition of Divine Omnipotence]], [[Lehi's Theodicy - Opposition in All Things]].
- **Soteriological** — the [[The Soteriological Problem of Evil - The Inconsistent Triad|inconsistent triad]] of exclusive salvation vs. the unevangelized is resolved by the restored doctrine of [[Postmortem Salvation Resolves the Soteriological Problem|postmortem salvation]].
- **Practical** — answered by Joseph Smith's own endurance at [[The Practical Problem of Evil - Joseph Smith at Liberty Jail|Liberty Jail]].

This devotional is the scholarly source behind the popular LDS apologetic that Mormon metaphysics defeats the problem of evil (cf. [[Co-Eternality Solves the Problem of Evil]]).[^4]
