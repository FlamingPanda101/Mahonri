# Paul Adams

Bisbee, Arizona man whose abuse of his own daughters became the centerpiece of the LDS "Spotlight" coverage examined in Ward Radio Part 6. An inactive lay member, Adams confessed a single past incident of molesting his daughter to his LDS bishop roughly a year after it occurred. The bishop brought in Adams's wife, contacted the abuse helpline, and advised the couple to call police — they refused, and over two years both parents assured the bishop the abuse had ceased. Adams was excommunicated; his wife and children kept attending. In 2017 Adams was caught distributing child pornography, arrested, convicted, and died by suicide in federal prison; his wife pled no contest to two counts of child abuse and was imprisoned.

## The case anti-Mormons equate with Spotlight
[[Michael Rezendes]] reported the case under the headline "Seven years of sex abuse: How Mormon officials let it happen," recasting one confessed incident and the parents' lies as an institutional cover-up. The Arizona Superior Court dismissed the suit against the church; a 2025 appeal proceeded to the Arizona Supreme Court. The episode argues the case is nothing like the Catholic *Spotlight* scandal — no clergy abused anyone, and the bishop urged reporting.
