# Jerome

**Jerome** (c. 342–420 AD) was a Doctor of the Church, biblical scholar, and translator of the Latin **Vulgate**. He is famous as the patristic figure most skeptical of the Deuterocanonical (apocryphal) Old Testament books.

## The Lone Exception on the Deuterocanon
[[Trent Horn]] notes that Jerome is "the one exceptional case" among the fathers who doubted the Deuterocanon — but stresses that **even Jerome did not appeal to self-authentication or feelings**. Jerome reasoned from the *Jewish authorities* in the region where he lived (the Hebrew canon), not from an interior witness of the Spirit. So Jerome cannot be enlisted as a Protestant precedent for [[John Calvin]]'s self-attestation model: he reached a narrower canon by external, traditional reasoning, not by "feeling it in his bones." See [[The Early Church Did Not Use Self-Authentication]] [^1].

> "Even Jerome doesn't claim that his view is self-evident from his feelings... He's going off of Jewish authorities in the area where he's at" [^2].
