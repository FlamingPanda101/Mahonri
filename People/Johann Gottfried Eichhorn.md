# Johann Gottfried Eichhorn

**Who:** German Protestant theologian and orientalist (1752–1827). A foundational figure in modern biblical criticism, sometimes called the "founder of modern Old Testament criticism."

**Role in biblical studies:** Along with [[Johann Benjamin Koppe]] and [[Johann Christoph Döderlein]], Eichhorn was among the late-18th-century scholars who began identifying passages in Isaiah that appeared to come from a later period, laying groundwork for the [[Deutero-Isaiah Theory]]. His *Introduction to the Old Testament* (1780–1783) was influential in applying historical-critical methods to the Hebrew Bible.
