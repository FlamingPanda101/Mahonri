# Ryland Hutchinson

**Role:** Co-host on [[Ward Radio]] (independent Latter-day Saint commentary podcast)
**Era:** Contemporary (active 2020s)
**Tradition:** The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints

## Bio

Ryland Hutchinson is a co-host on the [[Ward Radio]] program. In the "Hollow Faith of Pastor Grift" episode he plays the **data-skeptic / methodological** role on the panel: although he says "I'm not a scholar, I'm trying to understand the data," he repeatedly raises survey-methodology objections and is teased by [[Jonah Barnes]] as the resident "PhD" and "doubter." (He explicitly disclaims scholar status, so this note carries no scholarly authority rating.)

## On Survey Methodology

Ryland is the chief voice arguing that the clergy-belief numbers are unreliable: the binary "definitely believe" format produces mood-dependent, semantically slippery answers, and the gaps may overstate genuine disbelief. He insists any "grace" for wording should apply equally across traditions. See:
- [[Survey Data Is Unreliable Objection]]
- [[Definitely Believe Wording Skews Survey Results]]

## On Sola Scriptura and Belief Nuance

He argues that thoughtful sola-scriptura advocates hold inerrancy without wooden word-for-word literalism, and cautions the panel against reading too much into others' beliefs ("we should just ask them"). He also frames mainline soft answers as the product of Protestantism's tolerance for doctrinal latitude rather than apostasy. See:
- [[Sola Scriptura Is Not Word-for-Word Literalism]]
- [[Mainline Clergy Doubt Core Christian Doctrines]]
