# Stephen A. Douglas

Prominent Illinois jurist and U.S. senator, famous for the Lincoln-Douglas debates and as the Democratic candidate for president in 1860, losing to [[Abraham Lincoln]] (12 electoral votes to 108). He died about a year after the election.

## The Joseph Smith Presidency Prophecy
Douglas is the subject of the [[Stephen A. Douglas Presidency Prophecy]]. In an after-dinner conversation on 18 May 1843, recorded by [[William Clayton]], [[Joseph Smith]] reportedly told him he would "aspire to the presidency of the United States" but would "feel the weight of the hand of the Almighty" if he turned against the Saints. During the 1860 campaign Douglas denounced the Mormons; his subsequent crushing defeat and early death are read by believers as fulfillment. The host of [[Mormonism with the Murf - Joseph Smith's Top 20 Fulfilled Prophecies]] notes the fuller prophecy text is absent from Clayton's 1843 journal but was published (1856/1859) before the 1860 election.[^1]
