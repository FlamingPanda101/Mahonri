# Stephen Cranney

**Era:** Contemporary (active 2020s)
**Tradition:** The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (LDS / Mormon)
**Role:** Demographer / statistician, social scientist, author

## Bio

Stephen Cranney is a Latter-day Saint statistician and demographer who studies religion, fertility, well-being, and sexuality with quantitative methods. He has an unusually young appearance for a statistician (a running joke when he guests), is a father of eight, and has adjuncted at BYU. He is cited repeatedly in the *Light and Truth Letter* on the connections between happiness and religiosity, LGBTQ+ issues in the church, and the finding that religious people report better sex. On [[Ward Radio]] he argues that polygamy theoretically and empirically raised fertility because it raised the marriage rate, even though individual plural wives bore fewer children.
