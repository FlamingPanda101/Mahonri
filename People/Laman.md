# Laman

**Role:** Eldest son of Lehi, progenitor of the Lamanites
**Era:** Book of Mormon (c. 600 BCE in the narrative)
**Tradition:** Book of Mormon

## Bio

Laman is the eldest son of the prophet Lehi in the Book of Mormon. With his brother Lemuel he repeatedly rebels against his father and his younger brother Nephi, murmuring against their journey and even seeking Nephi's life. His descendants, the Lamanites, become the recurring adversaries of the Nephites throughout the record.

## Relevance to the vault

Laman appears in two vault arguments. In [[Christina Dymock]]'s Day-of-Atonement typology he is read as the Azazel/scapegoat — falsely branded a thief by [[Laban]] and "cast out" into the wilderness, sin imputed to an innocent party. Separately, a Deuteronomistic-reform reading explains the family conflict by proposing that Laman and Lemuel, raised under Josiah's reform, opposed Lehi's "pre-reform" theology as dangerously illegal — an internal-coherence point for dating the Book of Mormon's theological backdrop.
