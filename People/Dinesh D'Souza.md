# Dinesh D'Souza
**Role:** Christian apologist, author, and political commentator
**Era:** Contemporary (b. 1961)
**Tradition:** Christian (Catholic background; broadly conservative Christian apologetics)

## Bio
Dinesh D'Souza is an Indian-American author, filmmaker, and conservative political commentator who has become a prominent popular-level defender of Christianity in debates with atheists. Born in Mumbai, he emigrated to the United States and rose to public prominence through political writing before turning toward Christian apologetics (notably his book *What's So Great About Christianity*). In the Pangburn debate with [[Alex OConnor|Alex O'Connor]] he argues for a model of scripture as *accommodated revelation* — "a perfect revelation shown to a scribe who writes it in his own words" — and defends apocalyptic imagery as metaphor that is "the actual conduit to truth" when "words and images fail," using his mushroom-taste analogy for the limits of language.[^1] Confronted with the contradiction between the gospel nativity accounts, he characterizes the discrepancy as "trivial" and reframes Jewish–Christian disputation as a clash between literalist and metaphorical reading, citing Jesus's "temple of my body" saying.[^2] His apologetic leans on [[Divine Accommodation Means Revelation Meets Humans in Their Context|accommodation]] and [[Metaphorical Truth and Narrative Reading|metaphorical truth]] rather than on defending the texts as inerrant history.
