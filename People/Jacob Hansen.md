# Jacob Hansen

**Role:** Latter-day Saint apologist and content creator (Thoughtful Faith)
**Era:** Contemporary (active 2020s)
**Tradition:** The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (LDS / Mormon)

## Bio

Jacob Hansen is a well-known Latter-day Saint apologist and YouTuber (Thoughtful Faith) who engages in cross-confessional dialogues defending and explaining LDS theology. He stresses that Latter-day Saints "consider ourselves Christian" (depending on definition) and centers Christ as "the only means by which salvation can be attained," while distinguishing the LDS view from "credal" Christianity. [^1]

## Soteriology: Progression, Not a Binary

In dialogue with [[Gavin Ortlund]], Hansen argued LDS soteriology is a *spectrum of progression* toward one's divine potential rather than a saved/damned binary, using an acorn-to-oak-tree analogy. Rejecting truth "stunts growth" rather than damning; "hell" (outer darkness) is reserved for those who hate the light. See:
- [[LDS Progression Model vs Binary Heaven and Hell]]
- [[Acorn and Oak Analogy for Theosis]]
- [[Fruit Versus Theology - Are They Separable]]

## Christology and Hermeneutics

Hansen affirms Jesus is "fully divine in the predicative sense" but denies consubstantiality, arguing the Trinity is a later *interpretation* of underdetermined scriptural data — not explicitly taught by Jesus. He cites [[John 17.3|John 17:3]] ("the only true God" = the Father) and the fruit-over-theology emphasis of [[Matthew 3.9|Matthew 3:9]] / [[Mark 9.38-40|Mark 9:38-40]]. See:
- [[Trinity Is an Interpretation Not Explicit in Scripture]]
- [[John 17.3 - The Only True God Is the Father]]
- [[Identity of Jesus Is Not a Theology Quiz]]

## Metaphysics: Rejecting Creatio Ex Nihilo

Hansen's deepest distinctive is the rejection of creation from nothing: all beings exist eternally as "intelligences," a fundamental "spiritual realm" is uncreated, and God *organizes* pre-existing chaos. Humans are therefore the same *kind* as God; the Father has always been fully divine (Hansen rejects the eternal-regression-of-gods view). See:
- [[Creatio Ex Nihilo Is the Lynchpin Difference]]
- [[Humans Are the Same Kind as God (LDS Ontology)]]
- [[LDS Father Was Always Fully Divine (No Eternal Regression)]]
- [[Are Latter-day Saints Christians]]

## Apologetics Method: Socratic Questions for a Watching Audience

On a Ward Radio panel with [[Hayden Carroll]] working through atheist objections, Hansen framed the whole exercise as a method drill: turn every objection into a *question* (the questioner "plays offense" and exposes hidden premises), and remember that public apologetics is for the watching audience — done one-on-one it "just burns the relationship," because "ministry is not apologetics." He also restated the layered approach by which the Resurrection only becomes a live question *after* one grants theism. See [[Apologetics Is Socratic and Aimed at the Audience Not the Interlocutor]] and [[The Four-Level Conversation Structure of Apologetics]].[^2]

## The Pragmatic Argument for the Restored Gospel and the True-Church Trilemma

In a solo Thoughtful Faith monologue, Hansen extends his pragmatic/Pascalian argument from God's existence to the *church* question, arguing the seeker faces only three options (LDS church / another church / no true church) and that critics who say "no true church" yet damn Mormons for bad theology contradict themselves. See:

## Curating Book of Abraham, Enoch, and Old Testament Research

In long-form Thoughtful Faith videos Hansen (often with [[Hayden Carroll]] or [[Jonah Barnes]]) frames and transmits others' research: the Kirtland Egyptian Papers as a cipher, Joseph's deferral on Facsimile 2, Cirillo's Enoch parallels, and the "Great Forsaken." See:

## The Great Apostasy, the Brothers Slur, and Substitutional Patriarchy (Ward Radio)
On Ward Radio, Hansen argued the Great Apostasy "definitely happened": the creedal God matches Aristotle's unmoved mover, perfect-being theology reads the Bible through a Greek lens, loss of apostolic authority bred the christological factions, and Constantine's creeds won by state power. He also intervened on the Cliffe Knechtle reaction (traditional Christianity does *not* teach universal divine sonship — adoption only / image-bearers) and endorsed [[Trent Horn]]'s "substitutional patriarchy."
