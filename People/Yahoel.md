# Yahoel

**Role:** Angelic / divine mediator figure in Second Temple Jewish literature
**Era:** Greco-Roman period Judaism
**Tradition:** Second Temple Jewish apocalyptic (notably the Apocalypse of Abraham)

## Bio

Yahoel is a principal angelic mediator in Greco-Roman period Jewish literature whose very name embeds the divine name (YHW + El). He is the figure in whom "the name of God dwells," serving as a guide and revealer — a paradigm of a mediator who bears and manifests the divine name and presence while remaining distinct from God.

## Divine-Name Mediation

[[Dan McClellan]] cites Yahoel alongside [[Metatron]] as evidence that, in Greco-Roman period Judaism, the model of a mediator possessing and asserting the **divine name and divine identity** was already in circulation. McClellan ties this stream of name-bearing mediation back to [[Scriptures/Exodus 23.20-21|Exodus 23]] ("for my name is in him") and argues it — rather than later Trinitarian or Binitarian frameworks — is the conceptual "taproot" that made early Christians ready to accept that Jesus could manifest God's presence.
