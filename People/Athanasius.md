# Athanasius of Alexandria (c. 296–373)

**Role:** Bishop of Alexandria; champion of Nicene orthodoxy
**Era:** 4th century
**Tradition:** Early Church / pro-Nicene

## Bio
Athanasius was the foremost defender of the full divinity of the Son against [[Arius]] and the Arian party, central to the development of Trinitarian doctrine at and after the Council of Nicaea.

## Reading John to Build Trinitarian Doctrine
[[Hugo Mendez]] names Athanasius (with the Cappadocian Fathers) as an "intense reader of John" — the Gospel that "blows the whole cosmic cover off Jesus" and gave the councils the tools to define the Trinity. Méndez stresses that John's own theology is *not yet* Nicene ("the cake," vs. the "ingredients"). See [[John Gave Christianity Its High Christology and Pre-existence]] [^1].
