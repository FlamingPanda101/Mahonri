# Anthony Rogers

Reformed apologist and **Presbyterian Church in America (PCA)** pastor, known for formal debates against Catholic, Muslim, and Unitarian interlocutors. Defends the classic Reformation doctrine of Scripture as summarized in the Westminster Confession of Faith. Converted while in a jail cell, an experience he cites as personal evidence of Scripture's self-authenticating power.[^1]

## Sola Scriptura
Argues the affirmative that Scripture is the sole infallible rule of faith — self-authenticating, inspired/inerrant, materially and formally sufficient, perspicuous, and the ultimate norm of faith and practice — grounding each claim in biblical texts and the self-contained nature of the triune God. See [[Scripture Is Self-Authenticating Because God Is Self-Contained]], [[Gods Modus Operandi - Oral Word Then Committed to Writing]], and [[The Light Manifests Itself - Perspicuity of Scripture]].[^1]

## The canon
Holds that the canon can in principle be demonstrated *internally* from Scripture's own structural features (Hebrew connective prefixes, the "seams of Scripture," and forward-pointing texts like Deuteronomy 18) rather than requiring an external infallible magisterium. See [[The Internal-Seams Argument for the Canon]].[^2]
