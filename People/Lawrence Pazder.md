# Lawrence Pazder

Canadian psychiatrist who co-authored the 1980 book *Michelle Remembers* with his patient (and later wife) [[Michelle Smith]], igniting the satanic panic.

## Recovered memory therapy and *Michelle Remembers*
Pazder used "an experimental new therapy" — recovered memory therapy — to surface Smith's lurid, never-corroborated satanic-ritual-abuse "memories." He first accused the Church of Satan but withdrew the claim under threat of a defamation suit. See [[Michelle Remembers Sparked the Satanic Panic]] and [[Recovered Memory Therapy Manufactures Abuse Memories]] [^1][^2].

## Spreading the panic
Pazder became a sought-after expert, conducting ritual-abuse seminars to train police departments; *Michelle Remembers* was used as training material for social workers. He and Smith divorced their spouses, married each other, and earned millions in royalties "without ever proving one case of abuse." See [[The Satanic Panic Was Driven by Financial and Reputational Incentives]] [^3][^4].

## The grandfather of the hysteria recants (1994)
Ward Radio Part 4 notes that even Pazder — "the man who coined the term satanic ritual abuse" — was "forced to admit in 1994 that perhaps satanic abuse memories are more an expression of the deep level of violation caused by abusive family members than actual accounts of satanic ritual abuse." His retreat is part of the cluster of collapses in [[Leading SRA Therapists Were Discredited Sued or Delicensed]]. [^5]
