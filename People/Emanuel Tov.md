# Emanuel Tov

**Era:** Contemporary (b. 1941)
**Tradition:** Orthodox Jew
**Role:** Biblical textual critic; Dead Sea Scrolls editor

## Bio
Emanuel Tov is an emeritus professor of Bible at the Hebrew University of Jerusalem and edited the Dead Sea Scrolls Publication Project. As an Orthodox Jew with no stake in LDS claims, he is cited by [[Alex Arnold]] as an example of a hostile/neutral authority whose field would have to be wrong for the Book of Mormon evidence to fail.[^1]

## On the Dead Sea Scrolls and Textual Transmission
Arnold invokes Tov's authority over the scrolls in support of the argument that the oldest Isaiah manuscripts (Qumran, discovered 1947) preserve variant readings predating the King James base text, some of which align with Book of Mormon variants. See [[Book of Mormon Isaiah Matches the Dead Sea Scrolls]].[^1]
