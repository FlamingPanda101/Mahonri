# Joshua HS

**Role:** Reformed pastor; host of the *Reformation Red Pill* podcast
**Era:** Contemporary (active 2020s)
**Tradition:** Reformed / right-wing Christian apologetics

## Bio

Joshua HS is a Reformed pastor and the host of a podcast called *Reformation Red Pill*. He came to wider attention through viral social-media clips, including one in which a guest stated "the institution of slavery is not inherently evil" and that "it is not inherently evil to own another human being." [[Dan McClellan]] responded to a follow-up podcast episode in which Joshua attempted to defend biblical slavery as a benign, even compassionate, institution. McClellan also references Joshua in his book *The Bible Says* (chapter 15) over earlier viral posts about women's modesty.

## Biblical Slavery

Joshua argued that Old Testament slavery was misunderstood, that it was essentially paid "bondservant" debt-servitude comparable to credit-card debt, and that it explicitly repudiated the three features people associate with the antebellum American South (kidnapping, lack of rights, abuse). [[Dan McClellan]] rebuts each claim as factually wrong and, in places, based on misremembered, misattributed, or wholly invented ("hallucinated") texts. See:

## Women's Modesty

Joshua previously argued that women must dress modestly to avoid causing men to "stumble," coining "modest is hottest." [[Dan McClellan]] addressed this in *The Bible Says* ch. 15, arguing that biblical modesty refers only to avoiding displays of wealth and status, and that no biblical text tells women to cover up to prevent male lust. See [[Biblical Modesty Refers to Wealth Not Sexuality]].
