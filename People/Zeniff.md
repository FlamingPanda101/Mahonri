# Zeniff

**Role:** Founding king of the Nephite colony in the land of Nephi
**Era:** Nephite history (~200 BC, narrative setting)
**Tradition:** Book of Mormon figure (LDS scripture)

## Bio
Zeniff led a group of Nephites back from Zarahemla to reclaim the land of Nephi (Lehi-Nephi), where he was made king (Mosiah 9-10). His son was [[King Noah]], whose wickedness set the stage for the prophet [[Abinadi]]'s ministry and martyrdom. Noah's son [[Limhi]] eventually led the people back to Zarahemla. The name is pronounced "Zeniff" — a point the Ward Radio panel makes much of (host [[Cardon Ellis]] notes the official pronunciation guide gives "Zeniff," correcting the common reading "Zenith").

## In the Book of Mormon record
The book of Omni notes that [[Amaleki]]'s brother "went with [the people of Zeniff]" back to the land of Nephi and was never heard from again — a thread the *Key to the Keystone* reconstruction uses to argue that [[Abinadi]] (son of "Abinadom"/[[Abadom]]) descended from the record-keeping line and so could plausibly have known the brass-plates Isaiah by heart. See [[The Sting of Death Is Apocryphal Isaiah from the Brass Plates Not a Pauline Anachronism]].
