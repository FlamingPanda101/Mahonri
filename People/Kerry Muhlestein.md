# Kerry Muhlestein

**Era:** Contemporary (2000s–2020s)
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint (LDS)
**Role:** Egyptologist, professor of ancient scripture (BYU)

## Bio
Kerry Muhlestein is a Latter-day Saint Egyptologist (PhD, UCLA) and professor of ancient scripture at Brigham Young University who has excavated pyramids and archaeological sites in Egypt and published peer-reviewed Egyptological scholarship. He hosts the **Scriptures Are Real** podcast and is among the most prominent academic defenders of the [[Book of Abraham]].[^1] He stresses that his aim is "to figure out what's going on" and to follow the evidence, even when it leaves questions open.[^2]

## Book of Abraham — the Two Core Myths
Argues the leading attack — Joseph mistranslated the surviving papyrus — fails because the eyewitness historical sources say Joseph translated from a different, now-lost scroll, not the recovered fragments. See [[Joseph Was Not Translating the Surviving Papyrus]]. He separates **translating a text** from **interpreting a drawing**, noting Egyptians intended multiple meanings, so some of Joseph's facsimile readings match Egyptologically. See [[Translating a Text Versus Interpreting a Drawing Are Different Claims]] and [[Facsimile 3 Identifications Vindicate Joseph Smith by Coincidence]].[^1]

## Method and Epistemology
Catalogs the method-level mistakes people make about the Book of Abraham — unexamined assumptions, over-simplifying a multivalent 3,000-year record, trusting sound bites, half-hearted one-sided research, and the sophistry that revelation is not real knowledge. See [[Questioning Assumptions Is the First Defense of the Book of Abraham]], [[Everyone Has a Faith-Choice Bias and Admitting It Is More Objective]], and [[Revelation Is a Valid Source of Knowledge]].[^3]
