# Joshua M. Sears

**Role:** Associate professor of ancient scripture at Brigham Young University; author of "From Biology Major to Religion Professor" in *The Restored Gospel of Jesus Christ and Evolution* (BYU, 2025).

## Bio
Joshua M. Sears is an associate professor of ancient scripture at BYU. He received a BA in ancient Near Eastern studies from BYU (where he taught at the Missionary Training Center and volunteered as an EMT), an MA from The Ohio State University, and a PhD in Hebrew Bible from the University of Texas at Austin. His research interests include Israelite prophecy, marriage and families in the ancient world, and the publication history of Latter-day Saint scripture.[^1]

In his chapter — a personal reflection originally published in *BYU Studies* 63, no. 1 (2024) — Sears narrates his shift from a biology-major freshman who rejected evolution to a religion professor comfortable with it, crediting three factors: a clearer understanding that [[Doctrine Is Defined by the Unified Voice of the Highest Authorities|official doctrine is defined by the united voice of the apostles]], his study showing [[Genesis Is Theology Not Science - Sears's Reconciliation Journey|Genesis is theology rather than science]], and a growing comfort with ambiguity.[^2] He lives in Lindon, Utah, with his wife Alice (from Hong Kong) and their five children.[^1]
