# Gavin (Catholic Debater)

**Role:** Lay Roman Catholic apologist / debater
**Era:** Contemporary (2020s)
**Tradition:** Roman Catholic

> **Note:** This is *not* [[Gavin Ortlund]] (the Reformed Baptist scholar). This Gavin self-identifies in cross-examination as a Roman Catholic and argues a Catholic doctrine of God (catechism, purgatory, the Apostles' Creed's descent into hell, soul created at conception).

## Bio
Gavin argued the **affirmative** in the moderated debate [[Lets Talk w Hayden Carroll - Triune God Is Omnibenevolent Debate|"The Triune God Is Omnibenevolent"]] against LDS apologist [[Hayden Carroll]]. His full name and credentials are not given in the source; he presents as a lay apologist defending classical/creedal Catholic theism.[^1]

## Topics

### Omnibenevolence from Triune Love
Gavin's central thesis: omnibenevolence "follows necessarily from God's nature as triune love." Because [[1 John 4.8|God *is* love]] (ontologically, not metaphorically), and love that is eternal requires an eternal beloved, only a triune God can *be* love — and his willing of every creature's good is therefore prior to and independent of human response.[^2] See [[Omnibenevolence Flows from the Triune God as Eternal Love]].

### Universal Salvific Will
He marshals [[1 Timothy 2.4|1 Tim 2:4]], [[2 Peter 3.9|2 Pet 3:9]], [[Matthew 5.45|Matt 5:45]], and [[John 12.32|John 12:32]] for God's universal salvific will, carefully distinguishing omnibenevolence from universalism and from coercion ("forced love is not love").[^3] See [[Scripture Explicitly Affirms Universal Salvific Will]] and [[Omnibenevolence Does Not Equal Universalism or Coercion]].

### Against Conditional Access
A recurring polemic: a truly benevolent God cannot restrict his presence to "the worthy, the initiated, the washed, and the anointed." He names [[D&C 76]] and turns [[D&C 82.10]] against the LDS view, arguing access to God is "given downward, not earned upward."[^4] See [[Omnibenevolence vs Conditional Access Models]] and [[Conditional Access Tu Quoque Against the LDS God]].

### Response to the Creation Dilemma
Under cross-examination on Hayden's [[The Creation Dilemma]], Gavin concedes the dilemma's hardest premises — God freely creates the "sons of perdition" foreknowing their damnation — and ultimately retreats to **mystery** ("we will not know in this life") and to **prophetic necessity** (someone had to fulfill the Judas prophecy for the atonement). Critics note this is the weakest part of his case. See [[Appeal to Mystery Empties the Word Good]] and [[Appeal to Prophetic Necessity Rebutted]].
