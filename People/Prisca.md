# Prisca

First-century Christian missionary, wife of Aquila, named six times in the New Testament (often by the nickname Priscilla). With her husband she worked alongside Paul "in Christ Jesus" (Romans 16:3) and hosted a church in their home.

## Teacher of Apollos
Prisca and Aquila were "teachers and spiritual mentors to Apollos," the eloquent Alexandrian Jew who became a powerful missionary (Acts 18:24–26). [[Ariel Bybee Laughton]] notes that Prisca's name **precedes her husband's** four of the six times they are mentioned — including when they teach Apollos — leading some scholars to argue she may have been "the principal teacher of Apollos … a woman of 'excellent didactic qualities and missionary skills as well as outstanding education.'" A leading example of a woman exercising missionary leadership in a male-female companionship. See [[Women Led the First-Century Church as Apostles Deacons and Patrons]]. [^1]
