# Lorenzo Snow

LDS apostle (1814–1901) and later fifth president of the LDS Church. He is most
famous for crystallizing the doctrine of [[Human Deification in Mormonism]] from
the [[King Follett Discourse]] into a memorable couplet.

## The Lorenzo Snow Couplet

> As man is, God once was. As God is, man may become.[^1]

Per Benjamin Park, this phrase "has its own history within the broader culture"
of Mormonism, and the couplet captures in distilled form the radical theology
of the King Follett Discourse: that God progressed from mortality to godhood,
and humans can follow the same path.[^1]

## Modern Ambivalence

When [[Gordon B. Hinckley]] was asked about the Snow couplet in a 1997 national
interview, he responded: "I'm not sure we really teach that" — revealing the
enduring tension between the doctrine and efforts to emphasize LDS common ground
with mainstream Christianity.[^2]
