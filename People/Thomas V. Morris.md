# Thomas V. Morris

**Role:** Philosopher of religion
**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** Christian

## Bio

Thomas V. Morris is an American philosopher who taught for many years at the University of Notre Dame and wrote influential works in philosophical theology, notably *The Logic of God Incarnate* (Cornell University Press, 1986).

## The soteriological "scandal"

[[David Paulsen]] cites Morris as a sharp statement of the **soteriological problem of evil**. Morris asks how the many who lived and died before Christ — or in cultures untouched by the gospel — "can be saved through him," calling it a moral and religious "scandal" to claim a salvation "inaccessible to most people." Paulsen treats Morris's challenge as one of the premises in the [[The Soteriological Problem of Evil - The Inconsistent Triad|inconsistent triad]] that the restored doctrine of postmortem salvation resolves.
