# Terryl Givens

LDS scholar and professor of religion. Per Benjamin Park, Givens is the likely
author of the LDS Church's Gospel Topics essay "Becoming Like God," which
represents the current institutional approach to the [[King Follett Discourse]]'s
radical doctrines.

## The Theosis Framing

The essay connects Mormon exaltation to broader Christian traditions of theosis
(divinization) — particularly Eastern Orthodoxy — rather than presenting it as a
uniquely LDS innovation. This frames [[Human Deification in Mormonism]] as part of
a broader Christian conversation while retaining Mormonism's distinctive materialist
framework.[^1]

Per Park, this approach "emphasizes both the exceptionalism of Mormonism as well as
its connections to the broader Christian world — which is where I think the
Latter-day Saints currently are today in viewing the King Follett Discourse."[^1]

## "Where Christianity Went Wrong" (Keystone interview)

In a 44-minute Keystone interview, Givens narrates a "reconstituting of Christian history" centered on the nature of God. His baseline is that [[John the Evangelist|John]]'s "God is love" ([[1 John 4.8]]) names the foundational Christian discovery — love most fully revealed when Christ washed the apostles' feet — and that the [[Moses 7|weeping God of Moses 7]] restores it.[^2] He locates two datable corruptions (the overthrow of [[Origen]] and [[Pelagius]] — see [[The Two Great Wrong Turns - Overthrow of Origen and Pelagius]]), traces the privileging of sovereignty over love to [[Sovereignty Was Privileged Over Love and Creation Ex Nihilo Drove It|creation ex nihilo]], and delivers his "most scathing critique" against the claim that [[Gods Goodness Is Our Goodness - Against the Moral-Universe Divide|God's goodness is not our goodness]]. He affirms [[Social Trinitarianism Better Fits the Distinct Persons of Scripture|social trinitarianism]] ([[Jurgen Moltmann]]), a [[A Self-Limiting God - Reinterpreting Omniscience Omnipotence and Omnipresence|self-limiting God]] who need not foreknow the future, and a [[Heaven Is an Arena of Continued Struggle and Mutual Reconciliation|heaven of continued struggle and reconciliation]]. He notes mainstream Christianity has converged on LDS positions on universalism, embodiment, and passibility but [[Premortality Is the One LDS Doctrine Mainstream Christianity Will Not Move Toward|never on premortality]].

> "The fact that that question can even be in dispute is the most severe evidence of a going off the rails of Christianity."[^3]

## Convergence of Christianity toward LDS doctrines

Givens argues that Christians across the denominational spectrum are converging on what used to be considered uniquely Mormon heresies.[^4] He observes that LDS doctrines once "outside the margins of mainstream Christianity are now being reinvestigated just vigorously by theologians."[^5]
