# LaJean Carruth

LaJean Purcell Carruth is an expert in 19th-century Pitman shorthand working with the Church History Department in Salt Lake City. Because she can return to the original shorthand notes behind published sermons, she is uniquely positioned to evaluate how [[Brigham Young]] was represented in print.[^1]

When [[Daniel C. Peterson]] asked her the single biggest misrepresentation of Brigham Young, she answered: the *Journal of Discourses*. Its transcriber George D. Watt, when faced with ambiguous shorthand, tended to render Brigham as tougher and more autocratic than the originals warrant (e.g., "I am perfect in my generation" where Brigham said "*If* I am perfect").[^2] She is also a co-author, with [[Paul Reeve]] and [[Christopher Rich]], of *This Abominable Slavery*.[^3]
