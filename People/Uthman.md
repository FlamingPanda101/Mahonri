# Uthman

**Full Name:** Uthman ibn Affan
**Era:** c. 573/576 – 656 AD
**Tradition:** Islam (third Rashidun caliph)
**Role:** Caliph; standardizer of the Quranic text

## Bio

Uthman ibn Affan was the third caliph of the early Islamic community and a companion of [[Muhammad]]. He is most relevant to apologetics and textual history as the figure who, according to Islamic tradition (Sahih al-Bukhari, Book 6), ordered the **standardization of the Quran**: when reports reached him that Muslims in different regions were reciting the text differently, his advisers warned, "Save this nation before they differ about the book... as the Jews and Christians did before us." Uthman collected the variant written codices, established a single authorized text, and ordered the differing copies destroyed.

This "Uthmanic recension" is the pivot of the argument that the Quran underwent a **controlled transmission** rather than a free one: because variant readings were deliberately suppressed, modern scholars (e.g. [[Fred Donner]]) hold that we cannot easily trace the text back behind Uthman to verify what Muhammad recited. [[Wes Huff]] uses this contrast to argue the New Testament's free transmission is more recoverable than the Quran's.
