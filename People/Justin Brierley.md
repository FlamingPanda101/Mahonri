# Justin Brierley

**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** Christian
**Role:** Broadcaster; host of *Uncommon Ground* and formerly *Unbelievable?*

## Bio

Justin Brierley is a British Christian broadcaster, long-time host of the *Unbelievable?* debate program and now *Uncommon Ground with Justin Brierley* (Think Faith). He is the author of *The Surprising Rebirth of Belief in God* and *Why I'm Still a Christian*. In this episode he moderates the dialogue between [[Alex OConnor|Alex O'Connor]] and [[Glenn Scrivener]], opening with an "exercise in empathy" where each guest steelmans the other's position.[^1]

## Topics

### Moderating the Morality Debate
Brierley frames the question ("Did Christian faith give us our belief in kindness, equality, freedom, and consent?"), structures the conversation around compassion, equality, freedom, slavery/abolition, and consent, and repeatedly summarizes the two positions. He surfaces the link between O'Connor's [[Ethical Emotivism as O'Connor's Metaethic|emotivism]] and the historical-contingency thesis. See [[Shared Intuitions Despite Different Metaethics]].[^1]
