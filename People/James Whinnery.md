# James Whinnery
**Role:** Air Force flight surgeon; centrifuge / G-force researcher
**Era:** Late 20th century

## Bio
Dr. James Whinnery of the National Warfare Institute was an Air Force flight surgeon who, while studying the G-forces fighter pilots endure in high-speed jets, developed an **experimental technique by which near-death-like experiences could be induced in a controlled environment**. Pilots brought to the point of near-death by acceleration in "a gigantic centrifuge" reported near-death experiences under that stress [^1]. Melvin Morse highlights the significance of independent convergence: "Dr. Moody, a psychiatrist from Georgia; Dr. Whinnery, a flight surgeon for the Air Force; and I, a pediatrician from Seattle, each working independently, reached the same conclusions about near-death experiences" [^2]. Whinnery's work is apologetically notable because it produced NDE phenomena under reproducible laboratory conditions rather than relying solely on retrospective patient testimony.
