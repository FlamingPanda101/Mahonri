# Morton Smith

**Morton Smith** (1915-1991) was an American historian of ancient Christianity at Columbia University. He is best known for his 1958 discovery, at the Mar Saba monastery near Jerusalem, of a letter attributed to [[Clement of Alexandria]] (the *Letter to Theodore*) describing a longer, secret recension of the Gospel of Mark — what is now called **"Secret Mark."**

## Bio / Significance
Smith photographed the 18th-century manuscript copy at Mar Saba and published its transcription; the original subsequently went missing and was never subjected to modern forensic testing. On the strength of the letter's claim that Mark composed "a more spiritual gospel for the use of those who were being perfected" — withholding the "hierophantic" (temple) teachings — Latter-day Saint apologists such as [[Jonah Barnes]] cite it as evidence of a suppressed early-Christian temple gospel.

## Contribution
A substantial body of scholars regards the *Letter to Theodore* as a **modern forgery**, possibly Smith's own composition. This provenance dispute is the central weakness of any argument built on Secret Mark; the Latter-day Saint reply is that the suppressed-teaching pattern is independently attested elsewhere and does not rest on this letter alone. See [[Clement of Alexandria and Secret Mark Attest a Suppressed Temple Gospel]].
