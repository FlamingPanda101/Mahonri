# Robert Barron

**Era:** Contemporary (1959–present)
**Tradition:** Roman Catholic
**Role:** Bishop (Diocese of Winona-Rochester); founder of the Word on Fire Catholic ministries; theologian and apologist

## Bio
Robert Barron is an American Roman Catholic bishop, theologian, and one of the most prominent Catholic media apologists of the era, known for the *Word on Fire* ministry and the *Catholicism* documentary series. He holds a doctorate in sacred theology (Institut Catholique de Paris) and writes across philosophy of religion, Thomism, and culture.

## Positions in this source
Quoted at length in [[Jacob Hansen]]'s *Thoughtful Faith* "witness of authority" video as a non-LDS authority who converges on a probabilistic epistemology:

- **Knowledge by degrees of probability, not epidictic certitude.** Apart from "certain mathematical givens," nearly everything — all the sciences, all of history (e.g. Caesar's death in 44 BC) — is held to varying degrees of probability via unverified tradition. Religion is therefore not "uniquely unsure." See [[Knowledge Runs on Degrees of Probability Not Certitude]].
- **The clarity-of-science vs obscurity-of-religion split is a "false, phony dichotomy."** 100% certitude is an "Enlightenment fantasy"; the Enlightenment's own critics (Goethe, Pascal) saw that the "most important truths… are known in the twilight." See [[The Clarity of Science vs Obscurity of Religion Is a False Dichotomy]].
