# Jonathan Neville

**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint (LDS)
**Role:** Author and independent researcher; leading "Heartland" / New-York-Cumorah advocate

## Bio
Jonathan Neville is a Latter-day Saint lawyer (former Air Force JAG officer and New Mexico Supreme Court clerk) turned prolific author of more than ten books on church history, Book of Mormon geography, and the translation of the Book of Mormon. He is a central figure in the "Heartland" movement — though he prefers to frame his position as simply "the New York Cumorah" — and runs the *no more contention* blog, where he posts side-by-side comparisons of competing models. He came to the topic through Rod Meldrum after a career as a lawyer and businessman, having earlier been "a total FARMS guy" persuaded by [[John Sorenson]]'s Mesoamerican model.[^1] On *The Stick of Joseph* he was interviewed explicitly as the counterweight to the Mesoamerican (Kirk Magleby) view.

## On the origin of the Mesoamerican model
Neville argues the Mesoamerican geography is a 19th-century artifact, not a revealed setting: it grew from the 1841 Stephens-and-Catherwood travel books and from early member [[Benjamin Winchester]], whom Neville credits with authoring the 1842 *Times and Seasons* Mesoamerican articles that have long been attributed to Joseph Smith as editor. See [[The Times and Seasons Mesoamerican Articles Were Benjamin Winchester Not Joseph Smith]] and [[Oliver Cowdery's Letter VII States Cumorah Is in New York as Fact]].[^1]

## On geography and translation method
He fixes a New York Cumorah as a "pin in the map," reads [[D&C 125]]'s "Zarahemla" as an ancient-site identification, and answers Mesoamerican objections with the [[The New Madrid Earthquake Explains Third Nephi's Darkness Without Volcanoes|New Madrid earthquake]] and a [[Sea in the Book of Mormon Can Mean a Mighty River|lexical argument that "sea" can mean a mighty river]].[^2] On translation he holds the [[The Urim and Thummim Is Not a Real Parallel|Urim and Thummim]] was the instrument and the seer stone only a demonstration prop. Methodologically he urges "clarity, charity, and understanding" and a "faith model" of belief — see [[Clarity Charity and Understanding and the Faith Model]].
