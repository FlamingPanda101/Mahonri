# Rowan Williams

Anglican theologian and the 104th Archbishop of Canterbury; also a respected patristic scholar. His *Arius: Heresy and Tradition* is a major study of the Arian controversy.

## Cited by Moroni's Standard
[[Alex Arnold]] quotes Williams's *Arius: Heresy and Tradition* for the conclusion that the Arian controversy was "a thoroughly difficult theological question that the pre-Nicene tradition had not definitively answered," and that Nicaea "represented a decision rather than any discovery." This is central to the argument that the creed is a 4th-century choice between options, not a recognition of what the text plainly said. See [[The Trend Line Runs the Wrong Way for the Recovery Narrative]] and [[The Creeds Cannot Be the Neutral Baseline - The Circularity of Creedal Disqualification]].[^1]
