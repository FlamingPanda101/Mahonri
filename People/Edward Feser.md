# Edward Feser

**Role:** Philosopher; defender of Thomistic / classical theism
**Era:** Contemporary (b. 1968)
**Tradition:** Roman Catholic

## Bio

Edward Feser is a Catholic philosopher and prominent contemporary defender of Aristotelian-Thomistic metaphysics and classical theism. [[Blake Ostler]] cites Feser approvingly for candor about the Trinity, quoting him to the effect that "human reason cannot fully grasp the Trinity even after it has been revealed." [^1]

## The Trinity as Revealed, Not Reasoned

Ostler uses Feser (alongside [[Thomas Aquinas]]) to make the point that classical theologians themselves distinguish **revealed truths** (known through revelation, e.g., the Trinity) from truths reachable by **reason** — and that the Trinity falls in the former category, even being in a sense "contrary to reason." Ostler turns this into a dilemma: if the Trinity is a *revealed* doctrine it should appear in revelation (it does not, as no text says the three persons are one substance); if it is *reasoned*, it fails the tests of logic and exegesis.
