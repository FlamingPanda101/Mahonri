## Who
Maximus the Confessor (Byzantine theologian) wrote the *Mystagogia* (ca. AD 628–650), which contains the earliest complete text of the Byzantine rite and interprets the symbolism of the church building and its liturgy.

## Authority and Reason for Citation
Maximus is cited as a primary source for the developed Byzantine Eucharistic liturgy and for the cosmic symbolism of church architecture — including the church as combining heaven and earth and the role of the liturgy as a heavenly drama (Mystagogia 1–24). His work helps reconstruct how late-antique Christians understood their worship space as a meeting place of the heavenly and earthly realms.
