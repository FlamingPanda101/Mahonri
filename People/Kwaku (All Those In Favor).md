# Kwaku (All Those In Favor)

LDS content creator and returned missionary (served in Detroit), originally from **Ghana** and in the U.S. since age seven; recently married. He appears as the guest on [[All Those In Favor - Pastor Destroys Mormon Missionaries Former Missionaries React]], joining hosts [[Cade (All Those In Favor)|Cade]] and [[Baylor Johnson (Ward Radio)|Baylor Johnson]] to react to a viral "pastor owns Mormon missionaries" clip.

## In this source
Kwaku argues the Book of Mormon makes "complex things sound way easier," citing [[1 Nephi 11.18|the condescension of God]] in 2 Nephi to frame the Incarnation; he reframes the "test of a prophet" ([[Deuteronomy 18.20-22|Deuteronomy 18]]) as inseparable from how prophecy actually unfolds — Joseph Smith, like the [[Isaiah]] prophecies of double fulfillment, "figuring it out with you guys" as revelations are understood over time. He presses the "spiritual dishonesty" charge: that critics revere the Bible while condemning the same phenomena (polygamy, continuing revelation) in the Restoration.
