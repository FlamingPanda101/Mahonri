# Christopher Wood

**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** Non-LDS academic
**Role:** Assyriologist / Sumerologist

## Bio
Christopher Woods is a non-Latter-day-Saint Assyriologist and Sumerologist (Director of the Penn Museum; previously at the University of Chicago's Oriental Institute). *(Rendered "Christopher Wood" in the Ward Radio transcript.)*

## Cited For — Conceding the Olishem Match
Ward Radio cites Woods as a hostile-witness concession: even a non-LDS scholar "grudgingly admitted certainly Ulisum could be superficially linked on phonetic grounds to the Olishem in the Book of Abraham." Because the concession comes from outside the LDS apologetic community, the hosts treat it as strengthening the case that Joseph Smith's "Olishem" (a place absent from the Bible) matches the ancient toponym attested in a [[Naram-Sin]] inscription. See [[Olishem Is a Linguistic Geographical and Chronological Match]].[^1]
