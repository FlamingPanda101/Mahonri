# Naram-Sin

**Era:** Bronze Age, c. 2254–2218 BCE (reign)
**Role:** Akkadian king (historical figure)

## Bio
Naram-Sin was a king of the Akkadian Empire and grandson of Sargon of Akkad, attested in his own royal inscriptions from the late third millennium BCE — roughly the era traditionally associated with [[Abraham]].

## Cited For — Olishem / Ulisum
On Ward Radio, [[Kerry Muhlestein]] and [[Luke Hansen]] note that a royal inscription from Naram-Sin's reign records a place named **Ulisum (Ulishum)** in the Syria/Turkey region — the proposed ancient match for the plains of **Olishem** in [[Abraham 1.15-19|Abraham 1:10]]. Because O/U and S/Sh interchange in Semitic languages, "Olishem" and "Ulisum" form a linguistic match; and because the inscription dates to Abraham's general era and locates the place where the Book of Abraham would put it, the correlation is simultaneously linguistic, geographical, and chronological. The non-LDS Assyriologist [[Christopher Wood]] conceded the phonetic linkage. See [[Olishem Is a Linguistic Geographical and Chronological Match]].[^1]
