# Charles B. Thompson

Early Latter-day Saint (1814–1895) cited by [[Christopher Blythe|Chris Blyth]] as the **first person to write a book on the Book of Mormon** (in the 1830s). Thompson got into a dispute with [[Joseph Smith]] over interpretations of Revelation, later left to **found his own church**, and there taught **reincarnation** and a scheme of progressive **estates** — a ranked lineup in which everyone must in turn be a "Jesus" and a "Moses," with **Adam as the highest of those ranks** (though none "all the way God").[^1]

## Significance: an independent Adam-extrapolation
Thompson matters as a **control case** for the argument in [[How Brigham Young Extrapolated Adam-God from Joseph Smith]]. He developed an exalted-Adam hierarchy "with no connection to [[Brigham Young]]," apparently extrapolating — like Brigham — from Joseph Smith's identification of the **ancient of days** ([[Daniel 7.13-14|Daniel 7]]) as Adam. Blyth: "I think it's very possible Brigham's doing a really similar thing." Two independent figures arriving at an over-exalted Adam from the same Josephian seed makes Brigham's claim to have learned the idea from Joseph more historically credible.[^1]
