# Virginia Clemm

Virginia Eliza Clemm (1822–1847) was the wife and first cousin of the American writer [[Edgar Allan Poe]]. In 1836 she married Poe (then 27) when she was 13; the marriage was not a major public scandal in its day, and Poe continued to be celebrated as a literary figure.

## Relevance

She is cited in the vault as a historical comparator in the defense of [[Joseph Smith]] against accusations of predatory marriage. Her marriage to Poe is offered as evidence that older men marrying teenage girls was a recognized, non-scandalous practice in 19th-century America, so applying the modern label "pedophile" to such figures is anachronistic ("presentism").
