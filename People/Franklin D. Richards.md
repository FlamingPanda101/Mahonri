# Franklin D. Richards (1821-1899)

LDS **apostle** and, while presiding over the Church's European mission in Liverpool, the original **compiler of the Pearl of Great Price**. In 1851 he gathered scattered Joseph Smith writings, revelations, and translations — material otherwise accessible only in back issues of newspapers like the *Times and Seasons* and *Millennial Star* — into a single convenient pamphlet for British Saints.[^1]

## Originator of the Pearl of Great Price
[[Stephen Smoot|Steven Smoot]] credits Richards with the "brilliant idea" to "bring the mountain to Muhammad" — compiling Joseph Smith's serially published material into one accessible format. Crucially, the 1851 compilation was a **distribution tool, not a canonized standard work**; it would not be canonized until [[Orson Pratt]]'s standardized 1878 edition was sustained in 1880. Richards thus stands first in the multi-generational chain of compilers (Richards → Pratt → [[James Talmage]]) that the episode uses to argue canonization is a community process.[^1][^2]
