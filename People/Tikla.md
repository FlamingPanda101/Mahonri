# Tikla

**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint
**Role:** Panelist / scripture expositor on [[Ward Radio]] (the women of Ward Radio)

## Bio

Tikla (also addressed as "Tia") is a Latter-day Saint contributor on Ward Radio who, in the
women-of-Ward-Radio temple-symbolism episode, advances an exploratory theory that the
**square** is a masculine symbol and the **circle/compass** a feminine symbol, and that
"masculinity presides" within each person. She repeatedly frames her ideas as personal
theories open to pushback. She is joined by [[Andrea Woodmansee]] and host
[[Britney (the Shadow)|Britney the Shadow]].

## On masculine and feminine temple symbols

Tikla argues that the square (and the carpenter's/builder's square, the cross, foundation
imagery) encodes masculine attributes — stability, uprightness, justice, presiding — while
the circle and compass encode feminine attributes — nurture, encircling, compassion, cycles.
She reads the plumb-bob on the square as humility aligning the masculine to God, and proposes
that within each person the masculine should "preside." See
[[The Square and Compass Are Masculine and Feminine Temple Symbols]] and
[[Masculinity Should Preside Within Each Person Not Just Between the Sexes]].[^1]
