# Neal Rappleye

**Era:** Contemporary (2020s)
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint (LDS)
**Role:** Scholar, apologist, researcher

## Bio

Neal Rappleye is a Latter-day Saint researcher and apologist who has published
extensively on Book of Mormon historicity. He authored the chapter "Written Upon
Gold Plates: Comparing Witness Descriptions with Artifacts from the Premodern
World" in the FAIR volume *Defending the Book of Mormon*, in which he surveys 24
ancient metal-plate inscriptions to test whether the witness descriptions of the
gold plates are consistent with genuine ancient artifacts.[^1]

## Gold Plates Fabrication

Argues the witness descriptions act as a strict checklist that any fabrication
hypothesis must satisfy, and that real ancient inscriptions show the required
small, dense engraving is attested in antiquity. See
[[Witness Descriptions Constrain Any Fabrication Hypothesis]],
[[Reformed Egyptian Characters Were Small]], and
[[Translation Density Shows the Book of Mormon Text Fits]].[^2] He also argues
that the dimensions match a pre-Columbian tumbaga artifact made in the middle of
the first century AD — see [[Tumbaga Alloy Explains Golden Appearance and Green Oxidation]].[^3]
