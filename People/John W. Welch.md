# John W. ("Jack") Welch

**Era:** 20th–21st century
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint (LDS)
**Role:** Legal scholar; founder of FARMS

## Bio
John W. ("Jack") Welch is a BYU law professor (J.D., Duke) who founded FARMS and is famous for identifying **chiasmus** in the Book of Mormon. He has been a central editor and organizer of Latter-day Saint scriptural scholarship for decades.[^1]

## The Place-Name Jershon
[[Matthew Bowen]] credits Welch (with Bob Smith) for finding the **Jershon** wordplay "unavoidable" — a "slam dunk." Jershon derives from Hebrew *yarash* ("to inherit/possess") and appears precisely where the Nephites give the land to Lamanite converts "for an inheritance."[^1] See [[Jershon Means Place of Inheritance]].

## "An Unparallel"
Welch authored an article titled "An Unparallel" arguing against *View of the Hebrews* dependence. He observes that the specific "proofs" [[Ethan Smith]] uses — Hebrew words, Hebrew names, and customs supposedly found among Native Americans — do *not* appear in the Book of Mormon; instead the book uses names like Sam, [[Nephi]], and [[Lehi]] that have since received independent Hebrew/ancient attestation and never appeared in *View of the Hebrews*. Smoot credits Welch (along with Truman G. Madsen) with sustained study of the question when the Roberts material resurfaced in the 1980s. See [[Book of Mormon Lacks the Hebrew Markers View of the Hebrews Uses]].[^2]

## Chiasmus as Evidence of Ancient Authorship
[[Mike Day]] credits Welch with finding the chiastic structure that, on Day's account, an unlearned Joseph Smith would have had to reproduce "out of your head" decades before such structures were academically described in the Book of Mormon — part of the case that the dictation feat exceeds Joseph's capacity. See [[Chiasmus as Evidence of Ancient Authorship in the Book of Mormon]] and [[The Book of Mormon Was Translated Mosiah-First]].[^3]
