# John Cassian

**Full name:** John Cassian (c. AD 360–435)
**Role:** Monk and ascetic writer; founder of monastic communities in Gaul
**Era:** Late 4th–early 5th century CE

## Bio
Cassian transmitted the wisdom of Eastern (especially Egyptian) monasticism to the Latin-speaking West through his *Institutes* and *Conferences*. Heal cites his *Conferences* 14.8.4 as the classic articulation of the fourfold sense of scripture. [^1]

## The fourfold sense of scripture
Cassian distinguishes four legitimate senses of a scriptural passage: **literal/historical** ("knowledge of past and visible things"), **allegorical** (a type "prefiguring a mystery"), **anagogical** (prophecy about "what lies in the future"), and **tropological** ("moral explanation pertaining to correction of life"). His worked example reads "Jerusalem" four ways at once: the city of the Jews (history), the Church of Christ (allegory), the heavenly city "which is the mother of us all" (anagogy, Galatians 4:26), and the human soul (tropology). [^1]
