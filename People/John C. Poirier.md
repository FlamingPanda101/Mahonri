# John C. Poirier

Biblical scholar whose work on the New Testament usage of "word of God" / "word of the Lord" forms the scholarly backbone of [[Trent Horn]]'s argument in this video. (Transcribed in the auto-captions variously as "Pourier"/"Poier.")

## "Word of God" Never Refers to Scripture Itself
Poirier argues that Scripture uses "word of God" in two ways — as a reference to **prophecy** ("the word of the Lord came to Elijah…") or to the **apostles' preaching** — and that "the phrase word of God never appears as a reference to scripture itself," a convention "wholly out of keeping" with applying it to the Bible. He further notes that every NT "God said / the Spirit says" citation of the OT appeals to a *prophetic* passage. See [[Word of God Means Prophecy Preaching or Christ Not Scripture]], [[Hebrews 4.12 Word of God Is Christ or Gods Commands Not Scripture]], and [[John 10.35 Cannot Be Broken Means Cannot Be Nullified]] [^1].

## Theopneustos Meant "Life-Giving" Not "Inerrant" (via Keystone)
A [[Keystone]] explainer leans on Poirier's monograph (Library of New Testament Studies) on the meaning of *theopneustos* in [[2 Timothy 3.16-17|2 Timothy 3:16]]. Per Keystone, Poirier found the word was used in that period for **sandals, burial ointments, and streams of water** — life-giving things, not inerrant ones — so "God-breathed" meant *vivifying / life-giving*, not "without error." Poirier also dates the inerrantist "inspired" reading to [[Origen]] in the third century. See [[Theopneustos Was Used for Sandals Ointments and Streams Not Inerrancy]].[^2]
