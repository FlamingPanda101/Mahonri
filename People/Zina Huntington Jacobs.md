# Zina Huntington Jacobs

**Role:** Plural wife of [[Joseph Smith]]; later wife of [[Brigham Young]]; third general president of the Relief Society
**Era:** 1821–1901
**Tradition:** The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints

## Bio
Zina Diantha Huntington was sealed to [[Joseph Smith]] on October 27, 1841, while she was legally married to and living with Henry Jacobs ([[Henry Jacobs]]) and roughly **seven months pregnant** with Henry's child. Her son Zebulon Jacobs was born January 2, 1842; DNA testing of descendants confirms Zebulon was Henry's biological son, not Joseph's.

## Significance in [[Don Bradley]]'s argument
On Bradley's corrected chronology, Zina — not Louisa Beeman — is Joseph's *first* Nauvoo plural wife, and the fact that he sealed a heavily pregnant married woman is the lead datum against the "polygamy was about sex" thesis (1800s medical custom forbade sex late in pregnancy). Zina also preserved the firsthand account of the **angel with a drawn sword** by which Joseph said he was compelled to proceed. See [[Joseph Smith Married Pregnant Women Refuting the Sexual-Motive Thesis]] and [[The Angel with a Drawn Sword Coincides with the Orionid Meteor Shower]].
