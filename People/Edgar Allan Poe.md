# Edgar Allan Poe

**Who:** Edgar Allan Poe (1809–1849), American poet, short story writer, and literary critic. Widely regarded as one of the greatest American writers — a central figure of Romanticism, inventor of the detective fiction genre, and master of Gothic horror. Works include "The Raven," "The Tell-Tale Heart," and "The Fall of the House of Usher."

**Role in Apologetics:** Poe is cited by LDS apologists as a historical comparator in the defense of [[Joseph Smith]] against accusations of predatory marriage practices. His marriage to his cousin [[Virginia Clemm]] demonstrates that older-men-marrying-teenage-girls was a recognized, non-scandalous practice in 19th-century America.

## Marriage to Virginia Clemm
In 1836, Poe (age 27) married his first cousin Virginia Clemm, who was 13 years old. The marriage was not a major public scandal — Poe continued to be celebrated as a literary figure. This is offered as evidence that applying the modern label "pedophile" to 19th-century figures is historically anachronistic ("presentism").
