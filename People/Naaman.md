# Naaman

A Syrian (Aramean) army commander and leper in 2 Kings 5. The prophet Elisha, without coming out to meet him, sends word that Naaman must "wash in the Jordan seven times" to be cleansed. Naaman at first balks at so ordinary a remedy, but after obeying, "his flesh was restored like the flesh of a little child, and he was clean."

## Significance in the vault
[[Joe Heschmeyer]] opens his baptism case with Naaman as an Old Testament **type of baptism**: a simple, physical washing that seems "too ordinary" yet effects cleansing and a childlike rebirth — prefiguring how "the simple waters of baptism will save us, cleanse us, and cause us to be born again" ([[John 3.5]]). [[Ryan (NeedGod)|Ryan]] rejects the typology, calling Naaman's healing merely God cleansing him "through that means," not a picture of water baptism.
