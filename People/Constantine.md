# Constantine (Roman Emperor)

**Who:** Roman Emperor (r.306–337 CE) who convened the Council of Nicaea in 325 CE. First Roman emperor to convert to Christianity.

**Role in the Trinity:** Constantine called the Council of Nicaea to resolve doctrinal disputes that were causing disunity in the empire. [[Dan McClellan]] characterizes the council as Constantine saying "I don't care what you figure out — figure it out."[^1] Once the Nicene Creed was formulated, Constantine enforced it with imperial authority: "everybody sign on the dotted line or you get exiled."[^2]

**The Imperial Enforcement Thesis:** The [[Trinity - Historical Development|Trinity doctrine]] survived and became orthodoxy not because it was the clear teaching of the NT, but because "the institution of the church is the only reason... without the Roman Empire and all of the might and the coercion and the force behind it, it would have gone the way of the dodo long long ago."[^3]
