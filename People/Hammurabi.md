# Hammurabi

**Era:** c. 1810 – 1750 BCE
**Tradition:** Babylonian
**Role:** King of Babylon; promulgator of the Code of Hammurabi

## Bio

Hammurabi was the Babylonian king whose law code is one of the oldest substantially preserved legal texts.[^1]

## Topics

### Protecting the Weak Predates Christianity
[[Alex OConnor|Alex O'Connor]] cites the **prologue of Hammurabi's Code** — which states its purpose is "that the strong shall not be able to oppress the weak" — as a pre-Christian instantiation of the value Scrivener calls uniquely Christian. [[Glenn Scrivener]] counters that Hammurabi's laws are stratified by social class, so the protection is not universal. O'Connor replies that Scrivener's own favored origin (the Gregorian Reforms) is likewise non-universal and self-interested.
