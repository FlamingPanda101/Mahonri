# Paula Fredriksen

**Role:** Historian of ancient Christianity and Second Temple Judaism
**Affiliation:** Boston University (Aurelio Professor of Scripture, emerita); Hebrew University of Jerusalem (Distinguished Visiting Professor)
**Era:** Contemporary
**Authority:** Tier A — leading peer-reviewed historian of early Christianity, Paul, and ancient Mediterranean religion.

## Bio

Paula Fredriksen is a distinguished historian whose work focuses on Jesus, Paul ("the apostle to the pagans"), Augustine, and the relationship of nascent Christianity to its Jewish and Greco-Roman religious environments — including the "many gods, many lords" cosmos of antiquity. Notable books include *Paul: The Pagans' Apostle*, *From Jesus to Christ*, and *When Christians Were Jews*.

## Role in the Monotheism Debate

In the indexed video, [[Dan McClellan]] recommends a paper by Fredriksen as a piece of scholarship — by someone other than himself — addressing first-century Jewish/Christian monotheism and the relationship of New Testament Christology to its context, an alternative to the framing of [[Larry Hurtado]] and [[Richard Bauckham]]. > "if you would like a piece of scholarship from another scholar that addresses this I would highly recommend this paper by Paula Fredriksen." [^1]
