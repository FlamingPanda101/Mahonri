# St. Nicholas of Myra

**Who:** Bishop of Myra (in modern-day Turkey, c.270–343 CE). Attended the Council of Nicaea (325 CE). Later became the inspiration for the figure of Santa Claus.

**At Nicaea:** Tradition holds that during the heated debates at the [[Council of Nicaea]], Nicholas became so enraged at [[Arius]]'s arguments that he struck Arius in the face. McClellan references this tradition in discussing the contentious atmosphere of the council.[^1]

**Significance:** The story of Nicholas slapping Arius illustrates the volatile nature of the Nicene debates — this was not a dispassionate theological seminar but a politically charged confrontation enforced by imperial authority under [[Constantine]].
