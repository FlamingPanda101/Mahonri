# Henry B. Eyring

**Era:** Contemporary (1933–present)
**Tradition:** The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints
**Role:** Apostle (Quorum of the Twelve); former First/Second Counselor in the First Presidency

## Bio
Henry B. Eyring is an apostle of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, ordained to the Quorum of the Twelve in 1995 and serving in the First Presidency 2007–2018. He holds a doctorate from Harvard Business School and taught at Stanford before church service.

## Positions in this source
Quoted briefly in [[Jacob Hansen]]'s "witness of authority" video on the limits of perception:

- **Loss of faith as self-inflicted spiritual blindness.** Of those who report losing religious faith because of secular teachings, Eyring says "their problem does not lie in what they think they see; it lies in what they cannot see" — reframing disbelief as a deficit of spiritual sight rather than evidence against the truth.
