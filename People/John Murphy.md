# John Murphy

**Who:** John Murphy of Polo County, a 19th-century figure who claimed that, in conversation with him, [[David Whitmer]] had "denied my testimony of the Book of Mormon."

**Role in this source:** Murphy's claim is the trigger for one of the strongest pieces of witness evidence. In response, [[David Whitmer]] went door to door gathering signatures and published a statement in the Richmond newspaper: "If he did not understand me then, let him understand me now: I have never at any time denied my testimony." The town's leading banker, prominent attorney, former mayor, and sitting sheriff co-signed an attestation that Whitmer was a man of good character and sound mind.[^1]

## On the Witnesses' Reaffirmation
Murphy's accusation backfired, producing a documented, witnessed public re-affirmation that survives.
