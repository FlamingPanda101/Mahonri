# Montague Rhodes James (1862–1936)

**Role:** Cambridge medievalist, palaeographer, biblical scholar (and ghost-story author)
**Era:** Late 19th–early 20th century
**Tradition:** Anglican (non-LDS)

## Bio
M. R. James was a Cambridge scholar, Provost of King's College and later of Eton, best known to scholars for *The Apocryphal New Testament* (Oxford, 1924), the standard English collection of New Testament apocrypha for much of the 20th century.

## Cited on the date of the Acts of John in English
[[Alex Arnold]] notes that the first scholarly English translation of the [[The Acts of John Records Jesus Instituting a Mystery Ritual|Acts of John]] — the 2nd-century text whose circle-ritual scene (verses 94–96) parallels the endowment — appeared in James's 1924 collection, **82 years after** Joseph Smith introduced the endowment in 1842. The date is load-bearing for Arnold's "direction of the evidence" argument: the parallel content was not accessible in English to Joseph. See [[The Direction of the Evidence Runs Opposite to How Fraud Behaves]].[^1]
