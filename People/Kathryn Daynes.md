# Kathryn Daynes

An academic historian of Latter-day Saint plural marriage, author of *More Wives Than One: Transformation of the Mormon Marriage System, 1840–1910* (University of Illinois Press). Cited by [[Britney Chapman Nash]] as the author of "one of the best studies that's been done" on how many people actually practiced polygamy.[^1]

Daynes studied the community of Manti, Utah as a representative population over a roughly 40-year span, producing the datable percentages Nash uses to show polygamy's prevalence and steady decline: from the 1860 federal census onward, plural-family membership fell from 43.1% to 7.1% by 1900.[^2] See [[Polygamy Was a Minority Practice That Declined Steadily]].
