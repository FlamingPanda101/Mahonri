# Todd Compton

LDS historian and classicist (Ph.D., UCLA). In the Great Apostasy episode, Day cites Compton's work for the **etymology and nuance of *apostasia*** — that the Greek can mean either an act of rebellion/mutiny *or* a gradual falling away, the distinction on which Day's "gradual" reading rests. See [[Apostasy as Gradual Falling Away Not Single Event]].

## On the meaning of apostasy
Compton's analysis of *apo* ("away") + *histēmi* ("to stand") frames the entire definitional question of the episode: is the Great Apostasy a single revolt or a slow drift?
