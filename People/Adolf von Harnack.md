# Adolf von Harnack

German Lutheran theologian and church historian (1851–1930), among the most influential historians of doctrine of the modern era. Best known for his *History of Dogma* and the thesis of the "Hellenization of Christianity."

## Cited by Moroni's Standard
[[Alex Arnold]] cites Harnack's description of the process by which Greek philosophical categories became the primary interpretive lens for Christian doctrine — "the acute Hellenization of Christianity" — gradually displacing the more historically grounded categories of the Hebrew tradition. See [[Translation Versus Transformation - The Order of Operations in Hellenization]].[^1]

Note: the vault's [[Hellenization Was Not Corruption of an Original Pure Church]] records the counter-scholarship (Combs, via Elizabeth A. Clark) that Harnack's Hellenization-as-corruption model was itself shaped by 19th-century anti-Catholicism — so Harnack is cited by *both* sides of the vault's Hellenization debate.
