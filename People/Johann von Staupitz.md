# Johann von Staupitz

**Full name:** Johann von Staupitz (c. 1460–1524)
**Role:** Augustinian vicar general, theologian, mentor of Martin Luther
**Era:** Reformation (early 16th century)
**Tradition:** Roman Catholic (Augustinian)

## Bio

Johann von Staupitz was vicar general of the Observant Augustinians in Germany and dean of theology at the new University of Wittenberg. As Martin Luther's confessor and superior, he counseled the anxious young monk toward the grace of Christ and, decisively, directed him to take a doctorate in theology at Wittenberg. Though he remained within the Roman church until his death, his pastoral influence on Luther was profound.

## Relevance to the vault

Staupitz is central to the "external call" argument that Luther was an *accidental* reformer, not a self-appointed revolutionary. Because his ecclesiastical superior commissioned him by formal vow "to teach the Word of God," Luther's reforming work is framed as arising from a legitimate institutional mandate — deliberately contrasted with someone who acts merely on a private "burning in the bosom." This supports the vault's case that genuine reform arises from circumstance and authorized calling.
