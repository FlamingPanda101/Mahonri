# Samuel the Lamanite

**Role:** Lamanite prophet
**Era:** Book of Mormon (c. 6 BCE in the narrative)
**Tradition:** Book of Mormon

## Bio

Samuel the Lamanite is a prophet in the Book of Mormon (Helaman 13–16) who preaches repentance to the Nephites from atop the wall of Zarahemla. He delivers detailed prophecies of the signs of Christ's birth (a day, a night, and a day without darkness; a new star) and of Christ's death (three days of darkness). After prophesying, he escapes the wall amid a hail of arrows and stones and "was never heard of more among the Nephites."

## Relevance to the vault

Samuel features in a speculative Restoration "aside" about the identity of the magi. Because he vanishes from the record right after prophesying Christ's birth, one host floats the popular idea that the wise men could be Lehite/Nephite figures — even Samuel himself. The guest doubts the identification but grants Samuel is "in the pattern of the wise men," illustrating the kind of imaginative Book-of-Mormon harmonization the vault catalogs.
