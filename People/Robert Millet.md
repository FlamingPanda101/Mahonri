# Robert Millet

BYU professor of ancient scripture and LDS scholar. Known for his extensive
interfaith work with evangelical Christians and his role in LDS-evangelical dialogue.

## The Nauvoo Gathering

At a gathering between BYU professors and evangelical professors in Nauvoo, Millet
was among those who downplayed the [[King Follett Discourse]], suggesting its
doctrines might not be "fully canonized." [[David Paulsen]] forcefully pushed back,
declaring "I belong to the Church of King Follett. We are not moving away from
it."[^1]

This exchange reveals the enduring tension in LDS intellectual circles between
those who exalt in Mormonism's radical distinctiveness and those who seek to
emphasize common ground with mainstream Christianity (see [[Modern LDS Tensions on
King Follett Discourse]]).
