# Hippocrates

**Era:** c. 460 – c. 370 BCE
**Tradition:** Ancient Greek
**Role:** Physician; namesake of the Hippocratic Oath

## Bio

Hippocrates of Kos is the traditional "father of medicine," to whom the Hippocratic Oath is attributed (probably not authored by him, as O'Connor concedes).[^1]

## Topics

### Egalitarian Medical Ethic Predates Christianity
[[Alex OConnor|Alex O'Connor]] cites the Hippocratic Oath — "whoever's house I walk into, rich or poor, male or female, I am to serve them" — as a pre-Christian source of egalitarian medical ethics with language strikingly parallel to [[Galatians 3.28|Galatians 3:28]] ("neither male nor female"). His point: if this had appeared in Paul's letters, Christians would claim it proves Christianity grounds medical ethics. [[Glenn Scrivener]] concedes the value ("yay and amen to Hippocrates") but argues only Christianity *institutionalized* it into hospitals for the poor and sick *as such*. See [[The Hippocratic Oath Predates the Christian Medical Ethic]] and [[Institutionalization Is the Unique Christian Contribution]].[^1]
