# Stephen Smoot

**Full Name:** Stephen O. Smoot
**Era:** Contemporary (2020s)
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint (LDS)
**Role:** Scholar, apologist, lecturer

## Bio

Stephen Smoot is an LDS scholar and apologist with graduate-level academic
training in ancient Near Eastern studies / Egyptology. He participates in the
Stick of Judah lecture series hosted by [[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)|David
Butler]] and has presented on the Book of Abraham and ancient astronomy
("Abraham the Astronomer").[^1] He co-authored *A Guide to the Book of Abraham*,
a peer-reviewed special issue of BYU Studies.

## Open Letter to Trent Horn on the Book of Mormon

After [[Trent Horn]]'s August 2024 debate with [[Jacob Hansen]], Smoot published
an open letter (read by Hansen on Thoughtful Faith) advising Horn to retire
several weak arguments and offering point-by-point correctives:
- [[David Whitmer Was Told to Flee Far West Not to Leave the Church]] — the 1887
  *Address to All Believers* quote is misread; Whitmer was already excommunicated
  and was told to flee the Danites at Far West.
- [[The Evening Recitals Were Joseph Relaying Moroni's Information]] — Lucy Mack
  Smith's "amusing recitals" describe Joseph relaying Moroni's instruction, not
  inventing fiction.
- [[The Witnesses Statement in Cowderys Hand Is the Printers Manuscript]] — the
  signatures are in Cowdery's hand because that document is the printer's copy.
- [[History of the Church Is a Composite Work Not a Rapid Dictation]] — it is not
  analogous to the Book of Mormon's rapid dictation.
- [[The Book of Mormon Fits on the Plates - Sjodahl Hebrew Test]] — Sjödahl's 1923
  experiment and Jerry Grover's metallurgy work.
- Source: [[Thoughtful Faith - Debunking Trent Horns Flawed Arguments Against The Book of Mormon Open Letter by Stephen Smoot]] [^2]
