# Peter Hayman

**Role:** Scholar of Jewish studies (University of Edinburgh)
**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** Academic biblical / Jewish studies

## Bio

A. Peter Hayman is a scholar of ancient Judaism, widely cited for his 1991 article "Monotheism — A Misused Word in Jewish Studies?", which argued that "monotheism" is an anachronistic and misleading label for ancient Israelite and early Jewish religion, which acknowledged a hierarchy of divine beings under one supreme God.

## On Monotheism as a Misused Word

In the debate-prep conversation Hansen reads a quotation from [[Joshua Sijuwade]] that names Hayman among the scholars (with [[Nathan McDonald]], [[Paula Fredriksen]], [[Michael Heiser]], [[Larry Hurtado]], and [[Richard Bauckham]]) holding that ancient Jewish "monotheism" affirmed Yahweh's unique supremacy rather than denying other divine beings. This grounds the LDS argument that biblical religion taught a divine plurality-in-unity (the Godhead). See:
