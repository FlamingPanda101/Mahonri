# Katherine Smith

**Who:** Katherine (Catherine) Smith (1813–1900), younger sister of [[Joseph Smith]] and an **informal witness** who physically handled the plates.

**Role in this source:** [[Daniel C. Peterson]] uses 14-year-old Katherine as another unprimed, tactile witness. Joseph, fleeing a mob, handed her the plates covered in a frock and told her to hide them; she staggered under their weight, carried them into a bedroom, put them under the covers, and pretended to be asleep so any intruders wouldn't disturb her. She never saw the plates but never forgot that "there was a real object there" — and that it was very heavy.[^1]

## On Unprimed, Physical Encounters
Her experience supports the weight/tangibility evidence and undercuts hallucination and "priming."
- [[Informal Witnesses Were Not Primed]]
- [[No Shared Hallucination]]

## Tactile Encounter with the Plates
In a separate retelling she recalled "seeing a package on the table containing the gold plates," picking them up to "judge the weight" — they "were heavy like gold" — and rippling her fingers along the edges, feeling separate metal plates and hearing "the tinkle of sound that they made."[^2] Her tactile testimony parallels that of [[William Smith]] and [[Emma Smith]]; see [[Natural vs Supernatural Witnesses]] and [[Informal Witnesses of the Golden Plates]].
