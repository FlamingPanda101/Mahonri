# Nathan Smith

Nathan Smith (1762–1829) was a pioneering American surgeon and the founder of the Dartmouth Medical School (he later helped found the medical school at Yale). At Dartmouth he developed an experimental operation for treating bone infection (osteomyelitis) by removing diseased bone rather than amputating the limb — a procedure decades ahead of the Civil-War-era default of amputation.

In the [[Let's Get Real with Stephen Jones - Joseph Smith's Biggest Discovery (Don Bradley E0039)|Let's Get Real interview]], [[Don Bradley]] notes that the Smith family "providentially" lived near Dartmouth, so when seven-year-old [[Joseph Smith]] faced amputation in 1813, a physician trained in Nathan Smith's limb-saving method proposed the experimental surgery instead. Bradley stresses how rare the operation was — only "a few dozen people in the history of the United States" ever had it, Nathan Smith being "the only one who knew how to do it" — making Joseph almost certainly the only child ever to undergo it without anesthetic.[^1] See [[Joseph Smith's Childhood Leg Surgery Was a Rare Experimental Operation]].
