# William Chamberlain

**Role:** Philosopher and theologian; BYU professor in the 1911 evolution controversy
**Era:** Early 20th century (d. 1921)
**Tradition:** The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (LDS / Mormon)

## Bio

William Chamberlain (often spelled Chamberlin) earned an MA in philosophy from the University of Chicago with further graduate training at Harvard, where he studied under William James. [[Benjamin Park]] calls him "probably the first academic theologian/philosopher Mormonism produced" — a credentialed thinker rather than an amateur theologian in the mold of B.H. Roberts. He was one of the four professors in the [[The 1911 BYU Evolution Controversy and the Firing of Four Professors|1911 controversy]]; alone among them he was not given the ultimatum (for reasons Park leaves for a future video), but his ideas were soon judged unwelcome and he left. He landed at the Utah State Agricultural College (later Utah State University) and died of the flu in 1921. Park notes BYU once tried to rehire him, unsuccessfully. James McLachlan's *Dialogue* article "William H. Chamberlain and the Quest for Mormon Theology" treats his thought. He is closely associated with his brother [[Ralph Chamberlain]]. [^1]
