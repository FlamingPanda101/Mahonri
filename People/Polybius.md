# Polybius (c. 200–118 BCE)

**Role:** Greek historian; author of *The Histories*
**Era:** 2nd century BCE
**Tradition:** Greco-Roman historiography

## Bio
Polybius was a Greek historian best known for *The Histories*, his account of Rome's rise to Mediterranean dominance.

## Narrators Shifting Between He / We / I
[[Hugo Mendez]] cites Polybius (alongside [[Josephus]]) as an example of an ancient writer who moves between third-person ("he"), first-person-plural ("we"), and first-person-singular ("I") when speaking of himself as narrator — undercutting the argument that the "we" of John 21:24 requires a separate group of editors. See [[The We of John 21-24 Single Author Explanation]] [^1].
