# Stephen

**Role:** Deacon, first Christian martyr (protomartyr)
**Era:** Apostolic age (1st century CE)
**Tradition:** Biblical / New Testament

## Bio

Stephen was one of the seven men chosen to serve the early Jerusalem church (Acts 6) and is remembered as the first Christian martyr. Accused of blasphemy, he delivered a sweeping speech rehearsing Israel's history (Acts 7) and was stoned to death; the future apostle Paul (then Saul) consented to his killing. At his death he beheld a heavenly vision of God and the exalted Christ.

## Relevance to the vault

Stephen's dying vision in [[Acts 7.55]] is a load-bearing proof text for the vault's Godhead model. As he is stoned, he sees "two distinct beings" — the Son of Man *standing* at the right hand of God who is *seated* — with the Holy Ghost present as the power filling him. The vault uses this to argue the plain text shows distinct divine persons in a relational hierarchy rather than one indivisible substance, contrasting with Trinitarian readings.
