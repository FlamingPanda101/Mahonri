# Wes Huff

**Role:** Christian apologist
**Tradition:** Evangelical Christianity
**Era:** Contemporary (active 2020s)

## Bio

Wes Huff is a Christian apologist active on social media (Twitter/X) who creates content defending traditional Christian doctrines. He is known for popular-level apologetics that draw on ancient sources (biblical, patristic, and ancient Near Eastern) to argue for the antiquity and scriptural basis of Christian doctrines. His approach represents a common apologetic methodology of citing ancient texts as witnesses to doctrines without full engagement with the scholarly literature on those texts.

## Creation Ex Nihilo Debate

Huff posted a thread on X/Twitter arguing that creation ex nihilo is NOT a later Christian invention but is found in ancient Jewish and Christian sources, including:

- The Ebla archives (which he misidentified as "EA 250" — see [[Wes Huff Misidentifies EA250 as Ebla Text]])
- 2 Maccabees 7:28 — see [[2 Maccabees 7.28 Does Not Teach Creation Ex Nihilo]]
- Philo of Alexandria — see [[Philo Did Not Endorse Creation Ex Nihilo]]
- The Shepherd of Hermas
- Tatian and Theophilus of Antioch

Dan McClellan's response (see [[Dan McClellan - Wes Huff Creation Ex Nihilo Response]]) systematically critiques each claim, arguing Huff relies on outdated apologetic translations (Pettinato via Geisler — see [[Pettinato Ebla Translation Unreliable]]), ignores the scholarly consensus, and reads texts through the normative lens imposed by the very doctrinal development he denies.

McClellan's diagnostic: "Wes is just pulling uncritically from more than 50-year-old translations that have been widely panned and widely criticized by scholars as overly apologetic and as trying to read more significance into these texts than we really can. In other words, Wes is doing what Wes does."[^1]
