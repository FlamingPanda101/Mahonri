# Shad Brooks

**Role:** YouTuber (Shadiversity, Knight's Watch), fantasy author, and Latter-day Saint lay commentator
**Era:** Contemporary (active 2010s–2020s)
**Tradition:** The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (LDS / Mormon)

## Bio

Shad Brooks is an Australian content creator best known for **Shadiversity**, a YouTube channel on swords, castles, fantasy worldbuilding, and medieval arms and armor, and for **Knight's Watch**, where he discusses culture and media. He is also a fantasy author. A practicing Latter-day Saint, he has served in callings he describes as among his favorites — gospel-doctrine and Sunday-school teacher, seminary teacher, and, as a missionary, branch president. He notes his public YouTube presence "occupies a different role" (geeky, pop-culture content) and that Ward Radio is the "outlet" he had wanted for "deep gospel discussions."[^1]

## The Nature of Truth

Shad's central contribution in this conversation is a theory of truth grounded in **what truth causes**: truth is "far more than simply having correct facts," because correct facts can be arranged to deceive while a fiction (the [[Luke 10.30-37|Good Samaritan]]) can teach profound truth. His working formula — "truth is anything that makes you behave more like God; falsehood is anything that makes you behave less like him" — drives a chain from objective truth to objective morality to action. ## Revelation and Accommodation

He argues God deliberately **frames truth pedagogically** — figurative language, "milk before meat," and suffering imperfect practices (the law of Moses, slavery, tithing) — because behavior change matters more than bare facts, citing [[D&C 19]], [[D&C 29]], and [[Alma 29.8|Alma 29:8]]. He treats prophetic fallibility (including his own mission failings and [[Brigham Young]]'s "far out views") as something God providentially uses. See [[God Frames Truth Pedagogically to Change Behavior]] and [[Prophets Are Fallible Not Divine]].
