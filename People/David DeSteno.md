# David DeSteno

David DeSteno is an American social psychologist, professor of psychology at Northeastern University, and author of *How God Works: The Science Behind the Benefits of Religion*. He is the interlocutor in the [[Andrew Huberman]] conversation excerpted by [[Ether's Elephant]], and several of the video's key claims are his: that the question "does God exist" is "one science can't answer," that God's existence is not falsifiable because "you can't manipulate something about the mind of God," and that the measurable health and well-being benefits of religious community are larger than those of secular community ("the effect size is larger for religious community").

Ether's Elephant marshals DeSteno's points to argue that science is the wrong instrument for proving or disproving God, while the documented benefits of religious *practice* (not mere belief) support an experiential, revelation-based path to faith — "faith without works is dead."
