# Richard Bauckham

**Full name:** Richard John Bauckham
**Role:** New Testament scholar and theologian; Emeritus Professor, University of St Andrews; FBA
**Era:** Contemporary (b. 1946)
**Tradition:** Anglican; academic New Testament studies

## Bio

Richard Bauckham is a leading New Testament scholar known for *Jesus and the Eyewitnesses* and, especially relevant here, *God Crucified* / *Jesus and the God of Israel*, where he develops the influential category of the **"divine identity."** On Bauckham's thesis, the earliest Christians included Jesus within the unique identity of the one God of Israel — not by adding a second god, but by recognizing Jesus as sharing in what is distinctively true of YHWH (creator and sovereign, recipient of worship) — a "Christological monotheism" present in the earliest sources.

## The Divine Identity of Jesus

[[Gavin Ortlund]] invokes Bauckham's language to read [[Philippians 2.9-11|Philippians 2:9-11]]: Christians "in the 50s AD" were already "regarding [Jesus] as participating in the identity of God." [^1] Ortlund uses this to argue that high devotion to Jesus as divine is early and pre-conciliar, not a late construction. See:
