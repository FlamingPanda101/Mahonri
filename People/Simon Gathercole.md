# Simon Gathercole

**Role:** Professor of New Testament and Early Christianity, University of Cambridge
**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** Academic / evangelical NT scholarship

## Bio

Simon Gathercole is a Cambridge New Testament scholar cited by [[Michael Jones]] (Inspiring Philosophy) for the point that the Gospels' **internal anonymity reflects the genre conventions of ancient biography**, not later fabrication. Gathercole observes that "just about every biography from the ancient world was internally anonymous" — authors were identified by a title, a fly-leaf, or a tag on the scroll, not by naming themselves in the body. The few internally-named ancient *bioi* he found include a *fictional* biography (the life of Aelius in the *Historia Augusta*), so internal self-naming is not even a reliable marker of historicity. Plutarch, for instance, never names himself in any of the *Lives*.[^1]

This undercuts [[Bart Ehrman]]'s argument that the Gospels' anonymity counts against traditional authorship. See:
- [[Internal Anonymity Was the Convention of Ancient Biography]]
