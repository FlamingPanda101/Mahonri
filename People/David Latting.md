# David Latting

**Era:** Contemporary (2020s)
**Tradition:** Evangelical / Protestant (with stated affinity for Eastern Orthodoxy)
**Role:** Social-media critic of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints

## Bio

David Latting is an Evangelical content creator with roughly 298,000 followers who produces short-form Instagram videos and skits critiquing the LDS Church (and Islam), interspersed with "holy envy" content about Eastern Christianity and Orthodoxy.[^1] The [[Ward Radio]] panel reacts to his viral reel attacking newcomer apologist [[Maddy Packer]] within three weeks of her starting an account, and uses his specific claims as the foils for the episode. The panel reports that Latting had privately apologized to them after the Charlie Kirk events for "not letting [them] define what the beliefs were," but then resumed the same pattern — and that he "punched down" on Packer (298K followers vs. her ~522) instead of engaging her argument.[^2]

## Claims the Panel Rebuts

### "Mormons lack critical thinking" / condescension
Latting opens his reel by saying LDS discipleship-by-praxis "is just further evidence of the [lack of] critical thinking… found within the LDS church," which the panel treats as **delegitimization** rather than engagement — he never addresses [[Maddy Packer]]'s actual argument.[^3] See [[The Three Ds Rubric Diagnoses Anti-Mormonism as Bigotry]].

### "They've redefined *eternal*"
Latting argues LDS changed "eternal" from *self-existence* (timeless, uncreated) to mere *duration*, blurring the creator/creation distinction. The panel answers that the ante-Nicene fathers ([[Eusebius of Caesarea|Eusebius]], [[Origen]], [[Justin Martyr]]) themselves reserved self-existence (*autotheos*) for the Father and called the Son co-eternal-but-begotten — so the LDS view tracks early Christianity, and Latting's own tradition denies the Son is *autotheos*.[^4] See [[Autotheos Collides With Eternal Generation]], [[Subordinationism Was the Early Church View]], [[No Diminished Divinity Despite Subordination]].

### Smuggled *creatio ex nihilo*
Latting presupposes creation *ex nihilo* and a Hellenistic "one God" as the biblical baseline. The panel (Luke Hansen) replies that ex nihilo is a late-2nd-century invention read back into the text, not the Hebrew view.[^5] See [[Creation Ex Nihilo Originated Late 2nd Century CE]], [[Ex Nihilo Is a Christian Invention Not Greek Hellenization]].

### "You worship a completely different Jesus"
Latting's conclusion — "we're not speaking about the same person… this is why Islam and Mormonism are so parallel" — is met with the reference-vs-belief distinction: a wrong description of the Son's relation to the Father does not change the referent.[^6] See [[Same Reference Different Beliefs - You Worship the Same Jesus]] and [[Different Jesus Both Uncharitable and Precisely True]].

## Critic or Anti-Mormon?
The panel applies its [[Anti-Mormon Is Public Intentional Repeated Erosion of Faith|stimulus test]]: a legitimate critic updates when shown the scholarship (as Alex O'Connor reportedly does), whereas Latting recycles long-debunked tropes ("Jesus is the brother of Satan," "get your own planet") without engaging LDS scholars (B. H. Roberts, Blake Ostler), which the panel reads as moving him from criticism into religious bigotry — though they note his private apology and express hope for future good-faith dialogue.[^7] See [[Attacking the Restoration Only Proves the Need for It]].
