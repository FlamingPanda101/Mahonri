# Emer Harris

Emer Harris (1781–1869) was the elder brother of [[Martin Harris]] and a devout early convert to the Restoration. Sons of Nathan and Rhoda Harris, Quakers of upstate New York, both brothers became "seekers" anticipating a restored church before its 1830 organization. Emer is the direct ancestor of [[Dallin H. Oaks]]: Oaks's mother was Stella Harris Oaks, and Emer is Oaks's second great-grandfather — the genealogical hinge of the episode's argument that the 18th prophet descends from the Book of Mormon Harris witnesses. See [[The Harris Bloodline Connects the First Revelation to the 18th Prophet]].

## Conversion by the "Square" of the Bible
In remarks at the April 6, 1856 Provo stake conference — minutes [[Don Bradley]] found at the Church archives and once emailed to Oaks — Emer recounts a deliberate, comparative conversion: a former Baptist and "mechanic," he "tried things by the square," making a concordance to the Book of Mormon and laying the Bible against it "like a square" until it fit. He also relays Mulekite details (four men and four women of Jerusalem's royal house) that survive only from the lost 116 pages, learned through his brother Martin, the lost-pages scribe. See [[Emer Harris Tested the Book of Mormon by the Square of the Bible]].[^1]

## Receiver of the First Printed Book of Mormon
When Martin funded the printing at the Grandin Press, the very first copy was given to Emer. Though not yet baptized, he gained a testimony, later served a mission with Martin (during which Martin was briefly jailed in Pennsylvania for preaching), declared at an October 1831 conference that he was "determined to be for God and none else," and became a Utah pioneer.[^2]
