# George Brimhall

**Role:** President of Brigham Young University during the 1911 evolution controversy
**Era:** Early 20th century
**Tradition:** The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (LDS / Mormon)

## Bio

George Brimhall presided over Brigham Young University as Brigham Young Academy transformed into BYU (1903). [[Benjamin Park]] describes him as anxious to make BYU a publicly respected school — recruiting the higher-degree academics, including [[Ralph Chamberlain]] and the Peterson brothers, and giving them freedom to teach. When the [[The 1911 BYU Evolution Controversy and the Firing of Four Professors|1911 evolution controversy]] erupted, Brimhall sat on the investigating committee with five apostles and Superintendent [[Horace Cummings]]. After the firings, he called a student assembly, chastised the students who had petitioned for the professors, and announced the slogan **"the school follows the church"** — crystallizing BYU's subordination to ecclesiastical authority (see [[The School Follows the Church - Brimhall's Doctrine of Institutional Subordination]]).
