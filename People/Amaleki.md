# Amaleki

**Role:** Final record-keeper of the small plates in the book of Omni
**Era:** Nephite history (~130 BC, narrative setting)
**Tradition:** Book of Mormon figure (LDS scripture)

## Bio
Amaleki, son of [[Abadom]], wrote the closing verses of the book of Omni and delivered the small plates to [[King Benjamin]] because he had no children of his own (Omni 1:23-30). He records that he "had a brother who also went with them" — the people of [[Zeniff]] who returned to the land of Nephi — "and I have not since known concerning them" ([[Omni 1.30|Omni 1:30]]).

## Significance in the Abinadi reconstruction
The *Key to the Keystone* argument hangs on Amaleki's genealogy: his father is named **Abinadom**/[[Abadom]], a name nearly identical to **Abinadi**. The reconstruction proposes that Amaleki's vanished brother is the father of [[Abinadi]] (or Abinadi himself), placing the prophet in the record-keeping line that carried the brass plates — which would explain how Abinadi could quote Isaiah (including an apocryphal Isaiah) by heart.
