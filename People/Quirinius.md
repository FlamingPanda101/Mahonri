# Quirinius (Publius Sulpicius Quirinius)

**Who:** Roman governor (legate) of Syria beginning in 6 CE. Oversaw the census of Judea after Herod Archelaus was deposed and Rome assumed direct rule of the region.[^1]

## The Census Problem in Luke

[[Luke 2.2-3|Luke 2:2]] identifies Quirinius as governor of Syria at the time of Jesus's birth census. However, Quirinius's governorship began in 6 CE — approximately a full decade after the death of Herod the Great (~4 BCE). Luke 1 places Jesus's birth during Herod's reign. This creates an irreconcilable chronological contradiction.[^1]

## Apologetic "Prote" Argument

Some apologists argue the Greek word _prote_ in Luke 2:2 means "before" rather than "first" — rendering the passage: "This was the registration _before_ Quirinius began to govern in Syria." McClellan notes that for _prote_ to carry this meaning, it requires a genitive noun/participle immediately following, which is not the construction found in Luke 2:2. He characterizes this reading as "clearly just apologetic in origin" and "not a plausible reading" per scholarly consensus.[^2]
