# Werner Heisenberg

**Full Name:** Werner Karl Heisenberg
**Era:** 1901–1976
**Field:** Theoretical physics (quantum mechanics)
**Affiliation:** University of Leipzig; Kaiser Wilhelm Institute

## Bio

Werner Heisenberg was a German physicist and one of the principal architects of
quantum mechanics. He formulated matrix mechanics and the **uncertainty
principle**, and received the 1932 Nobel Prize in Physics.

## Topics

### The Uncertainty Principle and Free Will
[[Inspiring Philosophy|Michael Jones]] presents Heisenberg's uncertainty
principle as the historical turning point against determinism: position and
momentum cannot both be fixed, so the electron "could be here, here, or many
places simultaneously," and "no one can determine your future events given your
past history."[^1] Jones argues this leaves open "some kind of free will" by
removing the in-principle predictability that classical determinism required.
