# Thomas Aquinas

13th-century Catholic theologian and Doctor of the Church whose writing on
the saints' enjoyment of the sufferings of the damned is cited as a morally
appalling rationalization of hell.

## Key Quote (on Hell and the Saints)

> "In order that the happiness of the saints may be more delightful to them
> and that they may render more copious thanks to God for it, they are allowed
> to see perfectly the sufferings of the damned." [^1]

This passage (from the *Summa Theologica*) is critiqued in [[Hell - For Gods
Glory Defense Rebutted]] as representing an extreme version of the "for God's
glory" defense — one in which the saved derive *greater happiness* from
witnessing eternal torture.
