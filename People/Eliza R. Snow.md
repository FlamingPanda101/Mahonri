# Eliza R. Snow (1804-1887)

LDS poet and hymn-writer, General President of the **Relief Society** (the church's highest-ranking woman), and plural wife of both [[Joseph Smith]] and, after his death, [[Brigham Young]]. Cited in this video as evidence of how the general membership did — and did not — receive Adam-God.

## Adam-God in her writing
Snow's record is **mixed**, illustrating Hansen's "which Brigham Young are we going to believe?" point. She penned the hymn line "The God who talked with Adam face to face is speaking now in these latter days," and in *The Women of Mormonism* called Young's proclamation "that Adam was our Father and God" "the most important revelation ever oracled to the race." Yet across 1856-1872 she repeatedly wrote of "God our Father" and Adam as **distinct** beings. [[Luke Hansen]] uses her to show the doctrine was neither uniformly believed nor top-down enforced. See [[Cracking Down on Public Dissent Not Private Belief]] [^1].
