# Jeff Durbin

**Era:** Contemporary (2010s–2020s)
**Tradition:** Reformed Baptist
**Role:** Pastor, presuppositional apologist

## Bio
Jeff Durbin is the lead pastor of **Apologia Church** in Tempe, Arizona, and a prominent presuppositional street apologist (often active at events like Temple Lights and the Easter pageant). He is known for the [[Greg Bahnsen|Bahnsenian]] "by what standard?" challenge and for aggressive engagement with Latter-day Saints. He is a recurring foil in [[Hayden Carroll]]'s content.

## Topics

### Sola Scriptura "By What Standard"
Carroll plays a clip of Durbin asserting **sola scriptura** is "a doctrine based on revelation," then challenges him: where did God publicly identify the 66-book canon and declare it infallible? Carroll argues Durbin's claim is self-defeating — if revelation reveals sola scriptura, that revealing source is a higher authority than scripture. See [[The Three Unjustified Protestant Canon Presuppositions]], [[Theopneustos Means Life-Giving Not Infallible]].[^1]

### The Final Interpreter Problem
Carroll recounts that when Apologia members claimed "scripture interprets scripture," a Calvinist named Brad conceded that in practice "Jeff Durbin gets the final say — it's his church." Carroll uses this to expose a *de facto* human magisterium behind sola scriptura. See [[No Final Interpreter - The Protestant Adjudication Problem]].[^2]

### Hard Monotheism (Isaiah 43:10)
Carroll names Durbin's favorite verse as [[Isaiah 43.10|Isaiah 43:10]] ("before me there was no God formed") and argues Durbin reads later hard monotheism back into a text whose author held monolatry. See [[Deuteronomy 32 Dead Sea Scrolls Monolatry]], [[Subordinationism Was the Early Church View]].[^3]
