# Miranda Wilcox

**Role:** Associate Professor of English, Brigham Young University
**Field:** Early medieval religious culture in Western Europe; LDS historical consciousness
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint scholarship

## Credentials

Miranda Wilcox holds an MMS and PhD in Medieval Studies from the University of Notre Dame. "Her research focuses on early medieval religious culture in Western Europe, particularly the practices of professing faith and versifying scriptural narratives. She also writes about Latter-day Saint historical consciousness and identity formation." She co-edits the *Living Faith* book series published by the Maxwell Institute. [^1]

**Why cited / authority basis:** A credentialed medievalist synthesizing primary medieval sources (Bede, Ælfric, Francis and Clare of Assisi, Julian of Norwich, liturgical and dramatic texts). Authority is *B* (scholarly secondary), not *A*, because she interprets rather than witnesses the medieval evidence.

## Contribution

Wilcox wrote the Afterword, "Medieval Christians," in *Ancient Christians: An Introduction for Latter-day Saints* (Maxwell Institute, 2022). Her central argument reframes the Latter-day Saint apostasy narrative: rather than "perceiving medieval Christians as deluded by apostasy, we should revere them for keeping the light of Christ alive." She organizes the chapter around the [[Medieval Christians Translated Christ into Vernacular Culture|Incarnation]], [[Medieval Christians Imitated Christ Through Diverse Religious Vocations|Ministry]], [[Medieval Affective Devotion to the Suffering Christ|Passion]], and [[Medieval Liturgy and Drama Enacted Divine Presence|Resurrection]] of Christ as lived by medieval believers. Her related scholarship includes "Narrating Religious Heritage: Apostasy and Restoration" (*BYU Studies Quarterly*, 2021).
