# Enoch

Antediluvian patriarch who "walked with God" and "was not, for God took him" ([[Genesis 5.18-24|Genesis 5:24]]); in LDS scripture the prophet-founder of the city of Zion and head of the second dispensation ([[Moses 6]], [[Moses 7]]). The figure expanded at length in [[1 Enoch|the Book of Enoch]].

## Calling that opens with divine sonship
[[Don Bradley]] lists Enoch's prophetic call as an instance of the recurring dispensation-opening formula: "And he heard a voice from heaven saying, Enoch, my son, prophesy unto this people" (Moses 6:27) — God declaring relationship at the head of a dispensation. See [[Every Dispensation Opens With God Declaring Divine Sonship]].[^1]
