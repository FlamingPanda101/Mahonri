# Margaret Barker

**Era:** Contemporary (b. 1944)
**Tradition:** British Methodist (Methodist preacher)
**Role:** Independent biblical scholar; founder of "Temple Theology"

## Bio
Margaret Barker is a British Methodist biblical scholar and former president of the Society for Old Testament Study, known for her "Temple Theology" project reconstructing an older, pre-Deuteronomistic Israelite religion. [[Alex Arnold]] cites her as a non-LDS, non-evangelical authority.[^1]

## On the Suppressed Older Religion
Barker's thesis — that a first-temple religion featuring the divine council, a heavenly anointed one, and direct vision of God was edited out during the Deuteronomistic reform and exile — undergirds the argument that the Book of Mormon preserves pre-reform theology. See [[Deuteronomistic Reform Explains the Book of Mormon Theology]] and [[Unconscious Absorption Cannot Explain Suppressed Content]].[^1][^2] Note that her reconstruction is influential within LDS apologetics but remains a minority position in mainstream biblical studies.

## On the Endowment and First-Temple Ritual
[[Alex Arnold]] makes Barker the load-bearing independent witness for the temple argument: her reconstruction of First-Temple ritual theology — sacred white-linen garments, the high priest's transformation on Yom Kippur, anointing and veil ceremonies, deification, and divine-council cosmology — was developed from the Dead Sea Scrolls and ancient Near Eastern texts inaccessible to Joseph Smith, yet matches the endowment element-by-element. Because she reconstructed the ancient form *without* starting from LDS practice, Arnold argues the parallel cannot be circular. See [[The First Temple Contained the Endowment Pattern Barker Reconstructed Independently]] and [[The Direction of the Evidence Runs Opposite to How Fraud Behaves]] [^3].
