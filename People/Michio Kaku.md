# Michio Kaku

**Full Name:** Michio Kaku
**Era:** 1947–present
**Field:** Theoretical physics (string theory); science communication
**Affiliation:** City College of New York

## Bio

Michio Kaku is an American theoretical physicist, a co-founder of string field
theory, and one of the best-known popular-science communicators of his
generation.

## Topics

### Newtonian Determinism and the Uncertainty Escape
[[Inspiring Philosophy|Michael Jones]] quotes Kaku to *state* the determinist
picture before answering it: "Newtonian determinism says that the universe is a
clock, a gigantic clock that's wound up at the beginning of time and it's been
ticking ever since according to Newton's laws of motion. So what you're going to
eat 10 years from now on January 1st has already been fixed."[^1] Kaku then
notes that the **Heisenberg uncertainty principle** overturned this: "There's
uncertainty. You don't know where the electron is... God does play dice."[^2]
Jones uses Kaku's own summary as the bridge from classical determinism to a
quantum indeterminism friendly to free will.
