# Jonathan Stapley

Latter-day Saint historian whose research on the history of LDS liturgy, sealing, healing rituals, and priesthood underlies the catalog of discontinued temple practices in [[Keystone - Top 10 Abandoned LDS Temple Practices]]. Keystone credits him as indispensable: "If you've learned something new in this video, you probably have historian Jonathan Stapley to thank for it, whose work was indispensable for this," and points viewers to a companion interview with him on the temple.[^1]

His documented examples — children's proxy baptisms, baptisms and blessings of healing (including by women), prayer circles outside the temple, and women sealed to multiple living men — supply the concrete, datable backbone for the apologetic argument that temple change is historically routine. See [[Ten Abandoned Temple Practices Show Continuity Through Change]] and [[Women Were Once Sealed to Multiple Living Men]].
