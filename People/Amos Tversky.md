# Amos Tversky

**Full Name:** Amos Nathan Tversky
**Era:** 1937–1996
**Role:** Cognitive and mathematical psychologist

## Bio

Amos Tversky was an Israeli cognitive psychologist whose collaboration with [[Daniel Kahneman]] established the heuristics-and-biases research program underlying behavioral economics.

## Cited in this dialogue

[[Sam Harris]] pairs Tversky with [[Daniel Kahneman]] as the source of evidence that human moral heuristics yield contradictory outcomes under reframing and therefore need correction. [[Jordan Peterson]] counters that the heuristics remain generally reliable. See [[Empathy Does Not Scale - The Identifiable Victim Effect]].[^1]
