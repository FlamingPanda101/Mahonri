# Kyle R. Greenwood

**Role:** Academic dean (MA in Organizational Leadership), Development Associates International; associated faculty, Denver Seminary and Fuller Theological Seminary
**Field:** Hebrew Bible; history of biblical interpretation; scripture and cosmology
**Tradition:** Evangelical / broadly Christian biblical scholarship

## Credentials

Kyle R. Greenwood holds a PhD from Hebrew Union College. He is the author of *Scripture and Cosmology: Reading the Bible between the Ancient World and Modern Science* and editor of *Since the Beginning: Interpreting Genesis 1 and 2 through the Ages*. He participated in Brigham Young University's Reconciling Evolution workshop, the basis for the volume *The Restored Gospel of Jesus Christ and Evolution*. [^1]

**Why cited / authority basis:** A credentialed scholar of the Hebrew Bible and of the history of biblical interpretation. Authority is *B* (scholarly secondary), not *A*, because he interprets rather than witnesses the ancient evidence.

## Contribution

Greenwood wrote "When Worlds Collide: Scripture and Cosmology in Historical Perspective," tracing how biblical interpreters repeatedly renegotiated their reading of scripture when confronted with new cosmologies — from the three-tiered ancient Near Eastern cosmos, through Aristotelian geocentrism, to the Copernican revolution. His historical case study argues that [[Scripture Reflects Ancient Cosmology Not Modern Science|scripture assumes an ancient cosmology]] and that interpretation has always adapted to compelling external evidence, so the science-evolution "collision" can be constructive rather than destructive.
