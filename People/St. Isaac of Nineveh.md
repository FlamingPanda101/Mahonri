# St. Isaac of Nineveh

7th-century Syriac Christian bishop, monk, and theologian of the Church of the
East, renowned for his teachings on **divine compassion**. Quoted in arguments
against the compatibility of eternal conscious torment with a loving creator.

## Key Quote

> "It is not the way of a compassionate maker to create rational beings in
> order to deliver them over mercilessly to unending affliction and
> punishment — for things of which he knew even before they were fashioned,
> aware of how they would turn out when he created them, and who nonetheless
> he created." [^1]

This quote appears in [[Problem of Hell and Creation]] as early patristic
support for the claim that God cannot both be compassionate and create beings
he knows will suffer eternally.
