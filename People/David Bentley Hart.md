# David Bentley Hart

Eastern Orthodox theologian and philosopher, known for his **universalist**
critique of the doctrine of eternal conscious torment. His work bridges
patristic theology (especially Gregory of Nissa) with contemporary
philosophical argument.

## Key Arguments (as cited in the video)

### On the Injustice of Eternal Punishment

> "Retribution consisting in unending suffering imposed as recompense for the
> actions of a finite intellect and will must be by any sound definition
> disproportionate, unjust, and at the last nothing more than an expression
> of sheer pointless cruelty. Why are the actions of a finite being with
> finite consequences being punished eternally?" [^1]

### On Creation Ex Nihilo and Morality

Hart argues that the doctrine of creation from nothing carries **moral**
implications:

> "The moral destiny of all creation and the moral nature of God are
> absolutely inseparable." [^2]

Because Christians must say God creates freely, his final judgment reveals
him for who he is. Therefore, Hart concludes:

> "If God created everything from nothing, if God is choosing freely to
> create and God is truly the all-loving God revealed in Christ, then an
> eternal hell could not possibly exist. These propositions cannot possibly
> be reconciled." [^2]
