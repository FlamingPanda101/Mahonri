# Daniel Dennett

**Full Name:** Daniel Clement Dennett III
**Era:** 1942–2024
**Tradition:** Atheist (one of the "Four Horsemen" of New Atheism)
**Role:** Philosopher of mind, cognitive scientist

## Bio

Daniel Dennett was an American philosopher and cognitive scientist. He was the
co-director of the Center for Cognitive Studies and the Austin B. Fletcher
Professor of Philosophy at Tufts University. His major works include
*Consciousness Explained* (1991), *Darwin's Dangerous Idea* (1995), and
*Breaking the Spell* (2006).

Dennett was one of the "Four Horsemen" of New Atheism alongside [[Sam Harris]],
[[Richard Dawkins]], and [[Christopher Hitchens]].

## Topics

### Consciousness as Illusion
Dennett is cited in the video for his controversial position that consciousness
is an illusion — a view that [[Hayden Carroll]] presents as the logical
conclusion of materialist atheism:

> "Daniel Dennett says consciousness is an illusion... because consciousness,
> true consciousness, does not come from something that's not conscious."[^1]

Carroll's argument: if the Big Bang was not conscious, and non-conscious things
cannot produce consciousness, then materialist atheism cannot account for
consciousness. Dennett's eliminativism about consciousness is presented as the
honest, consistent atheist response — denying consciousness exists rather than
claiming it emerges from non-conscious matter.[^1]

Atheists who reject Dennett's eliminativism are, on Carroll's view,
inconsistent: they want consciousness without a conscious first cause.

See: [[Atheism's Four Bad Implications]], [[Consciousness and First Cause
Problem for Atheism]].
