# John Eaton

**Full Name:** John H. Eaton (1927–2007)
**Era:** 20th century
**Tradition:** Anglican; British Old Testament scholarship
**Role:** Reader in Old Testament, University of Birmingham

## Bio
John Eaton was a British biblical scholar of the Psalms and the prophets, working in the cult-functional tradition descending from Sigmund Mowinckel and the "myth and ritual" school. His best-known works are *Kingship and the Psalms* (1976) and *Festal Drama in Deutero-Isaiah* (1979), which reconstruct an ancient Israelite autumn festival of enthronement and apply it to the interpretation of the Royal and Enthronement Psalms and of Isaiah 40–55.

## On the Festal Drama and the King
Eaton argued that behind Isaiah 40–55 lies a ritual **festal drama** of the pre-exilic autumn New Year festival, in which the Davidic king ritually fought cosmic chaos, suffered, descended, was redeemed, married, anointed, and enthroned as Yahweh's representative — and that the Servant Songs portray *the king of the festival* rather than a single historical individual. He held that "Second Isaiah" was not one author but a **school of prophets** transmitting and finally textualizing an essentially oral tradition. [[Mike Day]] and [[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)]] use Eaton to argue the Book of Mormon could preserve Deutero-Isaianic material as old oral tradition.
