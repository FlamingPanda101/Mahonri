# Muhammad Hijab

**Role:** British Muslim apologist, debater, *da'wah* figure
**Era:** Contemporary (active 2010s–2020s)
**Tradition:** Sunni Islam

## Bio
Muhammad Hijab is a high-profile British Muslim apologist known for combative online debates with Christian, atheist, and ex-Muslim interlocutors. In this *Capturing Christianity* stream he functions as the **off-stage antagonist**: the hosts say the broadcast was conceived only after Hijab declined to debate [[Avery (God Logic)|Avery]] on a neutral platform on [[The Islamic Dilemma]]. According to [[Cameron Bertuzzi]], Hijab originally agreed to a co-stream (Bertuzzi moderating, on Bertuzzi's channel) but then changed the terms — demanding a Christian opponent with 500,000+ subscribers, accusing Bertuzzi of being a "biased moderator," and ultimately streaming on his own channel instead.[^1] Hijab never appears on the call despite repeated on-air invitations (and a $5,000 super-chat offer to lure him on).[^2]

## Role in This Source
Hijab does not present any argument here; he is invoked as an example of *da'wah* "games" — the alleged pattern of refusing neutral-platform debate on Islamic topics while freely attacking the Trinity. Bertuzzi notes Hijab had previously debated [[William Lane Craig]] on his channel, so the "biased moderator" charge struck the hosts as a pretext.[^3] The hosts also reference a parallel Hijab livestream running concurrently with "Libby" (Flippiano / "the Orthodox Muslim").[^1] For Hijab's substantive theology of scripture, see the Muslim-side replies catalogued in [[The Guardian and Criterion Reply to the Islamic Dilemma]] and [[The Tahrif Debate Among Classical Muslim Scholars]] (positions defended by callers, not by Hijab himself).
