# Dan Vogel

Dan Vogel is an independent historian of early Mormonism, best known as the editor of the five-volume documentary collection *Early Mormon Documents*. He is described in this source as the person who "knows the early Mormon documents better than" anyone and "the foremost expert on documents pertaining to the 1820s."[^1] Although Vogel writes from a naturalistic/critical perspective on [[Joseph Smith]], his documentary scholarship is treated as authoritative even by believing historians.

## Treasure Hunting Historiography

His 1994 *Dialogue* article cataloging the locations where [[Joseph Smith]] is alleged to have dug for treasure is described as the "famous" historiographical starting point — an attempt to "actually catalog the different places where Joseph Smith dug for treasure" and thereby estimate how involved he really was.[^2] The source critiques the article for offering "no real source criticism" — it is "basically just kind of a preliminary catalog" of where people *said* digging happened, with a list of 18 places, several of which "probably never happened."[^3]
