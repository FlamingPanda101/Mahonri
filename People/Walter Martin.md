# Walter Martin

Walter Martin (1928-1989) was an evangelical apologist often called "the father of the countercult movement," best known for **The Kingdom of the Cults** and an earlier volume on Mormonism. His work shaped how generations of evangelicals categorized Latter-day Saints, Jehovah's Witnesses, Christian Science, and Scientology together as "cults." [[Kyle Beshears]] names Martin's books among the evangelical resources he read in his early twenties — and notes that the Mormonism they described did not match what LDS missionaries presented, prompting his own deeper study. [^1]
