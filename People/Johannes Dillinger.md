# Johannes Dillinger

Johannes Dillinger is a European historian cited in this source as an exemplary, disinterested authority on the history of treasure hunting. He is "a European historian, not a Latter-day Saint" who "doesn't know or care about us" and "has a whole book on treasure hunting in Europe and North America over the last half millennium."[^1] His work is recommended as "a great place to start" for understanding treasure hunting as a centuries-long Western cultural phenomenon rather than a peculiarly Mormon oddity.

## Treasure Hunting as a Cultural Phenomenon

Dillinger's scholarship supports the source's central contextual claim: that [[Joseph Smith]]'s treasure seeking sits inside a long Western tradition stretching back through European folk practice and Roman treasure law, not an isolated act of fraud.[^1] See [[Treasure Hunting as Historical Cultural Practice]].
