# Hugh W. Nibley (1910–2005)

**Era:** 20th century
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint (LDS)
**Role:** Historian of antiquity; pioneering LDS apologist

## Bio
Hugh Nibley earned a Ph.D. in ancient history from UC Berkeley and spent most of his career at BYU. He is widely regarded as the founder of modern Latter-day Saint apologetic scholarship, drawing the Book of Mormon into conversation with the ancient Near East decades before the field matured.[^1]

## Book of Mormon Names
[[Matthew Bowen]] credits Nibley as the pioneer who first proposed possible **Egyptian** etymologies for Book of Mormon names, working with the limited data available in his era and "paving some ground" for later scholars.[^1] Nibley was the first to suggest that **Alma** could mean "young man," "a sign," or other possibilities — a lead later refined by [[Paul Hoskisson]] and [[Terry Szink]]. See [[Alma Means Young Man - Wordplay on Hiddenness]]. He also connected **Pacumeni** to the Greek form *Pachomios*. See [[Pahoran Paanchi Pacumeni - Egyptian Names in One Chapter]].
