# Nathan Schluter

A non-Latter-day Saint academic cited by [[Keystone]] as an outside (and therefore non-self-interested) witness for the claim at the center of its temples argument. Writing in *Crisis* magazine, Schluter states that "the single greatest contributor to our social pathologies such as addiction, mental illness, suicide, and violent crime is the breakdown of marriage and the family."[^1]

Keystone uses Schluter's statement to establish — from a source outside the Church — that family disruption is an upstream cause of downstream social problems, which sets up the argument that the temple's family-sealing ordinance treats the disease rather than the symptoms. See [[Temples Treat Social Pathology at the Root Not the Leaves]].
