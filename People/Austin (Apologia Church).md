# Austin (Apologia Church)

## Who
Austin is a lay **member of [[Jeff Durbin]]'s Apologia Church** (Tempe, Arizona) and a Reformed **presuppositional apologist**. [[Hayden Carroll]] held an hour-long conversation with him; Durbin describes him as "somebody who's a part of our church body" — "one of his apologetic pupils."[^1] He is not a credentialed scholar and appears only in recorded apologetic exchanges.

> **Disambiguation:** this Austin is *not* the same person as the Latter-day Saint panelists [[Austin Falter]] or [[Austin Fife]], nor the British-Columbia apologist [[Brother Oz (Austin)]].

## Why he matters here
Austin is the interlocutor whose conversation with Carroll prompted Durbin's reaction video, which Carroll in turn rebuts in [[Lets Talk w Hayden Carroll - Apologia Church Apologetics Are Weak|"Apologia Church Apologetics Are Weak"]]. In the played clips Austin defends the consubstantial (one-being) nature of God by appealing to "scripture as a whole" rather than to a single proof text; Carroll characterizes this (disputed by Durbin) as cherry-picking, and presses Austin to produce any biblical author teaching consubstantiality.[^2] The exchange is the seed for Carroll's broader critique of the presuppositional method.
