# Brill Hernandez

**Era:** Contemporary (2020s)
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint (LDS) — convert (~2015)
**Role:** Media director for the FIRM Foundation / "Book of Mormon Evidence"; podcast producer

## Bio
Brill Hernandez is a Latter-day Saint convert (of Mexican and Salvadoran descent) who runs a media company and serves as media director for [[Rod Meldrum]]'s Heartland Book of Mormon project. He recounts converting about ten years ago "a total Meso guy," then "going down the rabbit hole" and weighing the Mesoamerican and Heartland models against each other before adopting the Heartland position.[^1]

## Relevance to the vault
Hernandez is Meldrum's on-air partner in the Book of Mormon geography AMA, framed as the show's "Mesoamerican heartlander." His contribution is mainly the "Hebrew traditions" argument: that as a partly-Central-American convert he looked for Hebraic ritual/architectural continuity among the Maya and Aztec and "saw nothing that correlates," whereas (he claims) the Hopewell mound-builders show "major Hebrew symbolism" documented even by the Smithsonian.[^2] See [[The Newark Earthworks Encode the Plan of Salvation]] and [[The Great Lakes Are the Book of Mormons Seas]].
