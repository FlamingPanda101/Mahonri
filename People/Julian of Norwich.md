# Julian of Norwich

**Role:** Mystic, anchoress, theologian
**Era:** c. 1343 – after 1416 CE
**Tradition:** Medieval Christianity (Norwich, England)

## Bio

[[Miranda Wilcox]] presents Julian as the chapter's exemplar of medieval mysticism. In May AD 1373, while severely ill, Julian "experienced a series of sixteen showings during which she witnessed Christ's Passion and conversed with him." [^1] Over several decades she composed two accounts of her visions (*A Revelation in Love*). By AD 1393 she had become an **anchoress**, enclosed in a small dwelling attached to a church in Norwich, advising those who sought her spiritual guidance. [^1]

## Theology of Divine Love

Julian's *Showings* center on Christ's self-giving love made manifest in his crucified face. She learned that sin "is behovely (necessary)" yet "all shall be well," that the Atonement is not an economic transaction (God "exacts no price... for nothing is owed"), and that compassion for others is "Christ in him." [^2] After decades of reflection she concluded that "love was our Lord's meaning." [^3] Wilcox writes that Julian "fashions herself as a Christian Everywoman who cultivates a personal relationship with Jesus." [^2] See [[Julian of Norwich's Showings of Divine Love]] and [[Medieval Affective Devotion to the Suffering Christ]].

> "Sin is behovely (necessary), but all shall be well, and all shall be well, and all manner of things shall be well." — Christ to Julian [^2]
