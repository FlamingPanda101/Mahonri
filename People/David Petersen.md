# David Petersen

**Full name:** David L. Petersen
**Role:** Professor of Old Testament / Hebrew Bible (Emeritus)
**Era:** Contemporary (active 1970s–present)
**Tradition:** Academic (Candler School of Theology, Emory University)

## Bio

David L. Petersen is a senior scholar of the Hebrew Bible, best known for his work on the Pentateuch and the prophetic literature. He served as the Franklin N. Parker Professor of Old Testament at Emory University's Candler School of Theology and is a past president of the Society of Biblical Literature.

## Genesis Source Criticism

Dan McClellan includes Petersen's 2025 Genesis volume (Old Testament Library) in his recommended bibliography on the formation of the creation accounts, noting that it "is also good, but it does not go into as much detail because it has to talk about the entire book of Genesis in one volume" — unlike the other recommended works that treat only Genesis 1–11.[^1] Petersen's work belongs to the mainstream tradition affirming that Genesis 1:1–2:4a (Priestly) and Genesis 2:4b–3 (non-Priestly) are two originally independent creation accounts. See [[Two Independent Creation Accounts in Genesis]].
