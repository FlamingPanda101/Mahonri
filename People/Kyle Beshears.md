# Kyle Beshears

Evangelical scholar, pastor, and author of **40 Questions about Mormonism** (Kregel Academic, 2026), part of Kregel's "40 Questions" series. Raised in Northwest Indiana, he came to Mormonism through an old family copy of the Book of Mormon and the countercult film *The God Makers*, then spent years studying the Latter-day Saint tradition "on its own terms." His PhD dissertation examined [[James Strang]]'s church after Strang's death; he has presented at the Mormon History Association and a BYU symposium and is a board member of the John Whitmer Historical Association. His self-described primary interest is Latter-day Saint *history*. He writes a Substack including the essay "What Constitutes Evangelical Anti-Mormonism." [^1][^2]

## Method: holding the tension
Beshears frames his work as occupying the center between anti-Mormon caricature and syncretism — representing Mormonism accurately even where he severely disagrees, grounded in the greatest commandment to love one's neighbor. See [[Holding the Tension Between Anti-Mormonism and Syncretism]], [[Love of Neighbor Requires Understanding]].

## On the "cult" label
He argues Mormonism is not a cult in the popular sense and that calling members "cultists" is counterproductive. See [[Mormonism Is Not a Cult]], [[Calling Someone a Cultist Is Counterproductive]], [[Cult and Evangelical Are Both Loaded Words]].

## On the Book of Mormon
He denies [[Joseph Smith]] authored it yet calls it "American Pseudepigrapha," not ancient scripture, and reads "after all we can do" as "despite all we can do." See [[Book of Mormon as American Pseudepigrapha]], [[Joseph Smith Did Not Author the Book of Mormon]], [[After All We Can Do Means In Spite of All We Can Do]], [[Could God vs Would God on the Book of Mormon]].

## On whether Mormons are Christian
He refuses a black-and-white answer, distinguishing the institution's departure from orthodoxy from individuals in "potential or actual communion with Christ." See [[Are Mormons Christian (Beshears)]], [[Different Jesus Both Uncharitable and Precisely True]].

## What he learned from Latter-day Saints
LDS communalism reshaped how he reads the New Testament. See [[Saints Not Believers - LDS Communalism Lesson]], [[Theological History vs Historical Theology]].
