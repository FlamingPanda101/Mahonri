# Zedekiah

**Era:** Reigned ~597–586 BC
**Tradition:** Old Testament; last king of Judah

## Identity

Zedekiah was the last king of Judah, installed by Nebuchadnezzar and reigning until Jerusalem's destruction in 586 BC. 2 Kings 25:7 records that his sons were slain before his eyes and he was blinded and taken to Babylon.

## Apologetic significance

The Book of Mormon claims [[Mulek]], a son of Zedekiah, survived and migrated to the New World — an apparent contradiction with 2 Kings 25:7's "all his sons." Latter-day Saint apologists note (a) the biblical text need not be exhaustive and (b) Jeremiah 38:6 preserves a "Malchiah son of *the king*" (MalkiYahu ben-hammelek) who may be the surviving son, with a corroborating bulla.
