# Ammon

**Ammon** is a prominent missionary in the Book of Mormon — a son of King Mosiah who, with his brothers, undertakes a long mission to the Lamanites (Mosiah 27-28; Alma 17-26), most famously serving King Lamoni and converting the people who become the [[Anti-Nephi-Lehi|Anti-Nephi-Lehies]]. (He is distinct from the earlier Ammon who leads the search party to the people of Limhi, Mosiah 7-8 — though the same narrative thread in Mosiah 21 frames the episode below.)

## Contribution
In the [[The Stick of Joseph - Sacred Temple Connection to Priesthood Keys|Stick of Joseph interview]], Ammon supplies a Book-of-Mormon precedent for priesthood restraint: in [[Mosiah 21.27-28|Mosiah 21:27-28]], King Limhi and many of his people "were desirous to be baptized; but there was none in the land that had authority from God. And Ammon declined doing this thing, considering himself an unworthy servant." The hosts read "unworthy" as plausibly meaning *unauthorized* — desire and faith were not enough without conferred authority. (He does baptize later, in Alma 19, once the order is in place.) See [[A Vision Does Not Confer Priesthood Authority]].
