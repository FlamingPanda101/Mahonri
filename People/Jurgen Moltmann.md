# Jurgen Moltmann

German Reformed systematic theologian (1926–2024), best known for *Theology of Hope*, *The Crucified God*, and *The Trinity and the Kingdom*. A central modern advocate of a **suffering (passible) God** and of **social trinitarianism** — the view that Father, Son, and Spirit are three distinct subjects (centers of consciousness) united in love, rather than one subject in three relations.

## Cited by Terryl Givens (Keystone)
[[Terrell Givens|Terryl Givens]] invokes Moltmann as "the only theologian of real standing" he knows who "plainly espouses social trinitarianism." Givens paraphrases the argument: the persons "clearly have to be distinct centers of consciousness, and if they're distinct centers of consciousness then they're distinct persons." Givens further reports Moltmann's worry "that maybe too much was compromised" by the way the Trinity finessed the monotheism problem, agreeing that the doctrine "create[s] more problems than [it] solve[s]."[^1] See [[Social Trinitarianism Better Fits the Distinct Persons of Scripture]].

Moltmann's passibility also stands behind the broader convergence claim that mainstream theology has migrated toward a God who genuinely suffers — see [[Passibility - Mainstream Theology Now Affirms a God Who Suffers]].
