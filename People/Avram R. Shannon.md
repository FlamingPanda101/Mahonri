# Avram R. Shannon

**Role:** Associate Professor of Ancient Scripture, Brigham Young University
**Field:** Hebrew Bible; ancient religions; Second Temple and rabbinic interpretation
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint scholarship

## Credentials

Avram R. Shannon is an associate professor of ancient scripture at Brigham Young University, with degrees from BYU, Oxford, and The Ohio State University, specializing in Hebrew and ancient religions. His research focuses on the ways communities understand and deploy scripture in their discourse and religious life. [^1]

**Why cited / authority basis:** A credentialed scholar of the Hebrew Bible and ancient Near Eastern religion writing for a Latter-day Saint readership. Authority is *B* (scholarly secondary), not *A*, because he interprets rather than witnesses the ancient evidence.

## Contribution

Shannon wrote "The Genesis Creation Account in Its Ancient Context," arguing that Genesis must be read as its ancient Israelite authors and editors understood it. He shows that the LDS Church is not committed to a literal reading of Genesis, that Genesis weaves together [[Genesis Weaves Two Creation Accounts From Edited Sources|two distinct creation accounts]] from edited prophetic sources, that its cosmology is the [[Scripture Reflects Ancient Cosmology Not Modern Science|ancient three-tiered cosmos with a solid firmament]], and that creation in Genesis 1 is fundamentally an act of [[Genesis 1 Creation Is Ordering and Separation Not Ex Nihilo|ordering and separation]] rather than creation out of nothing.
