# Stephen C. Meyer

**Role:** Philosopher of science; Director of the Discovery Institute's Center for Science and Culture; former geophysicist and college professor; leading advocate of Intelligent Design
**Era:** Contemporary (b. 1958)

## Bio

Stephen C. Meyer is a philosopher of science and prominent advocate of **Intelligent Design**, associated with the Discovery Institute, where he directs the Center for Science and Culture. He received his Ph.D. in the history and philosophy of science from the University of Cambridge, served as a geophysicist with the Atlantic Richfield Company (ARCO) from 1981–1985, and taught philosophy at Whitworth College from 1990–2002.[^bio1] His books include *Signature in the Cell: DNA and the Evidence for Intelligent Design* (HarperOne, 2009), the *New York Times* best seller *Darwin's Doubt: The Explosive Origin of Animal Life and the Case for Intelligent Design* (HarperOne, 2013), and *Return of the God Hypothesis: Three Scientific Discoveries that Reveal the Mind Behind the Universe* (HarperOne, 2021).[^bio2] His central scientific argument is that the specified, digital **information in DNA** points to an intelligent source, and that this same logic — joined to cosmological fine-tuning — implies a transcendent designer. See his essay [[The Scientific Case for Intelligent Design]]. (An LDS-channel transcript renders his name phonetically as "Steven C. Meer.")

## Information in DNA / against randomness
In the Ward Radio episode the hosts invoke Meyer's signature argument — that the coded, sequence-specific **information in DNA** points to an intelligent source rather than chance — as the parallel to their [[Infinite Monkey Theorem Against Random Chiasmus|infinite-monkey rebuttal]]: random processes cannot generate ordered information, whether in genomes or in chiastic prose [^1]. The hosts mention a prior Ward Radio interview with Meyer on *Return of the God Hypothesis* [^2].
