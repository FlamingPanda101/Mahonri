# Anthony Sweat

**Role:** Professor of Church History and Doctrine, Brigham Young University
**Era:** Contemporary (active 2020s)
**Tradition:** The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints

## Bio

Anthony Sweat is a BYU professor specializing in Church history and doctrine (and a religious artist). In a clip featured by [[Jacob Hansen]]'s Thoughtful Faith, Sweat offers a widely-cited model for reconciling "prophets won't lead us astray" with "prophets make mistakes": he distinguishes **leading astray** from **making mistakes** (his children would say their parents have made many mistakes but did not lead the family astray), and uses an **airplane analogy** — God lets his prophet-pilots fly the plane through delays and course corrections, but "he will not let the plane crash," bringing "the covenant saints into its celestial destination." He pairs this with [[Brigham Young]]'s charge not to "blindly follow."[^1]
