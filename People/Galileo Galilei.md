# Galileo Galilei

**Galileo Galilei** (1564–1642) was an Italian astronomer, physicist, and mathematician whose telescopic observations supported heliocentrism. His conflict with church authorities — culminating in his 1633 condemnation — became the emblematic clash between scriptural interpretation and natural science.

## As a Case Study in the Literalism Threshold

[[Dan McClellan]] invokes Galileo to show that the literal/figurative boundary in scripture is set by tradition and contested under scientific pressure. Galileo "said, you know, I think we might be going about this the wrong way, and had an awful lot of pushback," and the dispute required getting "pretty deep into where we're drawing the line between what is figurative and what is not." [^1] McClellan adds that a Chicago-Statement-style inerrancy "in Galileo's day" would have produced "a much different public school system today," because it gives "no wiggle room" for science to correct a literal reading. [^2] See [[The Threshold Problem - Where to Draw the Literal-Figurative Line]] and [[Chicago Statement on Inerrancy Redefines Literal]].
