# Casey Paul Griffiths

A Latter-day Saint scholar in the Religious Education department at Brigham Young University. He worked in the LDS Seminaries and Institutes program for about eleven years before joining BYU, co-hosts the *Church History Matters* podcast, and has authored and co-authored several books and articles on church history. Interviewed by [[David Snell]] on the Keystone channel about the [[King Follett Discourse]].

## On the King Follett Discourse
Griffiths treats the [[King Follett Discourse]] as the Apex of Joseph Smith's teaching but argues it is [[King Follett Is Arrangement Not New Substance|arrangement, not new substance]], drawing on [[D&C 93]] and [[Abraham 4.1|the Book of Abraham]]. He stresses the four-scribe textual situation and the amalgamated published text.

## On Doctrinal Categories
He offers a [[Four-Tier Doctrinal Taxonomy]] (core / supporting / policy / esoteric), classing human exaltation as core but [[God Was Once a Man Is Esoteric Not Core Doctrine|"God was once a man" as esoteric]], and reads [[Hinckley I Dont Know That We Teach It|Gordon B. Hinckley's 1997 answer]] as honest acknowledgment of that uncertainty.

## On Metaphysics and the Problem of Evil
He builds an argument from [[Intelligence Is Uncreated and Eternally Has Agency|D&C 93's uncreated intelligence]] to [[Co-Eternality Solves the Problem of Evil|a theodicy]] and the claim that [[Ex Nihilo Determinism Negates Moral Praise|ex nihilo creation implies determinism]], citing [[David Paulsen]] and [[Truman Madsen]].

## On Nature of God and Exaltation
He defends a [[God and Man Are the Same Species|"same species" view of God and man]] (acorn-and-oak, after [[Tad Callister]]), an [[Anthropomorphic God Grounds Heavenly Mother and Eternal Families|anthropomorphic relational God]], and argues that [[Becoming Like God Is Not Usurping God|exaltation is not usurping God]].

## On Apostasy and Canon
He limits the [[Son of Perdition Requires the Highest Bar of Knowledge|"son of perdition" category]] to an extreme few and explains why [[King Follett Need Not Be Canonized|the discourse need not be canonized]].
