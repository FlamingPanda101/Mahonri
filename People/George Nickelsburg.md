# George Nickelsburg

George W.E. Nickelsburg (1934–) is an American biblical scholar and emeritus professor of religion at the University of Iowa. He is one of the foremost authorities on Second Temple Jewish literature, particularly 1 Enoch and apocalyptic texts.

## 1 Enoch Commentary

Nickelsburg, together with [[James VanderKam]], authored the definitive two-volume Hermeneia commentary on 1 Enoch:

- **Volume 1 (2001):** 1 Enoch 1–36, 81–108 (Book of Watchers, Epistle of Enoch, Birth of Noah, Final Book)
- **Volume 2 (2012):** 1 Enoch 37–82 (Book of Parables, Book of Luminaries, Dream Visions)

Dan McClellan describes this as the go-to resource: "If you want a very detailed, very technical discussion I would highly recommend that." [^1] The Hermeneia translation was also published separately as a standalone volume (~$14), making it the most academically rigorous English translation available. McClellan recommends it over the widely circulating 1906 R.H. Charles translation, describing most cheap editions as "from the 19th century or are garbage." [^2]

## Scholarly Division of 1 Enoch

Nickelsburg and VanderKam's seven-part division of 1 Enoch is the standard reference in scholarship:
1. Book of Watchers (chs. 1–36)
2. Book of Parables (chs. 37–71)
3. Book of Luminaries (chs. 72–82)
4. Dream Visions (chs. 83–90)
5. Epistle of Enoch (chs. 91–105)
6. Birth of Noah (chs. 106–107)
7. Final Book of Enoch (ch. 108)
