## Who
Benedict of Nursia wrote his *Rule* in AD 516 to organize the practices of monastic communities. The Rule became the foundational document of Western monasticism and shaped the structure of communal daily prayer.

## Authority and Reason for Citation
Benedict is cited for codifying the Liturgy of the Hours (Divine Office). His Rule prescribed eight hours for prayer — Lauds (daybreak), Prime (~6:00 a.m.), Terce (~9:00 a.m.), Sext (noon), None (~3:00 p.m.), Vespers (sunset), Compline (completion of the workday), and Midnight — a routine through which the church collectively sought to live the ideal "pray without ceasing" (1 Thessalonians 5:17). These services were obligatory for clergy and monastics but optional for laypersons.
