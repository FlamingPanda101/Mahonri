# Robert Blatchford

**Role:** English journalist and popular atheist polemicist; author of *God and My Neighbour* (1903)
**Era:** 1851–1943
**Tradition:** Atheist (and socialist)

## Bio

Robert Blatchford was an English socialist journalist best known to apologetics for his 1903 anti-Christian book *God and My Neighbour*. [[T. Benjamin Spackman]] dates his key argument to "1902 or 3" and calls him "a fundamentalist atheist... because he has very simplistic assumptions that don't even really go with his atheism."[^1]

## The "no Adam, no atonement" syllogism

Blatchford is the originator of the chain still heard today (and, Spackman notes, "on ex-Mormon Reddit"): a perfect God would inspire a perfect book and a perfect religion; the Bible and Christianity are not perfect; therefore God does not exist — extended with "evolution is real, therefore there's no Adam... no fall... no need for Jesus or atonement... we don't need churches. They're just oppressive."[^2] The argument "was answered almost immediately," both on its logic and its assumptions, by Christian writers — "pretty quickly G. K. Chesterton the Christian apologist in England is writing responses to Blatchford," as were various pastors.[^3] What proved historically consequential is that the young-earth creationist [[George McCready Price]] later borrowed Blatchford's *logic* to argue against evolution. See [[The Blatchford-Price Syllogism - No Adam No Atonement]].

## Why cited / authority basis

Cited *historically* as the source of a flawed but durable polemical syllogism, not as a scholarly authority. Authority **C**.
