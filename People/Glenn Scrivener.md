# Glenn Scrivener

**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** Christian (Anglican; evangelist)
**Role:** Director of Speak Life; author, apologist, public speaker

## Bio

Glenn Scrivener is an Australian-born, UK-based evangelist and ordained minister, director of the ministry **Speak Life**. He is the author of *The Air We Breathe: How We All Came to Believe in Freedom, Kindness, Progress, and Equality* — described in this dialogue (self-deprecatingly) as "Tom Holland light" or "Dominion for dummies," a confessionally Christian, accessible presentation of [[Tom Holland]]'s *Dominion* thesis.[^1]

## Topics

### The "Air We Breathe" Thesis
Scrivener argues that modern Western values — compassion, equality, freedom, progress, consent — are so thoroughly shaped by "20 centuries of Christianization" that they have become invisible to us, like the air we breathe. He defends the strong claim that such developments are "unthinkable without Christianity," using the image of the cross as "the stone that drops into the pond" producing "ripple effects." See [[The Dominion Thesis - Western Values Are Christianized]] and [[Unthinkable Without Christianity vs Blossoming Thesis]].[^1]

### Compassion, Hospitals, and the Cross
He grounds Western compassion in the "transvaluation of all values" at the cross — the highest figure descending to the lowest place — and credits Christianity with *institutionalizing* care (hospitals, orphan care, abolition of infanticide and gladiatorial games). See [[The Compassion Revolution of the Cross]] and [[Institutionalization Is the Unique Christian Contribution]].[^2]

### Equality from Genesis
Following [[Tom Holland]] and [[John Locke]], he traces equality and rights to the *imago Dei* of [[Genesis 1.26-27|Genesis 1:26-27]]. See [[The Christian Origin of Equality]] and [[Genesis 1 Image of God as Seedbed of Rights]].[^3]

### Narrative Reading of Scripture
Against O'Connor's proof-texting, he insists Christians read the Bible as a single story culminating in Christ crucified, taking troubling texts (Binding of Isaac, Exodus 22:29, slavery laws) "through the cross." See [[The Narrative Reading of Scripture vs Proof-Texting]].[^4]

### Falsifiability and Pagan Practices
He names abortion and euthanasia as genuinely *pagan*, non-Christian practices, and argues the falsifiability demand is illegitimate because history has no control group. See [[Abortion and Euthanasia as Pagan Not Christian]] and [[The Falsifiability Demand Against the Air-We-Breathe Thesis]].[^5]
