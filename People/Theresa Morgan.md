# Theresa Morgan

**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** Christian (Anglican priest and scholar)
**Role:** Oxford classicist and theologian; historian of Greco-Roman popular morality

## Bio

Theresa Morgan is an Oxford scholar of ancient ethics and early Christianity.[^1]

## Topics

### Roman Reciprocity Morality
[[Glenn Scrivener]] cites Morgan's observation that a common Roman aphorism was "every day do something to help your friends and harm your enemies" — the second half of which moderns recoil from. Scrivener uses this to argue Roman in-group/out-group morality made evolutionary sense, and that the cross's "transvaluation of all values" overturned it. See [[The Compassion Revolution of the Cross]].[^1]
