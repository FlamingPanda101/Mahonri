# William Clayton

Personal secretary to [[Joseph Smith]] and one of four contemporaneous scribes
who recorded the [[King Follett Discourse]] in April 1844. Clayton wrote in
longhand, providing one of the fullest accounts of the sermon.[^1]

His record, combined with [[Thomas Bullock]]'s shorthand version, formed the
primary basis for the polished version published in the *Times and Seasons* in
August 1844.[^2]
