# William Paley

**Full Name:** William Paley
**Era:** Enlightenment (1743–1805)
**Tradition:** Anglican (Church of England)
**Role:** Theologian, philosopher, apologist

## Bio

William Paley was an English clergyman and philosopher best known for his
*Natural Theology* (1802), which presented the watchmaker analogy — the
argument that just as a watch implies a watchmaker, the complexity of living
organisms implies an intelligent designer. This was the dominant design argument
of [[Joseph Smith]]'s era.

## Topics

### Biological Design Argument (Watchmaker)
Paley's argument was biological: complex organisms (the eye, for example)
exhibit design and therefore require a designer. This argument was famously
challenged by Darwin's theory of natural selection (1859). [[Hayden Carroll]]
distinguishes Paley's biological design from the cosmological fine-tuning
argument: "The physical constants, the scientific ones, the theologians didn't
know."[^1] See [[Fine-Tuning - Book of Mormon Anticipation of Modern Cosmology]] and [[Fine-Tuning and the Teleological Argument from Alma 30]].
