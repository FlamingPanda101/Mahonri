# Ugo Perego

**Era:** Contemporary (2000s–2020s)
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint (LDS)
**Role:** Population geneticist, author

## Bio

Ugo Perego is a population geneticist whose published expertise is in the genetics
of Native American populations — the precise field relevant to "DNA and the Book of
Mormon" debates. He studies human DNA to trace population movements and migrations
through large group-DNA samples. He co-authored, with [[John Butler (DNA scientist)]],
the 2025 Deseret Book volume *Let's Talk about DNA and the Book of Mormon*.[^1]

## DNA and the Book of Mormon

Rappleye presents Perego as one of the most qualified people to address the topic and
trusts his judgment that DNA does not disprove the Book of Mormon once founder effect,
genetic drift, and host-population admixture are understood. See
[[No DNA Evidence Requires Specifying What Signature to Expect]] and
[[Founder Effect and Genetic Drift Erase a Small Founding Signal]].[^2]

## Rejecting Haplogroup X2A as Evidence

Crucially, Perego (with Butler) explicitly declines to use Haplogroup X2A as evidence
*for* the Book of Mormon — it is far too ancient (~10,000 BC) and only a cousin lineage
of Near-Eastern X. Rappleye cites his refusal as a model of intellectual honesty. See
[[Haplogroup X2A Is Not Valid Evidence for the Book of Mormon]].[^3]
