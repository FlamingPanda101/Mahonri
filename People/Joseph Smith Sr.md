# Joseph Smith Sr

Joseph Smith Sr (1771–1840) was the father of [[Joseph Smith]] and patriarch of the Smith family. In this source he appears in two contexts: he accompanied his son to help [[Josiah Stowell]] dig for the legendary Spanish silver mine in 1825, per [[Lucy Mack Smith]]'s history;[^1] and he is named as the family member for whom there is the strongest evidence of divining-rod use — "probably Joseph Sr. for sure, almost for sure."[^2]

The source also notes that several of the hostile [[Hurlbut Affidavits Bias Problem|Hurlbut statements]] accused Joseph Sr of being "a total alcoholic," a charge that does not match positive testimony from others or the documented hard work the family put into developing their farm.[^3]
