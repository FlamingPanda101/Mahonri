# Fayette Lapham

**Era:** 1830s, Palmyra, New York
**Tradition:** Non-Mormon businessman; a cousin of [[Martin Harris]]'s mother

## Identity

Fayette Lapham was a young Palmyra businessman, a cousin of Martin Harris's mother, who (with a friend) interviewed [[Joseph Smith Sr]] in the 1830s about the coming forth of the Book of Mormon and the contents of its stories. He later published an account of the interview.

## Apologetic significance

[[Don Bradley]] treats the Lapham interview as "one of our best sources" on the Lost 116 Pages. In it, Joseph Sr. relays a narrative — not in the surviving Book of Mormon — of a leader (Bradley argues [[Mosiah]] I) led by the Liahona to the interpreters, told to cover his face with an animal skin to use them. Because Lapham was a non-Mormon recording it from a primary witness, Bradley uses it as independent evidence that endowment/seer-instrument patterns predate Joseph's Freemasonry. See [[The Jaredite and Nephite Interpreters Carry the Endowment Ascent Pattern]].
