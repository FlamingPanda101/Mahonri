# Mulek

**Era:** ~6th century BC (Book of Mormon)
**Tradition:** Book of Mormon figure (asserted historical son of King Zedekiah of Judah)

## Identity

Mulek is named in the Book of Mormon as a son of King [[Zedekiah]] of Judah who survived the Babylonian destruction of Jerusalem and led a second migrating colony to the New World ([[Omni 1]]; Helaman 8:21). His people, the "people of Zarahemla" (often called Mulekites), later merged with the Nephites. The Book of Mormon presents him as the founder of a distinct Davidic line out of Jerusalem alongside Lehi's family.

## Apologetic significance

- **Linguistic/archaeological vindication.** What critics long called an obvious blunder (a non-biblical surviving royal son) has gained support: Jeremiah 38:6's "Malchiah son of Hammelech" is better read *MalkiYahu ben-hammelek* — "son of *the king*" — and a clay seal reading *Malkiyahu ben hamelek* surfaced in Jerusalem. See [[Mulek Son of Zedekiah Was Vindicated by Linguistics and a Seal]].
- **Olmec correlation (Grover).** [[Jerry Grover]] argues the name "Mulek" appears in the [[The Characters Document Translated as Abbreviated Hieratic and Maya|characters document]] as a "walking fish" glyph functioning as a *vizier* title, and that La Venta monuments depicting a bearded, mature foreign arrival date (by his glyph reading) to ~531/511 BC — consistent with a Mulek arrival after the prophesied fall of the Jaredite/Olmec order. See [[Olmec Bearded Figures and an Academic Concession of Possible Transoceanic Contact]].
