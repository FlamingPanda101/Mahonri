# Richard Van Wagoner

Richard S. Van Wagoner (1946–2010) was a Latter-day Saint historian, best known for *Mormon Polygamy: A History*. [[Keystone]] cites him to puncture the sensationalized nineteenth-century image of plural marriage.

## The Harem Image Was a Creation of Outside Travelers
Van Wagoner argued that "contrary to popular 19th century notions about polygamy, the Mormon harem dominated by lascivious males with hyperactive libidos did not exist." The "image of unlimited lust was largely the creation of gentile travelers to Salt Lake City more interested in titillating audiences back home than in accurately portraying plural marriage."[^1] See [[The Scandinavian Trafficking Claim Is Sensationalized Folklore]] and the related [[Brigham Young's Polygamy Was Not a Sensual Harem]].
