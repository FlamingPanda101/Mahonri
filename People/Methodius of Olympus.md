# Methodius of Olympus

Early Christian bishop of Olympus, a town in southwest Turkey (c. AD 260–311), and a theologian known for *The Banquet of the Ten Virgins* and works on the resurrection.

## Significance
Methodius "may have been the first to use the term *Fall* to describe Adam and Eve's transgression" — a term absent from Genesis 3 and the rest of the Bible. He wrote that Adam, "by transgressing the commandment (of God) … suffered a terrible and destructive fall, being thus reduced to a state of death." He also held a positive view of human nature as soul and body together, made "after the very original likeness of the Father and the only-begotten Son." His coinage marks how late the very vocabulary of "the Fall" entered Christian theology. See [[Original Sin Was a Late Western Development]].
