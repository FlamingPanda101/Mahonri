# Keystone

A Latter-day Saint apologetics YouTube channel that produces explainer videos and point-by-point responses to popular criticisms of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. Its content falls into two recurring modes: (1) **scholar interviews**, in which the host puts questions to credentialed Latter-day Saint historians — e.g. [[Jonathan Stapley]] on temple liturgy, [[Britney Chapman Nash]] on plural marriage, [[W. Bradford Wilcox]] on family social science; and (2) **response videos** rebutting critical creators such as [[Johnny Harris]] and Mark Gagnon, or documents like the [[Jeremy Runnells|CES Letter]].

Keystone's signature method is to grant an interlocutor's facts where they are right, isolate the *framing* or *sourcing* as the real problem, and re-weight contested evidence against the fuller record. The channel is rated **tier C** (informed, cited commentary): episodes routinely cite scholarship and primary documents, but the conclusions are confessional apologetics. Interviewed scholars are rated on their own merits in their People notes.
