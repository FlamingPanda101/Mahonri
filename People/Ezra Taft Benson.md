# Ezra Taft Benson

**Era:** 20th century (1899–1994)
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint (LDS)
**Role:** Church President / prophet; former U.S. Secretary of Agriculture

## Bio

Ezra Taft Benson served as the 13th President of the Church of Jesus Christ of
Latter-day Saints (1985–1994) and earlier as U.S. Secretary of Agriculture under
President Eisenhower. He is especially associated with emphasizing the Book of
Mormon.[^1]

## The Power in the Book

Rappleye invokes Benson's teaching that "there's a power in the book if people would
only read it," giving it as the experiential ground of his own conviction: the
deciding factor is not external proof but the book's transformative power, which sets
the convergence evidence in its proper place. See
[[Convergence Not Proof Is the Standard for Book of Mormon Historicity]].[^2]
