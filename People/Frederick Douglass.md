# Frederick Douglass

**Full name:** Frederick Douglass (born Frederick Augustus Washington Bailey)
**Role:** Abolitionist, orator, statesman, memoirist
**Era:** 1818–1895
**Context:** Antebellum and post–Civil War United States

## Bio

Frederick Douglass was an American abolitionist who escaped from slavery and became one of the most influential writers and orators of the 19th century. His autobiography, *Narrative of the Life of Frederick Douglass, an American Slave* (1845), is a foundational first-person account of the brutality of antebellum chattel slavery and is treated by historians as primary documentary evidence.

## Cited as Witness to Antebellum Brutality

In the *Reformation Red Pill* episode, Joshua HS invoked Douglass's biography ("the horrors of Frederick Douglass") to contrast the alleged compassion of Old Testament slave law with the cruelty of American slavery.
