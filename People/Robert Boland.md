# Robert Boland

## Who
Robert Boland is a **Latter-day Saint apologist and independent researcher** living in Southwest Ireland. He holds degrees in **theology and anthropology** from the **Pontifical University of Ireland at Maynooth**, has worked as a **senior research analyst** for the **BH Roberts Foundation** and **Scripture Central**, and is proprietor of the **Scriptural Mormonism** blog and podcast.[^1] His stated future projects include a monograph on the development of early Latter-day Saint Christology and another on the history of the Adam-God theory.[^1]

## Why he matters here
In [[Thoughtful Faith - Sola Scriptura Debate|the Thoughtful Faith sola scriptura debate]] Boland argues the **negative** against [[Kelly (Brain Perspective)|Kelly Powers]]. His method is a deliberate **internal critique**: he **concedes Mormonism is false for the sake of argument** and places the entire burden on the Protestant to demonstrate that the 66-book canon is *formally sufficient* and the sole infallible rule of faith.[^2] His case is densely **lexical and source-driven** — marshalling BDAG, EDNT, TDNT, and a wall of Protestant, Catholic, and Lutheran scholars (citing even Calvin, Hodge, and James White against the Protestant position).

## Key arguments (linked concepts, distinctive to this debate)
- [[Profitable Is a Fractional Word Not a Claim of Sufficiency]] — his lexical reading of ōphelimos in 2 Timothy 3:16.
- [[Hezekiah and Josiah Bound a Word of God Never in the Bible]] — his 2 Chronicles 29/35 defeater.
- [[James Read Amos 9 Through the Septuagint at the Jerusalem Council]] — his Acts 15 argument.
- [[The Canon-as-Article-of-Faith Dilemma]] — his signature dilemma using Jude, 1 Enoch, and the missing Corinthian epistle.
- [[Conscience Is the Protestants True Ultimate Authority]] — his Hodge-based reframing of the Protestant rule of faith.

## Debate posture
Insistent on the **burden of proof** ("you're shifting the burden") — he repeatedly declines Powers' Mormonism-focused cross-examination questions as off-topic. His core thesis echoes the vault's existing material that [[Sola Scriptura Could Not Have Been True During the Apostolic Age|sola scriptura cannot operate during times of public revelation]] and that the very notion of *formal sufficiency* is an exegetical impossibility, condemned as a "tradition of men" (Matthew 15 / Mark 7).
