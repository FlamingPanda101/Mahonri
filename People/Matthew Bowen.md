# Matthew L. Bowen

**Era:** Contemporary (2010s–2020s)
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint (LDS)
**Role:** Scholar of ancient scripture; onomastics specialist

## Bio
Matthew L. Bowen grew up in Orem, Utah, graduated from BYU-Provo with a major in English and a minor in classical studies (Greek emphasis), then did post-baccalaureate work in Egyptian and Semitic languages before earning an M.A. and Ph.D. (defended 2014) at the Catholic University of America in Washington, D.C. He has taught at BYU-Hawaii since 2012, where he is a professor of ancient scripture.[^1]

He is the leading scholar on **onomastic wordplay (paronomasia)** in the Book of Mormon. His distinctive contribution is showing not merely that the names are attested or reconstructable in Hebrew/Egyptian, but that the narrators *knew the meanings and played on them* in the text.[^2] His books include *Name as Key-Word*, *Ancient Names in the Book of Mormon* (both Interpreter Foundation), and the chapter "Striking While the Irony Is Hot: Hebrew Onomastics and Their Function within the Book of Mormon Text" in the Routledge volume *Perspectives on Latter-day Saint Names and Naming*.[^3]

## Onomastic Wordplay Thesis
Bowen argues that names function as keywords whose meanings the ancient authors deliberately exploit. See [[Book of Mormon Names Show Authorial Wordplay]] and the level-of-evidence distinction in [[Attestation Versus Wordplay - Two Levels of Evidence]].
