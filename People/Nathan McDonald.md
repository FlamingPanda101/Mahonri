# Nathan McDonald

**Role:** Hebrew Bible / Old Testament scholar
**Affiliation:** University of Cambridge (Professor of the Hebrew Bible)
**Era:** Contemporary
**Authority:** Tier A — author of a peer-reviewed monograph that is a standard reference on the topic.

## Bio

Nathan MacDonald is a biblical scholar specializing in Deuteronomy, the development of Israelite religion, and the concept of monotheism. His monograph *Deuteronomy and the Meaning of Monotheism* (Mohr Siebeck, FAT II series) is a benchmark study arguing that the term "monotheism" — a modern, post-Enlightenment construct — maps poorly onto Deuteronomy, and that Deuteronomy's "no other" language functions to express devotional exclusivity and Yahweh's presence/proximity within his domain rather than the ontological non-existence of other gods.

## Deuteronomy and the Meaning of Monotheism

[[Dan McClellan]] cites the book as the definitive academic treatment supporting the reading that the negation rhetoric ("there is no other," "none besides me") does not denote ontological exclusivity but **proximity / presence within someone's domain / rhetorical relevance**. McClellan specifically recommends **chapter 2, section 3**, on [[Deuteronomy 4.2|Deut 4]]:35 and 39. [^1]

> "I would highly recommend Nathan McDonald's book Deuteronomy and the meaning of monotheism where he demonstrates that that kind of rhetoric is used in many different places not to refer to any kind of ontological exclusivity or the ontological non-existence but to refer to proximity or the presence of something within someone's domain." [^1]
