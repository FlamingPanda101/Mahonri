# F.C. Bauer

Ferdinand Christian Baur, the 19th-century German theologian who founded the **Tübingen school** of New Testament criticism. In the [[The Stick of Joseph - Jesuss Brother Corruption in Christianity and the Great Apostasy|Stick of Joseph episode]], [[Mike Day]] names him (as "FC Bower") as the last scholar before the modern revival to take [[James the brother of Jesus|James]] seriously — crediting Baur with the thesis that the New Testament preserves "two completely different perspectives on Christianity," a **Jamesian church** and a **Pauline church** that argued, with the Pauline side winning. Day notes Baur "lost the discussion" and the Tübingen school "was out of favor for a long time," but says its core ideas are "back" via [[Robert Eisenman]] and others. See [[Paul Invented Christianity as a Cloak for James's Nazirite Judaism]] and [[Paul vs James and the Judaizer Conflict]].
