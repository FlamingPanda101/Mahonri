# King Follett

A close associate of [[Joseph Smith]] in Nauvoo. He died on March 9, 1844, after
being part of an accident while digging a well. His death was the occasion for
Joseph Smith's most famous sermon — the [[King Follett Discourse]] — delivered at
the April 1844 general conference.[^1]

The Follett family asked Joseph Smith to deliver a funeral sermon, though various
things kept him from doing so until the general conference on April 6, 1844, where
Smith "used the occasion to expound on broader theological concepts" far beyond a
typical funeral oration.[^2]
