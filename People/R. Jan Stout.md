# R. Jan Stout

"Accredited and distinguished psychiatrist" who evaluated "Bill," the son-in-law of [[Marion Smith]] falsely accused in the ISAT satanic-ritual-abuse case. Stout "concluded that Bill was very likely not a perpetrator" — one of several independent authorities (the police, a separate Salt Lake County investigator, and a Johns Hopkins psychiatrist) who cleared Bill before he was nonetheless destroyed by the litigation and died by suicide.

## Cleared an innocent man
Stout's evaluation is part of the convergent record of exoneration that makes Bill's case the series' cleanest example of an accusation surviving its own disproof. See [[An Innocent Cleared by Every Investigator Was Still Driven to Death]]. [^1]
