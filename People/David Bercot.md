# David Bercot

**Role:** Author, editor, and popularizer of early-Christian (ante-Nicene) writings; trained as an attorney
**Tradition:** Anabaptist / early-church restorationist
**Era:** 20th–21st century
**Authority:** Tier C — influential lay author and anthologist on the early church, not an academic patrologist.

## Bio

David Bercot is best known for the book ***Will the Real Heretics Please Stand Up*** and for editing *A Dictionary of Early Christian Beliefs*, anthologies of ante-Nicene Christian writings. Working in an Anabaptist, "primitive Christianity" frame, he argues that the earliest Christians held beliefs (including on the Trinity and discipleship) that diverge from much of later mainstream Christianity, and that the creeds were not used as a salvation litmus test in the earliest centuries.

## Role in the Nicene Creed Discussion

In the indexed Ward Radio episode, the guest cites Bercot as a non-LDS authority for the early-church view of the Trinity and the original context of *homoousios*. He recommends "David Bercot, [an] Anabaptist scholar that has a great book called *Will the Real Heretics Please Stand Up*," plus accessible YouTube videos on "what the early Christians believed about the Trinity." [^1] The guest summarizes Bercot's thesis: "the early Christians basically just believed in the Nicene Creed, but they didn't believe it was a litmus test to see if you were Christian." [^2]

As with [[Beau Branson]], the appeal is cross-traditional and selective — Bercot is not Mormon and the guest concedes these scholars "would take issue with other beliefs that we had." [^3] See [[Latter-day Saints Affirm the Nicene Creed in Its 4th-Century Context]] and [[The Nicene Creed Was Left Ambiguous Unlike the Athanasian Creed]].
