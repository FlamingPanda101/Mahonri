# Andreas Wagner

**Full name:** Andreas Wagner
**Role:** Professor of Old Testament
**Era:** Contemporary (active 2000s–present)
**Tradition:** Academic (University of Bern, Switzerland)

## Bio

Andreas Wagner is professor of Old Testament at the University of Bern, Switzerland. His research focuses on the anthropomorphic depiction of God in the Hebrew Bible, prayer and ritual texts, and the intersection of biblical studies with linguistics and cognitive science.

Dan McClellan cites Wagner's *God's Body: The Anthropomorphic God in the Old Testament* — the English translation of a German original — as part of the scholarly consensus on divine corporeality. [^1] The book systematically examines the Hebrew Bible's depiction of God with human-like physical features: face, eyes, ears, mouth, hands, feet, and a body capable of spatial movement and physical interaction.

## Divine Corporeality in the Hebrew Bible

Wagner's work is significant for its systematic approach: rather than starting from the assumption that anthropomorphic language is "merely metaphorical," Wagner examines the texts on their own terms and concludes that the Hebrew Bible genuinely presents God as embodied. His linguistic analysis supports the broader scholarly consensus that divine corporeality was the baseline assumption in early Israelite religion, later overwritten by Greek philosophical categories.
