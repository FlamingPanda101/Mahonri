# John Dehlin

Founder and host of the **Mormon Stories** podcast and the central recurring figure in the [[Thoughtful Faith - Who Are the REAL Anti-Mormons|"Who Are the REAL Anti-Mormons?"]] livestream, where [[Jacob Hansen]] and [[Steven Pynakker]] disagree sharply over how to classify him. Excommunicated from the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, he is treated by Hansen as a paradigm case of an "anti-Mormon" and by Pynakker as a sincere would-be reformer. (Spelled "Dyn"/"Delin" in the auto-transcript.)

## Hansen's view: a sincere destroyer
Hansen calls Dehlin "very clearly" an anti-Mormon — his channel is "literally dedicated" to "public, intentional, repeated erosion of faith in the church's fundamental claims." [^1] Yet Hansen concedes a prior public reversal: he believes Dehlin is *sincere* and "wants to do good," trying (as Dehlin sees it) to make the church better by exposing what he thinks is "unhealthy and bad and untrue." [^2] Hansen's argument is that sincerity is beside the point — to remake the church's fundamentals is to destroy it (see [[Reforming a Thing's Essence Is Destroying It Not Reforming It]]) — and that Dehlin's stated "the church does more good than bad" is at war with a corpus that is "~1% positive" (see [[The Say-Versus-Do Disconnect in Critics]]). His excommunication is cited as the result of going outside [[Reform Through Priesthood Channels Not Public Attack|priesthood channels]].

## Pynakker's view: a reformer who platforms faith
Pynakker resists "wants to destroy the church" as "dangerous language," likening Dehlin to internal Catholic critics (Gary Wills) and to the Tanners — critics who "want to stay and reform." [^3] He credits Dehlin with good-faith outreach: a Mormon Stories episode (Dr. Christopher Thomas, *A Pentecostal Reads the Book of Mormon*) is what returned Pynakker himself to faith — "if that episode did not air... I'm probably still an atheist." [^4] Pynakker reports off-camera conversations in which ex-Mormon hosts grant the church is "a net positive," and offers to facilitate a Hansen–Dehlin (or Hansen–[[Kolby Reddish]]) dialogue.

## Confession-room accusations (Ward Radio Part 6)
Ward Radio Part 6 names Dehlin's Mormon Stories as the channel that exploits the confidentiality of LDS clergy interviews: because no one can verify "what actually happens in those confession rooms" and the bishop is oath-bound to silence, an accusation like "he asked about my underwear" is "an excellent, unverifiable, unfalsifiable accusation that [Dehlin] can lob ad infinitum." The episode frames this as a one-way street weaponizing confessional privilege against volunteer lay clergy. See [[Confessional Privilege Creates a One-Way Street for Accusations]].[^5]
