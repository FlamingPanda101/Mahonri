# Jared Lee

**Role:** Associate Professor of Biology; Division Chair, Mathematics and Natural Sciences, Southern Virginia University
**Field:** Evolutionary genetics; evolution-and-faith reconciliation
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint scientist

## Credentials
Jared Lee is an evolutionary biologist with a PhD in evolutionary genetics and a member of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. He teaches at Southern Virginia University, where most students and faculty are Latter-day Saints, and his interest lies in the intersection of evolution and faith. His research has taken him through the United States, Costa Rica, China, and the Philippines.[^1]

**Why cited / authority basis:** A credentialed evolutionary biologist writing for a Latter-day Saint readership about how he and his students reconcile evolution with faith. Authority is *B* (scholarly, on-topic, in-faith), not *A*, because he writes as an expert commentator rather than a primary witness or doctrinal authority.

## Contribution
Lee wrote "Living with Uncertainty Helps Us Reconcile Evolution and Faith." Drawing on regular conversations with undergraduates who assume he can resolve their evolution-and-faith questions, he argues that the path forward is learning to live with uncertainty rather than demanding resolution. He distinguishes sharply between doubt and uncertainty, shows that uncertainty is intrinsic to *both* faith and science, and offers three practices for living faithfully with open questions — culminating in a model of reconciliation (open dialogue) rather than resolution.[^1] He thanks Jamie Jensen and Ben Spackman for their input on the chapter.
