# Edward Partridge

The first **bishop** of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (called 1831), responsible in the early 1830s for administering the law of consecration and allotting inheritances to the Saints gathering to Zion (Jackson County, Missouri).

## Significance in the vault
Partridge is the historical referent of the "one mighty and strong" prophecy in [[D&C 85]]. Per the 1905 First Presidency statement, Partridge — then "out of order," neglecting his bishopric and "putting forth his hand to steady the ark" — was warned that if he failed, another "mighty and strong" would be sent to take his place and "fall by the shaft of death" was the threatened judgment against *him*. Because Partridge repented and became, in [[Casey Paul Griffiths|Griffiths]]'s words, "one of the most honored names in the Church" (tarred and feathered before a Missouri mob yet disarming them by his Christlike manner), the occasion for sending the "one mighty and strong" passed away. See [[The One Mighty and Strong Was Edward Partridge Not a Future Reformer]].
