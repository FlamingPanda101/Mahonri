# Patrick Mason

**Role:** Leonard J. Arrington Chair of Mormon History and Culture, Utah State University
**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (academic historian)

## Bio

Patrick Q. Mason is a leading academic historian of Mormonism. In a clip [[Jacob Hansen]] quotes *critically*, Mason argues "it doesn't take a PhD in history to know that we've not always consistently taught that marriage between a man and a woman is the only marriage ordained of God" — pointing to the 19th-century practice and theology of plural marriage as evidence that LDS marriage teaching has shifted over time.[^1]

Hansen's rebuttal is that each plural marriage was still a man-and-woman union (per [[Greg Matson]]), that the *doctrine* (monogamy norm with authorized exceptions, [[Jacob 2]]) never changed, and that polygamy therefore sets no precedent for redefining marriage. See:
- [[Polygamy Doctrine Did Not Change Only the Exaltation-Requires-Polygamy Idea Did]]
- [[Gay Marriage Is Not Analogous to the Priesthood Ban or Polygamy]]
