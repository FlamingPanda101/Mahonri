# Maximilien Robespierre

**Era:** 1758–1794 (18th century)
**Tradition:** French Revolution
**Role:** Revolutionary leader

## Bio

Leading figure of the French Revolution, associated with the guillotine and the **Reign of Terror**. He reportedly kept a small copy of [[Jean-Jacques Rousseau|Rousseau]] in his vest pocket.[^1]

## Role in the Argument

For [[Brian Thomassen]], Robespierre is the political embodiment of the **Rousseauian** logic: the general will, made manifest through the great legislator, justifies whatever the legislator deems necessary — "Subdue by terror the enemies of liberty and you will be right; the government of the revolution is liberty's despotism against tyranny." Under the French model, liberty is founded in equality (via the general will) and fraternity is "necessarily exclusionary — those for and those against," so dissenters "must be destroyed… by the guillotine." He is the contrast to [[George Washington]], who relinquished power. See [[Rousseau Is the Anti-Enlightenment Lynchpin of the French Revolution]].[^2]
