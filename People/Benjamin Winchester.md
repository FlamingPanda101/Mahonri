# Benjamin Winchester

**Era:** 1817–1901
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint (early; later excommunicated)
**Role:** Early missionary, Seventy, and newspaper editor

## Bio
Benjamin Winchester was an early and zealous Latter-day Saint — reportedly the youngest adult on Zion's Camp at sixteen, among the first ordained Seventies, and branch president in Philadelphia. A close associate of [[Parley P. Pratt]], he founded a newspaper, *The Gospel Reflector*, largely to rebut anti-Mormon attacks, and republished [[Oliver Cowdery]]'s historical letters in it. A "very contentious person," he was reprimanded by Joseph Smith, fired from the *Times and Seasons* by the Quorum of the Twelve (late 1841 / early 1842), and eventually excommunicated after a conflict with William Smith.

## Relevance to the vault
[[Jonathan Neville]] makes Winchester the linchpin of his account of how the Mesoamerican Book of Mormon geography began: excited by the 1841 Stephens-and-Catherwood travel books, Winchester promoted linking Central American ruins to the Book of Mormon as a kind of marketing strategy, and — Neville argues from word-print and content analysis — authored the 1842 *Times and Seasons* Mesoamerican articles long attributed to Joseph Smith as nominal editor. See [[The Times and Seasons Mesoamerican Articles Were Benjamin Winchester Not Joseph Smith]]. (The attribution is contested; Matt Roper and others credit those articles to the editorial circle in Nauvoo.)
