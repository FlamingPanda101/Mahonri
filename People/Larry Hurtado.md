# Larry Hurtado

**Full name:** Larry W. Hurtado (1943–2019)
**Role:** New Testament scholar; historian of early Christianity
**Affiliation:** University of Edinburgh (Emeritus Professor)
**Era:** 20th–21st century
**Authority:** Tier A — leading peer-reviewed authority on early Christology and Jewish monotheism.

## Bio

Larry Hurtado was a major scholar of Christian origins, best known for his work on the early "devotion to Jesus" within a Second Temple Jewish monotheistic framework — *One God, One Lord* (1988) and *Lord Jesus Christ* (2003). He argued that the earliest Christians incorporated Jesus into the worship of the one God of Israel in a way that was both novel and self-consciously monotheistic ("binitarian" devotion within Jewish monotheism).

## Role in the Monotheism Debate

In the indexed video, [[Gavin Ortlund]] recommends Hurtado (alongside [[Richard Bauckham]]) for "monotheism of the first century Jewish context in which Christianity came about and how the christology of the New Testament relates to that." [[Dan McClellan]] situates Hurtado — together with [[Benjamin Sommer]] — among scholars who, in his view, "massage" or revise the definition of monotheism in order to keep applying it to the Bible while preserving ideological continuity with their own communities; McClellan says he has addressed Hurtado's and Bauckham's scholarship before and will again in his forthcoming *Oxford Research Encyclopedia of Religion* essay.
