# Arthur Zander

**Role:** Latter-day Saint branch president in Hamburg; outspoken Nazi
**Era:** Nazi Germany (1930s–1940s)
**Tradition:** The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (LDS); Nazi party member

## Bio

Arthur Zander was the branch president of [[Helmut Hübner]] in Hamburg and, per [[Stephen Smoot]], "an outspoken vocal Nazi" — infamously posting *Juden ist der Zutritt verboten* ("No Jews allowed") signage at the branch building. Smoot cites him as the clearest LDS example of the minority who aligned with the regime out of ideological conviction, set against heroes like Hübener and [[Paul Herbert Schick]].[^1]

## Role in the Excommunication

Zander, with a local mission leader, signed the warrant posthumously excommunicating Hübener. Smoot is careful to separate Zander's pro-Nazi motive from the very different, survival-driven reasoning of district president [[Otto Berndt]], who consented under Gestapo pressure. See [[Helmut Hubner's Excommunication Was a Local Survival Measure Later Reversed]].[^2]
