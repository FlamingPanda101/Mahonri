# Bret Weinstein

**Full Name:** Bret Weinstein
**Era:** Contemporary (1969–present)
**Tradition:** Atheist / secular evolutionary biologist
**Role:** Evolutionary biologist, former professor (Evergreen State College), podcaster (*DarkHorse*)

## Bio

Bret Weinstein is an American evolutionary biologist and public intellectual, formerly a professor at Evergreen State College. In the Vancouver dialogue between [[Jordan Peterson]] and [[Sam Harris]], he served as moderator and active third interlocutor.

## Role in this dialogue

Weinstein repeatedly tried to broker between [[Sam Harris]]'s moral realism and [[Jordan Peterson]]'s narrative metaphysics by reframing religious belief in **evolutionary-adaptationist** terms — proposing that religious texts function as cognitive **heuristics** that encode fitness-enhancing behavior from past environments, while insisting (with Harris) that such "useful fictions have to be retired" once we understand the underlying variables. See [[Religious Texts as Evolutionary Heuristics]].

He introduced the chimpanzee "raiding party" data ([[Jane Goodall]]'s Gombe observations) as evidence that group-based atrocity predates and runs deeper than religion. See [[Tribal Violence Predates Religion - The Chimpanzee Evidence]].
