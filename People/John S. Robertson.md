# John S. Robertson

**Era:** 20th–21st century
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint (LDS)
**Role:** Historical linguist; Mayanist

## Bio
John S. Robertson is a leading Mayanist with a Harvard PhD in historical linguistics and a former chair of the **BYU linguistics department**.[^1]

## On Stubbs's Work
In the [[Informed Saints - Book of Mormon Evidence Hidden in Ancient American Language|Informed Saints interview]], [[Brian Stubbs]] cites Robertson as having written two positive reviews of his Uto-Aztecan-Semitic/Egyptian work, alongside favorable notice from Dirk Elzinga (a Uto-Aztecan/Numic specialist), Royal Skousen, and the non-LDS Uto-Aztecanist Eric Elliott.[^1] Robertson is listed among the credentialed reviewers Stubbs offers as scholarly support for a thesis he concedes is outside the field's consensus. See [[Uto-Aztecan Contains a Large Semitic and Egyptian Lexical Layer]].
