# Mosiah

**Era:** ~3rd–2nd century BC (Book of Mormon)
**Tradition:** Book of Mormon figure (Mosiah I)

## Identity

Mosiah I is the Nephite king who, warned by the Lord, led the righteous out of the land of Nephi to the land of Zarahemla, where they joined the people of [[Mulek]] ([[Omni 1]]). He interpreted the engravings on a large stone brought to him (Omni 1:20–22), and is the father of [[King Benjamin]]. His name plausibly combines "Moses" and "Josiah." (Distinct from his grandson Mosiah II, who translated the Jaredite plates in [[Mosiah 28]].)

## Apologetic significance

[[Don Bradley]] argues that a lost-116-pages narrative (preserved in the [[Fayette Lapham]] interview with [[Joseph Smith Sr]]) describes Mosiah I being led by the Liahona to the interpreters, covering his face with an animal skin to use them — a Tabernacle/veil pattern echoing Moses on Sinai and anticipating Joseph Smith's own use of a hat. This supplies the missing account of how the Nephites obtained the interpreters. See [[The Jaredite and Nephite Interpreters Carry the Endowment Ascent Pattern]].
