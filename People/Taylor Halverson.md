# Taylor Halverson

**Role:** Latter-day Saint scholar and educator; professor at BYU's Marriott School of Business
**Era:** Contemporary, American
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint

## Bio

Taylor Halverson is a Latter-day Saint scholar, author, and educator affiliated with Brigham Young University's Marriott School of Business. He writes and teaches widely on scripture study, the ancient Near East, learning and creativity, and self-branding as a "master learner," promoting an openness to "receive truth, let it come from whence it may." He is a prolific producer of study guides and popular religious-education content.

## Relevance to the vault

Halverson appears chiefly as the subject of a Ward Radio "roast" rather than as a cited authority. In [[Ward Radio - Adam and Eves Epic Rap Battle and Calling Out BYU Scholars]], the hosts read an email in which Halverson declined — unread — to read [[Jonah (Plain and Precious Publishing)|Jonah Barnes]]'s book, calling himself "highly skeptical that Jonah has said anything new or meaningfully accurate." They juxtapose this against his "master learner" self-branding to charge LDS academic "niceness culture" with gatekeeping hypocrisy, and offer him a $500 charity challenge to actually engage the material. The segment is used to distinguish genuine academic rigor (which the hosts affirm is needed) from what they frame as dismissiveness toward outsider scholarship.
