# Alpheus Cutler

Alpheus Cutler (1784–1864) was an early Latter Day Saint leader and the founder of the **Cutlerite** Church (the Church of Jesus Christ, Cutlerite). He received his **second anointing** under [[Joseph Smith]] — the sixth person ever to receive it, just before [[Brigham Young]] — and preached in Nauvoo that "my salvation depends on sustaining the Twelve." Given a mission by Joseph to work among Native Americans, he stopped his 1847 trek west in Iowa, and after seeing "two back-to-back half moons" (interpreted as Joseph's predicted sign of the church's reorganization) he organized his own church. He claimed Joseph had privately ordained him one of **seven** prophets, becoming leader on the death of Joseph's uncle John Smith, and styled himself "head counselor over the church."

## Doctrine and the Cutlerite Movement
Cutler's church is the only branch besides Brigham Young's to preserve the 1844 **endowment** (performed in the chapel's "upper room"), though it lost **sealing** in the 1850s after Cutler suspended plural marriage "until Jesus Christ returns" and lived thereafter as a monogamist — his remaining plural wife, a former wife of [[Orrin Porter Rockwell]], was remarried within the community. The Cutlerites practice the United Order, are anti-polygamy, and by the time of [[Christopher Blythe]]'s account had dwindled to about six elderly members awaiting a "one mighty and strong."
