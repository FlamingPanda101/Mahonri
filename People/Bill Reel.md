# Bill Reel
**Role:** Ex-Latter-day Saint critic and podcaster

## Bio
Bill Reel is a former Latter-day Saint who became a prominent critic of the Church and a podcaster (associated with *Mormon Discussions*). In [[Jacob Hansen]]'s engagement, Reel is presented as a representative "anti-Mormon" interlocutor whose worldview Hansen analyzes for internal inconsistency.[^1]

In a recorded exchange, Reel affirms that human beings have no free will or agency — appealing to science ("everything is cause and effect") and to genetics/epigenetics to argue that even racists, serial killers, and child predators are "predisposed" rather than choosing their behavior.[^2] Hansen's critique is that Reel simultaneously wants to condemn racism, pedophilia, and the Church as morally reprehensible, which (Hansen argues) is incoherent given Reel's denial of moral agency — a double standard Reel visibly "struggles with" on camera ("can they choose whether or not to actually have sex with children... yes and no").[^3] Reel also presses his own line of attack, arguing that the Church's shift from "free agency" to "moral agency" shows it "thinks it has a right to determine what's right and wrong" while being "unable to discern such."[^4] See [[Bill Reel's Free-Will Double Standard - Determinism Cannot Ground Moral Blame]] and [[The Sin of an Inconsistent Worldview]].
