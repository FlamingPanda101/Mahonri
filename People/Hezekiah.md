# Hezekiah

**Who:** King of Judah (c. 715–686 BCE), son of Ahaz. His reign is recorded in 2 Kings 18–20, 2 Chronicles 29–32, and Isaiah 36–39. He is portrayed as a reformer who centralized worship in Jerusalem and resisted Assyrian domination.

**Role:** [[Jacob Wright]] identifies Hezekiah (along with [[Josiah]]) as a key figure behind the "united monarchy" narrative — a political fabrication designed to claim the legacy of the destroyed northern kingdom of Israel for Judah. The claim that David and Solomon ruled a united Israel was, in Wright's view, a Judean political program under Hezekiah and Josiah to absorb the defeated northern population.
