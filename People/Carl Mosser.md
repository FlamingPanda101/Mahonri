# Carl Mosser

**Era:** Contemporary (1990s–2020s)
**Tradition:** Evangelical Protestant
**Role:** Biblical scholar, theologian

## Bio
Carl Mosser is an Evangelical scholar of biblical studies and historical theology, known both for serious academic work on **deification (theosis)** in the Christian tradition and for co-authoring, with [[Paul Owen]], a widely cited assessment of Evangelical engagement with Mormonism.

## Topics

### "Are We Losing the Battle?" (1997)
Mosser and Owen's paper, *Mormonism and the Problem of Loss: Are We Losing the Battle? And Do We Not Know It?* (1997), is cited twice by the [[All Those In Favor - Three Tactics to Evangelize Mormons|All Those In Favor]] hosts as a *hostile* witness — the authors are critics of Mormonism, yet conceded that "the sophistication and erudition of LDS apologetics has risen considerably while evangelical responses have not."[^1] The hosts use it to argue the gap has only widened, with Latter-day Saint scholars now publishing with presses like Oxford while critics such as [[Mark Driscoll]] and [[Sean McDowell]] still recycle 1830s talking points.[^2]
