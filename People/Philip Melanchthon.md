# Philip Melanchthon

**Full name:** Philipp Melanchthon (1497–1560)
**Role:** Reformer, theologian, author of Lutheran confessions
**Era:** Reformation (16th century)
**Tradition:** Protestant (Lutheran)

## Bio

Philip Melanchthon was a humanist scholar and Martin Luther's closest collaborator, often called the "teacher of Germany." He authored the *Augsburg Confession* (1530) and its *Apology*, as well as the *Treatise on the Power and Primacy of the Pope* (1537), and his *Loci Communes* was the first Protestant systematic theology. He was the chief intellectual systematizer of the early Lutheran movement.

## Relevance to the vault

Melanchthon supplies the precise distinction the vault uses to argue that rejecting the papacy was protest, not schism: in the *Treatise* he denied papal authority by *divine right* (as necessary for salvation) while conceding the Reformers could in principle accept a Roman primacy by *human right* (organizational convenience). Refusing an unwarranted divine claim is thus framed as obedience to conscience, not rebellion against God's church.
