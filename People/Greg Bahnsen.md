# Greg Bahnsen

**Era:** 1948–1995
**Tradition:** Reformed (Calvinist) / Theonomist
**Role:** Philosopher, presuppositional apologist

## Bio
Greg L. Bahnsen was a Reformed philosopher and apologist, the most influential **second-generation presuppositionalist** after [[Cornelius Van Til]]. He held a Ph.D. in philosophy from the University of Southern California and is best known for the 1985 debate with atheist Gordon Stein ("the great debate") and for systematizing Van Til's transcendental argument for the existence of God. He died in 1995.

## Topics

### Disarm vs. Dodge — The Gunman Illustration
[[Hayden Carroll]] builds his apologetics course around a recorded Bahnsen lecture (dated by Carroll to "the 80s or 90s") in which Bahnsen contrasts two strategies for facing a man with a revolver: **dodging the bullets** (answering individual attacks) versus **disarming the gunman** (dismantling the worldview behind the attacks). Bahnsen urges disarming. Carroll adopts this as his core method and relishes turning the presuppositional method back on evangelicals. See [[Disarm vs Dodge - Bahnsen's Two Apologetic Strategies]].[^1]

### Internal Critique
Bahnsen's method centers on the **internal critique** — showing a worldview to be incoherent on its own terms without importing outside assumptions — which Carroll repackages as his "can you critique my worldview without presupposing your own?" challenge. See [[Internal Critique - Two Questions for Every Critic]].[^2] Carroll also paraphrases a remembered Bahnsen claim that presuppositional apologetics "destroys every worldview except" those with a prophet, a revelation, and a book — citing "Mormons and the Muslims" (a paraphrase, not a verified quotation; Bahnsen the Calvinist would not have endorsed LDS theology).[^3]
