# Ed Barnhart

**Era:** Contemporary (active 2000s–2020s)
**Tradition:** Secular / mainstream academic
**Role:** Mesoamerican archaeologist; director, Maya Exploration Center

## Bio

Ed Barnhart is a mainstream Mesoamerican archaeologist trained at the University of Texas and the head of the Maya Exploration Center. He produced the Mesoamerica installment of a Great Courses lecture series. He is **not** a Latter-day Saint and explicitly disavows endorsing pre-Columbian transoceanic contact.

## Why he appears in the vault

In a Great Courses lecture clip reacted to on Ward Radio, Barnhart catalogs Olmec art from La Venta — bearded figures, turbans, a clay bust with non-Mesoamerican features — and concedes that "sometimes we don't have a better explanation at the moment" than Mediterranean (Phoenician) influence, while pleading "don't shoot the messenger."[^1] [[Jerry Grover]] uses the concession as a mainstream-academic data point consistent with the Book of Mormon's Jaredite/Mulekite migrations.
