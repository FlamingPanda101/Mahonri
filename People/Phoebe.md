# Phoebe

First-century Christian woman of Cenchreae (the eastern port of Corinth), named by Paul in Romans 16:1–2.

## Deacon and patron
Paul calls Phoebe "a deacon [Greek *diakonos*] of the church at Cenchreae" — using the same masculine office-word he applies to his own ministry and to Apollos, Tychicus, and Timothy. [[Ariel Bybee Laughton]] argues the Greek structure and her designation to a *specific* church indicate "a recognized position of honor and responsibility." She was likely the **bearer of Paul's letter to the Romans** and possibly an official delegate of her congregation. Paul also names her as his *prostatis* (benefactor/patron), suggesting she was a woman of means who supported his and others' missionary work. The feminine *diakonissa* ("deaconess") did not appear before the 4th century, so Phoebe's title is fully the masculine office-word. See [[Women Led the First-Century Church as Apostles Deacons and Patrons]] and [[Romans 16.1-7|Romans 16:1-2]]. [^1]
