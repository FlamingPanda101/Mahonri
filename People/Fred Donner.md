# Fred Donner

**Full Name:** Fred McGraw Donner
**Era:** Contemporary
**Field:** Near Eastern history / early Islamic studies
**Role:** Peter B. Ritzma Professor of Near Eastern History, University of Chicago

## Bio

Fred Donner is an American historian of early Islam, the Peter B. Ritzma Professor of Near Eastern History at the University of Chicago. He earned his PhD from Princeton and taught Middle Eastern history at Yale before moving to Chicago. His books include *The Early Islamic Conquests*, *Narratives of Islamic Origins*, and *Muhammad and the Believers*.

Donner is associated with a revisionist-influenced but moderate position in Islamic-origins scholarship: he holds that the **traditional Islamic origins narrative** — long accepted "with considerable confidence" — is "far less secure than we once thought," because the Muslim literary sources (chronicles, biographical dictionaries) that describe the life of the Prophet were mostly written **two or three centuries after the events**, rest on "almost no actual documentation," and at points conflict with earlier non-Muslim (mainly Christian) accounts. He alludes to Ernest Renan's 19th-century claim that Islam was "born in the full light of history" precisely to argue the opposite.
