# Jan Shipps

**Role:** Historian of religion; pioneering non-LDS scholar of Mormonism
**Tradition:** Methodist (never converted to the LDS Church)

## Bio
Jan Shipps was a **Methodist** historian who became one of the foremost outside scholars of Mormonism. A devout Methodist who never joined the LDS Church, she was nonetheless consistently "appreciative and understanding of it," and is remembered as a pioneer bridge-builder in the academic study of the tradition. She lived into her 90s.

## Mormonism (1985)
Her landmark book **_Mormonism: The Story of a New Religious Tradition_** is, in [[Grant Underwood]]'s words, "a real milestone in Mormon studies"; Underwood notes its 40th anniversary in [[Informed Saints - Do Mormons Believe in the Trinity]]. He invokes Shipps as an early, credible contributor to the contested question of "what is Christian and what is Christianity" — a non-Mormon scholar from whom both Latter-day Saints and their critics "could benefit," and part of the case in [[Early Christianity Was Plural Not One Perfect Tree]] that the "Christian" label cannot be cleanly owned or policed.
