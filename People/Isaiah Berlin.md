# Isaiah Berlin

**Era:** 1909–1997 (20th century)
**Tradition:** Secular history of ideas
**Role:** Philosopher, historian of ideas

## Bio

Latvian-British philosopher and historian of ideas, known for his studies of Romanticism and the Counter-Enlightenment.

## Role in the Argument

[[Brian Thomassen]] quotes Berlin to define **Romanticism** — the German-rooted movement that *replaces* the Enlightenment and underwrites the French Revolution's [[Jean-Jacques Rousseau|Rousseauian]] strand. Berlin describes Romanticism as "a longing for the unbounded and the indefinable, for perpetual movement and change, an effort to return to the long-forgotten sources of life" — a rejection of rationality and propositional truth for the intuitive. See [[Rousseau Is the Anti-Enlightenment Lynchpin of the French Revolution]].[^1]
