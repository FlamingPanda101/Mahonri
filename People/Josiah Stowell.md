# Josiah Stowell

Josiah Stowell was a Pennsylvania-area farmer and landowner who in 1825 hired [[Joseph Smith]] to help locate a legendary lost Spanish silver mine near Harmony, Pennsylvania.[^1] The episode is significant for three reasons: it is the one treasure hunt [[Joseph Smith]] explicitly acknowledges in his own canonized history; it is when Joseph first met his future wife [[Emma Smith]]; and Stowell's later courtroom testimony is a key data point in the 1826 trial.

## Recruiting Joseph Smith

According to [[Lucy Mack Smith]], Stowell came to Manchester, New York, to recruit Joseph because Joseph "had means or keys whereby which he could discern things not otherwise visible to the natural eye" — a reference to his seer stones, since this predates the Nephite interpreters.[^2] See [[Josiah Stowell Hires Joseph Smith 1825]].

## Testimony at the 1826 Trial

At the 1826 proceeding, Stowell — the supposed "victim" — testified *in defense* of Joseph, stating that Joseph had looked into a seer stone and accurately described Stowell's distant land, home, and outbuildings in sufficient detail to identify the property.[^3] The charge had been brought not by Stowell but by his nephew [[Peter Bridgeman]]. The source treats Stowell's defense of Joseph as the single strongest reason the conviction is doubtful and as the host's personal "takeaway."[^4] See [[Josiah Stowell Defends Joseph Smith]].
