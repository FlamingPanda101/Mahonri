# Captain Moroni

**Role:** Nephite military commander
**Era:** Book of Mormon (c. 73–56 BCE in the narrative)
**Tradition:** Book of Mormon

## Bio

Captain Moroni is the chief Nephite military commander in the book of Alma, appointed at age twenty-five. He is celebrated for raising the "Title of Liberty," fortifying Nephite cities, and defending his people against the Lamanites and the king-men. The text praises him as a man of perfect understanding whose heart gloried in the liberty and freedom of his country (Alma 48:11–17). He is distinct from [[Angel Moroni]], the later prophet-record-keeper.

## Relevance to the vault

Captain Moroni is invoked as a model of an engagement ethic: "wise as serpents, harmless as doves." In a discussion of how Latter-day Saints should respond to online hostility, his offering Zerahemnah a covenant of peace (rather than pressing for slaughter) is cited as the pattern of a peacemaker who ends conflict rather than inflaming it — supporting the vault's "peacemaker not pushover" posture.
