# Claus Westermann

**Full name:** Claus Westermann (1909–2000)
**Role:** Old Testament scholar
**Era:** 20th century
**Tradition:** Academic (German, University of Heidelberg)

## Bio

Claus Westermann was one of the most influential German Old Testament scholars of the 20th century. He taught at the University of Heidelberg and produced major commentaries on Genesis, Isaiah, Psalms, and Lamentations. His Genesis 1–11 commentary is a standard reference in source criticism and remains widely cited.

Dan McClellan includes Westermann in his recommended bibliography of Genesis commentators who all affirm the two-source flood hypothesis: "You can go back to Claus Westermann's commentary on Genesis 1–11." [^1]

## Genesis Source Criticism

Westermann's Genesis commentary systematically identifies and distinguishes the Priestly (P) and non-Priestly (J) sources in the Primeval History, including the flood narrative. His work is part of the century-long scholarly tradition that McClellan describes as "one of the most long-standing, widely agreed upon, best-supported source critical arguments that there is in the Hebrew Bible."
