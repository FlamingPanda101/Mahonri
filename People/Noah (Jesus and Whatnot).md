# Noah (Jesus and Whatnot)

**Era:** Contemporary (2020s)
**Tradition:** Roman Catholic
**Role:** Social-media apologist / content creator

## Bio
Noah is a young Roman Catholic apologist who posts as **"Jesus and whatnot"** on Instagram. He is not a credentialed scholar, but the [[All Those In Favor - Three Tactics to Evangelize Mormons|All Those In Favor]] hosts single him out as "extremely intelligent, very well spoken," and as the only critic they have seen publicly correct his own past mistakes about LDS belief. (Distinct from the Genesis patriarch [[Noah]].)

## Topics

### The retraction video
Noah's earlier "Three Reasons to Reject the Cult of Mormonism" was followed, three months later, by a correction video acknowledging he "got a lot wrong." He withdraws the "[[Gods of Their Own Planets Is a Caricature of Exaltationist Theosis|gods of their own planets]]" caricature, reframing exaltation as "exaltationist theosis"; concedes the LDS reading of [[Matthew 16.18|Matthew 16:18]] (the rock is divine revelation, per verse 17); and treats the [[Great Apostasy Is Not a Scriptural Term in LDS Standard Works|great apostasy]] charitably, locating the real Catholic–LDS disagreement in tradition and councils rather than caricature.[^1] His stated goal is honest: he wants Latter-day Saints to become Catholics, and urges fellow critics to "stop making such low-tier arguments… and start actually understanding what our brothers and sisters believe."[^2]

### Why the hosts hold him up as a model
For the hosts, Noah embodies the episode's thesis (see [[Faith Has Become a Debate Sport Not a Salvation Effort]]): unlike [[Mark Driscoll]] and [[Sean McDowell]], he read a recent LDS apologetic source, named his goal, and corrected himself — "that's humility, that's not wanting to bear false witness." They reciprocate by urging viewers to "steelman the papacy" and stating they would be Catholics if the Church disappeared.[^3]
