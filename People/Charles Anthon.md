# Charles Anthon

Professor of classical languages at Columbia College, the scholar at the center of the [[Charles Anthon Transcript Episode]]. [[Martin Harris]] brought him a copy of the "Reformed Egyptian" characters Joseph had transcribed from the plates, in 1828.

## Two conflicting accounts
- **Harris / Joseph Smith History:** Anthon verified the characters as authentic ("Egyptian, Chaldaic, Assyric, and Arabic") and gave a certificate, then destroyed it on learning their source [^1].
- **Anthon's own account:** the document was a hoax, "a compilation derived from an alternate of Greek and Hebrew letters, crosses and flourishes," "evidently copied after the Mexican calendar given by Humboldt" [^2].

The episode grants "it's clear someone was lying" and argues that either way [[Martin Harris]] came away convinced — see [[Charles Anthon Transcript Episode]] and [[Metallurgical Fraud Theory Rebutted]] [^3].
