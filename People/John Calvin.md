# John Calvin

16th-century Protestant Reformer whose theology of **predestination** is cited
in arguments about the moral implications of eternal damnation.

## Key Quote (on Hell and Divine Glory)

> "God pre-ordained for his own glory and the display of his attributes of
> mercy and justice, a part of the human race without any merit of their own
> to eternal salvation, and in another part, in just punishment of their sin,
> to eternal damnation." [^1]

This is critiqued in [[Hell - For Gods Glory Defense Rebutted]] — the claim
that God's glory can be obtained *through* the eternal torture of souls is
argued to be morally indefensible and theologically self-defeating (glory
contingent on external outcomes contradicts divine self-sufficiency).
