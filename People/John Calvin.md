# John Calvin

**Full Name:** Jean Cauvin (John Calvin)
**Era:** 1509–1564
**Tradition:** Reformed (Protestant — founder of Calvinism)
**Role:** Theologian, pastor, primary Reformation figure

## Bio

John Calvin was a French theologian and pastor during the Protestant
Reformation. He was the principal figure in the development of the system of
Christian theology later called Calvinism. His *Institutes of the Christian
Religion* (1536, expanded through 1559) is one of the most influential works of
Protestant systematic theology. Calvin's theology emphasizes the sovereignty of
God, predestination, and total depravity.

## Topics

### Calvinism and Free Will
In the video, [[Hayden Carroll]] notes that Calvinists are uniquely consistent
among creedal Christians on the question of free will:

> "There is one group of Christians who do agree with the atheists and it's the
> Calvinists... They're very consistent. They say, 'God — everything that
> happens happens because God wanted it to happen.' And so in that way they
> would say, 'Yeah, we don't have libertarian free will.'"[^1]

Carroll argues that Calvinism shares the same determinism problem as atheism:
both deny libertarian free will. The difference is only *who* determined you —
God (Calvinism) or material causes (atheism). Both make human beings dominoes
in a causal chain they did not initiate.

However, Carroll also identifies an inconsistency: Calvinists claim we have a
"will" (enslaved to God or the devil) but not a "free will." Carroll questions
whether this distinction survives scrutiny given that God predetermined
everything, including who is enslaved to whom.[^2]
