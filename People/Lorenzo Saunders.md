# Lorenzo Saunders

Lorenzo Saunders was a Palmyra-area contemporary of [[Joseph Smith]] — a former treasure-hunting associate who became a committed critic and "dedicated chunks of his life to disparage the Smiths."[^1] In LDS apologetics he functions as a textbook **hostile / unlikely witness**: his late-life interviews, given to discredit Joseph, inadvertently corroborate specific details of the gold-plates account.

## The "No Fresh Dirt" Admission
Across multiple accounts Saunders denied there was any hole in the hill; in one, however, he conceded a hole *was* there but located it precisely — "on the summit on the west side" — matching [[Joseph Smith]] and [[Oliver Cowdery]] (a detail he would only know from being there, and he never went west with the Saints).[^2] He further volunteered that he examined the excavation only weeks after Joseph took the plates, found it had been dug "years before," with **no fresh dirt** — which Gailey argues unintentionally confirms Joseph's account of first opening the box in 1823 and merely clearing it on annual visits thereafter.[^3] See [[Hostile and Unlikely Witnesses Corroborate the Stone Box]].
