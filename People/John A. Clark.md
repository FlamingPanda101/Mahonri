# John A. Clark

A nineteenth-century Protestant clergyman and early critic of Mormonism. His account is cited by the [[Jeremy Runnells|CES Letter]] for a report that [[Martin Harris]] said he "saw [the plates] with the eye of faith."[^1]

[[Keystone]] notes the quote is **thirdhand** — Clark is relaying what an unnamed Palmyra gentleman claimed to have heard from Harris — so "we have no idea who this claim is ultimately coming from or how accurate it is." The fuller quote adds that the plates were "covered with a cloth," consistent with Harris's pre-translation handling rather than his later uncovered witness. Keystone further shows that the CES Letter's two separate "eye of faith" Palmyra references actually trace to this single source, copied and pasted from an anti-Mormon website.[^1]
