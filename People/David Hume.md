# David Hume

**Era:** 1711–1776
**Tradition:** Scottish Enlightenment; empiricist, skeptic of religion
**Role:** Philosopher

## Bio

David Hume was a Scottish Enlightenment philosopher famous for his empiricism and
his skeptical treatment of religion and miracles, especially the essay "Of
Miracles" in *An Enquiry Concerning Human Understanding*.

## Maxim on Miracles

The panel invokes Hume's maxim — that one should believe a miracle only if its
falsehood (the alternative natural explanation) would be more miraculous than the
miracle itself — and turns it back on the critic: positing that
[[Joseph Smith]] fabricated witness-consistent gold plates in his garage is, they
argue, *more* miraculous than receiving them from an angel. See
[[Humes Maxim Applied to the Gold Plates]].[^1]
