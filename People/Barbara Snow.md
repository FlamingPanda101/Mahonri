# Barbara Snow

Utah therapist who, beginning in Lehi in 1985, became the central figure of the Mormon satanic panic. Her interview techniques — isolating children for hours without breaks, refusing to record sessions, asking leading questions, and giving physical affection only when children produced abuse disclosures — generated cascading, mutually corroborating accusations across multiple Utah communities (Lehi, Bountiful, and others), in every case without physical evidence.

## Manufacturing Allegations Through Interview Method
Snow's methodology, not any underlying crime, was the only common thread linking otherwise unconnected cases. See [[Barbara Snows Interview Methods Manufactured Abuse Allegations]] and [[Identical Cross-Case Allegations Point to the Interviewer Not the Crime]] [^1].

## The Hadfield Case
She named Allen Hadfield as an abuser and supplied the April 9, 1986 "no-alibi" date — a night she knew he was unaccounted for because she had phoned him. See [[The Allen Hadfield Trial as a Miscarriage of Justice]] and [[The No-Alibi Date as Manufactured Evidence]] [^2].

## Investigated and delicensed (Part 4)
Ward Radio Part 4 documents Snow's later downfall. She allegedly double-billed a third-party insurer (illegal) and broke into a patient's home with a metal baseball bat to destroy a computer, generating "real evidence" in a police report. Most significantly, in 2007 the Utah Division of Occupational and Professional Licensing charged that she had *implanted* false memories of sexual abuse — a theory law enforcement tested by feeding her false information that "immediately began appearing in the children's accounts." She agreed to probation and license suspension and was ordered to complete a psychological evaluation. When [[Allen Hadfield]] appealed, the Utah State Court cited "the suggestive and coercive nature of Dr. Snow's techniques," and the 10th Circuit called her conduct "disturbing and irresponsible." Adult former interviewee [[David Cox]] gave a firsthand account of her berating him into "fictitious memories." See [[Leading SRA Therapists Were Discredited Sued or Delicensed]] and [[Barbara Snows Interview Methods Manufactured Abuse Allegations]]. [^3][^4][^5]

## The Marion Smith / ISAT case (Part 5)
Ward Radio Part 5 details an earlier Snow case: therapist [[Marion Smith]] brought her own grandchildren to Snow at the Intermountain Sexual Abuse Treatment Center, and after "extensive interviews" Snow "reported triumphantly" that the children acquiesced — then expanded the accusations outward to a gas-company president, grocery-chain president [[Richard Miles]], the local bishop, and apostle [[Russell M. Nelson]]'s daughter [[Brenda Miles]]. The cases produced no physical evidence and collapsed. See [[The Accusation Wheel Self-Recruits Accusers Through Therapy]] and [[Accusations Cannot Transmute Into Evidence]]. [^6]
