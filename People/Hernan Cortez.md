# Hernan Cortez

**Role:** Spanish conquistador; conqueror of the Aztec Empire
**Era:** 16th century (1485–1547)
**Tradition:** Secular (Spanish Catholic)

## Bio

Hernán Cortés was the Spanish conquistador who led the 1519 expedition to the Mexican mainland and the conquest of the Aztec Empire. His own letters to the crown survive as primary sources.

## Relevance to the vault

Cortés is the **documentary baseline** in [[Ward Radio - Deep Dive Debunking Doubts About Horses in the Book of Mormon]] for when European horses are said to have reached the North American mainland (1519). Wikipedia and a genomics study peg the spread of horses across the continent to Cortés's animals — but [[Daniel Johnson]]'s argument is that Cortés's own records defeat this: he carefully tracked his small number of horses (the Spanish prized them above crew), gifted a few to natives, and returned to find those had been *eaten*. With so few horses, many of them mares, and a ~344-or-48-year window, the demographic math cannot produce the continent-wide horse populations documented decades later. See [[The Spanish Horse Math Cannot Explain Continent-Wide Horses by 1567]].
