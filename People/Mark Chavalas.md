# Mark Chavalas

**Role:** Historian and scholar of ancient Mesopotamia
**Era:** Contemporary (active 1990s–2020s)
**Tradition:** Academic

## Bio

Mark W. Chavalas is a historian specializing in ancient Mesopotamia and the relationship between Mesopotamian civilization and the Hebrew Bible. His *Mesopotamia and the Bible: Comparative Explorations* (co-edited with K. Lawson Younger Jr., 2003/2004) provides a mature scholarly assessment of the Ebla controversy after the initial sensationalist claims had been thoroughly evaluated and largely rejected.

## Summary of the Ebla Controversy's Resolution

Chavalas's survey captures the field's movement from early apologetic excitement to sober reassessment:

> "The second epigrapher, Archi, spent some effort in refuting many of the premises of Dahoud and Pettinato, ignoring most of the sensationalism, primarily from the American press. He argued that the supposed evidence for Adonai at Ebla was questionable and ambiguous, and that the kings of Ebla were not anointed like the kings of Israel... There is no Genesis creation story. Pettinato 1977 and Ebla place names do not easily correspond to the Bible names or the cities of the plain. The Eblaite connections with the Hebrew language are unclear."[^1]

Chavalas's conclusion is a decisive verdict:

> "The excitement concerning the Ebla material has somewhat died down... In the past 15 years, only a minuscule amount of effort has been put to comparisons of Ebla and the Bible compared to the large amount of work on the civilization of Ebla itself. This is preferable."[^1]
