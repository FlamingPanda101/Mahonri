# Solomon Spalding

Solomon Spalding was an early American writer whose unpublished romance about Roman adventurers in the New World is the basis of the discredited **Spalding theory** of Book of Mormon authorship.

## The Spalding Theory

The theory holds that Spalding wrote a romance about Roman adventurers in the New World and that this manuscript — supposedly transmitted through [[Sydney Rigdon]], who is alleged to have met [[Joseph Smith]] earlier than the record shows — is the real source of the Book of Mormon.[^1] The source flatly characterizes it as "all this baloney that's been completely discredited by any good historian," noting that "among historians who have actual graduate training in history, there's nobody who takes that," even though it "still gets pushed around" in comment sections.[^2] See [[Spalding Theory of Book of Mormon Origin]].
