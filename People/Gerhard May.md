# Gerhard May

**Role:** Church historian and theologian
**Era:** 20th century (active 1970s–1990s)
**Tradition:** Academic (German)

## Bio

Gerhard May was a German church historian and theologian whose *Creatio Ex Nihilo: The Doctrine of 'Creation out of Nothing' in Early Christian Thought* (German original: *Schöpfung aus dem Nichts*, mid-1970s; English translation by A.S. Worrall, T&T Clark, 1994) is the foundational scholarly work on the historical development of the doctrine of creation ex nihilo. His study traces the emergence of the doctrine in early Christian thought and establishes the consensus timeline: creation ex nihilo was first articulated in the late 2nd century CE by Tatian, Theophilus of Antioch, and Irenaeus.

## Creation Ex Nihilo Scholarship

May's work is the standard reference cited by virtually all subsequent scholarship on the topic. His key findings:

- **2 Maccabees 7:28 does not teach creation ex nihilo.** May includes an extended discussion and "flatly rejects the notion that it teaches creation out of nothing."[^1]
- **Philo of Alexandria rejected creatio ex nihilo.** May devotes a full section to Philo, demonstrating that Philo held to creation from pre-existent matter consistent with his Middle Platonic philosophical commitments.[^2]
- **The doctrine originates with Tatian, Theophilus, and Irenaeus** in the second half of the 2nd century CE, driven by anti-Gnostic polemic and philosophical debates about the resurrection and the nature of matter.[^3]
