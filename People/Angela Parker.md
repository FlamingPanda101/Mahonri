# Angela Parker

Reverend Dr. Angela Parker, an associate professor of New Testament and Greek. A non-Latter-day Saint biblical scholar whom [[Keystone]] contacted for an outside opinion on the meaning of [[2 Timothy 3.16-17|2 Timothy 3:16]]. She agreed, on the record, that the verse "is definitely not a proof text for biblical inerrancy."[^1]

Keystone cites her (alongside an anonymous published scholar and Dr. Anna Cwikla) as independent corroboration of [[John C. Poirier]]'s argument that *theopneustos* ("God-breathed") was mistranslated as "inspired" in a way aimed at reinforcing inerrancy that the text does not assert. See [[Theopneustos Was Used for Sandals Ointments and Streams Not Inerrancy]].
