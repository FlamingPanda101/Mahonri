# Noemi Mattis

Psychologist and co-chair of the Utah Governor's Task Force on ritual abuse (formed c. 1990–91). The governor chose her expecting a "voice of calm rationale": she had described herself to the *Deseret News* (September 1991) as "a New York humanist Jew and a quintessential skeptic, part of a group of people who didn't even believe in Satan," and had viewed abuse allegations skeptically, comparing the evangelical hysteria to the panic that drove her family out of Nazi Germany. [^1]

## From skeptic to true believer
On the task force she reversed course. The Ward Radio series presents her as the prime exhibit of the Utah dynamic in which skeptics joined the panickers (see [[Anti-Mormonism United Skeptics and Evangelicals Against the LDS Church]]). The episode links her conversion to emerging recovered-memory theories applied to Holocaust trauma — Mattis had been a young child when her family escaped the Nazis. See [[Noemi Mattis Turned From Skeptic to True Believer on the Ritual Abuse Task Force]]. [^2]

## Explaining away the missing evidence
Confronted with no bodies, skeletons, or death certificates, Mattis claimed "doctors and morticians were involved in Utah's satanic cults," explaining why bodies "were never found," and asserted ritual abuse is known through "the cumulative experience of many therapists across the world." See [[False Memory Reports Caused Documented Wrongful Convictions]]. [^3][^4]
