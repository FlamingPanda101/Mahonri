# Christopher Blythe

Latter-day Saint folklorist and historian of the schismatic Restoration movements, introduced on Ward Radio as a BYU professor of folklore ("basically just pioneer gossip"). His earliest articles were on the Cutlerites, and he has personally visited and interviewed members of the surviving factions — Cutlerite, Bickertonite, Strangite, RLDS/Community of Christ, and Hedrickite — and even worked a summer (2011) for the Community of Christ giving tours of Joseph Smith's homes. He hosts the podcast **Angels and Seer Stones**. He converted to the church at age 13 (1995) and discovered through a relative (RLDS historian Pearl Wilcox, his great-aunt) that his own 1845 ancestors had become RLDS.

## On the Six Factions
On [[Ward Radio - What Happened to the First 6 Factions of Mormonism]], Blythe narrates the fate of each post-1844 movement for hosts [[Cardon Ellis]] and the co-hosts. His recurring theses: that rejecting polygamy unravels sealing and the plan of salvation ([[Rejecting Polygamy Unravels Sealing and the Plan of Salvation]]); that the Cutlerites uniquely preserved the 1844 endowment ([[The Cutlerites Preserved the Endowment Outside Utah]]); that multi-generational growth is a rough test of a movement's durability ([[Growth Past the Second Generation Validates a Movement]]); and that James Strang's Letter of Appointment is a forgery ([[The Strangite Letter of Appointment Is a Forgery]]). His personal favorite faction is the Cutlerites. [^1]
