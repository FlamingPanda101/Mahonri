# Brother Oz (Austin)

## Who
**Brother Oz** (first name **Austin**) is an **evangelical Protestant apologist** and YouTuber who, in his own words, "does apologetics content and reacts to atheists."[^1] He signs off the conversation by inviting viewers to subscribe to his channel ("my name is brother Oz... go subscribe to my YouTube unless you're Mormon — don't, it's bad for my algorithm"), and host [[Hayden Carroll]] addresses him as "Austin."[^1] He says he lives in **BC** (British Columbia) and speaks Spanish in addition to English.[^2] He is **philosophically/metaphysically oriented** — "theologically obsessed... ever since I was a kid" — and repeatedly invokes [[Thomas Aquinas]], the Logos, and classical-theist categories; he is sympathetic to (but does not simply identify with) the Reformed presuppositionalism of [[Jeff Durbin]], [[James White]], Van Til, and [[Greg Bahnsen]], and admires [[Gavin Ortlund]] and Mike Winger.[^3]

## Why he matters here
In the conversation [[Lets Talk w Hayden Carroll - Evangelical Pastor and Mormon Clash on Scripture and God|"Evangelical Pastor and Mormon Clash on Scripture and God"]] Oz is the **evangelical voice**, defending biblical authority and the Trinity against Carroll's Latter-day Saint case. His method is **metaphysical and Logos-centered** rather than proof-texting: he grounds Scripture's authority in Christ → apostles → a fallible-but-real apostolic deposit, and grounds *all* meaning, ethics, and epistemology in a triune God who is "knower, knowledge, and known." Note: the channel framing calls him a "pastor," but the transcript does not establish a pastoral office — he self-describes only as an apologetics content-creator, so "pastor" should be treated as the video's label, not a verified role.

## Key arguments (linked concepts)
- [[Relationality Requires Three - The Trinitarian Grounding of Meaning]] — his master argument: love presupposes the number three; Christ as Logos grounds objective meaning.
- [[The Infinite Regress of Infallible Interpreters]] — his rebuttal to the "no final interpreter" charge.
- [[Suffering as the Frame That Displays the Painting]] — his aesthetic theodicy: all things, including hell, exhibit God's attributes.
- [[A Text Cannot Be Infallible Because Language Is Fallible]] — his "true but not precise" account of Scripture, conceding fallible transmission.

## Debate posture
Conversational, concessive on details (granting fallible translation/transmission and rejecting an *infallible* magisterium), but uncompromising on the metaphysical core — that meaning, identity, and morality collapse without a triune Logos. He frames the LDS Godhead as leaving an "ontological, epistemological void" and openly hopes Carroll will "become a Christian," while Carroll counters that Latter-day Saints already worship Christ. He criticizes Calvinist double-predestination yet, under pressure on the problem of evil, defends a "for the glory of God" theodicy that Carroll labels "very Calvinistic." See [[A Constrained God Solves the Problem of Evil Better Than Creedal Christianity]].
