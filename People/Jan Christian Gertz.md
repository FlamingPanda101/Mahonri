# Jan Christian Gertz

**Full name:** Jan Christian Gertz
**Role:** Professor of Old Testament
**Era:** Contemporary (active 2000s–present)
**Tradition:** Academic (University of Heidelberg, Germany)

## Bio

Jan Christian Gertz is professor of Old Testament at the University of Heidelberg, occupying the chair once held by Claus Westermann. He is a leading figure in contemporary European source criticism and redaction criticism of the Pentateuch. His Genesis 1–11 commentary in the Historical Commentary on the Old Testament (HCOT) series is a major scholarly resource.

Dan McClellan recommends Gertz's commentary: "You can check out Jan Christian Gertz's commentary from the Historical Commentary on the Old Testament series, Genesis 1–11." [^1]

## Genesis Source Criticism

Gertz's commentary systematically identifies and analyzes the Priestly (P) and non-Priestly sources in the Primeval History, including the flood narrative. His work represents the continuing vitality of the source-critical tradition in German-language scholarship, building on the foundations laid by Julius Wellhausen and carried forward by scholars like Westermann.
