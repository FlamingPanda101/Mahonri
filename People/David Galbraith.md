# David Galbraith

David Galbraith was a Latter-day Saint educator in Israel who headed the "Israel group" that preceded the BYU Jerusalem Center. In the conversion narrative of [[Avraham Gileadi]], Galbraith is the man Gileadi wrote to after seeing a Jerusalem Post ad, walking five miles each way to Bethany for eight weeks of instruction; recognizing in Galbraith "a man full of the spirit of the Lord," Gileadi was baptized by him in the Pool of Siloam.

## Relevance

He appears as a pivotal figure in [[Avraham Gileadi]]'s Jewish-to-Latter-day-Saint conversion story — the local Church representative through whom Gileadi entered the [[Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints]] in Jerusalem.
