# Daniel Kahneman

**Full Name:** Daniel Kahneman
**Era:** 1934–2024
**Role:** Psychologist; Nobel laureate in Economic Sciences (2002)

## Bio

Daniel Kahneman was an Israeli-American psychologist whose work with [[Amos Tversky]] on heuristics and cognitive biases founded behavioral economics. He is the author of *Thinking, Fast and Slow* (2011).

## Cited in this dialogue

[[Sam Harris]] invokes "the work of people like Kahneman and Tversky" to argue that our moral heuristics can be placed in frames that produce contradictory outcomes and so require correction. [[Jordan Peterson]] grants the heuristics are gameable but insists that being gameable into contradiction does not make them "deeply flawed," since they evolved because they "produce a reliable result" most of the time. See [[Empathy Does Not Scale - The Identifiable Victim Effect]].[^1]
