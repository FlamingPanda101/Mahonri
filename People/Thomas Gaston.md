# Thomas Gaston

**Role:** Scholar of early Christian thought; author on Platonism and the apologists
**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** Not Latter-day Saint (cited as an independent witness to Platonic influence)

## Bio

Thomas E. Gaston is the author of the paper **"The Influence of Platonism on the Early Apologists,"** quoted at length by [[Jacob Hansen]]'s Thoughtful Faith channel to argue that the synthesis of Christianity with Platonism "did not originate with the apostles." Gaston's thesis: Paul "rejects outright the idea that philosophy could be [a] means of revelation," yet "from the end of the second century, Platonism became ingrained in Christian discourse about God." Apologists like [[Justin Martyr]] "did not adopt Platonism cynically, but seem to have genuinely believed that Platonism was true," identifying "the Christian God with the God of the philosophers" and emphasizing divine transcendence — key shifts away from Paul's "imminent view of God."[^1]

Gaston's argument is used to support the Latter-day Saint claim that creedal theology reflects post-apostolic Hellenization. See:
