# N.T. Wright

Anglican New Testament scholar and former Bishop of Durham, among the most influential living Pauline and early-Christianity scholars; author of the multi-volume *Christian Origins and the Question of God*.

## Romans 3:2 — "Oracles," Not "Words" of God
Quoted by [[Trent Horn]] on [[Romans 3.2|Romans 3:2]]: "Nowhere else in early Christian writings are Israel's scripture designated as God's oracles. Israel was entrusted with what the gentile nations might have perceived as divine oracles." Wright thus distinguishes the *logia* (oracles/pronouncements) from the category "Scripture," undercutting the use of the verse to prove Scripture is "the words of God." See [[Romans 3.2 Says Oracles Logia Not Words Logos]] [^1].
