# Orson Hyde

Member of the LDS **Quorum of the Twelve Apostles** and a primary-source participant in the Adam-God controversy. Where fellow apostle [[Orson Pratt]] was the doctrine's lone public *opponent*, Hyde was one of its public *defenders* — historian [[Benjamin Park]] lists him alongside [[Wilford Woodruff]] and [[Heber C. Kimball]] as apostles who "both preached and defended it" [^1].

## Defending Brigham Young against Orson Pratt
Hyde's role in the dispute was to **rebut Pratt's dismissal of Adam-God by appeal to prophetic infallibility.** Park quotes him: "Will God suffer his mouthpiece to go in error? No, he would remove him and place another there. Brother Brigham may err in the price of a horse... but in the revelations from God, where is the man that has given 'thus saith the Lord' when it was not so? I cannot find one instance." Park glosses the move: Hyde held that "Brigham Young can't be wrong because he's prophet and God would not allow the prophets to go astray" — the very "mouthpiece cannot err" logic that the modern church's fallible-prophet framing rejects. See [[Adam-God Doctrine - What Brigham Young Taught]] and [[Was Adam-God Official Binding Teaching]] [^2].
