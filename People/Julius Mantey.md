# Julius Mantey

Baptist Greek grammarian, co-author of the *Manual Grammar of the Greek New Testament* (Dana & Mantey). In the 1950s Mantey proposed that the Greek preposition *eis* could be **causal** ("because of"), which would let "be baptized... *for* the forgiveness of sins" in [[Acts 2.38]] mean "be baptized *because* your sins are forgiven."

## Significance in the vault
Mantey is the source of the very argument [[Ryan (NeedGod)]] advances in the baptism debate. [[Joe Heschmeyer]] notes that Mantey was a Baptist explicitly trying to "explain away Acts 2:38," that Mantey himself admitted "none of the Greek lexicons agree with him," and that [[Ralph Marcus]] refuted his examples in the *Journal of Biblical Literature*. See [[The Causal Eis Reading of Acts 2.38 Lacks Greek Support]].
