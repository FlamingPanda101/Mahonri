# Aaron Shafovaloff

**Role:** Evangelical apologist and street/relational missionary to Latter-day Saints
**Era:** Contemporary (active 2000s–2020s)
**Tradition:** Reformed Baptist evangelical Protestant; self-described "creedal Christian" and "catholic evangelical"

## Bio
Aaron Shafovaloff is an evangelical apologist based in the Salt Lake City area (Draper, Utah) who has spent years in stranger-evangelism and relational dialogue with Latter-day Saints. A computer programmer by trade, he traces his calling to a seven-week college mission internship with Utah Partnerships for Christ that began with a first evangelism encounter at Temple Square. He volunteers with [[Mormonism Research Ministry]] and the Utah Christian Research Center, equipping evangelicals to converse with Latter-day Saints while "definitely proclaiming the Protestant gospel."[^1] He produced the 2014 documentary project *God Never Sinned* surveying LDS belief about whether the Father was once a sinful mortal.[^2]

He identifies as a Reformed Baptist who affirms the Nicene and Athanasian creeds and the Chalcedonian two-natures definition — "you could call me a catholic evangelical with respect to those historic creedal beliefs."[^3]

## Contribution in this debate
Against [[Jacob Hansen]] on the Maybe God podcast, Shafovaloff advances several distinctive lines:
1. **Same terms, different referents.** Shared vocabulary ("God," "grace," "saved") masks different definitions, so the real work is unpacking the substance behind the words — like Nicaea forcing definitions out of Arian biblical language. See [[Covenant Relationship Over Theology Quiz]] (contrasting frame) and the Source note.
2. **The diversity-tolerance test.** What a community *tolerates diversity over* reveals what it actually worships: LDS belief about whether the Father was once a sinful mortal is wildly diverse, evangelical belief that "God was always God" is zero-divergent. See [[The Diversity Test - What a Community Tolerates Reveals Its God]].
3. **True prophets cannot be theological disasters.** A signature principle distinguishing the evangelical from the LDS view of prophets. See [[True Prophets Cannot Be Theological Disasters]].
4. **Emptied, not expanded, canon.** The LDS framework lacks infallible books, so it has emptied the canon rather than added to it. See [[The LDS Tradition Emptied the Canon Rather Than Expanded It]].
5. **The Protestantism-in-the-Book-of-Mormon problem.** KJV New Testament language appears in texts set ~600 BC. See [[Why Is There So Much Protestantism in the Book of Mormon]].
6. **Radical receiving and self-authentication.** The church recognizes, not confers, canonical authority (drawing on [[Gavin Ortlund]] and [[Michael Krueger]]). See [[Self-Authenticating Canon - Feel It in Your Bones]].

On the nature of God he defends divine transcendence, aseity, and incomprehensibility — God as the *most high* who "is not a big one of us."
