# Parley P. Pratt

**Who:** Parley Parker Pratt (1807–1857), an early apostle of the Latter-day
Saint movement, missionary, poet, and one of the most influential early LDS
authors.

**Role in this debate:** Saints Unscripted notes that some scholars suggest a
parenthetical **addition** to the Book of Mormon text may have arisen from
conversations [[Joseph Smith]] had with Pratt. Critics seize on this to ask
whether Joseph "was allowing others to influence his translation" — undermining
the claim that each word came by direct revelatory power. The video's reply: such
small clarifying additions are consistent with the LDS view that revelation is
captured in imperfect human words and may be revisited. See
[[Parenthetical Additions to the Book of Mormon Reflect a Developing Translation]].

## On the Translation Process

The Pratt connection is offered (tentatively) as an explanation for clarifying
parenthetical text. The apologetic framing is that a developing,
spirit-over-letter model of translation makes collaborative refinement
unproblematic rather than damaging.
