# Alvin Smith

Alvin Smith (1798-1823) was the eldest brother of [[Joseph Smith]], who died at age 25. His death and the question of his eternal fate loom large in restoration origins: the much-repeated story holds that a Presbyterian minister taught the [[Lucy Mack Smith|Smith family]] that Alvin was damned for lacking the proper baptism/election — a perceived harshness that, in the traditional telling, helped motivate Joseph's later universalist and salvation-for-the-dead theology. [[Kyle Beshears]] argues this episode is misremembered, reconstructing it as a later shift to Calvinist election-and-reprobation preaching rather than a funeral insult. [^1]
