# Robin Collins

**Era:** Contemporary
**Role:** Philosopher of science and religion (Messiah University)

## Bio
Robin Collins is the foremost contemporary defender of the **likelihood** form of the fine-tuning argument: fine-tuning is much more expected under theism than under naturalism, so it confirms theism without claiming to prove it. His "The Teleological Argument" in *The Blackwell Companion to Natural Theology* is a standard academic statement of the case.

## Contribution in These Videos
[[Trent Horn]] cites Collins for the claim that the kind of *stable complexity* needed for embodied minds cannot be had from hydrogen gas alone — "contrary to what one might see on Star Trek, an intelligent life form cannot be composed merely of hydrogen gas; there is simply not enough stable complexity." This anchors Horn's reply to the "God could just make sentient hydrogen" objection. See [[The Weakless Universe Just Pushes the Fine-Tuning Back a Step]] and [[Electrons in Love - The Psychophysical-Laws Objection Rebutted]].
