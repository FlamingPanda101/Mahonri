# Martin Noth

**Era:** 1902–1968
**Tradition:** Christian (German Protestant)
**Role:** Old Testament scholar

## Bio
Martin Noth was a leading German Old Testament scholar best known for proposing the **Deuteronomistic History** hypothesis — that Deuteronomy through 2 Kings forms a unified history edited by a single school. [[Alex Arnold]] names him as an originator of the scholarly framework he says was unavailable in 1830.[^1]

## On the Deuteronomistic History
Noth's hypothesis (developed further by [[Frank Moore Cross]]) is the foundation of the claim that a specific scribal program systematically suppressed older theology around the time of [[Josiah]]'s reform. Arnold argues Joseph Smith could not have known this framework when writing [[1 Nephi 1.1|1 Nephi 1]]. See [[Deuteronomistic Reform Explains the Book of Mormon Theology]].[^1]
