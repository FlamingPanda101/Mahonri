# John C. Reeves

**Role:** Scholar of Second Temple, apocryphal, and Manichaean literature
**Tradition:** Non-LDS academic (cited as a neutral/outside expert)

## Bio

John C. Reeves is the scholar [[Jonah Barnes]] cites for the English translation of the **Apocalypse of Enosh** passage in the [[Mani|Cologne Mani Codex]]. On [[Cardon Ellis]]'s **Ward Radio**, Barnes attributes the rendering to Reeves' book ***Heralds of That Golden Realm***, **page 142**, emphasizing that Reeves is among the "non-member professional Greek experts" — i.e., not a Latter-day Saint with an apologetic motive.[^1]

## Why He Matters Here

His translation is the evidentiary backbone of [[The Cologne Mani Codex Parallels Moroni and the Brass Plates]]: it is Reeves' wording ("write down these hidden things … upon … bronze plates and deposit them in the wilderness … until my revelation is ready to be revealed to all the brethren") that the hosts read as echoing the [[Angel Moroni|Moroni]] / [[Nephi]] buried-plates motif. Because Reeves translated the codex long after Joseph Smith and with no LDS stake, the hosts present his rendering as immune to the charge of believer-bias.[^2]
