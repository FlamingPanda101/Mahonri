# Antonio Damasio

**Role:** Neuroscientist; researcher on emotion, consciousness, and decision-making
**Era:** Contemporary
**Field:** Neuroscience / cognitive science

## Bio

Antonio Damasio is a neuroscientist best known for the *somatic marker hypothesis* and for arguing, on the basis of laboratory and clinical studies, that emotion is not opposed to reason but is a necessary substrate of rational decision-making. Patients with damage to emotion-processing neural systems (e.g., the ventromedial prefrontal cortex) retain intelligence yet become incapable of making sound practical decisions about their own welfare.

## Relevance here

[[Blake Ostler]] cites Damasio (alongside [[Oliver Sacks]]) against the secular-materialist claim that spiritual experience is "mere emotion" and therefore untrustworthy. Ostler's point: persons lacking properly functioning emotions are incapable of fully cognizing or making rational decisions, so "reason presupposes and requires emotion, feeling and affect." The neural integration runs through structures such as the amygdala, the limbic system, and tonically active neurons in the caudate that feed emotional input from the amygdala into the reasoning of the prefrontal cortex.
