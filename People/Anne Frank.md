# Anne Frank

**Role:** German-Dutch Jewish diarist and Holocaust victim
**Era:** 1929–1945
**Known for:** *The Diary of a Young Girl*, written in hiding in Amsterdam before her family was discovered; died in the Bergen-Belsen concentration camp.

## Bio
Anne Frank was a Jewish teenager whose posthumously published diary became one of the most widely read first-person accounts of the Holocaust. She and her family hid from Nazi persecution for two years before being captured; she died in Bergen-Belsen at fifteen.

## In Apologetics
In [[Jacob Hansen]]'s debate over original sin and unconditional election, Anne Frank functions as the central **test case** for the justice of Calvinist soteriology. Because she was Jewish and (so far as the historical record shows) never accepted Christ, the Calvinist interlocutor concludes she was a "vessel of wrath" — even a "daughter of the devil" by the [[Matthew 13.36-43|wheat-and-tares]] binary — who deserved both the Holocaust and an eternity worse than it.[^1] Hansen uses the case to press his core intuition: a being who "tortures little girls who never did anything wrong" and is then called "all good" empties the word *good* of meaning. The example anchors [[The Wheat and Tares as Sons of the Devil Sown by Satan]] and [[The Calvinist God Cannot Love Because He Controls Everything]]; it parallels the newborn-infant case in the [[The Infant-Damnation Reductio Against Inherited Guilt Plus Required Profession|infant-damnation reductio]].
