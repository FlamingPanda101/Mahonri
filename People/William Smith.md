# William Smith

**Who:** William Smith (1811–1893), younger brother of the prophet [[Joseph Smith]] and an early member of the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles. As a member of the Smith family, his recollections are cited as testimony to the family's standing in the Palmyra–Manchester community before Joseph's revelations made them controversial. [^1]

## On the Smith Family's Reputation
[[David Snell]] invokes William Smith to supply the missing context behind the hostile [[Philastus Hurlbut]] affidavits: William said people were fine with the family "until Joseph had his vision and started talking about his revelations and then everybody just turned against us." This reframes the neighbors' harsh statements as a reaction to Joseph's prophetic claims rather than a neutral baseline judgment of the family's character. See [[Hurlbut Affidavits Need Their Hostile Context]] and [[Hurlbut Affidavits Bias Problem]]. [^1]
