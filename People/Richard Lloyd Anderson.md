# Richard Lloyd Anderson

**Who:** Richard Lloyd Anderson (1926–2018), Latter-day Saint historian and longtime professor at Brigham Young University, best known for the definitive biographical study of the Book of Mormon witnesses.

**Role in this source:** [[Daniel C. Peterson]] repeatedly recommends Anderson's *Investigating the Book of Mormon Witnesses* (Deseret Book, 1981) as "one of the great works of scholarship" in LDS history. Anderson analyzed each witness's pre-witness and post-witness career and concluded they came across as "believable, credible, sane, respectable people" — making any debunking "a big task."[^1]

## On the Witnesses' Credibility
Anderson's life-by-life method underwrites Peterson's argument that there is no evidence any witness was a liar, deceiver, or unstable.
