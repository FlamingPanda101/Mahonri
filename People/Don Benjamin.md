# Don Benjamin

**Role:** Old Testament and ancient Near East scholar
**Era:** Contemporary (active 1980s–2020s)
**Tradition:** Academic biblical studies

## Bio

Don C. Benjamin is a scholar of the Old Testament and ancient Near East. He co-authored with [[Victor Matthews]] the textbook *Old Testament Parallels: Laws and Stories from the Ancient Near East*, which places biblical texts in conversation with ancient Near Eastern literary parallels. The textbook notes that the Ebla text TM.75.G1682 follows a pattern "also found in the Enuma Elish stories from Babylon, a hymn to Ptah and a hymn to the Aten from Egypt as well as the creation of the heavens and the earth in the book of Genesis"[^1] — contextualizing the text within the broader ANE cosmogonic pattern of creation as ordering, not creation from nothing.
