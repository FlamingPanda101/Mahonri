# Simon Kochen

**Full Name:** Simon Bernhard Kochen
**Era:** 1934–present
**Field:** Mathematics and logic (model theory, foundations of quantum mechanics)
**Affiliation:** Princeton University (Henry Burchard Fine Professor emeritus)

## Bio

Simon Kochen is a mathematician and logician best known for the
**Kochen–Specker theorem** (1967), a cornerstone result showing that quantum
mechanics cannot be reproduced by non-contextual hidden-variable theories.

## Topics

### The Free Will Theorem
With [[John Conway]], Kochen proved the **Free Will Theorem** (2006, 2009),
arguing that if experimenters are free to choose measurement settings, then the
measured particles' responses are equally undetermined by prior history.
[[Inspiring Philosophy|Michael Jones]] presents the theorem as ruling out a
realist-deterministic universe in favor of an indeterministic one if free will
is to exist.[^1] (The transcript renders his name phonetically as "Cochen.")
