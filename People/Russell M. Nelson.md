# Russell M. Nelson

Seventeenth president of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. In the Informed Saints interview, Dyer invokes Nelson's teaching that "the greatest miracles will happen in the coming years" as a faith-based ground for optimism about religious recovery despite measured decline — framing the data's downturn as non-deterministic and reversible.[^1]

## Cited for Hope Amid Decline

Dyer pairs Nelson's statement with Robert Putnam's cyclical social-connectedness curve to argue that "we're down, but certainly we shouldn't count ourselves as out." See [[Demographic Indicators Predict a Bright LDS Future]] and [[Decline in Religion Tracks the Broader Collapse of Social Connectedness]].

## Target of satanic-panic accusations
The Ward Radio *Glenn L. Pace Memo* series (Part 4, "The Fever Breaks") closes by noting that Utah's "satanic hangover" was "amplified when the president of the church himself, Russell M. Nelson, became the target of the accusations" — the most recent reach of the recovered-memory SRA narrative into LDS leadership. See [[The Satanic Panic Reached the LDS Church in Utah]]. [^2]

## The Brenda Miles case and the cover-up claim (Part 5)
Nelson's daughter [[Brenda Miles]] and son-in-law [[Richard Miles]] were accused in the [[Marion Smith]] / ISAT satanic-ritual-abuse case shortly after Nelson was ordained to the Quorum of the Twelve in 1985. A 2018 relitigation named Nelson directly, alleging he pressured law enforcement and the FBI to quash the case; investigators [[Michael George]], [[Diana Stevens]], and [[Leslie Lewis]] testified there was no church interference, and the judge dismissed the case with prejudice. The modern "Skull and Bones" theory about Nelson rests on confusing the University of Utah's (publicly-listed) Owl and Key honor society with Yale's secret society. See [[No Evidence the LDS Church Suppressed the Investigations]], [[Accusations Cannot Transmute Into Evidence]], and [[The Nelson Skull and Bones Claim Confuses Utah's Owl and Key With Yale]]. [^3]

## Blamed for the Adams abuse case (Part 6)
Part 6 documents how journalist [[Michael Rezendes]] framed the [[Paul Adams]] abuse case — in which an inactive lay member abused his own daughters and the bishop urged reporting — as Nelson's institutional failure, and how the [[We Are the People Utah]] podcast folds it into the satanic-ritual-abuse narrative. See [[The Spotlight Analogy Equates a Celibate Cardinal With a Volunteer Bishop]]. [^4]
