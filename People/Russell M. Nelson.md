# Russell M. Nelson

Seventeenth president of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. In the Informed Saints interview, Dyer invokes Nelson's teaching that "the greatest miracles will happen in the coming years" as a faith-based ground for optimism about religious recovery despite measured decline — framing the data's downturn as non-deterministic and reversible.[^1]

## Cited for Hope Amid Decline

Dyer pairs Nelson's statement with Robert Putnam's cyclical social-connectedness curve to argue that "we're down, but certainly we shouldn't count ourselves as out." See [[Demographic Indicators Predict a Bright LDS Future]] and [[Decline in Religion Tracks the Broader Collapse of Social Connectedness]].
