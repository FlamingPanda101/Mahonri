# Nicodemus

A Pharisee and "ruler of the Jews" (a member of the Sanhedrin) who came to Jesus by night in [[John 3.5|John 3]]. Jesus tells him a person must be "born again" / "born from above," and when Nicodemus misunderstands this as a second physical birth, Jesus clarifies that one must be "born of water and of the Spirit."

## Significance in the vault
The Nicodemus dialogue is the battleground for [[John 3.5|John 3:5]] in the baptism debate. [[Joe Heschmeyer]] argues "born of water and the Spirit" is a clear reference to water baptism (the universal reading "for centuries"), and that Jesus expected Nicodemus, "a teacher of Israel," to recognize the water-and-Spirit motif from the Old Testament (e.g. [[Ezekiel 36]]). [[Ryan (NeedGod)]] counters that the passage never uses the word *baptism* and that "born of water" means either natural birth or the [[Ezekiel 36]] sprinkling of clean water (forgiveness of sins), not the ordinance. See [[John 3.5 Born of Water and the Spirit Is Disputed Between Baptism and Spiritual Rebirth]].
