# Paul Y. Hoskisson

**Era:** 20th–21st century
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint (LDS)
**Role:** Assyriologist; director of the Book of Mormon Onomasticon

## Bio
Paul Y. Hoskisson holds a Ph.D. in ancient Near Eastern studies and is an emeritus BYU professor who directed the **Book of Mormon Onomasticon**, a scholarly catalog of every proper name in the text with proposed etymologies.[^1]

## Book of Mormon Names
[[Matthew Bowen]] credits Hoskisson with arguing that **Alma** means "lad" (see [[Alma Means Young Man - Wordplay on Hiddenness]]), with the analysis that the *-ish* element in **Abish** means "man" (see [[Abish Means My Father Is a Man - Vision of Her Father]]), and with a proposed reading of **Pacumeni** as *Pa-kem*, "the blind one" (see [[Pahoran Paanchi Pacumeni - Egyptian Names in One Chapter]]).[^1] Bowen notes Hoskisson's methodological caution — preferring a real attestation over a reconstruction, as with [[Sariah Attested as an Ancient Female Name|Sariah]].
