# Shalom Spiegel

**Role:** Scholar of medieval Hebrew literature; author of *The Last Trial*
**Era:** 1899–1984
**Tradition:** Jewish (Jewish Theological Seminary)

## Bio
Shalom Spiegel was a professor of medieval Hebrew literature at the Jewish Theological Seminary. His monograph ***The Last Trial*** (Hebrew *Me-Aggadot ha-Akedah*; English translation by Judah Goldin, 1967) gathers the rabbinic and piyyut traditions in which [[Isaac]] is **actually slaughtered and burned to ashes** on the altar and then revived by the "dew of resurrection" — arguing these are a genuine, ancient strand of Jewish memory ("the blood of Isaac" bought Israel's chosenness), not merely a polemical borrowing from Christianity.

## Significance in the Ward Radio argument
[[Jonah Barnes]] leans on Spiegel to support the claim that the "real" Akedah — in which Isaac dies and is resurrected — was the original messianic-resurrection story that the [[Josiah|Deuteronomist]] editors covered up in [[Genesis 22]]. See [[Joseph Smith Restored a Messianic Akedah in Which Isaac Was Actually Sacrificed]].
