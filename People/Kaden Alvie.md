# Kaden Alvie

**Role:** Latter-day Saint commentator / discussion participant (per the referencing notes)
**Era:** Contemporary

## Bio

Kaden Alvie could not be independently identified beyond the notes that cite him; this is a short stub. In the vault he appears as a Latter-day Saint commentator and discussion participant — associated with the "All Those In Favor" conversations — who relays and applies the work of credentialed scholars rather than advancing primary research of his own. He is noted in one instance as "helping a BYU professor develop" an argument.

## Relevance to the vault

Alvie is cited as the voice carrying several scholarly arguments into the vault's apologetics. In [[Alma 32 Light Alludes to Genesis 1 Creation]] he relays [[David Bokovoy]]'s reading of Alma 32 as an allusion to the Genesis 1 creation account. In [[The Uncreated Element Is Only the Most Fundamental Substrate]] he advances the reading that Joseph Smith's "uncreated elements" mean only the *most fundamental* physical stratum, citing [[J.P. Moreland]] on a possible pre-Big-Bang "quantum foam." He also reaches for [[Moroni 8.18|Moroni 8:18]] as scriptural support for divine immutability against the charge that Latter-day Saints teach a changing God.
