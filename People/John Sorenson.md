# John L. Sorenson (1924–2021)

**Era:** 20th–21st century
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint (LDS)
**Role:** Anthropologist; Book of Mormon geography scholar

## Bio
John L. Sorenson was a Latter-day Saint anthropologist (Ph.D., UCLA) and emeritus BYU professor, best known for developing the **Mesoamerican limited-geography model** of the Book of Mormon (*An Ancient American Setting for the Book of Mormon*; *Mormon's Codex*).[^1]

## Book of Mormon Names
[[Matthew Bowen]] cites Sorenson, with [[Paul Hoskisson]], for the suggestion that **Pacumeni** might derive from *Pa-kem*, "the blind one."[^1] See [[Pahoran Paanchi Pacumeni - Egyptian Names in One Chapter]]. Bowen also recalls Sorenson's older *Ensign* article asking whether the Book of Mormon, if written by Hebrew-speakers, reflects the Hebrew language — the very question Bowen's onomastic program pursues. See [[Book of Mormon Names Show Authorial Wordplay]].
