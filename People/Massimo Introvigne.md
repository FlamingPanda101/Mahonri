# Massimo Introvigne

Italian sociologist of religion cited by the Ward Radio series as the historian who best explains why the satanic panic broke differently in Utah. He is quoted observing that "when … the Satanism scare hit Mormon country, rationalist and post-rationalist counter-Mormons did not divide along predictable lines … All evangelical counter-Mormons accepted the reality of the satanic conspiracy." [^1]

## On why Utah was unique
Introvigne supplies the central explanatory frame of [[Anti-Mormonism United Skeptics and Evangelicals Against the LDS Church]]: shared hostility to the LDS Church collapsed the usual skeptic-vs-believer divide that elsewhere checked the panic. [^1]

## On the breeder myth
He is also quoted on the absence of evidence for ritual "breeding": "the local police and FBI, despite extensive investigations, have never found the slightest evidence of breeding practices having actually taken place." See [[The Breeder Myth Was Invented to Explain Missing Bodies]]. [^2]
