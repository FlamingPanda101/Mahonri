# Antony Flew

**Role:** Analytic philosopher
**Era:** 1923–2010
**Tradition:** Atheist for most of his career (later a deist)

## Bio

Antony Flew was a leading British analytic philosopher and, for most of his life, the foremost academic defender of atheism — best known for his 1955 essay "Theology and Falsification." (Late in life, in *There Is a God*, he announced he had come to accept a deistic creator on design grounds.)

## The divine-complicity argument

In [[David Paulsen]]'s talk, Flew supplies the **strongest form of the logical problem of evil**. Where [[David Hume|Hume]]'s formulation rests only on God's goodness and power, Flew folds in omniscience and creation, concluding that "an omnipotent, omniscient God must be an **accessory before (and during) the fact to every human misdeed**, as well as being responsible for every non-moral defect in the universe." [^1] Paulsen treats this as the benchmark the Restoration must answer — and argues it succeeds precisely by denying absolute creation. See [[Flew's Divine Complicity Argument]].
