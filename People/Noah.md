# Noah

The patriarch of the Flood narrative (Genesis 6–9). In the priesthood-lineage scheme of the [[Book of Abraham]] and the [[Grammar and Alphabet of the Egyptian Language]], blessings descend "from under the hand of Noah" — kingly blessings to the line of [[Ham]], priestly blessings to the line of Shem. After the Flood, Genesis 9 records the curse on Canaan, the seed of later "[[curse of Ham]]" readings.

## In This Source
[[Don Bradley]] also cites Noah among the patriarchs whose human failings (drunkenness after the Flood, Genesis 9:21) show that "even the people that God chooses are very, very human" — pointing beyond themselves to Christ. See [[Prophets Point to Christ Through Their Failings]] and [[The Curse as Division of Labor Not Punishment]].
