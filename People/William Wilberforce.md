# William Wilberforce

**Era:** 1759–1833
**Tradition:** Christian (evangelical Anglican)
**Role:** British MP and leader of the parliamentary abolition campaign

## Bio

William Wilberforce led the parliamentary campaign that abolished the British slave trade (1807) and slavery (1833), driven by deep evangelical conviction.[^1]

## Topics

### Christian Abolitionism in the UK
[[Glenn Scrivener]] cites Wilberforce — and the friend who urged him to bring abolition to Parliament after writing a Latin essay on whether it is right to own humans and feeling "God had spoken to him" — as the clearest case of Christian-motivated abolition. [[Alex OConnor|Alex O'Connor]] concedes the UK case is "profoundly Christian" but argues abolition in France and Haiti was not. See [[Abolition Tracks Material Conditions Not Christian Conviction]] and [[Christianity Abolished Slavery Twice]].[^1]
