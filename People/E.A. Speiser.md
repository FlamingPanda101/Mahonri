# E.A. Speiser

**Full name:** Ephraim Avigdor Speiser (1902–1965)
**Role:** Assyriologist and biblical scholar
**Era:** 20th century
**Tradition:** Academic (Jewish)

## Bio

E.A. Speiser was one of the leading Assyriologists and biblical scholars of the mid-20th century. He served as chairman of the Department of Oriental Studies at the University of Pennsylvania and was a key figure in integrating ancient Near Eastern studies with biblical scholarship. His Anchor Bible commentary on Genesis (1964) remains a standard reference.

## Genesis 1:1 as Temporal Clause

Speiser argued influentially that the Hebrew of [[Genesis 1.1|Genesis 1:1]] is properly read as a temporal clause rather than an independent statement of creation from nothing:

- The unpointed Hebrew can be read: "When God began to create the heavens and the earth — the earth being formless and void..."
- On this reading, verse 2 describes the STARTING CONDITION, not the result of a prior act
- This reading is consistent with the broader ANE cosmogonic pattern where creation involves ordering pre-existent chaotic elements rather than generating matter from nothing

Speiser's grammatical argument supports the scholarly consensus that Genesis 1, in its original context, does not teach creation ex nihilo.
