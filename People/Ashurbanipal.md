# Ashurbanipal

**Role:** King of Assyria; builder of the great library at Nineveh
**Era:** Neo-Assyrian Empire (reigned c. 669–631 BCE)
**Tradition:** Ancient Near East (Assyria)

## Bio

Ashurbanipal was a king of the Neo-Assyrian Empire, son of [[Esarhaddon]] and grandson of [[Sennacherib]], reigning over Assyria at the apex of its power. He is famous for assembling the **Library of Ashurbanipal** at Nineveh, a vast collection of cuneiform tablets — including the *Epic of Gilgamesh* and many literary, omen, and administrative texts — whose recovery in the nineteenth century transformed the modern study of the ancient Near East. Before his accession he was named crown prince by Esarhaddon, and the loyalty oath securing that succession survives as a key Assyrian treaty document.

## Relevance to the vault

Ashurbanipal appears inside the **Vassal Treaty (succession oath) of Esarhaddon** of 672 BCE, the text the vault discusses as the literary template behind [[Deuteronomy]]. Section 24 of that treaty commands the vassals to "love" crown prince Ashurbanipal "like yourselves" — and [[Dan McClellan]] points to this as the very covenant-love idiom of the Shema in [[Deuteronomy 6.4|Deut 6:4]] ("you shall love the LORD your God with all your heart, might, and strength") and of the "as yourself" of the second great commandment. He is thus a concrete data point in the argument that Deuteronomy's covenant language is patterned on Assyrian vassalage. See [[Deuteronomy Was Patterned After the Vassal Treaty of Esarhaddon]].
