# Moses

**Role:** Israelite prophet and leader; mediator of the Exodus
**Era:** Narrative setting in the second millennium BCE (story composed centuries later)
**Tradition:** Hebrew Bible ([[Exodus]] and the Torah)

## Bio
Moses is the central human figure of the Exodus narrative — the prophet through whom YHWH confronts [[Pharaoh]], announces the plagues, and leads Israel out of Egypt. In the hardening texts, God repeatedly speaks *to Moses*, instructing him in advance that Pharaoh's heart will be hardened so that God's wonders may be multiplied. Moses is also the figure to whom God reveals the divine name (see [[Exodus 3.14|Exodus 3:14]]).

## Role in the Hardened-Heart Narrative
God's hardening declarations are delivered as private speech to Moses ("And the LORD said unto Moses..."), framing the plague cycle as a scripted demonstration. McClellan notes the stated purpose is partly for Moses himself — that he and later generations would "tell your children and grandchildren" and "know that I am the Lord."
