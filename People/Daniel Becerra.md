# Daniel Becerra

Latter-day Saint scholar of early Christianity and patristics (PhD, Duke University; faculty, BYU ancient scripture), specializing in early Christian asceticism, moral formation, and ethics.

## Cited in *Ancient Christians*
Becerra authored the chapter "Becoming Like God: Incarnation, Moral Formation, and Eternal Progression," the volume's sustained treatment of patristic **theosis/deification**. [^1] He organizes deification into three phases — a past event (the deifying effect of Christ's Incarnation), a present process (moral formation / overcoming the passions), and a future possibility (eternal progression after death). He traces the exchange formula from Justin Martyr through Irenaeus, Athanasius, and Gregory of Nazianzus (who coined *theosis*), and contrasts Origen's bounded progression with Gregory of Nyssa's never-ending *epektasis*. Notably, Becerra candidly identifies where patristic deification and LDS exaltation diverge: the fathers held God and humans to be **different species**, not the same kind at different stages. See [[Christ Became Man That We Might Become God - The Exchange Formula]], [[Same-Species-as-God as the Core LDS Distinctive in Theosis]], [[Gregory of Nyssa's Epektasis - Eternal Progression Toward God]].
