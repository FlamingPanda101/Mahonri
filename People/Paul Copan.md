# Paul Copan

**Role:** Evangelical philosopher, theologian, and apologist

## Bio

Paul Copan is an evangelical analytic philosopher and theologian (Pledger Family Chair of Philosophy and Ethics, Palm Beach Atlantic University) known for popular-level apologetics and for defending *creatio ex nihilo*. Together with [[William Lane Craig]] he authored the two works reviewed in [[Blake Ostler - Out of Nothing A History of Creation ex Nihilo|Ostler's *Out of Nothing*]]: the chapter "Craftsman or Creator? An Examination of the Mormon Doctrine of Creation and a Defense of Creatio ex nihilo" (in *The New Mormon Challenge*, 2002) and the book *Creation out of Nothing: A Biblical, Philosophical, and Scientific Exploration* (Baker, 2004). The biblical-exegetical sections of these works largely reproduce Copan's earlier article "Is Creatio ex Nihilo a Post-Biblical Invention? An Examination of Gerhard May's Proposal" (*Trinity Journal*, 1996), which responds to [[Gerhard May]].[^1]

## Position on Creation Ex Nihilo

Copan and Craig argue that creation out of nothing is biblical and was the established belief of the earliest church, not a later philosophical development. [[Blake Ostler]] argues their case rests on two question-begging assumptions — that "create" means *ex nihilo*, and that texts describing organization of matter still "implicitly" assume a prior creation from nothing — and on an *ad hoc* "two-stage creation" device applied to text after text.[^2] See [[Copan and Craig Use a Debaters Brief That Begs the Question]] and [[Two-Stage Creation Is an Ad Hoc Rescue Device]].
