# David Peterson

**Note on identity:** This is the Old Testament scholar (commonly David L. Petersen) cited in McClellan's video, distinct from [[Daniel C. Peterson]], the LDS apologist and Islamic-studies professor at BYU.

**Role:** Old Testament scholar; biblical commentator
**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** Academic biblical studies (historical-critical)

## Bio

David Peterson is the author of the Genesis volume in the **Old Testament Library** commentary series (published 2024/2025), described by Dan McClellan as the most recent of five leading critical Genesis commentaries he surveys. His treatment of Genesis 2:4 represents the mainstream critical reading of the verse as a redactional transition between two distinct creation accounts.[^1]

## On the Genesis 2:4a Toledot Formula

Peterson reads Genesis 2:4a as a redactional bridge: the half-verse "was introduced as a transition between the priestly and pre-priestly accounts of creation and as an introduction to the latter." He notes the *toledot* ("descendants/successors") formula appears 11 times in Genesis, and in *all* instances "the formula stands immediately before the material it introduces" — so 2:4a introduces what *follows* (the garden narrative), identifying humanity as the "progeny of the heavens and the earth." McClellan reads Peterson as probably favoring the *later-redactor* origin for 2:4a. See [[Toledot Formula Is a Redactional Bridge Not a Summary]].[^1]
