# Philo of Alexandria

**Who:** Hellenistic Jewish philosopher from Alexandria, Egypt (c.20 BCE – 50 CE). Sought to harmonize Jewish scripture with Greek philosophy (particularly Platonism and Stoicism).

**Role:** Key source for understanding the *logos* (Word) concept in 1st-century Judaism. Philo described the *logos* as both an extension/emanation of God and as a "second God" — a concept parallel to and potentially influential on the [[John 1-1 - Qualitative Theos (And the Word Was God)|prologue of John]].

## The Logos as Emanation and Second God
[[Dan McClellan]] cites Philo as evidence that the *logos* theology in John's prologue draws on Hellenistic Jewish philosophical categories rather than later Trinitarian ones. Philo's *logos* is "an extension of God but also referred to as a second God" — a semi-autonomous divine agency, not a co-equal person of a triune being.[^1]

## Relevance to John's Christology
Philo demonstrates that 1st-century Jewish thinkers could speak of a divine figure subordinate to and emanating from the one God without violating Jewish monotheism — the conceptual space John's Christology occupies. See [[Divine Name Theology]].
