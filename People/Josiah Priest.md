# Josiah Priest

Josiah Priest was a popular early-19th-century American author of speculative antiquarian and historical works. He was one of several contemporaries of [[Ethan Smith]] who circulated the [[Hebraic-Indian theory]] — the idea that Native Americans descend from ancient Israelites. See [[The Hebraic-Indian Theory Was a Common Period Idea]].[^1]

## Relevance to the Book of Mormon Debate

Priest matters to the *View of the Hebrews* discussion for a second reason: when [[Joseph Smith]], as editor of the *Times and Seasons* in 1842, cited *View of the Hebrews* as *evidence for* the [[Book of Mormon]], he did so **secondhand through Josiah Priest** — quoting Priest, who in turn quoted excerpts of Ethan Smith. Smoot reads this as evidence that Joseph's exposure to *View of the Hebrews* was secondary, not the intimate firsthand familiarity a plagiarist would require. See [[Joseph Smith Cited View of the Hebrews as Evidence]].[^2]
