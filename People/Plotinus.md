# Plotinus
**Role:** Neo-Platonist philosopher (founder of Neo-Platonism)
**Era:** c. 204–270 AD

## Bio
Plotinus was the third-century philosopher who founded the Neo-Platonist tradition, developing the philosophy of [[Plato]] into a systematic metaphysics centered on an absolutely simple, transcendent first principle he called "the One." [^1] In Edward Feser's *Five Proofs of the Existence of God*, the second proof is named "the Neo-Platonic Proof" precisely because it reasons, with Plotinus, from the composite character of ordinary things to a noncomposite ultimate source — "a cause which, following the Neo-Platonic philosopher Plotinus, we might call the One." [^1] Feser notes that Plotinus and other Neo-Platonists located intellect in a second divine reality *after* the One, a detail one need not adopt to embrace the general Neo-Platonic strategy for arguing to God's existence.
