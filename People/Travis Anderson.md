# Travis Anderson

**Era:** Contemporary (2020s)
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint (LDS)
**Role:** Apologist, content creator

## Bio
Travis Anderson is an LDS apologist associated with the "Restored Truth" content brand, cited repeatedly by [[Hayden Carroll]] as a model practitioner of presuppositional-style apologetics and Old Testament divine-council arguments.

## Topics

### "Baseless Presupposition" Line
Carroll credits Anderson with a "golden line" for handling unjustified assumptions: "Yeah, you can believe that, but that's just a baseless presupposition" — and the related "so you believe it because feelings?" See [[I'm Right So You're Wrong - The Hidden Presupposition Pattern]], [[The Three Unjustified Protestant Canon Presuppositions]].[^1]

### Deuteronomy 32 and the Dead Sea Scrolls
Carroll points to Anderson / Restored Truth for popularizing the Dead Sea Scrolls reading of [[Deuteronomy 32.8-9|Deuteronomy 32:8-9]] (Yahweh as a son of the Most High) as evidence of early Israelite monolatry. See [[Deuteronomy 32 Dead Sea Scrolls Monolatry]].[^2]

### Univocality
Carroll notes Anderson does "a really good job talking about univocality" — the assumption that the Bible speaks with one voice — as an unjustified Protestant presupposition. See [[The Bible Is a Library Not Univocal]].[^3]
