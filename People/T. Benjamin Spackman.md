# T. Benjamin Spackman

**Role:** Historian of religion; 2022–2023 Mormon Studies Fellow, University of Utah; editor and contributor, *The Restored Gospel of Jesus Christ and Evolution*
**Field:** Near Eastern Studies (Semitics); American religious history; LDS creation/evolution thought
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint scholarship

## Credentials

T. Benjamin Spackman graduated from BYU in Near Eastern Studies, received an MA and completed PhD coursework in Near Eastern Languages and Literature (Semitics) at the University of Chicago, and completed a PhD in American Religious History at Claremont Graduate University. His dissertation, *"The Scientist Is Wrong": Joseph Fielding Smith, George McCready Price, and the Ascendency of Creationist Thought among Latter-day Saints in the Twentieth Century*, explores the intellectual roots of twentieth-century LDS creation/evolution conflict. He writes at BenSpackman.com. [^1]

**Why cited / authority basis:** A credentialed historian of religion and Semitics scholar tracing the intellectual history of LDS scriptural interpretation. Authority is *B* (scholarly secondary), based on synthesis of primary archival sources rather than primary witness.

## Contribution

Spackman wrote "(No) Death before the Fall? The Basis and Twentieth-Century History of Interpretation." He argues that [[No Death Before the Fall Is a Twentieth-Century Interpretation Not Settled Doctrine|"no death before the Fall" (NDBF) is not settled LDS doctrine]] but the product of three interpretive assumptions — [[Concordism Assumes Scripture and Science Describe the Same Thing|concordism]], [[Democratic Perspicuity Denies the Need for Context|perspicuity]], and inerrancy — that Joseph Fielding Smith held but that Talmage, Widtsoe, Clark, McKay, and multiple First Presidencies rejected. He frames interpretation as [[Interpretive Assumptions Are the Glasses We Read Scripture Through|a set of "glasses" (assumptions)]] and commends the principle of [[Divine Accommodation Means Revelation Meets Humans in Their Context|divine accommodation]].
