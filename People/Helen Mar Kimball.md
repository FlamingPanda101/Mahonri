# Helen Mar Kimball

**Who:** Helen Mar Kimball (1828–1896), later Helen Mar Kimball Whitney, was the daughter of LDS apostle [[Heber C. Kimball]]. She was sealed to [[Joseph Smith]] as a plural wife in 1843 when she was "just shy of 15 years old." After Smith's death, she married Horace Whitney and became a prominent figure in LDS women's organizations. She wrote autobiographical works including *Why We Practice Plural Marriage* (1884) and *Plural Marriage as Taught by the Prophet Joseph Smith*.

**Role:** Kimball is a key figure in debates about early LDS polygamy. Her sealing to Joseph Smith at age 14 is one of the most-cited examples by critics who accuse Smith of predatory behavior.

## Sealing to Joseph Smith
Kimball's sealing to Smith occurred at her father [[Heber C. Kimball]]'s request. LDS apologetics frames this as a dynastic sealing — intended to bind the Smith and Kimball families together — rather than a consummated sexual marriage. See [[Joseph Smith and Helen Mar Kimball Sealing]].

## Historical Context of Her Age
While 14 was young even by 1840s standards, marriage in the mid-teens was not uncommon in rural 19th-century America (roughly 1 in 10–15 marriages). The concept of "teenager" as a distinct life stage did not exist; adulthood was marked by puberty, not a specific age. See [[Nineteenth-Century Marriage Age Norms]].
