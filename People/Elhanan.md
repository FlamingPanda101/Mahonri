# Elhanan

**Who:** A Bethlehemite warrior in [[David]]'s service, named in [[2 Samuel 21.19|2 Samuel 21:19]] as the man who killed Goliath the Gittite, "the shaft of whose spear was like a weaver's beam." The qualifying detail about the spear is lifted verbatim from the description of Goliath in 1 Samuel 17:7, making it clear the same giant is in view.

**Role in the contradiction:** In the earliest preserved tradition, Elhanan — not David — is the slayer of Goliath. The famous David-and-Goliath story of [[1 Samuel]] 17 is, on the academic consensus, a later retelling that transferred Elhanan's victory to David as the David legend developed. The Chronicler (or his source) then altered the text of [[1 Chronicles 20.5|1 Chronicles 20:5]] so that Elhanan kills "Lahmi the brother of Goliath," obscuring the clash with the David narrative. See [[Elhanan Killed Goliath - Chronicles Harmonized the Contradiction]].
