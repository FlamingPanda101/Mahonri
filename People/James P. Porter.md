# James P. Porter

**Full name:** James P. Porter
**Role:** Comparative physiologist; former dean, BYU College of Life Sciences (2015–2022)
**Era:** Contemporary (career 1980s–2023)
**Tradition:** The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (LDS)

## Bio

James P. Porter earned BS and MS degrees in zoology from BYU (1974, 1976) and a PhD in
endocrinology from the University of California, San Francisco (1982). After a postdoctoral
fellowship at the University of Iowa and a faculty position at the University of Louisville
School of Medicine, he joined BYU's Department of Zoology in 1998, served as dean of the
College of Life Sciences from 2015 to 2022, and retired in 2023.[^1] His research field is
physiology — the relationship between body form and function.

## On Teaching Evolution at BYU

As dean, Porter answered inquiries (some forwarded from Church leaders and university
president Kevin J Worthen) asking why BYU teaches evolution. He chose not to argue
item-by-item but to justify *why students need to learn evolution* — which he fully
supports — while assuring writers that BYU professors teach it believing God is the
Creator.[^2] Candidly admitting his own lingering discomfort with universal common
ancestry, Porter argues it is not evolution but the *forced choice* between faith and
science that triggers faith crises, and that students should be given space to grapple "by
study and also by faith." See [[The Real Faith Crisis Is Being Forced to Choose Between Faith and Science]].[^3]
