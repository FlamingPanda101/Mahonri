# John Locke

**Era:** 1632–1704
**Tradition:** Christian (latitudinarian Anglican)
**Role:** English Enlightenment philosopher

## Bio

John Locke was an English philosopher whose theory of natural rights and government by consent profoundly shaped [[Thomas Jefferson]] and the American founding. In the transcript his name is mis-rendered "Lockach."[^1]

## Topics

### Biblical Grounding of Rights
[[Glenn Scrivener]] argues Locke derived his rights language from [[Genesis 1.26-27|Genesis 1-2]] and the image of God, and from medieval canon-law theology — "speaking in these absolute terms about the rights of man that are grounded in natural law... getting all this language from medieval theologians." Jefferson then secularized Locke. See [[Genesis 1 Image of God as Seedbed of Rights]] and [[Rights Talk Traces to Medieval Canon Lawyers]].[^1]

### Intolerance of Atheists (O'Connor's Counter)
[[Alex OConnor|Alex O'Connor]] counters that Locke thought atheists could not be trusted and could not make legally binding agreements — "so much for freedom of religion" — undercutting the claim that Locke's Christianity produced modern religious liberty. See [[Rights as Self-Interest Asserted Against Power Not Christian Blossoming]].[^2]
