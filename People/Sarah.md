# Sarah

**Role:** Wife of [[Abraham]]; mother of [[Isaac]]
**Era:** Patriarchal period (biblical setting)
**Tradition:** Hebrew Bible / Old Testament matriarch

## Bio
Sarah (Sarai) is Abraham's wife, who bore [[Isaac]] in her old age after the promise of [[Genesis 22|Genesis]]. She insisted that [[Hagar]] and her son [[Ishmael (son of Abraham)|Ishmael]] be sent away (Genesis 21), the background to the "only son" language of the Akedah. In the Ward Radio discussion of the binding of Isaac, the hosts note (half-jokingly) that Sarah is absent from the Akedah narrative — "there are no moms in any of these stories" — leaving Abraham and Isaac to act alone.
