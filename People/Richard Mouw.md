# Richard Mouw

**Role:** Evangelical philosopher/theologian; President Emeritus, Fuller Theological Seminary
**Tradition:** Evangelical Protestant (Reformed)

## Bio
Richard J. Mouw is a prominent evangelical philosopher and ethicist. He taught at **Calvin College** and served for two decades as **president of Fuller Theological Seminary** — described in [[Informed Saints - Do Mormons Believe in the Trinity]] as "a flagship of evangelical Christianity," or at least a particularly open and embracing strand of it.

## Bridge-Building with Latter-day Saints
Mouw is best known in an LDS context as a leading **bridge-builder** between evangelicals and Latter-day Saints, having taken significant personal and professional risk among his evangelical colleagues to do so. [[Grant Underwood]] recounts that Mouw publicly worked to retire the "cult" terminology applied to Mormons and to dismantle the "mean-spirited animus" of the anti-cult movement, investing time and personal capital "to build bridges rather than reinforce walls." Mouw endorsed Underwood's *Latter-day Saint Theology Among Christian Theologies* (his blurb appears on the back cover), and Underwood — who hosted a Mouw lecture during his tenure in the Richard L. Evans Chair — paid him the tribute, "when I grow up, I want to be a Christian like Rich Mouw." [^1]
