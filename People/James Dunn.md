# James Dunn

British New Testament scholar (Durham University) and a leading proponent of the **New Perspective on Paul**. In *The Theology of Paul the Apostle*, Dunn argues that Paul can simultaneously commend good works and condemn "works of the law," sometimes calling both simply "works," so that context must decide which is meant.

## Significance in the vault
[[Joe Heschmeyer]] enlists Dunn (alongside Matthew J. Thomas) to argue that Paul's polemic against "works" targets **boundary-marking works of the Mosaic law** (especially circumcision), not human action or obedience as such — undercutting [[Ryan (NeedGod)|Ryan]]'s claim that *any* deed (including baptism) gives ground for boasting. On this reading, Ephesians 2:9 ("not of works") and Ephesians 2:10 ("created... for good works") are not in tension. See [[Works of the Law Means Mosaic Boundary Markers Not Obedience as Such]].
