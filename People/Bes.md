# Bes

**Role:** Egyptian protective household deity
**Era:** Ancient Near East / pharaonic Egypt
**Tradition:** Egyptian religion

## Bio

Bes is an ancient Egyptian apotropaic god, depicted as a squat, bandy-legged dwarf with a lion-like or grotesque bearded face, often shown frontally (unusual in Egyptian art). He was a popular protective deity of households, childbirth, mothers, and children, and his image spread widely across the eastern Mediterranean and into the Levant.

## Relevance to the vault

Bes is referenced in the vault's debate over Israelite aniconism. The famous Kuntillet Ajrud "Yahweh and his Asherah" drawing includes figures with "Bes-style" (bovine-appearing) imagery that some scholars argue depict the deity — relevant to whether early Israel and Judah possessed divine images before aniconism developed. The vault treats both the genuineness of the iconography and the contested Yahweh identification as data points in the aniconism question.
