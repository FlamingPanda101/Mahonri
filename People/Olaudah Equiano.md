# Olaudah Equiano

**Era:** c. 1745 – 1797
**Tradition:** Christian (formerly enslaved)
**Role:** Abolitionist writer; author of *The Interesting Narrative*

## Bio

Olaudah Equiano was a formerly enslaved African who became a leading British abolitionist and author (mis-rendered "Ecuano" in the transcript).[^1]

## Topics

### Imago Dei Against Slavery
[[Glenn Scrivener]] cites Equiano's argument from [[Genesis 1.26-27|Genesis 1-2]] — that humanity was made to "have dominion rather than to be slaves," so "if God did not make man slave, how dare man make man slave" — as evidence the Bible's image-of-God theology fueled Black abolitionism. See [[The Old Testament Was a Hammer for Abolitionists]] and [[Genesis 1 Image of God as Seedbed of Rights]].[^1]
