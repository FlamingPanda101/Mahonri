# Josh Gehly

**Era:** Contemporary (2020s)
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint (LDS)
**Role:** Archaeologist (Penn State trained), author, content creator

## Bio
Josh Gehly is a Latter-day Saint researcher trained in anthropology/archaeology at Penn State and author of *Witnessing Miracles: Historical Evidence for the Resurrection and the Book of Mormon*. He presents archaeological and historical evidences for the Book of Mormon, appearing on Ward Radio alongside geologist [[Jerry Grover]].[^1]

## Nahom Is the Strongest Book of Mormon Evidence
Argues **Nahom** is the single best archaeological evidence for the Book of Mormon: a "shot in the dark" prediction (a foreigner, [[Ishmael]], dying and being buried at an already-named place) that converges with the NHM altars, tribal/place names, ancient maps, the eastward turn to Bountiful, and a funerary stela bearing Ishmael's name from the right era. He frames it against the maximalist/minimalist debates of biblical archaeology (Finkelstein, Dever) and claims Nahom "exceeds almost all biblical sites" — "this is what a bullseye looks like." See [[Nahom Exceeds Biblical Sites by Archaeologys Own Standards]], [[Nahom and NHM Converge with Nephis Eastward Turn]], and [[The Ishmael Funerary Stela in South Arabia]].[^1]
