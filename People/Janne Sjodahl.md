# Janne Sjodahl

Janne M. Sjodahl (1853–1939) was an early-twentieth-century Latter-day Saint scholar and writer. [[Keystone]] cites him for a practical experiment answering the recurring objection that the Book of Mormon could not have fit on the golden plates.

## The 1923 Hebrew Parchment Test
According to Keystone, Sjodahl in 1923 took a 7-by-8-inch piece of parchment and had a friend translate part of the Book of Mormon into Hebrew, then hand-copied 14 English pages' worth of text onto that single page. Extrapolating, the entire Book of Mormon could fit onto roughly 21 plates — well within a 40-plate set, even after allowing for the lost 116 pages and a larger font.[^1] Mormon's statement that the writers would have written in Hebrew "if our plates had been sufficiently large" implies reformed Egyptian was significantly more compact than Hebrew, allowing for an even larger font.[^2] See [[The Book of Mormon Fits on the Plates - Sjodahl Hebrew Test]].
