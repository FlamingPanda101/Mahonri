# Ed Decker

**Era:** Late 20th–early 21st century
**Tradition:** Evangelical Protestant (former Latter-day Saint)
**Role:** Counter-cult activist, author, filmmaker

## Bio
Ed Decker is an ex-Mormon counter-cult activist, founder of Saints Alive in Jesus, and co-author/producer of *The God Makers* — a 1980s book and film that became a touchstone of sensationalist anti-Mormon media.

## Topics

### Cited as a discredited source
In the [[All Those In Favor - Three Tactics to Evangelize Mormons|All Those In Favor]] episode, [[Mark Driscoll]] points viewers to "Ed Decker" for doing "your homework" on Mormonism. The hosts react with derision — "not serious scholarly muscle, nothing going on there, no teeth"[^1] — using the citation to illustrate their broader complaint that Evangelical critics still rely on long-discredited counter-cult material (Decker, [[Norman Geisler]]) instead of engaging current LDS scholarship. The hosts also flag that showing *The God Makers* in churches is itself the kind of "anti-Mormon media" that primes hostility — see [[Anti-Mormonism Is Attacking the Person Not the Idea]].
