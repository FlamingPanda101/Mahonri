# Billy Johnson

A foundational Latter-day Saint pioneer in Ghana and "a pillar of the Church" there, who later (per [[Daniel C. Peterson]]) became a stake patriarch in the Accra area.[^1]

Peterson relays a story (collected in interviews by Marcy Brown, ~early 2000s, reportedly once on lost film) in which the Ghanaian government expelled all foreign missionaries (early 1980s). As the leading Latter-day Saint left in charge, Johnson prayed for guidance and reported that [[Brigham Young]] appeared to him in a vision, opening with the Brigham-esque line "Buck up, Johnson. Help is on its way," and giving instruction.[^2] Peterson uses the account to argue that even if Brigham held racist ideas in mortality, "he's now beyond that" — proof that one can "grow beyond your earthly box."
