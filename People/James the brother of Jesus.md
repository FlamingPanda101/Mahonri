# James the brother of Jesus

The brother of Jesus and leader ("bishop") of the Jerusalem church, distinguished in the episode from the two apostolic Jameses (James son of Zebedee / "the Great," and James son of Alphaeus). Often called **James the Just** or "James the Less."

## Role in the early church
Day and Butler present James as the Torah-observant leader holding the Jerusalem community together — "nobody's living the law better than James" — walking a tightrope between Jewish observance and faith in Jesus. He represents one of the **divergent first-century Christianities**, in tension with [[Paul the Apostle|Paul]]'s law-free Gentile mission. See [[Paul vs James and the Judaizer Conflict]].

## "I judge" — Acts 15
In [[Acts 15|Acts 15]] it is James, not [[Peter]], who renders the decision ("Wherefore my sentence is..."; "I judge"), which Day argues challenges the LDS "Peter is in charge" model and reads more naturally as a Jerusalem-headquartered movement under James. See [[Acts 15 Shows James Deciding Not Peter]].

## Scholarly notes
- [[Robert Eisenman]]'s *James the Brother of Jesus* makes James the central figure of earliest Christianity.
- Some scholars suggest the gospels minimize James's apostolic status to play down the influence of Jesus's family; some even propose the multiple Jameses may collapse into one figure.
- [[Josephus]] records James as a righteous man whose unjust killing contributed to Jerusalem's downfall.
