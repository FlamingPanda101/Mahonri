# Everett Ferguson

**Role:** Historian of early Christianity; patristics scholar
**Era:** Contemporary (b. 1933)
**Tradition:** Churches of Christ

## Bio

Everett Ferguson is a leading scholar of early Christianity and author of *Baptism in the First Five Centuries* (Eerdmans, 2009), the standard scholarly reference on the history of baptism, and *Early Christians Speak*. He is a member of the Churches of Christ — a tradition that affirms baptismal regeneration but rejects infant baptism — and [[Trent Horn]] notes his two central conclusions are "amenable to his denomination": that the historical evidence for *baptismal regeneration* is abundantly clear while the evidence for *infant baptism* is not.[^1]

## Baptism in the First Five Centuries

Ferguson is the principal scholarly authority engaged (by both [[Gavin Ortlund]] and [[Trent Horn]]) in the infant-baptism debate. He reads early-3rd-century catacomb inscriptions recording baptism shortly before death as evidence that infant baptism *developed out of* emergency deathbed baptism, and is skeptical that [[Irenaeus of Lyons]]'s "born again to God — infants and children" refers to baptism (because Irenaeus says "reborn," not "regenerate"). Trent contests both readings. See:
