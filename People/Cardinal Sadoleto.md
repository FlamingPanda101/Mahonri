# Cardinal Sadoleto

**Full name:** Jacopo Sadoleto (1477–1547)
**Role:** Cardinal, humanist, bishop of Carpentras
**Era:** Reformation (16th century)
**Tradition:** Roman Catholic

## Bio

Jacopo Sadoleto was an Italian humanist, cardinal, and bishop of Carpentras, esteemed for his elegant Latin and irenic temperament. In 1539 he wrote an open letter to the city of Geneva urging it to return to the Roman church. John Calvin's *Reply to Sadoleto* (1539) answered him point by point and became a classic statement of the Reformed cause.

## Relevance to the vault

Sadoleto is the addressee whose letter draws out the Reformers' conservative self-understanding. In replying, [[John Calvin]] insists "our agreement with antiquity is far closer than yours," claiming only to renew the ancient form of the church distorted by the Roman pontiff. The vault uses this exchange — alongside [[Richard Müller]] — to argue the Reformation was reform between traditionalism and revolution, not a novel break with the historic faith.
