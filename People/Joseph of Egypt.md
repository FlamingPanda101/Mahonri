# Joseph of Egypt

**Joseph**, eleventh son of Jacob (Israel) and firstborn of Rachel, is sold into Egypt by his brothers, rises to become **vice-regent of Egypt**, and preserves his family through famine (Genesis 37-50). Fluent in Egyptian and ruling over his elder brothers, he is the archetypal younger son who inherits the **birthright**, which passes to his son **Ephraim**.

## Bio / Significance
In the Hebrew Bible Joseph prophesies on his deathbed that Israel will return to the promised land and asks that his bones be carried out of Egypt (Genesis 50:24-25). The **Joseph Smith Translation** and the **Book of Mormon** ([[2 Nephi 3]]) greatly expand his prophetic role: he foresees Moses, Aaron, the Exodus, the latter-day seer ("a choice seer... his name shall be called after me"), and the conquest of Canaan in detail. He is the head of the lineage — Ephraim and Manasseh — through which Lehi's family ([[Nephi]], [[Laban]], the [[Brass Plates Bypassed the Textual Corruption Chain|brass plates]]) descends.
