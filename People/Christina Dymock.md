# Christina Dymock

LDS author of roughly 200 published books — mostly fiction under the pen name **Lucy McConnell**, plus a few gospel-themed nonfiction titles. Her professional craft is the structural analysis of narrative: she "reverse-outlines" other novelists' books down to their skeletons to see what makes them work. Applying that method to **First Nephi**, she claims to have discovered that its outline reproduces the cycle of the **seven Hebrew feasts** of [[Leviticus 23]] — a pattern she reports no prior commentator had published.

## The Seven-Feasts Thesis
On [[The Stick of Joseph - Nephi Encoded the Seven Hebrew Feasts into the Book of Mormon|the Stick of Joseph]] she argues that [[Nephi]] structured 1 Nephi as the festival year, each section opening with the Lord speaking to [[Lehi]], and reproducing each feast's liturgy and ritual objects ([[Nephi Encoded the Seven Hebrew Feasts as the Outline of First Nephi]]). Her signature moves are literary: reading the otherwise-anomalous casting of lots as the **two goats** of the Day of Atonement ([[Laban and Nephi Enact the Two Goats of the Day of Atonement]]), the lulav-and-etrog irony of the tree-of-life vision, and Nephi's encoding of the Omer's "days of counting" as numerical groups of three and four in the Isaiah chapters ([[Nephi Encodes the Days of Counting as Groups of Three and Four in the Isaiah Chapters]]). She frames the interleaving of the fall feasts before Passover as a deliberate signal toward the Second Coming ([[Nephi Interposes the Fall Feasts Before Passover to Signal the Second Coming]]).

Co-host [[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)|Dave Butler]] supplies the scholarly scaffolding (Wellhausen, [[Margaret Barker]], the Enoch/Jubilees calendar disputes); Dymock repeatedly defers to him on the Hebrew and historical details. A book-length treatment is forthcoming from Plain and Precious Publishing.
