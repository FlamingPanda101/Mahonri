# Lincoln H. Blumell

**Lincoln H. Blumell** is a professor of ancient scripture (religious education) at [[Brigham Young University]] and a papyrologist whose primary research is editing unpublished Greek papyri, inscriptions, and documentary texts from Roman and Byzantine Egypt — receipts, wills, petitions, contracts, and especially private letters. His doctoral dissertation on early Christian letters from Egypt became his first book. [^1]

## The Elect Lady Discovery
Blumell argues that the recipient of [[2 John 1.1|2 John]] — universally rendered "the elect lady" and read as a metaphor for a church — was in fact a named woman called *Eklekte* (Latin *Electa*). He dates the idea to spring 2023 while on a dig in Egypt, and published the full case roughly two years later as *Lady Eklekte: The Lost Woman of the New Testament* (Fortress Press). His papyrological habit of emending the orthographic and grammatical errors of unpunctuated, unspaced ancient texts is what let him see that the standard Greek of 2 John 1:1 is itself broken. See [[Eklekte Is a Named Woman Not the Elect Lady of 2 John]] and [[The 2 John Forgery Theory Rests on Misreading the Address]]. [^2]

He frames the find as a methodological lesson — get into the primary sources in the original language, and hold even "settled" scholarship with healthy skepticism, because a single new papyrus can force a paradigm shift. Cf. [[Dive Into the Primary Sources Before Repeating the Talking Point]]. [^3]
