# Kathleen McFarlane

Director at the Children's Institute International in Los Angeles who conducted the controversial child interviews at the heart of the [[McMartin Preschool Trial Produced No Convictions|McMartin preschool trial]]. (Transcript auto-captions render the name variously as "McFarland/McFarlane"; commonly known as Kee MacFarlane.)

## Suggestive interviewing in the McMartin case
McFarlane "sold herself as a psychotherapist but lacked any professional licenses." She conceptualized the **anatomically correct doll** and used highly suggestive techniques — telling each isolated child what other children had supposedly said, and inviting them to "pretend or speculate." Researchers later found her method produced false allegations 58% of the time vs. ~17% on average. She was put on the stand to defend her methods. See [[Suggestive Interviewing Induces False Allegations]] [^1][^2][^3].
