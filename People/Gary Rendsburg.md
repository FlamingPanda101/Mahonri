# Gary Rendsburg

**Full name:** Gary A. Rendsburg
**Role:** Professor of Jewish History / Hebrew Bible scholar (Rutgers University)
**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** Academic Jewish studies; observant Jew

## Bio
Gary A. Rendsburg is a leading scholar of the Hebrew Bible — its language, composition, and literary art — with particular expertise in Genesis and the Pentateuch. [[Trevan Hatch]] describes him as "a well-known, very well-known Genesis scholar out of Rutgers and very observant Jew," and notes Rendsburg contributed a chapter to a volume Hatch edited on Latter-day Saints and Jews (*The Learning of the Jews*).

## Contribution to the source
In the [[The Stick of Joseph - Clues Reveal the Unknown Editor of Genesis|Stick of Joseph]] conversation, Rendsburg supplies the **framing quotation** for Hatch's whole thesis that [[Genesis Was Finalized as Davidic-Solomonic Apologetic|Genesis was finalized as Davidic apologetic]]: that the scribes "wrote a national epic incorporating all of the earlier traditions back to Abraham and embedded into that narrative anticipations of the present" — telling the story of the past while reflecting on the present, "the major accomplishment of the anonymous authors in Jerusalem who created the book of Genesis." Hatch also cites Rendsburg's relatively *early* dating of Genesis (into the 10th century, the 900s) while noting that even on that early dating the final form postdates David and Solomon.
