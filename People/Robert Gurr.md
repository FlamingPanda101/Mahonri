# Robert Gurr
**Role:** Latter-day Saint apologist and debater

## Bio
Robert Gurr is an independent Latter-day Saint apologist who appears as a recurring guest on Christian and interfaith debate channels. He describes himself as a lifelong member and "true blue believer" who passed through a faith crisis roughly 25 years ago, studied the evidence, and returned to the Church as the worldview that "seemed to be the best fit for all of the evidence."[^1]

In the debate *Is the LDS Church True?* against [[Bill Reel]], Gurr argued the affirmative along three lines: (1) a "consistent skepticism" epistemology borrowed from Sherlock Holmes — "when you have eliminated the impossible, whatever remains... must be the truth" — turned against ex-Mormon atheism for accepting abiogenesis and unguided evolution on minimal evidence;[^1] (2) that Joseph Smith *restored* doctrines (believer's baptism, laying on of hands, anointing/chrismation, a social godhead, deification, premortality) actually taught by the earliest Christian writers before the creeds;[^2] and (3) an idiosyncratic reading of pre-Columbian Mesoamerican sources (Diego Durán, Ixtlilxóchitl, the codices) as preserving a Book of Mormon-shaped migration narrative, with the Hill Cumorah relocated to the Valley of Mexico (Lake Texcoco / Chapultepec).[^3] On Joseph Smith's polygamy he defended a presentism rebuttal and treated most of the marriages as non-consummated "ward" adoptions.[^4] See [[Restoration Matches Pre-Nicene Christianity - Gurr's Argument]], [[Skepticism Must Be Applied Consistently to Atheism Too]], and [[Mesoamerican Sources Preserve a Book of Mormon Migration - Gurr]].
