# Bill Arnold

**Full name:** Bill T. Arnold
**Role:** Professor of Old Testament Interpretation
**Era:** Contemporary (active 1990s–present)
**Tradition:** Academic / Evangelical (Asbury Theological Seminary)

## Bio

Bill Arnold is the Paul S. Amos Professor of Old Testament Interpretation at Asbury Theological Seminary. He is a productive scholar who bridges confessional and academic biblical studies, holding a Ph.D. from Hebrew Union College. His Genesis commentary in the New Cambridge Bible Commentary series represents academic-press-level scholarship on the book.

Dan McClellan includes Arnold's commentary in his recommended reading list: "You can check out Bill Arnold's new Cambridge Bible commentary volume on Genesis." [^1]

## Genesis Source Criticism

Despite teaching at an evangelical seminary, Arnold's Cambridge commentary affirms the scholarly consensus on source criticism of Genesis, including the two-source flood hypothesis. This is significant: it demonstrates that source criticism of Genesis is accepted even by scholars working within confessionally conservative institutions when they engage the evidence on academic terms.
