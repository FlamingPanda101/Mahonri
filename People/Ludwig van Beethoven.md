# Ludwig van Beethoven (1770-1827)

German composer, a central figure of the transition from the Classical to the Romantic era in Western music.

## Tonal ambiguity as a model of Hebrew time
Cited (with Tchaikovsky) for exploiting **tonal ambiguity** — withholding the defining third of a chord so the listener's brain assumes a tonality, then "rewriting" what it heard when the resolving note arrives. The talk uses this as the analogy for Hebrew *rhythmic* (not linear) time, where present repentance retroactively rewrites the meaning of the past. See [[Time as Rhythm Not a Line]] [^1].
