# Ron Hendel

**Full name:** Ronald S. Hendel
**Role:** Professor of Hebrew Bible and Jewish Studies
**Era:** Contemporary (active 1990s–present)
**Tradition:** Academic (University of California, Berkeley)

## Bio

Ron Hendel is the Norma and Sam Dabby Professor of Hebrew Bible and Jewish Studies at UC Berkeley. He is a leading figure in contemporary source criticism and textual criticism of the Hebrew Bible. His 2024 Anchor Yale Bible commentary on Genesis 1–11 represents the most recent major scholarly treatment of the Primeval History.

Dan McClellan includes Hendel's commentary in his recommended bibliography: "You can check out Ron Hendel's Genesis 1–11 commentary from last year in the Anchor Yale Bible commentary series." [^1]

## Genesis Source Criticism

Hendel's commentary affirms the two-source flood hypothesis (Priestly and non-Priestly accounts interwoven) and identifies the animal count discrepancy as one of the contradictions between them. His work represents the continuation of the scholarly tradition from Speiser (1964) through Westermann through Gertz to the present. See [[Noahs Ark Source Criticism Two Flood Accounts]].

Hendel is also known for his work on textual criticism (*The Text of Genesis 1–11*) and the history of Israelite religion (*The Memory of Abraham*).
