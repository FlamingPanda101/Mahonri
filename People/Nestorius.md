# Nestorius

Fifth-century Bishop of Constantinople, condemned at the First Council of Ephesus (AD 431).

## Significance
Nestorius held that in Christ "humanity and divinity operated separately." He affirmed Christ as consubstantial with both God and humanity, but with the two natures "functioning independently" — to the point that he denied Mary could be called *Theotokos* (Mother of God), since he saw Jesus's birth as an expression of his humanity, not his divinity. The Council of Ephesus condemned this, rejecting "the idea that Jesus's humanity accomplished some things without his divine nature" and the depiction of Christ as effectively two persons. His position helped define one boundary that the Chalcedonian Definition later avoided ("not parted or divided into two persons"). See [[Ephesus and Chalcedon Defined the Two Natures of Christ]].
