# Steven Taysom

A religious-studies scholar and biographer of [[Joseph F. Smith]], cited by historian [[Jonathan Stapley]] in the [[Keystone - LDS Historian on What We Get Wrong About the Endowment|Keystone endowment interview]]. Stapley invokes Taysom's argument that Western religions — and religions in general, "particularly those focused on an unchanging God" — endure precisely when they "give their adherents... a sense that their faith is unchanging while simultaneously changing."[^1]

The thesis explains the tension at the heart of Latter-day Saint temple history: a tradition that prizes the constancy of God must nonetheless adapt its forms to stay relevant, and (Stapley says) "Latter-day Saints do that really well." See [[Religions Persist by Feeling Unchanging While Actually Changing]].
