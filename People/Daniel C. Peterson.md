# Daniel C. Peterson

Dr. Daniel C. ("Dan") Peterson is a Latter-day Saint scholar — PhD and emeritus professor of Islamic Studies and Arabic at Brigham Young University — described in the interview's introduction as "both famous and infamous on the internet."[^1] He is the founder of the Interpreter Foundation and a producer of the *Becoming Brigham* documentary series and the dramatic film *Six Days in August*, both of which frame much of his commentary in this source.

He is a faithful apologist who nonetheless presents himself as willing to revise his own views in light of new scholarship (notably reversing his reading of the Utah 1852 slavery legislation).[^2] He came to the Church largely on his own in a part-member family, baptized his own non-member father the night before his mission, and later served as a bishop — biographical details he uses to illustrate that people can change and that historical/doctrinal certainty often softens with experience.[^3]
