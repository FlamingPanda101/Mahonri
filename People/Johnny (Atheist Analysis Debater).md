# Johnny (Atheist Analysis Debater)

Johnny is the atheist, pro-choice interlocutor who debated [[Trent Horn]] on the now-defunct **Atheist Analysis** channel (moderated by "Chris") in the exchange Horn re-posted as [[The Counsel of Trent - My Abortion Debate Youve Never Seen|"My Abortion Debate You've Never Seen"]]. No surname is given on the recording; this note is distinct from the unrelated YouTuber [[Johnny Harris]].

## Position in the debate

Johnny grants that the unborn are *biologically* human but denies that "human DNA" alone confers the **right to life** — his recurring slogan is that "a blueprint is not a building." He argues personhood is the real qualifier and that he (and "a lot of people") cannot find a non-arbitrary place to draw it. His constructive case leans on **bodily autonomy** framed through self-defense law (Castle Doctrine, Stand Your Ground, and an AOJP analysis), the **organ-sharing** parallel, and a series of reductios (the burning embryo-lab, frozen embryos, failed implantation, skin-cell cloning) that overlap the vault's [[The IVF Reductio Against Conception-as-Ensoulment]].

Under Horn's cross-examination he concedes he "lean[s] more towards nihilism," that a rat does not share our right to life only because "we value the human species," and ultimately grounds the infant's right to life in collective human *decision* ("we've decided") — the move Horn turns into the [[Personhood by Majority Vote Is a Dangerous Criterion|majority-vote reductio]]. He repeatedly characterizes both sides' lines as "somewhat arbitrary" and ends conceding the difficulty of arguing a women's-bodies question as a man.
