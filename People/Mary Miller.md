# Mary Miller

**Era:** Contemporary (1980s–2020s)
**Tradition:** Academic (non-LDS)
**Role:** Mesoamerican art historian

## Bio

Mary Miller is a preeminent scholar of Maya and Mesoamerican art, longtime professor
at Yale and author of standard reference works in the field. Rappleye introduces her
and co-author [[Simon Martin]] as "absolute top tier" and "really legit people in
Mesoamerican studies."[^1]

## Maya Scimitar-Like Blade

Miller and Martin described a depiction of a Maya warrior holding a "scimitar-like
flint blade" — cited by Rappleye as evidence that curved/scimitar-like weapons are not
anachronistic in the New World, since neutral top-tier specialists apply the term. See
[[Scimitars Are Not an Anachronism in the Old or New World]].[^2]
