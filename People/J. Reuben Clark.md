# J. Reuben Clark (1871-1961)

LDS leader, lawyer, and diplomat (US Under Secretary of State and Ambassador to Mexico) who served decades in the **First Presidency**. Cited here for his landmark statement on when LDS teaching carries scriptural authority.

## "When Are Church Teachers' Words Entitled to the Claim of Scripture?" (1954)
Clark's 1954 address (originally a talk, later published) exegetes **[[D&C 68.md|D&C 68]]** to argue that inspired speech can be scripture — but that **only the President of the Church** "has the right to receive revelations for the church... or to give authoritative interpretations of scripture that shall be binding." [[Luke Hansen]] cites it to show the LDS hermeneutic on canon was still being formalized into the mid-20th century; [[Joe Heschmeyer]] cites the same passage to show the President's authority is "bigger than a turbulent plane." See [[Was Adam-God Official Binding Teaching]] and [[Tabernacle Infallibility - Elders of Israel Misread]] [^1].
