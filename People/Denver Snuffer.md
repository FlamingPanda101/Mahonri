# Denver Snuffer

**Role:** Excommunicated former Latter-day Saint; figurehead of the "Remnant" movement
**Era:** Contemporary, American
**Tradition:** LDS (excommunicated 2013)

## Bio

Denver Snuffer Jr. is an American author and attorney, excommunicated from the LDS Church in 2013, who became the central figure of the loosely affiliated **"Remnant" movement** (sometimes blurred with the "doctrine of Christ" movement). Among the movement's attractions, per [[Ward Radio]], is **polygamy denial**: the claim that [[Joseph Smith]] never practiced plural marriage, that it was instead introduced by [[Brigham Young]] and his associates, and that [[Doctrine and Covenants 132|D&C 132]] is a Brigham-era forgery.

## Relevance to the vault

Snuffer is named in [[Ward Radio - Did Joseph Smith Even Polygamy Bro]] as the figure whose movement [[Don Bradley]] is responding to. The episode argues the denial is historically untenable: Section 132's incidental details match Nauvoo in July 1843, the revelation chastises Joseph (which a Brigham forgery would not), and a web of interlocking pro- and anti-polygamy sources ties plural marriage firmly to Joseph. See [[D&C 132 Internal Details Match Nauvoo July 1843]], [[D&C 132 Chastises Joseph So Brigham Did Not Forge It]], [[The Interlocking Polygamy Sources Defeat the Forgery Theory]], and [[Polygamy Denialism]].
