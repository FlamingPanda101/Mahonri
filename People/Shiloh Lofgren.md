# Shiloh Lofgren

An active Latter-day Saint apologist who runs the small YouTube channel **Gray Area Faith**, focused on apologetics, theology, interfaith dialogue, and debate. A BYU-Idaho graduate (business management) who served a two-year mission in Houston, Texas, he describes a youth period of near-atheist agnosticism from which the witness of the Book of Mormon brought him back to belief.

In the cited materials he (1) presents the restored gospel to an evangelical audience — apostasy, restoration, the Book of Mormon, the plan of salvation, deification, baptism for the dead, and the three degrees of glory — using the King James Bible as common ground; and (2) takes the negative in a formal debate against Reverend Dr. [[Timothy J. Whitehead]] on the resolution that the Bible is the sole infallible rule of faith. His distinctive sola-scriptura arguments: the Bible never *teaches* sola scriptura ([[Sola Scriptura Could Not Have Been True During the Apostolic Age]]); the church, not the text it produced, is "the pillar and ground of the truth" ([[The Church Is the Pillar of Truth and Scripture Is Its Byproduct]]); God "breathed on" the apostles and gave them authority to bind and forgive ([[God Breathed on the Apostles Not Only on the Scriptures]]); and an infallible *original* the church no longer possesses cannot be its functioning rule ([[An Infallible Autograph We No Longer Possess Cannot Be the Rule of Faith]]).
