# Seth M. Bybee

Seth M. Bybee is a professor in the **Department of Biology at Brigham Young University** and an evolutionary biologist who specializes in the evolution of insects (particularly dragonflies and beetles) and in color visual systems. He teaches introductory biology for nonmajors (Biology 100), entomology, and evolutionary biology.

He authored the chapter **"The Scientific Evidence for Human Evolution"** in *[[The Restored Gospel of Jesus Christ and Evolution]]* (BYU, 2025), surveying the major lines of evidence — comparative anatomy and homology, ontogeny, vestigial traits, molecular/genetic data (shared DNA, chromosome 2 fusion, hybridization with Neanderthals and Denisovans), phylogeny, biogeography, the hominin fossil record, and observed microevolution — and framing them as compatible with belief in a purposeful, divine creation of humanity. He reports that at BYU, teaching this evidence alongside an accurate understanding of Church teachings *increases* rather than diminishes student testimony.

His own reconciliation is that the **one uniquely human feature is the divine spirit** — created premortally and placed by God in a body derived from the physical creation — so that the attributes enabling spirituality predate Adam and the "test of mortal life" is to overcome the "natural man" encoded in our biology.
