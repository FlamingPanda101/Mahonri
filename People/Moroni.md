# Moroni

**Era:** ~4th–5th century AD (Book of Mormon); appears to Joseph Smith 1823+
**Tradition:** Book of Mormon prophet; resurrected messenger in LDS history

## Identity

Moroni is the final Nephite prophet, son of [[Mormon]], who abridged the book of Ether, added the book of Moroni, and buried the gold plates (with the interpreters) in the hill in New York. As a resurrected angel he is the LDS messenger who directed Joseph Smith to the plates beginning in 1823. He carried the interpreters that [[Mosiah]] II had used and that descended from the Jaredites (see [[The Jaredite and Nephite Interpreters Carry the Endowment Ascent Pattern]]).

## Apologetic significance

- His annual instruction of Joseph at the hill from 1823, before the 1827 retrieval, is invoked to explain Lorenzo Saunders' hostile "no fresh dirt" observation — the excavation was years old by 1827. See [[Dan Vogel's East-Side Hole Is the Lone Source Against the West-Side Stone Box]].
- He universalizes the brother of Jared's ascent (Ether 4), inviting all to "come unto the Lord in the same way." See [[The Brother of Jared Models the Endowment Ascent Through the Veil]].
