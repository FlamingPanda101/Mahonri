# Sextus Empiricus

**Era:** c. 2nd–3rd century CE
**Role:** Greek physician and Pyrrhonist philosopher
**Tradition:** Pyrrhonist skepticism

## Bio

Sextus Empiricus was a Greek physician and philosopher whose surviving works — chiefly the *Outlines of Pyrrhonism* — are the principal source for ancient Pyrrhonist skepticism. He systematized the skeptical "modes," including the regress (one of Agrippa's tropes), as a tool for inducing suspension of judgment (*epoché*).

## Relevance to Epistemology

In this video, [[Kevin McCain]] cites Sextus Empiricus as an example of using the **epistemic regress problem** to argue *for* skepticism: if every reason demands a further reason, justification can never be completed. McCain notes that, against this skeptical deployment, the regress's more constructive role is to clarify the structure of justification. See [[The Epistemic Regress Problem]].
