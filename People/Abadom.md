# Abadom

**Role:** Nephite record-keeper in the book of Omni; father of [[Amaleki]]
**Era:** Nephite history (~150 BC, narrative setting)
**Tradition:** Book of Mormon figure (LDS scripture)

## Bio
Abadom (the text reads "Abinadom") is named in [[Omni 1.30|Omni]] as the father of [[Amaleki]], the last writer on the small plates. He kept the plates and passed them to his son.

## Significance
[[Jonah Barnes]]'s *Key to the Keystone* makes the onomastic observation that **Abinadom** sounds nearly identical to **Abinadi** ("Abinadom" → "Abinadi"). Combined with [[Amaleki]]'s note that a brother "went with [Zeniff]" and vanished, this is offered as circumstantial evidence that the prophet [[Abinadi]] belonged to this record-keeping (and brass-plates-bearing) royal line. The panel concedes the identification is speculative — "those may be coincidences" — resting on name similarity and a missing-brother inference, not on any explicit statement in the text. See [[The Sting of Death Is Apocryphal Isaiah from the Brass Plates Not a Pauline Anachronism]].
