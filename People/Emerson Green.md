# Emerson Green

**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** Agnostic
**Role:** Philosopher; host of the *Counter Apologetics* podcast/channel

## Bio
Emerson Green is an agnostic philosopher who hosts *Counter Apologetics*, "reads Plantinga, debates William Lane Craig, and generally spends his time poking holes in religious arguments." [[Alex Arnold]] uses him as the framing figure of the episode: around 2023 Green set out to write an essay testing the popular skeptic claim — associated with [[Sam Harris]] — that Mormonism is "the least plausible form of Christianity," and concluded the opposite.[^1]

## On the Philosophical Defensibility of LDS Theology
Green's verdict, after reading "the critics, the defenses, and [working] through the philosophy," was that "LDS theology, specifically because of what it removes from classical Christianity, is actually among the more philosophically defensible options on the table." Arnold stresses that Green "is not converting, but he followed the argument to where it led."[^1] His best-known formulation of the convergence: "If I didn't become a Mormon, but I did somehow convert to some form of Christianity, I'd probably just reinvent about 70% of Mormonism and then technically not be a Mormon."[^2] He is the lead exhibit in [[Independent Philosophers Keep Reconstructing LDS Theology]].

## On Animal Suffering
Green, with [[Alex OConnor|Alex O'Connor]], also names evolutionary animal suffering — predation and death across hundreds of millions of years before humans — as "the hardest version" of the problem of evil for finite theism. See [[Animal Suffering Is the Hardest Test for Finite Theism]].[^3]
