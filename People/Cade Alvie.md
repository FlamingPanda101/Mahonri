# Cade Alvie

**Who:** Latter-day Saint content creator and host of the YouTube reaction channel **All Those In Favor** (co-hosting with [[Baylor Johnson (Ward Radio)|Baylor Johnson]]); also appears as a panelist on [[Ward Radio]]. A returned missionary (served in West Texas) who recently moved into a new ward.

**Role:** Podcast host / panelist and popular-level apologist (not a credentialed scholar). His surname is rendered "Cade Alvie," and his first name appears as "Kaden" in some auto-transcripts.

## All Those In Favor — Pastor Reaction
On [[All Those In Favor - Pastor Destroys Mormon Missionaries Former Missionaries React]] he narrates and drives most of the commentary, reacting to a viral "pastor owns Mormon missionaries" clip alongside Baylor and guest [[Kwaku (All Those In Favor)|Kwaku]]. He supplies the bulk of the apologetic pushback: that the pastor's "part God, part man" trap presupposes the [[Hypostatic Union]] (Chalcedon, 451) and a Greek-philosophical doctrine of [[Divine Impassibility Is Classical-Theist Heresy|impassibility]]; that the JST does not read as the pastor claimed (see [[The Pastor Misattributed the Anarthrous Reading to the Joseph Smith Translation]]); and that missionaries should function as guides, not debaters (see [[Missionaries Are Tour Guides Not Question Answerers]]). He cites [[Blake Ostler]] on the Godhead as a "relationship of indwelling unity" and [[Daniel Wallace]] on the anarthrous *theos* of [[John 1.1|John 1:1]].

## Relaying Scholarship in the "All Those In Favor" Discussions
He frequently carries credentialed scholars' arguments into the conversation rather than advancing primary research of his own — noted in one instance as "helping a BYU professor develop" an argument. In [[Alma 32 Light Alludes to Genesis 1 Creation]] he relays [[David Bokovoy]]'s reading of Alma 32 as an allusion to the Genesis 1 creation account. In [[The Uncreated Element Is Only the Most Fundamental Substrate]] he advances the reading that Joseph Smith's "uncreated elements" mean only the *most fundamental* physical stratum, citing [[J.P. Moreland]] on a possible pre-Big-Bang "quantum foam." He also reaches for [[Moroni 8.18|Moroni 8:18]] as scriptural support for divine immutability against [[Noah Biser]]'s charge that Latter-day Saints teach a changing God.

## Ward Radio Appearance
Panelist on the Ward Radio episode reacting to [[Trent Horn]]'s "substitutional patriarchy" argument, discussing the cultural decline of male leadership and the family-first thesis. See [[Substitutional Patriarchy - The Government Replaces the Husband]].[^1]
