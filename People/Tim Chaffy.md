# Tim Chaffy

**Who:** Apologist and writer for Answers in Genesis. Authored the article critiqued in [[Data Over Dogma - Prophecies Nativity and Sorry Apologetics]] claiming Jesus's birth fulfilled prophecies from the Hebrew Bible.

## Credentials

McClellan and Beecher noted his doctorate is from an accredited theological seminary (unlike [[James White]], whose doctoral credentials they consider illegitimate), but the content they analyze is popular apologetics, not peer-reviewed scholarship.[^1]

## Prophecy Fulfillment Claims

Chaffy's article argues that the nativity fulfilled multiple Old Testament prophecies, including Isaiah 7:14 (virgin birth), Micah 5:2 (Bethlehem birthplace), and Matthew's "he shall be called a Nazarene" prophecy. McClellan critiques these as wrenching passages out of context and relying on mistranslations.[^2] See [[Isaiah 7.14 Virgin Birth Prophecy Is Mistranslated]] and [[Nazarene Prophecy Has No Known Hebrew Bible Source]].
