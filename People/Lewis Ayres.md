# Lewis Ayres

Catholic historical theologian at Durham University. His *Nicaea and Its Legacy* is described as "the most comprehensive modern treatment" of the period from Nicaea (325) to Constantinople (381).

## Cited by Moroni's Standard
[[Alex Arnold]] leans on Ayres to document the post-Nicene turbulence — the five exiles of [[Athanasius]], the Arian/semi-Arian formulas of successive emperors, and the instability of the Nicene formula until Theodosius's decree of 381 — and again for the Chalcedonian period. He is listed among the non-LDS scholars "that creedal Christianity considers its own authorities." See [[The Chalcedonian Schism Refutes the Standard It Defines]] and [[The Trend Line Runs the Wrong Way for the Recovery Narrative]].[^1]
