# Bart Ehrman

Leading historical-critical scholar of early Christianity (UNC Chapel Hill), specialist in New Testament textual criticism and the diversity of early Christian movements. In the Great Apostasy episode he is cited on two points:

- **The hard sell of early Christianity:** Ehrman on how implausible the Christian message sounded to philosophically-educated Greeks/Romans — that a man Rome had executed was the Messiah now in heaven. This sets up why the apologists turned to Greek philosophy. See [[Greek Philosophy Corrupted the Christian Concept of God]].
- **Marcion was not a Gnostic:** Day reads an Ehrman quote distinguishing Marcion from Gnosticism — Marcion held there were two gods (not many), did not regard the world as a cosmic disaster, and did not teach divine sparks freed by *gnosis*. See [[The New Testament Canon Is a Reaction to Marcion]].
