# Francis of Assisi

**Role:** Founder of the Franciscans; mendicant; exemplar of *imitatio Christi*
**Era:** 1181/1182 – 1226 CE
**Tradition:** Medieval Christianity (Assisi, Italy)

## Bio

Francesco di Bernardone renounced his wealthy family and "devoted [himself] to following Christ's footsteps by practicing physical asceticism, material poverty, and a nexus of spiritual values, including service, humility, and love." [^1] He founded a family of religious orders: the Friars Minor (Lesser Brothers), the Poor Clares (with [[Clare of Assisi]]), and the Brothers and Sisters of Penance.

## Conversion Among the Lepers

[[Miranda Wilcox]] presents Francis as an exemplar of medieval penitent living. In his own *Testament*, Francis described his conversion through encountering socially shunned lepers:

> "The Lord gave me, Brother Francis, thus to begin doing penance in this way: for when I was in sin, it seemed too bitter for me to see lepers. And the Lord himself led me among them and I showed mercy to them. And when I left them, what had seemed bitter to me was turned into sweetness of soul and body. And afterward I delayed a little and left the world." [^1]

This encounter revealed his vocation "to follow the footprints of Christ and to fully embrace the logic of the Cross." [^1] He instructed his followers to "live according to the pattern of the Holy Gospel," with penance, peace, and mercy as the hallmarks of Franciscan life. [^2] See [[Medieval Christians Imitated Christ Through Diverse Religious Vocations]].
