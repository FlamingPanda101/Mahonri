# Alfred Rosenberg

**Full name:** Alfred Rosenberg (1893–1946)
**Role:** Nazi Reichsleiter; chief ideologue of National Socialism
**Era:** Nazi Germany
**Tradition:** National Socialism (non-Christian; neo-pagan ideologue)

## Bio

Alfred Rosenberg was a Reichsleiter — one of only roughly a dozen to sixteen men holding that rank, one or two steps below Hitler — and, in [[Stephen Smoot]]'s words, "the ideological architect of national socialism," who supplied the intellectual underpinning for its worldview and the Holocaust. He was convicted of crimes against humanity and hanged at Nuremberg.[^1]

## The 1938 Report

A 1938 report in the Gestapo dossier was written *to* Rosenberg assessing the Latter-day Saints. Smoot treats its candor as decisive because it was for private internal use: it states that the Church's American leadership "is still shamelessly agitating against national socialist Germany," that the Mormons "show no interest in political events," that members "categorically refuse to use the German salute," and — pivotally — that "the doctrine of the Mormons is incompatible with the national socialist worldview." See [[The Nazis Deemed Latter-day Saint Teachings Ideologically Incompatible with National Socialism]].[^2]
