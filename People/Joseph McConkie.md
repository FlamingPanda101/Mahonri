# Joseph McConkie

BYU professor of ancient scripture and prolific LDS author (son of [[Bruce R. McConkie]]). In the Great Apostasy episode Day cites him for two things:

- **The loss of the divine inheritance.** In *Sons and Daughters of God*, McConkie frames the apostasy's most damaging effect as **estrangement from a literal Heavenly Father** — cutting believers off from God as Father changes everything. See [[Loss of the Divine Family Is the Core Apostasy]].
- **The creeds as indefensible mystery.** Day reads a McConkie quote: the Nicene/Athanasian creed "is not found in any gospel... directly contradicts the plain language of the New Testament... Its best defense is the admission that it is a mystery and as such is indefensible." See [[Nicene and Athanasian Creeds Apply Labels Not Explanations]].

(The transcript also renders a quote under the garbled name "Joseph Philanki," which appears to be the same author.)
