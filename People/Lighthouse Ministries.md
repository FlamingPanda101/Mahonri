# Lighthouse Ministries

Anti-Mormon publication operating in 1990s Utah, described by the Ward Radio series as a magazine that "would say anything to damage the church." When a leaked confidential memo by Presiding Bishopric counselor [[Glenn L. Pace]] reached them, they published it far and wide to peddle the narrative that "seemingly peaceful Latter-day Saints were actually sacrificing and eating babies." [^1]

(Note: this is the Tanners' Utah Lighthouse Ministry; the episode consistently calls it "Lighthouse Ministries." Not to be confused with the unrelated LDS Social Services therapy network, which is a separate institution discussed in the same episode.)

## A hostile witness who still balked
The evidentiary weight of Lighthouse here is adversarial: even this opponent of the Church doubted the memo's authenticity and conceded "it would be very difficult to cover up that many murders," yet published anyway because it was "too good an opportunity to attack and embarrass the LDS Church." See [[Even Lighthouse Ministries Doubted the Pace Memo's Credibility]]. [^1][^2]

## Declared a premature victory
Lighthouse credited the Pace memo with prompting the Utah Governor's Commission for Women and Families to form a ritual-abuse subcommittee — but that subcommittee was formed in February 1990, *before* the memo. See [[The Pace Memo Did Not Cause Any Church or State Action]]. [^3]
