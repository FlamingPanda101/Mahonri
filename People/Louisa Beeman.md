# Louisa Beeman

**Role:** Plural wife of [[Joseph Smith]]
**Era:** 1815–1850
**Tradition:** The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints

## Bio
Louisa Beeman (daughter of [[Alva Beeman]]) was long regarded as [[Joseph Smith]]'s *first* Nauvoo plural wife, on the strength of an 1869 affidavit by her brother-in-law [[Joseph Bates Noble]], who performed the sealing and gave the date as **April 5, 1841**. She was the one early plural wife who was single (the others were already married), making her the apparent fit for the "young, single, sexually available" profile critics assume.

## Significance in [[Don Bradley]]'s argument
Bradley argues the April 5, 1841 date is wrong: [[Joseph Bates Noble]] gave conflicting dates over the years, was described by his own biographer as "terrible with dates" and "a big boaster," located the sealing at his own Nauvoo house (which did not yet exist in spring 1841), and April 5 happens to be the date of *Noble's own* first plural marriage in 1843. Re-dating Louisa to spring 1842 moves [[Zina Huntington Jacobs]] into first place — and with her, the pattern of pregnant brides that undercuts the sexual-motive thesis. See [[Joseph Smith Married Pregnant Women Refuting the Sexual-Motive Thesis]].
