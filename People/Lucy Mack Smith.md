# Lucy Mack Smith

Lucy Mack Smith (1775–1856) was the mother of [[Joseph Smith]] and the author of an early family history of Joseph and the Smith family, a primary source for the family's New York period.

## On the 1825 Stowell Treasure Hunt

In her history, Lucy recounts how [[Josiah Stowell]] came to Manchester, New York, to hire Joseph because Joseph "had means or keys whereby which he could discern things not otherwise visible to the natural eye."[^1] The source notes this is dated 1825, before Joseph possessed the Nephite interpreters ("the Urim and Thummim spectacles"), so Lucy is describing his use of seer stones.[^2] She also recounts that Joseph and his father [[Joseph Smith Sr]] went down and assisted Stowell for a time.

## On the 1813 Leg Surgery
Lucy's *Biographical Sketches* is the primary source for the harrowing 1813 account of her son [[Joseph Smith]]'s pre-anesthetic bone surgery, which [[Don Bradley]] mines in the [[Let's Get Real with Stephen Jones - Joseph Smith's Biggest Discovery (Don Bradley E0039)|Let's Get Real interview]]. Lucy records the boy's cry "Oh father, the pain is so severe, how can I bear it?"; his refusal of brandy and wine; his insistence that "I will have my father sit on the bed and hold me in his arms"; his sending her far from the house; and her returning twice when his screams carried, until she was restrained. Bradley argues the account's authenticity is supported precisely because Lucy weaves in the alcohol refusal without ever disclosing the family's drinking — a context a fabricator would not embed. See [[Joseph Smith Refused Alcohol to Set an Example for His Father]] and [[Joseph Smith's Childhood Leg Surgery Was a Rare Experimental Operation]].[^3]
