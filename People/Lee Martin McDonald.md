# Lee Martin McDonald

**Lee Martin McDonald** is a Baptist New Testament scholar widely regarded as one of the leading academic authorities on the formation of the biblical canon. His major works include *The Formation of the Christian Biblical Canon* and *The Canon Debate* (co-edited with James A. Sanders).

## The Canon Was Not Closed in the Second Century
[[Trent Horn]] quotes McDonald — a *Protestant* scholar — to undercut self-authentication from inside Protestant scholarship:

> "The notion of a closed New Testament canon was not a second century development in the early church, and there were still considerable differences of opinion about what should comprise that canon even in the fourth and fifth centuries" [^1].

If even committed believers disagreed about the canon's contents into the 4th–5th centuries, the canon cannot be self-evidently recognizable by every Spirit-led reader. See [[The Early Church Did Not Use Self-Authentication]] and [[Reformers Disagree With One Another on the Canon]].
