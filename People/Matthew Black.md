# Matthew Black

**Era:** 1908-1994
**Tradition:** Non-Latter-day Saint (Church of Scotland)
**Role:** Scottish biblical scholar; Dead Sea Scrolls and Enoch specialist

## Bio
Matthew Black was Professor of Biblical Criticism at the University of Edinburgh (1952-54), then Professor of Divinity and Biblical Criticism at the University of St Andrews (1954-78) and President of the Society for Old Testament Study. He was a leading editor and translator of Dead Sea Scrolls and pseudepigraphal material and authored standard works on the Enoch corpus.

## In the Ward Radio sources
[[Jonah Barnes]] cites Black (relayed secondhand, partly via [[Hugh Nibley]]'s associate Bruce Porter) for a remark about the Mahijah / Mahawai name appearing both in [[Joseph Smith]]'s [[Moses 6|Book of Moses]] and in the *Book of the Giants*: Black reportedly said "someday we will find out where Joseph got that — someday a source will turn up." Barnes frames this as an "academic god of the gaps," i.e., a tacit admission that the correspondence is hard to explain naturalistically. See [[The Book of the Giants Names Mahijah Matching Joseph Smith's Enoch]].[^1]
