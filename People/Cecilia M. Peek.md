# Cecilia M. Peek

Latter-day Saint classicist and ancient historian (faculty, BYU), specializing in Greek and Roman antiquity. She applies classical Greek philology to the development of early Christian theological terms.

## Cited in *Ancient Christians*
Peek authored the chapter "Receiving Christ: Atonement, Grace, and Eternal Salvation." [^1] Her distinctive contribution is the **lexical and cultural history of *charis* (grace)** and *sōtēria* (salvation): she shows how Paul took a Greco-Roman term saturated with reciprocity and worthiness and transformed it into the free, unmerited gift given to the undeserving. She surveys postapostolic conceptions of grace (1 Clement, Justin, Irenaeus, Clement of Alexandria), the four scholarly atonement models (Illuminator, Restorer, Victor, Victim), and the Augustine–Pelagius controversy, closing with how LDS theology participates in these currents. See [[Paul Redefined Charis as Unmerited Gift Against Greco-Roman Reciprocity]], [[Four Ancient Atonement Models]], [[The Pelagian Controversy on Grace and Human Ability]].
