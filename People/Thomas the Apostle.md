# Thomas the Apostle

**Thomas** (called Didymus, "the Twin") was one of Jesus's Twelve Apostles, best known in the canonical Gospels for doubting the Resurrection until he touched the risen Christ ([[John 20.28|John 20:28]], "My Lord and my God"). In later tradition he is the apostle to **India**.

## Relevance
The apocryphal *Acts of Thomas* (early 3rd c.) narrates Thomas being sold into slavery and shipped to India, where he founds a church. The **St Thomas Christians** of India (the "Indo-Syrian"/Malankara tradition) claim continuous descent from his mission — one of the contiguous, non-Roman apostolic churches Ward Radio argues survived the apostasy-by-conquest. His martyrdom story, like most of the Twelve, derives from this late apocryphal Acts (cf. [[Apostolic Martyrdom Traditions Are Late Apocryphal Legend]]).
