# Jonathan Grimshaw

Employee in the LDS Church Historian's Office in the mid-19th century. About a
decade after the [[King Follett Discourse]] was delivered, Grimshaw produced a
standardized copy based on all four contemporaneous accounts ([[Thomas Bullock]],
[[William Clayton]], [[Willard Richards]], [[Wilford Woodruff]]).[^1]

His amalgamation had "some repetition and some awkward insertions" but became part
of the official *History of Joseph Smith* — the serialized history published in
church newspapers that later became the basis for the *History of the Church*
volumes.[^1]

This version was what [[BH Roberts]] later worked from and what was suppressed in 1912.
See [[King Follett Discourse Publication History]].
