# William Law

Second Counselor in the [[Joseph Smith]] First Presidency who became one of Smith's
most prominent dissenters in Nauvoo. He was a key publisher of the *Nauvoo
Expositor*, the newspaper whose destruction led to Smith's arrest and death at
Carthage Jail.

## Reaction to the King Follett Discourse

Law saw the [[King Follett Discourse]] as evidence of Joseph Smith's heresy. When
the *Nauvoo Expositor* was published just weeks after the sermon, it "included
details from the sermon showing that Joseph Smith was teaching blasphemy."[^1]

The discourse thus became part of the documentary case that dissenters used to
argue Smith had fallen from his prophetic calling.
