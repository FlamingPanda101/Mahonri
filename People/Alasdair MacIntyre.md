# Alasdair MacIntyre

**Full name:** Alasdair MacIntyre (1929–2025)
**Role:** Moral philosopher
**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** Roman Catholic (convert); Aristotelian-Thomist virtue ethics

## Bio

Alasdair MacIntyre was a Scottish-American philosopher and one of the most influential moral thinkers of the late twentieth century. His 1981 book *After Virtue* argued that modern moral discourse is incoherent fragments of older traditions, and called for a recovery of Aristotelian virtue ethics. He later converted to Catholicism and developed a Thomistic-Aristotelian account of the virtues and rationality.

## Relevance to the vault

MacIntyre features in the moral-argument material as a pastoral "bridge" recommendation: even where metaphysical arguments fail to move an atheist toward God, *After Virtue* is offered as a way past a thin "as long as everyone consents / cause no unnecessary pain" ethic toward a richer virtue ethics — said to be potentially "more effective… than all the apologetics books in the world." He thus represents the vault's strategy of meeting moral skeptics on the ground of virtue.
