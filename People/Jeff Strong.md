# Jeff Strong

Latter-day Saint author and lay researcher. A former global president of Johnson & Johnson, he later taught at Brigham Young University and served as president of the Arkansas Bentonville Mission (2018–2021, ~700 missionaries over three years). He is the author of *Torn: Why People We Love Are Leaving the Church and What We Can Learn From Them* (published April 17, 2026).[^1]

## Why He Took Up the Question

Strong's work began personally: his oldest son, Kale, emailed home from the MTC saying he was coming home and would leave the Church — two weeks after Strong, as bishop, had presided over Kale's missionary farewell. Several families in his ward had also stepped away, and none fit the stereotype in his head of people who "just don't have what it takes." Unable to reconcile the people he knew with the narrative he had inherited, he set out to understand why sincere, devout people leave.[^2] See [[Culture Can Crash Over Doctrine in a Faith Crisis]].

## The Disaffiliation Study

To avoid "pontificating without data," Strong recruited several LDS researchers and ran what he describes as the largest LDS disaffiliation study ever done (~15,000 survey respondents, wrapped up the year before publication). The study underlies *Torn* and is slated for separate publication. It found four primary reasons for leaving and, in his reading, a decisive *cultural* undercurrent beneath them.[^3] See [[The Four Groups of LDS Disaffiliates]] and [[Most Who Leave Drifted Away Rather Than Rejected Faith]].

## The "Soil" Model of Culture

Strong's central metaphor is *culture as soil* (Latin *colere*, "to cultivate"): healthy soil both protects/stabilizes and nourishes, and he argues LDS cultural soil is imbalanced toward protection at the expense of nourishment. He names four tensions — standards vs. acceptance, sanctuary vs. growth, obedience vs. agency, and conformity vs. harmony. See [[Mistaking Tradition for the Gospel Sets Up Brittle Faith]] and [[Cultural Pressure to Conform Produces Pretense]].

## On High-Stakes Conversations

With author Joseph Grenny (*Crucial Conversations*; The Other Side Academy), Strong researched the conversations families have when a loved one is in faith transition, concluding the *quality of the conversation* predicts outcomes more than the person's beliefs do — and coining the warning against "talking under the influence" of fear and anger. See [[High-Stakes Faith Conversations Matter More Than the Belief]].
