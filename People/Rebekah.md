# Rebekah

Wife of [[Isaac]], mother of Jacob and Esau, and a matriarch of the covenant. In [[Genesis 24|Genesis 24]] she is chosen as Isaac's bride by [[Abraham]]'s servant, given gifts, and brought from Mesopotamia to marry a husband she has not met — consenting freely ("I will go").

## As the Pattern of the Hebrew Wedding
[[Andrea Woodmansee]] treats Rebekah's marriage as the earliest clear example of the three-phase ancient Hebrew wedding: the **father's servant** (not the groom) selects the bride, she receives gifts *before* the wedding, she is "called out of Babylon" into the covenant family, and she exercises the bride's right to accept or veto the union. The pattern anchors the claim that ancient weddings type the temple and Christ as bridegroom. See [[Ancient Hebrew Weddings Are a Three-Phase Type of the Temple]].
