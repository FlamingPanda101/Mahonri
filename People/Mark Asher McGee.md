# Mark Asher McGee

LDS historian best known, in this vault, as co-author with [[Don Bradley]] of the close study of the [[Kinderhook plates]] — "President Joseph Has Translated a Portion," published in *Producing Ancient Scripture* (University of Utah Press) and condensed in *A Reason for Faith* (BYU Religious Studies Center).

In the [[Informed Saints - The Hoax That Should Have Fooled Joseph Smith|Informed Saints episode]], the panel leans on Bradley and McGee's reconstruction: by matching the characters on [[John Taylor]]'s published facsimiles against the [[Grammar and Alphabet of the Egyptian Language]], they argue [[Joseph Smith]] "translated" at most a single boat-shaped glyph rather than receiving any revelation — the basis of [[Joseph Smith Did Not Translate the Kinderhook Plates by Revelation]].[^1] (The panel relays his name variously as "Mark Ashers McGee" and "Mark Esters McGee.")
