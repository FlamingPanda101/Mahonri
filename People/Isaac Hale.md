# Isaac Hale

Isaac Hale of Harmony, Pennsylvania, was the father of [[Emma Smith]] and thus [[Joseph Smith]]'s father-in-law. He never accepted Joseph as a prophet, never joined the church, never went west, and regarded Joseph with open suspicion — feeling Joseph had "stolen his daughter away."[^1] This makes his testimony to the plates a strong **unlikely witness**: a lifelong skeptic with every motive to debunk.

## Hefting the Box
When Joseph and Emma arrived to settle, Hale refused to let them into his house unless Joseph showed him the plates. Joseph declined to uncover them but let Hale **hold the box and shake it** — an interaction Hale recorded that yields an independent, hostile attestation to the size and heft of a real, tangible artifact.[^2] The plates had reached Pennsylvania hidden in a barrel of beans to survive a search of the cart.[^3] See [[Hostile and Unlikely Witnesses Corroborate the Stone Box]] and [[Informal Witnesses of the Golden Plates]].
