# Katy Faust

**Era:** Contemporary (2010s–2020s)
**Tradition:** Conservative / evangelical Christian
**Role:** Children's-rights advocate, author, founder of *Them Before Us*

## Bio

Katy Faust is an American writer and activist who founded *Them Before Us*, a movement arguing that marriage and family policy should be evaluated primarily through the lens of children's rights — above all a child's right to be known and loved by his or her biological mother and father. With Stacy Manning she co-authored *Them Before Us: Why We Need a Global Children's Rights Movement*, and she has since published a second book applying a child-centric lens to politics and government policy. She was raised in Portland, Oregon; her parents divorced when she was ten, after which her mother entered a long-term same-sex partnership — a biography she invokes to argue that loving her mother is precisely *why* she defends the mother-father norm, since the premise of family redefinition implies her own mother was "optional."[^1] She more recently started a recommendation-only matchmaking service.

## Positions in this dialogue

In her third conversation on [[John Anderson (media)|John Anderson]]'s podcast, Faust argued that:

- **Children have a natural right to their biological mother and father**, so the slogan "love makes a family" functionally means "child loss makes a family." See [[Love Makes a Family Means Child Loss Makes That Family]].
- **Loss to intentionality wounds differently than loss to tragedy** — children who lose a parent to death are mourned-with and fare better than those who lose a parent to divorce or by design (surrogacy, donor conception). See [[Loss by Intentionality Wounds Differently Than Loss by Tragedy]].
- **The post-2005 "no-difference" same-sex-parenting studies are methodologically weak** (recruited samples, tiny n, no real outcome data), and contradict decades of consensus on family structure. See [[The No-Difference Same-Sex Parenting Studies Are Methodologically Weak]].
- **"You become what you behold"** — children default to the family patterns they witness, so transmitting healthy marriage requires modeling and discipleship. See [[You Become What You Behold - Modeling as the Engine of Family Transmission]].
- **Child rights dethrone sexual autonomy**, "the ultimate god of the left," which is why defending children provokes a quasi-religious reaction. See [[Child Rights Confront Sexual Autonomy as the Highest Value]].
- **Dating is a learnable skill the courtship culture suppressed**; she urges a return to low-stakes casual dating. See [[Bring Back Casual Dating - The Courtship Overcorrection]].

She references **Paul Sullins**'s population-based research on same-sex-parenting outcomes, **Josh Zeps** (a debate interlocutor), and [[Jordan Peterson]]'s "highest value" and "what cries is not always a baby" formulations.
