# Henry Abramson

**Role:** Historian of Jewish history; educator and YouTube lecturer (Touro College)

## Bio

Henry Abramson is an academic historian of Jewish and Eastern European history and a dean
at Touro College who produces widely viewed YouTube lectures on Jewish history. On the Ward
Radio episode the hosts react to his video "Jewish genetics and the Lemba tribe of Africa,"
which summarizes the Lemba's Bantu-speaking identity, their oral tradition of descent from
white men who came by boat from a place called **Senna**, and the genetic studies finding
Semitic Y-chromosome markers among them.[^1]

## On the Lemba

Abramson reports the Lemba (~70,000, primarily South Africa and Zimbabwe) carry an oral
tradition of descent from men who came from **Senna** by boat, that they founded a city they
named Senna after their place of origin, and that genetic testing found Semitic markers —
material the hosts marshal as a real-world plausibility parallel to Lehi's transoceanic
voyage. See [[The Lemba Tribe's Semitic DNA and Oral Migration Tradition Parallel Lehi]].[^1]
