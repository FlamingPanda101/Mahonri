# Larry Siedentop

**Era:** Contemporary (b. 1936)
**Tradition:** Secular / agnostic
**Role:** Oxford political philosopher; author of *Inventing the Individual*

## Bio

Larry Siedentop is an American-born Oxford political philosopher whose *Inventing the Individual: The Origins of Western Liberalism* argues that Christianity, especially Pauline and medieval thought, produced the Western concept of the individual.[^1]

## Topics

### Inventing the Individual
[[Glenn Scrivener]] cites Siedentop's "great book" for the claim "we invented the individual," while distinguishing the (good) Christian individual from later *individualism*, which he treats as a post-Christian deviation. Brierley lists Siedentop among the secular historians who, like [[Tom Holland]], are agnostic about Christianity yet trace Western liberalism to it. See [[The Dominion Thesis - Western Values Are Christianized]] and [[Abortion and Euthanasia as Pagan Not Christian]].[^1]
