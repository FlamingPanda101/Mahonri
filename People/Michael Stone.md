# Michael Stone

**Michael E. Stone** is an Israeli-American scholar (Hebrew University of Jerusalem) and a leading authority on **Armenian apocryphal and pseudepigraphal literature**, the Pseudepigrapha of the Old Testament, and the Dead Sea Scrolls. He has translated and edited numerous Armenian Adam-and-Eve and related texts.

## Bio / Significance
Stone's translations of the Armenian *Adam and Eve* / Penitence-of-Adam traditions (published in the late 20th century, roughly 150 years after Joseph Smith) are cited by [[Jonah Barnes]] on Ward Radio for a striking line in which the serpent tells Eve that **"God himself was a man like you when he ate of the fruit... he became God of all because of that"** — read as an ancient attestation of the Latter-day Saint teaching that God was once a man.

## Contribution
The apologetic claim is a *prolepsis*: a doctrine ([[God Was Once a Man]]) Joseph Smith taught in the 1844 King Follett Discourse, surfacing in an Armenian apocryphon he could not have read. See [[The Armenian Adam and Eve Says God Was Once a Man]].
