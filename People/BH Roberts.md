# BH Roberts

Brigham Henry Roberts (1857–1933) was the most prominent LDS theologian and
historian during the Progressive Era. He wrote several history volumes and was
"fascinated with Joseph Smith," considering the [[King Follett Discourse]] "the
culmination of everything Joseph Smith had been teaching."[^1]

## Champion of the King Follett Discourse

Roberts published a version of the discourse in church newspapers in 1909 and
prepared it for inclusion in the *History of the Church* series (1912). But
while Roberts was away on a mission, the First Presidency — [[Joseph F. Smith]]
and [[Charles Penrose]] — removed pages 302–317 from the typescript, suppressing
the discourse.[^2]

When Roberts discovered the omission, he was "so furious that he demands the
church print 10,000 copies of just those pages including the King Follett Discourse
so everyone could read them." The pages were re-added when the *History of the
Church* was republished in 1952.[^3]

## Spirit Birth Interpretation

Roberts was "in the camp saying that there were intelligences that God organized
into spirits" — the two-step view where raw intelligence-matter is formed into
spirit children. This put him at odds with Charles Penrose, who believed spirits
themselves were co-eternal with God.[^4] This dispute was one reason Penrose was
comfortable suppressing Roberts's version.

## Later Critique

Later champions of the discourse like [[Joseph Fielding Smith]] and [[Bruce R. McConkie]] disagreed with Roberts's footnotes and interpretations on [[Infant Exaltation]] and [[Spirit Birth and Intelligences]].[^5]
