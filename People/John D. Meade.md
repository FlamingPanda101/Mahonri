# John D. Meade

**Role:** Old Testament scholar; specialist in the biblical canon and textual criticism
**Tradition:** Evangelical Christianity
**Era:** Contemporary (active 2020s)

## Bio

John D. Meade is a professor of Old Testament with a specialization in the **Septuagint**, the biblical **canon**, and **textual criticism**. McClellan introduces him as a professor at Midwestern Baptist Theological Seminary; Meade is also associated with Phoenix Seminary and the Text & Canon Institute. He authored an essay on the development of the Christian canon in the *New Oxford Handbook of Textual Criticism of the Bible*.[^1] These are genuine scholarly credentials — which is why McClellan frames the criticism below as an *oversight* a trained text-critic should not have made, rather than mere amateur error.

## Cited as the Foil on Isaiah 9:6

In [[Dan McClellan - Responding to Wes Huff and John Meade on Isaiah 9-6]], Meade appears in a ~45-minute discussion on [[Wes Huff]]'s channel about [[Isaiah 9.6|Isaiah 9:6]] and [[Isaiah 7.14|Isaiah 7:14]] as messianic prophecies. McClellan engages Meade's segment on Isaiah 9:6 specifically, charging that Meade:

- Truncated his reading of the **Septuagint** of the verse, stopping at "messenger of great counsel" and omitting the expansive remainder that renders the divine names as a clause — see [[The Septuagint of Isaiah 9-6 Is Expansive Not Truncated]].[^1]
- Presented the **Codex Sinaiticus** marginal note giving the Hebrew sense as if it were contemporary with the 4th-century manuscript and evidence of pre-Jerome Christian concern for the Hebrew, when the note is a fifth-to-seventh-century correction — see [[The Codex Sinaiticus Marginal Note Postdates Jerome]].[^2]

McClellan addresses his appeal for correction *directly to Meade* (rather than to Huff, whom he treats as a bad-faith actor), inviting timestamps that would show where he misread the argument.[^3]
