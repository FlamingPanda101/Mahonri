# Andrew Byers

**Role:** New Testament scholar; specialist in the Gospel of John
**Era:** Contemporary (active 2010s–2020s)
**Tradition:** Academic biblical studies (identifies as a Christian)

## Bio
Andrew Byers is a New Testament scholar whose monograph *Ecclesiology and Theosis in the Gospel of John* (Cambridge University Press, Society for New Testament Studies Monograph Series, 2017) argues explicitly that the Fourth Gospel carries a **soteriology of divinization (theosis)** — that becoming "one" with God in John is a form of deification, not mere association.

## John's Soteriology Is Divinization
[[Dan McClellan]] quotes Byers' conclusion at length to ground the claim that John 10:34 (citing [[Psalm 82.6|Psalm 82:6]]) and John 17 together depict humanity and divinity as **porous**. In Byers' words, the prayer "that they may be one as we are one" "envisions an ecclesiology of divine participation as believers enter the Father-Son interrelation as family members newly generated and in the process of divinization," so that "oneness is deification as well as divine association." See [[Johannine Divinization Soteriology]], [[John 17.20-23|John 17:20-23]], and the source note [[Dan McClellan - The Bible Says We Will Become Gods]].
