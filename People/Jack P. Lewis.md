# Jack P. Lewis

American biblical scholar (1919-2018) of the Churches of Christ, Harvard-trained (Ph.D.), long associated with Harding School of Theology. He is widely cited for his study of how particular verses have been interpreted across history — including a well-known treatment of the meaning of "the gates of Hades" in Matthew 16:18.

In the [[Capturing Christianity - Debate The Great Apostasy Joe Heschmeyer vs Jacob Hansen|Heschmeyer–Hansen debate]], [[Jacob Hansen]] enlists Lewis to support reframing [[Matthew 16.18|Matthew 16:18]]: Lewis holds that "the gates of Hades" is "a figure of speech for death, which cannot keep the Christian imprisoned," and warns that the verse "should not be twisted into being considered a proof text" for the indefectibility of the institutional church. Hansen uses this (with the Catholic Confraternity commentary) to argue that the promise is about Christ conquering death, not about the perpetuity of a visible governing church — so the verse does not refute a Great Apostasy. [[Joe Heschmeyer]] replies that even on the "power of death" reading, a 1,700-year loss of the institutional church *would* be death prevailing.
