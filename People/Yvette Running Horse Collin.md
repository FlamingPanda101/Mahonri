# Yvette Running Horse Collin

**Era:** 20th–21st century (contemporary)
**Tradition:** Native American (Indigenous scholar)
**Role:** Scholar and advocate of Native horse culture

## Bio

Yvette Running Horse Collin is a Native American scholar who, by the account given on *Discover the Book of Mormon*, wrote her doctoral dissertation at the **University of Alaska Fairbanks** challenging the standard claim that the horse was introduced to the Americas by Europeans. [[Kirk Magleby]] describes her as "a vocal advocate from the Native American community" who "loves horses" and is "attempting to deconstruct a Eurocentric mythology" — the assumption that Europeans had to bring a benighted people the horse.[^1]

## The "Eurocentric Mythology" Thesis

Her argument, as relayed, is that Native nations "have always had the horse," that their origin myths place the creation of the horse alongside the creation of humankind, and that Indigenous testimony deserves "a place at the table" in scholarship on horse history. Magleby notes the dissertation "has been somewhat controversial because not everybody's prepared to believe an indigenous voice." He parallels her plea to the Latter-day Saint case that the [[Book of Mormon]] is a neglected "ancient American codex" deserving a hearing. See [[Native American Testimony That Horses Were Always Here]].[^1]
