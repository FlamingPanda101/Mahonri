# Trygve Mettinger

**Full name:** Trygve N. D. Mettinger
**Role:** Scholar of the Hebrew Bible / Old Testament; specialist in Israelite religion and ancient Near Eastern iconography
**Tradition:** Academic biblical studies
**Affiliation:** Lund University (Sweden), emeritus

## Bio

Trygve Mettinger is a Swedish biblical scholar best known for his work on the history of Israelite religion, the divine name and presence theology, and especially the question of **aniconism** (image-free worship). His monograph *No Graven Image? Israelite Aniconism in Its Ancient Near Eastern Context* is a foundational treatment arguing that early Israelite/Judahite cult imagery was non-representational. He has also written influentially on the "Shaphan" presence-theology traditions and on dying-and-rising-god motifs in the ancient Near East.

## De Facto Aniconism

In *Data Over Dogma*'s episode on seeing the face of God, [[Dan McClellan]] invokes Mettinger's thesis of a **de facto aniconism**: that the absence of anthropomorphic divine statuary in ancient Israel and Judah stemmed from material, economic, and market limitations of these small territorial states rather than from an explicit institutional ban on images. Their divine representations were symbolic (e.g., the standing stone) rather than figural. McClellan presents this as one position in an ongoing scholarly debate over whether the God of Israel was ever given anthropomorphic representation. See [[De Facto Aniconism Mettinger]], [[Israelite Aniconism Was Later Development]], [[Standing Stone Massebah as Material Medium of Divine Presence]].
