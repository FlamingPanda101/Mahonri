# Mike Winger

**Role:** Evangelical pastor and YouTube Bible teacher (BibleThinker)
**Era:** Contemporary, American
**Tradition:** Evangelical Protestant

## Bio

Mike Winger is an American evangelical pastor who runs the popular "BibleThinker" YouTube ministry, producing long-form teaching and apologetics videos. Among them is a video advertised as "irrefutable proof Mormonism is false," built around the [[Book of Abraham]]: he argues that because the surviving Joseph Smith papyrus dates to the second century BC (c. 1,800 years after Abraham), reads as a routine funerary text, and is dismissed by early-twentieth-century Egyptologists, Joseph Smith's translation is demonstrably fabricated.

## Relevance to the vault

Winger is the **foil** of the Ward Radio rebuttal [[Ward Radio - Debunking Mike Wingers Irrefutable Proof Mormonism is FALSE]]. Hosts [[Cardon Ellis]] and [[Luke Hansen]] argue that (1) the "written by his own hand" phrase is normal ancient copyist usage, not a claim of autograph; (2) every scholar Winger quotes traces to a single 1912 questionnaire circulated by an Episcopal bishop, not to current scholarship; and (3) every argument Winger makes against the Book of Abraham parallels the arguments atheists make against the Bible's own historicity — which Winger rejects when his own scripture is the target. See [[The Hand of Abraham Phrase Is Ancient Copyist Usage Not Autograph]], [[Wingers Egyptologists All Trace to a 1912 Episcopal Questionnaire]], and [[The Anti-Book-of-Abraham Argument Is the Atheist Argument Against the Bible]].
