# Stephen T. Davis

**Role:** Philosopher of religion
**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** Christian

## Bio

Stephen T. Davis is an American philosopher of religion, longtime professor at Claremont McKenna College, who has written widely on the resurrection, hell, and universalism.

## "Oohku" and the fate of the unevangelized

[[David Paulsen]] quotes Davis's article "Universalism, Hell, and the Fate of the Ignorant" (*Modern Theology*, 1990) for its vivid statement of the **soteriological problem of evil**: the case of **Oohku**, a woman in the interior of Borneo c. 370–320 B.C. who "could not have" heard of Christ — "born in the wrong place and at the wrong time." Davis's intuition that a just and loving God would not condemn her supplies a premise in the [[The Soteriological Problem of Evil - The Inconsistent Triad|inconsistent triad]] Paulsen resolves.
