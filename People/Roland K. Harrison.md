# Roland K. Harrison

**Full name:** Roland Kenneth Harrison (1920–1993)
**Role:** Old Testament scholar
**Era:** Contemporary (20th century)
**Tradition:** Protestant (evangelical/Anglican)

## Bio

R. K. Harrison was a British-Canadian biblical scholar and professor of Old Testament at Wycliffe College, University of Toronto. His massive *Introduction to the Old Testament* (1969) became a standard conservative reference work, and he served as general editor of major commentary and reference series. He defended traditional positions on the dating and unity of Old Testament books.

## Relevance to the vault

Harrison appears as the scholarly background to the vault's argument that Isaiah is apocalyptic and applies simultaneously to Isaiah's own day and the end times. Avraham Gileadi is said to have studied the synchronous structure of Isaiah (discovered by [[William Brownlee]] in the Dead Sea Isaiah scroll) from the doctoral dissertation of a colleague of Harrison at Toronto, locating Gileadi's literary approach within a recognized academic lineage.
