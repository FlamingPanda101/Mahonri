# Aubrey Johnson

**Full Name:** Aubrey Rodway Johnson (1901–1985)
**Era:** 20th century
**Tradition:** British Old Testament scholarship (Welsh)
**Role:** Professor of Semitic Languages, University College Cardiff; FBA

## Bio
Aubrey R. Johnson was a leading mid-century scholar of the Hebrew Bible in the cult-functional tradition, known for *The Cultic Prophet in Ancient Israel* (1944; 2nd ed. 1962) and *The Cultic Prophet and Israel's Psalmody* (1979), as well as *Sacral Kingship in Ancient Israel*. He argued that prophecy was not always anti-institutional: there were **cultic prophets** attached to the temple, organized in guilds, who prophesied through music (1 Chronicles 25). [[Mike Day]] and [[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)]] read him as a "proto-Margaret Barker." See [[Margaret Barker]].

## On the Cultic Prophet and the Creed of the Psalms
Johnson held that David appointed guilds of **singing, prophesying temple musicians** (Asaph, Heman, Jeduthun) whose work is embedded in the Psalms; that these guilds likely lost prophetic credibility after wrongly prophesying "peace" before Babylon, surviving only as musicians; and that the cultic prophets are *older* than the Deuteronomistic school, so where Deuteronomy and the Psalms overlap the Psalms have priority. In an epilogue he concludes that the Psalms' recurring "way, the truth, and the life" and "the righteous shall live by his faith" are a **creed** that bears directly on the birth of Christianity. See [[Cultic Prophets Were Singing Temple Musicians]], [[The Cultic Prophets Creed Was the Way the Truth and the Life]], [[The Psalms Predate and Were Not Rewritten by the Deuteronomists]].
