# Francisco Pizarro

**Role:** Spanish conquistador; conqueror of the Inca Empire
**Era:** 15th–16th century (c. 1471–1541)
**Tradition:** Secular (Spanish Catholic)

## Bio

Francisco Pizarro was the Spanish conquistador who led the conquest of the Inca Empire in the 1530s, capturing the emperor [[Atahualpa]].

## Relevance to the vault

In [[Ward Radio - Deep Dive Debunking Doubts About Horses in the Book of Mormon]], [[Jonah Barnes]] argues that the *cultural source* of the belief that pre-Columbian America had no horses is the famous story of Pizarro riding up to [[Atahualpa]] on horseback, the Inca allegedly terrified of an animal they had never seen. The video's point is that this single dramatic episode — high in the Andes, told by the Spanish to assert technological and divine superiority — became the lens through which everyone (including [[Joseph Smith]]'s era) assumed horses were wholly absent, even though the same assumption does not survive the later spread-of-horses evidence elsewhere. See [[The Pizarro-Atahualpa Story Is the Source of the No-Horses Assumption]].
