# Paul the Apostle

Paul of Tarsus (c. 5–64/67 CE), apostle to the Gentiles. Author of the earliest Christian writings (~50s CE), including Romans, 1-2 Corinthians, Galatians, Philippians, 1 Thessalonians, and Philemon. Never met the historical Jesus; his letters are theological rather than biographical.

## On the Scandal of the Cross
[[Tom Holland]] reads Paul as himself *embarrassed* by the crucified Christ at the center of his preaching — "a stumbling block" to Jews and "foolishness to the Greeks" ([[1 Corinthians 1.23|1 Cor 1:23]]) — because crucifixion was a slave's death; Paul "constantly tries to preempt objections," direct evidence the cross's inversion of Roman values was felt as scandalous at the origin. Holland also notes the risen Christ's words to Paul ("kicking against the pricks," Acts) echo Euripides' *Bacchae*. See [[The Cross as Moral Inversion of Roman Values]] and [[Christianity Emerged from Many Rivers Not From Nothing]].
