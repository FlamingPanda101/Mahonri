# David Whitmer

**Who:** David Whitmer (1805–1888), one of the **Three Witnesses** to the Book of
Mormon and a key firsthand source on how the book was produced. The translation
occurred partly in his family's home in Fayette, New York.

**Role in this debate:** Whitmer is the source for the widely cited "tight
control" account of translation — that [[Joseph Smith]] would see English words
appear on the seer stone, read them off to scribe [[Oliver Cowdery]], and the
words would not disappear until correctly transcribed and repeated back. Critics
use this account to argue the translation left little room for Joseph's
interpretive judgment, which would make any later **editing** of the text
theologically awkward.

## On the Translation Process

The Saints Unscripted host pushes back on the strong reading of Whitmer's
testimony, noting that (1) Joseph himself only ever claimed to translate "by the
gift and power of God," and (2) the canonized [[D&C 9|Doctrine and Covenants 9]]
describes a more participatory method ("you must study it out in your mind").
The host concludes Whitmer is "either mistaken or that perhaps we wildly
misinterpret what he meant." See [[David Whitmer Tight Control Account vs the Participatory Translation Model]].

## Reaffirmed Under Threat of Death (McLellin's Record)
Former apostle [[William McLellin]] — cited by [[Keystone]] — recorded asking Whitmer and [[Oliver Cowdery]] in 1833, with their lives in danger, whether the Book of Mormon was true; Whitmer affirmed it ("we could not be deceived"). McLellin also saw Whitmer reaffirm his testimony in June 1879 "as sincerely and solemnly" as in 1831. See [[William McLellin Affirmed the Book of Mormon While Rejecting Church Leadership]].
