# Cyril of Alexandria

Fifth-century Bishop of Alexandria (c. AD 376–444), the dominant theological force in the Christological controversy against [[Nestorius]].

## Significance
Cyril led the opposition to [[Nestorius]] at the First Council of Ephesus (AD 431), defending the title *Theotokos* (Mother of God) and the unity of Christ's person. His *Second Letter to Nestorius* was read and "accepted as doctrinally accurate" at the Council of Chalcedon (AD 451), alongside [[Leo the Great]]'s Tome — making Cyril one of the authorities whose writings shaped the Chalcedonian Definition of Christ's two natures in one person. (His Christological emphasis on Christ's unity also later fed the *miaphysite* tradition that split from Chalcedon.) See [[Ephesus and Chalcedon Defined the Two Natures of Christ]].
