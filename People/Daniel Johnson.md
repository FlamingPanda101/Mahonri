# Daniel Johnson

**Era:** 20th–21st century (contemporary)
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint (LDS)
**Role:** Independent researcher and apologist on Book of Mormon horses

## Bio

Daniel Johnson is a Latter-day Saint researcher from the Atlanta, Georgia area who studies the question of pre-Columbian horses in the Americas as it bears on the [[Book of Mormon]]. On the *Discover the Book of Mormon* channel he joins host [[Kirk Magleby]] to make the case that the "no horses before the Spanish" consensus is overstated.[^1]

## Argument on Horses

Johnson assembles the documentary and iconographic case: Maya draft-animal and wheeled-effigy imagery; the circular "if it looks like a horse it must be post-contact" dating he reports reading in scientific papers (see [[If It Looks Like a Horse It Must Be Post-Contact - Circular Dating of New World Horses]]); the corpus of cave and cenote horse remains from the Yucatán — Mercer's *Hill Caves of Yucatan*, the Loltún Cave, and the Mayapán cenote material published in the *Journal of Mammalogy* (1957) — including extinct species (*Equus conversidens*, *E. mexicanus*) once misidentified as modern (see [[Pre-Columbian Horse Remains Found in Yucatan Caves and Cenotes]]); and the point-of-contact testimony that Native nations already possessed full horse cultures the Spanish stallion-heavy expeditions were ill-suited to seed (see [[Native American Testimony That Horses Were Always Here]]).[^1]
