# Jack Welch

John W. ("Jack") Welch is a prominent Latter-day Saint scholar, longtime BYU professor, founder of FARMS, and editor associated with *BYU Studies*. He is widely known for his work on chiasmus in the [[Book of Mormon]].

## "An Unparallel"

Welch authored an article titled "An Unparallel" arguing against *View of the Hebrews* dependence. He observes that the specific "proofs" [[Ethan Smith]] uses — Hebrew words, Hebrew names, and customs supposedly found among Native Americans — do *not* appear in the Book of Mormon; instead the book uses names like Sam, [[Nephi]], and [[Lehi]] that have since received independent Hebrew/ancient attestation and never appeared in *View of the Hebrews*. Smoot credits Welch (along with Truman G. Madsen) with sustained study of the question when the Roberts material resurfaced in the 1980s. See [[Book of Mormon Lacks the Hebrew Markers View of the Hebrews Uses]].[^1]
