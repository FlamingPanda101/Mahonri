# Doug Wilson

**Era:** Contemporary (late 20th–21st century)
**Tradition:** Reformed (Communion of Reformed Evangelical Churches)
**Role:** Pastor, author, polemicist

## Bio
Douglas Wilson is a Reformed pastor in Moscow, Idaho, and a prolific author and debater within conservative evangelical and Reformed circles. He is cited here not as an interlocutor but as a **friendly-fire witness** quoted by [[Hayden Carroll]].

## Topics

### The Protestant Canon Has No Table of Contents
Carroll quotes Wilson conceding the Protestant **canon problem** from the inside: "The problem with contemporary Protestants is that they have no doctrine of the table of contents of the Bible... the Bible just is, and any questions about how it got here are dismissed as a nuisance," with the result that "conversions of thoughtful evangelicals to Rome proceed apace." Carroll deploys this admission against [[Jeff Durbin]]'s presuppositional confidence in the 66-book canon. See [[The Three Unjustified Protestant Canon Presuppositions]] and [[The Fallible Canon Is Hostage to Critical Scholarship]].[^1]

### A Baptist Who Came to Accept Infant Baptism
[[Trent Horn]] cites Wilson (a Baptist who changed his mind) on how the New Testament's silence cuts both ways: there is no NT example of an infant being baptized — but equally none of the child "walk-the-aisle, then get baptized" practice Wilson himself underwent at age 10. See [[The Argument from Silence Cuts Both Ways on Infant Baptism]].[^2]
