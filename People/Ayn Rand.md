# Ayn Rand

**Full Name:** Ayn Rand (born Alisa Zinovyevna Rosenbaum)
**Era:** 1905–1982
**Tradition:** Atheist; founder of Objectivism
**Role:** Novelist and philosopher

## Bio

Ayn Rand was a Russian-American novelist and philosopher, best known for the novels *The Fountainhead* (1943) and *Atlas Shrugged* (1957) and for founding the philosophy she called **Objectivism**. Objectivism holds that reality is objective, reason is the only means of knowledge, and the proper moral purpose of life is the pursuit of one's own **rational self-interest** — a principled ethical egoism that treats altruism as a moral error.

## Topics

### Rational Egoism (cited as the endpoint of secular morality)
[[Jacob Hansen]] quotes Rand to illustrate where a purely rational, secular account of morality leads once "the only well-being you experience is your own":

> "Maybe you've heard it: happiness comes from making other people happy. ... And that's the trouble. Let's aim at the day when people will not hear it anymore because it isn't true. ... Why is it good to want others to be happy but not yourself? You can make others happy when and if those others mean something to you selfishly. If you love them then you want to make them happy. ... You can allow only those whom you value, and if they contribute to your happiness." [^1]

Hansen uses Rand (alongside [[Friedrich Nietzsche]]'s will to power) as a *consistent* atheist witness that morality, reasoned to the bottom without God, becomes self-interest — a conclusion he says "should make you uncomfortable," and which he answers by positing a God who connects the well-being of all.
