# Spencer W. Kimball

Spencer W. Kimball (1895–1985) was the twelfth president of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (1973–1985), best known for the 1978 revelation (Official Declaration 2) extending the priesthood to all worthy male members regardless of race.

## On God's Light Among the Nations
[[Daniel C. Peterson|Daniel Peterson]] cites a First Presidency statement issued under President Kimball (the February 1978 statement on God's love for all mankind) that paid tribute to [[Muhammad]] as having received "a portion of God's light," alongside other "great leaders" such as Confucius and Plato. Peterson offers this as evidence that the Church has historically extended a generous, pluralistic reading of inspiration to non-LDS religious figures.[^1] See [[Early LDS Leaders Praised Muhammad as Inspired]], [[Other True Prophets Do Not Undercut Joseph Smith]].
