# Seth (Transcendent Philosophy)

**Role:** Atheist content creator, blogger, and debate interlocutor
**Era:** Contemporary (active 2020s)
**Tradition:** Atheism / naturalism

## Bio

Seth runs the YouTube channel and blog *Transcendent Philosophy*, where he writes on philosophy of religion and related topics. He became acquainted with [[Jacob Hansen]] as an online "interlocutor" in Hansen's Facebook group *Thoughtful Saints*, and the two staged a formal debate on whether atheism or theism better explains reality.[^1] (Hansen once refers to him as "Seth Garrett" in passing, but Seth identifies himself only as Seth of Transcendent Philosophy.)

## Argues Theism Fails on Epistemology

In debate, Seth frames the existence-of-God question as one of epistemology — "god-based vs. god-less" ways of reaching the facts — and argues theism has a poor track record across mythology, spiritual experience, morality, and logic. His unifying charge is that theism is a god-of-the-gaps project that retreats as natural explanations advance. See:
- [[Theism Is a God-of-the-Gaps Project Doomed by the Track Record]]
- [[Naturalistic Debunking of Spiritual Experience]]
- [[The Contradiction Test for God's Existence]]
- [[The Test World Does Not Look Like a Test]]

## Defends Fine-Tuning Without a Designer

Against the design argument, Seth grants necessity as a third option, reframes fine-tuning as selection bias (the universe looks tuned for black holes, not humans), and defends a parsimonious, inflation-backed multiverse coupled with cosmic natural selection — appealing to Sean Carroll, Lee Smolin, and Brian Greene. He argues that random variation plus selection produces the "illusion of fine-tuning" at every level, perhaps including the laws of physics. See:
- [[The Multiverse and Cosmic Selection Reply to Fine-Tuning]]
- [[Fine-Tuning - Why the Universe Is Best Explained by a Mind]]
