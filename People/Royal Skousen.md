# Royal Skousen

**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint scholarship
**Role:** Linguist; director of the Book of Mormon Critical Text Project (BYU)

## Bio
Royal Skousen is a Latter-day Saint linguist best known for directing the **Book of Mormon Critical Text Project**, the decades-long effort to reconstruct the earliest text of the Book of Mormon from the surviving Original Manuscript and Printer's Manuscript. His work catalogues thousands of textual variants and proposes the most likely original readings where the manuscripts and early printings diverge.

## On the "Sword" of the Justice of God
[[Keystone - This Is Why LDS Temples Are Mindblowing|Keystone]] cites Skousen for the reading that the divider between the wicked and the righteous in [[Lehi]]'s dream ([[1 Nephi 8|1 Nephi 8]]) was originally the **sword** of the justice of the eternal God — "and the brightness thereof was like unto the brightness of a flaming fire" — and that the current "**word** of the justice" is a miscopy from the original manuscript.[^1] Keystone uses the emended reading to strengthen the parallel between Lehi's dream and Eden's flaming sword. See [[Lehi's Dream Recapitulates the Eden-Temple Return]].
