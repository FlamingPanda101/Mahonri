# Esau

Elder twin son of [[Isaac]] and Rebekah; sold his birthright to his younger brother [[Jacob (patriarch)|Jacob]] for a "mess of pottage" (Genesis 25) and lost the patriarchal blessing ([[Genesis 27|Genesis 27]]). Treated within the vault as scriptural narrative.

## The non-wicked elder brother as a type of the forfeited firstborn
[[Andrea Woodmansee]] and [[Stick of Joseph|The Stick of Joseph]] hosts note that Esau is an *outlier* among the displaced-firstborn stories: unlike Reuben or Cain, "Esau doesn't seem like he's a super bad dude — he's just a regular guy." This makes the Jacob/Esau episode read less as a wickedness story and more as a story of a firstborn who *forfeits* his birthright so the next can have it — a pattern read as a type of Christ giving us his birthright. See [[The Blessing of Jacob Is a Veil Recognition Scene]] and [[Two Brothers Where the Younger Is Chosen Are Types of Christ and Lucifer]].
