# James Tabor

Scholar of Christian origins and the Dead Sea Scrolls who has written "a couple, three very interesting books on this subject" of [[James the brother of Jesus|James]], earliest Christianity, and the [[Paul vs James and the Judaizer Conflict|Paul–James]] divergence. In the [[The Stick of Joseph - Jesuss Brother Corruption in Christianity and the Great Apostasy|Stick of Joseph episode]], [[Mike Day]] pairs Tabor with [[Robert Eisenman]] as a (probable) atheist whose secular, "material point of view" reconstruction of the early church still highlights the historical primacy of James — the brother who took over and ran the movement for ~30 years while Paul, the former persecutor claiming a vision, ultimately won the followers. Day invokes Tabor's secular framing to dramatize how "crazy" that outcome looks without a divine perspective. See [[Paul Invented Christianity as a Cloak for James's Nazirite Judaism]].
