# Gaye Strathearn

Latter-day Saint scholar of the New Testament and early Christianity, associate professor of ancient scripture at Brigham Young University. Her published work includes studies of Gnostic and Valentinian texts (the Gospel of Philip, the Gospel of Judas).

## Contribution
Author of Chapter 6, "Human Nature: Creation and the Fall," in [[Ancient Christians - An Introduction for Latter-day Saints]]. She surveys three patristic interpreters of human nature — [[Irenaeus of Lyons]], [[Origen]], and [[Augustine]] — to show the early Church had no systematic doctrine of human nature and reached strikingly different conclusions on the [[Image of God - Patristic Interpretations|image of God]], the [[Irenaeus on the Fall as Childlike Growth Not Corruption|Fall]], the [[Origen on the Preexistence and Fall of Souls|preexistence of souls]], and [[Original Sin Was a Late Western Development|original sin]]. She highlights points where LDS readers resonate with the pre-Augustinian Greek Fathers.
