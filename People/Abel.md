# Abel

Second son of Adam and Eve, murdered by his brother [[Cain]] ([[Genesis 4|Genesis 4]]) — the first murder and the first martyr.

## The blood of Abel
Jesus invokes "the blood of Abel" as the beginning of prophet-murder ([[Luke 11.47-51|Luke 11:47-51]]). Apocryphal tradition holds the earth refused to receive his body (and his blood would not dry) until Adam was buried first. See [[Blood of Abel - Unburiable Tradition]].

## How Abel was killed (apocryphal traditions)
Apocryphal Adam-and-Eve texts describe Cain luring the wary Abel to the field and killing him with great difficulty (sawing the throat with a flint), sometimes drinking his blood to seize his life. See [[The Apocrypha Reveals How Cain Killed Abel by the Throat]] and [[Cain Weaponizes Empathy to Lure Abel - The First Crybully]].
