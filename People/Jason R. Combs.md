# Jason R. Combs

Latter-day Saint scholar of early Christianity and the New Testament at Brigham Young University. His academic work includes "'Christ' after the Apostles: The Humanity and Divinity of the Savior in the Second Century" and broader study of early Christology.

## Contribution
Author of Chapter 7, "Divine Nature: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit," in [[Ancient Christians - An Introduction for Latter-day Saints]] — the volume's treatment of how Trinitarian/Godhead doctrine developed. He traces the path from [[Tertullian Coined Trinitas and Held Subordinationism|Tertullian]] (who coined *trinitas* and held subordinationism) through [[Homoousios Was Coined to Refute Arius|Nicaea]], [[The Nicene Creed Said Little About the Holy Spirit|Constantinople]], and [[Ephesus and Chalcedon Defined the Two Natures of Christ|Ephesus and Chalcedon]]. He argues the creeds drew heavily on scripture, corrects the "without body, parts, or passions" phrase as a 1530 Protestant formula, and cautions against the simplistic LDS "Greek philosophy corrupted God" thesis while affirming the LDS embodied God.
