# Grant Palmer

Grant H. Palmer was a longtime Church Educational System instructor who became a naturalistic critic of Latter-day Saint origins, best known for *An Insider's View of Mormon Origins*. On the [[Ward Radio]] stream he is named as a key source-text and influence behind the [[CES Letter]].[^1]

## Role in the CES Letter's Sources

[[Michael Peterson]] argues that the introduction to Palmer's *Insider's View of Mormon Origins* closely parallels both Runnells's "Open Letter to Quentin L. Cook" (Oct 2012) and, a year later, the CES Letter itself — positioning Palmer's book as part of the recycled body of material that the letter's sympathetic "honest seeker" framing was designed to make palatable. Peterson notes that [[Jeremy Runnells]] ordered Palmer's book early in his deconstruction, "kept coming back to" it, and on Nov 25, 2012 publicly linked readers to a Palmer video that attempts a "total takedown" of LDS truth claims. See [[Adversarial Church-Bashing Predates the CES Letter by Months]] and [[The Shiny Wrapper Was the Bait of the CES Letter]].[^2]
