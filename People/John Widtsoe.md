# John Widtsoe

**Full name:** John A. Widtsoe
**Role:** Chemist/scientist and later Apostle of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints
**Era:** Early 20th century
**Tradition:** The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (LDS / Mormon)

## Bio

John A. Widtsoe was among the first generation of elite-credentialed LDS academics. His 1906 book *Joseph Smith as Scientist* argued the Prophet's teachings could be fused with cutting-edge science, emblematic of the assimilation-era push to reconcile science and faith. Though not yet an apostle, he was respected enough that his feedback **softened the 1909 "Origin of Man" First Presidency statement**: where early drafts explicitly denounced Darwinian evolution, the final version (per his counsel) prioritized scriptural truth while leaving room for evolutionary inquiry (see [[The 1909 Origin of Man Read in Its Historical Context]]). [[Benjamin Park]] notes Widtsoe was later called to the Quorum of the Twelve, part of the Church's apparent course-correction after the [[The 1911 BYU Evolution Controversy and the Firing of Four Professors|1911 firings]]. He is grouped with [[James Talmage]] as the public face of LDS scientific assimilation. [^1]
