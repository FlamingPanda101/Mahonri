# Ham

A son of [[Noah]] whose line, per [[Genesis 10|Genesis 10]], includes Cush, Mizraim (Egypt), Phut, and Canaan. The "curse of Canaan" (Genesis 9:25) attached to his descendants became, in the 18th–19th centuries, "the most commonly-used text from the Bible to support slavery" of people of African descent.

## The Curse of Ham Reinterpreted
[[Don Bradley]] argues that [[Joseph Smith]], in the [[Book of Abraham]] and the [[Grammar and Alphabet of the Egyptian Language]], reframes the curse on Ham's line not as enslavement but as a division of stewardship — kingly blessings rather than priestly ones — and ultimately as a blessing of kingship ([[Pharaoh]]). See [[The Curse as Division of Labor Not Punishment]] and [[Cursed to Be Kings - The Pharaoh Reversal]].
