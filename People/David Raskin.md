# David Raskin

University of Utah professor cited by the Ward Radio series as one of the academic voices that named the Utah panic for what it was. He told the *Salt Lake Tribune* that Utah was "gripped in mass hysteria and fear of a non-existent evil cult satanic ritualistic child abuse," and that the state committee "was chasing a fantasy despite a lack of supporting evidence." [^1]

## Role in the collapse
Raskin's verdict belongs to the cluster of credentialed and official conclusions — alongside investigator [[Mike King]]'s findings ([[Ritual Abuse Crime Unit Found Ninety-Nine Percent Wild Goose Chases]]) and the Utah courts — that the cases were baseless. See [[False Memory Reports Caused Documented Wrongful Convictions]] and [[Public Belief in Satanic Ritual Abuse Outlasted the Evidence]]. [^1]
