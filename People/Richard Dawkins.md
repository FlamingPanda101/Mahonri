# Richard Dawkins

**Full Name:** Clinton Richard Dawkins
**Era:** Contemporary (1941–present)
**Tradition:** Atheist (one of the "Four Horsemen" of New Atheism)
**Role:** Evolutionary biologist, author, public intellectual

## Bio

Richard Dawkins is an English evolutionary biologist and prominent atheist
activist. He held the Charles Simonyi Chair for the Public Understanding of
Science at Oxford University from 1995 to 2008. His most famous works include
*The Selfish Gene* (1976), *The Blind Watchmaker* (1986), and *The God
Delusion* (2006).

Dawkins is one of the "Four Horsemen" of New Atheism alongside [[Sam Harris]],
[[Christopher Hitchens]], and [[Daniel Dennett]].

## Topics

### Atheism and Morality
Dawkins is quoted in the video for his famous statement about the moral
implications of atheism:

> "The universe we observe has precisely the properties we should expect if
> there is at bottom no design, no purpose, no evil, no good, nothing but
> pitiless indifference."[^1]

[[Hayden Carroll]] uses this quote to demonstrate that consistent atheism leads
to moral nihilism — the absence of objective moral distinctions. See [[Moral Argument Against Atheism]].

### Design Argument
Dawkins' *The Blind Watchmaker* argues that natural selection can produce the
appearance of design without a designer — the central atheist response to the
teleological argument. Carroll's [[Fine-Tuning and the Teleological Argument from Alma 30]] directly engages this line of reasoning.
