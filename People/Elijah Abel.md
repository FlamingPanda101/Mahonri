# Elijah Abel

An early Black Latter-day Saint ordained to the priesthood — an elder and a Seventy — during the lifetime of [[Joseph Smith]], around the same period Joseph translated the [[Book of Abraham]]. His ordination is primary evidence that Joseph Smith did not read [[Abraham 1.21-27|Abraham 1]]'s "cursed as pertaining to the priesthood" as a racial priesthood bar.

## Significance for the Priesthood Question
[[Don Bradley]] cites Abel as the clearest case showing Joseph "actually does have Black men ordained to the priesthood... right around the same time that he translates this." Host [[Stephen Jones]] notes he has portrayed (played) Elijah Abel and researched him extensively. See [[Joseph Smith Ignored Race-Based Scriptural Distinctions]] and [[Testing Interpretation by the Prophet's Actions]].
