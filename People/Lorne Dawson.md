# Dr. Lorne Dawson

Retired Canadian professor and one of the leading scholars of new religious
movements (NRMs) from the "classical period" of the 1980s–2000s. His research
bridged NRM studies with processes of radicalization (particularly post-9/11).

## Key Scholarly Contributions

- Authored *Understanding Cults: The Sociology of New Religious Movements*
- His empirical work on NRM membership (two massive independent studies
  showing <1% join rate, 90% leave within 2 years) was instrumental in
  courts rejecting brainwashing testimony as unscientific.
- Traces the history of "cult" from neutral sociological term → evangelical
  pejorative → pop-culture insult.

## Core Positions

1. **"Cult" is not a useful analytical term** — it hides more than it reveals.
2. **Brainwashing has no scientific basis** — rejected by APA, ASA, and courts
   worldwide.
3. **People join NRMs through normal processes** — curiosity, seeking
   meaning, identity formation.
4. **NRM joiners are not weak or gullible** — they're overwhelmingly educated,
   middle-class, from secular backgrounds.
5. **High-demand religious groups are sociologically normal and often healthy**
   — "churches that dilute their standards too much fail."
6. **Early Christianity was an NRM** — "from a Roman citizen's perspective, it
   would fit all the pejorative concepts of cult."
