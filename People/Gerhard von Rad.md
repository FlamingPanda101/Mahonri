# Gerhard von Rad

**Who:** German Old Testament scholar (1901–1971), a major 20th-century figure in Pentateuchal criticism and the theology of the Hexateuch. (Day jokes about the pronunciation — "vonrad," not "ro-t" — and notes you must spell it *von Rad* to find him.)

**Role in this discussion:** [[Mike Day]] and [[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)|Dave Butler]] invoke von Rad to extend [[Julius Wellhausen]]'s argument that the wilderness sanctuary is a literary projection. Von Rad argued there are **two originally independent Moses traditions** — the **Exodus / wilderness-journey** tradition and the **Sinai** tradition — "totally different" complexes later fused in the Hexateuch. He also supplies the Hexateuch-narrative argument Day reads aloud: the patriarchal stories give "no historical knowledge of the patriarchs, but only of the time when the stories about them arose," the later age "projected... into hoar antiquity... like a glorified mirage." See [[The Tabernacle Is a Retrojection of the Temple]] and [[The Patriarchal Cult Mirrors Pre-Reform Practice That Lehi Revives]].
