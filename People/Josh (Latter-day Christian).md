# Josh (Latter-day Christian)

**Role:** Latter-day Saint content creator and apologist (TikTok handle "Latter-day Christian")
**Era:** Contemporary (active 2020s)
**Tradition:** The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints

## Bio
Josh, who goes by "Latter-day Christian" on TikTok, is a Latter-day Saint apologist who responds to common Protestant and Evangelical objections to the Church. He co-hosts with [[Colton Miller]] on the [[Stick of Joseph]] episode reviewing anti-Mormon arguments (Galatians 1:8, Jesus/Lucifer as brothers, polygamy, grace vs. works), where he frequently appeals to the underlying Greek (e.g. *eikon* in Colossians 1:15) and to context to defuse proof-texts.

## On the Godhead, grace, and the Greek text
Josh's recurring move is to check the original Greek before accepting a proof-text's claimed meaning: he reads Colossians 1:15's "image of the invisible God" as *eikon* (icon/representation, not the thing itself), and John 10:30's "one" as *hen* (unity, not numerical identity of being). On salvation he frames LDS faith as a James-and-Sermon-on-the-Mount obedience ("faith without works is dead") that still rests on the grace of Christ. See:
