# James Bevan Weetman

**James Bevan Weetman** (also given as "Bert" or "James Albert" Weetman) was a Latter-day Saint **mission president in Chihuahua, Mexico**, during the Mexican Revolution.

## Bio / Significance
According to the *Meridian Magazine* account relayed in the [[Ward Radio - The Secret Mormon Exploits of Pancho Villa|Ward Radio episode]], Weetman and a stake president (Bentley) were captured by [[Pancho Villa]]'s men and accused of being spies for the American army. Weetman demanded audience with the leadership, was put on trial expected to end in execution, and instead turned the proceeding into a multi-hour discussion of the Church's work, the Book of Mormon, and the gospel — after which Villa granted the missionaries papers of safe passage.

## Contribution
Weetman is the named firsthand source for the **posthumous visitation**: decades later he reportedly saw [[Pancho Villa]] at the foot of his bed pleading for help to enter a "beautiful city," after which Weetman secured a First Presidency authorization and performed Villa's temple work at the Mesa Arizona Temple. The chain of custody for the story (his grandson is the husband of the article's author) is what the hosts cite for its credibility. See [[Pancho Villa's Posthumous Visitation and Temple Work]].
