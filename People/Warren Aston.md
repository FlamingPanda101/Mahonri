# Warren Aston

**Role:** Independent researcher and fieldworker (Lehi's Arabian trail)
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint (LDS)

## Bio

Warren Aston is an Australian Latter-day Saint researcher best known for decades of on-site exploration of [[Lehi]]'s Arabian journey. He is the author of *In the Footsteps of Lehi* (1994) and *Lehi and Sariah in Arabia* (2015), and a co-author (with [[Neal Rappleye]] and Godfrey Ellis) of the 2024 Interpreter volume *Into Arabia*. He is credited with pursuing the Nehem lead after noticing Ross/Terry Christensen's 1978 *Ensign* note, traveling to Yemen, and identifying additional maps, variant spellings, and the extensive burial grounds along the Wadi Jawf.

## Role in this video

[[Neal Rappleye]] repeatedly cites Aston as the field authority behind the case:

- **Following up the Nehem lead.** Aston noticed the old *Ensign* note about a "Nehem" map on his commute, contacted FARMS, went to Yemen, and found additional maps and variant spellings of the NHM name. [^1]
- **Inscriptions and burials.** Aston's 2012 BYU Studies article and 2015 book flagged other NHM-bearing inscriptions, and he documented the thousands of above-ground "turret tomb" burials that, Rappleye notes, trace one of the eastward trails out of the Wadi Jawf — see [[Nahom Names a Tribal Region Not the Altar Findspot]] and [[Nahom and NHM Converge with Nephis Eastward Turn]]. [^2]
- **Bountiful and Khor Kharfot.** Aston favors **Khor Kharfot** (over George Potter's Khor Rori) as Nephi's inlet, and argues the ship's timber came from local natural resources — proposing the coconut palm and, more recently, tamarind heartwood as candidates — see [[Bountiful and Khor Kharfot Match the Book of Mormon]]. [^3]
