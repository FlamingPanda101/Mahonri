# Richard Müller

**Full name:** Richard A. Muller (b. 1948)
**Role:** Historian of Reformed and post-Reformation theology
**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** Protestant (Reformed)

## Bio

Richard A. Muller is a senior professor of historical theology at Calvin Theological Seminary and the foremost authority on the development of Reformed orthodoxy after Calvin. His four-volume *Post-Reformation Reformed Dogmatics* and works such as *The Unaccommodated Calvin* reshaped the field by showing deep continuity between the Reformers, medieval scholasticism, and Protestant scholasticism.

## Relevance to the vault

Müller is cited to establish that the magisterial Reformation was doctrinally *conservative*: "the doctrines of God, the Trinity, creation, providence, predestination, and the last things were taken over by the magisterial reformation virtually without alteration." This supports the vault's thesis that the Reformers were reformers, not revolutionaries — renewing the ancient church rather than inventing a new theology, alongside [[John Calvin]]'s appeal to antiquity against [[Cardinal Sadoleto]].
