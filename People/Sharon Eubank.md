# Sharon Eubank

Sharon Eubank is a Latter-day Saint humanitarian leader, former director of [[Latter-day Saint Charities]] and former first counselor in the General Relief Society Presidency. She is the author of *Doing Small Things with Great Love* (the title drawn from a [[Mother Teresa]] quotation), a collection of 92 stories from her humanitarian career.

In the [[Let's Get Real with Stephen Jones - Sharon Eubank on Doing Small Things with Great Love (E0040)|Let's Get Real interview]] with host [[Stephen Jones]], Eubank argues that the "premier skill" of humanitarian and ministering work is not money or power but the relational question "What do you want? What's going on in your life?" — the "exchange" being worth more than money.[^1] She draws her core conviction — that "you are powerful where you live" — from watching her Sri Lankan interpreter Shanta minister more effectively after the 2004 tsunami than she could with a global NGO's funds.
