# J.L. Mackie

**Full Name:** John Leslie Mackie
**Era:** 1917–1981
**Tradition:** Atheist (analytic philosophy)
**Role:** Australian philosopher; author of *Ethics: Inventing Right and Wrong* (1977) and *The Miracle of Theism* (1982)

> [!note] Not to be confused with [[J.L. Schellenberg]] (the divine-hiddenness philosopher), with whom the vault's notes are careful to distinguish him.

## Bio

J.L. Mackie was a leading atheist philosopher best known in meta-ethics for **moral error theory** — the view that ordinary moral judgments purport to describe objective, intrinsically prescriptive facts, but that **no such facts exist**, so *all* positive moral claims are systematically *false*. His **argument from queerness** holds that objective values, if they existed, would be metaphysically and epistemologically "queer" — utterly unlike anything else in the natural world. He also formulated the modern **logical problem of evil** (1955) and critiqued theistic arguments in *The Miracle of Theism*.

## Role in the vault

[[Trent Horn]] turns Mackie's queerness argument **against atheism** in [[The Counsel of Trent - The Moral Madness of Atheism]]. Horn quotes Mackie: objective, intrinsically prescriptive features "supervening upon natural ones constitute so odd a cluster of qualities and relations that they are most unlikely to have arisen in the ordinary course of events without an all-powerful god to create them." Mackie took the *oddness* as reason to **deny** objective morality (hence error theory); Horn takes the same oddness as evidence that *if* there are objective moral truths, **theism best explains them**. See [[The Modest Moral Argument from Particular Puzzling Acts]] and [[Moral Argument Against Atheism]].[^1] His *Ethics: Inventing Right and Wrong* is also the work [[C.S. Lewis]]'s defenders explicitly reject in the vault's discussion of the cross-cultural moral law ([[Illustrations of the Tao - Cross-Cultural Evidence for Universal Moral Law]]).
