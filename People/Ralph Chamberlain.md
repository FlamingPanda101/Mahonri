# Ralph Chamberlain

**Role:** Biologist (PhD, Cornell); BYU professor dismissed in the 1911 evolution controversy
**Era:** Early 20th century
**Tradition:** The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (LDS / Mormon)

## Bio

Ralph Chamberlain (often spelled Chamberlin) held a PhD in biology from Cornell and was one of four BYU professors at the center of the [[The 1911 BYU Evolution Controversy and the Firing of Four Professors|1911 evolution controversy]]. With his brother [[William Chamberlain]] and the Peterson brothers, he taught organic ([[Charles Darwin|Darwinian]]) evolution and argued it could be reconciled with the LDS gospel. After the apostolic committee's ultimatum, he resigned. He went on to the University of Pennsylvania for a time and eventually returned to the University of Utah, where he became a respected scientist. [[Benjamin Park]] cites him as part of the cohort that, in the long run, "won" the argument as BYU later embraced evolutionary biology. [^1]
