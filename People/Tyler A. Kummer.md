# Tyler A. Kummer

**Tyler A. Kummer** is an assistant teaching professor in the Department of Biology at Brigham Young University, where he serves as coordinator for Bio 100. He previously taught science in Alpine School District and has been an adjunct professor at BYU since 2017. His research focuses on the learning of evolutionary trees and the assessment of evolution understanding.[^1]

## What Evolution Is and Is Not
With co-author [[Jamie L. Jensen]], Kummer wrote "Wonderful Forms of Life Have Been and Are Being Evolved," a primer defining evolution as **descent with modification** and clarifying that it is *not* a theory of the origin of life. See [[What Evolution Is and Is Not - Descent with Modification]] and the evidence survey in [[The Evidence for Common Descent]].

## Correcting Misconceptions
As a biology educator, Kummer targets the misconceptions that block acceptance — "humans came from monkeys" (ladder thinking), the micro/macro distinction, "individuals evolve," and the "randomness is too improbable" argument. See [[Common Misconceptions About Evolution Corrected]].
