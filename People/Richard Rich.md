# Richard Rich

**Era:** Contemporary (b. mid-20th century; active animator from the 1960s)
**Tradition:** The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (LDS / Mormon)
**Role:** Animator and director; ordained patriarch

## Bio

Richard Rich is a Latter-day Saint animator and director who began at Disney in the 1960s (working on *Robin Hood* and *The Fox and the Hound*), later directed his own features including *The Swan Princess*, and produced the *Daddy, Laman, and Lemuel* / Living Scriptures animated videos familiar to LDS children. On [[Ward Radio]], [[Cardon Ellis]] holds him up as the model of a serious, sanctified **patriarch**: Ellis recounts that when he invited Rich onto the show, Rich repeatedly declined, explaining that giving patriarchal blessings had become his full-time spiritual focus and he wanted nothing — even a podcast he enjoyed — to distract his faculties from that calling. Ellis uses Rich (alongside [[Oak Norton]]'s father) as evidence that "the one calling in the church you can't fake is a patriarch."
