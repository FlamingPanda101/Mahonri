# James Cobb

James T. Cobb was a stepson of [[Brigham Young]] who became disillusioned with the church and set out to prove the Book of Mormon was a sham. In 1880 he wrote to the former apostle [[William McLellin]], hoping McLellin would supply damaging material against the book.[^1]

Cobb's plan backfired: McLellin's reply affirmed the Book of Mormon in the strongest terms and urged Cobb to "cease your opposition and strife against the book, for you might just as well fight against the Rocky Mountains." When Cobb suggested McLellin and [[David Whitmer]] "ought to come out and tell something, some darkness relative to that book," McLellin answered, "We should lie if we did, for we know nothing against its credibility or divine truth."[^2] See [[William McLellin Affirmed the Book of Mormon While Rejecting Church Leadership]].
