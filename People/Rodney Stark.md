# Rodney Stark

American **sociologist of religion** (1934-2022), best known for applying rational-choice and demographic modeling to religious history. His book *The Rise of Christianity* (1996) argues that early Christianity grew at a steady, plausible rate (roughly 40% per decade) from a tiny first-century movement into a major share of the Roman population by the mid-fourth century — *before* Constantine's patronage — explaining the growth sociologically rather than as a top-down imperial imposition.

In the [[Capturing Christianity - Debate The Great Apostasy Joe Heschmeyer vs Jacob Hansen|Heschmeyer–Hansen debate]], [[Joe Heschmeyer]] cites Stark's estimate of roughly **33 million Christians by about the year 350** to argue that the post-apostolic church does not look like "a movement that's failed" or apostatized, but one bearing the fruit of explosive, organic growth — undercutting [[Jacob Hansen]]'s picture of a church that lost its life once the apostles died. (Heschmeyer pairs the point with [[Bart Ehrman]]'s similar account in *The Triumph of Christianity*.)
