# Sennacherib

**Role:** King of Assyria (Neo-Assyrian Empire)
**Era:** Iron Age / Ancient Near East (reigned 705–681 BCE)
**Tradition:** Mesopotamian / Assyrian

## Bio

Sennacherib was king of Assyria from 705 to 681 BCE, son of Sargon II and father of [[Esarhaddon]]. He campaigned aggressively in the Levant, destroying Lachish and besieging Jerusalem under Hezekiah (2 Kings 18–19; Isaiah 36–37), and rebuilt Nineveh as his magnificent capital. He was assassinated by his own sons, and Esarhaddon succeeded him.

## Relevance to the vault

Sennacherib appears as a fixed point in Assyrian royal genealogy: Esarhaddon's treaties open by titling their author "King of the World, King of Assyria, son of Sennacherib." This matters to the vault's discussion of Deuteronomy's literary form, where the Neo-Assyrian vassal-treaty pattern (preamble naming the suzerain's lineage, divine witnesses, stipulations, curses) is argued to be the template behind the structure of Deuteronomy.
