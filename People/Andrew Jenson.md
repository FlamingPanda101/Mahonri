# Andrew Jenson

**Role:** Assistant Church Historian; compiler of early plural-marriage records
**Era:** 1850–1941
**Tradition:** The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints

## Bio
Andrew Jenson was an assistant Church Historian who, in 1886-87, compiled and published the first systematic list of [[Joseph Smith]]'s plural wives, gathering affidavits from surviving participants. His publication of [[Joseph Bates Noble]]'s affidavit fixed April 5, 1841 in many people's minds as the date of Joseph's first Nauvoo plural marriage (to [[Louisa Beeman]]).

## Significance
[[Don Bradley]] accepts Jenson's list as foundational data but argues the Noble-supplied date it enshrines is unreliable, requiring a re-ordering that puts [[Zina Huntington Jacobs]] first. See [[Joseph Smith Married Pregnant Women Refuting the Sexual-Motive Thesis]].
