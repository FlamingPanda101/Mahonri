# Ishmael (Book of Mormon)

**Era:** c. 600 BC (Lehite migration)
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint scripture (Book of Mormon)

## Who He Is
Ishmael is the patriarch of a second family who joins [[Lehi]]'s party in the wilderness so that Lehi's and Ishmael's children can marry (1 Nephi 7; 16). He **dies and is buried at a place called Nahom**, after which his daughters mourn exceedingly and the party turns eastward toward Bountiful ([[1 Nephi 16.34|1 Nephi 16:34]]).

## Archaeological Significance
Ishmael is the subject of one of the strongest Book of Mormon archaeological convergences. A funerary stela bearing **Yasmaʿil** (the South Arabian form of Ishmael), dating to roughly the 6th century BC and originating in the Wadi Jawf / Nahom region, fits a foreigner from the Jerusalem area dying near Nahom — discovered in the archaeological record by [[Neal Rappleye]]. See [[The Ishmael Funerary Stela in South Arabia]], [[Nahom and NHM Converge with Nephis Eastward Turn]], and [[Nahom Exceeds Biblical Sites by Archaeologys Own Standards]]. The name was common in 600 BC Jerusalem but uncommon in that part of Arabia at that date — strengthening the "right name in the right place" inference.[^1]
