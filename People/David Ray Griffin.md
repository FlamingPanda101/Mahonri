# David Ray Griffin

**Era:** Contemporary (1939–2022)
**Tradition:** Christian (process theology)
**Role:** Professor emeritus, Claremont School of Theology; process philosopher and theologian

## Bio
David Ray Griffin was professor emeritus of philosophy of religion and theology at the Claremont School of Theology and a leading exponent of process theology (in the tradition of Whitehead and Hartshorne). [[Alex Arnold]] cites him as a non-LDS thinker who, "with no engagement with LDS theology," reconstructed a finite-theist position central to the Restoration.[^1]

## On Finite Theism and the Problem of Evil
Griffin "concluded that finite theism, or a God who works within eternal law rather than above it, is the only intellectually honest answer to the problem of evil." Arnold notes that his 1976 book *God, Power, and Evil* "made the argument decades before LDS apologists were framing [it] in those terms."[^1] He is a central exhibit in [[Finite Theism Is the Honest Answer to the Problem of Evil]], and Arnold also credits him with arguing the constrained-process response to evolutionary animal suffering "from process philosophy" — see [[Animal Suffering Is the Hardest Test for Finite Theism]].[^2]
