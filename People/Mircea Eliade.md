# Mircea Eliade

Romanian-American historian of religions, long at the University of Chicago, known for his comparative theory of myth and ritual — sacred vs. profane time and space, the "eternal return," and the thesis that a culture's **cosmogony serves as the paradigm** for how its people are to live.

## Cosmogony as a model for living
[[Don Bradley]] (who says he first read Eliade as a BYU student) invokes this thesis to argue that the creation account in the [[Book of Moses]] functions as a template for human conduct: because the Father creates through the Only Begotten and humans are in the Son's similitude, people are to act "in the name of the Son." See [[The Creation Story Is a Model for How to Live]].[^1] His framework is also echoed in the vault's temple-cosmogony material ([[The Temple Reenacts the Primordial Mound Rising from the Waters of Chaos]]).
