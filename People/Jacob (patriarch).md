# Jacob (patriarch)

Younger twin son of [[Isaac]] and Rebekah, later renamed Israel ([[Genesis 32.24-28|Genesis 32]]); the eponymous father of the twelve tribes. Treated within the vault as scriptural narrative. (Distinct from the Book of Mormon prophet Jacob, brother of [[Nephi]].)

## The blessing of Jacob as a temple recognition scene
[[Andrea Woodmansee]] reads the strange episode of [[Genesis 27|Jacob obtaining Esau's birthright blessing]] (Rebekah putting goat hair on his hands, blind Isaac identifying him by touch) as a veiled temple ceremony: a son who, *because of who he has become*, now holds the birthright comes before a father behind a veil (Isaac's blindness) and is identified by the **marks on his hands** — a type of Christ, the true birthright Son recognized by the marks in his hands. See [[The Blessing of Jacob Is a Veil Recognition Scene]] and [[Two Brothers Where the Younger Is Chosen Are Types of Christ and Lucifer]].
