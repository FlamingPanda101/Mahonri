# Abraham

The patriarch and "father of the faithful," with whom God made the Abrahamic covenant of land, posterity, and blessing (Genesis 12; 15; 22). In the LDS canon the [[Book of Abraham]] supplies a first-person backstory absent from Genesis: Abraham as a seeker of God, nearly sacrificed by his own father, rescued in a theophany.

## As a Backstory Figure
[[Don Bradley]] argues Genesis presents Abraham as a "flat character" who never states his motives, whereas [[Abraham 1.1-4|Abraham 1]] gives his inner desires — home, peace, family, knowledge, priesthood. See [[Abraham as a Backstory to Genesis]] and [[Seeking God Versus Arbitrary Election]].

## Abraham's First Vision
His rescue and call in [[Abraham 1.15-19|Abraham 1:15-19]] follow the same pattern as Moses 1 and [[Joseph Smith]]'s First Vision. See [[Abraham's First Vision Pattern]].

## The Binding of Isaac
His near-sacrifice as a youth illuminates his trust when later asked to offer [[Isaac]] ([[Genesis 22|Genesis 22]]), and the event points to the Father's sacrifice of His Son. See [[The Binding of Isaac as a Type of Christ]] and [[Prophets Point to Christ Through Their Failings]].
