# Bill McKeever

Bill McKeever is a longtime evangelical critic of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, founder of Mormonism Research Ministry, who (per [[Hayden Carroll]]) "has been doing anti-LDS stuff since the 60s" and belongs to the older [[Sandra Tanner]] generation of countercult ministry.[^1] He is a fixture at events outside LDS gatherings such as the Mesa Temple pageant.

## The "plates were too heavy" argument
At a Ratio Christi conference where [[Hayden Carroll]] spoke after him, McKeever reportedly spent half of a 60-minute talk arguing that the golden plates "would have been too heavy for Joseph Smith to run through the woods with," bringing a weighted model and inviting the audience to lift it.[^2] Carroll treats this as a textbook self-defeating objection — it presupposes Joseph was no prophet while claiming to disprove that he was, and is inconsistent with the critic's own belief in biblical miracles. See [[The Plates Were Too Heavy Is a Self-Defeating Anti-Mormon Argument]].
