# Kolby Reddish

**Who:** A former Latter-day Saint, attorney, and former criminal prosecutor from Boise, Idaho (BYU–Provo; undergraduate degree in micro/molecular biology) who became a frequent voice on critical podcasts — Mormon Stories (episode 1550 details his faith crisis), Mormonism Live, and a series with RFM. He resigned from the church with his wife around 2023.[^1]

**Why he matters here:** In the [[Mormonism with the Murph - Evaluating the Testimony of the Witnesses of the Gold Plates|Murph dialogue]], Reddish supplies the critic's side on the gold-plates witnesses, and his legal/scientific background shapes a distinctive, non-polemical case.

## His Faith Crisis and Posture
Reddish locates the catalyst of his 2021 faith crisis in his sitting bishop being charged with child sex abuse and his ward "rallying around" the accused — compounded by his earlier work prosecuting child-abuse cases, which sharpened the [[The Problem of Evil Does Not Disprove God|problem of evil]] for him and pushed his theism toward deism. He frames his deconversion as honest and gradual, modeling [[The Charitable-but-Critical Believer Posture|epistemic humility]] and refusing to call believers dishonest.[^2]

## On the Witnesses
- Even as a believer he never found the witnesses compelling; as an attorney he treats eyewitness testimony for supernatural claims as inherently insufficient — not from a charge of lying or [[Witnesses Conspiracy Theory Rebutted|conspiracy]], but because **memory reframes** spiritual experience. His proof was finding his own mission journal and seeing he had unconsciously embellished a "dark spirit" experience over a decade. See [[Naturalistic Debunking of Spiritual Experience]].[^3]
- He grants a **plate artifact** existed and follows [[Dan Vogel]] that [[Joseph Smith]] likely made rudimentary tin plates, citing the four-year "delay" in obtaining them. But he disagrees with Vogel less on the *historiography* than he agrees with him on the conclusion — see [[John Whitmer Spiritual Eyes Objection Rebutted]] for where Murph pushes back on the Eight.[^4]
- He raises the **"magical worldview"** not to call the witnesses gullible but to *contextualize* their claims, and presses the **special-pleading** problem of better-attested rival miracles like Fatima — see [[Mutual Vulnerability of Supernatural Claims]].[^5]
- His master argument is epistemological: [[Every Testable Joseph Smith Claim Fails Only Subjective Evidence Survives|whenever Joseph makes a testable claim it fails]] (stone box, Book of Abraham papyri, Zelph, Kinderhook, and his fresh Great-Isaiah-Scroll finding), so subjective witness testimony cannot carry the divine claim.[^6]
