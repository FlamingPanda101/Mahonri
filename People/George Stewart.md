# George Stewart

**Era:** 20th–early 21st century (d. 2014)
**Tradition:** Academic (non-LDS)
**Role:** Mesoamerican archaeologist

## Bio

George Stewart (almost certainly George E. Stuart, the National Geographic
Mesoamerican archaeologist and founder of the Center for Maya Research) is cited by
Rappleye via a relayed anecdote. Stewart reportedly said publicly that scholars had
excavated only ~5% of Mesoamerica and "we don't know squat," and in private among
peers lowered the figure to 1%.[^1]

## Extent of Mesoamerican Excavation

The anecdote anchors the point that only a tiny fraction of the Americas has been dug,
so absence of evidence today is weak and paradigm-shifting discoveries remain possible.
See [[Only Two Percent of the Americas Has Been Excavated]].[^2]
