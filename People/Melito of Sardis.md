# Melito of Sardis

**Full name:** Melito, Bishop of Sardis (fl. second century AD; d. c. 180)
**Role:** Bishop, preacher, and theologian of Asia Minor
**Era:** 2nd century CE

## Bio
Melito was "by all accounts, one of the most important Christian voices of the second century." Ancient sources (Eusebius, *Eccl. Hist.* 4.26, and a letter of Bishop Polycrates of Ephesus in *Eccl. Hist.* 5.24) say "he always lived his life in the Holy Spirit." Apart from a few fragments, his only surviving complete work is the Holy Week homily *On the Pasch* (*On Pascha*, "On the Passover"), which was "only discovered and published in the last century." [^1]

## On Pascha
Melito's homily is "a rhetorically and typologically rich exploration of the meaning of the suffering and death of Christ," reading Christ's death through the Exodus Passover. For Melito the Passover lamb is only "a type, an impression, a sketch of the true story" — Christ's redemption of the world at Easter. The sermon culminates in a Christ-voiced invitation: "I am the Passover of salvation, I am the lamb slain for you; I am your ransom, I am your life, … I am your resurrection, I am your king." Heal notes Melito's style "is not to everyone's taste," and that the homily reflects the anti-Judaism endemic to its era. [^2]
