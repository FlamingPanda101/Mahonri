# Pulcheria

Eastern Roman empress (Augusta), AD 399–453. Elder sister of Emperor [[Theodosius II]]; after their father died she was, as a girl, "the power behind the throne" while her younger brother reigned.

## Significance
Pulcheria was "directly involved in the next major ecumenical councils at Ephesus and Chalcedon." The Council of Ephesus (431) "convened at the instigation of Pulcheria and others." After [[Theodosius II]] died heirless in a 450 riding accident, she "moved quickly," marrying the senator [[Marcian]] to make him emperor and herself official empress (he honored her vow of celibacy, so she kept her religious authority). One of her first acts was to have Marcian convene the Council of Chalcedon (451). [[Leo the Great]] wrote to her asking her to intervene against [[Eutyches]]. Her role illustrates how thoroughly imperial politics — and a powerful woman's agency — drove the formulation of orthodox Christology. See [[Ephesus and Chalcedon Defined the Two Natures of Christ]].
