# Don Bradley

LDS historian specializing in the foundational documents and translation projects of early Mormonism. He holds an MA in history (Utah State University) and is the author of *The Lost 116 Pages: Reconstructing the Book of Mormon's Missing Stories*. A former critic who left and later returned to the Church, he frequently appears on faithful podcasts to apply historical method to contested topics.

In the *Let's Get Real* interview with host [[Stephen Jones]], Bradley argues that the "racial curse" in [[Abraham 1.21-27|Abraham 1]] has been misread through the anachronistic lens of the post-1852 [[priesthood ban]], and he introduces a primary document — the [[Grammar and Alphabet of the Egyptian Language]] — that, he says, has never before been used in this debate.[^1]

## On the Book of Abraham and the Curse
He reframes the [[Book of Abraham]] as a *backstory* to Genesis ([[Abraham as a Backstory to Genesis]]), reads the Pharaoh "curse" as a division of stewardship rather than racial punishment ([[The Curse as Division of Labor Not Punishment]]), and argues it actually blesses the line of [[Ham]] with kingship ([[Cursed to Be Kings - The Pharaoh Reversal]]).

## On Historical Method
He insists on facing forward from [[Joseph Smith]]'s 1835 vantage rather than backward through later history ([[Reading Abraham 1 Without the Priesthood-Ban Lens]], [[Chronological Prejudice Against the Past]]) and on testing interpretation by the prophet's actions ([[Testing Interpretation by the Prophet's Actions]], [[Joseph Smith Ignored Race-Based Scriptural Distinctions]]).

## On Prophets and Translation
He distinguishes a prophet from an academic translator ([[A Prophet Is Not an Academic Translator]]), judges scripture by its fruits ([[Judge the Book of Abraham by Its Fruits]]), and reads the [[Binding of Isaac]] as a Christ-type ([[The Binding of Isaac as a Type of Christ]]).

## On the Leg Surgery, the Word of Wisdom, and Grief (E0039)
In a second, intensely personal [[Let's Get Real with Stephen Jones - Joseph Smith's Biggest Discovery (Don Bradley E0039)|Let's Get Real interview]] given while grieving the recent death of his son Donnie, Bradley calls his reading of [[Joseph Smith]]'s 1813 childhood leg surgery "the most foundational" insight he has found — a discovery made while he was an ex-Mormon atheist. He argues the boy refused alcohol to set a sober example for his father ([[Joseph Smith Refused Alcohol to Set an Example for His Father]]), supplies deep context for the [[Word of Wisdom]] in [[Emma Smith]]'s near-fatal childbirth ([[Emma's Difficult Childbirth Is the Immediate Context of the Word of Wisdom]]), and reflects on historical method ([[A Question Is a Lens That Shapes What the Historian Sees]], [[Abductive Reasoning Is How Historians Infer the Best Explanation]]), the problem of evil, and resurrection hope ([[Resurrection Is the Beginning of a New Creation]], [[Grace as Self-Compassion for the Sake of Those Who Love You]]).
