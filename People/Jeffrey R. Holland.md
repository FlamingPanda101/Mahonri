# Jeffrey R. Holland

**Era:** Contemporary (b. 1940)
**Tradition:** The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints
**Role:** Apostle (Quorum of the Twelve)

## Bio
Jeffrey R. Holland is a member of the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. He is cited here for his October 2008 General Conference address **"My Words Never Cease,"** an extended scriptural argument against the closed-canon doctrine, which [[Hayden Carroll]] plays at length to supply the positive Latter-day Saint alternative to *sola scriptura*.

## Topics

### Continuing Revelation and the Open Canon
In the played excerpt Holland argues that "virtually every prophet of the Old and New Testament has added scripture to that received by his predecessors" — if Moses sufficed, why Isaiah, Jeremiah, Ezekiel, and the rest? He quotes [[N.T. Wright]] ("all authority in heaven and on earth is given to *me*," not to "the books you are all going to write"), so "scripture itself points away from itself and toward... God himself." The ultimate source of authority is the **living God**, of which scripture is a manifestation, not a ceiling. He ties this to [[Joseph Smith History 1.68-69|Joseph Smith's]] reliance on James 1:5 and the sustaining of Thomas S. Monson as prophet. See [[God Himself Not a Book Is the Ultimate Authority]], [[Sola Scriptura Muzzles God]], and [[The Word of God Was Never Confined to the Written Word]].[^1]
