# Wayne May

**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint (LDS)
**Role:** "Heartland" advocate, publisher, and artifact collector

## Bio
Wayne May is a Latter-day Saint advocate of the "Heartland" Book of Mormon geography, publisher of *Ancient American* magazine, and a collector of North American artifacts (head plates, breast plates, and other objects he associates with Book of Mormon civilizations). He leads tours of the Ohio and Mississippi-valley mound sites.

## Relevance to the vault
May appears in the Neville interviews as a fellow Heartland figure: the hosts and [[Jonathan Neville]] reference visiting "Wayne's collection" and the Ohio mounds, and cite his reading of the Frederick G. Williams "30th parallel" note. He represents the artifact-and-mound-builder strand of the [[Oliver Cowdery's Letter VII States Cumorah Is in New York as Fact|New-York-Cumorah]] case. See [[The New Madrid Earthquake Explains Third Nephi's Darkness Without Volcanoes]].[^1]
