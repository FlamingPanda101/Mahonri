# Marion Smith

Utah therapist and the instigator of the Salt Lake Valley satanic-ritual-abuse episode reconstructed in Ward Radio Part 5. In 1982 she co-founded the Intermountain Sexual Abuse Treatment Center (ISAT) and trained at the leading edge of the new recovered-memory field, "right after *Michelle Remembers* but years before the McMartin trial, the Hadfield trial, or [[Barbara Snow]] having her license revoked." When her grandchildren's babysitter was accused, she accepted the word of a 9-month-old and a 2-year-old, routed her own grandchildren to Barbara Snow, and set off an expanding chain of accusations that eventually named a gas-company president, grocery-chain president [[Richard Miles]], the local bishop, and [[Brenda Miles]] (daughter of apostle [[Russell M. Nelson]]). Two of her daughters co-authored a 1992 book accusing Salt Lake suburbanites of SRA, with a forward by [[Paul Whitehead]].

## The accusation wheel
Smith built the clinic, supplied its subjects, and accepted pre-verbal "disclosures," driving a feedback loop in which accused adults and child witnesses multiplied each other. See [[The Accusation Wheel Self-Recruits Accusers Through Therapy]] and [[Accusations Cannot Transmute Into Evidence]]. [^1][^2]

## "Bill"
The wheel eventually landed on her own son-in-law "Bill"; even after he was cleared by police, investigators, and a Johns Hopkins / [[R. Jan Stout]] evaluation, Smith pressed lawsuits and a second book, after which Bill died by suicide — which she reportedly called "a cause of … shock and rejoicing." See [[An Innocent Cleared by Every Investigator Was Still Driven to Death]]. [^3]
