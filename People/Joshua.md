# Joshua

**Joshua** (Hebrew *Yehoshua*, "Yahweh saves") son of Nun, of the tribe of **Ephraim**, is Moses' attendant and military commander who succeeds him and leads Israel's **conquest of Canaan** (the Book of Joshua). He and Caleb are the two faithful spies (Numbers 13-14); he is commissioned as Moses' successor (Deuteronomy 31) and parts the Jordan, takes Jericho, and apportions the promised land among the tribes.

## Bio / Significance
As an Ephraimite, Joshua descends from **[[Joseph of Egypt]]** through Joseph's birthright son Ephraim. In the conquest narrative he is the type of the leader who brings the covenant people *into* their inheritance — a role Latter-day Saint readers connect typologically to [[Nephi]] (the new conqueror entering a new promised land) and ultimately to Christ (whose name is the Greek form of *Yehoshua*).

## Contribution
In the [[Ward Radio - The Sword of Laban Was the Sword of Joshua|Ward Radio interview with Don Bradley]], Joshua is the hinge of the relic-genealogy argument: a statement (via [[Francis Gladden Bishop]], from [[Martin Harris]]) says the sword later possessed by [[Laban]] "was caused to be made by [[Joseph of Egypt|Joseph]] of old in Egypt... and was in the hand of Joshua when he led the house of Israel into the land of Canaan." Because Joshua, Ephraim, and Laban are all of the Ephraimite/Josephite line, the sword passes down a military lineage from Joseph → Joshua → ultimately Laban. See [[The Sword of Laban Was the Sword of Joshua Forged by Joseph in Egypt]]. The separate figure [[Joshua the High Priest in Zechariah 3 Is an Explicit Temple Investiture|Joshua the high priest of Zechariah 3]] is a different, post-exilic Joshua.
