## Who
Cyril of Jerusalem (ca. AD 313–386) was the bishop of Jerusalem and a key witness to fourth-century baptismal and Eucharistic liturgy. His catechetical lectures — the *Protocatechesis* and the five *Mystagogical Catecheses* — prepared initiates to participate in the Eucharistic mysteries and explained the meanings of baptism and the Eucharist.

## Authority and Reason for Citation
Cyril is a primary source for reconstructing the late-fourth-century rite. He is cited for his instruction that celebrants receive the Eucharistic elements with the right hand while holding the left hand underneath "in the shape of a throne," symbolically receiving the royal body of Christ (Mystagogical Catechesis 5.21), and for his exposition of the Lord's Prayer petition "Thy will be done."

## Best Quote
