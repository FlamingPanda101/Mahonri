# Tucker Carlson

Popular political commentator who hosted the ~2.5-hour interview (with guest [[Nathan Apffel]]) that dedicated roughly 15 minutes to criticizing the finances of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. He is engaged throughout [[Latter Daily Saints - The Mormon Church Profits from War Claim Ep 109|this episode]] as the interlocutor who amplifies and escalates Apffel's claims.

- **Escalation to "lobbying for war":** Carlson takes Apffel's defense-stock point further, suggesting the Church promoted the COVID vaccine because it holds Pfizer and is "probably lobbying to declare war on people because they know it'll make them money." The hosts call this out: "There is zero evidence the church does that... actually contrary evidence."[^1] See [[Broad-Market Investing Is Not a Lobbying Tool]].
- **The "forced tithing" assumption:** Carlson assumes members must pay tithing to remain in good standing; the hosts rebut him directly — "You're dead wrong, Tucker" — explaining the temple-recommend question asks whether one is a "full tithe payer" (which can be true at zero dollars) and that no bank statements are checked.[^2]
- **Treated as the megaphone, not the originator:** The hosts note the segment, on a video with 1.4 million views, will "definitely not... contribute to people's positive opinions about the church," and they find it "deliciously ironic" that Carlson criticizes leaders "like they're all living high on the hog."[^3][^4] See [[Conflating the Church With Prosperity-Gospel Grifters]].
