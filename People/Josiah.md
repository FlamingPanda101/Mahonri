# Josiah

**Who:** King of Judah (c. 640–609 BCE), grandson of Manasseh. His reign and religious reforms are recorded in 2 Kings 22–23 and 2 Chronicles 34–35. He is credited with centralizing worship at the Jerusalem Temple and the discovery of the "Book of the Law."

**Role:** [[Jacob Wright]] identifies Josiah (along with [[Hezekiah]]) as a key figure behind the "united monarchy" narrative — a political program to claim the legacy of the destroyed northern kingdom of Israel. Wright describes both kings as engaged in a "make Israel great again" project, framing Judah's absorption of the northern population as a return to an idealized past under David and Solomon.
