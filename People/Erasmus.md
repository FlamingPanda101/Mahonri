# Erasmus

**Era:** Renaissance / Northern Humanism (c. 1466–1536)
**Tradition:** Catholic (reform-minded humanist; a forerunner the Reformers claimed, though he broke with Luther)
**Role:** Biblical philologist, editor, satirist

## Bio

Desiderius Erasmus of Rotterdam was the leading Christian humanist of his age, famous for the satire *In Praise of Folly* and for the editorial program *ad fontes* — "back to the sources." In 1516 he published the **Novum Instrumentum omne**, the first printed Greek New Testament, gathering the available Greek manuscripts to correct the centuries-long practice of translating the Bible *from* [[Jerome|Jerome's]] Latin Vulgate rather than from the originals. His text, refined through later editions, became the basis of the **Textus Receptus** ("the received text") and thus the textual foundation of the King James Bible.

The Ward Radio hosts treat him with open affection — "Erasmus is like one of my heroes" — and the host recalls playing a Dungeons & Dragons mage named Erasmus. [^1]

## Contribution — the Johannine Comma

Erasmus is the pivot of the episode's argument. His first two editions (1516, 1519) **omitted** [[1 John 5.7|1 John 5:7]] because no Greek manuscript he examined contained it. Challenged by churchmen for dropping the great Trinity verse, he (on the panel's telling) was handed a single Greek manuscript "found in England" that conveniently included the clause; suspecting it had been produced to corner him, but under pressure, he added it to his third edition (1522), whence it entered the *Textus Receptus* and the KJV. "Erasmus writes later on in the letter... 'I think they just made this garbage up.'" [^2] The historically attested manuscript is **Codex Montfortianus**; the dramatic "wager" framing is later legend, but the omission, the challenge, and the reluctant insertion are real.
