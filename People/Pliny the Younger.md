## Who
Pliny the Younger was a Roman governor (of Bithynia-Pontus) who, around AD 112, wrote to the emperor Trajan describing the practices of Christians in his province (Letters 10.96–97). His testimony is a key early non-Christian (external) source on Christian worship.

## Authority and Reason for Citation
As a hostile-but-observant outsider, Pliny provides independent corroboration of early Christian worship. He is cited for his report that when Christians met to worship they "sing responsively a hymn to Christ as God" — describing antiphonal singing and confirming a primitive high Christology in which Christ was worshipped as divine.

## Best Quote
> Christians "sing responsively a hymn to Christ as God." [^1]
