# Michael Coe

**Role:** Eminent Mesoamerican archaeologist (Yale)
**Era:** 20th–21st century (1929–2019)
**Tradition:** Secular (non-Latter-day Saint)

## Bio

Michael D. Coe was a foundational figure in Maya and Mesoamerican archaeology, Charles J. MacCurdy Professor Emeritus of Anthropology at Yale, and author of standard works including *The Maya* and *Breaking the Maya Code*.

## Relevance to the vault

Coe is invoked in [[Ward Radio - Fake Book of Mormon Artifacts]] as a double-edged example. The hosts acknowledge he was **outspoken against the Book of Mormon** (a point a caller raises as bias), yet [[Jerry Grover]] credits his methodology and publications as "very good." Coe also illustrates how academic judgments can reverse: he *argued against* the authenticity of the Grolier Codex (now the Maya Codex of Mexico), which was later upheld by multiple studies as the oldest surviving book in the Americas — a case the panel uses to show that poor provenance can be overcome by forensics, and that "fake" verdicts are sometimes wrong. See [[Forensics Can Override Poor Provenance]].
