# Peter Brown

**Peter Brown** is among the most influential historians of late antiquity (Princeton, emeritus), credited with effectively founding the modern field. His works *The Cult of the Saints* (1981) and *The Ransom of the Soul: Afterlife and Wealth in Western Christianity* (2015) are standard references.

## Wealth, Almsgiving, and the Economy of the Afterlife
Catherine Gines Taylor draws on Brown to describe how wealthy believers were "encouraged to give and could thereby hope to ascend into the starry heavens, almost by apotheosis." Brown's work documents how one's afterlife experience was "intimately mingled with the material ability to be remembered" and how the joining of heaven and earth by means of money "affected the view of the soul" — arguments about wealth and salvation that "would remain intertwined within the interior discourses of the Christian church for centuries." See [[Almsgiving Became the Economy of Salvation for the Dead]] [^1].
