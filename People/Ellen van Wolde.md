# Ellen van Wolde

Ellen van Wolde, Professor of Old Testament / Hebrew Bible at Radboud University Nijmegen (Netherlands) and a pioneer of cognitive-linguistic approaches to scripture. In [[Alex OConnor - There Are Multiple Gods in the Bible Not Just One - Dan McClellan]], [[Dan McClellan]] cites her argument that the verb *bara* in Genesis 1 does not mean "to create" but "to separate" — so the creation account is fundamentally about dividing pre-existing material into categories (light from dark, waters above from waters below) rather than producing matter from nothing.

This supports the broader reading that Genesis 1 presupposes pre-existent matter. See [[Bara Means to Separate and Genesis Is Organization Not Creation From Nothing]] and [[Creation Ex Nihilo Originated Late 2nd Century CE]]. (Her separation thesis is debated; some philologists maintain the traditional "create" sense.)
