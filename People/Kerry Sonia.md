# Kerry Sonia

**Full name:** Kerry M. Sonia
**Role:** Scholar of the Hebrew Bible and ancient Israelite religion
**Affiliation:** Colby College (Religious Studies)
**Era:** Contemporary
**Authority:** Tier A — author of a peer-reviewed scholarly monograph on the topic.

## Bio

Kerry Sonia is a biblical scholar specializing in death, the dead, and ritual in ancient Israel. Her monograph **_Caring for the Dead in Ancient Israel_** (Society of Biblical Literature, 2020) examines the cult of the dead, ancestor veneration, and mortuary ritual, and how these practices relate to (and overlap with) sanctioned worship in the Hebrew Bible.

## Role in the Discussion

[[Dan McClellan]] recommends Sonia's book as "a wonderful discussion of how ancestor worship worked," in support of his point that the deceased could be designated *elohim* and that temple worship in the Hebrew Bible overlaps significantly with the ancestor worship the text elsewhere denigrates. [^1]
