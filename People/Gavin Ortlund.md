# Gavin Ortlund

**Role:** Reformed Baptist theologian, pastor, and apologist; host of the *Truth Unites* YouTube channel
**Era:** Contemporary (active 2010s–2020s)
**Tradition:** Reformed / Baptist; classical theism

## Bio

Gavin Ortlund holds a Ph.D. in historical theology from Fuller Theological Seminary and is the president of Truth Unites, an apologetics ministry, as well as a pastor. He is a published author whose work engages historical theology, classical theism (including divine simplicity), and ecumenical apologetics. On his *Truth Unites* channel he conducts irenic dialogues and responses across confessional lines. He self-describes as holding "a pretty much classical view" of the Trinity, affirming divine simplicity and classical theism. [^1]

## The Trinity as a First-Rank Issue

In a civil dialogue with LDS apologist [[Jacob Hansen]], Ortlund argued the Trinity is "first rank" — a doctrine one cannot be saved without — because it determines *whom* and *how* we worship. [^2] He framed the stakes with a recurring "Cynthia vs. Sally" analogy (mistaking a person's identity is not a trivial error) and grounded the doctrine's importance in the God-man atonement logic of [[Anselm of Canterbury]]'s *Cur Deus Homo*. ## Christology and Scripture

Ortlund built his case on Jesus' self-claims and early high-Christology worship: [[John 8.24|John 8:24]] / [[John 8.58|John 8:58]] ("I am" / before Abraham), [[John 20.28|John 20:28]] (Thomas's confession), and [[Philippians 2.9-11|Philippians 2:9-11]] read through [[Richard Bauckham]]'s "divine identity" category. ## Creation and Classical Theism

Ortlund affirms creatio ex nihilo, divine aseity, and a "thick ontological distinction between God and all that is not God," citing [[Psalm 90.2|Psalm 90:2]], [[Nehemiah 9.6|Nehemiah 9:6]], and [[Michael Heiser]]'s "species-unique" framing of Yahweh among the *elohim*. He identifies creatio ex nihilo as the "lynchpin" dividing him from LDS theology. See:
