# Friedrich Nietzsche

**Full Name:** Friedrich Wilhelm Nietzsche
**Era:** 1844–1900
**Tradition:** Atheist (post-Christian)
**Role:** Philosopher, cultural critic

## Bio

Friedrich Nietzsche was a German philosopher whose work deeply influenced
existentialism, postmodernism, and 20th-century thought. His major works
include *Thus Spoke Zarathustra*, *Beyond Good and Evil*, and *The Genealogy of
Morals*. Nietzsche famously declared "God is dead" and explored the
consequences of the collapse of Christian moral frameworks.

## Topics

### Nihilism
Nietzsche is quoted in the video to illustrate the psychological endpoint of
atheism:

> "Nihilism as a psychological state will have to be reached. It is necessary
> first when we have sought a meaning in all events that is not there. So the
> seeker eventually becomes discouraged. Nihilism then is the recognition of
> the long waste of strength, the agony of the in vain insecurity, the lack of
> any opportunity to recover or to regain composure."[^1]

[[Hayden Carroll]] uses this quote to argue that atheism, when lived
consistently, leads to psychological nihilism — a state of despair and
meaninglessness. Carroll notes that "when you die, you die. Like there's no
eternal purpose."[^2]

However, Nietzsche did not advocate nihilism as a resting place; he sought to
*overcome* it through the will to power and the creation of new values. This is
a potential counterpoint: Nietzsche diagnosed nihilism as a transitional
illness, not a final destination.[^3]
