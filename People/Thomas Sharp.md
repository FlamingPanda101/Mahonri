# Thomas Sharp

**Era:** 19th century (1818–1894)
**Tradition:** Anti-Mormon (non-Latter-day Saint agitator)
**Role:** Editor of the *Warsaw Signal*; anti-Mormon political organizer

## Bio
Thomas Sharp was the editor of the *Warsaw Signal*, a newspaper in Warsaw, Hancock County, Illinois, and the most prominent anti-Mormon agitator in the events leading to the **martyrdom of [[Joseph Smith]] and [[Hyrum Smith]]** at Carthage on 27 June 1844. He organized an explicitly **anti-Mormon political party** to counter the Latter-day Saints' bloc voting in Nauvoo, and used the *Signal* to inflame public hostility against the Saints' fast-growing religious, economic, and political power.

## Why he matters
Sharp is the human pivot of the argument that the martyrdom was a **coordinated political assassination, not a random mob** (see [[The Martyrdom Was a Coordinated Political Assassination Not a Mob]]). Two weeks before the killings his paper printed an extermination call — "War and extermination is inevitable. Citizens arise one and all... Let it be made with powder and ball" — echoing the 1838 Missouri extermination order and aimed specifically at Joseph. [[Dallin H. Oaks]]'s documentary study *Carthage Conspiracy* (with Marvin Hill) names such figures as the planners behind the deaths. Sharp was among those tried for the murders; the defendants were acquitted. He stands as the documented *local* engine of premeditation, distinct from the vault's more speculative national-conspiracy gloss.
