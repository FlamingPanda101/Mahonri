# Socrates

**Role:** Greek philosopher
**Era:** Classical Greece (c. 470–399 BCE)
**Tradition:** Greek philosophy

## Bio

Socrates was an Athenian philosopher regarded as a founder of Western philosophy. He wrote nothing himself; he is known through the dialogues of his student [[Plato]] and the memoirs of [[Xenophon]]. Famous for the dialectical "Socratic method" of probing questions, he was tried and executed by Athens on charges of impiety and corrupting the youth in 399 BCE.

## Relevance to the vault

Socrates anchors the classical credentials of Xenophon, whom the vault cites for a Greek-idiom parallel ("parents bring forth children out of nothing") predating 2 Maccabees. Establishing that Xenophon — like Plato — was a student of Socrates underscores that he was "a core central thinker within classical thinking," not a fringe figure, which strengthens the weight given to his linguistic usage.
