# Willard Richards

One of four contemporaneous scribes who recorded the [[King Follett Discourse]] in
April 1844. Richards wrote in "a minutes style version of notes," providing a
more condensed account than [[William Clayton]]'s longhand or [[Thomas Bullock]]'s
shorthand.[^1]

Richards was one of the men with [[Joseph Smith]] at Carthage Jail when Smith was
killed in June 1844 — just two months after the King Follett Discourse was delivered.
