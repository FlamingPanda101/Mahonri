# Richard Elliott Friedman

**Role:** Biblical scholar; specialist in the composition of the Hebrew Bible
**Era:** Contemporary (b. 1946)
**Tradition:** Jewish / Academic

## Bio
Richard Elliott Friedman is the Davis Professor of Jewish Studies at the University of Georgia and the author of *Who Wrote the Bible?* (1987) and *The Bible with Sources Revealed*, popular and academic presentations of the Documentary Hypothesis (the J/E/P/D source analysis of the Pentateuch).

## Cited on the Deuteronomistic editing
[[Alex Arnold]] cites Friedman (alongside [[Israel Finkelstein]] & Silberman and [[Frank Moore Cross]]) as mainstream, non-LDS documentation that a school of [[Hezekiah Josiah and the Deuteronomist Reforms|Deuteronomistic]] scribes reshaped the older Israelite tradition — centralizing worship, enforcing exclusive Yahwism, and flattening the divine-council framework — which Arnold argues removed the pre-existent-heavenly-Messiah tradition Jesus later drew on. See [[The Deuteronomists Edited Out the Pre-Existent Divine Messiah]].[^1] Friedman's source-critical work is also invoked across the vault's [[Documentary Hypothesis Strengthens the Restoration]] cluster.
