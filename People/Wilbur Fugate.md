# Wilbur Fugate

One of the three Illinois men behind the [[Kinderhook plates]] forgery of 1843, and the one who eventually confessed. With Robert Wiley and the blacksmith Bridge Whitten, Fugate cut and acid-etched six bell-shaped brass plates, aged them to look ancient, planted them in a burial mound near Kinderhook, and had them "discovered" before witnesses — hoping [[Joseph Smith]] would translate them.

Per the [[Informed Saints - The Hoax That Should Have Fooled Joseph Smith|Informed Saints episode]], Fugate later said the hoax "started out as a joke" after reading [[Parley P. Pratt]]'s *Voice of Warning* and its line that "truth will spring forth out of the earth" — evidence (urged by [[Don Bradley]] and [[Mark Asher McGee]]) that the motive was a prank rather than a calculated anti-Mormon entrapment.[^1] His 1879 affidavit to W. P. Harris and his 1878 letter to James T. Cobb are the primary confessions; the Cobb letter also claims Joseph wanted the plates sent to the Antiquarian Society of Philadelphia before translating them — a late, secondhand detail that nonetheless supports [[Joseph Smith Sought Secular Authentication of Artifacts He Believed Ancient]].[^2]
