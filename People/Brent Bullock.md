# Brent Bullock

Bountiful, Utah resident and the original defendant in the [[Marion Smith]] / ISAT satanic-ritual-abuse case (rendered "Brett" in the auto-transcript at one point). He was implicated alongside the 16-year-old babysitter whose accusation seeded the episode, "convicted by a split bench," and served roughly a decade. Bullock maintained his innocence and refused a sex-offender treatment program that would have shortened his sentence by four years because entry required confessing to a crime he denied — so he served four years longer than comparable offenders.

## Innocence maintained against incentive
Bullock's refusal to "sell out" for early release is cited as a marker of a wrongful conviction produced by coercive interviewing rather than evidence; the dissenting Judge Stewart wrote that "the record is replete with instances of the use of coercion, threats, pressure, and suggestion by both Dr. Snow and several parents." See [[Accusations Cannot Transmute Into Evidence]] and [[An Innocent Cleared by Every Investigator Was Still Driven to Death]]. [^1][^2]
