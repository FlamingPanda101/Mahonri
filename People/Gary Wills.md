# Gary Wills

**Era:** Contemporary (1934–present)
**Tradition:** Catholic
**Role:** Historian, author, public intellectual

## Bio
Garry Wills is an American historian and author, a Pulitzer Prize winner (for *Lincoln at Gettysburg*), recipient of the National Medal for the Humanities, and a classicist proficient in ancient Greek and Latin. A devout Catholic, he holds graduate degrees from Jesuit institutions and is known across the political spectrum for rigorous, often contrarian scholarship.

## Cited For — The Brodie Review
On Ward Radio, Wills is held up as the model of a serious historian dismantling shoddy work. Reviewing [[Fawn Brodie]]'s psychobiography of [[Thomas Jefferson]] (*Thomas Jefferson: An Intimate History*) **before** the 1998 DNA episode, Wills wrote one of the most savage academic reviews on record: "Two vast things, each wondrous in itself, combined to make this book a prodigy — the author's industry and her ignorance. One can only be so intricately wrong by deep study and long effort enough to make Miss Brodie the fasting hermit and the very saint of ignorance." The hosts note that Wills himself was a left-of-center Obama voter, underscoring that his objection was methodological, not partisan. See [[Psychohistory Dresses Conjecture in the Veneer of Academia]].[^1]
