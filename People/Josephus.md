# Josephus

**Who:** Flavius Josephus (c. 37–100 CE), Jewish historian and former military commander who defected to the Romans. Wrote *The Jewish War* and *Antiquities of the Jews*.

**Role in biblical studies:** Josephus personally witnessed the Roman siege and destruction of Jerusalem and the temple in 70 CE. His accounts provide detailed eyewitness descriptions of the destruction that McClellan references when discussing the temple's fall — including Roman soldiers pushing stones off the temple walls that cratered the stone sidewalks below.

## Second Temple History
Josephus's eyewitness accounts of the temple's destruction form a key primary source for [[Second Temple History]]. See also [[Fig Tree Cursing as Metaphor for Temple]], since Mark's gospel (~70 CE) metaphorically depicts this same destruction.
