## Who
Ambrose was the influential bishop of Milan in the fourth century who helped spur the development of hymn singing in Latin Christianity. He was a mentor figure to Augustine.

## Authority and Reason for Citation
Ambrose is cited as a foundational figure in Western (Latin) hymnody. His morning hymn *Aeterne rerum conditor* ("Eternal Founder of the World"), written for dawn prayer services, uses the image of the crowing rooster to recall Peter's denials, tears of remorse, and the Lord's forgiveness — giving early Christians hope that they too could repent and be renewed each day.

## Best Quote (from the hymn)
> "Look on us, Jesus, in our wavering, and seeing us correct us; for if you look on us our sins leave us and our guilt is washed away in tears." [^1]
