# Brittany Wilson

**Full name:** Brittany E. Wilson
**Role:** New Testament scholar
**Era:** Contemporary (active 2010s–present)
**Tradition:** Academic

## Bio

Brittany Wilson is a New Testament scholar specializing in the Gospel of Luke, Acts, and the theme of divine embodiment and visibility in early Christianity. Dan McClellan describes her as having done "a bunch of wonderful work on the visibility of God in the New Testament." [^1]

Her book *The Embodied God: Seeing the Divine in Luke-Acts and the Early Church* examines how Luke-Acts navigates the tension between the Jewish tradition of a visible, embodied God and the growing Greek philosophical influence that rendered God invisible and immaterial. She demonstrates that Luke-Acts preserves significant elements of divine corporeality even as the broader theological trajectory moved toward incorporeality.

## Divine Visibility in the New Testament

Wilson's work is directly relevant to the contradiction between OT theophanies and NT statements about God's invisibility. While John 1:18 states absolutely that "no one has ever seen God," Luke-Acts contains moments where the divine is perceived in visible, even bodily form (e.g., Stephen's vision of Jesus at God's right hand in Acts 7:55–56). Wilson's scholarship documents that the NT is not monolithic on divine visibility — different authors navigate the tension differently.
