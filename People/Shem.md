# Shem

**Shem** is a son of **[[Noah]]** who survives the Flood and is the ancestor of the Semitic peoples and, through his line, of **Abraham** (Genesis 5-11). Beyond the genealogies and the blessing of Genesis 9:26 ("Blessed be the LORD God of Shem"), the biblical record says strikingly little about his life or ministry.

## Bio / Significance
The **silence** about Shem's ministry is the hook for a long interpretive tradition: a major patriarch who inherits the land of Salem and the priesthood line, yet whose deeds go unrecorded. **Jewish tradition** (e.g. Targumic and Talmudic sources) identifies Shem with **[[Melchizedek]]**, the priest-king of Salem.

## Contribution
In the [[Ward Radio - The Secret Identity of Melchizedek Revealed|Ward Radio episode]] (hosts [[Cardon Ellis]], [[Kwaku (All Those In Favor)|Kwaku]], and Jonah Barnes — drawing on David J. Ridges' *Old Testament Made Easier* and a 1973 *Ensign* article), Shem is argued to be **Melchizedek**: he inherits Salem (where Melchizedek is king), the priesthood passes from Noah through his line ([[D&C 84.14|D&C 84:14]]), Abraham would have received the priesthood from him, Jewish tradition equates them, and [[Joseph F. Smith]]'s vision of the redemption of the dead (D&C 138) names Shem among the great patriarchs while omitting Melchizedek. See [[Shem Was Melchizedek]].
