# Jared Halverson

**Era:** Contemporary, American
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint (LDS)
**Role:** Religious educator and scholar; PhD in American religious history (Vanderbilt); host of *Unshaken*

## Bio
Jared Halverson is a Latter-day Saint scholar and longtime Church Educational System teacher who grew up in religiously diverse Los Angeles, served a mission, and earned a PhD in American religious history at Vanderbilt Divinity School, with a specialization in **anti-religious rhetoric** — how skeptics across traditions (anti-Mormonism, anti-Methodism, anti-Catholicism, anti-Semitism) attack faith. He hosts the *Unshaken* podcast. He describes himself not as a "garden variety" apologist but as someone with "thick skin and a soft heart" who has "a lot of empathy for people who are struggling in their faith." (He is a different person from [[Taylor Halverson]].)

## On posture toward critics
Halverson wants Latter-day Saints trained as impartial *judges* of truth rather than defense *lawyers*, and reframes member/ex-member arguments as interfaith dialogue to keep them charitable. He stresses that faith is "non-provable and non-disprovable," so morally "faith is superior to knowledge" even if epistemically the reverse. See [[Thick Skin With a Soft Heart and the Interfaith Reframe]].[^1]

## On scripture and authority
His signature argument is that settling disputes by appeal to the Bible "just makes us one more Protestant denomination": Protestantism is a *scribal/biblical* religion, whereas the restoration rests on prophets who teach "as one having authority, and not as the scribes." Using [[Daniel 2|Nebuchadnezzar's dream]], he argues that interpretive authority must be matched to revelatory authority — anyone can interpret, but only God can reveal. See [[Scribal Religion Versus Authoritative Religion]] and [[Revelatory Authority Must Match Interpretive Authority]].[^2]
