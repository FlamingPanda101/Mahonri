# Stephen Burnett

A disaffected Latter-day Saint whose 1838 letter is cited by the [[Jeremy Runnells|CES Letter]] for several claims about the Book of Mormon witnesses. Burnett reported hearing [[Martin Harris]] "state in public that he never saw the plates with his natural eyes only in vision or imagination," and that Harris had only hefted the covered plates in a box, seeing them "only as he saw a city through a mountain."[^1]

[[Keystone]] argues Burnett's report is unreliable on three counts: even hostile critic [[Dan Vogel]] grants that "imagination" was "an embellishment by Burnett himself" rather than Harris's word; the "city through a mountain" line is garbled and uninterpretable; and Burnett conflates Harris's *pre*-translation experience of hefting the covered plates with his later angelic witness of the uncovered plates.[^1] Burnett himself records that after the meeting Harris "arose and said he was sorry for any man who rejected the Book of Mormon, for he knew it was true," and that others present (Joseph Coe, Cyrus Smalling) left still believing — evidence, Keystone says, that Harris felt misrepresented.[^2]
