# Metatron

**Role:** Exalted angelic / divine mediator figure in Second Temple and rabbinic Jewish tradition
**Era:** Greco-Roman period through rabbinic Judaism
**Tradition:** Jewish mystical and rabbinic literature (e.g., 3 Enoch, Talmud)

## Bio

Metatron is the supreme angelic mediator of later Jewish tradition, sometimes called the "lesser YHWH," who bears the divine name and exercises divine authority while remaining distinct from God. He is closely associated with (and sometimes identified as) the exalted human Enoch and with the **messenger of Adonai**.

## The Rav Idit Tradition and Exodus 23

[[Dan McClellan]] recounts a rabbinic passage in which a heretic challenges **Rav Idit** about Exodus 24:1, where God tells Moses, "Come up to **Adonai**" rather than "Come up to me." Rav Idit answers that the "Adonai" in view "is not [the supreme] Adonai — that's Metatron, whose name is like his master's," and he grounds this with [[Scriptures/Exodus 23.20-21|Exodus 23:21]], "for my name is in him." McClellan uses this to show that the rationalization of divine mediation through a **name-bearing agent** who can be called by the divine name was "already baked into" Greco-Roman and rabbinic Judaism — the same logic he argues is "the taproot of early Christology."
