# Noah Biser

Noah Biser is a Catholic apologist cited in the vault as the interlocutor who pressed the objection that if matter is co-eternal with God, "the Mormon God is not even truly a God" — because being the sole eternal existence is treated as a divine attribute. His argument is quoted from an LDS-vs-Catholic debate on creation.

## Relevance

His objection is the foil for [[Baylor McKay Johnson]]'s charge of [[Semantic Gerrymandering Redefines God to Exclude Pre-Existing Matter|semantic gerrymandering]] — winning the dispute by loading the definition of "God" with the contested Thomistic attribute. The exchange also frames [[Kaden Alvie]]'s appeal to Moroni 8:18 against Biser's claim that Mormons teach a God "undergoing change."
