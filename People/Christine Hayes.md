# Christine Hayes

**Role:** Sterling Professor of Religious Studies, Yale University (Hebrew Bible, classical Judaism)
**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** Academic biblical / Jewish studies

## Bio

Christine Hayes is a prominent scholar of the Hebrew Bible and classical Judaism whose Yale "Introduction to the Old Testament (Hebrew Bible)" lectures are widely used. She is cited by [[Jacob Hansen]]'s Thoughtful Faith channel for her argument that the picture of God as static, immutable, and impassible is "an outcome of a certain western philosophical tradition... grounded very much in Greco-Roman notions of the divine," whereas "the God that we meet in biblical tradition is a dynamic God" who learns, investigates, and "change[s] course in response to" human action.[^1]

This point is used in Latter-day Saint apologetics to argue that the creedal God reflects Hellenization rather than the Hebrew text. See:
- [[The Biblical God Is Dynamic Not Static (Christine Hayes)]]
- [[The Creedal God Is the Platonic One Not the God of the Bible]]
- [[Anthropomorphic God in the Hebrew Bible]]
