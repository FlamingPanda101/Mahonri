# Napoleon

**Era:** 1769 – 1821
**Tradition:** Secular (post-Revolutionary France)
**Role:** Emperor of the French

## Bio

Napoleon Bonaparte reinstated slavery in the French colonies in 1802, reversing the Revolutionary-era abolition of 1794.[^1]

## Topics

### Reinstating Slavery — The Reversibility Problem
[[Alex OConnor|Alex O'Connor]] uses Napoleon's reinstatement to puncture the "Christianity abolished slavery" narrative: France abolished slavery (plausibly partly on rights/Christian-ish and utilitarian grounds), then Napoleon brought it back on economic grounds — "Napoleon said slavery is good for the economy, and back it comes." If abolition gets credited to Christianity, what gets the *reinstatement*? O'Connor argues this short oscillation shows material/economic drivers, not theological conviction, were decisive. See [[Abolition Tracks Material Conditions Not Christian Conviction]].[^1]
