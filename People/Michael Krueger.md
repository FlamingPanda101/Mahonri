# Michael Krueger

**Michael J. Kruger** is a Reformed New Testament scholar, president of **Reformed Theological Seminary (Charlotte)**, and the foremost modern academic defender of the **self-authenticating canon**. His books *Canon Revisited* (2012) and *The Question of Canon* (2013) argue that the canon carries intrinsic divine qualities recognizable by the covenant community.

## The Self-Authenticating Canon
In this video [[Joe Heschmeyer]] treats Kruger as the most serious contemporary proponent of self-authentication and raises several objections:

- **Backing into the Catholic model:** Kruger relocates canon-recognition from the individual believer to the *church* guided by the Spirit — which, Heschmeyer argues, is functionally the Catholic position dressed in Reformation language. See [[Self-Authentication via Church Consensus Backs Into the Catholic Model]] [^1].
- **Thermostat strawman:** Kruger's thermometer-vs-thermostat illustration misrepresents the Catholic view as the claim that the church *makes* books inspired by decree — a view Vatican I explicitly condemns. See [[Krugers Strawman of the Catholic View of the Canon]] [^2].
- **General-revelation analogy:** Kruger argues that if creation (general revelation) clearly speaks of God, "how much more" would the canon (special revelation). Heschmeyer replies this fails at the *particular* level — you cannot tell whether the Epistle of Jude belongs in by inspecting it. See [[Krugers General Revelation Analogy Fails at the Particular Level]] [^3].
- **Presuppositionalism:** Kruger is placed in a presuppositional framework that forbids appeal to external evidence, which Heschmeyer calls unbiblical human philosophy. See [[Presuppositional Self-Authentication Is Unbiblical Human Philosophy]] [^4].
