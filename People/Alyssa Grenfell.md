# Alyssa Grenfell

**Era:** Contemporary (2020s)
**Background:** Former Latter-day Saint; ex-Mormon content creator
**Role:** YouTuber / influencer

## Bio

Alyssa Grenfell is one of the most popular ex-Mormon creators online, producing video content on topics she presents as validating her decision to leave the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints — including Book of Mormon historicity, anachronisms, and her own deconversion.[^1] She is a recurring foil for Latter-day Saint apologists responding to ex-Mormon "influencer" content (cf. the channel [[Ether's Elephant]], which splices ex-Mormon clips to argue they conflate anecdote and culture with doctrine).

## Topics

### Book of Mormon Anachronisms
In a video answered by [[Hayden Carroll]], Grenfell argued the Book of Mormon is "full of anachronisms" — citing elephants, iron and steel, wheat, and chariots in pre-Columbian America, and the absence of archaeological evidence for the large-scale battles the text describes — concluding that [[Joseph Smith]] composed the book in the 19th century.[^1] Carroll's rebuttal contests each example and argues the broader anachronism data now trends the other way; see [[Confirmed Anachronisms Are Reverse-Anachronisms Joseph Could Not Have Known]], [[The Anachronism List Is Trending Toward the Book of Mormon]], [[Mesoamerican Metallurgy Is Trending Earlier Toward Book of Mormon Times]], and [[Mesoamerican Warfare and Fortifications Match the Book of Alma]].[^2]
