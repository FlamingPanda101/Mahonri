# Marvin Perkins

**Role:** Latter-day Saint speaker, music producer, and educator on race and the restored gospel
**Era:** Contemporary (presentation cited c. 2012)
**Tradition:** The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints

## Bio
Marvin Perkins is a Black Latter-day Saint best known for the "Blacks in the Scriptures" project, which works through every passage in the Old Testament, New Testament, and Book of Mormon where people are described in terms of "black" or "white" skin and argues that the scriptural language is idiomatic (gloom, spiritual state) rather than racial. The [[Stick of Joseph]] channel revived his presentation in [[The Stick of Joseph - The Definition of a Christian Has Been Corrupted|a re-aired video]] hosted by [[Jackson Paul]] (see the race episode), framing it as the best scripturally-backed answer to the charge that the Book of Mormon is racist.

## On race and the scriptures
Perkins' core moves: (1) the words *black* and *white* are used of people only a handful of times in all scripture, and KJV footnotes gloss the Hebrew as "gloomy"/"dejected"; (2) the 1981 LDS scripture edition added footnotes redirecting the Book of Mormon "skin" passages to the Topical Guide entries on spiritual *darkness*/*blindness*; and (3) a **curse** in scripture is a *separation from God because of sin*, not a change of skin color. He frames the whole misreading as "philosophies of men" versus "the doctrine of the church." See:
