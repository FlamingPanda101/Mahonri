## Who
Mark D. Ellison is a Latter-day Saint scholar of early Christianity specializing in early Christian art and visual culture (PhD, Vanderbilt University; dissertation on visualizing Christian marriage in the Roman world). He authored chapter 6 of *Ancient Christians*, "Connecting with Christ: Rituals and Worship," and contributed many of his own photographs of churches, baptisteries, and mosaics (Saint Sabina, the Ravenna baptistery, the Church of the Multiplication at Tabgha).

## Authority and Reason for Citation
As a specialist in early Christian art and ritual, Ellison reads worship practices (prayer, hymns, baptism, Eucharist) through both texts and material culture. He is cited for his treatment of the orant posture, the Lord's Prayer, lamp lighting, hymn singing, the baptismal liturgy, infant baptism, and the Eucharist — and for his charitable, evidence-first method in comparing ancient ritual to Latter-day Saint worship.

## Key Contributions in This Source
- Framed worship through *lex orandi lex credendi* — worship as the expression of belief.
- Urged careful, charitable use of the term "apostasy" (see [[Apostasia Means Deliberate Rebellion Not Mere Change]]).
- Showed that infant baptism preceded its theological rationale, and surveyed debates over immersion vs. pouring.
- Offered a personal reflection linking Isaiah's vision and the Eucharist to his own sacrament-meeting experience.
