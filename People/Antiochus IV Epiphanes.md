# Antiochus IV Epiphanes

**Who:** Seleucid (Greek) king (r. 175–164 BCE) who persecuted Judea, desecrated the Jerusalem temple, and outlawed Jewish practice — provoking the Maccabean Revolt (167–160 BCE).

**Role in biblical studies:** Antiochus is the historical referent behind the apocalyptic "prophecies" of the [[Daniel|Book of Daniel]]. The critical consensus dates Daniel to the 160s BCE *during* his persecution: its symbolic visions of successive empires and a blasphemous final king track the events of Antiochus's reign so precisely (and then fail at the point of his actual death) that they read as encouragement written for Jews living through the crisis rather than predictions from the Babylonian exile centuries earlier. See [[The Book of Daniel Was Written in the Second Century BCE]].
