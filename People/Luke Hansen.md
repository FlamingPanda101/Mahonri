# Luke Hansen

**Era:** Contemporary (2020s)
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint (LDS)
**Role:** Lay apologist, content creator ("Doctrine and Governance" / "Clip Master Luke"), Ward Radio panelist

## Bio

Luke Hansen is a Latter-day Saint content creator known as "Clip Master Luke" and associated with the "Doctrine and Governance" channel (he describes that channel as "more political"). He appears as a recurring panelist on [[Ward Radio]] livestreams hosted by [[Cardon Ellis]]. On X he goes by "Luke F Ham."[^1] He is not academically credentialed; his contribution is street-level apologetic argumentation — a compact predestination syllogism he deploys in live conversations and at debates, and (in interfaith settings) a brisk case for the LDS definition of "Christian." He repeatedly disclaims expertise on the philosophy of time, patristics, and the early liturgies.

## Topics

### The "Three Facts About God" Predestination Argument
Hansen's signature move is reducing the predestination problem to three jointly-sufficient premises — God knows all of time, is sovereign/all-powerful, and creates from nothing — and challenging interlocutors that they "cannot accept all three and reject predestination." The Latter-day Saint exit is to deny creation *ex nihilo* (the Book of Abraham's "midst of spirits").[^2] See [[The Three Facts About God Entail Predestination]], [[God Found Spirits He Did Not Create - Book of Abraham Solve]], [[Calvinism Is the Logical Conclusion of Classical Theism]].

### Foreknowledge Is Not Causation
He reframes the common "foreknowledge isn't causation" rebuttal as a covert denial of divine sovereignty, and notes the Calvinist-vs-Arminian impasse as evidence the creeds misdescribed God.[^3] See [[Foreknowledge Is Not Causation Objection]].

### Molinism and Open Theism
Hansen distinguishes Molinism (retains exhaustive foreknowledge → predestination survives, "whistling past the graveyard") from open theism (denies foreknowledge → dissolves the dilemma), and supplies a brief steelman of open theism on the stream.[^4] See [[Molinism Does Not Solve Predestination]], [[Open Theism as a Dilemma Solve]].

### Are Mormons Christian (interfaith interview)
In a long, deliberately irenic conversation with the Eastern Orthodox creator [[On Planks and Pieces - Are Mormons Christian w Luke Hanson|On Planks and Pieces]], Hansen makes the LDS case for the "Christian" label: Christian = *disciple of Jesus Christ* (not Trinitarian), grounded in the earliest creed of 1 Corinthians 15 and love of neighbor. His most distinctive moves there are a **three-step burden** on the trinitarian (see [[The Trinitarian Carries a Three-Step Burden Not Just a Proof-Text]]), a **positive** reading of the [[Matthew 16.18|Matthew 16]] keys as the power to bridge the living and the dead (see [[The Keys of Peter Bridge the Living and the Dead]]), and a **tu quoque** turning the "1,800-year apostasy gap" objection into the unevangelized problem (see [[The Unevangelized Gap Is a Tu Quoque Against the Apostasy Objection]]). He also fields the priesthood ban, Book-of-Mormon genetics, modalism in the Book of Mormon, and an infallibility-vs-reliability epistemology.[^5]
