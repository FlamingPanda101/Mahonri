# Robert Greene

**Role:** Best-selling author and cultural commentator
**Era:** Contemporary (b. 1959)
**Tradition:** Secular (raised Jewish; describes himself as drawn to ancient paganism and Zen practice)

## Bio
Robert Greene is the author of *The 48 Laws of Power*, *The Art of Seduction*, *Mastery*, and *The Laws of Human Nature*. In 2018, two months after finishing *The Laws of Human Nature* — whose final chapter was on death — he suffered a near-fatal ischemic stroke that left him physically limited. The brush with death reframed his current project, a book on **the sublime** (working title *The Law of the Sublime*).

## Contribution to the source
In the [[Alex OConnor - Your Mind Is Not Your Brain|Alex O'Connor interview]], Greene argues, as research for the book, that **the mind is not the brain** — that the brain is a *filtering / reducing* mechanism, not the producer of consciousness, and that near-death and psychedelic experiences lift that filter to reveal more reality, not less. He leans on the "filter" tradition (Aldous Huxley, Henri Bergson), Bruce Greyson's *After*, and cases like Jill Bolte Taylor and "veridical" NDEs (the red-shoe-on-the-roof type). His larger thesis is that the **sublime** — a mix of pleasure and pain that takes one outside the "circle" of cultural convention — is a real, cultivable experience that organized religion once delivered and that a reductive, algorithmic modern culture has lost. As a self-described secular author, he functions in the vault as a *foil*: a non-religious voice whose anti-materialism and openness to immaterial mind are useful for steelmanning the apologetic case, even as his constructive claims rest on popular rather than peer-reviewed sources.
