# Laban

**Role:** Jerusalem official, keeper of the brass plates
**Era:** Book of Mormon (c. 600 BCE in the narrative)
**Tradition:** Book of Mormon

## Bio

Laban is a powerful man in Jerusalem who holds the brass plates containing the scriptures and the genealogy of Lehi's family (1 Nephi 3–4). After he refuses to surrender the plates and tries to kill Lehi's sons, Nephi finds him drunk and, constrained by the Spirit, slays him with his own sword, dons Laban's clothing, and obtains the plates. His name means "white one."

## Relevance to the vault

Laban is a central node in [[Christina Dymock]]'s typological thesis that 1 Nephi 4 enacts the Day of Atonement. The casting of lots maps the chapter onto Yom Kippur: on this reading Laban accuses [[Laman]] (the Azazel/scapegoat) as a thief, while Nephi — donning Laban's identity — plays the high priest who slays the "la-Yahweh" goat, with Laban's name ("white") pairing with the color of the Yahweh goat. The slaying of Laban is called "the key that unlocked" the seven-feasts reading.
