# David O. McKay

**Role:** Ninth President of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (1951-1970)
**Era:** 1873-1970
**Tradition:** The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints

## Bio
David O. McKay served as President of the Church from 1951 until his death in 1970. He is cited within the vault for a frequently-quoted statement on race that predates the 1978 revelation: that there "is not now and there has never been a doctrine in this church that Negroes are under a divine curse."

## On race and curses
[[Marvin Perkins]] uses McKay's statement as the doctrinal anchor for the argument that the LDS "curse" passages were never about skin color: if a whole race cannot be under a divine curse, then the Book of Mormon "curse of the Lamanites" must be read as the scriptural sense of *curse* — a separation from God because of individual sin (cf. Alma 3:19, "every man that is cursed brings upon himself his own condemnation"). Perkins ties this to the Second Article of Faith ("men will be punished for their own sins, and not for Adam's transgression"). See [[A Curse Is a Separation From God Not a Skin Color]].
