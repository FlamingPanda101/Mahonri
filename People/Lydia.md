# Lydia

First-century convert at Philippi, "a worshiper of God" and a dealer in purple cloth (a luxury trade), baptized with her household by Paul (Acts 16:14–15).

## Patron and leader of a house church
[[Ariel Bybee Laughton]] presents Lydia as a prominent merchant, household manager, and patron of a house church who "insists that Paul and Silas accept her hospitality," effectively "electing them to preside over the Church in her house for a time." After their imprisonment and release, Paul and Silas return to her house to worship (Acts 16:40). Laughton concludes she "likely presided at many of the Church meetings and meals that took place in her home when the Apostles were not in residence." A key example of a woman leading a house church by virtue of property and household authority. See [[Women Led the First-Century Church as Apostles Deacons and Patrons]] and [[Acts 16.14-15|Acts 16:14-15]]. [^1]
