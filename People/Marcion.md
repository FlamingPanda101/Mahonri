# Marcion

**Era:** 2nd century CE (c. 85 – c. 160)
**Tradition:** Early Christianity (declared heretical)
**Role:** Theologian who rejected the Old Testament

## Bio

Marcion of Sinope was a 2nd-century Christian teacher who argued that the wrathful creator-God of the Old Testament could not be the same God revealed in Jesus. He compiled a work, the **Antitheses**, juxtaposing the sayings and doings of the Old Testament God with those of Jesus to show "these cannot be the same kind of person," and produced a truncated canon (an edited Luke and ten Pauline epistles). The proto-orthodox church rejected him.[^1]

## Topics

### The Marcionite Dilemma
[[Alex OConnor|Alex O'Connor]] invokes Marcion to frame his central pressure on the thesis: Christianity's "attachment to Judaism" means a Christian must either become a *Marcionite* (jettison the OT as wrong) or *own* the brutal OT texts and explain why Yahweh's actions were acceptable. [[Glenn Scrivener]] later notes that antebellum slave-owners effectively "did what Marcion proposed" with the **Slave Bible**, cutting ~90% of the OT. See [[Christianity's Attachment to Judaism Undermines the Thesis]] and [[The Slave Bible Confirms the OT Was Dangerous to Slaveholders]].[^1]
