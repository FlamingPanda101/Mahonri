## Who
Matthew J. Grey is a Latter-day Saint scholar of the New Testament and ancient Christianity, with expertise in the archaeology and material culture of the Greco-Roman and Jewish world of early Christianity. He authored chapter 5 of *Ancient Christians*, "Sacred Spaces & Places of Worship: From House Churches to Monumental Basilicas." His photographs (e.g., of the model of first-century Jerusalem) appear in the volume.

## Authority and Reason for Citation
As a believing scholar trained in the archaeology of early Christian worship spaces, Grey models the careful, evidence-first comparison of ancient Christian sacred space with Latter-day Saint practice. He is cited here for his survey of house churches, *domus ecclesiae* (esp. Dura Europos), and monumental basilicas, and for his sustained caution against "parallelomania" in drawing temple connections.

## Key Contributions in This Source
- Surveyed the full arc of early Christian sacred space from first-century house gatherings to sixth-century basilicas.
- Detailed the "templization" of Christian worship space and the basilicas' self-understanding as successors to the Jerusalem temple.
- Articulated three methodological cautions for comparing ancient ritual to modern temple worship (see [[Avoiding Parallelomania Between Ancient Rites and LDS Temple]]).
