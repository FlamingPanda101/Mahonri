# Robin Faith Walsh

**Role:** New Testament scholar (University of Miami); author of *The Origins of Early Christian Literature*
**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** Academic biblical studies

## Bio
Robin Faith Walsh is a New Testament scholar whose work reframes where early Christian literature comes from. A doctoral student of [[Stanley Stowers]], she argues that early Christian texts are products of educated **literary** networks, not primarily of communities of faith.

## Literary Communities, Not Faith Communities
[[Hugo Mendez]] is "very sympathetic" to Walsh (and Stowers before her): rather than treating ancient churches as the formative site of the texts, we should locate the New Testament writers among highly literate individuals who read, shared, and reworked one another's drafts. See [[Early Christian Writers Were Literate Networks Not Faith Communities]] [^1].
