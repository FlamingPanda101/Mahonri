# Raphael Patai

**Full Name:** Raphael Patai
**Era:** 1910–1996
**Tradition:** Jewish (anthropologist / Near Eastern studies)
**Role:** Ethnographer and scholar of ancient Jewish myth and ritual

## Bio

Raphael Patai was a prolific scholar of Jewish folklore, anthropology, and the ancient Near East, best known for *The Hebrew Goddess* and *Man and Temple in Ancient Jewish Myth and Ritual*. He documented traditions that the cherubim over the ark were depicted as a male-and-female pair in intimate embrace, a motif some ancient observers reportedly regarded as scandalous.

On The Stick of Joseph, [[Mike Day]] and [[Dave Butler]] invoke Patai (alongside the suggestion that the cherubim's wings formed a *throne* or seat for the divine presence) while discussing what the *kapporet* (mercy seat) over the ark looked like and whether it was meant to be sat upon.
