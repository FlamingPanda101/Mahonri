# Theodosius II

Eastern Roman Emperor (AD 401–450). Crowned as a child after his father's death in 408, with his elder sister [[Pulcheria]] wielding much of the real power.

## Significance
Theodosius II "called" the First Council of Ephesus (AD 431), together with [[Pulcheria]]. His death — he "was riding his horse on a hunting expedition when the horse stumbled" and was badly injured, dying soon after in July 450 — left no heir and triggered the political maneuvering by which [[Pulcheria]] married [[Marcian]] and the Council of Chalcedon (451) was convened. His reign exemplifies how the formulation of Christological orthodoxy was bound up with imperial succession crises. See [[Ephesus and Chalcedon Defined the Two Natures of Christ]].
