# Cain

First son of Adam and Eve; murdered his brother [[Abel]] ([[Genesis 4|Genesis 4]]).

## The mark and the unhidden sin
Apocryphal traditions develop Cain's punishment: he could not bury Abel's body or hide the blood, losing the "coat of skins" inheritance and bearing his sin openly. One tradition says Abel's blood would not leave Cain's skin — possibly the "mark of Cain." See [[Blood of Abel - Unburiable Tradition]].

## How Cain killed Abel and "murder to get gain"
Apocryphal Adam-and-Eve traditions describe Cain sawing through Abel's windpipe with a flint stone after luring him to the field by feigning need — read alongside Joseph Smith's "swear by thy throat" oath and the "Master Mahan / murder to get gain" motif.
