# Praxeas

An early-third-century figure (c. AD 200) about whom "we know little." According to [[Tertullian]], Praxeas brought his ideas to Rome from Asia Minor (modern Turkey). He is known almost entirely through Tertullian's hostile treatise *Against Praxeas*.

## Significance
Praxeas is the archetypal **modalist**. He "believed that the Father [is] identical with the Son in the sense that both are one person" — that there is only one person called God, manifesting in different "modes" (Father, Son, Spirit) at different times. His view has been called [[Modalism Monarchianism and Sabellianism|modalism, monarchianism, and Sabellianism]]. [[Tertullian]] coined the Latin *trinitas* (Trinity) precisely to refute Praxeas, mocking the modalist position: scripture nowhere says "I am my own Son, today have I begotten myself." See [[Tertullian Coined Trinitas and Held Subordinationism]].
