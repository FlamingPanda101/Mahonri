# Sabellius

An early-third-century theologian (c. AD 220) who gave his name to **Sabellianism**, a form of modalism.

## Significance
Sabellius "argued against any distinction between the persons of God the Father and his Son, Jesus Christ. Sabellius even called God the 'Son-Father.'" His name became one of the three standard labels for the modalist heresy — alongside [[Praxeas]]'s modalism and monarchianism. The persistence of "Sabellianism" as a term shows how seriously the early Church treated the threat that over-stressing God's oneness collapses the three persons into one. See [[Modalism Monarchianism and Sabellianism]].
