# Paul Draper

**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** Agnostic
**Role:** Professor of philosophy, Purdue University

## Bio
Paul Draper is a professor of philosophy at Purdue University and one of the leading contemporary specialists on the *evidential* problem of evil. [[Alex Arnold]] cites him as the philosopher who states the hardest objection to finite theism most carefully.[^1]

## On the Evidential Problem of Evil
Draper "has pressed this carefully, saying that the distribution of pain and pleasure in the natural world tracks biological fitness, not moral purpose. The pattern is more consistent with atheism than with any form of theism."[^1] Arnold raises this in the context of evolutionary animal suffering — "hundreds of millions of years of predation and death before any human being existed" — and treats it as the strongest objection in the episode, one the LDS finite-theist framework relocates but does not fully resolve. See [[Animal Suffering Is the Hardest Test for Finite Theism]] and [[Finite Theism Is the Honest Answer to the Problem of Evil]].
