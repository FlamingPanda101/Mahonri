# Mark Hofmann

**Role:** Forger of purported early-Latter-day-Saint documents; bomber and murderer
**Era:** Contemporary (b. 1954), American
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint (ex-member; raised LDS)

## Bio

Mark Hofmann is an American forger who, during the late 1970s and early 1980s, manufactured and sold a stream of fraudulent documents purporting to bear on early Latter-day Saint and American history — most notoriously the "Salamander Letter" and the "Anthon Transcript" forgeries. As his scheme unravelled and debts mounted, he killed two people with pipe bombs in Salt Lake City in 1985 in an attempt to cover his tracks; a third bomb injured him. He pleaded guilty to murder and forgery in 1987 and is serving a life sentence. His fabrications and the investigation that exposed them are a landmark case in document authentication.

## Relevance to the vault

Hofmann appears as a *parallel*, not a doctrinal source. In [[Joseph Smith Sought Secular Authentication of Artifacts He Believed Ancient]], the point is that when Hofmann's forgeries surfaced, the Church bought them "precisely to try to get them authenticated," bringing in outside experts — evidence that subjecting claimed artifacts to scholarly scrutiny is institutionally characteristic, not evasive. The same note's steelman cuts the other way: that the flattering "Joseph sought authentication" picture rests partly on "a hostile forger's decades-late recollection," the weakest possible support. The episode also functions in [[Informed Saints - The Hoax That Should Have Fooled Joseph Smith]] as a case study in sincere prophetic fallibility, since the Church itself broke the forgery story.
