# Sean McDowell

**Era:** Contemporary (2000s–2020s)
**Tradition:** Evangelical Protestant
**Role:** Apologist, professor, author

## Bio
Sean McDowell is an Evangelical apologist and a professor at the Talbot School of Theology, Biola University. He is a prolific popular-level author and YouTube speaker and works closely with [[William Lane Craig]]. The [[All Those In Favor - Three Tactics to Evangelize Mormons|All Those In Favor]] hosts note his respected work on the martyrdom of the original apostles, while disputing his anti-Mormon material.

## Topics

### "Don't Be Fooled" — the Galatians 1 / grace attack on Mormonism
In a sermon clip ("Don't Be Fooled: False Gospels Are Everywhere," dated 24 Feb 2022) keyed to [[Galatians 1.6|Galatians 1:6-9]], McDowell argues Mormonism "proclaims a false gospel," citing [[Ephesians 2.8-9|Ephesians 2:8-9]] against [[2 Nephi 25.23|2 Nephi 25:23]] ("by grace… after all that we can do") and insisting "your works contribute nothing to your salvation."[^1] The hosts answer that he has the church history backwards: the synergism Latter-day Saints share with Catholicism and Orthodoxy is ancient, while his own *sola fide* and forensic justification are the genuine post-Reformation novelty — see [[Synergism Is Ancient and Sola Fide Is the Real Innovation]] and [[Ephesians 2-8-9 - Saved by Grace Not Works Rebutted]]. They fault him for leaning on discredited counter-cult sources ([[Norman Geisler]], [[Ed Decker]]) instead of current LDS scholarship.[^2]
