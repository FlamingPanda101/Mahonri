# Isaac

Son of [[Abraham]] and Sarah, the son of the covenant. The near-sacrifice of Isaac (the "Binding of Isaac," [[Genesis 22|Genesis 22]]) is a central test-of-faith narrative and, in Christian and LDS reading, a type of the Father's offering of His Son.

## As a Type of Christ
[[Don Bradley]] notes that Genesis 22 calls Isaac Abraham's "only son" though Ishmael exists — "portending the Only Begotten." Read against the backstory of Abraham's own near-sacrifice, the event reveals Abraham's trust and points to the Atonement. See [[The Binding of Isaac as a Type of Christ]].

## The "Cover Up" Reading
In a separate Ward Radio episode, [[Jonah Barnes]] argues a stronger thesis: that the original Akedah was a messianic *resurrection* in which Isaac was actually slain and revived — citing rabbinic "blood of Isaac" traditions ([[Shalom Spiegel]]'s *The Last Trial*), the Elohim/Yahweh and lamb/ram seams in [[Genesis 22]], and [[Hebrews 11.17|Hebrews 11]]/[[James 2.20-23|James 2]]/[[Acts 3.13-15|Acts 3]] — which the [[Josiah|Deuteronomists]] allegedly edited out and Joseph Smith partly restored. See [[Joseph Smith Restored a Messianic Akedah in Which Isaac Was Actually Sacrificed]].
