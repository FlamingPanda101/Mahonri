# Allen Hadfield

Lehi, Utah father of two and member of the Lehi 8th Ward. Originally a supporter of therapist [[Barbara Snow]] and part of an advocacy group demanding action on the abuse claims, he became the only adult charged in the Lehi cases after his own children — under nine months of pressure in Snow's therapy — accused him of molestation. He was narrowly convicted in 1986 but escaped the usual 10-year minimum, receiving 10 years probation and 6 months in jail. He always maintained his innocence and refused to admit guilt even when it was required for court-ordered therapy.

## The Trial
His prosecution rested on Snow's testimony and the "no-alibi" date of April 9, 1986. A later court-ordered therapist, [[Robert Card]], found him "vibrantly normal" with no sign of deviance. The community raised funds for his defense. See [[The Allen Hadfield Trial as a Miscarriage of Justice]] and [[The No-Alibi Date as Manufactured Evidence]] [^1].
