# Papias

**Role:** Bishop of Hierapolis; early premillennial witness
**Era:** c. 60 – 130 CE
**Tradition:** Early Christianity (Asia Minor)

## Bio

Papias was bishop of Hierapolis in the early second century. His writings survive only in fragments quoted by later authors. According to [[Eusebius of Caesarea]], Papias "was among the first to seriously promote the premillennial idea that Jesus would reign for a thousand years on earth." [^1]

## Earliest Witness to Premillennialism (Chiliasm)

[[Irenaeus of Lyons]] devoted "large parts of *Against Heresies* 5.33 to direct quotes of Papias regarding the situation of the just during the Millennium." [^1] Eusebius, who did not share the premillennial view, "was quite critical of Papias's literal interpretation of one thousand years and blamed him for Irenaeus's similar premillennial leanings." [^1] Papias thus stands at the head of the literalist, sense-oriented millennial tradition that [[Augustine]] would later displace. See [[Early Christian Premillennialism Was the Earliest Millennial View]].
