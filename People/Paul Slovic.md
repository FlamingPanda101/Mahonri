# Paul Slovic

**Full Name:** Paul Slovic
**Era:** Contemporary (1938–present)
**Role:** Psychologist; researcher on risk perception and the psychology of altruism

## Bio

Paul Slovic is an American psychologist known for research on risk perception and on "psychic numbing" / the collapse of compassion — the finding that altruistic response can *decline* as the number of identifiable victims rises.

## Cited in this dialogue

[[Sam Harris]] cites "a classic moral study done by Paul Slovic" in which subjects show a maximal compassionate response to one named, pictured needy child, but a *diminished* response when told of the same girl plus her needy brother. Harris uses this as a paradigm "moral illusion" requiring correction by quantification; [[Jordan Peterson]] resists treating the gameable result as proof the underlying heuristic is flawed. See [[Empathy Does Not Scale - The Identifiable Victim Effect]].[^1]
