# Ryan (NeedGod)

Reformed Baptist evangelist who runs **NeedGod.net**, an evangelism-and-apologetics ministry (street evangelism plus near-daily YouTube live broadcasts). Based in Australia.

## Significance in the vault
Ryan argued the **negative** ("water baptism does not save") against [[Joe Heschmeyer]] in The Apologetic Dog debate. His core case: salvation is by faith alone in Christ's finished work (Abraham as the model, Romans 4); baptism is a commanded but non-saving act of obedience; and treating it as required makes the gospel performance-based ("one foot on the bus, one foot on the ground"). His chief exegetical moves — the causal-*eis* reading of [[Acts 2.38]], the textual unreliability of [[Mark 16.16]], and "born of water" as natural birth/Ezekiel-36 in [[John 3.5]] — are the ones Heschmeyer most directly contests. See [[Salvation Is by Faith Alone and Baptism Is a Non-Saving Act of Obedience]].
