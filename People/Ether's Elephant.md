# Ether's Elephant

A faithful Latter-day Saint YouTube channel that produces short, fast-cut apologetic and commentary videos defending the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, typically by responding to ex-Mormon and critical content. The creator presents as an active member ("we active members") rather than as a scholar. The channel name fuses the Book of **Ether** with the parable of the blind men and the **elephant** (cf. [[John Godfrey Saxe]]) — a fitting emblem for its recurring theme that critics describe only the part of the faith they happened to touch and mistake it for the whole.

## Don't Listen to ExMormon Influencers
A montage video splicing clips from popular ex-Mormon "influencers" to argue that their content misrepresents actual LDS belief — chiefly by conflating personal anecdote, local-leader teaching, and LDS culture with official doctrine. It ends on a teaser that the gravest misrepresentation concerns the nature of God, deferred to a promised sequel. See [[Ethers Elephant - Dont Listen to ExMormon Influencers]] and [[Conflating Anecdote and Culture With Doctrine Misrepresents the Church]].
