# Dionysius the Areopagite (Pseudo-Dionysius, c. 500)

Anonymous late-fifth / early-sixth-century mystical theologian who wrote under the name of the Athenian convert of Acts 17:34. His corpus (the *Ecclesiastical Hierarchy*, *Mystical Theology*, etc.) profoundly shaped later Eastern and Western mysticism.

## The classic definition of theosis
Becerra cites Dionysius for the concise, settled definition of **theosis**: "the attaining of likeness of God and union with him so far as is possible." [^1] The qualifier "so far as is possible" became the standard patristic guardrail — real likeness and union with God, but bounded by the Creator/creature distinction. See [[Theosis as Likeness and Union So Far as Possible]].
