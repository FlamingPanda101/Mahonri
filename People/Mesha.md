# Mesha

**Who:** King of Moab in the 9th century BCE (reigned c. 840 BCE). Known primarily from the Mesha Stele (Moabite Stone), a victory monument discovered in 1868 at Dhiban, Jordan.

**Role:** The Mesha Stele records Mesha's successful rebellion against Israelite domination and attributes Moab's previous subjugation to divine anger: "Chemosh was angry with his land." [[Jacob Wright]] uses this as a crucial parallel to biblical defeat theology — both the Mesha Stele and the Bible use the same theological grammar (divine anger → defeat), but they diverge in direction: the Mesha Stele moves toward present triumph, while the Bible makes defeat the center of ongoing reflection.
