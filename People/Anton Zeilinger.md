# Anton Zeilinger

**Full Name:** Anton Zeilinger
**Era:** 1945–present
**Field:** Experimental quantum physics
**Affiliation:** University of Vienna; Austrian Academy of Sciences

## Bio

Anton Zeilinger is an Austrian physicist and pioneer of quantum information
science, famous for experiments on quantum entanglement, quantum teleportation,
and tests of Bell's inequality. He shared the **2022 Nobel Prize in Physics**
for this work.

## Topics

### Experimental Violation of Local Realism
[[Inspiring Philosophy|Michael Jones]] cites Zeilinger's experiments as having
"demonstrated that intuitive features of realism must be abandoned" — closing
the door on local hidden-variable theories that determinists had hoped would
restore a clockwork universe.[^1] On this reading, a particle's measured
properties are not fixed independently of observation, undercutting the
deterministic picture.
