# Eutyches

Fifth-century head of an important monastery in Constantinople. He had attended the Council of Ephesus and spoken against [[Nestorius]].

## Significance
Eutyches is the namesake of **monophysitism**, declared a heresy at the Council of Chalcedon (AD 451). Overreacting to Nestorius, he held: "I acknowledge that our Lord came into being from two natures before the union [the Incarnation]; but after the union I acknowledge one nature" — and that one nature was the *divine*. He affirmed Christ *homoousios* with the Father but thought it blasphemous that Christ was also *homoousios* with humanity. [[Leo the Great]]'s *Tome* answered him: if Christ "bore nothing of our human nature to the cross, then how could we humans be saved by him?" His teaching defined the second boundary Chalcedon avoided ("without confusion, change, division, or separation"). See [[Ephesus and Chalcedon Defined the Two Natures of Christ]].
