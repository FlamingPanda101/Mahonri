# John Wesley

English cleric and theologian (1703–1791), founder of the **Methodist** movement and namesake of the Wesleyan / Arminian theological tradition. Wesley taught an **imparted** righteousness — sanctification as a real change worked in the believer — rather than the merely **imputed**, forensic righteousness of Reformed double imputation.

## Used in / Role in the argument
[[Trent Horn]] uses Wesley and his Methodist heirs as a **reductio** against the claim that [[Double Imputation Is Not the Gospel|double imputation]] is "the heart of the gospel": if it were, then "followers of John Wesley... who believe in an imparted righteousness rather than an imputed righteousness would have a false gospel" — a conclusion the Reformed critics ([[John MacArthur]], [[Voddie Baucham]]) will not accept. Wesley thus marks the boundary of intramural Protestant disagreement that does *not* trigger the "false gospel" charge. [^1]
