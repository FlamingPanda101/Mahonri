# Joseph Peterson

**Role:** Academic (PhD, University of Chicago); BYU professor in the 1911 evolution controversy
**Era:** Early 20th century
**Tradition:** The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (LDS / Mormon)

> Note: [[Benjamin Park]] alternates the spellings "Peterson" and "Petersen" for this family in the transcript. Not to be confused with the unrelated [[Daniel C. Peterson]].

## Bio

Joseph Peterson held a PhD from the University of Chicago and was one of the four BYU professors at the center of the [[The 1911 BYU Evolution Controversy and the Firing of Four Professors|1911 evolution controversy]], teaching organic evolution as reconcilable with LDS belief. After the apostolic committee's ultimatum and the firing of his brother [[Henry Peterson]], Joseph resigned (with [[Ralph Chamberlain]]). He moved to the University of Utah, where he resigned again in 1915 when that institution had its own evolution crisis — showing, [[Benjamin Park]] notes, that the conflict was not confined to BYU.
