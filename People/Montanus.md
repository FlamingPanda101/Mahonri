# Montanus

Second-century Christian (fl. c. 170 AD, Asia Minor) who, with two prophetesses, **Priscilla** and **Maximilla**, led an ecstatic-prophecy movement known as the "New Prophecy" (later "Montanism"). The transcript renders his name "Montanus / Montaneous."

In Day's framing, Montanus is pivotal: he was *acknowledging* something everyone could see — that the gifts of the Spirit and prophecy had waned from the church. The institutional response was not "you're right, we've lost revelation" but rather the hardening claim that **prophecy and revelation belong to the apostolic past**. Day links this directly to the minister who told [[Joseph Smith]] that all revelation ceased with the apostles. The overcorrection, in Butler's pendulum terms, pushed the church further toward central control and away from charisma. See [[Montanus and the Overcorrection Against Prophecy]].
