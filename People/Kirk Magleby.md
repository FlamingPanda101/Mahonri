# Kirk Magleby

**Era:** 20th–21st century
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint (LDS)
**Role:** Nonprofit administrator and apologist for Book of Mormon scholarship

## Bio
Kirk Magleby is Vice President of Operations for the Ancient America Foundation and one of the three early directors of FARMS (the Foundation for Ancient Research and Mormon Studies), which he helped launch in Provo in 1979–1980 alongside [[Jack Welch]] and [[John Sorenson]]. He later served (2015–2023) as the volunteer executive director of Book of Mormon Central, since developed into Scripture Central. By his own account he worked "behind the scenes... recruiting donors, managing projects, and paying bills" rather than as a publishing scholar.[^1]

## The Academic-Respectability Narrative
On the *Discover the Book of Mormon* channel, Magleby narrates the [[Book of Mormon]]'s five-decade rise "from an ignored laughingstock to respectability as an object of considerable academic interest," tracing it as the fulfillment of a 1976 personal revelation that he should help make the book "academically respectable." His two distinctive framings are that elite presses now publish the text (see [[Elite Academic Presses Publishing the Book of Mormon Mark Its Respectability]]) and that earlier critics dismissed it without reading it (see [[Critics Dismissed the Book of Mormon Without Reading It]]).[^1]
