# Ignatius of Antioch

**Ignatius of Antioch** (c. 35–108 AD) was Bishop of Antioch and one of the Apostolic Fathers. His seven authentic letters, written en route to martyrdom in Rome, are among the earliest Christian writings outside the New Testament.

## Early Christians Rarely Cited the NT as Scripture
[[Trent Horn]] uses the era of Ignatius — the early second century, just after the apostolic age and the close of public revelation — as a chronological benchmark: authors of this period **barely cite the New Testament as Scripture**, far more often quoting the Old Testament as Scripture. This is evidence against self-authentication: if the canon were as obvious as telling black from white, the earliest post-apostolic writers would already treat the NT books as recognized Scripture, and they do not. See [[The Early Church Did Not Use Self-Authentication]] [^1].
