# Horace Cummings

**Role:** Church Education Superintendent / Commissioner whose 1911 report triggered the BYU firings
**Era:** Early 20th century
**Tradition:** The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (LDS / Mormon)

## Bio

Horace Cummings was the Church Education Superintendent commissioned to investigate complaints that BYU was "going haywire" doctrinally. His report, dated **January 21, 1911**, found professors teaching evolution "as a demonstrated law," applying it to the Fall, Atonement, and Resurrection, and treating the Bible "as a collection of myths, folklore, dramas" — and, most alarmingly to church leaders, found students "wholesale" embracing those teachings and doubting scripture. [[Benjamin Park]] notes Cummings was "no fan of evolution" and framed his findings to provoke leaders by tying evolution to threatened scriptural truths. The report precipitated the apostolic committee that fired the four professors (see [[The 1911 BYU Evolution Controversy and the Firing of Four Professors]]); its full text survives in Richard Sherlock's *Sunstone* essay "Campus in Crisis: BYU 1911."
