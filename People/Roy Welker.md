# Roy Welker

**Full name:** Roy A. Welker
**Role:** President of the German-Austrian Mission (c. 1934–1937)
**Era:** Nazi Germany (1930s)
**Tradition:** The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (LDS)

## Bio

Roy Welker presided over the German-Austrian Mission in the mid-1930s. [[Stephen Smoot]] uses the dossier's memoranda to show that on 30 April 1936 Welker and senior LDS representatives were summoned to the secret state police office and told that "in the event of anti-state propaganda activities, the strictest state police measures would be carried out against the sect."[^1]

## The Circular Letter to Missionaries

Days later, on 6 May 1936, Welker issued a circular letter to the missionaries of the German-Austrian Mission instructing them to avoid politics entirely: "It is not our province at all to talk politics... We are bound by our principles and teachings to honor, uphold and sustain the laws of the land. Let us be doing it 100%... If anyone attempts to draw you into discussions involving politics... you are to resist such temptation. There are those who would make such attempts to entrap you." For good measure he sent a copy to the Gestapo (preserved in the dossier).[^2] Smoot reads Welker's stance as the on-the-ground enactment of the Church's accommodationism — and argues the older "spineless cowards" reading collapses once the explicit Gestapo threat is in view. See [[The Church's Accommodationist Survival Strategy in Nazi Germany]].[^3]
