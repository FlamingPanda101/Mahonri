# Marie Curie

Polish-French physicist and chemist (1867–1934), pioneer of research on radioactivity. Cited in the [[Keystone - Polygamy Expert What Changed My Mind|Keystone polygamy episode]] not for her science but for a maxim that historian [[Britney Chapman Nash]] uses to frame her counsel on approaching the topic of plural marriage.[^1]

## Best Quote
> "Nothing in life is to be feared. It is only to be understood. Now is the time to understand more so that we may fear less."[^1]

Nash applies this to the stigma around LDS polygamy: acquiring real knowledge and understanding calms the fear that members often feel when they approach the subject (see [[Understanding Replaces Fear - the Knowledge Cure for the Polygamy Stigma]]).
