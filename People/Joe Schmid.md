# Joe Schmid

**Tradition:** Non-theist / atheist (philosophy of religion)
**Role:** PhD candidate in philosophy; creator of the *Majesty of Reason* YouTube channel and publishing philosopher of religion

## Bio

Joe Schmid is a non-theistic philosopher of religion known for unusually charitable, technically rigorous engagement with theistic arguments — he is frequently cited approvingly by both atheist and Christian apologists for steelmanning the other side. He runs the channel *Majesty of Reason*.

## Role in the vault

Appears as the **non-theist who pre-reviewed [[Trent Horn]]'s script** for [[The Counsel of Trent - The Moral Madness of Atheism]], brought on "to make sure all the philosophical ground is covered properly." His significance is a striking **hostile-witness concession**: he grants that a God-grounded account of the sexual faculty's sacredness (a faculty "set aside… as distinctively sacred… an icon of the Trinity") is "much better" than bare natural-law theory, and admits that *atheistic* explanations of why painless perverted sex acts are wrong "can get strained, so you might doubt that they're plausible" — while still maintaining such non-theistic accounts exist and are "non-silly." See [[Misusing the Sexual Faculty Is Wrong Apart from Harm or Consent]] and [[The Modest Moral Argument from Particular Puzzling Acts]].[^1]
