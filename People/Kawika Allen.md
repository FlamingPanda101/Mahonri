# Kawika Allen

A Latter-day Saint counseling psychologist and professor at Brigham Young University, originally from Laie, Hawaii, who also spent part of his youth in a rough area near Cape Town, South Africa — an experience he credits with drawing him toward the study of mental health and human behavior. His research program concerns **religiosity, scrupulosity (religious OCD), perfectionism, and legalism** and their effects on the mental health of religious people, especially Latter-day Saints.

On Scripture Central's *Let's Get Real with Stephen Jones*, Allen argues that religious perfectionism is fundamentally an **anxiety disorder expressed religiously** ([[Scrupulosity Is an Anxiety Disorder Expressed Religiously]]); that **legalism** — trying to earn God's love by works alone — drives the "never good enough" cycle ([[Legalism Tries to Earn Gods Love Through Works Alone]]); that **guilt** (corrective, Christ-directed) must be distinguished from **shame** (identity-attacking) ([[Guilt Is Corrective but Shame Attacks Identity]]); and that the remedy is to **stay with Christ** in the moment of distress rather than waiting to "qualify" for grace ([[Stay With Christ in the Moment of Scrupulosity]]). He cites [[Steven Robinson]]'s *Believing Christ* for the distinction between believing *in* Christ and *believing* him.
