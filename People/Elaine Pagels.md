# Elaine Pagels

Leading historian of Gnosticism and early Christianity (Princeton), best known for *The Gnostic Gospels*. In the Great Apostasy episode Day cites Pagels on the diversity of Gnostic movements — noting that some Gnostics denied the apostles saw the resurrected Jesus and some denied a physical resurrection at all. Her work supports Day's point that "Gnosticism" is not one thing you can "slice and stick on a plate," and that early Christianity contained a continuum of christologies. See [[Docetism and Early Christological Diversity]], [[Early Christianity Was Plural Not One Perfect Tree]].
