# Clement of Rome

Late first-century bishop of Rome, an Apostolic Father whose letter *1 Clement* (c. AD 96) is among the earliest surviving Christian writings outside the New Testament.

## "Word of God" as the Christian Message
Cited by [[Trent Horn]]: Clement says the apostles were "established in the word of God" — paralleling Luke's description of their receiving the Christian message, not a written text. For the earliest fathers, Horn argues, the "word of God" was the gospel/charisma or Christ himself, not a set of books. See [[Calling Scripture the Word of God Is a Late Patristic Development]] [^1].
