# Christopher Rich

Christopher B. Rich is a lawyer (a former JAG officer in the U.S. military) and, at the time of the interview, a PhD candidate in history at the University of Utah. He is a co-author, with [[Paul Reeve]] and [[LaJean Carruth]], of *This Abominable Slavery*.[^1]

[[Daniel C. Peterson]] recounts that when Rich first read Utah's 1852 "so-called slave bill" with a lawyer's eye, his reaction was: "This is not a slave bill. Why has everybody told me it was?" — a reading that anchors the argument that the statute regulated *servitude* with a graduated-emancipation intent, not chattel slavery.[^2]
