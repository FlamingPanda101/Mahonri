# Luke Barnes

**Era:** Contemporary
**Role:** Astrophysicist; postdoctoral and lecturing positions (Sydney Institute for Astronomy; Western Sydney University)

## Bio
Luke A. Barnes is an Australian astrophysicist best known for his technical work defending the *reality* of cosmic fine-tuning. His PASA review article catalogues the fine-tuning cases in the literature, and *A Fortunate Universe* (co-written with Geraint Lewis, an atheist astronomer) argues that the constants and initial conditions of nature fall in a narrow life-permitting range — a fact both authors affirm even though they disagree about whether theism explains it.

## Contribution in These Videos
[[Trent Horn]] leans on Barnes (and Lewis) repeatedly to fix the *premise* that fine-tuning is real and to correct atheist misstatements:
- That a breathable-density universe would gravitationally collapse in about a day; that the gravitational constant is not even needed to make the fine-tuning case; and that the parameter extremes are "disintegrating atoms... the crush of a black hole," not merely "too hot or too cold." See [[Fine-Tuning Targets the Constants Not the Universe's Hospitality]] and [[Carroll Conflates the Gravitational and Cosmological Constants]].
- That only a handful of peer-reviewed papers challenge the fine-tuning cases and none defends the claim that most values would permit life — cited as the state of the literature in [[The Weakless Universe Just Pushes the Fine-Tuning Back a Step]].
