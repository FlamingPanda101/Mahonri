# Cyprian of Carthage

Third-century bishop of Carthage in Roman North Africa (d. AD 258, martyred), a leading Latin church father and theorist of episcopal authority and church unity.

## Consolidation of patronage
[[Ariel Bybee Laughton]] presents Cyprian as the figure who restructured the Roman patronage system around the bishop's office. He used his wealth and connections to make the episcopacy "the primary benefactor of the community," personally overseeing the disbursement of church funds, and urged wealthy Christians to channel all charitable giving *through the Church*. He may have been "the first to insist that it is superior for an individual to donate to the Church rather than directly to the poor." This "consolidation of charity" displaced the personal-patronage networks through which women had exercised power. See [[Bishops Consolidated Patronage and Displaced Women Patrons]]. [^1]

He invoked patronage rhetoric — "how much more illustrious and great is the glory of having God and Christ as the spectator of the gift?" (*De opere et eleemosynis* 21) — to recast donors as patrons earning honor before God by giving through the bishop. [^2]
