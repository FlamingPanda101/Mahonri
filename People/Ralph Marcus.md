# Ralph Marcus

Scholar of Hellenistic Judaism and Greek (University of Chicago); Loeb translator of Philo and Josephus. In the *Journal of Biblical Literature* (1951, "On Causal *Eis*," and a follow-up, "The Elusive Causal *Eis*"), Marcus systematically rebutted [[Julius Mantey]]'s claim that the Greek preposition *eis* can mean "because of."

## Significance in the vault
Marcus is the scholarly hinge of the [[Acts 2.38]] dispute. [[Joe Heschmeyer]] invokes Marcus to argue that no Greek scholar can find a clear causal use of *eis*: Marcus "flatly" stated Mantey was "mistaken in his construing and rendering of all these passages," and even [[Daniel Wallace]] (who himself denies baptismal regeneration) concedes Marcus "ably demonstrated that the linguistic evidence of a causal *eis* fell short of proof." See [[The Causal Eis Reading of Acts 2.38 Lacks Greek Support]].
