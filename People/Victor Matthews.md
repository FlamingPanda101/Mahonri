# Victor Matthews

**Role:** Old Testament scholar
**Era:** Contemporary (active 1980s–2020s)
**Tradition:** Academic biblical studies

## Bio

Victor H. Matthews is an Old Testament scholar and professor. He co-authored with [[Don Benjamin]] *Old Testament Parallels: Laws and Stories from the Ancient Near East* — a textbook that places biblical texts alongside ancient Near Eastern literary parallels for comparative study. The work is widely used in introductory Old Testament courses.

## Translation of TM.75.G1682

In *Old Testament Parallels*, Matthews and Benjamin included a translation of the Ebla tablet TM.75.G1682 just a few years after [[Giovanni Pettinato]]'s English publication (1981). Their translation was more cautious than Pettinato's:

> "You are the creator of the heavens and the earth. There was no earth until you created it. There was no light until you created it."[^1]

Dan McClellan notes that while this translation "moves away from Pettinato's more explicit attempt to try to read creation ex nihilo into the text," it is still "highly problematic" because it slants the text toward a Genesis-parallel reading[^1] — a reading that [[Alfonso Archi]]'s epigraphic work shows is not supportable since the text is "quite incomprehensible" beyond its opening words.

The contrast between Pettinato's, Matthews & Benjamin's, and Archi's translations illustrates the fundamental problem: when authoritative scholars cannot agree on what the text says, it cannot bear the weight of a precise theological claim like creation ex nihilo. See [[Pettinato Ebla Translation Unreliable]].
