# Charles Murray

**Charles Murray** (b. 1943) is an American libertarian political scientist and author associated with the American Enterprise Institute, known for works on social policy and class.

## In This Vault

In the [[Jonathan Haidt - The Elephant the Rider and Epistemic Humility|Haidt interview]], the interviewer attributes to Murray the point that "when the government takes over certain functions, then certain aspects of natural society just tend to atrophy" — because if the state provides a service, "ordinary people are not going to volunteer to do that thing," and the "natural sympathy" expressed through voluntary action is crowded out. [^1] [[Jonathan Haidt]] endorses this as "a perfect example" of a "subtle" systemic insight — "an understanding of how systems can crush personal caring and responsibility and relationships" — which he uses to illustrate the high-systemizing libertarian cognitive style described in [[Moral Foundations Theory]]. [^2]
