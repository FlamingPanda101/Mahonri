# Cyrus the Great

**Who:** Founder of the Achaemenid Persian Empire (r. 559–530 BCE). Conquered Babylon in 539 BCE.

**Role in biblical studies:** Cyrus is named explicitly in [[Isaiah 45.1-7|Isaiah 45:1]] as YHWH's "anointed one" (*mashiach*, translated *christos* in Greek). He allowed the Judahite exiles to return and rebuild the Jerusalem temple after conquering Babylon. This naming is a key piece of evidence for the [[Deutero-Isaiah Theory]], since Isaiah 45 was traditionally attributed to the 8th-century prophet Isaiah — roughly 200 years before Cyrus appeared on the scene.

## Deutero-Isaiah
The naming of Cyrus by name in Isaiah 45 is one of the strongest clues that Isaiah 40–55 was composed in the post-exilic period (~520 BCE), not by the 8th-century prophet. See [[Deutero-Isaiah Theory]], [[Prophecy and Specificity - Cyrus Named by Name]].

## Second Temple
Cyrus's decree allowing the Judahites to return and rebuild their temple (~538 BCE) initiated the Second Temple period. See [[Second Temple History]].
