# Jared Warren

Latter-day Saint psychologist and professor of psychology at Brigham Young University, specializing in **positive psychology** — what he describes as "the science of well-being." His emphasis is the difference between "surviving and actually thriving": the recognition "that there's more to a good life than just the absence of psychopathology."[^1]

## On Purpose, Gratitude, and "Honoring the Search"

Commenting on the [[Sam Hardy]] and [[Justin Dyer]] deidentification study, Warren observes that belonging to a religion cultivates purpose and gratitude, and asks where a young person who leaves will find these instead. He stresses that religion is *not* the only source of these positive functions and names the constructive alternative "honoring the search" — overcoming dissonance and discomfort to reach a place of flourishing, on a path unique to each person. See [[Honoring the Search - Finding Purpose After Leaving]] and [[A Science of Human Flourishing]].[^1]
