# Mark Smith

Mark S. Smith is one of the leading academic scholars of Israelite religion and the emergence of biblical monotheism, best known for *The Early History of God* and *The Origins of Biblical Monotheism*. His work documents the development from a divine-council polytheism (Yahweh among the *bene elohim*) toward later monotheism — the very ancient Near Eastern background that LDS apologists invoke.

## Cited as a Credible Christian Scholar, Not a Liberal Atheist
In [[Thoughtful Faith - The LDS Godhead in the Bible (Response to Sam Shamoun)]], [[Blake Ostler]] invokes Smith against [[Sam Shamoun]]'s caricature of consensus biblical scholarship as atheistic. Ostler stresses that Smith "has been the president of the Catholic Bible Association... he's a committed Catholic" who "actually wants to be honest with his evidence as best he can." [^1] Smith's scholarship underwrites the [[Ancient Judaism Affirmed a Divine Spectrum]] reading and the heavenly-council interpretation of texts like [[Deuteronomy 32.8-9|Deuteronomy 32:8-9]] that drives [[Pan-Canonical Univocality Is Invalid Interpretation]].
