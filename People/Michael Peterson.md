# Michael Peterson

Michael Peterson is a Latter-day Saint writer and lay researcher — a full-time financial professional who began writing on Church topics around 2021 after [[Public Square Magazine]] invited him to contribute. He is the principal author (with co-author Jacob Hess, who joined in 2023) of a roughly 70-page report arguing that the marketed *origin story* of [[Jeremy Runnells]]'s [[CES Letter]] was fabricated.[^1]

## The CES Letter Origin-Story Investigation

Peterson says the project grew out of Sarah Allen's 2021 piece "The Dishonest Origins of the CES Letter," which prompted him to undertake a fuller investigation in which "every rock would be turned over." Over roughly a year of research he assembled dated forum and Reddit posts to argue that Runnells was an active, adversarial critic for months before writing the letter — recasting the "honest seeker asked by a CES director" narrative as a "shiny wrapper" engineered to entice faithful members into reading recycled anti-Mormon material. See [[The Shiny Wrapper Was the Bait of the CES Letter]] and the timeline evidence in [[Adversarial Church-Bashing Predates the CES Letter by Months]].[^2]

He also argues the CES director named in the letter's title was an "incidental actor" rather than an official Church channel, and (citing Brian Hales) that addressing the letter to him lent it "a false sense of credibility."[^3]
