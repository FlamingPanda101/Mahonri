# Chris Blyth

Latter-day Saint historian and **folklorist** specializing in Mormon "the weird" — the stories, legends, and contested teachings of the LDS tradition. He earned a master's in history as "one of the first people to do a... masters under a Mormon studies chair," studying under **Philip Barlow**, then a PhD in American religious history at **Florida State University**, using folklore as his method. He has worked at BYU and for the **Joseph Smith Papers**, and co-hosts the podcast **Angels and Seer Stones** (also on Facebook/YouTube).[^1]

## On Adam-God
Blyth is the guest of honor in Ward Radio's Adam-God "reaction" episode and supplies its scholarly backbone. His key contributions:

- He **concedes Brigham Young taught Adam-God**, having personally spent "a year or two" trying to make the *two-Adams / "Adam is only a title"* reading work before abandoning it when he found places where [[Brigham Young]] "pretty clearly is like, yes, Adam of the Garden is the father of our spirits."[^2]
- He argues the **two-Adams reading arose as a reaction to LeBaron fundamentalism** in the 1950s–60s. See [[Two-Adams Theory Arose to Counter LeBaron Fundamentalism]].[^3]
- He reconstructs **how Brigham extrapolated the doctrine from Joseph Smith** via the ancient-of-days/Daniel 7 identification and the 1847 Winter Quarters vision. See [[How Brigham Young Extrapolated Adam-God from Joseph Smith]].[^4]
- He frames Adam-God's **payoff as identifying Eve with Heavenly Mother**. See [[Eve Is Heavenly Mother as the Payoff of Adam-God]].[^5]
- On the church's internal divisions, he affirms doctrinal pluralism ("I don't believe we're all supposed to be aligned on every issue"), personally holds to **infinite regression of gods** while separating it from Adam-God, and ultimately redirects seekers to **personal revelation** over partisan apologetic camps. See [[Two Churches Within a Church on Adam-God]].[^6]
