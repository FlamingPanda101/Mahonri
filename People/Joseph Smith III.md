# Joseph Smith III

Joseph Smith III (1832–1914) was the eldest surviving son of [[Joseph Smith]] and [[Emma Smith]], and the first Prophet-President of the **Reorganized Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints (RLDS)** — today the Community of Christ. The RLDS had already operated for about eight years (gathering scattered Strangites, Williamites, and Thompsonites) and received at least four revelations before he formally accepted leadership in **1860**.

## Strategy and Doctrinal Drift
Joseph III adopted his mother [[Emma Smith|Emma]]'s position that his father never practiced plural marriage ("it never happened"), against [[William Smith]] and William Marks, who held that Joseph had practiced it but gone astray. On contested Nauvoo doctrines he favored a "not yet / don't talk about it" approach rather than open denial: in an 1865 meeting he declined to make policy on the plurality of gods (the [[King Follett]] teachings) beyond "we're not going to talk about it," and he taught that **baptism for the dead** was a "local commandment" limited to Nauvoo. Per [[Christopher Blythe]], this strategy "weeded out" doctrines over a generation — a pattern completed when later RLDS leaders moved such Doctrine and Covenants sections to an appendix and then removed them entirely. See [[Ward Radio - What Happened to the First 6 Factions of Mormonism]], [[Rejecting Polygamy Unravels Sealing and the Plan of Salvation]].
