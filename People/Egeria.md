## Who
Egeria was a woman from a monastic community in Spain who made a pilgrimage to the Holy Land in the late fourth century and recorded a detailed pilgrimage diary (*Egeria's Travels* / *Itinerarium Egeriae*) describing the rituals and worship she witnessed.

## Authority and Reason for Citation
Egeria is an invaluable eyewitness to late-fourth-century liturgy in Jerusalem. She is cited for her description of the perpetually burning lamp in Christ's tomb at the Church of the Holy Sepulchre and the nightly kindling of the congregation's candles from it, and for her vivid account of the joyful, noisy response of newly baptized neophytes as their bishop expounded the mysteries of the ritual they had just experienced.

## Best Quote
> "The bishop relates what has been done, and interprets it. As he does so, the applause is so loud that it can be heard outside the church. Indeed, the way he expounds the mysteries and interprets them cannot fail to move his hearers." [^1]
