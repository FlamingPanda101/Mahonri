# Aphrahat

**Aphrahat, the Persian sage** (born c. 280 in modern Iran, died c. 345 AD), was an early Syriac Christian monk and perhaps a bishop. His only surviving work is the *Demonstrations*, a collection of twenty-three homilies, each on a different gospel topic.

## The Sleep of the Dead and the Heavenly Reward
Aphrahat held that the dead "sleep" in their graves until the resurrection — but in his conception, "the righteous anticipate the moment of their resurrection while the wicked dread it." At the resurrection humans are reconstituted, judged, and escorted to a fitting location. His description of heaven uses nature imagery characteristic of Syriac Christianity: a region of pleasant air and glorious light, with beautiful trees "whose fruits fail not," spacious and unlimited, where "the inheritance shall not be divided and no man shall say to his fellow: 'This is mine and that is thine.'" He taught the righteous are free from passions, "have no sexual differences, and are restored to youthful vigor." See [[Syriac Poetry Pictures Heaven as a Garden with Degrees of Glory]] [^1].
