# Neil Sinhababu

**Era:** Contemporary
**Role:** Analytic philosopher (National University of Singapore)

## Bio
Neil Sinhababu is an analytic philosopher working in metaethics, philosophy of mind, and philosophy of religion. (His name is sometimes rendered "Neil S. Babu.") He is the author of the objection to the fine-tuning argument that [[Trent Horn]] engages — the "electrons in love" / psychophysical-laws argument set out in his paper "Divine Fine-Tuning vs Electrons in Love."

## Contribution in This Video
Sinhababu reframes the fine-tuning argument as being about *conscious embodied agents* rather than life-as-we-know-it, then argues that under different **psychophysical laws** even a hydrogen-only universe could host conscious agents (protons and electrons that "feel delight," "yearn to be together," and "fall in love"). So, he claims, conscious agents may not be improbable, and a theist cannot call this metaphysically impossible while affirming non-physical minds (God, angels). Horn answers that minds existing apart from bodies does not entail minds existing in *any* body. See [[Electrons in Love - The Psychophysical-Laws Objection Rebutted]].
