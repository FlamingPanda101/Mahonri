# Clement of Alexandria

**Clement of Alexandria** (died c. 215 AD) led the Christian catechetical school in Alexandria, Egypt. His major work, the *Stromateis* ("Miscellanies"), is a large, deliberately unsystematic compendium of Christian knowledge.

## The Descent of Christ to the Realm of the Dead
About a century after [[Ignatius of Antioch]], Clement gave a "more consciously reasoned and systematic treatment" of Christ's preaching to the dead. His first principle was that it is Christ's work to save all who are willing "by the preaching [of the gospel], to believe on Him, wherever they were." Reasoning that "God is no respecter of persons," he concluded the apostles preached to worthy Gentiles in the underworld just as they had on earth — citing the [[Shepherd of Hermas|Shepherd of Hermas]]'s vision of apostles and teachers descending to baptize the righteous dead. He limited the audience to those who "fell asleep in justice and great purity": righteous Israelites, the prophets, and Gentiles who had lived by philosophy. See [[The Descent of Christ to the Underworld Was Universally Attested]] [^1].
