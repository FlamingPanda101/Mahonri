# John Barclay

Lightfoot Professor of Divinity at Durham University and a foremost scholar of the Apostle Paul, especially the theology of grace. His *Paul and the Gift* (2015) and *Paul and the Power of Grace* (2020) reframed scholarship on how Paul deployed *charis* against ancient gift conventions.

## Cited in *Ancient Christians*
Cecilia Peek relies on Barclay for the ancient backdrop against which Paul's grace was revolutionary: "Most gifts and benefits in the ancient world were distributed discriminately to fitting or worthy recipients." [^1] Barclay's account of gift reciprocity (Greek hospitality, Roman patronage, even human-divine sacrifice) sets up Peek's claim that Paul's free, unmerited grace to the *undeserving* inverted the entire system. See [[Paul Redefined Charis as Unmerited Gift Against Greco-Roman Reciprocity]].
