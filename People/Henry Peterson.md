# Henry Peterson

**Role:** Psychologist (MA, Harvard); BYU professor in the 1911 evolution controversy
**Era:** Early 20th century
**Tradition:** The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (LDS / Mormon)

> Note: [[Benjamin Park]] alternates the spellings "Peterson" and "Petersen" for this family in the transcript. Not to be confused with the unrelated [[Daniel C. Peterson]].

## Bio

Henry Peterson earned an MA in psychology from Harvard and was one of the four BYU professors in the [[The 1911 BYU Evolution Controversy and the Firing of Four Professors|1911 evolution controversy]]. He was the **first of the four to be fired**. After his dismissal he published an editorial in the local Provo newspaper arguing that evolution need not be feared and could be reconciled with LDS belief — "these aren't skeptics or secularists." He went on to a long career at the Utah State Agricultural College. He is the brother of [[Joseph Peterson]]. [^1]
