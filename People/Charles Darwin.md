# Charles Darwin

**Charles Darwin** (1809–1882) was an English naturalist whose *On the Origin of Species* (1859) established evolution by natural selection. His account of the origins of humanity and the diversity of life directly challenged literal readings of Genesis.

## As a Trigger for the Literalist Reaction

[[Dan McClellan]] alludes to Darwin's work — "the nature of the species, if you will" — among the 19th-century developments (alongside deep geological time and the discovery of dinosaurs) that "challenged traditional interpretations of the Bible," provoking the defensive turn: "the response was to lean into literalism." [^1][^2] The teaching of evolution in public schools became the explicit battleground at the 1925 Scopes Monkey Trial. See [[Literalism Is a Modern Reactionary Invention]] and [[The Fundamentals and the Birth of Fundamentalism]].
