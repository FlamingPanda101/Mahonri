# Corianton

Corianton is a son of [[Alma the Younger]] in the Book of Mormon, addressed in Alma 39-42. Having gone astray during missionary labor, he receives from his father one of the most theologically rich discourses in LDS scripture — on resurrection, restoration, and especially the relationship between **justice and mercy** in the plan of redemption.

## On Eternal Law and the Atonement
Alma's answers to Corianton's doubts ([[Alma 42.13|Alma 42:13]], [[Alma 42.22-25|Alma 42:22-25]]) are the primary scriptural basis for the doctrine that God operates within eternal law and "would cease to be God" if justice were robbed by mercy.
