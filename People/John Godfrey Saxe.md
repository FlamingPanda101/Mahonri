# John Godfrey Saxe

**Era:** 19th century (1816–1887)
**Tradition:** Secular / American literary
**Role:** American poet and humorist

## Bio
John Godfrey Saxe was a 19th-century American poet best known for popular humorous verse. His most enduring poem is "The Blind Men and the Elephant," a rhymed retelling of an ancient South Asian parable, which is the "ancient parable… put to rhyme by an American poet" quoted in the *Thoughtful Faith* video.

## Positions in this source
Saxe is quoted as the author of the poem framing the video's partial-knowledge argument:

- **The blind men and the elephant.** Six blind men of Hindustan each touch one part of an elephant and over-generalize (leg = tree, tusk = spear, tail = rope, trunk = snake), "each partly in the right and all in the wrong." The verse illustrates how genuine partial truth, grounded in personal experience, breeds unwarranted certainty about the whole.
