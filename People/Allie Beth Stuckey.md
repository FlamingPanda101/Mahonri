# Allie Beth Stuckey

**Role:** Conservative Christian commentator and podcast host (*Relatable*)
**Era:** Contemporary (active 2010s–2020s)
**Tradition:** Evangelical Protestant (Reformed-leaning Baptist)

## Bio

Allie Beth Stuckey is a conservative Christian commentator and host of the *Relatable* podcast. She writes and speaks from a Reformed-leaning evangelical Baptist perspective on faith, politics, and culture. In her interview/debate with Latter-day Saint apologist [[Jacob Hansen]], she states her aim is "for me to clarify what the LDS church teaches and what they actually believe," positioning Hansen as "a really good advocate for what you believe." [^1]

## The "Are Mormons Christians?" Case

Against Hansen, Stuckey argues that Latter-day Saints are not Christians in the saving sense because the LDS gospel adds priesthood ordinances to grace through faith and worships a different God. Her recurring criterion is the binary of Acts 4:12 ("no other name... by which one must be saved") and John 14:6 — one is either "in Christ or out of Christ," with no celestial spectrum. [^2] See:

## Sola Fide and the "Different Jesus"

Stuckey grounds her soteriology in Ephesians 2 ("by grace... through faith... not a result of works") and the spiritual deadness of the unregenerate, concluding that Christ's sacrifice alone gives "the fullness of relationship with God." [^3] On Christology she defends Nicene monotheism and the Trinity, pressing John 1:1, Isaiah 43:10, and Malachi 3:6 against the LDS picture of Jesus as the literal divine Son who descended in nature.
