# William Schryver

**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints
**Role:** Independent researcher on the Kirtland Egyptian Papers

## Bio
William Schryver is an independent Latter-day Saint researcher, a computer programmer by trade, known for a database-driven analysis of the so-called **Kirtland Egyptian Papers** (the Egyptian Alphabet documents and the *Grammar and Alphabet of the Egyptian Language*). His presentation is re-narrated at length in the Thoughtful Faith Book of Abraham video. (The video's auto-transcript mis-renders his name as "Shrivever" and "Trior.")[^1]

## On the Egyptian Alphabet as a cipher
Schryver argues, by inventorying the "substantial words" in the Alphabet/Grammar explanations, that those documents are *dependent on* a pre-existing [[Book of Abraham]] (and on prior revelations like [[D&C 76|D&C 76]] and [[D&C 88|88]]) rather than being the tool that produced it — and that the project was actually an idiographic "pure language" **cipher**, several of whose characters derive from Masonic ciphers. See [[The Kirtland Egyptian Papers Are a Cipher Project Not a Translation Tool]].[^1]
