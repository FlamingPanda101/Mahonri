# Eugene Foster

**Era:** 20th–21st century (1927–2008)
**Role:** Pathologist, geneticist

## Bio
Dr. Eugene A. Foster was a retired American pathologist affiliated with Tufts University and the University of Virginia. He led the team behind the November 1998 *Nature* article "Jefferson fathered slave's last child," which compared Y-chromosome haplotypes from descendants of [[Thomas Jefferson]]'s paternal line and of [[Sally Hemings]]'s son Eston.

## Cited For — Repudiating the Headline
On Ward Radio, Foster is the linchpin of the argument that the Jefferson–Hemings story was overstated. Within weeks of the splashy *Nature* headline, Foster published clarifications (and gave interviews) insisting the study **could not prove** Thomas Jefferson specifically was the father. He wrote that "the title assigned to our study was misleading" and that the genetic findings "do not prove that Thomas Jefferson was the father of Sally Hemings's children" — only that the father was *a* Jefferson, a category that includes Thomas's brother [[Randolph Jefferson]]. The hosts cite his official January 2000 assessment, still listed as the source on the Monticello website even as that site asserts Thomas's paternity as fact. See [[The Jefferson DNA Study Was Misreported as Proof]].[^1]
