# Harriet Tubman

**Era:** c. 1822 – 1913
**Tradition:** Christian (formerly enslaved)
**Role:** Underground Railroad conductor; abolitionist

## Bio

Harriet Tubman escaped slavery and led many others to freedom via the Underground Railroad.[^1]

## Topics

### "The Great Moses" of the Black Church
[[Glenn Scrivener]] invokes Tubman as the "great Moses" of the Black church, citing the deeply Old-Testament, Exodus-shaped spirituality of the abolitionist movement — against [[Alex OConnor|Alex O'Connor]]'s claim that the Exodus is Israelite-specific and was "misread" by the Black church. See [[The Old Testament Was a Hammer for Abolitionists]] and [[The Exodus Is Israelite-Specific Not Universal Abolition]].[^1]
