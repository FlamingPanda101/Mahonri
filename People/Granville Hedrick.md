# Granville Hedrick

Granville Hedrick (1814–1881) was the leader under whom the **Hedrickites** — the Church of Christ (Temple Lot) — organized in the 1860s, after the Civil War. The movement was associated with [[David Whitmer]] and sought to return the church "to the way the church would have been at the time of the Book of Commandments." It is distinctive among the breakaway factions for holding that [[Joseph Smith]] became a **fallen prophet** — that everything went astray at **Zion's Camp (1834)**, the first of Joseph's promises they say demonstrably failed.

## The Church of Christ (Temple Lot)
The Hedrickites returned to Independence, Missouri, and hold the "Zion's plat," including the cornerstone of the original temple lot, next to the Community of Christ temple. Their scripture is limited to the Bible and the Book of Mormon, with the Book of Commandments used as a non-binding guide. An offshoot, the Church of Christ (Elijah Message), adds *The Word of the Lord*, a collection of revelations attributed to John the Baptist appearing to men in the 1830s about building the new temple. Per [[Christopher Blythe]], the Temple Lot church has held on to membership across time at roughly Bickertonite levels, while the Elijah-Message offshoot has dwindled. See [[Ward Radio - What Happened to the First 6 Factions of Mormonism]], [[Growth Past the Second Generation Validates a Movement]].
