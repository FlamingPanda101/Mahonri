# Trent Horn

Catholic apologist with **Catholic Answers**, host of *The Council of Trent* podcast and author of *The Case for Catholicism*. Known for rigorous, citation-heavy engagement with Protestant and secular arguments.

## Justification / James 2
Argues James 2:24 refutes *sola fide* while also correcting Catholic misuses of the verse — distinguishing initial (grace alone) from ongoing (increased by works) justification. See [[James 2-24 - Justified by Works Not Faith Alone]] and [[Initial vs Ongoing Justification]] [^1].

## Infant Baptism (Response to Gavin Ortlund)
Horn's heavily-cited case for infant baptism (responding to [[Gavin Ortlund]]) runs on a doctrine/discipline distinction and a theology-first argument, then rebuts the argument from silence and the catacomb-inscription data. See:
- [[Baptismal Regeneration Is the Doctrine Infant Baptism Is the Discipline]]
- [[The Argument from Silence Cuts Both Ways on Infant Baptism]]
- [[Credobaptists Cannot Agree on the Age of Baptism]]
- [[Catacomb Baptism Inscriptions Are Emergency Cases Not the Norm]]
- [[Tertullian's Opposition Presupposes an Established Practice]]
- [[Augustine Clarified Infant Baptism He Did Not Invent It]]

## Book of Mormon Plates Word-Count Argument (Rebutted)
In an Instagram reel, Horn argued the 270,000-word Book of Mormon could not have fit on the golden plates — each character would carry ~80 English words, unlike ancient scripts (e.g. the plates of Darius, ~200 words/page). [[Keystone]] rebuts this: Horn's numbers imply only ~42 characters per plate-side (an unsupported assumption), and he displayed a *3/4-scale* model holding ~128 characters/side (full-scale ~230). See [[Trent Horns Word-Count Argument Uses Wrong Parameters]] and [[The Book of Mormon Fits on the Plates - Sjodahl Hebrew Test]].[^3]
