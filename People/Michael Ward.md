# Michael Ward

**Role:** C.S. Lewis scholar; literary scholar and theologian; Anglican clergyman
**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** Christian (Anglican)

## Bio

Michael Ward is a leading C.S. Lewis scholar, best known for *Planet Narnia*, the widely praised study arguing that Lewis structured the seven *Chronicles of Narnia* on the symbolism of the seven medieval planets. An ordained Anglican clergyman who has held teaching and research posts at Oxford and elsewhere, Ward writes for both scholarly and general audiences. [^1]

In *After Humanity: A Guide to C.S. Lewis's The Abolition of Man*, Ward provides the reception history, occasion, background, and legacy of Lewis's 1943 lectures, together with a line-by-line commentary defending Lewis's case for the objectivity of value (the *Tao*) against modern subjectivism. See [[After Humanity - Michael Ward]] and [[C.S. Lewis]]. [^1]
