# Oliver Sacks

**Role:** Neurologist and author of clinical case studies on the brain
**Era:** 1933–2015
**Field:** Neurology / neuroscience

## Bio

Oliver Sacks was a neurologist celebrated for narrative clinical case studies (*Awakenings*, *The Man Who Mistook His Wife for a Hat*) that explored how brain conditions reshape perception, emotion, and the sense of self.

## Relevance here

[[Blake Ostler]] groups Sacks with [[Antonio Damasio]] as neuroscientists whose laboratory and clinical studies show that persons "lacking properly functioning emotions are incapable of ability to fully cognize and are incapable of making rational decisions in cases involving their own welfare." Ostler uses this to rebut the secular-materialist dismissal of spiritual experience as untrustworthy "mere emotion." [^1] See [[Reason Presupposes Emotion - The Neuroscience Reply to Materialism]].
