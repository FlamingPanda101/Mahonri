# Jonathan Goldstein

**Role:** Scholar of Hellenistic Judaism; Anchor Bible commentator
**Era:** 20th century (active 1970s–1980s)
**Tradition:** Academic Jewish studies

## Bio

Jonathan Goldstein was a scholar of Hellenistic Judaism best known for his Anchor Bible commentaries on 1 Maccabees (1976) and 2 Maccabees (1984). He was personally committed to defending the doctrine of creation ex nihilo and WANTED to find it in 2 Maccabees 7:28 — but his own scholarly analysis prevented him from doing so.

## 2 Maccabees 7:28 Commentary

Goldstein's Anchor Bible commentary on 2 Maccabees 7:22–29 is notable because he was a defender of creation ex nihilo who nonetheless concluded the text does not teach it:

> "Origen took 2 Maccabees 7:22-29 as the first unequivocal statement in scripture of the doctrine of creation ex nihilo. In my commentary to the passage, however, I prove that Origen was mistaken. Though the passage displays profound philosophical knowledge, it does not assert creation ex nihilo."[^1]

Goldstein's candor is significant: if even a scholar who PREFERS the traditional reading concedes the text doesn't support it, that's powerful evidence.

### Debate with David Winston

Goldstein published an article in the *Journal of Jewish Studies* (1984) attempting to argue that the context of 2 Maccabees 7 doesn't necessarily rule out creation ex nihilo. [[David Winston]] responded in the same journal the same year, demonstrating that Goldstein's argument was not accurate and agreeing the text does not teach creation ex nihilo. Goldstein later acknowledged Winston "had him on a bunch of things" but attempted to salvage portions of his case.[^2]
