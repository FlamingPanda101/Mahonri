# Lydia Novakovich

**Role:** New Testament scholar; author of a Baylor Handbook on the Greek text of John
**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** Protestant (academic biblical studies)

## Bio

Lydia Novakovich (Lidija Novakovic) is a New Testament scholar whose work appears in this vault as one of the standard reference grammars on the Fourth Gospel: *John 1–10: A Handbook on the Greek Text* in Baylor University Press's handbook series. These handbooks provide clause-by-clause analysis of the Greek text and are used as technical scholarly references rather than popular exposition. She also works on the resurrection and on the use of scripture in early Christianity.

## Relevance to the vault

Novakovich is cited as one of four standard works (alongside BDAG, [[Daniel Wallace]], and [[John McHugh]]) that [[Dan McClellan]] marshals for the *intermediate-agency* reading of [[John 1.3|John 1:3]]. Her handbook glosses the Greek *dia* construction as "secondary, intermediate agency," adding that "the primary ultimate agent of creation is God." That distinction — ultimate agency (the Father, with *hypo*) versus intermediate agency (the Logos, with *dia*) — is the grammatical backbone of the claim, developed in [[John 1-3 - The Logos Is the Intermediate Agent of Creation Not the Originator]], that John 1:3 makes the Logos God's agent of creation rather than its originator, and so does not by itself assert that the Logos *is* the God of Israel.
