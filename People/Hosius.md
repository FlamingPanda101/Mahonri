# Hosius

Hosius (Ossius) of Corduba (c. 256–359) was a Spanish bishop who served as the emperor [[Constantine]]'s chief ecclesiastical advisor and is widely thought to have presided over, or heavily shaped, the proceedings at the Council of Nicaea (325).

## Cited as Constantine's Ambassador at Nicaea
In [[Thoughtful Faith - The LDS Godhead in the Bible (Response to Sam Shamoun)]], [[Blake Ostler]] identifies Hosius as "the ambassador of Constantine," part of the imperial apparatus by which Constantine controlled the council — inviting bishops (including previously excommunicated Arians) to his palace where "he could control things." [^1] The detail supports [[Nicaea Did Not Settle the Trinity]]: the outcome reflected imperial management of disunity, not a transparent reading of the biblical text.
