# Karol Wojtyła (John Paul II) (1920-2005)

Polish philosopher, priest, and Pope (1978-2005). Before his pontificate he wrote *Love and Responsibility* (1960), a personalist analysis of sexual ethics that prefigured his later *Theology of the Body* catecheses. A central architect of modern Catholic teaching on marriage, the body, and the dignity of the person.

## On marital intimacy as mutual self-gift
[[Trent Horn]] cites *Love and Responsibility* in arguing that pornography and self-pleasure corrupt the marital act, which should be a harmonious gift of both spouses rather than the arousal of one. Wojtyła: "intercourse must not serve merely as a means of allowing sexual excitement to reach its climax in one of the partners, i.e. the man alone, but that climax must be reached in harmony, not at the expense of one partner, but with both partners fully involved." Used to show the Church does not ignore sexual frustration within marriage but orders it toward genuine union. See [[Pornography Corrupts Distinct Gendered Desires]] and [[1 Corinthians 7.3-5]] [^1].

## On the meaning of suffering (cited against assisted suicide)
[[Trent Horn]] cites John Paul II's teaching that suffering is not inherently pointless — it can be **united to Christ's suffering** for a greater spiritual good (cf. the encyclical *Salvifici Doloris*) — to rebut the charge that opposing assisted suicide just means wanting people to suffer. See [[Refusing Extraordinary Means Is Not Choosing to Suffer]] (source: [[The Counsel of Trent - The Assisted Suicide Dystopia Is Here]]).[^2]
