# Tabitha

First-century Christian disciple of Joppa (also called Dorcas), "known for her good works and charity," raised from the dead by Peter (Acts 9:36–39).

## Patron and leader of widows
[[Ariel Bybee Laughton]] reads Tabitha as a patron to a community of Christian widows, providing them clothing and likely other means, "earning their gratitude, respect, and the care of her body when she died." She notes that Tabitha is "the only person called a *mathētria*" — the **feminine** form of "disciple" — which has led some scholars to suggest she personally knew and followed Jesus in Galilee and may have known Peter well. The Greek terminology "suggests … she was a leader of this community of Christian widows at Joppa." See [[Women Led the First-Century Church as Apostles Deacons and Patrons]]. [^1]
