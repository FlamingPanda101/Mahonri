# Dan McClellan

**Who:** Public biblical scholar, Ph.D. Co-host of the *Data Over Dogma* podcast.
**Role:** Presents academic critical consensus on the Bible, often critiquing apologetic uses of scripture. Approaches the text as a historian, not a theologian.

## Data Over Dogma

The podcast "increases public access to the academic study of the Bible and religion and combats the spread of misinformation about the same." Co-hosted with [[Dan Beecher]].

## Nativity & Prophecy Fulfillment

Argues that Matthew's nativity account is constructed to make Jesus appear to fulfill Hebrew Bible prophecies, and that the nativity accounts in Matthew and Luke irreconcilably contradict each other. See [[Isaiah 7.14 Virgin Birth Prophecy Is Mistranslated]], [[Matthews Nativity Contradicts Lukes Nativity]], [[Census of Quirinius Is Historically Unworkable]].

## The Virgin Birth

Holds that the virgin birth is a "posthumous secondary accretion to the Jesus tradition," absent from Paul and Mark, appearing only in later gospels as concerns about Jesus's divine origins intensified. See [[Virgin Birth Is a Later Development in the Jesus Tradition]].

## Midrash and Matthew's Use of Scripture

Acknowledges Matthew follows Jewish interpretive practices (dynamic hermeneutics) but resists calling it "midrash" as a formal label, and rejects the apologetic claim that the original audience would have understood it as mere "creative play" rather than a hard historical claim. See [[Isaiah 7.14 Virgin Birth Prophecy Is Mistranslated]].

## The Census of Quirinius

Argues Luke 2:2's census is historically unworkable: no empire-wide census requiring ancestral hometown travel existed; Herod was a client king who managed his own taxation; Quirinius didn't govern Syria until 6 CE, a decade after Herod's death. Critiques the "prote" apologetic as "clearly just apologetic in origin." See [[Census of Quirinius Is Historically Unworkable]] and [[Tacitus Cleitae Passage Does Not Support Roman Census of Client Kingdoms]].
