# Fanny Alger

**Role:** Identified as Joseph Smith's earliest plural wife (Kirtland era)
**Era:** 19th century (1816–1889)
**Tradition:** LDS (Kirtland period)

## Bio

Fanny Alger is generally identified by historians as Joseph Smith's first plural wife, in Kirtland, Ohio in the mid-1830s. In [[Brian Hales]]'s telling, what is striking is her *non-denial*: when later questioned about the relationship (by her brother) — at a time when she was no longer connected to the church and had every opportunity to flatly deny it — she instead declined to discuss it, treating it as "her own business" rather than denying it occurred.[^1]

## Polygamy Testimony

Alger appears on the long list of plural-wife eyewitnesses that [[Brian Hales]] marshals against the polygamy-conspiracy theory; her refusal to deny, like the near-total absence of recantations among the named wives, fits the [[The Confidentiality Model Best Fits the Polygamy Evidence|confidentiality model]] rather than a fabricated-by-Brigham model.
