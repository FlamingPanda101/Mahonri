# Nathan Apffel

Documentary filmmaker who analyzes the finances of religious organizations and "audits" prosperity-gospel mega-churches. A guest on the Tucker Carlson show, he is the source of the central claim engaged in [[Latter Daily Saints - The Mormon Church Profits from War Claim Ep 109|this episode]]: that the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints "profits from war" because Ensign Peak Advisors holds defense-contractor stock. (The hosts mispronounce his name throughout as "Applegate" and "Affol.")

- **Worldview:** Apffel's theology, the hosts explain, "doesn't allow for any organized institution" — he holds that all institutional religion is corrupt and that people should "worship Jesus in a decentralized way," "every man his own priest, every man his own pope."[^1][^2] This anti-institutional premise frames his entire analysis of the Church. See [[Conflating the Church With Prosperity-Gospel Grifters]].
- **The "profits from war" claim:** He cites Ensign Peak's holdings in Lockheed Martin, Northrop Grumman, Raytheon, and L3 Harris and concludes "the LDS church profits from war."[^3] The hosts grant the holdings but reject the inference of malice. See [[Profiting From War Charge Confuses Passive Holdings With Endorsement]].
- **Method (mega-church auditing):** His usual targets are figures like Kenneth Copeland — preachers with "private jets" and "mansions" claiming nonprofit status. The hosts argue he wrongly imports this grifter template onto the Church. See [[Conflating the Church With Prosperity-Gospel Grifters]] and [[Grift Does Not Track With Disbelief Rebuttal]].
- **Other claims:** He is also remembered for asserting that bishops verify tithing by checking members' bank statements — which the hosts flatly deny ("No, they won't really do that").[^4]
