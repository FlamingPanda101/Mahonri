# R.P.C. Hanson

Richard Patrick Crosland Hanson — Anglican bishop (Church of Ireland) and one of the foremost scholars of the Arian controversy. His *The Search for the Christian Doctrine of God* is a standard treatment of the 4th-century debates.

## Cited by Moroni's Standard
[[Alex Arnold]] cites Hanson's conclusion, after decades of study, that the 4th-century church "wasn't retrieving an established apostolic doctrine. It was searching for one" — a key plank in the argument that Nicaea was a construction rather than a recovery. See [[The Trend Line Runs the Wrong Way for the Recovery Narrative]] and [[The Creeds Cannot Be the Neutral Baseline - The Circularity of Creedal Disqualification]]. Arnold lists him among the non-LDS authorities "that creedal Christianity's own institutions trained, employed, and cited."[^1]
