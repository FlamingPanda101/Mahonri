# Elizabeth Cady Stanton

A leading nineteenth-century American suffragist and women's-rights activist (rendered "Elizabeth Katie Stanton" in the transcript). Cited by [[Britney Chapman Nash]] as having "used the fury that was pointed at polygamy" to redirect attention to the broader unfairness of *all* nineteenth-century marriage — arguing, in effect, "marriage in general is unfair," not polygamy alone.[^1]

Nash invokes Stanton to support the point that monogamy in the period was not the egalitarian baseline critics imagine: monogamous American women frequently could not own property, vote, escape domestic violence, or keep custody on divorce. See [[Nineteenth-Century Utah Women Had More Rights Than Monogamous American Women]].
