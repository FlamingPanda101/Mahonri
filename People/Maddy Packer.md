# Maddy Packer

**Era:** Contemporary (2020s)
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint (LDS)
**Role:** Newcomer apologist / content creator ("The Sure Foundation")

## Bio

Maddy Packer is a Latter-day Saint apologist who runs the Instagram account **"The Sure Foundation"** (tagline drawn from Helaman 5:12 — "building faith on truth, deconstructing everything else"). She grew up as one of very few LDS students at a Catholic school (7th–12th grade) in New Mexico, where she was "constantly defending why I was LDS," and describes a "deep prompting" to enter public apologetics.[^1] Within three weeks of starting her account she became the target of a viral reel by Evangelical critic [[David Latting]], which prompted this [[Ward Radio]] episode in her defense.[^2]

## Her Core Argument: Define a Christian by Praxis, Not Metaphysics

Packer's reel — the whole occasion for the episode — argued that a Christian is best **defined by what one does** (following Christ's commandments, mercy, covenant relationship) rather than by assent to a metaphysical formula such as the Trinity. "I don't really care what you think about Jesus. I'm proving that I follow the Jesus in the Bible by what I do."[^3] This is the [[Orthopraxy over Orthodoxy]] move applied to the question of who counts as Christian. See [[Define Christian First and the Concession Follows]].

Her supporting points across the stream:
- **The Trinity is an arbitrary gate.** The creedal *homoousios* test is "post-biblical," has been disputed for centuries, and the word *homoousios* "does not appear in the Bible at all." Making it the boundary of Christianity is therefore arbitrary.[^4] See [[Understanding the Trinity Is Not Required for Salvation]], [[The Trinity and Consubstantiality Are Not in the Bible]].
- **Scripture cuts the other way.** She grounds praxis-over-belief in [[Matthew 7.21]] ("not everyone that saith Lord, Lord… but he that doeth the will of my Father").[^5]
- **Worldview anachronism.** The biblical figures were not thinking in "Greek philosophical metaphysical" categories of substance and essence; that is read back onto a Hebraic worldview.[^6] See [[Greek Categories Are Anachronistic to the Hebrew Bible]].
- **"Different Jesus" is a demonization lever.** She observes that the real function of "you worship a different Jesus" is to license the further charge that LDS worship a *demon*.[^7] See [[The Three Ds Rubric Diagnoses Anti-Mormonism as Bigotry]].
- **Internal critique at the table.** Doing campus outreach with the prompt "the Trinity is not biblical," she asks interlocutors to define the Trinity; she reports none can do so without committing a heresy — so a doctrine almost no layperson can state coherently cannot be the test of salvation.[^8] Cf. [[Internal Critique - Two Questions for Every Critic]].

She also affirms that membership and belief are *meant* to travel together: belief in Christ as savior is "what's supposed to drive you to keep your covenants," even though the Church has no belief-monitoring mechanism beyond the self-reported **temple recommend** interview.[^9]
