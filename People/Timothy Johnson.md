# Timothy Johnson

Member of the Stayton Ward in central Oregon whose case Ward Radio Part 6 calls "the key piece … Michael Rezendes and his muckrakers missed." In 2019 Johnson confessed inappropriate conduct with a minor to his bishop. Oregon law did not then require leaders to report a confessed abuse, "but they felt they should, and they did" — and Johnson was sentenced to 15 years in prison for first-degree sodomy, sexual abuse, and unlawful sexual penetration of a girl under 16.

## The lawsuit for reporting
Two years later, Johnson's wife (joined by the children as plaintiffs) sued the church *for reporting* the abuse, alleging it deprived them of his "companionship, society, love, and income" and seeking $10 million plus $40,000 for Johnson's criminal-defense bills. The case establishes the reporting Catch-22: the church is sued whether it reports or not.
