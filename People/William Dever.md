# William Dever

Professor emeritus of Near Eastern archaeology at the University of Arizona and a leading figure in Syro-Palestinian ("biblical") archaeology.

## Feminine Divine / Asherah

Dever's popular-academic book *Did God Have a Wife? Archaeology and Folk Religion in Ancient Israel* (Eerdmans, 2005) synthesizes archaeological evidence — including the Kuntillet Ajrud and Khirbet el-Qom inscriptions referencing "Yahweh and his Asherah" — to argue that goddess worship and a feminine divine consort were widespread in pre-exilic Israelite folk religion before being suppressed by monotheistic reform movements.

Cited by [[Feminine Divine in Ancient Near Eastern Worship]] as evidence that mainstream scholarship recognizes a feminine divine in ancient Israelite worship, consistent with LDS teaching on a Heavenly Mother.
