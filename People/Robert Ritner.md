# Robert Ritner

**Full Name:** Robert Kriech Ritner (1953–2021)
**Era:** Modern
**Tradition:** Secular / academic (non-LDS)
**Role:** Egyptologist, University of Chicago (Oriental Institute)

## Bio

Robert Ritner was a prominent American Egyptologist at the University of Chicago
who wrote a full study of the Joseph Smith papyri and is among the harshest
academic critics of the Book of Abraham, translating the surviving fragments as
ordinary Greco-Roman Book of Breathings funerary text rather than a record of
Abraham.

## Topics

### Critic of the Book of Abraham
In [[The Stick of Joseph - Temple Knowledge Encoded in Egyptian Mythology]], the
hosts treat Ritner as the credentialed face of the standard critique — he "wrote
a whole book on the Joseph Smith papyri and hammers Joseph Smith."[^1] Their
rebuttal is not that Ritner mistranslates but that he allegedly *withholds*: he
"knows it inside and out and he holds back on this whole idea of this being
Abraham," declining to discuss the Osiris myth "in the way it's supposed to be
spoken of" — i.e., that the lion-couch figure is never really Osiris but a
[[Osiris Is a Type Not a Person - The Blessed Dead Become Osiris|type one becomes]].[^1]
See [[The Facsimiles Encode Abraham's Endowment Through the Osiris Myth]].
