# Rechab

**Rechab** (also Rachab) is the eponymous forefather of the **Rechabites**, a Jerusalem-era clan known canonically from [[Jeremiah 35|Jeremiah 35]] and elaborated in the apocryphal *History of the Rechabites* (the *Zosimus* narrative). In [[Jeremiah 35|Jeremiah 35]] the Rechabites are bound by the command of **Jonadab son of Rechab** to drink no wine and to build no houses, sow no seed, and plant no vineyards, but to dwell as nomads in tents — a fidelity the Lord rewards with the promise that Jonadab "shall not want a man to stand before me for ever."

## Bio / Significance
In the apocryphal *History of the Rechabites*, the monk **Zosimus** is led by an angel across an impassably large body of water to a righteous people who declare "we are the children of Rechab" — a community that, when "Jeremiah the prophet declared that the city of Jerusalem was to be handed over to destroyers," paid attention, pleaded for the city, then was driven out for "worshiping God the Son of God himself" and departed across the waters to a promised land.

## Contribution
On Ward Radio, [[Jonah Barnes]] proposes Rechab and the Rechabites as concrete candidates for the unnamed "prophets of old, whom they had cast out and stoned and slain" that [[Lehi]] invokes in [[1 Nephi 1.8-19|1 Nephi 1:19-20]] — a "proto-Lehi" whose flight from doomed Jerusalem for messianism produces a six-point parallel to the Book of Mormon (Jerusalem just before the captivity; renaming after a righteous father; belief that Jerusalem will fall; flight into the wilderness; a promised land across the waters; the divine sonship of the Messiah).
