# Susa Young Gates

Daughter of [[Brigham Young]], cited by [[Keystone - Top 10 Abandoned LDS Temple Practices]] as the documented case study for women once being sealed to multiple living men. She was sealed to Alma Dunford in the Salt Lake Endowment House in 1872; after a civil divorce she married Jacob Gates in the St. George Temple in 1880, and was technically sealed to both living men until [[Wilford Woodruff]] approved a cancellation of her sealing to Alma Dunford in 1890.[^1]

Her case anchors [[Women Were Once Sealed to Multiple Living Men]]: the present one-living-man rule for women is a later policy (hardening in the 1910s–early 1920s), not an unchangeable doctrine — Keystone notes this arrangement "was pretty normal in early Utah before sealing cancellations became more normal around the 1890s."[^2]
