# Ron Esplin

Ronald K. Esplin is, in [[Daniel C. Peterson]]'s description, "a premier Latter-day Saint historian" and an expert on the 1844 succession crisis. He was interviewed for Peterson's *Becoming Brigham* project and authored the Interpreter article "Joseph Smith: A Life Lived in Crescendo," which Peterson calls "the definitive statement" on succession.[^1]

Esplin's key finding: in 1844, after Joseph Smith's death, the only faction planning to carry out Joseph's *full* program was the Quorum of the Twelve led by [[Brigham Young]]; every other claimant (e.g., [[Sydney Rigdon]]) wanted to dispense with or devalue some aspect of what Joseph taught.[^2]
