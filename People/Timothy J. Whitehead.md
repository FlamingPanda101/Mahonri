# Timothy J. Whitehead

A Reformed Baptist pastor in Maryville, Tennessee, and adjunct professor at North Greenville University, with a bachelor's in chemistry (University of Tennessee) and a master's and doctorate from Southern Baptist Theological Seminary. He takes the **affirmative** in a formal debate against [[Shiloh Lofgren]] on the resolution that "the Bible is the sole infallible rule of faith that makes modern revelation unnecessary," hosted at a Presbyterian church in partnership with Apologia Church Utah.

His case is a presuppositional/Reformed defense of *sola scriptura* in four arguments: (1) a necessary distinction between **truth** (infallible, from God) and **knowledge** (our fallible interpretation); (2) everyone must appeal to an **ultimate self-authenticating standard**, and a true ultimate standard witnesses to itself while false ones self-destruct ([[Every Worldview Has a Self-Authenticating Ultimate Standard]]); (3) the Bible is "truth without any mixture of error," with inspiration ([[2 Peter 1.20|2 Peter 1:20]]) keeping revelation separate from fallible human interpretation; (4) all other authorities (science, courts, church) are fallible and depend on Scripture. On canon he argues the self-attesting, **Deuteronomy-18 infallibility standard** is what excludes both the deuterocanon and Joseph Smith's claimed revelations ([[Deuteronomy 18 Infallibility Standard Against New Revelation]]).
