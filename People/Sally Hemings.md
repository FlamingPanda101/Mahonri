# Sally Hemings

**Era:** 18th–19th century (c. 1773–1835)
**Role:** Historical figure (enslaved at Monticello)

## Bio
Sarah "Sally" Hemings was an enslaved woman at [[Thomas Jefferson]]'s Monticello plantation. A long-running claim — originating in political slander during the Adams–Jefferson rivalry, later "memorialized" by [[Fawn Brodie]]'s Freudian psychobiography, and seemingly confirmed by a 1998 DNA headline — holds that Jefferson fathered six of her children.

## Cited For
On Ward Radio she is the test case for how a rumor hardens into "fact." The hosts argue the documentary and genetic evidence actually points to a different Jefferson male, most plausibly Thomas's brother [[Randolph Jefferson]], who is recorded socializing in the slave quarters and was present at the relevant time. The geneticist [[Eugene Foster]] himself stated the DNA study proved only that *a* Jefferson, not Thomas specifically, fathered Eston Hemings.
