# Ruslan KD

**Role:** Evangelical YouTuber, podcaster, and apologetics interviewer
**Era:** Contemporary (active 2010s–2020s)
**Tradition:** Evangelical Protestant (raised Armenian Apostolic / Oriental Orthodox)

## Bio

Ruslan KD is an evangelical content creator who hosts long-form interviews and debates with figures across the religious and irreligious spectrum. He frames himself as a follower of "the Jesus of the New Testament" who evaluates other traditions against a *mere Christianity* / creedal baseline. In dialogue with [[Jacob Hansen]] he repeatedly stresses common ground — both reject atheism, both affirm "Jesus of Nazareth is the way out of this mess" — before pressing where Latter-day Saints diverge.[^1]

He notes he grew up Armenian Apostolic, part of the Oriental Orthodox communion, and no longer identifies that way; he points to the ancient African and Ethiopian churches as evidence that racial hierarchy is untenable in church history. He mentions his wife is black and that race questions are personal to him.[^2]

## Posture Toward Latter-day Saints

Ruslan publicly condemned an attack on an LDS meetinghouse and built a friendship with Hansen from there, insisting "we can separate the faith from the people."[^1] His core contention is that the creator/creation distinction (he cites [[Romans 1.16|Romans 1]] and Jesus as Yahweh in [[Hebrews 1.3|Hebrews 1]]) is where the LDS view of God genuinely deviates, and that adding [[Joseph Smith]] yields, in his view, "a totally different Jesus." He concedes the *fruits* of Latter-day Saints (family, conduct) are admirable while denying that fruit settles whether it is the same Jesus. See:
