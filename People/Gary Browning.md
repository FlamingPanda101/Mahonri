# Gary Browning

**Era:** 20th–21st century
**Tradition:** The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (LDS / Mormon)
**Role:** BYU Russian-language professor; early mission president in Moscow

## Bio

Gary Browning was a BYU Russian-faculty linguist and one of the first mission presidents in Moscow after the Church opened in the former Soviet Union. In the patriarchal-blessing-tribes story recounted on [[Ward Radio]] by [[Jonah Barnes]], Browning is described as a second, independent vetter: after a traveling patriarch (Elder Schütze) began assigning Russian converts to unusual tribes of Israel (Gad, Reuben, Naphtali, Simeon) in their patriarchal blessings, church leadership sent Browning — himself called as a patriarch, and not told why — to check the work. Browning, working independently, reportedly found the same distribution of tribes, which (in the narrator's account) prompted the Quorum of the Twelve to investigate and ultimately [[Russell M. Nelson]]'s 2004 visit to Siberia. Presented secondhand and unverified.
