# Alexander of Alexandria

Alexander of Alexandria (bishop, d. 326) was the chief opponent of [[Arius]] (a presbyter in his own diocese) and a leading advocate of the position that prevailed at the Council of Nicaea — that the Son is consubstantial (*homoousios*) with the Father. He is the mentor of [[Athanasius]], who carried the anti-Arian cause forward.

## Cited as a Protagonist of the Nicene Controversy
In [[Thoughtful Faith - The LDS Godhead in the Bible (Response to Sam Shamoun)]], [[Blake Ostler]] names Alexander as one of "the two primary protagonists," set opposite [[Eusebius of Caesarea]]. [^1] Ostler's point in [[Nicaea Did Not Settle the Trinity]] is that the homoousios position Alexander championed was a minority view before the council and required decades of further conflict, not that it fell self-evidently out of Scripture as [[Sam Shamoun]] claims.
