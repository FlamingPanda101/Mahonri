# George Q. Cannon (1827-1901)

LDS apostle, member of the **First Presidency**, and one of Mormonism's most influential 19th-century publishers and editors. Cited here for his high view of the *Journal of Discourses*.

## The Journal of Discourses as a "standard work"
The preface to *Journal of Discourses* vol. 8 (attributed to Cannon's circle) states the JoD "deservedly ranks as one of the standard works of the church... an additional reflector of the light that shines from Zion's Hill." [[Joe Heschmeyer]] uses this to argue the JoD — where Adam-God was published — carried scriptural weight in the 19th century; he clarifies Cannon "made an argument that it *should* be considered" a standard work. See [[Journal of Discourses Was Treated as Scripture]] [^1].

## On Divorce in Plural Marriage
In an 1879 statement cited by [[Keystone]], Cannon held that "the liberty upon this point of divorce rests with the woman," and that if a wife desired separation the husband was "bound also to give her and her offspring a proportionate share of his whole property." See [[Polygamous Utah Had the Nations Easiest Divorce Especially for Women]].[^2]
