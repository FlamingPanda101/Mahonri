# J.P. Moreland

**Role:** Analytic philosopher of religion and Christian apologist
**Era:** Contemporary (20th–21st century)
**Tradition:** Evangelical Protestant

## Bio

James Porter "J.P." Moreland is Distinguished Professor of Philosophy at Talbot School of Theology (Biola University). Trained in both science (chemistry) and philosophy, he is a leading evangelical voice in philosophy of mind, metaphysics, and natural theology. With [[William Lane Craig]] he co-edited *The Blackwell Companion to Natural Theology*, and he authored *Scientism and Secularism* (a book-length critique of scientism), among many other works defending substance dualism and theistic arguments.

## Contribution in This Source

In [[All Those In Favor - Joseph Smith vs Catholic on Creation]], [[Kaden Alvie]] cites Moreland — describing him as a "legendary Christian apologist" with degrees in science and philosophy who edited the Oxford/Blackwell companion alongside William Lane Craig — for the claim, made in Moreland's book on scientism, that it is "theoretically possible for there to be something that happened before the Big Bang, some kind of **quantum foam**." The hosts use this to support [[The Uncreated Element Is Only the Most Fundamental Substrate|the reading that Joseph Smith's "uncreated elements" denote only the most fundamental physical substrate]] — a "quantum soup" below the threshold of observation.

**Important caveat:** Moreland is an evangelical defender of *creatio ex nihilo*, not of LDS cosmology. His point concerns the *bare logical possibility* of a pre-Big-Bang state (against scientism's overreach), and does not constitute an endorsement of eternal, uncreated matter. The video's appropriation is a charitable borrowing of his concession, not a representation of his view of creation.
