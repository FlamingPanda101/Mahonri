# R.H. Charles

Robert Henry Charles (1855–1931) was an Irish biblical scholar, theologian, and translator. He served as Archdeacon of Westminster and was a fellow of the British Academy. He is best known for his work on Jewish apocalyptic and pseudepigraphal literature.

## 1 Enoch Translation (1906)

Charles's English translation of 1 Enoch, originally published in 1906, was the standard scholarly translation for nearly a century. Dan McClellan notes that "most people today who go to a bookstore or go online to try and find a version of the Book of Enoch are going to find R.H. Charles's translation." [^1]

However, McClellan cautions that this translation is now dated. Most cheap editions of 1 Enoch available commercially "are from the 19th century or are garbage." [^2] For contemporary scholarship, he recommends the Hermeneia translation by [[George Nickelsburg]] and [[James VanderKam]] instead.

## The Apocrypha and Pseudepigrapha of the Old Testament (1913)

Charles edited the monumental two-volume collection *The Apocrypha and Pseudepigrapha of the Old Testament* (Oxford, 1913), which included his translation of 1 Enoch along with many other pseudepigraphal texts. This edition remained the standard English-language collection of Jewish pseudepigrapha until James Charlesworth's *Old Testament Pseudepigrapha* (1983–1985).

## Legacy

Charles's work was foundational in bringing pseudepigraphal literature — including 1 Enoch — into mainstream English-language biblical scholarship. While his translations are now superseded by more recent critical editions incorporating the Qumran discoveries, his role in the [[Book of Enoch - Western Rediscovery 17th-19th Centuries|Western rediscovery of 1 Enoch]] is significant.
