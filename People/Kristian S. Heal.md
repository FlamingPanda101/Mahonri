# Kristian S. Heal

**Role:** Latter-day Saint scholar of early Christianity and Syriac studies; faculty at Brigham Young University's Neal A. Maxwell Institute for Religious Scholarship.
**Field:** Early Christian studies, Syriac literature, patristic exegesis and homiletics.

## Contribution to this volume
Heal authored chapter 1, "Preaching Christ: Scripture, Sermons, and Practical Exegesis," surveying early Christian preaching from its synagogue origins through the great patristic preachers. His Syriac expertise is on display in his treatment of [[Jacob of Serugh]], [[Isaac the Teacher]], and the Syriac lectionary tradition. He argues that the surviving corpus of "several thousand sermons" is "the surest guide to what the earliest Christians were taught and believed," and that early Christian preaching consistently "reoriented all scripture towards Christ." [^1]

He has published on Syriac biblical interpretation (e.g., "Joseph as a Type of Christ in the Syriac Tradition," *BYU Studies Quarterly* 41.1) and co-authored "Early Christian Biblical Interpretation" for *The Bible and the Latter-day Saint Tradition*. [^2]
