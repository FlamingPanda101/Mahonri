# Thomas Paine

**Era:** 1737–1809
**Tradition:** Deist (wrote *The Age of Reason* against Christianity)
**Role:** Revolutionary pamphleteer (*Common Sense*)

## Bio

Thomas Paine (mis-rendered "Payne" in the transcript) wrote *Common Sense*, credited in the dialogue with convincing the American colonists to pursue revolution rather than mere parliamentary representation. He "writes thoroughly against Christianity," lived in exile, and died nearly alone with his bones later lost.[^1]

## Topics

### Christian Language Despite Anti-Christianity
[[Glenn Scrivener]] notes Paine summarized his philosophy as "to act justly, love mercy, and work for human flourishing" — the first half lifted from [[Micah 6.8|Micah 6:8]], with a "humanistic gloss" replacing "walk humbly with thy God." Scrivener uses this as evidence even avowed enemies of Christianity breathed its air. [[Alex OConnor|Alex O'Connor]] uses Paine as evidence the most important founder "saw himself as working contra" Christianity. See [[Secularized Founders Used Christian Language]].[^1]
