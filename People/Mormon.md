# Mormon

**Role:** Nephite prophet, military commander, and abridging editor of the record
**Era:** Book of Mormon (4th century CE in the narrative)
**Tradition:** Book of Mormon

## Bio

Mormon is the prophet-historian after whom the Book of Mormon is named. Living in the final, war-torn era of Nephite civilization, he led Nephite armies and abridged the large plates of Nephi into the record that bears his name, adding his own editorial commentary throughout. He delivered the completed plates to his son [[Moroni]] (the later angelic messenger), who finished and buried them.

## Relevance to the vault

Mormon functions as the editorial voice that frames the Book of Mormon's prophecies and warnings. The vault invokes his book (alongside Ether) for the LDS reading that declining mainline Christian belief fulfills the prophesied "awful situation" of apostasy — a "therapeutic deism" of corrupted Christianity. As the named compiler, he is the author behind much of the text used in the vault's prophecy and Book-of-Mormon-authorship discussions.
