# Donald McKim

Reformed (Presbyterian) theologian and church historian, author of *Theological Turning Points: Major Issues in Christian Thought* — a book Day read on his mission and uses to structure the Arian controversy. Day calls the Christology question ("who is Jesus?") "turning point number one."

## On Arius and the Nicene crisis
McKim supplies the famous tavern poem mocking [[Arius]] ("God begat him; and before he was begotten, he was not") and the historical detail that Arians and their opponents were killed during the controversy. Day uses McKim to argue that for the average Christian these were "highbrow" distinctions few cared about, and that the councils proceeded by debate, crisis, and decision rather than prayer or revelation. See [[Heresy Was Decided by Votes Not Demonstrable Truth]], [[Nicene and Athanasian Creeds Apply Labels Not Explanations]].
