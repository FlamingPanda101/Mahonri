# George A. Smith

George A. Smith (1817–1875) was an early Latter-day Saint apostle, cousin of Joseph Smith, and later a counselor in the First Presidency under Brigham Young. (Not to be confused with his grandson George Albert Smith, the eighth Church president.)

## On Islam
In a sermon recorded in volume 3 of the *Journal of Discourses*, George A. Smith spoke at length and unusually positively about "Muhammadanism" (the period term for Islam), telling the young people he had been reading a book on the subject and found it "really, really interesting." [[Parley P. Pratt]] then rose and continued on the same theme. [[Daniel C. Peterson|Daniel Peterson]] cites these back-to-back 1850s Salt Lake City sermons as evidence that early LDS leaders honored Muhammad as inspired rather than using him as a smear.[^1] See [[Early LDS Leaders Praised Muhammad as Inspired]].
