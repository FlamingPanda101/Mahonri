# Goliath

**Goliath of Gath** is the Philistine champion whom the shepherd **[[David]]** kills with a sling-stone and then beheads **with Goliath's own sword** (1 Samuel 17). The episode is the paradigm of the unlikely deliverer who founds a new dynasty by felling the enemy's giant.

## Bio / Significance
The narrative supplies the template later readers map onto [[Nephi]] and [[Laban]]. A textual crux at 2 Samuel 21:19 credits the killing of "Goliath the Gittite" to Elhanan, generating debate over the original tradition; this is treated separately in the vault ([[Dan McClellan - David Didnt Kill Goliath]]).

## Contribution
In the [[Ward Radio - The Sword of Laban Was the Sword of Joshua|Don Bradley interview]], Goliath anchors the David-and-Goliath parallel: Bradley cites Ben McGuire's argument that roughly a dozen elements of the David/Goliath story recur in the same order in [[Nephi]]'s slaying of [[Laban]] — Nephi as "the new David," Laban as "the new Goliath," each beheaded with his own sword, the victor inheriting the blade. See [[Nephi and Laban Reenact David and Goliath]].
