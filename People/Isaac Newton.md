# Isaac Newton

**Era:** 1643–1727 (17th–18th century)
**Tradition:** Scientific revolution / Enlightenment
**Role:** Physicist, mathematician

## Bio

English physicist and mathematician, author of the *Principia*.

## Role in the Argument

Newton appears in Dave's example of the Enlightenment's **abandonment of final causes**: when Newton announced universal gravitation — objects acting on each other "at a distance" — critics objected that he had not supplied an efficient or material mechanism. Dave offers this (tentatively) as an illustration of explanation without teleology, before the conversation pivots to [[Charles Darwin|Darwin]] vs. [[Jean-Baptiste Lamarck|Lamarck]] as the cleaner example. See [[The Constitution Is an Enlightenment Document]].[^1]
