# Alex O'Connor

**Full Name:** Alex J. O'Connor
**Online Alias:** CosmicSkeptic
**Era:** Contemporary (1999–present)
**Tradition:** Atheist (studied philosophy at Oxford)
**Role:** YouTuber, public philosopher, debater

## Bio

Alex O'Connor (known online as CosmicSkeptic) is a British YouTuber and public
philosopher who studied philosophy and theology at St John's College, Oxford.
His channel, with over 1 million subscribers, features discussions on
philosophy, religion, and atheism. He has debated prominent Christian
apologists including [[William Lane Craig]], [[Trent Horn]], and [[Frank
Turek]].

## Topics

### Recognition of LDS Theological Consistency
O'Connor is cited in the video for his notable acknowledgment that LDS theology
is more internally consistent than mainstream Christianity:

> "Alex O'Connor teases all the credal Christians and says, 'You guys make fun
> of the Mormons, but they make way more sense than you guys.'"[^1]

Carroll further notes that O'Connor was asked "Which Christian would you be?"
and replied, "I'd be a Mormon."[^1] This is a significant concession from a
prominent atheist philosopher: LDS theology, while still rejected, is
acknowledged as more logically coherent than creedal Christianity.

### Emotivism and Morality
O'Connor identifies as an "emotivist" regarding morality — when he says
something is "wrong," he means merely "I don't like that" rather than asserting
an objective moral truth. Carroll cites this as the consistent atheist
position.[^2]

### Personal Experience Requirement
O'Connor has stated that what it would take for him to believe is "a personal
experience with God" — essentially a Road to Damascus conversion experience.
Carroll and Butler note this as a sincere admission that evidence alone may not
suffice without personal revelation.[^3]
