# Hayden Carroll

**Full Name:** Hayden Carroll
**Era:** Contemporary (2020s)
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint (LDS)
**Role:** Apologist, campus debater, YouTuber

## Bio

Hayden Carroll is an LDS apologist who runs the YouTube channel "Let's Talk with
Hayden Carroll." His niche, self-described, is to "do what we see Steven
Crowder and... Charlie Kirk" do — setting up a table on college campuses
(primarily Arizona State University) with provocative banners to engage students
in unscripted theological conversations.[^1]

His banners include "Atheism is Unreasonable and Stupid" and "The Trinity is
Evil." Despite the provocative wording, Carroll reports that "99% of people, no
matter what banner I have up, are pretty nice. They sit down, they want to have
a conversation or they're curious about it."[^2]

Carroll's apologetic style combines:
- Fine-tuning / teleological arguments from cosmology
- Moral arguments against atheism (quoting Dawkins, Rosenberg, Nietzsche)
- LDS distinctive doctrines (co-eternality, pre-existence, eternal law) as
  unique solutions to philosophical problems
- Street-epistemology style Socratic questioning

He appeared on the [[Stick of Joseph]] podcast with David Butler in February
2026 to discuss his conversations with atheists on campus.

## Topics

### Fine-Tuning / Teleological Argument
Carroll presents the fine-tuning argument as his foundational apologetic,
arguing that physical constants and initial conditions of the universe make
design overwhelmingly more probable than chance. He notes that Alma 30:44
presents a teleological argument centuries before Aquinas.[^3] See
[[Fine-Tuning and the Teleological Argument from Alma 30]].

### Atheism's Implications
Carroll argues that consistent atheism entails four unpalatable implications:
no free will, no objective morality, no genuine consciousness, and nihilism. He
quotes prominent atheists ([[Sam Harris]], [[Richard Dawkins]], [[Daniel
Dennett]], [[Alex Rosenberg]]) who concede these implications.[^4] See
[[Atheism's Four Bad Implications]].

### Free Will and Co-Eternality
Carroll argues that LDS doctrine of co-eternality — that human intelligences
are uncreated and co-eternal with God — uniquely resolves the determinism
problem that plagues both atheism and mainstream Christianity's doctrine of
creation *ex nihilo*.[^5] See [[Free Will - Atheist Determinism vs LDS
Co-Eternality]].

### Pre-Existence and John 9
Carroll points to John 9:1-3, where the apostles ask Jesus whether a man born
blind sinned (implying pre-mortal existence), as evidence that early Christians
may have believed in pre-existence.[^6] See [[Pre-Existence Implied by John
9]].
