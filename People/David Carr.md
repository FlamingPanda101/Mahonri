# David Carr

**Who:** Professor of Old Testament at Union Theological Seminary (New York). Author of *Writing on the Tablets of the Heart: Origins of Scripture and Literature* (Oxford University Press, 2005), a major work on the educational function of ancient texts including the Hebrew Bible.

**Role:** Carr argues that the Bible is fundamentally an educational text — a curriculum for training elites in ancient Israel and the broader Hellenistic world. [[Jacob Wright]] builds on Carr's thesis but pushes back, arguing the pedagogical impulse emerged from the margins (a defeated people) rather than from Hellenistic elite training.
