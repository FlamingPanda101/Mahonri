# W. W. Phelps

William Wines Phelps, an early Latter-day Saint, editor, hymn-writer, and scribe to [[Joseph Smith]]. He worked alongside [[Oliver Cowdery]] on the Egyptian materials in Kirtland.

## Role in the Egyptian Papers
[[Don Bradley]] names Phelps among the scribes who worked with Joseph Smith on the papyri and the [[Grammar and Alphabet of the Egyptian Language]], the document Bradley uses to recover how the curse in [[Abraham 1.21-27|Abraham 1]] was understood. See [[Grammar and Alphabet of the Egyptian Language]].

## The Pure-Language Cipher (Schryver thesis)
[[William Schryver]] argues Phelps was the driving force behind the "Egyptian Alphabet" project, which Schryver reads as an idiographic "pure language" **cipher** rather than a translation tool: Phelps wrote a six-character cipher "specimen of some of the pure language" to his wife Sally in May 1835 — before the papyri arrived — and reused those characters (some identical to Masonic ciphers) in the Egyptian Alphabet. See [[The Kirtland Egyptian Papers Are a Cipher Project Not a Translation Tool]].
