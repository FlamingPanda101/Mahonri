# Christian Mario

**Role:** Eastern Orthodox apologist and debater (online / X Spaces)
**Era:** Contemporary (active 2020s)
**Tradition:** Eastern Orthodox Christianity

## Bio

Christian Mario is an Eastern Orthodox apologist who engages in cross-confessional debates defending creedal and Orthodox Christianity. In an X (Twitter) Space he debated Latter-day Saint apologist [[Jacob Hansen]] (Thoughtful Faith) on epistemology and the nature of God, prosecuting the classical-theist and Palamite case against the LDS *organizer*-God. He frames Orthodoxy as resting not on Cartesian certainty but on "historical revelation" — beginning with the person of Christ and defined "through the ecumenical councils" (Nicaea) — and grounds knowledge of God in "participation in God's uncreated energies" rather than "abstract reason." [^1]

## Epistemology

Against Hansen's appeal to personal supernatural experience, Mario argues that "calling something supernatural truth without a public revelation" is "emotionalism that's made to seem theological," since any mystic can claim private certainty. He presses Hansen's "no perfect rational certainty" thesis as a self-refuting **performative contradiction** ("a rationally certain statement about certainty") and offers the patristic counter-definition of knowledge as "the illumination of the *nous*" through "synergy with divine grace." ## Metaphysics: The Orthodox Case Against the Organizer-God

Mario's central argument is that whatever reality the LDS God "inhabits" is by definition more fundamental than God, so "the most fundamental reality just is God" and the LDS deity is "demoted to a creature." He drives toward the charges that LDS cosmology is **eternal dualism** (co-eternal light/darkness, evil with positive being) and that **two self-existent realities cannot coexist**. His one constructive move is the **essence/energies distinction** as Orthodoxy's escape from pantheism. See:
