# Craig Keener

**Full name:** Craig S. Keener (b. 1960)
**Role:** New Testament scholar
**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** Protestant (evangelical)

## Bio

Craig Keener is a professor of New Testament at Asbury Theological Seminary and one of the most prolific biblical scholars of his generation, known for massive socio-historical commentaries on Acts, Matthew, and John. His two-volume *Miracles: The Credibility of the New Testament Accounts* (2011) compiles hundreds of contemporary eyewitness reports of healings and other miraculous claims from around the world.

## Relevance to the vault

Keener's research is invoked in the vault's "message-bearing miracles" trilemma: a Catholic apologist grants that generic faith-healings happen through anyone — Catholics, Protestants, even non-Christians — citing that most of Keener's catalogued cases are healings carrying no denominational message. This concedes that benevolent miracles do not validate a particular church, sharpening the question of which miracles actually authenticate a divine messenger.
