# Peter Shirts

A nineteenth-century Latter-day Saint settler who lived in the remote regions of southern Utah and, by [[Keystone]]'s account, "wasn't much of a people person." [[Keystone]] uses him to illustrate that [[Brigham Young]] sometimes *denied* requests for plural marriage rather than rubber-stamping them.

In response to Shirts's petition to take another wife, Brigham Young replied that "until you are willing to live where a family can be safe and have a reasonable opportunity for social enjoyment and improvement, I am of the opinion that it will be altogether best for you to continue to lead the life of a hermit. For I know of no woman worth a [whit] who would be willing to agree with your wild, unsocial ways for any length of time."[^1] See [[Plural Marriage Proposal Required a Chain of Consent]].
