# Sylvester Graham

A Presbyterian minister and leading figure of the 1830s American health-reform movement; inventor of the Graham cracker. Graham preached a radical dietary regimen (the "Graham diet") that condemned stimulants — coffee, tea, meat, spices, condiments, alcohol — as overstimulating to body and morals, and advocated a largely vegetarian diet of whole grains, fruits, and vegetables.

## Significance in the vault
[[Scott Woodward|Woodward]] and [[Casey Paul Griffiths|Griffiths]] cite Graham as evidence that the [[Word of Wisdom]] ([[D&C 89]]) was *not* received in a cultural vacuum: Graham's ideas circulated widely in 1830s Ohio (notably at Oberlin College, with which several Church members were affiliated), and "most of the topics covered in this revelation directly reflect the prevailing health concerns and recommendations of the day." This anchors the argument that Section 89 is responsive to its 1833 context. See [[The Word of Wisdom Was Not Received in a Cultural Vacuum]].
