# Alister McGrath

Oxford historical and systematic theologian, author of standard textbooks and anthologies in Christian theology (*Christian Theology: An Introduction*; *Historical Theology*; *The Christian Theology Reader*).

## Cited in *Ancient Christians*
Both Cecilia Peek and Daniel Becerra lean on McGrath's reference works for the historical-theology framing of the atonement and the Pelagian controversy. McGrath supplies the key contrasts: Pelagian grace as "external and passive" (natural faculties + enlightenment) versus Augustinian grace as "internal and active" — "the real, redeeming presence of God in Christ within us"; and the formula "since perfection is possible it is obligatory." [^1] His translated source excerpts (e.g., Pelagius's *Letter to Demetrias*, Augustine's *Of Nature and Grace*) are quoted throughout.
