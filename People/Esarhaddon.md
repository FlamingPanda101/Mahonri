# Esarhaddon

**Who:** King of Assyria (reigned c. 681–669 BCE, ~12 years), son of [[Sennacherib]] and father of [[Ashurbanipal]]. A ruler of the Neo-Assyrian Empire at the height of its imperial reach.

**Why he matters here:** Esarhaddon is associated with the **Vassal Treaty of Esarhaddon** (the Assyrian *adê* or loyalty oath, 672 BCE), which [[Dan McClellan]] presents as the literary template behind the book of [[Deuteronomy]]. He did not invent the vassal-treaty form but "mastered" it — the preamble titling him "King of the World, King of Assyria," the long roster of divine witnesses, the exclusive-loyalty stipulations, and the elaborate blessing-and-curse sanctions are the structure critical scholars see reused in Deuteronomy's covenant. In the analogy, "Esarhaddon is God, and the Israelites are whatever vassal nation Esarhaddon has just whooped up on."
