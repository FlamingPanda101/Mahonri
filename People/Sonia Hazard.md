# Sonia Hazard

**Era:** Contemporary (2020s)
**Tradition:** Academic / secular religious studies
**Role:** Scholar of American religious history

## Bio

Sonia Hazard is an academic scholar of American religion who proposed, in a
published article, a naturalistic account of the Book of Mormon gold plates:
rather than fabricating them, [[Joseph Smith]] may have *found* real metal
objects — specifically **stereotype printing plates** — near the Hill Cumorah,
and built a religious narrative around them.[^1]

## The Printing-Plate Theory

Hazard's "third and final scenario" (which she calls "the most straightforward
though less probable") suggests stereotype or copper printing plates were lost in
transit along the postal road that hugged the western base of the hill between
Canandaigua and Palmyra, and that Smith found them "much as he said he did."[^2]
The Ward Radio panel rebuts this on physical grounds — printing plates are far
too thick, lack binding rings and punch holes, and clank rather than rustle. See
[[Sonia Hazards Printing-Plate Theory Fails the Physical Description]].
