# Mark (Holy Rebellion)

Mark is the guest historian interviewed on this episode of *The Holy Rebellion*. He is introduced as a neighbor and fellow ward member of host [[Christian (Holy Rebellion)|Christian]] who "has been working on the Joseph Smith Papers for 24 years."[^1] He states, "I'm a historian. I have a PhD in history. I've been studying Joseph Smith professionally for a long time."[^2] His surname is not given in the source, so he is recorded here only as "Mark."

He cites and evaluates the historiography throughout — [[Dan Vogel]]'s 1994 treasure-locations article, [[D. Michael Quinn]]'s *Early Mormonism and the Magic World View*, and [[Johannes Dillinger]]'s treasure-hunting history — and recommends the Joseph Smith Papers legal series, noting his friend David Grua headed its legal papers.[^3]

## Method and Argument

Mark's recurring move is to apply standard historical method (corroboration, bias evaluation, contemporaneity, source criticism) and to refuse the pejorative term "magic" in favor of "folk belief/practice."[^4] He affirms that treasure seeking, seer stones, and divining rods are well documented for Joseph, while most sensational add-ons (magic circles, animal sacrifice, necromancy) are not credibly attested.[^5]
