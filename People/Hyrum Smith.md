# Hyrum Smith

**Full Name:** Hyrum Smith
**Era:** 1800–1844
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint (LDS)
**Role:** Brother of [[Joseph Smith]]; Church Patriarch; martyr

## Bio

Hyrum Smith was the older brother of the prophet [[Joseph Smith]] and a witness and defender of the [[Book of Mormon]] from its earliest days. He died alongside Joseph in the 1844 mob attack at Carthage Jail, and Latter-day Saints number the two brothers together as testators who sealed their witness to the Book of Mormon with their lives.

## Topics

### The Martyrs as Testators of the Book of Mormon
In a [[Jeffrey R. Holland]] general-conference address replayed by [[Hayden Carroll]], Hyrum is recounted reading aloud from the twelfth chapter of the book of Ether ([[Book of Mormon]]) as the brothers set out for Carthage — "Thou hast been faithful, wherefore thou shalt be made strong, even unto the sitting down in the place which I have prepared in the mansions of my Father" — and turning down the corner of the page as part of "the everlasting testimony for which these two brothers were about to die." Holland's argument: men hours from death would not stake their lives, honor, and hope of salvation on a book they had "fictitiously created out of whole cloth."[^1] This anecdote anchors the closing testimony of the source and supports [[Confirmed Anachronisms Are Reverse-Anachronisms Joseph Could Not Have Known]] and the wider [[The Book of Mormon Keystone Argument|keystone case]].

### Polygamy — Taught It and Testified To It
[[Brian Hales]] notes Hyrum learned plural marriage from Joseph, taught it to others (e.g. [[Ebenezer Robinson]], who recorded Hyrum saying he heard "the voice of the Lord give him the revelation on polygamy... to his brother Joseph"), and gave some "fairly full-throated" affirmations — though Hyrum's few denial-type statements fit the same confidential-practice model as Joseph's. See [[Joseph Smith Never Issued a Blanket Denial of Polygamy]] and [[The RLDS Church Were the Original Polygamy Deniers and Recanted]].[^2]
