# D. Jill Kirby

**D. Jill Kirby** is the author of "Living in the Afterlife: Heaven, Hell, and Places Between," chapter 12 of *Ancient Christians: An Introduction for Latter-day Saints* (Maxwell Institute, 2022).

## Synthesizing Patristic Views of the Afterlife
Kirby surveys how early Christians used **scripture and reason** (faith and reason) to answer questions scripture left open: the descent of Christ to the dead, purgation, hell, and heaven. She traces a shift from Christ-as-preacher (Ignatius, Clement) to Christ-as-conqueror-of-hell (the *Gospel of Nicodemus*), and shows [[Augustine]] setting the Western "boundary of salvation" at death. She presents hell through the *Apocalypses of Peter and Paul*, and heaven through the mystical poetry of Syriac Christianity ([[Aphrahat]], [[Ephrem the Syrian]]). Her recurring LDS application: the early Christians' *lack* of resolution reminds us to "wait upon the Lord" and be "anxiously engaged" in this life. See [[Most Early Christians Believed in an Intermediate State Between Death and Resurrection]] [^1].
