# Mark J. Cares

Evangelical countercult author and founder of **Truth and Love Ministry** (a 501(c)(3) based in Nampa, Idaho; now retired). His book *Speaking the Truth in Love to Mormons: Bridges Not Barriers* (2nd ed. 1997) and his free online course teach Protestants how to witness to Latter-day Saints via **"Five Pillars"**: see Mormons as victims not enemies; treat them with love and respect; focus on "Mormon stress points"; speak the "Mormon language"; witness Christ rather than debate Mormonism.

## On Ward Radio's Reaction
On [[Ward Radio - We Bought Every Anti-Mormon Book and React]], the hosts make Cares the central case study of the episode. They **credit** his approach for centering the gospel of Christ over the usual catalog of "racist quotes," and grant that his glossary of LDS terminology ("Mormonese") is often genuinely useful. But they argue his **first pillar — "see Mormons as victims" — is inherently condescending**, presupposing that the only saveable Mormons are those too ignorant to have read their own scriptures (see [[See Mormons as Victims Is Condescending Not Loving]]). They also note he makes a self-defeating concession: his 1997 claim that "nothing of substance has changed" in LDS teaching is used to refute the modern "rebranding" charge (see [[Dated Anti-Mormon Books Refute the Rebranding Charge]]). His charge that Latter-day Saints are "saved by works" is answered by pointing out the same would indict Catholics and the Orthodox (see [[LDS Salvation Is by Grace Not Earned by Works]]).
