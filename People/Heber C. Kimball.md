# Heber C. Kimball

**Who:** Heber Chase Kimball (1801–1868), early Latter-day Saint leader and one of the original Twelve Apostles called by [[Joseph Smith]] in 1835. He served as First Counselor to [[Brigham Young]] in the First Presidency from 1847 until his death. He was the father of [[Helen Mar Kimball]].

**Role:** Kimball is a significant figure in debates about early LDS polygamy because he personally requested that his daughter Helen Mar be sealed to Joseph Smith — an act that LDS apologetics frames as evidence that the sealing was dynastic (binding the Smith and Kimball families) rather than predatory.

## His Daughter's Sealing to Joseph Smith
Kimball — a senior apostle — sought the sealing of his 14-year-old daughter [[Helen Mar Kimball]] to [[Joseph Smith]] for dynastic reasons: to bind the Smith and Kimball families together. According to the apologetic argument, this was not a unilateral action by Smith but involved the family's active participation and approval. See [[Joseph Smith and Helen Mar Kimball Sealing]].

## Witness to the Succession and to Brigham's Calling
As [[Brigham Young]]'s closest friend and right-hand man (they joined the Church together), Heber C. Kimball figures in [[Daniel C. Peterson]]'s account of the 1844 succession. Peterson notes that Kimball was one of (at least) two witnesses — with Joseph Young — who heard [[Joseph Smith]] say of Brigham, at their first meeting, "That man will succeed me in the leadership of the Church."[^1] Peterson even speculates Kimball might have been able to lead the westward trek had Brigham failed. See [[Brigham Young Saved the Church Through the Keys]].
