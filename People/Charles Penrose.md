# Charles Penrose

LDS apostle and member of the First Presidency under [[Joseph F. Smith]].
Together with Joseph F. Smith, he was responsible for the 1912 suppression
of the [[King Follett Discourse]] from the *History of the Church*.

## The Spirit Birth Dispute

Penrose "fundamentally disagreed with BH Roberts's interpretation of spirit
birth." Roberts argued that God *organized* intelligences into spirits (a
two-step process). Penrose believed "that the spirits themselves were eternal
with God" — that individual personhood/selfhood is co-eternal, not formed by
God.[^1]

This theological disagreement was a key motivation for Penrose's willingness to
remove Roberts's version of the discourse from the official history.[^1]

## Role in Suppression

As part of the First Presidency with Joseph F. Smith, Penrose approved the
removal of pages 302–317 (containing the King Follett Discourse) from the
*History of the Church* typescript while [[BH Roberts]] was away on a mission.[^2]
