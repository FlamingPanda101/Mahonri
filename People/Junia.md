# Junia

First-century Christian, named with her husband Andronicus in Romans 16:7 as "prominent among the apostles" and as a convert to Christ before Paul.

## A woman called apostle
Paul applies *episēmos* ("prominent") to rank Junia *among* the apostles, not merely as known to them. [[Ariel Bybee Laughton]] argues this may mean Junia, with Andronicus, "had manifest the 'signs, wonders, and mighty works' Paul considered the markers of his own apostolic office" — that they were "commissioned agents of Jesus Christ who held a special calling of leadership and authority." She and Andronicus had been imprisoned with Paul. See [[Junia Is a Woman Called Prominent Among the Apostles]] and [[Romans 16.1-7|Romans 16:7]]. [^1]

## Erasure by translation
Many translations rendered her name as the masculine "Junias" "simply on the assumption that she could not have been a woman if she was called 'apostle' by Paul." Laughton cites Hope Stephenson's review of the evidence ("Junia, Woman and Apostle"). [^2]
