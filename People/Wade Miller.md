# Wade Miller

**Era:** 20th–21st century (d. recent)
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint (LDS)
**Role:** Geologist, vertebrate paleontologist

## Bio

Wade Miller was a geologist and vertebrate paleontologist (emeritus, BYU). He was the
lead author of the *Texas Journal of Science* study reporting horse bones from central
Mexico in stratigraphic layers whose associated carbon material dated to Book of Mormon
times.[^1]

## Pre-Columbian Horse Remains

Miller's team could not directly date the deteriorated horse-bone collagen but dated
carbon material in the same clean stratigraphic layers to spans including ~1600 BC,
1500–400 BC, 500–400 BC, and ~AD 70–225. Rappleye spoke with Miller before his death;
Miller maintained the stratigraphy was clean with no evidence of disruption. See
[[Horses Are Trending Not Settled as an Anachronism]].[^2]
