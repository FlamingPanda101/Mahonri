# David Brakke

Historian of late-antique Christianity, especially Egyptian monasticism, Gnosticism, and the career of [[Athanasius]]. Author of *Athanasius and the Politics of Asceticism* (1995) and the influential article "Canon Formation and Social Conflict in Fourth Century Egypt."

## Cited in *Ancient Christians*
[[Thomas A. Wayment]] draws on Brakke for the thesis that the New Testament canon emerged from **social conflict** — Athanasius pushing a distinctly Athanasian canon against Melitians and charismatic teachers. Brakke supplies the framing that the conflict crystallized "when Arius's teachings appeared to Alexander to transgress the limits of acceptable diversity," and that the liberal Alexandrian acceptance of a broad canon is visible in the eclectic Nag Hammadi tractates. See [[Canon Formation Was a Power Struggle Over Authority]]. [^1]
