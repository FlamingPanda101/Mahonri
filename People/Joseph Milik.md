# Joseph Milik

Jozef Tadeusz Milik (1922–2006) was a Polish biblical scholar and a member of the original international team of editors assembled to publish the Dead Sea Scrolls. He was a Catholic priest and one of the most prolific early editors of the Qumran manuscripts.

## Enoch Fragments at Qumran

Milik's most significant contribution to Enoch studies is the 1976 volume *The Books of Enoch: Aramaic Fragments of Qumran Cave 4*, published in the *Discoveries in the Judean Desert* (DJD) series. This volume contains the critical edition of all the Aramaic Enoch fragments discovered at Qumran.

Dan McClellan notes: "You can actually see all of those [Enoch] fragments if you go pick up a copy of... a 1976 volume edited by Joseph Milick [sic]... that contains all of the Enoch fragments." [^1]

## Significance for Enoch Studies

The Qumran Enoch fragments, published by Milik, confirmed that:
1. 1 Enoch was originally composed in **Aramaic** (not Hebrew), as scholars had hypothesized
2. The text circulated widely in Second Temple Judaism, well before Christianity
3. Multiple recensions of the text existed simultaneously at Qumran
4. 1 Enoch was among the most copied texts in the Qumran library — 11 fragmentary manuscripts, outnumbered only by Genesis, Deuteronomy, Isaiah, and Psalms

These Aramaic fragments, along with scattered Greek fragments and the much later Ethiopic manuscripts, provide the textual basis for contemporary critical editions.
