# Thomas Bullock

One of four contemporaneous scribes who recorded the [[King Follett Discourse]] in
April 1844. Bullock wrote in "his own personal version of shorthand," providing
one of the key source documents for reconstructing [[Joseph Smith]]'s most famous
sermon.[^1]

His shorthand account, combined with [[William Clayton]]'s longhand version, formed
the primary basis for the polished account published in the *Times and Seasons* in
August 1844.[^2]
