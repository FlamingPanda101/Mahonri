# James E. Talmage

**Role:** Latter-day Saint apostle, geologist, and author
**Era:** 19th–20th century (1862–1933)
**Tradition:** LDS

## Bio

James Edward Talmage was a Latter-day Saint scientist and apostle, trained as a geologist, who served as president of the University of Utah and authored doctrinal classics *Jesus the Christ* and *The Articles of Faith*.

## Relevance to the vault

Talmage is cited in [[Ward Radio - Fake Book of Mormon Artifacts]] as a model of faithful skepticism: applying his scientific training, he obtained Michigan Relic specimens, tested them in his Deseret Museum laboratory (c. 1911), observed the freshness of saw and tool marks, and concluded the artifacts were **frauds**. The hosts note the irony — and the integrity — that two of the best debunkers of forged "Book of Mormon" artifacts (Talmage and the LDS archaeologist Richard Stamps) were themselves Latter-day Saints. See [[The Michigan Relics Are 100 Percent Forgeries]] and [[Defending Faith Requires Ruling Out the Fakes]].
