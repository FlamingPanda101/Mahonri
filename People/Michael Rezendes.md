# Michael Rezendes

Investigative journalist (rendered "Resendez" in the auto-transcript) who was part of the Boston Globe *Spotlight* team that broke the 2002 Catholic clergy-abuse scandal and won a Pulitzer Prize. He later reported the LDS [[Paul Adams]] case for the Associated Press under the headline "Seven years of sex abuse: How Mormon officials let it happen," a piece the AP named a top investigative project and gave its "Best of AP" award.

## Contested framing of the Adams case
Ward Radio Part 6 argues Rezendes imported the *Spotlight* template onto a structurally different case: he allegedly "upgraded" Adams's one confessed incident to "seven years of rape," described the excommunication as "keeping the matter secret," recast the abuse helpline as a tool to "divert abuse allegations away from law enforcement," and "made it President Nelson's fault." The episode also charges that he "happened to miss the [[Timothy Johnson]] case," in which the church was sued *for reporting* abuse. See [[The Spotlight Analogy Equates a Celibate Cardinal With a Volunteer Bishop]] and [[The Reporting Catch-22 and the Johnson Case]]. [^1][^2]
