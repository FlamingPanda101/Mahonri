# Britney Chapman Nash

Latter-day Saint historian specializing in LDS women's history, working for the Church History Department. Author of *Let's Talk About Polygamy*, a book built around the personal stories and first-person writings of women — and men — who actually lived plural marriage, rather than around statistics or third-party interpretation.[^1]

She is the interview subject of the [[Keystone - Polygamy Expert What Changed My Mind|Keystone polygamy episode]], in which she recounts how her own view of plural marriage shifted — moving from feeling obligated to defend a practice she did not understand toward peace, and changing especially "where men were concerned" after she studied men's lived experience as the neglected "other half of the story."[^2]

Note: distinct from [[Britney Ward Radio|Britney "the Shadow"]], a separate Latter-day Saint commentator on Ward Radio.
