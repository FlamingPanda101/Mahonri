# Joshua Sijuwade

**Role:** Analytic philosopher and theologian (philosophy of religion / analytic theology)
**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** Christian

## Bio

Dr. Joshua Sijuwade is an analytic philosopher and theologian who publishes on models of God, divine ontology, and the Trinity. Hansen calls him "a brilliant scholar" he "really respects," and notes that Avery (God Logic) has debated him. Hansen reads two Sijuwade passages approvingly in the debate-prep conversation.

## On Ancient Jewish Monotheism as a Divine Spectrum

Hansen quotes Sijuwade arguing that Islam, claiming to restore Abrahamic monotheism, in fact *reverses* Judaism's core ontological claim: ancient Judaism acknowledged multiple divine beings under one supreme God, whereas Islam denies any other divine beings exist. In a second passage Sijuwade cites a roster of biblical scholars — Peter Hayman, Nathan MacDonald, Paula Fredriksen, Michael Heiser, Larry Hurtado, and Richard Bauckham — for the view that ancient Jewish monotheism affirmed Yahweh's unique *supremacy* (a qualitative concept) rather than denying other divine beings, with divinity conceived as a *spectrum* not a binary.
