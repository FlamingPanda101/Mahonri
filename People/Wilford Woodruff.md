# Wilford Woodruff

LDS apostle and fourth president of the LDS Church (1807–1898). He was one of
four contemporaneous scribes who recorded the [[King Follett Discourse]] — keeping
a record in his personal diary.[^1]

## Recording the Infant Exaltation Teaching

Woodruff's diary captured the most explicit statement of [[Infant Exaltation]]:

> Eternity is full of thrones upon which dwell thousands of children reigning on
> the thrones of glory, not one cubit added to their stature.[^2]

## Later Recollection and Revision

Decades after the sermon, Woodruff recalled it as "the most powerful spiritual
oration he had ever heard." When he dedicated the Salt Lake Temple (five decades
after the discourse), he cited the King Follett Discourse.[^3]

Park notes that Woodruff "would later claim that Joseph Smith developed this
theology after the sermon, saying that, well no, they'll eventually grow" —
a revision of the infant exaltation teaching that became "a key point of debate
with future interpreters."[^4]
