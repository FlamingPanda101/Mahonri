# Anti-Nephi-Lehi

Book of Mormon figure — the converted Lamanite king who renames his people the **Anti-Nephi-Lehies**, delivers the great pacifism speech in [[Alma 24.12-13|Alma 24]], and leads them to **bury their weapons** and refuse to take up arms even unto death ([[Alma 24.22|Alma 24:22]]). His son inherits the kingdom. The episode notes uncertainty over what the name "Anti-Nephi-Lehi" means.

## The pacifism speech and its chiasms
His speech is the text in which [[Jonah (Plain and Precious Publishing)|Jonah]] claims a [[Concentric Chiasmus in Alma 24|concentric double chiasmus]] — one on [[Stain Chiasmus in Alma 24|"stain"]] and one on [[Brethren Chiasmus in Alma 24|"brethren"]] — both pivoting on his line about not staining swords with the blood of brethren [^1].

## The five motif and buried weapons
His five-fold thanksgiving ([[Alma 24.7|Alma 24:7]] ff.) and the buried-weapons covenant feed the episode's [[The Five Motif in Alma 24|five motif]] and the speculative [[Hiawatha Belt Parallel - Buried Weapons Under a Tree|Hiawatha-belt parallel]] [^2].
