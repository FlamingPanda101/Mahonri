# Jeffrey Trumbower

**Jeffrey A. Trumbower** is a scholar of early Christianity whose monograph *Rescue for the Dead: The Posthumous Salvation of Non-Christians in Early Christianity* (Oxford, 2001) is the standard academic treatment of how early Christians sought salvation for the unbaptized and non-Christian dead.

## Vicarious Baptism and Posthumous Salvation
Catherine Gines Taylor relies on Trumbower for the reading that Paul in [[1 Corinthians 15.29|1 Corinthians 15:29]] "does not object to" baptism for the dead and that the passage "points to an actual rite for the benefit of a dead person." Trumbower documents the [[Marcion|Marcionite]] practice (reported by [[John Chrysostom]]) of a living proxy lying beneath the deceased to answer the baptismal questions and receive the water on their behalf — limited to those who "already had faith and were simply not yet baptized." D. Jill Kirby also cites him on [[Augustine]] setting death as the boundary of salvation. See [[1 Corinthians 15.29 Points to an Actual Rite of Baptism for the Dead]] and [[Marcionites Practiced Proxy Baptism for the Unbaptized Dead]] [^1].
