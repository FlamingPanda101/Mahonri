# Ambrosiaster

The conventional name ("pseudo-Ambrose") for an anonymous late-fourth-century Latin commentator on the Pauline epistles. Little is known of the author's life.

## Significance
Ambrosiaster is the crucial link in the chain that produced [[Augustine]]'s doctrine of [[Original Sin Was a Late Western Development|original sin]]. His commentary rested on a **mistranslation** of Romans 5:12: the Greek *eph hō* ("because" all have sinned) was rendered in Latin as *in quo* ("in whom"). Ambrosiaster glossed it: "In whom that is, in Adam all sinned … all sinned in Adam as a lump (*quasi in massa*). Once he was corrupted by sin, those he begat were all born under sin." This reading "provides the scriptural proof-text for Augustine's theology" of inherited guilt — and Augustine's "mass of clay … mass of sin" language directly echoes it.
