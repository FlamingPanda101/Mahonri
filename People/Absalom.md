# Absalom

Third son of King [[David]] who rebelled against his father and was killed when his head caught in the boughs of an oak as he rode beneath it (2 Samuel 18). David's lament — "O Absalom, my son, my son!" — is one of the Hebrew Bible's most quoted expressions of paternal grief.

## "My son, my son" and the Abraham parallel
[[Don Bradley]] notes that the doubled tender address "my son, my son" appears only twice in scripture: David over Absalom, and the Lord over [[Abraham]] in the Book of Abraham (the Lord stretching out his hand: "My son, my son"). He reads the verbal echo as marking the depth of God's paternal love in the dispensation-opening pattern. See [[Every Dispensation Opens With God Declaring Divine Sonship]].[^1]
