# Howard Zinn

**Era:** 20th–21st century (1922–2010)
**Role:** Historian, activist

## Bio
Howard Zinn was an American historian and political activist, best known for *A People's History of the United States* (1980), a hugely popular bottom-up narrative of American history written from an explicitly leftist standpoint.

## Cited For — The "Woke History" Foil
On Ward Radio's Fawn Brodie episode, [[Cardon Ellis]] uses Zinn as the modern icon of what he calls politicized, agenda-driven history — singling out Zinn's treatment of Christopher Columbus as (in his telling) "intentional ideological propaganda" rather than honest error, after his own deep dive into the Columbus primary sources. The hosts fold Zinn into a thesis they apply to [[Fawn Brodie]]: that the academic history establishment slants left and writes hostile, source-selective narratives — and that Latter-day Saints were the *first* targets of this method (Brodie, 1946) before it spread to figures like Jefferson and the Founders. See [[Anti-Mormon Pseudo-History Pioneered the Woke History Method]].[^1]
