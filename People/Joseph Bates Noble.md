# Joseph Bates Noble

**Role:** Early Latter-day Saint; performed the sealing of [[Louisa Beeman]] to [[Joseph Smith]]
**Era:** 1810–1900
**Tradition:** The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints

## Bio
Joseph Bates Noble performed (and is the source for the traditional date of) [[Joseph Smith]]'s sealing to his sister-in-law [[Louisa Beeman]]. In an 1869 affidavit used by [[Andrew Jenson]], he gave the date as April 5, 1841, and claimed to have performed the first plural marriage and fathered the first plural-marriage child of the dispensation.

## Significance in [[Don Bradley]]'s argument
Bradley treats Noble as an unreliable witness for the date: a descendant-biographer called him "terrible with dates" and "a big boaster"; his "first child of the dispensation" boast is demonstrably false ([[Heber C. Kimball]] and others had earlier plural children); under questioning in the Temple Lot case he gave a range from 1840 to 1842 and located the marriage at his own Nauvoo house, which was not built until after fall 1841. Crucially, April 5 is the date of *Noble's own* first plural marriage (1843), suggesting confusion. Discrediting the date is what lets Bradley re-order the wives so [[Zina Huntington Jacobs]] is first. See [[Joseph Smith Married Pregnant Women Refuting the Sexual-Motive Thesis]].
