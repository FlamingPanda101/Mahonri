# John Welch

**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint (LDS)
**Role:** Legal scholar; longtime editor of *BYU Studies*

## Bio
John W. ("Jack") Welch is a Latter-day Saint legal scholar and longtime editor of *BYU Studies*, best known for identifying **chiasmus** — the inverted-parallel literary structure of ancient Hebrew texts — in the Book of Mormon as a young missionary in 1967. The discovery became a cornerstone of arguments that the Book of Mormon reflects genuine ancient composition.

## Relevance to the vault
[[Mike Day]] credits Welch with finding the chiastic structure that, on Day's account, an unlearned Joseph Smith would have had to reproduce "out of your head" decades before such structures were academically described in the Book of Mormon — part of the case that the dictation feat exceeds Joseph's capacity. See [[Chiasmus as Evidence of Ancient Authorship in the Book of Mormon]] and [[The Book of Mormon Was Translated Mosiah-First]].[^1]
