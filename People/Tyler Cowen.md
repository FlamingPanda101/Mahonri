# Tyler Cowen

**Role:** Academic economist and public intellectual
**Era:** Contemporary, American
**Tradition:** Secular (non-Latter-day Saint)

## Bio

Tyler Cowen is the Holbert L. Harris Professor of Economics at George Mason University and director of the Mercatus Center. He co-authors the influential *Marginal Revolution* blog and hosts the *Conversations with Tyler* interview series. He is not a Latter-day Saint.

## Relevance to the vault

Cowen is the non-member economist at the center of [[Ward Radio - Economics Professor Says Mormonism Cures Poverty Better Than Social Progressivism]]. Responding (c. 2011) to a reader question on his blog — "would a massive conversion of low-income people to Mormonism reduce poverty?" — he answered that as a *viable solution* (not a viable *policy*), "actually, yes," because Mormonism supplies a **sociological/cultural fix** rather than a cash transfer, addressing habits, family structure, education, and community. He elsewhere quipped that as addictive substances and distractions proliferate, "the Mormons are going to inherit the earth," and that he encourages productive people "at the margin to become more Mormon." See [[Mormonism as a Sociological Cure for Poverty]] and [[The Poor Are Partially Complicit in Their Own Fate]].
