# Emanuel Swedenborg
**Role:** Swedish scientist, philosopher, and Christian mystic
**Era:** Enlightenment (1688-1772)
**Tradition:** Christian visionary / spiritualist

## Bio
Born in Stockholm in 1688, Swedenborg was a respected natural scientist who made recognized contributions to anatomy, physiology, and psychology before a religious crisis in mid-life turned him toward the spiritual.[^1] In his later years he reported sustained communication with spiritual beings and produced detailed descriptions of life after death.[^1] Moody cites Swedenborg as one of four historical sources — alongside the Bible, [[Plato]], and the Tibetan Book of the Dead — whose accounts strikingly parallel modern near-death testimony, noting especially that Swedenborg describes the newly dead not realizing they are dead, a detail absent from the Bible and Plato yet present in the Tibetan text he could not have read.[^2]
