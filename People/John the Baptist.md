# John the Baptist

**John the Baptist** is the New Testament forerunner of Jesus — the wilderness prophet who preached repentance and baptized by immersion in the Jordan, and who baptized Jesus (Matthew 3). He is independently attested by Josephus (*Antiquities* 18.5.2). As a son of the priest Zacharias, he stands within the **Aaronic** (Levitical) line.

## Significance in LDS Thought
In Latter-day Saint belief, John returned in **May 1829** as a resurrected messenger and conferred the **Aaronic Priesthood** on [[Joseph Smith]] and [[Oliver Cowdery]] by the laying on of hands, the priesthood "which holds the keys of the ministering of angels, and of the gospel of repentance, and of baptism by immersion for the remission of sins" ([[Joseph Smith History 1.68-69|JS-History 1:68-69]]; [[D&C 13]]).

## Contribution
In the [[The Stick of Joseph - Sacred Temple Connection to Priesthood Keys|Stick of Joseph interview]], John the Baptist anchors the bottom of [[Caleb Williams (Godwin)|Godwin's]] priesthood plate as the figure who "brings those keys from Aaron... all the way down into this dispensation," paired with [[Peter]], James, and John (Melchizedek). His conferral on Joseph Smith *after* study and inquiry is the central illustration of the claim that authority is given explicitly, not assumed from visions. See [[A Vision Does Not Confer Priesthood Authority]] and [[Priesthood Keys Frame the Seven Dispensations]].
