# Philo Dibble

Early Latter-day Saint whose diary (written in 1892, many decades after the events) is the source for the [[Falling of the Stars Prophecy]] account.

## Source of the Falling-Stars Account
Dibble did not witness the prophecy himself; he recorded what [[Joseph Hancock]] related to him — that a skeptic produced a written copy of Joseph Smith's 40-day star-fall prophecy on the 39th night, the night the 1833 Leonid meteor storm fell. The host of [[Mormonism with the Murf - Joseph Smith's Top 20 Fulfilled Prophecies]] checked Dibble's diary date (1892) and treated the late, secondhand chain of transmission as the chief weakness of the account, illustrating [[Absence of Contemporaneous Sources Undermines a Prophecy]].[^1]
