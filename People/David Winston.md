# David Winston

**Role:** Scholar of Hellenistic Judaism; Philo of Alexandria specialist
**Era:** 20th century (active 1970s–1990s)
**Tradition:** Academic Jewish studies

## Bio

David Winston is a leading scholar of Hellenistic Judaism, best known for his work on Philo of Alexandria. He published extensively on Philo's philosophy, theology, and cosmogony, including a landmark study of Philo's theory of creation in *Religious Syncretism in Antiquity* (1975) and responses to Jonathan Goldstein in the *Journal of Jewish Studies* (1984).

## Philo's Cosmogony

Winston demonstrated conclusively that Philo of Alexandria did NOT endorse creation ex nihilo. His key findings:

> "Philo taught that God created the world from a pre-existent matter. Indeed, he thought that Moses had anticipated Plato in this matter by teaching in Genesis that there was water, darkness, and chaos before the world came into existence."[^1]

Winston showed that Philo's frequent language of God "calling the non-existent into existence" (ἐκ μὴ ὄντων) reflects standard Middle Platonic idiom — "non-existent" means formless, passive matter occupying the realm of non-being, not absolute nothingness. God's creative act in Philo is the imposition of form, order, and function on pre-existent chaotic matter.[^1]

## 2 Maccabees 7:28 Debate

In 1984, Winston responded to [[Jonathan Goldstein]]'s attempt to defend a creation ex nihilo reading of 2 Maccabees 7:28. Winston demonstrated that Goldstein's argument was not accurate and agreed that the verse does not teach creation ex nihilo — a position Goldstein largely conceded in his response.[^2]
