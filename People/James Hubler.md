# James Hubler

**Role:** Scholar of classical and Christian philosophy
**Era:** Contemporary (1990s)
**Tradition:** Academic

## Bio

James Hubler completed his Ph.D. dissertation at the University of Pennsylvania in 1995, titled *Creatio, Matter, Creation, and the Body in Classical and Christian Philosophy through Aquinas*. His dissertation traces the development of creation theology from classical Greek philosophy through medieval Christian thought.

## Contributions to Creation Ex Nihilo Studies

### On 2 Maccabees 7:28

Hubler includes a lengthy discussion of 2 Maccabees 7:28 in his dissertation and, consistent with the scholarly consensus, rejects the notion that it teaches creation ex nihilo.[^1]

### On Philo of Alexandria

Hubler identified a key textual indicator that Philo did not believe matter was created. In Philo's *Quis Rerum Divinarum Heres Sit*:

> "The best textual evidence for uncreated matter comes from *Quis Rerum Divinarum Heres Sit* where Philo excludes matter from the things God praises at the consummation of creation. God praises all the things which he has created, but Philo does not include matter."[^2]

Hubler further observed that Philo's theodicy depends on eternal matter:

> "Despite his many borrowings from the Stoics, Philo has retained the dualism of Plato and like Plato makes eternal and intractable matter the source of difficulty and necessity in the world. Difficulty and evil does not flow from the source of good and reason. It is not from God."[^2]
