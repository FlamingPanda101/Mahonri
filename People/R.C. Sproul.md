# R.C. Sproul

**R.C. Sproul** (1939–2017) was a prominent Reformed theologian, founder of **Ligonier Ministries**, and a leading popularizer of Calvinist theology in the late 20th century.

## "A Fallible Collection of Infallible Books"
[[Joe Heschmeyer]] cites Sproul's famous concession that, on the Protestant view, the Bible is **"a fallible collection of infallible books."** Heschmeyer turns this into a problem: if the *collection* (the canon list) is fallible and rests on human criteria like apostolicity, then critical scholarship can at any time overturn an attribution (e.g. Pauline authorship of Hebrews or the Pastorals) and destabilize the canon. The Protestant therefore "can't really have a closed canon." See [[The Fallible Canon Is Hostage to Critical Scholarship]] [^1].

> "R.C. Sproul famously refers to the Bible as a fallible set of infallible books, but it's fallible in some really obvious ways" [^1].

## On Infant Baptism's Early Attestation
In a separate context, [[Trent Horn]] cites Sproul (a credobaptist) conceding that infant baptism "is testified to early" and that "there isn't opposition to it" in the early church — the only early debate being whether to wait eight days (the circumcision analogy). Horn uses the admission to argue the historical record favors an early pedobaptist discipline. See [[Baptismal Regeneration Is the Doctrine Infant Baptism Is the Discipline]].[^2]
