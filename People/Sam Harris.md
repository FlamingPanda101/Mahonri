# Sam Harris

**Full Name:** Samuel Benjamin Harris
**Era:** Contemporary (1967–present)
**Tradition:** Atheist (one of the "Four Horsemen" of New Atheism)
**Role:** Philosopher, neuroscientist, author, podcaster

## Bio

Sam Harris is an American philosopher, neuroscientist, and prominent atheist
public intellectual. He holds a PhD in cognitive neuroscience from UCLA. He is
the author of *The End of Faith* (2004), *Letter to a Christian Nation* (2006),
*The Moral Landscape* (2010), and *Free Will* (2012). He co-founded Project
Reason and hosts the *Making Sense* podcast.

Harris is one of the "Four Horsemen" of New Atheism alongside [[Richard
Dawkins]], [[Christopher Hitchens]], and [[Daniel Dennett]].

## Topics

### Free Will Determinism
Harris argues that free will is an illusion. In his book *Free Will* (2012), he
contends that human choices and intentions "arise from prior causes" —
genetics, environment, and unconscious brain processes — meaning humans have no
libertarian free will. [[Hayden Carroll]] discusses Harris's position: "he does
these little studies about how before someone says something, things happen in
their brain first... before things are even consciously even thought
about."[^1]

Harris had a notable debate with [[Jordan Peterson]] on this topic. Carroll
uses Harris as an example of a "consistent" atheist who follows materialism to
its logical conclusion.[^2]

Harris is discussed in: [[Free Will - Atheist Determinism vs LDS
Co-Eternality]], [[Atheism's Four Bad Implications]].

### Moral Landscape
Despite his determinism, Harris attempts to ground objective morality in
science (*The Moral Landscape*, 2010), arguing that "well-being" can be
measured. Critics (including other atheists) note the tension between
determinism and moral responsibility.[^3]
