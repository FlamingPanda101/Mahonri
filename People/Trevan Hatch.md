# Trevan Hatch

**Role:** BYU scholar of the ancient Near East and early Judaism; subject librarian
**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint (academic biblical / Second Temple studies)

## Bio
Trevan Hatch holds a faculty appointment in the Brigham Young University library, where he is responsible for the Middle East, ancient Near East, Jerusalem Center, anthropology, and archaeology collections — "basically everything related to the ancient world or religion" except the Latter-day Saint materials. His scholarship centers on the Hebrew Bible, Second Temple Judaism, and the historical Jesus; he edited a volume on Latter-day Saints and Jews (*The Learning of the Jews*) and refers to a forthcoming Jesus book, *A Stranger in Jerusalem*.

## Contribution to the source
In this [[The Stick of Joseph - Clues Reveal the Unknown Editor of Genesis|Stick of Joseph]] conversation, Hatch argues that **the book of Genesis was given its final edited form during or shortly after the Davidic–Solomonic monarchy as pro-Davidic apologetic** — the "unknown editor of Genesis" being a court scribe. He builds the case from:
- **The younger-brother / fratricide motif** that climaxes and disappears at Solomon over Adonijah ([[The Younger-Brother Motif Legitimizes Solomons Succession]]).
- **Judah–David and Joseph–David literary parallels**, including the [[Genesis 38|Judah-and-Tamar]] insertion ([[Judah and David Are Written as Literary Doubles]]).
- **Geography** (boundaries of Genesis 15, Mount Moriah, the Gihon spring, [[Melchizedek]] at Salem) and **anti-rival polemic** (Ammon, Moab, Edom) that fit David's kingdom ([[Genesis Was Finalized as Davidic-Solomonic Apologetic]]).
- A pedagogical **three-category taxonomy of inspiration** ([[Scripture as a Spectrum Not a Binary]]).

He leans on mainstream source critics — [[Gary Rendsburg]], [[Richard Friedman]], [[Wellhausen Thesis on Hebrew Bible Origins|Julius Wellhausen]] — while reading the data through a Restoration lens (the Book of Mormon's [[Nephi]] as a "new David"; the redactor as a visible editor like [[Mormon Is an Ethnocentric Redactor Focused on the Witness of Christ|Mormon]]).

## Contribution — prophetic authority (Cwic Show)
In the [[Cwic Show - The Official Position on Official Positions|Cwic Show interview]] about his chapter "What's the Church's Official Position on Official Positions?", Hatch argues there is **no agreed criterion** for an official LDS position ([[There Is No Agreed Criterion for an Official Church Position]]); that LDS leaders have voiced **two competing authority traditions** — living prophet vs. standard works ([[The Living Prophet Versus the Standard Works Are Two Competing LDS Authority Traditions]]); uses the **caffeine reversal** as a worked example of that tension ([[The Caffeine Reversal Exposes the Prophet-Versus-Scripture Tension in the Word of Wisdom]]); flags how "follow the prophet in all things" gets **weaponized** ([[Follow the Prophet in All Things Is a Slogan That Gets Weaponized]]; cf. [[The Thinking Has Been Done Was Repudiated by George Albert Smith]]); and proposes a **stewardship boundary** — prophets are decisive over the institution and ordinances but not over all truth ([[Prophetic Stewardship Is Over the Institution and Ordinances Not Over All Truth]]).
