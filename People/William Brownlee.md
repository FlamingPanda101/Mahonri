# William Brownlee

**Role:** Biblical scholar and early Dead Sea Scrolls researcher; specialist on Isaiah
**Era:** 20th century (1917–1983), American
**Tradition:** Protestant

## Bio

William Hugh Brownlee was an American biblical scholar and one of the first researchers to work directly on the Dead Sea Scrolls after their discovery. He is associated above all with the **Great Isaiah Scroll** (1QIsaᵃ), in which he identified a large-scale literary patterning of the book, and with his study *The Literary Message of Isaiah*. His proposal — that the entire book of Isaiah is bound together by an overarching, deliberate structure — challenged the dominant source-critical division of the book.

## Relevance to the vault

Brownlee is the scholarly anchor for [[Avraham Gileadi]]'s holistic reading of Isaiah. Gileadi reports that the key structure he built on "was discovered by William Brownlee in the great Isaiah scroll of the Dead Sea Scrolls" — a *synchronous* structure meaning "the entire book is one scenario." Gileadi studied it from a doctoral dissertation, then spent a further decade of post-doctoral work before publishing *The Literary Message of Isaiah*. The upshot, developed in [[Isaiah Is Apocalyptic and Applies Simultaneously to Isaiahs Day and the End Time]], is that book-spanning literary structures turn Isaiah into a single end-time prophecy and undercut the [[Deutero-Isaiah Theory]] — "There's no Deutero-Isaiah. That's nonsense."
