# Thomas A. Wayment

Latter-day Saint scholar of the New Testament and early Christianity, professor of Classical Studies at Brigham Young University. Produced a widely-used scholarly translation of the New Testament for Latter-day Saints (2018).

## Role in *Ancient Christians*
Author of Chapter 3 (in the PDF numbering), **"Creating Canon: Authority, New Prophecy, and Sacred Texts."** His thesis: the canon began as a *rule of faith* (authoritative teaching), not a book-list; canon formation was a power struggle between charismatic teachers and bishops; and an open canon, like a closed one, is an instrument of ecclesiastical authority. He explicitly *problematizes* the popular notion that canonization was simply an attempt to suppress an "authentic" early Christianity. See [[Canon Began as a Rule of Faith Not a List of Books]], [[Canon Formation Was a Power Struggle Over Authority]], [[Athanasius Cited a Noncanonical Book to Defend the Rule of Faith]], [[An Open Canon Allows Unbounded Ecclesiastical Authority]], and [[Closing the Canon Was Used to Declare Prophecy Ended]].
