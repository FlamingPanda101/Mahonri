# Bruce Porter

A Latter-day Saint scholar with degrees in ancient Near Eastern and Middle East studies (specializing in Semitic languages and religious history) and author of over a dozen books, best known for his research on the Pearl of Great Price. [[Jonah Barnes]] repeatedly invokes him as a corroborating expert on the volume's textual and publication history. On [[Ward Radio - Pearl of Great Price Explained|Ward Radio]], Barnes notes that Bruce Porter quoted [[Hugh Nibley]]'s line that "the Pearl of Great Price is the book that answers all of the questions," and cites Porter as someone who would "corroborate a lot of what I'm saying" about the origins of the [[Book of Moses]] — including the point that no one is certain who first applied the title "Book of Moses" to the 1830 revelations.[^1]

On the *Gospel Lessons* channel (host Kevin Prince), Porter presents a "false traditions" reading of the priesthood from a self-described 40-year chronological study of everything Joseph Smith said on the subject. His distinctive claims: priesthood is an **authority to administer ordinances and record them**, not a "healing power" ([[The Priesthood Is Authority to Administer Not a Power to Heal]]); Joseph taught **three** priesthoods including a **patriarchal** one held equally by a sealed husband and wife ([[Joseph Smith Taught Three Priesthoods Including the Patriarchal]]); the **matriarch** is the highest priesthood-holder in the home ([[The Highest Priesthood in the Home Is the Matriarch]]); and the Melchizedek priesthood exists to substitute for a **missing or unworthy patriarch** ([[The Melchizedek Priesthood Substitutes for a Missing or Unworthy Patriarch]]). These are presented as his own synthesis — informed but not magisterial. (Not to be confused with [[James P. Porter]] in the vault.)
