# Nimrod

**Who:** "Mighty hunter before the Lord" and founder of Babel/Babylon in [[Genesis 10|Genesis 10:8-10]]; in later apocryphal tradition (e.g. Jubilees-type legends) the archetype of the antichrist and builder of the Tower of Babel.

**Role:** [[Todd McLauchlin]] makes Nimrod the antitype in his apocryphal "garment of power" genealogy: the holy garment of [[Adam]] passes through [[Enoch]] and [[Noah]], is stolen by [[Ham]], and reaches Nimrod via Cush. In the legend Nimrod is a "great hunter" only because the garment makes the animals come to him (as they came to Noah) — but he uses it to *spread death*, "murder to get gain," and to replicate Enoch's city through the Tower of Babel, a counterfeit temple-order. The brother of Jared and his people come out of this Babel society ([[Ether 2|Ether 2]] calls their valley "Nimrod"), which frames the brother of Jared's ascent to the veil as a man recovering true priesthood from a corrupted one. See [[The Garment of Power Descended From Adam to Nimrod]] and [[The Brother of Jared Models the Endowment Ascent Through the Veil]].
