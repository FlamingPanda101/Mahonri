# Salvatore Cirillo

**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** Non-Latter-day Saint
**Role:** Scholar; author of a master's thesis on the Book of Enoch and Joseph Smith

## Bio
Salvatore Cirillo is a non-Latter-day Saint scholar who wrote a master's thesis (Durham University) examining the relationship between the ancient Enoch corpus and [[Joseph Smith]]'s Enoch material in the [[Moses 6|Book of Moses]]. He set out to determine how Joseph could have produced his Enoch content and, per the Thoughtful Faith presentation, concluded that the parallels between the ancient texts and Joseph's rendering are "substantial" and "beyond dispute," spending the paper trying to explain how Joseph could have gained access to sources that were largely untranslated or undiscovered in the 1830s.[^1]

## On the Enoch parallels
Cirillo's catalogue of overlaps — the Son of Man identification, Enoch foreseeing Noah and the flood, the personified weeping earth, the Mahijah/Mahawai name, "lad" for the aged Enoch, and others — is the spine of [[Cirillo Catalogs Substantial Enoch Parallels Beyond Dispute]]; his availability problem is developed in [[The Enoch Rediscovery Timeline Postdates Joseph Smith]].[^1]
