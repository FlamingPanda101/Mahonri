# Lehi

A prophet and patriarch who led his family out of Jerusalem; father of [[Nephi]]. He received a vision (the Tree of Life) and urged his sons to seek the same revelation for themselves.

## As a Seeker
[[Don Bradley]] lists Lehi among the seekers who found God by taking initiative, the pattern he reads in [[Abraham 1.1-4|Abraham 1]] — and speculates that Lehi's emphasis on his sons inquiring of the Lord ("Have you inquired of the Lord? Have you asked Him?") may reflect this Abrahamic seeking pattern, possibly known from the brass plates. See [[Seeking God Versus Arbitrary Election]].

## Preaching the Messiah in Jerusalem
In 1 Nephi 1:19-20 Lehi is mocked for denouncing Jerusalem's wickedness but threatened with death the moment he preaches a coming Messiah — equating his danger with "the prophets of old, whom they had cast out and stoned and slain." [[Jonah (Plain and Precious Publishing)|Jonah Barnes]] reads this alongside Jesus, Peter, and Abinadi as evidence that [[Prophets Were Killed for Preaching the Messiah Before 600 BC|prophets were martyred for Messiah-preaching]] before 600 BC [^lehi1].
