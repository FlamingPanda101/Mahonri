# George McCready Price

**Role:** Seventh-day Adventist author; "father of young earthism" (young-earth / flood geology creationism)
**Era:** 1870–1963
**Tradition:** Seventh-day Adventist

## Bio

George McCready Price was a self-taught Seventh-day Adventist writer whose "flood geology" supplied much of the intellectual scaffolding for twentieth-century young-earth creationism — the reason [[T. Benjamin Spackman]] calls him "the father of young earthism."[^1] He is the same figure named in Spackman's doctoral work on the rise of creationist thought among Latter-day Saints, where Price's ideas are traced into LDS anti-evolution writing (notably via Joseph Fielding Smith).

## The Blatchford syllogism, repurposed

In the *Keystone* interview, Price is the hinge of an irony. The "no Adam → no Fall → no atonement → no Christianity" chain originated with the British atheist [[Robert Blatchford]] as part of an argument *for* atheism and against evolution. Price, "a fundamentalist but on the Christian side, picks up Blatchford's syllogism and thinks it works as an argument against evolution" — accepting Blatchford's *logic* while rejecting his conclusion, and stripping it of "Blatchford's other assumptions." Detached this way the syllogism "gets very popular," but, Spackman stresses, "the logic is pretty flawed."[^1] See [[The Blatchford-Price Syllogism - No Adam No Atonement]].

## Why cited / authority basis

Cited *historically*, to document the pedigree of a still-common anti-evolution / anti-Christianity argument, not as an authority on science or scripture. Authority **C**.
