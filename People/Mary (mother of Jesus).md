# Mary (mother of Jesus)

The mother of [[Jesus of Nazareth]], present at his first miracle and named throughout the Gospels and Acts. In the apologetics vault she appears chiefly in connection with the wedding at Cana.

## At the Wedding at Cana
In the Stick of Joseph reading of [[John 2.1-11|John 2:1–11]], it is Mary who sets the miracle in motion: with the wine run out and "not knowing what to do, she turns to her son for help." When Jesus asks what she would have him do, Mary gives no direct answer; instead she charges the servants present, "Whatsoever he saith unto you, do it." That charge is what makes the servants' subsequent obedience — drawing out and serving what appeared to be dirty purification-water — "no small faith."
