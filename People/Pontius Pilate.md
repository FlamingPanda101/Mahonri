# Pontius Pilate

Roman prefect of Judaea (c. 26-36 AD) who interrogated and sentenced Jesus. In the talk he is cast as "the ultimate Greek" — an educated, Hellenized ruling-class thinker whose question "What is truth?" embodies the empirical, propositional Greek epistemology.

## "What is truth?" as the Greek question
In John 18:37 Pilate asks Jesus "What is truth?" — treated as the paradigmatic Greek demand for truth as a static, written, seeable fact, set against Jesus's Hebrew answer that truth is lived and heard. See [[What Is Truth - Pilate and the Greek Question]] [^1].
