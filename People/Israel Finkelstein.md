# Israel Finkelstein

**Role:** Archaeologist and biblical historian
**Era:** Contemporary (b. 1949)
**Tradition:** Jewish / Academic

## Bio
Israel Finkelstein is an Israeli archaeologist at Tel Aviv University and, with Neil Asher Silberman, co-author of *The Bible Unearthed: Archaeology's New Vision of Ancient Israel and the Origin of Its Sacred Texts* (2001), which lays out an archaeological and textual case that much of the Hebrew Bible took shape in the late monarchy and exile.

## Cited on the Josianic editorial program
[[Alex Arnold]] cites Finkelstein and Silberman (with [[Richard Elliott Friedman]] and [[Frank Moore Cross]]) as mainstream, non-LDS scholars who lay out "the archaeological and textual case" for a [[Hezekiah Josiah and the Deuteronomist Reforms|Deuteronomistic]] reshaping of Israelite religion under [[Josiahs Reform and the First Temple Make Restoration a Coherent Category|Josiah]]. Arnold uses this to argue the divine-council / pre-existent-Messiah tradition was edited out of the mainstream canon. See [[The Deuteronomists Edited Out the Pre-Existent Divine Messiah]].[^1]
