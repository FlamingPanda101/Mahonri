# Joseph Henrich

**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** Secular (evolutionary anthropology)
**Role:** Professor of Human Evolutionary Biology, Harvard; author of *The WEIRDest People in the World*

## Bio

Joseph Henrich (mis-rendered "Henrik") is a Harvard evolutionary anthropologist whose book *The WEIRDest People in the World* argues that the Western Church's "Marriage and Family Program" reshaped Western psychology toward individualism.[^1]

## Topics

### The Christian Marriage and Family Program
[[Glenn Scrivener]] cites Henrich to argue that [[1 Corinthians 7.3-5|1 Corinthians 7]] together with [[Matthew 19.3-9|Matthew 19]] constitute a "Christian sexual revolution" that "completely upended our ideas of the human person, the individual, and equality" — and that modern feminism is unthinkable without this intellectual genealogy. See [[Paul Pioneered Mutual Bodily Ownership]] and [[The Christian Origin of Equality]].[^1]
