# Richard Bushman

**Who:** Richard Lyman Bushman, emeritus professor of history at Columbia University and the foremost academic biographer of [[Joseph Smith]], author of *Joseph Smith: Rough Stone Rolling* (2005) and a recent cultural history of the gold plates. Evangelical scholar [[Kyle Beshears]] lists him among the biographers he read — alongside [[Fawn Brodie]], [[John Turner]], and [[Dan Vogel]] — in preparing to write about Joseph Smith. [^1]

## On the Witnesses' Religious Experience
Beshears credits Bushman's latest gold-plates book with persuading him that "there is an element of religious experience or subjectivity for the witnesses that has to be present," because the witness experience "isn't just an ordinary thing" — and that it is unreasonable to expect the witnesses to describe a supernatural encounter "in a way that would satisfy a modern empiricist audience." This supports the LDS rebuttal that subjective/visionary framing does not negate a real artifact. See [[Spiritual Eyes Described Real Things in the 1800s]] and [[All Three Witnesses Upheld Their Testimony for Life]]. [^1][^2]

## As a Primary-Source Model
Bushman exemplifies the standard Beshears tried to follow: read across friendly and critical biographers, then go to the Joseph Smith Papers themselves. See [[Read the Primary Sources Before Judging a Person]]. [^3]
