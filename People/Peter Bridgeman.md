# Peter Bridgeman

Peter Bridgeman was the nephew of [[Josiah Stowell]] and the party who brought the 1826 disorderly-person charge against [[Joseph Smith]].[^1] The source highlights the irony that the charge came not from the supposed "victim" (his uncle Stowell, who hired and defended Joseph) but from a relative who apparently disbelieved or disliked the seer-stone activity and sought to stop it to "save his uncle."[^2]

This detail is load-bearing for the defense: because the supposed victim testified *for* Joseph, the prosecution was driven by a third party, which the source argues is "probably why he was discharged."[^3] See:
