# Amy-Jill Levine

**Role:** Professor of New Testament and Jewish Studies
**Era:** Contemporary
**Tradition:** Academic (Jewish scholar of the New Testament)

## Bio

Amy-Jill Levine ("A.-J. Levine") is a prominent Jewish scholar of the New Testament and Second Temple Judaism, University Professor of New Testament and Jewish Studies emerita at Vanderbilt Divinity School and a professor at Hartford International University for Religion and Peace. Her work reads Jesus and the Gospels firmly within their first-century Jewish context, countering the supersessionist contrast of a "legalistic" Judaism against a "loving" Christianity.

## Contribution to the source

In the *Cwic Show* [[Cwic Show - Be Ye Therefore Perfect feat Trevan Hatch|interview]], [[Trevan Hatch]] cites Levine for a grammatical observation on [[Matthew 5.48|Matthew 5:48]]: the verse is **not an imperative** ("be perfect!") but a **future indicative** — an *if/then* statement. On her reading, "if you love your enemies as God loves his enemies, *then* you will be perfect, even as your Father in heaven is perfect," which the preceding [[Matthew 5.43-45|Matthew 5:45]] ("that ye may be children of your Father") already illustrates. This reinforces Hatch's thesis that the verse caps a love-your-enemies sermon rather than commanding individual flawlessness. See [[The Therefore in Matthew 5.48 Ties Perfection to Loving Enemies]].

Levine's broader insistence — that the ethic of love and forgiveness is rooted in the Hebrew scriptures, not invented over against them — also underwrites Hatch's point that the Torah's *imitatio Dei* ([[Leviticus 19.18|Leviticus 19:18]], [[Deuteronomy 10.18-19|Deuteronomy 10:18-19]]) is the soil of Jesus's command.
