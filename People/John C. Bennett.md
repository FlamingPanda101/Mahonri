# John C. Bennett

**Role:** Nauvoo-era Latter-day Saint leader turned hostile exposé author
**Era:** 19th century (1804–1867)
**Tradition:** LDS (excommunicated)

## Bio

John Cook Bennett was an early and briefly prominent figure in Nauvoo — assistant president to Joseph Smith and the city's first mayor — before being excommunicated in 1842 over sexual misconduct. He retaliated with a sensational exposé (*The History of the Saints*) accusing the Church of running organized "spiritual wifery" and harems, including within the Relief Society.

## Relevance to the vault

Bennett functions in the Ward Radio polygamy episodes as a **byword for distorted accusation**. In [[Ward Radio - Did Joseph Smith Even Polygamy Bro]], [[Don Bradley]] and the hosts note that some of Joseph's and Hyrum's public denials of polygamy are best read as responses to Bennett's specific charge of organized harems — a point they say polygamy-denial advocates omit, because acknowledging it explains the denials without requiring that Joseph practiced no plural marriage at all. The hosts also use Bennett comedically as the archetype of the "anti-Mormon who twists history." See [[The Eyewitness Asymmetry Refutes the Polygamy Conspiracy Theory]] and [[Public Denials of Polygamy Targeted Bennetts Harem Charge]].
