# Nicholas J. Frederick

**Role:** Associate Professor of Ancient Scripture, Brigham Young University
**Field:** History of Christianity; intertextuality of the Bible and Mormon scripture
**Tradition:** Latter-day Saint scholarship

## Credentials

Nicholas J. Frederick holds a bachelor's degree in Classics and a master's in Comparative Studies, both from Brigham Young University, and a PhD in the History of Christianity with an emphasis in Mormon Studies from Claremont Graduate University. His research "primarily revolves around the intertextual relationship between the Bible and Mormon scripture." [^1]

**Why cited / authority basis:** A credentialed scholar of early Christian history synthesizing primary patristic sources (Justin, Irenaeus, Tertullian, Clement, Origen, Augustine) for a Latter-day Saint readership. Authority is *B* (scholarly secondary), not *A*, because he interprets rather than witnesses the ancient evidence.

## Contribution

Frederick wrote chapter 13, "Facing the End: The Second Coming of Jesus Christ and the Millennium," in *Ancient Christians: An Introduction for Latter-day Saints* (Maxwell Institute, 2022). He traces how early Christians processed the [[The Delay of the Parousia Drove Christian Doctrinal Change|delay of the Parousia]] — from Jewish [[Christianity Inherited Jewish Day-of-the-Lord Eschatology|day-of-the-Lord eschatology]], through [[Early Christian Premillennialism Was the Earliest Millennial View|early premillennialism]] and the [[Clement and Origen Allegorized Away the Physical Second Coming|Alexandrian allegorization]], to [[Augustine Recast the Millennium as the Present Age of the Church|Augustine's amillennial settlement]] — and draws parallels to Latter-day Saint millenarian expectation.
