# Mosiah Hancock

**Role:** Latter-day Saint memoirist; nephew-by-marriage of Fanny Alger
**Era:** 19th century (1834–1907)
**Tradition:** LDS

## Bio

Mosiah Lyman Hancock was a Latter-day Saint pioneer and the son of [[Levi Hancock]]. Late in life (1890s) he wrote an autobiography that includes a much-cited account of the [[Joseph Smith]]–[[Fanny Alger]] relationship: he reports that in **1833** his father Levi performed Joseph's marriage to Fanny as part of a "swap" (Levi would get Clarissa Reed in exchange for securing Fanny for Joseph).

## Relevance to the vault

In [[Ward Radio - Debunking Joseph Smiths Fake Affair with Fanny Alger feat Don Bradley]], [[Don Bradley]] treats Mosiah's account as **riddled with holes**: it is written ~60 years after events that happened *before Mosiah was born*; it adds the claim that Levi performed the "first plural marriage of the dispensation" to his father's autobiography only *after Levi had died* (Levi never claimed it himself); it assumes a servant girl living in Joseph's house was presumed to marry him (an anachronistic projection of later polygamy); and Mosiah defends his own memory by saying he had "always kept the Word of Wisdom" — not a historical argument. Bradley uses the better-documented Levi-Hancock and Eliza-Jane-Webb evidence to relocate the relationship to **July 1836** (Fanny aged 18), not 1833 (aged 15). See [[The Mosiah Hancock 1833 Date for Fanny Alger Is Unreliable]] and [[The Fanny Alger Relationship Dates to July 1836 Not 1833]].
