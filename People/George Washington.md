# George Washington

**Era:** 1732–1799 (18th century)
**Tradition:** American founding / Enlightenment
**Role:** General, statesman, first U.S. President

## Bio

Commander of the Continental Army and first President of the United States.

## Role in the Argument

Washington is [[Brian Thomassen]]'s **emblem of the Enlightenment limiting principle** — the American model that *restrains* power, contrasted with the French revolutionary trajectory that ends in [[Napoleon]]'s imperial absolutism. On finishing his second term he wrote: "Having now finished the work assigned me, I retire from the great theater of action," and went home — the antithesis of Napoleon's "I am the throne." The founders, unlike the French revolutionaries, did not have to kill each other. See [[America Was the Necessary Seedbed for the Restoration]].[^1]
