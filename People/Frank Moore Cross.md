# Frank Moore Cross

**Era:** 1921–2012
**Tradition:** Christian (American)
**Role:** Harvard biblical scholar, epigrapher, Dead Sea Scrolls authority

## Bio
Frank Moore Cross was the Hancock Professor at Harvard and one of the most influential 20th-century scholars of the Hebrew Bible, Northwest Semitic epigraphy, and the Dead Sea Scrolls. [[Alex Arnold]] cites him as a non-LDS authority establishing the Deuteronomistic editorial program.[^1]

## On the Deuteronomistic Reform
Cross's work on the Deuteronomistic History is invoked (alongside [[Martin Noth]], [[Margaret Barker]], and [[Michael Heiser]]) to argue that [[Josiah]]'s 621 BC reform was part of a systematic scribal program suppressing divine council theology, open theophany, and pre-existent Messiah language — a framework Arnold says was unavailable in 1830. See [[Deuteronomistic Reform Explains the Book of Mormon Theology]].[^1][^2]
