# Steven Robinson

BYU professor and LDS scholar. Known in the context of the [[King Follett Discourse]]
for being among those who sought to downplay the sermon's radical distinctives in
interfaith settings.

## The Nauvoo Gathering

At a gathering between BYU professors and evangelical professors in Nauvoo, Robinson
was one of several LDS scholars who characterized the King Follett Discourse's
doctrines as possibly "fringe doctrines" or questioned whether they were "fully
canonized." This alarmed [[David Paulsen]], who forcefully countered that he belonged
to "the Church of King Follett."[^1]

This exchange crystallized the divide between LDS scholars who embrace Mormon
theological exceptionalism and those who emphasize common ground with mainstream
Christianity (see [[Modern LDS Tensions on King Follett Discourse]]).
