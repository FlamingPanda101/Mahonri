# Raymond Brown

Eminent Catholic New Testament scholar (Sulpician priest; Union Theological Seminary). Day repeatedly identifies as "a huge Raymond Brown guy" and builds his reading of the Johannine literature on Brown's work.

## On the Johannine community and the temple's destruction
Brown's thesis (as Day relays it): before 70 AD, Jewish followers of Jesus and other Jews worshipped together; the destruction of the temple forced a separation, after which the Christians signaled to Rome "we are not these rebel Jews." Reading the Gospel of John through that lens (Pilate's hand-washing, "my kingdom is not of this world," "I find no fault in him") makes its apparent anti-Judaism intelligible as community self-definition. See [[New Testament Books Reflect Rival Communities]].

## On the pendulum of church order
Brown's *The Churches the Apostles Left Behind* describes every spiritual organization swinging on a pendulum between **central control** and **charisma** (gifts of the Spirit) — the framework Butler uses to explain the Montanist crisis. See [[Montanus and the Overcorrection Against Prophecy]].
