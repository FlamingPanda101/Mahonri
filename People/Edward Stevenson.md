# Edward Stevenson

Edward Stevenson (1820–1897) was a Latter-day Saint missionary and later a member of the First Council of the Seventy, best remembered for traveling among and **interviewing the surviving Book of Mormon witnesses**, recording their accounts.[^1] (Not to be confused with the modern apostle [[Gary E. Stevenson]].)

## Independent Corroboration of the Stone Box's Fate
Stevenson returned to Palmyra and spoke with a local who could show him where the stone box had been on the hill but reported it had since **washed down** and its stones been carried off (through plowing and farming).[^2] Because Stevenson's first interview with [[David Whitmer]] was in **1877**, while Whitmer had already described the box washing down the hill in an **1875** Chicago newspaper interview, the two reports cannot be a relay — they stand as **independent** attestations of an obscure local fact.[^3] See [[Independent Attestation of What Happened to the Stone Box]].
