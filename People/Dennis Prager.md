# Dennis Prager

**Full Name:** Dennis Mark Prager
**Era:** Contemporary (1948–present)
**Tradition:** Jewish (religious conservative)
**Role:** Radio host, conservative political/cultural commentator, author, co-founder of PragerU

## Bio

Dennis Prager is an American conservative radio talk-show host, columnist, and
author. He is co-founder of Prager University (PragerU), an organization that
produces short educational videos on political, cultural, and religious topics
from a conservative perspective. Prager writes and presents widely on the
relationship between religion (especially Judeo-Christian values), reason, and
morality, and is the author of *The Rational Bible*, a commentary on the Torah.

## Topics

### Reason Is Only a Tool, Not a Source of Goodness
In the PragerU video on ethics, Prager argues against the Enlightenment
assumption that reason alone produces good people and a good world. He holds
that reason is morally neutral — "no more intrinsically moral than a knife" —
and can equally justify good or evil depending on what one already wants.[^1]

> "Reason leads to good only when you want it to. Just as it leads to bad when
> you want it to."[^1]

### Athens vs. Jerusalem
Prager frames the moral foundations of Western civilization as a contrast
between reason-based Athens and faith-based Jerusalem, crediting biblical faith
(not Greek reason) with teaching the world the infinite preciousness of human
life — including keeping "sickly babies" alive rather than exposing them.[^2]
