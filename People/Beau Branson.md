# Beau Branson

**Role:** Philosopher of religion; specialist in Trinitarian theology and the Monarchy of the Father
**Tradition:** Eastern Orthodox
**Era:** 21st century
**Authority:** Tier B — credentialed philosopher (PhD, Notre Dame) on the doctrine of the Trinity.

## Bio

Beau Branson is a philosopher of religion known for defending the **Monarchy of the Father** — the patristic and Eastern Orthodox view that God the Father is the unique unoriginate source (*arche*) within the Godhead — as the original, pro-Nicene understanding of the Trinity. He maintains a multi-part YouTube series on the topic and argues that much modern Western Trinitarianism has departed from this earlier framework.

## Role in the Nicene Creed Discussion

In the indexed Ward Radio episode, the guest (Orthocardia) repeatedly cites Branson as a non-LDS patristic authority for two claims: (1) that the early church and the Nicene Creed encode the **monarchy of the Father** and the Son's subordination, and (2) for the "original context of *homoousios*" as "same essence." The guest recommends "Dr. Beau Branson['s] ... six-part series on YouTube ... called Monarchy of the Father." [^1] He candidly notes Branson is not Mormon and "would take issue with other beliefs that we had." [^2]

The guest's appeal is selective: Branson defends *Nicene orthodoxy*, not the LDS Godhead, so his work supports the monarchy-of-the-Father theme without endorsing the LDS extension of *homoousios* to all humanity. See [[I Believe in One God the Father Almighty Encodes the Monarchy of the Father]] and [[Homoousios Means Same Essence Which Mormons Extend to All Humanity]].

> "Go look into the Eastern Orthodox view of the monarchy of the Father. Dr. Beau Branson has a six-part series on YouTube ... called Monarchy of the Father." [^1]
