# T.B. Stenhouse (1824-1882)

Scottish-born Mormon journalist and editor who became a leading dissenter, author of the 1873 exposé *The Rocky Mountain Saints*. Cited here as a contemporary witness to how little the membership accepted Adam-God.

## Witness against Adam-God's reception
Stenhouse (1873): "The mass of Mormonism does not believe the doctrine of the Adam deity, but of them all only one, [[Orson Pratt]], has dared to make a public protest against that doctrine." [[Luke Hansen]] uses this to argue Adam-God was never a top-down, broadly enforced teaching; [[Joe Heschmeyer]] reads the same quote as evidence that quiet dissent was widespread but **public** dissent was unsafe. See [[Cracking Down on Public Dissent Not Private Belief]] [^1].
