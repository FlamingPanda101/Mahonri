# James Bruce (explorer)

James Bruce of Kinnaird (1730–1794) was a Scottish traveler and explorer whose journeys to Ethiopia (then the Kingdom of Aksum/Abyssinia) resulted in the rediscovery of the Book of Enoch by Western Europe.

## The Ethiopian Expedition and Enoch Manuscripts

In 1773, Bruce returned to Europe from Ethiopia carrying three copies of the Book of Enoch in Ge'ez (Ethiopic). Dan McClellan notes that these "three copies disagreed quite a bit with each other" — they were "pretty late manuscripts" and "not very trustworthy." [^1] Despite their textual limitations, these manuscripts were the catalyst for the Western rediscovery of a text that had been unknown to the vast majority of Christendom for roughly 1,700 years.

## Significance in Enoch Studies

Bruce's manuscripts launched the chain of translations and editions that reintroduced 1 Enoch to Western scholarship:
- German translation (1801)
- First English translation (1821)
- First published Ethiopic text in Europe (1838, 105 chapters)
- Recension to 108 chapters (later)
- First critical edition (1851)
- [[R.H. Charles]]'s translation (1906)

Before Bruce, knowledge of 1 Enoch in Europe was fragmentary and secondhand — based on patristic references (Tertullian, Origen) and brief mentions by travelers.
