# Mani

**Role:** Founder-prophet of Manichaeism
**Era:** 3rd century CE (Sasanian Persia / Parthia)
**Tradition:** Manichaeism (a dualistic, Gnostic-classed religion)

## Bio

Mani was a third-century prophet who arose in the region of present-day Iran (Parthia/Persia), of partly Jewish descent, claiming to be a prophet of the Messiah and the **final prophet** completing the messages of Zoroaster, Jesus, and Buddha. He founded **Manichaeism**, a dualistic faith framing the cosmos as a struggle between good and evil. On [[Cardon Ellis]]'s **Ward Radio**, [[Jonah Barnes]] stresses that almost nothing of Mani's own teaching survives directly: he is known largely through hostile heresiologists (e.g. [[Hippolytus of Rome|Hippolytus]]) who simply branded him a heretic, and through the few texts that survive — making him "one of the least understood" religious founders.[^1]

## Why He Matters Here

- **The Cologne Mani Codex.** The codex discovered in 1969 is a biographical account of Mani; its **Apocalypse of Enosh** section is the source of the brass-plates / hide-in-the-wilderness passage the hosts read as a [[Angel Moroni|Moroni]] parallel. See [[The Cologne Mani Codex Parallels Moroni and the Brass Plates]].[^2]
- **Lost-and-found canon.** Mani's liturgy lists ~five books he studied, one being the **Book of the Giants** — long known of but unrecovered until it surfaced among the Dead Sea Scrolls, a precedent the hosts use for vindication-by-discovery (cf. [[Book of Enoch - Dead Sea Scrolls Attestation]]).[^3]
- **Survival and demise.** Manichaeism spread east to western China and survived in mountainous regions before being largely destroyed by Roman persecution and the rise of Islam. The hosts even float that Mani may have been "kind of prophetic" — God "trying out a new place at a new time."[^4]
