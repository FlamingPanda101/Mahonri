# Nick Bostrom

**Full Name:** Nick Bostrom
**Era:** Contemporary (1973–present)
**Tradition:** Secular / non-religious
**Role:** Philosopher, Director of the Future of Humanity Institute (Oxford)

## Bio

Nick Bostrom is a Swedish-born philosopher at Oxford University known for his
work on existential risk, AI safety, and the simulation hypothesis. His 2003
paper "Are You Living in a Computer Simulation?" argued that at least one of
three propositions is true: (1) civilizations go extinct before reaching
simulation capability, (2) advanced civilizations aren't interested in running
simulations, or (3) we are almost certainly living in a simulation.

## Topics

### Simulation Argument
Bostrom's simulation argument has been embraced by some atheists as a
naturalistic alternative to theism. [[David Butler]] notes the irony: LDS
theology already describes mortal life as a "second estate" — a probationary
state away from "base reality" (God's presence). Atheists who find Bostrom's
simulation plausible have conceded the conceptual framework of a created,
non-ultimate reality with a higher intelligence behind it.[^1] See [[Simulation
Theory Aligns with LDS Second Estate]].
