# Norman Russell

Patristics scholar and the foremost modern authority on **deification (theosis)**, author of the standard reference *The Doctrine of Deification in the Greek Patristic Tradition* (Oxford, 2004).

## Cited in *Ancient Christians*
Daniel Becerra draws on Russell for the New Testament vocabulary that fed patristic deification — the Pauline and Johannine language of adoption, sanctification, becoming "new creatures," being "one with Christ," and Christ living "in them." [^1] Russell's work documents how this scriptural language of unity, vitality, kinship, and participation became the technical apparatus of the *theosis* tradition. See [[Christ Became Man That We Might Become God - The Exchange Formula]].
