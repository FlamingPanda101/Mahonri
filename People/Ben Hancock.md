# Ben Hancock

**Role:** Host of the *For All The Saints* podcast
**Era:** Contemporary (2020s)
**Tradition:** The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (LDS)

## Bio

Ben Hancock hosts the *For All The Saints* podcast, a Latter-day Saint history and faith program. He is British, and in this episode draws on his own UK-side vantage point — noting the parallel arc of anti-Saint persecution in the United Kingdom into the 1970s — while interviewing [[Stephen Smoot]] about a Gestapo dossier on the Church in Nazi Germany.[^1] He guides the conversation through the surveillance findings, the swastika-flag photograph of [[Heber J. Grant]], mission president Roy Welker's letters, and the resistance of [[Helmut Hübner]], pressing for the historical context behind each "shocking" item rather than the social-media soundbite.[^2]

He frames the value of Smoot's research at the close: it "challenges and corrects a lot of previous assumptions of the church's stance during this era," is historically interesting, and is "faith promoting as well" — while flagging that some popular takes on this period are "really cynical."[^3]
