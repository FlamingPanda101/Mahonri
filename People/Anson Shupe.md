# Anson Shupe

Sociologist and critic of the LDS Church who investigated the Utah ritual-abuse cases. He concluded there was no evidence of a child sexual abuse ring and that Allen Hadfield "was no more likely to be an abuser than were his neighbors," identifying the psychotherapists' conjecture as the source of the entire affair.

## On Church Non-Interference
As an LDS critic, Shupe was well-positioned to detect Church meddling — and found none. He wrote that leaders were "undoubtedly relieved" to see the controversy die, but "there is no evidence that they played any role in discouraging further prosecution. The scandal collapsed of its own weight, not on account of outside pressure."
