# Daniel Wallace

**Who:** Notable Greek grammarian, professor of New Testament Studies at Dallas Theological Seminary. Evangelical Christian scholar.

**Key citation:** Wallace, despite being a conservative Trinitarian, acknowledges that *theos* in [[John 1.1|John 1:1c]] is **qualitative, not definite** — meaning it does not identify the Word as *the* God of Israel. Wallace then argues it is still compatible with Trinitarianism by asserting Jesus is "the second person of the Trinity" rather than the totality of God.[^1]

**Significance:** McClellan uses Wallace as a supporting witness — even a conservative scholar concedes the grammar is qualitative. The disagreement is whether this qualitative reading supports later Trinitarian theology. McClellan argues Wallace's rescue requires importing post-Nicene assumptions (consubstantiality, persons vs. being) that the author of John did not possess.[^2]
