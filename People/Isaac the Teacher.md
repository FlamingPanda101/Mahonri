# Isaac the Teacher

**Role:** Fifth-century Syriac Christian preacher and poet
**Era:** 5th century CE
**Tradition:** Syriac Christianity

> **Disambiguation:** Not to be confused with the Old Testament patriarch [[Isaac]] (son of Abraham) or with the seventh-century mystic [[Isaac of Nineveh]].

## Bio and contribution
Isaac the Teacher is cited by Heal as an example of the preacher's imaginative art — "appealing to the imagination of his hearers in descriptive pictures." His homily on the Magi (Matthew 2:1–12) paints a vivid contrast between the festal excitement expected in Persia and the silent unawareness the wise men actually found in Judea: "A great number of sentient beings were in the land, but they were asleep in the sleep of the unrighteous … God is in this land, but its inhabitants are still sleeping!" Heal translated the passage from Vatican Syriac 120 and notes the text "exhibits anti-Judaism." [^1]
