# Epicurus

**Role:** Ancient Greek philosopher
**Era:** 341–270 BC
**Tradition:** Greek philosophy (founder of Epicureanism)

## Bio

Epicurus was an ancient Greek philosopher and founder of Epicureanism, a school emphasizing a materialist cosmology and the pursuit of tranquil pleasure (*ataraxia*).

## The Epicurean dilemma

[[David Paulsen]] opens his treatment of the **logical problem of evil** with the dilemma traditionally attributed to Epicurus: "Either God is unwilling to prevent evil or He is unable. If He is unwilling, then He cannot be perfectly good; if He is unable, then He cannot be all powerful. Whence then evil?" [[David Hume]] later restated the same contradiction; Paulsen argues both formulations are too narrow because they omit the premises of absolute creation and foreknowledge (see [[Flew's Divine Complicity Argument]]).
