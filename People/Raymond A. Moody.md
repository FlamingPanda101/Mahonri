# Raymond A. Moody

**Role:** Psychiatrist and philosopher; pioneer of near-death studies
**Era:** Contemporary (b. 1944; *Life After Life* published 1975)
**Tradition:** Raised Presbyterian; member of the Methodist Church

## Bio

Raymond A. Moody, Jr., holds both an M.D. and a Ph.D. in philosophy. While teaching philosophy he began collecting first-hand accounts from patients who had been resuscitated from clinical death, and in *Life After Life* (1975) he coined the term **"near-death experience"** and described the recurring **composite NDE** that has shaped popular and scientific understanding of dying ever since. [^1] He later held the Bigelow Chair of Consciousness Studies at the University of Nevada, Las Vegas, and pursued consciousness research, including the rediscovery of the **psychomanteum** (his "Theater of the Mind"). [^2]

Moody describes his own outlook as religious but non-dogmatic — raised Presbyterian, a member of the Methodist Church — and explicitly disclaims statistical or theological proof, presenting his cases instead as an invitation to serious investigation. [^2] See [[Life After Life - Raymond Moody]].

## Key Work

- [[Life After Life - Raymond Moody]] (1975) — the founding text of near-death studies.
