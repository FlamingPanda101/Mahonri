# B.H. McCullagh

**Era:** Contemporary (late 20th–21st century)
**Tradition:** Secular / academic (non-LDS)
**Role:** Philosopher of history / historiographer

## Bio
C. Behan McCullagh (cited in the sources as "B.H. McCullagh," "C. B. McCullagh," and "Behan McCullagh") is an academic philosopher of history, author of *Justifying Historical Descriptions* (Cambridge University Press, 1984) and *The Truth of History*. His work sets out the standard **criteria for inferring the best explanation in history** — that a hypothesis should have greater explanatory scope and power, be more plausible, be less ad hoc, and so on — and is widely used in the philosophy of historical method.

## Why he matters
McCullagh is invoked in the vault as a **neutral, non-LDS methodological authority** for the minimal-facts/abductive approach to the gold plates. Apologist [[Josh Gailey]] grounds his analysis explicitly in McCullagh's logic of history — prefer early to late, firsthand to hearsay, enemy/unsympathetic to friendly, and multiply-attested sources, then infer the best explanation — insisting "it's not Josh's rulebook." Citing McCullagh shifts the debate from "Latter-day Saints special-plead" to "do the *agreed* historiographical criteria, applied evenhandedly, license the conclusion?" See [[The Minimal Facts Method Applied to the Gold Plates]] and [[Dan Vogel's East-Side Hole Is the Lone Source Against the West-Side Stone Box]]. He thus supplies the borrowed, field-standard rubric that several Book-of-Mormon-witness arguments rest on, alongside historians such as [[Don Bradley]].
