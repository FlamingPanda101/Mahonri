# Abraham Lincoln

Sixteenth President of the United States (1809–1865). In this source Lincoln functions as a comparative benchmark in [[Daniel C. Peterson]]'s argument about presentism and race.

Peterson opens his FAIR conference talk by reading a quotation about Black people not being equal to whites and not holding leadership over whites — letting the audience assume it was [[Brigham Young]] — and then revealing it was Abraham Lincoln, to show that such views "were in the air" and shared even by abolitionists.[^1] He further argues Lincoln "was on the side of the angels" in trying to end slavery *without* a civil war through compromise and the proposed use of federal land sales to compensate owners and resettle freed slaves (the policy backdrop to Liberia) — the same realist logic Peterson attributes to Brigham.[^2]
