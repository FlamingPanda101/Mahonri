# Charles Callis

Latter-day Saint mission president (Southern States Mission) in the early 20th century who later served as an Apostle in the Quorum of the Twelve. He is cited in [[Ward Radio - Why Everyone Is Jealous of Our High Demand Religion]] for an oft-quoted 1911 aphorism on the purpose of missionary labor.

## On Building Missionaries
The hosts attribute to Callis the line that hardship in the field produces the missionary even when it does not produce converts: "Trudging around in the rain and the storm and talking to people might not be an efficient way to do missionary work, but it's the greatest way to build missionaries."[^1] The quote anchors the argument that the mission's value is self-formation rather than baptismal output — see [[The Mission Builds the Missionary Even Without Converts]].
