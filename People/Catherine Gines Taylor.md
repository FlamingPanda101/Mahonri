# Catherine Gines Taylor

**Catherine Gines Taylor** is an art historian of early Christianity and author of "Inclining Christian Hearts: Work for the Dead," chapter 11 of *Ancient Christians: An Introduction for Latter-day Saints* (Maxwell Institute, 2022).

## Work for the Dead in the Material Record
Taylor's distinctive contribution is to merge the **art-historical record** (catacomb frescoes, sarcophagi, epitaphs, the Walesby lead tank, the Brignoles stele) with textual sources to recover early Christian "work for the dead." She argues this devotional record was at times "in tension with" official texts and that families undertook salvific acts — intercessory prayer, vigils, almsgiving, vicarious baptism, visionary redemption — partly out of dissatisfaction with the church's lack of codified intercession for the ordinary dead. She also highlights women as primary agents in funerary ritual, later assimilated under ecclesiastical authority. See [[Work for the Dead Is Attested in Early Christian Material Culture]] [^1].
