# Francesca Stavrakopoulou

**Full name:** Francesca Stavrakopoulou
**Role:** Scholar of the Hebrew Bible and ancient Israelite/Near Eastern religion
**Affiliation:** University of Exeter — Professor of Hebrew Bible and Ancient Religion; Head of the Department of Theology and Religion
**Era:** Contemporary (active 2010s–2020s)
**Authority:** Tier A — credentialed specialist; peer-reviewed publications on Israelite religion and the materiality of the divine.

## Bio

Francesca Stavrakopoulou is a British biblical scholar best known for her work on ancient Israelite religion, the body and materiality of God, and the place of goddess worship — especially **Asherah** — in pre-exilic Israel and Judah. She is widely associated with the public argument that Yahweh had a consort, Asherah, before the religious reforms of the late monarchy and the exile.

She was [[Dan McClellan]]'s doctoral dissertation supervisor at the University of Exeter, and McClellan describes her as "a very good friend of mine" and "the head of theology and religion at Exeter University." [^1]

## Role in the Discussion

In responding to a critic, McClellan defends a video that had cited a (then ~decade-old) article by Stavrakopoulou, and endorses her position without qualification: "She is 100% right. Asherah worship — recognition of Asherah as the consort or wife of Yahweh — was 100% normative prior to the reign of Josiah, his campaign of cult centralization, and then the events of the exile." [^2]

Her claim — that Asherah-as-consort was normative until the Josianic reform — is the originating scholarship behind the dating argument McClellan develops; see [[No Pre-Josiah Text Vilifies Asherah]].

> "That Oxford scholar is now the head of theology and religion at Exeter University. That is Francesca Stavrakopoulou. She is a very good friend of mine and my dissertation supervisor. She's also 100% right." [^1]
