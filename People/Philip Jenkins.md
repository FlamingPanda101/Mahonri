# Philip Jenkins

Historian of Christianity and global religion, author of *Jesus Wars: How Four Patriarchs, Three Queens, and Two Emperors Decided What Christians Would Believe for the Next 1,500 Years*. In the [[The Stick of Joseph - Jesuss Brother Corruption in Christianity and the Great Apostasy|Stick of Joseph episode]], [[Mike Day]] cites *Jesus Wars* for its depiction of how violent and politicized the post-Constantinian church became — quoting that once imperial power married Christianity, "the leaders of Christianity make the Sopranos look weak." Day uses Jenkins to support the thesis that [[Marrying Imperial Power to Religion Is the Decisive Corruption|the marriage of imperial power to religion]] was the decisive corruption of the Great Apostasy.
