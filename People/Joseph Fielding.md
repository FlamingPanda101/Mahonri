# Joseph Fielding

Early LDS convert, brother of Mary Fielding Smith (who married [[Joseph Smith]]'s
brother Hyrum Smith). Present at the April 1844 general conference where Joseph
Smith delivered the [[King Follett Discourse]].

## Reaction to the Sermon

Joseph Fielding was among those who found the discourse profoundly convincing:

> He declared that nobody in attendance hearing him deliver that sermon would
> conclude that he was a fallen prophet.[^1]

In other words, Fielding saw the discourse as definitive evidence that Joseph
Smith was still a true prophet — a direct rebuttal to the dissenters who claimed
Smith had fallen.[^1]
