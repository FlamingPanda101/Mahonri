# Karen Jo Torjesen

Scholar of early Christianity and women's history, author of *When Women Were Priests* (1995), a study of women's leadership in the early church and its later suppression.

## Cited in *Ancient Christians*
[[Ariel Bybee Laughton]] relies on Torjesen for the argument that [[Tertullian]] redefined church office in Roman legalistic terms — using *ius* ("right"), the same word for a citizen's legal rights, to describe clerical privileges. Torjesen's key insight: "so long as church leadership continued to model itself on the familiar role of household manager, there was no cultural barrier to women assuming leadership roles." The shift from a household-manager model to a public-rights model is what excluded women. See [[Tertullian Reframed Church Office as Roman Legal Privilege]]. [^1]
