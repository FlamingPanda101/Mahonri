# Steve Reed

**Role:** Latter-day Saint blogger and independent scripture student (blog "One Climbs", ~14 years)
**Era:** Contemporary (active 2020s)
**Tradition:** The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints

## Bio
Steve Reed runs the long-running blog *One Climbs* (some 700 published posts), which he describes as a corpus of gospel study built to pass on to his children rather than for clicks. He was introduced to the [[Stick of Joseph]] audience via producer Chris Jordan and appeared on the channel to present a reading of Alma 32-33 through Hebrew letter-symbolism and **gematria** (the numeric values of Hebrew letters). He leans on Mormon-mysticism and Hebrew-symbolism sources (David Littlefield's *Mormon Mysticism*; Michael Schneider's *A Beginner's Guide to Constructing the Universe*; Dave Butler's *In the Language of Adam*).

## On Alma 32 and Hebrew symbolism
Reed's thesis: Alma's sermon to the poor Zoramites is saturated with deliberate patterns of the numbers **four** and **three** (which sum to **seven**), keyed to the meanings of the fourth, third, and seventh letters of the Hebrew alphabet, and structured as a **temple-ascension** narrative — the seed/tree/fruit mapping the three rooms, ending in the *nehushtan* (believe-look-heal). He frames the Book of Mormon as "written by someone who knew Hebrew for an audience that knew Hebrew." See:
