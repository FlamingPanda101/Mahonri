# Zenos

**Era:** Pre-exilic Israelite prophet (per the Book of Mormon)
**Tradition:** Israelite / Book of Mormon
**Role:** Prophet quoted from the brass plates

## Bio
Zenos is an Israelite prophet repeatedly quoted in the Book of Mormon — most famously the author of the allegory of the olive tree ([[Jacob 5]]), the longest single chapter in the book — yet he appears nowhere in the surviving Old Testament. The Book of Mormon also cites a related prophet, Zenock. [[Mike Day]] argues Zenos was a *northern* (Elohist-tradition) prophet whose teaching that the Messiah-God "would come down and die" is exactly the kind of pre-reform, anthropomorphic, Messianic material the [[Deuteronomistic Corruption of the Old Testament|Deuteronomist reformers]] are said to have edited out — which is why he survives only on the brass plates, "a northern record."[^1]

## Relevance to the vault
Zenos functions as a test case for the [[Brass Plates Bypassed the Textual Corruption Chain|brass-plates]] thesis: a named prophet, absent from the Masoretic canon, preserving the divine-Messiah theology the [[The Book of Mormon Matches Pre-Reform First Temple Theology|Book of Mormon claims was purged]]. His olive-tree allegory anchors the tree-of-life imagery that recurs from [[1 Nephi 8]] through Alma. See [[Internal Cross-References to the Unwritten Small Plates]].
