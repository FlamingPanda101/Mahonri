# James VanderKam

James C. VanderKam is an American biblical scholar and the John A. O'Brien Professor of Hebrew Scriptures emeritus at the University of Notre Dame. He is one of the world's foremost authorities on the Dead Sea Scrolls, 1 Enoch, and Second Temple Judaism.

## 1 Enoch Scholarship

With [[George Nickelsburg]], VanderKam co-authored the definitive two-volume Hermeneia commentary on 1 Enoch (Fortress Press, 2001–2012) — the standard critical resource on the text. Their seven-part division of 1 Enoch (Book of Watchers, Book of Parables, Book of Luminaries, Dream Visions, Epistle of Enoch, Birth of Noah, Final Book) is the scholarly standard.

The Hermeneia translation based on this commentary is, per Dan McClellan, "the most academic translation that's available today" — far superior to the widely available 1906 R.H. Charles translation that most people encounter in bookstores. [^1]

## Dead Sea Scrolls Expertise

VanderKam's expertise in the Dead Sea Scrolls is directly relevant to 1 Enoch, since 11 fragmentary Aramaic manuscripts of 1 Enoch were found at Qumran — making it one of the best-attested texts in the Qumran library, outnumbered only by Genesis, Deuteronomy, Isaiah, and Psalms. This attestation demonstrates 1 Enoch's importance in Second Temple Judaism.
