# Jean-Baptiste Lamarck

**Era:** 1744–1829 (18th–19th century)
**Tradition:** Pre-Darwinian natural philosophy
**Role:** Naturalist

## Bio

French naturalist whose evolutionary theory held that traits change through **intent and inheritance of acquired characteristics** — the giraffe's neck lengthens because it stretches "to get those top juicy leaves," passing the gain to offspring. His ideas later "fell out of favor."[^1]

## Role in the Argument

Lamarck is [[Brian Thomassen]]'s **perfect foil** for illustrating the Enlightenment's rejection of teleology. Where [[Charles Darwin|Darwin]]'s model is **non-teleological** — speciation by natural selection and random mutation, "no purpose behind the change" — Lamarck's is **teleological**: "the intent informs the change." The contrast concretizes the Enlightenment disposition (efficient/material causes only, final causes removed) that Thomassen ties to the Constitution. See [[The Constitution Is an Enlightenment Document]].[^2]
