# Brian Hales

**Role:** Latter-day Saint documentary historian; anesthesiologist by profession
**Era:** Contemporary (active 2000s–2020s)
**Tradition:** LDS

## Bio

Brian C. Hales is the author of the most exhaustive documentary study of Joseph Smith's plural marriage — the multi-volume *Joseph Smith's Polygamy: History and Theology* (with a companion document database) — and a 2018 *Journal of Mormon History* article that tallied every alleged Joseph Smith "denial" of polygamy and found them few and qualified. He argues that essentially all professional historians agree Joseph practiced and instituted plural marriage, and that the modern online denial movement survives only by discounting the overwhelming testimonial and documentary record.[^1]

## Polygamy and the Implications Cascade

Interviewed by [[Jacob Hansen]], Hales reframes the polygamy-denial debate away from the documents and toward consequences: denying Joseph's polygamy is not a free move but forces rejecting four further pillars of LDS faith (the integrity of seven church/Relief-Society leaders, [[Doctrine and Covenants 132|D&C 132]], the temple endowment, and the living apostles' teaching). ## The Confidentiality Model and the Denials

Hales holds that Joseph never issued a blanket denial — he condemned spiritual wifery and *unauthorized* polygamy — and that the evidence best fits a "keeping it confidential" hypothesis (Hales: justified lying to protect lives is scriptural). See:
