# Creation Ex Nihilo Debate

Map of Content for the debate over the historical origins of the doctrine of creation out of nothing (*creatio ex nihilo*).

## Source

- [[Dan McClellan - Wes Huff Creation Ex Nihilo Response]] — Dan McClellan's systematic rebuttal of Wes Huff's claims (YouTube, 2025)

## Core Rebuttals (from McClellan's video)

### The Ebla Archive Claim
- [[Ebla Archives Do Not Support Creation Ex Nihilo]] — The Ebla tablet TM.75.G1682 does not affirm creation ex nihilo; the text is largely incomprehensible per epigraphers
- [[Pettinato Ebla Translation Unreliable]] — Pettinato's 1981 translation is apologetically motivated, widely discredited
- [[Wes Huff Misidentifies EA250 as Ebla Text]] — Huff confused the Amarna archive (EA) with Ebla (TM); the error signals uncritical reliance on secondary sources

### The 2 Maccabees 7:28 Claim
- [[2 Maccabees 7.28 Does Not Teach Creation Ex Nihilo]] — The 50+ year scholarly consensus (May, Goldstein, Winston, Bockmuehl, Anderson) is that this verse teaches creation from unformed matter, not from nothing

### The Philo of Alexandria Claim
- [[Philo Did Not Endorse Creation Ex Nihilo]] — Philo held to creation from pre-existent matter (Middle Platonic dualism); he could not reach creation ex nihilo

### The Historical Claim
- [[Creation Ex Nihilo Originated Late 2nd Century CE]] — First articulated by Tatian (c. 160), Theophilus (c. 169), and Irenaeus (c. 180) in response to Gnostic and philosophical debates
- [[Retroactive Reinterpretation of Creation Texts]] — The originators retroactively reread earlier texts through the new doctrinal lens; this lens became normative
- [[Greco-Roman Jewish Creation View Was Material Substrate]] — The pre-ex-nihilo consensus across Hellenistic Judaism was creation from pre-existent, formless matter

## Key Scriptures

- [[2 Maccabees 7.28|2 Maccabees 7:28]] — "God did not make them out of things that existed" (οὐκ ἐξ ὄντων)
- [[Genesis 1.1|Genesis 1:1]] — "In the beginning God created the heavens and the earth" (בְּרֵאשִׁית בָּרָא)
- [[Psalm 104.5-9|Psalm 104:5–9]] — referenced by Matthews & Benjamin as ANE parallel
- [[Wisdom 11.17|Wisdom of Solomon 11:17]] — "created the world out of formless matter" (ἐξ ἀμόρφου ὕλης) — the "smoking gun"
- [[Romans 4.17|Romans 4:17]] — "calls into existence the things that do not exist" (retroactively reinterpreted)
- [[Hebrews 11.3|Hebrews 11:3]] — "what is seen was not made out of things that are visible" (retroactively reinterpreted)

## Key Concepts to Understand

- **οὐκ ἐξ ὄντων vs. ἐξ οὐκ ὄντων** — "not out of existing things" vs. "out of things that are not" — the linguistic marker of the doctrinal shift
- **τὸ μὴ ὄν (to mē on)** — "non-being" in Greek philosophy = formless matter, not absolute nothingness
- **_Ex nihilo nihil fit_** — "Nothing comes from nothing" — the philosophical axiom the doctrine had to overcome (see [[Parmenides]])
- **_Tohu wa-bohu_** — "Formless and void" (Genesis 1:2) — pre-existent chaos, not nothing
- **Middle Platonism** — The philosophical framework of Philo and Hellenistic Judaism; dualism of active God + passive matter
- **Wisdom of Solomon 11:17** — The "smoking gun" verse: creation "out of formless matter" (ἐξ ἀμόρφου ὕλης), see [[Wisdom 11.17|Wisdom 11:17]]
- **Retroactive reinterpretation** — The originators of creation ex nihilo reread earlier texts through the new lens; see [[Retroactive Reinterpretation of Creation Texts]]

## Key Ancient Philosophers (for context)

- [[Aristotle]] — *Physics* 1.8: "non-being to being" = privation of form, not creation from nothing
- [[Plato]] — *Timaeus*: Demiurge orders pre-existent matter; the standard cosmogonic model
- [[Parmenides]] — Formulated *ex nihilo nihil fit*; the axiom the doctrine had to overcome
- [[Origen]] — 3rd-century church father; read 2 Macc 7:28 as ex nihilo anachronistically
- [[Celsus]] — Pagan critic whose mockery of the resurrection drove ex nihilo's development
