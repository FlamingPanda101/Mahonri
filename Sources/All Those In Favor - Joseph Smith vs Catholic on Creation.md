# All Those In Favor — Joseph Smith vs Catholic on Creation

A Latter-day Saint reaction video in which hosts Baylor McKay Johnson and Kaden Alvie respond to Catholic apologist Noah Biser's charitable critique of the LDS view of creation, defending [[Creatio Ex Nihilo Is the Lynchpin Difference|creation *ex materia*]] (organizing pre-existing chaos) against [[Creation Ex Nihilo Is Not Biblical|creation *ex nihilo*]] on scriptural, scholarly, and philosophical grounds.

## Summary
- **The disagreement is *ex nihilo* vs. *ex materia*.** The hosts affirm that, unlike most caricatures of Mormonism, this one is accurate: the LDS God *organized* eternal, unformed "waters of chaos" into a formed cosmos rather than calling matter from nothing — and they thank Biser for stating their view in terms they accept [^1]. (See [[Creatio Ex Nihilo Is the Lynchpin Difference]], [[Greco-Roman Jewish Creation View Was Material Substrate]].)
- **"Uncreated elements" means only the *most fundamental* substrate.** Responding to Joseph Smith's claim that "the elements are uncreated," Kaden argues "element" denotes whatever is physically most fundamental, so most observable things may count as created — a reading he says is unremarkable in cosmology, citing [[J.P. Moreland]]'s "quantum foam" before the Big Bang [^2]. (NEW: [[The Uncreated Element Is Only the Most Fundamental Substrate]].)
- **Genesis 1:1–2 and the "waters" favor creation from chaos.** Biser concedes the Hebrew of [[Genesis 1.1|Genesis 1:1]] permits a "formed-from-unformed" reading; the hosts press that the pre-existing "waters" the Spirit hovers over are most plausibly primordial matter, and cite a scholarly compilation (Jackson Washburn) against *ex nihilo* in Genesis [^3]. (See [[Genesis 1 Creation Is Ordering and Separation Not Ex Nihilo]], [[Ruach Elohim - The Feminine Spirit Hovering Over Creation]].)
- **Alma 32's "light is discernible" alludes to Genesis 1.** Citing [[David Bokovoy]] (2014), Kaden argues [[Alma 32.27-43|Alma 32]]'s logic that "whatsoever is light… is discernible" echoes Genesis 1's making of unformed into formed (light as the first discernible thing) — sophisticated creation theology woven into the Book of Mormon [^4]. (NEW: [[Alma 32 Light Alludes to Genesis 1 Creation]].)
- **[[2 Peter 3.5-6|2 Peter 3:5–6]] teaches an earth "formed out of water."** The hosts add 2 Peter 3:5–6 — heavens of old and an earth "formed out of water and by means of water" — as a New Testament proof text for creation from a watery substrate [^5].
- **The deuterocanon is not univocal on *ex nihilo*.** Biser's strongest text is [[2 Maccabees 7.28|2 Maccabees 7:28]] ("did not make them out of things that existed"); the hosts answer (a) scholars read it within a Greco-Roman matter/form framework, not *ex nihilo* (Gerhard May; Jonathan Goldstein, whose opponent conceded the point — see [[2 Maccabees 7.28 Does Not Teach Creation Ex Nihilo]]), citing [[Xenophon]] using identical "creating children" language; and (b) the same canon contradicts the reading, since [[Wisdom 11.17|Wisdom 11:17]] says God "created the world out of formless matter" [^6]. (See [[Creation Ex Nihilo Originated Late 2nd Century CE]], [[Pan-Canonical Univocality Is Invalid Interpretation]].)
- **"Semantic gerrymandering."** Against the philosophical charge that a God with co-eternal matter "isn't truly God," Baylor coins **semantic gerrymandering**: redrawing the definition of "God" to exclude opponents and guarantee a win, paralleling "God by definition doesn't damn people to hell, so you don't believe in God" [^7]. (NEW: [[Semantic Gerrymandering Redefines God to Exclude Pre-Existing Matter]].)
- **Classical attributes are a Greek overlay, not the scriptural God.** The hosts grant that LDS theology is incompatible with the God of post-230 AD Thomistic/Aristotelian metaphysics — and treat that fusion of philosophy with revelation as the fruit of the [[Great Apostasy]]; they note even Aristotle did not teach creation *ex nihilo* [^8]. (See [[Classical Theism Cannot Be Reconciled With the Bible]].)
- **The [[King Follett Discourse]] is not the doctrinal hinge Biser thinks.** On "God himself was once as we are," the hosts reply it is a non-canonical, historically contested, multiply-transmitted sermon; that the Father's becoming man is meant "in the same sense Jesus was a man" (the incarnation itself arguably involves change); and that the *doctrine* of the Church — taught "frequently and by many" per Elder Anderson — is simply that the Father has a body [^9]. (See [[King Follett Need Not Be Canonized]], [[King Follett Discourse Publication History]], [[King Follett Is Arrangement Not New Substance]].)
- **God's unchangeableness is settled by canon, not King Follett.** Over against the "passive potency / God is changing" charge, Kaden goes to [[Moroni 8.18|Moroni 8:18]] — "God… is not a changeable being… unchangeable from all eternity to all eternity" — as the true-blue Book of Mormon statement [^10]. (See [[Intelligence Is Uncreated and Eternally Has Agency]].)
