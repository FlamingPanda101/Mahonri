# Thoughtful Faith — The LDS Godhead in the Bible (Response to Sam Shamoun)

[[Jacob Hansen]] (host of *Thoughtful Faith*) and LDS philosopher [[Blake Ostler]] respond to a video by [[Sam Shamoun]] and [[Cameron Bertuzzi]] that critiqued an earlier Hansen–Ostler conversation on the Trinity. Shamoun's challenge — *"Prove to me your Mormon gods from the Bible"* — frames the whole episode. [^1] Rather than rebut Shamoun line by line, Ostler walks a chain of New Testament texts (John 17, Philippians 2, 1 Corinthians 8 and 15, Daniel 7, Revelation 3, John 1, Colossians, 1 John 3, Hebrews 1) to argue that the Bible itself teaches an LDS-style Godhead: distinct divine persons united by an *indwelling glory* (the *pleroma*), with the Father as the "only true God" who confers the divine name, kingdom, and glory on the Son — a glory the Son in turn shares with his disciples.

- **Ostler's central methodological charge is that Shamoun proof-texts under an assumption of biblical inerrancy and univocality ("pan-canonical" interpretation), refusing all biblical scholarship and reading Protestant categories back into the ancient Near Eastern text.** He cites [[Mark Smith]] (past president of the Catholic Biblical Association) as a committed Christian scholar Shamoun dismisses as a "liberal." [^2][^3] See [[Pan-Canonical Univocality Is Invalid Interpretation]], [[Apologetics vs Critical Scholarship]].
- **He builds the Godhead from John 17: the oneness Jesus prays for is *indwelling/inherence*, never *homoousios* — the same oneness extended to the disciples.** The Father is the "only true God" (*ho theos*), and the Son is *sent*, *given* a name, and *given back* the glory he had "before the world began." [^4][^5] See [[John 17 Oneness Is Indwelling Not Consubstantiality]], [[The Only True God Is the Father (One God One Lord)]].
- **Philippians 2 is read as kenosis** — the pre-existent Son "emptied himself" (*kenoo*) of full divinity to become a servant, then was *exalted* and *given the name above every name* (originally Yahweh) — which Ostler argues is impossible on classical immutability and exposes the [[The Hypostatic Union Atonement Conundrum]] (only God can atone, yet God cannot suffer or die). [^6][^7] See [[Philippians 2 Kenosis - The Son Emptied Himself of Divinity]], [[The Divine Name Yahweh Was Given to Jesus]].
- **A throne/enthronement motif ties Daniel 7, 1 Corinthians 15, Hebrews 1, and Revelation 3 together:** the Son of Man approaches the Ancient of Days, receives kingdom and dominion, subdues all enemies, then *hands the kingdom back to the Father* (1 Cor 15:24-28) and is enthroned at God's right hand — and the *same* throne and glory are offered to the victorious disciple (Rev 3:21). [^8][^9] See [[Daniel 7 Son of Man Is Enthroned by the Ancient of Days]], [[1 Corinthians 15 Christ Hands the Kingdom Back to the Father]], [[Disciples Share the Throne and Divine Glory]].
- **On the Trinity proper, Ostler concedes the Nicene Creed is mostly acceptable to Latter-day Saints; the only two things LDS reject are *creatio ex nihilo* and *homoousios* (consubstantiality), neither of which is in the Bible.** He notes the Arian majority before Nicaea, the political character of the councils, that no one must understand the Trinity to be saved, and that creedalism replaced relationship with theology. [^10][^11] See [[The Trinity and Consubstantiality Are Not in the Bible]], [[Nicaea Did Not Settle the Trinity]], [[Understanding the Trinity Is Not Required for Salvation]], [[Creedalism Replaced Relationship With Theology]].
