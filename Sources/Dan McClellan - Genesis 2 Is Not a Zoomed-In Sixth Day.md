# Dan McClellan — Genesis 2 Is Not a Zoomed-In Sixth Day

- **The core thesis:** There is no plausible case that the second creation account ([[Genesis 2.4-7|Genesis 2:4b]]–end of Genesis 3) is a "zoomed-in" version of the sixth day of [[Genesis 1.1|Genesis 1]]. Saying so does not resolve multiple direct contradictions between two very different accounts, and the majority of scholars agree these were two independent, originally distinct creation accounts.[^1] See [[Genesis 2 Is a Zoomed-In Sixth Day - Rebutted]], [[Two Independent Creation Accounts in Genesis]].
- **Order of composition:** The account in Genesis 2:4b–3 is almost certainly the *earlier* of the two; the Priestly account (Genesis 1:1–2:4a) was composed later, in response to and as a correction of things the Priestly authors disliked about the earlier account.[^2] See [[Priestly Account Is Later and Corrective]].
- **Plants contradiction:** Genesis 2:5–7 describes an earth with neither wild nor cultivated vegetation — no rain, no human to till — and the human ([[Genesis 2.4-7|Genesis 2:7]]) is created *before* any plant life. This directly contradicts [[Genesis 1.11-13|Genesis 1]], where all vegetation is created on day three, three days before humanity.[^3] See [[Humanity Created Before Plants in Genesis 2]], [[Siach and Eseb - Wild vs Cultivated Vegetation]].
- **Animals contradiction:** In Genesis 2:18–19 God forms every animal of the field and every bird of the air *after* the human, seeking a helper. This contradicts [[Genesis 1.24-26|Genesis 1:24–26]], where animals are made on day six *before* humanity, and fish and birds on earlier days.[^4] See [[Humanity Created Before Animals in Genesis 2]], [[Sequence of Creation Differs Between Genesis 1 and 2]].
- **Method / meta-critique:** The two passages "are only harmonizable if your need to harmonize them overrules your ability to think critically about them. It's just putting dogma over data." The differing-but-overlapping terminology is itself evidence of two authors.[^5] McClellan recommends Carr (2021), Gertz (2023), Hendel (2024), and Petersen (2025) for the underlying scholarship.[^6] See [[Harmonization Overrides Critical Thinking - Dogma Over Data]], [[Vocabulary Differences Signal Two Authors]].
