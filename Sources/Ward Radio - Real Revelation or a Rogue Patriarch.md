# Ward Radio — Real Revelation or a Rogue Patriarch

A long-form [[Ward Radio]] episode hosted by [[Cardon Ellis]] with [[Jonah Barnes]] ("associate professor of all things apocryphal"), [[Brad Whitbeck]], and [[Oak Norton]]. Barnes recounts a firsthand mission story from Siberia in which a traveling patriarch began assigning Russian converts to *unusual* tribes of Israel (Gad, Reuben, Naphtali, Simeon), a second vetting patriarch independently found the same pattern, and [[Russell M. Nelson]] reportedly flew to Siberia in 2004 to gather the saints by tribe — which the hosts read as a literal first-fruits of the gathering of the [[The Gathering of the Lost Ten Tribes|lost ten tribes]], later "backed up" by a DNA-migration map. The bulk is **testimonial and new to the vault**; the DNA-as-disproof rebuttals overlap the existing DNA cluster.

## Summary

- **A traveling patriarch's unusual tribal assignments in Siberia, independently corroborated, are read as evidence of patriarchal revelation.** Barnes's mission-journal account: Elder Schütze gave patriarchal blessings naming rare tribes; a second patriarch (and BYU Russianist [[Gary Browning]]), sent to check without being told why, found the same; the Quorum of the Twelve investigated and [[Russell M. Nelson]] gathered Siberian members into their tribes in 2004 (6 of the "nine-and-a-half" tribes represented). See [[Patriarchal Blessings Reveal Tribal Lineage and Cannot Be Faked]] and [[The Gathering of the Lost Ten Tribes]]. [^1]
- **The patriarch is "the one calling you can't fake" — a costly, sanctified office.** Barnes, Oak Norton (whose father was a patriarch), and the hosts describe patriarchs (e.g. animator [[Richard Rich]]) stripping their lives down to prepare; the blessing physically drains the patriarch (compared to Sidney Rigdon collapsing after D&C 76). The argument: an administrative calling can be faked, but channeled revelation cannot. See [[Patriarchal Blessings Reveal Tribal Lineage and Cannot Be Faked]]. [^2]
- **A DNA-migration map is offered as later scientific confirmation of the gathering geography.** The hosts show a near-Eastern DNA-marker map converging on the Caucasus (north of the Euphrates, where Josephus last placed the ten tribes) then scattering west to Europe and east to Siberia — claiming the patriarchs identified the tribes in 2004 *before* the genetics, so science verified the patriarchs. The DNA point is impressionistic and not the same kind of claim as the population-genetics objection to Book of Mormon historicity. See [[Patriarchal Blessings Reveal Tribal Lineage and Cannot Be Faked]] and the existing DNA cluster, [[Genealogical Descent Is Not Genetic Descent]]. [^3]
