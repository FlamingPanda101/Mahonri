# Data Over Dogma — Why the Bible Began with Jacob Wright

- **The "Why" Question:** Wright argues biblical scholarship has neglected *why* the Bible came into existence — a messy philosophical question Wellhausen answered as a "transition to religion," which Wright contends misreads what actually emerged: a project of peoplehood, not religion.
- **Defeat + Division = Bible:** The two essential factors behind the Bible's formation are (1) military defeat (Assyrian conquest of Israel 722 BCE, Babylonian conquest of Judah ~586 BCE) and (2) the political division between Israel and Judah. Defeat is necessary but not sufficient — the North-South split is the other critical ingredient.
- **The Bible as Curriculum:** Wright's second major thesis is that the Bible is fundamentally a *pedagogical project* — an educational curriculum for forming a people without a kingdom, democratizing wisdom from elites to average families (Proverbs shifts from court tutor → biological mother and father).
