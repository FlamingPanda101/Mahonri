# The Stick of Joseph - King Josiah Deuteronomy and the Book of Mormon (Mike and Dave)

A "Reading Books with Mike and Dave" episode (hosted by Brad Whitbeck, with "pizza guy" Austin Falter) in which **[[Mike Day]]** and **[[Dave Butler]]** introduce **[[Moshe Weinfeld]]**'s classic *Deuteronomy and the Deuteronomic School* (1972) as the one book to read on the Deuteronomist reforms, then draw out its relevance for the Book of Mormon.

## Summary
- **Weinfeld's thesis, summarized:** Deuteronomy is the product of a *school* of scribes and lawyers active before Josiah and into the exile; its ideology is Yahweh-alone, Jerusalem-alone, "name theology" (God's *name*, not God, dwells in the temple), and it knows little of how sacrifice works. The school "invented" the Levites and secular slaughter and back-edited the histories (Joshua–Kings) to praise law-keeping kings. See [[Deuteronomistic Corruption of the Old Testament]] and [[Deuteronomy Was Patterned After the Vassal Treaty of Esarhaddon]].
- **Josiah re-evaluated.** Reading [[2 Kings 23.5|2 Kings 23]] and [[2 Chronicles 35.22-24|2 Chronicles 35]], the hosts argue Josiah burned rival priests on their own altars and may have been killed by his own archers; using the Book of Mormon as a lens, [[Laman]] and Lemuel emerge as *pious* Deuteronomists and Lehi as one of the hunted [[The Kemarim Were the Melchizedek Priests Josiah Purged|kemarim]]. They note Weinfeld dates the **synagogue's origin to the reform itself** (pre-Lehi). See [[The Synagogue Predates Lehi in the Deuteronomic Reform]].
- **The covenant/treaty pattern.** Day argues the vassal-treaty form Weinfeld finds in Deuteronomy (preamble, history, terms, witnesses, blessings/cursings, deposit) also structures King Benjamin's speech ([[Mosiah 1]]–6) and the 1842 temple endowment — suggesting the pattern is very old. See [[The Vassal-Treaty Covenant Pattern Links Deuteronomy King Benjamin and the Endowment]]. Nephi's "great and spacious building" is read as a *foreign* (not merely odd) building — see [[The Great and Spacious Building Is Apostate Temple Insiders Not Outsiders]].
