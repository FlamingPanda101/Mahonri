# Ancient Christians: An Introduction for Latter-day Saints

A Maxwell Institute (BYU, 2022) scholarly introduction to Christianity's first six centuries, written for Latter-day Saints. Each chapter surveys an ancient Christian belief/practice, drawing on patristic primary sources and material culture, and (where apt) connects to LDS thought and the Restoration's "primitive church" framing.

## Summary
- Frames early Christianity through the LDS lens of **apostasy and restoration** while treating ancient Christians as fellow disciples, not merely foils [^1].
- Surveys the development of **scripture/exegesis, canon, church organization (incl. women's roles), worship, the nature of God and humanity, atonement/grace, deification, work for the dead, the afterlife, and eschatology** across the patristic era.
- A B-tier scholarly corroborating source for the vault's [[Ward Radio - The Great Apostasy Deep Dive with Mike Day and Dave Butler|Great Apostasy]], Trinity-development, theosis, and canon material.
