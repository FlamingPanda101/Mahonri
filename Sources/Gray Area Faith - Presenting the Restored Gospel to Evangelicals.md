# Gray Area Faith - Presenting the Restored Gospel to Evangelicals

**Format:** Live presentation + Q&A | **Presenter:** [[Shiloh Lofgren]] (Gray Area Faith) | **Audience:** evangelical Christians | **Topic:** a friendly, common-ground exposition of Latter-day Saint faith

[[Shiloh Lofgren]] presents the restored gospel to a room of evangelicals, modeling an irenic apologetic method: open on **common ground** in Christ, defuse tension by playfully naming "heresy" together before each distinctive, and carefully distinguish **authoritative doctrine** from **opinion/speculation**, building from the audience's own King James Bible toward Latter-day Saint distinctives. He walks through the apostasy and restoration, priesthood authority, the Book of Mormon and its witnesses, the nature of God and man, the plan of salvation (premortal life, spirit world, three degrees of glory, baptism for the dead), soteriology, and the LDS view of scripture.

- The method itself — common ground, disarming humor, doctrine-vs-speculation labeling, the KJV as shared text — is the throughline. See [[Present the Restored Gospel by Starting on Common Ground]].
- **Apostasy/restoration**: the New Testament church of "apostles and prophets" ([[Ephesians 2.19-22|Eph 2:20]]) suffered a "falling away" ([[Acts 20.29-30|Acts 20:29]]); the Reformation signaled lost authority, answered by Joseph Smith's restoration. See [[Great Apostasy and Need for Restoration]] and [[Both Sides Overcorrected in the LDS-Evangelical Divide]].
- **Priesthood**: Aaronic and Melchizedek authority restored by Peter, James, and John. Cf. [[The Priesthood Is Authority to Administer Not a Power to Heal]].
- **Book of Mormon**: weighs in on infant baptism ([[Moroni 8.12-22|Moroni 8:10]]) and the felix culpa of the Fall ([[2 Nephi 2|2 Nephi 2:22-25]]); defended by the eight, three, and informal witnesses (Mary Whitmer). Cf. [[Informal Witnesses of the Golden Plates]] and [[Three vs Eight Witnesses Different Kinds of Experience]].
- **Nature of God and man**: rejects divine simplicity/impassibility (Westminster) in favor of a God with a glorified body and humans as "heirs of God" ([[Romans 8.14-17|Romans 8:17]]) growing into His attributes. Cf. [[Human Deification in Mormonism]].
- **Plan of salvation**: premortal life, an intermediate spirit world where the gospel is preached ([[1 Peter 3.18-20|1 Peter 3:19]]; [[1 Peter 4.6|1 Peter 4:6]]), baptism for the dead ([[1 Corinthians 15.29|1 Cor 15:29]]), and three degrees of glory (1 Cor 15:41 — sun, moon, stars). See [[Three Degrees of Glory in the Bible]] and [[Every Person Gets a Fair Opportunity Through Proxy Ordinances]].
- **Soteriology**: faith, repentance, baptism-covenant, enduring to the end, with Christ's grace "every step of the way" (Elder Christofferson). Cf. [[LDS Salvation Is by Grace Not Earned by Works]] and [[Works Are the Fruit of Faith Not the Price of Salvation]].
- **Scripture**: inspired but not infallible — even the Book of Mormon's title page concedes "the mistakes of men"; prophetic revelation is received much as ordinary members receive personal revelation, only with authority over the church. Cf. [[Fallible Humans Cannot Produce an Infallible Bible]].
- **Close**: the Book of Mormon as a *symbol/type of the resurrection* (twelve witnesses, a Mary, a stone box, buried then raised then taken up), which Lofgren credits with bringing him from near-atheism back to Christ.
