# Let's Talk w/ Hayden Carroll — Why Jesus Would Reject Protestantism

A ~50-minute single-presenter video in which [[Hayden Carroll]] argues that the Protestant tradition of *sola scriptura* ("scripture alone") is the **root** of every Latter-day Saint disagreement with evangelicals, and that, properly understood, Jesus and the biblical prophets never operated under it. Carroll first lets prominent online Protestants ([[James White]], Jeff Durbin, Mike Winger) and Catholic apologists ([[Trent Horn]], [[Pat Madrid]]) define and critique the position in their own words, then dismantles it point by point. The video closes with an extended clip of [[Jeffrey R. Holland]]'s general-conference sermon "My Words… Never Cease" defending continuing revelation and the open LDS canon. It does **not** substantially treat *sola fide* / grace-vs-works despite the framing; the entire body is an attack on the Protestant rule of faith.[^1]

## Summary
Carroll structures the case around the **five foundational tenets** of *sola scriptura* he says Protestants hold, arguing that if even one is false the whole position collapses — and that all five fail:[^2]

- **(1) Scripture is "God-breathed" and therefore infallible.** Carroll attacks the inference from θεόπνευστος (*theopneustos*, [[2 Timothy 3.16-17|2 Tim 3:16]]). Following the Protestant scholar D.A. Carson, he argues the word's meaning cannot be read off its parts (the *root fallacy*) and that it is a *hapax legomenon*, so "infallible" is an added inference; the *active* sense "God-breathing / life-giving" (cf. [[Genesis 2.7|Gen 2:7]], God breathing life into Adam) is grammatically available. He also argues a *fallible* human cannot perform an *infallible* action. See the new [[The Root Fallacy - Theopneustos Parts Do Not Yield Infallible]] and the existing [[Theopneustos Means Life-Giving Not Infallible]].

- **(2) The 66 books are the final authority.** "Where does the Bible say that the Bible is the final authority? It doesn't." The canon was compiled *after* the last book was written, so the Bible "could not refer to the Bible"; Protestants simply *presuppose* the canon. Carroll deploys [[Frank Turek]]'s law-of-non-contradiction move ("is it *true* that there's no truth?") against the claim. See [[Sola Scriptura - Scripture Self-Attests Its Authority]], [[The Canon Premise Is an Infallible Rule Outside Scripture]], [[Canon Cannot Self-Reference - Compilation Postdates the Books]].

- **(3) Scripture contains all that is necessary for salvation; nothing may be added (closed canon).** Quoting the Westminster Confession ("nothing at any time is to be added, whether by new revelations of the spirit"), Carroll argues this would reject *God himself* if he spoke again — the essence of the LDS "apostasy" charge. He presses the *canon catch-22* (you can appeal neither to the Bible nor to anything outside it without making that source higher), citing [[Pat Madrid]], [[Trent Horn]], [[Gavin Ortlund]], Doug Wilson's "no doctrine of the table of contents," and [[R.C. Sproul]]'s "a fallible list of infallible books." He rebuts [[Hebrews 1.1-2|Heb 1:1]] and [[Revelation 22.18-19|Rev 22]] as closing-the-canon prooftexts. See [[A Fallible List of Infallible Teachings - The Symmetry of Uncertainty]], [[Closing the Canon Was Used to Declare Prophecy Ended]], [[Hebrews 1-1-2 - Jesus the Last Prophet Rebutted]], [[The Three Unjustified Protestant Canon Presuppositions]].

- **(4) Scripture is clear (perspicuous) on the essentials.** Carroll asks *which* doctrines are essential and *who* decides, using a long [[Gavin Ortlund]] clip in which Ortlund concedes there is no Protestant list of essential doctrines. If scripture were clear on essentials, "why has no Protestant church since the Reformation compiled the comprehensive list?" See [[Perspicuity Fails the Hard-Cases Test]].

- **(5) Scripture interprets scripture.** Carroll argues a text cannot interpret itself and needs a living *adjudicator*, analogizing to the U.S. Supreme Court, a judge ruling on a "borrowed" lawnmower, and a gym owner's authority over a policy handbook — the absence of such an authority being why "hundreds of Protestant denominations" exist. See the new [[Scripture Cannot Interpret Itself - The Supreme Court Analogy]] and the existing [[No Final Interpreter - The Protestant Adjudication Problem]].

- **Positive LDS case.** The remedy is *continuing revelation* through "living oracles." Carroll invokes [[2 Peter 3.15-16|2 Peter]]'s "no private interpretation," the pattern of each prophet adding scripture (Moses → Isaiah → Jeremiah…), and a long [[Jeffrey R. Holland]] sermon citing [[N.T. Wright]] (authority belongs to *Jesus*, not "the books you are going to write") to argue scripture "points away from itself toward the fact that final true authority belongs to God himself." See [[God Himself Not a Book Is the Ultimate Authority]], [[A Living Canon Is a Feature Not a Bug]], [[Sola Scriptura Muzzles God]].
