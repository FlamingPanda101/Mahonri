# Ward Radio - Brave Academic Admits Book of Mormon Premise is Possible

Host [[Cardon Ellis]] with [[Jerry Grover]] and [[Josh Gailey]] react to a Great Courses clip in which mainstream Mesoamerican archaeologist [[Ed Barnhart]] concedes that some Olmec art at La Venta is "hard to otherwise explain" than by Mediterranean (Phoenician) contact.

## Summary
- **The academic concession.** Barnhart catalogs La Venta's bearded, turbaned figures and a non-Mesoamerican clay bust, notes Phoenicians were the era's best sailors (1500–300 BC), and admits "sometimes we don't have a better explanation at the moment" — while disavowing endorsement ("don't shoot the messenger"). The hosts read this as academia being "forced" to entertain transoceanic contact. See [[Olmec Bearded Figures and an Academic Concession of Possible Transoceanic Contact]].
- **Grover's Mulek correlation.** Grover places [[John Sorenson]]'s Mesoamerican Zarahemla model near La Venta, reads La Venta Monument 13's glyphs as a date (~531/511 BC) for a mature bearded foreigner's arrival, and ties the "walking fish" *vizier* glyph to [[Mulek]] (whose name he finds in the [[The Characters Document Translated as Abbreviated Hieratic and Maya|characters document]]). The purple-dye (murex/snail) parallel and the [[Chiapa de Corzo cylinder seal]] add trace Egyptian evidence.
- **Method note.** The hosts argue archaeology has "no metric" to accept transoceanic contact short of overwhelming genetic impact, so honest anomalies get suppressed — a sociology-of-academia complaint rather than a positive proof.
