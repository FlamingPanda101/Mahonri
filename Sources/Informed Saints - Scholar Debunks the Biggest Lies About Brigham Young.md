# Informed Saints - Scholar Debunks the Biggest Lies About Brigham Young

**Channel:** Informed Saints | **Guest:** Dr. [[Daniel C. Peterson]] | **Topic:** the reputation and historical misconceptions of [[Brigham Young]]

A long-form interview in which Dr. Daniel C. Peterson — historian of religions and producer of the Interpreter Foundation's *Becoming Brigham* documentary series — works through the most common "lies" and caricatures about Brigham Young: the sensual polygamist, the iron-fisted theocratic tyrant, and the murderer (of both the Fancher party at Mountain Meadows and of Joseph Smith). Peterson's recurring method is to set the popular caricature against the primary sources and the testimony of people who actually knew Brigham, recovering a more multidimensional, often kindly figure without whitewashing his genuine rough edges.

## Summary

- **The harem caricature is false.** Peterson notes that 19th-century (and some present-day Christian) critics compared Brigham to a Turkish sultan with a "seraglio," a comparison he calls explicitly racist. Many of Brigham's plural marriages were status-conferring marriages to women who lacked legal standing on the frontier; the celebrated apostate Ann Eliza Webb (whom Hugh Nibley dismantled in *Sounding Brass*) was, in Peterson's reading, a "grifter."[^1] The traveler Richard Burton — who knew real Middle Eastern polygamy and visited Utah hoping for sensuality — found Brigham's household "downright Victorian" and saw no resemblance at all.[^2] See [[Brigham Young's Polygamy Was Not a Sensual Harem]].
- **The "Cowboy Hitler" autocrat caricature is false.** Peterson argues Brigham was wittier and kinder than his reputation ("Well, don't go"; patting Mark Twain on the head). His will was repeatedly *not* enforced even on Church-owned buildings (the squat St. George Temple tower he hated; the compromise pulpit-stairs of the St. George Tabernacle), and the territorial legislature genuinely debated and overrode him — Orson Pratt opposed his slavery bill, per Paul Reeve and W. Jean Garrard's *This Abominable Slavery*.[^3] See [[Brigham Young Was Not the Autocrat of Utah]].
- **He did not order the Mountain Meadows Massacre.** The premise that "nothing in the territory happened without his decree" is false; his express rider's order to leave the Fancher train alone arrived too late. Peterson's argument: even granting (for the sake of argument) that Brigham was evil, no one ever accused him of being *stupid*, and the massacre was a stupid, ruinous disaster he was horrified by.[^4] See [[Brigham Young Did Not Order the Mountain Meadows Massacre]].
- **The theory that Brigham orchestrated Joseph Smith's murder is a "nutcake theory."** It rests on dubious "ballistic evidence" from an 1844 killing; it contradicts Brigham's total, documented loyalty to Joseph (the Far West cornerstone; "Joseph, what would you have me do?"; dying words "Joseph, Joseph, Joseph"); and the actual killers (Thomas C. Sharp et al.) publicly boasted of the deed and never named Brigham at trial.[^5] See [[The Brigham-Killed-Joseph-Smith Theory Is a Nutcake Theory]].
- **The Danite death-squad trope is anti-Mormon fiction.** The image of throat-slitting Danites enforcing Brigham's will collapses under its own evidence (alleged "blood-atonement victims" turn up alive in California years later); Ann Eliza only "heard" Danite hoofbeats *in her mind* after crossing into Wyoming. Craig Foster's study finds Utah under Brigham no more violent than other frontier territories.[^6] See [[The Danites Slitting Throats Trope Is Anti-Mormon Fiction]].
- **Why have faith in Brigham as a prophet:** he was essential to the Church's survival. Had Sidney Rigdon prevailed (without the priesthood keys, uncommitted to temples or the gathering), the result would have been "a kind of odd Protestantism with an extra book." The priesthood line of nearly every modern leader — "you don't get Russell M. Nelson without Brigham Young" — runs through him, and he standardized the temple endowment.[^7] See the existing notes [[Brigham Young Saved the Church Through the Keys]] and [[Brigham Young as the Fall Guy]].
