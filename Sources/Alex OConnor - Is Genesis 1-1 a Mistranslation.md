# Alex O'Connor — Is Genesis 1:1 A Mistranslation? @_magnify

A long-form conversation in which atheist YouTuber [[Alex OConnor|Alex O'Connor]] (CosmicSkeptic) interviews **[[Christopher Magnify]]** (@_magnify), a biblical-languages shorts creator, on the claim that the very first verse of the Bible is so heavily filtered through later languages and philosophies that the English reader is "getting the tip of the iceberg."[^1] Magnify itemizes roughly **seven distinct translation problems** in Genesis 1:1 — things English *adds*, things it *omits*, and matters of vocabulary that drift over time.[^1] The episode's running thesis is that translation is "the importation of philosophy into the text without realizing it," each layer (Greek, then Germanic) a "philosophical palimpsest" stacked on the Hebrew.[^1]

Most of the grammatical heart of the conversation — that the verse may read *"When God began to create"*, that *tohu wa-bohu* is pre-existing chaos rather than a product of creation, and that this favors ordering pre-existent matter over *creatio ex nihilo* — restates material already developed at length in the vault's ex-materia cluster: see [[Bereshit Is a Construct Form Forcing a Dependent-Clause Reading]], [[Genesis 1 Creation Is Ordering and Separation Not Ex Nihilo]], [[The Septuagint Rendered Tohu wa-Bohu With Plato's Term for the Unseen]], and [[Creation Ex Nihilo Is Not Biblical]]. O'Connor frames the pre-existing-matter reading through his own **mereological nihilism** (objects are just arrangements of matter that minds label), which he says first attracted him to this interpretation in a 2020 exchange with [[William Lane Craig]].[^1] What is *genuinely new* here are two specifics absent from that cluster, developed in their own notes.

## Summary

- **Periods and pace (style, not doctrine).** Ancient Hebrew has no punctuation; inserting full stops after each clause "removes the information that this is an event happening rapidly." Magnify and O'Connor invoke translator **Robert Alter**, who argues the original reads like "an explosion" — Genesis 1's first verses meant to be read "in one breath" — and that style is "a vital medium through which ideas are communicated," not mere embellishment.[^1] Alter renders *tohu wa-bohu* as the sing-song "**welter and waste**" rather than the "stately" *formless and void*.[^1]
- **"In the beginning" vs. "When God began to create" (already in the vault).** The second word is a verb with no tense in ancient Hebrew; read as an infinitive ("in the beginning of God's creating") the first "panel" of the comic-strip already contains primordial material, and v. 2's shapeless earth and waters confirm stuff was "already there." Restates [[Bereshit Is a Construct Form Forcing a Dependent-Clause Reading]] and [[Genesis 1 Creation Is Ordering and Separation Not Ex Nihilo]].
- **Greek encroachment on *tohu wa-bohu* (already in the vault).** Magnify calls the Hebrew a made-up, "Helter Skelter" / sing-song word used only one other time in the Bible; "form and formlessness" is "a Greek encroachment," a philosophical category imported by the translators. Overlaps [[The Septuagint Rendered Tohu wa-Bohu With Plato's Term for the Unseen]].
- **The two untranslated words: *et* (NEW).** Of the seven Hebrew words in the verse, two — the direct-object marker אֵת (*et*, "aleph-to-tav," like "A to Z") before *heavens* and again before *earth* — are simply dropped in English; mystics read it as a ceremonial word to "remember the universe of things you are calling that object out from." Developed in [[The Untranslated Et Direct-Object Marker in Genesis 1.1]].
- **Lost grammatical gender (NEW).** Hebrew nouns are masculine or feminine with no neuter; the third day shows "masculine grass and trees coming from the feminine Earth," meaning the English reader loses gendered information that fundamentalists nonetheless claim grounds a "biblical view of gender." Developed in [[Hebrew Grammatical Gender Is Lost in Translation Undercutting a Biblical View of Gender]].
- **Capitalization as imported philosophy.** Capital "God" and sentence-initial capitals carry over from a Germanic "worldview that nouns have priority in the universe," whereas ancient Hebrew prioritizes **verbs** (verb precedes subject) — "not the biblical worldview."[^1]
- **"Heavens and the earth" is a merism.** A figure of speech (like "high and low") meaning *everything*, not a sequence of two created items — reinforcing "when God started to create everything."[^1]
- **Eve from Adam's "rib" / "helper" (parallel mistranslations).** Later in Genesis, *tsela* means a "side/half," not a specific rib bone (Hebrew lacks a word for an individual rib); and Eve as Adam's "helper" understates a Hebrew term closer to "rescuer." Magnify argues both are softened in ways that "minimize the role of Eve."[^1]
- **English as a mercantile language.** O'Connor and Magnify riff that English evolved to "ease commerce," saturating biblical God-talk with financial metaphor (debt, account, the price paid, being "saved"), a frame alien to nomadic Israel.[^1]
