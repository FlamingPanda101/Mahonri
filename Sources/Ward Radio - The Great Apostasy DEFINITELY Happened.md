# Ward Radio — The Great Apostasy DEFINITELY Happened (feat. Jacob Hansen)

A Ward Radio episode in which host **[[Cardon Ellis]]**, with **[[Jacob Hansen]]**, **[[Jonah Barnes]]**, and **[[Austin Falter]]**, argues that the Great Apostasy "definitely happened." Jacob's organizing question: *why does the God of Aristotle sound exactly like the God of the creeds?*

## Summary
- **The Aristotle test.** Aristotle's God is an unmoved mover, pure actuality, "timeless, spaceless, immaterial, without passions, without parts, without change" — which is the language of the Athanasian Creed ("without body, parts, or passions"). The biblical God, by contrast, is embodied (the risen Christ) and passionate (grieves, relents, smells the sacrifice). The creedal God is the Greek philosophers' God, not the Bible's. This corroborates the existing [[Unmoved Mover Reshaped God the Father]] (from a different Ward Radio episode).
- **Perfect-being theology runs backwards.** The creedal method *starts* from "that than which no greater can be conceived" (Greek), reasons to an immaterial/timeless/spaceless being, *then* forces the Bible to fit ("perfect being theology"). Augustine openly said he was making Christianity palatable to Greeks. See [[Perfect-Being Theology Reads the Bible Through a Greek Lens]].
- **Loss of central authority → factionalism.** While the apostles lived they adjudicated doctrine (Acts; Peter and the unclean foods); when they died there was no central authority, so the "great christological debates" fractured the church — and Paul's epistles are pre-emptive damage control against creeping false teaching ("let no man deceive you… there will be a falling away," 2 Thess 2:3, which uses the word *apostasia*). See [[Loss of Apostolic Authority Caused the Christological Factions]].
- **Constantine standardized by force.** Constantine adopts Christianity, finds the factions warring, convenes the largest faction, and the **Nicene Creed** draws the line: align or you are not a Christian — enforced by the *state* for ~1500 years. The trinitarian result is "incoherent" and survived "not because people were convinced but because the church and governments that owned it took over the world." See [[The Creeds Won by State Power Not Persuasion]].
- **Posthumous excommunication.** Jacob/Cardon claim the creedal church *posthumously excommunicated* early fathers (Origen) who predated and disagreed with the creeds — coercion, not argument. (The vault treats the related power-struggle in [[Canon Formation Was a Power Struggle Over Authority]] and [[The Two Great Wrong Turns - Overthrow of Origen and Pelagius]].)
- **The Ordo Romanus Primus anecdote.** When Rome's authorities reached outlying communities still practicing pre-creedal "quote-Mormon-unquote" rites (a washing/anointing tunic of immortality), the locals said "this is just what Jesus told us to do," and the Roman record faults them for "following custom rather than reason."
- **Payoff.** If the apostasy is real, it strips the "Mormons aren't real Christians" weapon — "better to be a heretic than to be wrong."
