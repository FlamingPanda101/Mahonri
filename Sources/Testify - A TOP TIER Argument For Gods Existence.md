# Testify — A TOP TIER Argument For God's Existence

A Protestant apologetics presentation of the **fine-tuning argument** and rebuttals to its main objections.

- **Fine-tuning: the universe's constants are improbably life-permitting, which points to design** (gravitational constant to 1 in 10^40; cosmological constant to 1 in 10^120; strong nuclear and electromagnetic forces). See [[Cosmic Fine-Tuning of the Constants Points to a Designer (Testify)]].
- **The anthropic objection proves too much** — the firing-squad and "Doug the puddle" analogies show that "we couldn't observe a dead universe" does not dissolve the need to explain our improbable survival. See [[The Anthropic and Puddle Objections to Fine-Tuning Prove Too Much]].
- **The multiverse merely relocates the problem and lacks evidence** — a universe-generator would itself need fine-tuning, so the move only "kicks the can down the road." See [[The Multiverse Reply to Fine-Tuning Relocates the Problem (Testify)]].
- **God-of-the-gaps and "hostile universe" objections fail; a Bayesian treatment favors design** — even a tiny prior for a designer is boosted by the fine-tuning evidence to a high posterior. See [[Fine-Tuning Is a Bayesian Inference to Design Not a God of the Gaps]].

This is a Protestant source making a natural-theology argument; the concept notes carry an *Apologetic Engagement* + *LDS Relevance* treatment, since the design inference is shared ground that the LDS tradition frames as a cosmos *organized* by God.
