# Let's Talk w/ Hayden Carroll — The Triune God Is Omnibenevolent | Live Debate

A ~90-minute formal moderated debate on the resolution: **"The triune God is omnibenevolent"** — where *triune God* = the God affirmed by Catholics and Protestants, and *omnibenevolent* = "without exception willing the good of every moral subject." **Gavin** (Roman Catholic) argues the **affirmative**; **[[Hayden Carroll]]** (Latter-day Saint, host) argues the **negative**. Chris moderates. Format: 15-min openings, 10-min rebuttals, 15-min cross-examinations, 5-min closings.[^1]

## Summary
- **Gavin's affirmative case** is threefold: (1) omnibenevolence follows necessarily from God's nature as *triune love* — [[1 John 4.8|God *is* love]], which is only intelligible if God is eternally relational; (2) scripture *explicitly* affirms God's universal salvific will ([[1 Timothy 2.4|1 Tim 2:4]], [[2 Peter 3.9|2 Pet 3:9]], [[Matthew 5.45|Matt 5:45]], [[John 12.32|John 12:32]]); (3) the triune God's benevolence does not collapse into coercion, favoritism, or "conditional access." His grace is unconditional ([[Romans 5.8|Rom 5:8]], "while we were yet sinners") and access is "given downward, not earned upward." [^2] See [[Omnibenevolence Flows from the Triune God as Eternal Love]], [[Scripture Explicitly Affirms Universal Salvific Will]], and [[Omnibenevolence vs Conditional Access Models]].
- **Hayden's negative case** rests on a single argument he calls **the creation dilemma**: God foreknows who will be eternally damned; God is free to refrain from creating them and would lose nothing; God creates them anyway; yet God is said to will their good. These cannot all be true. He formalizes it as [[The Five Incompatible Claims of Creedal Theism|five incompatible creedal claims]] (omniscience, free omnipotence, creation ex nihilo, eternal hell, willing-all-good) with "no sixth option."[^3] See [[The Creation Dilemma]].
- Hayden frames the whole debate as an **internal-coherence test**, not an attack on theism or a defense of Mormonism: "my purpose is not contempt but coherence." He concedes the biblical God and even free will — the dilemma bites *before* freedom, at the creation decision. See [[The Debate Is About Coherence Not the LDS God]], [[The First Morally Decisive Moment Is Creation Not Rebellion]], and [[Free Will Is a Red Herring to the Creation Dilemma]].
- Hayden pre-rebuts the standard defenses: **free will** (red herring — creation is prior), **greater good** ([[The Greater Good Defense Instrumentalizes Persons|utilitarian instrumentalizing]]), **mere permission** ([[Foreknowledge Plus Power Turns Permission Into Choice|foreknowledge + power = choice]], "the button was still pressed"), and **mystery** ([[The Appeal to Mystery Empties the Word Good of Meaning|the medicine analogy]] — redefining "good" makes it incoherent, not mysterious). His scriptural anchor is [[Matthew 26.24|Matthew 26:24]] ("better for Judas if he had not been born") — see [[The Judas Counterexample]].
- **Cross-examination:** Gavin spends his round attacking the *LDS* God's "conditional access" / "bound" covenant model ([[D&C 82.10|D&C 82:10]], [[D&C 76]]) — a [[Conditional Access Tu Quoque Against the LDS God|tu quoque]] Hayden flags as off-resolution. Hayden's round methodically locks in each creedal premise and drives Gavin to **mystery** and **prophetic necessity** ([[Appeal to Prophetic Necessity Rebutted]]) as his only answers to Judas, and extracts the admission that God could have created [[God Could Have Created Only the Saved|only the saved]] (Mary as a sinless free agent). A muddled exchange on [[Who Creates the Soul - Humans or God|who creates the soul]] ends with Gavin conceding God freely creates.
- **Gavin's defensive line:** [[Justice Does Not Negate Omnibenevolence|justice does not negate benevolence]] and [[Benevolence Requires a System of Justice|benevolence requires a system of justice]] — to which Hayden replies the resolution is about *omnibenevolence*, not justice, and justice toward the guilty cannot explain *creating* the foreknown-damned. Gavin's foreknowledge model is [[Divine Foreknowledge as Real-Time Branching|"real-time branching"]] (the *Adjustment Bureau* illustration), which Hayden finds incompatible with absolute foreknowledge.
