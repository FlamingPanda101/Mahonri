# The Stick of Joseph — OBVIOUS Signs That Christ Was Edited Out of the Old Testament

LDS apologetic interview on the [[Stick of Joseph]] channel featuring guest [[Jonah Barnes]] (Plain and Precious Publishing, author of *Key to the Keystone*). The episode argues that the explicit, pre-exilic preaching of a coming, dying-and-rising **Messiah** — and the **martyrdom of the prophets who preached it** — was edited out of the surviving Old Testament, so that the New Testament and the Book of Mormon both presuppose a messianic record the Hebrew Bible no longer contains. This is the same thesis carried in the channel's [[The Stick of Joseph - The Key to Unlocking Lehis Brass Plates|Brass Plates]] and [[The Stick of Joseph - Was Jesus Edited From the Old Testament|Was Jesus Edited]] episodes; this video focuses tightly on the "prophets killed for the Messiah" datum and the apocrypha that supplies it.

## Summary
- **Four witnesses say prophets were slain for preaching the Messiah — but the Old Testament names none.** [[Lehi]] (1 Nephi 1:19-20), Jesus (Matthew 23:37; Luke 11), Stephen/Peter (Acts 7:51-52), and [[Abinadi]] (Mosiah 13) all assert that earlier prophets explicitly preached a coming Messiah and were stoned, cast out, and slain for it — a record absent from Genesis-through-Malachi, so the critic's "anachronism" charge against the Book of Mormon equally indicts the New Testament. Carried in [[Prophets Were Killed for Preaching the Messiah Before 600 BC]] [^1][^2][^3][^4].
- **The critic's own objection backfires (Eber Howe).** [[Philastus Hurlbut|Eber Howe]]'s *Mormonism Unveiled* charged Joseph Smith with inventing prophet-martyrdoms for Messiah-preaching; Barnes's reply is that Jesus and Stephen assert the very same thing — "what were they reading that we don't have?" [^5].
- **The apocrypha supplies the missing martyrdoms and the missing prophecy.** Barnes mines texts recording Isaiah sawn in half and the deaths of Micah, Jeremiah, and Habakkuk; the **Testament of Levi** (Testaments of the Twelve Patriarchs) has Levi tell his own priestly descendants they will "raise him up on a tree and crucify" the Son of God — too specific for the academy and exactly the kind of self-incriminating text a priestly editor would suppress [^6][^7].
- **Jesus quotes the Book of Enoch.** Barnes reads Luke 6:24 ("woe unto you that are rich") as Jesus quoting [[1 Enoch]] — a text "nobody knew about" until the Ethiopic recovery — as evidence the New Testament writers shared a body of prophecy "that they all knew and we don't have." See [[Book of Enoch - Quoted as Scripture in Jude]] [^8].
- **Why it was removed — and who.** A prophecy naming the tribe of Judah, the tribe of Levi, and the reigning priesthood as the future murderers of God was "politically, personally volatile"; an intern-scribe compiling the texts under the [[Hezekiah Josiah and the Deuteronomist Reforms|Deuteronomist reform]] would destroy or brand-as-hoax a scroll saying *your supervisor will murder the Messiah* rather than risk exile or death. The texts at issue are largely **northern**, dovetailing with [[The Surviving Old Testament Is the Southern Kingdom's Edition]] [^9][^10].
- **The cumulative challenge.** If pre-Christian Messiah-preaching is impossible, why is the most important prophecy in history *not* explicit in the Old Testament, while Jesus and the apostles insist it was preached and its preachers killed? "Who's wrong?" [^2].
