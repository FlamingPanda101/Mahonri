# Let's Get Real with Stephen Jones - Joseph Smiths First Revelation and Dallin H Oaks

**Show:** Let's Get Real (Episode E0045) | **Host:** [[Stephen Jones]] | **Guest:** [[Don Bradley]] (historian, *The Lost 116 Pages*) | **Title:** "How Joseph Smith's 1st Revelation… Gives Us Our 18th President, Dallin H. Oaks | Don Bradley E0045"

A devotional history episode (recorded early October 2025, just as [[Dallin H. Oaks]] is to become the 18th president of the Church) tracing a single providential thread: from Joseph Smith's earliest revelations through the [[Martin Harris]] family down to Oaks's succession. Bradley's framing claim is that the Restoration was never a one-man project — "even though Joseph Smith becomes the prophet… he's not the only person who was necessary" — and that recovering the **Harris family's heritage of witness** reveals "a continuity across time that would be easy to miss if we don't piece the larger story together."

- **The middle initial is the thread.** Oaks is *Dallin Harris Oaks*; his mother was Stella Harris Oaks, descended from Emer Harris, elder brother of Martin Harris. So the 18th prophet comes through the bloodline of the Book of Mormon's chief financial witness — a continuity Bradley reads as providential, not random.[^1] See [[The Harris Bloodline Connects the First Revelation to the 18th Prophet]] and [[Emer Harris]].
- **Martin Harris was a witness of *Jesus Christ*, not only of the plates.** In Joseph Smith's 1832 history, "the Lord appeared unto him in a vision," placing Martin (with [[Oliver Cowdery]] and Joseph) among *three* who saw the Savior at the founding — and it was Martin, by revelation, who was told to take the characters to [[Charles Anthon]].[^2] See [[Martin Harris Was a Witness of Christ Not Only of the Plates]].
- **The first Urim-and-Thummim revelation pointed Joseph to Martin Harris.** On October 6, 1827 (Feast of Tabernacles, per Lucy Mack Smith), Joseph's first use of the Nephite interpreters asked *how* the work would be funded; the Lord showed him Martin — the revelation that brings about the Book of Mormon's publication "and therefore, by extension, now gives us our 18th prophet."[^3] See [[Joseph Smiths First Interpreter Revelation Pointed to Martin Harris]].
- **King Benjamin's speech, not seeing the plates, secured Martin's testimony.** Translation resumed after the lost pages at the start of [[Mosiah 18|Mosiah]] / Benjamin's sermon (a text whose setting is itself the Feast of Tabernacles); the *words* about Christ "tabernacling" in flesh — King Benjamin's "his name shall be Jesus Christ" ([[Mosiah 5.7|Mosiah 5]]) — strengthened Martin enough to testify in court without ever having seen the plates.[^4] See [[King Benjamins Speech Strengthened Martin Harriss Testimony More Than Seeing the Plates]].
- **Emer Harris "tried it by the square."** In 1856 Provo stake-conference minutes Bradley found at the Church archives, Emer describes converting by making a concordance and laying the Bible on the Book of Mormon "like a square," and relays Mulekite details (four men and four women of the royal house) that survive only from the lost 116 pages.[^5] See [[Emer Harris Tested the Book of Mormon by the Square of the Bible]].
- The thematic payoff: Martin helped *choose the original Twelve* (the Three Witnesses selected the 1835 Quorum), and his great-great-grand-nephew Oaks joined that same quorum in 1984 — a "heritage of faith" in which the apostolic role is, definitionally, to be a witness of Christ (Oaks, "Witnesses of Christ," 1990; "Taking Upon Us the Name of Christ," 1985).[^6]
