# My Faith Crisis and a Message to Those Who Struggle
[[Jacob Hansen]] combines three early videos into one personal testimony of the decade-long "faith renovation" (2008–2018) he undertook after returning from his mission and watching nearly all his siblings and half his extended family leave the Church. He recounts discovering that a mature testimony rests on three dependent conversations — God, the New Testament Jesus, and the Restoration — and that most faith crises arise because belief was built top-down rather than from the foundation up. He closes with pastoral counsel to those who struggle: examine your motives honestly, start with the positive case, and treat the work as a renovation, not a demolition.

## Summary
- He reframes his experience not as a "faith crisis" but as a 10-year "faith renovation," sparked when his older brother lost belief in God (not over polygamy or anachronisms) and introduced him to the New Atheists.[^1]
- He realized testimony rests on three propositions, each depending on the one before it — God exists, the New Testament Jesus embodied that God, and Joseph Smith was authorized to restore Christ's church — and that many people's testimonies are "totally inverted," believing in God *because of* Joseph rather than the reverse.[^2]
- Using the Salt Lake Temple's bedrock renovation as an image, he argues that "secularism has been pouring acid on the foundations of belief," so struggling Saints must go down to Level 1 (the existence of God) and rebuild rather than throw "paint over a cracked foundation."[^3]
- His pastoral message: be honest about whether your struggle is moral or intellectual, examine your own epistemology, and "start with the positive case" — weigh the best reasons to believe before exposing your structure to the critics' "earthquake."[^4]
- He insists the goal is to *build*: "this is a renovation, not a demolition," because beyond doubt lies "a dark hole of nihilistic hell," and the adventure of life is figuring out what we can believe in.[^5]
