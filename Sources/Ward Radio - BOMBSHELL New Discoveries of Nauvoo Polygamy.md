# Ward Radio — BOMBSHELL New Discoveries of Nauvoo Polygamy

Historian [[Don Bradley]] presents (with hosts [[Cardon Ellis]], [[Kwaku El]], [[Brad Whitbeck]], and [[Sean Bailey]]) a re-dating of [[Joseph Smith]]'s earliest Nauvoo plural marriages that, he argues, overturns the standard "polygamy was about sex" reading and reframes Joseph's motive as religiously sincere.

## Summary

- **Re-dating the first wife dissolves the "young single bride" pattern.** The traditional first Nauvoo plural wife is [[Louisa Beeman]] (single, sealed April 5, 1841 per [[Joseph Bates Noble]]'s affidavit, published by [[Andrew Jenson]]). Bradley shows Noble is an unreliable date-witness (his biographer called him "terrible with dates"; his "first plural child" boast is false; he placed the sealing at his Nauvoo house, not built until after fall 1841; and April 5 is the date of *Noble's own* 1843 marriage). Re-dating Louisa to spring 1842 makes **[[Zina Huntington Jacobs]]** the first Nauvoo plural wife — and Zina was already married and ~7 months pregnant. See [[Joseph Smith Married Pregnant Women Refuting the Sexual-Motive Thesis]].
- **A deliberate pattern of pregnant, married brides.** Bradley lines up the corrected order: Zina (7 months pregnant, DNA confirms son was her legal husband [[Henry Jacobs]]'s), [[Presendia Huntington Buell]] (~2/3 likely pregnant), [[Mary Elizabeth Rollins Lightner]] (7-8 months pregnant). Since 1800s medicine forbade late-pregnancy sex, choosing about-to-deliver women is the *opposite* of seeking immediate sex — the pattern, read abductively, points to a non-sexual, "raising-up-seed"/adoption-like sealing logic. This is the counter-case to [[Joseph Smith's Polygamy as a Predatory Pattern - Reel]] and to [[The Problem With Calling Joseph Smith a Con Man]].
- **The angel with the drawn sword lines up with a real meteor shower.** [[Zina Huntington Jacobs|Zina]] said Joseph delayed proposing until "an angel with a drawn sword" compelled him (~shortly before Oct 27, 1841). Bradley finds [[Orson Hyde]] independently recorded a "bright glittering sword… an arm with a perfect hand… took hold on the hilt" in the night sky on Oct 19, 1841 from a ship near Beirut — which Bradley identifies as the **Orionid meteor shower** (debris of Halley's Comet, radiating near Orion's upraised arm), peaking Oct 19 at ~1 a.m. He argues this shows a sincere prophet responding to perceived heavenly signs, not a con man. See [[The Angel with a Drawn Sword Coincides with the Orionid Meteor Shower]].
