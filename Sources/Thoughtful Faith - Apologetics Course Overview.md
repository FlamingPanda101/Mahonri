# Thoughtful Faith — Apologetics Course Overview

A short (~7-minute) introduction in which [[Jacob Hansen]] explains the purpose, structure, and method of his "Thoughtful Faith" apologetics **course** — his standing answer to the recurring question, "Where did you learn all this?" and "What books or podcasts do you suggest?"[^1] The course is presented as a **curated library of videos, podcasts, and other resources** from both LDS and non-LDS thinkers who have shaped his understanding.[^2] But Hansen insists the most important thing was not the content itself: it was understanding the **"anatomy of my beliefs"** — that his faith rests on **three independent conversations** that bring increasing clarity to life's big questions.[^3]

## Summary
- **The organizing thesis is the "anatomy of belief": three independent conversations.** Hansen's testimony is staged as three sequential best-explanation judgments — *reality* points to God; *God* points to the New Testament Jesus; *Jesus* points to the Restoration in the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints.[^3] Because each is examined on its own footing, faith does not collapse like a domino chain. See [[Anatomy of Belief - Three Independent Conversations]].
- **Inherited belief is fragile; each proposition must be examined independently.** Hansen repeatedly watched people lose belief in Joseph Smith and "almost immediately become atheists," and realized many (himself included) had simply *inherited* belief in God and Christ without ever examining those propositions independently.[^4] Asking "If Joseph Smith was not who the church claims, would I still believe in God?" — and not being sure — launched his investigation. See [[Inherited Belief Is Fragile - Examine Each Proposition Independently]].
- **The course is built on a four-level conversation structure.** Level 1 = the fundamentals of reality (what am I, meaning, knowledge, reasons for a higher power) — the conversation with agnostics and atheists. Level 2 = the nature of God and whether Jesus told the truth about God — the conversation with the "spiritual but not Christian." Level 3 = the unique doctrines of the Restoration — the conversation with other Christians. Level 4 = topics specific to Latter-day Saints' internal conversations about their own doctrines.[^5] See [[The Four-Level Conversation Structure of Apologetics]].
- **An ecumenical foundation: borrow the best non-LDS Christian resources for Levels 1–2.** Hansen argues Christians of other denominations are "on the same page" with Latter-day Saints in establishing that God exists and that the New Testament Jesus is the Savior, and have "amazing resources" there — [[C.S. Lewis]]'s *Mere Christianity* is his exemplar of Level 2 content. Ignoring these works "does ourselves a great disservice."[^6] See [[Ecumenical Foundation - Borrow Non-LDS Christian Resources for Levels One and Two]].
- **Diagnosis of secular decline.** The Restoration's message has always landed hardest where a Level 1–2 foundation already existed — hence boatloads of disaffected European Protestants and Catholics historically, versus a "barren and gray" field in now-secular Europe. Latter-day Saints, Hansen warns, have themselves forgotten the Level 1 and 2 conversations that the Restoration is built upon.[^7]
- **The payoff is conversational range and deeper personal faith.** Mastering each level equips a person to meet others "at any level they are at"; the course mirrors Hansen's own "10-year faith remodeling journey" from Level 1 upward.[^8]
