# The Stick of Joseph - Jared Halverson on Why Holy Scripture Is Not Enough

Hosts Jackson and Hayden interview **[[Jared Halverson]]** — CES educator and PhD in American religious history (Vanderbilt), whose academic specialty is anti-religious rhetoric. Filmed before the hosts' military deployment. The conversation moves from Halverson's posture toward critics to a sustained argument that the restoration's distinctive is *revelatory authority*, not better biblical interpretation.

## Summary
- **Be a judge, not a lawyer.** Because religion is "non-provable" and "non-disprovable," Halverson wants Latter-day Saints trained as impartial judges of truth rather than defense attorneys; he models "thick skin with a soft heart" and reframes member/ex-member arguments as interfaith dialogue. See [[Thick Skin With a Soft Heart and the Interfaith Reframe]].
- **The core argument (the episode's centerpiece):** quoting the Bible to settle a dispute "just makes us one more Protestant denomination." Protestantism is a *scribal/biblical* religion; the restoration is built on prophets who teach "as one having authority, and not as the scribes." See [[Scribal Religion Versus Authoritative Religion]].
- **Nebuchadnezzar's dream** ([[Daniel 2]]) is his proof: the wise men admit only God can *reveal* a dream, yet anyone can *interpret* one — so interpretive authority must be matched to revelatory authority. Eternal marriage and baptism for the dead "grew out of" revelation to Joseph Smith ([[D&C 132|D&C 132]], [[D&C 128|127–128]]), not [[1 Corinthians 11.11|1 Corinthians 11:11]] or [[1 Corinthians 15.29|15:29]]. See [[Revelatory Authority Must Match Interpretive Authority]].
