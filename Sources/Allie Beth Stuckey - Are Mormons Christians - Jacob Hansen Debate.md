# DEBATE: Are Mormons Christians? LDS Apologist vs. Evangelical Christian

On her *Relatable* podcast, evangelical commentator [[Allie Beth Stuckey]] interviews and debates Latter-day Saint apologist [[Jacob Hansen]] (Thoughtful Faith) on the question "Are Mormons Christians? Do we believe in the same gospel?"[^1] Stuckey's thesis is that Latter-day Saints are not Christians in the saving sense because the LDS gospel adds priesthood ordinances to grace-through-faith and worships a different God; Hansen's thesis is that Latter-day Saints *are* Christians — just not *creedal* Christians — because they center faith in Christ's atonement and hold the restored fullness of His original church.

## Participants
- [[Jacob Hansen]] (Latter-day Saint apologist, Thoughtful Faith) vs. [[Allie Beth Stuckey]] (evangelical commentator, *Relatable*)

## Summary
- **Origins and apostasy.** Hansen recounts Joseph Smith's First Vision and frames the Restoration as recovering the *fullness of the institutional church* and *priesthood keys* (given to Peter), not the claim that no believers existed for 1,800 years. Stuckey presses: if the gospel was "essentially the same," "by what definition was the church apostate?" Hansen answers it was the priesthood authority that was lost.[^2] See [[Apostasy Was Complete When the Keys Departed]] and [[Car Wreck Analogy for Diagnosing Apostasy]].
- **Salvation — spectrum vs. binary.** Hansen describes three degrees of glory plus outer darkness (reserved for those who, like a possible Judas, knowingly hate the light), framing salvation as how much light one is "willing to accept." Stuckey insists on a binary: Acts 4:12 and John 14:6 mean one is either "in Christ or out of Christ," with no middle celestial tiers.[^3] See [[Salvation Is Binary In Christ or Out of Christ Not a Spectrum]].
- **Grace plus ordinances — the necessary-but-not-sufficient claim.** Hansen affirms faith, repentance, baptism, and the gift of the Holy Ghost (Acts 2:38, Article of Faith 4), then adds that these ordinances and the temple "second half" must be administered by the *restored priesthood*. Stuckey's repeated cross-exam: a Baptist does all of those things, so the real LDS gospel is "grace, faith, baptism *plus going through the Mormon church*." Hansen: those are "necessary but not sufficient conditions" (John 3:5).[^4] See [[Priesthood Authority Is the Necessary But Not Sufficient Dividing Line]] and [[Sola Fide vs Faith Plus Ordinances]].
- **Sola fide and spiritual deadness.** Stuckey grounds salvation in Ephesians 2 ("by grace... through faith... not a result of works"): the dead in sin cannot reach up, so we "contribute nothing." Hansen counters with the car-on-the-cliff/helicopter analogy — Christ's grace does the saving, but we must freely take His hand — and reads 2 Nephi 25:23 ("after all we can do") as "*in spite of* all we can do."[^5] See [[Ephesians 2-8-9 - Saved by Grace Not Works Rebutted]] and [[After All We Can Do Means In Spite of All We Can Do]].
- **Christology and the Trinity.** Hansen affirms Jesus is "fully divine" and the "only begotten" whose origin is the Father (citing Basil), but denies *consubstantiality*: three persons sharing one *nature*, not one *being*. He reads "I and the Father are one" through John 17 (indwelling unity, as disciples are one) and John 1:1 as "the Word was as to His nature deity" (citing James White). Stuckey defends the Nicene Trinity and one being in three persons.[^6] See [[John 17 Oneness Is Indwelling Not Consubstantiality]], [[Consubstantiality]], and [[Different Jesus Both Uncharitable and Precisely True]].
- **Theosis and divine kinship.** Hansen's "most radical" doctrine: every person is a literal child of God with "a spark of divinity," supported by Acts 17 (*genos/genus* = "kind"), Genesis 1:26 read through Genesis 5:3, Psalm 82, Romans 8:16-17, and the church fathers' exchange formula (Athanasius, Cyprian, Irenaeus). He clarifies the official church position rejects the "own planet" caricature and that Christ is the *source* of divinity humans depend on. Stuckey replies she can disagree with the fathers and reads these texts as adoptive heirship and worship, not becoming gods.[^7] See [[Human Deification in Mormonism]], [[Christ Became Man That We Might Become God - The Exchange Formula]], [[Acorn and Oak Analogy for Theosis]], and [[Humans Are the Same Kind as God (LDS Ontology)]].
- **Monotheism vs. henotheism.** Hansen argues the Old Testament is a *henotheistic/monolatrous* text (citing Heiser, Scommer-style scholarship, the "Packers/Green Bay" boast reading of Isaiah 43:10 and Isaiah 47/Zephaniah's "I am, and there is none besides me"), with Yahweh as Most High over real lesser *elohim*. Stuckey holds strict monotheism (Isaiah 43:10; "like me there is no other"); Hansen notes that even creedal trinitarianism strains "hard" monotheism, agreeing with the Muslim critique on that narrow point.[^8] See [[Henotheism and Divine Pedagogy in the Old Testament]], [[Isaiah 43-10 - No God Formed Before Me Rebutted]], [[Psalm 82 Is About Divine Council Not Human Judges]], and [[If You Want Strict Monotheism Be a Muslim]].
- **The incarnation, Christ's descent, and "As man now is, God once was."** Hansen reads Philippians 2 as Christ *descending in the fullness of divinity* into mortality and re-ascending — explicitly disowning the popular Lorenzo Snow couplet ("As man now is, God once was") as a non-canonical maxim he is "not comfortable" defending, and rejecting the view that Jesus or Satan had a "human origin story." Stuckey replies the incarnation did not change Christ's nature.[^9] See [[Embodied God in Christian Scholarship]].
- **Prophets, prophecy, and God's nature.** Hansen denies prophetic inerrancy (Peter on pork, contingent prophecies like Nineveh) and argues God adjusts circumstances without changing His essential nature; Stuckey holds Deuteronomy 18 / Malachi 3:6 (true prophets are not wrong; God does not change).[^10] See [[Prophets Are Fallible Not Divine]] and [[Other True Prophets Do Not Undercut Joseph Smith]].
- **The closing gospel statements.** Both are invited to state "the good news." Hansen: Christ died for all, offers His hand and the gift of grace, and the LDS church offers the restored priesthood ordinances to bring believers into full communion. Stuckey: "by grace you have been saved through faith... not your own doing"; Christ's once-for-all sacrifice (Hebrews) is *enough* — "There aren't three levels of heaven," only full relationship with God or hell.[^11]
