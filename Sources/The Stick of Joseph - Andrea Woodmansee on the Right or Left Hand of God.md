# The Stick of Joseph — On What It Means to Be on the RIGHT or LEFT Hand of God

A short clip (credited to [[Andrea Woodmansee]]; the spoken exposition is [[Dave Butler|David Butler]] with the hosts) reading [[Mosiah 5.8|King Benjamin's "right hand / left hand of God"]] and the [[Matthew 25.31-46|sheep-and-goats judgment]] as **Day of Atonement** imagery. The unifying claim: being on the **right hand** means having the lot drawn to be the *la-Yahweh* goat — godlike, called by the name of Christ — while the **left hand** is the *la-Azazel* lot, called "by some other name" and driven out. The piece extends the vault's existing two-goats cluster ([[Barabbas and Jesus Are the Two Goats of the Day of Atonement]], [[Laban and Nephi Enact the Two Goats of the Day of Atonement]]) into King Benjamin's sermon.

## Summary

- **Right/left hand = the two goats' lots.** On the Day of Atonement the high priest (bearing the Name on his forehead, "which probably means he *is* Yahweh") draws lots over two identical goats: the right-hand lot makes one *la-Yahweh* (sacrificed, its blood atoning); the left-hand lot makes the other *la-Azazel* (driven into the wilderness, hurled into a pit). Reading the *lamed* as "as," one goat *is* Yahweh and one *is* Azazel. See [[The Right and Left Hand of God Are the Day of Atonement Goats]] and [[Leviticus 16]].[^1][^2]
- **Benjamin is discreetly evoking Azazel.** [[Mosiah 5.8|Mosiah 5:9-10]] — those who know the name are "found at the right hand of God," but "whosoever shall not take upon him the name of Christ... must be called by some other name; therefore, he findeth himself on the left hand of God." Butler notes Benjamin is "being discreet, because the only other name is Azazel." To have the la-Yahweh lot drawn is to "become godlike," to be called by the name of Christ (= Yahweh). See [[The Name Written in the Heart Is the Yahweh Lot of Atonement]].[^3]
- **Matthew 25 is a temple/Atonement scene, not a farm story.** The Son of Man coming in glory with the holy angels ([[Matthew 25.31-46|Matthew 25:31]]) is the same scene as Revelation's enthronement and the Day of Atonement enacted yearly — "not a farm story... a temple story." Butler flags an *innovation*: where Leviticus has goat-vs-goat, Matthew puts all the **sheep** on the right (the flock of the Lord, the sacrifice) and the **goats** on the left (driven out) — possibly an old memory or a development.[^4][^5]
- **Knowing the name / voice is the divider.** Back in [[Mosiah 5.8|Mosiah 5:12-13]], the sheep "hear and know the voice" and retain "the name written always in [their] hearts"; the goats do not — "how knoweth a man the master whom he hath not served, and who is a stranger unto him?" So the sheep/goat division is *recognition* (knowing the name and voice), tying this clip to the channel's [[Knowing Christ Means Bearing His Image Not Performing Works|"I never knew you"]] theme.[^6]
- **What separates sheep from goats is mercy to "the least of these."** The Lord's own criterion ([[Matthew 25.31-46|Matthew 25:35-45]]) is feeding the hungry, clothing the naked, visiting the prisoner — done "unto one of the least of these." The condemned "don't even know what they did wrong." The clip cuts off mid-question ("is it just good works?"), leaving the relationship-vs-works tension open (cf. [[Jesus Taught Salvation Involves Doing Not Faith Alone]]).[^7]
