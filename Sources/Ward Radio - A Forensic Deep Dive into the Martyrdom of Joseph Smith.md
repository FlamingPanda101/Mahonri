# Ward Radio — A Forensic Deep Dive into the Martyrdom of Joseph Smith

A Ward Radio episode ("NCIS Carthage") in which host **[[Cardon Ellis]]** interviews forensic-reconstruction researcher **[[Shannon Tracy]]**, with **[[Luke Hansen]]** (Ward Radio News) supervising via Zoom. Tracy lays out a proposed documentary that would 3D-laser-scan Carthage Jail, rebuild the crime scene digitally, and reconstruct the June 1844 killing of [[Joseph Smith]] and [[Hyrum Smith]] step by step from the physical and documentary record. The conversation doubles as a walkthrough of what Tracy says is already "settled" forensically about the martyrdom.

## Summary
- **Forensics throws data out, not in.** Tracy frames the method as crime-scene reconstruction: collect everything (laser scan of the jail, ballistic-gel tests, the doctor's wound report, the recovered pocket watch), then *eliminate* what does not fit rather than start from a theory — a deliberate contrast with rival "who shot Joseph Smith" documentaries that begin with a thesis [^1]. See [[Forensic Reconstruction Eliminates Theories Rather Than Inventing Them]].
- **Two pistols, not an arsenal — and the door did not lock.** The documentation puts only two pistols in the upstairs room; the jail door was broken and would not latch, so the four men were holding it shut by hand against a mob of roughly five or six on the landing [^2]. See [[The Carthage Door Did Not Lock and Only Two Pistols Were in the Room]].
- **The pepper-box misfired by design.** Joseph's 1827 Allen & Thurber pepper-box pistol fired only three of six rounds; the shaking of each shot tended to dislodge the percussion caps, so the first barrel is the most reliable and reliability drops down the cylinder [^3]. See [[Joseph Smith's Pepper-Box Pistol Misfired Half Its Rounds at Carthage]].
- **The pocket watch was crushed, not shot.** [[John Taylor]]'s watch shows a star of broken enamel on the front and pushed-out dimples on the back that match the internal gear-works — evidence it was *compressed* against the window sill when he fell, not struck by a ball; Taylor's own earliest account never mentions a bullet hitting it [^4]. See [[John Taylor's Pocket Watch Was Crushed by a Fall Not a Bullet]].
- **Eyewitness memory at a crime scene is unreliable.** Tracy applies a standard forensic caveat: victims caught in the trauma "fill in" gaps later; Willard Richards (trapped behind the door) and Taylor both reconstruct events they could not fully have seen, which is normal and does not imply fabrication [^5]. See [[Trauma Victims Reconstruct Crime-Scene Gaps So Survivor Accounts Vary]].
- **Garments swapped for plain underwear.** Tracy claims Joseph and Hyrum, expecting possible imprisonment, had changed out of their temple garments into ordinary long underwear to avoid the sacred clothing being "compromised" — heading off the anti-Mormon "you can't be murdered in your garments" jab [^6].
- **The Carthage Greys stood aside; the Nauvoo Legion was disarmed.** Half the Warsaw mob disbanded after the governor's note, but the rest, joined by a Carthage signal, came on in blackface; the Carthage Greys (the guard) stepped aside, and the Nauvoo Legion had been ordered by Joseph to stand down and was disarmed by Governor [[Thomas Ford]], so no armed defense was possible [^7]. See [[The Nauvoo Legion Was Disarmed and Outside Carthage's Jurisdiction]].
- **"These anti-Mormons haven't changed."** The hosts argue the Warsaw Signal's rhetoric justifying violence ("they deserved it") is a 19th-century analogue to modern outrage culture, and that a "thin veneer of civilization" is all that separates today's critics from the mob [^8]. See [[The Thin Veneer of Civilization Argument]].
