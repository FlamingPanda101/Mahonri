# Ward Radio — The REAL History of Adam's Garment

[[Cardon Ellis]] hosts **[[Jonah Barnes]]** on the **Garment of Adam** as the hidden thread of Genesis: a celestial garment ("garment of light," then "coats of skins") that passes Adam → Noah → Ham → Nimrod → Esau → Jacob → Joseph, drawing on [[Stephen D. Ricks]]'s essay and the apocryphal *Life of Adam and Eve*, *Jubilees*, and *Jasher* — and argued to match the **Book of Mormon** ([[Alma 46.21-22|Alma 46]]) against the received text of Genesis.

## Summary
- **Genesis is "a history of Adam's garment."** God's word *sews* the coats of skin himself as a *kaphar* (covering → atonement) so Adam can stand in the divine presence; one apocryphal vein makes it a serpent-skin — the immortal vestment stripped from Lucifer and given to Adam, explaining Satan's bitter hatred of *Adam* specifically [^1].
- **The relic chain matches the Book of Mormon's Joseph-coat.** Ham steals the folded garment (the real "nakedness of Noah" scene); it passes to Nimrod (his hunting prowess) and is recovered when Esau, fleeing Nimrod's men, trades it to Jacob for pottage — then becomes Joseph's "coat of many colors." [[Alma 46.21-22|Alma 46:23-24]] cites Jacob's prophecy that a *remnant of Joseph's coat was preserved and had not decayed* — remnant theology absent from Genesis but present in the brass-plate version [^2].
- **The book of Jasher agrees with the Nephites, not Genesis.** Captain Moroni's people say Joseph's garment was "trodden under foot"; Genesis only says it was dipped in blood — but *Jasher* says the brothers dipped it *and trampled it in the dust*, matching the Book of Mormon (and discovered long after Joseph Smith) [^3].
- **Nephi and Laban replay Esau and Nimrod.** Nephi cutting off Laban's head and donning his "goodly" garments parallels Esau beheading Nimrod for the garment — read as the Garment of Adam descending through the "northern strangers" / Joseph's righteous remnant to the New World; the Deuteronomist/Josiah reformers excised it because it symbolized the corporeal Messiah and the temple [^4].
