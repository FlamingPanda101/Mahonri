# Ward Radio - View of the Hebrews and the Book of Mormon

**Show:** Ward Radio | **Hosts:** Neil & Jasmine ([[Ward Radio]]) | **Guest:** [[Stephen Smoot|Stephen O. Smoot]] | **Title:** "The Mormon Necronomicon — View of the Hebrews"

A long-form interview in which LDS scholar [[Stephen Smoot]] dismantles the most common naturalistic theory for the [[Book of Mormon]]'s origin — that [[Joseph Smith]] plagiarized it from [[Ethan Smith]]'s 1823 book *View of the Hebrews*. Smoot, who owns an original 1825 second edition and is writing a book-length manuscript on the question, situates *View of the Hebrews* historically, traces the genealogy of the plagiarism charge, taxonomizes the alleged "parallels" as parallelomania, and shows the proposed [[Oliver Cowdery]] link is chronologically impossible.

- *View of the Hebrews* is not scripture or a novel but a missionary-anthropological tract by [[Ethan Smith]], a learned Congregational minister in Poultney, Vermont, arguing that Native Americans descend from the ten lost tribes of Israel — one expression of the broader [[Hebraic-Indian theory]] that was already waning by the 1820s.[^1] See [[View of the Hebrews Is Not Scripture or a Novel]] and [[The Hebraic-Indian Theory Was a Common Period Idea]].
- The plagiarism accusation is *not* contemporary: no critic in Joseph Smith's lifetime — not even Ethan Smith himself — ever made it; it first appears in 1902 and only becomes a staple after [[Fawn Brodie]] popularized it in 1945.[^2] See [[No Contemporary Accusation of Plagiarism]] and [[Fawn Brodie Popularized the View of the Hebrews Theory]].
- The list of "parallels" (from [[B.H. Roberts]]'s private study via the [[CES Letter]]) collapses into three failure categories — non-parallels, superficial parallels, and shared biblical sources — the hallmark of [[parallelomania]].[^3] See [[The View of the Hebrews Parallels Are Parallelomania]], [[The Urim and Thummim Is Not a Real Parallel]], [[The Quetzalcoatl Parallel Names Moses Not Christ]], and [[The Ethan and Ether Parallel Roberts Rejected]].
- The strongest "parallels" are either generic (destruction of Jerusalem, denouncing pride, seas of many waters) or trace to a shared biblical source — e.g. the "land where man never dwelt" line both books get from 2 Esdras in the Apocrypha.[^4] See [[Shared Biblical Sources Are Not Plagiarism]] and [[A Parallel List Without Textual Analysis Carries No Burden]].
- The proposed [[Oliver Cowdery]] "missing link" fails on chronology: Cowdery did not meet Joseph Smith until April 1829, two years after translation had begun, and Joseph was already describing the plates and their contents before *View of the Hebrews* even reached its second edition.[^5] See [[The Oliver Cowdery Link Is Chronologically Impossible]] and [[Joseph Smith Cited View of the Hebrews as Evidence]].
- Latter-day Saints — beginning with [[Joseph Smith]] himself in the 1842 *Times and Seasons* — long cited *View of the Hebrews* *positively* as evidence for the Book of Mormon; BYU even published its own edition, refuting the "deep dark secret" conspiracy framing.[^6] See [[Joseph Smith Cited View of the Hebrews as Evidence]] and [[BYU Published View of the Hebrews]].
