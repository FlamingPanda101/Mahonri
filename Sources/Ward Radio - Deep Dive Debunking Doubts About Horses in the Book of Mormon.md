# Ward Radio — Deep Dive Debunking Doubts About Horses in the Book of Mormon

A [[Ward Radio]] episode hosted by [[Cardon Ellis]] with [[Jonah Barnes]] and [[Brad Whitbeck]], presenting the research of independent Latter-day Saint researcher [[Daniel Johnson]] on the horse anachronism. The signature move is to **turn the objection around**: rather than arguing only for pre-Columbian horses, Johnson argues the *Spanish-introduction* story collapses under its own demographic math. The episode is interlaced with a running comedic "brony" bit. The **demographic-math and Pizarro-origin arguments are new to the vault**; the native-testimony, mares, and Texas-Journal material overlaps and corroborates the existing horse cluster.

## Summary

- **The "no pre-Columbian horses" assumption traces to the Pizarro–Atahualpa story.** A single Andean conquest narrative — the Inca emperor allegedly terrified of a horse — became the cultural basis for assuming horses were absent across all the Americas, including in [[Joseph Smith]]'s day; the assumption then propagated into reference works. See [[The Pizarro-Atahualpa Story Is the Source of the No-Horses Assumption]]. [^1]
- **The Spanish-horse math cannot produce continent-wide horses by 1567.** Documented native sightings predate the Pueblo Revolt (Blackfoot 1754, Shoshone <1700, Jamestown 1669, Kiowa 1590, Sonora 1567); the Spanish brought few horses, mostly mares; [[Hernan Cortez]] gifted some and found them eaten; and at the *observed* feral growth rate (tripling per 50 years, not doubling per 5), 16 lost horses yield ~48 by 1567, not thousands. The genetics paper's 1519 anchor is an explicit *assumption*. See [[The Spanish Horse Math Cannot Explain Continent-Wide Horses by 1567]]. [^2]
- **Native nations had mature horse cultures and horse-bearing creation myths at first contact.** Horses appear in clothing, trade, husbandry, and origin stories wherever Europeans arrived — hard to square with sudden European introduction. (Corroborates the existing [[Native American Testimony That Horses Were Always Here]].) [^3]
- **Mesoamerican horse bones date into Book-of-Mormon windows.** The *Texas Journal of Science* horse-bone dates and the Loltún Cave remains are cited as "the longer we wait, the more we find." (Corroborates [[Horses Are Trending Not Settled as an Anachronism]].) [^4]
