# The Stick of Joseph — Was the Divine Feminine Erased from the Bible?

A rare on-camera interview on the [[The Stick of Joseph - Are Christians Becoming Mormon with Brian Thomassen|Stick of Joseph]] channel with **[[Margaret Barker]]** (hosts [[Jackson Paul]] and [[Hayden Paul]]) on "the great lady" — the divine feminine figure Barker argues was systematically written out of the Hebrew Bible. Presented "like a detective novel," she lays out archaeological and philological traces of a mother-goddess who stood in the first temple and was deliberately erased.

## Summary
- **The most common first-temple archaeological find around Jerusalem is a smashed nursing-mother figurine — evidence of a deliberately destroyed Lady.** Thousands of "Judean pillar figurines" (a nursing mother with big eyes and large breasts, lower body a cone) have been excavated near the temple; many were *systematically smashed*, which Barker reads as a concerted effort to destroy her, not mere "pagan falling away" or "dolls" [^1][^2]. See [[The Judean Pillar Figurines Were the Smashed Mother in the First Temple]].
- **"El Shaddai" is most naturally translated "the God with breasts," not "God Almighty."** Barker argues *shaddai* derives from *shad* (breast); "God Almighty" in [[Exodus 6.3|Exodus 6:3]] is "not an accurate translation" but part of the program of burying the Lady. "We found hundreds of them," she says of the breasted figurines [^3]. (This corroborates the vault's [[Shaddai Is the Breasted God and Points to the Mother]] from an independent, non-LDS scholar.)
- **The "him that dwelt in the bush" of Joseph's blessing was originally a *woman* who dwelt in the bush.** In [[Deuteronomy 33.16|Deuteronomy 33:16]], the unpointed Hebrew and an archaic feminine ending (Sarah/Sarai) make "the favor of *her* that dwelt in the bush" the better reading — the burning bush being associated with the tree and, in later Orthodox iconography, with Mary [^4][^5]. See [[Deborah and Asenath Preserve the Lady of the Bush]].
- **Asenath, Deborah, and the bush-lady are linguistic survivals of the same mother figure.** *S-N-H* (bush) + the feminine ending yields **Asenath**, who in the *Joseph and Aseneth* legend lives high in a temple and is visited by the dawn angel amid bees and honeycomb; **Deborah** ("bee") is read via the archaic d/g interchange as *gevirah*, the "great lady"/queen-mother, "wife of *lapidot*" (torches/lamps, not a husband's name), judging Israel under the palm tree ([[Judges 4|Judges 4:4-5]]) — a holy-of-holies figure tending the menorah lights [^6][^7]. See [[Deborah and Asenath Preserve the Lady of the Bush]].
- **A palm branch was a token of having been entrusted with the secret knowledge of the holy of holies.** Sitting under the palm marks Deborah's initiated status; this is why the Palm Sunday crowd waving palms and crying *hosanna* ("save us," not "hurray") were people who "remembered the significance" of the king coming to cleanse the temple and "save us from this lot" up in it [^8][^9]. See [[The Palm Branch Signified the Secret Knowledge of the Holy of Holies]].
