Three returned missionaries on the LDS reaction channel **All Those In Favor** — host [[Cade (All Those In Favor)|Cade]], co-host [[Baylor Johnson (Ward Radio)|Baylor Johnson]], and guest [[Kwaku (All Those In Favor)|Kwaku]] — react to a viral clip in which a Protestant pastor (Nick Jones) presses two missionaries on the person of Christ, *sola scriptura*, the test of a prophet, monotheism, and "another gospel." The hosts steelman the pastor where he is right (the Logos of [[John 1.1|John 1:1]] is Christ, not the Bible) while arguing his "ownership" depends on importing post-biblical Greek metaphysics and on misrepresenting what Latter-day Saints actually hold.

## Summary

**Christology and the hypostatic union.** The pastor's "part God, part man" trap presupposes the [[Hypostatic Union]] defined at Chalcedon (451) — the divine/human-nature split the hosts say "makes a mess of the atonement," since on that scheme only Christ's human nature suffers. They counter from [[Mosiah 3.8|Mosiah 3]] ("God himself… shall come down") and the [[1 Nephi 11.18|condescension of God]], and locate the corruption in [[Greek Philosophy Corrupted the Christian Concept of God|Greek philosophy's]] [[Divine Impassibility Is Classical-Theist Heresy|impassibility/aseity]]. They invoke [[Blake Ostler]]'s account of the Godhead as a "relationship of indwelling unity" to explain why premortal spirits lack the deity required to atone — answering the pastor's own question from [[Alma 34.32-33|Alma 34:10-11]] ("not a sacrifice of man… but an infinite and eternal sacrifice").

**John 1:1 and the pastor's self-own.** The pastor cites [[Daniel Wallace]] correctly that the anarthrous *theos* means "the Word was divine" (see [[John 1-1 - Qualitative Theos (And the Word Was God)]], [[John 1-1c Anarthrous Theos and Subordinate Divinity]]) — then credits the Joseph Smith Translation with "getting it right," though the JST rewrites the verse entirely. This original critique is captured in [[The Pastor Misattributed the Anarthrous Reading to the Joseph Smith Translation]].

**Sola scriptura and "going beyond the Bible."** The pastor's "show me from the Bible why we can't go beyond the Bible" is met with the [[The Three Unjustified Protestant Canon Presuppositions|self-referential canon problem]] and [[God Himself Not a Book Is the Ultimate Authority]]; his appeal to [[Hebrews 1.1-2|Hebrews 1]] (God spoke "last" through the Son) is turned back — God kept speaking after the crucifixion.

**Test of a prophet, monotheism, polygamy.** [[Deuteronomy 18.20-22|Deuteronomy 18]] is reframed as [[Deuteronomy 18 Condemns the Presumptuous Prophet Not the Mistaken One|condemning the presumptuous, not the mistaken, prophet]] and as self-refuting for distant prophecies — and as applicable to Jesus himself via [[Matthew 24.34|Matthew 24]] ("this generation"). The pastor's [[Isaiah 43.10|"no god formed before me"]] monotheism is answered by [[Monotheism Is a 17th-Century Invention|the 1700s coinage of "monotheism"]] and [[The Plural of Genesis 1 Is the Divine Council Not the Trinity or Royal We|divine-council]] theology, with [[2 Corinthians 4.4|Satan as "the god of this world"]] cited as a god made after Yahweh. The "burning in the bosom" / [[Jeremiah 17.9|"heart is wicked"]] exchange is met by noting *heart* in its ancient context means the reasoning mind, not emotion. The closing **Sister Wives** segment is dismissed as a 25-second research failure conflating an unrelated polygamous sect with the Church.

**Missionary method.** Threaded throughout: the elders are commended for refusing to speculate — see [[Missionaries Are Tour Guides Not Question Answerers]].
