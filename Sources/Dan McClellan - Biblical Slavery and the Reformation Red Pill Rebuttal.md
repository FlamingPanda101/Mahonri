# Dan McClellan — Biblical Slavery and the Reformation Red Pill Rebuttal

A point-by-point response to Reformed pastor [[Joshua HS]] (of the *Reformation Red Pill* podcast), who defended the viral claim that "it is not inherently evil to own another human being" by arguing Old Testament slavery was benign, paid, debt-bondage that repudiated the abuses of American slavery. McClellan grants the Bible condones slavery but argues it is therefore morally wrong, and dismantles each apologetic claim as exegetically and historically false.

- **The Bible does endorse slavery — and that makes the Bible wrong, not slavery acceptable.** McClellan agrees with Joshua that Scripture condones slavery "from beginning to end," but draws the opposite conclusion: "we know better now. The Bible was wrong," and moral authority lies in consensus, not the text.[^1] See [[Hebrew Bible Endorses Slavery from Beginning to End]], [[The Bible Was Wrong and Consensus Determines Morality]].
- **There were two slaveries, not one — and the apologetic ignores the worse one.** Israelite debt-bondage ([[Leviticus 25.39-43|Leviticus 25:39-43]], [[Deuteronomy 15.12-15|Deuteronomy 15]]) was milder, but [[Leviticus 25.44-46|Leviticus 25:44-46]] authorizes permanent, heritable **chattel slavery of foreigners**; debt slaves were never "paid," and the credit-card analogy collapses given bodily ownership, lawful beating, and forced breeding.[^2] See [[Two Distinct Slaveries Debt-Bondage and Chattel]], [[Hebrew Bible Endorses Chattel Slavery of Foreigners]], [[Debt Slaves Were Not Paid Wages]], [[Biblical Slavery Is Like Credit Card Debt Rebutted]], [[Ethnic vs Racial Slavery Is Not a Significant Difference]].
- **The "Old Testament repudiates antebellum slavery" claim fails on all three counts.** The kidnapping ban ([[Exodus 21.16|Exodus 21:16]] / [[Deuteronomy 24.7|Deuteronomy 24:7]]) protects only freeborn Israelites; the "rights/love the sojourner" texts ([[Deuteronomy 10.19|Deuteronomy 10:19]]) concern free resident aliens, not slaves; and far from prohibiting abuse, [[Exodus 21.20-21|Exodus 21:20-21]] permits beating a slave to death if death is delayed a day or two.[^3] See [[Old Testament Repudiates Antebellum Slavery Rebutted]], [[Kidnapping Prohibition Protects Israelites Not the Slave Trade]], [[Sojourner Passages Are Misread as Slavery Protections]], [[Bible Permits Beating Slaves Short of Immediate Death]], [[Covenant Code Lexical Precision on Social Status]].
- **Law-for-law, the secular antebellum code was more humane than the Bible.** The Louisiana Civil Code of 1825 forbade beating a slave to death; the Bible imposes no penalty for a delayed-death beating. Comparing idealized biblical *law* to documented Southern *atrocity* is apples-to-oranges; the honest comparison reverses the apologetic verdict.[^4] See [[Louisiana 1825 Code Protected Slaves More Than the Bible]], [[Laws on the Books vs Facts on the Ground]], [[Hebrew Slavery Was Not More Compassionate Than the Antebellum South]].
- **Joshua's defense rests on misremembered, misattributed, and hallucinated texts — and functions as attention-seeking costly signaling.** McClellan documents a wrong citation, a misread clause, an invented "Leviticus 24," a forgotten proof-text, and a mistranslated verb, framing the whole exercise as identity-driven provocation rather than exegesis.[^5] See [[Apologist Misremembers and Hallucinates Bible Passages]], [[Defending Slavery as Costly Signaling for Attention]], [[Biblical Modesty Refers to Wealth Not Sexuality]], [[Apologetics as Identity Politics Over Evidence]].
