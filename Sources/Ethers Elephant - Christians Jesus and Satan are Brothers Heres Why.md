A [[Ether's Elephant]] video answering the common evangelical talking point that Latter-day Saints believe "Jesus and Satan are brothers." The video's move is not to deny the kinship language but to *recontextualize* it: in LDS ontology **nothing** is created from nothing — humans, angels, demons, and God are "ontologically the same... just at different points in progression" — so calling Lucifer a "brother" of Christ under God the Father does **not** imply (as the critic assumes) that Christ is a created being. The crux, the video says, is the identity of Christ, who is "from eternity to eternity" and uncreated, exactly as orthodox Christians also affirm.

The piece then turns the problem of evil against the creedal view: if God created everything *ex nihilo*, then he created Satan knowing he would rebel and even "designed into him" heightened powers for evil — which, the video argues, makes God look like the author of evil. The LDS co-eternal model is offered as both more coherent and "more benevolent and beautiful," with God combating evil that exists *external* to him rather than authoring it.

## Summary

- **"Brothers" only bites if Jesus is created — and LDS theology denies he is.** "We do not believe that Jesus Christ was created. He's not a created being. He's from eternity to eternity." The brotherhood holds "under God the Father," but "neither is Satan and neither are we" created from nothing.[^1] The title page's "Jesus is the eternal God" is invoked.[^2] → [[Jesus and Satan Are Brothers Only If Nothing Is Created]]; cf. [[God and Man Are the Same Species]] and [[Humans Are the Same Kind as God (LDS Ontology)]].

- **Angels are a separate species for creedal Christians; one species for Latter-day Saints.** The Christian interlocutor describes Satan as a created angel — "a different creature... a different base unit." The LDS reply: "we are all ontologically the same... co-eternal beings with God," differing only in role and progression.[^3] → connects to the deeper [[Humans Are the Same Species as God (Acts 17 Offspring)]] cluster.

- **God did not author evil — agency did.** "God did not create evil or Satan's rebellious nature. Rather, Lucifer chose to rebel," per [[Moses 4]] and [[Isaiah 14.12-15|Isaiah 14]]. The video cites [[1 Corinthians 14.33|1 Cor 14:33]] ("God is not the author of confusion") and [[James 1.13|James 1:13]] ("God cannot be tempted with evil, neither tempteth he any man").[^4] → [[Jesus and Satan Are Brothers Only If Nothing Is Created]]; cf. [[Co-Eternality Solves the Problem of Evil]].

- **The creedal *ex nihilo* God looks like evil's designer.** "It almost makes God seem like he created evil because he created Satan... knowing that that angel was going to fall." Worse, Satan was "given the ability" for outsized harm. The co-eternal alternative locates evil in free agents, "so God is not creating evil... but rather evil is created by people who chose to create it."[^5] → [[Jesus and Satan Are Brothers Only If Nothing Is Created]]; cf. [[Creation Ex Nihilo Leads to Determinism]] and [[The First Morally Decisive Moment Is Creation Not Rebellion]].

- **The Old Testament barely mentions Satan.** Citing [[Jonah Barnes]]'s *Heat of the Keystone*, the video notes the OT mentions "the devil only four times" (mostly generic "devils"), and that some scholars conclude "Satan as an adversary to God is... an invention of Christianity" — which the video reads as evidence the fuller picture was *restored* through latter-day scripture.[^6] → [[The Old Testament Barely Mentions Satan]]; cf. the academic treatment at [[Satan - Development of the Figure]] and [[Book of Enoch - Origin of the Satan Figure]].
