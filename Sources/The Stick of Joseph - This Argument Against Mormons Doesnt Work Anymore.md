# The Stick of Joseph — THIS Argument Against 'Mormons' Doesn't Work Anymore

LDS apologetic reaction video on the [[Stick of Joseph]] channel: guest-hosts [[Colton Miller]] and [[Josh (Latter-day Christian)|Josh]] ("Latter-day Christian") respond to three Protestant clips arguing the Church is unbiblical. They work through (1) **Galatians 1:8** and the angel Moroni, (2) the **Jesus/Lucifer-as-brothers** trope plus **polygamy**, and (3) **Colossians** Christology and **grace vs. works**. The episode overlaps the vault's Galatians cluster, the Jesus-Satan-brothers rebuttal, and the grace/works material, and adds a war-in-heaven "two plans" framing of the brotherhood.

## Summary
- **Galatians 1:8 targets the Judaizers, not future angels.** Paul writes to the Galatian churches against Judaizers requiring circumcision and law-works *in addition to* faith; "another gospel" is that legalism, not angelic visitation. Miller cites Matthew Poole's commentary that Paul's "angel from heaven" line is *rhetoric* — a true messenger of God would never contradict the gospel — so it is not a rule that any angelic message is false. Carried in [[Galatians 1-8 - Angel Preaching Another Gospel]] and [[Galatians 1-8 the Word We Limits the Curse]] [^1][^2].
- **Even granting the angel reading, Moroni gave no new gospel.** Moroni showed Joseph Smith where the plates were; the Book of Mormon teaches the *same* gospel (3 Nephi 27:13-16; Mosiah 3:17, "no other name... whereby salvation can come... only in and through the name of Christ"). See [[Galatians 1-8 Latter-day Saints Preach Pauls Gospel]] [^3][^4].
- **The UNO-reverse: Paul was not a trinitarian.** Miller argues that *Paul's* gospel — the standard Galatians 1:8 invokes — does not include a triune God: his epistle greetings ("Grace... from God our Father, and from the Lord Jesus Christ," Ephesians 1, Colossians 1) name **two separate beings**, so a trinitarian preacher may be the one in violation. Acts 7:55 (Stephen sees the Son "standing on the right hand of God") is offered as two beings, not one. See [[Differentiated Worship Vocabulary in the New Testament Refutes the Trinity]] [^5][^6].
- **Jesus and Lucifer are spirit brothers — the war-in-heaven "two plans."** The hosts grant the brotherhood and reframe it: all are spirit children of God; Lucifer and Jesus presented competing plans in the premortal council (Lucifer: compel all, take the glory; Jesus: preserve agency, atone, glory to the Father), and Lucifer's pride in losing that vote is his fall (Jude 1:6 "first estate"; Revelation 12 "third of the host"). Angels are not a separate created species but humans in another phase. New concept: [[Jesus and Lucifer Were Two Plans in the War in Heaven]] [^7][^8][^9].
- **Polygamy: the Bible never condemns it explicitly, and Jacob 2 gives the rule.** Cliff's "the Bible never says polygamy is good" is met with 2 Samuel 12:8 (God "gave thee thy master's wives") and Genesis 32 (the Lord blesses the polygamist Jacob/Israel). [[Jacob 2|Jacob 2:30]] supplies the governing rule: monogamy is the standard ("one wife... concubines he shall have none") *unless* the Lord commands otherwise to "raise up seed unto me" — which may mean specific children, not mere quantity. Overlaps [[Catholic Marriage History Undercuts the Polygamy Attack]] [^10][^11].
- **Colossians 1:15 — Jesus is the *eikon*, the image, not the creation.** Josh reads "image of the invisible God, the firstborn of all creation" via the Greek *eikon* (icon/representation — same root as a video-game avatar) and *prototokos* / "preeminence" (*proteuo*, "holding first place"): Jesus holds first place among God's children as Savior, and is the image of the Father, a *separate* being — not a created thing and not numerically the Father. John 10:30's "one" is *hen* (unity), not one substance. See [[New Testament Hymns Affirm Christs Divinity]] and [[John 10-30 - I and the Father Are One Rebutted]] [^12][^13].
- **Grace vs. works: faith without works is dead, but grace saves.** Against "Mormons believe they're saved by works," the hosts cite Mosiah 3:17 and the Book of Mormon's pervasive grace language, read [[2 Nephi 25.23|2 Nephi 25:23]] ("after all we can do" = the broken heart and contrite spirit, not earning), and frame LDS faith as a James-style obedience (the wise/foolish builders, Matthew 7) that still rests wholly on Christ's grace. See [[After All We Can Do Means In Spite of All We Can Do]] [^14][^15].
