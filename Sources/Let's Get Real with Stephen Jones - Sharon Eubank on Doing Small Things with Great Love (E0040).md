# Let's Get Real with Stephen Jones - Sharon Eubank on Doing Small Things with Great Love (E0040)

**Show:** Let's Get Real (Episode E0040) | **Host:** [[Stephen Jones]] | **Guest:** [[Sharon Eubank]] (former director, Latter-day Saint Charities) | **Title:** "Money Can't Save the World - Here's What Can | Sharon Eubank E0040"

An interview built around Eubank's book *Doing Small Things with Great Love*, arguing that the engine of humanitarian and ministering work is not money or power but **faith expressed as relationship** — "the exchange." She illustrates the thesis with stories from a career running the Church's global relief, repeatedly correcting the assumption that scale and funding are what matter most.

- **Small acts are all we have; you are powerful where you live.** "Big acts are just a lot of small acts strung together"; ordinary disciples are "powerful where they live" because they speak the language, know the culture, and are present every day. The watershed was watching her Sri Lankan interpreter Shanta minister more effectively after the 2004 tsunami than she could with a global NGO's money.[^1] See [[Small Acts Are the Only Thing We Have]] and [[You Are Powerful Where You Live]].
- **The exchange is worth more than money.** The "premier skill" is the first question — "What do you want? What's going on in your life?" Money "creates opportunity" but "isn't the main driver"; dignity, agency, friendship, and being *seen* are. Jesus "doesn't have a fund," yet sees the Samaritan, the tax collector, the soldier.[^2] See [[The Exchange Is Worth More Than Money]].
- **Don't assume the need — ask.** Repeated cases (the soccer-field lights vs. the health clinic; the women's-shelter Christmas that shamed the mothers) show that imposing what *we* think people need backfires, while honoring agency — even letting people choose "wrongly" and learn — heals. The Marilyn/panhandler story is her own confessed blind spot.[^3] See [[Ask What People Want Instead of Assuming Their Need]].
- **Two philosophies; seek your neighbor's interest.** All scripture reduces to two options "since the Council in Heaven": prosper by managing the creature (Korihor) or seek the interest of your neighbor (Jehovah, D&C). The rich fool ([[Luke 12.16-21|Luke 12]]) stored up for himself and died that night.[^4] See [[Two Philosophies - Self-Interest Versus the Interest of Your Neighbor]].
- **Zion, agency, and faith for what we can't fix.** Her favorite verse, [[Jacob 2.17|Jacob 2:17]], reads "free with your substance" as more than money; [[D&C 58|D&C 58:6-11]] paints Zion as "a feast of fat things… for the poor." For what cannot be changed (Ukraine, Gaza), she turns to prayer and faith — Mother Teresa kneeling with the dying, the nun praying for peace in Bethlehem. The book's title is itself a Mother Teresa line: "We can't all do large things, but we can all do small things with great love."[^5] See [[Free With Your Substance Means More Than Money]] and [[Faith for the Things We Cannot Change]].
