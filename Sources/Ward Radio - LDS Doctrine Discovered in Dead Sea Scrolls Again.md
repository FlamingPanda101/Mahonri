# Ward Radio — LDS Doctrine Discovered in Dead Sea Scrolls… Again!

A Ward Radio episode in which host **[[Cardon Ellis]]** and **[[Jonah Barnes]]** argue that **[[D&C 138]]** (Joseph F. Smith's 1918 vision of the redemption of the dead) restores the ancient, widely-attested doctrine of the "harrowing of hell" — and is the "fullness of the record of John" promised in **[[D&C 93]]**.

## Summary
- **The "record of John" is the harrowing of hell.** [[D&C 93]] promises the faithful "the fullness of the record of John." Barnes argues this is **John the Baptist** — the only prophet to know Christ in mortality *and* who crossed the veil before him to prepare the way in the spirit world — and that [[D&C 138]] is that fullness. See [[D&C 138 Restores the Harrowing of Hell Recorded in the Gospel of Nicodemus]].[^1]
- **"There is no hell" — only the spirit world before judgment.** The hosts argue the fire-and-brimstone "hell" is extra-biblical (the word from a "German goddess"); what scripture describes is *sheol* / spirit prison and spirit paradise, an intermediate state where no one is yet judged, which Christ opened. See [[Hell Is Not in the Bible It Is the Spirit World Awaiting Judgment]].[^2]
- **Apocryphal attestation.** The descent is in [[1 Peter 3.18-20|1 Peter 3:18-20]] / 1 Peter 4:6, the Gospel of Nicodemus (Acts of Pilate), the Armenian "History of John," the Divine Investiture of St. Michael, 2 Maccabees, and the Apostles' Creed ("he descended into hell"). See [[The Descent of Christ to the Underworld Was Universally Attested]].[^3]
- **Brett McDonald's parallel table.** [[Brett McDonald]]'s essay "A Document Without Parallel" sets D&C 138 beside the Gospel of Nicodemus, showing the same gathered prophets (Adam, Eve, Abel, Noah, Shem, Abraham…) and the same scene of Christ descending and the dead rejoicing — directly answering Gordon B. Hinckley's description of D&C 138 as "a document without parallel." See [[D&C 138 Restores the Harrowing of Hell Recorded in the Gospel of Nicodemus]].[^4]
- **Why creedal Christianity lost it.** The hosts attribute the loss to the Greco-Roman, Platonic devaluation of the body and a God too "perfect" to descend — the same ecumenical-council impulse that produced the hypostatic union.[^5]

## Notable framing / rhetoric
- Saturday before Easter should be the big day (spirit-world preaching / temple work); the "empty tomb rolls" recipe bit; "play Doom 2 on the Saturday before Easter."[^6]
