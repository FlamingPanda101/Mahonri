# Dan McClellan — Correcting Alex O'Connor and Gavin Ortlund

[[Dan McClellan]], a credentialed Hebrew Bible scholar writing from [[Critical Scholarship|critical-scholarship]], corrects **both** atheist [[Alex OConnor|Alex O'Connor]] **and** Christian apologist [[Gavin Ortlund|Gavin Ortlund]] on the same factual error in their back-and-forth over [[Wes Huff|Wes Huff]]'s Joe Rogan claim that the Great Isaiah Scroll is "word for word identical" to the Masoretic Text. The shared mistake: both men claim the ~2,600 differences between [[Donald W. Parry|1QIsaᵃ]] and the Masoretic Text are *mostly spelling* differences. McClellan shows that is "entirely false" — the published critical edition (DJD 32) keeps **separate lists** of orthographic and textual variants, and the 2,600-plus figure is the *textual* (meaning-bearing) list, which contains **no** spelling variants.

His punchline numbers: 1QIsaᵃ aligns with the Masoretic Text in **fewer than 92.5%** of its words — *not* the "more than 95%" or "word for word identical" repeated by Huff, Ortlund, and the older apologetic sources ([[Norman Geisler|Geisler]] & Archer). McClellan grants both speakers grace for extemporaneous misspeaking; his real target is Ortlund's *equivocation* on Huff's behalf, which he reads as the signature move of apologetics: performing confidence to reassure an audience rather than engaging the data. The percentages also bear on the LDS use of DSS Isaiah — see [[Book of Mormon Isaiah Matches the Dead Sea Scrolls]].

## Summary

- **The disputed Huff claim.** Huff told Joe Rogan that what shocked scholars about the Great Isaiah Scroll was that it was "word for word identical to the Masoretic Text." McClellan: "there is absolutely no sense in which it is correct to say that the Great Isaiah scroll is word for word identical to the Masoretic Text," and the notion that it *shocked* scholars is itself false.[^1]
- **Both O'Connor and Ortlund miscall the variants as "spelling."** O'Connor (rebutting Huff) said the 2,600 variants are mostly "tiny issues like spelling"; Ortlund agreed the 2,600 figure is "potentially very misleading" because the differences are orthographic. McClellan: "this is entirely wrong — both Alex and Gavin make this mistake… the notion that the list of more than 2,600 textual variants includes spelling differences… is entirely false."[^2] See [[Orthographic and Textual Variants Are Separate Lists in DJD 32]].
- **DJD 32 keeps the two lists separate.** The critical edition of the Cave 1 Isaiah scrolls (Discoveries in the Judaean Desert vol. 32) publishes a list of orthographic variants *and a separate* list of textual variants. The editors define the textual list as readings differing "in more than mere orthography or meaning-neutral morphology," including only a negligible number of ambiguous cases — i.e. the list is *explicitly* meaning differences.[^3]
- **The actual counts.** McClellan reports 25,608 words in the Masoretic Isaiah and says he personally counted 2,771 variants listed for 1QIsaᵃ, of which 1,943 (70%) are differences from the Masoretic Text; every such variant is at least one word, some up to 19 words.[^4] See [[1QIsaiah-a Aligns With the Masoretic Text Under 92.5 Percent]].
- **"Fewer than 92.5%."** Even assuming one word per variant, that yields 92.5% alignment with MT, "but the actual number is lower than that" because some variants span multiple words. So 1QIsaᵃ "matches the Masoretic Text in fewer than 92.5% of the words." These are textual/meaning variants, not orthographic.[^5]
- **Minor vs. major meaning differences ≠ theology.** McClellan preempts the objection: when textual critics call variants "minor" or "major" they mean the *level* at which meaning changes (a portion of a word, a word, a phrase, a clause, a sentence) — not theological significance. "Minor" = a word or part of a word; "major" = phrases, clauses, sentences. There are many *major* meaning differences between 1QIsaᵃ and MT, "and that is simply not up for debate."[^6]
- **Isaiah 2:9b–2:10 is missing from 1QIsaᵃ.** O'Connor noted that the end of [[Isaiah 2.9|Isaiah 2:9]] and all of [[Isaiah 2.9|Isaiah 2:10]] are simply absent from the Great Isaiah Scroll. McClellan agrees about that scroll and adds the critical-scholarship reading: 1QIsaᵃ preserves the *earlier* text, and the MT plus reflects a **secondary addition** — corroborated by the fact that the ~20 other Qumran Isaiah copies containing those verses disagree among themselves.[^7] See [[Isaiah 2.9|Isaiah 2:9]].
- **Ortlund's "other manuscripts have it" move is a non-sequitur.** Ortlund reassured the audience that ~20 additional Qumran Isaiah copies do contain Isaiah 2:9b–10. McClellan: that is "no relevance to Wes's claim or to Alex's criticism," and if the point is that nothing was lost, it is misleading, since the consensus is that the longer reading is the secondary one.[^8]
- **The "general point was correct" defense fails.** Ortlund repeatedly retreats to "but the general point Wes was making is correct." McClellan: "there was no more general point — there was a specific claim in response to a specific question, and that claim was precise, quantified, and wrong." Generic praise of DSS transmission ("an astonishing tribute to careful copying") is fine; *quantifying* it for apologetic ends "runs into problems."[^9]
- **The 95% / "letter for letter" sources are also wrong.** The "more than 95%" figure (and "almost letter for letter with the Leningrad manuscript" for 1QIsaᵇ) traces to [[Norman Geisler|Geisler]] & Archer. McClellan: 1QIsaᵇ shows 183 textual variants plus 161 orthographic differences per DJD 32, which says "previous descriptions of the near-identity of 1QIsaᵇ with M need to be tempered." "So as usual, Geisler and Archer is incorrect."[^10] See [[1QIsaiah-a Aligns With the Masoretic Text Under 92.5 Percent]].
- **Grace for misspeaking, not for equivocation.** McClellan concedes that speaking extemporaneously for hours in a high-pressure interview "is always going to result in… misspeaking" — he has done it himself ("I say that Moses had animals on the Ark in a recent video") and corrected it. The test is accountability: "are you able to take accountability… or are you going to try to equivocate and wriggle out of it? Gavin is trying to equivocate." Huff has acknowledged forthcoming corrections; Ortlund has not.[^11]
- **The diagnosis of apologetics.** McClellan's closing thesis: apologetics' priority is "not getting the data correct" but "performing competence and confidence so that your audience who already agrees with you can be reassured that they are justified in that agreement." Neither Ortlund nor Huff has expertise here, so they repeat figures "from other apologists."[^12]
