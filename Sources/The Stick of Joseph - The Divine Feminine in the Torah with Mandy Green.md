# The Divine Feminine in the Torah with Mandy Green

Independent scholar [[Mandy Green]], introduced by host [[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)]], delivers a lecture in *The Stick of Joseph* live lecture series arguing that God the Mother "has always been there" in the Hebrew text of Genesis, that she was "decidedly removed, redacted, altered, changed, written over" by later reform, and that learning to see her patterns and symbols recovers a truth central to Latter-day Saint doctrine of Heavenly Parents.

## Summary
- **The Mother was edited out, but her traces remain in Genesis 1-3.** Green opens by acknowledging the "profound grief around the divine feminine" and states that "God the Mother was decidedly removed, redacted, altered, changed, written over in the text," yet promises to "show you she's always been there" by reading the Hebrew of the creation account, "one of the most tampered-with texts to date."[^1][^2] See [[Wisdom Personified as the Mother in Heaven]], [[Josiah's Reform Removed the Mother from the Temple]].
- **Three feminine "clues" in the first two verses.** *Elohim* is a Hebrew plural (Joseph Smith: "the head of the gods called a council of the gods"; 203 plural instances of *Elohim* in the Dead Sea Scrolls), so deity is plural from the start; the *ruach Elohim* ("Spirit of God") is a feminine noun "hovering" — a verb meaning to fold over or incubate, with maternal/avian imagery; and *Wisdom* (the Wisdom of Solomon's creative agent "who, being but one, can do all things") is "a key word for our mother in heaven."[^3][^4][^5] See [[Ruach Elohim - The Feminine Spirit Hovering Over Creation]], [[Wisdom Personified as the Mother in Heaven]].
- **Eden inverts Wisdom.** Green reads the serpent (anciently "the symbol of the son of God") as usurping Wisdom's role: Eve desires to become *wise*, but the serpent "inverts" knowledge (a masculine construct) and wisdom (a feminine construct); the fruit-bearing Tree of Life is itself a symbol of the Mother. She ties this to the Judaean Asherah pillar — a tree-trunk base topped by a woman with unusually large eyes "because wisdom gives you sight."[^6][^7][^8] See [[Asherah Pillar Iconography Depicts Wisdom Who Gives Sight]].
- **Where she was lost: the two-temple shift.** "After about the time of Isaiah, King [[Hezekiah]]," and solidifying under [[Josiah]] (age 12), the first-temple religion — Melchizedek priesthood, ascension, the menorah/tree in the Holy of Holies, a divine Mother — gives way to a law-centered, Aaronic, Jerusalem-centralized cult; sacred groves are burned, "she's totally removed from the temple," and the Law increases "almost 500%."[^9][^10] See [[Josiah's Reform Removed the Mother from the Temple]], [[Hezekiah Josiah and the Deuteronomist Reforms]].
- **Wisdom survived into early Christianity and the Restoration.** Green marshals [[Philo of Alexandria|Philo]] ("Jesus, Son of wisdom, his mother, through whom the universe came into being"), the *Teachings of Silvanus* and [[Clement of Alexandria|Clement]] ("return to your first father, and Wisdom your mother"; the high-priestly garment "woven from every wisdom"), Ben Sira, and the LDS canon — the Word of Wisdom's promise of "wisdom and great treasures of knowledge" and James 1:5 ("if any of you lack wisdom, let him ask of [[Elohim]]"), the verse that "started the restoration of the church."[^11][^12][^13][^14] See [[Wisdom Personified as the Mother in Heaven]].
