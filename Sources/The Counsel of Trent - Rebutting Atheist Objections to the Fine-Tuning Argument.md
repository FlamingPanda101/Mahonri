# The Counsel of Trent — Rebutting Atheist Objections to the Fine-Tuning Argument

[[Trent Horn]] walks through the most popular atheist objections to the fine-tuning argument (the ones that surface under "fine-tuning debunked") and answers each by insisting the argument is about the **constants and laws**, not about the universe being optimally hospitable.

## Summary
- The recurring atheist error (Thomas Westbrook / Holy Kool-Aid and others) is treating "fine-tuned" as a synonym for "designed for maximal life," when fine-tuning is a neutral claim about a narrow life-permitting range among a vast space of possibilities [^1].
- Objections from extremophiles (the tardigrade) fail: surviving extreme conditions for minutes is not the same as life *forming* under them [^2].
- [[Sean Carroll]]'s claim that the expansion rate "probability is one" conflates the gravitational constant with the cosmological constant; physicists Michael Strauss and Horn cannot reproduce it, and the cosmological constant is "the most notorious case of fine-tuning in physics" [^3].
