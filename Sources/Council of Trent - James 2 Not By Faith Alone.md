# Council of Trent — James 2: Not By Faith Alone

[[Trent Horn]] argues that the Protestant replies to **James 2:24** ("a man is justified by works and not by faith alone") fail — *and* that some Catholic uses of the verse misread the Church's own teaching.

## Summary
- The two Protestant escapes — "**different kind of faith**" (dead vs. true) and "**different kind of justification**" (before men, not God) — both collapse on the Greek and the context [^1].
- The Catholic view: **initial** justification is by grace alone (apart from faith or works) at baptism; **ongoing** justification is increased by works — and the Council of Trent cites James 2:24 only for the *increase* [^2].
- Justification is God *making* us righteous (infusion), not merely *declaring* it (legal fiction) [^3].
