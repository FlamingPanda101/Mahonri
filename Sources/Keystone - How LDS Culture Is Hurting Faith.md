# Keystone — How LDS Culture Is Hurting Faith

Interview with Jeff Strong — former global president of Johnson & Johnson, returned mission president, and author of *Torn: Why People We Love Are Leaving the Church and What We Can Learn From Them* — presenting a large LDS disaffiliation survey (~15,000 respondents) and his thesis that an imbalanced *culture*, not doctrine, is the decisive "undercurrent" driving many faithful people away.

## Summary
- **Four primary reasons people leave — but culture is the amplifier.** Strong's survey found four stated reasons for disaffiliation: lifestyle (~6%), church history (42%), social-issue/doctrinal disagreement (33%), and negative church experience (~18%). He argues the fourth — a *cultural* "wave" — "crashes over the top of the other three, making them worse," so that members with serious history or social concerns often stay if their church experience nourishes them, and leave if it does not.[^1] See [[Culture Can Crash Over Doctrine in a Faith Crisis]].
- **Leavers are sincere and invest years.** Of ~15,000 surveyed, 85% of those who stepped away "spent more than 100 hours over several years," and 49% spent effectively unlimited hours over at least five years trying to resolve their concerns — contradicting the "lazy / looking for an excuse" narrative.[^2] See [[The Four Groups of LDS Disaffiliates]] and [[Most Who Leave Drifted Away Rather Than Rejected Faith]].
- **Four cultural "soil" tensions.** Using *culture = soil* (Latin *colere*, "to cultivate"), Strong names four imbalances where the soil over-protects and under-nourishes: hard soil (standards vs. acceptance), stony shallow soil (sanctuary vs. growth), thorn-choked soil (obedience vs. agency), and single-crop soil (conformity vs. harmony).[^3]
- **Mistaking tradition for the gospel makes faith brittle.** Cherished traditions get reinforced until people sink roots into them rather than into the gospel; when a storm comes the tradition fails and they conclude "the gospel's not true," when only the tradition was. Cultural perfectionism (mis-reading Matt 5:48 "perfect" as "unblemished" rather than "whole/complete") is a prime example.[^4] See [[Mistaking Tradition for the Gospel Sets Up Brittle Faith]].
- **Conformity pressure breeds pretense.** "Single-crop soil" pressures everyone to be the same kind of Latter-day Saint; people who cannot say "I know" (vs. "I believe") get cultural signals that "there's not space for me," producing pretense and eventual withdrawal. Strong's corrective: "Standards exist to elevate people, not sort them" — members gather, God sorts.[^5] See [[Cultural Pressure to Conform Produces Pretense]].
- **The high-stakes conversation matters more than the belief.** Research with Joseph Grenny found 83% of people in faith transition reach out to talk; 40% of those conversations go badly; talking to a devout parent doubles and a devout leader quadruples the odds of going badly. Strikingly, "the conversation itself is more important than what the person actually believes and whether they stay or go" — go well and they "stay and wrestle"; go badly and "they will continue the wrestle, but somewhere else."[^6] See [[High-Stakes Faith Conversations Matter More Than the Belief]].
