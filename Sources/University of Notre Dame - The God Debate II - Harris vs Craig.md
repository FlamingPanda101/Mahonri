# The God Debate II — Harris vs Craig

A 2011 University of Notre Dame debate on the question *"Are the foundations of moral values natural or supernatural?"* Both debaters affirm that **objective** moral values and duties exist; they clash over what *grounds* them. [[William Lane Craig]] argues God is the only adequate ontological foundation, while [[Sam Harris]] argues that the well-being of conscious creatures grounds an objective, science-based morality with no need of God — and presses the problem of evil, hell, Euthyphro, and biblical morality against Craig's theism.

## Participants
- [[William Lane Craig]] (theistic foundation of morality) · [[Sam Harris]] (naturalist foundation — well-being)

## Summary
- **Shared starting point.** Both affirm objective moral values and duties; Craig even praises *The Moral Landscape* ([[The Moral Landscape - Sam Harris]]) for boldly rejecting relativism — citing Harris's own line that genitally mutilating a little girl is "horribly, objectively wrong." The whole debate is about *grounding*, not applied ethics.[^1]
- **Craig's two conditional contentions.** (1) If God exists, we have a sound foundation for objective values (grounded in God's nature) and duties (grounded in his commands); (2) if God does not exist, we have no such foundation. He stresses he is *not* arguing that God exists tonight.[^2]
- **Craig's case against naturalism.** On atheism humans are "accidental byproducts of nature"; herd morality is a biological adaptation (citing Ruse and Darwin), with no objective bindingness. Harris merely *redefines* "good" as well-being — "nothing but word play" — leaving the is/ought gap and (given Harris's denial of free will) the "ought implies can" problem unanswered.[^3]
- **Harris's positive case.** Morality concerns the well-being of conscious creatures; consciousness is the only place value can live, and "the worst possible misery for everyone" is an undeniable bad that anchors a real continuum with right and wrong ways to move along it. Science is already in the values business (the water/health analogies) — facts and values are not cleanly separable.[^4]
- **Harris's offensive against theism.** He presses the problem of evil (9 million children dying yearly; unanswered prayers), the problem of the unevangelized (Hindus and Muslims "doomed" through no fault of their own), Euthyphro, and biblical atrocities, charging that divine command theory is "psychopathic" because it makes any commanded act good by fiat — offering "no retort to the jihadist" except "you've got the wrong God."[^5]
- **Craig's replies.** Distinguishes moral *ontology* from *semantics* and *epistemology*, and *universality* from *objectivity*; calls the problem of evil and biblical-morality charges "red herrings" irrelevant to two conditional claims, and turns evil into an argument *for* God (evil exists → objective values exist → God exists). Deploys a modal "knockdown argument" that Harris's moral landscape cannot be identical to the continuum of well-being.[^6]
