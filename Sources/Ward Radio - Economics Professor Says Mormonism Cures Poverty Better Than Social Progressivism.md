# Ward Radio — Economics Professor Says Mormonism Cures Poverty Better Than Social Progressivism!

A [[Ward Radio]] episode hosted by [[Cardon Ellis]] with [[Jonah Barnes]], built around the non-LDS economist [[Tyler Cowen]]'s claim that mass conversion to Mormonism would reduce poverty more effectively than progressive policy — because the faith is a **cultural/sociological cure**, not a cash transfer. The hosts use it to rebut both the "the Church hoards money" and "tithing makes members poor" tropes. The **Cowen-poverty material is new to the vault**; the tithing/welfare rebuttal overlaps the existing church-finances cluster.

## Summary

- **Mormonism functions as a sociological cure for poverty.** Cowen (c. 2011) answered "would mass conversion to Mormonism reduce poverty?" with "as a viable solution, actually, yes" — because it changes habits, family structure, education, and self-reliance, the *roots* of poverty that policy (agnostic to culture) cannot touch; he judges it a better anti-poverty agenda "than the progressive left." See [[Mormonism as a Sociological Cure for Poverty]]. [^1]
- **The thesis is resisted because it implies agency.** Cowen notes it "makes the poor look less like victims and more like a group partially complicit in their own fate"; the hosts endorse the *partial*, efficacy-affirming version while rejecting a "just stop being poor" caricature. See [[The Poor Are Partially Complicit in Their Own Fate]]. [^2]
- **Prosperity follows discipleship as a side effect, not a goal.** The Book-of-Mormon "keep the commandments and prosper" promise is a byproduct, contrasted with prosperity-gospel preachers who chase money; and the "tithing impoverishes members" charge is rebutted (the Church often spends more on the needy than they tithe; runs self-reliance programs; could fund itself on reserves). See [[Prosperity Follows Discipleship as a Side Effect Not a Goal]]. [^3]
