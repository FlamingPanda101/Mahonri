# Data Over Dogma - Is Jesus God in the Gospel of John?

Dan McClellan argues that the Gospel of John does NOT teach that Jesus is the God of Israel in the Trinitarian sense. He examines John 1:1, 1:18, 8:58, 10:30, 10:33, and 20:28 — the six key texts used to claim Jesus's deity in John — and shows each falls short of an ontological identification with God. The episode also traces how the Trinity doctrine developed between the 2nd and 5th centuries CE, crystallizing at the Council of Nicaea (325 CE) under Roman imperial pressure.

- **John 1:1c** — "and the Word was God" mistranslates a qualitative *theos* (no definite article). The Greek means "the Word was deity/divine," not "the Word was THE God."
- **The Trinity** — a later philosophical systematization (2nd-5th c.), not indigenous to any NT author. Enforced by imperial coercion at Nicaea.
- **Divine Name theology** — Jesus bears the divine name as an authorized agent, which explains "I AM" statements and Thomas's "my Lord and my God" without ontological identity.
