# How Evolution Might Show Us Purpose and Meaning

[[Jacob Hansen]] (Thoughtful Faith) interviews **[[Samuel T. Wilkinson]]**, an associate professor of psychiatry at the Yale School of Medicine, about his 2024 book *Purpose: What Evolution and Human Nature Imply About the Meaning of Our Existence* (Simon & Schuster). Wilkinson — a BYU mechanical-engineering and Johns Hopkins-trained physician — argues that the standard claim "life has no purpose" is a *philosophical assumption* smuggled into evolution, not a scientific result, and that recent biology (especially the convergence work of [[Simon Conway Morris]]) shows evolution is "seeded with inevitabilities" rather than purely random. From the non-randomness of evolution and the structure of human nature, he reverse-engineers an account in which family relationships, moral agency, and the human capacity for love point toward a purposeful cosmos. The conversation closes with Wilkinson's striking claim that science can "show that we're designed for" the LDS *Family Proclamation*.

## Summary
- **"Life has no purpose" is an assumption, not a finding.** Wilkinson stresses that no experiment ever concluded that existence is meaningless; that verdict rests on the premise that evolution is "a totally random and haphazard process" — a premise he says is "simply not the case." [^1] See [[Meaninglessness Is a Philosophical Assumption Not a Scientific Result]].
- **Evolution is constrained, not random.** Citing Cambridge biologist [[Simon Conway Morris]] and the phenomenon of convergence (eyes evolving independently ~40 times), Wilkinson argues evolution is "constrained to go in certain directions" — like injection-molded plastic filling a predetermined mold. [^2][^3] See [[Evolutionary Convergence Implies a Directed Process]].
- **Human nature is built from opposing dispositions.** Levels-of-selection ("survival of the fittest *what*?") produced a "dual potential": selfishness *and* altruism, aggression *and* cooperation. Combined with free will (LDS "agency"), this makes life "a test of character." [^4][^5] See [[The Dual Potential of Human Nature as Moral Test]].
- **Evolution is "pro-family," and love is its deepest product.** The helplessness of human infants drove the evolution of profound parental love (attachment theory, oxytocin); the parent-child bond was "exapted" to make long-term pair-bonding possible. The deepest human joys come through family relationships. [^6][^7] See [[Evolution Points to Family as the Locus of Meaning]].
- **The purpose framing is "reverse engineering."** Quoting Charles Townes — "if the universe has a purpose or meaning, this must be reflected in its structure" — Wilkinson reads non-randomness, the dual nature, and family bonding as nature *implying* a purpose, which for him resonates intensely with the LDS *Family Proclamation*. [^8][^9]
