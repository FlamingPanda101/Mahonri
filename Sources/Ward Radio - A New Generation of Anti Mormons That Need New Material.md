# Ward Radio — Oh look! It's a NEW Generation of Anti Mormons that need NEW Material!

A Ward Radio reaction episode in which host **[[Cardon Ellis]]**, with apologists **[[Dalton Williams]]** (Unashamed Truth), **[[Devin Barnes]]** (Latterday Logic), and **[[Braden Gospel Guardian|Braden]]** (Gospel Guardian), respond to an Instagram reel ("Are Mormons really Christian?") and argue its "new generation" objections are the same recycled material.

## Summary
- **"Become like God" is taught by the church fathers.** Against the reel's claim that deification is a uniquely Mormon error, the panel reads the patristic "exchange formula" — Irenaeus, Clement of Alexandria, Ephrem, Gregory of Nazianzus, Gregory of Nyssa, Athanasius — from a non-LDS interfaith source. See [[All the Church Fathers Taught Apotheosis]] and [[Christ Became Man That We Might Become God - The Exchange Formula]].[^1]
- **Genesis 3 affirms it; it was not only Satan's lie.** The reel says "you can become a god" is "the same lie from Genesis." The panel answers with [[Genesis 3.5|Genesis 3:5]] (the serpent: "ye shall be as gods") *and* [[Genesis 3.22|Genesis 3:22]] (God: "the man is become as one of us") — God affirms the very thing, so it was not simply a lie. See [[Genesis 3 Affirms Humans Became Like God It Was Not Only a Lie]].[^2]
- **"Define Christianity" before contrasting it.** The hosts press that "in Christianity, God is…" collapses under "which Christianity?" — Catholic, Presbyterian, Pentecostal — until it reduces to "the reformed Baptist church of the baby Jesus… in their hometown." See [[Define Christianity Before Contrasting It With Mormonism]].[^3]
- **"After all we can do" = "in spite of," not "earn the rest."** [[2 Nephi 25.23|2 Nephi 25:23]] is glossed via 1830s English (and Jonathan Edwards) as "despite all we can do." See [[After All We Can Do Means In Spite of All We Can Do]].[^4]
- **Jesus/Lucifer "brothers" does not mean created.** Being a sibling under God the Father does not make Christ a created being; the Book of Mormon's title page calls him "the eternal God." See [[Jesus and Satan Are Brothers Only If Nothing Is Created]].[^5]
- **"New scripture means truth was never stable" backfires.** The panel counters with Peter's vision opening the gospel to the Gentiles (Acts 10) — new revelation reshaping the covenant without truth being "unstable" — and cites theosis proof-texts ([[2 Peter 1.4|2 Peter 1:4]], [[Romans 8.14-17|Romans 8:17]], 1 John 3:2, Revelation 3:21, Matthew 5:48).[^6]
- **The asymmetry of steelmanning.** Cardon argues LDS apologists know evangelical proof-texts (God is spirit, etc.) better than evangelicals know the LDS-supporting verses (baptism for the dead in 1 Corinthians 15:29), and laments online critics who "haven't even read the Bible."[^7]

## Notable framing / rhetoric
- Sustained mockery of the anonymous creator ("Angel with Tattoos") — the unread, pristine Book of Mormon ("hot off the press"), mispronouncing "Pearl of Great Price"/"Trinity," Star Trek "next generation"/Klingon bits.
- A more conciliatory closing note (from Dalton): the younger critics seem more sincere ("backwards love") and less hateful than the prior generation; open invitations to David Lattin et al. to come on the show.
