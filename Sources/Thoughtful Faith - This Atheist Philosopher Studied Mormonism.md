# Thoughtful Faith - This Atheist Philosopher Studied Mormonism

[[Jacob Hansen]] presents an extended interview with the agnostic philosopher [[Emerson Green]] (host of *Counter Apologetics*), conducted on the *New Heretics* podcast. Green — raised Protestant, "unambiguously atheist for ten years," now agnostic — explains why he defends Latter-day Saints despite not being one: he finds Mormonism philosophically advantaged (finite theism, materialism, an eternal universe, panpsychism-friendly metaphysics), thinks there is a "non-zero amount of evidence" for the Book of Mormon, and judges the customary scorn heaped on Mormonism to be a socially-acceptable double standard. The bulk of the episode walks through Cameron Bertuzzi's ("Capturing Christianity") "15 reasons Islam and Mormonism are fake," which Green and his host find self-refuting.

- Mormonism's **finite God** and naturalist-friendly metaphysics make it "the least renovating" move from naturalism to theism (Trent Horn's observation) — see [[Naturalism Is a Short Step to Mormonism]].[^1]
- The **"phantom prophecy"** charge (Genesis 50:33 / 2 Nephi 3) is a double standard, since the Bible also references lost texts (Q, the books of Adam) — see [[Phantom Prophecy Both Prophets Re-Reveal Lost Predictions of Themselves]].[^2]
- Bringing up Book of Mormon **proper names** backfires: the names avoid Q/X/W (as ancient Hebrew does) and a dozen "gibberish" names were later independently verified — see [[Q X and W Are Absent From Book of Mormon Names as From Ancient Hebrew]] and [[Book of Mormon Names Show Authorial Wordplay]].[^3]
- The **"19th-century influence"** and **View of the Hebrews** arguments prove too much (Paul quoted Stoics; Aquinas used Aristotle) and rest on confusing the text with interpretations of it — see [[Cultural Influence on Scripture Proves Too Much Against Christianity Too]] and [[Conflating the Book of Mormon Text With Interpretations of It]].[^4]
- The **private-revelation / eyewitness** double standard: Joseph Smith preached publicly and produced 19 signed witnesses, versus anonymous secondhand reports of the 500 — see [[The Eyewitness Double Standard Favors the Gold Plates Over the Resurrection]].[^5]
- Green's stated remaining obstacle is **theism itself** (the problem of evil), which he thinks bites finite theism too via [[Animal Suffering Is the Hardest Test for Finite Theism|animal suffering]]; on Joseph Smith's character he sides with *Rough Stone Rolling* against the con-man model — see [[The Fuller Picture of Joseph Smith Undercuts the Self-Conscious-Fraud Model]].[^6]
