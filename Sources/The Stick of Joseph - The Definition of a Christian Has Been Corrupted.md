# The Stick of Joseph — The Definition of 'a Christian' Has Been Corrupted

LDS apologetic video on the [[Stick of Joseph]] channel: host [[Hayden Carroll]] (recording from a military deployment at 29 Palms) argues that the question "are Latter-day Saints Christians?" is wrongly settled by the third- and fourth-century **creeds**, and proposes a different metric — a **fruits-based, "doeth the will of the Father"** definition drawn from the Sermon on the Mount. He plays and comments on [[Jeffrey R. Holland]]'s 2007 General Conference address, which concedes the LDS view of the Godhead "breaks with post-New Testament Christian history" while denying the Trinity is a New Testament doctrine. Overlaps the vault's [[Are Latter-day Saints Christians]] and [[Define Christian First and the Concession Follows]]; adds the Matthew 7 fruits definition and the John 17 oneness argument applied to the label question.

## Summary
- **The creedal standard is "bunk."** Carroll concedes that *if* "Christian" means believing the Catholic/Protestant nature of Christ (the Nicene Trinity), then Latter-day Saints are not — but he rejects that as the standard Christ set. He asks Protestants and Catholics for "a little bit of humility" about *why* they believe the creeds are true, given the documented contention at the councils and that dissenters "were just cast out as heretics." See [[Are Latter-day Saints Christians]] [^1][^2].
- **Holland's concession: the Trinity is not in the New Testament.** The clip has [[Jeffrey R. Holland]] quote the Harper's Bible Dictionary ("the formal doctrine of the Trinity as it was defined by the great church councils of the fourth and fifth centuries is not to be found in the New Testament") and the LDS Bible Dictionary, framing the LDS Godhead as a *return* "to the doctrine taught by Jesus himself," and narrating Nicaea (325 AD) and the later creeds that declared God "without body, parts, or passions." See [[The Trinity and Consubstantiality Are Not in the Bible]] and [[Without Body Parts or Passions Is a 1530 Protestant Creed]] [^3][^4].
- **John 17 oneness is unity, not one substance.** Carroll's exegetical anchor: in the intercessory prayer Jesus prays that all believers "may be one... *as* thou Father art in me, and I in thee" (John 17:21-22) — the same oneness he shares with the Father he asks us to share, so it cannot be a numerical oneness of substance with three persons. See [[John 17 Oneness Is Indwelling Not Consubstantiality]] [^5].
- **A new definition: "Christian" = belonging to Christ by doing the Father's will.** The etymology of *Christianos* is "follower of / belonging to Christ." Carroll reads Matthew 7:20-23 ("by their fruits ye shall know them"; "not every one that saith Lord, Lord... but he that doeth the will of my Father"; "I never knew you") as Christ's *own* membership test: those Christ recognizes are those *trying to follow him* and not working iniquity — not those who pass a Trinity quiz. New concept: [[Christian Means Belonging to Christ by Doing the Fathers Will]] [^6][^7].
- **The lived-Christianity concession cuts against the creedal gate.** Carroll notes that Catholic and Protestant apologists routinely concede Latter-day Saints "are the greatest people ever... so kind... service-oriented" yet consign them to hell solely for rejecting the Nicene Trinity — which, he argues, inverts Jesus' own fruits test. He closes by re-affirming the Godhead as one "in purpose... in love, in gentleness, kindness, justice, grace, mercy," echoing Holland [^8].
