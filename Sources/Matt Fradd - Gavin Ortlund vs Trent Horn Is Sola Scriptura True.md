# Matt Fradd — Gavin Ortlund vs Trent Horn: Is Sola Scriptura True

A formal moderated debate on the resolution **"Is sola scriptura true"** — whether Scripture is the *only infallible rule* for the church's faith and practice. **[[Gavin Ortlund]]** (Reformed Baptist pastor, Ph.D. historical theology) takes the **affirmative**; **[[Trent Horn]]** (Catholic Answers) takes the **negative**; **[[Matt Fradd]]** hosts. Both men agree the Bible *is* an infallible rule; the entire debate turns on Ortlund's premise 2 — that there is *no other* infallible rule. Format: 15-min openings, 7- and 4-min rebuttals, 20-min cross-examinations each, 30-min Q&A, 5-min closings.[^1]

## Summary
- **Ortlund's affirmative** is a single syllogism resting on two considerations — Scripture's **nature** (it alone is the God-breathed speech of God, so "God is unique, therefore his speech is unique; that which isn't the speech of God is subordinate to that which is") and Scripture's **role** (it is consistently placed *over* other legitimate authorities, e.g. Jesus over tradition in Mark 7). He then argues the *alternatives* (ongoing post-apostolic infallibility) have four "decisive problems": no precedent in the OT, no instantiation in the NT, unknown in the early church, and overturned by later church history.[^2] His nature-argument and Horn's rebuttal that the load-bearing premise is unproven are already captured in [[The Word of God Fallacy - The Load-Bearing Premise Is Unproven]]; his canon move (recognition ≠ possession of infallibility, the burning bush) in [[The Church Recognizes Scripture It Does Not Make It]].
- **Ortlund's framing device** is the **"80% Rule"**: ~80% of critiques attack a caricature, collapsing the magisterial-Protestant middle ("only infallible *rule*," not "only authority," not "explicit source for every doctrine"). See the new [[The 80 Percent Rule - Sola Scriptura Is Caricatured Into the Excluded Middle]].
- **Horn's negative** is primarily a **logical** attack on premise 2: inferring "the only infallible rule" from "no other has been proven" is an **appeal to ignorance**, the fallacy of treating *one* member of a kind as the *only* member — "white is one color of swans; people thought it was the only color until black swans." See the new [[One Member of a Kind Is Not the Only Member - The Black Swan Reply]].
- Horn's three positive arguments — material sufficiency entails sola scriptura is unbiblical and therefore false; apostolic/patristic silence about a shift to sola scriptura; and that other infallible rules (sacred tradition, the ecumenical councils) demonstrably exist — overlap heavily with existing vault notes ([[Theopneustos Means Life-Giving Not Infallible]], [[Profitable Is a Fractional Word Not a Claim of Sufficiency]], [[Word of God Means Prophecy Preaching or Christ Not Scripture]], [[The Canon Premise Is an Infallible Rule Outside Scripture]], [[A Living Magisterium Settles What Sola Scriptura Cannot]]).
- **The councils gambit (new):** Horn observes that the Arian heretic Maximinus appealed to **2 Timothy 3:16** and "those words not found in the scriptures are under no circumstance accepted by us" to reject Nicaea's non-biblical *homoousios* — so sola scriptura's own logic underwrote Arianism, and [[Augustine]], [[Ambrose of Milan]], and Theodoret answered by appeal to the *council's authority*, not to bare Scripture. See the new [[The Arian Used Sola Scriptura to Reject Nicaea]].
- **Cross-examination — the symmetry war:** Ortlund presses Horn into conceding there is "a **fallible list of infallible teachings**" (no fixed number of papal/traditional infallible pronouncements). Horn parries that the Protestant has the mirror problem — a fallible list of essential doctrines, a fallible canon, unsettled textual variants — so the concession cuts both ways. See the new [[A Fallible List of Infallible Teachings - The Symmetry of Uncertainty]].
- **Ortlund's historical defeater:** the 2018 catechism reversal on the **death penalty** (from "irreformable" per Feser to "inadmissible") and **Florence's** "no salvation outside the church" vs Vatican II — supposedly irreformable teachings reformed. Horn replies it takes *two* infallible teachings to make a contradiction, the originals were never infallibly defined, and the same tension afflicts the Protestant Bible (slavery in Leviticus). See the new [[Irreformable Teachings Reformed - The Death Penalty Defeater]].
- **The hinge dispute** is whether the phrase "word of God" in Mark 7 denotes *written Scripture* (Ortlund) or only oral prophetic/apostolic proclamation (Horn) — already covered by [[The Word of God Is Not Confined to the Written Word]] and [[Word of God Means Prophecy Preaching or Christ Not Scripture]]. Horn also reframes scripture/tradition with a Trinitarian analogy — "prior but not higher," as the Spirit proceeds from the Father yet is equally divine.[^3]
