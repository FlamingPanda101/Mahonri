# The Stick of Joseph — NEW Hebrew Symbolism Found in Alma 32 (Steve Reed)

LDS apologetic interview on the [[Stick of Joseph]] channel: hosts [[Hayden Carroll]] and [[Jackson Paul]] with guest [[Steve Reed]] (blogger, *One Climbs*) presenting a reading of **Alma 32-33** through Hebrew letter-symbolism and **gematria** (the numeric values of Hebrew letters). Reed argues Alma's sermon to the poor Zoramites is saturated with deliberate patterns of **four** and **three** (summing to **seven**), keyed to the meanings of the corresponding Hebrew letters, and structured as a **temple-ascension** narrative ending in the brazen serpent. The framing payload: such patterning evidences an ancient Hebrew author, not a "23-year-old farm boy." Overlaps [[Alma 32 Light Alludes to Genesis 1 Creation]] and the vault's temple/serpent cluster; adds the gematria/number-pattern thesis.

## Summary
- **Hebrew letters are numbers, and Psalm 29 shows the technique.** Reed opens by establishing gematria and uses Psalm 29 (eleven verses) as a control: seven occurrences of "the voice of the Lord," seven of God's powers over nature, three of "ascribe" and four of "glory" (3+4=7), with verse 7 ("divideth the flames of fire") keyed to the sword-symbolism of the seventh letter *zayin*. This shows the patterning "isn't isolated to the Book of Mormon." See [[Psalm 29.1-2|Psalm 29]] [^1].
- **Patterns of four saturate Alma 32.** Reed counts fours everywhere: four "poor"s clustered around the hill Onidah (which the BYU onomasticon glosses "he knows my affliction"); the four effects of the planted word (enlarge / enlighten / delicious — first letters spelling "seed"); planted/swelleth/sprouteth/grow; the four things cast out (synagogues, seed from the heart, the tree from the ground, eyes toward the Son of God); four witnesses (Zenos, Zenock, Moses, Alma); and sixteen ("4×4") mentions of "seed" and of "believe." See [[Alma 32.27-43|Alma 32]] [^2][^3].
- **The number four is keyed to the fourth Hebrew letter (dalet).** *Dalet* (fourth letter) means "the weak/poor" — one who hangs the head down — and also a "door/pathway to a new beginning." Alma repeats four "to the poor Zoramites," bringing the theology into their situation (hence preaching from a hill named "he knows my affliction"). New concept: [[Gematria and Patterns of Three and Four Structure Alma 32]] [^4].
- **Patterns of three are keyed to the third letter (gimel), and 4+3=7.** Reed then counts threes (experiment ×3, scriptures ×3, heal ×3, faith-diligence-patience ×3, nine = 3×3 mentions of "nourish") and keys them to *gimel* (third letter) — "to deal kindly / nourish," pictured as a camel (sustenance/endurance) and a rich man giving to the poor — so Alma (rich in the word) descends as the *gimel* to lift the *dalet* (poor). Four (the poor) plus three (the benefactor) = seven (completion); Alma 34:3 even carries "4-3" → "reap the rewards of your faith and diligence and patience and long-suffering" [^5].
- **The sermon is a temple-ascension narrative.** Reed reads seed → tree → fruit as the three temple rooms (plant/nourish/feast = outer court / holy place / holy of holies), with multiples (eight = transition/veil, keyed to *het*; ten = completion/mercy-seat, keyed to *yod*/hands → ten "mercy"s and the Day of Atonement) layering the Tree of Life, the Ark/Mercy Seat, and the *nehushtan* into Alma's sermon. Connects to [[The Sermon on the Mount Is an Ordinance in Three Rooms]] and [[Lehi's Dream Encodes the Three Temple Rooms by Hebrew Name-Puns]] [^6][^7].
- **Alma 33 ends in the brazen serpent: believe, look, heal.** Drawing on [[Numbers 21.8-9|Numbers 21]], Reed notes the people "would not look" because they did not believe — so the order is believe → look → heal, a three-part movement Alma turns into Christ ("awake and arouse your faculties... cast about your eyes... begin to believe in the Son of God"). Zenos's nested prayer (wilderness / field / house / closet) maps the temple's graded spaces. See [[The Nehushtan Is a Healing Divine Serpent the Nephites and Early Christians Valued]] [^8][^9].
- **Mesch and nahash (serpent/Messiah) and the Psalms-as-temple-script.** Reed cites the gematria equality of *nahash* (serpent) and *mashiach* (Messiah) (from Dave Butler's *In the Language of Adam*), and — riffing with the hosts — that Jesus on the cross quoting Psalm 22 ("my God, my God") invokes a psalm that "is the temple ordinance... the script for the temple" (crediting Mike Day), so Christ declares himself the one ritually worshiped there. See [[John 3.14 Compares Christ to the Serpent Not Just the Lifting Up]] [^10][^11].
- **The cumulative argument and its hedges.** Reed's repeated frame: like the Great Pyramid, the density of intentional patterning shows "many people were involved" with "a lot of intelligence" — i.e., an ancient Hebrew author writing for a Hebrew audience, not Joseph Smith cribbing Jonathan Edwards's "faith is a seed" sermon (the critic's claim). He repeatedly concedes individual patterns "might be a coincidence," "God just kind of winking at you," and that this "scratches the surface." The closing third of the episode turns to a devotional discussion of the Trinity / "one God" question (Alma 33:1), the lectures-on-faith "mind of God," and overcoming sin by ascending in mind rather than "fighting" it [^12][^13].
