# Thoughtful Faith — Can Eternal Truth Be Altered? Dealing With Change In The "Mormon" Church

**Channel:** Thoughtful Faith ([[Jacob Hansen]])
**Format:** Compilation of an earlier multi-part series on doctrinal change
**Featured clips:** [[Anthony Sweat]] (airplane/leading-astray analogy), [[Patrick Mason]] and [[Bill Reel]] (critics quoted), [[Greg Matson]] (polygamy explanation)

## Summary
- Lays out a framework for what *can* and *cannot* change in the Church: a thing is defined by its **essential characteristics**, and the Church distinguishes **doctrine** (core truth claims) from **practice/policy** and **culture**, which are expected to change with context. See [[Essential Characteristics Define What the Church Is]] and [[Doctrine vs Practice vs Culture - The Three-Tier Taxonomy]].
- The governing epistemology is the **collective prophetic witness**: prophets need not be infallible, only more reliable than any rival source; a teaching is weighed by how well-attested it is *across dispensations*, and changes must be a *change*, not a *contradiction* of the collective witness. See [[Change Is Not the Same as Contradiction]], plus the existing cluster [[The Collective Prophetic Witness]], [[The Law of Witnesses Establishes Truth]], and [[Fallible But Reliable - Why Authority Still Counts]].
- Applies the model to hard cases: the **priesthood ban** was 1850s false doctrine borrowed from Protestant race theory (corrected *back* to the collective witness in 1978); **polygamy** never changed in doctrine (monogamy is the norm, plural marriage authorized at times per [[Jacob 2]]); and **gay marriage** is *not* analogous to either. The critic's rapid-fire list of "changed teachings" is a [[Steel Man Versus Gish Gallop in Apologetic Dialogue|Gish Gallop]] that collapses into a few categories. See [[The Priesthood Ban Was 1850s False Doctrine From Protestant Race Theory]], [[Polygamy Doctrine Did Not Change Only the Exaltation-Requires-Polygamy Idea Did]], [[Gay Marriage Is Not Analogous to the Priesthood Ban or Polygamy]], and [[The Changed-Teachings Gish Gallop Collapses Into a Few Categories]].
