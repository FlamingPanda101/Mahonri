# Jesus Referenced the Apocrypha — Blood of Abel to Zechariah

Ward Radio episode where Jonah (author of *The Lost Gems of Genesis* and *The
Key to the Keystone*) demonstrates that Jesus's cryptic reference to "the blood
of Abel unto the blood of Zacharias" (Luke 11:47-51) draws on apocryphal
traditions familiar to His audience — and constitutes a savage rebuttal to the
Pharisees' hand-washing legalism.

## The Biblical Passage

**Luke 11:37-51** — Pharisees criticize Jesus for not washing before eating.
Jesus responds:
> "Woe unto you! for ye build the sepulchres of the prophets, and your
> fathers killed them... Therefore also said the wisdom of God, I will send
> them prophets and apostles, and some of them they shall slay and
> persecute: That the blood of all the prophets, which was shed from the
> foundation of the world, may be required of this generation; From the
> blood of Abel unto the blood of Zacharias, which perished between the
> altar and the temple."

## The Apocryphal Backstory

### Abel's Blood (Genesis 4 / Apocrypha)
The Bible gives the bare fact: Cain killed Abel. The apocryphal traditions
(attested in multiple independent sources) fill in what "everybody knew":

- **The earth rejected burial**: Cain tried to bury Abel's body, but the earth
  refused. The earth had declared: "I will not allow anyone to be buried until
  Adam is buried first" — Adam was the first taken from the earth, and must be
  the first returned.
- **The blood never dried**: Abel's blood remained visible, a perpetual
  testimony against Cain.
- **Cain permanently stained**: Some traditions say Cain bore Abel's blood on
  his skin forever — possibly the "mark of Cain."
- **Abel's body preserved**: The corpse "remained intact as though alive and
  asleep" — no decomposition — until Adam's death, at which point it could
  finally be buried.

**Sources attesting this**: Armenian History of the Sons of Adam, Cave of
Treasures (Ephraim the Syrian), Greek Life of Adam and Eve, Penitence of Our
Forefather Adam, the Quran.

### Zechariah's Blood (2 Chronicles 24 / Mishnah)

**Biblical account (2 Chronicles 24:20-21)**:
> "The Spirit of God came upon Zechariah the son of Jehoiada the priest...
> and they conspired against him, and stoned him with stones at the
> commandment of the king in the court of the house of the LORD."

Zechariah was a priest's son and prophet who preached messianic prophecies
(including Zechariah 9:9 — "thy King cometh... riding upon an ass"). They
stoned him to death **inside the temple courtyard** — on holy ground.

**Apocryphal continuation (Mishnah Sanhedrin 96b)**:
200 years later, Nebuchadnezzar entered Jerusalem to destroy it. When he
entered the temple, he found **Zechariah's blood still boiling on the stones**
— it had never stopped since the murder. He asked what it was, and the priests
said, "That is the blood of Zechariah, who preached about the Messiah and we
murdered him here." Nebuchadnezzar killed priests by the thousands trying to
appease the blood — it only stopped after mass slaughter.

### The "Lives of the Prophets" Corroboration
After Zechariah's murder, "the priests in the temple could no longer see any
angels, heard the voice of God, and the altar was not lit. The temple lost its
sanctity." Zechariah is considered the last messianic prophet before the temple
was defiled and Judah fell under condemnation.

## Jesus's Point
The Pharisees lecture Jesus about ritual hand-washing (a Deuteronomistic
innovation). Jesus fires back:

> "You want to talk about washing? The blood of Abel never washed away. The
> blood of Zechariah boiled on your temple stones for 200 years. Your
> fathers murdered the prophets — including the one who preached about ME —
> in this blood-soaked, defiled temple. And YOU lecture ME about
> cleanliness?"

This is not random "A to Z" shorthand — it's a specific, culturally understood
reference to two famous instances of blood that **refused to be cleansed**.

## Apologetic Significance

1. **Jesus used Apocrypha**: The stories He references are not in the Old
   Testament canon. Jesus's audience knew them — they were part of the oral
   and literary tradition. If Jesus treated extra-biblical texts as legitimate
   reference points, the LDS openness to additional scripture is consistent
   with His example.

2. **The Deuteronomistic corruption**: Jonah's broader thesis — Deuteronomy
   was "found" in this same defiled, blood-soaked temple and represents a
   corrupt rewriting by the same school that killed the prophets. The
   hand-washing laws Jesus rejects are Deuteronomistic innovations.

3. **Multiple attestation**: The Abel and Zechariah traditions appear across
   independent sources (Jewish, Christian, Armenian, Ethiopic, Islamic) —
   the same historiographical method used to verify Alexander the Great's
   conquests.

4. **"Everybody gets on us for reading extra-biblical texts. You know who
   else read them? Jesus read them."** — Cardon
