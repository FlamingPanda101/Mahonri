# Ward Radio — Jesus Referenced the Apocrypha

[[Cardon Ellis]] and [[Jonah (Plain and Precious Publishing)|Jonah]] argue that Jesus's phrase "the blood of Abel unto the blood of Zachariah" ([[Luke 11.47-51|Luke 11:47-51]]) is not a generic "A to Z," but a pointed reference to two **apocryphal blood traditions** his audience knew.

## Summary
- "Everybody gets on us for reading these extra-biblical texts — you know who else read them? Jesus read them." Jesus's allusions legitimize the traditions [^1].
- The **blood of Abel** (earth would not let his body be buried; blood never dried) and the **blood of Zechariah** (boiling for 200 years in the temple) are both blood that "stains forever" — the point of Jesus's rebuke to hand-washing critics [^2].
- These traditions implicate **Deuteronomistic corruption**: the prophet-killing school that "found" Deuteronomy in the blood-soaked temple [^3].
