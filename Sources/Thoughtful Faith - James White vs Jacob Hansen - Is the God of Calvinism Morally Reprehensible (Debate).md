# Full Debate: James White vs Jacob Hansen — Is the God of Calvinism Morally Reprehensible?

A formal moderated debate, held Good Friday, on the resolution **"The God of Calvinism is morally reprehensible"** (affirmative: [[Jacob Hansen]]; negative: [[James White]]). Hansen argues the case not as a Latter-day Saint but as a stand-in for the "97% of Christians" who reject Calvinism, pressing a simple syllogism: the true God is all-loving, the God of Calvinism is not all-loving, therefore the God of Calvinism is not the true God. White refuses to defend Calvinism on Hansen's emotional terms and instead argues that only a transcendent Creator-God (not "an exalted man from another planet") can ground the very notion of "morally reprehensible," and that Scripture (Romans 9, Ephesians 1:11, Daniel 4) teaches God's exhaustive sovereignty over all that comes to pass.

## Participants

[[Jacob Hansen]] (affirmative — Latter-day Saint apologist, *Thoughtful Faith*) vs. [[James White]] (negative — Reformed Baptist theologian, Alpha & Omega Ministries; author of *The Potter's Freedom*). Moderated; format = opening statements, rebuttals, two rounds of cross-examination, closing statements, and audience Q&A.

## Summary

- **Hansen's core argument is a moral syllogism, not a Mormon one.** He explicitly declines to argue from LDS premises: "Premise one, the true God is all loving. Premise two, the God of Calvinism is not all loving. Therefore, the God of Calvinism is not the true God." [14:30][^syll] He walks through TULIP point by point, illustrating each with a (fictional) story of a fisherman who saves only some of several drowning little girls and watches the rest die. [03:31]–[11:30][^tulip]
- **White reframes the debate as a contest of theology-proper: a transcendent God vs. "an exalted man from another planet."** "One of us has a God with a scripture... and one of us has an exalted man from another planet. Those are two completely different paradigms." [19:32][^paradigm] He argues only an eternal, uncreated Creator can be the standard that makes "morally reprehensible" meaningful, citing Daniel 4, Psalm 89:14, Romans 3, and Romans 9. [20:00]–[31:00][^white-texts]
- **The clash over the author of evil.** Hansen presses that if God put the tree in Eden intending the Fall, and decrees "whatever comes to pass" including rape (which White affirms, on the ground that otherwise it would be "purposeless"), then God, not the creature, is the ultimate origin of evil. [65:30]–[70:00][^author] White answers with the primary/secondary causation distinction and Acts 4 (Pilate, Herod, the Romans, and the Jews did "what your hand and your purpose predestined to take place"). [67:01]–[68:00][^acts4]
- **The grounding-of-morality exchange.** White repeatedly demands Hansen "define what you mean by goodness"; Hansen grounds morality in love and the "nature of reality itself," calling love a "brute fact." [90:00]–[92:00][^brute] White retorts that "brute facts are irrational" (citing Greg Bahnsen vs. Stein) and that grounding morality in human "experience" is insufficient. [92:30]–[94:00], [104:02][^experience]
- **Dueling appeals to the early church.** Hansen cites Justin Martyr, Irenaeus, and Clement of Alexandria as teaching libertarian free will against Calvinist determinism; White answers that Justin was a Greek philosopher "not influenced almost at all by the Apostle Paul," and that Matthew 23:37 is a judgment text, not a proof of resistible grace. [72:31]–[75:01][^fathers]
- **Closing C.S. Lewis appeal.** Hansen ends with [[C.S. Lewis]]: "free will is what made evil possible... [but] is also the only thing that makes possible any love or goodness or joy worth having." [97:02]–[98:30][^lewis] White closes that a God "big enough" to sovereignly use even evil for His glory (Ephesians 1) is the God of Scripture, and that Joseph Smith "repudiated Psalm 90:2." [104:33]–[105:31][^white-close]
