# Ward Radio - Debunking Anti-Mormon Free Mason and Seer Stone Myths

Host [[Cardon Ellis]] with historian [[Don Bradley]] (author of *The Lost 116 Pages*), [[Brad Whitbeck]], and [[Sean Bailey]]. Featured guest **Don Bradley** lays out the research that, while he was a non-believer working on his MA, persuaded him the temple endowment's structure predates Joseph Smith's 1842 Freemasonry.

## Summary
- **The interpreters as compass and square.** Per [[Joseph Smith Sr]] (the [[Fayette Lapham]] interview) and [[Lucy Mack Smith]], the top plate of the gold plates bore Masonic-style compass-and-square engravings with the two triangular interpreters set over them — which Bradley reconstructs as an equilateral + right triangle, sealable as a Star of David. See [[The Nephite Interpreters Were Shaped Like the Masonic Compass and Square]].
- **A lost Nephite interpreter-acquisition narrative.** The Lapham interview preserves a story (Bradley argues, of [[Mosiah]] I) of a leader led by the Liahona to the interpreters and told to cover his face with an animal skin — a Tabernacle/veil pattern paralleling the [[Brother of Jared]]'s ascent in [[Ether 3]] and answering how the Nephites got the interpreters ([[Mosiah 28.11-16]]). See [[The Jaredite and Nephite Interpreters Carry the Endowment Ascent Pattern]].
- **The seer-stone discovery and theosis.** From Dr. [[William Purple]]'s notes on the 1826 hearing: Joseph looked into [[Sally Chase]]'s green stone, saw his own white stone 150 miles off under a tree's roots, recovered it, and said he had "acquired one of the attributes of deity, an all-seeing eye." Bradley reads the touched-eyes First Vision and this seer gift as deification (theosis) present from day one. See [[Joseph's Seer-Stone Discovery Frames Theosis From the Start of the Restoration]] and [[The JST of Psalm 14 Shows the First Vision's Two Personages Were Early]].
