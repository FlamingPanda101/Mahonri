# Capturing Christianity — GodLogicApologetics vs. Muslim Callers on the Islamic Dilemma

A live call-in stream in which [[Cameron Bertuzzi]] (*Capturing Christianity*) and co-host [[Avery (God Logic)|Avery]] (channel *God Logic*) field Muslim callers on **"the Islamic dilemma"** — the argument that the [[Quran 5.47|Qur'an affirms the Torah and Gospel as true revelation]] while simultaneously contradicting them, leaving Islam false either way.[^1] The stream was conceived as a backup after [[Muhammad Hijab]] declined to debate on a neutral platform; Hijab never appears, and the hosts work through the dilemma with a series of callers of varying sincerity.[^2]

## Summary
- **Avery lays out the dilemma.** The Qur'an repeatedly calls the previous scriptures true, preserved revelation and commands people to judge by them, yet contradicts them (denying Christ's crucifixion, divine sonship, God as Father). If the Bible is true the Qur'an is false for contradicting it; if the Bible is corrupted the Qur'an is false for calling a corrupt book true. Either road is "a dead end."[^3] See [[The Islamic Dilemma]], [[The Quran's Testimony to the Previous Scriptures]].
- **[[Quran 10.94|Surah 10:94]] is the centerpiece.** Avery argues this verse — "if you are in doubt about what We have revealed to you, ask those who read the scripture before you" — makes the *previous scriptures the standard of truth over the Qur'an*, not the reverse.[^4] See [[Surah 10-94 Makes the Bible the Standard for the Quran]].
- **The classical *tahrif* (corruption) debate.** Avery notes that medieval Muslim scholars split: Ibn Qayyim reports a party (incl. Ibn Abbas, al-Bukhari) holding the prior text was *preserved* but misinterpreted, using the very same proof-texts (Q 6:115; Sunan Abu Dawud 4449) that Christians now use, while the textual-corruption party offered "no evidence."[^5] See [[The Tahrif Debate Among Classical Muslim Scholars]].
- **The "guardian / criterion" reply.** Multiple callers answer that the Qur'an is a *muhaymin* (guardian) and *criterion* over the prior scriptures — accept what agrees with the Qur'an, reject what conflicts. Avery counters that no verse says this, and that [[Quran 2.85|Surah 2:85]] forbids believing part of the scripture while rejecting the rest, so the Qur'an affirms *all* of it, not a selection.[^6] See [[The Guardian and Criterion Reply to the Islamic Dilemma]].
- **[[Quran 3.81|Surah 3:81]] and [[Quran 3.55|Surah 3:55]] as secondary strands.** Avery cites 3:81 (the prophets' covenant to believe a coming messenger who "confirms what is with you") and 3:55 (the followers of Jesus made "superior... until the day of resurrection") to argue Jesus' message could never be corrupted or lost — the "Batman" caller's claim that Christianity "had to fail" for Islam contradicts the Qur'an's own promise.[^7] See [[Surah 3-55 The Followers of Jesus Remain Superior Until the Resurrection]].
- **An atonement digression with a sincere Indian caller ("Abishek").** When a self-described "middle-way" Muslim presses the fairness of substitutionary atonement, Avery walks through [[John 10.17-18|John 10:17–18]] (Jesus lays down his life voluntarily), [[Romans 5.8|Romans 5]], and [[Romans 6.3-4|Romans 6]] (grace is not a license to sin; the believer dies to sin in baptism), and a free-will theodicy for the origin of sin.[^8] See [[Substitutionary Atonement Is Not a License to Sin]].
- **Polemical asides.** The hosts spend much airtime on Hijab's no-show, a $5,000 super-chat offer to lure him on, the *da'wah* community's "infighting," and assorted tangents (Aisha's age, the cousin-marriage/IQ debate with Daniel "Pikachu," the Ezra / Q 9:30 identification dispute with Khalil Andani).[^9] These are commentary, not argument.
