# Light and Truth — Saved by Grace or Works?

A "Light and Truth" outreach handout (a trifold with an expanded appendix) answering the common Evangelical charge that the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints teaches a **"works-based" salvation**. Its thesis: Latter-day Saint soteriology is **grace-based** — humanity is saved by the merits, mercy, and grace of Christ, not by earning salvation through works.

## Summary
- Marshals a cascade of **Book of Mormon** passages teaching salvation through the merits/grace of Christ, not the law (1 Nephi 12:10-11; 2 Nephi 2:3-8; 2 Nephi 10:24; 2 Nephi 25:23; Mosiah 2-4; Alma 22:14; 24:10; 34:9-15; Moroni 7:28,41), alongside D&C, Pearl of Great Price, and New Testament texts (Romans 3:23-28; Ephesians 2:8-10) [^1].
- Stacks **modern prophetic statements** that salvation cannot be earned — Dallin H. Oaks ("Man cannot earn his own salvation"), Bruce R. McConkie ("we are saved by grace... without works"), Dieter F. Uchtdorf ("Salvation cannot be bought with the currency of obedience"), plus Ballard, Holland, Christofferson, Renlund, and Kearon [^2].
- Defuses the standard **prooftexts** (2 Nephi 25:23 "after all we can do"; Article of Faith 3) and argues that weaponizing them is the same fallacy as using James 2:24 or Matthew 7:21 to accuse Protestants of works-salvation — verses must be read in context [^3].
- **Apologetic use:** the vault's dedicated **grace-vs-works** resource; complements [[Light and Truth Letter - Austin Fife]] and the debate concept [[Sola Fide vs Faith Plus Ordinances]]. The same grace framing opens Hayden Carroll's [[Hayden Carroll - Ratio Christi Campus Talk]].
