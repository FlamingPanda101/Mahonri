A [[Ward Radio]] episode filmed inside a private collection of historical books, documents, and artifacts, whose owner (host **Reid**) walks the panel — with [[Cardon Ellis]] and panelists Tom and others — through the personal scriptures of Latter-day Saint Church presidents. The centerpiece is [[Spencer W. Kimball]]'s 1938 set (the [[Book of Mormon]], [[Doctrine and Covenants]], and [[Pearl of Great Price]] his wife Camilla gave him, used to 1955), bound with roughly **50 pages of his own typewritten notes**. The collector opens with the room's "controversial" world artifacts — Anne Frank's diary in 59 languages, Oskar Schindler's Omega watch — before pivoting to church history and Kimball's marginalia: his marking patterns, a wry one-word margin joke at [[Helaman 13.31|Helaman 13:31]], his "I desire the same thing" note at [[D&C 7]], and — most strikingly — question marks beside the [[Pearl of Great Price]] prooftexts then used to justify the priesthood-and-race restriction, written decades before he received the 1978 revelation.

## Summary

- **The prophets mark their scriptures "just like we do" — but Kimball went further.** Beyond annotating and highlighting, he glued in ~50 typewritten pages and "even highlights his own notes," adding original material. The collector frames the whole as faith-promoting; ~75,000 visitors a year, including missionaries, are shown the artifacts.[^1] See [[A Prophet's Scripture Marginalia Reveals His Devotional Interior]].
- **Marking patterns, by the numbers.** Most verses marked overall in **[[Alma 32.27-43|Alma]]** (longest book); highest percentage in a long book is **[[3 Nephi]]** (~55%, 405 of ~750 verses); highest percentage overall is **[[Enos 1.1-2|Enos]]** (91%, 27 of 30). The longest unbroken run is **89 verses**, all of [[Helaman 13.31|Helaman 13–15]] (the prophecy of Samuel the Lamanite), bracketed then highlighted within.[^2]
- **The Helaman 13:31 joke.** Within the Samuel-the-Lamanite block, beside the warning that cursed riches "become slippery, that ye cannot hold them," Kimball wrote one wry word and a question mark — the panel's guess: "Taxes?" Surrounding shorthand tracks the signs of Christ's birth ("Christ, five years"; "Star, BC five years"; "Samuel, too late").[^3] See [[Helaman 13.31|Helaman 13:31]].
- **"I desire the same thing" at D&C 7.** Beside the revelation on John the Beloved's request to tarry and bring souls to Christ, Kimball's note echoes the verse's own verb — a personal aspiration that "blew away" the collector.[^4] See [[D&C 7]].
- **Question marks on the priesthood-ban prooftexts — decades early.** Beside "any of the scriptures in the [[Pearl of Great Price]] that were used to justify Blacks not getting the priesthood," and beside the word "Negro," Kimball wrote question marks — reading as "Is that really what that's supposed to be?" Because this 1938–1955 set predates his presidency by decades (he was a newly called apostle), the panel sees a future prophet "questioning the status quo in real time... that's how revelation works," with God "preparing him."[^5] See [[Kimball Questioned the Priesthood-Ban Prooftexts in His Margins Decades Before the Revelation]].
- **The notes as "his internet."** The bound typewritten commentary functioned as Kimball's portable reference library — "with this he could give a talk on any subject." His funeral-prep notes cluster at [[Alma 40.11|Alma 40]] and Alma 32, including annotations on children who die young "so mothers will have a chance."[^6]
- **Frame: looking back on how God prepares people.** The hosts tie Kimball's pre-revelation wrestle to a general pattern (e.g. [[Russell M. Nelson]]'s decades of teaching on the gathering of Israel), and joke about their own future scriptures full of question marks — God preparing servants long before the assignment.[^7]
