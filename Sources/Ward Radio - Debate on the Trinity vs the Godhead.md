# Ward Radio — Debate on the Trinity vs The Godhead

A [[Ward Radio]] livestream framed as a "debate" on the creedal **Trinity** versus the LDS **Godhead** — plus the ontology of Christ and modern prophets. The scheduled evangelical content-creator opponent withdrew at the last minute (the hosts decline to name him), so [[Cardon Ellis]], [[Travis Anderson]], and [[Christian (Holy Rebellion)]] (with Mattie Packer and Adam Becker) lay out the LDS positions and steelman the opposition themselves. Most of the substantive theology is in the first ~70 minutes across three topics — prophets, the Trinity, and the ontology of Christ — after which the show pivots to an unrelated Instagram creator round-up. See the sibling sources [[Ward Radio - The Nicene Creed Is Not an Abomination]] and [[Ward Radio - Was Joseph Smith a Trinitarian]] for the wider Trinity cluster.

## Summary

- **Prophets did not end with the Bible.** [[Hebrews 1.1-2|Hebrews 1:1-2]] ("in these last days he has spoken to us by his Son") is offered as the lead Protestant prooftext for cessation; the hosts answer that the verse says God *has always* called prophets and in zero ways declares he will stop, and that the cessationist reading would also throw out every book of the NT written after Hebrews. See [[Hebrews 1-1-2 - Jesus the Last Prophet Rebutted]]. [^1]
- **Deuteronomy 18:22 does not define the false prophet by failed prediction.** The hosts note the text condemns the prophet who speaks "presumptuously," and that the failed-prediction standard would indict Jonah (Nineveh), Paul (imminent return), and even Jesus ("this generation"). See [[Deuteronomy 18 Condemns the Presumptuous Prophet Not the Mistaken One]] and [[Prophetic Failed-Prophecy Test Is Self-Refuting for Cessationists]]. [^2]
- **Revelation 22:18-19 ("add not") refers to the book of Revelation, not the Bible.** The Bible was compiled centuries later as a collection, Revelation was not written last, and the same "add not" formula appears in Deuteronomy and Proverbs. See [[Revelation 22 - Add or Take Away Rebutted]]. [^3]
- **Galatians 1:8 ("another gospel") boomerangs on creedal Christendom.** Catholics, Orthodox, and rival Protestant bodies all preach different "gospels," yet only Mormons are excluded; the LDS gospel (Christ the Son of God, virgin birth, sinless life, atoning death, resurrection) is the New Testament gospel. See [[A False Gospel Is One That Contradicts the Kingdom Message]]. [^4]
- **Fallible-but-reliable, not infallible.** Ascribing infallibility to anything but God is treated as idolatry; the Bible (like a prophet, or a doctor) can be reliable and point to God without being inerrant. See [[Fallible But Reliable - Why Authority Still Counts]] and [[Reliability Not Infallibility Standard]]. [^5]
- **Ephesians 2:19-22 and 4:11 show a continuing, not foundational-only, office.** The church is "built upon the foundation of apostles and prophets," and the replacement of Judas shows the structure was designed to continue "for the perfecting of the saints." See [[Appeal to Prophetic Necessity Rebutted]]. [^6]
- **The Trinity is cobbled from many books; no single biblical author describes it.** Defenders assemble Father/Son/Spirit verses from the NT with "one God" verses from Isaiah; what would be needed is *one author* describing consubstantiality, which never occurs. **NEW:** [[No Single Biblical Author Describes the Trinity]]. [^7]
- **"Co-equal" collides with "eternally begotten."** If the Son is eternally begotten of (and contingent on) the Father while the Father is not contingent on the Son, the relation is asymmetric and cannot be co-equal; an immutable, timeless God who *enters* time and a body and *exits* again is a further contradiction. **NEW:** [[Asymmetric Contingency Refutes Trinitarian Co-Equality]]. [^8]
- **The unmoved-mover objection to a "created" Christ is Hellenistic, not biblical.** The hosts deny Christ was created (the Book of Mormon calls him "the eternal God") and reject "outside time, space, and matter" as the definition of deity; God is "that than which no greater can be conceived" *within* this reality. See [[The Uncaused Cause Is Aristotle Not Scripture]] and [[Unmoved Mover Reshaped God the Father]]. [^9]
- **The hypostatic union and modalism.** The "two natures" formula is read as unnecessary once divinity and humanity are not opposed natures but stages of one kind of being; the panel notes most Christians, asked to describe the Trinity, give three beings or accidentally describe modalism, and that a super-chat alleges the hypostatic union "destroys the atonement." See [[The Hypostatic Union Atonement Conundrum]], [[Most Christians Describe Three Beings Not the Trinity]], and [[Understanding the Trinity Is Not Required for Salvation]]. [^10]
- **Baptism for the dead answers the fate of the unevangelized.** [[1 Corinthians 15.29|1 Corinthians 15:29]] is cited as a practice creedal Christians "shrug" at, while the restored gospel uses it to solve the problem of the billions who never heard of Christ. See [[1 Corinthians 15.29 Points to an Actual Rite of Baptism for the Dead]]. [^11]
