# The Stick of Joseph — The Key to Unlocking Lehi's Brass Plates

Interview on the **Stick of Joseph** YouTube channel with [[Jonah (Plain and Precious Publishing)|Jonah Barnes]] (Plain & Precious Publishing) promoting his book *The Key to the Keystone: How Apocryphal Text Unlocks the Book of Mormon's Brass Plates* [^1]. The conversation defends the Book of Mormon against the charge that its **pre-Christian messianism** is an anachronism, arguing that ~40 apocryphal texts mined in the book preserve exactly the loud, explicit Messiah-talk that the surviving Old Testament lacks — content plausibly carried on the [[Brass Plates Bypassed the Textual Corruption Chain|brass plates of Laban]] from the northern tradition.

Two of the episode's arguments are developed as standalone claims below; the rest restates material the vault already holds and is wikilinked rather than duplicated.

## Summary

- **The anachronism charge.** Critics (and "the academics") find it impossible that detailed, fleshed-out New-Testament-style messianism — a suffering Messiah named, baptized in Jordan, risen after three days — was preached 600 years before Christ. The Book of Mormon does exactly this from 1 Nephi 1, "right out of the gate," with no apologetic easing-in, and Lehi's Jerusalem audience reacts to the word *Messiah* as if it were familiar [^2]. This is the canon-specific instance of [[Scripture Was a Broader Category Than the Bible - The Anachronism of a Closed Canon|the closed-canon anachronism]].
- **"Apocrypha" defined two ways.** D&C 91's capital-A *Apocrypha* meant the Catholic deuterocanon (Tobit, Wisdom, Bel and the Dragon); Barnes uses the Greek sense — *apokryphos*, "hidden away" — for the whole field of texts (~40 mined in the book) that, like the Book of Mormon after 1829, claim ancient prophetic origin from the same world as the Bible [^3].
- **Jesus and the apostles quote books we no longer have.** Luke 6:24's "woe unto you that are rich, for ye have received your consolation" is a near-verbatim quotation of *1 Enoch* 94:8; Matthew 23:37, Acts 7:51-52, and John 1:14 likewise echo lost texts the New Testament writers assumed their audience knew [^4]. The vault already documents this — see [[Jesus Referenced the Apocrypha]] and [[The Parables of Enoch Are Pre-Christian Not a Post-Jesus Forgery]].
- **NEW — Historic-manuscript dating is a "glorified guess."** The [[Testament of Naphtali Was Vindicated by the Dead Sea Scrolls|Testament of Naphtali]] was academically dated to the 16th–17th century as a "pretension to Antiquity," then turned up at Qumran (1946–47) dating to the first century BC — exposing the circular rule by which any Christ-mentioning text is auto-dated post-Christ [^5].
- **NEW — Pre-600 BC prophets were martyred for preaching the Messiah.** [[Prophets Were Killed for Preaching the Messiah Before 600 BC|Lehi, Jesus, Peter, and Abinadi]] all assert that prophets of old were stoned and slain for testifying of a coming Messiah — testimony absent from the surviving Old Testament but supplied by the apocryphal martyrdom accounts of Isaiah, Jeremiah, and others [^6].
- **The "why."** Messianic prophecy that named Judah, Levi, and the priestly class as the killers of the Son of God was "politically, personally volatile" — the kind of text a Deuteronomist editor would suppress to avoid implicating his superiors. The Testament of Levi, where Levi tells his own descendants they will crucify God, is the showcase example [^7].
