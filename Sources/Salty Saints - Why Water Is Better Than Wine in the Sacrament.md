# Salty Saints - Why Water Is Better Than Wine in the Sacrament

**Show:** Salty Saints | **Host:** [[Hayden Holm]] | **Guests:** [[David Sword of Laben]] (Sword of Laban), [[Jeff Borders]] (*The Antichrist Playbook*) | **Title:** "John Crist Jokes About LDS Sacrament — But Scripture Shows Why Water is Better Than Wine"

Prompted by a clip of comedian **John Crist** riffing on Latter-day Saints using *water* for communion ("what if it's tap? what if you did Fanta?"), the panel grants his generous "it's all a symbol anyway" point ([[D&C 27.5|D&C 27:5]], "it mattereth not") and then digs past it to three theses: that some early Christians used water rather than wine; that water is actually a *better* symbol of Christ than wine; and that the sacrament's deepest meaning is to *sustain* the new life entered at baptism.

- **Caution first — drop the Word of Wisdom cliches.** The panel explicitly disowns the apologetic cliches (Jesus drank only grape juice; ancient wine was low-alcohol; they drank wine for hygiene): "none of this is true and it doesn't help." See [[Drop the Word of Wisdom Cliches]].[^1]
- **Some early Christians used water, not wine.** Citing Irenaeus (c. 180, on the Ebionites), Epiphanius (375, Marcionites/encratites), Clement of Alexandria (those who "celebrate the Eucharist with mere water"), Cyprian (c. 250), Paul in Romans 14, and scholar Paul Bradshaw's *Eucharistic Origins*, David argues water-and-bread Eucharist "may stretch back to the very beginning," not a later deviation — so LDS practice is not an oddity.[^2] See [[Some Early Christians Used Water Not Wine in the Eucharist]].
- **Water out-symbolizes wine.** Old Testament wine usually signifies the *cup of wrath*; water signifies Jehovah and salvation — living water ([[John 4.10-14|John 4]], [[John 7.37-38|John 7]]), water from the rock that "was Christ" ([[Exodus 17.6|Exodus 17:6]], [[1 Corinthians 10.4|1 Corinthians 10:4]]), the fountain of living waters, the Red Sea as a veil. Water's symbolic range overlaps the sacrament far more than wine's.[^3] See [[Water Is a Better Symbol of Christ Than Wine]].
- **Baptism doesn't wash away sins — it is death-and-rebirth; the sacrament sustains that new life.** David argues (with Romans 6, Colossians 2, Titus 3, Mosiah 18) that immersion symbolizes following Christ through death into newness of life, *not* a literal washing of sins (forgiveness follows repentance, which precedes baptism); the bread and water then *sustain* the reborn disciple like manna and water in the wilderness, with the sacrament table as a "tree of life."[^4] See [[The Sacrament Sustains the New Life of Baptism]] and the updated [[Baptism at 8 Is a Gift Not a Burden]].
