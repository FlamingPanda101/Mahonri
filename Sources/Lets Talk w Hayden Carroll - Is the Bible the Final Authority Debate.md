# Let's Talk w/ Hayden Carroll — Is the Bible the Final Authority? | Live Debate

A ~90-minute formal moderated debate on the resolution: **"The current 66-book Protestant canon is the infallible and ultimate authority of doctrine for followers of Jesus Christ today."** **Kelly** (evangelical "born-again" Protestant, channel "Brain Perspective") argues the **affirmative** (defending *sola scriptura*); **Hayden Carroll** (Latter-day Saint, host) argues the **negative**. Chris moderates. Format: 15-min openings, 10-min rebuttals, 15-min cross-examinations, 5-min closings.[^1]

## Summary
- **Kelly's affirmative case** is cumulative and inductive: scripture testifies to its own authority (2 Tim 3:16–17), Jesus quoted the OT "over 300 times," the NT cites the OT "over 1,600 times," the early church fathers cited nearly the whole NT, and even LDS apostles (Ballard, Brigham Young) call the Bible the "bedrock" and "foundation" of faith — therefore the 66 books are the standard by which any later prophet (Joseph Smith) must be tested.[^2] See [[Sola Scriptura - Scripture Self-Attests Its Authority]] and [[Scripture as the Test of Prophets and Revelation]].
- **Hayden's negative case** rests on a single pivot he calls **authorization**: for a canon to be *infallible and ultimate* (not merely inspired/reliable), God must have *publicly and unambiguously designated* this specific 66-book collection — and no such divine act is on record. The burden of proof is Kelly's; Hayden need not offer an alternative.[^3] See [[Authorization - Divine Designation Required for an Infallible Canon]] and [[The Canon Question Is Logically Prior to Canon Authority]].
- Hayden presses the **interpretive-authority** problem: even granting an ultimate text, *who* is the final interpreter when sincere believers disagree? Protestantism created this problem at the Reformation and offers no living adjudicator — the predictable fruit is schism.[^4] See [[No Final Interpreter - The Protestant Adjudication Problem]].
- Hayden charges Kelly's self-attestation argument is **circular** ("pointing to the text to validate the text") and notes that the canon was settled by **tradition / human recognition centuries later**, that no first-century Jewish OT canon was fixed, and that the church once received books (Tobit, Wisdom) it later rejected — undercutting any claim to an infallible recognition process.[^5] See [[The Circularity Charge Against Self-Attesting Scripture]] and [[The Church Once Accepted Books It Later Rejected]].
- Cross-examination side-skirmish: Hayden concedes the LDS canon is **not identical** to the Protestant 66 — Latter-day Saints regard the **Song of Solomon as non-inspired** (Joseph Smith), so "we have 65... plus more"; Kelly counters that LDS.org lists all 39 OT / 27 NT books.[^6] See [[LDS Reject the Song of Solomon as Inspired]].
