# Ward Radio - Frank Turek and Other Evangelicals Are Slowly Being Forced to Take 'Mormons' Seriously

Host [[Cardon Ellis]] with [[Jonah Barnes]] react to a *Cross Examined* campus clip in which LDS apologist [[Hayden Carroll]] presses [[Frank Turek]] on the inerrancy/infallibility of the Bible and on sola scriptura.

## Summary
- **The fallible-author dilemma.** Carroll concedes the New Testament is *reliable* (via standard criteria) while denying *infallibility*: humans are fallible, so a human-authored text could err; an inerrant book made by fallible authors would make them "like God." Turek's appeals — "Jesus said scripture can't be broken," the apostles were "confirmed by miracles" — are met with the rejoinder that there was no "Old Testament" or "New Testament" in AD 30, and that the canon was contested for centuries. Connects to existing canon notes ([[A Text Cannot Be Infallible Because Language Is Fallible]], [[The Canon Question Is Logically Prior to Canon Authority]]).
- **The John 10:35 catch.** Turek quotes "the scripture cannot be broken" (John 10:35) for inerrancy; Carroll points out the verse Jesus is defending is "ye are gods" ([[John 10.34-36|Psalm 82:6]]) — so Turek is leaning on a deification proof-text while omitting its content. See [[John 10-35's Unbreakable Scripture Is the Deification Text Ye Are Gods|John 10:35's Unbreakable Scripture Is the Deification Text Ye Are Gods]].
- **The double standard.** The hosts' thesis: apologists argue *like believers* for the resurrection (inference to the best explanation from witnesses) but switch to arguing *like atheists* when the same method is applied to the gold plates' 11+ witnesses — and Turek mis-states that Luke and Paul were resurrection eyewitnesses. See [[Apologists Drop the Resurrection Evidential Standard When Judging the Gold Plates]].
