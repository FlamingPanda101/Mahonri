# Thoughtful Faith — Live Debate: Mormon vs Protestant vs Catholic

A three-way live interfaith debate, posted by [[Jacob Hansen]] on his *Thoughtful Faith* channel, moderated by Catholic podcaster [[Cameron Bertuzzi]]. Hansen (Latter-day Saint) engages the Catholic apologist [[Joe Heschmeyer]] and the Reformed-Baptist apologist [[Aaron Shafovaloff]] (rendered "Aaron Shafawal" in the auto-transcript). Each participant submitted three questions in advance; the format runs one prepared question per panelist, dialogue among the three, and a closing audience Q&A.[^1] Because all three traditions are represented, the conversation ranges across the classic dividing lines — the [[Sola Scriptura - Scripture Self-Attests Its Authority|authority]] question, the [[Debate - Sola Scriptura and the Canon|canon]], the [[The Godhead Model vs the Trinity Model|nature of God]], [[Kenotic Christology - Jesus Emptied Himself and Did Not Know the Hour|Christology]], [[Postmortem Salvation Resolves the Soteriological Problem|salvation]], [[The Creation Dilemma|the problem of hell]], [[Creatio Ex Nihilo Is the Lynchpin Difference|creation]], and abortion/ensoulment — and explicitly defers the LDS-specific "great apostasy" debate to a follow-up event between Hansen and the two Christians the next night.

## Participants
- [[Jacob Hansen]] — Latter-day Saint apologist (*Thoughtful Faith*)
- [[Joe Heschmeyer]] — Catholic apologist (Catholic Answers / *Shameless Popery*)
- [[Aaron Shafovaloff]] — Reformed-Baptist / evangelical apologist (Mormonism Research Ministry)
- [[Cameron Bertuzzi]] — moderator (Catholic; *Capturing Christianity*)

## Summary
- **What doctrines are essential for salvation?** Shafovaloff answers with "theological triage" (after Gavin Ortlund's *Finding the Right Hills to Die On*): Scripture itself ranks "matters of first importance" (1 Cor 15) and weightier matters; the thief on the cross was saved with minimal conscious belief and "no middleman," yet denying Christ's deity or preaching "another gospel" (Galatians 1) severs one from Christ. Heschmeyer presses that the "another gospel" verse is contested (the New Perspective on Paul reads it as Mosaic works), so how does the private reader adjudicate without a church council — "can we trust ourselves to get it right?" Shafovaloff: Protestants look *outside* themselves to Scripture, not to their own deceptive hearts.[^2] See [[Covenant Relationship Over Theology Quiz]] and [[Sola Scriptura Cannot Navigate the Heresy Minefield]].
- **Was King David fully forgiven?** Shafovaloff asks Hansen about D&C 132 (David "lost his exaltation") and the LDS teaching that David is "spending a term in hell." Hansen: he claims no knowledge of anyone's final salvific status; murder cannot be *fully* forgiven in this life but can be atoned for in the next, since the only unforgivable sin is the sin against the Holy Ghost. The exchange turns on postmortal repentance vs. the Protestant use of David (Psalm 51, Romans 4) as the showcase of immediate grace to the ungodly.[^3] See [[Postmortem Salvation Resolves the Soteriological Problem]], [[Grace Is Unconditional Love Not an Earned Wage]], and [[LDS Salvation Is by Grace Not Earned by Works]].
- **If God wants to save all, why can't He? (asked of both Heschmeyer and Shafovaloff)** Heschmeyer deploys C.S. Lewis (omnipotence excludes the nonsensical) and the Thomistic *antecedent vs. consequent will*, with a dinner-and-dessert parenting analogy: God wills all to be saved but respects the conditions creatures may refuse; this is a *volitional* choice for a greater good, not an *external constraint*. Hansen replies that this implies a reason God does not "snap His fingers" — which to a Latter-day Saint looks like an eternal law constraining God. Shafovaloff distinguishes God's *common love* (sun on the just and unjust) from His *special, electing love* for the elect (Ephesians 1), and concedes the fit of the two is "outside my intellectual jurisdiction." Hansen's sharpest push: why call it "love" toward the non-elect who are "sent to the torture chamber regardless of their choices," and why create such people at all?[^4] See [[Why Doesnt God Only Create the Saved]], [[The Creation Dilemma]], [[Coerced Love Is Not Love - Agency Against Irresistible Grace]], [[Omnibenevolence Does Not Equal Universalism or Coercion]], and [[Free Will Is the Price of Love - Tureks Free-Will Theodicy]].
- **Is abortion murder, and when does life begin?** Hansen reads the official LDS statement (opposing elective abortion, narrow exceptions for rape/incest/life of mother), then argues from delayed ensoulment (citing Augustine) that abortion "can be murder but not necessarily." His signature move is a *reductio*: if life begins at conception and every lost fertilized egg equals a dead infant, then IVF parents are murderers and (given that 30–60% of fertilized eggs fail to implant) "sex is incredibly risky" and ought to be banned in favor of single-embryo IVF — an absurd conclusion. Heschmeyer counters on natural-law grounds and forces a key concession from himself and Hansen: *homicide is not always murder* — intent and knowledge (mens rea) are required, so an indoctrinated woman who aborts without knowing differs from a premeditated killer.[^5] See [[The IVF Reductio Against Conception-as-Ensoulment]], [[Homicide Is Not Always Murder - The Mens Rea Distinction]], [[LDS Abortion Position Follows From Pre-Existence Not Cultural Caving]], and [[A Body from the Physical Creation Joined to a Premortal Spirit]].
- **Eternal generation, aseity, and the Trinity.** Shafovaloff asks Heschmeyer how vital the *eternal generation of the Son* is, and whether it makes the Son inferior. Heschmeyer gives the persons/natures grammar (one essence, three persons; the rock has a nature but no person; God is three persons in one nature), then frames the Father's begetting of the Son as the *internal movement of divine love* (lover/beloved) — an order without inferiority ("soft monarchy," not Arian demigod). In the Q&A this sharpens into an *aseity* clash: a questioner and Shafovaloff (quoting Basil — "the Father is greater as cause and origin") argue the Son's existence is *contingent* on the Father (the light-bulb-always-on analogy), so only the Father is fundamental; Heschmeyer answers in the grammar of *processions*, not contingency.[^6] See [[The Godhead Model vs the Trinity Model]], [[Autotheos Collides With Eternal Generation]], [[Begotten Implies Source and Origin]], [[Substance Is Equivocal in the Trinity]], and [[Understanding the Trinity Is Not Required for Salvation]].
- **Did Jesus have two minds?** Shafovaloff asks whether the incarnate Christ had one all-knowing and one limited mind. Heschmeyer insists on the technical terms — *two intellects and two wills*, one person — and repeatedly refuses Hansen's framing of "two centers of consciousness" as too imprecise (it risks Nestorian "two Jesuses"). Hansen and Shafovaloff press the Gethsemane "not my will but thine" and "why have you forsaken me" cases; Heschmeyer answers with the unmixed-but-united two natures (against monophysitism) and Gregory of Nazianzus' "what is not assumed is not healed." Hansen's underlying objection: a real kenosis (a mind that genuinely does not know all things) means the gulf between God and man is *bridgeable*, which undercuts the classical incompatibility.[^7] See [[One Mind Dilemma Against Two Natures]], [[Hypostatic Union Creates Two Jesuses]], [[Kenotic Christology - Jesus Emptied Himself and Did Not Know the Hour]], [[Gethsemane Two Wills Refute Same-Being Trinity]], and [[The Hypostatic Union Atonement Conundrum]].
- **Lectures on Faith, "personages," and plural marriage in early D&C.** Hansen asks Shafovaloff to defend the canon; Hansen is himself pressed on the 1835 Lectures on Faith ("two personages in the Godhead," the Spirit as God's "mind") and on the 1835/1844 D&C section that denied plural marriage while it was secretly practiced. Hansen: the Lectures were uncanonized committee lectures, "personage" in 1828 usage allowed an embodied being, and D&C 76 (same era) clearly teaches three divine members; on polygamy, monogamy is the norm (Jacob 2) with God-authorized exceptions (Aquinas, Luther, the house of Israel). A questioner charges the retained section was "deceptive"; Hansen invokes the hiding-Jews-from-Nazis analogy for concealment under threat.[^8] See [[The Godhead Model vs the Trinity Model]], [[King Follett Need Not Be Canonized]], and [[Plural Marriage Was Sealing Not Sex]].
- **Blake Ostler, the regress of gods, and the Father's mortal worship.** Shafovaloff asks whether Hansen agrees with [[Blake Ostler]] that during the Father's mortal condescension another filled the role of God. Hansen declines to defend Ostler's speculation, distinguishes *binding LDS doctrine* (the standard works) from private theological theorizing (the King Follett Discourse is not canon), quotes Parley P. Pratt against an "infinite regress," and reframes the puzzle as the *same* conundrum as the incarnate Christ giving obedience to the Father.[^9] See [[King Follett Need Not Be Canonized]] and [[Kenotic Christology - Jesus Emptied Himself and Did Not Know the Hour]].
- **The canon: did tradition give us the Bible?** Hansen's canon question to Shafovaloff: if the Bible cannot list its own books, isn't tradition the ultimate authority? Shafovaloff's reply — the bride *received* and *recognized* Scripture; she did not *make* it ("my sheep hear my voice"; Moses needed no second voice at the burning bush; Christians are "radical receivers"). Hansen counters historically: it was particular *councils* claiming authority, not the diffuse body of believers, that fixed the canon. Shafovaloff (citing Ortlund) replies the Gospel of John was in wide reception *before* the councils deliberated.[^10] See [[The Church Recognizes Scripture It Does Not Make It]], [[Self-Authenticating Canon - Feel It in Your Bones]], [[The Church Authoritatively Declares the Canon]], [[Thermometer vs Thermostat - The Church Declares the Canon]], and [[Knowing Scripture Is the Word of God Requires an Authority Outside Scripture]].
- **Creation ex nihilo as a late invention.** An LDS questioner cites Benjamin Sommer/Bernard-style scholarship that the theophanies depict an embodied God and that *creatio ex nihilo* is a second-century development (the Bible depicts creation from chaos). Heschmeyer rejects trusting "cynical" scholars whose method would equally undercut LDS claims; Shafovaloff turns the question into *worship* (Romans 11; Colossians 1 — "from Him and through Him and to Him are all things"), arguing that creation *ex materia* would mean God deserves no credit for our fundamental existence. Hansen quotes Justin Martyr (creation "out of shapeless matter") and James Hubler's thesis that *ex nihilo* emerged as an anti-Gnostic apologetic.[^11] See [[Creatio Ex Nihilo Is the Lynchpin Difference]], [[Creation Ex Nihilo Originated Late 2nd Century CE]], [[Bara Means Organize and Ex Nihilo Is a Second-Century Invention]], and [[Genesis 1 Creation Is Ordering and Separation Not Ex Nihilo]].
- **Are pre-Nicene Christians damned, and is the Trinity even biblical?** An LDS questioner argues the Trinity was not codified until the late 4th century, so the apostles and Second-Temple Jews (with their "second power in heaven") would be damned. Heschmeyer: culpability requires knowing-and-willing rejection (his own children are "Trinitarian heretics" without fault), and Paul (Romans 2 conscience; Romans 10 "have they not heard") shows God judges by light received, not by a "theology quiz." Hansen and Shafovaloff add that Jesus made *love*, not Trinitarian formulae, the mark of discipleship (John 13:35), and cite Richard Swinburne ("even if you regard the NT as an infallible source... you cannot derive from it a doctrine of the Trinity").[^12] See [[Understanding the Trinity Is Not Required for Salvation]], [[Trinity Cannot Be Derived From the New Testament - Swinburne]], [[Salvation by Theology Damns Almost Everyone]], [[Covenant Relationship Over Theology Quiz]], and [[Most Christians Describe Three Beings Not the Trinity]].
- **Knowledge vs. love of God; what saves "the guy doing his best"?** Asked what is required of someone who never grasps the theology, Shafovaloff points to the cross — encounter the Christ of John's Gospel, be exposed as a sinner (Luke 18), call on the Lord and receive the Spirit. Heschmeyer agrees "salvation is not a theology quiz" but argues true love *wants* to know the beloved (the birthday/marriage analogy; Benedict XVI on the magi and the shepherds at the manger). Hansen's LDS answer: "you will get what you truly want" — God gives "everything you are willing to receive," the question is only how far you will follow.[^13] See [[Covenant Relationship Over Theology Quiz]] and [[Creedalism Replaced Relationship With Theology]].
- **Worship reserved for God alone (veneration vs. latria).** A questioner asks Heschmeyer for a single act reserved to God and withheld from Mary/the saints. Answer: the offering of *sacrifice* as divine honor (*latria*) — Jesus prays everywhere but sacrifice happened only at the Jerusalem temple / Mount Gerizim; now offered collectively in the Eucharist (1 Cor 10) and individually as Romans 12:1 self-offering.[^14] See [[Worship Is Devotion to What Can Save Not Ritual Appeasement]] and [[Common Reference - Worshipping Adam as God]].
- **Why expect no restoration? The "divine mic drop."** Closing question: what would it take to believe God restored a corrected church through a prophet? Heschmeyer: if Jesus meant the church to need later correction, He would have *foretold* it — every Old/New Testament cataclysm (Babylonian captivity, temple destruction) is explicitly prophesied, but no equivalent prophecy points forward to Joseph Smith (the JST has to *add* one). Shafovaloff: the covenants climax in Christ, who "ratifies a fixed new covenant" — "a giant divine mic drop"; the New Testament, itself the new-covenant document, "doesn't anticipate a newer new covenant."[^15] See [[No Old-Covenant-Style Prophecy Foretells the Restoration]], [[Apostasy Was Complete When the Keys Departed]], and [[LDS Theology as Restoration of Early Christianity]].
- **Closing courtesies.** Each says something they admire in the others' traditions: Hansen praises the LDS emphasis on family, Catholic ecclesiology, and the Protestant gift of religious liberty; Shafovaloff praises Protestant evangelistic zeal, retrieval of the historic Trinity, and the LDS community's donation to the family of the man who attacked an LDS meetinghouse in Michigan; Heschmeyer praises Protestant missionary courage. The tone is explicitly "MMA fight then a hug."[^16]
