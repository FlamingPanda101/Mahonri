# Thoughtful Faith — The Trinity vs the Godhead Debate Prep

[[Jacob Hansen]] walks his friend [[Jonah Barnes]] through the arguments he plans to use in an upcoming debate ("Is the God of the Bible a Trinity?") against the Trinitarian YouTuber [[Avery (God Logic)|Avery]] of *God Logic*, to be held at Ruslan's Blessed God Summit. The throughline is a single diagnostic move: the entire Trinity debate "comes down to the word *God* and what they mean by that term," so every Trinitarian claim must be sorted by asking whether *God* names a **who** (a person) or a **what** (a nature/essence). On that test, Hansen argues, classical Trinitarianism either collapses into modalism, smuggles in polytheism, or quietly equivocates — whereas the LDS **Godhead model** (three persons, one united will/council, one shared divine nature) fits the biblical data without contradiction.

- **The whole debate reduces to a "who vs what" equivocation on the word *God*.** Trinitarians say "three persons, one God," but if the persons are the Father, Son, and Spirit (the *whos*), then the "one God" in the middle of the diagram must be a *what* (a nature). Three persons sharing one divine nature is no more "one being" than three dogs sharing canine nature are "one dog." Pressed to say whether "God is one" means one *who* or one *what*, the Trinitarian must answer "a what" — at which point the LDS side agrees ("there is only one Godhead") and the argument is over. [^1][^2][^3] See [[The Who or What Test for the Word God]], [[Three Persons Sharing a Nature Are Still Three Beings]], [[The Godhead Model vs the Trinity Model]].
- **"Jesus is God" cannot mean identity, and the hypostatic union is two-people-in-disguise.** Read as the *is* of identity (J=G, F=G ⟹ J=F) it yields modalism, a confessed heresy; read as a title ("fully divine") it yields the harmless Godhead. The "two natures, one person" escape only works for *non-exclusive* natures; divinity (omniscient, immutable, uncreated, impassible) and humanity (limited, changing, created, suffering) are mutually exclusive like square and triangle, so "one mind" with/without all knowledge forces a choice. [^4][^5][^6] See [[Jesus Is God Means Title Not Identity]], [[Hypostatic Union Creates Two Jesuses]], [[Divine and Human Natures Are Mutually Exclusive]], [[One Mind Dilemma Against Two Natures]].
- **The scriptures contradict, not merely fail to teach, same-being Trinitarianism.** "Not my will but thine," "my God my God why hast thou forsaken me," "my God and your God," "the Father is greater than I," "the only true God" (the Father), and the Father *begetting* the Son all presuppose two distinct beings, not one being relating to itself. [^7][^8][^9] See [[Gethsemane Two Wills Refute Same-Being Trinity]], [[The Father Is Greater Than I]], [[Begotten Implies Source and Origin]], [[My God and Your God - Jesus Shares Our Relationship to God]].
- **Old Testament "monotheism" is covenant loyalty and a boast of supremacy, not numerical/ontological oneness.** The Shema's *echad* means "Yahweh alone" (allegiance), not "one being"; "no god besides me" is the well-attested ANE royal taunt (Heiser's Packers analogy; Zephaniah ironically puts it in a *city's* mouth). Scholars (Sijuwade, Hayman, MacDonald, Fredriksen, Heiser, Hurtado, Bauckham) hold ancient Judaism affirmed multiple divine beings under one supreme God — a divine spectrum, the very Godhead Joseph Smith restored; strict numerical monotheism is a 17th-century concept, so a true monotheist "should be a Muslim." [^10][^11][^12][^13] See [[Shema Echad Means Covenant Loyalty Not Ontology]], [[None Beside Me Is an ANE Boast of Supremacy]], [[Ancient Judaism Affirmed a Divine Spectrum]], [[Monotheism Is a 17th-Century Invention]], [[Bene Elohim Are Sons Not a Different Species]].
- **Oneness texts are unity, not identity; "mystery" is special pleading.** John 17 asks that disciples "be one as we are one" — a oneness of will/glory/relationship Jesus invites *humans* into, impossible if it meant shared being. Calling the resulting contradiction a "mystery" is not humility but special pleading: it asks believers to abandon, in this one case, the everyday logic that three fully-X things are three X's. [^14][^15][^16] See [[John 17 Oneness Is Unity Not Shared Being]], [[Mystery Is Special Pleading Not Humility]], [[Worship Is Devotion to What Can Save Not Ritual Appeasement]].
