# Ward Radio — The Hidden Feminine Symbolism of LDS Temple Ritual

An all-women Ward Radio panel (hosted by **Tia**, with **[[Britney Ward Radio|Britney]]**, Rachel, Joy, and convert guest Megan) reads the LDS temple as a sustained **allegory of woman, the womb, and birth** — offered explicitly as a devotional "side tangent," not as doctrine ("this is not where my testimony lies; my testimony is in Jesus Christ").

## Summary
- **The temple is a woman / a womb; the worshipper is the child.** "Born again" points past baptism to the temple, where the new-birth ordinance is "pulled out into all of its glory" — and every parallel casts the patron as the cozy baby inside a womb, never as the mother [^1].
- **The placenta is literally nicknamed the "tree of life"** in midwifery; its functions (oxygen/"inspiration," cleansing, nourishment, immunity/"protection from evil," hormonal "messages") are mapped onto the Tree of Life feeding the child through the umbilical cord (read as the iron rod) at the navel [^2].
- **The veil is a birth membrane.** The amniotic sac is a "veil"; a rare birth inside the intact sac is obstetrically called a **"veiled birth"** (en caul), so "your mother's veil was rent" when she delivered you — tied to the temple veil rent at Christ's crucifixion and reframing women's temple veil as a fitting symbol of sacred presence [^3].
- **Labor as the closest living type of the Atonement** ("is there no other way?"), and a pastoral reframe for childless women: our deepest temple identity is *child* of Heavenly Parents, not parent [^4].
