A **WARD RADIO** livestream hosted by **[[Cardon Ellis]]** with guests **[[Callie Baker]]** and **[[Austin Baker]]** (the *About Our Hope* Instagram account), **[[Britney Ward Radio|Britney]]**, and (via Zoom) **[[Tikla]]** of the Women of Ward Radio. Callie presents a list of **distinctive LDS doctrines "every Gen Z should know"** and the panel discusses how to commend them. The recurring thesis: cradle members underrate how *unique* their theology is, and recovering that sense would replace embarrassment with gratitude and confidence.

## Summary

- **Members don't see their own distinctives.** Callie's framing: people raised in the Church assume "all our beliefs are kind of just similar to everyone else's," not recognizing the unique theology that "makes the church so incredible" — knowing it would help Gen Z *want* to stay [^1].
- **Forever families.** Marriage and family relationships continue into the next life — a belief Callie says is found nowhere else, illustrated by a viral "pinky promise marriage" TikTok trend of Christian couples vowing to be "best friends in heaven since we won't be married" [^2].
- **The pre-existence, and its loss in the Great Apostasy.** The panel calls premortal existence "basically Mormon doctrine" among the early Fathers and frames its disappearance as proof of an apostasy — naming [[Clement of Alexandria]], [[Justin Martyr]], and [[Origen]] (the latter, they say, posthumously condemned) — see [[Pre-existence Was a Mainstream Early-Christian Belief Later Purged]] [^3].
- **Eternal progression and exaltation.** Presented as the capstone of a "complete" plan of salvation that answers the modern meaning-crisis ("so many people are falling into this almost nihilism") [^4].
- **"We take God seriously."** A clip from a convert ("Coffee for a Dream") prompts the claim that Latter-day Saints are reverent and literal about God and scripture, viewing him as an approachable, embodied Heavenly Father rather than "a concept or some floating feeling" [^5].
- **Heavenly Mother and an embodied, gendered God.** Callie calls belief in a Heavenly Mother "insane theology... no one else believes that," tied to women's potential to become "a goddess and a priestess," and contrasts it with religions that treat the body as a curse or expect a genderless afterlife [^6].
- **The redeemed Eve.** The LDS reading of [[Eve]]'s choice as courageous and foresighted (quoting [[Russell M. Nelson]], "the courage and vision of our mother Eve") is contrasted with "the curse of Eve" narrative — yielding the panel's joke-turned-merch line "Eve did nothing wrong" [^7].
- **The Apocrypha as a window on lost beliefs.** Cardon gives an extended (self-admittedly imprecise) tour of "apocrypha" big-A and small-a — the Catholic deuterocanon, 1–3 Enoch in the Ethiopic Bible, the Shepherd of Hermas, the Gospel of Thomas, the Apocalypse of Peter (said to have inspired Dante) — to argue early Christians, e.g., honored Eve [^8].
- **An open canon.** "If God wants to tell us something, he's allowed to. He's not restricted by the set of books that a council put together," anchored in [[John 21.24|John 21:25]] ("the world itself could not contain the books") [^9].
- **Vicarious work for the dead.** Callie reframes baptism for the dead as one of "the most inclusive" doctrines — everyone who never had a fair chance still gets to accept Christ — and the panel debates a "rebrand" (vicarious temple work) anticipating the [[Ward Radio - We Need to Vibe Check Baptisms for the Dead|vibe-check episode]] [^10].
