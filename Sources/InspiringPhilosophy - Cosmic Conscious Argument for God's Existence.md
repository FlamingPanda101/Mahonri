# Cosmic Conscious Argument for God's Existence
A formal argument by [[Inspiring Philosophy|Michael Jones]] that the scientific evidence for an emergent universe and fundamental consciousness leads logically to theism — what Michio Kaku and Eugene Wigner called "cosmic consciousness."[^1] Building on JP Moreland's argument from consciousness, Jones contends that if consciousness is fundamental and irreducible yet came to be in time, its only adequate explanation is a personal, necessary source we call God.[^2] He then defends the argument against panpsychism, solipsism, and the quantum "omniscient observer" objection.[^3]

## Summary
- **The structure (Wigner's logic formalized).** Premise 1: contingent minds have either a personal or a natural explanation (borrowed from JP Moreland). Premise 2: quantum mechanics and other sciences imply the natural universe emerged from consciousness. Conclusion 1: the natural universe cannot explain contingent minds. Premise 3: the explanation is personal. Premise 4: a personal source is what we call God. Conclusion: God exists as the reason conscious agents exist.[^2]
- **Reductionism is the dividing line.** If the mind cannot be reduced to smaller natural or informational parts, it must come from a source that is itself consciousness — a necessary, personal source, since persons come from persons.[^4]
- **Panpsychism is just reductionism in disguise.** Even if all matter is conscious, we still need to explain our unified consciousness; combining micro-consciousnesses runs into William James's "combination problem," and (per Charles Hartshorne) panpsychism is actually a *realism*, not idealism, so it collapses into a naturalistic origin of consciousness.[^5]
- **Solipsism collapses into theism, not realism.** David Deutsch argues solipsism is unstable and reduces to realism; Jones counters that the uncontrollable, governing aspect of experience the solipsist cannot command is still mental and "godlike," so solipsism actually points to theistic idealism.[^6]
- **The quantum "non-omniscient God" objection fails.** The *Quantum Enigma* objection says an omniscient observer would have collapsed everything already. Jones replies that God, being timeless and immaterial, does not obtain knowledge in space-time or cause collapse; he knows all instants at once, so there is never a "before" in which he lacked knowledge.[^7]
