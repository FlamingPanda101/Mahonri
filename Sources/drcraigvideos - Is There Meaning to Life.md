# Is There Meaning to Life?

An animated [[William Lane Craig]] explainer arguing that whether life has objective meaning, value, and purpose depends on whether God exists. If God does not exist, the universe is all there is, we are unintended byproducts of nature, there is no absolute moral standard, and death is annihilation — so on naturalism life is ultimately absurd. Craig closes with his signature dilemma: every atheist must choose between being happy and being consistent, whereas Christianity makes a happy *and* consistent life possible.

## Summary
- Significance is conditional on God: if God does not exist, "our lives would have no objective meaning, value, or purpose," and many atheists themselves admit that on atheism "life is absurd." [^1]
- No God means no design and no moral standard: we are "accidental byproducts of nature" with "no purpose for us being here," and (quoting [[Richard Dawkins]]) a materialistic universe has "no design, no purpose, no evil, no good, nothing but pointless indifference." [^2]
- Atheism cannot ground morality: appeals to evolution or to "human flourishing" fail — flourishing is species-centric and was invoked by Hitler and Sanger — so "there is no legitimate basis for saying that behaving one way is worse than behaving any other way." [^3]
- Death and cosmic heat death void all meaning: kind and cruel lives end alike in "nothingness," and since "the whole universe and mankind with it will die out," everything "comes to nothing"; Bertrand Russell concluded we must build "on the firm foundation of despair." [^4]
- Theism grounds meaning, yielding the happy/consistent dilemma: if Christianity is true each person is here for a reason, life does not end at the grave, and God is the standard of goodness — so "you can live a life that's both happy and consistent," which the atheist cannot. [^5]
