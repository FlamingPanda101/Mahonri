# Five Proofs of the Existence of God

Edward Feser's 2017 defense of **five demonstrative arguments for God** drawn from the classical tradition — Aristotelian, Neo-Platonic, Augustinian, Thomistic, and Rationalist — followed by a derivation of the divine attributes and a chapter answering common objections. Feser argues these are not probabilistic "god of the gaps" inferences but, properly understood, **strict metaphysical demonstrations** whose conclusion is the God of classical theism.

## Summary
- **The Aristotelian Proof:** from the reality of change (act and potency) to a purely actual, unchanging cause — Pure Actuality [^1].
- **The Neo-Platonic Proof:** from the existence of composite things to an absolutely simple, non-composite source — the One [^2].
- **The Augustinian Proof:** from the reality of abstract objects, universals, and necessary truths to a divine intellect that grounds them [^3].
- **The Thomistic Proof:** from the real distinction between essence and existence in things to that whose essence *is* existence — Subsistent Being Itself [^4].
- **The Rationalist Proof:** from the Principle of Sufficient Reason and contingency to an absolutely necessary being [^5].
- **The nature of God** (ch. 6): the proofs converge on one God who is simple, immutable, eternal, immaterial, omnipotent, omniscient, and perfectly good; (ch. 7) replies to the standard objections to natural theology [^6].
- **Apologetic use (TF Level-1, existence of God):** the strongest **classical** natural theology — a powerful ally *against atheism*. For a Latter-day Saint vault, engage it critically: Feser's conclusions about **divine simplicity, immutability, timeless eternity, and creation ex nihilo** are precisely the classical-theist commitments LDS theology questions. Notes should present the arguments faithfully and mark these points of divergence honestly.
