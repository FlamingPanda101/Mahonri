# Dan McClellan - Responding to Criticisms of a Video of Mine

A point-by-point reply in which [[Dan McClellan]] re-defends a claim from a four-year-old video against a Christian content creator who reasserts the "apostasy" framing of Israelite religion. The specific topic is **Asherah as the consort/wife of Yahweh and the dating of the anti-Asherah polemic**: McClellan endorses [[Francesca Stavrakopoulou]]'s position that Asherah worship was normative before [[Josiah]], and argues there is **no biblical text confidently datable prior to Josiah that condemns Asherah** — so the apologetic "of course there was Asherah worship, because there was apostasy" reading depends on *retrojecting* later opposition into earlier periods. He treats the exchange as a textbook case of "putting dogma over data."

## Summary

- **The defended claim is about dating, not theology.** "There are no biblical texts that scholars can confidently date prior to the reign of King Josiah... that demonize or vilify or condemn Asherah or Asherah worship." Recognition of Asherah as Yahweh's consort "was 100% normative" before Josiah's cult-centralization and the exile. The condemnation literature begins "around the reign of Josiah," with a historical explanation for why it appears suddenly then. [^1] See [[No Pre-Josiah Text Vilifies Asherah]].
- **The "apostasy" rebuttal is a presupposition, not a datum.** The critic's argument — there was Asherah worship *because* there was apostasy — "presupposes that Asherah worship was condemned throughout the entire history of ancient Israel," ignoring that the texts narrating pre-Josiah periods were, on the critical view, "actually written in later time periods." "You have no data that support the conclusion that worshiping Asherah prior to the reign of Josiah actually represents any kind of apostasy. It's a presupposition." [^2] See [[No Pre-Josiah Text Vilifies Asherah]] and [[Putting Dogma Over Data]].
- **Burden-of-proof inversion: stasis is the extraordinary claim.** Against the demand that critical scholars must *prove* cultural change, McClellan answers that "all human societies change," so "it is the notion that these societies did not change over the course of a thousand years that would require an argument." Defaulting to no-change "goes against literally all the data." [^3] See [[Stasis Is the Claim That Needs Proof Not Change]] and [[Synchronic Change in the Conception of Deity]].
- **"It's just the parts that talk about Asherah."** When the critic claims scholars would have to prove "the Torah and prophets were all invented after Josiah," McClellan replies it is "not all of them" — only the Asherah-condemning material — and that critical scholarship "has already done that": the arguments are in the literature, and the video is "where I'm just sharing the findings of the scholarship," not where the scholarship is done. He points to the Asherah chapter of his book *The Bible Says So*. [^4]
- **Audience capture, not argument, sustains the dogma.** The creator "can get away with it because... the overwhelming majority of their audience comprises folks who have already accepted the very same presupposition," so "no case needs to be made." Their performance is "performing confidence and conviction and competence for an audience that doesn't know better." [^5] See [[Apologetics as Identity Politics Over Evidence]] and [[Apologists Engage Pre-Digested Caricatures of Critical Scholarship]].
- **The closing appeals beg the question.** "Jesus affirmed the Shema, so I'm sticking with the Bible" presupposes "that the letters in red actually came from Jesus and were actually inspired" — which McClellan denies, "because the letters in red frequently contradict themselves." The recurring diagnosis: "This is putting dogma over data and pretending that the data have not adequately engaged the dogmas." [^6] See [[Putting Dogma Over Data]].
