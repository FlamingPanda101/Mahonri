# Ward Radio — The Apocryphal Secret Behind Lucifer's Fall (with Jonah Barnes)

A Ward Radio episode in which host **[[Cardon Ellis]]** interviews **[[Jonah Barnes]]** about a claim from *The Lost Gems of Genesis*: that a dominant apocryphal theme — **Lucifer fell because he was jealous of Adam** — is a motif Joseph Smith seems *not* to address in [[Moses 4]] (where the rebellion is over being the redeemer) but in fact captures in [[Abraham 3.25|Abraham 3]], which Barnes re-reads as the premortal selection of the *first man* rather than of the redeemer.

## Summary
- **The apocryphal "elephant in the room."** Across many texts (Life of Adam and Eve / *Vita Adae* 6:2, the Cave of Treasures, the Tales of the Prophets, the Divine Investiture of St Michael), God commands the angels to worship/honor the newly created Adam; Lucifer refuses — "I am fire and spirit, he is dust" — and is cast out *because of Adam*. The fall is consistently tied to envy of Adam, not to a redeemer contest. See [[Lucifer Fell Out of Jealousy of Adam in the Apocrypha]].
- **The "checkbox" Joseph seemingly missed.** Barnes says this bothered him for years: Joseph "nails" countless apocryphal bullseyes, but Moses 4's council-in-heaven is about choosing the *redeemer* ("I will redeem all mankind… give me thine honor"), with no Adam-envy. See [[Moses 4 and Abraham 3 Are Two Different Premortal Scenes]].
- **The Abraham 3 re-reading.** Barnes argues [[Abraham 3.22-23|Abraham 3:22-27]] is a *different* scene: "one like unto God" is **Michael** (Hebrew *mi-ka-el*, "who is like God"); the chapter never uses the word "redeem"; "we will go down… and make an earth" is *creation*, and "whom shall I send?… I will send the first" selects the *first man*. So Lucifer's fall here is over being passed for **Adam/Michael**, matching the apocrypha. See [[Abraham 3 Is the Selection of Adam Not the Redeemer]].
- **The "DNA evidence" analogy and the Book of Abraham.** Barnes argues that this Abraham-3 ↔ apocrypha match is "DNA" that cannot be forged, so the endless fixation on the papyri / facsimiles misses the real evidence in the *text*. He also issues a side "challenge" doubting that Brigham Young ever actually taught the Adam-God theory (see existing vault note [[Adam-God Theory]]).
- **Premortality is in the Bible too.** Revelation 12:7 (war in heaven; Michael vs. the dragon) is read as symbolic premortal conflict, and Barnes claims the creedal world excommunicated the pre-existence (cf. [[Origen on the Preexistence and Fall of Souls]]).
