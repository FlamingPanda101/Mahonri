A ~57-minute *Dialogue Gospel Study* conversation (hosted by [[Christian Kimball]] for *Dialogue: A Journal of Mormon Thought*, 11 January 2026) in which biblical scholar [[Dan McClellan]] coaches a Latter-day Saint audience on reading the Old Testament with academic honesty as the 2026 Come Follow Me curriculum turns to the Hebrew Bible.

## Approach — an "invitation to wrestle"
McClellan opens with admiration ("holy envy") for the Jewish tradition of scripture study, which treats scripture not as a rigid rulebook or flawless constitution but as an "invitation to wrestle." His thesis throughout: Christian communities should drop the demand that the Bible be **univocal** — one single, non-contradictory voice — and instead let each text operate on its own historical and cultural terms. (This "holy envy / invitation to wrestle" framing is the same one McClellan voices in [[Benjamin Park - Mormons and Scripture Live Discussion with Dan McClellan]]; the anti-univocality thesis is documented at [[The Bible Is a Library Not Univocal]].)

## Summary of claims (with concept links)

- **"Hebrew Bible" ≠ "Old Testament."** The Tanakh and the Christian Old Testament are differently ordered and differently translated: the Christian OT ends on [[Malachi 4|Malachi]]'s Elijah prophecy (pointing forward to Christ), while the Tanakh's three-part structure (Torah/Nevi'im/Ketuvim) ends on [[2 Chronicles 36|2 Chronicles]] and [[Cyrus the Great|Cyrus]]'s decree to rebuild the temple. Christian translations also nudge the Hebrew toward Christian theology. → **CREATED** [[The Hebrew Bible and the Christian Old Testament Are Not the Same Book]].
- **Isaiah 7:14 is mistranslated.** *almah* means "young woman" (already pregnant), and the oracle was a timeline to King [[King Ahaz|Ahaz]] — not a virgin-birth prophecy about Jesus. → [[Isaiah 7.14 Virgin Birth Prophecy Is Mistranslated]] (existing; this session appended as a second voice).
- **The closed canon is post-biblical.** No God-dictated closed list; "canon is entirely post-biblical." → [[Scripture Was a Broader Category Than the Bible - The Anachronism of a Closed Canon]] (existing; appended). Manuscript base = Leningrad Codex (~1008 CE) + Dead Sea Scrolls + Septuagint → [[The Manuscript Question Determines Your Bible]].
- **The oldest texts are embedded archaic poems.** Hebrew postdates ~1100 BCE; the oldest material is the [[Judges 5|Song of Deborah]], [[Exodus 15|Song of the Sea]], and [[Deuteronomy 32|Song of Moses]]; earliest prose is 9th–8th c. BCE northern writing. → **CREATED** [[The Oldest Biblical Texts Are Archaic Poems Embedded in Later Narrative]].
- **History rewritten by the victors.** When Assyria destroyed the Northern Kingdom (722 BCE), Judah survived, appropriated the name "Israel" and the northern literature, and its scribes produced the Deuteronomistic History (Joshua–Kings) casting the north as apostate and the southern reformers ([[Hezekiah]], [[Josiah]]) as righteous. [[Josiah]]'s "discovery" of the law justified violent cult centralization through Jerusalem, wiping out competing temples, ancestor worship, and [[No Pre-Josiah Text Vilifies Asherah|Asherah]] worship. → [[Deuteronomists Rewrote the Histories of Israel]] (existing; appended); see also [[Hezekiah Josiah and the Deuteronomist Reforms]], [[Josiah's Reform Removed the Mother from the Temple]].
- **The exile hardened Judahite identity.** After Babylon took Judah, the post-exilic program of Ezra and Nehemiah enforced strict ethnic boundaries, anti-idol polemics, and even forced divorces of non-Judahite wives to protect a fragile Judahite identity. → **CREATED** [[The Babylonian Exile Produced Ezra-Nehemiah's Ethnic Boundary Program]].
- **The afterlife was invented as theodicy.** When righteous Jews were oppressed by empires, the "you suffer because you sinned" model broke down ([[Ecclesiastes]] and Job wrestle with it), so Greco-Roman-era Jews relocated reward and punishment to the afterlife; annihilationism, temporary torment, and eternal torment appear in non-canonical texts ([[1 Enoch]]) and seep into later biblical texts. → [[Divine Punishment in the Afterlife Emerged as Theodicy Under Imperial Oppression]] (existing; appended).
- **Daniel is 2nd-century BCE.** The youngest book in the Hebrew Bible, written in the 160s BCE during the Maccabean Revolt against [[Antiochus IV Epiphanes]]; back-dated to the exile, its apocalyptic "prophecies" are encouragement for 2nd-century Jews (prophecy after the fact). → **CREATED** [[The Book of Daniel Was Written in the Second Century BCE]].
- **Stop forcing univocality.** The Bible contains problematic narratives (God commanding the Canaanite genocides); it is a collection by imperfect people with political, tribal, and rhetorical agendas. Forcing it to be a flawless single voice of God does violence to the text and to our own morality. → [[The Bible Is a Library Not Univocal]] (existing; appended); cf. [[Univocality Forces Centering Aligned Texts and Marginalizing the Rest]], [[Pan-Canonical Univocality Is Invalid Interpretation]].

## Notes on use
McClellan is a non-confessional scholar and a recurring **foil** in this vault: on Isaiah 7:14, the post-biblical canon, and "stop forcing univocality" his scholarship cuts *against* traditional Christian apologetics; on the Deuteronomist removal of Asherah / the divine Mother, the divine council, and the evolving afterlife it *supports* LDS-relevant themes.
