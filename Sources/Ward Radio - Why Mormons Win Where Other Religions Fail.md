The hosts of [[Ward Radio]] — [[Jonah Barnes]] and a co-host (referencing Cwic Media's "Quu" and family in Arizona, i.e. [[Cardon Ellis]]) — react to a Discord prompt, "How Mormons became the most religious religion," and to a viral X.com thread by a user named Clint summarizing the **2024 Pew Research Religious Landscape Study** (37,000 Americans). The episode's thesis is sociological rather than doctrinal: the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints has become *the archetypal religion of America* — the default image of "a religious person," the group satirists reach for when mocking faith — and Pew's data, the hosts claim, empirically backs this up. Much of the runtime is comedy (a 2120 birth-rate "heat map" of LDS-vs-Amish county dominance, the State of Deseret map tracing the Santa Monica Pier, prepper-Mormon jokes about Adam-ondi-Ahman), but three substantive arguments are worth isolating from the banter.

## Summary

**1. Latter-day Saints are the empirical archetype of American religiosity.** Reading off Clint's thread, the hosts list the metrics on which Pew reportedly ranks LDS members the Christian group *most likely* to score: become more religious over life, pray daily, volunteer, call the Bible extremely/very important (above evangelicals), attend weekly in person, read scripture monthly, pray before meals, belong to a congregation, share a spouse's faith, have children under 18 at home, and "think about the meaning and purpose of life at least weekly." The argument is that these are *ordinary* devotional markers, not zealotry, and that topping them makes the Church the default exemplar of religion the way a quarterback is the default "jock." Captured in [[Latter-day Saints Are the Empirical Archetype of American Religiosity]]. This extends, but does not duplicate, the vault's [[High-Demand Religion Improves Retention and Well-being]] and [[Demographic Indicators Predict a Bright LDS Future]].

**2. Being the most religious is a double-edged sword — and the mockery is a costly signal.** Because the Church is the archetype, it is the one *The Book of Mormon* musical (seven Tony Awards) and the horror film *Heretic* use as a stand-in to mock *faith in general*. The hosts argue the mockery therefore certifies LDS seriousness rather than absurdity ("that's what Christ was too, that's what Paul was too"), and — sharper — that its *selectivity* exposes the mockers: a culture that would "scatter like roaches" at a *Muhammad* musical mocks Mormons freely, so it should "drop the act" of being pluralist and "admit you're a tribalist." See [[The Mockery of the Most Religious Is a Costly Signal of Faith]]. One host lampshades the persecution framing with the self-aware joke "we're so Christlike, bro," guarding against [[Equal Treatment Mistaken for Persecution]].

**3. The caricatured conservatives are, by the same survey, the most pro-pluralism.** The buried irony: the group "lampooned as the most conservative" ranks most likely to say society is *strengthened* by many religions (including the non-religious), that one can be moral without God, that religion does more good than harm, and that science and religion are compatible — Pew also calls them the "least judgmental." The hosts ground this in soteriology: as "non-hellists" who reject damnation of the unevangelized, Latter-day Saints can sincerely affirm every faith as a good. See [[Pew Data Shows the Caricatured Conservatives Are the Most Pro-Pluralism]], which adds an *empirical* layer to the vault's existing [[God Can Lead People Through Other Religions]] and [[Religious Inclusivism Answers the Pluralism Challenge]].

The episode's recurring theological foil — the "*sola scriptura* / *sola fide*" creedal traditions as "isolationists" who emblazon "Faith Alone / Christ Alone / Grace Alone / Scripture Alone" on a Draper, Utah steeple "bigger than the cross," and who are "creedists, not Christians" — is **already thoroughly covered** in the vault and so spawns no new concept here. The relevant existing notes are wikilinked below. The hosts' contrast of the LDS "God is your father, He loves you, read the scriptures and His grace covers the rest" pitch against the creedal demand to first affirm the *homoousios* / hypostatic union restates [[Creedalism Replaced Relationship With Theology]] and [[Sola Scriptura Muzzles God]].
