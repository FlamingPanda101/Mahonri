# Ward Radio — The Book of Mormon vs Deuteronomy and the Lost Book of Moses

A long-form [[Ward Radio]] episode hosted by [[Cardon Ellis]] with [[Jonah Barnes]] ("associate professor of all things apocryphal") and [[Dave Butler]], promoting Barnes's book *Key to the Keystone*. Barnes argues — with a repeated "please don't excommunicate me" disclaimer — that the fifth book on the [[brass plates]] was not Deuteronomy but the lost **Assumption (Testament) of Moses**, and that the Deuteronomist reform overwrote Moses's last words with the bloated, scribe-serving book of Deuteronomy. This central thesis is **new to the vault**; the surrounding Deuteronomist-reform framing reinforces the existing cluster.

## Summary

- **The "five books of Moses" Lehi held need not be the canonical Pentateuch — and the fifth was the Assumption of Moses.** [[1 Nephi 5.11|1 Nephi 5:11]] says the brass plates held "the five books of Moses" but does not enumerate them; Barnes proposes Genesis-Exodus-Leviticus-Numbers plus the **Assumption of Moses** (Moses's dying testament before Canaan) in place of Deuteronomy — which a vassal-treaty law-book is too late and too redundant to be as a *fifth* book. See [[The Assumption of Moses Was the Fifth Book of Moses Replaced by Deuteronomy]]. [^1]
- **Deuteronomy has a suspicious "discovery" story and Nephi's sermon tracks the Assumption of Moses, not Deuteronomy.** [[2 Kings 22|2 Kings 22]] has the scribes "find" a self-exalting law-book and press it on the boy-king [[Josiah]]; meanwhile Nephi's long sermon in [[1 Nephi 22.20-21|1 Nephi 22]] (scattering, Gentiles, Satan bound, blood/fire/smoke) parallels the Assumption of Moses almost in order, and the "prophet like me" line the scholars cite as proof of Deuteronomy likely sat in the *torn-off* ending of the Assumption of Moses. See [[The Assumption of Moses Was the Fifth Book of Moses Replaced by Deuteronomy]]. [^2]
- **The Book of Mormon contradicts Deuteronomy precisely where the Assumption of Moses does: Moses was translated, not buried.** [[Deuteronomy 34.5-6|Deuteronomy 34]] says Moses "died... and he buried him," but [[Alma 45.18-19|Alma 45]] assumes everyone knew "the Lord took Moses unto himself" (translation), matching the New Testament (Transfiguration; [[Jude]]'s dispute over Moses' body) and the Assumption of Moses — so Deuteronomy, not the Book of Mormon, is the outlier. See [[The Assumption of Moses Was the Fifth Book of Moses Replaced by Deuteronomy]]. [^3]
- **The whole deuteronomistic history is absent from the brass plates — an "infiltration" into the canon.** Barnes notes Joshua, Judges, Samuel, and Kings (the deuteronomistic package) do not appear on the brass plates, while Genesis-Numbers, Isaiah, Jeremiah, Micah, and Chronicles-type material do; he frames Deuteronomy as a corrupting interpolation recasting God "in the image of the scribe." See [[The Deuteronomistic History Books Are Absent From the Brass Plates]] and the existing [[Deuteronomistic Corruption of the Old Testament]]. [^4]
