# The Stick of Joseph - The First Temple Nephi and Isaiah and Ancient Priesthood Patterns (Mike and Dave)

A "Reading Books with Mike and Dave" episode hosted by **[[Brad Whitbeck]]** (sitting in for one of the Paul brothers), in which **[[Mike Day]]** and **[[Dave Butler]]** discuss **[[Menahem Haran]]**'s *Temples and Temple-Service in Ancient Israel* (Oxford, 1978) and draw out its relevance for the Book of Mormon and the temple. The episode also plugs [[Jonah Barnes]]'s book *Lost Gems of Genesis*.

## Summary
- **Haran's pre-exilic dating of P.** The hosts review the Documentary Hypothesis (J, E, D, P) and Haran's minority position — backed by [[Michael Heiser]] and [[Richard Elliott Friedman]] — that the priestly source (P) is **pre-exilic**, the "constitution" of [[Hezekiah]]'s reforms just as D was the constitution of [[Josiah]]'s. The tabernacle as described is a "priestly utopia," a retrojection of Hezekiah's temple. See [[Hezekiah Josiah and the Deuteronomist Reforms]].
- **Garments make an angel; the high priest wears the veil.** Haran (pp. 210, 27-28) holds the linen vestments make the priest an angel, and that the high priest's robes are made of the same stuff as the temple veil — so the priest who emerges on the Day of Atonement *is* the veil, i.e. the body of the Lord. See [[The Garment Is the Veil of the Temple and the Body of the Lord]].
- **A hidden bread-and-wine ordinance.** Haran (pp. 216-217) notes wine vessels in the temple's inner room with no rite of pouring wine out, and argues wine was *consumed* with the showbread ([[Leviticus 24]]) every Sabbath — a forgotten ordinance Butler ties to [[Melchizedek]] ([[Genesis 14.18]]) and the sacrament. See [[The First Temple Had a Hidden Bread-and-Wine Ordinance]].
- **Nephi as priest-king.** Because Haran says temple ritual was the esoteric prerogative of an elite priestly class, Butler infers [[Nephi]] (who calls himself a king and a priest) was writing from that priestly position. See [[Nephi Wrote as a First-Temple Priest-King]].
- **2 Nephi 31-32 as a temple ascent.** Butler reads Nephi's "doctrine of Christ" (entering the gate, the tongue of angels, knocking and entering the light behind the veil, [[2 Nephi 32.5-6]]) as a veiled temple-ascent text. See [[Second Nephi 31-32 Encodes a Temple Ascent to the Veil]].
