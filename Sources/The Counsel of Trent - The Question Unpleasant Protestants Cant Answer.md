# The Counsel of Trent — The Question "Unpleasant Protestants" Can't Answer

[[Trent Horn]] argues that "unpleasant Protestants" — those who say Catholicism preaches a *false gospel* and so is not even Christian — cannot consistently and biblically answer the question **"What is the gospel?"** When pressed, they substitute a denomination-specific doctrine (e.g. Reformed [[Double Imputation Is Not the Gospel|double imputation]]) for the biblical [[Definition of Gospel|gospel]], a move that would unchurch most other Protestants too. The same disagreement, Horn says, exposes the failure of [[The Light Manifests Itself - Perspicuity of Scripture|perspicuity]] and points to the need for a [[A Living Magisterium Settles What Sola Scriptura Cannot|living magisterium]].

## Summary
- The word **gospel** (Greek *euangelion*, "good news") is used in Scripture almost always without a definition; in Jesus's earthly ministry it meant the good news that **God's kingdom is breaking into the world**, drawing on Isaiah's announcement of God's victory over his enemies [^1]. See [[The Gospel Is the Good News of God's Kingdom Not a Doctrinal Formula]].
- Following his colleague [[Jimmy Akin]], Horn says the core of the gospel is that **God's kingdom has arrived and all opposing powers will be overthrown through his Son**; individual salvation is a *happy consequence* of the gospel, not its essence — "the gospel is just not about us" [^2]. See [[The Gospel Is the Good News of God's Kingdom Not a Doctrinal Formula]].
- A minimal biblical definition: the gospel is **the good news of God's kingdom entering the world that saves God's people through the life, death, and resurrection of Jesus Christ** ([[1 Corinthians 15.1-4|1 Cor 15:1-4]], [[Romans 1.16|Rom 1:16]]). It never says the good news is "salvation through faith alone / grace alone / double imputation." Because Catholicism affirms this, the Catholic Church does **not** preach a false gospel [^3]. See [[Catholicism Does Not Preach a False Gospel]].
- "Unpleasant Protestants" reach the false-gospel charge by two mistakes: (1) equating *the gospel* with *all essential doctrines*, and (2) treating *their denomination's* essential doctrines as essential to Christianity itself — so their distinctives become part of "the gospel" [^4]. See [[Two Mistakes That Turn a Denomination's Distinctives Into the Gospel]].
- [[Voddie Baucham]] and [[John MacArthur]] both identify the "heart of the gospel" with Reformed **double imputation** (our sin to Christ, Christ's righteousness to us — MacArthur on [[2 Corinthians 5.21|2 Cor 5:21]]). Protestant theologian [[Randal Rauser]] replies that this "is not the gospel — it's an *interpretation* of the gospel," and conflating the two leads to treating dissenters as sub-Christian [^5]. See [[Double Imputation Is Not the Gospel]].
- If double imputation were the gospel, then **Methodists** (followers of [[John Wesley]], who teach *imparted* not *imputed* righteousness) and anyone reading [[2 Corinthians 5.21|2 Cor 5:21]] as the incarnation ([[Romans 8.3|Rom 8:3]], "sinful flesh") or as Christ becoming a *sin offering* would also have a false gospel — a reductio the critics will not accept [^6]. See [[Double Imputation Is Not the Gospel]].
- The gospel must be neither over- nor under-defined: in [[Galatians 1.6|Galatians 1]] Paul reserves the charge of "another gospel" for doctrines that **contradict the core message** — e.g. denying Christ's death and resurrection (Islam) or requiring circumcision (making one become a Jew first), not for every theological error [^7]. See [[A False Gospel Is One That Contradicts the Kingdom Message]].
- Finally, even *pleasant* Protestants face an unanswerable question on [[Sola Scriptura - Scripture Self-Attests Its Authority|sola scriptura]]: **which doctrines are essential, and how do we know?** The Westminster Confession claims essentials are "clearly propounded" in Scripture, yet learned Protestants cannot even agree whether Catholicism preaches a true gospel — evidence that authority requires not bare Scripture but a **living, infallible magisterium** [^8]. See [[A Living Magisterium Settles What Sola Scriptura Cannot]] and [[Perspicuity Fails the Hard-Cases Test]].
