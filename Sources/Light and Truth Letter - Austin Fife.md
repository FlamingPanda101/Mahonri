# Light and Truth Letter

Austin Fife's 2024 open letter (subtitle *My Search for More Light and Truth*) — a sustained, source-by-source **response to the [[CES Letter]]** and other critics of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (Mormon Stories, ex-member and critical-Christian commentary). Framed as the fruit of the author's own ten-year search after losing his faith in 2014, it turns the critics' "search for truth" framing back on them.

## Summary
- Opens by cataloguing the **manipulation tactics and logical fallacies** Fife says critics rely on (false premise, non-sequitur, questionable cause, argument from ignorance, burden-shifting, hasty generalization, Texas sharpshooter, etc.) [^1].
- Works **topic by topic** through the standard CES-Letter battery — Book of Mormon translation and historicity (e.g., parallels like the Narrative of Zosimus), the Book of Abraham, the witnesses, polygamy/polyandry, First Vision accounts, Kinderhook, Masonry, DNA and anachronisms, prophetic fallibility, and Church finances — arguing the critics' case repeatedly commits the catalogued fallacies [^2].
- Method: grant the hardest data, then show the critic's *inference* (not the fact) is what fails; restore context the critics omit.
- **Apologetic use:** the vault's broad **"answering the CES Letter / challenges"** resource — a counterpart to [[CES Letter]] and a companion to the channel-sourced rebuttals already catalogued.
