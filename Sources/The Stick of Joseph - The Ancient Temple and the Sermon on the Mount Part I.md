# The Stick of Joseph — The Ancient Temple and the Sermon on the Mount Part I

Part I of a long-form conversation on the [[The Stick of Joseph - Are Christians Becoming Mormon with Brian Thomassen|Stick of Joseph]] channel with author [[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)|David Butler]] (joined by [[Mike Day]] and hosts [[Hayden Carroll]] and Jackson), reading **the Sermon on the Mount through ancient-temple ritual symbolism**. Butler's governing thesis — restated from the channel's temple cluster — is that "the Book of Mormon is written by temple initiates for temple initiates," so [[Nephi]], Jacob, Mormon, and Moroni encoded their [[The Book of Mormon Already Contains the Endowment|endowment]] experiences expecting the endowed reader to recognize them. The episode's distinctive payload is the claim, credited to [[Jack Welch|John (Jack) Welch]], that **the Sermon on the Mount is not a sermon but an ordinance** that plays out across the three rooms of Solomon's Temple.

## Summary
- **"Mysteries" means initiatory rites, and revelation is initiatic, not secret.** Butler opens on [[1 Nephi 1.1|1 Nephi 1:1]] — Nephi's "the goodness and the mysteries of God" — arguing *mystēria* originally named "secret and sacred ordinances" (Eleusis, Demeter; a *mystēs* is an initiate). His epistemology: "there are no actual secrets; everything is being shouted from the rooftops all the time," but meaning unfolds only as the seeker becomes "a receiver" — Mike's radio-antenna analogy. This frames the whole reading and feeds [[The Sermon on the Mount Is an Ordinance in Three Rooms]] [^1][^2].
- **Solomon's Temple divides space into three named rooms.** From Exodus, 1 Kings 6–7, and 2 Chronicles 3–4: the *ulam* (porch, with pillars Jachin "he will establish" and Boaz "in him is strength"); the *heikhal* (nave/holy place — an old Sumerian word *E.GAL*, "big house," that "literally means any large or spacious building," a hint Butler flags); and the *debir* (holy of holies), a perfect cube whose root connects to *davar*, "word" (the Oracle, place of speech). This is the architectural backbone for [[Gradations of Holiness Structured the Ancient Temple]] [^3][^4].
- **You always "go up" to the temple — an ascent ideology baked into Hebrew.** One ascends from room to room and "comes down" on leaving; an immigrant to Israel is an *oleh*, "an ascender." Reinforces the temple-as-ascent reading and the mountain symbolism of [[The Garden of Eden Was a Prototype Sanctuary|Eden on the mountain]] [^5].
- **The menorah is a tree — the world-tree at the cosmic axis.** The seven-branched lamp in the *heikhal* is "clearly a tree" bearing almond blossoms; its branches map the seven classical planets and its central stem the world-axis resting on the foundation stone. Developed in the vault under [[Lehi's Dream Recapitulates the Eden-Temple Return]] and the seven-blessings count in [[Beatitudes as the Entry Requirements of the Temple Ordinance]] [^6].
- **The foundation stone (Eben Shetiyah) and the Well of Souls.** The *debir* held the Ark atop the foundation stone (still extant beneath the Dome of the Rock); the *Mishnah* (Yoma) records that after the Ark's loss the high priest applied the atonement blood to the rock itself. Beneath it is the cavern "the Well of Souls," tied to flood/abyss waters (the stone as a cork) and to temple-river imagery (Ezekiel 47; Revelation 22) [^7][^8].
- **Bread in the temple typologically points to Christ.** Manna ("what is it?"), white and honey-sweet, was kept "before the Lord" (Exodus 16; the Ark per Hebrews 9); the frankincense-flavored showbread was eaten by the Levites "in remembrance." Jesus identifies himself with the bread of heaven (John 6) and stages the Last Supper "up" in an upper room, fusing manna + showbread with his "in remembrance of me" [^9][^10].
- **THE CORE CLAIM — the Sermon on the Mount is an ordinance in three parts.** Welch's thesis (which Butler says he only grasped on re-reading a decade later): the Sermon resists being a "talk," and is better read as an initiatory rite enacting the garden story across the temple's three rooms. Matthew 5:1 stages it — Jesus "went up into a mountain, and when he was set" (Hebrew *yashav* = sit **and** dwell), so "the sitter on top of the mountain is God." Full argument in [[The Sermon on the Mount Is an Ordinance in Three Rooms]] [^11][^12].
- **The Beatitudes are entry requirements; "blessed" = the deified.** *Makarioi* / Hebrew *ashrei* ("O the happiness of"); citing LeGrand Baker, the *makarioi* were "those likened to the gods." Butler flags nine beatitude-statements but only seven distinct blessings, matching the menorah's seven branches — a structural seam he says pays off in Jacob's 2 Nephi 9. See [[Beatitudes as the Entry Requirements of the Temple Ordinance]] [^13].
- **Salt that loses its savor = the covenant penalty, enacted twice in the Book of Mormon.** "Cast out, and trodden under foot" ([[Matthew 5.13|Matt 5:13]]) is a death-sanction for covenant violation — suffered by [[Alma 30.27|Korihor]] (an apostate covenant man, not a secular skeptic) and sworn in the [[Alma 46.21-22|Title of Liberty]]. See [[The Salt-Savor Covenant Penalty Enacted by Korihor and the Title of Liberty]] [^14][^15].
- **Graded laws, an officiant-Moses, and a personated adversary.** "Light of the world . . . candlestick" teaches the initiate to *shine* (the "shining ones," the divine; cf. the Transfiguration's Moses–Elijah–Jesus). The law-of-Moses verses imply a Moses–Elijah–the Lord officiating sequence; the altar instruction ("leave there thy gift before the altar") reads as stage direction; and the "adversary" of Matt 5:25 is the *antidikos*/*satan*, a priest personating the legal accuser. The forbidden oath (Matt 5:33-37) is mirrored by the Jaredite secret-combination oath of [[Ether 8.13-18|Ether 8]]. See [[The Forbidden Oath Is the Dark Mirror of the Covenant Oath]] [^16][^17].
- **"Be ye perfect" = "you will be an initiate."** *Esesthe téleioi* is future indicative, and *téleios* carries the sense "ritually complete / endowed," making 5:48 a change-of-status/new-name moment ("King Follett land"). The Hebrew *shalem*/*shalom* underwrites the "peaceable followers of Christ" who "enter into the rest" (= holy of holies) in **Moroni 7** and the "mysteries and peaceable things" of **D&C 42:61**. See [[Teleios Means Initiate - You Will Be Endowed Not Be Flawless]] [^18][^19].
