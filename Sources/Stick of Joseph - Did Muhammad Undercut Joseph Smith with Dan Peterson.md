# Stick of Joseph — Did Muhammad Undercut Joseph Smith? (with Dan Peterson)

A *Stick of Joseph* interview in which the host questions Dr. [[Daniel C. Peterson|Daniel Peterson]] — professor emeritus of Islamic Studies and Arabic at Brigham Young University and author of the biography *Muhammad: Prophet of God* — about the recurring 19th- and 21st-century claim that [[Muhammad]] and his production of the Quran "undercut" the story of [[Joseph Smith]] and the Book of Mormon.[^1] The conversation closes by noting a companion interview with Casey Paul Griffiths on the King Follett discourse.[^12]

## Summary
- Peterson argues the Joseph–Muhammad comparison is **"apples to oranges"**: the Quran is a single divine voice parcelled out over ~22 years and is best compared not to the Book of Mormon but to the **Doctrine and Covenants**, whereas the Book of Mormon is a millennium-spanning narrative dictated in roughly two months.[^2] See [[Muhammad and Joseph Smith Are Apples to Oranges]], [[The Quran Parallels the Doctrine and Covenants Not the Book of Mormon]].
- A second structural disanalogy: **Muhammad had no corroborating witnesses and no physical artifacts**, while Joseph Smith repeatedly experienced visions in company and produced tangible objects (gold plates, Urim and Thummim, breastplate).[^3] See [[Muhammad Had No Corroborating Witnesses or Physical Artifacts]].
- Peterson treats the comparison historically as an **Islamophobic smear** — 19th-century critics yoked Joseph to the already-discredited Muhammad (especially via polygamy), while early Utah leaders inverted it and spoke warmly of Muhammad as an inspired man.[^4] See [[Joseph-Muhammad Comparison as Islamophobic Smear]], [[Early LDS Leaders Praised Muhammad as Inspired]].
- He affirms a striking pluralism: **other true prophets do not threaten Joseph Smith** because prophets are fallible and God can give partial light to many peoples; Peterson will not rule out that Muhammad really was inspired, even as Islam's denial of the **deity and divine sonship of Christ** marks a real doctrinal divide.[^5] See [[Other True Prophets Do Not Undercut Joseph Smith]], [[Muhammad May Have Been a True Inspired Prophet]], [[Islam Denies the Deity and Divine Sonship of Christ]], [[Same-Species-as-God as the Core LDS Distinctive]].
- On Islam itself Peterson corrects two popular distortions — the supposedly **pristine textual history of the Quran** (true relative to the Bible, but a WWII bombing of the Berlin variant archive "simplified" that history) and the equation of Islam with violence: **jihad** originally meant striving, not holy war, and modern terrorism (including suicide bombing, invented by Marxist Tamil Tigers) is largely political, not Quranic.[^6][^7] See [[Quranic Textual History Is Cleaner Than the Bible's]], [[Jihad Does Not Originally Mean Holy War]], [[Islamic Violence Is Mostly Political Not Quranic]].
- He commends three practices of Islam as objects of **"holy envy"** for Latter-day Saints: disciplined five-times-daily prayer, reverence for the physical scriptures, and religion as a seven-day-a-week, whole-life identity.[^8] See [[Holy Envy - What Latter-day Saints Can Learn from Islam]].
