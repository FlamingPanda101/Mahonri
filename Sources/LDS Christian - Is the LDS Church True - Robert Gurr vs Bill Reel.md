# LDS Christian — Is the LDS Church True? | Robert Gurr vs. Bill Reel

A moderated debate (host Stephen Andrew Bolan, Proverbs Guys Ministries) structured as three timed position segments, an hour of open discussion, and closing statements. [[Robert Gurr]] takes the affirmative that the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints is true; [[Bill Reel]], a former LDS bishop turned "counter-apologist," argues that the accumulation of problems makes the truth-claims untenable. The clash is as much about *method* — what standard of evidence is fair, and which side keeps "moving the goalpost" — as about any single issue.

## Participants
[[Robert Gurr]] (affirmative — LDS apologist) vs. [[Bill Reel]] (negative — ex-Latter-day Saint critic, former bishop). Moderated by Stephen Andrew Bolan of Proverbs Guys Ministries.

## Summary
- **Gurr's framing: "consistent skepticism" turned against atheism.** Borrowing Sherlock Holmes ("when you have eliminated the impossible, whatever remains... must be the truth") and the gullibility–denial spectrum, Gurr argues a fair standard sits in the middle, and that ex-Mormon atheists apply *maximal* skepticism to the Restoration but *minimal* skepticism to abiogenesis, consciousness-from-matter, and unguided evolution. He presses Steve Benson's question to [[Sandra Tanner]] back onto Reel: why not apply the same rigor to atheism?[^1] See [[Skepticism Must Be Applied Consistently to Atheism Too]].
- **Gurr's positive case I — the Restoration matches pre-Nicene Christianity.** Marshalling Clement, Ignatius, Justin Martyr, Irenaeus, Tertullian, Hippolytus, Cyprian, Cyril, and Augustine, Gurr argues the earliest Christians taught believer's baptism for remission of sin (infant baptism arriving only ~215 AD with Hippolytus, universalized by Augustine), the gift of the Spirit by laying on of hands, anointing/chrismation as the moment one was *called* Christian, a *social* and subordinationist godhead ("two gods," *duos theous*) rather than the Trinity, plus deification, premortality, and free will — all lost in the apostasy and restored by Joseph Smith.[^2] See [[Restoration Matches Pre-Nicene Christianity - Gurr's Argument]].
- **Gurr's positive case II — Mesoamerican sources as a second witness.** Reading Diego Durán, Ixtlilxóchitl, and codices (Boturini, Aubin, etc.), Gurr claims the Aztec/Toltec migration narrative parallels the Book of Mormon: exiles leaving by sea, a directional "idol" (the Liahona), fair-skinned ancestors, seven tribes, a virgin-born "god of air and water" (Quetzalcóatl) identified with the morning star/Venus (cf. Christ as the "bright morning star"), and a civilization-ending last stand on a hill in a land of "many waters" (relocating Cumorah to the Valley of Mexico / Lake Texcoco).[^3] See [[Mesoamerican Sources Preserve a Book of Mormon Migration - Gurr]].
- **Reel's framing: the least-conjecture test, and the goalpost-moving pattern.** Reel insists the question is not whether an answer *exists* but which explanation "makes the most sense with the least amount of stretching," and that Mormonism repeatedly starts with a definite claim, meets disconfirming data, and then retreats the claim to something *unfalsifiable* (Israelite ancestry → DNA; Book of Abraham "by his own hand" → catalyst theory; inspired Bible → flawed commentary; tight translation → expansion theory).[^4] See [[The Least-Conjecture Test and the Goalpost-Moving Pattern]].
- **Reel's issue 1 — Book of Mormon anachronisms as a collective pattern.** Steel, swords, chariots, horses, large-scale grain (wheat/barley), coinage, mass literacy, and Israelite DNA are all absent from the pre-Columbian record; individually deniable, collectively they suggest "they were never there." He flags the apologetic tactics of reinterpretation ("horse doesn't mean horse"), demanding lowered expectations, and citing weak examples (wheeled *figurines* for chariots, "little barley," a single contested horse bone).[^5] See [[Book of Mormon Anachronisms Are a Collective Pattern - Reel]].
- **Reel's issue 2 — the Book of Abraham and the three apologetic exits.** The surviving papyri and Kirtland Egyptian Papers tie Joseph's "translation" to fragments that Egyptologists (LDS and non-LDS, per the Gospel Topics essay) agree are ordinary funerary texts naming neither Abraham nor his story. Reel maps the only three apologetic moves — unknown method, missing scroll, catalyst theory — and argues each requires dismissing Egyptology, multiplying missing documents (now *three* scrolls), or redefining "translation" into unfalsifiability. In the open discussion Gurr concedes he uses the **catalyst theory**.[^6] See [[The Book of Abraham Has Only Three Apologetic Exits - Reel]].
- **Reel's issue 3 — Joseph Smith's polygamy as a predatory behavioral pattern.** Reel walks the grooming sequence (target the vulnerable, build trust as a father-figure, isolate, desensitize, invoke prophetic authority and an angel with a sword, apply eternal pressure, use intermediaries, impose secrecy, silence dissenters) and the case of 16-/17-year-old Lucy Walker — orphaned of her mother, her father sent on a mission by Smith, given a one-night then a second-night ultimatum with "the gates of heaven shall be closed against you." Gurr's rebuttal leans on presentism, frontier life-expectancy, and treating most sealings as non-consummated "ward" adoptions; Reel answers that a developing frontal cortex makes religiously-coerced marriage of a 14-year-old "wrong, wrong, and wrong" in any century.[^7] See [[Joseph Smith's Polygamy as a Predatory Pattern - Reel]] and [[Presentism Defense of Joseph Smith's Polygamy - Gurr]].
- **Open-discussion flashpoints.** The morality exchange (Reel grounds ethics in lived intuition and harm-avoidance; Gurr presses the moral-foundation and "you're an atheist" line, which Reel rejects as a mis-boxing — "atheist mystic"); the horses/ice-age exchange (Gurr: an ice age can't kill horses in tropical Mexico, so the consensus deserves skepticism; Reel: that is "what-if" reasoning against a 98.7% consensus); the steel→bronze translation move; the KJV-parallels-as-plagiarism charge (1 Cor 12 ≈ Moroni 10; Mark 16's long ending ≈ Mormon 9); the Adam Clark commentary in the JST; the Kinderhook plates; the gold-plates weight; and David Whitmer's roadside-Moroni "walking to Cumorah in Palmyra" testimony.[^8]
