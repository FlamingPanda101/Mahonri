# Ward Radio - All the Church Fathers Secretly Taught Mormon Doctrine (Apotheosis)

**Channel:** Ward Radio · **Host:** [[Cardon Ellis]] · **Guests:** [[Luke Hansen]], [[Jonah Barnes]]
**URL:** https://youtu.be/zQBsNBHCjOw

## Summary
- Central claim: the church fathers of the first five centuries taught **apotheosis** (deification) — humans becoming gods, lowercase-g, beneath the Father — and the hosts "claim them as Mormons." They march through quotes from [[Justin Martyr]], [[Irenaeus of Lyons]], [[Theophilus of Antioch]], [[Hippolytus of Rome]], [[Clement of Alexandria]], [[Origen]], [[Athanasius]], and [[Gregory of Nazianzus]], plus [[C.S. Lewis]] and [[Thomas Aquinas]].
- Pre-patristic layer: Old Testament apocrypha/pseudepigrapha (Armenian & Egyptian *Books of Adam and Eve*, the *Books of Enoch*, *Apocalypse of Elijah*, *Ascension of Isaiah*) routinely show **lowercase gods everywhere** — when Lucifer claims to be "a god," no one finds it strange. Jonah argues the New Year-name ([[Revelation 2.17|Revelation 2:17]], the white stone) and the sacrament's "take upon you the name of the Son" encode theosis ("lesser Yah"/mini-Jehovah).
- Loss story: the radical 1st-century version was diluted, then voted out in contentious councils; surviving "cushy Rome" clergy wrote it out. Closes with the "banal/earthy godliness" thesis — weeding, child-rearing, fixing a faucet are the truly godlike acts, against the monastic ideal.
