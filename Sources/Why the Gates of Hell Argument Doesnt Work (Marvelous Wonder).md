# Why the Gates of Hell Argument Doesn't Work (Marvelous Wonder)

- **The "gates of hell" objection to apostasy rests on two confusions** — that an institution surviving proves it is not in error, and that "hell" in [[Matthew 16.18|Matthew 16:18]] means eternal torment rather than *hades/sheol*, the realm of the dead. See [[The Gates of Hell Objection to Apostasy Conflates Survival With Soundness]].
- **"Gates of hell" = the place of the dead, which Christ's restored temple work unlocks:** Because *hades* is where Christ descended (1 Peter 3-4; D&C 138), the promise is best fulfilled by the Restoration's proxy ordinances (baptism for the dead) that open the spirit prison — so the LDS Church is the one body *actually* keeping the promise. See [[Temple Work for the Dead Fulfills the Gates of Hell Promise]].
- **Elijah's sealing keys (Kirtland, 1836) turn the hearts of the fathers and children** through temple sealing, extending salvation to those who died without the gospel — the answer to why a loving God lets billions die unevangelized. See [[Temple Work for the Dead Fulfills the Gates of Hell Promise]].

This is a Latter-day Saint source. Concept notes drawn from it carry the LDS framing directly; the steelmanned opponent is the Catholic/Protestant "indefectibility" reading of Matthew 16, already documented in [[The Gates of Hades Are Death and Do Not Besiege the Church]].
