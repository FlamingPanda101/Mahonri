# Ward Radio — Concentric Chiasmus in Alma 24

[[Cardon Ellis]] hosts [[Jonah (Plain and Precious Publishing)|Jonah]] (Plain & Precious Publishing), who presents a claimed "previously undiscovered" **double, concentric chiasmus** in King [[Anti-Nephi-Lehi]]'s pacifism speech in [[Alma 24.12-13|Alma 24]] — two chiastic structures (one on the word *stain*, one on *brethren*) that pivot on the same central verse. The episode is explicitly framed as fun, speculative argument-generation, with both hosts joking about its lack of peer review.

## Summary
- Jonah argues the rare word **"stain"** ([[Alma 24.12-13|Alma 24:12-13]]) appears in three grammatical forms (noun, verb, past participle) arranged as a five-point chiasm (noun–verb–past-participle–verb–noun), pivoting on the atonement verse [^1].
- He claims the word **"brethren"** forms a *second*, larger chiasm that begins before and ends after the stain-chiasm yet centers on the same verse — hence "concentric"; he calls it a "[[Concentric Chiasmus in Alma 24|kayazm inception]]" [^2].
- He ties the **Hebrew "liquid" semantics of *atonement* (kpr, "to cover")** to the verse's clustered blood/wash/sprinkle imagery, arguing Joseph Smith "could not have known" the cover-etymology — an [[Atonement as Covering - Joseph Could Not Have Known|argument from translator ignorance]] [^3].
- He floats a **"five" motif** linking the speech's fives (five thanksgivings, five-point chiasms, the [[Alma 24.22|1,005 slain]]) to the **five-nation [[Hiawatha Belt Parallel - Buried Weapons Under a Tree|Hiawatha "bury the weapons" belt]]**, suggesting a persisting New-World peace tradition [^4][^5].
- Both hosts repeatedly concede the lack of peer review and lampoon credentialism (the "PhDs are like airbags" riff), and Jonah offers the Mesoamerican vs. Heartland model jab as a joke, then pivots to the [[Infinite Monkey Theorem Against Random Chiasmus|infinite-monkey argument]] that ordered language cannot arise by chance [^6][^7].
