# The Stick of Joseph - How Was the Book of Mormon Actually Translated (Mike Day)

Hosts Jackson and Hayden (Daniel) interview **[[Mike Day]]** — a longtime CES seminary/institute teacher and PhD candidate in classical Greek and Biblical Hebrew — on the *order* in which the Book of Mormon was dictated and why that order is itself an evidence of authenticity. The conversation repeatedly defers to historian **[[Don Bradley]]**'s reconstruction of the lost 116 pages.

## Summary
- The translation proceeded **Mosiah-first**: after the 116 pages were lost, Joseph and [[Oliver Cowdery]] resumed in the book of Mosiah (~April 7, 1829) and worked to the end, translating the small-plates books (1–2 Nephi through Words of Mormon) *last*, in the [[David Whitmer|Whitmer]] home, in only ~75 working days. See [[The Book of Mormon Was Translated Mosiah-First]].
- This order makes the text's internal cross-references hard to fake: from Mosiah to Moroni the narrative quotes and alludes to small-plates material (e.g. [[Moroni 10]] quoting five small-plates authors; the [[Alma 36]] and [[Alma 50]] echoes of [[1 Nephi 1.8|Lehi's vision]] and Nephi's "manner of happiness") that *had not yet been dictated*. See [[Internal Cross-References to the Unwritten Small Plates]].
- Day situates the record in the 7th-century BC Deuteronomist controversy (Josiah ~640–609 BC), arguing Lehi preserves the pre-reform, anthropomorphic, Messianic temple religion the [[Deuteronomistic Corruption of the Old Testament|reformers edited out]] — and that an unlearned 23-year-old reproducing these "fights" is "gold." On the [[Deutero-Isaiah Theory|Deutero-Isaiah]] question he holds Nephi quotes the genuine pre-exilic Isaiah. Includes the channel's recurring [[Most Correct Book Does Not Mean Error Free|"most correct ≠ inerrant"]] and [[Authenticity Is What the Millennial Generation Wants From the Church|authenticity]] themes.
