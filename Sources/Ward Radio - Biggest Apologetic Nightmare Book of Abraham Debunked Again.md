# Ward Radio — Biggest Apologetic Nightmare: Book of Abraham Debunked Again

A [[Ward Radio]] reaction episode in which [[Cardon Ellis]] and guest [[Jonah Barnes]] (Plain and Precious Publishing) answer an evangelical video — by a channel called "Testify" featuring a "Frank Turk"/[[Frank Turek]]-style narrator — that brands the Book of Abraham "perhaps the biggest apologetic nightmare for Mormons" and pairs it with the recycled "Joseph Smith is Muhammad / self-certifying prophet" trope. The hosts' core move is to expose a **provenance-vs-content double standard**: the critic refuses to read the *text* of the Book of Abraham (talking only about papyri, mummies, and Egyptologists) while, for the Book of Mormon, waving away the witnesses and plates to attack the content — and they argue the text itself impossibly anticipates apocrypha discovered decades after Joseph's death.

## Summary

- **The "provenance vs content" double standard.** The critic attacks the Book of Abraham purely on *provenance* (the papyri) and never engages the text, but attacks the Book of Mormon purely on *content* (anachronisms) while dismissing its provenance (the witnesses); content matters only when convenient. See [[The Book of Abraham Debate Plays a Provenance vs Content Double Standard]]. [^1]
- **Egyptian is not "easily translatable."** The claim that the papyri are "now easily translatable" with content "nothing to do with Abraham" is false — give any seven-glyph sequence to a room of Egyptologists and they argue; the facsimiles have lacunae, so no one knows exactly what the originals showed. See [[Ancient Egyptian Is Not Easily Translatable]]. [^2]
- **Lion-couch legs-apart marks a living/exalted figure, not a forgery.** Critics said Abraham's parted legs in Facsimile 1 prove a botched lion-couch (embalming) scene; [[Bruce Porter]] photographed temple lion-couch scenes with legs apart, signaling a *living* or *exalted* being, not death. See [[Lion-Couch Legs Apart Signal a Living Exalted Figure Not a Forgery]]. [^3]
- **The text impossibly predicts post-1842 apocrypha (existing vault claim, corroborated).** Barnes's "top five proofs" — human sacrifice in Abraham's day, Abraham's opposition to idols, Chaldea importing Egyptian idolatry, Abraham as astronomer, Lucifer's envy of Michael (Abr. 3:21-28) — all surface in the *Apocalypse of Abraham* (1898), *Jubilees*, and *Jasher*, recovered after Joseph's death. This corroborates [[Apocryphal Sources Corroborate the Book of Abraham After 1842]] and [[Later Texts Corroborate Details Joseph Smith Could Not Have Known in the Book of Abraham]]. [^4]
- **Joseph never canonized or explained the Book of Abraham.** It was *not* "quickly canonized"; Joseph never wrote an introduction or stated his intent; Franklin D. Richards compiled the Pearl of Great Price as a missionary tract in 1851 and it was canonized only in 1880 — "because you were too busy slaughtering him." This reframes "Joseph presented these facsimiles as…" as an unfounded trope. See [[Questioning Assumptions Is the First Defense of the Book of Abraham]] and [[A Prophet Is Not an Academic Translator]]. [^5]
