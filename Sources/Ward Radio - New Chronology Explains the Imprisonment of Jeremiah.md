# Ward Radio — New Chronology Explains the Imprisonment of Jeremiah

A short "bonus materials" [[Ward Radio]] segment hosted by [[Cardon Ellis]] with independent Book of Mormon researcher [[Jerry Grover]] (bmslr.org, where his books are free). Grover lays out a new chronology of Lehi's departure that (he argues) resolves a long-standing puzzle: how [[Nephi]] could know [[Jeremiah]] was already imprisoned. The segment interlocks Grover's dating with [[Don Bradley]]'s Passover thesis and touches the [[brass plates]] as a *northern-kingdom* record and the Zenos/teacher-of-righteousness speculation. The Jeremiah-chronology argument is **new to the vault**; the northern-kingdom and Zenos material overlaps existing notes.

## Summary

- **A break in the Babylonian siege explains how Nephi knew Jeremiah was imprisoned.** Grover dates Lehi's departure to ~January, then the Babylonians lifted the siege of Jerusalem to repel an Egyptian relief army; during that break Jeremiah tried to leave the city and Jerusalem's own leaders jailed him (not the Babylonians). Nephi's party returned for the plates and Ishmael during that same break — so Jeremiah was *already* in prison, no vision required. See [[The Break in the Babylonian Siege Explains Nephis Knowledge of Jeremiahs Imprisonment]]. [^1]
- **The chronology corroborates Don Bradley's "Laban killing was a Passover" thesis.** [[Don Bradley]]'s *The Lost 116 Pages* argues the slaying of [[Laban]] mirrors the Passover/Exodus (Laban drunk from a feast; the death of Pharaoh's firstborn); Grover's independently derived departure date puts the return-for-the-plates in the Passover window during the siege break, so the two reconstructions reinforce each other. See [[The Break in the Babylonian Siege Explains Nephis Knowledge of Jeremiahs Imprisonment]]. [^2]
- **The brass plates were a northern-kingdom (Joseph) record with its own prophetic tradition.** Grover argues the plates were not the Judahite Old Testament but the record of the northern tribes — which had their own prophets (possibly the source of [[Zenos]] / Zenock) — so "missing" Book of Mormon prophets need not be missing from *Judah's* canon. This reinforces existing notes. See [[The Surviving Old Testament Is the Southern Kingdom's Edition]] and [[Lehi Was of Manasseh So the Book of Mormon Is a Northern Israelite Record]]. [^3]
