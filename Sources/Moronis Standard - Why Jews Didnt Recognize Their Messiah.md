# Why Jews Didn't Recognize Their Messiah: The Prophecies They Deleted

[[Alex Arnold]] of *Moroni's Standard* argues that the Jewish establishment's failure to recognize Jesus was not merely spiritual stubbornness but the predictable result of a centuries-long editorial process: the [[Hezekiah Josiah and the Deuteronomist Reforms|Deuteronomistic reform]] and later canon-narrowing **removed the divine-council / pre-existent-heavenly-Messiah tradition** that Jesus was most explicitly drawing on (the "Son of Man" of [[Daniel 7.13-14|Daniel 7]] and the [[The Parables of Enoch Preserve a Preexistent Divine Messiah|Parables of Enoch]]). The communities that *kept* the older, broader texts — the Essenes at Qumran, Galileans, the Ethiopian and Samaritan communities — were disproportionately the ones who recognized Jesus first; those closest to the standardized canon resisted him.

Arnold's authorities are mainstream and non-LDS — [[Richard Elliott Friedman]], [[Israel Finkelstein]] & Silberman, [[Frank Moore Cross]], [[Bruce Metzger]], [[Bart Ehrman]] — and he frames the whole as evidence for the LDS *apostasy* (something was genuinely lost) and *Restoration* (a prophet, not better scholarship, is how God answers an authority problem). He flags the circular-dating debate over the Parables as a live scholarly dispute.

## Summary
- **The problem is textual, not just moral.** Jesus claimed (Luke 24:44, [[Luke 24.44|all "the law, the prophets, and the Psalms"]]) that the whole Hebrew tradition pointed to him; if the most scripturally educated people missed it, either they were uniquely blind or "the text no longer said what it originally said." See [[Jewish Non-Recognition of Jesus Is a Textual Problem Not Only a Spiritual One]].
- **The Deuteronomists flattened the divine council.** A school of scribes under [[Josiahs Reform and the First Temple Make Restoration a Coherent Category|Josiah]] (documented by [[Richard Elliott Friedman|Friedman]], [[Israel Finkelstein|Finkelstein/Silberman]], [[Frank Moore Cross|Cross]]) centralized worship and removed the [[The Plural of Genesis 1 Is the Divine Council Not the Trinity or Royal We|divine-council]] framework in which a pre-existent heavenly deliverer made sense ([[Deuteronomy 32.8-9|Deut 32:8]] "sons of God"). See [[The Deuteronomists Edited Out the Pre-Existent Divine Messiah]].
- **The transmission and canon were human and contested.** No autographs survive; [[Bruce Metzger|Metzger]] titled his foundational work with the word *corruption*; ~400,000 NT variants exist, ~4,000 significant — including the [[1 John 5.7|Johannine Comma]] (1 John 5:7, absent before the 15th c.), the woman caught in adultery, and Mark's long ending. Different traditions canonize different books (66 / 73 / 78–81). See [[The Bible Was Transmitted and Canonized by Fallible Human Process]].
- **The most important excluded text is 1 Enoch's Parables.** [[The Parables of Enoch Preserve a Preexistent Divine Messiah|1 Enoch 37–71]] describes a pre-existent heavenly "Son of Man" who dwells with God before creation, sits on the throne of glory, and judges — the exact title Jesus used of himself ~80 times. Eleven Enoch manuscripts were at Qumran; [[Jude]] quotes it as prophecy; the [[Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church]] never removed it. The argument that the Parables must post-date Jesus is circular (it assumes detailed messianic prophecy is impossible). See [[The Parables of Enoch Preserve a Preexistent Divine Messiah]] and the vault's [[Son of Man - Development of the Title]].
- **The pattern: who kept the old texts recognized Jesus.** Essenes (Enoch, Jubilees, Book of Giants), Galileans, Ethiopian Jews, Samaritans (older Torah) — receptive; the temple-centered establishment reading the standardized canon — resistant. See [[The Communities That Preserved Older Texts Recognized Jesus First]].
- **Translation itself shifts the text.** [[Isaiah 7.14|Isaiah 7:14]]: Hebrew *alma* ("young woman") became Greek *parthenos* ("virgin") in the Septuagint, which Matthew quotes — a theologically driven choice, not the Hebrew original.
- **The LDS payoff: apostasy as gradual narrowing, Restoration as the answer.** 1 Nephi 13's "plain and precious things... taken away" and the Book of Mormon's pre-exilic, pre-Deuteronomistic Christology (produced 1829, before the Dead Sea Scrolls in 1947) describe the loss before the scholarship confirmed it. The fix is an authority problem only a prophet can solve — "you cannot use the canon to establish the canon." See [[The Restoration Answers an Authority Problem Scholarship Cannot Solve]].
