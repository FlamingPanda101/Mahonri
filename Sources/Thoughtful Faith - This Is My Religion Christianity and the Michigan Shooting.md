# Thoughtful Faith - This Is My Religion: Christianity and the Michigan Shooting

An unscripted, "off the cuff" reflection by [[Jacob Hansen]] (Thoughtful Faith) recorded in the aftermath of a shooting at a Latter-day Saint meetinghouse near Grand Blanc, Michigan. Hansen recounts how a GoFundMe for the *shooter's* family — begun by Dave Butler from within the [[Ward Radio]] text thread and amplified by the wider LDS online community — raised over $200,000, and reads donor messages, a Fox News quote, and a lengthy opinion column by the conservative commentator Jonah Goldberg on the strange "you're not Christians" animus that accompanied the tragedy. The video's thesis is that enemy-directed charity is the lived substance of the faith, and that hatred aimed at Latter-day Saints for their beliefs alone is the most reactionary form of bigotry in America. Handle the underlying tragedy soberly: real worshippers were killed, and the fund is for a grieving family that includes a chronically ill child.

## Summary

- **The fundraiser's origin (00:30–01:33).** Dave Butler, a member of the [[Ward Radio]] group text, saw people on X floating the idea of helping the shooter's family — whose son has a chronic illness and whose earlier fundraiser had failed — and started a GoFundMe. The LDS online community shared it; expecting maybe $5,000, they instead raised "well over $200,000."

- **The donors, not the personalities, are the heroes (01:33–03:31).** Hansen insists the credit belongs to small-dollar givers ($10–$50) and reads their messages: prayers for the Samford/Sanford family; a veteran's relative whose great-aunt's helper and friend's husband were killed, writing "No hate, just love and prayers"; another quoting the hymn *Where Can I Turn for Peace*. Organizer Dave Butler told Fox News the children are not guilty and "deserve help like any other innocent person does." This grounds [[The Grand Blanc GoFundMe as Charity Toward an Enemys Family]].

- **The theology of "as I have loved you" (04:01–05:31).** Hansen reads [[John 13.34|John 13:34]]–[[John 13.35|John 13:35]] ("a new commandment... love one another, as I have loved you... by this shall all men know that ye are my disciples") and stresses the *manner* of Christ's love: it is freely given and unearned. He notes that ex-Mormons and people of other faiths also donated, widening the circle beyond the in-group.

- **Goldberg on anti-Mormonism (05:31–10:32).** Reacting to a wave of "you're not Christians" rhetoric, Hansen reads a Jonah Goldberg column at length: extreme anti-Mormonism is "overwhelmingly theological and abstract," hating Latter-day Saints "solely for doctrines and not for what they actually do." Unlike racism, antisemitism, or anti-communism — which at least claim to indict *actions* — "Mormon haters can't point to Mormon terrorism" or crime or welfare fraud. Goldberg recalls the "rankest bigotry" he received over National Review's 2012 Romney endorsement ("heresy, demonic this, Antichrist that... they just believe the wrong thing") and concludes that hating people for belief alone is "premodern, tribal, reactionary, and evil," comparable to the wars of religion and the Albigensian Crusade. Hansen answers, "Amen." This grounds [[Extreme Anti-Mormonism Is Reactionary Hatred for Belief Alone]].

- **Closing hymn (10:32–end).** Hansen plays a hymn ("I would be my brother's keeper... I would learn the healer's heart") as a summary of what he calls the heart of his religion: "loving other people and following the person who loved us first."
