# Ward Radio — The Great Replacement (of Men) is Happening

A Ward Radio episode in which host **[[Cardon Ellis]]**, with **[[Jacob Hansen]]**, **[[Cade Alvie]]**, **[[Baylor Johnson]]**, and **[[Hayden Carroll]]**, reacts to **[[Trent Horn]]**'s *Council of Trent* video "What's Making Young Women So Liberal." The framing borrows the loaded "great replacement" label and applies it to *men* — Horn's thesis that the state is replacing the husband/father in women's lives.

## Summary
- **Substitutional patriarchy (Horn's thesis).** Patriarchy = men are especially suited to *lead* in family relationships (Horn cites [[Ephesians 5.23|Ephesians 5:23]] and Pius XI's *Casti Connubii*). As feminism vilified men (Me Too, the bear-vs-man meme, *The Handmaid's Tale* imagery), a void opened, and *government* stepped into the provider/protector role the husband was meant to fill. See [[Substitutional Patriarchy - The Government Replaces the Husband]].
- **The data points marshaled.** Young women have moved sharply liberal (and men conservative) across the US, UK, Germany, South Korea; abortion was the #1 issue for women under 30; the "Life of Julia" Obama infographic has Uncle Sam as Julia's de facto husband/father; married women are least likely to vote Democrat (45%) vs. never-married women (72%); 87% of abortions are obtained by unmarried women. (Sourced from Horn's video — verify before reuse.)
- **Servant-leadership, not tyranny.** The hosts gloss "presiding"/headship as the husband being "first into the trenches," blazing a trail in full consultation with his wife — not a boss whipping the wagon. Cf. existing [[The Patriarchal Order Is the Order of Fatherhood Not Male Rule]] and the related episode [[Ward Radio - How Masculinity Presides and Feminists Agree]].
- **Family-first for both sexes.** The repeated thesis: nothing fulfills more than family; careers exist to *support* family; this applies equally to men ("screw those guys too" who prioritize career). They distinguish *intentionally* self-prioritizing singles from faithful single members who want marriage — explicitly exempting the latter.
- **LDS application and the retention worry.** Women are leaving the Church at historically high rates (cf. existing [[Younger Women Are Disaffiliating at Higher Rates]]); the hosts fault a "therapizing of the gospel" (self over family) and ask what *program* actually empowers men now that Scouts/programs are truncated — landing on **priesthood keys** as the structural reminder that men must "lead out."
- **The fix and the hope.** "If men will go, women will follow"; the proposed cure is romance/men leading + culturally re-centering family; Gen Z/Alpha are said to be swinging back toward communal values as the "fruits" of self-centered lifestyles become visible (the "TikTok makes them visible" point).
- **Running bit.** Each segment ends with Cardon deadpanning to Jacob, "I just have one question: why do you hate women?"
