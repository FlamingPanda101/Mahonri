# Let's Talk w/ Hayden Carroll — How The Creeds Have Corrupted Christianity

The video recording of a talk [[Hayden Carroll]] gave to a **Ratio Christi** (Evangelical campus apologetics) chapter on a Salt Lake City retreat that visits BYU and Temple Square. Despite the YouTube title's framing around "the creeds," the talk itself is Carroll's prepared case for "why am I a Latter-day Saint and not a Protestant," and it locates the *root* of the creedal disputes (Trinity included) one level deeper — in the doctrine of **Sola Scriptura** and the nature of scripture. He opens with an invitation borrowed from Stephen Covey ("listen to understand rather than to respond") [^1]. This is the delivered version of the same script catalogued at [[Hayden Carroll - Ratio Christi Campus Talk]].

## Summary
- **Plan of Salvation (grace first).** Carroll establishes common ground from "the beginning" — the Fall of Adam and Eve, the Atonement, and salvation by grace through faith manifested in works: "anyone who proclaims that salvation is caused by any human act preaches blasphemy." He stacks Book of Mormon and D&C passages and an [[Dallin H. Oaks]] quote, reading 2 Nephi 25:23 as [[After All We Can Do Means In Spite of All We Can Do|"in spite of all we can do"]] [^2].
- **Rejection of Sola Scriptura** via three problems — interpretation, logic, and canon — which he calls the root of nearly every LDS vs. Protestant disagreement, "the Trinity and most of all other biblical interpretations" included. He grants this is *why* the creedal disputes never resolve: the parties talk past each other on the prior authority question [^3]. See [[The Three Problems with Sola Scriptura - Interpretation Logic Canon]].
- **Positive alternative:** God Himself, not any book, is the ultimate authority, who reveals truth by His Spirit (1 Corinthians 2:9-14, Westminster Confession 1.5); even [[James White]] grounds his assurance of salvation in the Spirit's witness, not the Bible [^4]. See [[God Himself Not a Book Is the Ultimate Authority]] and [[James White Appeals to the Spirit Not the Bible for Assurance]].
- **Not "feelings alone."** Carroll pre-empts the "burning in the bosom" caricature with five converging mediums of revelation — senses, reason, intuition, moral fruits ([[Matthew 7.16-20|"by their fruits"]]), and authority — that "triangulate truth" [^5]. See [[Triangulating Truth - Five Mediums of Revelation]].
- **Why he stays — the keystone.** "The heart cannot embrace what the mind cannot believe," so he grounds his faith in the **Book of Mormon as keystone** (Ezra Taft Benson): if it is an authentic record the Restoration follows; "if it's not, we have no religion." The dictation feat (269,320 words, 6,852 sentences, ~65 days, one draft) is set against Joseph Smith's documented lack of education, witnessed by friend, foe, and family alike [^6]. See [[The Book of Mormon Keystone Argument]], [[The Heart Cannot Embrace What the Mind Cannot Believe]], and [[The Consensus of Joseph Smith's Contemporaries That He Was Uneducated]].
