# Thoughtful Faith — Mormon Beliefs On Abortion Are More Controversial Than You Think

**Channel:** Thoughtful Faith ([[Jacob Hansen]])
**Featured guest:** "Luke" (interlocutor)
**Foils discussed:** [[Dan McClellan]] (anti-feminism narrative, rebutted); the "shout your abortion" movement; clips of LDS apostles

## Summary
- Develops the LDS abortion position as a coherent middle: at conception there is a **sacred human body in development** but not yet full personhood; personhood is "something of a spectrum," which is why abortion grows more serious over time and why the Church's narrow exceptions (rape, incest, life/health of the mother, lethal fetal defect) make sense without conceding elective abortion. This corroborates [[LDS Abortion Position Follows From Pre-Existence Not Cultural Caving]] and [[The IVF Reductio Against Conception-as-Ensoulment]] (both updated).
- The **consistency dilemma**: if abortion is murder "all the way through," then a woman who knowingly takes the morning-after pill should be jailed for life like any murderer — a conclusion even hard pro-lifers won't enforce, which Hansen uses to show their intuitions already track a spectrum. See [[The Morning-After-Pill Prosecution Dilemma]].
- **Politics is a game of inches**: Latter-day Saints who refuse to support any pro-life law lacking a rape exception thereby vote for the worse status quo; one must choose the lesser evil to "move the ball." See [[Abortion Politics Is a Game of Inches Not Purity]].
- **Rebuttal of [[Dan McClellan]]**: McClellan frames the pro-life movement as a reaction to feminism (and, via Jerry Falwell, to losing the racial-segregation fight) — Hansen argues this is "dogma, not data," ignores that little girls are aborted, and conflates the feminist waves. See [[The Pro-Life-as-Anti-Feminism Narrative Is Dogma Not Data]].
- **Diagnosis of the sexual revolution**: abortion's real driver is the elevation of *sexual autonomy* as the defining feature of the self — a "social imaginary" Hansen traces from Rousseau through Freud and Marx (echoing Carl Trueman / Charles Taylor). The gospel locates human worth in relationship to God and covenant, not sexual expression. See [[The Sexual Revolution Genealogy From Rousseau to Freud]] and [[Child Rights Confront Sexual Autonomy as the Highest Value]].
