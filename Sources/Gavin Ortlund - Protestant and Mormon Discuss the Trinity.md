# Gavin Ortlund — Protestant and Mormon Discuss the Trinity (with Jacob Hansen)

A friendly, non-debate dialogue between [[Gavin Ortlund]] (Reformed Baptist, classical-theism Trinitarian, host of *Truth Unites*) and [[Jacob Hansen]] (Latter-day Saint apologist). The stated goal is *clarity*, not denunciation. The conversation never reaches the mechanics of the Trinity itself; instead it circles two prior questions — **how important** the doctrine is (is it a first-rank, salvation-deciding issue?) and **what each man means** by the words "God" and "Lord." It surfaces the deep paradigm gap created by the LDS rejection of *creatio ex nihilo*.

- **Ortlund frames the Trinity as a first-rank, salvation-relevant boundary marker** because *whom* and *how* we worship turns on whether Jesus is eternal, uncreated, and consubstantial with the Father; he repeatedly uses the "Cynthia vs. Sally" analogy — getting the identity of the person you relate to wrong is not a trivial "theology quiz." [^1][^2] See [[Trinity Is a First-Rank Salvation Issue]], [[Identity of Jesus Is Not a Theology Quiz]].
- **Hansen denies a binary heaven/hell sotiology**, recasting salvation as *progression* toward one's divine potential (the acorn-to-oak / kingdoms-of-glory model); on this view rejecting a doctrine merely "stunts growth" rather than damning. [^3][^4] See [[LDS Progression Model vs Binary Heaven and Hell]], [[Acorn and Oak Analogy for Theosis]].
- **Both men press the "explicitness" question**: Hansen argues the Trinity is a later *interpretation* of the scriptural "raw data" (three persons, one God) that Jesus never expounded, so tying salvation to it is unfair; Ortlund counters that all doctrine requires interpretation and that Jesus' own "I am" sayings make belief about him salvation-relevant. [^5][^6] See [[Trinity Is an Interpretation Not Explicit in Scripture]], [[All Doctrine Requires Interpretation Rebuttal]].
- **The lynchpin disagreement is *creatio ex nihilo***: Hansen rejects creation from nothing, holding that intelligences/beings and a fundamental "spiritual realm" have existed eternally and God *organized* pre-existing chaos; this erases the Creator/creature ontological line and makes humans the same *kind* as God. [^7][^8] See [[Creatio Ex Nihilo Is the Lynchpin Difference]], [[Humans Are the Same Kind as God (LDS Ontology)]].
- **Ortlund and Hansen agree Latter-day Saints are not "credal Christians"** and that the gap between their paradigms is "gargantuan," but disagree on whether that gap is damning; both close by affirming friendship and a desire to continue the dialogue on the nature of God and creation. [^9][^10] See [[Are Latter-day Saints Christians]].
