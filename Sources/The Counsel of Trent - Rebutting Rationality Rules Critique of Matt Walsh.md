# The Counsel of Trent — Rebutting Rationality Rules' Critique of Matt Walsh

[[Trent Horn]] defends [[Matt Walsh]]'s definition of a woman as "an adult human female" against [[Steven Woodford]] (Rationality Rules), who argues from intersex conditions that some biological males are women and that sex and gender come apart.

## Summary
- Woodford's case rests on rare intersex conditions (complete androgen insensitivity syndrome — "Jane") and a utility appeal that re-classifying such a person as male would be "cruel"; Horn answers that the difficulties pale beside those of severing gender from sex entirely [^1].
- Hard intersex cases are cases where we are unsure whether *male or female* applies — not evidence that the gendered concepts "man/woman" float free of biology; the "fallacy of the beard" shows boundary cases do not dissolve a category [^2].
- "Looks can be deceiving": appearance is a defeasible guide to reality (analogies to apparent-age, albinism in an ethnically Indian woman); reducing womanhood to correlated external features mistreats masculine-presenting women and flatters caricatures [^3].
