# Informed Saints — Book of Mormon Evidence Hidden in Ancient American Language

The [[Informed Saints]] hosts interview linguist [[Brian Stubbs]], a specialist in the **Uto-Aztecan** language family, on his claim that Uto-Aztecan contains a large, systematic layer of **Semitic (Hebrew/Aramaic) and Egyptian** vocabulary — material he reads as linguistic evidence for [[Book of Mormon]] peoples in ancient America. The conversation walks through cognate sets, the [[Sound Correspondences Distinguish Descent from Coincidence in Language|sound-correspondence method]] that distinguishes genuine descent from chance resemblance, and how the data maps onto the Nephite/Mulekite narrative.

## Summary
- Stubbs reports **~1,657 cognate sets** (about 60% of the ~2,700 in his Uto-Aztecan comparative work) that he traces to Hebrew, Aramaic, and Egyptian, split roughly **700 Semitic / 500 Egyptian** plus a divergent dialect set [^1][^2]. See [[Uto-Aztecan Contains a Large Semitic and Egyptian Lexical Layer]].
- His credibility hook: his *Uto-Aztecan: A Comparative Vocabulary* is a mainstream-praised work (Kenneth Hill, in the *International Journal of American Linguistics*, called it "a monumental contribution raising comparative Uto-Aztecan to a new level"); he concedes up front that the Semitic/Egyptian thesis is **not** the consensus of Uto-Aztecanists [^3].
- The method defense against "Brit-ish = man of the covenant" debunkery: it is a **numbers-plus-pattern** game — hundreds of cognates obeying *consistent* sound shifts (Semitic *b* → Uto-Aztecan *p*; voiced stops devoicing *b/d/g* → *p/t/k*; pharyngeal *ḥ* and *ʿ* rounding vowels; glottal stop → *w*) — and loanwords versus descent are told apart because borrowings don't obey the inherited correspondence set [^4][^5]. See [[Sound Correspondences Distinguish Descent from Coincidence in Language]].
- A distinctive "combination of meanings" argument: Egyptian *hotep* uniquely bundles three unrelated senses (be at peace / set or bury / hair fall out), and the *same* triple cluster survives in Uto-Aztecan — far harder to attribute to chance than a single word [^6].
- The data splits into two sound systems — a dominant set where *b* → *p* and a separate ~quarter where *b* → *kwa* — which Stubbs reads as a **Nephite (≈3/4) and Mulekite (≈1/4) dialect merger**, fitting the Book of Mormon's account of two Semitic groups that could not understand each other [^7][^8]. See [[The Uto-Aztecan B-to-P and B-to-Kwa Split Maps the Nephite-Mulekite Merger]].
- The "lion → mountain lion" semantic shift appears in *both* Hebrew and Egyptian strata, which the hosts tie to the alleged Book of Mormon lion anachronism (a loan-shift onto a New-World species) [^9].
- Epistemic capstone: language families "cannot be fabricated" the way an unearthed inscription can be dismissed as a hoax — thousands of living speakers of related languages are "beyond fabrication," making linguistic ties (if real) among the strongest available evidence [^10]. See [[Language Families Cannot Be Faked the Way Inscriptions Can]].
- Reception name-drops offered as endorsement: [[David H. Kelley]], [[John S. Robertson]], Dirk Elzinga, Royal Skousen, Eric Elliott, and Roger Westcott; the work reached Stubbs via [[John Sorenson]], and Stubbs answers two critics in a 44-point Interpreter rebuttal [^11][^12].
