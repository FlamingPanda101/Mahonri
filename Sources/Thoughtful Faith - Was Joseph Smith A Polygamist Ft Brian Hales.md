# Thoughtful Faith - Was Joseph Smith A Polygamist Ft Brian Hales

[[Jacob Hansen]] interviews [[Brian Hales]], author of the four-volume *Joseph Smith's Polygamy*. Rather than re-debate the documents, they explore a different question: **what a believer must give up if the polygamy deniers are right.** Hales argues that essentially all professional historians agree Joseph practiced plural marriage, and that the online denial movement ("monogamy affirmers") survives only by ignoring everything after 1844. His central thesis is an **implications cascade** — denial is not a free move; it forces the rejection of four further pillars of LDS faith. The conversation also dismantles the "carefully-worded denials" argument and surveys Emma Smith's contradictory testimony.

## Summary

- **The implications cascade (Hales's core argument).** If Joseph was not a polygamist, then a believer must *also* (1) brand the next 3 general Relief Society presidents and 4 church presidents — [[Eliza R. Snow]], [[Brigham Young]], [[John Taylor]], [[Wilford Woodruff]], [[Lorenzo Snow]], et al. — as 50 years of liars; (2) reject [[Doctrine and Covenants 132|D&C 132]] and with it the sealing authority and eternal families; (3) discard the temple endowment (written down by Wilford Woodruff in 1877, the *same* late-recollection source as his polygamy testimony); and (4) stand against the living apostles, who in 2025 affirmed in eight words that "Joseph Smith introduced the practice of plural marriage, not Brigham Young." See [[The Implications Cascade of Polygamy Denial]] and [[The Temple Endowment Rests on the Same Witnesses as Polygamy]].
- **Joseph never issued a blanket denial.** Hansen and Hales review every alleged Joseph "denial" and find them few and qualified — Joseph condemned *spiritual wifery* and *unauthorized* polygamy (the John C. Bennett scandal, Hyram Brown), never plural marriage as such. The "I can only find one [wife]" quip is not a flat denial. Both fit a [[The Confidentiality Model Best Fits the Polygamy Evidence|confidentiality model]]: Joseph practiced plural marriage while seeking to keep it confidential because lives were on the line (Hales: "justified lying is in the scriptures"). See [[Joseph Smith Never Issued a Blanket Denial of Polygamy]].
- **The RLDS were the original deniers — and recanted.** The Reorganized Church (Community of Christ) was founded on the claim Joseph was monogamous, yet its own historians admitted by the 1980s that he practiced plural marriage. Insider [[Ebenezer Robinson]], who learned the doctrine from [[Hyrum Smith]], wrote to [[Joseph Smith III]] that denial "sounds to me like a great lie." See [[The RLDS Church Were the Original Polygamy Deniers and Recanted]].
- **Emma's testimony splits at 1860.** Before the RLDS Church organized (1860), multiple non-pressured witnesses (William McLellin, Lovina Smith Walker, Joseph Lee Robinson/Coolidge) record [[Emma Smith]] acknowledging the practice; her later flat denials are filtered through RLDS missionaries. See [[Emma Smith's Polygamy Testimony Splits Before and After 1860]].
- **The eyewitness asymmetry and the libido-driven-fraud myth.** The list of eyewitnesses (church presidents, plural wives, the [[Doctrine and Covenants 132|D&C 132]] witnesses, even anti-Brigham figures) is enormous; only Sarah Lawrence ever recanted being a wife, and she never alleged a conspiracy. A successful conspiracy this large with *no one* breaking ranks is wildly implausible. Hales adds that the "libido-driven fraud" stereotype collapses on the evidence — and pivots believers back to the Book of Mormon as the "tangible miracle" Joseph could not have produced. See [[The Eyewitness Asymmetry Refutes the Polygamy Conspiracy Theory]].
