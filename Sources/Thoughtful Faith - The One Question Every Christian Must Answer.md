# Thoughtful Faith - The One Question Every Christian Must Answer

A monologue by [[Jacob Hansen]] presenting **the pragmatic argument for the restored gospel** (the church-level analogue of his pragmatic/[[Pascal's Wager|Pascalian]] argument for God's existence). Addressed to Christians — anyone who affirms the resurrection — Hansen argues that once you have faith in Jesus you are forced into "comparative Christianity," and that there are logically only **three options**: (1) the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints is the true church, (2) some *other* church is the true church, or (3) there is no true church. He then presses an internal tension in his critics: many simultaneously say there is no true church *and* that Latter-day Saints are hell-bound for rejecting trinitarian theology.

- Mormonism "presupposes the basic truth that the Jesus of the New Testament rose from the dead"; without that, it is "absurd." So the video is explicitly intramural Christian apologetics.[^1]
- The three-options framing and Hansen's openness to option two (quoting [[Brigham Young]]'s "gather every item of truth ... reject every error") — see [[The Three Options for the Seeker - True Church Other Church or No True Church]].[^2]
- The critic's dilemma: you cannot both hold "there is no true church" and "Mormons go to hell for bad theology," because the latter smuggles in a true church defined by a theological minimum — see [[The No-True-Church Critic Smuggles In a Creedal Minimum]].[^3]
- The LDS resolution: heaven is not binary; "good Christians everywhere" inherit glory, and eternal progression replaces the heaven/hell dichotomy — see [[LDS Progression Model vs Binary Heaven and Hell]] and [[The Burning-Building Argument for Urgency Cuts Against the Critic]].[^4]
