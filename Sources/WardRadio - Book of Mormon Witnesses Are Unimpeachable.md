# Ward Radio — Book of Mormon Witnesses Are Unimpeachable

A Ward Radio episode in which **[[Jonah Barnes]]** gives a "free preview" of an LDS Truth Claims course (credited to **[[Brett McDonald]]**) arguing that the Book of Mormon / golden-plate witnesses constitute the single strongest, most "airtight" evidence for Joseph Smith's prophethood. The format is a teacher/student dialogue with a skeptical co-host.

## Summary
- The witnesses split into **natural** (physical contact with the plates) and **supernatural** (angelic/divine encounters); the natural witnesses prove the plates existed, while the supernatural witnesses prove their divine origin, since "fabricated metal plates can't make people see visions" [^1].
- Beyond the official 11, there are **at least 11 additional informal witnesses** — Josiah Stowell, Catherine Smith, William Smith, Emma Smith, Mary Whitmer, Luke Johnson, Harrison Burgess and others — who encountered the plates by happenstance, not by staging [^2].
- McDonald's **BICC framework** tests each witness for **B**ias/conspiracy, **I**nconsistent statement, **C**haracter, and **C**ompetency; the episode runs all 22 witnesses through it and concludes none can be impeached [^3].
- Critics' two fallback theories are **metallurgical fraud** (fake plates) and **mass hallucination/hypnosis**; the episode contends the supernatural witnesses defeat the first and the science/history of hypnotism defeats the second [^4].
- Even hostile scholars **[[Fawn Brodie]]** and **[[Dan Vogel]]** concede the witnesses never recanted and so are forced into the "Joseph as master hypnotist" thesis, which the episode treats as a self-refuting admission of the case's strength [^5].
