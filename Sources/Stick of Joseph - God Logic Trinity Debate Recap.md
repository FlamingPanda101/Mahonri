# Stick of Joseph — God Logic Trinity Debate Recap

A *Stick of Joseph* livestream hosted by [[Cardon Ellis]] with [[Jacob Hansen]] (Thoughtful Faith), [[Colton Miller]], and [[Chandler Henry|Chandler "Chuck" Henry]] (Restored Truth), later joined by [[Cardon Ellis|Cardon]]. The panel recaps Hansen's debate at the "God Bless" conference against Protestant apologist [[Avery (God Logic)|Avery ("God Logic")]] on the resolution **"Is the biblical God a Trinity?"**, and responds clip-by-clip to a rebuttal video by Calvinist YouTuber [[Go With Jordan]] titled *"God Logic DEFEATED Jacob Hansen on the Trinity."*

## Summary
- The debate is framed around a **"three whos and one what"** taxonomy: both sides agree there are three persons and one "God," and the entire dispute reduces to whether that one "what" is a single *being* (Trinity) or a single *entity / Godhead* (LDS) [^1]. Hansen contends the burden is on the Trinitarian to show from scripture that "one God" means "one being," and that Avery never did so [^2]. See [[Three Whos and One What Framework]], [[The Court Analogy for the Godhead]].
- Hansen argues classical Trinitarianism is incoherent and that Avery repeatedly **conceded heresy** (denying divine impassibility, affirming two mutually-exclusive natures in one person) to applause from an audience that didn't recognize it — the LDS point being precisely that *classical theism cannot be reconciled with the Bible* [^3]. See [[Divine Impassibility Is Classical-Theist Heresy]], [[Natures Do Not Act Only Persons Do]], [[Classical Theism Cannot Be Reconciled With the Bible]].
- A linguistic throughline: the creeds read the Hebrew text through **Greek metaphysical "glasses"** (ontology, consubstantiality, *homoousios*) that did not exist in Second Temple Judaism; "oneness" language (the Shema, "one flesh," John 17) signals covenant unity, not numerical identity of being [^4]. See [[Oneness Means Unity Not Numerical Identity of Being]], [[Echad and the Shema as a Loyalty Pledge]], [[Greek Categories Are Anachronistic to the Hebrew Bible]].
- The whole New Testament is read as a **subordinationist** text: the Son receives authority, prays to, and is ultimately "subjected to" the Father ([[1 Corinthians 15.24-28|1 Cor 15:24-28]], [[1 Corinthians 11.3|1 Cor 11:3]], [[Mark 13.32|Mark 13:32]]), explained by an *ontological equality with relational subordination* model and the **divine-agency / divine-name** motif [^5]. See [[Subordination Within the New Testament]], [[Ontological Equality With Relational Subordination]], [[Divine Agency and the Divine-Name Bearer]].
- The panel also models **charity toward the creeds**: [[Chandler Henry|Chandler]] argues the Fathers' Trinity was a *good-faith* effort to make sense of scripture under persecution, paralleled to the modern redefinition of "gender" vs "sex" and to defenses of Joseph Smith's record-keeping [^6]. See [[Trinity Was a Good-Faith Effort Not Dishonesty]], [[Persons-and-Beings Were Redefined Like Gender and Sex]]. Book-of-Mormon counters to a "Modalist proof-text" close the loop: [[Mosiah 15 Father and Son Explained]].
