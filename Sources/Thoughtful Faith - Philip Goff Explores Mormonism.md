# Thoughtful Faith - Philip Goff Explores Mormonism

[[Jacob Hansen]] (Thoughtful Faith) interviews the philosopher of mind [[Philip Goff]] (Durham University) to map where Goff's **limited-God / panpsychist** views overlap with — and diverge from — Latter-day Saint theology. Hansen's framing premise is that Goff arrived *philosophically* (via fine-tuning, the problem of evil, and consciousness) at two positions that closely track the Restoration: a **God limited in power** and a **mind-suffused cosmos** resembling the LDS doctrine of intelligences.[^1] The conversation is unusually consonant on the diagnosis (both agree that solving the problem of evil *requires* limiting God) and sharply divided on the metaphysics underneath it — creation *ex nihilo*, whether the ultimate God is a person, and the bodily resurrection. This source is the LDS-facing companion to the round-4 [[Reasonable Faith - Does God Have Limited Power Craig vs Goff|Craig-vs-Goff]] build: where Craig attacks Goff's limited God, Hansen *welcomes* it and probes how far the kinship runs.

## Participants
- [[Jacob Hansen]] (Latter-day Saint apologist, Thoughtful Faith — host)
- [[Philip Goff]] (philosopher of mind, panpsychist, "heretical Christian"; debated [[William Lane Craig]] and appeared on [[Alex OConnor|Alex O'Connor]]'s show)

## Summary
- **Why Goff left atheism — fine-tuning and consciousness.** Goff recounts being a teenage atheist who returned to theism because both sides "get something wrong": atheism cannot explain the **fine-tuning of physics for life**, and traditional theism cannot explain suffering. He defends fine-tuning with John Leslie's **firing-squad analogy** (the marksmen who keep missing demand an explanation) and treats epistemic probability as the right measure against the "it had to be that way" / puddle rebuttal.[^2] On consciousness, he flags the *underexplored* question of **why consciousness evolved at all** when natural selection only "cares about behavior."[^3] The vault already holds these moves — see [[The Anthropic Principle and the Firing-Squad Reply]] and [[The Ice Sculpture Rebuttal to the Sentient Puddle]].
- **Goff's positive position — a God limited in power.** A loving all-powerful God "wouldn't create a universe like this... through such a horrific torturous process like natural selection." Goff's "wonderfully simple" hypothesis is **one necessary being** who is the source of all existence but for whom it is a *primitive fact* that he "can do some things and can't do others" — no base reality required. He folds in Eastern-Orthodox **theosis** (we "become gods with a small G") and a non-standard, *visionary* resurrection. See [[A Constrained God Solves the Problem of Evil Better Than Creedal Christianity]], [[God's Limited Power Restricts the Resurrection to a Visionary Ascension (Goff)]], and [[Theosis as Likeness and Union So Far as Possible]].
- **Hansen's LDS frame — organize, don't create from nothing.** Hansen lays out the Restoration alternative: we have *all* always existed as **intelligences** (Goff: "is that consciousness?" — Hansen: essentially yes); God is "the greatest amongst all intelligences" who **organizes chaos into a celestial order** rather than creating *ex nihilo*; the painter does not make the paint. He grounds this in **opposition in all things** ([[2 Nephi 2.11|2 Nephi 2]]), read aloud at length, and in a **fortunate-fall** theodicy of passing through a "realm of injustice." See [[Genesis 1 Creation Is Ordering and Separation Not Ex Nihilo]], [[Lehi's Theodicy - Opposition in All Things]], and the new [[Mortal Life as a Realm of Injustice We Pass Through]].
- **The parsimony face-off — primitive fact vs. base reality.** The core disagreement is *where the brute fact lives*. Goff: God's limits are a primitive fact *about God's own nature*, and one-necessary-being is simpler than the LDS many-necessary-beings + uncreated substrate. Hansen: a God whose own nature inexplicably bars him from a better world has a "deeply flawed nature," and locating the constraint in eternal reality is the more coherent move. See the new [[A Primitive Limit in God's Nature vs an External Eternal Constraint (Goff vs LDS)]].
- **The crucifixion dilemma for divine limitation.** Hansen's sharpest argument: *any* answer the traditional Christian gives to "why the cross — why doesn't God snap his fingers?" limits God. Either God *wanted* Jesus to suffer when other means existed (then "God is a perverse psycho") or the cross was "the only way" (then God is limited). Goff agrees they are "of one mind" that solving evil requires limiting God. See the new [[The Crucifixion Dilemma - Any Answer Limits God]].
- **Where they split — is the ultimate God a person?** Goff is drawn to the **mystical** traditions and holds the *essence* of God (the ultimate source) is "beyond understanding" and **not a mind**; only Jesus, as the union of that reality with the physical cosmos, is a mind. Pressed, he concedes the striking line that **God is the only thing that is *not* consciousness** (since for a panpsychist all physical reality *is* consciousness). Hansen insists God the Father is a *person* known through the risen Christ. See the new [[Goff - The Father Is Not a Mind, God Is the Only Non-Conscious Thing]] and [[A Reality More Fundamental Than God Just Is God]].
- **Resurrection and theosis — sharp disagreement, real kinship.** Goff denies a bodily resurrection (Easter = the first experience of theosis, resurrection identified with ascension), where Hansen makes **materiality** central (a glorified but real body). Yet both affirm theosis; the LDS distinctive is that human nature *fully realized* **is** the divine nature (acorn → oak, *no unbridgeable ontological gap*), against the Orthodox essence/energies gulf. See [[Acorn and Oak Analogy for Theosis]], [[Humans Are the Same Kind as God (LDS Ontology)]], and [[The Essence-Energies Distinction Distinguishes Orthodoxy From Pantheism]].
- **Close — experience and community.** Both land on **religious/mystical experience** as the decisive ground of faith (Hansen: "more like I've heard some amazing music than a mathematical formula"; Goff: "all knowledge is rooted in a decision to trust certain experiences") and on the value of *religious community* binding the spiritual life to the world.[^14]
