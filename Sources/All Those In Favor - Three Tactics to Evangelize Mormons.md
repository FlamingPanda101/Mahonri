The LDS reaction channel **All Those In Favor** — hosts [[Baylor Johnson (Ward Radio)|Baylor]] and [[Cade (All Those In Favor)|Cade]] — work through three recent videos that try to evangelize Latter-day Saints: a combative segment by [[Mark Driscoll]] ("Exposing the Lies of the Mormon Cult"), a Galatians-1 sermon clip by [[Sean McDowell]], and a notably charitable correction video by a young Roman Catholic, **Noah** (Instagram "Jesus and whatnot"). The episode is framed as a comparison: the two Evangelicals recycle the same 1830s talking points (the "[[Galatians 1.8|another gospel]]" / angel attack, the grace-vs-works charge, the Muhammad comparison), while the Catholic actually read current LDS apologetics and publicly retracted his earlier mistakes. The hosts use that contrast to argue the Evangelical project has become a debate sport rather than a salvation effort — see [[Faith Has Become a Debate Sport Not a Salvation Effort]].

## Summary

**The "different gospel" / Galatians 1:8 attack.** Both Driscoll and McDowell lead with [[Galatians 1.8|Galatians 1:8]] ("though an angel from heaven preach another gospel… let him be accursed"). The hosts answer with Paul's own definition of the gospel in [[1 Corinthians 15.1-4|1 Corinthians 15:1-4]] (Christ died, was buried, rose the third day) and note the Book of Mormon and [[Joseph Smith]] affirm exactly that — see [[Galatians 1-8 Latter-day Saints Preach Pauls Gospel]] and [[Definition of Gospel]]. The running gag: when Smith's words ("the fundamental principles of our religion are the testimony of the apostles… that he died, was buried, and rose again") are mistaken for Peter's, "he sounds suspiciously like an apostle."[^1] They argue the verse's real attraction for critics is the *angel* (Moroni), not the gospel — to which they reply that the angel Gabriel announced Christ's birth, "the same angel that started Islam."[^2]

**The grace-vs-works tactic and the synergism boomerang.** McDowell quotes [[Ephesians 2.8-9|Ephesians 2:8-9]] against [[2 Nephi 25.23|2 Nephi 25:23]] ("by grace… after all that we can do"). The hosts give two replies: first, the established LDS reading that "after all we can do" means *in spite of* all we can do (see [[After All We Can Do Means In Spite of All We Can Do]], [[LDS Salvation Is by Grace Not Earned by Works]]); second, the historical boomerang — the synergism Latter-day Saints share with Catholicism and Eastern Orthodoxy is the *ancient* view, whereas McDowell's solifidian forensic justification is the genuine post-Reformation novelty, so the "beware changes to the gospel" warning rebounds on him (see [[Synergism Is Ancient and Sola Fide Is the Real Innovation]]).

**The bait-and-switch caricature.** Driscoll's frame — Latter-day Saints lure converts with Christ-talk, then after baptism reveal the "real" religion of works and Joseph Smith — is dismantled as a fantasy no missionary recognizes: members love talking about their distinctives (the [[Book of Abraham]], the [[Book of Moses]], premortality), so there is nothing to hide. See [[The Convert-Then-Reveal Bait-and-Switch Caricature]].

**The Muhammad / "rebrand" tropes.** The reflexive comparison to Muhammad (and Jehovah's Witnesses) is read as an attempt to emotionally charge the case by association rather than argue it — compare [[Joseph-Muhammad Comparison as Islamophobic Smear]]. Driscoll's "rebrand" charge is answered with the observation that anti-Mormon books were attacking the same Christ-belief long before any PR pivot (see [[Dated Anti-Mormon Books Refute the Rebranding Charge]]), and that the Book of Mormon "with Jesus on every page" has been in print since 1830.[^3]

**Noah the Catholic and the exaltation caricature.** The third video is praised as the only public retraction the hosts have seen: Noah corrects his earlier claims, including the "Mormons believe they get their own planets" line, which he relabels a cartoonish misrepresentation of *exaltationist theosis* — see [[Gods of Their Own Planets Is a Caricature of Exaltationist Theosis]]. He also concedes the LDS reading of [[Matthew 16.18|Matthew 16:18]] (the rock is revelation) and frames the [[Great Apostasy Is Not a Scriptural Term in LDS Standard Works|great apostasy]] charitably. The hosts use Noah as the foil for their thesis about [[Faith Has Become a Debate Sport Not a Salvation Effort|motivation]].

**The scholarship gap.** Twice the hosts cite the 1997 evangelical assessment by [[Carl Mosser]] and [[Paul Owen]], *Mormonism and the Problem of Loss: Are We Losing the Battle? And Do We Not Know It?* — hostile authors conceding that "the sophistication and erudition of LDS apologetics has risen considerably while evangelical responses have not."[^4] McDowell and Driscoll are faulted for still leaning on discredited counter-cult sources like [[Ed Decker]] and [[Norman Geisler]] rather than answering current LDS scholarship.[^5]
