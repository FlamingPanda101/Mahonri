# Ward Radio — New Research Exposes the Truth About America's 'Nones' (with Jonah Barnes)

A Ward Radio episode in which host **[[Cardon Ellis]]** and **[[Jonah Barnes]]** report on political scientist **[[Ryan Burge]]**'s analysis (graphsaboutreligion.com) of the religiously unaffiliated — the "Nones" — and his AI-clustered four-part typology, which they map onto online ex-Mormon culture.

## Summary
- **The Nones are not one group — Burge finds four.** From a survey of ~12,000 (≈25% "none"), Burge used AI to cluster the unaffiliated into four roughly equal types: **NINOs** (None In Name Only, ~21% — still pray/attend), **SBNRs** (Spiritual But Not Religious, ~36% — crystals/yoga, skeptical of religion), **Duns** (done with and angry at religion), and **Zealous Atheists** (the most hostile). See [[Ryan Burges Four-Part Typology of the Nones]].[^1]
- **NINOs are surprisingly religious.** Over half pray daily and many "believe in God without a doubt," yet decline a label. The hosts suggest non-denominational Christianity has itself become a culture/"franchise" ("generalized therapeutic deism with a Bible").[^2]
- **Clergy unbelief.** Burge's data on mainline clergy: only ~70% report a "certain belief in God" — the hosts read this as evidence of institutional hollowing.[^3]
- **Zealous Atheists = "Reddit atheists" and the ex-Mormon subreddit.** Three-quarters tried to talk someone out of their religion in the past year, yet ~17% still attend a service and pray "a little" — because, Burge writes, "they still interact with religion enough to remind themselves why they dislike it." The hosts identify this with r/exmormon scouring General Conference and lesson manuals. See [[Zealous Ex-Mormon Atheists Engage the Church More Than Members Do]].[^4]
- **LDS as the new poster-child religion.** They argue the Church of Jesus Christ has become the "quintessential" target for run-of-the-mill atheists (cf. the film *Heretic*), where Catholics filled that role in the 1980s — a status with "pitfalls."[^5]

## Notable framing / rhetoric
- "Which 'none' is your aunt?" running bit (the Thanksgiving relative).
- Self-deprecating asides; sponsor reads (Scripture Notes). Praise for Burge's accessible data work and a wish to get him on the show.
- Anecdotal real-time "audit" of r/exmormon to illustrate the engagement-driven-by-grievance pattern.
