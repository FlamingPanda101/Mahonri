# Ward Radio — How Richard Bushman Lost His Faith and Got It Back

Host [[Cardon Ellis]] (with producer Luke Hansen) travels to New York to interview
[[Richard Bushman]] — emeritus Columbia historian, author of *Rough Stone Rolling* and *Joseph
Smith's Gold Plates*, and a stake patriarch — about how he holds together "two institutions"
(elite academia and faith), his epistemology, and the seer-stone translation controversy.

## Summary
- **The framing question:** Bushman uniquely stands "in both fields" — decorated Ivy-League
  historian *and* stake patriarch ("the closest thing we have to a shaman"). The hosts ask how he
  reconciles the supposed faith-vs-reason divide.[^1]
- **Epistemology of goodness / "I believe, not I know":** Bushman deliberately avoids "I know"
  ("know implies I have evidence that will convince *you*"); he says "I believe," and tests faith by
  *function* — a pragmatic "epistemology of goodness" (how do we decide something is truly good and
  adhere to it). Testimony should describe *how it works*, not assert private certainty. See
  [[Bushman's Epistemology of Goodness and I Believe Not I Know]].[^2]
- **Re-narrating his "loss":** he doubts he truly lost his testimony (he served a mission); his real
  problem was being unable to *explain* his belief respectably among Harvard peers. Recovery was the
  lifelong, piecemeal work of articulation.[^3]
- **The new atheists:** "clever and smarty" but "shallow," failing to engage suffering, meaning, and
  the yearning to be good; argument can neither prove nor disprove religion.[^4]
- **Enchantment vs. redemption:** he loves "enchantment" (inexplicable forces that give courage) but
  what *keeps* him is **redemption** — the yearning "to be better people than we are." His answer to a
  dean's "how can you believe?": "When I live the Mormon way, I'm the kind of man I want to be."[^5]
- **Translation method — both instruments:** Bushman holds Joseph used **both** the interpreters
  (Urim and Thummim) *and* a separate seer stone, with a "smooth curve" from earlier seer-stone use;
  the post-116-pages phase relied more on the seer stone. The official Church position (last ~15 years)
  follows strong eyewitness testimony (Emma, Whitmer, Harris); [[Jonathan Neville]] and Jim Lucas
  contest it. See [[Joseph Smith Used Both the Interpreters and a Seer Stone]].[^6]
