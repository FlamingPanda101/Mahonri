[[Hayden Carroll]] responds to Catholic Answers apologist [[Joe Heschmeyer]]'s video comparing [[Joseph Smith]] of Mormonism with [[Muhammad]] of Islam (featuring clips from Heschmeyer's *Shameless Popery* channel). Heschmeyer's target is what he calls the **literary argument** — "the human authors were too uneducated to have produced this on their own" — which Muslims press for the Quran and Latter-day Saints press for the Book of Mormon. His strategy is a **parity / reductio**: because the two books reach contradictory conclusions, an argument form that would prove both divine must be invalid. Carroll's reply is that the parallel collapses once you stop comparing the *shape* of the arguments and compare the *origin evidence* — and that several of Heschmeyer's moves rebound on his own Roman Catholic commitments.

## Summary
The bulk of the Heschmeyer↔LDS exchange (the "proves too much" reversal, the Book of Mormon vs Quran evidence-weight comparison, the dictation feat, the Galatians 1:8 angle, and the "magic trick" framing) is already developed in existing vault notes and is wikilinked rather than re-derived. This note captures the two contributions distinctive to *this* video — both turning on **source-handling and the documentary record** — plus the connective tissue.

- **Heschmeyer cherry-picks the Islamic tradition.** He leans on tradition to make Muhammad the "author" of the Quran while rejecting the adjacent tradition that Muhammad was illiterate (calling illiteracy "a motivated story"). Carroll asks by what criterion he keeps the part he needs and drops the rest, and hands him a cleaner argument: there is *no contemporary evidence* Muhammad dictated today's Quran. "You obviously don't trust [the tradition]; otherwise you'd be a Muslim." See [[Heschmeyer Selectively Trusts the Islamic Tradition He Needs]].
- **The Quran's origin lacks contemporary documentation.** Islamic tradition (Gabriel dictating over ~23 years) is established by records ~two centuries after Muhammad's death. Carroll recruits University of Chicago Islamicist [[Fred Donner]], who argues the "traditional Islamic origins narrative... is far less secure than we once thought" — sources are 8th–10th-century literary compilations resting on "almost no actual documentation." Carroll then turns this on Rome: a Catholic who discounts the Quran for missing documentation cannot rest on the supremacy of Rome, papal infallibility, or the Marian dogmas, which "have no historical evidence to support them." See [[The Quran Has No Contemporary Records Only Sources Centuries Later]].
- **The disanalogy with the Book of Mormon.** Carroll insists the two origins are "nothing at all alike": contemporary records show Joseph dictated ~269,000 words in ~65 working days in a single draft; stylometry shows ~20 distinct authorial voices, none bearing Joseph's fingerprint (he credits Marilyn Lynford's work); Hebraisms; ~20 witnesses to the plates, three sharing visions of heavenly beings; and Book of Mormon archaeology where the share of confirmed anachronism-claims rose from <7% to ~75%+ (he credits [[Matt Roper]]). The fuller versions live in [[Muhammad and Joseph Smith Are Apples to Oranges]], [[The Quran Parallels the Doctrine and Covenants Not the Book of Mormon]], and [[The Minimal Facts Method Applied to the Gold Plates]].
- **The documented hostile record on Joseph's intellect.** Carroll stacks contemporary critics and intimates — Rev. John A. Clark ("dull and utterly destitute of genius"), [[Lucy Mack Smith]] ("less inclined to the study of books than any child we had"), [[Isaac Hale]] ("not very well educated"), [[David Whitmer]] ("quite illiterate, knew nothing of writing composition"), Orsamus Turner ("less than the ordinary intellect"), and an 1831 newspaper ("mental powers... extremely limited") — to deny Heschmeyer a "secret genius" Joseph. The contrary naturalistic foothold (Lucy's evening recitals) is rebutted at [[Lucy Mack Smiths Evening Recitals Do Not Establish Pre-Composition]].
- **The "growth by the sword" and Nauvoo Legion remark.** Carroll flags as egregious Heschmeyer's comparison of the Nauvoo Legion's effect to the Islamic Caliphate, noting the Legion carried out no killings or conversions by the sword — and that the "barbaric history" charge fits the Roman church Heschmeyer belongs to.
- **Paul gets a pass.** Carroll notes both founders "claimed to be visited by a heavenly being and got new revelation from God" — which "sounds a lot like Paul," yet Paul is exempt "because reasons." On the angelic-revelation / Galatians 1:8 test see [[Galatians 1-8 - Angel Preaching Another Gospel]] and [[Galatians 1.8]].
- **Heschmeyer's real argument is the Catholic syllogism.** Carroll closes by quoting [[Trent Horn]]: "If Catholicism is true, the Book of Mormon is not divinely inspired; Catholicism is true; so the Book of Mormon is not divinely inspired." The Muhammad parallel is therefore decoration on a prior commitment, not the load-bearing case — cf. [[Could God vs Would God on the Book of Mormon]] and the "proves too much" reply in [[Joe Heschmeyers Quran Parallel Proves Too Much Against the Resurrection]].
