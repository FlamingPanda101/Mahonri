# Lets Talk w Hayden Carroll - Atheists Say We Have No Free Will

**Channel:** Let's Talk w/ Hayden Carroll | **Guest interlocutors:** Sid (19) and Raven, two atheist students
**Format:** Unscripted campus conversation | **Tradition:** [[Hayden Carroll|LDS street apologetics]]

## Summary

[[Hayden Carroll]] sits down with two atheist students and runs his two
"strongest arguments for theism" — the [[Laundry Dryer Analogy for Design|fine-tuning argument]]
(dials with ~10 billion settings each; folded laundry as the design intuition)
and the consciousness argument (a non-conscious particle cannot produce
consciousness, so the consistent atheist must call consciousness an illusion, as
[[Daniel Dennett]] does). From there he develops his familiar chain: atheism
entails no free will, and no free will entails no objective purpose, terminating
in nihilism — buttressed by quote cards from [[Daniel Dennett]], Bertrand
Russell, Jean-Paul Sartre, and [[Friedrich Nietzsche]].

What makes this conversation distinct from Carroll's other appearances is the
**quality of the atheist pushback**. Sid and Raven concede determinism — they
grant they have no [[Free Will - Atheist Determinism vs LDS Co-Eternality|libertarian free will]]
and accept the "we are dominoes" framing — but they refuse the next step. They
argue at length that *no free will does not entail no purpose*: purpose, they
say, is **function and impact on the world**, which a determined domino still
has. This forces Carroll to defend the premise that purpose *necessitates*
agency and a conscious observer — the live dialectic this source uniquely
documents (see [[No Free Will Does Not Entail No Purpose - Carroll's Reply]]).

The back half pivots to LDS metaphysics once Carroll asks the students to "grant
theism." He denies [[Creation Ex Nihilo Leads to Determinism|creation ex nihilo]]
(reading Hebrew *bara* as "organize"), affirms that souls/intelligences are
[[Intelligence Is Uncreated and Eternally Has Agency|eternal and uncreated]],
and — unusually — self-identifies as a **panpsychist**, holding that
consciousness is in everything and that pre-mortal intelligences possessed
*unequal* capacities, with those crossing a threshold becoming human (see
[[LDS Panpsychism and the Intelligence Threshold]]). He uses
[[LDS Premortal Consent Removes Gods Culpability|pre-mortal consent]] and
"judged according to the knowledge we have" to deflect the students' final
objection — that biology and trauma (a brain-injured 12-year-old turning
violent) determine who is "good" or "bad," so equal judgment is unfair.
