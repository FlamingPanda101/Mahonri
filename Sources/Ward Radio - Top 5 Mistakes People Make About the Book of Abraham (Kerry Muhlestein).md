# Ward Radio - Top 5 MISTAKES People Make About the Book of Abraham (with Kerry Muhlestein)

**Channel:** Ward Radio · **Host:** [[Cardon Ellis]] · **Guest:** [[Kerry Muhlestein]]
**URL:** https://youtu.be/903kVobBwTI

## Summary
- The five mistakes are largely **method** errors: (1) making unexamined assumptions and treating them as facts; (2) over-simplifying a 3,000-year, symbol-laden, multivalent Egyptian record; (3) failing to question sound bites; (4) pursuing answers half-heartedly by reading only one side and skipping the text; (5) believing the sophistry that revelation is not a valid source of knowledge. See [[Questioning Assumptions Is the First Defense of the Book of Abraham]].
- Muhlestein insists everyone has a faith-choice bias and that the claim of pure objectivity is itself the least objective posture — "cynicism is not intelligence." See [[Everyone Has a Faith-Choice Bias and Admitting It Is More Objective]].
- Defends **revelation as a viable epistemology** via Alma 32 (repeatable experiment on the word) and his UCLA knee story (felt knowledge the instruments could not detect, later vindicated by surgery). See [[Revelation Is a Valid Source of Knowledge]].
