# The Stick of Joseph - Nephi Encoded the Seven Hebrew Feasts into the Book of Mormon

Hosts [[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)|Dave Butler]] and Hayden interview novelist [[Christina Dymock]], who claims that the outline of **First Nephi** is the cycle of the **seven Hebrew feasts** instituted in [[Leviticus 23]] — Trumpets, the Days of Repentance, the Day of Atonement, Tabernacles, the Eighth Day, Passover, and the counted weeks to Pentecost — each feast-section opening with the Lord speaking to [[Lehi]] (or, finally, to [[Nephi]]) and reproducing that feast's distinctive liturgy, ritual objects, and themes. Dymock arrived at the pattern by "reverse-outlining" 1 Nephi the way she dissects novels, and reports that no prior commentator had published it. Butler layers in academic context — [[Margaret Barker]]'s temple reconstruction, the Wellhausen dating debate, the Enoch/Jubilees solar-vs-lunar calendar disputes — turning the episode into a running test of whether Nephi demonstrably knows pre-exilic festal liturgy. The hosts present the fit as one more cumulative evidence that the [[Book of Mormon]] was written by someone immersed in the law of Moses, not fabricated by [[Joseph Smith]].

## Summary

- **The thesis and its method.** As a novelist, Dymock reverse-outlines other authors' books to expose their skeletons; doing this to 1 Nephi she found its outline *is* the festival year of [[Leviticus 23]]. The diagnostic key: each new feast-section begins with the Lord giving Lehi a vision or speaking to him — "and my father had a vision" signals a new feast — patterned on Leviticus 23's repeated "and the LORD spake unto Moses" [03:02], [07:30], [08:01]. See [[Nephi Encoded the Seven Hebrew Feasts as the Outline of First Nephi]].
- **The interposition puzzle.** Although 1 Nephi 1 is set at the *commencement* of Zedekiah's reign (which [[Don Bradley]] reads as Passover season), the outline *opens* with the **fall** feasts — Trumpets/Yom Teruah — not Passover. Dymock argues Nephi did this deliberately: Christ's first coming fulfilled the three spring feasts, so the still-future fall feasts are the ones pointing to the Second Coming; she thinks Nephi "has seen our day." This interleaving is also why, she says, nobody caught the pattern before [12:00], [12:30]. See [[Nephi Interposes the Fall Feasts Before Passover to Signal the Second Coming]].
- **Trumpets / Yom Teruah (1 Nephi 1).** The shofar's "wake-up" blast = the prophets crying repentance; an article counts **nine** prophets in Jerusalem at Lehi's time (Jeremiah, Habakkuk, etc.), matching the nine-blast shofar call. The enthroned King on His throne amid angels (1 Ne 1:8) = the coronation of God as King. The book Lehi is given and reads (1 Ne 1:11) = the **middle book** of judgment opened at Rosh Hashanah, granting ten days to repent — which is why Lehi dwells on God's *mercy* [19:00], [20:00], [23:00], [24:00].
- **The Days of Repentance (the "1.5" feast).** The ten days of turning between Trumpets and Atonement; Nephi enacts his own turning by seeking and obtaining his own witness (1 Ne 2), then turns his family — Sam accepts, Laman and Lemuel refuse [26:00], [31:00].
- **Day of Atonement / Yom Kippur (1 Nephi 4).** The "key that unlocked it": the otherwise-anomalous **casting of lots** over Laman = the two identical goats. Laman, on whom the lot falls, is accused as a thief (a sin he did not commit — Nephi took the plates) and is driven into the wilderness like the **Azazel** goat; Nephi, who dons Laban's identity, plays the high priest who identifies with the sacrifice [31:32], [33:30], [34:01]. See [[Laban and Nephi Enact the Two Goats of the Day of Atonement]]. Butler adds that Nephi's evident knowledge of the Day of Atonement is an argument *against* Wellhausen's late-P dating [35:01]. See [[The Day of Atonement Is Pre-Exilic Because Nephi Knows It]].
- **Tabernacles / Sukkot (Lehi's dream).** Sukkot's ritual objects — a *stick* (the **lulav** of bound branches) and a *fruit* (the **etrog**/citron) — appear in the tree-of-life vision; Nephi ironizes them, turning the flimsy waved branch into the **iron rod** and the foundationless **great and spacious building** into the rickety sukkah, preaching that safety is in the Lord, not the structure — the theme of Sukkot [36:00], [38:00], [41:01].
- **The Eighth Day / Shemini Atzeret (the Liahona).** The extra intimate day after Sukkot, with its prayers for rain, maps to the **Liahona** as God's provision; the broken-bow episode and the Liahona are one story, and Nephi making a new bow = "planting a seed" so the rain (game) can come — the festal theme that the righteous king's people are granted rain [45:01], [47:00]. Butler floats the **bow = rainbow/keset** covenant sign (Genesis 9) [49:30].
- **Passover (the ship / ocean crossing).** The switch to the spring feasts; the Lord now speaks to **Nephi** (not Lehi) for the first time, telling him to build a ship/ark — Nephi becomes prophet, priest, and king. Bondage and deliverance, judgment by the sea (Egyptians drowned, Israel passes over), the binding of Nephi (a Passover-sacrifice / Akedah echo), and a Yizkor nod in his parents nearly going to a watery grave [51:30], [53:32], [58:30], [61:01].
- **First Fruits + the counted weeks to Pentecost/Shavuot (the Isaiah chapters).** Planting the Jerusalem seeds (1 Ne 18:24) = First Fruits; the Gathering-of-Israel/harvest themes of the Isaiah chapters = Shavuot. The headache: the Shavuot nod seemed thin — until Dymock realized Nephi encodes the **Omer "days of counting"** by listing items in groups of **three and four (=7)** throughout the Isaiah chapters (prophets named 7×, plates 14×), forcing the reader to *count* [63:00], [66:31], [67:00]. See [[Nephi Encodes the Days of Counting as Groups of Three and Four in the Isaiah Chapters]].
- **Why Nephi did it.** Butler and Dymock propose the feasts were "preparatory to the temple," so Nephi writes on four layers (primary story / gospel principles / gospel doctrine = the feasts / temple), possibly as **temple dramas** acted out at each festival; the festal year wraps his new-world people in covenant identity as the broken-but-still-Israel branch, and may even encode a **narrative calendar** to inculcate calendrical knowledge [70:30], [72:00], [75:30].
- **The Hanukkah and calendar controls.** Dymock notes the outline correctly *omits* Hanukkah (a second-temple, post-165-BC Maccabean feast Nephi could not have known) [05:31], [06:01]; a side-debate over whether the ancient civil year began in spring or fall, and Butler's claim that the Nephites kept a **solar** calendar (the "nine moons" anomaly in the Coriantumr account), frames whether 1 Nephi might even encode solar time-data [15:00], [80:03].
