# Ward Radio — The New Calling That Would Eliminate "Shelf-Breaking"

[[Cardon Ellis]], **[[Jacob Hansen]]**, and **[[Luke Hansen]]** argue the "shelf" analogy for faith is defective and propose replacing it with a **scale** — and building a deliberate apologetics-and-inoculation program (even a stake "apologist" calling) modeled on Brett McDonald's *LDS Truth Claims* lectures.

## Summary
- **The scale replaces the shelf.** Doubts don't "break a shelf"; belief is a scale you load with reasons to doubt (red/anti-Mormon issues) and reasons to believe — *spiritual* (yellow) and *secular/evidential* (blue) — and the goal is to load the believing side, especially the blue marbles the church rarely supplies [^1].
- **Load the positive case FIRST.** Per Brett McDonald, before examining any criticism you exhaust the best scholarly *reasons to believe*; most who leave "went to a church that was trying to give them spiritual experiences, not training them in apologetics," so the scale was already tipped before they got the positive case [^2].
- **The good, the true, and the beautiful.** The church does "the good" (good people) and "the beautiful" (temples, aesthetics) well but underplays "the true" (intellectual defense); belief should satisfy heart *and* mind — "we should be able to intellectually justify our beliefs" [^3].
- **Institutional proposal + "make Bible bashing great again."** A church-endorsed apologetics certification (kept structurally separate so it can update as evidence changes) to inoculate youth; and reclaiming charitable, curious debate — using Socratic questioning and steelmanning (Greg Koukl's *Tactics*) to build bridges rather than "own" opponents [^4].
