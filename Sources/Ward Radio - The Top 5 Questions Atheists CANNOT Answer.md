# Ward Radio — The Top 5 Questions Atheists CANNOT Answer

A Ward Radio episode in which host **[[Cardon Ellis]]** is joined by **[[Jacob Hansen]]** and **[[Hayden Carroll]]** to work through several common atheist objections — not by refuting them with statements but by formulating the **question** that exposes each objection's hidden assumption. Hansen frames the whole exercise as an apologetics-method drill ("questions are the person who's playing offense"), and closes by warning that apologetics is for a *watching audience*, not the person across the table. The "top 5" are really five-to-six steelmanned atheist positions with the Latter-day Saint reply attached.

## Summary
- **Method: turn every statement into a question.** The unifying thesis is Socratic: when you hear an argument, "don't respond with a statement — figure out how to formulate your statement into a question," because the questioner plays offense and exposes what the interlocutor cannot defend. Hansen adds the crucial caveat that this is *public* apologetics for onlookers; one-on-one it "just burns the relationship," and "ministry is not apologetics." See [[Apologetics Is Socratic and Aimed at the Audience Not the Interlocutor]].[^1]
- **Claim 1 — gratuitous (unnecessary) suffering.** The atheist (e.g. [[Alex OConnor]]'s starving, broken-legged deer) says unnecessary suffering is incompatible with an all-good, all-powerful God. The reply-question: *is it not possible that unjust suffering is necessary for God to accomplish his purposes?* — distinguishing un-justifiable micro-suffering from a macro plan, and noting the creedal God (creating *ex nihilo*, able to do otherwise) owns the problem in a way the constrained LDS God does not. See [[Gratuitous Evil - God Could Have Achieved His Purposes With Less]], [[Mortal Life as a Realm of Injustice We Pass Through]], [[Pre-Existence Solves the Problem of Evil]], [[The Soteriological Problem of Evil - The Inconsistent Triad]].[^2]
- **Claim 2 — the Bible's terrible morality (the Amalekites).** Reply-questions: *by what standard do you judge this wrong?* and *why apply a different reliability standard to the slaughter texts than to the rest you call unreliable?* Latter-day Saints need not hold an inerrant Old Testament ("maybe God did not command that"). See [[By What Standard - Morality Requires an Objective Standard]], [[Whose God Can Ground Morally Reprehensible]], [[A Critic Must Ground His Moral Truth Claims or He Is Borrowing Them]].[^3]
- **Claim 3 — the Bible is sexist.** Reply: define "sexist," then ask whether a Roman would have judged it so — and note Paul's radical *mutual* bodily ownership ([[1 Corinthians 7.3-5|1 Cor 7]]) and the servant-leadership redefinition (the sons of Zebedee; "he who is greatest shall be the servant of all"). Equality of worth with difference of role; the gospel sets a norm that admits exceptions. See [[Modern Gender Equality Is Not Grounded in the Bible]], [[Paul Pioneered Mutual Bodily Ownership]], [[Genesis 3.16 Means Rule Not Preside]].[^4]
- **Claim 4 — insufficient evidence for the Resurrection.** The key move: the Resurrection is only a question *once you grant theism*. Convert an **arbitrary** standard of evidence into a **comparative** one — "show me a more plausible account of God interacting with the world." First cross the God bridge, then the Christianity bridge, then the Restoration. See [[Comparing Models to Find the Most Coherent Jesus]], [[The Four-Level Conversation Structure of Apologetics]], [[Extraordinary Claims Require Extraordinary Evidence - Craig vs Hitchens]], [[If God Exists, Miracles Are Possible]].[^5]
- **Claim 5 — Jesus never claimed to be God (synoptics).** Reply-questions: did the synoptic *authors* intend to portray Jesus as more than a man? Did people *worship* him? Was he charged with *blasphemy* (which claiming mere messiahship would not warrant)? Does Paul (writing in the 50s–60s, before John) already treat Jesus as fully divine — defeating the "late development" thesis? And the conceptual caveat: first-century Jews lacked the *trinitarian* categories, so "did he claim to be God?" must be posed as "more than a man." See [[The Sanhedrin Blasphemy Charge Presupposes the Enochic Son of Man]], [[Blasphemy Did Not Require Claiming to Be Yahweh]], [[New Testament Hymns Affirm Christs Divinity]], [[John Gave Christianity Its High Christology and Pre-existence]].[^6]
- **Bonus — divine hiddenness.** Why do so many in Utah have a relationship with God and so many in Pakistan not? Reply via the parable of the talents — God judges the *return on what each was given*, and everyone receives some light of Christ; plus hiddenness is a positive condition of a probationary test (the "let them cry it out" / "kids must leave the house" analogy). See [[Schellenberg's Hiddenness Argument and the Christian Reply]], [[God Meets the Sincere Heart Outside His Jurisdiction]], [[parable of the talents]], [[Sufficient Evidence Exists for the Seeker but Desire Distorts Belief]].[^7]
