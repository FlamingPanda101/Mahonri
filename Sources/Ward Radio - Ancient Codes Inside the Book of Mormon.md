Ward Radio episode in which host [[Cardon Ellis]] interviews researcher [[Jerry Grover]] about claimed numerical structure in the Book of Mormon's year-counts. Grover argues that the year-intervals in [[4 Nephi]] and [[Ether 1.3|Ether]] (and, he says, the rest of the Book of Mormon) are built from **Mesoamerican and Hebrew "sacred numbers"** in the same way Maya codices (Dresden, Madrid, Borgia) structure their divinatory almanacs — making Mormon's abridgment, in Grover's phrase, "a religious codex." He grounds the pattern in his translation of the "characters document," where the copied number-sets consist preferentially of those sacred numbers added together. The work is available free at bmslr.org (Book of Mormon Science and Linguistic Research).

## Summary
- **The characters-document number-sets.** Grover's translation of the characters document turned up "a bunch of number sets" in Egyptian hieratic/demotic plus Mesoamerican forms (e.g., bar-and-dot fives, a hieratic "9"). He found dates built oddly — e.g., 53 written as "40 + 13" — and realized the scribe "preferentially" uses sacred numbers and their multiples, adding them to reach each value.[^1][^2] See [[The Characters Document Translated as Abbreviated Hieratic and Maya]].
- **The sacred numbers.** Hebrew: 5, 7, 10. Mesoamerican: 9, 13, 19, 20 (plus multiples). Grover ties 13 and 20 to the 260-day Maya ritual calendar (13 "months" of 20 days), and 19 to the Metonic cycle (lunar and solar years realigning, "the New Year's Day hits the same day every 19 years").[^3][^4] See [[Mesoamerican Sacred-Number Intervals Structure the Book of Mormon Year-Counts]].
- **Fourth Nephi's odd year-list.** [[4 Nephi]] reports years non-sequentially ("30 and 8... 30 and 9, 40 and first, 40 and second... 49... 50 and first..."), skipping some. Grover graphs the listed years and finds the *intervals* between them are 7, 9, 10, 13, 19, 20, 30 — the sacred set. And each listed year can itself be reached by summing sacred numbers.[^5][^6] See [[Mesoamerican Sacred-Number Intervals Structure the Book of Mormon Year-Counts]].
- **Ether's reign-lengths.** [[Ether 1.3|Ether]] gives reign-lengths, not a year-count, but Grover decomposes them into sacred sums (24 = 19+5; 42 = 7×6 or 19+10+13), and notes 19 (which he associates with "righteousness, maybe Christ") clusters around certain kings (e.g., Coriantum).[^7]
- **Maya codex precedent.** Grover shows the Dresden and Madrid codices working with base-13 interval-sets, and the Borgia (Aztec) using 13s and 7s to build 52 — establishing that "make the intervals hit the sacred numbers" is exactly what Mesoamerican almanacs do, including occasional "fudging" (Maya kings adjusting their own birth-years to land on sacred dates).[^8][^9]
- **Cardon's pushback on over-counting.** The host raises the obvious risk: once enough numbers are declared "sacred," any value is "a divine combination" of them. Grover concedes "you have to be careful," says some intervals don't match, and claims rule-bound application still shows the text "trying to do what it's trying to do."[^10] See the steelman in [[Mesoamerican Sacred-Number Intervals Structure the Book of Mormon Year-Counts]].
- **The takeaway for the average member.** Grover: it "won't add anything to the doctrine," but it indicates the year-counts "are not arbitrary" — evidence the Book of Mormon "is not just something written in the 19th century" but matches an ancient Mesoamerican mode of writing a religious/divinatory document.[^11]
