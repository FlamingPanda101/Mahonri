# The Stick of Joseph — Why ALL Christians Should Wear a Sacred Garment

A Plain and Precious Publishing interview on the [[The Stick of Joseph - Are Christians Becoming Mormon with Brian Thomassen|Stick of Joseph]] channel (recorded on the grounds of the Okinawa Temple) with **[[Jonah Barnes]]** and **[[Dave Butler|Professor Dave Butler]]**, hosts Kate and Mike, on a bonus chapter of Barnes's book *The Lost Gems of Genesis* (subtitle: "how the Apocrypha prove that Joseph Smith fixed the Bible"). The thesis: the temple garment is "the most Christian symbol in the world," and the modern mockery of "magic underwear" exposes how badly the covering-of-Christ was lost from Genesis.

## Summary
- **The "coats of skins" and the "garments of light" are two sides of one atonement pun.** [[Jonah Barnes]] and [[Dave Butler]] note the Hebrew for *skin* (*or*, ע-ו-ר) and *light* (*or*, א-ו-ר) are near-homophones (identical in modern Hebrew); a rabbinic opinion in *Genesis Rabbah* read Adam and Eve's [[Genesis 3.21|coats]] as "garments of light." The garment of *skin* = the mortal body; to return to Eden one must reverse the process and be re-clothed in a garment of *light* — "it's not your garment of skin that atones for you; you need to get covered in a garment of light" [^1][^2]. See [[The Garment of Skin and the Garment of Light]].
- **"Atonement" is Tyndale's coinage that buried the Hebrew *kaphar* = "covering."** Tyndale, facing *kaphar* ("a covering of blood") across the Old Testament, minted *at-one-ment* from a Latin liturgical term — so English readers "don't get covering, you get atonement," obscuring the garment-as-covering symbolism that runs from [[Genesis 3.21|Genesis 3]] through the whole canon [^3]. (Reinforces the vault's [[Atonement as Covering - Joseph Could Not Have Known]] and [[The Garment Is the Covering of Christ]].)
- **The high priest's robes matched the temple veil, so wearing the garment is putting on the body of the Lord.** [[Dave Butler]] argues the Day-of-Atonement high priest came out wearing robes that *matched the veil*; "wearing the veil means putting on the body of the Lord," which is why the temple veil tore when Jesus' body was destroyed — a sign of his identity as the great High Priest, not (as some Protestants claim) that the temple was abolished [^4][^5]. See [[The Garment Is the Veil of the Temple and the Body of the Lord]].
- **The garment is a "credibility-enhancing display" that anchors religious identity.** Barnes relays the world's leading scholar of new religious movements ([[Lorne Dawson]]) and the empirical literature on **CREDs** (credibility-enhancing displays): a child's best predictor of staying in the parents' religion is seeing costly sincere behaviors; a sacred garment one is even "a little ashamed" to discuss signals "something worthwhile" and a source of religious identity sociologists say would be terrible to lose [^6][^7]. See [[The Sacred Garment Is a Credibility-Enhancing Display]].
- **The "fight-club" secrecy is pearls before swine, not shame — and every tradition has sacred vestments.** They reframe temple secrecy: Christ "winks all the time," telling people not to cast pearls before swine; sacred things are "not ours to throw." A religion with no sacred space or vestment "should be really suspicious." The "magic underwear" jeer is ethnocentric — it is never aimed at a Hasidic Jew's *tzitzit* or a Sikh's turban — and only sounds odd to modern America, not to the rest of human history [^8][^9]. See [[Sacred Secrecy Is Pearls Before Swine Not Shame]].
- **Mircea Eliade's "return to creation" is a complementary reading of the garment.** [[Dave Butler]] adds [[Mircea Eliade]]: in sacred space one re-enacts the creation and is "made new"; putting on a new garment symbolizes a new, healed self — a both/and with the covering and veil readings (multivalent temple symbols) [^10]. See [[The Sacred Garment Is a Credibility-Enhancing Display]].
