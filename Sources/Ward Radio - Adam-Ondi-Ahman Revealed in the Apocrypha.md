Ward Radio episode in which host [[Cardon Ellis]] interviews [[Jonah (Plain and Precious Publishing)|Jonah Barnes]] of Plain & Precious Publishing (author of *The Lost Gems of Genesis*), with [[Britney Ward Radio|Britney]] reading the apocryphal citations aloud. The thesis: the cluster of claims [[Joseph Smith]] makes about [[Adam]] and Adam-Ondi-Ahman in [[D&C 107]] and [[D&C 116]] — Adam as a righteous priest/prophet, a deathbed gathering of his posterity to bestow a last blessing, a prophecy "unto the last generation," a sacred secret to be withheld from [[Cain]], and a "Book of Eve" — are matched in detail by the recently recovered *Life of Adam and Eve* literature (Greek, Latin, Armenian, Georgian/Slavonic, and "Egyptian" recensions) that Joseph could not have accessed in 1830. The episode is anchored by [[D&C 107|D&C 107:53–56]].

## Summary
- **Adam was righteous, a priest and a prophet.** Against the Protestant reading in which Adam "never gives a sacrifice... never does a darn righteous thing," Joseph's [[D&C 107|D&C 107:53–56]] has Adam gather Seth, Enos, Cainan, Mahalaleel, Jared, Enoch, and Methuselah — "all high priests, with the residue of his posterity who were righteous" — three years before his death into the valley of Adam-ondi-Ahman, bless them, and (being "full of the Holy Ghost") predict "whatsoever should befall his posterity unto the last generation." Barnes argues the Adam apocrypha independently portray a righteous, priestly, prophetic Adam.[^1][^2] See [[Adam Gathering His Posterity Before Death Matches the Adam Apocrypha]].
- **A sacred secret withheld from Cain = a temple ordinance.** The Greek *Vita* (3:2), an Armenian penitence text, and a Slavonic text all have God tell Adam not to reveal "the mystery which thou knowest" to Cain "the son of wrath." Barnes notes "mystery" is the ancient Greek word for *ordinance* — a temple ordinance Adam is to pass on.[^3]
- **The deathbed gathering matches verbatim.** The Latin *Vita Adae* (11:1) has Adam, knowing his days are ending, say "Let all my sons assemble themselves to me that I may bless them before I die" — which Barnes calls "exactly what Joseph describes in section 107." The Armenian *Penitence* (6:2) adds that the sons assembled "inside the place which Eve had entered" and "he prayed to the Lord God" — paralleling the congregation/prayer of D&C 107:56. These manuscripts date to the early Christian era and were first translated/accessible only in the later 19th–20th centuries.[^4][^5] See [[Adam Gathering His Posterity Before Death Matches the Adam Apocrypha]].
- **Adam lived on a different continent than Noah landed.** The "Egyptian Life of Adam and Eve" (53:7) has God prophesy of Noah, "I will bring them to a distant land, and the land in which you live now shall remain desolate" — distinguishing Adam's land from Noah's landfall (Ararat, Armenia / the Middle East). Barnes's inference: "the only place we're sure Adam-ondi-Ahman was not is the Middle East," supporting a New-World Adam.[^6] See [[Adam Lived on a Different Continent Than Noah Landed]].
- **The Book of Eve.** Several recensions have Eve — not Adam — gather and instruct the children as Adam dies, recounting the Creation and Fall as "the first scripture." The *Vita* (15–30 / "chapter 15") has Eve command the story be written on tablets of stone and clay so that whether the world is judged by water or fire one set survives — a deliberate dual-medium preservation. Barnes calls this "very Mormon": the Bible shows little concern for record preservation, while the Book of Mormon is "obsessed with it" (e.g., Christ reprimanding the Nephites for omitting Samuel the Lamanite; the plates as a "metallic contingency plan").[^7][^8] See [[The Book of Eve Has Eve Dictate the First Scripture]].
- **The cumulative "magic-trick" argument.** Barnes's payoff: whatever one thinks of "Missouri," Joseph gets "the who, the how, the where, the when" of the Adam-ondi-Ahman scene right against texts he could not have read — and a man who "wins every table in the casino" cannot be dismissed as lucky.[^9] (The same inference-to-best-explanation move catalogued in [[Book of Mormon as American Pseudepigrapha]] and [[Authentic Ancient Texts Contain Uncorroborated Unique Data]].)
