# Ward Radio - Answering Common BAD Assumptions About the Book of Abraham

**Channel:** Ward Radio · **Presenter:** [[Luke Hansen]] · **Featuring clips of:** [[Kerry Muhlestein]]
**URL:** https://youtu.be/r5ojK_VY04E

## Summary
- A point-by-point rebuttal of [[Cameron Bertuzzi]]'s (Capturing Christianity) "the Book of Abraham was fabricated, not translated" video. Hansen's core charge: Cameron's case rests on **unexamined assumptions**, chiefly that the Kirtland Egyptian Papers (the "Egyptian Alphabet and Grammar") are the *source* of the translation — when the historical record (per Muhlestein) makes that a mostly W.W. Phelps project, and one Phelps letter using "Egyptian" characters predates the Saints even knowing the papyri existed.
- On the facsimiles: the devastating 1912 Egyptologist quotes (Breasted, Petrie) are over a century old; symbols are notoriously hard to interpret and the 200 BC Theban context was a melting pot where Jews repurposed Egyptian art (Abraham on a lion couch; Abraham/Isaac/Jacob on the throne; Christ himself repurposes an Egyptian tale in the Lazarus parable, Luke 16).
- On the 1966 Met fragments: text and adjacent illustration correlate only ~50% of the time; Abr. 1:12 points "to the representation at the commencement"; witnesses said the Book of Abraham was on a *different* roll, and we have only ~2–3% of Joseph's papyri.
- On content (gods of creation, premortality): Cameron's "unknown to Jews/Christians" claim collapses given henotheism and pre-existence in the tradition (cites [[Trent Horn]] against him). Closes with five things "Cameron doesn't want you to know": **Olishem**, **Shinehah**, Zeptah/Egyptus, the apocryphal Abraham biography, and correct facsimile identifications — and concedes the spiritual approach is what ultimately matters.
