# Ward Radio — How Masculinity Presides and Feminists Agree

Host [[Britney (the Shadow)|Britney the Shadow]] convenes "the women of Ward Radio" — [[Tikla]] and
[[Andrea Woodmansee]] — for a deep dive into **masculine and feminine symbolism in the temple**,
centered on the **square** (masculine) and the **circle/compass** (feminine), and the thesis that
"masculinity presides" — both between the sexes and *within each person*.

## Summary
- Framing: modern culture has lost "symbolic literacy," vilifying mystery, secrecy, and the sacred; the
  temple's symbols therefore feel alien, blocking access to their richness. Recovering symbol-reading
  is the task. (Andrea cites McConkie & Parry that all covenants "will be written in the language of
  symbolism.")[^1]
- The **square** = masculine: perfect shape (four corners of the earth), the builder's tool that makes
  true right angles; word-clusters *uphold/upright/righteous/foundation*; tied to Christ as the
  cornerstone/foundation stone and to the tradition of Christ as a stonemason ("builder," *tektōn*).
  See [[The Square and Compass Are Masculine and Feminine Temple Symbols]].[^2]
- The **circle/compass** = feminine: the tool of a perfect symmetrical circle; *encircling, encompass,
  compassion* (compass + "to suffer with"); planning/nurture; cycles and infinity (π); read into Ruth's
  encircling robe and the [[1 Nephi 11.18|1 Nephi 11]] Tree of Life (interpreted as Mary).[^3]
- The two are paired in ancient artifacts: a plumb-bob square on an Egyptian mummy; the overlaid-squares
  "Seal of Melchizedek" rosette on early Christian mosaics (Ravenna), Masada fabric, and mummy robes;
  and (via [[Hugh Nibley]]'s *Temple and Cosmos*) a Chinese silk-tomb image of a king with a square and
  a queen with a compass, intertwined as serpents amid star-charts — masculine and feminine each bearing
  a creation tool.[^4]
- **"Masculinity presides" psychologized:** masculine/feminine are principles every person blends (not
  man/woman). Within the self, the masculine (boundaries, justice, stability) should preside over the
  feminine (openness, nurture) — and this *frees* the feminine. See [[Masculinity Should Preside Within Each Person Not Just Between the Sexes]].[^5]
- **The concession to feminism:** when feminists tell women to be assertive and set boundaries, they are
  (unknowingly) telling women to "let masculinity preside" — "that part they have right"; the error is
  the second step of branding masculinity bad in men and in the world. The reward for true balance is
  consistently *peace*.[^6]
