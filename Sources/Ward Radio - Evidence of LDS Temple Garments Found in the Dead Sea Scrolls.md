# Ward Radio - Evidence of LDS Temple Garments Found in the Dead Sea Scrolls

Host [[Cardon Ellis]] with [[Kwaku El]], [[Brad Whitbeck]], guest **[[Jonah Barnes]]**, and [[Don Bradley]]. Despite the "Dead Sea Scrolls" title (the hosts concede they mean apocrypha colloquially), the episode argues the temple garment's markings and symbolism are ancient — attested in Egyptian art, early-Christian iconography, ancient Chinese compass-and-square imagery, and apocryphal Adam literature.

## Summary
- **The garment of skins as the first atonement.** Drawing on the First Book of Adam and Eve and rabbinic tradition, Barnes traces the garment from Eden ([[Genesis 3.21|coats of skins]] sewn by Jehovah after the fig-leaf apron fails) down through Noah, Melchizedek, Abraham, and Joseph. He argues Joseph's "coat of many colors" is a mistranslation of a coat of **markings** — proved, he says, by blind Israel recognizing it by feel ([[Genesis 37|Genesis 37]]). See [[Joseph's Coat Was a Coat of Markings Not Many Colors]].
- **kaphar = covering, not "at-one-ment."** Barnes argues Hebrew has no word for "atonement"; *kaphar* (Yom Kippur) means "to cover," so the garment is the covering that lets mortals withstand God's glory — the atonement of Christ as a garment. Overlaps with [[Atonement as Covering - Joseph Could Not Have Known]].
- **The garment's four marks and the divine name.** Barnes/Bradley argue the garment's four symbols (one repeated) map onto the four-letter Tetragrammaton (YHWH, with a repeated letter), so wearing the garment is literally "taking upon us the name" of God — and the compass-and-square are ancient (Egypt, ~2000 BC China), not Masonic inventions. Updates [[We Wear the Divine Name - The Tetragrammaton on the High Priest and the Garment]].
