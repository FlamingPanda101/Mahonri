# Ward Radio — Debunking David Latting's Attack on Maddy Packer

A [[Ward Radio]] live stream (host **Cardon Ellis**, with **Brad Whipple**, [[Hayden Carroll]], **Michael Meyerano**, **Luke Hansen**, and newcomer LDS apologist [[Maddy Packer]]) reacting to an Instagram reel by Evangelical critic [[David Latting]] that attacked Packer's three-week-old apologetics account. Packer's reel had made one claim: that a Christian is best **defined by what one does** (discipleship), not by whether one affirms the right metaphysical formulation of God. Latting's response — that Mormons "lack critical thinking," worship "a completely different Jesus," and have "redefined eternal" — supplies the panel's material. The substance is a rolling defense of [[Orthopraxy over Orthodoxy]], a rebuttal of the [[Trinity Is a First-Rank Salvation Issue|Trinity-as-membership-test]], and a patristic counter-argument that the Father alone is self-existent.

## Summary

- **Define "Christian" by orthopraxy, not orthodoxy.** Packer's whole reel argued that "I follow the Jesus in the Bible by what I do," so the Trinity is an arbitrary gate for Christian identity. The panel backs her: holding [[Orthopraxy over Orthodoxy in LDS Salvation|orthopraxy over orthodoxy]] means the illiterate peasant's widow's-mite faith is not ranked below the theologian's, and the Trinity is a [[Trinitarianism Is Anachronistic to the Biblical Period|post-biblical, anachronistic]] formulation the New-Testament authors themselves would fail. [^1] [^2] See [[Define Christian First and the Concession Follows]] and [[Understanding the Trinity Is Not Required for Salvation]].
- **Homoousios is not in the Bible, so Sola Scriptura cannot demand it.** *Homoousios* is a 4th-century Greek word absent from scripture; Protestants who say the Bible is the *final authority* yet make a post-biblical creedal formula the test of Christianity are inconsistent — and the panel argues the Bible (e.g. [[Matthew 7.21]]) actually *contradicts* the belief-over-works gate. See [[The Trinity and Consubstantiality Are Not in the Bible]], [[Homoousios Was Coined to Refute Arius]], [[Creeds Get the Final Say in Practice Not the Bible]]. [^3] [^4]
- **"You worship a different Jesus" confuses reference with belief.** Latting closes by insisting LDS "worship a completely different Jesus Christ… we're not speaking about the same person." The panel answers with the same-reference rule: a wrong *description* of how the Son relates to the Father does not relocate the *referent* (the historical Jesus of Nazareth). Cardon's fresh illustration: "we believe [[David Latting]] is eternal — are we now speaking about a *different* David Latting?"; and Mary Magdalene, Peter, and the Twelve each related to the same Christ differently without it being a different Jesus. See [[Same Reference Different Beliefs - You Worship the Same Jesus]] and [[Different Jesus Both Uncharitable and Precisely True]]. [^5] [^6]
- **The Father alone is self-existent — the ante-Nicene fathers agree, refuting Latting's "redefinition of eternal" charge.** Latting claimed LDS "changed the definition of eternal" from self-existence to mere duration. Michael answers that LDS need not play that framework at all, and that Latting's *own* tradition denies the Son is self-existent: at Nicaea (325) **Eusebius** called the Father "the source of divinity" and the Son "inferior… begotten"; **Origen** said "the Father alone is unbegotten"; **Justin Martyr** (c. 155) called Christ the *deuteros theos* ("second god") and reserved *autotheos* ("self-existent God") for the Father. So the Son is co-eternal yet not self-existent — exactly the LDS view. See [[Autotheos Collides With Eternal Generation]], [[Subordinationism Was the Early Church View]], [[No Diminished Divinity Despite Subordination]], [[Born of the Father Before All Ages Affirms Christs Preexistence and Subordination]]. [^7] [^8] [^9]
- **Creation *ex nihilo* is a late-2nd-century Christian invention, not the Hebrew/Jewish view.** Latting smuggles in *creatio ex nihilo* and a creator/creation divide as the biblical baseline. Luke counters (citing **Peter Hayman** and the **Basilides** origin thesis, with **Jonathan Goldstein** the lone holdout for a Jewish origin) that ex nihilo was read back into the text only c. 400 AD; the Hebrews thought *relationally*, not in Greek metaphysical categories. See [[Creation Ex Nihilo Originated Late 2nd Century CE]], [[Ex Nihilo Is a Christian Invention Not Greek Hellenization]], [[Bara Means Organize and Ex Nihilo Is a Second-Century Invention]], [[Greek Categories Are Anachronistic to the Hebrew Bible]]. Yet Luke insists professing ex nihilo "does not affect your eternal salvation." [^10] [^11]
- **The Hellenization charge: neoplatonism forced a square peg into a round hole.** Hayden frames the creeds as the *helenization* of early Christianity — Plato's perfect-being theology jammed into the Bible, producing baseless "solves" (hypostatic union, divine simplicity) with no scriptural basis. See [[Greek Philosophy Corrupted the Christian Concept of God]], [[Hellenization Was Not Corruption of an Original Pure Church]], [[Independent Philosophers Keep Reconstructing LDS Theology]]. [^12]
- **The Three Ds: this is bigotry, not criticism.** Drawing on a rubric the panel got from the ADL in Israel (delegitimization, demonization, double standards), Cardon argues Latting hits all three in his opening sentences — and that releasing a "Mormons aren't Christian" pamphlet within 48 hours of the Grand Blanc/Michigan shooting of LDS worshippers is naked demonization. See [[The Three Ds Rubric Diagnoses Anti-Mormonism as Bigotry]] (new) and [[Anti-Mormonism Is Attacking the Person Not the Idea]]. [^13] [^14]
- **Critic vs. anti-Mormon: the stimulus test.** Cardon distinguishes a legitimate critic (e.g. Alex O'Connor, who *updates* when shown primary sources on the [[A Man of His Time Without Excusing the Sin|Fanny Alger]] dating and Don Bradley / Brian Hales scholarship) from an anti-Mormon who keeps recycling debunked tropes ("Jesus is the brother of Satan," "get your own planet") regardless of correction. Not responding to stimulus moves you from the critic camp to the bigotry camp. See [[Anti-Mormon Is Public Intentional Repeated Erosion of Faith]], [[A Member Can Be an Anti-Mormon]], [[The Charitable-but-Critical Believer Posture]]. [^15] [^16]
- **The judo move: attacking the Restoration only proves the need for it.** Cardon's "anti-Mormon rule number one": critics "never refute prophecy, they only fulfill it." By presupposing his 19th-century creedal Christianity is "the mold" and otherizing the LDS with disgust, Latting is not refuting the Restoration — he is dramatizing the [[The Great Apostasy|apostasy]] and proving the *need* for a restoration. See [[Attacking the Restoration Only Proves the Need for It]] (new). [^17] [^18]
- **Engagement ethic: wise as serpents, harmless as doves — peacemaker, not pushover.** Brad repeatedly resists the chat's calls to "fight back," arguing for peacemaking that ends conflict rather than inflaming it (modeled on [[Captain Moroni]] offering Zerahemnah a covenant of peace), while Cardon presses that Mormon culture is naively slow to recognize that some critics simply *hate* them and that online rhetoric has already produced arson. See [[Wise as Serpents and Harmless as Doves - Peacemaker Not Pushover]] (new). [^19] [^20]
- **Membership and belief (Aaron Shafovaloff's super-chat).** Pressed on whether continued belief in Jesus is required to *maintain* LDS membership, the panel answers that there is no belief-monitoring mechanism beyond the self-reported [[Temple Recommend]] interview; you can be on the rolls without full communion. Michael ties full Christian life to partaking of the sacraments — which on a quasi-Calvinist sacramentology require the Holy Ghost, which requires belief in Christ — so belief and praxis are *meant* to travel together. [^21] [^22]
