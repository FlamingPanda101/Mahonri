# Ward Radio — Ask Me Anything About LDS Church History (feat. Don Bradley)

A wide-ranging Ward Radio super-chat AMA with historian [[Don Bradley]], hosted by [[Cardon Ellis]] with [[Kwaku El]], [[Brad Whitbeck]], [[Jonah Barnes]], and [[Britney Chapman Nash]]. Questions range over Brigham Young vs. John Taylor, Deutero-Isaiah, the Anthon transcript, infinite regression of gods, Oliver Cowdery as co-translator, Freemasonry, and the recurring "Hyrum went to Dartmouth" theory.

## Summary

- **Historical method: messy records, best explanation, right order.** Bradley's throughline is methodological: the historical record is "messy" and yields evidence pointing in different directions, so the question is never "can I find a source for X?" but "what is the *best overall explanation* of the totality of the data — the model that explains the most in the simplest way?" Conspiracy theories typically fail because they explain the data in a needlessly complicated way and ignore the absence of any defector. He adds that "much of history is just getting the events in the right order." See [[Weigh Sources by Proximity and Multiple Attestation]] and [[The Best Explanation Beats Cherry-Picked Evidence in History]].
- **The "Hyrum Smith went to Dartmouth" theory debunked.** Asked about Randy Bell's claim (via Mormon Stories) that Mormonism's distinctive doctrines came out of [[Hyrum Smith]]'s education at Dartmouth, Bradley and the panel respond that Hyrum attended **Moore's Charity School** (a prep school on the Dartmouth campus), *not* Dartmouth College; he is not on Dartmouth's alumni rolls; [[Lucy Mack Smith]] called it merely "the academy at Hanover"; and the records confirm only ~one year of attendance. The deeper objection: the theory requires Hyrum to have absorbed, as a young teen, ideas he then sat on for 20-25 years and slipped into the Doctrine and Covenants rather than the Book of Mormon. See [[The Hyrum-Smith-at-Dartmouth Theory Fails the Records]].
- **Several substantive one-offs.** Bradley accepts a general scholarly consensus for a multi-author **Isaiah** (Deutero/Trito-Isaiah) and surveys LDS options for the Book of Mormon quoting "Deutero-Isaiah" (revelatory expansion à la [[Blake Ostler]]; modern-translation models); argues the Anthon transcript could plausibly be the **Adamic/Jaredite** back page of the sealed portion; finds **no infinite regression of gods** in the early sources (D&C 93, Abraham 3, D&C 124:41 point to one ultimate deity), with Joseph possibly shifting only in the June 16, 1844 sermon; previews his forthcoming argument that **[[Oliver Cowdery]] was a co-translator** (not merely scribe) of part of the Book of Mormon and co-revelator of D&C 20; and explains the **same-day Hyrum/Emma deed** (D&C 132 day) as Clayton's clerical error, not a forgery conspiracy. On a personal note he reflects that prophets, like the biblical patriarchs, will disappoint us — "our exemplar… is Jesus Christ," not Abraham, Jacob, or Joseph Smith. See [[Prophets Point to Christ Through Their Failings]].
