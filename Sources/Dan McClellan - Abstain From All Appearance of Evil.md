# Dan McClellan — Abstain From All Appearance of Evil

- **The core thesis:** The folk-ethics rule that Christians must avoid morally neutral behaviors that *someone else might misunderstand* as wrong — supposedly grounded in [[1 Thessalonians 5.22|1 Thessalonians 5:22]] KJV ("abstain from all appearance of evil") — rests on a mistranslation. The verse is not about how acts appear to onlookers.[^1] See [[Abstain from All Appearance of Evil Is a Mistranslation]].
- **The lexical point:** The Greek *eidos* (εἶδος) can mean "form / outward appearance" but also "variety, kind, or type." Scholars "pretty unilaterally" read the "kind/type" sense here, so the verse means "avoid every kind of evil" — i.e. avoid evil in all its varieties.[^2] (The auto-captions garble *eidos* as "ethos.") See [[1 Thessalonians 5.22|1 Thessalonians 5:22]].
- **The corrected reading:** In line with the majority of modern New Testament translations, the passage should read "avoid every kind of evil"; it does not forbid doing things that are not wrong just because someone else might misread them.[^3]
