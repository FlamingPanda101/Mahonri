# Ward Radio - The Impossible Science of Faking the Gold Plates

**Channel:** [[Ward Radio]] | **Panelists:** [[Neal Rappleye]], [[Stephen Smoot]], and Ward Radio hosts | **Date:** 2026-02-22

- The panel rebuts the popular "I could make gold plates in my garage" objection (voiced by debater [[Frank Turek]]), arguing that the claim ignores the witness descriptions, the prohibitive cost of the materials, and the absence of any contemporary accusation that [[Joseph Smith]] forged metal plates.[^1] See [[Gold Plates Fabrication Was Prohibitively Expensive]], [[No Contemporary Accusation That Joseph Smith Forged Plates]], and [[Witness Descriptions Constrain Any Fabrication Hypothesis]].
- They argue the golden color, thinness, and greenish oxidation reported by witnesses are best explained by a pre-Columbian **tumbaga** alloy (roughly 70% copper, 10% silver, 20% gold) surface-treated by depletion gilding — not by paint, tin, or printing plates.[^2] See [[Tumbaga Alloy Explains Golden Appearance and Green Oxidation]] and [[Sonia Hazards Printing-Plate Theory Fails the Physical Description]].
- The bulk of the episode answers [[Trent Horn]]'s "the text won't fit on 40 plates" argument: the witnesses never gave a plate count, scientific estimates put the stack in the hundreds (187–600), and a "translation density" model shows the entire Book of Mormon — including the lost 116 pages — fits comfortably.[^3] See [[Trent Horns Word-Count Argument Uses Wrong Parameters]] and [[Translation Density Shows the Book of Mormon Text Fits]].
- The panel stresses the small character size attested in real ancient metal inscriptions (as small as 1.6–2 mm, roughly 12-point font), which both makes the text fit and explains Book of Mormon authors' complaints about engraving.[^4] See [[Reformed Egyptian Characters Were Small]].
- The conclusion is epistemically modest: they do not claim this *proves* the plates were real, only that fabrication is not the *best explanation* — invoking [[David Hume]]'s maxim that one should accept a natural explanation only if it is less miraculous than the supernatural one.[^5] See [[Joseph Smith Lacked the Metallurgical Skill and Time]] and [[Humes Maxim Applied to the Gold Plates]].
