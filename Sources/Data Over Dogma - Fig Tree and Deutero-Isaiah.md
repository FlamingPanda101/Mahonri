# Data Over Dogma — The Fig Tree and Deutero-Isaiah

- **Fig Tree Cursing (Mark 11:12–25):** McClellan explains this as a "Markan sandwich" — a metaphor where the cursing of the fig tree bookends the cleansing of the temple, together representing judgment on the temple and Jewish leadership for failing to "bear fruit." Mark was written ~70 CE, around the destruction of the temple.
- **Deutero-Isaiah (Isaiah 40–55):** McClellan presents the academic consensus that Isaiah 40–55 was composed by a different author centuries after the 8th-century BCE prophet Isaiah. Evidence includes the naming of Cyrus by name (~200 years after Isaiah), Aramaic loanwords from the exilic period, the disappearance of Isaiah's name after chapter 39, and the shift from future prophecy to past-tense description.
- **Methodological Naturalism:** McClellan argues that critical scholarship *must* methodologically reject real prophecy — not from anti-supernatural bias, but because accepting real prophecy for one text while applying critical methods to all others (e.g., Joseph Smith, the Quran, the Bhagavad Gita) creates an inconsistent, unworkable double standard.
