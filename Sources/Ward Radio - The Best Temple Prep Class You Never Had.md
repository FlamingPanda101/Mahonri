# Ward Radio — The Best Temple Prep Class You Never Had

An informal Latter-day Saint "temple prep" explainer from [[Ward Radio]], hosted by [[Cardon Ellis]] with guest teachers [[Michael Myrrono (OrthoCardia)|Michael Myrrono]] (an evangelical-to-LDS convert, about to receive his endowment) and [[Baylor Johnson (Ward Radio)|Baylor Johnson]], plus panelist Shara. The premise: Myrrono's bishop asked an *un-endowed* convert to teach temple prep because he had studied the endowment so intensively — first for years "from an anti-Mormon place," now as a believer. The hosts "throw resources" at the audience, reframing the temple endowment against common criticisms (it's weird, it's Masonic plagiarism, it's works-righteousness) through etymology, Old- and New-Testament typology, [[Martin Buber]]'s I-Thou philosophy, and the [[The Endowment Is Learning Heaven Not Earning Heaven|"learning heaven not earning heaven"]] frame. The episode wikilinks the vault's existing temple cluster — [[Endowment Elements Predate Joseph Smith's Freemasonry]], [[The Aaronic-Melchizedek Dualism Maps the Temple's Kings and Priests]], [[Avoiding Parallelomania Between Ancient Rites and LDS Temple]] — rather than duplicating it.

## Summary

- **The garment is a wearable enactment of "putting on Christ."** Both "garment" (Greek, "to clothe upon") and "endow" (Latin *induere*, "to clothe upon") mean clothing-upon; [[Genesis 3.21|Genesis 3:21]]'s God-made coats of skins are "the covering grace of Jesus Christ," and the Hebrew *kaphar* ("atonement") literally means "to cover" — so the daily garment means "we wear Jesus every day." [^1] See [[The Garment Is the Covering of Christ]].
- **The endowment is "learning heaven, not earning heaven."** Borrowing Brad Wilcox's line, the hosts cast the endowment as a participatory rehearsal (originally a *play*) of John of Patmos's heavenly vision: the redeemed made "kings and priests" ([[Revelation 5.9-10|Rev 5:9-10]]) — extended to "queens and priestesses" — a "royal priesthood" not male-only ([[1 Peter 2.9|1 Peter 2:9]]), robed in white and washed in the Lamb's blood ([[Revelation 7.9-14|Rev 7:9-14]]). [^2] See [[The Endowment Is Learning Heaven Not Earning Heaven]].
- **The veil is the flesh of Christ.** [[Hebrews 10.19-22|Hebrews 10:19-20]] makes the veil Christ's flesh; the temple veil dramatizes that God, looking *through* the veil, sees only the purity of Christ rather than the initiate's imperfection. The women's veil represents *all* humankind (Eve as "mother of all"), not women specifically — the hosts' answer to the charge that removing it was "going woke." [^3]
- **Covenant is I-Thou, not I-It.** Via [[Martin Buber]], temple covenant-making should be relational and covenantal (revealing one's "brokenness but also belovedness"); it becomes "blasphemy" when transactional ("obey so God won't smite me," prosperity bargaining) — the same profanation as using a marriage partner "as a means to an end." [^4] See [[The I-Thou Temple Is Covenant Relationship Not Transaction]].
- **"Weird" is the critic's problem: ritual has been normal for 4,000 years.** North American Protestantism is a historically "sterilized" anomaly in which even baptism is "neutered"; the endowment recovers ritual "Christianity hasn't seen since the 1st through 3rd centuries" and teaches "through allegory... through symbolism." "It's only in the past 40 years post-war that we've stopped embracing ritual." [^5] See [[Ritual Has Been Normal for Four Thousand Years]].
- **The Masonic-plagiarism charge fails because the endowment is already in the 1830 Book of Mormon.** Following [[Don Bradley]] — a former critic the discovery helped bring back — the hosts point to [[Ether 3.14|Ether 3]] and [[1 Nephi 11.18|1 Nephi 11]] as "endowment passages" predating Joseph Smith's 1842 Masonry by twelve years; the symbology is "too complex" to have been fabricated. [^6] See [[The Book of Mormon Already Contains the Endowment]] and [[Endowment Elements Predate Joseph Smith's Freemasonry]].
- **Preparation is participation, and the church could do better at it.** Myrrono prepped partly by a silent week at a Trappist monastery in contemplative prayer; the hosts defend interfaith borrowing as native to the Restoration's "gather all truth" ethos while conceding LDS spiritual formation is thin next to 2,000 years of Catholic mysticism. Panelist Shara (mother of five) recommends collecting such videos, writing down their scriptures, and studying them before attending temple prep "because it's there on purpose." They float Ward Radio hosting an actual temple-prep seminar. [^7]
- **Recommended resources (mentioned, not separately noted):** Brad Wilcox's "we're not earning heaven, we're learning heaven" BYU devotional; Jonathan Stapley's *Holiness to the Lord*; Don Bradley's lectures on the endowment in the Book of Mormon; Cade's video "Weird Mormon Temple Rituals Explained" (All Those In Favor); the church's own endowment page listing the five covenants (law of obedience, sacrifice, the gospel, chastity, consecration), whose recent online transparency the hosts contrast with prior generations' reticence. [^8]
