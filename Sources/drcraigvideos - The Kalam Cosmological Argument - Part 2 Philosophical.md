# The Kalam Cosmological Argument — Part 2: Philosophical
The companion to [[drcraigvideos - The Kalam Cosmological Argument - Part 1 Scientific|Part 1 (Scientific)]]. Where Part 1 marshalled physics (thermodynamics, the Big Bang, the Borde–Guth–Vilenkin theorem) for the claim that *the universe began to exist*, this short animated [[William Lane Craig|Craig]] explainer makes the **philosophical** case for the same premise. It revives two arguments from the medieval Muslim theologian [[Al-Ghazali]] against a past-eternal universe: (1) an *actual infinite* cannot exist in reality, illustrated by [[David Hilbert]]'s paradoxical infinite hotel; and (2) even if it could exist, an actual infinite could never be *formed by successive addition* (the Saturn/Jupiter orbits and the falling-dominoes illustrations). Craig then answers two objections (the past events don't coexist; numbers are an actual infinite) and concludes that the universe's cause must be uncaused, "spaceless, timeless, immaterial, and enormously powerful, much like... God" — rebutting [[Daniel Dennett]]'s suggestion that the universe caused itself.

## Summary
- **Al-Ghazali vs. Aristotle.** [[Aristotle]] held the universe eternal in the past; [[Al-Ghazali]] objected that a beginningless universe entails an *actually infinite* number of past events, "but that's a problem, because the existence of an actually infinite number of past events leads to absurdity. It's metaphysically impossible."[^1] See [[Hilbert's Hotel - Why an Actual Infinite Cannot Exist]] and [[An Actual Infinite Cannot Be Formed by Successive Addition]].
- **Hilbert's Hotel.** [[David Hilbert]]'s fully-occupied infinite hotel can still take in new guests, and can lose infinitely many guests while the number remaining stays the same or becomes finite — so "we have a contradiction. We subtract identical quantities from identical quantities, and get different answers." Hence "an actually infinite number of things cannot exist in the real world."[^2]
- **Successive addition.** Two planets orbiting from eternity past would have completed the *same* (infinite) number of orbits though one always doubles the other; and "the final domino could never fall if an infinite number of dominoes had to fall first," yet today *is* reached — so the past series of events "could not possibly be infinite."[^3]
- **Objections answered.** That past events don't coexist is met by a hotel "under construction from eternity past, one room being added each year" (still an actual infinite, still absurd); that numbers are an actual infinite presupposes mathematical Platonism, "a highly disputed assumption that most philosophers reject."[^4]
- **The conclusion.** If the universe began, "whatever begins to exist requires a cause," so the universe has a cause. [[Daniel Dennett]]'s "the universe caused itself" is incoherent — "the universe would have to exist before it existed" — so the cause is "outside of the universe, spaceless, timeless, immaterial, and enormously powerful, much like... God."[^5] See [[The Cause of the Universe Must Be Personal, Timeless, and Spaceless]].
