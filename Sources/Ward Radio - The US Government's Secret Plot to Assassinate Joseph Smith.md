## Summary
- A martyrdom-anniversary panel arguing the deaths of [[Joseph Smith]] and [[Hyrum Smith]] at Carthage (27 June 1844) were **planned political assassinations**, not random mob violence — driven by fear of Joseph's growing religious, economic, and political power and his presidential candidacy. See [[The Martyrdom Was a Coordinated Political Assassination Not a Mob]].
- Recounts how the 1845 Carthage murder-trial documents were sealed and rediscovered by a young [[Dallin H. Oaks]], whose 10-year study with Marvin Hill became *Carthage Conspiracy*; and the internal betrayal by [[William Law]] and stake president William Marks. See [[The Carthage Conspiracy Trial Records Were Sealed Until Dallin Oaks]].
- Tells the little-known Dennison Harris / Robert Scott blood-oath spy episode — two teenagers who infiltrated a 200-man oath-bound conspiracy to kill Joseph and were nearly murdered. See [[The Dennison Harris and Robert Scott Blood-Oath Spy Account]].
