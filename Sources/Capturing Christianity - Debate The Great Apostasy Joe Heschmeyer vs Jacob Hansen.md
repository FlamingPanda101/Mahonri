# Capturing Christianity - Debate: The Great Apostasy | Joe Heschmeyer vs Jacob Hansen

A formal, moderated debate hosted by Capturing Christianity at St. John the Baptist Parish (Salt Lake City) between **[[Joe Heschmeyer]]** (Catholic Answers apologist, affirming there was *no* Great Apostasy) and **[[Jacob Hansen]]** (Latter-day Saint, host of *Thoughtful Faith*, affirming there *was* an institutional apostasy). The format runs 18-minute openings, 10-minute rebuttals, 15-minute cross-examinations each, audience Q&A, and 5-minute closings.

The debaters agree on the framing: if the Great Apostasy happened, Catholicism is false; if it did not, the foundational claim of Mormonism is false. They diverge sharply on *what* is being claimed. Hansen restricts the thesis to an **institutional** apostasy — a loss of the *fullness* of apostolic authority to receive binding public revelation — while explicitly affirming that the **body of believers** never disappeared and that the Spirit kept working through Catholics, Protestants, and Jews. Heschmeyer counters that Scripture promises an *indefectible visible kingdom* (Daniel 2, Matthew 16:18, Luke 1, Isaiah 9), that no prophecy foretells either an apostasy or Joseph Smith, and that a movement abandoned by God scatters at its founder's death (Gamaliel's test, Acts 5) — which the early church conspicuously did not do.

Much of the cross-examination turns on the **papacy**: Hansen presses that a continuous institution holding Peter's universal jurisdiction must be named or an apostasy follows, and tries to pin Heschmeyer to dating "the Catholic Church" to 1870/1965; Heschmeyer repeatedly redirects, insisting universal jurisdiction is a *papacy* debate distinct from the *apostasy* debate he agreed to.

## Summary

- **Hansen's thesis (opening, [09:00]–[24:00]).** Two options only: continuous institution or institutional apostasy. The Pope no longer even *claims* the authority Peter had to receive new binding public revelation (CCC: "no new public revelation is to be expected") — so something was lost. No one before Constantine treated the bishop of Rome as the ultimate infallible authority; [[Victor I]] failed when he tried to excommunicate outside his jurisdiction; emperors, not popes, called the great councils; Eusebius's history names no pope-like office; Hegesippus (c. 150) reports corruption creeping in once "the sacred choir of apostles became extinct." Uniquely Catholic dogmas (bodily assumption, icon veneration, papal infallibility, indulgences) are absent from the first century, asserted "without revelation," and yet enforced under threat of hell. [02:00]–[24:00]
- **Heschmeyer's thesis (opening, [24:38]–[42:30]).** The real LDS claim (per Joseph Smith and *Preach My Gospel*) is a **universal** apostasy in which all creeds are "abominations" — far stronger than "the papacy developed." A cataclysm that size would be prophesied, as the Babylonian captivity and the temple's destruction were; instead Scripture promises preservation (Spirit of truth, gates of Hades, Matthew 13 parables of unstoppable growth) and foretells an **indefectible kingdom set up during the Roman Empire** (Daniel 2). Amos 8 is about Samaria, not the early church. [24:38]–[42:30]
- **Rebuttals ([42:30]–[64:00]).** Hansen reframes the *gates of hell* as the *gates of death*, which Christ already broke open (Confraternity commentary; [[Jack P. Lewis]]), and argues God withholds prophets when people kill them (Matthew 23:37) — a prophet needed a land of religious liberty (1776/1830). Heschmeyer deploys **Gamaliel's test** (Acts 5: false movements scatter at the founder's death — Joseph Smith's 1844 succession crisis, the Sunni-Shia split — whereas the apostolic church grew, [[Rodney Stark]]'s numbers), insists *episkopos* ≠ apostle and revelation closed because it was *complete* in Christ (Jude 1:3, Hebrews 1), and corrects Hansen's Eusebius and Irenaeus citations.
- **Cross-examinations ([64:00]–[95:30]).** Hansen: without the papacy you lack the "fullness of the church," which is functionally the LDS apostasy claim; he presses for first-500-year evidence of the assumption and Vatican-I infallibility and cites Eno, LaDue, Cyprian (256, "no bishop of bishops"), and the 2016 Chieti statement that Roman primacy was understood "from the fourth century onwards." Heschmeyer: Daniel 2 already secures indefectibility; Hansen points to "zero passages" foretelling an apostasy; the "two churches" (institution vs. assembly) distinction is found nowhere in the New Testament.
- **Closings ([95:30]–end).** Hansen: an institutional apostasy is simply what history shows; the bride of Christ is bigger than the papacy; "come and see," judge by fruits. Heschmeyer: a 2,000-year continuous tradition teaches things (e.g., creation *ex nihilo*) that contradict Mormonism — either the Spirit guided that church or it taught abominations, not both; the early church's explosive growth is the fruit of a kingdom that never fell.
