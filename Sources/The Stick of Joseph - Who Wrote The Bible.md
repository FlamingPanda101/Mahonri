# The Stick of Joseph - Who Wrote The Bible

[[Mike Day]] and [[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)]] (with Brad Whitbeck and Jackson) review [[Richard Elliott Friedman]]'s *Who Wrote the Bible?* — an accessible presentation of the **Documentary Hypothesis** (JEPD). The hosts read the two interlaced flood narratives aloud and argue the edited-anthology model anticipated by Joseph Smith strengthens, rather than threatens, the Latter-day Saint view of scripture.

## Summary
- **Four sources, JEPD.** J (Yahwist, Judah) and E (Elohist, Ephraim) are the oldest tribal epics; P (Priestly, detail-obsessed, cultic) and D (Deuteronomy plus the Deuteronomistic History) follow. Wellhausen made the case persuasive c. 1878; the sources are detected by reading the text (divine names, doublets, vocabulary), not by finding separate scrolls.[^1][^2] See [[Wellhausen Thesis on Hebrew Bible Origins]], [[Documentary Hypothesis Strengthens the Restoration]].
- **The two interlaced flood stories.** The hosts read Genesis 6–9 twice — the J account (Yahweh, 40 days, a dove, seven pairs of clean animals) and the P account (Elohim/God, 150 days, a raven, two of each) — showing two complete, non-overlapping narratives spliced together to preserve both northern and southern versions.[^3][^4] See [[Bible Contradiction - Noahs Ark Animal Count]], [[Two Interlaced Flood Narratives Preserve Northern and Southern Versions]].
- **Doublets preserved for national unity.** Friedman's argument: after the northern refugees flooded Hezekiah's Jerusalem (Israel Finkelstein's population spike c. 720 BC), an editor wove J and E together — keeping the doublets so "we're all us," a Bible for both kingdoms.[^5] See [[Doublets Were Preserved to Unify Two Kingdoms]].
- **Joseph Smith as Mormon-the-redactor anticipated Wellhausen.** The Book of Mormon presents itself in 1829 as an edited theological anthology with a named, biased editor (Mormon) drawing on many sources — cutting-edge biblical scholarship 50 years before Wellhausen, and blasphemous to many even today.[^6] See [[Documentary Hypothesis Strengthens the Restoration]].
- **Inerrancy fails; "struggling with God" succeeds.** The flood contradiction (seven pairs vs. two) cannot both be inerrant; the LDS frame — scripture as the record of Israel *struggling with God* (the name Israel), with "the mistakes of men" — embraces the difficulty.[^7]
