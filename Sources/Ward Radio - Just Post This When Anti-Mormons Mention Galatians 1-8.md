# Ward Radio — Just Post This When Anti-Mormons Mention Galatians 1:8

A [[Ward Radio]] panel episode, hosted by [[Kaleb Jones]] with [[Jonah (Plain and Precious Publishing)|Jonah Barnes]] and [[Austin Falter]], built around the viral "Galatians 1:8 disproves Mormonism" sound bite (a montage assembled by [[David Snell]]'s channel "Keystone"). The hosts argue the verse, read in context, actually *supports* the Restoration rather than refuting it.

- **The "gotcha" stated and steelmanned.** Critics quote [[Galatians 1.8|Galatians 1:8]] — "though we, or an angel from heaven, preach any other gospel... let him be accursed" — and map it onto [[Angel Moroni]] bringing [[Joseph Smith]] "another gospel," concluding the LDS Church is accursed. The hosts concede it is a "digestible sound bite" that is genuinely hard to rebut in equally few words.[^1]
- **The "we" defeats the angel reading.** Paul includes himself ("though *we*") and even a literal angel from heaven; the hosts note that if an angelic source automatically disqualifies a message, Paul is saying even an apostle could be wrong — proving the test is the *content* of the gospel, not the messenger.[^2] See [[Galatians 1-8 the Word We Limits the Curse]].
- **Paul defines "the gospel" himself.** Rather than leaving "gospel" ambiguous, Paul specifies it in [[1 Corinthians 15.1-4|1 Corinthians 15:1-4]]: Christ died for our sins, was buried, and rose the third day. The Book of Mormon ([[3 Nephi 11.10-11|3 Nephi 11:10-11]]) and a [[Joseph Smith]] statement affirm exactly this, so the "another gospel" charge misfires.[^3] See [[Definition of Gospel]] and [[Galatians 1-8 Latter-day Saints Preach Pauls Gospel]].
- **The skipped verse 6 concedes an early apostasy.** The hosts highlight [[Galatians 1.6|Galatians 1:6]] — "I marvel that ye are so soon removed" — arguing every Pauline epistle documents a rapid, early apostasy, which is "an admission of bankruptcy in cradle Christianity" and supports the LDS [[Great Apostasy and Need for Restoration|need for a Restoration]].[^4] See [[Galatians 1-6 Marvel Ye Are So Soon Removed Early Apostasy]].
- **Sola scriptura "muzzles God."** Reading "gospel" as "the closed Bible" means even God could say nothing new at the Second Coming — yet [[Revelation 14.6|Revelation 14:6]] foresees an angel bringing "the everlasting gospel." If Galatians forbids angelic gospel-bringing, John contradicts Paul; the only harmonization is to drop the Galatians attack.[^5] See [[Revelation 14-6 Angel With the Everlasting Gospel]] and [[Sola Scriptura Muzzles God]].
- **The "Amen, brother" apologetic.** The hosts' counter-sound-bite: agree enthusiastically that anyone preaching against Christ's resurrection is accursed, then point out Latter-day Saints emphatically affirm the resurrection (and even narrate what the risen Christ did next, in 3 Nephi), disarming the accuser and opening real conversation.[^6] See [[The Amen Brother Apologetic Strategy]].
