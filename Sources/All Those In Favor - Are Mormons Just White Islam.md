# All Those In Favor — Are Mormons Just White Islam?

A single-presenter monologue answering the recurring claim that Mormonism is "American Islam" or "white Islam." The host first notes the comparison is usually deployed inside a buried syllogism — *Islam is bad; Mormonism is like Islam; therefore Mormonism is bad* — and insists Islam is in fact "rich, beautiful, and complex," so the argument fails even on its own terms. He then (1) traces the analogy's history as Protestant polemic and orientalist pseudo-scholarship, and (2) walks through the theological gulf between the two faiths, concluding the label is a reductive, often prejudiced smear that misrepresents both religions.

## Summary

- **The comparison is an old polemical tactic, not neutral comparative religion.** Equating Joseph Smith with Muhammad began in Joseph's own lifetime: early American Protestants — who had themselves been likened to heretics by Catholics — turned the tables on Mormons by linking Joseph to Muhammad, a figure their culture already presumed a fraud. The move is guilt by association. See [[Joseph-Muhammad Comparison as Islamophobic Smear]]. [^1]
- **The orientalist "scholarship" was agenda-driven.** Eduard Meyer, a German Hegelian historian, treated Mormonism as a documented reincarnation of an under-documented Islam (a proxy to study), jamming the data into a Hegelian "spirit of the age" frame; the amateur sociologist–clergyman Hans Thimme used the comparison to brand both faiths Christian heresies that "undervalue human sinfulness." See [[The White Islam Comparison Springs From Agenda-Driven Orientalism]]. [^2]
- **The doctrines of God are opposites, not parallels.** The LDS Godhead — three distinct persons who organized uncreated matter, with the Father as "the god at the top of the council of gods" — would be "entirely blasphemous to Muslims," whose absolute *tawhid* ("there is no God but Allah") treats even classical Trinitarianism as polytheism. See [[The Godhead and Tawhid Are Theological Opposites Not Parallels]]. [^3]
- **Revelation, agency, and canon diverge.** Latter-day Saints hold an open canon with continuing revelation through living prophets and affirm libertarian free agency; Islam regards Muhammad as the seal of the prophets, the Quran as final and unchangeable, and stresses submission (*islam*) to God's will (*insha'Allah*). See [[Open Canon and Living Prophets vs the Seal of the Prophets]]. [^4]
- **Human destiny is the sharpest contrast: deification vs. servitude.** LDS theology teaches *apotheosis* — humans (uncreated eternal intelligences) can progress to "the fullness of the mind and power and glory of the Father"; Islam holds that humans becoming divine or sharing God's nature is "entirely absent," the goal being faithful servanthood rewarded with paradise. See [[Deification Versus Submission Is the Deepest Mormon-Islam Divide]]. [^5]
- **Salvation differs: a Messiah vs. mercy for submission.** Latter-day Saints are saved through the grace and merits of Christ the Messiah; Islam has no salvific Messiah figure — Allah saves the sufficiently devoted who submit and keep the five pillars. [^6]
- **The superficial similarities don't establish a shared identity.** Shared prophets-and-revelation, dietary codes (Word of Wisdom / halal), opposition to idolatry, communalism, and Abrahamic descent are either very broad religious commonalities or contain decisive internal differences; cherry-picking them while ignoring the theology is what makes the label misleading. [^7]
- **The deeper lesson is about bin-sorting people.** The host argues such comparisons let people drop others into "good bin / bad bin" and gloss the nuance; better interfaith dialogue takes each tradition at its own word. He even suggests classical theism is theologically *closer* to Islam than Mormonism is. See [[The Marriage Analogy for Interfaith Dialogue]]. [^8]
