A short [[Ether's Elephant]] video arguing that the "Book of Mormon is full of anachronisms" critique mislabels its own objects. Using the dictionary definition — an anachronism is "a thing belonging or appropriate to a period other than that in which it exists" — the video points out that the contested items (horses, steel, etc.) *did* exist in the relevant time period; the real question is whether they existed in the right **geography**. So they are "geographical inconsistencies," not anachronisms (the Abraham-Lincoln-with-a-cellphone image is a true anachronism; the Book of Mormon cases are not).

Having relabeled the critique, the video then weakens it on two fronts: (1) only ~2% of the Americas has been archaeologically examined, and "absence of evidence is not evidence of absence"; and (2) the Book of Mormon is a *translation* into King James-era English, so some terms may be modern renderings of unfamiliar ancient referents and "shouldn't be taken as literal as people take them" — especially since the book itself admits "if there are faults they are the mistakes of men."

## Summary

- **Most alleged anachronisms are geographical, not chronological.** By the dictionary definition, an anachronism is out of place *in time*; the Book of Mormon items "weren't out of place in time. They're out of place in geography," so "a more appropriate way to refer to these would be geographical inconsistencies."[^1] → [[Most Book of Mormon Anachronisms Are Geographical Not Chronological]]; cf. [[Book of Mormon Anachronisms Are a Collective Pattern - Reel]] and [[One Anachronism Does Not Disprove the Book of Mormon]].

- **The relabeling matters because geography is a far weaker objection.** A genuine anachronism (a cellphone in 1860) is decisive; a "wrong place" claim is contingent on incomplete data. The video concedes it is still "a criticism" but "a lot weaker than you might think."[^2] → [[Most Book of Mormon Anachronisms Are Geographical Not Chronological]].

- **Only ~2% of the Americas is excavated, and absence of evidence is not evidence of absence.** "Experts tell us that we've only looked at about 2% of the Americas... 98% is not discovered yet"; not finding (e.g.) elephant bones "doesn't mean there weren't elephants."[^3] → reuses the vault's [[Only Two Percent of the Americas Has Been Excavated]] and [[Absence of Evidence Is Not Evidence of Absence in New World Archaeology]].

- **It is a King James-era translation, so vocabulary may be non-literal.** The book "was translated... using the language of the King James Bible," so "some of these words shouldn't be taken as literal as people take them," and the title page's "mistakes of men" caveat applies.[^4] → connects to existing [[Translation Introduces Anachronism Even in Divine Translation]], [[Dynamic Translation Answers Book of Mormon Anachronism]], and [[Translation Renders an Ancient Assembly With a Familiar Word]].
