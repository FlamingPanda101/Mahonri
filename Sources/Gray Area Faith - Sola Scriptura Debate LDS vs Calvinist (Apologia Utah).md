# Gray Area Faith - Sola Scriptura Debate LDS vs Calvinist (Apologia Utah)

**Format:** Moderated formal debate | **Resolution:** "The Bible is the sole infallible rule of faith that makes modern revelation unnecessary." | **Affirmative:** Reverend Dr. [[Timothy J. Whitehead]] (Reformed Baptist) | **Negative:** [[Shiloh Lofgren]] (Latter-day Saint, Gray Area Faith) | **Venue:** Berean Presbyterian Church, hosted with Apologia Church Utah

A substantive, cordial debate mapping the Calvinist case *for* and the Latter-day Saint case *against* sola scriptura. Both speakers had coordinated beforehand to avoid strawmanning.

### Affirmative case ([[Timothy J. Whitehead]]) — four arguments
1. **Truth vs. knowledge.** Truth is infallible and from God (the *logos*); our interpretation is fallible. Sola scriptura protects this distinction by keeping one infallible standard. See [[The Truth-Knowledge Distinction Protects an Infallible Standard]].
2. **Everyone has a self-authenticating ultimate standard.** You measure everything by your ultimate standard and measure it by nothing; a *true* ultimate standard witnesses to itself, a *false* one self-destructs. See [[Every Worldview Has a Self-Authenticating Ultimate Standard]].
3. **The Bible is truth without mixture of error** — inspiration (2 Peter 1:20-21) separates revelation from fallible human interpretation; canon is self-attesting against the [[Deuteronomy 18.20-22|Deuteronomy 18]] infallibility standard. See [[Deuteronomy 18 Infallibility Standard Against New Revelation]] and [[Sola Scriptura - Scripture Self-Attests Its Authority]].
4. **All other authorities (science, courts, church) are fallible** and presuppose Scripture's preconditions for knowledge.

### Negative case ([[Shiloh Lofgren]]) — four arguments
1. **The Bible never teaches sola scriptura** (James White's own concession: it was not operative during revelation; [[2 Thessalonians 2.15|2 Thess 2:15]]; [[1 Corinthians 4.6|1 Cor 4:6]] would forbid receiving 2 Corinthians). See [[Sola Scriptura Could Not Have Been True During the Apostolic Age]].
2. **Believers must follow living representatives, not only the text** — the church is "the pillar and ground of the truth" ([[1 Timothy 3.15|1 Tim 3:15]]); Christ commissioned *people* ([[Luke 10.16|Luke 10:16]]); God "breathed on" the apostles and gave authority to bind/forgive ([[John 20.17|John 20:21-23]]); disputes settled by a council receiving the Spirit ([[Acts 15|Acts 15:28]]). See [[The Church Is the Pillar of Truth and Scripture Is Its Byproduct]] and [[God Breathed on the Apostles Not Only on the Scriptures]].
3. **Sola scriptura has not produced doctrinal clarity** even on first-rank issues (baptism, the Trinity — citing the Ligonier *State of Theology* survey and Rahner). See [[Perspicuity Fails the Hard-Cases Test]] and [[Protestant Disunity Is Evidence Against Perspicuity]].
4. **The Bible we possess is not infallible** — the Johannine Comma and long ending of Mark were read as Scripture for centuries; an infallible *autograph* the church no longer has cannot be the functioning rule. See [[An Infallible Autograph We No Longer Possess Cannot Be the Rule of Faith]].

### Whitehead's key rebuttals
- Cessation follows the *covenant* structure: revelation tracks covenants; the new covenant is "new and everlasting" (Hebrews 13), so no further canon is anticipated.
- Every cited case of "living representatives" was *written down as scripture*; Protestants have a category of Spirit-*guidance* that is not *inspiration*.
- The Deuteronomy-18 standard is **infallibility when speaking for God**; LDS prophets, held to it, made failed predictions, so the LDS had to *lower* the standard of scripture to keep prophets level with it.
