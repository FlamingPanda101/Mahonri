# The Counsel of Trent — 3 Reasons I'm Not Mormon

[[Trent Horn]] presents what he calls his three biggest reasons for rejecting the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints — a Catholic critique offered as a follow-up to his martyrdom-argument episode, where skeptics press the parallel between the apostles and [[Joseph Smith]].

## Summary
- **Reason 1 — "Mormonism rejects the existence of God":** because LDS theology makes God an exalted, embodied, once-mortal being ("henotheism"), Horn argues it forfeits the classical-theist God (pure actuality, simple, the uncaused cause) and so the atheist's "what created God?" objection bites [^1].
- **Reason 2 — "I need a map of the Book of Mormon and there isn't one":** unlike the Bible, whose sites scholars of all faiths can locate, the Book of Mormon has no agreed geography and no archaeological confirmation; he adds that the Book of Abraham is positive *evidence against* Joseph Smith [^2].
- **Reason 3 — no Great Apostasy:** Catholics and Mormons agree *sola scriptura* is false and that Christ founded one authoritative church, but Horn argues no New Testament or patristic text teaches a *total* apostasy [^3].
