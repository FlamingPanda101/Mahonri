# Ward Radio — The Creation Dilemma and the Three Facts About God

A Ward Radio livestream hosted by [[Cardon Ellis]], joined via Zoom by [[Hayden Carroll]] (Let's Talk with Hayden Carroll), [[Luke Hansen]] (Doctrine and Governance), and [[Ben Kaiser]]. Riding the wave of the online "Trinity vs Godhead" debate cycle (sparked by [[Jacob Hansen]]'s unreleased debate with God Logic and the impending [[James White]] vs Jacob Hansen debate on Calvinism), the panel unpacks Carroll's self-published book *The Creation Dilemma* and the closely related "three facts about God" predestination argument Luke Hansen deploys in street conversations. The stated through-line: *why does the Trinity-vs-Godhead argument actually matter for how people live?* — the answer being that creedal "perfect being" theology plus creation *ex nihilo* logically collapses into either Calvinist predestination or an "evil God," whereas LDS doctrine of co-eternal, consenting intelligences dissolves the problem.[^1]

## Summary
- **The central argument is an internal critique, not an external one.** Carroll insists he is not arguing "I'm right, so you're wrong"; he assumes the creedal worldview and shows it contains a contradiction — a true *dilemma* / catch-22: accept all of God's classical attributes and you get an un-good God, or revise an attribute and you retreat from the creedal God.[^2] See [[The Creation Dilemma Is an Internal Catch-22]], [[The First Moral Act Is Gods Choice to Create]].
- **Luke Hansen's "three facts about God" syllogism** — God (1) knows all of time, (2) is sovereign/all-powerful, (3) creates the universe from nothing — entails that you are predestined to heaven or hell; you can only escape by denying one of the three, and the LDS move is to deny #3 (creation *ex nihilo*).[^3] See [[The Three Facts About God Entail Predestination]], [[Foreknowledge Is Not Causation Objection]].
- **Calvinism is framed as the consistent creedal position.** The Calvinist "bites the bullet"; the non-Calvinist classical theist holds the same three attributes but recoils from the conclusion — which Carroll/Hansen take as proof the creeds got God's nature wrong, evidenced by the very existence of the Calvinism-vs-Arminian impasse.[^4] See [[Calvinism Is the Logical Conclusion of Classical Theism]], [[The Mary Problem for Catholic Theism]].
- **The button and six-child analogies** are intuition pumps: would you press a button (or have a sixth child) knowing the resulting person will freely reject Christ and end in eternal conscious torment, when you are under no obligation and lose nothing by abstaining? Carroll argues an all-good, all-loving God cannot.[^5] See [[The Six-Child Analogy for the Creation Dilemma]], [[The Judas Problem - Better Never to Have Been Born]].
- **Standard creedal "solves" are pre-rebutted in the book:** the free-will defense (the lava-pool / wood-chipper analogy — you would restrain a friend, so permission is not loving), the greater-good/utilitarian defense, the mystery defense, the defense of divine permission, and Molinism (middle knowledge still leaves exhaustive foreknowledge and predestination intact).[^6] See [[Free Will Defense Fails - The Lava Pool Analogy]], [[Molinism Does Not Solve Predestination]], [[Why Doesnt God Only Create the Saved]].
- **The LDS "solve" runs through three doctrines:** creation from pre-existing material/co-eternal intelligences rather than *ex nihilo*; a premortal life in which spirits *consented* to enter the creation cycle; and the claim that God may be *bound by eternal law* to admit any intelligence that wills to come — shifting moral responsibility off God.[^7] See [[LDS Premortal Consent Removes Gods Culpability]], [[God Found Spirits He Did Not Create - Book of Abraham Solve]].
- **Why the debate matters (Ellis's framing question):** the Trinity-vs-Godhead fight is worth having not to win the formulation (LDS and creedal Trinity are "99% the same") but to avoid its worst downstream implications — predestination and a class of "second-class children of God."[^8a] See [[Why the Trinity vs Godhead Debate Actually Matters]].
- **Why it matters pastorally (Ellis's frame):** Ellis prioritizes orthopraxy over orthodoxy and argues the danger of "dark" theologies (hard Calvinism, "Mormons are atheists" rhetoric, materialist atheism) is that they erode the conviction that every person is a child of God, removing the moral "seatbelt" against cruelty.[^8] See [[Orthopraxy over Orthodoxy]], [[Dark Theology Erodes the Child-of-God Seatbelt]].
- **LDS hell vs creedal hell.** The panel contrasts eternal conscious torment with the LDS tiered afterlife: "eternal damnation" is eternal because it is *God's* (D&C 19/76 logic), most who suffer eventually progress out, and only the sons of perdition in outer darkness face a final state whose nature Joseph Smith said only its inhabitants would know.[^9] See [[Eternal Damnation Is Gods Not Endless]], [[Outer Darkness and Whether Progression Ends]].
- **Foreknowledge, open theism, and prophecy.** Carroll rejects open theism (can't square it with fulfilled prophecy and the lost 116 pages "seatbelt" backup) but grants it would dissolve the dilemma; he affirms God knows the actual future without being timeless (D&C 130:7).[^10] See [[Open Theism as a Dilemma Solve]], [[The 116 Pages as Evidence of Foreknowledge]].
