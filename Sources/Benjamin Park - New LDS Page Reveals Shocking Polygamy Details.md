# New LDS Page Reveals Shocking Polygamy Details

A historian's reading of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints' newly released **Gospel Topics Question-and-Answer page on polygamy** (one of three new Q&A pages launched the same week, alongside Joseph Smith's character and Book of Mormon translation). [[Benjamin Park]] treats the page not as something to "tear apart" but as a **historical document** that reveals where the institution currently stands — what it now concedes and what it still strains to reconcile.

- **Core thesis:** The page attempts an "impossible task" — affirming faith in Joseph Smith as a divinely appointed prophet while honoring a historical record that does not fit modern notions of prophetic infallibility. The result is "strained and structured arguments."
- **What it concedes:** Park praises real institutional progress — admitting Joseph had 30+ plural wives (several teenagers), that **20–30% of Latter-day Saints** participated (vs. Gordon B. Hinckley's old "2–5%"), that leaders taught polygamy was necessary for exaltation, and that **post-1890 (post-Manifesto) polygamy** continued with leadership approval.
- **What it strains:** Park flags the page's weakest moves — retrojecting the D&C 132 revelation to "as early as the 1830s," the "reluctant polygamist" trope, the soft framing of Emma Smith as "deeply committed," and overstating women's agency in Utah plural marriage.

This is a **critical/academic** source, not an apologetic one. Its claims often run *against* the LDS-apologetic notes in this vault and are recorded here to represent the mainstream historian's voice.
