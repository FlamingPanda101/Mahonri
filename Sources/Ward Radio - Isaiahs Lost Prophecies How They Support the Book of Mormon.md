# Ward Radio — Isaiah's Lost Prophecies: How They Support the Book of Mormon

A Ward Radio episode in which host **[[Cardon Ellis]]**, with **[[David John Butler]]** (author of *In the Language of Adam*) and **[[Jonah Barnes]]** (promoting his book *Key to the Keystone*), argues that lost prophecies of Isaiah — surviving in pseudepigrapha like the **Ascension of Isaiah** and the **Gospel of Nicodemus** — were known to [[Nephi]] via the brass plates and thereby vindicate the Book of Mormon.

## Summary
- **The Ascension of Isaiah closes the "missing ending" of Isaiah.** Barnes presents the apocryphal Ascension of Isaiah (a rediscovered Latin manuscript, first English edition ~1912) as the account of Isaiah's martyrdom: he ascends in vision, sees a virgin conceive the Son of God who is crucified by "lawless men," declares it before the court, and is sawn in two inside a tree trunk for it. See [[Apocryphal Prophet-Martyrdoms Corroborate the Book of Mormons Slain Messianic Prophets]].[^1]
- **Nephi and Isaiah report the same vision.** [[2 Nephi 11.2-3|2 Nephi 11:2-3]] has Nephi say Isaiah "verily saw my Redeemer even as I have seen him," and Jacob too — which the panel reads as the same "modular" ascension experience ([[1 Nephi 11]] paralleling the Ascension of Isaiah "tick for tick"). See [[Nephi and Isaiah Shared the Same Ascension Vision]].[^2]
- **The Great Forsaken / Deuteronomistic reform.** The hosts frame a pre-Christian apostasy (~700-550 BC) in which scribes excised the suffering Messiah and the divine sonship; Lehi (~600 BC) carried a brass-plates Isaiah that escaped this editing. See [[The Great Forsaken - A Pre-Christian Apostasy of the Scribes]] and [[Deuteronomistic Reform Explains the Book of Mormon Theology]].[^3]
- **The "sting of death" is apocryphal Isaiah, not a Pauline anachronism.** Abinadi, Aaron, and Mormon use "the sting of death… swallowed up," which the Gospel of Nicodemus attributes to Isaiah — so Paul ([[1 Corinthians 15.55|1 Cor 15:55]]) was quoting Isaiah, not coining it. See [[The Sting of Death Is Apocryphal Isaiah from the Brass Plates Not a Pauline Anachronism]].[^4]
- **Method: date the idea, not the manuscript.** Confronted that the Ascension of Isaiah's manuscripts are late (Norelli dates the text ~AD 150-200), the panel argues the *idea* can be far older than the surviving paper, and that the Book of Mormon is itself a datum that should reshape how we date the pseudepigrapha. See [[Dating an Idea Differs From Dating Its Manuscript]].[^5]

## Notable framing / rhetoric
- The recurring thumbnail line: "for a hundred years Joseph Smith looked like an idiot… now he's vindicated because of the Ascension of Isaiah."[^1]
- "The brass plates predate — they're a purer form of the Old Testament."[^3]
- Heavy promotion of Scripture Notes (sponsor) and Barnes's forthcoming *Key to the Keystone*, which uses a "Q-source" triangulation to "reconstruct the brass plates."[^6]
