Ward Radio reaction episode in which host [[Cardon Ellis]], with [[Jonah Barnes]] and [[Brad Whitbeck]], hosts guest **Tember Harward** (an active Latter-day Saint who experiences same-sex attraction, is married to a woman in the temple, and founded **Beacon SSA Ministry**) to react to Harward's treatment in a **Jubilee Media** "Mormons vs. Ex-Mormons" video. The framing: ex-Mormon panelists (including [[John Dehlin]] and a host the transcript calls "Jillian") "badgered" Harward — the one gay man on the panel — when his thriving in the Church contradicted their narrative that LGBTQ Latter-day Saints are destined for divorce and despair.

## Summary
- **The "traitor" treatment.** Responding to a viewer comment that "the verbal assault on Timber was atrocious," Harward says someone "throwing a wrench" into the theory of how gay people *should* feel in the Church draws heat; he was treated as a traitor / "the Uncle Tom of the LGBT community" for supporting Church doctrine.[^1] See [[Mixed-Orientation Marriages and LGBTQ Latter-day Saints Show Higher Wellbeing]].
- **He chose the covenant path knowingly.** Harward came "from gay relationships" and married a woman because "I knew that is God's standard"; he still has challenges but "this is where I'm happiest," and is "not the basket case... gonna off myself tomorrow" their worldview predicts.[^2] See [[Mixed-Orientation Marriages and LGBTQ Latter-day Saints Show Higher Wellbeing]].
- **The wellbeing statistics.** The hosts cite (secondhand) data — attributed to Mike Goodman and Justin Dyer in *Deseret News*, and to Jeff Bennion / North Star — that mixed-orientation marriages in the Church last "twice as long" / have "half the divorce rate," and that LGBTQ LDS youth are "more than twice" as well-adjusted (mental health, sense of community) as their secular counterparts.[^3][^4] See [[Mixed-Orientation Marriages and LGBTQ Latter-day Saints Show Higher Wellbeing]].
- **The suicide-claim rebuttal.** Against [[John Dehlin]]'s claim of uniquely high LGBTQ-LDS youth suicide, Harward cites a *Deseret News* guest opinion ("Church and LGBT youth suicide: inaccurate claims that do more harm than good," Goodman & Dyer) drawing on **secular** studies that religious LGBT individuals fare better than non-religious ones. Harward adds his own history: two suicide attempts while living "a secular lifestyle," versus access to grace and "eternal worth" in the gospel now.[^5] See [[Dehlins Faulty-Pool Study Skews LDS LGBTQ Outcomes]].
- **Dehlin's "faulty study."** Harward and the hosts argue [[John Dehlin]]'s survey drew "from a pool of a bunch of people who basically think the same," yielding skewed outcomes, and contrast it with the larger, more balanced "Four Options" study by Tyler Mansfield (faithful, SSA, married to a woman) paired with Tyler Lefever (ex-Mormon, gay, pro-LGBT).[^6] See [[Dehlins Faulty-Pool Study Skews LDS LGBTQ Outcomes]].
- **Respect across disagreement.** Harward credits a respectful private conversation with a panelist ("Dylan") and a couple of respectful panelists ("Dylan," the woman "in the colorful sweater"), arguing the community needs to "vehemently disagree... but still respect each other's agency."[^7]
- **A betrayal of editing.** Harward (a self-described "Hollywood guy") says Jubilee recorded ~3 hours, agreed verbally the full footage would go to Patreon, refused to let him keep his own recording, and edited his cancer/faith-crisis analogy so it read as "your same-sex attraction is cancer." The hosts spend the back half on AI-art memes the Discord generated of the panel.[^8]
