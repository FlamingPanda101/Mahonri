# Glenn L Pace Memo — The Satanic Panic and False Memory

- **Frames the Satanic Panic as a recurrence of Salem-style hysteria.** The episode opens with the 1692 Salem witch trials — 19 hanged, one pressed to death (4% of a 500-person town) on unprovable "spectral evidence" from two young girls — and argues the 1980s satanic ritual abuse (SRA) panic, which later reached LDS Salt Lake City, is the same phenomenon: imprisonments and shattered families built on uncorroborated accusation [^1]. See [[Satanic Panic Is a Recurrence of Salem-Style Hysteria]].
- **Identifies the 1980 book *Michelle Remembers* as the spark.** Psychiatrist [[Lawrence Pazder]] and patient [[Michelle Smith]] used "recovered memory therapy" to surface lurid, never-corroborated SRA memories; the accusations collapsed under scrutiny (no missing persons, an 81-day ritual that left no trace, the only named abuser conveniently deceased) yet ignited a national panic amplified by talk shows [^2][^3][^4]. See [[Michelle Remembers Sparked the Satanic Panic]] and [[Recovered Memory Therapy Manufactures Abuse Memories]].
- **Uses the McMartin preschool trial as the panic's worst case.** Suggestive interviewing by unlicensed therapist [[Kathleen McFarlane]] (anatomically correct dolls, telling children what others "said") produced impossible stories — sharks, levitation, tunnels — and the longest, most expensive criminal trial in U.S. history (7 years, ~$15M, zero convictions) from an accusation by a diagnosed paranoid schizophrenic, [[Judy Johnson]] [^5][^6][^7]. See [[McMartin Preschool Trial Produced No Convictions]] and [[Suggestive Interviewing Induces False Allegations]].
- **Grounds the debunking in false-memory science.** [[Elizabeth Loftus]] implanted false "lost in the mall" memories; [[Julia Shaw]] compares false memory to AI hallucination; [[David Rubin]]'s 9/11 flashbulb study and the "Bush saw the first plane live" / Mandela-effect cases show vivid, confident memories can be wholly false — making children's coached testimony unreliable [^8][^9][^10][^11]. See [[False Memory Syndrome Explains the SRA Accusations]] and [[Flashbulb Memories Are Reconstructed Not Recorded]].
- **Catalogs the panic's incentives and scale.** Roughly 12,000 unsubstantiated SRA cases were brought forward; therapists, talk shows, book deals, and police-training seminars (Pazder trained departments; *Michelle Remembers* was used as social-worker training material) created financial and reputational momentum that made dropping cases an embarrassment — a structure that later found "fertile soil" among the Latter-day Saints in Utah [^12][^13][^14]. See [[The Satanic Panic Was Driven by Financial and Reputational Incentives]] and [[The Satanic Panic Reached the LDS Church in Utah]].
