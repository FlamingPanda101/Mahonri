# Ruslan KD - Pressing a Mormon Apologist on if LDS Are Christians

Evangelical YouTuber [[Ruslan KD]] hosts Latter-day Saint apologist [[Jacob Hansen]] (Thoughtful Faith) for a long, deliberately non-combative conversation pressing the question "Are Mormons Christian?" — promoting Hansen's upcoming Trinity debate with GodLogic at the Bless God Summit. Ruslan presses on the definition of "Christian," creedal vs. non-creedal identity, the ontology of God, whether Latter-day Saints follow "the same Jesus," the gospel and how one is saved, and then walks through a kitchen-sink list of historical challenges (Book of Abraham, Kinderhook plates, the gold-plate witnesses, polygamy, Adam-God, the priesthood-and-race ban). Hansen's recurring frame: he is "a Christian first," takes the *prophetic tradition* as reliable-but-not-infallible, and is comparison-shopping the Protestant, Catholic, Orthodox, and Latter-day Saint "models" of the Jesus of the New Testament.

This source heavily overlaps existing notes from the [[Allie Beth Stuckey - Are Mormons Christians - Jacob Hansen Debate]] and [[Gavin Ortlund - Protestant and Mormon Discuss the Trinity]] dialogues; only the arguments genuinely new to this conversation are extracted below.

## Summary

- **Christian, not creedal Christian.** Hansen runs his standard move — Latter-day Saints follow the Jesus of the New Testament but reject the post-biblical creeds (Nicaea, Constantinople, the Athanasian Creed), locating the break at consubstantiality. See [[Latter-day Saints Are Christians Just Not Creedal Christians]], [[Three Persons Share One Nature Not One Being]].
- **Ontology, not biography, is the fault line.** Ruslan grants the LDS Jesus shares biographical detail but insists the creator/creation distinction ([[Romans 1.16|Romans 1]], [[Hebrews 1.3|Hebrews 1]]) is where they truly deviate; Hansen counters with theosis and the acorn/oak analogy — no ontological gulf, "same species" but differing in glory. See [[Acorn and Oak Analogy for Theosis]], [[Humans Are the Same Kind as God (LDS Ontology)]], [[God and Man Are the Same Species]], [[Worshiping the Right Jesus - Created vs Uncreated]].
- **Reliable, not infallible prophets.** Hansen takes the whole "prophetic tradition" as a collective witness; a lone prophet (Brigham Young on polygamy-for-exaltation or [[Adam-God Doctrine - What Brigham Young Taught|Adam-God]]) who contradicts the 99 is treated as the outlier. See [[Reliability Not Infallibility Standard]], [[Fallible But Reliable - Why Authority Still Counts]], [[Collective Witness Epistemology]], [[Outliers Against the Collective Prophetic Witness May Be Uninspired]].
- **The comparison-shopping posture.** Hansen's distinctive epistemic stance: he constantly weighs Latter-day Saints against the other Christian "models" because he is evaluating which best fits the Jesus of the New Testament — and says he would *convert* if convinced the Trinity were true. See [[Comparing Models to Find the Most Coherent Jesus]].
- **Joseph Smith historical challenges.** Witnesses' "spiritual eyes" ([[Spiritual Eyes Described Real Things in the 1800s]], [[Three vs Eight Witnesses Different Kinds of Experience]]), Kinderhook ([[Joseph Smith Did Not Translate the Kinderhook Plates by Revelation]]), and the Book of Abraham — for which Hansen adds the missing-papyri-burned defense. See [[Most of the Book of Abraham Papyri Burned in the Chicago Fire]].
- **The Quran comparison rebutted.** The "Joseph is just like Muhammad" parallel fails on witnesses and dating. See [[Muhammad Had No Corroborating Witnesses or Physical Artifacts]], [[The Quran Has No Contemporary Records Only Sources Centuries Later]].
- **Polygamy as sealing, not sex.** Ceilings ≠ marriages; zero children outside Emma; the Book of Mormon makes monogamy the norm. See [[Plural Marriage Was Sealing Not Sex]], [[Catholic Marriage History Undercuts the Polygamy Attack]].
- **The priesthood-and-race ban as Hamitic-hypothesis tradition, not revelation.** See [[The Priesthood Ban Was the Development Not Its Reversal]], [[Joseph Smith Ignored Race-Based Scriptural Distinctions]].
- **"Cult" deducts IQ points.** Hansen argues "cult" just means "a religion I think is weird" and would apply to the earliest Christians. See [[Cult - A Contaminated Pejorative Term]], [[Make the Accuser Define Cult]].
- **Ruslan's crypto-evangelical remnant.** Ruslan reports a cultural remnant of Latter-day Saints who are functionally evangelical but cannot renounce LDS identity; Hansen replies this presupposes salvation is right-theology. See [[A Crypto-Evangelical Remnant Within the LDS Church]].
- **Alex O'Connor's trajectory.** Hansen reads O'Connor as an agnostic atheist who matured past "young-punk" New Atheism — a model for taking Latter-day Saints seriously rather than caricaturing them. See [[Alex O'Connor]].
