# InspiringPhilosophy - The Case for Free Will

**Channel:** InspiringPhilosophy | **Presenter:** [[Inspiring Philosophy|Michael Jones]]
**Format:** Scripted apologetics video with cited quotations | **Topic:** Free will vs. determinism

## Summary

[[Inspiring Philosophy|Michael Jones]] answers the determinist case — framed
through [[Sam Harris]]'s *Free Will* — in three moves. **First**, he argues
Harris commits a *bait and switch*: drawing on [[Alvin Plantinga]]'s article,
Jones distinguishes the **maximal autonomy** Harris refutes (freely choosing
one's own genes, past, and character) from the **ordinary free will** required
for moral responsibility, which no advocate ever defined as maximal autonomy
(see [[Maximal Autonomy Is Not Free Will - Plantingas Bait and Switch]]).
**Second**, he contends the determinist argument shows only the *possibility* of
determinism, not a proven causal link — "if one has a reason for his actions,
that doesn't automatically mean that reason caused his action" — and offers
[[Ayn Rand]]'s positive account of freedom as the choice of what to *focus the
mind* on (see [[A Reason for an Action Is Not Proof It Was Caused]] and
[[Free Will Is the Choice to Focus the Mind - Ayn Rands Account]]). **Third**,
he makes his "most crucial" empirical move: the **Conway–Kochen Free Will
Theorem** plus the experimental defeat of local hidden variables ([[Anton Zeilinger]], Bell's inequality, Brukner–Kofler) show the quantum level is
genuinely indeterministic, so "no one can determine your future events given
your past history" (see [[The Conway-Kochen Free Will Theorem - Indeterminism Preserves Freedom]]).

Notably, Jones' quantum argument runs *opposite* to Harris's claim that
indeterminacy would *destroy* agency: where Harris says randomness is no
freedom, Jones treats quantum indeterminism as the precondition that *preserves*
freedom — a tension the vault tracks across the two notes.

## References

1. [[Maximal Autonomy Is Not Free Will - Plantingas Bait and Switch]] — the
   centerpiece: Harris refutes "maximal autonomy" (freely choosing your own
   character via infinite prior acts) and falsely advertises it as a refutation
   of the ordinary free will moral responsibility needs.
2. [[A Reason for an Action Is Not Proof It Was Caused]] — the determinist
   argument establishes only possibility; having a reason for an action does not
   show the reason *caused* it.
3. [[Free Will Is the Choice to Focus the Mind - Ayn Rands Account]] — Rand's
   positive thesis: freedom is not choosing our knowledge or circumstances but
   controlling which facts and ideas the mind focuses on.
4. [[The Conway-Kochen Free Will Theorem - Indeterminism Preserves Freedom]] —
   the empirical capstone: the Free Will Theorem and the experimental violation
   of Bell's inequality imply an indeterministic universe in which the future is
   not fixed.
5. [[The Illusion of Free Will - Harris's Hard Determinism]] — the opposing
   source being answered; note especially Harris's counter-claim that quantum
   randomness would *obliterate* agency rather than ground it.
