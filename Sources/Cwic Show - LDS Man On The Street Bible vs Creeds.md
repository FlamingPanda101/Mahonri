# Cwic Show — LDS Man On The Street: Bible vs Creeds

A Cwic Show interview in which host [[Greg Matsen]] (Cwic Media) talks with LDS campus apologist [[Hayden Carroll]] about Carroll's [[Charlie Kirk]]/Steven-Crowder-style "Let's Talk" table at Arizona State University, where he provokes unscripted conversations with provocative banners (atheism, trinitarianism, and a forthcoming "I'm a Mormon — ask me anything").[^1] The theological spine of the conversation is **whether the creeds match the Bible** — Matsen and Carroll argue the Nicene Trinity is a post-biblical, Hellenized imposition that creedal Christians prioritize over the plain text, even as they profess *sola scriptura*.[^2] The video opens with a long sponsor read for a Nile cruise and ranges across fine-tuning, free will, and missionary strategy before settling on the creeds.

## Summary
- **Banners and reception.** Carroll sets up "Let's Talk" tables (modeled on Crowder's "Change My Mind") with three provocative banners; he reports ~99% friendly engagement and four-to-eight substantive conversations per session, releasing videos twice a week.[^1] His atheism material runs the [[Fine-Tuning and the Teleological Argument from Alma 30|fine-tuning argument]] and the charge that consistent atheism forfeits free will, morality, consciousness, and purpose — see [[Free Will - Atheist Determinism vs LDS Co-Eternality]] and [[Consciousness and First Cause Problem for Atheism]].[^3]
- **Sola scriptura is borrowed from the creeds.** Matsen's framing claim: a Protestant's "the Bible is the supreme, infallible authority" structure "actually comes from the creeds" — the canon and its supremacy were settled by the very councils the creed-affirmer claims to bypass.[^4] Carroll fairly steelmans the reply: Protestants don't say the creeds override Scripture; they say the creeds *align* with it, the Bible "gets the final say," and a wrong creed "should be changed."[^5] His counter is that in practice they privilege the creedal interpretation over a plain reading — see [[Creeds Get the Final Say in Practice Not the Bible]] and the broader [[Sola Scriptura Arose to Wrest Authority From the Church|sola scriptura]] / [[The Three Problems with Sola Scriptura - Interpretation Logic Canon|interpretation-logic-canon]] cluster.
- **Creedal identity gates "Christian."** Matsen presses the diagnostic that a mainstream Christian's identity rests primarily on the creeds, not the Bible — "you and I aren't Christian because we don't believe in the Trinity; that's the number one issue."[^6] Cf. [[Latter-day Saints Are Christians Just Not Creedal Christians]] and [[Creedalism Replaced Relationship With Theology]].
- **Homoousios is not in the text.** Carroll's signature challenge to Trinitarians: "show me where consubstantiation — *homoousios*, the same substance — is taught in the biblical text." When they cite "the Father, the Son, and the Spirit are one," he answers that the word imposed centuries later is not the word in the text, and that in [[John 17.20-23|John 17]] Jesus prays his disciples "be one as we are one" without folding them into the divine substance.[^7] See [[John 17 Oneness Is Indwelling Not Consubstantiality]], [[Consubstantial Is a Mickey Mouse English Translation of Homoousios]], and [[The Trinity and Consubstantiality Are Not in the Bible]].
- **Hellenization and the logos.** Both argue the Trinity is "the Hellenization of Christianity" — early fathers put on "Greek glasses," appealing to "the God of [[Aristotle]]" (a phrase Carroll attributes to [[Jacob Hansen]]) and importing the philosophical *logos* (traced back to Heraclitus, 6th c. BC) onto John's very different *logos*, yielding the "perfect-being theology" of the 4th–5th centuries and [[Plotinus|Neoplatonism]].[^8] See [[Hellenization Was Not Corruption of an Original Pure Church]], [[Trinity Built to Satisfy Greco-Roman Philosophy]], [[John 1-3 - The Logos Is the Intermediate Agent of Creation Not the Originator]], and [[Romans 3.2 Says Oracles Logia Not Words Logos]].
- **The ecumenical hazard.** Carroll raises whether LDS bridge-building toward creedal Christianity risks "losing your back foot," invoking that Christ called the creeds an abomination — see [[The Olive Branch Risks Losing Your Back Foot - The Ecumenical Hazard]], against [[The Creeds Were an Abomination Meant Sectarian Dogmatism Not the Ecumenical Creeds]] and [[Ecumenical Foundation - Borrow Non-LDS Christian Resources for Levels One and Two]].
- **Missionary method.** Carroll finds that leading at the foundational "level one" (is there truth? is there order?) before naming distinctives works best, hands out five or six Books of Mormon a day, and reports that sharing LDS doctrine (e.g., an [[Eight Is a Developmental Threshold of Moral Accountability|age of accountability]] alternative to [[Original Sin Was a Late Western Development|original sin]]) resonates with theologically uncommitted hearers. He frames this with [[Jacob Hansen]]'s "pyramid of belief."[^9]
