# Lets Get Real with Stephen Jones - LDS Anxiety Expert Why Church Members Still Feel Unworthy (Kawika Allen E0036)

**Format:** Podcast interview | **Host:** Stephen Jones | **Guest:** Dr. [[Kawika Allen]] (counseling psychology, Brigham Young University) | **Topic:** why faithful Latter-day Saints feel "unworthy" — scrupulosity, maladaptive perfectionism, and grace

[[Kawika Allen]] reframes the experience of devout Latter-day Saints who "still feel broken." He argues that **religious perfectionism is at root an anxiety disorder** expressed religiously — clinically, **scrupulosity** (religious OCD) — and that **legalism** (earning God's love purely through works) and **maladaptive perfectionism** drive the cycle of feeling never "good enough." The antidote is not lower standards but a non-contingent, grace-anchored identity: *staying with Christ* in the very moment of distress rather than waiting to "qualify."

- "Broken" is the wrong frame; mortality is a learning process, not a verdict — the issue is misreading "be ye therefore perfect" without the "plan of mercy and compassion" on the *journey* of becoming perfect.[^1] See [[Maladaptive Perfectionism Leaves Mercy Out of Becoming Perfect]].
- Scrupulosity and perfectionism are "at its core an anxiety disorder" (DSM-framed), manifested religiously as repeated reassurance behaviors (texting the bishop, repeated temple visits).[^2] See [[Scrupulosity Is an Anxiety Disorder Expressed Religiously]].
- **Legalism**: people "feel that they need to earn God's love through purely works," forgetting faith, mercy, and compassion — "I have to read scriptures for three hours a day ... then he will love me."[^3] See [[Legalism Tries to Earn Gods Love Through Works Alone]].
- Worthiness is wrongly attached to *behaviors* rather than *identity* as a child of God; the core belief "I'm not good enough," repeated from childhood, fuses with identity.[^4] Cf. [[Self-Worth Decoupled From Performance Combats Perfectionism]].
- Guilt (Christ-directed, corrective, healthy) is distinct from shame (self-directed, "I am worthless," detrimental).[^5] See [[Guilt Is Corrective but Shame Attacks Identity]].
- The truth is not that the church or self must *look* perfect; "the truth is that people aren't perfect" — you do not have to be perfect to reside in truth.[^6]
- The Spirit is better modeled as a steady **furnace** than an intermittent **fire**: expecting a constant emotional "burning" sets people up to conclude they have lost the Spirit.[^7] See [[The Spirit Is a Steady Furnace Not an Intermittent Fire]].
- The intervention: in the moment of the scrupulous thought, **stay with Christ** — call on his name, bear testimony to yourself, "remember" — rather than waiting to be clean enough first.[^8] See [[Stay With Christ in the Moment of Scrupulosity]].
