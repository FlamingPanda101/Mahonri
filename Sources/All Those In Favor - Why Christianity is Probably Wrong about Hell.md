# Why Christianity is Probably Wrong about Hell

An LDS apologetics video arguing that the traditional Christian doctrine of
**eternal conscious torment** (ECT) is irreconcilable with a perfectly loving
and just God — especially when paired with the doctrine of **creation from
nothing** (*creatio ex nihilo*). The video critiques five common Christian
defenses of hell and presents the Latter-day Saint conception of the afterlife
(preexistent souls, degrees of glory, post-mortal progression) as a coherent
alternative.

## The Core Argument

God as the sole uncreated being who creates everything from nothing and
sustains all things in existence bears **ultimate responsibility** for the
attributes and fates of his creatures. If he creates souls he knows will
end up in eternal torment — and could have created them differently —
then he cannot be perfectly loving or perfectly just.

## Five Objections Addressed (and Refuted)

1. **Appeal to Sinfulness** — "We all deserve hell" → fails on proportionality
2. **Wind-Up-and-Go / Watchmaker Analogy** — God winds the universe and steps
   back → fails because God creates AND sustains all materials and laws
3. **Appeal to the Fall** — Adam's fault, not God's → fails because God still
   perpetually creates depraved souls from nothing
4. **Free Will Defense** — Hell is locked from the inside → fails given God's
   foreknowledge + creation from nothing + parental analogy
5. **For God's Glory** — Eternal damnation displays divine justice → morally
   repugnant; glory shouldn't be contingent on external outcomes

## Key Sources Cited

- **Jonathan Kvanvig**: Hell as "paradigm of gratuitous suffering" — no future
  good compensates
- **David Bentley Hart**: Universalist critique; creation ex nihilo implies
  God's moral nature determines creation's destiny
- **St. Isaac of Nineveh**: "It is not the way of a compassionate maker to
  create rational beings in order to deliver them over to unending affliction"
- **Gregory of Nissa**: *Creatio ex nihilo* is not merely ontological but
  moral
- **John Calvin**: Preordained souls for damnation "for his own glory"
- **Thomas Aquinas**: Saints behold the suffering of the damned for greater
  felicity
- **C.S. Lewis**: "The doors of hell are locked from the inside" (free will)

## LDS Alternative Presented

On the LDS view, souls preexist in a primitive state; God doesn't create them
*ex nihilo* as depraved. Mortality is a freely accepted plan for growth.
After death, all receive a **degree of glory** matching their willingness and
capacity — no binary heaven/hell, no eternal conscious torment, and
opportunity for post-mortal progression and reconciliation.

The video explicitly excludes **universalist Christians** from the critique,
noting that if everyone is saved, the problem doesn't apply. Also excludes
more revisionist/reduced conceptions of hell (e.g., annihilationism).
