# The Stick of Joseph — A New Way of Viewing Lehi's Journey in the Book of Mormon

A conversation with [[Dave Butler|David Butler]] and [[Mike Day]] (bonus content for chapter 9 of Butler's *In the Language of Adam*) arguing that [[Lehi]]'s wilderness itinerary in 1 Nephi is patterned specifically on the **Tabernacle** — not Solomon's Temple — and that the named travel-stops are Hebrew puns marking the courtyard, the door, the holy place, and the holy of holies. The reading extends the vault's [[Shazer Names the Fine-Twined-Linen Door of the Tabernacle|Shazer]], [[Lehi's Dream Encodes the Three Temple Rooms by Hebrew Name-Puns|Lehi's-dream]], and [[The Tabernacle Is a Retrojection of the Temple|tabernacle-retrojection]] cluster, and adds a fresh death-and-rebirth reading of the fainting episodes in Alma.

## Summary

- **Eden means "Bountiful" (Ziony Zevit).** Butler reports that non-LDS scholar Ziony Zevit's *What Really Happened in the Garden of Eden* translates the name **Eden** as "bountiful" — exactly the name Lehi's party gives the journey's end ([[1 Nephi 17.1|1 Nephi 17]]). Since Eden is the holy of holies, a journey ending in a place named **Bountiful/Eden** is a temple journey. See [[Eden Means Bountiful So Lehis Journey Ends in the Holy of Holies]].[^1]
- **The whole itinerary maps the Tabernacle.** Four named stops: **Valley of Lemuel** (with the river of Laman) = the **courtyard** (Lehi builds an altar and offers sacrifice three times — courtyard activity); **Shazer** = the **twined-linen door**; the long middle stretch (broken bow, Ishmael's death at Nahom) = the **holy place**; **Bountiful** = **Eden / the holy of holies**. See [[Lehis Journey Maps the Tabernacle Not Solomons Temple]].[^2][^3]
- **Why the Tabernacle, not the Temple.** Lehi's dream shows the *temple* corrupted (seized by "greedy mockers"), so the subliminal message is that the temple goes *with* him — a mobile temple, i.e. the Tabernacle. Butler also leans on scholarship that the Tabernacle description is closer to the actual first temple than Kings/Chronicles (which exaggerate). Coheres with [[The Tabernacle Is a Retrojection of the Temple]].[^4]
- **Shazer = *mashzar*, the fine-twined-linen door.** "Nothing happens" at Shazer (arrive, hunt, leave over two verses) because its only job is to be the *named door*: the root means "to twist/twine," and its Exodus form *mashzar* ("fine twined linen," *shesh mashzar*, 21× in Exodus) describes the tabernacle hangings, priestly garments, veil, and the entry screen. Already documented at [[Shazer Names the Fine-Twined-Linen Door of the Tabernacle]].[^5]
- **Nahom = the room of mourning and comfort.** The name **Nahom** plays on roots meaning to mourn / be comforted / repent / groan — and the death and burial of Ishmael at Nahom (and the broken-bow episode) enact mourning → murmuring → repentance → comfort, each ending with the Lord *providing food* (a feast). Butler ties **comfort** (via LeGrand Baker & Stephen Ricks' *Who Shall Ascend to the Hill of the Lord?*) to Second Isaiah's "comfort ye" as an *empowerment* — being raised from the netherworld and made a king/priest, not a pat on the head. See [[Nahom Is the Tabernacle Room of Mourning and Comfort]]; cf. [[Bountiful Lies Due East of Nahom]].[^6][^7]
- **The Alma fainting episodes are ritual death-and-rebirth.** The repeated pattern in Alma 17-36 (Lamoni, Lamoni's wife, Lamoni's father, Ammon, Alma) — taught the same four things (creation/Adam, the fall, the plan of redemption, the coming of Christ), then crying for mercy, fainting, being "taken for dead," seeing Jesus, and rising — is read not as ecstatic one-offs but as an **ordinance**: ritual death and rebirth with the spiritual gift of *hope*, having seen the Lord, brought to as the Lord descends, clothed in the robe of righteousness ([[2 Nephi 9.14|2 Nephi 9]]), and brought to the feast. See [[The Alma Fainting Episodes Are a Ritual Death and Rebirth Ordinance]].[^8][^9]
- **The veil and the empty quarter.** Between Nahom and Bountiful lies the **Rub' al Khali** ("the empty quarter," the largest sand desert) — "that quarter where there never had man been" (the Jaredites, Ether 2) — read as the *barrier/veil* through which one "sojourns" before entering the holy of holies. The verb *sojourn* (1 Nephi 17) and the Lord "providing means" mark the crossing. Coheres with [[Bountiful and Khor Kharfot Match the Book of Mormon]].[^10]
- **The holy of holies is "the room of the bee."** Butler and Day connect honey/bees to the *debir*: the bee is **Deborah** (*d'vorah*), and **Deborah** in Judges 4 is a prophetess who sits under a tree named Deborah on a mountain, where people approach her for judgment — mirroring Jesus on the mount (Matthew 5-7). The *debir* is therefore "the place of the bee"; manna tasted like honey and was kept in the ark; the Jaredites carry **deseret** (the honeybee) and "[[Ether 2.3|seeds of every kind]]." See [[The Holy of Holies Is the Room of the Bee Debir and Deborah]].[^11][^12]
- **Bountiful contains the holy-of-holies furniture.** In Bountiful Nephi meets God on a mountain, builds an **ark** (the ship of "curious workmanship," directions given on a mountain like the ark), puts **honey** (manna, Hebrews 9:4) and the **brass plates** (the law, like the tablets in the ark) into it, references the **Nehushtan** and the **rock/water of the Exodus** — supplying the rock, water, serpent, tree, and fruit of the *debir*. Coheres with [[The Nehushtan Is a Healing Divine Serpent the Nephites and Early Christians Valued]].[^13][^14]
- **The "so what": the temple is portable and cosmic.** Lehi expelled from the Zoramite temple (Alma 31-33) is not slowed, "because wherever he goes there is a tabernacle" — the temple "is not really about the temple... it's in you." The hosts' broader payoff: the Book of Mormon is "written by temple initiates for temple initiates," its depth is itself evidence against Joseph Smith fabricating it (cf. Solomon Spalding's shallow *Manuscript Found*), and reading it "as a visionary person" is the best temple prep. Coheres with [[The Book of Mormon Already Contains the Endowment]] and [[Read the Book of Mormon as a Translated Ancient Text]].[^15][^16]
