# Ward Radio — The Fever Breaks

Part 4 (the finale) of the Ward Radio *Glenn L. Pace Memo* series. Having argued the memo was unoriginal ([[Ward Radio - The Glenn L Pace Memo Debunked]]), this episode asks why the panic *persisted* in Utah longer than anywhere else, and then narrates how the national hysteria collapsed as its star therapists were exposed.

## Summary

- **Why Utah held on: anti-Mormonism fused the warring camps.** Everywhere else, panicking therapists were checked by skeptical rationalists; in Utah the accused VIPs *were* LDS leaders, so anti-Mormon evangelicals and anti-Mormon atheists "fought side by side" and the usual skepticism evaporated. Historian [[Massimo Introvigne]] is quoted: "rationalist and post-rationalist counter-Mormons did not divide along predictable lines." [^1][^2] See [[Anti-Mormonism United Skeptics and Evangelicals Against the LDS Church]].
- **The skeptic who became a believer.** Governor's task-force co-chair [[Noemi Mattis]] — a self-described "New York humanist Jew and quintessential skeptic" — flipped to defending the claims, attributing missing bodies to complicit "doctors and morticians" and asserting ritual abuse is known through "the cumulative experience of many therapists." [^3][^4] See [[Noemi Mattis Turned From Skeptic to True Believer on the Ritual Abuse Task Force]].
- **The "breeder" myth patched the missing-evidence hole.** Asked where the sacrificed babies' birth certificates were, [[Lauren Stratford]] claimed in *The Satanic Underground* to have been a ritual "breeder" whose babies were never registered — and she was later exposed as a lifelong serial fabricator (never pregnant, 40+ hospitalizations, three successive invented identities including a fake Holocaust survivor). [^5][^6] See [[The Breeder Myth Was Invented to Explain Missing Bodies]] and [[Lauren Stratford Was a Serial Fabricator of Victim Identities]].
- **The therapists fell one by one.** [[Bennett Brown]] settled abuse-implantation suits for $10.6M and $7.5M and lost his license; [[Barbara Snow]] was charged by Utah DOPL in 2007 with implanting false memories and had her license suspended; even [[Lawrence Pazder]], who coined "satanic ritual abuse," conceded in 1994 the memories were "more an expression of … violation … than actual accounts." [^7][^8] See [[Leading SRA Therapists Were Discredited Sued or Delicensed]].
- **The science caught up — and named the real harm.** A 1996 National Institute of Justice DNA study traced 90% of studied wrongful convictions to false-memory eyewitness IDs; 200+ Cook County confessions were thrown out as suggestion-induced; the AMA told therapists to record interviews. The real victims were the McMartins, Hadfields, Burnhams, the Yost family, and the defamed Church — culminating in accusations later aimed at church president [[Russell M. Nelson]] himself. [^9][^10] See [[False Memory Reports Caused Documented Wrongful Convictions]].
