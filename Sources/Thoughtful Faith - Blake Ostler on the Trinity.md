# Thoughtful Faith — Blake Ostler on the Trinity

[[Jacob Hansen]] interviews Latter-day Saint philosopher [[Blake Ostler]] in preparation for Hansen's debate with the Trinitarian YouTuber [[Avery (God Logic)|Avery]] of the channel *God Logic*. Hansen role-plays the Trinitarian opening arguments and pushes back throughout; Ostler supplies a systematic LDS/social-trinitarian rebuttal rooted in Ancient Near Eastern context, kenotic Christology, and analytic philosophy of religion. The throughline: classical Trinitarianism reads a Greek metaphysical "monotheism" *back into* texts whose thought-world was a divine council, name-bearing agency, and honor/shame adoption — an [[Isogesis - Reading Metaphysical Monotheism Back Into the Text|illicit isogesis]] that is both unscriptural and logically incoherent.

- **Negation texts (Isaiah 43–46) are ANE taunts of incomparability, not metaphysical monotheism.** Ostler argues the very same "I am, and there is none else" formulas were spoken by [[Cyrus the Great|Persian kings]] like Xerxes who *knew* other kings existed; they assert preeminence and demand sole allegiance (worship of another god = treason), not the non-existence of other gods. Even granting one God, that would not yield three persons in one substance. [^1][^2] See [[Babylon Taunt Formula Shows Negation Is a Boast of Superiority]], [[Negation Texts Assert Incomparability Not Non-Existence]], [[Worship of Another God Was Political Treason Not Metaphysical Error]].
- **Yahweh is the Father, who gave the Son the divine name by adoption** — not a "multi-personal Yahweh." Ostler grounds Jesus' use of the divine name and "I am" sayings in the [[Messenger Shaliach Tradition Explains Bearing the Divine Name|shaliach (messenger) tradition]] and ANE adoption, where the heir inherits everything including the benefactor's very name and authority. The Son bears *Yah* but never *El Elyon* (Most High), a name unique to the Father. [^3][^4] See [[Yahweh Is the Father Who Gave the Son the Divine Name by Adoption]], [[The Son Bears Yah but Never El Elyon]], [[The Father and Son Distinct - Even Trinitarians Deny Jesus Is the Father]].
- **The classical Trinity is logically incoherent — it cannot count to three.** If Yahweh is the Father, Yahweh is the Son, and the Son is distinct from the Father, the three claims are inconsistent: identity yields modalism, distinctness yields polytheism, and "one substance" only escapes by making *substance* equivocal (Aristotle's substance would yield *three*). The Trinity is a [[Trinity Is a Scholarly Creation Not a Revealed Doctrine|reasoned scholarly construct]], not a revelation, and is arguably contrary to reason. [^5][^6] See [[The Trinity Cannot Count to Three]], [[Substance Is Equivocal in the Trinity]], [[Latin Trinity and Eastern Trinity Both Fail]].
- **LDS Christology is kenotic and relational: the Son's divinity is real but dynamic.** Christ emptied himself ([[Philippians 2.9-11|Philippians 2]]) and held the divine properties "in potentiality" while mortal, dependent on the Father for knowledge, power, and re-exaltation. Divine properties are dynamic and unbounded — "like a baseball game, not a golf game" — actuated by [[Co-inherence as Mixing Light to Form the Godhead|co-inherence]] (mutual indwelling) in loving relationship. There is [[No Diminished Divinity Despite Subordination|no diminished divinity]]: the Son has every property necessary to be fully divine. [^7][^8] See [[Kenotic Christology - Divine Properties in Potentiality]], [[Divine Properties Are Dynamic Like Baseball Not Golf]], [[Divinity Requires Loving Relationship]].
- **LDS "modalistic-sounding" scriptures resolve in context, and worship of the Son is warranted.** Mosiah 15 and Ether 3 explain *why* Christ is "the Father and the Son" (will swallowed up in the Father; begotten of the Father in the flesh); these read no more modalistically than John's "he who has seen me has seen the Father." Worship goes to the Son — unlike the rebuking [[The Angel in Revelation Who Refuses Worship|angel of Revelation]] — because the Father has exalted him and the persons mutually indwell. [^9][^10] See [[Book of Mormon Father-and-Son Texts Resolve in Context]], [[Worship of the Son Is Warranted Through Indwelling]], [[Personages vs Subsistent Relations - What Person Means]].
