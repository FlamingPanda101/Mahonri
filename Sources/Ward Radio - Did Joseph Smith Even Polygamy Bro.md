# Ward Radio — Did Joseph Smith Even Polygamy, Bro?

A [[Ward Radio]] episode hosted by [[Cardon Ellis]] with [[Kwaku El]] and [[Brad Whitbeck]], featuring historian [[Don Bradley]]. It responds to the **"Remnant" / doctrine-of-Christ movement** (associated with [[Denver Snuffer]]) which holds that [[Joseph Smith]] never practiced polygamy and that [[Doctrine and Covenants 132|D&C 132]] is a [[Brigham Young]] forgery. Bradley argues the document's incidental details, its self-chastisement of Joseph, and a web of interlocking sources all point to Joseph and Nauvoo, not 1850s Utah. Kwaku deliberately "mans" the denial ("pneumo") position. The **D&C-132 detail-matching and self-chastisement arguments are new to the vault**; the broader denial material overlaps the existing polygamy cluster.

## Summary

- **D&C 132's incidental details match Nauvoo on July 12, 1843.** The "don't put your property out of your hands" verse (132:57) aligns with [[William Clayton]]'s journal for that day (the Joseph–Emma agreement, deeding property and the steamboat *Maid of Iowa* to [[Emma Smith]]) and with a surviving deed dated July 12, 1843 — applying the same incidental-detail test Bradley used to vindicate the First Vision. See [[D&C 132 Internal Details Match Nauvoo July 1843]]. [^1]
- **The revelation faults Joseph, which a Brigham forgery would not.** Verses 50/56/60 speak of Joseph's sins, transgressions, and trespasses against Emma; Brigham insisted Joseph "did nothing wrong," and Joseph's genuine revelations characteristically rebuke him. See [[D&C 132 Chastises Joseph So Brigham Did Not Forge It]]. [^2]
- **Independent, geographically dispersed sources interlock like puzzle pieces.** An anti-polygamy Nauvoo document (the Olney back-side list at Yale) names Joseph's wives in 1843, matching later Utah testimonies; Franklin D. Richards's private notebook on the levirate law interlocks with Mercy Fielding Thompson's marriage record. No coordinated later forgery explains the undesigned coincidences. See [[The Interlocking Polygamy Sources Defeat the Forgery Theory]]. [^3]
- **Whether Joseph practiced polygamy is separable from fears about eternal polygamy.** Bradley decouples the historical question from the pastoral motive for denial and from the "a prophet can't sin" premise, while conceding he would personally *prefer* Joseph had not practiced it. See [[Whether Joseph Practiced Polygamy Has No Bearing on Eternal Polygamy]]. [^4]
