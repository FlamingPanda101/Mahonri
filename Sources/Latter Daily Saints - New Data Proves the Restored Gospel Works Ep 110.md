# Latter Daily Saints - New Data Proves the Restored Gospel Works Ep 110

Latter Daily Saints reacts to the Institute for Family Studies' 2026 Family Structure Index — on which Utah ranks #1 and Idaho #2 — and reads the result as empirical "fruit" of the restored gospel, since those two states have the nation's highest concentration of Latter-day Saints. The episode then pivots to Dallas Jenkins (co-founder of *The Chosen*) repeatedly affirming that Latter-day Saints "love the same Jesus."

## Summary

The hosts open with Matthew 7:16-20 ("by their fruits ye shall know them") and the long-running evangelical objection that "fruits" means *right belief in the Trinity*, not lived outcomes; they argue instead that observable family and well-being outcomes are exactly what Jesus meant.[^1] They present the Institute for Family Studies' **2026 Family Structure Index**, on which **Utah ranks #1 (score 102)** with the highest share of married prime-age adults and of teens living with married parents, plus the sixth-highest fertility rate; **Idaho ranks #2**.[^2] Their central inference: it is "no coincidence" that the top two states have the highest LDS populations in the country.[^3]

They add a cluster of corroborating rankings cited secondhand (attributed to a "Clint Teppel" index and US News): Utah as #1 for volunteerism, economic outlook, births to married mothers, weekly church attendance, and charitable donation, and "best state in the nation" for four consecutive years; Utah is also cited as sometimes the "happiest state."[^4] A caveat is voiced in passing — higher elevation correlates with higher suicide rates — acknowledging confounds "if we're being honest."[^5]

The hosts tie the family-structure data to the **law of chastity**, framed not as a "don't" rule but as "get married, be faithful, have children, in that order."[^6] They argue single-parent households are among the biggest predictors of incarceration and poverty, and cite infidelity/divorce statistics (e.g., people who cheat are ~2x as likely to divorce; ~60% of relationships with infidelity divorce within five years; ~80% of secret-then-discovered affairs end in divorce).[^7] They connect this to the Family Proclamation's warning that family disintegration brings "the calamities foretold by ancient and modern prophets."[^8]

The second half covers Dallas Jenkins (*The Chosen*) drawing evangelical criticism (a Protestia repost) for saying Latter-day Saints "love the same Jesus" — a position he reaffirms on John Bytheway and Hank Smith's show and, five years earlier, on *Saints Unscripted* with David Snell, vowing to "go down swinging" defending his LDS friends.[^9] The hosts read 1 Nephi 12:17 (the "mists of darkness" as the devil's temptations) to argue that bearing false witness about LDS belief clouds honest seekers from the "fruit" — the love of God — at the tree of life.[^10] They close on a brief comment-reaction segment (Tucker Carlson on Church finances; Luke 22:35-36 on purse and scrip) and the channel's tagline that without a "gospel lens" you only get a quarter of the story.[^11]
