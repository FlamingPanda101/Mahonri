# Keystone - Everything Johnny Harris Gets Wrong About Mormons

A point-by-point Keystone response to [[Johnny Harris]]'s consolidated long-form video on LDS history. Keystone treats Harris as sincere and broadly accurate on facts, locating most of the problem in *framing* and selective omission rather than flat error — while flagging one "egregious error" (the B.H. Roberts misquotation) that does cross into factual mistake. The reply ranges across Joseph Smith's treasure-digging, the Book of Mormon's translation and witnesses, New World archaeology, Brigham Young's polygamy and the Mountain Meadows Massacre, the temple recommend, the "control" thesis, and ex-member rhetoric.

## Summary
- **Treasure-digging was Christian folk religiosity, not witchcraft or fraud.** Citing non-LDS historian Alan Taylor and anthropologist Manuel Padro, Keystone reframes young Joseph's seer-stone treasure seeking as a materialist extension of Yankee folk Christianity (liberating spirits bound to buried wealth), which mainline Protestants condemned as "demonic" because it resembled old Catholic practice.[^1] See [[Treasure Hunting as Historical Cultural Practice]], [[Folk Magic Claims About the Smith Family]], and [[Presentism in Folk Belief Judgment]].
- **The eleven witnesses are the elephant in the room.** Harris stresses that "no one else" saw the plates while omitting the eleven witnesses (printed in every Book of Mormon) plus unofficial witnesses; Keystone calls this "the biggest problem with this entire video," noting even Fawn Brodie and Dan Vogel fell back on a "hypnotized" explanation.[^2] See [[Witnesses Spiritual Eyes Selective Emphasis]] and [[Inference to the Best Explanation for the Witnesses]].
- **There was no curtain during the actual translation.** The "behind a curtain" image conflates Joseph copying characters (later carried by [[Martin Harris]] to [[Charles Anthon]]) with the open, no-notes dictation of the surviving text; this defuses the "concealment" suspicion without proving prophethood.[^3] See [[There Was No Curtain During the Book of Mormon Translation]] and [[Dictation Speed and Method Argue Against Fabrication]].
- **The Book of Mormon introduction was added later; absence of New World evidence is overstated.** The "principal ancestors → among the ancestors" change is to an editorial introduction, not the ancient text; and less than 1% of Mesoamerica has been professionally surveyed, while the Old World segment yields finds like Nahom.[^4] See [[Absence of Evidence Is Not Evidence of Absence in New World Archaeology]], [[Only Two Percent of the Americas Has Been Excavated]], [[Nahom Inscription Confirms the Book of Mormon Geography]], [[The Limited-Geography Reading Predates the DNA Debate]], and [[God Withholds the Slam Dunk to Preserve Faith]].
- **Brigham Young did not order Mountain Meadows; young marriage was multi-causal.** Keystone acknowledges the massacre as a "dark stain" but stresses Brigham's message was "let them go in peace," arriving too late; and it situates early-Utah young marriage in presentism, frontier demographics, and polygamy's math, correcting the implication of multiple 13-year-old wives.[^5] See [[Brigham Young Did Not Order the Mountain Meadows Massacre]], [[Brigham Young Was Not the Autocrat of Utah]], [[Young Marriage in Early Utah Was Multi-Causal Not Mormon Deviance]], and [[Presentism Defense of Joseph Smith's Polygamy - Gurr]].
- **The "control" thesis is the age-old Korihor charge; correlation is normal institutionalization.** Harris's repeated "control control control" echoes Korihor's accusation that priests "keep them down... in bondage" (Alma 30:27), while the church's growing standardization is the ordinary route — per [[Max Weber]] — by which charismatic movements survive their founder.[^6] See [[Alma 30.27|Alma 30:27]], [[Institutionalization Is the Unique Christian Contribution]], [[Boundary Maintenance Drove Doctrinal Systematization]], and [[Stasis Is the Claim That Needs Proof Not Change]].
- **The temple recommend is a reasonable standard, not mere loyalty control.** Ancient Israelite temple access likewise required righteousness, truth-telling, and kept oaths; verifying that patrons believe and strive to live the covenants they make is not unreasonable.[^7] See [[Gradations of Holiness Structured the Ancient Temple]].
- **B.H. Roberts was playing devil's advocate — the "most egregious error."** Harris's Roberts quotation is the adversary's brief, not Roberts's verdict; Roberts's own letter to Heber J. Grant disclaims the conclusions and affirms faith "not only unshaken but unshakable."[^8] See [[B.H. Roberts Was Playing Devils Advocate Not Losing Faith]] and [[The Book of Mormon Keystone Argument]].
- **Presentation, not facts, is the real lever; the enlightened-ex-member frame treats believers as a spectacle.** Keystone grants Harris's facts but critiques music/animation/word-choice engineered to make viewers "feel the way he wants," and the posture that treats believing Latter-day Saints "like zoo animals" — while conceding believers do the same and citing President Nelson's call to keep loving those who leave.[^9] See [[The Enlightened Ex-Member Frame Treats Believers as Zoo Animals]] and [[The Critics' Manipulation Tactics and Logical Fallacies Toolkit]].
