# Dan McClellan — "The Bible Has No Contradictions" Is Just a Feeling

A YouTube Short in which [[Dan McClellan]] argues that inerrantist certainty that the Bible contains no contradictions is an emotional commitment — a *feeling* of conviction — rather than a conclusion reached by logic, reason, or evidence. He grounds the claim in a public "experiment" he ran on Twitter/X against defenders of an Answers in Genesis post, predicting and then observing their failure modes. This source feeds [[Inerrantist Certainty Is a Feeling Not a Finding]].

## Summary
- An Answers in Genesis tweet asserted that "if anything contradicts the Bible, it's not true"; McClellan replied that this "puts us in a bit of a pickle because the Bible contradicts the Bible in a number of places," which drew a wave of strangers insisting the Bible has no contradictions and demanding he prove otherwise.[^1]
- He announced a predictive **experiment**: defenders would offer "weak apologetics" read online or in apologetics books, fail to answer his expert criticism, and then "either appeal to authority or argue by assertion or run like a squirrel." He reports that "so far every last one of them has done precisely what I said."[^2]
- The diagnosis: their certainty "is not based on logic or reason. It's based on feelings." He generalizes — "certainty itself is a feeling. Conviction is a feeling" — so what inerrantists defend is the way an idea makes them feel, not a finding about the text.[^3]
- Because they "really want it to be true," they "lower the evidentiary and the logical bar" so it is "much easier to get over"; the operative goal is "whatever makes them feel validated and justified in their dogma, in their credence, in their belief" while imagining they hold "ironclad reasoning and logic and evidence."[^4]
- When it is exposed that they do not know the arguments well enough to assess their strength, "they freak out and they just don't respond or they just keep repeating the same assertions" — a behavioral tell of motivated reasoning.[^5]
- Closing thesis: "The Bible is full of contradictions. That's not up for serious debate. If you think otherwise, think hard about where that's coming from. It's coming from your feelings."[^6]
