# Cwic Show — Be Ye Therefore Perfect (Trevan Hatch)

A *Cwic Show* (host [[Greg Matson]]) interview with BYU scholar [[Trevan Hatch]] on [[Matthew 5.48|Matthew 5:48]] ("Be ye therefore perfect"), based on an article Hatch co-wrote with the Latter-day Saint Doctrine librarian. Hatch argues the verse has been read as an isolated "proof text" for an endless quest for individual flawlessness, when in its Jewish literary context it is the capstone of a *love-your-enemies* sermon — "be therefore loving," not "be therefore flawless."

- Hatch shows the Greek *teleios* and Hebrew *tamim* carry a range — mature, complete, whole, mighty, efficacious, fully-invested — not moral faultlessness; the Theological Dictionary of the New Testament finds no NT sense of "gradual advance to moral perfection."[^1] See [[Teleios Means Mature and Whole Not Flawless]].
- The decisive move is the connective **"therefore"**: [[Matthew 5.43-45|Matthew 5:43–45]] commands loving enemies "that ye may be children of your Father," so 5:48's "be therefore perfect" means *be perfect in loving your enemies*, as God sends rain on just and unjust.[^2] See [[The Therefore in Matthew 5.48 Ties Perfection to Loving Enemies]].
- Three lines of evidence that 5:48 belongs to the love-pericope, not the whole sermon: it sits mid-sermon (not at the end of Matthew 7); Luke's parallel ([[Luke 6.36|Luke 6:36]]) reads "be **merciful**" inside a love-context; and Codex Sinaiticus's paragraph demarcation groups vv. 43–48 as one block.[^3] See [[Luke 6.36 and Sinaiticus Show Matthew 5.48 Belongs to the Love Pericope]].
- Hatch corroborates with [[Matthew 19.21|Matthew 19:21]] (be *teleios* → sell your goods, give to the poor) and [[1 John 4.18|1 John 4:18–21]] (perfect love casts out fear; one who hates his brother is a liar), and roots Jesus's ethic in the Torah's *imitatio Dei* ([[Leviticus 11.44|Leviticus 11:44]], [[Leviticus 19.2|Leviticus 19:2]], [[Deuteronomy 10.18-19|Deuteronomy 10:18–19]]).[^4] See [[Be Perfect Defined Elsewhere as Caring for the Poor]].
- Pastoral payoff: BYU's own social scientists find LDS perfectionism is *not* church-induced (see [[Toxic Perfectionism Lower Among Religious Than Non-Religious]]); reading the verse in context replaces an inward checklist with outward charity.[^5] See [[Reading Perfection in Context Cures the Inward Checklist]].
