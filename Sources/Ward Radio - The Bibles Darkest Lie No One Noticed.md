# Ward Radio — The Bible's Darkest Lie No One Noticed

A [[Ward Radio]] panel — host [[Cardon Ellis]] with guests [[Austin Falter]] and [[Jonah Barnes]] — argues that the single clearest "Trinity in the Bible" proof text, [[1 John 5.7|1 John 5:7]] (the **[[1 John 5-7 - Johannine Comma Interpolation|Comma Johanneum]]**), does not exist in any early Greek manuscript and was a Latin scribal interpolation that pressured its way into [[Erasmus|Erasmus's]] *Textus Receptus* and thence the King James Bible.

## Summary

- **The thesis ("the darkest lie"):** [[1 John 5.7|1 John 5:7]] — "the Father, the Word, and the Holy Ghost: and these three are one" — is the *banner* verse trinitarians cite to prove the doctrine and to deny that Latter-day Saints are Christians, yet "there is no Greek of the first epistle of John 5:7. It doesn't exist." [^1]
- **The Erasmus narrative:** [[Erasmus]] (1516) went back from the Latin Vulgate to assemble the Greek manuscript corpus (the *Textus Receptus*, "the text we have received"), found the Comma in none of them, was challenged by churchmen, was then handed a single Greek manuscript "found in England" that conveniently contained it, and "writes later on in the letter, he says, 'I think they just made this garbage up.'" Under threat of excommunication he included it. See [[Erasmus Was Handed a Fabricated Manuscript to Restore the Comma]]. [^2]
- **The manuscript evidence:** the panel walks through scans of the three great uncials — **Codex Vaticanus** (image 1441), **Codex Sinaiticus**, and **Codex Alexandrinus** — showing each runs straight from the end of v. 6 (*alḗtheia*, "truth") to v. 8 (*pneuma / hydōr / haima* — spirit, water, blood), with no v. 7 between them. "The original Greek skips from verse six to verse eight. There is no verse seven." [^3]
- **"Added in," not just removed:** the hosts invert Joseph Smith's "plain and precious truths... removed from the stick of Judah" — here a precious *corruption* was *added in*. Trinitarians who weaponize [[Revelation 22.18-19|Revelation 22:18–19]] ("if any man shall add unto these things") built their core proof text by *adding* to scripture. See [[The Comma Inverts the Plain and Precious Truths Were Removed Argument]]. [^4]
- **From Latin into the Vulgate:** scholars trace the clause into the Latin only around the 4th century — the era when the church, newly accepted by the empire, "wanted to play nice with all the other Greco-Roman scholars" and fit the Aristotelian "no body parts or passions" God. The creeds came first; "then the scripture got in their way and they said, 'We got to put this somewhere.'" Cf. [[Trinity Built to Satisfy Greco-Roman Philosophy]], [[Trinity Survived Only Through Imperial Coercion]]. [^5]
- **The "no one used it anyway" rebuttal answered:** to the modern apologist reply (e.g. [[James White]]: "we don't even use that to prove the Trinity") the hosts list pre-critical-text divines who *did* affirm it — [[John Wesley]], [[John Calvin]], John Gill, [[Athanasius]], John Cassian, Matthew Henry, [[Cyprian of Carthage|St. Cyprian]] — so "this is the foundation of trinitarian belief." [^6]
- **The weaponization-to-violence argument:** the Comma is the text used to brand LDS as non-Christian "heretics," and the hosts tie that drumbeat — naming [[Mark Driscoll]], Mike Winger, Apologia Studios, [[James White]], [[Jeff Durbin]] — to the real-world Grand Blanc, Michigan church shooting by a gunman who called Mormons the Antichrist. See [[The Comma Is the Banner Verse Weaponized Against Latter-day Saints]]. [^7]
- **The KJV-Only tension:** modern critical translations quietly drop the Comma as unsubstantiated, even while evangelical polemics keep the anti-Mormon charge. Cf. [[KJV Onlyism and the Textus Receptus vs Critical Text]], [[Manuscripts Were Altered by Scribes Over Centuries]]. [^8]
