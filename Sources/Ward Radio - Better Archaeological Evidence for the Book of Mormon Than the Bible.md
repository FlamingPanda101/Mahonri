# Ward Radio - There's Better Archaeological Evidence for the Book of Mormon Than the Bible (feat. Josh Gehly)

**Channel:** Ward Radio · **Host:** [[Cardon Ellis]] · **Guests:** [[Josh Gehly]], [[Jerry Grover]]
**URL:** https://youtu.be/cbev5a3ZFEo

## Summary
- [[Josh Gehly]] argues the single strongest archaeological evidence for the Book of Mormon is **Nahom**: a "shot in the dark" prediction (a foreigner named [[Ishmael]] buried at a named place) that converges with the NHM altars, tribal/place names, ancient maps, and a funerary stela bearing Ishmael's name from the right time and place. See [[Nahom and NHM Converge with Nephis Eastward Turn]] and [[The Ishmael Funerary Stela in South Arabia]].
- Frames Nahom against the **standards of biblical archaeology** — the minimalist/maximalist debates (Finkelstein, Dever) — and claims Nahom "exceeds almost all biblical sites" and is "what a bullseye looks like." See [[Nahom Exceeds Biblical Sites by Archaeologys Own Standards]].
- [[Jerry Grover]] adds the frankincense-trail route corroboration and the multivalent NHM wordplay (mourning, consolation, hunger, complaining) matching the text; the turn due east reaches Bountiful in Dhofar. See [[Bountiful Lies Due East of Nahom]].
