# Ward Radio - Debunking Anti-Mormon Dan Vogel and the BIG Hole in His Argument

Host [[Cardon Ellis]] with [[Josh Gailey]] (Penn State archaeology; author of *Witnessing Miracles*) and [[Ed Thomas]] respond to critic [[Dan Vogel]]'s position on the hole and stone box at the New York hill, walking through all 16 relevant historical sources under a stated historiographical rubric.

## Summary
- **Same minimal-facts method as the *Informed Saints* episode, vs. a named opponent.** Gailey establishes a rubric (prefer early, firsthand, enemy/unsympathetic, and multiply-attested sources; inference to the best explanation per [[B.H. McCullagh]]/Behan McCullagh) and lays out 16 sources. The new contribution over [[Informed Saints - The Historical Evidence for Gold Plates Critics Cant Answer]] is the head-to-head with [[Dan Vogel]]'s specific claim. See [[Dan Vogel's East-Side Hole Is the Lone Source Against the West-Side Stone Box]].
- **The source tally.** Five sources place the hole on the **west side / summit** (Joseph, [[Oliver Cowdery]], [[Edward Stevenson]]'s unnamed local, and the hostile [[Lorenzo Saunders]]); five attest a **stone box** (Joseph, Oliver, [[Martin Harris]], [[David Whitmer]], the unnamed local); exactly **one** late, indirect, unsympathetic source (Frederick Mather, 1880) puts the dig on the **east side**, and another notes an east-side hole was dug by later Rochester treasure-seekers. Vogel relies on the lone Mather source.
- **The hostile "no fresh dirt" datum.** Lorenzo Saunders, who hunted the hill the Sunday after the plates were taken, found an old excavation with no fresh dirt — unintentionally corroborating Joseph's claim to have first opened the box in **1823** (per [[Moroni]]'s annual instruction), not 1827. Reinforces [[Hostile and Unlikely Witnesses Corroborate the Stone Box]].
