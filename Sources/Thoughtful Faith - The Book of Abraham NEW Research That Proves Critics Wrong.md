# Thoughtful Faith - The Book of Abraham: NEW Research That Proves Critics Wrong

[[Jacob Hansen]] and [[Hayden Carroll]] mount a three-part defense of the [[Book of Abraham]] against the charge that the recovered papyri and the "Kirtland Egyptian Papers" prove [[Joseph Smith]] a fraud. Part one (with documents read by researcher [[William Schryver]]) argues from eyewitness accounts that much of the papyri is lost and that the Egyptian Alphabet/Grammar documents are not a translation tool but an idiographic **cipher project** dependent on a pre-existing Book of Abraham. Part two (Tim Barker) shows Joseph explicitly declined to translate the very JSP XI characters critics cite. Part three (Egyptologist [[Kerry Muhlestein]]) reframes the facsimiles as drawings whose meaning is multivalent and time-bound.

- Eyewitnesses (Phelps, Cowdery, Caswall, Quincy, Charlotte Haven) describe **two long rolls plus framed fragments**, with red-and-black ink the surviving Book of Breathings lacks — so we do not have all the papyri; much burned in the 1871 Chicago fire — see [[Most of the Book of Abraham Papyri Burned in the Chicago Fire]].[^1]
- Schryver's thesis: the Alphabet/Grammar's "substantial words" are unique proper nouns drawn *from* the Book of Abraham (the "Lord of the Rings list" analogy); most characters are non-Egyptian, some Masonic-cipher derived; the documents are a **pure-language cipher key**, not a deciphering tool — see [[The Kirtland Egyptian Papers Are a Cipher Project Not a Translation Tool]].[^2]
- Tim Barker / [[John Gee]] on **JSP XI and Facsimile 2**: the hypocephalus lacunae were filled with JSP XI hieratic, and Joseph said its translation "will be given in the own due time of the Lord" — i.e. he expressly did *not* translate the characters critics attribute to him — see [[Joseph Smith Declined to Translate the Hypocephalus Characters of Facsimile 2]].[^3]
- Muhlestein on the **facsimiles**: interpreting a drawing differs from translating a text; Egyptologists' readings shift across a 3,000-year span and were sometimes proven wrong (Gee's labeled hypocephalus); Greco-Roman-era priests like Hor mixed **Jewish and Egyptian** iconography, and all three facsimiles are elsewhere associated with Abraham — see [[Egyptians Mixed Jewish and Egyptian Religious Iconography]], [[Egyptologists Disagree Across Time and Place on What a Drawing Means]], and the existing [[Translating a Text Versus Interpreting a Drawing Are Different Claims]].[^4]
- Hansen keeps the **catalyst theory** as a live option alongside the missing-scroll theory; he notes he confirmed with [[Sandra Tanner]] the old Tanner research the critics still recycle — see [[A Prophet Is Not an Academic Translator]].[^5]
