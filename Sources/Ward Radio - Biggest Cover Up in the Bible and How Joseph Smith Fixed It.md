# Ward Radio — Biggest Cover Up in the Bible (and How Joseph Smith Fixed It)

Apocrypha author [[Jonah Barnes]] presents the binding-of-Isaac chapter from his book *The Lost Gems of Genesis* to host [[Cardon Ellis]] and [[Britney Chapman Nash]], arguing that the original Akedah was a messianic resurrection story in which [[Isaac]] was actually sacrificed — a story the Deuteronomists "covered up," and that Joseph Smith partially restored.

## Summary

- **Genesis 22 shows editorial seams.** [[Jonah Barnes]] argues the chapter has been "monkeyed with": it calls Isaac Abraham's "only son" though [[Ishmael (son of Abraham)|Ishmael]] exists; the speaker shifts from **Elohim** (Genesis 22|first half) to **Yahweh/Jehovah** (second half); four go up the mountain (Abraham, Isaac, two servants) but only [[Abraham]] is said to return ([[Genesis 22|Gen 22:19]] — "where's Isaac?"); and the substitute animal changes from a *lamb* (promised, v. 8) to a *ram* (sacrificed, v. 13). He reads these as fingerprints of [[Josiah|Deuteronomist]] editors who disliked child sacrifice, resurrection, and messianism. See [[Joseph Smith Restored a Messianic Akedah in Which Isaac Was Actually Sacrificed]].
- **Jewish and New Testament traces of a slain, resurrected Isaac.** Drawing on [[Shalom Spiegel]]'s *The Last Trial*, the Book of Jasher, the Slavonic Palea, and Pseudo-Philo, Jonah argues a genuine Jewish tradition held that Isaac was actually killed and burned to ashes, then revived by the "dew of resurrection" — and that Isaac, far from passive, urged his own binding ("the Aqedah"). He reads [[James 2.20-23|James 2]] ("justified by works… when he had offered Isaac"), [[Hebrews 11.17-19|Hebrews 11]] ("accounting that God was able to raise him… from the dead"), and the Isaac references in [[Acts 3.13-15|Acts 3]] as New Testament knowledge of a death-and-resurrection Akedah. See [[Joseph Smith Restored a Messianic Akedah in Which Isaac Was Actually Sacrificed]].
- **Joseph Smith's restored key: Abraham knew the resurrection.** The "lynchpin" is JST Genesis 15, where Abraham is shown the days of the Son of Man and the resurrection ("though thou wast dead, yet am I not able to give it thee?… he must first be quickened"), reconciling [[John 8.56|John 8:56]] ("Abraham rejoiced to see my day"). On this reading the Akedah is about *faith in Christ's power over death*, not blind obedience halted at the last second — the hosts contrast this with what they call a "Deuteronomist/legalist" reading of obedience. Builds on [[The Binding of Isaac as a Type of Christ]] and [[Deuteronomistic Corruption of the Old Testament]].
