A short [[Ether's Elephant]] video that uses a recorded conversation between neuroscientist [[Andrew Huberman]] and psychologist [[David DeSteno]] to argue that belief in God is compatible with — and can even be approached through — the scientific method. The thesis is blunt: "if you think science is incompatible with belief in God, you either don't understand God or you do not understand the scientific method." The video splices in [[William Lane Craig]] enumerating classes of truth science cannot establish, and closes by previewing the DNA-and-Book-of-Mormon question taken up in a sequel.

The piece runs the scientific method as an analogy for faith: *observe* (the documented well-being of religious communities), *hypothesize*, and *experiment* by living the practice — "seek and ye shall find," "faith without works is dead." It is careful to concede that science cannot *prove* God (God's existence is not falsifiable) and that the outcome of such a personal experiment is evidence "for you and only you" — i.e., personal revelation, not public proof.

## Summary

- **Science and God are compatible because science cannot adjudicate God.** Huberman: "All the elements of science are entirely compatible with the idea of there being a God."[^1] DeSteno adds that God's existence is "one science can't answer" because it is not falsifiable — "you can't manipulate something about the mind of God," so causality cannot be inferred either way.[^2] → developed in [[Science Is Agnostic Not Atheistic]] and [[If God Exists, Miracles Are Possible]].

- **There are whole classes of truth science cannot establish.** Quoting [[William Lane Craig]], the video lists five: logical/mathematical truths (science presupposes them), metaphysical truths (other minds, an external world, that the past is real), ethical truths (science can't show the Nazi camp doctors did evil), aesthetic judgments, and science itself ("science cannot be justified by the scientific method").[^3] → [[Five Classes of Truth Science Cannot Establish]]; cf. the vault's [[Scientism Cannot Describe or Explain All Reality]] and [[Scientism Is Self-Refuting]].

- **Explaining a miracle's mechanism does not make it less miraculous.** The lightning-as-AED parable: discovering *how* a cardiac-arrest survival happened "does not make it less miraculous" and "doesn't negate my belief in God because I can explain it." Both the religious (miracles must be inexplicable) and the scientists (a mechanism means "just science," not God) are said to err.[^4] → [[Discovering a Miracle's Mechanism Does Not Make It Less Miraculous]]; quotes [[Moroni 7]] on confessing God's hand "in all things."

- **Faith can be tested experimentally — and the test is personal revelation.** The video maps observation → hypothesis → experiment onto religious practice, citing DeSteno that religious *practice* (not mere belief) shows up in the well-being literature with a larger effect size than secular community. "Faith without works is dead." The result is "personal evidence for you and only you."[^5] → [[The Scientific Method Applied to Faith Yields Personal Revelation]]; cf. [[Personal Revelation Is the Most Legitimate Path to God]].

- **LDS belief is not threatened by science; tension comes from over-literal reading.** Huberman: "The tension comes when you become very tied to actual texts... and positions of people interpreting what they think God did." The video frames Latter-day Saints as inviting others to "find out for themselves" rather than demanding assent, and previews that DNA does *not* prove the Book of Mormon false.[^6] → cf. [[The Warfare Model of Science and Religion Is a Myth]] and [[The DNA Disproof Is a Proof by Contradiction With a False Premise]].
