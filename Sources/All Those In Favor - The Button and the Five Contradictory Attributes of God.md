# All Those In Favor — The Button and the Five Contradictory Attributes of God

A focused presentation of the **problem of hell** using a "button" thought experiment: if you could see a person's whole life in advance and knew it would end in eternal conscious torment, would pressing the button to create them be loving? The video argues that **five attributes mainstream Christianity ascribes to God cannot all be true at once**, walks through and rebuts five (really six) standard defenses, and offers an LDS resolution rooted in pre-existence and a literal Father–Son rather than the creedal Trinity.

## Summary
- **The button thought experiment**: bringing a person into existence whose life you *foreknow* ends in unending suffering — when you could decline and lose nothing — cannot be an act of love; this is the moral intuition that drives the whole argument [^1].
- **The five-attribute inconsistency**: God's omniscience (knows the whole future), omnipotence-plus-freedom (nothing forces him to create), creation *ex nihilo* (he starts each life), the reality of hell (some are damned), and omnibenevolence (wants what is best for all) cannot all be simultaneously true [^2].
- **Six defenses rebutted**: free will, greater purpose, willing-vs-permitting, God's glory, "we can't understand God's ways," and the appeal to mystery — each is shown to fail because the *first* decision in the causal chain was God's choice to create [^3].
- **The Judas argument**: Jesus says it would have been better for Judas never to have been born — Scripture itself concedes a fate worse than non-existence, which a free creator could have avoided [^4].
- **The LDS resolution**: the contradiction is an artifact of the **creeds and councils**, not the God of the Bible; recover a literal Heavenly Father and Son, pre-mortal existence (you were not created from nothing), and the problem dissolves [^5].
