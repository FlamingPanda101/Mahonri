# Ward Radio - We Bought Every Anti-Mormon Book and React

A [[Ward Radio]] livestream hosted by [[Cardon Ellis]] with guests [[Baylor Johnson (Ward Radio)|Baylor Johnson]] (of *All Those In Favor*) and [[Michael Myrrono (OrthoCardia)|Michael Myrrono]] (*OrthoCardia*, an ex-evangelical who converted to the Church about a year before recording). The three drive to the "Last Book Store" in downtown Los Angeles, buy roughly nine or ten anti-Mormon (and a few pro-LDS) books, and react on air. Most of the criticisms surveyed are the genre's standard fare — already catalogued elsewhere in this vault — so this source mostly **wikilinks** existing concepts; two genuinely new specifics get their own notes.

## Summary

- **Reacting to the countercult classics.** The stack includes [[Mark J. Cares]]' *Speaking the Truth in Love to Mormons* (1997), Ron Rhodes-accredited material, a 1998 *Dictionary of Mormonism*, *Secret Ceremonies* by Deborah Laake, *Mormonism Now and Then* (1961), and — front-and-center in the store's religion section — [[Robert Millet]]'s pro-LDS *By What Authority?*. The hosts joke that Millet is an "undercover cop" the store is "promoting the heck out of."[^1]

- **NEW: "See Mormons as victims" — Truth and Love Ministry's Five Pillars.** The hosts pull up Cares' ministry website (truthinlove.org) and walk its free witnessing course: *see Mormons as victims, not enemies; love and respect them; focus on "Mormon stress points"; speak the "Mormon language"; witness Christ rather than debate*. They grant pillars 2 and 5 are an improvement but argue pillar one ("victim") is inherently condescending — and contrast it with *Preach My Gospel* ch. 10, which tells missionaries to adapt to each person and follow the Spirit, never to target other faiths' "stress points." See [[See Mormons as Victims Is Condescending Not Loving]].[^2]

- **NEW: dated books refute the "rebranding" charge.** Against the claim that the Church only recently "rebranded" to affirm Christ, the hosts cite the critics' own dated literature: Cares (1997) conceding "nothing of substance has changed," a 1998 critic's dictionary affirming Mormons hold Jesus "lives and is divine" (and using the term "Godhead"), and a 1961 book already complaining of a "rebrand." See [[Dated Anti-Mormon Books Refute the Rebranding Charge]].[^3]

- **The "Godhead" / Trinity exchange.** Myrrono affirms he is "a Trinitarian" in the sense of Father, Son, and Holy Ghost being one, while noting "Trinity" is post-biblical and the New Testament term is *Godhead* — which even the Oxford Dictionary uses. The crew runs through subordinationism, the *filioque*, and the 1054 schism. Wikilinks: [[The Godhead Is Not Polytheism]], [[Subordinationism Was the Early Church View]], [[Filioque Split Greek East from Latin West]], [[Most Christians Describe Three Beings Not the Trinity]].[^4]

- **Synergistic vs. monergistic salvation.** Responding to Cares' charge that the LDS teach salvation by works, the hosts defend a *synergistic* salvation (sacraments and discipleship genuinely participate) shared with Catholics and the Orthodox, against Calvinist monergism — "salvation by grace alone, not by theology alone." Wikilinks: [[LDS Salvation Is by Grace Not Earned by Works]], [[Salvation by Theology Damns Almost Everyone]], [[Orthopraxy over Orthodoxy in LDS Salvation]], [[Justification - Infusion vs Legal Fiction]].[^5]

- **The "different Jesus / a Jesus that cannot save" trope.** Myrrono recalls once believing the LDS "preach a different Jesus." Wikilinks: [[Same Reference Different Beliefs - You Worship the Same Jesus]], [[Different Jesus Both Uncharitable and Precisely True]].[^6]

- **"They'll lose their family" objection.** A Rhodes-accredited book claims a convert from Mormonism risks losing spouse, children, and parents (an LDS wife being "encouraged to divorce"). The hosts call this false and note *Secret Ceremonies'* own author thanks her devout LDS parents for "putting our relationship ahead of their beliefs" — so the polemic cuts both ways. Relates to [[Anti-Mormonism Is Attacking the Person Not the Idea]].[^7]

- **Polygamy and Martin Luther.** Reading *Secret Ceremonies*, the hosts grant the author's fair points (polygamy was minority, long-ended, "had no place in our lives") and note that [[Martin Luther]] himself sanctioned a bigamous marriage (Philip of Hesse) on *sola scriptura* grounds. Relates to [[Catholic Marriage History Undercuts the Polygamy Attack]].[^8]

- **"Choose your miracle" — Enoch and Mahijah.** Against the "worst anti-Mormon book ever" (which mocks LDS miracle accounts), the hosts argue the critic who rejects miracles must still explain the Enochic name Mahijah, Nahom, and Olishem — invoking Hugh Nibley's account that the atheist scholar Matthew Black conceded Joseph Smith would have needed a still-undiscovered "lost source text." Wikilinks: [[Moses 6-7 Restores Enochic Content Unavailable to Joseph Smith in 1830]], [[Nahom Inscription Confirms the Book of Mormon Geography]].[^9]
