# Data Over Dogma — Left Behind: The Book of Enoch

## Summary

- **The Book of Enoch (1 Enoch)** is an influential pseudepigraphal work composed over several centuries (~300 BCE to late 1st century CE), originally in Aramaic, later translated into Greek and Ethiopic. It expands the Genesis 6 "sons of God" story into 108 chapters covering the fall of the Watchers, the origins of evil, eschatological judgment, and cosmic order. [^1]
- **It was quoted as authoritative scripture by the Epistle of Jude** (Jude 1:14–15), widely used at Qumran (11 fragmentary manuscripts — better represented than almost any biblical book except Genesis, Deuteronomy, Isaiah, and Psalms), and profoundly shaped New Testament concepts of Satan, hell, angels, the Son of Man, and postmortem judgment. [^2]
- **It fell out of the Western/Orthodox canon by the 4th century** due to concerns about its pre-flood survival claim, internal contradictions (telling the same story multiple ways), and the apologetic project of 2nd-century Christian intellectuals. It survived only in the Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church canon — the most expansive canon in Christendom — and was rediscovered by Western scholars in the 17th–19th centuries. [^3]
