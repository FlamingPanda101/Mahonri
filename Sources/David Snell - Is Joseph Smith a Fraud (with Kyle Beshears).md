# David Snell — Is Joseph Smith a Fraud (with Kyle Beshears)

A cross-traditional interview in which Latter-day Saint apologist [[David Snell]] hosts evangelical scholar-pastor [[Kyle Beshears]] (author of *40 Questions about Mormonism*, Kregel Academic) for an unusually charitable conversation. The two model the interfaith method Beshears advocates: understand the other position accurately before disagreeing. The episode moves through Beshears's origin story (the countercult film *The God Makers* and Walter Martin's *Kingdom of the Cults*), why he is more interested in LDS *history* than evangelical history, the loaded word "cult," the "are Mormons Christian?" question, the central question of whether Joseph Smith was a fraud, two pointed LDS critiques of the book's handling of the Book of Mormon witnesses and the Hurlbut affidavits, and a closing reflection on what separates legitimate polemics from anti-Mormonism.

- **Beshears refuses the "fraud" verdict without affirming "prophet."** After reading all of Joseph Smith's journals and personal correspondence over six months, he concluded Joseph was "an extremely complicated man" — sincere in his desire to heal a divided Christianity, pastoral in his Nauvoo ministry — yet someone he cannot follow. He distinguishes his discomfort with the Book of Mormon from his *deeper* discomfort with the Doctrine and Covenants, where God speaks in the first-person active voice through a prophet. [^1][^2][^3][^4] See [[Joseph Smith Was Sincere Not a Fraud]], [[Read the Primary Sources Before Judging a Person]], [[Doctrine and Covenants Is Harder Than the Book of Mormon for Evangelicals]], [[Refusing the Pious Fraud Category]].
- **A Latter-day Saint vs. evangelical difference: theological history vs. historical theology.** Beshears observes that evangelicals are "a people of a historical theology" (they prize the doctrines of the Reformation but cannot place Geneva on a map), while Latter-day Saints are "a theological history" — an unfolding story begun in 1820 in which they see themselves as active participants. Recognizing this asymmetry, he argues, opens space for better conversation. [^5][^6] See [[Theological History vs Historical Theology]], [[Latter-day Saints as Participants in an Unfolding Story]].
- **Two LDS critiques of the book's evidence handling.** Snell pushes back on (1) the witnesses section, which pairs John Whitmer's maintained testimony against Martin Harris's "spiritual eyes" language but omits that *all three* original witnesses upheld their testimonies for life; and (2) the use of the harsh Hurlbut affidavits against the Smith family without explaining their hostile, money-driven, dirt-gathering provenance. Beshears largely concedes both, citing word-count limits and his rule of leading with what evangelicals already "know." [^7][^8][^9][^10] See [[All Three Witnesses Upheld Their Testimony for Life]], [[Spiritual Eyes Described Real Things in the 1800s]], [[Hurlbut Affidavits Need Their Hostile Context]], [[Lead With What the Audience Knows Then Counter]].
- **The threshold of anti-Mormonism is attacking the person, not the idea.** Beshears argues it is *not* anti-Mormon to deny that Joseph was a prophet or that the Book of Mormon is scripture — those are basic statements of evangelical conviction; one crosses into anti-Mormonism when one stops engaging the ideas/history and begins disparaging the people, or refuses to do the homework to represent beliefs accurately. He diagnoses the underlying idol as "victory." [^11][^12][^13] See [[Anti-Mormonism Is Attacking the Person Not the Idea]], [[Inaccuracy as a Form of Disrespect]], [[Victory as an Idol in Apologetics]].
- **The greatest commandment and Peter's "fear."** Beshears names the book's most important section as a call to the greatest commandment: you cannot love a neighbor who is only a caricature in your head, so you must discuss agreements *and* disagreements. He re-reads 1 Peter 3:15 ("gentleness and respect"), noting the Greek for "respect" is *phobos* (fear) — directed at God, not the interlocutor: am I doing what pleases God in this moment? [^14][^15][^16] See [[Love of Neighbor Requires Understanding]], [[Peter's Respect Is Fear of God Not the Interlocutor]], [[The Marriage Analogy for Interfaith Dialogue]], [[The Pelagianism Erratum]].
