# Let's Talk w/ Hayden Carroll — Apologia Protestant CHALLENGES Mormon

The full, hour-long unscripted conversation between [[Hayden Carroll]] and **Austin** (see [[Austin (Apologia Church)]]), a lay presuppositional apologist from [[Jeff Durbin]]'s Apologia Church, filmed when Apologia members came to evangelize at the Mesa Temple Lights. This is the **source conversation** that Durbin later played and reacted to, and which Carroll rebuts point-by-point in the sibling video [[Lets Talk w Hayden Carroll - Apologia Church Apologetics Are Weak]]. The exchange has two movements: a long **method/authority** duel over *sola scriptura*, the canon, and presuppositionalism (Carroll's signature "justify your standard" pressure), followed by a **theology proper** duel over the Trinity, consubstantiality, divine plurality, and whether God is (or was) a man. Austin repeatedly concedes he will not — and at one point says he cannot — justify the canon's infallibility, which Carroll treats as the whole game.

## Summary
- **Presuppositionalism stalls against a coherent rival worldview (NEW).** Carroll's structural thesis: he invokes [[Greg Bahnsen]]'s own (recalled) concession that "you cannot use presuppositional apologetics on Mormons" because the LDS worldview is internally coherent — so the transcendental "disarm" finds no seam and Austin is left merely asserting his standard. See [[Presuppositionalism Cannot Refute a Coherent Rival Worldview Like Mormonism]].[^1] Background method: [[Bahnsen's Method - Arbitrariness, Presuppositions, Inconsistency]], [[Internal Critique - Two Questions for Every Critic]], [[Disarm vs Dodge - Bahnsen's Two Apologetic Strategies]].
- **The genealogy contradiction as a battering ram (NEW).** Carroll's most-developed argument: Matthew names **Jacob** as Joseph's father ([[Matthew 1.16|Matt 1:16]]); Luke names **Heli** ([[Luke 3.23|Luke 3:23]]). He argues this cannot be solved "with the text alone" and is fatal to a method that *asserts* infallibility. See [[The Genealogy Contradiction - Matthew Names Jacob, Luke Names Heli as Joseph's Father]]; cf. [[Harmonization Defends a Dogma Not a Conclusion]] and [[Gospel Contradictions Are Not Trivial - They Touch Messianic Identity]].[^2]
- **"Justify your standard" — the self-attestation / circularity loop.** The spine of the first half: Austin grounds the canon in "God has spoken," and when pressed to justify *that* this 66-book collection is God's infallible word, says "I don't need to justify that... that's not my job."[^3] Carroll names this classic circular reasoning. Covered by [[The Circularity Charge Against Self-Attesting Scripture]], [[Presuppositional Self-Authentication Is Unbiblical Human Philosophy]], [[Scripture Is Self-Authenticating Because God Is Self-Contained]], [[God Himself Not a Book Is the Ultimate Authority]].
- **The canon was never publicly designated.** Carroll's standing demand — "where did God say *this* collection is His word and the final authority?" — and the canon dilemmas (66 vs. 73 books, Hebrews' anonymity as a "canon problem"). See [[The Three Unjustified Protestant Canon Presuppositions]], [[Authorization - Divine Designation Required for an Infallible Canon]], [[The Canon Question Is Logically Prior to Canon Authority]], [[Knowing Scripture Is the Word of God Requires an Authority Outside Scripture]].[^4]
- **2 Timothy 3:16 and theopneustos.** Austin proof-texts [[2 Timothy 3.16-17|2 Tim 3:16]]; Carroll replies that θεόπνευστος ("God-breathed") means life-giving, not infallible, and that the "scripture" in view was the OT, not the 66-book NT-inclusive canon. See [[Theopneustos Means Life-Giving Not Infallible]], [[The Root Fallacy - Theopneustos Parts Do Not Yield Infallible]], [[No Verse Calls a Human Writing the Word of God]].[^5]
- **The unevangelized / moral-law dilemma.** Austin says one cannot know "murder is wrong" without the Bible, yet holds the moral law is written on every heart (Romans 1, [[Romans 1.16|Rom 1]]) such that the unevangelized are condemned — a contradiction Carroll exploits. Already documented: [[The Unevangelized Dilemma Refutes Cant-Know-Morality-Without-Scripture]]; cf. [[A Critic Must Ground His Moral Truth Claims or He Is Borrowing Them]].[^6]
- **Mosaic / anonymous-authorship double standard.** Austin claims Moses wrote Genesis and Matthew wrote Matthew "because it says so"; Carroll notes the Gospels are anonymous and presses why anonymous Hebrews counts as scripture while Mosaic authorship is asserted without evidence. See [[The Anonymous-Authorship Double Standard on Genesis]].[^7]
- **Jonah as a false-prophet *tu quoque*.** Austin invokes the Deuteronomy 18 false-prophet test ([[Deuteronomy 18.20-22|Deut 18:20-22]]) against Joseph Smith; Carroll counters that Jonah ([[Jonah 3.4|Jonah 3:4]]) prophesied Nineveh's destruction in 40 days and it did not come to pass — yet Jonah is a canonical prophet. See [[Scripture as the Test of Prophets and Revelation]].[^8]
- **The Trinity / consubstantiality is not in any single verse.** The second half: Austin offers Deut 6:4, Psalm 110:1 ([[Psalm 110.1|Ps 110:1]]), and John 14 ([[John 14.9|John 14:9]], [[John 14.28|14:28]]) for "one being, three persons, consubstantial"; Carroll answers that none teaches *homoousios*, that no biblical author describes the Trinity, and that the doctrine imports fourth-century Greek metaphysics (neoplatonism). See [[No Single Biblical Author Describes the Trinity]], [[The Trinity and Consubstantiality Are Not in the Bible]], [[Consubstantial Is a Mickey Mouse English Translation of Homoousios]], [[John 17 Oneness Is Indwelling Not Consubstantiality]], [[Gethsemane Two Wills Refute Same-Being Trinity]].[^9]
- **Divine plurality — "ye are gods," Psalm 82, and the god of this world.** Carroll deploys Psalm 82 ([[Psalm 82.1|Ps 82:1]], [[Psalm 82.6|82:6]]) and "have I not said ye are gods?" plus Satan as "the god of this world" against Austin's reading of Isaiah 43:10 ([[Isaiah 43.10|Isa 43:10]]) as strict monotheism; Austin replies "elohim" can mean heavenly beings and the others are false gods/idols. See [[Psalm 82 Is About Divine Council Not Human Judges]], [[Humans Are the Same Species as God (Acts 17 Offspring)]], [[Deuteronomy Acknowledges Other Gods While Using Negation Rhetoric]], [[The Shema Asserts Exclusive Relationship Not Sole Existence]].[^10]
- **Is God a man / was God once a man.** Carroll tries to read the [[King Follett Discourse]] and a Journal of Discourses citation; Austin objects the sources are posthumous recollections (and Joseph Smith was already dead for the 1844 JoD entry), and that the LDS community itself debates the [[God Was Once a Man Is Esoteric Not Core Doctrine|infinite-regress vs. monarchical-monotheism]] models. Austin presses the immateriality of God (spaceless, timeless), which Carroll resists. Cf. [[Philippians 2 Kenosis - The Son Emptied Himself of Divinity]], [[Worshiping the Right Jesus - Created vs Uncreated]].[^11]
- **Subordinationism *tu quoque* on the early Fathers.** Carroll argues Tertullian/Justin Martyr were subordinationists (the Son lesser than the Father) — heretics by today's creed — so Austin holds the Fathers to a standard they fail; Austin repeatedly declines to grant premises about church history he hasn't read.[^12]
- **A coda on Calvinism, assurance, and 1 John 3:9.** The opening and closing stretches trade on Reformed soteriology: total depravity / regeneration-before-faith, "why evangelize if election is fixed?", and a final contradiction challenge on [[1 John 3.1-3|1 John 3:9]] ("no one born of God makes a practice of sinning") against Austin's admission that he sins daily — the same *practice-vs-sin* harmonization Carroll calls another contradiction. Carroll also quips that "Calvinism is my backup plan."[^13]
