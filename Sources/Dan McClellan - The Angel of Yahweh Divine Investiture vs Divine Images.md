# Dan McClellan - The Angel of Yahweh Divine Investiture vs Divine Images

A reaction/commentary video by [[Dan McClellan]] on an edited clip from a debate between LDS apologist [[Jacob Hansen]] and Trinitarian debater [[Avery (God Logic)]] over whether the **angel/messenger of Yahweh** identifying as God supports the LDS doctrine of divine investiture or a Trinitarian Godhead. McClellan argues that *both* debaters rely on anachronistic frameworks and that the only contemporaneous category that explains the data is the **intuitive logic of divine images** plus **name-bearing mediation**.

## Summary

- McClellan grants the angel of Yahweh is "treated as if he's Yahweh" and that the messenger **explicitly identifies as God** in [[Scriptures/Genesis 31.13|Genesis 31:13]] ("I am the God of Bethel") and [[Scriptures/Exodus 3.6|Exodus 3:6]] ("I am the God of your father") — more explicitly than Jesus ever does. See [[The Messenger of Yahweh Explicitly Identifies as God]]. [^1]
- He critiques [[Jacob Hansen]]'s LDS doctrine of **divine investiture of authority** (articulated by [[James Talmage]] in his 1916 "The Father and the Son") as a confessional harmonization that "leans on authority rather than logic and data" and fails because no ancient West Asian agent could claim to *be* the king. See [[Divine Investiture of Authority Is an Anachronistic LDS Harmonization]] and [[An Agent Cannot Claim to Be the King in Ancient West Asian Literature]]. [^2] [^3]
- He argues the explicit "I am God" speeches arise from **editorial interpolation** (a later redactor inserting *malʾak* into theophanies of Moses, Jacob, Hagar, Gideon, and Manoah) and are rationalized by imagining the messenger as a sentient **divine image**. See [[The Angel of Yahweh Texts Are Editorial Interpolations]] and [[Divine Images Explain Jesus Relationship to God]]. [^4]
- He rejects [[Avery (God Logic)]]'s Trinitarian/Binitarian reading as "even less supported by the data," noting the Trinity was "constructed between the late 2nd and the 5th centuries CE" and did not exist when John was written. See [[Trinitarianism Is Anachronistic to the Biblical Period]]. [^3] [^5]
- He locates the real "taproot of early Christology" in **name-bearing mediation** ([[Scriptures/Exodus 23.20-21|Exodus 23:21]], "for my name is in him"), evidenced by [[Metatron]] in the Rav Idit tradition and [[Yahoel]], and applied to Jesus' divine-name manifestation in John. See [[Metatron and the Divine Name Are the Taproot of Christology]] and [[Jesus Never Claims Deity as Explicitly as the Messenger Does]]. [^6]
