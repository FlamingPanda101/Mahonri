# Scripture Central — A Deep Dive on the Word of Wisdom (Church History Matters)

- **The Word of Wisdom was not received in a cultural vacuum:** It interacts with the 1830s American temperance and health-reform movements (e.g. [[Sylvester Graham]]), whose concerns "most of the topics covered in this revelation directly reflect." See [[The Word of Wisdom Was Not Received in a Cultural Vacuum]].
- **It is a "living commandment" whose strict-abstinence form developed gradually:** Originally "not by commandment or constraint," it was elevated over roughly a century (Joseph F. Smith ~1905; temple-recommend requirement under Heber J. Grant ~1921). Joseph Smith himself drank wine and beer in moderation. See [[The Word of Wisdom Was a Gradual Living Commandment Not an Instant Prohibition]].
- **Some 1833 counsel is scientifically outdated (tobacco poultices for bruises/cattle; coffee and tea), so the code's basis is faith, not science:** The hosts concede the tobacco-and-coffee counsel hasn't aged like the alcohol counsel, and frame coffee/tea abstinence as a test of obedience (like pork for ancient Israel), grounded in divine accommodation. See [[The Word of Wisdom Reflects Its 1833 Context Including Outdated Health Counsel]].
