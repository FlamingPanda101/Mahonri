# Triggernometry - Tom Holland Islam Christianity and the West

A wide-ranging interview in which historian [[Tom Holland]] develops the *Dominion* thesis — that Western secular modernity is "the water we swim in," pervasively shaped by Christianity even where it imagines itself post-Christian — and extends it to a sharp contrast with **Islam**. Holland argues Christianity's distinctive inheritance is a **concept of the secular** (render unto Caesar; Augustine's two cities) that Islam structurally lacks, which is why a confident secular liberalism keeps misreading Islam as "just another religion" it can absorb.

## Summary

- **The cross as moral inversion.** Holland recovers what familiarity has dulled: crucifixion was the most humiliating, agonizing Roman death, reserved paradigmatically for slaves. Christianity's claim that the *crucified* man is God inverts the entire ancient value system (strength over weakness); [[Paul the Apostle|Paul]] is himself embarrassed by it (it is "foolishness to the Greeks," [[1 Corinthians 1.23|1 Cor 1:23]]). [[Friedrich Nietzsche]] alone among atheists "gets" the cross — and hates it as a slave-morality. See [[The Cross as Moral Inversion of Roman Values]].
- **Christianity's many rivers.** Holland resists a from-nothing reading: Christianity emerges from a matrix of Jewish scripture, Greek philosophy (Plato/Aristotle — "theology is footnotes to Plato"), Persian moral dualism, and the Roman idea of a universal order. The Greek gods are "the malign stepparents of Christianity" — charismatic, petty, cruel (Prometheus, eternally devoured) — whose rejection by the philosophers Christian theology absorbs. See [[Christianity Emerged from Many Rivers Not From Nothing]].
- **Abolition as Pentecostal wildfire.** Holland's mechanism for abolition: a *radical-Protestant* "read scripture with the Spirit" hermeneutic, fused with industrial-era moral revulsion, let Quakers and evangelicals declare slavery wrong even though the Bible nowhere says so. This "spread like wildfire," birthed the first activist movement, and produced **international law** as a framework transcending confessional lines — later turned against the Muslim slave trade. See [[Abolition Spread as Pentecostal Fire from Spirit-Read Scripture]] and the existing [[Christianity Abolished Slavery Twice]].
- **Wokeness is a Christian heresy.** Holland reads 2020 ("a deeply Christian movement") as Quaker-descended activism that has jettisoned the Christian scaffolding — toppling idols, taking the knee, white penitents — and treats slavery as *original sin* and an indelible stain. Cut from its roots, it "mutates very fast." See [[Wokeness Is a Christianity That Jettisoned Its Roots]] and the existing [[Secular Humanism Lives on Borrowed Christian Capital]].
- **Christianity has a concept of the secular; Islam does not.** The headline thesis: "render unto Caesar" ([[Matthew 22.21|Matt 22:21]]) and Augustine's *saeculum* generate a neutral space ("church and state," never "mosque and state"). Islam is a *totalizing* way of life with God-given law governing everything; it has no native category of the secular, which is why it is "uniquely indigestible" to a secular mindset that arrogantly assumes everyone shares its template. See [[Christianity Has a Concept of the Secular Islam Does Not]].
- **Islam's modern trauma and the reformation problem.** Triumphant for most of its history, Islam was shocked by Christian-power dominance from Napoleon's Egypt onward; Muslims responded by "Protestantizing" (jihad-as-internal, get-on-the-right-side-of-history) *or* by going fundamentalist-literalist (ISIS). But because the Quran is the literal, final word of God, a Reformation in the Protestant sense is structurally hard — "this is it." See [[Islam's Reformation Problem and the Literal Quran]].
- **Even atheists see through Christian eyes.** Holland's closing synthesis (which the hosts call the heart of it): a Western atheist who rejects Christ is still a product of Christianity and "looks at the world through Christian eyes." The "be kind, all roads lead to God" civic religion ("Paddingtonism") is itself a mutated post-Christianity — and Holland forecasts a "great choke point" with all four futures (resurgent Christianity, fascist might-is-right, woke heresy, growing Islam) in play at once. See [[Western Secularism Is Post-Christian Not Non-Christian]].
