# The Apologetic Dog — Does Water Baptism Save? (NeedGod vs Shameless Popery)

A formal debate moderated by [[Jeremiah Nortier]] between **[[Ryan (NeedGod)|Ryan]]** (Reformed Baptist, NeedGod.net; **negative** — baptism does not save) and **[[Joe Heschmeyer]]** (Catholic Answers / Shameless Popery; **affirmative** — water baptism saves).

- **Affirmative case: scripture repeatedly says baptism saves** (Mark 16:16; Acts 2:38; 1 Peter 3:21; Acts 22:16; Romans 6; John 3:5), and the early church unanimously read it that way. See [[The New Testament Repeatedly Says Baptism Saves]].
- **Negative case: salvation is by faith alone; baptism is a commanded but non-saving act of obedience** (Abraham/Romans 4; the "one foot on the bus" analogy). See [[Salvation Is by Faith Alone and Baptism Is a Non-Saving Act of Obedience]].
- **Disputed prooftexts:** [[Mark 16.16 Pairs Belief and Baptism but Its Authenticity Is Disputed]]; [[The Causal Eis Reading of Acts 2.38 Lacks Greek Support]]; [[John 3.5 Born of Water and the Spirit Is Disputed Between Baptism and Spiritual Rebirth]]; [[1 Peter 3.21 Says Baptism Now Saves but Both Sides Read It Oppositely]].
- **Underlying theology:** whether baptism is a "work" ([[Baptism Is Gods Work Not a Human Work That Earns Salvation]]; [[Works of the Law Means Mosaic Boundary Markers Not Obedience as Such]]); the appeal to the early church ([[The Unanimous Early Church Read Baptism as Saving]]); OT water types ([[Old Testament Water Types Prefigure Baptism]]).

This is a Protestant-vs-Catholic debate (no LDS participant). Concept notes carry *Apologetic Engagement* + *LDS Relevance*, since the Restoration sides with baptismal efficacy and extends it through proxy baptism for the dead.
