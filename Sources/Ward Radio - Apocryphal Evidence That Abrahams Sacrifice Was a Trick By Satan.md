# Ward Radio - Apocryphal Evidence That Abraham's Sacrifice Was a Trick By Satan!

**Channel:** Ward Radio · **Host:** [[Cardon Ellis]] · **Guests:** [[Jonah Barnes]], [[Oak Norton]], [[Brad Whitbeck]]
**URL:** https://youtu.be/xNzsPp0zu_o

## Summary
- A speculative reconstruction: piecing together the *Book of Jubilees*, *Book of Jasher*, pseudo-Jubilees (Qumran), the targums, and the untranslated **Old Slavonic Palaea**, [[Jonah Barnes]] argues the command to sacrifice Isaac may have originated with **Satan**, not God. Satan (overhearing Isaac and Ishmael argue over the covenant) impersonates the Lord to command the sacrifice, then plays *both sides* — appearing as himself to call it evil — trapping Abraham: obey and Satan "ends the covenant," disobey and Abraham fails the test of faith.
- Supporting hooks: Isaac asks to be **bound** so he cannot resist, trusting in the resurrection (an "Odysseus at the mast" motif); four of ~six apocryphal accounts attribute the idea to Satan; the story parallels Job (Satan's wager). The interpretive payoff is [[James 2.24|James 2:23]] — "Abraham *believed* God and it was imputed unto him for righteousness" — which says he *believed*, not that he *did* the work; on this reading good-faith intention, not completing a wrongful act, is what was counted to him.
- Why it was allegedly removed: the story features a **corporeal, active Satan**, bodily **resurrection**, and a **deceived Abraham** — all things later scribes disliked. [[Hebrews 11.17|Hebrews 11:17-19]] (Abraham "accounting that God was able to raise him up") shows Paul drawing on a non-canonical Abraham tradition. The panel keeps a steady "look for Christ in the story" counter-thread (Brad Whitbeck) and concedes the canonical Akedah carries its own meaning regardless.
