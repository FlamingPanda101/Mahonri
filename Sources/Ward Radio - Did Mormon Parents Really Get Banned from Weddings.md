Ward Radio (host Cardon Ellis with the "No Unhallowed Hand" crew — Joe, Drew, [[Britney Ward Radio]], and [[Austin Falter]]) responds to a TikTok short by ex-Mormon [[John Dehlin]] claiming that "a Mormon parent is not allowed to attend the wedding of their own [child] if they don't give 10% of their annual income to the church," and that non-believing or non-paying parents are left "outside the Mormon temple in shame." The panel's case is that this is a "cynical twisting of a social phenomenon": a *temporary American overcorrection* after the 1960s sexual revolution that pressured couples to be married and sealed the same day, which is **not** an eternal doctrine and **has since been resolved** by the civil-then-temple policy. They argue temple access is covenant-gated, not income-gated, that the word *profane* literally means "outside the temple," and that non-members are toured and welcomed, not shamed.

## Summary

- **The criticism stated.** Dehlin's short: a Latter-day Saint parent "is not allowed to attend the wedding of their own daughter or son if they don't give 10%," is left "outside the Mormon temple in shame… or even if they just lose their faith… or drink a cup of coffee," and this is "spiritual extortion… shaming to non-believers and non-members and destructive to family unity."[^1]
- **Central rebuttal — a post-Sixties overcorrection, not doctrine.** Only after the social upheaval of the 1960s did pressure arise to marry and be sealed *on the same day*; in that scenario non-recommend-holding relatives could not watch the sealing. This was an "extreme minority" American phenomenon, "since been resolved," and the host says Dehlin "knows this." Most weddings across LDS history happened *before* a later temple sealing.[^2] See [[The Same-Day Sealing Squeeze Was a Post-Sixties Overcorrection Not Doctrine]].
- **The prophets prove the point.** The first 20 prophets were sealed on a *different day* from their marriage: Howard W. Hunter waited ~14 years, [[Gordon B. Hinckley]] ~7-8 years, and Thomas S. Monson "was the first president of the church that was sealed the same day that he was married" — "only… two prophets ago." So same-day sealing is "a modern reaction that boomers had," which the host grants was "an overcorrection."[^3]
- **The host's own dissent.** Cardon Ellis repeatedly says he *disliked* the prior policy — "there was indeed a brief period of time where there was a policy that I felt was backwards. And I've been very vocal about that" — distinguishing a real, fixable mistake from Dehlin's "cult pornography" framing. Illustrates [[Mistakes Versus Leading Astray]].[^4]
- **Non-members are welcomed, not shamed.** [[Britney Ward Radio]]'s grandfather was in the Los Angeles Temple presidency; the panel reports the presidency would "leave their office and their job" to give non-recommend-holding families a personal tour of the visitors' center and grounds so they "felt welcomed" — directly contradicting the "sitting with a dunce cap" image.[^5] This is the anecdote-vs-doctrine point of [[Conflating Anecdote and Culture With Doctrine Misrepresents the Church]].
- **The "cup of coffee" exaggeration.** The panel mocks the claim that one cup of coffee strips a temple recommend: any bishop would say "Is that it?" — recommend-worthiness is about covenant fidelity, not a single slip.[^6]
- **A civil option already exists.** A family that wants everyone present can simply hold a civil wedding ("a courthouse… some beach somewhere") and still be free to seek the sealing; conversely a relative who thinks it is "all a bunch of hogwash" loses nothing by the sealing happening — "I might not like art museums, but I'm not going to keep people from going."[^7]
- **"Profane" means outside the temple.** Graded sacred space requiring some standard of worthiness to enter is normal "all over… ancient religion"; the word *profane* "literally means outside the temple," and the Greek *mysteries* originally meant *temple rituals* — so excluding the uninitiated from the inner rite is not a Mormon peculiarity.[^8] See [[Profane Literally Means Outside the Temple]].
- **The temple unites families, it does not divide them.** The sealing is "the entire reason why families can be together forever"; protecting the ordinance is "not meant to exclude but… to include family." The panel calls it self-contradictory for Dehlin to brand as "anti-family" the very rite that binds families eternally.[^9]
- **On Dehlin's motive and method.** The hosts attribute the misrepresentation to financial incentive (citing a reported ~$347-374k salary plus benefits) and to the dynamics of repeating a claim until one believes it; they argue this "5-year-old news" is recycled to make the Church "look extreme."[^10] On the broader pattern that short-form lies outrun long-form truth, see [[The Internet Rewards the Short Lie Over the Long Truth]] and (on the same critic) [[The Hostile Witness - John Dehlin Conceded the Trinitarian-Joseph Talking Point]].
- **Wider conversation.** A long second segment with the young guests surveys today's "front lines" of online criticism — Alyssa Grunfeld, comment-section amalgamation, and the [[Jeremy Runnells|CES Letter]] (rebutted, they note, by Sarah Allen, Jim Bennett, and the "Light and Truth Letter") — and Drew's return to activity via pro-LDS "Come Follow Me" content. This material is context, not part of the temple argument. See [[Dive Into the Primary Sources Before Repeating the Talking Point]].
