# Keystone — "What This Catholic Apologist Isn't Telling You About the Plates"

A [[Keystone]] point-by-point rebuttal of a [[Trent Horn]] (Catholic Answers) Instagram reel claiming the 270,000-word Book of Mormon could not have fit on the golden plates. The host plays the reel and then dismantles its hidden assumptions, then addresses the underlying question of whether the text could plausibly fit.

## Summary
- **Horn's reel.** Horn argues that with at most ~4 unsealed plates (6×8 inches), each character would have to represent 80 English words, which "ancient hieroglyphics don't work like" — citing the plates of Darius (~200 words per page) — implying reformed Egyptian was Joseph's imagination.
- **Horn's character count is far too low.** Reverse-engineering Horn's own numbers: 270,000 ÷ 80 = 3,375 characters total; ÷ 40 plates = ~84 per plate; with both sides, only ~42 characters *per side*. "That is not a lot of characters." Keystone contacted the Etsy maker of the very model Horn shows: it actually has ~128 characters on one side — over three times Horn's estimate. See [[Trent Horns Word-Count Argument Uses Wrong Parameters]] and [[Reformed Egyptian Characters Were Small]].
- **Horn used a scaled-down model.** The whole reel argues the plates were too small while showing a *3/4-scale* model. The full-scale model has ~230 characters on the front of the first plate (almost 5½× Horn's 42), and smaller-font models could hold even more.
- **Plate size and seal fraction are contested.** Joseph estimated 6×8 inches, but [[Orson Pratt]], [[Martin Harris]], and [[David Whitmer]] gave 7×8 or 6×9; the sealed fraction was variously ~2/3 or ~1/2. Larger estimates would hold more content — "a lot more wiggle room... than Trent is representing."
- **The text demonstrably fits (Sjodahl's 1923 test).** [[Janne Sjodahl]] in 1923 hand-copied 14 English pages of the Book of Mormon in Hebrew onto a single 7×8-inch parchment; extrapolated, the whole book fits on ~21 plates, leaving room for a larger font and the lost 116 pages. Mormon says they would have written in Hebrew "if our plates had been sufficiently large," implying reformed Egyptian was more compact than Hebrew. See [[The Book of Mormon Fits on the Plates - Sjodahl Hebrew Test]].
- **Small, fine engravings are attested.** Joseph's Wentworth letter says the plates were "filled with engravings... small and beautifully engraved"; [[John Whitmer]] reported "fine engravings on both sides"; Egyptologist [[John Gee]] found the font comparable to many ancient engraved Hebrew characters.
- **Conclusion.** Horn makes "unwarranted assumptions," misleads with a scaled-down model, and ignores century-old research. The host closes on the epistemics of the witnesses: he cannot prove the plates were real but believes the witnesses, as he believes the witnesses of the Resurrection.
