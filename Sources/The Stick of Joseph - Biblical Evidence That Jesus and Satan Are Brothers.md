# The Stick of Joseph — Biblical Evidence That Jesus and Satan Are Brothers (Response to Cliff Knechtel)

A monologue by host **Hayden Carroll** answering the common evangelical polemic — voiced here by apologist [[Cliff Knechtel]] on the Valuetainment channel — that Latter-day Saints are sub-Christian because "Mormonism teaches Jesus and Lucifer are brothers." Hayden's reply has two moves: (1) the charge only sounds absurd *on Trinitarian premises*; once one rejects the Trinity, Christ, Lucifer, and humans being spirit children of the same Father is coherent; and (2) the Bible is laced with a **"two brothers, younger/righteous one chosen, elder cast out"** motif (and the Day-of-Atonement two goats) that figures exactly this premortal relationship of Christ and Satan. The episode connects to the vault's [[The Younger-Brother Motif Legitimizes Solomons Succession|younger-brother]] and two-goats clusters.

## Summary

- **The charge presupposes the Trinity.** Trinitarians cannot grant the claim because for them Father and Son are the *same being*, so Christ (uncreated) could not be the brother of a created Lucifer. The LDS view — Father, Son, and Spirit as **three distinct beings one in purpose** — removes the contradiction; [[John 17.20-23|John 17:20-23]]'s "that they may be one as we are one" reads oneness as *unity of purpose* extended to disciples, not numerical identity. See [[Jesus and Satan Being Spirit Brothers Follows From Rejecting the Trinity]]; cf. [[I and the Father Are One Means Mutual Indwelling]] and [[The Court Analogy for the Godhead]].[^1][^2]
- **The Trinity is post-biblical.** Hayden locates the doctrine at Nicaea (325 AD, "Constantinople... emperor of Rome gathers everyone") as a philosophical formulation read back onto scripture, not stated in it. (The vault treats Nicaea more carefully elsewhere; see [[Homoousios Was Coined to Refute Arius]].)[^3]
- **The premortal council frame.** God the Father is the father of spirits; Christ, Lucifer, and humans are all his spirit children. Christ was chosen as the Only Begotten for his surpassing light and righteousness; Lucifer, in a [[Revelation 12.4|premortal council]], offered a rival plan to seize the glory and was cast out for pride — a pattern "alluded to in Revelation" and other ancient texts. See [[Two Brothers Where the Younger Is Chosen Are Types of Christ and Lucifer]].[^4]
- **The two-brothers motif as a Christ/Lucifer type.** Hayden runs the relentless Old Testament pattern in which the **elder/firstborn forfeits the birthright and a younger/righteous brother is chosen**, the elder cast out, and the chosen one in *similitude of Christ*: Cain/Abel, Jacob/Esau, Ishmael/Isaac (the "strongest," with [[Hebrews 11.17|Isaac called "only begotten"]] though Ishmael was firstborn), Reuben (loses the birthright to Joseph for sin), Adonijah/Solomon ("Prince of Peace"). In the Book of Mormon, Nephi over Laman and Lemuel. See [[Two Brothers Where the Younger Is Chosen Are Types of Christ and Lucifer]] and the related [[The Younger-Brother Motif Legitimizes Solomons Succession]].[^5][^6]
- **The Day of Atonement two goats.** From the Mishnah tractate *Yoma*, the high priest presents two identical goats: one bears the name **Yahweh** (sacrificed to redeem Israel), the other **Azazel** (a wilderness demon, "could be in similitude of Lucifer") and is driven out / thrown off a cliff. Two beings "of the same stuff": one chosen as the sacrifice for the world's sins, the other cast down. See [[Two Brothers Where the Younger Is Chosen Are Types of Christ and Lucifer]] and [[Leviticus 16]]; cf. [[Barabbas and Jesus Are the Two Goats of the Day of Atonement]].[^7][^8]
- **Jesus likens himself to the serpent.** [[Numbers 21.8-9|the brazen serpent]] lifted by Moses is applied by Jesus to himself ([[John 3.14 Compares Christ to the Serpent Not Just the Lifting Up|John 3:14]]) — Christ comparing himself to "the same type of being that Lucifer is" (the Eden serpent), yet as the Son of God lifted up for eternal life. Builds on [[The Nehushtan Is a Healing Divine Serpent the Nephites and Early Christians Valued]] and [[The Two Serpents of Eden and the Divine Name Switch]].[^9]
- **"Firstborn" need not mean biologically first.** Across these stories the one who receives the birthright is *not* the literal firstborn; "the first shall be last and the last shall be first." Hayden speculates the stories encode something about premortality: Lucifer as "son of the morning" (seemingly first) cast down for pride, Christ lifted up for goodness — one "becomes the firstborn" by choosing to follow God. He repeatedly flags this as "swimming in 'I don't know' soup," offers Knechtel an on-camera conversation, and urges viewers to read the stories themselves.[^10][^11]
