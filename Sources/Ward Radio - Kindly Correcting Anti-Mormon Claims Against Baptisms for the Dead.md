# Ward Radio — Kindly Correcting Anti-Mormon Claims Against Baptisms for the Dead

Host [[Cardon Ellis]], [[Jonah Barnes]] ("associate professor of all things apocryphal"), and Luke
Hansen respond to a Catholic YouTuber (channel "Catholic Truth," host Brian) who argues Latter-day
Saints "don't understand the Bible," using **baptism for the dead** as his example, and to Evangelical
commenters claiming LDS biblical literacy "plummeted" in a 2019 survey.

## Summary
- **The objection:** baptism for the dead rests on "the only verse in the whole Bible"
  ([[1 Corinthians 15.29|1 Corinthians 15:29]]), which is "ambiguous," "random," and that "most scholars
  don't understand" — "yet Mormons constructed an entire doctrine around it." The hosts note the irony of
  a Catholic arguing like a *sola scriptura* Evangelical.[^1]
- **Rebuttal (see [[The One Ambiguous Verse Objection to Baptism for the Dead Fails]]):**
  (1) there is a *second* verse, [[1 Peter 4.6|1 Peter 4:6]], which with the Great Commission
  ([[Matthew 28.19|Matthew 28:19]]) implies baptizing the dead; (2) "only one verse" proves nothing —
  many truths appear once, *and the objector's own celibacy/infant-baptism rest on one-or-zero verses*;
  (3) neither Latter-day Saints nor Catholics are *sola scriptura* — LDS continuing revelation
  ([[D&C 128]]/[[D&C 138]]) clarifies the ambiguous text, so it's not "built on" that verse; (4) "scholars
  don't agree, so dismiss it" is the Pharisees' move, and would gut the **Trinity** (which top Catholic
  apologists concede no one could derive solo); (5) Paul cites the rite to *prove the resurrection*, so
  it's load-bearing, not idle.[^2]
- **The biblical-literacy detour:** the hosts defend the claim that Latter-day Saints rank at/near the
  top in Bible knowledge. The alleged 2019 "25-point plummet" is a survey artifact — it conflates a
  Bible-only subscore with an all-topics score, uses a changed sampling mode (Ipsos panel vs random
  phone), oversamples/self-selects "Mormons," carries the highest margin of error, skews younger via the
  birthday method, and includes non-LDS-vocabulary items (Trinity, Purgatory, sola fide, Prosperity
  Gospel). On biblical questions alone LDS remained ~#2. See [[The 2019 Pew Biblical Literacy Plummet Is
  a Survey Artifact]].[^3]
- They close by inviting the Catholic creator ("Brian"/"Michael Shannon") onto the show for a "web
  redemption," affirming respect for Catholicism as a fellow non-*sola-scriptura*, authority-claiming
  church.
