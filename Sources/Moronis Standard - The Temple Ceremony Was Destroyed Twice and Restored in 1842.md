# The Temple Ceremony Was Destroyed At Least Twice in Ancient Times. Joseph Smith Restored It in 1842.

[[Alex Arnold]] of *Moroni's Standard* argues that the Latter-day Saint **endowment** is not a 19th-century invention (and not borrowed from Freemasonry) but the recovery of an ancient initiatory mystery that was suppressed in **two documented stages** — first by [[Josiahs Reform and the First Temple Make Restoration a Coherent Category|Josiah's 621 BC reform]], then by the early church councils that condemned and burned texts like the [[The Acts of John Records Jesus Instituting a Mystery Ritual|Acts of John]]. The episode's distinctive payload is the "**direction of the evidence**" argument: ancient sources that were *inaccessible to Joseph Smith* (the Dead Sea Scrolls in 1947, the *Acts of John* in 1924, [[Margaret Barker]]'s First-Temple reconstruction across the 1980s–2000s) keep *confirming* specific endowment content after his 1844 death — the opposite of how fraud behaves.

Arnold leans on non-LDS authorities — [[Margaret Barker]] (Methodist), [[John Walton]] (evangelical), [[Michael Heiser]], the patristics scholarship around the *Acts of John*, and historian [[Steven Bullock]] on Freemasonry — and repeatedly disclaims certainty, flagging that the extent of lay access in pre-exilic Israel "remains debated" and that he treads carefully around content kept sacred.

## Summary
- **The First Temple housed deification/divine-council theology that Josiah's reform stripped out.** Via [[Margaret Barker|Barker]] and [[Michael Heiser|Heiser]], the pre-reform temple contained divine-council cosmology ([[Psalm 82.1|Psalm 82]], [[Deuteronomy 32.8-9|Deut 32:8]] "sons of God"), a high priest who underwent transformation on Yom Kippur, sacred garments, anointings, veil ceremonies, and *direct access to God* for ordinary Israelites — the element the reform "eliminated the most completely." See [[The First Temple Contained the Endowment Pattern Barker Reconstructed Independently]].
- **The endowment matches Barker's reconstruction element-by-element.** Sacred clothing, new names, signs tied to covenants, a creation–fall–mortality–return drama enacted in first person, and a veil ceremony — structured as *dromena, legomena, deiktumena* (things done, said, shown). [[John Walton|Walton]] adds that Adam and Eve are priestly figures whose post-fall garments (*kothoneth*, Genesis 3) use the same Hebrew word as the Aaronic vestments of [[Exodus 28|Exodus 28]] — making Eden and the endowment "the same story in two forms." See [[The First Temple Contained the Endowment Pattern Barker Reconstructed Independently]] and [[The Garment Shares the Hebrew Word of the Priestly Vestments]].
- **The content was removed a second time by the early church.** The *Acts of John* (94–96), a 2nd-c. text the councils ordered burned, depicts Jesus forming his apostles into a circle, leading a responsive ritual with commanded silence, and instructing them to imitate his gestures — first translated into English in 1924, 82 years after the 1842 endowment. The same circle-ritual content recurs independently in the Gospel of Bartholomew and the Qasr el-Wizz codex (recovered in the 1960s). See [[The Acts of John Records Jesus Instituting a Mystery Ritual]] and [[The Mysteries Were Converted From Lived Experience Into Doctrine]].
- **Freemasonry lacks the specific content, so the borrowing charge fails on its own terms.** Joseph was initiated in March 1842, weeks before the May endowment, so suspecting a link is reasonable — but [[Steven Bullock]]'s documentation shows Masonry has degrees, tokens, oaths, and the Hiram Abiff legend (which the initiate *observes*), and lacks the first-person creation drama, veil ceremony, deification theology, and divine-council cosmology. The honest question is "*where specifically* is this content in Masonic ritual?" See [[The Masonry Objection Must Locate the Specific Content Not the General Pattern]] and the vault's [[Endowment Elements Predate Joseph Smith's Freemasonry]], [[Temple Origins Run Deeper Than Freemasonry]].
- **The direction of the evidence is the argument.** Fraud is exposed *over* time; here each decade of scholarship *adds* to the case — the Mahijah/Mahujah name in the Dead Sea Scrolls Book of Giants (matching 1830 LDS scripture), the [[Deuteronomy 32.8-9|Deut 32:8]] "sons of God" variant, Barker's ritual theology, and the *Acts of John* — all confirmed only after Joseph's death. See [[The Direction of the Evidence Runs Opposite to How Fraud Behaves]].
- **The temple's architecture encodes ancient cosmology.** Baptism for the dead in the lowest point (the primordial waters), higher ordinances ascending through the building (height = proximity to the divine), and eastward-to-westward orientation toward the Holy of Holies — with washings/anointings already in [[Exodus 28|Exodus 29]] and Leviticus 8, and proxy baptism referenced by Paul in 1 Corinthians 15.
- **Three explanations remain, and the argument holds only if all fail.** Coincidence (defeated by the *number* and *specificity* of correspondences), borrowing from Masonry (defeated by the missing content), and fraud (defeated by post-death confirmation) — leaving "come and see."
