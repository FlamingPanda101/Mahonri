# The Stick of Joseph — Sacred Temple Connection to Priesthood Keys

An interview on the [[The Stick of Joseph - Are Christians Becoming Mormon with Brian Thomassen|Stick of Joseph]] channel with artist **Caleb Williams** ([[Caleb Williams (Godwin)|"Godwin," the Library of Godwin]]) about his illustrated book *Doctrines and the Mysteries*, walking through one plate — a discourse on the power of the priesthood by [[George Q. Cannon]] — to unpack how [[Melchizedek|Melchizedek]]/Aaronic [[Priesthood Authority Is the Necessary But Not Sufficient Dividing Line|priesthood]], temple worship, and [[Apostasy Was Complete When the Keys Departed|priesthood keys]] interlock across the dispensations.

## Summary
- **The priesthood structure mirrors the temple's "kings and queens, priests and priestesses."** The hosts notice that [[Melchizedek]] is a *king* (Hebrew *melek-tsedeq*, "king of righteousness") and Aaron a *priest*, so the dual Aaronic/Melchizedek order maps directly onto the temple language of becoming kings/queens and priests/priestesses; the Melchizedek (higher) priesthood holds the **sealing power**, which — like an earthly throne passed by bloodline — adopts the sealed person into Christ's royal line [^1][^2]. See [[The Aaronic-Melchizedek Dualism Maps the Temple's Kings and Priests]].
- **A theophany or angelic visitation does not by itself confer authority to act.** Cannon stresses, and the hosts amplify, that despite the First Vision, Moroni, and other manifestations, Joseph Smith never tried to baptize or officiate until the priesthood was *explicitly* conferred ([[John the Baptist]] at the [[Aaronic Priesthood Restoration|Aaronic restoration]]; [[Peter]], James, and John for the Melchizedek) — proof, the hosts argue, of Joseph's sincerity and "temperate, sober-minded" restraint [^3][^4]. See [[A Vision Does Not Confer Priesthood Authority]].
- **Priesthood keys are the skeleton of all seven dispensations** — Godwin's plate shows Christ as the great High Priest at the top, [[Adam]] (the Ancient of Days) receiving the first keys, then Melchizedek/Aaron, Moses, Moroni, Elias and [[Elijah]], down to Joseph; the globe bears seven keyholes and Joseph holds a seven-sealed scroll (Revelation), each seal a dispensation whose head was committed keys. "The story of the priesthood is the story of humanity from the very beginning to the very end" [^5][^6]. See [[Priesthood Keys Frame the Seven Dispensations]].
- **Unrighteous dominion condemns the priesthood holder but (the hosts conclude) does not nullify the ordinance for the recipient.** Reading [[D&C 121.39|D&C 121]] ("amen to the priesthood... of that man") alongside Cannon's warning to "carefully keep within those limits," they distinguish *power* from *authority* from *keys*, and reason that the line of authority still flows to the recipient even when an individual administrator is "a broken vessel" [^7][^8]. See [[Unrighteous Dominion Condemns the Holder Not the Ordinance]].
- **God accepts the labors of sincere reformers who lacked priesthood authority.** Cannon names [[John Wesley|Wesley]], [[Martin Luther|Luther]], [[John Calvin|Calvin]], and Wycliffe as men of "the highest and purest motives" who could not validly administer saving ordinances yet whom God "whispered peace to," grounding the church's ecumenical posture; the hosts pair this with the [[The Creeds Were an Abomination Meant Sectarian Dogmatism Not the Ecumenical Creeds|"creeds (not Christians) are an abomination"]] reading from [[Joseph Smith|Joseph Smith History]] [^9][^10].
