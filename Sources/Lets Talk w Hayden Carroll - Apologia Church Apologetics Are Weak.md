# Let's Talk w/ Hayden Carroll — Apologia Church Apologetics Are Weak (Ft. Apologia Studios)

A rebuttal video in which [[Hayden Carroll]] responds to [[Jeff Durbin]]'s (Apologia Church / Apologia Studios) reaction video. Durbin had played a few minutes of Carroll's earlier hour-long conversation with **Austin**, an Apologia Church member (see [[Austin (Apologia Church)]]), and Carroll here interleaves Durbin's clips with point-by-point counters. The thesis is methodological: Carroll argues that the Reformed **presuppositional** method on display "is actually giving the strongest position he has — a foundation completely built on totally unjustified presuppositions," and that the more he engages "hostile creedal Christians," the weaker their position looks.[^1] The video closes with a long played excerpt of an LDS general-conference address on continuing revelation and a formal debate challenge to Durbin.

## Summary
- **Presuppositionalism = self-admitted circularity.** Carroll plays a clip of an Apologia member conceding "we believe the Bible is true because the Bible says it's true... the only reason we believe it's true is because God makes us believe... in terms of reasoning it is circular." Carroll uses this as an admission that the Reformed side cannot justify its own standard.[^2] This restates the dedicated [[The Circularity Charge Against Self-Attesting Scripture]] and [[Presuppositional Self-Authentication Is Unbiblical Human Philosophy]]; see also former Apologia apologist [[Daniel Constantino]]'s parallel concession.
- **Sola scriptura is an unjustified presupposition, not "revelation."** Durbin calls *sola scriptura* "a doctrine that's based upon revelation." Carroll's standing rebuttal: show where God *publicly* identified the 66-book canon and declared it infallible and ultimate — and if you appeal to revelation to establish *sola scriptura*, that revealing source outranks scripture, so the doctrine is self-defeating.[^3] Covered by [[The Three Unjustified Protestant Canon Presuppositions]], [[The Word of God Fallacy - The Load-Bearing Premise Is Unproven]], and [[Authorization - Divine Designation Required for an Infallible Canon]].
- **The unevangelized dilemma (NEW).** Durbin says one cannot know murder is wrong "before the Bible," yet his own theology condemns those who never heard the gospel because the moral law is "written on everybody's hearts" (Romans 1–2). Carroll presses the contradiction: if the law on the heart suffices to condemn the unevangelized, then one *can* know moral truth without the canon. See [[The Unevangelized Dilemma Refutes Cant-Know-Morality-Without-Scripture]].[^4]
- **"Pull rank" and the canon problem.** Carroll notes Durbin can only assert the canon's infallibility ("pull rank") — the same phrase [[Daniel Constantino]] used for why he left Apologia. He stacks the standard Protestant canon dilemmas: 66 vs. 73 books, [[R.C. Sproul]]'s "fallible collection of infallible books," and the fact that the early church received and later rejected books.[^5] See [[The Fallible Canon Is Hostage to Critical Scholarship]] and [[The Church Once Accepted Books It Later Rejected]].
- **2 Timothy 3:16 refers to the Old Testament.** When Durbin appeals to [[2 Timothy 3.16-17|2 Tim 3:16]], Carroll notes the "scriptures" Timothy knew "from a child" were the Old Testament, so the verse cannot establish the modern 66-book (NT-inclusive) canon as infallible.[^6] See [[Theopneustos Means Life-Giving Not Infallible]].
- **The Mosaic-authorship / anonymous-authorship double standard (NEW).** Durbin insists [[Moses]] wrote Genesis (on the authority of Jesus' quotations); Carroll cites the [[Documentary Hypothesis Strengthens the Restoration|documentary hypothesis]], [[Deuteronomy 34|Deuteronomy 34]] (Moses' own death narrated) and the woven J/E seams in [[Genesis 37|Genesis 37]] (the Ishmaelite-vs-Midianite contradiction) to deny single authorship — then asks why Durbin demands Mosaic authorship of Genesis while accepting *anonymous* books like Hebrews as scripture. See [[The Anonymous-Authorship Double Standard on Genesis]] and [[Internal Anachronisms Show Moses Did Not Write the Whole Pentateuch]].[^7]
- **The positive LDS case: God, not a book, is the ultimate authority.** Carroll closes with a long played clip of an LDS apostle ([[Jeffrey R. Holland]], "My Words Never Cease") arguing that "virtually every prophet... added scripture to that received by his predecessors," quoting [[N.T. Wright]] ("all authority... is given to *me*," not to the books) and [[Doug Wilson]] ("Protestants... have no doctrine of the table of contents of the Bible"). The ultimate authority is the living God, of which scripture is a manifestation.[^8] See [[God Himself Not a Book Is the Ultimate Authority]], [[Sola Scriptura Muzzles God]], and [[The Word of God Was Never Confined to the Written Word]].
- Carroll issues a **formal moderated-debate challenge** to Durbin on the resolution that the 66-book canon is the infallible and ultimate standard.[^9]
