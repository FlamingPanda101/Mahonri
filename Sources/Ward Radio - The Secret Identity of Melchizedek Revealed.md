A **Ward Radio** episode in which host **[[Cardon Ellis]]** with **[[Kwaku (All Those In Favor)|Kwaku]]** and **Jonah Barnes** argue the traditional identification that **[[Shem]]**, son of [[Noah]], is **[[Melchizedek]]**, the priest-king of Salem. The substance is drawn from **David J. Ridges'** *Old Testament Made Easier* (which supplies a seven-point list) and a **November 1973 *Ensign*** article, framed by the hosts' running gag that this is the "villain reveal / Switcheroo" moment of the Old Testament. The episode is half checklist, half comedy bit.

## Summary

- **The puzzle of Melchizedek.** Melchizedek appears in Genesis 14 "out of nowhere," with no recorded birth or ancestry, important enough that Abraham pays him tithes and the higher priesthood is named after him — yet the record is nearly silent about both him and Shem [^1].
- **Ridges' seven reasons (paraphrased).** (1) Shem inherited the land of **Salem**, and Melchizedek is **king of Salem**. (2) Shem reigned in righteousness and the priesthood came through him; **Melchizedek** means "king of righteousness." (3) Shem was the great high priest of his day; Abraham honored Melchizedek by seeking a blessing and paying tithes, and Abraham stands next to Shem in the patriarchal order. (4)/(5) **Jewish tradition** identifies Shem as Melchizedek. (6) [[Joseph F. Smith]]'s vision (D&C 138) names **Shem** among the great patriarchs but makes **no mention of Melchizedek**. (7) *Times and Seasons* (Dec. 15, 1844, p. 746) speaks of "Shem, who was Melchizedek" [^2].
- **The "smoking gun" scripture.** [[D&C 84.14|D&C 84:14]]: Abraham received the priesthood from Melchizedek, "who received it through the lineage of his fathers, even till Noah." The hosts note the phrasing is "fuzzy" — whether priesthood passes *through* Noah or *after* him via the next holder, Shem [^3].
- **The lineage gap.** In Joseph Smith's *Lectures on Faith* timeline (Adam → ... → Noah → Shem), Shem appears and then the record "just stops," while Melchizedek appears with no beginning — "Shem's life starts and Melchizedek's life ends," so the two could be one [^4].
- **The name-change analogy.** As Jacob became Israel for his righteousness, Shem (righteous, inheriting Salem, becoming its king) could have been given the throne-name Melchizedek [^5].
- **Comedy frame and caveats.** Extended bits on Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles, a hypothetical Megan Fox thumbnail, Theosophy, and Gnosticism punctuate the argument; the hosts repeatedly concede the topic is "insulated" and that almost no one has lost faith over Melchizedek's identity. They close affirming they personally find the Shem-Melchizedek identification persuasive [^6].
