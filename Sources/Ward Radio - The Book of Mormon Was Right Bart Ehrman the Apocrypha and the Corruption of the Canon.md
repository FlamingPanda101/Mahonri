# Ward Radio — The Book of Mormon Was Right! Bart D. Ehrman, The Apocrypha, and the Corruption of the Canon

A Ward Radio episode in which host **[[Cardon Ellis]]**, with **[[Jonah Barnes]]** (promoting *The Key to the Keystone*) and **[[Dave Butler]]** (*In the Language of Adam*), argues that **[[Bart Ehrman]]**'s textual-critical scholarship, followed to its conclusion, supports rather than refutes the Book of Mormon.

## Summary
- **Ehrman's claim.** Ehrman argues no Jew before Christianity read Isaiah 53 / the suffering-servant passages as predicting a crushed, executed Messiah — "to call Jesus the Messiah made no sense… virtually by definition" — so Christians read messianism *into* the Old Testament. The hosts grant this is correct *as a description of the surviving canon*. See [[Ehrman Is Right That the Canon Lacks the Suffering Messiah]].
- **The Book of Mormon "kicks down the door."** Where the Old Testament barely whispers the Messiah (always veiled in poem/allegory), the Book of Mormon "spouts messianism from the first page" — and Nephi/Jacob claim *all* the prophets testified of the Messiah (Jacob: none "save they did testify of" him). Lehi is nearly killed in Jerusalem for preaching it — you stone a prophet for *truth*, not nonsense. See [[The Book of Mormon Claims Messianism Was Preached From the Beginning]]. (Cf. existing [[Prophets Were Killed for Preaching the Messiah Before 600 BC]].)
- **The apocryphal wave of pre-Christian messianism.** A screen of texts (Egyptian Life of Adam and Eve, Ascension of Isaiah, History of John, Testament of Benjamin, Testament of Levi) purport to be pre-Christian yet contain specific suffering-Messiah content; scholars dismiss them as Christian forgeries *because* they mention Christ — circular, the hosts say, once the Book of Mormon establishes Christ-before-Christ. The **Testament of Naphtali** is the showcase: dismissed as a 16th-c. forgery, then found at Qumran (~100 BC). See existing [[Testament of Naphtali Was Vindicated by the Dead Sea Scrolls]].
- **Restorers, not innovators (Dave Butler).** The deeper "softened" thesis: first-temple religion had multiple descendants (rabbinic Jews, Christians, gnostics, Samaritans, Mandaeans); Jesus and John came to *restore* the ancient Israelite religion, not invent a new one — which is why the gospel writers are intimately Jewish yet furious at the *scribes* who "perverted the canon." See [[Christianity Is a Restoration of First-Temple Religion Not an Invention]].
- **"Commit to the bit."** Running gag: Ehrman (like Nietzsche and — for SEO — Donald Trump) is admirable because he *follows critical method all the way through*; the inconsistent move is the Christian who critiques the apocrypha critically but exempts the canon.
