# Ward Radio — The Nicene Creed Is Not an Abomination

- **Central reversal:** The guest (Orthocardia) argues that Latter-day Saints have *nothing* to disagree with in the **Nicene Creed of 325** once it is read in its original 4th-century context — the LDS quarrel is with the later *Athanasian* Creed and *Westminster Confession*, and with the modern (21st-century) interpretation of Nicene language, not with the 325 text itself. [^1]
- **"Creeds are an abomination" reinterpreted:** Citing **John W. Welch**, the guest argues that in 1820s American English "creed" meant "any system of principles which are believed and professed," so Joseph Smith's First Vision rebuke targeted the *divisive sectarian dogmatism* of Protestant New England, not the ecumenical creeds Joseph would not have known. [^2]
- **The Creed as anti-Trinitarian proof-text:** The guest claims the Creed's opening ("I believe in one God, the Father almighty") and "born of the Father before all ages" / "God from God" encode the **monarchy of the Father** and the subordination of the Son — so the Creed actually *refutes* co-equal Western Trinitarianism rather than supporting it. [^3]
- **Homoousios = "same essence," which Mormons embrace and extend:** The guest accepts *homoousios* in its 4th-century sense and "doubles down," asserting that not only is Christ the same essence as the Father, but *all humanity* is the same essence as the Father. [^4]
- **The Filioque as a later addition:** The phrase "and the Son" (Filioque) was added centuries after 325 and helped cause the 1054 Great Schism; the guest turns the Protestant/Catholic "you added to the gospel" charge back on them: "why did you add to the Nicene Creed 6 to 700 years after?" [^5]
