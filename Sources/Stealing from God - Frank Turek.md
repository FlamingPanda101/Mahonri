# Stealing from God

Frank Turek's 2014 cumulative case that **atheists must "steal from God"** — borrow realities that only theism can ground — in order to argue against Him. Turek organizes the book around the acronym **CRIMES**: Causality, Reason, Information & Intentionality, Morality, Evil, Science. In each domain he argues that the very tools the atheist uses presuppose a theistic universe.

## Summary
- **The "stolen concept" strategy:** the New Atheist case relies on causality, logic/reason, objective morality, the reality of evil, and the intelligibility of nature — features Turek argues are better explained by, or only make sense given, the God of theism [^1].
- **CRIMES, chapter by chapter:** Causality (the universe began and is fine-tuned → a cause beyond it); Reason (laws of logic and reliable rationality fit a mind, not matter in motion); Information & Intentionality (DNA's code and mental aboutness point beyond matter); Morality (objective moral obligations need a moral Lawgiver); Evil (the problem of evil presupposes an objective good); Science (science assumes an orderly, rational cosmos) [^2].
- **Positive case:** a "four-point case for mere Christianity" — truth exists, God exists, miracles are possible, and the New Testament is historically reliable [^3].
- **Apologetic use (TF Level-1, existence of God):** an accessible **ally** for theism against New Atheism. Mostly congenial to Latter-day Saint apologetics; note where specifics diverge from LDS theology (e.g., Turek assumes *creation ex nihilo* in the causality argument, which LDS thought rejects — flag rather than endorse).
