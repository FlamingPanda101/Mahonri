# Ward Radio — The Book of Mormon Is Undebunkable

A Ward Radio episode in which host **Cardell** interviews **[[Devin Barnes]]** of *Latter-day Logic* on why the Book of Mormon "is the silver bullet because it's undebunkable" and why Latter-day Saint theology is "the most logical of all religions." Barnes recounts building a TikTok live-stream apologetics ministry, then walks through his case that no naturalistic theory can explain the book and that LDS doctrine is uniquely coherent because its beliefs interlock as axioms.

## Summary
- **"Most logical" via an axiomatic system.** Barnes models LDS doctrine as interlocking axioms (uncreated matter, eternal spirits, three degrees of glory) tracked in an Obsidian "webbed framework" with "no isolated beliefs"; he contrasts this with mainstream Christianity's appeals to mystery and "God transcends logic" [^1].
- **The "undebunkable" thesis.** The Book of Mormon is naturalistically inexplicable; even granting every proposed source (Ethan Smith / *View of the Hebrews*, etc.) "would only comprise 10% of the Book of Mormon," leaving the olive-tree allegory, war chapters, Enos, Ether geography, and hundreds of internal place-names unaccounted for [^2]. See [[No Naturalistic Source Accounts for the Whole Book of Mormon]].
- **Top three reasons it's undebunkable:** linguistic/literary complexity, historical consistency, and the witnesses — a feat "even a PhD couldn't have produced... without decades of research and online access," let alone "with 18th-century tools" and the consistency of "the 27 people that interacted with the plates" [^3]. See [[Dictation Feat Exceeds Joseph Smith's Capacity]].
- **The witnesses and the real plates.** Beyond the formal witnesses, Oliver Cowdery's scribal notes evidence oral transcription (not copying a draft), and the mobs pursuing the plates show they were real metal, not the "tin" of Dan Vogel's pious-fraud theory — "why else would these mobs be trying to get the gold plates... if they were just made of tin?" [^4]. See [[Book of Mormon Witnesses Are the Strongest Evidence]].
- **Prophetic-credentialing parity.** Abraham, Noah, Jonah, and Moses were all called *without* evidence; Joseph Smith uniquely got tangible evidence (plates + witnesses) "because the world needed evidence" — so it is inconsistent to say you'd have believed Abraham as a neighbor but not Joseph Smith [^5]. See [[You Would Believe Abraham as Your Neighbor but Not Joseph Smith]].
- **Moses–Genesis translation parallel.** Moses produced Genesis about events before his lifetime "through revelation... no resources," "the same way Joseph Smith received the book of Moses" — normalizing revelatory translation [^6]. See [[Moses Received Genesis by Revelation as Joseph Smith Received the Book of Moses]].
- **Trinitarians "steal words."** To reconcile contradictions, creedal Christianity redefined synonyms (person/being, *consubstantiation*) "kind of like what the transgender activists did to the word gender" — superimposing a new meaning on historical texts [^7]. See [[Persons-and-Beings Were Redefined Like Gender and Sex]] and [[Consubstantial Is a Mickey Mouse English Translation of Homoousios]].
- **No Bible verses cut.** Barnes claims LDS theology requires deleting no scripture (baptism for the dead, three heavens, premortal existence, Satan among the "sons of God" in [[Job 1]]), whereas mainstream Christianity must ignore such verses [^8].
