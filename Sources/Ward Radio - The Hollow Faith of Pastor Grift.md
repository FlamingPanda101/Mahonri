# Ward Radio — The Hollow Faith of Pastor Grift

- The panel walks through [[Ryan Burge]]'s analysis of the **National Survey of Religious Leaders**, showing wide variance in what U.S. clergy "definitely believe" by tradition — Catholic and mainline Protestant clergy score strikingly low on the literal existence of [[Adam]] and Eve, [[Jesus of Nazareth|Jesus]]'s bodily resurrection, hell, and miraculous healing, while Evangelical and Black Protestant clergy score very high.[^1][^4][^9] See [[Mainline Clergy Doubt Core Christian Doctrines]].
- A running methodological debate frames the episode: one host (the self-described non-scholar with a PhD) repeatedly argues the binary "definitely believe / not" format makes survey data unreliable noise, while [[Jonah Barnes]] insists the *variance between groups* is real signal even if absolute numbers are soft.[^2][^7] See [[Survey Data Is Unreliable Objection]] and [[Variance Between Groups Is Real Signal Rebuttal]].
- [[Jonah Barnes]] argues the "grift" label fits poorly: the highest-believing clergy (Evangelicals) are the ones most accused of monetization, while the lowest-believing (mainline, Catholic) are comparatively poorly paid and do much of the on-the-ground charity — so disbelief and money-seeking do not track together.[^11][^15] See [[Grift Does Not Track With Disbelief Rebuttal]].
- The hosts repeatedly note that **bodily resurrection** and an **embodied Christ** create a tension for Trinitarian clergy — a youth pastor worried about heresy may hesitate to affirm a *bodily* risen Christ co-substantial with a bodiless Father, an argument they attribute to [[Jacob Hansen]].[^5] See [[Embodied Risen Christ Strains the Trinity]].
- The episode closes on the LDS reading of the data: declining mainline belief is framed as the prophesied "awful situation" of [[Ether 3.14|Ether]]/[[Mormon]], the "therapeutic deism" of a corrupted Christianity, and a vindication of [[Galatians 1.8|Galatians 1:8]] — while the apparent outlier "6%" believing in astrology is jokingly claimed for Latter-day Saints via [[Abraham 3.25|Abraham 3]].[^13][^17] See [[Mainline Decline as Prophesied Apostasy]] and [[The 6% Astrology Outlier and Abraham 3]].
