# Ward Radio — New Discoveries Reveal the Sin of The Giants and Why God Flooded the Earth

A Ward Radio episode in which host **[[Cardon Ellis]]** and **[[Jonah Barnes]]** argue that the antediluvian "sin of the giants" — narrated across the Books of Enoch, Jasher, the *Book of the Giants*, and other texts — was a deliberate campaign of sterilization, abortion, and child-sacrifice to cut off human reproduction and thereby prevent the incarnation of the Messiah, which is why God flooded the earth and "pressed reset."

## Summary
- **The giants (sons of Seth) introduced sterilization.** Drawing on Jasher 2:19-21 (wives made to "drink a draught that would render them barren… that their figures might not fade"), 1 Enoch, the *Book of the Giants* (a fragmentary Dead Sea Scrolls text), and Pirke de-Rabbi Eliezer ("we will restrain ourselves from multiplying"), Barnes argues sterility became "pandemic." See [[The Sin of the Giants Was Sterilization to Stop the Messiahs Birth]].[^1]
- **Joseph Smith's "tent-keepers" and "wild man."** [[Moses 6.37-38|Moses 6:37-38]] has people hiring "tent-keepers" (guards, against rampant theft/graverobbing — fulfilling the "slippery" curse) and calling Enoch "a wild man." Barnes reads "wild" via Webster's 1828 as *fertile/reproducing* — Enoch as a rare natural birth. See [[The Sin of the Giants Was Sterilization to Stop the Messiahs Birth]].[^2]
- **Enoch and Noah as wonder-children.** Enoch asks "why have you called me when I am but a lad?" — because, on this reading, there had been no births in 65 years; the Genesis Apocryphon's contested-paternity birth of Noah fits the same anomaly. See [[Book of Enoch - Birth of Noah as Wonder Child]].[^3]
- **The key verse: 3 Enoch 44:8 and Matthew 24:37.** God asks "how could I deliver my great right hand… by their hands?" — i.e., if every baby is aborted, the firstborn Son cannot be born; "the end of all flesh is before me" (Genesis 6) means God *witnessing* the end of flesh. Matthew 24:37 ("as the days of Noah") warns it recurs. See [[The Sin of the Giants Was Sterilization to Stop the Messiahs Birth]].[^4]
- **God tried war and famine first.** Before the flood, the panel says, God sent a war (Revelation concerning Adam) and a famine/drought (Genesis 5:29; Pirke de-Rabbi Eliezer) — details absent from the King James text but, they argue, "pegged" by Joseph Smith.[^5]
- **The Mandaean "Satan remembers" passage.** From the *Ladam* (French, Jacques Matter): Satan, knowing Jehovah would be born of the seed of Eve, plots to "tear them from their domestic hearths" and stop reproduction — a "pre-emergent" strategy against the Messiah. The gospel is framed as the opposite: fertility, family, vineyards.[^6]

## Notable framing / rhetoric
- Explicitly anti-abortion/"feminism"/"wokeism," repeatedly self-aware ("I don't want to get political… I'm just reading the texts").
- Comparative-mythology excursus: Phoenician El/Elus, Kronos, and Canaanite child-sacrifice deities who "consume their children" as inverted echoes of the antediluvian sin.
- Closing exhortation: "have more babies than you think you can, and sooner than you think you should."
