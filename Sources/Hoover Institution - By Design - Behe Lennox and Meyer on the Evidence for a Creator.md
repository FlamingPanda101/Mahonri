# By Design — Behe, Lennox, and Meyer on the Evidence for a Creator

An episode of *Uncommon Knowledge* (Hoover Institution), hosted by Peter Robinson, gathering biochemist [[Michael Behe]], mathematician [[John Lennox]], and philosopher of science [[Stephen C. Meyer]] to argue that recent science has *disconfirmed* Darwinism and points positively toward an intelligent cause.[^1] The three press three lines of attack on neo-Darwinism — the fossil record (abrupt appearance), the mathematics of building functional proteins, and the irreducible complexity of molecular machines — then defend the inference to a designing mind, distinguishing it from a god-of-the-gaps argument.[^2] A recurring sub-theme is whether design science must propose a *mechanism* (Behe explores one; Meyer argues a mental cause needs none) and why the design hypothesis provokes such institutional hostility.[^3]

## Participants
- [[Michael Behe]] · [[John Lennox]] · [[Stephen C. Meyer]] · host Peter Robinson

## Summary
- **The three claim Darwin has become *harder*, not easier, to believe.** Unlike Einstein's relativity, which successive observations keep confirming, they hold that Darwin's theory "has been progressively disconfirmed by multiple observations in multiple subdisciplines of biology."[^4]
- **Meyer — the fossil record shows abrupt appearance, not gradual branching.** The Cambrian explosion (and many later bursts) introduces new body plans suddenly, with no intermediates; 160 years of digging has produced *more* abrupt forms and *no* missing links, and Gould–Eldredge's punctuated equilibrium described this pattern without supplying a mechanism to build the new animals (i.e., the needed information).[^5]
- **Lennox — the maths of the origin of life don't work, and the information is *linguistic*.** Citing Fred Hoyle's calculations and biologists like Dennis Noble ("neo-Darwinism… needs to be replaced"), Lennox argues random processes cannot produce the language-like code of the genome; you "can't get from chemistry to code."[^6]
- **Behe — the cell is full of irreducibly complex molecular machines.** Like a mousetrap, machines such as the bacterial flagellum need all their parts at once to function, so they cannot be built by "numerous, successive, slight modifications"; and the quantitative *edge of evolution* (e.g., chloroquine resistance taking trillions of cells for two mutations) shows Darwinian processes can break genes far more easily than build new ones.[^7]
- **The positive inference: information signals a mind — and that is science, not a gap.** Using Darwin's own historical method (inferring "causes now in operation"), the only known cause of digital/specified information is intelligence; ID infers *design* (not the designer), and excluding it rests on methodological naturalism, a philosophical rule rather than a scientific finding.[^8]
