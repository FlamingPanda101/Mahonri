# Thoughtful Faith — Is the Calvinist God a Moral Monster (Debate Prep with Blake Ostler)

[[Jacob Hansen]] interviews Latter-day Saint philosopher [[Blake Ostler]] to prepare for Hansen's upcoming debate with the Reformed apologist [[James White]] on the question *"Is the God of Calvinism a moral monster?"* Hansen anticipates that White will try to redirect the debate onto Mormonism and will press a [[Divine Command Theory Is Metaethics Not Ethics|divine command]] grounding of morality, foreknowledge/predestination, and the charge that the LDS God is a fallible, changeable "Superman." Ostler supplies a systematic rebuttal rooted in metaethics, the [[The Law of Love Is the Single Ground of Morality|law of love]], [[Creation Ex Nihilo Entails Occasionalism and Precludes Free Love|open theism]], and the [[The Euthyphro Dilemma Defeats Divine Command Theory|Euthyphro problem]]. The throughline: a God who unconditionally decrees who suffers eternally cannot be loving, and "love" is a brute relational reality that not even God can redefine. [^1]

- **Divine command theory fails as both metaethics and practical guide.** Ostler distinguishes metaethics (the *ground* of right/wrong) from ethics (a practical guide), then argues divine command theory is impaled on the [[The Euthyphro Dilemma Defeats Divine Command Theory|Euthyphro dilemma]] — either goodness is independent of God (so it is not divine command theory) or "good" just means "whatever God commands" (so God could command murdering children for fun, as arguably at [[Joshua 8.26|Ai/the ban]]). As a practical guide it is [[Divine Command Theory Is Vacuous as a Practical Guide|vacuous]]: the Bible never addresses abortion or IVF, commands conflict (divorce in [[Matthew 19.3-9|Matthew]] vs. [[Mark 12.30-31|Mark]]/Luke), and "the vast vast vast majority of philosophers" reject it. [^1][^2][^3] See [[Divine Command Theory Is Metaethics Not Ethics]], [[The Euthyphro Dilemma Defeats Divine Command Theory]], [[Divine Command Theory Is Vacuous as a Practical Guide]].
- **Morality is grounded in the law of love, which not even God can redefine.** Against the charge that the LDS God is "subject to law," Ostler answers that the one law is the [[John 13.34|law of love]] — a brute relational fact ("love is what it is"), not a command God invents. Love is defined as "anything that fosters, heals, creates" the flourishing of relationships; evil destroys them. Christ taught love by *modeling* it ("love one another as I have loved you"), not by sheer fiat, and [[Love Transcends Moral Obligation|love transcends moral obligation]] — loving merely from duty is not love at all. [^4][^5][^6] See [[The Law of Love Is the Single Ground of Morality]], [[Love Transcends Moral Obligation]], [[Love Cannot Be Coerced or Created]].
- **Creation ex nihilo entails occasionalism and makes free love impossible.** If God sustains every creature in being moment-to-moment ([[Creation Ex Nihilo Entails Occasionalism and Precludes Free Love|divine sustenance]]), then every state, including "love," is recreated by God each instant — so God would merely be "giving love to himself." Genuine love requires uncreated, [[Uncreated Intelligences Make Free Love Possible|self-existent agents (intelligences)]] who consent. Ostler dates creation ex nihilo to the late second century ([[Theophilus of Antioch]], [[Tatian]]) under middle-Platonic influence, not the Bible. [^7][^8] See [[Creation Ex Nihilo Entails Occasionalism and Precludes Free Love]], [[Uncreated Intelligences Make Free Love Possible]], [[The Calvinist God Cannot Love Because He Controls Everything]].
- **The Calvinist God is a moral monster: he decrees damnation and calls it love.** A being who creates persons foreknowing-and-decreeing they will suffer in hell "for no act of their own" is not loving; creating [[The Calvinist God Cannot Love Because He Controls Everything|Lucifer]] to "demonstrate his wrath" is narcissistic theater. [[God's Wrath Is the Natural Consequence of Rejecting Love|God's wrath]] is rightly understood as the natural self-degradation that follows rejecting love, not arbitrary retributive anger; salvation is [[Grace Is Unconditional Love Not an Earned Wage|grace = unconditional love]]. [^9][^10] See [[The Calvinist God Cannot Love Because He Controls Everything]], [[God's Wrath Is the Natural Consequence of Rejecting Love]], [[Grace Is Unconditional Love Not an Earned Wage]].
- **Open theism answers foreknowledge, predestination, and prophecy.** [[Foreknowledge and Free Will Are Logically Incompatible|Exhaustive foreknowledge of free acts is incoherent]]; [[Simple Foreknowledge Makes God a Prisoner Not Sovereign|simple foreknowledge]] strips God of sovereignty, while Calvinist [[Meticulous Providence Makes God the Author of Evil|meticulous providence]] makes God the direct author of every rape and murder. Prophecy is preserved because [[All Prophecies Are Conditional|prophecies are conditional]] ([[Jeremiah 18.7-10|Jeremiah 18]], [[Jonah 3.4|Jonah/Nineveh]]), or are events God *intends to bring about himself*, or are causally determined outcomes — and God, as a [[God Fulfills Prophecy as a Master Chess Player|master chess player]] who knows all possibilities and has "all power to the fulfilling of his word" ([[1 Nephi 9.17|1 Nephi]]), guarantees his plan. [[Failed Prophecies Show Scripture Is Not Infallible|Failed prophecies]] (the unbuilt Independence temple; the [[Matthew 24.34|"this generation"]] second coming) show Ostler does not hold scripture infallible. [^11][^12][^13][^14] See [[Foreknowledge and Free Will Are Logically Incompatible]], [[Simple Foreknowledge Makes God a Prisoner Not Sovereign]], [[Meticulous Providence Makes God the Author of Evil]], [[All Prophecies Are Conditional]], [[God Fulfills Prophecy as a Master Chess Player]], [[Failed Prophecies Show Scripture Is Not Infallible]].
