Ward Radio live stream (super-chat AMA) in which host [[Cardon Ellis]], with co-hosts [[Brad Whitbeck]] and [[Britney Ward Radio|Britney]], host **Heartland** advocate [[Rod Meldrum]] (founder of the FIRM Foundation / "Book of Mormon Evidence") and his media director [[Brill Hernandez]] to make the case for a **North American** Book of Mormon geography against the **Mesoamerican** model. A separately-recorded podcast with Mesoamerican proponent [[Jerry Grover]] is referenced throughout (Grover answers several super-chats live in the chat). The episode is framed as a friendly "Heartland hippies vs. Mesoamerican maniacs" rivalry, with repeated insistence that neither camp is apostate.

## Summary
- **DNA / Haplogroup X.** Meldrum's origin story: a family leaving the Church over the DVD *DNA vs. the Book of Mormon* drove him to study why mtDNA in Native Americans is "Asiatic," not Hebrew. He argues **Haplogroup X** (found ~70% in the Algonquian-speaking Miꞌkmaq, and in Israel/Italy/Tunisia) marks a Near-Eastern migration to **northeastern North America**, choosing it over the Australo-Melanesian anomaly that defenders of a Mesoamerican model could cite.[^1][^2] See [[Haplogroup X Marks a Hebrew Migration to North America - Meldrum]] (and the contrasting vault note [[Haplogroup X2A Is Not Valid Evidence for the Book of Mormon]]).
- **Brad's founder-effect / drift challenge.** Co-host [[Brad Whitbeck]] raises the mainstream rebuttal — founder effect, bottleneck, genetic drift would erase a Hebrew signal without needing Haplogroup X. Meldrum's reply: those mechanisms "ignore the Book of Mormon itself," which (he says) gives no indication Lehi's group assimilated into a larger host population; they kept the Law of Moses for 600 years and made Nephi king.[^3][^4] See [[Founder Effect and Genetic Drift Erase a Small Founding Signal]].
- **No "other people" in the text.** Meldrum's recurring argument against the Mesoamerican "Lehi merged into a large Maya population" model: the Book of Mormon never mentions a host population; Nephi's people did not "fight their way in." (The vault catalogs the opposite reading at [[The Empty Continent Assumption Underlies the DNA Objection]].)[^4]
- **Mesoamerica looks Asiatic.** Meldrum: Maya temples and glyphs resemble Hindu/Cambodian (Angkor Wat) architecture; "everybody in the world" reads Mesoamericans as Asiatic-descended, not Hebrew.[^5]
- **The Great Lakes are the "seas."** Citing that biblical Hebrew uses one word for both the freshwater Sea of Galilee and the salt Mediterranean, Meldrum maps Sea West = Lake Michigan, East = Ontario, South = Erie, North = Superior, and locates the "narrow neck" between the Great Black Swamp and the Grand Kankakee Marsh (when the Lakes stood ~50 ft higher), crossable "in a day or a day and a half."[^6][^7] See [[The Great Lakes Are the Book of Mormons Seas]].
- **New Madrid, not volcanoes, for 3rd Nephi.** Meldrum's signature argument: [[3 Nephi 9|3 Nephi 8–9]]'s 28 destruction-observations match the **1811–1812 New Madrid earthquakes** (USGS paleoseismologist Martitia Tuttle finding a similar event ~AD 1; Audubon's "vapor of darkness"; piezoelectric "earthquake lights") far better than a volcano.[^8][^9] See [[The New Madrid Earthquake Explains Third Nephi's Darkness Without Volcanoes]].
- **The "Chile 30° south" prophecy debunked.** Meldrum argues the famous "they landed at 30° south latitude (Chile)" statement is not Joseph Smith's: word-print analysis traces it to **William McLellin** via the Frederick G. Williams/Bernhisel materials, not the Prophet.[^10] See [[The Great Lakes Are the Book of Mormons Seas]] and cf. [[Oliver Cowdery's Letter VII States Cumorah Is in New York as Fact]].
- **Newark Earthworks = Plan of Salvation.** Meldrum's set-piece: the Newark, Ohio earthworks (Great Circle, square, octagon) encode the entire Latter-day Saint Plan of Salvation — premortal circle, veil-gap, square (telestial earth), octagon (Melchizedek/Christ seal → terrestrial), narrow neck to the celestial circle — a ~2,000-year-old "temple complex" with no parallel in any other religion.[^11][^12] See [[The Newark Earthworks Encode the Plan of Salvation]].
- **Seer stone / "Stone in the Hat."** Meldrum and Whitbeck argue Joseph translated with the Urim and Thummim / interpreters (D&C says he used them in 11 revelations; Oliver Cowdery and Emma/Joseph Smith III/Lucy Mack Smith all attest the interpreters), and that the "stone in the hat" account comes mainly from later/apostate sources (David Whitmer, late Emma). Whitbeck pushes back that a seer stone *was* involved, just not as the sole method.[^13][^14] See [[Joseph Smith Used Both the Interpreters and a Seer Stone]].
- **Plants/animals, Mound Builders, currency, Quetzalcoatl, Miꞌkmaq glyphs.** Briefer threads: North American grasslands/grapes/barley for the Law of Moses; the documentary *Lost Civilizations of North America* (17 mostly non-LDS scholars) on suppressed Mound Builder history; the onti/measure-of-barley currency; the "bearded white god" (Quetzalcoatl) as Christ visiting "other sheep" (not proof of any geography); and Miꞌkmaq hieroglyphs resembling the characters document (via Wayne May / Shad Verity / nerd rodic).[^15][^16] See [[The Quetzalcoatl Parallel Names Moses Not Christ]].
- **Both could be partly right (hinterland).** On whether both models are true, Meldrum summarizes Mark Wright's "hinterland hypothesis" (primary lands in Mesoamerica; later Lamanite migration into North America explains Joseph's New-World statements) — and rejects it as "a good-faith effort that also explains [Joseph's words] away."[^17]
