# Ward Radio - Debunking Matt Fradds 7 Reasons Joseph Smith Is a False Prophet

A [[Ward Radio]] segment, led by [[Luke Hansen]], that walks through and rebuts [[Matt Fradd]]'s Daily Wire article *"Seven Reasons Why Joseph Smith Was a False Prophet."* Hansen voices Fradd's quotations via an AI "Aussie" voice (a copyright/clarity device, since a paywalled Daily Wire video uses the same script) and answers each of the seven reasons in turn. The episode functions as a companion to the panel-format [[Ward Radio - Catholics Critique Mormonism on the Daily Wire]], which engages overlapping Daily Wire material with [[Joe Heschmeyer]].

## Summary
Hansen frames the article as testable on its own terms and rebuts all seven reasons:

1. **Failed prophecies / Book of Abraham mistranslation.** The mistranslation charge draws on Facsimile 3. Hansen replies that the glyphs are too poorly preserved to securely fix the figures, and that even granting the identifications, Joseph "got it right" by coincidence: Isis had become a *pharaoh-figure* by the era of the papyri, and **Osiris and Abraham are interchanged** in ancient sources — including Jesus' own Luke 16 parable (per [[John Gee]]). See [[Facsimile 3 Identifications Vindicate Joseph Smith by Coincidence]].
2. **Contradicting revelations (one God vs. gods).** The word *God* is **multivalent** in LDS theology; within Alma 11 itself Amulek calls the Father "one God," then calls the whole Godhead "one Eternal God." Abraham 4's plural "gods" are the subordinate divine council, not rivals to the Father. See [[God Is a Multivalent Term in Latter-day Saint Scripture]] and the existing [[Doctrinal Development Is Not Doctrinal Contradiction]].
3. **No Book of Mormon archaeology.** Fradd applies "evidence" to the Bible but "proof" to the Book of Mormon; consistently applied, Old World anchors (Nahom, the river Lehi, Bountiful) and resolved anachronisms like Nephite cement count as evidence. See [[Convergence Not Proof Is the Standard for Book of Mormon Historicity]], [[Nephite Cement Construction Was Confirmed at Teotihuacan]], [[Book of Mormon Anachronisms Are a Collective Pattern - Reel]].
4. **Fabrication and plagiarism.** The "faults are the mistakes of men" title page disclaims infallibility; and per [[Richard Bushman]], the Book of Mormon is a *narrative not a treatise* that *overturns* Jacksonian racism, unlike *View of the Hebrews*. Hansen presses a boomerang: if plagiarism is needed for some parts, why not the parts with no source? See [[The Plagiarism Charge Boomerangs on the Critic]] and the existing [[Shared Biblical Sources Are Not Plagiarism]].
5. **Dubious character / polygamy.** A Catholic tu quoque: [[Thomas Aquinas]] and [[Martin Luther]] (and [[Michael Heiser]]) allow polygamy in some circumstances; the "teenage brides" were 17+, and Catholic canon law itself permitted marriage near age 12–14 until 1917 (Edward Peters), while Pope Alexander VI fathered illegitimate children in office. See [[Catholic Marriage History Undercuts the Polygamy Attack]].
6. **First Vision contradictions.** The 1832 account *mentions* one figure but does not *exclude* others; brevity and differing purpose explain the variance, paralleling Paul's varying Damascus accounts (Acts 9 vs. 26). Already covered by [[First Vision Accounts Are No More Contradictory Than the Gospels]].
7. **Polytheism / Isaiah 44:6.** "Monotheism" is a post-Reformation category; per [[Michael Heiser]], Isaiah's "none beside me" is a **boast of incomparability** (cf. Babylon in Isaiah 47:8–10), not a denial that other gods exist. Already covered by [[Negation Texts Assert Incomparability Not Non-Existence]] and [[None Beside Me Is an ANE Boast of Supremacy]].

Hansen closes by echoing [[Trent Horn]]'s advice — given on Fradd's own show — that critics should run a rebuttal past an apologist of the target religion before publishing.
