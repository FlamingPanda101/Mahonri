# Let's Talk w/ Hayden Carroll — We Brought Cookies To Mark Driscoll's Anti-Mormon Mega Church

[[Hayden Carroll]] and a companion set up outside [[Mark Driscoll]]'s **Trinity Church** in the Phoenix East Valley with cookies, copies of the Book of Mormon, and a sign reading "Mormons are Christians," to test whether anyone will engage — a deliberate **kindness-in-engagement experiment** in the spirit of [[The Charitable-but-Critical Believer Posture]]. Church volunteers act as "bouncers," parking trucks to box the pair in and waving congregants away ("Don't talk to the demons").[^1] The footage is bookended by clips of Driscoll preaching that Mormonism is "a cult… founded on lies, demonic deception," with "a counterfeit fake Jesus."[^2] Around the stunt, Carroll improvises an extended steelman-and-rebuttal of Driscoll's core charge — that Latter-day Saints "aren't Christian" because they worship "the wrong Jesus" — repeatedly demanding the **standard** by which the line is drawn. The substantive threads connect to [[Subordinationism Was the Early Church View]], [[Creatio Ex Nihilo Is the Lynchpin Difference]], the [[No Final Interpreter - The Protestant Adjudication Problem|Protestant adjudication problem]], and [[Worshiping the Right Jesus - Created vs Uncreated|which Jesus is the right Jesus]].

## Summary

- **The kindness experiment and the "bouncers."** The whole conceit is to be visibly gracious — cookies, Book of Mormon copies, "all I want to do is just talk" — while the church physically screens its members away. Carroll reads the optics: when "your pastor is telling you that Mormons are demonic… why would they come talk to us?"[^3] He even floats sitting in on the sermon ("He's definitely going to be talking about us").[^4] See [[The Charitable-but-Critical Believer Posture]] and [[Anti-Mormonism Is Attacking the Person Not the Idea]].

- **"Any standard, applied consistently, excludes others."** Carroll's master argument: to say Mormons aren't Christian, the critic "has to come up with a standard by which a person is not a Christian," and every candidate standard either lets in Catholics/Orthodox or throws out the early church.[^5] This is a [[Bahnsen's Method - Arbitrariness, Presuppositions, Inconsistency|by-what-standard / consistency]] move aimed at an [[Arbitrariness and Inconsistency - The Two Intellectual Sins|arbitrary, inconsistently applied]] boundary — "you're not in the club 'cause we tell you you're not in the club."[^6]

- **The early-church subordinationism gambit.** Carroll repeatedly deploys [[Justin Martyr]] and [[Clement of Rome]] — pre-Nicene figures who (he argues) held the Father greater than the Son, hence denied the Son was the *same being* — to force a dilemma: either they were not Christians, or one can deny consubstantiality and remain Christian.[^7] He reads the New Testament itself as "in its explicit writings a subordinationist text" (John 14:28).[^8] See [[Subordinationism Was the Early Church View]] and [[Subordination Within the New Testament]].

- **"Substance" is incoherent / no true Scotsman.** Pressed by a congregant that Father, Son, and Spirit share one "substance," Carroll argues that *substance* by definition is material, so an "immaterial substance" is a contradiction "smuggled in" under the cover of mystery — and that appealing to mystery to license a contradiction is a [[Trinity Is Fundamentally Contradictory|no-true-Scotsman]] move ("contradictions aren't mysteries; they're incoherence").[^9] See [[Substance Is Equivocal in the Trinity]].

- **Creation does not entail *ex nihilo*.** On "Jesus created all things, therefore Satan can't be his brother," Carroll's rebuttal is that "created all things" does not state creation *out of nothing* — "if I make a painting, did I create the painting?" — so the brother-of-Satan objection rests on an unstated [[Creatio Ex Nihilo Is the Lynchpin Difference|ex nihilo]] premise the text never supplies.[^10] See [[Creation Ex Nihilo Is Not Biblical]] and [[2 Maccabees 7.28]].

- **"Translated correctly" vindicated.** When the congregant retreats to "the original language most likely means cousins," Carroll notes that any time a Trinitarian appeals to the original language to overturn a translation, "they just prove our article of faith" — the Bible is the word of God "as far as it is translated correctly."[^11]

- **No Protestant list of essentials.** Carroll's recurring jab, crediting [[Trent Horn]]'s exchange with [[Gavin Ortlund]]: there is no document "all Protestants have signed" naming the fundamentals; when asked for the list, Ortlund said there isn't one, which Carroll takes to dissolve the "we agree on the essentials" claim and to expose the deeper [[No Final Interpreter - The Protestant Adjudication Problem|lack of an authoritative interpreter]].[^12] See [[The Fundamentals and the Birth of Fundamentalism]] and [[Trinity Is an Interpretation Not Explicit in Scripture]].

- **"Not Christian" really means "not saved."** Carroll reframes the dispute soteriologically: calling someone not-a-Christian is "a nice way of saying they're going to hell," so the critic owes a *list of salvific doctrines* — and scripture names only "faith in Jesus Christ," with no [[Trinity Is a First-Rank Salvation Issue|theological-triage]] criteria in the text.[^13] He notes most Protestants won't go as far as Driscoll's "no Mormons in heaven at all."[^14] See [[John 8.24 - Die in Your Sins as Salvific Belief]].

- **The "different gospel" / Galatians 1:8 charge, turned around.** To Driscoll's use of [[Galatians 1.8]] ("though we or an angel preach another gospel"), Carroll replies that by that measure Catholics and Orthodox also "preach a different gospel" — "that's why they're different religions" — so the verse cannot single out Latter-day Saints as uniquely non-Christian.[^15] See [[A False Gospel Is One That Contradicts the Kingdom Message]].

- **The "same reference" theory of worship (NEW concept).** Against the "object of your worship" argument (a wrong Christ = a different deity, like dating Jane while thinking she's Sally), Carroll distinguishes **reference** from **belief**: an atheist talking about Jesus still refers to "the same being," and mistaking Sally's father for her stepdad is a wrong *description* of the same person, not a different person.[^16] Captured in [[Same Reference Different Beliefs - You Worship the Same Jesus]]; compare [[Common Reference - Worshipping Adam as God]].

- **East–West Trinitarian incoherence and the filioque.** Carroll walks through the [[Filioque Split Greek East from Latin West|filioque]] as really being a fight over whether the Son is *fundamental* or *contingent*: the Catholic/Western "eternally begotten" Son is illustrated by an always-on light bulb that still depends on a power source, which (he argues, citing [[Basil the Great]]'s *Against Eunomius*) concedes the Eastern point that a begotten Son "derives from" the Father.[^17] See [[Begotten Implies Source and Origin]] and [[Contingent and Necessary Beings]].

- **The Calvinism "trap."** Carroll respects [[Gavin Ortlund]] for being philosophically rigorous, arguing that perfect-being theology pushed to consistency *should* yield Calvinism and divine simplicity — at which point "none of this jibes with the Bible" (e.g. [[1 Timothy 2.4]], God "wants all to be saved").[^18] He name-checks [[James White]]'s "some Catholics will be saved but the Pope's not one of them" as the same teach-vs-believe distinction he applies to Latter-day Saint apostasy.[^19]

- **The "petty God" outburst.** In his sharpest stretch Carroll grants Christopher Hitchens' "God is not great," arguing a deity who eternally tortures non-worshippers for an "ego trip" would make the defiant child "the good guy" — a vivid form of [[Hell for Gods Glory Is Narcissism Not Love]] and [[Even If the Trinity Is True Your God Is Evil]]. He immediately predicts he'll "get clipped out of context on that one."[^20]

- **Progression, not binary hell.** Carroll closes the worship thread with the Latter-day Saint frame: a sincere "Driscoite" can be saved and "go really, really high," but stalls without the fullness — baptism by proper authority ([[John 3.5]]) — and no one is stopped "without willing rejection of the truth," with a guaranteed chance in the next life.[^21] See [[LDS Progression Model vs Binary Heaven and Hell]].

- **The "Joseph wrote himself into Genesis" objection.** Carroll pre-empts the JST-Genesis-50 talking point: the charge presupposes the Bible is complete *and* that Joseph is a false prophet; remove both and it reduces to an argument from incredulity ("look how convenient"), the same incredulity skeptics aim at any miracle. He stresses Latter-day Saints never argue *from* it ("that'd be circular reasoning"), and frames the JST as inspired commentary a prophet has authority to give.[^22]
