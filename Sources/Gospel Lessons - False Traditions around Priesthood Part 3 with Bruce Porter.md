# Gospel Lessons - False Traditions around Priesthood Part 3 with Bruce Porter

**Format:** Interview | **Host:** Kevin Prince | **Guest:** [[Bruce Porter]] | **Topic:** what the priesthood *is* (a "false traditions" episode)

[[Bruce Porter]] argues that Latter-day Saints "revere the priesthood more sometimes than we revere the gospel" and have absorbed a false tradition that priesthood is a **power** — especially "the healing power of the priesthood." Drawing on a chronological survey of "everything Joseph Smith said about priesthood," he contends priesthood is properly an **authority to administer the gospel and its ordinances within a stewardship and have them recorded on the records of the church** — not a monopoly on spiritual power, miracles, or healing. He lays out a **three-priesthood schema** (Aaronic, Melchizedek, patriarchal) and argues that women sealed in the temple already hold the patriarchal priesthood, so the Melchizedek priesthood exists specifically to supply a *missing or unworthy patriarch*.

- "We don't have a monopoly on righteousness ... spiritual experiences ... even miracles"; the only LDS monopoly is "priesthood authority and the ordinances connected to it."[^1] See [[The Priesthood Is Authority to Administer Not a Power to Heal]].
- Scripture never invokes the priesthood in a miracle or healing; gifts "to heal" and "to be healed" are given to member and non-member alike, and faith — not priesthood — moves mountains.[^2] See [[The Priesthood Is Authority to Administer Not a Power to Heal]].
- Joseph Smith taught **three** priesthoods: Aaronic, Melchizedek, and patriarchal; the patriarchal is received in the temple by a man and woman sealed together, who "hold the patriarchal priesthood equally" in the home.[^3] See [[Joseph Smith Taught Three Priesthoods Including the Patriarchal]].
- The home's highest priesthood-holder is the **matriarch**, not a 16-year-old Aaronic priest; the church itself must "acquiesce" to the authority of father and mother in the home.[^4] See [[The Highest Priesthood in the Home Is the Matriarch]].
- The Melchizedek (Christ's) authority exists "to step in" for "the fatherless, the widows and the orphans" — i.e., a **missing or unworthy patriarch** — which is why women, already holding the patriarchal priesthood, do not hold the Melchizedek.[^5] See [[The Melchizedek Priesthood Substitutes for a Missing or Unworthy Patriarch]].
