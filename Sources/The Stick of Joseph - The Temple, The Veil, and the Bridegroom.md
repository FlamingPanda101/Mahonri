# The Stick of Joseph — The Temple, The Veil, and the Bridegroom

A second appearance by [[Andrea Woodmansee]] (with hosts Hayden and Jackson Paul), an improvised "teach us about the veil" session that builds a temple theology of **the veil**: not a wall but a permeable boundary between the sacred and the [[Profane Literally Means Outside the Temple|profane]] meant to be *traversed* with preparation. Its central, recurring claim is a **reversal**: under the old covenant the high priest wore the colored veil-fabric and Christ *descended* through the veil of mortality; after the cross the movement reverses, and mortals *ascend* through a heavenly veil — which is why the modern temple veil and ceremony rightly differ from the ancient. The episode is a companion to the [[The Stick of Joseph - The Hidden Hebrew Wedding Ritual and the Temple|Hebrew-wedding]] discussion and leans, like it, on [[Margaret Barker]].

## Summary

- **The veil divides sacred from profane and is meant to be crossed.** A veil marks "something sacred and precious on the other side"; concealment signals *sanctity*, not shame — the opposite of a feminist/authenticity culture that equates virtue with exposure. The word [[Profane Literally Means Outside the Temple|*profane*]] means "outside the temple." Unlike a wall, a veil "is meant to be traversed" with preparation. See [[The Veil Marks Sanctity Not Shame and Is Meant to Be Traversed]].[^1][^2]
- **Eternity and concealment share a Hebrew root.** Two Hebrew words for "eternity" and "conceal" derive from the same root (*'lm*), tied to *almah* (virgin); so to be pure/veiled and to access eternity belong together. (Asserted; not developed lexically.)[^3]
- **God dwells behind a veil/tent.** [[Isaiah 40.17|Isaiah 40:22]] ("stretcheth out the heavens as a curtain, and spreadeth them out as a tent to dwell in") and Exodus 26:33 (the veil dividing the holy place from the most holy) picture God enthroned behind a traversable curtain — "hard to lock a tent."[^4]
- **The cherubim/flaming sword are on the veil.** [[Genesis 3.21|Adam and Eve's expulsion]] set cherubim and a flaming sword to guard the way back; 2 Chronicles 3:14 puts cherubim *on the temple veil* — so the guard is also a *protection*, shielding the unprepared from a glory that would destroy them. Coheres with [[The Garden of Eden Was a Prototype Sanctuary]] and [[Guardians of Sacred Space and the Password Motif]].[^5]
- **The high priest wore the veil.** Per Philo, the ancient veil bore a "panoramic history of the earth," woven of blue/purple/crimson representing the elements (air/water/fire/earth) — and the high priest's robe was the *same* fabric and colors. So the priest "in essence is wearing the veil," which Woodmansee ties to Joseph's "coat of many colors" (a priesthood/birthright garment) and to the modern temple garment. See [[The High Priest Wore the Veil - The Garment Is the Material World]] and the vault's [[The Garment Shares the Hebrew Word of the Priestly Vestments]].[^6][^7]
- **Christ reversed the veil's direction.** Apocryphal writers note Christ passed *from* the heavenly *to* the earthly realm — descending through a veil of the elements, taking on mortal flesh. After the cross this reverses: mortals now ascend, taking on the *heavenly* realm's characteristics, so a Celestial (white) veil replaces the material (colored) one. On Yom Kippur the high priest swapped his colored robe for **white linen** to enter the holy of holies — a foreshadowing of that reversal. See [[The Veil Is Christ Reversing the Day of Atonement]].[^8][^9]
- **Atonement is at-one-ment (re-entering the presence), distinct from redemption.** Woodmansee distinguishes the **redemption** (Christ paying the bride-price in Gethsemane/the cross) from the **atonement** proper as *at-one-ment* — being brought back into God's presence ([[John 17.20-23|John 17]] "that they may be one"). The torn veil does not abolish temples (the Protestant reading) but signals "time to get a new veil." See [[Atonement as At-One-Ment Is Re-Entering the Presence Distinct From Redemption]].[^10][^11]
- **Christ is the veil (Hebrews 10).** [[Hebrews 10.19-22|Hebrews 10:19-20]] — boldness to enter "through the veil, that is to say, his flesh" — means the only remaining separation from God is Christ, who, having the keys of death and hell ([[Revelation 1.8|Revelation 1:18]]), is "no separation at all" but the way. Now *everyone* redeemed, not one high priest on one day, may cross. Coheres with [[The Garment Is the Covering of Christ]].[^12][^13]
- **Identification by marks: the Jacob/Esau "veil ceremony."** To traverse the veil one must be *identified* with Christ (take his name and marks), as a bride takes the bridegroom's name. Woodmansee reads [[Genesis 27|Jacob obtaining the blessing]] (blind Isaac identifying him by the marks on his hands) as a veiled temple ceremony: a son who has *become* the birthright-holder is recognized at the veil by his hands — as Thomas recognized the risen Christ by the marks in his hands. [[Esau]], a non-wicked elder brother, forfeits the birthright so the next may have it (a Christ pattern). See [[The Blessing of Jacob Is a Veil Recognition Scene]].[^14][^15]
- **White robes for everyone (Revelation 7; 2 Enoch).** [[Revelation 7.9-14|Revelation 7:9]] shows a numberless multitude in white robes with palms — not one high priest but everyone who has crossed the heavenly veil. 2 Enoch has Enoch stripped of earthly garments and made to shine "like the sun's ray," and Moses veils his shining face ([[Exodus 34.29-35|Exodus 34]]) — radiance that, like the sun, would harm the unprepared, so the veil is "spiritual sunglasses." Coheres with [[Moses Veil and Divine Radiance]].[^16][^17]
- **Zechariah 3 is an explicit temple investiture.** [[Zechariah 3|Zechariah 3:1-10]] stages the high priest Joshua (**Yeshua**) before the angel with Satan resisting, stripped of filthy garments, reclothed and mitred, warned (Hebrew "repeat/warn," not "protest") to walk worthily, promised to "judge my house," with the **BRANCH** ([[Isaiah 11.1]]) and the **fig tree** (seed-bearing fruit) at the end. Woodmansee calls it among "the most explicit temple things" in the Old Testament — impossible for a farm boy to have assembled from scratch. See [[Joshua the High Priest in Zechariah 3 Is an Explicit Temple Investiture]].[^18][^19]
- **Worthiness as worth, not a checklist.** "Worthy" is reframed as treating the ordinance as *of worth* — caring enough to prepare — rather than clearing an impossibly high bar; profaning the sacred (taking the name "in vain" = "for vanity / to be seen") is the opposite. The commandments themselves function as a veil that, once kept, lets one part the veil safely. Coheres with [[The Endowment Is Learning Heaven Not Earning Heaven]] and [[The I-Thou Temple Is Covenant Relationship Not Transaction]].[^20][^21]
- **Why the bride is veiled, and recent temple changes.** A bride is veiled until presented to the bridegroom, then unveiled in his presence (the father lifts the veil — [[Genesis 24|Rebekah]] veils only on meeting Isaac, and "lighted off" / *naphal* the camel in eager haste). Woodmansee offers as "a tantalizing possibility and nothing more" that recent removals of the temple veil for women (alongside dropping the angel-Moroni-less newer temples) may signal nearness to the Bridegroom's return — the bride unveiled "right before the wedding." See [[The Bride Is Unveiled Only in the Bridegrooms Presence]]; cf. [[Restoration Is Adaptation and Accommodation Not a One-to-One Copy]].[^22][^23]
- **The Book of Mormon is woven with it.** [[Alma 5.18|Alma 5:50]] and Alma 40:23-25 ("the righteous shine forth"), [[3 Nephi 9|3 Nephi]]'s shining king, and D&C 101:22-23 (stand in holy places; the veil "taken off" so "all flesh shall see me together") replay the shining/veil/restoration motif — offered as evidence Joseph Smith could not have fabricated the pattern.[^24][^25]
