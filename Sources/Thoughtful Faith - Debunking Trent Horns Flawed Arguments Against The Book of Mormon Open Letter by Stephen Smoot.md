# Thoughtful Faith — Debunking Trent Horn's Flawed Arguments Against The Book of Mormon (Open Letter by Stephen Smoot)

**Channel:** Thoughtful Faith ([[Jacob Hansen]])
**Author of the letter:** [[Stephen Smoot]] (full text on thoughtfulfaith.org)
**Foil:** [[Trent Horn]] (his Aug. 22 debate with Hansen on whether the Book of Mormon is inspired)

## Summary
- Hansen first frames Horn's debate strategy as a **Gish Gallop** (overwhelming with many individually weak attacks) and faults two specific characterizations — calling Joseph Smith's death a "shootout at a local jail" and implying the Church teaches the Catholic Church is "the church of the devil" (the First Presidency reprimanded Bruce R. McConkie for that). See [[Steel Man Versus Gish Gallop in Apologetic Dialogue]].
- Smoot's letter offers point-by-point correctives: the **David Whitmer "separate myself from the Latter-day Saints" quote is misread** (Whitmer was *already* excommunicated; God told him to flee Far West to escape the Danites, not to leave the Church), and **Lucy Mack Smith's "amusing recitals" are Joseph relaying Moroni's instruction**, not campfire fiction. See [[David Whitmer Was Told to Flee Far West Not to Leave the Church]] and the updated [[The Evening Recitals Were Joseph Relaying Moroni's Information]].
- Two more correctives: the witnesses' signatures appearing in **Oliver Cowdery's hand is irrelevant** because that document is the *printer's manuscript* (a scribal copy), and the witnesses repeatedly reaffirmed their testimony; and **the multivolume History of the Church is not analogous to the Book of Mormon** because it is a composite, team-edited work, not a rapid dictation. See [[The Witnesses Statement in Cowderys Hand Is the Printers Manuscript]] and [[History of the Church Is a Composite Work Not a Rapid Dictation]].
- Smoot also rebuts: the **gold-plates-too-small-to-hold-the-text** objection (Hudall's 1923 experiment fit ~6 chapters in Hebrew on a 7×8 sheet; Grover's metallurgy work) — see the updated [[The Book of Mormon Fits on the Plates - Sjodahl Hebrew Test]]; and the **Book-of-Mormon-Hebrew-names** objection (Horn must show *specific* borrowing, and cannot explain Matthew Bowen's documented paranomasia) — see [[Book of Mormon Names Show Authorial Wordplay]].
