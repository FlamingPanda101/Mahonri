# Thoughtful Faith - Why The Old Testament Is The LEAST RELIABLE Of All Scripture. And Why That's Good News

[[Jacob Hansen]] interviews [[Jonah Barnes]] (author of *All Things Apocryphal*) on the chapter Barnes calls **"the Great Forsaken"** — a pre-Christian apostasy, distinct from and arguably worse than the post-apostolic Great Apostasy, in which the scribes who compiled the Old Testament after the Babylonian exile redacted out "plain and precious things." The pair argue the Old Testament is the *least* reliable scriptural corpus, that this is detectable in mainstream Deuteronomistic-source scholarship, and that the proper response is not to find an infallible book but to rely on living revelation, prophets, and the Book of Mormon — which rescued the Saints "not from one apostasy but from two."

- The "Great Forsaken" named from [[Jubilees|Jubilees]] 1:14 ("they have forsaken my ordinances ... my sabbaths and my holy place") and traced across the apocrypha — see [[The Great Forsaken - A Pre-Christian Apostasy of the Scribes]].[^1]
- The post-exilic **Deuteronomistic** compilation (Deuteronomy–Kings) gave the scribes power to insert a "listen to the scribes, obey the law" theme and excise the suffering Messiah, the divine mother (Asherah), and the gospel-for-the-Gentiles — see the existing [[Deuteronomists Rewrote the Histories of Israel]] and [[The Deuteronomistic History Books Are Absent From the Brass Plates]].[^2]
- **Jesus warred against the scribes**: every time he quotes Deuteronomy he turns it *against* the Deuteronomists (irony), so citing his use of Deuteronomy does not prove he held it inerrant — see [[Jesus Quotes Deuteronomy Against the Deuteronomists]].[^3]
- In **3 Nephi 20–21** Christ recites [[Micah 5.2|Micah]] and inserts two verses absent from the received Micah (on the Messiah's sonship and the destiny of the Gentiles) — restoring what the scribes excised — see [[Christ Restores Suppressed Verses of Micah in Third Nephi]].[^4]
- The OT is **more corrupt than the NT**: the presenters grant the New Testament's textual reliability but insist the harder problem is the Old Testament's composition — see [[The Old Testament Is Less Reliable Than the New Testament]].[^5]
- The enemy is **"scribalism"** — the search for an infallible book to worship as an idol of ink and paper; the right answer is "reliable but not infallible" sources plus living guides, which is "way harder" — see [[Scribalism Is the Idolatry of an Infallible Book]] and [[No Infallible Source Only Reliable Guides]].[^6]
- Payoff: the Book of Mormon "rescued us not from one apostasy but from two" — see [[The Book of Mormon Rescued Us From Two Apostasies]].[^7]
