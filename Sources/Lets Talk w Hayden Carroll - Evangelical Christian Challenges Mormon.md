# Let's Talk w/ Hayden Carroll — Evangelical Christian CHALLENGES Mormon

A roughly 90-minute unscripted livestream in which evangelical apologist **[[Kelly (Brain Perspective)|Kelly Powers]]** ("Berean Perspective," self-described born-again Protestant since 1977) calls into **[[Hayden Carroll]]**'s stream and challenges him across the classic Mormon–Evangelical battlefield: the "demonic" charge, the Trinity and consubstantiality, [[1 Nephi 14.10|1 Nephi 14]]'s "two churches," Joseph Smith's [[Joseph Smith History 1.68-69|First Vision]] and [[King Follett Discourse|King Follett Discourse]], the canon, and the nature of God. The conversation repeatedly collapses into a single methodological dispute that the channel's own intro flags: Kelly's **double standard for epistemology pre-Bible and post-Bible**, and his inability (on Hayden's telling) to justify the canon by which he judges the Latter-day Saints.[^1] This source overlaps heavily with the sibling debate [[Lets Talk w Hayden Carroll - Is the Bible the Final Authority Debate]]; only genuinely new specifics are extracted below.

## Summary
- **The "demonic" framing and its real-world stakes.** Kelly affirms that the Mormon church is "demonic" — led astray by a [[2 Corinthians 11.14|Satan-as-angel-of-light]] spirit preaching "[[Galatians 1.8|another gospel]]" — while insisting individual Mormons are not "directly evil."[^2] Hayden's counter-question turns the label into an ethical liability: he cites the recent Michigan shooting of Latter-day Saints by a gunman who called them "the Antichrist," asking what follows if a group is genuinely branded antichrist.[^3] This sharpens [[When Catholics Argue Like Jews - The Demonic Charge Is Unfalsifiable]] with a concrete consequence-of-labeling angle.
- **1 Nephi 14 is allegory, not denominational census.** Hayden reads the "[[1 Nephi 14.10|great and abominable church]]" / "church of the Lamb" as a symbolic good-versus-evil dichotomy (rooted in [[1 Nephi 8|Lehi's tree-of-life vision]]), noting that LDS leaders who once read it as denomination-specific were corrected in the 1980s, and that [[Nephi]] in 600 BC belonged to no congregation at all.[^4] See [[Two Churches Are Symbolic Good and Evil Not Denominations]].
- **The Acts 15 lexical skirmish.** Kelly argues the apostles "disputed" one another in [[Acts 15|Acts 15]] (proving apostles disagree on doctrine, so a prophet's published book need not bind); Hayden restricts *dispute/much disputing* to "inquiring, questioning" the Judaizers, denying any apostle-versus-apostle doctrinal clash.[^5] Compare the vault's [[Acts 15 Shows James Deciding Not Peter]].
- **Prophetic opinion vs. binding doctrine.** Hayden's containment strategy for the [[King Follett Discourse|King Follett Discourse]] (e.g., "God was once a man," plurality of gods) is that a prophet's *un-canonized* sermon — here reconstructed from four scribes after Joseph's death — can carry personal opinion the Church need not ratify; the Church "has the authority to take what it takes as authoritative."[^6] Kelly calls this "Bible buffet" / picking and choosing. See [[Prophets Are Fallible Not Divine]] and [[King Follett Need Not Be Canonized]].
- **The epistemology double standard (the spine of the exchange).** Repeatedly, Kelly demands "how do you know what's true or false in Matthew, Mark, Luke, John?" and Hayden answers "the same way anyone knew truth *before* the Bible was penned" — [[Abraham]] and [[Moses]] had no book, only divine revelation — charging that Kelly applies sola scriptura as the test post-canon while exempting the patriarchs pre-canon.[^7] **New concept:** [[The Pre-Bible Post-Bible Epistemology Double Standard]].
- **The Pauline-parity argument.** Hayden's strongest tu quoque: a first-century believer who never met Jesus could only accept Paul's *new* revelation — celestial/terrestrial bodies in [[1 Corinthians 15.24-28|1 Corinthians 15]] — on an apostle's authority plus faith/Spirit, exactly the footing on which Latter-day Saints accept [[D&C 76|D&C 76]]'s three degrees of glory; the Evangelical "gives first-century Christians grace" he denies modern apostles.[^8] **New concept:** [[How Would a First-Century Believer Verify Paul - The Pauline-Parity Argument]].
- **Hebrews 1:3 and consubstantiality.** Kelly leans on *charaktēr tēs hypostaseōs* and BDAG/Swanson glosses ("substance, nature, essence") to put same-substance Trinitarianism in the text; Hayden concedes *same nature* but denies *same being/substance* is biblical, attributing it to fourth-century [[Hellenization Was Not Corruption of an Original Pure Church|Hellenization]] / neoplatonism.[^9] See [[Hebrews 1-3 Express Image Is the Imprint Not the Stamp]] and [[The Trinity and Consubstantiality Are Not in the Bible]].
- **John 5:19 — "the Son can do nothing of himself."** Both treat it as a live verse: some Latter-day Saints read "the Son does only what he *saw* the Father do" as implying the Father once did what the Son now does (a hook for [[God Was Once a Man]]); Kelly reads it contextually as Sabbath-healing unity/[[Social and Subordinate Trinitarianism Is the Early Church View|subordination-in-unity]], warning it generates an infinite regress of gods.[^10] See [[John 5.19-23]].
- **Sola scriptura and the Fathers.** Kelly's parade of patristic quotations ([[Irenaeus of Lyons|Irenaeus]], [[Tertullian]], [[Dionysius the Areopagite|Dionysius]], [[Gregory of Nyssa]], [[Cyril of Jerusalem]], [[Basil the Great]]) is met by Hayden's stricter definition: a high view of scripture is not *sola scriptura* (the 66 books as the sole infallible rule), and the canon was anyway fixed by tradition/recognition in the 300s.[^11] See [[The Early Church Did Not Operate Under Sola Scriptura]] and [[Knowing Scripture Is the Word of God Requires an Authority Outside Scripture]].
- **The Holy-Spirit impasse.** Kelly reports praying about the Book of Mormon and receiving a "no"; Hayden grants the symmetry — two sincere parties, two opposite witnesses — and concludes neither can *prove* the other wrong, only bear testimony, the "most fundamental form of knowledge."[^12] See [[Burning in the Bosom vs Self-Evidencing Light]].
