# Thoughtful Faith — Was Joseph Smith a Pedophile?

- **Defense via Historical Context:** The video argues that calling [[Joseph Smith]] a "pedophile" for his sealing to [[Helen Mar Kimball]] (age 14) is historically anachronistic — applying modern moral categories to the 19th century without accounting for drastically different marriage norms and life-stage expectations ("presentism").
- **Edgar Allan Poe Parallel:** [[Edgar Allan Poe]] married his 13-year-old cousin in 1836 at age 27 without public scandal, illustrating that older-man/teenage-girl marriage was normal enough in that era (~1 in 10–15 rural marriages) that even America's most celebrated poet was not condemned for it.
- **Four Historical Facts:** The video presents four contextual factors: (1) "teenager" as a social category didn't exist until the mid-20th century; (2) women's life path was marriage after puberty, not school/career; (3) no birth control or social safety net made sexual activity dangerous — parents steered daughters toward established providers; (4) marriage was primarily about economic security, with love secondary.
