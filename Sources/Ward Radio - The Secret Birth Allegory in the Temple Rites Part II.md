A **Ward Radio** women's-panel episode — the second installment of a "secret birth story in the temple" series — in which presenter **Tia** leads panelists **[[Britney Ward Radio|Britney]]**, **Megan**, and **Joy** through an extended catalog of ways the ordinances of the temple recapitulate **human gestation and birth**. It opens by reading viewer comments on Part I (mothers as worlds, placenta as Tree of Life, the umbilical cord cut by the flaming sword) and then adds new "birth-words" hidden in scripture and rite. The throughline: labor and delivery are the body's enactment of the plan of salvation — descent into mortality and the return through the veil — and Paul and Christ deliberately used **travail** as the metaphor for the Second Coming.

## Summary

- **Recap of Part I's birth/temple symbols.** The panel reviews the prior episode: the earth and water as mother, the **placenta as the Tree of Life**, the umbilical cord as the connection to the presence of God severed at the navel, being "cut off" by the **flaming sword / cherubim** mapped onto cutting the cord, and the **bag of waters as the veil** that is "rent" when a woman's water breaks [^1].
- **Water, Spirit, blood.** A cited comment (Rachel Harrison) ties [[Moses 6.60|Moses 6:60]] ("by the water ye keep the commandment, by the Spirit ye are justified, by the blood ye are sanctified") to the three elements of birth — the amniotic water, the spirit received in the womb, and the mother's blood shed in delivery [^2].
- **The veiled birth (caul).** A baby born with the amniotic sac unbroken is medically called a **"veiled birth"** / born "in the caul" — independent confirmation, the host argues, that the protective membrane is naturally called a *veil*; just as Christ's death **rent the veil of the temple**, a mother's veil is rent to bring a child into the world [^3].
- **The sacrament is veiled.** A comment notes the sacrament is covered with a white cloth; the panel links this to Christ's body on the altar/tomb and to a woman buried veiled in her temple robes [^4].
- **Birth pangs of the Messiah.** Tia introduces the Hebrew **"chevlei mashiach"** — "the birth pangs of the Messiah" — and reads the tribulations of the last days as a woman's **labor pains**; she anchors it in [[1 Thessalonians 5.3|1 Thessalonians 5:3]] ("sudden destruction... as travail upon a woman with child... they shall not escape") and the "no man knows the hour" parallel to an unpredictable due date [^5].
- **Christ's surrender as a mother's surrender.** The unmedicated-labor insight — fighting/tensing stalls labor; relaxing lets the baby descend — is read as the Savior "laying down his life," submitting to deliver us, paralleling Gethsemane's "let this cup pass" [^6].
- **Naming and clothing the newborn.** Two "obvious" birth-symbols: the baby is **given a name** (not self-chosen, often angel-announced in the Bible) and **clothed for the first time**, paralleling the washing/anointing and clothing and the temple new name [^7].
- **The vernix as anointing oil.** Newborns are coated in **vernix**, an oily antimicrobial skin-protectant now left on rather than washed off — read as the mother's own production of an **anointing oil** covering the whole body, paralleling the whole-body anointing of the initiatory [^8].
- **Crowning, quickening, opened eyes.** The panel mines more "birth-words": **crowning** (the head's appearance; also kingly coronation), **quickening** (archaically the first felt kick; scripturally "quicken our understanding"; also the word for translation/resurrection), and **eyes opened** for the first time outside the womb, tied to [[D&C 110|D&C 110:1]] ("the veil was taken from our minds and the eyes of our understanding were opened") [^9].
- **Recognizing the Father's voice.** Because a baby hears voices in the womb and recognizes them at birth, the panel reasons we can recognize the Father's voice "because I was once in his presence" — a comfort against the fear of the mortal/last-days transition [^10].
- **Crying out as prayer; closed/opened eyes.** A baby's cry at birth is read as a good sign and a type of "crying unto the Lord"; eyes closed then opened maps a temple motif of revelation [^11].
- **Latter-day Saint trivia: Joseph Smith's veiled birth.** The episode closes with the folk claim, drawn from a viewer comment, that **Joseph Smith was born in the caul** (a "veiled birth"), read as symbolic of his being "anointed a seer from the very beginning" — presented explicitly as folklore [^12].
