# The Stick of Joseph - How to Access the Power of Heaven Through Real Worship

Host **Dave Butler** interviews recurring guest **[[Todd McLauchlin]]** about worship — a theme McLauchlin says he has been learning "in the last eight or nine months" and now considers possibly "the most profound thing I may have ever learned in my life." The conversation distinguishes *worship* from *prayer* and from *priesthood*, and presents worship as an active practice and state of being rather than mere praise.

## Summary
- McLauchlin frames worship as a *practice that puts you into a state of being* — actively humbling oneself before God so that "the full spectrum of things that he has to give you" can come quickly; it "always culminates in prayer" but is communion, not negotiation. See [[Worship Is a State of Being That Draws Heaven Down]].
- A recurring diagnostic: most people pray to "the God you've imagined up into yourself" — a projection of one's own perfected ego (which is "why everyone believes God agrees with their politics") — so faith stays weak; worship corrects this by letting God reveal himself. It is also "a form of spiritual warfare" that displaces fear, "the first fruit of the fall." See [[Everyone Worships Something So Choose What to Be Conformed To]].
- The two apply the idea to LDS sacrament meetings: a meeting led "in the moment given by the Holy Ghost" (singing, gratitude, intercession) would be more authentic and powerful than the polished, scheduled program — echoing the channel's recurring [[Authenticity Is What the Millennial Generation Wants From the Church|authenticity]] theme. Grounded in [[Alma 32]] (the seed/tree of life) and [[Moroni 7]].
