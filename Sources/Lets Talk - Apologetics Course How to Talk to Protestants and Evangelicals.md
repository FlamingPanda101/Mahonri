# Let's Talk — Apologetics Course: How to Have Effective Conversations with Protestants & Evangelicals

A recorded ~90-minute **in-person apologetics course** (the channel's first), taught by [[Hayden Carroll]] to about 50 attendees in the Phoenix area. The stated goal: teach Latter-day Saints how to have "more effective conversations with our Protestant and evangelical friends." Carroll frames the course as the first in a projected series whose first three classes teach how to **disarm** an opponent's worldview and whose fourth teaches how to **offer an alternative**.[^1] The class opens with a clip of Elder Holland endorsing evidence-based apologetics ("the force of fact as well as the force of feeling"),[^2] uses [[Trent Horn]]'s Catholicism syllogism as a worked example of what *not* to do, adopts [[Greg Bahnsen]]'s "disarm the gunman" method, and builds the whole evening around attacking the **unjustified presuppositions** baked into critiques of the Church.

## Summary
- **The thesis of the course is a method, not a doctrine:** nearly every attack on the Church reduces to "I'm right, so you're wrong" — a hidden, unjustified presupposition smuggled into a syllogism. The apologist's job is to refuse the false premise and force the critic to *justify their standard before using it*.[^3] See [[I'm Right So You're Wrong - The Hidden Presupposition Pattern]], [[Internal Critique - Two Questions for Every Critic]].
- **Validity vs. soundness is the analytic core.** Carroll dissects Trent Horn's "If Catholicism is true, the Book of Mormon is not inspired" syllogism, granting it is *valid* but denying it is *sound* because the premise "Catholicism is true" cannot be proven — a faith claim cannot do the work of a logical proof.[^4] See [[Validity vs Soundness - Why the Catholicism Syllogism Fails]], [[The Reversibility Test for Bad Arguments]].
- **The governing scripture is Proverbs 26:4–5.** Carroll reads the paradoxical "answer not a fool / answer a fool" couplet as a two-step apologetic: v.4 = *do not grant* false presuppositions; v.5 = *do* expose their incoherence and show where they lead.[^5] See [[Proverbs 26 Two-Verse Apologetic Method]].
- **Against Protestants, the target is the doctrine of scripture.** Carroll names three unjustified presuppositions about the 66-book canon — that it is **infallible, sufficient, and the final authority** — and argues none was ever publicly designated by God; the canon also can't self-reference because compilation postdates the books, and 2 Timothy 3:16's *theopneustos* means "life-giving," not "inerrant."[^6] See [[The Three Unjustified Protestant Canon Presuppositions]], [[Canon Cannot Self-Reference - Compilation Postdates the Books]], [[Theopneustos Means Life-Giving Not Infallible]], and the existing [[Authorization - Divine Designation Required for an Infallible Canon]], [[The Canon Question Is Logically Prior to Canon Authority]].
- **On the nature of God**, Carroll argues the Nicene Trinity was "born of necessity" — an attempt to hold hard monotheism together with Christ's deity using imported Neoplatonic "perfect being" theology; he cites early **subordinationism** and the Dead Sea Scrolls reading of Deuteronomy 32:8–9 (Yahweh as a son of the Most High) as evidence the Hebrew Bible taught monolatry, not hard monotheism.[^7] See [[Subordinationism Was the Early Church View]], [[Deuteronomy 32 Dead Sea Scrolls Monolatry]], and the existing [[Trinity Built to Satisfy Greco-Roman Philosophy]], [[Isaiah 43-10 - No God Formed Before Me Rebutted]].
- **The "evil God" pivot:** even granting the Trinity / perfect-being theology, Carroll presses that a God who creates people *ex nihilo* foreknowing they will be damned is not good — Calvinists "bite the bullet" and accept it, while LDS co-eternality of intelligences dissolves the problem.[^8] See [[Even If the Trinity Is True Your God Is Evil]], and the existing [[Problem of Hell and Creation]], [[Free Will - Atheist Determinism vs LDS Co-Eternality]], [[Calvinism Consistency with Atheism on Free Will]].
- **Pastoral guardrails:** distinguish genuine seekers (answer them — "dodge bullets") from hostile attackers (disarm them); never add your own unjustified presuppositions; and remember the Spirit, not the argument, ultimately converts.[^9] See [[Genuine Seekers vs Bullet-Shooters]], [[Spirit Over Argument - The Limits of Apologetics]], [[God Can Lead People Through Other Religions]].
- **Bonus content move:** Carroll defends Book of Mormon "anachronisms" (a verse mentioning "a Bible") via **dynamic translation** — Joseph rendered ideas in words intelligible to modern readers, exactly as Bible translators do.[^10] See [[Dynamic Translation Answers Book of Mormon Anachronism]].
