# Did Joseph Smith Teach Men Can Become God?

An overview of the King Follett Discourse — Joseph Smith's most famous (and controversial)
sermon, delivered at general conference in April 1844, months before his death. Benjamin Park
traces the sermon's background, its radical theological content, and its complicated
publication and reception history within the LDS tradition.

- **Core claim:** Joseph Smith taught that God was once a man and that humans can become gods
  (exaltation/divinization) — a radical collapse of the creator/creature distinction.
- **Publication drama:** The sermon was nearly suppressed from the official *History of the
  Church* in 1912, removed by [[Joseph F. Smith]] and [[Charles Penrose]], before [[BH Roberts]]
  fought to restore it.
- **Enduring tension:** Modern LDS leaders and scholars oscillate between embracing the
  discourse's radical distinctiveness and downplaying it to emphasize common ground with
  mainstream Christianity.
