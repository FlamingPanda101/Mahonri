# The Stick of Joseph - Margaret Barker on the School of Isaiah and the Temple

Hosts Hayden and David Butler interview Methodist biblical scholar [[Margaret Barker]] (filmed near Burton-on-Trent, England) on the **Deuteronomistic reform** and the **school of Isaiah**, framed as the historical backdrop to [[Lehi]] leaving Jerusalem c. 600 BC. Barker reconstructs a First-Temple religion — vision of God, an incarnate Lord, the mother in heaven, angel-priests, the day of atonement — that the reformers purged, and which she argues the old believers (Isaiah's circle) preserved.

## Summary
- **The Deuteronomists rewrote the histories.** A scribal-lawyerly party (gaining influence from c. 750 BC, peaking under the boy-king Josiah, 623 BC) purged the temple of its symbols and "told the history as they wanted it told," so Samuel and Kings are a slanted record; the old believers called them "the apostate generation."[^1][^2] See [[Deuteronomists Rewrote the Histories of Israel]].
- **Two streams: vision vs. no-vision.** Deuteronomy insists "you saw no form, there was only a voice" (Deut 4:12), while Isaiah and Ezekiel see the Lord in human form; Barker argues Christianity is rooted in the first-temple visionary stream (the "pure in heart… shall see God"), recovering the face of the Lord (Rev 22:4) after the "age of wrath."[^3] See [[Two Streams of Israelite Religion - Vision Versus the Voice Alone]].
- **The mother of the Lord in the Great Isaiah Scroll.** Barker's signature textual claim: in the photographs of 1QIsaᵃ, Isaiah 7:11 reads "ask a sign of the **mother** of the Lord your God" (Hebrew *aleph*) where the Masoretic Text reads "of the Lord your God" (*ayin*) — a one-letter edit she says removed a Heavenly Mother.[^4] See [[The Great Isaiah Scroll Reads Mother of the Lord at Isaiah 7-11]].
- **Jesus came to restore the true (first) temple.** The temple is a microcosm of creation; the veil is matter; Jesus cleansing the temple "with a whip of cords" mimics the high priest's Day-of-Atonement sprinkling, declaring himself high priest of a polluted second temple, which he came to restore.[^5][^6] See [[Jesus Cleansed the Temple as the High Priest of the Day of Atonement]].
- **Priesthood of all believers.** In the old temple the mark of the Lord (a diagonal cross) marked every believer as priest; the torn veil opened the holy of holies to all, so a separate priestly caste has "no justification in Christian origins."[^7]
