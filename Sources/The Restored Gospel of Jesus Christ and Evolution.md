# The Restored Gospel of Jesus Christ and Evolution

A 2025 BYU College of Life Sciences volume arguing that **accepting the scientific theory of evolution is among the legitimate, faithful choices** a Latter-day Saint can make. Built for BYU students who feel forced to choose between biology and religion, it gathers essays under three epistemologies — what we know from **revelation**, what we know from **science**, and how to **reconcile** them — bookended by the Church's own official statements.

## Summary
- Frames the project as "learning by study and also by faith" (D&C 88:118): God created on purpose, but **"nothing has been revealed concerning evolution"** — leaving faithful latitude on *mechanism* [^1].
- **Religious epistemology:** scripture and ancient cosmology (Greenwood), the Genesis creation account in its ancient Near Eastern context (Shannon), continuing revelation (Frederick), and the 20th-century history of the "no death before the Fall" reading (Spackman) [^2].
- **Scientific epistemology:** why Latter-day Saints can trust science (Peck), accepting evolution "with joy" (Ogden), what evolution *is and is not* — descent with modification, not "humans were apes" (Kummer & Jensen), and the scientific evidence for human evolution (Bybee) [^3].
- **Reconciliation + official position:** teaching evolution faithfully at BYU (Porter, Whiting, Perego, Lee, Ferguson et al.), and Spackman's history of the 1909/1925 First Presidency statements, with all official statements appended [^4].
- **Apologetic use:** the vault's primary resource for the **science-and-religion / evolution** challenge — an in-faith demonstration that evolution and the Restoration are compatible.
