# Mormons & Scripture: A Live Discussion With Dan McClellan

A monthly live YouTube conversation between historian [[Benjamin Park]] and biblical scholar
[[Dan McClellan]] on how Latter-day Saints relate to scripture — pegged to the Church of Jesus
Christ of Latter-day Saints turning to the Old Testament in its 2026 Come Follow Me curriculum.
The two old friends (BYU undergrads; Park went to Cambridge, McClellan to Oxford) range across
the Joseph Smith Translation, the new LDS Bible-translation policy, the openness of the canon,
biblical (il)literacy in the church, the resurrection and the minimal-facts argument, and how a
scholar holds the line between rigor and a public audience.

## Summary

- **Joseph Smith fits the restorationist stream of the Second Great Awakening**, but went further
  — arrogating prophetic authority not merely to *interpret* but to *revise* and *compose*
  scripture. [[Dan McClellan]] reads the JST as **revision in the pseudepigraphic tradition**
  (Genesis Apocryphon, Targums): repackaging authoritative texts for a new community, "going on
  for over 2,000 years." See [[JST Is Revision in the Pseudepigraphic Tradition]] and
  [[A Prophet Is Not an Academic Translator]].
- **Smith's additions reassert biblical authority rather than challenge it.** Responding to
  [[Thomas Paine]]'s *Age of Reason* (which denied Moses wrote Genesis), the JST adds "I, Moses,
  wrote this record" — solving a hermeneutical crisis by anchoring the text in prophetic authority.
- **The new LDS Bible-translation policy** recommends different translations for different age
  groups (NRSVue praised, NIV grudgingly conceded). McClellan, a former church translation
  supervisor, says much of it was on his old "action item list." See
  [[LDS Multi-Translation Policy Shift]] and the irony that the **RSV** (1950) is what drove
  [[J. Reuben Clark]] to make the KJV the *exclusive* LDS Bible — now reversed.
- **Institutional change to scripture is deliberately "baby steps"** ("What About Bob"); a
  non-KJV Book of Mormon is "a bridge too far" until the church first issues its own revised KJV.
  See [[Baby Steps - LDS Scripture Reform Is Deliberately Incremental]].
- **The concept of a (closed) canon is entirely post-biblical** — already documented at
  [[Scripture Was a Broader Category Than the Bible - The Anachronism of a Closed Canon]];
  Jude quotes [[Book of Enoch - Quoted as Scripture in Jude|1 Enoch as prophecy]], so the
  canon-closers "overruled the Bible regarding what's inspired."
- **Reinterpreting hard texts to fit modern sensibilities**: Genesis 3:16 "rule" softened to
  "preside" ([[Genesis 3.16 Means Rule Not Preside]]); the "rod" of Proverbs reread as a
  shepherd's crook; Isaiah 28:10 gibberish turned into "line upon line." McClellan's thesis:
  [[We Negotiate With the Text - It Does Not Speak for Itself]].
- **Method for harmful passages**: begin from the historical-critical reading (yes, this text
  advocates genocide / corporal punishment) and *then* read productively — never pre-soften, or
  believers feel "lied to" when they discover the real meaning. See
  [[Start From the Critical Reading Then Negotiate]].
- **Non-English Latter-day Saints already live with translations that differ from the KJV** —
  e.g. the Portuguese Daniel 3:25 "a son of gods" McClellan supervised. See
  [[Daniel 3.25 Is A Son of Gods Not The Son of God]].
- **LDS biblical literacy declined as the church "domesticated"** into "the stepbrother of
  American evangelicalism" in the mid-20th century. See
  [[LDS Biblical Literacy Declined as the Church Domesticated]].
- **The stick of Judah and the stick of Joseph** (Ezekiel 37) are the two kingdoms reunited, not
  the Bible and the Book of Mormon. See [[The Stick of Judah and Joseph Are Two Kingdoms Not Two Books]].
- **The minimal-facts argument fails**: the disciples' post-mortem appearance experiences are
  better explained by ordinary grief phenomenology than by a resurrection. See
  [[Minimal Facts Fails - Grief Experiences Explain the Resurrection Appearances]].
- **Scholarship and faith occupy separate compartments**: McClellan "never criticizes faith,"
  only bad arguments and bad evidence; naturalist explanations for miracles (the wind splitting
  the Red Sea) are a self-defeating attempt to "beat scholarship at its own game." See
  [[Scholarship and Faith Are Separate Compartments]].
- **Holy envy**: McClellan developed envy for the Jewish framing of scripture as "an invitation
  to wrestle" while at the Oxford Centre for Hebrew and Jewish Studies. Cf.
  [[Holy Envy - What Latter-day Saints Can Learn from Islam]].
