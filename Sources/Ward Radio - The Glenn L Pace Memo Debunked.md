# Ward Radio — The Glenn L. Pace Memo "Debunked"

Part 3 of the Ward Radio *Glenn L. Pace Memo* series. Where Parts 1–2 ([[Glenn L Pace Memo - The Satanic Panic and False Memory]], [[Glenn L Pace Memo - The Mormon Satanic Panic]]) laid out the national and Utah panic, this installment performs a line-by-line reading of the 1990 memo by Presiding Bishopric counselor [[Glenn L. Pace]] and argues it is neither an investigation, original, specific, nor actionable.

## Summary

- **The memo was never an investigation.** Pace was handed a list of self-selected, self-reported abuse claims drawn from the vast [[Lighthouse Ministries|LDS Social Services]] therapy network (63 offices by 1990) and asked to follow up; the words "discover," "investigate," and "investigation" appear nowhere in the 12-page memo. All 60 interviewees were active therapy patients, nearly all diagnosed with dissociative/multiple-personality disorders. [^1][^2] See [[Pace Interviewed Self-Selected Therapy Patients Not a Cross-Section]] and [[The Pace Memo Was Not an Investigation but a Persuasive Essay]].
- **It is a persuasive essay, not a report.** Of 63 paragraphs, only 3 (paragraphs 2, 3, 19) discuss specifics; the rest is an apologia for accepting recovered-memory therapy. Pace expressly says his aim was "to obtain an intellectual and spiritual conviction as to the seriousness of this problem," and he refused to collect perpetrators' names — leaving 800+ people implicitly accused but no prosecution possible. [^3][^4] See [[The Pace Memo Names No Perpetrators Making Prosecution Impossible]].
- **Nothing in it is original.** Pace's motifs (baptism by blood, doctrinal inversion, mock burials, layered memories, and especially the "coven of 13") are lifted straight from *Michelle Remembers*, *The Satanic Underground*, and *The Satan Seller*; an AI stylometric comparison ranks *Michelle Remembers* and McMartin coverage as his top influences. His own numbers betray him: 60 victims × a coven of 13 ≠ 800 "on the Wasatch Front" when only 37 were even from Utah. [^5][^6] See [[The Pace Memo Recycled Existing Satanic Panic Tropes]] and [[Pace's Coven Math Refutes His Own 800-Perpetrator Estimate]].
- **Even the anti-Mormons doubted it.** Lighthouse Ministries published the leaked memo to embarrass the Church yet conceded it would be "very difficult to cover up that many murders." [^7] See [[Even Lighthouse Ministries Doubted the Pace Memo's Credibility]].
- **It changed nothing.** The Utah Governor's subcommittee on ritual abuse formed in February 1990 — *before* the memo — so the claim that the memo "actioned" church or state response is false; the church was already navigating SRA reports like every other large institution. [^8] See [[The Pace Memo Did Not Cause Any Church or State Action]].
