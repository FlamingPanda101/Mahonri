# Ward Radio — The Top Ten Lost LDS Writings of the Apocrypha

A Ward Radio livestream in which host **[[Cardon Ellis]]**, with **[[Jonah Barnes]]** ("the associate professor of all things apocryphal") and **[[Britney Ward Radio|Britney]]**, counts down the apocryphal and pseudepigraphal works Barnes argues contain the most Latter-day Saint doctrine. Barnes' organizing concept is the **prolepsis** (his coinage for the opposite of an anachronism): a text that "predicts" something it should not have been able to — e.g. the temple endowment turning up in ancient documents discovered long after Joseph Smith revealed it.

## Summary
- **Method — the prolepsis.** Barnes defines a *prolepsis* as an ancient text containing a detail later vindicated (the reverse of an anachronism), and treats Joseph Smith's revelation of the endowment as the paradigm case: it "sounded crazy" in the 19th century, then 20th-century discoveries showed the pattern in ancient sources.[^1]
- **#1 The Secret Gospel of Mark.** A letter attributed to [[Clement of Alexandria]] (discovered by **Morton Smith** in 1958 at Mar Saba) describing a fuller, "more spiritual" Gospel of Mark reserved for "those being perfected," withholding "hierophantic" (temple) teachings — read as evidence of a suppressed early-Christian temple gospel. See [[Clement of Alexandria and Secret Mark Attest a Suppressed Temple Gospel]].[^2]
- **The Life of Adam and Eve (multiple recensions).** Adam and Eve demand **tokens** to identify Satan when he impersonates an angel and he cannot produce them; and they receive **garments** sewn by the Lord (kafar = "to cover") enabling return to God's presence. See [[The Life of Adam and Eve Fills the Gaps in Joseph Smith's Moses 5]], [[Atonement as Covering - Joseph Could Not Have Known]], [[The Garment of Skin and the Garment of Light]].[^3]
- **The Books of Enoch and the Book of the Giants.** Barnes relays [[Hugh Nibley]]'s "slam dunk": the **Mahijah / Mahujah** figure who comes to inquire of Enoch in Joseph Smith's [[Moses 6|Book of Moses]] appears with the same name in the *Book of the Giants*, a Dead Sea Scrolls text published a century after Joseph Smith's death. See [[The Book of the Giants Names Mahijah Matching Joseph Smith's Enoch]].[^4]
- **Creation ex materia (seeded earth).** One Enoch tradition has God *organize* the earth (not create *ex nihilo*) and send angels to fetch **seeds** from another world to plant before it rains. See [[Bara Means Organize and Ex Nihilo Is a Second-Century Invention]].[^5]
- **The Armenian Adam and Eve — "God was once a man."** In the Armenian apocrypha (translated by **Michael Stone**, ~150 years after Joseph Smith), the serpent tells Eve "God himself was a man like you when he ate of the fruit... he became God of all because of that." See [[The Armenian Adam and Eve Says God Was Once a Man]].[^6]
- **The Divine Investiture of St Michael.** A document (from the Vatican collections) teaching pre-mortality (God forewarns Adam's fall; Christ volunteers) and that **Lucifer was the "first-formed" (protoplast)** who, on falling, was demoted from "first-formed" to "the accuser." See [[The Divine Investiture of St Michael Calls Lucifer the First-Formed]].[^7]
- **The Books of Baruch.** Baruch, scribe of Jeremiah, has his own pseudepigraphal books; Barnes likes them because [[Lehi]] was Jeremiah's Jerusalem contemporary, supporting the Book of Mormon's premise of multiple persecuted prophets at that moment. See [[Prophets Were Killed for Preaching the Messiah Before 600 BC]].[^8]
- **Closing — how to read the Apocrypha.** Barnes distinguishes capital-A **Apocrypha** (the deuterocanon the Protestants removed — the D&C 91 reference) from lowercase-a **apocrypha** / pseudepigrapha (the newly-found texts Joseph Smith never saw), and counsels reading "with the Spirit," hunting for proleptic "nuggets." Also names: the *Gospel of Philip* (temple stages), *Shepherd of Hermas*, *Ezra's Eagle* (4 Ezra), *Bel and the Dragon*, the *Gospel of Nicodemus* / Descensus (cf. [[D&C 138]]).[^9]
