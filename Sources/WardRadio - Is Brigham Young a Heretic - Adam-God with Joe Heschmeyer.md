# Ward Radio — Is Brigham Young a Heretic? (Adam-God with Joe Heschmeyer)

A follow-up to [[Joe Heschmeyer]]'s *Pints with Aquinas* critique of Mormonism: Ward Radio's **[[Luke Hansen]]** invites Heschmeyer to debate, **with** a Latter-day Saint rather than merely **about** them. The central question: is **[[Brigham Young]]** a heretic, and does the **Adam-God doctrine** prove Mormonism unworkable? The two work through Heschmeyer's four-point case that Adam-God was once official, binding teaching, then spar over reference, idolatry, and what standard a prophet must meet.

## Summary
- Both men **agree Brigham Young taught Adam-God** ("Our Father and our God... is Adam," April 9 1852); the dispute is whether it was ever *binding* and whether teaching it makes Young a false prophet [^1]. Heschmeyer presses that Young called it a "doctrine" tied to "salvation or damnation" and embedded it in the 1877 **Lecture at the Veil** [^2].
- Hansen's core defense is a **reliability-not-infallibility** standard: the LDS title page of the Book of Mormon disclaims infallibility, so a prophet's errors don't disqualify him the way Heschmeyer's Deuteronomy-18 test demands [^3]. Heschmeyer counters that even *reliability* should rule out a prophet who doesn't know "whether he's talking to Heavenly Father or Adam or Michael" [^4].
- The two reframe the fight as **orthopraxy vs. orthodoxy**: Hansen argues the LDS dividing line for salvation is the **testimony of Jesus** (Joseph Smith: all else is "appendages"; D&C 76), not creedal propositions, which is precisely what the LDS find "abominable" about the creeds [^5].
- On **reference**, Hansen argues Young still pointed at the true God of scripture and merely mis-identified him as the first man — a "common reference" like Lois Lane dating Clark Kent / Superman, or Vatican II saying Muslims worship the same God; Heschmeyer answers with the **golden calf** and **bronze serpent** as cases where true attributes ascribed to a false object is still idolatry [^6].
- They map the **henotheism** debate ([[Trent Horn]] / [[Pope Benedict XVI]] on Old Testament "divine pedagogy"; [[Michael Heiser]] on Isaiah 43) and end on warm **common ground**: LDS adopting Lent-adjacent Easter devotion and shared choral culture [^7].
