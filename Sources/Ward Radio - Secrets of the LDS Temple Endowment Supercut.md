# Ward Radio — Secrets of the LDS Temple Endowment (Supercut)

A 38-minute supercut of a [[Ward Radio]] live stream hosted by [[Cardon Ellis]] with [[Jonah Barnes]] ("associate professor of all things apocryphal") and Ed Thomas — all temple-endowed — arguing that the LDS temple **endowment** restores a secret early-Christian temple mystery attested across apocryphal and archaeological sources, none of it available to Joseph Smith. The endowment-as-restored-mystery thesis and several primary texts (Acts of John, Pistis Sophia, prayer circle) **already have notes**; this episode reinforces them and adds the **Greek endo→endowment** reading of Acts 1, the **Clement / Secret Mark** chain, and the **Coptic burial garments** as new specifics.

## Summary

- **Acts 1's "forty days" and "promise of the Father" point to a lost endowment of secret teaching.** The author refers to a "former treatise" of all Jesus "began" to teach up to the Ascension, including 40 days of resurrection instruction "pertaining to the kingdom of God" that the Gospels do not contain; the hosts tie "the promise" the apostles await to the Greek *endo-* ("to clothe upon"), the root of *endowment*. See [[The Lost Forty-Day Teaching Points to a Suppressed Temple Endowment]]. [^1]
- **Clement of Alexandria's letter describes a secret Gospel of Mark with hierophantic (temple) teachings.** [[Clement of Alexandria]]'s *Letter to Theodore* (Secret Mark) says Mark wrote a second, more "spiritual" gospel "for those being perfected," still withholding the *hierophantic* (temple-priest) teachings; the hosts read this as evidence the 40-day mysteries were written, kept secret, and lost — and that *mystērion* in Greek means a temple ordinance. See [[Clement of Alexandria and Secret Mark Attest a Suppressed Temple Gospel]]. [^2]
- **Gnostic and apocryphal works preserve garments, veils, prayer circles, names/seals/ciphers, and ordinance-order.** Pistis Sophia and the Books of Jeu (Bruce Codex), the Gospel of Philip ("baptism, chrism, eucharist, redemption, bridal chamber"), and the Acts of John (round dance / commanded silence) describe robe-changes, passing through concentric veils with secret names, and an ordinance sequence matching the temple. See [[Gnostic Apocrypha Preserve Temple Ordinance Patterns]], and the existing [[The Acts of John Records Jesus Instituting a Mystery Ritual]] and [[The Christian Prayer Circle Descends From an Ancient Temple Dance]]. [^3]
- **Coptic Christians were buried in temple-like garments — rosettes and a marked slit — excavated in 1981.** The Fag el-Gamous cemetery in Egypt (burials ~200 BC-AD 800) yielded shirts with rosette knots over each breast and over the right leg and a slit across the abdomen, on people buried facing east for the resurrection. The hosts argue these material garments link the first-century temple to the modern endowment, and Joseph Smith could not have known of them. See [[The Fag el-Gamous Coptic Burial Garments Mark an Ancient Temple Connection]]. [^4]
- **Method: "secret" is not "sinister," and a coming endowment change is fine.** The hosts insist sacred secrecy is biblical (Jesus taught the Twelve things he withheld from the crowd), that the endowment has been adjusted ~19 times, and Barnes floats splitting it into modular sections — the salvific covenants preserved, the instructional (book-of-Moses-like) portion movable. See the existing [[Sacred Secrecy Is Pearls Before Swine Not Shame]], [[An Ordinance Must Change to Stay the Same]], and [[Ten Abandoned Temple Practices Show Continuity Through Change]]. [^5]
