# The Stick of Joseph - Why LDS Garments Are the Most Christian Symbol Ever (Jonah Barnes)

A Stick of Joseph conversation (bonus content) between **[[Mike Day]]** and **[[Jonah Barnes]]** built around a chapter of Barnes's book on the temple garment. The thesis: far from being an embarrassment, the LDS garment is "the most Christian symbol ever" because it represents the body, blood, covering, and atonement of Christ.

> Note: this is a *distinct* video (id `pzcRy8b0mMI`) from the earlier Stick of Joseph garment episode [[The Stick of Joseph - Why ALL Christians Should Wear a Sacred Garment]] (id `6IotRPZLAAA`), which covers the same theme with overlapping material.

## Summary
- **The high priest wears the veil = the body of the Lord.** On the Day of Atonement the high priest emerges with the divine Name on his forehead, wearing robes that match the temple veil; "wearing the veil of the temple means putting on the body of the Lord." When Jesus dies, the veil tears — not (as some Protestants say) to signal the temple is obsolete, but as a sign of Jesus's identity as the great High Priest whose body *is* the veil. See [[The Garment Is the Veil of the Temple and the Body of the Lord]].
- **The garment as the covering/kafar of Christ.** Drawing on Genesis 3 and the Life of Adam and Eve (Egyptian recension 52:2 — the garment of skins is a "token of death"), Barnes argues the garment is a *covering* (Hebrew *kaphar*, atonement) symbolizing Christ's blood that blots out shame. See [[The Garment Is the Covering of Christ]].
- **"Magic underwear" is a bigoted double standard.** Mocking the garment while affirming "magic blood" cleansing sins, or Catholic transubstantiation ("magic bread"), is ethnocentric; every religious tradition has sacred investment (the Hasidic *tzitzit*, the Sikh *kirpan*). The chapter argues Latter-day Saints should talk openly about garment symbolism and be ready to give a defense (1 Peter 3:15).
