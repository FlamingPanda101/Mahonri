# Let's Talk w/ Hayden Carroll — Evangelical Pastor and Mormon Clash on Scripture and God

A friendly two-topic conversation — the **nature of Scripture** and the **nature of God** — between [[Brother Oz (Austin)]], an evangelical Protestant apologist, and [[Hayden Carroll]], a Latter-day Saint and the channel host. Despite the title, both men frame it as mutual understanding rather than a formal debate. The Scripture half turns on [[The Three Unjustified Protestant Canon Presuppositions]] (infallibility, sufficiency, finality) and [[No Final Interpreter - The Protestant Adjudication Problem]]; the God half turns on the [[The Godhead Model vs the Trinity Model|Trinity vs LDS Godhead]] and the [[A Constrained God Solves the Problem of Evil Better Than Creedal Christianity|problem of evil]].

## Summary
- **Authority and "doctrine over time."** Carroll opens by explaining the LDS structure of authority: Latter-day Saints reject prophetic *infallibility* and locate binding doctrine in what the apostles/prophets (the Quorum of the Twelve with the First Presidency) teach **collectively over time**, citing Acts 15 as the pattern. Brother Oz presses that this looks "bendy," using the priesthood-and-race history of [[Brigham Young]] as a test case; Carroll answers it was a *policy/practice*, not a canonized revelation, since apostles disagreed with it.[^1]
- **The three Protestant presuppositions.** Carroll's central challenge: show where God ever said the Protestant Bible is the **correct canon**, is **infallible**, is **sufficient**, and is the **final authority** — properties he says are presupposed (à la Van Til / [[Greg Bahnsen]] presuppositionalism) but never divinely designated. See [[The Three Unjustified Protestant Canon Presuppositions]] and [[The Canon Question Is Logically Prior to Canon Authority]].[^2]
- **Oz's authority chain — Christ → apostles → tradition.** Brother Oz grounds Scripture not in the book but in **Christ**, whose authority extends to the apostles' writings (*graphē*, 1 Cor 14, 2 Pet 3:15–16) and to a fallible-but-real **apostolic deposit** of handed-down tradition (2 Thess 2:15). Pressed by the [[Trent Horn]]–[[Gavin Ortlund]] question — why keep some apostolic traditions and reject others (e.g., the Eucharistic Real Presence) — Oz answers from **degrees of certainty**: transmission is a fallible "telephone game," so he is more confident of some traditions than others, and he rejects apostolic succession.[^3]
- **"A text cannot be infallible because language is fallible."** Carroll's distinctive epistemic move: words lack fixed meanings, so no translated/transmitted text can be infallible. Oz counters with the **Logos**: because Christ is the underlying logic of reality, meaning and identity are objective, not socially constructed — see [[A Text Cannot Be Infallible Because Language Is Fallible]] and [[Relationality Requires Three - The Trinitarian Grounding of Meaning]]. They converge that transmission is fallible yet the text is "true in what it accomplishes."[^4]
- **The interpreter regress.** Carroll deploys [[No Final Interpreter - The Protestant Adjudication Problem|the no-final-interpreter charge]]; Oz replies that demanding an **infallible interpreter of infallible books** spawns an infinite regress, so no system has one and certainty rests only in the Logos — see [[The Infinite Regress of Infallible Interpreters]].[^5]
- **Meaning-saturation vs the problem of evil.** Oz's positive case for the Trinity: relationality ("God is love") cannot exist below the number three — lover, love, beloved; knower, knowledge, known — so a triune God is the only ground of meaning, identity, and substance. He argues a Christian cosmos is **meaning-saturated** while the LDS cosmos leaves "a whole bunch of stuff that has literally no meaning." See [[Relationality Requires Three - The Trinitarian Grounding of Meaning]] and [[Suffering as the Frame That Displays the Painting]].[^6]
- **The hell exchange and the LDS rejoinder.** Oz presses the [[Hell - For Gods Glory Defense Rebutted|"glory of God"]] / Calvinist version of hell; Carroll replies from LDS premises — [[God Is Constrained by Eternal Law Not Unconstrained|God constrained by eternal law]], [[LDS Premortal Consent Removes Gods Culpability|premortal consent]], eternal matter (no [[Creation Ex Nihilo Is Not Biblical|creation ex nihilo]]), and compensation through the Atonement. Oz answers with [[Suffering as the Frame That Displays the Painting|suffering as the exhibition of God's attributes]] and rejects "joy" as the purpose of existence (Christ the "man of sorrows"); Carroll cites the LDS teaching that the design of existence is joy (2 Nephi 2:25; Moses 1:39).[^7]
- **Carroll corrects the "Mormons deny hell" myth.** In the final minutes Oz admits he had always understood Latter-day Saints to deny hell; Carroll corrects him from [[D&C 76]] and D&C 18 — those who knowingly sin and refuse Christ "must suffer even as I" — and attributes the misimpression to under-trained 18-year-old missionaries. See [[Latter-day Saints Do Affirm Hell - DC 76 and DC 18]].[^8]
