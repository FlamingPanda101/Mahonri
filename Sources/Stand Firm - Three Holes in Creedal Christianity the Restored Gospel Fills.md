# Stand Firm — Three Holes in Creedal Christianity the Restored Gospel Fills

A Latter-day Saint apologist reacts to a widely circulated conversation between agnostic philosopher [[Alex OConnor|Alex O'Connor]] and geneticist-turned-Christian [[Francis Collins]]. The narrator "roots for Alex," arguing that while O'Connor fails to disprove God, he successfully exposes three structural weaknesses in **creedal (mainstream) Christianity** that the **restored gospel** of [[The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints]] resolves [^1].

## Summary
- **Hole 1 — Biblical inerrancy.** O'Connor argues that the same moral intuition apologists use to argue *to* God is contradicted by Old Testament commands to slaughter Canaanite children and New Testament texts subordinating women; the narrator answers that Latter-day Saints reject inerrancy, hold the Bible "highly reliable" but not error-free, and may treat morally monstrous commands as **uninspired outliers** measured against the collective prophetic witness [^2][^3]. See [[Rejecting Biblical Inerrancy Solves the Moral Contradiction Problem]] and [[Outliers Against the Collective Prophetic Witness May Be Uninspired]].
- **Hole 2 — A radically unconstrained God.** In the fine-tuning exchange, Collins concedes God is in some sense "constrained" by the constants He chose, then retreats to "He chooses to be"; O'Connor presses whether God could have made an equally interesting universe with different constants. The narrator argues the LDS doctrine that **God is constrained by eternal law** dissolves the problem and is required by the logic of the Atonement [^4][^5]. See [[God Is Constrained by Eternal Law Not Unconstrained]] and [[An Unconstrained God Makes the Atonement Pointless]].
- **Hole 3 — How one comes to know God.** Trent Horn is quoted showing that "miracle" tests (regrown limbs, rearranged stars) collapse into the **God-of-the-gaps fallacy**; O'Connor independently affirms that **personal revelation/experience** is "the most legitimate way imaginable" to come to believe in God [^6][^7]. The narrator argues this is exactly the restored gospel's epistemology, grounded in [[Doctrine and Covenants 6.22-23|D&C 6:22-23]] [^8]. See [[Personal Revelation Is the Most Legitimate Path to God]] and [[Miracle-Tests for God Commit the God-of-the-Gaps Fallacy]].
- The video closes with Latter-day Saint apostolic teaching on the *patterns* of revelation — sudden light, gradual dawn, and "foggy day" guidance — to illustrate the experiential epistemology it commends [^9].
- A recurring polemical move: each objection that wounds creedal Christianity is presented as **already answered** by distinctive LDS doctrines (open canon, eternal law, embodied/constrained God, individual revelation), reframing atheist critique as inadvertent support for the restoration [^1][^4][^8].
