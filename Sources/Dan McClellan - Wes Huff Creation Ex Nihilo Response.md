# Dan McClellan - Wes Huff Creation Ex Nihilo Response

**Full title:** "Wes Huff is Wrong About Creation Ex Nihilo"
**Length:** ~24:32

## Summary

- **Wes Huff claimed** on X/Twitter that creation ex nihilo is not a later Christian invention, citing the Ebla archives (specifically "EA 250"), 2 Maccabees 7:28, Philo of Alexandria, the Shepherd of Hermas, Tatian, and Theophilus of Antioch as ancient witnesses to the doctrine.
- **Dan McClellan rebuts** each point systematically: (1) Huff misidentified the Ebla text — EA250 is an Amarna tablet, not Ebla; the correct tablet TM.75.G1682 uses a discredited apologetic translation by Pettinato; (2) the scholarly consensus for 50+ years is that 2 Maccabees 7:28 does NOT teach creation ex nihilo; (3) Philo held to creation from pre-existent matter, not ex nihilo; (4) creation ex nihilo was first articulated by Tatian (~160 CE) and Theophilus of Antioch (~169 CE) and Irenaeus, who then retroactively reinterpreted earlier texts.
- **Key methodological critique:** Huff is uncritically relying on outdated, apologetically-motivated translations and the "normative interpretive lens" imposed by the late-2nd-century originators of the doctrine, rather than engaging contemporary scholarship.
