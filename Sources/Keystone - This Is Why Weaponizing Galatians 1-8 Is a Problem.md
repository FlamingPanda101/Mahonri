# Keystone — "This Is Why Weaponizing Galatians 1:8 Is a Problem"

A [[Keystone]] rebuttal of one of the most common Bible verses used against Latter-day Saints, [[Galatians 1.8|Galatians 1:8]] ("though we, or an angel from heaven, preach any other gospel... let him be accursed"). The host argues the verse is not the "slam dunk" critics assume: it begs the question, the real issue is how Paul defined the gospel, and Latter-day Saints affirm exactly that gospel.

## Summary
- **The verse begs the question.** Throwing "Galatians 1:8" at Latter-day Saints is a "Swiss Army verse" used to dismiss a view without engaging it. The charge "you have a false gospel" rests on the unproven premise that the accuser "has the fully correct gospel" — circular reasoning (begging the question). It is "not persuasive, it's just judgmental." See [[Galatians 1-8 Begs the Question - The Swiss Army Verse]].
- **What is the gospel? Ask Paul.** "Gospel" is used broadly and narrowly, so the right question is how *Paul* defined it. In [[1 Corinthians 15.1-4|1 Corinthians 15:1-4]] Paul specifies: "Christ died for our sins according to the scriptures... was buried... rose again the third day" — a deliberately broad net. See [[Galatians 1-8 Latter-day Saints Preach Pauls Gospel]].
- **Latter-day Saints believe Paul's gospel.** [[Joseph Smith]] taught in 1838 that "the fundamental principles of our religion are the testimony of the apostles and prophets concerning Jesus Christ, that he died, was buried, and rose again the third day... and all other things which pertain to our religion are only appendages to it." The Book of Mormon has Christ define "the gospel" the same way (lifted up on the cross to draw all men). The Greek for "gospel" means "good news," and the good news is that Christ is Savior.
- **The "angel" point fails.** Paul is not condemning angelic visitations — Paul himself was visited by angels, and Revelation has an angel with "the everlasting gospel" ([[Revelation 14.6|Revelation 14:6]]). Paul's point: no matter the source, a message that Christ is *not* our Savior is wrong.
- **The "earned salvation" point fails too.** Galatians 1 responds to the idea that salvation comes by obeying the law of Moses. Latter-day Saints view temple ordinances as an expression of faith in Christ, not a replacement for it; the Book of Mormon repeatedly teaches salvation by Christ, and [[Dieter F. Uchtdorf|Elder Uchtdorf]] taught that "salvation cannot be bought with a currency of obedience. It is purchased by the blood of the Son of God."
