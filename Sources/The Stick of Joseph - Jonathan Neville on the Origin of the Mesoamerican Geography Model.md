# The Stick of Joseph - Jonathan Neville on the Origin of the Mesoamerican Geography Model

Hosts interview **[[Jonathan Neville]]** as a deliberate counterweight to a prior Kirk Magleby (Mesoamerican) episode, on where the Mesoamerican Book of Mormon geography model came from and on the translation method. The hosts repeatedly stress they "represent the other side" and want viewers to make an informed decision.

## Summary
- **The Mesoamerican model is an artifact, not a revelation.** Neville traces it to the 1841 Stephens-and-Catherwood travel books and to early member [[Benjamin Winchester]], arguing the 1842 *Times and Seasons* Mesoamerican articles were written by Winchester (not Joseph Smith, who was only the nominal editor). See [[The Times and Seasons Mesoamerican Articles Were Benjamin Winchester Not Joseph Smith]].
- **Letter VII anchors a New York Cumorah.** Neville's load-bearing datum: [[Oliver Cowdery]]'s 1835 "Letter VII" states *as fact* that the final battles occurred between two hills near Palmyra, New York — published repeatedly in church periodicals with Joseph's approval. To dismiss it, he argues, is to call Cowdery a liar and unravel his other testimony (John the Baptist, Peter-James-John). See [[Oliver Cowdery's Letter VII States Cumorah Is in New York as Fact]] and [[The Two-Cumorahs Theory]].
- **Method: "clarity, charity, understanding" and the "faith model."** Neville frames every geography model as an *interpretation* of the text (the text "is not self-executing"), insists prophets/apostles are needed to interpret, and offers a "faith model" (facts → assumptions → inferences → theory) for diagnosing why people reach different conclusions from the same facts. See [[Clarity Charity and Understanding and the Faith Model]]. On translation, he argues Joseph and Cowdery said the [[The Urim and Thummim Is Not a Real Parallel|Urim and Thummim]] was used and the seer stone was only a demonstration prop — citing [[Richard Bushman]]'s *Joseph Smith's Gold Plates*.
