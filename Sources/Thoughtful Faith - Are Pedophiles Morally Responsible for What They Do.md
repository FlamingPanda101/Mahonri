# Are Pedophiles Morally Responsible for What They Do?
A short clip in which [[Jacob Hansen]] hosts a critic of the Church (addressed as "Bill") who weaponizes the hardest possible case — child predation — to deconstruct moral responsibility itself. The critic argues from a geneticist's claim that child predators are genetically/epigenetically *predisposed* (and so "aren't choosing to do that") to the broader conclusion that if agency is "extremely limited or non-existent," then no coherent discussion of moral responsibility is possible — and that Latter-day Saint ethics, with its shift from "free agency" to "moral agency," is therefore confused. The exchange surfaces the deep dependence of moral responsibility on genuine [[Intelligence Is Uncreated and Eternally Has Agency|agency]] and the standoff between hard determinism and objective wrongdoing.

## Summary
- **Responsibility presupposes choice.** The opening premise both parties grant: "people are only morally responsible for the choices that they make... if you don't make a choice... you're not responsible for it because you didn't make the choice." [^1]
- **The critic's deconstruction: remove agency entirely.** "Now let's get rid of agency altogether... you don't even make the choice that you like" — the racist "didn't choose that," and "if I was born into a family where I had the predisposition to be a serial killer... I might turn into a serial killer with no choice of my own." The challenge: "how can we have a coherent discussion about moral responsibility... if you're not morally responsible for any of your choices because you ultimately don't make choices?" [^2]
- **The genetic-determinism appeal.** The critic relays a geneticist who said it is "demonstrable that it is genetic or epigenetic" and that "child predators aren't choosing to do that; they are predisposed genetically to doing such" — concluding "if there is no agency there just is no agency and we have to deal with that," and that science shows "at best your agency is nothing like what Mormonism taught you." [^3]
- **A performative contradiction is conceded.** Even the determinist admits "we all act as though we have agency even if some of us understand that that agency is extremely limited or non-existent," and that "we use terms in ways that doesn't actually reflect what's really going on, but it's the best we have." [^4]
- **Inclination is separated from action.** Pressed on whether "a person doesn't choose to be sexually attracted to children can they choose whether or not to actually have sex with children," the critic answers "yes and no" — some can make the choice and some "are determined by it" — implicitly conceding that for at least some agents the *act* is chosen even if the *inclination* is not. [^5]
- **Anti-LDS framing.** The clip is built around the claim that Mormonism "thinks it has a right to determine what's right and wrong but... seems unable to discern such," citing the historical shift in LDS vocabulary "from free agency to moral agency." [^3]
