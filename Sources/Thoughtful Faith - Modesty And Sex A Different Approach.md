# Thoughtful Faith — Modesty And Sex: A Different Approach?

**Channel:** Thoughtful Faith ([[Jacob Hansen]])
**Format:** Solo monologue (pastoral / apologetic)

## Summary
- Opens with apparent inconsistencies (BYU cheerleaders/volleyball uniforms; Temple-endowed Polynesian Cultural Center performers; a 1950s church-sold swimsuit; shoulders fine at the beach but not at a dance) to argue modesty is not "the arbitrary whims of current leaders" and is better taught as a **principle** than a list of rules — applauding the new *For the Strength of Youth* for dropping specific rules. This applies the [[Principles Are More Fundamental Than Commandments|principles-over-commandments]] model to dress.
- Core redefinition: **modesty is fundamentally about moderation and the avoidance of vanity/pride**, not merely revealing clothing — "the opposite of vanity," the mean between extremes. Both the club outfit and the ostentatious "pioneer dress to the dance" are immodest because both seek attention/praise. See [[Modesty Is Moderation and the Avoidance of Vanity]].
- Positive theology of the body: **sexual signaling in moderation is God-designed and good** (broad shoulders, makeup, ballet/ballroom beauty all trace to complementary sexual natures); the danger is the *extreme* — the "fire" analogy (a controlled flame is powerful and beautiful; a flamethrower to roast a marshmallow is destructive). See [[Sexual Signaling in Moderation Is God-Designed and Good]].
