# Ward Radio — The Lost Churches of the Apocrypha that Survived

[[Cardon Ellis]] hosts **[[Jonah Barnes]]** (with Quku) on the apostles' dispersal into the wider world and the **contiguous non-Roman Christian churches** — Armenian, the Indian St-Thomas church, Ethiopian — that trace their founding to individual apostles and survived the Islamic conquests.

## Summary
- **The apostasy "wasn't ink, it was blood."** The gospel was planted worldwide (Thomas → India, Bartholomew → Armenia, Andrew → Scythia/Scotland, Matthew → Parthia) and was mostly destroyed not by doctrinal drift but by *conquest* (Huns, Islamic expansion); Rome's claim to be "the universal church" is partly a survivor's claim — "we were the ones who survived" [^1].
- **Contiguous apostolic churches still exist and are disbelieved.** Armenia (first Christian nation, founded per tradition by Bartholomew), the Indian "St Thomas" church (claiming descent from Thomas, with a Hebrew inscription on metal attesting an early Jewish presence), and the Ethiopian Orthodox Church (preserver of many apocrypha, claimant of the Ark) all assert apostolic provenance equal to Rome's, and are routinely dismissed [^2].
- **The Acts of Matthew mirrors 3 Nephi.** An apocryphal *Acts of Matthew (in the City of the Priests)* has Matthew report that **Jesus Christ descended from heaven to a distant city and established his church there** — months after the Ascension — read as a non-Holy-Land parallel to Christ's appearance to the Nephites, prompting the question of where else Christ went in the ~8 months before 3 Nephi [^3].
- **Aside: St Nicholas slapped Arius** at Nicaea — used to argue the creeds were forged amid "Game of Thrones" contention, not serene consensus [^4].
