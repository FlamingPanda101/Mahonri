# Dan McClellan — The Fundamental Contradiction of the Trinity

A short-form video in which biblical scholar **[[Dan McClellan]]** answers viewer requests to spell out what he calls "the fundamental contradiction of the Trinity." He argues the doctrine is internally incoherent, that it was *constructed* under Greek philosophy rather than revealed in scripture, and that the Bible's own model for Jesus' relationship to God is **divine mediation / the logic of divine images**.

## Summary
- The Trinity reduces to a contradiction expressed as a math problem: A+B+C=G while A=G, B=G, C=G, yet A≠B, A≠C, B≠C — resolved only by declaring A/B/C "persons" (who) and G "substance/ousia" (what), a move he says redefines *person* and *substance* in unprecedented ways [^1].
- The doctrine was **slowly constructed** between the mid-2nd and 5th centuries CE to meet two non-negotiable "terms" — one God, and a clearly distinct Father/Son/Spirit — not discovered as something always true [^2].
- The construction happened "under the aegis of Greek philosophy" (Middle/Neo-Platonism) as apologists pivoted from persuading Jewish to **Greco-Roman intelligentsia**, leaving an irreducible "gap" philosophers still try to cover today [^3].
- The Bible's actual model is **divine mediation**, rooted in the Hebrew Bible (the messenger of Adonai who bears the divine name, Exodus 23) and developed through Second Temple Judaism — the "intuitive logic of divine images" by which an agent bears a deity's presence and authority while remaining a distinct person [^4].
- Because the doctrine cannot actually be made coherent, the Trinity functions as an **identity marker / costly signal / credibility-enhancing display** that one must affirm ("I can see the circle being squared") to count as Christian [^5].
