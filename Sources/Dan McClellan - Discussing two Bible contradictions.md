# Dan McClellan — Discussing two Bible contradictions

[[Dan McClellan]] walks through two well-known biblical contradictions — the death of Judas and the death of King Ahaziah — to make a meta-point about authority: the harmonizer who insists both accounts are accurate is not actually treating the *text* as authoritative, but is imposing an external *dogma* (inerrancy) and doing "violence" to the text to preserve it.

- **Death of Judas.** [[Acts 1.18-19|Acts 1:18–19]] (Judas buys a field, falls headlong, bursts open; field called Field of Blood after his gore) vs. [[Matthew 27.3-5|Matthew 27:3–5]] (Judas throws the silver in the temple and hangs himself; the *priests* buy the potter's field; "blood" = Jesus's blood money). The accounts disagree on the manner of death, who bought the field, when, and *why* it is the Field of Blood.[^1] See [[The Death of Judas Contradiction]].
- **Death of Ahaziah.** [[2 Kings 9.27|2 Kings 9:27]] (shot in his chariot near Ibleam, flees to Megiddo, dies there; *his own officers* carry him to Jerusalem and bury him) vs. [[2 Chronicles 22.8-9|2 Chronicles 22:8–9]] (captured hiding in Samaria, brought to Jehu, put to death; *Jehu's men* bury him). Incompatible location, manner, and who buries him.[^2] See [[The Death of Ahaziah Contradiction]].
- McClellan's analytic point: harmonizations require (a) inventing scenarios "not in evidence" and (b) asserting implausible interpretations (e.g., Daniel Wallace's middle-voice reading of "acquired" in Acts 1:18), and the conjunction of multiple independently-improbable claims is *less* probable than the least-likely one alone. See [[Stacked Improbable Harmonizations Multiply Down to a Sliver]].
- The framing thesis (the heart of the video) is the authority inversion: "Not impossible" is the wrong bar; if you must overrule what the text plainly says to save the dogma, your real authority is the tradition, not the text. This overlaps the vault's [[Not Impossible Is Not the Standard for Contradiction]] and [[Harmonization Defends a Dogma Not a Conclusion]].[^3]
