# Ward Radio - What Happened to the First 6 Factions of Mormonism

A Ward Radio episode on the post-1844 **succession crisis** and the original breakaway factions of Joseph Smith's church — the "where are they now?" of the Restoration. Hosts [[Cardon Ellis]] and the co-hosts interview **[[Christopher Blythe]]**, a BYU folklorist who has personally visited and interviewed members of the surviving sects, to "demystify" the graphic that circulates on the Mormon and ex-Mormon subreddits. The guest narrates the fate of each movement while the hosts argue the Brighamite line is the legitimate continuation. See [[Succession Resolved Through the Keys of the Twelve]], [[Brigham Young Saved the Church Through the Keys]].

## Summary

- **Framing: "atomization," not "schism."** Blythe reports that the scholar [[Jan Shipps]] (a Methodist) called the 1844 fracture an *atomization* — "we just all broke apart and took parts of the original church" — and Blythe himself avoids the word *schism* as too Catholic and too suggestive of an even split, since "ours is really the only remaining branch of any significant size." He lists small in-life pretenders Joseph already warned against (Francis Gladden Bishop, Isaac Russell) in the sermon "Try the Spirits," then the **six** post-martyrdom movements: Brigham Young, Sidney Rigdon, James Strang, the Cutlerites, the RLDS, and the Hedrickites/Church of Christ (Temple Lot). [^1]
- **Growth past the second generation is offered as the test of a movement.** Blythe's "line to get into America vs. the USSR" analogy: the ability to grow past the first or second generation "says a lot." He judges only **two** sects to still be growing — the Brighamites and the **Bickertonites** (drawing converts, many immigrants and disaffected Latter-day Saints; converts named include Mitiga and Jacob Isbell) — while the Community of Christ has had to "sell off assets just to be solvent." See [[Growth Past the Second Generation Validates a Movement]]. [^2]
- **The Cutlerites: the only branch besides Brigham's to keep the 1844 endowment.** [[Alpheus Cutler]] received his second anointing under Joseph Smith (the sixth person ever), gave Nauvoo sermons that "my salvation depends on sustaining the Twelve," but stopped his 1847 trek in Iowa for a Native-American mission and, after seeing "two back-to-back half moons," reorganized the church. The Cutlerite chapel in Missouri still performs endowments in an "upper room" but lost *sealing* in the 1850s. Cutler's succession claim: ordained one of seven private prophets by Joseph, becoming leader when Joseph's uncle John Smith died. Down to about six elderly members; they await a "one mighty and strong" / "Moses man." See [[The Cutlerites Preserved the Endowment Outside Utah]]. [^3]
- **Dropping polygamy keeps pulling sealing — and the whole plan of salvation — out with it.** A recurring thesis: the Cutlerites suspended plural marriage "until Jesus Christ returns" and lost sealing because "sealing implies the possibility of plural marriage"; Rigdon's and Bickerton's movements were polygamy-denying; and the RLDS, after sending pastors to liberal Bible schools in the 1960s, chipped away at the apostasy, then at baptism for the dead, then at the plan of salvation (Blythe's missionary anecdote: an RLDS "high priestess" had lost the pre-existence and the meaning of baptism for the dead). See [[Rejecting Polygamy Unravels Sealing and the Plan of Salvation]]. [^4]
- **The Rigdonites → Bickertonites.** [[Sydney Rigdon]], publicly ordained a prophet/seer/revelator, claimed a vision (a continuation of D&C 76) making him "guardian" until Joseph returns as a mighty angel; he reorganized a Council of Fifty ("the kingdom of God") in 1845, then a false prophecy collapsed the movement. A later 1867 revival in New York produced ~200 written revelations (including that the Nephites survive "among the Eskimo"). [[William Bickerton]] passed *through* Rigdon but then joined Brigham Young, left over polygamy, and styled himself a modern "Alma the Elder" with an independent revelation — so Bickertonites de-emphasize the Rigdon lineage. They practice tongue-speaking (Blythe's "holy envy"), footwashing, and a "red letter" Book of Mormon with a pointed introduction. [^5]
- **Tongue-speaking as a lost gift.** Blythe argues Latter-day Saints, the Shakers, and the Irvingites were the tongue-speaking churches of Joseph's era; the LDS Church "backed down" after ~1910 to avoid being confused with Pentecostalism, with talks against it under [[Heber J. Grant]] — ironically the same Grant who recounted being blessed in tongues by [[Eliza R. Snow]] as a child. [^6]
- **The RLDS / Community of Christ.** Grew from an 1851–52 William-Smith offshoot: a member received a revelation that [[William Smith]]'s claim (that [[Joseph Smith III]] would be prophet) was true but that William had "lost the birthright." Scattered Strangites, Williamites, and Thompsonites coalesced and operated for eight years before Joseph Smith III arrived in 1860. Joseph III adopted his mother's "it never happened" line on polygamy and a "not yet / don't talk about it" strategy (on plural gods, the King Follett teachings, baptism for the dead as a "local commandment") that weeded doctrines out over a generation; sections were moved to an appendix in the 1920s and later removed. Blythe worked for the Community of Christ in 2011. [^7]
- **The Strangites.** Prospered until Beaver Island, where [[James Strang]] crowned himself king and introduced an endowment called the **Order of the Illuminati**, plus animal sacrifice and an annual "King's Day" (July 8). His "Letter of Appointment" is widely judged a forgery — [[D. Michael Quinn]] first catalogued the competing succession orders, and the hosts do a live signature comparison ("looks janky") and stress Strang had been a member only **four months** and met Joseph **once**. [[Don Bradley]]: Joseph's problem "was not that he had too little succession plans but he had too many." See [[The Strangite Letter of Appointment Is a Forgery]]. Strang's *Book of the Law of the Lord* (from the plates of Laban, preceded by the small Voree plates) restores the Decalogue and orders sacrifice and the feasts. ~60–70 remain, gathered not at Beaver Island but at Voree, Wisconsin. [^8]
- **The Hedrickites (Church of Christ, Temple Lot).** Formed in the 1860s under Granville Hedrick, associated with [[David Whitmer]]; they hold the Independence "Zion's plat" and the cornerstone of the original temple. Uniquely they hold Joseph became a *fallen prophet* — everything went astray at Zion's Camp (1834) — and limit scripture to the Bible and Book of Mormon (with the Book of Commandments as a guide). An offshoot (Church of Christ "Elijah Message") adds *The Word of the Lord*, revelations from John the Baptist. See [[Granville Hedrick]]. [^9]
- **Closing favorites.** Asked which sect they prefer: Blythe picks the **Cutlerites** (for the original claims and the preserved endowment); Cardon picks the Cutlerites too (Strang "took things a little far"); Brad sticks with the **Bickertonites**. The episode is shot through with banter (recurring jokes about Brad becoming "king of the Strangites"). [^10]
