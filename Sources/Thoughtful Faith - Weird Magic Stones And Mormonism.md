# Thoughtful Faith - Weird Magic Stones And Mormonism

A satirical micro-short from Thoughtful Faith. A teacher mocks Latter-day Saints for believing [[Joseph Smith]] "used stones to aid in receiving revelation from God," then in the next breath returns to a Bible lesson on [[Genesis 44.5|Genesis 44:5]] — where "God's prophet [Joseph of Egypt] used a divining cup to peer into liquids in order to receive revelation." The joke is a *tu quoque*: the same instrument-mediated revelation derided as "weird magic" in Mormonism is recorded approvingly of a patriarch in the biblical text.

- The clip sets up the standard mockery — Mormons are "so weird" for the seer-stone claim.[^1]
- It immediately pivots to [[Genesis 44.5|Genesis 44:5]], where Joseph of Egypt's silver cup is the object "whereby indeed he divineth."[^2]
- The implied argument feeds the broader case that physical revelatory instruments are biblically attested, not inherently illegitimate folk magic. See [[Seer Stones and Divining Rods as Revelatory Means]] and [[Avoiding the Pejorative Magic]].
