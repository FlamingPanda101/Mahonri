# Thoughtful Faith — Debunking Bart Ehrman Ft Inspiring Philosophy

**Channel:** Thoughtful Faith ([[Jacob Hansen]])

## Summary
- A conservative defense of Gospel **reliability** (not inerrancy) against [[Bart Ehrman]]. On authorship: internal anonymity was the *genre convention* of ancient biography, and the unanimous early attribution to Matthew, Mark, Luke, and John — including two non-eyewitnesses (Mark, Luke) — is evidence *for* authenticity, since no forger would pick minor non-apostles. See [[Internal Anonymity Was the Convention of Ancient Biography]], [[Attributing Gospels to Mark and Luke Argues for Authenticity]], and [[Unanimous Patristic Attestation of the Gospel Authors]].
- On *whether* the writers had access to facts: [[Luke VanderLaan|onomastic congruence]] — the Gospels' personal-name distributions match the right first-century Palestinian frequencies, something fiction cannot fake (Luke explicitly used eyewitnesses). And the "high literary Greek" objection fails because ancient authors used trained **scribes/amanuenses**, Matthew was a literate tax-collector, and only John is contestable (he dictated). See [[Onomastic Congruence Shows the Gospels Used Eyewitness Sources]] and [[The High-Greek Objection Ignores Scribes and Amanuenses]].
- On **dating**: the abrupt ending of Acts (Paul still alive, awaiting trial before Caesar) dates Acts ~62 AD, pulling Luke and Mark earlier — an argument from *presence* (epic foreshadowing left unpaid), not silence. The "prophecy of the temple's fall requires a post-70 date" move merely presupposes prophecy is impossible. See [[The Abrupt Ending of Acts Dates It Before 62 AD]] and [[Post-70 Dating Presupposes Prophecy Is Impossible]].
- On **Christology**: Jones argues a high Christology is already explicit in Mark ("Lord of the Sabbath," preparing the way for Yahweh, the Son of Man on the clouds of Daniel 7) and that "Lord Lord" in Luke/Matthew echoes *Adonai Yahweh* — so John is not a late *development* but a more explicit statement of the same claim. See [[High Christology Is Already Present in Mark Not a Late Development]].
- On **reliability vs inerrancy**: scribal errors (David vs. Elhanan killing Goliath; carry-a-staff vs. carry-no-staff) do not defeat reliability, and demanding Quranic letter-perfection of the Bible is a category error (the Bible claims inspiration through fallible scribes, not "uncreated speech"). This updates [[Reliability Not Infallibility Standard]] and [[Pericope Adulterae Is a Later Interpolation]].
