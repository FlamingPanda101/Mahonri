# Stephen Meyer Debates Peter Atkins (Unbelievable)

A *Unbelievable?* episode (Premier Christian Radio, hosted by [[Justin Brierley]]) built around the UK DVD release of the intelligent-design documentary *Expelled*, featuring a sharp exchange between ID proponent [[Stephen C. Meyer]] (by phone) and atheist Oxford chemist [[Peter Atkins]], with filmmaker Mark Haville also in studio.[^1] The core dispute is whether the digital information in DNA points to an intelligent cause or can emerge by undirected natural processes, and — more fundamentally — whether intelligent design can count as science at all. (Several names are rendered phonetically in the transcript, e.g. "Steven C. Meer," "Justin Bryley.")

## Participants
- [[Stephen C. Meyer]] (intelligent design) · [[Peter Atkins]] (atheism/scientism) · host [[Justin Brierley]] · Mark Haville (Christian filmmaker, UK distributor of *Expelled*)

## Summary
- **The origin of biological information.** Meyer argues that the digital, sequence-specific code in DNA, like any information, always traces in our uniform experience to a mind, and that no undirected theory (chance, self-organization, RNA world) has explained its origin.[^2] Atkins replies that "structures include information" and arise without an agent — e.g. via the second law of thermodynamics and local "abatements of chaos."[^3]
- **Order vs. specified information.** Meyer concedes energy flow can produce *order* (vortices, spiral waves) but insists that is categorically different from the *specified complexity* of DNA, whose base sequence is not fixed by chemical bonds; Atkins calls the order/information distinction unconvincing and the appeal to a designer "a pure assertion."[^4]
- **Is design science?** Atkins: "there's no science at all involved" in ID — it is "a straw man in a cheap tuxedo."[^5] Meyer answers that ID uses the *same* historical/abductive method as Darwin (inference to the best explanation from cause and effect), so the exclusion rests on an arbitrary, materialist definition of science.[^6]
- **Scientism and "the context of inquiry."** Atkins holds science can in principle explain everything via simple universal laws and that no field is "an island"; Meyer warns that ruling out intelligent causes in advance may exclude the true explanation by stipulation.[^7]
- **"God of the gaps" vs. "intellectual laziness."** Atkins charges that ID is intellectually lazy, a science-stopper, and that teaching children "we can't know" is "child abuse"; Meyer counters that ID is an argument from what we *do* know (not ignorance), makes predictions (junk-DNA function, confirmed by ENCODE 2007), and that the founders of modern science assumed design without it ever stopping inquiry.[^8]
- **The Sternberg affair / suppression.** Much of the program recounts *Expelled*'s claim that scientists (Richard Sternberg, who published Meyer's Cambrian-explosion paper) were professionally punished for entertaining ID, illustrating the film's thesis that dissent from Darwinism is suppressed.[^9]
