# The Stick of Joseph - The Cultic Prophet in Ancient Israel

[[Mike Day]] and [[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)]] (with Brad Whitbeck) review [[Aubrey Johnson]]'s *The Cultic Prophet in Ancient Israel*. The book argues for **cultic prophets** — temple-attached, music-making prophets organized in guilds — whose doctrine is embedded in the Psalms. The hosts draw out the Christian prayer-circle / dance (choros), the "way, the truth, and the life" creed, and a retranslation of Luke 2:49.

## Summary
- **"Cultic" means of the temple.** The hosts defang the word: *cult* = care/worship (cf. *agri-culture*, the care of the land); a cultic prophet is a temple-worship prophet, not a Jim-Jones cult.[^1]
- **David's singing prophets.** 1 Chronicles 25 has David appoint guilds (Asaph, Heman, Jeduthun) "to prophesy with harps" (verb *naba*); these prophesying musicians are the temple's human counterpart to the singing angels of Isaiah 6, and may be the *qedeshim* Josiah purged.[^2] See [[Cultic Prophets Were Singing Temple Musicians]].
- **The Christian prayer circle / dance (choros).** Citing Nicoletta Isar's *Choros: The Dance of Adam*, the hosts note the Anastasis icon (Christ raising Adam and Eve by the wrist), the "Christian prayer circles," and the etymology linking *choros*, the pilgrim *hag/hajj*, and the circle dance around the altar.[^3] See [[The Christian Prayer Circle Descends From an Ancient Temple Dance]].
- **The cultic prophets' creed.** Johnson's epilogue: the Psalms repeatedly teach "the way, the truth, and the life" and "the righteous shall live by his faith" — a creed that bears on the birth of Christianity "to a far greater degree than is commonly recognized."[^4] See [[The Cultic Prophets Creed Was the Way the Truth and the Life]].
- **The Psalms predate the Deuteronomists.** Johnson argues the Psalms are old (Davidic-era) and were *not* rewritten by the Deuteronomic school; where they overlap, Deuteronomy borrowed from the Psalms, not vice versa.[^5] See [[The Psalms Predate and Were Not Rewritten by the Deuteronomists]].
- **Luke 2:49 — "among my father's people."** The Greek *en tois tou patros mou* has no noun; the hosts argue the natural supply (matching v. 44, where Joseph and Mary seek Jesus "among their kinsfolk") is "among my father's **people**" — possibly old-tradition temple teachers who recognized him.[^6] See [[Luke 2-49 May Read Among My Fathers People Not About My Fathers Business]].
