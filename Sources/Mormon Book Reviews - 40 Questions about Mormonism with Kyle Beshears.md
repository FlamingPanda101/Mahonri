# Mormon Book Reviews — 40 Questions about Mormonism (with Kyle Beshears)

A friendly first-look interview on the *Mormon Book Reviews* channel, hosted by [[Steven Pynakker]], with evangelical scholar-pastor [[Kyle Beshears]] about his new Kregel Academic volume **40 Questions about Mormonism** (release Feb 24 2026). Beshears frames the book as a *non-anti-Mormon* introduction written for evangelicals who know little about the Latter-day Saint faith, deliberately holding a "tension in the center" between the twin errors of anti-Mormon caricature and syncretism that pretends the differences do not matter. The conversation ranges over the loaded word "cult," whether Mormonism is "American," the nature and authorship of the Book of Mormon, the famous "after all we can do" verse, and the perennial "are Mormons Christian?" question.

- **The word "cult" is rejected as both inaccurate and pastorally counterproductive.** Beshears argues the popular sense of "cult" (Jim Jones, Heaven's Gate, a small, controlling group around one charismatic leader) does not fit a 16-million-member global church that tolerates a wide spectrum of views (he names [[Dan McClellan]] and [[Jacob Hansen]] as opposite poles it accommodates); worse, calling a member a "cultist" builds defensive walls and confirms the persecution narrative real cult leaders depend on. [^1][^2][^3] See [[Mormonism Is Not a Cult]], [[Calling Someone a Cultist Is Counterproductive]], [[Cult and Evangelical Are Both Loaded Words]].
- **The governing method is "do unto others": represent Mormonism accurately even where you most disagree.** Beshears grounds the duty to study Mormonism in the greatest commandment — you cannot love your LDS neighbor if what you believe about them is a caricature — and refuses both anti-Mormon contempt and the synretistic move that "differences don't matter." [^4][^5][^6] See [[Love of Neighbor Requires Understanding]], [[Holding the Tension Between Anti-Mormonism and Syncretism]], [[Would God Not Could God]].
- **Mormonism is not merely an American religion, and not merely the Utah church.** Roughly half of Latter-day Saints live outside the U.S.; Beshears tests Mormonism against the "world religion" criterion (thriving across many cultures) and judges it still struggles because it exports Utah/Intermountain-West norms; he also profiles non-Utah restoration branches — Community of Christ, the Bickertonites, and especially [[James Strang]]. [^7][^8][^9] See [[Is Mormonism Just an American Religion]], [[Mormonism Is More Than the Utah Church]], [[James Strang and the Strangite Movement]].
- **Beshears denies Joseph Smith authored the Book of Mormon — but calls it "American Pseudepigrapha," not ancient scripture.** He judges the text too theologically sophisticated for a man in his early twenties, posits an "original Book of Mormon beneath the manuscript" with Joseph as redactor/editor, yet still concludes it is falsely attributed and reflects early-19th-century American Protestant concerns. He reads "by grace... after all we can do" (2 Nephi) as 19th-century English for "*despite* all we can do" — a strikingly evangelical, grace-alone teaching. [^10][^11][^12][^13] See [[Book of Mormon as American Pseudepigrapha]], [[Joseph Smith Did Not Author the Book of Mormon]], [[After All We Can Do Means In Spite of All We Can Do]], [[Could God vs Would God on the Book of Mormon]].
- **On "are Mormons Christian?" Beshears refuses a black-and-white answer.** Mormonism has departed so far from creedal orthodoxy (especially on God's nature / the Godhead vs. Trinity) that evangelicals are right to push back; yet he affirms that *individuals* within the LDS church can be "in potential or actual communion with Christ," and notes even Rome rebaptizes LDS converts because the differences are real. He also revisits the Alvin Smith / Presbyterian funeral story as likely "misremembered" Mormon history. [^14][^15][^16][^17] See [[Are Mormons Christian (Beshears)]], [[Different Jesus Both Uncharitable and Precisely True]], [[The Alvin Smith Funeral Story Is Misremembered]].
