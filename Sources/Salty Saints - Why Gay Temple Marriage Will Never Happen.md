# Salty Saints - Why Gay Temple Marriage Will Never Happen

**Show:** Salty Saints | **Host:** [[Hayden Holm]] | **Guests:** [[Tikla Fe]] (Ward Radio Women), [[Jeff Borders]] (*The Antichrist Playbook*), [[Sam St. Laurent]] (Through the Mist) | **Title:** "Why Gay Temple Marriage Will Never Happen — Matrimony EXPLAINED"

A panel discussion arguing — primarily through [[Tikla Fe]]'s etymological reading — that Latter-day Saint temple sealing is intrinsically ordered toward motherhood and therefore cannot accommodate a same-sex union. The episode opens with an extended plea for charity toward LGBTQ individuals before building its doctrinal case.

- **Meet people where they are — without surrendering doctrine.** Tikla opens by honoring her late aunt (a lesbian-community leader in Portland) and arguing Latter-day Saints should "meet people where they are" (even wearing rainbow socks to make someone welcome) without that meaning a retreat from the family proclamation — and should stop *vilifying* those who try to make others feel welcome. Sam pushes back that church/temple emphasize a shared, label-transcending identity (white shirts, temple whites).[^1] See [[Meet People Where They Are Without Surrendering the Doctrine]].
- **Patriarchal order = the order of fatherhood, not male rule.** "Patriarchal" means *fatherhood* (patri) in its relation to leading/beginning (archy); so the priesthood is "an order of fatherhood" that makes men into good father-figures — "the best thing you can do for a woman and children is to have good fathers."[^2] See [[The Patriarchal Order Is the Order of Fatherhood Not Male Rule]].
- **Matrimony = the making of a mother.** Where most masculine-facing words (mankind, actors) encompass both sexes, the one exception that is feminine-facing yet encompasses men and women is *matrimony* — from *mater*, "mother." Tikla reads the temple sealing as "the holy order of making a mother": the entire covenant path (priesthood at 12 onward) trains a man to be worthy to be authorized to "unlock a woman's potential for motherhood." Hence a man-and-man sealing makes no sense of the order's name and purpose.[^3] See [[Matrimony Means the Making of a Mother]].
- **Chaos/order, the sealing text, and protecting women.** The panel adds temple square-circle symbolism (masculine order containing feminine "chaos"/mercy/life), notes the sealing liturgy would have to be rewritten line-by-line for a same-sex union, and frames the sealing as the Father "with a shotgun" protecting his daughters — even, Tikla says, protecting women who "beg not to be protected."[^4] See [[The Sealing Ordinance Is Worded for Man and Woman]].
