# Ward Radio — Debunking Mike Winger's "Irrefutable Proof Mormonism is FALSE"

A [[Ward Radio]] episode hosted by [[Cardon Ellis]] with [[Luke Hansen]], responding clip-by-clip to evangelical pastor [[Mike Winger]]'s video claiming "irrefutable proof" that Mormonism is false via the [[Book of Abraham]]. The hosts (who disclaim being Egyptologists) argue Winger massively overstates his case: his "silver bullets" rest on a misunderstanding of ancient copyist conventions, a century-old questionnaire, and arguments that — applied consistently — would also sink the Bible. The Winger-specific framing is **new to the vault**; several substantive points overlap and reinforce the existing Book-of-Abraham cluster.

## Summary

- **"Written by his own hand" is ancient copyist usage, not an autograph claim.** The 1,800-year gap between Abraham and the papyrus is exactly what one expects of a *copy*; the Shabaka Stone, Acts, and Pythagoras show ancient texts attribute copies to the original author. The same "late manuscripts" logic would disprove Genesis and Acts. See [[The Hand of Abraham Phrase Is Ancient Copyist Usage Not Autograph]]. [^1]
- **Winger's "renowned Egyptologists" all answered a single 1912 Episcopal questionnaire.** Every scholar he quotes (Petrie, Mace, Peters, von Bissing, etc.) responded to Bishop Franklin S. Spalding's 1912 solicitation to discredit the Book of Abraham — "the CES Letter of 1912," not current scholarship. See [[Wingers Egyptologists All Trace to a 1912 Episcopal Questionnaire]]. [^2]
- **Winger's arguments are the atheist arguments against the Bible.** "Late copies," "scholars say it's fabricated," and "no archaeological proof" are exactly what atheists say about Scripture — which Winger, an evangelical, rejects for his own book. See [[The Anti-Book-of-Abraham Argument Is the Atheist Argument Against the Bible]]. [^3]
- **Several Book-of-Abraham details were corroborated by texts found after 1842** (literate/astronomer Abraham, Egyptian and Levantine child sacrifice), and the Osiris↔Abraham facsimile swap matches Jesus' own Setne-based parable in [[Luke 16]] — while Winger's demand for "irrefutable proof" misreads how God gives evidence. See [[Later Texts Corroborate Details Joseph Smith Could Not Have Known in the Book of Abraham]], [[Facsimile 3 Identifications Vindicate Joseph Smith by Coincidence]], and [[God Gives Enough Evidence to Seek Not to Compel]]. [^4]
