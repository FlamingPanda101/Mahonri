# Luther Added a Word to The Bible! Did Your Pastor Tell You That?

[[Alex Arnold]] of *Moroni's Standard* argues that Protestant *sola fide* ("faith alone") is not a biblical doctrine recovered from corruption but a 16th-century invention by [[Martin Luther]] — who in his 1522 German New Testament rendered [[Romans 3.28|Romans 3:28]] "justified by faith **alone** (*allein*)," a word absent from the Greek, then defended the insertion by citing his own authority. The episode's spine: the phrase "faith alone" appears exactly **once** in scripture, [[James 2.24|James 2:24]], where the Bible explicitly *negates* it — so a Protestant standing on *sola scriptura* is building a primary doctrine on a phrase the only verse using it rejects.

Arnold contrasts three soteriologies (Protestant *sola fide*, Catholic faith-plus-sacraments, and the LDS grace-through-faith-repentance-baptism-covenant model) and argues the LDS position is "not adding to the gospel" but "reading the whole thing." His authorities are the primary texts themselves and Luther's own letter; the framing is polemical in places. Much of this overlaps the vault's existing Catholic-framed cluster — [[Luther and the Epistle of Straw]], [[James 2-24 - Justified by Works Not Faith Alone]], [[Faith Alone but Never Alone]] — but adds the distinctively LDS angles (exaltation vs. resurrection, proxy baptism, "after all we can do").

## Summary
- **Luther inserted *allein* into Romans 3:28.** His 1522 translation reads "justified by faith *alone* apart from the works of the law"; *allein* is in no Greek manuscript. When Catholic scholars objected, Luther defended it as required by German idiom and, in effect, because "Dr. Martin Luther would have it so." See [[Luther Inserted Allein Faith Alone Into Romans 3-28]] and [[Romans 3.28|Romans 3:28]].
- **"Faith alone" appears once in scripture — and is denied.** [[James 2.24|James 2:24]]: "a man is justified by works and **not by faith only**." James 2 is a sustained argument that bare mental assent is not saving ("even the demons believe and shudder," v.19; "faith without works is dead," v.26). Luther called James "an epistle of straw" and tried to relocate it — see [[Faith Alone Appears Once in Scripture and Is the Verse That Denies It]].
- **Jesus never taught faith alone; he taught doing.** [[Matthew 7.21|Matthew 7:21]] ("he that doeth the will of my Father"), [[Matthew 25.31-46|Matthew 25:31-46]] (the sheep/goats judged entirely on works), Matthew 24:13 ("endures to the end"), John 14:15 ("keep my commandments"). See [[Jesus Taught Salvation Involves Doing Not Faith Alone]].
- **Paul is misread by stopping at Ephesians 2:9.** [[Ephesians 2.8-9|Ephesians 2:8-9]] ("by grace... not of works") is immediately followed by v.10 ("created in Christ Jesus **unto good works**"); and [[Romans 2.6-7|Romans 2:6-7]] ("render to each according to his works... eternal life") sits a chapter before the faith-alone prooftext. What Paul rejects is *earning righteousness by the law of Moses*, not works as such. See [[Paul Read Whole Rejects the Law of Moses Not Good Works]].
- **The LDS model is grace-based, not works-based.** Salvation is by the grace of Christ working *through* faith, repentance, baptism ([[Acts 2.38|Acts 2:38]], John 3:5), the gift of the Holy Ghost, and covenant faithfulness. Universal **resurrection** is an unconditional free gift; **exaltation** is conditional and can be forfeited (D&C 82:10). "After all we can do" ([[2 Nephi 25.23|2 Nephi 25:23]]) means *in spite of* our effort. See the vault's [[LDS Salvation Is by Grace Not Earned by Works]], [[After All We Can Do Means In Spite of All We Can Do]], and [[Moroni 10.32|Moroni 10:32]].
- **The hard cases are handled by an infinitely fair God.** The thief on the cross predates the new covenant's binding ordinances (Hebrews: a covenant takes effect at death); children who die, the cognitively disabled, and the billions who never heard are covered by grace or by proxy ordinances ([[1 Corinthians 15.29|1 Corinthians 15:29]] — baptism for the dead, used by Paul without objection). See [[Every Person Gets a Fair Opportunity Through Proxy Ordinances]].
