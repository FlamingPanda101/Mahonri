# Ward Radio — Catholics Critique Mormonism on the Daily Wire

A Ward Radio livestream reacting to a ~80-minute segment in which Catholic apologist [[Joe Heschmeyer]] critiques the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints on Matt Fradd's *Pints with Aquinas* (now hosted on the Daily Wire). The panel — host [[Cardon Ellis]], [[Jacob Hansen]], [[Luke Irwin]], and [[Jasmine Rapley]] — organizes the rebuttal into four themes Heschmeyer raised: changing doctrine, philosophy, succession, and the future of Mormonism.

- **The "rebrand" / preferential-treatment frame:** Ellis argues the Catholic claim that the Church "rebranded" is a *cope* born of niceness culture — critics so used to preferential treatment now mistake ordinary pushback for the Church having changed, when in fact a seniority-based leadership model has produced doctrinal continuity for a century.[^1] See [[Equal Treatment Mistaken for Persecution]].
- **The double standard on doctrinal change:** The core rebuttal throughout is *tu quoque* with teeth — every charge Heschmeyer levels (Adam-God, race/priesthood, infallibility, succession crises, contingency, creation) is "on steroids" within Catholic history (papal infallibility invented late, Marian dogmas, slave-trading orders, burning heretics, the three-popes schism), yet Catholics claim they "never changed."[^2] See [[Catholic Doctrinal Development Is the Same Double Standard]] and [[Tabernacle Infallibility Misreads Brigham Young]].
- **The race/priesthood "switcheroo":** The panel argues the priesthood ban was the *development* (absorbed from surrounding Protestant curse-of-Cain folklore), not the 1978 lifting — Joseph Smith ordained Black men; the ban was the deviation.[^3] See [[The Priesthood Ban Was the Development Not Its Reversal]] and [[Book of Mormon Skin of Blackness Is Not About Race]].
- **The atheism charge and contingency argument:** Heschmeyer's one real argument is that the contingency argument that refutes atheism also refutes Mormonism, because LDS reject an immaterial, uncaused, ex-nihilo creator. The panel answers that this God is Aristotle's unmoved mover dressed up (proving the "pagan philosophy" point), that LDS revelation-based epistemology — not syllogism — is the LDS basis for God, and that pre-existence actually *solves* the problem of evil.[^4] See [[Latter-day Saints Are Atheists Charge Rebutted]], [[The Uncaused Cause Is Aristotle Not Scripture]], and [[Pre-Existence Solves the Problem of Evil]].
- **First Vision, succession, abortion, and the future:** Multiple accounts of the First Vision are no more contradictory than the four Gospels (Prothero quote); the succession crisis resolves cleanly via [[D&C 76|D&C]] 107 and the keys to the Quorum of the Twelve; the LDS abortion position follows from pre-existence theology, not from "caving to culture"; and LDS retention is among the highest of any U.S. tradition.[^5] See [[First Vision Accounts Are No More Contradictory Than the Gospels]], [[Succession Resolved Through the Keys of the Twelve]], [[LDS Abortion Position Follows From Pre-Existence Not Cultural Caving]], and [[LDS Retention Is Among the Highest of Any Tradition]].
