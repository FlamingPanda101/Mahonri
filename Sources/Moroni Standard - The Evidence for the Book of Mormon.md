# The Evidence for the Book of Mormon

Alex Arnold of *Moroni Standard* answers the recurring claim that "there is zero evidence for the Book of Mormon." He deliberately leans on scholars who are **not** LDS — Emanuel Tov, Frank Moore Cross, Margaret Barker, Bart Ehrman — and flags the one LDS source he uses (Matt Roper). His thesis: a fraud should accumulate problems under two centuries of scrutiny, whereas the Book of Mormon's list of alleged anachronisms has *shrunk*, and several independent lines of evidence (Dead Sea Scrolls variants, the Nahom inscription, chiasmus, stylometry, and Deuteronomistic-reform theology) postdate Joseph Smith and could not have been faked.

- **The anachronism list is collapsing, not growing.** Roper's 2019 survey of 1,000+ publications found 141 of 205 claimed anachronisms fully eliminated by *external, non-LDS* archaeology, with another ~11% trending toward elimination — the opposite of what fraud-under-scrutiny normally produces [^7][^8].
- **The text's Isaiah variants and theology match pre-standardization readings Joseph could not access.** Some Book of Mormon variants align with the 1947 Dead Sea Scrolls better than the KJV does, and the book's embodied/pre-existent Christ, divine council, and open theophany match the very theology Josiah's 621 BC Deuteronomistic Reform suppressed — a framework not assembled until 20th-century scholarship [^11][^12][^29][^33].
- **The internal evidence (dictation feat, stylometry, chiasmus) and the hard physical evidence (Nahom) both point away from single 19th-century authorship** [^14][^15][^18][^21].
