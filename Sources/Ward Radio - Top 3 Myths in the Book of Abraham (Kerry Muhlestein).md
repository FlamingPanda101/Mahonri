# Ward Radio - Top 3 Myths in the Book of Abraham (with Kerry Muhlestein)

**Channel:** Ward Radio · **Host:** [[Cardon Ellis]] · **Guest:** [[Kerry Muhlestein]]
**URL:** https://youtu.be/N8mz4qlp8t4

## Summary
- **Myth 1:** Joseph translated the surviving papyrus, Egyptologists read it as the Book of Breathings, so Joseph was wrong. Muhlestein: the historical eyewitness sources say he was translating from a different (now-lost) scroll, so the argument is "illusory." See [[Joseph Was Not Translating the Surviving Papyrus]].
- **Myth 2:** Joseph interpreted the facsimile drawings wrongly. Muhlestein distinguishes **translating a text** from **interpreting a drawing**, and notes Egyptians loaded drawings with multiple meanings; some of Joseph's readings match Egyptologically. See [[Translating a Text Versus Interpreting a Drawing Are Different Claims]] and [[Facsimile 3 Identifications Vindicate Joseph Smith by Coincidence]].
- **Myth 3** (launch to next segment): that nothing supports the Book of Abraham, which is "palpably wrong." Discusses the "facsimile from the Book of Abraham" labeling confusion and John Gee's hypocephalus vindication where all the experts were wrong.
