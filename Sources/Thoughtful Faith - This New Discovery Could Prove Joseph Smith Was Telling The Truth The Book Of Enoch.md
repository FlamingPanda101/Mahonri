# Thoughtful Faith - This New Discovery Could Prove Joseph Smith Was Telling The Truth (The Book Of Enoch)

[[Jacob Hansen]] frames and then plays an AI-generated "podcast deep dive" built from the master's thesis of a non-LDS scholar, **[[Salvatore Cirillo]]** (Durham University), who set out to explain how [[Joseph Smith]] could have produced his Enoch material (the "extract of the prophecy of Enoch" in [[Moses 6|Moses 6]]–[[Moses 7|7]], pub. 1830–1832) given that the fuller ancient Enoch corpus — [[1 Enoch|1 Enoch]], 2 and 3 Enoch, and the *Book of the Giants* — was largely untranslated or undiscovered in his day. Cirillo's conclusion (quoted): the parallels are "beyond dispute." The video stacks named parallels against a rediscovery **timeline** to argue Joseph could not have accessed the sources.

- The thesis verdict: "individually these parallels may appear small, but when viewed together these comparisons prove substantial similarities ... which are beyond dispute" — see [[Cirillo Catalogs Substantial Enoch Parallels Beyond Dispute]].[^1]
- The catalogued parallels: Enoch directly identified with the **Son of Man**; Enoch foreseeing **Noah's birth and the flood**; the **personified, weeping earth**; the **Mahijah/Mahawai** name; "**lad**" describing the aged Enoch; the river-reversal, "open thy mouth," secret oaths/works of darkness, the **book of remembrance**, the land of righteousness, and Enoch's **enthronement/deification**.[^2]
- The availability **timeline**: Bruce's ignored 1773 manuscripts; Laurence's obscure 1821 (1 Enoch); 2 Enoch not until 1896; 3 Enoch deciphered 1892; the Dead Sea *Book of the Giants* from 1946 — all after 1832 — see [[The Enoch Rediscovery Timeline Postdates Joseph Smith]].[^3]
- Even the skeptic [[Harold Bloom]] found Joseph's access to ancient concepts remarkable; the parallel to the **Mahijah** name is treated elsewhere in [[The Book of the Giants Names Mahijah Matching Joseph Smith's Enoch]].[^4]
- The closing challenge: "critics, are you going to look at all of the evidence or just the evidence that fits your bias?"[^5]
