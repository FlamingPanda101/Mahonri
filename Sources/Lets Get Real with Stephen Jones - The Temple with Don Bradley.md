# Let's Get Real with Stephen Jones - The Temple with Don Bradley

**Show:** Let's Get Real (Episode E0047) | **Host:** [[Stephen Jones]] | **Guest:** [[Don Bradley]] (historian, *The Lost 116 Pages*) | **Title:** "I've Never Understood The Temple Like This | Latter-day Saint, Don Bradley | E0047"

A long-form devotional interview answering the perception that Latter-day Saint temple worship is full of "weird ritual actions" unconnected to the Bible. Bradley's unifying thesis: every distinctive element — the dedication, the hosanna shout, the endowment, the garment — is **directly about Jesus Christ**, and "if we're not understanding these things in connection with Jesus Christ, we're understanding them wrong." The argument is built largely on **chronology** ("much of history is just getting the events in the right order").

- The spine of the episode reads the **1836 Kirtland Temple dedication as a day-for-day recapitulation of Christ's final week**: March 27 (dedicatory prayer, hosanna shout) was **Palm Sunday**; April 3 (the risen Christ's appearance accepting the house, [[D&C 110]]) was simultaneously **Easter and Passover**, with Elijah appearing on the Passover that leaves an empty seat for him.[^1] See [[The Kirtland Dedication Recapitulates Christ's Final Week]].
- The **hosanna shout and waving of the handkerchief** are recast as a reenactment of the **palm branches** at Christ's triumphal entry — welcoming the Lord into his house to cleanse and consecrate it, "on earth as it is in heaven."[^2] See [[The Hosanna Shout and Handkerchief Reenact the Palm Branches]].
- The **garment** is reframed from a modesty device to a **name-bearing** symbol: as the high priest wore the Tetragrammaton (Y-H-W-H) on his miter ([[Exodus 28.30|Exodus 28]]), so the wearer "puts on the name of Christ," decoding the [[D&C 109]] phrase that the Lord will "put his name upon them."[^3] See [[We Wear the Divine Name - The Tetragrammaton on the High Priest and the Garment]].
- Bradley recounts his own path from **ex-Mormon atheist back to the Church**, reading the practice of **restoration of blessings** (original baptismal date restored "as if you had never left") as an enactment of "I, the Lord, will remember his sins no more," and critiquing the cultural tendency to villainize those who leave.[^4] See [[Restoration of Blessings Enacts I Remember His Sins No More]].
- He restates his signature argument that the **endowment is already in the 1830 Book of Mormon** via the brother of Jared at the veil ([[Ether 3.14|Ether 3]]) — a man redeemed from the fall, tested, admitted to Christ's presence, and given white stones and a withheld/new name ([[Revelation 2.17|Revelation 2:17]]) — which he says he saw only after returning to the temple.[^5] This restates [[The Book of Mormon Already Contains the Endowment]] from a second source.
