# Holy Rebellion — Anachronisms, DNA, and Archaeology in the Book of Mormon

The [[Christian (Holy Rebellion)]] and [[Travis Anderson]] interview [[Neal Rappleye]], research associate with the Ancient America Foundation and former director of research at Book of Mormon Central, on the three most common historicity objections to the Book of Mormon: **anachronisms** (out-of-place plants, animals, and technology), **DNA** (no Near-Eastern genetic signal in Native Americans), and **archaeology** (no artifact saying "Nephite"). Rappleye's unifying move is methodological: read the Book of Mormon the way scholars read any translated ancient text, and the objections become anomalies to explore rather than defeaters.

## Summary
- **One anachronism does not sink the book.** Rappleye gives two reasons: (1) the Book of Mormon is a *translation*, and translation demonstrably introduces anachronism even into the Bible (KJV "candles" for first-century lamps), so the English text was never expected to be anachronism-free; and (2) past the iPhone-level obvious cases there is a gray zone where authentic ancient texts preserve uncorroborated, unique data — so demanding zero anachronisms means demanding nothing unique, which would discredit every long ancient text [^1].
- **The trajectory of anachronism claims runs toward the Book of Mormon, not away.** Citing [[Matt Roper]]'s inventory in *Interpreter* vol. 65 of **226** alleged anachronisms from 1830–2024, Rappleye reports ~**174 settled** (evidence found — cement, fortifications, scimitars, steel in the Old World), **31 trending** (some evidence, not settled — metallurgy, horses), and only **21 currently unsupported** [^2]. Old "smoking guns" critics once raised (gunpowder, cannons, steamboats) are simply not in the text [^3].
- **DNA does not disprove the book once you drop the empty-continent assumption.** Archaeology and the text itself both indicate other peoples were present (2 Nephi explicitly addresses "inhabitants of the world"). With a small founding group (founder effect, genetic drift) intermarrying into a large host population, the Near-Eastern signal is expected to vanish — exactly what the **2019 Philistine DNA study** shows: Greek DNA present in the first generation is gone a century later while the people remain culturally Philistine [^4]. The Haplogroup X2A argument used *for* the book also fails — it is ~10,000 BC and only a cousin lineage, not descended from Near-Eastern X [^5].
- **Old World archaeology shows strong convergence.** The river in the Valley of Lemuel (no rivers thought to reach the Red Sea until one was found in 1995), the **Nahom / NHM** tribal region exactly where Nephi's directions turn eastward along the Frankincense Trail, an Ishmael (Yasmaʿil) funerary stela from the right time and place, and the fertile **Bountiful** roughly due east — these are fixed geological/epigraphic anchors that cannot be moved to fit [^6].
- **Why Rappleye believes anyway.** No proposed objection is a defeater and no proposed evidence is a proof; the deciding factor is spiritual conviction, which liberates him to evaluate every claimed "evidence" critically rather than clinging to weak ones. "There's a lot of smoke" pointing seekers toward the fire that comes from reading the text itself [^7].
