# Ward Radio — Was Alma a High Priest of Heavenly Mother?

A [[Ward Radio]] live stream in which [[Cardon Ellis]] interviews [[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)|David Butler]] (with chat moderator Britney) on whether **Alma the Elder** baptized at the **Waters of Mormon** by the authority of the divine Mother. Butler walks "two roads" — (1) the **Virgin/Asherah** as the tree of life, and (2) **Shaddai** as the "breasted God" — and brings them together at [[Mosiah 18]], where the place is infested by *beasts* (the Mistress of beasts), set by *pure water* and a *thicket of trees* (the tree-goddess), and Alma baptizes "having authority from **El Shaddai**" ([[Mosiah 18.13]]). Much of the two-road setup duplicates existing vault notes; the **Alma-specific payoff is new**.

## Summary

- **Alma baptizes "having authority from El Shaddai" at the Waters of Mormon (new).** The scene is saturated with divine-feminine signs — beasts (the *hayot* of Shaddai), a fountain of *pure* water, a thicket of trees — and the man whose name puns on "Virgin" baptizes by the authority of the breasted God. See [[Alma Baptizes by the Authority of El Shaddai at the Waters of Mormon]]. [^1]
- **Mormon puns on Alma's name as Virgin / concealed / young man (new).** *Alma* (ʿalmah) = "virgin," *ʿelem* = "young man/stripling," *ʿalam* = "to conceal" — and Mosiah 17 puns on all three in quick succession ("a young man… he being concealed"), flagging Alma as a type of the Virgin. See [[Mormon Puns on Almas Name as Virgin Concealed and Young Man]]. [^2]
- **Eloah is a feminine form of "God" the Deuteronomists recast as demons (new).** In Deuteronomy 32:17 ("sacrificed… not to *Eloah*… to *shedim*"), *Eloah* is a feminine-for-God ("goddess") and *shedim* is consonantally identical to *shadayim* (breasts/Shaddai) — so the Deuteronomist is denying that the people sacrificed to a *goddess* (Shaddai), branding her a demon. See [[Eloah Is a Feminine Form of God Recast as Demons by the Deuteronomists]]. [^3]
- **"Alma" is one of the 72 boustrophedon names of God (new, speculative).** Read boustrophedon from Exodus 14:19-21 (three 72-letter verses), one of the 72 mystical names is *ʾLMʿ / Alma* ("eliminating negative thoughts / healing"). Butler offers this as a curiosity, not proof. See [[Alma Is One of the 72 Boustrophedon Names of God]]. [^4]
- **Two-road setup (corroborates existing notes).** The Virgin = the tree of life = Asherah ([[Nephi's Tree Is Asherah the Divine Mother]]); Shaddai = "breasted God" pointing to the Mother ([[Shaddai Is the Breasted God and Points to the Mother]]); "Lord God Almighty" = Yahweh El Shaddai, a throne-name absent from the OT but in the Book of Mormon ([[Yahweh El Shaddai Is a Throne-Name Preserved in the Book of Mormon]]); the Deuteronomists "wrote her out" ([[Deuteronomistic Corruption of the Old Testament]], [[Hezekiah Josiah and the Deuteronomist Reforms]]). The Judean pillar figurines are the smashed Mother ([[The Judean Pillar Figurines Were the Smashed Mother in the First Temple]]). [^5]
