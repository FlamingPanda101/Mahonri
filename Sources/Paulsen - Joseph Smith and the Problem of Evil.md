# Paulsen — Joseph Smith and the Problem of Evil

A BYU forum address ([September 21, 1999](https://speeches.byu.edu/talks/david-l-paulsen/joseph-smith-problem-evil/)) in which [[David Paulsen]], professor of philosophy at BYU, argues that the revelations of Joseph Smith **dissolve** the classical problems of evil that defeat mainstream Christian theodicy. Paulsen credits [[Truman Madsen]]'s *Eternal Man* and collaborative work with [[Blake Ostler]] as sources of his thinking [^1]. The talk is the primary scholarly statement behind the popular LDS claim (see [[Co-Eternality Solves the Problem of Evil]], [[Pre-Existence Solves the Problem of Evil]]) that Mormon metaphysics defeats the problem of evil.

## Structure — three problems of evil

Paulsen distinguishes three distinct problems and addresses each in turn [^2]:

1. **The Logical Problem of Evil** — the apparent contradiction between the world's evils and an all-loving, all-powerful Creator. Paulsen argues the standard ([[David Hume|Hume]]) formulation is *too narrow*: traditional theology adds two premises that intensify the problem — **absolute creation (ex nihilo)** and **absolute foreknowledge** — yielding [[Flew's Divine Complicity Argument]], on which an omniscient, omnipotent creator-from-nothing is "an accessory before the fact to every human misdeed" [^3]. He compares [[John Hick]]'s soul-making theodicy (which *keeps* ex nihilo and is forced into universalism) with Joseph Smith's, which denies ex nihilo and so escapes the dilemma. See [[Soul-Making Theodicy - Hick and Joseph Smith]], [[Hick's Universalism Collapses Into Determinism]], [[B.H. Roberts's Definition of Divine Omnipotence]], [[Lehi's Theodicy - Opposition in All Things]].

2. **The Soteriological Problem of Evil** — the apparent contradiction between "salvation comes only through Christ" ([[John 14.6]], [[Acts 4.12]]) and a perfectly loving Father, given the millions who died never hearing of Christ ([[Thomas V. Morris]]'s "scandal," [[Stephen T. Davis]]'s "Oohku"). Paulsen frames it as an [[The Soteriological Problem of Evil - The Inconsistent Triad|inconsistent triad]] resolved by a fourth premise — **postmortem evangelization and the redemption of the dead**. See [[Postmortem Salvation Resolves the Soteriological Problem]].

3. **The Practical Problem of Evil** — living trustingly in the face of overwhelming evil. Paulsen answers this less with argument than with Joseph Smith's own life, especially [[The Practical Problem of Evil - Joseph Smith at Liberty Jail|Liberty Jail]] and the revelations there ([[D&C 121.7-8]], [[D&C 122.7-8]]).

## Key claims defended

- The **trouble-making postulate is creation ex nihilo**; deny it and the logical problem dissolves because God "is not the total or even the ultimate explanation of all else" [^4]. Grounded in the [[King Follett Discourse]]: *baurau* means to organize from chaos, and "the mind or the intelligence which man possesses is co-equal [co-eternal] with God himself" [^5].
- Lehi's theodicy (2 Nephi 2): even an omnipotent God cannot bring about joy without righteousness, righteousness without freedom, or freedom without an [[Lehi's Theodicy - Opposition in All Things|opposition in all things]] [^6].
- [[B.H. Roberts's Definition of Divine Omnipotence|Omnipotence redefined]] (after [[BH Roberts]]) as the power to bring about any state of affairs *consistent with the natures of eternal existences* — yielding an "instrumentalist" view of evil as the means of soul-making [^7].
