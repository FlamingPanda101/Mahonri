# Ward Radio — The LDS Spotlight Scandal? (Part 5)

Part 5 of the Ward Radio *Glenn L. Pace Memo* series (the first of two episodes sharing the "LDS Spotlight Scandal?" title; see also [[Ward Radio - The LDS Spotlight Scandal Part 6]]). Where Parts 1–4 ([[Glenn L Pace Memo - The Satanic Panic and False Memory]], [[Glenn L Pace Memo - The Mormon Satanic Panic]], [[Ward Radio - The Glenn L Pace Memo Debunked]], [[Ward Radio - The Fever Breaks]]) traced the national panic, the Pace memo, and the collapse of the SRA movement, this installment reconstructs the **Marion Smith / ISAT** case that swept up [[Barbara Snow]], implicated apostle [[Russell M. Nelson]]'s daughter [[Brenda Miles]], convicted [[Brent Bullock]], and ended in the suicide of an innocent man — then dismantles the internet "Skull and Bones" conspiracy now attached to President Nelson.

## Summary

- **The accusation wheel spun outward from a self-recruiting therapy network.** In 1982 [[Marion Smith]] co-founded the Intermountain Sexual Abuse Treatment Center (ISAT); when her grandchildren's babysitter was accused, Smith took the word of a 9-month-old and a 2-year-old, brought her own grandchildren to [[Barbara Snow]], and the roster of accused adults and child witnesses inflated each other in a feedback loop that eventually named a gas-company president, grocery-chain president [[Richard Miles]], the local bishop, and [[Brenda Miles]] (daughter of newly-ordained apostle [[Russell M. Nelson]]). [^1][^2] See [[The Accusation Wheel Self-Recruits Accusers Through Therapy]].
- **Every accusation failed the evidence test, yet the believers never updated.** The alleged crimes (near-drownings, feces-drinking, rape, videotaped abuse, animal sacrifice) left "no marks, no blood, no bodily fluids, no trace," and no tapes were ever found; the absences were patched with unfalsifiable add-ons (the children were sedated; they were threatened with death). Alleged victims "went to newspapers and book deals before they went to law enforcement … today, they seek out podcasts instead of police. Unlike podcasts, police demand evidence." [^3][^4] See [[Accusations Cannot Transmute Into Evidence]].
- **"Bill" was cleared by every investigator and still destroyed.** Smith's son-in-law "Bill" was cleared by Salt Lake County investigators, the police (twice), and a Johns Hopkins / [[R. Jan Stout]] psychiatric evaluation; Smith's own children refused to put the granddaughters on the stand, killing the case — yet Bill lost his children, his marriage, repeated lawsuits, and finally his life to suicide, after which Smith wrote a second book about his "unproven abuse." [^5][^6] See [[An Innocent Cleared by Every Investigator Was Still Driven to Death]].
- **The Russell M. Nelson "Skull and Bones" claim confuses Utah's Owl and Key with Yale.** Internet sleuths tie Nelson to the "infamous Skull and Bones" of conspiracy lore — but that society is at *Yale*; Nelson attended the University of Utah, whose "Skull and Bones" club (under the Owl and Key honor society, founded 1909) *published its members' names*. The whole modern theory rests on this category error plus a lawyer's "keep this out of the public eye." [^7][^8] See [[The Nelson Skull and Bones Claim Confuses Utah's Owl and Key With Yale]].
- **No investigator was ever "coerced" — the 2018 relitigation collapsed and was dismissed with prejudice.** A 2018 suit (filed months after Nelson became church president) re-alleged the Miles charges and accused Nelson by name of pressuring law enforcement and the FBI. Investigators [[Michael George]], [[Diana Stevens]], and [[Leslie Lewis]] each testified that no church official interfered; the judge dismissed the case *with prejudice*. The We Are the People Utah podcast (and [[Tim Ballard]]) nonetheless keep naming Nelson, [[Ezra Taft Benson]], [[Thomas Monson]], [[Gordon B. Hinckley]], and [[Neil A. Maxwell]]. [^9][^10] See [[No Evidence the LDS Church Suppressed the Investigations]].
