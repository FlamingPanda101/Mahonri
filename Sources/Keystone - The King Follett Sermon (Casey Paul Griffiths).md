# Keystone — The King Follett Sermon (Casey Paul Griffiths)

- **The King Follett Discourse is the Apex of Joseph Smith's teaching, but it is arrangement more than new substance:** Delivered at the April 7, 1844 general conference (two and a half months before Smith's death) ostensibly as a funeral sermon for [[King Follett]], it pulls together ideas already revealed a decade earlier — chiefly [[D&C 93|D&C 93]] and the [[Abraham 4.1|Book of Abraham]] — into one radical package on the potential of human beings.[^1] See [[King Follett Is Arrangement Not New Substance]] and [[King Follett Discourse]].
- **Griffiths sorts doctrines into four tiers, and the two halves of the Lorenzo Snow couplet land in different tiers:** Core eternal, supporting, policy, and esoteric. Human exaltation ("man may become") is *core* and the least controversial idea in the sermon; "God once was a man" is *esoteric* — believed but barely understood — which is why [[Gordon B. Hinckley]] could say "I don't know that we teach it."[^2] See [[Four-Tier Doctrinal Taxonomy]], [[God Was Once a Man Is Esoteric Not Core Doctrine]], and [[Hinckley I Dont Know That We Teach It]].
- **Section 93's claim that intelligence is uncreated and eternally has agency cuts the Gordian knot of the problem of evil:** Because part of every person is uncreated and has always possessed agency, God is not the author of evil (a good decision is learned, not programmed), and ex nihilo creation by contrast would make humans pre-programmed robots whose good and evil are God's doing.[^3] See [[Intelligence Is Uncreated and Eternally Has Agency]], [[Co-Eternality Solves the Problem of Evil]], and [[Ex Nihilo Determinism Negates Moral Praise]].
- **God and man are the same species — acorn and oak — which grounds an anthropomorphic, relational God and the doctrine of eternal families:** A literally embodied, fatherly God who loves us implies a Mother in Heaven and the carrying of our relationships into the next life; this is contrasted with the classical "God without body, parts, or passions" and the "force"-like deity of much of Christendom.[^4] See [[God and Man Are the Same Species]] and [[Anthropomorphic God Grounds Heavenly Mother and Eternal Families]].
- **The sermon's "son of perdition" warning was aimed at specific dissenters, not ordinary members who leave:** Becoming a son of perdition requires seeing God "as a man knows the sun is shining" and then denying it — a bar Griffiths thinks almost no one clears; Smith was warning the [[William Law]] faction (publishers of the *Nauvoo Expositor*) who sought his blood, and "you could make the case these ideas get him killed."[^5] See [[Son of Perdition Requires the Highest Bar of Knowledge]].
- **King Follett need not be canonized because its substance already is, and exaltation is not usurping God:** Its ideas live in [[D&C 93]], [[D&C 76]], [[D&C 130.20-21|D&C 130]], and [[Abraham 4.1|Abraham]]; canonization sometimes circles back to old revelations (D&C 137/138 canonized 1979). Becoming like God is not the serpent's offer to replace God — a good father rejoices when children equal or surpass him, and God's love-based power "only multiplies" when shared.[^6] See [[King Follett Need Not Be Canonized]] and [[Becoming Like God Is Not Usurping God]].
