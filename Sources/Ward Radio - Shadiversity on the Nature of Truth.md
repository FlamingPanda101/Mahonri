# Ward Radio — Shadiversity on the Nature of Truth

A long-form Ward Radio conversation (hosts [[Cardon Ellis]], [[Austin Falter]], [[Jonah Barnes]], with [[Ben Kaiser]] and Brad Whipback) and guest [[Shad Brooks]] (Shadiversity), built around the question of **what truth actually is**. Shad argues — from both "secular logic" and Latter-day Saint scripture ([[D&C 88]], [[D&C 93]], [[D&C 19]], [[D&C 29]]) — that truth is *not* merely correct facts but is defined by **what it causes**: correct facts arranged to deceive are not "true," while a fiction like the Good Samaritan that leads one toward God is. From this he builds a chain — truth → law → morality → action — and applies it to figurative revelation, the problem of evil, prophetic fallibility, and why "their creeds were an abomination."

## Summary

- **Truth is more than facts; it is defined by its effect.** Shad opens with the claim that one can take true facts, omit context, and "mislead someone completely," versus a fiction that "teaches something profoundly important and truthful." Truth therefore has "an essential component in regards to what it causes in other people." His prior-stream formula: *truth is anything that makes you behave more like God; falsehood is anything that makes you behave less like him* [03:02]–[04:31]. See [[Truth Is Defined by What It Causes Not Merely Correct Facts]].
- **The Good Samaritan / Santa Claus / parenting threats.** Whether the Good Samaritan "factually happened" is "completely irrelevant" to its being "a very truthful story." A figurative threat ("I'll ground you for life") or Santa Claus is not a lie, because a lie requires *intent to deceive* plus the *effect* of moving someone away from God [04:31]–[09:31].
- **God speaks figuratively and pedagogically (milk before meat).** God "very often speaks to us in reference to beginnings and endings because that is our frame of reference," even though [[D&C 29|D&C 29:33]] says His works have "no end, neither beginning." [[Alma 29.8|Alma 29:8]] grants each nation truth "as he seeth fit" — the level that will "most optimally help them become like him" [12:00]–[18:00]. See [[God Frames Truth Pedagogically to Change Behavior]].
- **"Is it moral to be correct?" — the stumper.** After establishing objective truth (1+1=2 holds irrespective of perspective), Shad bridges to objective morality with a question he says has "stumped every single" atheist: *is it moral to be correct?* If there is no God there is no morality, so being correct is "unnecessary." But everyone intuits it *is* moral to be correct — rejecting truth ("DON'T EAT THAT — I reject that truth") leads to death [20:30]–[23:32]. See [[Is It Moral to Be Correct - Bridging Objective Truth to Objective Morality]].
- **Truth = light = law = morality (D&C 88).** [[D&C 88]] is read as stating an *equivalence*, not a sequence: "whatsoever is truth is light, and whatsoever is light is spirit." Light/truth "is the law by which all things are governed," even "the power of God." The earth keeping celestial law and being "sanctified," and [[D&C 29|D&C 29:34]] ("all things unto me are spiritual... never... a law which is temporal"), show God views even physics as *moral* commandments. Hence truth *is* morality [23:32]–[46:30]. See [[Truth Is Law Is Morality - The D&C 88 Equivalence Chain]].
- **Action is the greater expression of truth.** "Truth expressed and acted and shown is... orders of magnitude more important... than truth expressed in word." God is "vastly more concerned with how we act" than the facts we know — tied to Matt. 7:21 and the [[Matthew 21.28-32|two-sons parable (Matt. 21)]] (the son who said no but did the will). This grounds *why* "their creeds were an abomination": creedalism inverts the priority, exalting belief over behavior [46:30]–[52:30]. See [[Action Is the Greater Expression of Truth]].
- **Figurative "endless torment" is not a lie ([[D&C 19]]).** [[D&C 19|D&C 19:6-7]] explicitly says "endless torment" is written "that it might work upon the hearts of the children of men" — strong language for effect, not a literal claim of no end. Likewise [[D&C 43|D&C 43:24-25]]'s "how often would I have gathered you" shows God using maximal language to provoke right action [75:01]–[81:30].
- **Developmental accommodation: law of Moses, slavery, tithing, divorce.** God "suffered" lesser/imperfect practices (the law of Moses, slavery in the Bible, tithing as a lesser law than consecration) because people "weren't ready" for a higher law — "you can't jump from zero to 100." Joseph Smith's presidential plan to buy slaves' freedom was a higher law society rejected [62:00]–[68:31]. See [[God Frames Truth Pedagogically to Change Behavior]].
- **Prophetic fallibility as providential.** God calls the leader who will "have the greatest impact in bringing more people unto Christ," sometimes *because* of weakness. Shad's mission story: as a branch president he forgot to bring an elderly member the sacrament, which set off a chain reactivating her inactive daughter — God using his failing "to bring about his own ends." [[Brigham Young]]'s "far out views" still drew people toward Christ [68:31]–[73:31]. See [[Prophets Are Fallible Not Divine]].
- **The capstone: D&C 93 and the origin of existence.** Shad points to [[D&C 93]] ("intelligence... was not created or made, neither indeed can be"; "all truth is independent in that sphere in which God has placed it") and the [[King Follett Discourse]] as where one "unlocks the mystery of where God comes from." He notes [[Jordan Peterson]] vs [[Sam Harris]] reached the same "truth has a moral component" conclusion by logic alone (see [[Metaphorical Truth and Narrative Reading]]) [86:30]–[91:31].
