# The Stick of Joseph — The Documentary Hypothesis (Mike Day)

A live lecture in which [[Mike Day]] presents the [[Documentary Hypothesis Strengthens the Restoration|Documentary Hypothesis]] (JEDP) for a Latter-day Saint audience — surveying the source-critical case that the Pentateuch was woven from multiple traditions, steelmanning both the critical scholars (esp. [[Julius Wellhausen]]) and their opponents, and arguing that the [[Documentary Hypothesis Strengthens the Restoration|Book of Mormon is the linchpin]] that lets a believer sit honestly with the text's "messiness."

## Summary

- **Framing: "I'll show you my cards."** Day affirms a historical [[Moses]] and a historical [[Nephi]], but holds that what survives in the Pentateuch is "echoes of echoes" — edited well before Joseph Smith's day, just as the Doctrine and Covenants was demonstrably edited (he cites Robert Woodford's dissertation on every D&C editorial change). His "get-out-of-jail-free card" is Joseph Smith's statement that he believes the Bible "as it read when it came from the pen of the original writers," before "ignorant translators, careless transcribers, or designing and corrupt priests" introduced errors.[^1][^2]
- **What the DH is.** It addresses inconsistencies in the Pentateuch and the [[Deuteronomistic Corruption of the Old Testament|Deuteronomistic History]] (Deuteronomy, Joshua, Judges, Samuel, Kings). The Old Testament redactors, unlike [[Mormon Is an Ethnocentric Redactor Focused on the Witness of Christ|Mormon]], "don't show you their hand" — they stay in the background but leave "a trail of internal evidence." Day notes [[Philo of Alexandria|Philo]] already saw the Genesis 1 / Genesis 2 split, reading Genesis 1 as a spiritual creation in Platonic terms — "doing what Joseph does" with the JST, but not as a prophet.[^3][^4]
- **Doublets and triplets (30+).** Day has charted ~36: [[Two Independent Creation Accounts in Genesis|two creation accounts]], two accounts of [[Genesis 37|Joseph being sold]], three "she's my sister" wife-sister episodes, and two versions of how Israel acquired Shechem (purchased for 100 pieces of silver vs. taken by Simeon and Levi's slaughter — both sitting side by side in Genesis).[^5][^6]
- **Internal evidence the Pentateuch is not all Mosaic.** [[Internal Anachronisms Show Moses Did Not Write the Whole Pentateuch|Numbers 12:3]] calls Moses the most humble man ever (odd self-praise); [[Deuteronomy 34]] narrates Moses's death; [[Internal Anachronisms Show Moses Did Not Write the Whole Pentateuch|Genesis 12:6]] notes the Canaanites "were then in the land" (written after they were gone); and Deuteronomy 1:1 in the Hebrew says Moses spoke "on the other side of Jordan," locating the writer on the *west* side — where the Pentateuch says Moses never went. Early rabbis already noticed.[^7][^8]
- **Divergent slave laws.** [[Three Divergent Slave Laws Point to Separate Pentateuchal Sources|Three incompatible slave laws]] (Exodus/E, Deuteronomy/D, Leviticus/P) differ on payment at release — evidence of separate traditions reacting to each other, with the Exodus "Covenant Code" treated by scholars as the earliest stratum. Day's counsel to those troubled by it: "Sit in the mess... I can't fix it. I just acknowledge it."[^9][^10]
- **The four sources at a glance.** **J** (Yahwist, Judah/south) uses the divine name from Genesis 2 and is "the Hebrew Homer" — God is anthropomorphic, walking and smelling sacrifices. **E** (Elohist, north) keeps God remote, accessible via dreams and angels (which Day links to Lehi's and Nephi's experiences). **P** (Priestly) is highly ritualistic "lawyer-speech," obsessed with separation (*kodesh* = holy/separated), and uses *El Shaddai* until [[The P and E Sources Say Yahwehs Name Was Unknown Before Moses|Exodus 6]]. **D** (Deuteronomist) is into centralized worship and the Josianic reform.[^11][^12][^13]
- **The divine-name criterion.** [[The P and E Sources Say Yahwehs Name Was Unknown Before Moses|Exodus 6:3 (P) and Exodus 3 (E)]] both have God reveal the name Yahweh to Moses as new — "no one has known my name" — yet in J the name is used throughout Genesis. Scholars take the Genesis usage as the Yahwist.[^14]
- **Two models of how it came together.** After Assyria destroyed the north in 722 BC, literate refugees brought their writings to Jerusalem (whose population archaeologically skyrocketed; Hezekiah's tunnel witnesses the siege survival). J and E — contradictory but each authoritative to its group — were set side by side rather than harmonized, "kind of like the Gospels": Day prefers letting contradictory accounts stand. [[Hezekiah Josiah and the Deuteronomist Reforms|D came with Josiah's reforms]]; P's date is disputed ([[Richard Friedman]] ~700–650 BC; [[Julius Wellhausen]] put P last, in the exile).[^15][^16][^17]
- **The honest opposition (steelmanned).** [[Umberto Cassuto]]: the doublets are just doublets, let them sit. [[Kenneth Kitchen]]: "there is no objective independent evidence for any of these four compositions anywhere outside the pages of our existing Bibles" — show me a Qumran scroll of "the Book of J." [[David Carr]] modifies to "P and non-P"; neo-documentarians push back further. Day calls Kitchen's demand "a sound argument."[^18][^19]
- **Wellhausen's bias.** Responding to an audience member, Day grants [[Julius Wellhausen]] was a Lutheran with an anti-Jewish bent whose insistence that P (ritual law) was the *latest, degenerate* stage reflected that prejudice — and that Wellhausen died an atheist. Day's reply to "how do you keep your faith?": the *how* of source criticism can be sound even where the *why* is corrupted by the scholar's motives.[^20][^21]
- **Friedman's seven arguments.** Day runs through a scholar's case (linguistic change — J/E use older Hebrew than D/P, so single authorship is "like saying a valley girl wrote Hamlet"); distinctive terminology (Sinai in J/P vs. Horeb in E/D; Jethro vs. Reuel); different theology; color-coded narrative seams in the [[Genesis 37|Joseph]] and flood stories; and historical fit (E with northern concerns, P with priestly centralization).[^22][^23][^24]
- **The Aaronid–Levite power struggle.** P restricts temple access to the [[Aaron|sons of Aaron]] on pain of death; yet [[Exodus 32]] makes [[Aaron]] the golden-calf villain and has the Levites ordained by the sword — a textual conflict over "who's in charge." Day links the LDS Aaronic/Levitical priesthood restoration to this ancient tension.[^25][^26]
- **Why it matters — and the linchpin.** Understanding the Bible's composite nature explains why Bible-bashing is futile (he quotes Joseph Smith–History: "it was impossible... to come to any certain conclusion... who was right and who was wrong"). His resolution: [[Documentary Hypothesis Strengthens the Restoration|the Book of Mormon is the lens]] — a 23-year-old with no access to Spinoza, Philo, or Wellhausen produced a text that names sources, depicts editors, and systematically *reverses* Deuteronomy's claims (God can be seen, altars outside Jerusalem are valid, the mysteries can be known) in the first chapters of 1 Nephi. He rejects 1979 Chicago biblical inerrancy because "the Bible doesn't say that it's inerrant," and points to the Book of Mormon title page conceding its own mistakes. "In my opinion, the documentary hypothesis does not hurt the cause for restoration, it strengthens it."[^27][^28][^29]
