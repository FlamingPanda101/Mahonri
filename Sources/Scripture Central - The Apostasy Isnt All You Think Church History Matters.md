# Scripture Central — The Apostasy Isn't All You Think (Church History Matters)

Covers [[D&C 85]] and [[D&C 86]] (with a forthcoming part on D&C 87).

- **The "one mighty and strong" ([[D&C 85]]) was Edward Partridge, not a future reformer:** Per the 1905 First Presidency statement, it is a *conditional* warning to a bishop who had neglected his stewardship; because [[Edward Partridge]] repented, the prophecy was rendered moot — yet break-off movements keep prooftexting it. See [[The One Mighty and Strong Was Edward Partridge Not a Future Reformer]] and [[Steadying the Ark Names Overreach Beyond Ones Stewardship]].
- **The apostasy was the church "driven into the wilderness," not annihilated ([[D&C 86]], [[Revelation 12]]):** Christ himself testifies to the apostasy in the Parable of the Wheat and Tares; the Restoration is the church coming *out* of the wilderness (the very imagery in the Nauvoo Temple stonework). See [[The Apostasy Was the Church Driven Into the Wilderness Not Annihilated]].
- **The priesthood lineage continued hidden through the apostasy:** D&C 86:8-11 says the right to the priesthood "remained" through the covenant faithful — God's work "never paused." See [[The Priesthood Lineage Continued Hidden Through the Apostasy]].
- **"Let the wheat and tares grow together" is an LDS reply to the problem of evil:** God permits the wicked to mature alongside the righteous because removing the tares too early might damage the wheat — opposition is necessary for growth. See [[The Wheat and Tares Grow Together as a Reply to the Problem of Evil]].
