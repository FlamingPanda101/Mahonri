# Ward Radio — LOOK It's the Lehites I Mean the Lemba Tribe

Host [[Cardon Ellis]], [[Jonah Barnes]], and guest [[Dave Butler]] examine the **Lemba** people of
southern Africa — a Semitic-descended group with an oral tradition of a 6th-century-BC sea voyage from
Arabia, confirmed by genetics — as a **real-world plausibility parallel** to Lehi's transoceanic
migration (while explicitly *not* claiming the Lemba are Nephites).

## Summary
- The Lemba (~70,000; Zimbabwe, Mozambique, northern South Africa) were documented by ethnographer
  [[Henri Junod]] in 1908 as distinct from neighbors: blacksmiths/metallurgists, ritual slaughter,
  draining blood, refusing pork, male circumcision (*lhoma*, performed even for willing neighbors), no
  intermarriage, no monarchs, ancestor veneration.[^1]
- Their **oral tradition** (Junod, 1908): they came "from the other side of the sea," a storm broke
  their boat in two, half reached shore and half were lost — and they came from a place called **Senna**,
  naming their first African city Senna after it.[^2]
- **Genetics:** studies found Semitic Y-chromosome markers ("evidence from p12f2 and other Y-chromosome
  markers"), and they are now called "the black Jews of southern Africa"; their haplogroup maps toward
  the Arabian Peninsula. Henry Abramson's video summarizes the same material.[^3]
- **Geography:** an ancient Arabic map places **Senna/Sena in Yemen**, on the **frankincense trail**,
  near Oman — the same region as proposed **Bountiful** (Salalah/Khor Kharfot) and **Nahom** — so a
  group sailing from Senna departs the very coast Book of Mormon geographers identify with Lehi.[^4]
- **The kicker:** *non-LDS* anthropologists, using the Lemba's own generational reckoning, estimate they
  left Yemen "around the 6th century BC" — independently landing on Lehi's era. The hosts stress this is
  *convergence/plausibility*, defeating the "people couldn't cross oceans" and "origin claims must be
  fake" objections — not proof. See [[The Lemba Tribe's Semitic DNA and Oral Migration Tradition Parallel
  Lehi]].[^5]
- A running aside compares the Falasha/Beta Israel Jews of Ethiopia (Operation Moses, 1980s) as another
  group of African Jews, and riffs that "this is the way" (2 Nephi 31:21) is "in the Book of Mormon" —
  tying metallurgy and the brass-plates/temple-forge motif to Lehi's milieu.
