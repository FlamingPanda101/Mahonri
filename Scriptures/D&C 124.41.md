# D&C 124:40-41 — Ordinances hid from before the foundation of the world

> And again, verily I say unto you, how shall your washings be acceptable unto me, except ye perform them in a house which you have built to my name? ... For I deign to reveal unto my church **things which have been kept hid from before the foundation of the world, things that pertain to the dispensation of the fulness of times**. *(D&C 124:40-41)*

## Key Detail
This Nauvoo revelation promises that the temple ordinances are "things which have been kept hid from before the foundation of the world," now to be revealed in the dispensation of the fulness of times. In the [[Ward Radio - The Top Ten Lost LDS Writings of the Apocrypha|Ward Radio apocrypha episode]] a live caller (Brandon Cole) raises this verse as a tension: if the endowment was "kept hid," how can apocryphal texts show endowment-like ordinances existing anciently? The hosts answer by distinguishing the **endowment of power / specific covenants** (which can be uniquely restored) from the **endowment's narrative and patterns** (creation, fall, garments, tokens), which they argue are scattered across the apocrypha as "vestiges" of a faith lost by the third or fourth century. See [[The Secret Gospel of Mark Implies a Hidden Temple Gospel]] and [[The Two-Tier Canon Explains Patristic Citation of the Apocrypha]].
