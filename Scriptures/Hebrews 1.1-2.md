# Hebrews 1:1-2

> God, who at sundry times and in divers manners spake in time past unto the fathers by the prophets, Hath in these last days spoken unto us by his Son, whom he hath appointed heir of all things, by whom also he made the worlds; *(KJV)*

## Used against the Church
"God formerly spoke by prophets but *now* by his Son — so Jesus was the last prophet; modern prophets are illegitimate."

## In context
The verse contrasts *modes* of revelation, not abolishes prophets. The New Testament itself records **prophets after Christ** — [[Acts 11.27|Acts 11:27]] and [[Acts 21.10|Acts 21:10]] (Agabus). Jesus warned of *false* prophets to come, which presupposes true ones; and [[Amos 3.7|Amos 3:7]] says God does nothing without revealing it to his prophets. See [[Hebrews 1-1-2 - Jesus the Last Prophet Rebutted]].
