# Malachi 4:5-6

> Behold, I will send you Elijah the prophet before the coming of the great and
> dreadful day of the LORD: And he shall turn the heart of the fathers to the
> children, and the heart of the children to their fathers, lest I come and smite
> the earth with a curse.

## Context

The closing verses of the Old Testament. The prophecy of a forerunner — Elijah —
sent before the "great and dreadful day of the LORD." In the New Testament this
is partly applied to John the Baptist ([[Matthew 17.10-13|Matthew 17:11-13]];
cf. Luke 1:17, "in the spirit and power of Elias"), yet [[Matthew 17.10-13|Matthew 17:11]] still uses the future tense ("Elias truly shall come first and restore all
things"), leaving a future forerunner open.

## Key Exegetical Points

- **Quoted by [[Angel Moroni]] to [[Joseph Smith]].** At Moroni's first appearance
  (21 September 1823) he cited this passage — with a variant wording — as still
  future, framing the Restoration's work of turning hearts (temple/genealogy)
  and the literal return of Elijah.[^1]
- **Fulfilled in [[D&C 110]].** The Latter-day Saint reading takes Elijah's return
  literally: he appears in the Kirtland Temple on 3 April 1836 (Passover) to commit
  the sealing keys, echoing "the heart of the fathers to the children."[^1]
- **Forerunner-before-the-Second-Coming.** Early Fathers (Hippolytus, Justin Martyr,
  and others) read this Elijah as a precursor of Christ's *second* advent — a belief
  largely lost in later mainstream dogma. See
  [[The Coming of Elias Before the Second Coming Was an Early Christian Doctrine]].

## Cross-Tradition Readings

- **LDS:** Anchors the doctrine of Elijah's literal latter-day return and the
  sealing/temple work that turns hearts between generations.
- **Catholic/mainstream:** Generally read as fulfilled in John the Baptist; no
  future forerunner-prophet before the Second Coming is expected.
