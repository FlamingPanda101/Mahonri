# Exodus 23:20-21

> Behold, I send an Angel before thee, to keep thee in the way, and to bring thee into the place which I have prepared. Beware of him, and obey his voice, provoke him not; for he will not pardon your transgressions: for my name is in him. *(KJV)*

## Key Hebrew term
- **מַלְאָךְ (malʾāk)** — "messenger / angel," the agent God sends ahead of Israel.
- **שְׁמִי בְּקִרְבּוֹ (shemi b'qirbo)** — "my name is in him." In ancient Near Eastern thought a deity's *name* is a communicable vehicle of that deity's presence, agency, and authority. Because the divine name indwells the messenger, the messenger can do what only God does — here, hold authority over the **forgiveness of transgressions**.

## Significance
Dan McClellan cites this verse as the Hebrew Bible's clearest expression of **name-bearing divine agency**: an agent who speaks and acts *as* God and exercises a uniquely divine prerogative (pardon) while remaining a distinct figure from God. This is the "intuitive logic of divine images" he argues better explains Jesus' relationship to God than the later philosophical Trinity.
