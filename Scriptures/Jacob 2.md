# Jacob 2 — "that I might rid my garments of your sins"

> Yea, it grieveth my soul and causeth me to shrink with shame before the presence of my Maker, that I must testify unto you concerning the wickedness of your hearts... I this day am weighed down with much more desire and anxiety for the welfare of your souls than I have hitherto been... and ye yourselves know that I have hitherto been diligent in the office of my calling; but I this day am weighed down with much more desire... that I might rid my garments of your sins, I have come up into the temple this day that I might declare unto you the word of God. *(Jacob 2:2-3, abridged, Book of Mormon)*

## Key Detail
- Jacob "comes up into the temple" to "rid [his] garments of [the people's] sins" — the second of the two Jacob texts that define **magnifying an office** by blood-/sin-accountability for a people. [[Todd McLauchlin]] uses it with [[Jacob 1.19|Jacob 1:19]] as the controlling proof-text for reading "magnify their calling" ([[D&C 84.33-44|D&C 84:33]]) as [[Magnifying a Calling Means Coming Clean From the Blood and Sins of This Generation|coming clean from the blood and sins of this generation]] through sacrifice that softens hearts.

## The Marriage Rule (Jacob 2:27-30)
> Wherefore, my brethren, hear me, and hearken to the word of the Lord: For there shall not any man among you have save it be one wife; and concubines he shall have none... For if I will, saith the Lord of Hosts, raise up seed unto me, I will command my people; otherwise they shall hearken unto these things. *(Jacob 2:27, 30)*

- The controlling Book of Mormon text on plural marriage: **monogamy is the standing norm** ("one wife"), and **plural marriage is the authorized exception** God may command for a specific purpose ("raise up seed unto me"). [[Jacob Hansen]] uses it to argue the *doctrine* of polygamy never changed — only the practice and the disputed exaltation-requires-polygamy idea did. See [[Polygamy Doctrine Did Not Change Only the Exaltation-Requires-Polygamy Idea Did]].
