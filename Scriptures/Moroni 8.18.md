# Moroni 8:18 — God is "unchangeable from all eternity to all eternity"

> "For I know that God is not a partial God, neither a changeable being; but he is unchangeable from all eternity to all eternity." (Moroni 8:18)

## Key Detail

Part of Mormon's epistle to his son Moroni condemning infant baptism, this verse is a flat Book of Mormon affirmation of God's **immutability** and **impartiality**. Latter-day Saint apologists cite it as the *canonical* statement of God's unchanging nature — to be preferred over the non-canonical [[King Follett Discourse]] when critics allege that LDS theology makes God a changing being with "passive potency."

In [[All Those In Favor - Joseph Smith vs Catholic on Creation]], [[Kaden Alvie]] reaches for Moroni 8:18 as the "true-blue Book of Mormon" answer to [[Noah Biser]]'s charge that Mormons "openly teach that God is undergoing change." The hosts argue the LDS *doctrine* (taught frequently and by many; see [[King Follett Need Not Be Canonized]]) is that the Father has a glorified body — not that God is mutable — and Moroni 8:18 anchors that immutability in scripture.

**Tension to be honest about:** Read alongside the King Follett Discourse's "God himself was once as we are," Moroni 8:18 creates an internal LDS tension between divine immutability and divine progression. The video's resolution is to subordinate the non-canonical sermon to the canonical verse, and to read "God was once a man" (if granted at all) in the same sense as Christ's incarnation rather than as ongoing change in the Father's divine nature.
