# John 8:56

> Your father Abraham rejoiced to see my day: and he saw it, and was glad. *(KJV)*

## Significance
Jesus says [[Abraham]] "saw my day" — a vision of the Messiah that has no clear basis in canonical Genesis, which several commentators simply note as a "plain statement of fact" and move past. In the Ward Radio "Biggest Cover Up" episode, [[Jonah Barnes]] argues this is exactly what JST Genesis 15 restores: Abraham was shown the days of the Son of Man and the resurrection, so "Abraham rejoiced to see my day" because he learned his son [[Isaac]] could be raised — reconciling John 8:56 with the messianic Akedah. Compare the LDS messianic reading in [[Jacob 4.4-5|Jacob 4]].
