# Exodus

The second book of the Torah/Pentateuch, recounting Israel's liberation from slavery in Egypt, the giving of the Law at Sinai, and the construction of the Tabernacle. [[Jacob Wright]] identifies Exodus as the centerpiece of the "people's history" strand in the Bible — a northern Israelite tradition of liberation and peoplehood that the Judean editors "bracketed" ahead of the Davidic "palace history," making it possible to imagine an enduring identity that does not depend on monarchy.
