# Psalm 42:2

> My soul thirsteth for God, for the living God: when shall I come and appear before God? *(KJV)*

> My soul thirsts for God, for the living God. When shall I come and behold the face of God? *(NRSVUE)*

## Key Hebrew Terms

- **רָאָה / פְּנֵי אֱלֹהִים (ra'ah / pene Elohim):** "see / the face of God." As with the pilgrimage commandment, the verb is vocalized passive ("appear before") in the Masoretic tradition, but the underlying idiom is "behold the face of God" — preserved in the NRSVUE rendering. See [[Masoretic Vowels Reversed the Active Verb to Passive]].

## Context

A psalmist's longing to return to the temple and the divine presence. [[Dan McClellan]] cites this as evidence that "seeing the face of God" was an object of personal yearning, not merely a legal formula — a beatific vision experienced through the temple's divine image (the standing stone). He notes the yearning is for the divine *presence*, of which the stone is the material medium. See [[Beatific Vision in the Israelite Temple]].
