# 2 Corinthians 5:21

> For he hath made him to be sin for us, who knew no sin; that we might be made the righteousness of God in him. *(KJV)*

## Significance
[[John MacArthur]] called this "the greatest gospel verse in the Bible," glossing it as Reformed **double imputation**: "God punished Jesus for my sin, turns right around and treats me as if I lived his life... when he looks at the cross, he sees you; when he looks at you, he sees Christ." [[Trent Horn]] replies that the verse never calls this the gospel (even though *euangelion* appears nine times in 2 Corinthians) and that the verse is **exegetically underdetermined**: "he made him to be sin" could mean

- Christ became a **sacrificial sin offering** (the Hebrew *ḥaṭṭāʾṯ* means both *sin* and *sin offering*), or
- a reference to the **incarnation** — Christ coming "in the form of sinful flesh," as [[Romans 8.3|Romans 8:3]] says.

Neither reading entails double imputation, so the doctrine cannot be a self-evident gospel essential. See [[Double Imputation Is Not the Gospel]].

## Cross-references
- [[Romans 8.3|Romans 8:3]] — "in the likeness of sinful flesh," an alternative reading of "made... to be sin."
- [[John 3.16|John 3:16]] — which Horn rates a better one-verse gospel summary.
