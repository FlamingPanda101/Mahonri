# Deuteronomy 10:18-19 (KJV)

> "He doth execute the judgment of the fatherless and widow, and loveth the stranger, in giving him food and raiment. Love ye therefore the stranger: for ye were strangers in the land of Egypt."

**Note:** An *imitatio Dei* ("imitation of God") text in narrative-ethical form: *because* God loves and provides for the widow, orphan, and stranger, Israel must love the stranger too. The same "God does X, therefore you do X" logic Jesus uses in [[Matthew 5.43-45|Matthew 5:43-45]] (God sends rain on just and unjust, *therefore* love your enemies). Note the connective **"therefore"** — the same hinge Trevan Hatch flags in [[Matthew 5.48|Matthew 5:48]]. (Cf. [[Deuteronomy 10.17|Deuteronomy 10:17]], [[Deuteronomy 10.19|Deuteronomy 10:19]].)

## Used in
- [[Be Perfect Defined Elsewhere as Caring for the Poor]] — Hatch cites Deuteronomy's expansion of the Levitical holiness theme into love of the stranger as the prophetic-Torah backdrop to Jesus's love command.
