# Mark 10:14

> But when Jesus saw it, he was much displeased, and said unto them, Suffer the little children to come unto me, and forbid them not: for of such is the kingdom of God. *(KJV)*

## Key phrase
"**Suffer the little children to come unto me, and forbid them not: for of such is the kingdom of God**" — Jesus rebukes the disciples for keeping children from him and identifies the kingdom with them.

## Apologetic application
A motivating text in the patristic infant-baptism debate. [[Tertullian]] (c. AD 206), even while opposing infant baptism, implies that Mark 10:14 was already motivating Christians to baptize their infants — evidence the practice predates its formal rationale. [[Trent Horn]] uses this in defending pedobaptism as an early discipline. See [[Infant Baptism Preceded Its Theological Rationale]] and [[Augustine Clarified Infant Baptism He Did Not Invent It]].
