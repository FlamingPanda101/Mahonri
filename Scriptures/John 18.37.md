# John 18:37

> Pilate therefore said unto him, Art thou a king then? Jesus answered, Thou sayest that I am a king. To this end was I born, and for this cause came I into the world, that I should bear witness unto the truth. Every one that is of the truth heareth my voice. *(KJV)*

## Key Greek phrase
**πᾶς ὁ ὢν ἐκ τῆς ἀληθείας ἀκούει μου τῆς φωνῆς (pas ho ōn ek tēs alētheias akouei mou tēs phōnēs)** — "everyone who is of the truth hears my voice."

## In context
[[Anthony Rogers]] cites this repeatedly as evidence that Christ's word (and by extension Scripture) is **self-authenticating**: it imposes itself on "those who are of the truth," while those who reject it thereby show they are "not of the truth." Paired with [[John 8.58|John 8]] ("he who is of God hears the words of God"). Used to argue that interpretive failure reflects the hearer's spiritual state, not any defect in the word.
