# 1 Peter 3:15

> But sanctify the Lord God in your hearts: and be ready always to give an answer to every man that asketh you a reason of the hope that is in you with meekness and fear. *(KJV)*

## Key Greek term
The word translated "answer / defense" is **ἀπολογία (apologia)** — a legal term for a reasoned defense made before a tribunal. It is the root of the English word **apologetics** (which has nothing to do with saying "sorry"). The verse pairs the *reason* ("the force of fact") with **πραΰτητος (prautētos)** "meekness" and **φόβου (phobou)** "fear/reverence" — i.e., a defense given with humility.

## Significance
The foundational proof-text for Christian apologetics across traditions. [[Hayden Carroll]] opens his apologetics course with it and plays a clip of Elder Holland appealing to the same verse — "the force of fact as well as the force of feeling" — to authorize evidence-based apologetics for Latter-day Saints. The evangelical ministry **Apologia Church** takes its name from this verse, as Carroll notes.
