# Abraham 3:25

> And we will prove them herewith, to see if they will do all things whatsoever
> the Lord their God shall command them.

## Context

Abraham 3 records the vision given to Abraham through the Urim and Thummim,
revealing the pre-mortal council in heaven. In verses 22-28, the Lord shows
Abraham the intelligences that were organized before the world was, including
the "noble and great ones" (v. 22). Christ is chosen as the Redeemer, and the
plan of salvation is presented: spirits will be sent to earth for a mortal
probation.

## Key Exegetical Points

- **"We will prove them herewith":** Mortality is a test — a probationary
  state. This directly parallels simulation theory's concept of a constructed
  reality designed to test or observe subjects. [[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)|David Butler]] draws the connection: "We actually believe in the
  simulation. There was a time given to men of probation."[^1]
- **"To see if they will do":** The test is genuinely open — preserving the
  reality of agency and choice. The outcome is not predetermined, distinguishing
  LDS theology from deterministic or Calvinist frameworks.

## Cross-Tradition Readings

- **LDS:** A canonical account of the pre-mortal council and the purpose of
  mortal life as a probation. This is one of the distinctive LDS scriptures (in
  the Pearl of Great Price).
- **Non-LDS Christian:** Not recognized as scripture; the Book of Abraham is
  unique to the LDS canon.
