# Revelation 14:13

> And I heard a voice from heaven saying unto me, Write, Blessed are the dead which die in the Lord from henceforth: Yea, saith the Spirit, that they may rest from their labours; and their works do follow them. *(KJV)*

## Significance
The epigraph chosen by D. Jill Kirby for "Living in the Afterlife." It frames the chapter's central conviction that the Christian dead enter a state of blessed **rest** and that "their works do follow them" — i.e. mortal deeds bear on the afterlife. The verse pairs the hope of postmortem reward with the early-Christian insistence that one's eternal state flows from one's earthly orientation (compare [[Alma 34.32-33]]).
