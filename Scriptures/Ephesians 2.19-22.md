#type/scripture

## Text
"Now, therefore, you are no longer strangers and foreigners, but fellow citizens with the saints and members of the household of God ... [growing] into a holy temple in the Lord, in whom you also are being built together for a dwelling place of God." (Ephesians 2:19–22, NKJV)

## Use in Source
This passage serves as the epigraph for Matthew Grey's chapter on sacred spaces. It fuses the two key community metaphors Grey traces: the church as the new "family / household of God" (see [[The Church as the New Family and Household of God]]) and the church as the new "temple" — the dwelling place of God (see [[The Church as the New Temple Replacing Jerusalem]]). Grey cites it among the NT texts presenting the Christian community as the new spiritualized temple housing the divine presence on earth.

## Notes
The "dwelling place of God" / "holy temple" imagery undergirds the entire "templization" trajectory by which Christian worship space gradually took on temple-holiness, culminating in the monumental basilicas (see [[Templization of Christian Worship Space]]).
