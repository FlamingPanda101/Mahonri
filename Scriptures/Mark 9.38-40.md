# Mark 9:38-40

> And John answered him, saying, Master, we saw one casting out devils in thy name, and he followeth not us: and we forbad him, because he followeth not us. But Jesus said, Forbid him not: for there is no man which shall do a miracle in my name, that can lightly speak evil of me. For he that is not against us is on our part. *(KJV)*

## Context: The unknown exorcist
[[Jacob Hansen]] appeals to this episode to argue Jesus blesses faithful action in his name even from those with imperfect understanding ("if you're not against us you're for us"). [[Gavin Ortlund]] replies precisely: the text says nothing about the exorcist's *theology* — only that the disciples did not know him ("mere relational aloofness"); it therefore cannot establish "right action despite wrong doctrine."
