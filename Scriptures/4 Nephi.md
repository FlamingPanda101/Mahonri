# 4 Nephi

> And it came to pass that thirty and eight years had passed away, and also thirty and nine, and forty and one, and forty and two, yea, even until forty and nine years had passed away, and also fifty and one, and fifty and two; yea, and even until fifty and nine years had passed away. *(4 Nephi 1:6, paraphrased in the episode)*

## Key Detail
The single, short chapter covering ~300 years of post-resurrection Nephite peace and gradual decline. It is notable here for its **non-sequential year-list**, which reports certain years and skips others. [[Jerry Grover]] graphs these listed years and argues the *intervals* between them (7, 9, 10, 13, 19, 20, 30) are Mesoamerican/Hebrew "sacred numbers," and that each listed year can be reached by summing sacred numbers — evidence, he says, that Mormon structured the abridgment like a Maya religious codex. See [[Mesoamerican Sacred-Number Intervals Structure the Book of Mormon Year-Counts]].
