# 2 Samuel 8:18

> "And Benaiah the son of Jehoiada was over both the Cherethites and the Pelethites; and David's sons were **chief rulers**." (KJV)

The KJV's "chief rulers" renders the Hebrew *kohanim* — literally **priests**. Read straight, the verse says [[David]]'s sons were priests, making David a king who also dispenses priestly office. In the surrounding narrative David wears the **ephod**, leads the procession of the ark, and acts in liturgical roles — so the "priest" reading fits a David the text already portrays as cultically active. [[Mike Day]] uses this verse as a showcase example of how the KJV's smoothing conceals a startling datum that surfaces only in the Hebrew, and links it to the Book of Mormon's king-and-priest [[1 Nephi 1.1|Nephi]] ("his reign and ministry"). See [[David's Sons Were Priests Not Chief Rulers - 2 Samuel 8.18 Kohanim]].

[[Julius Wellhausen]] (*Prolegomena*, ch. 4, "Priests and Levites") reads the verse as evidence that David "ruled as a king and a priest," an office later narrowed to the Zadokites and the [[Aaron|sons of Aaron]]. The Chronicler's parallel (1 Chronicles 18:17) already rephrases the role as "chief about the king," showing an ancient interpretive move toward the administrative sense the KJV follows.
