# John 10:17-18

> Therefore doth my Father love me, because I lay down my life, that I might take it again. No man taketh it from me, but I lay it down of myself. I have power to lay it down, and I have power to take it again. This commandment have I received of my Father. *(KJV)*

## Key Detail
Jesus claims **voluntary authority** over his own death and resurrection — "no man taketh it from me, but I lay it down of myself." [[Avery (God Logic)|Avery]] uses this to defuse a Muslim caller's "unfairness" objection to substitutionary atonement: Christ is "not some helpless child" forced to the cross but the divine Son who lays down his life by his own *exousia* (authority/power) and takes it up again. The text thus supports both the **deity of Christ** (he raises *himself*) and the *self-giving* character of the atonement. See [[Substitutionary Atonement Is Not a License to Sin]].
