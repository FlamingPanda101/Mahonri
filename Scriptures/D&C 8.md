# D&C 8

An early revelation given through [[Joseph Smith]] to [[Oliver Cowdery]] at
Harmony, Pennsylvania, in April 1829, part of the set of sections 6–9 received as
Cowdery began scribal work and inquired about translating.[^1]

## The Rod / Gift of Aaron Passage

The source is uncertain whether the "gift of working with the sprout/rod"
language falls in section 6 or section 8, treating both as candidates.[^2] In
either case the early text describes [[Oliver Cowdery]] receiving information
through a rod, later editorially reframed as the "gift of Aaron" (1835),
connecting it to Old Testament rod imagery ([[Numbers 17|Numbers 17]]).[^3]
