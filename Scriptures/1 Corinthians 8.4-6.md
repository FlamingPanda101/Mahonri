# 1 Corinthians 8:4-6

> "As concerning therefore the eating of those things that are offered in sacrifice unto idols, we know that an idol is nothing in the world, and that there is none other God but one. For though there be that are called gods, whether in heaven or in earth, (as there be gods many, and lords many,) But to us there is but one God, the Father, of whom are all things, and we in him; and one Lord Jesus Christ, by whom are all things, and we by him." (KJV)

Paul says "an idol is nothing" (idol polemic) yet concedes "there be gods many, and lords many," then asserts exclusivity *for us*: "to us there is but one God, the Father." The phrase "of whom are all things" (*ex hou ta panta*) is read by some as strict monotheism; in McClellan's reading it parallels [[Nehemiah 9.6|Nehemiah 9:6]] — a creator deity over created-yet-real divine beings — i.e., exclusive-relationship language like the Shema, not a denial that other gods exist.
