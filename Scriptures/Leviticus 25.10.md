# Leviticus 25:10 — The Year of Jubilee

> And ye shall hallow the fiftieth year, and proclaim liberty throughout all the land unto all the inhabitants thereof: it shall be a jubile unto you; and ye shall return every man unto his possession, and ye shall return every man unto his family. *(KJV)*

## Key Hebrew term
- **דְּרוֹר (deror)** — "liberty / release," proclaimed in the Jubilee year; debts are cancelled and Israelites return to ancestral land. (Hebrew bondservants are released; foreign chattel slaves of [[Leviticus 25.44-46|Lev 25:44-46]] are not.)

## Significance
[[Glenn Scrivener]] points to the Jubilee as the Old Testament's "extraordinary year" of debt cancellation and land return — "the amazing social justice that would happen if we had a year of Jubilee in 2025" — and reads it as fulfilled in Christ, who "comes as Jubilee" for the nations. He cites the Jubilee Centre think-tank (40 years on debt relief). It is part of his argument that the OT prioritizes the poor, the widow, and the sojourner, and points beyond Israel-specific release toward universal liberation. See [[The Exodus Is Israelite-Specific Not Universal Abolition]] and [[Christianity Abolished Slavery Twice]].
