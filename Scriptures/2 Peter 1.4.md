# 2 Peter 1:4

> Whereby are given unto us exceeding great and precious promises: that by these ye might be partakers of the divine nature, having escaped the corruption that is in the world through lust. *(KJV)*

## Key Term: "partakers of the divine nature"
Greek *theias koinonoi physeos*. [[Blake Ostler]] cites this as scriptural warrant for human theosis: when humans enter "the same relationship that they are" — the loving, mutually-indwelling relationship of the Father, Son, and Holy Ghost — they "fully partake of the divine nature" and possess the full divinity the divine persons have, while remaining eternally grateful to the Father who issued the invitation. Grounds the LDS claim that humans are the same *kind* as God. See [[Divinity Requires Loving Relationship]] and [[Worship of the Son Is Warranted Through Indwelling]].
