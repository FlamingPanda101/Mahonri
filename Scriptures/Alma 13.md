# Alma 13 — high priests "after the order of the Son of God"

> And this is the manner after which they were ordained—being called and prepared from the foundation of the world according to the foreknowledge of God, on account of their exceeding faith and good works... called with a holy calling... after the order of his Son, in the manner which I have spoken; and this that they might teach his commandments unto the children of men, that they also might enter into his rest. *(Alma 13:3-6, abridged, Book of Mormon)*

## Key Detail
- Alma's discourse on the **Melchizedek high priesthood**, the Book of Mormon's fullest statement that the office is "after the order of the Son of God" — favoring the word **"order"** over "priesthood." [[Todd McLauchlin]] cites it alongside [[D&C 107|D&C 107:3]] to argue [[Priesthood Is an Order Patterned on the Atonement Not a Power|priesthood is an order/pattern, not a raw power]]; the chapter ties foreordination, faith, and entering God's rest.
