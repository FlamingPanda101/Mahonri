# Genesis 31:13

> I am the God of Bethel, where thou anointedst the pillar, and where thou vowedst a vow unto me: now arise, get thee out from this land, and return unto the land of thy kindred. *(KJV)*

## Key Hebrew term
- **מַלְאַךְ הָאֱלֹהִים / מַלְאַךְ יְהוָה (malʾak ha-elohim / malʾak YHWH)** — "the messenger/angel of God." In the surrounding narrative (Gen 31:11) it is *the messenger* who speaks to Jacob, yet the messenger says in the first person, "I am the God of Bethel" (**אָנֹכִי הָאֵל בֵּית־אֵל**, *anoki ha-El Bethel*). The agent identifies *as* the deity.

## Significance
[[Dan McClellan]] cites this as one of two places in the Hebrew Bible where the messenger of Yahweh **explicitly** identifies as God ("I am the God of Bethel"). He argues this first-person identification is best explained by the intuitive logic of divine images — a sentient walking divine image bearing the deity's presence — rather than by the later LDS doctrine of divine investiture of authority (which he says no contemporaneous source supports) or by Trinitarianism/Binitarianism (which he says is anachronistic).
