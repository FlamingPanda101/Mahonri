# Helaman 8 — Nephi called "a good man"

> ...there are laws and judgments of God ... and ye ought not to destroy him. Now ... there were some among them who ... said: Let this man alone, for he is a good man... *(Helaman 8, Book of Mormon, paraphrased context vv. 7-9)*

## Significance
When corrupt judges of the Gadianton persuasion seek to seize and do away with Nephi (son of Helaman), some defend him: "he is a **good** man." This narratively **fulfills** Helaman's blessing in [[Helaman 5.6-7|Helaman 5:6-7]] that it would "be said and written" of his sons "that they were good" — and confirms that the narrators understood **Nephi** (Egyptian *nfr*) to mean "good." Mormon highlights the prophecy-and-fulfillment, illustrating his editorial artistry.
