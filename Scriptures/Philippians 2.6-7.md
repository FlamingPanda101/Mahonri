# Philippians 2:6-7

> Who, being in the form of God, thought it not robbery to be equal with God: But made himself of no reputation, and took upon him the form of a servant, and was made in the likeness of men. *(KJV)*

## Significance
A foundational Christological text that, [[Jason R. Combs]] notes, generated the central questions of early Christology: "Was Jesus fully divine because he was 'equal with God,' or was he only like God being 'in the form of God'? Was Jesus fully human, or was he only 'made in the likeness of men'?" The hymn's affirmation that Jesus was "somehow both human and divine" set up the spectrum of answers — from [[Adoptionism and Docetism - The Two Christological Extremes|adoptionism and docetism]] to the eventual two-natures definition of Chalcedon. Sung by Christians as early as c. AD 54–64.
