# Ether 6

> And it came to pass that they were driven forth; and no monster of the sea could break them... and thus they were driven forth, three hundred and forty and four days upon the water. (Ether 6:10–11)

The Jaredite ocean crossing: the barges are driven before "a furious wind" toward the promised land, buried in the deep and surfacing again, lit by the shining stones, arriving after **344 days** upon the water.

## Relevance
On Ward Radio, [[Jerry Grover]] uses the 344-day figure as the quantitative anchor of his **Atlantic-crossing** model: average current speeds from the Canary Islands along the North-Equatorial current to Mesoamerica yield roughly that duration, which he contrasts with John Sorenson's earlier ~350-day Pacific estimate. The "driven before the wind" / "buried in the deep" language also grounds his reading of the vessels as sealed, deep-hulled barges (not submarines) capable of riding a storm, and the shining stones as their light source (cf. [[Ether 2.23-25]], [[Ether 3.14|Ether 3]]).
