# Genesis 1:20-23

> "And God said, Let the waters bring forth abundantly the moving creature that hath life, and fowl that may fly above the earth in the open firmament of heaven. And God created great whales, and every living creature that moveth, which the waters brought forth abundantly, after their kind, and every winged fowl after his kind: and God saw that it was good. And God blessed them, saying, Be fruitful, and multiply, and fill the waters in the seas, and let fowl multiply in the earth. And the evening and the morning were the fifth day." (KJV)

**Hebrew (key term):** עוֹף ... עַל־הָאָרֶץ עַל־פְּנֵי רְקִיעַ הַשָּׁמָיִם (*of... al-ha-aretz al-pene reqia ha-shamayim*, "birds... above the earth across the face of the firmament of the heavens"). The verb **בָּרָא** (*bara*, "created") is the characteristic Priestly creation verb.

## Context

Day five of the Priestly creation account: God creates sea creatures and **birds**. Crucially, the birds are made *before* humans, who are created on day six (Genesis 1:26–27). Dan McClellan uses this to expose the contradiction with Genesis 2:19, where "every bird of the sky" is formed *after* the human and the garden. The reversed order is one of several features distinguishing the Priestly account (Genesis 1:1–2:3) from the non-Priestly account (Genesis 2:4b–3:24).
