# Moroni 10:32

> Yea, come unto Christ, and be perfected in him, and deny yourselves of all ungodliness; and if ye shall deny yourselves of all ungodliness, and love God with all your might, mind and strength, then is his grace sufficient for you, that by his grace ye may be perfect in Christ. *(Book of Mormon)*

## Significance
[[Alex Arnold]] calls this "the whole LDS position in one verse": the **grace is there and it is sufficient**; the condition is one's *willingness to cooperate* with it ("deny yourselves of all ungodliness... love God"). It is the Restoration's framing of grace working *through* faith and discipleship — not works *earning* salvation, but grace *perfecting* the one who comes to Christ. Read alongside [[2 Nephi 25.23|2 Nephi 25:23]] ("by grace... after all we can do") and Ether 12:27, it grounds the LDS reply to the "works-based religion" charge. See [[LDS Salvation Is by Grace Not Earned by Works]] and the video summary in [[Moronis Standard - Luther Added a Word to the Bible]].

Often misread (by critics and some Latter-day Saints) as "do everything first, *then* grace activates"; the cited grace texts and prophets (Oaks, McConkie, Uchtdorf) correct that to grace as the *constant* and faithfulness as the *variable*.
