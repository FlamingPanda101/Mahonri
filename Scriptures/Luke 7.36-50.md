# Luke 7:36-50 — the woman with the alabaster box

> And, behold, a woman in the city, which was a sinner... brought an alabaster box of ointment, and stood at his feet behind him weeping, and began to wash his feet with tears... and anointed them with the ointment... Her sins, which are many, are forgiven; for she loved much. *(KJV)*

## Key phrase
"**Brought an alabaster box of ointment... and anointed them**" — a woman anoints Jesus at a Pharisee's house; cf. the parallel head-anointings in [[Matthew 1.16|Matthew 26]] and Mark 14, where Jesus says "she did it for my burial."

## Apologetic / typological application
[[Andrea Woodmansee]] reads the alabaster box as part of a bride's **dowry** — oil kept to anoint her husband's body at death — so the woman is performing a wedding rite, recognizing [[Jesus of Nazareth|Christ]] as the **bridegroom** who will redeem her from captivity to sin. She suggests the woman (introduced near [[Mary Magdalene]] in the narrative) was someone close to Jesus who knew he would die. See [[The Alabaster Box Was the Bride's Dowry Oil to Anoint the Bridegroom]]. The poured oil ("shiny" face) is flagged as a thread back to anointing/glory imagery.
