# Quran 10:94 — "ask those who read the scripture before you"

> So if you are in doubt, [O Muhammad], about that which We have revealed to you, then ask those who have been reading the Scripture before you. The truth has certainly come to you from your Lord, so never be among the doubters. *(Sahih International)*

## Key Detail
The verse addresses doubt **about the Qur'an** ("that which We have revealed to *you*") and prescribes a remedy: consult "those who have been reading the Scripture before you" — i.e. the Jews and Christians and their Torah/Gospel. The whole apologetic weight turns on the *direction of appeal*: the prior scriptures are invoked as the thing that *settles* doubt about the Qur'an, which [[Avery (God Logic)|Avery]] reads as making the Bible the epistemic **standard over** the Qur'an. See [[Surah 10-94 Makes the Bible the Standard for the Quran]].

A standard Muslim qualification: many classical commentators take the address ("if *you* are in doubt") as rhetorical — directed at the hearer/skeptic, since hadith report Muhammad denied ever doubting — which would soften the inference. Avery pre-empts this as irrelevant to the conditional's logic ("what does Allah tell him to do *if* he did?").
