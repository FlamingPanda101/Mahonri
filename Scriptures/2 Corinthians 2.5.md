# 2 Corinthians 2:5

> "But if any have caused grief, he hath not grieved me, but in part: that I may not overcharge you all." (KJV)

[[Dan McClellan]] cites this verse as a case of the King James Version's "overly literal" method producing "indecipherable English" — it slavishly follows the Greek word order, which does not help in a language (English) that lacks the Greek case system. He contrasts it with the updated NRSV ("he has caused it not to me, but to some extent — not to exaggerate it — to all of you"), which renders the *sense* rather than the form. See [[The KJV Used an Inadequate Overly Literal Translation Method]].
