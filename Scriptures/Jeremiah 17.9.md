# Jeremiah 17:9

> The heart is deceitful above all things, and desperately wicked: who can know it? *(KJV)*

## Key Hebrew term
**עָקֹב (ʿaqob)** — "deceitful, crooked, devious" (cognate to the name *Jacob*, the supplanter). The "heart" (לֵב, *lev*) in Hebrew denotes the seat of will, thought, and feeling — not merely emotion. The verse declares the inner self untrustworthy as a guide to truth.

## In context
[[Trent Horn]] and [[Joe Heschmeyer]] deploy this verse against [[John Calvin]]'s and [[Angel Moroni|Moroni]]'s appeal to an interior witness ("feel it in your bones," the "burning bosom"). If Scripture itself warns that the heart is "deceitful above all things," then a strong inner conviction that book X is divinely inspired is not a reliable predictor that X is in fact inspired.
