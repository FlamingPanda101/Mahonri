# 1 Samuel 28

> Then said the woman, Whom shall I bring up unto thee? And he said, Bring me up Samuel... And when the woman saw Samuel, she cried with a loud voice... And the king said unto her, Be not afraid: for what sawest thou? And the woman said unto Saul, I saw gods ascending out of the earth. *(1 Samuel 28:11–13, KJV)*

## Significance

The narrative of the **necromancer ("witch") of Endor**. Saul, having himself outlawed necromancy, is in a bind: Adonai will not answer him through the prophets, so in disguise he visits the necromancer of Endor and asks her to call up the dead Samuel — and it works. The woman reports seeing "**Elohim** [gods/a deity] rising up from the underworld," and the summoned Samuel, annoyed at being disturbed, foretells Saul's death in battle.

[[Dan McClellan]] cites this as evidence that **ancient Israel had a populated, conscious realm of the dead** ([[Sheol Was the Undifferentiated Realm of All the Dead|Sheol]]), not annihilation: Samuel still *exists* in a non-fleshly sense, can be summoned, and can be petitioned for information. That the dead Samuel is called *Elohim* attests the overlap between ancestors and deities in Israelite religion, and that **necromancy was "phenomenally common"** despite later prohibitions.
