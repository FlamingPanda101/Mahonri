# Doctrine and Covenants 28.13

"For all things must be done in order, and by common consent in the church, by the prayer of faith."

## Why it's cited

This verse establishes the LDS law of common consent — the principle that the actions and offices of the Church are to be ratified by the agreement of its members, not imposed unilaterally. Apologetics notes cite it to argue that the revealed Church order is participatory and decentralized in its sustaining of leaders and decisions, supporting a "gardening" rather than top-down model of governance and retention. It also serves as a doctrinal counterweight to characterizations of the Church as purely authoritarian, since order itself is defined here as proceeding by consent and the prayer of faith.
