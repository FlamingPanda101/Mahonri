# Psalm 110:1

> The LORD said unto my Lord, Sit thou at my right hand, until I make thine enemies thy footstool. *(KJV)*

## Key Term: "Sit thou at my right hand"
[[Blake Ostler]] calls this the single most-quoted scripture in the first 200 years of Christian thought, and "the vision people envisioned in their minds" of the Father-Son relationship: a Father seated on his throne who gives the greatest possible honor to the Son seated at his right hand. Ostler reads this through ANE monarchy and adoption — the coronation/right-hand seat marks the heir who, once enthroned, bears the benefactor's name, authority, and power. It establishes **two distinct beings** in a relationship of honor, not one being in three persons.
