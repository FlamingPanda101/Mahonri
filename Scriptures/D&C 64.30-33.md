# D&C 64:30-33

A revelation dated 11 September 1831 (the directive to [[Frederick G. Williams]] is dated 4 September 1831 in narrative summaries), containing the promise that Kirtland would remain a stronghold of the church for a fixed term:

> 30 ...I will not that my servant Frederick G. Williams should sell his farm, for I, the Lord, will retain a stronghold in the land of Kirtland, for the space of five years, in the which I will not overthrow the wicked, that thereby I may save some.

> 31 And after that day, I, the Lord, will not hold any guilty that shall go with an open heart up to the land of Zion...

> 33 Wherefore, be not weary in well-doing, for ye are laying the foundation of a great work. And out of small things proceedeth that which is great.

The five-year stronghold clause is the textual basis for the [[Kirtland a Stronghold for Five Years Prophecy]], cited as one of [[Joseph Smith]]'s comparatively well-sourced (canonical, contemporaneous) predictions.
