# Mosiah 21:27-28 — Ammon Declines to Baptize for Lack of Authority

> And it came to pass that king Limhi and many of his people were desirous to be baptized; but there was none in the land that **had authority from God**. And **Ammon declined doing this thing, considering himself an unworthy servant**. *(verse 27; current edition reads "King Mosiah had a gift from God" at v.28 — see the textual note below)* [^1]

## Key Detail
[[Ammon]] could have baptized King Limhi and his people on the strength of their faith, but did not — **"there was none in the land that had authority from God,"** and Ammon "considered himself an unworthy servant." In the [[The Stick of Joseph - Sacred Temple Connection to Priesthood Keys|Stick of Joseph interview]] the hosts read "unworthy" as plausibly meaning *unauthorized* (lacking conferred priesthood) rather than merely sinful — making this a Book-of-Mormon precedent for the principle that desire and faith do not substitute for delegated authority to administer ordinances. (Ammon later does baptize in Alma 19, after the proper order is in place.) The same chapter's v.28 is independently discussed for the **"King Benjamin → King Mosiah"** textual edit; see [[Mosiah 21.28]].
