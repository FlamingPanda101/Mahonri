# 1 Peter 2:9 — a chosen generation, a royal priesthood

> But ye are a chosen generation, a royal priesthood, an holy nation, a peculiar people; that ye should shew forth the praises of him who hath called you out of darkness into his marvellous light. *(KJV)*

## Key Detail
Peter applies temple/priestly language — "a **royal priesthood**, an holy nation" (echoing Exodus 19:6) — to the *whole* body of believers, not to an ordained clergy class only. Latter-day Saints read this as a New Testament warrant for a lay, universal priesthood and, crucially, one that is **not limited to men**: the "royal priesthood" of the redeemed includes the women John sees robed before the throne. It is the apostolic header under which the [[Revelation 5.9-10|Rev 5:9-10]] "kings and priests" and the temple's "kings and queens, priests and priestesses" language sit.
