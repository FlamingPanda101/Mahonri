# Isaiah 1:12

> When ye come to appear before me, who hath required this at your hand, to tread my courts? *(KJV)*

> When you come to appear before me, who asked this from your hand? Trample my courts no more. *(NRSVUE; footnote: "or see my face")*

## Key Hebrew Terms

- **לִרְאוֹת (lir'ot):** active Qal infinitive construct, "to see." The decisive form. Unlike finite verbs, the Qal and Niphal infinitive constructs of *ra'ah* have **different consonants**; the passive would require **לְהֵרָאוֹת (lehera'ot)** with an added *he* (ה) that the text lacks. See [[Infinitive Construct in Isaiah Proves Active Reading]].
- **פָּנַי (panai):** "my face." The consonants spell *lir'ot panai*, "to see my face" — God speaking of his own face being seen in the temple. The [[Masoretes|Masoretes]] supplied passive vowels onto consonants that can only be active.

## Context

Part of Isaiah's indictment of empty temple ritual ("trample my courts no more"). [[Dan McClellan]] treats this verse as the linchpin proving the pilgrimage idiom means "to see the face of God": here the consonants cannot bear the passive reading, exposing the [[Masoretes|Masoretic]] vocalization as an override. The NRSVUE concedes "or see my face" in a footnote at this verse while keeping the passive in the main text and the parallels.
