# Revelation 6:9-11

> I saw under the altar the souls of them that were slain for the word of God... And they cried with a loud voice, saying, How long, O Lord... And white robes were given unto every one of them; and it was said unto them, that they should rest yet for a little season, until their fellowservants... should be fulfilled. *(KJV)*

## Significance
A key text for the early Christian veneration of **martyrs** and the **intermediate state**. Catherine Gines Taylor cites it for the belief that martyrs "rest under the altar and cry with a loud voice" — crowned with victory yet still *waiting* for the rest of God's children to find refuge. D. Jill Kirby cites Rev 6:9 for the conviction that "the martyrs certainly went straight to heaven," which sharpened the question of what happened to the ordinary righteous who were *not* martyred (compare [[Luke 16.22]]). [[N.T. Wright]] grounds the Orthodox practice of praying *for* the saints (not only *with* them) on this waiting state.
