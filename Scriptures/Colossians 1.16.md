# Colossians 1:16

> For by him were all things created, that are in heaven, and that are in earth, visible and invisible … all things were created by him, and for him. *(KJV)*

## Significance
Source language for the Nicene Creed's opening: "creator of all things both visible and invisible." [[Jason R. Combs]] shows that the creed was woven largely from scripture — Deuteronomy 6:4 ("one God"), Colossians 1:16 ("visible and invisible"), 1 Corinthians 8:6 ("one Lord Jesus Christ"), John 1:14 ("only-begotten"). The Council of Nicaea departed from scriptural language only where interpretation was disputed (e.g. *homoousios*). Combs uses this to argue the creed "rarely veers away from scriptural language," a point relevant to LDS readers reassessing how much of Nicaea is biblically grounded.
