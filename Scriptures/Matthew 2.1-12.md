# Matthew 2:1-12

> Now when Jesus was born in Bethlehem of Judaea in the days of Herod the king, behold, there came wise men from the east to Jerusalem… and when they were come into the house, they saw the young child with Mary his mother, and fell down, and worshipped him. *(KJV)*

## In context
The arrival of the Magi, preached on by the fifth-century [[Isaac the Teacher]] as an exercise in imaginative rhetorical contrast. Isaac pictures the Magi finding Judea silent and unawakened — "God is in this land, but its inhabitants are still sleeping!" — contrasting the festival excitement of Persia with the stillness of the Holy Land. Heal cites it to illustrate how preachers appealed "to the imagination of [their] hearers in descriptive pictures." (Distinct from the existing note [[Matthew 2.23|Matthew 2:23]].)
