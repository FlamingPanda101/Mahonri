# 2 Chronicles 35:4 — Josiah Orders the Passover "by the Writing of David"

> And prepare yourselves by the houses of your fathers, after your courses, **according to the writing of David king of Israel, and according to the writing of Solomon his son**. *(KJV)*

## Significance
The companion to [[2 Chronicles 29.25|2 Chronicles 29:25]] in [[Robert Boland]]'s argument. Josiah regulates the temple service by "the writing of David... and of Solomon" — directives treated as authoritative and binding that are not preserved in the surviving biblical text. Boland uses the pair to argue that an inspired, conscience-binding *word of God* operated outside the canon, against the claim that the 66 books exhaust inspired revelation. See [[Hezekiah and Josiah Bound a Word of God Never in the Bible]].
