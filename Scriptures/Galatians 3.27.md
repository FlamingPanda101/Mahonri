#type/scripture

## Text
"All of you who were baptized into Christ have clothed yourselves with Christ." (Galatians 3:27, NASB; see also Revelation 7:9–17)

## Use in Source
Mark Ellison cites this verse as the basis for the white robe worn by the newly baptized. He highlights that the Greek verb for "to clothe or to put on" is *enduō*, "from which we get the English endow and endowment." The symbol of the robe developed particular richness in the Syriac tradition of a "Robe of Glory" — worn by Adam and Eve in Eden, lost at the Fall, and reclaimed at baptism as a terrestrial anticipation of eschatological Paradise.

## Notes
This *enduō* / endowment etymology is one of the most-cited points of contact between ancient baptismal vesture and Latter-day Saint temple vocabulary (see [[Baptismal Washing and Anointing in Early Christianity]]); the immediately following verse [[Galatians 3.28]] grounds the social equalization of house-church gatherings.
