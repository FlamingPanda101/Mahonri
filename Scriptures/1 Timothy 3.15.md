# 1 Timothy 3:15

> But if I tarry long, that thou mayest know how thou oughtest to behave thyself in the house of God, which is the church of the living God, the pillar and ground of the truth. *(KJV)*

## Key Greek term
**στῦλος καὶ ἑδραίωμα τῆς ἀληθείας (stylos kai hedraiōma tēs alētheias)** — "pillar and foundation/bulwark of the truth." The predicate is applied to **the church (ἐκκλησία)**, not to Scripture.

## In context
A primary Catholic counter-text. [[Trent Horn]] cites it to argue Scripture itself names the *church* — not the Bible — as the pillar and foundation of truth, undercutting the claim that the written word is the sole infallible rule of faith. Used alongside [[Matthew 16.18]] (the church Christ builds, against which the gates of hell will not prevail) to ground a living, visible, teaching authority.
