# 2 Nephi 9:14

> 14 Wherefore, we shall have a perfect knowledge of all our guilt, and our uncleanness, and our nakedness; and the righteous shall have a perfect knowledge of their enjoyment, and their righteousness, being clothed with purity, yea, even with the robe of righteousness. *(Book of Mormon, 2 Nephi 9:14)*

## Significance
Jacob's discourse on the Atonement teaches that at the judgment every person will possess a **"perfect knowledge"** — the wicked of their guilt, the righteous of their joy. This total, unflinching self-knowledge is precisely what near-death survivors describe in the panoramic [[NDE - The Life Review]]: an instantaneous, vivid, complete replay in which they see and recognize the whole of their lives. It complements Amulek's "bright recollection of all our guilt" in [[Alma 11.43|Alma 11:43]].
