# D&C 89 — The Word of Wisdom

> **2** To be sent greeting; not by commandment or constraint, but by revelation and the word of wisdom… **4** In consequence of evils and designs which do and will exist in the hearts of conspiring men in the last days, I have warned you, and forewarn you, by giving unto you this word of wisdom… **5** That inasmuch as any man drinketh wine or strong drink among you, behold it is not good… **7** And, again, strong drinks are not for the belly… **18** And all saints who remember to keep and do these sayings… shall receive health in their navel and marrow to their bones; **20** And shall run and not be weary, and shall walk and not faint. (D&C 89)

The revelation given through [[Joseph Smith]] at Kirtland in February 1833, in the council room of the School of the Prophets.

In the [[Let's Get Real with Stephen Jones - Joseph Smith's Biggest Discovery (Don Bradley E0039)|Let's Get Real interview]], [[Don Bradley]] reads the Word of Wisdom against its *deep* personal context rather than as an abstract list of prohibitions: it was given less than four months after [[Emma Smith]] nearly died bearing [[Joseph Smith III]] and while she struggled with her postpartum health, so the promises of "health in the navel," "marrow in the bones," and to "run and not be weary" answered a concrete need in the Smith household. Bradley also notes the earliest version of D&C 89's neighboring revelation to [[Joseph Smith Sr]] opens "remember temperance," plausibly advice tailored to the father's documented weakness for wine. He stresses the code reads as positive counsel — favoring "herbs," "vegetables," and plant foods over meat eaten "sparingly" — not merely a set of bans.[^1] See [[The Word of Wisdom Is Positive Health Counsel Not Just a List of Prohibitions]] and [[Emma's Difficult Childbirth Is the Immediate Context of the Word of Wisdom]].

> Note: Bradley repeatedly emphasizes that the Word of Wisdom (1833) did **not** exist at the time of Joseph's 1813 childhood leg surgery — the two are separated by two decades — which is the linchpin of his argument that the boy's refusal of alcohol cannot be explained by it. See [[Joseph Smith's Refusal of Alcohol Cannot Be Explained by the Word of Wisdom]].
