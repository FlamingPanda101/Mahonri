# Matthew 27:3-5 (NRSV)

> "When Judas, his betrayer, saw that Jesus was condemned, he repented and brought back the thirty pieces of silver to the chief priests and the elders. He said, 'I have sinned by betraying innocent blood.' But they said, 'What is that to us? See to it yourself.' Throwing down the pieces of silver in the temple, he departed; and he went and hanged himself."

**Note:** Matthew's account of the death of Judas — Judas returns the silver, throws it in the temple, and **hangs himself**; the *chief priests* then buy the potter's field (vv. 6-8), and it is the "Field of Blood" because it was bought with *blood money* (Jesus's blood), not Judas's. Directly conflicts with [[Acts 1.18-19|Acts 1:18-19]] (Judas buys the field; dies by falling and bursting open; named for his gore). Matthew also ties the purchase to a "fulfillment" of prophecy, McClellan's clue that Matthew has shaped the story.
