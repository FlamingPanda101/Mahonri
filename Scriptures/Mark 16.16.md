# Mark 16:16

> He that believeth and is baptized shall be saved; but he that believeth not shall be damned. *(KJV)*

Part of the "Longer Ending of Mark" (16:9-20), which is absent from the earliest Greek manuscripts (Codex Sinaiticus, Vaticanus) and marked as doubtful in many others.

## In the baptism debate
[[Joe Heschmeyer]] leads with this verse: Jesus plainly pairs belief *and* baptism for salvation. [[Ryan (NeedGod)|Ryan]] makes two replies: (1) **textual** — citing [[Daniel Wallace]] and [[Bruce Metzger]], he argues 16:9-20 is most likely not original to Mark, so it carries no authority; and (2) **grammatical** — even if retained, the verse states the *negative* condition only for unbelief ("he that believeth not shall be condemned"), not for being unbaptized, so baptism is indicative of believers rather than a condition of salvation. Heschmeyer counters that the Holy Spirit's inspiration does not depend on the human author, so "whether Mark wrote it or not, the Holy Spirit still inspired it," and that omitting baptism makes Jesus' explicit mention of it pointless. See [[Mark 16.16 Pairs Belief and Baptism but Its Authenticity Is Disputed]].
