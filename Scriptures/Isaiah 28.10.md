# Isaiah 28:10

> For precept must be upon precept, precept upon precept; line upon line, line upon line; here a little, and there a little. *(KJV)*

## Significance
The source of the LDS "line upon line, precept upon precept" doctrine of gradual revelation.
[[Dan McClellan]] notes that the Hebrew (*tsav latsav, qav laqav*) is widely recognized by scholars as
**deliberate gibberish** — paired with v. 11's "stammering lips and another tongue," the point is that
God's message *sounds like nonsense* to those outside the covenant. The German Einheitsübersetzung now
renders it as gibberish. If the church endorsed a critical translation, "where do we get that doctrine
from?" — a case study in doctrine anchored to a contingent KJV rendering. See
[[We Negotiate With the Text - It Does Not Speak for Itself]].
