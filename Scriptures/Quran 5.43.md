# Quran 5:43 — they have the Torah, in which is Allah's judgment

> But how is it that they come to you for judgement while they have the Torah, in which is the judgement of Allah? Then they turn away, [even] after that; but those are not [in fact] believers. *(Sahih International)*

## Key Detail
The verse expresses surprise that Jews would seek Muhammad's judgment "while they have the Torah, in which is the judgement of Allah." [[Avery (God Logic)|Avery]] presses two points: (1) the verse treats the Torah of Muhammad's day as *containing Allah's own judgments* — i.e. authentic and authoritative; and (2) it **rebuts the supersession move** ("we have the Qur'an now, so the prior scriptures are obsolete"), since the Qur'an here still defers to the Torah as a sufficient source of divine judgment. Both points feed [[The Quran's Testimony to the Previous Scriptures]] and the first horn of [[The Islamic Dilemma]]. Avery names this among his favorite verses (with [[Quran 5.47|5:47]] and 5:68).
