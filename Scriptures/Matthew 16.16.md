# Matthew 16:16

> And Simon Peter answered and said, Thou art the Christ, the Son of the living God. *(KJV)*

## Significance
The opening text of [[Jason R. Combs]]'s chapter on the divine nature. Combs notes that "even as Peter spoke these words he did not yet fully comprehend their meaning" — Peter immediately refused to accept that the Christ must suffer and die (Matthew 16:21–22). A fuller understanding of the nature of Christ, and therefore of God, "would come to Jesus's disciples slowly line upon line," with greater clarity after the Resurrection and Pentecost. The confession frames the entire centuries-long effort to articulate who the "Son of the living God" is.
