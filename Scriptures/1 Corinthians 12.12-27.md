# 1 Corinthians 12:12-27

> 12 For as the body is one, and hath many members, and all the members of that one body, being many, are one body: so also is Christ.
> ...
> 27 Now ye are the body of Christ, and members in particular.

Paul's image of the church as one body with many members, each contributing a distinct gift. In [[Ward Radio - Why Everyone Is Jealous of Our High Demand Religion]], [[Russell Moore]] invokes this passage to argue that "a critical aspect of membership is to use one's gifts to build up the church," and that evangelical youth disengage when they feel the church has no need of their gifts.

The hosts turn the passage back on him: if the body genuinely has many members with varied gifts, why exclude the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints — which, they argue, may simply possess "a spiritual gift of developing programs" that activate their youth? This drives [[Trust the Next Generation With Real Responsibility]] and connects to the latitude argument in [[Creedal Latitude on Baptism but Not the Trinity]].
