# Alma 24:12-13 (Book of Mormon)

> "Now my best beloved brethren, since God hath taken away our stains, and our swords have become bright, then let us stain our swords no more with the blood of our brethren. Behold, I say unto you, Nay, let us retain our swords that they be not stained with the blood of our brethren; for perhaps, if we should stain our swords again they can no more be washed bright through the blood of the Son of our great God, which shall be shed for the atonement of our sins."

The pivot verses of King **Anti-Nephi-Lehi**'s pacifism speech, in which the converted Lamanites vow never again to take up weapons. The passage clusters "liquid"/blood imagery (*stain, blood, washed, shed, atonement*) and is the proposed center of the claimed double chiasmus.

**Key term:** *atonement* — argued to reflect Hebrew **kpr / kaphar** ("to cover," to blot out / cover with blood), the [[Atonement as Covering - Joseph Could Not Have Known|cover-etymology]].
