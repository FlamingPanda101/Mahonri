# John 17:20-23

> Neither pray I for these alone, but for them also which shall believe on me through their word; That they all may be one; as thou, Father, art in me, and I in thee, that they also may be one in us... that they may be one, even as we are one. *(KJV)*

## Significance
The same "one" of [[John 10.30|John 10:30]]: Jesus prays his disciples be one **as he and the Father are one**. Since no one claims the disciples merge into one essence, the unity in view is of purpose and will — undercutting the use of "I and the Father are one" as a proof of one-substance Trinitarianism.
