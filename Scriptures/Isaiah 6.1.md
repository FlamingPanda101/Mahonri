# Isaiah 6:1

> In the year that king Uzziah died I saw also the Lord sitting upon a throne, high and lifted up, and his train filled the temple. *(KJV)*

## Key Term: the enthroned Lord seen in vision
[[Blake Ostler]] notes there is "no indication whatsoever" in Isaiah 6 that the figure on the throne is anyone other than **Yahweh** — and it would be inconceivable to the Hebrews that it was not. Yet the Gospel of John (John 12:41) interprets the one Isaiah saw as **Christ**. For Ostler this shows that, for John, the Son is the Yahweh who *interacts* with Israel in the Old Testament as the Father's commissioned agent — name-bearing agency, not the Son being a "multi-personal Yahweh" or the Father himself.
