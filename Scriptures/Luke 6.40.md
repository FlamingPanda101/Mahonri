# Luke 6:40 (KJV)

> "The disciple is not above his master: but every one that is perfect shall be as his master."

**Note:** "Perfect" here is *katartizō* (κατηρτισμένος) — "fully trained / fully formed," i.e., a disciple brought to completion is *like his teacher*. Read in isolation (as [[Matthew 5.48|Matthew 5:48]] often is), the verse can sound like a demand for flawlessness; in context it sits just after Luke's mercy-and-judging sermon, reinforcing that "perfect" language in the Gospels means maturation/likeness, not zero error.
