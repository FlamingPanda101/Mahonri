# Helaman 5:6-7 — "remember ... it was said ... they were good"

> Behold, my sons, I desire that ye should remember to keep the commandments of God; and I would that ye should declare unto the people these words. Behold, I have given unto you the names of our first parents who came out of the land of Jerusalem; and this I have done that when you remember your names ye may remember them; and when ye remember them ye may remember their works; and ... that ye may remember ... that it was said, and also written, that they were good. *(Helaman 5:6-7, Book of Mormon)*

## Significance
Helaman names his sons **Nephi** and **Lehi** after the founders and tells them that remembering their names should prompt them to recall that "it was said and written ... that they were **good**." This is an internal confirmation that the narrators understood **Nephi** to carry the meaning "good" (Egyptian *nfr*). The blessing is later fulfilled in Helaman 8, where corrupt judges call Nephi "a good man." It was this passage, Bowen says, that "sold" him that real onomastic wordplay was occurring.
