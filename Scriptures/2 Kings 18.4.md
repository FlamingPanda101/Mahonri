# 2 Kings 18:4 (Hezekiah breaks the Nehushtan)

> He removed the high places, and brake the images, and cut down the groves, and brake in pieces the brasen serpent that Moses had made: for unto those days the children of Israel did burn incense to it: and he called it Nehushtan. *(KJV)*

## Significance

The one biblical notice of the **destruction** of the [[Numbers 21.8-9|brazen serpent]] of [[Numbers 21.8-9|Numbers 21]]. In the [[The Stick of Joseph - The Ancient Meaning of the Serpent|Stick of Joseph reading]], several details overturn the conventional "idolatry corrected" gloss:

- **"Nehushtan" is a disparaging nickname, not the object's real name.** [[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)|Dave Butler]] argues Hezekiah *coins* the name as an insult — "sneaky thing, brassy thing" — so the true name used by those who valued the serpent is unknown. This explains why the word "Nehushtan" never appears in [[John 3.16|John 3:14]] or the Book of Mormon: the people who revered the serpent did not insult it. See [[The Nehushtan Is a Healing Divine Serpent the Nephites and Early Christians Valued]].
- **Hezekiah and Isaiah are in direct conflict.** Hezekiah destroys the serpent *because* Israel burned incense to it — yet his contemporary [[Isaiah 6.1|Isaiah]] sees the seraphim (read as Nehushtanim) amid the clouds of temple incense ([[Isaiah 6.1|Isaiah 6]]). The hosts read this as evidence of *multiple religions* inside the First Temple (citing Ziony Zevit), with the Book of Mormon preserving the suppressed pro-serpent strand.

This is the third of the Bible's four Nehushtan references — [[Numbers 21.8-9|Numbers 21]], 2 Kings 18, [[John 3.16|John 3:14]], and (probably) [[Isaiah 6.1|Isaiah 6]] — against roughly five more in the Book of Mormon.
