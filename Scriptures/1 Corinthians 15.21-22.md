# 1 Corinthians 15:21-22

> For since by man came death, by man came also the resurrection of the dead. For as in Adam all die, even so in Christ shall all be made alive. *(KJV)*

## Significance
A central text for early readings of Adam-and-Christ typology. [[Origen]] read "in Adam all die" universally, "not so much of one particular individual as of the whole human race," treating Adam as a symbol for all humanity rather than the source of inherited guilt. The verse also underlies the early Christian artistic motif (e.g. the Arles sarcophagus, c. 325) of Christ as the "new Adam" who overcomes the death and sin Adam introduced. It frames the contrast between the Greek Fathers' typological reading and [[Augustine]]'s doctrine of original guilt.
