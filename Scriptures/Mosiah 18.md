# Mosiah 18 — The Covenant to Bear Burdens and Be Witnesses

> **8** …as ye are desirous to come into the fold of God… and are willing to bear one another's burdens, that they may be light; **9** …and stand as witnesses of God at all times and in all things, and in all places that ye may be in, even until death… **10** …if this be the desire of your hearts, what have you against being baptized…? (Mosiah 18:8–10)

The baptismal covenant Alma articulates at the Waters of Mormon. [[Don Bradley]] and [[Stephen Jones]] invoke it as the scriptural charge that *every* member — not only apostles — covenants "to be witnesses of God at all times… even unto death," extending the witness-of-Christ heritage of the [[Martin Harris]] family to the whole Church. They pair it with [[Dallin H. Oaks]]'s teaching ("Witnesses of Christ," 1990) that the apostolic duty to witness "applies to every member," making the covenant the application of the episode's central theme to the listener. See [[Martin Harris Was a Witness of Christ Not Only of the Plates]].[^1]

> Note: the panel briefly conflates Alma's covenant (Mosiah 18) with King Benjamin's earlier name-of-Christ covenant (Mosiah 2–5), then corrects the slip on air.[^1]
