# 2 Chronicles 29:25 — Hezekiah Restores Temple Worship "by His Prophets"

> And he set the Levites in the house of the LORD with cymbals, with psalteries, and with harps, according to the commandment of David, and of Gad the king's seer, and Nathan the prophet: **for so was the commandment of the LORD by his prophets**. *(KJV)*

## Significance
A key text in [[Robert Boland]]'s case against *sola scriptura*. King Hezekiah enforces an arrangement of temple worship attributed to "the commandment of the LORD by his prophets" (David, Gad, Nathan) — men dead roughly 250 years — yet that specific prescription appears **nowhere else in the Bible**. Boland reads this as a binding, inspired *word of God* that exists outside the inscripturated canon, paired with the parallel [[2 Chronicles 35.4|2 Chronicles 35:4]] (Josiah). See [[Hezekiah and Josiah Bound a Word of God Never in the Bible]] and the broader [[The Word of God Is Not Confined to the Written Word]].
