# Doctrine and Covenants 20.32-34

"But there is a possibility that man may fall from grace and depart from the living God; Therefore let the church take heed and pray always, lest they fall into temptation; Yea, and even let those who are sanctified take heed also."

## Why it's cited

These verses are the canonical LDS rebuttal to the doctrines of irresistible grace and the unconditional perseverance of the saints. The passage states plainly that even the sanctified can fall from grace, so apologetics notes use it to argue that salvation involves continuing watchfulness and real human agency rather than a one-time, guaranteed status. It anchors LDS responses to Reformed determinism — the point that grace can be forfeited — in revealed canon, dated to the founding of the Church on April 6, 1830.
