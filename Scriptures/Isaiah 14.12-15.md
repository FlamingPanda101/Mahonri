# Isaiah 14:12-15

> "How art thou fallen from heaven, O Lucifer, son of the morning! ... For thou hast said in thine heart, I will ascend into heaven, I will exalt my throne above the stars of God ... I will be like the most High. Yet thou shalt be brought down to hell, to the sides of the pit." (KJV)

## Use in the vault

Cited by [[Ether's Elephant]] (with [[Moses 4]]) as a scriptural witness to Satan's self-chosen rebellion and fall — the LDS reading takes the passage as describing Lucifer's prideful choice rather than a created propensity for evil, supporting [[Jesus and Satan Are Brothers Only If Nothing Is Created]].

Against this, mainstream scholarship and the vault's [[Book of Enoch - Origin of the Satan Figure]] and [[Satan - Development of the Figure]] note that Isaiah 14 is explicitly a taunt against the **king of Babylon** (v. 4), using Canaanite "morning star" (*helel ben shachar*) mythology — the identification of this passage with Satan is a later Christian development, not the text's own referent. The note honors both the devotional and the historical-critical readings.
