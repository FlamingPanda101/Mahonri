# Jeremiah 36:31 — judgment after the king burns the scroll

> And I will punish him and his seed and his servants for their iniquity; and I will bring upon them ... all the evil that I have pronounced against them; but they hearkened not. *(Jeremiah 36:31, KJV)*

## Significance
After [[Jeremiah]]'s prophecy is read aloud, King Jehoiakim cuts and **burns the scroll** (Jeremiah 36:23), and the Lord pronounces judgment because the rulers "hearkened not." Ward Radio's guest uses the king burning Jeremiah's prophecy as the climax of the rejected-prophet pattern that the Book of Mormon independently reports ([[Helaman 8.7|Helaman 8]]; 1 Nephi 7:14) — Jeremiah imprisoned, opposed by false prophets, his words destroyed by the establishment — supporting the claim that Lehi's contemporaries knew and recorded a controversial, persecuted Jeremiah.
