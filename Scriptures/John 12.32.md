# John 12:32

> And I, if I be lifted up from the earth, will draw all men unto me. *(KJV)*

## Key phrase
"**Will draw all men unto me**" — Greek **πάντας ἑλκύσω πρὸς ἐμαυτόν**. *Helkyō* ("draw, drag") is debated: universal drawing of all people, or all *kinds*. The affirmative reads it as universal benevolent orientation that does not coerce ("forced love is not love").

## Apologetic application
[[Gavin (Catholic Debater)]] cites it to show God's benevolence is universal even when not universally accepted, distinguishing omnibenevolence from universalism and coercion. See [[Scripture Explicitly Affirms Universal Salvific Will]] and [[Omnibenevolence Does Not Equal Universalism or Coercion]].
