# Deuteronomy 4:12 (KJV)

> And the LORD spake unto you out of the midst of the fire: ye heard the voice of the words, but saw no similitude; only ye heard a voice.

## Significance
[[Margaret Barker]] reads this as a defining marker of the **Deuteronomist** position: at Horeb (Sinai) Israel heard a voice but "saw no form" — there is no vision of God and no incarnation. She sets this against the first-temple visionary stream (Isaiah 6, Ezekiel 1) in which the Lord is seen in human form. The contradiction is, on her reading, a fingerprint of two competing theologies inside the Hebrew Bible, and Christianity (the "pure in heart… shall see God") is rooted in the visionary side.
