# Mark 2:19-20 — the bridegroom and fasting

> And Jesus said unto them, Can the children of the bridechamber fast, while the bridegroom is with them? as long as they have the bridegroom with them, they cannot fast. But the days will come, when the bridegroom shall be taken away from them, and then shall they fast in those days. *(KJV)*

## Key phrase
"**As long as they have the bridegroom with them, they cannot fast... when the bridegroom shall be taken away... then shall they fast**" — Jesus answers why his disciples do not fast by casting himself as the **bridegroom** at a wedding feast.

## Apologetic / typological application
[[Andrea Woodmansee]] reads this as Hebrew-wedding speech: at a seven-day wedding feast the bridechamber guests do not fast. Jesus identifies himself as the bridegroom, so his presence is a feast, not a fast; his absence ("taken away") is why we fast now — the fast becomes a sign of longing for the bridegroom's return, a "physical longing... because the bread of life is not with us." This is one strand of the larger thesis that the whole gospel is structured as a wedding. See [[Ancient Hebrew Weddings Are a Three-Phase Type of the Temple]].
