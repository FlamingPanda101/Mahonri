# Abraham 1 — "by his own hand, upon papyrus"

> The Records of Abraham... written by his own hand, upon papyrus. *(Book of Abraham, superscription / Abraham 1)*

## Key Detail
- The superscription describes the [[Book of Abraham]] as "written by [Abraham's] own hand, upon papyrus." [[Mike Winger]] treats this as a fatal problem, since the surviving papyrus dates ~1,800 years after Abraham. [[Cardon Ellis]] and [[Luke Hansen]] reply that "by his own hand" is **standard ancient copyist usage** — a copy of an original was said to be by the original author's hand (cf. the Shabaka Stone, the books of Acts, Pythagoras released centuries after his death). See [[The Hand of Abraham Phrase Is Ancient Copyist Usage Not Autograph]].
- Abraham 1 also contains the human/child sacrifice and the attempt on Abraham's life that [[Joseph Smith]] tied to Facsimile 1; later-discovered texts corroborate child sacrifice "in the days of Abraham's father" and Abraham as an astronomer and a literate teacher. See [[Later Texts Corroborate Details Joseph Smith Could Not Have Known in the Book of Abraham]].
