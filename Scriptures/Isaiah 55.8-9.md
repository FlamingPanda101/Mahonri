# Isaiah 55:8-9

> For my thoughts are not your thoughts, neither are your ways my ways, saith the LORD. For as the heavens are higher than the earth, so are my ways higher than your ways, and my thoughts than your thoughts. *(KJV)*

## Significance
The scriptural seed of [[Apophatic Theology Describes God by Negation|apophatic theology]]. [[Jason R. Combs]] traces the logic: if God's ways are "higher than our ways," and human things are searchable, speakable, visible, mortal, and approachable, then "God cannot be any of these things" — a chain of reasoning that, extended, yields a bodiless and emotionless God. Combs uses the verse to show how apophatic (negative) theology has genuine scriptural roots, even as its extreme conclusions (incorporeality, impassibility) go beyond what scripture requires.
