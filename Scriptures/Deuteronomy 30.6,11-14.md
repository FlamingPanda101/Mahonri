# Deuteronomy 30:6,11-14

> 6 And the LORD thy God will circumcise thine heart, and the heart of thy seed, to love the LORD thy God with all thine heart, and with all thy soul, that thou mayest live.
> 11 For this commandment which I command thee this day, it is not hidden from thee, neither is it far off.
> 12 It is not in heaven, that thou shouldest say, Who shall go up for us to heaven... 13 Neither is it beyond the sea...
> 14 But the word is very nigh unto thee, in thy mouth, and in thy heart, that thou mayest do it. *(KJV)*

## In context

[[Blake Ostler]] reads circumcision of the heart as the scriptural image of an open, soft heart exposed to God — "taking the most vulnerable private part of a body and cutting it open to be exposed." The passage establishes that the divine word/commandment is *planted in the heart*, not stored in the head, grounding Ostler's claim that the heart is itself a God-given faculty or "extra sense" for knowing God. [[Paul the Apostle|Paul]] later reads the implanted *dabar* as Christ himself (see [[Romans 10.6-9|Romans 10:6-9]]). [^1]
