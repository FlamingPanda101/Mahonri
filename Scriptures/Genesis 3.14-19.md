# Genesis 3:14-19

> And the LORD God said unto the serpent … thou art cursed above all cattle … And unto Adam he said … cursed is the ground for thy sake; in sorrow shalt thou eat of it all the days of thy life. *(KJV, abridged)*

## Significance
A linchpin in the patristic case against inherited guilt. In Genesis 3 the *serpent* is cursed (3:14) and the *ground* is cursed (3:17) — but **Adam and Eve themselves are never cursed**. Early Christians leaned on this to argue that God did not condemn Adam and Eve as the corrupters of humanity; the consequences were expulsion and toil, not the transmission of guilt. [[Irenaeus of Lyons]] emphasized that "God's response to their choice was to curse the earth and Satan, not Adam and Eve." This reading stands directly against [[Augustine]]'s later doctrine of original sin.
