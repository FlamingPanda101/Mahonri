# Genesis 37 — Joseph Sold into Egypt

The opening of the Joseph cycle (Genesis 37–50): Jacob favors Joseph with the coat of many colors; his brothers, envious of his dreams, sell him into Egypt. Verse 3:

> Now Israel loved Joseph more than all his children, because he was the son of his old age: and he made him a coat of many colours. *(KJV)*

## Significance
This biblical narrative is the basis of the Quran's single sustained story — **Surah Yusuf** (chapter 12), titled "the story of Joseph." Daniel Peterson notes the Quran retells "pretty much" the biblical Joseph-in-Egypt account "with a few differences," and that this is the *longest* and only true narrative chapter in the entire Quran. The contrast illustrates that the Quran is not a narrative work like the Book of Mormon — it borrows and compresses one biblical story rather than spanning a thousand-year history. See [[Muhammad and Joseph Smith Are Apples to Oranges]].

The Hebrew name is *Yosef* (יוֹסֵף); in Arabic, *Yusuf* (يوسف).
