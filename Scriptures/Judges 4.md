# Judges 4

> And Deborah, a prophetess, the wife of Lapidoth, she judged Israel at that time. And she dwelt under the palm tree of Deborah between Ramah and Beth-el in mount Ephraim: and the children of Israel came up to her for judgment. *(Judges 4:4-5, KJV)*

## Key figure: Deborah the prophetess
[[Margaret Barker]] reads the introduction of [[Deborah]] as layered with titles of "the great lady." Her name means **bee** (cf. Asenath's honeycomb and bees); via the archaic interchange of *d* and *g* in old Hebrew script, "Deborah" can be read as ***gevirah***, the Hebrew for the **queen-mother / great lady** of the court. "Wife of *Lapidoth*" is better "lady of the **torches/lamps**" (*lapidot* is not a personal name), associating her with the menorah lights of a holy-of-holies setting; and she sits under the **palm tree**, which Barker treats as the token of one entrusted with the secret knowledge of the holy of holies. Prophetess, judge, bee, lady of the torches, great lady under the palm — all traces, she argues, of the suppressed mother figure.
