# Matthew 3:9

> And think not to say within yourselves, We have Abraham to our father: for I say unto you, that God is able of these stones to raise up children unto Abraham. *(KJV)*

## Context: John the Baptist on fruit over pedigree
Part of John the Baptist's rebuke of the Pharisees and Sadducees (cf. the parallel [[Luke 3.8|Luke 3:8]]), continuing "every tree that bringeth not forth good fruit is hewn down, and cast into the fire" (Matt 3:10). [[Jacob Hansen]] cites this to argue Jesus' (and John's) concern was *fruit* — a lived walk — rather than precise theology, since the religious experts likely had correct doctrine but lacked fruit. [[Gavin Ortlund]] replies that confession of Jesus' identity is itself part of true fruit.
