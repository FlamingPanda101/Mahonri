# Exodus 34:24

> For I will cast out the nations before thee, and enlarge thy borders: neither shall any man desire thy land, when thou shalt go up to appear before the LORD thy God thrice in the year. *(KJV)*

## Key Hebrew Terms

- **רָאָה (ra'ah):** "to see." As in [[Deuteronomy 16.16|Deuteronomy 16:16]], the finite verb's consonants can be read active ("to see [the face of] the LORD") or passive ("to appear"); the [[Masoretes|Masoretes]] vocalized it passive. See [[Masoretic Vowels Reversed the Active Verb to Passive]].
- **לֵרָאוֹת / direct-object marker + פָּנִים:** the construction with the object marker plus "face" again fits "see the face," per [[Dan McClellan]].

## Context

A parallel statement of the thrice-yearly pilgrimage commandment within the so-called "ritual decalogue" of Exodus 34. [[Dan McClellan]] groups it with [[Deuteronomy 16.16|Deuteronomy 16:16]] as a finite-verb case where the consonants are ambiguous — which is why translators default to the passive and why the decisive argument for the active "see the face of God" rests on the infinitive construct in [[Isaiah 1.12|Isaiah 1:12]].
