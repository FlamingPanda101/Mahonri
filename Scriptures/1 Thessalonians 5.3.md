# 1 Thessalonians 5:3 — Sudden destruction as travail upon a woman

> For when they shall say, Peace and safety; then sudden destruction cometh upon them, **as travail upon a woman with child**; and they shall not escape. *(KJV)*

## Key Detail
Paul pairs the familiar "day of the Lord... as a thief in the night" (v. 2) with the **less-quoted** v. 3, which figures the suddenness of the Second Coming as the onset of **labor pains** ("travail upon a woman with child"). In the [[Ward Radio - The Secret Birth Allegory in the Temple Rites Part II|Ward Radio birth-allegory episode]] the hosts make this verse central: Christ and Paul repeatedly use **labor/travail** as the metaphor for the Second Coming, and the "they shall not escape" clause is read against a laboring mother's inability to stop the birth once begun. The Hebrew *chevlei mashiach* ("birth pangs of the Messiah") names the same idea. See [[Birth Pangs of the Messiah Frame the Second Coming as Travail]].
