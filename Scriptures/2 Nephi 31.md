# 2 Nephi 31

> And now, if the Lamb of God, he being holy, should have need to be baptized by
> water, to fulfil all righteousness, O then, how much more need have we, being
> unholy, to be baptized... (v. 5)
>
> And then cometh a remission of your sins by fire and by the Holy Ghost... and
> the words of the Father unto the Son, saying: I will be merciful unto you... (vv. 17–18)
>
> And the Father said: Repent ye, repent ye, and be baptized in the name of my
> Beloved Son. (v. 11)

*(Selected verses, KJV-style Book of Mormon text.)*

## Context

Nephi's closing chapter ("the doctrine of Christ") narrates Christ's baptism and
records the Father and the Son **speaking separately**, with the Father
repeatedly distinguishing himself from the Son ("my Beloved Son," "the words of
the Father unto the Son"). Saints Unscripted invokes this chapter — present
unchanged since the 1830 edition — as internal evidence that the Book of Mormon
was never strictly Trinitarian/modalist, defusing the claim that the
[[1 Nephi 11.18|1 Nephi 11:18]] and [[1 Nephi 11.21|1 Nephi 11:21]] edits cover
up an earlier one-person theology.

## Key Exegetical Point

- **Distinct persons in dialogue:** A modalist text would not stage the Father
  and Son in mutual address. The narrative presupposes a social Godhead, which is
  the standard LDS reading of the Godhead as three distinct persons united in
  purpose.
