# 1 Nephi 11:21

> And the angel said unto me: Behold the Lamb of God, yea, even the Son of the Eternal Father! *(current edition)*

**1830 first-edition reading:** "And the angel said unto me, Behold the Lamb of God, yea, even **the Eternal Father!**"

## Context

A few verses after [[1 Nephi 11.18|1 Nephi 11:18]], the angel identifies the
Lamb of God. The 1830 text equated the Lamb directly with "the Eternal Father";
the 1837 edition inserted "the Son of" to read "the Son of the Eternal Father."
This is the second of the paired Christological edits cited as evidence of an
evolving theology. Apologists note the Book of Mormon's broader theology already
calls Christ "the Father" in a representational sense (cf. Mosiah 15) and that
[[2 Nephi 31|2 Nephi 31]] clearly distinguishes the persons of the Father and Son,
undercutting a strict-Trinitarian reading of the original.

## Key Exegetical Point

- **Christ as "the Father":** Book of Mormon prophets use "Father" titles for
  the Son (divine investiture of authority; Mosiah 15:1–4 — Christ is "the Father
  and the Son"). The 1837 edit disambiguates the relationship rather than
  reversing a doctrine.
