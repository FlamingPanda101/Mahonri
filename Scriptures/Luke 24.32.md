# Luke 24:32

> And they said one to another, Did not our heart burn within us, while he talked with us by the way, and while he opened to us the scriptures? *(KJV)*

## Key Greek phrase
**ἡ καρδία ἡμῶν καιομένη ἦν (hē kardia hēmōn kaiomenē ēn)** — "our heart was burning." The disciples on the road to Emmaus respond to the risen Christ "opening" (διανοίγω) the scriptures.

## In context
[[Anthony Rogers]] cites this in the Q&A to rebut [[Trent Horn]]'s charge that his self-authentication argument is just a Mormon-style "burning in the bosom." Rogers distinguishes: the burning heart is *why* a person accepts the word that is *objectively* self-authenticating — the subjective work of the Spirit, not the ground of authority itself. He likens it to the sun shining whether or not anyone sees it; God "gives people eyes" to see the light. See the contrast Horn draws between this and Book-of-Mormon testimony.
