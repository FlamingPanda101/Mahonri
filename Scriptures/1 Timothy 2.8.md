# 1 Timothy 2.8 — "lifting up holy hands"

> I will therefore that men pray every where, lifting up holy hands, without wrath and doubting. *(1 Timothy 2:8, KJV)*

## Key Detail
- Paul's instruction to pray "lifting up holy hands" is the scriptural reflex of the early Christian **orant posture** — standing with arms raised and hands open — the most-depicted prayer stance in early Christian art (e.g., the third-century *Donna Velata* fresco). Used in [[Early Christian Prayer and the Orant Posture]] alongside 1 Kings 8:22 and Mark 11:25 to document continuity between biblical and post-apostolic prayer practice.
