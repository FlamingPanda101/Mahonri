# Jacob 3:8 — "their skins will be whiter than yours"

> O my brethren, I fear that unless ye shall repent of your sins that their skins will be whiter than yours, when ye shall be brought with them before the throne of God. *(Jacob 3:8, Book of Mormon)*

## Significance
Jacob (younger brother of [[Nephi]]) tells the Nephites not to "revile against them because of the darkness of their skins" (Jacob 3:9) and warns that the Lamanites' skins may be "whiter" at the judgment bar — imagery that cannot be literal pigment, since God judges righteousness, not appearance. In the **descriptive-vs-prescriptive** reading, Jacob's correction partly contradicts Nephi's harsher "skin of blackness" framing, showing the two are recording shifting prophetic viewpoints rather than a fixed divine racial decree.
