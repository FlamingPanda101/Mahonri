# 1 Nephi 4:9 — The sword of Laban

> And I beheld his sword, and I drew it forth from the sheath thereof; and the hilt thereof was of pure gold, and the workmanship thereof was exceedingly fine, and I saw that the blade thereof was of the most precious steel. *(1 Nephi 4:9)*

## Key Detail
Nephi describes **Laban's sword** — gold hilt, exceedingly fine workmanship, a blade of "the most precious steel" — before using it to slay the sleeping Laban (1 Nephi 4:18) and later carrying it as a pattern to make more swords (2 Nephi 5:14). In the [[Ward Radio - The Sword of Laban Was the Sword of Joshua|Don Bradley interview]] this is the relic whose deep history Bradley reconstructs: forged by [[Joseph of Egypt]], wielded by [[Joshua]] in the conquest, descending the Ephraimite/Josephite line to [[Laban]], then to [[Nephi]] (who, like [[David]], beheads his Goliath with the enemy's own sword). See [[The Sword of Laban Was the Sword of Joshua Forged by Joseph in Egypt]] and [[Nephi and Laban Reenact David and Goliath]].
