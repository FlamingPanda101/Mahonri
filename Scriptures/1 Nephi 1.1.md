# 1 Nephi 1:1 — "born of goodly parents"

> I, Nephi, having been born of goodly parents, therefore I was taught somewhat in all the learning of my father... *(1 Nephi 1:1, Book of Mormon)*

## Key Term
- **Nephi** — most plausibly from Egyptian *nfr* ("good, fine, beautiful, goodly"); cf. *Nefertiti*. The R drops in pronunciation, yielding *Nephi*. The self-introduction "born of **goodly** parents" plays on the meaning of the name. "Goodly" carries both a moral sense (good) and an aesthetic sense (fine, fair, comely) — both senses recur across Nephi's record and the wider text.

## Context
No other Book of Mormon figure is introduced as "born of goodly parents." The motif of goodness/goodliness bookends Nephi's writings from 1 Nephi 1:1 to the end of 2 Nephi (a **narrative inclusio**) and recurs in Helaman 5 and Mormon's "O ye fair ones" lament.
