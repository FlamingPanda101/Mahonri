# John 7:37-38 — Rivers of Living Water

> **37** …Jesus stood and cried, saying, If any man thirst, let him come unto me, and drink. **38** He that believeth on me, as the scripture hath said, out of his belly shall flow rivers of living water. (John 7:37–38)

Cited in the [[Salty Saints - Why Water Is Better Than Wine in the Sacrament|Salty Saints sacrament episode]] alongside [[John 4.10-14|John 4]] as part of David's case that **water out-symbolizes wine** for Christ: Jesus explicitly identifies himself as the source of living water that quenches thirst forever, tying the sacrament's water back to the Old Testament "fountain of living waters" imagery of Jehovah.[^1] See [[Water Is a Better Symbol of Christ Than Wine]].
