# Deuteronomy 18:15

> The LORD thy God will raise up unto thee a Prophet from the midst of thee, of thy brethren, like unto me; unto him ye shall hearken. *(KJV)*

## Key Hebrew term
**אֵלָיו תִּשְׁמָעוּן (’elaw tishma‘un)** — "to him you shall listen/hearken." The verb **שָׁמַע (shama‘)** means both to *hear* and to *obey*.

## In context
[[Anthony Rogers]] uses this text in cross-examination as a Scriptural criterion that "forward-projects": Moses pre-authorizes a future prophet (ultimately [[Jesus of Nazareth]]) whom the people must heed, and that prophet in turn ratifies the OT canon ("if you believed Moses, you'd believe me") and authorizes the apostles. [[Trent Horn]] presses that "hearken/listen" is an *oral* activity, so Deuteronomy 18 actually shows the word of God coming through a *spoken* authorized voice, not the written word alone — and that no such figure ever publicly enumerated the NT canon.
