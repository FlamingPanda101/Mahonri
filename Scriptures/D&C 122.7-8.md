# D&C 122:7–8 — "all these things shall give thee experience"

> Know thou, my son, that all these things shall give thee experience, and shall be for thy good. The Son of Man hath descended below them all. Art thou greater than he? *(D&C 122:7–8)*

## Key Detail
- The climax of the Liberty Jail revelation and, for [[David Paulsen]], the heart of Joseph Smith's answer to the **practical problem of evil**: suffering is instrumental ("for thy good"), and the sufferer is never beyond the reach of a Christ who "descended below them all." Links the lived theodicy back to the instrumentalist reading in [[Lehi's Theodicy - Opposition in All Things]].
