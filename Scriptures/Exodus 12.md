# Exodus 12

> And they shall take of the blood, and strike it on the two side posts and on the upper door post of the houses… And the blood shall be to you for a token upon the houses where ye are: and when I see the blood, I will pass over you. *(KJV, vv. 7, 13)*

## In context
The Passover institution, read typologically by [[Melito of Sardis]] in his Holy Week homily *On the Pasch*. Melito dwells on the mystery that Israel was saved by the blood of a slaughtered lamb on the doorposts — "the death of the sheep became a wall for the people" — and asks the angel what he truly respected: "the slaughter of the sheep or the life of the Lord?" For Melito the Passover lamb is only "a type, an impression, a sketch" of Christ, the true Passover lamb slain for the world's redemption.
