# Exodus 3:6

> Moreover he said, I am the God of thy father, the God of Abraham, the God of Isaac, and the God of Jacob. And Moses hid his face; for he was afraid to look upon God. *(KJV)*

## Key Hebrew term
- **מַלְאַךְ יְהוָה (malʾak YHWH)** — "the messenger/angel of Yahweh." In Exodus 3:2 it is *the messenger of Yahweh* who appears in the burning bush, yet by 3:6 the speaker declares in the first person, "**I am the God of thy father**" (**אָנֹכִי אֱלֹהֵי אָבִיךָ**, *anoki elohei avikha*). [[Dan McClellan]] regards the shift from messenger (v.2) to God (v.4, 6) as evidence of a later editorial insertion of *malʾak* into a story that originally had God himself appear.

## Significance
The second of two Hebrew Bible passages where the messenger of Yahweh explicitly identifies as God. McClellan stresses the messenger here is "far more explicit than Jesus ever is" — Jesus never says "I am the God of your father." He argues the explicit self-identification originates in textual interpolation (a redactor inserting *malʾak* to muddy the identity) and is rationalized by the intuitive logic of divine images, not by divine investiture or the Trinity.
