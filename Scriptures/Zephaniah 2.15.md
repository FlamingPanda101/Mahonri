# Zephaniah 2:15

> "This is the rejoicing city that dwelt carelessly, that said in her heart, I am, and there is none beside me: how is she become a desolation, a place for beasts to lie down in!" (KJV)

A prophecy against **Nineveh** that ironically puts the royal boast **"I am, and there is none beside me"** (Hebrew *ani we'afsi od*) into the *city's* mouth. Used to show that "none beside me" was a known Ancient Near Eastern idiom of supremacy/incomparability, not a literal denial that other entities (cities — or, by analogy, gods) exist.
