# 1 Corinthians 11:5

> But every woman that prayeth or prophesieth with her head uncovered dishonoureth her head: for that is all one as if she were shaven. *(KJV)*

## Significance
Paul here assumes women **do** pray and prophesy aloud in the assembly (regulating only their head-covering). [[Alex OConnor|Alex O'Connor]] uses the tension with [[1 Corinthians 14.34-35|1 Cor 14:34-35]] ("women keep silence") to argue either Paul contradicts himself within three chapters, or — his view — 14:34-35 is a non-Pauline interpolation. [[Glenn Scrivener]] uses the same tension the other way: 11:5 proves the "silence" of ch. 14 must be a contextual correction of disorderly worship, not a blanket ban.
