# Psalm 22

> My God, my God, why hast thou forsaken me? ... they pierced my hands and my feet ... they part my garments among them, and cast lots upon my vesture. (Psalm 22:1, 16, 18)

A psalm of lament opening with the cry Jesus quotes from the cross ("Eloi, Eloi, lama sabachthani," [[Matthew 27.46|Matthew 27:46]]) and containing imagery read as the crucifixion (pierced hands/feet, parted garments, casting lots).

## Relevance
On Ward Radio, [[Jonah Barnes]] reads Psalm 22 not as despair but as a **high-priestly altar-prayer** recited with hands raised — supporting the thesis that the Psalms were temple liturgy. He notes the Gospels' odd move of giving the Aramaic line *and then translating it*, arguing this signals a sacred, near-"unutterable" prayer (cf. [[3 Nephi 19|3 Nephi 17]]) rather than a plain quotation. The psalm thus links the crucifixion to the temple Day-of-Atonement sacrifice.
