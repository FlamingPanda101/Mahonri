# John 17:22

Part of Christ's intercessory ("high priestly") prayer:

> And the glory which thou gavest me I have given them; that they may be one, even as we are one. *(KJV)*

## Significance
A keystone deification / oneness text. Trinitarians cite verse 21–22 for the unity of the Father and the Son; Ward Radio (and the patristic-apotheosis case) presses the **second half**: Christ says believers are to be one *in the same way* he and the Father are one — divine, glorified union, not a "gelatinous" merging of substance. The hosts charge that apologists like [[Gavin Ortlund]] read the Father–Son oneness while skipping the parallel clause extending it to disciples. See [[All the Church Fathers Taught Apotheosis]] and compare [[John 17.20-23]].
