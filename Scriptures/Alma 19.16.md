# Alma 19:16 — Abish and the vision of her father

> And it came to pass that they did call on the name of the Lord, in their might, even until they had all fallen to the earth, save it were one of the Lamanitish women, whose name was Abish, she having been converted unto the Lord for many years, on account of a remarkable vision of her father— *(Alma 19:16, Book of Mormon)*

## Key Term
- **Abish** — analyzed as *Abi* ("my father") + *ish* ("man"), i.e. "my father is a man." Abish — one of only six women named in the Book of Mormon, and a servant/slave — is introduced "on account of a remarkable vision of her **father**," playing on the *Abi-* element of her name. The Hebrew construction leaves it ambiguous whether the father had the vision (subjective genitive) or whether Abish had a vision *of* her father (objective genitive); the latter could imply a postmortal appearance of an ancestor.
