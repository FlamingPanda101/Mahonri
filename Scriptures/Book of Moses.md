# Book of Moses

A book of LDS scripture in the [[Pearl of Great Price]], comprising eight chapters drawn from [[Joseph Smith]]'s inspired revision of Genesis (the Joseph Smith Translation), produced beginning in June 1830. It presents an expanded creation and primeval history — a [[Moses 1.1-2|heavenly ascent and visionary preface]] (chapter 1), creation accounts (chapters 2-3), the Fall and Satan's rebellion ([[Moses 4]]), Adam's gospel ([[Moses 5.10-11|chapter 5]]), and the dispensations of Adam, [[Enoch]] ([[Moses 6]], [[Moses 7]]), and [[Noah]] (chapter 8).

## As the lost preface to the Bible
In the *Let's Get Real* interview, [[Don Bradley]] argues that **Moses Chapter 1** is the divinely intended preface or "hidden key" to the Bible, given to Joseph Smith just before he began revising Genesis. It supplies the relational frame Genesis omits — that God is Father and the human is his child — and was, per the text, lost from the Bible "because of the wickedness of men." See [[Moses 1 Is the Lost Preface and Hidden Key to the Bible]].[^1]
