# Mark 1:11

> And there came a voice from heaven, saying, Thou art my beloved Son, in whom I am well pleased. *(KJV)*

## Key point
At Jesus's baptism, the Father addresses the Son directly: "Thou art my beloved Son." [[Don Bradley]] highlights that this comes in Mark — "the earliest gospel" — at the very event that *begins* Jesus's ministry, and that [[Jesus of Nazareth|Jesus]] then "goes and teaches others" that God is Father. He folds it into the dispensation-opening pattern (deity declares sonship at the head of each dispensation): God applies to Jesus the same "you are my [beloved] Son" declaration he makes over [[Adam]], [[Enoch]], [[Abraham]], [[Moses]], and [[Joseph Smith]] — indeed "that's even how God does it... he does it to his Son in that order." See [[Every Dispensation Opens With God Declaring Divine Sonship]].[^1]
