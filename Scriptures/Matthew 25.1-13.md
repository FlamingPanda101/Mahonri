# Matthew 25:1-13 — the parable of the ten virgins

> Then shall the kingdom of heaven be likened unto ten virgins, which took their lamps, and went forth to meet the bridegroom... they that were ready went in with him to the marriage: and the door was shut... Watch therefore, for ye know not the day nor the hour wherein the Son of man cometh. *(KJV)*

## Key phrase
"**They that were ready went in with him to the marriage: and the door was shut**" — five wise and five foolish bridesmaids; the foolish run out for oil and are locked out of the night-time wedding feast.

## Apologetic / typological application
[[Andrea Woodmansee]] reads this not as a free-standing allegory but as a description of a real ancient Hebrew wedding: the bride and attendants do not know "the day nor the hour" because the **groom** does not — only his father sets the wedding. The lamps, reserve oil, fall season, trumpet warning, and bolted door are wedding mechanics; the foolish virgins shut out in the dark are the "outer darkness." See [[The Parable of the Ten Virgins Encodes Hebrew Wedding Customs]] and [[The Day No Man Knoweth Is Rosh Hashanah Wedding Idiom]]. The "ten" matches the ten witnesses an ancient bride brought; the oil (pressed from olives) ties wisdom and the tree of life to the anointing of the bridegroom.
