# Matthew 7:29 — "as one having authority, and not as the scribes"

> For he taught them as one having authority, and not as the scribes. *(Matthew 7:29, KJV)*

## Key Detail
The crowd's reaction to the Sermon on the Mount is the hinge of [[Jared Halverson]]'s argument: the scribes back every claim with chapter and verse ("Moses says... Isaiah says..."), but Jesus teaches from his own authority. Halverson reads this as the difference between a *scribal/biblical* religion and a prophetic, revelatory one — Latter-day Saints, he says, "quote scripture when we talk to you because that's your approach," but theirs is not a scribal religion.
