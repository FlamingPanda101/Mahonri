# Matthew 16.18-19

> And I say also unto thee, That thou art Peter, and upon this rock I will build my church; and the gates of hell shall not prevail against it. And I will give unto thee the keys of the kingdom of heaven: and whatsoever thou shalt bind on earth shall be bound in heaven: and whatsoever thou shalt loose on earth shall be loosed in heaven.

## Why it's cited

This is the single most contested text in debates over church authority, papal primacy, and the keys of binding and loosing. Roman Catholic apologetics read "this rock" as Peter and the foundation of an unbroken papal office, while critics argue the rock is Peter's confession or Christ himself and that the keys are not transferable to a Roman see. Notes cite it to argue that declining to recognize the papacy is faithful protest rather than schism, reframing the passage's authority claim. It is therefore a hinge text for ecclesiology and the legitimacy of competing claims to apostolic succession.
