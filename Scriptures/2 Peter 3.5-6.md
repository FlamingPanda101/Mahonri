# 2 Peter 3:5–6 — an earth "formed out of water"

> "For this they willingly are ignorant of, that by the word of God the heavens were of old, and the earth standing out of the water and in the water: Whereby the world that then was, being overflowed with water, perished." (KJV)

**NRSV:** "They deliberately ignore this fact, that by the word of God heavens existed long ago and an earth was formed out of water and by means of water, through which the world of that time was deluged with water and perished."

## Key Detail

Peter describes the primeval creation as the heavens existing "of old" and the earth being **"formed out of water and by means of water"** (Greek: ἐξ ὕδατος καὶ δι᾽ ὕδατος συνεστῶσα, *ex hydatos kai di' hydatos synestōsa*). The verb συνίστημι (*synistēmi*, "to set together, compose, hold together") describes *composing/constituting* the earth out of a watery substrate, not summoning it from nothing.

For Latter-day Saint apologists this is a New Testament echo of the same primordial "waters" found in [[Genesis 1.1|Genesis 1:1]]–2 — the unformed chaos over which the Spirit hovers — and thus supports [[Creatio Ex Nihilo Is the Lynchpin Difference|creation *ex materia*]] (organizing pre-existing matter) over creation *ex nihilo*. The passage pairs the watery *origin* of the world with the watery *judgment* of the Flood, treating both as acts "by the word of God."

**Steelman (classical-theism reading):** The passage's purpose is eschatological — rebuking scoffers who deny a coming judgment — not a metaphysical claim about whether the water itself was created. A classical theist reads "formed out of water" as describing the *ordering* phase of a creation whose raw material (the water) God had already made *ex nihilo* in 3:5's "the heavens were of old… by the word of God." On this view the verse is silent on the eternity of matter and so does not, by itself, establish *ex materia*.
