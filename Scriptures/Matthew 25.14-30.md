# Matthew 25:14-30 — The Parable of the Talents

> **15** …unto one he gave five talents, to another two, and to another one… **24** Lord, I knew thee that thou art an hard man… **25** And I was afraid, and went and hid thy talent in the earth… **29** For unto every one that hath shall be given… but from him that hath not shall be taken away even that which he hath. (Matthew 25:14–30)

In the [[Salty Saints - Fact-Checking Johnny Harris's Harm Story|Salty Saints response to Johnny Harris]], the panel applies the parable to Harris himself: the Church "gave Johnny about as many talents as Johnny could hand" — charisma, industriousness, language, a stable family, a subsidized education — and instead of multiplying them in the kingdom he "just buried them," walking away while keeping the blessings. The parable frames their "net positive" ledger argument that Harris's own catalogue of gifts indicts his "harm" thesis.[^1] See [[The Net-Positive Ledger Rebuts Johnny Harris's Harm Thesis]].
