# Acts 10

> While Peter yet spake these words, the Holy Ghost fell on all them which heard the word... For they heard them speak with tongues, and magnify God. Then answered Peter, Can any man forbid water, that these should not be baptized, which have received the Holy Ghost as well as we? *(KJV, Acts 10:44-47)*

## Key phrase
"**The Holy Ghost fell on all them which heard the word... can any man forbid water?**" — Cornelius and his household receive the Spirit and speak in tongues *before* water baptism, the one NT narrative where the Spirit is described as temporally *prior* to the rite (Peter recounts it in Acts 11:15, "as on us at the beginning").

## Apologetic application
A central datum in the Ortlund–Horn dialogue. **[[Gavin Ortlund]]** treats Cornelius as evidence that regeneration begins *at faith*, not at baptism — and argues it fits the wider pattern of Acts, where the Spirit's outpouring is never tied to the moment of baptism. **[[Trent Horn]]** concedes it is the *only* such case and quarantines it as a unique **"boundary case"** whose purpose (per Acts 11) is to break the kingdom open to Gentiles, showing they are fitting recipients of baptism without first becoming Jews — an *exception* that does not nullify the rule, paralleling the good thief as an exception to salvation-by-faith. See [[The Catechumen Is Already Regenerate Before the Water]].
