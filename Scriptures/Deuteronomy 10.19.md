# Deuteronomy 10:19

> Love ye therefore the stranger: for ye were strangers in the land of Egypt. *(KJV)*

## Key Hebrew term
- **גֵּר (ger)** — "sojourner / resident alien," a free non-Israelite living among Israel. A *ger* is **not** an enslaved person (*eved*); the two are distinct legal categories in the Hebrew Bible.

## Significance
Joshua HS appealed to commands to "love the sojourner" as proof that enslaved people in the Hebrew Bible were granted rights and dignity. [[Dan McClellan]] responds that these passages are about the free *resident alien*, not slaves, and that Joshua is "squinting too hard at passages about sojourers and imagining that they're talking about enslaved folks when they're not." Conflating *ger* with *eved* is the central exegetical error behind the apologetic claim that biblical slavery was humane.
