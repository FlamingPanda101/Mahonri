# Enos 1:1-2 — "knowing my father that he was a just man"

> Behold, it came to pass that I, Enos, knowing my father that he was a just man—for he taught me in his language, and also in the nurture and admonition of the Lord—and blessed be the name of my God for it— And I will tell you of the wrestle which I had before God, before I received a remission of my sins. *(Enos 1:1-2, Book of Mormon)*

## Key Terms
- **Enos** — the Hebrew word *ʾenosh* (אֱנוֹשׁ), a poetic word for "man" (cf. *ish*, *adam*). Enos introduces himself "knowing my father that he was a just **man**," echoing the meaning of his own name.
- **the wrestle ... before God** — alludes to Genesis 32, where **Jacob** (Enos's father is named Jacob) wrestles a "**man**" (*ish*) at Peniel ("face of God"). Enos's "wrestle before God" deliberately reworks his father's namesake story. The plural built on *ʾenosh* (*ʾanashim*) appears in the Genesis account ("with God [Elohim] and with men [ʾanashim]").
