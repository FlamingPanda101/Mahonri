# Joshua 8:26

> For Joshua drew not his hand back, wherewith he stretched out the spear, until he had utterly destroyed all the inhabitants of Ai. *(KJV)*

## Key Hebrew term
**ḥērem (חֵרֶם)** — "the ban" / devotion to destruction: the commanded total annihilation of a population, often including women, children, and livestock (cf. Josh 8:24-25; Deut 20:16-18; 1 Sam 15:3). The destruction of Ai is a paradigm instance.

## Key point
[[Blake Ostler]] cites the ḥērem at Ai as the scriptural realization of the cruel horn of the [[The Euthyphro Dilemma Defeats Divine Command Theory|Euthyphro dilemma]]: if "good" simply means "whatever God commands," then God commanding the killing of "every living thing... little children, every moving thing like scorpions and beetles" was *good*. Ostler calls this "totally capricious... arbitrary," demonstrating that divine command theory makes morality groundless.
