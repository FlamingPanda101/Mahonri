# Deuteronomy 5.7

> Thou shalt have none other gods before me.

## Why it's cited

As the first commandment in Deuteronomy's restatement of the Decalogue, this verse is central to debates over Israelite monotheism versus monolatry — whether the text demands that no other gods *exist* or merely that none be *worshipped before* Yahweh. Apologetics and source-critical notes cite it when arguing that Deuteronomy's covenant form mirrors the exclusive-loyalty clauses of ancient Near Eastern vassal treaties, where a suzerain forbids the vassal from serving rival overlords. The phrasing therefore becomes evidence for reading Deuteronomy against a treaty background rather than as an abstract theological creed.
