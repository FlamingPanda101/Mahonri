# Psalm 102

> A Prayer of the afflicted, when he is overwhelmed... He will regard the prayer of the destitute, and not despise their prayer. (Psalm 102, title and v. 17, KJV)

A psalm of the afflicted that, in the Masoretic/KJV tradition, has God regarding "the **prayer** of the destitute."

## Relevance
On Ward Radio, [[Jonah Barnes]] notes that the Dead Sea Scrolls reading has God regarding "the **worm** of the destitute" rather than "the prayer of the destitute." Linking *worm* (cf. Mark 9:48, "where their worm dieth not") to the *disembodied spirit / the dead*, he argues the original verse had God hearing the prayers **of the dead** — a vicarious-work-for-the-dead and resurrection theme a later scribe obscured by repeating "prayer" (so "he regarded the prayer... and despised not their prayer"). The variant is offered as a "receipt" that the canonical Psalms were edited.
