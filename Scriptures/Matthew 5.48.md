# Matthew 5:48 (KJV)

> "Be ye therefore perfect, even as your Father which is in heaven is perfect."

**Key Greek term:** *téleios* (τέλειος) — "complete, mature, brought to its end/goal," not "flawless." The verb sense is closer to attaining the intended completeness or wholeness of a thing than to moral faultlessness, which is why some argue the command is about maturation toward a goal rather than an impossible demand for zero error. This linguistic point underwrites the apologetic that the verse fuels grace, not toxic perfectionism.
