# John 20:17 — My Father and Your Father, My God and Your God

> "Jesus saith unto her, Touch me not; for I am not yet ascended to my Father: but go to my brethren, and say unto them, I ascend unto my Father, and your Father; and to my God, and your God." (KJV)

The resurrected Jesus to Mary Magdalene. Day and Butler use this to argue Jesus consistently calls God "Father," shares that same Father with his "brethren," and even calls the Father "my God" — language they read against creedal *homoousios* monotheism and in favor of a literal divine family. "In what world is there a son and a father without a mother?" See [[Loss of the Divine Family Is the Core Apostasy]].
