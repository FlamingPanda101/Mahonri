# Helaman 3:7 — Nephite cement construction

> And there being but little timber upon the face of the land, nevertheless the people who went forth became exceedingly expert in the working of cement; therefore they did build houses of cement, in the which they did dwell. *(Helaman 3:7)*

## Key Detail
Part of the account (Helaman 3:4–7) of Nephites migrating to a land *northward* that had *little timber*, where they became "exceedingly expert in the working of cement." Long catalogued by critics as an anachronism — early readers assumed ancient Native Americans built no cement — the detail is now treated as a **reversed anachronism**: highly developed lime-concrete construction is confirmed in central Mexico (notably Teotihuacán) in the right era, including in a heavily deforested region, matching the passage's pairing of scarce timber with cement expertise. See [[Nephite Cement Construction Was Confirmed at Teotihuacan]] and [[The Three Types of Anachronism - Verified Alleged and Reversed]].

The same detail anchors the [[By His Fruits - Book of Mormon Anachronisms Explained and Debunked|Heber J. Grant cement story]], in which a young skeptic claimed the cement claim disproved the book and Grant predicted descendants would find the evidence.
