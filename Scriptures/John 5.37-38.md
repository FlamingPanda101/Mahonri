# John 5:37-38

> And the Father himself, which hath sent me, hath borne witness of me. Ye have neither heard his voice at any time, nor seen his shape. And ye have not his word abiding in you: for whom he hath sent, him ye believe not. *(KJV)*

## Significance
The "God Loves Mormons" video uses "ye have neither heard his voice... nor seen his shape" (and John 1:18, "no man hath seen God") to argue the Father is invisible/unknowable, against LDS embodiment and premortal sonship. The Ward Radio panel answers that this cannot be flatly true: Moses ([[Exodus 33.11|Exodus 33:11]]), [[Isaiah the Prophet|Isaiah]], and [[Jeremiah]] all reported seeing/hearing God. So either the theophanies were of a distinct divine Son (which undercuts strict Trinitarianism), or the verse is a rebuke to a specific unbelieving audience ("ye have not his word abiding in you"), not a metaphysical absolute. Jesus' "if God were your Father, ye would love me" (8:42) is likewise read as spiritual likeness, not biology.
