# Daniel 2 — Nebuchadnezzar's Statue and the Stone Kingdom

> And in the days of these kings shall the God of heaven set up a kingdom, which shall never be destroyed: and the kingdom shall not be left to other people, but it shall break in pieces and consume all these kingdoms, and it shall stand for ever. Forasmuch as thou sawest that the stone was cut out of the mountain without hands... *(Daniel 2:44-45, KJV)*

Nebuchadnezzar dreams of a statue with a head of gold, breast and arms of silver, belly and thighs of bronze, legs of iron, and feet of iron mixed with clay. Daniel interprets the metals as four successive kingdoms. A **stone cut without human hands** strikes the statue and grows into a mountain filling the earth.

## Key interpretive crux

The traditional Christian reading takes the four kingdoms as Babylon → Medo-Persia → Greece → **Rome**, so the "stone" kingdom God sets up "in the days of these kings" is the church, **established during the Roman Empire** and explicitly promised never to be destroyed nor "left to other people."

## In context

In the [[Capturing Christianity - Debate The Great Apostasy Joe Heschmeyer vs Jacob Hansen|Heschmeyer–Hansen debate]] this is [[Joe Heschmeyer]]'s signature argument against a Great Apostasy: Daniel 2 dates the rise of an indefectible kingdom to the *Roman* era, not 19th-century America, and "the kingdom shall not be left to other people" directly contradicts a centuries-long loss of authority requiring a Restoration. Heschmeyer also notes that the LDS proof for an indefectible Restoration (D&C 138:44) itself just cites Daniel 2. [[Jacob Hansen]] declines to defend any particular LDS reading of the chapter or to name the fourth kingdom.
