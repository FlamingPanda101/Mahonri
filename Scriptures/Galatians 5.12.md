# Galatians 5:11–12 — "I Would They Were Even Cut Off"

> "And I, brethren, if I yet preach circumcision, why do I yet suffer persecution? then is the offence of the cross ceased. I would they were even cut off which trouble you." (KJV)

**Key Greek:** *apokopsontai* (ἀποκόψονται) — the verb means "to cut off / amputate," and in this context is read by mainstream translators as a wish that the pro-circumcision agitators castrate / emasculate themselves. The NASB renders it "I wish that those who are troubling you would even mutilate themselves." The KJV "cut off" is comparatively bland and is sometimes mis-glossed as merely "excommunication."

Butler reads this verbatim (via [[Peter Enns]]'s *The Bible Tells Me So*) to demonstrate that Paul and the Jamesian/Judaizer party were not "sharing different perspectives" but were in vicious open conflict. See [[Paul vs James and the Judaizer Conflict]].
