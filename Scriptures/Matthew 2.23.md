# Matthew 2:23 (KJV)

> And he came and dwelt in a city called Nazareth: that it might be fulfilled which was spoken by the prophets, He shall be called a Nazarene.

## The Missing Prophecy

There is no passage in the Hebrew Bible that says "He shall be called a Nazarene." This is "a reference to a prophecy that we have no knowledge of."[^1]

## Proposed Explanations

Two traditional apologetic answers exist:

1. **Nazirite connection:** "Nazarene" is supposed to be like "Nazirite" (one under a nazirite vow). The Greek transliterations are very close, but there is no Hebrew Bible prophecy that the Messiah would be a Nazirite either, and the two concepts are "not even related conceptually."[^2]

2. **Isaiah 11:1 — the "branch" (netzer):** [[Isaiah 11.1|Isaiah 11:1]] says "a branch shall grow out of his roots" — the Hebrew word for "branch" is _netzer_ (נֵצֶר). The claim is that this is a cryptic reference to _Nazarene_. McClellan calls this "squinting at this pretty hard" to make the connection.[^3]

McClellan suggests Matthew may have been scrambling: "We got to have a prophecy here. Come on, let's find something. Netzer is like — I can work with that. Let's do Nazarene. That sounds like he's from Nazareth."[^3]
