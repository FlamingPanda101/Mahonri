# Deuteronomy 34:5-6

> So Moses the servant of the LORD died there in the land of Moab, according to the word of the LORD. And he buried him in a valley in the land of Moab, over against Bethpeor: but no man knoweth of his sepulchre unto this day. *(KJV)*

## In context

The final chapter of Deuteronomy — and thus of the Pentateuch — narrates Moses' death and burial twice (vv. 5-6 and the closing summary). LDS apologists on [[Ward Radio]] flag the passage as anomalous: Moses cannot have written his own death notice, the doubled "he died... he was buried" reads like an insistence that Moses is truly dead, and it **directly contradicts** the Book of Mormon ([[Alma 45.18-19|Alma 45]]) and New Testament tradition (Moses appears at the Transfiguration; [[Jude]] cites a dispute over Moses' body), which hold that Moses was **translated**. The argument: the original fifth book of Moses (the [[Assumption of Moses]]) had Moses taken up, and the Deuteronomist replaced it with a death-and-burial ending. See [[The Assumption of Moses Was the Fifth Book of Moses Replaced by Deuteronomy]].
