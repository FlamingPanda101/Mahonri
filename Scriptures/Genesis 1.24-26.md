# Genesis 1:24-26

> "And God said, Let the earth bring forth the living creature after his kind, cattle, and creeping thing, and beast of the earth after his kind: and it was so. And God made the beast of the earth after his kind, and cattle after their kind, and every thing that creepeth upon the earth after his kind: and God saw that it was good. And God said, Let us make man in our image, after our likeness: and let them have dominion over the fish of the sea, and over the fowl of the air, and over the cattle, and over all the earth, and over every creeping thing that creepeth upon the earth." (KJV)

**Hebrew (key terms):** נַעֲשֶׂה אָדָם בְּצַלְמֵנוּ כִּדְמוּתֵנוּ (*na'aseh adam be-tzalmenu ki-demutenu*, "let us make humankind in our image, after our likeness" — note the plural "us"); בְּהֵמָה (*behemah*, "cattle"), חַיְתוֹ־אֶרֶץ (*chayto erets*, "beast of the earth").

## Context

In the Priestly account, land animals ("cattle, creeping things, wild animals") are made on **day six, before** the creation of humanity, while fish and birds were made on earlier days. McClellan uses this to show the contradiction with [[Genesis 2.18-19|Genesis 2:18–19]], where all animals of the field and birds of the air are formed *after* the human.[^1] He also flags that the terminology between the two accounts overlaps but differs, "precisely because these are two different accounts coming from two different authors."[^2]
