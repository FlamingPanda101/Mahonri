# 1 Nephi 8

> "And it came to pass that I beheld a tree, whose fruit was desirable to make one happy... And I beheld a rod of iron, and it extended along the bank of the river, and led to the tree by which I stood. And I also beheld a strait and narrow path, which came along by the rod of iron... And I saw... a great and terrible gulf divided them; yea, even the **word** [original-manuscript reading per [[Royal Skousen]]: **sword**] of the justice of the eternal God." (1 Nephi 8, KJV-style text)

## Context

Lehi's dream/vision of the **tree of life**: a dark and dreary wilderness, a tree whose fruit is the love of God, a rod of iron along a river, a strait and narrow path, a great and spacious building, and a gulf separating the wicked from the righteous. Nephi later receives and interprets the same vision ([[1 Nephi 11.18|1 Nephi 11:18]] ff.).

## Key Exegetical Point

- **Temple/Eden typology.** [[Keystone - This Is Why LDS Temples Are Mindblowing|Keystone]] reads the dream as a recapitulation of the Eden-return pattern that structures the temple: the tree on an elevation "like the mountain of the Lord," the "fountain of living water" echoing Eden's river, and a flaming sword barring the wicked — see [[Lehi's Dream Recapitulates the Eden-Temple Return]] and [[The Garden of Eden Was a Prototype Sanctuary]].
- **"Sword" vs "word" variant.** Per [[Royal Skousen]]'s Critical Text Project, the divider was originally the **sword** of the justice of God ("the brightness thereof... like unto a flaming fire"), miscopied to "word." The sword reading sharpens the Genesis 3:24 (flaming-sword-at-Eden) parallel.
