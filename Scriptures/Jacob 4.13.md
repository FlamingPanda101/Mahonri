# Jacob 4:13

> Behold, my beloved brethren, I speak unto you these things that ye may rejoice, and lift up your heads forever, because of the blessings which the Lord God shall bestow upon your children. For I know that ye have searched much, many of you, to know of things to come; wherefore I know that ye know that our flesh must waste away and die; nevertheless, in our bodies we shall see God. ... the Spirit speaketh the truth and lieth not. Wherefore, it speaketh of things as they really are, and of things as they really will be. *(Book of Mormon)*

## In context
The host quotes the clause **"the Spirit speaketh the truth and lieth not; wherefore it speaketh of things as they really are"** to argue that the Spirit gives direct, undistorted contact with reality — knowledge of "things as they really are" — even when human language and reason struggle to express that experience. It backs his claim that experience can yield certainty even where interpretation/wording can fail. (The transcript loosely attributes the line to "Jacob.")
