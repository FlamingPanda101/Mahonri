# Moses 6:31

> And when Enoch had heard these words, he bowed himself to the earth, before the Lord, and spake before the Lord, saying: Why is it that I have found favor in thy sight, and am but a lad, and all the people hate me; for I am slow of speech; wherefore am I thy servant? *(Moses 6:31, Pearl of Great Price)*

At his prophetic call, [[Enoch]] objects that he is "but a lad."

## Why it's cited
The Ward Radio panel argues this is the **only** time [[Joseph Smith]] ever used the word "lad" in any of his writings — and that Enoch is repeatedly called *Petit Anush* ("little Enoch, the lad," *katan*) in the Mandaean Enoch tradition (French, 1856) and the Bet ha-Midrash, texts unavailable to Joseph in 1830. In the Sin-of-the-Giants episode the "lad" detail is tied to a claimed 65-year cessation of births (Enoch as a rare natural birth).
