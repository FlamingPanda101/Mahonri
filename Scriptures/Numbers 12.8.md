# Numbers 12:8

> With him will I speak mouth to mouth, even apparently, and not in dark speeches; and the similitude of the LORD shall he behold: wherefore then were ye not afraid to speak against my servant Moses? *(KJV)*

## Key Hebrew Terms

- **תְּמוּנַת יְהוָה (temunat YHWH):** "the form/likeness of the LORD," which Moses beholds — implying God has a visible form. The same word *temunah* is used in the image prohibitions, sharpening the tension.
- **פֶּה אֶל־פֶּה (peh el-peh):** "mouth to mouth," parallel to the "face to face" intimacy attributed to Moses (cf. Exod 33:11), against [[Exodus 33.20|Exodus 33:20]].

## Context

God defends Moses to Aaron and Miriam, asserting that unlike other prophets (who receive visions and dreams), Moses speaks with God directly and beholds "the form of the LORD." [[Dan McClellan]] cites this — alongside Moses speaking with God "face to face, like a man speaks with his friend" — as another text where God is genuinely seen, conflicting with the "cannot see God and live" tradition. See [[Conflicting Traditions on Seeing Gods Face]].
