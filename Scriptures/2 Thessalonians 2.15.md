# 2 Thessalonians 2:15

> Therefore, brethren, stand fast, and hold the traditions which ye have been taught, whether by word, or our epistle. *(KJV)*

## Key Greek term
**παραδόσεις (paradoseis)** — "traditions, things handed over." Paul commands the Thessalonians to hold traditions delivered **either by word (διὰ λόγου) or by letter (δι' ἐπιστολῆς)** — explicitly pairing oral and written transmission as equally binding.

## In context
A cornerstone Catholic verse. [[Trent Horn]] cites it to argue apostolic tradition has two valid modes — oral and written — and that Scripture nowhere consolidates revelation to the written form alone. Horn pairs it with [[2 Thessalonians 2.6]] (the unwritten teaching about "what restrains the man of lawlessness," which Paul reminded them of orally but never wrote down) to show the apostles did *not* commit all their teaching to writing. [[Anthony Rogers]] replies the apostles always preached *from* the scriptures and that oral preaching was committed to writing per God's consistent pattern.
