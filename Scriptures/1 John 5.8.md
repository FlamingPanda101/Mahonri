# 1 John 5:8 — the earthly witnesses (spirit, water, blood)

> And there are three that bear witness in earth, the Spirit, and the water, and the blood: and these three agree in one. *(KJV)*

## Key Detail

This is the verse that the **earliest Greek manuscripts read immediately after v. 6** — with no [[1 John 5.7|1 John 5:7]] heavenly-witnesses clause between them. On the panel's reading of the three great uncials (**Codex Vaticanus** image 1441, **Codex Sinaiticus**, **Codex Alexandrinus**), the Greek runs from the end of v. 6 (*alḗtheia*, "truth") straight into v. 8's triad: *pneuma* (spirit), *hydōr* (water), *haima* (blood). "The original Greek skips from verse six to verse eight. There is no verse seven." [^1] The [[1 John 5-7 - Johannine Comma Interpolation|Comma Johanneum]] was later wedged *in front of* this verse, borrowing its "these three... agree in one" structure to manufacture a parallel heavenly triad of Father, Word, and Holy Ghost.

The original v. 8, read on its own, is not a statement about the inner life of the Godhead at all but about the threefold testimony to Jesus' messiahship within John's argument — a reading that disappears once the Trinitarian gloss is bolted on.
