# Moses 1.1-2

> The words of God, which he spake unto Moses at a time when Moses was caught up into an exceedingly high mountain, and he saw God face to face, and he talked with him, and the glory of God was upon Moses; therefore Moses could endure his presence.
> — Moses 1:1-2 (Pearl of Great Price)

## Key point
[[Don Bradley]] uses these opening verses to contrast the [[Book of Moses]] with [[Genesis 1.1|Genesis 1:1]]: Genesis opens in an **anonymous third-person voice** ("In the beginning God created..."), naming neither speaker nor setting, whereas Moses 1 opens with God speaking in the *first person*, names the hearer ([[Moses]]), locates the scene on a mountain (cueing a temple context), and explains that Moses is **transfigured** ("the glory of God was upon Moses") so he can endure God's presence. This transfiguration is later the basis of Moses' discernment of Satan (see [[Discernment Comes From Prior Experience of God's Glory]]). The verses establish *who is speaking* and *where*, the front matter Bradley treats as the Bible's missing preface — see [[Moses 1 Is the Lost Preface and Hidden Key to the Bible]].[^1]

The chapter then proceeds (vv. 3-7) to the formula "I am the Lord God Almighty... thou art my son," the deity-then-sonship order traced across dispensations in [[Every Dispensation Opens With God Declaring Divine Sonship]].
