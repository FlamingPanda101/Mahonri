# Romans 5:8

> But God commendeth his love toward us, in that, while we were yet sinners, Christ died for us. *(KJV)*

## Key phrase
"**While we were yet sinners**" — Greek **ἔτι ἁμαρτωλῶν ὄντων ἡμῶν**. The temporal force ("while still") is the load-bearing word: Christ's death precedes repentance, covenant obedience, or any worthiness criterion.

## Apologetic application
[[Gavin (Catholic Debater)]] cites this (alongside "Rom 8") to argue God's benevolence is **prior to and independent of human response** — "He didn't die for us after repentance... not after all we can do, but while we were still in sin." Used against "conditional access" / worthiness-gated models of grace. See [[Omnibenevolence Is Unconditional Grace Not a Contract]].
