# Luke 2:39 (KJV)

> And when they had performed all things according to the law of the Lord, they returned into Galilee, to their own city Nazareth.

## The "Own City" Problem

The Greek phrase translated "their own city" here is identical to the phrase in [[Luke 2.2-3|Luke 2:3]] where "everyone went to his own city" for the census. In Luke 2:39, the "own city" is Nazareth — not Bethlehem. This means either:

1. The census required travel to one's _current_ hometown, in which case Joseph and Mary left their own town (Nazareth) for Bethlehem — defeating the census's purpose; or
2. The census required travel to one's _ancestral_ hometown, in which case Luke inconsistently calls Nazareth "their own city" at the end of the passage.[^1]

## Contradiction with Matthew

Luke 2:39 has Mary and Joseph peacefully returning to Nazareth after fulfilling temple requirements — with no mention of flight to Egypt, slaughter of innocents, or fear of Archelaus. In Matthew's account, they only settle in Nazareth after returning from Egypt and discovering Archelaus rules Judea, making Bethlehem unsafe. Luke does not seem to know they were "literally chased out of the country by the king."[^2]
