# Ether 8:13-18

> "And it came to pass that Akish gathered in unto the house of Jared all his kinsfolk . . . And they all sware unto him, by the God of heaven, and also by the heavens, and also by the earth, and by their heads, that whoso should vary from the assistance which Akish desired should lose his head . . . And it came to pass that they formed a secret combination, even as they of old; which combination is most abominable and wicked above all, in the sight of God . . . For behold, it is they who did . . . carry them about in their hearts; yea, and Akish administered it unto them with the oaths which were given by them of old."

## Context

The founding of the Jaredite **secret combinations**: Akish, prompted by Jared's daughter, binds his kindred by oath into a murderous conspiracy to seize the kingdom. Moroni interjects that this is the same combination "even as they of old" (cf. Cain), warning the latter-day reader against it.

## Significance

[[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)|David Butler]] pairs this oath with **Matthew 5:33-37**, where Jesus forbids swearing "by heaven . . . by the earth . . . by Jerusalem . . . by thy head." The Jaredite conspirators swear by **exactly the forbidden list** — by the heavens, the earth, and their heads — *minus* Jerusalem (which they, in the New World, would not invoke). On Butler's reading this is a **dark-mirror inversion**: the righteous covenant ordinance teaches initiates the simple yes/no oath, while the [[The Forbidden Oath Is the Dark Mirror of the Covenant Oath|robbers' counterfeit]] is the elaborate self-maledictory oath of the conspiracy. The Gadianton/Jaredite robbers are the "dark mirror image" of the Book of Mormon's initiatic Visionary men. See [[The Forbidden Oath Is the Dark Mirror of the Covenant Oath]].
