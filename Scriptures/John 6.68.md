# John 6:68

> Then Simon Peter answered him, Lord, to whom shall we go? thou hast the words of eternal life. *(KJV)*

## Key phrase
"**Lord, to whom shall we go?**" — Spoken after many disciples were offended by a hard teaching and "went back, and walked no more with him" (John 6:66). Jesus asks the Twelve, "Will ye also go away?" Peter's answer reframes departure as a comparative question: leaving is not automatically an upgrade, because there is nowhere better to go — Christ alone has "the words of eternal life."

## Apologetic application
This is the implicit scriptural backbone of [[Jacob Hansen]]'s video [[Thoughtful Faith - Where Will You Go - Confronting the Meaning Crisis and the Deconstruction of Belief]] — the very title "Where will you go?" echoes Peter's question. Hansen's ocean allegory makes the same move: abandoning faith (the boat) for the open water is not freedom but drift toward nihilism, so the honest question for anyone deconstructing is *toward what?* Peter's logic is that of [[Where Will You Go - Deconstruction Rarely Leads Somewhere Better]]: the disciples who left had no superior destination. Empirically, see [[Most Who Leave the LDS Church Do Not Become Atheists]].
