# Jacob 4:4-5

> For, for this intent have we written these things, that they may know that we knew of Christ, and we had a hope of his glory many hundred years before his coming; and not only we ourselves had a hope of his glory, but also all the holy prophets which were before us. Behold, they believed in Christ and worshiped the Father in his name... it being a similitude of God and his Only Begotten Son. *(Jacob 4:4-5, Book of Mormon)*

[[Jacob (Book of Mormon)|Jacob]] testifies that the pre-Christian prophets knew of Christ, and reads Abraham's offering of [[Isaac]] as "a similitude of God and his Only Begotten Son."

## Why it's cited
- A Book of Mormon statement of pre-600-BC messianism, paralleling [[Abinadi]]'s prophetic knowledge of Christ — used in the brass-plates / apocryphal-Isaiah argument.
- The explicit "similitude of God and his Only Begotten Son" reading of the Akedah is the LDS textual anchor for the binding-of-Isaac-as-Christ-type discussion ([[Genesis 22]]).
