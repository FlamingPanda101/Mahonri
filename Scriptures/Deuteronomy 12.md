# Deuteronomy 12 — the one place for the name

> But unto the place which the LORD your God shall choose out of all your tribes to put his name there, even unto his habitation shall ye seek... Take heed to thyself that thou offer not thy burnt offerings in every place that thou seest: But in the place which the LORD shall choose... there thou shalt offer thy burnt offerings. *(KJV, Deut 12:5, 13-14)*

## Key Detail
Deuteronomy 12 is the charter text of **centralized worship**: sacrifice is permitted only at "the place which the LORD shall choose to put his name there" — one place (assumed to be Jerusalem, though the chapter never names it). [[Mike Day]] treats this as a concentrated statement of the Deuteronomic program — no altars outside the chosen place, God present only where his *name* dwells (a "name theology" that distances God from bodily presence) — and as one of the claims the Book of Mormon *reverses*: Lehi and Nephi build altars in the wilderness and see God directly. The chapter is central to the [[Hezekiah Josiah and the Deuteronomist Reforms|Josianic reform]] and the broader [[Deuteronomistic Corruption of the Old Testament|Deuteronomistic editing]].
