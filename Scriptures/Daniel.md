# Daniel

**What:** Apocalyptic book set in the Babylonian exile (6th century BCE). In the Jewish [[The Hebrew Bible and the Christian Old Testament Are Not the Same Book|Tanakh]] it is placed among the Ketuvim (Writings), not the Prophets.

**Dating:** The critical consensus holds Daniel is the **youngest book in the Hebrew Bible**, composed in the **160s BCE** during the Maccabean Revolt against [[Antiochus IV Epiphanes]]. Its "prophecies" of successive empires are read as *vaticinium ex eventu* (prophecy after the fact) written to encourage 2nd-century Jews under persecution. See [[The Book of Daniel Was Written in the Second Century BCE]].

**Theological significance:** [[Daniel 12|Daniel 12]] contains the Hebrew Bible's clearest statement of bodily resurrection and graded postmortem reward/punishment — part of the Hellenistic-era emergence of the afterlife as theodicy (see [[Divine Punishment in the Afterlife Emerged as Theodicy Under Imperial Oppression]]).
