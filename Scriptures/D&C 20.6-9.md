# D&C 20:6-9 — "The Fullness of the Gospel"

> ...after it was truly manifested unto this first elder that he had received a remission of his sins, he was entangled again in the vanities of the world; but after repenting, and humbling himself sincerely, through faith, God ministered unto him by an holy angel, whose countenance was as lightning, and whose garments were pure and white above all other whiteness; and gave unto him commandments which inspired him; and gave him power from on high, by the means which were before prepared, to translate the Book of Mormon; which contains a record of a fallen people, and the fulness of the gospel of Jesus Christ to the Gentiles and to the Jews also. *(Doctrine and Covenants)*

## Key Detail
This passage is the textual source for the line in the [[Book of Mormon]]'s introduction (added in 1981) that the book "contains the fulness of the everlasting Gospel." On [[Ward Radio - Pearl of Great Price Explained|Ward Radio]], [[Jonah Barnes]] argues this is widely misread: although D&C 20 was *written* in 1829, verses 6–9 are **recounting [[Angel Moroni|Moroni]]'s 1823 visitation** (the angel of "lightning" countenance and garments "white above all whiteness"), describing the plates as they then were — not certifying that the *published* 1829 translation is doctrinally complete. The same 1823 description appears in [[Joseph Smith History 1.68-69|Joseph Smith—History]]. Because the lost 116 pages and the sealed portion were never published, "by 1829 we only get 60% of" the 1823 record — which Barnes uses to motivate his theory that the [[Book of Moses]] recovers lost Genesis content. See [[The Book of Mormon Fullness of the Gospel Is a Misread of D&C 20 9]].
