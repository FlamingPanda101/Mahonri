# Psalm 51:5

> Behold, I was shapen in iniquity; and in sin did my mother conceive me. *(KJV)*

## Key phrase
"**In sin did my mother conceive me**" — David's confession of sinfulness from conception, a key proof-text in the Western doctrine of original sin.

## Apologetic application
Cited as a scriptural motivation behind infant baptism: if humans are sinful from conception, infants need the regeneration baptism confers. [[Augustine]] leaned on this logic; [[Trent Horn]] notes infant baptism and original sin rise and fall together historically (the same skeptical arguments target both). The LDS tradition reads such language as describing inherited *mortality/weakness*, not inherited *guilt* (cf. the Pelagian side resolved by Restoration scripture). See [[Infant Salvation and the Pelagius-Augustine Dispute over Original Sin]] and [[Augustine Clarified Infant Baptism He Did Not Invent It]].
