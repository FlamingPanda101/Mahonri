# Genesis 1:5

> "And God called the light Day, and the darkness he called Night. And the evening and the morning were the first day." (KJV)

The refrain "evening and morning, the Nth day" structures the Priestly creation week. A long-noted exegetical puzzle: there is "evening and morning, day and night" on Day One, yet the sun, moon, and stars are not created until Day Four ([[Genesis 1.11-13|Gen 1:14-19]]). The Hebrew *yom* ("day") therefore cannot straightforwardly mean a sun-measured 24-hour period here. [[Origen]] cited exactly this to deny that the six days were literal 24-hour periods.
