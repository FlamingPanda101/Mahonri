# 2 Nephi 11:2-3

> And now I, Nephi, write more of the words of Isaiah, for my soul delighteth in his words. For I will liken his words unto my people, and I will send them forth unto all my children, for he verily saw my Redeemer, even as I have seen him. And my brother, Jacob, also has seen him as I have seen him; wherefore, I will send their words forth unto my children to prove unto them that my words are true. *(2 Nephi 11:2-3, Book of Mormon)*

[[Nephi]] introduces his long Isaiah quotation by stating that Isaiah "verily saw my Redeemer, even as I have seen him," and that his brother Jacob saw him too.

## Why it's cited
The Ward Radio panel reads "even as I have seen him" as Nephi reporting that he, Jacob, and Isaiah underwent the *same* visionary experience — a "modular" ascension/temple vision. They pair it with the parallels between [[1 Nephi 11]] (Nephi's tree-of-life vision) and the apocryphal **Ascension of Isaiah**, arguing Nephi knew an Isaiah tradition (a fuller, brass-plates Isaiah) that the surviving Old Testament lost.
