# 1 Corinthians 5:9–10 — The Lost "Previous" Corinthian Letter

> **I wrote unto you in an epistle** not to company with fornicators: Yet not altogether with the fornicators of this world... *(KJV)*

## Significance
Paul refers to an **earlier letter to Corinth that is now lost** — so what is called "1 Corinthians" is, on this reading, technically a *second* letter. [[Robert Boland]] (quoting [[Kelly (Brain Perspective)|Kelly Powers]], Calvin, and Reformed apologist Robert Bowman, who all grant the letter was genuine and inspired yet not preserved) uses this to argue that an inspired, God-breathed apostolic text once existed *outside* the 66-book canon — undercutting the claim that the canon exhausts inspired scripture (*tota scriptura*). See [[The Canon-as-Article-of-Faith Dilemma]].
