# D&C 50

> And that which doth not edify is not of God, and is darkness. That which is of God is light; and he that receiveth light, and continueth in God, receiveth more light; and that light groweth brighter and brighter until the perfect day. ... Wherefore, he is possessor of all things; for all things are subject unto him, both in heaven and on the earth ... But no man is a possessor of all things except he be purified and cleansed from all sin. And if ye are purified and cleansed from all sin, ye shall ask whatsoever you will in the name of Jesus and it shall be done. *(D&C 50:23-24, 27-29)*

## Key passage: light groweth brighter, and asking in purity
A revelation on discerning spirits — distinguishing what is "of God" (light, edifying) from darkness. [[Todd McLauchlin]] reads vv. 23-29 as one continuous teaching on the [[The True Order of Prayer Is the Heartbeat of the Endowment|true order of prayer]]: receiving light grows it "brighter and brighter," ordination is the call to fulfill that order, the end-state is being "possessor of all things," and the gating condition is being "purified and cleansed from all sin" — only then is one told what to ask and given it. The closing instruction (if you behold a spirit you cannot understand, ask the Father in the name of Jesus) is read as "how to chase darkness from among you" through the true order.
