# Ephesians 3:20–21

> Now unto him that is able to do exceeding abundantly above all that we ask or think, according to the power that worketh in us, Unto him be glory in the church by Christ Jesus throughout all ages, world without end. Amen. *(KJV)*

## Used against the Church (by Catholic apologists)
[[Trent Horn]] cites this alongside [[Matthew 28.20|Matthew 28:20]] as an indefectibility text: glory is to be given to God "in the church... throughout all ages," which (he argues) is incompatible with a total apostasy of that church. See [[The New Testament Never Predicts a Total Apostasy]].

## LDS reading
Latter-day Saints grant that no text predicts the disappearance of every believer; they locate the apostasy in the loss of priesthood keys and the divine-family/temple pattern, recovered in the Restoration across dispensations. See [[Loss of the Divine Family Is the Core Apostasy]].
