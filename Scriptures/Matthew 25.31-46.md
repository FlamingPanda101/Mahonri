# Matthew 25:31-46

> When the Son of man shall come in his glory... before him shall be gathered all nations: and he shall separate them one from another, as a shepherd divideth his sheep from the goats: And he shall set the sheep on his right hand, but the goats on the left. *(KJV)*

## Key phrase
"**He shall separate them... as a shepherd divideth his sheep from the goats**" — the climactic final-judgment parable of Jesus's apocalyptic Mount of Olives sermon.

## Apologetic / historical application
[[Nicholas J. Frederick]] notes this parable's prominence in early Christian funerary and apse art: a late third/early fourth-century sarcophagus lid depicts the Lord separating sheep (on his right) from goats (on his left), and a later mosaic shows "Jesus as judge, dividing the sheep (the righteous) from the goats (the wicked)." It expresses the early Christian conviction in a coming divine judgment at the Second Coming.
