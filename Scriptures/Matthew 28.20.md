# Matthew 28:20

> Teaching them to observe all things whatsoever I have commanded you: and, lo, I am with you alway, even unto the end of the world. Amen. *(KJV)*

## Used against the Church (by Catholic apologists)
[[Trent Horn]] cites this as an **indefectibility** prooftext: Christ promised to remain with his church "always, until the end of the age," so the church he founded could not have undergone a total Great Apostasy. See [[The New Testament Never Predicts a Total Apostasy]].

## LDS reading
Latter-day Saints read the promise as given to the apostolic mission (the Great Commission), renewed when priesthood authority is restored, and not as a guarantee against the loss of priesthood keys and temple/family theology. The apostasy is framed as a gradual loss of authority, not the extinction of all believers — see [[Apostasy as Gradual Falling Away Not Single Event]] and [[Apostasy Was Complete When the Keys Departed]].
