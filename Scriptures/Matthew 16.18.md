# Matthew 16:18-19

> And I say also unto thee, That thou art Peter, and upon this rock I will build my church; and the gates of hell shall not prevail against it. And I will give unto thee the keys of the kingdom of heaven: and whatsoever thou shalt bind on earth shall be bound in heaven: and whatsoever thou shalt loose on earth shall be loosed in heaven. *(KJV)*

## Key Greek terms
**πέτρα (petra)** — "rock"; **δήσῃς / λύσῃς (dēsēs / lysēs)** — "bind / loose." Catholics read the binding-and-loosing grant as conferring teaching and disciplinary authority on Peter and the apostles.

## In context
[[Trent Horn]] grounds the church's authority to *authoritatively declare* the canon in this binding/loosing grant, and cites "the gates of hell will not prevail" to argue Christ established a perpetual, visible, indefectible church to guide believers — not merely a book. [[Anthony Rogers]] replies "there's no papal office in scripture ... all that stuff is being read into texts like Matthew 16."
