# Ezekiel 28 (KJV excerpt)

> "Son of man, take up a lamentation upon the king of Tyrus, and say unto him... Thou hast been in Eden the garden of God; every precious stone was thy covering... Thou art the anointed cherub that covereth... Thou wast perfect in thy ways from the day that thou wast created, till iniquity was found in thee." *(Ezekiel 28:12-15)*

**Note:** A lament over the **king of Tyre**, using a version of a Garden-of-Eden story to portray that *human* king as the prototypical first man, fallen through hubris. Like [[Isaiah 14.12-15|Isaiah 14]] (the king of Babylon), it is read by later Christian tradition as a description of Satan's fall, but on its own terms it is about a human ruler, not a divine rebel. No figure here is named or identified as Satan.
