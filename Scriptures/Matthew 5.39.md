# Matthew 5.39

> But I say unto you, That ye resist not evil: but whosoever shall smite thee on thy right cheek, turn to him the other also.

## Why it's cited

From the Sermon on the Mount, this verse is the locus classicus for Jesus' teaching on non-retaliation, and the specific detail of the *right* cheek has generated extensive comment. Apologetic notes cite it to argue that a backhanded blow to the right cheek carried ritual or honor-shame significance in the ancient world, connecting it to a proposed lost temple ordinance of smiting. It is also paired with Book of Mormon parallels (such as Alma 14) to argue for an ancient cultural backdrop behind the gesture rather than a purely ethical maxim.
