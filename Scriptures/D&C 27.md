# D&C 27 — Communion With the Saints Who Have Died

A revelation on the sacrament in which an angel tells Joseph Smith that he will one day partake of the sacrament with a "who's who" of past prophets and Saints who have died — Moroni, Elias, John, Peter, James, Adam (Michael), and others.

Day connects this to the Eastern Orthodox understanding of the Eucharist: in worship before the iconostasis one is in **communion with the saints, living and dead**. He superimposes that architectural/liturgical idea onto D&C 27 and the LDS temple, where Saints are likewise "in communion with the dead." See [[The Didache as Earliest Church Manual]] and [[Apostasy Preserved the Text Steelman]].

**Greek note:** *Eucharist* < *eu* ("good") + *charis* ("grace") — entering the good grace of / communion with Christ and the apostles.
