# John 13:34

> A new commandment I give unto you, That ye love one another; as I have loved you, that ye also love one another. *(KJV)*

## Key Greek term
**agapē (ἀγάπη)** — self-giving love. Note the pattern "as I have loved you": the standard of love is Christ's *modeled* action, not a bare command. The "new" commandment (*entolēn kainēn*) supersedes the Mosaic code.

## Key point
[[Blake Ostler]] makes this the textual heart of his claim that the **law of love is the single ground of morality**. Crucially, Christ does not say "love because I command it"; he says "love as I have loved you" — so love is taught by demonstration, grounding the argument that [[Love Transcends Moral Obligation|love transcends moral obligation]] and is not a divine-command artifact.
