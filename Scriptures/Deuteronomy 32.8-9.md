# Deuteronomy 32:8-9

> When the most High divided to the nations their inheritance, when he separated the sons of Adam, he set the bounds of the people according to the number of the children of Israel. For the LORD's portion is his people; Jacob is the lot of his inheritance. *(KJV)*

## Textual Note — "sons of God / Israel"
The KJV (following the Masoretic Text) reads "according to the number of the **children of Israel**." The Dead Sea Scrolls (4QDeut^j) and the Septuagint read "according to the number of the **sons of God** (*bene elohim* / *angelon theou*)," which most scholars regard as the older reading. On this reading, the **Most High (Elyon)** apportions the nations among the divine **sons of God** as patron deities, while keeping Israel (Jacob) as Yahweh's own personal portion.

Key Hebrew terms:
- **Elyon** (עֶלְיוֹן) — "Most High"
- **bene elohim** (בְּנֵי אֱלֹהִים) — "sons of God" (the divine-council members)

## Relevance to the Divine Council
This text underlies the divine-council framework that **[[Dan McClellan]]** invokes to read [[Psalm 82.6|Psalm 82:6]]: the nations each had a patron deity, and Israel belonged to Yahweh. When Psalm 82 deposes the patron deities for injustice, the psalmist can then call on Yahweh to "inherit all nations," because the other gods' seats have been vacated.
