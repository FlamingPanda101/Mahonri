# Exodus 15:11

> "Who is like unto thee, O LORD, among the gods? who is like thee, glorious in holiness, fearful in praises, doing wonders?" (KJV)

From the Song of the Sea. [[Dan McClellan]] cites it as the paradigm of the ancient Near Eastern **rhetoric of incomparability** — "Who is like you among the gods?" is a direct comparison that ranks Yahweh above the other gods, not a denial that they exist. The same incomparability rhetoric appears in Egyptian and Mesopotamian texts praising a favored deity. See [[Rhetoric of Incomparability Is Not Monotheism]].
