# Alma 14.14 — Alma and Amulek smitten on the cheek at Ammonihah

> Now it came to pass that when the bodies of those who had been cast into the fire were consumed... they took Alma and Amulek, and carried them forth to the prison... And it came to pass after they had thus suffered for many days... the chief judge... came and stood before them, and smote them on their cheeks, and said unto them: After what ye have seen, will ye preach again unto this people...? *(Alma 14:14, 18-19; cf. 14:14-25, Book of Mormon)*

## Key Detail
- At Ammonihah the chief judge and the city's elite **smite the bound Alma and Amulek on the cheek**, "again and again" — by the episode's count, seven times — and only then are the prisoners delivered. [[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)|David Butler]] reads this as a desecrated [[A Ritual Smiting of the Cheek Was a Lost Temple Ordinance|temple smiting-rite]] (the "humbled-then-enthroned" pattern), the sevenfold count matching Nibley's "seven ritual blows." A critic reads it as plain persecutory cruelty. Compare [[Helaman 4.12|Helaman 4:12]] and [[Matthew 5.39|Matthew 5:39]].
