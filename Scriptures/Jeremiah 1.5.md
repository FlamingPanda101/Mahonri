# Jeremiah 1:5

> Before I formed thee in the belly I knew thee; and before thou camest forth
> out of the womb I sanctified thee, and I ordained thee a prophet unto the
> nations.

## Key Hebrew Terms

- **אֶצָּורְךָ (etzorekha)** — "I formed thee" (from יָצַר, *yatzar*, to
  form/fashion, as a potter forms clay). This verb is used of divine creative
  activity (cf. Genesis 2:7, God "formed" man from the dust).
- **יְדַעְתִּיךָ (yeda'tikha)** — "I knew thee" (from יָדַע, *yada*, to
  know). This is not mere intellectual awareness but intimate, covenantal,
  personal knowledge (the same root used for "Adam knew Eve" = conception).
- **בַבֶּטֶן (ba-beten)** — "in the belly/womb." The preposition "before"
  (בְּטֶרֶם, *b'terem*) preceding this indicates a time prior to the
  formation in the womb.

## Exegetical Significance

The verse states that God *knew*, *sanctified*, and *ordained* Jeremiah
*before* his physical formation in the womb began. This is cited in [[Stick of Joseph - Atheism is Unreasonable and Stupid]] as part of a broader scriptural
pattern indicating pre-mortal existence:[^1]

- God's knowledge of Jeremiah is personal and specific ("thee" — singular),
  not merely foreknowledge of a future contingent being.
- The sequence is: (1) God knows Jeremiah, (2) God forms Jeremiah in the womb,
  (3) Jeremiah comes forth from the womb. Step 1 precedes step 2, suggesting
  Jeremiah existed as an object of God's knowledge before his physical body
  was formed.

## Cross-Tradition Readings

- **LDS:** Read as one of several biblical texts that imply pre-mortal
  existence. Consistent with D&C 138:56 ("before they were born... they
  received their first lessons"), Abraham 3:22-23 (pre-mortal council), and
  the overall LDS plan of salvation.
- **Traditional Christian / Calvinist:** Typically interpreted as God's
  sovereign foreknowledge and predestination — God knew Jeremiah in the sense
  of foreordaining his prophetic calling before birth, not that Jeremiah
  existed as a conscious entity before conception. The "knowledge" is of God's
  plan, not of a pre-existing person.
- **Jewish:** Generally read as divine election — God chose and designated
  Jeremiah for his prophetic mission before birth. Does not necessarily entail
  personal pre-existence.
