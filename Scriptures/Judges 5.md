# Judges 5

**What:** The **Song of Deborah**, a victory hymn embedded in the prose narrative of Judges. Widely regarded as one of the oldest passages in the Hebrew Bible.

**Role in biblical studies:** Along with the [[Exodus 15|Song of the Sea]] (Exodus 15) and the [[Deuteronomy 32|Song of Moses]] (Deuteronomy 32), the Song of Deborah is an example of **archaic Hebrew poetry** — fragmented older poems set into later narrative frames. On linguistic and metrical grounds these poems are dated earlier than the prose around them, making them candidates for the oldest surviving biblical material. See [[The Oldest Biblical Texts Are Archaic Poems Embedded in Later Narrative]].
