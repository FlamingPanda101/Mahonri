# Psalm 29:1-2

> "Give unto the LORD, O ye mighty, give unto the LORD glory and strength. Give unto the LORD the glory due unto his name; worship the LORD in the beauty of holiness." (KJV)

The Hebrew opens *havu la-YHWH bene elim* — "ascribe to Yahweh, O **sons of God**." Many scholars regard Psalm 29 as poetry originally addressed to the Northwest Semitic storm god **Baal** ("Rider of the Clouds"), reapplied to Yahweh: the sevenfold "voice of the LORD" (*qol YHWH*, vv. 3–9) describes thunder and lightning, and the geography points north (Lebanon, Sirion/Hermon, Kadesh) rather than to Israel. Evidence that Yahweh and Baal share the storm-deity divine profile.
