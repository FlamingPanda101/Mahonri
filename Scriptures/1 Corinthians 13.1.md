# 1 Corinthians 13:1

> "Though I speak with the tongues of men and of angels, and have not charity, I am become as sounding brass, or a tinkling cymbal." *(KJV)*

The phrase "sounding brass" renders the Koine Greek *chalkos ēchōn* (χαλκὸς ἠχῶν). On **Ward Radio**, [[Jonah Barnes]] cites this verse to argue that the **brass/bronze metal word** *chalkos* used by Paul is the same lexeme appearing in the [[Mani|Cologne Mani Codex]]'s phrase *ptychas chalkas* ("plates of brass/bronze") — bolstering his case that the Apocalypse of Enosh passage really does describe **plates of brass** (see [[The Cologne Mani Codex Parallels Moroni and the Brass Plates]]). He concedes scholars often render *chalkos* as "bronze" (an alloy) while maintaining it is the brass-plates vocabulary.[^1]
