# Malachi 4

**What:** The closing chapter of Malachi — and, in the Christian Old Testament's ordering, the **final chapter of the entire Old Testament**. It prophesies the return of Elijah "before the coming of the great and dreadful day of the LORD" (Malachi 4:5).

**Role in biblical studies:** The Christian canon places the prophetic books last and ends on Malachi's Elijah prophecy, so that the Old Testament closes by pointing *forward* — Christians read Elijah's return as fulfilled in John the Baptist / Jesus, bridging into the New Testament. The Jewish [[The Hebrew Bible and the Christian Old Testament Are Not the Same Book|Tanakh]] orders the books differently and ends instead on [[2 Chronicles 36]] (Cyrus's decree to rebuild the temple) — a note of restoration, not a messianic cliffhanger. [[Dan McClellan]] uses this contrast to show the canon's *order* is itself an interpretive, Christianizing choice.
