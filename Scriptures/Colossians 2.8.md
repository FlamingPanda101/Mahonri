# Colossians 2:8 — "spoil you through philosophy and vain deceit"

> Beware lest any man spoil you through philosophy and vain deceit, after the tradition of men, after the rudiments of the world, and not after Christ. *(Colossians 2:8, KJV)*

## Key Detail
- Paul's warning against being captured "through philosophy and vain deceit... after the tradition of men." Latter-day Saint apologists (and the Thoughtful Faith narrator) cite it to mark the contrast between **biblical teaching about God** and **Greco-Roman philosophical claims** about God — the very mixing that, on the Hellenization thesis, produced the creedal definition of the divine nature.
- Contested as a proof-text: the immediate target is debated (likely a specific syncretistic "philosophy" at Colossae), so it is rhetorically apt rather than decisive.
