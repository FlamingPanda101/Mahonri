# D&C 88 — The Light Which Is in All Things

> "And the light which shineth, which giveth you light, is through him who enlighteneth your eyes, which is the same light that quickeneth your understandings; Which light proceedeth forth from the presence of God to fill the immensity of space— The light which is in all things, which giveth life to all things, which is the law by which all things are governed..." (D&C 88:11–13)

Day reads this "light/law in all things" passage in the Great Apostasy episode to draw a careful contrast with Greek-philosophical immanence: God's light fills the immensity of space and is *in* all things and in your spirit, yet this is "not the same as what these guys [the philosophers] are saying." He also uses it, with [[D&C 93]], to argue the Father and Son are of the "same stuff" (*homoousios*) without being the same person. See [[Homoousios Same Substance Not Same Person]] and [[Greek Philosophy Corrupted the Christian Concept of God]].
