# D&C 7 — John the Beloved Tarries to Bring Souls to Christ

> And the Lord said unto me: John, my beloved, what desirest thou? ... And I said unto him: Lord, give unto me power over death, that I may live and bring souls unto thee. And the Lord said unto me: Verily, verily, I say unto thee, because thou desirest this thou shalt tarry until I come in my glory, and shalt prophesy before nations, kindreds, tongues and people. *(Doctrine and Covenants 7:1-3)*

## Significance
A revelation given to [[Joseph Smith]] and [[Oliver Cowdery]] (April 1829) translated from a parchment written and hidden by the Apostle John, answering whether John "tarried" on earth. In [[Spencer W. Kimball]]'s personal **Doctrine and Covenants**, his handwritten margin note beside this section reads "**I desire the same thing**" — consciously echoing the passage's own verb ("what desirest thou... because thou desirest this"). The [[Ward Radio - What Spencer W Kimball Wrote in the Margins of His Scriptures|Ward Radio]] collector calls it the note that "blew me away": a glimpse of a future prophet personally aspiring, like John, to live to bring souls to Christ.[^1]
