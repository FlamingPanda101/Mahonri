# 1 John 4:8

> He that loveth not knoweth not God; for God is love. *(KJV)*

## Key phrase
"**God is love**" — Greek **ὁ θεὸς ἀγάπη ἐστίν**. The predicate noun *agapē* is anarthrous (no article), which trinitarian apologists read as an ontological identity statement (God *is* love in his very being), not merely that God *loves*. Affirmative debaters argue this is only intelligible if God is eternally relational (triune): the Father loving the Son in the communion of the Spirit.

## Apologetic application
[[Gavin (Catholic Debater)]] makes this the cornerstone of the affirmative case for divine omnibenevolence: "God is not merely loving. God is love himself. That is only possible if God is triune." See [[Omnibenevolence Flows from the Triune God as Eternal Love]].
