# Exodus 15:23-25 (KJV) — The tree that sweetens Marah's bitter waters

> And when they came to Marah, they could not drink of the waters of Marah, for they were bitter... And he cried unto the LORD; and the LORD shewed him a tree, which when he had cast into the waters, the waters were made sweet.

## Key Detail

David Butler's tree-of-life reading uses Marah to resolve why the river in Lehi's vision is both "the love of God" and "the filthiness... the depths of hell": the *same* water is sweet in the tree's presence and bitter in its absence. Its moral valence is a function of proximity to the tree (the Asherah / divine Mother), not a contradiction.
