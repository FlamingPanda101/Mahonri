# Doctrine and Covenants 81.5

"Wherefore, be faithful; stand in the office which I have appointed unto you; succor the weak, lift up the hands which hang down, and strengthen the feeble knees."

## Why it's cited

This verse is a charge to active, sustaining ministry — to seek out and strengthen the weak and discouraged rather than leave them to fall away. Apologetics notes cite it as the revealed pattern of nourishing care: the leader's duty is to lift and support the struggling member, balancing protection with nourishment. It supports the argument that healthy retention and discipleship come from tending people directly, echoing the same gardening-over-crop-dusting model of ministry found in the early sections on priesthood duty.
