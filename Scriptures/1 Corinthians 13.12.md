# 1 Corinthians 13:12

> 12 For now we see through a glass, darkly; but then face to face: now I know in part; but then shall I know even as also I am known.

Paul's image of partial, mediated knowledge — a dim reflection in a polished-metal mirror (Greek **ἐσόπτρου**, *esoptron*) — set against a future of unmediated sight. [[Jacob Hansen]] invokes it to argue that no single witness yields total certainty: "there is no magic formula, there is no trump card... as Paul said we see through a glass Darkly and ultimately we walk by faith" ([[2 Corinthians 5.7]]).
