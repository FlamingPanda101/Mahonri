# Jacob 1:9 — Nephi anoints "a man" as king

> Now Nephi began to be old, and he saw that he must soon die; wherefore, he anointed a man to be a king and a ruler over his people now, according to the reigns of the kings. *(Jacob 1:9, Book of Mormon)*

## Key Detail
- Nephi anoints **"a man"** — not a named son — to succeed him as king, and the record is silent on a son-heir. Rappleye flags this as an undesigned clue compatible with early **genetic admixture**: if Nephi's heir was, e.g., an indigenous son-in-law, dynastic intermarriage with surrounding peoples would already be underway within the first generation. Compare Mosiah's later rise to the throne over the Mulekite descendant of the king of Judah.
