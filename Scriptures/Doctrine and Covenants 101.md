# Doctrine and Covenants 101 (1835) — The Article on Marriage

> Inasmuch as this church of Christ has been reproached with the crime of fornication, and polygamy: we declare that we believe, that one man should have one wife; and one woman, but one husband. *(1835 Doctrine and Covenants, Section 101 — the "Article on Marriage"; later removed and replaced by present-day D&C 132)*

## Key Detail
- This monogamy statement appeared as **Section 101** in the 1835 edition of the Doctrine and Covenants (it is *not* in the modern D&C 101). [[Don Bradley]] uses its date — published August 1835 — as a key timeline anchor in [[Ward Radio - Debunking Joseph Smiths Fake Affair with Fanny Alger feat Don Bradley]]: because the article explicitly says the church had *already been reproached* with "fornication and polygamy" by 1835, *something* involving polygamy was being discussed in the church before [[Emma Smith]] discovered the [[Fanny Alger]] relationship (July 1836). This undercuts the claim that polygamy was only retrojected onto Fanny later. See [[The 1835 Marriage Article Shows Polygamy Was Already in the Air]].
- (Caveat: the same logic that reads a denial as evidence of an underlying practice — "they would not deny it unless it were happening" — is the move critics use against Joseph's later denials; the note steelmans this.)
