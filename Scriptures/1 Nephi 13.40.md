# 1 Nephi 13:40

> ...that the Lamb of God is the Son of the Eternal Father, and the Savior of the world; and that all men must come unto him, or they cannot be saved. *(current edition)*

**Earlier-occurrence note:** This verse is part of the cluster where, in the
first edition, the name **Jesus** (and equivalents like "the Eternal God / the
Messiah who is the Lamb of God") appears before the point in the narrative
([[2 Nephi 10.3|2 Nephi 10:3]]) at which the name Jesus is said to be first
revealed to Nephi. Later editions adjusted some earlier occurrences from "Jesus"
to "Messiah" so the narrative's internal timeline of name-revelation stays
consistent.

## Context

The angel's interpretation of Nephi's vision affirms that the Lamb of God is the
Son of the Eternal Father. The Saints Unscripted video treats the name swaps in
this stretch (Jesus → Messiah) as a translator's narrative-consistency edit
rather than a doctrinal change, since "both Messiah and Jesus point to the same
being."

## Key Exegetical Point

- **"Messiah" vs. "Jesus":** *Messiah* (Hebrew *māšîaḥ*, "anointed one") and
  *Jesus* (Greek *Iēsous* < Hebrew *Yēšûaʿ*) denote the same person; the former
  is a title, the latter a personal name. Substituting the title for the name in
  pre-revelation passages preserves the story's claim that the specific name was
  disclosed later.
