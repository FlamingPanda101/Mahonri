# Genesis 49:25

> Even by the God of thy father, who shall help thee; and by the Almighty, who shall bless thee with blessings of heaven above, blessings of the deep that lieth under, blessings of the breasts, and of the womb. *(KJV)*

(With v. 24: "...by the hands of the mighty God of Jacob; from thence is the shepherd, the stone of Israel.")

## Key Terms: Abir / El / Shaddai — three divine names
In Jacob's blessing of Joseph, three differently-named divine beings appear in close succession in the Hebrew: **Abir Yaakov** ("the **Bull** of Jacob," KJV "mighty God of Jacob," with "God" italicized/inserted), **El** ("the God of thy father"), and **Shaddai** (KJV "the Almighty"). [[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)|Dave Butler]] reads these on a continuous-text basis as **three distinct gods** — the divine family — rather than three epithets of one God; see [[Genesis 49 Names Three Distinct Gods in Jacobs Blessing]]. The fertility blessing "of the **breasts** (*Shaddayim*) and of the **womb** (*Rechem*)" is read as a pun making **Shaddai** the "**breasted God**," a feminine divinity equated by [[Mark Smith]] with Asherah — see [[Shaddai Is the Breasted God and Points to the Mother]]. **Abir** ("Bull") anchors [[The Bull of Jacob Shows Yahweh Was Depicted as a Bull]]. The traditional reading takes all three as honorific titles of the one patriarchal God in poetic parallelism.
