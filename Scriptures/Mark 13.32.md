# Mark 13:32

> But of that day and that hour knoweth no man, no, not the angels which are in heaven, neither the Son, but the Father. *(KJV)*

## Key point
The Son does not know the day or hour of the second coming; the Father does. The panel presses this as a defeater for numerical-identity-of-being Trinitarianism: if the Son *is* the same being as the Father, how can the Father know what the Son does not? The Trinitarian reply ("in his human nature the Son didn't know") is treated as the kind of two-natures dodge addressed in [[Natures Do Not Act Only Persons Do]] — a fully *omniscient* divine nature cannot lack knowledge. See [[Subordination Within the New Testament]].
