# Genesis 2:19

> "And out of the ground the LORD God formed every beast of the field, and every fowl of the air; and brought them unto Adam to see what he would call them: and whatsoever Adam called every living creature, that was the name thereof." (KJV)

**Hebrew (key terms):** וְכֹל עוֹף הַשָּׁמַיִם (*we-kol of ha-shamayim*, "and every bird of the sky/heavens"). The opening verb is a **wayyiqtol** (*wayyitser*, "and he formed") — the default Hebrew narrative-sequence form, which ESV/LSB/NIV render as a pluperfect ("had formed") to relocate the act before the events of Genesis 2.

## Context

In the second creation account, this verse places the formation of "every animal of the field and every bird of the sky" *after* the creation of the human and the planting/placing in the garden. This inverts Genesis 1, where birds are made on day five (Genesis 1:20–23), before humans on day six. Dan McClellan argues this breaks the apologetic "zoom-in" harmonization: even if *eretz* could mean a localized land, *shamayim* ("sky") cannot be localized to Eden, so the birds here are the same global category as day five — yielding a clear contradiction in creation order.
