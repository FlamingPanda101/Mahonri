# 3 Nephi 11:10-11

> Behold, I am Jesus Christ, whom the prophets testified shall come into the world. And behold, I am the light and the life of the world; and I have drunk out of that bitter cup which the Father hath given me, and have taken upon me the sins of the world... *(Book of Mormon)*

## Significance
The risen Christ's self-declaration to the Nephites — what [[Ward Radio]] calls part of "the fifth gospel." It directly echoes [[1 Corinthians 15.1-4|Paul's gospel]]: Christ was prophesied ("whom the prophets testified"), took upon him the sins of the world, and (in the surrounding narrative) appears *resurrected*. This is offered as proof that the Book of Mormon preaches the *same* gospel Paul defines, so [[Galatians 1.8|Galatians 1:8]]'s "another gospel" does not apply. The hosts note this passage is "the crown jewel" of Joseph's revelations and the most heavily marked chapter in prophets' scriptures.
