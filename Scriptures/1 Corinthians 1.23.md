# 1 Corinthians 1:23

> But we preach Christ crucified, unto the Jews a stumblingblock, and unto the Greeks foolishness. *(KJV)*

## Key phrase
"**Christ crucified... unto the Jews a stumblingblock, and unto the Greeks foolishness**" — Paul concedes that the message of a crucified Messiah was scandalous to Jews and absurd to Greeks/Romans.

## Apologetic application
[[Tom Holland]] uses this as evidence of how *radical* the cross was in its own world: crucifixion was the most humiliating Roman death (reserved for slaves), so the claim that the crucified man is God inverted the entire ancient value system. [[Paul the Apostle|Paul]]'s own embarrassment ("foolishness to the Greeks") shows early Christians felt the offense; only [[Friedrich Nietzsche]], Holland argues, later "gets" the cross — and recoils from it as a slave-morality. See [[The Cross as Moral Inversion of Roman Values]].
