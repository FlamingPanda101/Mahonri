# Isaiah 7:14 (KJV)

> Therefore the Lord himself shall give you a sign; Behold, a virgin shall conceive, and bear a son, and shall call his name Immanuel.

## Translation Note

The Hebrew word translated "virgin" in the KJV is _alma_ (עַלְמָה), which means "young woman of marriageable age" — not necessarily a virgin. Most modern translations (NRSVUE, JPS Tanakh) render this "young woman." The Septuagint (Greek translation) rendered _alma_ as _parthenos_ (παρθένος, "virgin"), which the author of Matthew then quoted.[^1] The Hebrew construction uses an adjective meaning "pregnant" without a tense marker; the Septuagint supplies a future reading, while the Hebrew adjective overwhelmingly refers to a current or imminent pregnancy.[^2]

## Context (8th Century BCE)

The prophecy was addressed to King Ahaz during a crisis: a coalition of northern kingdoms threatened Judah. Isaiah pointed to a pregnant woman known to the king and said, "Before that child grows up, those threatening kingdoms will be gone." The sign was the child's name (Immanuel = "God with us") and the timeline, not a messianic prediction.[^3]
