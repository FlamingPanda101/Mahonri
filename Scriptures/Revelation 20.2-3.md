# Revelation 20:2-3

> And he laid hold on the dragon, that old serpent, which is the Devil, and Satan, and bound him a thousand years, And cast him into the bottomless pit, and shut him up, and set a seal upon him, that he should deceive the nations no more, till the thousand years should be fulfilled: and after that he must be loosed a little season. *(KJV)*

## Key phrase
"**A thousand years**" — the *only* place in the New Testament that a millennial reign appears. From the Latin *mille* + *annus* comes the term **Millennium**. Frederick: this passage "would come to dominate future eschatological discussions among Christians."

## Apologetic / historical application
[[Nicholas J. Frederick]] makes Revelation 20 the hinge of the chapter: the three later millennial positions ([[Three Millennial Positions Premillennialism Postmillennialism Amillennialism|premillennialism, postmillennialism, amillennialism]]) all arose as competing readings of this single text. [[Augustine]] reinterpreted the "thousand years" allegorically as the present age of the Church between Christ's first and second comings (see [[Augustine Recast the Millennium as the Present Age of the Church]]), while [[Irenaeus of Lyons]] and the early premillennialists read it literally. The following verses (20:8-10) describe the battle of Gog and Magog and Satan's final consignment to the lake of fire.
