# 2 Chronicles 36

**What:** The final chapter of 2 Chronicles, which is the **last book of the Jewish [[The Hebrew Bible and the Christian Old Testament Are Not the Same Book|Tanakh]]** (in its Ketuvim/Writings ordering). It ends with the decree of the Persian king [[Cyrus the Great]] permitting the exiled Jews to return to Jerusalem and rebuild the temple (2 Chronicles 36:22-23).

**Role in biblical studies:** Because the Tanakh closes here, the Hebrew Bible ends on a note of **historical hope and restoration** — the return from exile and the rebuilding of the temple — rather than on the forward-pointing Elijah prophecy of [[Malachi 4]] that closes the Christian Old Testament. [[Dan McClellan]] highlights this divergent endpoint to show that "the Hebrew Bible" and "the Old Testament" are differently shaped books.
