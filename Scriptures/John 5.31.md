# John 5:31 — "If I bear witness of myself, my witness is not true"

> If I bear witness of myself, my witness is not true. There is another that beareth witness of me... *(John 5:31–32, KJV)*

## Key Detail
- Jesus himself defers to the **law of witnesses**: he will not rest his claim on his own testimony alone but appeals to John the Baptist, his works, and the Father (John 5:31–37). The Thoughtful Faith narrator cites it to argue that even Christ "would not expect others to rely on his word alone," so an individual claiming *personal authority* against the collective prophetic witness violates God's pattern (cf. [[Deuteronomy 19.15]]).
- Anchors the LDS [[The Law of Witnesses Establishes Truth|law-of-witnesses]] epistemology and the [[Change Is Not the Same as Contradiction|change-not-contradiction]] model.
