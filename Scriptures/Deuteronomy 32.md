# Deuteronomy 32

**What:** The **Song of Moses**, a poem placed near the end of Deuteronomy. One of the oldest passages in the Hebrew Bible.

**Role in biblical studies:** Together with the [[Judges 5|Song of Deborah]] (Judges 5) and the [[Exodus 15|Song of the Sea]] (Exodus 15), the Song of Moses is a key specimen of **archaic Hebrew poetry** embedded in later narrative — dated earlier than its prose frame on linguistic grounds. See [[The Oldest Biblical Texts Are Archaic Poems Embedded in Later Narrative]]. Deuteronomy 32:8-9 is also central to the divine-council debate: in the Dead Sea Scrolls / Septuagint reading, the Most High apportions the nations "according to the number of the sons of God," with Yahweh receiving Israel (see [[Deuteronomy 32 Dead Sea Scrolls Monolatry]]).
