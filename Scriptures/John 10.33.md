# John 10:33

> The Jews answered him, saying, For a good work we stone thee not; but for blasphemy; and because that thou, being a man, makest thyself God. *(KJV)*

## The Greek — Missing Definite Articles
The KJV and most English translations obscure a critical grammatical detail. In Greek:
- "Being a man" — *anthropos on* (no definite article: qualitative — "being human")
- "Makest thyself God" — *theon* (again **no definite article**)

The parallelism suggests a qualitative reading: "You, being human (having human qualities), are making yourself divine (claiming divine qualities)." It is NOT "You, being the human, are making yourself THE God of Israel."[^1]

## Jesus's Response Confirms It
Jesus responds by quoting [[Psalm 82.6|Psalm 82:6]]: "Is it not written in your law, I said, Ye are gods?" If the accusation were that Jesus claimed to be THE God of Israel, this response would be a straw man — irrelevant. But if the accusation is claiming divine status, Jesus's response is perfectly targeted: "Scripture calls humans 'gods,' so I'm off the hook."[^2]

This confirms the Jews are accusing Jesus of claiming to be divine (a god), not claiming identity with the one God of Israel.
