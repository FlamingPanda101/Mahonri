# Romans 7:4

> Wherefore, my brethren, ye also are become dead to the law by the body of Christ; that ye should be married to another, even to him who is raised from the dead, that we should bring forth fruit unto God. *(KJV)*

## Key phrase
"**Married to another, even to him who is raised from the dead**" — the resolution of Paul's law-and-failure argument: deliverance and fruitfulness come through union with the risen Christ, not through fealty to the law.

## Apologetic / historical application
[[Brian Thomassen]] reads this as the payoff of Romans' core trope: having shown ([[Romans 7.7-9]], [[Romans 7.19]]) that an alien adoption of the Mosaic law only multiplies the sense of sin and ends in failure, Paul concludes that **real self-mastery (*enkrateia*) is found "within Christ," not through the law**.
