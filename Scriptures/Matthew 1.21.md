# Matthew 1:21

> And she shall bring forth a son, and thou shalt call his name JESUS: for he shall save his people from their sins. *(KJV)*

## Key phrase
"**Thou shalt call his name JESUS: for he shall save his people from their sins**" — the angel's naming of Jesus, explaining the meaning of the name.

## Apologetic / historical application
[[Miranda Wilcox]] notes that "the early English understood the Hebrew root of the name Jesus in Matthew 1:21 to mean 'healer,'" and so translated *Jesus* into Old English as **Hælend** ("healer"). [[Aelfric]] preached on this in a homily on the pool of Bethesda: "His name is Hælend, because he heals his people, just as the angel said about him before he was born." A vivid case of vernacular theological translation. See [[Old English Named Jesus the Healer Haelend]].
