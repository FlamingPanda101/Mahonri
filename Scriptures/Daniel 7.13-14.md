# Daniel 7:13–14

> I saw in the night visions, and, behold, one like the Son of man came with the clouds of heaven, and came to the Ancient of days, and they brought him near before him. And there was given him dominion, and glory, and a kingdom, that all people, nations, and languages, should serve him: his dominion is an everlasting dominion, which shall not pass away, and his kingdom that which shall not be destroyed. (KJV)

## Key Hebrew/Aramaic Terms

- **כְּבַר אֱנָשׁ (k'var enash):** "one like a son of man" — literally "like a son of man," i.e., a human-like figure in contrast to the beasts representing empires. In the vision's symbolism, this human figure receives dominion from the Ancient of Days.
- **עַתִּיק יוֹמִין (atiq yomin):** "Ancient of Days" — the divine figure enthroned in judgment, representing God.

## The "Son of Man" in the Book of Enoch

The [[Book of Enoch - Enoch as the Son of Man|Book of Parables (1 Enoch 37–71)]] identifies Enoch himself with this Danielic Son of Man figure. In the vision, Enoch is taken up to heaven and declared to BE the Son of Man — a mediator figure whose identity blurs with the divine.

Dan McClellan notes a significant textual variant: in one version of the Old Greek, the preposition is altered by a single letter so that "the son of man came TO the Ancient of Days" becomes "the son of man came AS the Ancient of Days," blurring the mediator's identity with God's own identity. [^1]

## Relevance to New Testament Christology

Jesus's preferred self-designation "the Son of Man" (ὁ υἱὸς τοῦ ἀνθρώπου) draws from this Danielic/Enochic tradition. The Enochic literature provides the conceptual background for a heavenly mediator figure who:
- Preexists in heaven before being revealed
- Sits on the throne of judgment
- Receives worship alongside God
- Functions as eschatological judge

These concepts, developed in 1 Enoch's Book of Parables, feed directly into early Christian understandings of Jesus's identity and role.

## How Different Traditions Read This

- **Jewish interpretation**: The "one like a son of man" is typically understood as a symbol for the righteous remnant of Israel, or the archangel Michael, or the Messiah — not a divine figure.
- **Christian interpretation**: Traditionally reads this as a messianic prophecy of Christ, especially given Jesus's use of the "Son of Man" title and the New Testament's application of the "coming with clouds" imagery to Christ's return (Matthew 24:30, Revelation 1:7).
- **Academic/critical**: Sees the Son of Man as a heavenly mediator figure within the divine council framework, later identified with specific figures (Enoch in 1 Enoch, Jesus in the NT).
