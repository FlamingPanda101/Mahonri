# Genesis 24 — Isaac and Rebekah

> And the servant took ten camels... and ... arose, and went to Mesopotamia, unto the city of Nahor... And they blessed Rebekah... Be thou the mother of thousands of millions... And Isaac brought her into his mother Sarah's tent, and took Rebekah, and she became his wife. *(KJV, abridged)*

## Key phrase
"**The servant took ten camels... and Isaac... took Rebekah, and she became his wife**" — [[Abraham]]'s servant is sent to choose a bride for [[Isaac]]; [[Rebekah]] is gifted, consents, travels, and marries a husband she has not met.

## Apologetic / typological application
[[Andrea Woodmansee]] treats this as the earliest clear instance of the three-phase Hebrew wedding pattern: the **father's servant** (not the groom) selects the bride, gives her gifts *before* the wedding, and brings her hundreds of miles — a bride "called out of Babylon" into the covenant family. Rebekah did not know the groom or the distance, yet she consents ("I will go") — the bride's accept-or-veto right. The pattern undergirds the master claim that ancient weddings type the temple and Christ-as-bridegroom. See [[Ancient Hebrew Weddings Are a Three-Phase Type of the Temple]].
