# Exodus 6:3 — "by my name JEHOVAH was I not known"

> And I appeared unto Abraham, unto Isaac, and unto Jacob, by the name of God Almighty [El Shaddai], but by my name JEHOVAH [YHWH] was I not known to them. *(KJV)*

## Key Detail
In the Priestly (P) source, God tells [[Moses]] that the patriarchs knew him as *El Shaddai* and that the name **YHWH** "was I not known to them." This is one of the cornerstone observations of source criticism: P (here) and E ([[Exodus 3.14|Exodus 3]]) present the name Yahweh as a *new* revelation at the time of Moses, whereas the Yahwist (J) uses the name freely from [[Genesis 2.4-7|Genesis 2]] onward (and explicitly at Genesis 4:26, where people "began... to call upon the name of the LORD"). The same author cannot consistently hold both, so scholars assign the Genesis usage to a distinct source. The conservative harmonization reads "not *known*" as "not experienced in the covenant character the name signifies," rather than "the syllables were unknown."
