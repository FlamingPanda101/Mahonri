# Ether 2:23-25 — "What will ye that I should prepare for you?"

When the brother of Jared explains that the barges will be dark, sealed against both fire and windows, the Lord does not supply the answer. Instead He lays out the constraints and turns the problem back on the prophet:

> "And the Lord said unto the brother of Jared: What will ye that I should do that ye may have light in your vessels?... For behold, ye cannot have windows, for they will be dashed in pieces; neither shall ye take fire with you... Therefore what will ye that I should prepare for you that ye may have light when ye are swallowed up in the depths of the sea?" (Ether 2:23, 25)

The brother of Jared then proposes the sixteen small molten stones, which the Lord touches so they shine ([[Ether 3.14]] cluster). The passage is a Book of Mormon paradigm for **agency-in-revelation**: God invites the prophet's own deliberation and initiative rather than dictating the solution.
