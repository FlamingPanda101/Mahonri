# Mormon 9:32–34

> "And now, behold, we have written this record according to our knowledge, in the characters which are called among us the reformed Egyptian, being handed down and altered by us, according to our manner of speech. And if our plates had been sufficiently large we should have written in Hebrew; but the Hebrew hath been altered by us also; and if we could have written in Hebrew, behold, ye would have had no imperfection in our record. But the Lord knoweth the things which we have written, and also that none other people knoweth our language..."

## Significance
Moroni's colophon states that the Nephite record-keepers wrote in "**reformed Egyptian**" but would have used **Hebrew** had the plates been larger — and that *both* languages had been "altered" over the Nephites' ~1,000-year history. In the [[Informed Saints - Book of Mormon Evidence Hidden in Ancient American Language|Informed Saints interview]] the hosts cite this passage to argue that the same people carried **both** a Semitic and an Egyptian linguistic inheritance — which lines up with [[Brian Stubbs]]'s finding that the Hebrew and Egyptian strata he isolates in Uto-Aztecan share the *same* set of sound correspondences, implying one bilingual source population.[^1] See [[Uto-Aztecan Contains a Large Semitic and Egyptian Lexical Layer]].

The verse also anchors the broader "language changes over time" framing: Moroni's admission that the language was altered is what makes the centuries of [[Sound Correspondences Distinguish Descent from Coincidence in Language|regular sound change]] Stubbs posits internally coherent. Compare [[Reformed Egyptian Characters Were Small]].
