# 1 Timothy 2:4

> Who will have all men to be saved, and to come unto the knowledge of the truth. *(KJV)*

## Key phrase
"**Will have all men to be saved**" — Greek **ὃς πάντας ἀνθρώπους θέλει σωθῆναι**. The verb *thelei* ("wills/desires") grounds the doctrine of God's **universal salvific will**. Debate turns on *antecedent* will (God genuinely wills the good of all) vs. *consequent* will (what God ordains given human resistance).

## Apologetic application
[[Gavin (Catholic Debater)]] cites it as explicit, non-ambiguous scriptural proof that God "wills the good of all without exception," distinct from universalism (it does not say all *are* saved). See [[Scripture Explicitly Affirms Universal Salvific Will]].
