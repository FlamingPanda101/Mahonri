# 2 Corinthians 5:7

> 7 (For we walk by faith, not by sight:)

Paul's contrast between **faith** (πίστις, *pistis*) and **sight** (εἶδος, *eidos*) as the mode of the present life. [[Jacob Hansen]] pairs it with [[1 Corinthians 13.12]] ("through a glass darkly") to close his epistemology lesson: because no witness is a trump card and certainty is partial, "ultimately we walk by faith." The verse is enlisted not to demote evidence but to mark the limit beyond which the [[The Collective Witness Model of Knowing|collective witness model]] still requires trust.
