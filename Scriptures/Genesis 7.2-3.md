# Genesis 7:2–3

> Of every clean beast thou shalt take to thee by sevens, the male and his female: and of beasts that are not clean by two, the male and his female. Of fowls also of the air by sevens, the male and the female; to keep seed alive upon the face of all the earth. *(KJV)*

## Key Hebrew Terms

- **שִׁבְעָה שִׁבְעָה (shiv'ah shiv'ah):** "seven seven" — the Hebrew idiom means *seven pairs* (literally "seven, seven, male and female"). This is not seven individual animals; it is seven pairs. The KJV's "by sevens" preserves the sense.
- **טָהוֹר (tahor) / טָמֵא (tamei):** "clean" / "unclean" — the non-Priestly source introduces a clean/unclean distinction absent from the Priestly account ([[Genesis 6.19-20|Genesis 6:19-20]]). Clean land animals: seven pairs. Unclean land animals: one pair. Critically, ALL birds (clean AND unclean alike) get seven pairs — no bird is categorized as "unclean" for purposes of the count.
- **עוֹף הַשָּׁמַיִם (oph hashamayim):** "fowls of the air" — the identical phrase used in Genesis 6:20 ("fowls after their kind"). Both passages use it to mean ALL birds, making the numerical discrepancy a direct contradiction.

## Source-Critical Context

Belongs to the **non-Priestly (Yahwist/J)** flood account. In the scholarly consensus, the J account was originally a separate flood narrative later interwoven with the Priestly (P) account by a redactor. The animal count is one of several irreconcilable differences between them (along with flood duration, cause of flood, divine name used, and chronology). See [[Noahs Ark Source Criticism Two Flood Accounts]].

## Why the Harmonization Fails for Birds

McClellan's key textual point: the apologetic harmonization says "two was the baseline, and seven pairs were added for clean animals that would be sacrificed." But this only works if the extra animals are CLEAN — yet birds get seven pairs regardless of clean/unclean status. The J text makes NO clean/unclean distinction among birds. So either:

1. ALL birds are seven pairs (per [[Genesis 7.2-3|Genesis 7:2-3]]), contradicting the two of ALL birds in Genesis 6:19–20; OR
2. The harmonizer must INVENT a clean/unclean bird distinction not present in either text — which is the very "third thing" rationalization McClellan critiques. See [[Rationalization C - The Third Thing Harmonization]].
