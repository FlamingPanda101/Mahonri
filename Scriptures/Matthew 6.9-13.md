# Matthew 6:9-13 (KJV) — The Lord's Prayer

> After this manner therefore pray ye: Our Father which art in heaven, Hallowed be thy name. Thy kingdom come. Thy will be done in earth, as it is in heaven... (vv. 9-13)

## Key Detail

Read by early Christians (Didache, Tertullian, Cyprian) as both a *pattern* and a *formula* for prayer — recited thrice daily and taught catechetically around baptism. Each line evokes moments from Jesus's own life, so reciting it thoughtfully drew believers into emulating Christ. Used to show continuity between the Restoration and ancient Christian devotional practice.
