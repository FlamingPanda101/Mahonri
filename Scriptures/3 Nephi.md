# 3 Nephi — the resurrected Christ's ministry among the Nephites

> The Book of Nephi, the son of Nephi, which is the son of Helaman. Records the great destruction at the time of the Crucifixion (3 Nephi 8–9), then the appearance of the risen Jesus Christ at the temple in Bountiful — his teaching of a Sermon-at-the-Temple, institution of the sacrament, calling of twelve disciples, healing and blessing of the children, and the founding of a Zion society. Often called the climax and "fifth Gospel" of the Book of Mormon.

## Key Detail
- The doctrinal center of gravity for the Book of Mormon's witness of Christ. Used in the vault both as a whole-book node (e.g., Spencer W. Kimball marked **55%** of its verses, the highest percentage of any long book — [[A Prophet's Scripture Marginalia Reveals His Devotional Interior]]) and for specific episodes like the post-destruction voice of Christ in [[3 Nephi 9|3 Nephi 9]]. Its mass-conversion and temple-theophany material recurs across the priesthood/temple essays.
