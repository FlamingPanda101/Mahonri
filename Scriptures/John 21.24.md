# John 21:24

> This is the disciple which testifieth of these things, and wrote these things: and we know that his testimony is true. *(KJV)*

## The Greek
*Houtos estin ho mathētēs ho martyrōn peri toutōn kai ho grapsas tauta, kai oidamen hoti alēthēs autou hē martyria estin.* The verse moves from a **third-person** description of "this disciple" to a sudden **first-person plural** — *oidamen*, "**we** know." The very next verse (21:25) shifts again to a **first-person singular** ("I suppose").

## Significance
This is the crux verse for Johannine authorship. The "he... we... I" sequence has been read as evidence of a separate editorial group ("we") completing or vouching for the work of one author. [[Hugo Mendez]] argues instead that the verse is stitched together from earlier verses of John (notably the "we have seen his glory" of [[John 1.14|John 1:14]]), and that a single ancient narrator routinely shifts between he/we/I — as in [[Polybius]] and [[Josephus]] — so the traditional single-author reading is "close to the truth." Because chapter 21 as a whole is, for Méndez, a later addition, the verse is also part of the case that the Gospel ends naturally at 20:30–31.
