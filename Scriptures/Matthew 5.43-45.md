# Matthew 5:43-45

> Ye have heard that it hath been said, Thou shalt love thy neighbour, and hate thine enemy. But I say unto you, Love your enemies, bless them that curse you, do good to them that hate you, and pray for them which despitefully use you; That ye may be the children of your Father which is in heaven: for he maketh his sun to rise on the evil and on the good, and sendeth rain on the just and on the unjust. *(KJV)*

## Key point
[[Blake Ostler]] cites the Sermon on the Mount as the place where Christ teaches that "the old law is done away" and is replaced by an *intensification* — the [[John 13.34|law of love]]. The "I say unto you" antitheses show God *adapting* his commands (from the law of Moses to the law of love), which Ostler argues would be impossible "if there were a single reality of what's right and wrong." The Father's indiscriminate sun and rain model love as natural flourishing rather than retributive bookkeeping.
