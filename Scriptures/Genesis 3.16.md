# Genesis 3:16

> Unto the woman he said, I will greatly multiply thy sorrow and thy conception; in sorrow thou shalt bring forth children; and thy desire shall be to thy husband, and he shall rule over thee. *(KJV)*

## Significance
The verse [[Spencer W. Kimball]] proposed reading as "preside" rather than "rule." [[Dan McClellan]]
counters that the Hebrew verb is *mashal* — "to rule, dominate, have dominion" — and that "preside"
is an anachronistic, egalitarian-flavored softening; the verse is "an ideology for the patriarchy,"
explaining how mid-first-millennium-BCE gender subordination came to be, alongside the parallel
"curses" of painful childbirth, agricultural toil, and humanity's hatred of snakes. A worked example
of [[We Negotiate With the Text - It Does Not Speak for Itself]].
