# Psalm 137:9

> "Happy shall he be, that taketh and dasheth thy little ones against the stones." (KJV)

The closing line of an imprecatory, post-exilic psalm of lament over the Babylonian captivity. The Hebrew *ashre* ("happy / blessed") pronounces a beatitude on whoever avenges Zion by killing Babylon's infants — siege-warfare imagery turned into a cry for retribution. Its plain sense is morally jarring, which made it a flashpoint in the history of interpretation: [[Origen]] refused the literal reading and allegorized the "little ones" of Babylon as impure thoughts to be dashed against the Rock (Christ).
