# Revelation 12:4

> And his tail drew the third part of the stars of heaven, and did cast them to the earth: and the dragon stood before the woman which was ready to be delivered, for to devour her child as soon as it was born. (KJV)

## Enochic Background

This verse draws directly from the [[Book of Enoch - The Watchers and Genesis 6 Expansion|Enochic Watcher tradition]]. The imagery of a chief malevolent angel falling like a star, followed by other angels/stars being cast down to earth, originates in 1 Enoch 86:1 where the lead Watcher (Asael/Shemihazah/Azazel) is seen as "a star [that] fell from heaven" and "arose and was eating and pasturing among the cattle," followed by "many stars descend and cast themselves down from heaven to that first star." [^1]

The Book of Revelation takes this Enochic imagery and applies it to the Dragon (Satan). The "third part of the stars" swept down by the Dragon's tail parallels the Enochic motif of the lead fallen angel bringing other angels with him in rebellion.

## Key Greek Terms

- **δράκων (drakōn):** "dragon" — the serpentine adversary identified as "the Devil and Satan" (Revelation 12:9). The term draws on chaos-monster imagery from the Hebrew Bible (Leviathan, Rahab) fused with the Enochic fallen-angel tradition.
- **τὸ τρίτον τῶν ἀστέρων (to triton tōn asterōn):** "the third part of the stars" — the angels who follow the Dragon in rebellion. The "third" fraction is unique to Revelation; 1 Enoch does not specify a fraction.
- **ἔβαλεν (ebalen):** "did cast" — the violent casting-down language mirrors the judgment on the Watchers in 1 Enoch 10, where the archangels are commanded to bind and cast the rebellious angels into darkness.

## Theological Development

This verse illustrates the trajectory of the Satan figure's development:
1. **Hebrew Bible**: The "satan" (הַשָּׂטָן, ha-satan) is a title meaning "the adversary/accuser," a member of the divine council (Job 1–2, Zechariah 3). Not yet a cosmic rebel.
2. **1 Enoch (Book of Watchers, ~300 BCE)**: Asael/Shemihazah is a leader of fallen angels who covenant to descend and corrupt humanity. This is the narrative origin of cosmic rebellion.
3. **Revelation (late 1st century CE)**: The Dragon/Satan is a full cosmic adversary leading an angelic rebellion, drawing on Enochic imagery but with independent theological development.
