# Leviticus 24

**Reference:** Leviticus 24 (Old Testament)

## Text
> "And thou shalt take fine flour, and bake twelve cakes thereof... And thou shalt set them in two rows, six on a row, upon the pure table before the LORD... And it shall be Aaron's and his sons'; and they shall eat it in the holy place: for it is most holy unto him of the offerings of the LORD made by fire by a perpetual statute." (Leviticus 24:5-9)

## Significance
Prescribes the **showbread** (bread of the Presence): twelve loaves set out before the LORD each Sabbath and then eaten by [[Aaron]] and the priests in the holy place. [[Menahem Haran]] notes that the temple's inner room also held **wine vessels**, even though no rite of pouring out wine is described — leading him to argue that wine was *consumed* with the showbread, a forgotten bread-and-wine ordinance. [[Mike Day]] and [[Dave Butler]] connect this to [[Melchizedek]]'s bread and wine ([[Genesis 14.18]]) and to the Christian sacrament instituted in [[Luke 22.42|Luke 22]].
