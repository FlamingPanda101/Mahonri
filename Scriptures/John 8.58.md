# John 8:58

> Jesus said unto them, Verily, verily, I say unto you, Before Abraham was, I am. *(KJV)*

## The "I AM" (ego eimi)
The Greek *ego eimi* ("I am") is grammatically unusual — the present tense "I am" follows the past tense "Abraham was" — creating a noticeable cue that something more is being signaled. This is widely recognized as a coded reference to the divine name.

## Two Traditions Fused in the Septuagint
The Greek *ego eimi* renders two distinct Hebrew phrases in the Septuagint (LXX):
1. **Exodus 3:14:** *ehyeh asher ehyeh* ("I will be what I will be" or "I am what I am") — translated as *ego eimi ho on* ("I am the one who is") then *ego eimi* ("I am") sent you.
2. **Deutero-Isaiah (and elsewhere):** *ani hu* ("I am he") — also rendered *ego eimi* in the LXX.

These two different divine self-identifications collapse into the same Greek phrase, making *ego eimi* pregnant with divine-name significance.

## Divine Name Agency, Not Ontological Identity
[[Dan McClellan]] argues that possessing and uttering the divine name is a claim to being the **authorized bearer** of the name — not a claim to be God ontologically. In ancient Near Eastern thought, the divine name was a communicable vehicle of divine agency: "when you impose the divine name, that charged the material media with the divine presence, which allowed the material media to presence the deity, manifest the presence, the power."[^1] Jesus is saying "guess who got the divine name" — a claim to divine agency that is a form of blasphemy, but not a claim to *be* the God of Israel.[^2]
