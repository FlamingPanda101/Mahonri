# Jacob 2:27-30

> Wherefore, my brethren, hear me, and hearken to the word of the Lord: For there shall not any man among you have save it be one wife; and concubines he shall have none... For if I will, saith the Lord of Hosts, raise up seed unto me, I will command my people; otherwise they shall hearken unto these things. *(Jacob 2:27, 30, Book of Mormon)*

## Key detail
The Book of Mormon establishes **monogamy as the norm** ("there shall not any man among you have save it be one wife") while reserving an explicit **divine exception**: "if I will... raise up seed unto me, I will command my people." Monogamy is the default; plural marriage is permissible only when God commands it for a purpose.

## Apologetic application
[[The Bald Coder]] cites this in passing — that although the Book of Mormon (like the 1835 D&C) makes monogamy the norm, Jacob 2 itself allows for commanded exceptions, so a "monogamy is the rule" proof-text does not by itself refute Joseph Smith's plural marriage. The verse is the standard LDS scriptural frame for plural marriage as a commanded exception, paralleling [[Doctrine and Covenants 132|D&C 132]].
