# Acts 1:18-19 (NRSV)

> "Now this man acquired a field with the reward of his wickedness; and falling headlong, he burst open in the middle and all his bowels gushed out. This became known to all the residents of Jerusalem, so that the field was called in their language Hakeldama, that is, Field of Blood."

**Note:** Luke's account of the death of Judas — Judas himself *acquires the field*, dies by **falling headlong and bursting open**, and the field is the "Field of Blood" because of *his* gore. This conflicts with [[Matthew 27.3-5|Matthew 27:3-5]] on the manner of death, who bought the field, when, and why it bears the name. The verb "acquired" (*ktaomai*) is where harmonizers (e.g. Daniel Wallace) appeal to a middle-voice/causative reading to detach Judas from the purchase — a possible-but-implausible move per McClellan.
