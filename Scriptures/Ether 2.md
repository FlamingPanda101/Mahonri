# Ether 2

Following the confounding of languages at the great tower, the Jaredites — the brother of Jared, Jared, their families, and their friends — gather their flocks, seeds, and provisions and journey into the wilderness, guided by the Lord. They cross many waters, dwelling in tents in a valley called Nimrod, where the Lord speaks to the brother of Jared from a cloud and directs their travels toward a choice land, a land of promise reserved for a righteous people. After traveling and pitching tents by the seashore in a place they call Moriancumer, they remain for the space of four years. The Lord then chastens the brother of Jared for three hours for failing to call upon him, and renews the covenant of the promised land. He commands the brother of Jared to build barges in which to cross the great sea. The barges are built tight like a dish, but the brother of Jared cries to the Lord that they have no light and no air within them; the Lord instructs him to make holes that can be opened and stopped for air, and asks what should be done about light, setting up the famous episode of the shining stones in the following chapter.

## Why it's cited
This chapter is cited in apologetics discussions of the Jaredite migration and the construction of the sealed barges — and especially in arguments about literary parallels between the Jaredite voyage and the Genesis flood/ark account. The note that uses it addresses the claim that shared biblical motifs (an enclosed vessel, a divinely directed ocean crossing, a covenant land) represent borrowing or plagiarism, arguing instead that common ancient sources and typological patterning, not copying, explain the resemblance.
