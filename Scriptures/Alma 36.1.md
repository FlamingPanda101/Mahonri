# Alma 36:1 — Alma's counsel to Helaman (the chiasmus chapter)

> My son, give ear to my words; for I swear unto you, that inasmuch as ye shall keep the commandments of God ye shall prosper in the land. *(Alma 36:1, Book of Mormon)*

## Key Structure
- **Chiasmus** — Alma 36 is the most-analyzed chiastic structure in the Book of Mormon: a roughly **17-layer** AB...X...BA pattern. Alma's conversion sits at the center, with **Jesus Christ** named as the precise pivot point and the thematic elements mirroring outward in both directions. Chiasmus is an ancient Hebrew/Near Eastern poetic device (inverted parallelism) not systematically recognized in Western scholarship until the mid-19th century.

## Context
[[John W. Welch]] identified the Book of Mormon's chiasmus in 1967. Physicists [[Boyd Edwards]] and [[Farrell Edwards]] later estimated the probability of Alma 36's structure arising by chance at between 1 in 10,000 and 1 in a million. The chapter is cited as evidence that the text reflects ancient composition Joseph Smith could not have engineered deliberately while dictating without notes.
