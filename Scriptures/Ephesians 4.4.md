# Ephesians 4:4-6

> There is one body, and one Spirit, even as ye are called in one hope of your calling; one Lord, one faith, one baptism, one God and Father of all, who is above all, and through all, and in you all. *(KJV)*

## In context
Paul's appeal to the unity of the church — "one Lord, one faith, one baptism." [[Shiloh Lofgren]] cites it against the doctrine of perspicuity: if Scripture alone were a sufficiently clear guide, the result should be the "one baptism" Paul commands, yet sola-scriptura Protestants disagree sharply on what baptism even *does* (regeneration vs. symbol) and whom it is for (infants vs. believers). The verse functions as a scriptural benchmark that modern denominational division fails to meet.
