# Ezekiel 36

> Then will I sprinkle clean water upon you, and ye shall be clean: from all your filthiness, and from all your idols, will I cleanse you. A new heart also will I give you, and a new spirit will I put within you... and I will put my spirit within you. *(KJV, vv. 25-27)*

A new-covenant promise linking the **sprinkling of clean water**, **spiritual rebirth** (a new heart), the **gift of the Spirit**, and **entrance into the people of God**.

## In the baptism debate
[[Joe Heschmeyer]] calls Ezekiel 36 his "personal favorite" Old Testament background to [[John 3.5]]: it foretells the same four things scripture says are fulfilled in baptism (cleansing by water, rebirth, the Spirit, and incorporation into God's people), which is why Jesus expected [[Nicodemus]], "a teacher of Israel," to recognize the water-and-Spirit motif. [[Ryan (NeedGod)|Ryan]] uses the *same* passage for the opposite end: "born of water" in John 3:5 means the Ezekiel-36 sprinkling that symbolizes forgiveness of sins, accomplished by the Spirit through faith — not the ordinance of water baptism. See [[Old Testament Water Types Prefigure Baptism]] and [[John 3.5 Born of Water and the Spirit Is Disputed Between Baptism and Spiritual Rebirth]].
