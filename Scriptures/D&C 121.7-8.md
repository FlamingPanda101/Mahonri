# D&C 121:7–8 — "peace be unto thy soul"

> O God, where art thou? And where is the pavilion that covereth thy hiding place? *(D&C 121:1)* ... My son, peace be unto thy soul; thine adversity and thine afflictions shall be but a small moment; and then, if thou endure it well, God shall exalt thee on high. *(D&C 121:7–8)*

## Key Detail
- Joseph Smith's anguished cry from **Liberty Jail** (winter 1838–39) and God's answer. [[David Paulsen]] uses it as the centerpiece of the **practical problem of evil**: even the prophet "experienced the troubling sense of God's absence," yet found that affliction is "but a small moment" with eternal purpose. The reply reframes suffering as exaltation-bearing rather than meaningless.
