# John 14:9

> Jesus saith unto him, Have I been so long time with you, and yet hast thou not known me, Philip? he that hath seen me hath seen the Father; and how sayest thou then, Shew us the Father? *(KJV)*

## Key Greek term
- **ἑωρακὼς ἐμὲ ἑώρακεν τὸν πατέρα (heōrakōs eme heōraken ton patera)** — "the one who has seen me has seen the Father." The seeing-language is one of *manifestation/presence*, not strict identity.

## Significance
A standard Trinitarian/deity-of-Christ proof text. Dan McClellan reads it instead through the **logic of divine images**: to see Jesus is to see the Father not because Jesus *is* the Father (in his framing, A≠B) but because Jesus **bears and manifests the Father's presence** — exactly as a divine image bears the presence of a god located elsewhere.
