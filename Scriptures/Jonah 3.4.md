# Jonah 3:4

> "And Jonah began to enter into the city a day's journey, and he cried, and said, Yet forty days, and Nineveh shall be overthrown." (KJV)

**Hebrew:** the unconditional-sounding oracle (*'od arba'im yom v'Ninveh nehpaket*) is *not* fulfilled — Nineveh repents and is spared (Jonah 3:10). The verse is the standard biblical proof that prophetic predictions can carry an *implicit conditionality*: God relents when the people repent. Even the Catholic interlocutor in the Ward Radio source concedes Jonah as the steelman for why Joseph Smith's unfulfilled temple prophecies need not mark him a false prophet under the Deuteronomy 18 test.
