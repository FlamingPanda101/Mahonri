# Mosiah 14

In Mosiah 14, the prophet [[Abinadi]] — on trial before King Noah and his priests — quotes the whole of [[Isaiah 53]] (the fourth Servant Song): "He is despised and rejected of men; a man of sorrows… he was wounded for our transgressions… he was led as a sheep to the slaughter." He then expounds it (Mosiah 15) as the suffering and atonement of Christ.

## Significance
[[Mike Day]] and [[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)]] read this through [[John Eaton]]'s festal drama: Isaiah 53 portrays the suffering, dying, redeemed *king*, and Abinadi — possibly the lost prince of the house of Nephi — both **quotes** that text and **enacts** it, standing humiliated and about to be executed before a usurper-king. The doubling (a Davidic-figure citing the suffering-king song while living it before Caiaphas-like Noah) is offered as an unconscious literary feat for a 19th-century dictation. See [[Abinadi Enacts the Suffering King While Quoting the Servant Song]] and [[The Servant Songs Portray the Suffering King of the Festival]].
