# Matthew 12:32

> And whosoever speaketh a word against the Son of man, it shall be forgiven him: but whosoever speaketh against the Holy Ghost, it shall not be forgiven him, neither in this world, neither in the world to come. *(KJV)*

## Significance
A key text in the early development of **purgatory** and remission of sins in the afterlife. As D. Jill Kirby notes in *Ancient Christians*, the phrase "neither in this world, neither in the world to come" implied to many early Christians that *some* sins could be forgiven after death. [[Augustine]] used the verse but set the West's boundary at one's orientation toward God *while living*: a life "neither good enough" nor "bad enough" might benefit from postmortem relief, but merit or demerit is acquired only in this life. The verse underlies the LDS reading of an intermediate state of continued spiritual progression (compare D&C 138).
