# Hosea 2:19-20 — "I will betroth thee unto me forever"

> And I will betroth thee unto me for ever; yea, I will betroth thee unto me in righteousness, and in judgment, and in lovingkindness, and in mercies. I will even betroth thee unto me in faithfulness: and thou shalt know the LORD. *(KJV)*

## Key phrase
"**I will betroth thee unto me for ever**" — the LORD speaks to the house of Israel in explicit **marriage/betrothal** terms.

## Apologetic / typological application
[[Andrea Woodmansee]] cites this as a anchor text for the thesis that Israel's covenant with God is a literal **betrothal**: a Hebrew betrothal (*erusin*) was binding and permanent ("once you're betrothed you were married"), so "I will betroth thee unto me forever" is wedding language, not mere metaphor. It sits alongside [[D&C 109|D&C 109:73-74]] (the Church "adorned as a bride") as evidence that scripture's marriage imagery encodes the ancient wedding model.
