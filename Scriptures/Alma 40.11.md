# Alma 40:11

> 11 Now, concerning the state of the soul between death and the resurrection—Behold, it has been made known unto me by an angel, that the spirits of all men, as soon as they are departed from this mortal body, yea, the spirits of all men, whether they be good or evil, are taken home to that God who gave them life. *(Book of Mormon, Alma 40:11)*

## Significance
Alma's revelation on the **intermediate state**: the moment the spirit leaves the body it is "taken home to that God who gave them life." This is the Restoration's frame for the conscious, disembodied existence reported in near-death experiences — the survivor's spirit perceives, acts, and is drawn toward a luminous presence and toward departed loved ones, then is sent back when "work on earth has not been completed." It grounds the [[NDE - New Views of Death]] (death as a homecoming, not annihilation) and the conscious out-of-body perception behind [[NDE - Corroboration of Veridical Details]]. Compare [[Most Early Christians Believed in an Intermediate State Between Death and Resurrection]].
