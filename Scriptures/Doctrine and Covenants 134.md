# Doctrine and Covenants 134

Section 134 is a declaration "of belief regarding governments and laws in general," adopted by a general assembly of the Church at Kirtland, Ohio, on August 17, 1835, and published with the first edition of the Doctrine and Covenants. It affirms that governments are instituted by God for the benefit of man, that He holds men accountable for their acts in relation to those governments, and that citizens are bound to uphold and sustain the laws of the lands in which they reside. It teaches that no government can stand in peace unless laws are framed and held inviolate that secure freedom of conscience, the right to life and property, and the free exercise of religion. The text was not received as a revelation in Joseph Smith's voice; it is generally attributed to Oliver Cowdery and represents the Church's formal statement on the proper relationship between religious societies and civil authority.

## Why it's cited

Apologetics and historical notes cite Section 134 as the canonical basis for the Church's posture of deference to civil government — the scriptural companion to the [[12th Article of Faith]]. It appears prominently in analysis of the Church's accommodationist survival strategy under hostile regimes (notably Nazi Germany), where LDS leaders pointed to this declaration to explain their stance toward state authority. It is the passage invoked to argue that submission to government in those circumstances was a documented application of LDS teaching rather than mere expediency or cowardice.
