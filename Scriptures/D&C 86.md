# D&C 86

> **1** ...concerning the parable of the wheat and of the tares: **2** Behold, the field was the world, and the apostles were the sowers of the seed; **3** And after they have fallen asleep the great persecutor of the church, the apostate, the whore, even Babylon... soweth the tares; wherefore, the tares choke the wheat, and drive the church into the wilderness. ... **8** ...with whom the priesthood hath continued through the lineage of your fathers— **9** For ye are lawful heirs, according to the flesh, and have been hid from the world with Christ in God— **10** Therefore your life and the priesthood have remained, and must needs remain through you and your lineage until the restoration of all things... (D&C 86)

A revelation given to [[Joseph Smith]] at Kirtland on 6 December 1832, while he and Sidney Rigdon were working on the Bible translation. It reinterprets the Parable of the Wheat and the Tares ([[Matthew 13.24-30]]) as an explanation of the Great Apostasy and the Restoration.

In [[Scripture Central - The Apostasy Isnt All You Think Church History Matters|Church History Matters]], [[Scott Woodward|Woodward]] and [[Casey Paul Griffiths|Griffiths]] stress two distinctives: (1) it is Christ himself testifying that an apostasy occurred — the church "driven into the wilderness" (an allusion to [[Revelation 12]]) rather than annihilated; and (2) the priesthood lineage "remained" through the covenant faithful even during the apostasy, so God's work "never paused." See [[The Apostasy Was the Church Driven Into the Wilderness Not Annihilated]] and [[The Priesthood Lineage Continued Hidden Through the Apostasy]].
