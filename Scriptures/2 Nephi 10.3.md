# 2 Nephi 10:3

> Wherefore, as I said unto you, it must needs be expedient that Christ — for in the last night the angel spake unto me that this should be his name — should come among the Jews...

## Context

In this verse Jacob reports that an angel revealed the name **Christ** to him.
The Saints Unscripted video and its critic treat this as the narrative moment at
which the savior's specific name is first disclosed within the Book of Mormon
storyline. Because the name (Jesus/Christ) appears in earlier passages of
[[1 Nephi 13.40|1 Nephi]] in the 1830 edition, later editions changed some of
those earlier occurrences from "Jesus" to "Messiah" so this verse remains the
true "first revelation" of the name. Apologists frame the edits as preserving
narrative consistency, not altering doctrine.

## Key Exegetical Point

- **The name-revelation anchor:** 2 Nephi 10:3 is the in-text benchmark for when
  the name is given. Editorial changes elsewhere are calibrated against it.
