# Abraham 1:21-27

> 21 Now this king of Egypt was a descendant from the loins of Ham, and was a partaker of the blood of the Canaanites by birth.
> 22 From this descent sprang all the Egyptians, and thus the blood of the Canaanites was preserved in the land.
> 23 The land of Egypt being first discovered by a woman, who was the daughter of Ham...
> 24 ...Egyptus, which in the Chaldean signifies Egypt, which signifies that which is forbidden;
> 25 When this woman discovered the land it was under water, who afterward settled her sons in it...
> 26 Pharaoh, being a righteous man... a partaker of the blood of the Canaanites...
> 27 Now, Pharaoh being of that lineage by which he could not have the right of Priesthood, notwithstanding the Pharaohs would fain claim it from Noah, through Ham...

The passage describing [[Pharaoh]] as "cursed as pertaining to the priesthood" through descent from [[Ham]] and the Canaanites. Historically read as a charter for the [[priesthood ban]]; [[Don Bradley]] argues it instead encodes a division of stewardship (kingly line, not priestly line), the flip side of a blessing.
