# Genesis 3:22

> And the Lord God said, Behold, the man is become as one of us, to know good and evil: and now, lest he put forth his hand, and take also of the tree of life, and eat, and live for ever. *(Genesis 3:22, KJV)*

After the Fall, God himself declares "the man is become as one of us, to know good and evil."

## Why it's cited
This is the LDS rejoinder to the charge that becoming "as gods" ([[Genesis 3.5|Genesis 3:5]]) was merely the serpent's lie: God affirms the outcome in the first person plural ("one of us"). The panel notes critics restrict this to knowing good from evil — but that is exactly what the serpent said, so the affirmation stands. The "one of us" plural also features in the vault's plurality-of-divine-beings material.
