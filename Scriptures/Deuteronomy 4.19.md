# Deuteronomy 4:19

> "And lest thou lift up thine eyes unto heaven, and when thou seest the sun, and the moon, and the stars, even all the host of heaven, shouldest be driven to worship them, and serve them, which the LORD thy God hath divided unto all nations under the whole heaven." (KJV)

The "host of heaven" (*tzeva ha-shamayim*) is a traditional West Asian designation for the gods. The verse says Yahweh **allotted** (*chalaq*) them to the other nations to worship — they are not for Israel. It is widely read as a later development of the inheritance theology of [[Deuteronomy 32.8-9|Deut 32:8–9]], presupposing the existence of the very beings the chapter's "no other" rhetoric (4:35, 39) supposedly denies.
