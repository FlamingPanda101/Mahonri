# 1 John 4:1–3 — Test the Spirits / Christ Come in the Flesh

> "Beloved, believe not every spirit, but try the spirits whether they are of God: because many false prophets are gone out into the world. Hereby know ye the Spirit of God: Every spirit that confesseth that Jesus Christ is come in the flesh is of God: And every spirit that confesseth not that Jesus Christ is come in the flesh is not of God: and this is that spirit of antichrist, whereof ye have heard that it should come; and even now already is it in the world." (KJV)

Day reads this as John's first-century campaign against **Docetism** (Greek *dokeō*, "to seem") — the belief that Jesus only *seemed* to have a body and only *seemed* to die. The insistence that Christ "is come in the flesh" is an anti-Docetic test. See [[Docetism and Early Christological Diversity]].
