# Luke 6:36 (KJV)

> "Be ye therefore merciful, as your Father also is merciful."

**Note:** Luke 6:36 is the Synoptic **parallel** to [[Matthew 5.48|Matthew 5:48]]. Where Matthew has "be ye therefore **perfect**," Luke has "be ye therefore **merciful**" — both sit inside a *love-your-enemies* discourse and share the "as your Father" structure. The divergence is a key datum that the two evangelists are rendering the same saying, and that "perfect" in Matthew means something close to "merciful / wholly loving," not "flawless."
