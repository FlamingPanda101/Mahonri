# Matthew 24:34

> Verily I say unto you, This generation shall not pass, till all these things be fulfilled. *(KJV)*

## Key Greek term
**genea (γενεά)** — "generation." The interpretive crux: whether it denotes Jesus' contemporaries (~40 years), a future end-times generation, or "this race/people." The dispute over its referent is exactly the harmonization Ostler contests.

## Key point
[[Blake Ostler]] calls this "the hardest [prophecy] for me as a Christian." Jesus in one breath predicts the temple's destruction (fulfilled in AD 70) *and* his return "in power and glory" before the generation passes — yet that return did not occur. Ostler argues that redefining *genea* for the second-coming clause while reading it literally for the temple clause is illegitimate "mental gymnastics," and that the simplest reading is that the prophecy failed — evidence that [[Failed Prophecies Show Scripture Is Not Infallible|scripture is not infallible]]. It also stands in tension with [[Mark 13.32|Mark 13:32]], where the Son disclaims knowledge of the day and hour.
