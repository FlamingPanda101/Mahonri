# Genesis 2:24 — "they shall be one flesh"

> Therefore shall a man leave his father and his mother, and shall cleave unto his wife: and they shall be one flesh. *(KJV)*

## Key phrase
"**Shall cleave unto his wife: and they shall be one flesh**" — the Edenic charter of marriage, spoken of [[Adam]] and Eve.

## Apologetic / typological application
[[Andrea Woodmansee]] notes the Hebrew verb for "cleave" here is *davak* (דבק) — the same verb used in "cleave unto the LORD your God" (e.g. Joshua 23:8). Jewish mysticism is *devekut*, "cleaving" to Yahweh, which she contrasts with Eastern mysticism's drive toward self-extinction (Nirvana): Jewish union is cleaving, not ceasing to exist. The marriage "one flesh" and Jesus's prayer that disciples "be one" as he and the Father are one (John 17) share the same oneness, tying covenant marriage to union with God.
