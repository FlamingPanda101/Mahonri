# 1 Nephi

The first book of the Book of Mormon, written by Nephi, the son of Lehi. It opens about 600 BC with the prophet Lehi warning Jerusalem of its coming destruction; commanded by God, he takes his family into the wilderness. His sons return twice to Jerusalem — first to obtain the **brass plates** (the scriptural record) from Laban, then to bring back the family of Ishmael so the sons can marry. Lehi and Nephi each receive the great vision of the **tree of life**, with its iron rod, river, and great and spacious building, which Nephi expands into a sweeping prophecy of the Messiah, the apostasy, and the latter-day restoration of scripture. After much rebellion by the elder brothers Laman and Lemuel, the family builds a ship and crosses the ocean to the **promised land** in the Americas, where the book closes with Nephi's people beginning to keep the commandments.

## Why it's cited

As the opening narrative of the Book of Mormon, 1 Nephi is the primary text for apologetic claims about the book's ancient setting — Lehi's pre-exilic Jerusalem milieu, his lineage from Joseph, and the "First Temple" theology (visions of God enthroned, a divine council, a Messiah in the flesh) that critics once called anachronistic but defenders argue reflects exactly the older Israelite religion being suppressed in Lehi's day. Its tree-of-life vision is also the seedbed for temple, Asherah, and condescension-of-God arguments.
