# Genesis 3:4-5

> And the serpent said unto the woman, Ye shall not surely die: For God doth know that in the day ye eat thereof, then your eyes shall be opened, and ye shall be as gods, knowing good and evil. *(KJV)*

## Significance
[[Irenaeus of Lyons]] made a striking concession about this verse: "To a certain extent, the serpent was correct when he told Adam and Eve that if they partook of the fruit they would 'be as the gods, knowing good and evil.' The problem was in the timing." For Irenaeus, Adam and Eve were innocent children "unaccustomed to, and unexercised in, perfect discipline" who needed to grow (cf. Paul's milk-before-meat, 1 Corinthians 3:2) before receiving the knowledge of good and evil. The verse thus supports his reading of the Fall as premature growth, not corruption — and resonates with the LDS view of the Fall as a step toward divine potential.
