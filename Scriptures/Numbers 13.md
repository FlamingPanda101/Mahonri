# Numbers 13 — The spies' report on the Nephilim of Canaan

> And there we saw the giants [Nephilim], the sons of Anak, which come of the giants: and we were in our own sight as grasshoppers, and so we were in their sight. *(Numbers 13:33, KJV)*

The twelve spies sent into Canaan return with a "evil report": the land "eateth up the inhabitants thereof," its people are "men of a great stature," and "there we saw the Nephilim, the sons of Anak."

## Key Term
- **Nephilim / "sons of Anak"** — the same term as [[Genesis 6.1-4|Genesis 6:4]]. [[Jonah Barnes]] argues these are not literal giants but the *fallen/sterilizing apostates* (the "men… no women in Canaan, only a kidnapped sex-slave"), so that the conquest targets a child-killing cult rather than ordinary humans. The host presses the counterpoint: "we were as grasshoppers… so we were in their sight" reads as *physical* stature, which Barnes concedes he must attribute to textual corruption.
