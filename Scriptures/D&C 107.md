# D&C 107 — "after the Order of the Son of God"

> Before [Melchizedek's] day it was called the Holy Priesthood, after the Order of the Son of God. But out of respect or reverence to the name of the Supreme Being, to avoid the too frequent repetition of his name, they, the church, in ancient days, called that priesthood after Melchizedek, or the Melchizedek Priesthood. *(D&C 107:3-4)*

## Key Detail
- The canonical statement that the higher priesthood is itself **"the holy order after the Son of God,"** originally "the order of Enoch," and was renamed *Melchizedek* only to avoid over-repeating the divine name. [[Todd McLauchlin]] makes this the anchor for [[Priesthood Is an Order Patterned on the Atonement Not a Power|reading priesthood as an order/pattern, not a possessed power]] — the same thing under three labels. Pairs with [[Alma 13|Alma 13]]'s "after the order of the Son of God."
