# John 1:18

> No man hath seen God at any time; the only begotten Son, which is in the bosom of the Father, he hath declared him. *(KJV)*

## The Textual Variant
This verse has a significant manuscript variant at the key phrase:

- **Reading 1:** *monogenes huios* — "only/unique Son" (KJV, majority of manuscripts)
- **Reading 2:** *monogenes theos* — "only/unique God" (found in some early manuscripts)

The difference in the earliest period of transmission would have been a single letter due to *nomina sacra* (sacred name abbreviations). The NRSV updated edition renders it "the only Son himself, God" — an attempt to split the difference that [[Dan McClellan]] calls "outright nonsensical."

## The Scholarly Dispute
[[Bruce Metzger]]'s *Textual Commentary on the Greek New Testament* notes *lectio difficilior* (the more difficult reading) favors *theos* — but the editors added a note that this is unlikely because "this would require the author actually have written this, and that just doesn't make any sense."[^1]

*Monogenes theos* would be the only occurrence of this phrase anywhere, while John uses *monogenes huios* several times. Consistency favors "Son." But theological commitments drive the preference — Trinitarians gravitate toward *theos*, while non-Trinitarians favor *huios*.[^2]
