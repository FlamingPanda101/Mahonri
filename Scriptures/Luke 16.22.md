# Luke 16:22

> And it came to pass, that the beggar died, and was carried by the angels into Abraham's bosom: the rich man also died, and was buried. *(KJV)*

## Significance
From the parable of the Rich Man and Lazarus, this verse supplies the image of the **"bosom of Abraham"** — a place of comfort for the righteous dead distinct from the place of torment. D. Jill Kirby cites it as the question driving early reflection: martyrs went straight to heaven ([[Revelation 6.9-11]]), "but what about the righteous who were not killed for their testimony?" The verse anchored early Christian belief in an **intermediate state** between death and the general resurrection, an idea congruent with the LDS spirit world (compare D&C 138).
