# Matthew 13:24-30 — The Wheat and the Tares

> Another parable put he forth unto them, saying, The kingdom of heaven is likened unto a man which sowed good seed in his field: But while men slept, his enemy came and sowed tares among the wheat, and went his way... Let both grow together until the harvest: and in the time of harvest I will say to the reapers, Gather ye together first the tares, and bind them in bundles to burn them: but gather the wheat into my barn. *(KJV)*

A tare is a weed (likely darnel) that resembles wheat in its early growth but chokes the wheat as it matures.

## In context
[[D&C 86]] recasts this parable as an explanation of the **Great Apostasy**: the field is the world, the apostles are the sowers, and after they "fall asleep" the enemy sows tares that "drive the church into the wilderness." [[Scott Woodward|Woodward]] and [[Casey Paul Griffiths|Griffiths]] note Joseph Smith's translation reorders the harvest so the *wheat* is gathered first (matching the latter-day gathering of the righteous before destruction), and that the parable's "let both grow together" supplies an LDS reply to the problem of evil — the wheat may need the tares to mature. See [[The Wheat and Tares Grow Together as a Reply to the Problem of Evil]].
