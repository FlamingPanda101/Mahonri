# 2 Thessalonians 2:5-6

> Remember ye not, that, when I was yet with you, I told you these things? And now ye know what withholdeth that he might be revealed in his time. *(KJV)*

## Key Greek term
**τὸ κατέχον (to katechon)** — "the thing that restrains/withholds" (v.6; cf. the masculine ὁ κατέχων, "the restrainer," v.7). The referent is famously obscure.

## In context
[[Trent Horn]] uses this as concrete proof that the apostles taught binding content *orally* that was never committed to writing: Paul *reminds* the Thessalonians of what he *told* them about who restrains the man of lawlessness, but he never wrote it down for later readers. Bible scholars list six or seven candidates (the Roman Empire, angelic power, etc.) and cannot reconstruct it. This refutes the claim that the apostles preached only what is in Scripture and that all revelation was confined to the written word.
