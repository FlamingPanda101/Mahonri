# Revelation 1:8

> I am Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the ending, saith the Lord, which is, and which was, and which is to come, the Almighty. *(KJV)*

## Key Term: "Alpha and Omega ... even he who was dead"
[[Blake Ostler]] notes that at the opening of Revelation the speaker of "I am Alpha and Omega" is **Yahweh/the Lord**, while later in the chapter (Rev. 1:17–18) the one styled "the first and the last" is the one "who was dead and is alive" — i.e., **Jesus**. Ostler presses: when was Yahweh/Jehovah ever dead? The shared title is explained by the [[Messenger Shaliach Tradition Explains Bearing the Divine Name|messenger tradition]] — Christ speaks the divine titles and attributes as the Father's name-bearing agent. He rejects the Trinitarian appeal to the hypostatic union here, since the text says the title-bearer is "he who was dead."
