# 1 Peter 3:18-20

> For Christ also hath once suffered for sins... being put to death in the flesh, but quickened by the Spirit: By which also he went and preached unto the spirits in prison; Which sometime were disobedient, when once the longsuffering of God waited in the days of Noah... *(KJV)*

## Significance
The locus classicus for the **descent of Christ to the realm of the dead** ("harrowing of hell"). D. Jill Kirby treats it together with 1 Peter 4:6 ("the gospel was preached also to them that are dead"). The patristic question was: *to whom* did Christ preach? [[Ignatius of Antioch]] used it against Judaizers; [[Clement of Alexandria]] reasoned Christ (and the apostles) preached to righteous Israelites and worthy Gentiles; [[Augustine]] found it "disturbing" and set strict limits, denying that unbelievers converted after death. A foundational text behind the LDS doctrine of postmortem missionary work ([[D&C 76]], D&C 138).
