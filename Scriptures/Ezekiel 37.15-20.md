# Ezekiel 37:15-20

> Moreover, thou son of man, take thee one stick, and write upon it, For Judah… then take another stick, and write upon it, For Joseph, the stick of Ephraim… and join them one to another into one stick; and they shall become one in thine hand. *(KJV, abridged)*

## Significance
The "two sticks" passage cited in LDS apologetics as a prophecy of the Bible (Judah) and the Book of
Mormon (Joseph/Ephraim) being joined. [[Dan McClellan]]'s response: the passage is "very clearly"
about the two divided kingdoms — Judah (south) and Israel/Ephraim (north) — being reunited as part of
the restoration of the nation, not two books of scripture. The reading has been "renegotiated… to read
into it something more relevant to us." Connects to the historical point that
[[Israel and Judah Never United|the two kingdoms were distinct entities]].
