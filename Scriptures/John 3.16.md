# John 3:16

> For God so loved the world, that he gave his only begotten Son, that whosoever believeth in him should not perish, but have everlasting life. *(KJV)*

## Significance
The verse most people reach for when asked to "summarize the gospel in one Bible verse." [[Trent Horn]] makes a pointed observation: even though many Protestants treat John's Gospel as sufficient to proclaim the gospel, **"the word *gospel* is not in John's gospel"** — *euangelion* never appears in the Fourth Gospel. At best John 3:16 is "a loose summary of the plan of salvation that flows from what the gospel is," not a definition of the gospel itself. Horn nonetheless rates it a *better* starting answer than appeals to [[2 Corinthians 5.21|2 Cor 5:21]] read through Reformed [[Double Imputation Is Not the Gospel|double imputation]].
