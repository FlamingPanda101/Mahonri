# 1 Thessalonians 2:13

> For this cause also thank we God without ceasing, because, when ye received the word of God which ye heard of us, ye received it not as the word of men, but as it is in truth, the word of God, which effectually worketh also in you that believe. *(KJV)*

**Key Greek:** "word of God" here is *logos tou theou*, applied to the **spoken/preached gospel** ("which ye heard of us"), explicitly contrasted with "the word of men." The referent is oral apostolic proclamation, not a written text — and 1 Thessalonians was likely the earliest NT book, so the "word of God" received could not be a recitation of the (not-yet-existing) NT.
