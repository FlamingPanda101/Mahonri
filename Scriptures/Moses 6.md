# Moses 6

Part of the Joseph Smith Translation / Book of Moses material. Moses 6 (with Moses 7) describes a people called "Canaanites" upon whom "there was a blackness came" and "a curse" (Moses 7:8), among whom the seed of Cain dwelt, and recounts that other peoples did not mix with them and the gospel was not preached to them at that time.

> (Moses 7:8) For behold, the Lord shall curse the land with much heat, and the barrenness thereof shall go forth forever; and there was a blackness came upon all the children of Canaan, that they were despised among all people.

[[Don Bradley]] cites this as a text that *seemed* to mandate segregation and gospel-withholding, yet [[Joseph Smith]] — at the very time he produced it — sent missionaries to the Lamanites (the functional "Canaanites"), declining to replicate the ancient exclusion.
