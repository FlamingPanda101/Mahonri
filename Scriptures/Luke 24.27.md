# Luke 24:27

> And beginning at Moses and all the prophets, he expounded unto them in all the scriptures the things concerning himself. *(KJV)*

> "And beginning with Moses and all the prophets, he interpreted to them in all the scriptures the things concerning himself." *(RSV, as cited by Heal)*

## In context
The risen Christ on the road to Emmaus. Heal makes this verse the charter of Christian Old Testament interpretation: because the good news of Jesus is "the interpretative key to understanding scripture," the Old Testament became for early Christians "a treasury of prophetic words and types." The verse is invoked by Melito of Sardis's *On the Pasch* as the model for finding Christ throughout Israel's scriptures, and underlies Jacob of Serugh's veil-of-Moses typology. (Note: the chapter text cites the Emmaus pericope with a typo as "Luke 27:13–35"; the road to Emmaus is Luke 24:13–35.)
