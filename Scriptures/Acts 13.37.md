# Acts 13:37

> But he, whom God raised again, saw no corruption. *(KJV)*

**Context:** Paul's sermon at Pisidian Antioch quoting **Psalm 16:10** ("thou wilt not suffer thine Holy One to see corruption") and applying it to the risen Christ. James Scott cites Psalm 16:10 (via Acts 13:37) as a case where God is *not* the speaker, so the OT-as-word-of-God formula seems to extend beyond prophecy. Trent Horn (with John C. Poirier) replies that David was viewed as **prophetically inspired**, so the NT still treats the words as oracle — preserving the "word of God = prophecy" pattern.
