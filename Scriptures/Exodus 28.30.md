# Exodus 28:30

> And thou shalt put in the breastplate of judgment the Urim and the Thummim;
> and they shall be upon Aaron's heart, when he goeth in before the LORD: and
> Aaron shall bear the judgment of the children of Israel upon his heart before
> the LORD continually.

## Key Terms

- **אוּרִים וְתֻמִּים (Urim and Thummim)** — commonly rendered "Lights and
  Perfections." A divinatory instrument worn by the high priest of Israel in the
  breastplate, used to receive divine answers.

## The Urim and Thummim as Revelatory Instrument

The source invokes the high priest's Urim and Thummim as the canonical
Old Testament precedent for receiving revelation through a **physical
instrument**, alongside Joseph of Egypt's divining cup and the rods of Moses and
Aaron.[^1] The argument: if Israel's high priest legitimately received
revelation through the Urim and Thummim, then [[Joseph Smith]]'s seer stones and
[[Oliver Cowdery]]'s rod can be understood the same way — as valid God-ordained
"means" — rather than as folk superstition.[^2]
