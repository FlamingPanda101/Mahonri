# Moses 1.6

> And I have a work for thee, Moses, my son; and thou art in the similitude of mine Only Begotten; and mine Only Begotten is and shall be the Savior, for he is full of grace and truth; but there is no God beside me, and all things are present with me, for I know them all.
> — Moses 1:6 (Pearl of Great Price)

## Key point
This is the proof-text for the **priority inversion** [[Don Bradley]] draws from Moses 1: because Moses is "in the similitude of mine Only Begotten," the likeness runs *Moses → Christ* — Moses is a son of God modeled on [[Jesus of Nazareth|Christ]], rather than Christ being merely "a prophet like unto Moses" ([[Deuteronomy 18.15|Deut 18:15]]). See [[Moses Is a Son of God in the Likeness of Jesus Not Jesus a Prophet Like Moses]]. The verse also pairs God's declaration of relationship ("Moses, my son") with the introduction of the Only Begotten as Savior, fitting the dispensation-opening pattern of [[Every Dispensation Opens With God Declaring Divine Sonship]].[^1]
