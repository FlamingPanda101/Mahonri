# D&C 85

> **7** ...I, the Lord God, will send one mighty and strong, holding the scepter of power in his hand, clothed with light for a covering, whose mouth shall utter words, eternal words; while his bowels shall be a fountain of truth, to set in order the house of God, and to arrange by lot the inheritances of the saints... **8** While that man, who was called of God and appointed, that putteth forth his hand to steady the ark of God, shall fall by the shaft of death, like as a tree that is smitten by the vivid shaft of lightning. (D&C 85)

An excerpt from a letter [[Joseph Smith]] wrote to [[W. W. Phelps]] on 27 November 1832, the revelatory portions of which were placed into the Doctrine and Covenants in 1876 by Orson Pratt under [[Brigham Young]]'s direction. It concerns the administration of the law of consecration in Zion (Missouri) and the keeping of Church records.

In [[Scripture Central - The Apostasy Isnt All You Think Church History Matters|Church History Matters]], [[Scott Woodward|Woodward]] and [[Casey Paul Griffiths|Griffiths]] call the "one mighty and strong" the "most misinterpreted prophecy in the whole Doctrine and Covenants," cited by "almost every break-off movement" to justify a self-appointed reformer. Quoting the 1905 First Presidency statement, they argue the prophecy is a *conditional* warning to Bishop [[Edward Partridge]] — who had neglected his duty and "put forth his hand to steady the ark" — that if he failed, another would replace him; because Partridge repented, the occasion passed. See [[The One Mighty and Strong Was Edward Partridge Not a Future Reformer]] and [[Steadying the Ark Names Overreach Beyond Ones Stewardship]].
