# 2 Timothy 3:14-17

> But continue thou in the things which thou hast learned and hast been assured of, knowing of whom thou hast learned them; And that from a child thou hast known the holy scriptures, which are able to make thee wise unto salvation through faith which is in Christ Jesus. All scripture is given by inspiration of God, and is profitable for doctrine, for reproof, for correction, for instruction in righteousness: That the man of God may be perfect, throughly furnished unto all good works. *(KJV)*

## Key Greek term
**θεόπνευστος (theopneustos)** — "God-breathed" (v.16), a rare compound (this is its only NT occurrence). Affirms divine origin/inspiration. **Disputed scope:** whether it entails *infallibility/inerrancy* or chiefly that God *gives life* through his word is contested. In the debate Kelly reads it as establishing scripture's doctrinal sufficiency; Hayden argues it concerns God giving life through his word and, crucially, does **not** identify *which* books are God-breathed (the canon question).

## In context
The "holy scriptures" Timothy knew "from a child" are the **Old Testament / Tanakh** — there was no closed 27-book NT yet. The passage asserts inspiration and usefulness ("profitable for doctrine"); it does not, on its face, define a 66-book canon or assert that canon is the *ultimate, infallible* authority over all others. This is the chief proof-text for *sola scriptura* and the chief battleground of the debate.
