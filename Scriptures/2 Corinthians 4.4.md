# 2 Corinthians 4:4

> In whom the god of this world hath blinded the minds of them which believe not, lest the light of the glorious gospel of Christ, who is the image of God, should shine unto them. *(KJV)*

## Key point
Raised in the recap as a counter-example to the (overstated) claim that Paul "never calls Jesus God." The panel notes Paul does apply *theos*-language in the Christ context here (and elsewhere) — but, consistent with the divine-image and divine-agency framework, "image of God" again distinguishes the Son from the Father he images rather than identifying them as one being. See [[Subordination Within the New Testament]], [[Divine Agency and the Divine-Name Bearer]].
