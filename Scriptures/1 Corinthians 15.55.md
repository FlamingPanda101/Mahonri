# 1 Corinthians 15.55

> O death, where is thy sting? O grave, where is thy victory?

## Why it's cited

Paul's triumphant taunt over death caps his great resurrection chapter, declaring that the grave has been robbed of its power through Christ's victory. It is cited in atonement theology, especially Christus Victor and "ransom by deception" models, where Christ's death and resurrection defeat and despoil the powers that held humanity captive. The verse supplies the language of death's defeat and the emptying of the grave's claim that those models build upon.
