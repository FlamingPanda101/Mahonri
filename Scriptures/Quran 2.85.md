# Quran 2:85 — believe in part and reject the rest?

> ...So do you believe in part of the Scripture and disbelieve in part? Then what is the recompense for those who do that among you except disgrace in worldly life; and on the Day of Resurrection they will be sent back to the severest of punishment. And Allah is not unaware of what you do. *(Sahih International)*

## Key Detail
[[Avery (God Logic)|Avery]]'s decisive counter to the "partial confirmation" and "guardian/criterion" replies (see [[The Guardian and Criterion Reply to the Islamic Dilemma]]). The verse condemns those who "believe in part of the Scripture and disbelieve in part," attaching "disgrace in worldly life" and "the severest punishment." Avery's inference: if selective belief is condemned, the Qur'an affirms **all** of the prior scripture, not a Qur'an-approved subset — so a Muslim cannot escape [[The Islamic Dilemma]] by accepting only the parts that agree with the Qur'an.

**Context dispute.** The surrounding verses ([[Quran 2.85|2:84–85]]) rebuke a specific Israelite generation for killing and expelling one another while ransoming captives — so the final caller ("S") argued the "do you believe in part?" clause is a rhetorical reproach tied to *those* social sins, "not a legal injunction." Avery countered that punishment is still attached to selective belief itself. Both the historical-context reading and Avery's all-or-nothing reading are defensible.
