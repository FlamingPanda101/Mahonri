# Luke 24:5-6

> Why seek ye the living among the dead? He is not here, but is risen: remember how he spake unto you when he was yet in Galilee. *(KJV)*

## Key phrase
"**He is not here, but is risen**" — the angels' Easter-morning announcement to the women at the empty tomb.

## Apologetic / historical application
[[Miranda Wilcox]] uses this verse (Vulgate: *Quid quaeritis viventem cum mortuis? Non est hic sed surrexit*) as the epigraph for the "Resurrection" section of the Medieval Afterword. The dialogue of Luke 24:1-8 was dramatized antiphonally in the late-tenth-century *Regularis Concordia* of Bishop Æthelwold (the *Quem quaeritis* trope: "Whom do you seek?" / "Jesus of Nazareth" / "He is not here; He is risen"), and reenacted in the York Resurrection mystery plays. See [[Medieval Liturgy and Drama Enacted Divine Presence]].
