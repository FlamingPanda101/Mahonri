# Luke 2:49 (KJV)

> And he said unto them, How is it that ye sought me? wist ye not that I must be about my Father's business?

## Translation Note
The Greek is *en tois tou patros mou* — literally "in the … of my Father," with the noun left unexpressed (the article *tois* is dative plural, masculine or neuter). Translators must supply the missing noun. "My Father's **house**" (neuter, the temple) is grammatically awkward because Luke elsewhere refers to the temple with a singular (*to hieron*), not a plural. "My Father's **business**" supplies "affairs." [[Mike Day]] and [[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)]] argue that the most natural supply, taking *tois* as masculine, is "among my Father's **people**" — which matches v. 44, where Joseph and Mary seek Jesus "among their kinsfolk." On this reading Jesus is found among his *true* Father's people, the temple teachers — possibly carriers of an older temple tradition who recognize him. (Raymond Brown noted the option but rejected it.) See [[Luke 2-49 May Read Among My Fathers People Not About My Fathers Business]].
