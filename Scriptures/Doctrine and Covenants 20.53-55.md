# Doctrine and Covenants 20.53-55

"The teacher's duty is to watch over the church always, and be with and strengthen them; And see that there is no iniquity in the church, neither hardness with each other, neither lying, backbiting, nor evil speaking; And see that the church meet together often, and also see that all the members do their duty."

## Why it's cited

This passage defines the office and duty of teachers (and, by the surrounding verses, priests) in the early Church: to watch over, be present with, and continually strengthen the members. Apologetics notes cite it as a scriptural model of close, sustained pastoral care — the "gardening" approach to member retention in which leaders tend the congregation persistently rather than intervening only sporadically. It supports the argument that the revealed pattern of ministry is ongoing presence and nourishment, not occasional contact.
