# Revelation 22:8-9

> And I John saw these things, and heard them. And when I had heard and seen, I fell down to worship before the feet of the angel which shewed me these things. Then saith he unto me, See thou do it not: for I am thy fellowservant, and of thy brethren the prophets, and of them which keep the sayings of this book: worship God. *(KJV)*

## Key Term: "I am thy fellowservant ... worship God"
[[Blake Ostler]] uses this as a decisive counterexample to the inference "claims divine titles → is God / is to be worshiped." An angel appears speaking as God (cf. the "first and the last" language), John falls to worship, and the angel sharply refuses: he is only a *fellow servant*. Ostler's point: bearing the divine name and speaking divine claims as a messenger does **not** make one God — yet the New Testament *does* direct worship to the **Son** (unlike the angel), because the Father has exalted the Son and the two mutually indwell. See [[The Angel in Revelation Who Refuses Worship]] and [[Worship of the Son Is Warranted Through Indwelling]].
