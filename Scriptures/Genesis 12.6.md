# Genesis 12:6 — "the Canaanite was then in the land"

> And Abram passed through the land unto the place of Sichem, unto the plain of Moreh. And the Canaanite was then in the land. *(KJV)*

## Key Detail
The clause "the Canaanite was *then* in the land" presupposes a writer whose own readers live *after* the Canaanites are gone (dispossessed by Israel) — i.e., long after Abraham, and so after [[Moses]] as well. [[Mike Day]] uses it as one of several internal "tells" that the Pentateuch was written/edited later than the events it narrates: "it's kind of written as it's supposing that the Canaanites have been wiped out... it's way after Abraham. And it does this in a couple places." (The same phrasing recurs at Genesis 13:7.) Traditional readers treat such clauses as later editorial glosses on a Mosaic core.
