# Isaiah 44:8

> Fear ye not, neither be afraid: have not I told thee from that time, and have declared it? ye are even my witnesses. Is there a God beside me? yea, there is no God; I know not any. *(KJV)*

## Used against the Church (by Catholic apologists)
[[Trent Horn]] presses "Is there a God besides me? I know not any" against LDS henotheism: if the God of this world were omniscient and had himself once been a man exalted by a prior God, "wouldn't he know about the God that he worshiped when he was a man?" See [[Mormonism Rejects Classical Theism So the God Objection Bites]] and [[Ancient Israelite Henotheism Was Not Mormon Henotheism]].

## In context — the trial of the gods
Like [[Isaiah 43.10|Isaiah 43:10]], this verse sits in the Isaiah 40–48 courtroom polemic against **manufactured idols** (44:9–20 mocks the man who carves a god from the leftover of his firewood). The "I know not any" is read by Latter-day Saints as Yahweh's incomparability over carved rivals and a boast of supremacy, not a census denying the divine council. See [[Babylon Taunt Formula Shows Negation Is a Boast of Superiority]].
