# Jacob 2:17 — Be Familiar With All and Free With Your Substance

> **17** Think of your brethren like unto yourselves, and be familiar with all and free with your substance, that they may be rich like unto you. (Jacob 2:17)

[[Sharon Eubank]] names this her "absolute favorite scripture in the Book of Mormon" in the [[Let's Get Real with Stephen Jones - Sharon Eubank on Doing Small Things with Great Love (E0040)|Let's Get Real interview]]. She stresses the audience: Jacob is addressing his own relatives, including the lineage of Laman and Lemuel who had tried to kill Nephi — so "think of your brethren like unto yourselves" is a form of the golden rule extended even to adversaries. She reads "free with your substance" beyond money to mean "the dignity, the agency, the friendship, the prayer," and "rich like unto you" as rich "in friendship, in resources, and opportunities, and talent" — the gospel reorienting disciples "away from worrying about physical things to worrying about each other as human beings."[^1] See [[Free With Your Substance Means More Than Money]].
