# 1 Chronicles 21:1 (KJV / Hebrew note)

> "And Satan stood up against Israel, and provoked David to number Israel."

**Key Hebrew term:** here *satan* (שָׂטָן) appears **without the definite article** (anarthrous) — unlike the *ha-satan* ("the accuser") of [[Job 1]] and [[Zechariah 3]]. This is the Chronicler's rewrite of [[2 Samuel 24.1|2 Samuel 24:1]], where it is *Yahweh* who incites David. The interpretive crux: is this still a generic "an adversary" (so the Chronicler offloads blame from God onto an unnamed accuser), or the **first occurrence of "Satan" as a personal name** in the Hebrew Bible? [[Esther Hamori]] (*God's Monsters*) argues the latter; Dan McClellan argues the former.
