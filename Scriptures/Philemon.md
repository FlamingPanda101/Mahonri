# Philemon (Epistle of Paul)

A single-chapter letter in which Paul writes to Philemon, a member of the Jesus movement, on behalf of Onesimus — Philemon's runaway slave, who has since become a believer. Paul sends Onesimus back to his master, asking that he be received kindly (and hinting he be freed).

## Significance
[[Brian Thomassen]] uses Philemon as a test case for Stoic embeddedness in the New Testament. The puzzle — why does Paul return a man to servitude rather than insist on his freedom? — dissolves on a Stoic reading: for the Stoics outward status (slave or free) is *adiaphoron*, "indifferent," so what matters is the heart, not the social station. (Epictetus, himself a former slave, was a leading Stoic.) On this view the letter could enter the canon "without any question." See [[Philemon Reads Coherently Through Stoic Indifference to Status]]. Compare the vault's broader treatment of New Testament slavery in [[The New Testament Does Not Teach Abolition]].
