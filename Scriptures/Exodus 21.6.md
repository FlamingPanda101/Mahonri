# Exodus 21:6

> Then his master shall bring him unto the judges; he shall also bring him to the door, or unto the door post; and his master shall bore his ear through with an aul; and he shall serve him for ever. *(KJV)*

## Textual / Lexical Note — *ha-elohim*
The KJV renders the Hebrew **ha-elohim** (הָאֱלֹהִים) as "the judges." This is one of the classic passages behind the post-biblical "*elohim* = human judges" interpretation. **[[Dan McClellan]]** argues this rendering is anachronistic: the term should be read as a reference to **God** (or possibly to household/shrine deities), not to human magistrates. He notes that the "human judges" reading of *elohim* is a post-biblical (2nd–3rd century CE) renegotiation that arose from trying to harmonize the many *elohim* "running around doing weird things" in Exodus 18, 21, 22, Genesis 6, and Psalm 82.

Key Hebrew term:
- **ha-elohim** (הָאֱלֹהִים) — "God" / "the god(s)," rendered "the judges" by translators following the later interpretation.
