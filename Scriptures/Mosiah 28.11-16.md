# Mosiah 28:11-16

> Therefore he [Mosiah] took the records which were engraven on the plates of brass, and also the plates of Nephi... and translated them by the means of those two stones which were fastened into the two rims of a bow... whosoever has these things is called seer, after the manner of old times. *(Mosiah 28:11-16, abridged, Book of Mormon)*

## Key Detail

Mosiah II uses the two interpreters (the "two stones... fastened into the two rims of a bow") to translate the 24 Jaredite plates. This is the Book of Mormon's explicit statement that the Nephites possessed and used the interpreters centuries before [[Moroni]] buried them — but the text never describes *how* the Nephites first obtained them. [[Don Bradley]] argues the missing acquisition account was in the Lost 116 Pages and is partially preserved in the [[Fayette Lapham]] interview ([[Mosiah]] I led to the interpreters by the Liahona).
