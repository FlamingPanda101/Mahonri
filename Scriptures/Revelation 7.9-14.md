# Revelation 7:9-14 — the great multitude in white robes before the throne

> After this I beheld, and, lo, a great multitude, which no man could number, of all nations, and kindreds, and people, and tongues, stood before the throne, and before the Lamb, clothed with white robes, and palms in their hands... These are they which came out of great tribulation, and have washed their robes, and made them white in the blood of the Lamb. *(KJV, abridged)*

## Key Detail
John of Patmos's vision of heaven gives three details the temple endowment re-enacts: (1) the redeemed are **clothed in white robes**; (2) they **stand before the throne** of God and the Lamb; (3) their robes are **washed white in the blood of the Lamb** — purity is Christ's, not the wearer's own. The multitude is explicitly drawn from "all nations, and kindreds, and people, and tongues," a universal rather than ethnic priesthood. Read alongside [[Revelation 5.9-10|Rev 5:9-10]] (made "kings and priests"), the passage supplies the scriptural picture for the claim that in the temple one is "learning heaven" — rehearsing this throne-room scene rather than earning entry to it.
