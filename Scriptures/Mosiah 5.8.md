# Mosiah 5:8

> And under this head ye are made free, and there is no other head whereby ye can be made free. There is no other name given whereby salvation cometh; therefore, I would that ye should take upon you the name of Christ, all you that have entered into the covenant with God that ye should be obedient unto the end of your lives. *(Book of Mormon)*

## Key point
The climax of [[King Benjamin]]'s sermon: salvation comes by **one name only**, and the covenant people "take upon them the name of Christ." Earlier in the address Benjamin reports the angel's words that "his name shall be Jesus Christ" and that the Messiah will come "in a tabernacle of clay." [[Don Bradley]] argues this name-of-Christ covenant is precisely the text that strengthened [[Martin Harris]]'s testimony as he scribed the resumed translation — answering Martin's stated longing for "a Church that could bear Christ's name legitimately" — and that "taking upon us the name of Christ" later became a signature theme of [[Dallin H. Oaks]] (1985 conference talk). See [[King Benjamins Speech Strengthened Martin Harriss Testimony More Than Seeing the Plates]].
