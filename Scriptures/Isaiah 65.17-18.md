# Isaiah 65:17-18

> For, behold, I create new heavens and a new earth: and the former shall not be remembered, nor come into mind. But be ye glad and rejoice for ever in that which I create: for, behold, I create Jerusalem a rejoicing, and her people a joy. *(KJV)*

## Key phrase
"**New heavens and a new earth**" — and specifically the re-creation of "Jerusalem a rejoicing, and her people a joy."

## Apologetic / historical application
[[Nicholas J. Frederick]] cites Isaiah 65 among the postexilic prophecies that supplied the imagery for Christian eschatology — the "new heaven and new earth" language reappears in [[Revelation 20.2-3|Revelation]] 21:1 and in Irenaeus's literal millennial hope. See [[Christianity Inherited Jewish Day-of-the-Lord Eschatology]]. The chapter notes that the San Vitale apse mosaic (Ravenna) evokes this new heaven and new earth.
