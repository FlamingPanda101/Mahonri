# 2 Nephi 30:6

> ...and their scales of darkness shall begin to fall from their eyes; and many
> generations shall not pass away among them, save they shall be a **pure** and a
> delightsome people. *(current edition)*

**1830 / pre-1981 reading:** "...save they shall be a **white** and a delightsome
people."

## Context

This is the most famous "white → pure" change in Book of Mormon textual history.
The 1840 edition briefly read "pure," reverted to "white" in subsequent
printings, and was permanently changed to "pure" in the 1981 edition (a few years
after the 1978 revelation extending the priesthood to all worthy males). Critics
argue the timing shows a sociopolitical, race-driven motive; Saints Unscripted
argues "white" and "pure" are near-synonyms in purity idiom and that the real
issue is the broader question of editing revealed text.

## Key Exegetical Point

- **"White" as purity, not race:** In KJV-register English "white" routinely
  denotes moral/ceremonial purity ("they shall be white," Daniel 12:10; "white as
  snow"). The edit makes that sense explicit for modern readers.
