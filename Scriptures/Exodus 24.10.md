# Exodus 24:10

> And they saw the God of Israel: and there was under his feet as it were a paved work of a sapphire stone, and as it were the body of heaven in his clearness. *(KJV)*

## Key Hebrew Terms

- **רָאָה (ra'ah):** "to see" — here in the active: Moses, Aaron, Nadab, Abihu, and seventy elders "saw the God of Israel." A direct narration of seeing God, with anthropomorphic detail (his "feet").
- **אֱלֹהֵי יִשְׂרָאֵל (Elohei Yisrael):** "the God of Israel," seen corporeally standing on a sapphire pavement.

## Context

During the Sinai covenant ceremony, the elders ascend and behold God enthroned, with a sapphire-like pavement beneath his feet, and they eat and drink. [[Dan McClellan]] cites this as a clear counter-text to [[Exodus 33.20|Exodus 33:20]]'s "no one can see me and live," evidence of the Hebrew Bible's conflicting traditions on divine visibility and its underlying conception of a corporeal God. See [[Conflicting Traditions on Seeing Gods Face]], [[Anthropomorphic God in the Hebrew Bible]].
