# Quran 3:55 — followers of Jesus superior until the Resurrection

> [Mention] when Allah said, "O Jesus, indeed I will take you and raise you to Myself and purify you from those who disbelieve and make those who follow you superior to those who disbelieve until the Day of Resurrection. Then to Me is your return, and I will judge between you concerning that in which you used to differ." *(Sahih International)*

## Key Detail
Allah promises that "those who follow [Jesus]" will be made **"superior to those who disbelieve until the Day of Resurrection."** [[Avery (God Logic)|Avery]] converts this triumph-promise into a **preservation guarantee**: if the followers of Jesus must remain superior until the end of the world, then their message could never have been corrupted, overcome, or lost — the opposite of *tahrif* (see [[The Tahrif Debate Among Classical Muslim Scholars]]). It also turns the Islamic-restoration thesis (that monotheist followers of Jesus were later overcome by Trinitarian "corruptors") against the Qur'an, since that would mean the disbelievers *did* become superior. See [[Surah 3-55 The Followers of Jesus Remain Superior Until the Resurrection]].

The key contested word is *fawqa* ("superior / uppermost"): classical *tafsir* often reads it as worldly or eschatological *ascendancy* (Christians, then Muslims, over the Jews who rejected Jesus), not as a guarantee that the New Testament *text* is uncorrupted — so the verse is suggestive for the dilemma rather than decisive.
