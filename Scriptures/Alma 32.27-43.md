# Alma 32:27-43

Alma's discourse comparing faith to a seed and inviting an *experiment upon the word*: plant the seed, and if it is good it will swell, sprout, and grow, demonstrating its goodness by its fruit.

> 27 But behold, if ye will awake and arouse your faculties, even to an experiment upon my words, and exercise a particle of faith...
> 28 Now, we will compare the word unto a seed. Now, if ye give place, that a seed may be planted in your heart... it will begin to swell within your breasts...
> 43 ...ye shall reap the rewards of your faith... and pluck the fruit thereof, which is most precious, which is sweet above all that is sweet...

[[Don Bradley]] points to this experimental epistemology — testing truth by its fruits in one's own life — as the proper way to evaluate the [[Book of Abraham]]. Pairs with the "by their fruits" test of [[Matthew 7.16-20|Matthew 7:16-20]].
