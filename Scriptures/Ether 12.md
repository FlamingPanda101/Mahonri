# Ether 12 — Moroni's discourse on faith; weakness made strong

> Wherefore, whoso believeth in God might with surety hope for a better world... And it was by faith that the law of Moses was given. But in the gift of his Son hath God prepared a more excellent way... And if men come unto me I will show unto them their weakness. I give unto men weakness that they may be humble; and my grace is sufficient for all men that humble themselves before me; for if they humble themselves before me, and have faith in me, then will I make weak things become strong unto them. *(Ether 12:4, 11, 27, Book of Mormon)*

## Key Detail
- Moroni's great chapter on **faith** ("faith is things which are hoped for and not seen," v.6) and the promise that God turns weakness to strength (v.27). [[Todd McLauchlin]] cites it with [[3 Nephi 9|3 Nephi 9]] as a Book of Mormon pattern where faith pulls down collective sanctification, supporting the claim that redemption is [[Magnifying a Calling Means Coming Clean From the Blood and Sins of This Generation|"magnified into" a people through faith and a leader's sacrifice]].
