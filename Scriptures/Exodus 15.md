# Exodus 15

**What:** The **Song of the Sea**, the victory hymn sung after the crossing of the Red Sea, embedded in the Exodus prose narrative. One of the oldest passages in the Hebrew Bible.

**Role in biblical studies:** With the [[Judges 5|Song of Deborah]] (Judges 5) and the [[Deuteronomy 32|Song of Moses]] (Deuteronomy 32), the Song of the Sea is a prime example of **archaic Hebrew poetry** — an older poem preserved inside a later narrative frame, dated earlier than its surrounding prose on linguistic grounds. See [[The Oldest Biblical Texts Are Archaic Poems Embedded in Later Narrative]]. (Exodus 15:11, "Who is like thee among the gods," is also a touchstone for the early-Israelite recognition of other divine beings.)
