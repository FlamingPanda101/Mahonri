# 1 Corinthians 3:13-15

> Every man's work shall be made manifest: for the day shall declare it, because it shall be revealed by fire; and the fire shall try every man's work of what sort it is... If any man's work shall be burned, he shall suffer loss: but he himself shall be saved; yet so as by fire. *(KJV)*

## Significance
The foundational scriptural seed for the doctrine of **purgatory**. D. Jill Kirby observes that Paul here presents fire as both *probative* (v. 13-14, testing the work) and *purifying* (v. 15, "saved; yet so as by fire"). Early Christians built their idea of a purgative experience on this passage, but — unlike medieval purgatory as a place of protracted torment — they "often envision[ed] it as a purgative *event*." The insisted-upon condition was that the person's life "must be founded in Christ," i.e. one must have been a disciple while living. Resonates with the LDS view that postmortem cleansing follows from earthly orientation ([[Alma 34.32-33]]).
