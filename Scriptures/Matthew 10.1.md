# Matthew 10:1 — Jesus Gives the Twelve Power

> And when he had called unto him his twelve disciples, **he gave them power against unclean spirits, to cast them out, and to heal all manner of sickness and all manner of disease.** *(KJV)*

## Key Detail
In the [[The Stick of Joseph - Sacred Temple Connection to Priesthood Keys|Stick of Joseph interview]], this verse is read as Jesus **deliberately conferring authority** on the Twelve — not merely the gifts of the Spirit available to any faithful disciple, but a *setting apart* with delegated power to act in his name. The hosts use the contrast to distinguish (a) miracles/gifts that flow from faith and the Holy Ghost from (b) authorized officiating in ordinances, and infer the conferral here was Melchizedek rather than Aaronic in character. It supports the broader argument that authority to act is *given*, not assumed from spiritual experience.
