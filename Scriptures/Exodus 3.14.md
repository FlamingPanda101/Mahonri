# Exodus 3:14

> And God said unto Moses, I AM THAT I AM: and he said, Thus shalt thou say unto the children of Israel, I AM hath sent me unto you. *(KJV)*

## The Hebrew
The Hebrew phrase is *ehyeh asher ehyeh* — "I will be what I will be" or "I am what I am." The divine name *YHWH* (the Tetragrammaton) is etymologically related to this root.

## The Septuagint Translation
In the Greek Septuagint (LXX), this is rendered:
- *Ego eimi ho on* — "I am the one who is" or "I am the being one"
- Then *ego eimi* — "I am" — sent you

This same *ego eimi* also translates the Hebrew *ani hu* ("I am he") in Deutero-Isaiah and elsewhere, fusing two distinct Hebrew divine self-identifications into one Greek phrase.

## Connection to John's Gospel
This Septuagintal *ego eimi* is the phrase Jesus uses in [[John 8.58|John 8:58]] ("Before Abraham was, I am") and throughout John's Gospel (the seven "I am" statements). [[Dan McClellan]] argues this is a claim to bear the **divine name** (divine agency), not a claim to BE God ontologically. See [[Divine Name Theology]].
