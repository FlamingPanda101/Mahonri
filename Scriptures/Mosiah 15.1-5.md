# Mosiah 15:1-5

> And now Abinadi said unto them: I would that ye should understand that God himself shall come down among the children of men, and shall redeem his people. And because he dwelleth in flesh he shall be called the Son of God, and having subjected the flesh to the will of the Father, being the Father and the Son — The Father, because he was conceived by the power of God; and the Son, because of the flesh; thus becoming the Father and the Son. *(Book of Mormon)*

## Key Term: "the Father and the Son"
A passage often pressed against Latter-day Saints as **modalistic** (the Father and the Son the "same being"). [[Blake Ostler]] answers that the text *explains itself in context*: Christ is called "the Father" because his will is "swallowed up in the will of the Father" so that whatever the Son does is the Father's will, and because the Son did the creation; he is "the Son" because of the flesh. Read in context the title is a *justified* relational claim, not an identity of persons — no more modalistic than John's "he who has seen me has seen the Father."
