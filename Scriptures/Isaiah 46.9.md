# Isaiah 46:9

> Remember the former things of old: for I am God, and there is none else; I am God, and there is none like me. *(KJV)*

## Key Term: "none like me"
Hebrew *eyn kamoni* ("none like me") is comparison language — incomparability — which [[Blake Ostler]] distinguishes sharply from [[Michael Heiser]]'s metaphysical reading of Yahweh as "species unique." Ostler argues the claim is one of *greatness and honor*, a boast of preeminence within the divine council, not an ontological assertion that nothing else shares Yahweh's kind. The Old Testament elsewhere calls other beings *elohim* and the divine council *bene elohim* (sons of God), a genetic likeness.
