# Romans 16:1-7

> I commend to you our sister Phoebe, a deacon of the church at Cenchreae. … Greet Prisca and Aquila who work with me in Christ Jesus … Greet Mary, who has worked very hard among you. Greet Andronicus and Junia, my relatives, who were in prison with me; they are prominent among the apostles, and they were in Christ before I was. *(NRSV)*

## Key Greek terms
- **διάκονος (diakonos)** — "deacon"; applied to **Phoebe**, the same masculine office-word Paul uses of his own ministry. The feminine *diakonissa* does not appear before the 4th century.
- **ἀπόστολος (apostolos)** — "apostle"; applied to **Junia**, a woman, called *episēmos* ("prominent") among the apostles.
- **προστάτις (prostatis)** — patron/benefactor; Paul calls Phoebe his.

## Significance
The cornerstone text of [[Ariel Bybee Laughton]]'s chapter on church organization. It names women as deacon (Phoebe), apostle (Junia), and missionary co-worker (Prisca), evidencing fluid, undefined offices open to women in the first-century church. Junia's name was later changed to the masculine "Junias" on the assumption a woman could not be an apostle. See [[Women Led the First-Century Church as Apostles Deacons and Patrons]], [[Junia Is a Woman Called Prominent Among the Apostles]], and [[Apostle and Deacon Were Fluid Undefined Titles in Paul's Day]].
