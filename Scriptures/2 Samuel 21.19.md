# 2 Samuel 21:19

> "And there was again war with the Philistines at Gob; and **Elhanan** the son of Jaare-oregim, the Bethlehemite, **slew Goliath the Gittite**, the staff of whose spear was like a weaver's beam." (cf. RSV)

This verse credits the killing of Goliath to [[Elhanan]] the Bethlehemite, not to [[David]]. The clause "the shaft of whose spear was like a weaver's beam" is taken straight from 1 Samuel 17:7's description of Goliath, signaling that the very same giant of the famous David story is meant — which produces a direct contradiction with [[1 Samuel]] 17.

On the text-critical analysis, this is the **earlier, more original** reading (see [[Lectio Difficilior - The Harder Reading Is Usually Earlier]]); the Chronicler's parallel at [[1 Chronicles 20.5|1 Chronicles 20:5]] is the later, harmonized form. The Masoretic text of this verse is itself slightly corrupt (Williamson and others note that *oregim*, "weavers," has been duplicated into Elhanan's father's name), but the core attribution to Elhanan stands.
