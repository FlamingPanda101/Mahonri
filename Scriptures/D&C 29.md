# D&C 29 — No Beginning or End; All Law Is Spiritual

> **33** ...Speaking unto you that you may naturally understand; but unto myself my works have no end, neither beginning; but it is given unto you that ye may understand, because ye have asked it of me and are agreed.
> **34** Wherefore, verily I say unto you that all things unto me are spiritual, and not at any time have I given unto you a law which was temporal... (D&C 29:33–34)

An 1830 revelation. Verse 33 has God stating that He speaks of "beginnings and endings" only so mortals "may naturally understand," while His own works "have no end, neither beginning." Verse 34 declares that "all things unto me are spiritual" and that God has never given "a law which was temporal."

[[Shad Brooks]] uses v. 33 to argue that God **accommodates language to our frame of reference** (we exist in time; He does not), and v. 34 to argue that even the laws of physics are, to God, *spiritual* — and therefore *moral* — laws, a key link in his "truth = law = morality" chain.[^1] See [[Truth Is Law Is Morality - The D&C 88 Equivalence Chain]] and [[God Frames Truth Pedagogically to Change Behavior]].
