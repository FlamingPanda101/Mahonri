# Matthew 6:11

> 11 Give us this day our daily bread.

The word the King James renders **"daily"** is the Greek **ἐπιούσιος** (*epiousios*) — a *hapax legomenon* that appears only here (and the Lukan parallel) in the entire New Testament, so there is no other occurrence to fix its meaning. [[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)|David Butler]] and [[Mike Day]] parse it as *epi* ("upon") + the participle of *einai* ("to be"), the existent one — yielding not "the bread for the existing day" but **"the bread that Yahweh [the Existent One] is upon,"** tying the petition to the temple feast and the Eucharist. See [[Epiousios Is Yahweh Bread Not Daily Bread]].
