# D&C 130:20-21

> There is a law, irrevocably decreed in heaven before the foundations of this
> world, upon which all blessings are predicated — And when we obtain any
> blessing from God, it is by obedience to that law upon which it is
> predicated.

## Context

Doctrine and Covenants 130 contains teachings given by [[Joseph Smith]] at
Ramus, Illinois, on April 2, 1843. This section covers a range of topics
including the nature of God the Father and Jesus Christ (bodily form), the
Holy Ghost (a personage of spirit), and eternal law.

## Key Exegetical Points

1. **Eternal Law Precedes Creation:** The law was "irrevocably decreed in
   heaven *before* the foundations of this world" — meaning it is not
   something God created *ex nihilo* but something that existed prior to and
   independent of the creation of this earth.

2. **Blessings are Conditional on Law:** The relationship between law and
   blessing is causal and inviolable — blessings are "predicated" (based upon,
   conditioned upon) obedience to law. This is not arbitrary divine favor but a
   consistent, lawful operation.

3. **Irrevocable:** The law cannot be revoked, changed, or suspended — even by
   God. [[David Butler]] connects this to the physical constants of the
   universe: like the law of entropy or the gravitational constant, these are
   "irrevocably decreed" and God operates within them rather than inventing
   them arbitrarily.[^1]

## Relevance to Fine-Tuning and Free Will

- **Fine-Tuning:** Butler explicitly connects D&C 130:20-21 to the fine-tuning
  argument: the physical constants (speed of light, gravitational force, strong
  nuclear force, etc.) are "laws irrevocably decreed in heaven before the
  foundations of this world." They are not arbitrary but necessary conditions
  for existence.[^1] See [[Fine-Tuning and the Teleological Argument from Alma 30]].

- **Free Will and Co-Eternality:** The concept of eternal law independent of
  God's will supports the LDS doctrine that God is God *because* He perfectly
  follows eternal law — He is not arbitrary. This grounds free will: if moral
  law is eternal and independent, then human choices have real moral weight
  that even God must respect. See [[Free Will - Atheist Determinism vs LDS Co-Eternality]].
