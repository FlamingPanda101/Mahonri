# Isaiah 4.1

> And in that day seven women shall take hold of one man, saying, We will eat our own bread, and wear our own apparel: only let us be called by thy name, to take away our reproach.

## Why it's cited

Sitting at the hinge of Isaiah's judgment-and-restoration oracle, this verse depicts the social collapse following the slaughter of the men of Judah, where surviving women plead merely to bear a man's name and remove their shame. Apologetic readings treat it as part of a tightly structured unit (Isaiah 3–6) in which the prophet encodes the violent dismantling of illegitimate worship and the reproach of a judged community. It is cited to show that Isaiah's imagery of shame, naming, and reproach forms a deliberate literary pattern rather than isolated verses.
