# Zechariah 3

> And he shewed me Joshua the high priest standing before the angel of the LORD, and Satan standing at his right hand to resist him... Now Joshua was clothed with filthy garments... Take away the filthy garments from him... I will clothe thee with change of raiment. And I said, Let them set a fair mitre upon his head. *(Zechariah 3:1-5, KJV)*

## Key phrase
"**Take away the filthy garments... I will clothe thee with change of raiment**" — the high priest Joshua (Hebrew **Yeshua**, the name of Jesus) is stripped of filthy garments, reclothed, mitred, and warned by the angel before being promised that he shall "judge my house" and walk among the standing ones (the divine council). The chapter ends with "the BRANCH," a stone with seven eyes, and every man under the vine and fig tree.

## Apologetic / historical application
[[Andrea Woodmansee]] calls this "one of the most explicit temple things" in the Old Testament: a recognition-and-investiture scene at the veil. She notes the priest-name **Joshua = Yeshua** (one takes Christ's name in the temple); reads the angel's "protest" via the Hebrew root meaning "to repeat / warn"; reframes the warning as instruction to approach worthily; and reads the closing **BRANCH** (cf. [[Isaiah 11.1]]) and **fig tree** (seed-bearing fruit) as tree-of-life and eternal-increase symbols at the threshold of the holy of holies. See [[Joshua the High Priest in Zechariah 3 Is an Explicit Temple Investiture]] and [[The Veil Is Christ Reversing the Day of Atonement]].
