# Acts 1:11

> Which also said, Ye men of Galilee, why stand ye gazing up into heaven? this same Jesus, which is taken up from you into heaven, shall so come in like manner as ye have seen him go into heaven. *(KJV)*

## Key phrase
"**This same Jesus... shall so come in like manner as ye have seen him go**" — the angelic promise at the Ascension that Christ's return will mirror his departure.

## Apologetic / historical application
[[Nicholas J. Frederick]] cites Acts 1:11 (with [[Matthew 24.30|Matthew 24:27, 30]]) as a foundational New Testament promise that grounded early Christian certainty in a literal, physical Second Coming — the very expectation whose perceived delay drove later doctrinal development. See [[First-Century Christianity Was Consumed by Eschatological Anxiety]] and [[The Delay of the Parousia Drove Christian Doctrinal Change]].
