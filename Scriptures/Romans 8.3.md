# Romans 8:3

> For what the law could not do, in that it was weak through the flesh, God sending his own Son in the likeness of sinful flesh, and for sin, condemned sin in the flesh. *(KJV)*

## Significance
[[Trent Horn]] cites Romans 8:3 as an **alternative reading** of the difficult phrase in [[2 Corinthians 5.21|2 Corinthians 5:21]] ("he made him to be sin"). Rather than Reformed [[Double Imputation Is Not the Gospel|double imputation]], "made... to be sin" can be understood as a reference to the **incarnation** — Christ "coming in the form of sinful flesh," exactly as Romans 8:3 says ("in the likeness of sinful flesh, and for sin"). On this reading Christ shares our condition and "condemns sin in the flesh" without any transfer of forensic guilt. The availability of this orthodox, non-imputation reading is part of why Horn denies that double imputation is a gospel essential.
