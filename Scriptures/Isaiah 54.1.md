# Isaiah 54:1

> Sing, O barren, thou that didst not bear; break forth into singing, and cry aloud, thou that didst not travail with child: for more are the children of the desolate than the children of the married wife, saith the LORD. *(KJV)*

## In context
The scriptural base of the earliest surviving Christian sermon, [[2 Clement Is the Earliest Surviving Christian Sermon|2 Clement]] (c. AD 140). The preacher "seems to base his sermon on" this passage, developing the theme of humanity's indebtedness to Christ and explaining how the passage "is referring to us." Heal notes that, like Jesus at Nazareth, the preacher "summons the scriptures from some distant historical past and places them firmly within his congregation's present day."
