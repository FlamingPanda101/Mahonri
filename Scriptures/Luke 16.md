# Luke 16 — Lazarus and the Rich Man

> And he said unto him, If they hear not Moses and the prophets, neither will they be persuaded, though one rose from the dead. *(Luke 16:31, KJV)*

## Key Detail
- In the parable of Lazarus and the rich man, the rich man begs to return from the dead to warn his brothers; Abraham replies that "though one rose from the dead," those who reject Moses and the prophets will not be persuaded. [[Cardon Ellis]] and [[Luke Hansen]] cite this in [[Ward Radio - Debunking Mike Wingers Irrefutable Proof Mormonism is FALSE]] to argue that **God deliberately limits evidence** so that faith must be sought spiritually — undercutting Mike Winger's demand for "irrefutable proof." See [[God Gives Enough Evidence to Seek Not to Compel]].
- The hosts also note (with [[John Gee]]'s scholarship) that the parable itself reuses an Egyptian tale (Setne Khamwas), with Abraham substituted for Osiris — paralleling how the Book of Abraham facsimiles reinterpret Egyptian vignettes. See [[Jews Reinterpreted Egyptian Iconography Including in the Bible]].
