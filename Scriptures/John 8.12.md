# John 8.12 — "I am the light of the world"

> Then spake Jesus again unto them, saying, I am the light of the world: he that followeth me shall not walk in darkness, but shall have the light of life. *(John 8:12, KJV)*

## Key Detail
- Jesus' declaration that he is "the light of the world," whose followers "shall not walk in darkness." In [[Lamp Lighting and Evening Prayer]] this is the verse early Christians remembered when lighting household lamps at dusk: the returning light symbolized Christ and his coming, and Christian-symbol lamps (later bearing the Chi-Rho) recalled the promise of "the light of life." Connects to the eastward-prayer and Holy-Fire traditions of the early church.
