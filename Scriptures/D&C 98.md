# D&C 98

> **98:5** — "And that law of the land which is constitutional, supporting that principle of freedom in maintaining rights and privileges, belongs to all mankind, and is justifiable before me."

> **98:6** — "Therefore, I, the Lord, justify you, and your brethren of my church, in befriending that law which is the constitutional law of the land."

## Why It Matters Here

[[Mike Day]] cites this as the second pillar (with [[D&C 101]]) for the inspired-Constitution idea, stressing that its principle of freedom **"belongs to all mankind"** — God's umbrella of equality *before the law*, not equality of natural endowment ("how do you explain LeBron James?").

[[Brian Thomassen]] makes a textual observation: here **"constitutional" is lowercase**, whereas "Constitution" in [[D&C 101]]:80 is capitalized. He reads the lowercase form as possibly invoking the *base meaning* of "constitution" — an **organizing / fundamental-law principle** (a town's constitution, a Masonic charter) — rather than the U.S. Constitution specifically. The distinction is offered tentatively, not dogmatically; both readings remain open. It feeds the broader caution that "inspired" is being *inserted* into texts that do not contain the word. See [[The Constitution Is Not Said to Be Inspired in the Canon - Only Established]].
