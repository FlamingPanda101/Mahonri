# Genesis 14:18

> 18 And Melchizedek king of Salem brought forth bread and wine: and he was the priest of the most high God.

[[Melchizedek]] meets Abraham with **bread and wine** and receives his tithes. [[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)|David Butler]] notes that *Salem* has no archaeological site — it exists only in literature (a poetic name for Jerusalem in the Psalms) — and the Hebrew *melek-shalem* can be read not as "king of [the place] Salem" but as "the king who is *shalem*," the **Peaceable / Perfect King**. On this reading the feasting priest-king is a manifestation of the Lord himself, Yahweh presiding over the temple feast of his own flesh and blood (the *epiousios* bread of [[Matthew 6.11|Matt 6:11]]); Alma 13 likewise titles him the Prince of Peace.
