# Hosea 11:1 (KJV)

> When Israel was a child, then I loved him, and called my son out of Egypt.

## Key Detail
In its original context this is **not a prediction** but a retrospective: God recalls the Exodus, calling the *nation of Israel* his "son" brought "out of Egypt." [[Matthew 2.13-15|Matthew 2:13–15]] later applies the second clause ("out of Egypt I called my son") to the infant Jesus's return from his flight to Egypt — a *typological* reuse of a text whose grammatical subject is corporate Israel, not a coming Messiah. In the [[Pangburn - Alex OConnor Exposes Christianity and Dinesh DSouza|O'Connor–D'Souza debate]], [[Alex OConnor|Alex O'Connor]] stresses that "in Hosea, God is talking here about the nation of Israel," and that "unlike the Matthew prophecy, the prophecy actually exists, and we can interrogate it."[^1]
