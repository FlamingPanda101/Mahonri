# D&C 138

[[Joseph F. Smith]]'s 1918 vision of the redemption of the dead (the spirit world and Christ's ministry to the departed).

## Why It Matters Here

In [[Brian Thomassen]]'s argument about **canon vs. a true-but-non-binding claim**, D&C 138 is the worked example: the vision was received in **1918** and could be "accepted as true" then, but it was not **binding** until it was canonized — added to the Pearl of Great Price in **1976** and moved into the Doctrine and Covenants as Section 138 in **1981**. The lesson he draws: "I can say this is true, but it is not binding." Applied to the Constitution, "all of these statements about the Constitution being inspired may or may not be true, but they're not binding on us unless we can point to something specifically that says so." See [[The Constitution Is Not Said to Be Inspired in the Canon - Only Established]] and [[Inspiration Presupposes Revelation in the Christian Theological Tradition]].
