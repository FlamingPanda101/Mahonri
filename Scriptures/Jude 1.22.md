# Jude 1:22

> "And of some have compassion, making a difference." (KJV)

[[Dan McClellan]] uses this verse as a triple illustration of why the King James Version obscures the biblical authors' meaning. (1) **Archaic idiom:** "making a difference" meant *making a distinction* in 1611 — the phrase did not acquire its modern "have a positive impact" sense until c. 1900. (2) **Inferior manuscripts:** the participle the KJV renders is, in the critical text, accusative, not nominative — so the verse is about *on whom* to have compassion (those who doubt / waver), not *how*. Modern translations therefore read very differently. See [[The KJV Was Already Out of Date in 1611]] and [[The KJV Rests on the Inferior Textus Receptus]].
