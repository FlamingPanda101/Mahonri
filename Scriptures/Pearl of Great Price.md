# Pearl of Great Price — the smallest volume of the LDS standard works

> A short selection of revelations, translations, and writings of Joseph Smith, compiled to supplement the Bible, Book of Mormon, and Doctrine and Covenants. It comprises five parts: the **Book of Moses**, the **Book of Abraham**, **Joseph Smith—Matthew**, **Joseph Smith—History**, and the **Articles of Faith**. First published in 1851 by Elder Franklin D. Richards in England, it was canonized as scripture by the Church in **1880**.

## Key Detail
- The **Book of Moses** (a revealed expansion of Genesis 1–6) and the **Book of Abraham** (Joseph Smith's translation from Egyptian papyri) are the volume's doctrinal heart, supplying premortal-council, cosmology, and patriarchal-covenant material the other standard works only hint at. **JS—Matthew** restores Matthew 24; **JS—History** narrates the First Vision and Moroni; the **Articles of Faith** distill belief. Because so much of the vault's temple/priesthood argument leans on Moses and Abraham, this overview anchors those forward-links.
