## Text
"Do you not know that all of us who have been baptized into Christ Jesus were baptized into his death? Therefore we have been buried with him by baptism into death, so that, just as Christ was raised from the dead by the glory of the Father, we too might walk in newness of life." (Romans 6:3–4, NRSV)

## Use in Source
Mark Ellison cites this passage as the doctrinal foundation for baptism as dying and rising with Christ. The third-century preference for baptism on the night before Easter "heightened the symbolism of burial and rising with Christ" (cf. Ephesians 2:5–6; Colossians 2:12–13). The fifth-century Ravenna baptistery mosaic, showing Jesus's own baptism directly over the octagonal font, reminded the newly baptized of exactly this Romans 6 imagery.

## Notes
The death-and-rebirth meaning of baptism links to the octagonal font (the "eighth day" of resurrection) and to the white robe of new life (see [[Early Christian Baptism as Death and Rebirth]]).
