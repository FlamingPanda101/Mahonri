# Deuteronomy 29.29

> The secret things belong unto the LORD our God: but those things which are revealed belong unto us and to our children for ever, that we may do all the words of this law.

## Why it's cited

This verse draws the line between divine mysteries reserved to God and the revealed commandments entrusted to the covenant community for obedience. It is cited in discussions of ongoing revelation and restored ordinances to argue that what was once "secret" can become "revealed," and that humans are accountable to act on revelation as it is given. The distinction between the hidden and the disclosed makes it a favored proof-text for debates over the sufficiency of past revelation versus the possibility of new disclosure.
