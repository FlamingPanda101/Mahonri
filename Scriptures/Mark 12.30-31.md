# Mark 12:30-31

> And thou shalt love the Lord thy God with all thy heart, and with all thy soul, and with all thy mind, and with all thy strength: this is the first commandment. And the second is like, namely this, Thou shalt love thy neighbour as thyself. There is none other commandment greater than these.

**Note.** Mark's version adds "with all thy **strength**" (Greek *ischys*, ἰσχύς) to heart, soul, and mind — the fourfold form [[Kyle Beshears]] quotes when grounding the duty to study and understand one's religious neighbor. Like the Matthean parallel, it fuses [[Deuteronomy 6.4|Deut 6:4-5]] with [[Leviticus 19.18|Lev 19:18]].
