# Exodus 33:20

> And he said, Thou canst not see my face: for there shall no man see me, and live. *(KJV)*

## Key Hebrew Terms

- **פָּנִים (panim):** "face." God declares his face cannot be seen and survived. The Septuagint (LXX) renders it "no one can see my face and live," explicitly about the face. See [[Conflicting Traditions on Seeing Gods Face]].
- **רָאָה (ra'ah):** "to see" — the same root that, in the pilgrimage commandment ([[Deuteronomy 16.16|Deuteronomy 16:16]]), is read as a command *to* see God's face.

## Context

In the aftermath of the golden calf, Moses asks to see God's glory; God hides him in a cleft of the rock, covers him with his hand, and shows only his back, declaring no one can see his face and live (Exod 33:20–23). [[Dan McClellan]] identifies this as the tradition behind translators' discomfort with "see the face of God" — the theological motive he argues prompted the [[Masoretes|Masoretic]] passive vocalization. It stands in tension with texts where people do see God ([[Exodus 24.10|Exodus 24:10]], [[Numbers 12.8|Numbers 12:8]], [[Genesis 32.30|Genesis 32:30]]).
