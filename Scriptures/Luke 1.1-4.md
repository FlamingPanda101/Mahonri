# Luke 1:1-4 — The Eyewitness Preface

> Forasmuch as many have taken in hand to set forth in order a declaration of those things which are most surely believed among us, even as they delivered them unto us, which from the beginning were eyewitnesses, and ministers of the word... it seemed good to me also, having had perfect understanding of all things from the very first, to write unto thee in order, most excellent Theophilus. *(Luke 1:1-4, KJV)*

## Key Detail
- Luke's formal preface *explicitly* disclaims being an eyewitness while claiming to draw on "those who from the beginning were eyewitnesses." [[Michael Jones]] cites it to (a) correct [[Bart Ehrman]]'s blanket claim that the Gospels "don't claim" eyewitness connection, and (b) support [[Luke VanderLaan]]'s argument that Luke interviewed eyewitnesses — corroborated by the Gospel's onomastic congruence.
- A model instance of the **reliability** standard: a careful, source-citing ancient historian, not an anonymous legend.
