# 1 Corinthians 4:6

> And these things, brethren, I have in a figure transferred to myself and to Apollos for your sakes; that ye might learn in us not to think of men above that which is written, that no one of you be puffed up for one against another. *(KJV)*

## Key Greek phrase
**μὴ ὑπὲρ ἃ γέγραπται (mē hyper ha gegraptai)** — "not beyond the things which are written." The exact referent of "what is written" is debated among scholars.

## In context
A verse Protestants sometimes cite for not going "beyond what is written." [[Trent Horn]] calls it incredibly vague and argues a literal reading is self-defeating: if the Corinthians took it strictly they could not receive 2 Corinthians, since that goes "beyond what had been written" to them — illustrating that sola scriptura could not hold during the apostolic age. Scholars are unsure of the meaning; one reading is that Paul invokes the way children trace (copy) written letters, fitting his "father to the Corinthians" analogy.
