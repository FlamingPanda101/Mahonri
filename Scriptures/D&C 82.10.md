# D&C 82:10

> I, the Lord, am bound when ye do what I say; but when ye do not what I say, ye have no promise. *(Doctrine and Covenants)*

## Key phrase
"**I, the Lord, am bound when ye do what I say**" — an LDS text describing God as covenantally *bound* to bless obedience.

## Apologetic application
In cross-examination [[Gavin (Catholic Debater)]] turns this against [[Hayden Carroll]], arguing an LDS God who is "bound" by human obedience and gates access through covenant looks like the very "conditional access" model Gavin critiques in trinitarian terms. Hayden answers "yes, God is bound when we do what he says," but insists this is irrelevant to the resolution: "Mormonism could be totally false and that would mean nothing... for the debate resolution." See [[Conditional Access Tu Quoque Against the LDS God]].
