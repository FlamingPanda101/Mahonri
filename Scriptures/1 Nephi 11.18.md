# 1 Nephi 11:18

> And he said unto me: Behold, the virgin whom thou seest is the mother of the Son of God, after the manner of the flesh. *(current edition)*

**1830 first-edition reading:** "Behold, the virgin which thou seest, is the mother of **God**, after the manner of the flesh."

## Context

In Nephi's vision of the tree of life, an angel shows him the virgin Mary at
Nazareth. The 1830 printing read "the mother of God"; later editions (1837
onward) inserted "the Son of" to read "the mother of the Son of God." A parallel
edit was made at [[1 Nephi 11.21|1 Nephi 11:21]] ("the Lamb of God, yea, even the
**Son of** the Eternal Father"). Critics read the original wording as evidence of
an early, more Trinitarian/modalist Christology that Joseph Smith later revised;
LDS apologists respond that "mother of God" was standard devotional language
(*Theotokos*) and that the Book of Mormon as a whole distinguishes the Father from
the Son.

## Key Exegetical Point

- **"Mother of God" vs. "mother of the Son of God":** In historic Christianity
  "Mother of God" (Greek *Theotokos*, "God-bearer") was an orthodox title
  affirmed at the Council of Ephesus (431), not a claim that Mary birthed the
  divine nature. The 1837 clarification removes the ambiguity for a 19th-century
  Protestant readership unaccustomed to the title.
