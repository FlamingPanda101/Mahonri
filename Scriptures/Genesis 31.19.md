# Genesis 31:19 — Rachel steals Laban's teraphim

As Jacob's household flees Laban, Rachel steals her father's **teraphim** (household gods) and hides them in a camel's saddle, sitting on them and pleading "the way of women is upon me" so Laban's search fails.

> And Laban went to shear his sheep: and Rachel had stolen the images that were her father's. *(Gen 31:19, KJV)*

## Key Detail
[[Dan McClellan]] cites this as evidence that **teraphim were small, plural, and a normal household possession** — "the dollar-store version" beside the life-size teraphim of [[1 Samuel 19]] ("the Costco version"). The text never explains *why* Rachel takes them, nor does the narrator condemn the act; the household gods are simply treated as ordinary property worth stealing and hiding. The episode is part of McClellan's broader observation that teraphim are nowhere condemned in early Israelite narrative. See [[Teraphim Were Normative Divine Images Never Condemned]]. (Distinct from [[Genesis 31.13]], the *El-Bethel* theophany verse.)
