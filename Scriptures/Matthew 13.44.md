# Matthew 13:44

> Again, the kingdom of heaven is like unto treasure hid in a field; the which
> when a man hath found, he hideth, and for joy thereof goeth and selleth all
> that he hath, and buyeth that field.

## Key Term

- **θησαυρῷ (thēsaurō)** — "treasure," a hoard of valuables. The parable
  presupposes the ordinary ancient practice of burying valuables in the ground
  for safekeeping, since banking was not widely available.

## Context: Treasure in the Ancient World

This source uses the parable to show that buried-treasure finding was a normal
feature of the ancient world that Jesus could assume his hearers understood.
The man does not simply take the treasure — he **buys the land**, reflecting an
ancient legal regime in which the landowner, not the finder, had the right to
treasure found on the property (contrast later "finders keepers" laws).[^1] The
parable is dated within roughly a century of the earliest known Roman treasure
law (2nd century).[^2]
