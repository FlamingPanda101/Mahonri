# Lamentations

The Book of Lamentations is a collection of five poems mourning the destruction of Jerusalem by the Babylonians in 586 BCE. [[Jacob Wright]] points to Lamentations as evidence that the biblical authors made defeat central and ongoing — some of it was written (and continued to be written) for centuries after the destruction. The message: "there was a beginning to something and an end to it — that was the kingdom. There's no going back."
