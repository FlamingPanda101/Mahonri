# 2 Kings 3:26-27

> "And when the king of Moab saw that the battle was too sore for him... he took his eldest son that should have reigned in his stead, and offered him for a burnt offering upon the wall. And there was great indignation against Israel: and they departed from him, and returned to their own land." (KJV)

After Elisha promises the Israel–Judah–Edom coalition total victory over Moab (3:18–19), King Mesha sacrifices his heir on the wall — presumably to the Moabite patron deity **Chemosh** — and the campaign collapses. The Hebrew *qetzef gadol* ("great wrath/indignation") has an unspecified agent; most critical scholars read it as Chemosh's effective intervention within his own purview, the mirror image of Yahweh's defeat of Sennacherib in his.
