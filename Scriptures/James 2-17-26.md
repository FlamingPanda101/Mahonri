# James 2:17-26

> "Even so faith, if it hath not works, is dead, being alone... Ye see then how
> that by works a man is justified, and not by faith only... For as the body
> without the spirit is dead, so faith without works is dead also."

## Apologetic Significance
The classic counterweight to Ephesians 2:8-9. James explicitly states that
justification involves works ("not by faith only" — the ONLY time "faith only"
appears in scripture, and it's negated). The key insight: Paul and James address
different errors (legalism vs. antinomianism) and aren't contradictory. True faith
produces works; works demonstrate faith.
