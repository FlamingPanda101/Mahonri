# Matthew 13:10-11

> And the disciples came, and said unto him, Why speakest thou unto them in parables? He answered and said unto them, Because it is given unto you to know the mysteries of the kingdom of heaven, but to them it is not given. *(KJV)*

## Key term — *mystērion*
The word translated "mysteries" is **μυστήριον (mystērion)**, which [[Dave Butler]] (relayed by [[Alex Arnold]]) argues is the specific technical term for *initiated knowledge* in the ancient mystery traditions — not a generic synonym for "something hard to understand." On this reading Jesus distinguishes a body of content "given" to insiders who have been through something from what is withheld from outsiders — initiation language that "only makes sense if there was a body of content specifically accessible to those who had been through... some initiation." Paired with [[1 Corinthians 2.6-7|1 Corinthians 2:6-7]] (Paul's wisdom spoken "among the perfect"), it grounds the argument that Jesus ran a mystery in the full ancient sense. See [[The Acts of John Records Jesus Instituting a Mystery Ritual]] and the vault's [[Teleios Means Initiate - You Will Be Endowed Not Be Flawless]].

## How different traditions read this
- **Mainstream Christian:** "mysteries" = truths about the kingdom revealed by grace to disciples, hidden from the hard-hearted; not a literal initiation rite.
- **LDS / temple reading:** *mystērion* carries the technical sense of initiated, ordinance-bound knowledge — Christ initiating disciples into the "mysteries of the kingdom," a pattern the endowment preserves.
