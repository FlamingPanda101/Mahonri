# D&C 109

> "That thy servants may go forth from this house armed with thy power, and that thy name may be upon them... that our garments may be pure, that we may be clothed upon with robes of righteousness, with palms in our hands, and crowns of glory upon our heads... that we may mingle our voices with those bright shining seraphs around thy throne, with acclamations of praise, singing Hosanna to God and the Lamb." — D&C 109:22, 76, 79

The **dedicatory prayer of the Kirtland Temple** (Sunday, 27 March 1836), the first temple of the Restoration. [[Joseph Smith]] stated the prayer was given to him **by revelation**, and it was later canonized. The prayer ties the temple to taking the **name of Christ upon us** ("thy name may be upon them," v. 22, 79), to the **garment / robes of righteousness** (v. 76), and to the **palm-branch / hosanna** liturgy echoing Jesus's triumphal entry on the same calendar day (Palm Sunday). On these grounds [[Don Bradley]] (with host [[Stephen Jones]]) reads the dedication as a deliberate recapitulation of Christ's final week.
