# Exodus 32:14 — "the LORD repented of the evil"

> And the LORD repented of the evil which he thought to do unto his people. *(Exodus 32:14, KJV)*

## Key Detail
- After Moses intercedes following the golden calf, the text says God "repented" / changed his mind. Used by [[Christine Hayes]] (and the Thoughtful Faith narrator) as a paradigm case that the **biblical God is dynamic** — one who "change[s] course in response to" human action — against the Greco-Roman "unmoved mover" who "never changes his mind."
- Classical theists read it as anthropomorphic accommodation (cf. Numbers 23:19, Malachi 3:6); the LDS / dynamic-God reading takes it as revelation of God's genuine responsiveness.
