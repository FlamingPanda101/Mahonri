# Exodus 17:6 — Water From the Rock

> **6** Behold, I will stand before thee there upon the rock in Horeb; and thou shalt smite the rock, and there shall come water out of it, that the people may drink… (Exodus 17:6)

In the [[Salty Saints - Why Water Is Better Than Wine in the Sacrament|Salty Saints sacrament episode]], David reads Moses striking the rock as Jehovah-as-living-water: the text has the Lord "stand on the rock" and water flow from it, which [[Paul the Apostle|Paul]] later identifies — "that Rock was Christ" ([[1 Corinthians 10.4|1 Corinthians 10:4]]). This anchors the panel's claim that water is "very strongly connecting… to Israelite salvation… to Jehovah," whereas wine in the Old Testament usually signifies the cup of wrath. It also folds into the wilderness-sustenance typology (water, manna, the brass serpent) that the panel maps onto the sacrament.[^1] See [[Water Is a Better Symbol of Christ Than Wine]] and [[The Sacrament Sustains the New Life of Baptism]].
