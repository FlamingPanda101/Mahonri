# Romans 9:11-14

> (For the children being not yet born, neither having done any good or evil … it was said unto her, The elder shall serve the younger. As it is written, Jacob have I loved, but Esau have I hated. *(KJV)*

## Significance
A driving text for [[Origen]]'s theory of the preexistence of souls. Origen could not reconcile God loving Jacob and hating Esau "while they were still in the womb before they had time to act one way or another to merit those judgments" with a just God — unless the souls had some prior existence in which they exercised free will. The verse (with Jeremiah 1:5 and Luke 1:41) formed Origen's scriptural case that God's pre-birth judgments presuppose premortal moral choices, vindicating divine justice. Later Christians rejected this reading.
