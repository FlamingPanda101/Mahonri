# Jeremiah 35

> The words of the covenant of Jonadab the son of Rechab, that he commanded his sons not to drink wine: and they have performed... Because ye have obeyed the commandment of Jonadab your father, and kept all his precepts, and done according unto all that he hath commanded you: therefore thus saith the LORD of hosts, the God of Israel; Jonadab the son of Rechab shall not want a man to stand before me for ever. *(Jeremiah 35:14, 18-19, KJV)*

In the days of Jeremiah (just before the Babylonian conquest), the prophet sets wine before
the **Rechabites** as a test. They refuse, because their forefather **Jonadab son of Rechab**
commanded his descendants never to drink wine, never to build houses, sow seed, or plant
vineyards, but to dwell in tents as nomads. The Lord praises their fidelity and promises that
Jonadab "shall not want a man to stand before me for ever."

## Why it's cited

This is the only canonical Old Testament passage on the Rechabites, and it dates them
precisely to Jeremiah's (and Lehi's) day. Apologetics notes use it as the anchor for the
claim that the Rechabites are the kind of righteous, nomadic, Jerusalem-era family that the
apocryphal *History of the Rechabites* expands into a Lehi-like flight from doomed Jerusalem —
making Rechab a candidate for the "prophets of old" Lehi invokes in [[1 Nephi 1.8-19|1 Nephi 1]].
The perpetual-posterity promise ("shall not want a man... for ever") is read as echoing the
covenant language of [[Enos 1.1-2|Enos]] ("thy seed will not be utterly destroyed").
