# John 13:35

> By this shall all men know that ye are my disciples, if ye have love one to another. *(KJV)*

## Key Greek term
**agapē (ἀγάπη)** — the same self-giving love of the preceding verse ([[John 13.34|John 13:34]]). The sign of discipleship is set not as correct belief or ritual but as *love shown to one another*: "by this" (*en toutō*) — by love — "all men" (*pantes*) will recognize Christ's disciples.

## Key point
Paired with [[John 13.34|John 13:34]], this verse makes mutual love the public, recognizable mark of belonging to Christ. [[Jacob Hansen]] reads the two verses together as the heart of the LDS response to the Grand Blanc tragedy: the visible charity of Latter-day Saints toward the shooter's family is offered as exactly the kind of love by which "all men know" whose disciples they are — see [[The Grand Blanc GoFundMe as Charity Toward an Enemys Family]]. The verse also undergirds the broader Latter-day Saint emphasis that fruit (love enacted), not creedal precision, is the diagnostic of discipleship.
