# Genesis 1:1

> "In the beginning God created the heaven and the earth." (KJV)

**Hebrew:** בְּרֵאשִׁית בָּרָא אֱלֹהִים אֵת הַשָּׁמַיִם וְאֵת הָאָרֶץ (*b'reshit bara Elohim et hashamayim v'et ha'aretz*)

**Key verb:** בָּרָא (*bara*) — "to create." Used exclusively of divine creative activity in the Hebrew Bible; never takes a material complement (God is always the subject, the material is never specified with an accusative of material).

## Context

Genesis 1:1 opens the Priestly creation account. The grammar is ambiguous: it can be read as (a) an independent clause ("In the beginning, God created the heavens and the earth") or (b) a temporal clause ("When God began to create the heavens and the earth..."). The latter reading, advocated by scholars like E.A. Speiser (Anchor Bible) and supported by the unpointed Hebrew text, would make verse 2's *tohu wa-bohu* the starting condition rather than the result of creation — consistent with the ancient Near Eastern cosmogonic pattern of creation as ordering of pre-existent chaos.

## Interpretations

### Traditional Reading
God created the universe from absolute nothingness (*creatio ex nihilo*) by divine fiat. Verse 1 describes the initial act of creation; verse 2 describes the state of the created-but-unordered earth.

### Academic Reading (Historical-Critical)
Genesis 1:1, in its original ancient Near Eastern context, describes the ordering and filling of pre-existent chaotic elements. The *tohu wa-bohu* (formless and void) and *tehom* (the deep) of verse 2 are simply there when the narrative begins — they are not said to be created. God's creative acts in Genesis 1 are acts of separation, ordering, naming, and filling — imposing form and function on pre-existent material, consistent with other ANE cosmogonies (e.g., Enuma Elish).

The verb *bara* does not entail creation from nothing. It is used within Genesis 1 for acts that clearly involve pre-existing material (e.g., 1:21 — God *bara* the sea creatures from the waters; 1:27 — God *bara* humanity from the dust in Gen 2:7).
