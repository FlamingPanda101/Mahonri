# Revelation 12

> And there appeared a great wonder in heaven; a woman clothed with the sun, and the moon under her feet, and upon her head a crown of twelve stars... And the woman fled into the wilderness, where she hath a place prepared of God, that they should feed her there a thousand two hundred and threescore days. *(KJV, vv. 1, 6)*

John's vision of a woman (the church / kingdom of God) who brings forth a child, is pursued by a seven-headed dragon, and flees into the wilderness to be nourished by God.

## In context
This is the controlling image behind the LDS apostasy-and-restoration vocabulary. [[D&C 86]] says the tares "drive the church into the wilderness" (Rev 12); the Restoration is then repeatedly described as the church coming *out* of the wilderness (D&C 5:14; 33:5; the Kirtland Temple dedicatory prayer; D&C 1:30). [[Scott Woodward|Woodward]] and [[Casey Paul Griffiths|Griffiths]] add that the Nauvoo Temple's sun-, moon-, and star-stones deliberately depict the Revelation 12 woman, announcing that the church has emerged from the wilderness. The phrase "restored church," they note, is not in Joseph Smith's vocabulary; the Lord speaks of the church *coming out of the wilderness* instead. See [[The Apostasy Was the Church Driven Into the Wilderness Not Annihilated]].
