# Acts 16:14-15

> A certain woman named Lydia, a worshiper of God … was listening to us … When she and her household were baptized, she urged us, saying, "If you have judged me to be faithful to the Lord, come and stay at my home." And she prevailed upon us. *(NRSV, paraphrase)*

## Significance
[[Ariel Bybee Laughton]] reads Lydia as a prominent merchant, patron of a house church, and household manager who "insists that Paul and Silas accept her hospitality," effectively "electing them to preside over the Church in her house" — and who likely presided herself at meetings and meals when the apostles were absent. After Paul and Silas are imprisoned and freed, they return to her house to worship (Acts 16:40). A key proof-text for women leading house churches.
