# Psalm 97:9

> For thou, LORD, art high above all the earth: thou art exalted far above all gods. *(KJV)*

## Key term
Hebrew **elohim** ("gods," אֱלֹהִים) — here naming other divine beings above which Yahweh is "exalted." The verse does not say the other gods do *not exist*; it ranks Yahweh supreme over them, which is why it is cited as a textual basis for Old Testament **henotheism**.

## Used against / for the Church
[[Trent Horn]] (citing [[Pope Benedict XVI]]) lists this verse as one where "the earliest parts of the Old Testament affirm henotheism." [[Luke Hansen]] uses that Catholic admission as a tu quoque against the disqualification of [[Brigham Young]].
