# Exodus 32 (The Golden Calf)

> And he received them at their hand, and fashioned it with a graving tool, after he had made it a molten calf: and they said, These be thy gods, O Israel, which brought thee up out of the land of Egypt. *(Exodus 32:4, KJV)*

## Key point
Aaron makes the calf; the people ascribe to it a *true* act — bringing Israel out of Egypt — yet it is idolatry. [[Joe Heschmeyer]] uses this to argue that attaching God's genuine attributes to a false object of worship is still idolatry, pressing against the [[Common Reference - Worshipping Adam as God|common-reference]] defense of Adam-God. [[Luke Hansen]] replies that the calf was made by Aaron (not the authorized oracle Moses) and modeled on foreign deities.
