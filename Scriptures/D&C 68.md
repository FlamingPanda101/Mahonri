# Doctrine and Covenants 68

> And whatsoever they shall speak when moved upon by the Holy Ghost shall be scripture, shall be the will of the Lord, shall be the mind of the Lord, shall be the word of the Lord, shall be the voice of the Lord, and the power of God unto salvation. *(D&C 68:4)*

## Significance
The proof-text for the LDS idea that inspired speech "shall be scripture." [[J. Reuben Clark]]'s 1954 essay *"When Are Church Teachers' Words Entitled to the Claim of Scripture?"* exegetes this verse — arguing that the authority is real but qualified, and that only the President of the Church may give binding revelation for the whole church. Cited in the dispute over whether the *Journal of Discourses* (and Adam-God) carried scriptural weight.
