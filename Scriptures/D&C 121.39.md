# D&C 121.39

From the Doctrine and Covenants (LDS Restoration canon), part of Joseph Smith's Liberty Jail revelation. Paraphrased by host [[Stephen Jones]] as scriptural warrant that knowledge of human nature is itself *learned by experience*.

> We have learned by sad experience that it is the nature and disposition of almost all men, as soon as they get a little authority, as they suppose, they will immediately begin to exercise unrighteous dominion.

**Key idea:** Even a divine indictment of human weakness is framed as something *learned by sad experience* — used in the interview to argue that God deliberately lets people (including prophets) discover truth through trial rather than dictation. The verse's warning about "unrighteous dominion" also informs the discussion of [[Brigham Young]]'s authoritarian reputation.
