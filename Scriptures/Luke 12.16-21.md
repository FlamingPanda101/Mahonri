# Luke 12:16-21 — The Parable of the Rich Fool

> **16** …The ground of a certain rich man brought forth plentifully… **18** I will pull down my barns, and build greater… **19** Soul, thou hast much goods laid up for many years; take thine ease, eat, drink, and be merry. **20** But God said unto him, Thou fool, this night thy soul shall be required of thee: then whose shall those things be…? **21** So is he that layeth up treasure for himself, and is not rich toward God. (Luke 12:16–21)

[[Sharon Eubank]] alludes to this parable in the [[Let's Get Real with Stephen Jones - Sharon Eubank on Doing Small Things with Great Love (E0040)|Let's Get Real interview]] to make the point that the man who stored up goods "for himself" — planning to "live an easy life from now on" — dies "that very night," and "what was all that for? Nothing." She uses it to contrast self-storing with seeking the interest of one's neighbor, the choice she frames as the perennial one "since the Council in Heaven."[^1] See [[Two Philosophies - Self-Interest Versus the Interest of Your Neighbor]].
