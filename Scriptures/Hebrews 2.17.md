# Hebrews 2:17

> Wherefore in all things it behoved him to be made like unto his brethren, that he might be a merciful and faithful high priest in things pertaining to God, to make reconciliation for the sins of the people. *(KJV)*

## Key point
Christ was "in all things" made like his brethren. The panel poses this as a biblical (not merely philosophical) challenge to the two-natures dodge: if scripture says Christ was made like us *in every respect*, where does scripture itself carve out a separate "divine nature" exempt from that likeness? The Trinitarian must answer biblically, not by importing the hypostatic-union framework. See [[Natures Do Not Act Only Persons Do]], [[Hypostatic Union]].
