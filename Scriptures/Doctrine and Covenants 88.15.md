# Doctrine and Covenants 88.15

"And the spirit and the body are the soul of man."

## Why it's cited

This verse defines the LDS doctrine that the "soul" is not a disembodied spirit but the union of spirit and body together. Apologetics notes cite it to support an embodied, materialist anthropology that parallels the LDS teaching of an embodied God, and to argue against the creedal devaluation of the physical body. Paired with [[Doctrine and Covenants 130.22]], it grounds the case that the Restoration affirms the goodness and permanence of the body — evidence, on the LDS reading, that later Christendom drifted away from a more anthropomorphic and embodied understanding inherited from the Hebrew scriptures.
