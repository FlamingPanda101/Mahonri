# Daniel 12

> And many of them that sleep in the dust of the earth shall awake, some to everlasting life, and some to shame and everlasting contempt. *(Daniel 12:2, KJV)*

## Significance

One of the **latest layers of the Hebrew Bible**, written in the middle of the 2nd century BCE under heavy persecution from the **Seleucid Empire**. [[Dan McClellan]] cites Daniel 12 — alongside [[Isaiah 66.24|Isaiah 66:24]] — as one of the earliest places where the concept of **postmortem divine punishment and reward** appears in the Hebrew Bible, with its language of resurrection to "everlasting life" versus "shame and everlasting contempt." Its timing supports the thesis that afterlife punishment emerged as a **theodicy** under imperial oppression: justice deferred to the life to come when the wicked plainly escaped it in this one.
