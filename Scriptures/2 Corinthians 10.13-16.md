# 2 Corinthians 10:13-16

> But we will not boast beyond limit, and we will observe the limits [Greek *canon*] God has appointed to us which reaches as far as you. … without boasting of work already done in another person's area [Greek *canon*]. *(trans. Thomas A. Wayment, NT for Latter-day Saints)*

## Key Greek term
- **κανών (kanōn)** — "a place that is measured and bordered," a measuring rod or boundary. Paul uses it for the **limits of his missionary territory**, both physical and spiritual.

## Significance
[[Thomas A. Wayment]] traces "the beginnings of a canonical conversation" to this passage: the earliest sense of *canon* is a **boundary of authority**, not a list of books. This grounds the thesis that canon began as a regional authority claim and a rule of faith before it became a fixed table of contents. See [[Canon Began as a Rule of Faith Not a List of Books]].
