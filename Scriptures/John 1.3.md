# John 1:3

> All things were made by him; and without him was not any thing made that was made. *(KJV)*

## The Greek
*panta di' autou egeneto, kai chōris autou egeneto oude hen ho gegonen* — "All things came to be **through him** (*di' autou*), and without him came to be not one thing that has come to be."

## The Grammar Issue
The KJV's "by him" obscures the construction: the Greek is ***dia* + the genitive** (*di' autou*), which marks **intermediate / instrumental agency** ("through"), not ultimate agency ("by," which Greek expresses with *hypo* / *apo*). The verb is *egeneto* ("came to be"), not a verb of originating creation. So the Logos is presented as the **agent through whom** creation occurs, with the **primary, originating agent** being God the Father.

[[Dan McClellan]] uses this against the argument that, because [[Genesis 1.1|Genesis 1:1]] makes God the creator and John 1:3 makes the Logos the creator, the Logos must *be* the God of Israel — documenting the *dia* = intermediate-agency reading from BDAG, [[Lydia Novakovich]] (Baylor Handbook), [[Daniel Wallace]] (1996), and [[John McHugh]] (ICC). The contrast is whether this supports a subordinate / mediator Christology (McClellan) or a distinct-person-but-same-essence Son (Trinitarian).
