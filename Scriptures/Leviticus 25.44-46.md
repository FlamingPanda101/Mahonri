# Leviticus 25:44-46

> Both thy bondmen, and thy bondmaids, which thou shalt have, shall be of the heathen that are round about you; of them shall ye buy bondmen and bondmaids. Moreover of the children of the strangers that do sojourn among you, of them shall ye buy, and of their families that are with you, which they begat in your land: and they shall be your possession. And ye shall take them as an inheritance for your children after you, to inherit them for a possession; they shall be your bondmen for ever: but over your brethren the children of Israel, ye shall not rule one over another with rigour. *(KJV)*

## Key Hebrew term
- **אֲחֻזָּה (ʾaḥuzzah)** — "possession / property," the same term used for inheritable land. Foreign slaves are heritable property "for ever," passed to one's children, in explicit contrast to fellow Israelites, over whom one must not rule "with rigour."

## Significance
[[Dan McClellan]] identifies this passage as the Hebrew Bible's clearest endorsement of **chattel slavery** — permanent, heritable ownership of foreign persons. He argues the only meaningful difference from transatlantic slavery is that biblical chattel slavery is **ethnically** based while the antebellum trade was **racially** (skin-color) based — "not a significant difference." Foreign slaves could be acquired from any seller; how the foreigners originally obtained the slaves "didn't matter to the Israelites." This rebuts the apologetic claim that biblical slavery was only voluntary debt-bondage.
