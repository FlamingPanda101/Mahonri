# 3 Nephi 19

> And it came to pass that when Jesus had thus prayed unto the Father, he came unto his disciples, and behold, they did still continue, without ceasing, to pray unto him; and they did not multiply many words, for it was given unto them what they should pray, and they were filled with desire. *(3 Nephi 19:24)*

## Key passage: "it was given unto them what they should pray"
After instituting the sacrament, the resurrected Christ stands among the Nephite disciples and commands them to pray; they are *given* the words to pray, become "white even as Jesus," and are encircled by the light of his countenance (vv. 25, 30). [[Todd McLauchlin]] reads the chapter as a canonical depiction of the [[The True Order of Prayer Is the Heartbeat of the Endowment|true order of prayer]] — the disciples receive the word, give voice to it, are unified and sanctified, and only then are "even greater things" opened. He pairs it with Christ's intercessory prayer (vv. 23, "that we may be one") and the Lord's Prayer pattern of the Sermon on the Mount ("after this manner... pray ye").
