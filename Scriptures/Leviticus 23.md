# Leviticus 23

> And the LORD spake unto Moses, saying, Speak unto the children of Israel, and say unto them… When ye be come into the land which I give unto you, and shall reap the harvest thereof, then ye shall bring a sheaf of the firstfruits of your harvest unto the priest. *(KJV, vv. 9–10)*

## In context
The master calendar of Israel's appointed feasts (*moedim*): Passover/Unleavened Bread and Firstfruits in the spring, the counted weeks to Weeks/Pentecost (**Shavuot**), then the fall cluster of Trumpets (**Yom Teruah**), the Day of Atonement (**Yom Kippur**), and Tabernacles (**Sukkot**) with its Eighth Day. The chapter's spring and fall festival blocks each open with the refrain **"and the LORD spake unto Moses."**

[[Christina Dymock]] uses that refrain as the structural diagnostic for her thesis that [[Nephi]] outlined First Nephi after this chapter: each feast-section of 1 Nephi begins with the Lord giving [[Lehi]] (and finally Nephi) a vision or command, mirroring Leviticus 23's "and the LORD spake unto Moses." The firstfruits law of vv. 9–10 is the template she reads behind 1 Nephi 18:24 (planting the Jerusalem seeds), and the chapter's seven feasts supply the seven-part skeleton.
