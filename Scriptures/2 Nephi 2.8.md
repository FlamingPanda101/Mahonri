# 2 Nephi 2:8 — "the inhabitants of the world"

> Wherefore, how great the importance to make these things known unto the inhabitants of the earth, that they may know that there is no flesh that can dwell in the presence of God, save it be through the merits, and mercy, and grace of the Holy Messiah... *(2 Nephi 2:8, Book of Mormon)*

## Key Detail
- Lehi counsels the young Jacob (born in the wilderness) on the importance of making the gospel known to **"the inhabitants of the earth."** If Lehi's family were the only people on the continent, the exhortation is puzzling — Jacob's brothers and cousins already knew. The verse is read as an internal textual clue that **other peoples** were present, undermining the "empty continent" premise of the DNA objection. (Cited in the source as "inhabitants of the world.")
