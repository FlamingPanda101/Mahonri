# D&C 6

A revelation given through [[Joseph Smith]] to [[Oliver Cowdery]] at Harmony,
Pennsylvania, in April 1829, one of four early revelations (sections 6, 7, 8, 9)
received around the start of Cowdery's work as scribe.[^1]

## The "Gift of Working with the Rod"

The source identifies this section (with some uncertainty between 6 and 8) as the
revelation telling Cowdery he has "the gift of working with the sprout."[^2] The
wording was revised across editions:

1. **Earliest manuscript:** "gift of working with the **sprout**."
2. **1833 Book of Commandments:** "working with the **rod**."
3. **1835 Doctrine and Covenants:** "gift of **Aaron**."[^3]

The revelation, in the voice of Christ, declares there is "no other power save
the power of God which can cause this rod of nature to work in your hands" —
framing the divining rod as a genuine **spiritual gift**, not a natural science
or folk superstition.[^4] See [[D&C 8|D&C 8]] and [[D&C 9|D&C 9]].
