# Mark 14:51-52 — The young man who fled naked

> And there followed him a certain young man, having a linen cloth cast about his naked body; and the young men laid hold on him: And he left the linen cloth, and fled from them naked. *(KJV)*

## Key Detail
At Jesus' arrest, Mark alone reports an unnamed **young man in a linen cloth** (*sindōn*) who, when seized, abandons the cloth and flees naked — a famously abrupt and unexplained detail. In the [[Ward Radio - The Top Ten Lost LDS Writings of the Apocrypha|Ward Radio apocrypha episode]], [[Jonah Barnes]] connects this verse to the disputed **Secret Gospel of Mark** (the Mar Saba letter attributed to [[Clement of Alexandria]], reported by Morton Smith in 1958/1960): in that text a young man comes to Jesus "wearing a linen cloth over his naked body" to be taught "the mystery of the kingdom of God" by night — read by the hosts as a veiled reference to esoteric **temple-style ordinances** edited out of canonical Mark. See [[The Secret Gospel of Mark Implies a Hidden Temple Gospel]].
