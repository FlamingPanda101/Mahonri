# Jeremiah 18:7-10

> At what instant I shall speak concerning a nation, and concerning a kingdom, to pluck up, and to pull down, and to destroy it; If that nation, against whom I have pronounced, turn from their evil, I will repent of the evil that I thought to do unto them. And at what instant I shall speak concerning a nation, and concerning a kingdom, to build and to plant it; If it do evil in my sight, that it obey not my voice, then I will repent of the good, wherewith I said I would benefit them. *(KJV)*

## Key Hebrew term
The verb rendered "repent" is **nāḥam (נָחַם)** — to relent, change one's intention, be moved to compassion. The passage explicitly conditions divine action on human response, with God reversing both threatened judgment and promised blessing.

## Key point
[[Blake Ostler]] reads this as the Bible's own statement that **all prophecies are conditional**: God's announced destruction or blessing is contingent on whether the nation repents or rebels. It is the prooftext anchoring the open-theist account of prophecy, paralleled by the [[Jonah 3.4|Nineveh]] narrative. Ostler also notes the passage "is totally inconsistent with the Calvinistic God," whose decree admits no relenting.
