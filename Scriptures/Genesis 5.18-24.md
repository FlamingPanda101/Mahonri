# Genesis 5:18–24

> And Jared lived an hundred sixty and two years, and he begat Enoch: And Jared lived after he begat Enoch eight hundred years, and begat sons and daughters: And all the days of Jared were nine hundred sixty and two years: and he died. And Enoch lived sixty and five years, and begat Methuselah: And Enoch walked with God after he begat Methuselah three hundred years, and begat sons and daughters: And all the days of Enoch were three hundred sixty and five years: And Enoch walked with God: and he was not; for God took him. (KJV)

## Key Hebrew Terms

- **וַיִּתְהַלֵּךְ חֲנוֹךְ אֶת־הָאֱלֹהִים (vayithalekh Chanokh et-ha-Elohim):** "And Enoch walked with God" — the verb הָלַךְ (halakh, "to walk") in the hithpael stem conveys an ongoing, intimate relationship. This phrase is used only of Enoch and Noah (Genesis 6:9) among the antediluvian patriarchs.
- **לָקַח (laqach):** "took" — "for God took him" (כִּי־לָקַח אֹתוֹ אֱלֹהִים). This verb is distinctive; it is NOT the standard verb for death (מוּת, muth, used for every other patriarch in the genealogy). The unusual wording spawned a rich tradition that Enoch did not die but was "translated" or "snatched up" to heaven.

## The Two Genealogies

Genesis contains two genealogies with a figure named "Enoch":

| Feature | Genesis 4 (Cain's line) | Genesis 5 (Seth's line) |
|---------|------------------------|-------------------------|
| **Enoch's father** | Cain (v. 17) | Jared (v. 18) |
| **Enoch's son** | Irad (v. 18) | Methuselah (v. 21) |
| **Associated city** | Cain builds a city named Enoch (v. 17) | No city |
| **Special destiny** | None recorded | "Walked with God" and was "taken" |
| **Position from Adam** | Third generation | Seventh generation |

Dan McClellan notes that Genesis 4 is "not written from a perspective that understands Adam and Eve and their children to be the only people on Earth" — it assumes an already-populated world. [^1] Genesis 5 presents Seth's line as the "more righteous line," and its Enoch — "the seventh from Adam" — is the figure claimed by the Book of Enoch.

## The 365 Years

Enoch's 365-year lifespan (compared to his son Methuselah's 969 years) is notably short — McClellan jokes he "must have gotten in a car accident." [^2] The number 365 corresponds to the days in a solar year, which connects symbolically to the Enochic [[Book of Enoch - 364 Day Solar Calendar|364-day solar calendar]] described in the Book of Luminaries. This numerical symbolism may have contributed to the Enochic tradition's association with calendrical and astronomical knowledge.
