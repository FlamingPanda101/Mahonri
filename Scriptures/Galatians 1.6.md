# Galatians 1:6

> I marvel that ye are so soon removed from him that called you into the grace of Christ unto another gospel. *(KJV)*

## Significance
The verse [[Ward Radio]] says critics "always skip." Two verses before the famous [[Galatians 1.8|Galatians 1:8]] curse, Paul expresses astonishment that the Galatians have *already* ("so soon") fallen away. Latter-day Saints read this as evidence that a [[Great Apostasy and Need for Restoration|rapid, early apostasy]] was underway even in the apostolic generation — undercutting the claim of an unbroken, uncorrupted "cradle Christianity."

The Greek **μετατίθεσθε** (*metatithesthe*, "you are being removed / are deserting") is present tense — the defection is in progress as Paul writes, not a distant future threat.
