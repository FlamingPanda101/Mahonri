# Alma 11:43

> 43 The spirit and the body shall be reunited again in its perfect form; both limb and joint shall be restored to its proper frame, even as we now are at this time; and we shall be brought to stand before God, knowing even as we know now, and have a bright recollection of all our guilt. *(Book of Mormon, Alma 11:43)*

## Significance
Amulek's resurrection sermon ties bodily restoration to a **perfect, undimmed memory** at the judgment: the resurrected "have a bright recollection of all our guilt," "knowing even as we know now." This "bright recollection" is the scriptural anchor for the [[NDE - The Life Review]], in which dying persons relive their lives with total, vivid accuracy and judge themselves by what they see — recognizing their own loving and selfish acts rather than receiving an arbitrary verdict. It pairs naturally with [[2 Nephi 9.14|2 Nephi 9:14]] ("a perfect knowledge of all our guilt") and Alma's doctrine of restoration in [[Alma 41.3-7|Alma 41:3–7]].
