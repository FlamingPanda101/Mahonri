# Matthew 2:13–15 (KJV)

> And when they were departed, behold, the angel of the Lord appeareth to Joseph in a dream, saying, Arise, and take the young child and his mother, and flee into Egypt, and be thou there until I bring thee word: for Herod will seek the young child to destroy him. When he arose, he took the young child and his mother by night, and departed into Egypt: and was there until the death of Herod: that it might be fulfilled which was spoken of the Lord by the prophet, saying, Out of Egypt have I called my son.

## Key Detail
This is the **flight to Egypt** — narrated only in *Matthew*, not Luke (a point [[Alex OConnor|Alex O'Connor]] misattributes to Luke in the debate, though the substance stands). Matthew explicitly states its purpose: "*that it might be fulfilled* which was spoken... Out of Egypt have I called my son," quoting [[Hosea 11.1|Hosea 11:1]]. The verse therefore wears its motive on its sleeve — the episode is framed as prophecy-fulfillment. This generates two skeptical pressures: (1) the original Hosea text is about the *nation of Israel*, not a Messiah, so the fulfillment is typological reuse; and (2) the flight contradicts [[Luke 2.39|Luke 2:39]], which has the family return peacefully to Nazareth with no Egyptian sojourn.[^1]
