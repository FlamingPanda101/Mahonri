# Acts 2:38

> Then Peter said unto them, Repent, and be baptized every one of you in the name of Jesus Christ for the remission of sins, and ye shall receive the gift of the Holy Ghost. *(KJV)*

## Key phrase — "for the remission of sins"
Peter's Pentecost answer to "what shall we do?" couples **repentance and baptism** with "**for** the remission of sins" (Greek *eis aphesin tōn hamartiōn*) — not, on its face, as a symbol of remission already received. [[Alex Arnold]] cites it (with John 3:5, "born of water and of the Spirit") to argue that baptism is how grace is received in covenant form, not a work that earns salvation: "the ordinance doesn't earn salvation; it's the handshake that seals the agreement." See [[Every Person Gets a Fair Opportunity Through Proxy Ordinances]] and the vault's [[Sola Fide vs Faith Plus Ordinances]].

## How different traditions read this
- **LDS / Catholic / Orthodox / much of the early church:** baptism is instrumentally tied to the remission of sins (baptismal regeneration), an ordinance through which grace is conveyed.
- **Much of evangelical Protestantism:** *eis* is read causally-backward ("because of," "with reference to") so baptism symbolizes a remission already granted by faith alone — a reading Arnold treats as motivated by the prior commitment to *sola fide*.
