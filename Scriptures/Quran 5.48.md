# Quran 5:48 — confirming and "muhaymin" over prior scripture

> And We have revealed to you, [O Muhammad], the Book in truth, confirming that which preceded it of the Scripture and as a criterion (*muhaymin*) over it... *(Sahih International)*

## Key Detail
The pivotal verse behind [[The Guardian and Criterion Reply to the Islamic Dilemma]]. The Qur'an is described as "confirming" (*musaddiq*) the prior Scripture **and** as *muhaymin* over it. The whole dispute turns on *muhaymin*:
- **Muslim reading:** guardian / overseer / corrective — the Qur'an *guards what is true and supersedes what is false* in the earlier books, which licenses accepting only the parts that agree with the Qur'an.
- **[[Avery (God Logic)|Avery]]'s reading:** a guardian "is not a judge or decider," so the verse points *back* to the prior scriptures as truth rather than disqualifying them; and no verse says "judge the Bible by the Qur'an," whereas [[Quran 10.94|10:94]] runs the dependence the other way.

The lexical range of *muhaymin* (guardian / witness / trustee / preserver / overseer) is genuinely contested, so this verse is the hinge on which the dilemma and its rebuttal both rest.
