# Omni 1:30

> And I, Amaleki, had a brother, who also went with them; and I have not since known concerning them. And I am about to lie down in my grave; and these plates are full. And I make an end of my speaking. *(Omni 1:30, Book of Mormon)*

[[Amaleki]], the last writer on the small plates, closes the record by noting his brother "went with them" — the people of [[Zeniff]] who returned to the land of Nephi — and vanished from his knowledge. Amaleki gives the plates to [[King Benjamin]] because he has no children.

## Why it's cited
This verse — together with the name of Amaleki's father, "Abinadom" ([[Abadom]]) — is the textual hook for the *Key to the Keystone* reconstruction that the prophet [[Abinadi]] was the vanished heir of the record-keeping (brass-plates) royal line. The argument is admittedly circumstantial (a missing brother + a near-identical name), but it would explain how Abinadi could quote the brass-plates Isaiah, including a now-lost apocryphal Isaiah.
