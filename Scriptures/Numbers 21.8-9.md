# Numbers 21:8-9 (The Bronze Serpent)

> And the LORD said unto Moses, Make thee a fiery serpent, and set it upon a pole... And Moses made a serpent of brass, and put it upon a pole, and... when he beheld the serpent of brass, he lived. *(KJV)*

## Key point
A God-*commanded* image — later destroyed when Israel began worshipping it (2 Kings 18:4, "Nehushtan"). [[Joe Heschmeyer]] uses it to locate the idolatry boundary: a divinely made object becomes an idol "once they're ascribing divine worship to the thing that isn't God." As a Catholic he affirms religious images are legitimate until worshipped — the same logic he applies to Brigham Young's worship of Adam.
