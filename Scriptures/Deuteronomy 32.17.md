# Deuteronomy 32:17

> "They sacrificed unto devils, not to God; to gods whom they knew not, to new gods that came newly up, whom your fathers feared not." (KJV)

The word rendered "devils/demons" is Hebrew **shedim** (שֵׁדִים). In critical scholarship *shedim* are a class of **foreign deity** (cf. Akkadian *šēdu* protective spirits; the Deir Alla inscription), not "demons" in the later Greco-Roman/Christian sense — that interpretation enters via the Septuagint (*daimonia*, c. 3rd century BCE). The contrastive clause is better read "to *shedim* and **not** to Eloah" (i.e., not to Yahweh).
