# Abraham 4.1

> And then the Lord said: Let us go down. And they went down at the beginning, and they, that is the Gods, organized and formed the heavens and the earth.
> — Abraham 4:1 (Pearl of Great Price)

From the Book of Abraham, which [[Joseph Smith]] produced largely in 1835 (with some 1842 work in Nauvoo). [[Casey Paul Griffiths]] identifies this chapter as Joseph Smith's stated source for the idea of a *council of gods* who "planned and executed... the creation of the earth," and points to it as the canonical home of the plurality-of-gods teaching that appears in the [[King Follett Discourse]].

Key term: the verb is *organized / formed* (not "created from nothing"), supporting the LDS reading that God shaped pre-existing materials — see [[Creation Ex Nihilo Is Not Biblical]] and [[Intelligence Is Uncreated and Eternally Has Agency]].
