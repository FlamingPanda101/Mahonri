# 1 Nephi 4.31

> And now, I, Nephi, being a large man in stature, and also having received much strength of the Lord, therefore I did seize upon the servant of Laban, and held him, that he should not flee. *(1 Nephi 4:31, Book of Mormon)*

This verse closes the account of Nephi obtaining the brass plates from Laban in Jerusalem. After two failed attempts to acquire the records (first by request, then by purchase with the family's wealth), Nephi returns alone by night, led by the Spirit, and finds Laban incapacitated. Following a command he initially resists, Nephi takes the plates and, disguised in Laban's clothing, secures the records from Laban's treasury with the help of Laban's servant Zoram. The killing of Laban is narrated as a divinely commanded act, justified in the text by the principle that it is better for one man to perish than for a whole nation to dwindle in unbelief; the present verse describes Nephi physically restraining Zoram afterward, noting his large stature and God-given strength.

## Why it's cited
The clause "being a large man in stature" is cited in apologetics arguments connecting Nephi to ancient temple/initiation imagery and kingship typology — that his physical stature and divinely granted strength mark him as a figure who could undergo a temple-style ordeal or stand in a priestly-royal role. The verse is also read alongside the broader account of recovering the brass plates as a deliberate Exodus- and Davidic-style narrative pattern (a champion securing covenant records by night).
