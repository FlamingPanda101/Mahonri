# 1 Corinthians 2:6-7

> Howbeit we speak wisdom among them that are perfect: yet not the wisdom of this world... But we speak the wisdom of God in a mystery, even the hidden wisdom, which God ordained before the world unto our glory. *(KJV)*

## Key terms — *teleioi* and *mystērion*
Paul distinguishes what he teaches publicly from "wisdom" spoken "among **them that are perfect**" (Greek **τέλειοι, teleioi**) — a word most translators render "the mature" or "the complete," but which in a first-century mystery context carried the connotation of **those who had been fully initiated**. He calls this content "the wisdom of God in a **mystery** (*mystērion*)... the hidden wisdom... which none of the princes of this world knew." [[Dave Butler]] (via [[Alex Arnold]]) reads this as initiation language paralleling [[Matthew 13.10-11|Matthew 13:11]]: there was a body of revealed content accessible to insiders who had been through something. See [[The Acts of John Records Jesus Instituting a Mystery Ritual]]; the vault treats *teleios* in [[Teleios Means Initiate - You Will Be Endowed Not Be Flawless]].

## How different traditions read this
- **Mainstream Christian:** *teleioi* = the spiritually mature believer; "mystery" = the gospel of the crucified Christ, once hidden, now revealed to all the faithful — no esoteric rite.
- **LDS / temple reading:** *teleioi* connotes the initiated, and the "hidden wisdom... ordained before the world" is content tied to ordinances — converging with the endowment's claim to preserve an initiatory mystery.
