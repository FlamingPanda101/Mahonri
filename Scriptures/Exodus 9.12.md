# Exodus 9:12

> And the LORD hardened the heart of Pharaoh, and he hearkened not unto them; as the LORD had spoken unto Moses. *(KJV)*

## The Hebrew
The narrator uses וַיְחַזֵּק (*wayĕḥazzēq*, "and the LORD strengthened/made firm") — God, not Pharaoh, is the grammatical subject, and the clause "as the LORD had spoken unto Moses" frames it as fulfillment of the plan announced in [[Exodus 4.21|Exodus 4:21]].

## Exodus 9:14-16 (cited in the same passage)
> For I will at this time send all my plagues upon thine heart, and upon thy servants, and upon thy people; that thou mayest know that there is none like me in all the earth. For now I will stretch out my hand, that I may smite thee and thy people with pestilence; and thou shalt be cut off from the earth. And in very deed for this cause have I raised thee up, for to shew in thee my power; and that my name may be declared throughout all the earth. *(KJV)*

The key phrase הֶעֱמַדְתִּיךָ (*heʿĕmaḏtîḵā*, "I have raised you up / caused you to stand") is the verse Paul later quotes in Romans 9:17 to argue divine sovereignty over Pharaoh. God states he could have destroyed Pharaoh already but deliberately preserved him "to shew... my power."
