# Alma 5:18

> 18 Or do ye imagine to yourselves that ye can lie unto the Lord in that day, and say—Lord, our works have been righteous works upon the face of the earth—and that he will save you? Or otherwise, can ye imagine yourselves brought before the tribunal of God with your souls filled with guilt and remorse, having a remembrance of all your guilt, yea, a perfect remembrance of all your wickedness... *(Book of Mormon, Alma 5:18)*

## Significance
Alma presses his hearers to imagine the judgment with "a **perfect remembrance** of all your wickedness." This "perfect remembrance" is a third witness, alongside [[Alma 11.43|Alma 11:43]] and [[2 Nephi 9.14|2 Nephi 9:14]], to the Book of Mormon's teaching that the dead recall their lives with total clarity — the doctrinal counterpart to the vivid, complete, self-judging replay reported in the [[NDE - The Life Review]].
