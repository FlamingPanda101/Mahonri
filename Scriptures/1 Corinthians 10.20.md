# 1 Corinthians 10:20

> "But I say, that the things which the Gentiles sacrifice, they sacrifice to devils, and not to God: and I would not that ye should have fellowship with devils." (KJV)

Paul says pagan sacrifice is offered "to devils/demons" (*daimonia*), not to God — even after declaring "an idol is nothing" ([[1 Corinthians 8.4-6|1 Cor 8:4]]). The idol is nothing; the entity behind it is real. McClellan reads this as confirming the indexing model (idol ≠ deity) and notes Paul's *daimonia* reflects the Hellenistic-era demonological reinterpretation, not the Hebrew *shedim* sense (a class of foreign deity).
