# Isaiah 44:6

> Thus saith the LORD the King of Israel, and his redeemer the LORD of hosts; I am the first, and I am the last; and beside me there is no God. *(KJV)*

## Key Term: "beside me there is no God"
Hebrew *mibbal'aday eyn elohim*. [[Blake Ostler]] argues this and its sister texts function as Ancient Near Eastern **taunts of incomparability** and demands for sole allegiance — the same boast a Persian king like Xerxes could make while knowing other kings existed — not a Greek-metaphysical denial that other *elohim* exist. The speaker is Yahweh, a single divine person (the Father), claiming preeminence and exclusive worship.

## Used against the Church (by Catholic apologists)
[[Trent Horn]] groups this verse with Isaiah 43:10 and 44:8 as classical-monotheism prooftexts against LDS henotheism. The LDS reply (Ostler, above) reads it as a taunt of incomparability, not a denial of the divine council. See [[Ancient Israelite Henotheism Was Not Mormon Henotheism]].
