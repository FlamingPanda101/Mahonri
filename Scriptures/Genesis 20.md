# Genesis 20 — Abraham, Sarah, and Abimelech of Gerar (the second wife-sister episode)

At Gerar, [[Abraham]] again calls Sarah his sister; King Abimelech takes her, but God warns him in a dream ("thou art but a dead man, for the woman... she is a man's wife"). Abimelech protests his innocence, returns Sarah with livestock and silver, and Abraham prays so the closed wombs of Abimelech's house are opened.

> But God came to Abimelech in a dream by night, and said to him, Behold, thou art but a dead man, for the woman which thou hast taken; for she is a man's wife. *(Gen 20:3, KJV)*

## Key Detail
[[Dan McClellan]] reads this as a **more developed** version of [[Genesis 12]]: it explains how the king finds out (a dream), adds Abimelech's protest of integrity and God's admission that *he* kept Abimelech from sinning, and introduces the **half-sister** rationalization ("she is indeed my sister, the daughter of my father but not... my mother") — read as an attempt to make Abraham "not a liar." McClellan notes the chapter uses *Elohim* throughout, except verse 18 (the only Tetragrammaton in the chapter), which he treats as a **later editorial addition** resolving the closed-wombs subplot. See [[The Wife-Sister Story Is a Tripled Doublet]], [[Polygyny Was Normative but Wife-Sharing Was the Transgression]].
