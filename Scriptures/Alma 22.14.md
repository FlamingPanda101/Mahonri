# Alma 22:14

> And since man had fallen he could not merit anything of himself; but the sufferings and death of Christ atone for their sins, through faith and repentance, and so forth; and that he breaketh the bands of death, that the grave shall have no victory, and that the sting of death should be swallowed up in the hopes of glory. *(Alma 22:14, Book of Mormon)*

[[Aaron]], teaching the Lamanite king, uses the same "sting of death… swallowed up" language as [[Abinadi]].

## Why it's cited
One of the four Book of Mormon occurrences of the "sting of death is swallowed up" motif ([[Mosiah 16.7-8|Mosiah 16]], here, [[Mormon 7.5|Mormon 7:5]], plus the echo at Alma 22:14). The Ward Radio panel treats the repetition across Nephite prophets as evidence the phrase came from a shared brass-plates source (an apocryphal Isaiah) rather than being separately lifted from Paul.
