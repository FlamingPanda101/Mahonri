# 1 Nephi 16.13

> And it came to pass that we did take our tents and depart into the wilderness, across the river Laman. And it came to pass that we did travel for the space of four days, nearly a south-southeast direction, and we did pitch our tents again; and we did call the name of the place Shazer. *(1 Nephi 16:13, Book of Mormon)*

This verse records the second leg of Lehi's family's journey through the Arabian wilderness after departing from the river Laman. Traveling roughly south-southeast for four days, they make camp and name the site "Shazer."

## Why it's cited
The place-name "Shazer" is cited in apologetics arguments on two fronts. First, as evidence for the Book of Mormon's historicity: "Shazer" plausibly derives from a Semitic/Arabic root for "trees" or a small grove (e.g. shajar), and a candidate location along a plausible south-southeast trail in Arabia fits, an argument for an authentic ancient itinerary. Second, in temple-symbolism readings, the name is connected to "fine twined linen" and the door/veil of the tabernacle, treating the journey's named stations as encoding sacred imagery. The verse thus does double duty: external geographic plausibility and internal temple typology.
