# Deuteronomy 34 — the death of Moses

> So Moses the servant of the LORD died there in the land of Moab, according to the word of the LORD. And he buried him in a valley in the land of Moab, over against Bethpeor: but no man knoweth of his sepulchre unto this day... And there arose not a prophet since in Israel like unto Moses, whom the LORD knew face to face. *(KJV, Deut 34:5-6, 10)*

## Key Detail
The closing chapter of the Pentateuch narrates [[Moses]]'s own death, burial, and the fact that "no man knoweth of his sepulchre unto this day," adding that "there arose not a prophet since in Israel like unto Moses." A narrator writing "unto this day" and "since" stands at a temporal distance *after* Moses — the textbook reason source critics (and many traditional readers, who attributed the verses to Joshua or Ezra) hold that at least the end of the Torah is post-Mosaic. [[Mike Day]] pairs it with the Book of Mormon's divergent account of Moses's end in [[Alma 36.1|Alma 45]] (translation rather than burial).
