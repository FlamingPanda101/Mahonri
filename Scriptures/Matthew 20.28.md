# Matthew 20:28

> Even as the Son of man came not to be ministered unto, but to minister, and to give his life a ransom for many. *(KJV)*

## Key phrase
"**The Son of man came... not to be ministered unto, but to minister**" — the model of self-giving service.

## Apologetic / historical application
[[Miranda Wilcox]] uses this verse (in its Latin Vulgate form) as the epigraph for the "Ministry" section of the Medieval Afterword. It grounds her account of how medieval Christians "felt called to imitate the life of Christ" and "discerned vocations to prayer, poverty, preaching, evangelizing, nursing, celibacy, pastoral care, contemplation, and other good works" — from the desert hermits to [[Francis of Assisi]] and [[Clare of Assisi]]. See [[Medieval Christians Imitated Christ Through Diverse Religious Vocations]].
