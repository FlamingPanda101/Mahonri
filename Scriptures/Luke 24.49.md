# Luke 24:49

> And, behold, I send the promise of my Father upon you: but tarry ye in the city of Jerusalem, until ye be endued with power from on high. *(KJV)*

## Significance
The scriptural taproot of the Latter-day Saint term **endowment**. The resurrected Christ, after giving the great commission, tells the disciples to wait in Jerusalem "until ye be endued [endowed] with power from on high" — a power they receive at Pentecost (Acts 2) and which propels the apostolic preaching. Historian [[Jonathan Stapley]] notes that this verse "is how the idea of endowment comes into our lexicon," and that the Doctrine and Covenants reproduces its exact pattern by commanding the Saints to tarry — not in Jerusalem but in Kirtland — "until you are endowed with power from on high." The emphasis on power *to go and build the kingdom* grounds the reading that the temple endowment equips disciples for present action, not only for the afterlife.
