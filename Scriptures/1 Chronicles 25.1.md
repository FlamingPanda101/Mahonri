# 1 Chronicles 25:1 (KJV)

> Moreover David and the captains of the host separated to the service of the sons of Asaph, and of Heman, and of Jeduthun, who should prophesy with harps, with psalteries, and with cymbals…

## Significance
This is the linchpin text for [[Aubrey Johnson]]'s **cultic prophet** thesis: David appoints temple musicians from three guilds (Asaph, Heman, Jeduthun) "who should prophesy" — the Hebrew verb is *naba*, "to prophesy." [[Mike Day]] and [[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)]] note this datum appears in Chronicles but not in Kings (a Deuteronomistic book), and read these prophesying musicians as the human counterpart to the singing angels of the temple vision (Isaiah 6) — possibly the *qedeshim* Josiah purged. See [[Cultic Prophets Were Singing Temple Musicians]].
