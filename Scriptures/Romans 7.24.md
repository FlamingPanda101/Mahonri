# Romans 7:24

> O wretched man that I am! who shall deliver me from the body of this death? *(KJV)*

## Key phrase
"**O wretched man that I am!**" — Greek *talaipōros egō anthrōpos*, the cry of the persona caught between the good it wills and the evil it does ([[Romans 7.19|Romans 7:19]]).

## Apologetic / historical application
[[Brian Thomassen]] argues [[Paul]] here **directly quotes Seneca** — *talaipōros... anthropos* — Seneca being a contemporary who wrote his own *Medea*. This is one plank of Thomassen's case that Romans 7 is a Greco-Roman literary performance built on the *Medea*/*akrasia* tradition, not autobiography. See [[Romans Argues Real Self-Mastery Comes Through Christ Not the Law]] and [[Paul's I in Romans 7 Is a Fictive Persona Not Autobiography]].
