# Mosiah 21:28

> And now Limhi was again filled with joy on learning from the mouth of Ammon
> that king Mosiah had a gift from God, whereby he could interpret such
> engravings; yea, and Ammon also did rejoice. *(current edition)*

**1830 first-edition reading:** "...that king **Benjamin** had a gift from God..."

## Context

The 1830 text named **King Benjamin** as the seer with the gift to interpret
engravings; later editions changed it to **King Mosiah** (Benjamin's son). The
problem: by the point in the narrative when Ammon reports this, Benjamin has
already died and Mosiah reigns, so "Benjamin" appears anachronistic. Possible
explanations canvassed in the video include an ancient scribal error, an editing
mistake, or an accidental slip by Joseph Smith. Researcher [[Don Bradley]] has
written on this change. Saints Unscripted concedes it may simply be one of the
"mistakes of men" the Book of Mormon openly acknowledges.

## Key Exegetical Point

- **A genuine textual problem, conceded:** Unlike the wording/Christology edits,
  this one is not easily harmonized; the apologetic move is to accept that the
  Book of Mormon contains human errors, citing the title-page admission and
  Joseph Smith's clarified sense of "most correct book."
