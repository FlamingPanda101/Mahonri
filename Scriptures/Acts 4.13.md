# Acts 4:13

> Now when they saw the boldness of Peter and John, and perceived that they were unlearned and ignorant men, they marvelled; and they took knowledge of them, that they had been with Jesus. *(KJV)*

## The Greek
*Agrammatoi... kai idiōtai* — "**unlettered** (illiterate) and **untrained/ordinary** men." *Agrammatos* literally means "without letters," i.e. lacking formal education in reading and writing.

## Significance
This is the verse [[Hugo Mendez]] cites to argue that **[[John the Evangelist|John the son of Zebedee]] could not have written the Gospel of John**: Acts presents Peter and John as *agrammatoi*, unlettered men, whereas the Gospel of John is a theologically and philosophically sophisticated literary work. Combined with John's profile as a Galilean fisherman with no Jerusalem connections, this makes the traditional attribution unlikely.
