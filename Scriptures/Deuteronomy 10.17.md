# Deuteronomy 10:17

> "For the LORD your God is God of gods, and Lord of lords, a great God, a mighty, and a terrible..." (KJV)

[[Dan McClellan]] reads "God of gods" as superlative rhetoric of *incomparability*: like "King of kings," the phrase presupposes other rulers to rank above. The boast only has force if the other gods are seen as existing and as powerful — the more powerful they are, the greater Yahweh is made to sound by being called the god over all of them. See [[Rhetoric of Incomparability Is Not Monotheism]] and [[Other Gods Are Real Divine Beings Not Merely Idols]].
