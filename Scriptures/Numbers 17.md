# Numbers 17

> And the LORD spake unto Moses, saying ... write thou every man's name upon his
> rod. ... And it came to pass, that on the morrow Moses went into the
> tabernacle of witness; and, behold, the rod of Aaron for the house of Levi was
> budded, and brought forth buds, and bloomed blossoms, and yielded almonds.
> (Numbers 17:1, 8, KJV)

## Key Term

- **מַטֶּה (matteh)** — "rod," "staff," also "tribe." Each tribe placed a rod
  by the Ark of the Covenant in the Holy of Holies overnight.

## Aaron's Budding Rod as Divine Sign

The source cites this episode as a biblical instance of a **rod functioning as a
revelatory/divinatory instrument**: each tribe of Israel placed a rod next to
the Ark in the tabernacle, and overnight Aaron's rod alone budded and sprouted,
revealing that the Levites would hold the priesthood.[^1] Mark explicitly calls
this "divination," because "by Aaron's rod budding and sprouting that shows that
the Levites are the guys who get the priesthood."[^2] This is used to argue that
[[Oliver Cowdery]]'s "rod of nature" / "gift of Aaron" stands in continuity with
Old Testament precedent rather than being mere folk magic.
