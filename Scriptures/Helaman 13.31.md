# Helaman 13:31 — "they become slippery, that ye cannot hold them"

> And behold, the time cometh that he curseth your riches, that they become slippery, that ye cannot hold them; and in the days of your poverty ye cannot retain them. *(Helaman 13:31, Book of Mormon)*

## Significance
Part of the prophecy of **Samuel the Lamanite** (Helaman 13–15), warning the Nephites that hoarded wealth would be cursed and "slippery." In [[Spencer W. Kimball]]'s personal scriptures this verse is the centerpiece of a remarkable annotation: he **bracketed 89 verses in a row** — the longest unbroken marked passage in his Book of Mormon — covering all of chapters 13, 14, and 15, then highlighted within. Beside verse 31 he wrote a single wry word with a question mark; the [[Ward Radio - What Spencer W Kimball Wrote in the Margins of His Scriptures|Ward Radio]] panel's guess for the joke was "**Taxes?**" The same bracketed block carries his terse study shorthand on the surrounding signs of Christ's birth ("Christ, five years"; "Two days, one night"; "Star, BC five years"; "Samuel, too late").[^1]
