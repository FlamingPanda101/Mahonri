# Luke 2:2-3 (KJV)

> (And this taxing was first made when Cyrenius was governor of Syria.) And all went to be taxed, every one into his own city.

## Chronological Problem

[[Quirinius]] (Cyrenius in KJV) governed Syria starting in 6 CE, after Herod Archelaus was deposed and Rome assumed direct rule of Judea. Luke 1 places Jesus's birth during the reign of Herod the Great, who died ~4 BCE — approximately a full decade before Quirinius's governorship.[^1]

## "His Own City" Ambiguity

Luke 2:3 says everyone went to "his own city." [[Luke 2.39|Luke 2:39]] uses the identical Greek phrase to describe Joseph and Mary returning not to Bethlehem but to "their own town of Nazareth." If "own city" means current hometown, they left their own city (Nazareth) to go to Bethlehem — defeating the stated purpose of the census.[^2]

## Historical Issues

No record exists of an empire-wide Roman census requiring travel to ancestral hometowns. Such a census would have crippled the economy and produced useless tax data. Rome's interest was in who lived and worked where — not where someone's ancestors came from. Herod, as a client king, handled his own taxation; Rome only demanded tribute.[^3]
