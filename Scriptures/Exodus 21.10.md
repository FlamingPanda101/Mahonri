# Exodus 21:10 (KJV)

> "If he take him another wife; her food, her raiment, and her duty of marriage, shall he not diminish."

**Note:** A Covenant-Code provision regulating plural marriage: a man who takes an additional wife may **not diminish** the food, clothing, or marital rights owed to the first. Latter-day Saint commentators read it as a built-in *limit* on plural marriage — the obligations are non-negotiable, so a man who cannot "deal justly" with multiple wives must not marry them. Selene Anderson uses it to rebut the caricature that LDS men could take "an infinite number of wives."

- [[Plural Marriage and the Law of Consecration Are the Same Law]] — Anderson cites it for the principle that plural marriage is bounded and woman-protective, not unlimited.
