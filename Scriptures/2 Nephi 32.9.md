# 2 Nephi 32:9

> But behold, I say unto you that ye must pray always, and not faint; that ye must not perform any thing unto the Lord save in the first place ye shall pray unto the Father in the name of Christ, that he will consecrate thy performance unto thee, that thy performance may be for the welfare of thy soul.
> — 2 Nephi 32:9 (Book of Mormon)

## Key point
[[Don Bradley]] cites this verse as the Book of Mormon parallel to the Book of Moses pattern that one should "do all that thou doest in the name of the Son." If the Father created the world *through* the Son (Moses 2-3; cf. [[John 1.1|John 1:1-3]]), then human acts of creation and action within that world are likewise to be done through [[Jesus of Nazareth|Christ]] — concretely, praying "in the name of Christ" so that one's "performance" is consecrated. It supports the argument that the creation account is a model for Christ-centered living and consecration. See [[The Creation Story Is a Model for How to Live]].[^1]
