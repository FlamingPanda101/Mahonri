# Luke 1:41

> And it came to pass, that, when Elisabeth heard the salutation of Mary, the babe leaped in her womb; and Elisabeth was filled with the Holy Ghost. *(KJV)*

## Use in the ensoulment debate
[[Joe Heschmeyer]] cites this in the abortion exchange as evidence that the unborn child possesses full personal life *very early* — the infant John the Baptist "leaps" in Elizabeth's womb in recognition of the newly conceived Christ, when Jesus is seemingly within a couple of weeks of conception. Used against [[Jacob Hansen]]'s delayed-ensoulment view, it suggests personhood (and even prophetic response) is present long before any patristic "animation" timeline would allow.

## In context
Hansen replies that he does believe ensoulment "probably happens very, very early on," so the verse is compatible with his position — what he denies is that *every* fertilized egg from the instant of conception is already an ensouled person (see [[The IVF Reductio Against Conception-as-Ensoulment]]). The verse is suggestive but does not, by itself, fix the *exact* moment the spirit becomes inextricably linked to the body — the open question on the LDS view (see [[A Body from the Physical Creation Joined to a Premortal Spirit]]).
