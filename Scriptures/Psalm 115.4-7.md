# Psalm 115:4-7

> "Their idols are silver and gold, the work of men's hands. They have mouths, but they speak not: eyes have they, but they see not: They have ears, but they hear not: noses have they, but they smell not: They have hands, but they handle not: feet have they, but they walk not: neither speak they through their throat." (KJV)

The catalogue of sensory incapacities is predicated of the cult statue ("the work of men's hands") — the classic **impotence rhetoric** of the idol polemic. Its rhetorical force ("it's not real") targets the manufactured object, contrasted with Yahweh who "is in the heavens: he hath done whatsoever he hath pleased" (v. 3).
