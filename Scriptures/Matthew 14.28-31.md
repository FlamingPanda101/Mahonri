# Matthew 14:28-31

> And Peter answered him and said, Lord, if it be thou, bid me come unto thee on the water. And he said, Come. And when Peter was come down out of the ship, he walked on the water, to go to Jesus. But when he saw the wind boisterous, he was afraid; and beginning to sink, he cried, saying, Lord, save me. And immediately Jesus stretched forth his hand, and caught him. *(KJV)*

## Significance
The basis of the **Peter-on-the-water** analogy for faith and works: faith without the step (or the step without faith) saves no one — both put Peter in position to be saved by Christ's grace. See [[Faith and Works - Two Blades of the Scissors]].
