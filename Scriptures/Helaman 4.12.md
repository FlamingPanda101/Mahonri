# Helaman 4.12 — smiting the humble "upon the cheek... a mock of that which was sacred"

> And it was because of the pride of their hearts, because of their exceeding riches, yea, it was because of their oppression to the poor, withholding their food from the hungry, withholding their clothing from the naked, and smiting their humble brethren upon the cheek, making a mock of that which was sacred, denying the spirit of prophecy and of revelation, murdering, plundering, lying, stealing, committing adultery... *(Helaman 4:12, Book of Mormon)*

## Key Detail
- In a catalogue of Nephite apostate sins, smiting the humble **"upon the cheek"** is set in apposition to **"making a mock of that which was sacred."** [[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)|David Butler]]'s pivot: one can only *mock* something sacred — so the cheek-smiting that was mocked must itself have been a sacred act, i.e., a [[A Ritual Smiting of the Cheek Was a Lost Temple Ordinance|lost temple smiting-ordinance]]. The cleanest internal proof-text for that thesis, paired with [[Alma 14.14|Alma 14:14-25]]. A critic reads the phrase as ordinary cruelty mocking what the victims held holy.
