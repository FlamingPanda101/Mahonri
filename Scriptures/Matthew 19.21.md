# Matthew 19:21 (KJV)

> "Jesus said unto him, If thou wilt be perfect, go and sell that thou hast, and give to the poor, and thou shalt have treasure in heaven: and come and follow me."

**Key Greek term:** *téleios* (τέλειος) — the same word as [[Matthew 5.48|Matthew 5:48]]. Here Jesus glosses what "being *teleios*" looks like in practice: *not* zero moral error, but wholehearted, costly devotion expressed as care for the poor.

## Used in
- [[Be Perfect Defined Elsewhere as Caring for the Poor]] — Trevan Hatch uses this verse (with the rich young ruler, NRSV) to show that *teleios* in Matthew is defined by self-giving toward others, confirming the love-reading of Matthew 5:48.
