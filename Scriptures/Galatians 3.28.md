# Galatians 3:28

> There is neither Jew nor Greek, there is neither bond nor free, there is neither male nor female: for ye are all one in Christ Jesus. *(KJV)*

## Key Greek term
- **οὐκ ἔνι ... οὐδὲ ... οὐκ ἔνι (ouk eni ... oude ... ouk eni)** — the connective for "male and female" (ἄρσεν καὶ θῆλυ, *arsen kai thēlu*, "male AND female," echoing Gen 1:27 LXX) differs from the "neither/nor" used for Jew/Greek and slave/free. [[Glenn Scrivener]] notes this changed conjunction in arguing Paul is not abolishing the male/female distinction but asserting a metaphysical unity "in Christ."

## Significance
A central proof-text for the Christian-origin-of-equality claim. [[Glenn Scrivener]] argues Christians "lean too much on Galatians 3:28" as if it spawned the rights revolution by itself; he reads it instead as a reflection of a "profound theological truth" (all share one human nature because Christ became man for all) that "rippled out." [[Alex OConnor|Alex O'Connor]] counters that the same egalitarian language ("rich or poor, male or female") appears centuries earlier in the [[Hippocrates|Hippocratic Oath]], and that Paul is plainly not a "gender abolitionist." See [[Galatians 3-28 Is a Theological Reality Not a Rights Charter]] and [[Western Values Predate or Arise Independently of Christianity]].
