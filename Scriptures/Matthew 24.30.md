# Matthew 24:30

> And then shall appear the sign of the Son of man in heaven: and then shall all the tribes of the earth mourn, and they shall see the Son of man coming in the clouds of heaven with power and great glory. *(KJV)*

## Key phrase
"**Coming in the clouds of heaven**" — Jesus's own promise of his return, paired in the chapter with the warning (24:9) that Christians "shall... be killed" before his coming.

## Apologetic / historical application
[[Nicholas J. Frederick]] cites Matthew 24:27, 30 (alongside [[Acts 1.11|Acts 1:11]]) as the dominical basis for the early Christian certainty that Jesus *would* return. The "coming in the clouds" imagery is echoed in [[1 Thessalonians 4.16-18|1 Thessalonians 4:17]] (the saints "caught up in the clouds") and depicted in the sixth–seventh century apse mosaic of Saints Cosmas and Damian, Rome. Matthew 24:9's prophecy of martyrdom framed how persecuted Christians like Perpetua read their suffering (see [[Early Christian Persecution and the Hope for a Better World]]).
