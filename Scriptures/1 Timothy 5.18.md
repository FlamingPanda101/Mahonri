# 1 Timothy 5:18

> For the scripture saith, Thou shalt not muzzle the ox that treadeth out the corn. And, The labourer is worthy of his reward. *(KJV)*

## Key Greek phrase
**λέγει γὰρ ἡ γραφή (legei gar hē graphē)** — "for the scripture says." Paul introduces *two* quotations under the single heading "scripture": Deuteronomy 25:4 (OT) and a saying matching Luke 10:7 ("the laborer is worthy of his wages").

## In context
[[Anthony Rogers]] cites this to rebut [[Trent Horn]]'s claim that [[2 Timothy 3.16-17|2 Timothy 3:16]] refers only to the OT: here Paul calls Luke's Gospel "scripture" alongside Deuteronomy, showing NT writings were already regarded as Scripture within the apostolic era. Rogers also notes [[2 Peter 3.15-16|2 Peter 3:16]] calls Paul's letters "scripture."
