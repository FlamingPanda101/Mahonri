# Romans 5:12

> "Wherefore, as by one man sin entered into the world, and death by sin;
> and so death passed upon all men, for that all have sinned."

## Used In

- [[Hell - Appeal to the Fall Rebutted]] — cited by Christians who appeal to
  the Fall of Adam as the reason humans lack the attributes required to avoid
  damnation. The argument: because Adam's sin brought sin and death into the
  world, God is not responsible for the depraved state of humanity.

## Apologetic Significance

This verse is the go-to proof-text for the **"blame Adam, not God"** defense
of hell. The rebuttal (in [[Hell - Appeal to the Fall Rebutted]]) argues that
this doesn't work because:

1. God still created Adam with the attributes to sin
2. God perpetually creates depraved souls from nothing post-Fall
3. If the Fall doesn't prevent God from creating *some* who are saved, why
   not all?
4. Either God is bound by Adam's actions (making him impotent) or is
   creating souls he knows are damned (making him unjust)
