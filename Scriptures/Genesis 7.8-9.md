# Genesis 7:8–9

> Of clean beasts, and of beasts that are not clean, and of fowls, and of every thing that creepeth upon the earth, There went in two and two unto Noah into the ark, the male and the female, as God had commanded Noah. *(KJV)*

## Source-Critical Significance

This verse is part of what source critics identify as a **redactional seam** — a passage where the editor/redactor who wove together the Priestly (P) and non-Priestly (J) flood accounts tried to smooth the transition. The verse acknowledges the clean/unclean distinction (from J, [[Genesis 7.2-3|Genesis 7:2-3]]) but then says the animals entered "two and two" (from P, [[Genesis 6.19-20|Genesis 6:19-20]]) — an attempt to hold both traditions together that reveals their original separateness.

Dan McClellan references this as part of the broader evidence that the flood narrative is composed of "two originally separate accounts that have been interwoven" — a conclusion he calls "one of the most long-standing, widely agreed upon, best-supported source critical arguments that there is in the Hebrew Bible." [^1]

## The Stitching Pattern

The entire flood narrative alternates between P and J verse blocks. The redactor preserved both accounts rather than choosing one, creating the apparent contradictions that apologetic harmonizations attempt to resolve after the fact. Genesis 7:8–9 is a visible seam in the stitching — it names both traditions' categories while defaulting to P's number.
