# Matthew 7:13-14

> 13 Enter ye in at the strait gate: for wide is the gate, and broad is the way, that leadeth to destruction, and many there be which go in thereat:
> 14 Because strait is the gate, and narrow is the way, which leadeth unto life, and few there be that find it.

After the triple petition (ask / seek / knock, [[Matthew 7.7-8|Matt 7:7-8]]) the initiate passes through a **narrow gate** that "leadeth unto **life**." [[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)|David Butler]] notes the Septuagint preserved a Hebrew pun whereby Adam names Eve **Zoe** ("life") because she is the mother of all the living, so "life" here evokes the **tree of life** — confirmed two verses on by "every good tree bringeth forth good fruit" ([[Matthew 7.16-20|Matt 7:16-20]]). The thistles and thorns warned of are the Greek *akanthai* and *triboloi* of the Eden curse, read as the wall around Eden that the gate breaches. See [[Thorns and Thistles Are the Wall of Eden Guarding the Tree of Life]].
