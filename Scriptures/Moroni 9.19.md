# Moroni 9 — "they delight in everything save that which is good"

> ...they have lost their love, one towards another; and they thirst after blood and revenge ... they delight in everything save that which is good... *(Moroni 9, Book of Mormon, vv. 5, 19)*

## Significance
In Mormon's anguished letter to his son Moroni about the Nephites' final moral collapse, the recurring **good/Nephi** wordplay turns dark: the Nephites — the "good/fair ones" (Egyptian **Nephi/nfr**) — now "delight in everything save that which is **good**." It is the negative pole of the goodliness thread that runs from [[1 Nephi 1.1|1 Nephi 1:1]] to Mormon's "O ye fair ones" lament.
