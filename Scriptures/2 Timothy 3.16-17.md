# 2 Timothy 3:16-17

> All scripture is given by inspiration of God, and is profitable for doctrine, for reproof, for correction, for instruction in righteousness: That the man of God may be perfect, throughly furnished unto all good works. *(KJV)*

## Key Greek terms
- **θεόπνευστος (theopneustos)** — "God-breathed," v.16. [[Trent Horn]] argues the early church applied this word to church fathers, councils, even epitaphs, so it does not by itself entail an *infallible rule of faith*; he also notes the translation issue in *pasa graphē* ("all/every scripture").
- **ὠφέλιμος (ōphelimos)** — "profitable, useful, helpful." Horn stresses Scripture is called *profitable*, not *sufficient*; in v.17 it is the **man of God** (ἄρτιος, "complete/equipped"), not Scripture, that is called sufficient/furnished.

## In context
The classic Protestant proof-text for inspiration and sufficiency. [[Anthony Rogers]] notes he did *not* lead with this verse but appeals to many texts that say Scripture is "from God"; he also observes the Latin Vulgate (authentic per the Council of Trent) renders it *inspired*. Horn replies (1) Paul means primarily the OT "holy scriptures" Timothy knew "from infancy," and (2) "every scripture" cannot mean every book is an all-sufficient rule of faith (else Philemon alone would suffice). Rogers counters that the context presupposes Timothy's acquaintance with NT writings, since Paul elsewhere cites Luke's Gospel as Scripture ([[1 Timothy 5.18]]).
