# Psalm 14:1

> The fool hath said in his heart, There is no God. *(KJV)*

## Joseph Smith Translation

In his 1832–33 Bible revision, Joseph Smith rewrote the opening of Psalm 14 from "*There is no God*" to, in effect, "**no man hath seen God**... except he hath... borne record of the Son" (paralleling [[John 1.18|John 1:18]]), and recast the rest of the psalm with First-Vision-like language ("they draw near to me with their lips, but their hearts are far from me"). [[Don Bradley]] and [[Walker Wright]] argue this shows Joseph was already encoding the First Vision (and the Father's involvement) into scripture in 1832 — at the same time he wrote the 1832 First Vision account.

## Why it's cited

Rebuts the skeptical claim that Joseph added the Father (the "two personages") to the First Vision only later (1835+): the JST of Psalm 14, datable to 1832–33, already reflects seeing the Father through the Son. See [[The JST of Psalm 14 Shows the First Vision's Two Personages Were Early]].
