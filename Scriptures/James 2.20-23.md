# James 2:20-23

> But wilt thou know, O vain man, that faith without works is dead? Was not Abraham our father justified by works, when he had offered Isaac his son upon the altar? Seest thou how faith wrought with his works, and by works was faith made perfect? And the scripture was fulfilled which saith, Abraham believed God, and it was imputed unto him for righteousness: and he was called the Friend of God. *(KJV)*

## Significance
James points to Abraham's offering of [[Isaac]] as the paradigm of faith completed by works. In the Ward Radio "Biggest Cover Up" episode, [[Jonah Barnes]] presses the phrase "**when he had offered Isaac**" — arguing that, since for James the *works* (not merely the willingness) are what justify, the original tradition must have had Abraham actually carry the sacrifice through, consistent with the rabbinic Akedah traditions in which Isaac dies and is resurrected. (The mainstream reading takes "offered" as the willingness God accepted; see the steelman in the concept note.)
