# 2 Kings 23:5 — Josiah purges the old worship

> And he put down the idolatrous priests, whom the kings of Judah had ordained to burn incense in the high places in the cities of Judah, and in the places round about Jerusalem; them also that burned incense unto Baal, to the sun, and to the moon, and to the planets, and to all the host of heaven. *(2 Kings 23:5, KJV)*

## Key Term
- **Deuteronomistic Reform** — the account of King [[Josiah]]'s religious centralization (c. 621 BC) in 2 Kings 22–23: a found "book of the law," destruction of high places and altars, removal of priests, and consolidation of worship at the Jerusalem Temple. Hebrew **kohen / kemarim** distinguishes legitimate priests from the "idolatrous priests" (kemarim) put down here.

## Context
Critical scholarship ([[Martin Noth]], [[Frank Moore Cross]], later [[Margaret Barker]] and [[Michael Heiser]]) treats this episode as the visible edge of a wider Deuteronomistic editorial program that suppressed older traditions — divine council, open theophany, and pre-existent/embodied Messiah language. Apologists argue [[Lehi]]'s pre-reform theology in 1 Nephi 1 maps precisely onto what was being outlawed at this moment, 21 years before Lehi left Jerusalem.
