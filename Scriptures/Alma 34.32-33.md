# Alma 34:32-33

> For behold, this life is the time for men to prepare to meet God; yea, behold the day of this life is the day for men to perform their labors... do not procrastinate the day of your repentance until the end; for after this day of life, which is given us to prepare for eternity, behold, if we do not improve our time while in this life, then cometh the night of darkness wherein there can be no labor performed. *(Book of Mormon)*

## Significance
Amulek's teaching that "this life is the time... to prepare to meet God." D. Jill Kirby explicitly compares [[Augustine]]'s conclusion — that "the decisions of this life are spiritually definitive," setting death as the **"boundary of salvation"** in the West — to Alma 34:33. Both insist that one's afterlife state *follows from one's mortal orientation*. The comparison is double-edged: it shows a genuine LDS-patristic convergence on the decisiveness of mortal probation, while LDS doctrine still allows postmortem progression and missionary work (D&C 138) for those who never had a full chance.
