# Wisdom of Solomon 11:17

> "For thy Almighty hand, that made the world of matter without form, had not lost the means to send among them a multitude of bears or fierce lions." (KJV Apocrypha / Douay-Rheims rendering)

**Greek (LXX):** ἡ παντοδύναμός σου χεὶρ καὶ κτίσασα τὸν κόσμον ἐξ ἀμόρφου ὕλης (*hē pantodynamos sou cheir kai ktisasa ton kosmon ex amorphou hylēs*)

**NRSV:** "For your all-powerful hand, which created the world out of formless matter, did not lack the means to send upon them a multitude of bears or bold lions."

**Key phrase:** ἐξ ἀμόρφου ὕλης (*ex amorphou hylēs*) — "out of formless matter"

## Context

The Wisdom of Solomon was composed in Greek, likely in Alexandria, around the 1st century BCE — roughly contemporary with 2 Maccabees and within the same Hellenistic Jewish milieu. The author is philosophically literate in Greek thought (Platonic, Stoic) and seeks to present Jewish wisdom in a Hellenistic idiom.

This verse is part of a passage reflecting on God's creative power as the basis for His ability to execute judgment. The author argues that since God created the world from formless matter, He certainly has the power to send plagues or wild beasts against sinners.

## Significance for the Creation Ex Nihilo Debate

This verse is a **"smoking gun"** in the creation ex nihilo debate. Written in the same Hellenistic Jewish environment as 2 Maccabees 7:28, it explicitly states that God created the world **"out of formless matter"** (ἐξ ἀμόρφου ὕλης) — directly echoing Plato's *Timaeus* (where the Demiurge fashions the cosmos from pre-existing chaotic matter).

The phrase ἐξ ἀμόρφου ὕλης is unambiguous:
- **ἄμορφος (*amorphos*)** = "formless, shapeless" — the privation of form
- **ὕλη (*hylē*)** = "matter, material, stuff" — the Aristotelian/Platonic technical term for the material substrate

If 2 Maccabees 7:28 were truly teaching creation ex nihilo (creation without any pre-existing material at all), it would directly contradict Wisdom of Solomon — a text from the same period, the same language, and the same intellectual world. The fact that Wisdom 11:17 explicitly teaches creation from formless matter strongly supports the scholarly consensus that this was the normative Hellenistic Jewish view, and that 2 Maccabees 7:28 likewise reflects creation from unformed matter, not from nothing.
