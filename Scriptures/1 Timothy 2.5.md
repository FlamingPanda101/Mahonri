# 1 Timothy 2:5

> For there is one God, and one mediator between God and men, the man Christ Jesus. *(KJV)*

## Key phrase
"**One mediator... the man Christ Jesus**" — Greek **εἷς γὰρ θεός, εἷς καὶ μεσίτης θεοῦ καὶ ἀνθρώπων, ἄνθρωπος Χριστὸς Ἰησοῦς**. *Mesitēs* (mediator) is used to argue access to God runs through Christ alone, "given downward," not earned upward through institutional rites.

## Apologetic application
[[Gavin (Catholic Debater)]] cites it (with [[John 2.19-21|John 2:19-21]] and [[Hebrews 10.19-22|Hebrews 10:19-22]]) to contrast the triune God with "conditional access" models where presence is "institutionally gated" or escalated through "progressive worthiness" (he names [[D&C 76]]).
