# 1 Peter 4.6

> For for this cause was the gospel preached also to them that are dead, that they might be judged according to men in the flesh, but live according to God in the spirit. *(1 Peter 4:6, KJV)*

Peter states that the gospel was preached "also to them that are dead." Read together with
[[1 Peter 3.18-20|1 Peter 3:18-20]] (Christ preaching "to the spirits in prison"), it
describes a postmortem proclamation of the gospel to the dead.

## Why it's cited

Apologetics notes pair this verse with [[1 Corinthians 15.29|1 Corinthians 15:29]] to rebut
the claim that baptism for the dead "rests on only one ambiguous verse." If the gospel is
preached to the dead, and the Great Commission ([[Matthew 28.19|Matthew 28:19]]) commands
baptizing all who believe, then a provision for the dead to receive baptism follows from the
logic of the gospel itself — the doctrine is not built on a single proof-text. The verse also
grounds the Latter-day Saint teaching of postmortem missionary work ([[D&C 138]]).
