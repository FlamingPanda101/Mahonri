# Helaman 1:1-9 — Pahoran's three sons and the succession crisis

> ...there began to be a serious difficulty among the people of the Nephites. For behold, Pahoran had died ... And these are their names ... Pahoran, and Paanchi, and Pacumeni. *(Helaman 1, Book of Mormon)*

## Key Terms (Egyptian names in one chapter)
- **Pahoran** — attested at the Amarna letters as *Pa-ḥuru* / Egyptian *Pa-Ḥori*, "the Syrian / the Canaanite." Cf. biblical Egyptian-type names like *Pashhur*, *Panehesy*.
- **Paanchi** — a "dead ringer" for Egyptian *Pa-ʿankh*, "the living one / the one who is alive," from *ʿnkh* (ankh = life). The supporters who back Paanchi swear **life-oaths** ("by their everlasting Maker"), since *ankh* also yields the verb "to swear (by one's life)."
- **Pacumeni** — less certain; possibly Greek *Pachomios* (Nibley) or *Pa-kem*, "the blind one" (Sorenson/Hoskisson).

## Significance
A single chapter moves from **attested** (Pahoran) to **dead ringer** (Paanchi) to **plausible** (Pacumeni) Egyptian names — and the succession crisis seeds the oath-bound secret combinations that destroy Nephite society.
