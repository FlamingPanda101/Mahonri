# Deuteronomy 16:16

> Three times in a year shall all thy males appear before the LORD thy God in the place which he shall choose; in the feast of unleavened bread, and in the feast of weeks, and in the feast of tabernacles: and they shall not appear before the LORD empty. *(KJV)*

## Key Hebrew Terms

- **רָאָה (ra'ah):** "to see." The consonantal text reads the verb in the active Qal — "[they] shall see the face of the LORD" — but the [[Masoretes|Masoretic]] vowel pointing makes it the passive Niphal ("be seen / appear"). See [[Masoretic Vowels Reversed the Active Verb to Passive]].
- **פְּנֵי / פָּנִים (pene / panim):** "face." The text has "the face of (the LORD)," and uses the direct-object construction rather than the preposition *liphnei* ("before"), favoring "see the face" over "appear before." See [[Pilgrimage Commandment Was to See Gods Face]].
- **רֵיקָם (reqam):** "empty(-handed)." Ties the pilgrimage to mandatory offerings. See [[Seeing Gods Face as Costly Signaling and Offering]].

## Context

The clearest statement of the thrice-yearly pilgrimage commandment, requiring all males to come to the central sanctuary at the three festivals (Unleavened Bread, Weeks, Tabernacles) bearing offerings. [[Dan McClellan]] argues the original sense was a command to **see the face of God** at the temple — a beatific vision focused on the divine image — later reinterpreted as merely "appear before." The consonants here are ambiguous (active or passive), which is why the decisive grammatical case is [[Isaiah 1.12|Isaiah 1:12]].
