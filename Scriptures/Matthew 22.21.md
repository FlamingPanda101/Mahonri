# Matthew 22:21

> They say unto him, Caesar's. Then saith he unto them, Render therefore unto Caesar the things which are Caesar's; and unto God the things that are God's. *(KJV)*

## Key phrase
"**Render unto Caesar the things which are Caesar's; and unto God the things that are God's**" — Jesus distinguishes two rival claims (the political order and God), the seed of the Western distinction between temporal and spiritual authority.

## Apologetic application
[[Tom Holland]] traces the Western **concept of the secular** to this saying (developed by [[Augustine]] into the doctrine of the two cities / the *saeculum*). On his account it is precisely what generates "church and state" — and what Islam, lacking a native category of the secular, does not have, making Islam "uniquely indigestible" to a secular liberal mindset. See [[Christianity Has a Concept of the Secular Islam Does Not]].
