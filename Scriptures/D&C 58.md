# D&C 58 — Anxiously Engaged in a Good Cause

> **6** For this cause I sent you—that you might be obedient… **7** and also that you might be honored in laying the foundation… of Zion… **8** that a feast of fat things might be prepared for the poor… **26** For behold, it is not meet that I should command in all things… **27** Verily I say, men should be anxiously engaged in a good cause, and do many things of their own free will, and bring to pass much righteousness; **28** For the power is in them, wherein they are agents unto themselves. (D&C 58)

[[Sharon Eubank]] reads verses 6–11 aloud in the [[Let's Get Real with Stephen Jones - Sharon Eubank on Doing Small Things with Great Love (E0040)|Let's Get Real interview]] as her picture of what Zion looks like: "a feast of fat things… for the poor," every person "seeking the interest of his neighbor." She pairs it with the surrounding "anxiously engaged… the power is in them" language to ground her thesis that ordinary disciples are "powerful where they live" and that big acts are "just a lot of small acts strung together."[^1] See [[Small Acts Are the Only Thing We Have]] and [[You Are Powerful Where You Live]].
