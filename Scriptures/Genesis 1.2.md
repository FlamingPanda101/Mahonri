# Genesis 1:2

> "And the earth was without form, and void; and darkness was upon the face of the deep. And the Spirit of God moved upon the face of the waters." (KJV)

**Hebrew:** וְהָאָרֶץ הָיְתָה תֹהוּ וָבֹהוּ וְחֹשֶׁךְ עַל־פְּנֵי תְהוֹם וְרוּחַ אֱלֹהִים מְרַחֶפֶת עַל־פְּנֵי הַמָּיִם (*w'ha'aretz haytah tohu wa-bohu w'choshek al-p'ne tehom w'ruach Elohim m'rachefet al-p'ne hammayim*)

**Key terms:**
- תֹהוּ וָבֹהוּ (*tohu wa-bohu*) — "formless and void / wild and waste"; an assonant hendiadys for unordered, uninhabitable chaos. *Tohu* may be etymologically linked to *tehom*.
- תְהוֹם (*tehom*) — "the deep / abyss"; a feminine noun (despite lacking the feminine ending) cognate with the Babylonian chaos-sea *Tiamat* of *Enuma Elish*.
- רוּחַ אֱלֹהִים (*ruach Elohim*) — "wind/spirit of God"; *merachefet* ("hovering / sweeping") is a participle denoting continuous action.

## Context

On the dependent-clause reading of [[Genesis 1.1|Genesis 1:1]], verse 2 is the heart of the case for *creatio ex materia*: it describes the **state of things before God's first creative act**. The *tohu wa-bohu*, the darkness, the *tehom*, and the primordial waters are simply *there* when the narrative opens — none of them is said to be created. Creation then proceeds as the separation and ordering of these pre-existent elements (light from darkness, waters above from waters below, dry land from sea), matching the ancient Near Eastern cosmogonic pattern.

## Interpretations

### Traditional Reading
Verse 2 describes the created-but-not-yet-ordered earth in the gap after the absolute *creatio ex nihilo* of verse 1. God first made everything from nothing (v. 1), then organized it (v. 2 onward).

### Academic Reading (Historical-Critical)
The triad *tohu wa-bohu* / *choshek* (darkness) / *tehom* (the deep) names primordial Chaos that pre-exists creation; the verse is a circumstantial clause attached to v. 1. The Septuagint rendered *tohu wa-bohu* with the Platonic-flavored ἀόρατος καὶ ἀκατασκεύαστος ("unseen and unorganized"), reading the verse through a pre-existent-matter cosmology — see [[The Septuagint Rendered Tohu wa-Bohu With Plato's Term for the Unseen]].

## Key Scholars on This Verse

- [[Umberto Cassuto]] — reads *tohu wa-bohu* as the unformed raw material (water above, solid matter beneath) the Creator prepares like a potter's clay
- [[Gary Rendsburg]] — argues the omission of "and it was good" on day two flags that the preexistent water of *tehom* is never created in Genesis 1
- [[Marvin Sweeney]] — following Rashi, reads v. 1–2 as describing an earth that "pre-existed creation in a state of chaos"
