# Deuteronomy 24:7

> If a man be found stealing any of his brethren of the children of Israel, and maketh merchandise of him, or selleth him; then that thief shall die; and thou shalt put away evil from among you. *(KJV)*

## Significance
[[Dan McClellan]] cites this as the **parallel** to [[Exodus 21.16|Exodus 21:16]], noting that here the victim is explicitly identified as "his brethren of the children of Israel." This confirms that the biblical death penalty for man-stealing protects **freeborn Israelites**, not foreigners, and does nothing to prohibit or mitigate the slave trade in general. Joshua HS cited "Deuteronomy 21:16" (a misattribution of Exodus 21:16) to argue the law required slave-buyers to verify slaves were not kidnapped; McClellan responds that the law is widely agreed to be a prohibition on kidnapping freeborn Israelite men only.
