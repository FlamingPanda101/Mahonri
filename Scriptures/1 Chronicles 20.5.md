# 1 Chronicles 20:5

> "And there was again war with the Philistines; and Elhanan the son of Jair **slew Lahmi the brother of Goliath** the Gittite, the staff of whose spear was like a weaver's beam." (cf. RSV)

The Chronicler's parallel to [[2 Samuel 21.19|2 Samuel 21:19]]. Where 2 Samuel has [[Elhanan]] kill **Goliath** himself, Chronicles has him kill **Lahmi the brother of Goliath** — removing the conflict with the David-and-Goliath story of [[1 Samuel]] 17.

The change is effected by the slightest of consonantal alterations, which only resemble one another in the **Aramaic square script** adopted during the Babylonian exile (not the older paleo-Hebrew): *bêt* ("the Bethlehemite") is reread as the object marker *ʾet* plus a new proper name *Laḥmi*, and the accusative particle is reshaped into *ʾaḥî* ("brother of"). Because the name "Lahmi" (which would mean roughly "bread") fits no known Philistine naming convention and isn't even a plausible Philistine name, the verse is judged a **textual corruption / deliberate harmonization** rather than independent data.
