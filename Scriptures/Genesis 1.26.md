# Genesis 1:26

> And God said, Let us make man in our image, after our likeness: and let them have dominion... *(KJV)*

## Key Term: plural "us / our" with singular subject (Elohim)
A standard Trinitarian proof-text: God speaks of himself in the plural. The panel turns it around — *Avery's* argument from Genesis's plural verbs / singular nouns just shows **multiple persons spoken of as a single entity**, i.e. the Godhead, not one being. Chandler links it to the **divine council** motif (cf. [[Psalm 82.1|Psalm 82:1]], John Walton's *The Lost World of Genesis One*): a plurality acting in unity. "Let *us* make man in *our* image" reads naturally as more than one person, not as one being talking to itself.
