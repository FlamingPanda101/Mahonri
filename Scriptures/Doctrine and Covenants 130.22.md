# Doctrine and Covenants 130.22

"The Father has a body of flesh and bones as tangible as man's; the Son also; but the Holy Ghost has not a body of flesh and bones, but is a personage of Spirit. Were it not so, the Holy Ghost could not dwell in us."

## Why it's cited

This verse is the clearest LDS scriptural statement that God the Father is a corporeal, embodied being, directly opposing the classical creedal description of God as "without body, parts, or passions." Apologetics notes use it to argue that the Restoration recovered an anthropomorphic understanding of Deity closer to the Hebrew Bible, and that the immaterial-God formula of later Christendom is a post-apostolic innovation rather than apostolic doctrine. It also grounds the LDS distinction among the three members of the Godhead — Father and Son embodied, the Holy Ghost a personage of Spirit.
