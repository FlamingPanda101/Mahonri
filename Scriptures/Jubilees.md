# Jubilees

The **Book of Jubilees** is a Second-Temple Jewish apocryphal/pseudepigraphal work (also called *Little Genesis*) retelling Genesis and early Exodus in a 49-year "jubilee" chronology. It is canonical only in the [[Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church]]; it is not in the Latter-day Saint, Catholic, or Protestant canons. Copies were recovered among the Dead Sea Scrolls at Qumran.

## Key Detail (apologetic use)
[[Jonah Barnes]] takes the name **"the Great Forsaken"** from Jubilees 1:14, where God foresees a future generation that "have forsaken my ordinances and my commandments and the festivals of my covenant, my sabbaths and my holy place" — the Hebrew *shakach* ("to forsake"). Barnes reads Jubilees (and other apocrypha) as a self-aware counter-narrative claiming the scribes corrupted the truth. See [[The Great Forsaken - A Pre-Christian Apostasy of the Scribes]].

> Critical caution: Jubilees is itself a late, sectarian composition that in places post-dates the very "forsaking" it describes, so using it as a memory of a pre-exilic redaction is contested (noted in the steelman of [[The Great Forsaken - A Pre-Christian Apostasy of the Scribes]]).
