# John 14:28

> "Ye have heard how I said unto you, I go away, and come again unto you. If ye loved me, ye would rejoice, because I said, I go unto the Father: for my Father is greater than I." (KJV)

Jesus states **"my Father is greater than I"** (Greek **ὁ πατὴρ μείζων μού ἐστιν**, *ho patēr meizōn mou estin*). Used to argue that if Father and Son were the *same being*, one could not be "greater" than the other; the verse fits the Godhead model of distinct persons in a hierarchy of glory.
