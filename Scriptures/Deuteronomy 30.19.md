# Deuteronomy 30:19

> "I call heaven and earth to record this day against you, that I have set before you life and death, blessing and cursing: therefore choose life, that both thou and thy seed may live." (KJV)

A famous covenant-ratification formula: God gives the law, then sets out blessings for obedience and curses for disobedience. In critical scholarship two features echo the **Vassal Treaty of [[Esarhaddon]]**: (1) the **summons of heaven and earth as witnesses**, paralleling the treaty's invocation of "all the gods of heaven and earth," and (2) the **blessing-and-curse sanction structure** characteristic of the Assyrian vassal treaty (*adê*). The verse is cited as evidence that Deuteronomy's covenant was patterned after that imperial treaty form.
