# John 2:19-21

> Jesus answered and said unto them, Destroy this temple, and in three days I will raise it up. Then said the Jews, Forty and six years was this temple in building, and wilt thou rear it up in three days? But he spake of the temple of his body. *(KJV)*

## Key phrase
"**He spake of the temple of his body**" — used to argue **Christ himself is the temple**, so access to God is no longer mediated by a physical, institutionally controlled sacred space.

## Apologetic application
[[Gavin (Catholic Debater)]] cites it (with [[Hebrews 10.19-22|Heb 10:19-22]] and [[1 Timothy 2.5|1 Tim 2:5]]) to argue access to God is "given downward, not earned upward," contrasting the triune God with worthiness-gated temple systems. See [[Omnibenevolence vs Conditional Access Models]].
