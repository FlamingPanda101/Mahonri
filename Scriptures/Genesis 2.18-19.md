# Genesis 2:18-19

> "And the LORD God said, It is not good that the man should be alone; I will make him an help meet for him. And out of the ground the LORD God formed every beast of the field, and every fowl of the air; and brought them unto Adam to see what he would call them: and whatsoever Adam called every living creature, that was the name thereof." (KJV)

**Hebrew (key terms):** עֵזֶר כְּנֶגְדּוֹ (*ezer kenegdo*, "a helper as his partner / corresponding to him"); וַיִּצֶר (*wayyitser*, "formed," same potter-verb used of the human in [[Genesis 2.4-7|Gen 2:7]]) ... חַיַּת הַשָּׂדֶה (*chayyat ha-sadeh*, "animal of the field") and עוֹף הַשָּׁמַיִם (*oph ha-shamayim*, "bird of the air").

## Context

In the second creation account the animals are formed *after* the human, as a (failed) search for a fitting partner. McClellan emphasizes that "animal of the field" here does **not** mean the fenced-in domestic field but the animals of the earth generally, and "birds of the air" means all birds — all created after humanity.[^1]

This is the inverse of [[Genesis 1.24-26|Genesis 1]], where land animals are made on day six *before* humanity and birds and fish on still earlier days — a direct contradiction of sequence.[^2]
