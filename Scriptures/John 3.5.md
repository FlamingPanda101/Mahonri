# John 3:5

> "Jesus answered, Verily, verily, I say unto thee, Except a man be born of water and of the Spirit, he cannot enter into the kingdom of God."

## Use in the sources

[[Hayden Carroll]], in the cookies-to-Driscoll video, cites this verse to ground the claim that **baptism by proper authority is a real condition Christ set**, not an optional add-on. In his progression framework, even a sincere follower of [[Mark Driscoll]] "moving in the right direction" eventually cannot "progress any further unless you embrace the fullness of the gospel" — and the concrete checkpoint is "born in water and of the spirit," which he and his interlocutor identify as baptism.[^1] This functions as the soft boundary in his [[LDS Progression Model vs Binary Heaven and Hell|progression-not-binary-hell]] model: rejection only counts when one *knowingly* refuses a witnessed truth, so the unbaptized are stopped only by "willing rejection of the truth."[^2]

It connects to his worship argument — see [[Same Reference Different Beliefs - You Worship the Same Jesus]] — by relocating the real stakes from *correct metaphysics of Christ* to *covenant practice* Christ commanded.
