# 2 Nephi 9:39

> O, my beloved brethren, remember the awfulness in transgressing against that
> Holy God, and also the awfulness of yielding to the enticings of that cunning
> one. Remember, to be carnally-minded is death, and to be spiritually-minded is
> life eternal.

## Context

2 Nephi 9 contains Jacob's great sermon on the Atonement. Jacob explains that
without the resurrection and redemption through Christ's atonement, all would
become subject to the devil and "our spirits must have become like unto him"
(v. 9). The contrast between "carnally-minded" and "spiritually-minded" in
v. 39 is a culmination of the sermon's theme: the carnal perspective sees only
this world and its temporal concerns; the spiritual perspective sees eternal
realities.

## Key Exegetical Points

- **Carnally-minded (σαρκικὸς φρονῶν):** The person whose frame of reference is
  limited to the physical/temporal realm — "thinking within the four sides of
  the game board" as [[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)|David Butler]] puts it in
  his Monopoly analogy.[^1] This is not merely sinful desire but a restricted
  horizon that mistakes the temporal for the ultimate.
- **Spiritually-minded:** The person who sees temporal experiences as means to
  eternal ends — covenant relationships, eternal joy, returning to God's
  presence. This maps to the meta-purpose in Butler's Monopoly analogy: playing
  for fellowship and joy, not just winning.

## Cross-Tradition Readings

- **LDS:** Read within the framework of the plan of salvation, where life is a
  probationary state oriented toward eternal progression. The carnal mind
  represents fixation on mortality's game; the spiritual mind sees the eternal
  stakes.
- **Traditional Christian:** Also read as a contrast between flesh and spirit
  (cf. Romans 8:6-7), but without the LDS framework of eternal progression and
  pre-mortal existence.
