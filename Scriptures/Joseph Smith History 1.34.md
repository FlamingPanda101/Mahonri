# Joseph Smith History 1:34 — the messenger names the buried record

> He called me by name, and said unto me that he was a messenger sent from the presence of God to me, and that his name was **Moroni**; that God had a work for me to do... *(Joseph Smith—History 1:33, Pearl of Great Price)*

## Key Detail
When this history was first printed under Joseph Smith's editorship in the *Times and Seasons*, the messenger was named **Nephi**, not Moroni; church historians corrected it decades later. [[Jonathan Neville]] uses this as evidence that Joseph did not closely edit the *Times and Seasons* — supporting his argument that the 1842 Mesoamerican articles in the same paper were authored by [[Benjamin Winchester]], not by Joseph as nominal editor. (Neville also notes the Cumorah hill is *not* named in the canonized Joseph Smith—History.)
