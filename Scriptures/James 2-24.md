# James 2:14-26

> "What doth it profit, my brethren, though a man say he hath faith, and have
> not works? can faith save him? ... Ye see then how that by works a man is
> justified, and not by faith only. ... For as the body without the spirit is
> dead, so faith without works is dead also."

## Apologetic Significance
The ONLY place in scripture where the phrase "faith alone" appears — and it's
explicitly negated. The lynchpin of the Catholic case against *sola fide*.

**Key contextual observations**:
- James never treats "faith" as bad — the problem is faith being alone
- The body/spirit analogy (2:26): the spirit is DISTINCT from the body and
  gives it life — works are DISTINCT from faith and give it life
- The Greek doesn't distinguish "kinds" of faith — "Can faith save him?" not
  "Can THAT faith save him?" (contra NIV/ESV)
