# Genesis 32:24-28 — Jacob wrestles a man at Peniel

> And Jacob was left alone; and there wrestled a man with him until the breaking of the day. ... And he said, Thy name shall be called no more Jacob, but Israel: for as a prince hast thou power with God and with men, and hast prevailed. *(Genesis 32:24, 28, KJV)*

## Key Terms
- **wrestled** — Hebrew *wayyeʾaveq* (וַיֵּאָבֵק), the night-long struggle; cf. the place name *Peniel* ("face of God," [[Genesis 32.30|Genesis 32:30]]).
- **a man** — Hebrew *ish* (אִישׁ), a divine being described as a "man."
- **with God and with men** — *ʾelohim* and *ʾanashim* (plural of *ʾenosh*, "man").

## Significance
This is the source story **Enos** reworks in [[Enos 1.1-2|Enos 1:1-2]]. Enos — whose name *is* the Hebrew word for "man" (*ʾenosh*) and whose father is named **Jacob** — recounts "the wrestle which I had before God," deliberately patterning his autobiography on his namesake father's wrestle with the "man"/God at Peniel.
