# Proverbs

The Book of Proverbs is a collection of wisdom sayings and instructions, traditionally attributed to Solomon. [[Jacob Wright]] argues that Proverbs represents a democratization of wisdom: in Egyptian parallels, the "father" is a court tutor training a "son" for elite service, but in Proverbs the "father" and "mother" become biological parents, moving wisdom from the court to the family.

## Wright's Thesis
Proverbs is key evidence for Wright's [[Bible as Pedagogical Curriculum]] thesis — the Bible as an educational curriculum for forming a people without a kingdom, where wisdom replaces military might as the key to enduring identity.
