# Moses 7

Part of the Book of Moses (Joseph Smith Translation material, 1830) in the Pearl of Great Price. Moses 7 records **Enoch's cosmic vision** and is, together with [[Moses 6]], by a wide margin the most extensive Enoch material in any Christian or Jewish scripture outside [[1 Enoch]] itself.

## Contents

- **Enoch's vision and ministry** — continuing from [[Moses 6]] (Enoch's call and preaching).
- **A pre-existent Son of Man figure** — the vision references the Son of Man, resonant with the Enochic Son of Man of the Parables ([[1 Enoch]] 46, 48).
- **The City of Zion translated** — Enoch establishes Zion, a city of one heart and one mind, which is taken up bodily into heaven (Moses 7:18–21, 69).
- **The Weeping God** — in one of the most theologically striking passages in LDS scripture, Enoch sees God weep and asks, "How is it that the heavens weep?" God's answer describes grief over the wickedness and suffering of his children (Moses 7:28–37).

> (Moses 7:28) And it came to pass that the God of heaven looked upon the residue of the people, and he wept; and Enoch bore record of it, saying: How is it that the heavens weep, and shed forth their tears as the rain upon the mountains?

## Significance

- **The grieving, emotionally present God** of Moses 7 is sharply at odds with the impassible God of classical Greek-influenced theology (the God who by definition cannot suffer), yet consistent with the ancient Enochic tradition and with the LDS doctrine of a personally invested Heavenly Father. See [[Divine Impassibility Is Classical-Theist Heresy]].
- Moroni's Standard argues that the Son-of-Man, Zion, and weeping-God content of Moses 6–7 **maps onto ancient Enochic material that was not available to Joseph Smith as a structured theological system in 1830** — the first English translation of 1 Enoch (Richard Laurence, 1821) being obscure, and the scholarship needed to synthesize these parallels not developed until the 20th century. See [[Moses 6-7 Restores Enochic Content Unavailable to Joseph Smith in 1830]].
