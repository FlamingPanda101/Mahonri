# Exodus 21:16

> And he that stealeth a man, and selleth him, or if he be found in his hand, he shall surely be put to death. *(KJV)*

## Key Hebrew term
- **אִישׁ (ʾish)** — "man," and in the Covenant Code (Exodus 21) it specifically denotes a **native Israelite man**. The Covenant Code is lexically precise: *ish* (Israelite man), *isha* (woman), *ben/bat* (child), *ger* (foreigner), *eved* (enslaved person). Punishments vary by the social status of perpetrator and victim.
- The clause "whether he has sold him **or** he is found in his hand" modifies the **kidnapper** — i.e., whether the kidnapper still holds the victim or has already sold him, the kidnapper is executed. It does **not** make a third-party purchaser liable.

## Significance
[[Dan McClellan]] argues this verse prohibits the **kidnapping of freeborn Israelite men**, not the slave trade in general. It is the parallel of [[Deuteronomy 24.7|Deuteronomy 24:7]], which explicitly names an Israelite victim. Just as kidnapping a free white man was illegal in the antebellum American South without undermining chattel slavery, this law did nothing to mitigate or prohibit Israelite slaveholding. Joshua HS misattributed it to "Deuteronomy 21:16" and misread it as making any possessor of a kidnapped person liable.
