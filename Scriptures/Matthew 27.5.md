# Matthew 27:5 — The Death of Judas

> And he cast down the pieces of silver in the temple, and departed, and went and hanged himself. *(KJV)*

Greek: **ἀπήγξατο** (*apēnxato*, "hanged himself"). Contrast Acts 1:18, where Judas **buys a field** with the reward of iniquity and, "falling headlong, he burst asunder in the midst, and all his bowels gushed out" (*πρηνὴς γενόμενος ἐλάκησεν μέσος*). The two accounts also disagree on who purchased the field and on the origin of the name "Field of Blood" (Aceldama). This is one of [[Dan McClellan]]'s textbook examples that harmonizers can only call "not impossible" to reconcile.
