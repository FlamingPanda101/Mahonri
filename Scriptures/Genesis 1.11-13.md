# Genesis 1:11-13

> "And God said, Let the earth bring forth grass, the herb yielding seed, and the fruit tree yielding fruit after his kind, whose seed is in itself, upon the earth: and it was so. And the earth brought forth grass, and herb yielding seed after his kind, and the tree yielding fruit, whose seed was in itself, after his kind: and God saw that it was good. And the evening and the morning were the third day." (KJV)

**Hebrew (key terms):** דֶּשֶׁא (*deshe*, "vegetation/grass"), עֵשֶׂב מַזְרִיעַ זֶרַע (*eseb mazria zera*, "herb yielding seed"), עֵץ פְּרִי (*ets peri*, "fruit tree").

## Context

In the Priestly account, *all* vegetation — grass, seed-bearing plants, and fruit trees of every kind — is created on **day three**, three days before humanity (day six). McClellan uses this to refute the "zoomed-in sixth day" harmonization: [[Genesis 2.4-7|Genesis 2:5–7]] explicitly has the human created *before* any plant life, the reverse of this order.[^1] He also notes (against the harmonization) that in the second account "no trees seem to have existed until God planted the garden in or next to Eden."[^2]
