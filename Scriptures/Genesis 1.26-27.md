# Genesis 1:26-27

> And God said, Let us make man in our image, after our likeness: and let them have dominion over the fish of the sea, and over the fowl of the air, and over the cattle, and over all the earth, and over every creeping thing that creepeth upon the earth. So God created man in his own image, in the image of God created he him; male and female created he them. *(KJV)*

## Key Hebrew terms
- **צֶלֶם (tselem)** — "image"; **דְּמוּת (demut)** — "likeness." The *imago Dei*.
- **רָדָה (radah)** — "have dominion / rule." Scrivener stresses that humans are made to *rule*, not to be slaves — a verse he ties to abolition via [[Olaudah Equiano]] ("if God did not make man slave, how dare man make man slave").

## Significance
[[Glenn Scrivener]] (following [[Tom Holland]] and [[John Locke]]) makes Genesis 1 the ultimate seedbed of the rights revolution: human worth and dignity flow from being made in God's image, and human vocation from being made to "have dominion" rather than to be enslaved. Holland is quoted: "the ultimate seedbed of the American Revolution was ... the book of Genesis." [[Alex OConnor|Alex O'Connor]] presses that if rights were truly self-evident from this, they would be far more widespread, and that the value of protecting the weak appears in pre-biblical law (Hammurabi). See [[Genesis 1 Image of God as Seedbed of Rights]] and [[Western Values Predate or Arise Independently of Christianity]].
