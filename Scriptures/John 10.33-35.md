# John 10:34-35

> Jesus answered them, Is it not written in your law, I said, Ye are gods? If he called them gods, unto whom the word of God came, and the scripture cannot be broken; *(KJV)*

## Key Greek terms
**ὁ λόγος τοῦ θεοῦ (ho logos tou theou)** — "the word of God"; **ἡ γραφή (hē graphē)** — "the scripture." Jesus quotes Psalm 82:6 ("ye are gods"), introduces it as "written in your law," and says "the scripture cannot be broken" (οὐ δύναται λυθῆναι).

## In context
A key text in [[Anthony Rogers]]' rebuttal: he argues Jesus here calls *written Scripture* "the word of God" explicitly, refuting [[Trent Horn]]'s claim that the phrase never denotes a written document. Horn replies that "the word of God came to them" points back to Psalm 82's setting — God speaking *orally* to the judges or the divine council — so the verse records Scripture *reporting* the spoken word of God, not calling the scroll itself the word of God. Rogers pairs this with [[Matthew 15.6]] (Jesus calling the written "honor your father" command the word of God).
