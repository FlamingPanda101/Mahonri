# Mosiah 18:13 — Alma baptizes "having authority from the Almighty God"

> And when he had said these words, the Spirit of the Lord was upon him, and he said: Helam, I baptize thee, having authority from the Almighty God, as a testimony that ye have entered into a covenant to serve him until you are dead as to the mortal body; and may the Spirit of the Lord be poured out upon you; and may he grant unto you eternal life, through the redemption of Christ, whom he has prepared from the foundation of the world. *(Mosiah 18:13, Book of Mormon)*

## Key Term
- **"the Almighty God"** — back-translates to Hebrew **El Shaddai**. [[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)|David Butler]] reads Alma's baptismal-authority formula at the Waters of Mormon as invoking **Shaddai, the "breasted God"** (the divine Mother) — the scene being marked by the *beasts* (the *hayot* of Shaddai), a fountain of *pure water*, and a *thicket of trees* (the tree-goddess/Virgin). On this reading Alma — whose name puns on *ʿalmah*, "Virgin" — baptizes by the authority of "the father and the mother" together.
