# Luke 22:42

> Saying, Father, if thou be willing, remove this cup from me: nevertheless not my will, but thine, be done. *(KJV)*

## Key point
In Gethsemane the Son distinguishes **his will** from the Father's and submits one to the other. A panel super-chat flags this as a favorite anti-numerical-identity text: a single being cannot submit its will to itself. It illustrates the LDS reading that the persons each have a will yet act in perfect harmony — unity of will, not one will of one being. See [[Subordination Within the New Testament]], [[Oneness Means Unity Not Numerical Identity of Being]].
