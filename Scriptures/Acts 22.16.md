# Acts 22:16

> And now why tarriest thou? arise, and be baptized, and wash away thy sins, calling on the name of the Lord. *(KJV)*

Ananias's words to Saul/Paul, recounted in Paul's own conversion testimony.

## In the baptism debate
[[Joe Heschmeyer]] presses this against the faith-alone view: by Acts 22, Paul has already encountered the risen Christ, believed, and called him "Lord" — yet Ananias still tells him his sins must be "washed away" through baptism, so faith alone had not yet removed them; "something more" remains. [[Ryan (NeedGod)|Ryan]] replies that the washing is tied grammatically to "calling on the name of the Lord" (i.e. faith), not to the water, and that elsewhere (Acts 26) Paul says forgiveness comes "through faith." Like [[Acts 2.38]], the verse turns on whether the action of baptism is instrumental to the remission of sins. See [[Acts 22.16 Has Pauls Sins Washed Away at Baptism After He Already Believed]].
