# Numbers 24.17

> "I shall see him, but not now: I shall behold him, but not nigh: there shall come a Star out of Jacob, and a Sceptre shall rise out of Israel, and shall smite the corners of Moab, and destroy all the children of Sheth." (KJV)

## In context

Part of the fourth oracle of Balaam. In the [[The Stick of Joseph - The Secret Identity of the Wisemen|Stick of Joseph wisemen episode]], [[Andrea Woodmansee]] presents this as the keystone prooftext behind the magi: the "Star out of Jacob" is the sign of the coming Son of Man that the [[The Wise Men Were Bearers of Preserved First-Temple Covenant|temple-covenant magi]] were watching for.[^1] She notes that some Aramaic **Targums** render "a Star out of Jacob" as "a *Messiah* out of Jacob," explicitly equating the star with the Messiah; the verse's parallelism ("Out of Jacob shall come he that shall have dominion," v.19) reinforces the link. The star-prophecy circulated widely in the period — at **Qumran**, and in **Josephus** and **Justin Martyr** — so the magi need not have invented their expectation.[^2]

This connects the Bethlehem star to the wider messianic-star tradition; cf. [[Revelation 22.16|Revelation 22:16]] and Revelation 2:28, where Jesus is "the bright and morning star."
