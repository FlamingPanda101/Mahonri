# Matthew 19:3-9

> ... Moses because of the hardness of your hearts suffered you to put away your wives: but from the beginning it was not so. And I say unto you, Whosoever shall put away his wife, except it be for fornication, and shall marry another, committeth adultery: and whoso marrieth her which is put away doth commit adultery. *(KJV, abridged)*

## Significance
[[Glenn Scrivener]] uses Jesus' divorce teaching as the template for how God "regulates things that he hates": there was a creation pattern (Gen 1-2), humanity fell into "hardness of heart," God *regulated* the non-ideal situation (Deuteronomic divorce law), and Christ came to redeem it. He applies the same logic to [[Exodus 21.20-21|slavery laws]] — regulation of an evil, not endorsement. [[Alex OConnor|Alex O'Connor]] notes the divorce rule is itself "renegotiated" across the Gospels (Mark allows no exception; Matthew adds the adultery exception), evidence of "constant renegotiation in the Bible." [[Joseph Henrich]] pairs Matthew 19 with [[1 Corinthians 7.3-5|1 Cor 7]] as the "Christian sexual revolution." See [[The Exodus Is Israelite-Specific Not Universal Abolition]].
