# 1 Corinthians 15:6

> After that, he was seen of above five hundred brethren at once; of whom the greater part remain unto this present, but some are fallen asleep. *(KJV)*

## Key Detail

Paul appeals to ~500 living witnesses of the risen Christ. Apologists (e.g., Frank Turek) cite this as mass corroboration of the resurrection — "if you don't believe me, check with them." Latter-day Saint apologists turn the same standard back: the post-resurrection appearances Paul references occur during the 40-day period largely absent from Acts, and the "500" episode is elaborated in apocryphal texts the apologist does not accept as canon — so the believer is *inferring* witnesses no extant first-hand account names, exactly the kind of inference they refuse the Book of Mormon's 11+ named plate witnesses. See [[Apologists Drop the Resurrection Evidential Standard When Judging the Gold Plates]].
