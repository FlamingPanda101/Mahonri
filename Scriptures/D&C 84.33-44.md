# D&C 84:33-44 — The Oath and Covenant of the Priesthood

> For whoso is faithful unto the obtaining these two priesthoods of which I have spoken, and the magnifying their calling, are sanctified by the Spirit unto the renewing of their bodies… and also all they who receive this priesthood receive me, saith the Lord… And this is according to the oath and covenant which belongeth to the priesthood. Therefore, all those who receive the priesthood, receive this oath and covenant of my Father, which he cannot break, neither can it be moved. But whoso breaketh this covenant after he hath received it, and altogether turneth therefrom, shall not have forgiveness of sins in this world nor in the world to come. *(D&C 84:33-41, abridged)*

## Key Detail
- The canonical "oath and covenant of the priesthood" passage. [[Todd McLauchlin]] argues the chapter is a **temple text** (cf. vv. 2-4, gathering to Zion "the place of the temple") that *teaches about* the oath and covenant rather than *being* it — the oath and covenant being the [[Calling and Election Is the Oath of the Father Sealed in the Endowment|endowment's sealed calling and election]]. He reads "magnifying their calling" (v. 33) as [[Magnifying a Calling Means Coming Clean From the Blood and Sins of This Generation|coming clean from the blood and sins of this generation]], and the forfeiture clause (v. 41) as turning from a *sealed election*, not a missed assignment.
