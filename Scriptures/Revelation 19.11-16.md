# Revelation 19:11-16

> And I saw heaven opened, and behold a white horse; and he that sat upon him was called Faithful and True, and in righteousness he doth judge and make war... And he was clothed with a vesture dipped in blood: and his name is called The Word of God. And the armies which were in heaven followed him upon white horses... And he hath on his vesture and on his thigh a name written, KING OF KINGS, AND LORD OF LORDS. *(KJV)*

## Key phrase
"**Clothed in red and riding a white horse**" — Frederick's summary of the triumphant return of Christ with the heavenly armies, who "cleanses the world of all that pollutes it." This is "the most explicit blueprint for how some early Christians may have envisioned the end of this world."

## Apologetic / historical application
[[Nicholas J. Frederick]] uses Revelation 19 as the dramatic climax of the book of Revelation's eschatological vision, leading directly into the binding of Satan and the millennial reign of [[Revelation 20.2-3|Revelation 20]]. It supplied the imagery for early premillennial expectation of a literal, victorious Parousia.
