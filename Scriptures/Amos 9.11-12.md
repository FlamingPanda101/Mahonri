# Amos 9:11–12 — The Tabernacle of David (MT vs Septuagint)

> In that day will I raise up the tabernacle of David that is fallen... **That they may possess the remnant of Edom**, and of all the heathen, which are called by my name, saith the LORD. *(KJV, following the Masoretic Text)*

The **Septuagint** reads differently — not "possess the remnant of **Edom**" but "**that the remnant of men / the rest of mankind** may seek the Lord." James cites the **Greek** form at the Council of Jerusalem.

## Significance
At [[Acts 15|Acts 15:16-17]] James grounds the admission of Gentiles without circumcision on Amos 9:11-12 — but in the Septuagint version, whose universalist sense diverges from the Hebrew (and from the Qumran readings, which apply it to Israel's takeover of Edom). [[Robert Boland]] argues this shows the apostolic church reasoning from a particular *translation* and from contemporary revelation (Peter's vision), not from "scripture alone." See [[James Read Amos 9 Through the Septuagint at the Jerusalem Council]]. Compare the vault's treatment of LXX expansion at [[The Septuagint of Isaiah 9-6 Is Expansive Not Truncated]].
