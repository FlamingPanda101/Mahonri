# Deuteronomy 28:53

> "And thou shalt eat the fruit of thine own body, the flesh of thy sons and of thy daughters, which the LORD thy God hath given thee, in the siege, and in the straitness, wherewith thine enemies shall distress thee." (KJV)

The climactic **siege curse** of Deuteronomy 28 (the chapter "that famously has the curses in it"). In critical scholarship this cannibalism-under-siege curse is one of the closest points of contact between Deuteronomy and the **Vassal Treaty of [[Esarhaddon]]**, whose §47 likewise curses the oath-breaker: "in your hunger eat the flesh of your sons… may one man eat the flesh of another." The parallel — including the treaty's added threat of a lost afterlife (no one to pour libations to one's ghost) — is part of the case that Deuteronomy's covenant sanctions were patterned after the Assyrian treaty form.
