# John 7:53–8:11 — The Woman Caught in Adultery (Pericope Adulterae)

> And every man went unto his own house. Jesus went unto the mount of Olives... *(KJV, 7:53–8:1)*
>
> ...He that is without sin among you, let him first cast a stone at her... Neither do I condemn thee: go, and sin no more. *(KJV, 8:7, 8:11)*

The *pericope adulterae*: a woman caught in adultery is dragged before Jesus to test whether she should be stoned; he answers, "He that is without sin among you, let him first cast a stone," and the accusers leave.

## Textual Status
This passage **does not appear in any manuscript of John before the fifth century**, even though many earlier manuscripts of John survive. It is therefore widely judged a later interpolation. One early reference attributes the story (or something like it) to a different gospel altogether — the *Gospel of the Hebrews*. The episode's form — Jesus put on the spot and escaping with a brilliant one-liner — also reads more like a **Synoptic** controversy story than typical Johannine narrative.

## Significance
[[Hugo Mendez]] identifies this as one of two clear additions to John (the other being chapter 21), distinguishing the issue of *additions* from his thesis of substantial single authorship of the rest of the Gospel.
