# Romans 7:7-9

> What shall we say then? Is the law sin? God forbid. Nay, I had not known sin, but by the law... For without the law sin was dead. For I was alive without the law once: but when the commandment came, sin revived, and I died. *(KJV)*

## Key phrase
"**I was alive without the law once: but when the commandment came, sin revived, and I died.**" Paul speaks in the first person singular ("I") about life before, and failure after, taking on the demands of the law.

## Apologetic / historical application
[[Brian Thomassen]] argues the "I" here is a **fictive "I"** (*prosōpopoeia*), a Greek rhetorical device in which the writer assumes a character to drive a point — here a [[Paul]] persona of a Roman adopting the Mosaic law and finding himself failing. Read this way, the passage is not Paul's autobiography but a staged demonstration that an *alien*, cross-civilizational adoption of the law "lacks force" and sets one up to fail — preparing the conclusion that real self-mastery comes only through Christ. See [[Paul's I in Romans 7 Is a Fictive Persona Not Autobiography]] and [[Romans Argues Real Self-Mastery Comes Through Christ Not the Law]].
