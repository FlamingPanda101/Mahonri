# 2 Nephi 9:41

> ...the keeper of the gate is the Holy One of Israel; and he employeth no servant there; and there is none other way save it be by the gate; for he cannot be deceived, for the Lord God is his name. *(KJV/BoM)*

## Key phrase
"**The keeper of the gate is the Holy One of Israel... he employeth no servant there.**" Jacob's image of meeting the Lord at the gate of salvation.

## Apologetic / historical application
[[Dave Butler]] reads this in the "I Never Knew You" episode as a **recognition scene** continuous with the shut wedding door of [[Matthew 25.1-13|the ten virgins]]. Because "the Lord God is his name" sits in a passage saturated with Eden imagery, Butler argues it means the gatekeeper is *the same being* (the Lord God) who oversaw Eden and expelled Adam at the fall — so passing the gate is "not a matter of going to strangers to have a test applied to you... it is going back to the gate to be recognized as returning family."
