# Isaiah 30:9-10

> That this is a rebellious people, lying children, children that will not hear the law of the Lord: which say to the seers, See not; and to the prophets, Prophesy not unto us right things, speak unto us smooth things, prophesy deceits. *(Isaiah 30:9-10, KJV)*

Isaiah laments a people who tell the seers "see not" and demand "smooth things."

## Why it's cited
The Ward Radio Isaiah episode treats this as a "breadcrumb" left in the surviving canon: Isaiah complaining of censorship — that his hard, messianic prophecies were unwelcome and suppressed. It is offered as internal corroboration for the "Great Forsaken" thesis (a pre-exilic scribal apostasy editing out the suffering Messiah), alongside parallel complaints in Micah and Amos.
