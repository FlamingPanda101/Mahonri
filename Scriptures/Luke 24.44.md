# Luke 24:44

> And he said unto them, These are the words which I spake unto you, while I was yet with you, that all things must be fulfilled, which were written in the law of Moses, and in the prophets, and in the psalms, concerning me. *(KJV)*

## Significance
The risen Jesus claims that **the entire Hebrew scriptural tradition** — "the law of Moses, the prophets, and the psalms" (a way of naming the whole Tanakh) — pointed to him, not "a handful of ambiguous passages." [[Alex Arnold]] makes this the hinge of his argument: if the scriptures clearly testified of Jesus and the most scripturally educated people in the world still missed him, then either they were uniquely blind or "the text no longer said what it originally said" — pointing to the editorial removal of the divine-council / pre-existent-Messiah tradition. See [[Jewish Non-Recognition of Jesus Is a Textual Problem Not Only a Spiritual One]]. (Earlier in the same chapter, on the Emmaus road, Jesus "beginning at Moses and all the prophets... expounded... the things concerning himself," Luke 24:27.)
