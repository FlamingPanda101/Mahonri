# Mormon 6:6

> And it came to pass that when we had gathered in all our people in one to the land of Cumorah, behold I, Mormon, began to be old; and knowing it to be the last struggle of my people, and having been commanded of the Lord that I should not suffer the records which had been handed down by our fathers, which were sacred, to fall into the hands of the Lamanites... I made this record out of the plates of Nephi, and hid up in the hill Cumorah all the records which had been entrusted to me by the hand of the Lord, save it were these few plates which I gave unto my son Moroni. *(Book of Mormon)*

## Significance
The verse naming the **hill Cumorah** as the place where Mormon hid the records before the final Nephite battle. It is central to Book of Mormon geography debates because it distinguishes *two* deposits: Mormon hid the bulk of the records in Cumorah but gave "these few plates" (the abridgment Joseph Smith later received) to his son [[Moroni]] — implying the plates Joseph recovered in New York were **not** the cache buried at the battle site. [[Keystone]] argues that early Latter-day Saints did not call the New York hill "Cumorah" until the mid-1830s, likely on a misreading of this verse, and that "Book of Mormon geography doesn't match up with the New York location." See [[The New York Hill Is Not the Book of Mormon Cumorah]].
