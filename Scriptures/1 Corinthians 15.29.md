# 1 Corinthians 15:29

> Else what shall they do which are baptized for the dead, if the dead rise not at all? why are they then baptized for the dead? *(KJV)*

## Significance
The single New Testament reference to **baptism for the dead**. In *Ancient Christians*, Catherine Gines Taylor argues that Paul "does not object to their practice, and the passage points to an actual rite for the benefit of a dead person," citing Jeffrey Trumbower. She holds the practice was limited and "may have been particularly reserved for those who had been preparing for baptism but who had died before those rites were completed." Most prolific patristic discussion of vicarious baptism comes from Egypt or Greek/Egyptian-influenced theologians, and the [[Marcion|Marcionites]] are the group [[John Chrysostom]] explicitly reports performing it. A central LDS proof-text for temple baptism for the dead.
