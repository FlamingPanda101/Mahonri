# Micah 6:8

> He hath shewed thee, O man, what is good; and what doth the LORD require of thee, but to do justly, and to love mercy, and to walk humbly with thy God? *(KJV)*

## Significance
[[Glenn Scrivener]] cites this to argue that the deist founder [[Thomas Paine]] — when he summarized his philosophy as "to act justly, love mercy, and work for human flourishing" — was unconsciously echoing Micah 6 and then "adding the humanistic gloss" (replacing "walk humbly with thy God" with "human flourishing"). Scrivener offers this as an example of "profoundly Christian ideas" that have been secularized — the "air we breathe." See [[Secularized Founders Used Christian Language]].
