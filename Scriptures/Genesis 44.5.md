# Genesis 44:5

> Is not this it in which my lord drinketh, and whereby indeed he divineth? ye
> have done evil in so doing.

## Key Term

- **נָחֵשׁ יְנַחֵשׁ (naḥesh yenaḥesh)** — "indeed divineth," from נָחַשׁ (to
  practice divination). The verse explicitly states that Joseph of Egypt's
  silver cup was used for divination.

## Joseph of Egypt's Divining Cup

The source cites "Joseph of Egypt's divining cup" as a biblical precedent for a
righteous figure using a physical instrument for divination/revelation, listed
alongside the [[Exodus 28.30|Urim and Thummim]] and the rods of Moses and
Aaron.[^1] The point: scripture itself records a patriarch employing a
divinatory object, undercutting the claim that [[Joseph Smith]]'s seer-stone use
is inherently illegitimate "folk magic."[^2]
