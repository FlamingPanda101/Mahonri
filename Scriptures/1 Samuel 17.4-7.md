# 1 Samuel 17:4-7

> "And there went out a champion out of the camp of the Philistines, named **Goliath, of Gath**, whose height was six cubits and a span... and **the staff of his spear was like a weaver's beam**; and his spear's head weighed six hundred shekels of iron." (KJV, abridged)

This is the famous Goliath whom [[David]] slays with a sling and a stone in the David-and-Goliath narrative of 1 Samuel 17. The distinctive tag "the staff of his spear was like a weaver's beam" recurs at [[2 Samuel 21.19|2 Samuel 21:19]], where the killing of "Goliath the Gittite" is instead credited to **[[Elhanan]]** the Bethlehemite — producing a direct contradiction over who killed Goliath. In the *Prolegomena* episode, [[Mike Day]] and [[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)|Dave Butler]] read these two passages back to back as a worked example of biblical doublets/contradictions, and Butler floats that "David" (meaning "beloved," never reused as a personal name) may be a **throne-name** whose bearer's birth name was Elhanan ("God has been gracious"). See [[Elhanan Killed Goliath - Chronicles Harmonized the Contradiction]].

On the academic consensus, [[2 Samuel 21.19|2 Samuel 21:19]] is the earlier reading and the Chronicler's parallel ([[1 Chronicles 20.5|1 Chronicles 20:5]], "Lahmi the brother of Goliath") is a later harmonization.
