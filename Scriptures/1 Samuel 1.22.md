# 1 Samuel 1:22

> But Hannah went not up; for she said unto her husband, I will not go up until the child be weaned, and then I will bring him, that he may appear before the LORD, and there abide for ever. *(KJV)*

## Key Hebrew Terms

- **רָאָה (ra'ah):** "to see." In the [[Masoretes|Masoretic]] vocalization Samuel will "appear before" (passive) the LORD; the underlying consonants belong to the same idiom [[Dan McClellan]] argues originally meant "see the face of the LORD." A Qumran reading tradition also bears on the verse. See [[Pilgrimage Commandment Was to See Gods Face]].

## Context

Hannah's vow to bring the weaned Samuel to the sanctuary at Shiloh "to appear before the LORD." [[Dan McClellan]] lists the Hannah-and-Samuel narrative among the texts whose Hebrew is "saying that they're there to see the face of God," reinterpreted as appearing before or being in the presence of God. It shows the same vision-idiom operating in the temple context outside the Pentateuch and Isaiah.
