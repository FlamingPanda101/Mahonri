# Luke 4:16-22

> And he came to Nazareth, where he had been brought up: and, as his custom was, he went into the synagogue on the sabbath day, and stood up for to read… And he closed the book, and he gave it again to the minister, and sat down. And the eyes of all them that were in the synagogue were fastened on him. And he began to say unto them, This day is this scripture fulfilled in your ears. *(KJV)*

## In context
Heal treats Jesus's sermon at the Nazareth synagogue as the foundational template for the Christian sermon. From it he extracts a threefold pattern: (1) a **liturgical setting** in a building dedicated to worship; (2) a **scripture reading** (Isaiah 61) preceding and grounding the sermon; and (3) **application to the present** — "Today this scripture has been fulfilled in your hearing." On this last point the scriptures "cease to be embers from the past and instead become the living fire of prophecy." The passage also evidences the synagogue origin of the reading-then-preaching liturgical structure inherited by early Christianity.
