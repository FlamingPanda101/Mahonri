# Jacob 1.14

> But I, Jacob, shall not hereafter distinguish them by these names, but I shall call them Lamanites that seek to destroy the people of Nephi, and those who are friendly to Nephi I shall call Nephites, or the people of Nephi, according to the reigns of the kings. ([Jacob 1:14](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/jacob/1.14))

**Gloss.** Jacob defines "Nephite" and "Lamanite" by political and religious **allegiance** ("seek to destroy" vs. "friendly to Nephi"), not by strict genealogy, and ties the labels to "the reigns of the kings." The terms function as covenant/loyalty categories, which is why groups can change designation over time and why the names behave more like sociopolitical than purely ethnic markers.

**Why it matters.** This verse anchors the argument that Book of Mormon ethnonyms ("Lamanite") are loyalty/covenant terms rather than racial or genetic ones — relevant both to DNA-based criticisms of the Book of Mormon and to figurative readings of its "skin" language.
