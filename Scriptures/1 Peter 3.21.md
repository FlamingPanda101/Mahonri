# 1 Peter 3:21

> The like figure whereunto even baptism doth also now save us (not the putting away of the filth of the flesh, but the answer of a good conscience toward God,) by the resurrection of Jesus Christ. *(KJV)*

## Key phrase
"**Baptism doth also now save us... the answer of a good conscience toward God**" — the NT's most explicit statement that baptism *saves*, immediately qualified that its saving power is not the bodily washing but the inward "answer" (or *appeal/pledge*) of conscience to God, grounded in Christ's resurrection.

## Apologetic application
The hinge text of the Ortlund–Horn baptism dialogue. **[[Trent Horn]]** reads it as baptism functioning as a real instrumental cause of salvation (with faith as a necessary associated condition). **[[Gavin Ortlund]]** reads "baptism saves" as **metonymy** — baptism named as standing in for the whole reality of faith and union with Christ that it publicly seals — pressing that the verse itself locates the saving element in the "appeal to God," not the act in the water. See [[Baptism Saves but Is Not the Cause of Salvation]].
