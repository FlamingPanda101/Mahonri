# 1 Thessalonians 4:16-18

> For the Lord himself shall descend from heaven with a shout, with the voice of the archangel, and with the trump of God: and the dead in Christ shall rise first: Then we which are alive and remain shall be caught up together with them in the clouds, to meet the Lord in the air: and so shall we ever be with the Lord. Wherefore comfort one another with these words. *(KJV)*

## Key phrase
"**The dead in Christ shall rise first: Then we which are alive and remain shall be caught up...**" Paul lays out the *order* of the resurrection at the Second Coming. The passage is the epigraph and anchor text of Nicholas J. Frederick's chapter on the Second Coming.

## Apologetic / historical application
[[Nicholas J. Frederick]] uses this passage to show Paul's confident expectation of an imminent, physical return — Paul "seems to hope" to be among "we who are alive, who are left," apparently expecting to witness the Parousia himself. It illustrates the early Christian conviction in a literal Second Coming and the [[The Already and Not Yet Tension in Early Christian Eschatology|"already / not yet" tension]]. The verse also became a touchstone for later "rapture" debates over pre- vs. post-tribulation timing.
