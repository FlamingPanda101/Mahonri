# Matthew 1:23 (KJV)

> Behold, a virgin shall be with child, and shall bring forth a son, and they shall call his name Emmanuel, which being interpreted is, God with us.

## Comparison with Original

Matthew quotes [[Isaiah 7.14|Isaiah 7:14]] from the Septuagint (_parthenos_ = "virgin"), not from the Hebrew (_alma_ = "young woman"). Matthew also shifts the subject: Isaiah 7:14 says the mother (the young woman) "shall call his name Immanuel"; Matthew 1:23 says "they shall call his name Emmanuel" — an unnamed group rather than the mother, possibly meaning "the followers of Jesus will refer to him as if he were God among us."[^1]

## The Name Problem

Despite Matthew quoting this prophecy, Jesus is never called "Emmanuel" by anyone in the New Testament. The prophecy says "shall call his _name_ Emmanuel," not "shall refer to him by the _title_ of Emmanuel."[^2]
