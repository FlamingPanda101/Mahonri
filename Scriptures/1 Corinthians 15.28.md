# 1 Corinthians 15:28

> And when all things shall be subdued unto him, then shall the Son also himself be subject unto him that put all things under him, that God may be all in all. *(KJV)*

**Context:** Part of Paul's resurrection chapter. The phrase "God... all in all" became the proof-text for Origen's account of the *telos* of human progression: final union with God in which "the mind will no longer be conscious of anything besides or other than God." For Origen this union is the point at which progression ends.
