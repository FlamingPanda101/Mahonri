# Doctrine and Covenants 25 — The revelation to Emma Smith ("an elect lady")

The revelation given through Joseph Smith to his wife Emma Smith in July 1830, addressed to her as "an elect lady," commissioning her to compile a hymnal and to teach and expound.

> "Hearken unto the voice of the Lord your God, while I speak unto you, Emma Smith, my daughter... Thou shalt be ordained under his hand to expound scriptures, and to exhort the church, according as it shall be given thee by my Spirit. ... And thou shalt be ordained to expound scriptures, and to exhort the church... And thy time shall be given to writing, and to learning much." (D&C 25:1, 7, 8 — paraphrased/conflated in the video)

## Key Detail

The section is the LDS scriptural cornerstone for **women's authorized public religious voice**: a woman is told by revelation to "expound scriptures" and "exhort the church," and to give her time to "writing and learning much." [[Jacob Hansen]] deploys it (alongside the founding of the [[Eliza R. Snow|Relief Society]] as a "kingdom of priests") to rebut the claim that the Restoration only ever silenced women — arguing the earliest revelations of the movement explicitly commissioned a woman to teach. Critics note the *expound/exhort* commission is not priesthood ordination to office and that "ordained" here is debated in meaning; defenders read it as a real, divinely sanctioned teaching role centuries before "feminism normalized women speaking publicly."
