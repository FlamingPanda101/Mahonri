# 1 Corinthians 9:5

> Do we not have the right to be accompanied by a believing wife, as do the other apostles and the brothers of the Lord and Cephas? *(NRSV)*

## Significance
[[Ariel Bybee Laughton]] cites this to show that at least some apostles and church leaders — including Peter (Cephas) — were married and "traveled with their wives," who worked alongside them to spread the gospel. Clement of Alexandria (*Stromata* 3.6.53) reports several apostles, including Paul, were married. This supports the picture of female missionary companions in early Christian work. See [[Women Led the First-Century Church as Apostles Deacons and Patrons]].
