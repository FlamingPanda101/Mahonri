# Isaiah 41:24

> "Behold, ye are of nothing, and your work of nought: an abomination is he that chooseth you." (KJV)

From the "trial of the gods" section of Deutero-Isaiah. The idols are called "nothing" (Hebrew *ayin* / *me-ayin*) and "of nought" (*efes* — "less than nothing"). In context the same vocabulary (*ayin*, *efes*, *tohu*) is applied to existing nations and rulers (Isa 40:17, 23; 41:11–12), so the terms denote irrelevance and incomparability rather than non-existence.
