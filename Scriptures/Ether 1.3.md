# Ether 1.3

> And as I suppose that the first part of this record, which speaks concerning the creation of the world, and also of Adam, and an account from that time even to the great tower, and as many things as transpired among the children of men until that time, is had among the Jews — therefore I do not write those things which transpired from the days of Adam until that time. *(Ether 1:3-4, Book of Mormon)*

Moroni's preface to his abridgment of the Jaredite record. He explains that he will not
rehearse the history from Adam down to **"the great tower,"** because that portion is
already "had among the Jews" (i.e., in Genesis). The Jaredite story proper begins at the
tower, where the languages were confounded.

## Why it's cited

This verse is the textual anchor for the observation that the Book of Mormon calls the
structure only **"the great tower"** — never "the Tower of Babel." The proper name "Babel"
appears in the Book of Mormon only in modern chapter headings, not in the inspired text.
Apologetics notes use this to argue (a) that the Jaredite record is not committed to the
historicity of "Babel-the-event" as a global language-origin myth, and (b) that the Book of
Ether supplies independent detail about a *local* confounding among the brother of Jared's
people and friends.
