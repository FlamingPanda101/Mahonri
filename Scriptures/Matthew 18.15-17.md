# Matthew 18:15-17 — Tell It to the Church

> ...if he shall neglect to hear them, tell it unto the church: but if he neglect to hear the church, let him be unto thee as an heathen man and a publican. *(Matthew 18:17, KJV)*

Jesus' procedure for confronting a sinning brother: privately first, then with two or three witnesses, and finally before **the church** (Greek *ekklēsia*), whose judgment is binding. The same passage continues with the binding-and-loosing grant ("whatsoever ye shall bind on earth") extended to the community.

## In context

In the [[Capturing Christianity - Debate The Great Apostasy Joe Heschmeyer vs Jacob Hansen|Heschmeyer–Hansen debate]], [[Joe Heschmeyer]] presses this verse (alongside [[1 Timothy 3.15|1 Timothy 3:15]], "the pillar and ground of the truth") to argue that "tell it to the church" requires a *visible, adjudicating institution*, not an amorphous body of sincere seekers — you cannot bring a grievance before, or be expelled by, an invisible assembly. [[Jacob Hansen]] grants that *ekklēsia* here refers to an institution, while maintaining the word elsewhere means the body of believers — prompting Heschmeyer to ask where Scripture ever distinguishes the two senses.
