# Isaiah 40-55

The section of Isaiah known as "Deutero-Isaiah" (Second Isaiah).

## Overview
Isaiah 40–55 marks a dramatic shift from chapters 1–39. The earlier chapters focus on judgment and the Assyrian crisis under Hezekiah; these chapters focus on comfort, restoration from exile, and strong monotheistic declarations. The name "Isaiah" never appears in this section.

Key themes:
- Comfort for exiled Israel (40:1-2)
- The suffering servant songs (42, 49, 50, 52-53)
- Cyrus as YHWH's anointed (45:1)
- "I am the LORD and there is no other" declarations of radical monotheism
- The fall of Babylon and restoration of Zion

## Used in
- [[Deutero-Isaiah Theory]] — The academic consensus holds these chapters were composed ~520 BCE during/after the Babylonian exile, not by the 8th-century prophet Isaiah.
- [[Divine Name Theology]] — Contains *ani hu* ("I am he") declarations, notably [[Isaiah 43.10]], that inform New Testament divine-name agency theology.
