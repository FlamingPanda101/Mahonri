# Genesis 19.5

> And they called unto Lot, and said unto him, Where are the men which came in to thee this night? bring them out unto us, that we may know them.

## Why it's cited

This verse anchors the narrative of Sodom's destruction and is invoked in studies of how the Hebrew Bible deploys sexual violence and the threat of forced "knowing" as a marker of a city under judgment. In apologetic and comparative readings it is paired with prophetic texts (such as Isaiah 3–6) to argue that scriptural authors used the language of violation and shame to depict covenant breakdown and the removal of legitimate worship. The passage thus functions less as a stand-alone moral proof-text than as a literary template for divine judgment that later prophets echo.
