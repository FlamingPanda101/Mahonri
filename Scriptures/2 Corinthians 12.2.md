# 2 Corinthians 12:2

> I knew a man in Christ above fourteen years ago, (whether in the body, I cannot tell; or whether out of the body, I cannot tell: God knoweth;) such an one caught up to the third heaven. And I knew such a man, (whether in the body, or out of the body, I cannot tell: God knoweth;) How that he was caught up into paradise, and heard unspeakable words, which it is not lawful for a man to utter. *(KJV, vv. 2-4)*

## Significance
Paul's account of being "caught up to the third heaven" is the New Testament's clearest description of an out-of-body visionary rapture. Three features map directly onto the modern near-death experience: (1) the explicit uncertainty of whether the event was "in the body" or "out of the body," matching the [[NDE - Out of the Body|out-of-body]] element; (2) entry into "paradise," a distinct postmortal-style realm; and (3) the **ineffability** of what was heard — "unspeakable words, which it is not lawful for a man to utter" — paralleling [[NDE - Ineffability]]. The passage anchors the case for [[NDE Parallels in the Bible]] and for an ancient Judeo-Christian visionary tradition.
