# Galatians 6:14

> But God forbid that I should glory, save in the cross of our Lord Jesus Christ, by whom the world is crucified unto me, and I unto the world. *(KJV)*

## Key phrase
"**Glory... in the cross of our Lord Jesus Christ**" — Paul's boast in the crucifixion alone.

## Apologetic / historical application
[[Miranda Wilcox]] uses this verse (Vulgate: *Mihi autem absit gloriari nisi in cruce Domini*) as the epigraph for the "Passion" section of the Medieval Afterword. It frames medieval **affective devotion** to the suffering Christ — the meditative contemplation of the cross that generated compunction and compassion. See [[Medieval Affective Devotion to the Suffering Christ]].
