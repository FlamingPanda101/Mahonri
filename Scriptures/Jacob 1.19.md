# Jacob 1:19 — "we did magnify our office"

> And we did magnify our office unto the Lord, taking upon us the responsibility, answering the sins of the people upon our own heads if we did not teach them the word of God with all diligence; wherefore, by laboring with our might their blood might not come upon our garments; otherwise their blood would come upon our garments, and we would not be found spotless at the last day. *(Jacob 1:19, Book of Mormon)*

## Key Detail
- The Book of Mormon's own definition of **"magnify" an office**: assuming blood-accountability for a people so their "blood might not come upon our garments." [[Todd McLauchlin]] makes this the controlling text for reading "magnifying their calling" in [[D&C 84.33-44|D&C 84:33]] as [[Magnifying a Calling Means Coming Clean From the Blood and Sins of This Generation|coming clean from the blood and sins of this generation]] through sacrifice. Compare [[Jacob 2|Jacob 2:2]] ("rid my garments of your sins") and [[King Benjamin]]'s garment language ([[Mosiah 5.7|Mosiah 5:7]]).
