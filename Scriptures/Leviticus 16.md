# Leviticus 16

> And Aaron shall cast lots upon the two goats; one lot for the LORD, and the other lot for the scapegoat [Azazel]. And Aaron shall bring the goat upon which the LORD's lot fell, and offer him for a sin offering. But the goat, on which the lot fell to be the scapegoat, shall be... let go... into the wilderness. *(Leviticus 16:8-10, KJV)*

## Key phrase
"**One lot for the LORD, and the other lot for Azazel**" — on the Day of Atonement the high priest casts lots over two identical goats; one is slain *for/as Yahweh* and its blood atones in the holy of holies, the other (the *la-Azazel* goat) is driven into the wilderness (in the Mishnah, *Yoma*, hurled off a cliff). [[Stick of Joseph|The Stick of Joseph]] notes the **preposition *lamed*** can read "as," so one goat *is* Yahweh and one *is* Azazel.

## Apologetic / historical application
[[Stick of Joseph|The Stick of Joseph]] (drawing on [[Margaret Barker]] via [[Dave Butler]]) uses the two-goats rite as the master template behind multiple texts: the sheep/goats judgment of [[Matthew 25.31-46|Matthew 25]] (the [[The Right and Left Hand of God Are the Day of Atonement Goats|right and left hand]] = the Yahweh and Azazel lots), the name taken into the heart of [[Mosiah 5.8|Mosiah 5]], the [[Two Brothers Where the Younger Is Chosen Are Types of Christ and Lucifer|two-brothers motif]] (one identical figure chosen, the other cast out as Lucifer/Azazel), and (elsewhere in the vault) [[Barabbas and Jesus Are the Two Goats of the Day of Atonement|Barabbas and Jesus]] and [[Laban and Nephi Enact the Two Goats of the Day of Atonement|Laban and Nephi]].
