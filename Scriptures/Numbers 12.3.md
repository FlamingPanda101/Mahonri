# Numbers 12:3 — "the meekest man on earth"

> (Now the man Moses was very meek, above all the men which were upon the face of the earth.) *(KJV)*

## Key Detail
The verse is a parenthetical narratorial aside praising [[Moses]]'s humility "above all the men... upon the face of the earth." Source critics (and earlier, the rabbis) flag the oddity of the *most humble* man recording his own supreme humility — a small but pointed indicator that the Pentateuch is narrated *about* Moses by a later hand, not autographed by him. (Compare the neighboring [[Numbers 12.8|Numbers 12:8]], where God defends Moses by saying he beholds "the form of the LORD.")
