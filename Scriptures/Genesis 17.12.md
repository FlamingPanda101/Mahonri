# Genesis 17:12

> And he that is eight days old shall be circumcised among you, every man child in your generations, he that is born in the house, or bought with money of any stranger, which is not of thy seed. *(KJV)*

## Key phrase
"**He that is eight days old shall be circumcised**" — the covenant sign applied to infants on the eighth day, before any profession of faith.

## Apologetic application
The eighth-day circumcision precedent shaped the early baptism debate two ways. (1) It served as a scriptural analogue for applying the covenant sign to infants (a pedobaptist argument). (2) In the 3rd century it generated the *only* live controversy [[Trent Horn]] notes — whether infant baptism should likewise wait until the eighth day — which [[Cyprian of Carthage]]'s council rejected, judging "the mercy and grace of God is not to be refused to anyone born of man." That the debate was over *timing*, not *whether*, is itself evidence infant baptism was already the norm. See [[Augustine Clarified Infant Baptism He Did Not Invent It]].
