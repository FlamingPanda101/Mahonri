# 2 Samuel 24:1 (KJV)

> "And again the anger of the LORD was kindled against Israel, and he moved David against them to say, Go, number Israel and Judah."

**Note:** In the older (Deuteronomistic) version of the census story, it is **Yahweh himself** who incites David to number Israel — and then God punishes David for it. The Chronicler's parallel, [[1 Chronicles 21.1|1 Chronicles 21:1]], rewrites this so that "**Satan**" (an anarthrous *satan*) does the inciting, apparently to offload the morally awkward action from God. The Kings/Samuel-vs-Chronicles pair is both a source-critical divergence and the crux for whether "Satan" ever appears as a name in the Old Testament.
