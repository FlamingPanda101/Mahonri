# 1 Nephi 5

While Lehi's sons are away obtaining the brass plates, his wife Sariah mourns, complaining that Lehi is a visionary man who has led the family into the wilderness to perish and that her sons are dead. Lehi comforts her, affirming his vision but acknowledging her grief; when the sons return safely with the records, Sariah is comforted and declares that she now knows the Lord commanded their journey and protected her children, and the family offers sacrifice and thanksgiving. Lehi then examines the brass plates and finds they contain the five books of Moses, a record of the Jews from the beginning down to the reign of Zedekiah, the prophecies of the holy prophets, and a genealogy of his fathers. Searching the genealogy, Lehi discovers that he is a descendant of Joseph who was sold into Egypt. He prophesies that these plates will go to all his seed and never perish, rejoicing that his posterity will be preserved and that the records will carry his line's knowledge forward.

## Why it's cited
This chapter is cited for its genealogical claim that Lehi is a descendant of Joseph (and, elsewhere, specifically of Manasseh) — used to argue that the Book of Mormon presents itself as a northern-kingdom Israelite record rather than a Judahite/Jewish one, with implications for its religious milieu and its relationship to the brass-plates scriptural canon. The discovery that the plates contain the Law of Moses and the prophets also grounds arguments about which scriptural traditions the Lehite community carried.
