# Genesis 3:5

> For God doth know that in the day ye eat thereof, then your eyes shall be opened, and ye shall be as gods, knowing good and evil. *(Genesis 3:5, KJV)*

The serpent's claim to Eve: eating the fruit will make them "as gods, knowing good and evil."

## Why it's cited
Anti-Mormon polemic treats "ye shall be as gods" as Satan's lie, and so brands the LDS doctrine of deification "the serpent's lie." The Ward Radio panel answers that the very same outcome is affirmed by God two verses later at [[Genesis 3.22|Genesis 3:22]] ("the man is become as one of us, to know good and evil") — so becoming "as gods" in the knowledge of good and evil was *not* simply a lie; the deception lay elsewhere (e.g., timing/means/"in the day… ye shall surely die").
