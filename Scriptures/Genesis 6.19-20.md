# Genesis 6:19–20

> And of every living thing of all flesh, two of every sort shalt thou bring into the ark, to keep them alive with thee; they shall be male and female. Of fowls after their kind, and of cattle after their kind, of every creeping thing of the earth after his kind, two of every sort shall come unto thee, to keep them alive. *(KJV)*

## Key Hebrew Terms

- **שְׁנַיִם (shenayim):** "two" — the Priestly source (P) specifies exactly two of every kind, male and female, for all land animals (*behemah*) and all birds (*oph*). No clean/unclean distinction is made — ALL animals come in pairs.
- **בְּהֵמָה (behemah):** "cattle" / "land animals" — the broader category of land-dwelling creatures. The Priestly account makes no distinction between clean and unclean land animals.
- **עוֹף (oph):** "fowls" / "birds of the air" — critically, the P account says TWO of ALL birds. This is where the contradiction with Genesis 7:2–3 becomes inescapable.

## Source-Critical Context

This passage belongs to the **Priestly (P)** flood account. In the scholarly consensus (see [[Noahs Ark Source Criticism Two Flood Accounts]]), Genesis 6–9 weaves together two originally independent flood narratives: the Priestly account (P) and the non-Priestly/Yahwist account (J). They differ on multiple details — the duration of the flood, the number of animals, the cause of the flood, and the terminology for God (Elohim vs. YHWH).

For the animal count specifically: the Priestly source says **two of every kind** (Genesis 6:19–20), while the non-Priestly source says **seven pairs** of clean animals and birds (Genesis 7:2–3). The contradiction includes ALL birds — P says two, J says seven pairs — with no clean/unclean distinction among birds in J to soften the discrepancy.

## The Apologetic Harmonization and Its Failure

Apologists argue "two" is the baseline and "seven pairs" is an extra for sacrificial purposes. But McClellan demonstrates this fails because:
1. Both accounts specify "ALL the birds of the air" — not a subset for sacrifice
2. P says two of ALL birds; J says seven pairs of ALL birds
3. J makes a clean/unclean distinction for land animals but NOT for birds: "seven pairs of all the birds of the sky" — no bird gets only one pair
4. The harmonization invents a "third thing" not supported by either text
