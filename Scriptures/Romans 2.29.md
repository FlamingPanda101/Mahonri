# Romans 2:29

> But he is a Jew, which is one inwardly; and circumcision is that of the heart, in the spirit, and not in the letter; whose praise is not of men, but of God. *(KJV)*

## Key phrase
"**Circumcision is that of the heart, in the spirit, and not in the letter**" — Paul relativizes the physical rite to the inward reality it signifies; the outwardly circumcised whose hearts are unchanged are not truly "Jews," and true circumcision is a Spirit-wrought matter of the heart.

## Apologetic application
**[[Gavin Ortlund]]** uses the circumcision sign/signified relationship as the engine of his **metonymy** case for baptism: just as a Jew could say "be circumcised, coming to the living God" — meaning *become a Jew*, of which circumcision is the sign and salvation part of the whole package — without circumcision *causing* the relationship, so "baptism saves" names the whole reality the rite represents. Romans 2:29, he argues, *teaches what physical circumcision is*. **[[Trent Horn]]** counters that the parallel fails because New Covenant baptism is deliberately *superior* to circumcision and actually effects what it signifies (Colossians 2:11-12), so circumcision's deficiency cannot be read back onto baptism. See [[Baptism Saves but Is Not the Cause of Salvation]].
