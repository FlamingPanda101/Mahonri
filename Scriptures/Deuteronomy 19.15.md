# Deuteronomy 19:15 — "two or three witnesses"

> One witness shall not rise up against a man for any iniquity, or for any sin... at the mouth of two witnesses, or at the mouth of three witnesses, shall the matter be established. *(Deuteronomy 19:15, KJV)*

## Key Detail
- The Hebrew **law of witnesses**: a matter is established only "in the mouth of two or three witnesses." Paul echoes it (2 Corinthians 13:1; 1 Timothy 5:19), and the Thoughtful Faith narrator makes it the scriptural backbone of the **collective prophetic witness** — truth is established by corroborating testimony across prophets, not by a single voice (cf. [[John 5.31]], where even Jesus applies the principle).
- Used to reject treating any one leader's speculation as decisive against the diachronic consensus.
