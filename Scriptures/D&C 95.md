# D&C 95 — Chastening on the Kirtland Temple; "many ordained, few chosen"

> Behold, verily I say unto you, there are many who have been ordained among you, whom I have called, but few of them are chosen. They who are not chosen have sinned a very grievous sin, in that they are walking in darkness at noon-day. *(D&C 95:5-6)*

## Key Detail
- A revelation chastening the early Saints for delaying the **Kirtland Temple** ("the building of mine house," v. 8) by which the Lord "designed to prepare mine apostles" (v. 4). [[Todd McLauchlin]] leans on vv. 5-6 because they **equate being *ordained* with being *called*** — leaving "chosen" (elect/sealed) as a *further* step — to argue that ordination is only a calling into the order, not the [[Calling and Election Is the Oath of the Father Sealed in the Endowment|oath and covenant]] itself. He pairs it with [[D&C 121.32|D&C 121:34]] ("many are called, but few are chosen").
