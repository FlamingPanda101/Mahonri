# D&C 43 — "How Often Would I Have Gathered You"

> **24** O ye nations of the earth, how often would I have gathered you together as a hen gathereth her chickens under her wings, but ye would not!
> **25** How oft have I called upon you by the mouth of my servants, and by the ministering of angels, and by mine own voice, and by the voice of thunderings, and by the voice of lightnings, and by the voice of tempests, and by the voice of earthquakes, and great hailstorms, and by the voice of famines and pestilences of every kind... but ye would not! (D&C 43:24–25)

An 1831 revelation echoing the gathering lament of Matthew 23:37. God catalogs the escalating means — servants, angels, His own voice, then thunder, tempest, earthquake, famine, pestilence — by which He has called the nations to repentance "but ye would not."

[[Shad Brooks]] reads this as God using **maximal, even alarming, language and means for behavioral effect** — paralleling it with Jacob 5's "what more could I have done for my vineyard?" — to support his claim that God's rhetoric is calibrated to provoke right action.[^1] See [[God Frames Truth Pedagogically to Change Behavior]].
