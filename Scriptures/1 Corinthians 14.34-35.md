# 1 Corinthians 14:34-35

> Let your women keep silence in the churches: for it is not permitted unto them to speak; but they are commanded to be under obedience, as also saith the law. And if they will learn any thing, let them ask their husbands at home: for it is a shame for women to speak in the church. *(KJV)*

## Key Greek term
- **γυνή (gynē)** — means both "woman" and "wife." Scrivener appeals to the "wife" sense (wives talking over preaching husbands) to harmonize this with [[1 Corinthians 11.5|1 Cor 11:5]], where women pray and prophesy aloud.

## Significance
[[Alex OConnor|Alex O'Connor]] argues this verse contradicts [[1 Corinthians 11.5|1 Cor 11:5]] (women praying and prophesying) three chapters earlier. His preferred solution: Paul **did not write it** — it is a marginal gloss imported from [[1 Timothy 2.11|1 Tim 2:11]] that entered the text, evidenced by its **floating position** in early manuscripts (it appears after v. 40 in the Western tradition). He notes the irony that he, "a skeptic of the biblical tradition," can absolve Paul of sexism, "whereas the Christian has to own them." [[Glenn Scrivener]] prefers a contextual reading (disorderly Corinthian worship). See [[1 Corinthians 14 Was Not Written by Paul]] and [[Modern Gender Equality Is Not Grounded in the Bible]].
