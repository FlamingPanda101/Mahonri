# Doctrine and Covenants 26

> And all things shall be done by common consent in the church, by much prayer and faith. *(D&C 26:2)*

## Significance
The "law of common consent." Used by [[Luke Hansen]] to argue that no single prophet or apostle — including [[Brigham Young]] — can unilaterally declare his own teaching to be binding canon; canonization requires presentation to, and ratification by, the body of the church. Central to the rebuttal that Adam-God was never formally canonical despite being published in the *Journal of Discourses*.
