# 2 Nephi 3 — Joseph of Egypt's prophecies of a latter-day seer

> A seer shall the Lord my God raise up, who shall be a choice seer unto the fruit of my loins... and his name shall be called after me; and it shall be after the name of his father. *(2 Nephi 3:6-15, abridged)*

## Key Detail
Lehi quotes the **first-person prophecies of [[Joseph of Egypt]]** preserved on the brass plates: Joseph foresees Moses, a spokesman (Aaron), the latter-day seer ("his name shall be called after me," and after his father — read by Latter-day Saints as Joseph Smith Jr., son of Joseph Sr.), and the bringing-forth of scripture to his seed. These detailed Joseph-prophecies are **not in the King James Genesis**, which descends from the tribe of Judah.

## Key Detail (apologetic use)
In the [[Ward Radio - The Sword of Laban Was the Sword of Joshua|Don Bradley interview]], 2 Nephi 3 is one of the internal-evidence pillars that the **brass plates were begun by Joseph of Egypt**: a record handed down by Joseph's descendants ([[1 Nephi 5]]), containing Joseph's own genealogy and first-person prophecies and written in Egyptian, is best explained if Joseph himself started it. See [[The Brass Plates Were Begun by Joseph of Egypt]].
