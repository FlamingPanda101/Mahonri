# 1 Timothy 2:11-12

> Let the woman learn in silence with all subjection. But I suffer not a woman to teach, nor to usurp authority over the man, but to be in silence. *(KJV)*

## Significance
[[Alex OConnor|Alex O'Connor]] argues that [[1 Corinthians 14.34-35|1 Cor 14:34-35]] is a marginal gloss imported *from* this passage — someone noted 1 Timothy 2:11 in the margin of 1 Corinthians, and it migrated into the text, explaining its "floating" position in early manuscripts. 1 Timothy is itself widely regarded by critical scholars as deutero-Pauline (written after Paul). O'Connor notes the apologetic claim that it addressed a specific situation ("the cult of Artemis" at Ephesus, women taking unauthorized authority) but says "we don't have that" — the canonical text reads as a general injunction. See [[1 Corinthians 14 Was Not Written by Paul]] and [[Modern Gender Equality Is Not Grounded in the Bible]].
