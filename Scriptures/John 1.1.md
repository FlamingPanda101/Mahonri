# John 1:1

> In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God. *(KJV)*

## The Greek
Three clauses in the Greek: (1) *En arche en ho logos* — "In the beginning was the Word"; (2) *kai ho logos en pros ton theon* — "and the Word was with [the] God" (definite article *ton* indicates the God of Israel); (3) *kai theos en ho logos* — "and the Word was God/deity."

## The Grammar Issue (Clause 3)
In the third clause, *theos* (God) appears **without the definite article** and **before the verb**. In Greek copular sentences (X is Y), a pre-verbal anarthrous predicate noun is typically **qualitative**, not definite — it describes what the subject *is like*, not who the subject *is*. If John meant "the Word was THE God," it would read *ho theos en ho logos*.

[[Dan McClellan]] presents this as the "academic consensus of Greek grammarians" — even conservative scholars like [[Daniel Wallace]] concede *theos* here is qualitative. The question is whether the qualitative reading supports later [[Trinity - Historical Development|Trinitarian theology]] (Wallace) or a pre-Trinitarian "divine agency" Christology (McClellan).
