# Hebrews 1:3

> Who being the brightness of his glory, and the express image of his person, and upholding all things by the word of his power, when he had by himself purged our sins, sat down on the right hand of the Majesty on high; *(KJV)*

## Key Term: "express image of his person" (*charaktēr tēs hypostaseōs autou*)
The Greek word *hypostasis* — later the technical creedal term for "person" — appears here. The panel concedes the word is biblical but argues its 1st-century sense ("person," the impress/stamp of a thing) is not the 4th-century metaphysical "subsistence of one being." The verse's *imagery* (the Son as the **express image / imprint** of the Father, seated at his **right hand**) depicts two distinct things — the wax seal and the stamp, the king and his right-hand man — not one being. See [[Hebrews 1-3 Express Image Is the Imprint Not the Stamp]].
