# Genesis 5:29

> And he called his name Noah, saying, This same shall comfort us concerning our work and toil of our hands, because of the ground which the Lord hath cursed. *(Genesis 5:29, KJV)*

Lamech names his son Noah, anticipating comfort "concerning… the ground which the Lord hath cursed."

## Why it's cited
The Ward Radio Sin-of-the-Giants episode reads the otherwise-unexplained "ground which the Lord hath cursed" as a pre-flood **famine/drought** — one of the chastisements (war, then famine) God sent before resorting to the flood. Joseph Smith's Book of Moses, they argue, supplies this drought detail (God "sending no rain"), as does Pirke de-Rabbi Eliezer ("we don't need your rain").
