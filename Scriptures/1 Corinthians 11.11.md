# 1 Corinthians 11:11 — Neither the man without the woman in the Lord

> "Nevertheless neither is the man without the woman, neither the woman without the man, in the Lord." (KJV)

## Key Detail

Standing in the same passage that has Paul ranking "the head of the woman is the man" ([[1 Corinthians 11.3|1 Cor 11:3]]) and assuming women pray and prophesy with covered heads ([[1 Corinthians 11.5|1 Cor 11:5]]), verse 11 supplies the **mutual-dependence** correction: in the Lord, neither sex is complete or self-sufficient without the other. Latter-day Saint readers use it as a New Testament proof-text for **complementarity** — the claim that male and female are interdependent halves ordered toward a single whole, mirrored in the temple sealing of [[Doctrine and Covenants 132|D&C 132]] where exaltation is attained only by a man and woman together. The verse is double-edged in modern gender debates: it neither flattens the sexes into interchangeability nor isolates them into independence, but binds them.
