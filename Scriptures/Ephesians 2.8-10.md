# Ephesians 2.8-10

> For by grace are ye saved through faith; and that not of yourselves: it is the gift of God: Not of works, lest any man should boast. For we are his workmanship, created in Christ Jesus unto good works, which God hath before ordained that we should walk in them.

## Why it's cited

This passage is the central battleground for the relationship between grace, faith, and works in salvation. Its first clause grounds salvation in unmerited grace received through faith, "not of works," while its third verse insists believers are "created... unto good works" — letting both sides of the grace-versus-works debate appeal to it. Apologetic notes cite it to argue that works are the fruit and evidence of saving faith rather than its purchase price, and to frame disputes such as the Pelagian controversy over human ability and divine grace.
