# 2 Nephi 5:21

> "And he had caused the cursing to come upon them, yea, even a sore cursing, because of their iniquity. For behold, they had hardened their hearts against him, that they had become like unto a flint; wherefore, as they were white, and exceedingly fair and delightsome, that they might not be enticing unto my people the Lord God did cause a skin of blackness to come upon them."

The single most-cited verse behind the claim that the Book of Mormon teaches a race-based curse. Latter-day Saint readers argue the "skin of blackness" is not about African ancestry (no Africans existed in the Book of Mormon world) and is better read metaphorically, or as marking (clothing/body paint), or as a non-pigmentary curse — and that the text's overall thrust *condemns* Nephite contempt for the Lamanites. The 1981 LDS edition changed "white and delightsome" (2 Ne 30:6) to "pure and delightsome," reflecting the disavowal of racial readings.
