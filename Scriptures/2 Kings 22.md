# 2 Kings 22

> And Hilkiah the high priest said unto Shaphan the scribe, I have found the book of the law in the house of the LORD... And it came to pass, when the king had heard the words of the book of the law, that he rent his clothes. *(KJV, vv. 8, 11)*

## In context

The account of [[Josiah]]'s reign in which the high priest Hilkiah "finds" the **book of the law** in the temple during a renovation (after an audit of temple silver that the text says required no reckoning "because they dealt faithfully," v. 7). The young king Josiah — orphaned and raised by the scribal establishment, crowned at 8, about 21 at this point — tears his clothes and sends to the prophetess Huldah to authenticate it. Scholars identify this "found" book with an early form of **Deuteronomy**.

LDS apologists on [[Ward Radio]] read the story with suspicion: a self-serving book that exalts the scribes and condemns everyone else, conveniently discovered by the scribes, authenticated by the scribes, and pressed on a vulnerable king they had raised. The argument: this is the moment the Deuteronomist reform overwrote the older fifth book of Moses.
