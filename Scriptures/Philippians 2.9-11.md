# Philippians 2:9-11

> Wherefore God also hath highly exalted him, and given him a name which is above every name: That at the name of Jesus every knee should bow, of things in heaven, and things in earth, and things under the earth; And that every tongue should confess that Jesus Christ is Lord, to the glory of God the Father. *(KJV)*

## Key Term: "the name which is above every name"
Often identified as the divine name (*kyrios* / YHWH). The passage applies Isaiah 45:23's exclusive-worship language ("every knee shall bow") to Jesus, which Richard Bauckham reads as Jesus' inclusion in "the divine identity" already in earliest Christian devotion. The closing phrase "to the glory of God the **Father**" simultaneously *distinguishes* the Son from the Father — fuel for both Trinitarian (divine identity) and subordinationist/LDS (predicative divinity, distinct persons) readings.
