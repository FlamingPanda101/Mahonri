# Alma 46:21-22

> "And it came to pass that when Moroni had proclaimed these words, behold, the people came running together with their armour girded about their loins, rending their garments in token, or as a covenant, that they would not forsake the Lord their God . . . Now this was the covenant which they made, and they cast their garments at the feet of Moroni, saying: We covenant with our God, that we shall be destroyed, even as our brethren in the land northward, if we shall fall into transgression; yea, he may cast us at the feet of our enemies, even as we have cast our garments at our feet to be trodden under foot, if we shall fall into transgression."

## Context

Moroni raises the **Title of Liberty** to rally the Nephites against Amalickiah's bid for kingship. The people respond by rending their garments and casting them underfoot while swearing an oath.

## Significance

[[David Butler (Stick of Joseph)|David Butler]] reads this as a deliberate **enactment of a covenant penalty** — the same penalty stated in [[Matthew 5.13|Matthew 5:13]] ("cast out, and . . . trodden under foot of men"). The Israelites perform a self-maledictory oath: *may we be done to as this garment is done to* if we break covenant. The garments cast at the feet "to be trodden under foot" make the abstract Sermon-on-the-Mount sanction physically concrete, which (on Butler's argument) is evidence that the phrase is a recognized **ritual covenant formula**, not a passing metaphor. The same penalty is *suffered* by the apostate Korihor in [[Alma 30.27|Alma 30]]. See [[The Salt-Savor Covenant Penalty Enacted by Korihor and the Title of Liberty]].
