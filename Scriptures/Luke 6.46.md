# Luke 6:46 — "Lord, Lord"

> And why call ye me, Lord, Lord, and do not the things which I say? *(Luke 6:46, KJV; cf. Matthew 7:21-22)*

## Key Detail
- [[Michael Jones]] (citing Jason Staples, Michael Patrick Barber, and others) argues the doubled "Lord, Lord" is a divine self-designation: in no Greek text of the era does anyone claim to be "Lord, Lord" except as the Septuagint's rendering of *Adonai Yahweh* (e.g., Ezekiel's *kyrie kyrie*). So Jesus in Luke/Matthew is "explicitly claiming to be God" — supporting the claim that a high Christology is present in the synoptics, not just John.
- A developing scholarly argument rather than consensus; cited here as part of the conservative case against an evolving Christology.
