# 1 Enoch

**1 Enoch** (also *First Enoch*, the *Ethiopic Apocalypse of Enoch*) is a composite Second Temple Jewish apocalypse attributed to Enoch, "the seventh from Adam" ([[Genesis 5.18-24|Genesis 5:18–24]]; the patriarch whom "God took"). It is **not a single book** but five sections by different authors written across several centuries, the oldest layer dating to roughly 300 BCE and originally composed in Aramaic. It survives in full only in Ge'ez (Ethiopic), is preserved in the 81-book canon of the [[Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church|Ethiopian Orthodox Church]], and is attested in fragments at Qumran. See [[Book of Enoch - Overview and Structure]].

## The Five Sections

1. **The Book of the Watchers** (chs. 1–36) — the fall of the angelic Watchers and their union with human women, an expansion of [[Genesis 6 Sons of God|Genesis 6:1–4]]. See [[Book of Enoch - The Watchers and Genesis 6 Expansion]].
2. **The Book of Parables / Similitudes** (chs. 37–71) — the section most important for Christology; contains the pre-existent, enthroned **Son of Man**. The one section **not** found at Qumran.
3. **The Astronomical Book / Book of Luminaries** (chs. 72–82) — the 364-day solar calendar. See [[Book of Enoch - 364 Day Solar Calendar]].
4. **The Book of Dreams** (chs. 83–90) — includes the Animal Apocalypse. See [[Book of Enoch - The Animal Apocalypse]].
5. **The Epistle of Enoch** (chs. 91–108) — the latest layer, ~late 1st century CE.

## Key Passages for the Son of Man

- **1 Enoch 1:9** — quoted nearly verbatim in [[Jude 1.14-15|Jude 1:14–15]] and called "prophecy."
- **1 Enoch 46** — Enoch sees two figures in the throne room: the "Head of Days" (white as wool, echoing the Ancient of Days of [[Daniel 7.13-14|Daniel 7:13–14]]) and beside him a being "whose face had the appearance of a man," identified as "the Son of Man."
- **1 Enoch 48:2–3, 6, 10** — the Son of Man was **named before creation** ("before the sun and the signs were created"), hidden with God, and called "the Anointed One" (Messiah).
- **1 Enoch 51:3** — "the Chosen One shall sit on my throne."
- **1 Enoch 62** — the eschatological judgment: kings and rulers are "seized with pain" when they see the Chosen One enthroned, because they denied "the Lord of Spirits and his Anointed."

## Significance

1 Enoch supplies the conceptual background for Jesus's most frequent self-designation, the **Son of Man** (used ~80× in the Gospels). Its Parables present the most explicit pre-Christian portrait of a pre-existent, divinely enthroned Messiah distinct in person from the Father — a portrait that Moroni's Standard argues maps onto LDS Christology rather than the Nicene formulation, and that illuminates the blasphemy charge at Jesus's trial.
