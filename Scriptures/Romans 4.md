# Romans 4

> For what saith the scripture? Abraham believed God, and it was counted unto him for righteousness... Now to him that worketh is the reward not reckoned of grace, but of debt. But to him that worketh not, but believeth on him that justifieth the ungodly, his faith is counted for righteousness. *(KJV, vv. 3-5)*

Paul's argument that Abraham was justified by faith — and *before* his circumcision (Genesis 15:6 precedes Genesis 17) — so that he is "the father of all who believe" without works.

## In the baptism debate
This is [[Ryan (NeedGod)|Ryan]]'s central text: Abraham is "the model of how anyone gets saved," justified by being "fully convinced that God was able to do what He had promised" (Rom 4:21), apart from any deed that could give ground for boasting (Rom 4:2). Since circumcision (the OT sign) did not justify Abraham, Ryan argues, baptism (which Catholic Answers calls circumcision's replacement) cannot be required to be counted righteous. [[Joe Heschmeyer]] replies — citing [[James Dunn]] — that the "works" Paul excludes in Romans 4 are specifically **works of the law** available only to the circumcised (made explicit in v. 9), not human action or obedience as such. See [[Salvation Is by Faith Alone and Baptism Is a Non-Saving Act of Obedience]] and [[Works of the Law Means Mosaic Boundary Markers Not Obedience as Such]].
