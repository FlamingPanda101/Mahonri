# Revelation 14:6

> And I saw another angel fly in the midst of heaven, having the everlasting gospel to preach unto them that dwell on the earth, and to every nation, and kindred, and tongue, and people. *(KJV)*

## Significance
[[John the Evangelist|John]] foresees a *future* angel bringing "the everlasting gospel." [[Ward Radio]] uses this as the decisive harmonizing argument against the [[Galatians 1.8|Galatians 1:8]] attack: if Paul's words forbid an angel from ever bringing the gospel, then John is wrong. The only way to make Paul and John agree is to read Galatians as condemning a *false* gospel's content, not angelic delivery — which then permits an angel (e.g. [[Angel Moroni]]) to restore the everlasting gospel. Latter-day Saints identify this angel with the Restoration.

The Greek **εὐαγγέλιον αἰώνιον** (*euangelion aiōnion*, "everlasting/eternal gospel") signals one timeless gospel, consistent with [[1 Corinthians 15.1-4|Paul's gospel]] rather than a contradictory "other gospel."
