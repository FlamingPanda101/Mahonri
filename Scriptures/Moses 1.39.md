# Moses 1.39

> For behold, this is my work and my glory—to bring to pass the immortality and eternal life of man.
> — Moses 1:39 (Pearl of Great Price)

A revelation given to [[Joseph Smith]] (June 1830), part of the Book of Moses. [[Casey Paul Griffiths]] calls it the most-quoted scripture in the LDS canon, framing it as God's declared *purpose*: the deification and endless life of human beings. In the [[King Follett Discourse]] context it supplies the teleology behind an [[Anthropomorphic God Grounds Heavenly Mother and Eternal Families|anthropomorphic, purposeful God]] — members can say "I know what he's going for."

Key term: "eternal life" (LDS usage) is technical — not mere endless existence but *exaltation*, the kind of life God lives, distinguished from "immortality" (resurrection alone).
