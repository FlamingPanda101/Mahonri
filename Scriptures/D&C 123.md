# D&C 123

> For there are many yet on the earth among all sects, parties, and denominations, who are blinded by the subtle craftiness of men ... and who are only kept from the truth because they know not where to find it — Therefore, that we should waste and wear out our lives in bringing to light all the hidden things of darkness ... these should be attended to with great earnestness. ... Therefore, dearly beloved brethren, let us cheerfully do all things that lie in our power; and then may we stand still, with the utmost assurance, to see the salvation of God. *(D&C 123:12-13, 17)*

## Key passage: the small helm and the hidden things of darkness
Part of Joseph Smith's Liberty Jail correspondence. [[Todd McLauchlin]] and [[Hayden Paul]] open their conversation here: the saint, like Joseph "captive to a system way bigger than us," is told that a "very large ship is benefited very much by a very small helm" (v. 16) — so Latter-day Saints steer a tumultuous world not through politics but by "wasting and wearing out our lives in bringing to light all the hidden things of darkness," beginning with the darkness in oneself. This frames the video's case for the [[The True Order of Prayer Is the Heartbeat of the Endowment|true order of prayer]].
