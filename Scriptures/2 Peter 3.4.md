# 2 Peter 3:4

> And saying, Where is the promise of his coming? for since the fathers fell asleep, all things continue as they were from the beginning of the creation. *(KJV)*

## Key phrase
"**Where is the promise of his coming?**" — the question of the "scoffers" who, by the turn of the second century, doubted the reality of Jesus's Second Coming because of its delay.

## Apologetic / historical application
[[Nicholas J. Frederick]] cites 2 Peter 3:4 as the New Testament's own evidence that the *delay of the Parousia* had already become a crisis. Peter's reply runs through the chapter: God works on his own schedule, the delay exists so all "should come to repentance" (see [[2 Peter 3.9|2 Peter 3:9]]), and "the day of the Lord will come as a thief in the night" (3:10). See [[The Delay of the Parousia Drove Christian Doctrinal Change]]. The "day = thousand years" idea of 2 Peter 3:8 was later used (and, Frederick notes via Menn, misused) to support the seven-thousand-year scheme.
