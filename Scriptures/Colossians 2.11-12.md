# Colossians 2:11-12

> In whom also ye are circumcised with the circumcision made without hands, in putting off the body of the sins of the flesh by the circumcision of Christ: Buried with him in baptism, wherein also ye are risen with him through the faith of the operation of God, who hath raised him from the dead. *(KJV)*

## Key phrase
"**Circumcised... in putting off the body of the flesh... buried with him in baptism, wherein also ye are risen with him**" — Paul juxtaposes circumcision and baptism, presenting baptism as the New Covenant counterpart in which believers are united to Christ's death and resurrection "through the faith of the operation of God."

## Apologetic application
**[[Trent Horn]]** leans on this text against **[[Gavin Ortlund]]**'s circumcision-based metonymy argument: he notes the Greek lacks the conjunction "and" that English versions insert at v. 12, tying "putting off the body of flesh in the circumcision of Christ" directly to "buried with him in baptism." Horn reads this as showing baptism is *superior* to circumcision — really accomplishing what it signifies (cleansing from sin, adoption, union with Christ) rather than merely foreshadowing it — so the sign/signified slippage Ortlund finds in circumcision (Romans 2:29) does not transfer to baptism. The "through faith" clause also lets Horn block a magical, faith-less *ex opere operato* reading. See [[Baptism Saves but Is Not the Cause of Salvation]].
