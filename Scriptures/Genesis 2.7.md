# Genesis 2:7

> And the LORD God formed man of the dust of the ground, and breathed into his nostrils the breath of life; and man became a living soul. *(KJV)*

## Significance
A key proof text in patristic debates over human nature. [[Irenaeus of Lyons]] read this "breath of life" as the moment God gave humans the spiritual element that constitutes the divine *likeness* (beyond the bodily *image*), making them "living souls." [[Origen]] instead read it as a *second, distinct creation* — the physical body formed from dust after the prior creation of souls (Genesis 1:26–27) — reflecting his dualist, Platonizing anthropology in which the body "weighs down the soul." The verse thus anchored opposing views of whether the body is a positive part of human nature (Irenaeus) or a corrupting tomb (Origen).
