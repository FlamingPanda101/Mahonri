# Galatians 4:26

> But Jerusalem which is above is free, which is the mother of us all. *(KJV)*

## In context
Quoted by [[John Cassian]] (*Conferences* 14.8.4) in his exposition of the fourfold sense of scripture. In Cassian's worked example, "Jerusalem" read anagogically is "the heavenly city of God 'which is the mother of us all' (Galatians 4:26)" — distinct from its historical (the city of the Jews), allegorical (the Church of Christ), and tropological (the human soul) senses.
