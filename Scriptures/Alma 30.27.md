# Alma 30:27 (Book of Mormon)

> "And thus ye lead away this people after the foolish traditions of your fathers, and according to your own desires; and ye keep them down, even as it were in bondage, that ye may glut yourselves with the labors of their hands, that they durst not look up with boldness, and that they durst not enjoy their rights and privileges."

These are the words of **Korihor**, the Book of Mormon anti-Christ, accusing the priests of using ordinances and observances to "keep [the people] down ... in bondage." In LDS apologetics the passage is the archetypal articulation of the secular charge that religious demand exists to subjugate rather than to liberate adherents.
