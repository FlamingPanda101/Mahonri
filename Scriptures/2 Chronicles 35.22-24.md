# 2 Chronicles 35:22-24 — the death of King Josiah

> Nevertheless Josiah would not turn his face from him, but disguised himself... And the archers shot at king Josiah; and the king said to his servants, Have me away; for I am sore wounded. *(2 Chronicles 35:22-24, KJV)*

## Key Detail
Josiah disguises himself to fight Pharaoh Necho at Megiddo and is killed by "the archers." In the Mike-and-Dave Deuteronomy episode, [[Dave Butler]] raises the "tantalizing possibility" that, since the text never says *whose* archers, Josiah may have been shot by his own men — given that he had burned rival priests on their altars ([[2 Kings 23.5|2 Kings 23]]). Offered as speculation, "not doctrine," but consistent with reading Josiah as a violent reformer rather than an unambiguous hero.
