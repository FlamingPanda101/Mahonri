# Helaman 6.28

> Yea, it is that same being who put it into the hearts of the people to build a tower sufficiently high that they might get to heaven. And it was that same being who led on the people who came from that tower into this land; who spread the works of darkness and abominations over all the face of the land, until he dragged the people down to an entire destruction, and to an everlasting hell. *(Helaman 6:28, Book of Mormon)*

Mormon's editorial aside on the secret-combinations tradition traces it back to the adversary,
"that same being who put it into the hearts of the people to build **a tower** sufficiently
high that they might get to heaven."

## Why it's cited

A third Book of Mormon reference using only "a tower." Two distinct apologetic readings draw
on it: (1) the wording "a tower sufficiently high that they might get to heaven" is read as
*temple* language — a place of ascent to God — supporting the tower-as-temple thesis; and
(2) it again shows the Book of Mormon never names "Babel," so the Jaredite tower need not be
identified with the Genesis language-origin myth.
